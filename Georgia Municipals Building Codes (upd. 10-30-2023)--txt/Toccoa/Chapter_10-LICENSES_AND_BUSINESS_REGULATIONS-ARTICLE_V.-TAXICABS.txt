ARTICLE V. - TAXICABS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-126. - Definition.
For the purposes of this article, the term "taxicab" shall mean and include taxicabs and all vehicles used to transport passengers for hire.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-1)
Sec. 10-127. - Condition of vehicle; required markings.
(a)
No automobile with less than four doors shall be operated as a taxicab on the streets in the city.
(b)
The inside of all taxicabs shall be clean and free from litter, trash and bottles.
(c)
No taxicab shall be operated on the streets of the city which have bent or torn exterior parts or broken glass or anything which is unsightly.
(d)
Each taxicab shall be equipped with an inside dome light which shall light up when the rear door is open and the dome light shall cause enough light so that the photo identification card of the taxicab driver may be clearly seen.
(e)
Each taxicab shall be equipped with turn signals and brake lights.
(f)
It shall be necessary for each taxicab to have a sign painted on each side and on the rear of the vehicle in letters not less than three inches high, the word "Taxicab." There shall also be painted on each side and on the rear of each taxicab, numbers not less than two inches high, such numbers to be separate and distinct from that of any other taxicab in the city.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-2)
Sec. 10-128. - Identification when available for hire; restrictions on nonfare passengers.
Each taxicab operator shall indicate that the taxicab is being operated for fare paying passengers by placing a four-inch by six-inch card inside the lower right hand corner of the windshield which shall be plainly visible, such card being plainly marked "For Hire." No taxicab shall be operated for fare paying passengers without such a card being properly placed and no nonfare passengers shall sit in the front seat of the taxicab while the card is being displayed.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-3)
Sec. 10-129. - Fare cards to be displayed.
Each taxicab operator shall have attached to the upper half of the rear of the front seat of such taxicab a schedule of their current rates.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-4)
Sec. 10-130. - License required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or cause to be operated on the streets of the city any taxicab unless and until a valid business license for the operation of the vehicle shall have been first issued by the city to the owner of the vehicle each and every year in which the business is carried on.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-22)
Sec. 10-131. - Liability insurance required.
(a)
Before any license required by this article is issued to any person for the operation of any taxicab the owner thereof, for each such vehicle, shall file with the city, a liability insurance policy to indemnify for injuries to persons or property as follows:
(1)
For injuries and damages for any one person injured in an accident in the operation of the taxicab, an amount of not less than $25,000.00.
(2)
For injury or damages to more than one person injured in any one collision or accident in the operation of the vehicle, in an amount of not less than $50,000.00.
(3)
For injuries and damages to property in the operation of the vehicle at any time in any accident or collision in an amount of not less than the sum of $10,000.00.
(b)
Any policy of liability insurance filed pursuant to subsection (a) of this section shall be executed by a company authorized to do business in the state and shall contain a provision obligating the company issuing the policy to give at least ten days' written notice to the city prior to cancellation.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-23)
Sec. 10-132. - Issuance, revocation of licenses, permits generally.
Any and all licenses or permits issued pursuant to this article are to be issued at the discretion of the city. Any such permits or licenses so issued for the use of a vehicle or for an operator of any vehicle may be revoked after a hearing before the city commission and at the discretion of the city commission without assigning any reason for the revocation except that in its discretion the further operation of the vehicles and the issuance of the operator's permit are not in the public interest, convenience, necessity and that it is not desirable for the safety and general welfare of the citizens of the community.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-5)
Sec. 10-133. - Licenses, permits subject to future terms, conditions.
Any and all licenses or permits granted pursuant to this article shall be subject to any future ordinances and regulations further regulating and governing the operation of taxicabs, or to additional and other regulations, or changing, modifying or withdrawing in whole or in part the approval of any privilege for the operation of such vehicles, as may in the judgement of the city commission be consistent and to the best interest for the public welfare and safety.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-6)
Sec. 10-134. - Franchises.
(a)
The city, in the interest of public safety and the general welfare of the citizens of the city, does hereby grant taxicab franchises, subject to the payment of license fees and taxes as provided in this Code.
(b)
Such franchises will be granted by the city commission upon the recommendation of the city manager.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-21)
Secs. 10-135—10-145. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - OPERATOR'S PERMIT

Sec. 10-146. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or cause to be operated or to allow the operation on the streets of the city any taxicab by any person other than the licensed owner of the vehicle, unless and until the operator has first been issued an operator's permit by the city to drive a taxicab in the city.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-41)
Sec. 10-147. - Application.
Each person desiring a permit to operate a taxicab shall make application on forms provided by the city.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-42)
Sec. 10-148. - Identification card to be displayed; format and contents.
Each taxicab operator shall attach to the upper half of the rear of the front seat, so as to be plain and visible to those passengers sitting in the rear seat, a photo identification card, which card shall be at least three inches by five inches and shall contain a 1½-inch by 1½-inch glossy full face, light background photograph of the operator, his name and his taxicab operator's permit number.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-43)
Sec. 10-149. - Fares collected at termination of trip.
It shall be unlawful for any taxicab operator to operate a taxicab for fare-paying passengers on credit and any fare charge shall be collected immediately upon completion of the trip.
(Code 1985, § 8-4-44)
Secs. 10-150—10-170. - Reserved.
