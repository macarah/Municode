ARTICLE II. - GAS[2]

Cross reference— Gas code, § 5-66 et seq.

Sec. 23-31. - General responsibility of gas department.
The gas department shall be responsible for providing gas service to the city.
(Code 1985, § 6-2-1)
Sec. 23-32. - Meter required.
No person shall consume gas from the city natural gas system unless the gas to be consumed shall first pass through a meter which shall measure the quantity of gas to be consumed.
(Code 1985, § 6-2-12)
Sec. 23-33. - Deposit required.
Each tenant or lessee at any location serviced by the city gas department shall deposit with the city the current deposit per connection prior to connecting the gas service to the leased building and the owner or tenant of a mobile home shall deposit with the city the current deposit per mobile home prior to connecting the gas service to the mobile home.
(Code 1985, § 6-2-13)
Sec. 23-34. - Extension of gas mains and service lines.
A gas main line extension will be made by the city, up to 200 feet per customer, of various size pipe up to two inches in diameter along streets and highways. Any extension in excess of 200 feet shall be paid for by customer. All three-quarter-inch service lines from the main extension are to be free on each tap up to 100 feet, with additional pipe over the first 100 feet to be installed at cost.
(Code 1985, § 6-2-14)
Sec. 23-35. - Charge for reconnection of gas or lighting pilot lights.
A charge of $15.00 during normal business hours and a charge of $20.00 after normal business hours shall be made to reconnect gas service or to light pilots on appliances where the gas has been turned off either for nonpayment of bills or at the request or by action of the owner or tenant. The consumer contracting for gas service shall be responsible for the payment of the gas bill. No such gas service shall be resumed until the past due gas bill, including the cut-on charge, shall be fully paid.
(Code 1985, § 6-2-15)
Sec. 23-36. - Requirement for new residential structures.
Gas service shall not be provided to any new residential structure unless such residence shall use natural gas or heating and at least one other year-around appliance.
(Code 1985, § 6-2-16)
