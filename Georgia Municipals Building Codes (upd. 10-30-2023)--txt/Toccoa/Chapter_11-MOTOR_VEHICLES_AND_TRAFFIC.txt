ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 11-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted; penalty.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372, the Uniform Rules of the Road are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provisions of this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-12.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-1)
Sec. 11-2. - Penalty for violation.
Any person who shall violate any provision of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-15)
Sec. 11-3. - Signs, signals, devices—Authorized.
(a)
The city, is authorized to designate and maintain appropriate traffic control and pedestrian control signs, markings and devices.
(b)
All traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the "Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices" adopted by the Georgia Department of Transportation. All signs and signals required hereunder for a particular purpose shall, so far as practicable, be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this code shall be official traffic control devices of the city.
(c)
Any violation of any traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device established under this section shall be a violation of this Code.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-1)
Sec. 11-4. - Same—Obedience.
All traffic shall obey and be directed by official traffic control signals, signs and markings erected at street intersections and other locations now or hereafter approved by the city.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-13)
Sec. 11-5. - Temporary traffic regulations.
In cases where traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested upon occasions of parades, at theaters and other public assemblages where large numbers of vehicles are assembled, the police chief may make temporary rules directing and regulating the traffic in these congested districts, and any person, after being warned of the temporary traffic regulations, who shall violate them shall be liable therefor as for other violations of this Code.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-2)
Sec. 11-6. - Securing vehicle loads.
No person shall operate or load any vehicle on the public streets and roads of this city unless the vehicle is constructed, loaded and securely covered so as to prevent any of its load from dropping, escaping or shifting in such a manner as to create a safety hazard or in such a manner so as to litter the streets and roads of the city.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-3)
Sec. 11-7. - Obstruction of view and use by trees and shrubs; notice.
(a)
No person owning, occupying or in any way controlling property in the city, shall permit any tree, bush or shrub on the property to project over any street or sidewalk of this city so as to obstruct or interfere with the view and use of persons walking or riding in a vehicle on the street or sidewalk, or of other persons or vehicles approaching from cross or intersecting streets.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, whether the owner, tenant, agent or person controlling property in the city, to fail to remove any tree, bush or other obstruction recited in this section after ten days' notice by the city to do so.
(c)
If the owner, tenant, agent or person controlling the property which contains the obstruction fails to remove the obstruction within ten days of the notice, the city may remove the obstruction and assess the cost against the owner; such cost shall be collected by execution in the same manner as tax executions in favor of the city are collected.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-4)
Sec. 11-8. - Off-road vehicles.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-7-5, as amended, the city does hereby regulate vehicles under the State of Georgia "Off-Road Vehicle Act" as follows:
(1)
Off-road vehicles, as defined in the Off-Road Vehicle Act, may be operated within the city between the hours of 4:00 p.m. and 7:00 p.m. on Monday through Friday and from 12:00 noon until 7:00 p.m. on Saturdays; such off-road vehicles shall not be operated in the city on Sunday. The time as herein stated shall be the prevailing time then in use in the city.
(2)
In an R-I, single-family district, or in an R-II, two-family residential district, according to the classification of such districts as established by chapter 24, off-road vehicles as defined in the O.C.G.A. § 40-7-3, as amended, shall be operated only on the property used by the off-road vehicle's owner as a residence.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-5)
Sec. 11-9. - Abandoned vehicles.
Any motor vehicle remaining on the streets of the city for a consecutive period of 72 hours or more shall be presumed to have been abandoned and any member of the city police department may take possession of such an automobile and cause it to be impounded in an appropriate place.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-8)
Sec. 11-10. - Speed limit for trains.
It shall be unlawful for any railroad company to operate any train within the corporate limits of the city at a rate of speed greater than 45 miles per hour.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-12; Ord. No. 88-034, § 1, 10-10-88)
Secs. 11-11—11-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OPERATION OF VEHICLES

Sec. 11-31. - Weight limits for certain streets; exceptions.
(a)
No vehicle weighing in excess of 8,000 pounds shall go upon or pass over the named portions of certain streets. The schedule of such streets is on file in the city offices.
(b)
There are excepted from subsection (a) above. All vehicles delivering orders to the occupants or owners of property served by the above mentioned streets where such vehicles are actually being used for delivering such orders are exempt.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-6)
Sec. 11-32. - Following emergency vehicles.
The operator of any vehicle, other than one on an emergency call, shall not follow any emergency vehicle, as defined under state law, closer than 300 feet at such time as the emergency vehicle is on an emergency trip.
(Code 1985, § 9-1-7)
Cross reference— Fire prevention and protection, ch. 8.
Sec. 11-33. - Speed limits.
The maximum permissible speed for the operation of vehicles in the city shall be determined by the city from time to time and posted on the streets of the city.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-11)
Sec. 11-34. - One-way streets established.
Upon those streets and parts of streets and in those alleys designated by the city, vehicular traffic shall move only in the indicated direction when signs indicating the direction of traffic are erected and maintained at every intersection where movement in the opposite direction is prohibited.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-14)
Sec. 11-35. - Riding motorized vehicles on sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for anyone to ride a motorized vehicle on any of the sidewalks in the city. The area known as the "mall area" which includes that area formerly known as Doyle Street shall be considered to be a sidewalk as contemplated in this section. It shall also be unlawful for anyone to ride a bicycle on the "mall area."
(Code 1985, § 10-1-6)
Secs. 11-36—11-55. - Reserved.

Sec. 11-56. - Vehicles to be parked within marked spaces.
Whenever a space is marked off on any highway for the parking of an individual vehicle, every vehicle there parked shall be parked within the lines bounding that space.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-31)
Sec. 11-57. - Parking prohibited at all times in certain locations.
It is unlawful for the owner or operator of any motor vehicle or trailer to park the vehicle in the city in prohibited locations as specified by posted signs. The schedule of such locations is on file in the city offices. This requirement shall not apply to delivery or moving vehicles that are conducting business when such vehicles have insufficient access to a private driveway and parking on a public street would impede the flow of traffic or create hazardous conditions.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-32; Ord. No. 87-034, § 9-2-32, 9-28-87)
Sec. 11-58. - Parking prohibited in certain locations, certain days and hours.
It is unlawful for the owner or operator of any motor vehicle or other vehicle to park the vehicle in any of the places on the days and between the hours indicated and specified by posted signs indicating the prohibited parking. It is unlawful to park on the city's sidewalks.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-33)
Sec. 11-59. - Parking time limited in certain locations, certain days and hours.
It is unlawful for the owner or operator of any motor vehicle, or other vehicle, to park the vehicle or allow the vehicle to remain parked in any of the places on the streets and alleys of the city at any time on the days and between the hours indicated and specified by posted signs limiting parking time in certain locations on certain days and hours.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-34)
Sec. 11-60. - Special purpose parking zones established, parking otherwise prohibited.
It is unlawful for the owner or operator of any motor vehicle or other vehicle to park the vehicle or allow the vehicle to remain parked in any location on the streets and alleys of the city established and designated as special purpose parking zones indicated and specified by posted signs approved by the city commission.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-35)
Sec. 11-61. - Parking tickets; failure to pay; penalty.
Any person who has received as many as three parking tickets without having responded to same, shall be summoned to appear before the municipal judge and shall be subject to be punished as prescribed in section 1-12.
(Code 1985, § 9-2-36)
Sec. 11-62. - Fire lanes.
It shall be unlawful to park or operate any unauthorized vehicle in an area designated a fire lane. The state fire marshal shall designate and approve the street curbing, boundaries, and markings of the fire lane. The area of the fire lane shall have painted thereon the words "FIRE LANE" and "NO PARKING." Appropriate signs shall also be placed alongside the fire lane. The police chief and/or his designee shall have the authority to enforce this section by the issuance of a citation and/or the immediate removal of the vehicle at the owner's expense.
(Ord. of 11-28-11, § I)
