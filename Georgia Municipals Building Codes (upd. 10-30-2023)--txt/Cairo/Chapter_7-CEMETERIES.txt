ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 7-1. - Permit to dig grave.
No person shall dig, or cause to be dug, or direct or permit or allow to be dug any grave in a city cemetery upon any lot owned or controlled by such person without first obtaining a permit from the cemetery supervisor or such other person as may be authorized by the city manager showing the exact location of such grave. The city shall mark the four (4) corners of the grave space. Upon request by the funeral director, the city shall mark the location of interment but the city assumes no responsibility for ascertaining the proper location.
(Code 1965, § 8-3; Ord. No. 421, § 1, 11-23-92; Ord. No. 515, 2-8-99)
Sec. 7-2. - Permit to erect tombstones, etc.
No person shall erect, place or cause to be erected or placed any monument or tombstone or any curbing or coping in any city cemetery without first obtaining a permit to do so from the cemetery supervisor or such other person as may be authorized by the city manager showing the exact location thereof.
(Code 1965, § 8-5; Ord. No. 421, § 2, 11-23-92)
Sec. 7-3. - Digging in walkways, etc.
No person shall dig, or direct or instruct any person to dig any grave in any city cemetery of the city in any walkway, driveway or passageway, or erect any tombstone, monument, curbing or coping in any of the walkways, driveways, or passageways of the cemetery.
(Code 1965, § 8-4)
Sec. 7-4. - Using cemetery as walkway.
No person shall walk across or use as a walkway any part of any cemetery grounds in the city except the regular driveways, within the cemetery or except on regularly designated and established walkways between the private burial lots in the cemetery, nor shall any person park a vehicle thereon.
(Code 1965, § 8-1)
Sec. 7-5. - Vaults.
All interments hereafter made shall be in outer burial containers, sometimes called vaults, of concrete or stronger substance (such as stainless steel). The vault shall not be required for an indigent burial. (Indigence to be certified by Georgia Department of Family and Children Services.) The city will allow the use of a box in lieu of a vault.
(Ord No. 515, 2-8-99)
Sec. 7-6. - Supervision of sales; custodian of cemetery plats.
The city clerk shall handle all sales of cemetery lots and shall be the custodian of the master maps or plats of cemeteries located within the city.
(Ord. No. 397, § 2, 4-22-91)
Sec. 7-7. - Cost of cemetery lots.
Cemetery lots located within the city shall be sold at the prices established by the governing body.
(Ord. No. 397, § 2, 4-22-91)
Sec. 7-8. - Approval of conveyances.
(a)
No conveyance after the initial purchase of a lot in any cemetery shall be made without approval of the city.
(b)
The charge of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for each conveyance and legal and recording fees that may be incurred by the city shall be charged to the purchaser and collected by the city.
(c)
There will be no swapping of cemetery lots within the city owned cemeteries. If so desired after the purchase of a lot the purchaser may resell the lot to a third party at which time there will be an administrative fee of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) and any associated legal and recording fees.
(Ord. No. 397, § 2, 4-22-91; Ord. No. 585, § 1, 12-9-02)

Sec. 7-10. - Regulations.
The following regulations shall apply to all city owned cemeteries:
(1)
There shall be a minimum of eighteen (18) inches of ground cover over each vault.
(2)
All graves should be water packed if buried underground.
(3)
All excavation material shall be removed from the cemetery lot and placed at a location designated by the city.
(4)
Two (2) infant burials may occur on the same lot if vaults total less than eight (8) feet in length.
(5)
Any child vault over four (4) feet in length shall be buried in one (1) grave space.
(6)
There will be a record fee charge for each grave space or lot sold in any cemetery.
(7)
Upon the following conditions, the city shall permit up to six (6) infants to be buried within a lot:
a.
The casket shall be less than two (2) feet in length.
b.
The Department of Family and Children Services shall certify the parents of the infant to be indigent.
c.
The city shall permit the use of a box in lieu of a vault.
(8)
There shall be a maximum of two (2) cremation burials in each grave space.
(9)
All vaults shall be properly sealed.
(10)
All cemeteries shall be open to the public from sunrise until sunset daily.
(Ord. No. 617, § 1, 7-12-04; Ord. No. 628, § 1, 8-22-04; Ord. No. 638, § 1, 4-10-06; Ord. No. 639, § 1, 5-8-06)
Sec. 7-11. - Policy regarding grave adornment or decoration.
Grave sites may be decorated in a manner that is not generally offensive to public taste in such manner as interested parties desire. Such adornment or decoration shall be within the grave site area and shall in no manner obstruct or interfere with the maintenance of the cemetery. Adornment or decoration shall be the sole responsibility of the person or entity placing same. Such decoration or adornment may be removed by the City of Cairo if same shall be offensive, or interfere with the maintenance of the cemetery. The City of Cairo shall have no liability regarding the damage or destruction of grave adornment or decoration unless the City of Cairo shall have intentionally or by gross negligence damaged or destroyed the adornment or decoration.
(Ord. No. 696, § 1, 6-26-08)

ARTICLE II. - GREENWOOD CEMETERY AND CRESTVIEW CEMETERY

Sec. 7-20. - Greenwood Cemetery and Crestview Cemetery designated.
The cemetery located on the Thomasville-Cairo Highway lying in the northeastern section of the city, is hereby designated as Greenwood Cemetery, and Crestview Cemetery is located adjacent to and north of Greenwood Cemetery.
(Code 1965, § 8-11)
Sec. 7-21. - Marking corner of lots.
No coping, fence, hedge, planting, wall, ditch, bank, or enclosure of any kind or nature shall be placed, planted or used or have the appearance of being used for outlining, enclosing, marking off or making or designating any boundary line of any lot or plot in Greenwood Cemetery or Crestview Cemetery, except six-inch square white marble or concrete markers to mark the four (4) corners of any lot. The markers shall be flush with the ground and set within the boundaries of the lot.
(Code 1965, § 8-15; Ord. No. 397, § 3, 4-22-91)

Sec. 7-22. - Maximum height of markers.
No tombstone, marker, monument, slab or memorial shall be more than thirty-six (36) inches in height unless same shall be in the form of a mausoleum. Design of such mausoleum shall be brought before mayor and council for approval. Upon approval, such mausoleum shall be constructed within no less than eighteen (18) months.
(Code 1965, § 8-16; Ord. No. 397, § 3, 4-22-91; Ord. No. 658, § 1, 4-9-07)
Note— See the editor's note following § 7-21.

The provisions of this article pertaining to use of lots shall be incorporated in and made a part of the deed of conveyance to any lot or plot in Greenwood Cemetery or Crestview Cemetery.
(Code 1965, § 8-17; Ord. No. 397, § 3, 4-22-91)

ARTICLE III. - FOREST LAWN CEMETERY

Sec. 7-30. - Forest Lawn Cemetery designated.
The cemetery located on 11th Avenue, N.W. is hereby designated as Forest Lawn Cemetery.
(Ord. No. 397, § 4, 4-22-91)
Sec. 7-31. - Restrictions, nature of monument permitted, etc.
No lot shall be transferred from one party to another without approval of the city. No coping, fence, hedge, planting, wall, ditch, bank or enclosure of any kind or nature shall be placed, planted or used or have the appearance of being used for outlining, enclosing, marking off or making or designating any boundary line of any lot or plot in said cemetery. No burial vaults shall be permitted above ground level. All burial vaults shall use some type of concrete vault. All vaults must be buried eighteen (18) inches beneath the top soil. No lots shall be permitted with cement or rocks around monuments. No concrete slabs or ledgers shall be placed on grave. No monument shall exceed seventy-two (72) inches in height.
(Ord. No. 397, § 5, 4-22-91)
Sec. 7-32. - Provisions to be incorporated in conveyances.
The provisions of this article pertaining to use of lots shall be incorporated in and made a part of the deed of conveyance to any lot or plot in Forest Lawn Cemetery.
(Ord. No. 397, § 6, 4-22-91)
Sec. 7-33. - Sale of lots.
Grave spaces shall be sold only in one (1) section until such time as all grave spaces within that section have been sold.
(Ord. No. 515, 2-8-99; Ord. No. 617, § 1, 7-12-04)
Sec. 7-34. - Regulations regarding crypts.
(a)
All crypts (mausoleums) shall be made of marble or granite. No concrete crypts shall be allowed.
(b)
Crypts shall be no more than thirty-six (36) inches in height.
(c)
All crypts must have concrete foundation and a blue print showing foundation requirements as follows:
(1)
Concrete shall have no less than three thousand (3,000) PSI.
(2)
Concrete shall be at least one (1) foot deep.
(3)
Concrete shall contain one (1) inch re-bar six (6) inches up from ground level.
(4)
Rock of number seven (7) size must be six (6) inches deep for drainage as specified in blue print.
(5)
All crypts must face east and west.
(Ord. No. 515, 2-8-99; Ord. No. 528, § 1, 6-13-2000; Ord. No. 617, § 2, 7-12-04)
Sec. 7-35. - Regulations regarding columbarium.
The city may construct, shall maintain, and does establish the following rules and guidelines regarding any columbarium located in Forest Lawn Cemetery:
(1)
Transfer of ownership will only be allowed with written confirmation of the City of Cairo.
(2)
The City of Cairo shall be solely responsible for the upkeep of the columbarium. The City of Cairo is not responsible or liable for theft or damage to the contents of the columbarium.
(3)
Possession of the key to unlock each burial niche within the columbarium will be held by the City of Cairo. The provisions regarding the use of a niche shall be incorporated within the document transferring title to the niche.
(4)
The document relating to transfer of a burial niche shall be as prescribed by the City of Cairo.
(5)
A burial niche may only be unlocked or opened for interring cremains or upon the permanent removal of cremains before sealing. Following sealing of a niche, a niche may not be reopened. Any necessary reopening of a niche will require payment of a fee established by mayor and council.
(6)
All burial niches must be marked when cremains are interred. All markings shall be done through the City of Cairo in such manner as the City of Cairo shall prescribe.
(7)
The cost of a burial niche shall be five hundred dollars ($500.00) and each sealing and marking of a niche as permitted shall result in a fee of three hundred dollars ($300.00).
(Ord. No. 765, § 1, 3-23-15)
