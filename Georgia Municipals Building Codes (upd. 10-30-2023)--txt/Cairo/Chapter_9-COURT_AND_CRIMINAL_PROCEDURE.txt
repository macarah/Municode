Sec. 9-1. - Creation of court; powers; presiding officer.
(a)
There is hereby established a court to be known as the "Municipal Court, City of Cairo," which shall have jurisdiction and authority to try offenses against laws and ordinances of said city and to punish for a violation of the same.
(b)
Such court shall have the power to enforce its judgments by the imposition of such penalties as may be provided by law; to punish witnesses for nonattendance, and to punish also any person who may counsel or advise, aid, encourage, or persuade another whose testimony is desired or material in any proceeding before said court, to go or move beyond the reach of the process of the court; to try all offenses within the territorial limits of the city constituting traffic cases which under the laws of the state are placed within the jurisdiction of municipal or police courts to the extent of and in accordance with the provisions of such laws and all laws subsequently enacted amendatory thereof; to establish bail and recognizances to insure the presence of those charged with violations and to prescribe the conditions of forfeiture of the same; and to administer oaths and to perform all other acts necessary and proper to the conduct of said court.
(c)
The presiding officer of the court is the municipal judge who shall be known as judge of said court.
(d)
Said court shall be convened at such times as designated in this chapter or at such times as deemed necessary to keep current the dockets thereof.
(e)
The court shall sit at a place designated by the governing body.
(Ord. No. 255, § 2, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-2. - Municipal judge.
(a)
No person shall be qualified or eligible to serve as municipal judge unless such person shall have attained the age of twenty-one (21) years, shall not have been convicted of a crime involving moral turpitude and shall be qualified to vote in this county. Until such time that the governing body shall appoint a judge, the mayor, or in the mayor's absence the mayor pro tem shall act as judge. The compensation of the judge shall be fixed by the governing body.
(b)
The judge may appoint a judge pro hac vice to serve in the absence of the judge. Said judge pro hac vice shall have the same qualifications as the judge and shall be known as associate judge of said court.
(c)
Before entering on duties of office, the judge shall take an oath before an officer duly authorized to administer oaths in this state, that such judge will truly, honestly and faithfully, discharge the duties of the office to the best of such judge's ability without fear, favor, or partiality. This oath shall be entered upon the minutes of the meeting of the governing body.
(Ord. No. 255, § 2, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-3. - Jurisdiction.
(a)
The judge shall have power to impose fines, costs and forfeitures for the violation of any law or ordinance of the city.
(b)
Said municipal court is hereby empowered to punish violators of the rules, regulations, and ordinances of the city and may fix punishment for offenses within its jurisdiction being a fine in such amount as is permitted by state law or imprisonment for such time as is permitted by state law, or any combination of the two. Said fines may be collected by execution issued against all property of the defendant, both real and personal, if any to be found.
(c)
Said judge shall have the authority to punish for contempt by imposing a fine of two hundred dollars ($200.00) or imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days, or any combination of the two (2). The judge shall be, to all intents and purposes, a justice of the peace, so far as to enable such judge to issue warrants for offenses committed within the limits of the city, and to commit the offenders to jail or admit them to bail in bailable cases for their appearance at a regularly scheduled term of court of competent jurisdiction to be held in the city.
(d)
The court shall also have concurrent jurisdiction with that of a justice of the peace over offenses against the criminal laws of the state committed within the corporate limits.
(e)
Except as may be herein otherwise specified, the court is specifically invested with all jurisdiction and powers throughout the entire area within the corporate limits granted by state laws generally to mayors, municipal, recorders', and police courts, and particularly such laws as authorize the abatement of nuisances.
(f)
Said judge is hereby authorized to administer oaths.
(Ord. No. 255, § 2, 6-29-82; Ord. No. 683, § 1, 4-28-08; Ord. No. 782, § 1, 4-25-16)
Editor's note— Pursuant to instructions from the city, the fine of "six hundred dollars ($600.00)" in subsection (b) of § 9-3 has been changed to read "one thousand dollars ($1,000.00)," thereby reflecting the maximum amount of fine as set forth in § 8 of Art. No. 275 of the 1985 General Assembly.
Sec. 9-4. - Right of appeal.
The right of appeal and procedures pertaining to appeal bonds to the superior court from the municipal court shall lie in the same manner under the same procedure as generally prescribed for appeals and appeal bonds from the court of probate. An appeal to the superior court shall be a de novo proceeding, and the city shall be entitled to reimbursement of its costs therefor.
(Ord. No. 255, § 2, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-5. - Court costs; issuance of process.
(a)
In all cases in the municipal court, the costs incurred and allowable herein may be computed under the provisions of the laws of the state fixing costs in the justice of the peace courts of said state, or the governing body may establish a schedule of fees to defray costs of operation and the city shall be entitled to reimbursement of the costs, meals, transportation and caretaking of prisoners bound over to the superior court for violations of state law.
(b)
The governing body may also provide a uniform scale of costs of the clerk and police officers of said city for all services in the arrest and prosecution of offenders in the municipal court and in the issuance and collection of tax and other executions; and for their collections and payment into the city treasury.
(c)
The judge is hereby authorized to issue subpoenas to compel the attendance of witnesses to said court and to issue such other processes as may be necessary for the proper administration of said court.
(Ord. No. 255, § 2, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-6. - Sessions of court—Generally.
Sessions of the municipal court shall be convened at regular intervals and held on dates and times as set by the municipal court judge and may continue from hour to hour or day to day as the business of the court may necessitate, provided, any session may be dispensed with when there is no business pending in the court and no case ready for trial.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82; Ord. No. 683, § 2, 4-28-08)
Sec. 9-7. - Same—Special sessions.
(a)
The judge may hold special sessions when it is determined by the judge to be necessary or there is urgent cause for the trial of accused persons, before the next regular sitting of the court. The necessity or urgency of the cause shall be presumed from the fact of a session being held.
(b)
All defendants shall have at least six (6) hours notice of the session.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-8. - Rules of practice.
(a)
Practice in the municipal court shall be governed by the rules of the superior courts of this state as far as practicable, under the ordinances and charter of the city, and, where not provided otherwise by the charter and ordinances of the city, the court may prescribe additional rules of practice where there are no rules applicable.
(b)
The judge shall have the powers of a superior court judge to control the proceedings in said court.
(Ord. No. 255, §§ 2, 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-9. - Court bailiff; police attendance at court.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the chief of police or the chief's assistant or some member of the police force designated by the judge to attend each session of the court to act as bailiff, and the duty of each police officer making the arrest or having knowledge of the case for or against the defendant, to attend the trial of any person brought before the court.
(b)
Any police officer who is off duty during the hours that the court is in session and has been specifically notified to be present in court as a witness by the chief of police, judge, or court clerk shall be present at the appointed date and time.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82; Ord. No. 683, § 3, 4-28-08)
Sec. 9-10. - Clerk of court; subpoenas; dockets.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the city clerk, or an appropriate designee of the governing body, to attend each session of the municipal court and serve as clerk of court.
(b)
The clerk of court shall:
(1)
Prepare all subpoenas and warrants and maintain a docket thereof that will show the name of the person for whom the subpoena or warrant was issued, its date, at whose instance it was issued, and to whom delivered.
(2)
The judge may require that the clerk maintain a duplicate court docket and use the signed duplicate copy of the docket to make necessary entries in the master docket after each session of court, the master or original to be retained by the judge and the duplicate to be examined for completeness and signed by the judge.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-11. - Arrest and detention.
Every person arrested by a police officer of the city shall be held in custody by the arresting officer by and for the city, pending disposition as provided in this chapter.
(Code 1965, § 26-18(1))
Sec. 9-12. - Commitment hearing; jury trial; binding over.
(a)
All charges cognizable as violations of state laws shall be handled by a commitment hearing, provided however, that they may be adjudicated in the municipal court whenever the court would have jurisdiction over the offense, and the defendant does not demand a jury trial.
(b)
When a defendant is bound over to the appropriate state court, such defendant may be released under any of the following methods:
(1)
If the defendant has made bond to the city such defendant may be released on the same bond.
(2)
If the defendant has not made bond before being bound over, such defendant shall make bond at the county jail book-in and arraignment notice will then be given to the defendant and the defendant must be delivered, in person, by the city police, to the county authorities.
(c)
There shall be no jury trials in the municipal court; where one is required by law or where one is rightfully demanded, said case shall be bound over to the appropriate court by the municipal judge.
(Code 1965, § 26-21; Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-13. - Appearance bond; procedure for accepting.
Offenses against the ordinances of the city shall be bailable as follows:
(1)
The arresting officer, chief of police, recorder or any other officer designated by the chief of police, may accept bond of the accused payable to the city conditioned upon the appearance of the accused at the next session or any subsequent session of the municipal court as stated in the bond, to answer the charge.
(2)
The bond shall be in writing in an amount established in a schedule approved by the recorder.
(3)
A person arrested for violation of a city ordinance may be released on such person's recognizance, may be released on bond without security or in lieu thereof may deposit cash or such person's driver's license with the arresting officer.
(4)
Any person released under subsection (3) above shall receive a receipt for any driver's license deposited or, if released on the person's own recognizance, shall sign and receive a copy of the citation or ticket describing the violation for which the person was arrested.
(5)
All persons arrested or notified by citation or ticket of parking violations shall be released on their own recognizance.
(Code 1965, § 26-19; Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-14. - Forfeiture of appearance bonds.
(a)
The municipal judge or the judge's designee shall call the case in its regular order, and, if the accused is not in court and does not answer the call, and, if no good and sufficient reason is given for nonappearance, the judge shall enter on the docket where the case is stated "Bond forfeited," or similar words.
(b)
Upon forfeiture of any bond, the cash deposited as security shall be paid to the city clerk, and it shall become the property of the city.
(c)
Upon the forfeiture of any bond signed by a person as surety, the city clerk shall issue an execution against the principal and surety on the bond for the amount thereof which shall be collected as tax executions are collected.
(Code 1965, § 26-20; Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-15. - Pleas.
(a)
Upon a case being called, the municipal judge shall inform the defendant of the charge against such defendant and shall inquire as to how such defendant desires to plead thereon. Thereupon, the defendant shall enter one of the following pleas:
(1)
Guilty;
(2)
Not guilty;
(3)
Nolo contendere (with the permission of the court); or
(4)
A former judgment of conviction or acquittal of the offense charged.
(b)
If the defendant refuses to enter a plea, the court shall enter a plea of not guilty and set the case down for trial.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-16. - Continuance of cases.
The municipal judge may refuse to continue a case, except for good cause shown.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-17. - Appearance of complaining witnesses.
A material or complaining witness in any contested prosecution shall appear at the session at which the prosecution is docketed. The prosecution may be dismissed if the material witness fails to appear.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-18. - Judgment and sentences.
Upon a judgment or plea of guilty the court shall impose sentence. The court may:
(1)
Impose a fine, with or without a commitment for confinement, compulsory work or both;
(2)
Commit the defendant to confinement, compulsory work or both;
(3)
Suspend the execution of the sentence in whole or in part;
(4)
Defer the execution of the sentence or any portion or portions thereof to one or more fixed dates in the future; or
(5)
Impose such other form of judgment or sentence as the court deems appropriate, including but not limited to emotional maturity training, driver education, and to probate or suspend any of the foregoing sentences upon such conditions as the court shall deem appropriate.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)

Sec. 9-19. - Enforcement of fines by levy and execution; payment in advance of court date.
(a)
For fines that may be due by any defendant, execution may be issued by the city clerk and collected as tax executions are collected.
(b)
Persons charged with traffic violations may, prior to the time for their court appearance, plead guilty in writing and pay to the city as their fine the amount set as the appearance bond for the offense charged; except that those persons charged with driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs and those charged with an offense in connection with an automobile collision shall be required in all cases to appear before the court for disposition of the case.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-20. - Contempt of court.
Failure to appear pursuant to any summons or subpoena, disrespectful conduct or failure to comply with any other order or judgment of the court shall constitute contempt of court and be punishable as provided in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
Sec. 9-21. - Court dates; contempt warrants; appeals.
(a)
Court date will be made known to the defendant at the time of the arrest process and shall be properly indicated on the copy of charges given to the defendant by the arresting officer.
(b)
Warrants for contempt of court charges may be issued following each session of court.
(c)
Appeals may be taken from the municipal court in accordance with applicable state law.
(Ord. No. 255, § 3, 6-29-82)
