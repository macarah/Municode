ARTICLE II. - SHALLOW WELLS

Sec. 24-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Contractor means a person licensed to install wells, pumps or plumbing equipment.
Sewage works means all facilities for collecting, pumping, treating and disposing of sewage owned by the city.
Shallow well means a privately owned, drilled or bored well utilizing the local shallow sand aquifer, having a capacity of less than 100,000 gallons per day.
Superintendent means the director of public works of the city or his authorized deputy, agent or representative.
Water supply system means the source, supply, storage, distribution, purification and pumping facilities of the city.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. I, 8-11-82)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 24-27. - Permit.
(a)
No unauthorized person shall drill, bore or otherwise install a shallow well, or modify an existing shallow well, without first obtaining a written permit from the superintendent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld if the applicant is in compliance with the terms and conditions contained in this article.
(b)
Permits shall be applied for at city hall in person. A one-time permit fee shall accompany the application. Permits shall be renewable on an annual basis.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. II, §§ 1, 2, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-28. - Modification of existing wells.
For purposes of this article, deepening or rehabilitation of an existing well shall be regarded as development of a new well.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. II, § 3, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-29. - Initial inspection.
Shallow wells constructed or modified after the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives shall not be operated until inspected by the superintendent or his designee and found in compliance with the provisions contained in this article.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. II, § 4, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-30. - Compliance with applicable regulations.
The type, depth, size and location of a shallow well shall comply with all city codes, county health ordinances and regulations of the state department of human resources and department of natural resources.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. II, § 5, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-31. - Right of entry.
(a)
The superintendent and other duly authorized employees of the city bearing proper credentials and identification shall be permitted to enter all properties for the purposes of inspection, observation, measurement, sampling and testing pertinent to effects on the community water or sewer system in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(b)
While performing the necessary work or inspections on private properties as provided in this section, the superintendent or other duly authorized employees of the city shall observe all safety rules applicable to the premises established by the contractor or property owner.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. V, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-32. - Correction of violations.
(a)
The superintendent and other duly authorized employees of the city bearing proper credentials and identification identifying them as employees are authorized and empowered to serve written notice to any and all violators of this article, the notice to be delivered to the address shown on the request for the permit.
(b)
All persons notified of violations of this article shall have a period of time not exceeding 30 days for the correction of the violation. An extension of 30 days may be granted in the discretion of the superintendent for bringing the well into compliance with this article.
(c)
Persons who fail to make the appropriate corrections to bring the well into compliance with this article within the time limits provided in this section shall be subject to a maximum fine of $1,000.00 per day for each day that the violation continues after the allotted time period for corrections.
(d)
Violators who fail to bring their well into full compliance in the allotted time period as provided in this section may also have their permits revoked by the superintendent after opportunity for hearing, which hearing shall be requested by the person notified of the violation. The hearing shall be held within 30 days by the superintendent.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. VI, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-33. - Work to be done by licensed contractor.
The drilling, boring or rehabilitation of a shallow well and the associated pipe work shall be done or installed by a licensed well driller or plumber.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. III, § 1, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-34. - Interconnections with city water system.
No person shall make any interconnection between a shallow well system or its pumps, tanks or hoses and the city water supply system in any manner, through either temporary or permanent piping.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. III, § 2, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-35. - Minimum size of lot.
No shallow well shall be permitted on a lot less than 10,000 square feet in size.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. IV, § 2, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-36. - Distance from sewers or septic tanks.
No person may locate a well less than 50 feet from a septic tank or less than 100 feet from a septic tank absorption field or less than ten feet from a sewer. Greater distances from these type facilities may be required by the city due to topography and soil conditions.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. IV, § 3, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-37. - Discharge of water to public sewers.
No person may discharge any runoff, excess discharge or any flow whatsoever originating from a shallow well into facilities connecting with the city sewage treatment works.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. IV, § 5, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-38. - Use of water.
A permitted shallow well shall be used for nonpotable outdoor purposes only.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. IV, § 1, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-39. - Piping water to other premises.
No person may pipe water to another residence, business or facility. The permit issued under this article shall be for one individual's use only.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. IV, § 4, 8-11-82)
Sec. 24-40. - Authority to prohibit use.
(a)
The superintendent or his designee shall be authorized, upon written notice to all permit holders, to prohibit further use of all shallow wells when conditions are such that the public water supply for the city or the aquifer from which it draws is adversely affected in any way by the operation of the shallow wells.
(b)
Any violations of any order issued under the emergency powers contained in this section shall be treated as are other violations provided for in this article.
(Ord. No. 1982-3, art. VII, 8-11-82)
