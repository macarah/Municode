Chapter 12 - ELECTIONS[1]

State Law reference— Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.

Sec. 12-1. - District; polling place.
The area within the corporate limits shall constitute an election district in the city. Those persons living within the designated election district shall vote at the City Hall.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-1; Code 1998, § 26-1)
Sec. 12-2. - Officials.
The Municipal Election Superintendent shall be a person or committee selected by the City Council in a public meeting and such selection shall be recorded in the minutes of such meeting. In the event that the Mayor and Council fails to make an appointment, the City Clerk shall serve as the Municipal Superintendent.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-2; Code 1998, § 26-2; Ord. No. 19-2008, 7-7-2008)
Sec. 12-3. - Regular elections; time for holding.
The City Council shall cause an election to be held at the City Hall or such other place in the city as the City Council shall direct and designate. All general municipal elections shall be held as provided by law, on the Tuesday next following the first Monday in November of each year.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-4(a); Code 1998, § 26-4)
State Law reference— Date of municipal elections, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-9.
Sec. 12-4. - State law applies.
All elections shall be held and conducted in accordance with the Georgia Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.), as now or hereafter amended.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-5; Code 1998, § 26-5)
