ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 40-1. - Water connection required; application.
modified
All persons having homes, stores, shops, offices or places of business, either as owner, tenant or occupant, in the city shall be connected to the city water system. They shall make application to the city to tap onto the main at the street nearest such home, store, office, shop or place of business.
(Code 1935, § 107; Code 1987, § 11-1; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-2. - Water cutoff required between meter and house.
modified
All water customers shall install a water cutoff between the house and the city's water meter.
(Mo. of 2-11-1982; Code 1987, § 11-13; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-3. - Tap fees.
modified
(a)
Residential. Residential water, and sewer connection fees, shall be charged, per connection, based on a rate schedule maintained and available at city hall.
(1)
A three-fourths-inch meter will normally be provided for residential water service. Tap fees for larger services will be according to those listed below for commercial services.
(b)
Commercial. Commercial water and sewer connection fees, shall be charged, per connection based on a rate schedule maintained and available at city hall.
(1)
The city reserves the right to negotiate higher or lower tap fees based on special circumstances determined by the city for commercial and industrial customers, multi-unit dwellings (such as apartments), and for residential subdivision connections.
(2)
All connections are subject to approval of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources rules and requirements.
(3)
Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary in this article, a commercial development or an apartment complex may utilize a master water meter to be billed by the city for the entire development or complex, provided the development or complex has more than four units. A holder of a master water meter may meter or require its tenants to meter individual usage and reimburse the holder of the master meter for the water and sewer usage; provided, however, said reimbursement shall not exceed the cost of the water or sewer paid to the city by the master meter holder.
For all commercial developments or apartment complexes served by a master meter, water and sewer tap fees shall be based on the size of the master meter. All development must meet the requirements outlined in the 2010 Georgia Water Stewardship Act.
(Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 3; Ord. of 3-11-1982; Ord. of 5-7-1985(1); Ord. of 10-3-1988; Ord. of 11-3-1997; Ord. of 2-4-1999; Code 1987, § 11-2; Ord. No. 2003-2, 4-14-2003; Ord. of 2004-1, § 11-2, 6-14-2004; Ord. No. 2006-1, 3-13-2006; Ord. No. 2006-2, 3-13-2006; Ord. No. 2010-3, 3-8-2010; Ord. No. 2018-1 , 5-14-2018; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-4. - Security deposits for service.
modified
(a)
The customer shall make application for utility service, in person, at the municipal complex or online and at the same time shall make a cash security deposit or be billed for a deposit for utility service based on a rate schedule maintained and available at city hall.
(b)
Additionally, all commercial rental properties will be required to make a deposit of two months' minimum utility bill.
(c)
The customer's deposit will be held for one year whenever the customer is the owner. On the first anniversary of the date the owner's deposit was received, the customer may request a full refund of their deposit, provided all previous bills have been paid before the tenth of the month on which they were due. Should the record of payment not be in accordance with the city regulation, the deposit will be held until such time as the owner customer has paid such bills by the city regulation for 12 months consecutively. Deposits on rental property will be held and applied to the customer's last utility bill.
(Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 1; Ord. of 3-11-1982; Ord. of 9-4-1984; Ord. of 3-27-2000; Code 1987, § 11-3; Ord. No. 2010-2, 3-8-2010; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-5. - City's responsibility and liability for water service line.
modified
(a)
The city shall run a water service line from its distribution line to the property line where the distribution line exists or is to be constructed and runs immediately adjacent and parallel to the property to be served. No service charge, other than the connection fee referred to in section 40-3, will be made for a three-fourths-inch meter. A proportionately greater charge than the connection fee will be made for a meter of larger dimensions.
(b)
The city may make connections to service other properties not adjacent to its lines upon payment of reasonable costs for the extensions of its distribution lines as may be required to render such service.
(c)
The city may install its meter at or near the property line or, at the city's option, on the consumer's property within three feet of the property's line.
(d)
The city reserves the right to refuse water and sewer service unless the consumer's lines or piping are installed in such a manner as to prevent cross connections or backflow.
(e)
Under normal conditions the consumer will be notified of any anticipated interruptions of service by the city.
(Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 5; Code 1987, § 11-4; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-6. - Consumer's responsibility and liability for water service.
modified
(a)
Water furnished by the city shall be used for consumption by the consumer, members of the consumer's household and employees only. Except as provided in subsection 40-3(b), the consumer shall not sell water to any other person or permit any other person to use such water. Water shall not be used for irrigation, fire protection, or other purposes, except when water is available in sufficient quantity without interfering with the regular domestic consumption in the area served. Disregard for this rule shall be sufficient cause for refusal or discontinuance of service.
(b)
Where the meter or meter box is placed on the premises of a consumer, a suitable place shall be provided by the consumer therefor, unobstructed and accessible at all times to the meter reader.
(c)
The consumer shall furnish and maintain a private cutoff valve on the consumer's side of the meter.
(d)
The consumer's piping and apparatus shall be installed and maintained by the consumer at the consumer's expense, in a safe and efficient manner, and in accordance with the sanitary regulations of the state board of health.
(e)
In order to be received as a consumer and entitled to receive water from the city's water system, all applicants must offer proof that any private wells located on their property are not physically connected to the lines of the city's covenant and agree that, so long as they continue to be consumers of the city, they will not permit the connection of any private wells on their property to the city's water system.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to tap, connect with, disturb, damage, turn on or off any main, service connection or meter of any public utility in the city, whether such device is within the public ways or on private premises, unless such person is acting by the consent of or under direction of the utility owning such property.
(1)
Proof that any appliances, tools, devices or instruments of any kind have been used to improperly pass water around the meter so as to prevent the meter from duly registering shall constitute prima facie evidence of the commission or the intent to commit the acts described in subsection (f) of this section.
(2)
Where there is no evidence to the contrary, the following shall be presumed to be responsible for the illegal act set forth in subsection (f) of this section:
a.
The person performing any of the illegal acts;
b.
The person who, with knowledge of such violation, receives the benefit of such service without proper charge as a result of the improper action; and
c.
The person in whose name the utilities are in at the time of the illegal act.
(Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 6; Code 1987, § 11-5; Ord. of 12-7-1992; Ord. No. 2006-3, 3-13-2006; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-7. - Access to premises and extensions of system.
modified
(a)
Duly authorized agents of the city shall have access at all hours to the premises of the consumer for the purpose of installing or removing city property; inspecting piping, reading and testing meters; or for any other purpose in connection with the utility service and its facilities.
(b)
Extensions to the system shall be made only when the consumer shall grant or convey or shall cause to be granted or conveyed to the city a permanent easement or right-of-way across any property traversed by the water, sewer lines.
(Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 7; Code 1987, § 11-6; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-8. - Water and sewer monthly rate schedules.
modified
Water and sewer rates shall be set by the mayor and city council by resolution and a copy of the rates kept on file in the office of the city clerk. All monthly charges or fees which are provided for, charged, or collected herein by the City of Byron on a monthly basis may be adjusted annually based on the amount of increases in the index numbers of the Consumer Price Index (CPI) issued by the Bureau of Labor Statistics, United States Department of Labor, or other effective economic indicators. Sec. 40-10. - Minimum charges.
modified
The minimum charge, as provided in the utility rate schedules, shall be made for such connection subscribed for. Utility service furnished for a given lot shall be used on that lot only, and, except for water for fire protection, the city shall not under any condition furnish utility service free of charge to anyone.

Sec. 40-11. - Change of occupancy.
modified
Not less than three days' notice must be given, in person or in writing, at the city hall, or online to discontinue water and sewer service or to change occupancy. The outgoing party shall be responsible for all water consumed up to the time of departure or the time specified for departure, whichever period is longer. The new occupant shall apply for service within 48 hours after occupying the premises, and failure to do so will make such person liable for paying for the water, gas and sewer service furnished since the last meter reading.
(Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 3; Code 1987, § 11-10; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-12. - Billings; collecting.
modified
(a)
Bills to customers for utility service shall be mailed out on such days of each month as may be determined desirable by the city. Bills shall be due on the first day of the month and shall be paid at the city hall or online; a failure to receive bills or notices shall not prevent such bills from becoming delinquent, nor relieve the customer from payment of the bills. The failure to pay charges duly imposed shall result in the imposition of the following penalties:
(1)
Nonpayment by the tenth of the month will be subject to a penalty of ten percent of the delinquent account.
(2)
Nonpayment by the 20th of the month may result in utility service being shut off from the user's property and an additional delinquent fee based on a rate schedule maintained and available at city hall per water service provided to the address. Whenever the 20th of the month falls on a Friday, Saturday, Sunday or legal holiday, the customer shall have through the next business day to pay the bill.
(3)
Nonpayment for 60 days after original due date will allow the city, in addition to all other rights and remedies, to terminate the agreement, and in such event, the user shall not be entitled to receive, nor the city obligated to supply, any utility service under this agreement.
(b)
Service disconnected for nonpayment of bills will be restored only after bills are paid in full and such security deposit as may be required by the city is made.
(Code 1935, § 108; Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 9; Code 1987, § 11-11; Ord. No. 2002-9, 5-13-2002; Ord. No. 2005-4, 6-13-2004; Ord. No. 2014-3, 10-13-2014; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2022-6 , adopted Feb. 14, 2022, amended the title of § 40-12 to read as herein set out. The former § 40-12 title pertained to billings; collecting.
Sec. 40-13. - Discontinuance of service.
modified
(a)
When utility service is discontinued and all bills paid, the security deposit shall be refunded to the consumer by the city.
(b)
Upon discontinuance of service for nonpayment of bills, the security deposit will be applied by the city toward settlement of the account. Any balance will be refunded to the consumer; however, if the security deposit is insufficient to cover the bill, the city may proceed to collect the balance as well as the cost of collection in the usual way provided by law for collection of debts.
(c)
The city reserves a right to discontinue its service without notice for the following additional reasons:
(1)
To prevent fraud or abuse.
(2)
The consumer's willful disregard of the city's rules.
(3)
Emergency repairs.
(4)
Insufficiency of supply due to circumstances beyond the city's control.
(5)
Legal processes.
(6)
Direction of public authorities.
(7)
Strike, riot, fire, flood, unavoidable accident.
(Code 1935, § 112; Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 10; Code 1987, § 11-12; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-14. - Complaints; adjustments.
modified
(a)
If the consumer believes the utility bill to be in error, such consumer shall present the claim to the department of public works, before the bill becomes delinquent. Such claim, if made after the bill has become delinquent, shall not be effective in preventing discontinuance of service as provided in this article. The consumer may pay such bill under protest, and the payment shall not prejudice the claim.
(b)
The city will make a special meter reading at the request of a consumer for a fee based on a rate schedule maintained and available at city hall. However, if such special reading discloses that the meter was overread, no charge will be made.
(c)
Meters will be tested at the request of the consumer upon payment to the city of the actual costs of making the test. However, if the meter is found to over register beyond three percent of the correct volume, no charge will be made.
(d)
If the seal of a meter is broken by other than the city's representative or if the meter fails to register correctly or is stopped for any cause, the consumer shall pay an amount estimated from the record of such consumer's previous bills and/or from other proper data.
(Ord. of 6-8-1978, § 11; Code 1987, § 11-14; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 40-15. - Dimensions for installation of mainlines.
modified
All utility companies will be required to install their mainlines in accordance with the following dimensions:
(1)
Power mains shall be installed 12 feet from the street edge of the pavement at a minimum depth of four feet.
(2)
Gas mains shall be installed four feet from the street edge of the pavement on the side opposite the water main at a minimum depth of three feet. Installation shall be on the south or east side of the street.
(3)
Water mains shall be installed four feet from the street edge of the pavement at a minimum depth of four feet. Installation shall be on the north or west side of the street.
(4)
Sewer mains will normally be installed near the street centerline, with depth determined by operational requirements.
(5)
Telephone mains shall be installed eight feet from the street edge of the pavement at a minimum depth of two feet.
(6)
Cable television lines shall be installed one foot from the street edge of the pavement at a minimum depth of two feet.
(Ord. of 12-6-1993; Code 1987, § 11-15; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)

Sec. 40-17. - Utility services outside city.
modified
(a)
When a property owner requests utility services to property not within the corporate city limits, at the discretion of the city, the owner will be required to:
(1)
Petition for annexation, if the property is contiguous to the existing city limits, prior to commencement of construction of utility lines; or
(2)
Execute a restrictive covenant consenting to annexation of such property into the city when the property becomes contiguous.
(b)
When such property is not contiguous and execution of the covenant is required, the owner will also be required to submit a certificate of title to the city attorney to verify ownership of the property. The covenant will be recorded in the property records at the county courthouse and will stipulate that, if the property becomes contiguous in the future and the property owner refuses to petition for annexation, the city will have the authority to terminate utility services.
(c)
Construction of utility lines will not begin until the requirements of this policy are met, which shall be indicated by authorization of the city council.
(Ord. No. 2002-12, § 11-17, 9-9-2002; Ord. No. 2022-6 , 2-14-2022)
