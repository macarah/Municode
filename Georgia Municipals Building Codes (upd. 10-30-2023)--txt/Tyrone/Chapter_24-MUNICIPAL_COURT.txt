ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Creation.
There is hereby created a court to be known as the "Municipal Court of the Town of Tyrone," which shall have the powers and duties provided by the Charter and state law.
(Code 1984, § 2-3-1)
Sec. 24-2. - Prosecution of non-traffic ordinances by citation.
Prosecution of non-traffic ordinance violations in the municipal court may be instituted by citation, without the necessity of a warrant, where the officer issuing the citation deems it unnecessary to make an arrest or require an appearance bond to be made by the defendant against whom the charge is directed in either of the following instances:
(1)
Where the violation occurs in the presence of a police officer of the town or other official or other person otherwise authorized by ordinance to issue citations; or
(2)
Upon written complaint of a citizen setting forth the details of the alleged violation.
(Code 1984, § 2-3-6)
State Law reference— Trial of ordinance violations upon citation, O.C.G.A. § 36-32-10.2.
Sec. 24-3. - Contents of citation.
The citation so issued shall bear teste in the name of the judge of the municipal court, and shall contain the signature of the clerk of the court and the police officer or other official or person issuing the citation. It shall command the presence of the person to whom it is directed to be and appear in the municipal court on a date not less than seven days after the date of issuance and service, and shall specifically allege the violation charged by specifying the offense, the date and time of its commission as near as practicable, the ordinance number alleged to have been violated, and the identity of the person making the complaint if other than the officer, official or person issuing the citation. The citation shall bear a distinctive number corresponding to the docket number of the case. It shall be issued in triplicate with the original or white-colored citation filed with the court clerk, the yellow-colored copy being handed to and served upon the person to whom the citation is directed, and the blue-colored copy being retained by the issuing officer, official or other person authorized by ordinance to issue such citation. The person to whom such citation is directed shall personally acknowledge service thereon.
(Code 1984, § 2-3-7)
Sec. 24-4. - Form of citation.
The citation shall be in substantially the following form:
Town of Tyrone, County of Fayette, State of Georgia:
Number ________ To: Defendant.
You are hereby commanded, laying all other business aside, to be and appear in the Municipal Court of the Town of Tyrone on the _______ day of _______ 20___ at _______ o'clock ___.m. to answer the charge of the violation of Town ordinance number _______, alleged to have been committed by you on the _______ day of _______ 20___ at _______ o'clock ___.m. at said offense being more particularly described as follows ________:
The above violation was committed in the presence of the undersigned officer, official or other person (or) the name of the person making the complaint upon which this citation is issued is ________.
Herein fail not, under penalty of $200.00 fine and/or ten days in jail.
Witness the Hon. ________ Judge of said Court, this day _______ of 20___
EXPAND
_____
Court Clerk, Police Officer, Official or other authorized Person
  Service of the foregoing citation is hereby acknowledged.
This _______ day of _______ 20___
Defendant ________
(Code 1984, § 2-3-8)
Sec. 24-5. - Interpretation of other authority.
Whenever an ordinance of the town directs, authorizes or requires in non-traffic cases the issuance of a summons or citation, it shall be deemed and interpreted to be in compliance therewith to proceed as authorized by sections 24-2, 24-3, 24-4 and 24-6.
(Code 1984, § 2-3-9)
Sec. 24-6. - Penalty for failure to appear.
The failure of a defendant to appear in court as directed by a citation issued under the provisions of sections 24-2 through 24-5 may be treated by the judge of the municipal court as contempt and punishable as provided by the Charter of the town.
(Code 1984, § 2-3-10)
Sec. 24-7. - Cumulative procedure.
Sections 24-2 through 24-5 shall in no way be interpreted as prohibiting the application for or the issuance of a town warrant in any instance where the officer deems such course to be appropriate, and should be interpreted as constituting cumulative procedures insofar as the prosecution and disposition of non-traffic ordinance violations are concerned.
(Code 1984, § 2-3-11)
Secs. 24-8—24-10. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OFFICE OF THE PROSECUTING ATTORNEY

Sec. 24-11. - Intent.
In enacting this article, it is the intent of the town to comply with Georgia law, particularly O.C.G.A. tit. 15, ch. 18, art. 5.
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 24-12. - Establishment of office.
The office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court is hereby established for the purpose of providing representation of the Town of Tyrone in matters pertaining to ordinance violations of the town and state offenses enforceable in the municipal courts as allowed by Georgia law. The prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall be a part time position.
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 24-13. - Qualifications.
Any person appointed as the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court of the Town of Tyrone shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia and admitted to practice before the trial and appellate courts of this state. Nothing in this article shall prevent the town from appointing the town attorney to be the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court, so long as all other requirements under the law are met.
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 24-14. - Oath.
The prosecuting attorney for the municipal court of the Town of Tyrone shall take and subscribe to the following oath:
"I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as prosecuting attorney of the Municipal Court for the Town of Tyrone."
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 24-15. - Term of office.
Unless otherwise provided by the Charter for the Town of Tyrone, the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall serve a term of office of one year.
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 24-16. - Jurisdiction, duties, and authority.
The office of the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court of the Town of Tyrone shall have the duty and authority to represent the town as defined by the Town Charter and by state law, particularly as described in O.C.G.A. § 15-18-96.
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 24-17. - Assistant prosecuting attorney.
The prosecuting attorney of the municipal court may appoint one or more assistant prosecuting attorneys whose appointment shall be ratified by the town council before becoming an assistant prosecuting attorney for the municipal court. Such assistant prosecuting attorney shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia or satisfy the provisions of the Third Year Practice Act, found in the O.C.G.A. § 15-18-22.
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 24-18. - Ratification.
This article shall ratify all actions that have been taken by the persons acting in the positions of the office of the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court to date as authorized by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2012-08-A, § 2, 7-19-2012)
