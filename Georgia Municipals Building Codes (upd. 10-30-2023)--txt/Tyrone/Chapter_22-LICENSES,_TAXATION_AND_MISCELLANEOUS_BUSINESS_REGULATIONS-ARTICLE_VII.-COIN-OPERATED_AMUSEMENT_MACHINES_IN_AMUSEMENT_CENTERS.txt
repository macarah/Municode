ARTICLE VII. - COIN-OPERATED AMUSEMENT MACHINES IN AMUSEMENT CENTERS

Sec. 22-310. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Amusement center means any establishment where any coin-operated amusement machine is provided for play to the public and more than 50 percent of the total gross revenue of the establishment is generated from coin-operated amusement machines.
Amusement center proprietor means any proprietor of an amusement center as both terms are defined herein.
Coin-operated amusement machine.
(1)
The term "coin-operated amusement machine" means every machine of any kind or character used by the public to provide amusement or entertainment whose operation requires the payment of or the insertion of a coin, bill, other money, token, ticket or similar object and the result of whose operation depends in whole or in part upon the skill of the player, whether or not it affords an award to a successful player pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(b)—(f), and which can be legally shipped interstate according to federal law. Examples of bona fide coin-operated amusement machines include, but are expressly not limited to, the following:
a.
Pinball machines;
b.
Console machines;
c.
Video games;
d.
Crane machines;
e.
Claw machines;
f.
Pusher machines;
g.
Bowling machines;
h.
Novelty arcade games;
i.
Foosball or table soccer machines;
j.
Miniature racetrack, football or golf machines;
k.
Target or shooting gallery machines;
l.
Basketball machines;
m.
Shuffleboard machines;
n.
Kiddie ride games;
o.
Skeeball machines;
p.
Air hockey machines;
q.
Roll down machines; and
r.
Any other similar amusement machine that can be legally operated in the state.
(2)
The term "coin-operated amusement machine" does not include the following:
a.
Coin-operated washing machines or dryers;
b.
Vending machines which for payment of money dispenses products or services;
c.
Gas and electric meters;
d.
Pay telephones;
e.
Pay toilets;
f.
Cigarette vending machines;
g.
Coin-operated vending machines;
h.
Coin-operated scales;
i.
Coin-operated gumball machines;
j.
Coin-operated television sets that provide cable or network programming;
k.
Coin-operated massage beds;
l.
Coin-operated pool table or coin-operated billiard table as defined in O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1(3);
m.
Machines that are not legally permitted to be operated in the state;
n.
Every machine of any kind or character used by the public to provide music whose operation requires the payment of or the insertion of a coin, bill, other money, token, ticket or similar object such as jukeboxes or other similar types of music machines.
Operator means any person, firm, corporation, partnership or association who sets up for operation by another and leases or rents for the purpose of operation by another any machine as herein regulated, whether such leasing or renting be for a fixed charge or rental, or on the basis of a division of the income derived from such device, or otherwise.
Proprietor means any person, firm, corporation, partnership, association or club who, as the owner or lessee, has under his or its control any establishment, place or premises in or at which a coin-operated amusement machine is placed or kept for use or play, or an exhibition for the purpose of use or play by the general public.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-9-1), 11-18-2004)
Sec. 22-311. - Occupational tax.
(a)
Required. No person, firm or corporation shall engage in the business of an operator or proprietor of coin-operated amusement machines, as the terms are herein defined, without first having paid the required occupational tax as set forth in article II, division 4 of this chapter and without first having obtained the license required under this article.
(b)
Application. Application for tax certificate and license hereunder shall be fined in writing with the town clerk, on a form to be provided by the town, and shall specify:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant, and if a firm, corporation, partnership or association, the principal officers thereof and their addresses.
(2)
Proprietors shall list the address of the premises where the licensed machines are to be operated, together with the character of the business as carried on at such place.
(3)
Proprietors shall list the number of machines to be maintained on the premises.
(4)
The name and address of the owner of the machines, if other than the proprietor. This information shall not be released to the general public without the consent of the licensee.
(5)
Whether or not the person or persons having management of or supervision over the applicant's business have been convicted of a crime, misdemeanor or the violation of any municipal ordinance, the nature of such offense and the punishment assessed therefor.
(c)
Fee. The proper tax fee shall accompany such application. Such tax shall be non-assignable and non-transferable, and, in the case of a proprietor, shall apply only to the premises for which such tax has been paid.
(d)
Character qualifications. No person shall be permitted to operate an amusement center who has or employs a person who has, within five years of issuance or renewal, been convicted of, or pled guilty or nolo contendere to, any felony involving any federal or state offense or any federal or state misdemeanor involving drug-related offenses or offenses involving moral turpitude.
(e)
Revocation of license. In the event that it comes to the attention of the mayor and town council that an amusement center has created a nuisance to the surrounding community or the operator or proprietor has violated any provision of this section, the following procedure will be followed:
(1)
The town clerk shall issue a notice of objection that shall include the address of the location and the specific reasons why the amusement center in question is alleged to be a nuisance or alleged to be in violation of any provision of this section. The town clerk will then notify the proprietor that a notice of objection has been filed and will set a date for a hearing before the mayor and council. Extensions and necessary investigations will be granted and conducted at the discretion of the town clerk.
(2)
At the time of the hearing, any resident, the operator, the proprietor and the town clerk or other witnesses may be heard. Thereafter, the mayor and council shall issue a notice of decision that will call for the dismissal of the objection, removal of the machines or such other remedy as they deem appropriate under the circumstances that are consistent with the purpose of this section.
(3)
The decision of the mayor and council shall be in writing and shall state the reasons for their decision and shall be based upon the merits of the case.
(4)
The decision of the mayor and council shall be binding upon the proprietor and operator of the amusement center under consideration, subject to appeal to the proper court.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-9-2)—(7-9-5), 11-18-2004)
Sec. 22-312. - Operating regulations.
All businesses operating as an amusement center hereunder shall be subject to the following regulations:
(1)
Devises to be kept in plain view; gambling devices prohibited. All machines shall at all times be kept and placed in plain view of and open and accessible to any person who may frequent or be in any place of business where such machines are kept or used. Nothing in this section shall be construed to authorize, permit or license any gambling device of any nature whatsoever.
(2)
Inspection. The chief of police shall inspect or cause the inspection of any place or building in which any such machines are operated or set up for operating, and shall inspect, investigate and test such machines as needed.
(3)
Attendant required. It shall be unlawful for any proprietor to open his business to the public unless an attendant is present. Said attendant shall be of sufficient mental and physical capacity so as to be able to provide aid to patrons if needed or desired. Said attendant shall not be less than 18 years of age.
(4)
Loitering. As used in this section, the term "loitering" shall mean remaining idle in essentially one location and shall include the concepts of spending time idly, loafing or walking about aimlessly, and shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation licenses to operate an amusement center to permit loitering on or in the immediate vicinity of any machine or business regulated hereunder in such a manner as to:
a.
Created or cause to be created a danger of a breach of the peace;
b.
Create or cause to be created any disturbance or annoyance to the comfort and response of any person;
c.
Obstruct the free passage of pedestrians or vehicles;
d.
Obstruct, molest or interfere with any person lawfully in a public place.
(5)
Shirt and shoes required. All proprietors shall require shorts and shoes to be worn at all times by any person frequenting their premises.
(6)
Operating hours. Operating hours for the operation of coin-operated amusement machines shall be between the hours of 10:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight.
(7)
License. No separate license fee for such an establishment shall be required except as set forth in article 2, division 4 of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-9-6), 11-18-2004)
