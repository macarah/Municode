Chapter 10 - EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT AND EMERGENCY SERVICES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
(a)
Imposition. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charge as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4) upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public service answering point in the amount of $0.75.
(b)
Collection. Prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charges collected by sellers shall be remitted to the commissioner of the department of revenue at the times and in the manner provided by O.C.G.A. tit. 48, ch. 8, with respect to the sales and use tax imposed on prepaid wireless calling service.
(c)
Administrative provisions. The clerk of the Town of Tyrone is hereby directed to file with the state revenue commissioner a certified copy of the ordinance from which this section derives and amendments thereto in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), by December 31, 2011.
(d)
Depositing of funds; Use of funds. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(5), funds received by this town from charges imposed by this section shall be deposited in the Emergency Telephone System Fund maintained by this town pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(d)(2) and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction. All such funds shall be used exclusively for the purposes authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(f).
(Ord. No. 2011-12, § 1, 9-1-2011)

Sec. 10-19. - Defined.
As used in this article, the term "emergency management" means the preparation for and the carrying out of all emergency functions, other than the functions for which the state and federal governments are primarily responsible, to prevent, minimize, and repair injury and damage resulting from disasters caused by enemy nuclear attack, sabotage or other hostile action, or by acts of nature, fire, explosion, civil disorders or other causes. These functions include, without limitation:
(1)
Firefighting services;
(2)
Law enforcement services;
(3)
Medical and health services;
(4)
Rescue services;
(5)
Engineering services;
(6)
Warning services;
(7)
Communications;
(8)
Defense from radiological fallout;
(9)
Welfare services;
(10)
Evacuation, when and where necessary;
(11)
Emergency transportation;
(12)
Plant and utility protection;
(13)
Restoration of public utility services; and
(14)
Other functions related to protection and well-being of the population of the town;
together with all other activities necessary or incidental to preparation for and the carrying out of the above functions.
(Code 1984, § 3-3-1)
Sec. 10-20. - Office of the emergency management director.
There is hereby established the Town of Tyrone Emergency Management Office. The mayor and council shall appoint a director of emergency management for the town. When appointed, the director will be a deputy director on the county emergency management staff and is charged:
(1)
To represent the governing officials of the town on matters pertaining to emergency management;
(2)
With assisting town officials in organizing town departments for emergency operations;
(3)
With assisting in developing in conjunction with the county emergency management director, the plan for emergency functions for the county ordinance;
(4)
With rendering reports such as required by governing officials; and
(5)
With procuring, with authority of governing officials, a facility to be used as the town emergency operating center.
(Code 1984, § 3-3-2)
Sec. 10-21. - Emergency powers.
In the event of an actual enemy attack upon the United States or any other disaster which may affect the lives and property of the citizens of the town, the mayor or, in his absence, his legally appointed successor may declare that a state of emergency exists and thereafter shall have and may exercise for such period as such state of emergency exists or continues the following emergency powers:
(1)
To enforce all rules, laws, and regulations relating to emergency management and to assume direct operational control over all emergency management forces;
(2)
To seize, take for temporary use, or condemn any property for the protection of the public;
(3)
To sell, lend, give, or distribute all or any such property or supplies among the inhabitants of the town, to maintain a strict accounting of property or supplies distributed and for funds received for such property or supplies; and
(4)
To perform and exercise such other functions and duties, and take such emergency actions as may be necessary to promote and secure the safety, protection and well being of the inhabitants of the town.
(Code 1984, § 3-3-3)
Sec. 10-22. - Volunteers.
All persons, other than officers and employees of the town, performing emergency functions pursuant to this chapter, shall serve with or without compensation. While engaged in such emergency functions, volunteers shall have the same immunities as town officers and employees.
(Code 1984, § 3-3-4)
Sec. 10-23. - Penalties.
Persons violating any provision of this chapter, rule, order, or regulation made pursuant to this chapter, upon conviction thereof, will be punished as a misdemeanor.
(Code 1984, § 3-3-5)
