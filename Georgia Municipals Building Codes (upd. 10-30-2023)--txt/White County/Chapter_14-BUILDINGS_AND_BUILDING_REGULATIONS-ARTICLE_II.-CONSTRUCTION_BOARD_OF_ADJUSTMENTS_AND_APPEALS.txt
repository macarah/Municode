ARTICLE II. - CONSTRUCTION BOARD OF ADJUSTMENTS AND APPEALS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Editor's note— Res. No. 2005-4, adopted April 12, 2005, amended the Code by repealing former art. II, §§ 14-31—14-39, and adding a new art. II. Former art. II pertained to the building department inspection advisory committee, and derived from Res. No. 93-27, adopted July 13, 1993.

Sec. 14-31. - Established.
There is hereby established a construction board of adjustments and appeals in and for the county.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-32. - Composition.
The construction board of adjustments and appeals shall be composed of five members, each of whom shall be or have been members of the construction community. The members of this committee shall be paid such compensation as is established by the board of commissioners from time to time. The members may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-33. - Terms.
The members of the construction board of adjustments and appeals shall serve at the pleasure of the board of commissioners for terms of three years and until their successors are appointed and qualified; provided the original members appointed thereto shall serve staggered terms.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-34. - Officers.
The construction board of adjustments and appeals shall elect from its membership a chairman, vice chairman and a secretary.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-35. - Meetings.
The construction board of adjustments and appeals shall meet at the call of the chairman or as the board of commissioners may determine necessary.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-36. - Rules, regulations.
The construction board of adjustments and appeals shall adopt rules and regulations for the transaction of business, findings and determinations, which shall be recorded as a public record.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-37. - Staff.
The staff of the construction board of adjustments and appeals shall be allocated from the building inspection department staff by the county manager.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-38. - Report.
The construction board of adjustments and appeals shall make periodic reports as may be required by the board of commissioners.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-39. - Duties and responsibilities.
The construction board of adjustments and appeals shall have the authority, as further defined in section 14-40, to the hear appeals of the chief building official's interpretations of the Code. The board shall have no authority to waive requirements of the Code.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-40. - Appeal of a chief building official decision.
The owner of a building, structure or service system or his duly authorized agent, may appeal a decision of the chief building official to the construction board of adjustment and appeals whenever any one of the following conditions are claimed to exist:
(1)
The chief building official rejected or refused to approve the mode or manner of construction proposed or materials to be used in the installation or alteration of a building, structure or service system.
(2)
The provisions of the Code do not apply to the specific case.
(3)
An equal or more desirable form of installation can be employed in the specific case.
(4)
The intent and meaning of the Code or any of the regulations there under have been misconstrued or incorrectly interpreted.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-41. - Appeals procedure.
Appeal to the chief building official. The appellant shall file a written appeal, using the "White County Building Inspection Appeal Form" within three days from the issuance of the inspection report. The chief building official shall have three business days from the time the appeal is submitted to render a written decision.
The appellant shall have seven days after the chief building official renders a decision to file an appeal of the decision to the construction board of adjustments and appeals. The appeal shall be in writing, using the "White County Building Inspection Appeal Form" and forwarded to the county manager who will notify the secretary of the construction board of adjustments and appeals.
In the case of a building, structure, or service system, which, in the opinion of the chief building official, is unsafe, unsanitary or dangerous, the chief building official shall limit the time for such notice of appeals to a shorter period.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-42. - Granting of appeals.
The construction board of adjustments and appeals may modify the application of a Code provision to a particular case when, in its opinion, the enforcement thereof would do manifest injustice and would be contrary to the spirit and purpose of the Code and also finds all of the following:
(1)
Special conditions and circumstances exist which are peculiar to the building, structure or service system involved and which are not applicable to others.
(2)
The special conditions and circumstances do not result from the action or inaction of the applicant.
(3)
The granting of an appeal will not confer on the applicant any special privilege that is denied by the Code to other buildings, structures or service systems.
(4)
The granting of an appeal is the minimum modification that will make possible the reasonable use of the building, structure or service system.
(5)
The granting of an appeal will be in harmony with the general intent and purpose of the Code and will not be detrimental to the public health, safety and general welfare.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Sec. 14-43. - Conditions.
In granting the appeal, the board shall prescribe a reasonable time limit within which the action shall be commenced or completed or both. In addition, the board may prescribe appropriate conditions and safeguards in conformity with the Code. Violation of the conditions of these conditions shall be deemed a violation of the Code.
(Res. No. 2005-4, 4-12-05)
Secs. 14-44—14-70. - Reserved.
