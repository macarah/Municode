Sec. 42-1. - Fire department—Created; duties.
There is hereby created the county fire department. It shall be referred to as Carroll County Fire Rescue and EMA. The fire department shall employ personnel and maintain equipment at county expense for the suppression of fires, for fire prevention through inspection and public education, for emergency medical service and for investigation of fire causes.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-2. - Fire chief; inspectors, investigators; emergency employees.
(a)
Fire chief. There is hereby created the office of fire chief. Except as otherwise required by law, the fire chief shall be in charge of the fire department and shall be responsible for the operation of that department. The fire chief shall administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
Employees. The fire chief shall employ such employees as are required for the operation of the fire department. The procedure for the hiring and employment of such personnel shall be that set forth in the county civil service personnel policies and the fire department standard operating guidelines, as amended.
(c)
Inspectors and investigators. The fire chief may designate inspectors and investigators who shall check for violations of this chapter and any other county code requirements dealing with fire and life safety. The fire chief may assist local government entities and authorities within the county with inspections and investigations upon request.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-3. - Arson arrests.
The fire chief or other designated employees acting under his authority shall be sworn as peace officers with the power of arrest for crimes relating to arson.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-4. - Authority to cut utility wires.
While engaged in the suppression of a fire, or control of a dangerous situation or other emergency scene, the fire chief or the fire chief's designated agent is authorized to terminate any electrical, telephone or cable service considered necessary for reasons of safety.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-5. - Standard fire code adopted by reference.
(a)
There is hereby adopted the Georgia State Minimum Standard Fire Prevention Code, latest edition, as adopted by the state, in its entirety including all applicable appendices. At least one copy of the fire prevention code is on file in the office of the fire department.
(b)
Notwithstanding the standards and requirements adopted set forth in subsection (a), effective January 1, 2006, the minimum fire flow for all buildings other than one- or two-family dwellings will be specified in Table B105.1 of the International Fire Code. A reduction in the required fire flow rate to a minimum of 1,000 gallons per minute may be allowed where the building or structure is equipped throughout with an approved automatic sprinkler system in accordance with chapter 9 of the International Fire Code. Subsection 42-5(b) shall be in full force and effect until December 31, 2010, at which time it shall expire and be repealed in its entirety.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1; Res./Ord. of 12-20-05, § 3)
Sec. 42-6. - Penalties.
(a)
Whenever in this chapter any act is prohibited or is made or declared to be unlawful or an offense, or whenever in this chapter the doing of an act is required or the failure to do an act is declared to be unlawful, where no specific penalty is provided therefor, the violation of any such provision of this chapter shall be punished by a fine not exceeding $1,000.00 or 60 days imprisonment, or both, except as otherwise provided by general law. The fines imposed therefor may be collected by execution. Each day any violation continues shall be a separate offense.
(b)
The board of commissioners hereby designates the fire chief and the persons assigned and fulfilling the positions thereunder, including but not limited to, inspectors and investigators as those responsible for completing, signing, issuing, and serving citations for the purposes of enforcing this chapter.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-7. - Burning of certain materials prohibited.
No material or substance which emits noxious or poisonous gases such as nitrogen dioxide, hydrogen cyanide or chlorine or other poisonous gases that will not readily dissipate in the atmosphere may be burned within the county.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-8. - Open burning.
(a)
In general. Unless otherwise exempted, no person shall kindle an open fire in any public or private place outside any building, unless the person has obtained a permit from the state forestry commission in advance. Fires started in violation of this section shall be promptly extinguished by the persons responsible for same upon notice by the fire chief or the fire chief's duly designated agent. During the existence of windy or dry weather conditions, all exceptions are void and no open fires shall be permitted.
(b)
Open burning on premises heretofore described shall require a person in charge of such open burning to be physically present, with an effective means of controlling the fire immediately available and no fire shall be started or allowed to burn nearer than 30 feet from a property line or any structure.
(c)
All such open burning shall be attended until the last embers of the fire have been extinguished; provided further that all such burning will be in conformity with state air quality control rules and regulations.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-9. - Obstructing fire hydrants or fire lanes.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct with building materials or in any other manner whatsoever within 15 feet of any cistern or fire hydrant, which would obstruct approaches to the same by any member of the fire department. No person shall put any post or structure of any nature whatsoever within 18 inches of any fire hydrant without first obtaining written permission from the fire chief.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct with any vehicle or other object any designated fire lane.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-10. - Restrictions within the vicinity of a fire, dangerous conditions or emergency scene.
No person except duly authorized representatives of the fire department, or representatives of any law enforcement agencies shall be allowed within the immediate vicinity of any fire, dangerous condition or emergency scene, unless specifically authorized by the fire chief or the officer in charge at the time. Any person found to have refused the orders and directions of the fire chief or the officer in charge of a fire, dangerous condition or emergency scene shall be guilty of violating this chapter.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-11. - Following fire apparatus.
The driver of any vehicle other than one on official business shall not follow any fire apparatus or other emergency vehicle traveling in response to an alarm closer than 500 feet or park such vehicle within 500 feet of any parked emergency vehicle at the scene of an alarm.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-12. - Crossing fire hose.
No vehicle shall be driven over any unprotected hose of the fire department when laid on any street, roadway or private driveway, to be used at any fire or alarm of fire, without the consent of the fire department official in command.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-13. - Obstructing firehouse exitway or fire equipment.
It shall be unlawful for any person willfully or knowingly to park any vehicle or place any obstruction of any nature whatsoever in front of the firehouse exitway. It shall be unlawful for any person, willfully or knowingly to obstruct or restrict or place an obstruction of any nature whatsoever that obstructs or restricts the movement of fire equipment.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-14. - Malfunctioning fire alarms, fee therefor.
(a)
Recognizing that there are excessive costs incurred when the fire department responds to a false alarm and recognizing further that numerous false alarms are initiated by malfunctioning alarm systems, the fire department is authorized to assess a fee for false alarms which result in unnecessary responses by fire equipment as follows:
(1)
The third false alarm in any calendar month and subsequent false alarms occurring on the same day as the third false alarm—$50.00 each for such false alarm.
(2)
Subsequent false alarms up to and including five in the calendar month—$75.00 each for such false alarm.
(3)
Each subsequent alarm in excess of five in the calendar month—$100.00.
(b)
No fee shall be assessed for false alarms caused by storms, power losses, or malfunctioning of telephone lines provided that the occupant/owner shall act responsibly and promptly to correct the malfunction or shall notify the fire department of the false nature of the alarm in time to prevent the equipment from responding. The burden of establishing the fact of such malfunction or act of God rests upon the occupant/owner of the subject property.
(c)
The fees set forth herein shall be assessed against the occupant of the facility from which the false alarm originates. The penalty for failure to remit the fine imposed by the fire department within 30 days of its imposition shall be as set forth in section 42-7 of this chapter.
(Res./Ord. of 10-21-03(1), § 1)
