Sec. 70-1. - Getting on or off moving trains.
It shall be unlawful for any person not connected with the running of any train and not a passenger on a train, or not engaged in the act of taking passage, to get on or off any moving train or locomotive in the city.
(Code 1983, § 12-20(a))
Sec. 70-2. - Crossing tracks occupied by trains.
It shall be unlawful for any pedestrian or person operating or in control of any vehicle to cross or attempt to cross the railroad tracks at any of the streets and avenues while a train, engine or car is moving over the streets or while a warning of its approach is being given either by a signaling device or by a flagman.
(Code 1983, § 12-20(b))
Sec. 70-3. - Speed limit.
It shall be unlawful for the engineer, conductor or any person in charge of any train, car or locomotive to run the train, car or locomotive at a speed greater than 25 miles per hour in the city.
(Code 1983, § 12-20(c))
Sec. 70-4. - Obstructing crossings.
It shall be unlawful for the engineer, conductor or any person in charge of any locomotive or train to permit the locomotive or train to stand upon or block any street crossing or sidewalk in the city for more than five minutes at any one time.
(Code 1983, § 12-20(d))
Sec. 70-5. - Signaling device or flagman generally.
It shall be unlawful for any person to move any train, engine or cars over any of the streets or avenues described in this chapter unless the signaling devices, when installed, are operating so as to give warning of the approach or unless a flagman is provided to give warning of such movement.
(Code 1983, § 12-20(e))
Sec. 70-6. - Erecting signal devices.
The railroad operated over crossings may place and erect street crossing signal devices wholly or partially upon the sidewalks adjacent to the streets and upon both sides of such crossing, which erection is declared to be a lawful and proper safety device upon such sidewalks.
(Code 1983, § 12-20(f))
Sec. 70-7. - Installation of devices containing bells.
No signaling device containing bells shall be installed by any of the railroads passing through the city at any crossing of any street in the city without written application to the mayor and board of aldermen and prior permission granted by proper action of the mayor and board of aldermen.
(Code 1983, § 12-20(g))
