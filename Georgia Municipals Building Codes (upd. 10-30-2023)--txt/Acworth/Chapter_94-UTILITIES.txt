Sec. 94-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Utility means electric, sanitation, cable television or internet service.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
Sec. 94-2. - Obtaining utility service unlawfully.
It shall be unlawful for any person to use any device, artificial or otherwise, for the purpose of obtaining any utility service from the city when such service is not officially authorized by the city.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
Sec. 94-3. - Deposits, rates and charges.
The deposits, rates and charges for utilities furnished by the city shall be as fixed by the mayor and board of aldermen.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
Sec. 94-4. - Collection of charges for utility services.
(a)
The city shall have the right to collect from a customer, and the customer shall be liable for, all fees imposed by the city and all charges for utilities supplied by the city to the customer.
(b)
If the customer fails to pay the amount due for utilities provided to him by the city, the city is authorized to disconnect and discontinue providing utility services to the premises, and is authorized to bring a civil action against the customer and/or the occupant to recover the amount due.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
Sec. 94-5. - Refunds of utility deposits.
(a)
The city shall refund a utility deposit prior to termination of service under the following conditions:
(1)
Such refund may only be made with respect to an owner-occupied residence upon proof of ownership;
(2)
During the 12 months preceding the request for a refund, the customer must not have incurred any late charges on his account, had his service disconnected or issued any check in payment of his account that was not paid for any reason; and
(3)
The customer must request the refund in writing.
(b)
If a customer is entitled to a refund in accordance with this section, such refund will be credited to the customer's account with the city.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
Sec. 94-6. - Separate meters.
If power is available to a lot, there shall be a separate power meter to each unit on each lot.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
Sec. 94-7. - Water and wastewater systems code adopted.
The city adopts the county water and wastewater systems code, Utilities, chapter 122, article II of the Cobb County Code of Ordinances. A copy of such code shall be on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
Sec. 94-8. - Replacement of electric meter seals; fee.
(a)
For the replacement of any seal on the electric power meter of any customer served by the city's electrical power system, there is established a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(b)
If the city determines, in its discretion, that the amount of power used by any such customer for the month during which any such seal is replaced is less than the expected usage for such customer during such month, based upon the historical usage for the location at which such seal is replaced, the amount of the difference between the expected usage for such month and the actual usage for such month for that location shall also be paid to the city.
(c)
If a seal on an electric power meter of a customer served by the city is found by a city employee to be broken, such employee shall replace such seal or cause the seal to be replaced.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, 1-18-2001)
