Sec. 54-31. - Composition; rank.
(a)
The city police department shall consist of the chief of police and department personnel as approved by the city council during the budget process. Reporting relationships and department organization will be designated by the chief of police as required for efficient and effective operation of the department. The chief of police may effect personal changes downward and laterally with approval by the city manager during the fiscal year provided that those changes do not exceed the allocated number of positions and funding approved by the city council durinq the budget approval process. Any requests for additional allocated positions or funding must be approved by the city council prior to implementation
(b)
A detailed breakdown of the organization of these divisions is found in the police operations manual. Changes in the organizational structure will be brought back to the mayor and council at the next duly called meeting.
(c)
Positions of police captain or above will be appointed by the chief of police.
(d)
In the event of resignation, removal, death or disability of the chief of police, the officer next in rank shall perform the duties of the chief until removal of such disability or until a successor has been appointed.
(Code 1989, § 4-1-1; Ord. No. 89-12-18, 12-18-1989; Ord. No. 01-11-13, 11-13-2001; Ord. No. 04-03-23, 3-23-2004; Ord. No. 009-2007, 4-6-2007)
Charter reference— Specific powers, § 1.13.
Sec. 54-32. - Appointment and powers of police chief.
(a)
The police chief shall be appointed by the city manager and shall be the department director of the police department, reporting directly to the city manager. The chief of police shall be authorized to give the oath of office to each police officer.
(b)
The chief of police is responsible for the operation and administration of all departmental activities.
(c)
The chief of police shall formulate departmental policies, rules, regulations, and orders that govern the delivery of police services and see that these rules and regulations and all special and general orders are understood and enforced. Such rules and regulations shall be consistent with all city ordinances and state and federal laws.
(Code 1989, § 4-1-2; Ord. No. 01-11-13, 11-13-2001; Ord. No. 05-09-13, 9-13-2005; Ord. No. 009-2007, 4-6-2007)
Charter reference— Specific powers, § 1.13.
Sec. 54-33. - Qualifications.
(a)
The chief of police shall have the authority to determine requisite qualifications for positions within the police department to include sworn and civilian personnel.
(b)
Any person hired by the city for the position of police officer must meet the qualifications for certification by the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Council and complete state mandated training prior to full-time appointment.
(c)
Persons hired prior to completing the state mandated training shall have no arrest powers until this training has been completed and shall be compensated under the special regulations provision.
(d)
Prior to any employment by the police department, as either a sworn or civilian employee, all applicants will undergo testing and background investigations as directed by the chief of police.
(Code 1989, § 4-1-3; Ord. No. 009-2007, 4-6-2007)
State Law reference— Georgia Peace Officers Standards and Training Act, O.C.G.A. § 35-8-1 et seq.
Sec. 54-34. - Disposition of stolen, abandoned or impounded property.
(a)
All property which comes into the custody of the police department, known to be stolen, suspected to be stolen, has been impounded, or where the ownership is unknown, shall be held for a period of 90 days unless claimed by the owner.
(b)
The owner of the property, upon proving ownership and paying all expenses and costs, including reasonable charges for storage, shall have the right to have such property returned to him.
(c)
If, subsequent to the 90-day period the property is unclaimed, the chief of police shall advertise the sale of the property as required by the city's legal counsel and city clerk. Such advertising shall take place at least 14 days before the date of sale and shall give a description of the property to be sold and specify the time and place of sale.
(d)
If the property remains unclaimed up to and including the time of sale, it shall be sold as advertised and the proceeds places in a city treasury account as proscribed by law pertaining to disposal of tangible property.
(e)
Contraband property may be disposed of by order of the municipal judge or court having jurisdiction.
(Code 1989, § 4-1-4; Ord. No. 009-2007, 4-6-2007)
