Sec. 30-1. - Applicability of state rules and regulations.
The rules and regulations promulgated by the state election board which pertain to municipal elections, together with the provisions of the Georgia Municipal Code contained in O.C.G.A. tit. 21, ch. 2 (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.), shall govern the conduct of municipal, general and special elections in the city. A copy of the rules and regulations shall be on file in the city clerk's office.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-1)
Sec. 30-2. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this chapter shall be budgeted and appropriated annually, and from time to time.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-2)
Sec. 30-3. - Election officials.
The city council shall by resolution annually appoint a municipal election superintendent, election managers, registrars, absentee ballot clerk and any other officials as are necessary, all of whom shall exercise those qualifications, powers and duties set forth in the Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. tit. 21, ch. 2 (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.).
(Code 1989, § 2-2-3)
State Law reference— Power and duty of superintendent, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70; appointment of chief manager and assistant, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-90; qualification of registrars, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-214; appointment of absentee ballot clerk, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-380.1.
Sec. 30-4. - Voter registration deadline.
The deadline for registration of voters in city elections shall be pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-224.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-11)
Sec. 30-5. - Notice of candidacy.
Notice of candidacy shall be completed pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-21)
Charter reference— Qualifying, nomination of candidates, § 5.12.
State Law reference— Filing notice of candidacy, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
Sec. 30-6. - Qualification fees.
Qualification fees will be established pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-131.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-22)
State Law reference— Fixing and publishing of qualification fees, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-131.
Sec. 30-7. - Campaign financing disclosure.
All candidates for municipal public office shall keep all such records and file all such reports as are required by the Georgia Campaign and Financial Disclosure Act.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-23)
State Law reference— Contributions made to candidates, records and reports required, O.C.G.A. § 21-5-30.
Sec. 30-8. - Polling place.
The polling place for city elections shall be as fixed from time to time by resolution of the city council.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-31)
Charter reference— Division of city into six wards, § 5.20.
State Law reference— Polling places, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-260.
Sec. 30-9. - Write-in votes.
Write-in votes shall be used in all municipal elections, except that no write-in votes shall be counted in a primary, runoff primary or runoff election.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-32)
State Law reference— Limitation of write-in vote, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-133; procedures for marking ballots, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-435; write-in votes permitted, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-358; write-in vote secrecy, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-373.
Sec. 30-10. - Absentee ballots.
Absentee ballots shall be used in all municipal elections, and such use shall be governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. tit. 21, ch. 2, art. 10 (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-380 et seq.).
(Code 1989, § 2-2-33)
Sec. 30-11. - Vote required for election.
Candidates for nomination for any public municipal office in any general or special election shall be nominated or elected by a majority of the votes cast to fill such nomination for public office.
(Code 1989, § 2-2-34)
Charter reference— Majority determines election, § 5.10.
