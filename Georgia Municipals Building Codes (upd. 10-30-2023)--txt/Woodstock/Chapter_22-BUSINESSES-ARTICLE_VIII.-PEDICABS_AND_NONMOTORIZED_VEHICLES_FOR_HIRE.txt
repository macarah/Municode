ARTICLE VIII. - PEDICABS AND NONMOTORIZED VEHICLES FOR HIRE

Sec. 22-230. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business registration means the process by which a business or a practitioner of a profession or occupation gives evidence of compliance with the requirements of chapter 86, taxation.
City means the City of Woodstock.
Driver means any person who drives or operates a pedicab on the streets of the city for a regulated pedicab company.
Driver's permit means the written authority granted by the city for an individual to drive or operate a pedicab within the city.
Horse-drawn carriage means any hack or carriage which is operated by being drawn by a horse, mule or other beast of burden for the transportation for hire of passengers.
Occupation tax certificate means the right and privilege granted by the city for the operation of a business incorporating the use of one or more pedicabs within the corporate limits of the city.
Pedicab means any nonmotorized or motor-assisted vehicle with three or more wheels operated by one person for the purpose of, or capable of, transporting passengers in seats or a platform made a part of the pedicab. This definition shall not include a bicycle built for two where the operators are seated one behind the other, nor shall it include a bicycle with trainer or beginner wheels affixed thereto, nor shall it include a wheelchair or other vehicle for the purpose of transporting a handicapped person, nor shall it include a tricycle built for a child or an adult with a seat for only one operator and no passenger.
Pedicab business or company means a business involving the use of one or more pedicabs on the streets of the city and the holder of an occupation tax certificate to operate a pedicab business under the provisions of this article whether a person, firm, partnership or corporation.
Street shall mean and include any city-owned street, alley, lane, avenue, court or public place in the city.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-231. - Business registration and license required.
(a)
Each pedicab business which operates within the city shall be required to obtain the necessary business registration in the manner specified in chapter 86, article III, pertaining to occupational license taxes, as well as to obtain a license in the manner specified in this article.
(b)
The application for the pedicab license described in subparagraph (a) shall be made on forms provided by the city and shall provide proof that the pedicab company owns the pedicabs which it intends to operate pursuant to the license, a copy of the employment agreement between pedicab company and its drivers, and any additional information as may be necessary to define completely the business operation of the pedicab company. Renewal of the pedicab license shall be required on January 1 of each year.
(c)
If the application for the pedicab license required by this article is submitted in proper form and is approved by the city manager or his designee, then the license will be issued upon the payment of any fees due, which are to be set by the council from time to time and are stated in the fee schedule.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-232. - Insurance.
(a)
Indemnity for benefit of city. Any pedicab business operating under this article shall hold the city, its officers, agents, servants and employees harmless against any and all liability, loss, damages or expense which may accrue to the city by reason of negligence, default or misconduct of the pedicab company in connection with the rights granted to each pedicab company hereunder. Nothing in this article shall be considered to make the city, its officers, agents, servants or employees liable for damages because of any negligent act or omission or commission by any pedicab company, its servants, agents, drivers or other employees during the service and operation by the pedicab company of a pedicab business or either in respect to injury to persons or with respect to damage to property which may be sustained. The physical health and fitness of a driver to operate a pedicab is solely the responsibility of the pedicab business.
(b)
Insurance for benefit of passengers. Any pedicab company desiring to obtain an occupation tax certificate to do business in the city shall give and maintain a policy of indemnity from an insurance company authorized to do business in the state of Georgia for each pedicab. The minimum coverage shall be $100,000.00 for bodily injury to any one person, $300,000.00 for injury to more than one person when sustained in the same accident, and $25,000.00 for property damage resulting from one accident. The indemnity insurance shall inure to the benefit of any person who shall be injured or who shall sustain damage to property caused by the negligence of a pedicab company, its servants or agents.
(c)
Blanket policy. Any pedicab company or person operating a pedicab in the city shall give a separate policy of indemnity insurance for each separate pedicab for hire, except where such pedicab company or person actually owns or holds legal title to more than one pedicab, in which event such pedicab company or person may give one policy of indemnity insurance covering all the pedicabs actually owned. This latter provision, however, shall not apply to any group of persons separately owning pedicabs who may be jointly operating or doing business under a regulated pedicab name.
(d)
Comprehensive general liability insurance. Each pedicab company shall maintain from a licensed insurance company comprehensive general liability insurance in the amount of $1,000,000.00 for its operation of pedicab(s) within the city.
(e)
Notice when voided. Before any policy of insurance required by this article is voided for any cause, nonpayment of premium or otherwise, notice thereof shall be given, in writing, to the city's business license manager at least ten days before the same shall take effect.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-233. - Pedicab driver's permit.
(a)
Permit required. No person shall operate a pedicab for hire upon the streets of the city, and no person who owns or operates a pedicab company shall permit a pedicab to be driven, and no pedicab operating under a pedicab company regulated by the city shall be driven at any time for hire unless the driver of the pedicab shall first have obtained and shall have then in force a pedicab driver's permit issued under the provisions of this article.
(b)
Application. Any person desiring a permit required by this article shall submit an application in writing to the city, on a form provided by the city that shall include but not to be limited to the age of the applicant, his address, and whether he has been convicted of a violation of any of the laws of the state or of this Code or other ordinances of the city, and if so, when and of what offense, and the sentence of the court.
(c)
Driver's fitness; training. Each application for a driver's permit shall be accompanied by an affidavit on a form provided by the city from the applicant indicating that the applicant is physically capable of operating the pedicab. In the case of renewal of a driver's permit, the affidavit shall be updated every two years. The applicant shall also attest by affidavit, on a form provided by the city, that the applicant has received adequate training to operate the pedicab, and the applicant shall provide documentation of the training received.
(d)
Qualifications of applicant. An applicant for a driver's permit under this article must not be less than 16 years of age, with no physical infirmities which might make the applicant an unsafe pedicab driver. No permit shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of operating a motor vehicle while under the influence of intoxicating beverages or drugs within one year prior to the date of the application for such permit, or who has been convicted of the offense three or more times within five years prior to the date of the application for the permit.
(e)
Driver's permit fee. Before any permit is granted under this article, a driver's permit fee shall be paid by the applicant as adopted by city council with resolution from time to time.
(f)
Current state driver's license required. Any person applying for a pedicab driver's permit under this article must show that he has a current motor vehicle operator's license issued or approved by the State of Georgia and that the license is not under suspension or revocation.
(g)
Examination of applicant; issuance or denial of permit. It shall be the duty of the chief of police, or his designee, to examine the applicant, his references, police and traffic record, and other vouchers and thereafter to either grant or refuse the permit. In the event of a refusal to grant a permit, the applicant shall have the right to enter an appeal as provided in subsection (o) of this section.
(h)
Permit to be displayed in pedicab. The permit issued under the provisions of this article shall be placed on display in the pedicab to be operated by the holder of the permit, where the permit will be in clear view of passengers at all times when the pedicab is for hire.
(i)
Alteration of permits prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person willfully to alter, deface, obliterate or destroy a pedicab driver's permit or cause or allow the same.
(j)
Permit not transferable. Any pedicab driver's permit issued under this article is not transferable and is to be used solely by the person to whom it is issued.
(k)
Duration of permit; renewals. Any pedicab permit shall be in effect for the calendar year. Permits may be renewed, upon application and payment of the required fee, for each calendar year thereafter subject to the requirements of this section, unless the permit for the preceding period has been revoked or is under suspension.
(l)
Suspension of the driver's permit. The chief of police or the city manager or his designee shall have the authority to suspend a driver's permit for the following reasons:
1.
Making any false statement in the application for the permit, or
2.
Operating a pedicab in violation of any provision of this article or state law.
The driver shall have the right to drive the pedicab until a final hearing on the suspension is held unless otherwise prohibited by state law. The suspension shall be lifted upon dismissal or dropping of the charges described, upon satisfactory correction of any false statement in the application, upon correction of the violation of any provision of this article, upon ruling in favor of the driver in any hearing before the city council, or at the expiration of 60 days from the date of suspension. From the decision of the chief of police or the city manager or his designee to suspend a permit, the holder of such permit shall have the right to appeal to the city council, as approved in subsection (o) of this section.
(m)
Revocation of driver's permit. In the event that any driver holding a permit under this article at any time ceases to meet the qualifications described in this section or fails to correct satisfactorily any false statement made in the application for the permit, or fails to operate his pedicab in accordance with the provisions of the article, the city manager shall be empowered to revoke permanently the permit.
(n)
Driving after suspension or revocation. It is unlawful for any person to operate a pedicab for hire for the transport of passengers during any period in which his permit to do so is suspended or revoked in accordance with the provisions of this article or if his Georgia driver's license has been suspended or revoked.
(o)
Appeals. An appeal to the city council from a ruling of the city manager, his designee, or the chief of police shall be made within ten days of notice to the applicant regarding the ruling. The appellant shall be informed within ten days of the ruling in writing of the reasons for the decision. The appellant shall have the right to present evidence, examine the evidence of the appellee, and to cross-examine any witnesses. Appellant shall have the right to be represented by counsel.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-234. - Identification and marking generally.
Every pedicab shall have a sign plainly painted on each side of the vehicle, in letters not less than two inches high, containing the full name of the pedicab company operating the vehicle.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-235. - Numbers generally.
There shall be painted on each side and on the rear of each pedicab a number at least two inches high, the number to be separate and distinct from that on any other public vehicle or taxicab in the city. The number shall be agreed upon by the pedicab company and the city manager or his designee and shall not be altered or changed without the consent of the city manager or his designee.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-236. - Registration of number and names of owner and operator.
The number assigned to a pedicab in accordance with this article together with the names of the owner and driver of the pedicab shall be registered with the city.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-237. - Safe mechanical condition of pedicab required.
Every pedicab operated on the streets of the city shall be maintained in a safe mechanical condition, with all safety equipment remaining intact and operative at all times when the pedicab is in service.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-238. - Cleanliness of pedicab required.
Each vehicle operating under this article shall be kept painted and in a clean and sanitary condition, free of litter and debris and at all times suitable for public transportation of passengers.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-239. - Vehicle inspections required.
Each pedicab shall be inspected by a mechanic at a reputable bike shop who is experienced and qualified in the field of pedicab or bicycle inspection for compliance of the provisions of this article and shall pass said inspection before the vehicle may be used as a pedicab in the city. The mechanic shall sign off on a checklist provided by the city, as adopted by the city council with resolution from time to time. A new inspection must take place with each renewal of a pedicab license to ensure continued compliance with the provisions of this article. Each pedicab involved in an accident shall be inspected again before it may be returned to service transporting passengers for hire.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-240. - Authority for removal of pedicabs from the streets.
The chief of police or his designee shall have the authority to remove from operation on the streets of the city any pedicab which is in violation of this article, and to prohibit operation of the pedicab until all deficiencies have been corrected. An order of the chief of police or his designee to remove a pedicab from the streets may be appealed to the city council as set forth in subsection (o) of section 22-233.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-241. - Rates of fare-rate card required.
No owner or driver of a pedicab shall charge a greater sum for the use of the pedicab than the published and advertised rates which shall be displayed in each pedicab. Rates shall be displayed in such place as to be conspicuous and to be in clear view of all passengers. If different than the regular advertised rates, rates for special events and occasions such as weddings shall also be displayed and published in conjunction with the regular advertised rates.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-242. - Restriction on number of passengers.
No driver shall permit more persons to be carried in a pedicab as passengers than the rated seating capacity of the pedicab. For purposes of this section, a child under the age of five shall not be counted as a passenger.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-243. - Pedicab driver movement prohibited under certain conditions.
No driver shall collect fares, make change, or take on or discharge passengers while his pedicab is in motion.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-244. - Property left in pedicab by passenger.
Any pedicab driver discovering in any pedicab under his control personal property left in the pedicab shall report the loss to the pedicab company for whom he or she works and deliver all the property to the office of the pedicab company within 12 hours after the discovery of the property. The driver's report shall include brief particulars to assist the pedicab company in identifying the owner of the property. The pedicab company shall retain the property on behalf of the owner for at least 60 days.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-245. - Safety equipment required.
Each pedicab shall be equipped with reflectors and electrically powered lights, including turn signals. Electrically powered lights shall be in use when operating during the hours of darkness and when raining. The lights and reflectors shall be mounted so that they are visible from a distance of 500 feet in any direction.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-246. - Pedicab operations.
(a)
Fixed place of business required. Each pedicab business, as a condition for holding a license under the provisions of this chapter, shall establish and maintain a fixed headquarters on private property for the operation of the company's business. The headquarters shall conform to ordinances of the city and shall provide adequate off-street parking space for all pedicabs not in service on the streets. The company headquarters shall not be moved except by the approved transfer of the company's license to another location.
(b)
Area of operation. Pedicabs shall only operate on streets with a posted speed limit of 25 mph or less. City council may elect to adopt a map by resolution from time to time which shows the routes accessible to pedicabs. In addition, pedicabs are permitted anywhere within the city with the written consent of the city manager. Pedicabs are not permitted on any sidewalk or trail.
(c)
Loading and unloading. No pedicab shall stop or stand in the travel lanes of any street. For the purpose of loading or unloading passengers, a pedicab may remain standing upon a street if the pedicab is in any legal parking stall, designated loading zone, or any other location that does not impede pedestrian or vehicular traffic. Otherwise, a pedicab driver must comply with on-street parking regulations regarding loading zones and fire zones. The city manager may set forth specific additional locations where pedicabs are allowed to park or stand. While a pedicab is in operation for the solicitation or transportation of passengers, no pedicab driver shall leave a pedicab unattended for a period of more than 15 minutes at a time unless the pedicab is parked in one of those specific locations identified by the city manager as locations where a pedicab may park or stand.
(d)
Direct route. The most direct route practicable shall be used by the driver of a pedicab to carry passengers safely and expeditiously to their requested destination, unless otherwise requested by the passenger.
(e)
Operational log. Pedicab companies shall maintain an operational log for each pedicab owned and operated under that company's pedicab license. The operational log shall list the dates and times of the operation of each pedicab and the name of any pedicab driver operating the pedicab during those dates and times.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-247. - Traffic violations.
(a)
Every person operating a pedicab upon a roadway shall ride as near to the right side of the roadway as practicable, except when turning left, when avoiding hazards to safe cycling, when the lane is too narrow to share safely with a motor vehicle, when traveling at the same speed as traffic, or while exercising due care when passing a standing vehicle or one proceeding in the same direction; provided, however, that every driver shall exercise reasonable care and shall give due consideration to the other applicable rules of the road.
(b)
Pedicabs shall be prohibited from stopping in traffic or delaying any on-street traffic for the purpose of loading or unloading passengers or for any other purposes.
(c)
Every driver shall be subject to the provisions of this article and shall operate his or her pedicab in accordance with the traffic laws of the State of Georgia and the city.
(d)
Pedicabs shall be operated with caution and due care for safety at all times, and drivers shall take extra precautions in areas of congestion or heavy traffic.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-248. - Compliance required.
Failure to comply with this article or any of the laws, ordinances, and regulations of the city can result in suspension or revocation of a pedicab driver's permit and shall be punishable as an offense as provided in this Code.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-249. - Horse-drawn carriage.
The operation of a horse-drawn carriage is expressly prohibited within the corporate limits of the city except for those participating a parade, procession, or for ceremonial purposes, as approved by the city.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-250. - Exemptions.
The city and all affiliates thereof, joined by county, state, and federal governments, are expressly exempt from this article.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
Sec. 22-251. - Violation—Penalty.
Any person violating the provisions of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 per violation or by imprisonment for a period not to exceed 90 days, or by both such fine and imprisonment. In addition to such fine or imprisonment, violation of this article shall also be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of the pedicab license or driver's permit issued hereunder.
(Ord. No. 4962, 2-12-2018)
