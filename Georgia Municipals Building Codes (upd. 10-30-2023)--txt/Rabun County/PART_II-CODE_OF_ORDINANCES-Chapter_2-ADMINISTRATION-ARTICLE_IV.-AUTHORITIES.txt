ARTICLE IV. - AUTHORITIES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-161. - Legislatively created boards and authorities.
Certain boards and authorities for the county, individually or in association with other local government units, have been created by legislative acts, including, but not limited to, the following:
(1)
Rabun County Airport Authority, created by 1975 Ga. Laws (Act No. 186), p. 3003, as subsequently amended;
(2)
Rabun County Economic Development Authority, created by 1992 Ga. Laws (Act No. 657), p. 4912, as subsequently amended;
(3)
Rabun County Building Authority, created by 1994 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1027), p. 4564, as subsequently amended;
(4)
Clayton-Rabun County Water and Sewer Authority, created by 1994 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1170), p. 6403, p. 3563, as subsequently amended;
(5)
Rabun County Convention and Visitors Bureau Authority, created by 2005 Ga. Laws (Act No. 269), p. 3878, as subsequently amended);
(6)
Rabun County Board of Education, created by 1978 Ga. Laws (Act No. 883), p. 3430, as subsequently amended;
(7)
Rabun County Board of Elections, created by 2006 Ga. Laws (Act No. 495), p. 3712.
The acts creating the authorities specified herein are included in Part I of this Code.
Secs. 2-162—2-190. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[5]

State Law reference— Downtown development authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.; city and county development authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.; municipal business improvement districts, O.C.G.A. § 36-43-1 et seq.; redevelopment authority of municipalities, O.C.G.A. § 36-44-1; county and municipal urban development, O.C.G.A. § 36-61-1 et seq.; training for members of local development authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-62A-21; exception to training requirements for directors of downtown development authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-62A-22.

Sec. 2-191. - Creation; purpose and intent.
Pursuant to the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq., the county has created a development authority for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the county thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry and, in the furtherance of that purpose, to finance projects for air and water pollution control facilities by industries.
(Res. of 4-27-1971)
Sec. 2-192. - Composition; terms.
The development authority shall be composed of not less than seven nor more than nine members appointed from time to time by the board of commissioners. Two members of the authority shall be appointed for two-year terms, two members of the authority shall be appointed for four-year terms, and three members of the authority shall be appointed for six-year terms. Each member shall serve until his successor is qualified.
(Res. of 4-27-1971; Res. of 2-9-1976; Res. of 12-17-2013 )
Sec. 2-193. - Organization; rules of procedure.
The development authority shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the this Code and state law, as they now exist and as are amended or modified from time to time.
(Res. of 4-27-1971; Res. of 2-9-1976)

DIVISION 3. - HOSPITAL AUTHORITY[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— County and municipal hospital authorities, O.C.G.A. § 31-7-70 et seq.

Sec. 2-225. - Creation; purpose and intent.
Pursuant to the Hospital Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 31-7-70 et seq., the county has created a hospital authority for the purpose of providing medical services and facilities needed by county citizens and developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in said county thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry and, in the furtherance of that purpose, to finance such hospital facilities.
Sec. 2-226. - Composition; terms.
The hospital authority shall be composed of five members, each of whom shall be taxpayers of the county and none of whom shall be an officer or employee of the county, appointed by the board of commissioners. Two members of the authority shall be appointed for two-year terms, two members of the authority shall be appointed for four-year terms, and three members of the authority shall be appointed for six-year terms. Each member shall serve until his successor is qualified.
Sec. 2-227. - Organization; rules of procedure.
The hospital authority shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the this Code and state law, as they now exist and as are amended or modified from time to time.
Sec. 2-228. - Compensation.
Each member of the hospital authority shall receive a per diem reimbursement for attendance at each regular and special board meeting in the amount established from time to time by the board of commissioners.

Sec. 2-250. - Creation; purpose and intent.
Pursuant to the Residential Care Facilities for the Elderly Act, O.C.G.A. § 31-7-110 et seq., the county has created a resident care facilities for the elderly authority for the purpose of providing special services and facilities needed by elderly persons and developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in said county thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry and, in the furtherance of that purpose, to finance residential care facilities for the elderly.
(Res. of 11-22-1983)
Sec. 2-251. - Composition; terms.
The residential care facilities for the elderly authority shall be composed of seven members, each of whom shall be taxpayers of the county and none of whom shall be an officer or employee of the county, appointed by the board of commissioners. Two members of the authority shall be appointed for two-year terms, two members of the authority shall be appointed for four-year terms, and three members of the authority shall be appointed for six-year terms. Each member shall serve until his successor is qualified.
(Res. of 11-22-1983)

The residential care facilities for the elderly authority shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the this Code and state law, as they now exist and as are amended or modified from time to time.
(Res. of 11-22-1983)
