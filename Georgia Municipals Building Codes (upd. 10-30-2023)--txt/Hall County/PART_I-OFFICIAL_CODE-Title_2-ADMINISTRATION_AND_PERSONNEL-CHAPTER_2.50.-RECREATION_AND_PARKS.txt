ARTICLE I. - ADMINISTRATION AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

2.50.010. - Definitions and interpretation of provisions.
A.
Interpretation of provisions.
1.
Words used in the singular include the plural and words used in the plural include the singular.
2.
Words used in the present tense include the future tense.
3.
The word "building" includes the word "structure."
B.
Definitions. For the purpose of this chapter certain words or terms used in this chapter shall be defined as follows:
1.
Alcoholic beverages means intoxicating beverages, malt beverages, wine or any other beverage containing any alcohol whatsoever.
2.
Building means any structure attached to the ground which has a roof and which is designed for the shelter, housing or enclosure of persons, animals, or property of any kind.
3.
Litter means garbage, refuse, paper, rubbish, debris, trash and all other waste material, whether natural or artificial.
4.
Person. The word "person" includes the words "individuals," "firms," "partnerships," "corporations," "associations," and "governmental bodies" and all other legal entities.
5.
Recreation facilities means all recreation areas and parks, including land, buildings, lakes, swimming pools, and all other property and buildings owned, leased or managed by the county or the county recreation authority or their designated agents or department.
6.
Vehicle means any motor driven equipment, automobile, truck, motorcycle, bicycle or sled.
7.
Weapon means a pistol, firearm, rifle, hatchet, ax, shotgun, air gun, slingshot or other similar device.
8.
Consumer fireworks means any small fireworks devices containing restricted amounts of pyrotechnic composition, designed primarily to produce visible or audible effects by combustion, that comply with the construction, chemical composition, and labeling regulations of the United States Consumer Product Safety Commission as provided for in Parts 16 CFR 1500 and 1507, the United States Department of Transportation as provided for in 49 CFR 172, and the American Pyrotechnics Association as provided for in the 2001 American Pyrotechnics Association Standard 87-1, and additionally shall mean Roman candles.
9.
Fireworks means any combustible or explosive composition or any substance or combination of substances or article prepared for the purpose of producing a visible or audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration, or detonation, including blank cartridges, firecrackers, torpedoes, skyrockets, bombs, sparklers and other combustibles and explosives of like construction, as well as articles containing any explosive or flammable compound and tables and other devices containing an explosive substance.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 1; Code 1991, § 2.20.010; Res. of 9-10-15(1), §§ 1, 2)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1.20.010 et seq.
2.50.020. - Recreation and parks department created; purpose.
The Hall County Recreation and Parks Department is created to function as a line department directly responsible to the board of commissioners of the county. The primary purpose of the county recreation and parks department is to provide, maintain and conduct parks, playgrounds, recreation centers, and to carry out all other recreational activities and services within the geographical boundaries of the county.
(Res. of 12-8-80, § 2; Code 1991, § 2.20.020)
2.50.030. - Authority of board of commissioners; purchasing and contracts.
The board of commissioners of the county reserves sole and exclusive authority to buy, sell, lease and convey real estate, and to enter into all contracts for all recreational and park purposes. All goods and services shall be purchased in accordance with the county's purchasing manual, and the county purchasing department shall be utilized on all such acquisitions.
(Res. of 12-8-80, § 3; Code 1991, § 2.20.030)
Cross reference— Revenue and finance, tit. 3.
2.50.040. - Recreation and parks director.
The top administrative officer of the county recreation and parks department shall be the recreation and parks director. The director shall exercise overall responsibility and management of the department, and shall be directly responsible to the county administrator. The recreation and parks director shall be appointed by the county administrator.
(Res. of 12-8-80, § 4; Code 1991, § 2.20.040)
2.50.050. - Composition of parks and leisure services board.
A.
The parks and leisure services board is composed of eight persons serving without pay, each member being appointed by the Hall County Board of Commissioners.
B.
The five voting members are to be appointed individually by the chairman and each of the four district commissioners.
C.
The three ex-officio members are nominated by the group they individually represent and the nominees are then confirmed by the commissioners. The ex-officio members consist of a sitting commissioner, an active Hall County School Board member and a member of the agricultural center advisory committee. The board members may not be a full time regular county employee; however, the board member may be an elected official of the county.
(Res. of 4-22-2010, § 1)
Editor's note— Res. of Apr. 22, 2010, § 1, deleted former § 2.50.050, and enacted a new § 2.50.050 as set out herein. Former § 2.50.050 pertained to composition of recreation and parks board. See the Code Comparative Table for complete derivation.
2.50.060. - Terms of parks and leisure services board members.
A.
The terms of office for the members of the parks and leisure services board shall be for four years, or until their successors are appointed and qualified. The terms may be staggered in order to maintain continuity of service;
B.
The board will be composed of residents of Hall County;
C.
Each member will serve one four-year term and will be eligible to serve additional, consecutive four-year terms if appointed by the county commission;
D.
The board member terms will coincide with the commissioner's term who selected the individual for appointment to the board;
E.
The ex-officio appointment will be made annually by the body they represent.

2.50.061. - Meetings.
A.
The parks and leisure services board meetings will be held on a monthly basis.
B.
The first regular meeting in January of each year shall be called the organizational meeting. The purpose of the meeting shall be the election and installation of officers and other business that may need to come before such meetings.
C.
The parks and leisure services board shall meet and organize by electing one of their members as chairman and such other officers as may be necessary; vacancies in such board occurring otherwise than by expiration of term shall be filled by the county board of commissioners only for the unexpired term.
D.
The procedure for conducting all meetings shall be in accordance with Robert's Rules of Order.
E.
Any agenda items to be considered should submitted to the chairman of the parks and leisure services board and the parks and leisure services director no later than three business days prior to the scheduled meeting date.
F.
Special meetings may be called by the chairman of the board, the parks and leisure services director, or on the written request of at least three members of the board. Notice of such special meetings shall be served on other members by phone or in writing at least 24 hours in advance of the meeting.
G.
Three voting members shall constitute a quorum at any regular or special meeting.
H.
The absence for three consecutive regular meetings of any member of the board without due and acceptable excuse, shall constitute a vacancy on the board.
I.
All meetings are open to the public.
(Res. of 4-22-2010, § 3)
2.50.070. - Powers and duties of parks and leisure services board.
The parks and leisure services board shall act in an advisory capacity to the parks and leisure services director and the Hall County Board of Commissioners to assist in determining the best, most effective, and most economical manner in which to provide recreational activities and parks to meet the various leisure interests of the citizens of the county. The specific duties of the parks and leisure services board shall include the following:
A.
To promote recreation to the citizens of the county of all ages and interests;
B.
To solicit input from citizens and bring their requests and concerns to the board and/or parks and leisure services director, and communicate to citizens information and/or concerns relative to matters involving parks and recreation in Hall County.
C.
To assist in formalizing and in overseeing the implementation of policies regarding programs of both athletic and general recreation through coordination with the parks and leisure services director;
D.
To review and advise the commissioners of the county on matters of park development, park location, personnel selection, and purchasing;
E.
To advise the parks and leisure services director in the formulation of the parks and leisure services department budget;
F.
To seek public input as to parks and leisure services development and programs, and to assure the basic elements of the master plan are followed;
G.
To review and approve the official minutes of all board meetings as prepared by the parks and leisure services department, prior to the transmittal of such minutes to the county administrator's office;
H.
To act in an advisory capacity to the parks and leisure services department in promoting, marketing, programming, planning and other leisure services related aspects of the parks and leisure services program including the long range planning for resources for capital projects for rehabilitation, design and/or development of recreation and park facilities.
I.
To assist and advise the parks and leisure services director in the establishment of essential policies, rules and regulations relating to the planning, acquisition, disposition, operation, use, care and maintenance of areas and structures owned, leased or otherwise acquired by Hall County for use as parks;
J.
To assist and advise the parks and leisure services department staff liaison in the development of a continuing plan for the parks and leisure services program;
K.
To assist and advise the parks and leisure services department in establishing priorities at budget time for those items other than administrative functions relating to parks and leisure services policy;
(Res. of 4-22-2010, § 4)
Editor's note— Res. of Apr. 22, 2010, § 4, deleted former § 2.50.070, and enacted a new § 2.50.070 as set out herein. Former § 2.50.070 pertained to powers and duties of recreation and parks board. See the Code Comparative Table for complete derivation.
2.50.071. - Staff support.
Staff support will be assigned by the parks and leisure services director to process the administrative work of the board, prepare agendas, record meeting minutes and to provide staff guidance/update. The director shall prepare or cause to be prepared a manual for each member of the board to include the following information:
A.
Copy of ordinance authorizing the parks and leisure services board.
B.
By-laws of the board.
C.
List of fees and charges used by the department.
D.
Copy of current annual budget.
E.
Site plans of each park area owned and operated by the county and list of their facilities and equipment.
F.
Any other information helpful in acquainting new board members of the procedures and operation of the park and leisure services board.
(Res. of 4-22-2010, § 5)

2.50.080. - Littering prohibited.
It is unlawful for any person to throw or deposit litter on the grounds, streets, sidewalks, fountain, pond, lake, swimming pool, stream or other body of water in any recreation facility, except within public receptacles and in such a manner that the litter will be prevented from being carried or deposited by the elements upon any part of the recreation facility. Where public receptacles are not provided, all such litter shall be carried away from the park by the person responsible for its presence and shall be properly disposed of elsewhere.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 2; Code 1991, § 2.20.080)
Cross reference— Littering, § 8.60.090 et seq.
2.50.090. - Weapons prohibited.
It is unlawful for any person to take on a recreational facility any weapon; or to shoot, use, carry or employ any weapon or similar device in a recreation facility.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 3; Code 1991, § 2.20.090)
2.50.091. - Consumer fireworks and fireworks prohibited.
Without a valid permit issued by the Hall County Fire Services Department, it shall be unlawful for any person to take into or onto a Hall County park or Hall County recreation facility any consumer fireworks or fireworks as defined in this chapter. It is also unlawful to shoot, explode, use, ignite, carry or employ any consumer fireworks or fireworks or similar devices in any Hall County park or recreation facility without a valid permit.
(Res. of 9-10-15(1), § 3)
2.50.100. - Throwing objects prohibited.
It is unlawful for any person to throw any stone or missile at any person or at any public or private building or sign in a recreation facility.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 4; Code 1991, § 2.20.100)
2.50.110. - Obscene language and fighting words.
It is unlawful for any person, without provocation, to use obscene language or fighting words in a recreation facility.
(Res. of 10-9-89, § 1; Code 1991, § 2.20.110)
2.50.120. - Fires restricted.
It is unlawful for any person to build or maintain a fire in a recreation facility except in designated areas which are clearly marked by signs.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 6; Code 1991, § 2.20.120)
2.50.130. - Damaging vegetation prohibited.
It is unlawful for any person to cut trees, tree limbs, shrubbery, flowers or other vegetation or dig up or damage trees, flowers, or shrubbery or other vegetation in a recreation facility.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 7; Code 1991, § 2.20.130)
2.50.140. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited generally.
It is unlawful for any person to possess, use, consume, and/or sell any alcoholic beverage in a recreation facility owned and/or operated by Hall County, Georgia, except as may be specifically authorized by the Official Code of Hall County for professional sporting events. However, alcoholic beverage possession, use, consumption, and/or sale may be allowed in a recreation facility owned and/or operated by Hall County, Georgia for events that are approved by the Hall County Board of Commissioners (or its designee) and properly licensed or permitted by the Hall County Business License Director. In determining whether to allow the possession, use, consumption, and/or sale of alcoholic beverages within a county owned or operated recreation facility, the board of commissioners (or its designee) may consider the following factors:
(1)
Whether allowing the sale or use of such beverages would violate state or local law;
(2)
Whether allowing the sale or use of such beverages would violate covenants, leases, or other binding documents relating to the recreation facility at issue;
(3)
Whether the proposal is appropriately limited in time and scope;
(4)
Whether the proposal would adversely affect the health or safety of any person;
(5)
Whether the proposal would adversely affect traffic and/or parking or create any dangerous traffic situation;
(6)
Whether the proposal would adversely affect the facility or create the possibility of destruction of public property, including recreation facilities;
(7)
Whether the proposal could adversely affect residents and/or businesses in the vicinity of the recreation facility at issue; or
(8)
Any other factors which might rationally relate to the propriety of the request for permission to possess, use, consume, or sell alcohol in the recreation facility at issue.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 8; Code 1991, § 2.20.140; Res. of 2-11-10(1), § 1; Res. of 4-26-12(1), § 1; Res. of 3-24-22(1) , § 1)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages prohibited at Corps of Engineers operated parks, § 2.50.260; open containers of alcoholic beverages in vehicles, ch. 9.10.
2.50.150. - Alcoholic beverage advertising prohibited.
It is unlawful for any person to advertise any alcoholic beverage either on uniforms or by public display in a recreation facility.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 9; Code 1991, § 2.20.150)
Cross reference— Malt beverages and wine, ch. 5.30.
2.50.160. - Vehicles restricted to designated areas.
It is unlawful for any person to drive any vehicle in a recreation facility except upon roadways and paths constructed, designated and maintained for vehicular traffic. Recreation facility employees whose duties require them to drive maintenance vehicles and equipment shall be exempt from the limitations set forth in this section.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 10; Code 1991, § 2.20.160)
Cross reference— Vehicles and traffic, tit. 10.
2.50.170. - Parking restricted to designated areas.
It is unlawful to park a vehicle in a recreation facility except in those areas designated by appropriate signs as vehicle parking areas.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 11; Code 1991, § 2.20.170)
Cross reference— Vehicles and traffic, tit. 10.
2.50.180. - Parking limited to facility users.
It is unlawful for any person to park in a recreation area if the person is not utilizing the recreation facility.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 12; Code 1991, § 2.20.180)
Cross reference— Vehicles and traffic, tit. 10.
2.50.190. - Commercial activity restricted.
It is unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale any merchandise or operate a concession or engage in any commercial activity in a recreation facility without written authorization of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 13; Code 1991, § 2.20.190)
Cross reference— Business licenses and regulations, tit. 5.
2.50.200. - Hours open to public restricted.
It is unlawful for any person to enter or be within a recreation facility between the hours of 12:01 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. daily except as follows:
A.
Authorized recreation facility personnel;
B.
Overnight campers who are within officially designated overnight camping areas only. Use of a recreation facility may be further restricted in those areas where the county recreation and parks board shall from time to time designate by posting on the affected premises the time the facilities are available for public use.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 14; Code 1991, § 2.20.200)
2.50.210. - Hours for vehicles restricted.
It is unlawful for any person to drive a vehicle in or on a recreation facility or attempt to park or park a vehicle on any of the streets, avenues, or parking areas or at any location within a recreation facility between the hours of 12:01 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. daily except as follows:
A.
Authorized recreation facility personnel;
B.
Overnight campers who are within officially designated overnight camping areas only. Use of a recreation facility may be further restricted in those areas where the county recreation and parks board shall from time to time designate by posting on the affected premises the time the facilities are available for public use.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 15; Code 1991, § 2.20.210)
Cross reference— Vehicles and traffic, tit. 10.
2.50.220. - Use of recreation facilities.
It is unlawful for any person to use or attempt to use any of the facilities within a recreation facility except during the hours and in accordance with the rules and regulations established by the recreation authority.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 16; Code 1991, § 2.20.220)
2.50.230. - Interference with use prohibited.
It is unlawful for any person to engage in any activity within a recreation facility that could cause injury to other persons or interfere with the use and enjoyment of the recreation facility by other persons.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 17; Code 1991, § 2.20.230)
2.50.240. - Compliance with rules and regulations.
It is unlawful for any person to violate any rules or regulations relating to the use of the recreation area as established by the recreation authority where such rules and regulations have been approved and adopted by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 18; Code 1991, § 2.20.240)
2.50.250. - Violations; penalty.
Any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership violating any provision of this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereinafter be amended, or shall fail to do anything required by this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the Magistrate or State Court of Hall County, and upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in the Official Code of Hall County, Georgia section 1.50.020 for each violation in the discretion of the court.
(Res. of 3-12-84, § 19; Code 1991, § 2.20.250; Res. of 6-10-04(1), § 1)
2.50.260. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited at Corps of Engineers operated parks.
A.
For the purposes of this section, certain words or terms used in this section shall be defined as follows:
Alcoholic beverages means intoxicating beverages, malt beverages, wine or any other beverage containing any alcohol whatsoever.
Person includes the words "individuals," "firms," "partnership," "corporation," "associations," and governmental bodies and all other legal entities.
B.
Consumption, possession or the use of alcoholic beverages is prohibited in all posted park locations operated by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers.
C.
Pursuant to a cooperative law enforcement contract between the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers and Hall County, Georgia, Hall County Sheriff's Department personnel shall have authority to enforce, as a County resolution, Corps of Engineers regulations prohibiting alcoholic beverage consumption, possession, and use in Corps of Engineers operated parks where so posted.
D.
1.
Any person who violates or fails to comply with any provision of this ordinance [section] shall be guilty of a violation of a county ordinance and shall be amenable to the process of the Magistrate or State Court of Hall County, Georgia.
2.
Any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership violating any provision of this section as the same exists or as it may hereinafter be amended, or shall fail to do anything required by this section as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the Magistrate or State Court of Hall County, and upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in the Official Code of Hall County, Georgia section 1.50.020 for each violation in the discretion of the court.
(Res. of 11-15-91; Code 1991, § 2.20.260; Res. of 12-12-96, § 1; Res. of 6-10-04(1), § 1)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages prohibited in recreation facilities, § 2.50.140; open containers of alcoholic beverages in vehicles, ch. 9.10.
