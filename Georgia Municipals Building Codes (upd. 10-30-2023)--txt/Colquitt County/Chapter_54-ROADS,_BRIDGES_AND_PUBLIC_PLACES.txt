ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 54-1. - Pipes, wires, etc.
No person shall install, construct, maintain, repair, renew, remove or relocate pipes, mains, conduits, cables, wires, poles, towers or other equipment along, over or under any public road maintained by the county or the state in the unincorporated areas of the county without a permit issued by the board of commissioners. No person shall do any act for which such a permit is required except upon the terms and conditions of the permit and such other rules and regulations as the board of commissioners may promulgate.
(Code 1981, § 20-1)
Sec. 54-2. - Abandonment of roads.
(a)
Any person, including the county administrator and any other county officer, desiring to close any road which is a part of the county road system, shall inform the board of commissioners of the location of such road, the names and addresses of all parties known to own real property adjacent to the road proposed to be closed, and the reasons for desiring the abandonment by the county of such road.
If a request for such closure is made by a person who is not a county officer, such request shall be made on a form to be provided by the county administrator which shall be submitted to the county clerk together with the fee adopted by the board of commissioners, which may be amended from time to time as the need arises.
(b)
The county administrator will review the application containing the information set forth in subsection (a) of this section, and if such application appears to be meritorious, the county administrator shall give written notice by registered mail to all adjacent landowners notifying such landowners that a public hearing will be held to determine whether the road in question should be abandoned. Such written notice will be given at least 15 days prior to such hearing and direct any party objection to such abandonment to show cause at such hearing why the road should not be abandoned. If the county administrator deems the application to be unfounded or without sufficient merit, no further action will be taken unless specifically directed by a majority of the board.
(c)
The county administrator will place notice in the official organ of the county giving a description of the road to be abandoned, and further giving notice to the public of the public hearing and directing all interested parties to appear and show cause why the road should not be abandoned. Such advertisement shall run a minimum of two times within three weeks prior to said public hearing.
(d)
The board of commissioners will decide whether to abandon such road at such hearing, or at such other time as the board elects, and shall effect such abandonment by executing a "certificate of abandonment" to be filed with the county administrator and by directing county employees to stop maintenance of such road.
(e)
The land comprising the road abandoned shall revert to the adjacent landowners with each landowner's boundary line being extended to the center of such abandoned road; provided, a landowner can establish by a preponderance of the evidence that the road, or a section thereof, should revert to him and not to both such adjacent landowners.
(Code 1981, § 20-3; Ord. No. 2020-ORD-1, 4-7-20)

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 54-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alleys means minor ways which are used primarily for vehicular service access to the rear or side of properties otherwise abutting on a street.
Arterial road means a road used primarily for faster and/or heavier traffic and includes all federal, state and major paved roads.
Collector road means a road used to carry traffic from minor or access roads to the major system of arterial roads and highways and promises a traffic potential greater than that of minor roads.
Cul-de-sac means short minor residential road with only one end open to vehicular traffic and being permanently terminated at the other end by a vehicle turnaround.
Dead-end road means a road with only one end open to vehicular traffic and not provided with a vehicle turnaround at the other end.
Easement means a grant by a property owner of the use, for a specific purpose, of a piece of land by the general public, a corporation, or a person.
Fee simple deed means a deed without any liens.
Minor residential road means a road used primarily for access to the abutting properties.
Road means a way dedicated for vehicular traffic by the general public whether designated as a street, highway, parkway, road, avenue, boulevard, land, place or other similar designations.
(Code 1981, § 20-25)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 54-32. - Purpose and intent.
The public health, safety, morals and general welfare of the county requires the orderly, harmonious, and progressive development of the area within the county, and in the interest of this goal this article is adopted to:
(1)
Encourage the development of economically sound and stable communities;
(2)
Ensure the establishment of an economically maintainable network of roads in the county;
(3)
Ensure safe and convenient traffic, both vehicular and pedestrian, access to new land developments; and
(4)
Ensure harmony and compatibility between new roads and existing county roadways, state and federal highways, and streets of the several municipalities.
(Code 1981, § 20-22)
Sec. 54-33. - Jurisdiction.
From and after the adoption of the ordinance from which this article was derived all roads proposed for the unincorporated portion of the county, except for through streets and roads that may be included in a major thoroughfares plan adopted by the board of commissioners, shall be built by the landowner or developer proposing such road and shall be located and built in the manner and to the standards herein prescribed.
(Code 1981, § 20-23)
Sec. 54-34. - Limited responsibility of county.
No road will be accepted for county maintenance unless the provisions of this article have been met. The approval of the plans required by section 54-53 constitutes no implied responsibility on the county for any assistance in construction, either in materials, labor or equipment, these being entirely the responsibility of the developer. Specifically the developer will move at his cost all utility lines and poles, install all required drainage facilities, install all culverts for access to abutting property, construct and pave the roadway and improve the entire right-of-way.
(Code 1981, § 20-24)

DIVISION 2. - PROCEDURES

Sec. 54-51. - Preliminary plats—Contents.
Any person desiring to construct and pave a road in the county shall, prior to the beginning of any construction of such road or the offering for sale of any parcel of land not abutting an existing public roadway, prepare preliminary plats of the proposed road. Such plan must be at a scale sufficient to depict clearly all points pertinent to the proposal such as curve radii, intersections and off-sets.
(1)
All preliminary plats shall include the following specific data concerning proposed improvements unless waived by the planning commission:
a.
Streets: Right-of-way and roadway widths; similar data for alleys, if any.
b.
Other rights-of-way or easements; location, width and purpose.
c.
Sites, if any, to be reserved or dedicated for playgrounds or other public uses.
d.
Title, numerical scale, graphic scale, north arrow, date.
(2)
The preliminary plat shall be accompanied by:
a.
Drainage areas, profiles showing existing ground surface and proposed street grades, and preliminary plan of proposed sanitary and stormwater sewers with grades and sizes indicated. All elevations shall be based on sea level datum plane.
b.
Written request for variance from the terms of this article.
c.
Existing roads immediately adjacent on all sides shall be shown on the sketch or referenced by distance. The location of existing roads less than one-half mile from the proposed street shall be dimensioned in feet, accurate to within 50 feet. The location of those between one-half mile and one mile distant shall be dimensioned in feet, accurate to within 100 feet while the location of those over one mile distant shall be dimensioned in miles and tenths of a mile, accurate to within one-tenth of a mile.
(3)
If the proposed road is to be located in a subdivision, then the preliminary plat shall also be accompanied by a certificate from the county board of health, that all health department rules and regulations concerning the development of a subdivision have been complied with.
(Code 1981, § 20-31)
Sec. 54-52. - Same—Review, recommendations.
(a)
The developer shall meet with the county administrator and road superintendent to review the contents of the preliminary plat specified above.
(b)
The requirements of the regulations including paving standards will be discussed. The road superintendent will review the location, type, and size of under-the-roadway drainage culverts, if required, and the location and design of ditches, ditch spillways, back-slopes and drainage easements required on adjoining property.
(c)
The developer shall note any recommendations made by the road superintendent and/or county administrator for the purpose of preparing a final plan.
(d)
Pursuant to this section, the county administrator shall notify the board of commissioners serving the district in which the proposed road will be located of the proposal and the intended use of the proposed road.
(Code 1981, § 20-32)
Sec. 54-53. - Final plan.
(a)
Five copies of a final plan containing the information required in the original proposal, modified to include the suggestions of the road superintendent and the county administrator, along with five copies of each required drainage easement shall be submitted to the road superintendent. If, in his opinion, the plan meets all of the planimetric requirements of these regulations, he shall note such approval on all copies and apply his initials. He shall then forward all copies to the county administrator. The county administrator shall obtain on all copies the initials of the board of commissioners serving the district in which the proposed road will be located. Following the approval of the board of commissioners, the county administrator shall execute, initial and date each copy. He shall forward two copies to the developer, retain two copies for his files and return one copy to the road superintendent.
(b)
Upon receiving a copy of the plan, the developer may proceed with on-the-ground location of the roadway.
(Code 1981, § 20-33)
Sec. 54-54. - Variance—Application, recommendation for commission.
(a)
When an owner or developer believes that the strict adherence to these regulations imposes an undue hardship, written application for a variance may be filed with the board of commissioners at least ten days prior to any regular meeting. The application shall cite the section from which a variance is desired, the alternative that is being proposed, and the reason for the request.
(b)
The county administrator will confer with the road superintendent and a recommendation shall be prepared for the board of commissioners.
(c)
The applicant or his agent may appear before the board of commissioners in person at its regular meeting to further explain the request.
(d)
Where the board of commissioners finds that because of topographic or other conditions peculiar to the site, literal enforcement of a provision of this article may result, in an individual case, in unnecessary hardship to the developer, it may vary the regulations where, in the opinion of the board of commissioners such variation will not have the effect of nullifying the intent and purpose of these regulations. Any variance shall be recorded in the minutes of the board of commissioners meeting together with the reasoning used to justify it.
(Code 1981, § 20-34)
Sec. 54-55. - Same—Conditions.
In granting variances and modifications, the board of commissioners may require such conditions as will secure substantially the objectives of the standards or requirements so varied and modified.
(Code 1981, § 20-35)
Sec. 54-56. - Same—Modification of plans.
(a)
If the approved variance substantially affected the prepared plans required by section 54-53, the plans shall be modified and reapproved as provided for in that section.
(b)
If the variance is approved prior to the submission of required plans, a note stating "includes variance approved by county commission on (date)" shall be included on the submitted plans.
(c)
If plans have been approved as provided for in section 54-53 and the variance granted are minor, the road superintendent shall note and initial the approved variance on each of four copies of the approved plan.
(Code 1981, § 20-36)
Sec. 54-57. - Preconstruction inspection.
When the developer has adequately, through centerline stakes, right-of-way clearance or similar means, located the proposed road, he shall notify the road superintendent who shall inspect the layout. If, in his opinion the layout follows the approved plan, he shall so note that fact on the developer's copy of the approved plan and date the note. He shall make the same notation on his copy of the approved plan. Upon such approval and upon compliance with section 54-58, construction may begin.
(Code 1981, § 20-37)
Sec. 54-58. - Notification of utilities of intent to grade or excavate.
Prior to actual grading or excavation the developer or his agent (contractor, if applicable) shall notify any and all utility companies, including municipalities, that are known to have underground facilities in the area of the intention to grade or excavate.
(Code 1981, § 20-38)
Sec. 54-59. - Inspection and approval upon completion of construction.
Upon completion of all construction the developer shall notify the road superintendent who shall inspect the entire project for conformity with the approved plan and these regulations. When he is able to so do, he shall note that approval and the date on the developer's, the commissioner's, the administrator's and his own copies of the approved plan.
(Code 1981, § 20-39)
Sec. 54-60. - Notification of department of transportation.
As soon as practical after construction approval the county administrator shall, for mapping purposes, notify the district engineer's office of the state department of transportation of the new road and its location.
(Code 1981, § 20-40)
Sec. 54-61. - Performance bond or certified check.
Upon acceptance of the road by the county, the developer shall deliver to the county a performance bond or certified check payable to the board of commissioners in an amount sufficient to reimburse the county for any costs that it might have to assume to provide a road of the intended quality at the end of a one year warranty period from the date of acceptance.
(Code 1981, § 20-41)
Sec. 54-62. - Title.
Upon the satisfactory completion of all the conditions of this article the county shall accept the road and the developer shall give a fee simple title by warranty to the county.
(Code 1981, § 20-42)

DIVISION 3. - REQUIRED STANDARDS

Sec. 54-76. - Alignment of collector roads.
(a)
Collector roads are to be aligned so as to contribute to the circulatory network of the county. To achieve this objective, the road superintendent may require that they be terminated so that future rights-of-way may continue on the same alignment into other property. If land abutting a proposed or existing collector road in the vicinity of another proposed collector road is owned by the applicant, then the board of commissioners may require that the road be constructed through the property to achieve an intersection, provided the proposed land development for which a collector road is to be built is one-fourth mile or less from such intersection.
(b)
Minor residential roads are to be aligned so as to follow the contours of the existing terrain as much as possible while simultaneously considering the best land utilization by the developer.
(c)
At the city limits of incorporated places and in the urbanizing areas adjacent thereto proposed collector roads shall, wherever possible, continue the existing street patterns.
(d)
Curves on collector roads shall have a radius sufficient to achieve a sight distance of 400 feet, and on minor residential roads, 150 feet.
(Code 1981, § 20-51)
Sec. 54-77. - Intersections.
(a)
Block lengths shall not be less than 400 feet where residential subdivisions are to be built.
(b)
Intersections with collector or arterial roads shall not be located closer than 400 feet of the crest of a hill.
(c)
Each proposed road intersection shall be, if at all possible, at right angles but in no case shall any intersection angle be less than 75 degrees. If the basic alignment of a proposed road has to be deflected to achieve the required intersection angle, the sight distance from the centerline of the road in the curve to the intersection shall be at least 400 feet for a collector and 150 feet for a minor residential road.
(d)
No proposed intersection shall be offset less than 200 feet, whether the intersection is on a proposed road or between a proposed road and an existing road where the proposed new road is, in effect, a continuation of an existing road.
(Code 1981, § 20-52)
Sec. 54-78. - Dead ends and cul-de-sacs.
(a)
Where a collector road is terminated as provided for in section 54-76 a turnaround is to be provided and if necessary, a temporary easement granted to the county for the use of undeveloped future right-of-way for that purpose.
(b)
Minor residential roads designed so as to be permanently dead-ended or as a cul-de-sac shall terminate in a turnaround constructed to the same standards as the road and a right-of-way as required in section 54-79.
(Code 1981, § 20-53)
Sec. 54-79. - Dimensions.
(a)
A minor residential road shall have a right-of-way of at least 60 feet. A collector road shall have a right-of-way of at least 70 feet.
(b)
A collector road shall have a finished base course of at least 25 feet centered within the required right-of-way.
(c)
A minor residential road shall have a finished base course of at least 21 feet centered within the required right-of-way.
(d)
A five-foot shoulder shall be provided on either side between the finished base course and the facing slope of the required drainage ditch.
(e)
The drainage ditch shall have a depth of at least 2½ feet below the crown of the road. The road superintendent may require more depth in consideration of the drainage area to be served. The facing and back slopes of the ditches shall have a ratio of three to one except that, in consideration of soil stability, the back slope may be at a different angle at appropriate location along the road when suggested or approved by the road superintendent.
(f)
Appropriate adjustments in the backslope angle are to be made at any ditch drainage spillways.
(g)
Where a road is built on a fill of more than 2½ feet, the ditch requirements are not applicable but the slope of the fill shall not exceed three to one.
(Code 1981, § 20-54)
Sec. 54-80. - Grades.
(a)
Cuts and fills shall be made so that the vertical alignment allows safe passing and stopping sight distances as required for a 30 miles per hour speed design.
(b)
Grades in excess of five percent may require additional erosion control measures as determined by the road superintendent.
(Code 1981, § 20-55)
Sec. 54-81. - Signs.
All streets and roads shall be signed in accordance with the state department of transportation rules and regulations for the protection of the traveling public.
(Code 1981, § 20-56)
Sec. 54-82. - Ground cover.
All shoulders and drainage slopes, front and back, within roadways shall have a hearty ground cover of any permanent vegetation or plant material.
(Code 1981, § 20-58)
Sec. 54-83. - Utilities.
(a)
Before any utilities are placed on any county roads the utility company shall furnish the county a drawing of the proposed construction and the county issue a permit to the utility company to do the work.
(b)
All utilities crossing the right-of-way shall be encased within the road bed structure and shall be placed three feet below the ditch bottom. Those running parallel with the right-of-way or roadway shall be placed in the outer five feet of the right-of-way at a minimum depth of 2½ feet.
(Code 1981, § 20-59)

ARTICLE III. - DRIVEWAYS AND ENCROACHMENT CONTROL

Sec. 54-101. - Permits.
(a)
Permits will be obtained for any person to construct a new driveway or improve any existing driveway on roads owned or controlled by the county.
(b)
Application for permit will be made on the appropriate county forms. Forms may be obtained from the board of commissioners' office.
(c)
Construction permit approving driveway will be issued by the county administrator or his designee.
(d)
The county makes no charge for permits.
(e)
Permits may be revoked if any of the conditions are violated.
(Ord. of 7-2-91, § I)
Sec. 54-102. - Specifications.
(a)
The size of the pipe shall be determined by the county department of roads and bridges.
(b)
A driveway on paved roads shall tie into the shoulder point as shown on the detail drawings attached to the ordinance from which this article was derived and on file in the office of the county clerk.
(c)
A driveway on dirt roads shall tie into the shoulder point of road.
(d)
A driveway shall not exceed 50 feet in length.
(e)
There shall be no less than 12 feet between driveways.
(f)
A dead ramp may be used on the break of a hill not to exceed 50 feet in length.
(g)
Slopes shall be placed on right-of-way and shall be no steeper than 4:1.
(h)
Only safety headwalls or those specifically approved by the county administrator or his designee, may be used.
(Ord. of 7-2-91, § II)
Sec. 54-103. - Cost responsibility.
The applicant shall pay the following charges related to driveway pipe installation:
Pipe .....Cost plus 10%
Encroachment permit .....$45.00
Labor .....$250.00
Earth fill (dirt), per load .....$125.00
(Ord. of 7-2-91, § III; Ord. No. 2005-4, § 1, 8-2-05)
Sec. 54-104. - Exceptions.
(a)
If there was never a ditch at the proposed location and the county pulls ditch, then county will install driveway at pipe price.
(b)
The county will install driveways on grade projects and maintain for one year after project completion.
(c)
The county will repair any damage caused by county forces to driveways.
(d)
The county will maintain driveways as needed in the course of regularly scheduled road maintenance up to the edge of the county right-of-way.
(Ord. of 7-2-91, § IV; Ord. No. 96-9, 10-21-96)
Sec. 54-105. - Liability and responsibility.
(a)
Applicants to whom permits have been granted shall at all times indemnify and save harmless the county and agents of the county from responsibility for any damages or liability arising from the maintenance, repair, operation or use of any installation, part of installation, construction procedures or actual construction approved under the permit.
(b)
The applicant will move, relocate and remove any installation or construction placed on the county right-of-way when ordered in writing to do so without cost to the county.
(Ord. of 7-2-91, § V)
Sec. 54-106. - Penalty and fines.
All fines and penalties imposed for the violation of this article will be assessed by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 7-2-91, § VI)

DIVISION 2. - SOUND AMPLIFICATION DEVICES ON COUNTY PROPERTY[4]

Footnotes:

Sec. 54-146. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Community means the citizens of the county.
County property means the exterior of the county courthouse and the real property surrounding and adjoining the county courthouse, such adjoining real property known as the courthouse square in Moultrie, Colquitt County, Georgia.
Sound amplification device and/or equipment means any device or equipment, whether electric, electronic or of another sort, used to amplify voices, music or any other sounds.
(Code 1981, § 6-61)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 54-147. - Permit required.
No sound amplification device or equipment may be used or operated on, upon, through, or connected to any county property without first obtaining a permit approved by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1981, § 6-62)
Sec. 54-148. - Permit application; contents; grant, denial of permit; fee.
(a)
The permit referred to in this division shall be obtained by making application to the board of commissioners through the office of the county administrator on a form prescribed for such purpose by the county administrator. The form shall state the name, address and phone number of the applicant, the name and address of the organization represented by applicant, if there is one, the date requested for a permit, the hours of use of amplification devices and/or equipment on county property, the type of amplification devices which shall be used, the approximate number of amplification devices which shall be used, and a statement as to why the proposed event for which amplification is desired is in the best interest of the community. The permit application shall further contain the following statement: "I agree and certify that all amplification device(s) and/or equipment used on county property on the above dates shall be used in such a manner so as not to interfere with the operation of businesses located in the immediate area surrounding the county property." The application shall be signed by the applicant and submitted by the applicant together with the application fee to the county administrator.
(b)
The county administrator shall review the application and determine if such application meets the requirements of this division, including the payment of the permit fee, and if the application meets such requirements, the same shall be brought before the board of commissioners at their next meeting for approval or denial by the board. If all the requirements of this division have not been met, the county administrator shall notify the applicant and retain the application until such requirements have been met. The board of commissioners may deny the permit application for good cause, including determinations by the board of commissioners that the proposed permit would not be in the best interest of the community, or that there is a substantial likelihood that the proposed permit would unduly interfere with the operation of the businesses located in the immediate area of the county property, or that the applicant or the organization represented by applicant has in the past not complied with the provisions of this division. The board may grant or deny the permit in whole or in part. Once a permit has been granted, the county administrator shall note on the application the fact of the grant, any modifications of the application required by the board pursuant to the grant, shall sign same, and return a signed copy to the applicant, which copy shall serve as the permit.
(c)
All applications shall be made and signed by an individual citizen of the county, and all permits shall be issued in the name of such individual citizen. Once a permit is issued, such citizen shall be responsible for full compliance with the terms of this division. The application fee shall be $25.00 per application.
(d)
All applications for permits shall be made and signed by the applicant at least three calendar weeks prior to the proposed beginning permit date.
(Code 1981, § 6-63)
Sec. 54-149. - Penalty for violation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to intentionally violate any of the provisions of this division. Each violation of this division may be punishable as provided in section 1-11.
(Code 1981, § 6-64)
