ARTICLE I. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 24-1. - Reserved.
Sec. 24-2. - Purpose, authority, and enactment
The mayor and council of the city find that the historical and cultural heritage of the municipality is among its most valued and important assets and that the preservation of this heritage is essential to the promotion of the health, prosperity, and general welfare of the people of the city and of the public generally. Further, the city acknowledges that the municipality's cemeteries, graveyards, and burial grounds are uniquely precious and vulnerable thus deserving of devoted stewardship; that the city is richly blessed with the heritage and history of its people who are now deceased; that many native pioneers and early residents were integral to the community's development; and that the resting places and memorials of those who have passed are valuable to current and future generations and are within the heart of the Madison Historic District.
Therefore, with the intent of establishing a commission for the purpose of caring for and managing the municipality's cemeteries, graveyards, and burying grounds, as herein defined; protecting and safeguarding the municipality's cemeteries; and regulating the use of such areas for the utility, education, pleasure, and welfare of the people of the municipality; the mayor and council of the city of hereby ordain and enact into law the cemetery chapter.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-3. - Jurisdiction.
The provisions of this chapter shall govern all land, buildings, structures and improvements within the boundaries delineated on the map entitled "Designated City Cemetery Areas" which is herein attached as "Exhibit A" of an amendment of August 12, 2002.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-4. - Short title.
This chapter shall be known as and may be cited as the "Cemetery Ordinance."
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-5. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the term:
(1)
Archeologist means any person who is a member of or meets the criteria for membership in the Society of Professional Archaeologist and can demonstrate experience in the excavation and interpretation of human graves or any person who is employed by the state or by any county or municipal governing authority as an archeologist.
(2)
Burial means the disposition of human remains by earthen burial in a grave.
(3)
Burial object means any item reasonably believed to have been intentionally placed with the human remains at the time of burial or interment or any memorial, tombstone, grave marker or shrine which may have been added subsequent to interment. Such term also means any inscribed or uninscribed marker, coping, curbing, enclosure, fencing, pavement, shelter, wall, stoneware, vegetation, pottery or other grave object erected or deposited incident to or subsequent to interment.
(4)
Burial rights means the deeded interest to a lot or space designated for interments.
(5)
Burial space means a single space within a lot designed for the interment of one human body.
(6)
Cemetery means all land in and owned by or under the supervision of the city devoted to the interment of human remains, either burial park for earth interments or a mausoleum for vault or crypt interments or a combination of one or more thereof, and all land hereafter acquired by the city for such purpose.
(7)
City means the City of Madison, Morgan County, Georgia.
(8)
City care means the cemetery lot maintenance performed by the city including the trimming of vegetation, preservation of graves and the removal of rubbish for all time thereafter; city care does not include work on monuments, markers, structures or other landscape elements or other unusual care.
(9)
Commission means the Madison Cemetery Stewardship Commission created and established pursuant to section 24-46 of this chapter.
(10)
Cremains means the cremated remains of a human body.
(11)
Crypt means a wholly or partially underground structure of suitable stone for the entombment of human remains or cremains.
(12)
Descendant means a person or group of persons related to a deceased human by blood or adoption in accordance with O.C.G.A. 19-1-1 et seq., as amended.
(13)
Disinterment means the removal of the buried remains of a deceased person, the casket, if any, and the burial receptacle, if any, from the ground.
(14)
Foundation means the base upon which a marker, monument or mausoleum is installed.
(15)
Funeral procession means multiple vehicles traveling closely in an orderly fashion from a church or chapel to graveside burial site.
(16)
Interment means the permanent disposition of the remains of a deceased human being by cremation, inurnment, entombment or burial.
(17)
Lot means the subdivision of land within a cemetery for the interment of one or more person(s).
(18)
Marker means a grave marker upon the lot to identify individual graves. Also known as a grave marker, headstone, etc.
(19)
Mausoleum means a stone or other suitable structure for the aboveground entombment of the dead.
(20)
Monument means a memorial erected on a lot principally to identify the family name.
(21)
Person means any individual, partnership, association, society, organization or corporation.
(22)
Remains means the bodies of deceased human beings in any stage of decomposition, including cremated remains.
(23)
Vault means a prefabricated receptacle for a casket or human remains usually placed below ground.
(24)
Visitor means any person or group of persons who enter the cemetery.
Secs. 24-6—24-25. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CITY CARE PROVISIONS

Sec. 24-26. - Power of mayor and council.
The mayor and city council shall have the power and authority to:
(1)
Provide, own and furnish, in and near the city, cemeteries for the burial of the dead;
(2)
Regulate and control the interments in such cemeteries;
(3)
Exercise police control and to care for such cemeteries; and,
(4)
Guarantee city care to lots in such cemeteries.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-27. - Administration.
The city manager, or his/her designee, shall be responsible for management of all municipal cemeteries. All record registration and fee payment shall be made to the city clerk's office.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-28. - Fees.
Fees for the municipal cemeteries shall be those established by the mayor and council and listed in the adopted schedule of fees.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-29. - Sale proceeds.
All proceeds from cemetery sales will be placed in a separate fund. None of the principal shall be expended under any circumstances. The proceeds of the interest generated therefrom will be used solely for the upkeep of city cemeteries.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Secs. 24-30—24-45. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - CEMETERY STEWARDSHIP COMMISSION

Sec. 24-46. - Creation of the commission.
The title of the commission shall be the "Madison Cemetery Stewardship Commission" (hereinafter referred to as the "Commission"). The commission shall have authority as established in Sections 203 and 204 hereof or as directed by the mayor and council of the city, shall be considered a part of the planning and development functions of the city government, and shall be administered by the staff of the city clerk's office and the planning and development department.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-47. - Membership of the commission; number, appointment, terms, and compensation.
The commission shall consist of seven members, who have demonstrated special interest, experience, or education in funerary history, architecture, management, preservation, landscape architecture, archeology or a related field. At least five commissioners shall be registered voters of the city and one shall be a liaison member of the historic preservation commission. Commission members shall be appointed by the mayor and council of the city and shall serve three-year terms. In order to achieve staggered terms, initial appointments shall be four members for two-year terms and three members for three-year terms. Commission members shall serve without compensation and are subject to all applicable municipal policies and code of ethics.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-48. - Powers of the commission; general.
The commission shall be authorized to:
(1)
Perform cemetery management activities as an official agency of the city care program as assigned by the cemetery administrator;
(2)
Advise and work with cemetery administrator to establish best maintenance measures regarding cemeteries and to establish additional programs or standards as may be necessary to augment the municipality's city care;
(3)
Prepare and maintain an inventory and conduct surveys of all burial objects, lots and/or features within a cemetery;
(4)
Make such investigations and studies of matters relating to cemeteries as the mayor and council or the commission itself may from time to time deem necessary or appropriate for the purposes of this chapter;
(5)
Recommend to the mayor and council the restoration or preservation of any cemetery or features therein;
(6)
Provide volunteer labor and/or services as well as funds for unique care of the cemetery as necessary, including on an as needed basis private or family markers that may be abandoned, desecrated, neglected, unmarked or threatened;
(7)
Conduct educational or special programs regarding cemetery preservation as a means of increasing public awareness and support;
(8)
Establish appropriate and necessary liaisons and contacts with other appropriate boards, commissions, departments, organizations, and municipalities within the county, region and state;
(9)
Establish procedures within the municipal planning and development department, with the aid of cemetery experts as may be necessary, regarding the review of zoning actions involving property immediately adjacent to city cemetery areas; provide recommendations to the county planning commission on all zoning actions within the purview of this article; and assist the building inspections department regarding the enforcement of all such stipulations and conditions;
(10)
Consult with cemetery experts in the historic preservation division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, the Georgia Trust for Historic Preservation, Inc., the Association for Gravestone Studies or other consultants and professionals as necessary for the purposes of this chapter;
(11)
Seek out state and federal funds for cemetery stewardship and make recommendations to the mayor and council concerning the most appropriate use of any funds acquired;
(12)
On behalf of and for the city, the commission may receive donations, grants, funds, or gifts of property, provided that the commission shall not obligate the city without prior consent and that such funds do not displace appropriated governmental funds; and,
(13)
Promote the acquisition by the city of additional cemeteries as necessary.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-49. - Same—Rules of procedure.
The commission shall adopt rules for the transaction of its business; shall provide for the time and place of regular meetings, and for the calling of special meetings. A quorum shall consist of a majority of the members.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-50. - Same—Cemetery regulations.
The commission shall adopt regulations for the management, care and use of municipal cemeteries. The commission shall have the flexibility to adopt and amend rules of procedure without amendment to this chapter. Such regulations and rules of procedure shall become effective after review and approval by the mayor and council of the city.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-51. - Conflict of interest.
At any time the commission reviews a project in which a member of the commission has ownership or other vested interest, that member shall abstain from voting or discussion of the project, other than answering direct questions.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-52. - Records of the commission.
A public record shall be kept of the commission's resolutions, proceedings, and actions. The commission shall keep a copy of all cemetery surveys, planning studies, rules and regulations applicable to cemeteries.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-53—24-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - REGULATIONS, APPEALS AND PENALTIES

Sec. 24-71. - Generally.
Cemetery regulations, as formulated by the cemetery stewardship commission in accordance with section 24-50, shall apply to all designated municipal cemetery areas.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-72. - Old Cemetery and New Cemetery.
No person, other than those having vested rights in Old Cemetery or New Cemetery as herein described and as depicted on the designated city cemetery area map, shall be permitted to bury their dead in Old Cemetery or New Cemetery. No further grave plots will be sold by the city in Old Cemetery or New Cemetery.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-73. - Appeals.
Any person adversely affected by any action of the commission may appeal to the mayor and council. The appeal must be filed with the city clerk within ten business days after the commission's decision has been made. Appeals from decisions of the city may be taken to the Superior Court of the county in the manner provided by law for convictions of municipal ordinance violations, namely, via petition for writ of certiorari.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 24-74. - Penalties.
Violations of any provisions of this chapter shall be punished in the same manner as provided by local law for the punishment of violations of other validly enacted ordinances and regulations of the city.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
Secs. 24-75—24-90. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - LEGAL STATUS PROVISIONS

Sec. 24-91. - Affirmation of existing local ordinances.
Nothing in this chapter shall be construed as to exempt property owners from complying with the existing city building code, nor to prevent any property owner from making any use of his property not prohibited by other statutes, ordinances, or regulations.
(Amd. of 8-12-02, 1(Exh. A))
