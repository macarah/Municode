Chapter 47 - OPEN BURNING
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 47-1—47-50. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - SPECIFIC REGULATIONS

Sec. 47-51. - In general.
No person shall cause, suffer, allow or permit open burning in any area within the city limits until first obtaining a permit from the city fire department.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-52. - Allowances.
After obtaining said permit, open burning will be allowed for the following purposes:
(1)
Reduction of leaves on the premises on which they fall by the person in control of the premises.
(2)
Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for the production or harvesting of crops.
(3)
The burning over of any forest land by the owner of such land.
(4)
Fires set for purposes of training fire-fighting personnel when all guidelines are met by state agencies.
(5)
Disposal of tree limbs from storm damage.
(6)
For weed abatement, disease and pest prevention.
(7)
Open burning in other than predominantly residential areas for the purpose of land clearing or construction or right-of-way maintenance provided the following conditions are met:
a.
Prevailing winds at the time of the burning are away from the major portion of the area's population;
b.
The location of the burning is at least 1000 feet from any dwelling located in a predominantly residential area;
c.
The amount of dirt on or in the material being burned is minimized;
d.
Heavy oils, asphalt materials, items containing natural or synthetic rubber, or any materials other than plant growth are not being burned; and
e.
No more than one pile 60 feet by 60 feet, or equivalent, is being burned within a nine acre area at one time.
(8)
Disposal of all packaging materials previously containing explosives, in accordance with U.S. Department of Labor Safety Regulations.
(9)
Open burning of vegetative material for the purpose of land clearing using an air curtain destructor provided the following conditions are met:
a.
Authorization for such burning is received from the fire department and state forestry office;
b.
The location of the air curtain destructor is at least 300 feet from any occupied structure or public road. Air curtain destructors used solely for utility line clearing or road clearing may be located at a lesser distance upon approval by the EPA and DNR divisions;
c.
No more than one air curtain destructor is operated within a ten acre area at one time or there must be at least 1000 feet between any two air curtain destructors;
d.
Only wood waste consisting of trees, logs, large brush and stumps which are relatively free of soil are burned in the air curtain destructor;
e.
Tires or other rubber products, plastics, heavy oils or asphaltic based or impregnated materials are not used to start or maintain the operation of the air curtain destructor;
f.
The air curtain destructor is constructed, installed and operated in a manner consistent with good air pollution control practice for minimizing emissions of fly ash and smoke; and
g.
The cleaning out of the air curtain destructor pit is performed in a manner to prevent fugitive dust.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-53. - Authority to put out fires.
The fire department or any city official shall have the authority to order any fire put out if it is determined there is a danger to public safety, a nuisance or significant environmental harm.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-54. - Times of permitted burning.
All permitted burning shall be carried out between dawn and dusk and all fires shall be extinguished by sunset. However, no burning of any nature shall be allowed on Sundays.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-55. - Location of permitted burning.
All burning shall be located on private property so as not to interfere with traffic on public streets or sidewalks.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-56. - Certain organizations exempt.
Organizations such as fire departments, churches, and groups organized for the training and education of children (such as Boy Scouts and Girl Scouts) may obtain a permit without complying with section 47-4.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-57. - Safety measures.
During any open burning, a competent person of at least 18 years of age shall be present during the entire burning process and control such burning. Such person shall have a hose connected to a water source or other fire extinguishing equipment readily available for use.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-58. - No burn days.
No burning of any kind shall be allowed within the city on a no burn day declared by the city fire department.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-59. - Conditions of permit.
All permits are issued on a daily basis and are good only for the day of issue and while weather conditions support a safe burn.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-60. - Amount of fires.
Only one fire may be in progress at any time except as noted in paragraph 47-2(9)c.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-61. - Refusal of permit.
A permit may be refused if the city can remove the product more safely than it can be burned, or other services are reasonably available which could remove or take the product.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-62. - Distances of burns.
No fire may be burned closer than 500 feet to any building, structure or any other improvement to the subject real property.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-63. - No burning of certain items.
The following items may not be burned: tires, any item which may emit gaseous vapors or odors, any item whose odors are likely to be injurious to public health, safety and welfare, any hazardous materials as the same are defined by state and/or federal laws, statutes and regulations. Nothing in this section shall prohibit the burning of nontreated wood.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-64. - Authority of officials.
The city fire department or any city official has the authority to enter any property to inspect items to be burned, and to return at any time to inspect progress of burning and to insure the fire is out.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-65. - Allowed burning without permit.
The following are allowed burning by the state without obtaining a permit:
(1)
For the purposes of cooking food, such as grilling.
(2)
Operation of devices using open flames such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters, and other flame-making equipment where approved safety measures are used and state guidelines are followed.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
Sec. 47-66. - Penalty.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof shall be subject to a fine of not more than $1,000.00. Each day such violation is committed or permitted to continue shall be deemed a separate offense. Any person violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall become liable to the city for any expense, loss or damage caused by the violation. The violation of any provision of this chapter may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings in the courts of competent jurisdiction in this state. Equitable proceedings for restraining order or injunction in the superior court may be maintained, notwithstanding the fact that the violation also constitutes a crime, and notwithstanding that other adequate remedies of law may exist.
(Ord. of 7-24-00, § 1)
