ARTICLE XV. - BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT

Sec. 14-471. - High level jobs beneficial to community.
The city recognizes that certain targeted businesses in fields, such as technology and life sciences, bring high level jobs to the city and are especially beneficial to the community.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 1, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-472. - In city's best interest to offer strategic inducements.
In order to compete for targeted businesses it is in the city's best interest to have the availability of offering strategic inducements.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 2, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-473. - Targeted business defined.
The city shall define targeted business as any preferred business or industry type as identified by the city, Partnership Gwinnett or a special impact project of such magnitude as may be determined by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 3, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-474. - Businesses eligible for inducements determined by city manager.
Targeted businesses meeting defined threshold levels (as outlined in section 14-476), may be eligible for inducements as determined by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 4, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-475. - Types of inducements; city manager authorized.
The city manager shall be authorized to offer inducements to targeted businesses meeting threshold requirements and those inducements may include the following:
(1)
Reduction of development permit fees.
(2)
Reduction of building permit fees.
(3)
Reduction of inspection and re-inspection fees.
(4)
Reduction of application fees for public hearings.
(5)
Reduction of business occupation taxes.
(6)
Reduction of any current or future impact fees.
(7)
Acceleration of the plan review and permitting process.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 5, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-476. - Threshold levels for inducements.
Inducements may be offered to targeted businesses which meet the following threshold levels:
(1)
Tier 1: Targeted business adds at least 25 new jobs and the jobs pay an average salary at least 1.25 times the county average for that industry.
(2)
Tier 2: Targeted business adds at least 100 new jobs and the jobs pay an average annual salary of at least 1.25 times the county average for that industry or $60,000 plus health benefits, whichever is greater.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 6, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-477. - Funding commitment from state.
In determining whether to offer inducements, the city manager may consider whether the targeted business has received a funding commitment from the state contingent upon the business' relocation or expansion within the state.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 7, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-478. - New building or three-year commitment to space within the city.
In determining whether to offer inducements, the city manager may consider whether the targeted business builds a new building or makes a lease commitment of at least three years for space within the city.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 8, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-479. - Targeted business receiving an inducement must not relocate.
Any targeted business which receives any inducement under the provisions of this article shall agree to not relocate out of the city for the entire period during which the inducement is granted. Should the targeted business relocate outside the city or fall below 80 percent of any threshold requirement, the targeted business shall immediately reimburse the city for the full value of all inducements it received.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 9, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 14-480. - City manager authorized to sign confidentiality agreements with businesses.
The city manager or his designee are authorized to sign confidentiality agreements with prospective businesses pursuant to this article, provided that those confidentiality agreements are made subject to the provisions of the Georgia Open Records Act.
(Ord. No. 2013-02-07, § 10, 3-5-2013)
