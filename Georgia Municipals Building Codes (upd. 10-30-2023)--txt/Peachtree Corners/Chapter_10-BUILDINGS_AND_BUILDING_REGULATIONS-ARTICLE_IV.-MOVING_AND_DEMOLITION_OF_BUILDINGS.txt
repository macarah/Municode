ARTICLE IV. - MOVING AND DEMOLITION OF BUILDINGS

Sec. 10-95. - Permit required to move building; fees; issuance by department of planning and development; applicant's performance surety; indemnification.
(a)
A person owning or leasing a building or a portion of a building to be moved shall not move nor authorize the moving of any house, dwelling, apartment building or other similar structure or any portion thereof from within the city to a point outside the city or from within the city to another point within the city, or from a point outside the city to a point within the city or from a point outside the city through the city to another point outside the city without first having obtained a permit as provided for herein. A moving permit shall not be required if the route of travel is located entirely on roads under the jurisdiction of the state department of transportation. A person shall not be issued a permit to move or authorize the moving of any house, dwelling, apartment building or other similar structure or any portion thereof in the city or into the city or from the city to a point outside the city or through the city until he has paid a moving permit fee, as follows:
(1)
For moving such structure or a portion thereof wholly within the city, a moving permit fee shall be paid to the county.
(2)
For moving such structure or any portion thereof from inside the city to a point outside the city and for moving such structure from outside the city to a point inside the city, a moving permit fee shall be paid to the county.
(3)
For moving a house or any portion thereof through the city, a moving permit fee shall be paid to the county.
(4)
The moving permit fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(b)
Where application for a permit to move a structure or a portion thereof, as defined in subsection (a) of this section, has been filed and the requisite moving permit fee paid, where written agreement regarding cleanup of the site has been signed and where all other applicable ordinances of the city have been complied with and all other permit fees paid, the director of the department of planning and development, or the director's duly authorized representative, shall be authorized to issue a moving permit to the applicant.
(c)
A permit to move a structure or any portion thereof from inside the city to a point inside or outside the city shall not be issued until the applicant has posted performance surety in the amount of $5,000.00 with the department of planning and development. The performance surety may be in the form of cash deposited with the county, or a bond, irrevocable letter of escrow or letter of credit from a bank or other financial institution in a form acceptable to the director of the department of planning and development or city attorney. The performance surety shall be released to the applicant after the structure has been moved, the applicant has placed the premises or location in the condition as set forth in section 10-100, and any damages to city property caused by the move have been repaired by the applicant. In the event the permit holder should fail to comply with these requirements, all or any portion of the performance surety shall be applied by the county to the cost of the county performing the work to the premises or location from where the structure was moved or performing the repairs to county and city property damaged as a result of the move. In the application for the moving permit, the applicant shall grant in writing unto the county, its agents or employees, the right to enter upon the premises to perform the work.
(d)
The application for a moving permit shall include information as to the route, date, and time of the move which shall be subject to the approval of the department of transportation.
(e)
The mover shall, in writing, indemnify the city and the county and hold the city and the county harmless from any and all damages which the city or the county may suffer and from any and all liability claims, including interest thereon, demands, attorney's fees, and costs of defense, or judgment against it, arising from damages caused by the move of the structure.

Sec. 10-96. - Permit to move building onto vacant land; application; fee; public hearing.
(a)
Whenever any person desires to move or authorize the moving of a house or other similar structure onto any vacant residential land in the city (other than land zoned RA-200), he shall file an application for merit therefor with the county and shall pay a fee in addition to any fees or bonds specified in section 10-95. Upon the filing of the application, the director of the department of planning and development, or the director's duly authorized representative, shall cause to be posted upon the land on which the structure is to be moved a sign of not less than four square feet which shall display thereon the fact that the application has been filed to move the structure onto the land, and that a public hearing will be held before the mayor and council at which hearing all parties interested in the matter would have the right to appear and be heard. The aforesaid sign shall be located at the property in close proximity to a public road or thoroughfare and so that same can be clearly seen by the public. The director of the department of planning and development shall further cause to be sent a letter by regular mail to all abutting property owners of record, as indicated by the county tax commissioners records, giving notice of the public hearing and containing the same information as required for the sign. Prior to the public hearing before the mayor and council, the city planning commission shall be provided the opportunity to review the application and shall forward a recommendation to the board of commissioners. The application fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(b)
Wherever any person desires to move or authorize the moving of any apartment, duplex or similar structure onto any vacant multifamily land (must be zoned RM) in the city, he shall pay the same fee and the same procedure will be followed as in subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
After a public hearing, as required in subsection (a) of this section, has been held and provided the applicant has complied with all applicable ordinances and has paid the additional fee specified in section 10-95 of this article, as may be required, the director of the department of planning and development, or the director's duly authorized representative, shall issue a moving permit to move the structure in accordance with the decision made by the mayor and council.
(d)
Whenever any person desires to move or authorize the moving of any commercial building, trade or business structure, industrial building, or similar structure onto any vacant appropriately zoned land within the city, he shall submit all plans and specifications as required for new construction projects as required by the city subdivision regulations. The applicant will also pay all fees applicable to new construction projects and the review of his plans and specifications will follow the same review procedure as a new construction project as specified in the construction and development regulations.
(e)
The owner and permit holder shall comply with any conditions or stipulations of approval placed upon the moving permit by the mayor and council or the department of planning and development.

Sec. 10-97. - Placement on vacant land without permit; submission of plans.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to move any house or other structure onto vacant land without a moving permit. A permit to move a house or other building onto vacant land in the city shall not be granted, nor shall a house or other structure be placed on vacant land in the city, until the person desiring a permit to move a house or other structure shall have submitted all required plans and specifications showing the proposed additions or changes to be made on the structure. Ordinances applicable to applying for permits to move houses or other buildings onto vacant land in the city and in improving houses and other buildings moved onto vacant land in the city shall be followed and all resultant fees paid.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to move any structure as defined in section 10-95 onto any land within the city (whether the move is to be temporary or permanent) unless a moving permit has been granted therefor as provided in this article. The applicant shall further comply with any conditions or stipulations of approval placed upon the moving permit by the mayor and council or the department of planning and development. Any violation of this section shall be a continuing violation and each day of violation shall constitute a new offense.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, ยง 14-88)
Sec. 10-98. - Demolition of structures.
(a)
Whenever any person desires to demolish any house, dwelling, apartment or other similar structure formerly used for human habitation or any commercial, industrial or other structure formerly used in the conduct of any trade or business, he shall, before being entitled to the issuance of a demolition permit, agree in writing that he shall, following the demolition of the structure, take all necessary action, including that specified in section 10-100, to ensure that the premises or location where the structure is demolished shall be left in a sanitary condition and free from all trash, debris and structures which might become a harboring place for insects, rodents or vermin.
(b)
A permit to demolish a structure shall not be issued until the applicant has posted a cash bond in the amount of $500.00 with the department of planning and development. The cash bond shall be returned to the applicant after the structure has been demolished and the applicant has placed the premises or location in the condition as set forth in this article. In the event the permit holder should fail to comply with this requirement, all or any portion of the cash bond shall be applied by the county to the cost of the city, the county, their agents or employees entering upon the premises or location in the condition as specified in this article. A permit or bond shall not be required for the demolition of barns, sheds or other accessory buildings where the structure was not used for human habitation or any commercial, industrial, trade or business purpose, or any building not having gas, water, electricity and other utilities connected.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to demolish any house, dwelling or other similar structure formerly used for human habitation or any commercial, industrial or other structure formerly used in the conduct of any trade or business without a permit. Any violation of this section shall be a continuing violation, and each day of violation shall constitute a new offense.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, ยง 14-89)
Sec. 10-99. - Applicant; written agreement required; contents.
Whenever any house, dwelling, apartment or other structure formerly used for human habitation, or whenever any commercial building or other structure formerly occupied in the conduct of any trade or business, shall be removed from a location within the city to another and different location either within or without the city or there is to be a demolition of the structure, the applicant for the permit to move such structure or demolish the structure shall, before being entitled to the issuance of a permit for removal or demolition, agree in writing that he will, following removal or demolition of the structure, take all possible action, including that specified in section 10-100, to ensure that the premises or location from which the structure is removed or demolished shall be left in a sanitary condition and free from all trash, debris and structures which might become a harboring place for insects, rodents or vermin. The applicant shall also be responsible for proper removal of all hazardous material in compliance with U.S. Environmental Protection Agency regulations contained in 40 CFR 61, Subparts A and M and 29 CFR 1910 and 1926. The applicant shall further be responsible for the proper disposal of solid waste containing asbestos in accordance with the Georgia Asbestos Safety Act and the Georgia Air Quality Act of 1978.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, ยง 14-90)
Sec. 10-100. - Duty to restore premises to safe and sanitary condition.
After the removal of the structure as set forth in section 10-99, the owner of the location or premises on which the structure was located shall be required to take necessary action to ensure that such location or premises shall be left in a safe and sanitary condition and shall be specifically required to do the following:
(1)
Remove all trash, debris, garbage and other similar material.
(2)
Fill all holes and depressions caused by the removal of any part of the structure, or caused as a result of compliance with this article, which might become a harboring place for insects, rodents or vermin.
(3)
Tear down and remove all underpinnings, pilasters, steps, plumbing connections and fixtures above ground level.
(4)
Seal, close-off, and make gastight and watertight all water, gas and sewer lines.
(5)
Fill or cover all wells with a concrete cap.

Sec. 10-101. - Time limits for premises restoration; failure to restore premises.
All persons to whom this article is applicable shall comply with the provisions of this article within 30 calendar days following the date of the removal of any structure specified in this article. If any such person shall fail to comply with the provisions of this article, the city, the county, their agents, employees, designees or contractors, are authorized to enter upon the premises and to perform the work necessary thereon to comply with this article. The work may be performed by city forces, county forces or by parties employed by the city or county and the cost of the work charged against the person failing or refusing to comply. Any person, firm or corporation applying for a permit as provided in this article grants to the city and the country an easement on the premises to perform the work.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, ยง 14-92)
Sec. 10-102. - Transporting of structures; when structure may be transported; insurance coverage; removal and replacement of roadway obstacles; removal of trees; law enforcement officer escort; notification of move.
(a)
Structures regulated in this article shall only be moved on Mondays through Thursdays between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 3:00 p.m., unless otherwise approved in writing by the department of transportation.
(b)
Any individual, firm or corporation transporting any house, dwelling, apartment or other structure formerly used for human habitation, or any commercial building or other structure formerly occupied in the conduct of any trade or business, across or along the public roads or highways of the city, shall be required to furnish proof that it has, in effect, public liability insurance in the amount of at least $500,000.00 combined single limit; to protect the property and persons who may be damaged as a result of the moving of the structure.
(c)
All mailboxes, highway signs, and other movable obstacles to the move of the structure and located in the street right-of-way shall be removed as the structure approaches such an obstacle and re-erected immediately after the structure passes such obstacles in equal or better condition than prior to removal.
(d)
Trees located in the street right-of-way shall not be removed or trimmed without the written permission of the department of transportation.
(e)
During the move, all structures shall be provided with an escort by a county law enforcement officer at the mover's expense.
(f)
For moving a structure or any portion of a structure for which a moving permit has been issued, the mover shall provide at least two calendar days notice to the department of police services prior to the date of moving.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, ยง 14-93)
Sec. 10-103. - Eminent domain proceedings; exemption from fees.
When an owner of a single-family dwelling who resides in the dwelling is effectively evicted by eminent domain proceedings or other governmental action, and desires to move his dwelling to another properly zoned location in the city where he will thereafter reside as their home, such owner shall be required to obtain a moving permit as specified in this article but shall not be required to pay the moving permit fee.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, ยง 14-94)
Sec. 10-104. - Time of completion of moved structure.
All structures for which a moving permit was required shall be completed within six months of approval of a moving permit with completion to include fine grading and landscaping of the property to which the structure was relocated. The director of the department of planning and development shall be authorized to grant one extension of completion time not to exceed 30 calendar days beyond the original six-month completion period. An application for such extension shall be made in writing and shall be made prior to expiration of the six-month completion time.
