ARTICLE XVI. - ENGINEERING PLANS OF RECORD REQUIREMENTS

Sec. A. - Requirements for the engineering plans of record part of the record documents.
The engineering plans of record part of the record documents shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
They shall conform with the requirements of the final engineering plan except where modified by this article.
(2)
They shall show all data, dimensions, plans, profiles, stationing, elevations, materials, etc., corrected to conform with actually constructed improvements.

ARTICLE XVII. - RECORD DOCUMENTS

Sec. A. - Recording plat.
When the record documents are approved, the mayor and council shall authorize the placing of an appropriate certification of such action on the copy of the plat to be recorded. The subdivider shall thereafter promptly have the approved plat of record recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Fulton/Coweta County. The subdivider shall then provide a copy of the recorded plat to the city administrator.
Sec. B. - Authorized building construction.
Until the final plat has been approved by the mayor and council:
(1)
No street or utility improvement shall be deemed to have been accepted or to have otherwise have been given legal status by the mayor and council.
(2)
No building or other structures within the subdivision shall be erected, moved, added to or structurally altered unless so permitted by the regulations.
Sec. C. - Maintenance bond or escrow required.
As a condition precedent to approval by the mayor and council of the record documents, the subdivider shall have provided a maintenance bond or escrow account guaranteeing maintenance and/or correction of defects in improvements of which the subdivider is notified during a one-year period following the date on which the mayor and council approves the final plat. The maintenance guarantee shall be in a form acceptable to the mayor and council and shall provide funds in an amount equal to ten (10) percent of the total estimated construction cost of all improvements.
If maintenance and/or correction of defects is not made within thirty (30) days after mailing written notice to the subdivider at his last known address, the city shall have the authority to make or cause to have made such maintenance and/or correction at the expense of the bonding company or escrow account. Provided, however, at the discretion of the mayor and council based upon (a) weather conditions, (b) labor market, (c) material market, or (d) circumstances beyond the control of the subdivider, the maintenance period may be extended for a definite period of time, sufficient to make the necessary corrections by an agreement in writing executed by the city, the subdivider and his surety. Provided, further, that the subdivider shall be responsible for any damages done to work already completed by him as part of required improvements.
