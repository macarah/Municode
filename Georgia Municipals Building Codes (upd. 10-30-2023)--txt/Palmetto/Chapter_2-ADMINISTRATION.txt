DIVISION 2. - CITY ADMINISTRATOR

Sec. 2-41. - Appointment.
The city administrator shall be appointed by the mayor with the advice and consent of the council, and shall serve at their pleasure.
(Ord. No. 88-02, § 2-3(a), 6-6-88)
Sec. 2-42. - Bond.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city administrator shall give a good and sufficient bond payable to the governing body; such bond to be fixed and approved by the governing body.
(Ord. No. 88-02, § 2-3(b), 6-6-88)
Sec. 2-43. - Duties of office.
The city administrator shall have the following duties:
(1)
To maintain a uniform system of accounts and keep such other records and accounts as may be required by this Code, statute or ordinance; and
(2)
To furnish the council with monthly statements detailing all receipts and payments of funds for the month; and
(3)
To prepare and submit to the city council prior to the beginning of each fiscal year a budget of proposed expenditures for the ensuing year, showing in as much detail as practicable the amounts allotted to each department of the city government and the reasons for such estimated expenditures; and
(4)
To keep a current inventory showing all city property, real and personal, and its location; and
(5)
To perform such other duties as may be prescribed by this Code, or required by ordinance, regulation or direction of the governing body.
(Ord. No. 88-02, § 2-3(c), 6-6-88)
Sec. 2-44. - Salary.
The salary for the position of city administrator shall be set from time to time by the governing body.
(Ord. No. 88-02, § 2-3(d), 6-6-88)

DIVISION 4. - COMPENSATION FOR ELECTED OFFICIALS

Sec. 2-53. - Generally.
The compensation for the mayor and councilmembers of the City of Palmetto shall be as follows:
(1)
Mayor. The mayor of the City of Palmetto shall be compensated for services rendered to the city. The amount of said compensation shall be three thousand dollars ($3,000.00) per year.
(2)
Mayor pro-tem. The mayor pro-tem of the City of Palmetto shall be compensated for services rendered to the City. The amount of said compensation shall be two thousand dollars ($2,000.00) per year.
(3)
Councilmembers. The councilmembers of the City of Palmetto shall be compensated for services rendered to the city. The amount of said compensation shall be one thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500.00) per year.
(Ord. No. 07-06, § 1, 7-9-07)

Sec. 2-61. - Bank accounts.
The city administrator, with the approval of the governing body shall maintain such bank accounts as, from time to time, are necessary or convenient for the transaction of city business. Each such account shall have as signatories thereon the mayor, mayor pro tem, city administrator, and a member of the finance committee as designated by resolution of the governing body. Any transaction upon such account shall require the signatures of at least two (2) of the above signatories.
(Code 1977, § 9-5; Ord. No. 87-1, 1-5-87)
Sec. 2-62. - Budgetary controls.
(a)
Adoption of budget. All budgets adopted by the city shall be adopted in accordance with and pursuant to O.C.G.A. Section 36-81-1 et seq.
(b)
Expenditures. No expenditures shall exceed the line item budget amount without an amendment to the budget. Department heads shall have the authority to transfer budget line items within their department, limited to five hundred dollars ($500.00) by submitting a budget amendment sheet to the city administrator. The city administrator shall have the authority for all budget transfers over five hundred dollars ($500.00) but less than five thousand dollars ($5,000.00). These transfers can be between funds which are from the contingency fund. Any budget transfer over five thousand dollars ($5,000.00) requires the approval of the mayor and council. All budget amendments shall be recorded on the budget amendment forms for the permanent record that contains the appropriate authorizing signatures.
(Ord. No. 90-02, 2-5-90)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 90-02, adopted Feb. 2, 1990, added § 2-3, which section has been redesignated by the editor as § 2-62 for purposes of classification.
