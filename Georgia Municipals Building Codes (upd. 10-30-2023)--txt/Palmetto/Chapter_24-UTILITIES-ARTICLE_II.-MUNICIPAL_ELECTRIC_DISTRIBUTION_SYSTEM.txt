ARTICLE II. - MUNICIPAL ELECTRIC DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM

Sec. 24-26. - Electrical rates and meter deposits.
The rates for electricity and the amount of meter deposit to be charged consumers shall be as prescribed by resolution of the governing body; a schedule is to be maintained in the office of the city administrator.
(Code 1977, § 17-3)
Sec. 24-27. - Unlawful restoration of service.
Whenever electrical service shall have been discontinued or cut off by the city, it shall be unlawful for any person, except an authorized agent or employee of the city, to restore the service.
(Code 1977, § 17-5)
State Law reference— Damaging, injuring or interfering with utilities, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25.
Sec. 24-28. - Tampering, interfering with meters, apparatus; unlawful use, diversion of service.
Whoever unlawfully and intentionally injures or destroys, or permits to be injured or destroyed, any meter, pipe, conduit, wire, line, post, lamp or other apparatus belonging to the city and a part of its system or works for the transformation or distribution of electricity for lighting or power purposes, or unlawfully and intentionally prevents an electric meter from duly registering the quantity of electricity supplied or in any way interferes with its proper or just registration, or, without the consent of the city, unlawfully and intentionally diverts any electric current from any wire of the city, or otherwise unlawfully and intentionally uses or causes to be used, without the consent of the city, any electricity transformed or distributed by it, shall be guilty of an offense.
(Code 1977, § 17-4(a))
State Law reference— Damaging, injuring or interfering with utilities, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25.
