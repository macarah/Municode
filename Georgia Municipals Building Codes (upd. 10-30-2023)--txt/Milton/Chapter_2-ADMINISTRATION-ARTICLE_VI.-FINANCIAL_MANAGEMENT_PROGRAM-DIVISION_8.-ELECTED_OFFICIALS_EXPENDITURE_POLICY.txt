Sec. 2-530. - Scope.
This division provides guidance on providing reimbursement/allowances on authorized expenditures for the mayor and city council. This division shall cover those costs incurred for conducting official business relating to elected positions.
(Ord. No. 06-11-05, 11-21-2006)
Sec. 2-531. - Appropriations.
In conjunction with the annual budget process, the city council shall authorize appropriations consistent with the annual adopted operating budgets.
(Ord. No. 06-11-05, 11-21-2006)
Sec. 2-532. - Reimbursement.
(a)
Direct reimbursement method.
(1)
Operating expenditures relating to elected officials shall be reimbursed using a direct reimbursement for expenses incurred on the city's behalf in the official capacity as an elected official.
(2)
The direct reimbursement based expenditures shall require receipts. As defined by the Internal Revenue Service, this type of transaction will be on an accountable reimbursement plan, which will not be taxed by the city. The primary means for paying for items under this method will be through the submission of receipts by elected officials to the city, thereby providing justification for the subsequent reimbursement.
(b)
Qualified expenditures.
(1)
All operating expenditures incurred by the official relating to the conducting of official business on behalf of the elected position shall qualify for reimbursement. Those expenditures relating to travel shall be covered in a separate policy using separately appropriated funds.
(2)
Types of qualified expenditures for the direct receipt reimbursement basis include:
a.
General office supplies;
b.
Customary mileage;
c.
Business meals;
d.
Professional memberships;
e.
Educational materials;
f.
Copying/printing; and
g.
Cellular charges.
This list is noninclusive and is provided as an example of types of qualified expenses.
(Ord. No. 06-11-05, 11-21-2006)
