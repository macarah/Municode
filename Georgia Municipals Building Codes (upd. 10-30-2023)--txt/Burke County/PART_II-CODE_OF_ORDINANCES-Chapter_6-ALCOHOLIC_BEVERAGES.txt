ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Distilled spirits or liquor means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Malt beverage and beer means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Retailer or retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes but is not limited to all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this Code section.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 6-2. - Sales on election days.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20(b)(2)(B), the sale by wholesale and retail of alcoholic beverages, to wit, distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages, shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; however, nothing in this section shall authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place during such time as the polls are open.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)

ARTICLE II. - LICENSING OF ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES

Sec. 6-36. - Sales; licenses; qualifications.
(a)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold in the unincorporated area of the county except at an established place of business that has been issued a license as provided in this article.
(b)
A license shall be granted only to natural persons who are either owners, shareholders, directors, officers or managers of the proposed business. Each license shall be valid only for one establishment at the address stated in the license. No license shall be granted to any person who is not at least 21 years of age, of good moral character, financially responsible, and who meets all other requirements of state and federal laws and regulations governing the sale of such beverages, and who has submitted proper application and complied with all provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-37. - Duration.
All alcoholic beverage licenses shall be issued for a period of one year at a time, effective from January 1 through December 31 of each year.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-38. - Classes; licenses fees.
(a)
Class A. A class A license shall authorize the sale of beer and wine in the broken package for consumption on the premises, only in restaurants, private clubs and golf courses. As used in this subsection, "restaurant" shall mean a business that serves hot food prepared on premises and which derives at least 60 percent of its total annual gross income from the sale of prepared meals. "Private club" shall mean a bona fide membership club affiliated with a nationally chartered organization which has at least 25 local regular dues-paying members and/or qualifies as a tax-exempt, nonprofit organization under the United States Internal Revenue Code. Such restaurants and clubs shall have a seating capacity of a least 20 people. "Golf courses" shall mean regular, standard golf courses of not less than nine holes. The annual fee for such license shall be $300.00.
(b)
Class B. A class B license shall authorize the sale of beer and wine only in the unbroken package for take-out and consumption off the premises. The annual fee for such license shall be $300.00.
(c)
Class C. A class C license shall authorize the sale of distilled spirits in the broken package for consumption on the premises, only in restaurants, private clubs and golf courses. As used in this subsection, "restaurant" shall mean a business that serves hot food prepared on premises and which derives at least 60 percent of its total annual gross income from the sale of prepared meals. "Private club" shall mean a bona fide membership club affiliated with a nationally chartered organization which has at least 25 local regular dues-paying members and/or qualifies as a tax-exempt, nonprofit organization under the United States Internal Revenue Code. Such restaurants and clubs shall have a seating capacity of a least 20 people. "Golf courses" shall mean regular, standard golf courses of not less than nine holes. The annual fee for such license shall be $800.00.
(d)
Separate licenses required. A separate license shall be required for each separate establishment regardless of ownership.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-39. - Applications; advertisement; fee.
(a)
All applications for licenses under this article shall be made in writing on forms to be furnished by the county administrator and shall contain such information as the administrator may require. The application shall disclose that the applicant has furnished the sheriff's department with the fingerprints of the applicant as required by state law.
(b)
Each such application shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable fee of $125.00, to be paid by certified check, cashier's check or money order. Upon receipt thereof and determination by him that the application is in proper form, the administrator shall cause a notice of the application to be published once a week for three weeks in the newspaper in which sheriff's advertisements for the county are published.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-40. - Contents of application.
(a)
All applications for licenses under this article shall be verified by or on behalf of the applicant and shall disclose, in addition to such other information as may be required:
(1)
The name, residence, location and mailing address of the applicant.
(2)
Whether the license is to be used in a business that is an individual proprietorship, partnership, corporation or trade name. If a corporation, the application shall include the names and addresses of all shareholders, directors, officers and real persons at interest. If a partnership, the application shall state the names and addresses of all partners therein. If a trade name or individual, the application shall also state the names and addresses of all owners or parties having an interest therein. In any event, the application shall state the names of all persons holding any note, mortgage, or bill of sale to secure debt against the business.
(3)
The location at which the establishment is to be conducted, and a description of the building in which the business is to be conducted, indicating the size and type of construction.
(4)
Whether the applicant or any partner, shareholder, director, officer, owner or real party in interest has been convicted during the last ten years of any crime, misdemeanor or a violation of any municipal ordinance (other than minor traffic offenses) in any state, county, municipal or federal court, and the particulars of same.
(5)
The prior business addresses of the applicant for the past ten years.
(6)
Names and addresses of three persons who have known the applicant (or the shareholders, directors, officers, agents thereof) for the past five years.
(7)
Whether the applicant or any shareholder, officer, director or other persons connected with the applicant holds or has held any beer, wine or liquor license in the state at any time prior to the application, and the particulars thereof.
(8)
The name and address of the person who will manage the establishment or be responsible for its operation.
(9)
A plat or drawing prepared by a licensed surveyor or engineer showing the proximity of the proposed place of business to any of the institutions or facilities set forth in section 6-47.
(10)
The certification required by section 6-62.
(11)
Such other information as the county administrator or other county authority may require, either initially, at any time during consideration of the application, or at any time thereafter.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-41. - Notice to sheriff.
The county administrator shall also furnish a copy of each application for a license under this article to the sheriff, who shall file his recommendation with the county commissioners' office within 15 days thereafter.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-42. - Objections.
Any person residing or doing business in the county may file objections in writing to the application for a license under this article. Such objections shall be filed not later than three days after publication of the last advertisement required under section 6-39. Such objections shall state:
(1)
The names and addresses of the objectors;
(2)
The interest of objectors and how they would be affected by the granting of the license;
(3)
The distance in miles from the objector's residence or place of business to the establishment of the proposed licensee;
(4)
The nature of the objection and the grounds thereof; and
(5)
Where objection is filed on behalf of more than three persons, the name and address of one person or representative of the group authorized to receive notices relating to the application and all hearings thereon.
It shall not be a valid objection to the issuance of a license that the objector is opposed generally to the sale of alcoholic or malt beverages.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-43. - Notice to applicant.
A copy of all objections filed as provided in section 6-42 shall be mailed to the applicant for a license under this article at the address stated in the application, by certified mail, return receipt requested. Both the objectors and the applicant shall be given notice of the time and place of the hearing on the application by certified mail, return receipt requested, deposited in the U.S. mail at least five days prior to the hearing on the license.
If the county administrator determines that the application for a license under this article is in proper form, that no valid objections have been filed thereto, that the sheriff has not recommended against grant of the license, and that no valid reason appears why the license should not be granted, he may issue the license without hearing upon receipt of the applicable fee, paid by certified check, cashier's check or money order.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-45. - Contested applications; denial.
No license under this article shall be denied except after opportunity for a hearing has been given. If the county administrator determines that valid objections have been filed to the application, or if the sheriff has recommended against the application, or if the administrator has reason to believe that grounds exist for denying the license, or if the administrator is uncertain as to whether the license should be granted, he shall schedule the matter for hearing before the commissioners and give notice of the time and place of the hearing. Such notice shall also be accompanied by a written statement of the grounds upon which the sheriff or county administrator opposes grant of the license; and if not already mailed as provided in section 6-43, a copy of all written objections filed to the application by any other person. Such notice shall be given within the same time and manner provided by section 6-43.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-46. - Hearing procedure.
At the hearing provided for in section 6-45, all witnesses shall be sworn and subject to cross examination. The strict rules of evidence prevailing in courts of law shall not apply. The commissioners may designate any member of the state bar as hearing officer to conduct the hearing either in or outside its presence. If outside their presence, the hearing officer shall make written findings of fact, conclusions of law and recommendations and submit them to the commissioners within ten days after conclusion of the hearing. A copy of the findings, conclusions and recommendations shall be mailed to the applicant or his representative by certified mail, return receipt requested; and he shall be entitled to file written exceptions thereto within five days after the date of such mailing. Upon failure to file such exceptions, the applicant consents to a decision on the application by the commissioners without further hearing. If the applicant files such exceptions within the required time, the matter shall be heard at the next regular or special meeting of the commissioners held not sooner than five days thereafter; and the applicant or his representative shall be notified of the hearing. At all hearings, the county authorities shall be entitled to open and conclude. No objections filed by any person shall be considered unless such person or a representative is present at the hearing and presents evidence in support of the objection. If the applicant fails to appear at any hearing on the application, the application shall be denied forthwith without hearing; and if the applicant fails to furnish any information or to produce any records reasonably required by county authorities, the application may be denied without consideration of the merits thereof. Except when prevented by providential cause or due to the fault of the applicant, all applications shall be finally determined within six months of filing thereof.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-47. - Proximity; schools; churches; playgrounds.
(a)
No license shall be issued under this article when the place of business of the licensee is located within 200 yards of any church, library, school, park or playground. As used herein, "park or playground" shall include, but is not limited to, parks or playgrounds owned or operated by the county or any municipality in the county.
(b)
All distances set forth in subsection (a) shall be measured by way of the nearest traveled road, street or highway. Measurement starts at the property line of the school, church, library, parks and playgrounds to the entrance of the building or proposed building where beverages are sold.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-48. - Standards.
In considering applications for licenses under this article, the commissioners shall be guided by the following:
(1)
The moral character, reputation and prior criminal record of the applicant or of any shareholder, director, officer, agent or employee thereof, or of any person having any interest in the proposed establishment.
(2)
The credit standing of the applicant and his reputation for payment of bills, debts and obligations.
(3)
Whether the location of the proposed establishment would present a hazard to pedestrian or vehicular traffic.
(4)
The existence and number of existing licenses within the immediate vicinity of the location of the proposed establishment.
(5)
The character of the community or neighborhood in which the establishment is to be located, whether residential, commercial or industrial.
(6)
Whether due to proximity or other reasons, the proposed establishment would present a danger that substantial numbers of minors would be brought into contact with the establishment or persons frequenting same.
(7)
Any valid objections made in writing by any person.
(8)
Whether the applicant has furnished false information in connection with the application.
(9)
Whether the proposed establishment has complied with all other state, federal and local laws or regulations, including but not limited to those promulgated by the state and local boards of health, and the state fire marshal.
(10)
The number of such establishments or licenses already owned or held by the applicant or any other person connected therewith.
(11)
If applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages, whether or not he has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
(12)
If applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages, the manner in which he conducted the business thereunder as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
(13)
Whether the applicant or anyone connected therewith has previously had an alcoholic beverages license suspended or revoked.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-2014)
Sec. 6-49. - Transfers.
No license granted under this article shall be transferred, but a new application for license must be filed.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-2014)
Sec. 6-50. - Renewal.
Licenses under this article are granted for one year at a time, and no person acquires any property interest or expectancy of continuation by reason of the grant of any license under this article. All applications for renewal of an existing license shall be filed in writing between November 1 and December 1 of each year and shall furnish such information as the tax commissioner may require and shall be accompanied by the license fee set forth in section 6-38. The renewal license shall be granted unless the tax commissioner or county manager has reason to believe that good cause exists for denying the renewal, in which event the applicant shall be given notice in writing setting forth the grounds upon which the license renewal may be denied. Such notice shall be mailed to the applicant by certified mail at least five days prior to the hearing to be held thereon, and shall give notice of the time and place of the hearing. In all other respects, the same procedures and requirements shall govern the granting or denial of the renewal license as set forth in sections 6-45, 6-46 and 6-48. Alcoholic beverage licenses expire on December 31st of each year.
In the event that a license is not renewed by the deadline listed above, a ten percent penalty will be assessed. All past due prior year license fees must be paid plus the assessed ten percent annual penalty.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-2014; Ord. No. 20-02 , § 1, 10-13-2020)
Sec. 6-51. - Sales regulated; illegal acts.
(a)
Licensees operating under a class A and/or class C license shall not engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises between the hours of 1:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. on weekdays, between the hours of 1:00 a.m. Sunday to 12:30 p.m. Sunday and Sundays from 12:00 midnight until 6:00 a.m. Monday.
(b)
Licensees operating under a class B license shall not engage in the sale of beer and wine between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. on weekdays, between the hours of 12:00 midnight Saturday night to 12:30 p.m. Sunday and Sundays from 12:00 midnight until 6:00 a.m. Monday.
(c)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold to any person who is obviously already under the influence thereof, or of any drugs.
(d)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold, bartered or given away to any minor, nor to any person who is mentally incompetent.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-2014)
Sec. 6-52. - Inspection.
All establishments operating under a license granted under this article shall be subject to inspection by the sheriff or other state or county authorities at any time, without prior notice, warrant or authority.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-2014)
Sec. 6-53. - Revocation.
Any license granted under this article may be revoked, after notice and opportunity for hearing, for:
(1)
Violation or conviction of any state or federal law or regulation, other than minor traffic and other minor offenses having no relationship to the applicant's fitness to sell alcoholic beverages.
(2)
Violation of any provision of this article or of any other state or county regulations.
(3)
Furnishing false information in connection with any license application or renewal application. The applicant shall be given notice in writing, stating the grounds for such revocation, and the time and place of the hearing thereon. Such notice shall be mailed to the licensee by certified mail, return receipt requested, at least five days prior to the hearing, or shall be served in person on the licensee at least three days prior thereto. In all other respects, the same procedures and provisions set forth in sections 6-45, 6-46 and 6-48 shall govern such revocation proceedings.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-2014)
Sec. 6-54. - Ineligibility.
It shall be unlawful for any elected or full-time appointed employee of the county, or his spouse or minor children, to hold any license under this article.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-55. - Duty to commence business.
All applications for licenses under this article must, within 180 days after the approval of the license, open for business the establishment referred to in the application and license, and begin the sale of the product or products authorized by the license. Failure to open the establishment and begin the sale as referred to above within the 180-day period shall serve as automatic forfeiture and cancellation of the unused license, and no refund of license fee shall be made to the license holder. Extensions of time under this section shall be granted only by the county commission.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-56. - Ceasing operation.
Any holder of a license under this article who shall begin the operation of the business and sale of the product and products as authorized in the license, but who shall for a period of three consecutive months thereafter cease to operate the business and sale of the product or products authorized in the license, shall, after the three-month period, automatically forfeit his license, which license shall, by virtue of failure to operate, be canceled without the necessity of any further action.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-57. - Display.
The county license required by this article shall at all times be kept plainly exposed to view at the place of business of the licensee.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-58. - Posting of prices.
Licensees under this article shall indicate plainly by tags or labels on the bottles or containers or on the shelf immediately below where the containers are placed, or on an exposed sign prominently placed, the price of all alcoholic beverages exposed or offered for sale.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-59. - Applications; data, information requirement.
All applicants for licenses under this article shall furnish all data, information and records requested of them by the county; and failure to furnish such data, information and records within 30 days from the date of such request shall be grounds for dismissal of the application. Applicants, by filing an application, agree to produce for oral interrogation any person involved in any transaction pertinent to the application requested by the county administrator. Failure to produce such evidence or person within 30 days after being requested to do so shall result in the dismissal of the application.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-60. - Order of consideration of applications.
All applications presented shall be noted as to the date and exact time of filing and such applications shall be presented, heard and considered by the board of commissioners in the order of their filing.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-61. - Regulations maintained on premises.
It shall be the duty of the management of the premises licensed under this article to maintain a copy of this chapter on such premises, and to instruct each and every employee on the terms of such chapter.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-62. - Regulations; certification.
No person shall be approved for a license pursuant to this article or approved for employment by a licensee, unless such person shall have read this chapter in full text, and certify in writing the fact of such reading and the knowledge of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-63. - Employee's identification card.
All employees of any establishment licensed pursuant to this article shall obtain an identification card as prescribed by the county sheriff's department, and must comply with all state and federal laws and regulations regarding alcoholic beverages. No employee shall be allowed to work for a licensee under this article without the identification card as prescribed by the sheriff's department, and no such identification card shall be issued to any person who has been convicted by any state or federal court of any felony or of any laws or regulations governing alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-64. - Building requirements.
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold under any license granted pursuant to this article in any mobile home or other temporary structure, in any dilapidated structure, nor in any building or other structure not safe for human habitation.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 6-65. - Waiver.
All notices and other procedural rights and provisions in this article provided may be waived, and shall be deemed to have been waived unless timely objection has been made thereto. No defect in any notice, statement of grounds, or of the proper mailing or service thereof shall be valid unless written objection pointing out the grounds thereof are filed with the office of the county commissioners in the courthouse at least two days prior to any scheduled hearing or action thereon, except where due to providential cause such objections could not be filed within that time, but in any event, such objections shall be filed at the earliest practicable time.
(Ord. No. 14-02 , § 1, 12-9-14)
