ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION, ENFORCEMENT, APPEAL, VIOLATIONS, AMENDMENTS

Sec. 2.01. - Administration and enforcement.
(a)
This ordinance shall be administered, interpreted, and enforced by the manager of development services under the direction of the planning director.
(b)
In any case in which activities are undertaken in violation of this ordinance, not in compliance with the provisions of a permit issued by the city, or without authorization of a permit that would otherwise be required, the manager of development services is authorized to order that all unauthorized or improper work be stopped, direct correction of deficiencies, or take any other legal or administrative action appropriate to the severity of the violation and degree of threat to the public health, safety and welfare.
(c)
It shall be the duty and responsibility of the manager of development services to maintain an accurate and up-to-date compilation of this ordinance, including appendices, and all amendments, and to publish such compilation and make it available to the public for a fee set by the city council.
(d)
The manager of development services shall have the power to conduct such investigations as he may reasonably deem necessary to carry out duties as prescribed in this ordinance, and for this purpose to enter at reasonable times upon any property, public or private, for the purpose of investigation and inspection of the sites for which permits have been issued.
(e)
No person shall refuse entry or access to any authorized representative or agent of the city, the conservation committee, the local soil and water conservation district, or the environmental protection division who requests entry for the purposes of inspection, and who presents appropriate credentials; nor shall any person obstruct, hamper or interfere with any such representative while in the process of carrying out his official duties.
Sec. 2.02. - Variance or appeal.
(a)
The manager of development services under the direction of the planning director may approve variances to the design and construction elements of this ordinance if the procedure and requirements herein are followed. The elements of design and construction that the manager of development services under the direction of the planning director may grant are as follows:
(1)
Design elements for streets;
(2)
Design standards for streets;
(3)
Sanitary sewer system;
(4)
Public water distribution system;
(5)
Driveway and right-of-way encroachment requirements;
(6)
Construction specifications; and
(7)
Construction details.
(b)
In exercising the authority, the manager of development services shall, upon a finding that the requirements of section 2.04(2)a through e, if applicable, have been met.
(c)
Any decision of the manager of development services may be appealed to the city council as outlined in this ordinance.
(d)
Requests for variance to the section of this ordinance other than those outlined in section 2.02(a) or an appeal of a decision of the manager of development services that is a result of the variance process outlined in section 2.02(a) shall be submitted to the planning commission for their review and recommendation to the city council. The city council shall grant or deny the variance or appeal.
(e)
Applications for variances or appeals shall be submitted with all required information along with any required fee to the planning and development department. Such submission shall be within 90 days of the questioned manager of development services decision. The manager of development services shall coordinate the review of each variance or appeal request by all other affected city departments and shall summarize such comments and/or recommendations as may be received for the planning commission's review and recommendation and city council for their review and action.
(f)
Upon consideration of any of the variances or appeals and the purposes of this ordinance, the city council may attach such conditions to the granting of the variances and appeals as they deem necessary to further the purpose of this ordinance and other ordinances and standards.
Sec. 2.03. - Variance or appeal information.
Each application for a variance or an appeal shall be filed, in writing, with the manager of development services; and the application shall contain at least the following information:
(1)
Identification of property concerned by street address or legal description;
(2)
A statement identifying the legal interest of the applicant;
(3)
A statement identifying the specific provision of this ordinance having the variance or appeal being sought;
(4)
A statement identifying the special conditions and circumstances that should qualify for a variance or appeal; and
(5)
A statement identifying any special conditions, methods, design procedures, and/or construction procedures being submitted as the proposed alternative to this ordinance.
Sec. 2.04. - Powers and duties.
The city council shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
To hear and decide variances or appeals where it is alleged there is error in any order, requirement, decision or determination made by the manager of development services in the enforcement of this ordinance.
(2)
To authorize in specific cases a variance or an appeal from the terms of this ordinance as will not be contrary to the public interest, where, owing to special conditions, a literal enforcement of the provisions of this ordinance will in an individual case result in unnecessary hardship, so that the spirit of this ordinance shall be observed, public safety and welfare secured, and substantial justice done. Such variance or appeal may be granted in such individual case of unnecessary hardship upon finding by the city council that:
a.
There are extraordinary and exceptional conditions pertaining to the particular piece of property in question because of its size, shape or topography;
b.
The application of this ordinance to this particular piece of property would create an unnecessary hardship;
c.
Such conditions are peculiar to the particular piece of property involved;
d.
Relief, if granted, would not cause substantial detriment to the public good or impair the purpose and intent of this ordinance; however, no variance or appeal may be granted for a use of land, building or structure that is prohibited in a given district by the zoning and subdivision regulations.
e.
Granting of the variance or appeal will achieve the same quality and integrity of construction as that standard contained in this ordinance; and
(3)
To decide on other matters where a decision of the city council may be specifically required by the provisions of this ordinance. In exercising these powers, the city council may reverse or affirm, wholly or in part, or may modify the order, requirements, decision or determination, and to that end shall have all the powers of the manager of development services and may issue or direct the issuance of a permit. The city council, in the execution of the duties for which appointed, may subpoena witnesses and in the case of contempt may certify such fact to the municipal court.
Sec. 2.05. - Certiorari from decisions.
Any person aggrieved by any decision of the city council shall have the right of certiorari to the superior court within 90 days after the decision of the city council is rendered.
Sec. 2.06. - Amendments.
(a)
Authorized. This ordinance, including appendices, may be amended by the city council as specified in this section.
(b)
Requirements for change. When the public necessity, convenience, general welfare or good development practice justifies such action, and after review and report of the planning commission, the city council may undertake the necessary steps to amend this ordinance.
(c)
Procedure for amendments. Requests to amend this ordinance shall be processed in accordance with the following requirements:
(1)
Initiation of amendments. A proposed amendment to this ordinance may be initiated by the city council, the planning commission, the planning director, the manager of development services or by application filed with the manager of development services by a developer or citizen.
(2)
Application procedure. Each request for amendment of this ordinance shall be submitted in a form as prescribed by the manager of development services along with such fee as shall be established by the city council. Applications for amendments must be submitted in proper form by the last Friday of the month prior to the month of the planning commission hearing the applicant wishes to attend in order to be heard at that meeting.
(3)
The application first shall be submitted to the planning commission for review and recommendation to the city council. For information, each member of the planning commission will be provided with a copy of each amendment. The planning commission shall conduct a hearing, consider all information and forward a recommendation on to the city council. If the planning commission fails to forward a recommendation it shall be deemed to have recommended approval on the proposed amendment.
(4)
After the application has been approved, denied or amended by the planning commission, the application shall be submitted to the city council for review and action. For information, each member of the city council will be provided with a copy of each amendment. The city council shall follow its rules for review and processing. The amendment shall only become part of this ordinance if the city council votes for approval.
