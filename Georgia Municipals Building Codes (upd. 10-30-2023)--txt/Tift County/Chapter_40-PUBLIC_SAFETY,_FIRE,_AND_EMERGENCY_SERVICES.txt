Chapter 40 - PUBLIC SAFETY, FIRE, AND EMERGENCY SERVICES
ARTICLE I. - DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY

Sec. 40-1. - Department of public safety created.
In agreement with the governing officials of the county there is hereby created a department of public safety. The department is charged with the management and administration of the office of emergency management, office of emergency communications, and the division of fire/rescue services.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-2. - Position of director of public safety created; duties.
There is established the position of director of public safety. The director of public safety shall act as the county's emergency management director, emergency communications director, and fire suppression and prevention director.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-3. - Continuity of government and operations.
(a)
The county manager shall identify the specific order of succession among the county organization to ensure continuity of leadership, organization, and essential services during emergency circumstances. The county manager shall advise the board whenever any changes are made to the order of succession. Each county division and department shall also establish an internal chain of succession. Divisions and departments charged with keeping public records shall identify essential records needed for the operation of government during a disaster or other emergency. Public records that are vulnerable shall be protected by appropriate measures, to include storage locations. Designated divisions and departments are responsible for the development of respective plans, procedures, guidelines, protocols that support the local emergency operations plan. All divisions and departments shall participate in continuity of government and operations activities to ensure continuation of essential functions during an emergency or service disruption.
(b)
County division/department directors will appoint emergency management liaisons to manage and coordinate their functional responsibilities and assist in the implementation of the local emergency operations plan.
(c)
County division/department directors shall also ensure support and recovery team members, including emergency operations center staff, are adequately identified, trained, and equipped to provide 24-hour coverage when required. In a declared state of local emergency, employees shall act in accordance with responsibilities outlined in the local emergency operations plan, or as directed by the county manager.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-4. - Order of succession, department of public safety.
In the absence or incapacitation of the director of public safety, the chief of fire/rescue services shall direct the activities of the department of public safety.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Secs. 40-5—40-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT

Sec. 40-21. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person violating any provision of this article, or any rule, order, or regulation made pursuant to this article, shall upon conviction, be punishable for committing a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-22. - Emergency management director, duties.
In agreement with the governing officials of the county, there is hereby established the office of emergency management within the department of public safety with the director of public safety appointed as emergency management director. The emergency management director is charged with the following duties:
(a)
Represent the governing officials of the county and cities therein on matters pertaining to emergency management.
(b)
Assist county and city officials in organizing county and city departments for emergency operations.
(c)
Develop, in conjunction with county and city departments the county plan for emergency functions set forth in section 42-36. Such plan will be in consonance with the state natural disaster operations plan and nuclear emergency operations plan, and shall be submitted to the governing officials of the county and cities therein for approval.
(d)
Maintain the office of emergency management and carry out the day-to-day administration of the county emergency management program, including the submission of required reports to the state emergency management agency.
(e)
Submit reports as required by governing officials in keeping with good management practices, e.g., financial, daily activity, etc.
(f)
Obtain, with the authority of governing officials, a facility to be used as the county emergency operating center.
(g)
Coordinate the activities of the county emergency operating center staff during periods of an emergency, and under the supervision of county governing officials.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)

Sec. 40-24. - Powers during an emergency or disaster.
In the event of manmade or natural disaster, actual enemy attack upon the United States, or any other emergency which may affect the lives and property of the citizens of the county, the chairman of the board of commissioners, jointly with the mayors of the affected cities, or in their absences their legally appointed successors, may determine that an emergency or disaster exists and thereafter shall have and may exercise for such period as such emergency exists or continues, the following powers:
(a)
Enforce all rules, laws and regulations relating to emergency management, and to assume direct operational control over all emergency management resources.
(b)
Seize or take for temporary use, any private property for the protection of the public.
(c)
Sell, lend, give, or distribute all or any such property or supplies among the inhabitants of the county and to maintain a strict accounting of property or supplies distributed and for funds received for such property or supplies.
(d)
Perform and exercise such other functions and duties, and take such emergency actions as may be necessary to promote and secure the safety, protection and well-being of the inhabitants of the county.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-25. - Volunteers.
All persons, other than officers and employees of the county and cities therein, performing emergency functions pursuant to this article, shall serve with or without compensation. While engaged in such emergency functions, duly assigned volunteers shall have the same immunities as county and city officers and employees.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-26. - National incident management system.
The national incident management system of March 1, 2004, developed by the United States Department of Homeland Security is hereby adopted by reference, as amended.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)

Sec. 40-35. - Establishment and purpose of article.
The board of commissioners, pursuant to the authorization of O.C.G.A. § 46-5-133, adopts this article authorizing the creation of an emergency telephone systems to construct, expand, acquire, extend, improve, equip, operate, and maintain an enhanced emergency telephone 911 system to support the public safety agencies in the more efficient and timely provision of life saving services, to reduce the destruction of property, to aid in quicker apprehension of criminals and to coordinate communications with public safety agencies with the ultimate goal of public economy.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-36. - Definitions.
All terms set forth in this article shall be construed according to the definitions as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 46-5-122.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-37. - Funding.
(a)
The subscriber of an exchange access facility shall be billed for a monthly 911 charge by the service supplier in the amount as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges. This charge shall be effective for telephone subscriber bills issued after October 20, 1993, and shall remain in effect until amended by a resolution of the board of commissioners. All exchange access facilities billed to federal, state, or local governments shall be exempt from this charge. Each service supplier shall collect the 911 charge on behalf of the board of commissioners as part of its normal monthly billing process and shall list the 911 charge as a separate entry on each bill.
(b)
Every telephone subscriber in the service area shall be liable for the 911 charge imposed under this section until it has been paid to the service supplier. The service supplier shall provide the board of commissioners within 60 days with a list of the names and addresses of each subscriber who has refused to pay the 911 charge; and a collection action may be initiated by the board of commissioners for the charges imposed, together with reasonable costs and attorneys' fees associated with that collection action.
(c)
The county shall be ultimately responsible to any service supplier for all emergency 911 system installation, service, equipment operation and maintenance charges. Any taxes due on emergency 911 system service provided by the service supplier will be billed to the county; and state and local taxes do not apply to the 911 charge billed to telephone subscribers.
(d)
A service supplier may retain an administrative fee equal to three percent of the gross 911 charge receipts remitted to the county. The remaining amount shall be due quarterly to the board of commissioners, and shall be remitted no later than 60 days after the close of a calendar quarter. The 911 charges shall be deposited and accounted for in a separate restricted revenue fund known as the emergency telephone system fund maintained by and on behalf of the county.
(e)
Money from the emergency telephone system fund shall be used only to pay for:
(1)
The lease, purchase and maintenance of emergency telephone equipment, including necessary computer hardware, software, and database provisioning, addressing and nonrecurring costs of establishing a 911 system.
(2)
The rates associated with the service supplier's 911 service and other service suppliers recurring charges.
(3)
The actual costs of salaries of employees hired by the county solely for the operation and maintenance of the emergency 911 system.
(4)
Office supplies of the public safety answering points used directly in providing emergency 911 system services.
(5)
The cost of leasing or purchasing a building used as a public safety answering point, provided the street addressing plan is complete in the service area.
(6)
Payment pursuant to a contract with the service supplier for an emergency 911 system.
(f)
All service suppliers shall maintain records of the amount of the 911 charge collected for a period of at least three years from the date of the collection.
(g)
The board of commissioners shall, be resolution, reaffirm the necessity for the 911 charge beginning with the thirteenth month following the month in which the emergency 911 system service is first provided and during such month annually thereafter.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-38. - Advisory board; creation; composition; functions; compensation.
(a)
An advisory board is hereby created which shall consist of the sheriff, representatives from other public safety agencies which respond to emergency calls under the system, and other individuals knowledgeable of emergency 911 systems and emergency needs of the citizens of the county. Such advisory board shall not exceed 13 members.
(b)
The advisory board shall assist the board of commissioners in:
(1)
Reviewing and analyzing the performance of public safety agencies and developing 911 system requirements.
(2)
Recommending steps of action to effect the necessary coordination, regulation, and development of the 911 system.
(3)
Identifying mutual aid requirements necessary to affect the 911 system.
(4)
Assisting in the promulgation of necessary rules, regulations, operating procedures, schedules, and other policy and administrative devices as shall be deemed necessary and appropriate.
(5)
Providing other services as may be deemed appropriate by the local government.
(c)
The members of the advisory board shall not be compensated from monies deposited into the emergency telephone system fund.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-39. - Liability and exemption.
(a)
A service supplier, including any telephone company and its employees, directors, officers, and agents, is not liable for any damages in a civil action for injuries, death or loss to persons or property incurred by any person as a result of any act or omission of a service supplier or any of its employees, directors, officers, or agents, except for willful or wanton misconduct, either in connection with developing, adopting, implementing, maintaining or operating any emergency 911 system or in the identification of the telephone number, address or name associated with any person accessing an emergency 911 system.
(b)
Neither the county, nor any of its employees, directors, officers, or agents, except in cases of wanton or willful misconduct or bad faith, shall not be liable for death or injury to the person or for damage to property as a result of developing, adopting, establishing, participating in, implementing, maintaining, or carrying out duties involved in operating the 911 emergency telephone system or in the identification of the telephone number, address, or name associated with any person accessing an emergency 911 system.
(c)
The county shall not be required to release, indemnify, defend, or hold harmless any emergency 911 system provided from any loss, claim, damage, or other action or any liability whatsoever which arises out of subsection (b) of this section unless the county has agreed to assume such obligation.
(d)
All provisions of this article shall be construed so as to consistent with, and shall further be supplemented by, all provisions of O.C.G.A § 46-5-120 et seq., to which reference is hereby made and provisions of which are hereby incorporated by reference.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-40. - Staff training and quality assurance.
(a)
All communications personnel shall receive training and certification in accordance with National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) and Association of Public Safety Communication Officials (APCO) guidelines.
(b)
The department will establish and maintain a quality assurance program in accordance with NFPA and APCO guidelines.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-41. - Emergency dispatch protocols.
(a)
The department will adopt fire, medical, and law enforcement dispatching protocols that meet the standards established by NFPA and APCO.
(b)
The director of public safety shall update the protocols as required.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)

ARTICLE IV. - DIVISION OF FIRE/RESCUE SERVICES

Sec. 40-50. - Division of fire/rescue services created.
(a)
In agreement with the governing officials of the county there is hereby created a division of fire/rescue services under the county department of public safety.
(b)
The division of fire/rescue services shall be staffed with the state mandated minimum number of volunteer suppression or state certified firefighters to provide and/or perform the following functions within the geographic limits of the county 24 hours a day, 365 days per year:
(1)
Protection of life and property against fire;
(2)
Protection of life and property for homeland security against weapons of mass destruction;
(3)
Provision of first responder and basic life support against medical emergencies;
(4)
Provision of first responder services to motor vehicle accidents against disentanglement and emergency medical needs;
(5)
Provision of first responder services to emergencies involving hazardous materials, high angle rescues, tunnel rescues, trench rescues, water rescues, and building collapse;
(6)
Protection of life and property against emergencies at the airport;
(7)
Administration and enforcement of appropriate statutes, ordinances, and regulations related to fires and explosions;
(8)
Regulation of the installation and use of any equipment that prevents a hazard as to fires or explosions, including fire escapes, emergency exits, fire alarm systems, and fire extinguishing equipment that may be installed in or outside any vehicle, structure, building, grounds, or underground passageway;
(9)
Initiation and conduct of programs of public education in fire prevention and safety.
(10)
Detect and prevent arson.
(c)
The county shall purchase and maintain insurance coverage in a sufficient amount for coverage on each member of the division of fire/rescue services to pay claims for injuries sustained enroute to, during, and returning from emergency calls, disasters, scheduled training sessions, and other department-required activities. Upon cancellation or reinstatement of any policy for the coverage of each member or the department, the executive director of the state firefighter standards and training council shall be notified immediately.
(d)
The department will adhere here to all rules and regulations promulgated by the state firefighter standards and training council.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-51. - Position of chief of fire/rescue services created; duties.
There is established the position of chief of fire/rescue services. The chief of fire/rescue services shall act as the county's representative for the provision fire prevention, fire suppression, and rescue operations within the territorial limits of the county and, as such, is directly responsible for the direction and administration of the department. The chief of fire/rescue service may, with the approval of the county manager and the director of public safety may appoint a deputy chief of fire/rescue services.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-52. - Rules and regulations authorized for fire department.
Under the direction of the director of public safety, the chief of fire/rescue services shall maintain written rules and regulations for the operation of the department as are necessary.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-53. - Hiring and professional qualification requirements.
(a)
All applicants for open firefighter positions shall meet the following criteria:
(1)
Be at least 18 years of age.
(2)
Have a valid Georgia driver's license.
(3)
Have a high school diploma or general educational development diploma.
(4)
Be in good physical condition and successfully complete a departmental physical exam.
(5)
Complete, within one year, an approved Georgia firefighter I course.
(6)
Have not been convicted of a felony within the past ten years.
(7)
Have good moral character as determined by a background investigation.
(8)
Successfully complete a departmental physical ability test.
(b)
All driver/operators shall be certified in accordance with NFPA guidelines.
(c)
All fire officers shall be certified in accordance with NFPA guidelines.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-54. - Probationary period for fire recruits.
Any person selected as a fire recruit shall be duly notified and, upon accepting and reporting for duty, shall be appointed for a probationary period that shall commence upon appointment and continue for 12 months.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-55. - Uniforms and equipment.
The chief of fire/rescue services shall issue and maintain uniforms and equipment in accordance with NFPA guidelines. All members of the department shall maintain a professional appearance and adhere to uniform guidelines.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-56. - Unauthorized use of uniforms and department name/logos.
It shall be unlawful for any person, without authority, to wear the uniform or any distinctive part thereof or anything similar to a distinctive part thereof, which has been designed and approved by the chief of fire/rescue services.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-57. - Authority at fires.
(a)
The chief of fire/rescue services or fire officers under his direction shall have full control over all fire apparatus of the department in service at any fire. It shall be the duty of the chief of fire/rescue services or his designee to superintend the department while performing any public duty in fighting a fire or performing rescue operations and to give general and specific directions as to the manner of fighting fires, the use of hose and apparatus, the use of rescue equipment, and the specific duties and assignments of the various members of the department in attendance at a fire. The chief of fire/rescue services may immediately suspend any member of the department for insubordination at any fire or rescue scene.
(b)
The chief of fire/rescue services or his designee shall, in all cases of fire, fire alarms, and rescue operations, have full control and occupy sufficient ground or space necessary for the operation of all fire companies at any scenes, and for the protection of life and property, and shall be protected in the discharge of their duties by the law enforcement officers of the county.
(c)
Officers and members of the department, when at the scene of a fire or other emergency scene, may direct or assist the county law enforcement officers in directing traffic in the immediate vicinity.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-58. - Authority to cut wires.
The chief of fire/rescue services or other member of the department is authorized to cut any wires, electric or otherwise, which shall be deemed necessary to preserve life or property without incurring any liability on the part of the county therefor.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-59. - Records of fires.
The chief of fire/rescue services or his designee shall keep a permanent record of the cause, origin and circumstances of every fire and the damages resulting therefrom immediately after the occurrence of any fire.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-60. - Obstruction of or tampering with fire hydrants and apparatus prohibited.
(a)
No person shall place ashes, cinders, dirt rubbish, building material, or any other material around or in close proximity to any fire hydrant so as to cause a hindrance or delay in access thereto, or prevent the free use thereof by the department. No person shall, in any way, interfere with or tamper with any fire hydrant or attempt to take water therefrom without special authority form the chief of fire/rescue services or his designee.
(b)
Without the consent of the chief of fire/rescue services, no person not an active member of the department shall at any time ride upon any of the fire apparatus of the department, nor shall any person make use of any fire apparatus, hose, or other equipment of the department, other than for the purpose for which the equipment was intended, without the consent of the chief of fire/rescue services.
(c)
No person, without the consent of the chief of fire/rescue services, shall, at any time, make alteration to or add/delete equipment from any fire apparatus assigned to the department.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Secs. 40-61—40-69. - Reserved.
Sec. 40-70. - Certain acts interfering with firefighting activities.
No person, except an active member of the department, shall at any time enter within the territory or vicinity of any fire or rescue scene, when the area is roped off or access is denied by county law enforcement or fire officers, or interfere with or attempt to operate any of the apparatus or equipment of the department or any fire hydrant, or interfere by giving orders to any individual, unless authorized to do so by the chief of fire/rescue services or fire officers under his direction; however, a county commissioner, the county manager, any law enforcement officer, the owner or occupant of the property, and any other persons as may be specifically authorized by the chief of fire/rescue services or his designee, may enter restricted areas.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-71. - Obstruction of fire officers prohibited.
No person shall, in any way, obstruct or prevent the director of public safety, chief of fire/rescue services, or fire marshal in the discharge of his duties, by denying or attempting to deny access to any premises owned or occupied by the person, or by failing or refusing to furnish correct information requested by the director of public safety, chief of fire/rescue, or fire marshal in the investigation into the cause, origin or circumstances of any fire.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-72. - Fire drills.
The chief of fire/rescue services or authorized representative shall have the authority to conduct fire drills in all public buildings located within the county. Public buildings shall include but not be limited to all buildings owned by the county and any privately owned structure if the owners or occupants thereof request this service of the chief of fire/rescue services.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-73. - Fire and rescue calls outside county limits.
The department shall answer all calls upon authorization of the chief of fire/rescue services outside the county limits.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-74. - Parking in fire lane prohibited.
No person shall, at any time, park, stand, or stop any motor vehicle or place any other property in a fire lane, so designated by signs and/or painted curbing or other markings indicating a fire lane; however, this section shall not apply to the parking of an authorized emergency vehicle.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Secs. 40-75—40-80. - Reserved.
Sec. 40-81. - County not liable for enforcement pertaining to maintenance and clearing of fire lanes.
(a)
The county assumes no liability for any damage, injuries, or deaths from enforcement, or lack of enforcement, of the laws pertaining to maintenance and clearing of the fire lanes. The county has the authority to issue individual citations for fire lane violations, but the property owner has the ultimate responsibility to clear fire lanes.
(b)
Any vehicle obstructing a fire lane in violation of this section may be removed at the direction of the chief of fire/rescue services or his designee, or a law enforcement officer, and the costs of removal and storage shall be borne by the owner of such vehicle and paid for by him or her prior to the release of such vehicle.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-82. - Enforcement.
The provisions of this article shall be enforceable by the director of public safety, chief of fire/rescue services or his designee, or any law enforcement officer authorized to enforce the ordinance of the county.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Sec. 40-83. - Penalty.
A fine of not less than $100.00 shall be imposed upon any person who violates the provisions of section 40-74.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
Secs. 40-84—40-89. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - ADVANCED LIFE SUPPORT AMBULANCES

Sec. 40-90. - Advance life support for ambulance service.
All licensed ambulances transporting ambulance patients from origins within the county shall be staffed and equipped to provide advanced life support as defined in the rules of the department of human resources and public health.
(Ord. No. 2015-02, 12-14-15)
