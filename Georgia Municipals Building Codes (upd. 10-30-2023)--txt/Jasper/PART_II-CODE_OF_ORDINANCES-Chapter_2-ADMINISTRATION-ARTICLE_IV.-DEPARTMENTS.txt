ARTICLE IV. - DEPARTMENTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-179. - Departmental organization.
(a)
The administrative service of the city shall be divided into the following departments with the following department head positions:
(1)
Finance department, finance director.
(2)
Water and wastewater department, water and sewerage superintendent.
(3)
Street maintenance department, street superintendent.
(4)
Police department, police chief.
(5)
Fire department, fire chief.
(6)
Building and licensing department, building official.
(b)
Certain departments are further described in this chapter. The police department is further described in chapter 54, pertaining to law enforcement. The fire department is further described in chapter 42, pertaining to fire prevention and protection. Finally, a description of each department's composition, the method of appointment and the duties of the head of each department shall be found by department title in the list of job descriptions on file in the city clerk's office.
(Code 1979, § 3-601)
Sec. 2-180. - Department heads generally.
All department heads shall:
(1)
Be immediately responsible to the mayor for the effective administration of their respective department and all activities assigned thereto;
(2)
Keep informed as to the latest practices in their particular field and inaugurate, with the approval of the mayor, such new practices as appear to be of benefit to the service and to the public;
(3)
Submit annual reports of the activities of their respective department to the mayor;
(4)
Establish and maintain a system of filing and indexing records and reports in sufficient detail to furnish all information necessary for proper control of departmental activities and to form a basis for the periodic reports to the mayor;
(5)
Have power, when authorized by the mayor, to appoint and remove, subject to personnel regulations, all subordinates under him; and
(6)
Be responsible for the proper maintenance of all city property and equipment used in their respective departments.
(Code 1979, § 3-602)
Sec. 2-181. - Intradepartmental cooperation.
Each department shall cooperate with every other department and shall furnish, upon the direction of the mayor, any other department such service, labor, and materials as may be requisitioned by the head of each department, as its own facilities permit.
(Code 1979, § 3-602)

DIVISION 2. - BUILDING AND LICENSING DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-201. - Established.
The city has established a department to be called the building and licensing department and the person in charge shall be known as the building official. The qualifications for the building official and other code enforcement personnel shall be as established by the city.
(Code 1979, § 42-1.4)
Sec. 2-202. - Restrictions on employees.
An officer or employee connected with the department, except one whose only connection is as a member of the board established by this Code, shall not be financially interested in the furnishing of labor, material, or appliances for the construction, alteration, or maintenance of a building, structure, service, system, or in the making of plans or of specifications thereof, unless he is the owner of such. This officer or employee shall not engage in any other work which is inconsistent with his duties or conflicts with the interests of the department.
(Code 1979, § 42-1.4.1)
Sec. 2-203. - Records.
The building official shall keep, or cause to be kept, a record of the business of the department. The records of the department shall be open to public inspection.
(Code 1979, § 42-1.4.2)
Sec. 2-204. - Liability.
Any officer or employee, or member of the construction board of adjustment and appeals, charged with the enforcement of this Code, acting for the applicable governing authority in the discharge of his duties, shall not thereby render himself personally liable, and is hereby relieved from all personal liability, for any damage that may occur to persons or property as a result of any act required or permitted in the discharge of his duties. Any suit brought against any officer or employee or member because of such act performed by him in the enforcement of any provision of this Code shall be defended by the city attorney until the final termination of the proceedings.
(Code 1979, § 42-1.4.3)
Sec. 2-205. - Reports.
The building official shall submit annually a report covering the work of the building and licensing department during the preceding year. He may incorporate in said report a summary of the decisions of the board of adjustment and appeals during said year.
(Code 1979, § 42-1.4.4)
Sec. 2-206. - General authority of building official.
The building official is hereby authorized and directed to enforce the provisions of the technical codes. The building official is further authorized to render interpretations of the technical codes, which are consistent with its intent and purpose.
(Code 1979, § 42-2.1)
Sec. 2-207. - Right of entry; inspections.
(a)
Whenever necessary to make an inspection to enforce any of the provisions of this Code, or whenever the building official has reasonable cause to believe that there exists in any building or upon any premises any condition or code violation which makes such building, structure, premises, electrical, gas, mechanical or plumbing systems unsafe, dangerous or hazardous, the building official or his designee may enter such building, structure or premises at all reasonable times to inspect the same or to perform any duty imposed upon the building official by these technical codes, provided that if such building or premises is occupied, he shall first present proper credentials and request entry.
(b)
If the building, structure, or premises is unoccupied, he shall first make a reasonable effort to locate the owner or other persons having charge or control of such and request entry. If entry is refused, the building official shall have recourse to every remedy provided by law to secure entry.
(Code 1979, § 42-2.2.1)
Sec. 2-208. - Inspection warrants.
When the building official shall have first obtained a proper inspection warrant or other remedy provided by law to secure entry, no owner or occupant or any other persons having charge, care or control of any building, structure, or premises shall fail or neglect, after proper request is made as herein provided, to promptly permit entry therein by the building official for the purpose of inspection and examination pursuant to this Code.
(Code 1979, § 42-2.2.2)
Sec. 2-209. - Stop work orders.
Upon notice from the building official, work on any building, structure, electrical, gas, mechanical or plumbing system that is being done contrary to the provisions of the technical codes or in a dangerous or unsafe manner, shall immediately cease. The notice shall be in writing and shall be given to the owner of the property, or to his agent, or to the person doing the work, and shall state the conditions under which work may be resumed. Where an emergency exists, the building official shall not be required to give a written notice prior to stopping the work.
(Code 1979, § 42-2.3)
Sec. 2-210. - Authority of building official to revoke permits.
(a)
The building official may revoke a permit or approval, issued under the provisions of this Code, in case there has been any false statement or misrepresentation as to the material fact in the application or plans on which the permit or approval was based.
(b)
The building official may revoke a permit upon determination by the building official that the construction, erection, alteration, repair, moving, demolition, installation or replacement of the building, structure, electrical, gas, mechanical or plumbing systems for which the permit was issued is in violation of, or not in conformity with, the provisions of this Code.
(Code 1979, §§ 42-2.4.1, 42-2.4.2)
Secs. 2-211—2-228. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DEPARTMENT OF FINANCE

Sec. 2-229. - Composition.
The department of finance shall consist of the city clerk or the director of finance and such other officers and employees as may be provided for by the mayor and council.
(Code 1979, § 3-603)
Sec. 2-230. - Appointment of director.
The director of finance shall be appointed by the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of city council, and shall exercise general supervision over all officers of the city regarding the proper management of the fiscal concerns of their respective offices.
(Code 1979, § 3-603)
Sec. 2-231. - Duties of director.
The director of finance shall have the following duties:
(1)
To prepare and keep all financial records of the city;
(2)
To make all purchases of materials, supplies, or equipment for the city that are authorized by the council, in the manner provided by law and subject to the limitations imposed by law;
(3)
To keep a current account of the amount appropriated each year and the sums spent out of each appropriation showing the unexpended balance for each item;
(4)
To keep in proper books a full and accurate account of all the moneys received and disbursed by him in behalf of the city, specifying the date of receipt and disbursement, from when received and to whom disbursed, and on what account received and disbursed, and how paid;
(5)
To prepare and keep for the city treasurer all bond registers and other records required by law to be kept by the treasurer, and to prepare for signature and publication the annual treasurer's report and any other report required by law;
(6)
To receive and have custody of all moneys paid to the city and to disburse city moneys as provided by law;
(7)
To receive and collect all license fees, permit fees, charges for the use of city property or special services rendered by the city, sums due to the city on any contract, and all other sums due the city other than those which are by law paid directly to another municipal officer; and
(8)
To perform any and all such additional duties as may be assignee to him from time to time by the mayor or council.
(Code 1979, § 3-603)
Secs. 2-232—2-255. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - WATER AND WASTEWATER DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-256. - Composition.
The water and wastewater department shall consist of the water and sewerage superintendent and all officers and employees assigned thereto by the mayor or council.
(Code 1979, § 3-604)
Sec. 2-257. - Appointment of superintendent.
The water and sewerage superintendent shall be appointed by the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of the city council, and shall have control and supervision over all officers and employees assigned to the department, subject to the control of the mayor.
(Code 1979, § 3-604)
Sec. 2-258. - Duties of superintendent.
Duties of the water and sewerage superintendent shall be:
(1)
To have charge of and be responsible for the care, maintenance, and operation of the city water distribution system, the sanitary sewer system and disposal plant.
(2)
To perform such additional duties as may be assigned to him from time to time by the mayor.
(Code 1979, § 3-604)
Secs. 2-259—2-279. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - STREET MAINTENANCE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-280. - Composition.
The street maintenance department shall consist of the street superintendent, who shall be the head of the department, and all officers and employees assigned thereto by the mayor or council.
(Code 1979, § 3-605)
Sec. 2-281. - Appointment of superintendent.
The street superintendent shall be appointed by the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of city council, and shall have control and supervision over all officers and employees assigned to the department, subject to the control of the mayor.
(Code 1979, § 3-605)
Sec. 2-282. - Duties of superintendent.
The street superintendent shall have the following duties:
(1)
To provide for and supervise the cars, maintenance, construction, and extension of all streets, sidewalks, street gutters and drains, alleys, and public ways; and the street lighting system;
(2)
To have charge of and supervision over all public property of the city, including all parks, parkways, playgrounds, municipal cemeteries, buildings, and utilities not assigned to some other department or officer;
(3)
To have charge of the collection and disposal of garbage and refuse;
(4)
To have charge of and be responsible for the condition of all motor vehicles and other equipment of the city, and of all buildings or places in which the same are housed or kept;
(5)
To be the custodian of all tools, equipment, and other personal property belonging to the city and not assigned to the care of another public officer of the city; and
(6)
To perform such additional duties as may be assigned to him from time to time by the mayor.
(Code 1979, § 3-605)
Secs. 2-283—2-312. - Reserved.
