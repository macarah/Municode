ARTICLE VI. - SOLICITATIONS ON ROADWAYS[5]

--- (5) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2009-11-38, adopted Nov. 30, 2009, deleted the former Ch. 22, Art. VI, §§ 22-145—22-148 and enacted a new Art. VI as set out herein. The former Art. VI pertained to solicitations on roadways and derived from Ord. No. 2007-02-07, § 1(ch. 11, art. 10, §§ 1—3), 2-26-2007.

Sec. 22-145. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Public area means an area to which the public or a substantial group of persons has access, including, but not limited to, alleys, bridges, buildings, driveways, parking lots, parks, playgrounds, plazas, sidewalks and streets that are open to the general public.
Solicit means to request an immediate donation of money or other thing of value from another person including employment, business or contributions, or to request the sale of goods or services.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-146. - Violations.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-147. - Permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, organization or corporation to solicit within any public right-of-way without a permit in accordance with sections 22-120 and 22-121. Appeal procedures shall be as referenced in said article V of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-148. - Applicability.
This article regulates the time, place and manner of solicitations and shall not apply to any persons exercising their clearly established constitutional right to picket, protest or engage in other constitutionally protected activity.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Secs. 22-149—22-179. - Reserved.
