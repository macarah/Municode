Chapter 26 - MUNICIPAL COURT
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 26-1. - Creation of the court.
In accordance with section 5.10 of the Charter, the city hereby creates the municipal court of the city. The powers and jurisdiction of said court are described in section 5.13 of the Charter.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 1, § 1, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-2. - Chief judge and judge pro tem.
(a)
In accordance with section 5.11 of the Charter, the council shall appoint the municipal court chief judge with compensation to be fixed by the council.
(b)
In accordance with section 5.11 of the Charter, the mayor shall also appoint additional judges pro tems as necessary to serve as requested.
(c)
The chief judge shall serve a term of four years, coincident with the term of the mayor, but may be removed for cause by a vote of five members of the city council or upon action taken by the judicial qualification commission. The judge pro tem shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor.
(d)
The chief judge may only be removed in accordance with the provisions of section 5.11(d) of the Charter.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 1, § 2, 12-19-2006; Ord. No. 2007-06-09, § 1, 6-28-2007)
Sec. 26-3. - Additional personnel.
(a)
Clerk of the court. The city manager shall have the authority to hire and fire a municipal court clerk who shall be the administrator of said court.
(b)
Police officers. Police officers of the city police department, the county police department or the county sheriff's department, or any other law enforcement officer, may execute warrants and other writs in furtherance of the court's jurisdiction and orders.
(c)
Bailiff. At least one city police department officer or an officer from the county sheriff's department will serve as a bailiff whenever the municipal court is in session.
(d)
Solicitor. In accordance with section 4.12 of the Charter, the city attorney shall be the prosecuting officer or solicitor in the municipal court.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 1, § 3, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-4. - Additional support.
(a)
Indigent defense. The city shall provide indigent defendants with counsel as provided by state law.
(b)
Translator. The city may provide translation services as required by state law.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 1, § 4, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-5. - Court sessions.
The court shall be in session at least one day per month and other times as determined necessary by the municipal court clerk to keep the court dockets current; the court clerk shall direct staffing of said sessions.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 1, § 5, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-6. - Processing fees; fines.
(a)
The municipal court chief judge may recommend a schedule of fees to assist the court in its operation and budget. The council must approve any such schedule, but if the council does not act within 30 days of the court's recommendation, the fee schedule will become effective.
(b)
The council may set city fines for violations of city ordinances.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 1, § 6, 12-19-2006)
Secs. 26-7—26-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TRIALS AND JUDGMENTS

Sec. 26-31. - Rules.
The municipal court chief judge shall adopt specific rules of procedure. No rules shall be inconsistent with the laws of the state or the Constitution of the United States. Such rules shall be made available on the city website or by other means determined by the council.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 2, § 1, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-32. - Contempt of court.
The court may find persons in contempt of court and punish said persons in accordance with section 5.13 of the Charter.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 2, § 2, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-33. - Fines.
(a)
For fines that may be due by any defendant, execution may be issued by the court clerk and collected as provided by law. The municipal court shall have the discretion to enter fines consistent with this Code and with state and federal law.
(b)
Persons charged with traffic violations or other offenses as specified by the judge may, prior to the time for their court appearance, plead guilty in writing and pay to the city their fine in the amount set as the appearance bond for the offense charged. Those persons charged with driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs and other serious offenses, as determined by the municipal court judge, shall be required in all cases to appear before the court for disposition of the case.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 2, § 3, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-34. - Sentences.
(a)
In addition to any applicable fines, and upon a judgment or plea of guilty or pretrial diversion, the municipal court chief judge may:
(1)
Impose sentence of confinement or compulsory work, or both;
(2)
Commit the defendant to confinement or compulsory work, or both;
(3)
Suspend the execution of the sentence in whole or in part;
(4)
Place the defendant on probation; or
(5)
Defer the execution of the sentence or any portion thereof to one or more fixed dates in the future.
(b)
The court may punish for violations within its jurisdiction a fine not exceeding $1,000.00 or imprisonment for six months, or both, except as otherwise provided by this Code or state law.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 2, § 4, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-35. - Appeals.
Unless specified elsewhere in this Code or the Charter, all appeals from decisions of the municipal court shall be appealable, by right of certiorari, to the county superior court of under the laws of the state regulating the granting and issuance of writs of certiorari.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 2, § 5, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-36. - Failure to appear.
new
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any defendant lawfully summoned to answer charges in the municipal court, via citation or otherwise, to fail to appear at the time and place specified in the summons, notice, or other notification document or to fail to provide a satisfactory explanation for this absence.
(b)
Said offense shall be punishable by fine in an amount not to exceed $200.00 or by confinement in jail for a period not to exceed three days.
(c)
The hearing, arraignment and/or trial subject of the summons, notice, or other notification document may be continued to such time as the municipal court may direct, and upon motion by the prosecutor, the court shall issue an order requiring the police chief, or other members of the city police department, or its designee, who shall be a certified law enforcement officer, to arrest the defendant so summoned and bring the defendant before the court to answer both the initial charges and the charge of failure to appear.
(Ord. No. 2022-05-07 , 5-2-2022)

ARTICLE III. - BAIL

Sec. 26-59. - General provisions.
Bail and bond considerations must be consistent with section 5.13 of the Charter.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 3, § 1, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-60. - Parking violations.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 17-6-11, any person cited for a traffic offense shall be released in lieu of bail upon showing his driver's license. This provision does not apply to any charges of driving under the influence or other serious offenses designated by the municipal judge.
(b)
All persons arrested or notified by citation or ticket of parking violations shall be released on their own recognizance.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 3, § 2, 12-19-2006)
State Law reference— Driver's license in lieu of bail, O.C.G.A. § 17-6-11.
Sec. 26-61. - Professional bondsmen.
No professional bondsman shall be accepted as surety on a bail bond unless he holds a current business license in the city, is approved by the chief of police in accordance with standards promulgated by the chief and has fully complied with all other city requirements for bonding companies.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 3, § 3, 12-19-2006)
Sec. 26-62. - Failure or refusal to give bond.
If the accused or a witness shall fail or refuse to give the bond and security, the person so failing or refusing may be confined or kept under guard, so as to be present to abide the trial or to testify, as the case may be.
(Ord. No. O2006-12-18, ch. 4, art. 3, § 4, 12-19-2006)
