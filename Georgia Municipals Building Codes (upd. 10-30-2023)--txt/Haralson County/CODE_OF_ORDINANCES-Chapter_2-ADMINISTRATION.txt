Sec. 2-1. - Board of commissioners established.
The county board of commissioners was established by 2001 Ga. Laws, Extraordinary Session, page 247. Prior to such enactment, the county was a sole commissioner county.

ARTICLE II. - BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-21. - Exercise of governmental authority.
Except as otherwise provided in this Code or by state law, the governmental powers of the county shall be exercised by the board of commissioners in the manner provided by state law and by the provisions of this article as may be amended from time to time.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 1-1)
Sec. 2-22. - Staggered terms for members.
Beginning in 2008, members of the county board of commissioners for district 2 and district 4 shall be elected to serve two-year terms, and beginning in 2012, members for district 1 and district 3 and the chairman of the county board of commissioners shall be subject to reelection. Thereafter, each of the members of the county board of commissioners shall serve four-year terms.
(Res. of 11-20-2007)
Sec. 2-23. - General powers.
The board of commissioners shall have the power and authority to fix and establish, by appropriate resolution entered on its minutes, policies, rules, regulations, and ordinances governing all matters reserved to its exclusive jurisdiction. Such policies, rules, regulations, and ordinances, when so adopted with proper entry thereof made on the minutes of the board of commissioners, shall be conclusive and binding. The board of commissioners shall exercise all administrative powers which are necessarily and properly incident to its function as a policy-making or rule-making body or which are necessary to compel enforcement of its adopted resolutions. The following powers are vested in the board of commissioners and reserved to its exclusive jurisdiction:
(1)
To levy taxes;
(2)
To make appropriations;
(3)
To fix rates and charges for services provided by the county;
(4)
To authorize the incurring of indebtedness;
(5)
To order work done where the cost is to be assessed against benefited property, and to fix the basis for such assessment;
(6)
To authorize and provide for the execution of contracts;
(7)
To establish, alter, open, close, build, repair, or abolish public roads, bridges, and ferries, according to law; provided, however, that the chairman shall have the authority to accept subdivision plats when the requirements established by the board of commissioners for subdivisions have been met;
(8)
To establish, abolish, or change election precincts and militia districts according to the law;
(9)
To accept, for the county, the provisions of any optional statute where the statute permits its acceptance by the governing authority of the county;
(10)
To exercise all powers, duty, and authority in respect to zoning and planning;
(11)
To create and change the boundaries of special taxing districts authorized by the law;
(12)
To fix the bonds of county officers where same are not fixed by statute;
(13)
To enact any ordinances or other legislation which the county may be given authority to enact;
(14)
To determine the priority of capital improvements;
(15)
To call elections for the voting of bonds;
(16)
To exercise all of the power and authority formerly vested by law in the sole commissioner of the county together with the power and authority which is now, or may be, delegated by law to the governing authority of the county, by whatever name designated;
(17)
To appoint retained legal counsel and an independent county auditor and provide for their compensation;
(18)
To require all county officers to report on the general or specific conduct of the financial affairs of their respective offices; and
(19)
To make appointments to such other boards established by law to which the board of commissioners shall be entitled to appoint. This specific power shall not be exercised except by a majority vote of the entire board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 1-2)
Sec. 2-24. - Duties of chairman.
The chairman shall be the chief elective officer of the county government and shall preside over the meetings of the board of commissioners. In addition to such other duties as set forth in this section, the duties of the chairman shall include, but shall not be limited to, the following:
(1)
Maintaining an office at the county government complex;
(2)
Presiding at all public meetings;
(3)
Representing the county government at ceremonial functions;
(4)
Being available to the constituency on a regular basis;
(5)
Coordinating intergovernmental activity among municipalities, other counties, the state, and the United States and any agencies thereof;
(6)
Being empowered to submit motions to the board of commissioners for action;
(7)
Sharing equal responsibility with other commissioners for budget approval and amendments, and their approval;
(8)
Taking the lead role in initiating needs assessments, evaluations of county services, and policy development;
(9)
Appointing subcommittee chairmen with the approval of the board of commissioners; and
(10)
Voting on all matters submitted to the board of commissioners for a vote, unless the chairman has a legal conflict of interest with regard to the matter before the board; provided, however, that the chairman shall have no veto power over any matter before the board for a vote.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 1-3)
Sec. 2-25. - Vice-chairman.
At the first regular meeting of every second year, the board of commissioners shall elect from its members a vice-chairman. In the event of death, disqualification, or resignation of the chairman, the vice-chairman shall perform the duties and authority of the chairman until a new chairman is chosen as provided by law. The vice-chairman shall preside at meetings of the board of commissioners in the absence of the chairman and shall serve for the calendar year in which elected. In the event of a vacancy in the office of vice-chairman, the board of commissioners shall elect a new vice-chairman to serve for the remainder of the calendar year.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 1-4)
Sec. 2-26. - Legal representation and indemnification for county commissioners.
(a)
The county will undertake to defend all civil, criminal, or quasi-criminal actions brought or maintained against any county commissioner arising out of the performance of the county commissioner's duties or in any way connected therewith, whether based upon negligence, violation of contract rights, or violation of civil, constitutional, or common law or statutory rights, to the extent such defense is not provided pursuant to a policy of insurance issued to the county government. This resolution shall be construed to mean that the county will and is authorized to expend funds to pay for attorney's fees, court costs, deposition costs, witness fees and compensation and all other like costs, expenses and fees in the event or to the extent that such expenses, fees and costs are not otherwise covered by a policy of insurance.
(b)
The county shall also indemnify and hold the county commissioners harmless against, and the county shall pay for the benefit of the county commissioners, any and all claims or civil judgments rendered against the county commissioners, or any of them, to the extent such claims arise out of the performance of the duties of the county commissioners or are in any way connected therewith.
(c)
This section is intended to, and shall, provide defense and indemnification to the county commissioner to the fullest extent authorized by law, as the same exists or may hereafter be amended but, in the case of any such amendment, only to the extent that such amendment permits the county to provide broader indemnification and defense to the commissioners than permitted prior thereto.
(d)
This section shall be deemed a contract right in favor of any commissioner of the county serving while this section remain in effect, and no repeal of this section hereafter shall be effective as to any such commissioner.
(Ord. of 7-10-1996)

DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS

Sec. 2-56. - Location and time; calling special meetings; open to public.
(a)
Location. All meetings of the board of commissioners shall be held at the county government complex located at 155 Van Wert Street, Buchanan, Georgia, unless otherwise provided by notice or resolution.
(b)
Regular meetings. The board of commissioners shall hold regular meetings on such days and at such times as established by the board of commissioners from time to time.
(c)
Special meetings. The chairman or any two members of the board of commissioners may convene the board whenever the chairman or the two members deem that the public business demands it.
(d)
Open to public. All meetings at which official actions are to be taken shall be open to the public.
(e)
Work sessions. The board of commissioners may conduct work sessions at its pleasure for the purpose of reviewing matter brought to its attention, which shall be open to the public.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 2-1)
Sec. 2-57. - Rules of procedure.
Robert's Rules of Order shall govern the conduct of the board meetings. Meeting procedure shall be as follows:
(1)
Call to order. All meetings of the board of commissioners shall be open to the public. The chairman, or in the chairman's absence, the vice-chairman, shall take the chair at the hour appointed for any regular, temporarily adjourned, special, or called meeting, and shall immediately call the board to order.
(2)
Agenda. It shall, upon consultation with each of the members of the board of commissioners, be the responsibility of the chairman to create the agenda for each meeting of the board of commissioners. Any citizen who desires to have an item placed on the agenda shall notify the clerk no later than 5:00 p.m. the Tuesday preceding the regularly scheduled meeting at which the matter is to appear on the agenda.
(3)
Attendance record. The county clerk shall enter in the minutes the names of commissioners who are present and those who are absent.
(4)
Quorum. A majority of all the members elected to the board of commissioners shall constitute a quorum at any regular or special meeting, and an affirmative vote of a majority of such number shall be sufficient to permit the conduct of all business.
(5)
Approval of minutes. Unless a reading of the minutes of a meeting is requested by a commissioner, such minutes may be approved without a reading if the county clerk has previously furnished each member with a copy not later than the Friday preceding the next meeting.
(6)
Reports by committees. Any business coming before the board may be referred to a committee of one or more commissioners or to a department head for further study. It shall be the duty of each such committee to make a timely report on matters referred to such committee.
(7)
Public comment on agenda items. Any person not a member of the board of commissioners who desires to address the board on any item on the agenda for the instant meeting shall first secure permission of the presiding officer to do so, and shall then stand, step forward, give such person's name and address in an audible tone of voice for the record, and direct such person's remarks to the board of commissioners as a body rather than to any particular member, limiting such remarks to five minutes. If such person addressing the board anticipates that such remarks will require more than five minutes such material as would otherwise be presented shall be submitted to the county clerk in writing not less than five days prior to the scheduled meeting date. The clerk shall distribute copies of the submitted material to the board of commissioners for their review prior to the scheduled public meeting of the board. If such person wishes to distribute the offered matter in its entirety to the general audience in attendance at the public meeting such person shall obtain approval of the county clerk not less than 24 hours prior to the scheduled meeting. Without the express permission of the chairman during the conduct of the meeting, each presentation will be closed after five minutes by direction of the chairman.
(8)
Public comment on nonagenda items. Any person not a member of the board of commissioners who desires to address the board on any matter not on the instant agenda shall first sign in with the clerk prior to the start of the meeting and then, after securing the permission of the presiding officer to speak, shall stand, step forward, give such person's name and address in an audible tone of voice for the record, and direct such person's remarks to the board of commissioners as a body rather than to any particular member. Comments on nonagenda matters shall be limited to ten minutes total, and if more than one person requests to address the board on a nonagenda matter, then each such person's time shall be limited proportionately to the number of persons who desire to address the board on nonagenda items. The chairman shall have the discretion to extend the time for comments on nonagenda matters.
(9)
Recording vote. The yeas and nays of the commissioners present shall be recorded on the minutes on any vote taken.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-58. - Adoption of ordinances, resolutions, contracts and agreements.
Unless otherwise provided in this Code, all ordinances, resolutions, contracts, and agreements of the county shall be prepared, approved, introduced, and adopted in the following manner:
(1)
Preparation. All ordinances shall be prepared by the county attorney. No ordinance shall be prepared for presentation to the board of commissioners unless ordered by a majority vote of a quorum of the board, or requested in writing by the chairman or by the county attorney on such attorney's own initiative.
(2)
Administrative staff approval. All ordinances, resolutions, contract documents, and agreements, before presentation to the board, shall have been approved as to form and legality by the county attorney.
(3)
Introduction. Ordinances, resolutions and other matters or subjects requiring action by the board of commissioners must be introduced and sponsored by a member of the board, except that the county attorney may present ordinances, resolutions, and other matters or subjects to the board, and any commissioner may assume sponsorship thereof by moving that such ordinances, resolutions, matters, or subjects be adopted.
(4)
Adoption. The adoption of any ordinance, resolution, or other action of the board shall require a majority affirmance of a quorum of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-59. - Minutes.
The board of commissioners shall appoint a county clerk, who shall keep a proper and accurate book of minutes. The book of minutes of the commission shall contain all the acts, orders, and proceedings of the commission in chronological order. The minutes books of the commission shall be open to the public for inspection at all times during regular office hours, and certified copies of any entries in the minutes books shall be furnished by the clerk to any person requesting same upon payment of a reasonable fee, to be paid into the county treasury as are other funds, to be assessed by the commission in an amount sufficient to defray the cost of preparing same.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 2-4)
Sec. 2-60. - Order of business.
All regular meetings of the board of commissioners should follow an established order of business. The order is as follows:
(1)
Call to order;
(2)
Minutes;
(3)
Agenda;
(4)
Announcements;
(5)
Invited guests;
(6)
Resolutions and proclamations;
(7)
Bid openings;
(8)
Reports from committees and departments;
(9)
Public participation on agenda items;
(10)
Consent agenda;
(11)
Old business;
(12)
New business;
(13)
Executive session (as necessary);
(14)
Public participation on non-agenda items;
(15)
Adjournment.
(Ord. of 4-7-2015)
Sec. 2-61. - Decorum of board members.
(a)
All members of the board of commissioners must conduct themselves in a professional and respectful manner. All remarks should be directed to the chair and not to individual commissioners, staff or citizens in attendance. Personal remarks are inappropriate. A commissioner may not speak at a meeting until he has been recognized by the chair. All comments made by a commissioner shall address the motion that is being discussed.
(b)
The chair shall enforce these rules of decorum. If a commissioner believes that a rule has been broken, he can raise a point of order. A second is not required. The chair can rule on the question or he can allow the board to debate the issue and decide the issue by majority vote.
(Ord. of 4-7-2015)
Sec. 2-62. - Public participation.
(a)
Public participation on agenda items. In accordance with this section, Haralson County residents shall be afforded an opportunity to address the board of commissioners on regular meeting agenda items. In the order of business, public participation on agenda items shall occur immediately after reports from committees and departments. Public participation on agenda items shall be conducted as follows:
(1)
The speaker must be a Haralson County resident or an owner of real property located within Haralson County;
(2)
The speaker may address any consent agenda, new business, or old business item which is on the current meeting agenda;
(3)
The speaker must register on the publication participation sign in sheet maintained by the clerk prior to the meeting. The sign in sheet shall be open for registration beginning ten minutes prior to the advertised meeting time and remain open until the meeting is called to order. The speaker shall register their name, address and the agenda topic about which they will speak;
(4)
The speaker must limit their comments to their selected agenda topic;
(5)
The speaker must be recognized by the chair before addressing the board;
(6)
Each speaker will be allotted three minutes to speak. No speaker may yield any of their allotted time to a different speaker.
(b)
Public participation on non-agenda items. In accordance with this section, by unanimous consent of the board, Haralson County residents may be afforded an opportunity to address the board of commissioners on items which are not a part of the current meeting agenda. In the order of business, public participation on non-agenda items shall be the final meeting agenda item. Public participation on non-agenda items shall be conducted as follows:
(1)
The speaker must be a Haralson County resident or an owner of real property located within Haralson County;
(2)
The speaker must submit their name and the specific topic of their comments to the chairman through the county clerk by the close of business (5:00 p.m.) on the Wednesday preceding the board of commissioners' next scheduled meeting and the speaker must schedule a time and meet with the commission chairman or the county clerk to discuss the identified topic prior to the upcoming board of commissioners meeting. At the conclusion of the meeting between the speaker and the chairman or the county clerk, the speaker shall indicate if the topic has been resolved or if the speaker and topic are to be placed on the agenda for public participation at the next regular board of commissioners meeting;
(3)
Each speaker must limit their comments to their designated topic;
(4)
The speaker must be recognized by the chair before addressing the board;
(5)
Each speaker will be allotted three minutes to speak. No speaker may yield any of their allotted time to a different speaker.
(c)
Each speaker is limited to one speaking opportunity during each meeting day.
(d)
No person may use the public participation portions of a meeting to advocate for or against the nomination, appointment or election of any candidate for any elective office.
(e)
Ineligible speakers. The following classes of individuals are ineligible to participate as a speaker:
(1)
Individuals, including members of their campaign committees, who have filed a declaration of intention to accept campaign contributions, from the date of filing until the date that the election results for the office sought are certified.
(2)
Individuals, including their attorneys, agents or assigns, who are parties to litigation involving Haralson County and/or its elected officials, agents or employees, if such litigation is currently pending in either state or federal court or who have filed a pending ante litem notice with Haralson County.
(3)
Individuals who are former employees of Haralson County, including their attorneys, agents or assigns, until the passage of 90 days following the date of termination, cessation or resignation of their employment.
(f)
Decorum of members of the public. Members of the public shall not make inappropriate or offensive comments at a board meeting and are expected to comply with the rules of decorum that are established for commissioners. All remarks should be directed to the chair and not to individual commissioners, staff or citizens in attendance. Personal remarks are inappropriate. A member of the public may not speak at a meeting until he has been recognized by the chair. All comments made by a member of the public shall address the topic that is being discussed. Individuals who violate any rules of the board may be ruled out of order by the chair or called on point of order by a commissioner. A majority vote of the board will rule on the point of order. All individuals in attendance, commissioners, staff and audience members, shall conduct themselves in a civil manner and shall treat all parties with respect and dignity. An individual who violates the rules of decorum and has been ruled out of order may be removed from the meeting at the direction of the chair or the parliamentarian.
(g)
Public hearings. The board of commissioners may schedule public hearings for the purpose of soliciting public comment on any subject of interest to the board. Hearings may be held immediately prior to, during, or following a meeting of the board or at such other places and times as the board may determine. No official action shall be taken at any such public hearing except when scheduled during the course of a regular or called board meeting.
(Ord. of 4-7-2015)
Sec. 2-63. - Parliamentarian.
The county attorney in attendance shall serve as the parliamentarian for meetings of the board of commissioners and shall be responsible for enforcing time limits and rules regarding public participation.
(Ord. of 4-7-2015)

ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-77. - Authority regarding organization of county government.
The internal organization of the county government shall be established and altered by the board of commissioners. Existing departments may be abolished and their functions transferred to other departments, additional departments may be created, and any two or more departments may be consolidated.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-1)
Sec. 2-78. - Employee handbook adopted.
The county has compiled and maintains the county employee handbook, the provisions of which, as amended, are incorporated in this division as if fully set forth herein.
Sec. 2-79. - Duties and responsibilities of officers.
Each officer shall perform all duties required of the office by state law or county ordinance, and such other duties not in conflict therewith as may be required by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-2; Res. of 7-8-2005)
Sec. 2-80. - Duties and responsibilities of department heads.
All department heads shall have the following responsibilities:
(1)
Be immediately responsible to the board of commissioners for the effective administration of their respective departments and all activities assigned thereto;
(2)
Keep informed as to the latest practices in their particular field and initiate, with the approval of the board of commissioners, such new practices as appear to be of benefit to the county and to the public;
(3)
Submit reports quarterly or as otherwise required by the board of commissioners of the activities of their respective departments to the board of commissioners;
(4)
Establish and maintain a system of filing and indexing records and reports in sufficient detail to furnish all information necessary for property control of departmental activities and to form a basis for the periodic reports to the board of commissioners;
(5)
Prepare budgets annually or as otherwise directed, as required by the board of commissioners;
(6)
Have power, when authorized by the board of commissioners, to appoint and remove, subject to personnel regulations, all subordinate employees; and
(7)
Be responsible for the proper maintenance of all county property and equipment used in their respective departments.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-2; Res. of 7-8-2005)
Sec. 2-81. - Departmental cooperation.
Each department shall cooperate with every other department and shall furnish, upon the direction of the board of commissioners, any other department such service, labor, and materials as may be requisitioned by the head of the department.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-2)
Sec. 2-82. - Open records.
All county records, except those which by ordinance, state law, or court order are prohibited from being open to public inspection, shall be open for personal inspection by any citizen of the state during the hours of operation of the administrative service as prescribed in section 2-83.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-2)
Sec. 2-83. - Operating hours of county administrative offices.
All units in the administrative service shall have the following operating hours:
(1)
Be open between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. on weekdays and shall be closed on Saturday, Sunday, and legal holidays, except as otherwise directed by the board of commissioners; provided that, any elected county official may extend the hours of operation of offices under such official's direct supervision when such official deems it reasonably necessary for the convenience of the public.
(2)
Make a daily deposit with the county depository designated by the board of commissioners of any monies received from the public.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-2)
Sec. 2-84. - Certain elected officials of the county to be covered under workers' compensation.
The board of commissioners, sheriff, probate judge, clerk of court, and tax commissioner shall be covered under the Association County Commissioners of Georgia group self-insured workers' compensation fund while performing their elected duties under the workers' compensation laws of the state as stated in O.C.G.A. § 34-9-1.
(Res. of 12-1-1987, § I; Res. of 2-1-2000(1), § I)
Secs. 2-85—2-111. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - SPECIFIC OFFICERS

Sec. 2-112. - County attorney.
The board of commissioners shall have the authority to employ a county attorney who shall receive an annual retainer in an amount determined by the board, payable in equal monthly installments from the funds of the county. The county attorney shall be entitled to such other reasonable compensation for services rendered to the county as may be approved by the board.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-3)
Sec. 2-113. - County auditor.
The board of commissioners shall have the authority to employ a county auditor who shall receive compensation in an amount determined by the board, payable in equal monthly installments from the funds of the county. The county auditor shall be entitled to such other reasonable compensation for services rendered to the county as may be approved by the board.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-4)
Sec. 2-114. - County clerk.
The board of commissioners shall have the authority to employ a county clerk who shall receive compensation in an amount and in such increments as determined by the board.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 3-5)
Secs. 2-115—2-141. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - BOARDS, AUTHORITIES, COMMISSIONS AND COMMITTEES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-142. - Legislatively created boards and authorities.
Certain boards and authorities for the county, individually or in association with other local government units, have been created by legislative acts including, but not limited to, the following:
(1)
Haralson County Board of Elections, created by 2001 Ga. Laws (Act No. 98), p. 3933, as subsequently amended;
(2)
Haralson County Family Connection Authority, created by 1999 Ga. Laws (Act No. 113), p. 4083, as subsequently amended;
(3)
Haralson County Hospital Authority, created by 1998 Ga. Laws (Act No. 926), p. 4506, as subsequently amended;
(4)
Haralson County School Building Authority, created by 2004 Ga. Laws (Act No. 448), p. 3563, as subsequently amended;
(5)
West Georgia Airport Authority, created by 1968 Ga. Laws (Act No. 895), p. 2769, as subsequently amended;
(6)
West Georgia Radio Authority, created by 2004 Ga. Laws (Act No. 837), p. 4521, as subsequently amended; and
(7)
West Georgia Regional Water Authority, created by 1988 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1187), p. 4926, as subsequently amended.
Sec. 2-143. - Planning commission.
(a)
The planning commission shall consist of not less than five members and no more than seven members, who shall be residents of the county, or own real property therein, appointed by the board of commissioners.
(b)
A majority of the members of the planning commission shall at all times consist of persons who hold no other public office in the county.
(c)
The member shall serve staggered terms of four years.
(d)
Any vacancy in membership will be appointed for the unexpired term by the county, who shall also have the authority to revoke the appointment for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing.
(e)
All members shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Ord. of 3-10-1987, § I)
Sec. 2-144. - Recreation authority.
The county recreation authority has been established by the county and shall be governed by bylaws, rules, and regulations promulgated by it. The recreation authority is authorized and empowered to manage the operation of a county recreation league.
(Res. of 3-3-1992)
Sec. 2-145. - Joint hospital authority.
By concurrent resolutions, the county and the city of Bremen have created a joint hospital authority which shall be governed as agreed from time to time by the county and the city; provided, however, that vacancies in the governing board shall be filled as follows:
(1)
If the member of the governing board of the authority whose position has become vacant was a resident of the city at the time of his appointment, then a member to fill the vacancy shall be appointed by the governing authority of the city; and
(2)
If the member of the governing board of the authority whose position has become vacant was not a resident of the city at the time of his appointment, then a member to fill the vacancy shall be appointed by the governing authority of the county.
(Res. of 2-20-1998)
Sec. 2-146. - Solid waste management authority.
(a)
There is hereby activated a solid waste management authority pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-50 et seq.
(b)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 12-8-54, the board of directors shall consist of five members to be appointed as follows:
Position 1. The board of commissioners or its designee;
Position 2. The Bremen Mayor or his designee;
Position 3. The Buchanan Mayor or his designee;
Position 4. The Tallapoosa Mayor or his designee;
Position 5. The Waco Mayor or his designee.
(c)
The terms of the directors in subsection (b) of this section shall be for four years as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-54 and shall run concurrently with the terms of their respective elected offices.
(d)
The powers, provisions, and terms of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-55 concerning quorums; O.C.G.A. § 12-8-57 concerning limitation on liability of members, officers, or employees of the authority; O.C.G.A. § 12-8-58 concerning bonds or other obligations and limitations and procedures for issuance; O.C.G.A. § 12-8-59 concerning bonds or other obligations not being indebtedness of state or political subdivision; and O.C.G.A. § 12-8-59.1 are hereby incorporated by reference and made applicable to the authority established herein.
(Res. of 1-2-1995; Res. of 2-24-1995)
Secs. 2-147—2-175. - Reserved.

Subdivision I. - In General

Sec. 2-176. - Applicability.
Nothing in this section shall be construed to in any way affect the right of any municipal corporation in the county to create a development authority as authorized by the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq., or to perform any function authorized by said law when said development authority is already in existence.

Sec. 2-177. - Establishment.
There is hereby activated in the county the public body corporate and politic known as the Development Authority of Haralson County, Georgia, which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-178. - Depositories.
All funds of the corporation shall be deposited in the name of the corporation in such bank or other financial institutions as the board of directors may from time to time designate and shall be drawn out on checks, drafts, or other orders signed on behalf of the corporation by such persons as the board of directors may from time to time designate.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-179. - Seal.
The seal of the authority shall be as adopted by the authority. If the seal is affixed to a document, the signature of the secretary-treasurer shall attest the seal. The seal and its attestation may be lithographed or otherwise printed on any document and shall have, to the extent permitted by law, the same force and effect as if it had been affixed and attested manually.

Subdivision II. - Board of Directors

Sec. 2-197. - Composition of board of directors; terms.
The board of directors of the authority shall be comprised of seven members who shall be taxpayers residing in the county. No director shall be an officer or employee of the county. Each shall serve a term of four years, ending at noon on the last day of the term; provided, however, that each director shall serve until his successor is appointed and qualified for the position.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-198. - Power of directors.
The board of directors shall manage the business of the corporation and subject to any restrictions imposed by law or by the authority's bylaws.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-199. - Organization.
The board of directors herein of the authority shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities, and exercise its powers prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-200. - Meetings of the board.
(a)
The annual meeting of the board of directors for the purpose of electing officers and transacting such other business as may be brought before the meeting shall be held each year at a date, time, and place as may be provided for by the board. The board of directors may by resolution provide for the time and place of other regular meetings and no notice of such regular meetings need be given.
(b)
Special meetings of the board of directors may be called by the chairman or by any two directors, and written notice of the time and place of such meetings shall be given to each director by first class or air mail at least four days before the meeting or by telephone, e-mail, facsimile transmission, or in person at least two days before the meeting.
(c)
Any director may execute a waiver of notice, either before or after any meeting, and shall be deemed to have waived notice if he is present at such meeting. Neither the business to be transacted at, nor the purpose of, any meeting of the board of directors need be stated in the notice or waiver of notice of such meeting. All meetings must be held within the state.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-201. - Quorum and vote requirement.
A majority of the directors in office shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business at any meeting. No action may be taken by the board without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the board.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-202. - Compensation.
The directors shall receive no compensation for their services as directors or officers of the corporation, but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-203. - Amendment of bylaws.
The board of directors shall have the power to alter, amend, or repeal its bylaws or adopt new bylaws. Action by the directors with respect to the bylaws shall be taken by an affirmative vote of a majority of all of the directors.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Secs. 2-204—2-229. - Reserved.
Subdivision III. - Officers

Sec. 2-230. - Executive structure of the corporation.
The officers of the corporation shall consist of a chairman, a vice-chairman, a secretary-treasurer, and such other officers as may be elected by the board of directors. The chairman and the vice-chairman shall be elected from the directors of the corporation. The other officers of the corporation need not be elected from the directors.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-231. - Election of officers.
The officers of the corporation shall be elected by the board of directors for terms of one year. The officers shall continue to hold office until their successors are elected and qualified. An officer shall be eligible to succeed himself in office.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-232. - Chairman.
The chairman shall be the chief executive officer of the corporation and shall give general supervision and direction to the affairs of the corporation, subject to the direction of the board of directors. He shall preside at all meetings of the board of directors.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-233. - Vice-chairman.
The vice-chairman shall act in the case of the absence or disability of the chairman.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-234. - Secretary-treasurer.
The secretary-treasurer shall keep the minutes of the proceedings of the board of directors and shall have custody of and attest the seal of the corporation, and shall also be responsible for the maintenance of proper financial books and records of the corporation.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-235. - Contracts and deeds.
All contracts, deeds, and other instruments shall be signed on behalf of the corporation by such officer, officers, agent, or agents as the board of directors may from time to time by resolution provide.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-236. - Other duties and authority.
Each officer, employee, and agent of the corporation shall have such other duties and authority as may be conferred upon him by the board of directors or delegated to him by the chairman.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)
Sec. 2-237. - Removal of officers.
Any officer may be removed at any time for cause by the board of directors.
(Res. of 10-14-1982)

DIVISION 3. - JOINT COUNTIES DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-268. - Purpose and intent.
It is hereby declared that there is a need for a joint development authority to function in Carroll County, Haralson County, Heard County, and Polk County. Such authority, to be known as the Joint Development Authority of Carroll County, Haralson County, Heard County, and Polk County, is hereby created and activated. Said authority, herein called the authority, shall transact business pursuant to, and exercise the powers provided by the provisions of, the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Res. of 12-6-1994(2), § 1)
Sec. 2-269. - Establishment.
The authority is created and activated by this proper resolution of the governing bodies of Carroll County, Haralson County, Heard County, and Polk County as a joint authority pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.1.
(Res. of 12-6-1994(2), § 2)
Sec. 2-270. - Directors.
(a)
The board of directors of the joint county authority shall be composed of nine directors. Each of the member counties shall appoint two members who are taxpayers residing in the appointing county. The ninth member of the board shall be appointed at the expiration of each term by one of the member counties on a rotating basis, as follows: Haralson County, Carroll County, Heard County, Polk County.
(b)
No director shall be an officer or employee of any member county.
(c)
The directors shall receive no compensation for their services, but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(d)
If at the end of any term of any member a successor to such member has not been appointed, the member whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is appointed.
(e)
A majority of the members of the authority shall constitute a quorum, but no action may be taken by the authority without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the authority.
(Res. of 12-6-1994(2), § 3)
Sec. 2-271. - Officers.
The directors shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice-chairman and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may, but need not be, a director.
(Res. of 12-6-1994(2), § 4)
Secs. 2-272—2-290. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - JOINT COUNTY-CITY DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-291. - Establishment.
There is a present and future need for a joint development authority to function within the county and the City of Buchanan. Therefore, there shall be activated within the county and the City of Buchanan a political and corporate body known as the Joint Development Authority of Buchanan-Haralson County, which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Res. of 3-1-2005(2))
Sec. 2-292. - Board of directors.
(a)
The board of directors of the authority shall consist of seven members, three of which shall be appointed by the county (county appointees), three of whom shall be appointed by the city (city appointees), and one of whom shall be alternatively appointed by the city and county (rotating appointee).
(b)
The term of county appointees shall be four years and the term of the rotating appointee shall be two years. The term of city appointees shall be as established by the city.
(c)
The rotating appointee shall be initially appointed by the city to serve a two-year term, and the appointment of said position thereafter shall rotate between the county and the city.
(d)
The three county appointees shall include the chairman of the county board of commissioners and two other persons, each of whom is a taxpayer and resident of the county.
(e)
The board of directors herein appointed shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities, and exercise its power and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq., as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Res. of 3-1-2005(2))
Sec. 2-293. - Audit and review of authority actions.
All actions and activities of the authority shall be subject to the same audit and review procedures applicable to the county.
(Res. of 3-1-2005(2))
Sec. 2-294. - County and city not responsible for authority obligations.
Neither bonds or other obligations of the authority nor indebtedness incurred by the authority shall constitute an indebtedness or obligation of the county or the city, nor shall any act of the authority in any manner constitute or result in the creation of an indebtedness of the county or the city, all as is more particularly set forth at O.C.G.A. § 36-62-10.
(Res. of 3-1-2005(2))

ARTICLE V. - FINANCE

Sec. 2-322. - Adoption of budget.
(a)
The chairman shall submit annually to the board of commissioners a proposed budget governing expenditures of all county funds, including capital outlay and public works projects for the following fiscal year. The board of commissioners shall thereafter hold a public hearing on the budget, giving notice thereof at least ten days in advance by publication of such notice and the proposed budget in the official organ of the county and by posting same at the courthouse door. The budget shall then be reviewed and adopted or amended by a majority of a quorum of the board of commissioners prior to the beginning of the new fiscal year to which is applies, which budget, when so adopted or amended by the board of commissioners, shall constitute the board's appropriation of all funds for such year.
(b)
The budget so adopted may be revised during the year only by formal action of the board of commissioners in a regular meeting, and no increase shall be made therein without provision also being made for financing same.
(c)
The proposed budget submitted by the chairman shall be accompanied by a report containing information and data relating to the financial affairs of the county pertinent to arriving at and establishing the annual budget.
(d)
A copy of the budget and of each revision or amendment to same shall be transmitted by the chairman to the grand jury of the superior court of the county within ten days from the time such budget or amendment thereof is adopted by the commission.
(e)
The board of commissioners may from time to time, upon a majority vote thereof, change the ending and beginning date of the fiscal year for the county or may operate on a calendar basis; provided, however, that in the event of such change, the budget shall be submitted at least two months prior to the beginning date of the next fiscal year or January 1, whichever shall apply, and shall be adopted at least one month prior to said beginning date.
(f)
The procedures for budget preparation, submission to the governing authority, review by the governing authority, public review, notice, and hearings shall be provided for in O.C.G.A. 36-81-1 et seq.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 4-1)
Sec. 2-323. - Conformance with budget required; limitations on expenditures.
No expenditures of county funds shall be made except in accordance with the county budget, or amendments thereto, adopted by the commission. The chairman shall enforce compliance with this provision by all departments of county government and to this end shall institute a system of allotments of all moneys appropriated and budgeted. All expenditures shall have the approval of the chairman of the board.
(Res. of 1-4-2005, § 4-2; Res. of 3-22-2005(1))
Sec. 2-324. - Designate individuals with signatory authority.
The chairman of county board of commissioners, the county clerk, and the county treasury are authorized to endorse county checks in the form of negotiable instruments with county banking institutions.
(Res. of 3-1-2005(1))

ARTICLE VI. - COUNTY PROPERTY AND BUILDINGS

Sec. 2-352. - Smoking not permitted in county facilities.
All county buildings are declared no-smoking facilities, and smoking is prohibited in all offices, corridors, restrooms, and refreshment areas of all county buildings.
(Ord. of 3-2-1993)
Sec. 2-353. - Livestock pavilion facility.
The county or its designees shall manage the county livestock pavilion (Callaway Pavilion). The county extension service, vocational agriculture department, and all other county governmental agencies shall have priority access to the pavilion. When the facility is not in use for a county agency or other designee, the pavilion may be leased to private individuals at the rate established from time to time by the board of commissioners, but not less than $150.00 per day. The county may adopt, amend, and alter from time to time its policies, rules, and forms that may be necessary to the management of the pavilion.
(Res. of 5-1-2007)
