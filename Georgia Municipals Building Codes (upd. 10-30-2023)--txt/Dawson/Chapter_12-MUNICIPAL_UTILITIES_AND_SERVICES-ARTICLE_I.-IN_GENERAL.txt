ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Drought contingency plan.
(a)
The following uniform requirements are adopted for the critical, emergency situation of the city's water supply and for the health and safety of the city water users:
(1)
No outside watering of any kind, including street flushing, public fountains, park irrigation, watering lawns, gardens, shrubbery, etc.
(2)
No commercial and/or public water use such as swimming pools, and motor vehicle washing of any kind. (Close city swimming pool)
(3)
No commercial and home laundry use except during the hours of 7:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m. only.
(b)
The enforcement actions of this section shall be taken on those users not complying with this section in the following manner:
(1)
Inspection by duly authorized officers bearing proper credentials and identification shall be permitted to enter all properties for the purpose of inspection, observation measurement and testing in accordance with the provisions of this section.
(2)
Any person found to be violating the provisions of this section shall be served written notice stating the nature of the violation and shall be issued a warning ticket for this violation.
(3)
On second violation, water service will be terminated and a reconnect fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) will be charged.
(4)
Each subsequent violation shall be charged a reconnect fee of two hundred dollars ($200.00) for each violation.
(Res. of 10-16-86)
Sec. 12-2. - Extension of water and sewer lines outside of city.
(a)
The city will allow water and sewer to be run outside the city limits at double the current rates for future customers.
(b)
The sewer service will be limited to single-family dwellings only.
(c)
Each application for water and sewer must be approved by the city council.
(d)
All new water and sewer hook-ups shall pay double the current in-city tap fee per house.
(e)
All developers shall meet city subdivision standards as to the size of lots and street frontages and street improvements, such as paving and drainage.
(f)
Total cost of water, sewer, paving and drainage shall be paid by the developer.
(g)
All engineering, when needed, will be paid by the developer.
(h)
The city must have plans of work to be done two (2) weeks before work begins.
(i)
All work will be inspected by the city and must be approved by the city.
(j)
All plumbers and contractors will have a state license.
(Ord. of 12-10-81, §§ 1—10)
Sec. 12-3. - Deposits for gas and water service.
Gas customers are required to keep on deposit with the city an amount equal to one hundred dollars ($100.00) for gas and fifty dollars ($50.00) for water, which deposit may be appropriated for the payment of gas and/or water bills and other charges which are in default.
(Code 1974, § 20-107)
Sec. 12-4. - Reading gas and water meters of new customers.
No gas or water meter will be read for billing purposes unless such meter has been in operation for a period of at least fifteen (15) days. Meters installed within fifteen (15) days of the meter reading date will be read and billed the following month for the total charges up to the date of such reading.
(Code 1974, § 20-108)
Sec. 12-5. - Persons authorized to connect water and gas.
It shall be unlawful for any person, except a representative of the city, to cut on or connect any customer or other person with the water or gas system of the city, whether for such person's self or for others.
(Code 1974, § 20-110)
Cross reference— Extending water and gas to subdivisions, app. B, § III-A(8).
Sec. 12-6. - Water service pipe connections.
(a)
All lateral or service water pipes for either public or private use and all necessary fixtures connected therewith shall be installed and maintained by the city from the main to the curbline.
(b)
A charge will be made for each connection as prescribed by the council from time to time.
(Code 1974, § 20-109)

Sec. 12-8. - Natural gas rates as fixed.
The monthly rates for natural gas to residential and commercial customers shall be as prescribed by the council from time to time.
(Code 1974, § 20-106)
Sec. 12-9. - Billing—Water.
The bills for water service shall be due and payable on the first day of each month. Such bills will be based upon meter readings of the latter part of the preceding month.
(Code 1974, § 20-102)
Sec. 12-10. - Same—Gas.
The bills for natural gas shall be due and payable on the first day of each month. Such bills will be based upon meter readings of the latter part of the preceding month.
(Code 1974, § 20-103)
Sec. 12-11. - Same—Delinquency.
All customers failing to pay the regular monthly bills for water and/or gas on or before the fifteenth day of the month shall be assessed a ten-percent penalty. Customers whose bills are not paid by the twentieth of the month shall be subject to have such services disconnected; when so disconnected, and such services will not be restored until such bills are paid, and a cut-on fee for gas and water as prescribed by the council from time to time.
(Code 1974, § 20-104)
Sec. 12-12. - Sale of unmetered water by city.
The city shall be authorized to sell water to those individuals, groups, or companies that desire to purchase water from the city and shall be authorized to charge an administrative fee for such service.
(Ord. of 7-10-03(1), § 1)
Sec. 12-13. - Rates and fees for sale of unmetered water.
The city shall sell unmetered water at the rate of commercial rates. The city may charge an administrative fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) to cover expenses in delivering the unmetered water to the purchaser. If the amount of water purchased from the city exceeds fifty thousand (50,000) gallons, the administrative fee will be seventy-five dollars ($75.00).
(Ord. of 7-10-03(1), § 1)
