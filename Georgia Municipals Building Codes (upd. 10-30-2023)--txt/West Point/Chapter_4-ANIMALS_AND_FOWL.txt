ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Running at large generally.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or keeping any mules, horses, cattle, hogs, goats, chickens, ducks, geese or other domestic animals or fowl to allow or permit them to run at large within the city. Any such person who allows or permits any such domestic animal or fowl to trespass upon the premises of any person, or to injure or damage his property, shall be guilty of a violation of this section.
(Code 1967, § 4-1)
Sec. 4-2. - Hogs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to harbor or keep hogs within the corporate limits of the city other than hogs which have been killed or slaughtered and prepared for market.
(Code 1967, § 4-2)
Sec. 4-3. - Chicken houses.
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect, maintain or use any house, lot or place for keeping any chickens, ducks, geese, guineas or other domestic fowl within fifty (50) feet of any dwelling house located within the corporate limits of the city without the written consent of every adult person residing in such house within the fifty (50) feet.
(Code 1967, § 4-3)
Sec. 4-4. - Keeping of cows regulated.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to keep any cattle, horses, mules, donkeys, sheep or goats within the corporate limits of the city confined in any area that does not provide for each animal so kept an area equal to that of a tract of land of one (1) acre.
(b)
The keeping of any such animal within the corporate limits of the city confined in any area less than that prescribed herein shall for each twenty-four (24) hours constitute a separate offense, and such offense shall be deemed a nuisance and subject to abatement, as well as punishment for the person responsible.
(Code 1967, § 4-4; Ord. of 6-12-95)
Sec. 4-5. - Cruelty to animals.
Any person who overrides, overdrives, overloads, drives when overloaded, tortures, torments, deprives of necessary sustenance, cruelly beats, mutilates or cruelly kills, or causes or procures to be overridden, overdriven, overloaded, driven when overloaded, tortured, tormented, deprived of necessary sustenance, cruelly beaten, mutilated or cruelly killed any domestic animal, either as owner or otherwise, shall on conviction be punished according to the provisions of section 1-14.
(Code 1967, § 4-5)
Secs. 4-6—4-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - IMPOUNDING

Sec. 4-31. - Animals at large to be impounded; sold.
When any horse, mule, jack, jennet, cow, calf, hog, sheep, goat, dog or any other animal is found running at large within the corporate limits of the city, it shall be seized and impounded by the chief of police or any other police officer or the superintendent of streets of the city. When any such animal is seized and impounded as herein provided, the chief of police shall proceed forthwith to sell the same at public auction to the highest bidder for cash, within the legal hours of sale, in front of the city hall after first giving five (5) days' notice of the time and place of sale, together with a description of the animal to be sold, by posting a notice at a conspicuous place at the city hall and by publishing one (1) time in a newspaper published in the county and distributed in the city, and shall pay to the city clerk-treasurer the proceeds received from such sale, after first deducting the cost and expense incident to the seizing, impounding, feeding, keeping and selling of such animal as hereinafter provided.
(Code 1967, § 4-16)
Sec. 4-32. - Charges for advertising impounded animals for sale; pound fee.
The city shall be entitled to and shall receive the sum of five dollars ($5.00) for advertising and selling each and every animal which may be advertised and sold or advertised for sale as provided for in the preceding section, together with the additional sum of one dollar ($1.00) for each day or fraction thereof each of such animals is kept and fed by the city pending the sale thereof as hereinabove provided.
(Code 1967, § 4-17)
Sec. 4-33. - Redemption of impounded animals.
The owner of any animal so seized and impounded shall have the right to have the same released at any time prior to the sale thereof by paying to the city clerk-treasurer or chief of police the sum that may be due for seizing and impounding, keeping, feeding and advertising the same as herein provided.
(Code 1967, § 4-18)
Sec. 4-34. - Owner entitled to proceeds of sale.
The owner of any animal seized, impounded and sold under this article shall have the right, within sixty (60) days after the sale thereof, to receive from the city clerk-treasurer the proceeds of such sale less the costs and expenses incident thereto, upon making proper and satisfactory proof of ownership of such animal so sold as demanded by the city clerk-treasurer.
(Code 1967, § 4-19)
Sec. 4-35. - Unauthorized release of animals from pound.
Any person who breaks into the city pound or releases any animals impounded under the laws and ordinances of the city shall be guilty of an offense and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-14 for such offense. This section does not apply to the release of any such animals under authority of the city law by a police officer of the city.
(Code 1967, § 4-20)
Secs. 4-36—4-55. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - DOGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-56. - Vaccination and tag required.
(a)
All dogs within the city shall be vaccinated against rabies on or before the first day of March of each year, and each dog shall wear a tag showing that it has been so vaccinated. Dogs being brought into the city after the first day of March shall be vaccinated immediately.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to keep, maintain, or allow to be kept on the premises occupied by anyone within the corporate limits of the city, or allow to go or run at large within the corporate limits of the city, any dog not vaccinated and tagged.
(Code 1967, § 4-31)
Sec. 4-57. - Dogs at large; leashes required.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or keeping any dog in the city to allow such dog to run at large upon the streets, sidewalks and public areas of the city unless such dog shall be secured by a leash controlled by a person competent to hold, manage and control such dog while so leashed.
(Code 1967, § 4-33)
Sec. 4-58. - Dogs on private property.
It shall be lawful for any person to keep a dog without a leash so long as such dog is restrained or contained on the private property of the owner of such premises.
(Code 1967, § 4-34)
Sec. 4-59. - Impounding of unleashed dogs running at large; redemption by owner.
(a)
Whenever any dog is found running at large, not under leash, and being off the premises of the owner or keeper of such dog, then the duly authorized agents or employees of the city shall be authorized to catch and confine such dog and after confining such dog for a period of not less than three (3) days to dispose of such dog so confined in a humane manner.
(b)
The owner of any dog confined or impounded, as provided in subsection (a), may reclaim such dog by making claim at the Valley Humane Society or any other authorized agent acting on behalf of the city and paying such redemption fees as may be required by that agent.
(Code 1967, § 4-35)
Sec. 4-60. - Penalty for violating sections 4-57 through 4-59.
The owner of any dog convicted of violation of sections 4-57 through 4-59 shall on conviction be punished in accordance with the provisions of section 1-14.
(Code 1967, § 4-37)
Secs. 4-61—4-75. - Reserved.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-76. - Definitions.
As defined and used in this article, when used in this article, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal bite means a seizing by an animal with the teeth or jaws of such animal so that a person or other animal has been nipped, gripped, wounded, or pierced.
Animal control officer means that person designated by the chief of police of the city to carry out and enforce the provisions of this article. In the absence of such designation, the chief of police shall be deemed the animal control officer.
Animal shelter means a facility designated by the city council and used to temporarily confine and house animals that have been seized or confiscated.
Complaint means any written or oral report of an incident or condition alleged to be in violation of this article or any written or oral request for action under or compliance with the provisions of this article.
Dog at large means that a dog is on any public street, alley, park, or other public grounds, or that a dog is off the premises of the owner or person who has custody of the dog, and not constrained by a leash or under the immediate control of the owner or person who has custody of the dog.
Domestic animal means an animal that is a member of a species, which through long association with man, has been bred to a degree which has resulted in changes affecting the temperament, color, conformation, or other attributes of the species to an extent that makes animals of the species unique and different from wild individuals of their kind.
Exotic animals means animals that do not occur naturally, either presently or historically, in the United States.
Exposed to rabies means that a person or animal has been seized with the teeth or claws of another animal, so that the skin of the person or animal seized has been nipped or gripped, or has been wounded or pierced and includes suspected or confirmed contact of saliva of the seizing animal with a break or abrasion of the skin or with any mucous membrane of the person or animal seized, as determined by a licensed physician.
Guard dog means a dog which is trained or used for the express purpose of protecting and guarding property.
Guide dog means a dog, especially trained for the purpose, that is used as a guide to ambulation by a person who is totally or partially blind.
Hearing dog means a dog, especially trained for the purpose, that is used as a guide to ambulation by a person who is hearing impaired.
Licensed veterinarian means a veterinarian duly licensed to practice veterinary medicine in any state.
Livestock or farm animal means any animal that is considered livestock or a farm animal, such as chickens, horses, mares, jacks, jennies, colts, cows, calves, yearlings, bulls, oxen, sheep, goats, lambs, hogs, shoats and pigs.
Mauling means the infliction of a serious injury by an animal to a human or another animal. A mauling is differentiated from a bite by the degree of injury. Serious injury differentiates a bite from a mauling
Native animals mean animals that occur naturally, either presently or historically, in the United States and its territories. In addition, native wild animals include those animals introduced into the United States that have become widespread and are generally considered native.
Neutered means made incapable of sexually reproducing offspring (male animals).
Nuisance means an incident or episode whereby an animal:
(1)
Is allowed to act or perform in such a manner as to cause damage or unsanitary conditions to public property or to the property of another.
(2)
Enters any place where food is stored, prepared, served, or sold to the public, except guide dogs that are accompanying a totally or partially blind person and hearing dogs that accompany a hearing impaired person.
(3)
Chases, runs after, or jumps at vehicles using public streets or alleys.
(4)
Feeds from, turns over, or otherwise disturbs garbage containers.
(5)
Scratches or digs in flower beds, gardens or soil, or damages the property of persons other than the owner.
(6)
Is allowed to mate in full or partial view of the public, except for animals inside enclosures designed to keep mating from unwanted public viewing.
(7)
Disturbs the peace by constantly or incessantly barking or howling, or by constantly or incessantly making other disturbing noises.
(8)
Running at large.
Owner means any person having a right of property of the animal, or who keeps or harbors the animal, or who has it in his care (to include feeding the animal), or acts as its custodian, or who permits the animal to remain on or about any premises occupied by such person.
Potentially vicious animal means:
(1)
Any animal of any species, domestic or wild, native or exotic where a report has been filed or a complaint made about or concerning the animal with the police department and evidence shows the animal to have behaved in a threatening manner, without provocation, toward humans, or which habitually snaps, growls, snarls, jumps upon or otherwise threatens persons who are lawfully using public sidewalks, streets, alleys, parks, recreation fields, or other public place; or
(2)
Any animal of the following breed or one that has the appearance and characteristics of or a combination of any of those breeds according to the standards established by the American Kennel Club or the United Kennel Club, excluding bona fide guide dogs, dogs used for search and rescue, or dogs used in law enforcement:
a.
Pit bull dog, Bull Terrier, American Pit Bull Terrier, the American Staffordshire Terrier, or a Presa Canario.
b.
Rottweiler.
c.
Chow Chow.
d.
Doberman Pinscher.
e.
German Shepherd.
f.
Wolf-hybrid.
Restraint/enclosure means when any of the following conditions exist:
(1)
The animal is confined in a building, fenced pen or yard, electrified fence, or electronic fence on its owner's property or on the property of another person with the other person's consent, adequate to prevent contact with the general public or any persons who enter the premises with the actual or implied permission of the owner or keeper of the animal.
(2)
The animal, when off the premises of the owner, is under the immediate and effective control of a responsible person adequately restrained by leash sufficient to prevent the animal from engaging any other human or animal.
Running at large means any dog not within a proper enclosure or under adequate restraint as defined by this article.
Spayed means made incapable of sexually reproducing offspring (female animals).
Stray means any dog not tagged with a rabies inoculation tag and/or a personal identification tag.
Vaccination means the administration of anti-rabies vaccines or other vaccines by any licensed veterinarian or his authorized representative at such intervals as required by state law.
Vicious animal means any animal of any species, domestic or wild, native or exotic that:
(1)
Attacks a human being or other domestic animal without provocation, provided that the animal shall not be deemed vicious if the threat, injury or damage was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort upon the premises occupied by the owner or keeper of the dog, or was teasing, tormenting, abusing or assaulting the animal, or has in the past, abused or assaulted the animal or was committing or attempting to commit a crime, as long as the owner was not aware of the current or past provocation and failed to take actions to stop the provocations, or;
(2)
According to the records of the city police or a county law enforcement agency, has inflicted injury on a human being without provocation, or;
(3)
According to the records of the city police or a county law enforcement agency, has killed a domestic animal without provocation while off the owner's property, or;
(4)
Is harbored primarily or in part for the purpose of fighting or any animal trained for fighting.
Wild animals means animals other than those defined as domestic, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians, which are either native or exotic.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-77. - Tethering of animals.
It shall be unlawful to tether any animal on a chain, rope or cable. All animals shall be housed indoors or in a secure outdoor area that allows for free movement sufficient for the animal's size.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-78. - Humane care of animals.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to overload, maim, tease, torture, or abuse any animal. No animal may be intentionally killed unless the animal is injured and/or is destroyed according to this article.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deprive any animal of shelter which shall protect the animal from the elements.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deprive any animal of necessary medical attention when the animal is in a condition in which failure to provide medical attention would result in death or unreasonable pain or suffering.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deprive any animal of sufficient food and water as often as the feeding habits of such animal require to prevent dehydration, starvation or a significant risk to the animal's health from lack of food or water.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to put out or cause to be put out any poison within the city for the purpose of harming any domesticated animal.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to release or leave any animal on any private or public property with the intention of abandoning such animal.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-79. - Restriction on number of dogs.
Not more than four (4) dogs shall be allowed in or on any residence of the owner or the person who has custody of the dogs, except by special approval by the city council, or except for a mother animal temporarily nursing a large litter of recently born puppies. The police department and/or authorized agent shall investigate the residence applying for a variance to this section before the presentation of the special application to the council. This restriction shall not apply to kennels or other dog-related businesses that are licensed and permitted according to federal and/or state and/or municipal statutes.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-80. - Licensing (potentially vicious or vicious animal).
(a)
In order to insure and monitor compliance with the requirements of this article no potentially vicious or vicious animal shall be kept, harbored or maintained in the city unless such animal has been registered by the owner with the city clerk.
(b)
Each registration shall include:
(1)
Owner's name.
(2)
Owner's address.
(3)
Owner's telephone number.
(4)
Name of the animal.
(5)
Breed, color, gender, and distinguishing marks of the animal.
(6)
Physical address where the animal will be housed.
(7)
Proof of inoculation and tag.
(8)
Proof of liability insurance or adequate surety bond as required in this article.
(9)
Proof of proper enclosure as required in this article.
(10)
Proof of spaying or neutering of vicious dog. (Not required for potentially vicious dog).
(c)
Each dog required to be licensed pursuant to the provisions of this article shall at all times wear a secure collar or harness with a tag securely fastened thereon indicating the name and address of the owner, the type or breed of the animal, that the animal has been duly registered, and the date of such registration. The license issued hereunder shall not be transferable from animal to animal or from owner to owner.
(d)
The fee for licensing pursuant to this article shall be twenty-five dollars ($25.00).
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-81. - Dog owner liability insurance; surety bond.
(a)
A policy of insurance or surety bond in the amount of at least one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of each potentially vicious animal against liability for personal injuries or property damage inflicted by the potentially vicious animal.
(b)
A policy of insurance or surety bond shall be required in the amount of at least one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of each vicious animal against liability for personal injuries or property damage inflicted by the vicious animal.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-82. - Landlord dog owner liability insurance; surety bond.
(a)
Landlords renting property to a person who owns, houses, maintains, or in any way keeps on the landlord's rental property a dog listed defined as potentially vicious or vicious by this article shall maintain a policy of insurance or surety bond in addition to the policy of insurance or surety bond required to be maintained by the dog owner. This liability insurance or surety bond shall be in the amount of at least one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the landlord who rents to the owner of each potentially vicious animal against liability for personal injuries or property damage inflicted by the vicious animal.
(b)
Landlords renting property to a person who owns, houses, maintains, or in any way keeps on the landlord's rental property a dog listed defined as vicious by this article shall maintain a policy of insurance or surety bond in addition to the policy of insurance or surety bond required to be maintained by the dog owner. This liability insurance or surety bond shall be in the amount of at least one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the landlord who rents to the owner of each vicious animal against liability for personal injuries or property damage inflicted by the vicious animal.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-83. - Proper enclosure for potentially vicious or vicious animal.
(a)
Every owner of any animal declared a potentially vicious or a vicious animal shall at all times maintain for said animal a proper enclosure. A proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping which shall keep an animal while on the owner's or landlord's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed or locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of younger children and designed to prohibit the dog from escaping said enclosure. Any such enclosure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and if the animal is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom and top of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the animal from escaping either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the animal.
(b)
At any time that a vicious or potentially vicious animal is not confined in a proper enclosure as described herein, the animal shall be muzzled in such a manner as to prevent it from biting or injuring any person or animal, and kept on a leash with the owner or custodian in attendance having immediate physical control of the leash.
(c)
An exception to this article is hereby provided for any potentially vicious or vicious animal being attended to by a veterinary professional, or while participating in any lawful animal show, contest or exhibition.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-84. - Mandatory spaying/neutering.
(a)
Subject to the express exceptions provided in subsection (2) below, it shall be unlawful to keep, harbor or maintain in the city any unspayed or unneutered animal declared a vicious animal that is over four (4) months of age.
(b)
The prohibition set forth in subsection (1) above shall not apply if a veterinarian licensed in this state shall certify in writing that a specific animal declared a potentially vicious or vicious animal is medically unfit to undergo the required spay or neuter procedure because of a physical condition which would be substantially aggravated by such procedure or would likely cause the animal's death. Such writing shall also include a description of the likely duration of the physical condition which prohibits the procedure. As soon as such condition ceases to exist, it shall be the duty of the owner or person who harbors or keeps such animal to promptly comply with this article.
(c)
Failure to comply with this code section of this article may result in the unsprayed/unneutered animal declared a potentially vicious or a vicious animal being seized and impounded at an authorized animal shelter and retained for ten (10) days, during which time the owner may reclaim the animal upon proof of arrangements to comply with this article. Should the owner fail to make and give proper notice of the arrangements to comply with this article within ten (10) days from impoundment, the animal shall be destroyed.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-85. - Impoundment.
(a)
Any dog not properly insured, licensed, vaccinated or not wearing a collar with the proper information stated earlier in this article, found abandoned, or not under restraint, may be seized and impounded by the proper authority. Dogs found not on a restraint may be released to the owner if the owner can be reasonably found and time permits with only a warning or citation, depending on the circumstances.
(b)
Animals impounded will be transported to an animal shelter under contract with the City of West Point to be disposed of (adoption, euthanized, etc.) pursuant to the policies of that shelter.
(c)
An owner, keeper, custodian, or person redeeming a currently licensed animal from the authorized animal shelter shall furnish proof of current rabies vaccination and current license specifically applicable to the animal being redeemed. Such owner, keeper, custodian, or responsible person shall also present to the animal shelter an authorization for release issued by the city clerk at the time the authorization issued is redeemed. Upon presentation of the authorization for release and upon payment of the boarding charges to the animal shelter by the owner, keeper, custodian, or responsible person, the animal may be released to the owner or guardian of the impounded animal. A release fee of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) must be paid to the city clerk, or his or her representative, prior to the release.
(d)
A dog for which a license is required, but whose license has expired, a currently unlicensed dog, defined as a dog wearing one (1) or both of its required tags, but at least one (1) has expired, or a stray dog may be released from impoundment to its owner under the following conditions:
(1)
The owner must pay the regular boarding charge to the facility where the animal is confined.
(2)
If the animal has a city license, but does not have a current rabies vaccination, the owner must take the animal to a licensed veterinarian or his authorized representative for the vaccination.
(3)
The owner must then show proof of the vaccination to the animal control officer, rabies control officer, the health officer, or the city clerk.
(4)
If the owner has a current rabies vaccination, but is not required to have city license, the owner only has to show proof of the vaccination to the city clerk.
(e)
Stray animals that are impounded will be housed at the animal shelter for a maximum of seven (7) days. Any stray not claimed after seven (7) days may be euthanized at the discretion of the animal shelter. If an animal is claimed by the owner, prior to being euthanized, the owner will be responsible for all fees associated with the housing and care of the animal or animals.
(f)
Vicious animals or potentially vicious animals that are impounded will be housed at the animal shelter for a maximum of twenty-one (21) days. Any vicious or potentially vicious animal not claimed after twenty-one (21) days may be euthanized at the discretion of the animal shelter. If a vicious or potentially vicious animal is claimed by the owner prior to being euthanized, the owner will be responsible for all fees associated with the housing and care of the animal or animals.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-86. - Fines.
(a)
Animal control officers, city police officers, deputy sheriffs, and/or marshals are empowered to issue citations to the owners, keepers, custodians, or persons responsible for animals when such owners, keepers, custodians, or persons responsible are in violation of this article. Upon an adjudication that such owners, keepers, custodians, or persons responsible are in violation hereof, the following minimum fines shall be assessed:
(1)
The owner, keeper, custodian, or person responsible for dogs not licensed, vaccinated, or not wearing a collar, running at large, or is declared a nuisance shall be fined with the fine for the first violation being fifty dollars ($50.00), the fine for the second violation being one hundred fifty-four dollars ($154.00), the fine for the third violation being two hundred forty-eight dollars ($248.00). After a third violation, the animal may be declared a habitual nuisance by the animal control officer or chief of police, and the owner, keeper, custodian, or responsible person shall be required to permanently remove the animal from the city.
(2)
The owner, keeper, custodian, or person responsible for vicious or potentially vicious animals not properly insured shall be fined with the fine for the first violation being two hundred forty-eight dollars ($248.00), the fine for the second violation being five hundred dollars ($500.00) and a third violation being five hundred dollars ($500.00) and the confiscation of the dog.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-87. - Injured animal.
If an animal is struck and injured by a vehicle or is suffering from another cause and it is deemed by a licensed veterinarian, animal control officer, police officer, deputy sheriff, or marshal to be beyond help, the animal may be humanely destroyed without a period of confinement. In such cases, it shall be the responsibility of the animal's owner to dispose of the animal's carcass in a manner that meets state and federal health regulations. If the owner of the animal cannot be determined, the city shall be responsible for properly disposing of the carcass.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-88. - Animal bite.
Any person bitten or mauled by an animal shall notify the police department for the completion of an incident report. Attempts will be made to locate and capture the animal involved in the bite. If captured, the animal shall be required to be quarantined for a period of at least ten (10) days in a proper enclosure as defined in this ordinance or other enclosure that will not allow the animal contact with humans or other animals. If the chief of police, animal control officer, county marshal, or other law enforcement officer has reason to believe that it is in the best interest of the city for the animal to be housed at a veterinary clinic or other facility, any cost for this housing and care will be the responsibility of the owner. Such cost must be paid prior to the animal's release from such care.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-89. - Potentially vicious or vicious animals off premises of owner, etc.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep or harbor a potentially vicious or a vicious animal in the City of West Point, Georgia unless all provisions of this article are met.
(b)
Any potentially vicious or vicious animal whose owner, keeper, custodian, or responsible person is not in compliance with this article or found off the premises of its owner, keeper, or custodian, may be seized by any police officer, animal control officer, deputy, marshal, or any other authorized agent of the City of West Point and, upon establishing the vicious character of the animal by a court of competent jurisdiction, the animal may be destroyed or otherwise disposed of by state statutes, with the owner being liable for all costs of boarding, treatment, and disposal of the animal.
(c)
This section shall not apply to animals under the control of law enforcement or military agencies, nor to guard dogs, which are kept for the protection of property by licensed commercial establishments; provided the animal is restrained by an adequate cage or fence (excluding electronic fences) preventing contact with general public or any persons who enter the premises with the actual or implied consent of the owner or keeper of the animal.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-90. - Penalties.
Except for warnings described in this article and specific fines set forth above, any person violating the provisions of this article shall be subject to a fine of not less than fifty dollars ($50.00) or more than five hundred dollars ($500.00) and/or imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days, plus court cost, as well as the cost of boarding, treatment, and disposal of the animal where applicable.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-91. - Animal control board.
The mayor and council shall create an animal control board of sufficient number of persons to hear, and act upon, hearings of animal control issues.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-92. - Hearings.
(a)
The chief of police of the City of West Point, or his or her designee (such designee specifically to include, but not be limited to the animal control officer), is hereby designated as the person responsible for determining when:
(1)
A dog is a potentially vicious animal;
(2)
A dog is a vicious animal;
(3)
When a dog is a nuisance;
(4)
A domestic animal is a stray animal;
(5)
A domestic animal is an injured animal;
(6)
Any animal's behavior or condition is in violation of any provision of this article; or
(7)
When any owner, keeper, custodian or responsible person for an animal is in violation of this article.
(b)
Any determination by the chief of police, or his or her designee, of any of the conditions or violations set forth above which will or may result in the destruction or euthanasia of the animal, shall, when feasible or possible, be communicated to the owner, keeper, or custodian of the animal.
(c)
An appeal of the determination of the chief of police, or his or her designee, shall be to the animal control board. Such appeal must be in writing and must be received by the city clerk within two (2) business days of receipt by the owner, keeper, or custodian of the determination by the chief of police, or his or her designee.
(d)
The animal control board shall schedule an appeal proceeding to hear the evidence with regard to the determination made by the chief of police, or his or her designee, and to decide the legitimacy of that determination. Such appeal shall be heard within ten (10) business days of the receipt of such written appeal. The animal control board shall adopt, reject, or modify, in its discretion, the determination of the chief of police, or his or her designee. Such decision of the animal control board shall be final.
(e)
Any person eligible to file an appeal hereunder may sign a waiver of appeal if so desired. In such case, if euthanasia would be the final result of the determination by the chief of police, or his or her designee, the animal may be euthanized and no additional costs will accrue to the owner, keeper, or custodian.
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
Sec. 4-93. - Compliance.
(a)
Animals will not be released from confinement until proof of vaccination is demonstrated.
(b)
Persons advised of violations of the following violations will be provided thirty (30) days to come within compliance:
(1)
The necessity for insurance.
(2)
Tethering of an animal.
(3)
Restriction on the number of dogs.
(4)
Licensing.
(5)
Proper enclosure.
(6)
Mandatory spaying or neutering (vicious dog only).
(Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1(Exh. A), 8-9-10)
