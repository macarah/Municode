ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Generally.
For the mutual protection and benefit of every lot purchaser in the city cemetery, the rules and regulations included in this chapter are adopted. All property owners within the cemetery and all lots hereafter to be sold shall be subject to this chapter.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(intro. ¶))
Sec. 18-2. - Opening graves.
(a)
A request in writing is required of the lot owner or authorized funeral director before a grave is to be opened, giving the name and location of the grave in reference to the lot. The name of the individual or firm and the approximate time of the grave opening shall be given.
(b)
A request in writing is required for any type work or improvements done on any lot. This request must be filed at city hall before any type of work is begun. The request should state the nature of the work, the lot owner, the name of the company or individual doing work, and the date and time the work is to begin. A signed permit will be issued by the city authorizing approved work. Any person doing any work in the cemetery without written approval by the city shall be subject to a penalty or fine in the amount of $25.00.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(1))
Sec. 18-3. - Disposal of waste.
Excess dirt and waste in the cemetery will be disposed of at sites to be determined by an official of the city. No dirt will be allowed to be placed along side any fencing, walks or roads unless approval is given by an official of the city.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(2))
Sec. 18-4. - Plantings.
No shrubs, trees and herbage, except grass, may be planted in the cemetery unless authorized by the city.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(3))
Sec. 18-5. - Memorials.
No memorial, such as family marker, grave marker, slab or corner post, shall be placed in the cemetery either on a grave or lot unless it is made of concrete, marble, granite or bronze. Corner posts or nameplates shall be placed flush with the finished grade of the surrounding ground.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(4))
Sec. 18-6. - Driving trucks on lots.
All trucks, such as used by gravediggers, monument erectors, and other workers, driving on cemetery lots must have some support under their wheels before driving onto lots.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(5))
Sec. 18-7. - Workers' responsibility for cleanup.
Any person doing any type of work such as gravedigging, monument erecting, coping installation, slab installation, and similar activities, shall be responsible for the cleaning and removal of any waste, broken concrete, slabs, dirt, and other materials and disposal of waste material in a proper place and not in the cemetery.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(6))
Sec. 18-8. - Care of inside lots.
The neatness and proper care of inside lots in the cemetery bordered by coping shall be the responsibility of the lot owner; and the owner shall keep the inside lot cut and free of high grass, weeds, shrubs, and other growth.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-1(7))
Sec. 18-9. - Burial vault.
It is unlawful for any person to place or cause to be placed in the cemetery any burial vault that is not placed at least 12 inches below the surface of the ground.
(Code 1980, § 4-3-2)
Sec. 18-10. - Maintenance.
The City of Vienna Cemetery on Woodward Street has been well maintained and unkempt for many years. There is historic significance within the Vienna City Cemetery, and in an effort to pertain and record this history, and a continuous effort to maintain the cemetery, an opening/closing grave fee will be administered and collected from any one performing activities within the cemetery.
(1)
Any person doing any type of work in the Vienna City Cemetery must notify city hall. A signed permit will be issued by the city authorizing work. All funeral homes are subject to an opening and closing assessment of $100.00 to be payable before services.
(2)
All vault companies not obtaining a permit under a funeral home will be required to obtain a permit from the City of Vienna of $50.00. This fee will be due before any work of admittance into city cemetery.
(3)
Any person doing work without approval will be subject to a penalty or fine.
(4)
No shrubs or trees may be planted in the cemetery. All existing shrubs and trees will be removed. No walking or vehicle traffic should be in the cemetery after dark.
(5)
All work performed within the city cemetery should be done according to city ordinance. A copy will be provided as requested.
(Ord. of 7-8-02)
Sec. 18-11. - Lot rates.
Be it ordained by the City Council of Vienna and it is hereby ordained by the authority thereof that the following rates shall be adopted as the cemetery lot rates for the Vienna Cemetery to become effective July 9, 2002:
One Grave lot: $150.00
(Ord. of 7-8-02)
Secs. 18-12—18-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - NORTHWEST SECTION OF THE VIENNA CITY CEMETERY[2]

Sec. 18-31. - Generally.
For the mutual protection and benefit of every lot purchaser in the Northwest Section of the city cemetery, the rules and regulations included in this chapter are adopted. All property owners and visitors within the cemetery and all lots hereafter to be sold shall be subject to this chapter formulated and adopted by the Mayor and City Council of the City of Vienna.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-32. - Allotment of space.
No allotment of space in the city cemeteries shall be made except upon application to the city administrator, signed by the person in whose name the allotment is to be made. Every such application shall contain a stipulation to the effect that, in consideration of the allotment of the space so applied for, the applicant shall abide by all the rules and regulations made and hereafter enacted by the city council for the management and control of cemeteries. Every allotment of space in the city cemeteries shall be deemed to be made subject to the condition that all such rules and regulations shall be complied with.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-33. - Sale of lots generally.
The city administrator/city clerk shall be responsible for the plat for each cemetery, and shall sell the lots and graves at such price as set by the mayor and council. Giving a receipt thereof to the purchaser, specifying the number and location thereof, and enter the same on his/her records. Such receipt and entry shall give the purchaser a title to the lot or grave, subject to such regulations as the council may establish for the government of the cemetery. All grave spaces or lots in city-designated cemeteries shall be sold to any and all persons regardless of race, creed, color, nationality or religious belief.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-34. - Interments, disinterments and cremains.
(a)
Interments and disinterments.
(1)
Properties shall not allow interments to be made in their lots for remuneration, nor shall any transfer or assignment of any lot or of any interest in a lot be valid without the written consent of the city.
(2)
No disinterments shall be allowed except by consent of the city, and upon the written order of the owners of the lot.
(3)
No lot shall be used for any other purpose than for the burial of the human dead.
(b)
Cremains.
(1)
In the event of cremated (urn) remains:
a.
Only one cremated (urn) remains may be buried in one grave space if that grave space at the time of burial contains one coffin remains.
b.
No more than two cremated (urn) remains may be buried in one grave space. If the grave space contains two cremated (urn) remains no other adult remains can be buried in the grave space.
c.
If one cremated (urn) remains is buried in the grave space only one adult coffin remains can be buried in the grave space.
(2)
The owner of the grave space in the Northwest Section of the cemetery may provide for and/or install a ground-flush pillow grave marker or a ground-flush foot marker for the cremated remains.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-35. - Grave site opening: permit required.
(a)
A request in writing is required of the lot owner or authorized funeral director before a grave is to be opened, giving the name and location of the grave in reference to the lot. A permit fee of $100.00 and an opening and closing fee of $225.00 will be due and payable to the City of Vienna before any grave in the Northwest Section shall be opened by the city's contracted agent.
(b)
The burial permit shall be obtained a minimum of 24 hours in advance of said site opening. Said notice may be waived by the city administrator in extenuating circumstances.
(c)
All funeral homes are required to verify that all fees have been submitted to the City of Vienna. Graves shall be opened and closed by the contracted agent of the city and shall conform to all applicable state laws. Only the city's contracted agent shall open and close any graves in the Northwest Section of the Vienna City Cemetery.
(d)
Under no circumstances shall any party attempt to open a grave site prior to proper identification by the City of Vienna.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-36. - Grave opening.
(a)
Dimensions. All gravesites shall have an opening sufficient in size to accommodate the vault.
(b)
Groundcover. All graves shall be dug to a depth sufficient to allow a minimum groundcover of 12 inches.
(c)
Soil removal methods. In no event shall the removal of excess material result in the damage to surrounding lots, nor cause ruts to develop, nor cause damage to surrounding monuments, etc. In the event of such damage, the person, firm or corporation responsible shall pay to the City of Vienna its cost of repair of such damage.
(d)
Soil compaction. All gravesites opened shall have the soil returned to the site compacted to prevent settling of such soil after completion of the burial.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-37. - Disposal of waste.
All excess soil removed from a gravesite shall be deposited at a site designated by the city public works director. No dirt will be allowed to be placed beside any fencing, walks or roads within the cemetery.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-38. - Plantings.
(a)
All grading, landscape work, and improvements of any kind, and care of the cemetery, and all trees and shrubs moved and herbage of any kind shall be done by the city. Permanent planting of shrubs and flowers are to be made by the city.
(b)
The whole cemetery is to be landscaped, and to keep it uniform it is necessary that all plantings be under the control of the city including the following additional care: removal of rubbish when necessary, raking and cutting grass, trimming of trees and shrubbery and other care that the city may deem necessary. The city herewith retains supervision of all lots in the city cemeteries and upon hearing before the city council, the city may prohibit, modify or cause to be removed, any structure, object or improvement which has been placed subsequent to the adoption of these rules and regulations, and which may be considered objectionable, unsightly or injurious to the lot upon which the same are located, adjoining lots, or the cemetery in general.
(1)
The city may remove cut flowers, pot plants, and the containers and stands for the same, when the flowers or plants have become unsightly. For the purpose of this subsection "unsightly" is defined as applying to cut flowers or pot plants when the same have shattered blooms, wilted or dead leaves and blooms, bleached or discolored leaves and colored foliage, to the extent of one-fourth of the original total of the same. Nevertheless, the removal of cut flowers, pot plants, containers, and stands for the same such lots by the city, as hereinbefore set forth, is hereby authorized.
(2)
The city may remove artificial flowers, silk flowers and foliage, artificial shrubs, pictures, stuffed animals, bric-a-brac, memorabilia, and similar items whenever the same become water-logged, scattered, discolored or damaged.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-39. - Memorials.
(a)
No above ground slab (also known as ledger) will be permitted in this portion of Vienna City Cemetery named as the Northwest Section further designated as Blocks A - J. Bronze, granite or marble ledgers (pillows) will be allowed in Blocks A - J, if they are flush with ground level. The dimensions of the slab (ledger, pillow) shall be at least one foot by two feet but not to exceed one foot by three feet.
(b)
Notwithstanding the above provisions of this section, no above ground slab shall be permitted in the section of the Vienna City Cemetery, currently known as Northwest Cemetery (newest cemetery section), Blocks A - J.
(1)
No coping, curbing, fencing, hedging, grave mounds, borders or enclosures of any kind shall be allowed around any lot in the Northwest Section Blocks A - J.
(2)
No loose gravel shall be allowed on any lot the Northwest Section Blocks A - J.
(3)
The city reserves the right to remove such gravel if so placed.
(c)
All gravesites must be marked within 90 days of burial with markers made of bronze, granite or marble markers and all should be flush with the ground level. The dimensions of the marker (pillow) shall be at least one foot by two feet but not to exceed one foot by three feet.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-40. - Burial of veterans.
(a)
All of the burial lots lying, being and situated in the Northwest Section Block number A - Vienna City Cemetery, are hereby set aside and reserved as a burial ground for honorably discharged deceased veterans of the Armed Forces of the United States, subject to all provisions, limitations and restrictions contained in this chapter, and such lots are hereby dedicated to such purposes.
(b)
The full and complete title to and the right to the control of the lots set aside for burial of veterans is hereby retained in the city and the burial of deceased veterans therein shall be a privilege only, and no person shall acquire any vested right or title to any portion of such lots, excepting only the privilege of burying a deceased veteran therein subject to the restrictions and limitations provided in this chapter.
(c)
When the family or next of kin of any honorably deceased veteran eligible for burial in that portion of the cemetery hereby set aside shall desire the privilege of burying such deceased veteran in such burial ground, the next of kin shall make application to the city administrator/clerk for the privilege of such burial. All such applications shall be in writing, shall give such information as may be required as to the age, birthplace, service in the Armed Forces of the United States, and the dates of such service of such deceased veterans, the kind and character of the discharge received by such deceased veteran upon the termination of his service and any other information called for or required. When required, the next of kin shall furnish proof of such service. If the city clerk is satisfied that such deceased veteran is entitled to such privilege of burial, he shall thereupon issue a permit for such burial and shall designate the portion of such burial ground in which such grave shall be located, which shall be entered on such permit. If the required application, the information called for and proof of service of such deceased veteran is not made and furnished, or if the city clerk is not satisfied that such deceased veteran is entitled to the privilege of such burial, no permit shall be issued.
(d)
All graves located in the burial ground reserved for veterans shall be uniform and all (pillow) ledgers placed at the head of such grave shall be of bronze, marble or granite. No monuments, markers or memorials shall be erected upon or near any grave in such burial ground excepting only that a flat headstone of the type furnished by the United States of America for the graves of deceased veterans may be placed at the head of such grave. Neither the family, nor next of kin of any deceased veteran buried in such burial ground shall have the right to place any other type of slab over or erect any other type of monument or headstone at the head of or near any such grave.
(e)
No burial site located in the veteran's memorial section shall have corner markers and/or any other monument/marker than the designated bronze, marble or granite markers placed flush with ground level.
(f)
All improvements, plantings and beautification by private persons in the area of the cemetery reserved for burial of veterans must be approved by the city administrator/clerk. The city administrator/clerk may remove violating improvements and plantings.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-41. - Driving of trucks and/or equipment on lots.
All trucks and equipment, such as used by grave diggers, monument erectors, and other workers, driving on cemetery lots must have some support under their wheels before driving onto lots.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-42. - Workers' responsibility for cleanup.
Any person doing any type of work such as grave-digging, monument erecting, and similar activities, shall be responsible for the cleaning and removal of any waste, broken concrete, slabs, dirt, and other materials and disposal of such waste material in a proper place and not in the cemetery.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-43. - Care of inside lots.
(a)
The neatness and proper care of the lots in the Northwest Cemetery shall be the responsibility of the contracted agent of the City of Vienna. The contracted agent of the City of Vienna will mow the grass on the lots, but will not weed the areas around any flower vases.
(b)
The city public works department may prune, remove or transplant any tree, shrub, plant or flower growing upon any lot when, in its discretion, the maintenance may be necessary or shall add to the general appearance in the plan of a city-designated cemetery.
(c)
The city shall have the right to install, maintain, operate, alter and repair pipe lines or gutters for sprinkler systems, drainage and landscape purposes, as well as the right to use land outside actual burial plots for public cemetery purposes other than for burial.
(d)
No person shall cut, prune, remove or transplant any flower, shrub, tree or plant from any section without permission from the city administrator or authorized city employees.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-44. - Burial vaults.
(a)
In order to maintain a high standard of care, a grave vault made of a permanent-type material such as natural stone, metal, concrete or fiberglass shall be placed in a grave so that the top of the vault will not be less than 12 inches below ground level at any point so as to eliminate unsightly and/or dead spots in grass sod.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury a person in the City of Vienna Cemetery in a steel vault less than five feet from the top of the ground to the bottom of the grave or vault; and in a concrete/cement vault no person shall be buried less than four feet from the top of the ground to the bottom of the vault.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-45. - Maintenance.
(a)
The City of Vienna Cemetery has been well maintained for many years. All grading, landscape work and improvements of any kind shall be performed only by the City of Vienna personnel or approved contractors of the City of Vienna.
(b)
There is an historic significance within the Vienna City Cemetery, and in an effort to pertain and record this history, and in a continuous effort to maintain the cemetery, an opening/closing grave assessment fee will be administered and collected.
(c)
Any person doing any type of work in the Vienna City Cemetery must notify City Hall. A signed permit will be issued by the city authorizing work.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-46. - Signs, notices or advertisements.
No sign, notice or advertisement of any kind will be permitted upon any part of the grounds of the cemeteries, except those erected by the City of Vienna in connection with the operation of the cemeteries.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-47. - Cemetery hours.
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter the City of Vienna Cemetery between the hours of sunset and sunrise.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-48. - Speed of vehicles.
No person shall drive a vehicle within the confines of the cemeteries at a speed in excess of 15 miles per hour.
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Sec. 18-49. - Lot rates.
Be it ordained by the Mayor and City Council of the City of Vienna and it is hereby ordained by the authority thereof, that the following rates shall be adopted as the cemetery lot rate for all of the City of Vienna Cemeteries to become effective, June 1, 2014.
•
One grave lot: $500.00
•
Two graves lot: $1,000.00
•
Four graves lot: $2,000.00 (small family plot)
•
Eight graves lot: $4,000.00 (large family plot)
(Ord. of 6-9-14(2))
Secs. 18-50—18-75. - Reserved.
