Chapter 49 - PERSONNEL

Sec. 49-1. - Personnel handbook.
The city shall establish a personnel handbook for all municipal employees, which shall be entitled and referred to as the City of Demorest Employee Handbook. The manual shall be initially established by vote of the mayor and city council. Amendments and updates to the handbook following its initial enactment may be made by the city manager from time to time as required by federal and state laws and the needs of the city, however, the mayor and council shall retain the ultimate right to amend the personnel handbook in their discretion. In addition to other sections deemed necessary by the mayor and city council, the handbook shall include policies regarding equal employment opportunity and non-discrimination and non-harassment on the basis of age, color, disability, gender, genetic information, national origin, race, and any other protected characteristic provided by law in the context of employment opportunities. The handbook also shall specify a complaint procedure for employees to report allegations or concerns of discrimination or harassment, and shall prohibit retaliation against any employee for making such a report or otherwise engaging in protected activity. The city manager shall ensure that all employees and officials are provided a copy of the employee handbook.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, § 1, 3-6-2012)
Sec. 49-2. - Employment at will.
The City of Demorest is an "at will" employer, and does not utilize a civil service system. This chapter, the provisions of the employee handbook, and any policies and procedures established by the city are intended to serve as guides for assisting in the administration of personnel policies and procedures and ensure compliance with state and federal employment and wage and hour laws, but shall not be binding on the city and can be amended, applied, interpreted, suspended, or terminated by the city in its discretion as set forth herein. Neither this chapter, the employee handbook, nor any other policies and procedures of the city relating to personnel, employment, or wages shall create a contractual or property interest or expectancy for employees of continued employment, benefits, pay, or employment conditions at any time.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, § 1, 3-6-2012)
Sec. 49-3. - Job descriptions.
The city shall maintain job descriptions containing specifications for each position held by one or more municipal employee. Job descriptions shall describe the duties of the position, the qualifications necessary, the licenses required, the supervisor to whom the position reports, the expected salary range, the essential functions of each position, the physical and mental requirements necessary to perform the essential functions of each position, and such other information as is necessary to recruit effectively for each municipal position. Job descriptions and the specifications therein may be amended from time to time by the city council, pursuant to § 3.10(a) of the City Charter, and should be so amended in order to maintain up to date job descriptions.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, § 1, 3-6-2012)
Sec. 49-4. - Benefits.
The city shall establish and provide for such benefits as it sees fit in order to attract and retain qualified individuals for its workforce. Information concerning the benefits available, including eligibility for benefits and the cost to employees, shall be found in the personnel manual. The city shall be authorized to contract with benefits providers for the purpose of providing insurance, retirement, pension plans, and other benefits deemed necessary by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, § 1, 3-6-2012)
Sec. 49-5. - Records.
The city shall keep such records and make such reports as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, § 1, 3-6-2012)
Sec. 49-6. - Equal employment opportunity.
The city recognizes that each employee and applicant for employment is important to the successful functioning of city governmental operations. Therefore, it is the policy of the city to provide equal employment opportunities without regard to age, color, disability, gender, genetic information, national origin, race, veteran or familial status, or any other characteristic protected by law. This policy relates to all aspects of employment, including but not limited to recruitment, hiring, placement, promotion, demotion, discipline, compensation, transfer, layoff, recall from layoff, benefits, and all other aspects of employment by the city. The city also does not condone, and specifically prohibits, harassment of employees by other employees, supervisors, or officials on the basis of any of the above-referenced protected characteristics. In accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act, the city will make reasonable accommodations for disabled employees and applicants for employment.
The office of the city manager shall publicize this policy throughout the city's operational and employment areas by providing all employees and officials with a copy of the employee handbook and by such other means as is deemed necessary, so as to make it known to all officials and employees of the city. The office of the city manager shall also ensure that all employees and officials have access to posters published by the Department of Labor or the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission regarding adherence by employers to statutes such as the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act, the Drug Free Workplace Act of 1988, the Equal Pay Act of 1963, the Fair Labor Standards Act, and any other posters required by federal or state employment laws and subsequent enactments.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, § 1, 3-6-2012)
Sec. 49-7. - Administration.
The city manager shall be responsible for administering the personnel policies and procedures contained in the City Charter, this chapter, and the employee handbook, and for maintaining all personnel files and records. As part of his/her duties, the city manager shall have the responsibility of making all decisions regarding hiring, firing and disciplining all non-department head employees. The mayor and council retain the authority for making hiring decisions regarding department heads pursuant to § 3.10(e) of the City Charter. Likewise, pursuant to this section of the City Charter, the city manager has authority for terminating or suspending department heads.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, § 1, 3-6-2012)
