Sec. 6-26. - Occupation tax required for business dealings.
(a)
For the year 1996 and succeeding years thereafter, each person engaged in any business, trade, profession, or occupation in the unincorporated part of Harris County, whether with a location in the county or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in the state, but exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax for said business, trade, profession, or occupation; which tax and any applicable registration shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in the county. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the county, such business tax registration shall be shown to any proper agent of the county, upon his/her request.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-27. - Construction of terms; definitions.
(a)
Wherever the term "county" or "Harris County" is used herein, it shall be construed to mean the unincorporated part of Harris County, Georgia.
(b)
As used in this article, the term:
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
Dominant line means the type of business, within a multiple-line business, that the greatest amount of income is derived from.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form I.R.S. W-2 but not a form I.R.S. 1099.
Location or location of office. A location shall be deemed to include any structure or vehicle where a business, profession or occupation is conducted; any part or operation of a business or practitioner conducted within a one-mile radius of where the main or dominant activity is performed by said business or practitioner. It shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office.
Occupation tax (formerly known as "business licenses") means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession, or business for revenue raising purposes.
Person shall be held to include sole proprietors, corporations, partnerships, nonprofits, or any other form of business organization.
Practitioner of profession or occupation is one who by state law requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation, including but not limited to those individuals listed in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18), but does not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
Regulatory fees means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, which are required by a local government as an exercise of its police power and as a part of or an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession, or business. The amount of a regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the county. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee. Development impact fees as defined by O.C.G.A. § 36-71-2(8) or other costs or conditions of zoning or land development are not regulatory fees.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-28. - Regulatory fee structure.
A regulatory fee is authorized for collection by the county as provided under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9 on those applicable businesses. A regulatory fee imposed under this section shall not include an administrative fee. The regulatory fee structure shall become effective as such time as the county acts to initiate regulation of certain appropriate businesses and/or practitioners.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-29. - Occupation tax levied; restrictions.
(a)
An occupation tax shall be levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices in the unincorporated part of the county and/or upon the applicable out-of-state businesses with no location or office in the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7 based upon the number of employees of the business or practitioner.
(b)
The occupation tax schedule for number of employees is set out below. The tax rate is determined by the number of employees for each business, trade, profession, or occupation. The number of employees of the business or practitioner is computed on a full-time position basis or full-time position equivalent basis, provided that for the purposes of this computation, an employee who works 40 hours or more weekly shall be considered a full-time employee and that the average weekly hours of employees who work less than 40 hours weekly shall be added and such sum shall be divided by 40 to produce full-time position equivalents. A business or practitioner with more than one type of service, operation or product at the same location shall total the number of employees in computing its tax liability under this article. In addition, a business or practitioner with no employees is taxed per the following schedule. The occupation tax schedule will be developed and updated from time to time by the county department of community development or the board of commissioners.

Sec. 6-30. - Paying occupation tax of business with no location in state.
Registration and assessment of an occupation tax is hereby imposed on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the state if the business's largest dollar volume of business in the state is in Harris County and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of the county for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of the county.
This limitation shall only apply when the business or practitioner has provided to the county satisfactory proof of the applicability of this section.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-31. - Dominant line of business to be identified on business registration.
The business registration of each business operated in the local government's jurisdiction shall identify the dominant line of business that the business conducts.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-32. - Number of businesses considered to be operating in the county.
Where a person conducts business at more than one fixed location, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-33. - Professionals as classified in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18).
Practitioners of professions are described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18) shall elect as their entire occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax is based on the number of employees.
(2)
A fee of $250.00 per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the practitioner's office or location; provided, however, that a practitioner paying according to this paragraph shall not be required to provide information to the local government relating to the number of employees of the business or practitioner. The per-practitioner fee applies to each person in the business who qualifies as a practitioner under the state's regulatory guidelines and framework.
(3)
This election is to be made on an annual basis and must be done by February 15 of each year.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-34. - Practitioners exclusively practicing for a government.
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, the state, a county of the state, instrumentalities of the United States, the state, or a county of the state shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that purpose.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-35. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-2 to 48-13-28. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-36. - When tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
Each such occupation tax shall be for the calendar year 1996 and succeeding calendar years thereafter unless otherwise specifically provided. Said registration and occupation tax shall be payable November 15 of each year, for the upcoming and ensuing calendar year and shall, if not paid by January 1 of each year, be subject to penalties for delinquency and interest as prescribed in this article. When any person commences business on or after July 1 in any year, the occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax imposed for the entire year. On any new profession, trade, or calling begun in the county after January 1, 1996, or succeeding years thereafter, the registration and tax shall become due and payable upon the commencement of business in the county and, if not paid within 90 days of when said tax became due and payable, shall become delinquent, subjecting the practitioner or business liable for penalties and interest as prescribed in this article. The tax registration herein provided for shall be issued by the office of the department of community development and if any person, firm, or corporation whose duty it is to obtain a registration shall, after said registration or occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in the county, any of the kind of profession, trade, or calling subject to this article without having first obtained said registration, such offender shall, upon conviction by the magistrate judge, be punished by a fine of not less than $75.00 and not to exceed $500.00 or imprisonment not to exceed 60 days, either or both in the discretion of the magistrate judge.
(b)
In addition to the above remedies, the county, through a proper agent, including but not limited to the county attorney, may proceed to collect in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95; Ord. No. 06-00, 8-1-2000)
Sec. 6-37. - Information requested by county of businesses and practitioners.
(a)
A business or practitioner doing business in the unincorporated part of the county shall submit, if requested by the director of community development, county clerk, county attorney or other proper agent of the county, within ten days of said request, information as would reasonably reveal the site of any location or office in the county or elsewhere and, in addition, provide reasonable evidence as to the payment of occupation taxes and/or regulatory fees by the business or practitioner to other governments in the state.
(b)
Failure of a business or practitioner to comply with this section shall subject said business or practitioner to that penalty as outlined in section 6-43 of this article.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-38. - Exemption on grounds that business or organization is nonprofit.
No business or organization on which a business registration or occupation tax is levied by this article shall be exempt from said registration or tax on the ground that such business is operated as nonprofit, unless said business can provide sufficient evidence (i.e. current determination letter) that said business or organization is properly recognized by the Internal Revenue Service as nonprofit.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-39. - Evidence of state registration required if applicable; state registration to be displayed.
(a)
Each person who is licensed by the secretary of state pursuant to O.C.G.A. title 43 shall provide evidence of proper and current state licensure before the county registration may be issued.
(b)
Each person who is licensed by the state shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the licensee's place of business and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-40. - Evidence of qualification required if applicable.
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency, or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of a county business registration, show evidence that such requirements have been met.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-41. - Liability of officers and agents; registration required; failure to obtain.
All persons subject to the occupation tax levy pursuant to this article shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for said business as described in this article, and in default thereof the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a registration. Every person commencing business in the county after January 1 or each year shall likewise obtain the registration herein provided for before commencing the same; and any person transacting, or offering to transact in the county, any of the kinds of business, trade, profession, or occupation without first having so obtained said registration, shall be subject to penalties and interest provided herein.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-42. - When registration and tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
Each such registration shall be for the upcoming and ensuing calendar year in which the registration was obtained unless otherwise specifically provided. There is hereby imposed a penalty upon each business which fails to apply for and obtain an appropriate business registration and pay all tax and fees for the upcoming and ensuing calendar year as provided herein before January 1, and by January 1 each year hereafter. A penalty for delinquency and interest is set out in section 6-45 of this article. Every person commencing business in the county after January 1 of each year shall obtain the registration required before commencing such business. Any person transacting or offering to transact in the county any business, trade, profession, or occupation without first having obtained said registration shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 6-43. Said penalties and interest shall be in addition to all other penalties, civil and criminal herein provided; and may be collected by the remedies herein provided for collection of the occupation tax, and shall have the same lien and priority as the occupation tax to which the penalty and interest is applied.
(b)
The registration herein provided for shall be issued by the office of the department of community development, and if any person, firm, or corporation whose duty it is to obtain a registration shall, after said occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in Harris County, any of the kind of business, trade, profession, or occupation without having first obtained said registration, such offender shall be subject to the penalties and interest provided herein.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95; Ord. No. 06-00, § 1, 8-1-2000)
Sec. 6-42.5. - Issuance of license contingent upon payment of all fees and taxes.
(a)
No license, occupation tax, registration or franchise of any type authorized and issued by the board of commissioners shall be issued to an otherwise qualified applicant unless the applicant has paid in full all accrued, outstanding or delinquent real or personal property taxes, licenses, occupational tax or franchise tax due at the date of the application for a license, occupation or registration of a franchise or renewal thereof.
(b)
No license, occupation tax, registration or franchise of any type authorized and issued by the board of commissioners shall be issued unless all accrued, outstanding or delinquent real or personal property taxes on the location as defined herein to be occupied by the applicant/licensee are paid in full, notwithstanding that the applicant/licensee does not own the real property, structure or vehicle, but is a lessee, occupant or tenant thereon. Location as used herein shall mean any real property, structure or vehicle where a business, profession or occupation is conducted.
(c)
For the purpose of this article, any tax that has been paid, formally appealed to the proper authorities are is being paid pursuant to a plan authorized and approved by the appropriate tax or revenue commissioner shall not be deemed to be delinquent.
(d)
The enforcement obligations specified hereunder shall be in addition to the enforcement provisions of any other ordinance, law or regulation. The enforcement provisions for the payment of any other taxes or licenses, occupation or other law, statute or regulation shall not be affected or diminished thereby.
(Ord. No. 04-99, §§ I, II, 12-7-99; Ord. No. 02-05, § 1, 7-19-2005)

Sec. 6-43. - Penalty for violation.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall, upon conviction before the magistrate judge, be fined in an amount of not less than $75.00 and not to exceed $500.00 or imprisonment not exceeding 60 days, either or both, in the discretion of the magistrate judge.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-44. - Businesses not covered by this article.
The following businesses are not covered by the provision of this article but may be assessed an occupation tax or other type of tax pursuant to the provisions of other general laws of the state or by local law:
(1)
Those businesses regulated by the Georgia Public Service Commission.
(2)
Those electrical service businesses organized under O.C.G.A. § 46-3-1 et seq.
(3)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including agribusiness.
(4)
Cooperative marketing associations governed by O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105.
(5)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.
(6)
Motor common carriers governed by O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15.
(7)
Those businesses governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355. (Businesses that purchase carload lots of guano, meals, meal, flour, bran, cottonseed, or cottonseed meal and hulls.)
(8)
Agricultural products and livestock raised in the state governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356.
(9)
Depository financial institutions governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(10)
Facilities operated by a charitable trust governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-55.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-45. - When occupation tax due and payable; delinquent penalty.
The amount of occupation tax shall be payable to the county, at the department of community development, on November 15 each year for the upcoming and ensuing calendar year and delinquent if not paid by January 1 each year. A penalty fee of ten percent of the total tax due will be assessed the practitioner or business liable for the tax if the occupation tax is applied for after January 1 of the year. In addition, interest shall accrue on delinquent occupation taxes at a rate of 1.5 percent per month and shall be assessed to the practitioner or business liable for the delinquent taxes.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95; Ord. No. 06-00, § 1, 8-1-2000)
Sec. 6-46. - Payment of occupation tax by newly established businesses.
In the case of a business subject to occupation tax for a calendar year, which was not conducted for any period of time in the unincorporated limits of the county in the preceding year, the owner, proprietor, manager, or executive officer of the business liable for occupation tax shall estimate the number of employees (noting the means of computation as outlined in section 6-29) from commencing date to the end of the calendar year and such tax shall be paid based on that number.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-47. - Inspections of books and records.
In any case, the director of community development, county clerk, county attorney, or other proper agents of the county may inspect the books of the business for which the returns of number of employees are made. The county's agent shall have the right to inspect the books or records for the business of which the return was made in the county, and upon demand of the proper agent of the county such books or records shall be submitted for inspection by a representative of the county within 30 days. Failure of submission of such books or records within 30 days shall be grounds for revocation of the tax registration currently existing to do business in the county. Adequate records shall be kept in the county for examination by the proper agent of the county. If, after examination of the books or records, it is determined that a deficiency occurs as a result of under reporting of the number of employees, a penalty of ten percent of the occupation tax that should have been paid, plus any deficiency, shall be assessed, along with accrued interest computed at 1.5 percent per month of the outstanding occupation tax accruing from the delinquency date of the unreported employees.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-48. - Enforcement of provisions.
It is hereby made the duty of the director of community development and the sheriff's department to see that the provisions of this article relating to occupation taxes are observed; and to summon all violators of the same to appear before the magistrate court. It is hereby made the further duty of the director of community development, sheriff, members of sheriff's department, and their assistants to inspect all registrations issued by the county, as often as in their judgment it may seem necessary to determine whether the registration held is the proper one for the business sought to be transacted thereunder.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-49. - Requirement of public hearing before tax increase.
After January 1, 1996, the board of commissioners shall conduct at least one public hearing a year when the revenue generated from occupation taxes for that particular year exceeds the revenue from occupation taxes from the preceding year. The purpose of said public hearing(s) is to be an integral part of that process of the determination by the county of how to appropriately utilize the additional revenue, pursuant to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-28.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-50. - Requirement of public hearing prior to certain action taken by county.
After January 1, 1996, the board of commissioners shall conduct at least one public hearing prior to the adoption of an ordinance or resolution regarding the occupation tax, pursuant to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-6.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 6-51. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention hereof that the specific shall control.
(Ord. No. 7-95, 12-19-95)
