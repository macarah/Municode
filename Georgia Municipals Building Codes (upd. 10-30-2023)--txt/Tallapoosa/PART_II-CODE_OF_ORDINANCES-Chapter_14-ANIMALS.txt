ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Fowl or livestock running at large prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or person in control of any domestic fowl or livestock to allow that domestic fowl or livestock to run at large within the city.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-1; Code 2005, § 14-1)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-4 et seq.
Sec. 14-2. - Impoundment of fowl or livestock running at large.
(a)
Any fowl or livestock found running at large in violation of section 14-1 shall be impounded by any officer of the police department and transported to such facility as designated by the mayor and city council.
(b)
The owner of any impounded animal, when known, shall be notified of such impoundment and of the proper steps necessary to secure release of the animal, including payment of such fees as fixed from time to time by the mayor and city council.
(c)
Animals impounded under this section shall be maintained for five days, at the end of which time they may be sold or otherwise disposed of in a humane manner, if not redeemed by their owner.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-2; Code 2005, § 14-2)
Sec. 14-3. - Cleanliness of pens and enclosures.
Any housing or enclosure used for the keeping of animals or fowl shall be well drained, free from accumulations of animal excrement and objectionable odors, and otherwise clean and sanitary.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-3; Code 2005, § 14-3)
Sec. 14-4. - Minimum distance of hog pens from neighboring premises.
The minimum distance a hog pen may be from any neighboring residence or business premises shall be 300 feet.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-4; Code 2005, § 14-4)
Sec. 14-5. - Minimum distance of fowl from neighboring premises.
It shall be unlawful to keep, pen or maintain upon one's premises, whether those premises are leased, owned or rented, any fowl of any kind or character within a distance of 150 feet of any property line of any of the adjoining property owners to the person maintaining and keeping the fowl.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-5; Code 2005, § 14-5)
Secs. 14-6—14-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DOGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 14-29. - Running at large prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or having control of any dog to allow or permit the dog to run at large within the city.
(b)
A dog is considered at large when it is not:
(1)
Within a structure;
(2)
Completely enclosed within a fence; or
(3)
Securely attached to a leash or chain under the control of a responsible person.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-11; Code 2005, § 14-31)
Sec. 14-30. - Collar and tag.
Each dog four months of age and older shall wear a collar and tag at all times while in the city. The tag shall be issued by the county health department or a licensed veterinarian and shall show evidence of a current valid rabies inoculation. The owner or person in control of the dog shall be responsible for complying with this section.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-12; Code 2005, § 14-32)
State Law reference— Removal of collar prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 16-9-71.
Sec. 14-31. - Animal control board.
The mayor and city council shall serve as an animal control board established for the purpose of enforcing the Dangerous Dog Control Law, provided that the mayor and city council may designate three persons to sit as an animal control board in their stead.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-15; Code 2005, § 14-33)
Cross reference— Boards and commissions, § 2-171 et seq.
Sec. 14-32. - Dangerous and potentially dangerous dogs.
(a)
No dangerous or potentially dangerous dog, as defined by O.C.G.A. § 4-8-21, shall be kept or maintained in the city without the owner first having complied with the requirements of the Dangerous Dog Control Law and having obtained a certificate of registration from the city. No certificate of registration shall be issued without payment of such fee as established from time to time by the mayor and city council.
(b)
Upon issuance of a certificate of registration the chief of police shall provide the dog owner with copies of the Dangerous Dog Control Law and this article.
(c)
Any dog confiscated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-27 shall be kept at the impound facility designated by the mayor and city council for the statutorily prescribed period of time. A daily boarding fee, as fixed from time to time by the mayor and city council, shall be charged for each day the dog is housed at city expense.
(d)
Failure to pay any fee assessed by the city for a certificate of registration or boarding of a confiscated dog shall be an offense and, upon conviction, shall be punishable as provided in section 1-7.
(Code 1990, § 11-3-16; Code 2005, § 14-34)
State Law reference— Dangerous dogs, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.
Sec. 14-33. - Enforcement of article and Dangerous Dog Control Law.
The police chief or his designee shall be responsible to enforce the provisions of this article and the Dangerous Dog Control Law.
(Code 2005, § 14-35)
State Law reference— Responsible Dog Ownership Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.

DIVISION 2. - IMPOUNDMENT

Sec. 14-55. - Authorized; notification of owner.
(a)
The police chief or his designee, pursuant to section 14-33, shall impound all dogs found at large in violation of this division.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the police chief or his designee when impounding an animal to notify the owner of such action regarding the animal immediately upon impoundment. Such notice shall state the name and business address of the officer responsible for impounding the animal, the name and address of the agency having custody of the animal, a description of the animal, the reason why the animal was impounded, and a statement of the time limits for the owner to respond and request a hearing, as provided in section 14-57.
(c)
The notice shall be provided by personal service or by registered mail, certified mail, or statutory overnight delivery sent to the last-known address of the owner. Service of the notice which complies with O.C.G.A. § 9-11-5(b) shall in all cases be sufficient.
(d)
If the owner of such animal is unknown or cannot be found, service of the notice on the owner shall be made by posting the notice in a conspicuous place at the location where the animal was impounded and by publishing a notice once in a newspaper of general circulation in the county.
(e)
An animal impounded pursuant to this division is deemed to be in the custody of the police chief, who is responsible for enforcement of this article within the city.
(Code 2005, § 14-61)
State Law reference— Impoundment of animal and notification, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-11-9.4, 4-11-9.5.
Sec. 14-56. - Care of impounded animals; return of animal to owner.
(a)
It shall be the duty of any person impounding an animal under this division to make reasonable and proper arrangements to provide the impounded animal with humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary services. Such arrangements may include, but shall not be limited to, providing shelter and care for the animal at any state, federal, county, municipal, or governmental facility or shelter; contracting with a private individual, partnership, corporation, association, or other entity to provide humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary services for a reasonable fee; or allowing a private individual, partnership, corporation, association, or other entity to provide humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary services as a volunteer and at no cost.
(b)
Any person impounding an animal under this division or providing care for an impounded animal shall have a lien on such animal for the reasonable costs of caring for such animal. Such lien may be foreclosed in any court that is competent to hear civil cases, including, but not limited to, magistrate courts. Liens shall be foreclosed in magistrate courts only when the amount of the lien does not exceed the jurisdictional limits established by law for such courts.
(c)
The chief of police or his designee is authorized to return the animal to its owner, upon payment by the owner of all costs of impoundment and care and upon the entry of a consent order, unless such owner was, in a prior administrative or legal action in Georgia or any other state, found to have failed to provide humane care to an animal, committed cruelty to animals, or engaged in dog fighting in violation of the laws of this state or of the United States or any of the several states. Such consent order shall provide conditions relating to the care and treatment of such animal, including, but not limited to, the following:
(1)
Such animal will be given humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary services;
(2)
Such animal will not be subjected to cruelty; and
(3)
The owner will comply with this article.
(d)
The provisions of subsection (c) of this section shall not apply to an animal that was an object or instrumentality of a crime, nor shall any such animal be returned to the owner without the approval of the prosecuting attorney. The police chief or his designee having custody of an animal that was seized as an object or instrumentality of a crime may, with the consent of the prosecuting attorney, apply to the court having jurisdiction over the offense for an order authorizing the police chief to dispose of the animal prior to trial of the criminal case as provided by law.
(Code 2005, § 14-62)
State Law reference— Requirements for the care and disposition of impounded animals, O.C.G.A. § 4-11-9.3.
Sec. 14-57. - Failure of owner to respond to notice; request for hearing.
(a)
If the owner of an animal impounded pursuant to this division fails to respond in writing within five business days of the date the notice of impoundment was served, or if the owner is unknown or could not be found within 30 days of publication of the notice of impoundment, the impounded animal may be disposed of pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-11-9.6.
(b)
If the owner of an animal impounded pursuant to this division refuses to enter into a consent agreement with the city that such animal will be given humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary care, the owner may request, in writing, a hearing within five business days of the date the notice of impoundment was served on such owner, or, if the owner is unknown or could not be found, within 30 days of the date of publication of the notice of impoundment.
(1)
Such request for hearing shall be served upon the police chief or his designee having control of the animal.
(2)
If no hearing is requested within the time limits specified in this subsection (b) and the failure to request such hearing is due in whole or in part to the reasonably avoidable fault of the owner, the right to a hearing shall have been waived.
(Code 2005, § 14-63)
State Law reference— Impoundment of animal and notification, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-11-9.4, 4-11-9.5.
Sec. 14-58. - Hearing procedures.
(a)
Within 30 days after receiving a written request for a hearing under this division, the city shall hold a hearing, as provided in O.C.G.A. tit. 50, ch. 13 (O.C.G.A. § 50-13-1 et seq.), the Georgia Administrative Procedures Act. The hearing shall be held before the animal control board.
(b)
The hearing shall be public and all testimony shall be received under oath.
(c)
A record of the proceedings at such hearing shall be made and maintained by the hearing officer as provided in O.C.G.A. § 50-13-13.
(d)
The scope of the hearing shall be limited to whether the impounding of the animal was authorized by O.C.G.A. § 4-11-9.2(c).
(e)
The hearing officer shall, within five business days after such hearing, forward a decision to the police chief or his designee who has custody of the animal.
(f)
If the hearing officer finds that the animal was improperly impounded, the animal shall be returned to the owner and the cost incurred in providing reasonable care and treatment for the animal from the date of impoundment to the date of the order shall be paid by the city.
(g)
If the hearing officer finds that the animal was lawfully impounded, the hearing officer may:
(1)
Recommend that the police chief or his designee dispose of the animal as provided in section 14-59; or
(2)
Unless, in a prior administrative or legal action in the state or any other state, the owner has been found to have failed to provide humane care to an animal, committed cruelty to animals, or engaged in dog fighting in violation of the laws of this state or of the United States or any of the several states, recommend conditions under which the animal may, upon payment by the owner of all costs of impoundment and care, be returned to the owner. Such conditions shall be reduced to writing and served upon the owner and the police chief or his designee. Such conditions may include, but are not limited to, the following:
a.
Such animal will be given humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary services;
b.
Such animal will not be subjected to mistreatment; and
c.
The owner will comply with this article.
(h)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to an animal that was an object or instrumentality of a crime, nor shall any such animal be returned to the owner or disposed of without the approval of the prosecuting attorney.
(Code 2005, § 14-64)
State Law reference— Impoundment of animal and notification, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-11-9.4, 4-11-9.5.
Sec. 14-59. - Disposal of impounded animals.
(a)
The police chief or his designee having custody of an animal impounded pursuant to this division which is not returned to the owner as provided in O.C.G.A. §§ 4-11-9.3 and 4-11-9.5 may:
(1)
Dispose of the animal through sale by any commercially feasible means, at a public auction or by sealed bids; or
(2)
If, in the opinion of a licensed accredited veterinarian or a veterinarian employed by the city, such animal has a temperament or condition such that euthanasia is the only reasonable course of action, humanely dispose of the animal.
(b)
Any proceeds from the sale of such animal shall be used first to pay the costs associated with the impoundment, including, but not limited to, removal of the animal from the premises, shelter and care of the animal, notice, hearing, and disposition of the animal. Any funds remaining shall:
(1)
If the owner is unknown or cannot be found, be paid into the city treasury; or
(2)
If the owner is known, be paid to the owner.
(c)
The police chief or his designee is responsible for conducting the sale and shall keep a record of all sales, disbursements, and distributions made under this division.
(Code 2005, § 14-65)
State Law reference— Disposition of impounded animals, O.C.G.A. § 4-11-9.6.
