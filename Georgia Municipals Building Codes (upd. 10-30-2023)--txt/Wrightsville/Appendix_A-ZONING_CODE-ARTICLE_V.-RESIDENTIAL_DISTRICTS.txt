ARTICLE V. - RESIDENTIAL DISTRICTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 5-1. - Residential districts (R-1, R-2, R-3, and R-4); purpose.
To establish residential districts and to protect property in the districts from the depreciating effects of incompatible land uses. Residential uses are intended to be the primary use within each residential district. The residential districts are intended to preserve and protect the residential character of neighborhoods.
(Zoning Code 1997, § 7-1)

DIVISION 2. - R-1 SINGLE-FAMILY RESIDENTIAL DISTRICT (LOW-DENSITY)

Sec. 5-21. - Purpose and permitted uses.
(a)
Purpose. It is the intent of the R-1 District to establish a low-density, single-family residential district and to protect property in the districts from the depreciating effects of incompatible land uses. The regulations for this district are designed to encourage similar and complementary types of residential development together with associated recreational, religious and similar facilities.
(b)
Permitted uses.
(1)
Single-family dwellings.
(2)
Accessory buildings and uses of structures customarily incidental to permitted uses, provided such structures are located in the rear yard and not less than five feet from any property line.
(3)
Agriculture, excluding the commercial raising of livestock.
(4)
See the table of uses provided in this article.
(5)
Conditional uses, as prescribed in the table of uses and article VII of this appendix.
(6)
Satellite dish television antennas and signs, subject to the supplemental requirements of this zoning code.
(Zoning Code 1997, §§ 7-2.1, 7-2.2)

DIVISION 3. - R-2 SINGLE-FAMILY RESIDENTIAL DISTRICT (MEDIUM-DENSITY)

Sec. 5-36. - Purpose and permitted uses.
(a)
Purpose. It is the intent of the R-2 District to provide for a less restrictive type of single-family residential development, allowing a higher density than the R-1 District, while maintaining an environment similar to that found in the R-1 District.
(b)
Permitted uses.
(1)
All uses allowed in the R-1 Residential District.
(2)
Single-family dwellings.
(3)
Two-family dwellings.
(4)
Accessory buildings and uses of structures customarily incidental to permitted uses, provided such structures are located in the rear yard and not less than five feet from any property line.
(5)
Customary incidental home occupations, including the office of a physician, dentist, lawyer, artist, musician, architect, engineer, or the accommodation of not more than two boarders or roomers, provided there is no external evidence of such occupation except an announcement or professional sign not more than two square feet in area, and that the operations are conducted within a dwelling by not more than one person in addition to those persons living therein, and the place occupied is a part of the house. Customary home occupations are further subject to the requirements of section 8-19 of this appendix.
(6)
Planned unit developments (PUDs), subject to the requirements of this zoning code.
(7)
See the table of uses provided in this article.
(8)
Conditional uses as provided in article VII of this appendix.
(9)
Satellite dish television antennas and signs, subject to the supplemental requirements of this zoning code.
(Zoning Code 1997, §§ 7-3.1, 7-3.2)

DIVISION 5. - R-4 MOBILE HOME PARK DISTRICT

Sec. 5-66. - Purpose and permitted uses.
(a)
Purpose. It is the intent of the R-4 District to establish a medium- to high-density residential district and to protect property in the districts from the depreciating effects of incompatible land uses. This district is established to contain residential mobile home parks and to ensure the availability of sufficient space to augment this usually limited interior floor area of a mobile home or manufactured home.
(b)
Permitted uses.
(1)
Any use permitted in the R-3 Multifamily Residential District.
(2)
Mobile homes/manufactured homes on individual lots, provided they meet the provisions of this zoning code.
(3)
Mobile home parks, subject to the additional requirements of this division.
(4)
Accessory buildings and uses of structures customarily incidental to any permitted use, provided such structures are located in the rear yard and not less than five feet from any property line.
(5)
See the table of uses as provided by this article.
(6)
Conditional uses, as provided in article VII of this appendix.
(7)
Satellite dish television antennas and signs, subject to the supplemental requirements of this zoning code.
(Zoning Code 1997, §§ 7-5.1, 7-5.2)
Sec. 5-67. - Minimum requirements for mobile home parks.
The following minimum standards shall apply to mobile home parks in the city:
(1)
Each mobile home park shall have a minimum area of five acres and a maximum density of eight dwelling units per acre. Each individual mobile home/manufactured home space will be completed according to section 5-68 of this appendix, before occupancy of the park will be permitted.
(2)
Each individual mobile home shall meet the area, yard and height requirements established for the R-4 District in section 5-2 of this appendix.
(3)
Each park shall have a minimum frontage of 200 feet, including two driveways for entry and exit between 25 and 50 feet wide, and at least 100 feet between the two adjacent driveway pavement edges.
(4)
Each park shall contain a privately-owned, constructed and maintained street system with a minimum pavement width of 20 feet. Paving and base materials shall be approved by the city street department before any paving may commence.
(5)
Mobile home parks and each mobile home/manufactured home within the park shall be served by the city's water and sewerage system. The park developer shall be responsible for service connections.
(6)
Mobile home parks shall provide facilities for refuse collection.
(Zoning Code 1997, § 7-5.3)
Sec. 5-68. - Minimum individual space requirements within mobile home/manufactured home parks.
Each mobile home/manufactured home space within a mobile home/manufactured home park shall meet the following minimum requirements:
(1)
Each mobile home/manufactured home shall be located no closer than 25 feet from the center of any interior park street; 20 feet from any other mobile home/manufactured home; 35 feet from the abutting right-of-way of any public street; and 15 feet from any park property line.
(2)
Each mobile home/manufactured home space will have a minimum individual lot size of 4,000 square feet with a minimum frontage of 35 feet; and an accessway not less than 12 feet wide to each mobile home/manufactured home stand; and two off-street parking spaces at least nine feet by 19 feet each.
(Zoning Code 1997, § 7-5.4)

DIVISION 6. - DEVELOPMENTAL DISTRICT D-1 (VERY LOW-DENSITY)

Sec. 5-83. - Purpose and permitted uses.
(a)
Purpose. It is the intent of the D-1 District to establish a district on the fringe of urban growth where the predominant character of development has not yet been fully established, but where the current characteristics of use are primarily agricultural and residential. As urban growth continues, demand will grow for amendments to remove land from the development district and place it in more intensely developed zones. However, the intent of the development district is to reserve the bulk of the land for agricultural and low-density residential uses.
(b)
Permitted uses.
(1)
Agriculture, horticulture, animal husbandry (excluding feed lots, hog operations, dairy farming, and commercial chicken operations), forestry, and similar agriculturally-related uses.
(2)
Single-family dwellings.
(3)
Accessory buildings and uses of structures customarily incidental to any permitted use, including buildings used for agricultural purposes, provided that such structures shall not be less than five feet from any property line.
(4)
See the table of uses provided in this article.
(5)
Conditional uses as prescribed in article VII of this appendix.
(6)
Satellite dish television antennas and signs, subject to the supplemental requirements of this zoning code.
(Zoning Code 1997, §§ 6-1.1, 6-1.2)
Secs. 5-84—5-97. - Reserved.
