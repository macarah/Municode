Sec. 74-31. - Short title.

This article shall be known as the "Forsyth County Hotel, Motel and Lodging Excise Tax Ordinance" and may be cited and referred to as such.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 1, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 74-32. - Purpose and intent.
modified
The Forsyth County Board of Commissioners hereby declares the purpose and intent of this article is to levy an excise tax on rooms, lodgings and accommodations as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 in order to:
(1)
Promote the public health, safety, welfare, and economy of Forsyth County by allocating revenues received from this tax for the purposes of promoting, attracting, stimulating, and developing conventions and tourism; and
(2)
To allow for the provision of other local government services, such as economic development as authorized under Georgia law.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 2, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 74-33. - Imposition of tax and applicability.
modified
(1)
There is hereby levied and assessed, and there shall be paid, a tax of five percent of the rent for every occupancy of a guestroom in unincorporated Forsyth County.
(2)
This article shall apply to, and the tax herein levied shall be collected upon, the furnishing for value to the public of any room or rooms, lodgings, or accommodations facilitated or furnished by any innkeeper. Forsyth County may levy and collect an excise tax upon furnishing for value to the public of any room or rooms, lodgings, or accommodations facilitated or furnished by an innkeeper.
(3)
Where the rooms, lodgings, or accommodations taxable under this chapter are facilitated by a marketplace innkeeper, the marketplace innkeeper shall remit all taxes in the manners provided in this Ordinance and, such taxes shall be credited against the taxes imposed by this Ordinance on the furnishing for value to the public any room or rooms, lodgings, or accommodations.
(4)
Every person occupying a guestroom in a hotel in unincorporated Forsyth County is liable for the tax imposed by this chapter. Such person's liability is not extinguished until the tax has been paid to the county, except that a receipt from an innkeeper maintaining a place of business in the county or from a marketplace innkeeper who is authorized by the county manager or his/her designee, under such rules and regulations as same may prescribe to collect the tax and who is, for the purposes of this Ordinance, regarded as an innkeeper maintaining a place of business in unincorporated Forsyth County, which receipt is given to the occupant pursuant this article, is sufficient to relieve the occupant from further liability for the tax to which the receipt refers.
(5)
The tax shall be paid upon any occupancy on and after March 1, 2000, although this occupancy is had pursuant to a contract, lease, or other arrangement made prior to that date. Where rent is paid, charged, billed, or falls due on either a weekly, monthly, or other term basis, only the rent covering the period commencing on March 1, 2000, shall be subject to this tax.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 3, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 75-34. - Exemptions.
new
No tax shall be imposed under this article:
(1)
Upon a corporation, association, or other legal entity organized and operated exclusively for religious, charitable, or educational purposes, or for one or more such purposes, not part of the net earnings of which insures to the benefit of any private shareholder, member or individual;
(2)
Upon the continuous occupancy of any guestroom in a hotel in the county which occupancy has continued for a period of more than ten consecutive days; provided, however, that the tax authorized by the Ordinance shall apply to the occupancy of any guestroom during the first ten days of continuous occupancy;
(3)
Upon the rental of any room in a hotel in the county provided to any persons who certify that they are staying in such guestroom as a result of the destruction of their home or residence by fire or other casualty;
(4)
Upon facilities or to any rooms, lodgings or accommodations which are provided without charge;
(5)
Upon the rental of any room in a hotel in the county for use solely as a meeting room; or
(6)
Upon the rental of any room furnished for use by state or local government officials or employees when traveling on official business.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 4, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 74-35. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County means Forsyth County, a political subdivision of the State of Georgia.
Guestroom means a room occupied, or intended, arranges, or designed for occupancy by one or more occupants.
Hotel means any structure or portion of a structure, including, but not limited to, any lodging house, rooming house, dormitory, Turkish bath, bachelor hotel, studio hotel, motel, auto court, inn, lodge, cabin, campground, bed and breakfast, short-term rental, public club, or private club containing guestrooms, or any other place containing guest rooms and which is occupied, or intended or designed for occupancy, by guests for value, whether rent is paid in money, goods, labor, or otherwise. It does not include any jail, hospital, asylum, sanitarium, orphanage, prison, detention, or other building in which human beings are housed or detained under legal restraint.
Innkeeper means:
(1)
Any person that furnishes for value to the public any room or rooms, lodgings, or accommodations in a county or municipality and that is licensed by, or required to pay business or occupation taxes to, such municipality or county for operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin, campground, or any other place in which room or rooms, lodgings, or accommodations are regularly furnished for value.
(2)
A marketplace facilitator who facilitates the furnishing for value to the public any guest rooms on behalf of another person in any manner, including, but not limited to, promoting, marketing, advertising, taking reservations, collecting payment, or as otherwise defined by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50.2(2)(B).
Marketplace innkeeper means an innkeeper as defined in section 74-35, "innkeeper", subsection (2).
Occupancy means the use or possession, or the right to the use and possession, of any room or apartment in a hotel or the right to the use or possession of the furnishings or to the services and accommodations accompanying the use and possession of the room.
Occupant means any person who, for a consideration, uses, possesses, or has the right to use or possess any room in a hotel under any lease, concession, permit right of access, license to use, other agreement, or otherwise.
Permanent resident means any occupant as of a given date who has or shall have occupied or has or shall have the right of occupancy of any guestroom in a hotel for at least ten consecutive days next preceding such date.
Person includes any individual, firm, partnership, joint venture, association, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, non-profit corporation, limited liability company, cooperative non-profit membership, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, trustee, syndicate, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, the plural as well as the singular number, excepting the United States of America, the State of Georgia, and any political subdivision of either thereof, upon which the county is without power to impose the tax provided for in this article.
Rent means the consideration received for occupancy valued in money, whether received money or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credit, and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the innkeeper to the occupant, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Return means any return filed or required to be filed as provided in this article.
Tax refers to the tax imposed by this article.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 5, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 74-36. - Administration of ordinance; recordkeeping.
modified

Authority of county manager. The county manager or his/her designee shall administer and enforce the provision of this article.

Examination of records; audits. The county manager or his/her designee may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment, and other facilities or any innkeeper renting guestrooms and otherwise liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or if no return is made by the innkeeper, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(4)
Authority to require reports; contents. In the administration of this article, the county manager or his/her designee may require the filing of reports by any persons or class of persons having in such their possession or custody information relating to rentals of guestrooms which are subject to the tax. The reports shall be filed with the county manager or his/her designee when required and shall set forth the rental charged for each occupancy, the date of occupancy, an such other information as may be reasonably required.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 6, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 74-37. - Collection of tax by innkeeper; receipt of occupant; rules for collection schedules.
modified
(1)
Every innkeeper maintaining a place of business in unincorporated Forsyth County, as provided herein, and renting guestrooms in the county, and not otherwise exempted from this article, shall collect the tax imposed by this article at the time of collecting the rent from the occupant and, at the time of collecting the rent from the occupant give the occupant a receipt therefor. In all cases of transactions upon credit or deferred payment, the payment of the tax to the innkeeper may be deferred in accordance therewith, and the innkeeper shall be liable therefor at the time and to the extent that these credits are incurred in accordance with the rate of tax owning on the amounts thereof. Each innkeeper shall be liable for the full amount of taxes levied or imposed by this article on its transactions or the amount of tax collected by such innkeeper from all purchasers on all such transactions, whichever is greater.
(4)
In the event that the marketplace innkeeper fails to remit the full amount of taxes levied or imposed by this article to Forsyth County, the innkeeper shall be liable for the full amount of taxes levied or imposed by this article.

Sec. 74-39. - Registration of innkeeper; form and contents of registration; certificate of authority.
modified
(1)
Every person engaging or intending to engage within 60 days in business as an innkeeper in the county shall register with the county manager or his/her designee on a form provided by same. Persons engaged in such business as an innkeeper in the county must register not later than 15 days after the date of adoption of this article; provided, however, that failure to properly register with the county shall not relieve any person from the obligation of payment or collection of the tax after the date of imposition thereof.
(2)
The registration form shall set forth the name under which such person transacts business or intends to transact business, the location of this place of business, the official address for the innkeeper to which notices required or allowed under this article shall be served, and such other information as the county manager or his/her designee may require which shall facilitate the collection of the tax. The registration shall be signed by the owner if a natural person, or, in the case of an entity other than a natural person, by an authorized officer, manager, member, or other person authorized to bind the entity and act on such entity's behalf. A separate registration shall be required for each place of business of an innkeeper. In the event any information supplied to the county manager or his/her designee on the registration shall become obsolete or shall not be accurate, it shall be the responsibility of the innkeeper to ensure that the registration is updated to reflect the current and correct information. The county manager or his/her designees shall, after such registration, issue without charge, a certificate of authority to each innkeeper to collect the tax from each occupant. Each certificate of authority shall be prominently displayed in the location of the business to which it is applicable so as to be seen and come readily to the notice of all occupants and persons seeking occupancy. The certificate of authority shall be nontransferable and non-assignable and shall be returned immediately to the county manager or his/her designee upon the cessation of business at the location named or upon the sale or transfer of the business at that location.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 9, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 75-40. - Determinations; returns; payments.
new
(1)
Due date of tax. All amounts collected as tax during any given month pursuant to this article, shall be due and payable to Forsyth County on or before the 20th day of the following month (i.e., taxes collected for the month of March would be due on April 20). It is the intent of this part that the amount due and payable shall be submitted online, delivered in person, or postmarked on or before the 20th day of the month.
(2)
Contents of return. The tax return filed with the county manager or his/her designee, shall provide information showing the gross rent, rent from permanent residents, taxable rent, the amount of tax collected or otherwise due for the month for which the return is submitted, and such other information as may be reasonably required.
(3)
Collection fee allowed to innkeepers. Innkeepers collecting the tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for as a collection fee and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting, and repayment of the amount due to the county; provided, however, that innkeepers shall only be entitled to the collection fee in the event the payment of the amount due is not delinquent at the time of payment, as provided herein. The amount of the deduction or collection fee shall be three percent of the amount due.
(4)
Extension for filing return and payment of tax. For good cause, to be determined in the sole discretion of the county manager or his/her designee, the county manager or his/her designee shall be authorized to extend, for a period of time not exceeding one month, the time for making any return or payment of tax. The county manager or his/her designee shall be authorized, however, to only extend the time for making any return or payment of tax once for any single return or payment. Any extension granted pursuant to this part shall be in writing and shall state the reason for the extension and the date upon which the extension shall terminate. Any person to whom an extension has been granted and who makes a return and pays the amount due as tax within the period of extension shall pay in addition to the tax interest on the amount of such tax outstanding at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, for the period commencing on the due date of said return and amount due to the date of actual payment of the amount due.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 10, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 74-41. - Deficiency determinations.
modified
(1)
Re-computation of tax; authority; basis of re-computation. If the county manager or his/her designee is not satisfied with the return made by any person or the amount of the tax paid to the county by and person, the county manager or his/her designee is authorized to compute and determine the amount of tax required to be paid upon the basis of any known information.
(2)
Interest of deficiency. The amount of the deficiency determination, exclusive of penalties, shall be interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the due date of the tax as provided herein to the date of actual payment of the amount due (plus interest and penalties, if any).
(3)
Offsetting overpayments. In making a deficiency determination, the county manager or his/her designee may offset any overpayments for prior returns against underpayments, penalties, and interest owing from another return. The interest in underpayments shall accrue as provided for interest on deficiency determinations as set forth herein.
(4)
Penalty; negligence of disregard of rules and regulations. If any part of the deficiency for which a deficiency determination has been made is due to negligence on the part of any person or a disregard of this Ordinance or other authorized rules and regulations, a penalty equal to 15 percent of the amount of the deficiency shall be added to the total amount due for the deficiency.
(5)
Penalty; fraud or intent to evade tax. If any part of the deficiency for which a deficiency determination has been made is due to fraud or an intent to evade any provisions of this article or other authorized rules and regulations, a penalty equal to 25 percent of the amount of the deficiency shall be added to the total amount due for the deficiency.
(6)
Notice of county manager or his/her designee determination; service. The county manager or his/her designee shall give the innkeeper electronic or written notice of the deficiency determination, accrual of interest, and imposition of applicable penalties. The electronic notice shall be served to the email provided by the innkeeper, read receipt requested. The written notice shall be served personally or by certified mail, return receipt requested. If the written notice is served by certified mail, return receipt requested, the notice shall be addressed as the innkeeper's address appears on the last registration filed by the innkeeper pursuant to this Ordinance. In the case of service by written notice, service shall be deemed complete upon deposit of the certified mail in the United States post office.
(7)
Time limit for notice of deficiency determination. Except in the case of fraud, intent to evade this Ordinance or authorized rules and regulations, or complete failure to make a return as required herein, every written notice of deficiency determination required by this article shall be served upon the innkeeper as provided herein within three years following either the date the tax was originally due or three years following the date upon which the return is actually filed, whichever shall last expire. There shall be no time limit for service of the written notice of deficiency determination for cases of fraud, intent to evade this article or authorized rules and regulations, or failure to make a return as required herein.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 11, 1-20-2022)
Sec. 74-42. - Determination if no return made.
modified
(1)
Estimation of gross receipts. If any person fails to make a return as required herein, the county manager or his/her designee shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the person, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total rentals in the county which are subject to the tax. The estimate shall be made for all months in which the person failed to make the required return and shall be based upon any information in the county manager or his/her designee's possession. Upon the basis of this estimate, the county manager or his/her designee shall compute and determine the amount of tax required to be paid to the county, adding to the sum thus computed a penalty equal to 15 percent thereof.
(2)
Offsets. In making an estimate determination where no required return is made, the county manager or his/her designee is authorized to offset overpayments made for any previous returns against the amount of tax due based on an estimate determination for any month during which no return was made.
(3)
Interest. The amount of the tax due based on the estimate determination shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, commencing on the 20th day of the calendar month following the month during which no return was made and continuing until taxes due, plus interest and penalties thereon, are actually paid.
(4)
Penalties for fraud or intent to evade. If the failure of any person to file a return is due to fraud or an intent to evade this article or duly authorized rules and regulations, a penalty equal to 25 percent of the amount of the tax due based on this estimate determination shall be added to the amount due for the monthly period for which no return was made. The penalty provided for in this part shall be in addition to and not in lieu of any other applicable penalties in this article.
(5)
Notice. Promptly after making the estimate determination, the county manager or his/her designee shall notify the person failing to make the required return of the amount of tax due based on the estimate determination, plus the amount of accrued interest and penalties thereon. The notice shall be in writing and shall be served as provided in section 74-41(6) of this article.
(Ord. No. 86-B , Pt. 12, 1-20-2022)

modified

Sec. 74-44. - Collection of tax by county.
modified
(1)
Action for delinquent tax. Except in the case of fraud, intent to evade this article or authorized rules and regulations, or failure to file a return as required herein, at any time within three years after any amount of tax required to be collected becomes due and payable and at any time within three years after the delinquency of any amount of tax required to be collected, the county manager or his/her designee may bring an action in any court of competent jurisdiction in the name of the county to collect the delinquent amount together with interest and penalties as provided in this article, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees, and other fees and costs incident thereto. There shall be no time limit for bringing an action as provided herein in cases of fraud, intent to evade this article or authorized rules and regulations, or failure to make a return as required herein.
(2)
Duty of successors and assigns of innkeeper to withhold tax. If any innkeeper liable for any amount under this article sells, transfers, assigns, releases, quit-claims, or conveys any business operation which is subject to the tax, the innkeeper's successors and/or assigns shall withhold a sufficient amount from the purchase price of the business operation to cover any amounts which may be due as tax until the innkeeper produces a receipt from the county manager or his/her designee stating that the tax due has been paid or that no tax is due. In the event the successors and/or assigns of the innkeeper fail to withhold a sufficient amount from the purchase price of the business operation to cover any amounts which may be due as tax, the successors and/or assigns shall be liable for the amount of tax which is due. If any innkeeper quits, closes, or stops operating the business which is subject to the tax for any reason, the successors, heirs, and/or assigns of the innkeeper shall remain liable for any amounts which may be due as tax.
(3)
Tax credit for interest paid or erroneously or illegally collected. Whenever the amount of any tax, interest, or penalty has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by the county under this article, it may be offset by the county manager or his/her designee. If the innkeeper or marketplace innkeeper determines that there has been an overpayment or multiple payments, which fact has not been determined by the county manager or his/her designee, such innkeeper or marketplace innkeeper shall have a period of three years from the date of payment to file a claim with the county, in writing, stating the specific grounds upon which the claim is founded. The claim shall then be audited. If the claim is approved, the excess amount paid to the county may be credited on any amounts the due and payable from the innkeeper or marketplace innkeeper, or the county manager or his/her designee is authorized to refund the amount to the innkeeper or marketplace innkeeper. Notwithstanding anything contained in this section, no innkeeper or marketplace innkeeper shall have any right to file a claim for overpayment in the event that alleged overpayment is a result of an estimated determination as provided in section 74-42 of this article.

ARTICLE III. - ENERGY EXCISE TAX

Sec. 74-61. - Energy excise tax definitions.
As used in this article, the terms: "dealer;" "energy;" "local sales" and "use tax"; and "purchaser" shall have the same meanings as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110.
(Ord. No. 119, § 1, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
Sec. 74-62. - Imposition; rate; remitting; recovery; liabilities.
In accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110 et seq., there is imposed within the territorial limits of the special district created pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-111, an excise tax on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy when such sale, use, storage, or consumption would have constituted a taxable event for purposes of sales and use tax under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq. but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(Ord. No. 119, § 2, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
Sec. 74-63. - Excise tax rate; phase-in.
(a)
Such excise tax shall be levied and collected by the governing authority of Forsyth County pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-112 and 48-13-114 and as provided in this section.
(b)
Except as otherwise provided in this section, the rate of such excise tax shall be phased in, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112, over a four-year period as follows:
(1)
For the period commencing January 1, 2013, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2013, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 25 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(2)
For the period commencing January 1, 2014, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2014, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 50 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(3)
For the period commencing January 1, 2015, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2015, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 75 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2; and
(4)
On or after January 1, 2016, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 100 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(c)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2(c)(4), if a competitive project of regional significance under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3(92) is started in the special district, it shall not be subject to the phase-in period contained in subsections (b)(1), (2), and (3) of this section, and the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy in connection therewith shall be subject to the rate specified in subsection (b)(4) of this section notwithstanding the January 1, 2016 limitation in subsection (b)(4).
(d)
Following such initial imposition during the phase-in time period, on and after January 1, 2016, the rate of the excise tax levied and collected by the governing authority of Forsyth County shall be directed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(d).
(1)
Such rate shall be controlled by the maximum amount of local sales and use tax in effect in the special district, but in no event more than two percent (except in the case where the municipal option water and sewer tax is levied, in which case that rate shall not exceed three percent).
(2)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district decreases from two percent to one percent, the rate of the excise tax under this ordinance shall likewise be reduced at the same time such local sales and use tax rate reduction becomes effective.
(3)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district increases from one percent to two percent, the rate of the excise tax under this ordinance shall likewise be increased at the same time such local sales and use tax rate increase becomes effective.
(Ord. No. 119, § 3, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
Sec. 74-64. - Imposition; remittance; recovery.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(c), such excise tax shall be:
(1)
Imposed only at the time sales and use tax on the sale or use of such energy would have been due and payable under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-30 but for the exemption under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(2)
Due and payable in the same manner as would be required under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq., except as otherwise provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-110 et seq.;
(3)
A debt of the purchaser of energy until it is paid and shall be recoverable at law in the same manner as authorized for the recovery of other debts;
(4)
Remitted to the governing authority of Forsyth County by the dealer collecting such excise tax; and
(5)
A liability of the dealer at the applicable rate on the charges actually collected or the amount of excise taxes collected from purchasers, whichever is greater.
(Ord. No. 119, § 4, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
Sec. 74-65. - Exemption certificates.
(a)
The governing authority of Forsyth County, or the collecting officer appointed under section 74-68 of this article, shall make diligent efforts to identify all energy providers and purchasers in the special district and to ascertain whether such a purchaser has completed a uniform exemption certificate provided from the website of the Georgia Department of Revenue seeking to qualify such purchaser for the exemption under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2. Such certificate shall be utilized by such governing authority or collecting officer for the purpose of determining the applicability of the excise tax under this article.
(b)
An energy provider shall be authorized to rely upon such uniform exemption certificates. No penalty shall attach to such provider for failure to collect the excise tax under this ordinance when it has properly collected and remitted local sales and use taxes on such energy where the purchaser would have qualified for the uniform exemption certificate but failed to apply for such certificate or where the purchaser received such certificate but failed to file it with the energy provider.
(Ord. No. 119, § 5, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
Sec. 74-66. - Commencement of collections.
The excise tax imposed pursuant to this ordinance shall become effective on January 1, 2013, and shall be due and payable to the governing authority of Forsyth County monthly on or before the twentieth (20 th ) day of the month following each respective month in which such taxes are collected, and payment shall be accompanied by a return for the preceding monthly period showing the gross sales and purchases arising from all sales and purchases taxable under this article during the preceding calendar month.
(Ord. No. 119, § 6, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
Sec. 74-67. - Allocation, distribution, and expenditure of proceeds.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114(b), the excise tax proceeds shall be allocated and distributed by the governing authority of Forsyth County at the end of each calendar month. An amount equal to one percent of such proceeds collected by Forsyth County shall be paid into the General Fund of Forsyth County to defray the costs of collection and administration.
(b)
The remainder of such proceeds collected by Forsyth County shall be distributed in accordance with the intergovernmental agreement as required under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114(b) and (c). Within 30 days following the end of each calendar month, the remainder shall be distributed to the General Fund of Forsyth County and the general fund of each participating municipality in accordance with the applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114(c)(1) or (2) as follows:
(1)
If two local sales and use taxes are in effect in the special district, an amount equal to one-half of the proceeds of the excise tax shall be distributed to the General Fund of Forsyth County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in the county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the first local sales and use tax, and an amount equal to one-half of the proceeds of the excise tax shall be distributed to the General Fund of Forsyth County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in the county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the second local sales and use tax; or
(2)
If only one such local sales and use tax is in effect in the special district, then the proceeds of the excise tax shall be distributed to the general fund of Forsyth County and the General Fund of each participating municipality located in the county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the local sales and use tax.
(c)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, proceeds of the excise tax deposited in the general fund of Forsyth County may be expended for any lawful purpose as determined by the governing authority of Forsyth County.
(Ord. No. 119, § 7, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
Sec. 74-68. - Administrative provisions; audits; examinations; collecting officer.
(a)
The governing authority of Forsyth County shall be authorized to designate a collecting officer for collection and administration of the excise tax under this article.
(b)
The collecting officer shall be authorized to provide for procedures necessary to the proper implementation of this article, including, but not limited to, periodic auditing of dealers collecting and remitting the excise tax. The collecting officer, or the designee of the collecting officer, is specifically authorized to examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment, or other facilities of any purchaser which is required to remit the excise tax under this article.
(c)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-118, as part of the audit report required under O.C.G.A. § 36-81-7, the auditor shall include, in a separate schedule, a report of the revenues pertaining to the excise tax under this article.
(Ord. No. 119, § 8, 12-20-2012, eff. 1-1-2013)
