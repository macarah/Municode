Sec. 6-87. - Definitions.
(a)
Wherever the term "electrical inspector," "plumbing inspector," "heating and air conditioning inspector" or "gas inspector" is used [in the respective codes], it shall mean the person designated by the city manager to administer and enforce the provisions of said code.
(b)
Wherever the term "corporate counsel" is used in the electrical, plumbing, heating, air conditioning and gas codes, it shall mean the city attorney.
(c)
Wherever the term "municipality" is used in the electrical, plumbing, heating, air conditioning and gas codes, it shall mean the City of Dublin.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-88. - Enforcement.
The electrical, plumbing, heating, air conditioning and gas codes adopted by the provisions of this article shall be enforced by the chief building official.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-89. - Penalties.
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of the electrical, plumbing, heating, air conditioning and gas codes or fail to comply therewith, or who shall violate or fail to comply with any order made thereunder, shall, for each such failure or violation or noncompliance, be punished as provided in section 1-10 of this Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-90. - Competence of workers.
It shall be unlawful for any person to do any work of electrical, plumbing, heating and air conditioning installation, construction, or repair work unless such work is done by or under the supervision of a certificate issued by the state licensing board as required by the building official. It shall be unlawful for any person to construct, install or repair any work pertaining to gas construction unless such work is done by or under the supervision of a certificate issued by the city's gas fitter's examining board.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-91. - Permit application to be made by person doing work; transfer of permits.
The application or statement for any permit required by this chapter shall be made and submitted by the contractor who proposes to do the work specified in said application. Any permit issued under the terms of this chapter cannot be transferred to any other contractor except by first making application for transfer in writing, which shall be accompanied by a transfer fee of ten dollars ($10.00) which is in addition to the other fees specified by this chapter. No person shall do any such work under a permit issued to any other person unless such permit has first been transferred under the terms of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-92. - Revocation of certificate of registration, permits; appeals.
(a)
Any contractor certified by the state licensing board and holding a certificate of registration issued by the city, who violates any provisions of this chapter, or who certifies under oath to the building official that he has supervised the installation, construction or repair of any electrical, plumbing, heating, air conditioning and gas work when in fact he has not, shall be cited by the building official to appear before said appropriate board to show cause why his certificate of registration shall not be revoked. After such citation has been personally served upon said certified contractor, he shall have the right to appear before said board and offer such evidence in his behalf as he deems necessary. At the same time the evidence shall be considered by said board. The building official shall be qualified to testify. After hearing the evidence the board shall then by a majority vote find him guilty or not guilty as the evidence warrants. If found guilty, the board may revoke his certificate of registration or impose a suspension of his certificate for a period of up to ninety (90) days. If found not guilty, the charge shall be dropped. If said person cited to appear fails to appear at the specified time and place in the citation, his certificate of registration shall automatically be suspended by the board until such time as he does appear before the board to answer such charges.
(b)
Any person found guilty under this section shall have the right to appeal such decision to the recorder's court of the city. Such appeal shall be in writing within five (5) days after the decision of judgement appealed from. The hearing before the judge shall be de novo, and if after hearing the evidence the judge finds the board correct, the decision of the board shall be affirmed. If he finds in favor of appellant, such decision shall reverse the decision of the board.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-93. - Certification required.
No person shall do any electrical, plumbing, heating and air conditioning installation, constructing, or repairing within the city unless he holds a certificate from the state licensing board. It shall be unlawful for any person knowingly to employ another who does not hold a certificate from said board. Helpers under the direct supervision and direction of a person holding one (1) of said certificates and in his direct employment are excluded from the certificate of registration and certification requirements of this division; otherwise one who is a helper shall be included. No person shall construct, repair or make any gas installations unless he holds a competency card from the city's gas fitter's examining board and a certificate of registration issued by the city.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-94. - Business certificate of registration required.
Every person desiring to engage in the business of electrical, plumbing, heating and air conditioning installation; construction or repairs within the city shall obtain a certificate of registration from the city clerk therefor. Said certificate of registration shall be issued only after presentation of certificate from the state licensing board. A certificate of registration shall be issued for gas construction, installation and repairs within the city after the presentation of a competency card from the city. A certificate of registration shall not be issued unless he has taken the gas fitter's examination and issued a competency card from the city's gas superintendent.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-95. - Due date, certificate of registration; transferability.
All certificate of registration, occupation and professional taxes set and levied by this article shall be due and payable annually on January 1. In the event that any person commences business or commences practicing a profession subject to tax on any date after January 1 in any year, the tax shall be due and payable on the date of commencement of the business or of the business or the commencement of the practice of a profession subject to tax. The certificate of registration shall not be transferable and the full fee shall be charged for any year or fraction thereof.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-96. - Fraudulent use of contractor's name or certificate of registration.
No contractor shall allow his name or certificate of registration to be used by any person whether directly or indirectly for the purpose of evading this Code or the ordinances of the city. In case this section is violated, both the contractor and the person using his certificate of registration shall be deemed equally guilty and the act shall be considered a violation of this Code.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
