Chapter 6 - CEMETERIES

Sec. 6-1. - Work in cemeteries performed by public works department.
No work shall be done in the cemetery except under the direction of the public works department. The work of the public works department for all burials shall be to lay out the grave space in the appropriate location and to maintain permanent records of new and existing locations. No concrete work or work of any kind shall be done by the public works department on vaults, such work to be done only under the supervision of the public works department. In no event shall any materials for vaults or graves be supplied by or charged to the city.
(Code 1958; § 8-2; Code 1991, § 6-1; Ord. No. 03132000-1 , § I, 3-13-2000)
Sec. 6-2. - Damaging or interfering with graves, monuments, flowers, shrubs, etc., prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to deface or otherwise injure or improperly interfere with any monument, vault, gravestone, headboard, footboard, or grave enclosure; or any tree, shrub, flower, flower vase or wreaths growing or placed within grave enclosures or the general enclosure of any cemetery; or to break down or otherwise injure any of the gates, fences or other improvements of the same.
(Code 1958, § 8-4; Code 1991, § 6-2)
State Law reference— Criminal trespass and damage to property, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-20.
Sec. 6-3. - Trespassing and use as passageway prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to trespass or use the cemetery as a passageway from one part of the city to another.
(Code 1958, § 8-5; Code 1991, § 6-3)
Sec. 6-4. - Burial administrative fees; notice required.
modified
(a)
Burial fees. An administrative fee set by city council and kept on file and maintained in the office of the clerk of the city, will be charged for each burial. Damage to vaults, headstones, slabs, memorials, benches, trees, shrubbery, or other improvements will result in full replacement costs by the funeral director.
(b)
Notice required. The superintendent of cemeteries must be given 24 hours' notice before the hour of the funeral for the laying out of a gravesite.
(c)
Sunday funerals. For Sunday funerals, notice must be given before 4:30 p.m. on the preceding Friday.
(Code 1958, § 8-6; Code 1991, § 6-4; Ord. of 12-30-1988(7), § I; Ord. No. 03132000-1 , § II, 3-13-2000; Ord. of 10-24-2022(1) , § I)
Sec. 6-5. - Lots—Purchase prices specified.
modified
Prices of lots in the city cemeteries shall be set by the city council and kept on file and maintained in the office of the clerk of the city for the following:
(1)
Individual space, measuring five feet by ten feet.
(2)
Infant space (designated area).
(Code 1958, § 8-7; Code 1991, § 6-5; Ord. of 12-30-1988(7); Ord. No. 02282000-4 , § I, 2-28-2000; Ord. of 10-24-2022(1) , § II)
Sec. 6-6. - Lots—Restriction on number purchased; resale; records.
No person shall purchase in any one of the cemeteries in their own name more than four lots, and no lot shall be resold without the approval of the city manager and entry made of such transfer on the city's cemetery records in the office of the city clerk. No lots shall be resold for a price above that paid to the city.
(Code 1958, § 8-8; Code 1991, § 6-6; Ord. No. O-2021-01-011121 , § I, 1-11-2021)
Sec. 6-7. - Perpetual care.
modified
(a)
As of January 1, 2000, the city no longer accepts perpetual care payment. All city-owned and maintained cemeteries are considered perpetual care. Lots sold with perpetual care prior to January 1, 2000 will continue to be recognized as perpetual care.
(b)
Money held in trust for perpetual care of lots shall be deposited in a special account and shall be held, administered and invested in accordance with the laws of the state, and the income from such investment of funds used to provide perpetual care of such lot. The work on such lot shall cover and include raking, cutting of weeds and grass, and general care exclusive of watering, but shall not include work on monuments or tombstones or other unusual care.
(Code 1958, § 8-9; Code 1991, § 6-7; Ord. of 12-30-1988(7), § 1; Ord. of 1-27-1993, § I; Ord. of 10-24-2022(1) , § III)
State Law reference— Receipt of cemetery or burial lots in trust; annual returns; commissions, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-4; municipal corporation as trustee of funds donated to cemetery, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-5.
Sec. 6-8. - Vaults, construction; lot boundary markers; plantings.
All cemetery vaults must be constructed so that the tops will be at least 12 inches below the level of the ground. No coping, fence, wall, ditch, bank or enclosures of any kind or nature shall be placed or used or shall have the appearance of being used for outlining, enclosing, marking off or designating any boundary line of any lot or plot in such cemeteries except six-inch square white marble, granite or concrete markers may be used to mark the four corners of any lot. Said six-inch markers are to be flush with the ground and set within the boundaries of the lot, and the same shall apply to name plates. Any planting on cemetery lots is to be done on the approval of the supervisor of cemeteries.
(Code 1958, § 8-10; Code 1991, § 6-8)
Sec. 6-9. - Headstones, names on both sides prohibited; exceptions.
All headstones placed on lots shall have the family names, lettering or engraving on only the side which faces the graves, except that headstones placed in the middle of the lot and facing family graves on both sides, and headstones placed on that portion of the lot adjoining its cemetery drive and within two feet from such drive, may have such family names, lettering or engraving on both sides. Any headstones placed on lots which do not conform to the requirements set out in this section shall be removed from the cemetery by the person who ordered such headstones placed there.
(Code 1958, § 8-11; Code 1991, § 6-9)
Sec. 6-10. - Removal of flowers; trimming or removal of vegetation.
The cemetery superintendent shall have the authority, in the cemetery superintendent's discretion, to remove dead flower arrangements or badly deteriorated artificial flowers from graves. The superintendent may also trim, cut, or remove trees, shrubs, or other plantings that either block passage through the cemetery or encroach upon the lots of others, or which are causing damage to headstones or markers.
(Code 1991, § 6-10)
Sec. 6-11. - Burial on private property prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any human body or remains to be buried within the corporate limits of the city, except in an established and recognized municipal, church or privately operated cemetery. No burial of any human body or remains shall take place on any private property outside an established municipal, church or privately operated cemetery. Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7.
(Code 1991, § 6-11; Ord. No. 09142009-2 , § I(6-11), 9-14-2009)
