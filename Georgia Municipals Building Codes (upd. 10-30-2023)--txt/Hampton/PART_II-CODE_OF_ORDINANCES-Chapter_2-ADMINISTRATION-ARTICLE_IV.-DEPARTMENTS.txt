Editor's note— Ord. No. 451 , § 1, adopted July 10, 2018, amended Art. IV in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former Art. IV, §§ 2-146—2-154, pertained to similar subject matter, and derived from Ord. No. 396 , 10-28-14.
Charter reference— Power to create, alter, abolish municipal departments, §§ 1.12(38); executive branch, § 3.10 et seq.
Cross reference— Emergency management agency, § 30-61 et seq.; police department, § 50-31 et seq.

Sec. 2-146. - Enumeration.
The services of the city shall be provided through the following departments:
(1)
Administration department, under the charge of the city clerk.
(2)
Police department, under the charge of the chief of police.
(3)
Public works department, under the charge of the director of public works.
(4)
Economic development department, under the charge of the economic development director.
(5)
Community development department under the charge of the community development director.
(6)
Finance department, under the charge of the finance director.
(7)
Human resources department under the charge of the human resources director.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-147. - Administration department.
The administration department shall consist of the city clerk and all of the duties associated with the clerk's position as established by state and local laws. This department shall perform such other administrative functions as directed by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-148. - Police department.
The police department shall enforce the provisions of this Code and city ordinances, the laws of the state, and maintain order in the city.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-149. - Public works department.
The public works department shall repair, operate and maintain the sewage system, the water system, the electrical system, all city streets and all city property. This department shall now include the wastewater treatment functions.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-150. - Economic development department.
The economic development department shall coordinate revitalization efforts throughout the city, utilizing the National Main Street program strategies. The department is also responsible for economic development, special events, and overseeing various grants.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-151. - Community development department.
The community development department shall provide for plan review, building inspections, code enforcement, planning and zoning, and permits. This department works in cooperation with the economic development department (specifically the Main Street Program).
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-152. - Finance department.
The finance department shall maintain the city accounting system, collect all sums due the city, pay obligations due from the city when properly certified as due and payable, maintain custody of all city funds, and manage the city's procurement/credit card procedures.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-153. - Human resources department.
The human resources department will administer all personnel policies and provide for the documentation of employee coverage as required by federal and state codes.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Sec. 2-154. - Directors.
All directors shall be appointed by the city manager with the concurrence of the city council and subject to the position descriptions adopted by resolution of the council.
(Ord. No. 451 , § 1, 7-10-18)
Secs. 2-155—2-175. - Reserved.
