Chapter 26 - CEMETERIES[1]

Footnotes:

Cross reference— Parks and recreation, ch. 66; streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 74.

Sec. 26-1. - Control of city cemeteries.
The city shall have control of the Fayetteville Cemetery, and any other burial place now or hereafter owned or operated by the city.
(Ord. No. 0-38-95, § 7-1(a), 10-16-95)
Sec. 26-2. - Penalties.
Any violations under this chapter will be punishable upon conviction as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. No. 0-38-95, § 7-8, 10-16-95; Ord. No. 0-55-96, § I(2), 12-16-96)
Sec. 26-3. - Burials elsewhere.
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury or cause to be buried any dead body of any person in any place within the city, other than the public or city cemeteries or other public burying grounds.
Sec. 26-4. - Interment or burial of dead bodies in mausoleums or aboveground structures.
It shall be unlawful for any person to inter or cause to be interred the dead body of any person in a mausoleum or other aboveground structure, in the Fayetteville City Cemetery, or any other burial place now or hereafter owned or operated by the city.
(Ord. No. 0-6-77, § 1(7-7), 10-10-77)
Sec. 26-5. - Unlawful to be present in cemeteries after dark.
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or be upon the grounds or premises of any city cemetery at any time after sundown.
(Ord. No. 0-38-95, § 7-2(a), 10-16-95)
Sec. 26-6. - Tombstones, foundations, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect or set up any monument, slab or tombstone in any cemetery in the city without first providing a suitable foundation for same.
(Ord. No. 0-38-95, § 7-3, 10-16-95)
Sec. 26-7. - Injuring shrubbery.
It shall be unlawful for any person to dig up, remove or destroy any shrubbery or trees within the limits of any cemetery in the city, or to in any way mutilate or injure the same, unless with the permission of the owner thereof. It shall not be lawful for any person to break down or gather any wild or cultivated flowers growing therein.
Sec. 26-8. - Disturbing or defacing graves.
It shall be unlawful for any person to in any way disturb any grave, pull up or remove anything put or placed to mark a grave, or in any way deface the same, or any paling or other enclosure placed around a grave, or remove any plank, post or timber off the graveyard, or destroy, mutilate, deface, injure, remove, or in any manner interfere with any tomb, monument, gravestone, or other structure placed in any of the cemeteries.
