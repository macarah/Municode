ARTICLE V. - POOL AND BILLIARD TABLES[5]
Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Local government authority to license, regulate, and tax billiard rooms, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 12-160. - Hours of operation.
The city commission shall not grant any permit for the operation of any such place of business between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.
(Code 2000, § 10-241; Ord. No. 630)
Sec. 12-161. - Minors prohibited.
No minor shall be admitted to any room or place of business where any pool table or billiard table is operated.
(Code 2000, § 10-242; Ord. No. 630)
Secs. 12-162—12-190. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 12-191. - Business tax license requirement.
In addition to the permit required by this article, any person coming under the provisions of this article shall obtain a business occupation tax license for any activity coming under the provisions of chapter 36, article VII.
(Code 2000, § 10-261)
Sec. 12-192. - Permit required.
Any person operating and charging for the use thereof any pool or billiard table shall first, before engaging in such business, obtain a permit therefor.
(Code 2000, § 10-262; Ord. No. 630)
Sec. 12-193. - Application for permit.
Any person applying for a permit required by this division shall make application to the city commission, setting forth:
(1)
The location of the proposed business;
(2)
The number of tables to be located therein;
(3)
The name and address of the owner thereof; and
(4)
The name and address of the person who will be employed in such place of business.
(Code 2000, § 10-263; Ord. No. 630)
Sec. 12-194. - Fee.
The fee for any permit to be issued under this division shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 2000, § 10-264; Ord. No. 630)
Sec. 12-195. - Refusal authorized.
The city commission may, at its discretion, refuse to grant a permit for the operation of any billiard or pool tables for public use at any location which it deems unsuitable or undesirable for the location of such use, and also, if it deems it unwise for the permit to be granted to the person applying therefor.
(Code 2000, § 10-265; Ord. No. 630)
Secs. 12-196—12-213. - Reserved.
