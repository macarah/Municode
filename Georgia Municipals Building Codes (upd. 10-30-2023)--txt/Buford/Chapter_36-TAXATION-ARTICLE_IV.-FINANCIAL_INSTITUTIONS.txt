ARTICLE IV. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS[4]

State Law reference— Municipal authority to levy and collect business license tax from depository financial institutions, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 36-117. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Gross receipts means the same as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(Code 2000, § 70-131; Ord. No. 1240, § 1, 12-26-1983)
Sec. 36-118. - Business license tax.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations, and state building and loan associations an annual business license tax at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of such institutions.
(Code 2000, § 70-132; Ord. No. 1240, § 1, 12-26-1983)
Sec. 36-119. - Filing of return.
Each depository financial institution within the city shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts were measured. Such returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the state department of banking and finance and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this article shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in such return.
(Code 2000, § 70-133; Ord. No. 1240, § 2, 12-26-1983)
Sec. 36-120. - Minimum tax.
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this article, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this article shall be $1,000.00 per year.
(Code 2000, § 70-134; Ord. No. 1240, § 1, 12-26-1983)
Sec. 36-121. - Due date.
The due date of taxes levied by this article shall be April 1 of each year.
(Code 2000, § 70-135; Ord. No. 1240, § 2, 12-26-1983)
