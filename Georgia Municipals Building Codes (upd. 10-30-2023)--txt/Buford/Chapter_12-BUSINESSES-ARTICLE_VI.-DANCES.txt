ARTICLE VI. - DANCES

Sec. 12-214. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Public dance means any dance where the general public is invited and an admission charge is made or contributions collected.
(Code 2000, § 10-181; Ord. No. 990)
Sec. 12-215. - Permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to hold, organize, promote, or sponsor a public dance in the city unless a permit is obtained from the city commission.
(Code 2000, § 10-182; Ord. No. 990)
Sec. 12-216. - Qualification for permit.
The permit required by this article shall only be issued to a local club or organization holding regular meetings in the city, and organized for civic, religious, or educational purposes, and which organization is to receive all net profits from such public dance.
(Code 2000, § 10-183; Ord. No. 990)
Sec. 12-217. - Fee.
The fee for any permit to be issued under this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 2000, § 10-184; Ord. No. 990)
Secs. 12-218—12-242. - Reserved.
