(4)
The building permit and inspection fee for the demolition of a building or structure within the city shall be as established by ordinance.
(5)
The building permit and inspection fee for the installation of any tent for public gatherings within the city shall be as established by ordinance.
(g)

(h)
Plumbing permit and inspection fees. There are hereby established plumbing permit fees and inspection fees to be paid for work done within the city pursuant to division 3 of this article, and the fees for such plumbing permits and inspections are as established by ordinance.
(i)
Mechanical permit and inspection fees. There are hereby established mechanical permit and inspection fees for permits and inspections in the amounts established by ordinance.
(j)
The building official of the city shall be authorized to carry out such inspections and issue such permits as may be required by this Code, but he may only issue such permits upon the payment of the fees as herein provided. Such building official may make such inspections and investigations, notwithstanding any other provisions of this Code providing for any other person or entity to make such inspections. Pursuant to the authority contained in 1996 Georgia Laws (Act No. 1046), page 1632, § 3, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-26(d), relating to self-inspection of water and sewer projects by master plumbers or utility contractors, shall not be applicable within the city.
(k)
Permit and inspection fees required by this Code shall be paid for prior to obtaining the permits provided for in this Code. Where work for which a permit is required by this Code is begun or initiated prior to obtaining the required permit, the fees required by this Code shall be paid and, as a penalty for the failure to secure a permit, an additional amount equal to the original fee required shall be paid to the city; however, the payment of such penalty shall not relieve any person from otherwise complying with the requirements of this Code in carrying out the work for which the permit is required, nor shall the payment of any such penalty relieve such person from any other penalties prescribed in this Code relating to the work performed.

Sec. 14-30. - Building permits.
(a)
Work plan prerequisite to issuance. No building or other structure or part thereof shall hereafter be constructed, enlarged or altered until a plan of the proposed work, together with a statement of the materials to be used, shall have been submitted to the building official and fire department representative. If in accordance with the provisions herein contained, the building official shall issue a permit for the proposed construction.
(b)
Removal of nonpermitted, nonconforming structures. Structures hereafter erected without permit, or not in conformity with this chapter, shall be removed and the violator prosecuted.
(c)
Permit to precede work; compliance with technical codes required. No building shall be constructed, enlarged, altered or moved until a building permit has been obtained from the building official, and such official shall not issue such permit if the proposed construction, enlargement, alteration or moving would in any manner violate the housing, building, fire, plumbing, electrical, heating and air conditioning, zoning, or subdivision codes or regulations or any other codes or ordinances now or hereafter adopted by the city.
(d)
Application. All applications for building permits shall be made in duplicate upon forms provided for this purpose by the office of the building official with payment in the amount necessary to cover the cost of such building permit, together with all other exhibits and information as provided and set forth in section 94-28.
(e)
Persons responsible. The persons responsible for obtaining permits called for herein shall be the person actually doing the construction work or the person in charge of the construction work regardless of whether such person doing the actual construction work or in charge of the actual construction work is the owner, lessee or contractor.
(f)
Display. All building permits called for in this section shall be displayed in a manner on the property under construction or on which repairs are being made so as to be visible from the public street closest to such construction site.
(g)
Penalty for violation. All persons violating any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1962, § 5-2; Code 1986, § 5-2; Ord. No. O-88-03-10, 3-28-1988)
Sec. 14-31. - Water conservation restrictions.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable, or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet, or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit or a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling built shall not include a hotel or motel.
(b)
Residential buildings. On or after July 1, 1991, no construction may be initiated within the city for any residential building of any type which:
(1)
Employs a gravity tank-type, flushometer-valve, or flushometer-tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.6 gallons of water per flush; provided, however, this subsection shall not be applicable to one-piece toilets until July 1, 1992;
(2)
Employs a showerhead that allows a flow of more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 psi of pressure;
(3)
Employs a urinal that uses more than an average of one gallon of water per flush;
(4)
Employs a lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than two gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Employs a kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute.
(c)
Commercial buildings. On or after July 1, 1992, there shall be no construction of any commercial building initiated within the city for any commercial building of any type that does not meet the requirements of subsection (b) of this section.
(d)
Repair, renovation, addition to existing buildings. The requirements of subsection (b) of this section shall apply to any residential construction initiated after July 1, 1991, and to any commercial construction initiated after July 1, 1992, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement of toilets or showers or both.
(e)
Exemptions.
(1)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of subsections (b)—(d) of this section when:
a.
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing buildings;
b.
When such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this section were installed;
c.
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence;
d.
Units to be installed are:
1.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
2.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
3.
Toilets for juveniles.
(2)
The owner or his agent of a building undergoing new construction or repair or renovation who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (e)(1) of this section shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the building official for the city. A fee in the amount established by ordinance shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
(f)
Enforcement. This section shall be enforced by the office of the building official of the city. Citations for violations may be issued by the chief building official of the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-7; Ord. No. O-91-02-04, 2-25-1991)

DIVISION 2. - ELECTRICITY

Sec. 14-51. - Electrical inspector—Generally.
(a)
Position designated. The building official for the city shall perform the duties of electrical inspector.
(b)
Authority. The electrical inspector shall be the enforcing officer, supervising the enforcement of the provisions of the electrical code. It shall be unlawful for any person to fail or refuse to obey any order of the electrical inspector issued under the provisions of this article and such person shall be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-8.
(c)
Assistants. The electrical inspector, with the approval of the chief appointing authority of the city, may appoint such number of officers, inspectors, assistants and other employees as shall be authorized from time to time.
(d)
Records. The electrical inspector shall keep, or cause to be kept, a record of the business of the office. Such records shall be open to the inspection of the public at all times.
(e)
Duty. The electrical inspector is hereby authorized, empowered and directed to regulate the placing of electrical wiring and equipment for electric light, heat or power within the scope of the electrical code and to cause all such wiring and equipment to be so placed to accomplish the purpose of the electrical code.
(f)
Right of entry. The electrical inspector shall have the right in the discharge of such inspector's duties to enter any building, manhole, or subway, or to climb any pole for the purpose of examining and testing the electrical wiring and equipment therein or thereon. For this purpose, such inspector shall be given prompt access to such places on application to the person owning or in charge of same. It shall be unlawful for any person to refuse entry to the electrical inspector when such inspector is performing the duties prescribed in this article.
(g)
Permit application forms. The electrical inspector shall prescribe the manner and form of application for a permit for the installation and maintenance of electrical wiring and equipment subject to approval by the city council. Such inspector shall maintain or cause to be maintained, a supply of application forms, with all necessary instructions, available to the public during the normal business hours of such office.
(h)
Receipt of and action on application. The electrical inspector shall receive and consider all applications for permits and shall request such additional information as may be required in connection with same. The electrical inspector shall act upon an application for a permit as filed or amended without unreasonable or unnecessary delay.
(i)
Notice of fee. The electrical inspector shall determine the application fee as required by ordinance, and notify the applicant of said fee.
(j)
Charge of violations. The electrical inspector shall bring charges against any person who, after due notice of violation of the electrical code, shall fail or refuse to make the necessary corrections as provided in this chapter.
(k)
Warrant for violators. The electrical inspector shall cause charges to be brought for violation of this division against any person found to be installing, or known to have installed, electrical work or equipment when such person does not hold the certificate and permit required by this article.
(l)
Other duties. The electrical inspector shall perform such other duties as may be specified or implied to such inspector in other sections of this Code.
(m)
Liability. The electrical inspector, or any employee acting under such inspector's instructions and authority, in the discharge of such inspector's duties, as required by this article, shall not thereby be rendered liable personally and such inspector and employees are hereby relieved from all personal liability for any damage that may occur to persons or property as a result of any act required or permitted in the discharge of official duties.
(Code 1962, §§ 8-4, 8-5, 8-7; Code 1986, § 5-23)
Sec. 14-52. - Same—Conflict of interests of officers and employees.
No officer or employee connected with the electrical inspector's office shall be financially interested in the furnishing of labor, material or appliances or the construction, alteration or maintenance of electrical installations or in the making of plans or specifications therefor, unless such person is the owner of such building. No employee shall engage in any work which is inconsistent with such employee's duties or with the interest of the inspector's office.
(Code 1962, § 8-10; Code 1986, § 5-24)
Sec. 14-53. - Permit.
(a)
Required. No person shall install or repair any electrical wiring or equipment, except repairs that do not alter or change the original wiring or equipment, nor shall the load be increased, until a permit therefor has been secured from the electrical inspector.
(b)
Application. It shall be the duty of master electricians to apply for permits to install or alter electrical wiring or equipment within the scope of this article and the electrical code. Before applying for said permit, such electrician shall determine that the work or equipment contemplated is in compliance with the provisions of this article and the electrical code and shall furnish with the application all information requested by the electrical inspector. All applications for permits shall be written on forms supplied by the electrical inspector's office and shall be dealt with as provided elsewhere in this chapter.
(c)
Required to obtain before work commences. If any person commences any electrical installation or maintenance within the scope of this code before obtaining the necessary permit, such person shall be subject to the penalties prescribed herein.
(d)
Penalty for failure to obtain permit. Any person who shall commence any work within the scope of this article or the electrical code without first having obtained a proper permit therefor shall pay a penalty in the amount established as a penalty pursuant to section 14-29 in addition to the regular inspection fee for said work. In the event of repair work requiring a permit, the party shall notify the inspector and get a permit by the end of the next working day.
(e)
Issuance of permit. When and if satisfied that the application is in order and that the work described therein complies with this article and the electrical code, the inspector shall issue a permit to proceed with the work described in the application.
(f)
Refusal to issue. The inspector shall refuse to issue a permit if such inspector's examination of the application and all information pertaining thereto indicate that the work or equipment described or contemplated therein does not conform to the requirements of this article or the electrical code. The inspector shall return the application to the applicant with the refusal to issue a permit. Such refusal shall, when requested, be in writing and shall contain the reasons therefor. The inspector shall also refuse to issue a permit to any applicant who has failed or refused to comply with this article or the electrical code or any order of the city.
(g)
Permit granted in violation. Except as provided in this article, the electrical inspector shall not grant a permit to any person unless such person has fully qualified under all the terms of this article. Any permit granted in violation of this article shall be null and void.
(h)
Failure to correct defects within time limit. Any person certified as prescribed in this article, who fails to correct any defect in the work after having been duly notified for a period of ten days, shall receive no further permit until such defects have been corrected.
(i)
Revocation of permit or approval. The electrical inspector shall revoke any permit or approval issued by such inspector's office when, in such inspector's opinion, said permit was obtained by misrepresentation of the material fact or by false statement in the application or the plans.
(Code 1962, §§ 8-5—8-8; Code 1986, § 5-31; Ord. No. O-88-03-10, 3-28-1988)
Sec. 14-54. - Inspections.
(a)
Required. All new electrical work and equipment and such portions of existing systems as may be affected by new work, shall be inspected to ensure compliance with all the requirements of this article and the electrical code and to assure that the installation of the electrical system and equipment is in accordance with approved plans. It shall be the duty of the electrical inspector, either personally or by assistants, to inspect all electrical work and equipment within the scope of this article and the electrical code during the progress of such work, as well as when it is completed. Such inspection is to be made from time to time and must be thorough and complete.
(b)
Fees. Inspection fees shall be on a cash basis. Said inspection fee shall include a second inspection where defects or a violation are noted in the first inspection.
(c)
Notice of readiness. The master electrician shall determine when work, as required by the electrical inspector, is ready for inspection and shall notify the electrical inspector's office of same. The master electrician shall further notify the electrical inspector's office at the completion of any electrical installation within the scope of this article and the electrical code. Any person who shall fail to notify the inspector's office of readiness for inspection as required herein shall pay, in addition to the regular inspection fee, an additional fee in the amount established by ordinance.
(d)
Roughing-in inspection and approval. The inspector shall make roughing-in inspection within 24 hours after notice by a permit holder that such work is ready for inspection. Upon satisfactory completion of the roughing-in inspection, approval shall be noted on a label or card of suitable design showing the date and name of the inspector. Said notice shall be displayed in a prominent place at the site of the work.
(e)
Covering work. No part of the electrical system shall be covered until it has been inspected and approved as prescribed in this article.
(f)
Uncovering required upon order. If any part of the electrical system has been covered in violation of this article, it shall be uncovered upon order from the electrical inspector at the expense of the party so violating this article.
(g)
Final inspection. At the completion of any electrical installation of any work or equipment within the scope of this article or the electrical code, the electrical inspector shall make a final inspection to determine compliance. If such final inspection proves satisfactory to the electrical inspector, such inspector shall issue and attach inside the main electrical disconnecting means a suitable label of approval, showing the date, the inspector's name and the name of the person responsible for the work, and notify the electric distribution company.
(h)
Reinspection. The inspector shall make reinspections when necessitated by unsatisfactory work or equipment and shall charge therefor the fees provided.
(i)
Temporary installations. The inspector shall inspect temporary electrical installations and shall have authority to cause the same to be installed in accordance with this article and the electrical code.
(j)
Damaging inspection notice. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove, deface or damage any inspection notice posted as required by this article.
(Code 1962, §§ 8-5, 8-7; Code 1986, § 5-32)
Sec. 14-55. - Defective work; dangerous condition; connection; disconnection.
(a)
Notice, compliance. Upon notice from the electrical inspector to any owner, agent or person responsible for any wiring or equipment that is in violation of the provisions of this article or the electrical code, or has become unsafe, said owner, agent or person, shall cease to use the wiring or equipment until such person has completed such changes as may be ordered by the electrical inspector and same have been approved by the inspector. Upon notice from the electrical inspector that any part of the wiring or equipment inspected is not acceptable under the provision of this article or the electrical code, the master electrician in charge, or the owner or agent, shall make the necessary changes, and so notify the inspector.
(b)
Stop work orders. The electrical inspector shall have the power to issue stop work orders on any work within the scope of this article or the electrical code when such work is being done contrary to the provisions thereof or in a dangerous or unsafe manner. Such order shall be in writing and shall be given to the owner of such property or to the owner's agent, or to the person doing the work and shall state the conditions under which work may be resumed. Where any emergency exists, oral notice, given by the electrical inspector, shall be sufficient.
(c)
Disconnect orders. The electrical inspector shall have the power to order disconnection of any electrical installation or equipment within the scope of this article or the electrical code when such installation of equipment has been installed, or through use has become contrary to the provision thereof or is dangerous or unsafe. Such order shall be given to the owner of such installation or the owner's agent and shall state the conditions under which connection may be resumed. Where any emergency exists, oral notice, given by the electrical inspector, shall be sufficient. In any case of failure to comply, the electrical inspector shall, after due notice, order discontinuance of electrical service to the locality or building concerned and shall enforce said discontinuance until compliance with the requirements.
(d)
Notice of violation. In case of violations that, in the opinion of the electrical inspector do not require stop work or disconnect orders, such inspector shall notify the person responsible. Such notice shall state the violation and corrections required, and shall if required, be in writing.
(e)
Temporary connection. The electrical inspector may permit temporary connection during the course of construction for temporary light, heat or power, when in such inspector's opinion, such connection is required. The inspector shall determine the period of such requirement and shall at the end of that period, cause the temporary connection to be discontinued.
(f)
Refusal of connection. The electrical inspector may refuse to authorize a connection so long as, in such inspector's opinion, any violation of this article or the electrical code exists.
(Code 1962, §§ 8-5—8-8; Code 1986, § 5-33)
Sec. 14-56. - Ordering illegal work.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to order any wiring or equipment installed which violates any provision of this article or the electrical code.
(b)
In the event an owner, employer, or any person of authority over a master electrician shall order work done or equipment installed that violates the provisions of this article or the electrical code, it shall be the duty of the master electrician to immediately notify, in writing, the electrical inspector of all particulars concerning same.
(Code 1962, §§ 8-7, 8-8; Code 1986, § 5-34)
Sec. 14-57. - Maintenance of system, etc.
All electrical systems, both existing and new, and all devices, appliances and equipment attached therein shall be maintained in a safe manner. All devices and safeguards that are required by this article or the electrical code shall be maintained in good working order. The owner or owner's agent shall be responsible for the maintenance of such system, devices, appliances and equipment.
(Code 1962, § 8-7; Code 1986, § 5-35)
Sec. 14-58. - Maintaining clearance of service drops.
No person shall build, alter or erect any building or other structure that will affect the clearance required for service drops and point of attachment of same. Upon discovery of a violation of this provision, the person responsible shall be required to remove said building or other structure or to bear all expense involved in altering the service drop to obtain the necessary safe clearances.
(Code 1962, § 8-7; Code 1986, § 5-36)
Sec. 14-59. - Standards for materials.
All wires, devices, appliances and apparatus installed, used, sold or offered for sale within the city shall conform to the rules and regulations of this article and the electrical code, and, for the purpose hereof, they shall bear the label of Underwriter's Laboratories, Inc., or shall be approved by the authority enforcing the code. Such label or listing shall be prima facie evidence that said item conforms to this article and the electrical code.
(Code 1962, § 8-7; Code 1986, § 5-37)

DIVISION 3. - PLUMBING

Sec. 14-77. - Inspector's right-of-entry.
The plumbing inspector shall have the authority to enter any building at reasonable hours for the purpose of inspecting the plumbing. Any person attempting to prevent such inspection, whether owner, lessee, or agent, shall be in violation of this article.
(Code 1962, § 17-18; Code 1986, § 5-106)
Sec. 14-78. - Permit—Required.
No person shall install or repair any plumbing except repairs not involving the removing or alteration of soil, waste-vent pipes or traps, until a permit has been secured from the plumbing inspector. No less than four hours before any plumbing work is started, including drainage, sewage, extensions, or alterations, except as noted in this section, a permit must be secured from the plumbing inspector for such work to be done.
(Code 1962, § 17-9; Code 1986, § 5-108)
Sec. 14-79. - Same—Restriction.
No person engaged in the plumbing business shall allow such person's name to be used, directly or indirectly, by any other person for the purpose of securing a permit, sending in notices or returns or doing work under the license of any other person.
(Code 1962, § 17-15; Code 1986, § 5-110)
Sec. 14-80. - Inspections—Required.
It shall be the duty of the plumbing inspector, either personally or by assistants, to inspect all plumbing work during the progress of the work and upon completion.
(Code 1962, § 71-10; Code 1986, § 5-112)
Sec. 14-81. - Same—Notices.
Inspection notices upon blanks furnished by the plumbing inspector shall be filed in the office of the plumbing inspector by each person doing plumbing work, giving notice of the location, kind of work and the time for inspection, and the work will be inspected as soon as possible thereafter. When the work has been completed, the plumber must immediately notify the inspector. At the time the final inspection is made, the plumber or the plumber's designated representative shall be present. No plumbing work may be covered until the inspection is made. It shall be unlawful for any person to fail to comply with this procedure. Upon final inspection, if the inspector determines that the plumbing and material are in accordance with this article, a final inspection certificate shall be issued. If the inspector finds that any plumbing has not been properly installed or does not comply with this article, the inspector shall notify the owner of such property and, upon notice so to do, the owner shall make such changes and corrections in the plumbing as may be ordered by the inspector; failure to make such changes within five days after notice shall subject the owner, agent or lessee to punishment, as prescribed in section 1-8. Also, if the necessary correction is not made, the city may refuse to furnish water to the premises.
(Code 1962, § 17-11; Code 1986, § 5-113)

It shall be unlawful to make any installation for service of any such gas until a permit is secured from the building official may appoint for such purposes.

Sec. 14-109. - Meter installation.
One gas meter shall be installed for each dwelling unit, which shall specifically include but not be limited to one meter for each unit in an apartment-type building.
(Code 1962, § 11-3; Code 1986, § 5-125)
Sec. 14-110. - Gas not to be turned on unless owner or occupant is present.
The gas supply to any building using gas appliances or to which gas piping has been constructed or installed shall not be turned on unless the owner or occupant of said building or premises is physically present at the time.
(Code 1962, § 11-4; Code 1986, § 5-126)
