Sec. 66-1. - Prohibited acts in public parks and grounds.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cut, pluck, break down, damage or destroy any tree, shrub, plant, flower, grass or vines in any of the public parks or grounds of the city, or to bathe in, or throw any stick or other substance, to befoul or pollute the waters of any fountain, lake, spring, or stream, natural or artificial, within any public park or grounds of the city; or to damage, deface or destroy any gate, fence, railing, enclosure, fountain, water or gas fixture or other fixture or property of the city situated or being within said public park or grounds; or to otherwise intermeddle or injuriously interfere with any of the property in said parks and public grounds; or to drive any vehicle or animal, or to ride any animal upon the grounds or walks of any such public park or grounds of the city; or bring any animal within the park enclosure including any animals for the operation of a petting zoo; or to operate any motorized or non-motorized carnival type rides, train type rides, and water or splash rides in said parks and public grounds.
(Code 1962, § 6-9; Code 1986, § 16-1; Ord. No. O-2021-12, 7-22-2021)
State Law reference— Damaging property, O.C.G.A. § 16-17-22 et seq.
Sec. 66-2. - Damaging playground equipment.
It shall be unlawful for any person to vandalize or, in any manner, destroy or mutilate any playground equipment installed and maintained by the city in any public park.
(Code 1962, § 6-16; Code 1986, § 16-2)
State Law reference— Damaging property, O.C.G.A. § 16-17-22 et seq.
Sec. 66-3. - Hours of operation.
All public parks or playgrounds shall be open for public use; provided, however, that the public use thereof shall be limited to the period of time between 8:00 a.m. and 9:00 p.m. in those parks or playgrounds where such opening and closing times are conspicuously posted by the city or under its authority. It shall be unlawful for any person to enter upon or remain upon the premises, or any part thereof, owned by the city and known as playground property before the time said premises are opened to the public by the person in charge thereof or after said premises are closed to the public by the person in charge thereof in the afternoon or night.
(Code 1962, §§ 6-15, 15-30; Code 1986, § 16-3; Ord. No. O-88-08-31, 8-22-1988)
