Sec. 26-1. - Residence requirements.
In the event a member of the council moves such member's residence to without the district from which such member was elected, such office will become vacant immediately. There shall be no durational residency requirement as a condition to qualifying as a candidate for the council from a particular district other than what is already expressed in the city charter or general law of the state, except that all candidates for the council must attain their residency in the district they seek to represent at least by the time they qualify themselves as candidates.
(Code 1986, § 9-7; Court Order, § 8)
Sec. 26-2. - Nonpartisan elections.
Political parties shall not conduct primaries for city offices, and all names of candidates for city offices shall be listed without party designated.
(Code 1986, § 9-9; Court Order, § 10; Ord. No. O-94-06-17, 6-23-1994)
Sec. 26-3. - Charter and state law govern elections.
The city Charter and the state law will continue to govern elections for the city and the powers and duties of the officers of the city. This chapter shall not affect in any way the validity of any action taken by the city or by an officer or agent thereof.
(Code 1986, § 9-10; Court Order, § 11)
Sec. 26-4. - Voting machines.
Voting machines are authorized and directed to be used for recording and computing the vote at all municipal elections held by this city.
(Code 1986, § 9-13; Court Order, § 7A-7)
State Law reference— Voting machines, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-310 et seq.
