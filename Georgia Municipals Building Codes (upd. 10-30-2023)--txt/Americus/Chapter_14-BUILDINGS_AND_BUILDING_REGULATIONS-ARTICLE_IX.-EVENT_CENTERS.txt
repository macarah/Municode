ARTICLE IX. - EVENT CENTERS

Sec. 14-400. - Authorization of event centers.
Subject to the provisions of this article, the provisions of chapter 6, and the provisions of chapter 94 of the city Code of Ordinances, there shall be authorized within the corporate limits of the city event centers to operate as privately owned for-profit commercial business enterprises in accord with the terms of this article.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-401. - Definitions.
Event. An event consists of a celebration, reception, party, concert, holiday gathering, family gathering, corporate function, general gathering, birthday party, retirement event, employee appreciation event, wedding, wedding reception, anniversary celebration, funeral, and other similar parties, receptions, and events. An event does not include retail sales, amphitheaters, rodeos, circuses, or similar public events.
Event center. An event center is a commercial building or facility where events are permitted to occur under this article. Any such center shall be a permanent structure (and not a temporary structure) which meets all local and state-wide building codes which are applicable to the type of commercial building to be used as an event center. An event center building or facility, or the portion of a building or facility utilized as an event center, shall consist of not less than 4,200 square feet and not more than 10,000 square feet. No residential structure shall be considered to be an event center. An event center may host either public events or private events. An event center shall be open only for scheduled event-based operations.
Private event. A private event is an event held under this article which is an invitation-only event.
Public event. A public event is an event held under this article where the public is invited or allowed to attend. For purposes of this article, an event for which tickets or other evidence of authority to attend the event are required for persons in attendance at the event, whether such tickets are purchased or distributed without any cost or consideration, shall be considered to be a public event.
Zoning code. The zoning code means chapter 94 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Americus.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-402. - Business license.
The owner of an event center shall be required to obtain a business license for the operation of the event center under chapter 46 of the Code of Ordinances of the city. A separate business license shall be required for each event center within the corporate limits of the city. The fees for business licenses for an event center shall be as provided in the city Code.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-403. - Parking.
An event center shall have one parking space for each five seats in the center or one parking space for each 200 square feet of gross area of the event center, whichever is greater. With respect to calculating required parking spaces for an event center, the provisions of subsection 94-239(4) of the city Code shall be used for each event center.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-404. - Location of events.
All events held at an events center shall be held inside the event center building. The interior and exterior of the events center shall be well-lighted at all times during any event held at the events center.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-405. - Times of operation.
An event center shall not authorize the consumption or serving of alcoholic beverages later than is authorized under section 6-57.1 of the city Code and shall not operate or remain open after 2:00 a.m.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-406. - Noise control.
The noise level emanating from an event center shall not violate the provisions of section 62-110 or section 62-111 of the city Code.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-407. - Traffic control.
The owner of an event center, or the manager thereof, shall notify the city police department at least 72 hours in advance of any event occurring at the event center so that the police department can be available to direct and manage traffic flow to and from the event center before and after each event. The event center shall either hire and compensate private security or hire and provide compensation to at least two off-duty POST-certified law enforcement officers to provide security at each event center where it is anticipated that the number of persons in attendance at an event is likely to exceed 100 persons. The owner of the event center, each employee of the event center, and all attendees to events at the event center, shall comply with all lawful orders of the police department, the city fire and emergency services department, the county sheriff's department and all other law enforcement officers regarding activities and compliance with State law or local ordinances during any event at the event center.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-408. - Capacity limitations.
No event center shall be authorized to exceed capacity limitations with respect to the event center as established by the city fire marshal pursuant to applicable regulations.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-409. - Health and safety.
Each event center shall be obligated to comply with all applicable health and safety regulations during an event, including, but not limited to, health and safety regulations relating to the wearing of masks, social distancing, and other similar regulations established by state law, gubernatorial executive order, or local ordinances.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
Sec. 14-410. - Alcoholic beverages.
No alcoholic beverages may be consumed or served at a public event or a private event held at an event center except as provided in section 6-57.1 of the city Code.
(Ord. No. O-2021-1, pt. 1, 1-21-2021)
