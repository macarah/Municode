Article I - IN GENERAL

Sec. 15-20-1. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the term "public cemeteries" shall be deemed to include, where appropriate, all cemeteries and/or mausoleums within the city limits which are owned or controlled by the city.
(Code 1972, § 7-1; Ord. No. 96-47, § 1, 8-27-96)
Sec. 15-20-2. - Trust fund—Created; administration; annual audit and report.
There is hereby created a trust fund to be known as the "City of LaGrange Cemetery Trust Fund," which shall be invested, managed and expended by the city for the maintenance and improvement of any and all mausoleums and cemeteries now or hereafter owned by the city. The administrators of the cemetery trust fund shall be the mayor or a member of the council designated by the mayor who shall serve only while a member of the council and at the pleasure of the mayor; the city manager; and the assistant city manager. The administrators of the cemetery trust fund shall collect and hold the funds paid into the cemetery trust fund, invest the same and expend the income from the trust fund for the purposes provided in this section. The administrators may encroach upon the principal of the trust fund, but only after having received the concurrence of the mayor and council to such encroachment at a regularly scheduled public meeting of the mayor and council. The cemetery trust fund shall be audited annually at the same time the affairs of the city are normally audited. The administrators of the trust fund shall maintain such records and accounts as may be necessary and reasonable by accepted accounting practices in order to reflect clearly and accurately the status of such fund. A written report of the condition of such trust fund shall be made to the mayor and council at least annually but unless otherwise requested by the mayor and council, the annual report of the audit of the trust fund shall be acceptable.
(Code 1972, § 7-28; Ord. No. 07-12, § 4, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 5, 12-13-16)
State Law reference— Investment in certain insured deposits, O.C.G.A. § 7-1-793; city as trustee of cemetery funds, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-4 et seq.; investments generally, O.C.G.A. § 36-80-3; delegation of investment authority to finance officer, O.C.G.A. § 36-80-4; budgets and audits, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-1 et seq.; investment of bond proceeds, O.C.G.A. § 36-82-7; local government investment pool, O.C.G.A. § 36-83-1 et seq.; accounting for public funds, O.C.G.A. § 45-8-1 et seq.; investment in obligations of United States government, O.C.G.A. § 45-8-18.
Sec. 15-20-3. - Same—Payments into fund.
As each crypt in the mausoleum is sold an amount equal to ten (10) percent of the purchase price therefor shall be paid into and become a part of the cemetery trust fund and shall be administered as a part thereof.
(Code 1972, § 7-29)
Sec. 15-20-4. - Superintendent; appointment, supervision.
There shall be a superintendent of cemeteries, who shall be directly responsible to and under the supervision of the city manager.
(Code 1972, § 7-2; Ord. No. 20-24, § 11, 11-24-20)
Cross reference— Administration, Ch. 5-1 et seq.
Sec. 15-20-5. - Opening graves generally; permit.
(a)
The digging of graves at all public cemeteries in the city shall be performed by either employees of the City of LaGrange under the supervision of the superintendent of cemeteries or by a person, firm or corporation licensed to dig graves in the city; provided such person, firm or corporation shall in each instance have a permit to dig a grave in the public cemetery and shall otherwise meet and comply with the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
Any person, firm or corporation having a license to dig graves in a public cemetery must apply for and receive a permit from the superintendent of cemeteries of the city to dig each particular grave, must have and maintain public liability insurance in an amount not less than one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) workers' compensation insurance in the statutory amounts, and meet such other minimum insurance requirements as the city manager may from time to time require. Evidence of any such insurance must be continuously on file with the superintendent of cemeteries or evidence thereof presented to the superintendent of cemeteries with each request for a permit.
(c)
Additionally, any such person, firm or corporation in order to be licensed and to receive a permit from the superintendent of cemeteries must demonstrate the ability to perform the services required hereby to the satisfaction of the superintendent who shall have the right to refuse a permit or the right to revoke a permit after issued if in his opinion such revocation is in the best interest of the city and the public.
(d)
At the time of revocation of such permit, the superintendent of cemeteries shall provide such person, firm or corporation with notice of revocation and of their right to a hearing to appeal such revocation to the city manager.
(e)
Each permit shall be issued upon the express condition that the permittee agrees to indemnify, save, and hold the city, its officers, agents, or employees harmless of and from any damages to person or property or any claims therefor, and any costs or expenses relating thereto, which may arise, occur or result from any work which may be done pursuant to such permit.
(f)
Except as provided herein, it shall be the normal practice for any employees under the supervision of the superintendent of cemeteries to open and dig graves only for paupers or only in those cases deemed to be an emergency by the city manager, or his or her designee.
(Code 1972, § 7-3; Ord. No. 88-110A, § 1, 9-13-88; Ord. No. 96-47, § 2, 8-27-96; Ord. No. 20-24, § 12, 11-24-20)
Cross reference— Burial permits, § 15-20-23.
Sec. 15-20-6. - Records; enforcing ordinances and regulations; reports.
The superintendent shall keep a record of the owners of lots in the public cemeteries and shall not allow any burial on a lot without the authority of the owner. It shall be his duty to see that all regulations and ordinances of the city in reference to such cemeteries are enforced, and to report all violations of same. He shall maintain a system of records sufficient to show the owner of each lot and the name of each person interred in a lot, and such other information as may from time to time be required.
(Code 1972, § 7-5)
Sec. 15-20-7. - Objectionable trees and shrubs.
If the roots or branches of any trees or shrubs on any lot in a public cemetery shall become detrimental to adjacent lots or drives or unsightly or interfere with the orderly operation of the cemetery, the superintendent shall have the right to enter the lot and remove such trees and shrubs or such parts thereof as he shall determine to be detrimental, unsightly or which interfere with the proper use and maintenance of a public cemetery.
(Code 1972, § 7-4)
Sec. 15-20-8. - Wilted flowers, etc.
When flowers in public cemeteries become faded, dead or badly wilted, they shall be removed. Artificial flowers, floral arrangements and the like shall be removed without permission when in the opinion of the superintendent they have become faded, soiled, misplaced, scattered, objectionable or unsightly.
(Code 1972, § 7-6)
Secs. 15-20-9—15-20-20. - Reserved.

Sec. 15-20-23. - Burial permit.
A burial permit must be obtained from the superintendent and shall be made in duplicate whenever a grave in a public cemetery is to be opened. The permit shall contain the name, age, residence, date of death and place of death. The burial permit must be obtained twenty-four (24) hours before the time of burial.
(Code 1972, § 7-9; Ord. No. 88-110A, § 2, 9-13-88)
Cross reference— Permits for opening graves, § 15-20-5.

Sec. 15-20-24. - Burials according to plan.
All burials in public cemeteries will be made according to the plan on file in the office of the superintendent of cemeteries, as approved by the city manager, and no deviation from these plans shall be permitted.
(Code 1972, § 7-10; Ord. No. 20-24, § 13, 11-24-20)
Sec. 15-20-25. - Burial fees.
In those instances in which city employees are involved in the digging of graves, superintending burials and removing dirt, burial fees shall be paid to the city as follows:
(1)
For adult graves for funerals held Monday through Friday, three hundred dollars ($300.00); for funerals held on Saturday, on Sunday or on city holidays designated in section 5-1-3 of this Code, the burial fee shall be three hundred fifty dollars ($350.00);
(2)
Infant graves for funerals held Monday through Friday, ninety dollars ($90.00); for funerals held on Saturday, on Sunday or on city holidays designated in section 5-1-3 of this Code, the burial fee shall be one hundred five dollars ($105.00).
(Code 1972, § 7-11; Ord. No. 85-12, § 2, 6-27-85; Ord. No. 88-110A, § 3, 9-13-88; Ord. No. 90-32, § 2, 6-27-90)
Sec. 15-20-26. - Paupers.
The superintendent shall not bury any person as a pauper in a public cemetery unless such burial is approved by the county department of family and children services or the mayor, or in his absence, the city manager. A permit shall be obtained as in all other interments.
(Code 1972, § 7-12)
State Law reference— Interment of deceased indigents, O.C.G.A. § 36-12-5.
Sec. 15-20-27. - Burial in other than certain cemeteries.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any undertaker or any other person to bury or cause to be buried, or to in any manner aid or assist in the burial of the dead body of any human being in any cemetery in the city other than a public cemetery as defined in this section or a private cemetery established and approved as specified in article IV of this chapter.
(b)
As of the date of this section, as amended, the public cemeteries in the city are Eastview Cemetery, Hillview East Cemetery, Hillview Cemetery, Shadowlawn Cemetery, Southview Annex Cemetery, and Southview Cemetery.
(Code 1972, § 7-13; Ord. No. 95-22, § 1, 6-13-95; Ord. No. 96-20, § 3, 3-26-96; Ord. No. 98-23, § 1, 8-25-98)
Sec. 15-20-28. - Monument foundation specifications.
All foundations for monuments in public cemeteries shall consist of a mixture of one (1) part cement, two and one-half (2½) parts sand and four and one-half (4½) parts stone, and they shall be inspected by the superintendent before the monument is placed.
(Code 1972, § 7-14)
Sec. 15-20-29. - Grave depth; outer burial containers.
To authorize an interment in the city:
(1)
All graves shall be excavated to a depth of not less than four (4) feet.
(2)
All graves, except pauper graves authorized by section 15-20-26 and except graves having a length of less than five (5) feet, must have an outer burial container made of metal, concrete, fiberglass or equivalent material acceptable to or approved by the superintendent of cemeteries.
(Code 1972, § 7-15)
Sec. 15-20-30. - Approval of walls, etc.; cleanup.
Wall or lot enclosures in public cemeteries where allowed must be approved by the superintendent and foundations for same must be poured of concrete to rules and specifications as the city may prescribe. All replacements and repairs to walls and other lot enclosures must also be approved and inspected by the superintendent. All contractors and workers shall be responsible for cleaning up all materials and debris and removing same, and shall be responsible for any damage to adjacent lots, monuments, markers and property of others.
(Code 1972, § 7-16)
Sec. 15-20-31. - Removal of excess dirt.
Undertakers or any other person opening or closing graves in any public cemetery shall be responsible for the removal of all excess soil from the location, shall have at least a two-foot covering over all graves and shall not mound up the soil. If excess soil is left on or near any grave and has to be moved by city employees, a charge will be made for this work on an hourly basis to the person leaving the excess dirt.
(Code 1972, § 7-17)
Sec. 15-20-32. - Mutilating, etc., graves, structures or plants.
It shall be unlawful for any person to mutilate, destroy, deface, injure or remove any grave, tomb, monument, gravestone or any structure placed in the public cemeteries, or any fence, railing or other work for the protection or ornamentation of any tomb, monument or gravestone or for the ornamentation or protection of said cemeteries, or to wilfully destroy, cut, break, remove or injure any tree, shrub or plant that may have been planted, or that may be growing in said cemeteries or commit trespass in the limits of said cemeteries.
(Code 1972, § 7-18)
State Law reference— Criminal trespass and damage to property, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-20 et seq.; disturbance of contents of grave, O.C.G.A. § 31-21-44.
Sec. 15-20-33. - Disposition of trash, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person, while working on his lot or enclosure, to place upon the lot or enclosure of another, on, in or upon any walk or street in the public cemeteries, any trash, grass or other refuse or litter, but shall place the same where the superintendent shall direct.
(Code 1972, § 7-19)
Cross reference— Solid waste, Ch. 15-10.
Sec. 15-20-34. - Injury by trees, etc., on adjoining lots.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning a lot in a public cemetery having thereon any tree, shrub or other plan to allow said tree, shrub or plant to injure any monument or decoration in an adjoining lot. On complaint being made thereof to the superintendent, it shall be his duty to notify the person owning said tree, shrub or plant to remove the same, and in case the owner thereof shall fail or refuse to do so, the superintendent may notify the mayor and council, who may cause to be removed said tree, shrub or plant.
(Code 1972, § 7-20)

Article III - ENTOMBMENT

Sec. 15-20-46. - Generally.
(a)
This article shall apply only to publicly owned mausoleums and crypts.
(b)
All entombments in the mausoleum shall be performed by the appropriate personnel of the city. Such entombments will require a minimum notice of twenty-four (24) hours and requests for entombment shall be in writing on the "Interment Request" form provided by the city.
(Code 1972, § 7-25)
Sec. 15-20-47. - Flowers.
As there will be no way provided for the hanging of flowers on individual crypts, flowers shall be placed on the sidewalks at the base of each row of crypts, and shall be removed by the superintendent when they become faded, weatherbeaten or otherwise unsightly.
(Code 1972, § 7-21)
Sec. 15-20-48. - Lettering and designs on crypts.
All lettering on crypts shall be cut into the stone and no form of materials such as inset metal liners, plastic or otherwise shall be attached thereto except that an approved stone paint, gold in color, may be used to accentuate said lettering. The cutting or painting of all letters shall be at the expense of the owner of the crypt and all lettering must meet the following specifications:
(1)
Family names—Three (3) inches;
(2)
Given name—One and one-half (1½) inches;
(3)
Dates—One and one-fourth (1¼) inches;
(4)
Fraternal emblems, flowers and other designs may be cut into crypt doors, provided they are in good taste and consistent with other markings in existence and conform with the policies established by the mayor and council. All crypt fronts are to be provided by the city, and no other crypt fronts will be allowed.
(Code 1972, § 7-22)
Sec. 15-20-49. - Closing fee.
The fee for closing crypts is established at one-half of the normal grave-closing costs specified in section 15-20-25 of this chapter as the same now exists or may be hereafter amended. All closings shall be performed during daylight hours and only by the appropriate employees of the city.
(Code 1972, § 7-23; Ord. No. 96-47, § 3, 8-27-96)
Sec. 15-20-50. - Use of crypts.
Crypts shall be used only for the entombment of deceased human bodies or their ashes, cremation or other remains, including appropriate containers, urns or caskets.
(Code 1972, § 7-24)
Sec. 15-20-51. - Recording of original sales and transfers.
Original sales and all subsequent transfers of title under this article shall be recorded both in the office of Shadowlawn Cemetery of the city and in the deed records in the office of the clerk of superior court of the county.
(Code 1972, § 7-26)
State Law reference— Recordation and registration of deeds and other instruments, O.C.G.A. § 44-2-1 et seq.

Article IV - PRIVATE CEMETERIES

Sec. 15-20-61. - Definition.
A private cemetery is a cemetery intended to be used for burial by the landowner or with the landowner's permission and the use of which is or has been restricted by the landowner or the creator of a private cemetery to relatives or some other portion of the general public such as neighbors or friends, but not the community at large.
(Ord. No. 95-22, § 2, 6-13-95)
Sec. 15-20-62. - Requirements for authorization.
A private cemetery will be authorized and a permit therefor issued, pursuant to application, in accordance with the following requirements:
(1)
A plat of survey prepared by a registered land surveyor licensed in the State of Georgia shall be prepared and filed with the assistant city manager along with the application;
(2)
A cemetery survey shall indicate that the cemetery contains not less than two (2) acres;
(3)
The location of the proposed cemetery is compatible with adjacent land uses, existing or proposed highways and any other elements or factors deemed by the city to affect the public health, safety and welfare of the inhabitants of the area surrounding the proposed location; the proposed site must contain a buffered area having a width of not less than ten (10) feet which must be suitably landscaped and maintained with trees and shrubs; and
(4)
Adequate access is available by public street or private or restricted means of ingress and egress.
(Ord. No. 95-22, § 2, 6-13-95; Ord. No. 07-12, § 4, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 5, 12-13-16)
Sec. 15-20-63. - Superintendent to maintain inventory.
The superintendent of cemeteries of the City of LaGrange or his successor in office by whatever name used shall prepare and maintain an inventory of all private cemeteries established and permitted pursuant to the provision of this article. Other than maintaining in his office and in his records a plat or plats of each such private cemetery within the city and maintaining an inventory of private cemeteries in the city, the superintendent of cemeteries of the city shall have no responsibility for or duty to supervise the operation of any such private cemetery.
(Ord. No. 95-22, § 2, 6-13-95)
Sec. 15-20-64. - Rules and regulations.
The owner, creator, trust or any other legal entity which shall own, supervise or have control of a private cemetery shall have the right and responsibility of creating any rules or regulations necessary to or appropriate for the operation of such private cemetery to ensure that the same shall not at any time constitute a health hazard or be or constitute a nuisance.
(Ord. No. 95-22, § 2, 6-13-95)
Sec. 15-20-65. - Private cemetery not to be marketed as public cemetery.
A cemetery created pursuant to the provisions of this article shall be operated and maintained solely as a private cemetery and the lots and burial spaces therein shall not be marketed or sold to the general public and shall be maintained purely as a private or family cemetery. No private or family cemetery created pursuant to the provisions of this article shall at any time become a public cemetery unless the same shall be converted to and become a public cemetery pursuant to and in accordance with the appropriate laws in force at that time and as the same may thereafter be amended.
(Ord. No. 95-22, § 2, 6-13-95)
