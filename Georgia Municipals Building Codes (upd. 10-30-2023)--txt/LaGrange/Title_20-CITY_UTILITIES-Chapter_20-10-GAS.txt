Sec. 20-10-1. - Meter location.
(a)
The customer or builder shall provide a location for the gas meter acceptable to the city. The city reserves the right to refuse to set a meter in an unacceptable location.
(b)
Where there is only one (1) riser in a building, it must extend to a point within twenty-four (24) inches (measured in a horizontal direction) of the head of the service and to the right of the same.
(c)
When, to accommodate different tenants, two (2) or more meters are desired in a building, the city will set as many meters as there are separate customers, connecting the meters to one (1) service pipe, provided the risers of pipes leading to these different tenants are terminated according to the city specifications.
(d)
Meters shall not be located within less than six (6) feet of heating appliances, near furnaces, under houses with less than two (2) feet of clearance above the ground or placed in any situation where they will be inaccessible or liable to injury.
(e)
The city reserves the sole right to disconnect, remove or reset gas meters.
(Code 1972, § 24-29)
Sec. 20-10-2. - Separate meter for each living unit and building.
Except as otherwise provided by this chapter, separate gas meters are required, and shall be installed, in each living quarter, living unit or place of business in the city to which gas is supplied by the city.
(Code 1972, § 24-30)

Sec. 20-10-4. - Same—Subject to change.
The rates fixed in section 20-10-3 are based upon rates presently authorized by the Federal Power Commission. In the event the Federal Power Commission shall authorize an increase or decrease in the cost of natural gas to the city, the mayor and council at any regular or special meeting shall have authority by resolution to increase or decrease the cost of gas to customers of the city by an amount substantially equal to the increased or decreased cost to the city.
(Code 1972, § 24-33)
Sec. 20-10-5. - Free service.
No free service shall at any time be furnished from the gas system. All services shall be furnished in accordance with rates in effect, including services furnished to any county, municipal corporation or other public body.
(Code 1972, § 24-34)
Sec. 20-10-6. - Gas services connection charges.
(a)
The charge for installing natural gas mains, services, and meters within the city's electric territory shall be three hundred dollars ($300.00) per service connection. There shall be no charge for installing gas service to residential customers located outside of the city's electric territory, unless gas service is required under the terms of a written development agreement with the city, in which case the three hundred dollars ($300.00) per service connection shall apply. Extension of natural gas service into a new area shall be solely within the discretion of the city based upon the overall economic benefit of the addition.
(b)
The charge for installing gas services and setting gas meters for all classes of customers, other than residential customers, shall be an amount equal to the materials and labor costs to the city, exclusive of boring. No connection charge shall be applied if the annual revenue from the sale of gas to the customer is projected to exceed the above calculated cost by a factor of two (2).
(c)
From time to time, the mayor and council may establish and offer incentive programs, including but not limited to rebates, for customers choosing natural gas appliances.
(Ord. No. 90-8, § 1, 2-27-90; Ord. No. 93-39, § 1, 8-10-93; 04-24, § 1, 7-27-04; Ord. No. 08-20, § 2, 7-8-08)
