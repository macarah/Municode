Chapter 20-20 - UTILITIES DISCOUNT[7]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 18-09, § 1, adopted August 28, 2018, deleted the former chapter 20-20, §§ 20-20-1—20-20-7, and enacted a new chapter 20-20 as set out herein. The former chapter 20-20 pertained to similar subject matter. See the Code Comparative Tables for the chapter's derivation.
Cross reference— Electricity, Ch. 20-5; gas, Ch. 20-10; water and sewers, Ch. 20-15.

Sec. 20-20-1. - Eligibility.
There is hereby created a senior discount for utility account holders who are sixty-five (65) years of age or older and meet the qualifications established by the Georgia Department of Human Services for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP) program.
(Ord. No. 18-09, § 1, 8-28-18)
Sec. 20-20-2. - Application.
Eligible customers shall apply for the discount in the city's customer service office by providing proof of age and total gross annual household income. Household income includes alimony, child support, pension, wages, Social Security, SSDI, SSI, TANF/AFDC, Unemployment, and any other forms of income defined by the Georgia Department of Human Services for LIHEAP eligibility.
(Ord. No. 18-09, § 1, 8-28-18)
Sec. 20-20-3. - Discount.
The discount to be applied to each monthly utility bill will be based on the services the applicant receives from the city and shall equal eleven dollars ($11.00) for electric service, six dollars and thirty cents ($6.30) for natural gas service, one dollar and forty cents ($1.40) for water service, twelve dollars ($12.00) for a 68-gallon garbage can, and fifteen dollars ($15.00) for a 96-gallon garbage can.
(Ord. No. 18-09, § 1, 8-28-18)
