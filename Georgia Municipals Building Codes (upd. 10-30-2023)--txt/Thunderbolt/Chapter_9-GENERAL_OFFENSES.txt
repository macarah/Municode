Sec. 9-107. - Prohibition of possession or consumption of alcoholic beverages in public, parks.
1.
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or consume alcoholic beverages in the public parks of the Town of Thunderbolt except for the two waterfront parks in the Town of Thunderbolt presently known W.E. Honey Park and John J. Thomson Park or in any other park on a specific occasion for which the use of a park has been granted to a specific individual or organization for a specific function and for which the Town of Thunderbolt has given express permission for the consumption of alcohol for such function. Any person or organization wishing to use the park for a specific function must obtain a permit from the Town of Thunderbolt for such function and must specifically request, if desired, that alcohol be allowed to be possessed and consumed for such function. The Town of Thunderbolt shall consider such request and make all reasonable effort to grant such request provided that the Town of Thunderbolt has reasonable assurance based upon the information supplied in the request that the function and the possession and consumption of alcohol during such function will not breach the peace.
2.
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, the possession and consumption of alcohol in any public parks in the Town of Thunderbolt including the two waterfront parks referred to hereinabove shall not be allowed in glass containers or any other container which if broken might cause a hazard to the safety of individuals using the parks. (Council, 7/9/1997)

Sec. 9-109. - Curfew.
1.
Definitions.
A.
As used in this chapter, the term:
(1)
Child means any person under 17 years of age.
(2)
Loiter means to sit, stand, or otherwise remain or linger idly at, on or near a public place.
(3)
Parent means the natural parent or parents, guardian, or other person having the legal care, custody, or control of a child.
(4)
Public place means any public road, street, or highway or any private street or other area for the passage of automobiles which is open to the public; public or private areas open to the public which are used or intended to be used for the parking of automobiles; the grounds of public school buildings; public parks, playgrounds, athletic fields, or other public recreational areas; or other public or private places located outdoors which are open to the public.
B.
No parent shall knowingly allow a child for whom the parent is responsible to loiter between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 5:00 a.m. Nothing contained in this section shall be construed to apply to any child who is:
(1)
On an errand at the direction of the child's parent or guardian, without any detour or stop;
(2)
In a motor vehicle involved in interstate travel;
(3)
Engaged in an employment activity, or going to or returning home from an employment activity, without any detour or stop;
(4)
On the sidewalk abutting the child's residence or abutting the residence of a next-door neighbor if the neighbor did not complain to the police department about the child's presence;
(5)
Attending an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the town, county, school district, a civic organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the child, or going to or returning home from, without any detour or stop, an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the town, county, school district, a civic organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor;
(6)
Exercising First Amendment rights protected by the United States Constitution, such as the free exercise of religion, freedom of speech, and the right of assembly; or
(7)
Married or had been married or had disabilities of minority removed in accordance with law.
(8)
Involved in an emergency. As used herein the term "emergency" means an unforeseen combination of circumstances or the resulting state that calls for immediate action. The term includes, but is not limited to, a fire, a natural disaster, or automobile accident, or any situation requiring immediate action to prevent serious bodily injury or loss of life;
C.
Any person convicted of violating the provisions of subsection (B) of this section shall be punished as follows:
(1)
First offense: A fine of not more than $100.00; and
(2)
Second or subsequent offense: A fine of not more than $300.00.
D.
In lieu of assessing a fine under subsection (C) of this section, the court may require the person to undergo counseling designed to inform such person of the importance of a proper home environment and close supervision of minor children. (Council, 7/13/1988)
Sec. 9-110. - Parades.
1.
Definitions. The following words, where used in this chapter, unless the context requires otherwise, shall be deemed to have the following meanings:
(1)
Administrator. The town administrator shall be the administrator for the receipt and processing of applications for a permit for a private organization or group of private persons to hold a parade, assembly, demonstration, road closing, or other use of public property and roads for private purposes. The town administrator may be assisted by other employees of the Town of Thunderbolt.
(2)
Private organization or group of private persons. A private organization or group of persons shall be any firm, partnership, corporation, association, or group of individuals more than three in number, or their representatives, acting as a unit.
(3)
Private purpose. A private purpose shall be any purpose not commanded or directed by statute, ordinance, or other regulation to be performed by the state, county, or other governmental entity.
2.
Permit required. Every private organization or group of private persons who wishes to use public property or public roads within the municipal limits of Thunderbolt, Georgia, for private purposes in holding a parade, assembly, demonstration, road closing, or other activity, is hereby required to have a permit from the town for the privilege of engaging in any such activity within the town, unless such a permit is prohibited under state law or the activity is otherwise exempted by law, ordinance, or other valid regulation.
3.
Application fee. Every private organization or group of private persons required to procure a permit under the provisions of this chapter shall submit an application for the permit to the administrator, which application shall conform to the requirements of this section in addition to any other provisions of this chapter.
(1)
Unless otherwise provided herein, each application shall be a written statement upon forms provided by the administrator and submitted within a reasonable time prior to the planned activity, for security checks, verifications, and arrangements to be made, the administrator to act within ten days of the receipt of the completed application.
(2)
Each application shall contain the following information:
(a)
Name and home address of the applicant if an individual, or home office address if a corporation or partnership, and telephone where applicant may be contacted;
(b)
Date, time, and place where the proposed activity is to be carried on, including proposed routes of passage of parades or other proceedings;
(c)
Kind and class of activity to be carried on;
(d)
Names and home addresses of the partners, if a partnership;
(e)
Names and home addresses of the officers and directors, if a corporation;
(f)
Any additional information which the administrator may find reasonably necessary to the fair administration of this chapter, which may include a complete record of all arrests and convictions against the applicant and every partner, officer, or director of the applicant for violations of any and all laws and ordinances of the town, county, state or federal government, other than minor traffic violations.
(3)
Each application shall be signed and sworn to by the applicant if an individual, or by a partner if a partnership, or by an officer if a corporation.
(4)
All information furnished or secured under the authority of this chapter shall be kept in strict confidence by the Town of Thunderbolt; shall not be subject to public inspection; and shall be utilized by the officers of the town responsible for administering the provisions of this chapter.
(5)
False statements in any application for a license shall be grounds for immediate revocation of the permit, denial of the application and/or denial of future applications.
(6)
Any application, permit, and registration fees, as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council required under this chapter, shall accompany this application.
4.
Administration of ordinance. The administrator, shall administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter for the application for and issuance of permits under this chapter.
5.
Duties of administrator. The administrator, or an authorized representative, shall have, among others, the following duties:
(1)
To prepare and provide the necessary forms for the application for a permit and for the submission of any required information as may be necessary to properly administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter.
(2)
To review the application for completeness, collect whatever fee may be required; to designate or coordinate sites and set time schedules; to coordinate with the Chatham County authorities on all matters concerning said activities; and, where appropriate, to receive the approval of the department of transportation, state highway patrol, town police chief, and the sheriff, or any other necessary public officer, for the requested, activity.
(3)
To issue to the private organization or group of private persons a permit within ten days of such approval. The administrator may, however, where he deems it appropriate, defer the issuance of a permit until the application for the same shall have been approved by the mayor and council.
6.
Procedure for issuance.
(a)
When this chapter provides for the review of an application for a permit by a town officer designated therein, the administrator shall forward a copy of the application to that officer. The officer charged with the duty of reviewing the application shall make a recommendation thereon, favorable or otherwise, and shall return the recommendation to the administrator after receiving a copy of the application.
(b)
Upon the receipt of the recommendation of the reviewing officer as hereinabove provided, or upon the receipt of the application if no reviewing officer is designated, the administrator shall take action upon the recommendation and application, although he may refer the same to the mayor and council for consideration and action at its next public meeting.
(c)
Unless some significant interference with the rights of non-participant citizens exists, or the orderly flow of traffic would be obstructed unreasonably, or a clear and present endangerment to the citizens of the Town of Thunderbolt, or there be other unreasonable interference with the public welfare, peace, safety, health, good order, and convenience of the general public, the permit shall be granted.
(d)
Where more than one permit is sought for the same date or where the proposed route would result in interference as described in 6(c), the administrator shall have authority to designate reasonable sites and/or routes and set time schedules for the beginning and ending of the activity. The administrator shall have authority to cancel the permit where the activity fails to begin within a reasonable time after the time set for it to begin based on other activities for which permits have been granted or based on the unreasonable interference caused by such delay with the public welfare, peace, safety, health, good order, and convenience to the general public.
(e)
The administrator shall then issue a permit to the applicant, which permit shall state the nature of the activity authorized and bear the date of issuance and the signature of the administrator.
(f)
Any applicant whose application for a permit under the ordinance is denied by the administrator may appeal such denial to the Mayor and Council of the Town of Thunderbolt, which shall consider such appeal at the regularly scheduled meeting next following the receipt of the applicant's appeal.
(g)
The mayor and council shall consider the appeal by requiring the administrator to explain why the permit was denied. Unless the administrator can show significant interference with the rights of non-participating citizens; unreasonable obstruction of the orderly flow of traffic; a clear and present endangerment to citizens of the Town of Thunderbolt; other specific unreasonable interference with the public welfare, peace, safety, health, good order, and convenience of the general public; or failure to comply with the dictates of this chapter, the permit shall be granted.
(h)
In no event shall any private organization or group of private persons be permitted to bring signs, banners, posters, leaflets, handbills, or any other printed material of any size or shape containing any message intended to influence any judge, juror, witness, or other officer in the discharge of his duty at the Town Hall of the Town of Thunderbolt and the grounds upon which it stands. Further, no private organization or group of private persons may use the roads immediately adjacent to and those roads which lead directly to the Town of Thunderbolt Town Hall grounds for private purposes of holding a parade, assembly, demonstration, or other similar activity on any non-holiday weekday prior to 8:00 a.m. or after 5:00 p.m. or on any Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday prior to 1:00 p.m. or after 5:00 p.m.
7.
Violations and penalties.
(a)
Criminal offenses.
(1)
Violation of ordinance. Any person who violates or fails to comply with any provision of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and shall be amenable to the processes of the Municipal Court of the Town of Thunderbolt.
(2)
Punishment for violations. Any person or organization convicted of a misdemeanor for the violation of the terms of this chapter shall be punished by fine not to exceed $1,000.00, at the discretion of the Municipal Court of Thunderbolt.
(b)
Civil penalties. Any person, being an officer or employee of the Town of Thunderbolt, who shall violate any provision of this chapter may be discharged from public service or employment or be subjected to such other disciplinary action as may be appropriate.
8.
Defense to prosecution. It shall not be a defense to any prosecution under this chapter for failure to obtain a permit before engaging in any activity described in this chapter that a permit has in fact been issued unless, at his trial, the accused produces in court a valid permit or a certified copy thereof.

10.
Deposits and financial responsibility.
(a)
Nothing in this chapter shall relieve any person or persons or organization from responsibility for any injuries or damages to persons or property, private or public, occasioned by their acts or omissions arising from the activity for which any permit under this chapter was issued.
(b)
After examining the application for a permit and receiving the advice and assistance of any other officer named herein, the administrator, after consideration of the facts reported in the application and any such advice and assistance from another public officer as herein provided, including prior experiences involving the same or similar activity, may require, as a condition precedent to the issuance of the permit, that a fee be paid in an amount reasonably necessary to provide for, among other things, cleanup after the event, special first aid or medical resources, security for vehicles at any staging area, special traffic considerations, temporary toilet facilities, and similar special and extraordinary expenses occasioned by the permitted activity. This amount shall not exceed $500.00.
(c)
As a condition to issuing a permit, the administrator may require the private organization or group of private persons to provide personnel for trash cleanup of affected areas littered during the activity for which a permit is sought, the provision of first aid and medical resources if considered necessary by the administrator, proof of sufficient storage areas for a large influx of motor vehicles occasioned by the permitted activity, provision of temporary toilet facilities and other similar special and extraordinary items considered to be necessary for the permitted activity in the opinion of the administrator, based on his past experience with such activities and his coordination with other county, city and state officials. In no event shall the administrator require private organizations or groups of private persons to provide personnel for normal governmental functions, such as traffic control and police protection. If additional requirements are placed on private organizations or groups of private persons in accordance with this subparagraph, and those requirements are not met despite assurances by said private organizations or group of private persons, then said failure to comply with the aforementioned requirements shall be grounds for denial of any subsequent permit requested by said private organization or group of private persons and for any other claims for funds expended by the Town of Thunderbolt for those extraordinary expenses agreed to but not provided by the applicant.
(d)
If the private organization be other than individuals, a permit will not issue without the making of the necessary deposit; individuals may be excused from such a deposit on account of indigence upon the execution under oath by the individual applicant, or by each individual in a group applying for the permit, of a pauper's affidavit. Prior to the receipt of such an affidavit, the administrator shall advise the applicant orally or in writing of the penalties for the execution of a false document.

Sec. 9-112. - Peddlers, solicitors and canvassers.
A.
Definitions: The following words, terms and phrases when used in this section shall have the meanings set forth herein except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Booth and stand mean any table, shelf, counter, cart or other facility, whether mobile or fixed, which is placed on a street or other public place within the Town of Thunderbolt for the purpose of selling or offering to sell any goods or services to the public, or for soliciting for any purpose.
Canvasser means any person who is not engaged in the business of selling or offering either goods or services but is seeking only to obtain information or data for an educational or governmental purpose.
Panhandler shall mean any person maintaining a position on any street, alley, avenue, court, sidewalk or public right away or any other public place within the town seeking contributions of money, food or handouts of any sort from the general public.
Peddler means a person who has no permanent, or regular place of business within the Town and who engages in the business of selling or offering to sell goods or services by going from place to place within the town.
Solicitor means any person who solicit subscriptions by going from place to place within the town and who offers for sale subscriptions to magazines or subscriptions for other items or services.
Solicitor for charitable or religious purposes means any person who solicits contributions from the public either on the streets of the city or door-to-door, for any charitable or religious organization and who does not sell or offer to sell any item at a cost to a purchaser. No qualification as a charitable or religious organization shall exist unless the solicitor for a charitable or religious purposes is able to demonstrate a current exemption certificate from the Internal Revenue Service pursuant to section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.
B.
Prohibition: It shall be unlawful to be a peddler or solicitor (except a solicitor for charitable or religious purposes) within the town limits of the Town of Thunderbolt. It shall also be unlawful to be a panhandler within the Town limits of the Town of Thunderbolt.
C.
Permitted activities:
(1)
Operating food trucks is permitted within the town limits provided a proper permit has been issued after application for a permit has been made. The permit will set forth the specific date or dates, place and hours of operation that are allowed.
(2)
Soliciting for charitable or religious purposes is permitted provided a permit has been issued after application for such permit has been made. The permit for such activity shall expressly state the date or dates and hours of the day during which such solicitation shall occur.
(3)
Canvassing for educational or governmental purposes is permitted provided a permit has been issued for such activity by the town. The permit shall expressly state the date or dates and hours of the day during which such canvassing shall occur.
(4)
Selling or offering to sell goods or services at a special event is permitted provided the special event has been permitted by the town.
(5)
Selling or offering to sell goods or services from booths or stands shall be allowed on a temporary basis provided a permit has been issued after an application for such activity has been made. The permit shall expressly state the place for such booth or stand, the date or dates and hour of the day during which such activity shall occur.
D.
Permits: Applications for permits for allowed activities set forth in this ordinance shall be obtained from the clerk of the town. The permit shall be approved by the town administrator or the mayor and council if the town administrator seeks mayor and council advise on any requested permit. The person or persons granted any permit shall have such permit with them at all times during the permitted activity. Fees for any such permit shall be as set forth on a fee schedule kept by the clerk of council and established by the mayor and council from time to time.
E.
Penalty: Any person violating with the provisions of this section shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 per occurrence. Each day of activity shall be considered a separate occurrence.

Sec. 9-113. - Smoking prohibition.
A.
Definitions: The following words, terms and phrases when used in this section shall have the meaning set forth herein except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
E-cigarette means an electronic or oral device, such as one composed of a heating element, battery, and/or electronic circuit, that provides a vapor of nicotine and/or other substances, and the use or inhalation of which simulates smoking. The term shall include any such device, whether manufactured, distributed, marketed, or sold as an E-cigarette, E-cigar, or E-pipe, or under any other product name or descriptor.
A park will be recognized as a public green or recreation area, or an area devoted for a specified purpose. Town of Thunderbolt parks shall include those owned or maintained by the Town of Thunderbolt. This shall include but not limit to Honey Park, Thompson Park, Nellie Johnson Park, and Cessaroni Park.
Smoking means inhaling, exhaling, burning, or carrying any lighted or heated cigar, cigarette, or pipe, or any other lighted or heated tobacco product intended for inhalation, in any manner or in any form. "Smoking" also includes the use of an E-cigarette that creates a vapor, in any manner or in any form, or the use of any oral smoking device for the purpose of circumventing the prohibition of smoking in this section.
Smokeless tobacco product means snuff, chewing tobacco or other products that consist of a tobacco element that are used for non-smoking purposes.
B.
Prohibition: Smoking, the use of E-cigarettes (also known as vaping) and the use of smokeless tobacco products shall be prohibited in parks in the Town of Thunderbolt.
C.
Violations and penalties:
(1)
A person who violates the provisions of this section shall be guilty of a town ordinance violation, punishable by a fine and court fees not to exceed $1,000.00 on the first offence. Subsequent violations are punishable by a fine and court fees of a greater amount in the discretion of the court. Each day on which a violation of this section occurs shall be considered a separate and distinct violation.
(2)
Violation of this section is hereby declared to be a public nuisance, which may be abated by the town by the town administrator or their designee by restraining order, preliminary and permanent injunction, citation or other means provided by law, and the Town may take action to recover the cost of the nuisance abatement.
D.
Severability: If any provision, clause, sentence or paragraph of this section or the application thereof to any person or circumstance shall be held invalid, that invalidity shall not affect the other provisions of this section which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this section are declared to be severable.
E.
Signage: The town may place appropriate signs to advise the public that they are subject to fines for violating the provisions of this section.
