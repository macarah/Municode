ARTICLE II. - WATER RATES AND SERVICE RULES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Editor's note— The Res. of December 16, 2010 repealed the former Art. II, §§ 74-31—74-41, and enacted a new Art. II as set out herein. The former Art. II pertained to water rates and service rules and derived from Ord. of 6-7-1993, §§ 1—11; Ord. of 9-12-1994.

Sec. 74-31. - Water rate schedule.
Rates for water from the county water and sewer authority system for residential and commercial use shall be established from time to time by the county water and sewer authority and set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-32. - Application for water service.
The consumer shall make application for water service, in person, at the county water and sewer authority offices located in the county administrative center in Jasper, Georgia, and at the same time shall pay all fees required as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for water service.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-33. - Charges for initial water service.
Each consumer subscribing to use the water service of the county shall pay a connection fee, set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, multiplied by the number of connections desired for water service. This provision shall become effective after the water line has been constructed beyond the consumer's property.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-34. - Minimum charges.
The minimum charge, as provided in the rate schedule, shall be made for such connection subscribed for. Water furnished for a given lot shall be used on that lot only.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-35. - County's responsibility and liability.
The county shall run a service line from its distribution line to the property line where the distribution line exists or is to be constructed, and runs immediately adjacent and parallel to the property to be served. No service charge, other than the connection fee referred to in section 74-33 of this article, will be made for a ⅝-inch × ¾-inch meter. A proportionately greater charge than the connection fee above will be made for a meter of larger dimensions.
(1)
The county may make connections to service other properties not adjacent to its lines upon payment of reasonable costs for the extensions of its distribution lines as may be required to render such service.
(2)
The county may install its meter at or near the property line or, at the county's option, on the consumer's property within three feet of the property line.
(3)
The county reserves the right to refuse service unless the consumer's lines or pipings are installed in such a manner as to prevent cross connections or backflow.
(4)
Under normal conditions, the consumer will be notified of any anticipated interruptions of service by the county.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-36. - Consumer's responsibility and liability.
Water furnished by the county shall be used for consumption by the consumer, members of his household and employees only. The consumer shall not sell water to any other person or permit any other person to use such water. Water shall not be used for irrigation, fire protection, nor other purposes, except when water is available in sufficient quantity without interfering with the regular domestic consumption in the area served. Disregard for this rule shall be sufficient cause for refusal and/or discontinuance of service.
(1)
Where meter or meter box is placed on the premises of a consumer, a suitable place shall be provided by the consumer that is unobstructed and accessible at all times to the meter reader.
(2)
The consumer shall furnish and maintain a private cutoff valve on the consumer's side of the meter.
(3)
The consumer's piping and apparatus shall be installed and maintained by the consumer at the consumer's expense, in a safe and efficient manner, and in accordance with the sanitary regulations of the state health department.
(4)
In order to be received as a consumer and entitled to receive water from the county's water system, all applicants must offer proof that any private wells located on their property are not physically connected to the lines of the county water system and all applicants by becoming consumers of the county covenant and agree that so long as they continue to be consumers of the county they will not permit the connection of any private wells on their property to the county water system.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-37. - Access to premises and extensions of system.
(a)
Duly authorized agents of the county shall have access at all hours to the premises of the consumer for the purpose of installing or removing county property, inspecting piping, reading and testing meters, or any other purpose in connection with the water service and its facilities.
(b)
Extensions to the system shall be made only when the consumer shall grant or convey, or shall cause to be granted or conveyed, to the county a permanent easement of right-of-way across any property traversed by the water lines.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-38. - Change of occupancy.
Not less than three days' notice must be given, in person or in writing, at the county water and sewer authority offices located in the county administrative center in Jasper, Georgia, to discontinue water service or to change occupancy. The outgoing party shall be responsible for all water consumed up to the time of departure or the time specified for departure, whichever period is longer. The new occupant shall apply for water service within 48 hours after occupying the premises and failure to do so will make him or her liable for paying the water consumed since the last meter reading.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-39. - Meter reading; billings; collecting.
Bills to customers for water service shall be mailed out on such day or days of each month as established by the county water and sewer authority. The failure of water users to pay charges duly imposed shall result in the imposition of penalties as established by the county water and sewer authority. Water service shall not be reestablished until such time as customer tenders payment of fees and complies with existing policies as established by the county water and sewer authority. Rates and penalties charged to water customers shall be established from time to time by the county water and sewer authority.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-40. - Suspension of service.
The county reserves the right to discontinue its service without notice for the following additional reasons:
(1)
To prevent fraud or abuse.
(2)
Consumer's willful disregard of the county's rules.
(3)
Emergency repairs.
(4)
Insufficiency of water supply due to circumstances beyond the county's control.
(5)
Legal processes.
(6)
Direction of public authorities.
(7)
Strike, riot, fire, flood, unavoidable accident.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-41. - Complaints; adjustments.
If the consumer believes his or her bill to be in error, he or she shall present his or her claim, in person, at the county water and sewer authority offices located in the county administrative center in Jasper, Georgia, before the bill becomes delinquent. Such claim, if made after the bill has become delinquent, shall not be effective in preventing discontinuance in service as heretofore provided. The consumer may pay such bill under protest and such payment shall not prejudice his claim.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Sec. 74-42.1. - Enforcement.
(a)
No person shall use or allow the use of water in violation of the restrictions on outdoor water use contained in this article.
(b)
The county water and sewer authority shall be the enforcement authority for this article. The board of commissioners may also authorize other departments as may be deemed necessary to support enforcement.
(c)
Criminal and alternative penalties. Any violation of this section may also be enforced by a citation or accusation returnable to the magistrate court or by any other legal means as set forth in this Code.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Editor's note— Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1, repealed former § 74-42.1, which pertained to restrictions on outdoor watering of landscape and derived from the Res. of 12-16-2010. Sec. 74-42.2. - Repealer.
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict with this article are repealed.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Note— See the editor's note to § 74-42.1
Sec. 74-42.3. - Severability.
The sections, paragraphs, sentences, clauses and phrases of this article are severable. Should any section, paragraph, sentence, clause or phrase of this article be rendered invalid by any court of law, the remaining sections, paragraphs, sentences, clauses or phrases shall not be affected but shall continue in effect until amended or repealed by action of the governing authority of the county.
(Res. of 12-16-2010; Res. of 10-20-2016, § 1)
Note— See the editor's note to § 74-42.1
