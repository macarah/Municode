ARTICLE XIII. - ANTI-FRAUD POLICY

Sec. 2-377. - Purpose.
It is the intent of the county board of commissioners to establish this anti-fraud policy to enforce controls and to aid in the prevention and detection of fraud, theft, waste, or abuse against the county. This policy applies to any fraud, theft, waste, or abuse or suspected fraud, theft, waste or abuse involving an employee (including management), a consultant, vendor, contractor outside agency, or person doing business with the county or any other relationship with the county. The county does not tolerate any type of fraud, theft, waste or abuse. The county's policy is to promote consistent, legal, and ethical organizational behavior by:
(1)
Designating an ethics officer;
(2)
Assigning responsibility for reporting fraud, theft, waste or abuse; and
(3)
Providing guidelines to conduct investigations of suspected fraudulent behavior.
(Code 1991, § 2-360; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-378. - Ethics officer designated.
The county administrator is designated as the ethics officer under this article.
Sec. 2-379. - Applicability.
The provisions of this article shall apply to all departments, divisions or other units of the county government receiving appropriations from the board of commissioners and to other agencies, as the board of commissioners may deem appropriate.
(Code 1991, § 2-361; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-380. - Consequences of failure to comply.
Failure to comply with this policy subjects any employee (including management) to disciplinary action, including, but not limited to, immediate termination. Any consultant, vendor, contractor outside agency, or person doing business with the county or any other relationship with the county, including collusive relations between county employees and consultant vendor, contractor outside agency, or person doing business in any way with the county that fails to comply with this anti-fraud policy could result in cancellation of the business or other relationship between the entity and the county.
(Code 1991, § 2-362; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-381. - Criminal consequences.
The county reserves the right to refer any matter for prosecution if the results of an administrative investigation indicate the possibility of criminal activity.
(Code 1991, § 2-363; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-382. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abuse means the intentional, wrongful, or improper use of resources or misuse of rank, position, or authority that causes the loss or misuse of resources, such as tools, vehicles, computers, copy machines, etc. Examples of abuse include:
(1)
Using county equipment or supplies to conduct personal business;
(2)
An employee using non-confidential taxpayer information to get new customers for his outside business.
This definition of the term "abuse" is not intended to be construed as all inclusive.
Fraud refers to an intentional deception designed to obtain a benefit or advantage or to cause some benefit that is due to be denied. Examples of fraud include:
(1)
Forgery or alteration of a check, bank draft, or any other financial document;
(2)
Theft of a check or other diversion of a taxpayer's payment;
(3)
Making payroll checks out to "phantom" employees;
(4)
Falsifying time sheets or payroll records, including, but not limited to, reporting hours not worked or a supervisor not allowing the reporting of all hours worked by hourly paid employees;
(5)
Listing of beneficiaries who do not qualify (or no longer qualify) as family members for healthcare benefits;
(6)
Falsifying travel and entertainment expenses and/or utilizing county funds to pay for personal expenditures;
(7)
Writing off of delinquent receivables that have been collected;
(8)
Misappropriation of funds, securities, supplies, or other assets;
(9)
Impropriety in the handling of reporting of money or financial transactions;
(10)
Profiteering as a result of insider knowledge of county operations;
(11)
Selling confidential taxpayer information or using such information in the conduct of an outside business activity;
(12)
Disclosing to others the securities activities engaged in or contemplated by the county.
This list defining examples of the term "fraud" is intended to be illustrative only and is not intended to be all inclusive.
Theft means the act of taking something from someone or the county unlawfully.
Waste means the loss or misuse of county resources that results from deficient practices, system controls, or decisions. An example of waste is not taking advantage of available early bird conference registration discounts. The term "waste" is not intended to be construed as all inclusive.
(Code 1991, § 2-364; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-383. - Responsibility to report suspected fraud, theft, waste, or abuse.
Each employee is required to report any suspected fraud, theft, waste or abuse or other dishonest conduct to the employee's management or to the ethics officer. Suspected improprieties and/or misconduct concerning an employee's ethical conduct should be reported to management (department head or supervisor) or the ethics officer. Management (department head or supervisor) is required to report suspected fraud, theft, waste, or abuse or other dishonest conduct, including reports from employees or other individuals, to the ethics officer.
(Code 1991, § 2-365; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-384. - Responsibility to report.
The ethics officer shall have the exclusive authority to determine the merits of a report of suspected fraud. This determination shall be made with the assistance of an internal audit.
(Code 1991, § 2-366; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-385. - Identity protected for employees reporting fraud.
The identity of an employee or complainant who reports suspected fraud, theft, waste, or abuse will be protected to the full extent allowed by law.
(Code 1991, § 2-367; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-386. - Guidelines for handling a report.
Whether the initial report is made to management or the ethics officer, the reporting individual should receive the following instructions and information:
(1)
Do not contact the suspected individual in an effort to determine facts or demand restitution.
(2)
Allow the ethics officer to conduct the investigation. Do not further investigate the allegation.
(3)
Observe strict confidentiality. Do not discuss the case, facts, suspicions, or allegations with anyone unless specifically asked to do so by the ethics officer or the county attorney.
(4)
Retaliation will not be tolerated. The county will not tolerate any form of retaliation against individuals providing information concerning fraud or suspected fraud.
(5)
Every effort will be made to protect the rights and the reputations of everyone involved, including the individual who in good faith alleges perceived misconduct as well as the alleged violators.
(6)
The identity of an employee or other individual who reports in good faith a suspected act of fraud will be protected as provided by this policy.
(Code 1991, § 2-368; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-387. - Duty of ethics officer to employees involved in a report.
The ethics officer shall make every effort to protect the rights and the reputations of everyone involved in a report of suspected fraud, including the individual who in good faith alleges perceived misconduct, as well as the alleged violator. The ethics officer also shall make every effort to protect the identity of a person who in good faith reported the suspected fraud. However, disciplinary action may be taken as provided by this policy if a report is made in bad faith.
(Code 1991, § 2-369; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-388. - Preliminary investigation.
On receiving a report of suspected fraud, theft, waste or abuse, the ethics officer shall document the contact and conduct a preliminary investigation to determine the credibility of the report. If the report is credible, the ethics officer shall follow the investigation guidelines provided in this policy.
(Code 1991, § 2-370; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-389. - Procedure for report against the ethics officer.
In the event of a suspected case reported involving the ethics officer, the report and the authority to process the inquiry under the same guidelines as presented herein shall move to the county's legal counsel and chairperson of the board of commissioners. Further diligence of the reported case under this scenario will progress forward using the same criteria as provided within the anti-fraud policy.
(Code 1991, § 2-371; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-390. - Documenting determination.
On determining that a report is not credible or is not a report of fraud, the ethics officer shall document this determination. The ethics officer's documentation shall include support for the determination. The ethics officer will refer questions as to whether an action constitutes fraud to the internal audit division. The ethics officer shall maintain appropriate documentation regarding incidents of fraud and other fraud reports. The ethics officer shall develop and maintain guidelines for access to and security of this documentation.
(Code 1991, § 2-372; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-391. - Reporting confirmed fraud to state auditor.
(a)
The ethics officer is responsible for the full investigation and documentation of suspected fraud. The ethics officer has primary responsibility for the investigation of reported wrongdoing and all suspected fraud and for coordinating investigative activities.
(b)
In addition to reporting each suspected fraud to internal audit, the ethics officer is responsible for reporting confirmed fraud to the state auditor's office in the manner required by state law.
(Code 1991, §§ 2-373, 2-379; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-392. - Confidentiality of investigation.
Each employee involved in an investigation of suspected fraud shall keep the content of the investigation strictly confidential to the full extent provided by law. Investigation results shall not be disclosed or discussed with anyone other than those who have a legitimate need to know.
(Code 1991, § 2-374; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-393. - Preparation of incident report.
If an investigation substantiates fraudulent activities, the ethics officer will prepare an incident report, which will be made available to the board of commissioners and the county attorney. The ethics officer shall prepare the report as soon as possible after the fraud is confirmed and shall document the content of the investigation, the findings, and any disciplinary actions taken as a result of the findings.
(Code 1991, § 2-375; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-394. - Inquiries directed to ethics officer.
Any inquiries from the suspected individual, his attorney/representative, or any other inquirer shall be directed to the ethics officer. If necessary, the ethics officer will refer these inquiries to the appropriate person or entity.
(Code 1991, § 2-376; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-395. - Actions subjecting an employee to disciplinary action.
(a)
An employee who knowingly commits the following is subject to disciplinary action, including, but not limited to, termination:
(1)
Has engaged in any form of fraud, waste, theft, or abuse;
(2)
Suspects or discovers fraudulent activity and fails to report his suspicions as required by this policy; or
(3)
Intentionally reports false or misleading information.
(b)
Any member of management who does not pass to the ethics officer each and every report of suspected fraud made by an employee or other person is subject to disciplinary action, including immediate termination.
(Code 1991, § 2-377; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-396. - Mandatory fraud awareness training.
Each employee is required to attend at least one session of fraud awareness training during each fiscal year. After attending the training, the employee must sign a form indicating that they have attended the training and understood its content.
(Code 1991, § 2-378; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
Sec. 2-397. - Administration, interpretation and application of this policy.
The ethics officer is responsible for the administration, interpretation, and application of this policy.
(Code 1991, § 2-380; Ord. No. 2011-07, 11-9-2011)
