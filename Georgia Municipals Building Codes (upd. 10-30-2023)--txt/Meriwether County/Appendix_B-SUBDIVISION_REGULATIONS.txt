ARTICLE I. - PREAMBLE AND ENACTMENT CLAUSE

[Sec. 101. - Preamble and enactment clause.]
The purpose of this ordinance [appendix B] is to promote the health, safety, morals, convenience, order, prosperity, and general welfare of the municipality; to encourage economically sound and stable land development; to ensure the provisions of required streets, utilities, and other facilities and services to land development; to ensure the adequate provisions of safe and convenient traffic access and circulation, both vehicular and pedestrian, in land development; to ensure the provisions of needed public open spaces and public building sites in land development through the dedication or reservation of land for recreational, educational, and other public purposes; and to ensure that land developed in conformity with the Comprehensive Plan of Meriwether County, the county commissioners of Meriwether County, Georgia, do ordain and enact into law the following articles and sections. The subdivision regulations adopted by Resolution on October 14, 1991, as amended, is hereby repealed and replaced in its entirety by this subdivision ordinance which will be set out as appendix B to the Code of Ordinances, Meriwether County, Georgia.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE II. - SHORT TITLE

[Sec. 201. - Short title.]
This ordinance [appendix B] shall be known and may be cited as the Subdivision Regulations of Meriwether County, Georgia.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE III. - DEFINITIONS

Sec. 301. - Definitions.
When used in these regulations, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings given in this section. Terms not herein defined shall have their customary dictionary meanings where not inconsistent with the context of these regulations. The term "shall" is mandatory. When not inconsistent with the context, words used in the singular number include the plural, and those used in the plural number include the singular. Words used in the present tense include the future. The term "person" includes a "firm," "association," "organization," "company" or "corporation," as well as an individual. The term "building" includes the term "structure."
Comprehensive plan means any part or element or the overall plan for development adopted by Meriwether County as provided by the General Planning Enabling Act of 1957, No. 358, as amended.
County means Meriwether County, Georgia.
Drainage plan means any plan or element thereof which has been approved by the director of community development.
Easement means a grant by the property owner of use, by the public, a corporation, or person(s), of a strip of land for a specific reason.
Final plat means a plat of a tract of land which meets the requirements of these regulations and is in a form for recording in the office of the clerk of the superior court.
Lot means a portion or parcel of land separated from the other portions or parcels by description as on a subdivision plat or record of survey map or as described by metes and bounds, and intended for transfer of ownership or for building development. For purposes of this ordinance [appendix], the term "lot" does not include any portion of a dedicated right-of-way.
Major street plan means the major street plan as adopted by Meriwether County as an element of the county comprehensive plan.
Planning commission means the Meriwether County Planning Commission.
Preliminary plat means a tentative plan of a proposed subdivision submitted to the community development department for its consideration.
Sanitarian means the Meriwether County Health Department.
Street means a way for vehicular traffic, whether or not designated as such on the Major Thoroughfare Plan of Meriwether County, to travel through and within the county.
(1)
Major or arterial street means those streets designated as such on the Major Thoroughfare Plan of Meriwether County which serve as major traffic ways for travel through and within the county.
(2)
Secondary or collector street means a street used to carry traffic from the "minor streets" to the "major streets" and includes, but is not limited to, the principal entrance and circulation streets of a subdivision.
(3)
Minor or local street means a street used primarily for access to the abutting properties.
(4)
Alley means a minor way used for service access to the back or side of properties otherwise abutting on a street.
(5)
Cul-de-sac means a "minor street" having one end open to traffic and the other end permanently terminated with a paved turn-around.
(6)
Dead end means a stub street in a subdivision which could at a later time be continued into another portion of the subdivision.
Subdivider means the person, firm, corporation, syndicate or other legal entity having such a proprietary interest in the land to be subdivided as will authorize the maintenance of proceeding to subdivide such land under this regulation, or the authorized agent of such person, firm or corporation for the purpose of undertaking activities covered by these regulations.
Subdivision means all divisions of a tract or parcel of land into more than three lots, excluding parent parcel, for the purpose, whether immediate or future, of sale or building development.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE IV. - PLATTING JURISDICTION, AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 401. - Platting authority.
From and after the passage of this regulation, the community development department shall be the official authority, and no plat of land subdivided shall be entitled to record in the office of the clerk of superior court of Meriwether County, Georgia, unless it shall have the approval of the community development department inscribed thereon. The filing or recording of a plat of a subdivision without approval as required by this ordinance [appendix] is declared to be a misdemeanor.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 402. - Use of a plat.
The transfer of, sale of, agreement to sell, or negotiation to sell land by reference to or exhibition of, or other use of a plat of a subdivision that has not been given final approval as required by this ordinance [appendix] and recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Meriwether County, Georgia, is prohibited, and the description by metes and bounds in the instrument of transfer or other document shall not exempt the transaction from penalties.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 403. - Plat approval.
After this ordinance [appendix] shall have been put into effect, any subdivision that fails to meet the requirements of this ordinance [appendix] shall be disapproved by Meriwether County.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 404. - Acceptance of streets.
No public authority shall accept, improve or maintain any street not shown on an approved and recorded final plat unless such street shall have received the legal status of a public street prior to the adoption of the subdivision regulations set forth in the resolution of October 14, 1991.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE V. - TENTATIVE APPROVAL OF PRELIMINARY PLAT FOR SUBDIVISION

Sec. 501. - Application and fees.
All applications for plat approval for subdivisions shall be made to the community development department and accompanied by a fee determined from time to time by action of the board of commissioners. The applicable fee shall be set out in the county's consolidated fee schedule and made available for inspection during normal working hours in the community development office.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 502. - Application for preliminary plat approval.
Following the pre-application review of a proposed subdivision and the payment of fees, the subdivider shall submit to the community development department the following:
(1)
A letter requesting review and approval of a preliminary plat, giving the name and address of a person to whom the preliminary plat shall be sent.
(2)
Five copies of the preliminary plat and any other related documents to be utilized as follows:
a.
Community development department.
b.
Public works department.
c.
Health department.
d.
County commissioners.
The fifth copy shall be returned to the subdivider or his agent with a notation of the action taken by Meriwether County.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 503. - Review of preliminary plat for subdivisions.
(a)
The community development department shall check the plat for conformance to this ordinance [appendix] and shall give notification by mail to the person designated in the letter requesting preliminary plat review and approval.
(b)
Thereafter, the community development department shall give tentative approval or disapproval to the preliminary plat, including a statement of the reasons for disapproval if the preliminary plat is disapproved. One copy shall be returned to the subdivider or his agent and one copy added to the records of the community development department.
(c)
Approval of a preliminary plat does not constitute approval of a final plat. It indicates only approval of the layout as a guide to the preparation of the final plat. Preliminary plat approval shall expire and be null and void after a period of one year, unless an extension of time is requested by the subdivider or his representative and is approved by the community development department.
(d)
If action on a preliminary plat is not taken by the community development department within 45 days of the date of receipt (excluding weekends and holidays), the preliminary plat shall be considered approved, and a certificate of approval shall be issued on demand. However, the applicant for approval may waive this requirement and consent to an extension of time.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE VI. - PRELIMINARY PLAT SPECIFICATIONS FOR SUBDIVISIONS

Sec. 601. - Scale.
The preliminary plat shall be clearly and legibly drawn at a scale not smaller than 100 feet to one inch.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 602. - Ground elevations.
The preliminary plat shall show ground elevations, based on the datum plane of the U.S. Coast and Geodetic Survey (or other approved datum plane) with contour lines at intervals of not more than 20 feet for land that slopes more than five percent or contour lines at intervals of not more than five feet for land that slopes less than five percent.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 603. - Information to be provided on the preliminary plat.
The preliminary plat shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name and address of the owner of record and of the subdivider.
(2)
Proposed name of the subdivision and its acreage.
(3)
North point and graphic scale and date.
(4)
Vicinity map showing the location and acreage of the subdivision.
(5)
Exact boundary lines of the tract of bearing and distance.
(6)
Names of owners of record of adjoining property.
(7)
Existing streets, utilities, and easements on and adjoining the tract.
(8)
Proposed layout, including streets and alleys, to be reviewed by the community development department, with proposed street names, lot lines with dimensions, easements, land to be reserved or dedicated for public uses, and any land to be used for purposes other than single-family dwellings.
(9)
Block numbers and lot numbers.
(10)
Provisions for water supply, sewage and drainage, to be reviewed by the county sanitarian. Consult Meriwether County Health Department prior to submitting the application.
(11)
Minimum building front yard setback lines as required by the county zoning ordinance.
(12)
Soil types of the subdivision area from the U.S. Conservation Department Soil Study.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 604. - Certificate of preliminary approval.
A certificate of approval of a preliminary plat by the community development department shall be inscribed on the plat as follows:
Pursuant to the land subdivision regulations of Meriwether County, Georgia, all the requirements of preliminary approval having been fulfilled, this preliminary plat was given preliminary approval by the director of community development on ________, 20___.
This preliminary plat approval does not constitute approval of a final plat. This certificate of preliminary plat approval shall expire and be null and void on ________ (one (1) year from preliminary plat approval).

ARTICLE VII. - FINAL PLAT PROCEDURE

Sec. 701. - Application for final plat approval.
After the preliminary plat of the proposed land subdivision has been given tentative approval by the community development department, the subdivider may, within one year from tentative approval, submit to the Meriwether County Community Development Department:
(1)
A letter requesting review and approval of a final plat and giving the name and address of the person to whom the final plat shall be sent.
(2)
Five copies of the final plat and other related documents, as specified in article VI of this appendix.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 702. - Review of final plat.
(a)
The community development department shall check the final plat for conformance with the tentatively approved preliminary plat, and with the rules and regulations, and shall notify by mail the person designated in the letter requesting final plat review and approval.
(b)
Thereafter, the community development department shall approve or disapprove the final plat. A notation of the action shall be made on the original tracing and all prints of the final plat, including a statement of the reasons therefor if the final plat is disapproved.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 703. - Recording of final plat.
Upon approval of the final plat, it shall be recorded in the office of the clerk of superior court of Meriwether County, Georgia. (The subdivider shall be responsible for recording of such final plat in the office of the clerk of superior court, Meriwether County, Georgia.)
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )

ARTICLE IX. - GENERAL DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 901. - Suitability of land.
Land subject to flooding, improper drainage, or erosion or that is topographically or for other reasons unsuitable for residential use shall not be platted for residential use nor any other use that will continue or increase the danger to health, safety, or property destruction, unless the hazards can be and are corrected prior to final plat approval. No land shall be platted for use which would be in conflict with the floodplain or other zoning provisions in the county zoning ordinance and the state flood zone requirements. There shall be no construction other than sewer lines or utilities in areas construed as wetlands.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 902. - Name of subdivision.
The name of the subdivision must have the approval of the Meriwether County Community Development Department, with verification from E-911. The name shall not duplicate or closely approximate the name of an existing subdivision. Any new subdivision development more than 50 lots must provide a secondary means of ingress and egress to the development via a street accepted into the county road system.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 903. - Access.
Subdivisions shall be located so as to gain access over a public street. Any new subdivision development with more than 50 lots must provide a secondary means of ingress and egress to the development via a street or road meeting Meriwether County requirements and specifications, and accepted into the Meriwether County Road System.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 904. - Conformance to adopted major thoroughfare and other plans.
(a)
All streets, thoroughfares, and other features of the Major Thoroughfare Plan of Meriwether County, Georgia, shall be taken into consideration by the subdivider.
(b)
When features of other plans adopted by Meriwether County (such as school or other public building sites, parks, or other land for public uses) are located in whole or in part in a land subdivision, such features shall be either dedicated or reserved by the subdivider for acquisition within a reasonable time by the appropriate public agency.
(c)
All access points into a subdivision that require the construction and acceptance of a new county road or street may be required to provide deceleration and/or acceleration lanes constructed to Meriwether County specifications. This requirement will be based on street or road classification, traffic volumes, and size of the development at the determination of Meriwether County.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 905. - Large-scale development.
The requirement of these regulations may be modified in the case of a large-scale community or neighborhood unit, such as a housing project or shopping center which is not subdivided into customary lots, blocks, and streets, if the development is approved by Meriwether County and if it is in conformity with the purpose and intent of these regulations.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE X. - GENERAL REQUIREMENTS FOR STREETS AND OTHER RIGHTS-OF-WAY

Sec. 1001. - Continuation of existing streets.
Existing streets adjacent to a subdivision may be continued contingent upon prior approval by Meriwether County and shall meet road specification as required per this ordinance [appendix].
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1002. - Street names.
Street names require the approval of the community development department, after verification from E-911. Streets that are obviously in alignment with streets already existing and named shall be given the name of the existing street. Names of new streets shall not duplicate nor closely approximate those of existing streets.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1003. - Street jogs.
Street jogs with centerline offsets of less than 125 feet shall not be permitted.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1004 - Cul-de-sacs.
A cul-de-sac shall be provided at the closed end with a turn-around having a property line radius of at least 65 feet, and an outside pavement radius of at least 50 feet on non-curb and gutter sections, and 50 feet to back of curb on curb and gutter streets. Temporary turnarounds shall be provided for dead end streets which are scheduled to be extended at a later date, but must be bonded per county requirements and completed prior to expiration of bond. Each lot shall have a minimum of 50 feet of frontage.
No utilities may be placed under paved section of cul-de-sac.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1005. - Development along major street, limited access highway or railroad right-of-way.
Where a subdivision abuts or contains a major street, a limited access highway, or a railroad right-of-way, Meriwether County may require a street approximately parallel to and on each side of such right-of-way either as a marginal access street, or at a distance suitable for an appropriate use of the intervening land, with a non-access reservation, Due regard should be given to requirements for approach grades and future grade separation in determining distances. Such lots shall have no access to a major street (or limited access highway), but only to the access street. Acceleration and deceleration lanes may be required depending on traffic volumes and size of development, at the determination of Meriwether County.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1006. - Reserve strips.
Reserve strips which control access to streets, alleys, and public grounds shall not be permitted unless approved by Meriwether County.

ARTICLE XI. - STREET DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 1101. - Street geometric requirements.
(a)
All new streets without curb and gutter accepted into the county road system, shall meet the following geometric requirements. All streets serving new subdivisions with lot sizes smaller than five acres are required to construct new streets using curb and gutter specifications.:
(1)
Sixty-foot-wide right-of-way, legally deeded to the county, plus right-of-way for deceleration and acceleration lanes as required.
(2)
Thirty-foot-wide minimum total roadbed:
a.
Subdivision entry road, acceleration, and deceleration lanes as required shall have a minimum of eight-inch compacted 98 percent standard proctor graded aggregate base. Entry road must be a minimum of 20-foot wide and extend for a distance to be determined by Meriwether County, but no less than 50 feet into the subdivision.
b.
Balance of subdivision streets must be 20-foot-wide road base with a minimum six-inch compacted 98 percent standard proctor graded aggregate base;
c.
Minimum of two inches of 19mm asphaltic concrete binder.
d.
Minimum of two inches of 9.5mm or 12.5mm of asphaltic concrete topping with bituminous tack coat
e.
Asphalt construction shall conform to GDOT Section 400 Asphalt Construction Subsection 400.3.05 Paragraph E. for observed weather limitation on application.
(3)
Six-foot-wide shoulders, each side, minimum, compacted soil.
(4)
Six-foot-wide inside slope to ditch, each side, three to one slope or less.
(5)
Two-foot-wide by two-foot deep ditches, each side.
(6)
Six-foot-wide outside slope to ditch, each side, three to one slope or less.
(7)
All shoulders and slopes set out in grass (Fescue, Bermuda, or approved alternative) and growth to a permanent stand must be achieved.
(8)
Drainage. Drainage pipe under all driveways and street cross drains must be properly sized based on analysis of topography and hydrology of the development. Driveway pipe must be 20 feet minimum length, using county approved drainage pipe. Cross drainpipe shall be concrete and meet GDOT specifications. Headwalls meeting GDOT specifications all required at origin and terminus of culverts and crossdrains.
Street design requirements and construction are subject to inspection and approval by Meriwether County Public Works and Community Development Department. This includes, but not limited to proof rolls at various stages of construction.
(b)
Before, subdivision streets are to be accepted into the public road system of Meriwether County, the following must be accomplished:
(1)
The subdivision shall be platted and approved for recording, showing all street layouts, including street names.
(2)
Streets will be graded, based, drained, paved, and grassed by the subdivider to Meriwether County specifications. Only one permit for a new home will be allowed until road is completed and accepted into the Meriwether County Road System.
(3)
Streets will be accepted into the county road system as follows:
Streets must be constructed to all Meriwether County specifications and be approved by Meriwether County Public Works and Meriwether County Community Development Department prior to the application for acceptance into the county road system.
Request must include a warranty deed, legally conveying all rights-of-way to Meriwether County and water and sewerage utilities located within rights-of-way to Meriwether County Water and Sewerage Authority, free and clear from any liens. A maintenance bond or letter of credit is required in an amount equaling 150 percent of the roadway construction costs, made payable to Meriwether County for a three-year period following acceptance of roadway. If subdivision is less than 50 percent build out at the end of the three-year period, bond shall be extended until 50 percent build is achieved. At the end of the bonding period, Meriwether County shall inspect and require developer to make any necessary repairs to the roadway. Failure to make repairs in a timely manner will result in forfeiture of bonds. Funds from the bond forfeiture will be utilized by Meriwether County for necessary repairs to roadway.
The above refers to subdivision streets. Not included is any street that would be classified as a through street. Through streets, though built by the developer, would require different right-of-way widths and different paving specifications.
(c)
Curb and gutter streets. All new streets serving subdivisions with lot sizes smaller than five acres are required to be constructed using curb and gutter specifications.
(1)
Fifty feet right-of-way legally deeded to Meriwether County, plus right-of-way for deceleration and acceleration lanes as required.
(2)
Subdivision entry road, acceleration, and deceleration lanes as required shall be a minimum of eight-inch compacted 98 percent standard proctor graded aggregate base. Entry road must be a minimum of 20 feet width and extend for a distance to be determined by Meriwether County, but no less than 50 feet into subdivision.
(3)
Balance of subdivision streets shall be 20-foot wide road base with a minimum of six-inch compacted 98 percent standard proctor graded aggregate base.
(4)
Standard six-inch by twenty-four-inch roll back curbing.
(5)
Minimum of two inches 19mm Asphaltic concrete binder and minimum of two inches of 9.5mm or 12.5mm asphaltic concrete topping with bituminous tack coat meeting Meriwether County specifications.
(6)
Twenty-four-foot minimum back-of-curb to back-of-curb.
(7)
Asphalt construction shall conform to GDOT section 400-Asphalt Construction Standard for observed weather limitations on application.
(d)
Culverts and cross drains shall meet county specifications.
(1)
Concrete pipe meeting GDOT specifications and properly sized based on analysis of topography and hydrology of the development.
(2)
Headwalls meeting GDOT specifications are required at origin and terminus of culverts and cross drains.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )

For developments that abut only one side of the road, a right-of-way 40 feet wide from the centerline of the road shall be legally deeded to the county.

ARTICLE XII. - DESIGN STANDARDS FOR BLOCKS AND LOTS

Sec. 1201. - Block lengths and widths.
Block lengths and widths shall be as follows:
(1)
Blocks shall not be less than 1,500 feet, except in unusual circumstances.
(2)
Blocks shall be wide enough to provide two tiers of lots and minimum depth, except where abutting upon major streets, limited access highways, or railroads, or where other situations make this requirement impracticable.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1202. - Lot sizes.
(a)
Residential lots shall meet lot width, road frontage, and lot area requirements of the zoning ordinance.
(b)
Residential corner lots shall have adequate width to meet building setback requirements from both abutting streets.
(c)
Commercial and industrial lots shall be in conformity with the requirements of Meriwether County, Georgia zoning ordinance.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1203. - Lot lines.
All lot lines shall be perpendicular or radial to street lines unless not practicable because of topographic or other features and approved by Meriwether County.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1204. - Building lines.
Building lines (setbacks) shall conform to the requirements of the zoning ordinance. Lots which have reduced lot sizes to one acre or one and one-half acres shall have a minimum road frontage of 100 feet.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1205. - Lots abutting public streets.
Each lot shall abut a dedicated public street.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE XIII. - SURVEY OF COMPLETION OF IMPROVEMENTS

Sec. 1300. - Surety for completion.
In lieu of completion of the required improvements in a subdivision, the subdivider may, if approved by Meriwether County Board of Commissioners deposit surety for the completion of such improvements and present the final plat for approval. This provision shall not apply to roads, streets, or drainage structures which are subject to other bonding requirements per this ordinance.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1301. - Requirements.
To ensure the construction and installation of required improvements, the subdivider upon approval by the Meriwether County Board of Commissioners shall deliver to Meriwether County, Georgia, a certified check, surety bond or other acceptable surety in such aggregate amount as is estimated by the governing authority to be the total cost of the construction and installation of all public improvements which are the responsibility of the subdivider.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1302. - Conditions.
Bonds or other surety posted shall run to Meriwether County, Georgia, and provide that the subdivider, his heirs or successors, and assignees and their servants will comply with all applicable terms, conditions, provisions, and requirements of these regulations and any other applicable requirements; will faithfully perform and complete the work of constructing and installing said facilities or improvements in accordance with these regulations and any other applicable requirements; and that the subdivider shall be responsible to Meriwether County, Georgia, for any unnecessary expense incurred through the failure of the subdivider, his heirs, successors and assignees, or their agents or servants, to complete the work of said construction in an acceptable manner, and from any damages growing out of negligence in performing or failing to perform said construction and installation. Before acceptance, any surety shall be approved by Meriwether County. If a bond is offered, it shall be executed by a surety or guaranty company qualified to transact business in the State of Georgia.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1303. - Duration and release.
Bonds and/or other surety posted pursuant to these regulations shall be released or returned, as the case may be, at such time as the facilities guaranteed thereby have been installed and accepted. Acceptance shall be in writing, accurately identifying the improvements covered. Facilities shall not be accepted unless they conform to the applicable specifications and requirements.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1304. - Default.
If the construction or installation of any improvements or facilities other than streets and drainage structures for which a bond or other surety is posted is not completed within three months after substantial completion of any buildings or structures which said improvements or facilities are designed to serve, or within two years after the date of recording of the final plat, whichever is sooner, or if said construction or installation is not in accordance with the applicable specifications and requirements using the proceeds from such surety deposits to pay for such work, such work may be done under contract or by county personnel. To the extent that any portion of a cash deposit is not required or used, said excess cash shall be repaid to the person making the deposit.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1305. - Certification of receipt of surety for required improvements.
The following form shall be printed on the final plat when a preliminary plat is not used:
I hereby certify that a security bond certified check in the amount of $________ has been received to ensure completion of all required improvements in the subdivision plat attached hereto in the event of default by the developer.
Signature, Chairman County Commission, Meriwether County, Georgia
________, 20___
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
ARTICLE XIV. - VARIANCES AND WAIVERS

Sec. 1401. - Variance from regulations.
Where Meriwether County finds that extraordinary and unnecessary hardships may result from strict compliance with these regulations, it may vary the regulations so that substantial justice may be done and public interest secured, provided that such variations will not have the effect of nullifying the intent and purpose of the official map, the land use plan, or the zoning ordinance, if any or all of such exist.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1402. - Waiver of requirements.
Where Meriwether County finds that, due to the special circumstances of a particular plat, the provision of certain required improvements is not requisite in the interest of the public health, safety and general welfare or is inappropriate because of inadequacy or lack of connecting facilities adjacent or in proximity to the proposed subdivision, it may waive such requirements subject to appropriate conditions.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
Sec. 1403. - Conditions.
In granting variances and modifications, Meriwether County shall require conditions that will, in its judgment, secure substantially the objectives of the standards or requirements so varied or modified.
( Ord. No. 2020-03, § 1(Exh. A), 8-25-2020 )
