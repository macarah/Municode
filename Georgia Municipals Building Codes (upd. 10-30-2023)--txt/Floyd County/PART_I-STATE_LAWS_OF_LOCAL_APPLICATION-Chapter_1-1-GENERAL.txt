Chapter 1-1 - GENERAL

Sec. 1-1-1. - Nepotism.
(a)
(1)
No person who has a relative within the prohibitive degrees as defined herein working for Floyd County, Georgia, may apply for and/or accept a job of employment with the county manager's office, county clerk's office and/or county personnel office. Any person who is employed by the county at the time of the passage of this change and/or amendment shall be unaffected by said amendment as to termination of employment for violation. However, he or she will be affected in that after the effective date of this amendment, no one within the prohibitive degree will be allowed to transfer into the county manager's office, county clerk's office and/or county personnel office if that person has any other relative within the prohibitive degree working for Floyd County, Georgia.
(2)
No other employee who is related by blood or marriage within the extended family of any employee of Floyd County, Georgia, shall be eligible to and/or for employment by the county in the same department where said employee works.
(3)
Extended family shall be defined as follows: husband, wife, children, parents, brothers, sisters and parents-in-law.
(b)
(1)
No person who has a relative within the prohibitive degrees as defined herein working for Floyd County, Georgia, may apply for and/or accept a job of employment with the county manager's office, county clerk's office and/or county personnel office. Any person who is employed by the county at the time of the passage of this change and/or amendment shall be unaffected by said amendment as to termination of employment for violation. However, he or she will be affected in that after the effective date of this amendment, no one within the prohibitive degree will be allowed to transfer into the county manager's office, county clerk's office and/or county personnel office if that person has any other relative within the prohibitive degree working for Floyd County, Georgia.
(2)
No other employee who is related by blood or marriage within the extended family of any employee of Floyd County, Georgia, shall be eligible to and/or for employment by the county in the same department where said employee works.
(3)
This section shall not apply to any elected official of Floyd County, Georgia, nor shall it apply to any employee who is related by blood or marriage within the extended family as computed herein in subsection 1-1-1(a)(3) to any elected official within said employee's department if said employee was regularly employed within the department prior to the elected official legally assuming the duties and responsibilities of said office.
(c)
No member of the board of commissioners shall employ or vote to employ, or pay or vote to pay, any such ineligible person. No county warden or warden of the correctional institution, county manager or superintendent or clerk shall employ or authorize the employment of any such ineligible person, or continue or authorize the continuance of any such ineligible person in employment.
(d)
Any member of the board of commissioners of said county and/or county manager and/or superintendent and/or clerk of the board who shall knowingly violate any of the provisions of this Act [this section and section 1-6-2] shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction thereof shall be punished as prescribed by section 1065 of the Criminal Code [Penal Code 1910], [O.C.G.A. § 17-10-3], and shall furthermore be guilty of malpractice in office. Any county warden, or the warden of the correctional institution, who shall knowingly violate any of the provisions of the Act shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction shall be punished as prescribed by section 1065 of the Criminal Code [Penal Code 1910], [O.C.G.A. § 17-10-3], and shall be removed from office.
(Ga. L. 1931, p. 467, §§ 1, 2, 3, 5; Ord. of 10-25-77; Amend. of 9-8-98(1), § 1; Amend. of 3-23-99(1), § 1; Ord. No. 2000-002A, § 1, 6-27-00)
