DIVISION 2. - RULES OF ORDER AND PROCEDURE[2]

Sec. 2-40. - Meetings.
The mayor and council shall meet at the council chamber on the third Tuesday of each month at 7:00 p.m., unless otherwise ordered by the mayor and council. Regular mayor and council meetings shall be advertised in the local newspaper, television and radio at all possible cost, in a period of no less than 15 calendar days prior to the regularly scheduled meeting. Called or emergency meetings for the mayor and council must give notice no less than 24 hours prior to such meeting. Notice should be posted on the premises of the city hall and all other city owned facilities.
(Prior Code, § 2-1)
Sec. 2-41. - Minutes of previous meeting.
The presiding officer, whether the mayor, mayor pro tem, or a councilmember, shall call the meeting to order at the designated hour; the clerk shall, upon the appearance of a quorum, read the minutes of the preceding meeting. After said minutes have been corrected or amended, should the same be necessary, they shall be put upon their approval, and when approved, shall be signed by the mayor and the clerk.
(Prior Code, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-42. - Adjournment; special meetings.
The mayor and council may be adjourned to any fixed time. Special meetings may be called by the mayor whenever dire circumstances require it, and he shall call such meetings upon the application of any three members of the mayor and council.
(Prior Code, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-43. - Order of business.
No mayor and council business shall be transacted except in an official meeting of the mayor and council, and the presiding officer shall use reasonable diligence to notify all councilmembers of any special or called meeting. The chief of police or a police officer shall, when required, give notice to the members of the council of any such special or called meeting. The following order shall be observed in the transaction of business at regular meetings:
(1)
Reading of minutes.
(2)
Unfinished business.
(3)
Reports of committees.
(4)
Reports of officers.
(5)
New business.
(6)
Adjournment.
(Prior Code, § 2-4)
Sec. 2-44. - Rules governing conduct of meetings.
The mayor and council shall have the authority to adopt such rules governing its meetings as it may deem expedient. Except as provided by rules adopted by the mayor and council, in all matters of parliamentary procedure, Roberts' Rules of Order, Newly Revised, shall govern and apply.
(1)
The rules and order of business shall be observed in all cases, unless suspended temporarily for a special purpose of an emergency nature by a vote of all members present. Any member may move at any time for the suspension of any rule, and such motion must be seconded to entitle it to consideration.
(2)
If any member, in speaking or otherwise, transgresses the rules of the mayor and council, the presiding officer shall, or any member can, call him to order; in which case the member so called to order shall immediately cease discussion unless permitted by the presiding officer to explain; and the mayor and council if appealed to, shall decide the question without debate. If the decision is in favor of the member so called to order, he shall be at liberty to proceed; if otherwise, he shall not proceed without leave of the mayor and council to proceed in order.
(3)
Any member of the mayor and council may interrupt any other member who is speaking for the purpose of propounding a parliamentary inquiry or to make a point of order.
(Prior Code, § 2-6)
Sec. 2-45. - Conduct of spectators at meetings restricted.
(a)
Except during such periods of time at mayor and council meetings, as the mayor and council shall set aside for public discussion, it shall be unlawful for any person present as a spectator to interrupt or disturb the proceedings in any manner by voice, actions or otherwise.
(b)
During periods set aside for public discussion of any nature, any person desiring to speak shall secure the permission of the presiding officer by first silently raising his hand and being recognized. The use of profanity, obscene language, threats or any violent or abusive conduct by any person shall constitute a violation of this section.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the chief of police, upon the order of the presiding officer at any such meeting, to forcibly if necessary, evict any person violating the provisions of this section from the mayor and council meeting hall.
(Prior Code, § 2-7)
Sec. 2-46. - Questions of order.
The mayor or presiding officer shall decide all questions of order, but any member dissatisfied with his ruling shall have a right to appeal to the mayor and council, which may decide the question of order by a majority vote, which said vote shall be final and conclusive.
(Prior Code, § 2-8)
Sec. 2-47. - Majority to govern; tie vote.
In all matters pending before the mayor and council, a majority vote of those present shall govern, and in all cases of a tie, the mayor or the presiding officer shall give the deciding vote, but at no other time shall the mayor or presiding officer vote unless specially provided by ordinance or by the city Charter.
(Prior Code, § 2-9)
Sec. 2-48. - Notice to adjourn.
A motion to adjourn shall always be in order.
(Prior Code, § 2-11)

ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-70. - Election of city officers.
(a)
The mayor and council shall, at their first regular scheduled meeting in January of each year, at which members-elect are installed, must soon thereafter elect, by majority vote, the following officers:
(1)
Mayor pro tem.
(2)
Municipal judge.
(3)
Utility superintendent.
(4)
Any other officer as they may deem necessary for the proper administration of the government of the city.
(b)
The officers so elected, except as otherwise provided in this Code, shall hold their offices for and during the term of one year and until their successors are elected and qualified, or until they are suspended, discharged, removed or otherwise disqualified.
(Prior Code, § 5-1)
Sec. 2-71. - Qualification of age.
Any citizen of this state otherwise qualified, who has attained the age of 18 years, shall be eligible to hold any municipal office, except those of a judicial nature.
(Prior Code, § 5-2)
Sec. 2-72. - Oath of city officers.
All officers elected by the mayor and council shall, before entering upon the discharges of the duties of their respective offices, take and subscribe the following oath before some officer authorizes by law to administer the same:
I, ________, do solemnly swear that I will well and truly demean myself in, and faithfully discharge of the duties of, the office of ________, to which I have been elected, to the best of my ability, and that I will honestly account for and pay over all public moneys that may come into my hands during my term of office. So help me God.
(Prior Code, § 5-4)
Sec. 2-73. - Discharge of city employees—Grounds.
All employees of the city shall serve during good behavior, and no such employee shall be discharged except for just cause, such as, for example:
(1)
Termination of the need of the services an employee was hired to render.
(2)
Lack of funds available for wages or salary.
(3)
Failure or inability of the employee to perform his duties satisfactorily.
(4)
Insurrection or insubordination.
(5)
Conviction of crime under the laws of the United States or any state thereof.
(6)
Membership of affiliation with any organization promoting or advocating the overthrow of our government.
(7)
Discourtesy to the public in the discharge of his duties.
(8)
Any conduct prejudicial to the best interests and welfare of the city.
(Prior Code, § 5-5)
Sec. 2-74. - Same—Hearings; suspension.
No such employee shall be discharged for an alleged charge until a finding that just cause for dismissal exists shall be made by the mayor and council at a hearing on the matter before them. Such employee shall be given reasonable notice of the time and place of said hearing and of the alleged cause for dismissal, and at such hearing shall be given an opportunity to appear in his own behalf and to produce witnesses in his behalf. Provided, however, the mayor, with the approval of a majority of the members of the city council, may suspend an employee charged with alleged violations for dismissal pending the hearing thereon.
(Prior Code, § 5-6)
Sec. 2-75. - Fines; discharges; suspension; reprimand.
Any officer elected or appointed by the mayor and council, shall be subject to fines, removal, suspension or reprimand by the mayor and council for malpractice, incompetency or inefficiency in his discharge of duties, or for any conduct unbecoming an officer. His pay shall cease from the date of such suspension, removal or discharge.
(Prior Code, § 5-7)
Sec. 2-76. - Removal of officers.
Appointed officers provided for in the Charter shall be removed from office for any one or more of the following causes:
(1)
Incompetence, misfeasance or malfeasance in office;
(2)
Conviction of a crime involving moral turpitude;
(3)
Failure at any time to possess any of the qualifications of office as provided by the Charter or by law;
(4)
Knowingly violating any express prohibition of the Charter;
(5)
Abandonment of office or neglect to perform the duties thereof;
(6)
Failure for any other cause to perform the duties of office as required by the Charter or by state law; or
(7)
Removal of an officer pursuant to this section shall be accomplished by the following method: by the vote of three councilmembers after an investigative hearing. In the event an elected officer is sought to be removed by the action of the mayor and council, such officer shall be entitled to a written notice specifying the grounds for removal and to a public hearing that shall be held not less than ten days after the service of such written notice.
(Prior Code, § 3-1)
Sec. 2-77. - Records retention.
(a)
Records committee. There is hereby created a records committee for the City of Milner, said committee to consist of the city administrator, city clerk, and one member of the city council. It shall be the duty of the records committee to coordinate the city's records management plan; review and recommend approval, disapproval, or modification of records retention schedules to the city council; and to establish procedures for the maintenance and security of records. Review of records retention schedules shall be based upon legal, fiscal, administrative and historical retention requirements.
(b)
Meetings. The city council member who is appointed to the committee shall serve as the committee chairman and shall schedule meetings of the committee as necessary.
(c)
Retention schedule. The most recent "Retention Schedules for Local Government Paper and Electronic Records" as compiled by the Georgia Secretary of State Division of Archives and History is hereby adopted and by reference incorporated herein and made a part of this Code as completely and to the same extent as if all provisions thereof were fully recited in this section. Provided, however, that the records committee herein established and created may make recommendations to revise, amend, delete, or add schedules not in conflict with state law and as deemed advisable and necessary, and to address the retention of any record not otherwise addressed therein. Final approval authority for all recommendations by the records committee shall be vested in the city council.
(Ord. No. 0-13-01, § 1, 10-9-2013)

DIVISION 2. - CLERK AND TREASURER

Sec. 2-96. - Offices combined.
The offices of clerk and treasurer shall be, and the same are hereby, consolidated, to be known and designated as "clerk" or "clerk and treasurer."
(Prior Code, § 7-1)
Sec. 2-97. - Attendance at mayor and council meetings; records.
The clerk and treasurer shall attend all meetings of the mayor and council and keep a correct record of the proceedings of the same.
(Prior Code, § 7-2)
Sec. 2-98. - Records of minutes, licenses, books of account; insurance, fi. fas., deeds, and obligations.
The clerk and treasurer shall keep the following books and accounts:
(1)
A book of minutes, in which shall be recorded a correct record of the proceedings of all meetings of the mayor and council.
(2)
A correct record of all licenses issued.
(3)
A cash book, and such other books of accounts as the mayor and council may from time to time direct, showing of all cash items paid to the clerk for the city, and from what source the same came; and all cash paid out, and to whom and for what purpose paid.
(4)
A record of insurance carried by the city, showing amount of each policy, time taken out, date of expiration, and what property is covered by said insurance.
(5)
A fi. fa. docket each for business license fi. fas., delinquent tax fi. fas., and for each other separate and distinct kind of fi. fa. that the city may issue.
(6)
A deed record, in which shall be recorded all deeds made to the city, before the same are deposited for safekeeping.
(7)
A bills payable book (or account) showing all bonds and notes given by the city with their date, their maturity, amount of the same and rate of interest. The clerk shall enter opposite each such obligation the date when paid, the amount paid, to whom paid and how.
(Prior Code, § 7-3)
Sec. 2-99. - Issuance of licenses, notices processes, and orders.
The clerk and treasurer shall issue all licenses, summons, subpoenas, processes, orders and notices provided for by law or that may be required of him by the mayor or council.
(Prior Code, § 7-4)
Sec. 2-100. - Maintenance of all city records; public inspection.
All the records of the city of every nature and description shall be kept in the clerk and treasurer's office. No records shall be removed from this office without an order from the mayor and council, except by an officer of the city when it shall be necessary in the discharge of his duty.
(Prior Code, § 7-5)
Sec. 2-101. - Collection and payment of monies.
The clerk and treasurer shall collect all monies due the city from any source whatever, and shall pay out any sums demanded of upon proper authority. He shall be authorized to pay any accounts or other evidences of indebtedness against the city when the same shall have been ordered paid by a majority vote of the mayor and council; he shall likewise be authorized to pay out any monies due by the city, when presented with any voucher bearing the approval of the mayor and council and the signature of the mayor.
(Prior Code, § 7-6)

DIVISION 4. - CODE OF ETHICS[3]

Sec. 2-161. - Declaration of policy.
(a)
It is the policy of the city that the proper operation of democratic government requires that public officials be independent, impartial and responsible to the people; that governmental decisions and policy be made in proper channels of the governmental structure; that public office is not used for personal gain; and that the public have confidence in the integrity of its government. In recognition of these goals, a code of ethics is adopted.
(b)
This code has the following purposes:
(1)
To encourage high ethical standards in official conduct by members of the governing authority;
(2)
To establish guidelines for ethical standards of conduct for all such members of the governing authority by setting forth those acts and actions that are incompatible with the best interest of the city;
(3)
To require disclosure by such members of the governing authority of private financial or other interests in manners that affect the city; and
(4)
To serve as a basis for disciplining those who refuse to abide by its terms.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-1))
Sec. 2-162. - Intent.
It is the intent of this division that members of the governing authority avoid any action, whether or not specifically prohibited herein, which might result in, or create the appearance of, the following:
(1)
Using public office for private gain;
(2)
Impeding government efficiency or economy; or
(3)
Affecting adversely the confidence of the public in the integrity of those who conduct the affairs and business of the city.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-2)
Sec. 2-163. - Scope of persons covered.
The provisions of this division shall be applicable to the governing authority which, for purposes of this division, means the mayor, any and all members of the city council, and any and all members appointed to any board or commission of the city.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-3))
Sec. 2-164. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Benefit means:
(1)
Anything, regardless of its monetary value, perceived or intended by either the one who offers it or the one to whom it is offered to be sufficient in value to influence a member of the governing authority in the performance or nonperformance of an official action; or
(2)
Anything, regardless of its monetary value, which, under the circumstances, a reasonably prudent person would recognize as being likely to be intended to influence a member of the governing authority in the performance or nonperformance of an official action.
Censure means a public expression of severe criticism or reproach.
Complaint means a written sworn statement filed with the ethics committee containing specific allegations of misconduct by a member of the governing authority; provided, however, such allegations must be filed within six months after the complaining party knew or should have reasonably known of the alleged misconduct.
Formal reprimand means an action taken by the mayor and city council against a member of the governing authority found to be in violation of this chapter, issued in writing to the member, chastising the member for the violation. The record of the formal reprimand shall be inserted into the minutes of the city council.
Governing authority means the mayor, the city council, or any appointed board or commission of the city.
Government or city means the City of Milner, Georgia.
Immediate family means:
(1)
A member of the governing authority's spouse; and
(2)
A member of the governing authority's parents, parents-in-law, sisters, sisters-in-law, brothers, brothers-in-law, stepparents, stepbrothers or stepsisters.
Interest means any direct pecuniary benefit held by or accruing to a member of the governing authority as a result of a matter which is or which is expected to become the subject of an official action by or with the city. A member of the governing authority shall be deemed to have an interest in a matter involving:
(1)
Any person in the member's immediate family; or
(2)
Any business in which the member or a member of his immediate family is a director, officer, employee or shareholder.
Member means a member of the governing authority.
Official act or action means any legislative, administrative, appointive or discretionary act of the governing authority.
Reprimand means an expression of disapproval, either public or private.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-4))
Sec. 2-165. - Prohibitions.
(a)
Use of public property. A member of the governing authority shall not use city property of any kind for other than officially approved activities, nor shall a member direct city employees to use such property for other than official purposes.
(b)
Use of confidential information. A member of the governing authority shall not make use of, or permit others to make use of, for the purpose of furthering a private interest, city information not available to the general public.
(c)
Acceptance of gifts. A member of the governing authority shall not solicit or accept directly or indirectly anything of value from any person, corporation, or group which:
(1)
Has, or is seeking to obtain, contractual or other business or financial relationships with the city;
(2)
Seeks to have the member of the governing authority exercise a matter of discretion in his favor;
(3)
Has the appearance to influence the member of the governing authority in the performance of an official act or action.
(d)
Unauthorized use of city employees. A member of the governing authority shall not use his position to request or require a city employee to:
(1)
Do clerical work on behalf of the member's family, business, social, church, or fraternal interests;
(2)
Purchase goods and services to be used for the member's personal, business, or political purposes; or
(3)
Work for the member personally without offering just compensation.
(e)
Coercion by members of the governing authority. A member of the governing authority shall not use his position in any way to coerce, or give the appearance of coercing, another person to provide any financial benefit to him or persons within his immediate family, or those with whom the member has business or financial ties.
(f)
Prohibited contracts. The city shall not enter into any contract involving services or property with any member of the governing authority or with a business in which any member of the governing authority has an interest. This section shall not apply to the following contracts:
(1)
The designation of a bank or trust company as a depository for city funds.
(2)
The borrowing of funds from any bank or lending institution which offers the lowest available rate of interest in the community for such loan.
(3)
A contract entered into in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-10-6.
(4)
Contracts entered into under circumstances that constitute an emergency situation; provided that the mayor prepares a written record explaining the emergency.
(5)
Contracts entered into with the mayor or a member of the city council, or with a business in which the mayor or any member of the city council has an interest; provided that such contract is the result of a competitive bid, and disclosure of the nature of such person's interest is made prior to the time any bid is submitted.
(g)
Conflicts of interest. A member of the governing authority shall not knowingly have an interest in any official action.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-5))
Sec. 2-166. - Disclosure and actions following disclosure.
(a)
A member of the governing authority shall disclose the nature of any conflict of interest he has at the time such matter is presented to the mayor and council for discussion and/or action. Such disclosure shall be recorded in the minutes of the meeting and become part of the public record. Following any disclosure made pursuant to this division, the member of the governing authority shall not participate in ex parte communications with other members regarding the matter.
(b)
To avoid the appearance of impropriety, after the member of the governing authority has disclosed that he has a conflict of interest or a potential conflict of interest in any matter, the member shall leave his regular seat as a member of the governing authority and not return to it until deliberation and action on the matter is completed.
(c)
A member of the governing authority who has a conflict of interest involving a matter shall not participate in the discussion, debate, deliberation or vote, or otherwise take part in the decision-making process.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-6))
Sec. 2-167. - Ethics committee.
(a)
The mayor and city council shall appoint an ethics committee consisting of three persons. All members shall be residents of the city and shall serve a three-year term. Members of the ethics committee shall be nominated by the mayor and approved by an affirmative vote of the city council. The members of the ethics committee shall serve without compensation. The ethics committee shall have the following duties:
(1)
To review a complaint to determine whether the complaint is unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or fails to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction of the ethics committee, or is to be considered for further investigation.
(2)
Be empowered to dismiss those complaints which are unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or which fail to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction of the ethics committee; provided, however, that a rejection of such complaint by the ethics committee shall not deprive the complaining party of any action he might otherwise have at law or in equity against the respondent member of the governing authority.
(3)
To investigate the complaint and to hold a hearing within 30 days after the receipt of any complaint requiring further investigation and to receive and collect evidence and information concerning the complaint. At any hearing, the respondent member of the governing authority shall have the right to written notice of the allegations at least ten business days before the hearing, to be represented by counsel, to hear and examine the evidence and witnesses, to cross examine such witnesses and to present evidence and witnesses in opposition or in extenuation.
(4)
The decision of the ethics committee shall be reduced to writing and served upon all parties at interest. The ethics committee shall make recommendations to the mayor and city council whether to invoke the disciplinary actions described in section 2-169.
(b)
The mayor and city council shall take action upon the recommendation of the ethics committee at the next regular meeting of the mayor and council following receipt of the recommendation.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-7))
Sec. 2-168. - Receipt of complaints.
All complaints for a violation of this division shall be filed with the city clerk. The city clerk shall thereafter forward the complaint to the ethics committee.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-8))
Sec. 2-169. - Penalties.
Any member of the governing authority found to have knowingly violated any provision of this division shall be subject to reprimand, formal reprimand, or censure by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-9))
Sec. 2-170. - Right to appeal.
Any member of the governing authority or the complainant adversely affected by a decision of the mayor and city council may appeal to the county superior court within 30 days after the decision of the mayor and city council becomes final. The review by the superior court shall be limited to an inquiry of whether there was any evidence before the mayor and city council which supported the decision of the mayor and city council; provided, however, no action of the mayor and city council refusing or failing to take action pursuant to this division may be reviewed by the superior court.
(Ord. of ?, § 2(8-10))
DIVISION 5. - PROSECUTING ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-171. - Short title.
This division shall be known as the "Prosecuting Attorney of the Milner Municipal Court."
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-172. - Findings and intent.
This division is adopted to address requirements made under Georgia law for the city to pass an ordinance creating the office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court should the city choose to hire or have a prosecuting attorney of the municipal court. Under state law, in order to have a municipal court prosecutor, the city must pass this ordinance or resolution and provide to the Prosecuting Attorneys' Council of the State of Georgia a copy of the same. The city is also required to submit the name of the person appointed to be the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court within 30 days of such appointment in order to maintain the office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court. It is therefore the intent of the city to comply with Georgia law, particularly O.C.G.A. § 15.18.5 and to create this division.
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-173. - Establishment of office.
The office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court is hereby established for the purpose of providing representation of the City of Milner in matters pertaining to ordinance violations of the city and state offenses enforceable in the municipal court as allowed by Georgia law. The prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall be a part-time position.
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-174. - Qualifications.
Any person appointed as the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court of the City of Milner shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia and admitted to practice before the trial and appellate courts of this state. Nothing in this division shall prevent the city from appointing the city attorney to be the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court so long as all other requirements under the law are met.
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-175. - Oath.
The prosecuting attorney of the municipal court for the City of Milner shall take and subscribe to the following oath:
"I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as prosecuting attorney of the Municipal Court for the City of Milner."
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-176. - Term of office.
Unless otherwise provided by the Charter for the City of Milner, the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall serve a term of office of one year.
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-177. - Jurisdiction, duties, and authority.
The office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court for the City of Milner shall have the duty and authority to represent the city as defined by the City Charter and by state law, particularly as described in O.C.G.A. § 15.18.96.
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-178. - Assistant prosecuting attorney.
The prosecuting attorney of the municipal court may appoint one or more assistant prosecuting attorneys whose appointment shall be ratified by the city council before becoming an assistant prosecuting attorney for the municipal court. The assistant prosecuting attorney(s) shall meet the same qualifications as the prosecuting attorney.
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)
Sec. 2-179. - Ratification.
This division shall ratify all actions that have been taken by the persons acting in the positions of the office of the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court to date as authorized by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 14-4, § 1, 10-13-2014)

ARTICLE V. - PURCHASING

Sec. 2-225. - City purchasing agent.
(a)
Designated. The city administrator shall serve as city purchasing agent and shall have the powers and duties prescribed by this article.
(b)
Scope of purchasing authority. The agent shall have the power and it shall be his duty to purchase or contract for all supplies and contractual services needed by any using agency which derives its support wholly or in part from the city, in accordance with purchasing procedures as prescribed by this article and such rules and regulations as the agent shall adopt for the internal management and operation of the department of purchasing and such other rules and regulations as shall be prescribed by the chief administrator and the mayor and council.
(1)
Exceptions prohibited. The authority of the agent to negotiate all purchases for all using agencies shall not be abridged by excepting any particular using agency.
(2)
Unauthorized purchases. Except as herein provided, it shall be unlawful for any city officer or officers to order the purchase of any supplies or make any contract within the purview of this article other than through the purchasing agent, and any purchase ordered or contract made contrary to the provisions hereof shall not be approved by the city officials, and the city shall not be bound thereby.
(c)
Other powers and duties. In addition to the purchasing authority conferred in subsection (b) of this section, and in addition to any other powers and duties conferred by this article, the agent shall:
(1)
Minimum expenditure. Act to procure for the city the highest quality in supplies and contractual services at least expense to the city.
(2)
Encourage competition. Discourage uniform bidding and endeavor to obtain as full and open competition as possible on all purchases and sales.
(3)
Rules and regulations. Establish, and amend when necessary, all rules and regulations authorized by this article and any others necessary to its operation.
(4)
Purchasing analysis. Keep informed of current developments in the field of purchasing, prices, market conditions and new products, and secure for the city the benefits of research done in the field of purchasing by other governmental jurisdictions, national technical societies, trade associations having national recognition, and by private businesses and organizations.
(5)
Forms. Prescribe and maintain such forms as he shall find reasonably necessary to the operation of this article.
(6)
Standard nomenclature. Prepare and adopt a standard purchasing nomenclature for using agencies and suppliers.
(7)
Vendors' catalog file. Prepare, adopt, and maintain a vendors' catalog file. Said catalog shall be filed according to materials and shall contain descriptions of vendors' commodities, prices, and discounts.
(8)
Bulk purchases. Exploit the possibilities of buying in bulk so as to take full advantage of discounts.
(9)
Federal tax exemptions. Act so as to procure for the city all federal tax exemptions to which it is entitled.
(10)
Cooperation with department of finance. Cooperate with the department of finance so as to secure for the city the maximum efficiency in budgeting and accounting.

Disqualification of bidders. Have the authority to declare vendors who default on their quotations irresponsible bidders and to disqualify them from receiving any business from the municipality for a stated period of time.

Sec. 2-226. - Requisition and estimates.
All using agencies, either by or with the authorization of the head of the department under which the using agency operates, shall file with the agent detailed requisitions or estimates of their requirements in supplies and contractual services in such manner, at such times, and for such future periods as the agent shall prescribe.
(1)
Unforeseen requirements. A using agency shall not be prevented from filing, in the same manner, with the agent at any time a requisition or estimate for any supplies and contractual services, the need for which was not foreseen when the detailed estimates were filed.
(2)
Revisory power in agent. The agent shall examine each requisition or estimate and shall have the authority to revise it as to quantity, quality, or estimated cost; but revision as to quality shall be in accordance with the standards and specifications established pursuant to this article.
(Prior Code, § 12-6)
Sec. 2-227. - Encumbrance of funds.
Except in cases of emergency, the agent shall not issue any order for delivery on a contract or open market purchase until the city treasurer or other designated official shall have certified, after pre-audit, that there is, to the credit of the using agencies concerned, a sufficient unencumbered appropriation balance, in excess of all unpaid obligations, to defray the amount of such order.
(Prior Code, § 12-7)
Sec. 2-228. - Prohibition of interest.
(a)
Void orders or purchases; variance. Any purchase order or contract within the purview of this article in which the agent, or any officer or employee of the city, is financially interested, directly or indirectly, shall be void, except that before the execution of a purchase order or contract the mayor and council shall have the authority to waive compliance with this subsection when it finds such action to be in the best interests of the city.
(b)
Gifts and rebates. The agent and every officer and employee of the city are expressly prohibited from accepting, directly or indirectly, from any person to which any purchase order or contract is, or might be awarded, any rebate, gift, money, or anything of value whatsoever, except where given for the use and benefit of the city.
(Prior Code, § 12-8)
Sec. 2-229. - Competitive bidding required.
All purchases of, and contracts for supplies and contractual services, and all sales of personal property that has become obsolete and unusable shall, except as specifically provided herein, be based on competitive bids.
(Prior Code, § 12-9)
Sec. 2-230. - Formal contract procedure.
All supplies and contractual services, except as otherwise provided herein, when the estimated cost thereof shall exceed $5,000.00, shall be purchased by formal, written contract from the lowest responsible bidder, after due notice inviting proposals. All sales of personal property which has become obsolete and unusable, when the estimated value shall exceed $5,000.00, shall be sold by formal written contract to the highest responsible bidder, after due notice inviting proposals.
(1)
Notice inviting bids.
a.
Newspaper. Notice inviting bids shall be published once in at least one official newspaper in the city and at least five days preceding the last day set for the receipt of proposals.
b.
Scope of notice. The newspaper notice required in subsection (1)a of this section shall include a general description of the articles to be purchased or sold, shall state where bid blanks and specifications may be secured, and the time and place for opening bids.
c.
Bidders list. The agent shall also solicit sealed bids from all responsible prospective suppliers who have requested their names to be added to a bidders' list, which the agent shall maintain, by sending them a copy of such newspaper notice or such other notice as will acquaint them with the proposed purchase or sale. In any case, invitations sent to the vendors on the bidders' list shall be limited to commodities that are similar in character and ordinarily handled by the trade group to which the invitations are sent.
d.
Bulletin board. The agent shall also advertise all pending purchases or sales by a notice posted on the public bulletin board in the city hall.
(2)
Bid deposits. When deemed necessary by the agent, bid deposits shall be prescribed in the public notices inviting bids. Unsuccessful bidders shall be entitled to return of surety where the agent has required such. A successful bidder shall forfeit any surety required by the agent upon failure on his part to enter a contract within ten days after the award.
(3)
Bid opening procedure.
a.
Sealed. Bids shall be submitted sealed to the agent and shall be identified as bids on the envelope.
b.
Opening. Bids shall be opened in public at the time and place stated in the public notices.
c.
Tabulation. A tabulation of all bids received shall be posted for public inspection.
(4)
Rejection of bids. The agent shall have the authority to reject all bids, parts of all bids, or all bids for any one or more supplies or contractual services included in the proposed contract, when the public interest will be served thereby.
(5)
Bidders in default to city. The agent shall not accept the bid of a contractor who is in default on the payment of taxes, licenses or other monies due the city.
(6)
Award of contract.
a.
Authority in agent. The agent shall have the authority to award contracts within the purview of this article.
b.
Lowest responsible bidder. Contracts shall be awarded to the lowest responsible bidder. In determining lowest responsible bidder, in addition to price, the agent shall consider:
1.
The ability, capacity and skill of the bidder to perform the contract or provide the service required;
2.
Whether the bidder can perform the contract or provide the service promptly, or within the time specified, without delay or interference;
3.
The character, integrity, reputation, judgment, experience, and efficiency of the bidder;
4.
The quality of performance of previous contracts or services;
5.
The previous and existing compliance by the bidder with laws and ordinances relating to the contract or service;
6.
The sufficiency of the financial resources and ability of the bidder to perform the contract or provide the service;
7.
The quality, availability, and adaptability of the supplies, or contractual services to the particular use required;
8.
The ability of the bidder to provide future maintenance and service for the use of the subject of the contract;
9.
The number and scope of conditions attached to the bid.
c.
Award to other than low bidder. When the award is not given to the lowest bidder, a full and complete statement of the reasons for placing the order elsewhere shall be prepared by the agent and filed with the other papers relating to the transaction.
d.
Tie bids.
1.
Local vendors. If all bids received are for the same total amount of unit price, quality and service being equal, the contract shall be awarded to a local bidder.
2.
Outside vendors. Where subsection (6)d.1 of this section is not in effect, the agent shall award the contract to one of the tie bidders by drawing lots in public.
e.
Performance bonds. The agent shall have the authority to require a performance bond, before entering a contract, in such amount as he shall find reasonably necessary to protect the best interests of the city.
(7)
Prohibition against subdivision. No contract or purchase shall be subdivided to avoid the requirements of this section.
(Prior Code, § 12-10)
Sec. 2-231. - Small purchases.
(a)
Any contract or purchase order for supplies, services and construction not exceeding $5,000.00 may be made in accordance with small purchase procedures adopted by the purchasing agent; provided that contract requirements shall not be artificially divided so as to constitute a small purchase under this section. Insofar as it is practicable, no less than three businesses shall be solicited to submit quotations. The award shall be made to the business offering the lowest responsive quotation. The name of the business submitting a quotation and the date and amount of each quotation shall be recorded and maintained as a public record.
(b)
Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section, the purchasing agent may make purchases of goods, supplies and services that cost less than $250.00 without obtaining quotes. Purchases shall not be artificially divided so as to constitute a small purchase under this subsection.
Sec. 2-232. - Emergency purchases.
(a)
By agent; recorded explanation. In case of an apparent emergency which requires immediate purchase of supplies or contractual services, the chief administrator shall be empowered to authorize the agent to secure by open market procedure as herein set forth, at the lowest obtainable price, any supplies or contractual services regardless of the amount of the expenditure. A full report of the circumstances of an emergency purchase shall be filed by the agent with the mayor and council and shall be entered in the minutes of the mayor and council and shall be open to public inspection.
(b)
By head of departments. In case of actual emergency, and with the consent of the agent, and the approval of the chief administrator, the head of any using agency may purchase directly any supplies whose immediate procurement is essential to prevent delays in the work of the using agency that may vitally affect the life, health, or convenience of citizens.
(1)
Recorded explanation. The head of such using agency shall send to the agent a requisition and a copy of the delivery record together with a full written report of the circumstances of the emergency. The report shall be filed with the mayor and council as provided in subsection (a) of this section.
(2)
Emergency procedure. The agent shall prescribe, by rules and regulations, the procedure under which emergency purchases by heads of using agencies may be made.
(Prior Code, § 12-15)
Sec. 2-233. - Cooperative purchasing.
The agent shall have the authority to join with other units of government in cooperative purchasing plans when the best interests of the city would be served thereby.
(Prior Code, § 12-18)
Sec. 2-234. - Basis of purchases.
All purchases within the county and city shall be made strictly on the basis of value received, based on the quality of the thing purchased and the lowest price to be paid therefore, which is acceptable to the mayor and council.
