Chapter 38 - SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT[1]
Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq.; local solid waste plans, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-31.1.

ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Responsibility for disposal; storage of receptacles.
(a)
The responsibility for lawfully disposing of solid waste shall be with the individual citizen. No person shall keep or store solid waste outside of any residence or building, except in watertight containers with a securely-fastened lid.
(b)
All residents shall store their solid waste receptacles on non-pickup days at least five feet off the public right-of-way.
(Code 1992, § 12-101)
Sec. 38-2. - Penalties.
Any person convicted of violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be punished according to state law.
(Code 1992, § 12-107)
Secs. 38-3—38-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Sec. 38-21. - Collection.
The city shall provide garbage pickup to each residence and business weekly.
(Code 1992, § 12-103)
Sec. 38-22. - Disposal.
All disposal of solid waste shall be at disposal sites operated in accordance with state laws and within rules and regulations of the state department of natural resources, and at no other place.
(Code 1992, § 12-104)
Secs. 38-23—38-47. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - FEES AND BILLING

Sec. 38-48. - Fees.
Fees charged for the public collection and disposal of solid waste shall be established by the city council, from time to time.
(Code 1992, § 12-105)
Sec. 38-49. - Billing.
(a)
Against whom levied. Service charges in accordance with the schedule established under section 38-48 are levied against the owner and the occupant of the premises served, jointly and severally, but collecting from one shall relieve the other.
(b)
Charges for solid waste collection and disposal. Charges for solid waste collection and disposal service shall be billed and collected monthly in advance by the fifth day of each month and shall be payable by the owner or occupant at the office of the city clerk. When such payment has not been made by the fifth day of any month, a ten percent penalty shall be added and collected. When such payment has not been made by the tenth day of any month, service shall be discontinued until all arrears, including penalty, have been paid in full, and the following month has been paid in advance.
(Code 1992, § 12-106)
Secs. 38-50—38-78. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - PUBLIC NUISANCE

Sec. 38-79. - Public nuisance.
Any unauthorized accumulation of solid waste is hereby declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. The city, by written notice, shall notify the owner or occupant of any premises with such accumulation to remove it within two weeks or be deemed in violation of this chapter. A separate violation shall be deemed committed each day the condition continues.
(Code 1992, § 12-102)
