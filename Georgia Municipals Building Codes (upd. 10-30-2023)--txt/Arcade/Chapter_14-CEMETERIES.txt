Chapter 14 - CEMETERIES[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Georgia Cemetery and Funeral Services Act of 2000, O.C.G.A. § 10-14-1 et seq.; cemeteries, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-4 et seq.

Sec. 14-1. - Short title.
This chapter shall be known as the "Private and Church Cemetery Ordinance of Arcade, Georgia."
(Code 1992, § 37-101; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-101)
Sec. 14-2. - Site plan.
The site proposed for a cemetery shall not interfere with the development of a system of collector or larger streets in the vicinity of such site. In addition, such site shall have direct access to a public road or street. Said site plan shall be filed with the city clerk with a valid survey outlining all individual cemetery plots and appropriate setbacks as defined in section 14-4. The persons or entity engaged in such activity shall then come before the city council and, if approved, the applicant shall be issued a permit for such activity contained herein.
(Code 1992, § 37-102; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-102)
Sec. 14-3. - Site size.
Any new cemetery shall be located on a site containing not less than ten acres, except churches which shall have at least three acres.
(Code 1992, § 37-103; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-103)
Sec. 14-4. - Setbacks.
All graves or burial lots shall be set back not less than 25 feet from any property line or minor street right-of-way lines, and not less than 50 feet from any collector, arterial, expressway, or freeway right-of-way line.
(Code 1992, § 37-104; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-104)
Sec. 14-5. - Maintenance.
The entire cemetery property shall be landscaped and maintained.
(Code 1992, § 37-105; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-105)
Sec. 14-6. - Inspection.
The city council or its agents shall periodically inspect the sites of cemeteries to verify that the activities contained herein are being conducted in accordance with this chapter. If, through inspection, it is deemed that a person engaged in this activity has failed to comply with the adopted plan, a written notice shall be served upon that person or entity. The notice shall set forth the measures necessary to achieve compliance and shall state a time within which such measures must be completed. If a person or entity engaged in such activities fails to comply within the time specified, the person or entity shall be deemed in violation of this chapter.
(Code 1992, § 37-106; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-106)
Sec. 14-7. - Entry.
No person or entity shall refuse entry or access to any authorized city representative or agent who requests entry for the purpose of inspection, nor shall any person obstruct, hamper or interfere with any such representative while in the process of carrying out his official duties.
(Code 1992, § 37-107; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-107)
Sec. 14-8. - Civil penalties.
Any person or entity violating any provisions of this chapter shall be liable for a civil penalty of not less than $500.00 per day, by a sentence of imprisonment not exceeding 60 days in jail, or both fine and jail, or work alternative. Each day the violation continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1992, § 37-108; Ord. of 5-11-1998, § 37-108)
