ARTICLE VIII. - MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE SALES BY THE DRINK
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-324. - Regulations.
(a)
No sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises where sold shall be allowed except during the times for which provision is made in section 4-59.
(b)
In no event shall wine, malt beverages, or distilled spirits be sold during prohibited hours based upon the timely sale of tickets, chits, decanters, chips, tokens, or other similar devices.
(c)
Unless a wine or malt beverage license, or license for sale of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises where sold only, shall also hold a retail package license for the retail package sale of an alcoholic beverage, such licensee shall not sell, or permit the sale of, any alcoholic beverages by the package.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(G)(5); Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-325. - Entertainment at pouring outlets.
(a)
Bands or orchestras and patron dancing shall be permitted at pouring outlets:
(1)
Where adequate space exists;
(2)
Where all fire and safety regulations are met; and
(3)
Where the appropriate pouring license fee has been fully paid.
(b)
The licensee may provide electronically amplified music or live entertainment on any patio, deck, or in any other outdoor or open air dining area immediately adjacent to the licensed premises; provided, however:
(1)
Application for issuance of a temporary entertainment permit. An application for a temporary entertainment permit shall be submitted to the city clerk/treasurer with a nonrefundable payment of an administrative fee in the amount established by resolution no later than 15 days prior to the date of the event. No temporary entertainment permit shall conflict with any city sponsored special event, but may be approved upon coordination and consideration of dates, times, and schedules of activities. The following information shall be provided in the application:
a.
The name, address, and telephone number of the applicant.
b.
The applicant shall attach a map no smaller than 8.5 inches by 11 inches in size, clearly identifying the secured area proposed to be the location of the activity under the temporary entertainment permit. The proposed secured area shall take into account and provide for areas for public circulation around the secured area. The applicant shall describe in detail the size of the secured area proposed, and the manner and materials by which the applicant proposes to secure the area within which the activity under the temporary entertainment permit will be conducted. This description shall include the type and number of tables and seating arrangements to be located within the secured area, and describe the service area from which the provision of food and beverage services will be made.
c.
The applicant shall provide such additional information as the city manager may require from time to time on application forms to be provided by the city.
(2)
Administrative review and approval of a temporary entertainment permit.
a.
Upon receipt of an application hereunder, the city clerk/treasurer or his designee shall note on the application the day and time of receipt.
b.
The application shall be delivered to the city manager for review. In the event the city manager determines that the application is incomplete, the city manager shall return the application to the applicant, noting the nature of the deficiency. The city manager shall determine the completeness of the application within three business days of the date entered on the application by the city clerk/treasurer. In the event the applicant resubmits the application, the city clerk/treasurer or his designee shall note on the resubmitted application the day and time of receipt.
c.
The city manager is authorized to approve and issue a temporary entertainment permit.
(3)
Appeal of denial. Any applicant whose permit application has been denied may request a review by the city council by filing a written notice of such appeal within three business days of the notice of denial of the applicant's permit. The mayor and council shall set a hearing date within five business days of receiving such appeal request. At such a hearing, the applicant is entitled to be heard and present evidence in his behalf. The mayor and city council shall determine at the hearing whether the denial of the permit is justified. The determination by the council shall be binding upon the applicant.
(4)
Time and noise limits. No licensee shall provide any electronically amplified music or live entertainment on any patio, deck, or in any other outdoor or open air dining area after 11:00 p.m., nor exceeding a volume that would be disruptive or disturbing to any neighboring residences; provided, however, that the city manager may declare that the time provided in this subsection may be extended to 1:00 a.m., of the following day on certain days, such as New Year's Eve.
(5)
Compliance. Section 16-94 is complied with.
(6)
Removal of tents, stages, fencing, seating, tables, and other items from secured area. The applicant shall be required to remove all tenant, stages, fencing, seating, tables, and all other items provided by or belonging to the applicant from the secured area within 24 hours of the end of the event for which the permit is issued.
(c)
Failure to obtain a temporary entertainment permit or abide by the regulations for the temporary entertainment permit may result in a fine of not less than $1,000.00 per offense and possible jail time of six months. In addition, the chief of police shall be entitled to demand that a violation cease and desist, and shall be authorized to take such action as may be necessary to obtain compliance with a cease and desist order.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(G)(6); Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
State Law reference— Limitation on penalties, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-6(a)(2).
Sec. 4-326. - Prohibited conduct.
(a)
No license holder shall permit a person to perform any acts in an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises where sold that are in violation of O.C.G.A. § 16-6-8 regarding public indecency, nor permit any obscene, lewd or indecent entertainment therein.
(b)
No license holder shall permit a person to perform or simulate any act in an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises where sold which acts or simulation would constitute sexual conduct or sexually explicit nudity, as those words are defined in O.C.G.A. § 36-60-3.
(c)
No license holder of an established licensed to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises where sold shall hold, promote, sponsor or allow any contest, promotion, special night, event or other activity where patrons of the license-holding establishment are encouraged or allowed to engage in any of the conduct or to be attired as described in subsection (b) of this section.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(G)(7); Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 4-356. - Required.
(a)
Necessity for license.
(1)
It shall be unlawful in the city to pour or offer to pour, to sell for consumption on the premises, or to sell by the drink and not in its original package, any beer or malt beverages without having a malt beverage pouring license therefor issued by the city, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or of this division.
(2)
It shall be unlawful in the city to pour or offer to pour, to sell for consumption on the premises, or to sell by the drink and not in its original package, any wine without having a wine pouring license therefor issued by the city, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or of this division.
(3)
It shall be unlawful in the city to pour or offer to pour, to sell for consumption on the premises, or to sell by the drink and not in its original package, any distilled spirit without having a license for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold only therefore issued by the city, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or of this division.
(b)
Separate licenses required. A separate pouring license shall be required for each pouring outlet, and separate pouring licenses shall be required for the pouring of wine, malt beverages, and distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold only, at the same pouring outlet. Separate application must be made for each such license and outlet in accordance with the license application requirements imposed by this division.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(G)(1), (2); Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-357. - Applicant requirements.
(a)
Generally. All applicants for malt beverage pouring licenses must meet the same qualifications imposed by this division upon holders of retail package malt beverage licenses. All applicants for wine pouring licenses must meet the same qualifications imposed by this division upon holders of retail wine package licenses.
(b)
Licenses granted to brewpubs, hotels, lounges, private clubs, restaurants, special events facilities, and wine cafes only.
(1)
No application for either a wine or malt beverage pouring license shall be considered from, and no such license shall be granted to, any applicant whose business location for a proposed pouring outlet is not a brewpub, hotel, lounge, private club, restaurant, special event facility or wine cafe, as defined in section 4-1, respectively. No application for a license to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold only, shall be considered from, and no such license shall be granted to, any applicant whose business location for a proposed distilled spirit pouring outlet is not a brewpub, hotel, lounge, private club, restaurant or special event facility, as defined in section 4-1, respectively.
(2)
It is the intention of this section that malt beverage and wine by the drink be sold only at a brewpub, hotel, lounge, private club, restaurant, special event facility, or wine cafe, as herein defined. It is the intention of this section that distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold be sold only at a brewpub, hotel, lounge, private club, restaurant, or special event facility.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(G)(3), (4); Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 7-1-2013; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
