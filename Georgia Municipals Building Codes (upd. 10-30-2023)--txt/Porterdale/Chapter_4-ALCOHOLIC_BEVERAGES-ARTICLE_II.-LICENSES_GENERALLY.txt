ARTICLE II. - LICENSES GENERALLY
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-21. - Application factors for consideration of governing body.
The council, in passing upon an initial application for an alcoholic beverage license at the final meeting thereon, shall be guided by the following factors as to whether to grant or to deny such application.
(1)
The proximity of the proposed location to any other establishment selling alcoholic beverages of any nature, and the number of licenses to sell alcoholic beverages already issued within the city;
(2)
The character of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location;
(3)
The proximity of churches, schools, and playgrounds to the proposed location;
(4)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking facilities or other parking available for its patrons;
(5)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom;
(6)
The feelings and attitudes of those residing in the area adjacent to the proposed location;
(7)
The information set forth in the application;
(8)
Whether any type of alcoholic beverage license previously issued for such location was ever revoked for cause by the council;
(9)
Whether the applicant has ever sold alcoholic beverages illegally in the municipality;
(10)
The criminal record, if any, of the applicant and the outlet manager, their general good character and reputation, and their financial responsibility; provided that nonpayment of federal, state, county or city taxes shall be prima facie evidence of lack of financial responsibility;
(11)
The information required in the application or statement and whether all requirements as to notice and advertisements have been complied with; and
(12)
The history or reputation of the building or establishment proposed for the sale of alcoholic beverages for fighting, shooting, stabbing, or other acts of violence; gambling; illegal dealing in the alcoholic beverages or drugs; other violations of the law.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(13); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-22. - Package store.
Except as provided herein for licenses for the sale of distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages for consumption on the premises, and for private clubs and wine cafes, no license for the sale of alcoholic beverages at retail shall be issued to any applicant for any location other than a package store wherein the particular alcoholic beverage for which a retail license is sought is to be sold by the package only.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(14); Ord. of 8-3-2009)
Sec. 4-23. - Prohibited persons.
It shall be unlawful to grant a license for the sale of any alcoholic beverages to:
(1)
A person who is not of good moral character, as defined in section 4-26(b), or who has been convicted of a crime involving a violation of the ordinances of the city or any other city relating to the use, sale, taxability or possession of alcoholic beverages or a violation of the laws of the state or of the United States pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation or sale of any alcoholic beverages, or taxability thereof.
(2)
A person whose license under this chapter has been revoked for cause or who has had a license under this chapter revoked for cause.
(3)
Any individual who is a licensee who removes such licensee's residence from Butts, Henry, Jasper, Morgan, Newton, Rockdale, or Walton County, or otherwise becomes a prohibited person as defined in subsection (1) of this section, shall, within ten days of such event, make said fact known to the council and shall surrender such person's license upon demand by the council.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(18); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-24. - Restrictions on license.
(a)
No retail or wholesale license for the sale of alcoholic beverages, or license for consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises were sold only, shall be issued to an applicant unless said applicant shall either own the premises for which the license is sought or shall have a written lease for the premises for which the license is sought for the full period for which the license is to be issued. If said applicant shall merely lease the premises for which the license is sought, said applicant shall attach a copy of said lease to the application for the issuance of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
No retail or wholesale license for the sale of alcoholic beverages, or license for consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises where sold only, shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of said building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the city, regulations of the state revenue commissioner, and the laws of the State of Georgia. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the building official. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times. All premises for which a license for consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises where sold only shall issue shall afford therein adequate sanitary toilet facilities and shall be adequately illuminated so that all hallways, passageways, and open areas may be clearly seen by customers therein.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(21); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-25. - Application for relocation of alcoholic beverage business.
(a)
In the event that the holder of any retail alcoholic beverage license for a prescribed location in the City of Porterdale desires to move his retail alcoholic beverage business to another location in the city, said license holder shall make application to the council of the City of Porterdale for permission to relocate said business. Said application shall be in the same form and contain the same information as is required for an initial application for a retail alcoholic beverage license.
(b)
No fee shall be required for the processing of an application for relocation of a retail alcoholic beverage business.
(c)
Said applicant for relocation of a retail alcoholic beverage business shall comply with the provisions of this chapter pertaining to newspaper advertisement and the posting of signs upon the proposed new location of said business, as if said application for relocation of said business were making an initial application for said retail alcoholic beverage license.
(d)
In reviewing and acting upon an application for the relocation of a retail alcoholic beverage business and license, the council shall be guided by the same factors and considerations as if said application were an application for a new license.
(e)
Non-transferability. Except as provided herein, no license for the sale of any alcoholic beverage may be transferred from one person to another. Further, no license for the sale of any alcoholic beverage may be transferred from one location, or premises, to another without the prior approval thereof by the council in the manner described herein.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(23); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-26. - Manager.
(a)
When a license for the sale of any alcoholic beverage, either by package or for consumption on the premises, is applied for by a partnership or corporation, said applicant shall also name an individual manager who shall be responsible for managing and overseeing the proposed business of selling alcoholic beverages. Said manager must meet and maintain all requirements of an individual licensee as prescribed by this article, such as age and residence, as if said manager were individually applying for or holding such a license.
(b)
No person shall be named as or permitted to act as a manager under a license to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the council that such person is of good moral character. The term "good moral character," means that a person has not been convicted of a felony, or any crime not a felony if it involves moral turpitude, within the past ten years. The term "conviction," as used herein, includes pleas of nolo contendere or bond forfeiture when charged with such crime.
(c)
In the event such a partnership or corporation holding a license for the retail sale of an alcoholic beverage should change managers for whatever reason, said license shall immediately notify the city through the city clerk/treasurer or police chief of such change, and said new manager must meet all qualifications and requirements of this chapter for an individual holding a similar alcoholic beverage license.
(d)
Failure of a partnership or corporation holding a retail alcoholic beverage license to immediately notify the city clerk/treasurer of such a change in managers shall be grounds for suspension or revocation of said licensee's license.
(e)
In the event that such a named manager should cease to possess or maintain all of the qualifications and requirements as are required of an individual license holder for the sale of similar alcoholic beverages, the license of the partnership or corporation responsible for said manager may be suspended until said partnership or corporation names a manager who does meet and possess all such qualifications and requirements.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(17); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-27. - Distances from schools, churches and polling places.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued if the sales would violate O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21. Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the licensing of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages by licensees for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages from consumption on the premises where sold only, as to which licensees, there shall be no distance requirements from school buildings, educational buildings, school grounds, college campus, church buildings, or polling places; provided, however, that the city council may, in its discretion, issue or deny any license for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where there is evidence that the type and number of schools in the vicinity causes minors to frequent the immediate area, even though there is compliance with minimum distance from schools.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(4); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-28. - Limitation on ownership of alcoholic beverage licenses.
(a)
No person shall be issued more than one license for the retail package sale of beer or other malt beverages, nor shall any person be permitted to have a beneficial interest in more than one such retail malt beverage license issued under this chapter, regardless of the degree of such interest.
(b)
No person shall be issued more than one license for the retail package sale of wine, nor shall any person be permitted to have a beneficial interest in more than one such retail wine license issued under this chapter, regardless of the degree of such interest.
(c)
No person shall be issued more than one license for the retail sale of beer for consumption on the premises, also known as a retail beer pouring license, nor shall any person be permitted to have a beneficial interest in more than one such retail beer pouring license issued under this chapter, regardless of the degree of such interest; provided, however, that in the case of a lounge, the same person who is issued a license for a hotel operation may be issued a license for operation of a lounge associated therewith.
(d)
No person shall be issued more than one license for the sale of wine for consumption on the premises, also known as a retail wine pouring license, nor shall any person be permitted to have a beneficial interest in more than one such retail wine pouring license issued under this chapter, regardless of the degree of such interest; provided, however, that in the case of a lounge, the same person who is issued a license for a hotel operation may be issued a license for operation of a lounge associated therewith.
(e)
No person shall be issued more than one license for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold only, also known as a retail distilled spirit pouring license, nor shall any person be permitted to have a beneficial interest in more than one such retail distilled spirit pouring license issued under this chapter, regardless of the degree of such interest; provided, however, that in the case of a lounge, the same person who is issued a license for a hotel operation may be issued a license for operation of a lounge associated therewith.
(f)
For purposes of this section:
(1)
The term "person" shall include any person related to the holder of that license within the first degree of consanguinity and affinity as computed according to the canon law; and
(2)
The beneficiaries of a trust shall be considered to have a beneficial interest in any business forming a part of the trust estate.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(8); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-29. - Production of records.
In conjunction with any application for license, or in conjunction with any license which has been issued under the terms hereof, or in conjunction with the revocation, suspension or cancellation of any license, or in conjunction with any of the hearings contemplated by this chapter, or in conjunction with the payment or nonpayment of any excise tax levied or to be collected hereunder, the council shall have the right to subpoena all or any part of the records, books, documents, reports or invoices, of any licensee for the purpose of auditing the records of such licensee, securing compliance by such licensee with the provisions of this chapter, proving or disproving violation of any part of this chapter by any licensee, or to show payment or nonpayment of any taxes, fees, charges, or the like, due hereunder.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(12))
Sec. 4-30. - Grounds for suspension or revocation; notice; hearing; refund.
(a)
Any license which has been issued or which may be hereafter issued by the city to any licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages, either by package or for consumption on the premises, may be suspended for a time certain or revoked entirely for due cause as hereinafter defined, said suspension or revocation to be effective only after a hearing held before the council for the purpose of considering any such suspension or revocation. At least five days' prior written notice of a time, place and purpose of such hearing, including a specific statement of the charges upon which said suspension or revocation is being sought, shall be given to the holder of such license which is sought to be suspended or revoked.
(b)
A hearing held before the council pursuant to this section shall be conducted informally, and the technical rules of evidence shall not apply. All testimony shall be under oath and shall be recorded. The holder of the license sought to be suspended or revoked shall be entitled to be represented by legal counsel, if desired, shall be able to cross examine any witness produced by the city, and shall be able to present witnesses and evidence in such license holder's own behalf. Pursuant to such hearing, a decision to suspend or revoke the license under consideration shall be upon a majority vote of the council hearing the evidence. The license holder shall be promptly notified in writing of the council's finding and decision affecting such person's license.
(c)
In the event the holder of a license which is suspended or revoked by the city seeks to appeal said suspension or revocation to the appropriate tribunal, the cost of preparing a transcript of the hearing proceedings shall be borne by the license holder and must be paid in full at the delivery of said transcript.
(d)
Due cause for the suspension or revocation of any license shall include, but not be limited to, a violation of any laws or ordinances, both state and municipal, regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or a violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such businesses, or for the violation of any state or federal law, or for the violation of any city ordinance other than traffic ordinances, or for the failure of licensee or any employee of licensee to promptly report to the city police department any violation of the law or ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence on the premises, or for the violation of any validly promulgated law, ordinance or regulation pertaining to the sale of alcoholic beverages by any employee of the license holder involved in the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(e)
The mayor is hereby authorized to suspend any license issued hereunder for any emergency situation such as civil disorders or natural disasters or in any situation that the mayor deems such immediate suspension necessary for the protection of the health and welfare of the citizens of the city, and said suspension may be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until the mayor determines the emergency is over or until the next regular meeting of the council, or a meeting called prior to the next regular meeting, at which time the council, by majority vote, may determine that the emergency is ended.
(f)
When a license is suspended or revoked, the city shall not be required to refund any portion of the license fee to the holder of such suspended or revoked license.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(9))
Sec. 4-31. - Automatic revocation of city license upon revocation of state license.
Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license to sell at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages, the city license held by the same licensee to sell at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverage within the city shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any additional action by the council or any officer of the city.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(10))
Sec. 4-32. - False information in application.
Any material omission from or untrue or misleading information which is contained in an original, renewal or transfer application for a license hereunder shall be cause for the denial or refusal of a license, and if any license has previously been granted under such circumstances, the same shall constitute due cause for revocation of such license.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(11))
DIVISION 2. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE HANDLING PERMIT

Sec. 4-33. - Alcoholic beverage handling permit required; application.
(a)
Permit required. All persons involved in the sale or service of alcoholic beverages at businesses which hold a license to sell alcoholic beverages for on premises consumption, including owners and managers, shall not work more than ten business days after the date of their first work day without first securing an alcoholic beverage handling permit. Only one pouring permit per individual will be issued for employment at any and all establishments within the city.
(b)
Application. In order to apply for an alcoholic beverage handling permit, the applicant must submit an application to the city clerk on forms provided by the city clerk, which application shall include, but not be limited to:
(1)
Applicant's full legal name, date of birth, and social security number;
(2)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 35-3-35(a) and 35-3-35(a)(1.2), written authorization for release of personal information and criminal history record information;
(3)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 50-36-1, documentation complying with the SAVE Program;
(4)
A nonrefundable permit and application fee of $35.00; and
(5)
Such other documentation as may be required by the city clerk.
(c)
Criminal history background check usage regulations.
(1)
Information obtained and handled for any purpose pursuant to subsection (b)(2) above shall comply with all Georgia laws and the Federal Privacy Act.
(2)
No information given as part of the request for a criminal history and no record obtained pursuant to subsection (b)(2) above may be entered on any database.
(3)
No information given or obtained pursuant to subsection (b)(2) above shall be subject to the provisions of the Georgia Open Records Act.
(d)
All applications shall be sworn to by the applicant before a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths. The applicant must also certify that he or she has read and understands the legal restrictions related to the handling and sales of alcoholic beverages and the consequences that may be imposed if the applicant does not comply with such restrictions.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-34. - Considerations for granting applications for alcoholic beverage handling permits.
(a)
The city manager or his/her designee shall have the discretion to grant or refuse to grant any application for an alcoholic beverage handling permit hereunder; however, the city manager or his/her designee shall consider the following matters in connection with each application for an alcoholic beverage handling permit, which considerations are deemed to be in the public interest and welfare, as follows:
(1)
Whether the applicant has had any alcoholic beverage handling permit issued by the city or by any other licensing authority in the state relating to the sale or service of alcoholic beverages suspended within the past five years or revoked within the past ten years;
(2)
Whether the applicant has been dishonorably discharged from the armed services of the United States;
(3)
Whether the applicant is 18 years of age or older;
(4)
Whether the applicant is a citizen of the United States or a resident alien lawfully permitted for permanent residence;
(5)
Whether the applicant's reputation, character, and mental and physical capacity indicate an ability to competently fulfill the functions and duties performed by those holding alcoholic beverage handling permits;
(6)
Whether the applicant has been convicted of, pled guilty to, or entered a plea of nolo contendre to, or is presently charged with any felony, any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, any criminal offense related to lottery rules or regulations, any offense related to illegal gambling, soliciting for prostitution, pandering, letting of premises for prostitution, keeping a disorderly place, any offense related to the illegal possession or sale of illegal drugs or alcoholic beverages, any sex offense found in O.C.G.A. title 16, ch. 6, or any similar violation found in any other state or federal statute, any charges related to the manufacture or sale of alcoholic beverages, or a second offense of driving under the influence of alcohol within the last five years;
(7)
Whether the applicant has been found to have violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to the sales or service of alcoholic beverages within a five-year period immediately preceding the date of the application for an alcoholic beverage handling permit; and,
(8)
Whether the applicant has failed to meet any other criteria specified by this chapter or state law.
(b)
The city manager or his/her designee has the discretionary authority to consider any other objective criteria that is rationally related to the application, including any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, when making a decision to approve or deny an alcoholic beverage handling permit. If, in the judgment of the city manager or his/her designee, circumstances are such that granting the alcoholic beverage handling permit would not be in the best interest of the general health, safety, and public welfare, such circumstances shall be grounds for denying the application.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-35. - Denial of applications for alcoholic beverage handling permits.
(a)
If an application for an alcoholic beverage handling permit is denied, the city manager or his/her designee shall notify the applicant that the applicant's application was denied. The applicant will be provided an explanation in writing as to the basis for the denial of the application.
(b)
No person shall be issued an alcoholic beverage handling permit if it is determined that the person knowingly and willfully falsified, concealed, or covered up any material fact by any device, trick, or scheme while making application to the city manager or his/her designee for an alcoholic beverage handling permit under this division. Any such act on the part of an applicant shall also be a violation of this division and shall be punishable as set forth elsewhere within this division.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-36. - Continuing duty to report criminal convictions.
All alcoholic beverage handling permit holders shall have a continuing duty to notify the city in writing in the event that the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder is charged with, is convicted of or pleads guilty or nolo contendre to any criminal offense under the laws of this state or any other state or other jurisdiction after an alcoholic beverage handling permit has been granted.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-37. - Alcoholic beverage handling permit expiration and renewal.
Any alcoholic beverage handling permit issued hereunder shall expire one year from the date of issuance, unless earlier rescinded by the city manager or his/her designee. All persons holding alcoholic beverage handling permits and seeking to renew such permits shall submit an application to renew their permits in advance of the expiration date on forms prescribed by the city clerk or his/her designee. The city shall have ten business days to review such completed renewal application. Handling alcohol without a valid permit is expressly prohibited. In the event that the city does not issue a decision on the completed renewal application within this time period, then the application shall be deemed to have been granted.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-38. - Inspection and display of permits.
All alcoholic beverage handling permits issued hereunder shall remain the property of the city and shall be produced for inspection upon the demand of the chief of police or his/her designee or the city manager or his/her designee. Such permits shall be present on the premises at all times while the permittee is working. Any permit that is issued through administrative error may be rescinded by the city manager or his/her designee.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-39. - Administrative penalties and appeal hearings for alcoholic beverage handling permit holders.
(a)
The city manager or his/her designee shall provide written notice to an alcoholic beverage handling permit holder of the city manager's or his/her designee's intention to impose one or more of the following administrative penalties:
(1)
Suspend their permit;
(2)
Revoke their permit;
(3)
In addition, impose an administrative monetary penalty as follows: $50.00 for the first offense; $150.00 for a second offense within a 12-month period; an administrative monetary penalty not to exceed $1,000.00 for the third or subsequent offense within a 12-month period; or
(4)
Convey a warning for any violation of this division, of O.C.G.A. title 3, or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax Division of the Georgia Department of Revenue.
Such written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the facts supporting such violation, shall set forth the proposed administrative penalty(ies), and shall notify the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder of their right to appeal under the provisions of this division. Said written notification shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address set forth in the applicable application, and a copy of said written notification shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.
(b)
An alcoholic beverage handling permit holder may appeal the proposed administrative penalty(ies) by providing written notification to the city manager within ten calendar days of the date that said written notification was hand delivered to the premises. The written notification shall include the legal and factual basis upon which the applicant's appeal is based.
(c)
In the event that the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder does not timely appeal said proposed administrative penalty(ies), then said alcoholic beverage handling permit holder shall be deemed to agree with said proposed administrative penalty(ies). Further, in the event that said proposed administrative penalty(ies) include the imposition of an administrative monetary penalty, said administrative monetary penalty shall be due and owing to the city within five calendar days of the date of hand delivery of the written notification. The failure to timely pay any administrative monetary penalties shall subject the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder to further administrative penalties.
(d)
In the event that the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder does timely appeal said proposed administrative penalty(ies), then the city manager shall set an administrative hearing date that shall occur within 30 calendar days of the filing of the appeal request. Notice of said administrative hearing shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address shown on the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said written notice shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.
(e)
At the administrative hearing, the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder is entitled to be heard and present evidence on his/her behalf. The city manager shall determine whether the proposed administrative penalty(ies) of the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder as set forth in the written notification is justified using a preponderance of the evidence standard.
(f)
The city manager shall consider the following factors in determining whether the proposed administrative penalty(ies) of the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder as set forth in the written notification is justified:
(1)
Compliance with the criminal history requirements;
(2)
The history of and facts relating to previous violations of this chapter;
(3)
Non-alcohol related violations of this Code;
(4)
Violations of O.C.G.A. title 3 or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax Division of the Georgia Department of Revenue;
(5)
Documented efforts at avoiding violations of this chapter, of O.C.G.A. title 3, or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax Division of the Georgia Department of Revenue;
(6)
Community involvement; and
(7)
Any other objective and relevant facts.
(g)
The city manager, after hearing evidence, shall have the authority to confirm or modify the proposed administrative penalty(ies). The city manager shall issue a written decision on the appeal within 30 calendar days of the date of the hearing. All decisions by the city manager shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address set forth in the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said written decision shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.
(h)
Any alcoholic beverage handling permit holder may appeal the city manager's decision to the city council within ten calendar days of the date that the adverse decision is hand delivered to the premises. Notice of the appeal shall be delivered in writing to the clerk of the city. The written notification shall include the legal and factual basis upon which the applicant's appeal is based.
(i)
The city council shall set a hearing date within 30 calendar days of receiving the appeal request. Notice of said hearing shall be mailed to the applicant at the address shown on the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said notice shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred. At the hearing, the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder is entitled to be heard and present evidence on his/her behalf. The city manager or his/her representative may similarly be heard and present evidence in support of the imposition of the administrative penalty(ies). The decision of the city manager shall not be set aside unless the city council finds it to be:
(1)
Contrary to law or ordinances;
(2)
Unsupported by substantial evidence on the record as a whole; or
(3)
Unreasonable.
(j)
All decisions of the city council shall be final. The city council shall issue a written decision on the appeal within 30 days of the date of delivery of the hearing. All decisions by the city council shall be in writing and shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address set forth in the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said written decision shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-40. - Managers and employees; responsibilities.
(a)
No licensee shall allow any manager or employee who is required under this division to secure an alcoholic beverage handling permit to work beyond ten business days after their first work day unless the licensee has on file, on the premises, the current, valid alcoholic beverage handling permit of each such manager or employee. An individual working on the premises beyond ten business days after their first work day in an establishment holding a license to sell alcoholic beverages for on premises consumption without the required permit shall be considered in violation of this chapter, and the employee and licensee shall be subject to administrative penalties. An individual that works on the licensed premises beyond ten business days after their first work day without securing an alcoholic beverage handling permit shall be subject to prosecution in Porterdale Municipal Court and shall be subject to the penalty provisions contained in section 1-7 of this Code.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the permit holder to be aware of the expiration date of any permit issued hereunder, and to apply for renewal in sufficient time to avoid any lapse in the permit. The city may provide a reminder notice as a courtesy, but has no obligation to do so.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Sec. 4-41. - Time for compliance following effective date of division.
All persons currently involved in the sale or service of alcoholic beverages at businesses which hold a license to sell alcoholic beverages for on premises consumption, shall have 30 calendar days from the date this division becomes effective under the provisions of section 2-68 of this Code, within which to comply with the provisions of this division. Failure to do so shall be subject to imposition of the penalties set out in section 4-40 of this division.
(Ord. of 9-6-2016(1), § 2)
Secs. 4-42—4-52. - Reserved.
