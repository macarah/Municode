ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 26-1. - Uniform rules of the road.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 6, art. 14 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372 et seq.), O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 and O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 6 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq.) (except for O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-393 and 40-6-394) are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this municipality with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, any person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished by a fine of not more than $1,000.00 or imprisonment for not more than 60 days, or by both such fine and imprisonment.
(c)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-5-121, the municipal court of the City of Porterdale shall be authorized to impose punishment in accordance with Georgia Law for convictions of driving while a license is suspended or revoked.
(Code 2003, § 11-101)
State Law reference— Limitation on penalties, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-6(a)(2).
Sec. 26-2. - Use of speed detection devices.
It is the express desire of the city council that the Porterdale Police Department be permitted to use speed detection devices, as authorized by O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 14, and that the use of speed detection devices is approved.
Sec. 26-3. - Abandonment of motor vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to abandon or to leave unattended for a period in excess of three days any motor vehicle on any street, road, alley, or other public way in the municipality.
(Code 2003, § 31-109)
Secs. 26-4—26-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - STOPPING, STANDING AND PARKING[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200 et seq.; authority to regulate stopping, standing or parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(1).

Sec. 26-25. - General regulations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to permit any motor vehicle to stop, stand, or park in any of the following places and locations, except when necessary to avoid conflict with other traffic or in compliance with the directions of a police officer or traffic control device:
(1)
At any place where the vehicle would block the use of a driveway.
(2)
On any parkway.
(3)
At any place where official signs prohibit parking.
(b)
The chief of police of the city or any other person authorized by the mayor and council or the chief of police shall cause signs to be posted in all areas where parking is limited or prohibited by provisions of this section, indicating such limitations or prohibitions.
(c)
Any person, firm or corporation violating any provision of this section shall be fined not less than $25.00 nor more than $100.00 for each offense; and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Code 2003, § 11-201; Ord. of 1-5-2009)
State Law reference— Limitation on penalties, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-6(a)(2).
Sec. 26-26. - Unauthorized parking.
(a)
Unauthorized parking on private property prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle on any private property in an attempt to sell said vehicle unless the owner of the vehicle procures the express written consent of the owner or person in lawful possession thereof and the display of the vehicle is otherwise in compliance with the provisions of the zoning ordinance of the city and all other applicable city ordinances and state laws. In the event the identity of the driver of such vehicle is unknown, the owner or person in whose name such vehicle is registered shall be prima facie deemed to be in violation hereof.
(b)
Unauthorized parking on public rights-of-way. It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle on a public right-of-way in an attempt to sell said vehicle. In the event the identity of the driver of such vehicle is unknown, the owner or person in whose name such vehicle is registered shall be prima facie deemed to be in violation hereof. Any officer locating a vehicle in violation of this section shall place a notice on the vehicle warning that the vehicle will be towed if not removed within 24 hours. Any vehicle not removed within 24 hours may be towed in accordance with state law.
(c)
Overnight parking prohibited on publicly owned or operated property. It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle, including trailers, overnight on publicly owned or operated property. In the event the identity of the driver of such vehicle is unknown, the owner or person in whose name such vehicle is registered shall be prima facie deemed to be in violation hereof.
(d)
Residential parking.
(1)
In residential areas, parking must be either in the back alley or on a street which abuts the residential property. If for some reason beyond the control of a resident residing on the property or the property owner, parking in the back alley or street is not possible due to the absence of a back alley abutting the property, public safety concerns, or other legitimate reasons, a permit may be applied for to construct a driveway onto the rear or front yard of the property, subject to a demonstration of hardship before the Porterdale Historic Preservation Commission.
(2)
All private driveways and parking areas shall be surfaced in a porous pavement material, except where infeasible due to site specific constraints such as steep unstable slopes, poor soil, proximity of structural foundations, or other reasons which prevent the use of porous material. The material used for such paving must be approved by the city manager or his or her designee, subject to the provisions of section 119-436 of this Code and subject to the city's development regulations.
(e)
Penalty. The provisions of this section shall be enforced by the police department. Any person, firm or corporation violating any provision of this section shall be fined not less than $25.00 nor more than $500.00 for each offense, and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Code 2003, § 11-202; Ord. of 1-5-2009; Ord. of 10-26-2017(1) , § 1)
State Law reference— Limitation on penalties, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-6(a)(2).
Sec. 26-27. - Parking zones; parking overtime; separate offenses, marking system, fines, wheel locks, bonds and fees.
(a)
Parking zones.
(1)
Limited parking zones are hereby established on those streets or parts of streets described in Schedule 1, attached to the legislation from which this section is derived, in which zones the parking of vehicles upon streets shall be for a maximum of two hours between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. of any day except Sundays and public holidays. Parking for more than two hours in any limited parking zone shall be a violation of this section and each two-hour period in which any such violation shall continue shall be a separate offense.
(2)
The city's public works director is hereby authorized, subject to the approval of the city council by amendment of Schedule 1, attached to the legislation from which this section is derived, to establish parking zones at other locations upon those streets or parts of streets where it is determined upon the basis of an engineering and traffic investigation that limited parking shall be necessary to aid in the regulation, control and inspection of the parking of vehicles.
(b)
Parking overtime.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, allow, permit or suffer any vehicle of or operated by such person to be parked overtime or beyond the period of legal parking time.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, allow, permit or suffer any vehicle registered in the name of or operated by such person to stay parked in any designated parking space beyond the designated unit of time.
(3)
The police department shall adopt an appropriate marking system for identifying cars in order that this section may be adequately enforced.
(c)
Fines, bonds and fees.
(1)
The fine for a first parking offense under this section shall be $10.00.
(2)
Any person who fails to pay any parking offense within five days of receipt thereof shall pay double the fine for such parking offense.
(3)
Any person who receives two parking tickets within 30 days shall pay double the fine of the first parking offense.
(4)
Any person who receives three parking tickets within 30 days shall pay triple the fine of the first parking offense.
(5)
The vehicle owner shall, within five days of receiving the fifth unpaid parking ticket, satisfy all delinquent tickets or shall be subject to an additional penalty six times the fine of the first parking offense.
(6)
Court summons; bond for failure to appear. Additionally, such owner shall be issued a summons to appear in the municipal court of the city. If such owner fails to appear at the appointed time and date as specified in the summons, he shall be subject to arrest and shall post a $50.00 bond, plus an amount equal to all fines and penalties accrued to such date.
(Code 2003, § 11-203; Ord. of 1-5-2009)
State Law reference— Limitation on penalties, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-6(a)(2).

ARTICLE III. - GOLF CARTS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Golf carts, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-330 et seq.; local regulation of golf carts, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-331.

Sec. 26-58. - Findings; definition.
The city council finds that all streets located within the corporate limits of the city, other than the streets hereinafter specifically excepted, are designed and constructed so as to safely permit their use by motorized carts in combination with regular vehicular traffic. The term "motorized cart" means any motorized vehicle designed for the purpose and exclusive use of conveying one or more persons and equipment to play the game of golf in an area designated as a golf course. For such a vehicle to be considered a golf car or golf cart, its average speed shall be less than 15 miles per hour (24 kilometers per hour) on a level road surface with a 0.5% percent grade (0.3 degrees) comprising a straight course composed of a concrete or asphalt surface that is dry and free from loose material or surface contamination with a minimum coefficient of friction of 0.8 between tire and surface.
(Code 2003, § 11-301; Ord. of 4-5-2010)
Sec. 26-59. - Registration; inspection certification.
(a)
The owner of every motorized cart operated on the streets of the city shall cause such cart to be registered with the Porterdale Police Department within ten days of the date of its purchase. Upon such registration, the Porterdale Police Department shall issue two numerical identification decals to the owner and shall maintain a record of each identification number, along with the name and address of the registered owner. The identification decals shall be affixed to the front windshield and rear fender area of the cart so as to be fully visible when the cart is in operation.
(b)
A registration fee of $15.00 for each cart shall be payable at the time of registration, and the registration shall be effective for a period of five years. If the cart is transferred to another owner for operation in the city, the registration shall also be transferred to the new owner within ten business days of the date of transfer of the cart, and a transfer fee in the amount of $15.00 paid to the city.
(c)
The exhaust system of every registered cart which is powered by a gasoline engine shall be maintained in proper working condition. It is unlawful for any registered cart on which any device installed thereon by the manufacturer for the purpose of controlling exhaust emissions has been removed or altered so as to interfere with its operation to be operated on any city street.
(Code 2003, § 11-302; Ord. of 4-5-2010)
Sec. 26-60. - Operation regulations.
(a)
Operation of motorized carts in the city shall comply in every respect with the requirements of Georgia Law, including, without limitation, the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-330 and 40-6-331, all other statutory regulations applicable to vehicular traffic, and safety equipment including lighting equipment under O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 8, art. 1, pt. 2 (O.C.G.A. § 40-8-20), excepting only the provision of O.C.G.A. § 40-8-23(d) (requiring a light to illuminate a rear registration plate); brakes under O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 8, art. 1, pt. 3 (O.C.G.A. § 40-8-50 et seq.); horns, exhaust systems, mirrors, windshields, tires, safety belts and energy absorption systems under O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 8, art. 1, pt. 4 (O.C.G.A. § 40-8-70 et seq.), the provisions of which specifically are made applicable to operation of motorized carts under this article. These provisions include a requirement that low-speed vehicles display an amber strobe light in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 40-8-35.
(b)
Only persons possessing a valid driver's license or instruction permit issued by the State of Georgia, another state of the United States of America, or an appropriate international agency, shall be permitted to operate a motorized cart on the streets of the city. Operation of a motorized cart by a person holding an instruction permit shall be in compliance with the conditions of O.C.G.A. § 40-5-24(a) (that is, accompanied by a person at least 21 years of age who is licensed as a driver for a commercial or noncommercial Class C vehicle, who is fit and capable of exercising control over the vehicle, and who is occupying a seat beside the driver).
(c)
Motorized carts shall not be operated on sidewalks or walking trails in the city, unless otherwise specifically marked for use by motorized carts.
(d)
Motorized carts shall not be operated on the following streets within the city:
(1)
Crowell Road.
(2)
Covington Bypass Road.
(3)
Georgia Highway 81, west of the intersection of Highway 81 with Crowell Road and Covington Bypass Road.
Maps showing prohibited streets will be kept on file and available for public inspection during regular business hours at the Porterdale Police Department.
(e)
Motorized carts may cross the streets listed in subsection (d) of this section only at intersections with streets that cross the said streets.
(f)
Crossing of state highways by motorized carts shall only be permitted at crossings approved by the Georgia Department of Transportation.
(g)
Motorized carts shall not be operated on or across any street for which the speed limit is set at greater than 35 miles per hour, nor on or across any street on which such operation is determined by the mayor and council to be otherwise unsafe.
(h)
Motorized carts shall only be operated on city streets that are posted with signage authorizing such operation as required by O.C.G.A. § 40-6-331. Signage will be erected on a case by case basis when requested of and approved by the City of Porterdale mayor and city council.
(Code 2003, § 11-303; Ord. of 4-5-2010)
