ARTICLE II. - RETAIL SALES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-19. - Short title.
The ordinance from which this article is derived shall be known and may be cited as "The City of Lookout Mountain, Georgia Controlled Beverages Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 69, art. I, § 1, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-20. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means any person making application for a license in accordance with the provisions of this article.
Commissioner shall have the meaning set forth and further defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(5).
Community convenience commercial district means the area defined as such in the zoning ordinance.
Controlled beverages means liquor or distilled spirits, malt beverages and wine.
Existing licensee means a person or entity which holds a current, valid license, issued by a municipality in the Chattanooga metropolitan area, to sell beer and wine for consumption on the premises.
Georgia farm winery shall have the meaning set forth and further defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1(a)(2).
License means, unless otherwise designated or unless the context so requires, the authorization by the city to engage in the sale of controlled beverages at retail and/or the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises.
Licensee means, unless otherwise designated or unless the context so requires, any person licensed to engage in the sale of controlled beverages at retail and/or any person licensed to engage in the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises.
Liquor ordistilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned-in locality, whether a room or building, wherein controlled beverages shall be sold and/or consumed.
Private club has the meaning set forth in the zoning ordinance.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment, with a seating capacity of at least 50 persons, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its guests, and at which at least one meal per day shall be served at least six days a week, with the exception of holidays, and at which the serving of controlled beverages to be consumed on the premises shall be incidental to the principal business conducted on the premises.
Samples means a tasting of an individual wine in an amount of not more than two ounces, which may be given complimentary or for a fee.
Special event means an event lasting no longer than three consecutive days conducted for charitable or civic purposes (including, but not limited to, fundraisers to benefit schools or charitable organizations) or which are otherwise of significant benefit to the Lookout Mountain community.
Tasting room shall have the meaning set forth and further defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1(a)(3).
Tourist-oriented commercial district means the area defined as such in the zoning ordinance.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume, made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation, or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage.
(Ord. No. 69, art. I, § 2, 6-30-1977; Ord. No. 257, § 1, 6-16-2011; Ord. No. 285, § 1, 4-16-2015)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSING AND REGULATION OF RETAIL SALES FOR OFF-PREMISES CONSUMPTION

Sec. 4-44. - Unlawful activity.
No person shall engage in the sale of controlled beverages at retail in the city without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this article. Any person violating the rules and regulations set forth herein shall be subject to revocation of any license issued hereunder, and punishment as prescribed by the city. In addition to the rules and regulations herein set forth, each licensee doing business in the city under this article shall comply with all laws of the state, federal laws, and rules and regulations of the state revenue commission relating to the sale and distribution of controlled beverages in the state, and any violation of same shall subject said licensee to immediate suspension or revocation of any license issued hereunder and also shall subject the licensee to criminal prosecution by the proper authority as provided by law and the ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 69, art. II, § 1, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-45. - The applicant.
(a)
Qualifications. Any applicant for a license to sell controlled beverages at retail within the city must have the following qualifications:
(1)
The applicant must be a person of good moral character and at least 21 years old. The applicant shall not be an illegal alien.
(2)
The applicant shall not have been convicted within ten years of the date of his application under any federal, state, or local law for a criminal offense involving moral turpitude, nor shall any applicant have been convicted under any federal, state, or local law of any criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations, if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant will not maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with federal, state or other laws of the city.
(3)
The applicant shall not have had revoked for cause, such as a violation of regulations or improper operation, within three years next preceding the filing of his application, any license issued to the applicant by the county, state, or any other state or county, to sell controlled beverages of any kind.
(4)
The applicant shall not be an employee of the city.
(b)
Joint or corporate applicants. If the application covers a partnership, all members of the partnership must meet the qualifications herein set forth, and if the application is in the name of a corporation, all officers of said corporation must possess the qualifications herein set forth.
(Ord. No. 69, art. II, § 2, 6-30-1977; Ord. No. 229, § 1, 3-22-2007)
Sec. 4-46. - The application.
(a)
Form of application. An application for the sale of controlled beverages at retail shall be made on forms furnished by the city and shall be presented and processed in person by the applicant, and shall be accompanied by the following:
(1)
A completed application form with all attachments and requirements for a state license;
(2)
A personal performance bond in the amount of $500.00 payable to the city, and in a form satisfactory to the city, requiring the faithful observance and performance by the applicant of the rules and regulations contained in this article;
(3)
Evidence of the applicant's ownership of the building or proposed building for which the license is sought, or a copy of the lease, if the applicant is leasing the building;
(4)
The annual fee herein required either in cash or a certified check;
(5)
Financial statements on forms to be prepared by the city establishing the applicant's financial responsibility; and
(6)
In the case of the sale at retail of liquor, detailed plans and specifications of the building for which the license is sought or proposed plans and specifications and a building permit for any proposed building, either of which shall comply with the ordinances of the city, regulations of the state revenue commission, and the laws of the state.
(b)
Notice of hearing on application. Notice of the hearing of each application shall be advertised in the official newspaper of the city once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the city council at which the application is to be considered.
(c)
Time of hearing. Applications filed with the city in any calendar year shall be considered only at the regular monthly meeting of the city council in January of the next succeeding year.
(d)
Falsification of application. The making of any statement on an application for a license which shall be false or later found to be false shall constitute grounds for denial of or revocation of any license.
(Ord. No. 69, art. II, § 3, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-47. - Locational and siting considerations.
modified
(a)
Building. No license for the sale at retail of liquor shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete or unless a building permit shall have issued by the city on proposed plans and specifications. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with the ordinances of the city, regulations of the state revenue commission, and the laws of the state. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the premises are readily visible at all times from the front of the street on which the premises is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the premises.
(b)
Location. No license for the sale of controlled beverages at retail shall be granted to any person unless the entire premises of the proposed location is situated within the community convenience commercial district or the proposed licensee is a private club whose retail sales are limited to malt beverages and wine, and:
(1)
In the case of the sale at retail of malt beverages and wine, is situated more than 100 yards from any school or schoolhouse;
(2)
In the case of the sale at retail of liquor is situated more than 50 yards from the property line of any church and more than 200 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, the measurement in either case to be by way of the nearest traveled road, street, or highway to the nearest property line of the church, school or college campus, as the case may be.
(c)
Number of locations. Only one license for the sale of controlled beverages at retail shall be issued to any one person in the city, and each license shall cover only one premises. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the operator or manager of a private club having more than one premises may apply for and obtain a license to sell malt beverages and/or wine at retail at each premises where those controlled beverages are to be sold, and a separate license shall be required for each such premises.
(Ord. No. 69, art. II, § 4, 6-30-1977; Ord. No. 325 , § 1, 4-9-2020)
Sec. 4-48. - Regulation of operation of sales of controlled beverages at retail.
modified
(a)
Control of premises. Each licensee shall be the owner, operator or manager of the premises for which the license is held and shall be active in and solely responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(b)
Handling of controlled beverages. No licensee shall keep any controlled beverages stored in any bonded or other type warehouse in the city and no licensee shall enter into any type of arrangement whereby controlled beverages ordered by said licensee are stored for or on said licensee's behalf by any licensed wholesaler of controlled beverages. A licensee shall keep no inventory or stock of controlled beverages in any place except the premises owned, managed or controlled by the licensee.
(c)
Premises open to inspection. The premises of the licensee shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials authorized to conduct such inspections.
(d)
Employees of licensee. All managers, clerks or other employees of the licensee shall be of good character, and the licensee shall employ no person who is under 18 years of age in any capacity wherein such person would be engaged in the sale of controlled beverages.
(e)
Breach of peace. No licensee shall commit any breach of the peace or engage in any disorderly conduct, or act in any lewd, immoral or improper manner or allow any lewd, improper or immoral entertainment, conduct or practice upon the premises owned, leased or controlled by the licensee.
(f)
Gambling on premises. The licensee shall not permit to be conducted and shall not conduct or carry on any gambling, betting, games of chance, punch-boards, slot machines, pinball machines, lotteries or tickets or chances therein or any schemes of hazarding money or any other thing of value on any of the licensee's premises or in any room or building adjoining the same, owned, leased or controlled by the licensee.
(g)
Sale on premises only. No licensee shall sell or deliver any controlled beverages to any person except on the premises of the licensee. Except in the case of sales of malt beverages or wine by a duly licensed private club, such controlled beverages shall be only be sold for consumption off the premises of the licensee.
(h)
Time of sale. No licensee shall furnish, sell or offer for sale any controlled beverages on any day prior to 7:00 a.m. or after 12:00 midnight, and in addition thereto, at any of the other following times:
(1)
At any time on Thanksgiving Day, Christmas Day;
(2)
At any time when in violation of a city ordinance or regulation or special order of the city; or
(3)
On Sundays except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m.
(i)
Additional regulations concerning sale of liquor at retail. In the case of the sale of liquor at retail, the licensee shall sell or offer to sell in the original or unbroken package only and shall sell no package containing less than one-half of a pint.
(Ord. No. 69, art. II, § 5, 6-30-1977; Ord. No. 260, § 1, 9-29-2011; Ord. No. 315 , § 1, 2-14-2019; Ord. No. 325 , § 2, 4-9-2020)

DIVISION 3. - LICENSING AND REGULATION OF SALES OF CONTROLLED BEVERAGES FOR ON-PREMISES CONSUMPTION

Sec. 4-70. - Unlawful activities.
No person shall engage in the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises in the city without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this article. Any person violating the rules and regulations set forth herein shall be subject to revocation of any license issued hereunder and punishment as prescribed by the city. In addition to the rules and regulations herein set forth, each licensee doing business in the city under this division shall comply with all laws of the state, federal laws, and rules and regulations of the state revenue commission relating to the sale and distribution of controlled beverages in the state, and any violation of same shall subject said licensee to immediate suspension or revocation of any license issued hereunder, and also shall subject said licensee to criminal prosecution by the proper authority as provided by law and the ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 69, art. III, § 1, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-71. - The applicant.
(a)
Qualification. Any applicant for a license to sell controlled beverages for consumption on the premises within the city shall have the qualifications set forth in section 4-45(a), which qualifications are specifically incorporated herein by reference, and in addition thereto, must be the owner, operator or manager of a restaurant or private club.
(b)
Joint or corporate applicants. If the application covers a partnership, all members of the partnership must meet the qualifications herein set forth, and if the application is in the name of a corporation, all officers of said corporation must possess the qualifications herein set forth.
(Ord. No. 69, art. III, § 2, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-72. - The application.
(a)
Form of application. An application for the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises shall be made on forms furnished by the city, and shall be presented and processed in person by the applicant, and shall be accompanied by the documents required by section 4-46(a)(1) through (5).
(b)
Notice of hearing on application. Notice of the hearing of each application shall be advertised in the official newspaper of the city once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the city council at which the application is to be considered.
(c)
Time of hearing. For the remainder of the year 1977, applications filed with the city prior to the last day of any month shall be considered by the city council at the regular meeting of the council the next succeeding month. Thereafter, applications filed with the city in any calendar year will be considered only at the regular monthly meeting of the city council in January of the next succeeding year.
(d)
Falsification of application. The making of any statement on an application for a license which shall be false or later found to be false shall constitute grounds for denial of or revocation of any license.
(Ord. No. 69, art. III, § 3, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-73. - Locational and siting considerations.
modified
(a)
Location. No license for the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises shall be granted to any person unless the entire premises of the proposed location is situated within the community convenience commercial district or the tourist-oriented commercial district, and:
(1)
In the case of the sale for consumption on the premises of malt beverages and wine, is situated more than 100 yards from any school or school house;
(2)
In the case of the sale for consumption on the premises of liquor, is situated more than 1,000 yards from the property line of any church and 1,000 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, the measurement in either case to be by way of the nearest traveled road, street, or highway to the nearest property line of the church, school or college campus, as the case may be.
(b)
Private clubs. Anything in this section to the contrary notwithstanding, any private club may obtain a license for the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises even though not located within the community convenience commercial district or the tourist-oriented commercial district.
(c)
Number of locations. Each license for the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises shall cover only one premises, and a separate license shall be required for each such premises.
(Ord. No. 69, art. III, § 4, 6-30-1977; Ord. No. 302, § 1, 6-15-2017; Ord. No. 316 , § 1, 4-11-2019)
Sec. 4-74. - Regulation of operation of sales of controlled beverages for consumption on the premises.
modified
In addition to the regulations set forth in section 4-48(a) through (f), which regulations are specifically incorporated herein by reference:
(1)
Sale on premises only. No licensee shall sell or deliver any controlled beverages to any person except on the premises of the licensee. Except in the case of sales of malt beverages or wine by a duly licensed private club, such controlled beverages shall only be sold for consumption on the premises.
(2)
Limitation on size of container of controlled beverage. No licensee may purchase any liquor in a container size larger than one-half gallon. No licensee shall sell liquor in unbroken packages or any other controlled beverages in such a quantity as may be in excess of that which could be consumed on the premises.
(3)
Time of sale. No licensee shall furnish, sell or offer for sale any controlled beverages on any day prior to 7:00 a.m. or after 12:00 midnight and, in addition thereto, at any of the other following times:
(a)
At any time when in violation of a city ordinance or regulation or a special order of the city; or
(b)
On Sundays, except that licensed establishments meeting the minimum sales or income requirements set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7(j)(1) may sell controlled beverages on Sundays from 11:00 a.m. until 12:00 midnight; provided, however, that the foregoing restrictions on Sunday sales shall not apply to private clubs.

Sec. 4-92. - License authorized.
A Georgia farm winery may, subject to the provisions of this article, provide samples of its wine and the wine of any other Georgia farm winery for consumption on the premises, and may also sell such wine in closed packages for consumption off the premises, in a tasting room at a location that is one of the locations authorized by O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1(b). The license authorized by this article shall be limited to Georgia farm winery tasting rooms licensed by the state in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1 et seq., and the licensee shall be permitted to perform only those acts which are allowed by such statutes. No license or opportunity for a license is hereby created authorizing any other use, and sales of controlled beverages at retail by persons other than Georgia farm wineries shall continue to be prohibited in all zoning districts of the city other than the community convenience commercial district. No Georgia farm winery shall be permitted to sell wine on Sundays except for the sale of packaged wine at retail between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m.
(Ord. No. 257, § 2(art. IV, § 1), 6-16-2011; Ord. No. 260, § 2, 9-29-2011)
Sec. 4-93. - Unlawful activities.
No person shall engage in the dispensation or sale of wine in a tasting room without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this division. Any person violating the rules and regulations set forth herein shall be subject to revocation of any license issued hereunder and punishment as prescribed by the city. In addition to the rules and regulations herein set forth, each licensee doing business in the city under this division shall comply with all laws of the state, federal laws, and rules and regulations of the state revenue commission relating to the sale and distribution of controlled beverages in the state, and any violation of same shall subject said licensee to immediate suspension or revocation of any license issued hereunder, and also shall subject said licensee to criminal prosecution by the proper authority as provided by law and the ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 257, § 2(art. IV, § 2(1)), 6-16-2011)
Sec. 4-94. - The applicant.
(a)
Qualifications. Any applicant for a license to dispense and sell wine in a tasting room within the city shall have the qualifications set forth in section 4-45(a), which qualifications are specifically incorporated herein by reference, and in addition thereto, must be the owner of a Georgia farm winery.
(b)
Joint or corporate applicants. If the application covers a partnership or limited liability company, all members of the partnership or limited liability company must meet the qualifications herein set forth, and if the application is in the name of a corporation, all officers of said corporation must possess the qualifications herein set forth.
(Ord. No. 257, § 2(art. IV, § 2(2)), 6-16-2011)
Sec. 4-95. - The application.
(a)
Form of application. An application for the sale of wine in a tasting room shall be made on forms furnished by the city, and shall be presented and processed in person by the applicant, and shall be accompanied by the documents required by section 4-46(a)(1) through (5). Such application shall also be accompanied by proof satisfactory to the city that the Georgia farm winery which the applicant represents has been issued a farm winery license by the commissioner.
(b)
Notice of hearing on application. Notice of the hearing on each application shall be advertised in an official newspaper of the city once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the city council at which the application is to be considered. The application may be considered only at a regular monthly meeting of the city council.
(c)
Falsification of application. The making of any statement on an application for a license which shall be false or later found to be false shall constitute grounds for denial of or revocation of any license.
(Ord. No. 257, § 2(art. IV, § 3), 6-16-2011)
Sec. 4-96. - Locational and siting considerations.
(a)
Location. No license for the dispensation and sale of wine in a tasting room shall be granted to any person unless the entire premises of the proposed location is situated within the community convenience commercial district or the tourist-oriented commercial district. In addition, such premises must be situated more than 100 yards from any school or school house.
(b)
Number of locations. The owner of a Georgia farm winery shall be entitled to the issuance of only one license for the operation of a tasting room, and each license issued under this article shall cover only one premises.
(Ord. No. 257, § 2(art. IV, § 4), 6-16-2011)
Sec. 4-97. - Compliance with other provisions of this article.
Each licensee of a tasting room shall be subject to all provisions of this article applicable to sales of controlled beverages at retail and to all provisions of this article applicable to sales of controlled beverages for consumption on premises, and shall pay the fees applicable to licenses for the sale of controlled beverages at retail as well as the fees applicable to licenses for the sale of controlled beverages for consumption on premises.
(Ord. No. 257, § 2(art. IV, § 5), 6-16-2011)

DIVISION 5. - SPECIAL EVENT PERMITS[3]

Sec. 4-123. - Authorized.
Subject to the provisions of this division, a person or organization may apply to the city council for, and the city council may issue, a permit authorizing the person or organization to sell beer and/or wine at a special event. A special event permit authorizes the person or organization to sell beer and/or wine only for the limited time period and at the specific location specified in the permit. The time period for which the permit will be issued will be limited to three consecutive days, and the permit will only be valid for the single event specified in the permit.
(Ord. No. 285, § 2(art. V, § 1), 4-16-2015)
Sec. 4-124. - Unlawful activities.
No person or organization shall engage in the dispensation or sale of beer or wine at a special event without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this article. Any person violating the rules and regulations set forth herein shall be subject to immediate revocation of any permit issued hereunder and punishment as prescribed by the city. In addition to the rules and regulations herein set forth, each permittee shall comply with all laws of the state, federal laws, and rules and regulations of the state revenue commission relating to the sale and distribution of controlled beverages in the state, and any violation of same shall subject said permittee to immediate suspension or revocation of any permit issued hereunder, and shall also subject said permittee to criminal prosecution by the proper authority as provided by law and the ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 285, § 2(art. V, § 2), 4-16-2015)
Sec. 4-125. - Conduct of event.
All persons who are provided beer or wine at a special event must be of legal age, and the individuals conducting the special event will be responsible for obtaining proof of age from each person who is sold or otherwise provided beer or wine. If the special event is conducted on city property, each person who purchases or is otherwise provided beer or wine must wear a wrist band to verify that proof of age has been provided, and must sign a waiver of liability and indemnity agreement in a form provided by the city.
(Ord. No. 285, § 2(art. V, § 3), 4-16-2015)
Sec. 4-126. - The applicant.
(a)
Qualifications. Any applicant for a permit to dispense or sell beer and/or wine at a special event within the city shall have the qualifications set forth in section 4-45(a), which qualifications are specifically incorporated herein by reference.
(b)
Joint or corporate applicants. If the application covers a partnership or limited liability company, all members of the partnership or limited liability company must meet the qualifications herein set forth, and if the application is in the name of a corporation, all officers of said corporation must possess the qualifications herein set forth.
(c)
Organization as applicant. A charitable or other nonprofit organization may be an applicant for a permit to dispense or sell beer and/or wine at a special event. In this case, however, a minimum of two individuals holding positions of authority in the organization must meet the qualifications herein set forth and must agree in writing, on a form provided by the city, to be personally responsible for the conduct of the event.
(Ord. No. 285, § 2(art. V, § 4), 4-16-2015)
Sec. 4-127. - Application.
(a)
Form. An application for a permit to dispense or sell beer and/or wine at a special event within the city shall be made on forms furnished by the city, and shall be presented and processed in person by the applicant, and shall be accompanied by the documents required by section 4-46(a)(1), (2), and (5), along with the required permit fee in cash or certified check. Proof of liability insurance providing coverage for injuries relating to the sale of alcohol shall also be provided. If the special event is conducted on city property, such insurance shall be in an amount not less than $1,000,000.00 and shall name the city and its agents and officials as additional insureds.
(b)
Notice of hearing on application. Notice of the hearing on each application shall be advertised in an official newspaper of the city once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the city council at which the application is to be considered.
(c)
Expedited application process. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the city council may, in its sole discretion, issue a special event permit to an existing licensee without requiring the existing licensee to post a personal performance bond and without the requirement of an advertised public hearing. An existing licensee wishing to utilize the expedited application process must provide the city clerk with proof of the existing license, proof of financial responsibility, and proof of insurance, all supported by such documentation as the city, in its sole discretion, may require.
(d)
Falsification. The making of any statement on an application for a special event permit which shall be false or later found to be false shall constitute grounds for denial of or revocation of any permit.
(Ord. No. 285, § 2(art. V, § 5), 4-16-2015)
Sec. 4-128. - Locational and siting considerations.
(a)
Location. No permit for sale of beer and wine at a special event shall be issued to any person unless the entire premises of the proposed location is situated within the community convenience commercial district or the tourist-oriented commercial district; provided, however, that a permit for the sale of beer and wine at a special event in the single-family district may, in the sole discretion of the city council, be issued for events which are determined to be of particular benefit to the Lookout Mountain community, including, but not limited to, fundraisers for the city and for community schools.
(b)
Number of locations. A permit for the sale of beer and/or wine at a special event shall be issued for only one premises.
(Ord. No. 285, § 2(art. V, § 6), 4-16-2015)
Sec. 4-129. - Compliance with other provisions of this article.
Each permittee under this article shall be subject to all provisions of this article applicable to sales of controlled beverages for consumption on premises, and shall pay the fees for the permit from time to time established by the city council.
(Ord. No. 285, § 2(art. V, § 7), 4-16-2015)

DIVISION 6. - PROVISIONS APPLICABLE TO BOTH THE REGULATION OF ON-PREMISES AND OFF-PREMISES SALES

Sec. 4-157. - License.
(a)
Grant of license. The city council may, in its discretion, consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on any applicant, any application or the proposed location of any premises. If, in the judgment of the city council, circumstances are such that the granting of any license hereunder would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for not issuing any license.
(b)
License a privilege. Any license issued hereunder shall be a mere grant of privilege to carry on business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this division or any future ordinance, and subject to revocation in the manner herein provided. Any license issued hereunder shall clearly reflect the scope of authority granted to the licensee, and shall have printed on the front thereof the following:
"THIS LICENSE IS A MERE PRIVILEGE SUBJECT TO BEING REVOKED AND ANNULLED BY THE MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF LOOKOUT MOUNTAIN, GEORGIA, UPON NOTICE AND OPPORTUNITY TO BE HEARD."
(c)
Term. Any license issued hereunder shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and no such license may be renewed. A licensee who desires to continue in business during the next or subsequent calendar year must make a new application for such year on or before November 1 of the preceding year, which application may incorporate by reference those portions of previous applications not substantially changed or otherwise modified.
(d)
Assignment of license. No license issued hereunder shall be transferrable or assignable to any person or other location, and in the event that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender his license to the city.
(e)
Responsibility. Each licensee shall keep a copy of this division on the premises and shall instruct any person working therein with respect to the terms hereof, and each licensee shall at all times be familiar with the terms hereof. Each licensee shall be held responsible for any acts of said licensee's employees or agents in violation of this division or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commission, where applicable.
(Ord. No. 69, art. IV, § 1, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-158. - Unlawful activities of persons other than licensee.
(a)
Consumption on premises. It shall be unlawful for any person to open, drink or otherwise consume any controlled beverages at or upon the premises where such controlled beverages are purchased unless the owner, operator or manager thereof shall hold a license for consumption of controlled beverages on the premises.
(b)
Disorderly conduct. It shall be unlawful for any person to commit any breach of the peace or any disorderly conduct, or to act in any lewd manner or to allow any lewd entertainment, conduct or practice upon the premises of a licensee.
(c)
Manufacturing or transporting of controlled beverages. Except for wholesalers delivering controlled beverages to licensees in the city, it shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell, possess or transport for the purpose of sale any controlled beverages within the city.
(Ord. No. 69, art. IV, § 2, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-159. - Fees payable by licensees.
(a)
Amount. In addition to the excise taxes imposed by the ordinances of the city on the sale of controlled beverages, each applicant for a license issued hereunder shall pay to the clerk of the city at the time of the filing of the application, a fee as determined from time to time by the city council.
(b)
Forfeiture of fees and bond. Upon the violation of this division or any part thereof, or upon the licensee ceasing to do business for any other reason whatsoever, any fee payable hereunder and so much of the bond required to be posted by section 4-46(a)(2) as the city council shall determine based upon the seriousness of the violation after a hearing conducted in accordance with section 4-160 shall be forfeited to the city, and said licensee shall have no further right to or interest in said fee or bond.
(Ord. No. 69, art. IV, § 3, 6-30-1977)
State Law reference— License fees, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-4-48, 3-4-110, 3-5-42, 3-5-43, 3-6-40.
Sec. 4-160. - Revocation or suspension of license.
(a)
Procedure. Any license issued hereunder, or which may hereafter be issued by the city may be suspended or revoked for due cause (as hereinafter defined) after a hearing held for the purpose of considering such suspension or revocation. At least five days' prior written notice shall be given to the licensee, which notice shall contain a statement of the charges upon which such hearing shall be held.
(b)
Due cause. Due cause for the suspension or revocation of any license issued hereunder shall consist of any of the following:
(1)
The violation of any provision of this division or the violation of any law, ordinance or regulation made pursuant to lawful authority for the purpose of regulating the sale of controlled beverages.
(2)
The change in circumstances of any licensee in such a way that had such circumstances existed when the application of such licensee was filed, a license would not have been granted.
(3)
Any recurrent breaches of the peace, disturbances or altercations occurring on the premises of the licensee, or the failure of the licensee to report to the city any violation of the laws or ordinances governing the sale of controlled beverages of which the licensee has knowledge.
(c)
Re-application for license. When any license issued hereunder is suspended or revoked by the city, the city shall not accept or consider any application from the licensee whose license is suspended or revoked within 12 months from the time of such suspension or revocation.
(d)
Suspension in the event of disorder. The city council is hereby authorized to suspend the sale of controlled beverages under any license issued hereunder for any emergency situation such as civil disorders or natural disasters or in any situation that the city council deems such immediate suspension necessary for the protection of the health and welfare of the citizens of the city. Such suspension may be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until the city council determines the emergency is over or until the next regular meeting of the city council, whichever is first to occur.
(Ord. No. 69, art. IV, § 4, 6-30-1977)
Secs. 4-161—4-188. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - EXCISE TAXES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-189. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as "The City of Lookout Mountain, Georgia Taxed Beverages Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 72, art. I, § 1, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-190. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means that area lying within the corporate boundaries of the City of Lookout Mountain, Georgia, or, where the context may require, an officer, agent or official of the City of Lookout Mountain, Georgia.
License means the authorization by the city to engage in the sale of taxed beverages.
Licensee means any person who holds a license from the city to sell liquor by the drink.
Liquor ordistilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume.
Liquor by the drink means any liquor served for consumption on the premises.
Malt beverages means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverages" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Purchaser means any person who orders and gives present or future consideration for any taxed beverage.
Retailer means any person who sells or offers to sell liquor at retail.
Seller means any retailer, wholesaler or licensee.
Taxed beverages means liquor, wine and malt beverages.
Wholesaler means any person who sells or offers to sell malt beverages at wholesale.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage.
(Ord. No. 72, art. I, § 2, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-191. - Malt beverages.
(a)
There is hereby imposed and levied on each wholesaler selling malt beverages within the city an excise tax in the amount of:
(1)
$6.00 for each 15½-gallon container of malt beverages sold within the city, and being commonly known as tap or draft beer, provided that the tax imposed by this section shall be prorated for tap or draft beer containers of different sizes.
(2)
Four thousand, one hundred and sixty-six ten-thousandths of a cent ($0.004166) for each ounce of malt beverages sold within the city and which is not tap or draft beer, as described and taxed in subsection (a)(1) of this section.
(b)
No decal, stamp or other identifying mark will be required on malt beverages sold within the city.
(Ord. No. 72, art. II, § 1, 6-30-1977)
State Law reference— Municipal tax on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80 et seq.
Sec. 4-192. - Liquor by the drink.
There is hereby imposed and levied upon each purchaser of liquor by the drink an excise tax in the amount of three percent of the purchase price of said drink.
(Ord. No. 72, art. II, § 2, 6-30-1977)
State Law reference— Tax on sales by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-130 et seq.
Sec. 4-193. - Liquor.
There is hereby imposed and levied upon each retailer selling liquor by the package an excise tax at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(Ord. No. 72, art. II, § 3, 6-30-1977)
State Law reference— Authority for above tax, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-80.
Sec. 4-194. - Wine.
There is hereby levied upon each retailer selling wine by the package an excise tax at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(Ord. No. 72, art. II, § 3, 6-30-1977)
State Law reference— Authority for above tax, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60.
Secs. 4-195—4-211. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - COLLECTION OF TAXES

Sec. 4-212. - Exemption.
This division does not apply to malt beverages.
Sec. 4-213. - Collection by the seller.
Every seller of taxed beverages shall be authorized and directed to collect the taxes imposed and levied hereunder from purchasers of such beverages upon each such sale within the city.
(Ord. No. 72, art. III, § 1, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-214. - Credit or deferred payment sales.
In each case where the sale of taxed beverages shall be by deferred payment or credit, the seller is liable for the amount of tax at the time of the sale. In determining the amount of the deduction to which licensees are entitled under section 4-218, the sale shall be treated as if it were made for cash.
(Ord. No. 72, art. III, § 2, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-215. - Records.
Every seller of taxed beverages shall be required to maintain records of all sales taxed under this article. Such records must show separately the purchase price of taxed beverages and the amount of tax imposed by this article thereon. Records shall be maintained for a period of at least one year from the date of sale, and shall be kept available for inspection by the city.
(Ord. No. 72, art. III, § 3, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-216. - Reports.
(a)
Each seller who has sold taxed beverages within the city shall file a monthly report.
(1)
The reports from wholesalers must list the gross receipts from the sale of malt beverages itemized by container size and the amount of taxes collected on such sales;
(2)
The reports from licensees must show the gross receipts from the sale of liquor by the drink and the amount of taxes collected on such sales;
(3)
The reports from retailers must list the gross receipts from the sale of liquor itemized by container size and the amount of taxes collected on such sales.
(b)
All reports required under this section must be filed with the city on or before the tenth day of the month next following the month of sale.
(Ord. No. 72, art. III, § 4, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-217. - Due date of taxes.
The taxes imposed by this division shall be due and payable to the city on or before the tenth day of the month following the month of sale.
(Ord. No. 72, art. III, § 5, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-218. - Collection fee allowed licensees.
In reporting and paying the amount of tax due to the city, a licensee shall be allowed a deduction equal to that rate authorized for deductions from state tax under O.C.G.A. title 48, ch. 8, art. 1 (O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq.), provided that said tax is not delinquent at the time of payment.
(Ord. No. 72, art. III, § 6, 6-30-1977)
Sec. 4-219. - Penalties.
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a seller liable for penalties equal to ten percent of the total amount due during the first 30-day period following the date such report and remittance were due, and a further penalty of ten percent of the amount of such remittance for each successive 30-day period or any portion thereof during which such report and remittance are not filed. The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the seller making such report liable for a penalty equal to 50 percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report. Such failure to make a timely report or remittance, or the filing of a false or fraudulent report, shall also constitute grounds for the revocation of the license issued by the city to said seller.
(Ord. No. 72, art. III, § 7, 6-30-1977)
