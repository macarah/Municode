ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Elected officials covered by workers' compensation.
Certain elected officials of the county shall be covered under the association of county commissioners of the state group self-insured workers' compensation fund. While performing their elected duties, the board of commissioners, sheriff, probate judge, clerk of court, magistrate judges, volunteer firemen and tax commissioner shall be covered under the workers' compensation laws of the state.
(Res. of 12-8-1983, § I)

Sec. 2-3. - Enactment of ordinances and amendments.
Except in the case of extreme emergencies, as determined solely by the board of commissioners, all ordinances adopted by the board and having regulatory or penal effect, shall be adopted in the following manner:
(1)
All proposed ordinances shall be in written form.
(2)
Ordinances shall be additions or amendments to this Code. Resolutions shall be other actions of the county board of commissioners that are not intended to be additions or amendments to this Code. Resolutions are not regulated by this ordinance.
(3)
The title of a proposed ordinance or amendment and a summary thereof, or the proposed ordinance in full, at the discretion of the board of commissioners, shall be read by the chair or the chair's designee at two regularly scheduled meetings of the board of commissioners prior to its adoption and public input will taken at each hearing.
(4)
After two readings the proposed ordinance, at the discretion of the board of commissioners, may be adopted or rejected at a subsequent regularly scheduled meeting, or it may be tabled pursuant to the ordinance to provide parliamentary procedures of the county board of commissioners, as adopted on February 27, 1996.
(5)
After adoption of ordinances, the county clerk shall number ordinances consecutively, in the order of their final adoption, and shall copy them into a permanent record book used solely for this purpose; and shall do likewise for resolutions, using a separate series of numbers and a separate book. The original copy of all ordinances, resolutions and motions shall be filed and presented by the county clerk in the official minutes of the board of commissioners.
(6)
Copies of all adopted ordinances or amendments shall be forwarded to the codification agent in a timely manner for incorporation in this Code.
(Ord. of 6-10-2003)

DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS, ORGANIZATION AND PUBLIC ACCESS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Due notice requirements for other than regular meetings, requirement to prescribe the time, place and dates of regular meetings of governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1; exemptions from open meeting requirements, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-3.

Sec. 2-51. - Scope of division.
All meetings of the board of commissioners shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions of this division.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996)
Sec. 2-52. - Open meetings.
All meetings of the board of commissioners shall be held in accordance with the provisions of the Georgia Open Meetings Act, O.C.G.A. §§ 50-14-1—50-14-6. The public shall at all times be afforded access to all meetings other than executive sessions provided for in section 2-69.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-01)
Sec. 2-53. - Quorum.
A quorum must be present for conducting meetings of the board of commissioners. A quorum is a majority of all of the members of the board then in office. It is the duty of the chairman to enforce this rule. Any commissioner may raise a point of order directed to the chairman if he believes that a quorum is not present. If, during the course of a meeting, a commissioner leaves and a quorum no longer exists, the meeting shall not continue. If a quorum is not attained within 30 minutes, the meeting shall be suspended and reconvened at the call of the chairman.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-02)
Sec. 2-54. - Voting; majority.
Passage of a motion shall require the affirmative vote of a majority of commissioners present and voting at a meeting at which a quorum is present. Unless otherwise specified, a majority shall mean at least half the commissioners present, plus one, excluding abstentions. Where a two-thirds majority is specified, a majority vote shall mean at least two-thirds of the commissioners present, excluding abstentions.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-03)
Sec. 2-55. - Abstentions.
A commissioner shall vote on all motions unless he has a conflict of interest that would prevent him from making a decision in a fair and legal manner. If a conflict of interest does exist, the commissioner shall explain for the record his decision to abstain on any vote.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-04)
Sec. 2-56. - Chairman.
The board of commissioners shall elect a chairman from the board members at the beginning of each calendar year. The chairman of the board of commissioners is responsible for the orderly conduct of all commission meetings. In order to fulfill his duties, the chairman must enforce the rules of procedure that are adopted by the board of commissioners. The chairman shall be impartial and conduct the meetings in a fair manner. The chairman may introduce motions or second a motion and shall vote on all motions unless abstaining as provided by section 2-55.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-05)
Sec. 2-57. - Vice-chairman.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall elect a vice-chairman from the board members at the beginning of each calendar year. The vice-chairman shall fulfill the duties of the chairman if the chairman is not in attendance.
(b)
If the chairman and the vice-chairman are absent and a quorum is present, the remaining commissioners shall select a commissioner to serve as temporary chairman of the meeting until the chairman or vice-chairman is present at the meeting.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-06)
Sec. 2-58. - Agenda.
(a)
The county administrator, with the participation of the chairman of the board of commissioners, shall prepare an agenda of subjects to be acted on at each meeting. The agenda shall include items submitted by commissioners, the county attorney, citizens or civic groups in addition to other normal and customary subjects. The regular meeting agenda shall close at 1:00 PM on Friday preceding the Tuesday regular meeting. Work sessions may be called by the chairman at any time prior to the regular meeting for discussing but not voting on agenda items. Whenever a quorum of the board is present for agenda work sessions, the meeting shall be open to the public and all notice and record-keeping requirements must be met. The agenda shall be made available to the commissioners at least one business day before every board meeting.
(b)
Members of the public may request that a particular subject be placed on the agenda for the following meeting. To be considered, this request must be submitted in writing to the administrator and received no later than the close of business on Thursday preceding the Tuesday meeting.
(c)
The order of the agenda may be changed during a meeting by a majority vote of the board. A new subject that requires attention may be added by a majority vote of the board.
(d)
A copy of the final meeting agenda and a list of those members present shall be made available to the public for inspection within two business days of the adjournment of any meeting.
(e)
Nothing herein shall be construed to restrict the board from voting or otherwise acting at any lawfully called meeting.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-07; Res. of 2-13-2001)
Sec. 2-59. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Amd. of 7-24-2012 repealed § 2-59, which pertained to consent agenda and derived from Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-08.
Sec. 2-60. - Minutes.
(a)
The clerk of the board of commissioners shall promptly record the minutes for each board meeting. The minutes serve as the official written record of the board meeting and shall be open for public inspection once approved as official by the board but in no case later than immediately following the next regular meeting of the board. The minutes shall specify the names of commissioners present at the meeting, a description of each motion or other proposal made at the meeting, the commissioner who proposed each motion, the commissioner who seconded each motion, and a record of all votes. In the case of a roll-call vote, the name of each commissioner voting for or against a proposal shall be recorded. It shall be presumed that a commissioner has voted in the affirmative unless the minutes show otherwise. More detailed information may be included in the minutes at the request of the board.
(b)
The board must approve the minutes before they can be considered as an official record of the board. A copy of the minutes from the previous meeting shall be distributed to the commissioners at least one business day before the following meeting. The minutes of the previous meeting shall be corrected and approved by the board at the beginning of each meeting. A majority vote is required for approval. Conflicts about the content of the minutes shall be decided by majority vote. Upon being approved the minutes shall be signed by the chairman and attested to by the clerk of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-09)
Sec. 2-61. - Order of business.
(a)
All regular board of commissioners meetings shall follow an established order of business. The order is as follows:
(1)
Call to order.
(2)
Prayer.
(3)
Pledge of Allegiance.
(4)
Welcome.
(5)
Approve agenda.
(6)
Approve minutes of previous meeting(s).
(7)
Remarks by invited guests, committees, authorities.
(8)
Reports by constitutional officers and department heads.
(9)
County administrator's reports.
(10)
Chairman's report.
(11)
Commissioners' reports.
(12)
Old business.
(13)
New business.
(14)
Public comment.
(15)
Executive session (if required).
(16)
Adjournment.
(b)
Executive sessions may be held as required to consider personnel, real estate or legal matters as permitted by law and described in section 2-69.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-10; Res. of 3-12-2002; Amd. of 7-24-2012)
Sec. 2-62. - Decorum.
(a)
All commissioners must conduct themselves in a professional and respectful manner. A commissioner should speak only after he has been recognized by the chairman. All comments made by a commissioner should address the motion being discussed and should normally be directed to the chairman and not to other commissioners, staff or citizens in attendance. Personal remarks are inappropriate.
(b)
The chairman shall enforce these rules of decorum. If a commissioner believes that a rule has been broken, he can raise a point of order. A second is not required. The chairman can rule on the question or he can allow the board to debate the issue and decide the issue by majority vote.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-11)
Sec. 2-63. - Public participation.
(a)
Generally. Public participation in meetings of the board of commissioners is encouraged and shall be permitted in accordance with the provisions of this section.
(b)
Public comments. The final agenda item of the meeting shall be reserved for comments from the public. All members of the public who wish to address the board should, when possible, submit their name and the topic of their comments to the county administrator at least three business days before the board meeting in order for the commissioners to prepare an appropriate response if such is required. These individuals will be allotted five minutes to make their comments, and their comments must be limited to their chosen topic. These limits may be waived by a majority vote of the board.
(c)
Public participation on agenda items. By a ruling of the chair or a majority vote of the board, public comment on an agenda item may be allowed at the time the item is being considered by the board. These comments must be limited to the subject being debated. Members of the public should stand, state their name and address, and speak for no more than five minutes. Members of the public may speak only once on any individual agenda topic. These limits may be waived by a majority vote of the board. Any member of the public wishing to speak on a topic must first be recognized by the chairman before addressing the board.
(d)
Decorum. Members of the public shall not make inappropriate or offensive comments at a board meeting and are expected to comply with the rules of decorum established for the commissioners. Individuals who violate any rules of the board may be ruled out of order by the chairman or on a point of order made by any commissioner. A majority vote of the board will rule on the point of order. Any individual who violates the rules of decorum may be removed from the meeting at the direction of the chairman.
(e)
Public hearings. The board may schedule public hearings for the purpose of soliciting public comment on any subject of interest to the board. Hearings may be held immediately prior to or following a meeting of the board or at such other places and times as the board may determine. No official action shall be taken at such public hearing.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-12)
Sec. 2-64. - Parliamentarian.
The county attorney, if present, shall serve as parliamentarian for all board meetings. In the absence of the county attorney, the county administrator shall serve.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-13)
Sec. 2-65. - Amendments to rules.
Any amendments to the rules of order must be submitted by a commissioner, in writing, to the chairman, who shall place the proposed changes on the agenda for the next regularly scheduled board meeting. All amendments shall require a two-thirds vote of the board to be adopted.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-14)
Sec. 2-66. - Regular meetings.
Regular meetings of the board of commissioner shall be held at 6:00 p.m. on the second and fourth Tuesday of each month. All regular meetings shall be held in the commission meeting room at the county administrative and emergency services building. A notice shall be posted and maintained containing this information in a conspicuous place available to the general public at the regular meeting place of the board and on the bulletin board at the county courthouse.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-15; Amd. of 1-26-2010; Amd. of 1-8-2019)
Sec. 2-67. - Special meetings and rescheduled regular meetings.
A regular meeting of the board of commissioners may be canceled, rescheduled or moved to a new location within the county by the chairman for any reason. Other special meetings may be scheduled by the chairman or at the request of two commissioners. Whenever a rescheduled regular meeting or any other special meeting is to be held at a time and place other than the regularly scheduled time and place, written notice shall be posted for at least 24 hours at the place of the regular meetings. In addition, written or oral notice shall be given by the clerk, county administrator or chairman at least 24 hours in advance of the meeting to either the legal organ of the county or a newspaper having a circulation equal to that of the legal organ and to each member of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-16)
Sec. 2-68. - Emergency meetings.
When emergency circumstances occur, the board of commissioners may hold a meeting with less than 24 hours' notice to the public. When such meetings are to be held, public notice shall be given as prescribed in section 2-67 as soon as practical and possible. The notice shall include the subjects expected to be considered or subjects that were considered at the meeting. Minutes of emergency meetings shall include the reasons constituting the emergency and all actions taken as prescribed in section 2-60(a).
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-17)
Sec. 2-69. - Executive sessions.
(a)
Executive sessions of the board of commissioners may be held for the purpose of conducting business excepted from public access requirements of the Georgia Open Meetings Act, O.C.G.A. §§ 50-14-1—50-14-6 and relating to:
(1)
Potential or pending litigation matters;
(2)
Potential purchase or sale of real estate by the county government; and/or
(3)
Discussion of sensitive personnel matters where rights of employee privacy might be involved.
Any vote taken as a result of discussions in an executive session shall be taken in an open meeting.
(b)
No executive session shall be held except pursuant to a majority affirmative vote of the board of commissioners taken in a public meeting. The minutes of the public meeting shall reflect the names of the commissioners present, those voting for the executive session and the specific reason for the executive session. Minutes of the executive session may be maintained by the clerk of the board of commissioners at the discretion of the chairman or by a majority vote of the commissioners present. Any such minutes shall be maintained in a confidential file and shall not be subject to disclosure; except that disclosures of such portions of minutes identifying real estate to be acquired or sold by the board may be delayed only until such as the acquisition or sale of the real estate has been completed, terminated, abandoned or court proceedings have been initiated.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-18)
Sec. 2-70. - Suspending the rules of order.
Rules of order may be suspended in case of an emergency. A motion to suspend the rules requires a second, is debatable, and requires a two-thirds vote of the board. Rules governing quorum, section 2-53, voting methods and requirements covered by sections 2-54 and 2-55 as well as the notification to commissioners of meetings required by sections 2-67 and 2-68 and rules necessary for compliance with state law cannot be suspended.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-19)
Sec. 2-71. - Committees.
The chairman of the board of commissioners may appoint committees composed of members of the board, members of the board and citizens at large, or solely citizens at large to study matters involving the good and welfare of the county. Any such committee may make recommendations to the board of commissioners, but no committee shall be empowered to make any final decision on any matter before it. Whenever any committee is appointed, its duties, any limitations on the scope of its duties, and the times, places and periods of time for which the committee may operate shall be determined by the chairman with the concurrence of the board. The chairman shall serve as an ex officio member of all such committees.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-20)
Sec. 2-72. - Visual and sound recordings.
Visual, sound, and visual and sound recordings shall be permitted for all public meetings provided devices used for such recordings do not create an impediment to the normal conduct of meetings.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-21)
Sec. 2-73. - Illegal meetings and actions.
All meetings held by any commissioners in conflict with provisions of this division shall be deemed to be illegal, and any action taken at such meetings shall not be binding on the board of commissioners or county government.
(Ord. of 3-12-1996, § 1-22)
Sec. 2-74. - Chain of command.
In the absence of the county administrator and his or her designee, the chain of command for operations shall be the chairman, followed by the vice chairman, followed by the commissioner with the longest tenure on the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 5-26-2015)
Secs. 2-75—2-105. - Reserved.

DIVISION 3. - EXPANSION OF HOSPITAL AUTHORITY AREA OF OPERATIONS

Sec. 2-181. - Findings.
(a)
The county was authorized under the Hospital Authorities Law, codified as O.C.G.A. § 31-7-70 et seq. to create the Hart County Hospital Authority.
(b)
The county is authorized and required under the act to approve the expansion of any hospital authority that desires to expand its "area of operation" (as defined in the act) into the county.
(c)
The Royston Authority proposes to execute and deliver a promissory note, from the Royston Authority to Tri-County Bank of Royston, in the principal amount of $500,000.00 (the "note"), for the purpose of loaning the proceeds from the note to Cobb Foundation, Inc. (the "foundation"), pursuant to a loan agreement, dated as of the date of the note, between the Royston Authority and the foundation, in order to finance certain capital improvements at the Hart County Hospital located in Hartwell, Georgia (the "project").
(d)
The hospital authority of the City of Royston, Georgia (the "Royston Authority") has requested that the county grant approval to the Royston Authority to expand the Royston Authority's "area of operation" (as defined in the act) to include Hart County and the City of Hartwell, Georgia.
(Res. of 6-25-1996)
Sec. 2-182. - Declaration of public purpose.
The county finds, determines and declares that the approval of the Royston Authority to expand its area of operation is a lawful and valid public purpose in that it will further the public purposes intended to be served by the act.
(Res. of 6-25-1996, § 1)

DIVISION 4. - JOINT DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[7]

Footnotes:
--- (7) ---
State Law reference— Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.; Resource Recovery Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-63-1 et seq.; Georgia Development Authority Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-10-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-211. - Activated.
It is declared that there is a need for a joint development authority to function in Franklin, Hart and Stephens counties. Such authority, to be known as the "Joint Development Authority of Franklin, Hart and Stephens Counties" is created and activated. The authority shall transact business pursuant to, and exercise the powers provided by the provisions of, the Development Authorities Law, codified in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Res. of 1-28-1997, § 1)
Sec. 2-212. - Joint authority.
The joint development authority is created and activated by proper resolution of the governing bodies of Franklin, Hart and Stephens counties as a joint authority pursuant to the provisions of 1981 Ga. Laws, page 1419, and O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.1.
(Res. of 1-28-1997, § 2)
Sec. 2-213. - Directors.
(a)
The number of members of the joint development authority shall be 12. Four members shall be taxpayers residing in Franklin County, Georgia, and shall be appointed initially as provided in this division and thereafter by the board of commissioners of Franklin County, Georgia, as their respective terms expire. Four members shall be taxpayers residing in this county and shall be appointed initially as provided in this division and thereafter by the board of commissioners as their respective terms expire. Four members shall be taxpayers residing in Stephens County, Georgia, and shall be appointed initially as provided in this division and thereafter by the board of commissioners of Stephens County, Georgia, as their respective terms expire. The members shall receive no compensation for their services but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(b)
After expiration of the initial terms, the terms of office of all members shall be terms of four full years. Successors to the initial board of directors shall be appointed by the governing body of the county making the original appointment. If at the end of any term of any member a successor to such member has not been appointed, the member whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is appointed. A majority of the members of the authority shall constitute a quorum, but no action may be taken by the authority without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the authority.
(Res. of 1-28-1997, § 3)
Sec. 2-214. - Officers.
The directors of the joint development authority shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice-chairman and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may, but need not be, a director.
(Res. of 1-28-1997, § 4)
