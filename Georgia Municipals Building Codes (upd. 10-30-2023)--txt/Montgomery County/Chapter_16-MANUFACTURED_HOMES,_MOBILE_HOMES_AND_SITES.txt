ARTICLE II. - PERMIT FOR INSTALLATION OF ON-SITE SEWAGE MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS

Sec. 16-20. - Permit required.
Prior to the construction of any house or building, alteration, or addition, from which sewage must be disposed of by means of a septic tank or alternative system in the County, and/or prior to the location or relocation of any mobile home in the County, an on-site sewage management permit must be obtained from the County Health Department.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 1; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 1)
Sec. 16-21. - Application.
The application for the permit shall be filed with the Health Department and shall disclose the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The address of the location of the building that is to be constructed or the address of the building being repaired or the location and address of the mobile home being located or relocated upon the land in the County.
(3)
The number of bedrooms in residential structures or the anticipated water usage for commercial structures.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 2; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 2)
Sec. 16-22. - Evaluation; permit issuance.
It shall be the duty of the County Health Department Environmental Health Officer to evaluate and issue an on-site sewage management permit for those sites that comply with the County Board of Health's rules and regulations for on-site sewage management systems. The cost of said permit shall be set up by the County Board of Health. It shall be the duty of the sewage disposal system contractor, or property owner if no contractor is used, to prior to the commencement of construction request evaluation by the Environmental Health Officer to determine if the septic tank system complies with the rules and regulations for on-site sewage management system as promulgated by the County Health Department.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 3; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 3)
Sec. 16-23. - Affixing decal.
All decals issued pursuant to this article relative to mobile homes shall be affixed to the mobile home in such manner as to be clearly visible from the front and from the street or roadway.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 5; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 5)
Sec. 16-24. - Permit required for moving.
It shall be unlawful for any mobile home dealer or mover to locate or relocate a mobile home on any land in the unincorporated areas of the County, until the owner of said home has received a permit for an on-site sewage management system and obtained a decal from the Tax Commissioner and have the same affixed thereto.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 6; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 6)
Sec. 16-25. - Violation.
(a)
Any person, firm, or corporation who shall violate the provisions of this article by erecting, constructing, or repairing a building, or by moving, locating, or relocating a mobile home in the County, without first having applied and been granted a permit for an on-site sewage management permit for the proposed location by the Environmental Health Officer of the County; and in the case of locating or relocating a mobile home without obtaining a mobile home decal; shall be punished by fine or imprisonment or both not exceeding a fine of $500.00 or imprisonment 60 days or both. Violation of this subsection shall commence when the sewage disposal system is covered or when the house or building is occupied.
(b)
Violators of this article shall be prosecuted by the Montgomery County Attorney in the Magistrate Court, without a jury. The Magistrate Court may suspend any fine or sentence upon statement by the person signing affidavit that satisfactory compliance with the requirements of this section have been met. Review of convictions shall be by certiorari to the Superior Court.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 7; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 7)
Sec. 16-26. - Violation by sale.
Any person or firm that desires to sell property that is less than three acres, and is advertised or stated in any manner to a potential buyer to be a lot suitable for construction of a residential unit; must obtain an on-site sewage management permit or subdivision approval from the Montgomery County Health Department prior to the sale. Violators of this section shall be punished by fine or imprisonment or both not exceeding a fine of $500.00 or imprisonment for 60 days or both, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20. Violators of this section shall be prosecuted by the County upon the sworn affidavit of buyer of such property or upon the affidavit of an official of the County or the County Health Department in the Magistrate Court without a jury. The Magistrate Court may suspend any fine or sentence upon satisfactory evidence that proper restitution has been made to the named buyer caused by failure of seller to comply with this section. Review of convictions shall be by certiorari to the Superior Court.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 8; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 8)
Sec. 16-27. - Failure to correct violation.
Any person or firm who shall violate any provision of the County Department of Health's rules and regulations for on-site sewage management systems and has not fully corrected such violation after 60 days' notice by the Environmental Health Officer shall be punished by fine or imprisonment or both not exceeding a fine of $500.00 or imprisonment for 60 days' or both, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20. Violators of this section shall be prosecuted by the Board of Health upon the sworn affidavit of an official of the County or the Health Department in the Magistrate Court of the County, without a jury. The Magistrate Court may suspend any fine or sentence upon statement from person signing affidavit or from the County Board of Health that satisfactory compliance with this section has been obtained. Review of convictions shall be by certiorari to the Superior Court.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 9; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 9)
Sec. 16-28. - Agent responsible for the environmental health duties.
The County Environmental Health Officer as used in this article shall be the person appointed by the County Board of Health as the Board's employee or agent responsible for the environmental health duties of the County Board of Health.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. II, § 11; Res. of 9-3-1996, art. II, § 11)

ARTICLE III. - MOBILE HOME SITE PERMIT

Sec. 16-47. - Permit.
No mobile home shall be located and set up for occupancy, or relocated and set up for occupancy in the County without first obtaining and displaying thereon a mobile home site permit.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 1; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 1)
Sec. 16-48. - Application.
Prior to the issuance of a mobile home site permit, the owner thereof, or an agent of the owner, shall apply to the Tax Assessors of the County, or their delegate, for a mobile home site permit. The permit shall be in the form as prescribed by the Board of Tax Assessors of the County. Said application shall, without limitation, include the following:
(1)
Name of owner.
(2)
The location, by address and/or the tax map parcel number identification, of where the mobile home is to be set up for occupancy.
(3)
The previous location of the mobile home (address, map and parcel number, if in the County; County and State, if outside of the County; and name of seller if this is first occupancy of owner.)
(4)
Name of owner of real estate on which mobile home is to be situated.
(5)
The mailing address of owners.
(6)
The name and address of applicant, if different from owner.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 2; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 2)
Sec. 16-49. - Permit fee.
An applicant for a mobile home site permit shall pay a fee for said permit which fee must be paid prior to the issuance of said permit. The fee shall be in an amount as established by the Board of Commissioners from time to time.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 3; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 3)
Sec. 16-50. - Issuer of permit.
Upon the completion of the application for a mobile home site permit as set forth herein, and the payment of the permit fee, the Tax Assessors or their delegate shall issue a "Mobile Home Site Permit." The permit shall be in the design and color as deemed appropriate by the Board of Tax Assessors of the County.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 4; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 4)
Sec. 16-51. - Display of permit.
No mobile home shall be moved into or within the County for which a mobile home site permit is required by the provisions of this article, unless the mobile home site permit is affixed to the middle area of the left rear of the mobile home as it appears while being transported.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 5; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 5)
Sec. 16-52. - Utility services prohibited.
No public utility shall provide service to any mobile home which is hereafter located or relocated in the County without first determining that a mobile home site permit was issued for said mobile home as required by the provisions of this article.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 6; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 6)
Sec. 16-53. - Enforcement.
The Tax Assessors of the County or their agents, the Sheriff of the County, the Deputies thereof, Constables, and all other law enforcement officers shall be authorized to issue citations to violators of any provisions of this article. Such citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall have an identifying number by which it shall be filed with the Court, shall indicate the identity of the accused and the date of service, and shall be signed by the officer who completes and serves it.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 7; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 7)
Sec. 16-54. - Penalty.
Any person violating this article, or any provision hereof upon conviction, shall be punished as follows: by a fine of not less than $25.00 but not more than $500.00.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 8; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 8)
Sec. 16-55. - Court proceedings
(a)
Violations of this article shall be tried upon citation as described in section 16-25 and may be tried with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations. The County Attorney shall serve as prosecuting attorney.
(b)
Violations of this article shall be tried in the Magistrate Court and shall be tried in accordance with the Official Code of Georgia and/or applicable County ordinance.
(c)
Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 2003, ch. 15, art. III, § 9; Ord. of 1-6-1998, art. III, § 9)
Secs. 16-56—16-85. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - MANUFACTURED HOMES

Sec. 16-86. - Enactment and short title.
The Board of County Commissioners of the County hereby enacts and adopts the following article, which shall be known and cited as the "Montgomery County Manufactured Home Ordinance." Those incorporated areas within Montgomery County are encouraged to adopt this or similar articles regarding manufactured homes for use within their jurisdictions.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 1)
Sec. 16-87. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board of Commissioners means the Board of County Commissioners of the County.
Building official means the person, whom the Commissioners have appointed to administer and enforce the provisions of this article. This person may also be referred to as "Code Enforcement."
DCA means Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
HUD means U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development.
Installation means the construction of a foundation system and the placement or erection of a manufactured home on the foundation system. Such term includes without limitation, supporting, blocking, leveling, securing, or anchoring such home and connecting multiple or expandable sections of such home. Includes the connection to permanent utilities and placement of skirting and landing decks required by this article. The term "set-up" may be used interchangeably with the term "installation."
Installer means a person, agency, or entity responsible for performing an installation and who is required to be licensed pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-164 and 43-14-13(k).
(1)
Registered installer means an installer who has qualified, by completion of minimum educational requirements, to perform all aspects of installation, except the connection of the electrical service, sewer outlets and water system from the home to existing service supply.
(2)
Licensed installer means an installer who has qualified, by completion of educational requirements above and beyond those of a registered installer to perform all aspects of an installation, including the connection of the electrical service, sewer outlets and water systems.
Location permit means the term used by the Montgomery County Tax Commissioners Office and State of Georgia that means the "tax decal" or "tax sticker" issued upon payment of required taxes.
Manufactured home means a dwelling unit fabricated in an off-site facility for installation or assembly at the building site, bearing a label certifying it is constructed in compliance with the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, and amended, 42 U.S.C. 5401 et seq. (The HUD Code: Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards; Title 24 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), Part 3280 which became effective on June 15, 1976).
Manufactured home community means a lot or series of lots used as a residential area occupied by manufactured homes; conforming to herein, with appropriate and adequate-community services (for example: adequate garbage disposal; recreation facilities; utilities; streets and sidewalks provided by the developer) where the resident owns or rents the manufactured home and rents or owns the manufactured home's space.
Manufactured home mover registration form is the form devised and used by the building official, completed by the applicant, and that contains detailed information required to formally register for records purposes a person authorized to move a manufactured home into or out of Montgomery County.
Manufactured home park. SeeManufactured home community.
National manufactured housing construction and safety standards means the national building code for all manufactured homes built since June 15, 1976, written and administered by the LJS Department of Housing and Urban Development; also known as the "HUD Code."
Public sewer means the sanitary sewer service provided by the County or a municipality within the County.
Public water system (PWS) means a publicly owned water system for the provision of piped water to the public for human consumption that meets the bacteriological and chemical requirements of the Federal Environmental Protection Agency's "Safe Drinking Water Act" and is permitted to operate under the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, Environmental Protection Division.
Recreational vehicle means a self-propelled or towed vehicle designed as a temporary dwelling for travel or recreational uses.
Site permit means the form or permit issued by the County Tax Assessor's Office that authorizes the moving of the mobile home into or out of the County.
Travel trailer. See also Recreational vehicle.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 2; Ord. of 9-13-2010, § 1)
Sec. 16-88. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to set forth requirements from the date of its approval for placement of manufactured homes or recreational vehicles in the County. These requirements include, among other things, the acquisition of a building permit, tie-downs, connection to electrical, water, and waste water systems, and annual manufactured home tax decal.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 3)
Sec. 16-89. - Registration.
Every person, firm, corporation, or other entity holding title to or possessing a manufactured home which is placed or located within limits of the County, shall report the location of the manufactured home to and obtain a tax decal for the manufactured home from the Tax Commissioner, annually, no later than May 1 of each year.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 4)
Sec. 16-90. - Authority.
(a)
The Tax Commissioner is hereby authorized and empowered subject to the specific provisions of this article, to prescribe application, and site decal forms. The Tax Commissioner is empowered to require that applicant furnish such information in writing as may be reasonable required to effectuate the purpose hereof.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the Code Enforcement Officer or other appropriate authority of the County as designated by the County Board of Commissioners to enforce the provisions of this article, in conjunction with the Environmental Conservation, On-Site Sewage Management, and Permit article, as adopted by the County Board of Commissioners on August 7, 2001.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 5)
Sec. 16-91. - Building permit required.
(a)
It is a violation of this article for any mover, hauler, person, or other entity to move, or to have moved, a manufactured home into the County whose designation is within Montgomery County or to move a manufactured home out of the County whose location has been Montgomery County without first obtaining a site permit from the Tax Commissioner of the County authorizing such relocation.
(b)
The site permit authorizing the relocation of manufactured homes, shall be affixed to the manufactured home at all times such manufactured home is being transported within the confines of this County.
(c)
It shall be the responsibility of the Tax Commissioner to obtain proof that all State and County taxes theretofore accruing and payable with respect to such manufactured home have in fact been paid.
(d)
No site permit will be issued until an on-site sewage permit for a new installation or an "Existing On-Site Sewage Management System Performance Evaluation Report Form" for connection to an existing system is obtained from the Health Department.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 6)
Sec. 16-92. - Tax decal required.
Any owner of a manufactured home who has made application for the location of a manufactured home and whose application meets the requirements of this article, shall have or shall obtain a tax decal, upon payment of required charge for the same.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 7)
Sec. 16-93. - Administrative procedures.
(a)
HUD Code or DCA approved manufactured homes, as defined in section 16-87, will be permitted for location in Montgomery County. Pre-HUD manufactured homes located within the County prior to the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived may be relocated inside the County, subject, however, to other requirements for moving manufactured homes as provided herein, including site permit, on-site sewage permit, and all installation and final inspections as required herein. Mobile homes which met the Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards; Title 24 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), Part 3280 at the time of manufacture and have been subsequently remodeled, altered or refurbished must still meet all of the requirements of the HUD Regulations prior to entry into Montgomery County.
(b)
All manufactured homes must be located on an approved site which conforms to the standards outlined in this article and be no less than usable acreage that is acceptable by the Montgomery County Health Department for proper sewage.
(c)
Installation must comply with section 16-94 in order to obtain Building Official approval for occupancy. Such approval is necessary to obtain permanent electrical service. Manufactured homes will not be authorized to receive permanent electrical service until the provider of electrical service has been notified for approval for occupancy by the Building Official.
(d)
The above requirements do not apply to recreational vehicles. Recreational vehicles shall not be approved for permanent installation or permanent occupancy and may only be used for a maximum of an 18-month period as a temporary residence. All recreation vehicles must display a current State license and registration. Building permits will not be issued for recreational vehicles.
(e)
All manufactured homes entering the County must be in a condition which renders them immediately habitable upon proper installation in accordance with this article.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 8; Ord. of 9-13-2010, §§ 2, 3)
Sec. 16-94. - Manufactured home installation requirements.
(a)
Utility connections. All manufactured homes must be permanently connected to water, sewage, and electrical service.
(1)
Water. Water service may be public or private. All water service must be freeze proof at elevations above and below the ground.
(2)
Sewer. Installation of and/or connection to a new or existing on-site sewage systems must be permitted and/or final inspection completed by the Montgomery County Health Department.
(3)
Electrical. Electrical service must meet minimum requirements for safety.
(b)
Foundation. Unless placed on a permanent foundation, all manufactured homes must be installed in accordance with the Installation of Manufactured and Mobile Homes Rules and regulations established and published by the Georgia Safety Fire Commissioner (O.C.G.A. § 8-2-16 et seq.), installed with permanent skirting, secured with tie-down devices and provided with steps and landings. The installation of all required foundation requirements must be inspected and approved by the Building Official.
(c)
Skirting. Installation of skirting shall be required. Installation shall be in accordance with the manufacturer's installation instructions. Acceptable materials include masonry, stone, fiberglass, simulated brick or stone, vinyl or other materials manufactured solely for the purpose of skirting.
(d)
Tie-downs. Each manufactured home shall have tie-downs or other devices securing the stability of the manufactured home as per installation of Manufactured Homes and Mobile Homes Rules and Regulations established and published by Georgia Safety Fire Commissioner (O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160 et seq.).
(e)
Steps and landings. Steps and landings are required for all homes at all exterior entrances/exits. A minimum landing size of four feet by six feet of pressure treated lumber or elevated concrete is required. Wood landings are to be built on site, with posts placed 24 inches into soil for stability. Landings should be installed at the same elevation, but shall be no less than six inches below the elevation of the entrance/exit. Loose, stacked steps are strictly prohibited. If the landing is over 30 inches from the ground, sturdy handrails and guardrails are required.
(f)
Recreational vehicles. Permanent connection of recreational vehicles to water, sewer, or electrical service is prohibited. Installation of recreational vehicles on piers and jacks permanently is also prohibited.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 9)
Sec. 16-95. - Location.
(a)
Permitted locations. Manufactured homes shall be allowed in all unincorporated areas within the County and shall be regulated uniformly for compatibility with other housing constructed on site, subject to requirements and limitations set forth in this article.
(b)
Towing devices. All towing devices, wheels, axles and bolt-on hitches must be removed.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 10)
Sec. 16-96. - Local registration requirement for installers.
(a)
Anyone who moves, performs the set-up, or installs manufactured homes within Montgomery County is required to be registered, for records purposes, with the Building Official.
(b)
Installers will re-register annually at the beginning of each new year. Individuals will be required, when registering, to present their State installer license, which is issued by the State of Georgia. Suspension or revocation of the license by the State Of Georgia will automatically void the installer registration with Montgomery County.
(c)
The Building Official will maintain the list of registered installers and keep them informed of any changes or interpretations that may affect their proper installation of a home.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 11)
Sec. 16-97. - Final Inspection forms required.
(a)
The building official will develop and make available all those minimally required forms, applications, checklist, and procedure guides necessary to assist the consumer and installer(s) in complying with and maintaining the requirements of this article.
(b)
A final inspection compliance document will be issued by the Building Official (and maintained on file therein), only upon satisfactory completion of the requirements of this article.
(c)
All manufactured housing installations must meet the manufacturer's specifications and/or the specifications of this article before permanent utilities (water, sewage and electrical) may be activate or utilized.
(d)
Permanent electrical service for the manufactured housing will not be activated, and may be disconnected by order of the Building Official until such time as the manufactured housing installation meets all of the safety requirements of this article and the final inspection form from the Building Official is completed, and is provided by the citizen to the area electrical service provided.
(e)
In the case of hardship, temporary utility connections shall be approved, for a period not to exceed 60 days, in writing by the Board of County Commissioners.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 12)
Sec. 16-98. - Variances.
Upon application for a variance and consideration of the same, and a determination that extraordinary hardship may result from strict compliance with the provisions of this article due to unusual topographic, health, public safety, or other conditions beyond the control of the applicant, the Board of County Commissioners meeting in session may vary the regulations so that substantial justice may be done and the public interest secured, provided, however, that such variation will not have the effect of nullifying the intent or purpose of this article. Any variance thus authorized is required to be entered in the minutes and the reason that justified the departure is to be set forth.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 13)
Sec. 16-99. - Fees.
(a)
The following fees shall apply to manufactured homes:
(1)
Location (site) permit fee: Assessed by the County Tax Commissioner in conjunction with "tax decal" or "tax sticker."
(2)
On-site sewage system fee: Assessed by Montgomery County Health Department upon application for sewage system inspection.
(3)
Safety inspection fee: Paid by the owner, to include one final inspection.
(4)
Final inspection fee: Paid by the owner.
(5)
Any re-inspection required due to non-compliance will be at the above rates, paid by the owner.
(6)
Inspection fees: Paid in advance.
(b)
The fees in this section shall be in amounts as established by the Board of Commissioners from time to time.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 16)
Sec. 16-100. - Violations.
Any violation of this article will result in a penalty of $500.00, and will be pursued through the Magistrate Court.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1), § 17)
Sec. 16-101. - Manufactured housing required inspections.
(a)
Safety inspection. All of the following must be completed before calling for the inspection:
(1)
The home must be completely set up and tied down. We will inspect the piers, anchors, and straps.
(2)
The home must be completely installed under the home IAW Manual for on-site sewage management. Four-inch schedule 40 pipe must be used from the edge of the building to the on-site sewage system.
(3)
The electrical service must be connected. We will inspect the service pole, the outside and inside electrical panel.
(4)
The water service must be connected.
(5)
The inspector will need access to the inside of the home. Please make arrangements for access with the inspector.
(6)
Do not underpin (skirt) the home until the safety inspection has been approved.
(b)
Final inspection. The following items must be completed before calling for this inspection:
(1)
The mobile home must be underpinned.
(2)
The landings and steps (porches or decks) must be completed and properly placed.
(3)
The owner's copy of the on-site sewage final inspection form must be presented to the Building Official.
(c)
Permit and inspection information. The permit is valid for six months. Each time an inspection is made, it renews the permit for an additional six months. If the permit goes over six months without an inspection, it will expire unless you call for an extension before it expires.
(Ord. of 8-2-2005(1))
