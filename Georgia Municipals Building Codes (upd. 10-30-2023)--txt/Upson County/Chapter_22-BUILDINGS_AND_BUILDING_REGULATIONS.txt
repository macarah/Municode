Sec. 22-31. - Interpretation of chapter.
This chapter shall be deemed an exercise of the police powers of the county for the preservation and protection of the public health, peace, safety and welfare, and all its provisions shall be liberally construed for that purpose. This chapter shall be construed together with the other provisions of this Code and other ordinances of the county relating to the construction and use of buildings and structures, and in the event of the conflicts with those provisions or ordinances, the provision deemed to achieve the greatest promotion of the public welfare shall be deemed controlling.
(Ord. No. 187, § 1, 12-12-2006)
Sec. 22-32. - Penalties for violations.
Any person convicted in the magistrate court of the violation of any of the terms of this chapter or the codes adopted hereby shall be punished as provided in section 1-11 of this Code. Each day that a violation continues shall constitute a separate offense.

Sec. 22-64. - Fees.
(a)
Schedule of building permit fees.
(1)
Residential building permit fees are based on construction cost of $90.00 per square foot for heated living area, $35.00 per square foot for garage, and $30.00 per square foot for unfinished basement, porch, terrace, and carport area. Total valuation fees as follows:
a.
Two thousand dollars and less: No fees, unless inspection required, in which case a $25.00 fee for each inspection shall be charged;
b.
Two thousand one dollars to $50,000.00: Twenty dollars for the first $1,000.00 plus $5.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof, to and including $50,000.00;
c.
Fifty thousand one dollars to $100,000.00: Two hundred sixty-four dollars for the first $50,000.00 plus $4.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof, to and including $99,000.00;
d.
One hundred thousand dollars to $500,000.00: Four hundred sixty-five dollars for the first $100,000.00 plus $3.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof, to and including $500,000.00;
e.
Five hundred thousand one dollars and up: One thousand six hundred sixty dollars for the first $500,000.00 plus $2.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof.
(2)
Commercial/industrial building permit fees are based on construction cost of $85.00 per square foot. Total valuation fees as follows:
a.
Five hundred dollars to $3,000.00: Fifty dollars;
b.
Three thousand one dollars $50,000.00: Fifty dollars for the first $3,000.00 plus $6.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof, to and including $50,000.00;
c.
Fifty thousand one dollars to $100,000.00: Three hundred thirty-two dollars for the first $50,000.00 plus $5.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof, to and including $100,000.00;
d.
One hundred thousand one dollars to $500,000.00: Five hundred eighty-two dollars for the first $100,000.00 plus $4.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof, to and including $500,000.00;
e.
Five hundred thousand one dollars and up: Two thousand one hundred eighty-two dollars for the first $500,000.00 plus $3.00 for each additional thousand or fraction thereof.
(b)
Re-inspection fee for permitted jobs. After an inspection is requested, if the work is disapproved by the building inspector or if the building is not ready for inspection when the inspector arrives, a $35.00 re-inspection fee will be required to be paid prior to a re-inspection being made. If a second re-inspection is required, the fee will be $50.00 and the re-inspection fee will be required to be paid prior to the second re-inspection being made. If a third re-inspection is required, the fee will be $75.00 and the re-inspection fee will be required to be paid prior to the third re-inspection being made. Any further re-inspections after the third will be $100.00 each. All re-inspection fees must be paid before further inspections or new inspections are performed and before any certificate of occupancy is issued.
(c)
Miscellaneous construction permit/moving fee. For the moving of any structure, the fee shall be $100.00. These fees shall be paid by property owner not the tenant.
(d)
Demolition fee. Fee shall be one percent of the accessed value of the property being demolished with a minimum fee of $50.00.
(e)
Penalties. Where work for which a permit is required is started or proceeded prior to obtaining said permit, the fee herein specified shall be doubled. The payment of such double fee shall not relieve any person from fully complying with the requirements of applicable codes in the execution of the work, nor from any other penalties prescribed herein.
(f)
Plan-checking fee. When the valuation of construction exceeds $2,000.00, a plan is required to be submitted in compliance with chapter 105.3 of Standard Building Code (SBCCI), a plan-check fee shall be paid to the building official at the time of submitting the plan and specifications for checking. The amount of the plan-check fee shall be equal to one-half of the building permit fee as set forth herein. Such plan-check fee is in addition to the building permit fee.
(g)
Calculation of electrical permit fees:
(1)
Residential. Forty-five dollars for the first 200 amp service, plus $15.00 for each additional 200 amps or fraction thereof.
(2)
Commercial and industrial. Fifty-five dollars for the first 200 amp service, plus $25.00 for each additional 200 amps or fraction thereof.
(h)
Calculation of plumbing permit fees:
(1)
Residential. Forty-five dollars for the first 12 fixtures or traps plus $5.00 for each additional fixture or trap thereafter.
(2)
Commercial and industrial. Fifty-five dollars initial fee for issuing each permit, plus $5.00 for each fixture or trap.
(i)
Calculation of heating and air conditioning permit fees:
(1)
Residential. Forty-five dollars for the first 100,000 BTU and/or five-ton heat-pump, plus $20.00 for each additional 50,000 BTU and/or three-ton heat-pump or fraction thereof.
(2)
Commercial and industrial. Fifty-five dollars initial fee for issuing each permit, plus $5.00 for each additional $1,000.00 or fraction thereof, of valuation of the installation.
(j)
Contractor and subcontractor license fees. The contractor and subcontractor license fees will be established in accordance with the policies set forth in the county's occupation tax ordinance.
(k)
Exceptions to fee schedule. The fees specified herein shall apply to any person, firm, or corporation, except as follows:
(1)
The fees shall be waived for permits to repair or rebuild property damaged by storms, tornadoes, snow, hail, wind or other natural disasters. Such disaster must be verified by the building inspector. Applicable permits must be obtained, but at no charge.
(2)
All projects financed by federal, state, county and city funds are exempt from permit fees. Applicable permits must be obtained, but at no charge.
(l)
Blasting permits must be obtained in accordance with the requirements of Georgia Law and a copy of any permits obtained must be filed with the county building inspector.
(m)
Residential home sale and rental inspection fee. There shall be no charge for the initial inspection and the first follow-up inspection. There shall be a $50.00 fee for the second follow-up inspection. There shall be a $75.00 fee for the third follow-up inspection. There shall be a $100.00 fee for the fourth follow-up inspection and each additional inspection thereafter. All fees must be paid before the inspections are made. This inspection is good for two years and the property does not have to be re-inspected within said period even if the property is sold.
(Ord. No. 187, § 2, 12-12-2006; Ord. No. 199, §§ 1—4, 12-11-2007; Ord. No. 201, § 1, 12-13-2007)
Sec. 22-65. - Violations; presumption violator is owner.
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate the provisions of this chapter or the terms and standards adopted by reference thereby, to permit or maintain such violation, to refuse to obey any provision thereof, or to fail or refuse to comply with any provision or regulation except as variation may be allowed by action of the zoning board of appeals in writing. Proof of such unlawful act or failure to act or failure to comply shall be deemed prima facie evidence that such act or omission is the act of the owner of the property. Prosecution, or lack thereof, of either the owner or the occupant shall not be deemed to relieve the other.
(Ord. No. 187, § 2, 12-12-2006)
Sec. 22-66. - Enforcement; interpretations of rules; request for variance.
The building inspector shall have the responsibility for making interpretations of the rules adopted by this chapter, and for deciding upon the approval of equipment and materials, and for the granting of the special permission contemplated in a number of the rules. Any person aggrieved by the rulings of the building inspector with respect to this chapter may appeal as provided in sections 22-151—22-155. In conducting inspections of buildings, the building inspector shall apply the standards adopted by this chapter.
(Ord. No. 187, § 2, 12-12-2006)
Sec. 22-67. - Noncompliance; notice of violation; appeals; order to vacate.
In the event that the building inspector shall find deviations from or noncompliance with the terms and standards adopted by this chapter, he may issue a written notice of violation, which shall be posted on the premises, and copy of which shall be mailed to the last known address of the owner of the premises. An appeal from the notice of violation may be made in a request for variance to the zoning board of appeals within 30 days of the date of issuance. Notice of violation may, in the event the building inspector determines that there is a clear, present, immediate and dangerous hazardous condition, dangerous to life, include an order to vacate the premises, and noncompliance with such an order shall be a violation of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 187, § 2, 12-12-2006)

The permits required to be obtained by virtue of the provisions of any of the codes which have been adopted by, and incorporated by reference in section 22-61 shall, notwithstanding any provision contained in any of such codes thus adopted by reference, not be issued until the fees provided for in this division shall have been paid, which fees are hereby established for the following categories and set forth in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners:

Total cost of the proposed work.
(2)
Moving building or structure.
(3)
Demolition of building or structure.
(4)
Electrical wiring, additions or repair, in existing structures, of less than $2,001.00.
(5)
Installation of exterior signs.
(6)
Replacement or addition of heating and/or central air conditioning units.
(Ord. No. 63, § 13(A)—(F), 9-19-1991)
Sec. 22-122. - Plan checking fees.
When the valuation of the proposed construction exceeds $1,000.00 and a plan is required to be submitted by section 105.3 of the Standard Building Code adopted in section 22-61, a plan checking fee shall be paid to the building official at the time of submitting plans and specifications for checking. The plan checking fee shall be equal to one-half of the building permit fee as set forth in section 22-121. The fee paid under this section shall be credited to any fee that becomes due under section 22-121(1).
(Ord. No. 63, § 13(H), 9-19-1991)
Sec. 22-123. - Applicability.
The fees set out in section 22-121(1) shall apply to permits required to be obtained by virtue of the provisions of any of the standard published codes adopted by reference in section 22-61, but the costs of any portion of the work shall be counted only once in determining the amount of the required fee regardless of the fact that such work might otherwise be subject to a fee under more than one of such adopted codes. Notwithstanding any other section of this article or any provision of any of such standard codes which may provide to the contrary, the contractor with the ultimate responsibility for the work shall obtain the permit and pay the fee required under this division for the work, regardless of the fact that a subcontractor may actually perform the work.
(Ord. No. 63, § 13(I), 9-19-1991)
Sec. 22-124. - Failure to obtain permit.
Where work for which a permit as required by this article is started or proceeded with prior to obtaining the required permit, the fees specified shall, as an administrative penalty, be doubled, but the payment of such double fee shall not relieve any person from complying with the requirements of this article in the execution of the work nor from any other penalties prescribed in this article.
(Ord. No. 63, § 13(G), 9-19-1991)

Sec. 22-151. - Created; powers.
There is hereby created the Upson County Board of Adjustments and Appeals, referred to in this article as the "board of adjustments and appeals" to have and exercise the power to:
(1)
Hear and decide appeals from any order, requirement, decision, or determination made by the building official in the enforcement of article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter;
(2)
Hear and decide special exceptions to the terms of article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter upon which such board of adjustments and appeals under such regulations may be required to pass; and
(3)
Hear and decide specific variances.
(Ord. No. 56, § X(1), 9-21-1990)
Sec. 22-152. - Composition; terms.
(a)
The board of adjustments and appeals shall consist of five members approved by the county commission.
(b)
Each member shall serve for a term of three years and until his successor is duly appointed and qualified.
(Ord. No. 56, § X(2), 9-21-1990)
Sec. 22-153. - Rules of procedure.
(a)
The board of adjustments and appeals shall adopt rules for its governance and in harmony with article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter. Meetings of the board of adjustments and appeals shall be held at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the board may determine. The chairman or, in his absence, the acting chairman may administer oaths and compel the attendance of witnesses. All hearings of the board of adjustments and appeals shall be public. The board of adjustments and appeals shall keep minutes of its proceedings showing the vote of each member upon each question or, if absent or failing to vote, indicating such fact and shall keep records of its examinations and other official actions, all of which shall immediately be filed in the office of building official and on due cause shown.
(b)
The board of adjustments and appeals shall make written findings of facts and conclusions of law giving the facts upon which it acted and its legal conclusions from such facts in reversing, affirming, or modifying any order, requirement, decision, or determination which comes before it under article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter.
(c)
The concurring vote of a majority of the members of the board of adjustments and appeals shall be sufficient to reverse any order, requirement, decision, or determination of the building official or to decide in favor of the applicant on any matter upon which it is required to pass under article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter or to effect variation in article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 56, § X(3)—(5), 9-21-1990)
Sec. 22-154. - Appeals.
(a)
Any person aggrieved or any taxpayer affected by any decision of the building official made in his administration of article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter may appeal to the board of adjustments and appeals.
(b)
All such appeals must be taken within a reasonable time as provided by the rules of the board of adjustments and appeals by filing with the building official a notice of appeal specifying the grounds thereof. The building official shall forthwith transmit to the board of adjustments and appeals all the papers constituting the record upon which the action appealed from was taken.
(c)
An appeal shall stay all proceedings in furtherance of the action appealed from unless the building official certifies to the board of adjustments and appeals, after the notice of appeal has been filed with it, that because of the facts stated in the certificate a stay would, in his opinion, cause imminent peril to life or property. In such case, proceedings shall not be stayed except by order of the board of adjustments and appeals on notice to the building official and on due cause shown.
(d)
The board of adjustments and appeals shall fix a reasonable time for hearing appeals, shall give public notice and due notice to the parties in interest, and shall decide the appeal within a reasonable time. Upon the hearing, any party may appear in person or by agent or by attorney.
(e)
The board of adjustments and appeals may, in conformity with article II of chapter 18 and article II of this chapter, reverse or affirm, in whole or in part, or modify the order, requirement, decision, or determination appealed from and may make such order, requirement, decision, or determination as may be appropriate under the circumstances.
(Ord. No. 56, § XI, 9-21-1990)
Sec. 22-155. - Judicial review.
Every decision of the board of adjustments and appeals shall be final, subject however to such remedy as any aggrieved party might argue as a point of law to the Upson Superior Court.
(Ord. No. 56, § XII, 9-21-1990)

ARTICLE IV. - FLOW-RATE RESTRICTIONS ON PLUMBING FIXTURES[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Flow-rate restrictions on plumbing fixtures, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-3.

Sec. 22-181. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and includes the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable, or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling but does not include a hotel or motel.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-182. - Enforcement; penalty for violation.
(a)
This article shall be enforced by the county building official, and citations for violations shall be issued by the building official.
(b)
Any person violating this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11.
Sec. 22-183. - Residential construction.
On or after July 1, 1991, no construction may be initiated within the unincorporated area of the county for any residential building of any type which employs:
(1)
A gravity tank-type, flushometer-valve, or flushometer-tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.6 gallons of water per flush; provided, however, this subsection shall not be applicable to one-piece toilets until July 1, 1992;
(2)
A showerhead that allows a flow of more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds of pressure per square inch;
(3)
A urinal that uses more than an average of one gallon of water per flush;
(4)
A lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than two gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
A kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute.
Sec. 22-184. - Commercial construction.
On and after July 1, 1992, there shall be no construction initiated within the unincorporated area of the county for any commercial building of any type which does not meet the requirements of section 22-183.
Sec. 22-185. - Repairs, renovations and additions.
The requirements of section 22-183 shall apply to any residential construction initiated after July 1, 1991, and to any commercial construction initiated after July 1, 1992, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such building includes replacement of toilets or showers or both.
Sec. 22-186. - Exemptions.
(a)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of sections 22-183, 22-184 and 22-185 when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing building;
(2)
Such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation, would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this article were installed;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(b)
The owner or his agent of a building undergoing new construction or repair or renovation who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (a)(2), (3) or (4) of this section shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the county building official. A fee as provided in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
Sec. 22-187. - Limitations.
This article shall not apply to any construction of a residential building, the contract for which was entered into prior to July 1, 1991, and shall not apply to any construction of a commercial building, the contract for which was entered into prior to July 1, 1992.
Secs. 22-188—22-200. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - CARE OF PREMISES STANDARDS

Sec. 22-201. - Definitions.
As used in this article:
Person means any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company, or organization of any kind.
Property means any real property located within the territorial limits of Upson County, Georgia, but not within the municipal limits of either Thomaston or Yatesville, which does not comprise a street or highway.
(Ord. No. 175, § 1, 6-28-2005)
Sec. 22-202. - Violations.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of a building, structure or property in unincorporated Upson County to utilize the premises of such property for the open storage of any and all abandoned items and materials, including, but not limited to, ice boxes, refrigerators, stoves, other types of appliances, glass, building materials, building rubbish, weeds, tree limbs, dead trees, trash, garbage, debris and similar items.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of a building, structure, or property in unincorporated Upson County to keep the premises of such property overgrown with grass, weeds, vines, or other vegetation. This provision shall not apply to owners of properties that are completely vacant and devoid of active land uses other than agricultural uses.
(Ord. No. 175, § 2, 6-28-2005)
Sec. 22-203. - Penalties.
Any person who violates one or more of the provisions of section 22-202 of this article shall be punished in accordance with section 1-11 of the Code of Upson County, Georgia. Generally, this provides for the punishment by a sentence not to exceed 60 days imprisonment or a $1,000.00 fine, or both.
(Ord. No. 175, § 3, 6-28-2005)
Sec. 22-204. - Administration.
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement officer of Upson County to inspect, or cause to be inspected, property located in the county to determine whether or not there are any violations of section 22-202 of this article. Additionally, the zoning administrator and any building official of the county shall have the authority to inspect, or cause to be inspected, property for the presence of such violations. Upon discovery of any such violations, any of these named county officials shall promptly issue a citation to the offending person.
(Ord. No. 175, § 4, 6-28-2005)
