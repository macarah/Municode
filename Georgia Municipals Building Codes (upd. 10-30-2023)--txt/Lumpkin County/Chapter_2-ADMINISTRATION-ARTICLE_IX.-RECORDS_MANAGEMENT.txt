ARTICLE IX. - RECORDS MANAGEMENT

Sec. 2-786. - Department head duties; clerk as records management officer.
The county clerk is appointed as records management officer who shall be responsible for the administration of the provisions of this article.
(Res. No. 83-34, § 2, 10-5-1983)
Sec. 2-787. - Retention schedules; passwords and keys.
(a)
Each department head shall maintain all records associated with the operation of his department, and shall coordinate retention schedules with the county clerk. Department heads will review current records and records policies with the clerk at the clerk's direction, but not less frequently than annually.
(b)
Recommended destruction of department records shall be reviewed and approved by the clerk not less than 30 days prior to destruction.
(c)
Each department head shall maintain an indexed record of all material under his responsibility, including the location where such records are maintained, stored or otherwise held and the nature of the records, i.e., whether physical or electronic.
(d)
All electronic records shall be stored in a manner in which the county clerk may gain access when required.
(e)
The administrative passwords, safe combinations, and lock box and file cabinet duplicate keys with regard to each county department shall be stored in sealed envelopes by the county clerk.
(Res. No. 83-34, §§ 3, 4, 10-5-1983; Res. No. 2006-28, 5-18-2006)
Sec. 2-788. - Historical records.
The board of commissioners shall designate an archival depository to store records determined to have permanent historical value. No historical record of the county may be destroyed without prior review by and concurrence of the county clerk. The concurrence of the county clerk shall be presented to the board of commissioners, who may consult with regional libraries and historical societies prior to approving the destruction.
(Res. No. 83-34, § 6, 10-5-1983)
Sec. 2-789. - Alteration of records; violations.
No county record shall be altered or destroyed except in accordance with the provisions of this article. Any person altering or destroying a record except in accordance with the provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Res. No. 83-34, § 7, 10-5-1983)
