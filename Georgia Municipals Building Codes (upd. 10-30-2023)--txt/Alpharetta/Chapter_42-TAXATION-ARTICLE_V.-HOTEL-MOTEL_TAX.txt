Sec. 42-187. - Title.
This article shall be referred to and commonly known as the "Alpharetta Hotel/Motel Tax Ordinance." The authority to levy this tax is granted through O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(b)(2).
(Code 1989, § 8-21; Ord. No. 256, § 1, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-188. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Due date means the 20th day after the close of the monthly period for which tax is to be computed.
Guestroom means a room in any hotel (as defined in this section) which is occupied, or intended, arranged, or designed for occupancy, by one or more occupants as living quarters or as residential use.
Hotel means any building or structure kept, used or maintained as or advertised or held out to furnish for value to the public any room, lodging, or accommodations furnished by any person or legal entity licensed by or required to pay business or occupation taxes to the city for operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin, campground, or any other place in which rooms, lodging or accommodations are regularly furnished for value.
Monthly period means any calendar month of any year.
Occupancy means the use or possession of, or the right to use or possess, any guestroom, or the right to use or possess any of the furnishings, services or accommodations accompanying the use of possession of a guestroom.
Occupant means any person who, for a consideration, uses, possesses, or has the right to use or possess any guestroom under any lease, concession, permit, right of access, license to use or other agreement.
Operator/innkeeper means any person operating a hotel (as defined in this section) in the city, including, but not limited to, the hotel owner or proprietor of such premises, lessee, sublessee, lender in possession, licensee, or any other person otherwise operating such hotel.
Person means any natural person, receiver, administrator, executor, conservator, assignee, trust in perpetuity, trust, estate, firm, co-partnership, joint venture, club, company, business trust, domestic or foreign corporation, association, syndicate, society, or any group of individuals acting as a unit, whether mutual, cooperative, fraternal, nonprofit, or otherwise.
Rent orrental means the consideration or value received in money or otherwise for occupancy valued in money, whether received in money or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits, and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the operator to the occupant, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Return means any return filed or required to be filed as herein provided.
Tax means the tax imposed by this article, as provided by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(b)(2).
(Code 1989, § 8-22; Ord. No. 256, § 2, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004; Ord. No. 633, § 1, 6-22-2009)
Sec. 42-189. - Imposition and rate.
(a)
This excise tax shall be imposed on any person or legal entity licensed by or required to pay a business or occupation tax to the city for operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin, campground or any other place in which rooms, lodging, or accommodations are regularly furnished for value and shall apply to the furnishing for value of any room, lodging, or accommodation. Every person or entity subject to a tax levied as provided in this article shall be liable for the tax at the applicable rate on the lodging charges actually collected or, if the amount of taxes collected is in excess of the total amount that should have been collected, the total amount actually collected must be remitted.
(b)
Any tax levied as provided in this article is also imposed upon every person or entity who is a hotel guest and who receives a room, lodging, or accommodation that is subject to the tax levied under this article. Every such guest subject to the tax levied under this article shall pay the tax to the person or entity providing the room, lodging, or accommodation. The tax shall be of a debt of the person obtaining the room, lodging, or accommodation to the person or entity providing such room, lodging, or accommodation until it is paid, and shall be recoverable by law by the person or entity providing such room, lodging, or accommodation in the same manner as the recovery of other debts. The person or entity collecting the tax from the hotel guest shall remit the tax to the city in concurrence with the provisions of this article.
(c)
Commencing on October 1, 2015, there is hereby imposed and there shall be paid to the city an excise tax in the amount of eight percent of the gross rental charge for every occupancy of a guest room in a hotel in the city, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(b)(2).
(Code 1989, § 8-23; Ord. No. 256, § 3, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 397, § 1, 12-16-1997; Ord. No. 443, § 1, 6-6-2000; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004; Ord. No. 709, § 2, 8-17-2015)
Sec. 42-190. - Exemptions.
(a)
The tax authorized by this article shall not apply to charges made for any rooms, lodging, or accommodations provided to any persons who certify that they are staying in such rooms, lodging, or accommodations as a result of the destruction of their homes or residences by fire or other casualty.
(b)
The tax authorized by this article shall apply to the fees or charges for any rooms, lodging, or accommodations during the first 30 days of continuous occupancy, and shall not apply to charges imposed for any continuous occupancy thereafter.
(c)
The tax authorized by this article shall not apply to charges made for the use of meeting rooms and other such facilities or to any rooms, lodging, or accommodations provided without charge.
(d)
The tax authorized by this article shall not apply to the charges for any rooms, lodging, or accommodations furnished for a period of one or more days for use by state or local government officials or employees when traveling on official business. Notwithstanding the availability of any other means of identifying the person as a state or local government official or employee, whenever a person pays for any rooms, lodging, or accommodations with a state or local government credit or debit card, such rooms, lodging, or accommodations shall be deemed to have been furnished for use by a state or local government official or employee traveling on official business for purposes of the exemption provided by this section.
(Code 1989, § 8-24; Ord. No. 256, § 4, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004; Ord. No. 633, § 2, 6-22-2009)
Sec. 42-191. - Collection.
The tax levied herein shall be secured by the operator from the occupant when collecting the rent to which it applies.
(Code 1989, § 8-25; Ord. No. 256, § 5, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-192. - Registration of operator; form and content; execution; certificate of authority.
No person shall be granted a license as an operator in this city until such person meets all the requirements for operating a hotel within the city and registers with the finance director pursuant to this section. The registration set forth in this section shall require the name under which such person operates a hotel or intends to operate a hotel, the location of the hotel, and such other information which the finance director deems necessary in facilitating the collection of the tax. The registration shall be signed by the operator/owner, if a natural person, and, if the operator shall not be a natural person, then anyone authorized to sign on behalf of the operator. The finance director shall, after such registration, issue without charge a certificate of authority to the registered operator to collect the tax. A separate registration shall be required for each hotel of an operator. Each certificate shall state the name and location of the hotel to which it is applicable.
(Code 1989, § 8-26; Ord. No. 256, § 6, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-193. - Returns; due dates of taxes and payments; collection fee allowed operators.
(a)
Returns; time of filing; persons required to file; contents. On or before the 20th day of the month following each monthly period for which the tax is levied, a return for the preceding monthly period shall be filed with the finance director showing the gross rent, taxable rent, amount of tax collected or otherwise due for the related period, and such other information as may be required by the mayor and council or finance director.
(b)
Due date of taxes. The taxes required to be collected under this article shall be due and payable to the city monthly on or before the 20th day of every month following each respective monthly period for which the tax is levied.
(c)
Collection fee allowed operators. In reporting and paying the amount of tax due under this article, each operator shall be allowed a deduction of three percent of the amount due, but only if the amount due was not delinquent at the time of payment.
(Code 1989, § 8-27; Ord. No. 256, § 7, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-194. - Determination of deficiency; notice of determination; interest on deficiency.
(a)
Determination of deficiency. Should the finance director believe that any return filed pursuant to this article does not reflect accurately the amount of tax required to be paid to the city, the finance director may determine the amount which is believed to be the appropriate amount of tax due.
(b)
Notice of determination; service of notice. The finance director or his designee shall give written notice of the determination of deficiency. The notice may be served personally or by certified mail, return receipt requested. If notice is given by certified mail, it shall be addressed to the operator at his address as it appears in the records of the business license issued to the operator. Service by certified mail will be deemed complete on the date the return receipt is signed by the addressee. Should the addressee fail for any reason to accept delivery, notice shall be deemed given upon the expiration of five days after the notice is mailed by the finance director or his designee.
(c)
Collection of deficiency. Within 15 days after the operator receives a notice of determination of deficiency, the operator shall remit to the finance director the amount of deficiency owing, or provide the finance director with a satisfactory explanation in writing which sets forth the reasons such deficiency amount is not owing to the city.
(d)
Penalty. The amount of the deficiency shall bear a penalty of five percent or $5.00, whichever is greater, on the past due amount, from the original due date of the tax.
(e)
Interest. The amount of the deficiency shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the original due date of the tax.
(f)
Repeated failure to file accurate return. Upon an operator's filing two inaccurate returns within any 24-month period, the city shall have the right to revoke an operator's business license.
(Code 1989, § 8-28; Ord. No. 256, § 8, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-195. - Failure to make return; effect.
(a)
Failure to make return. If any operator fails to make any return required by this article, the finance director shall notify the operator in writing of such failure. The operator shall have 15 days from the date of the notice to remit the return, along with the tax owing. In addition to the tax owing, taxes shall bear interest at an annual rate equal to the bank prime loan rate as posted by the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System in statistical release H. 15 or any publication that may supersede it, plus three percent, to accrue monthly. Such annual interest rate shall be determined for each calendar year based on the first weekly posting of statistical release H. 15 on or after January 1 of each calendar year. Interest shall begin to accrue from the date the tax is due until the date the tax is paid. Should any operator fail to make a return after such 15-day period, the finance director or his designee shall review the operator's records and make a determination as to the tax owing for the monthly period for which the operator failed to make a return. After making a determination of the tax due, the finance director shall mail written notice of such determination to the operator. The operator shall have five days from the date of the notice to pay all sums owing, together with interest, from the 20th day of the month following the monthly period for which the determination is made. In addition, the operator shall pay to the city a penalty of five percent of the total amount of tax owing.
(b)
Repeated failure to file return. Upon an operator's failure to make two returns within any 24-month period, the city shall have the right to revoke an operator's business license.
(Code 1989, § 8-29; Ord. No. 256, § 9, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)

Sec. 42-196. - Administration of article.
(a)
Authority. The finance director shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the collection of the tax imposed by this article.
(b)
Records required from operators, etc.; form. Each operator required to make a return and pay any tax under this article shall keep and preserve:
(1)
Suitable records of the charges taxable under this article; and
(2)
Other books of account which are necessary to determine the amount of tax due.
(c)
Examination of records; audits. The finance director or his designee may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment and other facilities of any operator in the city in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or, if no return is made by the operator, to ascertain and determine the amount of tax owing to the city.
(d)
Authority to require reports; contents. In the administration of the provisions of this article, the finance director may require the filing of additional reports by any persons or class of persons having custody of information relating to the rental of guestrooms in the city. The reports shall be filed when required by the finance director and shall set forth such information as the finance director may require.
(Code 1989, § 8-30; Ord. No. 256, § 10, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-197. - Successors and assigns of operator; liability for tax.
If any hotel or operator liable for any tax, interest, or penalty imposed by this article sells his business or quits the business, a final return with payment shall be made within 15 days after the date of selling or quitting the business. The operator's successor or assigns, if any, shall be liable for any unpaid tax, interest, or penalties accrued under this article and unpaid by any former owner or assignor.
(Code 1989, § 8-31; Ord. No. 256, § 11, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-198. - Overpayment of tax; tax credit.
In the event an operator remits to the finance director any sum greater than the amount of tax owing, and such fact is brought to the attention of the finance director, the finance director shall credit the amount of overpayment to the operator's account and such credit shall be applied to the amount owing for a successive monthly period.
(Code 1989, § 8-32; Ord. No. 256, § 12, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-199. - Appropriation of sums collected.
All sums collected under this article shall be expended for any purpose authorized by law.
(Code 1989, § 8-33; Ord. No. 256, § 14, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 397, § 2, 12-16-1997; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
Sec. 42-200. - Penalties for violation.
Every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this article shall be punished according to the penalties described in section 1-8 for each offense; and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues. In addition, the city shall be entitled to bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction to collect any amount of delinquent tax, together with interest, court costs, and reasonable attorney's fees, and to recover any other damages which the city may suffer. The imposition or payment of a fine under this section shall not relieve the operator from the obligation to pay any and all taxes due under this article, including applicable interest and penalties.
(Code 1989, § 8-34; Ord. No. 256, § 13, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002; Ord. No. 538, 2-23-2004)
