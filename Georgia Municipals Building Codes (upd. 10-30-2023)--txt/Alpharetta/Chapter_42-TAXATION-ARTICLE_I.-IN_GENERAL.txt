ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - Liability insurance on city vehicles.
(a)
The director of finance is hereby authorized and directed to maintain automobile liability in limits not less than $100,000.00 bodily injury per person, $300,000.00 bodily injury per occurrence and $100,000.00 property damage.
(b)
All contractors doing business with the city shall provide certificates of the coverage described in subsection (a) of this section to the city, and the city shall maintain files on all owned and contracted vehicles.
(Code 1989, § 8-1; Ord. No. 159, § 1, 11-6-1978)
Sec. 42-2. - Tax exemption to residents 65 years of age or older.
(a)
All residents of the city who own and occupy their homes on January 1 of the year and who have attained the age of 65 years or older on or before such date, and do not have an income exceeding $10,000.00, which includes all moneys received by such person or such person's spouse, are hereby granted a $5,000.00 homestead exemption.
(b)
If disabled, a certificate provided by the attending physician licensed to practice medicine in the state certifying that the applicant is mentally or physically incapacitated to the extent that such person is unable to be gainfully employed, and that his condition is likely to be permanent, must be submitted to the tax commissioner. An affidavit must be signed by the applicant.
(Code 1989, § 8-3; Res. of 3-28-1983)
Sec. 42-3. - Freeport tax exemption.
(a)
Inventory of goods and the process of manufacture or production which shall include all partially finished goods and raw materials held for direct use or consumption in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business in the state is exempt from ad valorem taxation by the city, as provided in article VII, section 1, paragraph IV of the state constitution.
(b)
Inventory of finished goods manufactured or produced within the state in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business when held by the original manufacturer or producer of such finished goods is exempt from ad valorem taxation by the city, as provided by article VII, section 1, paragraph IV of the state constitution.
(c)
Inventory of finished goods which on the first day of January are stored in a warehouse, dock or wharf, whether public or private, and which are destined for shipment to a final destination outside the state, and inventory of finished goods which are shipped into the state from outside this state and stored for trans-shipment to a final destination outside this state is exempt from ad valorem taxation by the city, as provided in article VII, section 1, paragraph IV of the state constitution.
(Code 1989, § 8-4; Res. No. 148, 3-28-1983)
State Law reference— Ga. Const. art. VII, § II, ¶ III; freeport exemption, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2.
Sec. 42-4. - Fees for checks returned for nonpayment.
(a)
Effective with the passage of this section, a returned check fee of $25.00 shall be imposed on all checks returned to the city for nonpayment.
(b)
This fee may be adjusted, from time to time, by the finance director, provided that with such adjustments:
(1)
The city council is notified;
(2)
A public notice is posted announcing the new rate and effective date; and
(3)
The adjustment shall not be more than $20.00 or five percent of the face amount of the returned check, whichever is greater.
(Code 1989, § 8-5; Res. No. 378, 12-17-1990; Ord. No. 507, 9-23-2002)
