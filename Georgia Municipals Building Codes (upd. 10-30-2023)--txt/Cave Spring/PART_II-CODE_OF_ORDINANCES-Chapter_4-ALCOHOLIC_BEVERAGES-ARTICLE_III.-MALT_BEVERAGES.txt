ARTICLE III. - MALT BEVERAGES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-55. - Definitions.
Unless the context or the specific provisions of this section demand otherwise, the words and terms used in this article shall have the same definition and meaning as state law where used in O.C.G.A. title 3, chapter 5 (O.C.G.A. § 3-5-1 et seq.). The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Brewery means a manufacturer of malt beverages.
Brewpub means the definition currently stated in O.C.G.A., or as it is hereafter amended by the state legislature. As currently defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, the term "brewpub" means any eating establishment in which malt beverages are manufactured, subject to the barrel production limitation prescribed in O.C.G.A. § 3-5-36. As used in this definition, the term "eating establishment" means an establishment which is licensed to sell distilled spirits, malt beverages, or wines and which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food; provided, however, that when determining the total annual gross food and beverage sales, barrels of malt beverages sold to licensed wholesale dealers, as authorized pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-36(2)(D), or to the public for consumption off the premises, as authorized pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-36(2)(D) and (4), shall not be used.
Business location means the location or proposed location of a retail or wholesale malt beverage outlet.
Church means a freestanding building which is owned by a religious organization or association and primarily utilized for religious services, ceremonies, or instruction.
Malt beverage tap room means a place on the premises of a brewery, where the malt beverages manufactured at the brewery are offered for sale for consumption on the premises by individuals who are on the premises.
Measured means measured by way of the nearest travelled road, street, or highway from the front entrance of an outlet to the nearest travelled road, street, or highway, to the front entrance of an involved institution.
Outlet means a store for the sale of malt beverages.
Outlet manager means the person who does or will actually manage or operate the malt beverage store on a day-to-day basis.
Private residence means a dwelling for one or more persons or families.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-56))
Sec. 4-56. - Package sales generally.
(a)
The building or proposed building to house a retail outlet for the sale of malt beverages by the package shall meet all requirements of the building inspector, and plans, specifications, and/or photographs for the building or proposed building must be approved by city council.
(b)
Each outlet for the retail sale of malt beverages by the package shall post in a conspicuous place within the outlet a sign in letters at least two inches high, reading as follows: "Sale of malt beverages to any person under 21 years of age strictly prohibited."
(c)
No screen, partition or thing which prevents a clear view into the interior of a retail outlet for the sale of malt beverages by the package from the street, nor any booth within, shall be permitted.
(d)
No sale of malt beverage by the package shall be allowed except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 1:30 a.m. of the following day on weekdays, except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on Saturday, and except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:00 p.m. on Sunday, nor on other days prohibited by state law.
(e)
The state regulations relating to the sale and distribution of malt beverages by the package, as revised, promulgated by the state revenue department, are hereby incorporated into and made a part of this article as if fully set out in this section.
(f)
Any holder of a license to sell at retail malt beverages by the package shall make an immediate report to, and receive approval by, the city council of any change in the interests in or ownership of a malt beverage outlet and/or any change in the information stated in the original application for license.
(g)
In the case of the sale of an existing licensed business, provided that the report of the chief of police is satisfactory to the city council and the chief of police, the purchaser of such business may continue operation of such business without change and/or enlargement, pending action by the city council on the transfer of, or on a new, license. In such case, the purchaser or transferee shall, during or immediately after such purchase or transfer, make application for approval.
(h)
No drive-in windows for the sale of malt beverages shall be allowed.
(i)
The open parking area of any building or proposed building to house a retail outlet shall have an average maintained foot-candle intensity of at least one foot-candle with a minimum allowable intensity of three-tenths of a foot-candle. The covered parking area of any building or proposed building to house a retail outlet shall have an average maintained foot-candle intensity of at least two foot-candles with a minimum allowable intensity of five-tenths of a foot-candle.
(j)
Each outlet for the retail sale of malt beverages by the package shall install and maintain security cameras in the outlet of a type and number approved by the chief of police. Such cameras:
(1)
Shall be placed in the outlet to record activities in the check out or cash register area.
(2)
Shall be capable of producing a retrievable image on film or tape that can be made a permanent record and enlarged through projection or other means.
Such cameras shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to periodic inspection by the chief of police or his designee. If a robbery or other felony occurs in such outlet, the film or tape recording such event shall immediately be made available to the chief of police or his designee. All films, tapes, or images required by this section shall be retained by the outlet, in its original, viewable format, for a period of no less than 30 days after recording.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-57))
Secs. 4-57—4-85. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 4-86. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell or offer to sell any malt beverage at wholesale or retail within the corporate limits of the city without having the appropriate license for such manufacture or sale, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or of this article.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-71))
State Law reference— Local malt beverage license, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-40 et seq.
Sec. 4-87. - Separate license for each malt beverage outlet; transfer; surrender.
(a)
A separate license shall be required for each malt beverage outlet, and a separate application shall be made for each outlet.
(b)
Licenses under this article shall be transferable only upon prior approval by the city clerk and the police chief and upon payment of a transfer fee as provided for in the schedule of fees kept and maintained by the city clerk.
(c)
Immediately upon the sale or closing of an outlet, it shall be the duty of the licensee to surrender his license to the city clerk.
(d)
Any licensee who is an employee of a corporation, and who is separated from his employment, whether he resigns, retires or is fired, shall immediately notify the office of the city clerk of such separation. Should the city clerk's office not be open at such time, the police department shall be notified instead with notice given to the city clerk as soon as possible on the next day of normal business. If such employee removes the malt beverage license from the establishment, he shall immediately surrender such license to the city clerk or to the police department. Failure to surrender such license, if removed from the place of business, shall be a violation and shall be punished as provided by section 6.20 of the city Charter.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-72))
Sec. 4-88. - Classification of licenses; fees.
(a)
Type of licenses. Licenses under this article shall be classified as follows:
(1)
Retail package. Retail beer package store, which shall permit only the sale of malt beverages in packages at retail.
(2)
Wholesale. Wholesale malt beverage distributor, which shall permit only the sale of malt beverages at wholesale.
(3)
Manufacture. Manufacturers of malt beverages, which shall permit only the manufacture, bottling and packaging of malt beverages.
(b)
Fee established. The amount of the license fee for each class shall be as set forth in the fee schedule maintained by the city clerk or by state law.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-73))
Sec. 4-89. - Applications for malt beverage, wholesale malt beverage distributor, and brewpub licenses.
Applications for retail malt beverage licenses, applications for wholesale malt beverage distributor licenses, and applications for brewpub licenses shall be as follows:
(1)
Forms; submission. Each initial applicant shall make written application to the city council for the privilege of engaging in the sale (specifying wholesale or retail) of malt beverages, such applicant using forms provided by the city clerk.
(2)
Where filed; fee. The application shall be filed with the city clerk with a fee as set forth in the fee schedule maintained by the city clerk or by state law, for investigation of the applicant and of the proposed location and processing of the application.
(3)
Contents. The application shall state:
a.
As to the applicant: full name, birth date, present address and addresses for the past five years, business address and business addresses or employers and their addresses for the last five years, name of spouse and all minor children, complete record of all arrests, convictions and guilty pleas for violations of all laws, city, state and federal, of both applicant and spouse, and the disposition of all charges.
b.
As to the applicant's outlet manager (if not the same as the applicant), the same information as required of the applicant in this subsection.
c.
Exact location (street address and otherwise) of proposed business.
d.
Name and address of owner or landlord and/or resident manager of property proposed for location and whether or not there is a building proposed to be used already erected thereon.
e.
Trade name, or proposed trade name, of proposed business.
f.
Names and addresses of all persons having any financial interest in the outlet by way of ownership of building or stock, receipt of income from the business or otherwise.
g.
Such other information as may be required by the city council, the chief of police, the city manager, the city attorney or the city clerk, through interrogatories or otherwise.
(4)
Approvals. The application shall contain evidence of the following persons' approval of the licensee and of the owner and the manager of the malt beverage establishment:
a.
Building inspector (i.e., building permit, certificate of occupancy, or other evidence).
b.
Chief of police.
(5)
Oath. The application shall be sworn to.
(6)
State application. There shall be attached to the city application a copy of the applicant's application or proposed application for license from the state.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-74))
Sec. 4-90. - Advertisement; proof thereof.
(a)
Each applicant under this division shall advertise his application for license in a local daily newspaper once a week for two weeks. The advertisement shall appear after filing application and before the public hearing provided for in this division. The advertisement shall contain the name of the applicant, the purpose of the application, the proposed location of the business and its owners and the name of the proposed outlet manager, if known.
(b)
The applicant, before consideration will be given to his application, shall file with the city clerk a copy of the advertisement together with affidavits to the effect that it has been printed as required.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-75))
Sec. 4-91. - Action by city council.
(a)
After the time that proof of advertisement is filed, the city clerk shall notify the chief of police, who shall investigate the character of the applicant and the reputation of the location and make a full report to the city council. The city clerk shall also circulate the application to the building inspector for his investigation and recommendation or approval.
(b)
The city clerk, when assured that all requirements have been complied with, shall refer the application, together with any objections filed, to the city council. The city council shall, after investigation of the applicant and the proposed location, direct the city clerk to issue or refuse the license for which application is made, upon payment of the appropriate fee.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-76))
Sec. 4-92. - Considerations and guidelines for grant or denial.
The city council, in making its determination on an initial application, shall be guided by the following factors as to whether or not to grant a license under this division:
(1)
The nature of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location, that is, whether the same is predominantly residential, industrial or business.
(2)
The proximity of churches, hospitals, schools, college campuses, public libraries, public parks and playgrounds, private residences and alcoholic treatment centers owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein.
(3)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking facilities or other parking available for its patrons.
(4)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom.
(5)
The criminal record of the applicant and the outlet manager and general good character and reputation and their financial responsibility; provided that nonpayment of federal, state, county or city taxes shall be prima facie evidence of lack of financial responsibility.
(6)
A report of the chief of police and the building inspector.
(7)
The information required in the application or statement and whether all requirements as to notice, advertisement, fire codes, building, zoning, parking, lighting and legal matters have been met.
(8)
Evidence presented to the council for or against the application.
(9)
Whether or not the granting of the application is in the interest of the city.
(10)
Whether any license for sale of beer or wine or alcohol previously issued for the location has been revoked for cause by the city council.
(11)
Whether the applicant holds or possesses any other license for the sale of alcohol issued by the city.
(12)
The history or reputation of the building or establishment (proposed for outlet) for prostitution or other sex offenses; fighting, shooting, stabbing or other violence; gambling; illegal dealing in alcoholic beverages or drugs; and other violations of the law.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-77))
Sec. 4-93. - Grounds for denial.
(a)
Prohibited locations. It shall be unlawful for any person representing the city to grant a license for the sale of malt beverages within the following areas of the city:
(1)
Within a measured 100 yards of any church.
(2)
Within a measured 100 yards of any alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein.
(3)
Within a measured 100 yards of any school ground or college campus.
(4)
In any existing building or establishment having a history of, or reputation for, prostitution or other sex offenses; fighting, shooting, stabbing, or other violence; gambling; illegal dealings in alcoholic beverages or drugs; or other violations of the law.
(b)
Prohibited persons. It shall be unlawful for any person representing the city to grant a license for the sale of malt beverages to:
(1)
A person who is not of good character and reputation in the community in which he resides.
(2)
A person who has been convicted of a felony, or of a crime opposed to decency and morality, or who has been convicted of a crime involving violation of the ordinances of the city relating to the use, sale, taxability or possession of malt beverages, wine or liquor, or violations of the laws of the state and federal government pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation or sale of malt beverages, wine or intoxicating liquors, or the taxability thereof.
(3)
A person whose license under this chapter has been revoked for cause or who has had a license under this chapter revoked for cause.
(4)
A person who is under the age of 21 years or who is not of sound mind and memory.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-78))
Sec. 4-94. - Becoming a prohibited person.
Any licensee under this division who becomes a prohibited person, as defined in section 4-93(b), shall, within three days of the event, make the fact known to the city council and shall surrender his license upon demand by the city council in such case.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-79))
Sec. 4-95. - Renewal; denial of renewal.
(a)
All licenses under this division shall be issued on a calendar-year basis and shall be renewable as a matter of course upon payment of the appropriate fee, except as is set out in subsection (d) of this section.
(b)
Each licensee shall make a written application for renewal on or before November 15 of each year on forms approved by the city clerk, and the license fee shall be paid in full no later than December 31 of each year.
(c)
The application shall be referred to the chief of police, who shall report on the licensee's activity, if any, and upon activity at the location of the licensed business, if any, during the year.
(d)
If the chief reports any activity which constitutes probable cause for not renewing a license, the renewal shall not be made, and the matter shall be referred to the city council for its consideration.
(e)
The city council shall afford the licensee a public hearing, and in passing upon the application for renewal, it shall be guided by the factors applicable to an initial application set forth in section 4-92.
(f)
At the public hearing, the city council may grant, or deny for cause, the renewal of any license issued under this division.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-80))
Sec. 4-96. - Suspension, revocation, and monetary sanction—Generally.
(a)
Summary action. There shall be no summary seizure of licenses under this division, unless there exists in the city a state of emergency duly declared; after any such seizure, a hearing shall be held as soon as practicable.
(b)
Action by city council. The city council shall have full power and authority to suspend, refuse to renew, place on probation, or revoke a license to sell malt beverages, and to impose a monetary sanction not to exceed the amount set forth in the fee schedule kept and maintained by the city clerk, under the conditions set forth in this section. Before taking any such action except suspending a license for failure to pay an imposed monetary sanction when due, the city council shall conduct a hearing, at least five days' notice of which hearing shall have been given to the affected licensee. Sufficient grounds for the suspension, refusal to renew, probation, revocation of a license, or imposition of a monetary sanction include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
A licensee has failed to open a business within six months after approval of the license.
(2)
A licensee has ceased to operate the business for a period of three consecutive months.
(3)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has sold alcoholic beverages during a period of suspension.
(4)
A licensee gave false or misleading information in the original application or renewal process.
(5)
A licensee has failed to pay any fee, license fee, or other amount of money due to the city under this chapter or any other city licensing ordinance.
(6)
A licensee has failed to maintain any and all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a license as set forth in this division.
(7)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person that the licensee or the licensee's employee or agent, knew or should have known to be in a state of intoxication.
(8)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person under the age of legal consumption in the state.
(9)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other law, ordinance, or regulation governing the operation of an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages or which is reasonably related to the operation of such establishment.
(10)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other section of this chapter.
(11)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has been convicted or pled guilty or no contest to any violation of its license to sell or distribute alcoholic beverages issued by the state department of revenue, the city, the county and/or the county sheriff's office.
(12)
A licensee has failed to satisfy the criteria for obtaining a license to sell malt beverages.
(13)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any federal, state, or local laws or ordinances relating to the use and sale of drugs and alcoholic beverages.
(14)
When the continued operation of the licensed business is detrimental to the health, safety, or welfare of the public.
(c)
Suspension. The failure of a licensee to pay when due any monetary sanction assessed pursuant to the terms of this chapter shall result in the immediate suspension of all alcohol licenses issued by the city to such licensee. A suspension under this subsection shall continue until all monetary sanctions and other sums due the city by the licensee are paid in full.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-81))
Sec. 4-97. - Suspension, revocation, and monetary sanction—Changes of circumstances.
The approval of an application by the city council, and/or the granting of a license by the council, may be suspended or revoked, after notice and hearing, if any change in the information supplied, or in the physical layout represented in the plat submitted, occurs without the approval of the city council. An applicant anticipating any such change shall submit all details thereof to the city council.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-82))
