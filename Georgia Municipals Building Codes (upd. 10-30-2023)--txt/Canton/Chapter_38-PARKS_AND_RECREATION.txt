ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 38-1—38-17. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PARK REGULATIONS

Sec. 38-18. - Penalty and enforcement provisions.
A violation of this article shall be a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punishable in accordance with section 1-12, general penalty, as may be amended from time to time.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-24, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-19. - Enforcement officials.
The public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee, or department personnel thereunder, shall:
(1)
Establish, conduct and maintain a recreation system for the city in such a way as to employ the leisure of the people in a wholesome and constructive manner;
(2)
Provide for, conduct and supervise public playgrounds, indoor recreation centers, and other facilities owned or controlled by the city;
(3)
Provide and conduct activities on properties under the control of the city, on public properties with the consent of the authorities thereof, and on private properties with the consent of the owners;
(4)
Establish goals, procedures and programs to meet the city's recreational needs; these are on file at city hall.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-1, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-20. - Governing conduct.
(a)
These rules and regulations are developed to set standards of conduct in the parks owned and operated by the city. These rules are designed to ensure the safety and enjoyment of park participants.
(b)
Any person engaging in any activity which shall unreasonably interfere with the use and enjoyment of a park by citizens or who shall violate any ordinance of the city, park rules or regulations or law of the state shall leave the park upon notification by any authorized city employee or any law enforcement officer and shall not return to such park for a period of 24 hours. Refusal to leave such park when ordered or return to the park within the specified 24-hour period shall be unlawful and punishable as a misdemeanor.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to:
(1)
Create or maintain a nuisance as defined by state law or by this Code or pose a danger or threat to the health of another.
(2)
Willfully mark, deface, disfigure, injure, tamper with, displace or remove, any building, bridge, table, bench, fireplace, railing, paving, paving material, water line or other public utility or parts or appurtenance thereof, sign, notice or placard whether temporary or permanent, monument, stake, post, or other boundary marker, or other structure or equipment, facility or park property or appurtenance whatsoever, either real or personal.
(3)
Sell, possess or consume, by any person alcoholic beverages in any city park, except at a special event, as approved by mayor and city council.
(4)
Smoke outside of designated smoking areas pursuant to section 32-22.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-2, 4-2-2009; Ord. No. 2012-08, § 1, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 38-21. - Hours of operation.
No person shall be in a city park or upon any park land or park drive therein between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m., unless there is an organized sporting, civic or cultural event which has the approval of the mayor and city council to extend beyond the designated hours of operation. No other person other than city personnel conducting city business shall occupy or be present in such parks during these hours.
(Code 2002, § 38-31; Ord. of 4-1-1999, § 7-2-1(a); Ord. of 2-20-2003; Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-3, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-22. - Closing of parks.
All parks shall be opened daily to the public, but may be declared closed to the public by the mayor and city council at any time and for any interval of time, either temporarily or at regular or stated intervals. In the event of an emergency or conditions not suitable for the public, the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee with the approval of the city manager may temporarily close parks.
(Code 2002, § 38-32; Ord. of 4-1-1999, § 7-2-1(b); Ord. of 2-20-2003; Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-4, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-23. - Overnight sleeping or camping prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sleep in a city park after closing hours or to otherwise set up tents, shacks or any other temporary shelter for the purpose of overnight sleeping or camping. No person shall leave in a park after closing hours any movable structure or special vehicle to be used or that could be used for that purpose. Any person found to be violating this section shall be ordered and/or escorted off the park grounds by the city police.
(Code 2002, § 38-33; Ord. of 4-1-1999, § 7-2-1(c); Ord. of 2-20-2003)
Sec. 38-24. - Recreational vehicle operation restricted.
No person shall operate a motorized vehicle of any kind or nature in or on any park or other area owned or operated by the city for recreational purposes; provided that this section shall not apply to vehicles used for maintenance purposes or golf carts being operated on prescribed paths or vehicles using marked roadways, marked driveways, and parking areas. Class 1 and 2 electric bikes, and vehicles intended to assist with personal mobility, shall not be considered to be a "motorized vehicle."
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-5, 4-2-2009; Ord. No. 2023-0518-1 , § 1, 5-18-2023)
Sec. 38-25. - Driving vehicles on roadways; speed of vehicles.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive any vehicle upon or across any part of any public park of the city except upon roadways laid out and maintained for vehicular travel. This section shall not apply to park employees whose duties require them to drive park maintenance equipment over such park areas. The public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee shall coordinate with the proper city departments or authorities to ensure proper, appropriate and adequate signage is in place to aid the implementation of this section.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle upon any road in any city park at a speed in excess of 15 miles per hour.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee to cause signs specifying such limits to be placed at the entrance of each such park and at appropriate places within such parks so as to afford notice to the public of such limit.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-6, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-26. - Vehicle parking restricted.
The parking of vehicles shall be permitted in approved parking areas as long as such parking is in accordance with traffic laws, rules and regulations of the parks department. It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle upon any of the drives, roadways, avenues, or parking lots or at any other place within any city park when such person is not a user of the park or any of its related facilities.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-7, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-27. - Motor vehicles in Cecil Boling Park.
(a)
For the purposes of this section, the term "motor vehicle" means any vehicle which is self-propelled and includes, but not be limited to, any moped, motorized cart, motorcycle, or off-road vehicle as defined by O.C.G.A. §§ 40-1-1 and 40-7-3. The term "motor vehicle" shall not include Class 1 and 2 electric bikes, nor vehicles intended to assist with personal mobility.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle within Cecil Boling Park in any area that is designated by posted signs as being off limits to motor vehicles.
(Code 1985, § 7-1-1; Code 2002, § 38-1; Ord. No. 2023-0518-1 , § 2, 5-18-2023)
Sec. 38-28. - Animals.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to harbor, possess or be in charge of a restrained or unrestrained animal, with the exception of service animals for disabled persons, in prohibited designated areas, which are posted in any city park, without first obtaining approval of the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to harbor, possess or be in charge of any unrestrained animal on walking trails of any city park property or any green space walking trails.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to walk dogs except at the facility designated as the dog park.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-8, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-29. - Bicycles, skateboards, scooters, in-line skates; restriction.
No person shall ride a bicycle or other nonmotorized vehicle, including, but not limited to, skateboards, scooters or in-line skates in or on any athletic field owned or operated by the city for recreational purposes. Nonmotorized vehicles, Class 1 and 2 electric bicycles, and devices intended to assist with personal mobility are allowed on any paved paths located as part of a city park, on the trail between Heritage Park and Etowah River Park, and on the trail east of Etowah River Park.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-9, 4-2-2009; Ord. No. 2023-0518-1 , § 3, 5-18-2023)
Sec. 38-30. - Group use of parks.
Whenever any group desires to use a park for a particular purpose, such as a picnic, party, sports event or theatrical or other entertainment performance, a representative of such group shall first obtain a permit from city hall for such purpose, unless, the group is sponsored by the department as one of the department's scheduled programs. City hall shall grant the application for a permit if it appears that the group will not interfere with the general use of the park by individual members of the public, and if the group meets all other reasonable conditions which may be imposed by the department in this article, and in department regulations not contrary to this article. The permit will include the time, date, location and activity to be conducted by the group. A fee schedule for use of city parks is available at city hall. Such application shall contain a requirement for an indemnity bond, and/or onsite security to protect the city from liability of any kind or character and to protect city property from damage.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-10, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-31. - Use of picnic areas.
(a)
Park employees shall have the authority to regulate the activities in such areas when necessary to prevent congestion and to secure the maximum use for the comfort and convenience of all. Visitors shall comply with any directions given to achieve this end.
(b)
No person shall leave a picnic area before all fire is completely extinguished and before all trash in the nature of boxes, paper, cans, bottles, garbage and other refuse is placed in the disposal receptacles where provided. If no such trash receptacles are available, then refuse and trash shall be carried away from the area by the picnicker to be properly disposed of elsewhere.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-11, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-32. - Youth sports associations.
The use regulations of city parks by youth sports associations shall be the following:
(1)
Contract with the city for use of park facilities.
(2)
Adhere to all rules, regulations and ordinances related to their use of park facilities.
(3)
Be incorporated, tax-exempt, nonprofit associations.
(4)
Coordinate and get approvals from the parks and recreation department when scheduling park facilities for games and practices.
(5)
Provide and conduct youth sports programs that are in the best interest of city residents and the community as determined by the parks and recreation department.
(6)
Be allowed to erect sponsor signs on ball field fences and sponsor banners as determined by the parks and recreation department.
(7)
Be allowed to conduct fundraising activity and events upon approval of the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee.
(8)
Gain approval from the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee before making any minor improvements, alterations or work of any nature on any ball field, ball field fences or park facility. Major capital improvements, proposed to be funded or made by the sports association to facilities shall be approved by mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-12, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-33. - Parks and recreation facilities regulations governing use.
Listed as follows is the order of priority and category in which recreational facilities will be scheduled on an ongoing basis:
(1)
Groups.
a.
Any city-organized or -sponsored recreational activity;
b.
Organizations that the city has agreements with for use of facilities;
c.
Civic groups. Civic groups are defined as nonprofit organizations/clubs, which serve the citizens the city;
d.
All other organizations and groups.
(2)
The scheduling of the parks and recreation facilities will be at the sole discretion of the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee. The general rules and policies applicable to usage of parks and recreation facilities are listed as follows, and failure to abide by these rules could result in future usage of facilities being suspended:
a.
Scheduling of facilities will be coordinated and approved by the parks and recreation department.
b.
Facilities cannot be used for commercial purposes.
c.
Facilities used for fundraising purposes must be approved by the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee.
d.
Full payment and deposit is due at the time of reservation. If facilities are not used during the reserved time for any reason and no prior cancellation was given, no refunds will be made, except as provided in subsection (4) of this section for reimbursement of a cleaning/security deposit.
e.
A member of the organization must sign an affidavit of responsibility and fill out a facility reservation form provided by the parks and recreation department.
f.
No alcoholic beverages shall be allowed under any circumstance unless granted a permit by mayor and city council.
g.
Animals are not permitted in facilities without approval of the recreation department except at the dog park, with the exception of service animals.
h.
Areas shall be left clean. If it becomes necessary for city employees to do an extensive amount of cleaning, there will be a corresponding deduction as determined by the public works director/parks and recreation director or his designee in the cleaning and security deposit.
i.
The user must report any damages to the police or parks employee on duty at once. All groups will be held liable for any damages to any city facility or equipment used by members of their organization. Facility damages could lead to losing privileges to use facilities.
j.
All scheduled meeting times are subject to change. Prior notice will be given by the parks and recreation department if at all possible.
k.
The city is not responsible for any valuables or property left on the premises.
l.
Smoking is prohibited outside of designated smoking areas pursuant to section 32-22.
m.
No decorations shall be displayed, installed or permitted which may damage or deface the structure. Existing decorations may not be removed. The user of the facility is responsible for removing all of their own decorations and trash at the end of the reservation.
n.
No facilities can be reserved more than one year in advance.
o.
When facilities are not in use for scheduled activities and are available to the public for rent, the use of facilities is available on a first-come, first-served basis.
p.
Facility users will be required to have not less than one adult chaperone for each ten minors present during use of facilities.
q.
No facility will be used past 11:00 p.m. without permission from the parks and recreation department.
r.
There will be a cancellation fee of $35.00 if the facility rented and reserved is not cancelled at least 15 days prior to an event.
(3)
The mayor and city council may, by resolution, establish rental fees for any public property and shall provide by appropriate regulations for the use of any public facility. Violation of any regulation established by the mayor and city council and posted on the property or acknowledged by the user shall be deemed a violation of this Code and shall subject the offender to the penalty by law.
(4)
There is hereby created a cleaning/security deposit for rental of all city facilities. The cleaning/security deposit shall be set forth in the schedule of fees and charges per facility. It is refundable if and only when the facility is left in a clean state, and in the condition in which it was found. The decision of whether the deposit shall be refunded is solely up to the parks and recreation department and will not be refunded until the rented facility has been inspected by the city.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-13, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-34. - Boats.
Boat launching must be done within those boat launching areas specifically built and designed for those purposes.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-14, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-35. - Fireworks.
It is unlawful for any individual to shoot, explode, or launch any fireworks in any park in the city, or into any park in the city from outside the park confines, unless such fireworks are part of a commercial fireworks display sponsored by the city and conducted by a licensed and bonded fireworks specialist.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-15, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-36. - Meetings and assemblies.
Persons, corporations, or organizations conducting public meetings, assemblies, worship services, entertainment, demonstrations, or political rallies, within the general confines of a park or municipal area without a permit is prohibited.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-16, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-37. - Noise.
(a)
Prohibited. It is unlawful for a person to:
(1)
Make any loud noises that would disturb a reasonable person of ordinary sensibilities;
(2)
Engage in noisy disputes or conversation that would disturb a reasonable person of ordinary sensibilities;
(3)
Engage in any loud acts of behavior that would disturb a reasonable person of ordinary sensibilities; or
(4)
In any other manner, disturb the public peace, quiet, and order in any of the city parks.
(b)
Public address system. The operation or use of public address systems, whether fixed, portable or vehicle-mounted, is prohibited except when such use or operation is in connection with public gatherings or special events which have been approved by the city.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-17, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-38. - Pollution of water in parks.
It is unlawful for any person to pollute or disturb any spring, branch, pond, fountain, or other water owned by or leased to the city.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-18, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-39. - Posting signs; disposal of trash and refuse.
It is unlawful for any person to affix any bill, sign, or notice on any tree, building, or fixture in any of the parks. It is unlawful for any person to place any paper, books, refuse, or trash of any kind in any of the public parks, except in containers provided for such.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-19, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-40. - Urban camping.
It is unlawful to reside or to store personal property in any park owned by the city. Furthermore, it is unlawful to use any public place, including city parks, for living accommodation purposes or camping, except in areas specifically designated for such use or specifically authorized by permit.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-20, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-41. - Games.
It shall be unlawful for any person to endanger the general public in a park by taking part in or abetting the playing of any games involving throwing or otherwise propelling objects such as balls, stones, arrows, javelins, or model airplanes except in areas set apart for such forms of recreation. The playing of rough or potentially dangerous games such as football, baseball, and soccer is prohibited except on the fields, courts, or other facilities provided therefor. No person shall use any park or other area owned and operated by the city for recreation purposes as a field for golf practice, putting green or driving range except for those areas specifically so designated.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-21, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-42. - Disobeying orders.
No person shall disobey the lawful and reasonable order of a park employee or other city employee in the discharge of his duties for the purpose of safeguarding the park system, or disobey or disregard the notices, prohibitions, instructions, rules or regulations on any park sign.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-22, 4-2-2009)
Sec. 38-43. - Closing of park for special events.
The requirements for obtaining a permit for a special event that closes the park to the general public are as follows:
(1)
The organization sponsoring any special event which seeks to serve alcoholic beverages must first apply for and obtain a special permit from the city manager or his designee in order to serve alcoholic beverages at the special event. This permit must be posted in a conspicuous location at the special event at all times during the event. The special event permit shall specify the hours in which alcoholic beverages are permitted to be served.
(2)
The organization sponsoring any special event which seeks to serve alcoholic beverages shall provide, at his expense, at least one security officer, approved by the chief of police or his designee, at each delineated boundary area approved for the consumption of beer and/or wine.
(Ord. No. 20090402-A, § 38-23, 4-2-2009)
