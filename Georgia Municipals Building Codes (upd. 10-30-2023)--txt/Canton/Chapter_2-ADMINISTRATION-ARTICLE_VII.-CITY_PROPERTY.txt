ARTICLE VII. - CITY PROPERTY

Sec. 2-390. - Supervision and control of public property.
The mayor and city council shall have the general supervision and control of all property, real and personal, owned by or under the control of the city.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-1; Code 2002, § 2-34)
Sec. 2-391. - Authority to pass rules and regulations governing city property.
The mayor and city council shall have the right and authority from time to time to fix, by ordinances or resolutions which shall be spread on its minutes, rules and regulations governing any city property. When such rules have been made, it shall be the duty of the mayor and city council to cause the rules to be posted at some public place upon the premises affected thereby. After such rules are posted, any person violating the rules shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-2; Code 2002, § 2-35)
Sec. 2-392. - Personal use of city property.
It shall be unlawful for any city officer or other person to use or take from its place of keeping any of the tools, equipment, motor vehicles, or other personal property or any fixtures, for the purpose of using the tools, equipment, motor vehicles, or other personal property or fixtures otherwise than provided by the rules of the mayor and city council or for the personal use or benefit of such officer or other person.
(Code 2002, § 2-1)
