DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-299. - Fiscal year.
The fiscal year of the city shall commence on October 1 and end on September 30 of each year.
(Code 1985, § 3-1-1; Code 2002, § 2-346)
State Law reference— Fiscal year required to be established by ordinance, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3.
Sec. 2-300. - Budget; appropriations.
The proposed budget and appropriations resolution shall be prepared by the chief finance officer along with the finance committee and submitted to the mayor and city council no later than the first Thursday of August of each year. The mayor and city council shall schedule such public hearings on the proposed budget as required by state law. The annual operating budget shall be adopted by the mayor and city council on or before the first Thursday of September.
(Code 1985, § 3-1-2; Code 2002, § 2-347; Ord. No. 2021-1216-01 , § 1, 12-16-2021)
State Law reference— Annual budget generally, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3 et seq.
Sec. 2-301. - Fee schedule; fees established by resolution.
The city maintains a schedule of fees for permits, licenses, and other municipal charges. The amount of fees or charges imposed by the city including, but not limited to, those encompassed in the city's fee schedule, shall be established from time to time by resolution of the city council, as it deems necessary.

DIVISION 2. - RECOVERY OF EXPENDITURES

Sec. 2-321. - Purpose and intent.
It is intended that this division provides a means for recovering the cost of the city's and/or other agencies' resources used to respond to and/or provide extraordinary services for situations caused by persons or other entities.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-3; Code 2002, § 2-2(a); Ord. No. 2004-04-01, §§ I—IV, 4-1-2004)
Sec. 2-322. - General requirements.
(a)
All departments within the city are hereby authorized to take appropriate steps to generate an invoice for services when, in their judgment, it was necessary to render the services in the best interest of the citizens the city.
(b)
Services provided by the city may be the result of an accident, carelessness, willful intent, negligence, or failure to take actions that will eliminate reoccurring situations which impact the city's cost of providing services and its ability to provide services for all citizens.
(c)
In lieu of monetary reimbursement, the party receiving the services may replace supplies and equipment expended by the city with equal or greater quantities. Any such materials must be of equal or better specifications than that which was expended. This material reimbursement must be preapproved by the department head of the department being reimbursed.
(d)
Invoices shall be submitted to the city manager for approval prior to presenting the invoice to the party responsible for paying the reimbursement. An exception to this section will be procedures involving hazardous materials incidents.
(e)
During incidents involving a spill or release of a hazardous material, the city, along with other agencies shall be allowed to submit invoices to the responsible party for paying the reimbursement.
(f)
The cost used to calculate the invoice amount shall be based on fair market values for the services, labor rates for city employees, and the prevailing cost of goods and equipment utilized. Compensation for overtime hours worked will be calculated by the city at the rate of one and a half times the normal hourly rate for the employees performing the services.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-3; Code 2002, § 2-2(b); Ord. No. 2004-04-01, §§ I—IV, 4-1-2004)
Sec. 2-323. - Preparing and submitting invoices.
Department instructions, standard operating procedures, and instructions as published by the city finance department shall be followed when preparing invoices and obtaining approvals. The city finance department shall be responsible for tracking the invoices and collecting the reimbursements for the city.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-3; Code 2002, § 2-2(c); Ord. No. 2004-04-01, §§ I—IV, 4-1-2004)
Sec. 2-324. - Enforcement.
Department heads shall be responsible for determining when invoicing for reimbursement is appropriate. Failure to reimburse the city as specified by the invoice shall be processed in accordance with the procedures of the city finance department.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-3; Code 2002, § 2-2(d); Ord. No. 2004-04-01, §§ I—IV, 4-1-2004)
Sec. 2-325. - Other agencies.
(a)
Additional fees from the county fire department and/or other agencies may apply.
(b)
The county fire department's cost recovery fee schedule for hazardous materials incidents shall be established by the county fire department plus the actual replacement cost of all expendable equipment and goods used during the incident.
(c)
The county finance specialist will handle incidents involving the county's hazmat team.
(d)
All other agencies are required to handle invoicing per their policies.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-3; Code 2002, § 2-2(e); Ord. No. 2004-04-01, §§ I—IV, 4-1-2004)

DIVISION 3. - PAYMENTS BY CREDIT CARDS

Sec. 2-354. - Authorized.
All sums due the city, including, but not limited to, ad valorem taxes, occupation taxes, fines, forfeitures, penalties, license fees, permit fees and registration fees, may be paid by the use of credit cards. The chief finance officer, with the approval of the city manager, shall establish the procedures for acceptance of credit card payments for amounts due the city. The city manager is authorized to enter into agreements with credit card issuers to facilitate the acceptance of credit card payments by the city.
(Code 2002, § 2-376; Ord. of 7-7-1996, § 101)
Sec. 2-355. - Discounted fees approved.
A credit card agreement made pursuant to this division may provide for the acceptance of the credit card payments from the credit card company at a discount from the face amount of the payment or for the withholding of administrative fees from the face amount of the payment. Such discounted fee is granted in exchange for the improved governmental cash flow, reduction of administrative expenses and increased public convenience as a result of credit card payments.
(Code 2002, § 2-377; Ord. of 7-7-1996, § 102)
Sec. 2-356. - Cancellation of agreement.
Any agreement with a credit card issuer for the acceptance or payments via credit made pursuant to this division shall contain a provision that the agreement may be canceled at any time by the city.
(Code 2002, § 2-378; Ord. of 7-7-1996, § 104)
Sec. 2-357. - Personal liability for final payment.
No person making payment by credit card to the city is relieved from liability for the underlying obligation except to the extent that the city actually receives final payment of the obligation in cash or its equivalent. If final payment is not made by the credit card issuer or other guarantor of payment in the credit card transaction, the underlying obligation for the money due is not extinguished. All applicable penalties, interest and collection fees on the unpaid amount shall be due from the person making the payment by credit card. Such penalties, interest and fees on an unpaid amount shall accrue from the date the unpaid obligation was first due under the applicable statute or ordinance. The city shall follow the collection procedures for the unpaid amount that would have otherwise been applicable had the credit card payment not been attempted.
(Code 2002, § 2-379; Ord. of 7-7-1996, § 105)
Sec. 2-358. - Identity theft prevention program.
For the purpose complying with 16 CFR 681.2 and in order to detect, prevent and mitigate identity theft by identifying and detecting identity theft red flags and by responding to such red flags in a manner that will prevent identity theft and, pursuant to 16 CFR 681.1, the city has adopted an identity theft prevention program, a copy of which is available in the office of the city clerk.
