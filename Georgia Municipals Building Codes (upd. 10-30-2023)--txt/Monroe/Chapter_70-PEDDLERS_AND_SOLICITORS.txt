ARTICLE II. - PEDDLERS AND SOLICITORS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 70-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Regulated activity means the act of going from place to place, or from house to house, or upon public or private property for any of the following purposes:
(1)
Sell personal property, goods, wares, merchandise, or services;
(2)
Solicit or take orders for the sale of personal property, goods, wares, merchandise or services; or
(3)
Exhibit or show personal property, goods, wares, or merchandise or to explain services; or
(4)
Conduct a survey relative to any personal property, goods, wares, merchandise, or services.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-11(a))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-32. - Exemptions.
Persons who are licensed by the state or otherwise licensed by the city to carry on a particular occupation are exempt from the provisions of this article to the extent they are acting within the scope of their licensed occupation. Persons who sell, solicit, exhibit, take orders or conduct surveys on behalf of civic, charitable, religious, or educational nonprofit corporations or associations and route salesmen of dairy and bakery products, are not engaged in a regulated activity.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-11(b))
Sec. 70-33. - Offenses.
No person engaged in a regulated activity under this article shall:
(1)
Engage in a regulated activity without having first been registered as required by section 70-56, or engage in such with an expired registration;
(2)
Refuse to exhibit proof of registration under this article upon the request of a police officer or other officer of the city or to any person while he is engaged in a regulated activity;
(3)
Give false information on an application form;
(4)
Represent that he is an employee, agent or representative of a person that he does not, in fact, represent;
(5)
Refuse to leave the property or premises of another when requested to leave by the owner or occupant of such property or premises;
(6)
Engage in an uninvited regulated activity during the period from 6:00 p.m. of any day to and through 10:00 a.m. of the next succeeding day, or during any Sunday or legal holiday;
(7)
Make or use any false statement or misrepresentation to secure admittance to any building or structure within the city;
(8)
Make or use any false statement or misrepresentation concerning the price, terms of payment, quality, quantity or delivery of goods, wares, merchandise or services sold or solicited or exhibited for sale.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-18)
Sec. 70-34. - Produce peddlers prohibited from parking in fire district no. 1.
Produce peddlers shall not park on any street or on any private property within fire district no. 1 in the city.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-19)
Cross reference— Fire protection and prevention, ch. 46.

DIVISION 2. - REGISTRATION

Sec. 70-56. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in a regulated activity within the city without having first been registered by the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-12)
Sec. 70-57. - Application.
Any person desiring to be registered to carry on a regulated activity shall file an application with the city clerk. The application shall be on a form prescribed by the city clerk and shall require the following information:
(1)
Name of the applicant;
(2)
Business and residence addresses of the applicant;
(3)
Address to which all notices required under this article shall be sent;
(4)
Age, sex, weight, height and the color of eyes and hair of the applicant;
(5)
Name and address of the person that the applicant represents or is employed by, or whose merchandise is being sold;
(6)
Description of the kind and nature of the property, goods, wares, merchandise or services sold or solicited for sale by the applicant;
(7)
A state sales tax number;
(8)
Signature of the applicant;
(9)
Whether or not the applicant has ever been convicted of a violation of any ordinance of the city or any other municipality; and if so, a description of the nature of the violation, the date of conviction, and the name of the city involved;
(10)
Whether or not the applicant has ever been convicted of a violation of any statute of the United States, the state or any other state; and if so, a description of the nature of the violation, the date of the conviction, and the name of the state or other jurisdiction involved;
(11)
Whether or not the applicant has ever been refused or had a state or municipal license revoked on the grounds of fraud or misrepresentation, and if so, the type of license refused or revoked, the name of the governmental authority refusing or revoking the license, a statement of the grounds for refusal or revocation, and the date of the refusal or revocation.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-13)
Sec. 70-58. - Approval of registration.
Upon furnishing the information required in the application for registration under this division and payment of the applicable registration fee, the applicant shall be registered forthwith unless the information furnished shows that the applicant has been convicted of a crime involving moral turpitude or the provisions of section 70-61.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-13(b))
Sec. 70-59. - Classification.
There shall be three classes of registration under this division based upon the length of validity:
(1)
Class A, one week or seven days.
(2)
Class B, two months.
(3)
Class C, more than two months, provided the provisions of article IV of chapter 90 shall also be met.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-13(c))
Sec. 70-60. - Fee.
The applicant for registration under this division shall pay a fee therefor as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-13(c))
Sec. 70-61. - Refusal to register applicant.
(a)
The city clerk may refuse registration to any person for any of the following reasons:
(1)
The conviction of the applicant for a violation under this division within three years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(2)
The revocation of a prior registration under this division within three years immediately preceding the filing of the application; or the revocation of any other license issued by the United States, the state, any other state, or any other municipality within the United States based upon or involving fraud or misrepresentation and within three years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(3)
The application is under suspension under this division.
(4)
The conviction of the applicant of a violation under any statute of the United States, the state, or any other state, or under any ordinance of the city or any other municipality within the United States, which violation involved fraud, misrepresentation, or embezzlement and which conviction occurred within three years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(5)
The failure or refusal of the applicant to complete any part or all of the application form, or submitting false or inaccurate information on the application form, or failure to pay the registration fee.
(6)
The activities proposed to be conducted by the applicant are unlawful under the ordinances of the city or the laws of the United States or the state.
(b)
If the city clerk refuses to register the applicant, he shall notify the applicant in writing setting forth the grounds upon which his refusal to register is based.
(c)
The applicant may appeal from the city clerk's decision by filing a written notice of appeal with the city administrator within ten days after receipt of notice from the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-14)
Sec. 70-62. - Revocation or suspension.
(a)
The city clerk may, by service of notice, suspend for a period of not more than 180 days, or revoke any registration under the authority of this division for the following reasons:
(1)
The conviction of the registrant of any of the offenses set forth in section 70-61.
(2)
The conviction of the registrant under any ordinance of any city or the laws of the United States, the state, or any other state involving fraud, misrepresentation or embezzlement and within three years immediately preceding the filing of the application for the registration, or during the effective period of the registration.
(3)
The use of any fraud, misrepresentation, trick or deception in carrying out or promoting a regulated activity.
(4)
The refusal of the registrant to exhibit proof of registration under this division to any official or police officer of the city.
(5)
The representation upon the application form or otherwise, that the registrant is an employee, agent or representative of any person which the registrant does not, in fact, represent.
(6)
The refusal or failure of the registrant to leave the property or premises of others when requested to leave by the owner or occupant thereof.
(7)
The making of any false statement or misrepresentation by the registrant to gain entrance to any building or structure within the city.
(8)
The use of any false or fraudulent statement, whether written or oral, or any misrepresentation concerning the price, terms of payment, quality, quantity or delivery of personal property, goods, wares, merchandise or services.
(b)
The registrant may appeal any such suspension or revocation to the county superior court by certiorari under state law.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-15)
Sec. 70-63. - Appeals.
(a)
Appeals from the city clerk's action in denying, suspending or revoking a registration shall be heard by the mayor and city council and city attorney. Such appeal shall be conducted within 15 days after written notice of appeal is filed with the city administrator.
(b)
When acting as an appeals board, the council may receive all evidence and hear witnesses on matters germane to the appeal. They shall afford the registrant or applicant making the appeal an opportunity to present evidence, cross examine witnesses and to be heard. The council may affirm, modify or reverse the action taken by the city clerk, and shall render a decision within ten days of the hearing date.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-16)
Sec. 70-64. - Manner of giving notice.
(a)
All notices to be given to an applicant or a registrant under this division shall be given in any of the following manners:
(1)
Personal service; or
(2)
By first class mail to the address shown on the application form as required in section 70-57(3).
(b)
It shall be conclusively presumed that notice by first class mail was received by the applicant or registrant, and the failure of the applicant or registrant to receive such notice shall not invalidate the action taken pursuant to such notice.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-17)

ARTICLE III. - CHARITABLE SOLICITATIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 70-96. - Application of article.
No organization shall be considered a nonprofit organization within the meaning of this article except a tax-exempt organization within the meaning of the Internal Revenue Code or the Georgia Income Tax Law as such code and such law are now or hereafter amended.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-33)
Sec. 70-97. - Regulations governing activities.
Solicitation or canvassing upon the public streets, areas or parks, or calling from house to house in the city under this article shall be subject to the following regulations:
(1)
All solicitation, calling or canvassing from house to house in the city shall only occur between the hours of 10:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.; however, with the prior express permission of the occupant, door-to-door solicitation may be made between 6:00 p.m. and 9:00 p.m.
(2)
The number of solicitors or canvassers or callers from house to house in the city for any single firm, corporation or organization shall not exceed ten in number at any one time; however, applicants may amend their application to provide a current list of all persons soliciting at any one time.
(3)
Immediately prior to any solicitation of funds within the city, each solicitor, canvasser or caller shall present his identity card, issued by the chief of police, to each person solicited and request each person solicited to read such identity card. Each solicitor, canvasser or caller must inform each person solicited as to any minimum donation required for the acceptance of any merchandise, wares, goods or any similar items given by each solicitor, canvasser or caller prior to such acceptance by each person solicited.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-35)
Sec. 70-98. - Prohibited acts.
It shall be unlawful for a solicitor, canvasser or caller to do any of the following:
(1)
Falsely represent, directly or by implication, that funds being solicited are on behalf of any person other than such registered with the chief of police;
(2)
Solicit, canvass or call without the express prior permission of any occupant or property owner to solicit at any residence, apartment complex, or shopping mall, other than areas open to public parking, where there is posted a sign forbidding such activity;
(3)
Remain on private premises after being asked to leave the premises;
(4)
Continue solicitation after being refused upon the public streets, areas or parks; such action shall constitute harassment.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-36)
Sec. 70-99. - Annual fund raising drives.
Any nonprofit organization conducting annual fund raising or annual sales within the city shall not be required to obtain identity cards from the chief of police nor shall the numbers of such solicitors, canvassers or callers be restricted; however, such annual fund raising or annual sales shall not exceed seven days per year. Such nonprofit organization shall comply with all other provisions contained in this article.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-37)

DIVISION 2. - REGISTRATION

Sec. 70-121. - Required.
Any nonprofit association, corporation or organization desiring to solicit or canvass upon the public streets, areas or parks or to call from house to house in the city for the purpose of raising funds or seeking donations for any religious, charitable or eleemosynary organization shall register with the chief of police.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-31)
Sec. 70-122. - Information provided to city.
(a)
Any nonprofit association, corporation or organization desiring to solicit or canvass upon the public streets, areas or parks or to call from house to house in the city shall file, on a form to be supplied by the chief of police, the following information:
(1)
Name of applicant;
(2)
Permanent residence address;
(3)
Name, address and telephone number of employer, firm, corporation or organization represented;
(4)
Names and addresses of all persons assisting in the solicitation, canvassing or calling from house to house in the city;
(5)
Nature of any merchandise, wares, goods or any similar items to be sold or offered for sale or given away in conjunction with the solicitation, canvassing or calling from house to house in the city;
(6)
Proof of tax-exempt status shall be required when registering with the chief of police.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-32)
Sec. 70-123. - Issuance of identity card.
Upon proper registration with the chief of police under this division, an identity card shall be issued, to be valid for a period of 90 days from the date of issuance. Identity cards must be carried by all persons while engaged in solicitation, or canvassing upon the public streets, areas, or parks or calling from house to house in the city.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-34)

ARTICLE IV. - ORGANIZERS

Sec. 70-156. - Compliance.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the business of organizing or attempting to organize any person or group of persons for any type of organization within the city or to solicit funds for such organization or to act as agent for such organization without first complying with conditions set out in this article.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-1)
Sec. 70-157. - License required.
No person shall engage in any activity coming under this article without first obtaining a license to do so from the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-2)
Sec. 70-158. - Application.
Before any person shall engage in any of the activities coming under this article, he shall make written application to the city clerk setting forth:
(1)
Age of the applicant.
(2)
Gender of the applicant.
(3)
Name of the proposed organization that the applicant intends to represent.
(4)
If a corporation, the name of its officers.
(5)
Length of residence in the city.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-2)
Sec. 70-159. - Fee.
The application for a permit required by this article shall be accompanied by a certified check in the amount of the license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-2)
Sec. 70-160. - Approval required.
No application for license required by this article shall be approved without the consent of the city council in a regular or called session.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-2)
Sec. 70-161. - Eligibility.
No person shall be granted a license to act as organizer for any type of organization unless such person is more than 21 years of age and is of good moral character.
(Code 1988, § 9-3-3)
