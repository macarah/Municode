Chapter 6 - ADULT ENTERTAINMENT
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Purpose.
The purpose of this chapter is to regulate certain types of businesses, including, but not limited to, adult entertainment establishments to the end that the many types of criminal activities frequently engendered by such businesses will be curtailed; however, it is recognized that such regulation cannot be de facto approach prohibition. Otherwise, a protected form of expression would vanish. As to adult dance establishments, this chapter represents a balancing of competing interests; reduced criminal activity and protection of the city's neighborhoods through the regulation of adult entertainment establishments versus the protected rights of adult entertainment establishments and patrons.

Sec. 6-3. - Unlawful operation declared a nuisance.
Any adult entertainment establishment operated, conducted or maintained contrary to the provisions of this chapter shall be and the same is hereby declared to be unlawful and a public nuisance. The city may, in addition to, or in lieu of prosecuting a criminal action hereunder, commence an action or actions, proceeding or proceedings for abatement, removal or enjoining thereof, in the manner provided by law. The city shall further be authorized to take such other steps and apply to such court or courts as may have jurisdiction to grant such relief as will abate such nuisance and restrain and enjoin any person from operating, conducting or maintaining an adult entertainment establishment contrary to the provisions of this chapter. In addition, violation of the provisions of this chapter shall be per se grounds for suspension or revocation of any permit granted hereunder.

ARTICLE II. - LICENSING

Sec. 6-25. - Limitation on number of licenses issued.
modified
Should there be a sufficient number of current licenses to meet the needs and desires of the inhabitants of the city, no new licenses shall be issued. In determining the needs and desires of the inhabitants, the standard of review shall be that the market is virtually unrestrained as defined in Young v. American Mini Theaters, Inc., 427 U.S. 50, 96 S.Ct. 2440, 49 L.Ed.2d 310 (1976).

Sec. 6-26. - License required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in, conduct or carry on, or operate in or upon any premises within the city an adult entertainment establishment as defined in this chapter, without a license to do so.
(b)
No license so issued shall condone or authorize, or be deemed to condone or authorize, any activity if the same is deemed illegal or unlawful under state, federal, or municipal law. Any such license must be currently valid or the licensee shall have made proper application for renewal within the time required therefor under this chapter.
Sec. 6-27. - Application for license.
(a)
Any person desiring to obtain a permit to operate, engage in, conduct or carry on any adult entertainment establishment business shall make application to the city chief of police. Prior to submitting such application, the nonrefundable fee, established by this article, shall be paid to the city clerk to defray, in part, the cost of the investigations and reports required by this article. Upon payment thereof, the city clerk shall issue a receipt showing that such application fee has been paid.
(b)
The application for such a license shall not authorize the engaging in, operation of, conduct of or carrying on of any adult entertainment establishment business until the approval of the application as herein provided.
Sec. 6-28. - Application contents.
Each application for an adult entertainment establishment license shall contain, at a minimum, the following information.
(1)
The applicant's full name, social security number, and date of birth.
(2)
The present address and telephone number of the applicant.
(3)
Acceptable written proof that the applicant is at least 21 years of age.
(4)
The business, occupation and employment history of the applicant for the five years immediately preceding the date of application.
(5)
The business license history of the applicant and whether such applicant, in previous businesses in this or any other city, state or territory, has had any license or permit for an adult entertainment business or similar type of business revoked or suspended, the reason therefor, and the business activity or occupation of the applicant subsequent to such action of suspension or revocation.
(6)
If the applicant is a corporation, the name of the corporation shall be set forth exactly as shown in its articles of incorporation and charter, together with the place and date of incorporation, all states or other jurisdictions in which the applicant is authorized to do business (including written evidence of such authorizations), and the names and addresses of each of its current officers and directors.
(7)
If the applicant is a partnership, the applicant shall set forth the name, residence address and dates of birth of the partners.
(8)
If the applicant is a limited partnership, it shall furnish a copy of its certificate of limited partnership.
(9)
If one or more of the partners is a corporation, the provisions of this section pertaining to corporations shall apply to the corporate partner.
(10)
The applicant corporation or partnership shall designate one or more of its officers or general partners to act as its responsible managing officer. Such designated person shall complete and sign all application forms required of an individual applicant under this article, but only one application fee shall be charged.
(11)
Whether the applicant, any partners or any corporate officers or directors, if the applicant is a corporation, have been convicted of any crime evidencing a lack of good moral character, as defined in this chapter, in the past five years and, if so, a complete description of any such crime, including date of violation, date of conviction, jurisdiction where the crime or act occurred, and any disposition thereof, including any fine or sentence imposed and whether terms of any court-imposed sentence have been fully completed.
(12)
If the applicant is a person doing business under a trade name, a copy of all trade name documents properly recorded as required by law.
(13)
If the applicant is a corporation, a copy of the articles of incorporation, trade name affidavit, if any, and last annual report to the Georgia Secretary of State.
(14)
At least three character references from individuals who are in no way related to the applicant or individual shareholders, officers or directors of a corporation and who are not and will not benefit financially in any way from the operation of the business if the license is granted and who have not been convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude. The city clerk shall prepare forms consistent with the provisions of this section for the applicant who shall submit all character references on such forms.
(15)
The address of the premises to be licensed.
(16)
Whether the premises are owned or rented by the applicant.
(17)
A plat by a registered surveyor, licensed by the state, showing that the location of the proposed premises is in accordance with the provisions of this article and chapter 94, zoning.
(18)
Each application for an adult entertainment establishment license shall be verified and acknowledged under oath to be true and correct:
a.
If the applicant is an individual, by the individual;
b.
If a partnership, by the manager or general partner;
c.
If a corporation, by the president of the corporation;
d.
If any other organization or association, by the chief administrative official of such organization or association;
(19)
In addition to the information required herein, the application shall disclose the name, address, social security number and date of birth of all persons who have a financial interest of any type in the business that is applying for the license, and any other matter reasonably required by the chief of police.
Sec. 6-29. - Applicant to appear.
The applicant, if an individual, or the designated responsible managing officer, if a partnership, corporation, or other entity, shall personally appear at the city police department, before the chief of police, or his designee, and produce proof that the nonrefundable application fee, established by this article, has been paid, and shall also present the completed license application containing the required information to the chief or his designee.
Sec. 6-30. - Application; investigation; recommendations.
(a)
The city shall have 45 days to investigate the application and the background of the applicant. Upon completion of the investigation, the chief shall report his findings and recommendations to the mayor and city council, which shall consider the application within 30 days of receipt of the report from the chief.
(b)
After a hearing on the application, at which any interested persons present may be heard, the mayor and city council shall grant or deny the permit at its next regular meeting; but the mayor and city council may only recommend the granting of such permit if it finds:
(1)
The required application fee has been paid.
(2)
The application conforms in all respects to the provisions of this chapter.
(3)
The applicant has not knowingly made a material misrepresentation in the application.
(4)
The applicant has fully cooperated in connection with the investigation of his application.
(5)
The applicant, if an individual, or any officers or directors of the applicant, if the applicant is a corporation, or any of the partners, including limited partners, if the applicant is a partnership, have not been convicted, entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere, in a court of competent jurisdiction to an offense involving a sex-related crime or drug-related or alcohol-related felony or have been convicted, entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere, of an attempt to commit any of the aforementioned offenses, or convicted, entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere in any other state as to an offense which, if committed or attempted in this state, would have been the same or substantially the same offense as one or more of the stated offenses.
(6)
The applicant has not had an adult entertainment establishment license or other similar license or permit denied or revoked for cause in this city or in any other local government jurisdiction located in or out of this state prior to either the date of the application or the date of the granting of the application.
(7)
The building, structure, equipment, and location of such business, as proposed by the applicant, would comply with all applicable zoning and distance laws and would qualify to receive a certificate of occupancy under the law of this state and the articles of this city.
(8)
The applicant is at least 21 years of age.
(9)
That neither the applicant, nor any of the applicant's employees, agents, partners, directors, officers, or managers, has within five years of the date of the application, knowingly allowed or permitted any of the specified sexual activities defined herein to occur in or upon the premises where such adult entertainment establishment is to be located, nor has such applicant or other persons knowingly allowed or permitted the proposed licensed premises to be used as a place in which solicitations for any of the specified sexual activities occurred within the said time period.
(10)
That on the date the business for which a permit is required herein commences, and thereafter, there will be a responsible person actually on the premises to act as manager at all times during which the business is open.
(11)
That the proposed premises shall not be located at any location not authorized by this article or by chapter 94, zoning.
(12)
That the grant of such license will not result in a violation of this article or any other article or regulation of the city, or of any laws, rules or regulations of the state, or the United States.
(13)
Any other inquiry deemed necessary or desirable by the city to ensure the health, safety and welfare of the citizens of the city or the preservation of its neighborhoods.
(14)
That all of the requirements of this article, including, but not limited to this section, have been met by the applicant.
(c)
All hearings regarding such application shall be open to the public and advertised in the legal organ of the county as required by law.
Sec. 6-31. - Additional license requirements.
(a)
The licensee shall maintain with the city police department at all times a written statement of the name, address, and phone number of the manager or assistant manager in charge of the licensed premises and there shall at all times be a manager in charge and on the premises during operating hours.
(b)
Each licensee shall have a continuing obligation to comply with all provisions of this chapter.
(c)
The applicant may be required to appear at any hearing on the issue of the grant, denial, suspension, or revocation of the requested license governed by this article.
Sec. 6-32. - Persons prohibited as licensees.
Subject to the other requirements of this chapter, no license authorized by this chapter shall be issued to or held by:
(1)
An applicant who has not paid all required fees and taxes, including ad valorem taxes, for a business at that location;
(2)
Any person who is not of good moral character as defined in this chapter;
(3)
Any corporation, any of whose officers, directors, or shareholders are not of good moral character as defined in this chapter;
(4)
Any partnership or association, any of whose partners or members are not of good moral character as defined in this chapter;
(5)
Any person employing, assisted by or financial in whole or in part by, any person who is not of good moral character as defined in this chapter;
(6)
Any applicant who is not qualified to hold and conduct a business according to the laws of the United States, the state or the city.
Sec. 6-33. - License renewal.
Licenses for adult entertainment establishments shall be renewed on a calendar year basis, provided that the licensee continues to meet the requirements set out in this article. The renewal fee for the adult entertainment establishments licenses shall be as established by this article. Renewal applications shall be submitted by November 15 of each year prior to the year for which such license is requested.
Sec. 6-34. - Licenses nontransferable.
No adult entertainment establishment license may be sold, transferred or assigned by a licensee, or by operation of law, to any other person. Any such sale, transfer or assignment, or attempted sale, transfer or assignment, shall be deemed to constitute a voluntary surrender of such licensee and such license shall thereafter be null and void; provided, however, that if the licensee is a partnership and one or more of the partners should die, one or more of the surviving partners may acquire, by purchase or otherwise, the interest of the deceased partner without effecting a surrender or termination of such license, and in such case, the license, upon notification to the city, shall be placed in the name of the surviving partner, subject to compliance with all other requirements of this article.
Sec. 6-35. - Change of name.
(a)
No licensee shall advertise, operate, conduct, manage, engage in, or carry on an adult entertainment establishment business under any name other than the name of the business as specified on the license.
(b)
Any application for an extension or expansion of a building or other place of business where an adult entertainment establishment is located shall require inspection and shall comply with the provisions and regulations of this chapter, and all other articles, rules and regulations of the city.
Sec. 6-36. - Display of license.
An adult entertainment establishment licensee shall conspicuously display all licenses required by this chapter.
Sec. 6-37. - Annual report.
No later than March 1 of each year, an adult entertainment establishment licensee shall file a verified report with the city clerk showing the licensee's gross receipts and amounts paid to dancers for the preceding calendar year.
Sec. 6-38. - Revocation and appeal.
(a)
After notice and hearing, the chief of police shall be authorized to suspend or revoke a license previously granted hereunder. In the event the chief of police seeks to suspend or to revoke a license, the chief of police shall give written notification to the licensee of such action.
(b)
The notice shall contain a specification of the violation upon which such suspension or revocation is based and shall be served upon the licensee, by registered mail, return receipt requested, at least five days prior to the date of the hearing. The licensee shall be given written notice of the time and place of the hearing.
(c)
The chief of police shall be authorized to suspend or revoke a license in the event of the occurrence of any one or more of the following:
(1)
A licensee provided false or misleading information in the original or subsequent application process;
(2)
A licensee has knowingly allowed possession, use, or sale of controlled substances on the premises;
(3)
A licensee has knowingly allowed the violation of an article of the city or a violation of any criminal law of the state or of the United States to occur on the premises;
(4)
A licensee has violated any provision of this article;
(5)
A licensee has been convicted, during the license period, of any drug-related, alcohol-related or sex-related crime by the state, the city, or the United States regarding an offense which was committed on the premises, or which would otherwise violate the provisions of this chapter when such offense would serve to prohibit the licensee from obtaining a permit under this article;
(6)
A licensee fails to pay any fee, license fee, fine or other amount of money due the city under this article or any other license or occupational taxation article of the city.
(d)
In the event the chief of police shall suspend or revoke any license hereunder, the suspension or revocation shall be for a period of not less than one day nor more than 365 days, within the sound discretion of the chief of police; provided, however, that the licensee shall be authorized to continue his business operations until the date of any hearing scheduled in accordance with this article.
(e)
No licensee or other applicant may apply for a license during any period of suspension or revocation. In any hearing or investigation conducted by the chief of police, in connection with a possible suspension or revocation of a license issued hereunder, the chief shall consider, among other things, the severity of the allegations, the evidence submitted and the testimony presented, in making any decision on suspension, revocation and the duration of any suspension or revocation.
(f)
In the event of a suspension or revocation by the chief of police, the licensee may appeal the decision of the chief of police to the city council by filing a written notice of appeal within ten days from the date that the licensee receives written notification of the decision of the chief of police.
(g)
The written notification shall be mailed to the licensee by certified mail, return receipt requested. Thereafter, a hearing shall be scheduled before the city council within 45 days after the filing of the notice of appeal by the applicant.
(h)
After hearing by the city council, the city council may take such action as it finds warranted from the evidence submitted to it, including, but not limited to, the affirming of the action of the chief of police, the reversal of such action, or the imposition of modified sanctions upon the licensee.
Sec. 6-39. - License fees.
(a)
Application and renewal fees shall be paid to the city clerk upon application or renewal, as the case may be. The amount of the fees shall be as established from time to time by the city council.
(b)
Any licensee shall also be subject to all business license fees and occupational taxes imposed by the city pursuant to chapter 22.

ARTICLE III. - EMPLOYEES

Sec. 6-68. - Qualifications.
Employees of an adult entertainment establishment shall be at least 21 years of age. Any applicant for employment who has been previously convicted within the preceding five years of a sex-related crime, a drug-related crime, or an alcohol-related felony, or a crime involving moral turpitude shall not be eligible for employment as an adult entertainment establishment employee, and any employee who is convicted of a sex-related crime, drug-related crime, or alcohol-related felony while employed as an adult entertainment establishment employee shall not thereafter work at any premises requiring licenses under this chapter for a period of five years from the date of such conviction, unless a longer time is ordered as part of a court-ordered sentence.
Sec. 6-69. - Approval for employment.
Before any person may be employed at a licensed premises by a licensee hereunder, he shall file a notice with the chief of police of his intended employment on forms that require the information set forth in section 6-68. The city shall have 45 days to review and investigate the application submitted by the employee. If the employee is found to be qualified for such employment by the terms of this article, the chief of police shall grant approval of the employment. Upon approval, the employee may be employed by the licensee. If approval is denied, the prospective employee may, within ten days of said denial, apply to the chief administrative officer of the city for a hearing. The decision of the chief administrative officer after hearing the appeal shall be final.
Sec. 6-70. - Investigation fee.
An investigation fee in the amount established by the city council, or a receipt from the city clerk evidencing the payment of such fee at the time the notice is filed, shall accompany the notice of intended employment submitted to the chief of police.
Sec. 6-71. - Retention of employee records.
An adult entertainment establishment licensee shall maintain and retain for a period of two years the names, addresses, and ages of all persons employed therein.
Sec. 6-72. - Employment of minors prohibited.
No adult entertainment establishment licensee shall employ or contract with a person under the age of 21 years or a person not licensed pursuant to this chapter.

Sec. 6-103. - Advertising and posting.
The premises for which the license herein is sought either as an initial license or a renewal thereof, shall be posted with a notice in a form prescribed by the city chief administrative officer for at least 21 days prior to any hearing on such application. In addition, a newspaper advertisement shall be published in the county legal organ in a form prescribed by the city chief administrative officer at least two times during the ten days prior to any hearing on such license application or renewal. The posted notice and newspaper advertisements shall notify all interested persons of the date, time, location and purpose of the hearing on such application.

Adult entertainment establishment businesses shall be located only in a commercial zoning district as now or hereafter defined by the city's zoning articles and regulations on a parcel of property not less than three acres in size, with no less than 100 feet of road frontage.
Sec. 6-107. - Proximity to other buildings and facilities.
No adult entertainment establishment business shall be located:
(1)
Within 1,000 feet of any parcel of land which is zoned for multifamily or single-family uses or purposes;
(2)
Within 1,000 feet of any parcel of land upon which a church, school, governmental building owned or occupied by such government, library, civic center, public park or neighborhood playground is located;
(3)
Within 1,000 feet of any parcel of land upon which another establishment regulated or defined hereunder is located.
Sec. 6-108. - Measurements of distance.
For the purpose of this article, distance shall be by airline measurement from property line to property line, using the closest property lines of the parcels of land involved.
Sec. 6-109. - Permitted hours of operation.
All adult entertainment establishments shall be closed between 12:45 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. each day. They shall also be closed every Sunday and on Christmas Day.

Total nude dancing, appearing nude or dancing involving acts of public indecency as defined by this chapter are hereby prohibited in any adult dancing establishment. Sec. 6-133. - Touching of employees or patrons.
No adult entertainment establishment licensed employee shall fondle or caress any patron and no patron shall fondle or caress any adult entertainment establishment employee on the licensed premises.

All dancing by an employee at an adult dancing establishment shall occur on a platform intended for that purpose which is raised at least two feet from the level of the floor. No dancing shall occur closer than four feet to any patron.

ARTICLE VI. - MINORS

Sec. 6-158. - Admission of minors unlawful.
It shall be unlawful for a licensee to admit or permit the admission of minors into a licensed premises.

It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, barter or give, to any minor any service, material, device or thing sold or offered for sale from an adult bookstore, adult motion picture theater, adult massage parlor or adult dancing establishment or other adult entertainment enterprise.
