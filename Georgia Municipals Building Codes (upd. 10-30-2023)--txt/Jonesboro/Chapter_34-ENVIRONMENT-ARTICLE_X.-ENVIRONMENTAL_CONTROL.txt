ARTICLE X. - ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL

Sec. 34-376. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Abandoned or junked motor vehicle is defined as one that is in such a state of disrepair as to be incapable of operating under its own power or to operate safely upon public roads or streets as required by the laws of Georgia, or which does not have a current license plate or tag, or does not meet the requirements of O.C.G.A. Title 40 for operation on public roads.
(Ord. No. 2007-005, Art. I, 12-10-2007)
Sec. 34-377. - Abandonment of wrecked, junked, dismantled or inoperative motor vehicle.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner, tenant, lessee, occupant, or person in possession of any lot or parcel of land in the city to keep or permit to be kept or stored on said land any wrecked, dismantled, inoperative abandoned or junked motor vehicle, which is not completely enclosed in a building or a carport.
(b)
Subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to an automobile stored under car cover made for the vehicle's make and model. Such car cover shall not be transparent. Abandoned or junked vehicles shall be stored behind the front corner of a residence or inside a privacy fence. This exception is limited to two vehicles.
(c)
Subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to vehicles stored on premises that are zoned for the operation of an automobile or other repair service, or storage, lawfully zoned and permitted, and shall have been issued a business license for such operation by the city.
(Ord. No. 2007-005, Art. I, 12-10-2007)
Sec. 34-378. - Cleanliness of property; generally.
Every owner, tenant, lessee, occupant, or person in possession of any lot or parcel of land in the city is required to keep all land, within 100 feet of all improved structures, cleared of any grass, weed, brush, or any vegetation exceeding ten inches that is normally planted for land cover or erosion control.
(Ord. No. 2007-005, Art. I, 12-10-2007)
Sec. 34-379. - Notice.
(a)
In the event of a violation of this article, a warning may be issued in the form of mail, tacked to the property, or by phone stating that the owner, tenant, owner's agent or property manager of the property has five days to be in compliance or a citation will be issued with a mandatory court appearance.
(b)
If property is not brought into compliance within five days of the warning, a citation shall be issued.
(c)
A second violation of section 34-377 within a 12-month period shall result in an automatic citation.
(Ord. No. 2007-005, Art. I, 12-10-2007)
Sec. 34-380. - Penalties for violation.
Upon a conviction of violation of this article, the owner, tenant, owner's agent or property manager may be fined a maximum of $1,000.00 per violation; for continuous violation, each day of the violation shall constitute shall constitute a separate and distinct punishable violation.
(Ord. No. 2007-005, Art. I, 12-10-2007)
