ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 46-1. - Additional standards adopted.
For the purpose of prescribing regulations governing conditions hazardous to life and property from fire or explosion, there is adopted the state rules and regulations of the safety fire marshal, and by adopting the Life Safety Code, current edition (NFPA 101), and the whole thereof, of which one copy each is filed in the office of the city clerk. Such code is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length in this section, and the provisions of such code shall be controlled within the limits of the city.
(Code 1976, § 3-2024)
Cross reference— Construction codes, § 18-41.
Sec. 46-2. - Use of fire hydrants.
It shall be unlawful for any person to use water from any fire hydrant, whether public or private, for any purpose other than for the extinguishment of fires, or lawful city purposes.
(Code 1976, § 3-2005)
Sec. 46-3. - Tampering with hydrants.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tamper or interfere with any hydrant of the city, except by authority of the city.
(Code 1976, § 3-2006)
Sec. 46-4. - Obstructing fire station.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct in any manner the entrance to any fire station house.
(Code 1976, § 3-2007)
Sec. 46-5. - Fire department responsibility regarding hazardous material emergency response; recovery of cost.
(a)
Whenever there shall occur within the boundaries of the city any incident involving a chemical or material defined by federal, state or local law as a hazardous substance, or which is in fact hazardous in the natural state to the public health or safety, or when such chemical or material is involved in a release to the atmosphere, fire, chemical reaction, or detonation, and when the incident shall endanger any portion of the population or property of the citizens of the jurisdiction or shall expose anyone within such boundaries to potentially toxic or lethal agents, the fire department is authorized and directed to mitigate the incident.
(b)
Within this section, the "responsible person" shall be defined as the person or company operating any facility or vehicle involved in the incident or the property owner upon whose property the involved chemicals or materials were stored at the time of the incident.
(c)
All costs incurred by the city or any other agency or person involved in the mitigation of incidents defined in this section shall be borne by the responsible person.
(Ord. No. 96-11, § 3-2027, 3-21-1996)

ARTICLE II. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[2]

Footnotes:

Sec. 46-41. - Personnel.
The fire department of the city shall consist of such officers and members as the mayor and council may prescribe.
(Code 1976, § 3-2001)
Sec. 46-42. - Selection and government of members.
The fire chief and the other officers and members of the fire department shall be selected by the city manager and shall be subject to such lawful rules and regulations as may be adopted by the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 3-2002)
Sec. 46-43. - Fire chief's duty at fires.
The fire chief, and in his absence his designee, shall have sole direction of the operation and management of the fire department at fires, subject at all times to the orders of the city manager, and shall be clothed with the powers of a police officer of the city at fires.
(Code 1976, § 3-2003)
Sec. 46-44. - Power and duty of fire inspector as to flammables.
The fire marshal or fire inspector shall have the right and authority and it shall be their duty or that of either of them, upon discovering any unnecessary rubbish or other flammable or ignitable matter accumulated or stored in any building or on any premises described in this chapter, to order in writing that the matter be removed at once by the occupant of the building or premises or, if the building is unoccupied, by the owner or other person in charge.
(Code 1976, § 3-2004)

ARTICLE III. - OPEN BURNING

Sec. 46-81. - Permits and fees.
(a)
Permits for open burning at or on development sites for the purpose of clearing land for new construction shall be obtained from the office of the fire marshal.
(b)
The applicant shall complete an application for an open burning permit issued by the fire marshal's office. The applicant shall submit a plat or site plan showing the proposed location of the pit and information relating to the proximity of any occupied or unoccupied structure.
(c)
No burning shall commence prior to an inspection of the site and the pit by a representative of the fire marshal's office. Upon approval of the pit, the permit shall be valid for a period of 30 days. Permits may be extended for increments of 15-day periods, maximum of two extensions.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § 3-2030, 3-21-1996)
Sec. 46-82. - Fires requiring written permits.
(a)
The only type of fires permitted for the purpose of land clearing are pit fires. Nonpit fires are not permitted for land clearing within the city limits. All pit fires shall be conducted in strict accordance with the "Air Curtain Destructor Operating Guide and Procedures," as published by the Environmental Protection Division, attached to Ordinance No. 96-12 as Exhibit A.
(b)
Pit fires shall comply with the following stipulations:
(1)
The pit shall be at least 300 feet from any occupied or habitable structure. This distance may be increased if need is assessed by the fire marshal's office and validity exists.
(2)
Where a site cannot be excavated to the recommended depth due to high water table and/or subsurface rock, the pit may be constructed above ground level, subject to the same state standards and procedures for a pit below ground level.
(3)
Only wood wastes consisting of trees, logs, large brush, stumps, leaves, and untreated lumber may be burned in the pit.
(4)
Leaves, sawdust or other densely packed wood wastes, paper (any type), chemically treated, coated or impregnated wood, or nonwood construction materials shall not be burned. The amount of dirt/soil mixture shall be kept to a minimum.
(5)
No more than one air curtain destructor is allowed to be operated within a ten-acre permitted site at one time. The air curtain destructor must be under the supervision of a trained operator at all times during operation. If there is a need for multiple burning pits, there shall be a minimum of 1,000 feet between each burning pit.
(6)
Air curtain destructors shall be able to pass an even, nonturbulent flow of air across the top of the pit. Air curtain destructors with an air duct or manifold that has been altered by bends, dents, holes, etc., and are incapable of providing an even and nonturbulent flow of air across the top of the pit shall not be approved for use.
(7)
The air curtain destructor shall be operated in a manner to prevent air, land or water pollution, safety/health hazards or nuisances.
(8)
Tires or other rubber products, plastics, heavy oils or asphaltic base or impregnated materials shall not be used to start or maintain air curtain destructor operations.
(9)
No smoke emissions exceeding 40 percent opacity may be produced during operation except for a reasonable period during ignition and charging of the pit.
(10)
When the pit is cleared of ash, airborne particulate is to be minimized by wetting or mixing the ashes with dirt.
(11)
Diesel fuel or kerosene may be used to start pit fires.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § 3-2031, 3-21-1996)
Sec. 46-83. - Types of burning allowed without a written permit.
(a)
Fires for the purpose of keeping warm. Warming fires shall be contained within a metal barrel of 55-gallon capacity or less. Untreated wood or lumber shall be the only material or substance permitted to be burned. The container for the warming fire shall be not less than 50 feet from any structure. No on-ground warming fires shall be permitted. The outside temperature shall be at 50 degrees Fahrenheit or less.
(b)
Fires for cooking. Fires for the purpose of cooking food for human consumption do not require a written permit.
(c)
Bonfires.
(1)
No person shall kindle or maintain any bonfire or authorize any such fire to be kindled or maintained on any private or public land unless the location is no less than 50 feet from a structure with a size not to exceed 50 cubic feet (measuring two feet by five feet by five feet) of combustible materials and 100 feet from a structure with a size not to exceed 250 cubic feet (measuring ten feet by five feet by five feet) of combustible materials.
(2)
For purpose of clarification, bonfires shall be limited to activities sponsored by civic, educational, religious or other groups for purpose of celebration, etc. An example would be a high school pep rally.
(3)
Bonfires shall be constantly attended by a competent person until such fire is extinguished. Such person shall have a garden hose connected to a water supply, or other fire extinguishing equipment readily available for use. Access to communications to the fire department shall be maintained within 500 feet of the bonfire.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § 3-2032, 3-21-1996)
Sec. 46-84. - Burning restrictions.
The following rules govern all burning within the city limits, whether or not a written permit is required:
(1)
The fire marshal's office may temporarily prohibit any and/or all outdoor fires when atmospheric conditions or local circumstances make such fires hazardous.
(2)
No burning is permitted on Sundays or nights. Burning at construction sites for the clearing of land for new development shall not be conducted before or after daylight hours.
(3)
No burning is permitted when prevailing winds are in excess of 15 miles per hour in the direction of populated areas immediately adjacent to the permitted site.
(4)
No burning is permitted during an air pollution episode, such as an air pollution alert/warning/emergency declared by proper authorities.
(5)
No burning is permitted when there is a fog, rain or cloud bases that are diffused, or ill defined, or for at least one day following the passage of a cold front.
(6)
No burning of solid waste or household garbage is allowed.
(7)
No burning of any materials that emit a heavy dark smoke, such as rubber products, oils, roofing material, petroleum based products, etc., is allowed.
(8)
No burning when smoke presents a health hazard to persons in the vicinity of the fires is allowed.
(9)
No burning shall be conducted within 20 feet of flammable, combustible or explosive materials unless otherwise specified by the NFPA or Standard Fire Prevention Codes.
(10)
All fires shall be constantly attended by a competent person until extinguished.
(11)
There shall be a garden hose connected to a water supply or other fire extinguishing equipment readily available for use.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § 3-2033, 3-21-1996)
Sec. 46-85. - Summary abatement.
The staff of the fire marshal's office and personnel of the fire department shall have the authority to summarily abate any conditions which are in violation of any of the provisions of this article, and which may constitute an immediate threat to life and property. In no event shall a pit fire or other open burning continue after the fire marshal's office has revoked the permit.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § 3-2034, 3-21-1996)
