ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning. The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2 ("Alcoholic Beverages - Definitions"), shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this chapter. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this chapter, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Consumption on premises means the consumption of malt beverages or wine within the building wherein the same is sold and allows the licensee to pour the contents of the original package into a glass, mug or other container from which such beverages are customarily consumed and further allows the licensee to charge its customers by the glass, mug or other container from which such beverages are consumed. Malt beverages or wine purchased for consumption on premises shall not be taken or consumed outside the building wherein the same is sold.
Packaged and by the package mean by the bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container which shall not be opened inside the building where the same is sold nor on property outside of such building which is owned, rented, or controlled by the license holder.
(Code 2004, § 6-1; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 101, 12-11-2001)

ARTICLE II. - EXCISE AND LICENSE TAXES

Sec. 10-21. - Wholesale malt beverages and wine.
(a)
There is levied and imposed upon all wholesale malt beverages dealers an excise tax in the following amount:
(1)
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons;
(2)
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(b)
There is levied and imposed upon all wholesale wine dealers an excise tax in the amount of $0.22 per liter, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter, of wine sold by such wholesale dealers within the county.
(c)
Each wholesale dealer selling malt beverages or wine in the county shall file a report by the tenth day of each month showing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages and wine, by size and type of container, constituting a beginning and ending inventory for the month, sold within the county. Each such wholesale dealer shall remit to the county on the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made, the amount of excise tax due in accordance with this article.
(d)
No decal, stamp, or other identifying marking shall be required on malt beverages or wine sold within the county.
(e)
The excise tax provided for in this section shall be in addition to any license fee, tax or charge which may now or in the future be imposed upon the business of selling malt beverages or wine at retail or wholesale, within the county.
(f)
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to ten percent of the total amount due during the first 30-day period following the date such report and remittance were due and a further penalty of ten percent of the amount of such remittance for each successive 30-day period or any portion thereof during which such report and remittance are not filed. The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the wholesale dealer making such report liable for a penalty equal to 50 percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report.
(Code 2004, § 6-31; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 201, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-22. - Retail beer and wine dealers.
(a)
There is levied and imposed upon each retail malt beverage dealer within the county a privilege and license tax for the sale of malt beverages by the package only, a privilege and license tax for the sale of malt beverages for consumption on premises only, and a privilege and license tax for the sale of malt beverages by the package and for consumption on premises. The taxes imposed shall be paid with the license applications and annually thereafter on January 1.
(b)
There is levied and imposed upon each retail wine dealer within the county a privilege and license tax for the sale of wine by the package only and a privilege and license tax for the sale of wine for consumption on premises only and a privilege and license tax for the sale of wine by the package and for consumption on premises. The taxes imposed shall be paid with the license application and annually each year thereafter on January 1.
(c)
Any other provisions of this chapter to the contrary notwithstanding, the annual license taxes referred to in subsections (a) and (b) of this section shall be non-prorated license taxes and the full amount of the taxes will be required to be paid regardless of what time of year the licenses are issued.
(d)
The annual license taxes referred to in this section shall be in amounts as established by the board of commissioners, from time to time.
(Code 2004, § 6-32; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 202, 12-11-2001)

ARTICLE III. - LICENSES

Sec. 10-48. - Punishment for violation.
The violation of any of the provisions of this article shall be unlawful and shall be punished as provided in section 1-14.
(Code 2004, § 6-61; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 401, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-49. - Required; revocation and suspension.
(a)
The privilege of engaging in the business of a retail malt beverage or wine dealer shall in no event be granted to any person except by the issuance of a license as provided in this article, and such privilege shall be held and enjoyed by the licensee only so long as such licensee shall comply fully with the requirements of this article and promptly pay all taxes provided for in this article.
(b)
No retail malt beverage or wine license shall be issued:
(1)
To a person who has been convicted of any felony within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application, or to a person who has been convicted of any violation of any provision of O.C.G.A. title 3, chapter 3 within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application, or to a person who has been convicted of the offense of selling or furnishing alcoholic beverages as violation of a municipal or county ordinance within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application, or to a person who has been convicted of any two other misdemeanors or violations of municipal or county ordinances, except minor traffic violations, within a period of five years immediately prior to the filing of such application. For the purposes of this subsection, the term "convicted" or " conviction" shall include an adjudication of guilt or a plea of guilty, or a plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of bond.
(2)
To a person or firm whose place of business is conducted by a manager or other employees, unless such manager or other employees possess the same qualifications required by the license holder. By making application for a license under this article, an applicant, his manager and other employees consent and subject themselves to an investigation by the sheriff's department for the purpose of determining the fitness of the holder of the license or his manager or employees, to be granted such license or to manage or be employed in the licensed location, as the case may be.
(3)
For a location not properly zoned for the conduct of the business contemplated in the application.
(4)
For a location that does not have sufficient parking to accommodate the patrons of the business contemplated in the application.
(5)
For a location at which a previous alcoholic beverage license has been revoked or suspended unless the conditions which led to the previous revocation or suspension of such license no longer exist.
(6)
Where the granting of such license would constitute a violation of state law or regulation, or where there is a failure to meet the requirements for a state license for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(7)
To any person or firm seeking an alcoholic beverage license authorizing consumption on the premises unless such person or firm agrees to the suspension and surrender of such person's or firm's business license as provided in subsection (f) of this section.
(8)
To any person who provides false information in his application for an alcoholic beverage license, or in any documents attached thereto, or who fails to pay the required fees and taxes.
(9)
For any location within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, college campus, or alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government in the state, as measured by a straight line from the property line of the tract on which is located the business seeking an alcoholic beverage license to the property line of such school building, school grounds, college campus, or alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government in the state.
(c)
Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, an alcoholic beverage license may be renewed or reissued to an applicant holding a valid alcoholic beverage license on December 11, 2001, but only for the same location and class of consumption as is permitted under the then existing alcoholic beverage license.
(d)
The chairman of the board of commissioners shall have the ability to suspend any license issued under this article:
(1)
Whenever the license holder or his manager or other employees violate any of the terms of this article.
(2)
When the license holder, his manager or any other employee shall be charged with the violation of any federal, state or municipal criminal statute or ordinance.
(3)
For the failure of the license holder to ascertain and require that his managers and employees be and remain qualified to work and manage at the licensed location as contemplated by subsections (b)(1) and (2) of this section.
(4)
Where the conduct of the license holder's business constitutes a public nuisance or a disorderly house, as defined under state law.
(e)
In the event of a suspension, the chairman shall report such suspension at the next regular meeting of the board of commissioners, at which time the board shall decide whether to reinstate the license, continue the suspension or revoke the license, upon such terms and conditions as to the board may seem fit and proper.
(f)
If the board of commissioners continues the suspension of an alcoholic beverage license which authorizes on-premises consumption, or suspends or revokes such a license, the business license of the holder of such alcoholic beverage license authorizing on-premises consumption shall be suspended for a period of six months, which six-month period shall commence to run from the date that the board of commissioners took action to continue the suspension of the alcoholic beverage license, suspend or revoke such license, as the case may be.
(g)
All decisions approving, denying, suspending, or revoking any alcoholic beverage license shall be in writing, with the reasons therefor stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the applicant or license holder, as the case may be.
(h)
Any applicant or license holder, as the case may be, who is aggrieved by the decision of the board of commissioners, shall be afforded a hearing with an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine opposing witnesses, upon application made within ten days of such decision. Such hearing shall be held at the next regularly scheduled meeting of the board of commissioners.
(Code 2004, § 6-62; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 301, 12-11-2001; Ord. No. 2011-001, § 1, 5-10-2011)
Sec. 10-50. - Application; information and agreement therein.
Before any license required by this article shall be issued to any person, such person shall file with the county clerk a written application therefor, which application shall have attached thereto a certified check in payment of the initial tax provided for in section 10-21 and which application shall contain the following information and agreement:
(1)
Full name, address, social security number and birth date of the applicant and the applicant's manager or agent if different from the applicant, and whether the applicant is an individual, partnership or corporation; if the applicant is a partnership, the names and addresses of each partner must be shown.
(2)
The location or address of the place or business at which the applicant will engage in the retail malt beverage or wine business, if and when permission to do so is granted by the board of commissioners and a license issued.
(3)
The application shall also indicate whether the applicant seeks authority to sell malt beverages or wine by the package or for consumption on premises or both, and such application shall also contain such other information as the county clerk may, under the direction of the board of commissioners, from time to time, require.
(Code 2004, § 6-63; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 302, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-51. - Publication of application.
All information contained in the application for the privilege and license to sell malt beverages or wine in the county shall be published in the official newspaper of the county, once a week for four consecutive weeks immediately preceding the week in which the meeting to act upon the application shall be held.
(Code 2004, § 6-64; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 303, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-52. - Action on application; license issuance.
(a)
All applications for a license required by this article filed with the board shall be presented to the board of commissioners in a meeting duly assembled and, after investigating the fitness of the applicant and the applicant's manager or agent, if different from the applicant, and the fitness of the proposed location of the retail malt beverage or wine dealership, the board of commissioners shall approve or disapprove the application.
(b)
In case such application is disapproved, the clerk shall return to the applicant the check submitted with his application. In case such application is approved, the clerk shall issue a proper license to the applicant authorizing the applicant to engage in the business of a retail malt beverage or wine dealer at the business location designated in the application. It shall be indicated on the license whether the application is for authority to sell malt beverages or wine by the package or for consumption on premises.
(Code 2004, § 6-65; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 304, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-53. - Licenses nontransferable; expiration.
Licenses required by this article shall be applicable to and valid with respect only to the business location referred to therein, and shall not be transferable to any person other than the named licensee. Such licenses shall expire upon and be of no validity after December 31 next after their issuance.
(Code 2004, § 6-66; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 305, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-54. - Sale on election days.
The sale of alcoholic beverages in the county shall be permitted on election days pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20(2)(B) subject to any and all restrictions and limitations contained in this article.
(Code 2004, § 6-67; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 306, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-55. - Hours of sale.
(a)
No malt beverages or wine shall be sold by a licensee under this article except during the following hours: 6:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight, Monday through Saturday, and 12:00 midnight to 1:30 a.m. on Saturday.
(b)
No business establishment in which malt beverages or wines are licensed to be sold under this article for consumption on premises shall remain open between the hours of 12:30 a.m. and 6:00 a.m., Monday through Friday. No business establishment in which malt beverages or wines are licensed to be sold under this article for consumption on premises shall remain open between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. on Saturday. No person, except the owner, operator, license holder or his employee, shall be or remain on the premises of such business establishment during the hours when it is closed. On Sundays, between the hours of 12:30 a.m. and 12:00 midnight, no alcoholic beverage shall be possessed or consumed on the premises of any business establishment in which malt beverages or wines are licensed to be sold under this article for consumption on the premises.
(c)
The provisions of this section notwithstanding, no malt beverages shall be sold at a time prohibited by state law.
(d)
Every licensee under this article shall report to the sheriff of the county within the 24 hours thereof each and every incident in which there is a disturbance or breach of the peace or other crime committed on or about the licensed premises.
(Code 2004, § 6-68; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 307, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 10-56. - Possession or consumption of alcoholic beverages outside of licensed premises unlawful.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or consume alcoholic beverages on the premises of a license holder under this article except within the building on such premises as defined and allowed by this chapter.
(b)
It shall also be unlawful for any person to go upon or remain upon the premises of a license holder except for the purpose of becoming a customer who shall, without delay, enter the building after arriving at the premises and who shall, without delay, leave the premises after exiting such building.
(Code 2004, § 6-69; Ord. No. 2001-0019, § 308, 12-11-2001)
