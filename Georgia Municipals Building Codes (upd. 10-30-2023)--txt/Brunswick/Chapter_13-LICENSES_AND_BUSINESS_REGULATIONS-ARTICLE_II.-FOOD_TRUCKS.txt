ARTICLE II. - FOOD TRUCKS

Sec. 13-26. - Purpose.
The purpose of this section is to allow for the licensing and regulation of "food trucks" operating within the city limits of the City of Brunswick. In the interest of the public health, safety and welfare, the mayor and board of commissioners may exercise limited discretion in evaluating the proposed operation of a "food truck," or the like which requires a license in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-27 - Definitions.
Food means a raw, cooked, or processed edible substance, ice, beverage, or ingredient used or intended for use or for sale in whole or in part for human consumption, or chewing gum.
Food truck means a licensed, readily movable motorized wheeled vehicle or trailer, which is temporarily stored on a premise where food items are sold to the general public. Included in the definition are vehicles and equipment defined as "mobile food service unit" and/or "extended food service unit" contained and amended in Rules of Department of Public Health, Ga. Comp. R. and Regs. 511-6-1-.01 and 511-6-1-.08.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-28. - Food truck vendor requirements.
(a)
Prior to an application for any license to operate a food truck in the City of Brunswick, the food truck vendor shall first submit for inspection and be approved by the Glynn County Health Department, or by the Georgia Department of Agriculture if selling commercially packaged food.
(b)
The food truck vendor must obtain an annual City of Brunswick Business Tax Certificate. During the food truck's hours of operation, the vendor shall at all times display a current business tax certificate. The food truck vendor shall renew any business tax certificate in accordance with chapter 20 of this Code.
(c)
The food truck vendor shall obtain and display the Glynn County Health Department-issued Georgia Department of Public Health Mobile Food Service Permit at all times during its hours of operations.
(d)
The food truck vendor shall obtain written authorization from the property owner or registered agent of the property for food vending in writing. Such authorization must be maintained and accessible in the truck at all times during the hours of operations.
(e)
Prior to the issuance of a business tax certificate, the food truck vendor shall make available the operating food truck for inspection by the City of Brunswick Fire Department. In order to obtain the business tax certificate, the food truck vendor shall provide proof that the truck has been inspected by the City of Brunswick Fire Department and the truck is in compliance with the City of Brunswick Fire Department Mobile Food Vehicle Fire Safety and Specifications Practices.
(f)
Food truck vendors shall comply with all city, county, and state retail sales tax regulations.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-29. - Location requirements.
Food trucks shall locate on privately owned lots zoned local commercial, general commercial, GC-CORE, or highway commercial with all-weather surface (or gravel) parking areas, accessible by emergency vehicles, and having at least ten legally designated off-street parking spaces, subject to the approval of the property owner and subject to the following:
(a)
Written notice to the Glynn County Health Department seven days prior to operations begin. Such notice shall state the expected dates of operation and shall specify the physical location of operations.
(b)
The food truck shall be positioned at least 50 feet from the customer entrance of an existing restaurant during the food truck's hours of operation;
(c)
No food truck shall be operated within 600 feet of any public school in the city between the hours of 7:30 a.m. and 3:30 p.m. on days in which schools are actually in session;
(d)
The food truck shall be positioned on designated parking spaces and shall not block drive aisles, other access to loading/service areas, or emergency access and fire lanes;
(e)
No food truck shall be located within a building setback or landscape buffer;
(f)
The food truck must be positioned at least 15 feet away from fire hydrants, driveway entrances, alleys, handicapped parking spaces, tree trunks, and vegetation;
(g)
Food trucks shall not be located within 100 feet of gas pumps, underground gas storage tanks, and vent pipes;
(h)
Food trucks may not be located in any portion of a parking lot when and where such location would prevent the use of parking spaces during the regular hours of operation of the primary business on the lot, or otherwise interfere in a significant [manner] with the movement of motor vehicles, unless prior permission to use the parking spots has been provided by the property owner;
(i)
Cooking off the vehicle with a grill or smoker is prohibited;
(j)
No seating shall be provided, although high top tables are allowed but shall not exceed three high top tables total;
(k)
Vehicle-mounted roll-out awnings are allowed; and
(l)
Vehicle must vacate premises by 10:00 p.m. and return to base of operation daily.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-30. - Hours of operation.
No food truck shall be located in public space between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 5:00 a.m.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-31. - Safety and utilities.
(a)
Temporary connections to potable water are prohibited. All plumbing and electrical connections shall be in accordance with the building code.
(b)
Grease is to be disposed of in recyclable grease container at an existing restaurant or commissary (not in dumpsters or storm drains).
(c)
Grey water to be disposed of in the sanitary sewer at an existing restaurant or commissary (no open dumping or into storm drains) per Brunswick Public Works Department regulations.
(d)
Food trucks must comply with Brunswick Fire Department Mobile Food Vendor Fire Safety Specifications and Practices. The Brunswick Fire Department will inspect for compliance before a business license will be issued.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-32. - Nuisance prevention.
(a)
The food truck vendor shall not operate the food truck as a drive-in window.
(b)
The noise level from the food truck motor and generator or any other source must comply with the noise ordinance.
(c)
No vehicle using sound equipment or other method of attracting customers shall operate such equipment before 9:00 a.m. or after 9:00 p.m. daily or between the hours of 9:30 a.m. and 12:00 noon on Sundays. Furthermore, such equipment shall not be operated within one block of a church, or within one block of an existing restaurant, between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 9:00 p.m. on Sundays.
(d)
A covered trash receptacle shall be provided for customers. The food truck vendor and property owner shall be responsible for daily cleanup and proper disposal of litter.
(e)
If the food truck is proposed to operate after dark, the food truck vendor shall provide a lighting plan which includes use of shielded fixtures and shall not include spotlights. Illumination shall be designed and installed in a manner that minimizes upward light pollution. A combination of shielding, screening, and directing the lighting away from areas beyond the vehicle sign, together with the use of landscaping, if available, to soften the impact of reflected light, shall be used.
(f)
No signage shall be allowed other than:
(1)
signs permanently attached to the motor vehicle;
(2)
a portable, non-internally illuminated menu sign no more than six square feet in display area and no more than four feet in height that is placed on the ground in the customer waiting area.
(3)
Temporary signs in accordance with section 23-24-13(u) shall not be permitted on a parcel when a food truck is located on that parcel.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-33. - Supplemental use regulations for public property or certain city-owned facilities.
Food truck vendors may apply to operate on public property by submitting an request to the city manager's office. Any request must include proof that the regulations set forth in this article have been met. The city manager shall review each application and approve or reject the same.
A food truck vendor may operate on the following city-owned facilities: Mary Ross Waterfront Park, Sidney Lanier Park or other city parks as may be available where approved in advance upon proper written explanation to the facility manager designated for such purpose by the city commission, and subject to such terms and conditions as the said facility manager may from time to time promulgate in writing, which may include terms as to insurance, clean-up fees, deposits, security personnel, limits on the number of guests and other matters as the facility manager may require.
This section shall only serve to allow the operation of a food truck on public property under the specific circumstances set forth herein. Nothing in this code section shall release a food truck vendor from the location requirements set forth in section 13-29 or any other regulation or requirement set forth in sections 13-28 through 13-34.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
Sec. 13-34. - Penalty for violation of article.
All persons, firms, or corporations failing to comply with the mandatory provisions of this article or doing any act prohibited in this article shall be guilty of an offense, and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 15-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 1044, § 1, 11-4-2015)
