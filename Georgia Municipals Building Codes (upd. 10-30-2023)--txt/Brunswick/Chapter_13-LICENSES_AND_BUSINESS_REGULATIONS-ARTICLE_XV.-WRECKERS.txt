DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 13-556. - Title.
This article shall be known and cited as "the wrecker ordinance."
(Ord. No. 832, § 1, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-557. - Definitions.
Unless the context specifically indicates otherwise, the meaning of terms used in this article shall be as follows:
Chief means the chief of the police department of the city or any officer specifically designated by him to administer and carry out the provisions of this article.
Disabled means a motor vehicle disabled on a public way or other public place in such a manner as to obstruct vehicular or pedestrian travel or access to public or private property.
Operator means and includes all persons owning or operating wreckers within a wrecker service in the city and all officers, agents and employees of such persons who are engaged in the business of the wrecker service.
Wrecker means an automotive vehicle equipped and used for the purpose of towing or hauling wrecked or disabled automobiles or other vehicles.
Wrecker service means any person regularly engaged in the business of towing or hauling wrecked or disabled motor vehicles for hire within the city whether or not such wrecker service maintains an office within the city limits.
(Ord. No. 832, § 2, 2-19-1986)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 13-558. - Administration and enforcement.
It shall be the duty of the chief to administer and carry out the provisions of this article. All law enforcement officers of the city shall have authority to prosecute violations of this article.
(Ord. No. 832, § 3, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-559. - Penalty for violations.
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions or sections of this article, shall be punished upon conviction in the municipal court in accordance with section 1-6.
(Ord. No. 832, § 26, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-560. - Compliance required.
It shall be unlawful for any wrecker service or operator to tow or haul any motor vehicle which has been wrecked, abandoned or disabled on a public way or other public place within the jurisdiction of the city from the scene of the wreck, abandonment or disablement except in compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 832, § 4, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-561. - Removal of debris.
The removal of broken glass and other debris from the scene of an accident shall be part of the wrecker service's obligation pursuant to this article. When two or more different wrecker services are assigned to one accident, each shall be equally responsible for cleaning the wreck area.
(Ord. No. 832, § 9, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-562. - Use of emergency light.
A wrecker operator shall not display the amber emergency light except upon arrival at the scene where a call originated and when leaving the scene with a vehicle in tow.
(Ord. No. 832, § 10, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-563. - Obedience to traffic laws.
Each wrecker operator shall abide by all applicable traffic regulations.
(Ord. No. 832, § 10, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-564. - Responsibility for violations.
Each wrecker operator shall be responsible for any violation of any applicable law, rule or regulation incurred in the process of responding to a call.
(Ord. No. 832, § 10, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-565. - Storage area.
(a)
Each wrecker service shall maintain an area for storage of towed vehicles either within or without the city, sufficient to provide storage in a manner not inconsistent with applicable zoning ordinances and other laws. If outside the city, the storage area shall be no more than three miles from the city limits by the shortest route of vehicular travel.
(b)
The storage area shall be enclosed or fenced, shall be securely locked at all times when unattended, shall be lighted during night time, and shall be used for temporary storage only.
(c)
Towed or hauled vehicles shall not be parked or left standing on any city right-of-way at any time or under any circumstances.
(d)
Stored vehicles shall be subject to reclamation 24 hours a day.
(Ord. No. 832, § 11, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 837, § 2, 5-7-1986)
Sec. 13-566. - Comprehensive liability insurance coverage.
In addition to the motor vehicle liability insurance required by state law, each wrecker service shall maintain general comprehensive liability insurance on business operations providing a minimum of $25,000.00 single-limit coverage per occurrence. The chief may require each wrecker service to furnish copies of its insurance certificates showing the amounts of coverage and expiration of the policies required by this article and other laws.
(Ord. No. 832, § 12, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-567. - Identification of vehicles.
Each wrecker shall have the name, address and telephone number of its business displayed on both sides of the vehicle. Such information shall be permanently painted onto the side of the vehicle in block letters of contrasting color not less than 2½ inches in height. Attachment to the vehicle of removable signs for this purpose is strictly prohibited.
(Ord. No. 832, § 13, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-568. - Solicitation of business prohibited.
It shall be a violation of this article for any wrecker operator, wrecker service or other person to solicit business at the scene of a motor vehicle accident or other vehicular disablement, whether such solicitation be conducted from a wrecker vehicle, private automobile, other vehicle or on foot.
(Ord. No. 832, § 17, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-569. - Removal of vehicles restricted.
No vehicle shall be removed from the scene of a wreck, abandonment or disablement until it is released by the investigating officers. The chief or other officers of the city police department may direct that a vehicle to be removed shall be impounded and towed or hauled to the city's impound lot, and in such event it shall be a violation of this article for any wrecker operator to remove a vehicle to any other location.
(Ord. No. 832, § 19, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-570. - Records.
After towing or hauling a wrecked or disabled vehicle, a wrecker service shall immediately make a written record of the following information, which shall be maintained for a period of one year and which shall be made available for inspection and copying by the chief at all reasonable business hours:
(1)
The name of the owner of the vehicle;
(2)
The make, model and serial number of the vehicle;
(3)
The date and time towed or hauled;
(4)
The location of the wreck or disablement;
(5)
The place where such vehicle was stored or other disposition of the vehicle; and
(6)
An itemization of the charges made by the wrecker service for towing the vehicle and the charges made or accruing for storage of the same.
(Ord. No. 832, § 20, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 928, § 3, 11-20-1996)
Secs. 13-571—13-585. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - ROTATION LIST

Sec. 13-586. - Use required; exceptions.
At all times when necessary for the police department to remove any motor vehicle for any cause from any place within the jurisdiction of the city, no wrecker shall be used to tow or haul such vehicle unless that wrecker is on the city's rotation system; provided, however, that this article shall not be construed to prohibit the police department from using a wrecker owned by the city to tow or haul an abandoned vehicle or a vehicle to be impounded in connection with a criminal investigation or to prohibit the use of a wrecker not on the rotation system under circumstances where police jurisdiction is not involved. For purposes of this section, police jurisdiction shall be deemed to exist in all cases of vehicular wreck or collision, all cases in which a criminal offense is under investigation, all cases in which a vehicular disablement results in obstruction of use of or travel on a public place or way, and all cases of abandonment of a vehicle on a public place or way.
(Ord. No. 832, § 5, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 837, § 1, 5-7-1986)
Sec. 13-587. - Application for placement.
In order to be placed on the rotation system, the owner or operator of a wrecker shall file an application with the chief, supplying the following information:
(a)
Title certificate number of the wrecker and date issued and the name, home and business address and telephone number(s) of the owner or owners of the wrecker.
(b)
Complete home and business address and telephone numbers of the principal operator of the wrecker.
(c)
Description of the wrecker to be registered, including the make, model, manufacturer's serial number and capacities.
(d)
Names of the wrecker service operating the wrecker and all persons having a financial interest therein.
(e)
Persons, including all agents and employees, authorized to operate the wrecker and class of driver's license held by each operator.
(f)
A current three-year certified Motor Vehicle Report (MVR) from the Georgia Department of Driver Services (DDS) for each driver who will be operating a wrecker or tow truck.
(g)
The location and telephone number of the office or garage to and from which the wrecker is operator.
(h)
The name and address of the insurance company or companies providing motor vehicle liability coverage for the wrecker and wrecker service and general comprehensive liability coverage for business operations, the policy number of each insurance policy, and the policy limits provided by each policy.
(i)
Proof of a minimum of six months pre-paid insurance coverage or a certificate of insurance.
(j)
A copy of the company's current City of Brunswick business license.
(k)
A copy of the motor vehicle registration for the wrecker.
(Ord. No. 832, § 6, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 1045, § 1, 9-21-2016)
Sec. 13-588. - Inspection of vehicles.
All wreckers shall undergo annual safety compliance inspections in compliance with the department of transportation's motor carrier compliance division. Proof of the annual safety inspections shall be submitted to the chief before approving the application for placement on the rotation system. Thereafter, the chief shall inspect each wrecker's pertinent documents annually. Each wrecker owner may provide multiple wrecker units so long as each vehicle is inspected in accordance with this article. Provided however, that a wrecker owner or company shall only be afforded one slot on the wrecker list.
(Ord. No. 832, § 7, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 928, § 2, 11-20-1996; Ord. No. 1045, § 1, 9-21-2016)
Sec. 13-589. - Minimum specifications, equipment.
The following items and equipment must be maintained on all wreckers and tow trucks:
(a)
Each vehicle must be permanently marked on both sides of the body or cab of the vehicle, in letters and figures in sharp color contrast to the background and legible from a distance of 50 feet during daylight hours while the vehicle is stationary, with:
(1)
The name of the motor carrier (wrecker company) city of address, and telephone number; and
(2)
The carrier's state or federal USDOT number;
(b)
Valid registration tag and revalidation decal;
(c)
Valid emergency light permit issued by DPS;
(d)
Flashing or revolving amber light with 360-degree visibility;
(e)
Battery jumper cables;
(f)
Fire extinguisher;
(g)
Sweeping broom;
(h)
Flat shovel;
(i)
Container for debris;
(j)
Motorcycle straps (4); and
(k)
Tow away lamps (tail, stop and turn signal lights for vehicles being towed); and
(l)
Stopped vehicle warning devices (e.g. three bi-directional reflective triangles).
(Ord. No. 832, § 8, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 1045, § 1, 9-21-2016)
Sec. 13-590. - Preparation of call list.
It shall be the duty of the chief or his/her designee to prepare and maintain a rotational call list of wreckers which meet the qualifications, standards, and requirements enumerated in this article. Each wrecker company shall be afforded one slot on the wrecker list.
(Ord. No. 832, § 14, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 1045, § 1, 9-21-2016)
Sec. 13-591. - Use of call list—Generally.
(a)
At all times when necessary for the police department to remove any vehicle for any cause, the police radio operator, or such other person as may be designated by the chief, shall engage the services of those on the rotational call list by calling them in alphabetical order and in rotation, provided, however, that deviation from the alphabetical order shall be permitted if:
(1)
The next wrecker service on rotation does not have a wrecker available with sufficient capacity to handle the call, or
(2)
The owner or person in possession of a vehicle or person in possession of a vehicle about to be removed designates a wrecker service of his preference. In the event such person designates a wrecker service of his preference, that wrecker service shall be called whether or not it is next on the rotational call list, and such designation shall not interfere with such wrecker service's position on the call list.
(b)
If it is necessary to skip over one or more wrecker services because they do not have a wrecker with sufficient capacity to handle a particular call, the wrecker service or services skipped over shall not lose position on the rotation list, but the wrecker service which handles the call shall lose position.
(Ord. No. 832, § 15, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 837, § 3, 5-7-1986)
Sec. 13-592. - Same—Unauthorized.
All calls to wrecker services shall be made by the police radio operator, or such other person as may be designated by the chief, and in no event shall an investigating officer undertake to call directly for a wrecker service. Police officers are strictly forbidden from recommending any particular wrecker service to any person, and any police officer violating this provision shall be subject to appropriate disciplinary action.
(Ord. No. 832, § 16, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-593. - Call of wrecker to scene.
It shall be a violation of this article for any wrecker operator to proceed to the scene of an accident or vehicular disablement unless called as provided hereinabove. Upon being so called, a wrecker operator shall not block traffic and shall maintain his vehicle in such position as not to impede emergency vehicles and normal vehicular traffic.
(Ord. No. 832, § 18, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-594. - Fees.
A wrecker service shall pay an annual fee of $50.00 for placement on the rotation system and an application fee of $50.00. Such fees shall be paid to the City of Brunswick Finance Department for administrative costs associated with the enforcement of this article. There shall be no gross receipts tax or other license fees for wrecker operations, but such tax may apply to other business activities of a wrecker service.
(Ord. No. 832, § 21, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 1045, § 1, 9-21-2016)
Sec. 13-595. - Charges.
(a)
The mayor and city commission shall set forth an appropriate fee schedule to be binding on the wrecker services included on the rotational list. This fee schedule may be reviewed not more than once annually.
(b)
As approved in paragraph (a) of this section, no wrecker service shall charge more than the following fees for services provided when responding to a call received from the rotational call list:
(1)
Attaching and towing or hauling a vehicle .....$125.00
(2)
Storage per day, starting at 12:01 a.m. of the next calendar date following the tow date, per day .....$15.00
(3)
Outdoor storage, per day .....$10.00
(4)
All other services performed within a reasonable time, including but not limited to, righting an overturned vehicle, removing vehicle from marsh, cleaning accident scene, etc., to be prorated, per hour .....$30.00
(5)
Righting an overturned vehicle or winching, when recovery is more than 20 feet from edge of roadway or is in water or marsh, per hour .....$35.00
(Ord. No. 832, § 22, 2-19-1986; Ord. No. 837, § 4, 5-7-1986; Ord. No. 928, § 1, 11-20-1996; Ord. No. 1045, § 1, 9-21-2016)
Sec. 13-596. - When suspension authorized.
The chief may suspend the wreckers of any wrecker service from the rotation system upon any of the following grounds:
(1)
Charging fees in excess of those approved by the commission.
(2)
Failure of a wrecker to pass inspection.
(3)
Any violation of the provisions of this article or other law.
(Ord. No. 832, § 23, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-597. - Mandatory suspension.
A court conviction of violation of section 13-568 or section 13-593 shall result in mandatory suspension of the wrecker service for a period of one year.
(Ord. No. 832, § 24, 2-19-1986)
Sec. 13-598. - Notice of suspension.
Any wrecker service suspended from the rotation list by the chief shall be notified in writing of the suspension to take effect within ten days after notice, the reasons therefor in writing, the witnesses relied upon by the chief, and that the wrecker service shall be granted a hearing upon request before the city manager within such ten-day period.
(Ord. No. 832, § 25, 2-19-1986)
Secs. 13-599, 13-600. - Reserved.
