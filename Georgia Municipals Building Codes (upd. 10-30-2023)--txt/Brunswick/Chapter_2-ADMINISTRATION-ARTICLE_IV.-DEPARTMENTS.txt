DIVISION 2. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Fire prevention and protection, Ch. 10.
State Law reference— Local fire departments generally, O.C.G.A. § 25-3-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-186. - Composition.
The fire department shall consist of one chief and such subordinate officers and firefighters as the city commission may from time to time prescribe.
(Code 1953, § 16.1)
Sec. 2-187. - Order of command at fires.
At all fires the chief shall be supreme in command of the department and the entire apparatus that may be employed for the extinguishment of fires. In his absence the command shall devolve on the assistant chief. In the absence of both, the oldest fireman in commission present shall take command.
(Code 1953, § 16.2)
Sec. 2-188. - Examination of equipment, etc.; recommendations to city manager.
The chief of the fire department shall examine, or cause to be examined, twice a year or oftener, if he deems it necessary, the condition of the engine houses, engines, hose and all other apparatus of the department, and make a report of the same to the city manager, making such recommendations as to an enlargement of his department and the increase of its efficiency as he may deem proper.
(Code 1953, § 16.3)
Sec. 2-189. - Exclusive use of streets during fires.
The fire department shall have the free and exclusive use of the streets during a fire.
(Code 1953, § 16.4)
Sec. 2-190. - Unlawful to go near equipment in operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person not connected with the fire department of the city to go within 450 feet of any engine or other machine of the fire department while the same is engaged in fighting a fire in the city, unless such person shall have express permission so to do from the chief or other person in command of the fire department.
(Code 1953, § 16.5)
Sec. 2-191. - Interference with work or practice.
No person shall in any manner obstruct or interfere with the work or practice of the fire department.
(Code 1953, § 16.6)
Sec. 2-192. - Injuring, etc., department property.
No person shall handle, tamper with or in any manner interfere with or injure any property of the fire department.
(Code 1953, § 16.7)
Sec. 2-193. - Obstructing engine house, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct with rubbish or in any other way an entrance to any engine house, or other place where any apparatus of the fire department shall be stored or kept.
(Code 1953, § 16.8)
Sec. 2-194. - Bringing intoxicating liquor into engine house, etc.
Any member of the fire department who shall bring any intoxicating liquor into the engine house or yard, or outhouses to the same, shall be suspended ten days for the first offense and for the second offense, shall be discharged.
(Code 1953, § 16.9)
Sec. 2-195. - Discharge of intoxicated members.
Any member of the fire department who shall be under the influence of intoxicating liquor, either on or off duty, shall be discharged.
(Code 1953, § 16.10)
Sec. 2-196. - Disobedience of members.
Any member of the fire department guilty of disobedience either about the engine house or at a fire shall be suspended or discharged at the discretion of the chief.
(Code 1953, § 16.11)
Sec. 2-197. - Use of profane or indecent language.
Any member of the fire department using profane or indecent language around the engine houses or yards, or outhouses, or at a fire shall be suspended one day.
(Code 1953, § 16.12)
Sec. 2-198. - Leaving post of duty without permission.
Any member of the fire department leaving his post of duty without permission shall be suspended or discharged at the discretion of the chief.
(Code 1953, § 16.13)
Sec. 2-199. - Penalty.
Any person violating or failing to comply with any of the provisions of this division shall, upon conviction, be punished in accordance with section 1-6.
(Code 1953, § 16.14)

DIVISION 3. - DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC WORKS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Cross reference— Cemeteries and burials, ch. 7; streets and sidewalks, ch. 19; utilities, ch. 22.

Sec. 2-216. - Created.
The department of public works, as separate from the department of engineering, is hereby created.
(Ord. No. 511, art. II, § 1, 12-15-1965)
Sec. 2-217. - Director—Qualifications.
The head of the department of public works shall be its director who shall have the following qualifications at the time of his appointment:
(1)
Be a person of at least 21 years of age.
(2)
Have knowledge, experience and practice in the working of skilled and nonskilled laborers.
(3)
A general knowledge of material, methods and techniques commonly used in semi-skilled and laboring activities.
(4)
Ability and experience in supervising the work of a crew performing maintenance or minor construction functions and to obtain effective results.
(5)
Ability to understand and carry out written and oral instructions.
(6)
Ability to read and interpret blueprints and working drawings.
(7)
Skilled in the use of various hand and machine tools related to construction work.
(8)
Experience in supervising and planning construction and maintenance work.
(9)
Practice, experience and knowledge in the maintenance of automotive and mechanical machines and devices.
(10)
Experience, knowledge and practice in horticultural work and planning.
(11)
Knowledge and experience in setting up and carrying out safety programs in all work undertaken.
(12)
Setting up and maintaining work schedules and giving special instructions as required.
(13)
Have knowledge of and experience in water treatment and sewage collection and distribution systems and operation thereof.
(Ord. No. 511, art. II, § 2, 12-15-1965)
Sec. 2-218. - Same—Powers and duties.
The director of public works shall have charge of the supervision, maintenance and direction of work crews performing construction, maintenance and repair of all city property, city streets, sidewalks, parks, lanes, alleys within the city; and shall be required to:
(1)
Prepare, grade and pave and repair streets, sidewalks; make excavations, ditches, drains, bridges and similar projects and repair the same.
(2)
Inspect all work from day to day as the same progresses on any project coming within his jurisdiction.
(3)
Construct, maintain, repair and improve all water lines and sewer lines within the city.
(4)
Inspect, repair and improve all sidewalks in the city.
(5)
Install, maintain and repair traffic signs, mark traffic lines in streets and install and maintain signs naming streets.
(6)
Report to the city manager promptly any unsanitary condition existing in the city.
(7)
Have charge of city cemeteries and supervise the creation and maintenance of same.
(8)
Supervise the cutting of grass, removing of trees and stumps, digging and filling holes and trenches and relating ground streets and sidewalk maintenance.
(9)
Instruct subordinates in safety methods of work and maintenance of equipment.
(10)
Supervise the collection and disposal of all garbage and trash within the city.
(11)
Supervise and have charge of the operation of water and sewage systems and distributions plants.
(12)
Do all acts and things and to perform all work and service as from time to time shall be required by the city manager relating to public works.
(Ord. No. 511, art. II, § 3, 12-15-1965)

DIVISION 4. - DEPARTMENT OF RECREATION[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Establishment of recreation systems, O.C.G.A. § 36-64-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-236. - Established.
There is hereby created and established a department of the city to be known as and called the department of recreation.
(Ord. No. 599, § 1, 8-5-1970)
Sec. 2-237. - Powers and duties.
The department of recreation of the city is empowered to provide, maintain and conduct playgrounds, athletic fields, recreation centers and other recreational activities and facilities, and to maintain and equip playgrounds, athletic fields, recreation centers and buildings thereon, employ play leaders, playground directors, supervisors, recreation superintendents or such other officers and employees as it deems proper. The department of recreation shall have the power to conduct any form of recreation or cultural activity that will employ the leisure time of the people in a constructive and wholesome manner, and for such purposes is empowered to carry on such facilities and activities on any of the properties owned or controlled by the city, with the consent of the city commission, or on other properties with the consent of the city commission and owners thereof and shall have the power to join in and cooperate with other governments in connection with recreational activities.
(Ord. No. 599, § 2, 8-5-1970)
Sec. 2-238. - Fiscal authority.
The department of recreation shall submit a budget to the city for its approval. Upon the approval of such budget, the department of recreation shall be responsible for the administration of the provisions of the same. The department of recreation may receive fees and revenue from the operation of recreation facilities and programs, and may also solicit or receive any gifts or bequests of money or other personal property or any donation to be applied, principal or income, for either temporary or permanent use for playgrounds or other recreational purposes.
(Ord. No. 599, § 3, 8-5-1970)
Sec. 2-239. - Reports.
The department of recreation shall make full and complete monthly and annual reports to the city commission, and such other reports as, from time to time, may be requested by the commission.
(Ord. No. 599, § 4, 8-5-1970)
Sec. 2-240. - Expenditures restricted.
All expenditure of funds shall be within the budget of the department of recreation for any calendar year and with the consent of the city manager and director of finance, except that gifts and donations made to the recreation advisory board may be expended by such board in its discretion and for recreational purposes.
(Ord. No. 599, § 5, 8-5-1970)
