Sec. 13-301. - Compliance required.
All persons engaged in the city in the business carried on for the accommodation of the public in providing parking and specialized service facilities for automobiles, trucks and other self-propelled vehicles for short periods of time in an open-air or roofed location on private property, for which service or facilities a charge is made, shall comply with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1953, § 25.1)
Sec. 13-302. - License fee.
Each owner or operator of parking lots regulated by this article shall pay to the city the license fee now or hereafter provided for in article II of this chapter.
(Code 1953, § 25.2)
Sec. 13-303. - Schedule of charges and fees to be posted.
There shall be posted in a conspicuous place on each parking lot a schedule of charges and fees for the facilities or services provided. Such schedule shall be lettered and posted in such manner as to be legible to patrons of such business.
(Code 1953, § 25.3)
Sec. 13-304. - Solicitation of patrons; solicitation stands and areas.
Solicitation of patrons by the owners, operators, agents and employees of such parking lots is hereby prohibited on the streets, lanes, alleys, sidewalks, parks and other public property of the city, except in such areas or stands as may be designated by the police department of the city. In any one such designated solicitation area or stand, no single parking lot business shall have at any one time more than two employees, agents and solicitors. Such agents, employees, owners, operators and solicitors are prohibited from engaging in loud or boisterous talk or argument while on or about such solicitation areas or stands. The police department shall not create a solicitation stand in front of any place of business where the abutting property owners object to the same or where such stand would tend to create a traffic hazard.
(Code 1953, § 25.4)
Sec. 13-305. - City-owned parking lots.
(a)
The city manager is granted the power, authority, right, responsibility and duty to prescribe rules and regulations relating to the parking of vehicles on property within the city owned, leased or held by the city or other governmental agency or authority when requested in writing so to do by the commissioners or the proper and legal person or officer or official of a governmental agency or authority. Upon such request being made, and approved by the city commission, the city manager, after prescribing such rules and regulations for the parking of vehicles on such governmental owned property shall post on said property designated as a parking area a clearly visible sign upon which shall be written the rules and regulations and penalties under which vehicles may be parked, including but not limited to, hours of parking, length of time that vehicles may be parked, speed limits within said parking area, removal of vehicles from parking lot when parked over time allowed, stripping of parking areas, designation of stop signs, reservation of areas for employees and other persons, stripping and designation of walkways, and any other regulation which shall promote the safety and efficiency of the parking of such vehicles.
(b)
The city manager shall also have the power and authority to place and install parking meters in such parking lot or rent spaces to parking customers and prescribe payment for such space.
(Ord. No. 553, § 1, 1-19-1968)
