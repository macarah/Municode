Chapter 14 - CIVIL EMERGENCIES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 14-1—14-18. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT ORGANIZATION

Sec. 14-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Emergency means a condition as defined or declared by the President of the United States, the governor of the state or the mayor and council when the threat or actual occurrence of a disaster, epidemic or subversive activities in the city is of such severity and magnitude to warrant extraordinary public service and/or relief efforts.
Emergency management means measures necessary to provide for the mobilization, organization and direction of civilian populace and necessary support agencies to prevent or minimize the effects of fire, flood, storm, earthquake and epidemic as well as the effects of enemy or subversive activities directed against the populace, communities, industrial plants, facilities and other installations.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-1; Code 1996, § 26-31)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 38-3-3.
Sec. 14-20. - Penalty for violation of article.
During an emergency, no person shall:
(1)
Willfully obstruct, hinder or delay any member of the emergency management corps in the enforcement of any lawful rule or regulation issued pursuant to this article or in the performance of any duty imposed upon him by the virtue of this article.
(2)
Do any act forbidden by any lawful rules or regulations issued pursuant to this article if such act is of such a nature as to give assistance to the enemy or to imperil the life or property of any inhabitant of the city or to prevent, hinder or delay the defense or protection thereof.
(3)
Wear or carry or display without authority any mark or identification specified by the council of defense.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-10; Code 1996, § 26-32)
Sec. 14-21. - Council of defense created; membership.
The city council of defense is hereby created and shall include the following:
(1)
Mayor;
(2)
Emergency management director;
(3)
Chief of police;
(4)
Fire chief;
(5)
County health officer;
(6)
City manager;
(7)
Utility superintendent;
(8)
County welfare officer;
(9)
American Red Cross representative; and
(10)
Others, including representatives of business, labor, civic and patriotic organizations, as appointed by the city council.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-2; Code 1996, § 26-33)
Sec. 14-22. - Council of defense powers and duties.
It shall be the duty of the council of defense and it is hereby empowered to:
(1)
Develop a city emergency management plan. This plan shall provide for the effective mobilization of all the resources of the city, both private and public.
(2)
Prepare, and recommend for consideration by the city, an ordinance necessary to implement the emergency management plan.
(3)
Consider, and recommend to the mayor and council for approval, mutual aid plans and agreements.
(4)
Perform such other duties as directed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-3; Code 1996, § 26-34)
Sec. 14-23. - Director office created; appointment.
There is hereby created the office of the emergency management director. Such officer shall be recommended to the governor for appointment by the mayor and council. In the event such appointment is not made, the city manager is designated as the emergency management director. The city may also enter into an agreement with the office of the county emergency management director to perform the duties and functions of this office.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-4; Code 1996, § 26-35)
Sec. 14-24. - Director functions.
The emergency management director is hereby directed to:
(1)
Represent the mayor on all matters pertaining to emergency management;
(2)
Require and direct cooperation of city officials in the preparation and implementation of emergency management plans;
(3)
During periods of emergency, direct the services of city officers or employees; and
(4)
During periods of emergency, obtain vital supplies and equipment needed for the protection of life and property of people and bind the city for the value thereof and, if required immediately, to requisition the same.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-5; Code 1996, § 26-36)
Sec. 14-25. - Emergency management corps composition.
Officers and employees of the city, with volunteer forces enrolled to aid them during an emergency and all groups, organizations and persons who make the agreements on operation of law, charged with duties necessary for the protection of the life and property in the city during such an emergency, shall constitute the emergency management corps.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-6; Code 1996, § 26-37)
Sec. 14-26. - Emergency management corps distribution of functions and duties.
The functions and duties of the emergency management corps shall be distributed among the divisions as listed in section 14-27. Each division shall be under the direction of a chief and deputy chief appointed by the council of defense. The chiefs of divisions will include public officials such as sheriffs, chiefs of police and fire departments and heads of agencies dealing with transportation, communications, and other sundry services as well as private businessmen who, through their profession, knowledge and experience, are particularly qualified to head divisions requiring specialized knowledge and experience.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-7; Code 1996, § 26-38)
Sec. 14-27. - Emergency management corps divisions established; designated.
The divisions of the emergency management corps are established as follows:
(1)
Law enforcement division;
(2)
Fire services division;
(3)
Transportation division;
(4)
Medical and health division;
(5)
Communications division;
(6)
Welfare division;
(7)
Information and education division; and
(8)
Others, as determined by the council of defense.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-8; Code 1996, § 26-39)
Sec. 14-28. - Volunteers; compensation and immunities.
All persons, other than officers and employees of the city, volunteering in service pursuant to the provisions of this article shall serve without compensation. While engaged in such service they shall have the same immunities as persons and employees of the city performing similar duties.
(Code 1984, § 4-3-9; Code 1996, § 26-40)
