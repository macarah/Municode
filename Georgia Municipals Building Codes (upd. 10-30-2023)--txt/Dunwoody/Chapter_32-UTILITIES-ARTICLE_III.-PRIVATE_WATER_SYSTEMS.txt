ARTICLE III. - PRIVATE WATER SYSTEMS

Sec. 32-154. - Private water systems and vendors governed by chapter, licensed by county.
Private water systems and vendors shall be governed by all criteria in this chapter relating to water purity, protection of supply and system sanitation, and shall be licensed by the county or its designee.
(Comp. Ords. 2008, ch. 25, art. II, § 1)
Sec. 32-155. - Approval of plans.
All plans for private water systems shall be prepared by the county engineer and approved by the county prior to construction. Materials of construction shall meet minimum standards set by the county for the public water system.
(Comp. Ords. 2008, ch. 25, art. II, § 2)
Sec. 32-156. - Connection to public water system.
No connection shall be made between the county water system and a private water system except by approval of the county. Such approved connections shall be metered and equipped with backflow prevention devices approved by the county and subject to all conditions of this chapter.
(Comp. Ords. 2008, ch. 25, art. II, § 3)
Sec. 32-157. - No county or city warranty.
By its regulation of private water systems and suppliers, neither the county nor the city assumes any liability for the purity of such water or for damages caused by operators of private facilities.
(Comp. Ords. 2008, ch. 25, art. II, § 4)
Secs. 32-158—32-182. - Reserved.
