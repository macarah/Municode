ARTICLE II. - SANITARY TAX

Sec. 82-26. - Levied—Family dwelling unit.
There is hereby levied and assessed a monthly sanitary tax of $16.70 against each residential unit within the corporate limits of the city for the collection and removal of disposable matter permitted by this Code to be placed on the curb or adjacent to back alleys, and for the collection and disposal of garbage and rubbish required by this Code to be stored in a proper container; an additional $12.95 fee will be added for each additional container; and where any building contains therein more than one family unit or apartment, then the tax is hereby levied separately for each such family dwelling unit or apartment unless said building is identified by city records as being associated with a commercial dumpster account; and where any building contains therein more than one family unit or apartment and is identified by city records as being associated with a commercial dumpster account, an additional sanitary tax of $2.80 is hereby levied separately against each such family dwelling unit or apartment.
(Code 1965, § 15-2; Ord. No. 857, § 1, 5-7-96; Ord. No. 920, § 1, 11-7-00; Ord. No. 977, § 1, 2-1-05; Ord. No. 995, § 1, 1-12-06; Ord. No. 1014, § 1, 12-18-07; Ord. No. 1061, § 1, 1-3-12; Ord. No. 1072, § 1, 1-7-14)

Sec. 82-28. - Same—Commercial establishments not using dumpsters.
There is hereby levied and assessed on all commercial establishments located within the city which do not utilize dumpsters a monthly sanitary tax of $2.80 for the collection and removal of disposable matter permitted by this Code to be placed on the curb or adjacent to back alleys, and there is also hereby assessed a monthly sanitary tax of $17.60 against each such business for the collection and disposal of garbage and rubbish required by this Code to be stored in a proper container, plus an additional tax of $17.60 for each additional container used at each commercial establishment. Where any building contains therein more than one business unit, then said taxes are hereby levied separately against each such unit.

Sec. 82-29. - Utilization and size of dumpsters.
The utilization and size of dumpsters to be furnished by the city shall be within the discretion and control of the city manager based upon his determination as to the proper method of trash disposal and the volume of trash likely to be generated at the site of each dumpster location.
(Code 1965, § 15-4.1)
Sec. 82-30. - Certain industrial enterprises exempted from taxes levied by this article; industrial customers defined.
(a)
Those industrial enterprises located in the city that generate in excess of two cubic yards of disposable waste material per week shall have the right, at their option, to enter into a separate contract with a waste disposal company for the disposal of its solid waste, so long as the performance of such contract results in the disposal of such waste in a manner and sufficiently often as not to create unsightly or unhealthy conditions.
(b)
In order to qualify for such exemption, an industry must apply to the city manager for an exemption certificate, and attach to such application a photocopy of a fully executed contract with a duly licensed waste disposal company to dispose of the applicant's solid waste generated at its facility or facilities in the city. The applicant shall certify in its application that it will place all of its solid waste in the containers provided by its contractor and will not place any of its solid waste at any other place, specifically including at curb side. If the city manager determines the applicant qualified under this section he shall grant the applicant an exemption from mandatory sanitary taxes imposed by this article.
(c)
If the city manager makes a preliminary determination that an exempted enterprise is not complying with the provisions of this section, he shall give the enterprise written notice of that fact, and shall allow the enterprise ten days within which to contest such determination. If, after considering the contentions of the enterprise, if any, the city manager determines that the enterprise is not complying with the requirements of this section, he shall revoke the enterprises exemption, and such enterprise shall thereupon be required to pay the sanitary taxes imposed by this article, and to permit the city waste disposal contractor to resume collection of its solid waste.
(d)
The term "industrial enterprise" as used in this section shall mean a business enterprise located in the city that is actually engaged in the manufacturing of one of more tangible products from raw materials.
(Ord. No. 983, § 1, 8-16-05)
