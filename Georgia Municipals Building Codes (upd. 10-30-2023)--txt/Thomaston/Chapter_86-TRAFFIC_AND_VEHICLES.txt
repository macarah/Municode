ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - Uniform Rules of the Road adopted.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-370, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-395, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited in this chapter.
(Code 1965, § 20-1)
Sec. 86-2. - Operating improperly loaded vehicles.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate on any street in the city any motor vehicle which is so loaded that any part of its load spills or drops on any street or alley within the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate upon any street in the city any vehicle loaded with any refuse, trash, sawdust, shavings or other material when so loaded in such a place or condition that any of such load can be blown by the wind so as to be scattered upon the city streets or alleys or cause clouds of dust or particles from such vehicle.
(Code 1965, § 20-18)

ARTICLE II. - STOPPING, STANDING OR PARKING

Sec. 86-26. - Parking of vehicles regulated.
(a)
The parking of vehicles in the public streets of the city shall hereafter be done only in accordance with the terms and provisions of this section and the state rules of the road, as implemented by section 86-1, and as modified by this section.
(b)
The parking of vehicles in public streets of the city shall hereafter be regulated by signs or painted markings, or a combination thereof, and in the commercial areas of the city, and in such other specific areas as are, from time to time, designated by the city council. Each authorized parking space shall be clearly marked by painted lines.
(c)
In all those areas of the city where parking of vehicles in the public streets is permitted without any time limitation applicable thereto, no signs or markings of any type shall be required under this article, and such parking shall be done wholly in accordance with the state rules of the road.
(d)
In all those areas of the city where parking of vehicles in the public street is prohibited, it shall be the duty of the council to cause the entire length of curb along which parking is prohibited to be painted yellow or to cause official traffic signs to be placed adjacent to the edge of each such street informing the public of such prohibition.
(e)
In all those areas of the city where parking vehicles in the public street is permitted for limited periods of time only, it shall be the duty of the council to cause official traffic signs to be placed adjacent to the edge of each such public street informing the public of the time limits applicable to the parking of vehicles thereon.
(f)
All parking of vehicles in the public streets of the city shall be parallel to the curb or edge of the street in accordance with the state rules of the road, except in those areas wherein the council has caused parking spaces to be laid off and painted at an angle to the curb or edge of the street.
(g)
Official traffic control signs placed in accordance with the provisions of this section shall be placed in sufficient proximity to the street to which the signs applies to be legible from a vehicle being operated on such street. Whenever official traffic signs have been placed in position, approximately conforming to the requirements of this section, the signs shall be presumed to have been so placed by the official act of the city council, unless the contrary is established by competent evidence.
(h)
In those areas of the city where parking in the public streets is authorized and parking spaces have been marked by painted lines, it shall be unlawful to park any vehicle therein in such manner that any portion of the vehicle is located over any portion of any painted line delineating such parking space.
(i)
It shall be unlawful to park a vehicle on any portion of any public street of the city along which the curb is painted yellow, or along which have been erected signs giving notice of the prohibition against parking. Vehicles parked in areas of the public streets where parking is prohibited in such manner or at such place as to cause, in the judgment of the traffic officers of the city, a safety hazard to the public shall be removed therefrom under the direction and supervision of the officers, and the owner of such vehicle shall be required to pay, in addition to the penalties imposed in subsection (k) of this section, all the expense of removing such vehicle incurred by the city in order to regain possession thereof.
(j)
In all those areas of the city where the parking of vehicles is permitted in the public streets for limited periods of time, the parking of a vehicle for a time greater than allowed in accordance with the official signs posted thereon shall be unlawful, and, in addition, the failure to remove such vehicle prior to the time that such permitted parking time again expires shall constitute a separate offense under this section; provided, however, that signs prohibiting parking in excess of certain limited periods of time shall be effective only during the hours of 9:00 a.m. until 5:00 p.m. on Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday.
(k)
Any vehicle found to be parked in violation of the Georgia Uniform Rules of the Road, as implemented and modified by this code, or in violation of any ordinance or regulation of the city, shall be ticketed by a citation summoning the operator thereof to an appearance in the municipal court of the city to answer to such charge, and if found guilty, such operator shall be punished as provided in section 1-11 of this Code. Provided, however, that there shall be accepted a cash bond forfeiture in the amount of $5.00 for each such offense if such cash bond is actually posted within 24 hours of the issuance of the citation charging such parking offense. If a cash bond forfeiture is posted for such offense later than 24 hours of the issuance of the citation charging such offense, but within 72 hours of such issuance, the cash bond required will be in the amount of $15.00 per offense. If a cash bond forfeiture is posted for such offense later than 72 hours of the issuance of the citation charging such offense, but within 168 hours of such issuance, the cash bond required will be in the amount of $25.00 per offense. If a cash bond is not posted within 168 hours of the issuance of the citation, the offender charged shall be required to be and appear in the municipal court of the city to answer such charge.
(l)
If any member of the police department finds any vehicle parked on the public streets of the city which has accumulated five or more, outstanding, unsatisfied and unpaid parking citations, that officer shall be authorized to take the action necessary to render that vehicle immobile by seeing to the placement of a wheel locking device thereon. The operator of such vehicle shall, prior to the removal of such device, be required to pay all outstanding charges for such parking citations and all costs incurred for the placement and removal of said wheel locking device.
(Code 1965, § 20-29.1; Ord. No. 917, §§ 2, 3, 9-19-00)
Sec. 86-27. - Double parking.
It shall be unlawful to park or to allow to stand or stop any vehicle back of or behind any car parked next to the curb or back of or behind any parking spaces next to the curb, on any street or roadway in the city, except that it shall be permissible to so double park for a short time, in no event to exceed five minutes; provided, however, that the driver of such vehicle remains therein ready to move such vehicle at any time in order that other vehicles may have free ingress to and egress from the parking spaces which are affected by such double parking; further provided that the driver of such double parked vehicle does promptly move such vehicle so as to allow free and prompt ingress to and egress from the parking spaces affected thereby; further provided that the driver of such double parked vehicle obeys promptly any and all traffic directions by the city police officers.
(Code 1965, § 20-31)
Sec. 86-28. - Parking vehicles for purpose of repairing, selling or exhibiting for sale.
It shall be unlawful for any person engaged in the automobile or garage business to park on any public street of the city any automobile or other motor vehicle for the purpose of working on or repairing such vehicle, or for the purpose of selling or exhibiting for sale such vehicle.
(Code 1965, § 20-32)
Sec. 86-29. - Parking for longer than 24 hours.
(a)
For the purposes of this section, the word "vehicle" shall mean every motor vehicle designed, used or maintained by its owner for the transportation of persons or property.
(b)
No person shall leave standing or parked on any public street or alley of the city, excepting only those streets on which the parking of vehicles is now or hereafter controlled by parking meter devices, any motor vehicle for a longer period of time than 24 hours.
(c)
The police department and all members of the police department then on duty are hereby authorized to remove and tow away or have removed and towed away by commercial towing service any vehicle so illegally parked for more than 24 hours upon any public street or alley of the city, excepting only those streets on which the parking of vehicles is now or hereafter controlled by parking meter devices. Vehicles so towed away for illegal parking shall be stored by the chief of police in a safe place and shall be restored to the owner or operator of such car upon a payment of a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges within 24 hours after the time such car was removed, plus another fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for each additional 24 hours or fraction thereof that such vehicle is stored. If neither the owner or operator of such car shall have the vehicle restored to him within ten days after the vehicle was stored by the chief of police, then and in such event the chief of police shall sell the vehicle at public outcry before the courthouse door in the city, in the manner in which sheriff's sales are held under state law, paying from such proceeds first the restoration fee as provided in this section, plus all other expenses incurred by the city in such storage and sale and paying the balance to the person or persons legally entitled thereto.
(Code 1965, § 20-35.1)
Sec. 86-30. - Certain trucks prohibited from parking on certain streets under certain conditions.
(a)
No person shall park or leave standing or parked any truck with its motor running or with its auxiliary motor, refrigeration motor or any motor attached thereto running, at any time upon any of the following described public streets in the city:
(b)
Any street which abuts on either side of the public square of the city, or within one city block of the public square of the city and any portion of any street lying within one hundred yards of any occupied residence or dwelling house in the city.
(c)
Any motorized vehicle with a gross vehicle weight rating in excess of 15,000 pounds.
(Code 1965, § 20-36; Ord. No. 917, § 4, 9-19-00)
Sec. 86-31. - Parking of certain tractor-trailer rigs on residential streets.
No tractor-trailer rig or motor vehicle with a gross weight of 21,000 pounds or more shall be parked on a public street within the city which adjoins property which is located in a R-1, R-2, or R-3 residential zoning district of the city, unless parked there temporarily while completing a delivery or pickup from premises located adjacent thereto.
(Ord. No. 917, § 5, 9-19-00)

Sec. 86-61. - Manner of driving on.
An operator of a motor vehicle or other vehicle approaching or entering any street, avenue or way, or any portion thereof in the city, which has exhibited or placed thereon or thereby a sign showing that the traffic thereon is restricted to one way or to any certain direction, shall obey such sign and shall not operate such motor vehicle or other vehicle thereon except in the way and direction designated by such sign, nor shall he attempt to do so.
(Code 1965, § 20-8)

The speed limits established in this division shall become effective at each location upon the erection of signs indicating the speed limits set forth in this division.

Sec. 86-88. - Speed limit on alleys.
On the alleys of the city, being those public alleys running behind and in the rear of business establishments and residences in the city, notwithstanding any other provisions of this division, it shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle or other vehicle at a speed in excess of 15 miles per hour.
(Code 1965, § 20-28)

Such highways, streets, avenues, roads, drives and intersections in close proximity to schools for general education in the city as shall from time to time be designated by resolution of the mayor and council as school zones shall have a speed limit, when properly marked, by the operators of a motor vehicle or other vehicles not in excess of 15 miles per hour when in such school zone while children are assembling, departing therefrom or in recess on the grounds thereof. Any such resolution so passed by the mayor and council shall be incorporated in the minutes of the meeting at which the resolution shall be passed. Such resolution shall direct that the chief of police shall adequately mark such school zones by official traffic signs.
(Code 1965, § 20-29)

ARTICLE IV. - SPEED MONITORING SYSTEMS

Sec. 86-110. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the following terms, phrases and their derivatives shall have the meanings given herein:
Automated traffic enforcement safety device means a speed detection device that (1) is capable of producing photographically recorded still or video images, or both, of the rear of a motor vehicle or of the rear of a motor vehicle being towed by another vehicle, including an image of such vehicle's rear license plate; (2) is capable of monitoring the speed of a vehicle as photographically recorded pursuant to subparagraph (1) of this paragraph; and (3) indicates on each photographically recorded still or video image produced the date, time, location, and speed of a photographically recorded vehicle traveling at a speed above the posted speed limit within a marked school zone.
City shall mean the City of Thomaston.
Police or city police shall mean the City of Thomaston Police Department.
School zone shall mean the area within 1,000 feet of the boundary of any public elementary or secondary school.
Violation shall mean that a vehicle has exceeded the posted speed limit by more than ten miles per hour during the applicable time periods.
(Ord. No. 1129, 7-21-20)
Sec. 86-111. - Authorized.
Speed camera enforcement, using automated traffic enforcement safety device that is fixed is authorized within school zones in the city between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday.
(Ord. No. 1129, 7-21-20)
Sec. 86-112. - Administration.
A.
The city police, or agent on behalf of the city police, operating an automated traffic enforcement safety device provided for under O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18 shall maintain a log for the automated traffic enforcement safety device attesting to the performance of such device's self-test at least once every 30 days and the results of such self-test pertaining to the accuracy of the automated traffic enforcement safety device. Such log shall be admissible in any court proceeding for a violation issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18.
B.
The city police, or agent on behalf of the city police, shall perform an independent calibration test on the automated traffic enforcement safety device at least once every 12 months. The results of such calibration test shall be admissible in any court proceeding for a violation issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18.
C.
Prior to the placement of a device within a school zone, each school within whose school zone such automated traffic enforcement safety device is to be placed shall first apply for and secure a permit from the department of transportation for the use of such automated traffic enforcement safety device. Such permit shall be awarded based upon need. The department of transportation shall promulgate rules and regulations for the implementation of this paragraph.
D.
For a period of 30 days after the first automated traffic enforcement safety device is introduced by the city police, or agent on behalf of the city police, within a school zone, the owner of a motor vehicle shall not be liable for a civil monetary penalty but shall be issued a civil warning for disregard or disobedience of the speed limit within the school zone.
(Ord. No. 1129, 7-21-20)
Sec. 86-113. - Violations and civil monetary penalties.
Any person who shall violate any provision of this article shall be subject to the civil monetary penalties set forth in O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18(b)(l) as amended, including a fine in the amount of $75.00 for the first violation and $125.00 for the second or any subsequent violation, which shall be in addition to fees associated with the electronic processing of such civil monetary penalty, which shall not exceed $25.00.
Printed notices and/or citations shall notify drivers that they have been cited under O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18.
(Ord. No. 1129, 7-21-20)
Sec. 86-114. - Late fees.
The following late fees shall be added to any civil monetary penalty not timely paid: (a) Thirty days after the due date printed on the second notice: $25.00; (b) Ninety days after the due date printed on the second notice: an additional $25.00. Total late fees shall not exceed $50.00 per violation and shall be printed on all notices.
(Ord. No. 1129, 7-21-20)
Sec. 86-115. - Court.
Any person who desires to contest a notice of violation shall be allowed a hearing in municipal court for adjudication in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18.
(Ord. No. 1129, 7-21-20)
