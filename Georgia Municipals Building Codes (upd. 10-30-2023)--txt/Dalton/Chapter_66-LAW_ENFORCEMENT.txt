ARTICLE II. - POLICE FORCE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 66-31. - Composition.
The chief of police and police officers shall constitute the city's police force.
(Code 1983, § 17-1)
Sec. 66-32. - General functions.
The chief of police and police officers shall be general conservators of the peace, shall patrol the city and on call shall go at once to any part of the city where they may be needed for the performance of their duties. They shall have all the powers of peace officers generally in making arrests and preserving order.
(Code 1983, § 17-6)
State Law reference— Arrest of persons, O.C.G.A. § 17-4-1 et seq.
Secs. 66-33—66-37. - Reserved.
Sec. 66-38. - Obedience to public safety commission.
It shall be the duty of the police force at all times to render obedience to the orders of the public safety commission. All members when on duty shall conform to all rules and regulations prescribed by the public safety commission.
(Code 1983, § 17-12)

Cross reference— Officers and employees, § 2-121 et seq.

Sec. 66-66. - General duties.
(a)
The chief of police shall be and is vested with full power and authority to:
(1)
Execute all processes to him directed by the mayor and council or either of them or by the municipal judge.
(2)
Attend upon the council at its sittings and preserve order thereat.
(3)
Enforce and carry into effect to the utmost of his power the laws and ordinances passed and ordained and in force for the government of the city and the orders of the council.
(b)
He shall suppress all riots and disorderly conduct in the city and shall arrest such offenders, even without any special warrant, and forthwith carry them before the municipal court.
(c)
He shall abate nuisances and shall have the power to report all persons who shall violate the laws regulating retailing or any other law or city ordinance in force whatsoever.
(Code 1983, § 17-2)
State Law reference— Arrest of persons, O.C.G.A. § 17-4-1 et seq.
Sec. 66-67. - Disposition of revenue.
The chief of police shall be responsible for transferring into the general fund all revenues collected by the police department.
