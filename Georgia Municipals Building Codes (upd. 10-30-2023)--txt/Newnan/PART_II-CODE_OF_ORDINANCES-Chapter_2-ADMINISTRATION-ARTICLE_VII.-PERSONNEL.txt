ARTICLE VII. - PERSONNEL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-461. - Personnel policies.
(a)
A document outlining the personnel policies of the city shall be prepared by the city manager and adopted by resolution of the council.
(b)
In addition to this policy document, the city manager shall develop and maintain an administrative procedures manual which describes how personnel policies shall be implemented.
(Ord. No. 14-91, § 17-6, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-462. - Responsibility of city council.
The city council shall make all appointments to the municipal service coming within its statutory jurisdiction. The city council shall also authorize and approve by ordinance the pay plan for municipal service.
(Ord. No. 14-91, § 17-1, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-463. - Designation and duties of director of personnel.
(a)
The city manager, or his designated representative, shall serve as the director of personnel. He shall be responsible for the administration of the personnel program. The city manager shall appoint, suspend, and remove all city officers and employees except those elected by the citizens or those whose appointment is otherwise provided for by law. The city manager shall make appointments, dismissals, and suspensions in accordance with the city charter, the city Code and other policies adopted by council.
(b)
The city manager shall:
(1)
Make all appointments to the classified service and those temporary and parttime positions authorized by city council.
(2)
Maintain such personnel records necessary to demonstrate compliance with federal, state and local regulations and to meet the needs of personnel administration under this article.
(3)
Recommend changes in personnel policy to the city council.
(4)
Recommend revisions to the position classification plan to the city council.
(5)
Recommend revisions to the pay plan to the city council.
(6)
Develop and administer recruiting programs to assure an adequate supply of competent applicants to meet the city's needs.
(7)
Develop and coordinate training and educational programs for city employees to develop skills and enhance job performance.
(8)
Maintain a personnel policy and procedures manual specifically stating the processes for administration of personnel policies.
(9)
Perform such other duties as may be assigned by the city council not inconsistent with this article.
(Ord. No. 14-91, § 17-2, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-464. - Composition of classified service.
The classified service shall comprise all positions in the municipal service, now existing or hereafter created, which fall under the appointive authority of the city manager. The following are not included in the classified service:
(1)
The city manager;
(2)
The assistant city manager, however, this position is appointed by the city manager;
(3)
The city attorney;
(4)
The municipal judge;
(5)
The associate judge; and
(6)
Employees who are not employed on a fulltime, permanent basis.
(Ord. No. 14-91, § 17-3, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-465. - Merit principle.
All appointments, promotions and other personnel actions shall be made solely on the basis of merit and fitness.
(Ord. No. 14-91, § 17-4, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-466. - Equal employment opportunity.
It is the policy of the city to foster, maintain and promote equal employment opportunity. The city shall select employees on the basis of applicant's qualifications without regard to age, sex, race, color, creed, religion, political affiliation, or national origin, except where specific age, sex, or physical requirements constitute a bona fide occupational qualification necessary for job performance. Applicants with physical handicaps shall be given equal consideration for positions in which their physical handicaps do not represent an unreasonable barrier to satisfy performance of duties.
(Ord. No. 14-91, § 17-5, 12-9-91)
Secs. 2-467—2-480. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - BONDS OF CITY OFFICERS

Sec. 2-481. - Surety.
All officers of the city who are required to give bond shall make the same in some solvent surety company, and no bond of any officer as aforesaid shall be accepted with only personal sureties thereon.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-482. - Attestation and approval.
Such official bonds may be attested by any officer of the city council, by the city clerk, or by any officer of this state authorized to attest deeds, and they shall be approved by such board.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-483. - Conditions; joint and several liability.
The condition of such official bonds shall be for the faithful performance of the duties of the office, and such other conditions as may be specifically prescribed. Liability thereon shall be joint and several.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-484. - To whom payable.
The bonds of all officers of the city, when no other provision is made, shall be payable to the city.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-485. - Obligations.
The obligation of official bonds furnished as provided in section 2-484 shall be obligatory on the principal and sureties thereon:
(1)
For any breach of the condition during the time the officer continues in office or discharges any of the duties thereof.
(2)
For the faithful discharge of any duties which may be required of such officers by any law or ordinance passed subsequently to the execution of such bond, although no such conditions are expressed therein.
(3)
For the use and benefit of every person who is injured, as well as by wrongful act committed under color of his office, as by his failure to perform, or by the improper or neglectful performance of those duties imposed by law.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-486. - Not discharged by single recovery.
The official bonds furnished as provided in section 2-485 shall not be discharged by a single recovery, but proceedings may be had from time to time until the whole penalty is exhausted against the officer and his sureties, or either.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-487. - Validity of nonconforming bond.
When an officer acts under a bond which is not in the penalty payable and conditioned, or approved and filed, as required by law, such bond shall stand the same as if it were made in conformity to law.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-488. - Relief of surety.
On good cause shown and on notice to the principal, the city council may, in its discretion, relieve the sureties on any official bond furnished as provided in section 2-484, and cause such officer to give other security; and any such officer failing to give such other security shall be dismissed from his office.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Sec. 2-489. - Blanket bond coverage to be furnished.
Blanket bond coverage shall be furnished to all city employees.
(Ord. No. 14-91, Art. III, 12-9-91)
Secs. 2-490—2-500. - Reserved.

DIVISION 4. - DRUG AND ALCOHOL TESTING

Sec. 2-521. - Drug and alcohol use prohibited.
Substance and alcohol abuse by city employees during assigned working hours, in government buildings or on government grounds, or otherwise while on city business is prohibited. This shall include the use of illegal substances, the abuse of prescription medications, and the use or abuse of alcohol.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-42), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-522. - Preemployment testing.
All job applicants being considered for employment shall be required to pass a drug and alcohol screening test prior to being hired. All job applicants shall be informed in advance that such testing shall be required.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-43), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-523. - Random and periodic drug testing.
(a)
Employees in safety sensitive positions or employees whose primary job duties require maintenance or operation of a motor vehicle or motorized equipment shall be required to submit to a drug and alcohol screening test at random or on a periodic basis from time to time as determined by the department head and the city manager or his designee.
(b)
The following positions are considered to be safety sensitive positions:
(1)
City police department:
a.
All sworn personnel.
b.
School crossing guards.
(2)
City fire department:
All certified employees.
(c)
Employees in safety sensitive positions or whose primary job duties require operation of motor vehicles may expect to be tested on an annual basis.
(d)
The city manager, with the approval of the mayor and council, may designate additional positions which are deemed to be safety sensitive and subject to testing on a random or periodic basis.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-44), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-524. - Drug and/or alcohol screening test based on reasonable suspicion.
(a)
Reasonable suspicion means a reasonable belief based on specific objective facts and inferences drawn from those facts. Situations that may give rise to a conclusion that an employee is under the influence of drugs and/or alcohol include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
An employee is involved in a physical or verbal alteration on the job.
(2)
An employee has an excessive number of incidents or accidents on the job.
(3)
An employee exhibits unusual behavior such as slurred speech or unsteady walking or movement on the job.
(4)
An employee has an odor of alcohol on their person on the job.
(5)
An employee is in possession of alcohol or drugs on the job.
(b)
An employee shall be required to submit to drug and/or alcohol testing when there is reasonable suspicion to believe that such employee is under the influence of drugs and/or alcohol during assigned working hours or while otherwise on city duty or in control of city property.
(c)
Supervisors who suspect an employee is under the influence of drugs and/or alcohol shall immediately report the incident to their immediate supervisor or department head.
(d)
The determination of whether reasonable suspicion exists shall be made by the department head or by the highest ranking supervisory staff on-duty at the time. The facts underlying the determination of reasonable suspicion shall be disclosed to the employee at the time the demand to submit to testing is made.
(e)
Following the determination that reasonable suspicion exists, the employee shall be transported to and from the testing site by the employee's supervisor or a designee. Following the testing procedure, the person transporting the employee shall make appropriate arrangements to transport the employee home.
(f)
Supervisors shall be required to document in writing, within the next working day, the specific facts, symptoms or observations that formed the basis for their determination that reasonable suspicion existed to warrant the testing of an employee. All documents created in connection with the determination of reasonable suspicion shall be forwarded to the city manager.
(g)
The city shall develop a program of training to assist supervisory personnel in identifying drug and alcohol use among employees. Such training shall be directed toward helping supervisors to recognize the conduct and behavior giving rise to a reasonable suspicion of drug or alcohol use.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-45), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-525. - Testing after accidents or injury.
All employees involved in a work-related incident causing personal injury or property damage may be tested promptly for drug and/or alcohol use in accordance with department policies. Any employee involved in a motor vehicle accident while driving a city vehicle on a public roadway and who is determined to have been at fault in the accident by the investigating police officer shall be tested for drugs and alcohol.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-46), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-526. - Drugs to be tested for.
The selection of drugs to be tested for includes, but is not limited to, the following:
(1)
Marijuana.
(2)
Sedatives.
(3)
Methaqualone.
(4)
Opiates/narcotics.
(5)
Stimulants.
(6)
Amphetamines.
(7)
Benzadiazipines.
(8)
Hallucinogens.
(9)
Alcohol.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-47), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-527. - Prescription drug use.
(a)
Employees using prescription medication while on the job shall do so in strict accordance with medical directions. It is the employee's responsibility to notify the prescribing physician of the duties required by the employee's position and to ensure that the physician approves the use of the prescription medication while the employee is performing their duties.
(b)
The abuse and/or inappropriate use of legally prescribed drugs shall be prohibited. Job performance or attendance deficiencies resulting from abuse and/or inappropriate use shall be cause for a disciplinary action. If an employee's behavior or job performance gives rise to a reasonable suspicion that the employee is abusing or inappropriately using prescription medication, the employee may be required to submit to drug testing and to take leave until such time as the employee is cleared to return to work by the employee's physician, the medical review officer, and the personnel director.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-48), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-528. - List of medications.
Employees and job applicants shall at the time of testing provide a list of those prescriptions and over-the-counter medications that he has recently used. The list of medications shall be kept confidential until there has been a test result. The list of medications shall be disclosed only to the medical review officials who will determine whether the positive result was due to the lawful use of any of the listed medications.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-49), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-529. - Consent.
Before a drug and alcohol test is administered, employees and job applicants will be asked to sign a consent form authorizing the test and permitting release of test results to the employer and the medical officer. The consent form shall provide a space for employees and job applicants to acknowledge that they have been notified of the requirements of this division.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-50), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-530. - Refusal to consent.
(a)
Job applicants. Any applicant for a job with the city who refuses to consent to a drug and alcohol test shall be denied employment.
(b)
Employees. Any employee who refuses to sign the consent form or to submit to a drug and alcohol test as required in this division shall be subject to termination. Employees who fail to appear at the designated collection site to take the test when so directed shall be subject to termination.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-51), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-531. - Testing laboratory guidelines.
(a)
All testing procedures shall be administered and accounted for by an approved laboratory and/or medical facility that is operating in compliance with the National Institute of Drug Abuse (NIDA) guidelines. These procedures and guidelines shall be available for inspection by contacting the city manager's office.
(b)
Urine samples shall be provided in a private restroom stall or similar enclosure so that employees and applicants may not be viewed while providing the sample. Outer garments, bags, briefcases, purses, or other containers will not be permitted into the test area. The water in the commode shall be colored with dye to protect against dilution of test samples.
(c)
Whenever an initial test for drugs and alcohol is found to be positive, the laboratory will then carry out additional tests pursuant to laboratory testing guidelines to confirm that the initial positive indication was correct. If the second procedure also indicates the presence of drugs or alcohol, the test result is considered a confirmed positive result.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-52), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-532. - Confidentiality of test results.
All information from an employee's or job applicant's drug and alcohol test shall be confidential and only available to the department head, city manager and those with a need to know. Disclosure of test results to any other person, agency, or organization shall be prohibited unless written authorization is obtained from the employee or job applicant. The results of a positive drug or alcohol test shall not be released until the results are confirmed.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-53), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-533. - Disciplinary and termination policy.
(a)
First positive test. Except as otherwise stated in this section, on the first instance where an employee has a confirmed positive test for drugs and/or alcohol, the employee shall be suspended without pay and must participate in a drug or alcohol rehabilitation program. If the incident or occurrence which resulted in the employee's being tested involved violation of rules or policies other than the drug and alcohol ordinance, the employee may be subjected to disciplinary action, including termination, as deemed appropriate for the conduct.
(1)
Employees who test positive will be removed from their job and may not return to work until they pass a drug and/or alcohol test, are recommended for a return to duty by the medical reviewing officer, and consent to unannounced drug and/or alcohol testing for a period of one year.
(2)
Following a first confirmed positive drug test where an employee is referred for mandatory rehabilitation, the employee shall be subject to random testing for a period of one year.
(3)
If an employee is unable to return to work because of treatment, and has exhausted all his leave time—sick, vacation, compensatory—the existing personnel leave system policy will apply.
(b)
Second positive test. An employee who tests positive for a second time will be suspended without pay pending an immediate termination.
(c)
Immediate termination. The following reasons shall be grounds to recommend immediate termination of an employee:
(1)
Distributing controlled substances while on the job.
(2)
Operating a city vehicle or motorized equipment while illegally under the influence of drugs and/or alcohol.
(3)
Felony conviction for violation of drug laws.
(4)
Testing positive for drugs or alcohol while on probation under the provisions of this division.
(d)
Additional factors to be considered. Additional factors may be reviewed to determine appropriate disciplinary action for violations of this division. The factors include:
(1)
Public safety personnel shall be held to a higher standard.
(2)
Specific positions of leadership within the city shall be held to a higher standard: manager, assistant manager and department heads.
(3)
Prior violation of drug or alcohol laws and regulations.
(4)
Any other occurrence or circumstance which indicates need to impose enhanced disciplinary sanctions.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-54), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-534. - Voluntary employee assistance program.
Voluntary participation in an employee assistance program prior to an employee being requested to submit to a test is encouraged and such participation shall be kept confidential. No disciplinary action shall be implemented because an employee volunteers to participate in such a program. Employees who, prior to being requested to take a drug or alcohol test, voluntarily identify themselves as a drug user or alcohol abuser and obtain counseling and rehabilitation through the city's employee assistance program shall not be disciplined for their drug use and/or alcohol abuse if they thereafter refrain from violating the prohibitions of this division. Except as described above, all employees can be disciplined for violation of this division.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-55), 8-24-92)
Sec. 2-535. - Mandatory employee assistance program referral.
As provided in section 2-533, upon the first positive test or determination that an employee has been under the influence of drugs or alcohol, the city shall refer the employee to an employee assistance program (EAP) for assessment, counseling and rehabilitation. Referral to and compliance with the EAP is mandatory. Any employee who has been referred as a mandatory participant and who refuses counseling or rehabilitation shall be subject to suspension pending immediate termination.
(Ord. No. 17-92, § 1(17-56), 8-24-92)
