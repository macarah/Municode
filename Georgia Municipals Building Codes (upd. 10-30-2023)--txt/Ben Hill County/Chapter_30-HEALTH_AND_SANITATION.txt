Sec. 30-19. - Incorporated by reference.
There are currently in existence certain rules and regulations adopted by the Ben Hill County Board of Health with the purpose of preserving the public health, safety, and welfare of citizens of the county. This article specifically incorporates by reference the provision of the following rules and regulations which are in force at the time of adoption of this ordinance and as amended:
(1)
Rules and regulations pertaining to rabies control (adopted by the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule II as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to rabies control;
(2)
Rules and regulations for on-site sewage management systems (adopted by the state department of public health, as O.C.G.A. ch. 511-3-1 and the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule III as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to on-site sewage management systems;
(3)
Rules and regulations pertaining to on-site water supplies (adopted by the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule IV as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to on-site water supplies;
(4)
Rules and regulations for food service (adopted by the state department of public health, as O.C.G.A. ch. 511-6-1 and the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule V as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to food service;
(5)
Rules and regulations pertaining to public swimming pools, spas, and recreational water parks (adopted by the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule VI as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to public swimming pools, spas, and recreational water parks;
(6)
Rules and regulations for tourist accommodations (adopted by the state department of public health, as O.C.G.A. ch. 511-6-2 and the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule VII as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to tourist accommodations;
(7)
Rules and regulations pertaining to body art (adopted by the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule VIII as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to body art; and
(8)
Rules and regulations for portable sanitation (adopted by the state department of public health, as O.C.G.A. ch. 511-3-6 and the county board of health and being Ben Hill County Board of Health Rule IX as subsequently amended and updated), and policies of the county board of health pertaining to portable sanitation.
(Code 2000, § 30-31; Ord. No. 95-1, § I, 2-7-1995, Ord. of 8-5-2008, § I; Ord. No. 17-02, § I, 12-5-2017)
Sec. 30-20. - Implementation, enforcement.
The duty of implementation and enforcement of these regulations rest with the county board of commissioners and by virtue of this ordinance is hereby expressly delegated to the county board of health who is hereby charged with promulgation of sufficient procedures for insuring compliance with these rules and regulations by the citizens of the county. The county board of health is directed to implement procedures which will ensure that the citizens of the county will receive all procedural rights to which they are entitled under the laws of the state.
(Code 2000, § 30-32; Ord. No. 95-1, § II, 2-7-1995; Ord. of 8-5-2008, § II; Ord. No. 17-02, § II, 12-5-2017)
Sec. 30-21. - Violations; penalty.
Any violation of the rules and regulations hereby referenced shall be a violation of this ordinance and shall be a misdemeanor offense. Any violator shall be subject to the imposition of a fine not to exceed $500.00 and/or imprisonment for a period not to exceed 60 days. Any failure of payment of inspection fees listed within the fee schedule adopted by the county board of health and approved by the county board of commissioners within 60 days of first notice, may result in suspension of permit.
(Code 2000, § 30-33; Ord. No. 95-1, § III, 2-7-1995; Ord. of 8-5-2008, § III; Ord. No. 17-02, § III, 12-5-2017)
Sec. 30-22. - Court jurisdiction.
The magistrate's court of the county shall have jurisdiction over violators of this ordinance and all procedures for enforcement of such ordinance shall be as provided in Article 4, Chapter 10 of Title 15, Official Code of Georgia. Complaints of violation of any provision of this ordinance shall be brought before the magistrate's court by a designated representative of the county board of health and shall be prosecuted through that court.
(b)
Under the authority of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, title 31, chapter 3-4-(6) (O.C.G.A. §§ 31-3-4—31-3-6), the board of health and the board of commissioners do hereby declare that fees shall be established and collected for body art establishments and body art artists in the county. The fee will be set by ordinance by the board of county commissioners. This fee will include the permit for one facility and the one artist/operator. Additional artist permits for each additional artist will be set by ordinance by the board of county commissioners. The fees will include plan reviews and all routine compliance inspections for an establishment. Fees for body art establishments and body art artists shall be attached to the minutes of the board and made available for public review.
(c)
All money collected shall be handled in accordance with acceptable accounting procedures and deposited in the board of health general fund account of the county and used in accordance with the state law.
(Code 2000, § 30-34; Ord. No. 95-1, § IV, 2-7-1995; Ord. of 8-5-2008, § IV; Ord. No. 17-02, § IV, 12-5-2017)

ARTICLE III. - OUTDOOR WATER USE

Sec. 30-48. - Title.
This article shall be known as "Outdoor Water Use for Ben Hill County."
(Code 2000, § 30-40; Ord. No. 04-04, 9-7-2004)
Sec. 30-49. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Address means the house number (a numeric or alphanumeric designation) that, together with the street name, describes a physical location of a specific property. The term "even-numbered addresses" means a house number ending with the number 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, or no house number. The term "odd-numbered address" means a house ending with the number 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
Declared drought response level means one of four levels of drought that can be declared based on the severity of drought conditions, with one being the least severe and four being the most severe.
(Code 2000, § 30-41; Ord. No. 04-04, 9-7-2004)
Sec. 30-50. - Applicability of article.
This article applies to any entity, and its customers, permitted by the state environmental protection division (EPD) for water withdrawal or for operation of a drinking water system.
(Code 2000, § 30-42; Ord. No. 04-04, 9-7-2004)
Sec. 30-51. - Outdoor water schedule use during non-drought periods.
Outdoor water use other than exempted activities shall occur only as follows:
(1)
For odd-numbered addresses, outdoor water use is allowed on Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
(2)
For even-numbered addresses, outdoor water use is allowed on Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
(Code 2000, § 30-43; Ord. No. 04-04, 9-7-2004)
Sec. 30-52. - Outdoors water use schedule during declared drought response levels.
(a)
Drought declarations. The director of the environmental protection division is authorized to make drought declarations.
(b)
Declared drought conditions. During declared drought conditions, outdoor water use other than activities exempted by the state department of natural resources environmental division shall occur only during scheduled hours on the scheduled days.
(c)
Declared drought response level one. During level one drought conditions, outdoor water use may occur on scheduled days within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. to 12:00 midnight.
(1)
Scheduled days for odd-numbered addresses are Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
(2)
Scheduled days for even-numbered addresses are Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
(3)
Use of hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing is prohibited.
(d)
Declared drought response level two. During level two drought conditions, outdoor water use may occur on scheduled days within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m.
(1)
Scheduled days for odd-numbered addresses are Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
(2)
Scheduled days for even-numbered addresses and golf course fairways are Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
(3)
The following uses are prohibited:
a.
Using hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing.
b.
Washing hard surfaces, such as streets, gutters, sidewalks and driveways except when necessary for public health and safety.
(e)
Declared drought response level three. During level three drought conditions, outdoor water use may occur on the scheduled day within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m.
(1)
The scheduled day for odd-numbered addresses is Sunday.
(2)
The scheduled day for even-numbered addresses and golf course fairways is Saturday.
(3)
The following uses are prohibited:
a.
Using hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing.
b.
Washing hard surfaces, such as streets, gutters, sidewalks, driveways, except when necessary for public health and safety.
c.
Filling installed swimming pools except when necessary for health care or structural integrity.
d.
Washing vehicles, such as cars, boats, trailers, motorbikes, airplanes, golf carts.
e.
Washing buildings or structures except for immediate fire protection.
f.
Noncommercial fundraisers, such as car washes.
g.
Using water for ornamental purposes, such as fountains, reflecting pools, and waterfalls except when necessary to support aquatic life.
(f)
Declared drought response level four. During level four drought conditions, no outdoor water use is allowed, other than for activities exempted by the state department of natural resources environmental division, or as the environmental protection division may order.
(Code 2000, § 30-44; Ord. No. 04-04, 9-7-2004)
Sec. 30-53. - Exemptions.
(a)
This article shall not apply to the following outdoor water uses:
(1)
Capture and re-use of cooling system condensate or stormwater in compliance with applicable local ordinances.
(2)
Re-use of gray water in compliance with applicable local ordinances.
(b)
The following established landscape water uses are exempt from the outdoor water use schedules of this article:
(1)
Use of reclaimed wastewater by a designated user from a system permitted by the environmental protection division of the state department of natural resources to provide reclaimed wastewater.
(2)
Irrigation of personal food gardens.
(c)
Newly (in place less than 30 days) installed landscapes are subject to the following:
(1)
Irrigation of newly installed landscapes is allowed any day of the week, but only during allowed hours for the drought response level in effect, for a period of 30 days following installation. No watering is allowed during drought response level four.
(2)
For new landscapes installed by certified or licensed professionals, commercial exemptions apply.
(d)
The following golf course outdoor water uses are exempt from the outdoor water use schedules of this article:
(1)
Use of reclaimed wastewater by a designated user from a system permitted by the environmental protection division of the state department of natural resources to provide reclaimed wastewater.
(2)
Irrigation of fairways during times of non-drought and declared drought response level one.
(3)
Irrigation of tees during times of non-drought and declared drought response levels one, two and three.
(4)
Irrigation of greens.
(e)
The following commercial outdoor water uses are exempt from the outdoor water use schedules of this article:
(1)
Professional certified or licensed landscapers, golf course contractors, and sports turf landscapers: during installation and 30 days following installation only. Professional landscapers must be certified or licensed for commercial exemptions to apply.
(2)
Irrigation contractors: during installation and as needed for proper maintenance and adjustments only.
(3)
Sod producers.
(4)
Ornamental growers.
(5)
Fruit and vegetable growers.
(6)
Retail garden centers.
(7)
Hydroseeding.
(8)
Power-washing.
(9)
Construction sites.
(10)
Producers of food and fiber.
(11)
Car washes.
(12)
Other activities essential to daily business.
(13)
Watering-in of pesticides and herbicides on turf.
(Code 2000, § 30-45; Ord. No. 04-04, 9-7-2004)
Sec. 30-54. - Violations and penalties.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, organization, business, corporation or any entity to violate any of the provisions of this article.
(b)
Any person, firm, organization, business, corporation or any entity violating any of the provisions of this article shall be issued a warning for the first offense. The second offense as well as any subsequent offenses shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or 60 days in the county jail or both. Ordinance violations may be tried upon citation with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusation, as provided by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60. Citations shall be issued by the county board of commissioners or its designee. Service upon corporations, partnerships or other entities or organizations shall be effective by serving any managing agent located in the county, or by serving the registered agent, or any officer or partner of the corporation, partnership or organization or entity. The provisions of this article may also be enforced through the use of other remedies such as injunction for relief as provided by the laws of the state.
(c)
Whenever a corporation, partnership, organization or other artificial entity shall violate any of the provisions of this article, such violations shall be deemed to be also that of the individual directors, officers or agents of such corporation, partnership, organization or other artificial entity who shall have authorized, ordered, or done any of the acts constituting in a whole or in part such violation. In such cases, service shall also be made upon the individual director, officer or other agent accused of authorizing, ordering or doing the acts constituting in whole or in part violations of this article.
(Code 2000, § 30-46; Ord. No. 04-04, 9-7-2004)

ARTICLE IV. - RESTRICTIONS ON OUTDOOR WATERING OF LANDSCAPING

Sec. 30-82. - Title.
This article shall be known as "Ben Hill County, Georgia's Outdoor Landscape Watering Ordinance."
(Code 2000, § 30-50; Ord. No. 10-02, § 1(30-50), 12-7-2007)
Sec. 30-83. - Restriction on outdoor water of landscape.
Outdoor watering for purposes of planting, growing, managing, or maintaining ground cover, trees, shrubs, or other plants in the unincorporated area of the county, may occur only between the hours of 4:00 p.m. and 10:00 a.m.; provided, however, that this limitation shall not create any limitation upon the following outdoor water uses:
(1)
Commercial raising, harvesting, or storing of crops; feeding, breeding, or managing livestock or poultry; the commercial production or storing of feed for use in the production of livestock, including, but not limited to, cattle, calves, swine, hogs, goats, sheep, and rabbits, or for use in the production of poultry, including, but not limited to, chickens, hens, ratites, and turkeys; producing plants, trees, fowl, or animals; or the commercial production of aquacultural, horticultural, dairy, livestock, poultry, eggs, and apiarian products or as otherwise defined in O.C.G.A. § 1-3-3;
(2)
Capture and reuse of cooling system condensate or stormwater in compliance with applicable ordinances and state guidelines;
(3)
Reuse of gray water in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 31-3-5.2 and applicable local board of health regulations;
(4)
Use of reclaimed wastewater by a designated user from a system permitted by the environmental protection division of the state department of natural resources to provide reclaimed wastewater;
(5)
Watering personal food gardens;
(6)
Watering new and replanted plant, seed, or turf in landscapes, golf courses, or sports turf fields during installation and for a period of 30 days immediately following the date of installation;
(7)
Drip irrigation or irrigation using soaker hoses;
(8)
Hand watering with a hose with automatic cutoff or handheld container;
(9)
Use of water withdrawn from private water wells or surface water by an owner or operator of property if such well or surface water is on said property;
(10)
Watering horticultural crops held for sale, resale, or installation;
(11)
Watering athletic fields, golf courses, or public turf grass recreational areas;
(12)
Installation, maintenance, or calibration or irrigation systems; or
(13)
Hydroseeding.
(Ord. No. 10-02, § 1(30-51), 12-7-2007)
Sec. 30-84. - Enforcement.
(a)
No person shall use or allow the use of water in violation of the restrictions on outdoor water use contained in this article.
(b)
The board of commissioners or county manager shall authorize the person or department to be the enforcement authority for this article. The board of commissioners or county manager may also authorize other departments, as may be deemed necessary, to support enforcement.
(c)
Criminal and alternative penalties. Any violation of this article may also be enforced by a citation or accusation returnable to the magistrate court or by any other legal means as set forth in this Code.
(Ord. No. 10-02, § 1(30-52), 12-7-2007)
