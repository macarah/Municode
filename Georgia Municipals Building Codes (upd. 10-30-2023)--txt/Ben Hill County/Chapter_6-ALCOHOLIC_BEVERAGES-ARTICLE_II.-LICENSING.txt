ARTICLE II. - LICENSING

Sec. 6-36. - License required.
(a)
No alcoholic beverage may be sold, possessed for sale or distributed in the unincorporated area of the county except under a license granted by the county board of commissioners.
(b)
Licenses granted hereunder shall fall into one or more of the following classes:
(1)
Class A: On-premises consumption of beer (malt beverages).
(2)
Class B: Off-premises consumption of beer (malt beverages).
(3)
Class C: On-premises consumption of wine.
(4)
Class D: Off-premises consumption of wine.
(5)
Class E: Off-premises consumption of liquor (distilled spirits).
(6)
Class F: On-premises consumption of liquor (distilled spirits).
(6)
Class G: Wholesale distribution of beer (malt beverages).
(7)
Class H: Wholesale distribution of wine.
(8)
Class I: Wholesale distribution of liquor (distilled spirits).
(Code 2000, § 6-31; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002; Res. No. 2021-02, 2-2-2021)
Sec. 6-37. - Filing application.
(a)
Form; information to be provided.
(1)
A written application to the board of county commissioners for a license under this chapter shall be made on forms approved by board of county commissioners. All questions and information required by the application form should be filled in and subscribed to by all applicants, under oath, and shall disclose among other information whether the applicant has been convicted of any crime, misdemeanor or a violation of any municipal ordinance (except minor traffic violations) in any state, county, municipal or federal court, and the particulars of same; whether applicant has any existing liquor, beer or wine business in the state; whether the interest of applicant is total, partial, or exact extent of same, and, if partial, the names and addresses of all others having a partial interest in said business and the extent of such interest; and such other and further information as the as the commission shall deem necessary.
(2)
It is further required that all applicants for licenses allowing the sale of liquor shall submit with their applications personnel statements. These personnel statements shall be furnished to the board of county commissioners upon request and will be required for both applications for original licenses and renewals of existing licenses.
(3)
Every license application shall be accompanied by a plat prepared by a registered surveyor, showing the distance of the premises for which the license is being applied from the nearest church, school, library or public recreation area, or from any alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by a government entity, as measured under section 6-42. The license application shall be accompanied by a copy of the lease. If the license applicant leases the property and improvements, a copy of the lease shall be maintained on a current basis in the license file. If the license applicant is the owner of the real estate and improvements where the business will be located, a copy of the deed shall accompany the application and be maintained as a part of the license file.
(4)
The application shall also include, but shall not be limited to, the name and address of the agent for service of process, who must be a resident of the county, and the name of the manager. If the manager changes, the applicant must furnish the board of county commissioners with the name and address of the new manager and other information as requested within ten days of such change.
(b)
Date due. All applications, including required documentation, shall be filed with the board of county commissioners not less than 30 days prior to the date when the application is scheduled to be heard.
(c)
Fee. Every such application, excluding renewals and transfers, must be accompanied by a nonrefundable application fee in an amount to be set by ordinance from time to time and published in the schedule of fees on file in the office of the county clerk, said fee to be paid by certified check, cashier's check or money order made payable to the board of county commissioners, as applicable.
(d)
Untrue or misleading information; material omissions. Any untrue or misleading information contained in, or material omission left out of, an original, renewal or transfer application for a license hereunder shall be cause for the denial of the license; and if any license has been granted under such circumstances, the license shall be subject to revocation.
(Code 2000, § 6-32; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-38. - Inspection of application by county manager and investigation by sheriff; provision of information to county manager upon request.
(a)
If so filed as to comply with the requirements of this chapter, an application under this chapter shall be sworn to and directed to the county manager, who shall inspect the application and refer same to the county sheriff, who shall investigate the character and reputation of the applicant, owners, partners, officers of the corporation, shareholders, managers, employees, and other associated with the application, and the suitability of the location for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
All applicants shall furnish all data, information and records requested of them by the county manager and/or the county sheriff's department, and failure to furnish such data, information and records within 30 days from the date of the request shall automatically serve to dismiss, with prejudice, the application. Applicants, by filing an application, agree to produce for oral interrogation any person involved in any transaction pertinent to the application or any evidence relevant to the application as may be requested by the county manager or the county sheriff's department. Failure to produce such evidence or person within 30 days after being requested to do so shall result in the automatic dismissal of the application.
(Code 2000, § 6-33; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-39. - Review of application by the board of county commissioners.
(a)
Procedures generally. All applications for original alcoholic beverage licenses of any type shall be submitted to the board of county commissioners, which shall review the applications in such manner as the commission may determine to be necessary. After the review is complete, the board of county commissioners shall cause to be issued such license as it may determine to be warranted and needed by the applicant. An application must be approved by a majority of the board of county commissioners before a license will be issued to an applicant. If an application is denied by the commission, an applicant may request a hearing up to 60 days after the denial of the application. Such hearing shall be heard in the superior court of the county.
(b)
Order of consideration. All applications presented shall be noted as to the date and exact time of filing, and such applications shall be presented, heard and considered by the commission in the order of their filing.
(Code 2000, § 6-34; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-40. - Criteria for license approval; personal qualifications of applicants.
A license will be issued only to a person who is a citizen of the United States or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence in the United States. An applicant who is not a resident of the county may be issued a license only upon designating a resident of the county, who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license. Where an applicant is other than a natural person, it must be an entity organized and existing under the laws of the United States or one of its states, and an agent of the entity must meet the qualifications of this section.
(Code 2000, § 6-35; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-41. - Individuals not eligible for license.
(a)
The following individuals are not eligible for an alcoholic beverage license, even if they possess all of the qualifications listed in section 6-40:
(1)
A person or any of the officers and directors who have been convicted of a felony in any jurisdiction. A conviction, for purposes of this subsection, includes a guilty plea or plea of nolo contendere.
(2)
A person or any of the officers and directors who have been convicted of a nonfelonious crime of moral turpitude, lottery, or illegal possession and sale of narcotics or liquors within the five years preceding the filing of the application. A conviction, for purposes of this subsection, includes a guilty plea or a plea of nolo contendere.
(3)
A person whose license to sell alcoholic beverages has been revoked for cause in any state or territory of the United States within the ten years preceding the filing of the application.
(4)
A person who has knowingly falsified information or made any material misrepresentation on the application for a license under this chapter or any application under the predecessor ordinance for the county submitted within the ten years preceding the filing of the application.
(5)
A person who has been convicted of a violation of any federal, state or local law pertaining to the possession, manufacture or sale of alcoholic beverages within the ten years preceding the filing of the application. For purposes of this subsection, a conviction includes a guilty plea or a plea of nolo contendere.
(b)
Should any such applicant, partner or officer or director of any applicant entity, after a license has been granted, be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to a crime involving moral turpitude, or to the violation of any laws of the state regulating possession or the sale of narcotics, drugs, liquors, wine or beer or the lottery laws of the state, said license shall be subject after hearing to immediate suspension or revocation.
(Code 2000, § 6-36; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-42. - Location restrictions.
(a)
Zoning. No license shall be issued for the sale, either at wholesale or retail, of alcoholic beverages unless the licensee's place of business is located in an area within the county that is properly zoned for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
Proximity to churches, libraries, schools, and public recreation areas. No original license shall be issued hereunder where the place of business of the licensee is located within the following distances of the following establishments:
(1)
Church, library buildings, or public recreation areas:
a.
Distilled spirits, 100 yards.
b.
Malt beverage, 100 yards.
c.
Wine, 100 yards.
(2)
School building or school grounds:
a.
Distilled spirits, 200 yards.
b.
Malt beverage, 100 yards.
c.
Wine, 100 yards.
The schools or colleges referred to herein shall include only such state, county, city, church or other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges in the state which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b) and shall not include private schools or colleges within which only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
(c)
No license authorizing the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be issued for a new location unless such proposed location is at a greater distance than 300 feet from any alcohol treatment center owned and operated by a governmental entity.
(d)
No Class A or Class C license will be issued where the proposed location is more than two miles from the nearest city limits of the City of Fitzgerald.
(e)
All measurements to determine distances for the issuance of an alcoholic beverage license shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the front door of the structure from which alcoholic beverages are sold or offered for sale;
(2)
In a straight line to the nearest public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway;
(3)
Along such public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway by the nearest route;
(4)
To the front door of the building, or to the grounds, whichever is applicable.
(f)
No license in effect on the date of the adoption of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived shall be revoked before its day of expiration by reason of the method of measurement set out in this section if the license was granted in reliance on another method of measurement.
(g)
Further, the board of county commissioners may, in its discretion, issue or deny any license when there is evidence that the type and number of schools, churches, libraries or public recreation areas in the vicinity of the place of business of the licensee causes minors to frequent the immediate area, even though there is compliance with the minimum distances as provided herein.
(h)
No license shall be issued under this chapter to any premises which does not meet the requirements of all state, county, and city laws, ordinances and regulations which would apply to said premises, including, but not limited to, building, fire, and sanitation codes.
(i)
The restrictions on locations as stated herein are in addition to, and not in lieu of, any restrictions imposed by the laws of the state.
(j)
If an application is either withdrawn from consideration or denied, no application may be made by any person for the location for which the withdrawn or denied application was made for a period of 12 months.
(k)
If a license is suspended, no application may be made by any person for the location for which the suspended license was held, until such time as the period of suspension for said license expires.
(l)
If a license is revoked, no application may be made by a person for the location for which the revoked license was held for a period of 12 months from the date of revocation.
(Code 2000, § 6-37; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-43. - Additional restrictions.
(a)
In determining whether or not any license applied for hereunder shall be granted, renewed, transferred or issued to a new location, in addition to all the provisions of this chapter, the following shall be considered in the public interest and welfare:
(1)
Reputation, character. The applicant's reputation, character, trade and business association or past business ventures, mental and physical capacity to conduct this business.
(2)
Previous violations of liquor laws. If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic liquors, whether or not he has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
(3)
Manner of conducting prior liquor business. If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic liquors, the manner in which he conducted the business thereunder, especially as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
(4)
Location. The location for which the license is sought, as to traffic congestion, general character of neighborhood, and the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values.
(5)
Number of licenses in trading area. The number of licenses already granted for similar business in the trading area of the place for which the license is sought.
(6)
Dancing. If dancing is to be permitted upon the premises for which the license is sought and the applicant has previously permitted dancing upon any premises controlled or supervised by him, the manner in which he controlled or supervised such dancing to prevent any violation of any law, regulation or ordinance.
(7)
Previous revocation of license. If the applicant is a person whose license issued under the police powers of any governing authority has been previously suspended or revoked or who has previously had an alcoholic beverages license suspended or revoked.
(8)
Payment of taxes. If the applicant and business are not delinquent in the payment of any local taxes.
(9)
Congregation of minors. Any circumstances which may cause minors to congregate in the vicinity of the proposed location, even if the location meets the distance requirement under section 6-42.
(10)
Prior incidents. Evidence that a substantial number of incidents requiring intervention by the county sheriff's office have occurred within the immediate area of the proposed location in the unincorporated area of the county during the 12 months immediately preceding the date of application.
(11)
Previous denial or revocation. The denial of an application, or the revocation of a license, occurring within the preceding 12 months, which was based on the qualifications of the proposed location.
(b)
If the application is denied, the board of county commissioners shall cause a written report to be prepared showing the reason for the denial. The commission shall return the application showing its denial, together with the written report, to the county manager who shall notify the applicant of the denial within five days of the denial. Notice to the applicant shall be made in writing, showing the reason for the denial and the day and time of the next scheduled meeting of the commission.
(Code 2000, § 6-38; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-44. - Time limit for acquiring licenses once approved.
All licenses must be obtained not later than 90 days from the date of the approval of the application, and, if not so obtained, the license shall be void. Extensions of time under this section shall be granted only by the board of county commissioners.
(Code 2000, § 6-139; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-45. - License fee.
(a)
When due and payable. When a license has been approved and granted, the same shall be paid for by certified check, cashier's check or money order for the full amount of the license fee.
(b)
Amount. Each applicant shall pay a license fee in an amount set by ordinance and published in the schedule of fees on file in the office of the county clerk.
(c)
Proration. The full license fee must be paid for a license issued prior to July 1 of the license year. One-half of the full license fee shall be paid for a license issued after July 1 of the license year.
(d)
Refund. In case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of the calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund whatsoever.
(Code 2000, § 6-40; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-46. - Term of license.
All licenses granted hereunder shall be for the calendar year.
(Code 2000, § 6-41; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-47. - Presumption of ownership.
The alcoholic beverage kept, stored or found at the license holder's licensed place of business or at his warehouse shall be presumed to be his property.
(Code 2000, § 6-42; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-48. - Licenses not transferable; exceptions; fee; new ownership.
(a)
Generally. Licenses hereunder shall not be transferable, except as otherwise provided herein. Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more partners of a partnership holding a license from withdrawing from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license.
(b)
Exceptions.
(1)
Estate administration, etc. In case of the death of any person holding a license, or any interest therein, the same may be transferred to the administrator, executor, or the lawful heirs of the deceased person, if otherwise qualified.
(2)
Change of location. Should a transfer of a location be approved, there shall be no additional license fee, and the new location shall not be considered as a new license hereunder.
(3)
Addition of partners or stockholders. A licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business, or for building a new facility, and where it appears that the licensee himself receives directly none of the additional capital invested. Under this section an additional partner or new stockholder must be approved by the county manager and the county sheriff's department.
(4)
Procedure for application for exception. Any licensee desiring a transfer of a license pursuant to an exception hereunder shall notify the county manager of the basis of the exception and shall provide the county manager with any information and/or documentation requested in connection with the exception. The county manager shall then either approve or deny the exception. If the exception is approved, the license shall be transferred by the county manager.
(c)
Fee for transfers. There shall be a fee of an amount set by ordinance and published in the schedule of fees on file in the office of the county clerk for transfers pursuant to this section.
(d)
New ownership; retail alcoholic beverage licenses. No retail beer, wine or distilled spirits license shall be transferred, but where a change in ownership is contemplated, the new owner shall file a new application for a license. Changes in license ownership from one party at interest named in the original application to another party at interest as named in the original application, and changes of license from one employer or manager of any social club to a successor employer or manager of the same club, shall not be deemed a transfer of license within the prohibition against transfer contained in these regulations. In each instance the board of county commissioners shall be advised in writing of such change and a complete disclosure of all facts in connection therewith shall be made at the time such request for change is presented. An applicant for a change of license must follow the procedure for applying for an alcoholic beverage license. A transfer fee from manager to manager shall be in an amount set forth by ordinance and published in the schedule of fees on file in the office of the county clerk.
(Code 2000, § 6-43; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-49. - Renewal of licenses.
(a)
All licenses granted hereunder shall expire on December 31 of each year. Licensees who desire to renew their license shall file an application therefor, together with the requisite fee with the county manager for such renewal, upon forms approved by the county manager, on or before December 1 of each year.
(b)
All licenses to be renewed for the subsequent calendar year shall be submitted by the county manager to the commission for approval no later than December 15 of each year. Any licenses that have been placed on probation, suspension or have been revoked by the commission during the year shall be submitted on a separate list by the county manager for review and consideration for approval.
(Code 2000, § 6-44; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-50. - Denial of application to renew license.
(a)
The possession of a license under this chapter is a privilege granted by the county board of commissioners. The board of county commissioners is hereby authorized to deny the renewal of a license if it finds to exist any of the grounds constituting due cause to revoke, suspend or place on probation a license, as stated in section 6-52.
(b)
When the sheriff recommends that an application to renew a license be denied, he shall provide the county manager with a written report describing the reason for the recommendation. The county manager shall notify the commission and shall serve written notice on the licensee at least five days before the next scheduled meeting of the commission. The notice shall state the reason given by the sheriff for his recommendation to deny the renewal of the license and the time and place of the next scheduled meeting of commission.
(c)
The commission shall receive evidence in the same manner described in section 6-53.
(d)
At the conclusion of the evidence, the commission shall consider the evidence and shall decide to grant or to deny the renewal of the license.
(e)
If the application is denied, the commission shall cause a written report to be prepared showing the reason or reasons for the denial. The commission shall return the application showing its denial, together with the written report to the county manager who shall notify the applicant of the denial within five days of the denial. Notice to the applicant shall be made in writing, showing the reason for the denial and the day and time of the next scheduled meeting of the commission. The applicant may appeal the denial of the application, by serving notice on the commission requesting reconsideration of the application.
(Code 2000, § 6-45; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-51. - Cancellation of licenses.
(a)
A license issued under this chapter expires on December 31 of the year in which issued, unless it expires or is revoked earlier pursuant to this section and section 6-52.
(b)
A license issued under this chapter expires upon the individual licensee ceasing to act as licensee for any reason unless an extension is granted pursuant to subsection (c) of this section.
(c)
Failure to open for business. All applicants for licenses hereunder must, within 180 days after the approval of said license, open for business the establishment referred to in the application and license and begin the sale of the product or products authorized by the license. Failure to open the establishment and begin the sales as referred to above within the 180-day period shall serve as automatic forfeiture and cancellation of the unused license, and no refund of license fee shall be made to the license holder. Any extension of time under this section shall be granted only by the board of county commissioners.
(d)
Ceasing of operations. Any holder of a license hereunder who shall begin the operating of the business and sale of the product and products as authorized in the license, but who shall thereafter cease to operate the business and sale of the products authorized in the license for a period of at least nine months, shall automatically forfeit his license, which license shall, by virtue of said failure to operate, be cancelled without the necessity of any further action. Any extension of time under this section shall be granted only by the commission.
(Code 2000, § 6-46; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-52. - Probation, suspension and revocation.
Any license issued under this chapter may be put on probation, suspended and/or revoked by the board of county commissioners after a finding of due cause. Due cause for the probation, suspension or revocation of a license shall include but shall not be limited to, the following:
(1)
A violation of this chapter or any state or federal law governing the manufacture, transport, or sale of alcoholic beverages by the licensee or any person in his employ or in the employ of the establishment for which the license was issued.
(2)
The conviction of the licensee of a crime which would render him ineligible to apply for a license. The term "conviction," as used herein, includes a plea of guilty or nolo contendere.
(3)
Failure by the licensee, or any person in his employ or in the employ of the establishment for which the license was issued, to adequately supervise and monitor the conduct of the employees, patrons and others on the licensed premises, or on any property owned or leased by the licensee, including, but not limited to, parking lots and parking areas, or on any parking lots or areas which may be lawfully used by patrons of a licensed establishment, in order to protect the safety and well-being of the general public and of those using the premises.
(4)
The revocation of the license or permit of a business to operate by any state, county, city or other governmental authority due to a violation of any law applicable to the business including, but not limited to, building codes and health codes.
(5)
Falsifying information or making any material misrepresentation on the application for a license under this chapter.
(Code 2000, § 6-47; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-53. - Procedure for denial, probation, suspension and revocation.
(a)
Upon cause to believe that due cause exists for the denial of license issuance, or the probation, suspension or revocation of a license under this chapter, the county manager or head of any department authorized to inspect the licensed premises including the sheriff, shall file a complaint with the board of county commissioners. The county manager shall notify the licensee, in writing, of the time and place of the next meeting when such complaint shall be heard by the commission. Such notice shall be deemed sufficient when mailed to licensee at the address of the location at least five days prior to said hearing.
(b)
The commission shall hear the evidence offered by the complaining authority herein and the evidence offered by the licensee. The licensee may be represented by counsel, may offer testimony by witnesses or any other evidence and may question the witnesses of the complaining authority. At the close of the evidence, the commission shall revoke, suspend or sustain the license, or place the licensee on probation.
(c)
If the commission decides to deny issuance of a license, or revoke or suspend the license, all licenses issued to the location shall be surrendered to the county manager and no alcohol shall be sold, served or consumed on the premises.
(d)
If the commission decides to suspend the license, it shall state the number of days of the suspension. The licensee shall surrender the license to the county manager who shall return the license to the licensee at the end of the suspension period. No alcohol shall be sold, served or consumed at the location during the period of suspension.
(e)
If the commission sustains the license, the license shall remain in effect as issued.
(f)
If the commission decides to place the licensee on probation, it shall state the number of days of the probation. The licensee shall retain the license during the probation period.
(g)
The decision of the commission may be appealed by either the licensee or the complaining authority by serving notice in writing on the clerk of the board of commissioners within five days on the date of the hearing before the commission. The filing of a notice of appeal shall have no effect on the status of the license as decided by the commission and if the license was placed on probation, suspended or revoked, it shall remain so until said status is changed by the commission.
(h)
If a notice of appeal is filed with the county clerk he shall notify the appellant of the next scheduled meeting of the commission, at least five days before the date of the meeting; provided, however, that the five-day period may be waived by agreement of both parties.
(i)
At the appeal hearing, the commission shall hear the evidence offered by the complaining authority and the evidence offered by the licensee. The licensee may be represented by counsel, may offer testimony by witnesses or any other evidence and may question the witnesses of the complaining authority. At the close of the evidence, the commission shall affirm or reverse its prior decision.
(j)
If the commission decides to affirm its revocation of the license, all licenses issued to the location, if not already surrendered, shall be surrendered to the county manager and no alcohol shall be sold, served or consumed on the premises.
(k)
If the commission decides to affirm its suspension of the license, it shall state the number of days of the suspension. The licensee shall surrender the license or licenses, if not already surrendered, to the county manager shall return the license to the licensee at the end of the suspension period. No alcohol shall be sold, served or consumed at the location during the period of suspension.
(l)
If the commission reverses its prior action with regard to the license and the county manager has possession of the license or licenses, the license shall be promptly returned to the licensee.
(m)
If the commission or committee thereof decides to affirm its decision to place the licensee on probation, it shall state the number of days of the probation. The licensee shall retain the license during the period of the probation period.
(Code 2000, § 6-48; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-54. - Distance between locations of licensees.
(a)
No retail dealer license for the sale of distilled spirits shall be issued to any applicant whose proposed location is one and 500 yards or less from an existing location or establishment for which the county board of commissioners has issued a retail dealer license for the sale of distilled spirits.
(b)
The distance provided for herein shall be measured in the same manner as provided in section 6-42.
(c)
The distance requirements provided for herein shall not be construed or interpreted as prohibiting an applicant, who is otherwise qualified, from being approved and granted a license which constitutes a transfer in ownership of an existing license for distilled spirits which was previously held by an owner or operator of an existing establishment.
(Code 2000, § 6-49; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002; Ord. No. 05-001, § 1, 1-4-2005)
