Chapter 7.08 - GENERAL PARK REGULATIONS

7.08.010 - General park regulations.
A.
Generally. The following regulations shall govern activities ("specified activities") in the parks and recreational areas, exclusive of streets, roads, and public rights-of-way, owned or maintained by the city:
1.
No alcoholic beverages except during and at events officially sponsored by the city;
2.
No pets except as permitted in Title 7, Chapter 7.09 and Chapter 7.10 of this Code;
3.
No vehicles;
4.
No motor bikes;
5.
No bicycles or tricycles;
6.
No skateboards;
7.
No go carts;
8.
No scooters;
9.
No loitering in parked cars;
10.
No fundraising;
11.
No soliciting;
12.
No battery, electrical, or gas-powered flying objects;
13.
Park closed between nine-thirty (9:30) p.m. to daybreak; violators will be prosecuted;
14.
Vandals of city property will be prosecuted; see city code Section 11.04.020;
15.
Please keep park clean;
16.
Warning: area monitored by undercover security.
17.
No music shall be audible more than one hundred-fifty (150) feet from any park pavilion.
18.
Amplified music is expressly prohibited unless permitted through the city's special event permitting process. For purposes of this section, "amplified music" shall mean any music emitting from a radio, stereo or similar audio device which is made louder by an amplifier.
These regulations shall not apply to individuals who receive a permit or license permitting a specified activity pursuant to other provisions of the city code.
B.
Posting of Signs. The signs identifying regulations set forth in this section shall be displayed conspicuously in all city parks and recreational areas by the city manager.
C.
Penalty. Any person, firm or corporation violating any provision of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and punished as provided in Section 1.04.080 of this code.
D.
All dog waste must be promptly removed by dog owners and properly disposed of in designated receptacles.

7.08.020 - Reservations and fees for public parks.
A.
Reservation Required. The city council hereby requires any person or legal entity desiring to utilize the pavilions, gazebos and similar covered structures and facilities located on any public park within the city to make application and reservation for the use thereof and pay the appropriate rental fee.
B.
City Clerk as Authorized Agent. The clerk of the City of Stockbridge is hereby designated as the coordinator of the City of Stockbridge park reservations. The city clerk is authorized to act for and on behalf of the city of Stockbridge in directing and coordinating all reservations and payment of reservation fees; shall establish a park reservations form for the use of certain portions of the public park facilities of the city; and, shall document public park reservations and fee in the record of the governing body. The city clerk is authorized to delegate responsibilities under this subsection to city employees who are under the supervision of the city clerk and whose work shall be overseen by the city clerk.
C.
Rental Fees Generally. Fees for park facility rentals may be amended from time to time by the city council by ordinance. The reservation fee must be paid in full to the city clerk within seven (7) calendar days prior to the reservation date. If not paid in full the clerk shall be authorized to cancel the reservation and no refunds will be issued. Charitable and non-profit organizations, schools, and daycare centers are eligible for a waiver of rental fees, subject to sufficient documentation of said entity's status.

Chapter 7.09 - OFF-LEASH DOG PARKS

7.09.000 - Establishment and purpose.
The city is empowered to establish one (1) or more off-leash dog park(s) within the public parks of the city for the purpose of allowing the off-leash exercise of dogs, provided that any dog within such park must be under the control of an owner who is competent and knowledgeable relating to the behavior of each such dog owned.
(Ord. No. 13-337, § 1, 12-9-2013)
7.09.010 - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply to the terms used in this chapter:
"Dog owner" is defined as any person or legal entity, including, but not limited to a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dog. In the case of a dog owned by a minor, the term "dog owner" includes the parents or person in loco parentis with custody of the said minor.
"Dog park" is an enclosed and/or fenced facility, within a city public park and accessible to the public, designed by the city for the purpose of allowing dogs, under the control of their owners, to exercise and socialize while off-leash.
"Visual control" shall be defined as an owner being within seventy-five (75) feet of the dog at issue, able to control or recall the dog at all times, and preventing the dog from becoming involved in any altercation with other dogs or people.
"Voice control" shall be defined to include dogs that immediately come to their owner when so commanded by their owner.
(Ord. No. 13-337, § 1, 12-9-2013)
7.09.020 - Dog registration and fees.
(a)
All dogs using the dog park must be registered with the city clerk's office.
(b)
All registration fees must be paid at the time of registration submittal.
(c)
Registration fees shall be in an amount established by city council resolution.
(d)
The city council shall set registration fees in an amount that is reasonably calculated to reimburse the city for maintenance of the dog park(s).
(Ord. No. 13-337, § 1, 12-9-2013)
7.09.030 - Responsibilities of dog park users.
(a)
Owners must possess documentation that their dog(s)' vaccinations are current. Current vaccination tags must be displayed on each dog's collar at all times while on the premises of a dog park.
(b)
All dogs using a dog park shall be free of contagious or infectious diseases, be parasite free both externally and internally, and have no visible wounds or injuries.
(c)
No more than three (3) dogs per owner are allowed within a dog park.
(d)
The owner of each dog inside the dog park must also be inside the enclosed dog park and have both visual and voice control over each of their dogs at all times. Dogs shall not be left unattended at or inside a park.
(e)
All dogs must wear a collar at all times while in a dog park, however, spike, choke, and gentle-leader style electronic collars are not permitted within the dog park enclosure.
(f)
Owners must fill in any holes created at a dog park by their dog(s).
(g)
Owners must remove their dog(s) when they become engaged in excessive parking or are exhibiting signs of aggressive behavior.
(h)
All dog waste must be promptly removed by dog owners and properly disposed of in designated receptacles.
(i)
Owners must also abide by all rules posted by the city at any dog park. Owners are charged with the responsibility of familiarizing themselves with all posted city dog park rules.
(Ord. No. 13-337, § 1, 12-9-2013)
7.09.040 - Prohibitions.
To ensure the safety of all dogs and their owners, the following are not permitted in any dog park;
(a)
Animals other than dogs;
(b)
Female dogs while in heat;
(c)
Dogs having a previous history of aggressive behavior towards other animals or humans;
(d)
The use of bicycles, roller blades/skates, skateboards, and other similar types of equipment;
(e)
Glass bottles and similar breakable containers;
(f)
Alcoholic beverages;
(g)
Smoking; or
(h)
Professional dog trainers may not use any dog park facility in conjunction with the operation of their dog training business.
(Ord. No. 13-337, § 1, 12-9-2013)
7.09.050 - Liability.
(a)
Owners are responsible for and liable for all injuries and damages caused by their dogs.
(b)
Use of any city dog park shall constitute implied consent of a dog owner to all conditions of this ordinance and shall constitute a waiver of liability to the city. As such, users of any city dog park agree and undertake to protect, indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the city for any injury or damage caused by or to their dog(s) during any time that said dog is at a dog park facility.
(Ord. No. 13-337, § 1, 12-9-2013)
7.09.060 - Enforcement.
(a)
Any person found to be in violation of this dog park ordinance and/or rules posted for a dog park by the city is subject to removal from the facility and may be prohibited from future use of city dog parks, and may also have his or her dog impounded.
(b)
Any person who violates any provision of this ordinance or the rules posted for a dog park by the city may be subject to a fine of up to one hundred dollars ($100.00).
(Ord. No. 13-337, § 1, 12-9-2013)
Chapter 7.10 - DOGS ON PARK TRAILS

7.10.000 - Establishment and purpose.
This chapter is being adopted for the purpose of allowing the on-leash exercise of dogs on certain trails, provided that any dog on such trails must be on a leash and under the control of an owner who is competent and knowledgeable relating to the behavior of each such dog owned.
(Ord. No. 15-378, § 3, 10-12-2015)
7.10.010 - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply to the terms used in this chapter:
"Dog owner" is defined as any person or legal entity, including, but not limited to a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dog. In the case of a dog owned by a minor, the term "dog owner" includes the parents or person in loco parentis with custody of the said minor.
"Trail" is the Reeves Creek Trail.
(Ord. No. 15-378, § 3, 10-12-2015)
7.10.020 - Authorization of dogs on trail.
Subject to the conditions and requirements of this chapter, dog owners are permitted to exercise dogs on the trail.
(Ord. No. 15-378, § 3, 10-12-2015)
7.10.030 - Responsibilities of trail users.
A.
Owners must possess documentation that their dog(s)' vaccinations are current. Current vaccination tags must be displayed on each dog's collar at all times while on the trail.
B.
All dogs using the trail shall be free of contagious or infectious diseases, be parasite free both externally and internally, and have no visible wounds or injuries.
C.
No more than three (3) dogs per owner are allowed on the trail at any one time.
D.
Dogs shall not be left unattended on the trail.
E.
All dogs must be on a leash and wear a collar at all times while in the trail, however, spike, choke, and gentle-leader style electronic collars are not permitted within the trail.
F.
Owners must fill in any holes created on the trail by their dog(s).
G.
Owners must remove their dog(s) when they become engaged in excessive barking or are exhibiting signs of aggressive behavior.
H.
All dog waste must be promptly removed by dog owners and properly disposed of in designated receptacles.
I.
Owners must also abide by all rules posted by the city at any park. Owners are charged with the responsibility of familiarizing themselves with all posted city park rules.
(Ord. No. 15-378, § 3, 10-12-2015)
7.10.040 - Prohibitions.
To ensure the safety of all citizens, in addition to any other restrictions for city parks, the following are not permitted on the trail in connection with the exercise eon dogs on the trail:
A.
Animals other than dogs;
B.
Female dogs while in heat; or
C.
Dogs having a previous history of aggressive behavior towards other animals or humans.
(Ord. No. 15-378, § 3, 10-12-2015)
7.10.050 - Liability.
A.
Owners are responsible for and liable for all injuries and damages caused by their dogs.
B.
Use of the trail shall constitute implied consent of a dog owner to all conditions of this chapter and shall constitute a waiver of liability to the city. As such, users of the trail agree and undertake to protect, indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the city for any injury or damage caused by or to their dog(s) during any time that said dog is on the trail.
(Ord. No. 15-378, § 3, 10-12-2015)
7.10.060 - Enforcement.
A.
Any person found to be in violation of this chapter and/or rules posted for a trail by the city is subject to removal from the facility and may be prohibited from future use of the trail, and may also have his or her dog impounded.
C.
Any person who violates any provision of this Chapter or the rules posted for the trail by the city may be subject to a fine as permitted under the City Charter.
(Ord. No. 15-378, § 3, 10-12-2015)
Chapter 7.11 - YOUTH COUNCIL ADVISORY COMMITTEE

7.11.010 - Creation.
The City of Stockbridge Youth Council Advisory Committee is hereby established, and shall be referred to as the youth council advisory committee.
(Ord. No. 17-445, § 1(Exh. A), 11-13-2017)
7.11.020 - Membership.
A.
Composition.
1.
The youth council advisory committee shall be composed of up to twelve (12) qualified members.
2.
Each member shall be appointed by the mayor and council.
B.
Term of Office. All members shall be appointed for a term of two (2) years. An appointment to fill any vacancy shall be for the remainder of the unexpired term of office.
C.
Removal. Notwithstanding any provision to the contrary, a member may be removed by a majority vote of mayor and council.
D.
Qualifications. In order to be qualified, all members shall be residents of Henry County and shall pass a background examination.
(Ord. No. 17-445, § 1(Exh. A), 11-13-2017; Ord. No. 19-445, § 1, 5-28-2019; Ord. No. 19-485, § 1, 6-10-2019)
7.11.030 - Quorum.
A.
Five (5) members shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.
(Ord. No. 17-445, § 1(Exh. A), 11-13-2017)
7.11.040 - Officers.
A.
The youth council advisory committee shall elect one (1) of its appointed members as chair. The chair shall serve for one (1) year or until re-elected or a successor is elected.
B.
A second appointed member shall be elected as vice chair and shall serve for one (1) year or until re-elected or a successor is elected.
C.
The youth council advisory committee shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or an employee of the governing authority or a member of the youth council advisory committee.
(Ord. No. 17-445, § 1(Exh. A), 11-13-2017)
7.11.050 - Meetings.
A.
The youth council advisory committee shall establish annual meeting schedule. This schedule may be altered due to unforeseen circumstances at the discretion of the youth council advisory committee.
B.
Additional meetings may be called according to procedures established by the youth council advisory committee in accordance with the Open Records Act of the state.
(Ord. No. 17-445, § 1(Exh. A), 11-13-2017)
7.11.060 - Rules and operating procedures.
A.
The youth council advisory committee may establish and follow any by-laws and rules for the transaction of business subject to approval by the mayor and council.
B.
The youth council advisory committee shall maintain a record of its activity which record shall be a public record.
(Ord. No. 17-445, § 1(Exh. A), 11-13-2017)
7.11.070 - Duties and responsibilities.
A.
The youth council advisory committee shall have the following powers and duties:
1.
Prepare and recommend for adoption each year by the city a curriculum and budget for the youth council program.
2.
Review, evaluate and select youth candidates for participation in the program.
3.
Monitor and provide oversight of the youth council program throughout the year.
B.
All city staff shall, upon request and within a reasonable time, furnish to the youth council advisory committee such available information as the board may require for performance of its work.
(Ord. No. 17-445, § 1(Exh. A), 11-13-2017)
