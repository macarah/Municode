ARTICLE II. - VIDEO ARCADES

Sec. 4-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Game room means any establishment wherein more than two video, electronic or mechanical devices are used for the playing of games or recreation, for a charge.
(Ord. No. 73, § 1, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-20. - Intent.
It is the intention of the mayor and council, in enacting the ordinance from which this article is derived regulating video, electronic or mechanical devices used for the playing of games or recreation for a charge located within the city, that the city not become a center for truancy and to encourage school attendance.
(Ord. No. 73, § 2, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-21. - Business license required.
A video, electronic or mechanical device used for the playing of games or recreation for a charge shall only be operated after payment of the license fee as hereinafter set forth. Each video, electronic or mechanical device used for the playing of games or recreation located within the city shall have an annual license fee of $25.00 per calendar year payable on the date each machine is installed. This business license fee is also applicable to those businesses not defined as a game room as set forth in section 4-19. The annual license fee shall be paid without prorating the fee regardless of when during a calendar year a machine is installed.
(Ord. No. 73, § 3, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-22. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation to operate a game room between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 11:00 a.m. or to permit any person to remain in a game room between said prohibited hours, except regular employees performing necessary labor on the premises. It shall be unlawful to operate any game room on Sunday in the city.
(Ord. No. 73, § 4, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-23. - Lighting and maintenance requirements.
A game room or establishment in which there is located video, electronic or mechanical devices used for the playing of games or recreation for a charge shall be well lighted and shall not permit any screens, curtains, blinds, partitions or other obstructions to be used to prevent a clear view of the interior, which clear view must be maintained at all times. This provision, however, shall not be construed to prohibit the maintenance of washrooms and toilet rooms for proper purposes, nor the maintenance of closets for storage purposes exclusively.
(Ord. No. 73, § 5, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-24. - Adult attendant.
An adult attendant shall be in attendance at a game room at all times when same is open to the public.
(Ord. No. 73, § 6, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-25. - School hours.
It shall be unlawful for any person who has not reached the age of 17 years to play games or to be permitted to remain in the game room for any purpose during the hours between 8:00 a.m. and 3:00 p.m., Monday through Friday, while the county school system is in session.
(Ord. No. 73, § 7, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-26. - Gambling, drinking and drugs.
It shall be unlawful for any gambling, drinking of alcoholic beverages or use of drugs to be allowed or conducted on the premises of a game room.
(Ord. No. 73, § 8, 7-12-1982)
Sec. 4-27. - Punishment for violation.
Any owner of a game room or establishment in which there is located video, electronic or mechanical devices used for the playing of games or recreation for a charge or any of the agents or employees of any game room or establishment in which there is located video, electronic or mechanical devices used for the playing of games or recreation for a charge, who violates any of the provisions of this article shall be punished by revocation of the license of the owner to operate video, electronic or mechanical devices used for the playing of games or recreation for a charge within the city limits.
(Ord. No. 73, § 9, 7-12-1982)
