Chapter 36 - UTILITIES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 36-001—36-018. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - WATER AND SEWER SERVICES

Sec. 36-019. - Water and sewer connection required inside the city limits.
A.
All persons having homes, stores, shops, offices or places of business either as owner, tenant or occupant inside the city limits shall be connected to the city water and sewer system where available as determined by the city.
B.
Private wells for residential or commercial use are prohibited inside the city limits.
(Prior Code, § 37-001)
Sec. 36-020. - Water and sewer connection optional outside city limits; where available, connections to the city's water and sewer system are optional to residents outside the city limits.
A.
When a property owner requests utility services to property not within the corporate city limits, at the discretion of the city, the owner will be required to:
1.
Petition for annexation, if the property is contiguous to the existing city limits, prior to commencement of construction of utility lines; or
2.
Execute a restrictive covenant consenting to annexation of such property into the city when the property becomes contiguous.
B.
When such property is not contiguous and execution of the covenant is required, the owner will also be required to submit a certificate of title to the city attorney to verify ownership of the property. The covenant will be recorded in the property records at the county courthouse and will stipulate that, if the property becomes contiguous in the future and the property owner refuses to petition for annexation, the city will have the authority to terminate utility services.
C.
Construction of utility lines will not begin until the requirements of this policy are met, which shall be indicated by authorization of the mayor and council.
(Prior Code, § 37-002)
Sec. 36-021. - Connection fees for water and sewer.
A.
All water and sewer connections shall be executed by city personnel. Water and sewer connection fees shall be as determined from time to time.
B.
Any other dimensions for water and sewer connections not addressed in this section shall be determined on an as needed basis by the public works director.
C.
The city reserves the right to negotiate connection fees based on special circumstances as determined by the city for commercial and industrial consumers and for residential connections when applications are for a two inch water meter or larger.
D.
Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary in this article, a commercial development or an apartment complex may utilize a master water meter and be billed by the city for the entire development or complex, provided said development or complex has more than four units. A holder of a master water meter may meter and require its tenants to meter individual usage and reimburse the holder of the master meter for water and sewer usage; provided, however, said reimbursement shall not exceed the cost of the water or sewer paid to the city by the master meter holder.
(Prior Code, § 37-003)
Sec. 36-022. - Application for service.
Once availability of service is determined and connection fees are paid, the consumer shall make application for service at city hall. A picture ID is required of the person making application. If it is determined the applicant has an outstanding balance, payment in full of that balance must be paid before service shall commence.
(Prior Code, § 37-004)
Sec. 36-023. - Security deposit for service.
All individuals (residential and commercial) applying for service shall at the time of application pay a security deposit.
(Prior Code, § 37-005)
Sec. 36-024. - Rate schedule.
The water, sewer, and sprinkler/irrigation rates and criteria are as determined from time to time by ordinance.
(Prior Code, § 37-006; Ord. of 6-9-2009; Ord. of 8-11-2009; Ord. of 10-14-2008; Ord. of 7-14-2010, § 37-006)
Sec. 36-025. - Billing cycle and late fees.
A.
Bills shall be mailed the last business day of each month with a billing date of the first day of the following month.
B.
A ten percent penalty charge based on unpaid balances will be assessed the next business day after the tenth of each month. In the event the tenth day of the month falls on a nonbusiness day, the next business day will be considered the tenth day and penalties will accrue the day after.
C.
A delinquent charge as determined from time to time by ordinance will be assessed to each account that remains unpaid after the 20th of each month, at which point service is subject to disconnection. In the event the 20th day of the month falls on a nonbusiness day, the next business day will be considered the 20th day and the delinquent charge will accrue the day after.
D.
Failure to receive bills or notices shall not prevent bills from becoming delinquent nor relieve the consumer from payment of the bill or any subsequent late charges.
(Prior Code, § 37-007)
Sec. 36-026. - Change of occupancy and transfer fees.
A.
Not less than three days' notice shall be given, in person or in writing, at city hall to discontinue service or to transfer service to another location. The outgoing party shall be responsible for all water consumed up to the time of departure or the time specified for departure, whichever period is longer. The new occupant shall apply for service within 48 hours after occupying the premises and failure to do so will make such person liable for paying for the service furnished since the last meter reading.
B.
A transfer fee shall be collected at such time when a customer chooses to relocate service from one location to another within the city's water and sewer system.
C.
In the event that the customer does not have a sufficient deposit amount on an active account as described in section 36-023, payment of such shall be made at this time. In addition, any information required as described in section 36-022 that is not on file shall be provided as well.
(Prior Code, § 37-008)
Sec. 36-027. - Sprinkler meters.
A.
Sprinkler meters are for the specific purpose of outdoor watering only. Fees for sprinkler meter connections inside and outside the city limits are as determined from time to time.
B.
Any other dimensions for sprinkler meters not addressed in this section shall be determined on an as needed basis by the public works director.
C.
The city reserves the right to negotiate connection fees based on special circumstances as determined by the city for commercial and industrial consumers and for residential connections when applications are for a two inch meter or larger.
D.
Sprinkler meters shall be installed per work order when payment is made in full for the meter. Once the meter is installed monthly charges shall commence. Billing for sprinkler meters shall be for 12 months of every year for existing sprinkler meters as well as for new connections. Billing charges shall be the minimum charge plus any additional charges that may be applicable based on consumption.
E.
In order for monthly charges not to be incurred, one of the two following options must occur:
1.
Notification in writing by the consumer to the city that the meter is no longer needed and removal of the meter is requested. If for any reason in the future the consumer wishes to have the meter reinstalled, a fee as determined from time to time by ordinance will be charged. The meter will then be reinstalled upon payment of the fee and monthly billing on the meter will resume.
2.
Notification in writing by the consumer to the city that the consumer wishes to have the meter to remain installed but locked by city personnel there will be a cut off fee when the meter is locked and a cut on fee when the consumer wishes to have the meter unlocked. Once the meter is unlocked billing on the meter will resume.
(Prior Code, § 37-009)
Sec. 36-028. - City's responsibility and liability.
A.
The city shall run a water service line from its distribution line to the property line where the distribution line exists or is to be constructed and runs immediately adjacent and parallel to the property to be served. No service charge, other than the connection fee referred to in sections 36-021 and 36-027 will be made for a three-fourths-inch meter. A proportionately greater charge than the connection fee will be made for a meter of larger dimensions.
B.
The city may make connections to service other properties not adjacent to its lines upon payment of reasonable costs for the extensions of its distribution lines as may be required to render such service.
C.
The city may install its meter at or near the property line or at the city's option, on the consumer's property within three feet of the property line.
D.
The city reserves the right to refuse water and sewer service unless the consumer's lines or pipings are installed in such a manner as to prevent cross connections or backflow.
E.
Under normal conditions, the consumer will be notified of any anticipated interruptions of service by the city.
(Prior Code, § 37-010)
Sec. 36-029. - Consumer's responsibility and liability.
A.
Water furnished by the city shall be used for consumption by the consumer, members of the consumer's household and employees only. Except as provided in section 36-021, the consumer shall not sell water to any other person or permit any other person to use such water. Water shall not be used for irrigation, fire protection, or other purposes, except when water is available in sufficient quantity without interfering with the regular domestic consumption in the area served. Disregard for this rule shall be sufficient cause for refusal or discontinuance of service.
B.
Where the meter or meter box is placed on the premises of a consumer, a suitable place shall be provided by the consumer therefor, unobstructed and accessible at all times to the meter reader.
C.
The consumer shall furnish and maintain a private cutoff valve on the consumer's side of the meter as well as a backflow preventor.
D.
The consumer's piping and apparatus shall be installed and maintained by the consumer at the consumer's expense, in a safe and efficient manner, and in accordance with the sanitary regulations of the state board of health.
E.
In order to be received as a consumer and entitled to receive water from the city's water system, all applicants must offer proof that any private wells located on their property are not physically connected to the lines of the city's covenant and agree that, so long as they continue to be consumers of the city they will not permit the connection of any private wells on their property to the city's water system.
F.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tap, connect with, disturb, damage, turn on or off any main, service connection or meter of any public utility in the city, whether such device is within the public ways or on private premises, unless such person is acting by the consent of or under direction of the utility owning such property.
1.
Proof that any appliances, tools, devices or instruments of any kind have been used to improperly pass water around the meter so as to prevent the meter from duly registering shall constitute prima facie evidence of the commission or the intent to commit the acts described in subsection F. of this section.
2.
Where there is no evidence to the contrary, the following shall be presumed to be responsible for the illegal act set forth in subsection F. of this section:
a.
The person performing any of the illegal acts;
b.
The person who, with knowledge of such violation, receives the benefit of such service without proper charge as a result of the improper action; and
c.
The person in whose name the utilities are in at the time of the illegal act.
The consequences of such illegal acts shall be criminal charges imposed by the Butler Police Department and fines to be collected from such charges to be determined by the city's municipal court judge.
(Prior Code, § 37-011)
Sec. 36-030. - Access to premises and extensions of system.
A.
Duly authorized agents of the city shall have access at all hours to the premises of the consumer for the purpose of installing or removing city property; inspecting piping, reading and testing meters; or for any other purpose in connection with the utility service and its facilities.
B.
Extensions to the system shall be made only when the consumer shall grant or convey or shall cause to be granted or conveyed to the city a permanent easement or right-of-way across any property traversed by the water and or sewer lines.
(Prior Code, § 37-012)
Sec. 36-031. - Minimum charges.
The minimum charge, as provided in the utility rate schedules, shall be made for such connection subscribed for. Utility service furnished for a given lot shall be used on that lot only, and, except for water or fire protection, the city shall not under any condition furnish utility service free of charge to anyone.
(Prior Code, § 37-013)
Sec. 36-032. - Discontinuance of service.
A.
When utility service is discontinued either at the request of the consumer or due to nonpayment, the security deposit on file will be applied to the balance (if any) on the account and the remainder of the deposit (if any) shall be refunded by check and payable to the individual whose name appears on the utility account.
B.
In the event the security deposit is insufficient to cover the balance on the account and the customer does not remit payment to cover said amount, the individual whose name is on the account is subject to referral to a collection agency. Said individual shall be responsible for the amount due on the account as well as any addition charges that may be imposed by the collection agency. Additional charges may include but are not limited to, postage, second notices, collection fees, court processing fees and attorney fees.
C.
The city reserves the right to discontinue its service without notice for the following additional reasons:
1.
To prevent fraud or abuse;
2.
The consumer's willful disregard of the city's rules;
3.
Emergency repairs;
4.
Insufficiency of supply due to circumstances beyond the city's control;
5.
Legal processes;
6.
Direction of public authorities;
7.
Strike, riot, fire, flood, unavoidable accident.
(Prior Code, § 37-014)
Sec. 36-033. - Tampering with meter.
Charges will be filed if meter is tampered with in any way. Charge will be a minimum of $1,000.00. Services will not be connected/reconnected until the $1,000.00 and balance of bill is paid in full.
(Ord. of 5-10-2016)
Sec. 36-034. - Cut-off agreements.
Only two allowed per year; delinquent fee is applied.
(Ord. of 5-10-2016)
Sec. 36-035 - Connection/disconnection times.
No services will be connected or disconnected after 4:00 p.m. If services must be cut off/on, the on-call employee will receive two hours overtime.
(Ord. of 5-10-2016)
