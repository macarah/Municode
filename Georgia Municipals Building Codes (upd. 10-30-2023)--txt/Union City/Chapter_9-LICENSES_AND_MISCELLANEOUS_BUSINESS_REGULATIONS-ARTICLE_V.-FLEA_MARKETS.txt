ARTICLE V. - FLEA MARKETS

Sec. 9-116. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business premises means the area of a flea market described in a flea market operator's license or application for license, as provided for in this article.
Flea market means a market or business dealing primarily in inexpensive antiques, junk, reproductions, souvenirs, memorabilia, household items, personal effects and any other similar new or used items.
Flea market operator means a person who operates a flea market, as defined in this section, within the city.
(Ord. No. 78-3, 7-11-78)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 9-117. - License required.
Each person who desires to establish a flea market and does business within this municipality shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this article.
(Ord. No. 78-3, 7-11-78)
Sec. 9-118. - Application for license.
An application for a license under this article shall be made as provided in article I of this chapter, except that such application shall contain the following additional information:
(1)
Trade names used during the previous five (5) years by the applicant and each person signing the application, along with the locations of prior establishments;
(2)
Names and addresses of employees of each person signing the application during the previous five (5) years;
(3)
Name, the residence address and telephone number of each person employed or intended to be employed in the business as of the time the application is filed;
(4)
A sketch of the actual premises to be used in connection with the business, giving distances in feet and showing adjoining roads, property lines, buildings and uses; and
(5)
A description of the materials with which any buildings to be used in connection with the licensed business are or are to be made; a sketch giving distances, showing the location of such buildings on the business premises; and a diagram or plan giving distances and heights, showing floors, exits, entrances, windows, ventilators and walls.
(Ord. No. 78-3, 7-11-78)
Sec. 9-119. - Fee established.
The annual business license fee for each flea market dealer doing business in the city shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such fees is on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 78-3, 7-11-78)
Sec. 9-120. - Exemption.
Nothing in this article shall be held to apply to those persons, firms or corporations previously licensed by the city as junkyards or junk dealers, as defined in section 9-25.
(Ord. No. 78-3, 7-11-78)
