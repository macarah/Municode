DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 3-400. - Imposed on sales of malt beverages.
In addition to all other taxes or license fees imposed upon dealers engaged in the city in the business of selling malt beverages as defined in and under the laws of the state, and the ordinances of the city, there is imposed and levied upon all such dealers within the city an excise tax on malt beverages to be based, computed and collected at the rate of .4166 cents per ounce. Fractional parts of cases, barrels, kegs, drums or other containers shall be computed proportionately.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-401. - Tax on wholesale sales of vinous liquors and spirituous liquors; wholesaler's license fee.
In addition to all other taxes or license fees, there is hereby imposed and levied a special or excise tax upon the wholesale sales of vinous liquors and spirituous liquors with a wholesaler's license fee as follows:
(1)
Spirituous liquors, per liter .....$0.22
(2)
Vinous liquors, per liter .....0.22
(3)
Wholesaler's license fee, per year .....100.00
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 81-4, 4-21-81; Ord. No. 99-22, § 1, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-402. - Payment of taxes by wholesale dealers; monthly reports.
Wholesale dealers and distributors of such product shall report to the city clerk on or before the tenth day of the month, next succeeding the calendar month, of the total of such products sold or distributed within the city limits during the previous month and shall accompany such report with a payment of the tax due at the rate established in section 3-401 based upon the quantities of such products so sold the previous month. Each monthly report shall be accompanied by a sworn statement that such report is a true and correct report of all sales and shipments made within the city.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-403. - Wholesale dealers and distributors; records and reports.
The license tax imposed by this division shall be computable and payable monthly as previously set forth. Each wholesale dealer or distributor selling, shipping, or delivering malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors to any retail dealer in the city, whether delivered to the retail dealer's place of business in the city or elsewhere for resale in the city, shall as a condition to the privilege of carrying on the business in the city make a true and correct record of all sales, shipments or deliveries of such products to each retail dealer in the city and immediately transmit a true and correct copy of such record to the city clerk at city hall. The record shall show the name of each retail dealer to whom a sale, shipment or delivery is made, the quantity and such other information as may be called for by the city.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-404. - Failure to make reports.
(a)
If any wholesale dealer or distributor fails or refuses to make the report provided for in this division and to collect and transmit such taxes to the city, the city shall notify such party in writing, and if the report is not made within five (5) days from the date of the notice, the city may withdraw from the wholesale dealer or distributor the privilege of doing business in the city by revoking his license.
(b)
It shall be a violation of this article for any wholesale dealer or distributor to deliver any malt beverages, vinous liquors or spirituous liquors to any retail dealer in the city without making a report to the city as provided in this division.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-405. - Penalty.
Any person violating or assisting in the violation of any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction thereof in the recorders court, be subject to punishment as provided for in section 1-17. Such person so convicted shall also be subject to having his license revoked, if he shall be a licensed dealer in malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors, either at wholesale or retail.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Secs. 3-406—3-415. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - SALES OF SPIRITUOUS LIQUORS BY THE DRINK[8]

Footnotes:
--- (8) ---
Editor's note— Section 2 of Ord. No. 99-22, adopted Nov. 16, 1999, enacted a new title to Div. 2. Sections 3—10 deleted §§ 3-416—3-423 in their entirety and enacted similar provisions to read as herein set out. Former §§ 3-416—3-423 derived from Ord. No. 79-10, §§ 7.1—7.8, adopted Sept. 4, 1979.

Sec. 3-416. - Definitions.
As used in this division, the following words and terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Drink. Any spirituous liquors served for consumption on the premises which may or may not be diluted by any other liquid.
Licensee. Any person who holds a permit from the city to sell spirituous liquors by the drink.
Monthly. The calendar month of the year.
Purchase price. The consideration received for the sale of spirituous liquor by the drink valued in money, whether received in cash or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the licensee to the purchaser, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Purchaser. Any person who orders and gives present or future consideration for any spirituous liquor by the drink.
Tax. The tax imposed by this division.
(Ord. No. 99-22, § 3, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-417. - Tax Levied.
There is hereby imposed and levied upon every sale of spirituous liquor purchased by the drink in the city a tax in the amount of three (3) percent of the purchase price of the drink.
(Ord. No. 99-22, § 4, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-418. - Billing by licensee; liability.
Every licensee licensed for the sale of spirituous liquor by the drink operating a place of business in the city shall, at the time of collecting for food and drinks served, give to the purchaser a receipt on which the price of spirituous liquor served shall be itemized separately. Where the charges for food and drink are satisfied by credit or deferred payment, the payment of the tax to the licensee may be deferred in a like manner; however, the licensee shall be liable therefor at the time and to the extent that such credits are incurred.
(Ord. No. 99-22, § 5, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-419. - Tax collection; information to city.
Every licensee or his agent is hereby authorized and directed to collect the tax herein imposed from purchaser's spirituous liquor by the drink sold within his licensed premises. Such licensee or his agent shall furnish such information as may be requested by the city to facilitate the collection of this tax.
(Ord. No. 99-22, § 6, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-420. - Payment of tax by licensee; collection fee; penalty and interest.
(a)
Due date of taxes. All taxes collected by any licensee or agent under the provisions of this division shall be due and payable to the city monthly on or before the twentieth day of every month next succeeding each respective monthly, as set forth in this division.
(b)
Return; time of filing; persons required to file; execution. On or before the twentieth day of the month following each monthly period, a return for the preceding monthly period shall be filed with the city clerk in such form as the city may prescribe by every licensee or agent liable for the payment of tax required by this division.
(c)
Contents of return. All returns shall show the gross receipts from the sale of spirituous liquors by the drink, amount of tax collected or authorized due for the related period, and such other information as may be required by the city.
(d)
Delivery of return and remittance. The person required to file the return shall deliver the return, together with the remittance of the net amount of tax due to the city clerk at the city hall.
(e)
Collection fees allowed licensees. Licensees collecting the tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and paying the amount due, if the amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be three (3) percent for the first three thousand dollars ($3,000.00) of tax and one-half (½) percent of the amount of tax in excess of three thousand dollars ($3,000.00).
(f)
Penalty and interest for untimely payment. A penalty of fifteen (15) percent shall be imposed for failure to pay the full amount of tax when due and payable to the city. Delinquent amounts shall bear interest at the rate of one (1) percent per month, or fraction thereof, until paid.
(Ord. No. 99-22, § 7, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-421. - Deficiency determinations.
(a)
Recomputation of tax; authority to make; basis of recomputation. If the city clerk is not satisfied with the return of the tax or the amount of the tax required to be paid to the city by any person, he/she may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information within his/her possession or that may come into his/her possession. One (1) or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one (1) or more monthly periods.
(b)
Interest and penalty on deficiency. The amount of the determination, exclusive of penalties, shall bear interest at the rate of one (1) percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the twentieth day after the close of the monthly period for which the amount or any portion thereof should have been returned, until the date of payment. A penalty of fifteen (15) percent shall be imposed upon the amount of any determination.
(c)
Offsetting of overpayments. In making a determination, the city clerk may offset overpayment; for a period or periods, against underpayments, for another period or periods, against penalties, and against the interest on underpayments. The interest on underpayments shall be computed in the manner set forth in section 3-422(c).
(d)
Notice of city clerk's determination; service thereof. The city clerk, or his/her designated representative, shall give to the licensee written notice of his/her determination. The notice may be served personally or by certified mail; if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the licensee at his address as it appears in the records of the city clerk. In the case of service by mail of any notice required by this section, the service is complete at the time of deposit in the United States Post Office.
(e)
Time within which notice of deficiency determination to be mailed. Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three (3) years after the twentieth day of the calendar month following the monthly period to which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three (3) years after the return is filed whichever period should last expire.
(Ord. No. 99-22, § 8, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-422. - Determination if no return made.
(a)
Estimate of gross receipts. If any licensee fails to make a return, the city clerk shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the licensee from the sale of spirituous liquors by the drink, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total sales in the city which are subject to the tax. The estimate shall be made for the period or periods in respect to which the licensee failed to make the return and shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the city clerk. Upon the basis of this estimate, the city clerk shall compute and determine the amount required to be paid to the city, adding to the sum thus determined a penalty equal to fifteen (15) percent thereof. One (1) or more determinations may be made for one (1) or more periods.
(b)
Manner of computation; offset; interest. In making a determination, the city clerk may offset overpayments for a period or penalties, and against the interest on the underpayments. The interest on underpayments shall be computed in the manner set forth in subsection (c) of this section.
(c)
Interest on amount found due. The amount of the determination, exclusive of penalties, shall bear interest at the rate of one (1) percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the twentieth day of the month following a monthly period, for which the amount or any portion thereof should have been returned, until the date of payment.
(d)
Giving of notice; manner of service. Promptly after making his/her determination, the city clerk shall give to the person written notice to be served personally or by certified mail in the manner prescribed for service of notice of a deficiency determination.
(Ord. No. 99-22, § 9, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-423. - Reserved.
Sec. 3-424. - Collection of delinquent tax; duty of assignees; liability; offsetting erroneous collections.
(a)
Action for tax; time for. At any time within three (3) years after any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected becomes due and payable, and at any time within three (3) years after the delinquency of any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected, the city may bring in action in a court of competent jurisdiction to collect the amount delinquent together with penalties and interest, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees, and other legal fees incident thereto.
(b)
Duty of successors or assignees of operator to withhold tax from purchase money. If any licensee liable for any amount under this division sells out his business or quits the business, his successors or assigns shall withhold sufficient amount of the purchase price to cover such amount until the former owner produces a receipt from the city clerk showing that the tax has been paid, or a certificate stating that no amount is due.
(c)
Liability for failure to withhold, certificate of notice of amount due; time to enforce successor's liability. If the purchaser of a business fails to withhold the necessary amount from the purchase price as heretofore required, he becomes personally liable for the payment of the amount required to be withheld by him to the extent of the purchase price, valued in money. Within thirty (30) days after receiving a written request from the purchaser for a certificate, the city clerk shall either issue the certificate or mail notice to the purchaser at his address as it appears on the records of the city of the amount that must be paid as a condition of issuing the certificate. The time within which the obligation of a successor may be enforced shall begin at the time the licensee sells out his business or at the time that the determination against the licensee becomes final, whichever event occurs the later.
(d)
Tax credit, penalty or interest paid more than once or illegally collected. Whenever the amount of any tax, penalty or interest has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by the city under this division, it may be offset as provided in section 3-421(c). If the licensee determines that he has overpaid or paid more than once, which fact has not been determined by the city clerk, he will have three (3) years from the date of payment to file a claim in writing stating the specific ground upon which the claim is founded. The claim shall be audited. If the claim is approved by the city, the excess amount paid the city may be credited on any amounts then due and payable from the licensee by whom it was paid, or his administrators or executors.
(Ord. No. 79-10, § 7.9, 9-4-79; Ord. No. 99-22, §§ 11, 12, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-425. - Administration; enforcement; rules; records; confidentiality.
(a)
Authority of the city clerk. The city clerk shall administer and enforce the provisions of this section for the levy and collection of the tax imposed by this division.
(b)
Rules and regulations. The city clerk shall have the power and authority to make and publish reasonable rules and regulations not inconsistent with this division or other laws of the city and the state, or the constitution of this state or the United States for the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this division and the collection of the taxes hereunder.
(c)
Records required from licensee; form. Every licensee for the sale of spirituous liquor by the drink in the city shall keep such records, receipts, invoices and other pertinent papers in such form as the city clerk may require.
(d)
Examination of records; audits. The city clerk or any person authorized in writing by the city may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, inventory, equipment and other facilities of any licensee liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or if no return is made by the licensee, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(e)
Authority to require reports; contents. In the administration of the provisions of this division, the city clerk may require the filing of reports by any person or class of persons having in such person's possession or custody information relating to sales of alcoholic beverages which are subject to the tax. The reports shall be filed with the city clerk and shall set forth the price charged for each sale, the date or dates of sales, and such other information as the city clerk may require.
(f)
Disclosure of business of operators, etc.; limitation on rule. The city clerk or any person having an administrative duty under this division, shall not make known in any manner the business affairs, operations or information obtained by an audit of books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment and other facilities of any licensee or any other person visited or examined in the discharge of official duty, or the amount or source of income, profits, losses, expenditures or any particulars thereof, set forth or disclosed in any return, or to permit any return or copy thereof or any book containing any abstract or particulars thereof to be seen or examined by any person not having such administrative duty under this section, except in the case of judicial proceedings or other proceedings necessary to collect the tax hereby levied and assessed. Successors, receivers, trustees, executors, administrators, assignees and guarantors, if directly interested, upon satisfactory proof of interest, may be given information as to the items included in the measure and amount of unpaid tax or amounts of tax required to be collected, interest and penalties.
(Ord. No. 79-10, § 7.10, 9-4-79; Ord. No. 99-22, §§ 13, 14, 11-16-99)
Sec. 3-426. - Violations.
(a)
Any person found guilty of violating any of the provisions of this division shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor offense and upon conviction thereof in the city court shall be assessed a fine for each such offense in accordance with section 1-17.
(b)
Each such person shall be guilty of a separate offense for each and every day during any portion of which any violation of any provision of this division is committed, continued, or permitted by such person, and shall be punished accordingly.
(Ord. No. 79-10, § 7.11, 9-4-79)
