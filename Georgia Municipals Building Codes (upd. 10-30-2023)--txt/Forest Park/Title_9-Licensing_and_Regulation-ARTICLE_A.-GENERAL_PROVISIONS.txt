ARTICLE A. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 9-5-1. - Definitions.
The following words and phrases, when used in this chapter have the meanings as set out herein:
(1)
Taxicab is hereby defined to be a motor vehicle operated for hire, the destination of which is under the direction of the passenger transported therein.
(2)
Highway means any of the public streets, alleys, lanes, boulevards, avenues, drives, circles, or roads of the city.
(3)
Owner shall mean and include any person having control of the operation or maintenance and collection of the revenue derived from cabs.
(4)
Driver and operator shall mean and include any and every person in charge of driving or operating a taxicab, as defined herein, whether as owner, agent, employee or otherwise.
(5)
License shall mean the license or authority granted by the governing body to engage in the taxicab business within the city.
(6)
Driver's permit means the permission granted by the governing body to a person to drive a taxicab within the city.
(7)
Limousine service shall mean a service whereby a vehicle is held out for hire, including the services of the driver of said vehicle, for purposes of traveling from one destination to another for a specified fee, or is contracted for or engaged on an hourly basis. The provisions of this chapter of the Code of Ordinances are hereby made applicable to any limousine service which wishes to be located and operate within the city.
(8)
Operator of taxicab business shall mean any person who shall engage in the operation of furnishing taxi or limousine service within the corporate limits of the city, whether by being physically located within the corporate limits thereof, or by actively soliciting and regularly offering service to persons located within the corporate limits. It shall not include those operations delivering passengers to the city from points exterior to the city limits.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-2. - Compliance with chapter provisions prerequisite to operation.
It shall be unlawful for any taxicab to be operated within the limits of the city unless and until the owner and operator shall have complied with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-3. - Licensees responsible for violations by drivers.
Licensees under this chapter are responsible for violations of this chapter by their vehicle operators whether such operators are direct employees or independent contractors.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-4. - Requirement for registered agent; service of notice.
(a)
All businesses licensed under this chapter must have and continuously maintain in the county a registered agent upon whom any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter to be served upon the licensee or owner may be served. This person must be a resident of Clayton County. The licensee shall file the name of such agent, along with the written consent of such agent with the city and shall be in such form as prescribed.
(b)
Service of notice under this chapter shall be as follows:
(1)
By delivery to the owner or registered agent personally;
(2)
By registered or certified mail with postage prepaid deposited in the United States mail addressed to the owner's or occupant's last known address; or
(3)
If service of notice cannot be accomplished by using the methods stated in subsections (b)(1) or (b)(2) of this section, by posting a copy of the notice in a conspicuous place on the premises of the company base of operations in the county.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-5. - Appearance and hygiene of drivers; responsibility of licensees for driver dress and conduct.
Drivers shall, while on duty, practice good personal hygiene and wear proper dress while operating a vehicle for hire. Proper dress shall mean the wearing of shoes, ankle-length pants, as well as a shirt or blouse with sleeves and collar. Clothing must be clean and not visibly soiled. Licensees under this chapter are responsible for driver dress and conduct.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-6. - Drivers smoking, playing radios, possessing alcohol or controlled substances.
(a)
A driver while operating a vehicle for hire is not to smoke or play a radio or tape player if objected to by a passenger.
(b)
No person operating a vehicle under the provisions of this chapter shall transport or have in his possession any alcoholic beverage or illegal drug or controlled substance.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-7. - Storage or transport of hazardous materials.
The storage or transport of hazardous material in the exterior, interior compartment, or trunk of a taxicab is prohibited.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-8. - Lost articles.
Every taxicab driver shall inspect his or her vehicle after the departure of each passenger to ascertain that no article has been left behind. In the event that a passenger does leave an article in a cab, the driver shall immediately attempt to return it. If the driver is unable to immediately return the article, the dispatcher shall be notified and the article shall be turned in to the business office not later than the end of the driver's shift. The taxicab owner and business shall make a diligent effort to locate the owner of any lost article and shall retain the article at its business office for at least thirty (30) days.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-9. - Stands.
Taxicabs shall be operated only from an established place of business, and before using the streets as parking places, operators of such taxicab must secure a permit from the city traffic engineer to park at a particular place, which permit shall not be granted except by and with the consent of adjacent property owners, and no space shall allow more than two (2) vehicles in one (1) particular locality; provided that nothing herein shall prohibit the concentration of a larger number of taxicabs at places where the public is assembled in large groups, such as baseball and football games, city auditorium, churches, schools, and like gathering places.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-10. - Receiving and discharging passengers; away from traffic.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any taxicab operator to stop his or her taxicab for the purpose of discharging or receiving passengers except at a point as near his or her right-hand sidewalk or curb as possible.
(b)
Taxicab divers carrying passengers for hire on the streets of the city shall receive and discharge passengers through the door(s) away from the vehicular portion of the streets.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-11. - Safe and legal operation.
Every taxicab driver shall operate his or her taxi in accordance with the laws of the state and the city, and with due regard for the safety, convenience and comfort of passengers and the general public.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-12. - Rates of fare, meters; deception of passengers prohibited.
(a)
The governing body reserves the right to regulate the rates and fares from time to time to be charged by taxicabs and owners of taxicabs in the city.
(b)
The owner and driver of every taxicab operated in the city shall have posted at a conspicuous place inside the taxicab where all passengers may see the same, the rates charged for carrying passengers and the manner of determining the fare to be charged passengers. This provision shall not be applicable to limousines.
(c)
If a fare is to be determined by time or mileage or both, then the taxicab shall be equipped with a meter in good condition to record the amount to be charged on each trip, which amount shall be shown in figures visible to the passenger. The meters shall be subject to inspection from time to time by the city police department. This provision shall not be applicable to limousines.
(d)
No taxicab driver shall deceive or attempt to deceive any passenger who may ride in his or her taxicab or who may desire to ride in his or her taxicab as to the destination or the rate of fare to be charged, nor shall the driver convey or cause to be conveyed any passenger to a place other than the one (1) designated by the passenger.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-13. - Transfer.
All business licenses, driver permits and individual vehicle stickers required by this chapter are nontransferable.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-14. - Penalties.
(a)
Penalty for violation of chapter provisions. Any person who drives a taxicab upon the streets of the city without a permit required by this chapter or who drives a taxicab upon such streets while his or her driver's permit is suspended or revoked, or who violates any of the provisions of this chapter shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor.
(b)
Liability of vehicle owner. Any person who knowingly authorizes or permits a taxicab, limousine or other vehicle for hire, owned by him or her or under his or her control, to be operated for hire within the city by any person who is not authorized under this chapter, or who is not permitted under this chapter, or in violation of any of the provisions of this chapter, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. Each day that a person knowingly authorizes or permits a taxicab, limousine, or other vehicle for hire, owned or operated by him or under his control, to be driven within the city by any person who is not authorized under this chapter or in or in violation of any of the provisions of this chapter shall be considered a separate violation.
(c)
Revocation of driver's permit upon conviction. In addition to other penalties provided in the Charter of the city, this Code of Ordinances, and laws of the state, the judge of the municipal court of the city shall have the authority to revoke a taxicab driver's permit when the holder thereof is convicted of a violation of the Code of the Ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-15. - Conflicts between provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this chapter between specific and general provisions, it is the intention hereof that the specific shall control.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
