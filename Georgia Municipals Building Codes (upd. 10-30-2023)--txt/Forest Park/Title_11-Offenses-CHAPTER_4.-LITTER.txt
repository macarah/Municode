CHAPTER 4. - LITTER

Sec. 11-4-1. - Short title.
This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Litter Control Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 98-008, § 1, 5-18-98)
Sec. 11-4-2. - Definitions.
As used in this part, the term:
(1)
City means the City of Forest Park, Georgia.
(2)
Litter means all discarded sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste material, tin cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals, or other discarded materials of every kind and description which are not waste as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-51(6).
(3)
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof, any park, playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area owned by any public or private entity; and all residential or farm properties, timberlands, or forests.
(Ord. No. 98-008, § 1, 5-18-98)
Sec. 11-4-3. - Littering public or private property or waters.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to dump, deposit, throw or leave or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving of litter on any public or private property in the city or any waters in the city unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the state or by any of its agencies or political subdivisions for the disposal of such litter, and such person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a receptacle or container installed on such property; or,
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession, or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(b)
Vehicle loads causing litter. No person shall operate any motor vehicle with a load on or in such vehicle unless the load on or in such vehicle is adequately secured to prevent the dropping or shifting of materials from such load onto the roadway.
(c)
Any person who violates subsections (a) or (b) upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as follows:
(1)
In accordance with the provisions of section 1-1-8 of this Code;
(2)
The violator shall reimburse the city for the reasonable cost of removing the litter when the litter is or is ordered removed by the city; and
(3)
In the sound discretion of the court, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one (1) mile any litter he has deposited and any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the date of execution of sentence; or
(4)
In the sound discretion of the court, the person may be directed to pick up and remove any and all litter from any public property, private right-of-way, or with prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that he has deposited litter. Pick up and removal shall include any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(d)
The court may publish the names of persons convicted of violating this section.
(Ord. No. 98-008, § 1, 5-18-98; Ord. No. 04-21, § 1, 3-30-2004)
Sec. 11-4-4. - Prima facie evidence; rebuttable presumption.
(a)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped, or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane, or other conveyance in violation of O.C.G.A. § 11-4-3, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this part.
(b)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown, or left on public or private property in violation of O.C.G.A. § 11-4-3 is discovered to contain any article or articles, including but not limited to letters, bills, publications, or other writings which display the name of a person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this part.
(Ord. No. 98-008, § 1, 5-18-98)
Sec. 11-4-5. - Enforcement of this part.
All law enforcement agencies, officers, building inspectors, code enforcement officers and other appropriate officials of the city are authorized, empowered, and directed to enforce compliance with this part.
(Ord. No. 98-008, § 1, 5-18-98)
Sec. 11-4-6. - Receptacles to be provided, notice to public.
All public authorities, agencies or departments having supervision of properties of the city are authorized, empowered, and instructed to establish and maintain receptacles for the deposit of litter at appropriate locations where the property is frequented by the public.
(Ord. No. 98-008, § 1, 5-18-98)
Sec. 11-4-7. - Reward for information.
A monetary reward for information resulting in a conviction of a person of violating this chapter, may be established by the governing body of the city from time to time, in their sound discretion.
(Ord. No. 98-008, § 1, 5-18-98)
Sec. 11-4-8. - Penalty.
Penalties for violation of the provisions of this chapter shall be punished upon conviction, as provided in section 1-1-8 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 99-001, § 1, 1-19-99)
Sec. 11-4-9. - Amount of award; standards.
Pursuant to section 11-4-7 of the Code of Ordinances, City of Forest Park, Georgia, the mayor and council may grant a reward of up to one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) to any person providing to the city information resulting in a conviction of a person of violating this chapter. The mayor and council shall base the amount of the reward on the amount of litter involved and on the potential adverse effects of said litter on the aesthetics of the city or the health, welfare or safety of the citizens of the city.
(Ord. No. 99-005, § 1, 3-1-99)
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 99-005, adopted March 1, 1999, amended the Code by adding provisions designated as § 11-4-8. Inasmuch as there were already provisions so designated, the provisions have been redesignated as § 11-4-9, at the discretion of the editor.
