ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 46-1. - Reports by manufactured home park operators.
(a)
Every owner and operator of a manufactured home park is required to give the tax commissioner an inventory of all manufactured homes based in the park as of October of each year. This shall be done no later than October 15 of each year. The tax commissioner will make available an inventory of records as of October 1.
(b)
Every owner, manager and operator of a manufactured home park is required to report any manufactured home removal from the park prior to said home being removed, if known to the park manager, operator or owner, within ten days of removal.
(c)
Every owner, manager and operator of a manufactured home park shall furnish to the tax commissioner an updated lot map of each park, and any changes shall be reported January 1 of each year.
(d)
Every owner, manager and operator of a manufactured home park is required to notify the county of any change in ownership or name of any manufactured home park within 30 days of such change.
(e)
Penalty for violation, $1,000.00 or 60 days or both. Any violation of this section shall bear penalties as authorized by section 1-13, General penalty, of the Code of Ordinances of McDuffie County.
(Ord. of 9-12-06, § D)

ARTICLE II. - MOBILE HOMES AND TRAVEL TRAILERS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 46-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Mobile home means a detached, single-family dwelling unit with any or all of the following characteristics:
(1)
Designed for longterm occupancy, and containing sleeping accommodations, a flush toilet, a tub or shower bath, and kitchen facilities, with plumbing and electrical connections.
(2)
Prefabricated and designed to be transported on its own wheels, or on a flatbed or other trailer or detachable wheels.
(3)
Arriving at the site where it is to be occupied as a complete dwelling including major appliances and furniture and ready for occupancy except for minor and incidental unpacking and assembly operations, location on foundation supports, and connection to utilities and the like.
Mobile home park means any plot or tract of land on which two or more mobile homes are located or intended to be located.
Mobile office means a prefabricated structure designed to be transported on its own wheels, flatbed, trailer, or detachable wheels and used or intended to be used or occupied for the transaction of business or the rendering of a professional service.
Travel trailer means a motorized camper, converted bus, tent-trailer, or other similar vehicular or portable structure used or designed for temporary portable housing or occupancy while on vacation, for recreation, or for other trip and provided with sleeping accommodations.
Travel trailer park means any plot or tract of land on which two or more travel trailers are located or intended to be located.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), §§ 201—205; Res. of 3-4-87(2), §§ 201—205)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 46-27. - Scope of article.
These regulations are intended to provide policies and technical standards for the use of mobile homes and travel trailers in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 101)
Sec. 46-28. - Title of article.
This article shall be known as the "Mobile Home and Travel Trailer Regulations of the Unincorporated Areas of McDuffie County, Georgia."
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 102)
Sec. 46-29. - Enforcement of article.
Except as otherwise provided in this article, the county building inspector shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 704)
Sec. 46-30. - Mobile homes on individual lots.
Mobile homes on individual lots may be permitted in the county provided the site meets the minimum lot size, setbacks, and other requirements for residential uses as set forth in the county regulations relating to use of land and placement of structures. Mobile homes on individual lots shall be provided with the following minimum improvements to the site and lot:
(1)
A water supply and method of liquid waste disposal, subject to the approval of the county health officer.
(2)
Connection to public electrical power.
(3)
A mobile home shall be fully skirted and the skirting must be of a material specifically designed for that purpose.
(4)
No mobile home shall be located on an individual lot, parcel, or tract until the location has been approved by the county planning commission or the building inspector.
(5)
A mobile home, mobile office, or travel trailer may be placed on a lot occupied by a permanent building where it is necessary to provide for the preparation of a construction site or completion of a construction project. A permit authorizing temporary occupancy for the duration of construction project shall be required.
(6)
Mobile homes, mobile offices, or similarly prefabricated structures used for commercial or industrial purposes shall comply fully with the county's regulations regarding commercial and industrial development.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), §§ 500—503, 509—511; Res. of 3-4-87(2), §§ 500—503, 509—511)
Sec. 46-31. - Certificate of occupancy required.
After the effective date of the resolution from which this article is derived, no mobile home on an individual lot shall be occupied, and no mobile homes shall be moved into a mobile home park until after the building inspector shall have issued a certificate of occupancy therefor. The certificate of occupancy shall be evidence that the mobile home on an individual lot and the mobile home park has complied with this resolution.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 703; Res. of 3-4-87(2), § 703)
Sec. 46-32. - Existing mobile homes.
After the effective date of the resolution from which this article is derived, any existing mobile home on an individual lot may be continued and maintained although its location and other features may not be in conformity with this article. No existing mobile home on an individual lot shall be relocated to another site, and no mobile home shall be placed on a site vacated by an existing mobile home unless the relocation or placement complies with this article.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 705)
Secs. 46-33—46-45. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PARKS

Sec. 46-46. - Building permit required.
It shall be unlawful to commence the excavation for the construction of a mobile home or travel trailer park or the placement of a mobile home or travel trailer on a lot until a building permit authorizing such action has been properly issued. A building permit for such purposes shall not be issued until after the planning commission has approved the appropriate plans.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 700; Res. of 3-4-87(2), § 700)
Sec. 46-47. - Permit fee schedule for building permits.
Fees for building permits shall be paid by the developer or property owner according to the following schedule:
(1)
Placement of a mobile home or office on an individual lot or park space: $20.00.
(2)
Development of a mobile home park: $50.00 for the first section (15 spaces) and $50.00 for each additional section.
(3)
Development of a travel trailer park: $50.00.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 702; Res. of 3-4-87(2), § 702)
Sec. 46-48. - Application for permit to planning commission.
Prior to beginning the construction of a mobile home or travel trailer park, the developer or property owner shall first apply to the planning commission for review of construction or placement plans. The application shall include the following:
(1)
A plat of the property showing the dimensions of the lot, the names of adjacent property owners, the name of the street on which the lot fronts, the locations of the mobile homes on the lot, complete with distances from property lines and other mobile homes, the location of accessways and curb breaks, and other features required in sections 46-30, 46-53, or 46-54, whichever is applicable.
(2)
A letter of application accompanying the plat giving the name of the property owner, name of applicant, if different from property owner, address and telephone number of applicant, and any other information as may be required by the planning commission.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 301; Res. of 3-4-87(2), § 301)
Sec. 46-49. - Use restrictions.
In the exercise of its approval, the planning commission may impose such conditions regarding the location, character, or other features of the proposed use or building as it may deem advisable and necessary to ensure sound and compatible development.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 302)
Sec. 46-50. - Prohibited uses.
The planning commission may, as it deems necessary and proper, prohibit certain uses and structures where it can be shown that a permit for that use would create a situation detrimental to the public health, safety, and general welfare.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 306; Res. of 3-4-87(2), § 306)
Sec. 46-51. - Variances.
The county planning commission may grant variances upon certain circumstances. The burden of showing hardship and reasons why a variance should be granted shall be upon the applicant. Mere loss in property value shall not justify a variance.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 304; Res. of 3-4-87(2), § 304)
Sec. 46-52. - Time limit for variance applications.
All applications for variances shall be decided within 45 days of the date of application during which the applicant shall be provided with either a written notice of approval or denial.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), § 305; Res. of 3-4-87(2), § 305)
Sec. 46-53. - Minimum standards for development of mobile home parks.
The following minimum standards shall apply to mobile home park development:
(1)
Streets shall be privately owned, constructed, and maintained and shall have a minimum pavement width of 22 feet. Paving material must meet the approval of the county planning commission.
(2)
Park facilities shall include the following:
a.
An outdoor recreation area, at least ten percent of the total park area.
b.
A refuse collection system.
c.
A water supply and method of liquid waste disposal, subject to the approval of the county health officer.
(3)
Each mobile home space shall contain ample room to ensure that no mobile home will be located closer than:
a.
Twenty-five feet from the centerline of any interior park street.
b.
Twenty feet from any other mobile home.
c.
Thirty-five feet from the right-of-way line of any abutting public street.
d.
Fifteen feet from any adjoining property line.
(4)
Improvements to each mobile home space shall be required as follows:
a.
A minimum individual lot size of 4,500 square feet with a minimum frontage of 35 feet.
b.
An accessway not less than 12 feet wide to each mobile home stand.
c.
Two off-street parking spaces at least 9 by 19 feet each (on-street parking shall not be permitted).
(5)
Mobile home parks shall be located not closer than 300 feet of an existing residential dwelling.
(6)
Mobile home parks shall contain at least eight acres. There will be a minimum of 15 mobile home spaces developed at a time.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), §§ 400—406; Res. of 3-4-87(2), §§ 400—406)
Sec. 46-54. - Minimum standards for development of travel trailer parks.
The following minimum standards shall apply to travel trailer park development:
(1)
A travel trailer park shall contain at least one acre and ten travel trailer spaces.
(2)
Each travel trailer space shall contain ample room to ensure that no travel trailer will be located closer than:
a.
Twenty feet from the centerline of any interior park street.
b.
Twenty feet from any other travel trailer.
c.
Thirty-five feet from the right-of-way line of any abutting public street.
d.
Fifteen feet from any property line.
(3)
Park facilities shall include the following:
a.
Privately-owned paved streets at least 20 feet in width and paving material shall be subject to the approval of the county road commissioner.
b.
A refuse collection system.
c.
A park-wide water system.
d.
At least one sanitary sewage disposal station.
e.
At least one toilet and bathing facility, distinctly marked, for each sex and maintained in a sanitary condition and housed in a permanent building appropriately heated and ventilated.
f.
An outdoor recreation area containing at least 8,000 square feet.
(4)
Improvements to each travel trailer space shall include:
a.
A trailer coach stand composed of not less than six inches of compacted gravel and having a clear accessway at least ten feet wide to interior park streets.
b.
An individual refuse container.
c.
A weatherproof electrical connection.
d.
An outdoor water hydrant.
(Res. of 11-3-71(1), §§ 600—605; Res. of 3-4-87(2), §§ 600—605)
Secs. 46-55—46-75. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PERMITS FOR LOCATION AND RELOCATION OF MOBILE HOMES

Sec. 46-76. - Definition of term.
As used in this article the term "mobile home" means a mobile home or a relocatable home, as such terms are defined as the Uniform Standards Code for Manufactured Homes Act, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-130 et seq.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
Sec. 46-77. - Penalty for violation of article.
As provided in the state laws, any person failing to comply with or violating the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished as provided in section 1-13.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
Sec. 46-78. - Required.
(a)
Each and every owner of such mobile home or relocatable home shall, in the future, obtain from the county board of tax assessors a permit authorizing the location of such mobile home within the confines of this county, when such owner thereof is using the mobile home or intends to use the mobile home as a residence for himself or for others or for any other purpose.
(b)
Should such owner desire to move or relocate such mobile home, either within the confines of the county, or beyond the confines of the county, such owner must first obtain a permit from the county board of tax assessors authorizing such relocation.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
Sec. 46-79. - Proof of applicable taxes paid prerequisite to issuance.
Prior to the issuance of any such permit, either to locate or relocate such mobile home, the owner thereof shall exhibit proof to the county board of tax assessors that all applicable taxes have been paid on such mobile home. Further, if such mobile home is located within the confines of a municipality within this county, such owner shall also exhibit proof, upon request, that all applicable municipal taxes have been paid on such mobile home, prior to issuance of either permit provided for in this article.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
Sec. 46-80. - Issuance and decals.
The county board of tax assessors shall, upon the issuance or granting of such permit, also issue unto the owner of such mobile home a decal, which shall be green in color, and shall be affixed to the mobile home at all times that the mobile home is being used as a residence within the confines of this county. Further, the county board of tax assessors shall, upon request, and upon the payment of the fees authorized in this article, issue a decal, which shall be red in color, authorizing the relocation of such mobile home, either within the confines of the county, or within the confines of other counties in this state. In either case, such decal shall be affixed to the mobile home so as to be easily visible for inspection.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
Sec. 46-80.5. - Decal required.
(a)
All persons owning a manufactured home based in the county on January 1 of each year shall obtain from the tax commissioner a decal before May 1, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-492. This requirement pertains to all manufactured homes, including those exempt from taxation by homestead exemption or other provisions of state law.
(b)
Such decals shall be affixed to the manufactured homes in such a manner as to cause them to be easily visible for inspection.
(c)
Owners of manufactured homes brought into the county after January 1 of any year shall be required to report their manufactured homes to the office of the tax commissioner within ten working days of being located in the county.
(Ord. of 9-12-06, § C)
Sec. 46-81. - Notice of issuance to county of relocation.
Whenever a permit authorizing the relocation of a mobile home is issued and such mobile home is to be relocated within the confines of another county of this state, the county board of tax assessors shall thereupon notify the board of tax assessors of such other county of the date of the issuance of such permit.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
Sec. 46-82. - Fee for issuance and renewal.
A fee of $3.00 shall be payable to the county for the issuance of each permit required by this article, and for the annual renewal of permits authorizing the location of a mobile home within the confines of the county.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
Sec. 46-83. - Renewal date.
All such permits shall be renewed annually between January 1 of each year and April 1 of each and every year thereafter.
(Res. of 6-2-71)
