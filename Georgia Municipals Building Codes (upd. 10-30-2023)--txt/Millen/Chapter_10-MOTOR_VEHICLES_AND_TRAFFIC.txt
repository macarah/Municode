ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted; penalties.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 40, ch. 6, the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, the "uniform rules of the road," (O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 through 40-6-395, as amended), are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished by a fine of not more than $1,000.00, and imprisonment for not more than 90 days, or by both this fine and imprisonment.
(Code 1992, § 9-1-1)
Sec. 10-2. - Temporary traffic regulations.
In cases where traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested upon occasions of parades or other public assemblages where large numbers of vehicles are assembled, the police may make temporary rules regulating the traffic in these congested districts, and any person who, after being warned of the temporary traffic regulations, shall violate them, shall be liable therefor.
(Code 1992, § 9-1-2)

ARTICLE II. - TRAFFIC REGULATIONS

Sec. 10-23. - Speed restrictions.
(a)
Pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-183, and based upon the required engineering and traffic studies, the mayor and council may establish maximum speed limits on any street or portion of a street within the city in accord with the provisions of state law, and shall post traffic signs as required by state law on those particular streets or portions of a street giving notice of the maximum legal speed limit.
(b)
The maximum safe speed, and therefore the speed limit, for streets in the city which are part of the state highway system has been determined to be, and the speed limit shall be, 35 miles per hour.
(c)
The speed limit on all other streets in the city shall be 35 miles per hour, except as may be marked with a speed limit sign for a different speed by action of the mayor and council.
(Code 1992, § 9-2-1)
Sec. 10-24. - Traffic signs, signals and markings.
(a)
Upon approval by the mayor and council, the police department is authorized:
(1)
To designate and maintain, by appropriate traffic control signs, signals and devices, including speed breakers, marks and lines erected at intersections or upon the surface of roadways, crosswalks and intersections where there is particular danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway and at other such places as deemed necessary;
(2)
To establish safety zones of such kind and character and at such places as deemed necessary for the protection of pedestrians; and
(3)
To mark lanes for traffic on street pavements at such places as authorized by the mayor and council.
(b)
All traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the manual on uniform traffic control devices adopted by the state department of transportation. All signs and signals required hereunder for a particular purpose shall so far as practicable be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this article shall be official traffic control devices of the city.
(Code 1992, § 9-2-2)
Sec. 10-25. - One-way streets and alleys.
Upon those streets and alleys and parts of streets and alleys which are designated as one-way streets or alleys by signs giving notice thereof, vehicular traffic shall move only in the direction indicated. Upon approval of the mayor and council, signs indicating the direction of lawful traffic shall be placed at every intersection where movement of traffic in the opposite direction is indicated. All such signs now in place are ratified by the mayor and council.
(Code 1992, § 9-2-3)
Sec. 10-26. - U-turns prohibited.
No operator of any vehicle shall make a U-turn on any of the streets of the city.
(Code 1992, § 9-2-4)
Sec. 10-27. - Regulation of stopping, standing and parking.
(a)
The police department shall regulate the stopping, standing and parking of vehicles by signing or yellow painting on any of the public streets and alleys within the corporate limits, provided any such signs and markings shall be approved by the mayor and council.
(b)
No vehicle shall remain parked on any street within the city for a longer period than 12 hours. This shall not conflict with time limits or parking in restricted zones, as approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1992, § 9-2-5)
Sec. 10-28. - Regulation of truck and heavy vehicular traffic.
(a)
No person shall park a tractor-trailer rig or a vehicle weighing in excess of five tons on any street, lane, alley or easement in the city. Further, no person shall operate or drive a tractor-trailer rig or any vehicle with a gross weight of more than five tons on any street, lane, alley or easement in the city, other than streets that are part of the State Highway System and the following: Gray Street south of Winthrope Avenue.
Further, no tractor-trailer rig, or its separate components (sometimes also known, either separately or in combination, as a "big rig," or "transport," or "semi," or "18-wheeler") shall be parked on any private lot in the city in areas zoned R-1A, R-1B, R-2A, R-2B, R-3, R-MH, P-1 or B-1.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall prevent vehicles with a gross weight of five tons or more from entering other streets in Zone B-2 and the business section of the city for the purpose of collecting solid waste, loading or unloading freight, packages or merchandise and, in Zone B-2, patronizing businesses located in that Zone. Further, this section shall not apply to city or county fire and rescue vehicles, city service vehicles, school buses, pickup trucks or other service vehicles.
(c)
Any motor vehicle weighing more than five tons, with the exception of vehicles excepted as set out in section (b) above, when loaded or empty, making a scheduled or definite stop within the city for the purpose of loading or unloading shall proceed over or upon designated truck routes set out in this section to the nearest point of its scheduled or definite stop and shall proceed thereto, load or unload and return, by the most direct route to its point of departure from said designated truck route.
(d)
The owner, or any other person, employing or otherwise directing the driver of any vehicle shall not require or knowingly permit the operation of such vehicle upon a street in a manner contrary to this section.
(Code 1992, § 9-2-6; Ord. of 12-7-2010, § 2; Ord. of 1-8-2013, § 2; Ord. of 3-4-2014(1), § 2; Ord. of 6-6-2017(1), §§ 2—4; Ord. of 11-1-2022, § 2)
Sec. 10-29. - Penalties.
Violations of the provisions of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor offense punishable as provided in section 1-6 or as provided by state law.
(Code 1992, § 9-2-7)

ARTICLE III. - REGULATION OF TRAINS

Sec. 10-47. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Railroad Regulation Ordinance of the City of Millen, Georgia."
(Code 1992, § 9-3-1)
Sec. 10-48. - Crossing signals.
The use of whistles and horns by locomotives, railroad trains, or other vehicles operated upon the tracks of any railroad company, at blowposts and crossings within the corporate limits of the city shall be limited to the minimum necessary to warn vehicular traffic of the approach of the train, except in cases of extreme emergency. In lieu thereof, the engineer or person responsible for each such locomotive, railroad train or other vehicle may signal, or cause the same to be done, the approach of his engine, train, or vehicle to such crossing by constantly tolling a bell.
(Code 1992, § 9-3-2)
Sec. 10-49. - Blocking crossings.
Street crossings shall be kept clear of all railroad traffic except as is reasonably necessary for passage through the city, the switching of engines and cars, and the make up of trains, but in no event shall unattended cars or engines be left at a crossing in such a manner as to impede motor vehicle or pedestrian traffic.
(Code 1992, § 9-3-3)
Sec. 10-50. - Penalties.
Violations of the provisions of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor offense punishable as provided in section 1-6. Each day that a violation of the provisions of this article occurs or continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(Code 1992, § 9-3-4)
Secs. 10-51—10-73. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - REGULATION OF PERSONAL TRANSPORTATION VEHICLES AND MOTORIZED CARTS

Sec. 10-74. - Registration of personal transportation vehicles and motorized carts.
Prior to the use of any personal transportation vehicle or motorized cart, as those terms are defined by state law, the owner must register the vehicle with the city and pay a registration fee to cover the cost of processing such registration. Registration must be renewed and an additional fee paid if the address at which the vehicle is housed changes. The registration shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and city council, from time to time.
(Code 1992, § 9-4-1; Ord. of 4-3-2012)
Sec. 10-75. - Requirements for license of operator.
The person in physical control of any personal transportation vehicle or motorized cart on the streets or other public property of the city shall comply with all licensing requirements for the operation of motor vehicles on the highways of the state.
(Code 1992, § 9-4-2; Ord. of 4-3-2012)
Sec. 10-76. - Compliance with state regulations for equipment and operation.
The owner of a personal transportation vehicle or motorized cart operated on the streets or public property of the city shall comply with all requirements established by the state for the equipment required and the manner of operation of such vehicles.
(Code 1992, § 9-4-3; Ord. of 4-3-2012)
Sec. 10-77. - Limitation on places of operation.
Personal transportation vehicles or motorized carts may not be operated on any street in the city which is also part of the state highway system and may cross state highways only at crossings marked for that purpose by the Georgia department of transportation.
(Code 1992, § 9-4-5; Ord. of 4-3-2012)
Sec. 10-78. - Liability of owner for illegal operation.
The owner of a personal transportation vehicle or motorized cart is personally responsible for compliance with the provisions of this article by any person operating his or her vehicle and the violation of any provision of this article by an operator of the vehicle shall also constitute a violation by the owner.
(Code 1992, § 9-4-6; Ord. of 4-3-2012)
Sec. 10-79. - Penalty.
Any person who violates any of the provisions of this article shall be penalized as provided in section 1-6.
(Code 1992, § 9-4-7; Ord. of 4-3-2012)
