Chapter 24 - MUNICIPAL COURT[1]

ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Established.
There is established, pursuant to 1968 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1247), page 3712 et seq., a municipal court which shall have jurisdiction over violations of such act and this Code and other ordinances of the city. The court shall be presided over by a judge.
(Code 1972, § 13-1; Code 1994, § 62-1)
Sec. 24-2. - Summons, accusation, affidavit and warrant.
Forms for municipal court summons, accusation, affidavit and warrant shall be as directed and prescribed by the judge.
(Code 1972, § 13-6; Code 1994, § 62-2)
Sec. 24-3. - Copies of charges.
All copies of charges requiring persons to appear in the municipal court shall be dated the day of issuance, shall specify the charge being made, shall notify the person summoned of the date and hour for his appearance and shall be signed by the officer making the case.
(Code 1972, § 13-7; Code 1994, § 62-3)
Sec. 24-4. - Docket.
A municipal court docket shall be kept in the office of the city clerk on which shall appear the name of every defendant charged and the charges made. The judge or alternate judge shall make an entry on the docket of the punishment inflicted and the date of such. If a fine is imposed, the clerk shall make entries showing the date of collection and the disposition of the funds collected.
(Code 1972, § 13-8; Code 1994, § 62-4)
Sec. 24-5. - Bond schedule.
The municipal judge shall establish a schedule of bonds to be posted by persons accused of offenses against this Code or other city ordinances to guarantee the appearance of the offender in the municipal court. Property bonds or cash bonds shall be accepted. The judge is authorized to forfeit a bond when the accused does not appear in court in accordance with the summons or copy of charges given him. The clerk of court or his designee shall be responsible for approving property bonds and accepting cash bonds. He shall be personally accountable for all funds received by him as cash bonds. It is the intent of the mayor and council that cash bonds shall be administered by the city as set forth in this section and in accordance with O.C.G.A. §§ 9-10-10, 17-6-4 and 17-6-10.
(Code 1972, § 13-10; Code 1994, § 62-5)
Sec. 24-6. - Technology surcharge.
There shall be imposed by the municipal court a technology surcharge in the amount of $10.00 per offense for all offenses except those set forth in O.C.G.A. §§ 40-8-76 and 40-8-76.1. Said technology surcharge shall be in addition to all other fines and fees imposed by the municipal court. All revenue derived from the technology surcharge shall be utilized by the city to provide technology support for the police department and related court technology.
(Ord. No. 12-02, § 1(62-6), 2-13-2012)
Secs. 24-7—24-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - JUDGE

Sec. 24-31. - Qualifications.
To be eligible to serve as judge, one must be at least 21 years of age, a citizen of the state and the United States and must have had at least one year of legal practice in the state or three years' service as a judge, magistrate or any other higher judicial position, and an active member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia.
(Code 1972, §§ 2-9, 13-2; Code 1994, § 62-26; Ord. of 2-25-1980)
Sec. 24-32. - Oath.
Before entering upon the discharge of any duties, the municipal judge shall take an oath administered by the mayor. The form of the oath shall be as follows:
"I do solemnly swear that I will faithfully discharge the duties imposed upon me as judge of the City of Lake City for as long as I serve in such capacity and that I will administer justice to the best of my skill and ability without favor or affection for any person, so help me God."
(Code 1972, § 13-3; Code 1994, § 62-27)
Sec. 24-33. - Authority; holding court.
(a)
The judge shall have full authority as judge of the municipal court for the administration of its affairs. He shall prescribe the dates and times for holding court; provided, however, that court shall always be held in the same place where council meetings are held and court shall be held as often as necessary to dispose of the business pending before the court.
(b)
The judge shall have all of the powers conferred upon municipal courts by the laws of the state, including committals, binding over, forfeiture of bonds and the power of an ex officio magistrate in criminal cases.
(Code 1972, §§ 13-4, 13-9; Code 1994, § 62-28)
Sec. 24-34. - Alternate judge.
In the absence or disqualification of the judge, municipal court may be held by an alternate designated by the mayor and council. The alternate judge, when presiding, shall have all the duties, powers and authority of the judge. The mayor or mayor pro tem may be the alternate so designated.
(Code 1972, § 13-5; Code 1994, § 62-29)

ARTICLE III. - INDIGENT DEFENSE ADMINISTRATION[2]

--- (2) ---

Sec. 24-57. - Rules and procedures adopted.
The mayor and council adopts the rules and procedures set forth in this article for the defense of indigent criminal defendants. This article is a recompilation of rules in effect as of the time of its promulgation. No rule or procedure prescribed herein will be construed to supersede nor limit the power of the municipal court to appoint counsel and to order compensation and reimbursement from public funds, as the proper administration of justice may require.
(Res. No. 03-02, 2-10-2003)
Sec. 24-58. - Program compliance; designation of administrator.
(a)
The mayor and council elects to utilize the panel attorney system to provide indigent defense services.
(b)
The program established by this article shall be designed and administered to ensure compliance with all applicable laws, rules of court, and with the program guidelines promulgated from time to time by the state supreme court upon recommendation of the state indigent defense council.
(c)
The city manager is designated to serve as the indigent defense administrator (IDA) to be responsible to the mayor and council for the day-to-day operation of the program.
(Res. No. 03-02, § I, 2-10-2003; Ord. No. 2018-02, § 9, 4-9-2018)
Sec. 24-59. - Panel attorneys.
(a)
List. The IDA will maintain a list of attorneys designated by the mayor and council as eligible and willing to accept assignment of indigent cases. Application for inclusion of the panel shall be made on a form prescribed by the mayor and council and submitted through the IDA.
(b)
General eligibility requirements. Attorneys wishing to be on the list for assignment of indigent cases must:
(1)
Maintain membership in good standing with the State Bar of Georgia and with the Clayton County Bar Association;
(2)
Comply with these rules and procedures, and in all ways conduct their practice in accordance with the requirements of law and in conformance with the highest ethical standards;
(3)
Comply with the business licensing requirements applicable to the situs of their county office; and
(4)
Be actively engaged in the practice of law in the county. For the purpose of this requirement, the term "practice of law in the county" is defined to include the following:
a.
Maintenance of a private office for the attorney's exclusive, full-time use within the county.
b.
Said office must be suitable for, and regularly used to meet with clients, witnesses, and other business visitors, and attorneys must regularly receive mail and telephone calls at said office.
c.
Said office must contain, as a minimum, a desk and chair for the attorney's use on a full-time basis and is not to be shared with any other person.
(c)
Applications and general administration.
(1)
Application for inclusion on the panel for assignment of indigent cases shall include an affidavit of compliance with the general eligibility requirements as well as such specific indicia of compliance as the mayor and council may from time to time prescribe. The application will also include an agreement by the attorney to notify the mayor and council, through the IDA, of any change in information provided in the application.
(2)
The IDA shall periodically require all panel attorneys to re-certify their eligibility by means of the application process. The IDA or any mayor and council member may require a panel attorney to re-certify eligibility at any time.
(3)
Panel attorneys who wish to discontinue their service, either temporarily or permanently, must notify the IDA in writing giving the effective date. Attorneys will be required to complete all of their open cases unless relieved of this responsibility by the court before which a case is pending. In all such cases, panel attorneys must comply with all requirements set by statute or court rules pertaining to withdrawal as counsel of record.
(4)
Panel attorneys taking temporary leaves of absence will notify the IDA in writing of the dates they will be unavailable to attend court events.
(Res. No. 03-02, § II, 2-10-2003)
Sec. 24-60. - Determination of indigency and entitlement to counsel at public expense.
(a)
Financial eligibility shall be determined in accordance with paragraph 1.5 of the Guidelines of the Georgia Indigent Defense Council for the Operation of Local Indigent Defense Programs (hereinafter "guidelines").
(b)
The following persons are authorized to make eligibility determinations:
(1)
The IDA, or a principal assistant when designated by the IDA to act in that capacity on a temporary basis; or
(2)
Any judge or judicial officer of the municipal court.
(c)
The IDA will establish procedures to ensure compliance with section 24-59 pertaining to availability of counsel.
(Res. No. 03-02, § III, 2-10-2003)
Sec. 24-61. - Assignment of cases to panel attorneys.
(a)
Qualified cases will be assigned to panel attorneys on a rotational system. The IDA may make exceptions to this method of assignment in order to:
(1)
Ensure balanced workload among panel attorneys;
(2)
Assign complex cases to more experienced attorneys; and
(3)
Serve the interests of justice.
(b)
Panel attorneys are required to accept all cases as they are assigned unless prohibited from doing so by reason of conflict of interest or for other good cause. New charges against an indigent defendant upon whom a panel attorney has an open case will be assigned to that attorney, and attorneys who have open cases on an indigent defendant should respond to notices to appear on other charges concerning that same defendant.
(c)
Panel attorneys are required to visit incarcerated defendants within 48 hours of assignment (weekends and county holidays excluded).
(d)
Panel attorneys may, by prior agreement with the IDA, limit their practices to appeals only. No other limitations on practice are authorized.
(e)
The mayor and council will normally require that newly admitted attorneys and those attorneys of limited experience in the practice of criminal law be assigned cases from a restricted category of offenses for the first year of their service on the panel.
(Res. No. 03-02, § IV, 2-10-2003)
Sec. 24-62. - Fee schedule and administration.
(a)
Hourly rates: .....
(1)
Out-of-court time spent in investigation and preparation, per hour: .....$ 35.00
(2)
Time spent in court, per hour: .....50.00
(b)
Per diem rates (requires that attorney attend and actively represent a defendant in a hearing or other proceeding before a judicial officer): .....
(1)
Municipal court: .....
a.
Cases disposed by acquittal, nolo or guilty plea and sentence, and findings of guilty and sentence only: .....200.00
b.
All other cases: .....100.00
(c)
Dismissal, bench warrant, nolle pros, dead docket, or replacement by retained counsel subsequent to appointment, but prior to actual representation of defendant in court: .....100.00
(d)
Maximum limitations of fees for misdemeanors: .....750.00
(e)
Miscellaneous rules pertaining to fees:
(1)
Panel attorneys are presumed to be knowledgeable of criminal law and procedure and will not normally be compensated for time spent researching or consulting on the law. Exception may be made for appeals and unusual circumstances fully documented and justified in the claim for compensation, or if approved and ordered by the judge hearing the case.
(2)
All claims for compensation at the hourly rate will include a time sheet detailing the nature, dates, and time in hours allocated to services rendered.
(3)
Time claimed at the hourly rate may not include travel time or in-court waiting time.
(4)
Compensation at the hourly rate for attendance at calendar calls and status conferences will be limited to the lesser of one hour or time actually spent in court.
(5)
Normally, a panel attorney will be expected to represent a defendant from the time the case is assigned through completion of direct appeal. Exceptions must be authorized by the assigned trial judge. A single claim for compensation will be submitted after final disposition of the case unless an exception is ordered by the judge hearing the case.
(6)
All claims for fees and expenses will be presented to the IDA who shall forthwith adjudicate and pay the allowable amounts of such claims. If a claim exceeds the maximum limitation on fees allowable for the class of offense, it will be referred to the mayor and council for consideration of waiver and adjudication.
(7)
A panel attorney dissatisfied with a determination of allowable fees and expenses made by the IDA may submit, to the IDA or to any mayor and council member, a written request for review by the mayor and council of the action taken by the IDA. The request for review must be received within 30 days of the date of the check issued in settlement of the claim, and it may contain any additional matter the attorney cares to present. It will indicate whether the attorney desires to attend the mayor and council's meeting in person.
(8)
A panel attorney dissatisfied with the mayor and council's determination of allowable fees and expenses may request relief by petition to the court which heard the case. The court's determination will be final.
(Res. No. 03-02, § V, 2-10-2003)
Sec. 24-63. - Expenses.
Panel attorneys will be entitled to recover items of unreimbursed expense necessarily incurred in the defense of the indigent, subject to the following:
(1)
To recover expenses, panel attorneys must claim fees by hourly rate. Expenses will not be allowed in claims for compensation at the per diem rate. As an exception, and in order to encourage attorneys to accept a reasonable number of collect phone calls from incarcerated defendants only, actual costs up to a maximum amount of $25.00 per claim for such collect calls will be allowed on per diem claims.
(2)
Incidental out-of-pocket expenses for such items as toll telephone calls and copying must be accompanied by a receipt or cash register tape if they exceed a unit cost of $10.00.
(3)
Panel attorneys must obtain approval in advance, from the judge of the court before which a case is pending for the following types of expenses. The court order allowing the item of expense must accompany the claim for reimbursement. Failure to obtain prior judicial approval will result in the disallowance of the claimed expense and the attorney's personal liability to the service provider:
a.
Employment of experts;
b.
Stenographic or court reporting and transcribing services for proceedings not required by general law to be recorded, reported, or transcribed.
(4)
Items of allowable expense may be advanced to the attorney or to the service provider only when specifically authorized by order of the court. Accounting for advances must accompany the attorney's final claim for reimbursement.
(Res. No. 03-02, § VI, 2-10-2003)

ARTICLE IV. - PROSECUTING ATTORNEY

Sec. 24-85. - Short title.
This article shall be known as the "Lake City Office of the Prosecuting Attorney of the Municipal Court Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-30), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-86. - Findings and intent.
The ordinance from which this article is derived is adopted to address requirements made under state law for the city to pass an ordinance or resolution creating the office of the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court should the city choose to hire or have a prosecuting attorney of the municipal court. Under state law, in order to have a municipal court prosecutor, the city must pass this ordinance or resolution and provide to the state prosecuting attorneys' council a copy of the same. The city is also required to submit the name of the person appointed to be the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court within 30 days of such appointment in order to maintain the office of the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court. It is therefore the intent of the city to comply with state law, particularly article 5 of chapter 18 of title 15 of the Official Code of Georgia, Annotated, and to enact the ordinance from which this article is derived.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-31), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-87. - Establishment of office.
The office of the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court is hereby established for the purpose of providing representation of the city in matters pertaining to ordinance violations of the city and state offenses enforceable in the municipal court as allowed by state law. The prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall be a part-time position.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-32), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-88. - Qualifications.
Any person appointed as the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia and admitted to practice before the trial and appellate courts of the state. Nothing in this article shall prevent the city from appointing the city attorney to be the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court, so long as all other requirements under the law are met.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-33), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-89. - Oath.
The prosecuting attorney of the municipal court shall take and subscribe to the following oath:
"I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as Prosecuting Attorney of the Municipal Court for the City of Lake City."
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-34), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-90. - Term of office.
Unless otherwise provided by the city Charter, the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall serve a term as shall be prescribed by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-35), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-91. - Jurisdiction, duties, and authority.
The office of the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court shall have the duty and authority to represent the city as defined by the city Charter and by state law, particularly as described in O.C.G.A. § 15-18-96.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-36), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-92. - Assistant prosecuting attorney.
The prosecuting attorney of the municipal court may appoint one or more assistant prosecuting attorneys whose appointment shall be ratified by the mayor and council before becoming an assistant prosecuting attorney for the municipal court. Such assistant prosecuting attorney shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia or satisfy the provisions of the Third Year Practice Act, found in the O.C.G.A. § 15-18-22.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-37), 7-9-2012)
Sec. 24-93. - Ratification.
This article shall ratify all actions that have been taken by the persons acting in the positions of the office of the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court to date as authorized by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1(art. III, § 62-38), 7-9-2012)
