ARTICLE II. - AD VALOREM TAX

Sec. 36-19. - Levy; sanitation tax.
(a)
Levied. For the purposes of raising revenues and funds for the general administration of city government, there shall be levied and collected an annual ad valorem tax of five mills applied to a 40 percent valuation of fair market value upon all real and personal property, including money, notes, bonds and other evidences of indebtedness, money used in banking and every other species of property in the city owned or held therein subject to taxation.
(b)
Sanitation tax.
(1)
An annual sanitation tax for the collection and disposal of waste from improved single-family residential properties within the city is hereby levied and assessed against all improved single-family residential properties which are to be served or benefited by the city's removal of garbage therefrom and against the owners thereof within the city. Such sanitation tax shall be due in one annual payment and shall be assessed and collected by the city in the same manner, subject to the same penalties for late payment, and at the same time as other ad valorem taxes otherwise collected.
(2)
The sanitation tax rate for each year until changed by ordinance shall be assessed against the real property and to the real property owner and shall be as follows:
a.
Residential. Per single-family residential structure .....$204.00
(3)
For such sanitation tax, the city shall have a lien against such building lot and the improvements thereon and the owner thereof, which lien shall be prior and superior to any and all other liens except state and county tax liens, and except that it shall be equal to any liens the city may have against such property for city taxes.
(4)
In lieu of payment of this sanitation tax, all properties which are exempt from ad valorem taxes under state law shall be billed for all sanitation service received from the city via a monthly bill to the registered address of the owner of such property.
(Code 1972, § 16-1; Code 1994, § 94-26; Ord. No. 92-2, § 1, 4-13-1992; Ord. No. 11-01, § 2(86-10), 10-10-2011)
Sec. 36-20. - Duties of chief of police.
Under this article, it shall be the duty of the chief of police to:
(1)
Keep a file of all newspapers in which his official advertisements appear.
(2)
Keep an execution docket in which he shall enter a full description of all executions levied by him on any kind of property, showing the property levied on, the date of levy and the service of levy notice, together with all his acts and doings thereon.
(3)
Keep a book in which shall be entered a record of all sales made by him, describing the property and the process under which sold, the date of the levy and sale and the name of the purchaser and the price.
(4)
Keep the book required by subsection (3) of this section indexed for each entry therein.
(Code 1972, § 16-16; Code 1994, § 94-27)
Sec. 36-21. - Payment.
All ad valorem taxes due the city on all property in the city subject to taxation shall be due and payable annually on or before November 20 in each year. All unpaid taxes shall bear interest at 12 percent per annum from the date when due.
(Code 1972, § 16-2; Code 1994, § 94-28; Ord. of 8-8-1974)
Sec. 36-22. - Execution for failure to pay.
When any person shall fail to pay the ad valorem tax imposed by the city according to law, the city clerk shall issue execution for the tax. The execution shall be signed by the city treasurer, attested by the city clerk and directed to the chief of police to levy such on the goods, chattels, lands and tenements of the defendant or so much thereof as shall be sufficient to satisfy the taxes and costs. This execution shall bind all the property, real and personal, of the defendant and the costs thereof shall be the same as on tax collector's executions under the laws of the state. Upon receipt of the execution or fieri facias, it shall be the duty of the chief of police, forthwith, to levy such upon the defendant's property.
(Code 1972, § 16-10; Code 1994, § 94-29; Ord. No. 2018-02, § 8, 4-9-2018)
Sec. 36-23. - Notice and sale of real property.
When the chief of police levies on real property under this article, notice of the levy in form substantially similar to O.C.G.A. § 48-3-10, shall be given to the property owner and record owner of each security deed and mortgage affecting such property. If the real estate is vacant property, such notice shall be served upon the owner of the property, if a resident of the city, personally, or by leaving the notice at his most notorious place of abode. If such property is vacant and the owner is not a resident of the city, notice of the levy shall be given to him at his last known address, by certified mail, return receipt requested. All notice required in this section shall be given at least 20 days before the property is sold. Real property levied upon shall be advertised for sale once a week for four weeks next preceding the date of sale in the official organ of the county and notice shall be provided to the property owner as required by O.C.G.A. § 48-4-1. Sales shall be held on the first Tuesday in each month between the hours of 10:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. at the place where the last city election was held. Sales shall be conducted by the chief of police at public outcry to the highest bidder for cash.
(Code 1972, § 16-11; Code 1994, § 94-30; Ord. No. 2018-02, § 8, 4-9-2018)
Sec. 36-24. - Notice and sale of personal property.
When the chief of police levies on personal property under this article, he shall give notice of the levy to the owner if known. If the owner is a resident of the city, such notice shall be personally given or by leaving a copy of the notice at his most notorious place of abode. If the owner is a nonresident, notice of the levy shall be given him at his last known address by certified mail, return receipt requested. The requirements as to term of notice, advertisement and sale as set forth in section 36-23 and applying to real property shall apply to personal property under this section.
(Code 1972, § 16-12; Code 1994, § 94-31)
Sec. 36-25. - Making title after sale.
The chief of police is authorized to make proper bills of sale and deeds of title to real and personal property purchased at public sales pursuant to this article.
(Code 1972, § 16-13; Code 1994, § 94-32)
Sec. 36-26. - Purchase by the city.
The city treasurer may, on behalf of the city, bid the amount of ad valorem tax and costs on property sold for tax by the chief of police, where no one else bids that amount. The city clerk shall keep a record of all such purchases made by the city and file deeds and bills of sale executed by the chief of police to the city. The chief of police shall put the city clerk in possession of the property purchased by the city, and the city clerk shall be authorized to rent out the property and deposit rentals collected in the city treasury. The clerk shall keep a record of all amounts thus received for rent.
(Code 1972, § 16-14; Code 1994, § 94-33; Ord. No. 2018-02, § 8, 4-9-2018)
Sec. 36-27. - Redemption of property.
(a)
An owner desiring to redeem real or personal property purchased by the city at tax sale may do so within one year from the date of sale by satisfying his debt to the city.
(b)
Whenever any real property is sold under and by virtue of any tax execution issued in pursuance of the Charter and this Code, the owner thereof, his agent or attorney or the vendor of the land when the purchase money has not been paid shall have the right and privilege of redeeming the land thus sold within one year from the time of sale, by paying the purchaser thereof the amount paid by such purchaser for the land, with legal interest thereon, and a ten percent premium.
(Code 1972, § 16-15; Code 1994, § 94-34)
Sec. 36-28. - Sale of property purchased by the city.
Property purchased by the city at the tax sales for city taxes and which has been held by the city under deed made in pursuance of the sale for one year shall be sold and disposed of by the city as follows:
(1)
The city clerk shall present to the mayor and council at any regular or called meeting thereof a full description list of all property so purchased by the city and which has been so held by the city for one year.
(2)
The mayor and council may then, by resolution, direct a sale of all or such portion of the property as in the judgment of the mayor and council should be sold. Such direction being given, it shall be the duty of the chief of police to advertise such property for sale once a week for four weeks before a regular sheriff's sale day, and to have notice of such sale served on the owner by certified mail, return receipt requested, at their last known address. A return of the service of whatever notice is given shall be made by the chief of police or party giving the notice as in cases of regular tax sales, before the city hall door. The chief of police shall sell or have sold the property to the highest bidder for cash, and each piece of the property shall be sold separately.
(Code 1972, § 16-17; Code 1994, § 94-35; Ord. No. 2018-02, § 8, 4-9-2018)
Sec. 36-29. - Title of property sold given by city.
Purchasers at such sale as provided in section 36-28 shall receive from the city a quitclaim deed, to be executed by the mayor upon the payment of the purchase money to the city clerk or the chief of police. For failure to comply with the bids, the city shall have the same alternatives and remedies as prescribed by state law for sheriff's sales.
(Code 1972, § 16-18; Code 1994, § 94-36)
Sec. 36-30. - Reclaiming property sold.
Whenever any personal property has been levied upon by the chief of police by virtue of any execution in favor of the city, the chief of police may, in his discretion, allow the defendant in fi. fa. to replevy the property under the same rules and conditions as prescribed for sheriffs and constables in the state, and the bonds thus taken shall be collectible in the same manner prescribed for the collection of forthcoming bonds taken by sheriffs and constables of the state or the chief of police may receive money in pledge instead of bond.
(Code 1972, § 16-19; Code 1994, § 94-37)
