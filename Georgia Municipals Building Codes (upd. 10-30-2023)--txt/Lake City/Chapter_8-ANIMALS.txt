ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Purpose; agreement with county.
It is the purpose of the provisions of this chapter to alleviate the potential for health hazards as they relate to the keeping of animals, and to provide a more healthy atmosphere in which the citizens of the city may enjoy their prospective pursuits and rights in their individual properties. The mayor and council are authorized to enter into an agreement with the county or any agency or department thereof for the enforcement of this chapter. When such is the case, the rules of the party with whom the contract is made as to impoundment, storage, costs, fees, redemption and disposal shall prevail.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-2. - Definitions.
Animal establishment means any pet shop, kennel, grooming shop, auction, performing animal exhibition or other facility engaged in the handling of animals, excluding licensed veterinarians and veterinary clinics and hospitals.
At large means any animal is at large when it is not under control as provided in this chapter.
Certificate means a certificate issued at the time of vaccination of a dog or cat, which certificate shall bear thereon the signature of a licensed veterinarian; the license number; the name, color, breed and sex of the dog or cat; the name and address of the dog or cat's owner; and the date of the vaccination.
Commercial kennel means any premises wherein any person engages in the business of boarding, breeding, buying, letting for hire, training for a fee or selling animals, excluding veterinarians, veterinary clinics and veterinary hospitals.
Cruelty means every act, omission or neglect whereby unjustifiable pain, suffering, maiming or death may be caused or permitted to any animal.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, according to the records of Clayton County:
(1)
Causes a substantial puncture of a person's skin by teeth without causing serious injury; provided, however, that a nip, scratch, or abrasion shall not be sufficient to classify a dog as dangerous under this definition; or
(2)
Aggressively attacks in a manner that causes a person to reasonably believe that the dog posed an imminent threat of serious injury to such person or another person although no such injury occurs; provided, however, that the acts of barking, growling or showing of teeth by a dog shall not be sufficient to classify a dog as dangerous under this definition; or
(3)
While off the owner's property, kills a pet animal; provided, however, that this definition shall not apply where the death of such pet animal is caused by a dog that is working or training as a hunting dog, herding dog, or predator control dog.
Livestock means any animal of the equine, bovine, or swine class, including goats, sheep, mules, horses, hogs, cattle, and other grazing animals.
Private kennel means any person, excluding commercial kennels, pet shops, and veterinary clinics or hospitals, who keeps, harbors or knowingly permits to remain on or about his premises, more than five dogs or five cats or more than a combination of five dogs and cats over four months of age.
Proper enclosure means any enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owner's property. The dog shall be securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide the dog protection from the elements.
Under control means any animal shall be considered under control if it is confined by fence, pen, cage, trolley system, or secure enclosure on the premises of its owner or is secured by a leash when not confined or off the premises of the owner. When secured by leash, the animal must be under the direct control of a person of sufficient size and maturity to restrain the animal from trespassing or causing injury, death or damage to property.
Vicious animal means any animal that without provocation attacks, bites, or directly causes harm to a human being, domestic animal, or livestock. This phrase shall not be construed to include dogs that are part of a governmental operation.
Vicious dog means a dog that inflicts serious injury on a person or causes serious injury to a person resulting from reasonable attempts to escape from the dog's attack.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-3. - Manner of keeping animals and fowl.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, maintain or permit any animal or fowl in the city in such manner as to create an unsatisfactory condition or to cause offensive odors. Upon receipt of a complaint from a citizen of the city that such a condition exists, it shall be the duty of the code enforcement officer or the chief of police to inform the owner or the person in charge of the animal or fowl of the complaint made. Such owner or keeper shall be notified that he has a period of 72 hours within which the unsanitary condition or offensive odor must be abated. Failure to abate the condition or the odor within the time specified shall constitute an offense against this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-4. - Poison.
It shall be unlawful for any person to leave or deposit any poison or substance containing poison on any property not owned by such person.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-5. - Disposal of dead animals.
Dead animals on public or private property shall be removed by the owner, and it shall be unlawful to fail to do so. Should the owner fail to do so, the city shall remove and dispose of the carcass and charge the costs of removal and disposition to the owner.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)

ARTICLE II. - DOGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 8-27. - Confinement; leash required.
(a)
A dog owner residing in the city is required to confine his dog to the premises of the owner, and it shall be unlawful for any owner to fail to do so. However, the owner or some competent person who has the permission of the owner may have a dog off the owner's premises, provided that the dog is on a leash not more than six feet in length.
(b)
Premises shall include any portion of the owner's property. If outside the confines of a structure, the dog shall be confined within a fence or enclosure of sufficient height and construction to prevent the dog from escaping by jumping, digging or any other means, provided such fence or enclosure shall be at least twice as high as the dog measured from the ground to the top of the dog's head when standing on all paws but not more than six feet in height.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for the owner or person in control of a dog to restrain any dog by means of a tether, chain, cable, rope, leash, or cord which is attached to a fixed object or staked to the ground.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-28. - Impoundment of dogs running at large.
A dog at large is defined as any dog off the owner's premises and not on a leash as described in section 8-27. All dogs at large in the city shall be impounded by the proper authorities of the county.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-29. - Rabies vaccination required.
(a)
No person shall own, keep or harbor any dog, over four months of age, within the city unless such dog is vaccinated against rabies. Dogs under four months of age that are not vaccinated must be confined to its owner's premises at all times.
(b)
All dogs shall be vaccinated by a licensed veterinarian, who will issue to the owner a durable metal tag and a certificate of vaccination approved by the county's animal control unit, which must be attached to the collar or harness of all dogs harbored within the city and be worn at all times. Tags are not transferable from one dog to another.
(c)
It shall be the duty of all persons owning or having charge of any dog brought into the city from outside Clayton County to have the dog vaccinated and to obtain a vaccination certificate and metal tag within 14 days from the date of entry; however, when the owner or person having charge of such a dog produces evidence satisfactory to the county animal control officer that the dog has been vaccinated by a person duly licensed to practice veterinary medicine, the dog shall not be required to be vaccinated again until the most recent vaccination expires.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to attach a vaccination or license tag to any dog for which it is not issued, or to remove a vaccination or license tag or collar from any dog without the consent of its owner of custodian.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-30. - Maintenance of areas.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the owner of property to maintain a reasonably clean and sanitary condition, and free from extreme and unreasonably objectionable odor, all structures, pens, yards and areas adjacent thereto, wherein any dog is kept.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep any dog except under the following conditions:
(1)
Any housing or enclosure used by a dog, or any other domestic animal, shall be well drained, free from accumulation of animal excrement and objectionable odors and otherwise reasonably clean and sanitary.
(2)
Animal excrement shall be disposed of in a manner approved by the county board of health.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-31. - Maximum number of pets allowed without a kennel or animal establishment license.
Other than licensed animal establishments, veterinary clinics or hospitals, no person shall keep or harbor or knowingly permit to remain on or about his or her premises or under his or her control more than three dogs. Provided, however, that with a breeding permit issued by the Clayton County Animal Control Unit, a person may exceed this limit for not more than four months after the birth of a litter of puppies.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-32. - Prohibited treatment; cruelty.
It shall be unlawful for any person, either by commission or omission:
(1)
To confine any domestic animal in a vehicle in an inhumane manner such that the animal becomes overheated or lacks adequate ventilation or is otherwise harmed.
(2)
For any owner to fail to provide any domestic animal under his control with adequate proper food, adequate fresh water, or veterinary care. For the purpose of this subsection, adequate food shall mean food of a sufficient quantity of non-contaminated and nutritionally healthy sustenance that is appropriate to the species, breed, size, age and health of the animal, or at the direction of a licensed veterinarian; which is sufficient to prevent starvation, malnutrition, or risk to the animal's health. Garbage, spoiled, rancid, or contaminated food is not adequate food. Adequate water means clean, fresh, potable water sufficient to prevent dehydration and properly sustain health presented in a clean dish, free from contamination. Examples of inadequate water include, but are not limited to, snow, ice and rancid or contaminated water.
(3)
To fail to provide any domestic animal under his care with access to adequate shelter to protect it from all types of weather, 24 hours daily. For the purpose of this subsection adequate shelter means a protective covering for a domestic animal that provides adequate space and protection to maintain the animal in a state of good health, and that prevents pain, suffering, or significant risk to the animal's health. Adequate shelter shall consist of a completely enclosed structure with four sides, a constructed floor, and a roof with a door opening. It must be maintained clean, dry and compatible with current weather conditions, in addition to age, size, species and condition of the animal. The structure must be of sufficient size to allow the animal to stand, turn around, lie down and go in and out of the structure comfortably. To be adequate, some type of bedding that is quick drying, such as hay or pine straw, must be provided to maintain comfortable temperatures within the structure during times when the ambient, outside temperature is below freezing. In addition, the structure shall include a heavy plastic or rubber flap to cover the door and/or window openings during the months of November through March. From April through October, the structure shall either be shaded or moved out of direct sunlight. If the shelter is made of wood, it shall be raised at least two inches off the ground to prevent seepage or rotting. Examples of inadequate shelter include, but are not limited to, pet carriers, lean-tos, metal or plastic drums, boxes, abandoned vehicles, porches, decks, or material that does not provide sufficient protection from the elements.
(4)
To abandon any domestic animal.
(5)
To allow a domestic animal under his control to be kept in unsanitary conditions.
(6)
To keep or confine a domestic animal under his control in other than a humane manner.
(7)
To allow any domestic animal to be transported in any moving vehicle without the vehicle being enclosed or the animal confined so that it may not jump or fall out or be injured in any manner.
(8)
To unjustifiably cause death or physical pain or suffering to any domestic animal.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Secs. 8-33—8-59. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 8-60. - Authority; definitions.
(a)
The ordinance from which this division is derived is enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.
(b)
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq. ("Dangerous Dog Control—Definitions"), shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this division. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this chapter, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq., and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-61. - Appointment of dog control officer and animal control board.
(a)
The county, pursuant to contract with the city, shall designate a dog control officer who shall perform the duties and exercise the powers assigned to that position by the Dangerous Dog Control Law (O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.).
(b)
The county animal control boards are designated to serve as animal control boards for the city, pursuant to intergovernmental contract.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-62. - Registration.
(a)
The owner of a classified dog must secure a certificate of registration in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 4-8-27 (Dangerous Dog Control—Issuance of Certificate of Registration).
(b)
The annual fee for the certificate of registration shall be as established from time to time by the county.
(c)
Nonpayment of the registration fee shall be an offense and shall be punishable under section 1-8.
(d)
The dog control officer shall maintain a copy of all state laws and sections of this Code applicable to dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs which shall be available for inspection by the public during regular business hours. The dog control officer shall not be authorized to interpret these laws or give advice to owners.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-63. - Investigation.
The dog control officer may make such investigations and inquiries as may be necessary to identify dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs and their respective owners within the city. The dog control officer shall have the authority to investigate complaints as part of the process of identifying dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs. The dog control officer's authority to investigate shall include the power to enter onto public and private property for the purpose of periodic inspection.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-64. - Classification; notice and hearing.
(a)
When a dog control officer determines that a dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog, the dog control officer shall mail a dated notice to the dog's owner within 72 hours. Such notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's determination and shall state that the owner has a right to request a hearing from the city manager or his designee on the dog control officer's determination within 15 days after the date shown on the notice. The notice shall also provide a form for requesting the hearing and shall state that if a hearing is not requested within the allotted time, the dog control officer's determination shall become effective for all purposes under this division.
(b)
When a hearing is requested by a dog owner in accordance with subsection (a) of this section, such hearing shall be scheduled within 30 days after the request is received; provided, however, that such hearing may be continued by the city manager or designee for good cause shown. At least ten days prior to the hearing, the city manager or designee conducting the hearing shall mail to the dog owner written notice of the date, time, and place of the hearing. At the hearing, the dog owner shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence and the city manager or designee conducting the hearing shall receive other evidence and testimony as may be reasonably necessary to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's determination.
(c)
Within ten days after the hearing, the city manager or designee which conducted the hearing shall mail written notice to the dog owner of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, the notice of classification shall specify the date upon which that determination shall be effective. If the determination is that the dog is to be euthanized pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-26, the notice shall specify the date by which the euthanasia shall occur.
(d)
Judicial review of the city manager's final decision shall be by petition for writ of certiorari with the Clayton County Superior Court in accordance with state law.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-65. - Confiscation.
(a)
Dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs are subject to immediate confiscation pursuant to the grounds for confiscation in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-27.
(b)
Dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs that have been confiscated shall be taken to such impound facility as is designated from time to time by the mayor and council.
(c)
Dog owners shall pay a confiscation fee and the daily boarding fee as established by the county for each day the dog remains at the impound facility. Nonpayment of either or both of these fees shall be an offense and shall be punishable under section 1-8.
(d)
Dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs shall be returned to their owners pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-27 and after all fees have been paid.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-66. - Exemption.
A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties shall not be a dangerous dog within the meaning of this article. A dog shall not be a dangerous within the meaning of this article if the injury inflicted by the dog was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort or was tormenting, abusing or assaulting the dog or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused or assaulted the dog or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Secs. 8-67—8-88. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - LIVESTOCK

Sec. 8-89. - Running at large.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or the person having custody to allow any livestock or fowl to run at large in the city.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-90. - Impoundment; fees.
It shall be the duty of the proper authorities of the county to impound any livestock or fowl found running or roaming at large in the city. For each day of impoundment of any livestock or fowl, the county shall receive a fee from the owner or custodian in accordance with county policies prior to release.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-91. - Notice of impounding; disposition.
Upon the impoundment of any livestock or fowl, the officer impounding shall make a reasonable and continuing effort to notify the true owner or custodian. the animal may be redeemed by the owner or custodian by the payment of the impounding fees referred to in section 8-90. If an impounded animal is not redeemed in accordance with county policies, the county will dispose of the animal.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Secs. 8-92—8-99. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - ANIMAL ESTABLISHMENTS.

Sec. 8-100. - Permit generally.
(a)
Required. No person shall operate an animal establishment without first obtaining a permit from the county animal control unit and an occupation tax certificate from the city, nor may any person operate an animal establishment in a manner in violation of any provision of this chapter.
(b)
Selling, bartering or gratuitously distributing animals prohibited; exception. It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, barter or gratuitously distribute any animal, fowl or reptile in a public place without a license or permit as set forth herein. This prohibition shall include but shall not be limited to such distribution in commercial areas and along public roadways. Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit the gratuitous distribution of pets from private residential areas.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
Sec. 8-101. - Compliance with Code.
(a)
An animal establishment shall not sell, trade or give away any dog or cat over three months of age unless the dog or cat has been vaccinated as required by this chapter.
(b)
The County or Forest Park Animal Control unit, as applicable, shall be permitted to inspect all animals and the premises where such animals are kept at any reasonable time during normal business hours to ensure compliance with all provisions of this chapter.
(c)
Animal establishments exempted as provided in this Article may be inspected by the County Animal Control unit at any reasonable time during normal business hours to ensure compliance with all provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 9-9-2019)
