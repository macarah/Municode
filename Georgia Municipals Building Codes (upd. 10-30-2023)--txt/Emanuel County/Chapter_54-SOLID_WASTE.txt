Sec. 54-36. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Normal household trash and garbage means animal, vegetable and fruit refuse matter and other refuse matter ordinarily generated as byproducts of a household such as tin cans, bottles, paper, cardboard, plastics and wrapping or packaging materials, and specifically does not include items such as industrial waste, fence wire, building material, household appliances, furniture and dead animals.
Trash dumpsters means those certain trash containers owned by the county and placed along the rights-of-way in various locations in the unincorporated areas of the county for use by the citizens of the county for the disposal of normal household trash and garbage.
(Code 1992, § 42-1(a); Mo. of 5-17-1999)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 54-37. - Applicability of litter control law.
The state litter control law, which is defined for purposes of this section as O.C.G.A. §§ 16-7-40—16-7-48, is expressly incorporated as part of this section.
(Code 1992, § 42-1(b))
Sec. 54-38. - Violation of section or litter control law prohibited.
It shall be unlawful to violate any of the terms or provisions of the state litter control law now in existence or as hereafter amended, and it shall further be unlawful to violate any of the additional terms and conditions as set forth in this article.
(Code 1992, § 42-1(c))
Sec. 54-39. - Use of trash dumpsters for disposal of items other than household garbage.
It shall be unlawful to deposit in any trash dumpster provided for the citizens by the county, or to place in or near the right-of-way surrounding such dumpsters, any articles not covered by the definition of household garbage as provided in this article, or any articles specifically excluded from the definition of household garbage.
(Code 1992, § 42-1(d))
Sec. 54-40. - Penalties.
Violation of any of the terms of this article shall be prosecuted to the fullest extent possible in the magistrate court of the county. Violators may be subject to a maximum punishment of a fine of $500.00 or imprisonment for 60 days, or both. In addition, violators may be required to remove from any public street or highway or area adjacent to a county trash dumpster all litter which has been deposited in the area within one mile along the right-of-way adjacent to the dumpster.
(Code 1992, § 42-1(e))
Sec. 54-41. - Enforcement.
The sheriff of the county is requested and directed by the board of commissioners to vigorously pursue violations of this article and to enlist, if necessary, the cooperation of the state patrol, officers and agents of the state department of natural resources and any and all other government or law enforcement agencies that may assist in the investigation and prosecution of violations of the terms of this article.
(Code 1992, § 42-1(f))

Sec. 54-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses, and other non-manufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes. (O.C.G.A. § 12-9-22(4.1).)
Disposal facility means any facility or location where the final disposition of solid waste occurs and includes but is not limited to land filling and solid waste thermal treatment technology facilities. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(8).)
Industrial solid waste means solid waste generated by manufacturing or industrial processes or operations that is not a hazardous waste regulated under Part 1 of Article 3 of O.C.G.A. Title 12, Chapter 8, the "Georgia Hazardous Waste Management Act." Such waste includes, but is not limited to, waste resulting from the following manufacturing processes: electric power generation; fertilizer and agricultural chemicals; food and related products and by-products; inorganic chemicals; iron and steel products; leather and leather products; nonferrous metal and foundry products; organic chemicals; plastics and resins; pulp and paper; rubber and miscellaneous plastic products; stone, glass, clay, and concrete products; textiles; transportation equipment; and water treatment. This term does not include mining waste or oil and gas waste. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(12.1).)
Inert waste means wastes that will not or are not likely to cause production of leachate of environmental concern. Such wastes are limited to earth and earth-like products, concrete, cured asphalt, rock, bricks, yard trimmings, stumps, limbs, and leaves. This definition excludes industrial and demolition waste not specifically listed herein. Section 391-3-4-.06 of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Litter means discarded materials of every kind as referenced in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-42(1).
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash, and sanitary waste in septic tanks and means solid waste from single-family and multi-family residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste, but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural, or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(18).)
Open dump means a disposal facility at which solid waste from one or more sources is left to decompose, burn or to otherwise create a threat to human health or the environment as referenced in Section 391-3-4-.01(44) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any park; playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area; and residential or farm properties, timberlands, or forests in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-7-42(2).
Recovered materials means those materials which have known use, reuse, or recycling potential and have been diverted or removed from the solid waste stream for sale, use, reuse or recycling as referenced in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(25).
Recovered materials processing facility means a facility engaged solely in the storage, processing, and resale or reuse of recovered materials as referenced in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(26).
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage, or defect as referenced in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(31).
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires as defined in section 391-3-4-19(2.i) of the Georgia Rules of Solid Waste Management. Scrap tire generators may include, but are not limited to, retail tire dealers, retreaders, scrap tire processors, automobile dealers, private company vehicle maintenance shops, garages, service stations, and city, county and state governments.
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires not otherwise exempted under section 391-3-4-.19(5.g) for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user, or disposal facility as referenced in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(3.2).
Tire retailer means any person engaged in the business of selling new replacement tires as referenced in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(39).
Landfill means any disposal facility where any amount of waste (of any variety) is disposed of, and includes "disposal facilities" as defined above.
Recycling center means any facility designed to collect recovered materials or recyclable materials such as glass, newspaper, cardboard, metal, and so forth, but which is not designed for the processing, reuse, or disposal of such material.
Waste collection center means any facility designed to collect waste in connection with repacking, shipping and so forth, but which is not designed for the processing or disposal of such waste.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § I)
Sec. 54-62. - Waste disposal—General.
(a)
The owner or occupant of any premises shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposing of all litter, scrap tires, and municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste or any other type waste on the premises used or occupied by such person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to dump, open dump, or permit the dumping of litter, scrap tires, municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste, recyclables or any other type waste at any place in this county including, and without limitations, any public or private property in the county or any waters in this county unless:
(1)
The property is designated and approved by the board of commissioners for the disposal of litter, municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste, scrap tires, recovered materials or any other type waste and the person dumping is authorized to use such property;
(2)
The litter, municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste, recyclables, scrap tires and any other type waste are placed into a receptacle or container installed specifically for such purpose on the respective property. In the event no receptacle or container being designated to hold the particular type waste being disposed, said waste shall be placed on the property as directed by the county board of commissioners, its agents or employees; and
(3)
The property carrier all necessary waste handling permits required by EPD and any other applicable regulatory agency.
(c)
All persons defined as scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers, or tire retailers shall be subject to rules as defined in the Rules of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources Environmental Protection Division, Chapter 391-3-4-19 Solid Waste Management and handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq. and the Rules of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources Environmental Protection Division, Chapter 391-3-4 which are applicable to solid waste management.
(d)
All tire generation stations within the county upon removal of a scrap tire from any vehicle within the boundaries of their facility located within the county shall retain full and unqualified possession of said scrap tire at the facility where it was removed and may obtain those customary fees necessary to dispose of said scrap tire in accordance with EPD directives and guidelines; which said disposal fee total shall not exceed $1.00 over the net disposal cost of the tire retailer, rounded up to the next whole dollar amount.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § II)
Sec. 54-63. - Transporting solid waste and litter.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to drive or operate a vehicle in the county hauling municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste or any other type waste when waste leaks, flows freely or spills from said vehicle.
(b)
Any litter or municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste or any other type waste hauled on a moving vehicle shall be covered or secured in such manner that litter will not blow or escape from said vehicle while moving or parked on public or private property in the county.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § III)
Sec. 54-64. - Regulation of waste containers and receptacles.
(a)
The county board of commissioners has placed waste collection containers in several locations or collection areas and the county may, from time to time, hereafter provide for other such collection containers and collection areas in the unincorporated areas of the county and only household garbage trash sufficiently small in size as to not impair the intended use and maintenance of the collection containers may be dumped or placed in such containers provided by the county.
(b)
All waste containers or receptacles shall be maintained in as sanitary a manner as is reasonably possible consistent with their use for disposing of waste.
(c)
Persons using waste containers or receptacles shall deposit only authorized waste and refuge inside the container or receptacle. No person shall dump or place any item outside or around the container or receptacle or in the collection area.
(d)
No person shall deposit a scrap tire in any such container or receptacle unless such container or receptacle is specifically designated for that purpose.
(e)
No person shall deposit any burning or smoldering material in such container or receptacle.
(f)
No person shall set fire to the contents of any such container or receptacle.
(g)
No person shall in any way attempt to damage or destroy any waste container or receptacle.
(h)
No person shall deposit any unauthorized items into the said containers or receptacles. Unauthorized items include, but are not limited to, stoves, refrigerators, bed springs, tree limbs, leaves, air conditioning units, furniture, etc.
(i)
No person shall deposit any flammable or explosive materials in any such container or receptacle.
(j)
No dead animals shall be deposited in any such container or receptacle.
(k)
No person shall alter the location of any such container or receptacle without the written consent of the county board of commissioners.
(l)
No person, without the written consent of the board of commissioners, shall enter, examine, sift through the contents of, scavenge or remove any manner from any collection container or receptacle.
(m)
No person shall deposit waste at a county recycling center unless such waste is contained in a manner that it can be handled by the attendant and shall be deposited only during official hours of operation.
(n)
No person shall deposit waste, water or litter of any kind at a county solid waste collection and recycling center into county-owned receptacles or containers designated for the collection of recovered materials. Only authorized materials such as glass, aluminum, newspaper, cardboard, plastic, and tin and other accepted material may be deposited in the appropriate container designated for recovered materials.
(o)
No person shall deposit bricks, blocks, rocks, lumber, asphalt, cement, concrete, concrete products or waste, roofing, sheet rock, leaves, limbs, bushes, trees, or roots, into any container or receptacle except those containers or receptacles that may be specifically designated for those purposes only.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § IV)
Sec. 54-65. - Use by non-residents and commercial collectors.
(a)
The placement of household garbage and household trash in waste collection containers and receptacles in several locations or collection areas in and around the county is for the convenience and use, in accordance with the provisions of this article, of county residents only. It shall be unlawful and in violation of this article for any person to dump, place or leave any item at such waste collection area or in any collection container or receptacle unless such waste was generated in the county or the person disposing of the waste is a guest of a county resident or a casual visitor to the county.
(b)
It shall be unlawful and in violation of this article for any person engaged in the business of any type of garbage and waste collection to dump, place or leave garbage and waste collected in the conduct of business any garbage or waste collection area or any collection container or receptacle.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § V)
Sec. 54-66. - Regulation of landfills and waste collection and recycling centers.
(a)
Upon the enactment of this, no new landfills, waste collection or recycling centers shall be operated in the county without the written approval of the county board of commissioners.
(b)
No person shall deposit waste of any kind outside the gate of the landfill, waste collection or recycling center.
(c)
No scavenging operation of any kind shall be allowed at a landfill, waste collection or recycling center.
(d)
No person shall move, remove or cross any fence, gate or barrier at a landfill, waste collection or recycling center.
(e)
Fees (dumping, tipping, etc.) on waste brought to public landfills, waste collection or recycling centers will be set by the county board of commissioners to defray costs of these operations.
(f)
Hours of operation will be set to maximize the convenience of residents of the area.
(g)
All rules above apply to all public and/or private property in the county, all waters and/or waterways of the county, and all waste and recycling centers and the county landfill.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § VI)
Sec. 54-67. - Special use permit required.
A special use permit is required for permission to operate a landfill, waste collection center, recovered materials processing facility, or recycling center.
(1)
Application procedure. An application for a special use permit must be submitted by the owner of the subject property, or the authorized agent of the owner, as shown by notarized affidavit. The applicant must submit a fee as indicated below with the application. An application form shall be provided by the county. The applicant is required to submit the following information with the application:
a.
A site plan showing the subject property, containing (a) a survey of the boundaries of the subject property, (b) all lots within 500 feet of the subject property, and (c) the names of the owners of all such properties, as indicated by county tax records on the date of the application.
b.
A landscaping and buffer plan, showing roads, grading, buffers, fences, plantings, screenings, and similar features.
c.
An erosion and sedimentation control plan showing how storm water drainage will be handled during all phases of construction, operation, and remediation.
d.
A site plan showing the proximity to all residential homes and uses, commercial structures and uses, industrial structures and uses, roads, airports, and railroads, within 1,000 feet of the subject property.
e.
Topographic map of the site, showing elevations on a two-foot contour, and showing all wetlands and flood plains, streams, lakes and water features, and groundwater recharge areas.
f.
A site plan indicating expected noise levels at the perimeter of the property and at 100-foot intervals around the boundaries of the property, out to a distance of 500 feet.
g.
A description of hours and method of operation, a lighting plan, and a dust and debris mitigation plan to keep the streets and area clean.
h.
For a landfill, the following information is required:
1.
A remediation plan, showing the plans for restoring the site after closure of each landfill cell.
2.
A hydrological survey, showing the depth of the water table throughout the site at 100-foot intervals, showing the location of all public and private wells within one-half mile of the boundaries of the site, and showing the seasonal flow directions of the groundwater.
3.
Elevation renderings, showing lines of site and maximum landfill cell elevation.
4.
Certification that all state and federal agencies and departments that regulate landfills through statutes, regulations or advisories have been timely notified of the application and intent to construct a landfill.
(2)
Notice procedures.
a.
Once a completed application is submitted to the county, the clerk of the board of commissioners shall forward the same to the board. The applicant shall be required to send a letter via first class mail to all adjoining property owners, as shown on the tax records of the county on the date of the application, and to all property owners where any portion of their property is within 1,000 feet of the boundary of the landfill. The notice shall be required to state that the applicant is seeking a special use permit for the requested use, and shall state the date of the two board of commissioners hearings on the application. Certification of this notice must be provided to the board, along with a list of all owners, prior to the first hearing.
b.
The applicant shall also be required to pay for notice to be published at least twice in the county's legal organ prior to each hearing. Notice shall be published beginning at least seven but not more than 45 days prior to the date of each hearing. The notice shall state the time, place, and purpose of the hearing. Copies of all ads or a certification from the publisher must be submitted to the board prior to each hearing.
c.
The applicant shall also pay for and post signs on the subject property along the road frontage, which shall be placed in conspicuous locations on the property. Signs shall be erected at least 15 days prior to the first public hearing and must remain until a written decision is issued. One sign is required for each 300 feet (or fraction thereof) of road frontage (i.e. under 300-1, 301 to 600-2, etc.). Each sign must be a minimum of ten square feet in size, and labeled "Special Use Permit Notice" with letters a minimum of four inches high. All other letters must be a minimum of two inches high, and the bottom of the sign itself must be a minimum of three feet off the ground and not obscured from view. Each sign must state that a special use permit has been sought, the dates, times and places of the two hearings, and the use requested. The applicant must provide pictures of the signs in place to the board prior to the first hearing.
(3)
Hearing procedures.
a.
The board shall hold two hearings on the application. At the first hearing, the applicant shall present its application, but public comments will not be received. The board will have the opportunity to ask questions of the applicant, and board members shall request any further information desired by the board from the applicant. The board may also request independent experts be retained by the applicant, or by the county, to verify the applicant's information or develop new and additional information. The first hearing shall take place within 45 days of a completed application being submitted. The second hearing shall take place at the next regularly scheduled board meeting following publication of the second required notice, unless additional time is granted by the board, in which case it shall take place within 60 days of the first hearing.
If the board requests that an independent expert be retained on behalf of the county, the applicant shall be required to pay to the expert's estimated expenses prior to said expert being retained. The board shall hold said funds in escrow until payment is required and refund any unused funds to the applicant. The board shall issue no decision on an application request until the applicant has paid all expenses incurred under this section.
b.
At the second public hearing on a special use permit application, the applicant shall again present its application, and any supplemental information requested by the board. The board members shall have the opportunity to ask questions of the staff, any experts, the applicant, supporters and opponents. The hearing shall be transcribed or recorded so that it may be transcribed later if necessary. The applicant and supporters shall be granted equal time as opponents. The minimum time allowed per side is 15 minutes. More time than the minimum may be allowed by the board or the chairman if the application is complex, or the number of witnesses warrants a further extension. If the chairman determines that there are a great number of opponents with the same interest (for example, members of the same neighborhood), the chairman may require that a spokesman be designated. The opponents may also be represented by an attorney. The applicant, supporters and opponents shall have the right to provide testimony, including expert testimony, in support of their position. Each party shall have a reasonable opportunity to ask questions of the other side, in an orderly manner as controlled by the chairman.
c.
The board shall only consider evidence presented with the application or at the hearing. The board members shall not accept any ex parte evidence, and all evidence submitted shall be disclosed at or before the public hearing, to allow all sides the opportunity to review it.
(4)
Decision process.
a.
At the close of the evidence at the public hearing, the board shall discuss the application. No vote shall be taken at this time. At the close of discussion, the board shall recess the hearing to allow the county attorney's office time to prepare proposed written decision(s). The county attorney's office shall meet with each board member individually and ascertain his or her reasons for approval or denial, and shall prepare a written decision or decisions to be voted on by the board at a hearing within 45 days. Board members may also prepare their own proposed decisions.
b.
Written decisions should be based on the evidence in the record and the criteria in the ordinance.
c.
At the hearing, the chairman shall call for motions and any member may put forward a written decision to be voted on. Written decisions may be supplemented with further conditions proposed at that hearing. No vote shall be final until a written decision is adopted by the board.
d.
Any proposal shall be specifically conditioned to the site plan, plat maps, elevation renderings, and other detailed plans to insure that the development is erected in accordance with the presented materials. The approval may also impose additional conditions, if the conditions are designed to minimize the impact of the use on the neighboring property, streets and so forth.
(5)
Criteria for grant of special use permit. In considering whether to grant or deny a special use permit, the board shall consider the following criteria:
a.
Whether the proposed use will have a negative impact in regard to traffic on adjacent streets and in regard to adjacent residential, commercial and industrial uses.
b.
Whether there is sufficient and safe parking facilities, access and egress provided.
c.
Whether the proposed use is compatible with the surrounding properties, uses, and governmentally regulated facilities.
d.
Whether the proposed use will have a negative impact on health, safety, aesthetics, property value, or other elements of the community.
e.
Whether the proposed use is compatible with the county's comprehensive plan and solid waste management plan.
f.
Whether there are any environmentally sensitive areas on the subject property (including wetlands, streams, flood plains, etc). If there are such areas, whether the proposed plans adequately protect those areas.
g.
Whether the signage and outdoor lighting is consistent with the surrounding uses.
h.
Whether there is sufficient protective screening, either natural, topographic, or proposed, to screen the use from public view.
i.
Whether the property can be viably used for other purposes.
j.
Whether the hours of operation of the proposed use are consistent with surrounding uses.
k.
Whether noise, dust, debris or other external impacts of the use would negatively impact the surrounding uses.
(6)
Development requirements. The development requirements that apply to landfills, waste collection centers, recycling centers and recovered material processing facilities are as follows:
a.
Landfills. Minimum property area for landfills is 200 acres. The minimum undisturbed buffer around the entire perimeter of the property shall be 200 feet. If the undisturbed buffer does not contain sufficient density of hardwood and evergreen trees to screen the landfill from view at all times of the year, the applicant shall be required to improve the buffer with a minimum of [a] ten-foot earthen berm topped with [an] eight-foot opaque wooden fence, and to plant a 25-foot buffer of pine and hardwoods outside that berm, at a density approved by the county. Additionally buffering requirements may be imposed as circumstances warrant. Additionally, an undisturbed buffer of 50 feet must be maintained around all lakes, streams, and wetlands on the property. No portion of the non-buffer area of the site (as measured from the inner boundary of the buffer at the nearest point) shall be closer than 1,000 feet to any residentially used lot. A security fence must be erected around the landfill operations area. No more than 40 percent of the total property area may be disturbed as landfill cell area, with the remainder preserved as open space. No landfill cell is permitted within 200 feet of any lake, stream, or wetland buffer, not within 100 feet of any area within the 100-year flood plain. No landfill cell may exceed 65 feet in height above the original grade. Any scenic barrier, security fence, and/or earthen burn required by this section must be continually maintained in good repair and good condition by the permittee.
b.
Waste collection centers. Minimum property area for waste collection center is 50 acres. The minimum undisturbed buffer around the entire perimeter of the property shall be 150 feet. If the undisturbed buffer does not contain sufficient density of hardwood and evergreen trees to screen the waste collection center at all times of the year, the applicant shall be required to improve the buffer with a minimum of [a] ten-foot landscaped earthen berm topped with a six-foot opaque wooden fence. Additional buffering requirements may be imposed as circumstances warrant. Additionally, an undisturbed buffer of fifty feet must be maintained around all lakes, streams, and wetlands of the property. No portion of the non-buffer area of the site (as measured from the inner boundary of the buffer at the nearest point) shall be closer than 1,000 feet to any residentially used lot. A security fence must be erected around the landfill operations area, inside the buffer. No waste may be kept on a waste collection center property for more than three weeks before being transferred to a landfill.
c.
Recovered materials processing center. Minimum property area for recovered materials processing center is 20 acres. The minimum undisturbed buffer around the entire perimeter of the property shall be 100 feet. If the undisturbed buffer does not contain sufficient density of hardwood and evergreen trees to screen the landfill at all times of the year, the applicant shall be required to improve the buffer with a minimum of [a] six-foot landscaped earthen berm topped with a six-foot opaque wooden fence. Additional buffering requirements may be imposed as circumstances warrant. Additionally, an undisturbed buffer of 25 feet must be maintained around all lakes, streams, and wetlands on the property. No portion of the non-buffer area of the site (as measured from the inner boundary of the buffer at the nearest point) shall be closer than 1,000 feet to any residentially used lot.
d.
Recycling centers. Minimum property area for recycling centers is 20 acres. The minimum undisturbed buffer around the entire perimeter of the property shall be 100 feet. If the undisturbed buffer does not contain sufficient density of hardwood and evergreen trees to screen the landfill at all times of the year, the applicant shall be required to improve the buffer with a minimum of [a] six-foot landscaped earthen berm topped with a six-foot opaque wooden fence. Additional buffering requirements may be imposed as circumstances warrant. Additionally, an undisturbed buffer of 25 feet must be maintained around all lakes, streams, and wetlands on the property. No portion of the non-buffer area of the site (as measured from the inner boundary of the buffer at the nearest point) shall be closer than 1,000 feet to any residentially used lot. No recovered materials may be kept on recycling center property for more than three weeks before being transferred to a recovered materials processing facility. Waste that is not recyclable may not be accepted by nor stored at a recycling center.
(7)
Appeal procedures. In the event a special use permit application is denied, the applicant shall have the right to appeal to the superior court of the county within 30 days. The applicant shall file a copy of the appeal with the clerk of the board of commissioners, and the clerk shall forward a copy of the entire record (including all evidence submitted by all sides) to the superior court as soon as is practicable, along with the minutes of the hearings, copies of the tapes or transcriptions of the hearings, the written decision, a copy of the ordinance, and all other relevant ordinances, maps and documents.
(8)
General restrictions. The following restrictions apply to all landfills, waste collection centers, recycling centers and recovered material processing facilities.
a.
No facility shall be open, accept any waste or other materials, or be in operation earlier than 7:00 a.m. nor later than 7:00 p.m. Monday through Friday, and 8:00 a.m. through 3:00 p.m. Saturday. No operations are allowed on Sunday.
b.
Lighting for any facility shall be downward firing and shielded to minimize light pollution. Illumination from any facility shall not cast light on adjoining property or shine in such a manner as to cause traffic interference.
c.
Vehicles leaving the site of any facility shall be sufficiently clean not to deposit dirt, debris, or waste into the road or right-of-way.
d.
Ingress and egress shall be as designated by the county, and acceleration and deceleration lanes shall be provided for traffic entering and leaving any facility.
e.
No landfills, waste collection centers, recycling centers or recovered material processing facilities shall be established in violation of any state or federal statute, regulation or advisory, or without certification of compliance with all applicable statutes, regulations and advisories.
(9)
Fees. The fees for submission of a special use permit application and for each annual renewal in addition to paying the cost of newspaper advertising, are as follows:
Landfill .....$1,000.00
Waste collection center .....500.00
Recycling center .....500.00
Recycling materials processing center .....500.00
Each special use permit governed by this article must be renewed on an annual basis by filing an application to renew with the county board of commissioners at least 90 days prior to the permit's expiration date and pay in the fees enumerated above in addition to the costs of advertising the renewal application in the legal organ of the county.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § VI)
Sec. 54-68. - Violation and enforcement.
(a)
The county enforcement officer or his designated representative is hereby granted and shall hereafter have the power and authority to administer and enforce the provisions of this for the collection and disposal of waste in this county.
(b)
Any person who violates any portion of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as follows:
(1)
By a fine of not less than $200.00 nor more than $1,000.00 for each day the violation exists.
(2)
In the sound discretion of a court in which the conviction is obtained, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one mile any waste the person has deposited and any and all other waste deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the date of execution of sentence; or
(3)
In the sound discretion of the judge of a court in which the conviction is obtained, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public park, private right-of-way, or, with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that the person has deposited waste, any and all waste deposited thereon by anyone prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(Ord. of 8-16-2004, § VII)
