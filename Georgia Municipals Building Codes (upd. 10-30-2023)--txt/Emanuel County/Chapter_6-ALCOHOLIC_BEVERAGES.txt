Sec. 6-1. - Purposes.
(1)
The purposes of this chapter shall include, without necessarily being limited to, the following:
(a)
Compliance with and effectuation of the general state law;
(b)
Prevention and control of the sale of alcoholic beverages by unfit persons;
(c)
Prevention and control of the sale of alcoholic beverages in unsanitary, unsafe, or unfit locations.
(d)
Ensuring that any licenses issued for the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises are issued only to a legitimate restaurant, tavern, hotel, motel, lodge, wedding venue, or club where the issuance of such license is compatible to the operation of the business located on the premises and is not deemed by the county commissioners to be a detriment to the public health, safety, or welfare of the residents of Emanuel County, Georgia;
(e)
The protection of schools, homes, churches, parks, other institutions, and the citizens of Emanuel County, Georgia; and;
(f)
The protection of the public health, safety, and welfare for the benefit of all residents of Emanuel County, Georgia.
(2)
To the maximum extent permissible under state and federal law, the business of selling alcoholic beverages shall under this chapter be a privilege to be accorded in conformity with the foregoing and other public policies of the county, rather than a right. Likewise, the right to retain an issued alcohol license or the right to a renewal of a previously issued license are not absolute, but are themselves a privilege and not a right guaranteed to the license holder.
(3)
This chapter shall replace and supersede any other Ordinance of Emanuel County concerning the sale of alcoholic beverages, except those allowing for the taxation of alcohol which are not otherwise addressed in this chapter.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-2. - Definitions.
(1)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, spirits of wine, from whatever source or whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, wine, or fortified wine
Beer means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoration of barley, malt, hops, or any other product or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "beer" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Board means the Board of Commissioners of Emanuel County, Georgia.
Brown bag establishment means any private club, tavern, lodge, meeting room, restaurant, music hall, theatre, bowling alley, or any other establishment which provides food, entertainment, or recreation in the normal course of business in which the owners or their employees or agents knowingly allow patrons to bring in and consume on the premises the patrons' own alcoholic beverages where no consumption on the premises license has been issued.
Brown bagging means the act of a patron or patrons entering any private club, tavern, lodge, meeting room, restaurant, music hall, theatre, bowling alley, or any other establishment providing food, entertainment, or recreation in the normal course of business and bringing in and consuming the patrons' own alcoholic beverages.
Club means a private social or civic organization where people gather together for a common purpose, whether charitable, recreational, or for profit.
County means Emanuel County, Georgia, and when used in a geographical sense means the unincorporated area of the county.
County clerk means the clerk of the Board of Commissioners of Emanuel County, Georgia.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Hotel/motel means any establishment providing accommodations, meals, or other services for travelers and tourists.
Interest includes any pecuniary and/or any ownership interest, whether present or future, whole or partial, legal or beneficial, contingent or vested, direct or indirect, and any right, power, or authority of control.
License means the authorization granted by the board to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages in a specified manner for a calendar year, or the remaining portion of a calendar year.
Licensed area: in the event any area is to be included in the application over and above the actual building, which may be the licensed premises, meaning the surrounding area to be included, that shall be so designated on the application and shall be referred to as the licensed area.
Licensed premises includes not only the room wherein alcoholic beverages are sold or served, but also the entire building in which such room is located. However, no more than one building shall be deemed the "licensed premises" for a single license, regardless of whether an adjacent building may be connected to the "licensed premises" by a sidewalk, breezeway, enclosed walkway, or any other means of passage from one building to another.
Licensee includes an individual licensee and in the case of a partnership or corporation includes both the partnership or corporation and the named licensee.
Liter means metric measurement as currently used by the United States.
Lodge means any establishment set aside for people to get together for common purposes such as relaxation, hunting, fishing, etc.
Majority stockholder means the person, if any, who owns more than 50 percent of the voting stock of a corporation; if no person owns more than 50 percent of the voting stock of a corporation, the "majority stockholder" is the person owning more of the voting stock than any other person; and if two or more persons each own the same amount of voting stock of a corporation and each person owns more of the voting stock than any other person, than any one of such persons may act as the "majority stockholder."
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The word "manufacturer" also means:
(i)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits.
(ii)
In the case of beer, any brewer.
Minor means any persons under the age of 21 years.
Owner means any person or partner or stockholder or member owning any percentage of a business.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Person includes any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, limited liability company, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private, or quasi-public.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased, or controlled by the licensee and used by the licensee for the purpose of operating under the license including any public sidewalk or roadway adjacent to the premises which is used by the licensee to sell alcoholic beverages. This definition is intentionally broader than the definition of a "licensed premises" and it is meant to include the property surrounding the building of a licensed premises as well, as defined above.
Purchaser means and includes any individual, partnership, corporation, limited liability company, trust, or association purchasing alcoholic beverages from a licensee.
Restaurant means an establishment that serves prepared meals to customers and where at least 50 percent of gross sales revenue is derived from the combined sale of food and non-alcoholic beverages.
Retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only, to consumers and not for resale.
School means any school building, school grounds, college campus, educational facility, or educational building owned or run by or affiliated with a state, county, city, or private or religious entity, any church school, day care, or kindergarten, and to any building at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state. It shall not apply to home schools.
Tavern means a place where alcoholic drinks and liquor are sold to be consumed on the premises.
Wedding venues means any establishment maintained and created for the purpose of allowing the gathering of families or other individuals for weddings, reunions, or other types of events.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, or to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverages containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, and combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(2)
Unless a contrary intention is clearly apparent from the context, any term used in this chapter shall have the same meaning as when used in comparable provisions of the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. title 3.
(3)
As used in this chapter, the singular and plural shall each include the other, the masculine and feminine shall each include the other, and any verb tense may include any other verb tense.
(4)
As used in this chapter the term "may" is permissive and the term "shall" is mandatory.

Sec. 6-3. - License classifications; duration.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale any alcoholic beverages within the county except under a valid license issued under this chapter and in compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(2)
Annual license fees shall be payable in advance for an entire calendar year beginning January 1 and ending December 31 of the same year. Annual license fees shall be set forth in a schedule of fees and charges set by the board and as may be amended from time to time. The suspension or revocation of any license granted pursuant to this article shall not entitle the licensee to a return of any portion of the license fee.
(3)
Types of Licenses:

Class A Packaged Beer & Wine
Class A license shall permit the sale of beer and wine in unopened containers only and shall not authorize the consumption of beer and wine on the premises for which the license is granted and no alcoholic beverages shall be consumed on premises for which a Class A license is in effect.
Class B Beer & Wine by the Drink
Class B license shall be granted for restaurants, taverns, hotels, motels, lodges, wedding venues, clubs, or similar businesses only and shall permit the sale and consumption of beer and wine in a licensed premises as an incident to the underlying business, but only as incidental to said business. No alcoholic beverages may be consumed on the premise grounds outside of the licensed area. Consumption shall be limited to consumption by the drink, not by the bottle.
Class C Packaged Distilled Spirits
Class C license shall permit the sale of distilled spirits in unopened containers only and shall not authorize the consumption of distilled spirits on the premises for which the license is granted and no alcoholic beverages shall be consumed on premises for which a Class C license is in effect.
Class D Distilled Spirits by the Drink
Class D license shall be granted for restaurants, taverns, hotels, motels, lodges, wedding venues, clubs, or similar businesses only and shall permit the sale and consumption of distilled spirits on the licensed premises as an incident to the underlying business, but only as incidental to said business. No alcoholic beverages may be consumed on the premise grounds outside of the licensed area. Consumption shall be limited to consumption by the drink, not by the bottle.

Sec. 6-4. - Grant of licenses.
(1)
Licenses shall be granted for a calendar year and shall expire on December 31 of the year for which issued regardless of the date issued and there shall be no rebate of the annual license fee. A licensee who holds a license at the end of the year and who makes a timely application for a license for the next year (on or before January 15th of the year applied for) may continue in business under the old license until the application for the new license is granted or denied.
(2)
The fee for the issuance of a license shall be established by the board and shall be maintained on a schedule kept at the office of the board of commissioners. The board shall, from time to time, have the right to amend or modify the schedule as they deem appropriate.
(3)
From July 1—December 31, the fee for alcoholic beverage licenses shall be ½ of the fee amount listed in section 6-4(2). This fee shall remain constant and there shall be no further pro-ration. The annual application fee shall be as set by the county commissioners from time to time and shall be maintained on a schedule kept at the office of the board of commissioners. The board shall from time to time have the right to amend the application fee as they deem appropriate. Licenses granted during this period shall expire at 11:59:59 p.m. on December 31.
(4)
Licenses shall be granted only upon written application:
a.
A license issued to an individual shall be issued in the name of the individual.
b.
A license issued to a corporation shall be issued:
i.
In the name of the corporation; and
ii.
In the name of the majority stockholder or a principal officer of the corporation or the officer or employee of the corporation primarily responsible for the operation of the licensed premise, and such majority stockholder or officer or employee shall be the named licensee.
1.
In the case of a corporation, the majority stockholder or officer or employee who is to be the named licensee shall be the applicant and must meet the qualifications of an individual licensee, as provided herein.
2.
The board may require the fingerprinting and investigation of any of the officers and shareholders of the corporation if the board deems it necessary in making its investigation.
c.
A license issued to a partnership shall be issued
i.
In the name of the partnership; and
ii.
In the name of each of the general partners.
1.
In the case of a partnership, each general partner shall join as an applicant for the license and each general partner must meet the qualifications of an individual licensee, as provided herein.
2.
In the case of a partnership, each partner shall be responsible for the actions of the named licensee and the conduct of the licensed business. In the case of the corporation, the corporation shall be responsible for the actions of the named licensee and the conduct of the licensed business.
3.
The board may require the fingerprinting and investigation of any of the partners of the partnership if the board deems it necessary in making its investigation.
(5)
Applications for license or licenses and renewals shall be granted only by the Commissioners in regular session and no application shall be considered until the provisions of this subsection are completed as well as subsection (6), (7) and (8). A background check of the licensee and/or the manager of the licensed premises prepared by the Sherriff of Emanuel County shall be attached to all applications or renewals. The applicant shall be responsible for posting the notice and shall attach to the application a sworn statement that the notice has been posted as herein required. The notice shall state the name of the applicant and designee if applicable, the location for which the classes of license sought, existent classes of license which are conducting business on the same licensed premises. Upon the first application for a new class of license or licenses, the applicant shall also have an advertisement ran for two consecutive weeks in the Emanuel County's official organ giving notice of the application. The ad shall be 1/32 of a page in size. Such notice shall contain all information in the notice posted at the Emanuel County Courthouse and shall conform to the form notice in Exhibit "A."
(6)
The license fee shall be tendered with the application. In addition, licensee shall tender documentation with the application showing proof licensee is in full compliance with all county, state and federal ordinances, rules, regulations, codes and statutes including, but not limited to, proof of payment of all applicable sales tax and state and county valorem taxes.
(7)
Licensee must tender proof with the application or renewal that the premises to be used is in full compliance with all applicable county, state and federal fire and safety ordinances, rules, regulations, codes or statutes. The proof necessary shall include at a minimum a report from the appropriate fire chief, the Emanuel County Health Department, and Emanuel County Code Enforcement.
(8)
An applicant shall also supply emergency contact information for the licensee and/or manager.
(9)
Each license shall be issued for a particular location and licensee and shall not be transferable either as to location or the licensee. Any change in location or licensee shall require a new license application. Designees or registered agents may be changed during the year with written notice to the Board of Commissioners of Emanuel County and verification that they meet the requirements as stated in the qualifications section of this chapter.
(10)
No license shall be granted for a location:
a.
Within 300 feet of a church or chapel for religious or burial service.
b.
Within 1,000 feet of a school campus.
c.
For the purposes of this chapter, distances shall be calculated according to the nearest way of automobile travel over publicly maintained roads beginning at the front door of the proposed licensee establishment, traveling to the nearest maintained public highway, proceeding down that roadway or combination of roadways to the entrance of church, chapel, or school campus parking lot, then proceeding from that parking lot entrance to the church or chapel front door, where applicable and ending at the front door of a church or a chapel for religious or burial service or school campus.
(11)
In determining whether or not a license shall be granted or renewed, the commissioners shall give consideration to the property of the location for which the license is sought. Consideration for property is to be reviewed for the particular license sought by the applicant and shall consider all relevant circumstances of the proposed location, including traffic conditions, the character of the neighborhood and the class of license applied for, the premises involved, the need and availability of sheriff protection and supervision, concerns and feelings of residents in the surrounding area, as well as the past history and conduct of any applicant, as well as the past history and conduct of activities conducted on the premises sought to be licensed and the past history and conduct of activities conducted on the premises since the issuance of any previous alcohol license.
(12)
A licensee shall not have any vested rights in the license and a license shall not be renewable as a matter of right. A new application shall be required annually if a licensee desires to continue operation from year to year. Each application shall be considered as a new application even though the applicant may have a license for the same location for the previous year except as stated in subsection (5) above.
Multiple licenses on single premise. An applicant may apply for multiple licenses at a single licensed premise. However, no premises licensed as a package store may also be able to serve alcoholic beverages by the drink for:
(1)
On premise consumption. Approval of the licensed premises for one license shall not require the board to approve additional types of licenses.
(2)
After initial consideration of an application for license, the board of commissioners shall have the right to continue the hearing for purposes of further investigation, seeking additional information from the applicant, and/or seeking additional input from law enforcement and/or citizens of the community. The decision for the granting of said license shall only be granted once the county commission is satisfied that all relevant evidence has been obtained by the county commission that is necessary to make an appropriate decision.
(3)
Applications for either the original issuance of a license or the renewal of an existing license shall be submitted to the board of commissioners at least 60 days prior to the date a licensee would expect a license to be issued.

Sec. 6-5. - Qualifications.
(1)
A licensee, manager, or designee must be at least 21 years of age, of good moral character, and a citizen of the United States. A licensee must be a resident of Emanuel County, except that the named licensee shall not be required to be a resident of Emanuel County if the named licensee designates a resident of Emanuel County who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license (ie., a "designee). NOTE: In the event a designee is appointed as a responsible party, both applicant and designee must meet all requirements set forth herein for the issuance of a license.
(2)
A licensee, manager, or designee of the licensed premises shall not have been convicted of a felony or have been convicted of any violations of the laws of this state, or any other state, relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages or the sale or possession of drugs.
(3)
A licensee, manager, or designee shall not have had revoked, within the five years preceding his or her application, any license to sell alcoholic beverages issued by any governmental entity.
(4)
A licensee shall be the owner of the premises to be licensed or the holder of a written lease or deed for substantially the same period to be covered by the license. A copy of such written lease or deed shall be attached to the application.
(5)
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises shall be issued to any applicant who does not meet the requirements of section 6-1(d) and the definitions as set forth in section 6-2 as defined in this chapter.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-6. - Transfer.
A license shall be retained for each sales location and shall not be transferable from one location to another or from one person to another, provided, however, that in the case of death of a named licensee, such license may be transferred to the administrator, executor or adult heir or heirs of the deceased unless the board determines it would otherwise violate this ordinance. Such transfer upon death shall only be valid until the current license expires upon which time a new application must be filed. Further, if such transferee cannot meet all the requirements of this ordinance when the time comes to renew the license, it shall not be granted.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-7. - Change in business ownership.
(1)
If any licensee withdraws from, sells or otherwise transfers the licensee's interest in the licensed business, the licensee shall immediately notify the board and surrender the license.
(2)
In the case of such a withdrawal, transfer, or sale, a new application shall be made as for a new license.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-8. - Suspension/revocation.
(1)
Any violation of the provisions of this chapter, state law, or federal law, by any person known or unknown, on the premises of the licensed premises shall be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of the license for cause by the board.
(2)
The board, by resolution, may direct the sheriff of the county and his lawful deputies to remove the license from any place of business of any licensee upon a determination by the sheriff or his deputies that any provision of this chapter, or the laws of the state pertaining to the sale of alcoholic beverages, has been violated. Upon removal of the license as provided above, it shall be illegal for the licensee to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages until such time as the license has been reinstated as hereinafter provided.
(3)
The board shall revoke the license if three citations are issued within 90 days or six citations are issued within a 12-month period relating to the operation of the licensed premises or to the licensee. The term citation shall include any citation issued by the Sheriff of Emanuel County, the Emanuel County Ordinance officer, any local or state fire official, the Georgia Department of Revenue, or any other state agency.
(4)
As has been previously stated, businesses selling alcoholic beverages in Emanuel County are doing so as a result of a privilege granted to the applicant by the Emanuel County Board of Commissioners, and not as a matter of right. As such, the privilege of selling alcoholic beverages may be revoked at any time by request made to the county commissioners or upon their own motion in the event the activities conducted on the premises become unruly or operate in violation of noise or other local ordinances, or are deemed by the county commissioners to be detrimental to the health, safety, welfare, and peaceful enjoyment of the private property of those surrounding the licensed premises. The applicant shall not be entitled to any refund of the fee if a license is revoked or terminated.
(5)
Upon removal of a license, the board shall give written notice within three days to the licensee of the reasons for the removal, as well as notice of any suspension or revocation of the license. The licensee may, within ten days from receipt of the notice from the board, make written application to the board for a hearing on the matter of suspension or revocation, including the right of the licensee to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses. Failure of the licensee to request a hearing within the allotted period shall constitute a waiver of any right of further appeal of the board's decision regarding revocation or suspension. If a hearing is requested as provided above, the same will be held within ten days from receipt of the request by the board. The board, after hearing evidence, shall reach its decision and advise the licensee of the same. If the board determines, with or without a hearing, that a license shall be reinstated, the license shall be returned to the licensee on the effective date of the reinstatement as determined by the board.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-9. - Sales to underage persons prohibited.
(1)
Except as otherwise provided in this section:
a.
No person knowingly, directly or through another person, shall furnish, cause to be furnished, or permit any person in such person's employ to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person under 21 years of age;
b.
No person under 21 years of age shall purchase, attempt to purchase, or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverages;
c.
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent such person's age in any manner whatsoever for the purpose of illegally obtaining any alcoholic beverage;
d.
No person knowingly or intentionally shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of a person less than 21 years of age;
e.
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent his or her identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(2)
The prohibitions contained in paragraphs (a), (b), and (d) of subsection (1) of this section shall not apply with respect to the sale, purchase, or possession of alcoholic beverages for consumption:
a.
For medical purposes pursuant to a prescription of a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in this state;
b.
At a religious ceremony;
c.
When the parent or guardian of the person under 21 years of age gives the alcoholic beverage to the person and when possession is in the home of the parent or guardian and such parent or guardian is present.
(3)
The prohibition contained in subsection (a) of subsection (1) of this section shall not apply with respect to sale of alcoholic beverages by a person when such person has been furnished with proper identification showing that the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold is 21 years of age or older. For purposes of this subsection, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification, driver's license, or an identification card authorized under O.C.G.A. §§ 40-5-100 through 40-5-104. "Proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate or school I.D.
(4)
This section shall not prohibit employment of a person less than 21 years of age in licensed premises if such employment is lawful.
(5)
In any case where a person could reasonably and prudently be in doubt as to whether or not the person to whom an alcoholic beverage is to be sold or otherwise furnished is actually 21 years of age or older, it shall be the duty of the person selling or otherwise furnishing such alcoholic beverage to request to see and be furnished with proper identification as provided in subsection (3) of this section in order to verify the age of such person; and the failure to make such request and verification in any case where the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold or otherwise furnished is less than 21 years of age may be considered by the trier of fact in determining whether the person selling or otherwise furnishing such alcoholic beverage did so knowingly.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-10. - Times of operation and days of sale prohibited.
(1)
Class A and Class C licensees shall have no restriction on hours except those imposed by state or federal law, with the exception of Sunday sales, which shall not be permitted.
(2)
Class B and Class D licensees shall not allow alcoholic beverage to be sold or consumed beyond 12:00 midnight on any Monday through Saturday on the licensed premises. Class B and Class D licensed premises shall be closed and have no occupants at the licensed premises beyond one hour after midnight. (Although the serving and consuming of alcoholic beverages at any facility that has a Class B or D license must cease at 12:00 midnight on Monday through Saturday, the requirement for the premises to be vacated within one hour following 12:00 midnight shall not apply to any area or facility that provides overnight lodging if the person remaining on the premises is in fact lodging at the premises. This exception shall not apply to persons who are not paying for lodging at the premises)
(3)
Class B and Class D licensees shall not permit the sale or consumption of alcoholic beverages on the licensed premises on Sunday.
(4)
No licensee shall permit the sale or consumption of alcoholic beverages at the licensed premises on any day or any time when the sale thereof is prohibited by state or federal law.
(5)
The sale of alcoholic beverages shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; however, the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place is unlawful during such hours that the polls are open. Distance shall be calculated by the same provisions found in section 6-4(10), supra.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-11. - Building requirements.
(1)
No Class A or Class C license shall be issued for any premise where the building to be utilized for commerce is less than 1,000 square feet.
(2)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued for any premise where the building to be utilized for commerce has not passed inspection by the State Fire Marshall, the County Code Enforcement Officers, and the Emanuel County Health Department.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-12. - Sales to intoxicated persons; gambling; disorderly conduct, prohibited.
(1)
No licensee shall permit the sale of alcoholic beverages to any person who is noticeably intoxicated or allow the congregation of noticeably intoxicated persons on licensed premises.
(2)
No licensee shall permit any gambling, betting, illegal lottery, or other device for the hazarding of any money or other thing of value on the licensed premises, except that this prohibition shall not apply with respect to a properly licensed bingo game.
(3)
No licensee shall permit on the licensed premises any disorderly conduct, breach of the peace, or noise or activity which is disturbing to the surrounding neighborhood.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-13. - Breaking package or drinking on premises prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to open or consume any alcoholic beverages on premises of an establishment holding only a Class A and/or C license for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package. In each retail sales location holding a Class A and/or Class C license, there shall be permanently posted and clearly visible to customers, at least two notices in letters at least two inches high, the following language: NOTICE: IT IS UNLAWFUL TO OPEN ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE CONTAINERS ON PREMISES.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-14. - Brown bagging, brown bagging establishments prohibited.
Brown bagging and brown bagging establishments are prohibited in Emanuel County, Georgia unless an establishment has a current, valid Consumption on Premises license.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-15. - Excise taxes on beer.
(1)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon the sale of beer within the county, a specific excise tax as follows:
(a)
Where beer is sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15 ½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15 ½ gallons; and;
(b)
Where beer is sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(2)
The excise taxes provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the licensed wholesale dealer in beer. Each wholesale dealer selling beer within the county shall file a report with the county by the tenth day of each month showing the exact quantities of beer, by size and type of container, sold during the preceding calendar month within the county. Each wholesale dealer shall remit to the county on the tenth day of the month after the calendar month in which such sales were made the amount of excise tax due in accordance with this section. State Law Reference - Local excise tax on beer required, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80.

(1)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon the sale of wine within the county, a specific excise tax in the amount of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all factional parts of a liter.
(2)
The taxes imposed by this section shall not be levied with respect to:
(a)

(b)
Any sale of wine which is exempt from taxation by the state under the Constitution of the United States;

The excise taxes as provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and paid by the licensed wholesale dealing in wine, and each wholesale dealer of wine within the county shall file a report with the county manager by the tenth day of each month showing the exact quantities of wine, by size and type of container, sold during the preceding calendar month within the county. Sec. 6-17. - Excise taxes on distilled spirits.
(1)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon the sale of distilled spirits within the county, a specific excise tax in the amount of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all factional parts of a liter.
(2)
The excise taxes as provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and paid by the licensed wholesale dealing in distilled spirits, and each wholesale dealer of distilled spirits within the county shall file a report with the county manager by the tenth day of each month showing the exact quantities of wine, by size and type of container, sold during the preceding calendar month within the county. Each wholesale dealer shall remit to the county on the tenth day of the month after the calendar month in which such sales were made the amount of excise tax due in accordance with this section.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-18. - Grandfather clause.
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, the board's enactment of this chapter will not revoke or suspend any existing license of a licensee. Nor shall this enactment prevent a licensee who as of the date of the enactment of this chapter holds a then-current Class C license from applying for a Class B or Class D license because their business or licensed premises may not qualify as a restaurant under this chapter. Further, any licensee holding a valid Emanuel County Beer and Wine License at the time of this enactment shall be exempt from the application fee imposed by this chapter. The "grandfather" clauses of this section shall remain valid until a change occurs to the current license applied for, the current licensee, or the current location of the licensed premises. Except as provided herein by this section, upon the board's enactment of this chapter, any license issued pursuant to this chapter shall comply with all parts of this chapter.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-19. - Convenience stores.
A store or establishment commonly referred to as a convenience store, which typically sells gas, snack items, food, beverages, and are commonly frequented by children and families, shall not qualify for either a B or D license which allow for any consumption on the premises.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-20. - Issuance of provisional license.
Provided the applicant, manager, designee, and proposed location meet all other requirements for the issuance of a license, a license might be issued prior to the completion of construction or remodeling of a licensed location. Once construction or remodeling has been completed, applicant shall then have 45 days to submit all notices as required by the appropriate fire chief, Emanuel County Health Department, and Emanuel County Code Enforcement offices. If such deadlines are not met, then the provisional license shall be revoked, with the licensee being entitled to no refund.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-21. - Punishment.
(1)
It shall be a violation of this chapter for any licensee, manager, or designee to act in a manner contrary to the provisions set forth in this chapter.
(2)
It shall be a violation of this chapter for any patron, customer, invitee, or guest to violate the terms and conditions of this ordinance, specifically, but not limited to, the hours of operation as governed under this chapter.
(3)
Any person, firm, or organization found to be in violation of this chapter may be punished up to the maximum extent allowed in chapter 1, section 1-14 of the General Provisions of the Emanuel County Code.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
Sec. 6-22. - Right to audit.
By acceptance of an alcoholic beverage license, the licensee agrees to submit to random audits by the board or its agents for proof of conformity to any section of this chapter. Such audit may include financial statements and/or books of the licensee. Failure to participate in such audit shall be grounds for revocation of license.
(Ord. of 10-19-2020(1))
