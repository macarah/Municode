ARTICLE II. - PERMITS

Sec. 42-31. - Finding of fact.
The board of commissioners finds that the collection of ad valorem taxes on manufactured homes as defined by O.C.G.A. § 8-2-131, poses special problems and that in order to enable proper identification of the manufactured home and to collect ad valorem taxes thereon it is necessary to require certain permits therefor.
(Code 1987, § 7-26)
Sec. 42-32. - Definition.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Manufactured home means a manufactured home as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 8-3-131.
(Res. No. 98-007, § 1, 4-14-1998)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-4.
Sec. 42-33. - Permit required to locate in county.
Before any person shall locate or relocate a manufactured home within the limits of the county, he shall apply for and obtain from the tax commissioner a permit authorizing such location or relocation if such manufactured home is intended to be used by the owner thereof as a residence for himself or for any other purposes allowed by the ordinances of the county.
(Code 1987, §§ 7-27, 7-31)
Sec. 42-34. - Occupation as residence.
No person shall occupy any manufactured homes as a residence unless a current year's permit for the manufactured home shall have been issued therefor as required by this article.
(Code 1987, § 7-28)
Sec. 42-35. - Park operator's responsibility.
No owner or operator of a mobile home park shall permit any manufactured home to be located or relocated within the confines of such park for the residential use unless the permit required by this article shall have been obtained. No owner or operator of any mobile home park shall permit any manufactured home presently located within the limits of such park owned by such person to remain in such park unless a current year's permit authorizing such continued location as required by this article shall have been obtained.
(Code 1987, § 7-29)
Sec. 42-36. - Responsibility of utility service supplier.
No person supplying electricity, gas or water shall connect any electricity, gas or water into any manufactured home unless such homes shall have been issued a current year's permit as provided in this article.
(Code 1987, § 7-30)
Sec. 42-37. - Permit required to relocate outside county.
The owner of any manufactured home used as a residence before relocating the manufactured home from a point within the limits of the county to a point outside the limits of the county shall likewise make application to the tax commissioner for a permit authorizing such relocation.
(Code 1987, § 7-32; Ord. No. 21-07, § 2A, 10-26-2021)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 21-07, § 2A, adopted Oct. 26, 2021, changed the title of § 42-37 from "Permit and decal required to relocate outside county" to read as set out herein.
Sec. 42-38. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. 21-07, § 1, adopted Oct. 26, 2021, repealed § 42-38, which pertained to application and decal for location in county and derived from Code 1987, § 7-33.
Sec. 42-39. - Payment of taxes required.
Ad valorem taxes on the manufactured home shall be paid to the tax commissioner.
(Code 1987, § 7-34; Ord. No. 21-07, § 2B, 10-26-2021)
Sec. 42-40. - Prescribing forms; requiring information.
The tax commissioner is authorized and empowered to prescribe forms for such application and for any permit issued pursuant thereto, as well as any other document required in connection with such application or permit, and the tax commissioner may require that applicants furnish such information in writing as may be reasonably required to effectuate the purposes of this article.
(Code 1987, § 7-35)
Sec. 42-41. - Site approval required.
In connection with the application for the location or relocation of a manufactured home, the permit office shall determine whether or not the manufactured home is located on a site approved by the county. If the location or proposed location of such manufactured home shall not have been approved, the application therefor shall be denied.
(Code 1987, § 7-36)
Sec. 42-42. - Issuance of permit.
If the site of location or proposed location of a manufactured home shall have been approved for such use by the county, and if all ad valorem taxes due under the provisions hereof shall have been paid, the tax commissioner issues a permit. Such permit identifies the manufactured home involved and the site on which it is to be located shall be valid only for such structure and for such site.
(Code 1987, § 7-38; Ord. No. 21-07, § 2C, 10-26-2021)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 21-07, § 2C, adopted Oct. 26, 2021, changed the title of § 42-42 from "Issuance of permit and decal" to read as set out herein.
Sec. 42-43. - Annual application.
No person shall own or have located within the limits of the county any manufactured home unless application for a current year's permit shall have been made and a permit received in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1987, § 7-39)
Sec. 42-44. - Adding delinquency penalty, interest and costs.
If upon application for location or relocation of a manufactured home and as required by this article, it is determined that there are past due ad valorem taxes owing, either to the municipalities or to the county, the tax commissioner notifies the applicable city clerk or collects the county taxes or both. The tax commissioner may add penalties, interest and costs as provided by law.
(Code 1987, § 7-40)
Sec. 42-45. - Enforcement and penalty.
The provisions of this article shall be enforced by the county marshals. Prosecutions for violations of this article shall be upon citation as provided in O.C.G.A. §§ 15-10-62 and 15-10-63. The marshals are hereby authorized to issue citations for the enforcement of the provision of this article. Any person violating any provision of this article shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished as provided in section 1-19 of this Code.
(Code 1987, § 7-41)
