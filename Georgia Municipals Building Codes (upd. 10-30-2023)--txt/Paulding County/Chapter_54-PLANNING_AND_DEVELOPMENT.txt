Sec. 54-31. - Created.
There is hereby created in and for the unincorporated area of the county, a commission to be known as the Paulding County Planning Commission.
(Code 1987, § 16-1; Res. No. 98-27, § 1, 11-10-1998)
Sec. 54-32. - Composition.
(a)
The county planning commission shall consist of seven members. Nominations for membership authorized by subsection (b) shall be submitted to the board for a vote and confirmation.
(b)
The county planning commission shall be comprised of the following members:
(1)
A resident of the unincorporated portion of the county, the nomination of whom is to be made by the chair of the board of commissioners from a list of recommendations provided by the Superintendent of the Paulding County School District;
(2)
A resident of the unincorporated portion of the county, the nomination of whom is to be made by the Post 1 Commissioner;
(3)
A resident of the unincorporated portion of the county, the nomination of whom is to be made by the Post 2 Commissioner;
(4)
A resident of the unincorporated portion of the county, the nomination of whom is to be made by the Post 3 Commissioner;
(5)
A resident of the unincorporated portion of the county, the nomination of whom is to be made by the Post 4 Commissioner;
(6)
A resident of the City of Dallas, Georgia, the nomination of whom is to be made by mayor of the City of Dallas, Georgia; and
(7)
A resident of the City of Hiram, Georgia, the nomination of whom is to be made by the mayor of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(Code 1987, § 16-1; Res. No. 98-27, § 1, 11-10-1998; Res. No. 09-11, § 1, 4-14-2009; Ord. No. 22-03 , § 4, 4-12-2022)

Each member appointed to the county planning commission shall serve for a term that shall expire on December 31 of the designated year of appointment; however each member shall continue to serve until a successor shall be appointed to replace such member as provided in this article.

Sec. 54-37. - Powers and authority.
The county planning commission is hereby authorized and empowered to:
(1)
Conduct public meetings and public hearings on all matters affecting zoning amendments, zoning applications, text amendments to the zoning ordinance, hardship applications, special use permits and land use permits;
(2)
To inquire into all matters which are or may be affected by any public planning or zoning meeting or hearing;
(3)
To conduct such other public meeting, public hearing or business as may be requested by the board of commissioners;
(4)
To make and prescribe rules and bylaws regarding the conduct of the planning and zoning meetings and hearings, the rules and bylaws to be submitted to the board of commissioners for ratification and amendment prior to the use thereof.
(Code 1987, § 16-2; Res. No. 98-27, § 2, 11-10-1998)
Sec. 54-38. - Reimbursement of expenses.
Each member of the county planning commission shall be paid at a flat rate to help defray the costs and expenses incurred as a member of such commission at a rate of $100.00 per month, conditioned upon the scheduling of and the member's attendance at the monthly planning commission meeting. If the county planning commission or any member thereof is required to incur any expenses other than visits to proposed sites within the county, which are the subject matters of public hearings or public meetings, such commission members shall be reimbursed at the current rate or reimbursement as is available to the office of the board of commissioners pursuant to the written policy thereon.
(Code 1987, § 16-2.1; Res. No. 98-27, § 3, 11-10-1998; Res. No. 10-19, § 1, 8-10-2010)
Sec. 54-39. - Meetings.
The meetings of the county planning commission shall be held on the fourth Tuesday of the month at 2:00 p.m., beginning with the first meeting in January 1999.
(Res. No. 98-27, § 6, 11-10-1998)

ARTICLE III. - COOSA VALLEY AREA PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT COMMISSION[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Cross reference— Boards, commissions and authorities, § 2-191 et seq.

Sec. 54-61. - Established.
There is hereby established the Coosa Valley Area Planning and Development Commission, hereinafter called the commission, to be governed by the provisions of this article.
(Code 1987, § 16-3)
Sec. 54-62. - Composition.
The area planning and development commission established by this article shall consist of two members from each of the counties participating in the area planning commission. Of the two members from each county, one shall be appointed by the governing authority of the municipality wherein the county seat is located and the other to be appointed by the governing authority of such county.
(Code 1987, § 16-4(a))
Sec. 54-63. - Term of office.
The term of office for all members of the area planning and development commission shall be five years, except that the initial appointment by this county shall be for a term of four years.
(Code 1987, § 16-4(b))
Sec. 54-64. - Filling vacancies.
Any vacancy in membership of the area planning and development commission shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment which has become vacant.
(Code 1987, § 16-4(b))
Sec. 54-65. - Removal of members.
The appointing authority shall also have the power to remove any member area planning and development commission it may appoint for cause, on written charges, after public hearing.
(Code 1987, § 16-4(b))
Sec. 54-66. - Compensation.
All members of the area planning and development commission shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1987, § 16-4(b))
Sec. 54-67. - Officers.
The area planning and development commission established by this article shall elect a chair and vice-chair from among its appointive members. The term of office of the chair and vice-chair shall be for one year or until reelected or their successors are elected. The area planning and development commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the commission.
(Code 1987, § 16-5)
Sec. 54-68. - Meetings.
The area planning and development commission shall hold regular meetings at least once monthly, such meetings to be held at a time, place and date as prescribed according to a schedule which shall be approved and adopted by a majority of the members of the board of commissioners, and which schedule may be changed upon majority vote of the members of the board of commissioners. This schedule of meetings shall be posted and maintained in a conspicuous place accessible to the public outside of the regular meeting place of the area planning and development commission and at the office of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1987, § 16-5; Res. No. 97-09, § 1, 2-11-1997)
Sec. 54-69. - Rules.
The area planning and development commission shall adopt rules for the transaction of business provided for by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1987, § 16-5)
Sec. 54-70. - Records.
The area planning and development commission shall keep a record of its resolutions, transactions, findings, and determinations, which record shall be a public record.
(Code 1987, § 16-5)
Sec. 54-71. - Employees, staff.
The area planning and development commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work.
(Code 1987, § 16-5)
Sec. 54-72. - Intergovernmental cooperation.
In the performance of its duties, the area planning and development commission may cooperate with, contract with, or accept funds from federal, state, local, public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, may expend such funds, and may carry out cooperative undertakings and contracts.
(Code 1987, § 16-5)
Sec. 54-73. - Expenditures.
The area planning and development commission may make expenditures for the purchase of required equipment and supplies. The expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts to the commission and contract receipts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the participating governing authorities.
(Code 1987, § 16-5; Res. No. 97-09, § 1, 2-11-1997)
Sec. 54-74. - Powers and duties.
It shall be the function and duty of the area planning and development commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, and the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the entire Northwest Georgia area and each political subdivision therein. In general, the commission shall have such powers and duties and responsibilities as set forth in the state law.
(Code 1987, § 16-6)
Sec. 54-75. - Applications to planning commission.
(a)
Applications for subdivisions, hardship mobile homes, rezonings and/or special provisions must be heard by the county planning commission, at a regular monthly meeting, within 45 days of the date of the application.
(b)
An application may not be postponed by the applicant any more than two times. Following a second postponement by the applicant, reapplication and readvertisement, with all fees paid by the applicant, will be required.
(c)
An applicant for a subdivision, hardship mobile home permit, rezoning and/or special provisions permit must appear in person or by representative before the county planning commission, at its hearing on the matter in question. If the applicant does not appear as set out above, the matter before the planning commission, which was to be presented by that applicant, shall be recommended for disapproval.
(Code 1987, § 16-7)
