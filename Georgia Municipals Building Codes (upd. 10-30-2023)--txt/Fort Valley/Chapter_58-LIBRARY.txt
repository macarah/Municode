DIVISION 2. - BOARD OF TRUSTEES[2]

Sec. 58-51. - Name.
The name of the governing board of the Thomas Public Library shall be the Thomas Public Library Board of Trustees.
(Code 1982, § 12-20)
Sec. 58-52. - Composition.
The library board of trustees shall consist of six members, four of whom shall be appointed by the mayor and city council and two of whom shall be ex officio members consisting of the mayor and the president of the Thomas Public Library Auxiliary.
(Code 1982, § 12-21)
Sec. 58-53. - Terms of office of appointive members.
The appointive members of the library board of trustees shall serve for four years. The terms of the appointive members shall be staggered, with one term expiring each year.
(Code 1982, § 12-22)
Sec. 58-54. - Compensation of members.
No member of the library board of trustees shall receive any compensation for service on such board.
(Code 1982, § 12-23)
Sec. 58-55. - Filling membership vacancies.
When a vacancy occurs by resignation, death or expiration of a term, the library board of trustees shall make a recommendation to the mayor and city council for the appointment of a new member to such board for the unexpired term or for a four-year term if it is the replacement of a person whose term has expired.
(Code 1982, § 12-24)
Sec. 58-56. - Officers.
At its first meeting in each fiscal year, the library board of trustees shall elect a chairperson, vice- chairperson, secretary and treasurer, all of whom shall serve for one year, with the right to be reelected for additional one-year terms.
(Code 1982, § 12-25)
Sec. 58-57. - Scope of authority.
The library board of trustees shall have complete control and management of the Thomas Public Library and any branches thereof in the county.
(Code 1982, § 12-26)
Sec. 58-58. - Duties and responsibilities.
The library board of trustees shall have general supervision over all public libraries in the city and the county, and all powers and privileges incident thereto, including the following, which in no way limits the general powers of such board:
(1)
Adopt bylaws under which the library board of trustees shall operate.
(2)
Prescribe rules and regulations for the control, operation, management and use of the Thomas Public Library and any branches thereof in the county.
(3)
Establish branches of the public libraries.
(4)
Establish the compensation and qualifications for employees of the public libraries, including the selection and termination of employees.
(5)
Enter into contracts for the benefit of the libraries.
(6)
Expend the appropriated funds in accordance with the budget of the libraries.
(7)
Accept donations of money, land and other property for the use and benefit of the libraries, and the title to such lands shall be taken in the name of the city or county.
(8)
Hold property, including land, buildings, books and other equipment, for the use and benefit of the libraries.
(9)
Operate, control, manage and direct the policies of the public libraries, but shall not become the entity in which legal title to such properties is situated.
(10)
Do all such acts and things that may be necessary to carry out the purposes for which the library board of trustees was created.
(Code 1982, § 12-27)
Sec. 58-59. - Receiving funds; Lula Thomas Fund.
The library board of trustees shall receive appropriate funds from the county, city, state and United States government and shall be the final authority as to the disbursement of such funds in accordance with the regulations and provisions of law of the several existing governmental bodies. The library board of trustees shall also administer the Lula Thomas Fund in accordance with the directions and provisions of the last will and testament of Lula T. Thomas, and shall make an annual accounting to the mayor and city council, the members of which are the trustees thereof, at the expense of the city.
(Code 1982, § 12-28)
