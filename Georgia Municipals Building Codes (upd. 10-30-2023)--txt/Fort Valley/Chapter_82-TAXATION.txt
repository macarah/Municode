Chapter 82 - TAXATION[1]

Sec. 82-1. - Tax rate established annually.
In accordance with state law, the mayor and city council shall establish the ad valorem mill rate for the succeeding fiscal year, computed in the manner provided in the Georgia Public Revenue Code (O.C.G.A. tit. 48).
(Code 1982, § 19-1)
State Law reference— Municipal ad valorem taxes, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-350 et seq.
Sec. 82-2. - When taxes payable; publication of notice.
All taxes due the city shall be payable as provided by state law. Ten days before the time of payment of such taxes, the city shall publish once a notice thereof in a newspaper published in the city.
(Code 1982, § 19-2)
Sec. 82-3. - Issuance of execution on assessments for which taxes have not been paid.
In accordance with state law, the city clerk shall issue executions on all assessments upon which taxes have not been paid.
(Code 1982, § 19-4)
Charter reference— Collection of taxes, fees, etc., by execution, § 31.
State Law reference— Garnishment to collect taxes, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-360.
Sec. 82-4. - Execution procedure.
All executions of the city shall be issued as provided in sections 31 and 37 of the Charter, and shall be made returnable to the second regular meeting of the mayor and city council after the issuance thereof. Whenever any property is levied on under any execution issued by authority of the city, the procedure therefor shall be the same as that followed in sheriff's sales for state and county taxes, except that the officer making the levy may advertise the sale in any newspaper published in the city, and the sale may be conducted by the city marshal before the front door of the city hall and, provided further, where the property levied on is personalty, the sale shall be advertised by posting a notice at the door of the city hall and at two other places in the city ten days before the sale.
(Code 1982, § 19-5)
Sec. 82-5. - Collection of back taxes.
Whenever any property subject to taxation has not been returned for taxation or assessed in any year prior to the then present year and no taxes have been paid thereon for such prior year, the tax assessor shall proceed as provided in O.C.G.A. tit. 48.
(Code 1982, § 19-6)
Sec. 82-6. - Back taxes where no prior executions have been issued.
If tax returns on assessments have been made for any prior years and no taxes have been paid and no executions issued thereon, the city clerk shall give the owner of the property 30 days' notice that execution will be issued, and if the amount of the taxes thereon is not paid in such 30-day period, then execution shall issue and be collected as provided for other taxes.
(Code 1982, § 19-7)
Sec. 82-7. - City-county local sales and use tax levied.
A combination city-county local sales and use tax, at the rate of one percent, is hereby levied by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 14-11)
State Law reference— Joint county and municipal sales and use tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-80 et seq.
Sec. 82-8. - Procedure for property subject to taxation without prior year assessments.
Whenever any property subject to taxation has not been returned for taxation or assessed in any year prior to the then present year and no taxes paid thereon for such prior year, the tax collector shall be authorized to use the judicial in rem tax foreclosure procedure provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-4-76 et seq. for enforcement of tax liens.
(Res. No. 10-1216-65, 12-16-2010)
