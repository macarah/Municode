Sec. 6-1. - Registration of electors—Eligibility; challenge.
(a)
Eligibility. Any person who is a resident of the city for a period of thirty (30) days prior to any municipal election, and who is registered as an elector with the board of registrars of the county in which such person resides shall be eligible to vote in any election of the city.
(b)
Challenge of registration. Any elector of the municipality shall be allowed to challenge the right of registration of any person whose name appears upon the electors list or who is applying for registration with the registrar. The elector making the challenge shall appear before the registrar on or before election day, and state the grounds for the challenge in writing. The initial decision shall be made forthwith by the registrar with a right of appeal to the governing body of the city. If possible, the registrar will notify the challenged elector and provide him with an opportunity to answer the challenge. If the registrar does not find probable cause for sustaining the challenge, the challenged elector will be given the right to vote or the right to be registered to vote. If in doubt as to the merit of a challenge, the registrar shall notify the chief manager in the challenged elector's election district and the challenged elector shall be allowed to cast a challenged ballot.
(Code 1963, § 5A-4)

Sec. 6-2. - Same—Registration board.
(a)
Appointment; powers, duties; compensation. The governing body of the city shall appoint three (3) members of a board to be known as a registration board. The members shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties and shall be subject to such regulations as set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code and any regulations established by the governing authority.
(b)
Terms; resignations and removal. The members shall serve for a term of one year and until their successors are appointed and qualified, except in the event of resignation or removal as hereinafter provided. The governing body shall have the right to remove one or more of such members, at any time for cause after notice and hearing. Any member shall have the right to resign at any time by submitting a resignation to the governing body. In the event of any such removal or resignation of a member, such member's duties and authority as such shall terminate instantly. In case of the removal, death or resignation of a member, the governing body shall appoint a successor who shall serve the unexpired term if such term is specified. Each such appointment or change in designation shall be entered on the minutes of the governing body.
(Code 1963, § 5A-5)

Sec. 6-4. - Nonpartisan elections.
All elections held for the purpose of filling municipal offices in the city shall be nonpartisan, and no political party shall conduct primaries for the purpose of nominating candidates for such municipal elections.
(Code 1963, § 5A-7)
Sec. 6-5. - Candidate qualification fees.
(a)
The city clerk for the City of College Park, not later than February 1 of each year in which a general municipal election is held in the City, and at least twenty (20) days prior to a special election, in the case of a special election, shall cause to be published in a newspaper of general circulation in the city, the amount of each qualifying fee for each municipal office to be filled in the upcoming general or special election, as the case may be.
(b)
Except as provided in subsection (c) of this section, the fee for each such office shall be three (3) per cent of the annual salary of such office if such office is a salaried office.
(c)
Any candidate who subscribes and swears to an oath that he or she has no assets nor income to pay the qualifying fee may file a pauper's affidavit in lieu of payment of such qualifying fee. Such pauper's affidavit shall, under oath, affirm the candidate's poverty and his or her resulting inability to pay the qualifying fee otherwise required. The form of the affidavit shall be such as is prescribed by the Secretary of State for the State of Georgia and shall include a financial statement which lists the total income, assets, liabilities, and other relevant financial information of the candidate, as required, and shall indicate on its face that the candidate has neither the assets nor the income to pay the qualifying fee otherwise required. The following warnings shall be printed on the affidavit form prepared by the Secretary of State: "WARNING: Any person knowingly making any false statement on this Affidavit commits the offense of false swearing and shall be guilty of a felony."
(Code 1963, § 5A-6; Ord. No. 99-03, § 1, 2-1-99)

Sec. 6-6. - Election officials.
The following offices are created in connection with the conduct of elections for the city:
(a)
Superintendent. The governing body shall appoint a municipal election superintendent who shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body of the city. The superintendent shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(b)
Poll officers.
(1)
Chief manager. A chief manager for each election district shall be appointed by the governing body for each election. The chief manager shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(2)
Assistant managers. Two (2) assistant managers for each election district shall be appointed by the governing body of the city for each election. The assistant managers shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(3)
Clerks. Prior to the opening of the polls in each district of each election the governing body shall appoint a sufficient number of clerks to serve at such election. The clerks shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(Code 1963, § 5A-1)

Sec. 6-7. - Expenses.
The election superintendent is authorized to incur expenses in the preparation and conduct of an election, including the cost of purchasing identification badges for poll officers, the printing of ballots and voters certificates, and the purchasing of all other supplies necessary for the conduct of an election. The superintendent is further authorized to incur all necessary expenses to provide voting booths for the proper conduct of all elections.
(Code 1963, § 5A-2)
Sec. 6-8. - Lighting, heating of polling places.
The superintendent shall cause all polling places to be provided with sufficient light and heat.
(Code 1963, § 5A-3.1)
Sec. 6-9. - Vote required for election.
A candidate for municipal office may be elected in a general or special election by receiving a majority of the votes cast to fill such nomination or public office. Run-off elections shall be governed by the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(Code 1963, § 5A-9)
Sec. 6-10. - Contested elections.
(a)
Where determined. A contest case governed by the provisions of this section shall be determined in the county in which the city hall is located.
(b)
Grounds for contest. Any person who was a candidate in an election or any aggrieved elector shall be permitted to contest the election of any candidate upon any of the following grounds:
(1)
Malconduct, fraud or irregularity by any election official sufficient to change or place in doubt the result.
(2)
Ineligibility of the candidate for the office in dispute.
(3)
Illegal votes received, or legal votes rejected, sufficient to change or place in doubt the result.
(4)
An error in counting the votes or in declaring the result of an election, if such error would change the result.
(5)
Any other cause which shows that another was the person legally elected.
(c)
Petition to contest; notice; hearing. A petition to contest the results of a general or special election shall be filed with the city clerk within five (5) days after the results of an election are certified to the governing body. A hearing shall then be set before the governing body. Written notice shall be given to all affected candidates by the governing body. The notice shall state the time and place of the hearing and shall contain a copy of the petition of contest. The hearing will take place not less than seven (7) days, nor more than fourteen (14) days, after service of the notice upon the affected candidates.
(d)
Determination of validity. The governing body shall have the authority to examine the qualifications of electors concerning their right to vote, order a recount of ballots, examine the registration lists and perform such acts and conduct such examinations as may be deemed necessary to determine the validity of a contest of an election.
(e)
Appeal from determination. The final determination of the governing body may be appealed to the superior court in the manner of appeal from a court of probate, except that such appeal shall be made within ten (10) days after determination of the contest by the governing body by issuance of an order so signifying.
(Code 1963, § 5A-10)
State Law reference— Contested election, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-522 et seq.
Sec. 6-11. - Effect of qualifying of elective office on employment or appointment to city boards.
(a)
The employment status of any city employee shall be declared voluntarily resigned upon such employee qualifying in any election for municipal elective office within the city. Any such person who is contracted with the city for employment shall be deemed to have submitted the applicable notice of termination to the city.
(b)
The office of any appointed official, board member, commission member or authority member shall be declared vacant upon such individual qualifying, in any election for municipal elective office within the city.
(Ord. No. 2012-11, § 1, 5-15-12)
Sec. 6-12. - Ward residency for councilmembers.
(a)
No person shall be eligible to serve as a councilmember representing a ward of the city unless that person has been a resident of such ward for a continuous period of at least twelve (12) months immediately prior to the date of his or her qualification as a candidate for councilmember, and if elected, he or she shall continue to reside therein throughout his or her period of service.
(b)
For any regular elections occurring in 2017, no person shall be eligible to serve as a councilmember representing a ward of the city unless that person has been a resident of such ward for a continuous period of at least five (5) months immediately prior to the date of his or her qualification as a candidate for councilmember, and if elected, he or she shall continue to reside therein throughout his or her period of service.
(Ord. No. 2017-05, § 1, 3-20-17)
