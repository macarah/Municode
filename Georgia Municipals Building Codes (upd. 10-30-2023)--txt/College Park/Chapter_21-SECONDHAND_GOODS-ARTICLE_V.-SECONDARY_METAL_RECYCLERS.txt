ARTICLE V. - SECONDARY METAL RECYCLERS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 21-151. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Chief means the police chief or any member of the department of police designated by the chief.
Department means the department of police.
Ferrous metals means any metals containing significant quantities of iron or steel.
Fixed location means any site occupied by a secondary metals recycler as owner of the site or as lessee of the site under a lease or other rental agreement providing for occupation of the site by the secondary metals recycler for a total duration of not less than three hundred sixty-four (364) days.
License review board means the city license review board.
Money means a medium of exchange authorized or adopted by a domestic or foreign government as part of its currency.
Nonferrous metals means metals not containing significant quantities of iron or steel, including, without limitation, copper, brass, aluminum, bronze, lead, zinc, nickel and alloys thereof, excluding precious metals subject to regulation under article IV of this chapter.
Personal identification card means a state issued driver's license or government issued identification card or a similar card issued by another state, a military identification card, a passport or an appropriate work authorization issued by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service.
Purchase transaction means a transaction in which a secondary metals recycler gives consideration to a seller of goods having a value in excess of five (5) dollars in exchange for regulated metals property.
Regulated metals property means any item composed primarily of any nonferrous metals, but does not include aluminum beverage containers, used beverage containers or similar beverage containers.
Secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority means the chief of police or his or her designee until such time as a multi-jurisdictional operation regulating and monitoring secondhand dealers, pawn brokers, precious metals dealers, secondary metal recyclers, and like dealers is in place. Once a multi-jurisdictional regulatory and monitoring operation is in place, the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority shall mean that multi-jurisdictional operation.
Secondary metals recycler means any person who is engaged, from a fixed location or otherwise, in the business of paying compensation for ferrous or nonferrous metals that have served their original economic purpose, whether or not such person is engaged in the business of performing the manufacturing process by which ferrous metals or nonferrous metals are converted into raw material products consisting of prepared grades and having an existing or potential economic value.
Work permit identification card means a permit authorizing an individual to work at a secondary metal recycler issued pursuant to section 21-176.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-152. - Records required.
(a)
Engaging in the business of buying or accepting in barter or trade used or previously owned metals within the incorporated areas of College Park is hereby declared to affect the public interest due to the opportunity it affords for the disposal of stolen property.
(b)
In the public interest and as set forth herein, all secondary metals recyclers shall maintain records in sequential order by transaction number documenting all secondary metals transactions as set forth herein.
(1)
All secondary metals recyclers shall document an accurate description of each article of property sold, bartered, or traded to the secondary metals recycler. Such records shall include: the name and address of the secondary metals recycler; the name, initials or other identification of the individual entering the information on the ticket; the date of the transaction; the weight, quantity or volume and a description of the type of regulated metals property purchased in a purchase transaction; and, the amount of consideration given in a purchase transaction for the regulated metals property. The secondary metals recycler shall assign a sequential transaction number which shall follow the previous transaction number one by one with no numbers skipped or omitted, documenting each transaction.
(2)
A well-focused, properly exposed, color digital photograph of each article sold, bartered or traded to the secondary metals recycler shall be made at the time of acquisition of any article and of the article's serial number or identification number, which number shall be clearly visible and readable in the images if imprinted on the article. If the article was never imprinted with any type of serial number or identification number, then the image shall show the entire article. All such digital images shall be labeled and stored in such a manner that they are safe from corruption, readily identifiable, and readily available for review.
(3)
All secondary metals recyclers shall require all persons selling, bartering, or trading property to show proper identification prior to conducting a secondary metals transaction. Proper identification is defined as a government issued photo identification card such as a driver's license, military identification card, state identification card, or passport.
(4)
All secondary metals recyclers shall also document the name, address, telephone number, race, sex, height, weight, drivers' license or other government identification number, date of birth, and social security number of the person pledging, trading, pawning, exchanging, or selling the property, along with the date and time of the transaction. This documentation shall be made at the time of the transaction and labeled with the transaction number.
(5)
All secondary metals recyclers shall take a well-focused, properly exposed, color digital photograph of the person selling, bartering, or trading the property. The photograph shall clearly show a frontal view of the subject's face along with the secondary metals recycler's ticket transaction number. Digital images shall be labeled with the transaction number and stored in such a manner that they are safe from corruption, readily identifiable, and readily available for review by law enforcement personnel. Such images shall be stored for a minimum period of four (4) years from the date of creation.
(6)
All secondary metals recyclers shall obtain from each person selling, bartering, or trading any property, the fingerprint (using an electronic digital fingerprint scanner) of the right hand index finger, unless such finger is missing, in which event the print of the next finger in existence on the right hand shall be obtained with a notation as to the exact finger printed. The fingerprint shall be imprinted onto the transaction form in the designated area along with the signature of the person selling, bartering, or trading the property. The fingerprint must be clear and legible. In the event that more than one transaction form is required, a fingerprint and signature shall be obtained for each form. Fingerprints and the information required herein shall be obtained each time such person sells, barters, or trades any property.
(7)
Items of property that appear to be new, unused, and in their original packaging cannot be accepted by the secondary metals recycler unless the customer can supply a copy of the original sales receipt, or other proof of purchase from the place of purchase, to the secondary metals recycler who shall retain the receipt or proof of purchase on file, labeled with the transaction number.
(8)
All secondary metals recyclers shall store the above documentation, digital images, and digital fingerprints, which shall comprise the record of each transaction, together by the transaction number and shall maintain said records for a period of four (4) years and make them available to law enforcement personnel upon request. Both a hard copy and an electronic copy of these stored records shall be kept on-site, which must be kept safe from corruption, readily identifiable, and readily available for review, may, if otherwise permitted by law, serve as the permanent record of the secondary metals recyclers.
(9)
All secondary metals recyclers shall enter the complete record of each transaction as it occurs into the electronic automated reporting system designated by the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority via the internet to the administrator of the electronic automated reporting system. The administrator of the electronic automated reporting system will electronically transmit all transactions to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-153. - Inspection of regulated metals property and records.
During the usual and customary business hours of a secondary metals recycler, a law enforcement officer shall, after properly presenting identification as a law enforcement officer, have the right to inspect any and all:
(1)
Purchased regulated metals property in the possession of the secondary metals recycler; and
(2)
Records required to be maintained under section 21-152.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-154. - Hold notice.
(a)
Whenever a law enforcement officer has reasonable cause to believe that certain items or regulated metals property in the possession of a secondary metals recycler have been stolen, the law enforcement officer may issue a hold notice to the secondary metals recycler.
(b)
The hold notice shall be in writing, shall be delivered to the secondary metals recycler, shall specifically identify those items of regulated metals property that are believed to have been stolen and that are subject to the notice and shall inform the secondary metals recycler of the information contained in this section. The hold notice may be delivered via facsimile transmission; however, a hold notice delivered in such manner shall be valid for a period not to exceed twenty-four (24) hours from such transmission. The hold notice may be extended to the full period of time allowed in subsection (c) of this section by hand delivery to the secondary metals recycler by a law enforcement officer of the hold notice.
(c)
Upon receipt of the notice issued in accordance with this section, the secondary metals recycler receiving the notice may not process or remove the items or regulated metals property identified in the notice or any portion thereof from the place of business of the secondary metals recycler for thirty (30) calendar days after receipt of the notice by the secondary metals recycler, unless sooner released by a law enforcement officer.
(d)
At the expiration of the hold period the hold is automatically released, and the secondary metals recycler may dispose of the regulated metals property unless other disposition has been ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-155. - Applicability of certain sections.
Sections 21-152, 21-153 and 21-154 shall not apply to any regulated metals property purchased or received from any public utility; any industrial or manufacturing plant; any other commercial vendor that generates regulated metals property in the ordinary course of business; the United States or any agency thereof; any state, county or municipality or any department thereof; or any public authority or any scrap purchased at any judicial sale. However, all of such sections shall apply to any regulated metals property which is delivered to a secondary metals recycler by any agent or employee of any of the entities named in this section.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-156. - Identification of vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any secondary metals recycler to purchase regulated metals property in the city utilizing any vehicle which is owned, operated by or in the custody of such secondary metals recycler unless the vehicle shall have printed upon it, in large letters not less than six (6) inches in height, the name of the secondary metals recycler.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-157. - Unlawful activities.
(a)
It is unlawful for a secondary metals recycler to do or allow any of the following acts:
(1)
Purchase regulated metals property between the hours of 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m.;
(2)
Purchase regulated metals property at a location other than the place of business set forth in the license; or
(3)
Purchase regulated metals property in return for money from a trailer, a vehicle or any location other than a fixed location. However, regulated metals may be purchased from a non-fixed location with any negotiable or nonnegotiable instrument, including a check or draft or any other type of instrument purchased with money, and sold for the purpose of making payments or transfers to others.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any secondary metal recycler or any agent or employee of a secondary metal recycler who makes acquisitions of ferrous or nonferrous metals goods to:
(1)
Fail to make, transmit, and maintain entries in the permanent record as required by section 21-152(b);
(2)
Make or transmit any false entries therein;
(3)
Falsify, obliterate, destroy or remove from the place of business such permanent record;
(4)
Fail to submit daily reports as required in section 21-158;
(5)
Fail to collect, hold in reserve, and pay any per-transaction regulatory fee as required in section 21-158(e);
(6)
Refuse to allow a duly authorized law enforcement officer or regulatory inspector to inspect such permanent record or any pawnable goods in such pawnbroker's possession; or
(7)
Receive ferrous or nonferrous metals goods of any character or description from any person under 18 years of age.
(c)
Any person violating subsection (a) or (b) of this section, in addition to being subject to license revocation as set forth in section 21-176, shall be guilty of a City Ordinance violation and upon conviction thereof shall be subject to a fine of up to one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and six (6) months in jail.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-158. - Daily reports to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority; required format.
(a)
All secondary metals recyclers shall make a daily report, in such form as may be prescribed by the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority, of all secondary metals recycler transactions that occurred during the previous twenty-four (24) hours ending at 7:00 p.m. on the date of the report.
(b)
Daily reports shall include all information as required in section 21-152(b).
(c)
Every secondary metals recycler shall enter each transaction as it occurs into the designated electronic automated reporting system via the internet to the administrator of the electronic automated reporting system. The administrator of the electronic automated reporting system will electronically transmit all transactions to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority.
(d)
In the event that the electronic automated reporting system becomes temporarily or permanently disabled, secondary metals recyclers will be notified as soon as possible. Secondary metals recyclers that incur electronic system failures or other events that would cause partial or complete loss of electronic reporting should notify the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority forthwith with the reason of the failure. In this event, the secondary metals recyclers will be required to make records of transactions on paper forms. A digital camera will be used to collect the required pictures, which are to be and transferred to a CD for submittal. The paper forms must include all information as enumerated in section 21-61(b). Secondary metals recyclers shall maintain a minimum three (3) day supply of these paper forms. On a daily basis, all transactions not reported in the electronic automated reporting system, will be delivered to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority by the secondary metals recycler within two (2) hours of the end of the business day for every day until the disability or event has been corrected. Once the disability or event has been corrected, the secondary metals recycler shall input the complete record of all transactions taken on paper forms into the automated reporting system via the internet to the administrator of the electronic automated reporting system.
(e)
The secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority shall designate the required automated reporting system. There will be a regulatory fee assessed to each secondary metal recycler for each transaction: said fee shall be an amount of four dollars ($4.00) per transaction. The secondary metal recycler shall deduct this fee from the amount otherwise due to the seller at the time of the transaction and shall hold this fee in reserve to be paid to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority on a weekly basis at the close of each business week. The total amount of regulatory fees collected each week shall be paid by the secondhand metal recycler to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority at the close of each business week and shall be transmitted electronically or by check as approved by the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority.
(f)
The amounts paid to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority as regulatory fees shall be expended first to cover the costs of the automated reporting system, including but not limited to its lease or purchase, installation, maintenance, and updates, then to cover or supplement the costs of regulating and monitoring secondhand dealers, pawn brokers, precious metals dealers, secondary metal recyclers, and like dealers, including but not limited to personnel and administrative costs, and for programs aimed at the reduction of burglary and theft related offenses.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-159. - Restrictions upon sale, pledge of plumbing material.
(a)
Generally. It shall be unlawful for secondary metals recyclers to purchase or advance money on copper wire or copper lining of bathtubs, lead pipes or any kind of plumbing material sold as junk where offered for sale by others than persons of known good character.
(b)
Duties of secondary metals recyclers. In all cases where these goods are purchased or money advanced, secondary metals recyclers shall keep a record of the purchases or advances, showing the names of the persons from whom received, their residence, the goods so taken and the amount of money paid or advanced, and this shall be at all times open for inspection to members of the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority. It shall also be unlawful for secondhand metals recyclers to purchase these goods or to advance money thereon where their appearance indicates that they have been tampered with or there is an effort to conceal identity or if they are mutilated or otherwise show an effort to destroy evidence of the former location of the property. While the goods are being kept for thirty (30) days before being disposed of, they shall be so arranged that they can be inspected by the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority on demand.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-160. - Claiming interest in property.
(a)
Property which is in the possession of secondary metals recyclers and which has all of the characteristics set forth in section 21-161, below, shall be disposed of pursuant to section 21-162.
(b)
Any person claiming an interest in the property shall be known as a respondent.
(c)
A respondent or any other person claiming any ownership interest of any kind, or possessory right to the property shall have the right to appear at a hearing as set forth in section 21-162(b) and to present any and all evidence in support of such person's claim to the property.
(d)
Except as provided in section 21-162, the failure of any person to appear at such hearing shall constitute a waiver of any claim to the property by such person as against the city and shall authorize the hearing officer to enter a ruling consistent therewith.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-161. - Property included.
(a)
The city has reason to believe that the property was stolen.
(b)
Law enforcement has possession of the property or has placed a hold on the property as set forth in this section below:
(1)
Every secondary metals recycler within the city shall keep a permanent record at their place of business, in which a complete record of all transactions required to be reported under this article shall be entered in the English language in a clear and legible manner and at the time when the transaction takes place. Such record shall contain all the information required to be reported to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority under the provisions of sections 122-152 and 122-158 and shall be retained for no less than four (4) years from the date of the last entry.
(2)
The record of transactions required by subsection (b)(1) and the secondary metals recycler inventory shall be available for inspection by the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority during normal business hours.
(3)
Whenever there exists probable cause to believe that property in the possession of a secondary metals recycler is stolen, the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority may place a hold on the property for a period up to ninety (90) days. When a police officer or representative of the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority (hereinafter referred to as "representative") places a hold on the property, the police officer or representative shall initiate such hold by contacting the secondary metals recycler in person or by telephone and informing the secondary metals recycler of the hold and describing the item or items to be held. Within three (3) days of the initial contact, the police officer or representative shall deliver or mail to the secondary metals recycler a written notice of the hold. The written notice shall include a description of the item or items to be held.
(4)
Whenever property that is in the possession of a secondary metals recycler is subject to a hold and the property is required by a police officer in a criminal investigation or for use as evidence in a criminal proceeding, the secondary metals recycler upon reasonable notice, shall deliver the property to any police officer.
(5)
The secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority may extend a hold placed pursuant to this section for the purpose of criminal investigation or for use in any judicial proceeding, including that set forth in this article. Any extended hold shall be no longer than is reasonably necessary.
(6)
Whenever property that is in the possession of a secondary metals recycler is subject to a hold and the property is no longer required for the purpose of criminal investigation or any criminal proceeding, and more than one person can reasonably be anticipated to make a claim for possession of the property, the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority may follow the procedures set forth in this article for disposition of the property within forty-five (45) days of the conclusion of the criminal investigation or criminal proceeding.
(7)
Whenever property that is in the possession of the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority pursuant to the procedures set forth in this section is no longer required for the purpose of criminal investigation or for use as evidence in any criminal proceeding, the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority may follow the procedures set forth in this article for disposition of the property within forty-five (45) days of the conclusion of the criminal investigation or proceeding.
(8)
If a dealer has good cause to believe that any property in his or her possession has been previously lost or stolen, the dealer shall promptly report his or her belief to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority, together with the name of the owner, if known, and the transaction record associated with the dealer's receipt of the suspected lost or stolen item.
(c)
There are no pending charges in State or Superior Court against a suspect alleged to have stolen the property at issue.
(d)
Two (2) or more persons are known or believed to have made, or can reasonably be anticipated to make, a claim for possession of the property.
(e)
The city makes no claim to possession of the property.
(f)
The property will not be required to be retained for use as evidence in any legal proceeding other than the hearing under this article and law enforcement has no other lawful reason for holding the property.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-162. - Determination of property rights.
(a)
Initiation and service of the petition.
(1)
The petitioner shall file a petition with the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority which shall appoint a hearing officer that shall determine ownership of the property within forty-five (45) days of the conclusion of the criminal investigation or criminal proceedings involving the property. Such petition shall set forth the following:
a.
The facts establishing compliance with section 21-161;
b.
The name and address of each person described in section 21-161(d); or
c.
An accurate description of the property, any identifying marks or serial numbers, the police identification number(s), the location where the property is currently being held, and the person from whom seized, if the property was in fact seized.
(2)
The petition shall be served by personal service or by first class mail, postage prepaid, return receipt requested, upon all persons known to have an interest in the property, each person described and named in section 21-161(d), in all cases, the person from whom the property was obtained or who currently possesses the property subject to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority hold.
(3)
A copy of sections 122-160 through 122-163 of this article shall be served with each petition.
(b)
Hearing.
(1)
Notice.
a.
There shall be served with the petition a notice of hearing setting forth the date, time and place for the conduct of the hearing to determine the right of possession to the property. The hearing date shall not be sooner than twenty-five (25), nor more than forty-five (45), calendar days after the date of service of the petition and notice.
b.
Service shall be made to the last known address of all persons included in subsection (b) of this section.
c.
Service shall be complete upon receipt. If service is made by certified mail, the return receipt shall be prima facie evidence of service.
d.
Proof of service upon each potential claimant shall be delivered to the hearing officer.
(2)
Hearing officers.
a.
The secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority shall have the authority to appoint a hearing officer for the purpose of acting as a fact finder regarding disputes of ownership of property brought by a petitioner. The hearing officer shall be appointed for a two (2) year term and shall receive seventy-five dollars ($75.00) for each day or part thereof the hearing officer appears for such hearings. The hearing officer shall be a licensed attorney at law with at least three (3) years' experience in the practice of law. No person shall be appointed as a hearing officer who holds any interest in any licenses issued under the police powers of the city or who represents any interest which may be adverse to that of the city.
(3)
Conduct.
a.
The hearing shall be conducted informally by a hearing officer and the technical rules of evidence shall not apply, provided that the decision of the hearing officer shall in all cases be based upon substantial and reliable evidence. All parties shall have the right to be represented by counsel, to present evidence and testimony in support of their position, and to cross-examine adverse witnesses. All witnesses shall be placed under oath before testifying.
b.
The burden of proof in determining property ownership shall be by a preponderance of the evidence, and shall at all times be upon the person or persons challenging the possession of the party from whom the property was taken by law enforcement, even if the party from whom the property was taken does not appear at the hearing. If the property was not seized by law enforcement, the burden of proof shall at all times be upon the person or persons challenging the party who currently possesses the property subject to the hold.
c.
The hearing shall be recorded electronically or by other means.
d.
The decision of the hearing officer shall be issued within ten (10) calendar days of the close of the record. The decision shall be in writing, and shall be mailed postage prepaid to each respondent or claimant appearing. A copy of the decision shall also be sent to the respective law enforcement agencies.
e.
The decision of the hearing officer shall be final upon issuance.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-163. - Post-hearing.
(a)
Judicial review.
(1)
Any respondent or other party participating in the hearing who is aggrieved by the decision of the hearing officer may seek judicial review by way of special action to the superior court.
(2)
A complaint seeking special action review shall be filed within thirty (30) days of a final decision by the hearing officer.
(b)
Release of property.
(1)
Any person prevailing in a hearing or uncontested proceeding administered pursuant to this article shall be entitled to receive the property described in the petition after producing a copy of the decision in their favor and appropriate identification to the property's custodian;
(2)
A receipt shall be signed evidencing delivery of the property to the person identified in subsection (b)(1) of the section;
(3)
Any person with custody of the property described who is presented with a copy of the hearing officer's decision and appropriate identification shall release the property to the prevailing party.
(c)
Nothing in this article shall prevent any person from filing an action in a court of appropriate jurisdiction to establish ownership to the property.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSING AND PERMITTING

Sec. 21-171. - Annual license.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to operate as a secondary metals recycler, as defined in this article, in the city without a secondary metals recycler license. A proper application for renewal within the time required must be submitted and such license shall not be under suspension or permanently or conditionally revoked.
(b)
Every person, before operating as a secondary metals recycler within the city, shall first file an application with the police department for a license to operate such a business. The application shall be made on an annual basis. No license shall be issued until a fee in an amount established by action of the city council, a copy of which is on file in the office of the city clerk, is paid to the city.
(c)
The chief of police or any other official of the city designated by the city council, shall investigate each applicant for a license under this article and determine whether such applicant is a person of good character and has not been convicted of a criminal offense as indicated in this article.
(d)
The annual fee for each license to operate as a secondary metals recycler and each permit to be an employee of a secondary metals recycler shall be set forth by resolution of the city council and may be set in a fee schedule of the city. In addition, each secondary metals recycler shall be subject to the business and occupation taxes outlined in article I of chapter 11 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-172. - Application for license to operate as a secondary metals recycler.
(a)
Each applicant seeking a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler shall submit an application to the police department containing the following information:
(1)
The full name, age, street address, mailing address, phone number, facsimile number, e-mail address, Social Security number, and date of birth of the applicant.
(2)
The name and physical address of the proposed business.
(3)
The nature and character of the business.
(4)
If the applicant is a partnership, the name of the partnership and the full names and mailing addresses of the partners.
(5)
If the applicant is a corporation, the name of the corporation and the full names and mailing addresses of its officers, the members of its board of directors and its principal stockholders.
(6)
Any bank accounts listed in the name of the applicant, or maintained by the applicant, whether an individual, partnership or corporation.
(7)
Whether the applicant, or any partners, or any corporation officers or board members, or principal shareholders, has been convicted by the federal government, any state, or any city in the United States of any felony, any crime involving theft, fraud, or against property, or any violation of the "Family Violence Act." "Conviction" as used in this section shall include an adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere, or the forfeiture of a bond.
(8)
A listing of all real property which the applicant, partnership, or corporation is presently leasing, and the names and addresses of all lessees and lessors of such property.
(9)
A certified copy of the applicant's financial statement for the 12-month period ending one week before the filing of the application for license, and state of incorporation of such corporation, as well as the names and addresses of the agents and employees of such corporation for a period of two years immediately prior to the filing of such application.
(b)
The police chief or any other official of the city designated by the city council shall establish a form by which an application under this section is to be made and shall make copies of the form available to the public upon request. An applicant seeking to obtain a license under this section shall submit the application by completing the provided form.
(c)
All applications shall be completed by the applicant and sworn to and signed by the applicant in the presence of a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths.
(d)
Each applicant shall certify on the application that they have read this article and, if the license is granted, each licensee shall maintain a copy of this article on the premises.
(e)
Each applicant shall complete a waiver in order for a record of the applicant's criminal history to be obtained.
(f)
All applicants shall furnish all data, information and records requested of them by the city council, the police department or other official designated by the city council, and failure to furnish such data, information, and records within thirty (30) days from the date of such request shall automatically serve as grounds to deny the application. An applicant, by filing an application, agrees to produce for questioning any person(s) who are considered relevant to the ascertainment of facts relative to such license, as may be requested by the city council, the police department, or other official designated by the city council. The failure to produce such persons within thirty (30) days after being requested to do so may result in denial of the application.
(g)
Once an application, accompanying documents, and the required license fees are filed with the police department, the police department shall conduct a criminal investigation of the applicant and prepare a written criminal investigation report detailing all information relating to fingerprinting, criminal history, arrest data, and other matters pertinent to law enforcement. Upon completion of the criminal investigation report, the police department shall assemble the tendered application forms and accompanying documents relating to investigation and processing of the application and deliver such documents to the city clerk. If the criminal investigation report shows that the applicant fails to meet the requirements set by this article, or if the applicant fails to meet all other requirements outlined by this article, then the city clerk shall inform the applicant, in writing, that the application has been denied. The written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for the denial and shall inform the applicant of his right to appeal. Such appeal shall be before the city council in accordance with section 21-180. If an applicant desires to appeal a denial, the applicant must file a written request for an appeal with the city clerk within ten (10) business days of the date of the written notice informing the applicant of the denial of the license.
(h)
Any application for a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler that the city clerk determines satisfies all the requirements outlined in this article, including character requirements as contained in the criminal investigation report of the police department, shall be scheduled for review at the next regularly scheduled public meeting of the city council. At that meeting, the applicant and any person(s) opposed to such application have the right to present to the city council any information that the city council determines is relevant to the licensing decision. In making its determination on whether to approve or deny the application, the city council shall look to the qualifications set forth in this article and consider the public interest and welfare of the citizens of the city. The council shall have the discretion to grant or deny the application based on the information presented. A decision by the city council shall be made within thirty (30) days from the date of the public hearing, unless the decision is postponed for purposes of obtaining additional information deemed necessary for consideration of the application. Notice of the decision by the city council shall be mailed to the applicant. If the application is denied, such written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for the denial and shall notify the applicant of his right to appeal. Such appeal shall be taken solely in accordance with section 21-180.
(i)
In all instances in which an application is denied, the applicant may not reapply for the same type of license for at least one year from the date of denial.
(j)
Upon the issuance of a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler, the licensee must have and continuously maintain in the city a registered agent upon whom any process, notice, or demand required or permitted by law or under this ordinance may be served. This agent must be an individual and must be a resident of city. The licensee shall submit the name of such agent, along with the written consent of such agent, to the city clerk. The identity of the agent may also be submitted contemporaneous to filing the license application.
(k)
Upon approval by the city council of the application for a license, the city clerk shall issue a license in accordance with the approved application. If the applicant is an individual, the license shall be issued in the name of the individual. If the application is a corporation, the license shall be issued in the name of the corporation and in the name of the majority stockholder or a principal officer of the corporation. If the application is a partnership, the license shall be issued in the name of the partnership and in the name of one of the partners. All licenses issued shall be granted for the full calendar year or for the number of months remaining in the calendar year. Any applicant granted a license before July 1 shall pay the full license fee without proration. License fees for licenses granted on or after July 1 shall be one-half (½) the annual license fee. License fees are not refundable once the license is granted by the city.

Sec. 21-173. - Qualifications for issuance of license to operate as a secondary metals recycler.
Any person who desires to obtain a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler must meet the minimum qualifications set forth in this section. If the applicant is a partnership, each partner must meet the qualifications of any individual license and must make sworn statements of these qualifications as part of the application process. If the applicant is a corporation, each principal officer, each member of the board of directors, and each principal stockholder of the corporation must meet the qualifications as part of the application process.
(a)
No license to operate as a secondary metals recycler or renewal license to operate such a business shall be issued to any applicant where any individual, having an interest either as owner, partner, officer, or principal shareholder, directly beneficial or absolute, has been convicted by the federal government, any state, or any city in the United States of any felony, any crime involving theft, fraud, or against property, or any violation of the "Family Violence Act." "Conviction" as used in this section shall include an adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere, or the forfeiture of a bond.
(b)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who is less than eighteen (18) years of age.
(c)
No secondary metal recycler shall be operated at the same location or in the same premises as a pawnshop, a secondhand dealership, or a precious metals dealership. No license for a secondary metal recycler shall be issued for a location currently licensed for use as a pawnshop, as a secondhand dealership, or as a precious metals dealership.
(d)
No license shall be granted where the applicant has had any license, or any permit, to own or to operate a pawnshop, a secondhand dealership, a precious metals dealership, or a secondary metals recycler issued by any county, municipality, or other governmental subdivision suspended or revoked.
(e)
No license to operate as a secondary metals recycler shall be issued if the application contains a material omission, untrue or misleading information, or if the application fails to indicate the true ownership of the proposed business.
(f)
No license shall be issued where the applicant has failed to pay any required fee, including any fees or assessments owed to other departments of the city.
(g)
No license shall be issued for a location that is not in compliance with any federal, state, or local regulation including, but not limited to, a state certificate of occupancy, a city certificate of occupancy, the zoning ordinances, or the fire code.
(h)
No license shall be issued to a new applicant for a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler in any location which is within two thousand (2,000) feet of any existing licensed secondhand dealership, pawnshop, precious metals dealership, or secondary metal recycler.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-174. - Commencement and forfeiture of license to operate as a secondary metals recycler.
(a)
Any holder of a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler must, within six (6) months after the issuance of the license, open for business the establishment referred to in the license, unless such period is extended by the city clerk. Failure to open the licensed establishment as referred to in this subsection within the six (6) month period shall serve as an automatic forfeiture and cancellation of the license, and no refund of license fees shall be made to the license holder.
(b)
Any holder of a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler who begins the operation of the business as authorized in the license and, for a period of three (3) consecutive months thereafter, ceases to operate the business as authorized in the license, shall automatically forfeit his license, which license shall, by virtue of such failure to operate, be canceled without the necessity for any further action by the city clerk or the city council.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-175. - Suspension and revocation of license to operate as a secondary metals recycler.
(a)
The city council shall be authorized to suspend, revoke or place on probation with or without conditions, any license previously granted under the provisions of this article, upon hearing, with at least five (5) days written notice mailed to the owner at the licensed establishment or upon one or more the following, as:
(1)
An owner or holder of the license or permit gives false or misleading information in the original or renewal application for any license or permit required by this division.
(2)
An owner has knowingly allowed the continuous violation of an ordinance of the city or other law of the State of Georgia or federal law to occur on the premises.
(3)
An owner has violated any provision of this article.
(4)
An owner has been convicted of any felony or any crime involving theft, fraud or against property, by the State of Georgia or the city regarding an offense which was committed on the premises or which would otherwise violate the provisions of this article.
(5)
An owner fails to pay any fee, occupation tax, fine, transaction fee or other amount of money due to the city under this article or any other ordinance of the city.
(6)
An owner has failed to obtain, or has suffered the expiration, suspension or revocation of any paper or document necessary in pursuance of its business as may be required by any officer, agency, authority, or department of the city, state, or United States under authority of any law, ordinance or resolutions of the city, state or the United States.
(7)
An owner made any misrepresentation of fact, whether through advertisement or through any form of direct communication oral or written, which is intended to mislead the public or to mislead any party with whom the owner deals in pursuance of the regulated business. The term misrepresentation of fact as used herein shall embrace not only express misrepresentations but also misrepresentations arising by virtue of the owner's conduct, including acts and omissions.
(8)
An owner has failed to make an entry of any material matter in his or her permanent record; or falsified, obliterated, destroyed or removed from his or her place of business such permanent record.
(9)
An owner has failed to submit the daily report to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority within the prescribed time; or has submitted an illegible, incomplete or improper daily report to the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority.
(10)
An owner has refused to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer to inspect his or her permanent record, his or her daily report or any goods in his or her possession during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time.
(11)
An owner has failed to maintain a record of each transaction in ferrous metals, nonferrous metals, or other regulated metals property, and fingerprints and photographs for at least four (4) years.
(12)
An owner has accepted a purchase of property from a person under the age of eighteen (18) years or who the owner knows is not the true owner of such property.
(13)
An owner has failed to comply with any rules or regulations dealing with the implementation of this article by the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority.
(14)
An owner has failed to adequately supervise and monitor the conduct of the employees, patrons, and others on the licensed premises, or on any property owned or leased by the licensee, including but not limited to the parking lots and parking areas, or on any parking lots or areas which may be lawfully used by patrons of the licensed establishment, in order to protect the safety and well being of the general public and of those utilizing the premises.
(15)
Permitting the solicitation of patrons on the licensed premises for prostitution or any other illegal act where the owner, or the licensed establishment's employee or agent knew or should have known of such conduct.
(16)
In the case of bankruptcy, receivership, or levy of legal process upon the licensed establishment or property therein.
(17)
Except as provided in section 21-179, any change in the ownership of an entity owning a licensed outlet.
(18)
The violation of any other law, ordinance, or regulation governing the operation of establishments licensed as secondary metal recyclers or which are reasonably related to the operation of such businesses.
(b)
No license which has been issued to any person pursuant to this Article shall be suspended or revoked except upon one or more of the grounds stated in subsection (a) of this section and until after the holder of the license has been afforded a hearing.
(c)
No hearing as provided in subsection (b) of this section shall be held unless the holder of the permit has been given at least five (5) days written notice of the time, place and purpose of the hearing and a written statement of the charge upon which the hearing shall be held.
(d)
Charges upon which any permit issues pursuant to this division may be initiated by the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority, the license and permit unit of the secondhand goods regulatory and monitoring authority or the city police department through formal citation.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-176. - Annual employee work permit required.
No person shall be employed by, or work at, a secondary metal recycler in any capacity until such person has filed an application with the police department for a work permit identification card and has received said card. At the time of the filing of the application, the applicant shall also submit the required permit fee. The work permit identification card must be renewed annually.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-177. - Employees of secondary metal recyclers.
(a)
Qualifications. An employee of a secondary metals recycler shall not be less than eighteen (18) years of age. Any employee who has been convicted of any felony or any crime involving theft, fraud or against property shall not be permitted to work on the premises of a secondary metals recycler for a period of ten (10) years from the date of such conviction, unless a longer prohibition on such work is ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction. The term "on the premises" shall include all work done or services performed in the scope of employment elsewhere than on the regulated premises. "Conviction" as used in this section shall include an adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere, or the forfeiture of a bond.
(b)
Approval for employment. Each applicant seeking a permit to work as an employee of a secondary metals recycler shall submit an application to the police department containing the following information:
(1)
The full name, age, street address, mailing address, phone number, facsimile number, e-mail address, Social Security number, and date of birth of the applicant.
(2)
The name, physical address, mailing address, phone number, facsimile number, and e-mail address of the business where the applicant proposes to work.
(3)
The nature and character of the business where the applicant proposes to work.
(4)
If a partnership, the name of the partnership and the full names and mailing addresses of the partners.
(5)
Whether the applicant has within the preceding ten (10) years, a record for any conviction for any felony or crime involving theft, fraud or against property.
(c)
The police chief or any other official of the city designated by the city council shall establish a form by which an application under this section is to be made and shall make copies of the form available to the public upon request. An applicant seeking to obtain a permit under this section shall submit the application by completing the provided form.
(d)
All applications shall be completed by the applicant and sworn to and signed by the applicant in the presence of a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths.
(e)
Each applicant shall certify on the application that they have read this article.
(f)
Each applicant shall complete a waiver in order for the applicant's criminal history to be obtained.
(g)
Each applicant must be fingerprinted by the police department at the time the application is filed.
(h)
All applicants shall furnish all data, information and records requested of them by the city council, the police department or other official designated by the city council, and failure to furnish such data, information, and records within ten (10) days from the date of such request shall automatically serve as grounds to deny the application. An applicant, by filing an application, agrees to produce for questioning any person(s) who are considered relevant to the ascertainment of facts relative to such license, as may be requested by the city council, the police department, or other official designated by the city council. The failure to produce such persons within ten (10) days after being requested to do so may result in denial of the application.
(i)
Independent contractors. For purposes of this article, the description of an employee of or worker in a secondary metal recycler as an independent contractor shall not exempt that employee or worker from the requirement of obtaining a permit for such employment.
(j)
Once an application, accompanying documents, any other requested information, and the required investigative and license fees are filed, the city shall have ten (10) days to investigate the information submitted by the applicant. If the employee is found to be of good moral character, the police department shall grant an approval of employment and shall issue an annual work permit identification card authorizing the person to be employed by a precious metals dealer. Upon approval the employee may begin working on the regulated premises. It shall be the duty of the secondary metal recyclership to insure that the provisions of this section receive compliance. The employee is required to notify the police department of any change in information or circumstances which has occurred since the original approval was granted. If approval is denied, the applicant may, within ten (10) days, apply in writing to the police department for reconsideration setting forth in detail the reasons why reconsideration is warranted. If reconsideration is denied, the police department shall inform applicant of that decision in writing, setting forth in reasonable detail the reasons for the denial and shall inform the applicant of his right to appeal. Such appeal shall be before the city council in accordance with section 21-180. If an applicant desires to appeal a denial, the applicant must file a written request for an appeal with the city clerk within ten (10) business days of the date of the written notice informing the applicant of the denial of the license.
(k)
Suspension, revocation of work permit identification card. Conviction for violating the provisions of this article or any other ordinance of the city, rules or regulations of the city or conviction for any felony or any crime involving theft, controlled substances, fraud or a crime against property shall subject the employee to immediate suspension or revocation of the work permit identification card, and, in the case of the secondary metals recycler, shall subject the license holder to suspension of any license authorized by this article.
(l)
In all instances in which an application is denied, the applicant may not reapply for the same type of permit for at least one year from the date of denial.
(m)
The annual work permit identification card shall only be issued in the name of the individual applicant. All personal identification cards issued shall be granted for the full calendar year or for the number of months remaining in the calendar year. Any applicant granted a license before July 1 shall pay the full permit fee without proration. Permit fees for personal identification cards issued on or after July 1 shall be one-half (½) the annual permit fee. Permit fees are not refundable once the identification card is issued by the city.

Sec. 21-178. - Background checks.
The licensee authorized to operate as a secondary metals recycler shall perform criminal background checks on all employees hired after the date of passage of this article. The secondary metals recycler shall maintain such background check records for a minimum period of at least five (5) years after an employee leaves the secondary metal recycler's employ. The secondary metals recycler shall provide all information relevant to the criminal background checks on all new employees to the police department within thirty (30) days of the date of hire of said employee.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
Sec. 21-179. - Renewals and transfers.
(a)
Any holder of a license to operate as a secondary metals recycler shall apply for renewal of an existing license and shall pay the annual license fee no later than February 15 th of each calendar year in which it does business.
(b)
No license to operate as a secondary metals recycler shall be transferable except upon application to the police department in the same form and manner, and subject to the same requirements with respect to the transferee as are applicable in an original application. Any such license may be transferred only to another applicant doing the same business at the same place as the license holder to whom the license was originally issued. When permission for transfer has been granted, the original licensee or transferee shall cause the license to be delivered to the city clerk, who shall record such transfer, and the transferee shall pay a fee therefore as a condition precedent to engaging in operations under the license. The fee for such transfer shall be established from time-to-time by the city council.
(c)
Any holder of a work permit identification card authorizing the person to be employed by a secondary metals recycler shall apply for renewal of said card and shall pay the annual permit fee no later than February 15 th of each calendar year.
(d)
A work permit identification card authorizing the holder to be employed by a secondary metals recycler is not transferable under any circumstances and applies only to the person identified on that card.

Sec. 21-180. - Appeal procedure.
(a)
Upon receipt of a timely appeal of an administrative denial, or upon alleged violation of those items in section 21-175, the city clerk shall schedule a hearing before the city council and provide written notice to the adverse party of the time, place and date of the scheduled hearing. The city clerk shall also state in the written notice the basis for the administrative denial or the violation or occurrence alleged that forms the basis for the denial or potential suspension or revocation. After notice of hearing, matters scheduled for hearing may only be continued by agreement of the city attorney and the adverse party and/or counsel for the adverse party.
(b)
The city council shall have the duty of conducting hearings concerning the denial, revocation, or suspension of a license. The standard of proof on all issues in the hearing shall be a preponderance of the evidence and a determination will be made on the basis of the evidence presented at the hearing.
(c)
At the hearing, after presentation of the case against the adverse party, the adverse party will have an opportunity to present his case, to rebut the allegations made against him, and present whatever defenses he has. The adverse party shall have the right to be represented by an attorney, at the expense of the adverse party, and to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses. An opportunity for rebuttal shall be provided.
(d)
At the conclusion of the hearing, the findings and conclusions of the city council shall be forwarded to the city clerk and it shall be the duty of the city clerk to provide written notification via certified mail to the adverse party of the decision of the city council.
(e)
The decision of the city council shall be final unless appealed to the Superior Court within thirty (30) days of receipt of the city clerk's written notification to the adverse party of the council's decision.
(Ord. No. 2016-7, § 1(Exh. A), 2-15-16)
