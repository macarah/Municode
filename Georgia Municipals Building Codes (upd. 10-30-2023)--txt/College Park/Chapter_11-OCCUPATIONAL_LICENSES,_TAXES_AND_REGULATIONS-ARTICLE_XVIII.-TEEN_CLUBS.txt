ARTICLE XVIII. - TEEN CLUBS

Sec. 11-291. - Definitions.
As used in this Article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
(1)
Premises: In addition to the building and curtilage normally considered as appurtenant thereto, "premises" shall also include all public and private parking areas adjacent to the building on the same parcel, regardless of size.
(2)
Teen club: Any business establishment catering exclusively or primarily to individuals between the ages of thirteen (13) and nineteen (19) years, inclusive, and which is operated primarily as a place for such individuals to congregate for social, as opposed to commercial or educational, activity for an admission fee. "Teen club" shall not include any business establishment which is operated primarily as a place for the exchange of merchandise for consideration or any school, educational or city-owned facility.
(Ord. No. 96-11, § 1, 4-1-96)
Sec. 11-292. - Licensing.
(a)
Any person, firm, partnership or corporation desiring to operate a teen club with[in] the city shall obtain a license from the mayor and council, and such license shall be acquired in addition to the occupational tax certificate required by this code. Such person, firm, partnership or corporation shall make application to the mayor and council and shall:
(1)
Complete the application. Any untrue or misleading information contained in, or material omission left out of an original or renewal application for a license hereunder shall be cause for the denial thereof, and, if any license has been granted under these circumstances, same shall be cause for revocation;
(2)
Include a scale drawing of the premises including interior walls and also any parking spaces reserved for the licensed establishment; and
(3)
Pay the applicable license fee.
(b)
No person, partnership, business, firm or corporation shall be granted a license if any person, partner, officer or the owner of fifteen (15) per cent or more of the shares of or any interest in any such business, firm, partnership or corporation has been convicted of or has pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving moral turpitude, lottery, gambling, illegal possession or sale of any narcotics, drugs or chemicals for which the sale thereof is controlled or prohibited by law, the control of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages, or receiving stolen property, within the period of fifteen (15) years immediately prior to the filing of the application. Should any such person, partner, or officer or shareholder of a corporation, after a license has been granted, be convicted of or plead guilty or nolo contendere to any such crime, said license shall be subject to suspension and revocation. Withdrawal of an owner, officer or partner or enumerated shareholder, after pleading guilty or nolo contendere to the above enumerated crime shall not necessarily mitigate against the penalty imposed hereby.
(c)
The license shall be renewed each year. A change of ownership shall require a new license and a new license fee.
(d)
The fee for the teen club license shall be five hundred dollars ($500.00) per year if live entertainment is provided within the teen club, and two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) per year if no live entertainment is provided in the teen club.
(Ord. No. 96-11, § 1, 4-01-96)
Sec. 11-293. - Regulation of conduct on premises.
Any person, firm, business, partnership or corporation licensed hereunder or patronizing such business shall comply with the following rules and regulations pertaining to the operation of a teen club:
(1)
No teen club shall be opened for business prior to one hour after the close of any public secondary school of the Fulton County board of education or the Clayton County board of education; or, on days for which no school is scheduled, prior to 12:00 p.m.; or on Sundays, prior to 1:00 pm. All teen clubs shall close at or before 11:15 p.m. All patrons of teen clubs shall vacate the premises, including the parking areas, at or before 11:30 p.m.
(2)
No licensee shall permit any alcoholic beverages to be offered or consumed on, or brought onto the licensee's premises or in any parking area dedicated to the use of the premises.
(3)
No licensee shall permit any controlled substances to be offered or consumed on, or brought onto the licensee's premises or in any parking area dedicated to the use of the premises.
(4)
Teen clubs shall limit admission to individuals between the ages of thirteen (13) years and nineteen (19) years, inclusive, with the exception of chaperons as provided in paragraph (5) below.
(5)
Teen clubs shall have in attendance a minimum of three (3) chaperons or security officers for every one hundred (100) persons in attendance, one of whom shall be female. Chaperons or security officers shall be over twenty-five (25) years of age and shall comply with the requirements of this article.
(6)
Teen clubs shall maintain a current and accurate count of the number of individuals in its building at all times.
(7)
All areas of the licensee's premises outside its building shall have sufficient lighting thereof so as to allow observers from the closest public right-of-way to observe activities taking place upon its premises, outside its building.
(8)
No licensee may charge an admission fee or other charge, nor shall it deny admission to any individual entering its premises for the purpose of retrieving his child or children.
(9)
It shall be unlawful for a licensee hereunder to knowingly or recklessly allow, after a warning from any city official, any individuals to congregate for no apparent legitimate purpose upon its premises, outside its building.
(10)
Once a patron of a teen club has paid his admission and entered the building, said patron may not thereafter be re-admitted to the building upon leaving.
(11)
Teen clubs licensed hereunder shall at all times be open to the public authorities of the city for inspection.
(12)
No licensee shall permit the number of persons on the premises to exceed the occupancy established by the city fire marshal.
(Ord. No. 96-11, § 1, 4-01-96)
Sec. 11-294. - Employee investigation; fees.
(a)
No person may be employed or allowed to work as a security officer or a chaperon by an establishment holding a license hereunder until such person has been fingerprinted by the police department and has been issued a letter of clearance by the police department indicating the person has not, within the preceding five (5) years been convicted of or pled nolo contendere to any law catalogued by subsection 11-292(c) of this article. A letter of compliance shall be issued by the police department indicating the person is eligible for such employment. This letter must be kept on file at the licensed establishment where such person may be working. This section shall apply to security officers, chaperons, entertainers and musicians engaged in temporary work or independent contracting, as well as regular employees.
(b)
The police department shall have a search made relative to any police record of the person fingerprinted. In the event there is a violation of the laws as catalogued in subsection 11-292(c) of this article, the police department shall issue a letter to the person fingerprinted stating that the person is ineligible for employment. This determination may be appealed to the city manager.
(c)
Any letter of eligibility for employment issued hereunder shall expire twelve (12) months from the date of issue.
(d)
The mayor and council may prescribe reasonable fees for certifying the eligibility of employment.
(Ord. No. 96-11, § 1, 4-01-96)
Sec. 11-295. - Violations; penalties.
In the event that any owner, operator, patron or other person on the premises of a teen club is found to be in violation of the provisions of this article, he shall be cited for such violation and, upon conviction, shall be punished by fine or imprisonment, or both, as established by section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances, provided that any violation of the provisions of this article that is under the jurisdiction of the juvenile court shall be governed by the procedures regarding juveniles.
(Ord. No. 96-11, § 1, 4-01-96)
Sec. 11-296. - License suspension; revocation.
(a)
In addition to the penalties prescribed by section 11-295, the mayor and council, upon becoming aware of an alleged violation of this article or of any state laws or city ordinances by an owner, operator, patron or other person on the premises of a teen club, may notify the license holder to appear for a hearing at such time and place as established by the city council to answer to charges of the alleged violation and to show cause why his license should not be suspended or revoked. At least ten (10) days' written notice shall be provided to a license holder of any hearing under this section.
(b)
Any violation of a provision of this article or other city ordinances or state laws by an owner or operator of a teen club or by a patron or other person while on the premises of a teen club shall be considered as grounds for license suspension or revocation under this article.
(Ord. No. 96-11, § 1, 4-01-96)
