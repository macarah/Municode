ARTICLE IV. - SELF-SERVICE LAUNDRIES

Sec. 11-70. - Definition.
The term "self-service laundry" as used in this article shall mean any establishment in which coin-operated facilities are available for washing clothes or for drying clothes, or both, and which is open and available to the general public for use.
(Code 1963, § 14-40)
Sec. 11-71. - Business tax.
Persons desiring to engage in a self-service laundry business shall conform with the requirements of this article and in addition, shall be responsible for paying the occupation tax as set out in Article I of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 95-07, § 6, 11-7-95)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 95-8, § 6, adopted November 7, 1995, amended the Code by repealing § 11-71 and adding a new § 11-71. Former § 11-71 pertained to a required license, and derived from the Code of 1963, § 14-39.
Sec. 11-72. - Installation of equipment.
Installation of all equipment in such establishments shall be in conformity with the codes and ordinances relating to such installations.
(Code 1963, § 14-41)
Sec. 11-73. - Maintenance of premises; health of employees.
(a)
Premises used for self-service laundries must be kept in a clean and sanitary condition.
(b)
No refuse of any kind shall be permitted to accumulate on such premises; and the premises shall be kept free from rats, mice and vermin.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to employ any person afflicted with a venereal or any contagious disease in a self-service laundry.
(Code 1963, § 14-42)
Sec. 11-74. - Attendant required during certain hours.
It shall be unlawful to keep any such establishment open for business between 12:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. unless there is an adult attendant in charge of the premises during such hours.
(Code 1963, § 14-43)
Secs. 11-75—11-79. - Reserved.
