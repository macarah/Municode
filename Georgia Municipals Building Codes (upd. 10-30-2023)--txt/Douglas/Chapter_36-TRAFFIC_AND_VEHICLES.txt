ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 36-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 6 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq.) and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited in this chapter.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished in accordance with section 1-11.
(Code 1978, § 22-1; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-1)
Sec. 36-2. - Authority to adopt regulations, permanent and temporary.
The city manager is empowered to make and enforce regulations necessary to make effective the sections of this chapter and to make temporary regulations which are not inconsistent with the sections of this chapter.
(Code 1978, § 22-2; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-2)
Sec. 36-3. - Authority to determine crosswalks, stop intersections, traffic lanes, turning restrictions.
(a)
Subject to state law, the city manager is authorized to establish and to designate, and shall thereafter maintain, by appropriate devices, marks or lines upon the surface of the street, crosswalks approximately equal in width to the adjacent sidewalks at all intersections where in his opinion there is particular danger to pedestrians.
(b)
The city manager is authorized to mark lanes for traffic on street pavements at such places as he may deem advisable, consistent with the sections of this chapter.
(c)
The city manager is empowered to determine those intersections at which operators of vehicles shall not make a left turn, a right turn, or a U-turn, and shall place proper signs at such intersections.
(d)
The city manager is authorized to place and maintain, or cause to be placed and maintained, on each street intersection at through streets and at all other intersections as may be required from a traffic study, and at or near the property line of the through street, appropriate signs upon the streets (in addition thereto may place and maintain any appropriate devices or marks in the roadway), such signs, devices or marks to bear the word "Stop," and to be located in such a position and to be provided with letters of such size as to be clearly legible from a distance of at least 100 feet along the street intersecting the through street.
(e)
The city manager shall have the authority to determine the location of passenger zones and loading zones as described in this chapter and shall erect and maintain appropriate signs indicating the same.
(Code 1978, § 22-3; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-3)
Sec. 36-4. - Authority to close streets temporarily; designation by signs or barriers; compliance required.
The city manager is empowered to close any street or part thereof from traffic by vehicles or pedestrians, either or both, when, in the opinion of the city manager, it is deemed necessary or advisable while street construction, improvements or repairs are being made; or when traffic thereon would subject persons or property to extra hazard or danger from any cause; or to affect quarantine regulations; or to prevent noises in case of sickness. Such closed streets or portions of streets shall be designated by suitable signs or barriers to show that they are closed. It shall be unlawful for any person to enter upon any street or portion of street so closed.
(Code 1978, § 22-4; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-4)
Sec. 36-5. - Marking of official vehicles.
Any motor vehicle which is used on official business by any person authorized to make arrests for traffic violations in the city shall be distinctly marked on each side and the back with the name of the agency responsible therefor, in letters not less than four inches in height.
(Code 1978, § 22-6; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-5; Ord. of 4-25-2006)
Sec. 36-6. - Coasters, bicycles, roller skates, etc.; use of street and sidewalks restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any person upon a skateboard, roller-skates, coaster, toy vehicle, or similar device to be on any street or sidewalk. No person shall operate a bicycle on a sidewalk in the town center district.
(Code 1978, § 22-8; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-6; Ord. No. 12082003.01 , 12-8-2003)
Secs. 36-7—36-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - STOPPING, STANDING AND PARKING[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Authority to regulate stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(1).

Sec. 36-31. - Prohibited during certain hours in designated places.
(a)
The operator of a vehicle shall not leave such vehicle parked continuously between the hours of 1:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. upon any of the paved streets of the city, nor parked continuously in the same place on any city street for longer than 48 hours.
(b)
The city manager is authorized to prohibit parking on any portion of a paved street when it is deemed necessary or advisable and shall designate the same by painting the curb yellow.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park and leave standing any motor vehicle for any time whatsoever longer than shall be necessary to load or unload the contents thereof upon any space within the city known, designated or used as an alley.
(Code 1978, § 22-20; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-31)
Sec. 36-32. - Loading or unloading, location restricted.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to stop, stand or park such vehicle for a period of time longer than is necessary for the actual loading or unloading of passengers in any place marked as a passenger zone.
(Code 1978, § 22-21; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-32)
Sec. 36-33. - Parking where prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an operator of a motor vehicle or motorized cart (as defined in section 36-178) to park in the following areas:
(1)
No person shall park, stand where signs prohibit parking.
(2)
No person shall park in a fire lane or cause a fire lane to be obstructed.
(3)
No person shall park in a safety lane or next to a curb which is painted yellow.
(4)
No person shall park on any portions or obstruct a sidewalk.
(b)
Vehicles in violations of this section, left unattended may be towed at owners' expense.
Secs. 36-34—36-54. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - COMMERCIAL AND INDUSTRIAL VEHICLES

Sec. 36-55. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial/industrial vehicles means those vehicles weighing in excess of 10,000 pounds, loaded or unloaded and/or utilizing six wheels or more, engaged in the pickup, delivery or transfer of commercial and/or industrial merchandise, products, parts, materials, goods or services.
Industrial vehicle means any vehicle, motor-powered or non-motor-powered, utilized for the purpose of constructing, excavating, leveling, compacting, hauling, or other similar type uses, either wheeled or tracked, self-propelled or on a trailer. The combined weight of both vehicle and trailer exceeds 10,000 pounds and/or utilizes six wheels or more.
Manufactured homes, trailers, andmodular homes means those housing units utilized for living, business, sales or other similar uses and are non-motorized and must be towed, trailed or moved by a motorized vehicle of any type in excess of 10,000 pounds gross vehicle weight (GVW) and/or utilizing six wheels or more.
Operator means any person operating a commercial and/or industrial vehicle upon the public streets of the city.
Semi-trailers means those non-motorized units utilized for the purpose of transporting, hauling, delivering, etc., any merchandise, equipment, products, parts, materials, goods or services or other similar uses and must be towed, trailed or moved by a motorized vehicle of any type in excess of 10,000 pounds gross vehicle weight and/or utilizing six wheels or more.
(Code 1978, § 22-31; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-56; Ord. No. 10-12.1-92, § 101, 10-12-1992; Ord. No. 08242015(A), 8-24-2015)
Sec. 36-56. - City streets restricted for use.
All streets, roads, avenues, courts, drives, circles, lanes, etc., within the city are hereby prohibited for use of vehicles, trailers, etc., unless so designated by the state or the city as a truck route, truck bypass or some similar designation indicating availability of use by such vehicles, trailers, etc. Such truck routes shall be as identified and posted in the traffic schedule.
(Code 1978, §§ 22-32, 22-35; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-57; Ord. No. 10-12.1-92, § 201.1, 10-12-1992; Ord. No. 08242015(A), 8-24-2015)
Sec. 36-57. - Exceptions to truck route use.
Those owners or operators of vehicles, trucks, trailers, etc., and owning, leasing or operating a place of business within the city requiring the use of vehicles having over six wheels may utilize those streets determined to be the most direct route to a designated truck route, truck bypass, etc., or from a designated truck route, truck bypass, etc., to a predetermined business or address within the city; exceptions shall include all vehicles, trucks, etc., owned and operated by a federal, state, county or city government or agencies thereof. Additionally, all trucks delivering ready mix concrete and not exceeding six wheels shall be an exception.
(Code 1978, § 22-33; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-58; Ord. No. 10-12.1-92, § 201.2, 10-12-1992; Ord. No. 10-28.02-96, § 1, 10-28-1996; Ord. No. 08242015(A) , 8-24-2015)
Sec. 36-58. - Vehicles, trucks, trailers, etc., passing through the city; restrictions.
Those vehicles, trucks, trailers, etc., having no point of origin or destination within the city are hereby required to utilize only those routes designated by the state or the city as a truck route, truck bypass, etc., while traveling within the corporation limits of the city. The use of undesignated streets, roads, avenues, etc., by such vehicles, etc., shall subject the operator and/or owner thereof to the penalties defined in this article.
(Code 1978, § 22-34; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-59; Ord. No. 10-12.1-92, § 201.3, 10-12-1992; Ord. No. 08242015(A) , 8-24-2015)
Sec. 36-59. - Regulating commercial truck traffic in residential subdivisions.
(a)
Definition of commercial trucks. The following classes of vehicles are hereby declared to be commercial trucks:
Bobtail means a truck tractor without a trailer.
Dump trucks, earth movers, crane trucks, and other heavy equipment when such vehicle or equipment is not necessary for the construction, renovation, or maintenance of the road or structures within the subdivision.
Pole trailer means every vehicle without motive power designed to be drawn by another vehicle and attached to the towing vehicle by means of a reach or pole, or by being boomed or otherwise secured to the towing vehicle and ordinarily used for transporting long or irregular shaped loads, such as poles, pipes, or structural members capable, generally, of sustaining themselves as beams between the supporting connections.
Semi-trailer means every vehicle with or without motive power, other than a pole trailer, designed for carrying persons or property and for being drawn by a motor vehicle and so constructed that some part of its weight and that of its load rests upon or is carried by another vehicle.
Tractor means any self-propelled vehicle designed for use as a traveling power plant or for drawing other vehicles, but having no provision for carrying loads independently.
Truck means every motor vehicle designed, used, or maintained primarily for the transportation of property; except that class of vehicles known as pickup trucks shall not constitute commercial trucks unless they are towing a pole trailer or a semi-trailer.
Truck tractor means every motor vehicle designed and used primarily for drawing other vehicles and not so constructed as to carry a load other than a part of the vehicle and load so drawn.
(b)
Operation prohibited. The operation or parking of a commercial truck or other vehicle, as defined in subsection (a) of this section, regardless of whether such trailer or semi-trailer is attached to another vehicle, upon the residential streets of the city is hereby prohibited; provided, however, commercial trucks, while being used in making deliveries or taking on cargo for transportation from within a residential subdivision, shall not be prohibited and/or those vehicles meeting the requirements of subsection (c) of this section shall not be prohibited.
(c)
Owner/operators. Owners/operators that live within the city may park their bobtail trucks on their property, provided they can park it completely off of the roadway and/or right-of-way of the road.
(1)
Owners/operators that park their bobtail trucks on their property will do so without any damage to the roadway, sidewalks, curbing, gutters or any other city property. Owners/operators will be liable for any damage to city property caused by the operation of their vehicle under this section.
(2)
Owners/operators that park their bobtail trucks on their property within the city shall not perform any maintenance on the vehicle other than the washing of the vehicle.
(3)
Owners/operators of vehicles located within the city shall not allow their vehicles to run while parked upon their property, nor shall they utilize any radio system to create noise while parked in any residential area of the city.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-60; Ord. No. 08242015(A), 8-24-2015)
Sec. 36-60. - Penalties for violation.
Any person or operator found guilty of violation of the terms and conditions of this article shall be subject to punishment in the discretion of a court of competent jurisdiction by a fine of not more than $1,000.00 and/or imprisonment for not more than 180 days. Each incident shall be construed to be a separate violation, plus any cost of repair and/or replacement of any public property damaged and/or destroyed by the violator while acting in violation of the terms and conditions of this article.
(Code 1978, § 22-36; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-61; Ord. No. 10-12.1-92, § 505, 10-12-1992; Ord. No. 08242015(A) , 8-24-2015)
Sec. 36-61. - Enforcement.
(a)
The city clerk shall keep and maintain accurate maps setting out truck routes and streets upon which truck traffic is permitted; the maps shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk and shall be available to the public.
(b)
The city engineer shall cause those streets upon which truck traffic is prohibited to be clearly sign-posted to give notice that this article is in effect.
(c)
The chief of police shall have the authority to require any person driving or in control of any commercial vehicle not proceeding over a truck route or street over which truck traffic is permitted to show proof they fall within the exceptions or a citation may be issued for violation.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-62; Ord. No. 08242015(A) , 8-24-2015)
Secs. 36-62—36-80. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - TRAFFIC SCHEDULES

Sec. 36-81. - Definition.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Traffic schedule means the official list or schedule of the regulations compiled by the city and maintained in the city clerk's office for the following purposes:
(1)
Designation of the location of traffic control devices.
(2)
Designation of areas or zones where regulations are applied to parking, loading, bus stops or taxicab stands.
(3)
Establishment of speed limits.
(4)
Restriction or regulation of traffic at certain times on certain streets or to certain types, weights or sizes of vehicles.
(5)
Designation of the location of through streets, stop intersections, yield-right-of-way intersections, waiting lanes, one-way streets or truck traffic routes.
(6)
Establishment of regulations upon vehicle turns at designated locations.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-81)
Sec. 36-82. - Speed zones.
Speed limits are hereby established for certain streets and portions of streets. The location of such streets and portions thereof shall be as established and posted in the traffic schedule.
(Code 1978, § 22-10; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-82)
State Law reference— Authorization to alter or establish speed limits, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-371(a)(10), 40-6-183.
Sec. 36-83. - Stop streets.
Stop signs shall be erected at certain intersections as established and posted in the traffic schedule. Such signs shall require the driver of any vehicle to stop before entering the intersection. Unless otherwise specifically provided, stop signs shall not be erected on any such intersecting streets at which a traffic control signal light, flashing caution light or yield right-of-way sign may be placed and maintained.
(Code 1978, § 22-15; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-83)
State Law reference— Stop signs and yield signs, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-72.
Sec. 36-84. - One-way streets.
Certain streets or portions of streets as designated and posted in the traffic schedule are hereby designated as one-way streets, and travel along or upon the designated streets shall be in the direction indicated.
(Code 1978, § 22-19; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-84)
State Law reference— Power of local authorities to designate particular roadways for use by traffic moving in one direction, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(4); one-way roadways, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-47.
Sec. 36-85. - Parking prohibited at all times.
The stopping, standing or parking of vehicles at any time is prohibited on certain streets as posted and established in the traffic schedule.
(Code 1978, § 22-19; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-85)
State Law reference— Power of local authorities to regulate or prohibit stopping, standing or parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(1).
Sec. 36-86. - Two-hour parking.
(a)
The parking of motor vehicles, motorcycles, trailers, bicycles, carts or other like means of transport of any nature whatsoever shall be prohibited for a period exceeding two consecutive hours on the streets or portions of streets as posted and established in the traffic schedule, and on any other streets of the city directed to be posted for such restricted parking by the installation of two-hour parking signs by the board of commissioners.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, allow or permit any vehicle, motorcycle, trailer, bicycle, cart, etc., motorized or otherwise and registered or unregistered in the name of or operated by such person to be parked more than two consecutive hours in any parking space designated on the above-named streets or in any parking spaces designated by order of the board of commissioners to be posted.
(c)
The minimum distance required between the installation of two-hour parking signs shall be 300 feet, but may be less in the discretion of the police chief of the city.
(d)
The regulations embodied in this section governing two-hour parking shall be effective from Monday through Friday of each week from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., and on Saturdays from 9:00 a.m. to 12:00 noon, city-observed holidays excepted.
(e)
The penalty for violation of this section shall be as set forth in section 1-11.
(Code 1978, § 22-22; Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-86)
State Law reference— Power of local authorities to regulate or prohibit stopping, standing or parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(1).
Sec. 36-87. - School zone hours.
School zone hours are effective:
(1)
In the morning, from 45 minutes prior to commencement time to 15 minutes after commencement time—School days only.
(2)
In the afternoon from 15 minutes prior to dismissal time to 45 minutes after dismissal time—School days only.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-87; Ord. No. 3102008 , §§ 1.01, 1.02, 3-10-2008)

ARTICLE VI. - WRECKER AND TOWING SERVICES[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Removal of junked motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-4; flashing or revolving lights on vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-8-92; local ordinances, etc., relating to wrecker services, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10.1.

Sec. 36-139. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Inoperable motor vehicle means any motor vehicle, as defined in this section, which is incapable of being moved under its own power without additional work or repairs being completed to the motor vehicle, or without any additional components being added to or placed upon said motor vehicle.
Junked motor vehicle means any motor vehicle, as defined in this section, which does not have lawfully affixed thereto both an unexpired license plate and a current license decal, or the condition of such motor vehicle is in a state of disrepair, wrecked, dismantled, partially dismantled, inoperative, abandoned or discarded. The absence of a current car tag or decal from any vehicle shall be presumptive evidence that such vehicle is a junked motor vehicle.
Motor vehicle means any vehicle which is or is intended to be self-propelled and designed to travel on the ground and shall include, but shall not be limited to, automobiles, buses, motorbikes, motorcycles, motor scooters, trucks, tractors, go-carts, golf carts, four-wheelers, campers and trailers, as well as any part or parts thereof.
Passenger cars, light truck and similar vehicles means any vehicle weight up to 10,000 pounds of gross vehicle weight ("GVW").
Tow means towing, hauling and removing vehicles by the use of a wrecker.
Unlicensed vehicle means any car, truck van, motor home, camper, trailer, motorcycle or other vehicle not displaying a proper, valid/current proof of licensing from the state of license plate issuance or any car, truck, van, motor home, camper, trailer, motorcycle or other vehicle that is not bearing a valid/current license plate.
Vehicle to be towed unless otherwise indicated, a vehicle to be towed shall be:
(1)
A vehicle damaged to such an extent that it is unable to be moved safely under its own power;
(2)
A vehicle abandoned by its owner, driver, or other person;
(3)
A vehicle impounded or under the control of a city police officer or other lawfully empowered officer of the city or state;
(4)
A vehicle, the owner or operator or person in possession of which is under arrest or in the custody of the city police or other lawfully empowered officer of the city or state;
(5)
A vehicle, the owner or operator or person in possession of which is not in physical or mental condition to operate a vehicle;
(6)
Any junked motor vehicle, inoperable motor vehicle or unlicensed vehicle which has been declared as a public nuisance.
Wrecker means an automotive vehicle with hoisting apparatus and equipment for towing wrecked, disabled or abandoned automobiles or other vehicles. The term "wrecker" also includes any vehicle otherwise equipped and used for the purpose of towing wrecked, disabled or abandoned automobiles or other vehicles. The term "wrecker" includes "rollback" or "slide back" wreckers.
Wrecker service means any entity, corporation, partnership or individual person, either as principal, agent or employee, in the business of removing, towing and/or storing wrecked, disabled or abandoned automobiles or other vehicles by the use of a wrecker or other towing device within the city.
Wrecker service company means any person furnishing wrecker service within the city.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-131; Ord. No. 12142015, § 1, 12-14-2015 ; Ord. No. 11262018, § 86-131, 11-26-2018)
Sec. 36-140. - Conditions prerequisite to furnishing towing service at request of city.
Unless the person needing wrecker services specifically requests that a wrecker service unauthorized under this article be called, all persons engaged in the wrecker service business within the city shall not be called upon by police officers or other authorized employees of the city to furnish the service of towing any vehicle upon the streets of the city, unless the person engaged in furnishing the wrecker service shall have complied with the following conditions:
(1)
The wrecker service shall be available 24 hours a day, seven days a week, to provide the service.
(2)
The wrecker service shall have qualified licensed employees, skilled and knowledgeable in performing their duties, and available at all times to avoid delay.
(3)
The wrecker service shall operate at least an 8,000-pound dual cable winch or rollback wrecker with wheel lifts. The wrecker shall:
a.
Be in good operating condition, meeting all requirements of state law, and be equipped with a flashing light mounted on top of the tow car, or on top of the crane or hoist, which light will be visible for a distance of 1,000 feet, and mounted in such a manner that it can be seen both by approaching and rear traffic. To have flashing lights the wrecker company must have a permit from the state and have the sticker placed on the windshield of the wrecker.
b.
Be equipped with sufficient red flares, lanterns or warning lights and reflectors to be able to mark the scene of a wreck on both sides of the same, so as to warn all traffic on the highway of the emergency existing while the wreckers are in operation.
c.
Carry brooms, shovels, oil dry, sand, and fire extinguishers suitable for extinguishing fires in wrecked vehicles, and for cleaning up the debris, fluids and glass from the area where the wreck has occurred. The fire extinguisher shall be of a type capable of extinguishing a fire of flammable liquid.
(4)
Wrecker services placed on the call list to receive large wrecker calls shall have available heavy duty wreckers having a 26,001-pound minimum gross weight rating with a 20-ton winch, or an equivalent vehicle having the same minimum capabilities for moving vehicles, including large trucks, buses and loaded and unloaded tractor trailers.
(5)
The wrecker service shall have attached and displayed in a conspicuous place, on both the right and left sides of the wrecker, the name of the towing service or company, business address and business phone number. Magnetic or removable decals or signs do not satisfy the requirements of this subsection.
(6)
The wrecker service shall maintain in the wrecker service's name, at a minimum, the following insurance policies:
a.
An automobile liability insurance policy having a combined single limit of not less than minimum amounts set by state law;
b.
A comprehensive general liability policy providing bodily injury coverage limits of not less than minimum amounts set by state law per occurrence and property damage coverage limits of not less than minimum amounts set by state law per person and not less than minimum amounts set by state law annual aggregate;
c.
Cargo and garage keepers' liability insurance with a limit of not less than minimum amounts set by state law; and
d.
Workers' compensation insurance meeting the minimum coverage mandated by current state law.
If required by current state law, said insurance policies shall name the city as an additional insured under the policy, and shall provide for written notification at least 15 days prior to any modification, expiration, or cancellation of the policy. The wrecker service shall provide to the chief of police a full copy of the insurance policy no later than five days from execution of agreement or extension thereof or any modification or renewal of the policy. If a wrecker company is exempt from the requirement of having workers' compensation insurance based on state law, then the wrecker company must provide a notarized affidavit which states they are exempt and which outlines the reason for being exempt.
(7)
The wrecker service shall provide a storage area located within the county for the storage and safekeeping of vehicles towed under this article, but must maintain a 30-minute response time. The area shall be enclosed with a six-foot fence. A minimum of five feet will be constructed of solid wall made up of brick, cinder blocks, concrete, or a substantial chainlink fence. The remainder can be constructed with three strands of barbed wire. No wood fencing, mesh fencing or any other type fencing will be acceptable. The six feet will be measured from the top of the fence to the ground and measured six feet at all points. All fences must enclose the entire area where vehicles will be stored, and have a capacity for a minimum of ten cars. The fencing will have a gate which can be locked for entry of towed vehicles and the storage area should have sufficient lighting to light up the entire area vehicles are stored. Wrecker storage lots will be inspected annually by an official from the city police department.
(8)
The wrecker service shall employ operating personnel of wrecker units that shall be technically qualified and physically capable of responding immediately to all calls received.
(9)
The wrecker service must maintain registration with the state public service commission and possess all state-required permits. The wrecker service must also meet the state requirements through the state department of public safety and maintain Georgia Intrastate Motor Carrier (GIMC) Registration. Failure to have proper registration and meet state requirements will result in dismissal from the wrecker list or denial of application during enrollment period.
(10)
Each year wrecker companies will be required to provide a copy of the state DOT record of annual inspection with a copy attached to application and shows wrecker being in compliance for the wrecker rotation period being applied for.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-132; Ord. No. 12142015, § 2, 12-14-2015 ; Ord. No. 11262018, § 86-132, 11-26-2018)
Sec. 36-141. - Responsibility of wrecker service companies.
(a)
Wrecker service companies shall maintain a daily list of vehicles towed and/or stored for police-initiated towing and storage services. The list shall contain details as to the make, year, model, color, vehicle identification number, license plate number, name of owner, and the purpose of removing and/or storing the vehicle. The records shall be retained for a period of 12 months and shall be made available to the police department upon request during normal business hours, Monday through Friday (8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.).
(b)
Wrecker service companies shall be responsible to the vehicle owner for all damages done during towing and storage of any vehicles entrusted to them under this article.
(c)
It shall be the duty of every driver of a wrecker service vehicle to tow vehicles in a professional manner and to deal with the public in a professional and courteous manner.
(d)
Reasonable care shall be taken by wrecker service personnel to ensure no further damage will occur to the vehicle towed, either through carelessness or from natural elements, and the wrecker service shall use the proper equipment necessary (e.g., dollies, wheel lifts) to prevent further damage to the vehicles towed.
(e)
Wrecker service companies shall cooperate with all duly authorized law enforcement officers in the protection of any evidence or in any investigation arising out of a wreck or seizure of a vehicle by any officials.
(f)
Wrecker service companies shall have a response time of 30 minutes. Response time is the time elapsed from the time the 911 center dispatches the wrecker service until the time that the wrecker actually arrives at the location of the vehicle that is to be towed.
(g)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-276, it shall be the responsibility of the wrecker service company, prior to leaving the scene of any collision, to clean the pavement and right-of-way of all debris of the collision, including the removal of all glass from paved roadways. In the event that engine fluids have spilled on the roadway, the wrecker service shall provide and spread only that amount of oil dry material on the affected area in order to render the roadway safe for vehicular traffic.
(h)
A wrecker service company may release any vehicle to the registered owner, unless a hold has been placed on the vehicle by the arresting officer, or a court order prohibits the release. The wrecker service must verify and obtain proof of insurance of the vehicle being released.
(i)
Wrecker service companies shall not go to any accident scene unless:
(1)
Called by the police department;
(2)
Called by the vehicle owner/operator; or
(3)
There exists an extreme emergency immediately endangering life or property.
(j)
Wrecker service companies shall provide information to the police department under the following circumstances:
(1)
Whenever any wrecked or abandoned vehicle is removed without the request, direction or participation of the police department, the wrecker service performing the removal shall, if the owner is unknown, and within 72 hours of such removal, furnish to the 911 Center and police department the following information with respect to such vehicle: tag number and description, including year, model, make, color and vehicle identification number.
(2)
Upon receipt of such information, the police department shall immediately take steps to ascertain the identity of the vehicle owner and notify them of the time and place of removal and of the present whereabouts of the vehicle.
(3)
Nothing in this article shall relieve any wrecker service of any of the requirements imposed by virtue of O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1 et seq., or by virtue of any other law with respect to the duties, among others, to make diligent inquiries as to the ownership of vehicles and notification to owners.
(k)
Wrecker service companies performing non-consensual towing must abide by all state laws. If a wrecker service company is found to be in violation of the state non-consensual towing laws, the wrecker service company will lose its ability to be on the city's wrecker rotation and will be removed from the rotation list until the next application period (assuming all state issues have been resolved).
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-133; Ord. No. 12142015, § 3, 12-14-2015 ; Ord. No. 11262018, § 86-133, 11-26-2018)
Sec. 36-142. - Regulation of fees for services rendered.
(a)
Fees for on-call services rendered under this article shall not exceed the schedule of fees for services rendered and as agreed upon by all parties. The agreed upon fees allowed will be spelled out in a contract. The wrecker company owner will sign said agreement and have it notarized. Any violation of the agreement will be cause for taking the wrecker company off the wrecker rotation.
(b)
The wrecker service shall post all towing fees, reflecting the prices to be charged to the public. Fees shall be posted in open view to the public in the wrecker service's lobby. In addition, the wrecker service shall deliver to each owner or representative of every towed vehicle present at the scene of the tow a pre-printed disclosure form approved by the city police department that clearly provides:
(1)
The fees to be charged by the wrecker service;
(2)
Location of storage lots;
(3)
Times of vehicle release; and
(4)
Phone numbers of the wrecker service.
(c)
The wrecker service shall furnish the person who pays for wrecker services a written receipt on a pre-printed form approved by the city police department which contains the following information:
(1)
An itemization of all charges; and
(2)
The name of the person on the scene who recovered the vehicle.
(d)
The schedule of fees for on-call services rendered may only be modified by approval of the city police department and would cause for a new agreement to be put in place with appropriate signatures and notarization.
(e)
The maximum wrecker companies allowed on the wrecker rotation list will be 15. First consideration will be given to those wrecker businesses which have their business within the city limits. Next consideration will be given based on first-come first-served and those who are on the present wrecker rotation listing. Once the 15 wrecker companies are chosen, the rotation list will be final. If there are less than 15 wrecker companies who qualify for placement on the wrecker rotation list, the list will be final until the next enrollment period and rotation consideration. No wrecker companies will be added after the list is determined and a new wrecker rotation list begins. The wrecker rotation will be good from 12:00 midnight January 1 to 11:59 p.m. on December 31 each year.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-134; Ord. No. 12142015, § 4, 12-14-2015 ; Ord. No. 11262018, § 86-134, 11-26-2018)
Sec. 36-143. - Responsibility for payment.
All charges and fees for towing and storage of the vehicle towed shall be at the expense of the owner of the vehicle, and against the vehicle itself, as provided by state law.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-137; Ord. No. 12142015, § 7, 12-14-2015 ; Ord. No. 11262018, § 86-136, 11-26-2018)
Sec. 36-144. - Qualification of wrecker service companies.
(a)
Any wrecker service company desiring to be qualified to be placed on the rotating schedule for use by the city, under the conditions set out herein, shall file an application starting October 1 and before November 30 of the calendar year with the chief of police. Each company shall pay an annual regulatory fee for the cost of inspecting the wrecker. If a wrecker company has been found in violation of this article and is taken off the wrecker rotation, they will not be refunded any portion of any fees.
(b)
After the creation of the official on-call schedule, all wrecker services seeking to be placed on the rotating schedule shall, if qualified, be placed on the next calendar year, following receipt of an acceptable application before the November 30 deadline. If November 30 falls on a weekend, then the deadline will be 5:00 p.m. on the Friday before.
(c)
A continuing responsibility for eligibility shall include an adequate and reasonable response time to calls made to the scheduled wrecker service company to assure efficient service on a 24-hour basis. Companies failing to remain qualified will be notified of the problem, in writing, and shall be given a 15-day period to correct the condition. The wrecker service may be temporarily suspended from service and removed from the approved list during this period. If the company fails to correct the condition within the 15-day period, the company shall be removed from the wrecker service schedule. Appeals to decisions of the police department may be filed in writing with the city manager.
(d)
Wrecker service companies desiring to be qualified on the rotating schedule shall agree that the fee schedule published by the city shall apply to all calls initiated by a police officer or other authorized official to which the wrecker service responds while on call, to include wrecker services on rotation called by an officer for the tow of a wrecked vehicle.
(e)
Any wrecker service that has an owner or operator that has been convicted of a felony within five years will not be on the city's rotating schedule for police-initiated tows. All wrecker services desiring to be placed on or to remain on said rotating schedule agree to provide the city police department, at the time of seeking qualification under this article and thereafter, annually, with a release executed by all owners of each wrecker service and all operators for each wrecker service, which authorizes the city police department to conduct a criminal records search of said individuals. All wrecker services desiring to qualify or to remain qualified shall also provide a release to the city police department with respect to all new owners or operators of the wrecker service.
(f)
The authorized fee schedule shall be posted in a conspicuous place at the qualified wrecker service company's place of business, and each wrecker vehicle of the company shall carry a copy of the schedule, for the purpose of furnishing the same for vehicle owners or operators.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-138; Ord. No. 12142015, § 8, 12-14-2015 ; Ord. No. 11262018, § 86-137, 11-26-2018)

(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-139; Ord. No. 12142015, § 9, 12-14-2015 ; Ord. No. 11262018, § 86-138, 11-26-2018)

ARTICLE VII. - MOTORIZED CARTS

Sec. 36-178. - Findings; definitions.
(a)
The city commission finds that the municipal streets located within the corporate limits of the city, other than the streets hereinafter specifically excepted, are designated and constructed so as to safely permit their use by motorized carts (as hereinafter defined) in combination with regular vehicular traffic.
(b)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Authorized street means the public roadways of the city by whatever name (e.g., road, alley, avenue, highway, route, boulevard, etc.) that:
(1)
Has a posted speed limit of 35 miles per hour or less;
(2)
Provides for no more than one lane of vehicular traffic per direction;
(3)
Is not designated as part of either the state or federal highway system;
(4)
Has not been designated as an unauthorized street by ordinance or resolution by the city commission.
Driver means the person driving and having physical control over a motorized cart.
Motorized cart means any motor vehicle that has no less than three wheels, capable of a maximum level ground speed of less than 20 miles per hour with a maximum gross vehicle unladen or empty weight of 1,300 pounds, and is commonly referred to as golf carts. Specifically excluded from this definition are those motorized conveyances commonly referred to as ATVs (including four-wheelers, three-wheelers, mules, and gators) and mobility aids.
Owner means the person holding title to the motorized cart or who has a majority ownership interest of the motorized cart.
Parking area means those areas accessible to the public by motor vehicular traffic and which are designated for the temporary parking of motor vehicles, usually in places referred to as parking lots.
Unauthorized street means the public roadways of the city by whatever name which has been designated by the city commission by ordinance or resolution as an unauthorized street.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-141; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017)
Sec. 36-179. - Registration.
(a)
Before any motorized cart may be operated over an authorized street or parking area of the city it shall be registered with and inspected by the city marshal's office. If the motorized cart is compliant with local and state law, then a decal shall be issued from that department signifying its registration. The registration requirements include the following specifics:
(1)
Only those persons 18 years or older may register a motorized cart.
(2)
The person registering the motorized cart is the owner of the motorized cart.
(3)
The decal received from the city marshal's office upon registration shall be attached and displayed upon the motorized cart so as to be plainly visible.
(4)
The registration application shall be made on a form supplied by the city and shall contain the following information:
a.
Name and address of owner.
b.
Model, make, name and motorized cart identification number.
c.
Current driver's license number of owner.
d.
Such other information which the city may require.
(5)
The registration application shall be accompanied by a fee of $15.00 for one year and $30.00 for three years. No registration fees will be prorated.
(6)
The registration application shall be accompanied by evidence of personal liability insurance coverage consistent with the minimum requirements of state law for operational motor vehicles.
(7)
The registration shall be effective until such time as revoked or the motorized cart is transferred to a new owner. If the registered owner transfers ownership of the motorized cart, the registered owner must notify the city marshal's office of the transfer.
(8)
It shall be the owner of the motorized carts responsibility to ensure the information on the application remains current and accurate.
(b)
The city commission may, at its discretion, waive registration requirements for city sponsored special events of a limited duration.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-142; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017; Ord. No. 12282017, § 86-142, 12-28-2017)
Sec. 36-180. - Revocation.
The registration permit may be revoked if:
(1)
The permit holder no longer has a valid driver's license.
(2)
The owner or driver of a motorized cart fails to abide by the rules and regulations of this article.
(3)
The owner or driver of a motorized cart fails to abide by the traffic laws in the use of a motorized cart on an authorized street or parking area.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-143; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017)
Sec. 36-181. - Transfer.
Upon transfer of ownership of the motorized cart to a person who intends to operate it over the authorized streets and parking areas, the new owner must register the motorized cart as outlined in section 36-179. Such registration by the new owner must occur within ten days of the transfer of ownership.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-144; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017)
Sec. 36-182. - Operation regulations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to operate a motorized cart on any street within the city that is not an authorized street.
(b)
Only those persons who hold a valid motor vehicle driver's license may drive a motorized cart on the authorized streets and parking areas of the city. The driver must have their valid license on their person while operating the motorized cart. The driver must follow all restrictions and conditions as associated with the class and type of driver's license/permit as set forth from the licensing authority.
(c)
All drivers of motorized carts shall abide by all traffic regulations applicable to vehicular traffic when using the authorized streets and parking areas of the city.
(d)
A motorized cart shall not be operated on the sidewalks at any time.
(e)
A motorized cart may be operated over those authorized streets and parking areas only during daylight hours unless such motorized cart is equipped with functional headlights, taillights and turn signals.
(f)
Every driver of a motorized cart shall be granted all the rights and shall be subject to all the rules of the road and duties applicable to the driver of any other vehicle under this chapter except as to special regulations in this article and to those provisions of this chapter which by their nature can have no application.
(g)
All motorized carts are entitled to a full use of a lane on the authorized streets and parking areas of the city and no motor vehicle shall be driven in such a manner as to deprive any motorized cart of the full use of a lane.
(h)
The driver of a motorized cart shall not overtake and pass in the same lane occupied by the vehicle being overtaken.
(i)
No driver shall operate a motorized cart between lanes of traffic or between adjacent lines of rows of vehicles.
(j)
The driver of a motorized cart operating on the authorized street may cross a multi-lane road only at a signalized intersection designated for such purpose.
(k)
The driver of a motorized cart operating on an authorized street may cross a state highway or federal route only at intersections that have been designated for such purpose by the state department of transportation.
(l)
All laws and ordinances relative to alcohol and its use, including open container laws, which apply to traffic on the streets of the city shall also apply to drivers and occupants of motorized carts.
(m)
All drivers and passengers must remain seated at all times during the operation of the motorized cart. No person may sit on the driver's lap during the operation of the motorized cart.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-145; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017; Ord. No. 08122019(2), § 86-145, 8-12-2019)
Sec. 36-183. - Equipment.
Each motorized cart shall display an amber strobe light so as to warn approaching travelers to decrease their speed because of the danger of colliding with such vehicle. Such amber strobe light shall be mounted in a manner so as to be visible under normal atmospheric conditions from a distance of 500 feet from the front and rear of such vehicle.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-146; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017)
Sec. 36-184. - Liability.
Nothing in this article shall be construed as an assumption of liability by the city for any injuries to persons or property which may result from the operation of a motorized cart or by the failure of the city to revoke said permit.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-147; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017)
Sec. 36-185. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
Children under the age of four are prohibited from riding as a passenger of a golf cart. Children four years of age to eight years of age must be properly restrained in a child passenger restraint system approved by statute. Seat belts shall be properly used for all persons, between the age of eight years of age and 17 years of age, while the motorized cart is in motion.
(b)
Off-road vehicles (ATVs, UTVs, etc.) may not operate on any public roadway at any time, or on any city sidewalk and may only operate on private property with express written permission of the owner (O.C.G.A. § 40-7-4). Off-road vehicles include, but are not limited to, any motorized vehicle designed for or capable of travel over land, water, snow, ice, marsh, or other natural terrain and not intended for use predominantly on public roads; that includes, but not limited to, low pressure tire vehicles (four wheelers, three wheelers, quad runners, air boats, etc.).
(c)
Scooter, motorized mini-bikes, etc. These small cycles typically have a 50cc or smaller engine and may not be operated on any public roadway at any time or on any sidewalks within the city.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-148; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017; Ord. No. 05142018 , 5-7-2018)
Sec. 36-186. - Penalties.
Any person who violates the terms of this article shall be punished as follows: In addition to whatever violations the driver of the motorized cart may be subject to pursuant to state law and elsewhere found within this Code and the city Charter, the registered owner of the motorized cart shall be subject to the following civil penalties:
(1)
For the first offense, a fine of not less than $50.00; and, if not timely paid, revocation of the permit.
(2)
For the second offense, a fine of not less than $100.00; and, if not timely paid, revocation of the permit.
(3)
For the third offense, the registration for the motorized cart shall be revoked. In the event the registration is revoked under this section, the motorized cart cannot thereafter be re-registered by the same owner or any family member for a period of two years. Further, such other penalties shall be applies as provided in this Code and the city Charter in the sound discretion and judgment of the presiding judge of the municipal court of the city.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-149; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017)
Sec. 36-187. - Vehicle for hire.
Under no circumstances whatsoever can a motorized cart be used as a vehicle for hire.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 86-150; Ord. No. 10232017 , 10-23-2017)
Sec. 36-188. - Expiration.
Each registration will expire on December 31, of each year. A three-year registration fee will be valid for three years and will expire on December 31 of the third calendar year. (Example: If a three-year registration fee is paid on July 1, 2018, the license will expire on December 31, 2020.
(Ord. No. 12282017, § 86-151, 12-28-2017)
