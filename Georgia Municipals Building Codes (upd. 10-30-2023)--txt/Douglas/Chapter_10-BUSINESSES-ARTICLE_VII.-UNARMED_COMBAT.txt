ARTICLE VII. - UNARMED COMBAT

Sec. 10-228. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Promoter means the person primarily responsible for organizing, promoting, and producing a professional match, contest, or exhibition and who is legally responsible for the lawful conduct of such professional match, contest, or exhibition.
Promotion of unarmed combat means the organization, promotion, production, publicizing, or arranging of, or provision of a venue for, a competition of unarmed combat by a person who receives some compensation or commercial benefit from such competition.
Unarmed combat means:
(1)
Any form of competition or exhibition between human beings or one or more human beings and one or more animals in which one or more blows are struck which may reasonably be expected to inflict injury on a human being.
(2)
Any form of wrestling which, when force is used, may reasonably be expected to inflict injury on a human being.
Wrestling means:
(1)
A staged performance of fighting and gymnastic skills and techniques by two or more human beings who are not required to use their best efforts in order to win and for which the winner may have been selected before the performance commences; or
(2)
A performance of fighting and gymnastic skills and techniques by two or more human beings.
Sec. 10-229. - Unlawful to engage, conduct, promote or allow.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in, conduct, promote or allow unarmed combat as defined in section 10-228 within the corporate limits of the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly allow anyone to engage in or promote unarmed combat on their premises, that is under their custody and control.

The Georgia High School Athletic Association;

(4)
Amateur Athletic Union;

(6)

(7)

(9)
North American Sport Karate Association;
(10)
International Sport Kick Boxing/Karate Association;
(11)
World Kick Boxing Association;
(12)
United States Kick Boxing Association;
(13)
International Sport Combat Federation;
(14)
Professional Karate Commission;
(15)
International Kick Boxing Federation; or
(16)
The local affiliate of any organization listed in this subsection.
(b)
Unarmed combat shall not include:
(1)
Professional boxing licensed in accordance with rules and regulations of the state athletic and entertainment commission;
(2)
Professional wrestling governed or authorized by an organization licensed or exempted from licensure in accordance with this article;
(3)
Amateur boxing governed or authorized by an organization listed in subsection (a) of this section;
(4)
Amateur wrestling governed or authorized by an organization listed in subsection (b) of this section;
(5)
Any competition displaying the skills of a single form of an Oriental system of unarmed combative sports or unarmed combative entertainment, including, but not limited to, kickboxing, karate, or full-contact karate, that is held pursuant to the rules of that form and governed or authorized by an organization licensed by the commission in accordance with this article;
(6)
Shidokan when the competition is governed or authorized by an organization licensed by the commission in accordance with O.C.G.A. ยง 43-4B-4;
(7)
Mixed martial arts fighting when the competition is governed or authorized by an organization licensed by the commission in accordance with O.C.G.A. ยง 43-4B-4;
(8)
Other martial arts competitions, when governed or authorized by an organization licensed by the commission in accordance with O.C.G.A. ยง 43-4B-4; or
(9)
Wrestling in a form that blows or use of force applied to a human body that is not expected to cause injury.
(c)
The provisions of this article shall not apply to any matches, contests, or exhibitions of professional wrestling or to a promoter or organization that promotes, organizes, or governs such matches, contests, or exhibitions where such promoter or organization is a corporation that, at the time of such matches, contests, or exhibitions:
(1)
Is registered under the federal Securities Exchange Act of 1934; and
(2)
Has total assets of not less than $25,000,000.00.
Sec. 10-231. - Penalties.
Violation of the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
