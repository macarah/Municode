DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 8-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Airport means the Douglas Municipal Airport.
Airport commission means the appointed representatives of the board of commissioners or its specifically authorized agents or representatives.
Fixed base operator means any person approved by the airport commission and the board of commissioners to sell aviation petroleum products and other goods and services applicable to aviation at the airport.
Motor vehicle means any motor vehicle capable of operating only on the ground.
(Code 1978, § 4-20; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-1)
Sec. 8-20. - Incorporation of federal rules and regulations.
The air traffic rules and aircraft operations regulations of the Federal Aviation Administration as in effect and all additions thereto are made a part of the airport rules and regulations in this article as fully as if set forth herein.
(Code 1978, § 4-50; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-2)
Sec. 8-21. - Motorized vehicles on airport property.
(a)
Prohibited, exceptions. With the exception of aircraft, there shall be no motorized vehicles located on property comprising the airport of the city except in areas designated and marked by the city manager or his designee.
(b)
Penalty for violation. Anyone convicted of violating the provisions of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(c)
Restitution for damages. In addition, in the event of any conviction of any person for violation of this section, the sentencing court shall have the right to require restitution in full to the city for any and all damages to property.
(Code 1978, § 4-1; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-3)
Sec. 8-22. - Fixed base operations.
The city manager shall be responsible for developing and maintaining a current contractual agreement with a qualified fixed base operation. Final approval of such fixed base operations contract shall be by the board of commissioners with recommendations through the airport commission. The fixed base operator shall report directly to the city manager or his designee.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-4)
Sec. 8-23. - Only authorized activities allowed.
No commercial aeronautical operation, commercial operation of any kind or any type of operation or activity shall be conducted on the airport unless specifically authorized by the board of commission with the advice of the airport commissioner.
(Code 1978, § 4-22; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-5)
Sec. 8-24. - Implied agreement to comply with regulations.
The use of the airport or any of its facilities in any manner shall create an obligation on the part of the user thereof to obey all the regulations herein provided and adopted by the board of commissioners and the airport commission.
(Code 1978, § 4-23; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-6)
Sec. 8-25. - Assumption of risk; indemnification.
The privilege of using the airport and its facilities shall be conditioned on the assumption of full responsibility and risk by the user thereof, and the user shall release and hold harmless and indemnify the city and county, and their officers and employees from any liability or loss resulting from such use, as well as claims of third persons using the airport. The privilege of using the airport shall be upon the further condition that any person desiring to use such airport shall furnish a policy of indemnity against personal injury and property damage in such sum as the city commission requires.
(Code 1978, § 4-24; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-7)
Sec. 8-26. - Denial of use of facilities.
Any person violating any of the airport rules and regulations may be deprived of the use of the airport facilities for such period of time as determined by the board of commission in addition to any penalty or punishment imposed by a court.
(Code 1978, § 4-25; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-8)
Sec. 8-27. - Advertising; permission required.
No signs, advertisements or circulars may be posted or distributed at the airport without permission of the board of commissioners or city manager.
(Code 1978, § 4-27; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-9)
Sec. 8-28. - Motor vehicles—Use of designated facilities.
Vehicles at the airport shall be parked in the manner prescribed by the city manager or his designee while on the airport and as indicated by posted signs.
(Code 1978, § 4-34; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-10)
Sec. 8-29. - Motor vehicles—Parking by customers of fixed base operators.
Fixed base operator customers shall park in areas designated by the city manager or his designee.
(Code 1978, § 4-35; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-11)
Sec. 8-30. - Motor vehicles—Driving on ramp.
When aircraft are parked on the ramp or apron for the purpose of loading or unloading, no vehicle shall be driven on the ramp except service trucks and vehicles to pick up passengers and luggage.
(Code 1978, § 4-36; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-12)
Sec. 8-31. - Motor vehicles—Reporting of accidents.
Every motor vehicle and/or aircraft accident on airport property involving injury or property damage shall be reported to the city manager or his designee.
(Code 1978, § 4-37; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-13)
Sec. 8-32. - Motor vehicles—Careless or illegal operation.
No person shall operate any motorized vehicle or aircraft on airport property in a careless manner or negligent manner or in disregard for the safety of others, or in excess of posted speed traffic signs. The term "motorized vehicle" means any vehicle or motorcycle that is self-propelled by an engine or electric motor.
(Code 1978, § 4-38; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-14)
Secs. 8-33—8-52. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - AIRPORT COMMISSION

Sec. 8-53. - Membership.
The city airport commission shall consist of ten members appointed by the city commission and voting upon such confirmation at the first regular meeting in September of each year such appointments are scheduled. The tenth member of the airport commission shall be the director of the department in which oversees the day to day operations of the airport.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.1; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-41)
Sec. 8-54. - Terms of office.
The terms of office of the membership of the airport commission shall be for a period of three years.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.2; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-42)
Sec. 8-55. - Vacancy.
Any vacancy in the membership of the airport commission shall be filled for the unexpired term in the same manner as original appointments to the airport commission.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.3; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-43)
Sec. 8-56. - Removal.
The mayor of the city shall have the authority, upon confirmation of the majority of the city commission, to remove any member of the airport commission for cause upon written notification to the chairperson of the airport commission and to the member so removed.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.4; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-44)
Sec. 8-57. - Compensation.
All members of the airport commission shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.5; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-45)
Sec. 8-58. - Organization.
The membership of the airport commission shall annually elect its chairperson from its membership. The chairperson shall be eligible for re-election for two successive terms. The airport commission shall, by a majority vote of the members present and voting, elect a vice-chairperson who shall act in the absence of the chairperson. The airport commission shall appoint a secretary from among its membership or who may be employed by the commission at its discretion. The city manager and finance officer shall serve as ex officio members of the airport commission with no voting power.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.6; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-46)
Sec. 8-59. - Meetings; budget; expenditures.
(a)
Minutes of all regular and special meetings of the airport commission shall be kept in writing and transmitted to the mayor and each member of the city commission annually.
(b)
The airport commission shall in April of each year submit to the city manager a budget request for funds for the operation of the municipal airport for the next succeeding fiscal year commencing on July 1 of each year.
(c)
All expenditures of the airport commission shall be in keeping with the approved budget of the airport commission. All expenditures shall follow the purchasing procedures of the city.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.7; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-47)
Sec. 8-60. - General duties.
It shall be the duty of the airport manager to administer the operation of the municipal airport in a safe and efficient manner; to direct the fixed base operator in the conduct of his responsibilities; to maintain the airport facilities in good repair; to develop and upkeep the airport facility; to ensure the safety and welfare of the users of the municipal airport; and to promulgate plans and programs for the development of the future use of the municipal airport facility and surrounding area.
(Code 1978, § 4-89.8; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-48)
Sec. 8-61. - Authority generally.
The airport commission shall at all times act as an advisory commission to the board of commissioners. In addition, the airport commission is authorized to receive donations on behalf of the city. These funds shall be turned over to the city manager or his designee.
(Code 1978, § 4-21; Code 1993, pt. II, § 14-49)
Secs. 8-62—8-76. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - PENALTY

Sec. 8-77. - Penalty.
Any person violating any section of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and punished as provided in section 1-11.
