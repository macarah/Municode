ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Purpose.
The purpose of this chapter is to establish the position of animal control and rabies officers; to establish regulations for animals in order to protect the public health and welfare; to comply with provisions of state law; to provide for enforcement and penalties; and for other purposes.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 2)
Sec. 8-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Aggressive dog means a dog that trespasses upon the property of another and that exhibits aggressive behavior toward a human being, without provocation, which behavior places a person in fear for his well-being or safety.
Animal means all domesticated dogs, cats, and ferrets, male and female, singular and plural, actually in, or intended to be in, the custody and control of an owner, but not including any wild animal or horse, goat, pig, fowl, or any livestock.
Animal control officer means any county officer, agent or employee designated by the county to perform the duties of an animal control officer pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22(c).
Animal establishment means any pet shop, grooming shop, kennel, animal exhibition or animal shelter, except veterinary medical facilities, licensed research facilities, facilities operated by government agencies, or licensed animal dealers regulated by the United States Department of Agriculture under the provisions of 7 USC 2131 et seq.
Animal shelter means a facility designated or recognized by the governing authority for the purpose of impounding or caring for animals.
Board of health means the county board of health.
Certificate of vaccination means a certificate of vaccination on a form approved by the CDC and the Georgia Department of Human Resources and signed by the veterinarian administering the vaccine.
Certificate of registration means a form issued to declare a dog as a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog.
Dangerous dog, subject to the exemptions in section 8-55, means any dog that according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation, on public or private property.
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Dog means any member of the canine family
Guard dog means any dog that has been trained to attack persons or other animals independently or upon verbal approval command and any dog that, while not so trained, is reasonably expected to perform as a guardian of the property upon and within which it is located.
Kennel means an establishment for the purpose of breeding, selling or boarding dogs or cats or engaging in the training of dogs or cats.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust who or which own, possesses, keeps, or has custody or control of an animal within the jurisdiction of the governing authority.
Potentially dangerous dog, subject to the exemptions in section 8-107 means any dog that without provocation bites a human being on public or private property.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Rabies control officer means an officer appointed by the county board of health to perform the duties of a rabies control officer.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state or local court; or records of an animal control officer.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
Vaccine means any anti-rabies vaccine approved by the state department of human resources.
Vaccinate or inoculate means the injection of a specified dose of anti-rabies vaccine by a veterinarian into an animal.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 3)
Secs. 8-3—8-22. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 8-23. - Animal control unit created.
There is created for the county an animal control unit for regulations of dangerous dog animal control and rabies, and it shall be staffed with such personnel as may from time to time be authorized by the county.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 12(1))
Sec. 8-24. - Joint animal control services authorized.
The county shall be authorized to enter into an agreement with one or more other governing authorities to provide joint animal control services pursuant to the provisions of this article as provided by state law. Likewise, the county shall be authorized to enter into an agreement with nongovernmental entities to provide joint animal control services pursuant to the provisions of this article as provided by state law.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 12(1))
Sec. 8-25. - Authority of animal control officer; cooperation of law enforcement agencies.
An animal control officer is authorized to make whatever inquiry is deemed necessary to ensure compliance with the provisions of this article. All law enforcement agencies of the county shall cooperate with the animal control officers in enforcing the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 12(2))
Sec. 8-26. - Interfering with animal control unit and officers prohibited.
It shall be unlawful:
(1)
To interfere with or fail to cooperate with the animal control officer or other officer acting pursuant to this chapter;
(2)
To take or attempt to take any animal from a county vehicle used to transport an animal pursuant to this chapter; or
(3)
To take or attempt to take any animal from the animal control shelter or other impounding area used by the animal control unit for the purposes of this chapter without lawful authority.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 12(3))
Sec. 8-27. - Authority for destruction of animal posing immediate threat.
The animal control officer may destroy any animal which presents a threat of immediate harm to public health or safety or other innocent animals and which cannot be taken for impoundment without threat of serious harm to the animal control officer or his agents. The destruction shall be in the most humane method available to the officer on scene.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 12(4))
Sec. 8-28. - Issuance of citations.
Upon information known to, or a complaint lodged with, any officer or member of the animal control unit, or a law enforcement agency employee of the county that any owner or possessor of an animal is in violation of this chapter, the animal control officer or rabies control officer or any law enforcement officer may, after investigation, issue a summons requiring the owner or possessor of such animal to appear before the judge of the magistrate's court of the county on a day and time certain to stand trial for the violation.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 13)
Secs. 8-29—8-59. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - ANIMAL CARE AND CONTROL

Sec. 8-60. - Duty of owner to exercise care.
(a)
It shall be the duty of every owner or custodian of an animal or livestock within the county to exercise reasonable care and take all necessary steps and precautions to protect other people and property from injuries and damage that might result from the animal's behavior, regardless of whether such behavior is motivated by mischievousness, playfulness or ferocity. Every animal shall be restrained or controlled so as to prevent it from chasing vehicles or chasing menacing or attacking persons or other animals. Citation under this section shall not replace or be a substitute for enforcement of the provisions of this chapter dealing with dangerous or potentially dangerous or vicious animals, and an owner may be subject to enforcement under both sections.
(b)
Violations of this section shall be enforceable in the county's magistrate court and shall be subject to penalties as provided in section 1-11.
Sec. 8-61. - Removal of tracking or identification devices prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove any tracking collar or identification from any animal other than the person's own animal.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5)
Secs. 8-62—8-80. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - RABIES MANAGEMENT

Sec. 8-81. - Vaccination required.
Every owner of a dog, cat, or ferret shall cause the dog, cat, or ferret to be vaccinated against rabies, the first or primary vaccination to occur at no later than three months of age, followed by revaccination on an annual or triennial, in accordance with Compendium of Animal Rabies Prevention and Control or as described on the individual vaccine label. If a previously vaccinated animal is overdue for a booster, it should be revaccinated with a single dose of vaccine. Immediately following the booster, the animal is considered currently vaccinated and should be placed on a schedule depending on the labeled duration of the vaccine used.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5)
Sec. 8-82. - Certificates of vaccination.
Evidence of vaccination shall consist of a certificate of vaccination, furnished by the veterinarian administering the vaccine. The owner shall retain a copy of the certificate.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5)
Sec. 8-83. - Rabies clinics.
Any animal clinic within the county that provides rabies vaccination services must notify the county board of health of its intent to operate a rabies clinic not less than 15 days prior to commencing such operation. In emergencies or during quarantine the 15 days notice prior to the operation of a clinic shall not apply. Rabies vaccinations must be performed by a licensed veterinarian. Payment for such services shall be derived from vaccination fees charged to and paid by the animal owner or caretaker.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5(1))
Sec. 8-84. - Rabies control officer; appointment and duties.
A rabies control officer will be appointed by the county board of health. The rabies control officer shall:
(1)
Investigate and maintain a record of animal bites in the county.
(2)
Ensure proper confinement of an animal involved in a bite or, if the animal is dead or killed, preparation and submission of the head for laboratory examination.
(3)
Ensure proper disposition of animals exposed to known rabid animals.
(4)
Assist in county-sponsored rabies vaccination clinics.
(5)
Perform such other duties as provided in this chapter or from time to time by the sole commissioner or the county board of health.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5(2))
Sec. 8-85. - Confinement of certain animals required.
(a)
A dog or other animal, regardless of whether vaccinated, that has bitten a person or other animal shall be confined within the county for a period of ten days following the date of the bite except where conditions for confinement in an area outside the limits of the county meet the confinement requirements of this article, county board of health rules and regulations, and state law.
(b)
A dog or other animal, regardless of whether vaccinated, having signs suggestive of rabies shall be confined and in isolation until its death or until its freedom from suspicion of having rabies is established.
(c)
A dog or animal not vaccinated and bitten by a known rabid animal shall be immediately destroyed or, if the owner is unwilling to destroy the exposed animal, placed in strict isolation in an approved kennel for not less than six months at the owner's expense.
(d)
Any dog or other animal, the rabies vaccination of which is current as evidenced by the certificate of vaccination, that is bitten by a known rabid animal, may be revaccinated, then confined in an approved manner for not less than 30 days and thereafter released if no signs of rabies are present.
(e)
Every dog or other animal, regardless of whether vaccinated, confined pursuant to this section shall be kept confined and isolated during the entire quarantine period.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5(3.a.1—5))
Sec. 8-86. - Confinement area or facility standards.
The rabies control officer shall approve all confinement areas or facilities, ensuring that the confinement area is safe and secure, that it provides for isolation of the animal from all other animals and humans and that the area is clean and humane, providing adequate food, water, and exercise, and appropriate temperature and shelter from the elements to the animal.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5)
Sec. 8-87. - Duty to report animal bites and suspected rabies infections.
(a)
The owner or custodian of any animal having signs suggestive of rabies shall confine the animal and immediately notify the health board and the rabies control officer, reporting any information regarding persons bitten or attacked by said animal.
(b)
Any person bitten by an animal reasonably suspected of being rabid shall immediately notify the health board and the rabies control officer.
(c)
Any person within the jurisdiction of the county who is aware of any animal bite inflicted upon a human by any animal shall immediately report the bite to the animal control officer, the rabies control officer, or a public health official pursuant to state law.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5)
Sec. 8-88. - Redemption of animals impounded for lack of proof of vaccination.
Any vaccinated dog impounded because of a lack of proof of vaccination may be reclaimed by its owner by furnishing proof of rabies vaccination. Any unvaccinated dog or other animal impounded because of lack of proof of vaccination may be reclaimed by its owner by giving satisfactory assurances of compliance with the rabies vaccination requirement of this article within 72 hours of release.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5(3.a.6))
Sec. 8-89. - Disposition of unredeemed animals.
Any animal not redeemed within 72 hours after the owner is notified of clearance for release from quarantine or other confinement may be disposed of by euthanasia. Animals confined pursuant to this article with no registration or known owner, may be destroyed after ten days. The owner of the hospital or shelter is made an agent of the county for this purpose, and his actions in this regard are declared to be for a governmental purpose.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 7)

ARTICLE V. - DANGEROUS DOGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 8-107. - Exemptions from dangerous and potentially dangerous classification.
The terms "dangerous dog" and "potentially dangerous dog," as defined in this chapter, shall not include:
(1)
A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties; and
(2)
A dog who inflicts an injury if the injury was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort, or was tormenting, abusing, or assaulting the dog or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused, or assaulted the dog or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 4)
Sec. 8-108. - Jurisdiction of probate court; investigation of aggressive dog behavior.
If a dog acts as an aggressive dog, as defined in this chapter, that conduct may be referred to the probate judge for investigation by law enforcement.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 5)
Secs. 8-109—8-129. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CLASSIFICATION PROCEDURE

Sec. 8-130. - Investigations.
The animal control officer, upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the jurisdiction of the county from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer or county board of health, shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to determine the validity of such report and whether the dog should be classified as a dangerous dog, potentially dangerous dog or reclassified as a dangerous dog if previously classified as a potentially dangerous dog.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 8(1))
Sec. 8-131. - Classification as dangerous or potentially dangerous; notice to owner.
Should the animal control officer determine that a dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, the animal control officer shall classify such dog accordingly. The animal control officer shall then notify the dog's owner of such classification.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 8(2))
Sec. 8-132. - Form and contents of notice to owner.
The notice to the owner shall:
(1)
Be in writing and mailed by certified mail or statutory overnight delivery to the owner's last known address;
(2)
Include a summary of the animal control officer's findings that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
Be dated and state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the animal control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(4)
State that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the county board of health;
(5)
State that if a hearing is not requested, an animal control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous or a potentially dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes on a date specified in the notice, which shall be after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing; and
(6)
Include a form to request a hearing before the board of health and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering such request to the board.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 8(2a)—(f))
Sec. 8-133. - Hearing.
(a)
When the board of health receives a request for a hearing under the provisions of this article it shall schedule such a hearing within 30 days after receiving the request.
(b)
The board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time, and place of the hearing. Such notice shall be mailed to the dog owner at least ten days prior to the date of the hearing.
(c)
At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence. In addition, the board shall receive at the hearing such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the board may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify, or overrule the animal control officer's classification of the dog.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 8(3))
Sec. 8-134. - Determination; notice to owner.
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the board of health shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify the date upon which that determination is effective.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 8(4))
Secs. 8-135—8-151. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - POSSESSION RESTRICTIONS

Sec. 8-152. - Certificate of registration required.
(a)
No one shall have, own or possess within the jurisdiction of the county a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration.
(b)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who moves to this county from any other county within the state shall register such dog as required by this article within ten days after becoming a resident of the county.
(c)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the state shall register such dog as required by this article within 30 days after becoming a resident of the state and the county.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 9(1), (6), (7))
Sec. 8-153. - Requirements for issuance of certificate.
The animal control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog if the owner presents to the animal control officer or the animal control officer otherwise finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous or potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
The posting of the premises where the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the property. The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall receive such sign from the animal control officer at the time the owner pays the annual registration fee in the amount established by the sole commissioner; and
(3)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $25,000.00 issued by the insurer authorized to transact business in this state insuring the owner of the dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
(4)
A surety bond in the amount of $25,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person injured by the dangerous dog.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 9(2), (3))
Sec. 8-154. - Fees; certificate renewal.
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall pay an annual registration fee in the amount at the time the annual certificate of registration is issued. Certificates of registration shall be renewed in the month of the initial registration. The annual registration fee or renewal fee may be changed from time to time by the sole commissioner.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 9(4))
Sec. 8-155. - Notice of owner's intent to move from the county
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the animal control officer if the owner is moving from the county.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 9(5))
Sec. 8-156. - Duty of owner to give notice of loose, unconfined, sold or donated dog.
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the animal control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 9(8))
Sec. 8-157. - Dogs outside proper enclosure prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure as defined in this ordinance unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person. The muzzle shall be made in a manner that will prevent it from biting any person, but not cause injury to the dog or interfere with its vision or respiration.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of a potentially dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside unless restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 10)
Sec. 8-158. - Grounds for confiscation of dangerous dogs.
(a)
A dangerous dog shall be immediately confiscated by the animal control officer or by a law enforcement officer or by another person authorized by the dog control officer if:
(1)
The owner of the dog does not secure the liability insurance or surety bond required under the provisions of this article;
(2)
The dog is not validly registered as required in this article; or
(3)
The dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure or is outside a proper enclosure in violation of this article.
(b)
A potentially dangerous dog shall be confiscated in the same manner as a dangerous dog if:
(1)
The dog is not validly registered as required by this article;
(2)
The dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure as defined in this article; or
(3)
The dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure or is outside a proper enclosure in violation of this article.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 11(1), (2))
Sec. 8-159. - Redemption from confiscation.
Any dog that has been confiscated under the provisions of this article shall be returned to its owner upon compliance with the provisions herein as determined by the animal control officer and upon payment of reasonable confiscation fees. In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this section within 20 days of the date the dog is confiscated, the dog may be destroyed.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 11(3))
Sec. 8-160. - Right of entry.
The animal control officer shall be authorized to enter either public or private property when accompanied by certified law enforcement officer to impound any animal if the officer has reasonable cause to believe the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is in violation of this article. The animal control officer may, if applicable, in lieu of impoundment, issue a summons for violations.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 12(5))
Sec. 8-161. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog who violates the provisions of this article or whose dangerous dog is subject to confiscation under the provisions of this article shall be guilty pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28(a) of a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature and shall be guilty pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28(b) of a misdemeanor, both in addition to the penalties imposed by state law.
(b)
If the owner of a dangerous dog who has a previous conviction for a violation of this article knowingly and willfully fails to comply with the provisions of this article, and if the owner's dangerous dog attacks and bites a human being under circumstances constituting another violation of this article, such owner shall be guilty pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28(c) of a felony.
(c)
The owner of a dangerous dog who knowingly and willfully fails to comply with the provisions of this article , and whose dangerous dog aggressively attacks and causes severe injury or death of a human being under circumstances constituting a violation of this article shall be guilty pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28(d) of a felony.
(d)
In addition to the penalties for violations as described in this section, the dangerous dog involved shall be immediately confiscated by the animal control officer or by a law enforcement officer or another person authorized by the animal control officer and placed in quarantine for ten days as per the policy of the county board of health, and thereafter, the dangerous dog may be destroyed in an expeditious manner.
(e)
No owner of a dangerous dog who has complied with the requirements in this article shall be held criminally liable under state law for injuries inflicted by said owner's dog to any human being while on the owner's property pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28(f).
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 14)
Sec. 8-162. - Liability for damages.
It is the intent of this article that the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog. Under no circumstances shall the county or any county employee or official which enforces or fails to enforce the provisions of this article be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted:
(1)
By a dog that has been identified as being a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
By a dog that has been reported to the proper authorities as being a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
By a dog that the county has failed to identify as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(4)
By a dog which has been identified as being a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog but has not been kept or restrained in the manner described in this article; or
(5)
By a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required in this article.
(Ord. of 4-16-2009(2), § 15)
