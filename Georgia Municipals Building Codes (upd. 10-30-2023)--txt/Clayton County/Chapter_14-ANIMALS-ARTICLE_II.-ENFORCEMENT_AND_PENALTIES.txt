Sec. 14-31. - Enforcement responsibility.
The provisions of this chapter shall be enforced by the animal control unit and by the county police department.
(Code 1973, § 2-5-120)
Sec. 14-32. - Interference.
No person shall interfere with, hinder or prevent the animal control unit or any other officer in the performance of its duty or to seek to release any animal in the custody of the animal control unit, except as otherwise specifically provided in this chapter.
(Code 1973, § 2-5-121)
Sec. 14-33. - Right of entry.
(a)
The animal control officers, police officers and other enforcement officers of the county are hereby authorized to enter upon any premises or parcel of land for the purpose of seizing and impounding any animal found therein or thereon to be in violation of this chapter, including, but not limited to, an animal that has bitten a person within ten days.
(b)
The animal control unit may use any force necessary to remove any animal locked in a closed vehicle between the dates of April 1 and September 30. The operator of the vehicle may be charged with cruelty to animals. No injunction or action may be placed against the animal control unit or its officers for this action.
(Code 1973, § 2-5-122)
Sec. 14-34. - Equipment.
The animal control unit is authorized to employ any equipment it deems necessary to enforce the provisions of this chapter, including, but without limitation to, humane wire box traps; and the animal control unit may, subject to conditions it may determine, lend such traps or other equipment to private persons for the purpose of preventing nuisances resulting from animals running at large.
(Code 1973, § 2-5-123)
Sec. 14-35. - Penalties for violations.
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and punished as provided in section 1-12 of this Code. Each and every violation of the provisions of this chapter shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1973, § 2-5-124)
