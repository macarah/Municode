Sec. 38-1. - International Fire Code adopted.
The International Fire Code and subsequent editions and revisions of such code, with county amendments, are hereby adopted as a minimum standard for fire prevention in the city, except as otherwise specifically provided in this chapter, and are hereby made a part of this chapter as fully and to the same extent as if copied in this section in full. A copy of such code shall be maintained on file in the office of the city clerk, said code shall be a public record and shall be available for inspection by the public. In the event of any conflict between the provisions of the International Fire Code and the provisions of this Code, state law, or ordinances, rules or regulations, the provisions of this Code, state law, or ordinances, rules or regulations shall prevail and be controlling.
(Code 1982, § 4-2-21; Ord. No. 515, pt. I, 1-15-02; Ord. No. 584, pt. I, 2-19-08)
Sec. 38-2. - Life Safety Code adopted.
There is hereby adopted by the city the Life Safety Code, as published by the National Fire Protection Association, for the purpose of providing rules and regulations for the construction, erection, use and occupancy of buildings within the city with minimum standards for fire safety necessary in the public interest. A copy of the code is on file in the office of the city clerk. Except as otherwise specifically provided in this chapter, the Life Safety Code is hereby made a part of this chapter as fully and to the same extent as if copied in this section in full. In the event of any conflict between the provisions of the Life Safety Code and the provisions of this Code, state law, or ordinances, rules or regulations, the provisions of this Code, state law, or ordinances, rules or regulations shall prevail and be controlling.
(Code 1982, § 4-2-22; Ord. No. 515, pt. I, 1-15-02)
Sec. 38-3. - Adoption of state and county fire safety standards; enforcement of fire safety standards by county fire prevention bureau.
The minimum fire safety standards of the state and the fire prevention ordinances of the county, and any future amendments or additions thereto, are hereby incorporated by reference in this section and adopted as the ordinances of this city. The county is hereby requested to extend the services of the fire prevention bureau to the citizens of the city, and the personnel of the bureau are authorized to enforce such ordinances wIthin the limits of the city, and are hereby made deputy marshals thereof.
(Code 1982, § 4-2-23)
