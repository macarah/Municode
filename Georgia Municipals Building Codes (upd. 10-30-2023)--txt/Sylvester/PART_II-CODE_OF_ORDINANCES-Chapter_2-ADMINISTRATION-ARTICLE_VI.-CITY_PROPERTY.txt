Sec. 2-411. - Record of real estate; approval of title prior to purchase of real estate.
The city clerk shall keep a record of all real estate belonging to the city, together with all original title papers relating thereto, and shall note any sale of such real estate on the record. It shall be the duty of the city attorney to abstract and pass upon the title to all real estate purchased by the city, and no title shall be accepted without his written approval of the title to such real estate.
(Code 1965, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-412. - Sale of real estate.
All sales of real estate by the city shall be authorized by ordinance of the mayor and council.
(Code 1965, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-413. - Sale of personal property.
No personal property belonging to the city shall be sold except upon authority, by ordinance, from the mayor and council.
(Code 1965, § 2-4)
Sec. 2-414. - Inventories of personal property.
Every city official or employee charged with the keeping of any personal property shall submit an accurate inventory and return thereof annually to the mayor and council, on the first Monday in each year, showing the amount, disposition and condition of the property, and it shall be the duty of the mayor and council from time to time to cause inspection of such property.
(Code 1965, § 2-5)
Sec. 2-415. - Purchase of personal property.
All purchases of personal property shall be authorized by the mayor and council.
(Code 1965, § 2-6)
Sec. 2-416. - Use of city machinery.
No machinery belonging to the city may be loaned to anyone without special permission from the mayor and council.
(Code 1965, § 2-7)
Sec. 2-417. - Authorizing the purchase, acquisition, construction, etc. of telecommunication systems and facilities.
The mayor and council shall have the power to provide for the purchase, acquisition, construction, extension, operation, and maintenance of telecommunication systems and facilities both within and without the corporate limits of the city and to furnish telecommunication services to consumers for profit both within and without the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 2004-05, s; 1, 5-17-2004)
