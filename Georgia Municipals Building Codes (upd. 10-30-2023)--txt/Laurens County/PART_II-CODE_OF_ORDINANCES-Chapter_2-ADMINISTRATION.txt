ARTICLE II. - BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS[1]

Sec. 2-19. - Meetings.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall hold regular meetings on the first Tuesday of each month at 5:30 p.m. unless otherwise ordered by the board.
(b)
Special meetings may be held upon call by the chairman of the board of commissioners or any three members. Due notice of such meetings shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).
(c)
Meetings shall be held at the Laurens County Courthouse Annex, Dublin, Georgia.
(d)
There shall be a written agenda prepared prior to each board meeting. The chairman of the board of commissioners retains the discretion to recognize any person attending a county commission meeting.
(Code 1994, § 2-26; Mo. of 1-3-1977; Res. of 11-2-1982(1))
State Law reference— Meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1; due notice requirements for other than regular meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); excluded proceedings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-3.

ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES

Sec. 2-42. - Workers' compensation coverage.
Certain elected officials of the county shall be covered under the Association of County Commissioners of Georgia Group Self-Insured Workers' Compensation Fund. While performing their elected duties, the board of commissioners, sheriff, clerk of court, coroner, probate judge, tax commissioner, chief magistrate, magistrates, volunteer firefighters, emergency management, first responders, and county attorney shall be covered under the workers' compensation laws of the state.
(Code 1994, § 2-1; Res. of 12-20-1983; Res. of 11-17-1987, § I)
State Law reference— Workers' compensation, O.C.G.A. § 34-9-1 et seq.

ARTICLE V. - FINANCE[2]
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-92. - Fiscal year established.
It is hereby resolved by the board of commissioners that the fiscal year under which the county governmental agencies operate shall begin on July 1 and end on June 30 of each calendar year.
(Code 1994, § 2-46; Res. of 2-5-1985(1))
State Law reference— Requirement for governing authority to establish a fiscal year, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3.
Sec. 2-93. - County officials to submit annual budget requests.
All county officials who make annual budget requests to the board of commissioners will have their annual budget requests for the next fiscal year submitted to the county governing authority as established by the county administrator from year to year.
(Code 1994, § 2-47; Res. of 2-5-1985(1))
Sec. 2-94. - Approval of county budget.
The board of commissioners will approve the total county budget for the next fiscal year at the last public meeting of the board of commissioners in June.
(Code 1994, § 2-48; Res. of 2-5-1985(1))
Sec. 2-95. - Check signing procedure.
(a)
All payroll and general fund checks up to $2,500.00 will be signature stamped by a check-writer with the signatures of the chairman of the board of commissioners and the county administrator.
(b)
All checks of $2,500.00 or more will be manually signed by a member of the board of commissioners and the county administrator or, in his absence, the finance officer.
(c)
The finance officer will review and approve the county payroll and review and initial each voucher supporting the general fund checks.
(Code 1994, § 2-49; Ord. of 3-3-1987)
Sec. 2-96. - E-911 charge.
(a)
The board of commissioners do hereby request that Progressive Rural, Glenwood, Pineland, Contel, and Southern Bell/Bell South begin immediately billing a monthly charge upon each exchange access facility, as prescribed in O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.
(b)
The amount of such charge shall be $1.50 per month, per exchange access facility provided to the telephone subscriber.
(c)
Pursuant to the O.C.G.A. § 46-5-133, which provides for this charge to become effective 120 days after the passing of the resolution contained in this section, it is therefore requested the effective date shall be November 16, 1991.
(Code 1994, § 2-50; Res. of 7-16-1991)
State Law reference— Georgia Emergency Telephone Number 911 Service Act of 1977, O.C.G.A. § 46-5-120 et seq.

DIVISION 2. - BIDDING

Sec. 2-121. - Sealed bids required; exception.
(a)
Purchases of any item of more than $500.00 but less than $2,500.00 shall require obtaining informal bids.
(b)
Purchases $2,500.00 or greater shall require obtaining written bids.
(c)
Emergency purchases and sole source purchases are not subject to sealed bid procedures.
(Code 1994, § 2-61; Ord. of 6-18-1985; Ord. of 7-5-1988; Ord. of 4-6-1993)
Sec. 2-122. - Advertisement; changes.
(a)
All items offered for bid will be advertised once prior to the opening of the bids at a bid-opening conference giving date and time of the public invitation to bid meeting. The ads will run in the county legal gazette.
(b)
Changes concerning any aspect of the bid will be made known to all concerned by means of an addendum.
(Code 1994, § 2-62; Ord. of 6-18-1985; Ord. of 7-5-1988; Ord. of 4-6-1993)
Sec. 2-123. - Procedure.
(a)
Openings. Bid openings will be conducted by the county administrator or his representative. All bids will be opened, read aloud, and recorded and may be reviewed by any bidders or other parties present. Bids received after bid-opening deadline will be rejected unopened.
(b)
Review; recommendation. The bids will be reviewed by the county administrator and will be summarized for presentation to the board of commissioners, along with a recommendation for the bid award. The bid awarding will be placed on the agenda for the next regularly scheduled commission meeting or, if necessary, at a meeting called for this purpose.
(c)
Single bid. Notwithstanding the foregoing, when only one bid is received by the county administrator, the bid shall not be opened by the county administrator until the single sealed bid is presented for consideration to the board of commissioners at their next meeting. Prior to opening the bid, the board of commissioners shall determine whether to proceed with opening and considering the single bid, readvertising for the purchase or taking other action.
(Code 1994, § 2-64; Ord. of 6-18-1985; Ord. of 7-5-1988; Ord. of 4-6-1993)
Sec. 2-124. - Determination of best bid.
Subject to this division, it will be the policy of the county to accept the best bid submitted, as determined by the following criteria:
(1)
The cost of the item to be purchased;
(2)
The ability, capacity and skill of bidder to perform required services;
(3)
Whether bidder can perform service promptly or within specified time;
(4)
The character, integrity, reputation, judgment, experience, and efficiency of bidder;
(5)
The performance of previous contracts;
(6)
The suitability of equipment or material for county use; and
(7)
The ability of bidder to provide future maintenance and parts service.
(Code 1994, § 2-65; Ord. of 6-18-1985; Ord. of 7-5-1988; Ord. of 4-6-1993)
