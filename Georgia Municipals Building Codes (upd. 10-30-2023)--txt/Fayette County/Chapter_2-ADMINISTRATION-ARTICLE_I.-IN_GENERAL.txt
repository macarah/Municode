ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Power to cite for violations.
Each of the following persons is hereby empowered by the board of commissioners to issue citations for violations of county laws and ordinances to be heard in the county magistrate court:
(1)
County marshal.
(2)
Zoning administrator.
(3)
Building official.
(4)
Animal control officers.
(5)
Officers of the bureau of fire prevention.
(6)
County engineer.
(7)
Director of public works.
(8)
County clerk.
(9)
Magistrate court constables.
(10)
County sheriff and deputies.
(11)
Code enforcement officers.
(Code 1983, § 2-9; Code 1992, § 2-1; Ord. No. 2013-04, § 2, 6-27-2013)
State Law reference— Magistrate courts, O.C.G.A. § 15-10-1 et seq.; prosecution upon citation, O.C.G.A. §§ 15-10-62, 15-10-63.
Sec. 2-2. - Attendance at meetings.
Any member of an authority, commission, committee or board appointed by the board of commissioners who is absent for any three consecutive regularly scheduled meetings shall be dismissed from such authority, commission, committee or board by the board of commissioners. This shall not include absences for medical or emergency reasons. Any member of such authority, commission, committee or board failing to maintain an 80 percent attendance record at all regular and called meetings for the previous 12 months shall be subject to such dismissal by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1992, § 2-2; Ord. No. 89-08, 6-22-1989)
Sec. 2-3. - Records retention schedule; officer responsible.
(a)
The records retention scheduled is adopted by the board of commissioners of the county, this 25th day of August, 2016. The records retention schedule is adopted to ensure that all public records of the county are adequately maintained in such a way as to reduce costs and improve efficiency in record keeping.
(b)
The county clerk shall be the officer to coordinate and perform all responsibilities required under state law in the management of the public records for the county.
(c)
The county clerk shall recommend a records retention schedule. This schedule shall include an inventory of the type of records maintained and the length of time each type of record shall be maintained in the office or in a record-holding area. These retention periods shall be based on the legal, fiscal, administrative, and historical needs for the record. The recommendation from the county clerk for the records retention schedule for the county is attached as exhibit "A" hereto, with said exhibit "A" being incorporated into this article by this reference.
(d)
Upon approval by the board of commissioners of the county, such recommended records retention schedule shall become the records retention schedule of the county.
(Ord. No. 2016-10, § 1, 8-25-2016)
