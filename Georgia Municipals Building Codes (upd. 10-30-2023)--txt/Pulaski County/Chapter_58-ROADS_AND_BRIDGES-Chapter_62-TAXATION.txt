ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Payment of ad valorem taxes.
It is hereby adopted and ordered that all ad valorem taxes due and payable in Pulaski County shall be paid by the tax payer no later than the close of business on December 1 of each year hereafter.
(Ord. No. 2010-005, 12-20-2010)

ARTICLE II. - JOINT COUNTY AND MUNICIPAL SALES AND USE TAX[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Joint county and municipal sales and use tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-80 et seq.

Sec. 62-31. - Findings.
(a)
A referendum election was held in the county on September 15, 1981, pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-8-85, on the question of whether or not a retail sales and use tax of one percent would be levied within the special district within the county.
(b)
The results of the referendum election were certified on September 15, 1981, and the collection of retail sales and use tax of one percent was approved.
(Res. of 12-6-1997)
Sec. 62-32. - Levied; requirements.
(a)
There is imposed and levied within the county a joint sales and use tax at the rate of one percent.
(b)
The joint tax, except as to rate, shall correspond to the tax imposed and administered by O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq.; and no item or transaction which is not subject to taxation by O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq. shall be subject to the tax levied pursuant to this article, except that the joint use tax provided for in this article shall be applicable to sales of motor fuels as that term is defined by O.C.G.A. § 48-9-5.
(Res. of 12-6-1997)
Sec. 62-33. - Authority.
This article is adopted pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-8-86, and the levy of the tax shall be effective beginning with the first quarter of 1982, which begins on January 1, 1982.
(Res. of 12-6-1997)

ARTICLE III. - GROSS PREMIUMS TAX ON INSURERS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— General assembly authorized to provide for taxation of insurance companies based on gross direct premiums received from insurance policies within the unincorporated areas of counties, Ga. Const. art. IX, § IV, ¶ I(c).

Sec. 62-61. - Gross premiums tax on insurance other than life insurance.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2, there is levied an annual tax upon each insurer doing insurance business within the unincorporated area of the county in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year on all insurance, other than life insurance, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(2), (3), (4) and (5). Gross direct premiums shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a).
(Res. of 9-6-1983, § 1)

ARTICLE IV. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS BUSINESS LICENSE TAX[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Local depository financial institutions business license tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 62-91. - Levied.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is levied an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the county at a rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of the depository financial institutions. Gross receipts shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. Depository financial institutions shall mean state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
(Res. of 12-19-1983, § 1)
Sec. 62-92. - Minimum business license tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 62-91 shall be $1,000.00.
(Res. of 12-19-1983, § 2)
Sec. 62-93. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to the tax levied in this article shall file a return of its gross receipts with the commissioner of the county on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. This return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The commissioner of the county shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this article based upon the information provided in the return.
(Res. of 12-19-1983, § 3)
Sec. 62-94. - Due date of taxes.
Taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than 30 days after filing of the return proscribed by section 62-93 unless extended by the commissioner.
(Res. of 12-19-1983, § 4)

ARTICLE V. - FREEPORT EXEMPTION[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Freeport exemption, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2.

Sec. 62-131. - Enumeration of exempt tangible personal property.
There shall be exempt from county ad valorem taxation 100 percent of the following described tangible personal property:
(1)
Inventory of goods in the process of manufacture or production, which shall include all partly finished goods and raw materials held for direct use or consumption in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business in this state. This exemption shall apply only to tangible personal property which is substantially modified, altered or changed in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing, processing or production operations in this state.
(2)
Inventory of finished goods manufactured or produced within this state in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business when held by the original manufacturer or producer of such finished goods. This exemption shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is produced or manufactured.
(3)
Inventory of finished goods which, on January 1, are stored in a warehouse, dock or wharf, whether public or private, and which are destined for shipment to a final destination outside this state and inventory of finished goods which are shipped into this state from outside this state and stored for transshipment to a final destination outside this state. The exemption provided for in this subsection shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is stored in this state.
(Res. of 5-5-1986)
