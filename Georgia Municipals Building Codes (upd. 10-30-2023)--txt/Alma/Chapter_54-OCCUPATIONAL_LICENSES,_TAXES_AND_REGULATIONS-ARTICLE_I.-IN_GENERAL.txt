ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 54-1. - Tax levied.
The specific and occupation taxes set forth in this chapter are hereby levied and shall be collected in the amount hereinafter specified upon each and every person engaged in the business, trade or profession, or doing acts named or described herein within the corporate limits of the city during each year.
(Code 1981, § 14-1; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 1)
Sec. 54-2. - Registration of business required.
No person shall be engaged in, prosecute or carry on any business, trade or profession, either in whole or in part within the corporate limits of the city by maintaining an office, or engage in any business in any manner without having registered the name of said business with the city clerk and paid occupational tax as hereafter provided.
(Code 1981, § 14-2; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 2)
Sec. 54-3. - Deadline for payment.
Each such occupational tax shall be for each calendar year. Said occupational tax shall be due and payable January 1 of each calendar year and shall be delinquent if not paid by March 15 of each calendar year.
(Code 1981, § 14-3; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 3)
Sec. 54-4. - Tax based upon gross receipts.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Gross receipts means the total revenue of the business or practitioner for the period, including without being limited to the following:
(1)
Total income without deduction for the cost of goods or expenses incurred;
(2)
Gain from trading in stocks, bonds, capital assets or instruments of indebtedness;
(3)
Proceeds from commissions on the sale of property, goods or services;
(4)
Proceeds from rent, interest, royalty or divided income.
Location or office includes any structure or vehicle where a business profession or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project, or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession.
(b)
Each person with a location or office in the business, trade, profession, or occupation in the city shall pay an occupation tax based upon gross receipts in exhibits A and B attached to the ordinance from which this section is derived, which are available for public examination on file in the city business license clerk's office.
(1)
Gross receipts shall not include the following:
a.
Sales, use or excise tax;
b.
Sales returns, allowances and discounts;
c.
Interorganizational sales and transfers between or among the units of a parent-subsidiary controlled group of corporations as defined by 26 USC 1563(a)(1), or between or among the units of a brother-sister controlled group of corporations as defined by 26 USC 1563(a)(2), or between or among the wholly owned partnership or other wholly owned entities;
d.
Payments made to a subcontractor or an independent agent for services which contributed to the gross receipts in issue;
e.
Governmental and foundation grants, charitable contributions, or the interest income derived from such funds received by a nonprofit organization which employs salaried practitioners otherwise covered by this chapter, if such funds constitute 80 percent or more of the organization's receipts; and
f.
Proceeds from sales of goods or services which are delivered to or received by customers who are outside the state at the time of delivery or receipt.
(2)
The minimum tax based on gross receipts shall be $25.00 and nonrefundable.
(Code 1981, § 14-4; Ord. of 12-15-80, § 4; Ord. No. 1995-2, § 1, 3-6-1995)
Sec. 54-5. - Filing estimated gross receipts tax return.
All taxes levied on the basis of gross receipts under section 54-12 are levied on accounts of business to be transacted during each calendar year. However, for convenience of both the city and the taxpayer, and to eliminate the necessity of making numerous returns, those businesses subject to the tax levied under section 54-12 shall, on or before the times hereinafter set forth, file with the business license clerk the return or application hereinafter specifically provided for showing the gross receipts of that business during the calendar year immediately preceding the year for which the tax is due. This return or application shall be used as an estimate for making payment of the gross receipts tax for the year which it is filed, the actual and final amount of tax levied for business transacted in said year to be paid in accordance with a final return to be made after the termination of said year in accordance with the procedure hereinafter set forth.
(Code 1981, § 14-5; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 5)
Sec. 54-6. - Information required on tax return.
Every person subject to the occupational tax under section 54-12 for each calendar year shall at the end of the preceding year for which tax was due, and on or before March 15 of said year, make a return in writing to the city clerk, on a form supplied by said clerk for the purpose, setting forth under oath the following information:
(1)
Style name of the person, firm, company or corporation.
(2)
Location at which the trade, business or profession is to be conducted.
(3)
Exact nature of the trade, business or profession for which license is requested.
(4)
The correct brackets are set forth in the gross brackets classification schedules, that were provided as exhibit B, in the ordinance from which this provision was derived, that includes the full and true amount of the gross sales, receipts, premiums, commissions or other form of measurable returns from the trade, business, or profession during the preceding calendar year. As herein required, the report of gross sales, receipts, premiums, etc., shall include all business done within the state. All the information herein required shall be given under oath by the owner, or a member of the firm, or an officer of a corporation or by an authorized employee having exact knowledge of actual business done.
(5)
Where business is solicited, sales are made and goods delivered or transactions are completed and occupational tax paid on same in an incorporated town or city other than the city, this amount shall be deducted from the total receipts and such information shall be furnished with the application.
(Code 1981, § 14-6; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 6; Ord. No. 1995-2, § 2, 3-6-1995)
Sec. 54-7. - Pro rata remittance when business conducted less than full year.
Where a business subject to the gross receipts tax for a particular calendar year has been conducted for only a part of said year, amount of gross receipts for such a part shall be set forth in said return. The return shall also show a figure putting the receipts for such a part of a year on an annual basis, which figure shall bear a reasonable ratio to the amount of gross receipts for such year as the full year bears to such part. The figure shall be used as the estimate of the gross receipts of the business for the next calendar year.
(Code 1981, § 14-7; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 7)
Sec. 54-8. - Tax levied under section 54-12; dates payable, delinquent.
The amount of tax levied under section 54-12 based on the estimates as set forth in section 54-7, shall be payable to said city at the offices of the city clerk, on January 1 of each year, and delinquent if not paid on or before March 15 of each year.
(Code 1981, § 14-8; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 8)
Sec. 54-9. - Adjustment of estimated return.
After the end of each year, and on or before March 15 of each year, the owner, proprietor, manager or executive officer of the business liable for said gross receipts tax levied for the preceding year shall file with the business license clerk of said city a signed and sworn return setting forth the actual amount of correct bracket, are set forth in the gross brackets classification schedules, that were provided as exhibit B, in the ordinance from which this provision was derived, of the gross receipts of said business during the preceding calendar year.
(1)
If the amount of the gross receipts tax for the preceding year, based on the return provided for in this section and on the rate of said tax provided for in this chapter exceeds the amount of gross receipts tax theretofore paid by said business based on the estimate filed pursuant to section 54-5, the difference in said amount shall be due and payable by the taxpayer to the city on or before March 15 of the year in which the return is filed, and shall be delinquent if not paid on or before said date.
(2)
If the amount of the gross tax for the preceding year, based on the return provided for in this section and on the rate of tax provided for in this chapter, is less than the amount of the gross receipts tax theretofore paid by such business based on the estimate filed pursuant to section 54-5, the difference in said amount shall be refunded by the city to the taxpayer provided application is made therefor by the business within 30 days of its discontinuance; or, if said business continues to be conducted in said city during the year in which said return is filed, such difference in amounts may be credited by the city on the amount of business license tax to be paid to the city by said business for the year in which said return is filed.
(Code 1981, § 14-9; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 9)
Sec. 54-10. - Determination of license fee for new business.
If a business is begun for the first time on or before January 1 of any calendar year that this chapter is in effect, then the license fee herein assessed against such business shall be determined as follows: The city clerk, upon registration and application for license for such business shall issue a business license for the year in which said registration and application is made, and the estimated fee for such license shall be computed according to the estimate by the business of its taxable gross revenue for one calendar year. The annual estimated fee, as so determined, shall be divided by 12, in order to arrive at a monthly fee amount. Such monthly fee shall then be multiplied by the actual number of months remaining in said first year of which the business can operate; the product being the estimated license fee for the first year's business operation. The transaction of business during any month, or portion thereof, for the purpose of this provision, shall be deemed to have been the transaction of business for the entire month.
(Code 1981, § 14-10; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 10)
Sec. 54-11. - Businesses not listed.
Should any person, firm or corporation be in a business or commence a business not listed in this chapter, then the city clerk of said city shall set the special or license tax on such business. Any person, firm or corporation being dissatisfied with the decision of the said city clerk shall have the right of appeal to the city council, provided said person, firm or corporation makes such appeal in writing to said city council within 30 days of the disputed decision of said city clerk.
(Code 1981, § 14-11; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 11)
Sec. 54-12. - Gross receipts tax levy and classifications.
(a)
For the purpose of this chapter, every person, firm, company or corporation engaged in trade, business, or profession requiring licenses are classified in accordance to their main line of business as defined in the Standard Industrial Classification Manual, Executive Office of the President, Office of Management and Budget, and profitability classes are assigned in accordance with Statistics of Income, Business Income Tax Returns, U.S. Treasury Department, Internal Revenue Service. In the case where a person, firm, company or corporation is engaged in more than one line of business, taxes will be levied on the basis of the dominant business activity of the licensee.
(b)
The six separate classifications of class 1 through class 6, that were provided as exhibit A in the ordinance from which this provision was derived, so as to provide for the determination of the occupational tax to be levied as a fixed sum and measured by taxable gross revenue, the purpose of which is to recognize the ability of businesses to pay as determined by nationwide averages. All separate businesses, engaged in more than one business activity, shall be classified on the basis of their dominant business activity, except that persons whose dominant business activity is legally exempted or otherwise limited by city ordinances or by state law from the tax, shall be classified according to his/her principal subsidiary business, if any, which is subject to the levy and assessment of an occupational tax. The subclassification of all businesses within each classification as to fixed units of taxation, that is, bracket ranges of taxable gross revenue against which there are applied fixed rates, are set forth in the gross brackets classification schedules, that were provided as exhibit B, in the ordinance from which this provision was derived.
(c)
Determination of license fee herein levied and assessed shall be determined in accordance with the following formula:
(1)
Determination of the taxable gross revenue as defined by this chapter and the rules and regulations promulgated in connection therewith.
(2)
Classification of the subject business by referring to exhibit A, the classification schedule in the ordinance from which this provision was derived.
(3)
Determination of the license fee on volume of gross revenue that was provided as exhibit B, in the ordinance from which this provision was derived, gross brackets classification schedule, using the information as a result of the determination in subsections (c)(1) and (2) of this section.
(Code 1981, § 14-12; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 12)
Sec. 54-13. - Specific occupation, privilege, telephone service and insurance premium taxes.
(a)
Certain businesses, occupations, professions, trades, performances and events are hereby declared not subject to the schedules of taxation imposed by section 54-12, and are hereby taxed, for the year, payable March 15, if not otherwise indicated, or a prorated part of a year, in the amounts as established by the city council from time to time.
(b)
Businesses paying the privilege license fee for alcoholic beverages shall apply for renewal of their licenses and pay said fee by January 1 of each year, or cease sales of alcoholic beverages. The gross volume of alcoholic beverage sales may be deducted from total gross receipts of the business. Such businesses shall pay an additional occupational tax based on their dominant line of business if other than alcoholic beverages.
(c)
Each company shall pay, in addition to any license fee imposed in subsection (a) of this section, an amount equal to two percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year from policies of fire and casualty insurance issued upon property and business located within the corporate limits of the city. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this subsection, means gross direct premiums as that term is used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4 of the insurance code of the state. The term "fire and casualty insurance," as used in this subsection, means insurance coverage as that term is defined in sections O.C.G.A. §§ 33-7-3, 33-7-5 and 33-7-6 of the insurance code of the state.
(d)
Public utilities. The percent of gross receipts is as per special ordinances, contracts, or franchise agreement.
(e)
Taxation of professionals. Practitioners of professions are:
(1)
Physicians licensed under O.C.G.A. tit. 43, ch. 34;
(2)
Osteopaths licensed under O.C.G.A. tit. 43, ch. 34;
(3)
Chiropractors;
(4)
Podiatrists;
(5)
Dentists;
(6)
Optometrists;
(7)
Psychologists;
(8)
Veterinarians;
(9)
Landscape architects;
(10)
Land surveyors;
(11)
Practitioners of physiotherapy;
(12)
Public accountants;
(13)
Embalmers;
(14)
Funeral directors;
(15)
Civil, mechanical, hydraulic or electrical engineers;
(16)
Architects; and
(17)
Marriage and family therapists, social workers and professional counselors.
Such term shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
(f)
Practitioners of professions, as defined in subsection (e) of this section, shall elect for their occupation tax to be either:
(1)
The occupation tax as set forth in this chapter; or
(2)
A tax of $175.00 for each practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid as at the practitioner's office or location.
Such election shall be made prior to payment of occupation tax. Such election shall be changed for subsequent calendar years only by a written request filed by the professional on or before February 1 of the year in which the election is to be changed.
(g)
Out-of-state businesses. Persons or businesses from out of state with no office or location in the state subject to an occupation tax under this section shall be required to pay an occupation tax to only one local municipal government in the state. Such local government shall be the government of the municipal corporation or county in which the largest dollar volume of business is done or services are performed by the person. Such persons submitting documentation to the city showing payment of a local business or occupation tax to another local government in the state shall be exempt from the occupation tax levied by this section. Out-of-state businesses with no location in the state shall be assessed occupation taxes based on the gross receipts of the business as defined in O.C.G.A. § 14-13-7 which are reasonably attributed to sales or services in the state.
(h)
Real estate brokers are as follows:
(1)
They shall be subject to the occupation tax under the tax imposed by this article only if they maintain a principal or branch office in the city;
(2)
If their offices are located outside the city and they sell property inside the city they shall be subject to the occupation tax based upon the gross receipts from sales of property located within the city.
(Code 1981, § 14-13; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 13; Ord. No. 1985-1, 3-4-1985; Ord. No. 1985-5, 9-9-1985; Ord. No. 1995-2, § 3, 3-6-1995)
Sec. 54-14. - Delinquency penalty.
All licenses under this chapter which are not paid for 90 days from the due date of the tax or at time of commencement of business if later, will bear penalty at the rate of ten percent of the amount due and unpaid or $25.00, whichever is greater.
(Code 1981, § 14-14; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 14; Ord. No. 1990-2, 3-19-1990)
Sec. 54-15. - Penalty for failure to register.
Any person or other entity exercising or carrying on any trade, business, profession or operating any establishment within the city, for which a license or payment of an occupation tax is required by this chapter, without first having registered as provided in section 54-2 and paying the appropriate license or occupation fee, shall be liable for a fine not exceeding $200.00. For the purpose of this section, each day for which said license or occupation fee is not paid shall be deemed a new violation and subject to the same penalty.
(Code 1981, § 14-15; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 15; Ord. No. 1989-3, § 1, 4-17-1989)
Sec. 54-16. - Locations of businesses.
Occupational license granted under this chapter shall not authorize the holder to exercise or carry on the trade, business, or profession named herein at any other place than that mentioned in said license.
(Code 1981, § 14-16; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 16)
Sec. 54-17. - License or occupational tax receipt to be displayed.
All persons shall exhibit and display the license or occupational tax receipt issued to them in a conspicuous place in their business establishment if any in the city. Any transient or nonresident person, firm or corporation doing business within the city shall carry such license or occupational tax receipt either upon his/her person or in any vehicle or other conveyance which is used in such business and such person shall exhibit the same to any authorized enforcement officer of the city when so requested.
(Code 1981, § 14-17; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 17)
Sec. 54-18. - Change of address.
Any person moving from one location to another shall notify the city clerk of such move and the new address in writing on a form provided by the city clerk no later than the day of moving. The same license or receipt for occupational tax will be valid at the new location if said new location conforms to the zoning regulations of the city, as set forth in exhibit A to this Code.
(Code 1981, § 14-18; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 18)
Sec. 54-19. - Licenses or occupational tax receipts not transferable.
Business licenses or occupational tax receipts shall not be transferable and a transfer of ownership shall be considered in the same light as the termination of such business and the establishment of a new business. Therefore, a new occupational license tax shall be required for the new owner of the business.
(Code 1981, § 14-19; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 19)
Sec. 54-20. - Separate businesses.
Where a business is conducted at more than one location or place, each location or place shall be considered a separate business under the terms of this chapter and a separate occupational license tax shall be levied and paid.
(Code 1981, § 14-20; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 20)
Sec. 54-21. - Duration of license, or occupational tax period.
Such license, referred to in this chapter, shall automatically expire on December 31 of the year of its issuance.
(Code 1981, § 14-21; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 21)
Sec. 54-22. - Casual and isolated activity.
Nothing herein contained shall be interpreted so as to require any person who may engage in casual or isolated activity and commercial transactions, where such involve personal assets and are not the principal occupation of the individual, to obtain a business license or pay occupational tax therefor.
(Code 1981, § 14-22; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 22)
Sec. 54-23. - Responsible persons.
Whenever in this chapter, the term "dealer" or "person" is used, as principal, but in his, her or their absence any agent, clerk, or employee shall be subject to the penalties herein imposed, should the said business be carried on without registering and paying the required occupational license tax in the same manner as if he, she or they were the proprietors of said business.
(Code 1981, § 14-23; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 23)
Sec. 54-24. - Issue of execution shall be made for failure to pay license or occupational tax.
In addition to any other penalties provided hereinbefore for violation of this chapter, in the event any person shall fail to pay the tax provided herein as and when the same has become due, the clerk of the city shall issue an execution against the said person, firm, or corporation so delinquent, and his/her or its property, for the amount of license tax prescribed by this chapter for the carrying-on of said business.
(Code 1981, § 14-24; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 24)
Sec. 54-25. - Veteran's license.
Persons who can qualify for a state veteran's free license must secure such license from the state and present same to the city clerk, who will honor the same and issue a city license.
(Code 1981, § 14-25; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 25)
Sec. 54-26. - Exemptions.
No occupation tax shall be levied upon the following:
(1)
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by:
a.
The United States;
b.
The state;
c.
A municipality or county of the state; or
d.
Instrumentality of the United States or the state or a municipality or county of the state;
(2)
Those businesses regulated by the state public service commission;
(3)
Those electrical service businesses organized under O.C.G.A. tit. 46 ch. 3;
(4)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including any agriculture business;
(5)
Nonprofit agricultural product cooperative marketing associations pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105;
(6)
Motor common carriers pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15;
(7)
Persons purchasing guano, meats, meal, flour, bran, cotton seed or cotton seed meal or hulls in carload lots for distribution among the purchasers for use and not sale pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355;
(8)
Persons selling or introducing into the city agricultural products or livestock, including animal products, raised in the state when the sale and introduction are made by the producer of the product and the sale is made within 90 days of the introduction of the product into the city (see note 1 at the end of this section);
(9)
Persons who qualify for a state veteran's free license must secure such license from the state and present same to the city clerk, who will honor same and issue a city license;
(10)
State and local authority or nonprofit organization pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-13(5);
(11)
Depository institutions pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93 (see note 2 at the end of this section); or
(12)
Any business where the levy of such occupation tax is prohibited by the laws of the state or the United States (see note 3 at the end of this section).
Note.
1. See O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356.
2. See this statute regarding the collection on occupation tax from banks and other financial institutions.
3. The city may wish to provide an exemption from the occupation tax, administrative fee and/or regulatory fee for charitable organizations. If so, the chapter provisions providing an exemption should recite that the exemption is part of a plan by the city to encourage such business, pursuant to state law, and should be narrowly defined to allow only true charitable organizations to take advantage of the provision.
(Code 1981, § 14-26; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 26)
Sec. 54-27. - Administration of chapter.
The city clerk shall administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter for levy, assessment and collection of license fees and penalties imposed herein.
(Code 1981, § 14-27; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 27)
Sec. 54-28. - Rules and regulations.
The city clerk shall have the power and authority to make and publish reasonable rules and regulations not inconsistent with this chapter or other laws of the city and the state, or the constitution of the state or the United States, for the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this chapter and the collection of the license or occupational tax hereunder.
(Code 1981, § 14-28; Ord. of 12-15-1980, § 28)
Sec. 54-29. - Regulatory fee.
(a)
An annual regulatory fee shall be imposed on persons engaged in the following businesses: Every person subject to payment or regulatory fee levied by this chapter shall display a current regulatory fee certificate in a conspicuous place at the location for which such certificate was issued. If a tax payer does not have a permanent location within the city, the regulatory fee certificate shall be shown to any police officer (or any other person charged with enforcing this chapter) upon request.
(b)
The amount of the regulatory fee shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(Code 1981, § 14-29; Ord. No. 1995-2, § 5, 3-6-1995)
