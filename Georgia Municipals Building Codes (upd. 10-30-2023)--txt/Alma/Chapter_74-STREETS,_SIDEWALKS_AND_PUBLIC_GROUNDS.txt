ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 74-1. - Street names—Changing, etc.
The council by resolution may change, rename or name an existing or newly established street within the city at any time upon recommendation of the planning commission.
(Code 1981, § 20-1; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 14)
Sec. 74-2. - Same—Choosing name.
(a)
In the case of streets which bear no name, the city council shall be empowered to choose names which it feels are appropriate and which are suitable to the residents of said street.
(b)
In the case of streets which bear a name in common with another street or if the name is similar enough so as to cause confusion, the city council shall be empowered to rename one of the streets.
(Code 1981, § 20-2; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 12)
Sec. 74-3. - Same—Directional prefixes.
(a)
All streets perpendicular to and crossing the east-west baseline shall be given a prefix of "north" for the section which lies to the north of the baseline and a prefix of "south" for the section which lies to the south of the baseline.
(b)
All streets perpendicular to and crossing the north-south baseline shall be given a prefix of "east" for the section which lies to the east of the baseline and a prefix of "west" for the section which lies to the west of the baseline.
(Code 1981, § 20-3; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 6)
Sec. 74-4. - Access drives; furnishing by city.
The city will assist property owners in establishing ingress and egress to and from their property from city-owned public roads as follows:
(1)
The city will provide at no charge the labor, equipment and material necessary to provide a 20-foot-wide access drive at a location designated by the property owner. This shall be limited to one access drive per contiguous parcel of land. Any additional access drive will be provided only when the property owner pays for the additional cost of labor, equipment and material.
(2)
The city manager or the city manager's designated staff shall first approve the location, width and material quality and size based upon the criteria of proper traffic flow, drainage and road construction.
(3)
If the property is located on state routes, then state regulations shall prevail. The city shall provide additional access drives only to the extent that the private property owner will reimburse the city for the costs of labor, equipment and material.
(4)
In the event of undue hardships or exigent circumstances and upon application, the mayor and council may determine that an additional access drive should be installed at particular locations and may provide for such additional access drive at no additional expense to the owner or upon such terms and conditions as may be set forth by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1981, § 20-4; Ord. of 8-6-1973, § 1; Ord. No. 1986-4, 6-16-1986)
Sec. 74-5. - Merchandise, etc., on sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to display any goods, wares, merchandise or produce on the sidewalks of the city, or to place any shelf, stand, basket or other structure or receptacle for holding goods, wares, or merchandise or fruits, vegetables or other produce, upon the sidewalks of the city, when the same interferes with free flow of traffic.
(Code 1969, § 12-2; Code 1981, § 20-5)
Sec. 74-6. - Signs prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person, other than by government authority, to erect or place a sign, either temporary or permanent, on the right-of-way of any state or federal highway or on any street or sidewalk in the city.
(Code 1969, § 12-1; Code 1981, § 20-6)
Secs. 74-7—74-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - NUMBERING SYSTEM

Sec. 74-31. - Established; compliance.
There is hereby established a uniform system for numbering buildings fronting on all streets, avenues and public ways in the city. All houses and other buildings shall be numbered in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1981, § 20-20; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 1)
Sec. 74-32. - Baselines and quadrants designated.
(a)
The baseline for numbering properties along all streets running north and south shall hereinafter be referred to as the east-west baseline and shall be constituted as follows: Twelfth Street from the west city limits through its intersection with Pierce Street and eastward to its ending at Bead Street and from thence due east to its junction with Georgia State Route 32, on which the line shall continue eastward.
(b)
The baseline for numbering properties along all streets running east and west shall hereinafter be referred to as the north-south baseline and shall be constituted as follows: Pierce Street (U.S. 1) from the south city limits northward to its juncture with Twelfth Street and thence northward to Fourth Street. From this point the baseline shall leave Pierce Street and continue due north until the line rejoins U.S. 1 north.
(c)
Each building to the north of the east-west baseline and facing a street running in a northerly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location north of said baseline.
(d)
Each building to the south of the east-west baseline and facing a street running in a southerly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location south of said baseline.
(e)
Each building to the west of the north-south baseline and facing a street running in a westerly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location west of said baseline.
(f)
Each building to the east of the north-south baseline and facing a street running in an easterly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location east of said baseline.
(g)
All buildings on diagonal streets shall be numbered the same as buildings on northerly and southerly streets if the diagonal runs more north-south, and the same rule shall apply on easterly and westerly streets if the diagonal runs more east-west. All buildings on diagonal streets having a deviation of exactly 45 degrees shall be numbered the same as buildings on northerly and southerly streets.
(Code 1981, § 20-21; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 2)
Sec. 74-33. - Sequence and intervals.
(a)
The numbering of buildings on each street shall begin at the baseline. All numbers shall be assigned on the basis of one number for each 50 feet, except those fronting on the following streets:
(1)
On Twelfth Street between Route 32 bypass and Pierce Street;
(2)
On Fourteenth Street between Route 32 bypass and Pierce Street;
(3)
On Fifteenth Street between Route 32 bypass and Pierce Street;
(4)
Dixon Street between Wall Street and Fifteenth Street;
(5)
Church Street between Wall Street and Fifteenth Street;
(6)
Thomas Street between Twelfth and Fifteenth Streets.
(b)
The streets named in subsection (a) of this section shall be assigned numbers on the basis of one number for each 25 feet of frontage.
(Code 1981, § 20-22; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 3)
Sec. 74-34. - Odd-even sides; separate numbers per entrance.
(a)
All buildings on the right side of each street running from the baseline shall bear odd numbers. All buildings on the left side of each street running from the baseline shall bear even numbers.
(b)
Where any building has more than one entrance serving separate occupants, a separate number shall be assigned to each entrance serving a separate occupant. If the building is not located on a lot, parcel or tract which would permit the assignment of one number to each such entrance, letters shall be used, as set forth in section 74-37.
(Code 1981, § 20-23; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 4)
Sec. 74-35. - Numbers on streets not extending to baseline.
All buildings facing streets not extending through the baselines shall be assigned the same relative numbers as if said streets had extended to the baseline.
(Code 1981, § 20-24; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 5)
Sec. 74-36. - Display of assigned numbers.
(a)
The assigned numbers shall be displayed at all times.
(b)
The numbers shall be conspicuously placed immediately above, on or at the side of the main entrance so that the number can be seen plainly from the street line.
(c)
Whenever any building is situated such that the main entrance is more than 50 feet from the street line or said entrance is obscured, then the numbers may be placed upon a gatepost, fence, tree, post or other appropriate place so as to be easily discernable from the street.
(d)
The numerals shall be no less than three inches in height and be made out of a durable and highly visible material.
(Code 1981, § 20-25; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 7)
Sec. 74-37. - Use of letters of alphabet.
Where only one number can be assigned to a building, the owner, occupant or agent of such building, who shall desire distinctive numbers for different parts of said building, shall use the suffix A, B, C, etc., as may be required.
(Code 1981, § 20-26; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 8)
Sec. 74-38. - Street map.
For the purpose of facilitating a correct numbering, a map of all streets, avenues and public ways within the city showing the proper numbers of all houses or other buildings fronting upon all streets, avenues or public ways shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk. This map shall be open to inspection of all persons during the office hours of the city clerk.
(Code 1981, § 20-27; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 9)
Sec. 74-39. - Determining numbers.
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement officer to inform any party applying therefor of the numbers belonging to or embraced within the limits of any said lot or property as provided in this article. In case of conflict as to the proper number to be assigned to any building, the building inspector shall determine the number of such building.
(Code 1981, § 20-28; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 10)
Sec. 74-40. - New buildings.
Whenever any house, building or structure shall hereafter be erected or located in the city, in order to preserve the continuity and uniformity of numbers of the houses, buildings and structures, it shall be the duty of the owner to procure the correct numbers as designated from the building inspector for the property and to immediately fasten said numbers so assigned upon said building as provided by this article. No building permit shall be issued for any house, building or structure until the owner has procured from the building inspector the official number of the premises. Final approval of any structure shall be withheld by the code enforcement officer until permanent and proper numbers have been affixed to said structure.
(Code 1981, § 20-29; Ord. of 6-16-1975, § 11)
