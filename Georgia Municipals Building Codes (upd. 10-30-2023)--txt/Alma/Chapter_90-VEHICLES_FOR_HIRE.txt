Chapter 90 - VEHICLES FOR HIRE
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 90-1—90-18. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS

Sec. 90-19. - Taxicab licenses as prescribed; approval by council.
The license for the operation of taxicabs shall be that prescribed from year to year in the license schedule. No license shall be issued until approved by the council.
(Code 1969, § 17-1; Code 1981, § 23-20)
Sec. 90-20. - Liability insurance.
The owner or operator of taxicabs shall continuously keep on file with the city clerk a liability insurance policy with a casualty company licensed and doing business under the laws of the state, in the amounts required by state law, covering each vehicle operated, with an endorsement attached to each policy that the same is on file with the city, and that the insurer is bound to notify the city promptly and within ten days if said policy should be cancelled for any reason whatsoever.
(Code 1969, § 17-2; Code 1981, § 23-21)
Sec. 90-21. - Fixed place of business required.
All taxicabs must operate only from a fixed place of business.
(Code 1969, § 17-3; Code 1981, § 23-22)
Sec. 90-22. - Condition of vehicle.
All taxicabs shall be kept in good and safe mechanical condition at all times.
(Code 1969, § 17-4; Code 1981, § 23-23)
Sec. 90-23. - Identifying vehicle as taxicab.
The owner or operator of taxicabs shall paint on each side of each vehicle operated as such a permanent sign designating it as a taxicab. This sign shall be of such size as to be recognized by the general public.
(Code 1969, § 17-5; Code 1981, § 23-24)
Sec. 90-24. - Hiring only drivers of good character.
For the protection of the general public, the owner of taxicabs shall employ only operators who are sober and careful drivers.
(Code 1969, § 17-7; Code 1981, § 23-25)
Sec. 90-25. - Taxicab driver's permit—Chauffeur's license required; permit fee; issuance of temporary permit.
All persons desiring to drive a taxicab in the city shall first obtain a state chauffeur's license; and shall make application for a permit to the city clerk, who, upon receiving a fee from the applicant and upon a recommendation of the chief of police shall issue a temporary permit to the applicant. The amount of the fee shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(Code 1969, § 17-8; Code 1981, § 23-26)
Sec. 90-26. - Same—Persons not entitled to temporary permit; issuance of annual permit.
The temporary permit issued by the city clerk shall be valid from date of issue until the date of the next regular meeting of the city council. The applicant must, on the date of the next regular meeting of the council, appear before and be approved by the council. Upon approval by the council the applicant will be issued a permit valid for one year from date.
(Code 1969, § 17-9; Code 1981, § 23-27)
Sec. 90-27. - Same—Revocation for violation of law.
Any permit issued to drive a taxicab whether temporary or annual, shall be revocable upon any violation of any law or ordinance of the city or state, traffic or otherwise.
(Code 1969, § 17-10; Code 1981, § 23-28)
Sec. 90-28. - Transporting alcoholic beverages.
It shall be unlawful for any operator of a taxicab licensed to do business in the city to have or transport any alcoholic beverages in the taxicab which such person operates.
(Code 1969, § 17-11; Code 1981, § 23-29)
Sec. 90-29. - Transporting prostitutes.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or operator of a taxicab to transport prostitutes therein.
(Code 1969, § 17-12; Code 1981, § 23-30)
