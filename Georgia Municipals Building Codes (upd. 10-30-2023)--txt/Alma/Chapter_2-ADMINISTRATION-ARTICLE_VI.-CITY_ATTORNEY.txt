ARTICLE VI. - CITY ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-213. - Prosecuting and defending suits.
The attorney shall prosecute or defend all suits and actions at law or equity to which the city may be a party, or in which it may be interested, or which may be brought against, or by, any officer of the city on behalf of the city, or in the capacity of such person as an officer of the city. Acting with the advice and consent of the mayor and council, the city attorney shall also have the right to associate other attorneys as the city attorney may deem proper to assist in legal matters affecting the city. The city attorney shall be appointed annually by the mayor and council.
(Code 1981, § 2-90; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § b)
Sec. 2-214. - Enforcing judgments.
It shall be the duty of the city attorney to see to the full enforcement of all judgments or decrees rendered or entered in favor of the city, and of all interlocutory orders.
(Code 1981, § 2-91; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § c)
Sec. 2-215. - Advising officers; attending council meetings.
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor of the city, and shall render advice on all legal questions affecting the city, whenever requested to do so by any city official. Upon request by the mayor or by the city council, the city attorney shall reduce any such opinion to writing. The city attorney shall attend all regular meetings of the council and such special meetings as requested by the mayor or council and shall keep them informed as to the legal matters affecting the city and shall recommend to the council such actions on these matters as may be necessary and expedient for the welfare of the city.
(Code 1981, § 2-92; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § d)
Sec. 2-216. - Supervising special assessment, bond and condemnation proceedings.
It shall be the duty of the city attorney to supervise and to see to the completion of all special assessment proceedings, bond issue proceedings and condemnation proceedings.
(Code 1981, § 2-93; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § e)
Sec. 2-217. - Drafting documents.
It shall be the duty of the city attorney to draft or supervise the phraseology of any contract, lease or other document or instrument, to which the city may be a party; and upon request of the city council, to draft ordinances covering any subjects within the power of the city.
(Code 1981, § 2-94; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § f)
Sec. 2-218. - Compensation.
The city attorney shall receive for such officer's services such retainer fee as the council may authorize. Such fee shall be full compensation for attendance at all regularly held meetings and advisory services at said meetings. For any service not covered by the retainer, the city attorney shall receive such compensation as may be authorized by the city council.
(Code 1981, § 2-95; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § g)
Sec. 2-219. - Supervising legislation.
It shall be the duty of the city attorney to supervise the enactment and passage of ordinances and resolutions and to see that said enactment and passage are in compliance and conformity with the law.
(Code 1981, § 2-96; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § h)
Sec. 2-220. - Membership in city attorneys' association.
Immediately upon appointment of a city attorney, the city manager shall proceed to secure membership in the state city attorneys' association for the city attorney. The city shall bear the cost of said membership.
(Code 1981, § 2-97; Ord. of 8-19-1974, § i)
Secs. 2-221—2-248. - Reserved.
