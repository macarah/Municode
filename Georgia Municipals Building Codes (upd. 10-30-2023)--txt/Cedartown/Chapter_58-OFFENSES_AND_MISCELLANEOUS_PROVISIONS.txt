Sec. 58-1. - Resisting police officer.
It shall be unlawful for any person to resist, obstruct or oppose the chief of police or any police officer, in making or attempting to make an arrest of any person in this city, or in serving or attempting to serve any legal process or order issued by the authorities of the city. It shall also be unlawful and a violation of this code section for any person to approach or stay within thirty (30) feet of an arresting officer, after said officer has lawfully placed any other person under arrest, and after the officer has given a warning to stay away from the immediate area of the arrestee.
(Code 1969, § 15-1; Ord. No. 8-1998, § 1, 3-9-98)
State Law reference— Obstructing or hindering law enforcement officers, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-24.
Sec. 58-2. - Escape.
It shall be unlawful for any person lawfully arrested by the authority of the city to escape from the arresting officer or break or escape from jail or a work gang.
(Code 1969, § 15-6)
State Law reference— Escape, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-52.
Sec. 58-3. - Interference with private property.
It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with, injure or deface public or private property in this city.
(Code 1969, § 15-30)
Sec. 58-4. - Defacing buildings; billposting.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to write, paint, draw or carve, or cut letters of any kind or words or devices or in any way mutilate or deface any church, school, private house, wall or fence, belonging to another in this city, or to post any bills on the same without the owner's consent.
(b)
It shall also be unlawful to mark, write, carve, scratch, or in any manner deface any part of the city hall or other public building in the city, or the furniture and fixtures therein contained, or in anyway disfigure or mutilate the same.
(Code 1969, § 15-32)
Sec. 58-5. - Littering public buildings.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drop or deposit on any floor, corridor, passageway, steps, porch, or portico, or any other part of any public building, office, grounds, park or other area, any fruit, nuts, meat, bread, grease, or other articles of food, litter, garbage, refuse or in anyway stain or discolor any part of any public building.
(Code 1969, § 15-33)
Sec. 58-6. - Selling, distributing merchandise, circulars, other matter in front of auditorium.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or give away any goods, wares or merchandise, or distribute any literature or circulars upon the street or sidewalk immediately in front of the auditorium, except with the express written consent of the chairman of the auditorium commission. It is declared that the purpose of this section is to prevent annoyance and obstruction to persons entering or leaving the auditorium, and the littering of the lobby of the building and the area immediately surrounding the auditorium.
(Code 1969, § 6½-6)
Sec. 58-7. - Defacing streets, sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to write, print or draw, with any material whatever, on the streets or sidewalks of the city.
(Code 1969, § 15-34)
Sec. 58-8. - Prohibited times for use of city parks, other recreation facilities; Northwest Park, Peek's Park and Turner Street Park excepted.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter, occupy or otherwise remain within any public park, recreation facility, cemetery, building or other similar municipal property within the corporate limits of the city between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. in the city. All such public facilities and parks shall be considered closed to any occupancy, use, loitering or other activity within the park during the hours from 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m.; provided, however, that such use between these hours may be permitted if advanced approval is obtained in writing from the city manager, recreation director or other designate of the city.
(b)
Further, there are additional restrictions and separate curfews for Northwest Park, Peek's Park and Turner Street Park, as set forth in section 58-8.1. Strict enforcement and adherence to these curfews, and the potential for multiple violations thereof, are also more specifically defined in section 58-8.2 of this Code.
(Code 1969, § 15-37; Ord. No. 9-2002, § 1, 4-8-02; Ord. No. 3,2006, § 1, 2-13-06)
Sec. 58-8.1. - Northwest Park, Peek's Park and Turner Street Park curfew.
A mandatory curfew is hereby established within the confines of Northwest Park, Peek's Park and Turner Street Park. This curfew is 30 minutes after sunset (which for purposes of this section shall be considered twilight or the end of daylight hours) until dawn (the beginning of daylight hours). No person shall have the right to be within, remain, loiter, or otherwise congregate individually, or in groups, within these parks during the prohibited curfew hours unless that person is a part of an organized group which has been granted permission by the city, through its authority, to be and remain after hours within these parks. The leader of any group within these parks by permission shall maintain a permission slip, permit, or other document evidencing the group's lawful right of assembly, meetings, or use of park facilities and shall promptly, upon request, identify all parties who are a part of the group function.
(Ord. No. 9-2002, § 2, 4-8-02; Ord. No. 3,2006, § 2, 2-13-06)
Sec. 58-8.2. - Violation of Northwest Park, Peek's Park and Turner Street Park curfew.
Any person found in violation of the curfews established herein shall be subject to the penalties contained in the general penalty provisions of section 1-13 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Cedartown. As requested by the citizens of the community, the penalty section promulgated hereunder is established with significant penalties, as the court, in its discretion, (including incarceration, and/or fines and/or probation), may determine to impose. Further, the curfew violation offense of this section, shall be separate and apart from any offense of loitering, malicious mischief, or other violations which may occur when conduct is being manifested, to authorize charges for multiple-offense violators.
(Ord. No. 9-2002, § 3, 4-8-02; Ord. No. 3,2006, § 3, 2-13-06)
Sec. 58-8.3 - Peek's Park walking path rules and regulations.
The walking path at Peek's Park is designated as a "walking path only" from daylight until 11:00 a.m. each day. During those times, it shall be unlawful for any person to bicycle, skate, skateboard, or rollerblade on the concrete pathway. After 11:00 a.m. each day, these nonmotorized recreational devices are allowable; however, users of these recreational items must conduct themselves in a safe manner and yield to pedestrians at all times. Operation of any motorized vehicle or any other powered, wheeled device on the walking path is prohibited at all times, other than those designed for handicap mobility.
(Ord. No. 4-2003, § 1, 8-11-03)
Sec. 58-8.4 - Violation of Peek's Park walking path rules and regulations.
Any person found in violation of the rules and regulations contained in Code section 58-8.3 shall be subject to the penalties contained in the general penalty provisions of section 1-13 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 4-2003, § 1, 8-11-03)
Sec. 58-8.5. - Silver Comet Trail rules and regulations.
The Silver Comet Trail is designated as a "bicycle and walking path only" to be used only during daylight hours. Operation of any motorized vehicle or any other powered, wheeled device on the Silver Comet Trail is prohibited at all times, other than those designed for handicap mobility.
(Ord. No. 7-2005, § 1, 6-13-05)
Sec. 58-8.6. - Violation of Silver Comet Trail rules and regulations.
Any person found in violation of the rules and regulations contained in Code section 58-8.5 shall be subject to the penalties contained in the general penalty provisions of section 1-13 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 7-2005, §; 1, 6-13-05)
Sec. 58-9. - Smoking prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to inhale, exhale, burn, or carry any lighted cigar, cigarette, pipe, or any similar combustible substance in any form in any public buildings of the city, or in any indoor or outdoor parks and recreational facilities located within the city, including, but not limited to any portion of the Cedartown Municipal Complex, or any restrooms, athletic fields, parks, walking trails, playgrounds, and spectator and concession areas, unless there is a designated smoking area established by the recreation director.
(b)
Designated smoking areas may be established by the recreation director or the parks superintendent which should be more than 100 feet from a playing field, gymnasium, playground, or other program site or recreation area. Such areas may be properly marked by a sign containing the words "designated smoking area."
(c)
Any person convicted of violating this section shall be fined $100.00.
(Code 1969, § 6½-5; Ord. No. 17-1994, § 4, 12-12-94; Ord. No. 13,2010, § 1, 8-9-10)
Sec. 58-10. - Adult magazines, books and material; public sale.
It shall be unlawful for any person doing business in the city to openly and by purposes of any type of display, rack or other prominent merchandising devise, place in public view within any business establishment adult books, literature, magazines, or other such material. For purposes of this section, "adult books" shall be defined as any printed and/or pictorial work which appeals to a prurient interest in sex, is patently offensive according to contemporary community standards, and has no serious literary, artistic, political or scientific value. All such works shall be kept behind the counter, checkout area, or some other general area under control of management of the business establishment, so that these adult books are not openly displayed and accessible to the general public and may not be openly viewed by minors.
(Code 1969, § 15-49.1; Ord. No. 2-1993, § 3, 2-8-93)
Cross reference— Adult entertainment, § 10-36 et seq.
Sec. 58-11. - Discharging firearms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge any firearms, such as pistols, shotguns, rifles, machine guns, dynamite caps or other explosives within the corporate limits of the city unless:
(1)
In defense of person or habitation.
(2)
By an officer engaged in the discharge of his official duties.
(3)
By persons engaged in military duties.
(4)
With permission of the city manager.
(5)
Reserved.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge a BB, pellet pistol, paintball guns or similar type firearm from or into any public property, including but not limited to, city parks, streets or alleys. Also, it shall be unlawful for any person to discharge a BB, pellet pistol or other firearm from or into the property of another person without the expressed permission of the property owner.
(Code 1969, § 15-55; Ord. No. 16-2000, § 1, 12-11-00; Ord. No. 13, 2006, § 1, 10-9-06)
State Law reference— Discharge of gun or pistol near public highway or street, O.C.G.A. § 26-11-103; discharge of firearms on property of another, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-104; discharge of firearm on Sunday, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-105; Georgia Firearms and Weapons Act, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-120 et seq.; regulation of blasting operations, O.C.G.A. § 25-8-1 et seq.; regulation of fireworks, O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1 et seq.
Sec. 58-12. - Fireworks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to offer for sale, expose for sale, sell, use, ignite, explode or possess any fireworks, as described below, within the corporate limits of the city. The term "fireworks" shall mean and include any combustible or explosive composition, or any substance, or combination of substances, or any article prepared for the purpose of producing a visible or an audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration or detonation, and shall include firecrackers, torpedoes, skyrockets, Roman candles, dago bombs, sparklers, or other fireworks of like construction and any fireworks containing any explosive or flammable compound; except that the term "fireworks" shall not include any toy pistol paper caps or any device using such paper caps.
(Code 1969, § 15-56)
Sec. 58-13. - Obstructions in street, sidewalk, sewer.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place in any street or sidewalk or open sewer, shavings, refuse material or any obstruction whatever.
(Code 1969, § 15-58)
Sec. 58-14. - Loitering.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any person or group of persons to remain idle, stand idly around, linger or behave in a dilatory fashion in, on or about any public street, alley, park, playground, building or any privately-owned residence, or place of business, if such person or group of persons:
(1)
Is creating a hindrance to vehicular or foot traffic on the streets or sidewalks or in building entrances;
(2)
Is unable to give a proper accounting of themselves or refuse to identify themselves;
(3)
Is engaged in disorderly, disruptive, obscene or other obnoxious conduct; or
(4)
Would warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the general health, safety or welfare of persons or property in the immediate vicinity.
(b)
Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be punished, upon conviction, as provided in section 1-13.
(Code 1969, § 15-73; Ord. No. 5-1997, § I, 4-14-97)

Sec. 58-15. - Disturbing meetings, assemblies.
It shall be unlawful for any person to disturb any meeting lawfully assembled in this city, or for any person to remain or loiter in front of any theater, concert hall, ballroom, etc., during any performance therein, after having been directed by any officer or member of the police force to move away therefrom.
(Code 1969, § 15-75)
State Law reference— Preventing or disrupting lawful meetings, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-34.
Sec. 58-16. - Wearing of masks, hoods.
(a)
Statement of purpose. The city commission hereby expressly declares that public appearances, whether in motor vehicles or otherwise, of persons who are masked or hooded and unidentifiable, threaten the supremacy of the law and cannot be permitted in the city. The city commission herein exercises its police power to protect its citizens from intimidation, the public from crime by masked and hooded persons, and to give to the police fullest opportunity to detect, apprehend, and bring to justice violators of the law.
(b)
Definitions. For the purpose of this section:
(1)
The term "mask" includes any mask, device or hood whereby any portion of the face is so hidden or covered as to conceal the identity of the wearer.
(2)
The term "public place" includes all walks, alleys, streets, boulevards, avenues, lanes, roads, highways or other ways or thoroughfares dedicated to public use or owned or maintained by public authority; all buildings owned, leased or operated for the use of organizations enjoying any tax exempt privilege as a charitable use.
(c)
Prohibition. No person, while masked, shall be or appear on or in any public place in the city.
(d)
Exceptions. The following are exempted from the provisions of this section:
(1)
All workers while engaged in a work wherein a covering is worn for physical safety and protection against occupational hazards or because of the nature of the occupation, trade or profession.
(2)
Persons while wearing traditional holiday costumes.
(3)
Persons while engaged in theatrical productions or masquerade balls.
(4)
Persons wearing gas masks prescribed in civil defense drills and exercises or emergencies.
(Code 1969, § 15-83)
State Law reference— Wearing mask, hood or device which conceals identity of wearer, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-38.
Sec. 58-17. - Disorderly houses.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any person to live in, keep, maintain, operate or rent a house or other residence that is loud, boisterous, disorderly or otherwise creates a general common disturbance to the surrounding neighborhood or orderly citizens in the immediate vicinity of such house. A house shall be deemed "disorderly" within the meaning of this section if the noise or other disturbances created therein are regularly recurring, continuous, habitual or customary.
(b)
It shall also be unlawful and a violation of this section for any person to keep, maintain, operate or rent a house or other residence in which illegal drugs, other controlled substances and/or alcohol are freely available for sale or otherwise; or in which other lewd conduct, including, but not limited to, gambling, prostitution, adultery or fornication, is habitually or customarily carried on, and which creates a general disturbance to the neighborhood or immediate vicinity of such house or residence.
(c)
Any person found guilty of a violation of this section shall be punished, upon conviction, as provided in section 1-13.
(Ord. No. 6-1997, § 1, 4-14-97)
Sec. 58-18. - Public drunkenness.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any person to be and appear in an intoxicated condition in any public place or within the curtilage of any private residence that he does not own or have the permission of the lawful owner or occupant to be present, or to remain at the residence without permission; if said person's condition is manifested by boisterousness or unruliness, by an indecent condition or act, by vulgar, profane, loud, or unbecoming language, by conduct which creates a danger to himself or other citizens or the conduct is a hindrance to vehicular or foot traffic on the streets, or by any other irrational or erratic conduct.
(b)
Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-13 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 6-1998, § I, 2-9-98)
Sec. 58-19. - Loud music prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any person, within the corporate limits of the city, to play any music at an excessive volume, either in a public place or private location where it can reasonably be expected that other citizens will be disturbed. This shall include, but shall not be limited to, loud music emanating from automobile stereos, portable stereos, or other similar noise devices in cars, homes, parks or other places if this equipment is located in a place that is open to the public and causes disturbance or annoyance to adjoining property owners or other citizens in the immediate area.
(b)
Any person found guilty of violating this Code section shall be punished as provided in section 1-13 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 9-1998, § 1, 3-9-98)
Sec. 58-20. - Prosecution of state offenses.
Any municipal law enforcement officer shall have the right, pursuant to general arrest powers granted under the laws of the state, or the Charter [or Code] of Ordinances of the city, to arrest all persons for violation of the criminal laws of this state or violation of any ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 8-1998, § 2, 3-9-98; Ord. No. 15-1998, § 1, 4-13-98)
Sec. 58-21. - Gambling.
(a)
A person commits the offense of gambling when he:
(1)
Makes a bet upon the partial or final result of any game or contest or upon the performance of any participant in such game or contest;
(2)
Makes a bet upon the result of any political nomination, appointment, or election or upon the degree of success of any nominee, appointee, or candidate; or
(3)
Plays and bets for money or other thing of value at any game placed with cards, dice, or balls.
(b)
A person who commits the offense of gambling shall be punished as provided in section 1-13 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 8-1998, § 4, 3-9-98; Ord. No. 15-1998, § 1, 4-13-98)
Sec. 58-22. - Keeping a gambling place prohibited.
(a)
A person who knowingly permits any real estate, building, room, tent, vehicle, boat, or other property whatsoever owned by him or under his control to be used as a gambling place or who rents or lets any such property with a view or expectation that it be so used commits the offense of keeping a gambling place.
(b)
A person who commits the offense of keeping a gambling place shall be punished as provided in section 1-13 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 8-1998, § 5, 3-9-98; Ord. No. 15-1998, § 1, 4-13-98)
Sec. 58-23. - Public indecency.
(a)
A person commits the offense of public indecency when he or she performs any of the following acts in a public place:
(1)
An act of sexual intercourse;
(2)
A lewd exposure of the sexual organs;
(3)
A lewd appearance in a state of partial or complete nudity; or
(4)
A lewd caress or indecent fondling of the body of another person.
(b)
A person convicted of the offense of public indecency as provided in subsection (a) of this Code section shall be punished as provided in section 1-13 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 8-1998, § 6, 3-9-98; Ord. No. 15-1998, § 1, 4-13-98)
Sec. 58-24. - Open containers.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this Code section for any person to possess in any public place within the corporate limits of the city any open container containing an alcoholic beverage. For purposes of this Code section, the term "open container" shall mean any container which is immediately capable of being consumed from, or the seal of which has been broken, including but not limited to, bottle, cans, glasses, pitchers, cups, and similar containers.
(b)
This Code section shall not apply to customers or patrons in restaurants, taverns, or any other establishment that has a valid license or permit from the city for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink.
(Ord. No. 8-1998, § 7, 3-9-98; Ord. No. 15-1998, § 1, 4-13-98)
Sec. 58-25. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
Any person who shall do or engage in any of the following within the corporate limits of the city shall be guilty of disorderly conduct:
(1)
Any person who shall act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby any person is placed in danger or safety of his life, limb, or health.
(2)
Any person who shall act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby public property or property of any other person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged.
(3)
Any person who shall endanger lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence or threats of bodily harm.
(4)
Any person who shall cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health, or property of another or public property.
(5)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or other and cause, provoke or engage in any fight or brawl.
(6)
Any person who shall collect in bodies or in crowds and engage in unlawful activities.
(7)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or others and engage or attempt to engage in gaming.
(8)
Any person who shall frequent any public place and obtain money for another by an illegal and fraudulent scheme, trick, artifice or device, or attempt to do so.
(9)
Any person who assembles with another or others and engages in any fraudulent scheme, device, or trick to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person, or attempts to do so.
(10)
Any person who utters, in a public place or any place open to the public, any obscene words or epithets.
(11)
Any person who frequents any place where gaming or the illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed or tolerated.
(12)
Any person who shall use "fighting words" directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates turmoil.
(13)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or others and do bodily harm to another.
(14)
Any person who shall, by acts of violence, interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation.
(15)
Any person who shall congregate with another or other in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuses to clear such public way when ordered to do so by a peace officer or other person having authority.
(16)
Any person who damages, defiles, or disturbs public property or the property of another so as to create a hazardous, unhealthy, or physically offensive condition.
(17)
Any person who in any way interferes with any police officer or other arresting officer, while arresting or attempting to arrest any person for a violation of any ordinance of the county or penal laws of the state, or who interferes with the officers in any way in the discharge of any duty incumbent on them as such officers.
(18)
Any person, who by and in any manner, creates loud noises which disrupt, disturb, or otherwise interfere with the peace and tranquility of the public.
(19)
Any person who after having been directed by a police officer to move away therefrom, shall remain or loiter in front of any church or other place of public worship during services therein, or in front of any theater, concert hall, or in front of any business house in the county, or shall obstruct any public street, public highway, public sidewalk or any other public place or building by hindering or impeding or tending to hinder or impede the free and uninterrupted passage of vehicles, traffic or pedestrians.
(20)
Any person who fails or refuses to remove himself from the private property of another when requested to do so by the owner or occupant(s) thereof.
(21)
Any person who recklessly or knowingly throws or propels any stone, brick, piece of wood, or other object at any person, vehicle, house, building or other structure or object, or across or upon any private or public property.
(22)
Any person who defecates or urinates on or adjacent to any street or sidewalk, or in the halls, elevators, stairways, or any other area designated for public passage within any public or commercial buildings, or on any property open to the public view.
(23)
Any person who knowingly and willfully harasses or attempts to harass or mislead any police officer by false alarms or unauthorized use of any device of whatever nature to summon police aid without reasonable cause.
(24)
Any person who shall enter or remain in any public, private or parochial school building, who is not a regularly enrolled student, teacher or employee at that school, unless the person shall have first and immediately proceeded to the administrative or principal's agent and received permission to remain on the premises.
(25)
Any person who shall enter and remain in any public, private, or parochial school or on the surrounding school grounds after being directed to leave by the principal of the school or by someone with lawful authority.
(26)
Any person who shall create a disturbance in any public, private or parochial school or on the surrounding school grounds lawfully used for school activities while such recreational areas are in use or other activities are in progress thereon.
(b)
Any person or persons failing to comply with the mandatory provisions of this section or doing any act prohibited by this section shall be guilty of an offense and upon conviction in Municipal Court of Cedartown, Georgia, shall be punished by a fine in an amount not to exceed $1,000.00 and/or imprisonment for a period not in excess of 60 days.
(Ord. No. 8-1998, § 8, 3-9-98; Ord. No. 15-1998, § 1, 4-13-98; Ord. No. 5-2002, § 1, 3-11-02)

Sec. 58-28. - General prohibition against alcoholic beverages on city property; exceptions.
modified
(a)
Definitions. For purposes of this section:
(1)
Food caterer means any person who prepares food for consumption off the premises and has a license to do so.
(2)
Licensed alcoholic beverage caterer means any retail dealer who has been licensed pursuant to applicable city ordinances and O.C.G.A. § 3-11-2.
(3)
Private function means any social gathering, reception, meeting, or similar function organized in connection with a central cause or purpose for which attendees are present only upon personal and individual invitation and is not open to the public or by open invitation and for which there is no fee or any other consideration as a condition of attendance. Private function is intended to mean wedding receptions, corporate meetings/receptions, family and class reunions and other similar uses.
(4)
Premises means the interior of an approved city owned building and a perimeter equal to 20 feet around the building.
(5)
Other definitions pertaining to the consumption of alcoholic beverages, as defined in the City Code of Ordinances, shall be equally applicable to this section except where otherwise indicated.
(b)
No person, firm, or corporation that rents, occupies, or otherwise uses any city-owned and/or -operated facility, park, building or other general property area of the city, shall possess, use, consume, or make available for consumption any alcoholic beverages at any time on city property; either before, during or after any activity for which the property is being used. Further, no such party may otherwise be in possession of any alcoholic beverages while attending, or otherwise remaining upon any city property. The only limited exceptions to this general prohibition are contained in subsections (c) and (d) hereof.
(c)
The Cedartown Auditorium Commission, the Downtown Cedartown Association, and/or the Polk County Historical Society, Inc., may host or sponsor events at either the Cedartown Auditorium, the Cedartown Depot facility, the Hawkes Children's Library, and/or the former First National Bank building on West Avenue, where alcoholic beverages may be served, provided that said service shall be on the following limited basis:
(1)
The event must be sponsored and/or hosted for the nonprofit benefit of the Cedartown Auditorium Commission, the Downtown Cedartown Association, and/or the Polk County Historical Society, Inc.;
(2)
The event must only allow the use of duly licensed and/or experienced bartenders under the direction and supervision of license alcoholic beverage caterer or similar responsible and insured parties;
(3)
All net proceeds from any event sponsored by the auditorium commission and for which the auditorium serves as alcoholic beverage caterer shall be paid over to the auditorium commission. All net proceeds from any event sponsored by the city shall be paid over to the city;
(4)
Alcoholic beverages may be served beginning 90 minutes before an event or performance at the Cedartown Auditorium for which the auditorium commission serves as sponsor, and such sales may continue until either the end of the second intermission, or 30 minutes prior to the end of said event or performance, if there is only one intermission;
(5)
The responsible party shall ensure that all caterers, servers, and attendees adhere to all laws and ordinances involving the serving of any alcoholic beverages during any such function;
(6)
The city, through its auditorium commission, Downtown Cedartown Association, and/or the Polk County Historical Society, Inc., reserves the right to promulgate and enforce other reasonable appropriate requirements based upon the actual event being conducted. As an example if the function involves a "wine tasting" then no other form of alcoholic beverages would be served; or if it is in the best interest of the function to have any social hour or time of serving alcoholic beverages, limited to any specific time, then at the end of that time period service of alcoholic beverages shall be totally and completely concluded. Sec. 58-29. - Malfunctioning alarm systems.
(a)
Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
False alarm means a fire alarm signal or police signal from a privately owned fire or police signal device or system that is caused or created by anything other than an actual fire, burglary or robbery, in the premises where the device or system is located. Provided, however, that a false alarm shall not be construed to mean alarm signals caused by no fault of the user and caused by no apparent malfunction and also excluding an alarm signal caused by violent conditions of nature, such as, but not limited to lightning, tornadoes, or other extraordinary circumstances not reasonable subject to control by the alarm business or user.
False alarm service charge means an administrative fee imposed by the city to defray the needless expense to the city for responding to false alarms and the services received that other businesses and the citizenry do not receive. The amount of the service charge shall be as approved by the City of Cedartown Commission in the schedule of fees and charges.
Fire alarm signal means a signal indicating an emergency requiring immediate action of the fire department, including, but not limited to, an alarm from a manually operated fire alarm box, a water flow alarm, or an alarm from an automatic fire or smoke detection system or device.
Fire equipment trouble alarm means an alarm indicating some form of malfunction or trouble with the fire protection or fire detecting system or device being monitored. Such trouble alarm does not indicate the need for emergency fire department response to the site, but only that the person responsible for maintenance of the system being monitored is to be notified of the trouble alarm signal.
Police signal or burglar alarm signal means a signal from a manually operated or automatic device that indicates an emergency requiring immediate response by the police department.
(b)
Purpose. It is the purpose of this section to establish requirements for the monitoring and receiving of fire and burglar alarm signals, and fire equipment trouble alarms at the communications office of the fire department and the police department.
(c)
Connections. No signal other than a fire alarm signal or a fire equipment trouble alarm shall be connected to any alarm signal receiving equipment in the fire department. No signal other than a burglar, robbery or intrusion alarm shall be connected to any alarm signal receiving equipment in the police department.
(d)
Automatic dialers. It shall be unlawful to key automatic dialer fire or burglar alarms to the city emergency number or the city switchboard. Such alarms shall be keyed to a private service that can then notify the police or fire department.
(e)
Automatic cutoff system. All audible alarms, signals, bells or horns shall have an automatic cutoff system so that such alarm shall not operate for more than 20 continuous minutes for commercial alarms or ten continuous minutes for residential alarms.
(f)
Transmission of false alarms. The transmission of false alarms to the fire department or the police department in excess of the allowable number of such alarms as contained in the service charge schedule shall authorize the imposition of a false alarm service charge.
(g)
Accounting of false alarms. The police and fire departments shall maintain an accounting of false alarms on a monthly basis. Each calendar month shall constitute a new accounting period for determining the imposition of false alarm service charges.
(h)
Key holder must live near system. It shall be unlawful to connect a system to the police or fire department's receiving equipment without a key holder living within a 15-mile radius of the premises where the device or system is located, in order to cut off the alarm and attend to matters arising from the alarm.
(i)
Service charge rates. Service charge rates for alarm systems connected pursuant to this chapter shall be as follows:
(1)
For the first violation of this section at any premises in a month, there shall be no charge.
(2)
For the second violation of this section at any premises in a month, there shall be a fine as approved by the City of Cedartown Commission in the schedule of fees and charges.
(3)
For the third violation of this section at any premises in a month, there shall be a fine as approved by the City of Cedartown Commission in the schedule of fees and charges
(4)
For the fourth and any subsequent violations of this section at any premises in a month, there shall be a fine as approved by the City of Cedartown Commission in the schedule of fees and charges.
(5)
Service charges are not applicable to state, local or federal government agencies.
(j)
Termination of service. Service will be terminated after five false alarms in any calendar month. Service will be reinstated only after appropriate action has been taken to correct user deficiencies and the user has received approval from the appropriate chief.

Sec. 58-30.1. - Curfew for minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person 17 years of age or younger to idle, loiter or congregate with any other persons on or in the public streets, sidewalks or other public places within the city between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 5:00 a.m., Sunday through Thursday evening and between the hours of 11:30 p.m. and 5:00 a.m. Friday and Saturday. Provided that the provisions of this section shall not apply to a minor accompanied by his parent, legal guardian or other adult person having legal care and custody of the minor or where the minor is on an emergency errand or legitimate business, such as, but not limited to, returning home from work or from school-sponsored activities, and directed by his parent, guardian or other adult person having the legal care and custody of the minor.
(Ord. No. 5-2009, § 1, 3-3-09)
Sec. 58-30.2. - Parental responsibility.
It shall be unlawful for any parent, guardian or other person having custody or control of any minor 17 years of age or younger to permit, allow or encourage such minor to violate section 58-30.1. A person charged with violation of this section for the first time shall be given a warning citation. Upon subsequent violation, the person shall be charged with such violation and, if found guilty, such person shall be fined up to an amount as approved by the City of Cedartown Commission in the schedule of fees and charges. Upon the third and subsequent violations, such person shall be fined up to an amount as approved by the City of Cedartown Commission in the schedule of fees and charges per violation and the Georgia Department of Family and Children Services (DFACS) will be notified.
(Ord. No. 5-2009, § 2, 3-3-09; Ord. No. 7-2019 , § 2(Exh. A), 12-9-19)
Sec. 58-30.3. - Penalties for minors.
Any minor charged with violation of section 58-30.1 for the first time shall be escorted to the police department where they shall be given a warning and released only to a parent or legal guardian. Upon subsequent violation, any minor under the age of 17 who violates section 58-30.1 shall be subject to the jurisdiction of the juvenile court. Any minor 17 years of age who violates subsection 58-30.1 shall be subject to the jurisdiction of the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 5-2009, § 3, 3-3-09)
Sec. 58-31. - Aggressive panhandling prohibited.
(a)
As used in this section, the following terms shall have the following meanings:
After dark means any time from 30 minutes after sunset to 30 minutes before sunrise.
Aggressive panhandling means:
(1)
Approaching or speaking to a person, or following a person before, during or after soliciting, in a manner that would cause a reasonable person to fear bodily harm to oneself, or damage to or loss of property in one's immediate possession;
(2)
Soliciting from a person who is waiting in line for entry to a building or for another purpose;
(3)
Soliciting from a person who is eating, drinking, dining or sitting at a table in an outdoor dining area;
(4)
Continuing to solicit from a person after the person has given a negative response to an initial solicitation;
(5)
Intentionally touching or causing physical contact with another person without that person's consent in the course of soliciting;
(6)
Intentionally blocking or interfering with the safe or free passage of a pedestrian or vehicle by any means, including unreasonably causing a pedestrian or vehicle operator to take evasive action to avoid physical contact with the person soliciting; or
(7)
Using violent or threatening gestures toward a person being solicited; or speaking in an unreasonably loud volume under the circumstances or using profane or abusive language, either during a solicitation or after a refusal.
Automated teller machine means a device, linked to a financial institution's account records, which is able to carry out transactions, including, but not limited to: account transfers, deposits, cash withdrawals, balance inquiries, and mortgage and loan payments.
Outdoor dining area means an outdoor dining area of a restaurant or other establishment serving food for immediate consumption.
Public place means streets, highways, and roadways (including shoulders and medians), sidewalks, alleys, parking lots, plazas, parks, playgrounds, schools, transportation facilities and other public property, including city-owned property and any other property where public gatherings occur on a regular basis and/or where public meetings are conducted, such as schools, libraries, and places of worship.
Soliciting means asking in person for money or objects of value, with the intention that the money or object to be transferred at that time, and at that place. Soliciting shall include using the spoken, written, or printed word, bodily gestures, signs, or other means with the purpose of obtaining an immediate donation of money or other thing of value or soliciting the sale of goods or services.
(1)
Prohibited acts. It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to violate any of the following subsections of this section:
a.
No person shall engage in aggressive panhandling in any public place.
b.
No person shall solicit on private property if the owner or other person lawfully in possession of such property has notified the person that they are not allowed to solicit on the property.
c.
No person shall solicit within 20 feet of any automated teller machine or an entrance or exit of any bank or financial institution.
d.
No person shall solicit an operator or passenger of a motor vehicle while such persons are operating or occupying a motor vehicle; provided, however, that this paragraph shall not apply to persons soliciting other occupants of a vehicle in which the solicitor is lawfully traveling or to services rendered in connection with emergency repairs requested by the operator or passengers of such vehicle.
e.
No person shall solicit while under the influence of alcohol or a controlled substance.
f.
No person shall solicit in a group of two or more persons.
g.
No person shall solicit within six feet of an entrance to a building.
h.
No person shall solicit within 20 feet of an outdoor dining area.
i.
No person shall solicit in any public place after dark.
(b)
Penalties. Any person guilty of a violation of any of the provisions of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-13.
(Ord. No. 4-2014, §§ 2, 3, 8-11-14)
Sec. 58-32. - Abandoned shopping carts.
(a)
Definition. The term "shopping cart" when used in this section means a push cart of the type commonly provided by grocery stores, drugstores, or other merchants for the use of the public in transporting commodities inside and outside of stores.
(b)
Marking. Owners must clearly mark all shopping carts with the name of the owner.
(c)
Removal prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove a shopping cart from the premises of the owner of such shopping cart without the written consent of the owner or of his agent, servant, or employee given at the time of such removal. For the purpose of this section, the premises shall include the entire parking area set aside by the owner, or on the behalf of the owner, for the parking of cars.
(d)
Abandonment prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to leave or abandon any shopping cart on any sidewalk, right-of-way, street, or other property within the city other than the property of the owner of the shopping cart.
(e)
Confiscation. If a shopping cart is found on any property other than the premises of the owner of the shopping cart, the city may retrieve the cart and remove it to a storage location. Any shopping cart impounded by the city under this section may be reclaimed by the owner thereof by submitting evidence, satisfactory to the city, of ownership. Any shopping cart impounded under this section which has not been reclaimed by an owner as herein specified within 60 days shall be disposed of by the city in the same manner as other property seized by the city.
(f)
Penalty. Violators shall be punished as provided in section 1-13 of the Cedartown Code of Ordinances.
( Ord. No. 6-2018 , § 1, 8-13-18)
