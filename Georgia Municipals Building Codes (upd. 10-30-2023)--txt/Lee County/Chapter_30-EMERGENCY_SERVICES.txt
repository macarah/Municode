ARTICLE II. - ADVANCED LIFE SUPPORT

Sec. 30-31. - Requirements.
Every person providing ambulance service from origins within the area of the county shall be staffed and equipped to provide advanced life support (ALS) as presently defined in the rules of the state department of human resources public health, chapter 290-5-30.
(Ord. of 1-10-1997, § 1)
Sec. 30-32. - Penalty.
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall be deemed guilty of an offense and upon conviction in the magistrate court shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Ord. of 1-10-1997, § 2)
Secs. 30-33—30-50. - Reserved.

Editor's note— Ord. of 2-27-2018 repealed former Art. III, §§ 30-51—30-56, and enacted a new Art. III as set out herein. Former Art. III pertained to public safety department and derived from Ord. of 7-23-2013; Res. of 9-13-2016.

Sec. 30-51. - Establishment of the Lee County Fire and Emergency Services Department.
There is hereby established a "Lee County Fire and Emergency Services Department."
( Ord. of 2-27-2018 )
Sec. 30-52. - Department purposes and management.
The Lee County Fire and Emergency Services Department shall provide fire services and emergency medical services within the unincorporated area of Lee County. The department shall be under the management of the Chief of the Lee County Fire and Emergency Services Department (hereinafter "chief"). Each employee of the department shall be cross-trained as a fire fighter and an emergency medical services technician. The chief shall be authorized to establish reasonable time frames within which each employee of the department completes both firefighter certification training and emergency medical technician certification training.
( Ord. of 2-27-2018 )
Sec. 30-53. - Department budget.
The Lee County Fire and Emergency Services Department shall have its own budget within the Lee County budget adopted annually with such allocations as may be approved by the board of commissioners, and such budget shall be subject to amendment from time to time as may be deemed appropriate by the board of commissioners.
( Ord. of 2-27-2018 )
Sec. 30-54. - Chief of fire and emergency services department.
(a)
The Board of Commissioners of Lee County hereby establishes the position of chief of said department. Such position shall be included in the county's personnel regulations. Such position shall be a department head position within the county's personnel structure, and the employee employed as chief shall be paid such salary and benefits as may from time to time be established by the board of commissioners through its budgeting process.
(b)
The job description of such position shall be established in the county's personnel regulations and shall be subject to approval of the board of commissioners.
( Ord. of 2-27-2018 )
Sec. 30-55. - Other department management personnel.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall be authorized to establish the position of assistant chief-fire services within the county's personnel regulations. In the event that such position is established, such position shall exist within the county's personnel structure, and the person hired for such position shall be paid such salary and benefits as may from time to time be established by the board of commissioners through its budgeting process.
(b)
The job description of such position shall be established in the county's personnel regulations and shall be subject to approval of the board of commissioners.
( Ord. of 2-27-2018 ; Ord. of 11-10-2020 )
Sec. 30-56. - Duties of Chief of the Lee County Fire and Emergency Services Department.
The Chief of the Lee County Fire and Emergency Services Department shall, as one of his or her duties, establish departmental regulations so as to provide mandatory and ongoing training for employees of the department, including, but not limited to, inter-disciplinary training for employees of the department. Such departmental regulations shall be subject to approval by the board of commissioners. Further responsibilities of such chief shall include, but shall not be limited to, the development and management of budgets for the department and managing the day-to-day operations of the department. Both the assistant chief-fire services and the assistant chief-emergency medical services shall report directly to the chief of the department. The chief shall also have such other and further duties and responsibilities as may be provided in the county's personnel regulations or as prescribed by the board of commissioners from time.
( Ord. of 2-27-2018 )
Sec. 30-57. - Establishment of the E-911 emergency response department.
(a)
There is hereby established an "E-911 emergency response department."
(b)
The E-911 emergency response department shall provide E-911 emergency response services for Lee County. The department shall be under the management of a director of E-911 emergency response services (hereinafter "director.") The E-911 emergency response department shall have its own budget within the Lee County budget adopted annually with such allocations as may be approved by the board of commissioners, and such budget shall be subject to amendment from time to time as may be deemed appropriate by the board of commissioners.
(c)
The director of the E-911 emergency response department shall be an employee of Lee County, and the position shall be included in the county's personnel regulations. Such position shall be a department head position within the county's personnel structure, and the employee employed as director shall be paid such salary and benefits as may from time to time be established by the board of commissioners through its budgeting process. The job description of the director shall be established in the county's personnel regulations and shall be subject to approval by the board of commissioners.
( Ord. of 2-27-2018 )
