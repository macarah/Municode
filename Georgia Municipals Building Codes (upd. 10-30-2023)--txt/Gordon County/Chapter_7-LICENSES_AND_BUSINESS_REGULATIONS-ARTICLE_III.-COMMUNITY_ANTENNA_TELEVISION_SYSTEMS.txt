ARTICLE III. - COMMUNITY ANTENNA TELEVISION SYSTEMS[2]

Sec. 7-51. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Gordon County Community Antenna Television Franchise Ordinance."
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 1)
Sec. 7-52. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means any person or company authorized to do business in the state submitting an application for a community antenna television system in the county or who operates a CATV franchise in the county as of the effective date of this article.
CATV franchise means a community antenna television system franchise.
FCC means the Federal Communications Commission.
Gross subscriber revenues means all revenue derived directly or indirectly from all sources by a selected applicant(s) from its CATV system within the unincorporated areas of the county before any deductions or expenses whatsoever. Definition of gross subscriber revenues shall be expanded as may be allowed by future FCC rulings.
System means the lines, fixtures, equipment, attachments and all appurtenances thereto which are used in construction, operation and maintenance of a community antenna television system.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 2)
Sec. 7-53. - Nonexclusive franchise.
Each CATV franchise shall be nonexclusive, and the county may grant more than one (1) franchise for the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 3)
Sec. 7-54. - Application for franchise.
(a)
No CATV franchise shall be granted until the following conditions are met:
(1)
Notice shall be given to the board of commissioners of the intent of the applicant to apply for a CATV franchise.
(2)
All applications for a CATV franchise shall be in a form acceptable to the board which provides the information required herein.
(3)
All applications for a CATV franchise must be approved by the board.
(4)
If the applicant is not a corporation, its application shall contain the names and addresses of persons (including corporations) having a proprietary or equitable interest in its business operation and/or proposed CATV franchise sought by the applicant. The term "equitable interest" shall include all assignments for value, as well as all contingent assignments of any right or privilege under the proposed CATV franchise, and shall also include any benefit, payment or emolument whatsoever resulting from the grant of a CATV franchise.
(5)
If the applicant is a corporation not publicly held, as defined by the rules and regulations of the United States Securities and Exchange Commission, the statement shall furnish, additionally, the names and addresses of its officers, directors and shareholders, together with the number of shares held by each shareholder.
(6)
If the applicant is a corporation publicly held, as defined by the rules and regulations of the United States Securities and Exchange Commission, the statement shall contain the states in which the applicant is incorporated and/or qualified to do business and the names and addresses of its officers and directors.
(7)
The form of ownership of the facilities to be used in exercising the proposed CATV franchise.
(8)
Any cable television system currently owned or operated by the applicant, its experience in operating and constructing such systems, any applications pending for other systems, and the general financial resources of the applicant as well as those specifically available for construction and operation of the proposed CATV system.
(9)
Proposed services, including channel capacity, the number of channels to be included in basic services, the number of channels and types of services to be included in additional services, the number of two-way channels, the number of access channels and automated channels, facilities for local origination and a list of any additional services or facilities to be made available to the county and other public, institutional and community organizations.
(10)
A schedule of the subscriber rates for each of the proposed services.
(11)
The equipment to be included in the CATV system, the general routes of the feeder or trunk cable, the number and location of signal amplifiers to be used in redistributing signals, the proposed franchise area and the service schedule for the proposed franchise area.
(12)
The design and construction plans and standards to be used in constructing or renovating the CATV system and the proposed construction and service schedule.
(b)
All applications shall be open to public inspection during regular business hours.
(c)
Any material misrepresentation in an application or the omission of any material representation, which omission renders the included representations misleading, whether done negligently or intentionally, shall be grounds for rejection of the application or termination of any CATV franchise granted on the basis of such application.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 4)
Sec. 7-55. - Selection of applicants for negotiation.
The board of commissioners shall hold a public hearing to review the applications submitted under this article and after considering the applicant's character, financial, technical and other qualifications and the adequacy and feasibility of its construction arrangements, the board may negotiate with the applicant for the purpose of granting a nonexclusive CATV franchise.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 5)
Sec. 7-56. - Negotiation of proposed franchise.
Upon selection, the board of commissioners and the selected applicant shall proceed to negotiate and draft the terms and conditions of a CATV franchise which shall contain the minimum terms and conditions set out in the remainder of this article (provided a franchise is granted to the selected applicant).
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6)
Sec. 7-57. - Compliance with laws and regulations.
The selected applicant shall at all times during the life of this franchise be subject to all lawful exercise of the police power by the county and to such reasonable regulation as the county shall by ordinance provide. The construction, operation and maintenance of the system by the selected applicant shall be in full compliance with the National Electrical Code as from time to time amended and revised, and in full compliance with all other applicable rules and regulations now in effect or hereinafter adopted by the Federal Communications Commission, the county, the state and the United States government.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(I))
Sec. 7-58. - Liability and indemnification.
(a)
Liability coverage. It is expressly understood and agreed by and between the selected applicant and the county that the selected applicant shall save the county harmless from all loss sustained by the county on account of any suit, judgment, execution, claim or demand whatsoever arising out of the construction, operation and maintenance of the CATV system by the selected applicant. The selected applicant agrees to maintain and keep in full force and effect at all times during the term of the franchise sufficient liability insurance coverage to protect the county against any such claims, suits, judgments, executions or demands in a sum not less than five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00) per person in any one (1) claim, one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) as to any one (1) accident or occurrence, and not less than one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) for property damage as to any one (1) accident or occurrence, or in such larger sums on all coverage as may be required of the selected applicant by any other public utility in the county and shall provide the county with proof of such insurance.
(b)
Workers' compensation coverage. The selected applicant shall also maintain in full force and effect throughout the duration of its franchise sufficient workers' compensation insurance coverage to adequately and fully protect its agents and employees as required by law.
(c)
Resident company and agent. All insurance policies and bonds as are required by this section shall be written by a company or companies authorized and qualified to do business in the state. Certificates of all coverage required shall be promptly filed by the selected applicant with the county.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(II))
Sec. 7-59. - Conditions on street occupancy, system construction.
(a)
Use. All transmission and distribution structures, lines and equipment erected by the selected applicant within the county shall be so located as to cause minimum interference with the proper use of streets, alleys and other public ways and places and to cause minimum interference with the rights or reasonable convenience of property owners who adjoin any of such streets, alleys or other public ways and places.
(b)
Restoration. In case of any disturbance of pavement, sidewalks, driveways or other surfacing, the selected applicant shall have prior approval of the board of commissioners and shall, at its own expense and in a manner approved by the county, replace and restore such places so disturbed in as good condition as before the work commenced, and shall maintain the restoration in a condition approved by the county for the full period of one (1) year; and if the selected applicant fails to do so, the county may undertake such restoration at the expense of the selected applicant.
(c)
Relocation. If at any time during the period of the franchise the county shall lawfully select to alter or change the grade or alignment of any street, alley or other public way the selected applicant, upon reasonable notice by the county, shall remove, relay and relocate its equipment at its own expense.
(d)
Placement of fixtures. The selected applicant shall not place any fixtures or equipment where the same will interfere with any existing gas, electric, telephone or sewer and water lines, fixtures and equipment, and the locations by the selected applicant of its lines and equipment shall be in such a manner as to not interfere with the usual travel on the streets, alleys and public ways and the use of the same by gas, electric, telephone and water and sewer lines and equipment.
(e)
Temporary removal of wires for building moving. The selected applicant shall on the request of the county temporarily raise or lower its wires to permit the moving of buildings, but may reserve the right to recover its costs from the person or persons having the building.
(f)
No property right. Nothing in the franchise shall grant to the selected applicant any right of property in the county-owned property, nor shall the county be compelled to maintain any of its property any longer than, or in any fashion other than, in the county's judgment, its own business or needs may require.
(g)
Nonliability of county. The county shall not be liable for any damage occurring to the property of the selected applicant caused by employees of the county in the performance of their duties, nor shall the county be held liable for the interruption of service by actions of county employees in the performance of their duties, nor shall the county be held for the failure of the selected applicant to be able to perform normal service due to acts of God.
(h)
Permits, easements and agreements. The county shall not be required to assume any responsibility for the securing of any permits or agreements with other persons or utilities.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(III))
Cross reference— Roads and highways, Ch. 12.
Sec. 7-60. - Construction approval by county; correction of defects.
Except for additional connections for ten (10) residences or less, unless the same involves the cutting of a road, the selected applicant shall not erect any pole, run any line, make any attachment nor shall any construction of any kind be commenced without the prior approval of the board of commissioners. The approval as referenced herein shall be in the form of a permit issued by the board upon approval of:
(1)
Layout maps of the system authorized by the franchise showing plant routing, utility company poles to which the system facilities are to be attached and location of all trunk and distribution line amplifiers; or
(2)
A certification by applicant that all attachment requests and subsequent approvals required have been obtained.
The county shall have and maintain the right to inspect the construction, operation and maintenance of the system by the selected applicant to ensure the proper performance of the terms of the franchise. If the selected applicant should violate any of the terms of this article or any of the rules and regulations as may from time to time be lawfully adopted, the county shall immediately give the selected applicant thirty (30) days' written notice to correct such violation. If the selected applicant does not make the correction within thirty (30) days from the receipt of such written notice, the county may make such correction itself and charge the cost of same to the selected applicant.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(IV))
Sec. 7-61. - Number of channels.
(a)
The selected applicant shall maintain and relay to the subscribers to the system in the county not less than the number of channels provided to subscribers in the city from which the applicant originates transmission.
(b)
Carriage of television signals shall be consistent with those allowed by FCC rules. In addition to television signals allowed by the FCC signal carriage rules, the system shall provide one (1) twenty-four-hour local service channel with local weather information and public service messages.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(V))
Sec. 7-62. - Prohibition from engaging in radio and television sales, service and repair.
The selected applicant and any and all of its officers, agents and employees are specifically prohibited from engaging in the sale, service, rental or leasing of television receivers, radio receivers or television or radio receiver related parts and accessories with any person anywhere in the county, whether for a fee or charge, except for such special devices as may be necessary for the subscriber to receive the requested service. The selected applicant shall prohibit any of its officers, agents and employees from violating the terms of this section at all times, whether in the performance of the duties of the selected applicant or otherwise.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(VI))
Sec. 7-63. - Public service, education service.
(a)
Public service. The selected applicant shall provide, without charge, one (1) outlet to each public office building, recreation department building, fire station, police station and public and private school that is passed by its cable. If more than one (1) outlet is required at any of the locations, the selected applicant shall install at the cost of time and material only and in no event will there be a monthly service charge at the location.
(b)
Educational service. The selected applicant shall reserve at least one (1) television channel for the use of educational institutions within the county for the origination of educational television telecasts and the interconnection of all of such educational institutions.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(VII))
Sec. 7-64. - Service standards.
(a)
The selected applicant shall maintain and operate the system and render efficient service in accordance with the rules and regulations as now or hereafter may be established by the Federal Communications Commission or its successor.
(b)
The selected applicant shall be responsible for installation and maintenance of the service wiring from the cable point of entrance to the TV set.
(c)
The selected applicant shall investigate and resolve all subscriber complaints regarding the quality of service, equipment, malfunctions and similar matters expeditiously and in accordance with the following procedure:
(1)
The selected applicant shall during normal working hours have qualified personnel available to investigate and resolve subscriber complaints.
(2)
Upon notification of a service complaint, the selected applicant shall dispatch a qualified employee to investigate the complaint and adjust, repair or replace equipment as necessary to resolve the complaint. The selected applicant shall not be responsible for malfunctions in any subscriber's radio or television receiver.
(3)
Complaints shall be investigated and resolved within twenty-four (24) hours of notification. If upon investigation it is determined that the complaint cannot be resolved within twenty-four (24) hours due to circumstances beyond the control of the selected applicant, the subscriber shall be notified and given an estimation as to when the complaint will be resolved.
(d)
The selected applicant shall be required to provide permanent service to any permanent-type residence within a period of ninety (90) working days from the initial request for service from potential subscribers when the area requesting service has an average of thirty (30) residences per contiguous mile with existing service.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(VIII))
Sec. 7-65. - Company rules.
(a)
The selected applicant shall have the authority to promulgate such rules, regulations, terms and conditions governing the conduct of its business as shall be reasonably necessary to enable the selected applicant to exercise its rights and to perform its obligations under the franchise and to assure an uninterrupted service to each and all its subscribers; provided, however, that such rules, regulations, terms and conditions shall not be in conflict with the provisions of this article and shall be filed with the county and shall be subject to the approval of the county.
(b)
The selected applicant shall not refuse to hire or employ, nor bar or discharge from employment, nor discriminate against any person in compensation or in terms, conditions or privileges of employment because of sex, race, creed, color, marital status or national origin. This requirement shall apply to, but not be limited to, the following: employment, upgrading, demotion or transfer, recruitment advertising, layoff or termination, rates of pay or other forms of compensation and selection of training.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(IX))
Sec. 7-66. - Local office.
The selected applicant shall throughout the duration of any franchise maintain an office open to the public during reasonable business hours and a system manager for performing services and meeting all requirements provided herein. The office shall be located within the county or within a fifteen-mile radius of the county line; if the office is located beyond the certified telephone area in which the system operates, a toll-free line shall be provided for subscribers.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(X))
Sec. 7-67. - County rules, control of rates and payments.
The right is hereby reserved to the county to adopt, in addition to the provisions contained in this article and in existing applicable resolutions or ordinances, such additional regulations as the county may find necessary in the exercise of police power, provided such regulations by resolution or ordinance otherwise shall be reasonable and not in conflict with the rights herein granted. Except as otherwise herein limited, the county shall approve the rates and costs to the subscribers of the system in the county and the county shall have the right to set the amount of payments required of the selected applicant to the county.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XI))
Sec. 7-68. - Occupational licenses, taxes, etc.
The selected applicant shall pay annually, in addition to the franchise fee, such ad valorem and personal property taxes, inventory taxes, occupational licenses, fees and service charges as shall be appropriate to that general classification of business as set forth by law. It is herewith noted the selected applicant is not classified as a public utility and therefore not subject to payment of any type of utility taxes.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XII))
Cross reference— Taxation, Ch. 16.
Sec. 7-69. - Rates to subscribers.
The selected applicant may make such charges for its services as are reasonable. The selected applicant may increase these rates annually but no more than ten (10) percent per year, which percentage is cumulative up to, but not to exceed, thirty (30) percent without prior approval of the county. The applicant's subscribers located in the unincorporated areas of the county shall not be charged a greater rate than the applicants' subscribers located in the incorporated area from which the system originates for the same or equivalent service. A subscriber of the selected applicant may discontinue any service provided by the applicant at any time without further charge or penalty.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XIII))
Sec. 7-70. - Payments to county.
(a)
Gross subscriber revenue tax. The selected applicant shall pay to the county the standard or franchise fee based on gross subscriber revenues received from its operations in the county in an amount not to exceed that amount authorized under applicable federal law and regulations.
(b)
Manner of payment. All payments as required by the selected applicant to the county shall be made quarterly and shall be due within fifteen (15) days from first day of each calendar quarter unless the same shall fall on a weekend or legal holiday, in which event the same shall be paid on the next immediate work day. A late penalty of ten (10) percent shall be assessed on any payment made more than fifteen (15) days after the due date.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XIV))
Sec. 7-71. - Records and reports.
The county shall have access at all reasonable hours to the selected applicant's records relating to subscriber revenue, which are deemed necessary by the county to determine compliance with this article and the contract between the selected applicant and the county. An annual summary report showing gross subscriber revenues received by the selected applicant from the operation of the system within the county during the preceding twelve-month period and such other information as the county may require in support of same shall be given to the county by the selected applicant.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XV))
Sec. 7-72. - Transfer approval.
The selected applicant shall not sell or transfer its plant or system or any portion thereof, nor any right, title or interest in the same, nor shall the selected applicant transfer any rights under the franchise to any other person without prior approval of the board of commissioners, which approval shall not be unreasonably or arbitrarily withheld.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XVI))
Sec. 7-73. - Duration of franchise.
Any franchise granted pursuant to this article shall be for such period of time as may be agreed upon between the selected applicant and the county and shall be subject to renewal by the county by negotiations upon reasonable terms with the selected applicant for an additional period.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XVII))
Sec. 7-74. - Penalties.
(a)
Should the selected applicant, its successors or assigns violate any of the provisions of the franchise, any ordinance or any reasonable rules and regulations or other laws or should the selected applicant fail to promptly perform any of the provisions hereof, the selected applicant shall forfeit all its rights hereunder to the county after written notice and hearing before the board of commissioners or its duly appointed hearing officer to the selected applicant. The continuation of such violation, failure or default for a period of more than thirty (30) days after the hearing shall constitute default of the contract and loss of all rights and privileges thereunder. Failure to perform any of the provisions of the franchise shall constitute default on the part of the selected applicant, and upon default the selected applicant shall forfeit all of its rights and privileges under the franchise and at the option of the county have the agreement terminated without further notice, provided that acts of God or events or happenings beyond the control of the selected applicant shall not be grounds for termination.
(b)
In the event of the bankruptcy or receivership for the selected applicant, all rights herein given to the selected applicant shall, at the option of the county, be forfeited and terminated.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XVIII))
Sec. 7-75. - Termination of negotiations.
At any time the board of commissioners may terminate negotiations and begin negotiations with any other selected applicant or may select other applicants for negotiation.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XIX))
Sec. 7-76. - Granting of franchise.
(a)
After negotiations with the selected applicant are completed and the selected applicant has met all requirements of the county, the board of commissioners may by contract, which shall include all terms and conditions negotiated by the county and the selected applicant, grant a CATV franchise to the selected applicant, which shall be nonexclusive.
(b)
The county may issue as many CATV franchises as it deems appropriate without regard to any service area of any other CATV franchise.
(Ord. of 11-21-89, § 6(XX))
Secs. 7-77—7-100. - Reserved.
