ARTICLE III. - AUCTIONS, THRIFT STORES AND CONSIGNMENT STORES[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— Res. No. 15-12A, adopted Mar. 11, 2015, amended Art. III in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former Art. III, §§ 50-81—50-86, pertained to auctions, and derived from Code 1982, § 5-210(1)—(6).
State Law reference— Auctions generally, O.C.G.A. § 43-6-1 et seq.

Sec. 50-81. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Business means any business that accepts items for auction, sale or consignment basis.
Minor means any person younger than 18.
Owner means any business owner to include his/her employees that accepts items for auction. sale or consignment basis on behalf of the business.
Property owner/seller means person offering the property for sale or consignment.
Report means written records used by business detailing items purchased or consigned, i.e., consignment sheets, receipt, etc., that includes signature of property owner/consignor.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-82. - License required.
Each person who desires to conduct an auction, thrift store or consignment within this city shall be required to obtain a license therefore from the city clerk in the manner specified in this article.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-83. - Fee established.
The business license fee for each auction licensed under this article shall be $150.00.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-84. - License application.
(a)
Application for a license under this article shall contain the following additional information:
(1)
A statement setting forth when, where, and for how long the business sale will be conducted;
(2)
A certified copy of the state auctioneer's license of the person, if applicable, who is to conduct the auction; and
(3)
A full and true inventory of all the articles to be sold at the auction sale, together with an affidavit that all information contained in the application is true and that the inventory contains a true listing of all the articles to be sold at said auction, if applicable.
(b)
No auction sale shall be held except as set forth in the application and affidavit, and no other articles shall be sold except those included in the sworn inventory unless a new affidavit shall be filed listing the additional goods to be sold. No other person shall be allowed to conduct the auction sale except such person listed in the affidavit unless a new affidavit is filed setting forth such other person who may be employed to conduct such auction and cry off such goods, if applicable.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-85. - Bond.
Every owner for a license under this article shall file with the city clerk a surety bond running to the city with surety acceptable to and approved by the city clerk, conditioned that the said owner, if issued a license hereunder, will comply fully with all of the provisions of the ordinances of this city and the statutes regulating and concerning auctions, auctioneers. Thrift stores or consignment stores will render true and strict accounts of all his sales to any person employing him to make the same, will not practice any fraud or deceit upon bidders or purchasers of property from him at any auction sale or suffer or permit any person in his employ to practice any such fraud or deceit, and will pay all damages which may be sustained by any person by reason of any fraud, deceit, negligence or other wrongful act on the part of the licensee, his agent or employees in the conduct of any auction or in the exercise of the calling of auctioneer. A liability insurance policy issued by an insurance company authorized to do business in this state which conforms to the above requirements may be permitted by the clerk in his discretion in lieu of a bond.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-86. - Cappers, boosters, or by-bidders.
It shall be unlawful for any person to act or to employ another in any auction sale as a by-bidder or what is sometimes known as a "capper" or "booster," or to make or to accept any false or misleading bid, or to pretend to buy or sell any article sold or offered for sale at such business.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-87. - Maintenance of records required; contents; terms.
(a)
Every owner shall maintain records which shall contain in legible English the following information:
(1)
For purchases or consignments received from individuals for all items on sequenced numbered reports as specified under Secretary of States' Rules and Regulations for the State of Georgia for Georgia Auctioneers Commission, Conduct of Auctions, 55-5-.01 — Responsibilities of the Auctioneer as well as in this Code:
a.
An executed contract, which shall include:
i.
The name(s) and address(es), telephone number(s), and signature(s) of the seller(s) of the property offered;
ii.
The name(s) and address(es), telephone number(s), and signature(s) of the auctioneer (owner) in charge of the sale;
iii.
Date of execution;
iv.
Proposed date of and place that the auction is to be held;
v.
Terms of compensation;
vi.
Date of proposed settlement.
b.
A closing statement which shall include:
i.
The name(s) and address(es) of the seller(s) of the property offered;
ii.
The price at which the property sold;
iii.
The date and place of said auction;
iv.
The date the proceeds were delivered to said owner;
v.
Signature(s) of the seller(s); and
vi.
List of items sold.
c.
Photocopy of driver's license or other form of identification of seller.
(b)
Reports shall be submitted to the Glennville Police Department via hand-delivery, fax or email no less than weekly on or before 5:00 p.m. of Wednesday of each week.
(c)
Items purchased or consigned must be identified individually by sequenced number of report or grouped together collectively with identifiable sequenced number attached.
(d)
The records required by this Code section shall be maintained for a period of not less than three years.
(e)
The records required by this Code section shall be open to the inspection of any duly authorized law enforcement officer during the ordinary hours of business.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-88. - Identification required.
(a)
Each owner shall required that any person selling or placing articles on consignment shall display evidence of acceptable identification such as a duly issued state driver's license or identification and the owner shall photocopy the driver's license or other such evidence of identification on the required form for the transaction.
(b)
The owner will have the affirmative duty to compare all pictures, physical descriptions and signatures on the identification presented and shall not transact any business with a person who in the owner's opinion appears to be presenting false or altered identification. If the identification presented is not a State of Georgia issued identification, the owner will have the affirmative duty of providing the listed address from the identification as well as current Georgia address on the transaction paperwork. The owner is required to check the customer's identification for each transaction. If the records show identification other than that presented. this must be corrected during the current transaction.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-89. - Holding period of items.
Every owner will cause all item s purchased or consigned to be held from immediate sale or auction until items are either released by appointed law enforcement officer of the City of Glennville or waiting period not to exceed ten calendar days has expired. The items will remain onsite until either released by police officer or ten calendar days has expired.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-90. - Police holds on materials purchased or consigned.
Whenever a law enforcement officer shall have probable cause to suspect any property listed in the report is stolen property or constitutes evidence of a crime or that any individual has committed a crime or that possession of the property is criminal, the owner shall be notified in writing not to sell, destroy, alter or remove the property from the physical boundaries of the business facility. These hold notices will be provided by law enforcement in writing and will specifically identify the items corresponding to the sequenced report number to be held.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-91. - Exclusions.
Items that shall be excluded from hold shall include the following:
(1)
Items purchased from storage units and/or estate sales. However, documentation of purchase or consignment shall be provided.
(2)
Clothing.
(3)
Household goods to include pots, pans, flatware, sheets, pillows, trinkets, decorations, etc.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-92. - Minors.
No owner shall purchase or accept consignment for any property from a minor.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-93. - Stolen goods.
It shall be the duty of every owner to report to the police any article placed with him/her that he/she shall have reason to believe the item(s) was stolen or lost and found by that person attempting to place the item for auction.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-94. - Penalties for violation.
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 per violation or by imprisonment for a period not to exceed 90 days, or by both such fine and imprisonment. In addition to such fine or imprisonment, any person failing to comply with any provision of this article, or other laws, ordinances, or regulation as may be passed by the city in regulating the business, shall be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of the city business license issued hereunder.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
Sec. 50-95. - Exemptions.
Nothing in this article shall be held to apply to auction sales conducted by trustees or referees in bankruptcy, executors, administrators, receivers or other public officers acting under judicial process, nor to the sale of real property at business.
(Res. No. 15-12A, 3-11-2015)
