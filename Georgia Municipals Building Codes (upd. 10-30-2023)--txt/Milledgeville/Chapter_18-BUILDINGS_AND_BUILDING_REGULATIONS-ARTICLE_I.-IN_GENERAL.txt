ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Violations.
In lieu of or in addition to other penalties for violations of this chapter, any license issued by the city may be suspended or revoked therefor.
(Code 1986, § 5-1)
Sec. 18-2. - Liability for damages.
Nothing contained in this chapter shall be construed to relieve any person from liability or responsibility for damages or injury to life or property.
(Code 1986, § 5-2)
Sec. 18-3. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Inasmuch as Ord. No. O-02-02, adopted January 22, 2002, enacted provisions regarding administrative procedures for the enforcement of state minimum construction codes, the provisions of the former section 18-3, which pertained to similar subject matter and derived from the Code of 1986, § 5-3, have been removed from this Code at the discretion of the editor. For current provisions, the user's attention is directed to article VIII of this chapter.
Sec. 18-4. - Persons working on own premises.
Nothing contained in this chapter shall be construed as prohibiting an individual from constructing, installing or repairing his own residential structures or appliances, or installing, extending, replacing, altering or repairing structures or appliances on his own residential premises, or as requiring a license or a bond from an individual doing such work on his own residential premises; provided, however, all such work must be done in conformity with all other provisions of this chapter, including those relating to permits, inspection and fees.
(Code 1986, § 5-4)
Sec. 18-5. - CABO One- and Two-Family Dwelling Code/International Residential Code.
The latest edition of the CABO One- and Two-Family Dwelling Code/International Residential Code, including the appendix, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, is hereby adopted as the official one- and two-family dwelling code and residential code of the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-5; Ord. No. O-02-03, 1-22-2002)
Sec. 18-6. - Energy code.
The 1986 edition, with 1988 amendments, of the Georgia State Energy Code for Buildings, as filed in the office of the clerk and treasurer and identified by the signature of the mayor thereon, is hereby adopted as the official energy code of the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-6; Ord. of 7-26-1988, § 1; Ord. of 3-12-1991, § 1; Ord. of 11-24-1992, § 1)
Sec. 18-7. - Unsafe buildings.
The latest edition of the Standard Unsafe Building Abatement Code, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, is hereby adopted as the official standard code for the elimination or repair of unsafe buildings in the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-7; Ord. No. O-02-03, 1-22-2002)
Cross reference— Environment, ch. 42.
Sec. 18-8. - Existing buildings.
The latest edition of the Standard Existing Buildings Code, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, is hereby adopted as the official standard code for existing buildings in the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-8; Ord. of 11-24-1992, § 1; Ord. No. O-02-03, 1-22-2002)
Sec. 18-9. - Swimming pools.
The latest edition of the Standard Swimming Pool Code, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, is hereby adopted as the official standard swimming pool code of the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-9; Ord. No. O-02-03, 1-22-2002)
Sec. 18-10. - Excavation and grading.
The latest edition of the Standard Excavation and Grading Code, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, is hereby adopted as the official standard excavation and grading code of the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-10; Ord. No. O-02-03, 1-22-2002)
Sec. 18-11. - Accessibility codes for buildings and facilities.
The latest edition of Accessibility Codes for Buildings and Facilities, O.C.G.A. § 120-3-20, as adopted by the Office of Fire and Safety Commission, is hereby adopted.
(Ord. No. O-02-03, 1-22-2002)
Editor's note— Ord. No. O-02-03, adopted January 22, 2002, has been treated as amending section 18-11 in its entirety to read as herein set out. Formerly, section 18-11 pertained to handicap guidelines, and derived from the Code of 1986, § 5-11.
Sec. 18-12. - Amusement devices.
The latest edition of the Standard Amusement Device Code, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, is hereby adopted as the official standard amusement device code of the city.
(Ord. No. O-02-03, 1-22-2002)
Editor's note— Ord. No. O-02-03, adopted January 22, 2002, did not specify manner of inclusion; hence, inclusion as section 18-12 is at the discretion of the editor.
Secs. 18-13—18-40. - Reserved.
