Sec. 70-31. - Definition.
As used in this article, a "junkyard" is an establishment or place of business which is maintained, operated or used for storing, keeping, buying or selling junk, or for the maintenance or operation of an automobile graveyard. This definition includes scrap metal processors, recycling stations, auto wrecking yards, salvage yards, scrapyards, auto recycling yards, used auto parts yards and temporary storage of automobile bodies or parts awaiting disposal as a normal part of a business operation when the business will continually have like materials located on the premises. The definition includes garbage dumps and sanitary landfills.
(Code 1986, § 12-231)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-32. - Intent.
This article is not intended to authorize any violation of state law.
(Code 1986, § 12-232)
Sec. 70-33. - Visibility.
It shall be unlawful for any person to establish, maintain or operate a junkyard within the city which is visible from a publicly dedicated street or highway, except as provided in this article.
(Code 1986, § 12-233)
Sec. 70-34. - Screening.
Effective screening from view shall be accomplished by using natural and/or manmade materials, relocating junk on the site or using a combination of these methods. Screens shall have an effective height of eight feet, and no junk shall be stacked so as to be visible above this height. Entrances and exits shall likewise be screened in other than normal business hours. Screens shall be maintained to be effective as long as junk is stored on the site. Alternative types of screening are as follows:
(1)
Use of plant material. Planting of trees, shrubs, etc., of a sufficient size and density to provide yearround effective screening. Plants shall be selected to complement the existing highway and adjacent land use environmental conditions;
(2)
Earth grading. Construction of earth mounds, graded, shaped and planted to a natural appearance, to block visibility;
(3)
Architectural barrier. Utilization of fences, walls or other structural elements;
(4)
Relocation on site. The relocation of scrap inventory on the site to utilize an existing natural screen or screenable portion of the site; or
(5)
A combination of the screenings outlined in this section.
(Code 1986, § 12-234)
Sec. 70-35. - Future junkyards.
Except as provided in section 70-36, all junkyards shall be located only in areas zoned industrial according to the official zoning ordinance of the city. Screening requirements shall continue to apply to junkyards in industrial areas.
(Code 1986, § 12-235)
Sec. 70-36. - Existing junkyards.
Junkyards in existence on December 13, 1977 which are not in an area zoned industrial according to the official zoning ordinance of the city, and which are declared by the council to be an attractive nuisance to children and others in the area, shall be appropriately fenced or walled to discourage entry. Entrances and exits shall be locked during hours other than normal business hours. Existing junkyards which are not in an area zoned industrial, and which cease to actively be in business for a period of 90 days, shall not be reestablished after such time period.
(Code 1986, § 12-236)
Sec. 70-37. - Unlawful junkyards.
Any operating or abandoned junkyard found not to conform to this article shall be removed or obliterated by the owner within 60 days of notice by the council. Upon failure by the owner to do so, the council may have the junkyard removed or obliterated and submit by registered or certified mail a statement of expenses for payment by the owner. If payment is not received by the city within 30 days, the council may take legal proceedings to recover the expense.
(Code 1986, § 12-237)
