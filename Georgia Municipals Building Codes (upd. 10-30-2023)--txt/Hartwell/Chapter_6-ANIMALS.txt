ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Enforcement.
All city police officers shall have, and are hereby declared to have, full authority and power to impound animals, prosecute violations and violators in the municipal court and to otherwise enforce this chapter.
(Comp. 1976, § 16-204; Code 1989, § 4-21; Ord. of 5-3-1982, § 2; Ord. No. 2001-002, § 1, 3-4-2002)
Sec. 6-2. - Interfering with, hindering or molesting city agents prohibited.
No person shall interfere with, hinder or molest any city agent in the performance of any duty as provided in this chapter.
(Comp. 1976, § 16-204; Code 1989, § 4-25; Ord. of 5-3-1982, § 6)
Sec. 6-3. - Unlawful to keep livestock, fowl, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep any hog, cow, sheep, goat, pony, mule, horse, fowl, or other livestock, within the city.
(Comp. 1976, § 16-105; Code 1989, § 4-2; Ord. of 2-3-1975(1))
Sec. 6-4. - Slaughter pens and butchering stock prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep a slaughter pen or to butcher stock for market within the city.
(Comp. 1976, § 16-101; Code 1989, § 4-3; Ord. of 5-6-1912(1))
Secs. 6-5—6-34. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BIRD SANCTUARY

Sec. 6-35. - Designated.
The entire area embraced within the corporate city limits is hereby designated as a bird sanctuary.
(Comp. 1976, § 16-104; Code 1989, § 4-1; Ord. of 3-4-1957)
Sec. 6-36. - Trapping, shooting, etc., birds prohibited.
It shall be unlawful to trap, shoot, hunt, or attempt to hunt or shoot or molest in any manner any bird or wild fowl or to rob bird nests or wild fowl nests except as provided in this article.
(Comp. 1976, § 16-104; Code 1989, § 4-1; Ord. of 3-4-1957)
Sec. 6-37. - Nuisances; abatement.
(a)
If starlings or similar birds congregate in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or menace to health or property in the city health authorities' opinion, the city health authorities shall meet with the representatives of the clubs that instigated the bird sanctuary or any of them to discuss an appropriate solution.
(b)
If as a result of the meeting no satisfactory alternative is found to abate the nuisance, the birds may be destroyed in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by the health authorities under the chief of police's supervision.
(Comp. 1976, § 16-104; Code 1989, § 4-1; Ord. of 3-4-1957)
Secs. 6-38—6-62. - Reserved.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-63. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandon means the act of placing an animal on public property or within a public building, unattended or uncared for, or on or within the private property of another without the express permission of the owner, custodian or tenant of the private property. An animal shall also be considered abandoned when it has been unattended and without adequate food or water for a period in excess of 36 continuous hours regardless of where such animal may be found or kept.
Adequate food means sufficient quantity of non-contaminated and nutritionally healthy sustenance that is sufficient to prevent starvation, malnutrition, or risk to the animal's health. Garbage, spoiled, rancid, or contaminated food is not adequate food.
Adequate water means sufficient quantity of potable water.
Animal control facility means and includes any vehicle, building, structure, pasture, paddock, pond, impoundment area or premises where any animal is kept or housed by the animal control unit for the purpose of impounding or harboring stray, homeless, abandoned, unwanted, neglected or abused animals.
Animal control officer means an employee of the county duly empowered under the legal authority of the county to enforce the rules and regulations of this article.
Animals running at-large means any animal not under immediate control, not on a leash, not at heel, not beside a competent person, not in a vehicle driven or parked, or not confined within the property limits of the animal's owner. Hunting dogs, tracking dogs, and dogs used for agriculture are under the control of the owner when hunting or used with the landowner's permission.
Domestic animal means an animal that, through long association with humans, has been adapted to human living conditions.
Impoundment means the act of taking physical possession and control of an animal by an animal control officer or other officer empowered to act by law and transporting it to the animal control facility.
Owner means any person, firm, corporation, partnership, association, and/or other legal entity, who or which owns, possesses, harbors, keeps, feeds, maintains, or knowingly causes or knowingly permits an animal to be harbored or kept, or has an animal in his care, or who permits an animal to remain on or about his premises. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, any adult at whose residence a dangerous animal or potentially dangerous animal is kept or found shall be presumed to be an owner of such animal and shall have the burden of rebutting such presumption. If an animal has more than one owner, any one of such owners may be prosecuted for violations whether or not any other owners are also prosecuted.
Provocation means committing a willful trespass or other tort upon the premises occupied by the owner or possessor of the animal, or teasing, tormenting, or abusing the animal or committing or attempting to commit a crime against a person.
Vicious dog as defined in O.C.G.A. Title 4, Chapter 8.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)

DIVISION 2. - DUTIES, POWERS AND PROHIBITIONS RELATING TO CONTROL OF ANIMALS

Sec. 6-70. - Rabies vaccinations.
The owners and possessors of all animals in the city are hereby required to have such animals vaccinated against rabies by some licensed veterinarian in accordance with the latest "Compendium of Animal Rabies Vaccines and Recommendations for Immunization" published by the National Association of State Public Health Veterinarians. No person shall own, keep or harbor any animal over four months of age within the city unless such animal is vaccinated.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-71. - Restraint, running at large prohibited; duty of owner or custodian.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any owner or possessor of any animal to fail to keep the animal under restraint or control as provided for in this section for animals covered in subsequent sections.
(b)
A animal is considered not under restraint or control when it is running at large off the owner's property whether wearing a collar and tag or not. Reasonable care and precautions shall be taken to prevent the animal from leaving the real property limits of its owner, possessor, or custodian.
(c)
Animals found running at-large or in violation of this section may be immediately impounded by officers of the animal control unit or any police officer. The officers may pursue the animal onto private property to effect capture of such animal.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-71.5. - Howling or barking declared nuisance.
(a)
The howling or barking of any dog to such an extent or so continuously as to materially interfere with or affect the health, comfort, peace and quiet of the public is hereby declared to be a nuisance.
(b)
Any person who harbors, keeps in such person's possession, or has control of, any dog, the howling or barking of which constitutes a nuisance, and who also fails or refuses to abate such nuisance when requested in writing to do so by the chief of police, shall be charged with a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be fined not less than $100.00 or more than $500.00.
(Ord. No. 2015-07, § 1, 1-4-2016)
Sec. 6-72. - Tethering of animals prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tether, fasten, chain, tie, restrain, or cause an animal to be fastened, chained, tied or restrained, to houses, trees, fences, garages or other stationary or highly immobile objects by means of a rope, chain, strap or other physical restraint for the purpose of confinement, except in circumstances where all of the following requirements are met:
(1)
The tethering shall not be for more time than is necessary for the animal owner or custodian to complete a task that requires the animal to be physically restrained for no more than a four-hour period;
(2)
Any tether used to confine an animal temporarily must not be attached to a choke chain or a pinch/prong chain;
(3)
The tether shall be at least three times the body length of the animal, measured from the animal's nose to the back of the hindquarters;
(4)
The tether shall be free from entanglement;
(5)
The animal must have access to food, water and shelter; and
(6)
The animal shall be monitored periodically.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-73. - Abandoned animals.
(a)
Except as expressly permitted in this section, it shall be unlawful for anyone knowingly to abandon or to aid in the abandonment of any animal.
(b)
An animal relinquished through written agreement with animal control, licensed rescue group, licensed shelter, licensed veterinarians, individual, or other entity shall not be deemed abandoned but shall be considered relinquished to the entity.
(c)
Any person violating any provision of this section may, upon conviction, be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and/or 30 days in jail.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-74. - Transient animals.
Any animal shipped or transported through the city or entering the city only for the purpose of a temporary stay, when such stay shall not exceed 15 days, shall be exempt from collar and tag sections of this article; however, all other provisions of this article are applicable to such transient animals.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-75. - Public nuisance animal.
(a)
A public nuisance animal shall mean and include any animal that:
(1)
Is repeatedly found at-large;
(2)
Damages the property of anyone other than the owner;
(3)
Is vicious;
(4)
Attacks without provocation; or
(5)
Chases vehicles, bicycles, or people outside of the owner's property.
(b)
Any such public nuisance animal may be impounded and the owner or possessor charged for a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-76. - Disposition of impounded animals.
(a)
Any animal seized or impounded by an animal control officer or any officer empowered to act by law may be temporarily detained at a local animal control facility and/or detained at the animal shelter facility the city is currently in contract with. Custody of seized animals will be transferred from the city to the animal shelter facility the city is currently in contract with upon release of the animal to that animal shelter.
(b)
For any animal that is impounded for law enforcement purposes, at the owner's request and expense, such impoundment may be at a veterinary or licensed kennel of the owner's choosing as long as the owner cannot cause the animal to be released from the boarding facility without the approval of the prosecuting officer or agent and the owner pays all costs of such impoundment.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-77. - Tags.
It shall be the duty of each animal owner or possessor to provide a collar with an identification tag and current rabies vacation attached for each animal listing the owners name and phone number within 15 days of the owner's or possessor's taking possession of the animal. Such collar shall be on the animal at all times when the animal is not under the immediate control of the owner or possessor of the animal. Animals without tags may be considered in violation of section 6-71. Animals participating in organized or controlled competitions are exempt from this subsection only for the duration of the competition.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-78. - Interference with animal control officers.
It shall be unlawful to interfere with any animal control officer or other officer empowered to act by law, or to take or attempt to take any animal from the county vehicle used to transport the animal, or to take or attempt to take any animal from the animal control facility, or by any other method which would block or hinder any officer referred to in this section from performing his duties.
It shall also be unlawful for any person to relocate, alter, or interfere with the operation of any device, trap, equipment or item belonging to the County of Hart's animal control unit.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-79. - Duty of animal owners to be responsible owners.
It shall be the duty of every owner or custodian of any animal to exercise reasonable care and take all necessary steps and precautions to protect other people, property, and animals from injuries or damage which might result from their animal's behavior. If the owner or custodian of any animal is a minor, the parent or guardian of such minor shall be responsible to ensure compliance with all provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-80. - Removal [of] feral animal packs.
The animal control officer has the right to immediately seize and remove all or parts of any feral animal pack for public safety and public health concerns, including rabies, other zoonotic disease epidemics, and certain animal-to-animal diseases as identified by the animal control officer. The method of removal may be in the form of trapping or extermination that shall be designed to be as humane as possible under the circumstances as allowed by O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4(f)(2).
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-81. - Prohibition on feeding stray and feral animals.
(a)
Feral and stray animals constitute public safety, health, and environmental risks to domesticated animals, wildlife, and persons. Except as expressly permitted by this section, no person shall feed or harbor feral or stray animals.
(b)
Notwithstanding the general prohibition in subsection (a), a person may feed or harbor a feral or stray animal if such feeding is done as part of a good faith effort to capture the animal humanely for the purpose of surrender to the animal control unit within 48 hours of capture. When feeding for this purpose, the person shall notify the city before feeding starts and utilize proper city-approved capture devices.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-82. - Permitting animals in heat to roam free.
No owner or custodian of any animal in heat shall permit the animal to roam or run free beyond the limits of such owner's or custodian's property.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
Sec. 6-83. - Penalties.
Any person violating any provision of this article, with the exception of section 6-73, shall be first issued a warning for the first offense. Subsequent offenses for failure to comply with any provision of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor and shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-13, and in addition may be liable to pay all costs and expenses involved in such case.
(Ord. No. 2013-02, §§ 1, 2, 10-7-2013)
