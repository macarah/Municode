ARTICLE VI. - YARD SALES

Sec. 10-182. - Penalties.
The owner of any residence or the person to whom a permit is issued for the purpose of having a garage sale who shall fail to comply with any part of this article shall be subject to a fine of not more than $100.00 and imprisonment of not more than 30 days for each offense.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-183. - Permit required.
Any person desiring to conduct a yard or garage sale must first obtain a permit from the city. The permit shall be in the form prescribed by the city council and shall be prominently posted at the sale site for the entire duration of the sale.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-184. - Limitation on number and duration.
The maximum period for each yard or garage sale is two days consecutively. The maximum number of permits allowed per year to one applicant for a yard or garage sale is two per family or eleemosynary organization.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-185. - Location restriction.
Yard and garage sales may be held only at the home of the family or organization conducting the sale.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-186. - Hours and days of operation.
The hours of the sale shall be during daylight hours. No Sunday sales will be allowed.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-187. - Parking.
Parking shall be conducted in a manner that does not interfere with traffic flow or create traffic congestion.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-188. - Sale items to be owned by family member or organization.
No new or used merchandise may be purchased or brought in for the sale. All merchandise shall be that owned by the family or members of the eleemosynary organization conducting the sale.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-189. - Advertising restrictions.
Only one sign may be posted advertising the sale. The sign shall be located on the private property of the person conducting the sale, and not on utility poles, street or traffic signs. No public address system may be used.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-190. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
No alcoholic beverages of any type shall be served or given on or near the premises.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Sec. 10-191. - Inspection; citation and termination.
If upon inspection by any lawful officer or city official the officer/official finds that in the officer's/official's opinion any of the requirements of this article are being violated, a citation will be issued and the right to continue with the sale will be terminated at once.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 11-12)
Secs. 10-192—10-220. - Reserved.
