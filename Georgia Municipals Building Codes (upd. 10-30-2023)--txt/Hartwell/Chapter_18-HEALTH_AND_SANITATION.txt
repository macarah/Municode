Chapter 18 - HEALTH AND SANITATION
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - State department of community health rules adopted.
The following rules and regulations of the Georgia Department of Community Health (DCH), including all subsequent amendments, revisions or modifications thereto expressly adopted and incorporated by reference as if fully set forth in this section, and are further approved for enforcement:
(1)
Control of Rabies (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-2).
(2)
Food Service (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-14).
(3)
Tourist Accommodations (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-18).
(4)
Land Disposal of Domestic Septage (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-25).
(5)
On-Site Sewage Management Systems (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-26).
(6)
Drinking Water Supply (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-55).
(7)
Solid Waste (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-56).
(8)
Swimming Pools and Recreational Water Parks (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-57).
(9)
Special On-Site Sewage Management Systems (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-59).
(10)
The Georgia Smokefree Air Act of 2005 (Comp. R. & Regs. § 290-5-61).
Copies of those DCH rules and regulations are on file in the city clerk's office where the same are available for inspection and copying by the public during regular city operating hours.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 1, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-2. - State laws and city ordinances adopted.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 31-3-1 et seq., in addition to the rules and regulations of the DCH mentioned in section 18-1, the following rules and regulations have been enacted by the county board of health, and are hereby adopted and incorporated by reference as if fully set forth in this section by the city in accordance with state law, and are further approved for enforcement:
(1)
Rules and regulations concerning body art and tattooing, enacted by the resolution dated August 9, 2001, which are hereby set forth verbatim in this section and adopted by the city council upon lawful adoption of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived.
(2)
Rules and regulations concerning rabies control, enacted by the resolution dated November 12, 2009, which are hereby set forth verbatim in this section and adopted by the city council upon lawful adoption of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived.
Copies of those rules and regulations enacted by the county board of health and having been adopted by city council ordinance are on file in the city council clerk's office, where the same are available for inspection and copying by the public during regular city operating hours.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 2, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-3. - Minimum lot sizes adopted.
In addition, the rules and regulations concerning minimum lot sizes for the orderly and safe development of property utilizing on-site sewage management systems, having been enacted by the county board of health are hereby adopted and incorporated by reference as if fully set forth in this section. The city council may, however, require larger minimum lot sizes for utilization of on-site sewage management systems in zoning or land use districts providing for septic service, and in the event there is a conflict between the minimum lot sizes established by the county board of health and that established by the city council, the larger lot sizes established by the city council shall control and take precedence.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 3, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-4. - Fee schedule established.
A fee schedule for the implementation and confirmation of compliance with the referenced state promulgated rules and regulations and local board of health enactments mentioned in sections 18-1 through 18-3, including permit and inspection fees, has been established by the county board of health, and is hereby approved and adopted by the city council. A copy of this fee schedule, including all subsequent amendments, revisions or modifications thereto, is on file in the city clerk's office and is available for inspection and copying by the public during regular city operating hours.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 4, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-5. - Compliance with chapter required.
It shall be unlawful for any natural person or any legal entity including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, limited liability company, firm or trust, to violate any of the rules and regulations identified in sections 18-1 through 18-3, and/or to fail or refuse to pay any fee established by the county board of health, and approved and adopted by the city council, for the implementation and confirmation of compliance with the rules and regulations identified in sections 18-1 through 18-3, including all permit and inspection fees.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 6, 1-4-2010)
Secs. 18-6—18-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 18-29. - Penalties.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20, any person or any legal entity including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, limited liability company, firm or trust, violating any provision of the rules and regulations identified and adopted in this article shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by imprisonment in the county jail for not more than 60 days, or both. Each day during which a violation of any provision of the rules and regulations identified and adopted in this article shall occur or continue shall constitute, and shall be considered, a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 7, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-30. - Authority of city, state and county board of health.
The enforcement and prosecution of this article shall be within the jurisdiction of the city, state and the county board of health, and their authorized designees or agents, and all law enforcement personnel of the city.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 5, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-31. - Municipal court jurisdiction.
The city municipal court shall have original jurisdiction upon any offense charged pursuant to this article.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 8, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-32. - Suspension or revocation of permit.
In addition to the rights and remedies set forth in this chapter, or as otherwise authorized by law, the city, state and county boards of health retain and reserve the right to seek equitable relief, injunction, abatement or any appropriate action or proceeding to protect the public health, safety and welfare. Failure or refusal to pay any fee approved for the implementation and confirmation of compliance with the rules and regulations identified in sections 18-1 through 18-3, including all permit and inspection fees, may result in suspension and/or revocation of any permit issued by the county board of health.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 9, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-33. - Prosecution for violations.
(a)
Violations of all state DCH promulgated rules and regulations and all local board of health enactments identified and adopted in this chapter may be prosecuted by an accusation or citation with or without a prosecuting attorney in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63, and any successor statute.
(b)
The city council authorizes the city attorney or his designee to prosecute violations of this chapter, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 15-10-66, and any successor statute.
(c)
The county solicitor and his assistants shall also have the authority to prosecute all citations or accusations issued for violation of the rules and regulations identified and adopted in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 10, 1-4-2010)
Sec. 18-34. - Chapter not to prohibit state DCH from initiating other actions.
This chapter, and the enforcement and prosecution of this chapter by the city and county boards of health, and their authorized agents or designees, does not in any way effect, estop, prescribe, regulate, limit, or in any way control or prohibit the state DCH, and its agents or designees, from initiating or undertaking any action or proceeding to enforce compliance with its rules and regulations.
(Ord. No. 2010-01, § 11, 1-4-2010)
