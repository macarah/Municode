ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS

Sec. 50-20. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Driver, operator means any person in charge of or driving or operating a taxicab, as defined herein, whether as owner, agent, employee or otherwise.
Driver's permit means the permission granted by the governing body to a person to drive a taxicab within the city.
License means the license or authority granted by the governing body to engage in the taxicab business within the city.
Owner means any person having control of the operation or maintenance and collection of the revenue derived from taxicabs.
Taxicab means a motor vehicle operated for hire, the destination of which is under the direction of the passenger transported therein.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 1)
Sec. 50-21. - License—Required.
No person shall operate any taxicab business within the city until the license provided for in this article has been granted and issued.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 3)
Sec. 50-22. - Same—Application; insurance.
(a)
Application for a license for the operation of a taxicab business shall be made to the city clerk. Such application shall be in writing and include:
(1)
The make, model, year and color of each vehicle to be used in such business;
(2)
The full name and address of the owner of each such vehicle;
(3)
Proof of liability insurance in force on each such vehicle indemnifying the public against negligence and carelessness in the operation of such vehicle in an amount not less than $15,000.00 for property damage and $30,000.00 for personal injuries; and
(4)
A statement from the city police department that each such vehicle has met the required regulations set forth in section 15-30(2) through (6).
(b)
The privilege license fee for the operation of the taxicab business shall be paid at the time of filing the application.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 3)
Cross reference— License fee, § 22-19.
Sec. 50-23. - Same—Revocation.
(a)
The police commissioner may revoke any license issued under this article if in such official's opinion any provision of this article has been violated.
(b)
No refund of the license fee shall be made in the case of any license which is revoked.
(c)
Any licensee who has had their license revoked under this section, may appeal the revocation to the governing body, provided such appeal is made in writing and filed with the city clerk not later than five days from the date of the revocation.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 5)
Sec. 50-24. - Driver's permit—Required.
No person shall operate a taxicab licensed by the city without first having obtained a driver's permit as provided in this article.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 6)
Sec. 50-25. - Same—Application; fee.
(a)
Application for a driver's permit shall be made in writing and under oath to the city clerk and shall contain the following information about the applicant:
(1)
Full name;
(2)
Age, height and weight;
(3)
Home, street and post office addresses;
(4)
Description of any handicaps;
(5)
Results of an eye test administered by the chief of police; and
(6)
Whether applicant has ever been convicted of violating any federal, state, or city law, and if so, a summary of each conviction including time, place, and type of offense.
(b)
At the time the application is filed, the applicant shall pay to the city clerk the sum of $15.00.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 7)
Sec. 50-26. - Same—Investigation; issuance.
(a)
The city clerk and the police department are charged with the duty of investigating each application for a permit to drive taxicabs and reporting to the governing body the results of the investigation. The governing body shall then approve or refuse the application.
(b)
If the governing body does approve the application, the city clerk shall issue to the applicant a taxicab driver's permit bearing the name, address, age, signature and photograph of the applicant.
(c)
Such permit shall be in effect for the remainder of the calendar year. A permit for every calendar year thereafter shall issue to the applicant upon the payment of an amount set by the governing body.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 8)
Sec. 50-27. - Same—Temporary.
(a)
Upon the concurrence of the mayor, the city clerk, and the police commissioner, a temporary driver's permit for any person to drive a taxicab within the city may be issued for a period not to exceed 30 days, provided the recipient has a state driver's permit that has never been revoked, suspended, canceled or modified and which does not show the holder thereof to have been convicted of operating an automobile under the influence of intoxicants or for violating any state statute governing the operating of motor vehicles.
(b)
Every temporary driver's permit issued pursuant to the provisions of this article shall have written or printed thereon the name of the person to whom issued, the name of the taxicab company for whom such person is to drive, the age, height, and address of such driver, the date of issuance of such permit, and "Temporary Permit Expiring _____ Days from Date Hereof" which quoted words shall be in black face type.
(c)
Such permit shall be signed by the police commissioner and the mayor.
(d)
In no event shall a temporary driver's permit be of any force or effect beyond a period of 30 days from the date on which it was issued.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 9)
Sec. 50-28. - Same—Posting in cab.
Every driver who has been issued a permit under this article shall post such person's driver's permit in such a place as to be in full view of all passengers while such driver is operating a taxicab.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 10)
Sec. 50-29. - Same—Suspension and revocation.
(a)
Suspension. The police commissioner shall have authority to suspend any driver's permit for failure of the holder to comply with any of the provisions of this article. Such suspension shall not last for a period of more than 30 days.
(b)
Revocation by governing body. The governing body shall have the right to revoke any driver's permit for failure of the holder to comply with any of the provisions of this article. However, the revocation shall not be permanent unless the holder has been given notice and an opportunity to present evidence before the governing body.
(c)
Revocation by municipal court. The municipal court shall have the power to revoke any driver's permit issued under this article upon conviction of the holder of violating any provision of this article. Such revocation shall be in addition to any other penalty which the municipal court may choose to impose.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 11)
Sec. 50-30. - Regulations governing licensee.
All licenses for the operation of a taxicab business are issued subject to the following regulations:
(1)
Alcoholic beverages. No alcoholic beverages, in the possession of the operator of the taxicab, shall be transported in a taxicab;
(2)
Posting of rates. Each taxicab shall have posted on both sun visors the schedule of rates charged for carrying passengers;
(3)
Outside lettering. On the exterior of one door on each side of the taxicab and on the rear of the taxicab shall be printed in bold letters of not less than five inches the name of the licensee and the number of the licensee's business license;
(4)
Dome lights. All dome lights on taxicabs shall comply with the following specifications:
a.
Length: 12 to 14 inches,
b.
Height: 6 inches, maximum,
c.
Letters: 3 inches, minimum;
(5)
Inspection of vehicle. Every taxicab operating under this article shall be periodically inspected by a police officer of the city at such intervals as shall be established by the police commissioner to insure the continued maintenance of safe-operating taxicabs within the city. Each taxicab shall comply with such reasonable rules and regulations as may be prescribed by the police commissioner in order to provide safe transportation;
(6)
Sanitary condition. Every taxicab operating under this article shall be kept in a clean and sanitary condition;
(7)
Driver's permit. Only persons who have been issued a driver's permit under the provisions of this article shall operate a taxicab for hire.
(Ord. of 3-7-88, § 4)
