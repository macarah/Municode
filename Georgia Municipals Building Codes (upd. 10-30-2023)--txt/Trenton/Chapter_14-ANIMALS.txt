ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal means any domesticated animal including, but not limited to, any dog or cat.
Licensed veterinary means a person with a doctor of veterinary medicine degree licensed to practice veterinary medicine in this state or in the State of Tennessee or in the State of Alabama.
Nuisance includes, but is not limited to, defecation, urination, disturbing the peace, destroying private property, emitting noxious or offensive odors, emitting noxious or offensive sounds and noises, or otherwise endangering or offending the well being of the inhabitants of the city.
Owner of an animal means a person who owns, harbors or keeps an animal or knowingly permits an animal to be harbored or kept or has an animal in such person's care, or who permits an animal to remain on or about such person's premises for more than five days.
Premises means a parcel of land and the structures thereon.
Registered animal means an animal licensed by the city, as hereinafter required.
Vaccination means protection against rabies by inoculation with anti-rabies vaccine, given in an amount sufficient to provide immunity from rabies for a minimum of one year.
(Ord. No. 11-3-80, § 1; Ord. No. 08/02, § 2, 6-2-08)
Sec. 14-2. - Authority to go on private property.
Employees and agents of the city and persons under their control and supervision shall have the right to go on private property within the city for the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 15)
Sec. 14-3. - Rabies inoculation—Required annually; administering.
All animals over 90 days old within the city shall be inoculated annually so as to provide immunity from rabies for a period of at least one year. All inoculations shall be administered by a licensed veterinarian or an authorized representative of the public health department.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 3)
Sec. 14-4. - Same—Attaching to required collar or harness.
A valid rabies vaccination tag shall be securely attached to the animal's collar or harness, which collar or harness must be secured to and on the animal at all times, and it shall be the duty and responsibility of the owner to see that the rabies vaccination tag is so attached and secured at all times.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 4C)
Sec. 14-5. - Identification tag—Required; fees; application; period.
(a)
All animals over 90 days old within the city shall wear a collar or harness to which is attached a metal identification tag of the kind hereinafter prescribed.
(b)
Identification tags will be provided by the city upon presentation of a certificate of inoculation or vaccination for rabies to an employee of the city designated by the governing body and upon a payment of a fee as prescribed annually by the governing body. The fee for an unaltered animal may be greater than for an altered animal.
(c)
The application for the identification tag shall state the name and address of the owner, the name, breed, age, color and sex of such animal.
(d)
The license so granted by the city shall expire on March 31 of each calendar year, and shall be renewed on or before April 1 of each year.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 2A, D)
Sec. 14-6. - Same—Exemption.
Animals belonging to nonresidents of the city who do not keep their animals within the corporate limits of the city for 15 consecutive days shall be exempt from the provisions of section 14-5; provided, however, that all the other provisions of this chapter must be complied with.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 2B)
Sec. 14-7. - Same—Form.
Each city identification tag shall be so contrived as to permit it to be securely attached to the animal's collar or harness and shall have its respective number, the year in which issued and the words "City of Trenton" indented and impressed thereon. The design or color of such tag shall be varied from year to year so as to permit easy ascertainment as to whether or not the tag is current.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 4A)
Sec. 14-8. - Same—Attaching to required collar or harness.
A valid city identification tag shall be securely attached to the animal's collar or harness, which collar or harness must be secured to and on the animal at all times, and it shall be the duty and responsibility of the owner to see that the identification tag is so attached and secured at all times.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 4B)
Sec. 14-9. - Same—Record.
A complete record of animal tags issued by the city shall be kept by the city. Such records shall show when the tag was issued, the period of time for which the tag is valid, to whom the tag was issued and the address of such person, for what particular animal the tag was issued; and if the owner and keeper of the animal are different persons, then both names and addresses of each shall be entered.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 5)
Sec. 14-10. - Misuse of tag.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove a valid tag from any animal which has been tagged under the provisions of this chapter or to place a tag upon any animal for which the tag was not issued, or to procure a tag from the city or from any licensed veterinarian by misrepresentation, or to disfigure any tag or to alter the figures or words upon any tag. This provision shall apply to both the tag issued by the city and the tag for rabies inoculation issued by a licensed veterinarian or an authorized representative of the public health department.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 6)
Sec. 14-11. - Use of money.
All money collected for the issuance of identification tags and licenses shall go into the city treasury to help defray the expense of animal control.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 2C)
Sec. 14-12. - Restraint of animals.
The owner or keeper of each animal within the city shall be responsible for keeping it from becoming a nuisance or from endangering or injuring any other person or damaging any property, and to that end:
(1)
It shall be unlawful for the owner or keeper of any animal to allow such animal to go on the property of any other person or on any public right-of-way, roadway or street;
(2)
When allowed off of the property of the owner or keeper, all animals shall be accompanied by the owner or other responsible keeper and be restrained on a leash, rope, chain or other substantial leading device held by the owner or keeper or be in a vehicle driven or parked, from which the animal cannot escape.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 7)
Sec. 14-13. - Impoundment—Authorized; notice.
(a)
The following animals shall be subject to impoundment by the city and its agents and employees in any impoundment provided for that purpose by the city as follows:
(1)
All animals not properly inoculated or vaccinated as provided for herein;
(2)
All animals upon which a valid tag indicating inoculation against rabies is inappropriately attached as provided for herein;
(3)
All animals upon which a valid city identification tag is not secured as provided for herein;
(4)
All animals not being kept and maintained in accordance with section 14-12;
(5)
All animals found on the premises of any person other than the owner without permission of the owner of the premises upon which such animal is found; and
(6)
All animals found running loose in the city.
(b)
Notice of the impoundment of any animal must be given immediately by United States mail or by telephone call to the owner as indicated on the registration records of the city or on any other identification tag found on the animal.
(c)
The notice of impoundment shall contain a general description of the animal impounded, the kind of animal, the breed of the animal if readily ascertainable, the color of the animal and the sex of the animal.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 8)
Sec. 14-14. - Same—Redemption or other disposal of animal not claimed.
(a)
A registered animal shall be confined at the place of impoundment for a period of at least eight days from the date of mailing the notice to the owner or five days from the date of notice being given by telephone. An animal that is not registered shall be confined at the place of impoundment for a period of at least three days.
(b)
During the aforesaid period of time the owner of the animal may claim the animal and such animal may be redeemed by the owner by the payment to the city of the cost of feeding the animal during the confinement period and the cost of inoculation if such animal has not been inoculated within the preceding 12-month period.
(c)
The city may dispose of any animals not claimed and redeemed by the owner, as provided for herein, on the first day after the required impoundment period as hereinabove provided.
(d)
The following methods of disposal may be used by the city:
(1)
The animal may be placed in an adoptive home;
(2)
The animal may be sold;
(3)
The animal may be destroyed in such a humane method as the city may deem appropriate.
(e)
In the case of the sale of such animal, the city is hereby authorized to execute a bill of sale to any such purchaser, which shall be sufficient to vest title to the animal in such purchaser.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 9)
Sec. 14-15. - Rabid animal—General; confinement; notice; surrendering animal.
An animal that has rabies or shows signs of having rabies, and every animal bitten by another animal afflicted with rabies or that has been exposed to rabies shall be confined at once in a secure place by the owner or keeper of such animal. A person who knows or has reason to know that an animal is infected with rabies or has been exposed to rabies shall immediately upon learning of the infection or exposure notify the police department as to the place where the animal is confined and shall surrender such animal to the city, its agents or employees upon demand.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 10)
Sec. 14-16. - Same—Animal biting a person.
(a)
The owner or keeper of an animal that bites a person and a person bitten by an animal shall have a duty to report that occurrence to the city police department or city hall within 24 hours from such occurrence. The owner and/or keeper of an animal that bites a person shall surrender that animal to the city, its agents or employees if it is necessary to impound such animal for a period of observation.
(b)
A physician who renders professional treatment to a person bitten by an animal shall report the fact that such physician has rendered professional treatment to the police or city hall within 24 hours of such physician's first professional attendance of such person. The physician shall report the name, sex and address of the person bitten as well as the type and location of the bite. The physician shall give the name and address of the owner and/or keeper of the animal that inflicted the bite, if known, and any other facts that may assist the city in ascertaining the immunization status of the animal.
(c)
An animal that bites a person shall be confined securely for such a period of time as necessary, but not less than ten days. The owner of the animal shall bear the cost of confinement in such place of confinement as is deemed appropriate. Consent may be given to confinement on the owner's premises, but only if the animal had a current rabies vaccination at the time the bite was inflicted. The premises where the home confinement is to occur shall be inspected and approved for such purpose by the city, an agent or employee thereof.
(d)
A person who has custody of an animal that has bitten a person shall immediately notify the city if the animal shows any signs of sickness, abnormal behavior or if the animal escapes confinement. If the animal dies while it is in confinement, the person having custody of the animal shall notify the city and shall surrender the carcass of the animal to the city.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 11)
Sec. 14-17. - Same—Extermination.
The city, its agents and employees, are authorized to exterminate any rabid animal found within the city.
(Ord. of 11-3-80, § 12)

Sec. 14-18. - Definitions.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
(1)
Classified dog means any dog that has been classified as either a dangerous dog or vicious dog pursuant to this article.
(2)
Dangerous dog means any dog that:
a.
Causes a substantial puncture of a person's skin by teeth without causing serious injury; provided, however, that a nip, scratch, or abrasion shall not be sufficient to classify a dog as dangerous under this subparagraph;
b.
Aggressively attacks in a manner that causes a person to reasonably believe that the dog posed an imminent threat of serious injury to such person or another person although no such injury occurs; provided, however, that the acts of barking, growling, or showing of teeth by a dog shall not be sufficient to classify a dog as dangerous under this subparagraph; or
c.
While off the owner's property, kills a pet animal; provided however that this subparagraph shall not apply where the death of such pet animal is caused by a dog that is working or training as a hunting dog, herding dog, or predator control dog.
(3)
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dog. In the case of a dog owned by a minor, the term "owner" includes the parents or person in loco parentis with custody of the minor.
(4)
Serious injury means any physical injury that creates a substantial risk of death; results in death, broken or dislocated bones, lacerations requiring multiple sutures, or disfiguring avulsions; requires plastic surgery or admission to a hospital; or results in protracted impairment of health, including transmission of an infection or contagious disease, or impairment of the function of any bodily organ.
(5)
Vicious dog means a dog that inflicts serious injury on a person or causes serious injury to a person resulting from reasonable attempts to escape from the dog's attack.
(b)
No dog shall be classified as a dangerous dog or vicious dog for actions that occur while the dog is being used by a law enforcement or military officer to carry out the law enforcement or military officer's official duties. No dog shall be classified as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog if the person injured by such dog was a person who, at the time, was committing a trespass, was abusing the dog, or was committing or attempting to commit an offense under O.C.G.A. § 16-5-1, et. seq.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-19. - Investigations by animal control officer; notice to owner; hearings; determinations by hearing authority; judicial review.
(a)
For purposes of this Code section, the term:
(1)
Animal shelter shall have the same meaning as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 4-14-2.
(2)
Board means the City of Trenton animal control board.
(3)
Mail means to send by certified mail or statutory overnight delivery to the recipient's last known address.
(b)
Upon receiving a report of a dog believed to be subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog, an animal control officer shall make such investigations as necessary to determine whether such dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog.
(c)
When an animal control officer determines that a dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog, the animal control officer shall mail a dated notice to the dog's owner within 72 hours. Such notice shall include a summary of the animal control officer's determination and shall state that the owner has a right to request a hearing from the board regarding the animal control officer's determination within seven days after the date shown on the notice. The notice shall provide a form for requesting the hearing and shall state that if a request for a hearing is not received by the board within the allotted time, the animal control officer's determination shall become conclusive for all purposes under this article. If an owner cannot be located within ten days of the animal control officer's determination that a dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog, such dog may be released to an animal shelter or humanely euthanized, as determined by the sole discretion of the animal control officer.
(d)
When a hearing is requested by a dog owner in accordance with subsection (c) of this Code section, such hearing shall be scheduled within 30 days after the request is received; provided, however, that such hearing may be continued by the board for good cause shown, At least ten days prior to the hearing, the board shall mail to the dog owner written notice of the date, time, and place of the hearing. At the hearing, the dog owner shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence and the board shall receive other evidence and testimony as may be reasonably necessary to sustain, modify, or overrule the animal control officer's determination.
(e)
Within ten days after the hearing, the board shall mail written notice to the dog owner of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, the notice of classification shall specify the date upon which that determination shall be effective. If the determination is that the dog is to be euthanized pursuant to section 14-21, the notice shall specify the date by which the euthanasia shall occur.
(f)
Judicial review of the board's final decision may be had to the Dade County Probate Court in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 15-9-30.9. Judicial review of the probate court's final decision shall be to the Dade County Superior Court in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 5-3-22.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-20. - Impoundment.
A law enforcement officer or animal control officer shall immediately impound a dog if the officer believes the dog poses a threat to the public safety.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-21. - Euthanasia for dogs with repeat offenses.
A dog that is found, after notice and opportunity for hearing as provided by section 14-19 above, to have caused a serious injury to a human on more than one occasion shall be euthanized; provided, however, that no injury occurring before July 1, 2012, shall count for purposes of this article.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-22. - Certificates of registration; requirements for issuance of certificate; individuals excluded from receiving registration; limitation of ownership; annual renewal.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner to have or possess within this state a classified dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with the provisions of this section. Certificates of registration shall be nontransferable and shall only be issued to a person 18 years of age or older. No more than one certificate of registration shall be issued per domicile.
(b)
Unless otherwise specified by this section, a certificate of registration for a dangerous dog shall be issued if an animal control officer determines that the following requirements have been met:
(1)
The owner has maintained an enclosure designed to securely confine the dangerous dog on the owner's property, indoors, or in a securely locked and enclosed pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the dangerous dog from leaving such property; and
(2)
Clearly visible warning signs have been posted at all entrances to the premises where the dog resides.
(c)
Except as provided in subsections (e) and (f) of this section, a certificate of registration for vicious dog shall be issued if an animal control officer determines that the following requirements have been met:
(1)
The owner has maintained an enclosure designed to securely confine the vicious dog on the owner's property, indoors, or in a securely locked and enclosed pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the vicious dog from leaving such property; and
(2)
Clearly visible warning signs have been posted at all entrances to the premises where the dog resides;
(3)
A microchip containing an identification number and capable of being scanned has been injected under the skin between the shoulder blades of the dog; and
(4)
The owner maintains and can provide proof of general or specific liability insurance in the amount of at least $50,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the State of Georgia insuring the owner of the vicious dog against liability for any bodily injury of property damage caused by the dog.
(d)
No certificate of registration shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of two or more violations of this article.
(e)
No person shall be the owner of more than one vicious dog.
(f)
No certificate of registration for a vicious dog shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of:
(1)
A serious violent felony as defined in O.C.G.A. § 17-10-6.1;
(2)
The felony of dogfighting as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-37 or the felony of aggravated cruelty to animals as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4; or
(3)
A felony involving trafficking in cocaine, illegal drugs, marijuana, methamphetamine, or ecstasy as provided for in O.C.G.A. §§ 16-13-31 and 16-13-31.1 from the time of conviction until two years after completion of his or her sentence, nor to any person residing with such person.
(g)
Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of renewal of a certificate of registration for a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, an animal control officer shall verify that the owner is continuing to comply with provisions of this article. Failure to renew a certificate of registration within ten days of renewal date or initial classification date shall constitute a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-23. - Notifications by owner; change in ownership of dog; changes in residence.
(a)
The owner of a classified dog shall notify an animal control officer in writing within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose or has attacked a human and shall notify an animal control officer in writing within 24 hours if the dog has died or has been euthanized.
(b)
A vicious dog shall not be transferred, sold, or donated to any other person unless it is relinquished to a governmental facility or veterinarian to be euthanized.
(c)
The owner of a classified dog who moves from one jurisdiction to another within the State of Georgia shall register the classified dog in the new jurisdiction within ten days of becoming a resident and notify an animal control officer in writing of the jurisdiction from which he or she moved. The owner of a similarly classified dog who moves into the State of Georgia shall register the dog as required in section 14-22 within 30 days of becoming a resident.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-24. - Limitations on dog's presence off of owner's premises; penalty for violation; defense.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be off the owner's property unless:
(1)
The dog is restrained by a leash not to exceed six feet in length and is under the immediate physical control of a person capable of preventing the dog from engaging any other human or animal when necessary;
(2)
The dog is contained in a closed and locked cage or crate; or
(3)
The dog is working or training as a hunting dog, herding dog, or predator control dog.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a vicious dog to permit the dog to be:
(1)
Outside an enclosure designed to securely confine the vicious dog while on the owner's property or outside a securely locked and enclosed pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the vicious dog from leaving such property unless:
a.
The dog is muzzled and restrained by a leash not to exceed six feet in length and is under the immediate physical control of a person capable of preventing the dog from engaging any other human or animal when necessary; or
b.
The dog is contained in a closed and locked cage or crate.
(2)
Unattended with minors.
(c)
A person who violates subsection (b) of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor of high and aggravated nature.
(d)
An owner with a previous conviction for a violation of this article whose classified dog causes serious injury to a human being under circumstances constituting another violation of this article shall be guilty of a felony and upon conviction thereof shall be punished by imprisonment for not less than one nor more than ten years, a fine of not less than $5,000.00 nor more than $10,000.00, or both. In addition the classified dog shall be euthanized at the cost of the owner.
(e)
Any irregularity in classification proceedings shall not be a defense to any prosecution under this article so long as the owner of the dog received actual notice of the classification and did not pursue a civil remedy for the correction of the irregularity.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-25. - Confiscation by animal control officer; payment of costs for recovery; euthanasia.
(a)
A dangerous dog or vicious dog shall be immediately confiscated by an animal control officer or by a law enforcement officer in the case of any violation of this article. A refusal to surrender a dog subject to confiscation shall be a violation of this article.
(b)
The owner of any dog that has been confiscated pursuant to this article may recover such dog upon payment of all reasonable confiscation and housing costs and proof of compliance with the provisions of this article, unless such confiscation is deemed to be in error by an animal control officer or the board. All fines and all charges for services performed by a law enforcement or dog control officer shall be paid prior to owner recovery of the dog. Criminal prosecution shall not be stayed due to owner recovery or euthanasia of the dog.
(c)
In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within 14 days of the date the dog was confiscated, such dog shall be released to an animal shelter, as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 4-14-2, or euthanized in an expeditious and humane manner. The owner may be required to pay the costs of housing and euthanasia.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-26. - Governmental liability for enforcement.
Under no circumstances shall the City of Trenton or any employee or official of the City of Trenton be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog as a result of a failure to enforce the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-27. - Penalty for violation.
Except as otherwise specified in this article, any person who violates any provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
Sec. 14-28. - Application and compliance.
(a)
(1)
Any dog classified prior to July 1, 2012, as a potentially dangerous dog in the State of Georgia shall on and after that date by classified as a dangerous dog under this article.
(2)
Any dog classified prior to July 1, 2012, as a dangerous dog or vicious dog in the State of Georgia shall on and after that date by classified as a vicious dog under this article.
(Ord. No. 16/02, § 2, 6-13-16)
