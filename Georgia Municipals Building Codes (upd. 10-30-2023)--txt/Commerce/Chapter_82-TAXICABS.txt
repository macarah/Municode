Chapter 82 - TAXICABS[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2017-0900, adopted November 20, 2017, repealed ch. 82 and enacted a new ch. 82 as set out herein. Former ch. 82 pertained to vehicles for hire and derived from Ord. No. 93-008, adopted April 12, 1993.

Sec. 82-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Open stand means locations on the streets of the city that may be used by any taxicab on a nonexclusive, first-come-first-served basis, and not by private vehicles or other public conveyances.
Taxicab means a motor vehicle used to transport passengers for a fee or fare and which is fitted with a taximeter or other device that is used to compute such fee or fare. Taxicabs shall not include limousine carriers or ride share drivers as defined in O.C.G.A. §40-1-90(1) and (3).
Taxicab company means an entity or person operating a taxicab or providing taxi services as defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-90(5).
Taximeter means an instrument or device attached to a motor vehicle and designed to measure the distance traveled by such vehicle, or an instrument or device attached to a motor vehicle and designed to compute and indicate the fare or fee to be charged to the passenger.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-2. - Doing business defined.
Any taxicab company operating a taxicab within the incorporated boundaries of the city or with an established business relationship with independent contractors operating a taxicab shall be deemed doing business in the city under this article if such person is picking up passengers in the city and accepting or soliciting any consideration, charge or fee which is determined by agreement, by mileage, by the length of time the vehicle is used or by contract for the use of any motor vehicle or other vehicle designed or used for the purpose of transporting passengers.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-3. - Compliance.
No person or entity shall do business as a taxicab company in the city without first meeting the requirements of state law and obtaining a taxicab company business license. No person or entity shall operate a taxicab without a for-hire license endorsement or a private background check pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-5-39.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-4. - Taxicab company permit holder's responsibilities for violations.
Taxicab company business license's holder(s) are responsible for violations of this article by their employees and independent contractors, including company drivers and dispatchers.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-5. - Taxicab company permits generally.
(a)
An applicant for a taxicab company business license is required to provide information showing its qualifications on a form provided by the city clerk.
(b)
No taxicab company business shall be issued to an applicant unless the applicant has a valid and current license to do business in the state and all other licenses required by the state,
(c)
The business license required by the provisions of this article shall expire on December 31 of each year.
(d)
All applicants must:
(1)
Provide a verifiable dispatch location staffed by company agents or employees, have a published telephone number and have sufficient parking to accommodate its taxicabs when not in use;
(2)
Comply with requirements of state law for transportation referral service providers, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-190(8);
(3)
Provide a copy of the applicant's rate schedule and the daily hours of operation; and
(4)
Provide the name, address and telephone number of a responsible individual residing in the county who will be the registered agent for the purpose of service of process or receipt of citations.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-6. - Insurance.
All companies licensed pursuant to the provisions of this article shall maintain for the duration of their business license minimum insurance coverage as required by state law for vehicles and drivers.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-7. - Transfer and term of permits.
No taxicab company business license is transferable; and they shall expire annually as provided in this article. If not renewed in compliance with this article, all such licenses shall expire annually and be of no further force and effect.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-8. - Certificate.
The city manager may periodically require the owner or operator of a taxicab to submit a copy of the State of Georgia certificate of inspection for the information of the police department and the mayor and council. The certificate shall be in the form prescribed by Georgia law.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-9. - Suspension, revocation—Grounds.
The willful violation of any ordinance of the city or the laws of the state shall be grounds for the suspension or permanent revocation of a taxicab business license as set out in this division.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-10. - Same—Power of city manager.
When the mayor and council are not in session, the city manager shall have the authority and power, in his discretion, upon sufficient evidence or information, to suspend the business license of any taxicab owner or operator for a period of 30 days by a written order, a copy of which shall be served upon the owner or operator of a taxicab within two days after the suspension of his license, and he shall address a communication in writing to the council of his action in suspending such permit and the reason therefor.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-11. - Same—Appeal.
The owner or operator of a taxicab shall be entitled to a hearing before the mayor and council upon application therefor, for the body to determine whether or not the suspension of his license to operate a taxicab shall be made permanent or not within the city; provided such application is filed with the city clerk within five days from the date of suspension by the city manager. If no appeal is filed then the suspension may be stopped, continued or made permanent by the council at its next regular meeting,
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-12. - Operation of taxicab during suspension.
It shall be unlawful for an owner or operator or any driver employed by such owner or operator during the period of suspension of a taxicab business license to furnish service and a violation of this section shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-12. Such penalty shall be in addition to the suspension.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-13. - Disposition of business license during suspension.
Any taxicab license of any owner or operator in the city which has been suspended shall be returned to the city manager, to be retained by him during the period of suspension or until the suspension is revoked and relieved by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-14. - Revocation.
If the suspension of a taxicab license is made permanent and the license is revoked by the mayor and council, the license shall be canceled by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-15. - Taxicab zones.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park or cause to be parked any vehicle except a taxicab within those areas where the curbing is marked by lines and where there is a sign denoting the same as a parking space for taxicabs only, such areas being reserved for the use of taxicabs only.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-16. - Driver's license.
No taxi driver shall operate a taxicab in the city before the person who is to drive the taxicab is approved for driving a motor vehicle by the state department of public safety.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-17. - Owner's identification, rate card displayed.
(a)
Every taxicab shall display inside the cab, at a convenient place visible to passengers, a card not less than two inches in width by four inches in length, which shall have printed thereon the name, business address and telephone number of the owner and the rates charged for the use of such vehicle.
(b)
Every such car shall have displayed on each side thereof the name and telephone number of the owner in letters and numerals of such size as to be readily visible to the public, and not less than three inches high.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
Sec. 82-18. - Fare—Refusal to pay.
It shall be unlawful for any person to use a motor vehicle operated as a taxicab and refuse to pay therefor.
(Ord. No. 2017-0900, 11-20-17)
