Sec. 6-1. - Terms of office.
The terms of office for mayor and councilmen shall begin on the second Thursday in January following their respective election, and shall continue for four years and until their successors are elected and qualified, except as provided in section 2.08 of the city Charter.
(Ord. No. 442, § I, 12-21-88)
Charter reference— Terms of office, § 2.04.
Sec. 6-2. - Opening date.
The opening date to file notice of candidacy pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132(d)(3) for the office of mayor and councilmember of the City of Thomson, Georgia for the general election shall be the last Monday in August immediately preceding the general election.
(Ord. No. 459, § I, 10-11-90; Ord. No. 560, § I, 1-12-95 )
Sec. 6-3. - Closing date.
The closing date to file notice of candidacy pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132(d)(3) for the office of mayor and councilmember of the City of Thomson, Georgia for the general election shall end no later than 4:30 p.m. on the following Friday.
(Ord. No. 459, § II, 10-11-90; Ord. No. 560, § II, 1-12-95 )
Sec. 6-4. - County voter registrar to conduct city elections.
The City of Thomson authorizes and directs the McDuffie County Voter Registrar to conduct any and all elections held by the City of Thomson from August 10, 1995 forward.
(Ord. No. 484, 8-10-95)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 484, adopted Aug. 10, 1995, has been codified herein at the discretion of the editor as § 6-4.
