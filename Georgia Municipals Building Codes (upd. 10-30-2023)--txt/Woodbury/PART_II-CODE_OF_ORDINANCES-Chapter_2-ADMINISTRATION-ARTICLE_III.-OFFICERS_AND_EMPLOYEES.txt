DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-146. - Administrative policy and procedures for officers.
Each officer shall perform all duties required of his office by state law, the Charter, this Code, and such other duties not in conflict as may be required by the mayor.
(Code 1980, § 3-103(1); Code 2001, § 2-151)
Sec. 2-147. - Oaths.
All officers and employees required by the Charter or some other provision of law to take an oath shall, before entering upon the discharge of their respective duties, take and subscribe the following oath before an officer authorized by law to administer oaths:
I, ___________, do solemnly swear or affirm that I will support the Constitution of the United States, the Constitution of the State of Georgia, the Charter and ordinances of the City of Woodbury; and that I will to the best of my ability, faithfully perform the duties of the office of ___________ during my continuance therein, so help me God.
(Code 1980, § 3-104; Code 2001, § 2-152)
Sec. 2-148. - Bonds.
Except as otherwise provided by law, the mayor may require any department head, city official or employee, before entering upon the discharge of his duties, to give good and sufficient bond in any amount decided by the mayor. This bond shall be payable to the city for the faithful performance of such duties and to secure against corruption, malfeasance, misappropriations, or unlawful expenditures in office. The surety bond shall be obtained from a surety company licensed to do business in the state and approved by the mayor. The premiums shall be paid by the city.
(Code 1980, § 3-105; Code 2001, § 2-153)
Sec. 2-149. - Compensation.
Each officer and employee of the city shall receive such compensation as may be provided from time to time by resolution.
(Code 1980, § 3-106; Code 2001, § 2-154)
Sec. 2-150. - Accidents.
Employees involved in or having any knowledge of any accident involving any other person employed by the city or any property or equipment owned or operated by the city shall immediately report the accident and pertinent information to their department head, who shall forward such information to the office of the city clerk. The city clerk shall record the information on three copies of the applicable accident form, one copy to be forwarded to the insurance carrier, one copy to the city attorney, and one copy retained on file in the city clerk's office.
(Code 1980, § 3-601(2); Code 2001, § 2-155)
Sec. 2-151. - Municipal court judge.
The judge of the municipal court shall be appointed by the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of the council, and shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council. To be eligible to hold such office, the judge must meet the qualifications as set by state law.
(Code 1980, § 5-102; Code 2001, § 2-156)
Sec. 2-152. - Personnel provisions not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code, or in the ordinance adopting this Code, shall affect any ordinance or resolution relating to the city's personnel classification system, employee pay plan, retirement plan, or employee benefits. All such ordinances are recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out in this Code.
(Code 2001, § 2-157)
Secs. 2-153—2-172. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - MAYOR

Sec. 2-173. - General authority.
The mayor shall be the chief executive and administrative officer of the city government, shall enforce the laws of the city and shall require the faithful performance of all administrative duties.
(Code 1980, § 3-301; Code 2001, § 2-181)
Sec. 2-174. - Preparation of annual report.
The mayor shall prepare and present to the council such reports of the city's affairs, including a summary of reports of department heads, and such other reports as he deems appropriate or as the council may, by majority vote, require.
(Code 1980, § 3-302(5); Code 2001, § 2-182)
Sec. 2-175. - Executing legal documents.
The mayor shall sign and execute all contracts, deeds and bonds of the city duly authorized by the mayor and council.
(Code 1980, § 3-302(6); Code 2001, § 2-183)
Sec. 2-176. - Rulemaking powers.
The mayor shall have the powers to prescribe such rules and regulations as may be deemed necessary or expedient for the conduct of administrative agencies subject to his authority and to revoke, suspend or amend any rule or regulation of the administrative service by whomever prescribed.
(Code 1980, § 3-303(1); Code 2001, § 2-184)
Sec. 2-177. - Investigation.
The mayor shall investigate and examine or inquire into, either by himself or by any officer or person designated for the purpose by him, the affairs or operation of any department, including the power to employ consultants and professional counsel when so authorized by the council to aid in such investigations, examinations or inquiries.
(Code 1980, § 3-303(2); Code 2001, § 2-185)
Sec. 2-178. - Overriding.
The mayor may set aside any action taken by a department head and may supersede him in the functions of his office.
(Code 1980, § 3-303(3); Code 2001, § 2-186)
Sec. 2-179. - Delegation.
The mayor may direct any department to perform the work for any other department and authorize any department head or officer responsible to him to appoint and remove subordinates serving under such person.
(Code 1980, § 3-303(4); Code 2001, § 2-187)
Sec. 2-180. - Acting mayor.
In the event of a vacancy in the office of mayor, the council may appoint one of its members as acting mayor to serve until the vacancy is filled at a regular or special election as provided by law.
(Code 1980, § 3-305; Code 2001, § 2-188)
Secs. 2-181—2-198. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - CITY CLERK-TREASURER

Sec. 2-199. - Appointment.
The mayor shall, by and with the advice and consent of the city council, appoint a city clerk-treasurer.
(Code 2001, § 2-211; Ord. of 6-9-2003, § 1)
Sec. 2-200. - Term of office.
The city clerk-treasurer shall hold office, subject to removal for cause, until his successor is appointed and qualified.
(Code 2001, § 2-212; Ord. of 6-9-2003, § 2)
Sec. 2-201. - Bond.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city clerk-treasurer shall give a good and sufficient bond, payable to the city council. Such bond is to be fixed and approved by the mayor.
(Code 1980, § 3-403; Code 2001, § 2-213)
Sec. 2-202. - Duties as city clerk.
The city clerk-treasurer shall have the following duties in his capacity as city clerk:
(1)
To attend all meetings of the city council;
(2)
To keep correct and full minutes of the proceedings of city council, together with all ordinances and resolutions passed by it, in a properly indexed book or register kept for that purpose;
(3)
To receive all applications or petitions made to the city and to place them before the mayor and city council at the meeting of council next succeeding their receipt;
(4)
To issue all licenses, and keep a record of such licenses, and all badges and permits authorized by the council;
(5)
To attend all sessions of the recorder's court;
(6)
To keep an execution docket, in which he shall enter the names of all persons tried and fined by the municipal court, the nature of the offense, date of trial, amount of fine, and return of the police officer;
(7)
To issue all summonses, processes and subpoenas to witnesses that may be necessary in the enforcement of this Code or other rules, regulations and ordinances of the city council;
(8)
To be the custodian of the city seal and affix its impression on documents whenever required; and
(9)
To carefully preserve the records and documents belonging to the city which are not assigned to the custody of some other office, and to maintain a proper index to all such records and documents so that ready access to and use of such records and documents may be had.
(Code 1980, § 3-404; Code 2001, § 2-214)
Sec. 2-203. - Duties as city treasurer.
The city clerk-treasurer shall have the following duties in his capacity as city treasurer:
(1)
To receive all money due the city council, including taxes, and pay out the money only upon orders passed by the city council and signed by the mayor, or in his absence, the mayor pro tempore;
(2)
To keep a book of accounts showing all money received on behalf of the city and its source and disposition, which book shall be open for inspection by the public and city councilmembers;
(3)
To maintain a uniform system of accounts and keep such other records and accounts as may be required by statute or ordinance;
(4)
To furnish the mayor with quarterly statements detailing all receipts and payments of funds for the quarter; and
(5)
To carry out such other obligations as may be specifically delegated to or demanded of him by other provisions of this Code.
(Code 1980, § 3-405; Code 2001, § 2-215)
Secs. 2-204—2-229. - Reserved.
