ARTICLE III. - MASSAGE THERAPY LICENSING

Sec. 11-65. - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply to this article. Any word or phrase not defined below but otherwise defined in this article shall be given that meaning. All other words or phrases shall be given their common ordinary meaning unless the context clearly requires otherwise.
The term massage or massages or massage therapy shall mean the systematic and scientific manipulation and treatment of soft tissues of the body, including but not limited to the use of effleurage, petrissage, pressure, friction, tapotement, kneading, vibration, range of motion stretches, and any other soft tissue manipulation whether manual or by use of massage apparatus, and may include the use of oils, lotions, creams, salt glows, hydrotherapy, heliotherapy, hot packs, and cold packs. This term shall not include diagnosis, the prescribing of drugs or medicines, spinal or other joint manipulations, or any service or procedure for which a license to practice chiropractic, physical therapy, podiatry, or medicine is required by the state.
The term massage apparatus shall mean any manual, mechanical, hydraulic, hydrokinetic, electric, or electronic device or instrument or any device or instrument operated by manual, mechanical, hydraulic, hydrokinetic or electric power, which is utilized by a "massage therapist" for the purpose of administering a "massage"
The term massage apprentice shall mean any person who by direct or indirect physical contact with the body of another, or use and operation of "massage apparatus," administers a "massage" in connection with a formal program of apprenticeship and training.
The term massage establishment shall mean any business established for profit which employs one (1) or more "message therapists," or operates or maintains for profit one (1) or more "massage apparatus", and which, for good or valuable consideration, offers to the public facilities and personnel for the administration of "massages". This term shall not include hospitals or other professional health care establishments separately licensed as such by the state.
The term massage therapist shall mean any person who for good or valuable consideration administers a "massage"
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-66. - Licenses/permits required.
(a)
Message therapist license. It shall be unlawful for any natural person to administer massages as a massage therapist without having obtained a license in accordance with the requirements of this article.
(b)
Massage establishment license. It shall be unlawful for any person, natural or corporate, to operate a massage establishment without having obtained a license therefor; or for any person, natural or corporate, to allow a massage therapist to administer massages without having obtained a license in accordance with the requirements of this article.
(c)
Massage apprentice permit. It shall be unlawful for any natural person to administer massages as a massage apprentice without having obtained a permit in accordance with the requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-67. - Scope of regulations.
(a)
All licenses and permits issued under this article shall constitute a mere privilege to conduct the business so authorized during the term of the license or permit only and subject to all terms and conditions imposed by the city, county and state law.
(b)
Nothing in this article shall be construed to regulate, prevent, or restrict in any manner: (i) any physician, chiropractor, physical therapist, or similar professional licensed and regulated by the state while engaged in the practice of said profession; or (ii) any hospital or other professional health care establishments separately licensed as such by the state; or (iii) any other individual or entity expressly exempted from local legislation by the laws of Georgia.
(c)
Except as specified in subsection (b) of this section, the requirements of this article shall be in addition to all other licensing, taxing, and regulatory provisions of local, county, state or federal law, and shall not authorize violations of said other applicable laws.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-68. - Application process.
(a)
Any person desiring to obtain a massage establishment license or massage therapist license shall first make application to DeKalb County pursuant to the provisions of the Code of Ordinances of DeKalb County as codified in article VIII of chapter 15 and obtain a valid license from DeKalb County. Such county license shall be current and must be presented to the city clerk prior to obtaining a license in the city.
(b)
Applications to the city shall be made to the city clerk on forms provided by the city and shall contain the following minimum information:
(1)
The full legal name of the applicant, including all aliases, nicknames, pseudonyms or trade names currently or heretofore used by the applicant;
(2)
The current and all previous business and residence address of the applicant within three (3) years immediately preceding the date of application;
(3)
Written proof that the applicant is over eighteen (18) years of age;
(4)
The applicant's height, weight, and color of eyes and hair;
(5)
All convictions, pleas of guilty or pleas of nolo contendere for violations of any law and the grounds and disposition of any pending charges against the applicant;
(6)
If the applicant is a corporation or partnership, such corporation or partnership shall, in addition to the foregoing information, submit a complete list of the stockholders or partners, including names, current addresses and current occupations.
(c)
All license applications shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable fee in the amount of two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) to defray the cost of processing the application.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-69. - Minimum standards.
(a)
Message therapist. No applicant shall be issued a license as a message therapist unless all of the following standards are first met:
(1)
The applicant must have obtained and hold a current license as a message therapist from DeKalb County issued pursuant to Article VIII of Chapter 15, DeKalb County Code of Ordinances.
(2)
The applicant must be of good moral character. No applicant shall be found to have met this requirement if said applicant has been convicted, pled guilty, or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any felony, or to any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, within a period of five (5) years prior to the filing of the application; and
(3)
One of the following is established.
a.
The applicant must be the holder of a diploma or certificate earned by the applicant from a state certified school, representative of the fact that the applicant attended a course of message therapy education and study of not less than five hundred (500) classroom hours consisting of a curriculum of anatomy and physiology, basic massage theory, technique and clinical practice, approach to massage, allied modalities and disease awareness, and other such subjects; or
b.
The applicant must have had an apprenticeship and practical experience for a period of not less than one thousand (1,000) hours in a regularly licensed massage establishment in which the applicant has received massage therapy training and experience in anatomy and physiology, basic massage theory, technique and clinical practice, approach to massage, allied modalities and disease awareness, and other such subjects.
(b)
Massage establishment. No applicant shall be issued a license for a massage establishment unless all of the following standards are first met:
(1)
The applicant must have obtained and hold a current license as a message establishment from DeKalb County issued pursuant to Article VIII of Chapter 15, DeKalb County Code of Ordinances;
(2)
The applicant, including the partner applying on behalf of a partnership and an agent applying on behalf of a corporation, must be of good moral character. No applicant shall be found to have met this requirement if said applicant has been convicted, pled guilty, or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any felony, or to any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, within a period of five (5) years prior to the filing of the application;
(3)
A corporate applicant must be chartered under the laws of Georgia or authorized by the Secretary of State to do business in Georgia. The applicant shall be the owner or legal agent of the establishment. A massage therapist who is a sole proprietor shall not be required to obtain a massage establishment license;
(4)
A readable sign shall be posted at the main entrance identifying the establishment as a massage establishment, provided also that all such signs shall comply with the sign requirements of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Clarkston;
(5)
Minimum lighting shall be provided in accordances with the Uniform Building Code of DeKalb County, and additionally, at least one (1) artificial light of not less than forty (40) watts shall be provided and be kept illuminated in each enclosed room or booth;
(6)
Examining tables and other equipment in the establishment shall be specifically designed for and appropriate to the provision of professional therapeutic massage and related therapy. Ordinary beds shall not be permitted in any licensed massage establishment;
(7)
The establishment, prior to the issuance of any license hereunder, must be in compliance with all applicable building and life safety codes, and the building to be occupied must have a valid, current certificate of occupancy;
(8)
The establishment shall be located in a district within the city which is zoned MI (Light Industry District) and shall not be located within one thousand (1,000) feet of any establishment which is licensed under the city ordinances regulating adult entertainment establishments.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-70. - Issuance of license.
(a)
Review of applications. If a license application is submitted in proper form accompanied by proof that the applicant has a current license issued by DeKalb County in accordance with Article VIII of Chapter 15 of its Code of Ordinances, including all information required by this article, and accompanied by the correct fee, the application shall be accepted by the city clerk and a review of the application and an inspection and background investigation shall be conducted by the chief of police who shall submit a summary of his investigation to the city clerk.
(b)
Action on application. Upon receipt of this background investigation and completion of a review by the city clerk, the city clerk shall act on the application. The city clerk shall deny any application that:
(1)
Fails to meet the application requirements specified herein;
(2)
Fails to meet each of the minimum standards specified in section 11-69; or
(3)
Contains false information in the application or any attachments thereto.
Otherwise, the clerk shall approve the application and license shall be issued upon payment of any applicable city business or occupational taxes. All licenses issued pursuant to this article shall be valid through December 31 of the year in which they are issued. If an application is denied, the applicant shall not be authorized to reapply for a license under this article for a period of one (1) year from the date of denial. If the city clerk fails to act upon an application within in thirty (30) days of receipt of the completed application, it shall be accepted and a license shall be issued.
(c)
Appeals of denials of applications. In the event the city clerk denies a license or apprentice permit application, such denial shall be in writing, addressed to the applicant at the application address, and shall state the grounds upon which the denial is based. Within ten (10) days of the date of issuance of such notice, the applicant may appeal the denial by submitting a written notice of appeal to the city clerk. The city clerk shall schedule a hearing on the appeal before the city council, said hearing to take place within thirty (30) days of receipt of the notice of appeal and shall provide written notice thereof to the appellant. At the hearing, the appellant and the city clerk may each present evidence relating to the grounds for denial. The appellant may be represented by counsel or may appear in his own behalf.
The city council shall find for the appellant if it finds the city clerk's action was based on an erroneous finding of fact or that he acted in an arbitrary manner. In exercising its powers, the city council may reverse or affirm, or may modify, the decision appealed from, and to that end shall have all the powers of the city clerk and may issue or direct the issuance of a license or apprentice permit provided all requirements imposed by applicable law are met.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-71. - Transfers and sales prohibited.
All licenses and permits issued pursuant to this article are nontransferable
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-72. - Change of location.
A change of location of a massage establishment premises may be approved by the finance department provided all general ordinances are complied with and the distance and location requirements set forth in section 11-69 are satisfied at the new location. There shall be a fee of ten dollars ($10.00) for transfer of location.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-73. - Renewals.
All valid licenses may be renewed for an additional annual period provided a renewal application meeting all of the requirements for an initial license application is submitted to the city clerk between January 1 and March 1 of the year for which renewal is sought and it is approved by the city clerk according to the same standards for an initial license. The fee for annual renewal shall be two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00).
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-74. - Further requirements.
The following additional requirements shall apply to all license and permit holders and establishments.
(1)
All massage therapists and massage apprentices shall be completely clothed at all times when administering a massage. For the purposes of this provision, "completely clothed" shall mean having on the upper portion of the body appropriate undergarments and either a blouse or shirt which shall cover all the upper body save the arms, head and neck and shall mean having on the lower body appropriate undergarments plus either pants or skirt, and said pants or skirt must cover from the waist down to a point at least two (2) inches above the knee. All clothes worn in compliance with this subsection shall be entirely non-transparent.
(2)
Massage of the human genitals or anus within massage establishments is expressly prohibited.
(3)
The storing, serving, sale or consumption of alcoholic beverages within massage establishments is strictly prohibited.
(4)
Every person to whom a license or permit shall have been granted shall display said license or permit in a conspicuous place on the premises that is clearly visible to the public.
(5)
The city, through the police department, shall have the right to inspect any licensed massage establishment and its records, at any time and without notice, during business hours to insure compliance with this article.
(6)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of eighteen (18) to administered a massage or frequent a massage establishment unless such person carries with him at the time of such patronage a written order directing the treatment to be given by a regularly licensed physician. It shall be the duty of the operator of such massage establishment to determine the age of each person patronizing such massage establishment and a violation of this section shall be grounds for revocation of the license of such massage establishment and/or massage therapist and/or massage apprentice administering massage to any underage patron.
(7)
Licensed massage therapists shall have the authority to train individuals in massage therapy under their supervision and in their establishment only in accord with the following provisions.
a.
Apprenticeship programs. Massages administered with massage establishments for good or valuable consideration may be given by person not holding a license as a massage therapist only in connection with a formal program of apprenticeship and training. Such massages shall be given only by such persons holding a valid massage apprentice permit pursuant to subsection (7)b. below and only under the direct supervision of a licensed massage therapist at all times. Said licensed massage therapist shall be in the same room where the massage is being administered by said permitted massage apprentice during the entire time of the giving of the massage.
b.
Massage apprentice permits. No person shall administer a massage in connection with the formal apprenticeship and training program authorized in subsection (7)a. above without first having obtained a massage apprentice permit. Any person desiring to obtain a massage apprentice permit shall make application to the city clerk in accordance with section 11-68 of this article, except that the requirements of subsection (b)(6) of said section shall not apply. All applications for a massage apprentice permit shall be accompanied by a fee of two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00). The minimum standard applicable to issuance of a massage apprentice permit shall be that specified in section 11-69 (a)(1) and (2). If a massage apprentice permit application is submitted in proper form, including all information and exhibits required by this section and accompanied by the correct fees, the application shall be accepted and acted upon in accord with section 11-70. Massage apprentice permits shall expire on December 31 of each year and may be renewed for two (2) additional periods of one (1) year each in accord with section 11-73. The renewal fee shall be two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00.) Massage apprentice permits shall be subject to revocation for cause pursuant to the provisions of section 11-75 and subsection 11-76(a) of this article. All other applicable provisions of this article shall apply to said permit holders.
(8)
It shall be the duty of all persons holding a license for a massage establishment under this article to file with the city clerk the names of all employees other than those holding massage therapist licenses, their home address, home telephone numbers and places of employment. Changes in the list with the names of new employees must be filed with the city clerk within ten (10) days of the date of any such change.
(9)
It shall be the duty of any person granted a license under this article to maintain correct and accurate records of the names and addresses of the persons receiving treatment at such establishment, and the name of the person at the establishment administering the treatment. The records shall be subject to inspection at any time by the police department or the DeKalb County Department of Health.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-75. - Revocation of license.
(a)
No license issued hereunder shall be revoked except for due cause as herein defined, and after a hearing before the city council. Notice of such hearing shall be given in writing and served by mail, registered postage fully prepaid addressed to the license holder at his or her place of business or residence at least ten (10) days prior to the date of the hearing thereon. The notice shall state the grounds of the complaint(s) against the holder of such license and shall designate the time and place where such hearing will be held.
(b)
"Due cause" for suspension or revocation of such license shall be as provided in section 11-76 of this article.
(c)
In all hearings pursuant to this section, the following procedures shall prevail, and the proceeding shall be as informal as compatible with due process and justice.
(1)
The mayor shall read or cause to be read the charges and specifications against the licensee and any response to those charges made by the licensee.
(2)
The city council shall hear the evidence upon the charges and specifications as filed against the licensee and shall not consider any additional evidence beyond the scope of the charges, and may exclude evidence which is purely cumulative.
(3)
The order of proof shall be as follows. The city attorney shall present evidence in support of the charges, the licensee shall present his evidence. Evidence of each party may be supported by pertinent documents. Each party shall be allowed to present pertinent rebuttal evidence.
(4)
The licensee and city may be represented by counsel, and may present, examine and cross-examine witnesses. Additionally, the city council may interrogate all parties and witnesses to obtain necessary information. Following the presentation of evidence, the city council may have a reasonable time, not to exceed thirty (30) days within which to issue its decision.
(5)
The findings of the city council will be final unless within thirty (30) days of the date of the decision, the applicant files a petition for writ of certiorari to the superior court of DeKalb County.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-76. - Grounds for revocation.
(a)
The license of a massage therapist may be revoked upon one (1) or more of the following grounds:
(1)
Failure of the holder to maintain initial requirements for obtaining the license, including by not limited to, loss of or failure to renew the license granted by DeKalb County pursuant to Article VIII of Chapter 15 of the DeKalb County Code of Ordinances;
(2)
The holder is guilty of fraud in the practice of massage, or fraud or deceit in his being licensed in the practice of massage;
(3)
The holder is engaged in the practice of massage under a false or assumed name, or is impersonating another therapist of a like or different name;
(4)
The holder is addicted to the habitual use of intoxicating liquors, narcotics or stimulants to such an extent as to incapacitate such person to the extent that he/she is unable to perform his or her professional duties;
(5)
The holder is guilty of fraudulent, false, misleading or deceptive advertising or practices any other licensed profession without legal authority therefor;
(6)
The holder has violated any of the provisions of this chapter;
(7)
The holder has violated any laws relating to sodomy, aggravated sodomy, solicitation of sodomy, public indecency, prostitution, pimping, pandering, pandering by compulsion, masturbation for hire, distribution of obscene materials, distribution of material depicting nudity, or sexual conduct as defined under Georgia law, or has been convicted, pled guilty, or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any felony, or to any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude;
(8)
The original application, or renewal thereof, contains materially false information; or the applicant has deliberately sought to falsify information contained therein; or
(9)
There has been the occurrence of a fact which would have barred the issuance of the original license.
(b)
The license of a massage establishment may be revoked upon one (1) or more of the following grounds:
(1)
Failure of the holder to maintain initial requirements for obtaining the license, including by not limited to, loss of or failure to renew the license granted by DeKalb County pursuant to Article VIII of Chapter 15 of the DeKalb County Code of Ordinances;
(2)
The holder allows or permits any person who is not a licensed massage therapist to administer a massage in said establishment except as specifically authorized by section 11-66(c);
(3)
The premises in which the massage establishment is located are in violation of any federal, state, city or county laws designated for the health, protection and safety of the occupants or general public;
(4)
The premises are in violation of the DeKalb County or city building or life safety codes;
(5)
The original application, or renewal thereof, contains materially false information; or the applicant has deliberately sought to falsify information contained therein;
(6)
The holder of the license, including any person with an ownership interest in the license, has violated any laws relating to sodomy, aggravated sodomy, solicitation of sodomy, public indecency, prostitution, pimping, pandering, pandering by compulsion, masturbation for hire, distribution of obscene materials, distribution of material depicting nudity, or sexual conduct as defined under Georgia law, or has been convicted, pled guilty, or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any felony, or to any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude;
(7)
Any of the license holder's employees or agents has been convicted, pled guilty, or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any felony, or to any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, has violated any laws relating to sodomy, aggravated sodomy, solicitation of sodomy, public indecency, prostitution, pimping, pandering, pandering by compulsion, masturbation for hire, distribution of obscene materials, distribution of material depicting nudity, or sexual conduct as defined under Georgia law, in connection with the operation of the massage establishment or on or about the premises of the massage establishment;
(8)
Failure of the holder to actively supervise and monitor the conduct of the employees, customers and others on the premises in order to protect the health, safety and welfare of the general public and the customers; or
(9)
The holder or his employees associated with the establishment have allowed to occur or have engaged in a violation of any part of this chapter.
(c)
Any massage therapist, massage apprentice or massage establishment who has his or her license or permit revoked shall be disqualified from reapplying for such a license or permit for a period of twelve (12) months immediately following the date of revocation.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-77. - Violations, penalties.
(a)
Any person, firm, corporation or other entity violating the provisions of this article shall be guilty of violation of a city ordinance and shall be punished as provided section 1-7 of this Code. Violation of this article shall also be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of the license issued hereunder.
(b)
The violation of the provisions of this article may be abated as a nuisance.
(c)
The violation of all provisions of this article by any person may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings for injunction in any court of competent jurisdiction. Such actions may be maintained notwithstanding that other adequate remedies at law exist. Such actions may be instituted in the name of the governing authority of the city.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
Sec. 11-78. - Unlawful operation declared nuisance.
(a)
Any massage establishment operated, conducted or maintained contrary to the provisions of this article shall be and the same is hereby declared to be unlawful and a public nuisance. The city may, in addition or in lieu of all other remedies, commence an action or actions, proceeding or proceedings for abatement, removal or injoining thereof, in the manner provided by law.
(b)
No massage establishment shall operate at any location nor on any premises which does not comply with all building code, fire safety code, and other ordinances and laws of DeKalb County, the City of Clarkston, and the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 10-6-98)
