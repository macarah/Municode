Editor's note— Ord. No. 357, § 1, adopted March 1, 2011, repealed former Ch. 17, §§ 17-1, 17-2, in its entirety and enacted new provisions as herein set out. Former Ch. 17, pertained to similar subject matter and derived from the Code of 1968, §§ 21-1, 21-2, and Ord. No. 306, § 1, adopted 5-2-06.
Cross reference— Planning and zoning board, § 15-1 et seq.

Sec. 17-1. - DeKalb County subdivision regulations—Adopted.
The Subdivision Regulations of DeKalb County, Georgia, as amended through the date of adoption of this Ord. No. 357 and as currently codified as Chapter 14, Article III of the DeKalb County Code, are hereby adopted as the subdivision regulations of the city. At least one (1) copy of such subdivision regulations shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk and such regulations are the same hereby made a part of this Code.
(Ord. No. 357, § 1, 3-1-11)
Sec. 17-2. - Same—Revised.
The following terms, where used by the DeKalb County subdivision regulations, shall have the indicated meanings for subdivisions within the city:
Board of commissioners shall refer to the city council;
Chapter 27 shall refer to appendix A of this Code (the city's zoning ordinance);
Chief executive officer refer to the city manager;
County shall refer to the City of Clarkston;
DeKalb County comprehensive plan or comprehensive plan shall refer to the city's comprehensive plan;
Development department, development director, and planning director shall refer to the designated city planner;
Planning commission shall refer to the planning and zoning board;
Recorder's court shall refer to the city municipal court.
(Ord. No. 357, § 1, 3-1-11)
Sec. 17-3. - Intent of chapter.
The intent of this chapter is to provide for the review of proposed subdivisions by the city in accordance with the substantive provisions of the DeKalb County subdivision regulations using a separate procedure to be conducted by the city. Wherever the subdivision regulations call for review by a DeKalb County department, person, or entity not redefined by section 17-2, the most analogous city department, person or entity shall perform said review. In the absence of an analogous city department, person, or entity, such review shall not be required.
(Ord. No. 357, § 1, 3-1-11)
