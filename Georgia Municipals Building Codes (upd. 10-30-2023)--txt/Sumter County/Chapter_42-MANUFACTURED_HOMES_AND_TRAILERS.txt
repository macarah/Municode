DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 42-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Limited mobile home park.
(a)
The owner of a parcel of land upon which is located the owner's residence, and upon which parcel of land the owner intends to place no more than two mobile homes, exclusive of the owner's residence, for the exclusive occupancy of a relative of the owner, or permit no more than two relatives to place no more than a total of two mobile homes on the parcel of land for the exclusive occupancy of the relative and upon which the owner receives no rent for the use of the mobile home(s) the owner may place on the parcel, or the space upon which a relative may place no more than two mobile homes. Payment of utilities, pro-rata payment of taxes, or payment of set-up fees is not considered rent. Payment of money, or in kind services, or swap of valuables is considered rent.
(b)
The owner of the limited mobile home park is exempt from complying with the provisions of this article, with the exception of compliance with state health laws and regulations as they may apply.
(c)
At such time as a relative moves from a mobile home on the owner's parcel of land, or begins to pay the owner rent, and there is more than one mobile home on the parcel, other than the principal residence of the owner, the owner will have to:
(1)
Meet all of the requirements of this article, and be a mobile home park;
(2)
Move the second home from the parcel of land; or
(3)
Move another relative into the mobile home.
Mobile home is a generic term for a factory-built dwelling unit built on a permanent chassis and made to be readily transportable on its own running gear in one or more sections totaling no less than 320 square feet which is subject to the Motor Vehicle Certificate of Title Act, O.C.G.A. § 40-3-1 et seq., and meeting the construction and safety standards of the national code administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development. The term does not include recreational vehicles. Every mobile home shall:
(1)
Be suitable for year-round occupancy.
(2)
Be placed on a permanent masonry foundation or other substantial foundation that will comply with the state minimum building code and O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160 et seq., which provides for the installation of manufactured homes and mobile homes, for the erection and support of mobile homes, and to be substantially fixed to its site also in compliance with the state minimum building code and O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160 et seq.
(3)
Be connected to a water system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and present to the county inspector a letter or certification of such approval.
(4)
Be connected to an approved waste disposal system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and present to the county inspector a letter or certification of such approval.
(5)
Have all electrical supply systems and connections that comply with the current state minimum electric code.
(6)
Be landscaped with trees, shrubs and other plant material substantially equal to or better than one-family dwellings within 500 feet.
(7)
Have areas between the bottom perimeter of the mobile home and the ground covered with material that is suitable and adequately functional to adequately protect the underside of the mobile home and to keep the mobile home from being unsightly. The owner of the mobile home is responsible for the skirting. The skirting shall be installed only after the inspection of the mobile home or manufactured home by the county inspector and a certificate of occupancy has been issued. The skirting shall then be installed within 30 days of the issuance of the certificate of occupancy.
(8)
Have all electrical, plumbing, and heating and air connections, improvements and repairs made by an individual licensed by the state.
Mobile home park means:
(1)
A parcel of land, or several contiguous parcels of land owned either by the same legal entity, or managed by the same legal entity, which has been planned and improved for the placement of two or more mobile homes for non-transient use and occupancy, or for rental occupancy. The term "park" includes the term "community."
(2)
If the owner of a tract or parcel of land who has placed a mobile home on that tract or parcel of land as his residence has placed one additional mobile home on the tract of land, as long as neither the second mobile home, nor the space the mobile home occupies, is rented, and the mobile home is occupied by a family member of the principal residence, the two mobile homes located on the tract of land shall not be classified as a mobile home park.
Mobile home space means a plot of ground within a mobile home park designated for the accommodation of not more than one mobile home of single-family occupancy.
Modular home means a factory-fabricated transportable building consisting of building units designed to be incorporated at a building site on a permanent foundation as a permanent structure with the appearance of a conventional on site constructed building and to be used for residential purposes. A modular home is not a mobile home. The term "modular home" shall include "manufactured home" and "prefabricated home." Every modular home shall:
(1)
Be suitable for year-round occupancy.
(2)
Be placed on a permanent masonry foundation or other substantial foundation that will comply with the state minimum building code for the erection and support of mobile homes and to be substantially fixed to its site also in compliance with the state minimum building code.
(3)
Be connected to a water system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and present to the county inspector a letter or certification of such approval.
(4)
Be connected to an approved waste disposal system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and present to the county inspector a letter or certification of such approval.
(5)
Have all electrical supply systems and connections that comply with the current state minimum electric code.
(6)
Be landscaped with trees, shrubs and other plant material substantially equal to or better than one-family dwellings within 500 feet.
(7)
Meet the general construction and safety standards of the latest edition of the state minimum building codes.
(8)
Have the areas between the bottom perimeter of the modular home and the ground covered with material that is suitable and adequately functional to adequately protect the underside of the modular home and to keep the modular home from being unsightly. The owner of the modular home is responsible for the skirting. The skirting shall be installed only after the inspection of the modular home by the county inspector and a certificate of occupancy has been issued. The skirting shall then be installed within 30 days of the issuance of the certificate of occupancy.
(9)
Have all electrical, plumbing, and heating and air connections, improvements and repairs made by an individual licensed by the state.
Rent or rental means any remuneration, whether in money, barter or kind, that is given or received as consideration for the occupancy of a mobile home, modular home or manufactured home, or the occupancy of a tract, space or parcel of land upon which a mobile home is situated.
Service building means a building housing facilities such as recreational, maintenance, laundry and office structures necessary to the successful development and management of a mobile home park.
Travel trailer means a motorized camper, converted bus, tent-trailer or other similar vehicular or portable structure used or designed for temporary portable housing or occupancy while on vacation, recreational or other trip, and provided with sleeping accommodations. The travel trailer shall not be used for permanent housing or a permanent residence.
Set up means the preparing of the mobile home or manufactured home for occupancy by installing the mobile home as defined in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160(2), attaching the mobile home to utilities as opposed to placing the mobile home in inventory or in temporary storage.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 1; Ord. of 10-14-97)

Sec. 42-32. - Conflict with other regulations.
(a)
Whenever the regulations of this article require a greater width or size of yards, courts or other open space, or impose other restrictive standards that are required in or under any other statute or ordinance, the regulations and requirements of this article shall govern.
(b)
Whenever the provisions of any other statute or ordinance require more restrictive standards than are required by this article, the provisions of such statute or ordinance shall govern.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 2)
Sec. 42-33. - Decal required.
(a)
All persons owning a mobile home located in the county on January 1 of each year shall obtain from the tax commissioner a decal before April 1, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-493. This requirement pertains to all mobile homes, including those exempted from taxation by homestead exemption, or other provisions of state law.
(b)
The mobile home decal shall be affixed to each mobile home in such manner as to cause the decal to be easily visible for inspection.
(c)
Owners of mobile homes locating their mobile homes in the county after January 1 of any year shall be required to report the location of their mobile home to the tax commissioner's office and to the building inspector within ten working days.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 21)
Sec. 42-34. - Mobile home installation.
(a)
Prior to the installation and setup of a mobile home in the county, the person or legal entity planning to install the mobile home, or the owner of the home, shall apply for and receive a permit to install the mobile home in the county. The requirements for the installation and setup of a mobile home in the county are as follows:
(1)
Proof that an application has been made for a well and septic tank, or proof the well and septic tank have been accepted by the county health department, or proof that the mobile home will be served by an EPD approved water and/or sewage system.
(2)
Proof that an installer licensed by the state as per O.C.G.A. § 8-2-164 will install the mobile home.
(3)
Proof that the mobile home has been registered with the county tax commissioner.
(4)
Evidence of ownership of the lot where the mobile home will be installed, and if not owned by the owner of the mobile home, evidence of a rental agreement or some other agreement signed by the property owner.
A dealer shall not deliver and an installer shall not install a mobile home until the permit for installation is obtained and the permit is in the dealer's or installer's possession. The permit shall be displayed on the home during transport and installation or set-up.
(b)
The mobile home shall be installed in compliance with the installation of manufactured homes and mobile home requirements as stated in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160 et seq., and as amended.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 23; Amd. of 6-9-98)
Sec. 42-35. - Certificate of occupancy.
No newly installed mobile home or manufactured home in the county will be occupied until a certificate of occupancy has been issued by the county inspector. The requirements of the issuance of a certificate of occupancy are as follows:
(1)
Compliance with county minimum housing standards.
(2)
Compliance with the rules and regulations of the Uniform Standards Code for Manufacturer's Home Act (O.C.G.A. § 8-2-10 et seq. and as amended).
(3)
Compliance with county mobile home installation requirements.
(4)
Inspection by the county building inspector for compliance with the applicable ordinances.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 24)

Sec. 42-56. - Application for permission to develop or maintain a mobile home park.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to develop or maintain a mobile home park in the county unless it is duly permitted and pursuant to the provisions of this section. A conditional use permit is required for the establishment of a mobile home park.
(b)
The mobile home park shall be approved by the county health department before a permit is issued.
(c)
The application for the development of a mobile home park shall have attached to the application proof that all county taxes due from the applicant have been paid in full, and no permit may be issued until all taxes due from the applicant are paid in full.
(d)
The county inspector may recommend approval of a planned mobile home park when all conditions precedent to this article have been met.
(e)
An application and all accompanying plans and supporting data shall be filed in triplicate with the county inspector at least two weeks prior to a regular meeting of the board of commissioners. The application shall be in writing, and it shall include the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The location and description of the boundaries of the property intended for planned mobile home park development.
(3)
A complete mobile home park plan showing all existing conditions and proposed site development as required in this section including preliminary plans of all buildings, improvements and facilities constructed or to be constructed within the mobile home park (on a topographic map). Such plan shall be drawn at a scale of one inch equals not more than 100 feet.
(4)
Information as provided for in section 58-85.
(5)
Verification by the applicant, and the board of commissioners are authorized to revoke the mobile home permit if any false statements are made on the application.
(6)
Proof that an application has been made to the health department for approval of the mobile home park, or proof that approval of the health department has been obtained.
(7)
Any other information reasonably requested by the county inspector to determine the proposed park's compliance with legal requirements.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 3; Ord. of 10-14-97)
Sec. 42-57. - Dimensional and site development requirements (mobile home park plan).
(a)
Generally. The mobile home park plan shall show or propose all requirements listed in this article; and, in addition, all such requirements shall be complied with before a certificate of occupancy may be issued. The mobile home park plan shall, at a minimum, conform to the provisions of subsections (b)—(d) of this section.
(b)
Frontage and area. A mobile home park shall front for at least 100 feet on a major public thoroughfare and shall have a minimum area of at least five buildable acres.
(c)
Entrances and exits. The entrances and exits to the mobile home park shall be from a major public thoroughfare. All entrance and exit streets shall have a right-of-way width of at least 60 feet and a surface width of at least 28 feet and a distance of at least 100 feet from the public thoroughfare extending into the park, and after the first 100 feet shall be deemed an interior street.
(d)
Park boundaries. A mobile home park shall be bounded with a ten-foot buffer strip along all the exterior property lines of the park not bounding a major public thoroughfare. The buffer strip shall be planted with evergreen trees or shrubs that grow at least eight feet in height and provide a visual screen.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 4)
Sec. 42-58. - Minimum number of mobile home spaces.
Any legal entity that owns and rents two or more spaces shall be required to obtain a permit and will be subject to the terms of this article.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 5)
Sec. 42-59. - Required mobile home space area.
Each mobile home space shall contain at least 4,500 square feet of area, and will also be of sufficient size to comply with the county and state health laws. There shall not be more than eight spaces per gross acre.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 6)
Sec. 42-60. - Yard requirements.
(a)
Each space in a mobile home park shall be at least 52 feet wide and clearly defined.
(b)
There shall be a 20-foot side yard, the side yard being the space parallel to the longest dimension of the home.
(c)
There shall be a 40-foot minimum distance between homes, side-to-side, and a 40-foot minimum distance between homes, end-to-end.
(d)
No mobile home shall be located closer than 25 feet to any street and within the park or within 40 feet of any exterior public street.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 7)
Sec. 42-61. - Drainage.
The park shall be located on a well-drained and properly graded site. The site drainage improvements shall meet the requirements of section 34-31 et seq.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 8)
Sec. 42-62. - Interior streets and drives.
All mobile home parks shall contain a street system designed to provide convenient circulation within the park, and shall have unobstructed access to a public street or highway in accordance with requirements of the county. The following requirements shall apply to the development of a park's street system:
(1)
All interior streets shall have a right-of-way width of at least 40 feet unless a greater width for drainage purposes is required. The minimum surface width shall be 22 feet for an interior street.
(2)
All park streets shall be provided with a smooth, graveled all-weather surface having a minimum width of 22 feet, which shall be durable and well drained under normal weather conditions.
(3)
All park streets shall be maintained in a state of good repair at all times by the park owner.
(4)
Street base and surface construction materials shall be as required by the county's street and road standards. Written approval of the street system by the county inspector shall be required before the first mobile home building permit will be issued.
(5)
Driveways on individual spaces shall be at least eight feet in width.
(6)
All structures located on the space shall observe the required setback and distance requirements.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 9)
Sec. 42-63. - Off-drive parking.
(a)
No automobile parking shall be permitted on that portion of the interior street system of a mobile home park designated as the entrance or exit portion and being that portion of the street that is 60 feet in width and 100 feet deep as measured from the major public thoroughfare.
(b)
At least two automobile spaces shall be provided for each mobile home space. The parking spaces shall be provided on the individual spaces or on street parking lanes or on an off-street parking lot that is located within 200 feet of the mobile home spaces the parking lot serves.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 10)
Sec. 42-64. - Signs.
The following nonflashing and nonanimated signs are required under the conditions specified:
(1)
Park identification sign. One park identification sign shall be required not to exceed 24 square feet in the area for the purpose of denoting the name of the mobile home park.
(2)
Mobile home lot identification sign. One mobile home lot identification sign not exceeding one square foot in area shall be required for each mobile home lot. Such sign shall indicate the name, address and lot number of the occupant of the mobile home.
(3)
Street signs. All streets in the mobile home park shall be named in conformity with section 50-31 et seq., and street signs shall be erected at the park owner's expense. The sign identifying the lot shall be visible from the street.
(4)
Visibility. No sign shall be located so as to impede vehicular visibility at any intersection of street lines with one another or the edge of driveways with street lines.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 11)
Sec. 42-65. - Improvements to mobile homes and mobile home spaces.
(a)
Any addition to any mobile home in a mobile home park or modular home shall be free-standing, self-supporting, and built in conformity with the state minimum housing code.
(b)
Each mobile home space shall be given a lot number and accordingly marked to be visible from the street providing access thereto.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 12)
Sec. 42-66. - Special conditions and safeguards.
In recommending approval of any planned mobile home park, the board of commissioners may attach special conditions and safeguards to protect both the occupants of the park and the occupants of surrounding property, including such matters as protection against noise, lights, dust and fire. Where required to service these ends, walls, plantings, surfacing, or other natural or artificial means for protection may reasonably be required as a part of such special conditions on which the recommendation for approval of a mobile home park is based. Failure to meet such conditions shall be grounds for refusal of issuance of a certificate of occupancy.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 13)
Sec. 42-67. - Minimum size and sanitation facilities of mobile homes.
No mobile home that has less than 250 square feet of floor space and does not contain a built-in bathroom with water closet, lavatory and shower or tub in working condition shall be placed in a planned mobile home park.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 14)
Sec. 42-68. - Lighting.
All interior drives and walkways within a mobile home park shall be lighted at night with electric lamps spaced at intervals of no more than 150 feet. Such lighting shall meet the minimum requirements of the state mobile home regulations.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 15)
Sec. 42-69. - Refuse collection facilities.
Each mobile home park shall be provided with a sanitary method of solid waste collection and disposal meeting the requirements of the county and state.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 16)
Sec. 42-70. - Service buildings.
Service buildings such as shelters, restrooms, management offices and storage facilities are permitted in each mobile home park and are subject to the following requirements:
(1)
Such buildings and their parking areas shall not occupy more than ten percent of the gross land area of the park.
(2)
All service buildings shall be required to conform to the county building, plumbing and electrical codes adopted by the county.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 17)
Sec. 42-71. - Existing mobile home parks.
Mobile home parks in existence in 1992 shall not be subject to this division so long as the title to the mobile home park remains in the titled owner. If the title to the mobile home park is transferred, except as security for loans, the terms of this division and all revisions will apply to the transferee and to the mobile home park, limited to the following extent:
(1)
The transferor and transferee are required to notify the county building inspector of the transfer of title.
(2)
The requirements mentioned in this division may be submitted prior to the transfer of title, or may be submitted after the transfer of title. If submitted after the transfer of title, they shall be submitted within 30 days of the transfer of title.
(3)
An inventory of the mobile home park will be made to determine the areas of the park that do not conform to this division. The inventory shall be submitted to the county building inspector.
(4)
Also submitted to the county building inspector will be an itemized estimated cost that will be incurred to bring the mobile home park into compliance with the then current mobile home park, mobile home and manufactured home regulations.
(5)
The county building inspector will review the inventory and itemized cost and identify those changes that are necessary for health or safety reasons.
(6)
The county building inspector will review the inventory and list the changes required for health or safety reasons and the practical and inexpensive changes that can be made to bring the mobile home park into compliance.
(7)
If the transferee accepts the changes recommended by the county building inspector, the changes will be incorporated into a plan for renovation. If the transferee does not accept the changes recommended by the county building inspector, the transferee may appeal the recommended changes to the board of commissioners within five days of the county building inspector's recommendations.
(8)
Once the list of the changes is final, the transferee will proceed to create and prepare a plan for renovations and will make the renovations to the mobile home park. The time for completion of the renovations will be set by the county building inspector and will depend on the extent of the work and the availability of material and labor. Extensions may be granted upon written request detailing the reasons for the extension.
(9)
The owner of every mobile home park in the county shall comply with the requirements of section 42-74.
(10)
The owner of each mobile home park, and the owner of any mobile home situated in the county, shall comply with the skirting requirements of this division within six months of October 10, 1995.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 18)
Sec. 42-72. - Development compliance.
(a)
At least 100 percent of each phase, enlargement or expansion of all planned spaces, as well as all streets, utilities and other required improvements necessary to serve them pursuant to this division shall be completely constructed according to the mobile home park plan approved by the board of commissioners before the first mobile home building permit will be issued.
(b)
Failure of the developer or applicant to construct this minimum phase within two years after approval by the board of commissioners shall cause such approval to expire.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 19)
Sec. 42-73. - Inspection.
(a)
The building inspector is hereby authorized and directed to make inspections to determine the condition of mobile home parks located within the county, in order that such inspector may perform the duty of safeguarding the health and safety of occupants of mobile home parks and of the general public.
(b)
The building inspector shall have the power to enter at reasonable times upon any private or public property for the purpose of inspecting and investigating conditions relating to the enforcement of this division.
(c)
The building inspector shall have the power to inspect the register containing a record of all mobile homes and occupants using the mobile home park.
(d)
It shall be the duty of the owners or occupants of mobile home parks, or of the person in charge thereof, to give the building inspector free access to such premises at reasonable times for the purpose of inspection.
(e)
It shall be the duty of every occupant of a mobile home park to give the owner thereof, or the owner's agent or employee, access to any part of such mobile home park or its premises at reasonable times for the purposes of making such repairs or alterations as are necessary to effect compliance with this division or with any lawful order issued pursuant to the provisions of this division.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 20)
Sec. 42-74. - Title reports by mobile home park operators.
(a)
Every owner and operator of a mobile home park is required to give to the county inspector an inventory of all mobile homes located in the park as of January 1 of each year. The inventory shall be delivered to the county inspector not later than January 20 of each year.
(b)
Every owner, manager and operator of a mobile home park is required to report to the county inspector any mobile home moved into the mobile home park after January 1 of each year.
(c)
Every owner, manager and operator of a mobile home park shall furnish to the county inspector an updated lot map of each mobile home park; and changes to the map shall be reported by January 20 of each year.
(d)
Every owner, manager and operator of a mobile home park is required to notify the county inspector of any change in ownership or any change in the name of any mobile home park within 30 days of such change.
(e)
The county inspector will notify the tax commissioner of the information that has been furnished to him by the owner and operator of the mobile home park as may be needed by the tax commissioner for the updating of the tax commissioner's records.
(f)
This section applies to all mobile home park owners, including those in existence prior to the adoption of the original mobile home park regulations.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 22)
Sec. 42-75. - Penalties and incentives.
(a)
Failure to obtain a permit. If any person commences a mobile home park without first obtaining a permit, the person shall be subject to revocation of any permit or other authorization received from the county and associated with the establishment of the mobile home park.
(b)
Stop work orders. Upon notice from the county inspector, work on the mobile home contrary to the provision of this division, or in a dangerous or unsafe manner, shall be immediately stopped. Such notice shall be in writing and shall be given to the owner of the property, his authorized agent, or the person in charge of constructing the mobile home park on the property, and shall state the conditions under which work may be resumed. Where an emergency exists, no written notice shall be required.
(c)
Civil penalties. Any person violating any provision of this division shall be liable for a civil penalty of not less than $100.00 for the first violation and $500.00 for each violation thereafter. Those who violate this division three or more times may be barred from receiving permits to install mobile homes in the county.
(Ord. of 3-12-96, § 30)
Secs. 42-76—42-100. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - MANUFACTURED HOMES

Sec. 42-101. - Scope.
These provisions shall be applicable only to manufactured homes and shall apply to the following:
(1)
Construction, alteration, and repair of any foundation system for the installation of a manufactured home.
(2)
Installation, addition, alteration, repair, or maintenance of any new or used manufactured home, or any service equipment necessary to connect the manufactured home to water, fuel, electrical power, and sewage systems.
(3)
Alterations, additions, or repairs to existing manufactured homes.
(4)
Relocation into the unincorporated areas of Sumter County of any manufactured home that is not presently located within the unincorporated areas of Sumter County.
(5)
Relocation within the unincorporated areas of Sumter County of any manufactured home presently located within the unincorporated areas of Sumter County.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-102. - Additions, alterations, and repairs.
Additions, alterations, or repairs to manufactured homes and their service equipment shall comply with all the requirements of the provisions for new construction, except as specifically provided in this section. Non-structural alterations and repairs to an existing manufactured home may be made with materials equivalent to those of which the manufactured home is constructed if it causes no adverse effect on any structural or fire protection component, and subject to the code enforcement official's approval. Additions shall not be structurally supported by, or attached to, the manufactured home unless engineering calculations are submitted to substantiate any proposed structural connection. Additions to a manufactured home shall conform to one of the following:
(1)
Be certified under National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974 (42 U.S.C. Section 401, et seq.)
(2)
Be designed and constructed in conformance with the construction codes adopted by Sumter County.
(3)
Minor alterations, and repairs to existing manufactured homes and their service equipment may be made in accordance with the Codes in effect at the time the original installation was made subject to approval of the code enforcement official, and provided such alteration or repair will not cause the manufactured home or its service equipment to become unsafe or unsanitary.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-103. - Existing homes.
Manufactured homes lawfully in existence at the time of the adoption of this Article may have their use, occupancy, maintenance, and repair continued if the use, maintenance or repair does not create a hazard to life, health or property, as set forth in the Code of Sumter County.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-104. - Property maintenance.
All manufactured homes and their service equipment shall be maintained in a safe and sanitary condition. The owner or his/her designated agent shall be responsible for the maintenance of the manufactured home, accessory structures, and the associated service equipment.
Sec. 42-105. - Location.
Manufactured homes which are to be moved into Sumter County or relocated within Sumter County must comply with these provisions:
(1)
Except as provided for in subparagraphs (b), (c) and (d) of section (2) below, no used or previously owned manufactured home being moved into the unincorporated areas of Sumter County or being relocated within the unincorporated areas of Sumter County shall be allowed to locate for permanent or temporary occupancy if said manufactured home is more than ten years old from the date of manufacture.
(2)
A manufactured home which is currently situated in Sumter County and is more than ten years old from the date of manufacture may be relocated within the unincorporated areas of Sumter County if all of the following conditions are present:
a.
The relocation site is a "mobile home park" or "subdivision" as approved by the Sumter County Board of Commissioners, and as further defined in the applicable Sumter County Ordinances, with no current citations or violations of either the manufactured home owner or the manufactured home park/subdivision owner including, but not limited to:
1.

The issuance of any permit required by this section shall be conditioned upon the owner of said manufactured home submitting proof to the Tax Commissioner of Sumter County that all state and county taxes theretofore accruing and payable with respect to said home have in fact been paid.

Manufactured home location permits. No mover, hauler, person, firm, corporation, or any other entity shall:

Sec. 42-107. - Inspections.
All work for which a permit is required shall be subject to inspection by the code enforcement officer. It is the responsibility of the permit applicant to cause the work to be accessible and exposed for inspection purposes.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-108. - Final inspection.
When the manufactured home installation is complete, including landings, skirting, service equipment, soil erosion control, and finish grading, and the home is ready for occupancy, a final inspection shall be made. Utility service shall not be provided to any building service equipment that is regulated by this Code until a final inspection is performed and approved by the code enforcement officer.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-109. - Footings and foundations.
Footing and foundation design and installation shall conform to the following:
(1)
Class "A" manufactured homes must be placed on a permanent foundation, either slab or pier, which meets the requirements of the state building code.
(2)
Class "B" manufactured homes are not required to have poured concrete or permanent foundations. Piers must be spaced per manufacturer's instructions.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-110. - Skirting.
Each manufactured home requires skirting conforming to the following:
(1)
Class "A" manufactured homes must have a masonry curtain wall, unpierced except for the required ventilation and access, installed so that it encloses the area under the manufactured home to ground level.
(2)
Class "B" manufactured homes shall be completely skirted with either masonry material, or skirting material manufactured for this purpose, and the skirting shall reach to the ground level.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-111. - Landings and steps.
For every manufactured home located in the unincorporated areas of Sumter County, a landing is required for each exterior door. The minimum size of the landing is dependent upon the width of the door. The structure must include steps that lead to the ground level. Both the steps and landings must be constructed to meet the adopted building code of Sumter County.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)
Sec. 42-112. - Anchorage.
All manufactured homes must be tied down per manufacturer recommendations, and the requirements listed in Appendix H of the State Building Code.
(Ord. of 11-16-04(2); Ord. of 6-20-06)

ARTICLE IV. - MINIMUM HEALTH AND SAFETY STANDARDS FOR PRE-OWNED MANUFACTURED HOMES

Sec. 42-151. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section.
Applicant means any person seeking to install a pre-owned manufactured home in the unincorporated area of Sumter County.
Building inspector means the person appointed, employed, or otherwise designated as the director of planning, permits and inspections; the county building official or any of his or her assistants.
Certificate of occupancy means a document issued by the building inspector certifying that a pre-owned manufactured home is in compliance with applicable requirements set forth by this article, and indicating it to be in a condition suitable for residential occupancy.
Guarantee of condition bond means a surety bond to guarantee that the affidavit and photographs required by paragraphs (1) and (2) of subsection (a) of section 42-153 of this article reasonably portray or represents the existing condition of the pre-owned manufactured home proposed for relocation. In lieu of the bond, a cash deposit may be deposited with the county.
Install means to construct a foundation system and to place or erect a manufactured home on such foundation system. Such term includes, without limitation, supporting, blocking, leveling, securing, or anchoring such manufactured home and connecting multiple or expandable sections of such manufactured home.
Jurisdiction means the unincorporated areas of Sumter County, Georgia.
Manufactured home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air-conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein; except that such term shall include any structure which meets all the requirements of this paragraph except the size requirements and with respect to which the manufacturer voluntarily files a certification required by the secretary of housing and urban development and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401, et seq.
Pre-owned manufactured home means any manufactured home that has been previously used as a residential dwelling and has been titled.
(Ord. of 8-17-10(1), § 1)
Sec. 42-152. - Conditions.
All pre-owned manufactured homes located in the jurisdiction shall bear a label certifying it was constructed in compliance with the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401, et seq. (the HUD Code) and shall be installed in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160, et seq.
(Ord. of 8-17-10(1), § 2)
Sec. 42-153. - Permitting, inspection, certificate of occupancy and fees.
A permit shall be required to locate a pre-owned manufactured home in the jurisdiction. All permits shall be issued within five business days of receipt of all items listed in subsections (a)(1)—(4) of this section.
(a)
Permit. To obtain a permit, applicants shall provide to the building inspector:
(1)
An affidavit signed by the applicant that the pre-owned manufactured home meets health and safety standards required by this Act;
(2)
Photographs of the interior and exterior of the pre-owned manufactured home providing evidence that home meets the minimum health and safety standards of section 42-154 of this article;
(3)
A $1,000.00 refundable guarantee of condition bond or $1,000.00 refundable cash deposit; and
(4)
The permit and inspection fee required by subsection (d) of this section.
(b)
Inspection. Upon receipt of a permit, applicants may relocate the manufactured home on a residential site for the purposes of inspection. Applicant shall arrange for an inspection to be held once the installation of the manufactured home is complete.
(c)
Certificate of occupancy. A certificate of occupancy shall be issued to the applicant at such time that the building inspector certifies that the requirements of this article have been met.
(d)
Fee. A permit and inspection fee of $250.00 shall be charged to the applicant to cover the cost to the County to process the permit application and inspect the pre-owned manufactured home. Such fee shall cover the initial inspection and one followup inspection. The applicant shall be charged $100.00 for each additional followup inspection that may be necessary.
(e)
Alternative inspection. At the request of the applicant, the building inspector may, at his or her discretion, inspect a pre-owned manufactured home prior to its being relocated if the home is then located at another site within the county.
(Ord. of 8-17-10(1), § 3)
Sec. 42-154. - Minimum health and safety standards.
All preowned manufactured homes shall comply with the following before being issued a certificate of occupancy by the building inspector:
(a)
HUD Code. Every pre-owned manufactured home located in the jurisdiction shall be in compliance with the Federal Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act, 42 U.S.C. 5401-5445 (the HUD Code) and shall not have been altered in such a way that the home no longer meets the HUD Code.
(b)
Interior condition. Every floor, interior wall, and ceiling of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in sound condition. Doors and windows shall be operable, watertight and in good working condition. The floor system shall be in sound condition and free of warping, holes, water damage, or deterioration.
(c)
Exterior condition. The exterior of all pre-owned manufactured homes shall be free of loose or rotting boards or timbers and any other conditions that might admit rain or moisture to the interior portions of the walls or to occupied spaces. The exterior siding shall be free of rot and rust. Roofs shall be structurally sound and have no obvious defects that might admit rain or cause moisture to collect on the interior portion of the home.
(d)
Sanitary facilities. Every plumbing fixture, water, and waste pipe of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in a sanitary working condition when properly connected, and shall be free from leaks and obstructions. Each home shall contain a kitchen sink. Each bathroom shall contain a lavatory and water closet. At least one bathroom shall contain a tub and/or shower facilities. Each of these fixtures shall be checked upon being connected to ensure they are in good working condition.
(e)
Heating systems. Heating shall be safe and in working condition. Un-vented heaters shall be prohibited.
(f)
Electrical systems. Electrical systems (switches, receptacles, fixtures, etc.) shall be properly installed and wired and shall be in working condition. Distribution panels shall be in compliance with the approved listing, complete with required breakers, with all unused openings covered with solid covers approved and listed for that purpose. The home shall be subject to an electrical continuity test to assure that all metallic parts are properly bonded.
(g)
Hot water supply. Each home shall contain a water heater in safe and working condition.
(h)
Egress windows. Each bedroom of a manufactured home shall have at least one operable window of sufficient size to allow egress if necessary.
(i)
Ventilation. The kitchen in the home shall have at least one operating window or other ventilation device.
(j)
Smoke detectors. Each pre-owned manufactured home shall contain one operable battery-powered smoke detector in each bedroom and in the kitchen, which must be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations.
(Ord. of 8-17-10(1), § 4)
Sec. 42-155. - Enforcement.
(a)
Permanent connection to utilities shall not be approved until the building inspector has issued a certificate of occupancy.
(b)
Owners of pre-owned manufactured homes that are not in compliance upon a third inspection shall have their permit revoked and shall be required to remove the home from the jurisdiction at their own expense.
(c)
The guarantee of condition bond or cash deposit will be forfeited after 90 days from the date of inspection, unless all conditions and standards are met prior to the end of the 90 days or an extension has been issued in writing by the building inspector.
(Ord. of 8-17-10(1), § 5)
Sec. 42-156. - Penalties
Failure to remove a pre-owned manufactured home from the jurisdiction upon failure to receive a certificate of occupancy shall be punishable by a fine of $500.00. Each day any violation under this article continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(Ord. of 8-17-10(1), § 6)
