ARTICLE II. - BUSINESSES REGULATED

Sec. 16-43. - Life insurance.
(a)
Life insurers license fees. There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each life insurer doing business within the city, in an amount as established by the city council, from time to time. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 16-44, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in an amount as established by the city council, from time to time. For the purposes of this section, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1).
(b)
License fee for life insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations. For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales, offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of a life insurer for life insurance said insurer shall pay an additional license fee per location for each year that they are in business.
(c)
Life insurance agency license fees; independent life insurance agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed. There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which a life insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 16-44, in an amount as established by the city council, from time to time, for each such location within the city.
(Code 1998, § 32-202)
State Law reference— General assembly authorized to provide for taxation of insurance companies based on gross direct premiums received from insurance policies within the unincorporated areas of counties, Ga. Const. art. IX, § IV, ¶ I(c); powers of municipal corporations to impose license fees on life insurance companies, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8; local tax on life insurance companies, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1.
Sec. 16-44. - Construction contractors.
(a)
Plumbers. No master, contracting, or journeyman plumber, as such terms are defined in chapter 14, title 43 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, shall be permitted to engage in any plumbing business in this municipality, unless such person shall hold a valid license issued to him by the state division of master plumbers and journeyman plumbers. Any person desiring a license to engage in such profession shall be required to pass an examination pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-14-8. Municipalities are not prohibited from fixing, charging, assessing, or collecting any license fee, registration fee, tax, or gross receipt tax on any such profession.
(b)
Electrical contractors. No electrical contractor, as such term is defined in chapter 14, title 43 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, shall be permitted to engage in any electrical contracting business in this municipality unless such person shall hold a valid license issued to him by the state division of electrical contractors and passed an examination pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-14-8.
(c)
Conditioned air contractors. No conditioned air contractor, as such term is defined in chapter 14, title 43 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, shall be permitted to engage in any conditioned air contracting business in this municipality unless such person shall hold a valid license issued to him by the state division of conditioned air contractors and passed an examination pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-14-8.
(d)
Low-voltage contractor. No low-voltage contractor, as such term is defined in chapter 14, title 43 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, shall be permitted to engage in any low-voltage contracting in this municipality unless such person shall hold a valid license issued to him by the state division of low-voltage contractors and passed an examination pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-14-8.
(Code 1998, § 32-203)
State Law reference— Electrical contractors, plumbers, conditioned air contractors, low-voltage contractors, and utility contractors, O.C.G.A. § 43-14-1 et seq.
Sec. 16-45. - Peddling, soliciting, canvassing, and temporary merchants.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable organization means any person, group or organization which complies with Georgia Charitable Solicitations Act of 19B8, O.C.G.A. § 43-17-1 et seq.
Educational institution means an entity organized and operated exclusively for educational purposes and which either maintains a regular faculty and curriculum and has a regularly enrolled body of students in attendance at the place where its educational activities are regularly carried on, or is accredited by a nationally recognized, independent higher education accreditation body.
Peddler shall mean any person who engages in the business of selling any goods, wares, merchandise or services or who solicits orders for such goods, wares, merchandise or services by going from house to house or place to place and who does not have a permanent place of business.
Religious organization means an entity which conducts regular worship services or is qualified as a religious organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as now or hereafter amended, that is not required to file IRS Form 990, Return of Organization Exempt From Income Tax, under any circumstances.
Roadside stand whenever used in this section, means any trailer, tent, motor vehicle, or other facility or structure, not erected on a foundation that is erected and operated for the sale or solicitation of any merchandise. No roadside stand may be used as a food establishment as defined in O.C.G.A. § 26-2-370.
Soliciting as it applies to this section, shall mean any action which compels or attempts to compel another to purchase, donate or otherwise do business with the person doing the soliciting.
Temporary merchant is a person who sells produce, goods and merchandise for profit, from a stationary location or from a pushcart, bicycle cart, tent, motorized cart or any other motor vehicle or trailer, that is temporary in nature (six months or less in one location), and is not operated by or as an accessory use to the primary use existing on the premises.
Vendor means a roadside vendor or any person who sells, gives away through barter, offers to sell, or solicits to sell any fruit, vegetables, nuts, berries, citrus, cut flowers or any other similar item.
(b)
Registration required. Any person 18 years of age or older who is peddling, soliciting or canvassing within the city, shall be required to register and obtain an identification card as provided by this article.
(c)
Registration; fee; application for identification card. Any person 18 years of age or older desiring to sell, peddle, solicit or canvass within the city shall make application with the city through its designated agent and pay a nonrefundable registration fee of $25.00. The application shall provide the following information:
(1)
Name and description of applicant;
(2)
Address, both legal and local;
(3)
A brief description of the nature of the business and the goods to be sold;
(4)
The name and address of the applicant's employer, if other than the applicant, together with credentials establishing the exact relationship between the applicant and the employer (i.e. date of hire, position applicant holds);
(5)
If a vehicle is used, a description of the vehicle and tag number, as well as a copy of the driver's license of the designated driver;
(6)
Information on any convictions or pending cases of any crime, misdemeanor or violation of any municipal ordinance, the nature of the offense and the punishment or penalty assessed therefore;
(7)
The date(s) and time(s) which the applicant proposes to sell, peddle, solicit or canvass within the city; and
(8)
Anticipated location of selling, peddling or solicitation.
(d)
Investigation of applicants; consideration of approval. Eligibility of an applicant is contingent upon an investigation which reveals the applicant's history is free from convictions, charges or accusations of a nature which, in the judgment of a reasonable person, could jeopardize the safety and property of the citizens and of the city in general. Such convictions, charges and accusations include, but are not limited to:
(1)
Felonies;
(2)
Misdemeanors of a high and aggravated nature;
(3)
Misdemeanors which would constitute moral turpitude.
After proper inquiry and investigation, the police department shall determine whether the applicant is eligible to be permitted. Each eligible applicant shall be issued a registration certificate. If, after proper inquiry and investigation, the police department shall determine that the applicant is not eligible, the police department shall deny the application and file the application, investigation documentation and written recommendation to deny the applicant at the police department where it will be maintained for two years; and, notify the applicant by personal service or by first-class mail to the address shown on the application. It shall be conclusively presumed that notice by first-class mail was received by the applicant and the failure of the applicant to receive such notice shall not invalidate the action taken.
(e)
Exhibition of registration/identification card. Each registrant under this article shall at all times while in the city visibly display the registration and shall exhibit the same when requested to do so by any law enforcement officer or by any municipal authority and by any person being solicited. Possession of this registration card shall not in any way represent an endorsement or approval of any product or project by the city. No registration card shall be used at any time by any person other than the one to whom it is issued.
(f)
Unlawful acts. It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, peddle, canvass or solicit after sundown. It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, peddle, canvass or solicit without having registered with the police department in accordance with this article or to sell, peddle, solicit or canvass without having on his person and in his possession the registration issued in accordance with this article. It shall be unlawful for any person to alter or deface any permit or registration issued under this article. Violation of this section shall result in citation and punishment as set forth in the general provisions chapter of this Code. It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, peddle, canvass or solicit who is under the age of 18 years of age. It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or peddle food items prepared on site or which are otherwise prepared outside of a state-licensed facility, except for the following conditions:
City-sponsored or approved festivals (for city-sponsored or approved festivals or special events the vendor must obtain permission from the city or its designee to set up a booth and must pay the vendor fee for the event);
Charitable fundraisers on private property provided the property owner has given written consent.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, peddle, canvass or solicit on public property or within the right-of-way in the city limits including, but not limited to:
State Route 27.
State Route 34.
State Route 100.
It shall be unlawful for any vendor or itinerant vendor to operate within or upon, and sell, solicit, or barter, transact or solicit business without complying with the following requirements;
(1)
Possession of a valid permit, issued by the police department. This requirement shall not apply to those persons excepted by subsection (g) below;
(2)
Must be located on privately owned real property within the commercial, industrial, or highway frontage zoning districts, and with the written permission of the owner of the property;
(3)
Must comply with all other ordinances of the city specifically including sign ordinances and parking requirements;
(4)
Must be located at least thirty-five feet back from the paved edge of any roadway, and, if operated on a corner lot, shall not be operated within a sight distance triangle area formed by the street, property lines, and a line connecting them at points at least 35 feet from the intersection of the street lines maintaining at least 35 feet from any intersection. The measurement of 35 feet shall start at the edge of the roadway, inward;
(5)
There shall be a maximum of one vendor per business location;
(6)
There shall be a maximum of five vendors permits issued per day within the city.
(g)
Exceptions. The fee set forth in subsection (c) above shall not apply to:
(1)
Solicitations, sales or distributions made by educational, civic, religious or charitable organizations which have their principal place of activity in the city or which are otherwise approved by the mayor and council;
(2)
Persons selling, distributing or soliciting orders for farm and garden produce as raised or prepared by the vendor or members of his family, and homegrown so far as the sale of such commodities is authorized by law;
(3)
Persons selling, distributing or soliciting orders for local or area newspapers.
All persons and organizations as described in subsections (1), (2), and (3) above shall comply with all other requirements of this section.
(h)
Standards and guidelines for granting or denying approval. In addition to all other requirements stated in this chapter, the city shall consider the following standards and guidelines in reaching its decision on whether to grant or deny an application to peddle, solicit, canvass, or sell as a temporary merchant:
(1)
The report of the police investigation as required by this chapter, and any other reports that may be required;
(2)
The number of approvals already granted for similar entities within the city limits and in the trading area of the location for which an approval is sought;
(3)
The criminal record, general good character and reputation of the applicant and/or the affiliated organization;
(4)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom;
(5)
The nature of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location, that is, whether the neighborhood is predominantly residential, industrial or business;
(6)
Whether the applicant has satisfied all requirements pertaining to the application process;
(7)
Consideration of the health, safety, location, parking and welfare of the citizens and preservation of surrounding neighborhoods, with reference to such factors as noise, traffic and loitering;
(8)
Consideration of the health, safety, and welfare of the participants to the selling, peddling, soliciting and canvassing, including, but not limited to, age of participants, level of adult supervision, likelihood of subjection of participants to life threatening occurrences; and
(9)
The history or reputation of the location for criminal activity, including, but not limited to, prostitution or other sex offenses, crimes of violence, gambling, and illegal dealing in alcoholic beverages or drugs.
(i)
Temporary merchants. A temporary merchant shall comply with the following rules:
(1)
Display of products must be located outside of the road right-of-way, and all structures shall be located in accordance with the setback requirements based on the zoning district and classification of the property on which the activity is taking place.
(2)
May only be located on property where controlled vehicular access and adequate parking located outside of the road right-of-way is provided.
(3)
Meet criteria established by the standard building code for construction for any temporary structure on the premises, other than a tent. A building permit will be required for such structures.
(4)
Provide to the city a copy of a written agreement of the land owner permitting use of the property by the roadside vendor, accompanied by a plat of the property being used, if the temporary merchant is not the owner of the property on which the activity is taking place.
(5)
Comply with all City Code requirements, including site plan review and building plan review, upon erection of any structure requiring electrical service, temporary or permanent.
(6)
Comply with all requirements of this chapter relating to signs. The signs of temporary merchants will be treated as temporary signs for special events and shall meet the requirements set forth in the city's sign ordinance. The temporary sign may be maintained for a maximum period of six months.
A temporary merchant must obtain a permit from the city, and pay the occupation tax or registration fee prior to engaging in any selling or display activities set forth in this section.
If the temporary merchant does not construct a building or structure in compliance with the city building codes, then any tent, stand, or other temporary structure must be removed from the site at dusk during each day of operation.
Any temporary stand or tent must be maintained in a neat and orderly manner free of loose debris and trash. Proper trash receptacles should be provided and maintained.
(Ord. of 5-4-2017(1), Exh. A)

Sec. 16-46. - Parades.
(a)
Registration and permit. Any person who wishes to organize, form, or conduct a parade as defined herein shall be required to register such parade with the police chief at least 24 hours in advance of the event and to obtain a permit therefor.
(b)
Definition. For the purposes of this section, the term "parade" means any march, ceremony, demonstration, exhibition, or procession of any kind upon any public street of the city.
(c)
Application. Application for a permit to conduct a parade shall be made to the police chief in writing, shall be signed by the person responsible for the conduct of the parade, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The time proposed for the parade;
(2)
The route of the proposed parade;
(3)
The number of vehicles (if any) and number of persons whose participation is anticipated in the proposed parade;
(4)
The name and address of the person or organization sponsoring or promoting the proposed parade; and
(5)
The name and address of the person making the application for a parade permit.
(d)
Review of application. The police chief shall forward the application to the mayor, who shall review the information set forth in the application and ascertain the following: the extent of vehicular and pedestrian traffic to be anticipated at the time and place of and on the route of the proposed parade; the availability of police forces to escort the proposed parade and to direct traffic in conjunction with the proposed parade; and whether or not, in the light of all the circumstances, the proposed parade will unreasonably burden or interfere with the normal use of the streets or sidewalks of the city by the general public.
(e)
Disposition. In the event the mayor determines in view of all the circumstances that the proposed parade will unreasonably burden and interfere with the normal use of the streets or sidewalks of the city by the general public, he shall deny the request for a parade permit; and if he determines on the contrary that the proposed parade will not unreasonably burden or interfere with the normal use of the streets or sidewalks of the city by the general public, he shall grant the parade permit. In either case, the mayor shall indicate his disposition on the application and shall notify the applicant of the action taken.
(f)
Exemption. The provisions of this section shall be inapplicable to any parade which is conducted under the supervision of a practicing mortician in conjunction with any funeral.
(Code 1998, § 32-206)
Sec. 16-47. - Billiard and pool rooms.
(a)
License required. Each person keeping, operating, or maintaining a billiard table, pool table, or any other table of like character within the corporate limits of the city for use by the public must obtain a license from the city clerk/treasurer in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
Sale or use of intoxicating liquors. No alcoholic beverages shall be sold, served, or allowed to be used in or on the premises of billiard rooms or any place operated in connection therewith, except if such premises or establishment is an establishment which is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages and derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages; provided, however, that if alcoholic beverages are sold by the drink for consumption on the premises of a billiard room, the governing authority of a local government may prohibit the sale, serving, or use of alcoholic beverages therein unless the establishment derives at least 75 percent of its revenue from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages; and no local government may prohibit billiard rooms or the playing of billiards in any business which neither sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises nor engages in package sales of such beverages nor allows alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises.
(c)
Fee established. The annual business license fee for each operator of a billiard or pool room in the city shall be as established by the city council, from time to time.
(d)
Hours of operation. It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator, or manager of any billiard or pool room in the city to permit such establishment to be open for business between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. or between the hours of 12:00 midnight on Saturday and 6:00 a.m. on Monday.
(e)
Minors. It shall be unlawful for any person having charge of or control of any pool room or billiard room open to the public to admit into such room any minor, whether for the purpose of playing billiards or pool or not.
(f)
Clear view of premises required. The owner, operator, or manager of any billiard or pool room in the city shall not permit to be used on such premises any screens, shades, partitions, or other devices of like character which shall have the effect of obstructing the view through the windows or doors of the place where the billiard or pool tables are kept.
(g)
Doors. The doors of all billiard or pool rooms licensed under this section shall be kept unlocked whenever the tables are in use, or when any person other than the proprietor or his agent is present in such place.
(h)
Gambling. It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator, or manager of any billiard or pool room open for public use to allow gambling of any kind to occur upon such premises.
(i)
Exemption. The provisions of this section shall not apply to billiard tables or rooms operated by private industrial concerns, Young Men's Christian Associations, religious orders, charitable institutions, state, county, or city institutions, fraternal orders, or bona fide clubs using such tables for members or employees only.
(j)
Penalty. Every person, firm, or corporation operating a billiard room within a municipality without having applied for a license pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1 et seq., shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished according to the general penalties as described in section 1-8. Each day that the billiard room is operated without a license shall be a separate offense.
(Code 1998, § 32-207)
State Law reference— Local governments power to license, regulate, and tax billiard rooms, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.
Sec. 16-48. - Establishment selling food.
(a)
Necessary permits. Prior to opening or operating a food service establishment one must obtain the following valid permits:
(1)
Certificate of zoning compliance for the proposed location;
(2)
Valid food service permit from the county board of health;
(3)
Certificate from the fire marshal indicating an acceptable inspection of the premises. Each year upon renewal of the business license the licensee shall provide proof of a current food service permit from the county board of health.
(b)
Failure to obtain any of the required permits will result in the refusal to license a food service establishment and loss of any of the required permits or certificates will result in the revocation of a city business license. Violation of any of the permits may be enforced by the city as a city ordinance violation.
(c)
Grease traps. All restaurants and any other establishment selling food within the city limits shall hereinafter be required to install grease traps in accordance with the specifications set forth by the mayor and council or by the county board of health. Failure to comply with this regulation shall be grounds for revocation of the establishment's business license.
(Ord. of 10-2-2014(3), §§ 1, 2)
