Sec. 8-1-1 - Members; meetings.
modified
(a)
The Planning and Zoning Commission shall consist of eight (8) members who shall be residents of the City of Sandersville, with said eight (8) members to be appointed by the Mayor and City Council. It is hereby specifically designated that each Council Member that serves as a Council Member for a voting district shall nominate two (2) potential Planning and Zoning Commission Members from their respective district, and the Mayor and Council shall vote to either approve or disapprove these nominated members at the next regularly scheduled Council meeting following submission of the members to serve on the Planning and Zoning Commission. None of the members shall hold public office in the City.
(b)
The terms of the members shall be for three-year staggered terms.
(c)
Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the Mayor and Council who shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing.
(d)
All members shall receive compensation as set by the Mayor and City Council and may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(e)
Meetings shall be limited to two (2) per month, except in emergencies.
(f)
The Mayor or one (1) Council member may serve as an ex officio member of the Planning and Zoning Commission without power of voting or compensation.
(Ord. of 11/5/79; Ord. of 3-23-92; Ord. of 9/18/06)
Sec. 8-1-2 - Officers; rules of procedure.
(a)
The Planning and Zoning Commission shall elect its chairperson and vice chairperson from among its members. The term of the chairperson and vice chairperson shall be for one (1) year with eligibility for re-election. The Planning and Zoning Commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be a non-elected officer or employee of the municipality.
(b)
The Planning and Zoning Commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting.
(Ord. of 11/5/79)
Sec. 8-1-3 - Meetings open to public; records.
All meetings of the Planning and Zoning Commission shall be open to the public except as otherwise provided by law, and all records of the Planning and Zoning Commission shall be a public record.
State Law reference— Georgia Sunshine Law, O.C.G.A., § 50-14-1 et seq.
Sec. 8-1-4 - Staff; expenditures.
(a)
The Planning and Zoning Commission may appoint such employees and staff as necessary for its work and may cooperate with, contract with, or accept funds from federal, state, or local, public or semi-public agencies, or private individuals or corporations. It may expend such funds and may carry out such cooperative undertakings and contracts for such services as it may require.
(b)
The expenditures of the Planning and Zoning Commission shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the Mayor and Council.
(Ord. of 11/5/79)
Sec. 8-1-5 - Powers and duties.
(a)
The Planning and Zoning Commission shall make careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments. It shall prepare such plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morale, convenience, prosperity, or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of its political jurisdiction. In particular, the Planning and Zoning Commission shall:
(1)
Prepare a master plan for the development of the city.
(2)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the mayor and council a zoning ordinance and map for the city.
(3)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the mayor and council, regulations for the subdivision of land within the city, and administer the regulations that may be adopted.
(4)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the mayor and council, a plat or plats of an official map showing the exact location of the boundary lines of existing, proposed, extended, widening or narrowed streets, public open spaces or public building sites, together with regulations to control the erection of buildings or other structures within such lines, within the city or a specified portion thereof.
(b)
Unincorporated areas adjacent to the city may be added to the jurisdiction of the planning and zoning commission for general planning and for master plan preparation and administration of zoning ordinances, land subdivision regulations, and official maps, if the governing bodies of the county and municipality agree to the boundaries of such additional areas, procedures for joint action, procedures for the adoption and administration of ordinances and resolutions, regulations applying to the area, and the manner of obtaining equitable representation on the municipal planning commission. Such agreement shall be formally established by appropriate official action by the governing authorities involved.
(c)
The planning and zoning commission may:
(1)
Make, publish, and distribute maps, plans and reports, and recommendations relating to the plan and development of the city to public officials and agencies, public utility companies, civic, education, professional, and other organizations and citizens.
(2)
Recommend to the mayor and/or council members, programs for public improvements and the financing thereof.
(3)
Enter upon land, make examinations and surveys, and place and maintain necessary monuments and marks thereon; provided, however, that the planning and zoning commission shall be liable for any injury or damage to property resulting therefrom.
(d)
In general, the planning and zoning commission shall have powers as may be necessary to enable it to perform its functions and promote the planning of the city.
(Ord. of 11/5/79)
Sec. 8-1-6 - Cooperation required.
All public officials shall, upon request, furnish to the planning and zoning commission, within a reasonable amount of time, such available information as it may require.
(Ord. of 11/5/79)
