ARTICLE V. - EFFICIENT PLUMBING FIXTURES

Sec. 5-200. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to require that buildings constructed before January 1, 1993 be retrofitted with water-conserving plumbing fixtures also known as low-flow plumbing fixtures.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08)
Sec. 5-201. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Building official. Personnel selected by the City Manager or designee of the City of Doraville assigned the task of applying this article.
Buyer. An entity attempting to acquire by any means a building subject to this article.
Mayor and Council. The duly elected governing authority of the City of Doraville, Georgia.
Seller. An entity attempting to dispense of by any means other than a gift any building subject to this article.
Water-conserving plumbing fixture. Ultra low-flow toilets (ULFTs) that use a maximum of 1.6 gallons per flush; urinals that use a maximum 1.0 gallons per flush; showerheads that emit a maximum of 2.5 gallons per minute; and kitchen faucets that emit a maximum of 2.2 gallons per minute.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 3, 6-17-13)
Sec. 5-202. - Applicability and exceptions.
This article shall apply to all buildings located within the City limits of Doraville, Georgia that have plumbing connecting water to the building which were built before January 1, 1993. This article shall only be triggered upon sale of a relevant property and as set out below.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08)
Sec. 5-203. - Exemptions.
The requirement to retrofit the otherwise subject building shall not apply if:
(1)
The property is being advertised for foreclosure;
(2)
A buyer is demolishing the residence after it is purchased; provided, however, that any water service that is being obtained by the buyer may only be used for demolition or construction related purposes;
(3)
A buyer is a spouse, child or parent of the seller (including conveyances during the administration of the estate of such spouse, parent or child);
(4)
Cost to install the water-conserving plumbing fixtures exceeds one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) per toilet in a single-family residential building; or
(5)
The cost to install the water-conserving plumbing fixtures exceeds two thousand dollars ($2,000.00) per toilet in a commercial building.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08)
Sec. 5-204. - Application to buyers of buildings and certificate of compliance.
Before a buyer can connect to water service from Dekalb County, the buyer must fill out an application and attach a certificate of compliance that all plumbing fixtures and water-conserving fixtures. The certificate must be signed by a home inspector, a licensed plumber or an inspector with Dekalb County's Department of Watershed Management. Applications can be acquired from the Department of Watershed Management of Dekalb County. Applications shall be returned to Doraville City Hall.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08)
Sec. 5-205. - Seller's disclosures.
Sellers of buildings subject to this article are required to disclose to buyers before they sign a purchase and sale agreement that:
(1)
The property is located in the City of Doraville and contains a building constructed prior to 1993; and
(2)
Buyer will be obligated as a condition of applying for water service from DeKalb County to attach to the application a certificate of compliance signed by a home inspector, department of watershed management inspector or licensed plumber certifying that all plumbing fixtures at the building are water-conserving plumbing fixtures.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08)
Sec. 5-206. - Enforcement.
This article shall be enforced by the Building Official of the City of Doraville. All proof of compliance required to be provided to DeKalb County and must also be provided to the building official.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08)
Sec. 5-207. - Effective date of article.
For both residential and non-residential buildings, the effective date shall be January 2, 2009.
(Ord. No. 2008-27, § 1, 11-3-08)
Secs. 5-208, 5-209. - Reserved.
