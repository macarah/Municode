ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Licenses required for advertising.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the business of poster advertising, painted bulletin advertising, card and banner tacking or hanging, distributing of handbills or any other form of indoor or outdoor advertising without first obtaining a license to conduct such business.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, employer, agent or representative of any such person to post or attach to any building or otherwise to post, distribute or exhibit in the City any circular, card or advertisement for rent or sale of any property located in the City not the property of the person so advertising same without such person having first paid the license tax for doing such business.
(Code 1969, § 3-1)
Sec. 6-2. - Tearing down, mutilating advertisements unlawful.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tear down or mutilate any bill, placard or other advertisement legally posted.
(Code 1969, § 3-2)
Sec. 6-3. - Misrepresentation of goods or services.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person engaged in any business which includes the furnishing of services or materials in connection therewith to falsely represent, by advertisement or otherwise, that any services or materials offered for sale or in connection with a sale are of a certain brand or quality recognized in such trade or business, and then, with intent to defraud, furnish services or materials of a brand or quality inferior to that of those so represented or advertised.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to falsely represent, by advertisement or otherwise, that the goods, wares, or merchandise which such person offers for sale are of a certain brand or quality or that the goods will be sold at a certain percentage of their cost price or value and then sell goods, wares or merchandise of a brand or quality inferior to that or those represented or advertised, or sell such goods, wares or merchandise at a higher percentage of their cost value than that represented or advertised.
(Code 1969, § 3-3)
Sec. 6-4. - Fire, bankrupt, etc., sales.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to advertise by handbills, newspaper, radio, posters or otherwise that he is operating, offering or maintaining "fire sales," "bankrupt sales," "closing-out" or "going out of business sales" and similar businesses, whereby the public may be led to believe merchandise is being offered for sale at reduced rates on account of fire, wreck, bankruptcy, closing-out or discontinuance of business when in fact such sales are not bona fide fire, wreck, bankruptcy, closing-out or discontinuance of business sales, and the advertisement and representations are untrue and false.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person conducting any sale, whether by auction or otherwise of any goods, wares or merchandise which are or have been or which are claimed to be or to have been in or damaged by a fire or which are or have been or which are claimed to be or to have been sold or purchased on account of any fire, or which are or have been or are claimed to be or to have been the property of any bankrupt or person who has failed in business or has made a general assignment or which are being sold or offered for sale in any other way than through the usual customary channels of trade to sell or offer for sale any other goods, wares or merchandise not so held, purchased, affected or damaged, or to add or permit to be added to, or to bring into or permit to be brought into any store, warehouse or other building for the purpose of being sold at such sale.
(Code 1969, § 3-4)
Sec. 6-5. - Printed advertising matter—Littering.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person distributing, or causing to be distributed, circulars, handbills, papers or other printed advertising matter through the City to throw into or place in any yard, dwelling or place of business more than one (1) of the circulars, handbills, papers or printed advertising matter which such person may be distributing.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person distributing or causing to be distributed circulars, handbills, papers or other printed advertising through the City to throw or scatter the same upon any of the streets, sidewalks or alleys or other public places in the City.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person receiving circulars, handbills, papers or other printing in advertisement form from distributors, or for any other person to throw or scatter the same upon any of the streets, sidewalks, alleys or other public places in the City.
(Ord. No. 06-18, § 1, 7-19-06)
Sec. 6-6. - Same—Fastening to curbs, lampposts, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person to paste, print, nail, tack or otherwise fasten any card, banner, handbill, sign, poster or advertisement or notice of any kind, or cause the same to be done, on any curbstone, lamppost, pole, fence, wire, bridge or tree upon any public property within the City, or upon any private property without the written consent of the owner of such property.
(Ord. No. 06-18, § 1, 7-19-06)
Sec. 6-7. - Same—Depositing in or fastening to motor vehicles.
No person other than the owner or driver thereof shall deposit any advertising matter in any motor vehicle nor stick or otherwise fasten any advertising matter on any part of any motor vehicle.
(Ord. No. 06-18, § 1, 7-19-06)
Sec. 6-8. - Tinted windows.
All windows and/or glass doors of any licensed establishment regulated by articles III (pawnshops), VI (dealers in precious metals, gems and used watches), article IX (massage parlors), article XIV (billiard parlors), article XVI (tattoo/body crafting studios), and article XVII (commercial soft tissue touch or manipulation—CSTTOM) shall be transparent and, at all times the establishment is in operation, at least one (1) window and/or glass door on any facade of the building housing the establishment shall not be covered by shades, drapes or other window treatment so that the inside of the establishment is not fully visible from the outside. Currently existing licensees as of the date of adoption of this section shall have ninety (90) days from the adoption of this section to bring their licensed establishment into compliance with this requirement.
(Ord. No. 2012-06, § 1, 3-5-12)

Sec. 6-9. - New car washes to recycle water.
(a)
General provisions.
(1)
Purpose and intent. The purpose of this section is to reduce water consumption from commercial car wash facilities by requiring all new conveyor car washes to install operational recycled water systems.
(2)
Applicability.
a.
This section applies to all new conveyor car washes permitted and constructed after January 1, 2011, regardless of the water source.
b.
The provisions of this section do not apply to conveyor commercial car washes that were permitted or constructed before January 1, 2011.
c.
The provisions of this section do not apply to self-service car washes or in-bay car washes.
(b)
Definitions. The following words and phrases, whenever used in this section, have the meaning defined as follows:
(1)
In bay automatic car wash means a commercial car wash where the driver pulls into the bay and parks the car. The vehicle remains stationary while a machine moves back and forth over the vehicle to clean it, instead of the vehicle moving through the tunnel.
(2)
Conveyor car wash means a commercial car wash where the car moves on a conveyor belt during the wash. The driver of the vehicle can remain in the vehicle or wait outside of the vehicle.
(3)
Recycled water system means a water system that captures and reuses water previously used in wash or rinse cycles.
(4)
Self-service car wash means a commercial car wash where the customers wash their cars themselves with spray wands and brushes.
(c)
Commercial car wash water recycling requirement. All new commercial conveyor carwashes, permitted and constructed after January 1, 2011, must install operational recycled water systems. A minimum of fifty (50) percent of water utilized will be recycled.
(Ord. No. 2016-03 , § 1, 4-11-16)

Sec. 6-12. - Identification card required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any person to be upon the premises of a petroleum or wholesale propane terminal distribution facility or petroleum or wholesale propane shipment facility without having a Doraville Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card or a Visitor Identification Badge as set forth below.
(b)
The owner or operator of any petroleum or wholesale propane terminal distribution facility or petroleum or wholesale propane shipment facility who allows any person to enter upon the premises of such facility without a valid Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card, or to remain upon the premises without such a card shall be guilty of a violation of this section.
(Ord. No. 2008-21, § 1, 10-6-08)

Sec. 6-14. - Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Cards.
(a)
All drivers of petroleum or wholesale propane tank trucks, agents and employees of petroleum or wholesale propane companies, petroleum or wholesale propane terminal distribution facilities and petroleum or wholesale propane shipment facilities, must present a valid Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card to the attendant on duty prior to entrance upon the property of any petroleum or wholesale propane terminal distribution facility or petroleum or wholesale propane shipment facility. The card shall be permanently displayed upon the individual at all times when he or she is on the property of these facilities.
(b)
It shall be the duty of each petroleum or wholesale propane terminal distribution facility or petroleum or wholesale propane shipment facility, to restrict entrance to each such facility to only those individuals having in effect a valid Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card or in their possession a valid visitor identification badge. This shall include employees, staff, drivers, cleaning staff, supervisors, maintenance personnel and anyone else entering the premises.
(c)
The Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card shall contain the individual's picture, name and address, employer(s) and date of birth.
(d)
(1)
All prospective and current employees required to have a Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card shall apply for the card on forms furnished by the City of Doraville. The application form shall contain the applicant's consent to a criminal background check and a written authorization from the distributor or shipment facility employer or prospective employer. The application shall also contain the name and address of the applicant's employer and the facility or facilities for which the card will be used to gain access. A processing fee of one hundred dollars ($100.00) shall accompany each request. Late application or renewal will result in an additional administrative processing cost of fifty dollars ($50.00).
(2)
Upon receipt of the application, the Doraville Police Department shall investigate the applicant, including a criminal background check and a motor vehicle driving history, and shall issue the identification card, personally, when he or she is photographed at the Doraville Police Department for use on the card. A card shall be issued unless, during the course of the investigation of the applicant, a disqualifying act is discovered.
(3)
Upon discovery of any disqualifying act, the Doraville Police Department will notify the applicant and his or her employer that such an act has been discovered. Specifics, such as the nature or type of the act, will not be disclosed to other parties except as required by the Georgia Open Records law.
(4)
Any applicant or employer of an applicant who is denied an identification card, may appeal such denial, in writing, within ten (10) days of receipt of the denial, to the Mayor and City Council of Doraville. A decision shall be made within forty-five (45) days of the filing of the notice of appeal. No action by the Mayor or Council shall constitute a denial of the appeal.
(5)
The Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card shall not be transferable. Any changes in any of the information required in the card shall be immediately transmitted to the City of Doraville Police Department so a replacement card containing correct information may be used.
(6)
Applications for identification cards may be telefaxed or delivered personally to the Doraville Police Department. However, the identification card may only be delivered personally to the applicant at the time his or her photograph is made for inclusion on the card.
(7)
An employee who terminates his or her employment shall return his or her identification card to the former employer at the time of termination.
(8)
The Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card shall be regularly renewed every year on or before the applicant's birthday.
(Ord. No. 2008-21, § 1, 10-6-08; Ord. No. 2009-06, § 1, 3-19-09)

Sec. 6-15. - Designation of persons in charge and provision for nonpermitted persons.
(a)
Each petroleum or wholesale propane terminal distribution facility and petroleum or wholesale propane shipment facility shall designate a manager or person in charge of the facility for all times the facility is open.
(b)
A manager or other designated person in charge may permit a person who does not have a Petroleum or Wholesale Propane Carrier/Employee Identification Card on the facility's premises subject to the following:
(1)
The person's name, employer, telephone number, date and time of visit and purpose of the visit shall be maintained in a permanent log maintained at the facility's office.
(2)
Each such person shall be required to display a Visitor Identification Badge provided by the facility, identifying that person as a visitor. Each Visitor Identification badge shall be numbered and clearly identify the holder of the badge as a visitor. The number of the badge shall be entered in the log required by subsection (b)(1) above. The badge must be conspicuously displayed by the visitor at all times he or she is on the premises and shall be returned to the person in charge when the visitor leaves.
(Ord. No. 2008-21, § 1, 10-6-08)
