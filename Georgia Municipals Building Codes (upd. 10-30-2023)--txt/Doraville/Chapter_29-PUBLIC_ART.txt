Chapter 29 - PUBLIC ART

Sec. 29-1. - Definitions.
new
For the purposes of submissions under section 29-5 only:
Acceptable for public art shall include the following:
(1)
All forms of limited edition or one-of-a-kind original creations of visual art created by an artist;
(2)
Project features or enhancements which are unique and produced by a professional artist such as benches and fountains;
(3)
Murals or mosaics covering walls;
(4)
Natural art work;
(5)
Professional artist sculptures which can be free-standing, wall-supported and suspended and made of durable materials suitable to the site and the climate; and
(6)
Other suitable artworks as presented to, and approved by, the sole decision of the Art Committee.
Artist shall be defined as an individual, or a group of people, who meets one or more of the following criteria:
(1)
Realizes income through the sale, performance, publication, or commission of original works of art, is licensed to teach art in the state;
(2)
Has previously exhibited, presented, performed or published original works of art in museums, galleries or other recognized art venues and publications;
(3)
Has formal training or education in a field of art; and
(4)
Has received awards or other forms of recognition from arts juries, arts grant panels, and similar entities for his/her/their artistic abilities or accomplishments.
City-owned art shall be defined as a work of art commissioned or acquired by the City for the purposes of display on city property.
Not acceptable for public art shall include:
(1)
Business logos or art that incorporates a logo for the primary purpose of advertising a business;
(2)
Art that incorporates a distinguishable product for the primary purpose of advertising a business;
(3)
Directional elements such as super-graphics, signage or color coding except where these elements are integral parts of the original works of art;
(4)
Mass-produced art objects, such as fountains, statutory objects or playground equipment;
(5)
Reproduction by mechanical or other means of original works of art;
(6)
Decorative ornamental or functional elements created by the project architect instead of an artist commissioned for this purpose;
(7)
Electrical, water or mechanical service for activation of the works of art;
(8)
Installations which would encourage lack of maintenance or are representative of lack of maintenance or blight on any property;
(9)
Art exhibitions, performances, and educational activities;
(10)
Security and publicity concerning the works of art; and
(11)
Art used as signage.
Public art shall be defined as a work created by a visual artist as defined herein, as approved by the Art Commission created herein, for display in public space or visible from a public space as demonstrated by a public art easement.

Sec. 29-2. - City of Doraville Art Commission.
new
(a)
There is hereby created for the City a City of Doraville Art Commission composed of five (5) members with a variety of perspectives and experience in art, design, planning, and public place management as follows:
(1)
At least two (2) members shall be working artists, preferably one (1) in two-dimensional media and one (1) in three-dimensional media.
(2)
At least two (2) members shall be from the following fields: Arts management, design, planning or public place management.
(3)
All members shall be residents of the City or owners of a business with a physical location in the City.
(4)
At its discretion, the Commission may include an additional non-voting youth representative attending school and or college in the City.
(b)
Voting members of the Art Commission shall be appointed by the Mayor upon recommendations submitted by members of the City Council (including the Mayor) and approved by majority of the City Council.
(c)
Terms of the Art Commission shall be for four (4) years and shall be staggered as follows: One (1) initial Commission member shall be for a period of two (2) years, two (2) Commission members shall be for a period of three (3) years and two (2) Commission members shall be for a period of four (4) years. Thereafter, Commission members shall serve for a term of four (4) years each.
(d)
Art Commission members may not serve more than two (2) consecutive three-year terms.
(e)
Art Commission members who are no longer qualified to serve as same pursuant to this section may finish their current term but may not be reappointed for a future term unless they fit the proper qualifications established herein.
(f)
The Art Commission shall meet at least one (1) time per year and shall establish such regular and special called meetings as necessary to execute its authority and responsibility under this chapter.
(g)
The Art Commission shall appoint a Chairman and establish such by-laws and rules and regulations as it deems necessary for the discharge of its authority and responsibility.
(h)
The City's Economic Development Department shall support the Art Commission in the conduct of its business.

Sec. 29-3. - Role of the City of Doraville Art Commission.
new
The Art Commission shall have the following authority and responsibility:
(1)
Develop policies, procedures and guidelines to implement a public implementation plan;
(2)
Appoint task forces/selection committees for projects;
(3)
If requested, review and approve "projects plans" for proposed artworks that are acquired or commissioned with City funds and/or proposed for City-owned property;
(4)
If requested, review and approve the selection of artists and artist concepts for artworks commissioned with City funds and/or proposed for City-owned property;
(5)
Prepare an annual report to Council on public art activities;
(6)
Review and recommend public art proposals consistent with definitions and criteria for public art and themes adopted by Mayor and City Council;
(7)
If requested, recommend the acceptance of donations of public art for public property; and
(8)
Review and recommend public art recommendations in the comprehensive plans, area plans, overlay districts and development conditions.
(9)
In conjunction with the staff and City Attorney, to:
a.
Approve to form, substance, and requirements of all applications and/or documents required for submission of proposed projects pursuant to section 29-5 of this chapter, and
b.
Determine the date to begin with the acceptance of proposals pursuant to section 29-5.

Sec. 29-4. - Role of Mayor and City Council.
new
The role of Mayor and City Council shall be as follows:
(1)
Review and approve a public art master plan;
(2)
Confirm appointments to the Art Commission;
(3)
Review and approve public art projects after approval by Art Commission and determine whether the City will financially co-sponsor any such projects (only if on right of way or established city-owned property);
(4)
Review and approve acquisitions, loans, donations and de-accessions upon referral by the Art Commission; and
(5)
Develop approved themes, requirements, and locations for public art project submissions.

Sec. 29-5. - Public art project submissions.
new
(a)
Any artist, developer or Doraville property owner may submit an application for a public art installation in the City to the Economic Development Department. The application, which shall be on a form created by the Economic Development Department, shall provide the following information:
(1)
Name and resume of the proposed artist, including photographic or video representations of artist's existing work, preferably works of public art;
(2)
Artist rendering of the proposed public art installation, including specific design elements, medium, materials, and description of any work that will require local, state or federal permits.
(3)
The suggested location of installation and description of why said location is appropriate for this public art installation, as well as affidavit from the property owner with consent for said installation.
(4)
The desired length of time said public art installation will remain on the property (private property only);
(5)
Demonstration of property rights to install proposed public art, the proposed cost of installing same, and demonstration of the existence of the funds for said installation;
(6)
Proposed agreement between property owner and the City requiring property owner to erect and maintain the public art installation at the property owner's expense for the life of the installation;
(7)
How the public art installation meets the theme(s) and other criteria approved by the Mayor and City Council; and
(8)
A proposed and executed public art easement which shall grant the City an easement in the location of the public art and grant the City all authority to, in the City's discretion, maintain said installation and claim ownership in said installation (private property only);
(9)
Agreement by the artist that the City shall have the unfettered right, in its sole discretion, without notice to, approval of, or consultation with artist, to (i) relocate the artwork to another site or location, (ii) discontinue any required maintenance, if any, of the artwork, (iii) disassemble and remove the artwork in its entirety, or (iv) alter the artwork in connection with emergency and/or public safety considerations. Artist shall acknowledge and agree: (i) that City must retain the freedoms set forth in this section with respect to the artwork for public policy, municipal planning, public safety, environmental, budgetary and related reasons, (ii) that City's retention of such freedoms is reasonable under the circumstances.
(10)
Agreement by the artist that the City may make any material alteration or change to the artwork in a manner that would affect the artwork's intended character or appearance so long as the artist has been provided notice of such proposed alteration and, if requested by artist following such notice, the opportunity to provide reasonable input in an effort to accommodate any aesthetic or other concerns of artist with respect to any such proposed alteration.
(11)
Subject to the foregoing limitations of paragraphs (9) and (10), agreement that the artist waives any rights he or she may have regarding the Visual Artists Rights Act of 1990, 17 USC Section 106A.
(12)
Any other information as determined by the Economic Development Department and the Art Commission necessary for determination to accept or reject the installation.
(b)
Upon submitting the application, the Economic Development Department shall forward the application to the Art Commission which shall consider same in an upcoming regular or special meeting taking place at least thirty (30) days after the date of submission of the application. The Economic Development Department shall place a sign next to the proposed location with the date and time of the Art Commission meeting and a picture of the proposed installation.
(c)
The Art Commission shall consider the proposed installation on the basis of whether:
(1)
The application is complete and proper in all respects;
(2)
The proposal meets the theme(s) and requirements approved by the Mayor and City Council;
(3)
The suggested location is appropriate for the proposed, or any, public art installation;
(4)
The medium and materials utilized for the installations are appropriate and desirable;
(5)
The proposal meets all of the criteria and requirements as set forth in this chapter, and
(6)
And any other considerations the Committee finds relevant to its determination on the application.
The Art Commission shall approve or deny the public art installation proposal pursuant to the above criteria in its sole discretion. The decision of the Art Commission shall be final and not appealable.
(d)
If the Art Commission approves the proposed public art installation, they shall forward the application to the Mayor and City Council, which shall review the proposed maintenance agreement and public art easement and, upon recommendation of the City Attorney, approve or disapprove the maintenance agreement and public art easement on the basis of whether they serve the purpose for maintenance and existence of the installation as approved public art.

Sec. 29-6. - Erection, maintenance and removal of public art installations.
new
(a)
Upon approval of the maintenance agreement and public art easement, and recording of same, the property owner shall have the public art installed within the period stated in the application or within a reasonable time thereafter. If the public art is not substantially installed by that time, the Mayor and City Council may terminate the easement and the property owner would be required to file a subsequent application in order to erect said installation upon approval of same by the Art Commission.
(b)
If the public art installation comes into disrepair or is otherwise not properly maintained pursuant to the maintenance agreement, Code Enforcement may enforce the maintenance responsibility against the property owner. If the property owner fails to, or refuses, to maintain the installation, the Mayor and City Council may terminate the easement and require the installation to be removed by the property owner or, if the property owner fails to remove the installation within a reasonable amount of time, the Mayor and City Council may remove said installation at the property owner's expense pursuant to the City's established nuisance abatement process. Alternatively, the Mayor and City Council may undertake maintenance of same installation at the expense of the property owner pursuant to the City's established nuisance abatement process.
(c)
If the installation is on right of way or City-owned land, and the responsible party fails to or refuses to maintain the installation, the Mayor and City Council may ether maintain the public art installation at the City's expense, remove the installation, or contract with a non-profit agency to maintain the installation at the non-profit agency's expense.
(d)
Any public art installation on private property may be removed by the property owner at any time by unilaterally terminating the easement. Upon termination of the easement, the property owner shall be required to remove the installation within a reasonable time period. If the property owner fails to remove the entire installation within the said reasonable time period, the City may remove same at the property owner's expense pursuant to the City's established nuisance abatement process.
