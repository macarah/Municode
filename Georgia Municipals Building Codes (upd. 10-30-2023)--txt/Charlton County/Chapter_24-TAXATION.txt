ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Exemptions for certain classes of goods (Freeport exemption).
(a)
Authority for section. This section and the tax exemptions provided for in this section are made under authority of Ga. Const. art. VII, § I, ¶ III, as amended (1976 Ga. Laws (Act No. 118), pages 1755 through 1759).
(b)
Unfinished goods. There is hereby exempted from taxation 20 percent of the value of the inventory of goods in the county in the process of manufacture or production which shall include all partly finished goods and raw materials held for direct use or consumption in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business in the state. The exemption provided for in this subsection shall apply only to tangible personal property which is substantially modified, altered or changed in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing, processing or production operations in this state.
(c)
Finished goods in storage. There is hereby exempted from taxation 20 percent of the value of the inventory of finished goods which, on January 1, are stored in a warehouse, dock or wharf, whether public or private, in the county and which are destined for shipment to a final destination outside the state and inventory of finished goods which are shipped into the state from outside this state and stored for transshipment to a final destination outside this state. The exemption provided for in this subsection shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is stored in this state.
(Code 1999, § 17-1; Res. of 4-2-1981, §§ 1, 2, 4)

ARTICLE II. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Taxation of financial institutions generally, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-90 et seq.; authorization to levy and collect a business license tax from depository financial institutions, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 24-21. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Depository financial institution means any state or national bank, state building and loan association and federal savings and loan association.
Gross receipts means gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(Code 1999, § 17-26; Res. of 11-3-1983, § 1)
Sec. 24-22. - Levy; rate.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for each year an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the county at a rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of the depository financial institutions.
(Code 1999, § 17-27; Res. of 11-3-1983, § 1)
Sec. 24-23. - Minimum amount.
The minimum annual amount of the business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 24-21 shall be $1,000.00.
(Code 1999, § 17-28; Res. of 11-3-1983, § 2)
Sec. 24-24. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to this tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the board of commissioners on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. The return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The board of commissioners shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this article based upon the information provided in the return.
(Code 1999, § 17-29; Res. of 11-3-1983, § 3)
Sec. 24-25. - Due date.
Taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than 30 days after filing of the return prescribed by section 24-24 unless extended by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1999, § 17-30; Res. of 11-3-1983, § 4)

ARTICLE V. - BUSINESS OCCUPATION TAX[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Business and occupation taxes, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-2 et seq.

Sec. 24-114. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Charlton County Business and Occupational Tax Ordinance."
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 1)
Sec. 24-115. - Occupational tax required for business dealings in the county.
For each year, each person, or other nonexempt entity, engaged in a business, trade, profession or occupation in the unincorporated aspect of the county (or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in the state but exerting substantial efforts within the county as per O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7) shall pay an occupation tax for said business, trade, profession or occupation; a business tax registration certificate shall be conspicuously displayed in the taxpayer's place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in the county. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the county, such business tax certificate shall be shown to the clerk of the board of commissioners, or any designee of the county board of commissioners or to any law enforcement officer of the county, upon request.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 2)
Sec. 24-116. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The business and occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The tax only applies to those businesses and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5 through 48-13-26. Any other applicable business licenses imposed pursuant to general state law and/or local ordinance are not repealed, included or affected in any manner by this article.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 3)
Sec. 24-117. - Construction of term; definitions.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business tax or occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession or business for revenue raising purposes.
Jurisdiction means the unincorporated areas of the county.
Person includes sole proprietors, corporations, partnerships, nonprofits or any other form of business organization, but specifically excludes charitable nonprofit organizations qualified as a 501(c) entity by federal law.
Practitioner or professional means a person who by state law is required to possess state licensure in order to engage in such profession, practice or occupation.
(b)
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5 (Specific, Business, and Occupation Taxes—Definitions), shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this article. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this article, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5 and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 4)
Sec. 24-118. - Occupation tax levied; restrictions.
(a)
An occupation tax shall be levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices in the unincorporated area of the county and/or upon the applicable out-of-state businesses with no location or office in the state as per O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7 based upon the following criteria: A flat fee as per O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(a)(4).
(b)
The county board of commissioners shall, by resolution, adopt a flat fee, the amount of which shall be within the discretion of the board and may, from time to time, be altered, in compliance with section 24-140.
(c)
No business or practitioner shall be required to pay more than one occupation tax for each of its locations. No occupation tax shall be required from those real estate brokers, real estate agents, or real estate companies whose offices are located outside the jurisdiction and who sell property inside the jurisdiction.
(d)
Where a business subject to the tax levied by this article begins operation in the first half of the calendar year, then the full tax shall be paid; if the business begins operation after July 1, then one-half of the tax shall be paid.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 5)
Sec. 24-119. - Paying occupation tax of business with no location in the state.
The requirements of this article shall apply fully to those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the state if the subject person's or entity's largest dollar volume of business in the state is in the county and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of the county for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of the county.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 6)
Sec. 24-120. - Each line of business to be identified on business registration.
The business registration of each business operated in the county's jurisdiction shall identify the line or lines of business that the business conducts. No business shall conduct any line of business without first having that line of business registered with the county and that line of business being noted by the county government upon the business registration form which is to be displayed by the business owner.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 7)
Sec. 24-121. - The number of businesses considered to be operating in the county.
Where a person conducts business at more than one fixed location, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of the occupation tax.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 8)
Sec. 24-122. - Professionals as classified in state law.
Practitioners of professions as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18) shall pay the same flat fee as a tax as is required of all businesses. Provided however, any professional or practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, this state, a municipality or county of the state, instrumentalities of the United States, this state or a municipality or county of the state shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that practice.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 9)
Sec. 24-123. - When tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
Each such occupation tax shall be for the current calendar year, and succeeding calendar years thereafter unless otherwise specifically provided. Said registration and occupation tax shall be payable January 1 of each year and shall, if not paid by March 31 of each year, be subject to penalties for delinquency as prescribed in this article. On any new profession, trade or calling begun in the county, the registration and tax shall be due immediately upon beginning business. A ten percent penalty shall be imposed if the tax levied herein has not been paid within 90 days of its due date. The tax registration herein provided for shall be issued by the clerk of the county commission and if any person, firm or corporation whose duty it is to obtain a registration shall, after said registration or occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in the county, any of the kind of profession, trade or calling subject to this article without having first obtained said registration, such offender shall, upon conviction, be punished by a fine and/or imprisonment as provided in this article.
(b)
In addition to the remedies in subsection (a) of this section, the tax may be collected in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions; the clerk of the board of commissioners is authorized to issue executions against the delinquent taxpayer as provided for by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-26(a).
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 10)
Sec. 24-124. - Exemption on grounds that business is operated for charitable purpose.
No business on which a business registration or occupation tax is levied by this article shall be exempt from said registration or tax on the ground that such business is operated as a charitable nonprofit organization qualified as a 501(c) entity by federal law.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 11)
Sec. 24-125. - Liability of officers and agents; registration required; failure to obtain.
Any person subject to the occupation tax levy pursuant to this article shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for said business as described in this article, and in default thereof the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a registration. Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of a the county business registration, show evidence that such requirements have been met; no registration shall issue in the absence of such proof. Every person commencing business in the county after January 1 of each year shall likewise obtain the registration herein provided for before commencing the same; and any person transacting, or offering to transact in the county, any of the kinds of business, trade, profession or occupation without first having so obtained said registration, shall be subject to penalties provided herein.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 12)
Sec. 24-126. - Businesses not covered by this article.
The following businesses are not covered by the provisions of this article, but may be assessed an occupation tax or other type of tax pursuant to the provisions of other general laws of the state or by local law:
(1)
Those businesses regulated by the state service commission.
(2)
Those electrical service businesses organized under chapter 3, title 46 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
(3)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including agribusiness.
(4)
Cooperative marketing associations governed by O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105.
(5)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.
(6)
Motor common carriers governed by O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15.
(7)
Those businesses governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355. (Businesses that purchase carload lots of guano, meats, meal, flour, bran, cottonseed or cottonseed meal and hulls.)
(8)
Agricultural products and livestock raised in the state governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356.
(9)
Depository financial institutions governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(10)
Facilities operated by a charitable trust governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-55.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 13)
Sec. 24-127. - License duration.
Each license issued under the provisions of this article shall be valid for the calendar year in which issued.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 14)
Sec. 24-128. - Transfers prohibited.
No tax registration certificate issued under this article shall be transferable; and no registration certificate shall be exchanged for a registration certificate for a different type of business.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 15)
Sec. 24-129. - Application.
(a)
Every person subject to the tax levied under this article shall, on or before January 31 of each year, or prior to engaging in said business, file with the clerk of the county commission or other designee of the county board of commissioners an application for a county business and occupational tax registration certificate.
(b)
Every person subject to the tax shall make application for said registration certificate on forms provided by the county, setting forth the following information:
(1)
Name of business/occupation.
(2)
Location.
(3)
Exact nature of business/occupation for which a registration certificate is requested.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 16)
Sec. 24-130. - Veterans.
Persons who can qualify for a state veterans free license under O.C.G.A. § 43-12-1, must secure such license from the state and present same to the county clerk, who will honor same and issue a county business and occupation tax registration certificate without requiring payment of the tax and mark said registration certificate "Exempt from Tax."
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 17)
Sec. 24-131. - Displaying registration certificate.
All persons having paid the tax required by this article shall exhibit the registration certificate, or receipt therefor, issued them by the county clerk in a conspicuous place in the office or quarters of such establishment or place of business.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 18)
Sec. 24-132. - Location of business.
Registration certificates granted under this article shall not authorize the holder thereof to exercise or carry on the trade, business or profession named therein at any other place than that mentioned in said registration certificate.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 19)
Sec. 24-133. - Change of location.
Upon removal of any taxpayer from the premises at which the trade, business or profession identified on the registration certificate was authorized to be conducted, it may and shall be lawful for the county clerk, by endorsement upon such registration certificate, to authorize the taxpayer, removing as foresaid to any other location, to carry on the trade, business or profession under such certificate.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 20)
Sec. 24-134. - Refund to discontinued business.
When the tax fixed by this article has been paid during the first half of the calendar year and the person paying such tax discontinues the business during the first half of the calendar year, the county clerk may refund one-half of said year's tax, provided all other license fee and other taxes owed by the taxpayer to the county have been paid, and provided application therefor is made within 30 days after discontinuing business.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 21)
Sec. 24-135. - Restrictions.
No person shall be issued a county business and occupation tax registration certificate as provided for in this article who is required to hold a license issued by the state to engage in the business, profession or trade which the person seeks to engage in, or is required to obtain health permits, or any other such regulatory permit or license, unless the person has a valid and current state license, or shows evidence that all regulatory requirements have been met and attaches a copy of same to the application. Any revocation or suspension of a person's state license or permit will cause an immediate termination or suspension of said person's registration certificate issued by the county.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 22)
Sec. 24-136. - Enforcement.
The county sheriff, the deputies, constables and all other law enforcement officers, the county clerk, or other designee of the county board of commissioners shall be authorized to issue citations to violators of any provisions of this article. Such citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall be signed by the officer who completes and services it and the original shall be filed with magistrates court of the county or the state court of the county.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 23)
Sec. 24-137. - Penalty.
Any person violating any provisions hereof, upon conviction, shall be punished according to the general penalty described in section 1-13.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 24)
Sec. 24-138. - Court proceedings.
(a)
Violations of this article shall be tried upon citation as described in section 24-136 and may be tried with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusation.
(b)
Violations of this article shall be tried in the magistrate court or the state court.
(c)
Nothing in this article shall prevent the commissioners of the county from bringing any civil action to present, correct or abate any violation of this article. No penalty or remedy prescribed herein shall be considered exclusive of any other remedy, but shall be available in addition to any other penalty or remedy by law.
(d)
Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 25)
Sec. 24-139. - Applications of provisions or prior ordinance.
This article does not repeal or affect the force of any part of any ordinance heretofore passed where taxes or fees levied under such prior ordinance have not been paid in full. So much and such parts of ordinances heretofore and hereafter passed as provided for the issuing and enforcing of execution for any tax or assessment required by such ordinances, or that imposed fines or penalties for the nonpayment of such tax, or for failure to pay regulatory fees provided for in said ordinance or ordinances, or failure to comply with any other provisions thereof, shall continue and remain in force until such tax, fee or assessment shall be fully paid.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 26)
Sec. 24-140. - Requirement of public hearing before tax increase.
The board of commissioners shall conduct a least one public hearing before adopting any ordinance or resolution which will increase the rate of occupation tax as set forth in this article.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(02), § 27)
Secs. 24-141—24-165. - Reserved.

Sec. 24-166. - 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charge, as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4), upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public service answering point in the amount of $0.75.

Sec. 24-167. - Collection of 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
Prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charges collected by sellers shall be remitted to the commissioner of the department of revenue at the times and in the manner provided by chapter 8, title 48 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated with respect to the sales and use tax imposed on prepaid wireless calling service.
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(01), § 2)
Sec. 24-168. - Depositing of funds; use of funds.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(5), funds received by this county from charges imposed by this article shall be deposited in the emergency telephone system fund maintained by the county pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134 and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction; all such funds shall be used exclusively for the purposes authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(e).
(Ord. of 8-4-2011(01), § 4)
