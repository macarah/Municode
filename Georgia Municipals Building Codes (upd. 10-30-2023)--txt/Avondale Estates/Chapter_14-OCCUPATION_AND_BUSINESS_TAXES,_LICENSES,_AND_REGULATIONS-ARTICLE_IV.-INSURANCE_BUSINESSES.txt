ARTICLE IV. - INSURANCE BUSINESSES

Sec. 14-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Insurance company: A company which is authorized to transact classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
Life insurance company: A company which is authorized to transact only the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5 as class (1).
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-30), 9-23-91)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 14-112. - Insurance company's license fee generally.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each insurance company doing business within the city in the amount of forty dollars ($40.00). An insurance company shall be doing business within the city if premiums are received from policies insuring persons within the city. For each separate business location in excess of one (1) operated and maintained by such company within the city, there is hereby levied an additional license fee in the amount of forty dollars ($40.00).
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-31), 9-23-91)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8(b)(1).
Sec. 14-113. - License fee for insurance companies insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
There is hereby levied an additional annual license fee of fourteen dollars ($14.00) upon each insurance company for each separate business location not otherwise subject to a license fee under this section which is operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and which, in connection with the loans or sales involving term financing, offers, solicits, or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of the insurance company.
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-32), 9-23-91)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8(b)(2).
Sec. 14-114. - Insurance agency license fees; independent insurance agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers in the amount of one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00) for each separate location within the city.
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-33), 9-23-91)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8(d).
Sec. 14-115. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurance companies.
There is hereby levied an annual tax on each life insurance company doing business within the state based solely upon the gross direct premiums, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4, which are received during the preceding calendar year from policies insuring persons within the city. The rate of tax shall be one (1) percent of such premiums.
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-34), 9-23-91)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1.
Sec. 14-116. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurance companies.
There is hereby levied an annual tax upon the gross direct premiums, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2, of all foreign, alien, and domestic insurance companies doing business within the state, other than life insurance companies, which are received during the preceding calendar year from policies insuring persons within the city. The rate of tax shall be two and one-half (2½) percent of such premiums.
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-35), 9-23-91)
Sec. 14-117. - Due date for license fees; delinquency penalties.
Any insurance company subject to payment of a license fee as imposed by this article, is given until the end of the business day on February 1 of each year to pay such license fee. If the license fee is not paid by that date, there shall be added to the license fee a penalty of ten (10) percent plus five (5) percent for each additional month or fraction thereof during which failure continues, not exceeding twenty-five (25) percent in the aggregate, as a penalty for failure to pay the license fee within the time required by this section.
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-36), 9-23-91)
Sec. 14-118. - Administrative provisions.
A certified copy of this article, and any amendments thereto, shall be filed by the city clerk with the insurance commissioner of the state within forty-five (45) days of enactment. Such filing shall be a condition to the validity and enforceability of this article.
(Ord. No. 839, § 1(13-37), 9-23-91)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. §§ 33-8-8(c), 33-8-8.1(e), 33-8-8.2(b)(1).
Secs. 14-119—14-140. - Reserved.
