ARTICLE IV. - FACTORY-BUILT HOUSING[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Certification of factory-built housing, Ga. Code Ann., § 8-2-110 et seq.

Sec. 12-188. - Definition.
For the purpose of this article, the term "unit" or "modular home" shall mean any structure, or component thereof, designed primarily for residential occupancy, which is wholly or in substantial part made, fabricated, formed or assembled in manufacturing facilities for installation, or assembly and installation, on the building site.
Sec. 12-189. - Zoning.
Any lot on which a prefabricated unit is placed must conform to the city's zoning ordinance.
(Min. of 11-6-79)
Cross reference— Zoning, app. A.
Sec. 12-190. - Compliance with building codes.
All prefabricated units must meet the requirements of the Georgia State Building Code for new housing construction, the Georgia State Plumbing Code and the Georgia State Electrical Code.
(Min. of 11-6-79)
Cross reference— Buildings and building regulations, ch. 5.
Sec. 12-191. - Compliance with housing code.
All prefabricated housing units must meet the city's minimum standards housing code.
(Min. of 11-6-79)
Sec. 12-192. - Inspection—Required.
Each prefabricated housing unit must be inspected during construction by the building inspector or, if the unit is preassembled, it must be unfinished so that all construction, wiring and plumbing can be inspected by the building inspector.
(Min. of 11-6-79)
Sec. 12-193. - Same—Fee.
The fee for a city inspection of a modular home to be delivered in the city shall be one hundred twenty dollars ($120.00) per unit.
(Min. of 4-8-80)
Secs. 12-194—12-200. - Reserved.
