Sec. 20-1. - Violations.
Upon any violation of sections 20-2—20-4, a police officer of the city shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 1-8 and to suspension from office at the discretion of the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 2-9)
Sec. 20-2. - Personal deportment.
Each member of the police force of the city shall be quiet and orderly, civil in his conduct, shall refrain at all times from profane language and in the performance of his duty shall maintain full command and control of his temper.
(Code 1968, § 2-9)
Sec. 20-3. - Performance of duty.
Unless he has written permission from the chief of police for additional employment, each member of the police force of the city shall devote his whole time and attention to the business of the police department, and although certain hours are allotted to each member thereof for the ordinary performance of duty, he shall be prepared at all times to act immediately upon notice that his services are required. Punctual attendance, prompt obedience to orders and conformity to rules and regulations shall be rigidly enforced.
(Code 1968, § 2-9)
Sec. 20-4. - Use of violence.
No member of the police force of the city shall wilfully maltreat or use unnecessary violence towards a person, and only in the most urgent case of self-defense shall he use a weapon.
(Code 1968, § 2-9)
State Law reference— Use of force in defense of self or others, Ga. Code Ann., § 16-3-21.
Sec. 20-5. - Knowledge of ordinances.
Any person elected or employed as a city police officer shall be required to familiarize himself with this Code of Ordinances and the other ordinances of the city before he shall be eligible to hold said office.
(Code 1968, § 2-10)
Sec. 20-6. - Reserved.
Editor's note— An ordinance adopted July 10, 2012 (W-5-12) repealed § 20-6, which pertained to public assemblies and gatherings, and derived from § 17-40 of the 1968 Code. See § 15-72 et seq. for similar provisions.
Sec. 20-7. - Duty to aid in suppressing affray, breach of peace.
It shall be unlawful for any person in the city between the ages of eighteen (18) and fifty (50) years, upon being summoned, deputized or called upon by any member of the police force, the mayor or any member of the council to aid in suppressing an affray or breach of the peace, to refuse to do so.
(Code 1968, § 17-60)
Charter reference— Power of city to provide for the prevention and punishment of public disturbances, § 1-3(20).
Sec. 20-8. - Duty to aid in making arrest.
It shall be unlawful for any person in the city between the ages of eighteen (18) and fifty (50) years, upon being summoned, deputized or called upon by any member of the police force, the mayor or any member of the council, to refuse to arrest or to aid in arresting any offender against any of the laws of the state or ordinances of the city.
(Code 1968, § 17-59)
State Law reference— Immunity of persons assisting law enforcement officers, Ga. Code Ann., § 16-3-22.
Sec. 20-9. - Obstructing police officer.
It shall be unlawful for any person to oppose, resist or obstruct any police officer in the discharge of his duty.
(Code 1968, § 17-61)
State Law reference— Obstruction of officers, Ga. Code Ann., § 16-10-24.
Sec. 20-10. - Cursing, abusing officer making arrest.
When the chief of police or any police officer of the city shall make an arrest, it shall be unlawful for the person arrested, or any other person, to curse or abuse such officer performing his official duties.
(Code 1968, § 17-63)
Sec. 20-11. - Interfering with officer while making arrest.
It shall be unlawful for any person to hinder, obstruct or impede any police officer in the carrying or conducting of any prisoner to the station house or city jail, or to menace or threaten such officer while in the discharge of such duty; and no person shall follow said officer while in charge of a prisoner, after being commanded to disperse.
(Code 1968, § 17-62)
State Law reference— Hindering apprehension or punishment of a criminal, Ga. Code Ann., § 16-10-50.
Sec. 20-12. - Oath of office.
The duly appointed police chief of the City of Winder and his/her designee shall hereby be authorized to administer such oaths as prescribed by the Official Code of Georgia, specifically O.C.G.A. section 45-3-1, to any city peace officer.
Any prior oaths administered by the city police chief or the city clerk are hereby ratified by the city council. Said official oaths shall be filed in the records of the Winder Police Department. This ordinance/resolution of the City of Winder shall be effective the date adopted [July 10, 2012].
(Ord. (W-4-12) of 7-10-12)
