ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL[2]

Editor's note— Ord. (W-1-14) adopted Feb. 6, 2014, deleted the former Art. I, §§ 18-1—18-5, and enacted a new Art. I as set out herein. The former Art. I pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Min. of Nov. 8, 1977; and Ord. (10-W-89) adopted Aug. 8, 1989, §§ I—III.

Sec. 18-1. - Equal opportunity.
It shall be the policy of the city to assure fair treatment of all employees and applicants for employment in all aspects of personnel administration without regard to race, color, national origin, sex, religion or age and with regard for their privacy and constitutional rights as citizens. It shall be the responsibility of all department heads to carry out this policy in their respective departments.
(Ord. (W-1-14) of 2-6-14)
Sec. 18-2. - Holidays.
(a)
All full-time permanent employees shall be eligible for nine (9) paid holidays and such holidays shall be designated by city policy. Any additional holidays, shall be approved by city council.
(b)
When a holiday falls on a Sunday, it will be observed on the following Monday; holidays falling on Saturday will be observed the preceding Friday.
(c)
Full-time nonexempt personnel who are required to work on the actual day of the holiday shall be compensated for hours worked in addition to holiday pay specified in accordance with policy. Exempt personnel who are required to work on the actual day of the holiday shall accrue vacation time up to a maximum of eight (8) hours.
(Ord. (W-1-14) of 2-6-14)
Sec. 18-3. - Employee vacation and sick leave.
Vacation and sick leave shall be determined by city policy and approved by the mayor and council prior to any changes being implemented.
(Ord. (W-1-14) of 2-6-14)
Sec. 18-4. - Retirement ordinances not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance relating to retirement or pensions of city officers and employees, and all such ordinances are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
(Ord. (W-1-14) of 2-6-14)
Sec. 18-5. - Drug-free policy.
(a)
Generally. No employee of this city may illegally engage in the manufacture, distribution, dispensation, possession or use of a controlled substance at any time or place, including while at his workplace. Such unlawful activity will be considered a sufficient ground for personnel action, including dismissal from employment.
(b)
Notification. If an employee is arrested for or convicted (including a plea of nolo contendere) of violating any criminal drug statute of any jurisdiction to include driving under the influence, regardless of whether the alleged violation occurred at the workplace or elsewhere, the employee must notify the personnel officer or city administrator in writing of each arrest or conviction as soon as possible but, in no event, no more than five (5) calendar days after the arrest or conviction.
(c)
Violation; action; inquiries. Failure to comply with any part of this policy will result in personnel action, including dismissal from employment.
(Ord. (W-1-14) of 2-6-14)
Sec. 18-6. - Retiree health insurance.
(a)
All full-time employees retiring after January 1, 2013, who are at least sixty (60) years of age with a minimum of ten (10) years of full-time continuous, active service may continue their health care coverage after employment provided such health care coverage is elected and provided by the City of Winder at the time of retirement.
(b)
The city will contribute fifty (50) percent of the calculated premium cost of the retiree single coverage toward the cost of this retiree's health insurance up to the age of sixty-five (65). The retired employee is responsible for all remaining premium cost. Employees retiring prior to March 1, 2011, who are actively being provided spouse or family coverage will maintain this benefit until the retired employee reaches sixty-five (65) years of age or has a lapse in coverage. In the event the spouse of the retired employee reaches sixty-five (65) years of age first, the spouse (only) will be removed from coverage.
(c)
No current terminated vested employee or any employee who will subsequently receive a terminated vested retirement benefit is eligible to receive health insurance benefits from the city.
(Ord. (W-1-14) of 2-6-14)
Sec. 18-7. - Nepotism.
(a)
Relative. The term "relative" means father, mother, brother, sister, children (to include step children or adopted), grandfather, grandmother, grandchildren, or spouse.
(b)
In order to prevent conflicts of interest, the city shall not employ relatives of employees as defined by this section. Nepotism existing prior to the adoption of the section will be exempt; however, an employee may be requested to transfer to another supervisor or department if a conflict is determined by either the city administrator or mayor and council.
(c)
The mayor or city council shall not appoint or hire as an employee to receive any compensation from the city, any person who is a relative of the mayor or members of the council, nor shall the city administrator hire or appoint to any such position any relative of the mayor or any member of the council.
(d)
This section does not apply to non-compensated voluntary activities.
(Ord. (W-1-14) of 2-6-14)
Secs. 18-8—18-10. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CODE OF ETHICS

Sec. 18-11. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the term:
Complaint means a written sworn statement filed with the mayor or mayor pro-tem containing specific allegations of misconduct by a member; provided, however, such allegations must be filed within six (6) months of discovery of the alleged misconduct.
Interest means a pecuniary benefit accruing to a governing authority or any member of his/her family as a direct result of any payment received from the city. A governing authority or member of his/her immediate family shall be deemed to have directly benefited from any payment from the city if any such person shall own at least ten (10) per cent of the entity receiving such payment.
Governing authority or member of the governing authority means the mayor or any council member of the city and any member appointed to any board or commission of the city, currently in office.
Immediate family members are the governing authority member's spouse, children, parents, brothers and sisters.
Person having a complaint or complainant means any resident of the City of Winder, any real property owner in the City of Winder, any holder of a City of Winder occupational tax certificate or City of Winder business registration or license, City of Winder permit holder, or City of Winder utility customer.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-12. - Prohibitions.
No member of the governing authority shall:
(1)
By conduct give reasonable basis for the impression that any person can improperly influence him/her or unduly enjoy his/her favor in the performance of official acts; and or use their positions to induce or coerce a person in a way that provides financial benefits to themselves, their immediate family members or to any for-profit or non-profit organization in which they have an interest.
(2)
Directly or indirectly request, exact, receive, or agree to receive a gift, loan, favor, promise, or thing of value for him/herself or another person if it could reasonably be considered to influence the member in the discharge of official duties;
(3)
Disclose or otherwise use confidential information acquired by virtue of his/her official position for his/her or another person's private gain;
(4)
Use his/her official position to secure or to attempt to secure privileges, economic advantages, exemptions or the like for themselves or others that are not available to the general public, or similarly situated persons, or impede government efficiency or economy.
(5)
Engage in, accept employment with, or render services for any private business or professional activity when such employment or rendering of services is adverse to and incompatible with the proper discharge of official duties;
(6)
Engage in any activity or transaction that is prohibited by law now existing or hereafter enacted which is applicable to him/her by virtue of being a member of the governing authority;
(7)
Use his/her position to request or require a city employee to:
a.
Perform any work outside the employee's normal course of municipal employment;
b.
Purchase goods or services to be used for personal, business, or political purposes; and
c.
Work for the member personally without paying the employee just compensation;
(8)
Use government property of any kind for other than officially approved activities, nor shall he/she direct employees to use such property for any purposes other than those officially approved;
(9)
Use his/her position in any way to coerce, or give the appearance of coercing, another person to provide any financial benefit to him/her or immediate family members or any entity in which the member has an interest.
(10)
No elected official shall intimidate, threaten, coerce, discriminate against, or give the appearance of or attempt to intimidate, threaten, coerce or discriminate against any employee for the purpose of interfering with that person's freedom of choice in the regular discharge of his or her official duties or to cause the employee to violate regulations found in the employee handbook.
(11)
No elected official shall improperly influence any other city elected official to violate the standards of ethical conduct set forth in this code of ethics.
(12)
No elected official shall create a "hostile work environment" towards an employee as defined under federal law.
(13)
Prohibition (2) of this section shall not apply in the case of:
a.
An occasional non-pecuniary gift of insignificant value;
b.
An award publicly presented in recognition of public service;
c.
A commercially reasonable loan or other financial transaction made in the ordinary course of business by an institution or individual authorized by the laws of Georgia to engage in the making of such a loan or financial transaction;
d.
Campaign contributions made and reported in accordance with Georgia law. Prohibition (5) of this section shall not apply to a member of the governing authority who is a licensed professional and appears on behalf of any applicant in such professional capacity so long as disclosures required by section 18-13 are made to the board or commission chairperson thirty (30) days prior to any action being taken, and the member is associated with the project at the time the initial application is filed.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-13. - Disclosure of conflicts of interest.
An appointed member of the governing authority who has an interest that he/she has reason to believe may be affected by his/her official acts or actions or by the official acts or actions of the governing authority shall disclose the precise nature of such interest by written or verbal statement ten (10) days prior to, if possible, but always before the governing authority's taking official action on a matter affecting such interest and abstain from discussion and voting. An elected member of the governing authority shall disclose the nature of any interest he/she has at the time such matter is presented to mayor and council for discussion. Such written or verbal statement shall be recorded into the minutes of the meeting and thus become part of the public record. Following any disclosure made pursuant to this section the member shall refrain from all ex-parte communications with other members regarding the application in which he/she has an interest.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-14. - Disqualification.
A member of the governing authority shall disqualify himself/herself from participating in any official act or action of the city which results in a pecuniary benefit to the member or his/her immediate family member or a business or activity in which he/she has an interest, when such benefit is not available to the public at large.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-15. - Prohibited contracts.
The city shall not enter into any contract involving services or property with a member of the governing authority or his/her immediate family members or with a business or non-profit organization which a member of the governing authority has an interest. Provided, however, this section shall not apply to contracts entered into:
(1)
After sealed competitive bids, together with a disclosure of the nature of the members of the governing authority's interest.
(2)
For emergency situations, provided that the city administrator discloses the contract and explains the emergency at the next regular meeting of the governing authority.
(3)
In accordance with applicable Georgia law, including O.C.G.A. § 16-10-6.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-16. - Complaints.
Any person having a complaint against any member of the governing authority for an alleged ethics violation shall file in writing a detailed, signed, sworn complaint setting forth the particular facts and circumstances which constitute the alleged violation against the member. The complaint shall be filed with the city clerk. Upon receipt of a complaint by the city clerk, the mayor, or in the event the complaint regards the mayor, the mayor pro-tem shall appoint the two (2) members of council, who, along with the city attorney, shall constitute an investigating committee to determine whether the complaint sets forth significant facts and circumstances so as to warrant a hearing before the board of ethics. If the complaint regards the mayor and mayor pro-tem, the council shall elect two (2) members to serve on the investigating committee, provided the members elected are not named in the complaint. In the event the complaint does not set forth sufficient facts to constitute an alleged violation and is found unjustified, frivolous or patently unfounded, it shall be dismissed and the complainant notified immediately. Dismissal by the investigatory committee shall prevent re-filing by the complainant if a complaint arises in the same incident for at least a period of six (6) months; In the event the complaint is found to state sufficient facts to warrant a hearing before the board of ethics, the board shall be appointed as provided herein.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-17. - Board of ethics.
(a)
Composition of the board of ethics: The board of ethics of the city shall be composed of three (3) residents of the city to be appointed by the mayor and council. Each member of the board of ethics shall have been a resident of the city at least one (1) year immediately preceding the date of taking office and shall remain a resident of the city while serving as a member of the board of ethics, and shall be available for a period of two (2) years to be called upon to serve.
(b)
Should the investigating committee determine a complaint warrants a hearing before the board of ethics, the city clerk shall notify the board of ethics immediately. The board shall appoint a chair from among its members.
(c)
The members of the board of ethics shall serve without compensation. The governing authority of the city shall provide meeting space for the board of ethics. Subject to budgetary procedures and requirements of the city, the city shall provide the board of ethics with such supplies, staff, independent legal advice, and equipment as may be reasonably necessary for it to perform its duties and responsibilities.
(d)
The constituted board of ethics shall have the following duties and powers:
(1)
To establish procedures, rules and regulations governing its internal organization and conduct of its affairs;
(2)
To hold a hearing within thirty (30) business days after the receipt of complaint from the investigation committee;
(3)
To prescribe forms, approved by the city attorney, for the disclosure required in this article and to make available to the public information disclosed as provided in this section;
(4)
To receive and hear complaints of violations of the standards required by this article;
(5)
To make such investigation and response to a complaint as it deems necessary to determine whether any person has violated any provisions of this article;
(6)
To hold such hearings and make such inquiries as deemed necessary to investigate and rule upon complaints;
(7)
To report its findings to the governing authority for such action on as the governing authority deems appropriate.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-18. - Service of complaint; hearings and disposition of complaints.
The board of ethics as appointed herein set forth shall cause the complaint to be served on the member of the governing authority charged as soon as practicable. Service may be by personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested. A hearing shall be held within thirty (30) business days after receipt of the complaint from the investigation committee. The board of ethics shall conduct the hearing in accordance with the procedures and regulations it establishes. The ethics board can question parties and witnesses during the hearing; however, the formal rules of evidence and judicial procedure do not apply in ethic board hearings. Upon completion of the hearing, the board of ethics shall either dismiss the complaint or refer the complaint to city council for action as that body deems appropriate if the board finds convincing evidence that a violation has occurred. The decision of the board of ethics shall be rendered to the mayor and council within five (5) business days after completion of the hearing. The decision shall also be rendered to the complainant within five (5) business days.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-19. - Penalty and member rights.
(a)
Any member of the governing authority who knowingly violates any provision of the code of ethics provided in this article shall be subject to public reprimand or censure by the governing authority of the city. This provision is in addition to, and not exclusive of any and all penal and removal powers and procedures as found in the City of Winder Charter and/or Georgia law. No member of the governing authority who the subject of a complaint or who is a complainant shall participate in, vote, or preside in any action to determine his or her guilt nor his or her punishment.
(b)
At any hearing held by the board of ethics, the member of the governing authority who is subject of inquiry shall have the right to written notice of the allegations at least ten (10) business days before a hearing, to be represented by counsel, to hear and examine the evidence and witnesses and to present evidence and witnesses in opposition or in extenuation.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-20. - Appeals.
(a)
Any member of the governing authority or the complainant adversely affected by these findings of the board of ethics may obtain judicial review of such decision as provided in this section.
(b)
An action for judicial review may be commenced by filing an application for a writ of certiorari in the Superior Court of Barrow County within twenty (20) business days after the decision of the board of ethics. Judicial review shall be based upon the record. No party shall be entitled to a de novo appeal. Upon failure to timely request judicial review of the decision by writ of certiorari as provided in this section, the decision shall be final and binding upon all parties.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
Sec. 18-21. - Repeal of conflicting provisions.
All ordinances and resolutions or parts thereof previously adopted by the mayor and council establishing a Code of Ethics for the Governing Authority of the City of Winder in conflict with this article and not preserved hereby, are hereby repealed.
(Ord. (W-4-08) of 5-6-08)
