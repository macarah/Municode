ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Effect on buses.
The limitations of this chapter are not to pertain to a properly constructed bus, which has a seating capacity for more than nine (9) persons. However, where any bus, operated by any person within the city for hire in the transportation of passengers, does not have individual seats for each passenger, not more than three (3) passengers shall be seated on a seat therein, and in the event said seat shall not exceed the width of three (3) feet, not more than one passenger to each one and one-half (1½) feet shall be seated thereon for transportation.
(Code 1968, § 26-1)

ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 30-18. - Definitions.
Words and phrases used in this article shall have the meanings respectively ascribed herein:
Driver: Any person in charge of or operating a cab or taxicab, whether as owner, agent, employee or otherwise.
Highway: Any of the public streets, boulevards, avenues, drives or alleys of the city.
Owner: Any person having control of the operation or maintenance of, and collection of the revenue derived from, taxicabs, livery cars and sight-seeing cars.
Permit: The written authority granted by the mayor and council to engage in the cab or taxicab business in the city.
Taxicab or cab: A motor-driven vehicle of distinctive color or design with a seating capacity of not less than five (5) nor more than seven (7) persons including the driver, operated for hire from a fixed stand in the street or from garages, the destination of which is under the direction of the passenger or passengers transported therein.
Taximeter: Any meter which is an instrument or device attached to a taxicab and measuring mechanically the distance driven and the waiting time upon which the cab fare is based.
Waiting time: The time when a taxicab is not in motion, beginning with the arrival of the cab at the point to which it has been called and the time consumed while standing at the direction of a passenger or passengers.
(Code 1968, § 26-13)
Sec. 30-19. - Scope of article.
Every person engaged in the operation of any vehicle in the city for hire in the transportation of passengers, commonly known as taxicabs, shall be subject to the rules and regulations contained in this article pertaining to their operation upon the public streets.
(Code 1968, § 26-12)
Sec. 30-20. - Violations.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall, at the option of the mayor and council, have his permit or license, or both, revoked and in addition shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Code 1968, § 26-46)
Sec. 30-21. - Owner's license fee; amount, payment required.
All persons engaged in the taxicab business in the city shall pay to the city a license fee for engaging in such business, the amount of such fee to be as prescribed in section 13-31(150) of this Code.
(Code 1968, § 26-19)
Sec. 30-22. - Owner's identification, rate card displayed.
(a)
Every taxicab shall display inside the cab, at a convenient place visible to passengers, a card not less than two (2) inches in width by four (4) inches in length, which shall have printed thereon the name, business address and telephone number of the owner and the rates charged for the use of such vehicle.
(b)
Every such car shall have displayed on each side thereof the name and telephone number of the owner in letters and numerals of such size as to be readily visible to the public, and not less than three (3) inches high.
(Code 1968, § 26-35)
Sec. 30-23. - Taximeters—Required.
All taxicabs operating in the city shall be equipped with taximeters of the type required by this article; provided however, that in case of any unusual demand for taxicabs in the city the mayor and council may in their discretion suspend the taximeter requirements for a limited time and authorize licensed taxicabs to operate without a taximeter within the rates specified by the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 26-36)
Sec. 30-24. - Same—Use.
The use of taximeters on taxicabs shall be subject to the following rules and regulations:
(1)
Taximeters shall be fastened in front of the passengers, visible to them at all times, day and night; and after sundown, the face of the taximeter shall be illuminated. They shall be operated mechanically by a mechanism of standard design and construction, driven either from the transmission or from one of the front wheels by a flexible and permanently attached driving mechanism. They shall be sealed at all points and connections which, if manipulated, would affect their correct reading and recording.
(2)
Taximeters shall be subject to inspection from time to time by the police department. Any inspector or other officer of said department shall be authorized, either with or without complaint of any person, to inspect any meter and, upon discovery of any inaccuracy therein, to notify the person operating said taxicab to cease operation, whereupon such taxicab shall be removed from the highways until the taximeter is repaired and put in satisfactory working condition.
(Code 1968, § 26-37)
Sec. 30-25. - Regulation of rates.
The mayor and council shall have the right at any time to regulate the rates and fares to be charged by taxicabs in the city. Taxicab rates advertised and charged shall be computed only upon a meter basis. Rates and fees shall be filed as part of the business license application and updated annually as part of the license renewal process. Rates shall be posted inside the taxicab in a place visible to passengers.
(Code 1968, § 26-38; Ord. (W-45-06) of 12-5-06)
Sec. 30-26. - Fare—Refusal to pay.
It shall be unlawful for any person to use a motor vehicle operated as a taxicab and refuse to pay therefor the amount accrued on the taximeter.
(Code 1968, § 26-40)
Sec. 30-27. - Same—Receipts.
The driver of any vehicle covered by this article shall at all times, when requested by a passenger, render a receipt for the fare charged, either by a mechanically printed receipt or by a specially prepared receipt on which shall be set out the name of the owner, the license number or motor number, the amount of meter recording or charges and the date of the transaction.
(Code 1968, § 26-39)
Sec. 30-28. - Insurance.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a taxicab in the city unless a certificate or policy of automobile liability and property damage insurance has been issued, covering said taxicab, in the amounts of not less than fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) for injury to one (1) person, one hundred thousand ($100,000.00) for injuries to more than one (1) person in one (1) accident and twenty five thousand ($25,000.00) for property damage. Evidence of such insurance shall be produced by the operator upon request therefore. In the event state minimum insurance requirements exceed these amounts, these amounts will automatically increase to those set by the state.
(Code 1968, § 26-41; Ord. (W-42-06) of 12-5-06)
Charter reference— Power of city to fix the amount of public liability insurance on taxicabs, § 1-3(10).
State Law reference— Motor vehicle insurance, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-9-37.
Sec. 30-29. - Inspection of vehicle—Required.
Each vehicle operating as a taxicab in the city may periodically be inspected by the chief of police, and the chief of police shall have full authority to remove from the streets of the city any vehicle operating as a taxicab, not meeting required safety standards, until the necessary repairs are made and the vehicle is approved by the chief of police.
(Code 1968, § 26-42)
Sec. 30-30. - Same—Certificate.
The chief of police may periodically require the owner or operator of a taxicab to submit a certificate of inspection to the city clerk or his/her designee for the information of the police department and the mayor and council. Said certificate shall be in the form prescribed by the mayor and council, showing the mechanical condition of the vehicle and the serviceability of the body, and the condition of the tires on said vehicle and their serviceability. The prescribed form shall be obtained from the city clerk or his/her designee. A sticker will be issued annually upon registration and presentment of a completed safety standards inspection sheet as part of a new business license application or renewal. The sticker shall be affixed to the vehicle itself or displayed to show it has been registered or inspected.
(Code 1968, § 26-43; Ord. (W-30-06) of 12-5-06)
Sec. 30-31. - Suspension of operation of vehicles—Authority.
The chief of police may suspend from operation any taxicab found to be unsafe, and any vehicle so suspended shall not be operated again until repaired and put in proper condition for use, and then only after its condition has been approved by the chief of police. Likewise, the chief of police may suspend from operation any city driver's license or any taxicab if the taxicab is found in charge of any person other than the driver licensed by the city, and also when found in charge of an intoxicated driver.
(Code 1968, § 26-44)
Sec. 30-32. - Same—Effect.
The chief of police shall report his actions under the preceding section to the mayor and council for consideration, and the license of an intoxicated driver shall be taken up and deposited with the city clerk. It shall be unlawful for such driver to refuse to deliver the license to the chief of police on demand.
(Code 1968, § 26-44)
Sec. 30-33. - Same—Duration of suspension.
Any suspension from operation of a taxicab or license under the second sentence in section 30-31 will be for not more than fifteen (15) days.
Sec. 30-34. - Cruising.
It shall be unlawful for any person operating a taxicab to cruise, for the purpose of soliciting passengers, within the city.
(Code 1968, § 26-45)
Sec. 30-35. - Taxicab zones.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park or cause to be parked any vehicle except a taxicab within those areas where the curbing is marked by lines and where there is a sign denoting the same as a parking space for taxicabs only, such areas being reserved for the use of taxicabs only.
(Code 1968, § 16-115)
Charter reference— Power of city to regulate and rent taxicab parking spaces, § 1-3(10).
Sec. 30-36. - Transporting alcoholic beverages.
Persons operating taxicabs upon the highways of the city are prohibited from transporting alcoholic beverages in a taxicab when no passengers are present or as regulated by state.
(Ord. No. W-37-06, 12-5-06)
Secs. 30-37—30-46. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 30-47. - Required.
No person shall operate a taxicab upon the highways of the city until a permit has been granted after the application therefor has been approved and the fees for the permit have been paid.
(Code 1968, § 26-14)
Sec. 30-48. - Reserved.
Sec. 30-49. - Application—Contents; filing.
An application for a taxicab permit shall be in writing, addressed to the mayor and council and shall be filed with the city clerk or his/her designee. It shall contain the full name and address of the applicant, and if the applicant is a corporation, the names of its officers. It shall also state the business to be engaged in, the location from which the business is to be operated, the type of equipment to be used, the maximum number of passengers each vehicle is manufactured to carry, the number of vehicles to be operated and such other information as may be requested by the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 26-15; Ord. (W-30-06) of 12-5-06)
Charter reference— Power of city to limit the number of vehicles operated for hire, § 1-3(10).
Sec. 30-50. - Same—Investigation; approval.
Upon the filing of an application for a taxicab permit with the city clerk or his/her designee, the mayor and council shall refer the same to the police chief to investigate the applicant. The police chief shall make its report to the mayor and council at its next regular meeting and the mayor and council shall either approve or deny the application. If the application is approved, the city clerk or his/her designee shall issue the permit.
(Code 1968, § 26-16 Ord. (W-30-06) of 12-5-06)
Sec. 30-51. - Driver's license.
No permit shall be issued to any person to operate a taxicab in the city before the person who is to drive said taxicab is approved for driving a motor vehicle by the state department of public safety after an examination given by said department.
(Code 1968, § 26-17)
Charter reference— Power of city to require operators of vehicles for hire to be licensed, § 1-3(10).
State Law reference— Issuance of driver's licenses, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-5-20 et seq.
Sec. 30-52. - Appeal from denial.
Any person applying for a taxicab permit which is denied by the police chief for any cause, may appear before the mayor and council at their next meeting following the denial of said permit or at such time as the mayor and council shall designate, and request that said mayor and council review the application for permit and the reason for refusal. Said mayor and council shall review the application as requested, and, in their discretion, may overrule the police chief and order the permit issued on behalf of the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 26-18; Ord. (W-41-06) of 12-5-06)
Sec. 30-53. - Posted.
A taxicab permit shall be posted at all times in the taxicab vehicle in a conspicuous manner so that it can be easily seen by any passengers transported in said vehicle.
(Code 1968, § 26-21)
Sec. 30-54. - Transfer, encumbrance.
No taxicab permit granted to any person under this division may be sold, assigned, transferred, leased or mortgaged by the holder.
(Code 1968, § 26-22)
Sec. 30-55. - Suspension, revocation—Grounds.
The wilful violation of any ordinance of the city or the laws of the state shall be grounds for the suspension or permanent revocation of a taxicab permit as set out in this division.
(Code 1968, § 26-23)
Sec. 30-56. - Same—Power of mayor.
When the mayor and council are not in session, the mayor shall have the authority and power, in his discretion, upon sufficient evidence or information, to suspend the permit of any taxicab owner or operator for a period of sixty (60) days by a written order, a copy of which shall be served upon said owner or operator of a taxicab within two (2) days after the suspension of his permit, and he shall address a communication in writing to the mayor and council of his action in the premises in suspending such permit and the reason therefor.
(Code 1968, § 26-24)
Sec. 30-57. - Same—Appeal.
The owner or operator of a taxicab shall be entitled to a hearing before the mayor and council upon application therefor, for said body to determine whether or not the suspension of his permit to operate a taxicab shall be made permanent or not within the city; provided such application is filed with the city clerk within five (5) days from the date of suspension by the mayor.
(Code 1968, § 26-25)
Sec. 30-58. - Operation of taxicab during suspension.
It shall be unlawful for an owner or operator or any driver employed by such owner or operator during the period of suspension of a taxicab permit to furnish service under the permit issued by the city, and a violation of this section shall be punishable by the city as provided in section 1-8 of this Code. Such penalty to be in addition to the suspension.
(Code 1968, § 26-26)
Sec. 30-59. - Disposition of permit during suspension.
Any taxicab permit of any owner or operator in the city which has been suspended by the mayor shall be returned to the city clerk, to be retained by said clerk during the period of suspension and until the suspension by the mayor is revoked and relieved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 26-27)
Sec. 30-60. - When revoked.
If the suspension of a taxicab permit is made permanent and the permit is revoked by the mayor and council, the permit shall be canceled by the city clerk.
(Code 1968, § 26-28)
Secs. 30-61—30-71. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - CITY DRIVER'S LICENSE

Sec. 30-72. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive any of the vehicles regulated by the provisions of this article until he first shall have obtained a driver's license therefor, from the mayor and council, as provided in this division.
(Code 1968, § 26-29)
Charter reference— Power of city to require operators of vehicles for hire to be licensed, § 1-3(10).
Sec. 30-73. - Application—Submission.
Before any person shall drive any of the vehicles regulated by this article, he shall make written application to the mayor and council for a taxicab driver's license upon blanks furnished by the city.
(Code 1968, § 26-30)
Sec. 30-74. - Same—Contents.
Every applicant for a taxicab driver's license under this division shall provide the following information on the application therefor:
(1)
The age and home address of the applicant.
(2)
Whether the applicant is of sound physique.
(3)
Whether the applicant has good eyesight.
(4)
Whether the applicant uses intoxicating liquors, drugs or narcotics.
(5)
Whether the applicant has ever been convicted of violating any city, state or federal criminal law, and if so, the number of times, the kinds of offenses and the sentences imposed.
(6)
Any other such information as may be required by the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 26-31)
Sec. 30-75. - Same—Investigation.
The chief of police is hereby charged with the duty of investigating the facts stated in an application for taxicab driver's license before recommending to the mayor and council the granting of such license, and the mayor and council shall not grant such license to any person which is not at least eighteen (18) years of age nor to any person who in the opinion of the police department is not physically, mentally and morally fit to operate a taxicab upon the highways of the city. Background checks will be run on each driver at renewal each year and on each new driver upon application for a taxicab driver's business license.
(Code 1968, § 26-32; Ord. (W-45-06) of 12-5-06)
Sec. 30-76. - Fee.
The charge for the issuance of a taxicab driver's license shall be the amount prescribed in section 13-31(150) per calendar year, and said license shall be valid for the calendar year in which issued and only for that calendar year.
(Code 1968, § 26-33)
Sec. 30-77. - Issuance, display.
If the mayor and council shall grant a taxicab driver's license to an applicant, the city clerk or his/her designee shall issue to the holder thereof a badge or license card of a design, and bearing such words and numerals, as may be fixed by the mayor and council, including suitable space thereon to enable the applicant to attach his photograph of size and design likewise to be fixed by said mayor and council. Such person shall attach his photograph to his license card and shall keep said card posted in view of passengers at all times when operating a taxicab. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate any such vehicle upon the highways unless the driver has posted such license card, therein, in full view as provided in this section.
(Code 1968, § 26-34; Ord. (W-44-06) of 12-5-06)
