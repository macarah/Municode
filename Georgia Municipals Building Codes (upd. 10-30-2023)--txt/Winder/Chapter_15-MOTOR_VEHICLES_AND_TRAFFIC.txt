ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 15-1. - Rules of the road—Adopted.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 40, ch. 6, §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-397 of that chapter known as the uniform rules of the road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as the traffic regulations of the city as if recited herein.
(Code 1968, § 16-53; Ord. ( W-11-15 ) of 11-10-15, § 1)
Sec. 15-2. - Same—Penalties.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this article shall be punished by a fine of not more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) or by imprisonment for not more than one hundred eighty (180) days or by both such fine and imprisonment.
(Ord. ( W-11-15 ) of 11-10-15, § 2)
Sec. 15-3. - Ordinances not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance designating through streets, stop streets or intersections, yield-right-of-way streets or intersections, restricted-parking streets or no-parking streets, and all such ordinances are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
Sec. 15-4. - Applicability of regulations to public employees.
The provisions of this chapter shall apply to the driver of any vehicle owned by or used in the service of the United States, the state, the county or the city, and it shall be unlawful for any such driver to violate any of the provisions of this chapter except as otherwise permitted in this chapter or by state statute.
(Code 1968, § 16-3)
Sec. 15-5. - Obedience to orders or directions.
No person shall willfully fail or refuse to comply with any lawful order or direction of a police officer, member of the fire department, or other city workers given for the purpose of regulating traffic.
(Code 1968, § 16-1; Ord. (W-10-15) of 11-10-15, § I )
Editor's note— Ord. No. W-10-15, § I, adopted Nov. 10, 2015, changed the title of § 15-5 from "Obedience to police, firemen" to read as set out herein.
Cross reference— Fire department, § 9-18 et seq.; police, ch. 20.
State Law reference— Similar provisions, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-2.
Sec. 15-6. - Vehicles to be properly equipped.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a vehicle upon the streets of the city which is not equipped in conformity to and in compliance with applicable provisions of state law.
(Code 1968, § 16-75)
Sec. 15-7. - Enforcement.
It shall be the duty of the city police department to enforce the provisions of this chapter. Officers of the police department are authorized to direct all traffic, either in person or by means of a visible or audible signal, in conformity with the provisions of this chapter; provided that, upon fire or other emergency, or to expedite traffic or to safeguard pedestrians, officers of the police or fire department or any other department of the city may direct traffic, as conditions may require.
(Code 1968, § 16-60; Ord. (W-10-15) of 11-10-15, § II)
Editor's note— Ord. No. W-10-15, § II, adopted Nov. 10, 2015, changed the title of § 15-7 from "Passing vehicle proceeding in same direction" to read as set out herein.
Sec. 15-8. - Passing emergency vehicle.
No person driving a vehicle shall pass any fire truck operating emergency lights or apparatus while same is proceeding to an emergency response, nor shall any such person pass an ambulance while the same is being used to answer an emergency response.
(Code 1968, § 16-65; Ord. (W-10-15) of 11-10-15, § III)
Sec. 15-9. - Crossing fire hose.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride or drive a vehicle over hose or other apparatus in the street while the fire department is practicing, or while the same is being used to combat a fire.
(Code 1968, § 10-23; Ord. (W-10-15) of 11-10-15, § IV)
State Law reference— Crossing fire hose, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-248.

ARTICLE II. - TRAFFIC SIGNS, SIGNALS AND MARKINGS GENERALLY[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Traffic signs, signals and markings, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-20 et seq.; traffic-control devices required, § 40-6-371.

Sec. 15-26. - Effect of provisions requiring traffic signs.
No provision of this chapter for which traffic signs are required shall be enforced against an alleged violator if at the time and place of the alleged violation an official traffic sign is not in proper position and sufficiently legible to be seen by an ordinarily observant person. Whenever a particular section does not state that signs are required, such regulation shall be effective even though no traffic signs are erected or in place.
(Code 1968, § 16-22)
State Law reference— Official traffic-control devices to give notice of local traffic regulations, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Sec. 15-27. - Authority to place, maintain signs, signals, markings, devices.
The chief of police acting as traffic engineer shall place and maintain traffic-control signs, signals, markings and devices when and as required under the traffic ordinances and regulations of the city, to make effective the provisions thereof, and shall place and maintain such additional traffic-control devices as are deemed necessary to regulate traffic under the traffic regulations of the city or under state law, or to guide and warn traffic.
(Code 1968, § 16-21)
Sec. 15-28. - Effect of erection of traffic signal lights.
All provisions of this chapter providing for vehicles coming to a full stop before entering the intersections of stop streets with through streets or thoroughfares shall be modified to conform to the operation of traffic signal lights, whenever the same are established by proper ordinance or resolution.
(Code 1968, § 16-23)
Sec. 15-29. - One-way street signs—Required.
Whenever an ordinance of the city designates an alley or street as restricted to travel in one direction only, the chief of police as traffic engineer shall place and maintain signs giving notice thereof, and no such regulation shall be effective unless such signs are in place.
(Code 1968, § 16-28)
State Law reference— Authority of city to designate one-way streets, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Sec. 15-30. - Same—Location.
Signs indicating the direction of lawful traffic movement shall be placed at every intersection where movement of traffic in the opposite direction is prohibited.
(Code 1968, § 16-28)
State Law reference— Signs required on one-way streets, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Sec. 15-31. - Designation of through streets and thoroughfares.
Whenever an ordinance of the city designates and describes a through street or thoroughfare, it shall be the duty of the chief of police as traffic engineer to place and maintain stop sign or a yield-right-of-way sign on each and every street intersecting such through street or thoroughfare, or intersecting that portion thereof described and designated as a through street or thoroughfare by such ordinance of the city, and no such ordinance shall be effective unless such signs are in place.
(Code 1968, § 16-29)
State Law reference— Authority of city to designate through highways, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Sec. 15-32. - Designation of "stop" or "yield" intersections.
Whenever any ordinance or regulation of the city designates intersections as "stop" intersections or "yield-right-of-way" intersections, the chief of police as traffic engineer shall erect stop or yield signs at every intersection so designated, and no such ordinance or regulation shall be effective unless such signs are in place.
(Code 1968, § 16-30)
State Law reference— Authority of city to designate stop intersections, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Sec. 15-33. - Designation of hazardous intersections.
Whenever an ordinance or regulation of the city designates intersections as having particular hazards, upon other than through streets, the chief of police acting as traffic engineer shall determine whether vehicles shall stop at one or more entrances to any such intersection, and shall erect a stop sign at every such place where a stop is required.
(Code 1968, § 16-31)
Sec. 15-34. - Turning markers, buttons, signs.
The chief of police as traffic engineer shall place markers, buttons or signs within or approaching intersections indicating the course to be traveled by vehicles turning at such intersections, and such course to be traveled as so indicated shall conform to that prescribed by law or ordinance.
(Code 1968, § 16-33)
State Law reference— Authority of city to regulate the turning of vehicles, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Sec. 15-35. - Designation of crosswalks, safety zones, traffic lanes.
The chief of police acting as traffic engineer is hereby authorized:
(1)
To designate and maintain, by appropriate devices, marks or lines upon the surface of the roadway, crosswalks at intersections where in his opinion there is particular danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway, and at such other places as he may deem necessary;
(2)
To establish safety zones of such kind and character and at such places as he may deem necessary for the protection of pedestrians;
(3)
To mark traffic lanes on street pavements at such places as he may deem advisable for the expeditious movement of traffic, consistent with the traffic regulations of the city.
(Code 1968, § 16-35)
Sec. 15-36. - Play streets—Placement of signs.
Whenever an ordinance or regulation of the city declares any street or part thereof to be a play street, the chief of police acting as traffic engineer shall place appropriate signs or devices in the roadway indicating and helping to protect the same.
(Code 1968, § 16-36)
State Law reference— Authority of city to designate play streets, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Sec. 15-37. - Same—Duty of driver.
Whenever authorized signs are erected indicating that any street or part thereof is a play street, no person shall drive a vehicle upon any such street or portion thereof, except drivers of vehicles having business or whose residences are within such closed area, and then any such driver shall exercise the greatest care in driving upon any such street or portion thereof.
(Code 1968, § 16-36)
State Law reference— Authority of city to regulate traffic on play streets, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-371.
Secs. 15-38—15-53. - Reserved.

State Law reference— Right-of-way, Ga. Code Ann., § 40-6-70 et seq.

Sec. 15-54. - Duty to stop or yield upon entering through street.
Any person operating a vehicle from any intersecting street into or onto that portion of a street designated by the mayor and council as a through street shall, before driving such vehicle into or onto said street bring his vehicle to a full and complete stop, or yield the right-of-way, as directed. It shall be unlawful for any person to drive any vehicle from an intersecting street into that portion of a street designated as a through street without bringing his vehicle to a full stop or yielding the right-of-way, as directed, before driving the same from said intersecting street onto or into said through street.
(Code 1968, § 16-70)
Sec. 15-55. - Cars to stop at crossings.
All automobiles and other moving vehicles shall come to a complete stop when a train is signaling its approach, before crossing any railroad track within the city.
(Code 1968, § 20-4)
Cross reference— Railroads, ch. 21.

DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 15-85. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to promote, organize or hold, or to assist in promoting, organizing or holding, or to take part or participate, in any parade or procession or other public demonstration in the streets or other public ways of the city, unless a permit therefor has been secured from the city council.
(Code 1968, § 21-19)
Sec. 15-86. - Application.
To secure the permit required in this division, a written application shall be made to the city council, setting forth the hour and date; the probable number of persons, vehicles and animals which will be engaged in such parade, procession or other public demonstration; the purpose for which it is to be held; and the streets or other public ways over, along or in which it is desired to have or hold such parade, procession or public demonstration.
(Code 1968, § 21-20)
Sec. 15-87. - State permit.
Prior to the issuance of a permit under this division by the city, the applicant for such permit shall obtain any state permit required by law or by the regulations of the state department of transportation.
State Law reference— Control of mass gatherings, Ga. Code Ann., § 31-27-1 et seq.
Sec. 15-88. - Issuance.
The city council shall grant a written permit for a parade, procession or demonstration, as provided in this article, prescribing the streets or other public ways which may be used therefor, unless in its judgment the public welfare, peace, safety, health, decency, good order, morals or convenience require that such permit be refused.
(Code 1968, § 21-21)
Charter reference— Power of city to protect the general welfare, § 1-3(33), (37).
Sec. 15-89. - Time limitations.
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a parade, procession or demonstration at any time other than the time specified in the permit therefor.
(Code 1968, § 21-22)
Sec. 15-90. - Deviation from prescribed streets, ways.
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a parade, procession or demonstration along, upon or over any street or public way other than those set out in the permit issued as provided in this article.
(Code 1968, 21-23)
Sec. 15-91. - Time between issuance of permits.
At least one hundred eighty (180) days shall elapse between the issuance of successive permits to the same person, corporation or group.
(Ord. (2-W-87) of 3-3-87; Ord. (9-W-91) of 10-2-90)
Secs. 15-92—15-106. - Reserved.

ARTICLE IX. - ABANDONED AND JUNKED VEHICLES

Sec. 15-185. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the following terms and words shall have the indicated meanings:
Abandoned vehicle is any vehicle left unattended on public property for seventy-two (72) hours or more.
Junked motor vehicle is any motor vehicle which does not have lawfully affixed thereto both an unexpired license plate with current decal, and the condition of which is wrecked, dismantled, partially dismantled, inoperative, abandoned, or discarded.
Person shall mean any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company, or organization of any kind.
Private property shall mean any real property within the city which is privately owned and which is not public property as defined in this section.
Public property shall mean any street or highway which shall include the entire width between the boundary lines of every way publicly maintained for the purposes of vehicular travel, and shall also mean any other publicly owned property or facility including property owned or managed by the Winder Housing Authority, Barrow County, or Barrow County Board of Education.
Police department shall mean the chief of police and/or his lawfully appointed designee.
Vehicle shall include any device which moves on wheels or on tracks whether its own power or otherwise.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-186. - Storing, parking, or leaving dismantled or other such motor vehicle prohibited; exceptions.
No person shall abandon or permit the abandoning of any vehicle or parts of any vehicle of any kind upon any public property within the city for a period of time in excess of seventy-two (72) consecutive hours. No person shall allow a junked motor vehicle to remain on private property owned or leased by such person for more than a period of thirty (30) days. This section shall not apply to any vehicle or parts enclosed within a building on private property or to any vehicle or parts held in connection with a business enterprise, lawfully licensed by the city and properly operated in the appropriate business zone, pursuant to the zoning laws of the city, or to any motor vehicle in operable condition specifically adopted or designed for operation on drag strips or raceways, or any vehicle retained by the owner for antique collection purposes bearing a valid Georgia antique license.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91; Ord. (W-5-96) of 5-7-96)
Sec. 15-187. - Notice to remove.
Whenever it comes to the attention of the police department that any vehicle is located on private property in violation of this chapter in the city, a notice in writing shall be served upon the owner of the vehicle, if known, and upon the occupant of the land where the vehicle is located, or in case there is no such occupant, then upon the owner of the land or an agent thereof notifying them of the violation and requesting its removal in the time specified in this chapter.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-188. - Responsibility for removal.
Upon proper notice and opportunity to be heard, the owner of the abandoned or junked vehicle and the owner or occupant of the private property on which the same is located, either or all of them, shall be responsible for its removal. In the event of removal and disposition by the city, the owner of the vehicle and occupant and owner of the private property where same is located, shall be liable for the expenses incurred. Expenses must be paid before the owner of the vehicle may reclaim the vehicle.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-189. - Notice of procedure.
The police department of the city shall give notice of removal to the owner or occupant of the private property where it is located at least five (5) days before compliance is required. It shall constitute sufficient notice, when a copy of same is posted in a conspicuous place upon the private property on which the vehicle is located and upon the vehicle and duplicate copies are sent by certified mail to the owner or occupant of the private property and the owner of the vehicle or personal service may be made upon the owner or occupant of the land and the owner of the vehicle at his last known address.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-190. - Content of notice.
The notice shall contain the request for removal within the time specified in this chapter, and the notice shall advise that upon failure to comply with the notice to remove, the police chief or a designee thereof shall undertake said removal with the cost of removal to be levied against the owner or occupant of the property and the owner of the vehicle.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-191. - Removal of motor vehicles from property.
If the violation described in the notice has not been remedied within a five-day period for compliance, and a notice requesting a hearing is not timely filed, the police department shall have the right to take possession of the junked or abandoned vehicle and remove it from the premises. It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with, hinder, or refuse to allow such person or persons to enter upon private property for the purpose of removing a vehicle under the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-192. - Request for hearing.
The persons to whom the notice is directed or their duly authorized agents, may file a written request for a hearing before the municipal court of the city within the specified period for compliance prescribed in this chapter. If it is determined in the municipal court that this chapter has been violated, then the person responsible for the vehicle shall be punished by a fine not to exceed five hundred dollars ($500.00) per day, imprisonment not to exceed thirty (30) days, or ordered to remove the vehicle, or any combination thereof. Further, each day after the expiration of the notice shall be considered a separate offense.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-193. - Removal of motor vehicles from public property.
The police department shall have the right to take possession of vehicles which are in violation of this chapter from public property and streets which are a threat to the safety and welfare of the public or private property, blocking access to fire hydrants, driveways, and the like, without prior notice.
(Ord. (W-7-9) of 7-9-91; Ord. (W-5-96) of 5-7-96)
Sec. 15-194. - Notice of removal.
Within forty-eight (48) hours of the removal of a vehicle under this chapter, the police department shall given notice to the registered owner of the vehicle, if known, and also to the owner or occupant of the private property from which the vehicle was removed, that the vehicle, or vehicles has been impounded and stored for violation of this chapter. The notice shall give the location of where the vehicle, or vehicles, is stored, and the costs incurred by the city for removal. Service shall be in the same manner as sections 15-188—15-189.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-195. - Right to contract with independent contractors.
The city has the right to contract with independent contractors for towing, storage, and for sale of unclaimed vehicles.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-196. - Public sale.
The time and place of public sale for unclaimed vehicles shall be set and disposal provided in the same manner as the disposal of city property.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-197. - Redemption of impounded vehicles.
The owner of any vehicle seized under the provisions of this chapter may redeem the vehicle at any time after its removal but prior to the sale or destruction thereof upon proof of ownership and payment to the contractor of such sum and fee is determined by the terms of a contract and put to the city of all fines, costs, or fees assessed.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91)
Sec. 15-198. - Liability of owner or occupant.
Upon the failure of the owner or occupant of property on which abandoned vehicles have been removed by the city to pay the unrecovered expenses incurred by the city in the removal, a fi fa shall be issued by the city clerk for the amount of the expenses, less the amount obtained in the sale of the vehicles, and the fi fa shall be recorded upon the general execution docket of Barrow County.
(Ord. (W-7-91) of 7-9-91; Ord. (W-5-96) of 5-7-96)
