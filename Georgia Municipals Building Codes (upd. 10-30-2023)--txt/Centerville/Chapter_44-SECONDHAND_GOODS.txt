ARTICLE II. - PAWNBROKERS

Sec. 44-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Pawnbroker means any person engaged in whole or in part in the business of lending money on the security of pledged goods, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property on the condition that it may be redeemed or repurchased by the seller for a fixed price within a fixed period of time, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property from persons or sources other than manufacturers or licensed dealers as a part of or in conjunction with the business activities described in O.C.G.A. §§ 44-12-130 et seq., which sections are incorporated herein by reference thereto.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(a); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-20. - Activities constituting pawn business.
Any person who purchases jewelry, guns, pistols, firearms, weapons, rifles, household effects or other items of personal property and at the time thereof gives the seller the privilege of redeeming the property thereafter shall be deemed and held to be a pawnbroker within the intent and meaning of this article.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(b); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-21. - License required; fee; occupation taxes.
No person shall operate a pawnbroker business in the city unless he obtains a business in the manner provided in chapter 12 and pays the administrative and license fee provided in the city fee schedule in chapter 22. Pawnbrokers shall be subject to city occupation taxes in the same manner as all other businesses operating in the city.
(Code 1992, § 6-3; Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-22. - Qualifications for licensure.
(a)
All applicants for a pawnbroker's license must be of good character, and all managers, clerks or other employees shall be of like character or the application will not be considered. Prior to obtaining a license a certificate must be obtained from the chief of police that the applicant and his agents have no record of a criminal offense (minor traffic offense excluded) within the previous seven years.
(b)
No applicant for a license or an agent therefor who has a criminal record (except minor traffic offenses) shall be granted a license. The plea of nolo contendere and the forfeiture of bond or collateral shall be considered a conviction for the purposes of this section. Additionally, no applicant who has been convicted of violating any ordinance of the city or any law or regulation of the state or any political subdivision thereof, governing the sale of any alcoholic beverage shall be granted a license.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(c), (d); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-23. - Factors considered in determining good moral character.
Ascertainable standards of good moral character of applicant which may be considered by the city council in deciding if a license will be issued include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
Present and prior compliance with city ordinances.
(2)
Prior supervision over employees and customers.
(3)
Truthfulness in submitted application.
(4)
Criminal and civil court records.
(5)
Employment history.
(6)
Credit reports.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(f); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-24. - Renewal.
Except as provided in section 44-25, all pawnbroker licenses in this section may be renewed by payment of the required fees provided:
(1)
Applications for renewal of existing licenses are made in person by the applicants at the office of the city clerk. Such license fee shall be payable January 1 of each year and shall be delinquent if not paid by April 1 of each year;
(2)
The licensee and location are the same;
(3)
The licensee's circumstances would enable him to qualify for a license if the renewal was a new application; and
(4)
The licensee is still in compliance with the requirements of state and municipal laws and regulations pertaining to the original issuance of the license and pertaining to the operation of the business under the license at the time of application for renewal.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(g)(1)—(4); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-25. - Effect of complaint on renewal; complaint procedure.
If a complaint is received as to the licensee's alleged violation of state or municipal law or regulation before December 31 of the licensed year or 30 days after the application for renewal is received whichever date is later, the license shall not be renewed, but shall be referred to the city council for decision as to whether renewal shall be granted as follows:
(1)
The city council shall conduct a hearing after giving the licensee reasonable notice of the time and place of such hearing, as well as the purpose thereof.
(2)
The licensee may remain open for business until a decision by the city council is made and shall have a period of three days following that decision in which to pay the required fees if the decision of city council is to permit renewal of the license.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(g)(5); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-26. - Inspections.
Each pawnbroker shall hold and maintain his business subject to visitation and inspection by an authorized law enforcement officer.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(i); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-27. - Effect of refusal to permit inspection.
(a)
If any pawnbroker and any clerk, agent or employee of such pawnbroker shall refuse to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer who is certified by the state peace officer standards and training council or who is a federal officer to inspect his permanent record book or any goods in his possession during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time shall be subject to immediate closure by the governing authority of the city.
(b)
Upon receipt by the mayor or mayor pro tem of notice by an authorized inspection officer that a reasonable inspection has been refused by the pawnbroker or his agent, the license of the pawnbroker shall be suspended.
(c)
The pawnbroker shall have ten days after suspension notice by the chief of police or his designee to request a hearing before the governing authority of the city to show cause why the license should not be suspended. If application for hearing to show cause is filed with the clerk of the city within ten days from the notice of suspension the license shall not be suspended until after the hearing.
(d)
If the application is not filed with the clerk of the city within ten days from the notice of suspension, the suspension shall begin ten days from the date of the notice of suspension.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(i); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)
Sec. 44-28. - Duty to submit report to police department.
Each person holding a pawnbroker license in the city or conducting a pawnbroking business in the city, shall make a report to the chief of police of the city before 10:00 a.m., Monday through Saturday of every article or thing whether such purchase be conditional or with the privilege of redeeming on the part of the seller or any other pawnbroker type transaction which occurred on the previous day and such report shall specify the article or thing involved in the transaction giving a full description thereof, such as a number, mark and other details as will make identification of such article or thing clear or positive. Monday's report shall include Saturday and Sunday.
(Code 1992, § 6-3(h); Ord. No. 94-20, 12-27-1994; Ord. No. 95-3, 4-4-1995)

ARTICLE III. - JUNK DEALERS AND JUNKYARDS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Junk dealers, O.C.G.A. § 43-22-1.

Sec. 44-60. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Auto wrecking establishment means junkyard.
Automobile body shop means automotive repair garage.
Automotive repair garage means a business establishment involving maintenance, repair, conversion and other such services on cars, trucks, boats, motorcycles, recreational vehicles, and mobile/manufactured homes, typically including vehicle storage and incidental sales of parts, also referred to as automotive services.
Business premises or premises means the area of a junkyard as described in a junk dealer's license or application for license, as provided for in this article.
Junk means old iron, steel, brass, copper, tin, lead, or other base metals; old cordage, ropes, rags, fibers, or fabrics; old rubber; old bottles or other glass; bones; wastepaper and other waste or discarded material which might be prepared to be used again in some form; and motor vehicles, no longer used as such, to be used for scrap metal or stripping of parts. The term "junk" does not include materials or objects accumulated by a person as byproducts, waste, or scraps from the operation of his own business, or materials or objects held and used by a manufacturer as an integral part of his own manufacturing processes.
Junk dealer means a person who operates a junkyard, as defined in this section, within the city.
Junkyard means a yard, lot, or place, covered or uncovered, outdoors or in an enclosed building, containing junk as defined in this section, upon which occurs one or more acts of buying, keeping, dismantling, processing, selling, or offering for sale any such junk, in whole units or by pans, for a business or commercial purpose, whether or not the proceeds from such acts are to be used for charity.
Sec. 44-61. - License; fee; occupation tax.
No person shall operate as a junk dealer or junkyard in the city unless he obtains a business in the manner provided in chapter 12 and pays the administrative and license fee provided in the city fee schedule in chapter 22. Junk dealers and junkyards shall be subject to city occupation taxes in the same manner as all other businesses operating in the city.
Sec. 44-62. - Regulations.
The following general operating requirements shall apply to all junk dealers licensed in accordance with the provisions of this article:
(1)
The junkyard, together with things kept therein, shall at all times be maintained in a sanitary condition.
(2)
No space not covered by the license shall be used in the licensed business.
(3)
No water shall be allowed to stand in any place on the premises in such manner as to afford a breeding place for mosquitoes.
(4)
No weeds shall be permitted to attain a height of more than four inches.
(5)
No garbage or other waste liable to give off a foul odor or attract vermin shall be kept on the premises; nor shall any refuse of any kind be kept on the premises, unless such refuse is junk as defined herein and is in use in the licensed business.
(6)
No junk shall be allowed to rest upon or protrude over any public street, walkway, or curb, or become scattered or blown off the business premises.
(7)
Junk shall be stored in piles not exceeding ten feet in height and shall be arranged so as to permit easy access to all such junk for firefighting purposes.
(8)
No combustible material of any kind not necessary or beneficial to the licensed business shall be kept on the premises; nor shall the premises be allowed to become a fire hazard.
(9)
Gasoline and oil shall be removed from any scrapped engines or vehicles on the premises.
(10)
No junk or other material shall be burned on the premises in any incinerator not meeting the requirements of the building code; and no junk or other material shall be burned on the premises in the open except in accordance with the provisions of this Code.
(11)
No noisy processing of junk or other noisy activity shall be carried on in connection with the licensed business or any Sunday, Christmas, Thanksgiving, or at any time between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
(12)
The area on the premises where junk is kept (other than indoors) shall be enclosed, except for entrances and exits, with a solid, vertical wall or fence of a minimum height of eight feet measured from ground level. The fence or wall shall not contain any poster or advertising of any kind as may be permitted under chapter 46, pertaining to signs.
(13)
Entrances and exits shall not be wider or more numerous than reasonably necessary for the conduct of the licensed business.
(14)
No junkyard shall be allowed to become a nuisance nor shall any junkyard be operated in such manner as to become injurious to the health, safety, or welfare of the community or of any residents close by.
Sec. 44-63. - Recordkeeping.
Each acquisition of junk shall be recorded in a permanent type register kept on the business premises, giving the name and residence address of the person from whom the acquisition was made, a description of the junk acquired, and the date of the transaction. Such data shall be held available for inspection by any law enforcement officer.
Sec. 44-64. - Minors.
No junk dealer shall have any business dealings as a junk dealer with a minor, nor shall a junk dealer's license be issued to a minor, nor shall a junk dealer employ a minor to assist him in his business.
Sec. 44-65. - Stolen goods.
Every junk dealer who shall receive or be in possession of any goods, articles, or things of value which may have been lost or stolen shall upon demand produce such article or thing to any member of the police department for examination.
Sec. 44-66. - Vehicles.
Every vehicle used by a junk dealer in the conduct of his business shall bear thereon in legible characters the name and address of the owner and proprietor thereof.

ARTICLE IV. - PRECIOUS METALS AND GEM DEALERS

Sec. 44-70. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Precious gems means any precious or semi-precious stone, including but not limited to, diamond, ruby, sapphire or emerald which is cut and polished.
Precious metals means gold, silver or platinum or any alleged alloy containing gold, silver or platinum.
Precious metals and gems dealer means any person, partnership, sole proprietorship, corporation, association or other entity engaged in, either exclusively or incidentally to or in connection with other business, the business of purchasing, selling, bartering or acquiring in trade any precious metals or gems from persons or sources other than manufacturers or licensed dealers.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-71. - License application; exemption.
(a)
No person shall engage in business as a precious metals dealer in the city except under a license granted by the city.
(b)
All applications shall be made to the city clerk and shall be accompanied by an application fee of $150.00. All applications shall be in writing upon a form supplied to by the city clerk.
(c)
The application for the license shall state the name and address of the applicant, the street and number at which the applicant proposes to operate such business and the names and addresses of all persons having a financial interest in such business. Every person having an ownership interest in the business carried on by a precious metals dealer and all employees engaged in said business shall be at least 18 years of age. The application shall contain an accurate description of the premises upon which the business is to be conducted, along with the zoning classification therefor and such other information as may be required.
(d)
The application for license shall list all convictions, guilty pleas or pleas of nolo contender by or against the applicant(s) for a period of five years preceding the date of such application for such violation of any felony of any state or of the United States, together with a waiver sufficient to authorize investigation of the criminal record of the applicant, if any. All applicants shall swear and affirm that the information disclosed in the application is true and correct.
(e)
Any person who holds a valid pawnbroker license pursuant to article II of this chapter shall be exempt from the license requirements of this article, but shall comply with the requirements of sections 44-74 through 44-79 of this article if said license holder meets the definition of a "precious metals and gems dealer" as defined herein.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-72. - License, issuance.
(a)
No license shall be granted pursuant to this article to any applicant who has been convicted, pled guilty or pled nolo contender to any felony as described in subsection 44-71(d) above. Moreover, no license shall be issued to any applicant who is not a citizen of the United States or an alien lawfully admitted to be a permanent resident of the United States.
(b)
No license shall be granted to any person until all requirements of this chapter have been met and all amounts associated with the issuance of this license have been paid. No license shall be granted if the applicant has any outstanding or delinquent amounts for taxes, licenses, past due utilities or fees of any kind due the city.
(c)
All applications for license shall be granted or denied within 60 days of a completed application. Any application not granted upon by the city clerk's office within said period shall be deemed denied.
(d)
Any precious metals or gems dealer already engaging in such business prior to the adoption of this article shall file an application, in accordance with the terms of this article by November 1, 2014. If the mayor and council determine that any such application filed pursuant to this subsection should be denied, the applicant may continue to operate his/her business for a period of 30 days following the date on which the application is denied; provided, however, that at all times during which such applicant continues to operate his/her business he/she shall be subject to and comply with all the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-73. - Revocation; suspension.
Any person failing to comply with the provisions of this article or such other ordinances and regulations as may be adopted by the governing authority for the conduct of the business of a precious metals and/or gems dealer shall be subject to having the license to conduct such business revoked or suspended for a definite period of time at the discretion of the governing authority, after proper notice and hearing. Such forfeiture and suspension shall result from conviction or violation of any provision of this article or any other ordinance or regulation governing the conduct of the business for which such license shall have been issued. The governing authority may also consider as grounds for revocation or suspension a failure of the license application to contain any record of convictions required by section 44-71 of this article.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-74. - Record of transactions; inspection by police.
(a)
All precious metals and gems dealers shall keep books, in permanent form, wherein shall be entered, in English and in legible form, an accurate, detailed description of all precious metals and gems sold to them. Such description shall include:
(1)
The name of the maker of the article;
(2)
Any identifying mark or number and a description of the kind of material of which it is made so that such article can be reasonably and definitely identified;
(3)
A photograph of the item, made at the time and place of purchase, (which photograph shall be attached to the page whereon the corresponding entry is made);
(4)
The name, address and age of the person by whom such article was deposited or sold as verified by a valid, government-issued picture identification of the seller;
(5)
The date and hour the transaction was made;
(6)
A photograph or copy of the identification of the seller, made at the time and place of purchase, (which photograph shall be attached to the page whereon the corresponding entry is made); and
(7)
The signature of the seller.
(b)
The permanent record book shall be maintained for each purchase of precious metals and/or gems for a period of three years from the date of purchase of the last entry in said record book. Entries shall appear chronologically and shall be signed, in ink, by the purchaser at the end of each complete entry. No blank lines may be left between the entries. No obliterations, alterations or erasures may be made. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying its legibility. Such book and the articles pledged or sold shall at all times be subject to inspection and examination by the police. Upon refusal of the proprietors at any time to submit their shops to a thorough inspection by the police, the shops shall be immediately closed and the permit and license revoked.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-75. - Holding period for goods.
Any precious metals and gems dealer or person operating under a precious metals and gems dealers license who takes or buys precious metals or gems, taking full title thereto, shall hold unaltered, such goods so taken or purchased for at least ten days before disposing of the same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-76. - Reports of transactions.
Every precious metals and gems dealers shall complete a weekly report, in such form as may be prescribed by the chief of police, of all precious metals or gems taken or bought by him during the preceding seven-day (Monday—Sunday) period. Such report shall show the name and address of the precious metals and gems dealer; the time of each transaction; the serial number, if any, on the item; the amount paid, pledged, or advanced; a full description of the article, including the kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry and all identifying names, marks and numbers; along with a photograph of each article; and a description of the person selling, including the name, address, race, sex, weight and height and such other information as may be required by the chief of police. The form required by this section shall be hand delivered or transmitted electronically, along with the required photographs, to the Centerville Police Department each Monday morning by 10:00 a.m.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-77. - Purchases from minors.
It shall be unlawful for any precious metals and gems dealer, or his or her agents or employees to receive or purchase any precious metals or gems of any character or description from any person who is less than 18 years of age. Any precious metals or gems dealer or his or her agents or employees shall have the burden of requiring identification sufficient to show that any person making a sale is not less than 18 years of age.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-78. - Hours of operation.
The hours within which precious metals or gems dealers may remain open shall be from 9:00 a.m. until 9:00 p.m.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Sec. 44-79. - Violations and penalties.
The performance of any act prohibited by any section or provision of this article, or the failure to perform any act required thereby, shall constitute a violation and an offense hereunder and any person found guilty in the city court of a violation of any section or provision of this article shall be punished as provided in section 22-1 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2014-3, 8-5-2014)
Secs. 44-80—44-100. - Reserved.
