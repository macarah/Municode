DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-114. - Adoption of personnel policies.
(a)
The city council has adopted the Centerville Personnel Policies Manual that provides rules, regulations, and policies consistent with the City's Charter concerning:
(1)
The method of employee selection and probationary periods of employment;
(2)
The methods of promotion and transfer;
(3)
The hours of work, vacation, sick leave and other leaves of absence, overtime pay, and rules governing layoffs or similar reductions in force;
(4)
Such dismissal hearings as due process may require; and
(5)
Such other personnel policies, regulations, provisions, or notices as were and may be necessary to provide for the adequate and systematic handling of personnel matters.
(b)
A copy of the manual is available for inspection in the office of the city clerk.
Sec. 2-115. - Defense against legal action.
(a)
The city will undertake to defend all civil, criminal or quasi-criminal actions brought or maintained against officers, agents or employees, elected or appointed, of the city arising out of the performance of their duties or in any way connected therewith, whether based upon negligence, violation of contract rights, or violation of civil, constitutional, common law or statutory rights provided no defense shall be furnished any person charged with a criminal offense involving theft, embezzlement or other like crime with respect to the property or money of or in which the city has an interest.
(b)
Any claim or civil judgment rendered against any person whose defense the city undertakes and is authorized to undertake under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 45-9-21, shall be the responsibility of and paid by the city.
(c)
Because there are certain activities which are patently unauthorized the city may by special resolution decline to furnish a defense to officers, agents or employees wherein the discretion of the council existing at that time notice or notification of suit is made, the city determines that under the circumstances it should not provide a defense for such person.
(Code 1992, § 2-46; Ord. of 7-28-1980, §§ 1—3)
State Law reference— Authorization of purchase by municipal corporations of insurance covering injuries resulting from ownership of motor vehicles, waiver of governmental immunity, limitation of liabilities, O.C.G.A. § 33-24-51; immunity of municipality from liability for damages; waiver of immunity by purchase of liability insurance, O.C.G.A. § 36-33-1; personal liability of councilmembers and other municipal officers, O.C.G.A. § 36-33-4.
Sec. 2-116. - Old-age and survivors insurance.
(a)
It is hereby declared to be the policy and purpose of the city to extend at the earliest date possible under the agreement entered into by the state and the Federal Social Security Administration, to the employees and officials thereof, and its instrumentalities, not excluded by law or by this Code, the benefits of the system of old age and survivors insurance as authorized by the Federal Social Security Act, the state enabling act, and amendments thereto. In pursuance of this policy, and for that purpose, the city shall take such action as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(b)
The retirement plan for the employees of the city setting forth the joint trust agreement and the contract for the administration of the plan between the city and the state municipal employees benefit system is compiled and published separately by the city. An official copy is on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1992, § 2-47; Ord. of 11-1964, § 1)

DIVISION 2. - MAYOR

Sec. 2-146. - Duties.
The mayor shall have the following duties:
(1)
Preside at council meetings. The mayor shall preside at all meetings of the city council;
(2)
Executing legal documents. To sign and execute all contracts, deeds, and bonds of the city duly authorized by the city council; and
(3)
Other duties. To perform all duties assigned to the mayor in the city Charter or as may be assigned from time to time by the city council.
Sec. 2-147. - Mayor pro tempore.
The mayor pro tempore shall act in the stead of the mayor during a temporary absence or disability of the mayor. The position of mayor pro tempore shall be rotated among councilmembers on an annual basis at the first annual meeting in each year.

Sec. 2-179. - Director of operations.
(a)
The director of operations shall direct and supervise the administration of the streets, utilities, stormwater, sanitation and building code enforcement departments, offices and agencies of the city, except as otherwise provided by this Code or by law;
(b)
The director of operations shall attend all city council meetings and shall have the right to take part in discussion but may not vote.
(c)
The director of operations shall make such reports as the city council may require concerning the operation of city departments, offices and agencies subject to the director of operation's direction and supervision.
(d)
The director of operations shall perform other such duties as are specified by the city council.

State Law reference— Training for municipal clerks, O.C.G.A. § 36-45-20.

Sec. 2-199. - Appointment.
The city council shall appoint a city clerk who shall not be a councilmember. The city clerk shall be custodian of the official city seal, and maintain city council records required by this Code, the city Charter, or state law.
Sec. 2-200. - Duties.
In addition to such duties as may be from time to time assigned by the city council, it shall be the duty of the city clerk to do the following:
(1)
Keep and preserve the official city seal and all city records;
(2)
Attend meetings of the city council and keep the official minutes of its proceedings, including the names of members and other parties present and absent, the vote of each councilmember on each resolution, ordinance, motion, or other official action considered;
(3)
Prepare and certify copies of official records when requested, for which fees may be prescribed by ordinance;
(4)
Maintain a book or record of registration of franchises granted by the city pursuant to the city Charter; and
(5)
Perform such other duties as may be required by the city council.

DIVISION 5. - CITY ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-224. - Appointment; qualifications; oath; compensation.
The city attorney shall be appointed by and shall report directly to the city council, by and with the advice and consent of the city council, and shall serve until a successor is appointed and has qualified. No person shall be so appointed unless he is a member in good standing of the state bar. The city attorney shall receive compensation in the amount established from time to time by the city council.
Sec. 2-225. - Powers and duties.
The city attorney shall have such duties as are provided in the city Charter and as are assigned to him from time to time, consistent with the city Charter and state law, by the city council.
DIVISION 6. - CITY ACCOUNTANT

Sec. 2-226. - Appointment; qualifications; compensation.
The city accountant shall be appointed by the city council and shall serve until a successor is appointed and has qualified. No person shall be so appointed that is not a certified public accountant. The city accountant shall receive compensation in the amount established from time to time by the city council.
(Ord. No. 2016-5 , 6-21-2016)
Sec. 2-227. - Powers and duties.
(a)
The city accountant shall have such duties as are provided in the city Charter and as are assigned from time to time, consistent with the city Charter and state law, by the city council.
(b)
The city accountant shall cause to be prepared and submitted to the city council the annual operating budget.
(c)
The city accountant shall submit to the city council and make available to the public a report on the finances of the city at the city council meeting held on the third Tuesday of each month.
(d)
The city accountant shall keep the city council fully advised as to the financial condition and future needs of the city and shall make such recommendations to the city council concerning the financial affairs of the city as the city accountant deems necessary and prudent.
(Ord. No. 2016-5 , 6-21-2016)
