ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Uniform Rules of the Road adopted by reference.
Pursuant to Chapter 6 of Title 40 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, Code Sections 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, Chapter 6 of Title 40 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated known as the Uniform Rules of the Road and the definitions contained in Code Section 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited herein.
(Code 1977, § 8-1001; Ord. No. 16-90, § 1, 2-20-90)
Sec. 14-2. - Traffic signs, signals and markings.
(a)
Upon approval of the mayor and council, the police department is authorized to:
(1)
Designate and maintain, by appropriate traffic-control signs, signals and devices, including speed breakers, marks or lines erected at intersections or upon the surface of roadways, crosswalks at intersections where there is particular danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway and at such other places as deemed necessary;
(2)
Establish safety zones of such kind and character and at such places as deemed necessary for the protection of pedestrians; and
(3)
Mark lanes for traffic on street pavements at such places as authorized by the mayor and council.
(b)
All traffic-control signs, signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the Manual on Uniform Traffic-Control Devices adopted by the state transportation board. All signs and signals required hereunder for a particular purpose shall so far as practicable be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic-control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this part shall be official traffic-control devices of the city.
(Code 1977, § 8-2002)
Sec. 14-3. - Temporary traffic regulations.
In cases where traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested upon occasions of parades, at theaters and other public assemblages where large numbers of vehicles are assembled, the police may make temporary rules regulating the traffic in these congested districts. No person, after being warned of the temporary traffic regulations, shall violate any of same.
(Code 1977, § 8-1002)
Sec. 14-4. - Certain signs, devices, shrubbery, and structures prohibited; authority to remove.
(a)
Unauthorized signs or devices on public right-of-way prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to erect, place, or maintain within the right-of-way of any public street or roadway any sign, signal, or other device except for an official traffic-control device. It shall be unlawful for any person to erect, place or maintain in a place or position visible from any public street or roadway any shrub or other structure which either:
(1)
Hides from view or interferes with the effectiveness of any official traffic-control device;
(2)
Obstructs a clear view from any public street or roadway, to any other portion of such public road, to intersecting or adjoining public streets or roadways, or to property abutting such public road, in such a manner as to constitute a hazard to traffic on such streets or roads; or
(3)
Because of its nature, construction, overgrowth, or operation constitutes a dangerous distraction to or interferes with the vision of drivers of motor vehicles.
(b)
Authority for removal. Any sign, signal, device, shrub or other structure erected, placed, or maintained on the right-of-way of any public street or roadway in violation of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-25 is hereby declared to be a public nuisance, and the city or its representative may remove or direct the removal of same. Where any sign, signal, device, shrub or other structure is erected, placed, or maintained in violation of this section, but not on the right-of-way of any public street or roadway, the city or its representative may order the removal of such structure or shrubbery or to the owner of the land on which the structure or shrubbery is located. If such structure or shrubbery is not removed within fifteen (15) days after the giving of such order of removal, such officials are hereby authorized to remove or cause to be removed such structure or shrubbery and to submit a statement of expenses incurred in the removal to the owner of the structure or shrubbery or to the owner of the land on which the structure or shrubbery is located. If payment or arrangement to make payment is not made within sixty (60) days after the receipt of said statement, the city clerk shall be authorized to issue a fi. fa. on the property.
(Code 1977, § 10-2039)
State Law reference— Display of unauthorized signs, etc., prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-25.
Sec. 14-5. - Driving across private property to avoid an intersection.
It shall be unlawful for the driver of any vehicle to drive the same upon or through the private property of another for the purpose of cutting through or taking a short-cut without the permission of the person in possession, or entitled to possession, of the premises so as to avoid entering any intersection.
(Code 1977, § 8-2007)
Sec. 14-6. - Occupying driver's seat while under influence of intoxicating liquors or drugs.
No person shall occupy the driver's seat of a motor vehicle upon any street, alley or other public place in the city, immediately under the steering wheel, while under the influence of intoxicating liquors or drugs.
(Code 1977, § 8-1003)
Sec. 14-7. - Escort for funeral processions.
The police department is authorized to escort funeral processions in the city, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-6-76.
(Ord. No. 46-89, § 1, 9-18-89)
Sec. 14-8. - Traffic in the vicinity of a funeral procession.
All motor vehicles, whether proceeding in the same or in the opposite direction, shall stop when encountering a funeral procession within the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 33-97, § 1, 5-5-97)

Sec. 14-31. - Regulation of stopping, standing and parking.
(a)
The police department shall regulate the stopping, standing, and parking of vehicles by signing or yellow painting on any of the public streets and alleys within the corporate limits, provided any such signs and markings shall be approved by the mayor and council.
(b)
No vehicle shall remain parked on any street within the city for a longer period than twelve (12) hours. This shall not conflict with time limit parking in restricted zones, as approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1977, § 8-2005)
Sec. 14-32. - Cars for sale not to be parked on streets.
It shall be unlawful to park on the public streets of the city any automobile being offered for sale not in use by the owner.
(Code 1977, § 10-2036)
Sec. 14-33. - No automobile repairs to be made on streets.
It shall be unlawful to park automobiles on the public streets of the city for the purpose of making repairs thereon. It shall be unlawful for automobile dealers, garagemen or automobile mechanics to park an automobile on the public streets of the city that are kept for sale. It shall be unlawful for these or any other persons to work on or repair an automobile in the public streets of the city.
(Code 1977, § 10-2037)
Sec. 14-34. - On-street public parking; limitations.
All on-street public parking, where provided by the city and delineated as such by appropriately-painted spaces, shall be limited to a maximum of two (2) hours.
(Ord. No. 75-96, § 1, 10-7-96)
Sec. 14-35. - Reserved.
