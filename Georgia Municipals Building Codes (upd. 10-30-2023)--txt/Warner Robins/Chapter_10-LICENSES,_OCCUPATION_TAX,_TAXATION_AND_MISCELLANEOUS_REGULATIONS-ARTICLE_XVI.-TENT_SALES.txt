ARTICLE XVI. - TENT SALES

Sec. 10-401. - Used automobiles dealer extensions.
The following phrase, when used in this article, shall have the meaning ascribed to it in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Tent sales shall include any person, business, firm or corporation, or other legal entity which intends to sell merchandise from a non-permanent semi-enclosed platform and/or tent. "Tent sales" shall also include the sale of all fireworks lawful under Georgia law.
(Ord. No. 51-03, § 1, 7-21-03; Ord. No. 19-12, § 1, 6-4-12; Ord. No. 09-17, § 1, 3-6-17)
Sec. 10-402. - Permit required; application; information; fee; violation.
(a)
No tent sale vendor shall sell and deliver at the time of sale any article to the buyer without having first made application for a license to do business, which application shall recite:
(1)
The name of the vendor;
(2)
The name of the agent conducting the sale, if any;
(3)
The place where the sale is to be conducted;
(4)
A signed copy of site use authorization from property owner;
(5)
The hours of sale; and
(6)
The number of days during which the sale is to be conducted.
(b)
The application shall be accompanied by an application fee in the amount of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) and a copy of a current Georgia occupation tax permit held by the party wishing to hold such tent sale.
(c)
If issued, a permit will require the payment of a fee in the amount of one hundred dollars ($100.00) per day for the duration of the tent sale.
(d)
The following restrictions shall apply to all tent sales, except for tent sales of fireworks:
(1)
The tent used must be a minimum of six hundred (600) square feet;
(2)
The tent sale shall only last for a maximum of five (5) consecutive days;
(3)
There must be at least forty-five (45) calendar days between a single business' tent sales;
(4)
Merchandise sold at the tent sale must be the merchandise as sold in the business location;
(5)
The tent sale may only be located either on the parcel in which the business is located or within one hundred (100) feet of such parcel.
(e)
Any person who shall undertake to do business as a tent sales vendor without having complied with the terms of this article or who shall make any false statements in any application for such a permit shall be guilty of an offense.
(f)
Fines for such offense shall the maximum provided in the applicable section of the City Code and each day shall be deemed a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 51-03, § 1, 7-21-03; Ord. No. 09-17, § 1, 3-6-17)
Sec. 10-403. - Permitted locations; restriction.
Tent sales vendors shall be permitted to locate only in areas of the city having a zoning classification of C-2.
(Ord. No. 51-03, § 1, 7-21-03; Ord. No. 09-17, § 1, 3-6-17)
Sec. 10-404. - Lease required.
No tent sale vendor shall be permitted to operate his business within the city without proof of a written consent from the owner of the property upon which tent sale business is to be operated.
(Ord. No. 51-03, § 1, 7-21-03; Ord. No. 09-17, § 1, 3-6-17)
Sec. 10-405. - Orderly display.
Any displays in the tent sale must be arranged in an orderly manner and must be placed upon the paved space upon which the business is located.
(Ord. No. 51-03, § 1, 7-21-03; Ord. No. 09-17, § 1, 3-6-17)
Sec. 10-406. - Compliance with article.
All holders of permits for the operation of tent sales shall comply with the provisions in this article.
(1)
Proof of liability insurance must be provided;
(2)
Hours of the sale shall be no longer than 8:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m.; and
(3)
All sales must be held at least two hundred fifty (250) feet from the nearest residential area.
(Ord. No. 51-03, § 1, 7-21-03; Ord. No. 09-17, § 1, 3-6-17)
Sec. 10-407. - Tent sales of fireworks.
(a)
Tent sales of fireworks shall be permitted for a period of two (2) weeks prior to:
(1)
July 4th; and
(2)
New Year's Eve of each year.
(b)
All tent sales of fireworks shall comply with the existing provisions of chapter 10, article XVI.
(Ord. No. 19-12, § 1, 6-4-12; Ord. No. 09-17, § 1, 3-6-17)
Secs. 10-408—10-450. - Reserved.
