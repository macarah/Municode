ARTICLE XVII. - MISCELLANEOUS

Sec. 10-451. - Tax on the rental of motor vehicles.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-90 et seq., there is hereby imposed in the city an excise tax of three (3) percent upon the rental charge collected by a rental motor vehicle company when such charge constitutes a taxable event for purposes of sales and use tax under O.C.G.A. Article 1 of Chapter 8 of Title 48. The revenues from this tax shall be remitted into the city's general fund. This tax shall be imposed until repealed by the mayor and council of the city, or on December 31, 2038, whichever shall occur first, and shall be subject to the terms, conditions, and limitations contained in the said state law.
(Ord. No. 63-96, § 1, 8-5-96; Ord. No 32-16, § 1, 10-21-16; Ord. No. 07-20, 3-16-20)
Sec. 10-452. - Records required.
In addition to the requirements of state law, any person engaged in business as a scrap metal processor, as defined by state law at O.C.G.A. § 43-43-1, shall:
(1)
Keep in his/her place of business a book upon which should be entered at the time of any purchase all information required by state law; and
(2)
Require the seller and/or person who delivers such items to positively identify himself/herself by driver's license, Social Security number, military identification, or other photo identification that contains information regarding such person's name, birthdate, sex, race, and address. Such dealers shall also record the license plate number of the vehicle used to deliver the material. When buying autos, such dealers shall also maintain a photocopy of both the seller's and the deliverer's identification for seven (7) days; and
(3)
Require proof of ownership as evidenced by a title record, bill of sale, or tag receipt before accepting any auto for scrap and shall record the vehicle identification number (VIN) of any auto purchased in the record book required by subsection (1). Such dealers shall notify the police department of the purchase of such vehicle and shall wait forty-eight (48) hours, from the time notice was provided to the police department, before demolishing such vehicle for scrap; and
(4)
Require any seller of material to sign a statement stating that he is the rightful owner of the material sold or is entitled to sell the material; and
(5)
Require proof that refrigerant gases were removed by a technician licensed by the State of Georgia on any item that once contained or was designed to contain such gases. The identity and form containing the signature of such state-licensed technician shall be delivered to the police department within forty-eight (48) hours of receipt.
(Ord. No. 44-06, § 1, 8-7-06)
