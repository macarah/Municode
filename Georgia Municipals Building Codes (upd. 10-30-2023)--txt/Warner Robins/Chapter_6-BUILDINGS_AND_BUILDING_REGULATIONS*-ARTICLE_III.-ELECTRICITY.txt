ARTICLE III. - ELECTRICITY
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-76. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Journeyman electrician means any person actually doing the work of electrical installation, construction and repair. No license shall be required of a journeyman electrician but except as hereinafter provided he shall at all times be working under the supervision of a master electrician as hereinafter set out.
Master electrician means any person who shall have passed the examination hereinafter set out, and who shall have been issued a certificate by the board of examiners, all as hereinafter set out.
(Code 1977, § 9-1023)
Sec. 6-77. - Liability for damage unimpaired by article.
(a)
Nothing herein contained shall be construed to relieve any firm or company from liability in case of accident, to life, or damage to property, in the operation of its plant or plants.
(b)
Nothing contained in this article shall be construed to relieve any person from liability, or moral responsibility for damage or injury to life or property.
(Code 1977, § 9-1027)
Sec. 6-78. - Electrical inspector—Right of entry for inspection purposes.
The electrical inspector shall have the right in the discharge of his duties to enter any building under construction or completed, or any manhole or subway, or climb any pole, for the purpose of examining and testing all electrical wires, construction or appliances therein or thereon contained and for that purpose he shall be given prompt access to all buildings, public or private, and to all manholes, subways or poles.
(Code 1977, § 9-1062)
Sec. 6-79. - Same—Authority to condemn unsafe wiring, etc.
The electrical inspector shall have authority to condemn any electrical wiring, equipment or appliance which, in his opinion, is unsafe to life or property and after three (3) days' notice shall order the current discontinued from this electrical wiring equipment or appliance.
(Code 1977, § 9-1063)
Sec. 6-80. - Authority of electrical inspector—To cut out lights or current for violation of article.
If the electrical inspector finds a violation of any section of this article, he shall have the authority after due notice to cut out lights or current in any locality concerned and to enforce discontinuance thereof until requirements are met.
(Code 1977, § 9-1031)
Sec. 6-81. - Same—To condemn wiring, etc.
The electrical inspector shall have authority to condemn any electrical wiring, equipment and appliance which he finds is unsafe to life or property, and after three (3) days' notice shall order the current discontinued for that electrical wiring, equipment and appliance.
(Code 1977, § 9-1032)
Sec. 6-82. - Failure to correct defective work; effect.
Any person that shall fail to correct any defects in his work within five (5) days after having been duly notified of defects by the electrical inspector shall not receive any further permit until the defect or defects have been corrected. Immediately after the correction of the defect or defects, the electrical inspector shall be notified of these corrections.
(Code 1977, § 9-1033)
Sec. 6-83. - Revocation of license or permit; notice; appeal.
After five (5) days' notice any license or permit granted to any electrician may be revoked by the board of examiners should this electrician be found guilty of violation of this article or any ordinance governing electrical work. Any person found guilty under this section shall have the right to appeal the decision to the municipal court and there have a trial on the evidence to determine whether the decision was justified. This appeal shall be made in writing within five (5) days after the decision or judgment appealed from.
(Code 1977, § 9-1035)
Sec. 6-84. - License required; term, transferability.
(a)
Every person desiring to engage in the business of electrical construction and of the installation of wiring and apparatus for electric light, heat or power in the city shall before doing so, obtain a business license from the city clerk therefor. The license shall be issued by the city clerk after the presentation of certificate from the board of electrical examiners or presentation of a valid state-wide license.
(b)
License granted under this article shall be for one (1) year, shall not be transferrable and the full fee shall be charged for any year or fraction thereof, and it shall be unlawful to do any electrical work in the city without license.
(Code 1977, §§ 9-1024, 9-1025)
Sec. 6-85. - Master electrician required to do or supervise work.
It shall be unlawful to do any work of electrical installation, construction, or repair unless done by or under the personal supervision of a master electrician, as defined in this article.
(Code 1977, § 9-1026)
Sec. 6-86. - Fire damage.
(a)
Approval of electrical department prerequisite to resumption of services. Electrical services shall not be resumed on or in the premises of any building or structure damaged by fire without the approval of the electrical inspector. Approval will be given if the building or structure complies with all laws and ordinances.
(b)
Ruling from electrical inspector required for replacement of wiring. Before beginning work of any electrical installation damaged by fire, a ruling shall be obtained from the electrical inspector, as to what part of the wiring must be replaced. The inspector shall order any wiring that is unsafe or not in compliance with all laws and ordinances to be removed. All old wiring not in use shall be removed.
(c)
Inspector; authority to cut wire or service. In the event of fire in any building or structure, the electrical inspector shall have full power and authority to cut and remove any electrical wire or service to any building.
(Code 1977, §§ 9-1065—9-1067)
Secs. 6-87—6-100. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMITS AND INSPECTIONS

Sec. 6-101. - Permit required; application; details.
(a)
No person shall commence any kind of electrical installation, alteration or repairs until a permit has been issued by the electrical inspector.
(b)
Applicant for a permit shall furnish the inspector a statement of the electrical installation, with a written application or statement under oath to be administered by the inspector or his assistant showing the locality and giving all installation alterations or repairs to be done and the approximate cost including labor and materials to do the work for which the permit is being requested.
(Code 1977, § 9-1051)
Sec. 6-102. - Exceptions to permit requirements.
This article shall not cover minor electrical repairs involving no new work, alteration or change that may be necessary or incident to the maintenance only in good condition of an established plant, installation or system of wiring, but this qualification does not extend to installation of new circuits or the extension of any circuits already installed, or the installation of any apparatus, devices or materials for which a permit is required under this article.
(Code 1977, § 9-1053)
Sec. 6-103. - Application to be filed with electrical department; forms.
Before beginning any work of electrical installation an application for permit shall be made to the electrical inspector on forms furnished for that purpose which application shall contain an accurate and detailed account of the electrical work contemplated as provided for in all forms furnished.
(Code 1977, § 9-1052)
Sec. 6-104. - Information required for issuance of permit; fees; issuance of permit.
(a)
Any person proposing to do wiring work and installation of electrical apparatus or fixtures for use in connection with electricity shall before undertaking the work or making the installations file with the city electrical inspector a statement in writing which shall give the proposed location by street and number and the name of the person for whom the work is to be done and also shall set out in detail the amount of wiring, electrical work, electrical apparatus, or other electrical devices or fixtures for use in connection with the statements prepared in the office of the city electrical inspector; with this information shall be included the amount of fees set out in this article.
(b)
When the fees have been paid, the building inspection office or the electrical inspector shall approve the application, if it meets all necessary requirements, and issue a permit therefor.
(c)

(Code 1977, § 9-1054; Ord. No. 37-90, § 3, 6-18-90; Ord. No. 56-93, § 1, 10-18-93; Ord. No. 12-97, § 1, 1-21-97; Ord. No. 76-02, §§ 2—4, 7, 11-18-02; Ord. No. 27-04, §§ 2—4, 7, 6-21-04; Ord. No. 50-05, 10-17-05; Ord. No. 54-05, § 1, 11-7-05; Ord. No. 24-11, § 1, 7-18-11)
Sec. 6-105. - Inspector authorized to require general plan.
If requested by the electrical inspector, the applicant shall file a general plan of construction and detailed description of apparatus, device, appliances or fixtures to be used in the work.
(Code 1977, § 9-1055)
Sec. 6-106. - Proceedings to collect unpaid fees, etc.
If any person shall do any work covered by this article without filing a statement as herein provided and paying the fees herein ordained the electrical inspector shall make out a bill therefor and transmit it to the city clerk, who shall issue a fi. fa. in the name of the city against such a person in the usual form of executions issued by the city and turn it over to the marshal who shall proceed to levy and collect it in the same manner as he does any other for collection, such as levy, advertisement and sale, etc.
(Code 1977, § 9-1056)
Sec. 6-107. - New applications to be rejected until all unpaid fees are paid.
If any person shall violate this article, and shall undertake any electrical work requiring permit and payment of fees as herein provided, and shall thereafter make applications to the office of the electrical inspector for permit to do other and different and independent electrical work covered by this article, the electrical inspector shall not consider the application and shall not grant it unless the fees for the previous work, which were not paid, shall have been first paid. In this event, if fees for this previous work are then paid, then the electrical inspector shall consider the new application.
(Code 1977, § 9-1057)
Sec. 6-108. - New application required for additional work.
If after obtaining permit for electrical work, it is found necessary to change or increase the load of electric current, an application for permit shall be filed covering the additional work.
(Code 1977, § 9-1058)
Sec. 6-109. - Alterations in wiring or rated load prohibited.
No alterations shall be made in the wiring of any building or structure for light, heat or power or increase in the rated load as fixed by the electrical inspector.
(Code 1977, § 9-1059)
Sec. 6-110. - Wiring; covering or concealing; inspection.
It shall be unlawful for any persons, their agents or employees to cover or conceal any electrical wiring in the city except upon the following conditions:
Whenever it is desired to conceal any electrical wiring, the electrical inspector shall be notified and it shall be his duty to inspect the wiring and if approved by him as being in conformity with this article, the rules prescribed hereunder, the statutes of the state and the approved methods of construction for safety to life and property, he shall issue a certificate to be known as a certificate of inspection which shall authorize the concealing of the wiring.
(Code 1977, § 9-1064)
Sec. 6-111. - Failure to pay permit fees; effect.
As a cumulative remedy, if any person shall violate this article, and shall undertake any electrical work requiring permit and payment of fees, as herein provided and shall thereafter make application to the office of the city clerk for permit to do other and different and independent electrical work, covered by this article, the electrical inspector shall not grant it unless the fees for the previous work are then paid; then the electrical inspector shall consider the new application for other and different work as herein provided.
(Code 1977, § 9-1034)
Sec. 6-112. - Reinspection fee.
There shall be reinspection fee of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for all additional inspections required in excess of two (2) per required inspection.
(Ord. No. 76-02, § 9, 11-18-02; Ord. No. 24-11, § 1, 7-18-11)
Secs. 6-113—6-130. - Reserved.

Sec. 6-131. - Adoption of electrical code.
A certain document, one (1) copy of which is on file in the office of the city clerk, being marked and designated as the National Electrical Code, 2014 edition, which code is the standard for electrical wiring and apparatus of the National Fire Protection Association, is hereby adopted as the electrical code of the city, and each and all of the regulations, provisions, penalties, conditions and terms of this electrical code are hereby referred to, adopted and made a part of this article as if set forth verbatim herein.

Sec. 6-137. - Conduit for service entrance wires.
Service entrance wires in all buildings shall be run in two (2) inch minimum rigid conduit from meter base to a weatherhead for attachment of utility company's wires. This conduit shall extend a minimum of two (2) feet and a maximum of four (4) feet above the roof line at the point where the conduit passes through or by the roof. Conduit shall be securely strapped to the building, and in buildings with an overhang, shall pass through the overhang and be properly flashed, so as to be rain tight.
(Code 1977, § 9-1082; Ord. No. 46-91, § 1, 8-19-91)
