ARTICLE IV. - DEPARTMENTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-232. - Departmental organization.
The administrative service of the city shall be divided into such departments or agencies as may be established from time to time by the city council. The city manager shall appoint a director or head of each department or agency who shall be its principal officer. Departments may include, but are not limited to, an administrative department, the street department, the solid waste and landfill department, the wastewater department, the natural gas department, and the animal control department, as well as other departments created as deemed appropriate or necessary by the city council.
(Code 1999, § 3-801; Code 2011, § 2-254)
Sec. 2-233. - Performance of duties.
It shall be a violation of this Code for any department head, official, or employee to fail to perform the duties enumerated herein.
(Code 1999, § 3-803; Code 2011, § 2-255)
Sec. 2-234. - Responsibility of department heads.
Every department head is immediately responsible to the city manager for the effective administration of his respective department. Each department head shall be responsible for the administration and direction of the affairs and operations of his department or agency, subject to the direction and supervision of the city manager. Each such department head shall:
(1)
Be immediately responsible to the city manager for the effective administration of his respective department and all activities assigned thereto;
(2)
Keep informed as to the latest practices in his particular field and inaugurate, with the approval of the city manager, such new practices as appear to be of benefit to the service and to the public;
(3)
Submit annual reports of the activities of his respective department to the mayor;
(4)
Establish and maintain a system of filing and indexing records and reports in sufficient detail to furnish all information necessary for proper control of departmental activities and to form a basis for the periodic reports to the city manager;
(5)
Have power, when authorized by the mayor, to appoint and remove, subject to personnel regulations, all subordinates under him; and
(6)
Be responsible for the proper maintenance of all city property and equipment used in his respective departments.
(Code 1999, § 3-804; Code 2011, § 2-256)
Sec. 2-235. - Intradepartmental cooperation.
Each department shall cooperate with every other department and shall furnish, upon the direction of the city manager, any other department such service, labor, and materials as may be requisitioned by the head of each department, as its own facilities permit.
(Code 2011, § 2-257)
Sec. 2-236. - Reports to city manager.
Within 30 days after the close of each fiscal year, the city manager shall obtain a report from the various city departments for the previous year and shall recommend such adjustments as said city manager may see fit. The city manager shall have the power to require reports from all officers and employees of the city as he may deem necessary to carry out his executive responsibilities.
(Code 1999, § 3-101; Code 2011, § 2-258)

DIVISION 2. - SPECIFIC DEPARTMENTS

Sec. 2-266. - Administrative department.
The administrative department shall perform all administrative duties for the city council and for the city which are not expressly assigned by law, ordinance, or resolution to another department or officer.
(Code 1999, § 3-805; Code 2011, § 2-280)
Sec. 2-267. - Solid waste services by contract.
Advanced Disposal, Kathleen offices, shall be responsible for the collection and disposal of refuse accumulated within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1999, § 3-806; Code 2011, § 2-281)
Sec. 2-268. - Street department.
(a)
Department function. The street department shall be responsible for the proper construction and maintenance of streets, sidewalks, parks, buildings, public utilities, and other movable and immovable property of the city.
(b)
Duties of director. The director of the street department shall:
(1)
Make regular inspections of the streets, alleys, and sidewalks within the city; make a written memorandum of same; and cause all defects and obstructions to be remedied at the earliest opportunity; especially shall he carry out this duty after storms or heavy rainfall.
(2)
Ensure that all highways, streets, and sidewalks of the city are maintained in a clean condition; and enforce and carry out all city ordinances and regulations concerning city streets and sidewalks.
(3)
Enforce the provisions of all technical codes adopted by the city council, not enforced by another officer.
(4)
Supervise and inspect the operations of all public utility systems provided by the city not supervised and inspected by another officer.
(Code 1999, §§ 3-307, 3-808; Code 2011, § 2-282)
Sec. 2-269. - Police department.
The police department shall be responsible for the prevention of crime, the enforcement of federal and state laws and city ordinances, the apprehension of violators of such laws and ordinances, and the protection of persons and property against injury from crime.
(Code 1999, § 3-309; Code 2011, § 2-283)
Sec. 2-270. - Fire department.
The fire department shall be responsible for the prevention of uncontrolled fires, loss of life and property in fires and loss of life from asphyxiation or drowning; the confining of fires to the place of origin; the extinguishment of uncontrolled fires; and the performance of such additional rescue work as its equipment and the training of its personnel make practicable.
(Code 1999, § 3-818; Code 2011, § 2-284)
Secs. 2-271—2-288. - Reserved.
