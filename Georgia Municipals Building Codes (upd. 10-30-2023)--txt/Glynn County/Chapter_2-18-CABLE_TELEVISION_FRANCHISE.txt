2-18-1 - Short Title.
This ordinance shall be known, and may be cited as the "Glynn County Cable Television Franchise Ordinance."
2-18-2 - Definitions.
For the purpose of this ordinance, the following terms, words, and their derivations shall have the meanings given herein. Where not inconsistent with the context, words used in the present tense include the future, words used in the plural number include the singular number and words in the singular number include the plural number. The words "shall" is always mandatory and not merely directory.
a.
Board of Commissioners is the Board of Commissioners of Glynn County, Georgia.
b.
Company is a grantee, its successors and assigns, or rights under a Franchise License issued by the Board of Commissioners of Glynn County.
c.
County is Glynn County, a political subdivision of the State of Georgia.
d.
Cable Television System means unless the context otherwise clearly requires, as used in this ordinance, a non-broadcast facility consisting of a set of transmission paths and associated generation, reception, associated generation, reception, transmission, and control equipment, under common ownership and control, that distributes or is designed to distribute to subscribers the signals of one or more television broadcast stations and programs received via satellite, microwave, video tape, video discs, or produced by the franchised cable system.
e.
FCC is the Federal Communications Commission.
f.
Franchise License is a non-exclusive license issued by the Board of Commissioners pursuant to the provisions of this ordinance.
g.
Gross Subscriber Service Revenues shall mean all monthly service revenues received by License from the operation of its cable television system including but not by way of limitation, that portion of the monthly pay television service revenue retained by the License.
h.
Licensee it successors or assigns, is a holder of Franchise License issued by the Board of Commissioners.
i.
Person is any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation or organization of any kind.
2-18-3 - Grant of Authority.
a.
Upon the issuance of a Franchise License by the County to a Licensee, the Licensee shall have the right and privilege to construct, erect, operate and maintain a cable television system for the reception, amplification and distribution of video and/or audio signals to subscribing members of the public for a fee, for the period of time specified in the Franchise License with an option or options to renew the franchise for additional periods of time as specified in the Franchise License.
b.
The Franchise License shall grant the holder thereof the right and privilege to construct and maintain said cable television system in, upon, along, above, over and under the roads, alleys, public ways and public places now laid out or dedicated and all extensions thereof, and additions thereto, in the unincorporated area of Glynn County, subject to terms and conditions set out in this ordinance.
c.
The right to use and occupy said roads, alleys, public ways and places for the purpose herein set forth will not be exclusive, and the County reserves the right to grant a similar use of said roads, alleys, public ways and places to any person at any time during the period of a Franchise License.
d.
In connection with the right and privilege granted in a Franchise License, the Board of Commissioners prior to the issuance of a Franchise License, will examine and approve the legal, character, financial, technical and other qualifications of a proposed Licensee, as well as the adequacy and feasibility of its construction arrangements, as part of a public proceeding affording due process.
2-18-4 - Compliance with Applicable Laws and Ordinances.
a.
A Licensee shall, at all times during the life of its License, be subject to the lawful exercises of police power of the County.
b.
The License will agree to promptly comply with all valid local, state and federal regulations, including the rules and regulations of the FCC.
c.
Any amendments or modifications of Section 76.31 of the Rules of the FCC or State Regulations dealing with equal employment opportunities, shall be incorporated into any Franchise License issued pursuant to the provisions of this ordinance immediately after the adoption of such modification or at the time of the renewal of a Franchise License, whichever occurs first.
2-18-5 - Indemnification.
The Licensee shall, as a part of its Franchise Licensee, agree to indemnify, protect and save the County harmless from and against any and all losses, cost or expenses resulting from damage to any property or bodily injury or death to any person, including payments made under any workmen's compensation law, which arise out of or occur by reason of the exercise by the holder of Franchise License of the rights granted in the Franchise License. The Licensee shall also be required to carry insurance to protect itself and the County from and against all claims, demands, actions, judgments, costs, expenses and liabilities which may arise or result, directly or indirectly from or by reason of such loss, injury or damage. The insurance policy shall specifically provide that the County shall be a named insured. The amounts of such insurance against liability due to physical damage to property shall be not less than $500,000.00 as to any one claim and not less than $1,000,000.00 aggregate in any single policy year; and against liability due to bodily injury or to death of persons, not less than $500,000.00 as to any one person, and not less than $1,000,000.00 as to all such claims arising from any one accident. The Licensee shall also carry such insurance as it deems necessary to protect it and the County from any and all claims under the workmen's compensation laws in effect that may be applicable to the Company. All insurance required by this Section shall be and remain in full force and effect for the entire period of the Franchise License. A certificate of insurance shall be filed with the County Administrator.
2-18-6 - Construction Standards.
a.
The Licensee shall equitably and reasonably extend the system so as to enable it to render service to all economically feasible areas within the County within one year after the issuance of its Franchise License.
b.
With regard to the Licensee's construction, operation and maintenance of the cable television system, the following standards shall apply:
1.
The construction maintenance and use of the Licensee's cable television system shall comply with the standards for materials and engineering and all other provisions of the National Electric Safety Code and the National Electrical Code.
2.
The County shall have the right to approve all construction and installation work performed subject to the provisions of this ordinance and to make such inspections as it shall find necessary to ensure compliance with this ordinance and all other laws, or rules and regulations issued pursuant thereto, of governmental agencies having jurisdiction over Cable Television Systems.
2-18-7 - Road Vacation or Abandonment.
In the event any road, alley, public highway or utility easement, or any portion thereof, used by the Licensee shall be vacated by the County or the use thereof, used by the Licensee shall be vacated by the County or the use thereof discontinued by the Licensee during the term of any Franchise, the Licensee shall forthwith remove all of its facilities therefrom except for buried cables, unless specifically required to remove them by the County, and upon the removal thereof, restore, repair or reconstruct the road area where such removal has occurred in such conditions as may be required by the County. The Licensee will give advanced notice to the County of its intention to abandon any underground cable. In the event of failure, neglect or refusal of the Licensee, after 30 days notice by Board of Commissioners to repair, improve or maintain such street proportions, the County may do such work or cause it to be done, and the cost thereof as found and declared by the County shall be paid by the Licensee, and collection may be by Court Action or otherwise.
2-18-8 - Conditions on Street Occupancy.
a.
Use. All transmission and distribution structures, lines and equipment erected by a Licensee within the unincorporated area of Glynn County shall be so located as to cause minimum interference with the proper use of all roads, alleys and other public ways and places, and to cause minimum interference with the rights and reasonable convenience of property owners who adjoin any of the said roads, alleys or other public ways and places.
b.
Restoration. In case of any disturbance of pavement, sidewalk, driveway or other surfacing, the Licensee shall, at its own cost and expense and in a manner approved by the County, replace and restore all paving sidewalk, driveway or surface of any road or alley disturbed to as good condition as before said work was commenced.
c.
Relocation. In the event that any time during the period of a Franchise License, the County shall lawfully elect to alter or change the grade or width of any street, alley or other public way, the Licensee, upon reasonable notice by the County, shall remove, relay and relocate its poles, wires, cables, underground conduits, manholes and other system fixtures at its own expense.
d.
Temporary Removal of Wire and Building Moving. The Licensee shall, on the request of any person holding a building moving permit issued by the County, temporarily raise or lower its fees to permit the moving of buildings. The expense of such temporary removal, raising or lower of wires shall be paid by the person requesting the same, and the Licensee shall have the authority to require such payment in advance. The Licensee shall be given not less than 120 hours advance notice to arrange for such temporary wire changes.
e.
Trimming of Trees. The Licensee shall have the authority to trim trees upon and overhanging any road, alley or other public way so as to prevent the branches of such trees from coming in contact with its wires, cables or other equipment.
2-18-9 - Police Use.
a.
The Licensee shall, whenever possible and practicable, use the poles owned and maintained by the County and/or the utility company(ies) which serve Glynn County. When the use of such poles is not practicable or satisfactory and reasonable rental agreements cannot be entered into with said parties, the Licensee shall have the right to erect and maintain its own poles, as may be necessary for the proper construction and maintenance of the television distribution system.
b.
In all sections of the County where the cables, wires, or other like facilities of public utilities are placed underground or are required to be so placed underground at any time in the future, the Licensee shall place its cables, wires or other like facilities underground to the maximum extent that existing technology reasonably permits the Licensee to do so.
2-18-10 - Employment Practices.
The Licensee will establish and adhere to the following policies and procedures:
a.
Equal opportunity in employment shall be afforded all qualified persons by the Licensee and no person shall be discriminated against because of race, color, religion, national origin or sex.
b.
An equal opportunity notice will be posted in the office of the Licensee which states as follows: "Equal Opportunity Employer—discrimination because of sex, race, color, religion, or national origin is prohibited and you may notify the Equal Opportunity Commission or the Federal Communications Commission if you believe you have been discriminated against."
c.
The policy set forth in (b) above will be set forth on the employment application forms of the Licensee in red ink.
d.
Those in positions to hire will be specifically instructed to examine all hiring policies to make certain that the same comply with the foregoing declarations.
e.
Each employee of the Licensee will be advised of the foregoing equal opportunity policy.
f.
No selection techniques or tests that may have the effect of discriminating against minority groups or females will be used.
g.
Whenever recruiting is done through schools, schools with significant minority group enrollments will be given equal consideration.
h.
Contact with leadership in minority and human relations organizations will be maintained to encourage referral of qualified minority and/or female applicants.
2-18-11 - Reserved Channel.
The Licensee shall reserve one channel for use by public and parochial elementary, secondary, and college level schools, In the event the reserved channel is not used by persons or organizations eligible to use this channel, he Licensee may devote the available time to other purposes of its own choosing.
2-18-12 - Regulation.
a.
The Licensee shall, while operating under a Franchise License, maintain efficient cable television service in Glynn County. However, the Licensee shall not be liable for loss or damage caused by interruption or failure of service due to accident or breakdown to lines or equipment, strike, riot, act of God, or the public enemy, or such other causes as are beyond its control, or due to shutdowns for reasonable periods to make repairs to equipment; but the Licensee shall in such cases exercise proper diligence in repairing such equipment and resume operation of same without unnecessary delay.
b.
The Licensee will adopt and the Board of Commissioners will approve, procedures for the investigation and resolution of complaints regarding its cable television operations. Notice of the procedures for reporting and resolving complaints shall be given to each subscriber at the time of initial subscribing to the cable television system operated by the Licensee. Further, the Licensee shall maintain a local business office or agent in the County or the City of Brunswick for the purpose of receiving notice of, investigating and seeing that proper steps are taken to effect the resolution of any problems relating to service or other aspects of its cable television operations. The local representative of the Licensee shall have primary responsibility for the continuing administration of the Franchise License, and for the implementation of the procedures of this subsection.
c.
The County shall have access at all reasonable hours to all of the Licensee's plans, contracts, and engineering records, records showing the Licensee's charges for pay television services, statistical, customer and service records relating to the property and the operation of the Licensee and to all other records required to be kept hereunder.
d.
A copy of any and all rules, regulations, terms and conditions adopted by the Licensee for the conduct of its business shall be filed with the County Administrator; and a copy shall also be available for public inspection at the office of the Licensee.
e.
The Franchise License shall not in any way be construed as a license or permit to the License to engage in the sale or service of radio or television sets or equipment. As part of the consideration for the granting of the Franchise License, the Licensee will agree not to engage in the sale or service of TV sets, appliance or parts.

2-18-13 - Rates.
The rates or charges which the Licensee assesses its subscribes for cable television services shall be at all times fair and reasonable. A copy of the Licensee's schedule of charges for installation and furnishing of service shall be filed with the County Administrator not less than 60 days prior to the effective date of such charges.
2-18-14 - Payment to the County.
a.
The Licensee shall pay to the County, for and in consideration of the right and privilege to conduct cable television operations pursuant to this ordinance and the Franchise License, an annual fee in an amount equal to three percent of its gross subscriber service revenues as defined in this ordinance, derived from its cable television operations in the unincorporated area of Glynn County, with each year's fee due and payable to the Glynn County Finance Department by January 31 for the preceding calendar year. All revenues not paid when due shall bear interest from date at the rate of 12 percent per annum.
b.
In the event of revocation of termination of the Franchise License, the first annual fee payment shall be prorated from the immediately preceding January 1 to the date of termination of service.
2-18-15 - Forfeiture of Franchise.
a.
The Board of Commissioners may declare a forfeiture of the Franchise License and revoke the same in the event the holder thereof:
1.
Substantially violates any material provision of this ordinance, here such violation remains uncured for a period of 30 days subsequent to the receipt by the Licensee of a written notice of such violation, except where such violation is not the fault of the Licensee or is due to excusable neglect; or
2.
Practices any fraud or deceit upon the County.
b.
Such Forfeiture shall be declared by a resolution of the Board of Commissioners duly adopted after 20 days' notice to the Licensee, and shall in no way affect any of the County's rights under this ordinance or any provision of law; provided, however, that before the Franchise License may be terminated and cancelled under this Section, except for non-payment of monies due to the County from Licensee, the Licensee shall be provided with an opportunity to be heard at a public hearing before the Board of Commissioners upon ten days' written notice to the Licensee of the time and place of the public hearing; provided that said notice shall affirmatively cite the reasons alleged to constitute a cause for revocation; and provided further that notice of said public hearing shall be published in a local newspaper of general circulation at least five days before the hearing.
2-18-16 - Miscellaneous.
Failure to enforce or insist upon compliance with any of the terms or conditions of this ordinance shall not constitute a general waiver or relinquishment of any such terms or conditions, but the same shall be and remain at all times in full force and effect.
2-18-17 - Franchise License.
The provisions of this ordinance shall automatically be incorporated into the terms and conditions of any Franchise License issued by the Board of Commissioners.
2-18-18 - An Ordinance Granting a Cable Television Franchise to Century Venture Corporation d/b/a Adelphia Cable Company.
Section 1. BACKGROUND.
CENTURY VENTURE CORPORATION d/b/a ADELPHIA CABLE COMPANY ("Adelphia") has applied to Glynn County (the "County") for a franchise to provide cable television services within the County. Adelphia and the County's negotiating representatives have engaged in negotiations and have presented a proposed cable television franchise agreement.
Section 2. GRANT OF FRANCHISE.
A non-exclusive franchise is hereby granted to CENTURY VENTURE CORPORATION d/b/a ADELPHIA CABLE COMPANY to provide Cable Services within the County.
Section 3. APPROVAL OF FRANCHISE AGREEMENT AND GUARANTEE AGREEMENT.
The franchise granted in Section 2 of this ordinance is subject to all of the terms, conditions, covenants, and agreements contained in the Non-Exclusive Cable Services Franchise Agreement By and Between Glynn County, Georgia and Century Venture Corporation d/b/a Adelphia Cable Company (the "Franchise Agreement"), attached as Exhibit A to this ordinance.
Section 4. EXECUTION OF AGREEMENTS.
The Chairman of the Board of Commissioners is hereby authorized and directed to execute and seal the Franchise Agreement attached as Exhibit A to this ordinance upon receipt of at least four copies thereof fully executed by all other parties.
Section 5. REPEAL OF CONFLICTING ORDINANCES
Passage of this ordinance shall repeal any conflicting ordinance , provision or provisions heretofore enacted by the Glynn County Board of Commissioners.
Section 6. EFFECTIVE DATE.
This ordinance shall be in full force and effect following its passage, approval, and publication in the manner provided by law.
This ordinance Amendment shall become effective upon passage (4-17-03).
