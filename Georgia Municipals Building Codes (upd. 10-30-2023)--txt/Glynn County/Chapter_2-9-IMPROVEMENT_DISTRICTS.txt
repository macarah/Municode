ARTICLE I. - IMPROVEMENT DISTRICTS

2-9-1 - Special Improvement Districts—Established; Authority.
There is hereby established in the county, certain improvement districts, under authority of an amendment to the sate constitution, ratified November 4, 1930, for the purpose of providing sanitation, fire protection, and police protection therein, and to build and improve the roads and streets therein, which shall be known as "Glynn County Special Improvement Districts", and under authority of an amendment to the state constitution, ratified November 7, 1972 and found in Georgia Law 1972, Page 1552, and generally known as Amendment 19.
2-9-2 - Special Improvement Fire Protection District.
a.
Official Map. The Board of Commissioners hereby establishes an official map entitled Glynn County Special Improvement Fire Protection District Map", dated December 15, 1983, which shall be filed in the Office of the Board of Commissioners of Glynn County, in the Courthouse at Brunswick, Georgia.
b.
Fire Protection Division. All areas of the unincorporated area of Glynn County lying within the boundary lines delineated in green on the official map shall constitute the Glynn County Fire Protection District.
2-9-3 - Special Police Protection District.
Sea Island. Beginning at a point where the low water mark of the Hampton River intersects the low water mark of the Atlantic Ocean; thence along the low water mark of the Atlantic Ocean that joins Sea Island to a point where Black Banks River intersects the Atlantic Ocean that joins Sea Island to a point where Black Banks River intersects the Black Banks River; thence in a northeasterly direction along Village Creek to a point where Village Creek intersects the Hampton River; thence along the western bank of Hampton River to the point of beginning. For further identification reference is made to the map of said county identified as county index map and county tax maps on file in the office of the county tax assessor.
2-9-4 - Urban Service Tax District.
a.
Official Map. The Board of Commissioners hereby establishes an official map for the urban tax district entitled "Urban Service/Tax District," dated November 6, 1980. The official map shall be filed in the office of the Board of Commissioners of Glynn County, Glynn County Courthouse, Brunswick, Georgia.
b.
Boundaries. All areas of the unincorporated area of the county lying within the boundary lines delineated on the official map by red lines shall constitute a part of the urban tax district.

2-9-50 - Noise Abatement—Established; Authority.
a.
Authority, Name. This ordinance is adopted by the Board of Commissioners of Glynn County pursuant to Article IX, Section II, Paragraph I of the Constitution of Georgia (1983) and shall be referred to as the "Glynn County Noise Abatement Ordinance."
b.
Findings. The Board of Commissioners of Glynn County has determined that noise is a major source of environmental pollution which represents a threat to the serenity and quality of life in Glynn County and that noise exposure may be a cause of adverse physiological and psychological effects as well as economic loss. It is the intention of this ordinance to limit noise in housing areas and other noise sensitive urban areas located in the unincorporated area of Glynn County, excluding Jekyll Island and the Federal Law Enforcement Training Center.
2-9-51 - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply in the interpretation and enforcement of this ordinance, unless otherwise specifically stated.
Construction shall mean any site preparation assembly, erection, substantial repair, alteration or similar action, for or on public or private rights-of-way, structures, utilities or real property.
Consumer Fireworks means any small fireworks devices containing restricted amounts of pyrotechnic composition, designed primarily to produce visible or audible effects by combustion, that comply with the construction, chemical composition, and labeling regulations of the United States Consumer Product Safety Commission as provided for in Parts 1500 and 1507 of Title 16 of the Code of Federal Regulations, the United States Department of Transportation as provided for in Part 172 of Title 49 of the Code of Federal Regulations, and the American Pyrotechnics Association as provided for in the 2001 American Pyrotechnics Association Standard 87-1, and additionally shall mean Roman candles.
Emergency shall mean any occurrence or set of circumstances involving actual or imminent physical or psychological trauma or property damage which demands immediate action.
Emergency Work is work made necessary to restore property to a safe condition following a public calamity or work required to protect persons or property from an imminent exposure to danger.
Fireworks means any combustible or explosive composition or any substance or combination of substances or article prepared for the purpose of producing a visible or audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration, or detonation, including blank cartridges, firecrackers, torpedos, skyrockets, bombs, sparklers, and other combustibles and explosives of like construction, as well as articles containing any explosive or flammable compound and tablets and other devices containing an explosive substance.
Industrial District shall mean a Basic Industrial District, Limited Industrial, or a General Industrial District as defined in the Zoning Ordinance of Glynn County.
Mechanical Sound-making Device shall mean any radio receiving device, television, stereo, musical instrument, phonograph sound amplifier or other machines or devices for the producing, reproducing or amplifying of sound and/or noise.
Motor Vehicle shall have the same meaning as O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1; which includes every vehicle which is self-propelled other than an electric personal assistive mobility device (EPAMD).
Noise shall mean any sound which disturbs humans or which causes or tends to cause an adverse psychological or physiological effect on humans.
Person shall mean any individual, partnership, firm or corporation, or any combination of one or more of them.
Plainly Audible shall mean any sound emanating from the specific sound-producing sources set forth herein which can be heard from the distances set forth herein, using the following sound measurement standards: Measurement shall be by the auditory senses of a person standing at a distance no less than the required minimum distance from the source of the sound. For music and other noise, words and phrases need not be discernible. For music and other noise, bass reverberations are included.
Plainly Audible within an adjoining residential dwelling shall mean any sound emanating from the specific sound-producing sources set forth herein which can be heard inside a residential dwelling at the distances set forth herein, using the following sound measurement standards: Measurement shall be by the auditory senses of a person standing within any living area of a residential dwelling at a distance of at least five feet from the exterior wall of the residential dwelling with all exterior doors and windows closed. For music and other noise, words and phrases need not be discernible. For music and other noise, bass reverberations are included.
Public Right-of-Way shall mean any street, avenue, highway, lane or similar place which is owned or controlled by a governmental entity.
Public Space shall mean any real property including any structure thereon which is owned or controlled by a governmental entity.
Real Property Boundary shall mean an imaginary line along the ground surface, and its vertical extension, which separates the real property owned by one person from that owned by another person, but not including intra building real property divisions.
Times shall mean Eastern Standard Time or Daylight Savings Time as observed in the State of Georgia.
Zoning Districts shall mean those districts established by the Glynn County Zoning Ordinance.

2-9-52 - Exceptions.
The provisions of this ordinance shall not apply to:
(1)
The emission of sound for the purpose of alerting people to the existence of danger;
(2)
The emission of sound in the performance of emergency work;
(3)
Agricultural activities;
(4)
Rail and air transportation and public transportation vehicles;
(5)
The emission of sound from a manufacturing, processing or assembly operation performed within an Industrial District;
(6)
Church or clock carillons, bells or chimes;
(7)
The emission of sound in the discharge of weapons at a firing range previously issued a permit by Glynn County;
(8)
The discharge of weapons in an area zoned Forest Agricultural under the Glynn County Zoning Ordinances;
(9)
Reserved.
(10)
Burglar alarm when properly maintained in accordance with rules and regulations to be promulgated by the Glynn County Police Department and approved by the Board of Commissioners; or
(11)
Sound volumes produced by radio, tape player, or other mechanical sound-making device or instrument from within a motor vehicle on a street or highway, which sound is controlled by the O.C.G.A. § 40-6-14. O.C.G.A. § 40-6-14 makes it unlawful for any person operating or occupying a motor vehicle on a street or highway to operate or amplify the sound produced by a mechanical sound-making device from within the motor vehicle so that the sound is plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from the motor vehicle.

2-9-53 - General Noise Activities Prohibited.
(a)
Restrictions of 300 feet for 7:00 a.m. through 11:00 p.m.
1.
Mechanical sound-making devices. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to play, use, operate, or permit to be played, used, or operated any mechanical sound-making device at such a volume and in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible at a distance of 300 feet or more from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
It shall also be unlawful for any person or persons to play, use, operate, or permit to be played, used, or operated any mechanical sound-making device at such a volume and in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible within an adjoining residential dwelling at a distance of 300 feet or less from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
2.
Human-produced sounds. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to yell, shout, hoot, whistle, sing, or create any other human-produced sound (including but not limited to the above) on the public streets or sidewalks or on private property so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible at a distance of 300 feet or more from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
It shall also be unlawful for any person or persons to yell, shout, hoot, whistle, sing, or create any other human-produced sound (including but not limited to the above) on the public streets or sidewalks or on private property so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible within an adjoining residential dwelling at a distance of 300 feet or less from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
3.
Party noise. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons in charge of a party or other social event that occurs on any private property to allow that party or social event to produce noise in such a manner that such noise is plainly audible at a distance of 300 feet or more from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
It shall also be unlawful for any person or persons in charge of a party or social event that occurs on any private property to allow that party or social event to produce noise in such a manner that such noise is plainly audible within an adjoining residential dwelling at a distance of 300 feet or less from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
For the purposes of this subsection, a "person in charge of a party or other social event" shall mean any adult person who resides in or on the premises, or who rents the premises, involved in such party or social event and is present at such party or social event. For the purposes of this subsection, "noise" shall mean the same sounds, or any combination thereof, as described in paragraphs 1. or 2. above.
(b)
Restrictions of 100 feet for 11:00 p.m. through 7 a.m.
1.
Mechanical sound-making devices. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to play, use, operate, or permit to be played, used, or operated any mechanical sound-making device at such a volume and in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
It shall also be unlawful for any person or persons to play, use, operate, or permit to be played, used, or operated any mechanical sound-making device at such a volume and in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible within an adjoining residential dwelling at a distance of 100 feet or less from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
2.
Human-produced sounds. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to yell, shout, hoot, whistle, sing, or create any other human-produced sound (including but not limited to the above) on the public streets or sidewalks or on private property so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
It shall also be unlawful for any person or persons to yell, shout, hoot, whistle, sing, or create any other human-produced sound (including but not limited to the above) on the public streets or sidewalks or on private property so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible within an adjoining residential dwelling at a distance of 100 feet or less from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
3.
Party noise. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons in charge of a party or other social event that occurs on any private property to allow that party or social event to produce noise in such a manner that such noise is plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
It shall also be unlawful for any person or persons in charge of a party or social event that occurs on any private property to allow that party or social event to produce noise in such a manner that such noise is plainly audible within an adjoining residential dwelling at a distance of 100 feet or less from the source of the noises or sounds between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
For the purposes of this subsection, a "person in charge of a party or other social event" shall mean any adult person who resides in or on the premises, or who rents the premises, involved in such party or social event and is present at such party or social event. For the purposes of this subsection, "noise" shall mean the same sounds, or any combination thereof, as described in paragraphs 1. or 2. above.
(c)
Restrictions for areas within apartments, condominiums, townhouses, duplexes, or other such residential dwelling units.
Except for persons within commercial enterprises that have an adjoining property line or boundary with a residential dwelling unit, it is unlawful for any person to make, continue, or cause to be made or continued any noise in such a manner as to be plainly audible to any other person a distance of five feet beyond the adjoining property line wall or boundary of any apartment, condominium, townhouse, duplex, or other such residential dwelling units with adjoining points of contact.
For the purposes of this subsection, "noise" shall mean human-produced sounds of yelling, shouting, hooting, whistling, singing, or creating any other human-produced sound (including but not limited to the above) or mechanically-produced sounds made by radio-receiving device, television, stereo, musical instrument, phonograph sound amplifier or other machines or devices for the producing, reproducing, or amplifying of sound, or any combination thereof.
For the purposes of this subsection, "property line or boundary" shall mean an imaginary line drawn through the points of contact of (1) adjoining apartments, condominiums, townhouses, duplexes or other such residential dwelling units with adjoining points owned, rented, or leased by different persons; or (2) adjoining common areas or adjoining exterior walls. Said property line or boundary includes all points of a plane formed by projecting the property line or boundary including the ceiling, the floor, and the walls.
(d)
Other restrictions.
1.
Public Property.It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to play, use, operate, or permit to be played, used, or operated any mechanical sound-making device at such a volume and in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from the source of the noises or sounds in or on a public park or other public property.
2.
Sales by "Hawking" or "Barking". It shall be unlawful for any person to offer for sale or sell anything by shouting or outcry within any residential area.
3.
Loading and Unloading. It shall be unlawful for any person to load, unload, open, close or handle boxes, crates, containers, building material, refuse, garbage cans, dumpsters, motor vehicles, or similar objects between the hours of 8:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. of the following day so as to produce noise that is plainly audible across a residential real property boundary line.
4.
Vehicle or Motor Boat Repair or Testing. It shall be unlawful for any person to repair, rebuild, modify or test any motor vehicle, motorcycle or motorboat in such a manner as to produce noise that is plainly audible across a residential real property boundary.
5.
Power Model Vehicles. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or permit the operation of powered model vehicles in a public or private space out-of-doors within 150 feet of a residential real property boundary.
(e)
Power tools and lawn, garden, and tree trimming equipment.
The provisions of this Section shall not apply to the operation of electrical power tools or motor-powered, muffler-equipped lawn, garden and tree trimming equipment by a golf course facility between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and l0:00 p.m. The provisions of this Section shall not apply to the operation of electrical power tools or motor-powered, muffler-equipped lawn, garden and tree trimming equipment by any other user between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m.
(f)
Fireworks. It shall be unlawful to use, ignite, or cause to be ignited any fireworks or consumer fireworks, as defined by O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1, that are plainly audible across a real property boundary line except as follows:
(1)
Consumer fireworks may be used, ignited, or caused to be ignited on any day beginning at the time of 10:00 a.m. and up to and including the ending time of 9:00 p.m. In addition, consumer fireworks may also be used, ignited, or caused to be ignited on January 1, July 3, July 4, and December 31 of each year after the time of 9:00 p.m.. and up to and including the time of 11:59 p.m. and on January 1 of each year beginning at the time of 12:00 Midnight and up to and including the ending time of 1:00 a.m.;
(2)
In addition to those days set forth in subsection 2-9-53(f)(1), consumer fireworks may also be used, ignited, or caused to be ignited on any other day after the time of 9:00 p.m. and up to and including the time of 11:59 p.m. if a permit for such use or ignition has been issued by the Glynn County Police Chief pursuant to ordinance Section 2-9-57;
(3)
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Section, the use or ignition of any fireworks or consumer fireworks is prohibited on any property owned or operated by or under the custody and control of Glynn County unless a permit has been issued by Glynn County pursuant to Chapter 2-26 of the Glynn Code of Ordinances and provided further that any such use or ignition pursuant to such a permit may begin no earlier than 10:00 a.m. and end no later than 11:59 p.m.;
(4)
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Section, the use or ignition of consumer fireworks as provided for herein may not be in a location where such use or ignition is prohibited pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-10-2; and
(5)
Any person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership desiring to conduct a public exhibition or display of fireworks, whether or not before a proximate audience, shall first obtain a permit from the Glynn County Probate Court Judge pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-10-4. Any such public exhibition or display of fireworks for which a permit is issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-10-4 shall be exempt from the provisions of this ordinance pertaining to noise; provided, however, that the permit holder must comply with any and all other applicable county ordinances and state laws, including those county ordinances pertaining to the use of property owned or operated by or under the custody and control of Glynn County.

2-9-54 - Regulation of Sound Equipment and Sound Amplifying Equipment.
(a)
Mechanical Sound-making Devices Permit. Notwithstanding the provisions of this ordinance concerning mechanical sound-making devices, the Chief of Police has the discretion to grant written permission authorizing the use of mechanical sound-making devices at such a volume and in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, noises or sounds which are plainly audible at a distance of 1,000 feet from the source of the noise or sound between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m. Such written authorization may be given for the following:
1.
Public health and safety purposes;
2.
Fairs, carnivals and similar activities;
3.
Parades, processions, excursions and associated festivities;
4.
Outdoor concerts and theatrical performances;
5.
Outdoor neighborhood functions such as lawn and pool parties, street dances and similar activities;
6.
Civic and religious celebrations;
7.
Recreational and athletic activities.
In determining whether to grant such permission, the Chief of Police should consider the following factors:
1.
Level or volume, duration, and types of the proposed noises or sounds;
2.
Source or nature of the noise or sounds (i.e. musical instruments, amplified music, etc.);
3.
Level and intensity of any background noise;
4.
Proximity of sleeping facilities or quiet zones (i.e. hospitals and hotels);
5.
Zoning classification of the area;
6.
Population density of the area;
7.
Whether the noise is recurrent, intermittent, or constant; and
8.
Whether the applicant or location has previously violated this ordinance.
(b)
Commercial Advertising—Sound Equipment Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to use, operate, or permit to be used or operated any mechanical sound-making device which is cast upon the public streets or other public property for the purpose of commercial advertising or which serves to attract the attention of the public to any building, structure, or vehicle in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible from any public streets or other public property.
2-9-55 - Construction.
(a)
Between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m., construction noise of any type, including, but not limited to, noise caused by the erection (including excavation), demolition, alteration, or repair of any building, as well as the operation of any earth-moving equipment, crane, saw, drill, pile driver, steam shovel, pneumatic hammer, hoist, or any similar equipment, shall not be plainly audible across a residential real property boundary. At any other time, the construction noise described above shall not be plainly audible within any residential district more than 300 feet beyond the property boundary from which the noise emanates.
(b)
The provisions of this Section shall not apply to:
1.
Emergency work or repair work performed by or for governmental entities or public service utilities.
2.
The use of domestic power tools or equipment as set forth in Section 2-9-53(e) of this ordinance.
3.
Construction equipment that has attached in good operative condition the best noise-attenuating device as recommended by the manufacturer.
2-9-56 - Animals and Birds.
It shall be unlawful for any person to own, possess or harbor a barking dog, or any animal or fowl which howls, barks, growls, cries, yelps, whines or emits audible sounds that are unreasonably loud or disturbing and which are of such character, intensity and duration as to disturb the peace and quiet of the neighborhood. For the purposes of this Section, "barking dog" shall mean a dog that barks, bays, cries, howls, yelps, whines or makes any other noise continuously and/or incessantly for a period of five minutes or barks intermittently for ten minutes or more to the disturbance of any person at any time of day or night regardless of whether the dog is physically situated in or upon private property; provided, however, that a dog shall not be deemed a "barking dog" for purposes of this ordinance, if at the time the dog is barking or making any other noise, a person is trespassing or threatening to trespass upon private property in or upon which the dog is situated or close proximity thereto, or for any other legitimate cause which teased or provoked the dog.
2-9-57 - Variance.
(a)
Variance Permit. A variance permit to cause or create a noise at sound levels which would otherwise be in violation of this ordinance may be granted by the Chief of Police if:
1.
Additional time is necessary for the applicant to alter or modify the activity in order to comply with the provisions of this ordinance; or
2.
The activity, operation or noise source will be of a temporary duration and cannot be done in a manner that would comply with the provisions of this ordinance; or
3.
No reasonable alternative is available to the applicant.
(b)
Conditions. Any permit granted pursuant to this Section shall contain therein all conditions placed upon holder of said permit as condition to the permit being granted, including but not limited to the effective date, time of day, location, sound level limit or equipment limitation. Application for such a permit shall be made in writing in such form as is acceptable to Glynn County through the office of the Chief of Police.
2-9-58 - Administration Appeals and Penalties.
(a)
The Chief of Police shall implement, administer and enforce the provisions of this ordinance and shall issue orders requiring the abatement of all violations and the revocation of permits issued.
(b)
Any person aggrieved by a decision or order rendered by the Chief of Police, after exhausting all administrative remedies, shall have the right to appeal to the Magistrate Court of Glynn County.
(c)
Each and every day a violation continues shall be deemed a separate offense. Each and every violation of this ordinance, including separate violations on the same day, shall be deemed a separate offense.
(d)
Any person in violation of this ordinance, upon conviction, shall be guilty of an ordinance violation punishable by a warning for the first offense and thereafter punishable by a fine of at least $100.00, but not to exceed $1,000.00; or by imprisonment of at least 24 hours, not to exceed 60 days, or both. Any person who violates this ordinance may be subject to immediate arrest. The imposition of a penalty under the provisions of this Section shall not prevent the revocation of any permit issued pursuant to this ordinance.
2-9-59 - Administration Appeals and Penalties.
(a)
The Chief of Police shall implement, administer and enforce the provisions of this ordinance and shall issue orders requiring the abatement of all violations and the revocation of permits issued.
(b)
Any person aggrieved by a decision or order rendered by the Chief of Police, after exhausting all administrative remedies, shall have the right to appeal to the Magistrate Court of Glynn County.
(c)
Any person in violation of this ordinance, upon conviction, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be subject to such penalties as are provided by law for other misdemeanors. Each and every day a violation continues shall be deemed a separate offense.
