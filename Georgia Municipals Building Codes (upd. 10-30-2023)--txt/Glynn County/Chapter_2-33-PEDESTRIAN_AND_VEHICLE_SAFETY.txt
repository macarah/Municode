Chapter 2-33 - PEDESTRIAN AND VEHICLE SAFETY

2-33-1 - Title.
new
This Chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Pedestrian and Vehicle Safety Ordinance of Glynn County".
( Ord. No. O-2023-07 , § I, 6/1/2023)
2-33-2 - Purpose.
new
This ordinance regulates the time, place, and manner of pedestrians and drivers in right-of-way and shall not apply to anyone exercising their rights to constitutionally-protected activity.
( Ord. No. O-2023-07 , § I, 6/1/2023)
2-33-3 - Definitions.
new
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this Ordinance, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning. When not inconsistent with the context, words in the plural number include the singular number, and words in the singular include the plural number.
Approach shall mean to move nearer in distance to someone.
Designated roadways shall mean the interstate/intrastate system (including interstate/intrastate entrance and exit ramps and any Georgia Department of Transportation right-of-way), and arterial and collector roadways and rights-of-way. For purposes of this definition, collector and arterial roadways and rights-of-way shall be those roadways (and their rights-of-way) classified as a collector or higher in the Subdivision Regulations of Glynn County, Georgia. This definition also encompasses the first 450 feet of local roadways intersecting with these designated roadways and any areas within the rights-of-way not designated or intended for pedestrian use.
The portions of these designated roadways subject to this Chapter include the portions within the area open for vehicular traffic (including medians), plus four feet outside of the shoulders and/or curbs.
Legally parked shall mean a vehicle that is standing, stopped, or parked m an area designated, or legally authorized, for parking.
The traveled portion of a designated roadway shall mean any portion of a designated roadway (including travel lanes, turn lanes, bike lanes and shoulders) that is normally used by moving motor vehicle traffic that is not an area designated for or routinely used as a parking area for the general public.
Median shall mean any area separating traffic lanes on a roadway. A median includes, but is not limited to, any paved or unpaved, marked or unmarked, landscaped or non-landscaped, portions of a roadway which exist between lanes of traffic.
Public rights-of-way shall mean land, property or interest therein, usually but not required to be a strip, acquired for or devoted to a county road, sidewalk, curb, gutter, drainage inlet, manhole or water valve.
Ordinance shall mean the Pedestrian and Vehicle Safety Ordinance of Glynn County and any portion thereof.
( Ord. No. O-2023-07 , § I, 6/1/2023)
2-33-4 - Prohibited Acts.
new
Except as provided herein, it shall be unlawful for any person in the unincorporated areas of Glynn County, excluding Jekyll Island, to make use of the public rights-of-way in a manner that interferes with the safe and efficient movement of people and property from place to place on a public road or right-of-way. Unless otherwise authorized by Glynn County, it shall be unlawful for any person in the unincorporated areas of Glynn County, excluding Jekyll Island, to commit any of the following acts:
(a)
Occupancy of median. It is unlawful for any person to stop, stand, or otherwise occupy or remain in a median on any designated roadway when that person is not in the process of lawfully crossing the road in accordance with applicable traffic and safety laws. Stopping, standing, or otherwise occupying a median through two consecutive opportunities to cross in accordance with applicable traffic and safety laws is prima facie evidence of a violation of this Subsection.
(b)
Physical interaction on roadway. It is unlawful for any person to engage in any physical interaction between a pedestrian and an occupant of a motor vehicle, including but not limited to the transfer of any product or material, while the motor vehicle is not legally parked and is located on the traveled portion of a designated roadway.
(c)
Commercial use of public rights-of-way. It is unlawful to use any public right-of-way for commercial activity. Commercial activity prohibited by this Section includes, but is not limited to, vending or sale of goods, display of goods for sale, storage of goods for sale in connection with commercial activity, or repair or manufacturing of goods. Signage is not "commercial activity" regulated by this Chapter; however, there shall be no signage placed or displayed in any median. Use of a vehicle to transport goods and services is not "commercial activity" regulated by this Chapter.
(d)
Activities on private property. It is unlawful for any person to knowingly approach another person, unless such other person consents, for the purpose of passing any material, item or object to, displaying a sign to, or requesting any material, item or object from such other person on private property if the owner, tenant, or lawful occupant has asked the person not to enter the property, or has posted a sign clearly indicating that approaches are not welcome on the property.
(e)
Activities in certain areas. It is unlawful for any person to knowingly approach another person within six feet of such person, unless such other person consents, for the purpose of passing any material, item or object to, displaying a sign to, or requesting any material, item or object from such other person in the public right-of-way within a radius of 30 feet from:
(1)
Any bus stop;
(2)
The entrance or exit of any public toilet facility; or
(3)
An automatic teller machine (ATM), provided that when an ATM is located within an ATM facility, such distance shall be measured from the entrance or exit of the ATM facility.
( Ord. No. O-2023-07 , § I, 6/1/2023)
2-33-5 - Exemptions.
new
Nothing in this Chapter shall prohibit the following:
(a)
Law Enforcement, fire and rescue, or other government employees or contractors acting within the scope of their lawful authority;
(b)
A person lawfully conducting inspection, construction, maintenance, repair, survey, or other similarly authorized services;
(c)
A person responding to lend aid during an emergency situation or who is within the roadway due to a mechanical or physical defect in his or her vehicle that occurred while driving in the roadway;
(d)
Entering or exiting a bus or other public transit system;
(e)
Use of public roads and rights-of-way that have been closed to vehicular traffic for a special event permitted by the appropriate governmental entity; and
(f)
A person or entity in possession of a valid permit issued pursuant to the Public Conduct Ordinance of Glynn County, so long as such person is in compliance with the requirements of that Chapter and the terms of the permit and safety plan, and any person engaged in the activity related to such permitted organization or individual.
( Ord. No. O-2023-07 , § I, 6/1/2023)
2-33-6 - Penalties.
new
(a)
The Chief of Police shall implement, administer and enforce the provisions of this Ordinance.
(b)
Each and every day a violation continues shall be deemed a separate offense. Each and every violation of this Ordinance, including separate violations on the same day, shall be deemed a separate offense.
(c)
For the first violation of this Ordinance, the Chief of Police or his or her designee shall issue a warning to the person in violation, and shall document the issuance of the warning.
(d)
After receiving a warning pursuant to Section 2-33-6(c) hereof, any person in violation of this Ordinance, upon conviction, shall be guilty of an ordinance violation punishable by a fine of at least $100.00, but not to exceed $1,000.00; or by imprisonment of at least 24 hours, not to exceed 60 days; or both. Any person who violates this Ordinance may be subject to immediate arrest.
( Ord. No. O-2023-07 , § I, 6/1/2023)
