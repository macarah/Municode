Sec. 10-65. - Adopted.
There is hereby adopted by the city, for the purpose of establishing rules and regulations for the construction, alteration, removal, demolition, equipment, use and occupancy, location and maintenance of buildings and structures, that certain building code known as the International Building Code, 2006 edition, as amended by the state department of community affairs, save and except such portions as are hereinafter deleted, modified or amended or are in conflict with other provisions of this chapter or other ordinances of the city. Such code and revisions are hereby adopted and incorporated as a part hereof as fully as if set out at length herein and the provisions thereof shall be controlling in the construction of all buildings and other structures within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1962, § 5-1; Code 1986, § 5-41; Ord. No. 213, § 1, 7-12-1976; Ord. No. 273, § 2, 11-5-1982; Ord. No. 309, § 1, 11-4-1985)
Sec. 10-66. - Fire district.
The fire district of the city shall be as follows: One West Screven—starting at a point 200 yards west of Tallakas Road north 200 yards, then east to Stevens Street continuing east to Highland Street south on Highland to Screven; 200 feet either side of Screven east to Usher Street; south on Usher to Hill Street; west on Hill to Highland; south on Highland to Chapel Street; west on Chapel Street to Lee Street; north on Lee Street to Stewart Street; west on Stewart Street to Walker Street; north on Walker to Forsyth Street; Forsyth to Seymore; south on Seymore to Johnson Street; west on Johnson Street to Laurel Street; south on Laurel 200 feet either side of Hill Street continuing west down Forsyth at Laurel and ending at Sunset Drive; continuing on North Washington 200 feet either side to Courtland; continuing on South Courtland Street to the Seaboard Coastline Railroad—City of Quitman Industrial Park boundaries.
(Code 1962, § 5-4; Code 1986, § 5-44; Ord. No. 273, § 4, 10-5-1982)
Sec. 10-67. - Self-inspection of water and sewer projects by master plumbers.
Pursuant to the authority contained in section 3 of Act 1046 Georgia Laws, 1996 (1996 Ga. Laws (Act. No. 1046) § 3, page 1632), subsection (d) of code section 8-2-26 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. § 8-2-26(d)), relating to self-inspection of water and sewer projects by master plumbers or utility contractors, shall not be applicable within the city.
(Ord. No. 459, § 1, 6-4-1996)
