ARTICLE II. - GUN RANGES

Sec. 6-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial shooting/archery range means an area designated and operated for the sport shooting of firearms or for archery which is not available for such use by the general public without payment of a fee, membership contribution, or dues or by invitation of an authorized person.
(Ord. No. 2017-01, § 1, 9-28-2017)
Sec. 6-20. - Regulation of commercial shooting/archery ranges.
(a)
Any outdoor range shall be designed, constructed, and maintained in accordance with the guidelines and regulations contained in the Range Source Book, section II, chapter 2, Outdoor range design criteria, published by the National Rifle Association (NRA) in 2012, a copy of which is available at the county sheriff's department, with the exception that any regulation established by the ordinance from which this article is derived that stands in contradiction to the NRA guidelines shall control.
(b)
In the case of an outdoor range, it shall not be permitted adjacent to or across the right-of-way from any residential dwelling, unless there is a 2,000-foot setback from the property line adjacent to the dwelling, or consent is obtained from any affected owner of the dwelling for a lesser distance.
(c)
Any outdoor range shall have at least a 300-foot planted or naturally forested buffer or berm from any property line.
(d)
The down-range direction of an outdoor range shall be in a direction that is the least likely to cause any harm or damage in the case of a gross accident, but in no case shall bear directly upon a street, dwelling or place of business.
(e)
A berm of at least 20 feet in height shall run down-range and to the outside of the outdoor range and encompass the shooter's discharge point. At the end of the range there shall be some type of bullet trap, whether earthen or of a manufactured/constructed nature, and which shall be of a suitable height, but no less than 20 feet in the case of an outdoor range.
(f)
There shall be some means of protection between each shooter bench or position, in the case of a lateral discharge.
(g)
Any exterior lighting shall be directed away from adjacent properties.
(h)
A six-foot minimum fence shall completely encompass at least the physical outdoor shooting range.
(i)
Adequate ventilation shall be provided for indoor facilities.
(j)
Operational hours for any outdoor range shall be 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday, and 1:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m. on Sundays, subject to relief from such restriction as determined and granted by the county sheriff on a temporary basis.
(k)
In the case of compound bows, recurved bows or other forms of weapon ranges, as well as the above, the sheriff may waive certain conditions, or place additional conditions, as deemed necessary.
(Ord. No. 2017-01, § 2, 9-28-2017)

ARTICLE III. - ANIMAL FEEDING OPERATIONS

Sec. 6-30. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal feeding operation means an agricultural enterprise where animals are kept and raised in confined situations. AFOs congregate animals, feed, manure, and urine, dead animals, and production operations on a small land area. Feed is brought to the animals rather than the animals grazing or otherwise seeking feed in pastures, fields, or on rangeland.
Poultry house means a confined area containing 50 or more chickens.
( Ord. No. 2021-04 , § 1, 5-4-2021)
Sec. 6-31. - Regulation of animal feeding operations.
Buildings and structures related to animal feeding operations (excluding dairies), including but not limited to, poultry houses, feed lots, hog parlors, hog lots, or other structures containing livestock manure shall be set back a minimum of 400 feet from the front property line, 200 feet from the side and rear property lines, 100 feet from state water and 400 feet from any residence not occupied by the owner or caretaker. Structures related to dairies must be set back 200 feet from any property line. This ordinance does not affect grandfather structures, provided that the above setback or formerly approved variances are maintained.
( Ord. No. 2021-04 , § 1, 5-4-2021)

ARTICLE IV. - OCCUPATIONAL TAX[1]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, adopted June 8, 2021, set out provisions intended for use as article III, §§ 6-27—6-39. For purposes of classification, and at the editor's discretion, these provisions have been included as article IV, §§ 6-37—6-49.

Sec. 6-37. - Purpose.
This article is hereby adopted for the purpose of complying with the state law set forth at O.C.G.A. § 48-13-15 et seq., to base said occupation tax upon the number of employees, and to provide for methods of registration, payment and enforcement thereof.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-38. - Administration and enforcement.
The county commissioners shall administer and enforce the levy, assessment and collection of occupational taxes imposed by this article.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-39. - Construction of terms; definitions.
(a)
Wherever the term "Glascock County" is used herein, such term shall be construed to mean Glascock County, Georgia.
(b)
As used in this article, the term:
(1)
Full-time employee means a person who works in an occupation, trade, profession, business or calling, having a business location within the county, for 30 hours or more per week for four consecutive or non-consecutive weeks during a calendar year, but does not include an independent contractor who pays an occupation tax to any Georgia local government for the business activity involved.
(2)
Number of employees means:
a.
For an occupation, profession, trade, business or calling operating and having a location within the county during the calendar year prior to the year in which the tax is being levied, the number of natural persons employed from time to time as full-time employees during said prior calendar year.
b.
For an occupation, profession, trade, business or calling not operating and having a business location within the county during the calendar year prior to the year in which the tax is being levied, the number of natural persons employed from time to time as full-time employees during the first three months of operation at a location within the county.
(3)
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession, or business for revenue raising purposes.
(4)
Person shall be held to include sole proprietors, corporations, partnerships, nonprofit, or any other form of business organization, but specifically excludes charitable proceeds for charitable purposes.
(5)
Practitioners of professions and occupations shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-40. - Occupation tax levied.
(a)
An occupation tax is levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices in the unincorporated area of Glascock County or upon the applicable out-of-state businesses with no locations or offices in Georgia, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, based upon the number of employees of the business or practitioner.
(b)
Occupation tax schedule:

  (c)
The number of employees includes full-time and full-time equivalent working for the business. The fractional working times of part-time employees are combined and rounded to provide a more accurate estimate of full-time equivalent employees. The owner and other family members working for the business, whether paid or not, shall be included in this count.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-41. - Professionals as classified in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c), paragraphs (1)—(20).
Practitioners of professions or occupations who, by state law, require state license regulating such professions or occupations shall elect as their entire occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax based on number of employees as set forth in section 48-13-9(c).
(2)
A fee of $200.00 per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the office or location of the practitioner.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-42. - Taxes levied based on business locations.
(a)
The county shall not require the payment of more than one occupational tax for each location that a business or practitioner shall have in the county.
(b)
The county shall not require an occupation tax on those real estate brokers, agents, or companies whose offices are located outside the county and sell property in the county.
(c)
An occupation tax is hereby levied on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the State of Georgia if the largest dollar volume of the business in Georgia is in Glascock County and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of Glascock County.
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of Glascock County.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-43. - Practitioners exclusively practicing for the government.
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by, and who is employed in practice exclusively by, the United States, the states, a municipality or county of the state, instrument abilities of the United States, the states, or a municipality or county of the states shall not be required to pay an occupation tax fee for that practice.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-44. - Businesses not covered by the ordinance.
The following businesses are not covered by the provisions of this article but may be assessed an occupation tax or other type of tax pursuant to the provisions of other general laws of the state or by local law:
(1)
Those businesses regulated by the state public service commission.
(2)
Those electrical service businesses organized under O.C.G.A. tit. 46, ch. 3. Cooperative marketing associations governed by O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105.
(3)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.
(4)
Motor common carriers governed by O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15.
(5)
Those businesses governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355. (Businesses that purchase carload lots of guano, meats, flour, bran, cottonseed, or cottonseed meal and oils.)
(6)
Agricultural products and livestock raised in the State of Georgia, governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356.
(7)
Depository financial institutions governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(8)
Facilities operated by a charitable trust, governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-55.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-45. - Purpose and scope of tax and fees.
(a)
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade or calling.
(b)
Regulatory fees paid by businesses or practitioners are to defray cost for investigations or inspections of such businesses or practitioners as a protection to the public health, safety, or welfare, or in the course of enforcing a state or local building, health, or safety code.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-46. - When tax due and payable; effects of transacting business when taxes delinquent.
(a)
All occupation taxes and regulatory fees levied by the article shall be due and payable annually, on January 1. In the event a person commences business on any date after January 1 in any year, the tax shall be pro-rated and shall be due and payable on the date of the commencement of the business for the part of the year remaining.
(b)
Should occupation tax or regulatory fees imposed by this article remain due and unpaid for 90 days from the due date of the tax or fees, the person liable for the tax or fee shall be subject to, and shall pay, a penalty of ten percent of the tax or fee.
(c)
When any person commences business on or after January 1 in any year, the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax imposed for the entire year.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-47. - Evidence of qualification required, if applicable.
(a)
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualifications, certificates of competency, or any other regulatory matter, shall first, before the issuance of a county registration, show evidence of such qualification.
(b)
Each person who is licensed by the state shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the place of business of the licensee, and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(c)
Each person who is licensed by the secretary of state, pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 43 shall provide evidence of proper and current state licensure before the county registration may be issued.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-48. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
Violations of this article, or of any other ordinance or regulation of the county, shall constitute grounds for suspension or revocation of a business registration. The provisions and requirements of this article shall be enforced by injunction.
(b)
Penalties. Violations of the article shall be tried in the Magistrate Court of Glascock County as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63; or, upon accusation by the county attorney, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-62, and shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed an amount county attorney is designated as prosecuting attorney on behalf of the county. Citations for violations of the article shall be issued by agent of the county authorized for the purpose by resolution of the board of commissioners.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
Sec. 6-49. - Option to establish exemption or reduction in occupational tax.
The board of commissioners may, by subsequent ordinance or resolution, provide for an exemption or reduction in occupation tax to one or more types of businesses or practitioners or occupations or professions as part of a plan for economic development. Such exemptions or reductions shall not be arbitrary or capricious and the reasons shall be set forth in the minutes of the board.
( Ord. No. 2021-07 , § 1, 6-8-2021)
