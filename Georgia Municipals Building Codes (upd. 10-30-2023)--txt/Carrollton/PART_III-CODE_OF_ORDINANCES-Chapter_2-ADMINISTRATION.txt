ARTICLE II. - MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Elections, ch. 42.

Sec. 2-26. - Time and place for regular meetings.
(a)
The mayor and city council shall convene for business on the first Monday in each month, with such meetings being conducted at 6:00 p.m.
(b)
The meetings of the mayor and city council, and all public meetings of boards and agencies of the city shall be at the council chamber at 115 West Center Street, Carrollton, Georgia, or such alternative place within the corporate limits of the city as the mayor and city council may from time to time designate. Provided, however, that if the mayor and city council designate a place other than the council chamber at 115 West Center Street, Carrollton, Georgia for a particular regular meeting, due notice of such alternative place shall be given in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).
(Code 1975, § 2-38; Res. of 7-15-91; Res. No. 04-10, 4-5-04; Res. No. 13-2014, 12-1-14; Res. No. 03-2015, 5-4-15; Res. No. 01-2016, 1-4-16)
State Law reference— Meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1; requirement to prescribe the time, place and dates of regular meetings of governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).
Sec. 2-27. - Special meetings.
(a)
Special meetings of the mayor and city council may be called at any time by the mayor or by any two councilmen. Notice of such special meeting shall be given to the city manager, whose duty it shall be to serve or cause to be served on the remaining members of the council notice of such special meeting at least six hours before it is due to convene.
(b)
Due notice of such special meetings shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).
(Code 1975, § 2-39)
State Law reference— Due notice requirements for other than regular meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); excluded proceedings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-3.
Sec. 2-28. - Quorum.
Any three members of the city council, or any two members and the mayor, shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.
(Code 1975, § 2-40)
Sec. 2-29. - Vote required.
A majority of the votes cast shall be necessary to an action by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1975, § 2-41)
Sec. 2-30. - Presiding officer designated.
(a)
The mayor shall be chairman of the city council when present. In the absence of the mayor, the mayor pro tem shall preside, and in the absence of the mayor and the mayor pro tem, any councilman chosen by the council may preside.
(b)
At the first regularly scheduled meeting in each year, the mayor and city council shall elect by majority vote one councilman to serve as the mayor pro tem for a one-year term.
(Code 1975, § 2-42; Res. No. 02-2016, 1-4-16)
Sec. 2-31. - Duties of presiding officer; appeals from chair.
The chairman shall preserve order and decorum, appoint all committees of the city council unless otherwise directed by the council, and perform such other duties and exercise such other powers as are usual and customary and authorized or required by parliamentary law.
(Code 1975, § 2-43)
Sec. 2-32. - Order of business; agenda.
The order of business at all meetings of the mayor and city council shall be in accordance with an agenda to be approved at the beginning of the meeting. In the absence of an emergency or other exigent circumstances, the Friday before the regular meeting of the mayor and city council set forth in section 2-26 or at least one business day prior to any special meeting of the mayor and city council set forth in section 2-27, the city manager shall:
(1)
Make available to the mayor and city council a proposed agenda and any supporting documentation for the meeting; and
(2)
Cause to be posted at the meeting site the proposed agenda.
In addition to any items placed on the agenda by the city manager, the mayor or any two city councilmen may request that an item be placed on the agenda, in which event the city manager shall add the item to the agenda.
(Code 1975, § 2-44; Res. No. 04-2016, 1-4-16; Res. No. 01-2020, 1-6-20)
Sec. 2-33. - Rules of order.
In the absence of provisions to the contrary, meetings of the mayor and city council shall be generally governed by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised.
(Code 1975, § 2-45)
Sec. 2-34. - Salary.
The mayor and city councilmen shall be paid such salary as the mayor and city council shall fix at the beginning of their terms, to be paid at such times as it may fix, provided that it shall not be diminished or increased during their term of office.
(Code 1975, § 2-46)
Sec. 2-35. - Reimbursement of expenses.
In addition to their regular salaries, the mayor and city councilmembers shall be reimbursed for their reasonable expenses.
(Code 1975, § 2-47)
Sec. 2-36. - Mayor may vote.
The mayor shall have the right to vote on any question coming before the city council, whether in case of a tie vote or not, except upon questions where he is disqualified by reason of interest.
(Code 1975, § 2-48)
Sec. 2-37. - Minutes required.
Minutes of all meetings of the mayor and city council shall be kept accurately reflecting all business transacted. Such minutes show the members present and, on request of any member or the mayor, shall show how each member votes on matters coming before the council.
(Code 1975, § 2-50)
Sec. 2-38. - When ordinances to take effect.
Unless otherwise provided all ordinances passed by the mayor and city council shall take effect immediately after passage.
(Code 1975, § 2-51)
State Law reference— Authority to adopt municipal ordinances, resolutions or regulations, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-3.
Sec. 2-39. - Members may supervise or investigate city matters.
The mayor and city council may name any member of the council to supervise or investigate any city function or matter of city business.
(Code 1975, § 2-66)
Secs. 2-40—2-60. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - CITY MANAGER

Sec. 2-81. - Office of supervisor created.
In accordance with 1961 Ga. Laws, page 2945, there is hereby created the office of supervisor of the City of Carrollton, who shall hold office at such time and at such compensation, and perform such duties as the mayor and city council may direct or prescribe.
(Code 1975, § 2-63)
Sec. 2-82. - Supervisor to be called manager.
The city supervisor shall have the title and be known as the city manager, and any reference in this Code or any ordinance or resolution of the city to the city manager shall be interpreted to mean the city supervisor authorized by 1961 Ga. Laws, page 2945.
(Code 1975, § 2-64)
Sec. 2-83. - Powers, duties.
(a)
The city manager shall be general supervisor and in control of the affairs of the city, and shall have power to collect taxes, tax returns, licenses, make purchases for and on behalf of the city and for all departments; and all complaints shall be filed with him, and such investigations as necessary made by him, and reports of all his actions and doings shall be made to the mayor and city council; all under the immediate direction, supervision, control and approval of the mayor and city council.
(b)
The city manager shall perform such other duties as directed by the mayor and city council from time to time. No contracts shall be made by the manager on behalf of the city without the consent and approval in writing of the mayor and city council. All actions of the city manager are to be subject to the approval of the mayor and city council.
(c)
The city manager shall be ex-officio clerk of the city and shall perform any and all duties of the clerk at any time and all times as may be necessary or expedient.
(Code 1975, § 2-65)
Secs. 2-84—2-105. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - CITY CLERK AND TREASURER

Sec. 2-106. - Offices combined; appointment, functions.
The offices of city clerk and treasurer are hereby combined. In view of the fact that the city manager is declared to be ex officio city clerk by 1961 Ga. Laws, page 2945, the council shall appoint a deputy clerk who shall have the title of city clerk and treasurer and whose functions as clerk shall be subject to the control and direction of the manager.
(Code 1975, § 2-78)
Sec. 2-107. - Duties of clerk.
The city clerk and treasurer shall have the following duties as city clerk to:
(1)
Seal and attest documents. The clerk shall seal and attest all documents as shall require this formality.
(2)
Keep minutes, preserve ordinances. The clerk shall keep the minutes of the city council and preserve all ordinances enacted in an ordinance book.
(3)
Keep records. In addition to the record of ordinances and other records which the clerk is required to keep, he shall keep a register of all licenses and permits issued, and the payments thereon; a record showing all of the officers and regular employees of the city; and such other records as may be required by the city council.
(4)
Have custody and use of seal. The clerk shall be the custodian of the city seal, and shall fix its impressions on documents whenever this is required.
(5)
Have custody of documents. The clerk shall be the custodian of all documents belonging to the city which are not assigned to the custody of some other officer.
(6)
Keep index. The clerk shall keep and maintain a proper index to all documents and records kept by him, so that ready access thereto may be had.
(7)
Maintain Code. The clerk shall maintain this Code of Ordinances and shall insert or note all amendments hereto.
(8)
Perform additional duties as required. In addition to the duties herein provided, the clerk shall perform such other duties and functions as may be required by the council.
(Code 1975, § 2-79)

DIVISION 4. - CITY ATTORNEY[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
Cross reference— Courts, ch. 38.

Sec. 2-131. - Appointment.
The city attorney shall be appointed by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1975, § 2-92)
Sec. 2-132. - Powers and duties.
The city attorney shall be head of the legal department of the city and supervise such assistants as may be appointed. The city attorney shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
Opinions. The city attorney shall give his legal opinion upon all questions of law arising out of any ordinances, suits, claims, or demands for or against the city, and shall give such opinion in writing unless otherwise so requested, which such opinions shall be filed and preserved among the records of the city clerk.
(2)
Legal advisor. He shall act as legal advisor to all city officials, boards and commissions as far as their or its official duties are concerned, when requested to do so by such officials, boards and commissions.
(3)
Ordinances. He shall report to the city council any defects that he may discover in any ordinance, and shall draw such ordinances as he may be requested by the city council.
(4)
Contracts and litigation. He shall, when directed to do so by any city official, board or commission, draw all contracts between the city and other parties, prosecute and defend all suits or other matters in which the city shall be a party, take appeals in such cases as the interest of the city requires, and prosecute or defend the city's interest in all the courts of the state and of the United States, all subject, however, to the direction of the city council.
(5)
Sign legal papers. He is authorized to sign the name of the city to bonds and papers of whatever kind necessary in legal proceedings for the prosecution of any suit in court when directed to do so by the city council.
(6)
Deliver effects of office to successor. He shall transmit to his successor a docket of all cases pending wherein the city is a party, as well as all books, papers and documents in his possession belonging to the city.
(Code 1975, § 2-93)

DIVISION 3. - STADIUM AUTHORITY[7]

Footnotes:
--- (7) ---
Cross reference— Parks and recreation, ch. 70.

Sec. 2-176. - Created.
There is hereby created and established an authority to be known as the Carrollton Stadium Authority referred to in this division as the stadium authority.
(Code 1975, § 17-19)
Sec. 2-177. - Composition; meetings.
The stadium authority shall consist of the board of directors of the Carrollton Booster Club, Inc., and their successors in office with the terms of members of such authority to correspond with the terms of office of the directors of such booster club. Such authority may adopt the bylaws of the booster club as their own and their meetings may be either joint or separate.
(Code 1975, § 17-20)
Sec. 2-178. - Officers; bylaws.
The stadium authority shall elect its own officers from within its membership and shall adopt bylaws for the conduct of its own affairs.
(Code 1975, § 17-21)
Sec. 2-179. - Compensation of members.
The service of the members of the stadium authority shall be a public service and the members shall receive no compensation; however, they may be reimbursed for expenses out of funds of the authority.
(Code 1975, § 17-22)
Sec. 2-180. - Reports, budgets.
The stadium authority shall furnish to the mayor and city council at least annually a statement of its financial condition and submit a budget for the approval of the mayor and city council prior to October 1 of each year.
(Code 1975, § 17-23)
Sec. 2-181. - General powers.
The powers of the stadium authority shall include, but not be limited to, the scheduling, leasing or contracting for the use of the stadium by the general public and such persons as the authority may deem advisable, including the fixing of rentals, the granting of concessions and the general operation of such stadium.
(Code 1975, § 17-24)
Sec. 2-182. - Facilities managed by authority.
The stadium authority shall be charged with the management and operation of the stadium to be constructed adjacent to the senior high school of the city and such other facilities as may be transferred to it by the mayor and city council, the school board or the Carrollton Parks, Recreation and Cultural Arts Department.
(Code 1975, § 17-25)
Sec. 2-183. - Action by council on authority activities.
(a)
Minutes of all meetings of the stadium authority shall be forwarded to the city clerk and by him to the mayor and city council, who shall consider the action of all previously unconsidered minutes at its regular council meeting.
(b)
The mayor and city council may override any action by the stadium authority at the first meeting of the council after the adoption of the action by the authority. The failure to override the action of the authority by the council at the next regular meeting of the council after receipt of the minutes by the clerk shall ratify and affirm the action of the authority.
(Code 1975, § 17-26)
Secs. 2-184—2-195. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - PLANNING COMMISSION[8]

Footnotes:
--- (8) ---
Cross reference— Buildings and building regulations, ch. 18; historical preservation, ch. 62; subdivisions, app. A; zoning, app. B.
State Law reference— Coordinated and comprehensive planning by counties and municipalities, O.C.G.A. § 36-70-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-196. - Commission established.
The Carrollton Planning Commission, referred to throughout this division as the planning commission, is hereby created and established to be organized and empowered as provided in this division.
(Code 1975, § 18-1)
Sec. 2-197. - Appointment, terms, vacancies, removal, compensation.
The planning commission shall consist of nine members. Members shall be appointed by the mayor and city council. The terms of the members shall be for three years. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the mayor and city council, who shall have the authority to remove any city-appointed member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1975, § 18-2)
Sec. 2-198. - Commission organization, rules, staff and finances.
The planning commission shall elect its chairman from among its members. The term of the chairman shall be one year with eligibility for reelection. The planning commission may appoint a secretary who may be an officer or employee of the municipality. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting. All meetings of the planning commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public, and all records of the planning commission shall be public records. The planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require. The expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the city council.
(Code 1975, § 18-3)
