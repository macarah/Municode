ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business means any business, trade, occupation, profession, avocation, or calling of any kind, subject, by the provisions of this article, to a business license fee.
Engaged in business. Any person shall be deemed to be engaged in business, and thus subject to the requirements of this article, when he performs any act of selling any goods or services or solicits business or offers goods or services for sale for payment in an attempt to make a profit, including the sales or services of the character as made by a wholesaler or retailer or involved in any of the functions performed as a manufacturer, either as an owner, operator, or agent in any business, trade, profession, occupation within the city.
Manufacturer means a person who, either directly or by contracting with others for the necessary labor or mechanical services, manufacturers for sale or commercial use any articles, substances or commodities; including all the activities of a commercial nature wherein labor or skill is applied, by hand or by machinery, to materials so that as a result thereof a new, different, or useful article of tangible personal property or substance of trade or commerce is produced, and including the production or fabrication of specialty or custom-made articles, and including the making, fabricating, processing, refining, mixing, slaughtering, packing, aging, curing, preserving, canning, and preparing and freezing of fresh foods, fruits, vegetables, and meats.
Retailer means a person who sells to the consumer or any other person for any purpose other than for resale in the form of tangible personal property.
Services means accommodating or performing a duty or work by a person utilizing time or talents for direct or indirect remuneration.
Wholesaler means a person who sells to jobbers or to another person other than the consumer anything in the form of tangible personal property.
(Code 1981, § 6-1)
Sec. 14-2. - Registration required.
Every person engaging in any business in the city upon which a license fee is due under this article, shall register said business with the city clerk on or before February 15 of each year, upon forms to be supplied by the city clerk, giving the name, address and type of business and such other information as may be required by the city for the purpose of determining the amount of the license fee to be assessed by this article.
(Code 1981, § 6-2)
Sec. 14-3. - Business license issued; violation for failure to pay and obtain license.
(a)
Upon payment of the occupation tax, administrative fee, and regulatory fee, or any such combination thereof as required in chapter 22, the applicant, person, business, trade, profession, or occupation shall receive a business license which shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business.
(b)
It shall be a violation of this article for any person to operate or conduct a business, trade, profession, or occupation in the city, or to exercise, conduct or operate any business subject to an occupation tax or regulatory fee, without having first filed a return for payment of the applicable occupation tax, administrative fee, and regulatory fee (or such combination as applicable to such person or business) as required by chapter 22. Where a person is subject to paying an occupation tax, administrative fee, and regulatory fee, such person shall obtain one business license which shall reflect payment of all such charges.
Sec. 14-4. - Penalties.
Any person failing to register and pay the license fee due under the terms of this article on or before February 15, or in the case of a new business, within five days of commencing business, shall be subject to a penalty as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-21(a), and interest on any amount due on the license fee shall be at the rate of 1.5 percent per month.
(Code 1981, § 6-4)
Sec. 14-5. - Enforcement.
The city clerk shall issue executions for the collection of all outstanding business license fees and any penalty or fee assessed by this article. Such executions shall be collected in the manner provided by law for the collection of taxes due in the city, or by any reasonable means authorized by Charter section 6.18.
(Code 1981, § 6-9)
Sec. 14-6. - Exemption for new industries.
New industrial plants in the city employing 50 or more employees shall be issued a business license for the first year or portion thereof of operation at no cost.
(Code 1981, § 6-10)
Sec. 14-7. - Schedule of license fees.
The business license fees will be assessed and re-evaluated by the mayor and council during the month of November in each year immediately preceding the year in which fees are to be levied, using the criteria set forth in chapter 13 of title 48 of the O.C.G.A. A copy of these fees shall be maintained in the office of the city clerk and made available for public examination.
(Code 1981, § 6-11)
Secs. 14-8—14-34. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - INSURANCE

Sec. 14-35. - Annual license fee
There is hereby enacted for all insurance companies or independent agents, and all other insurers doing business within the corporate limits of the city, unless excluded under state law, an annual license fee of $15.00.
Sec. 14-36. - Administrative provisions.
The fees described herein shall become due and payable on January 1. Any such license fee duly imposed by the mayor and council, not paid by February 15 shall be delinquent.
(Code 1981, § 6-21)
Sec. 14-37. - Enforcement.
The fees levied by this article may be enforced by execution in the same manner as taxes owed to the city, or by any reasonable means authorized by Charter section 6.18. A violation of this article shall be grounds for refusing or revoking the license of the violator and the companies represented, and the person responsible shall be punished, upon conviction, in accordance with section 1-13.
(Code 1981, § 6-22)
