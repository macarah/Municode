ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-2—40-6-395 of that chapter known as the Uniform Rules of the Road and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the county, with like effect as if recited in this section.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished in accordance with section 1-11, unless otherwise provided by state law.
Sec. 62-2. - Unattended vehicles prohibited on right-of-way.
It shall be unlawful to park any vehicle continuously on any street in the unincorporated areas of the county for longer than a four-hour period.
(Res. of 5-20-97)
Editor's note— A resolution of May 20, 1997, did not specifically amend the Code; hence, inclusion of the substantive provisions of such resolution as § 62-2 was at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 62-3. - Pedestrians prohibited on Echeconnee Creek bridge; fishing from bridge or right-of-way.
(a)
Pedestrians are prohibited on the bridge on State Route 247 across Echeconnee Creek.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any individual to fish in any public or private waterway in the county while standing on a bridge or right-of-way.
(c)
Conviction for either of the offenses specified in subsections (a) and (b) of this section shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $35.00 and not more than $100.00.
(Code 1976, § 7-1002)
Sec. 62-4. - Parking prohibited on bridge at Houston Lake Dam and on Lake Joy Dam on Lake Joy Road.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for anyone to park a vehicle of any kind along the shoulders of State Route 127 within 200 yards of either end of the bridge located at Houston Lake Dam in Houston County, Georgia.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for anyone to park a vehicle of any kind at any point on Lake Joy Dam within the right-of-way of Lake Joy road in Houston County, Georgia.
(c)
Conviction of a violation of this section shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $35.00 and not more than $100.00.
(Min. of 7-19-94)
Sec. 62-5. - Parking prohibited along Moody Road at Moody Lake.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any individual to park a vehicle of any kind at any point along the right-of-way of Moody Road at Lake Moody.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any individual to fish in any public or private waterway in the county while standing on a bridge or public right-of-way.
(c)
Conviction of either of these offenses specified in subsections (a) or (b) of this section shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $35.00 and not more than $100.00.
(Res. of 8-2-05)
Sec. 62-6. - Truck traffic prohibited on Napier Avenue.
In order to help ensure the safety and protect the property of the individuals living on Napier Avenue and the inadequacy of the road to support heavy truck traffic, no trucks over three axles shall be allowed to travel on Napier Avenue.
(Amd. of 3-7-17)
Sec. 62-7. - Three-way stop at the intersection of Abercorn Street and Bull Street.
In order to help ensure the safety and the proper flow of traffic for the traveling public at the intersection of Abercorn Street and Bull Street a three-way stop shall be erected.
(Amd. of 9-18-18)
Secs. 62-8—62-35. - Reserved.

The ordinances and resolutions from which such traffic schedule derives are not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect.

Sec. 62-36. - Generally.
(a)
Residential areas.
(1)
Unless otherwise approved by the board of commissioners, there shall be a standard speed limit of 30 miles per hour or the designed speed, whichever is lower, established for the streets of all residential neighborhoods that are developed after November 28, 1989.
(2)
The responsibility for posting speed zones in residential area developments shall be determined by the public works department of the county.
(3)
Those signs used for the posting of residential speed zones shall have a white background and black lettering.
(4)
Any request made for a variance from the standard speed zone shall be heard and determined based upon the recommendations of the sheriff's department and approval by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Nonresidential and unincorporated areas. Unless otherwise approved by the board of commissioners, there shall be a standard speed limit of 45 miles per hour established for the nonresidential and unincorporated areas of the county. Variances from the standard speed zone are to be determined by the board of commissioners based upon the recommendations of the public works department.
(c)
Continuation of existing speed limits. All deviations from subsections (a) and (b) of this section established prior to November 28, 1989, shall remain in effect as authorized by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1976, §§ 7-1006—7-1008; Amend. of 10-6-09)
