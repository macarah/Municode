ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 34-1. - Provisions saved from repeal.
Section 4-2-23 of the 1982 Code on the subject of open burning is not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect pending a revision of the subject matter by the city.
Secs. 34-2—34-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OPEN BURNING[2]

--- (2) ---
Editor's note— An ordinance adopted Nov. 7, 2006,, supplied provisions to be added to the Code as §§ 35-1—35-16. At the discretion of the editor, with the concurrence of the city, these provisions have been included as §§ 34-21—34-36 to read as set out herein.

Sec. 34-21. - In general.
No person shall cause suffer, allow or permit open burning in any area within the city limits until first obtaining a permit from the city marshal.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-22. - Allowances.
After obtaining said permit, open burning will be allowed for the following purposes:
(1)
Reduction of leaves on the premises on which they fall by the person in control of the premises.
(2)
Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for the production or harvesting of crops. (Agricultural zoning only)
(3)
Fires set for purposes of training fire-fighting personnel when all state agency guidelines are met.
(4)
Disposal of tree limbs from storm damage.
(5)
For weed abatement, disease and pest prevention.
(6)
Open burning in other than predominantly residential areas for the purpose of land clearing or construction or right-of-way maintenance provided the following conditions are met:
a.
Prevailing winds at the time of the burning are away from the major portion of the area's population;
b.
The location of the burning is at least 1,000 feet from any dwelling located in a predominantly residential area;
c.
The amount of dirt on or in the material being burned is minimized;
d.
Heavy oils, asphalt materials, items containing natural or synthetic rubber, or any materials other than plant growth are not being burned; and
e.
No more than one pile 60 feet by 60 feet, or equivalent, is being burned within a nine-acre area at one time.
(7)
Disposal of all packing materials previously containing explosives, in accordance with U. S. Department of Labor Safety Regulations.
[(8)
Reserved.]
(9)
Open burning of vegetative material; for the purpose of land clearing using an air curtain destructor provided the following conditions are met:
a.
Authorization for such burning is received from the state forestry office;
b.
The location of the air curtain destructor is at least 300 feet from any occupied structure or public road. Air curtain destructors used solely for utility line clearing or road clearing may be located at lesser distance upon approval by the EPA and DNR divisions;
c.
No more than one air curtain destructor is operated within a ten-acre area at one time or there must be at least 1,000 feet between any two air curtain destructors;
d.
Only wood waste consisting of trees, logs, large brush and stumps which are relatively free of soil are burned in the air curtain destructor;
e.
Tires or other rubber products, plastics, heavy oils or asphaltic based or impregnated materials are not used to start or maintain the operation of the air destructors;
f.
The air curtain destructor is constructed, installed and operated in a manner consistent with good air pollution control practice for minimizing emissions of fly ash and smoke; and
g.
The cleaning out the air curtain destructor pit is performed in a manner to prevent fugitive duct.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-23. - Authority to put out fires.
The city marshal, fire chief, police chief, or city administrator shall have the authority to order any fire put out if it determined there is a danger to public safety, a nuisance or significant environmental harm.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-24. - Time of permitted burning.
All permitted burning shall be carried out between dawn and dusk and all fires shall extinguished by sunset.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-25. - Location of permitted burning.
All burning shall be located on private property so as not to interfere with traffic on streets or sidewalks.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-26. - Certain organizations exempt.
Organizations such as fire departments, churches, and groups organized for the training and education of children (such as Boys Scouts, Girl Scouts, and Schools) may obtain a permit without complying with section 34-23.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-27. - Safety measures.
During any open burning, a competent person at least 18 years of age shall be present during the entire burning process and control such burning. Such person shall have a hose connected to a water source or other fire extinguishing equipment readily available for use.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-28. - No burn days.
No burning of any kind shall be allowed within the city on a no burn day declared by the Georgia Forestry Commission.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-29. - Conditions of permit.
All permits are issued on a daily basis and are good only for the of issue and while weather conditions support a safe burn.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-30. - Amount of fires.
Only one fire may be in progress at any time except as noted in subsection 34-22(9)c.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-31. - Refusal of permit.
A permit may be refused if the product can more safely be removed than it can be burned, or other services are reasonably available which could remove or take the product. The decision of the city is final.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-32. - Distances of burns.
No fire may be burned closer than 50 feet to any building, structure or any other improvement to the subject real property.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-33. - No burning certain items.
The following items may not be burned: tires, any item which may emit gaseous vapors or odors, any items where odors are likely to be injurious to public health, safety and welfare, any hazardous materials as the same are defined by the state and/or federal laws, statutes and regulations. Nothing in this section shall prohibit the burning of non-treated wood.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec 34-34. - Authority of officials.
The city marshal, fire chief, police chief, city administrator, U.S. Forestry Service or Georgia Forestry Commission has the authority to enter any property to inspect items to be burned, and to return to inspect progress of burning and to insure the fire is out.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-35. - Allowed burning without a permit.
The following are allowed burning by the state without obtaining a permit:
(1)
For the purposes of cooking food, such as grilling.
(2)
Operation of devices using open flames such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters, and other flame-making equipment where approved safety measured are used and state guideline are followed.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
Sec. 34-36. - Penalty.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, as provided in section 1-12 and upon conviction thereof shall be subject to a not more than $1,000.00. Each day a violation is committed to continue shall be deemed as a separate offense. Any person violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall become liable to the city for any expense, loss or damage caused by the violation. The violation of any provision of this chapter may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings in the courts of competent jurisdiction in the state. Equitable proceedings for restraining order or injunction in the superior court may be maintained, notwithstanding the facts that the violation also constitutes a crime, and notwithstanding the other adequate of law may exist.
(Ord. of 11-7-2006)
