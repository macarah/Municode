ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Compost material means vegetable material, yard waste, leaves, tree trimmings, and grass clippings.
Curbside means the side of the pavement bordered by the curb that is on the property owner's side of the curb line.
Dry solid waste means old furniture, appliances, Styrofoam, dog and cat litter, razor blades, wire, nonrecyclable plastics, glass and paper products, and other non- decomposable materials.
Garbage means all vegetable matter, food scraps, baby diapers, ice cream cartons, tissue paper, food packaging, nonrecyclable materials, and other deleterious substances from kitchens, grocery stores, restaurants, drugstores, and hotel rooms, not to include sewage.
Recyclable materials means plastic beverage bottles and jugs, glass bottles and jars, corrugated cardboard, newspaper, bimetal and aluminum cans, magazines, telephone books, automobile batteries, used motor oil, dry paint cans, metal, and large appliances.
Scavenging means the salvaging of or rummaging through the recyclable materials, garbage, disposed products or solid waste of another by any person, who is not authorized by the city to pick up and remove the recyclable materials, garbage or solid waste, without the consent of the owner or generator of the recyclable materials, garbage or solid waste.
Trash means boxes, broken toys, old garbage cans.
(Code 1977, §§ 22-20, 22-40; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 8-16-21; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 86-2. - Preparation of waste; placing trashcans for collection; bundling.
modified
(a)
If the house, building or premises from which the trash is to be collected and removed is adjacent to any alley, the owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of such premises shall be required to keep the trashcan at the entrance of the alley in order that it may be easily accessible to the person collecting and removing the trash. If it is not practicable to collect and remove the trash from an alley, the owner, occupant, tenant or lessee of the premises shall place the cans at such point as the proper agent of the city shall find and designate to be the most accessible for collecting and removing.
(b)
If trash is of such a nature that it cannot be put in the receptacle, it shall be carefully placed in tied bundles or other suitable containers under 60 pounds so it can be removed conveniently.
(c)
Trash of this nature including hedge cuttings shall be placed adjacent to the street for easy pickup.
(d)
Heavy accumulation such as construction or remodeling waste, brick, broken concrete, dirt, mulch, sand and gravel, automobile frames and parts, dead trees and other bulky, heavy material shall be disposed of at the expense of owner or person controlling the same.
(e)
All garbage or trash that is mixed with water or other liquids shall be drained before putting in the garbage or trashcan or receptacle.
(f)
All animal matter that is subject to decomposition may be placed at the curbside for disposal by the sanitation department or animal control officer. All animal matter must be properly bagged.
(g)
Any business, school or apartment house having service as listed under "containerized pick-up service" shall not be subject to the restrictions of subsections (d) and (e) of this section.
(h)
All garbage or trash must be properly bagged in trash bags. Absolutely no loose debris in carts.
(Code 1977, § 22-27; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-2.1. - Prohibited solid waste.
modified
The following items shall be prohibited for disposal within the city: hazardous waste, any item containing refrigerant, pressure cylinders, oxygen tanks, paint, rubber tires, yard waste, inert building material, motor oil, and cooking oil. These items are to be disposed of at the appropriate recycling facility or approved landfill.
(Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 8-16-21; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-2.2. - Services provided.
modified
(a)
Garbage collection. The city provides once weekly collection of garbage and dry solid waste.
(b)
Composting. The Keep Smyrna Beautiful Commission provides educational materials on composting in one's backyard.
(c)
Curbside recycling. The city or its contractor provides weekly collection of recyclable materials. The plastic container will be provided by the city. This fee will be included on the monthly sanitation bill from the city.
(d)
Yard waste program. The city will provide weekly collection of small limbs, shrubs, and bagged leaves and branches. This material is to be no more than six inches in diameter, no greater than five feet in length, and must be separated from the garbage. Pick up is limited to two piles no greater than five feet in height each week. Each residence will receive one yard waste collection per week, except during weeks holidays when fall, when no yard waste is collected. Yard waste bags may not exceed 20 pounds each and each property is limited to 20 bags per pick-up. Bags must be composed of recyclable paper. The city will not pick up dirt, rocks, mulch, logs or stumps. The city will not be responsible for contactors or tree arborist debris.
(Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 8-16-21; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-2.3. - Charge for pickup—Furniture and large appliances.
modified
The city will impose a fee per item in accordance with the schedule adopted by council for the collection of large furniture such as sofas, beds, and tables, and large appliances such as, hot water heaters, and stoves placed at street curb. The city will not collect items identified in section 86-2.1.
(Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 8-16-21; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-2.4. - Same—Abandoned household items.
modified
Upon collection by the city of abandoned household items, the owner of the property from whence they originated shall pay a fee that will be determined on a case by case basis, based on costs incurred by the city.
(Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-3. - Deposits.
Any person desiring to operate a business in the city shall pay a deposit for sanitation services. The deposit shall be held by the city upon the closing of the business until after the rendering of the final bill due the city for services. The amount of deposit shall be set by mayor and council.
(Code 1977, § 22-28)
Sec. 86-4. - Service rates—Generally.
modified
Rates or charges for city sanitation service shall be as adopted by the mayor and council, and available through the city clerk's office. Service rates shall periodically adjust in the same manner and at the same percentage that the rates that suppliers or service providers charge the city, and the adjustment shall become effective at the time that the new rate charged by the supplier is effective.
(Code 1977, § 22-29; Ord. No. 2013-01 , 1-22-13; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-5. - Same—Carnivals at shopping centers.
For carnivals, setting up in the various shopping centers, the city shall install two six-yard containers. The fee for such containers shall be collected at the time the occupation tax certificates are issued.
(Code 1977, § 22-30; Ord. No. 94-18, § 3, 12-5-94)
Sec. 86-6. - Denial or revocation of occupation tax certificate for nonpayment.
modified
No occupation tax certificate shall be issued by the city to any person who is indebted to the city for the collection and removal of garbage and trash from the premises of the business operated by such person. Any failure to pay for the collection and removal of garbage by the city from such premises which results in the suspension of such service by the city shall constitute sufficient grounds for the revocation of the certificate issued by the city for such business. All occupation tax certificates revoked by the city on account of such suspension shall be done only after a public hearing before the mayor and council, at which the holder of such occupation tax certificate shall be given the opportunity to be heard on whether he/she has failed to pay for the collection and removal of garbage and trash from the premises of the business at the rates specified in this chapter, and the reasons therefor. Upon a determination by the mayor and council at the public hearing that the garbage collection and removal service to such business has been properly suspended for failure to pay for such service to the city, the mayor and council shall be authorized to revoke the certificate of such business in the city, and after revocation, such certificate shall be reinstated only after payment of all sums due the city for the garbage collection and removal service to that business.
(Code 1977, § 22-31; Ord. No. 94-18, § 3, 12-5-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-7. - Collection to be carried on in systematic and efficient manner.
modified
The collection, removal and disposal of all garbage, trash and recyclable materials shall be carried on in a systematic and efficient manner, keeping the entire city in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Code 1977, § 22-32; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-8. - Inspection and enforcement.
modified
It is hereby the duty of the sanitation department to make inspection trips at regular intervals to determine whether garbage, trash, and recyclable materials are being properly collected, removed and disposed of as required by the provisions of this chapter or any other applicable health ordinance. If it is found that this chapter or any other applicable health ordinance is being violated, appropriate and timely action shall be taken to ensure full compliance with its provisions.
(Code 1977, § 22-38; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-9. - Scavenging prohibited.
modified
(a)
Scavenging is prohibited without the consent of the owner or generator of the recyclable materials, garbage, or solid waste.
(b)
Any person violating the provisions of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as for a misdemeanor as provided by the laws of the state.
(Code 1977, § 22-41; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)

ARTICLE II. - CONTAINERS

Sec. 86-31. - Duty to provide; for garbage.
modified
Every owner, occupant or tenant or lessee using or occupying any building, house or structure within the city, for residences, churches, schools, colleges, lodges, commercial businesses, and other purposes, shall provide and maintain garbage cans and receptacles of sufficient number and size as specified in this article, to hold the garbage and trash that would normally accumulate on the premises, except that the mayor and council may furnish receptacles in certain instances where it is deemed to be in the best interest of the city as provided elsewhere in this article. In all cases, every owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of any home, building, or commercial business shall pay at least a minimum charge for garbage pickup, as adopted by the city council and on record with the city clerk.
(Code 1977, § 22-21; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-31.1. - Time limit for containers at streetside.
modified
A garbage can, bag, box or other such container shall not be placed or allowed at streetside prior to 6:00 p.m. on the evening prior to the scheduled day of collection; nor shall said container remain placed or allowed at streetside longer than 9:00 p.m. on the day of collection.
(Ord. No. 94-08, 5-16-94; Ord. No. 2009-8, 7-20-09; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-32. - Underground garbage cans.
modified
(a)
On all residences constructed after November 17, 1976, the cost of installation of underground garbage cans, as required by the city, shall be paid at the time the building permit is purchased. Such payment shall be acknowledged by separate receipts from an authorized employee of the city. The cost referred to shall be determined annually by the building inspector, with the advice of the purchasing department and the consent of the mayor and posted with the receipt books on or before February 1 of each calendar year.
(b)
The city, for the fee mentioned in subsection (a) of this section, shall install one 30-gallon can, located no more than four feet and no less than two feet from the curbside. In order to relocate underground cans, the sanitation supervisor will be notified of relocation for final approval.
(c)
If additional cans are necessary to maintain proper refuse disposal, the second can may be purchased at the city hall by the persons occupying such residence. The second can will be installed only after payment has been received at the city hall.
(d)
Cost of replacing underground garbage can not due to usual wear and tear will be paid by the homeowner.
(Code 1977, § 22-22; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-32.1. - Roll-out containers.
modified
City will supply 96-, 65- or 35-gallon roll-out containers to single-family developments as determined by the mayor and council. The retail cost of these containers to each customer shall be determined by mayor and council and kept on file in the office of the city clerk. Extra roll-out containers can be requested for an additional charge. All homeowner's will be responsible for sanitizing and cleaning their containers. The city is not liable for damage to private containers used for yard waste or recycling services.
(Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-33. - Requirements as to rubbish and trash.
Each of such owners, occupants, tenants, or lessees shall provide containers for rubbish and trash of not less than ten and no more than 32-gallon capacity, constructed of galvanized iron, tin or other suitable material with a tightfitting lid or cover and with handles sufficiently strong for workers to empty conveniently, except as provided elsewhere in this article. Plastic or paper bags of such construction to hold such matter may be used, provided tops are tied and closed.
(Code 1977, § 22-23)
Sec. 86-34. - Lids or covers of containers to be kept secure and fastened.
modified
The lids or covers of all garbage and trash containers shall always be kept secure and fastened so that flies and other insects may not have access to the contents thereof. Such lids or covers shall be removed while the containers or receptacles are being filled or emptied.
(Code 1977, § 22-24; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-34.1. - Screening of dumpsters.
modified
All new dumpsters installed for residential and commercial properties after the effective date of this section shall be screened from view by a fence or decorative masonry wall one foot taller than the dumpster on three sides and provide an operable gate for collection. In-ground compactors shall be screened by landscaping. Aboveground compactors shall be screened by a fence or decorative masonry wall one foot taller than the compactor on three sides and provide an operable gate for collection. Temporary dumpsters provided for carnivals, etc., are excluded from this requirement.
(Ord. No. 97-04, 1-21-97; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-35. - Depositing in container of another to avoid pickup service.
modified
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit any garbage, trash, dirt or dead animals in any container provided or maintained by any owner, occupant or tenant or lessee using or occupying any building, house or structure within the city for residences, schools, churches, colleges, lodges, or commercial businesses, in any quantity to avoid providing or subscribing to a service for the pickup of such garbage, trash, dirt or dead animals at the residence or the site of the operations of the business of such persons. Upon conviction, this offense shall be punishable by a $200.00 fine or 30 days in jail. A reward of $50.00 may be paid by the city to the individual giving information that leads to conviction under this section.
(Code 1977, § 22-25; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Cross reference— Animals, ch. 14.

modified
Every owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of a house or building used for residential, business or commercial purposes or other purposes, is required to maintain constant supervision and surveillance over the garbage and trash receptacles on his/her premises. If either the garbage or trash receptacles should not be emptied and the contents removed by an agent or representative of the city, or other duly authorized persons, for a period of five days, such owner or occupant must notify the department of sanitation of this fact within five days.

ARTICLE III. - PERMIT FOR PRIVATE COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Sec. 86-56. - Required.
modified
No person, except the duly authorized agents or employees of the city, shall empty garbage or trash receptacles or convey or transport garbage or trash on the streets, alleys, and public thoroughfares of the city without a written permit granted and issued by the mayor and council.
(Code 1977, § 22-33; Ord. No. 2023-03 , 2-20-23)
Sec. 86-57. - Filing application; investigation.
Any person desiring a permit for the collection, removal and disposal of garbage, trash and recyclable materials shall make application for such permit to the mayor and council, which shall make or cause to be made such investigation as it may consider necessary in order to determine whether or not the public convenience and necessity require the granting of such permit, and whether the applicant is a fit and proper person to conduct such business. The mayor and council shall also determine the amount to be charged for such permit.
(Code 1977, § 22-34; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94)
Sec. 86-58. - Contents of application.
The application for a private collection and disposal permit shall set forth the name and address of the applicant; the trade name under which the applicant does or proposes to do business; the number of vehicles the applicant desires to operate; the class, size and design of each vehicle; whether or not the applicant has been convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude; sufficient information for the city to obtain a commercial credit report; proof of public liability insurance to cover the operation of his/her vehicles for damage to the property of others while operating such vehicles on the streets of this city; the nature and character of the service the applicant proposes to render; the experience the applicant has had in rendering such service; the patrons for whom the applicant proposes to render this service, and such other information as the mayor and council may require.
(Code 1977, § 22-35)
Sec. 86-59. - Nontransferable; revocation.
All permits granted by the mayor and council for the collection, removal and disposal of garbage, trash and recyclable materials shall be nontransferable. Such permits may be revoked by the mayor and council at any time when, in its judgment, such action is deemed to be in the best interest of the public.
(Code 1977, § 22-36; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94)
Sec. 86-60. - To be attached to vehicle or kept in possession of person rendering service; inspection.
In all cases where permits have been issued for the collection, removal and disposal of garbage, trash and recyclable materials, such permit shall be in the possession of the person rendering such service and shall be subject to inspection at all times. The permit shall be painted on both sides of the vehicle used for such purpose.
(Code 1977, § 22-37; Ord. No. 94-15, 11-7-94)

ARTICLE IV. - LITTER

Sec. 86-81. - Declaration of intent.
It is the intention of the mayor and council to prohibit all littering on public or private property, and to curb thereby the desecration of the beauty of the city and harm to the health, welfare and safety of its citizens caused by individuals who litter.
(Code 1977, § 11-27)
Sec. 86-82. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Litter means all sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste material, metal cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals or discarded materials of every kind and description, including loose or scattered handbills, newspapers, posters and other such items which may be carried by the wind or water.
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any park, playground, refuge or conservation or recreation area or building; and residential, business or farm properties, timberlands or forests.
(Code 1977, § 11-28)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 86-83. - Unlawful activities.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, deposit, place, throw or leave, or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving of litter on any public or private property in the city, unless:
(1)
Such property is designated by the city for the disposal of such litter, and such person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
Such litter is placed into a closed litter receptacle or container installed on such property; and
(3)
Such person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession, or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner so that the litter may not be scattered or carried by wind or water, and otherwise consistent with the public welfare.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place solid waste or refuse into a private or commercial container or on any property or location different from where the solid waste or refuse was generated, except:
(1)
When such container has been approved by the proper authority of the city, as designated by the mayor and council; and
(2)
Such container is designated and intended for use by the location where the solid waste or refuse was generated.
(c)
It shall be presumed that solid waste or refuse containing matter identifying the owner was under the possession and control and belonged to such owner at the time it was discarded, and, therefore, that such owner discarded the solid waste or refuse in the manner such solid waste or refuse shall be found, until otherwise proven.
(d)
The city administrator is hereby authorized, but not required, to pay $50.00 as a reward to any person, except a city employee, for information leading to a conviction of any person violating the provisions of this section.
(e)
Violations of this section shall be punishable as a misdemeanor as provided in this Code.
(Code 1977, § 11-29)
Sec. 86-84. - Prima facie evidence.
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, placed, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of section 86-83, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance is the person who disposed of the litter.
(Code 1977, § 11-30)
