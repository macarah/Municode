Chapter 18 - BUSINESSES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Door-to-door sale of meat, groceries or other goods.
(a)
License requirement; cost. Every person who engages in the door-to-door sale of meat, groceries or other goods, wares and merchandise in the county shall be first required to obtain a license from the county clerk, at a cost of $50.00 for each such person in such activity.
(b)
Penalty. Any person who engages in the door-to-door sale of meat, groceries or other goods, wares and merchandise without a license shall be guilty of a violation of this section, and upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-17. Each sale shall constitute a new and separate offense.
(c)
Exemptions. There shall be excluded from and exempted from the requirements of this section church and religious organization, all charitable and nonprofit organizations or firms, and all associations affiliated with a school, college or other institution of learning.

ARTICLE II. - PAWNBROKERS

Sec. 18-30. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Owner means any person, partner or stockholder owning 25 percent or more of a business.
Pawnbroker means a person, whether owner, manager or employee, whose business is to lend money on security of personal property deposited with him or left in pawn.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-32)
Sec. 18-31. - Penalty.
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished and may be charged upon a citation, or accusation to the Magistrate Court of Marion County, Georgia, and, upon conviction, shall be fined not less than $100.00 and no more than $500.00 and/or 60 days in the county jail. Each day of a violation of this article shall be a separate and new offense.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-40)
Sec. 18-32. - Applicability.
This article shall apply to all pawnbrokers in the county.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-31)
Sec. 18-33. - License fees.
All businesses involving pawnbrokers shall pay an annual license fee of $250.00 to the clerk of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-33)
Sec. 18-34. - Revocation of licenses.
(a)
Generally. Licenses issued under this article may be revoked upon a conviction of the licensee or an employee of the licensee or plea of guilty or nolo contendere by the licensee or an employee of the licensee in court of a violation of any of this article's provisions; upon a conviction of the licensee or an employee of the licensee or plea of guilty or nolo contendere by the licensee or an employee of the licensee in any state of any felony involving any offense or any misdemeanor involving theft-related offenses; or if the licensee or an employee of the licensee violates any of the provisions of this article.
(b)
Procedures. The following procedures shall be followed in the revocation of licenses under this article:
(1)
Notice shall be given to the holder of a license at least two weeks in advance that the board of commissioners, will hear a recommendation that the license be revoked, and this notice shall include the specific grounds on which this recommendation is made.
(2)
The clerk of commissioners shall notify the license holder, the director of finance, and the sheriff, or the director of public safety, as to the time that a recommendation for revocation will be heard.
(3)
The board of commissioners shall hear a recommendation for revocation at one of its regular meetings, at which time the holder of a license and any other interested parties will be heard.
(4)
No license shall be revoked until a majority of the board of commissioners so decides at the time of or subsequent to the hearing on the recommendation for revocation.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-34)
Sec. 18-35. - Character requirements.
No license shall be issued to or renewed for any person who conducts a business in which the owner or manager or any employee has, within five years of issuance or renewal, been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to any felony involving any offense or any misdemeanor involving theft-related offenses, in any state, and all licensees shall submit the identity of all owners and managers and employees to the county sheriff's department and to the revenue collection division on a continuing basis as changes occur for the purpose of allowing a complete check in order that these character requirements are fulfilled.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-35)
Sec. 18-36. - Location of business.
All businesses regulated by this article shall be conducted at an identifiable street address which shall be provided on a license or renewal application which itself shall be turned over to the county sheriff's department, and no licensee shall change the address for any business without giving advance notice to both the revenue collection division and the county sheriff's department.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-36)
Sec. 18-37. - Requirements upon receiving pawn or purchasing items.
All businesses regulated by this article shall fulfill the following requirements when receiving in pawn or purchasing:
(1)
Require the pawner or seller to positively identify himself by driver's license, military ID card, alcohol beverage card, Social Security number, or any other means of identification that contains the following information:
a.
Birth date, sex, race, address, telephone number; and
b.
Any other relevant physical descriptive information;
all of which will be contained on a form prescribed and provided to the business by the county sheriff's department.
(2)
Record on the same form which describes the pawner or seller a complete description of the merchandise pawned or purchased, including kind, style, material, color, design, and all identifying names, marks and numbers, and any distinguishing marks such as initials or serial numbers, as well as the time it was obtained by the pawner or purchases, and from whom it was obtained.
(3)
For a period of seven days from the date of pawning or purchasing, post in a clearly visible or accessible place on the premises designated in the license for doing business the forms containing the information in subsections (a) and (b) of this section, and hold on these at the premises in a place readily available for inspection by both law enforcement officials and the general public, all of these designated items in the same condition as they existed when first pawned or purchased.
(4)
Require the pawner or seller to sign the county sheriff's department form on a signature line which shall be preceded by the following sentence: "I do solemnly swear or affirm that the information provided by me on this form is true and correct." Adjacent to the pawner's or seller's signature there shall be affixed the right thumbprint of the pawner or seller, and the pawnbroker shall use ink and pad approved by the county sheriff's department.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-37)
Sec. 18-38. - Daily reports.
The sheriff or his deputy shall require pawnbrokers to make daily written reports on all of their purchases or items received in pawn, and these reports may contain, in addition to such descriptive information as is required in the sheriff's department forms, information concerning the price paid for specific items.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-38)
Sec. 18-39. - Receiving in pawn or buying from minors prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any business regulated by this article to receive in pawn or trade or to buy from minors goods of any character or description. A minor, for the purpose of this article, is an individual 17 years of age or under.
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01), § 18-39)
Sec. 18-40. - Pawn shop weekly report.
Itemize all pawns taken in within the past week giving the following information about each pawn:
(1)
Name, address, social security number and telephone number of each person who pawned any item with you.
(2)
Full description of each item pawned including, but not limited to make, model and serial number.
(3)
Attach a copy of the thumb print of each person making a pawn.
I certify that the above constitutes each and every person or legal entity who has pawned any items with me and a description of each and every item pawned with me during the past week.
___________
Owner or Representative
(Ord. of 12-12-2006(01))

ARTICLE III. - POOL TABLES AND GAMING DEVICES

Sec. 18-71. - Licensing of pool tables.
(a)
License requirement. All persons having and operating pool tables in a public business or their homes whereby a fee is charged for the use of such pool tables shall be required to purchase a license from the board of commissioners; and such license shall cost $50.00 for the first pool table and $25.00 for each additional pool table. Such license shall be good for one year and shall begin on January 1 of each year and shall end on December 31.
(b)
Penalty. Any person operating a pool table as provided in subsection (a) of this section without such license will be guilty of a violation of this section and, upon conviction, shall be fined not more than $500.00.
(Res. of 12-6-1988)
Sec. 18-72. - Licensing of video games.
(a)
License requirement. All persons having and operating video games in a public business or in their homes whereby a fee is charged for the use of such video games shall be required to purchase a license from the board of commissioners; such license shall cost $25.00 for each video game. Such license shall be good for one year and shall begin on January 1 of each year and shall last until December 31.
(b)
Penalty. Any person operating a video game as provided in subsection (a) of this section without such license will be guilty of a violation of this section and, upon conviction, shall be fined not more than $500.00.
(Res. of 12-6-1988)

Sec. 18-101. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Entertainment.
(1)
The term "entertainment" means any noise or music emanating from or produced by any person, group, instrument or instrumental recording or audio visual device used for the performance of entertainment for an audience, regardless of whether an admission fee or remuneration is paid, and regardless of whether for profit or charity.
(2)
The term "entertainment" does not mean:
a.
Any music played over an installed music system solely during the dining of customers in a restaurant and played at a level less than normal conversation;
b.
Any music or noise at private, social functions such as wedding receptions, barbecues, and the like;
c.
Any music or noise from any school events or school athletics;
d.
Any noise or music from the use of any public property;
e.
Any noise or music from nonprofit, religious or charitable organizations conducting annual events, or occasional fundraising events, or sounds emitted during normal religious worship and/or activity;
f.
Any noise or music from political rallies or meetings;
g.
Any noise or music from occasional or periodic commercial sales promotions; and
h.
Any noise or music from seasonal holiday-related events for which the main attraction or draw thereof is not musically related.
Outdoor means any area outside the exterior walls and covering roof of any building. The term "outdoor" shall also include, but not be limited to, any amphitheater, pavilion, porch, veranda, courtyard, deck, patio, pool or tent.
Sec. 18-102. - Penalties.
(a)
Generally. Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and, upon conviction in the magistrate court of this county, shall be fined in an amount up to $1,000.00, imprisoned up to 60 days, or both, for each violation, as provided for by state law. Each day such violation continues shall constitute a separate offense and shall subject such person to a fine or incarceration specified by this section for each such offense.
(b)
Revocation of licenses. Notwithstanding any fines which may be levied or punishment imposed by the magistrate court, the board of commissioners shall have the right to revoke any business, alcohol or other license issued under any county ordinance for violation of this article.
Sec. 18-103. - Prohibited conduct.
(a)
Outdoor entertainment shall be not permitted in the county at any time.
(b)
Outdoor entertainment is prohibited in all zoning districts.
(c)
No audience participation events such as wet tee shirt contests, bikini contestss, mud wresting or the like, whether inside a building or outdoors, as defined in this article, shall be allowed.

ARTICLE V. - LOGGING AND PULPWOOD HARVESTING; USE OF COUNTY RIGHTS-OF-WAY

Sec. 18-131. - Security bond or letter of credit.
(a)
Prior to beginning a tree harvesting operation in the county, all timber buyers shall post a security bond or a letter of credit in the amount of $2,500.00 with the county clerk for each separate tract of land from which trees are to be removed. For each such tract, the logger shall also provide:
(1)
His name, address and telephone number.
(2)
The name, address and telephone number of the foreman on each tract.
(3)
The name, address and telephone number of the owner of each tract.
(4)
The approximate location of the site for access to county roads for each tract.
(5)
The anticipated date for commencement of removal of trees for each tract.
(6)
The anticipated date for completing the removal of trees for each tract.
(b)
Upon notification, the superintendent of public works shall inspect the site to determine if a culvert is required along such county right-of-way and the size and length of culvert, if any is required. If it is determined that a culvert is needed, it must be installed by the operator under the supervision of the director of public works. When the superintendent of public works makes his initial inspection of the site, the existing condition of the access point will be documented. All access points shall be constructed at an angle leading in the direction in which the log truck and/or pulpwood truck is exiting so that the truck shall not be required to make a sharp turn when the truck enters the county roadway.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § I)
Sec. 18-132. - Posting of one security bond by parent tree harvesting operations.
(a)
If a parent tree harvesting operation has more than one tree harvesting operation in the county, then the parent tree harvesting operation may post one security bond or letter of credit to cover all of its tree harvesting operations in the county.
(b)
Should a security bond or letter of credit be reduced by payment for damages to a road right-of-way or roadway caused by one or more of the tree harvesting operations operating under the parent tree harvesting operation, then the board of commissioners may require additional security bonds or letters of credit.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § I(B))
Sec. 18-133. - Regulations during operations.
(a)
All logging or pulpwood loading operations shall be conducted at a loading site off the county rights-of-way and behind the established ditch line of county roads.
(b)
Loading or skidding of logs will not be permitted on county roads.
(c)
When a log truck leaves the loading site, it shall take the route that is nearest to a state or federal highway, and then travel along the state or federal highway to its destination or to a point outside of the county.
(d)
Ditches shall be kept clear of all debris or residue at all times to allow for proper drainage.
(e)
County roads at the access site shall be kept serviceable at all times for the passing public, emergency vehicles, school busses, mail carriers, etc.
(f)
Operators will use crushed stone or some other material, if necessary, on access roads to prevent the transport of excessive amounts of mud from the operations area onto county roads.
(g)
The county superintendent of public works, with the concurrence of the board of commissioners, has the authority to grant exceptions to the rules set out in this section.
(h)
The board of commissioners hereby authorizes the superintendent of public works to order all logging operations to cease if it is determined that a timber buyer is failing to comply with this article.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § II)
Sec. 18-134. - Concluding operations.
(a)
Operators shall give oral or written notification upon completion of operations to the county public works department or the county commissioners' office within 72 hours.
(b)
Harvesters may remove culverts installed by same upon completion of their operations.
(c)
Upon site inspection by the county public works department and the determination that the road and access site are in a proper state of repair from harvesting operations, the superintendent shall provide a written notice of release of liability to the operator within ten days of notification from the operator. Should said notice of the release of liability not be made on a timely basis, operator shall not be held responsible or liable under this article.
(d)
If it is determined by the superintendent of the county public works department either during or at the conclusion of operations that the county road and access site are not in a proper state of repair, the operator shall have ten days to correct the site, at the operator's expense, or to appeal the decision.
(e)
Upon receipt of a written appeal, the board of commissioners shall have five days to impanel an arbitration board. The arbitration board shall consist of three people from the county: one person selected by the board of commissioners, one person selected by the operator, and one person mutually agreed upon by the two persons selected. Once the arbitration board has been selected, the arbitration board shall have no more than 30 days from the receipt of the letter of appeal in which to reach a decision. The decision of the arbitration board shall be final.
(f)
Failure to comply with the above requirements for repairs will give the county the right to make the repairs and charge the expense therefor to the operator and deduct said repairs from the $2,500.00 deposit. If the deposit is insufficient to pay for said costs of repairs, then the timber buyer and/or operator shall be billed for the excess.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § III)
Sec. 18-135. - Penalty.
Violations of any provisions of this article shall result in a fine up to $1,000.00 or imprisonment up to six months in the probate court of the county.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § I)
