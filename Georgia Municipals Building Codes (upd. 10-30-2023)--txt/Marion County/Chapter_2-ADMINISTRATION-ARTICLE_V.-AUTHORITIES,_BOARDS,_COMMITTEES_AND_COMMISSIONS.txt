DIVISION 2. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-221. - Purpose.
There is determined to be a pressing, existing and future need for a development authority, as more fully described and defined in the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.), to function in the county for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the county, thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry.
(Res. of 12-7-1971)
Sec. 2-222. - Creation.
There is activated in the county the public body corporate and politic known as the "Development Authority of Marion County, Georgia," which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.), and particularly section 2 of such law.
(Res. of 12-7-1971)
Sec. 2-223. - Membership.
There are elected as members of the board of directors of the development authority seven persons who are taxpayers residing in the county, none of whom is an officer or employee of the county.
(Res. of 12-7-1971)
Sec. 2-224. - Term.
Each member of the board of directors of the development authority shall serve in such capacity until his successor shall be elected.
(Res. of 12-7-1971)
Sec. 2-225. - Organization; powers and duties.
The development authority shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities, and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.) as it exists and as it may be amended or modified.
(Res. of 12-7-1971)
Sec. 2-226. - Creation of other development authorities.
The creation of the county development authority shall not in any way affect the right of the City of Buena Vista or any other municipal corporation in the county to create a development authority as authorized by the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.).
(Res. of 12-7-1971)
Sec. 2-227. - Notification of secretary of state.
The clerk of the board of commissioners shall immediately furnish to the secretary of state a certified copy of the resolution from which this division is derived in compliance with the mandate set forth in section 2 of the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.).
(Res. of 12-7-1971)

DIVISION 3. - WEST CENTRAL GEORGIA AREA PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT COMMISSION

Sec. 2-251. - Established.
The board of commissioners, in cooperation with the governing authorities of other area counties and municipalities, establishes the West Central Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission to be governed by the provisions of this division.
(Res. of 10-1-1963)
Sec. 2-252. - Membership.
The West Central Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission shall consist of two members from each of the counties participating in the area planning commission. Of the two members from each county, one shall be appointed by the governing authority of the municipality wherein the county seat is located (by the governing authorities of participating municipalities within such county) and the other shall be appointed by the governing authority of such county.
(Res. of 10-1-1963, § 1)
Sec. 2-253. - Term of office; removal of member.
The term of office for all members of the West Central Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission shall be five years, except that the initial appointment by the county shall be for a term of one year. Any vacancy in membership of the West Central Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment which has become vacant. The appointing authority shall also have the power to remove any member it may appoint for cause, on written charges, after public hearing.
(Res. of 10-1-1963, § 2)
Sec. 2-254. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
The West Central Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission shall elect a chairperson and vice-chairperson from among its appointive members. The term of office of the chairperson and vice-chairperson shall be for one year or until reelected or their successors are elected. The commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the commission. The commission shall meet at least once each month at the call of the chairperson and at such other times as the chairperson or board may determine, shall adopt rules for the transaction of business and shall keep a record of its resolutions, transactions, findings and determinations, which record shall be a public record. The commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work. In the performance of its duties, the commission may cooperate with, contract with, or accept funds from federal, state, or local, public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, may spend such funds, and may carry out such cooperative undertakings and contracts. The commission may make expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts to the commission and contract receipts, within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the participating governing authorities.
(Res. of 10-1-1963, § 3)
Sec. 2-255. - Powers and duties.
It shall be the function and duty of the West Central Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the entire West Central Georgia area and each political subdivision therein. In general, the West Central Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission shall have such powers and duties and responsibilities as set forth in the General Planning Enabling Act of 1957, as amended (Act No. 358).
(Res. of 10-1-1963, § 4)

DIVISION 4. - E-911 ADVISORY BOARD

Sec. 2-271. - Established.
Pursuant to the Georgia Emergency Telephone Number 9-1-1 Service Act of 1977 (O.C.G.A. § 46-5-120 et seq.), as amended, the board of commissioners does hereby create the county E-911 advisory board, consisting of the sheriff, representatives from other pubic safety agencies which respond to emergency calls under the system, and other individuals knowledgeable of emergency 911 systems and the emergency needs of the citizens of the local government, said board not to exceed 13 members.
(Res. of 11-12-2002)
Sec. 2-272. - Meetings.
The E-911 advisory board shall convene at the call of the chairperson, so designated by members of the board, and/or at the call of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 11-12-2002)

DIVISION 5. - MIDDLE FLINT REGIONAL E-911 AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-291. - Established; activation.
(a)
Authority created. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-138, Dooly, Macon, Marion, Schley, Sumter, Taylor and Webster Counties create the Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority.
(b)
Activation. The Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority is activated as of January 1, 2003.
(Res. of 12-10-2002)
Sec. 2-292. - Powers.
The Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority shall have all powers necessary or convenient to carry out and effectuate the purposes and provisions of O.C.G.A. § 46-5-138.
(Res. of 12-10-2002)
Sec. 2-293. - Membership.
The Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority shall have an eight-member board of directors whose members shall serve staggered, three-year terms, with the boards of commissioners of Dooly, Macon, Marion, Schley, Taylor and Webster Counties having one appointment each, and the Sumter County Board of Commissioners appointing two members.
(Res. of 12-10-2002)
Sec. 2-294. - Residency requirement.
All members of the Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority shall be legal residents of the counties from which they are appointed.
(Res. of 12-10-2002)
Sec. 2-295. - Initial appointments.
The initial appointment of the Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority shall be effective January 1, 2003, for staggered terms as follows:
(1)
The initial appointee from Dooly County shall serve for one calendar year.
(2)
The initial appointee from Macon County shall serve for two calendar years.
(3)
The initial appointee from Marion County shall serve for three calendar years.
(4)
The initial appointee from Schley County shall serve for one calendar year.
(5)
Of the initial appointees from Sumter County one shall serve for two calendar years, one for three calendar years.
(6)
The initial appointee from Taylor County shall serve for one calendar year.
(7)
The initial appointee from Webster County shall serve for two calendar years.
(Res. of 12-10-2002)
Sec. 2-296. - Boards of commissioners to determine membership.
Members of the Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority shall serve at the pleasure of their respective boards of commissioners.
(Res. of 12-10-2002)
Sec. 2-297. - Effective date.
The resolution from which this division is derived shall not take effect until duly enacted by the respective boards of commissioners of all seven counties identified in this division.
(Res. of 12-10-2002)

DIVISION 6. - HOSPITAL AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-321. - Creation and activation.
(a)
Declaration of need. There is need and necessity for a hospital authority, as defined in an Act approved March 27, 1941 (1941 Ga. Laws, pages 241—250), to function, to exist and to be operated in and for the county.
(b)
Creation and organization. There is activated, created and organized a hospital authority to be known as The Hospital Authority of Marion County, Georgia, as provided in and by the said Act approved March 27, 1941 (1941 Ga. Laws, pages 241—250).
(Res. of 2-4-1964)
Sec. 2-322. - Powers.
The hospital authority shall be deemed to exercise public and essential governmental functions and shall have all of the powers necessary or convenient to carry out and effectuate the purposes and provisions of said Act, including, but without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the following powers:
(1)
To sue and be sued;
(2)
To have a seal and alter the seal; to make and execute contracts and other instruments necessary to exercise the powers of the hospital authority;
(3)
To acquire, lease and operate hospital projects and to provide construction, reconstruction, improvements, alteration and repair of any such project and to lease and rent for any number of years any lands, buildings, structures or facilities in any existing or hereafter established hospital project and to establish rates and charges for the use of the services of the hospital authority;
(4)
To accept gifts, grants or devices of any property;
(5)
To acquire by the exercise of the right of eminent domain any property essential to the purposes of the hospital authority;
(6)
To sell, lease, exchange, transfer, assign, pledge, mortgage or dispose of any real or personal property or interest therein;
(7)
To mortgage, pledge, assign any revenues, incomes, tolls, charges or fees received by the hospital authority;
(8)
To issue certificates of indebtedness for the purposes of providing funds for carrying out the duties of the hospital authority, provided such certificates shall not extend beyond a period of 30 years from the date of issuance thereof and provided further that such certificates shall be repayable from the revenues realized from the project or any part thereof;
(9)
To borrow money for any corporate purposes;
(10)
To appoint officers, agents and employees;
(11)
To make use of any facilities afforded by the federal government or any agency or instrumentality thereof; and
(12)
To exercise any and all powers now or hereafter possessed by private corporations performing similar functions.
(Res. of 2-4-1964)
Sec. 2-323. - Composition; officers; liability.
(a)
Appointment and term. The hospital authority shall consist of a board of nine trustees. The trustees shall be appointed for terms of four years from the dates of expiration of their respective terms of office; except that any person appointed to fill a vacancy (created during a term) shall be appointed only for the unexpired term of the trustee whom he succeeds. Any member of the hospital authority shall be eligible for reappointment. All appointments shall be made by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Officers. The board of trustees of the hospital authority shall elect one of their members as chairperson and another as vice-chairperson; he shall also elect a secretary and treasurer, and the latter need not be a trustee.
(c)
Liability. No trustee or other person executing certificates or other obligations on behalf of the hospital authority shall be personally liable thereon.
(Res. of 2-4-1964)
