DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-126. - City clerk's reports.
The city clerk shall make a full and itemized statement of the transactions of his office, showing all amounts received and all amounts paid out. The clerk shall submit such reports to the council at the end of each month. Such reports shall be filed in the office of the clerk for reference at any time.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001)
Sec. 2-127. - City manager's authority over surplus funds.
The city manager may set aside all surplus funds which have already accumulated and which may accumulate in the future, to be kept in a separate account for the purpose of making permanent improvements and paying off the bonded indebtedness of the city. The city manager may determine what shall constitute a surplus.
(Code 1976, § 5-1002)
Sec. 2-128. - Claims against the city.
If upon the presentation of claims against the city, the council by resolution duly adopted in open council meeting directs the city clerk to pay off such claims, it shall be the duty of the treasurer to pay the claim out of any funds in his hand not raised for extraordinary purposes, taking receipts therefor from each payee for the sum paid to each, referring in each receipt to the particular resolution of the council by date of the meeting at which it was adopted, under which payment was made. Such receipt shall be his sufficient voucher for all money so paid out by him.
(Code 1976, § 5-1003)
Sec. 2-129. - Interest on city invoices.
All city invoices except utility service bills not paid within thirty (30) days of date of invoice shall bear interest at the maximum rate allowed by law.
(Code 1976, § 5-1004)
State Law reference— Legal rate of interest, O.C.G.A. § 7-4-2.

DIVISION 2. - PURCHASES

Sec. 2-141. - Definition.
In this division "purchasing agent" means the purchasing agent and code compliance officer as approved by the city.
(Code 1976, § 5-4001; Ord. No. 42-95, § 3, 10-12-95)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 2-142. - Purchases to be made through purchasing agent; exception.
No officer or employee of the city shall purchase for and on behalf of the city any material or supplies, goods, wares or merchandise of any kind or character except through the purchasing agent; and no voucher, check or other method of payment shall be honored if this method is not followed. It shall be considered a breach of duty on the part of any officer or employee of the city to make any purchase, or aid and abet the making of any purchase in any other manner than through the purchasing agent; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to purchases as specifically provided in sections 2-144 and 2-145.
(Code 1976, § 5-4002)
Sec. 2-143. - Purchase orders required.
Purchase orders are not required, except when specifically requested by the purchasing agent.
(Code 1976, § 5-4003; Ord. No. 29-20, § 1, 11-19-20)
Sec. 2-144. - Limited purchases without prior purchase order.
In order to expedite purchasing, each department head may make purchases of up to two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500.00) without receiving a prior purchase order; if required.
(Code 1976, § 5-4004; Ord. No. 29-20, § 2, 11-19-20)
Sec. 2-145. - Emergency purchases.
In all cases where there is an immediate need for any material or supplies, goods, wares or merchandise by any department, such department shall write on its requisition to the purchasing agent the words, "for emergency purposes". When a requisition so marked is received by the purchasing agent it shall be his duty to give such requisition immediate attention. In the event of an emergency arising after office hours and requiring immediate action on the part of the department involved for the protection of the best interests of the city, or should a like situation arise on a Sunday or holiday and when it is not possible or convenient to reach the purchasing agent, any purchase necessary shall be made by the department in charge, and such purchase shall be reported to the purchasing agent without delay. In the event of a declaration of an emergency by the city, the purchasing agent is authorized to make purchases on verbal request from any department head; and where necessary department heads are authorized to make purchases independent of the purchasing agent. In such instances, such purchases shall be reported to the purchasing agent as soon as the circumstances permit.
(Code 1976, § 5-4005)
Sec. 2-146. - Discretion to be exercised in making purchases where quality is involved.
In all purchases where quality as well as price is involved, the purchasing agent shall exercise discretion in making the purchase.
(Code 1976, § 5-4006)
Sec. 2-147. - Council approval and city manager approval required for certain purchasing.
(a)
The purchasing agent shall make no purchases that include any item, the price of which exceeds two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500.00) but is less than seven thousand five hundred dollars ($7,500.00) without the approval of the city manager, who may prescribe terms under which the purchases may be made.
(b)
The purchasing agent shall make no purchase that includes any item the price of which exceeds seven thousand five hundred dollars ($7,500.00) without the approval of the council, which may prescribe the terms under which the purchase may be made, including advertisement for bids.
(c)
The purchasing agent shall be authorized to purchase any items which do not exceed two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500.00) as he is requested to do so on behalf of the city by the department heads and/or the city manager.
(Code 1976, § 5-4007; Ord. No. 42-95, § 3, 10-12-95; Ord. No. 29-20, § 3, 11-19-20)
Sec. 2-148. - Authority to reject all bids and readvertise.
Except where prohibited by law or by the wording of an advertisement for bids, the authority permitted to accept bids shall be permitted to reject all bids or parts of bids and readvertise for bids, where the public interest will be served thereby.
(Code 1976, § 5-4008)
Sec. 2-149. - Bids to be filed and subject to inspection.
All bids for goods or materials of any kind or character purchased by the city shall be kept on file by the purchasing agent. After an award has been made, the bids shall be subject to inspection at any time during normal business hours by any citizen of the city.
(Code 1976, § 5-4009)
Sec. 2-150. - Electronic transmission of information.
(a)
Electronic commerce shall include but not limited to on-line vendors registration, acceptance of bids and proposals by electronic mail, electronic or virtual purchasing malls and catalogs, notifications of solicitations and download capability and acceptance of electronic (digital) signatures.
(b)
Notwithstanding any other provisions, this chapter applies to records generated, stored, processed, communicated, or used for any purpose by the city for purchasing, procurement, acquisition, services, or disposition of personal property. This shall apply to all city contracts except for public works contracts.
(c)
The finance director or his/her designee is authorized to promulgate procedures to coordinate, create, implement, and facilitate the use of common approaches and technical infrastructure, as appropriate, to enhance the utilization of electronic commerce, electronic records, electronic signatures, and electronic security procedures by and for the city for these purposes.
(d)
The finance director shall be authorized to develop, implement, and facilitate procedures for the use of electronic records, electronic signatures, and security procedures for all other purposes. The finance director is authorized to promulgate methods, means, and standards for secure electronic procurement transactions.
(Ord. No. 78-05, § 1, 9-1-05)
Sec. 2-151. - Electronic signatures and records.
(a)
An electronic record satisfies any rule requiring a document to be in writing. An electronic signature satisfies any rule of law requiring a signature. Any electronic record is signed as a matter of law if it contains a secure electronic signature.
(b)
An electronic signature is deemed to be secure if it is created by application of a security procedure that is commercially reasonable and provided the electronic signature can be verified. Further, it is considered secure if it can be linked to the electronic record to which it relates in a manner such that, if the record is changed, the electronic signature is invalidated.
(c)
An electronic record is deemed to be secure if it is created by application of a security procedure that is commercially reasonable and agreed to be the city. The electronic record will be deemed secure when it can be verified not to have been altered since a specified point in time.
(Ord. No. 78-05, § 2, 9-1-05)
