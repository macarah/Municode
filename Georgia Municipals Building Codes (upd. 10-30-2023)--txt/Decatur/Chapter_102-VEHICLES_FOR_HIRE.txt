ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 102-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Doing business means picking up passengers in the incorporated area of the city and accepting or soliciting any consideration, charge or fee which is determined by agreement, by mileage, by the length of time the vehicle is used or by contract for the use of any motor vehicle or other vehicle designed or used for the purpose of transporting passengers. Any person shall also be deemed as doing business if such person has established a business relationship with independent contractors or operates vehicles for hire on such person's own behalf for the purpose of transporting passengers in the city.
Taxicabs, limousines and other passenger-carrying vehicles means any motor vehicles or other vehicles for hire designed or used for the purpose of transporting passengers for consideration or charges which are determined by agreement, contract, mileage or by the length of time the vehicle is used. Such term does not include vehicles regulated by the state public service commission.
Vehicles for hire means taxicabs, limousines and passenger-carrying vehicles.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, §§ 22-1, 22-2, 4-6-92)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 102-2. - Compliance with chapter.
No person shall conduct the business of operating vehicles for hire in the city without first meeting the requirements of this chapter.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-3, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-3. - Licensees responsible for violations by drivers.
Licensees under this chapter are responsible for violations of this chapter by their vehicle operators whether such operators are direct employees or independent contractors.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-4, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-4. - Requirement for registered agent; service of notice.
(a)
All businesses licensed under this chapter must have and continuously maintain in the county a registered agent upon whom any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter to be served upon the licensee or owner may be served. This person must be a resident of the county. The licensee shall file the name of such agent, along with the written consent of such agent with the city and shall be in such form as prescribed.
(b)
Service of notice under this chapter shall be as follows:
(1)
By delivery to the owner or registered agent personally;
(2)
By registered or certified mail with postage prepaid deposited in the United States mail addressed to the owner's or occupant's last known address; or
(3)
If service of notice cannot be accomplished by using the methods stated in subsections (a)(1) or (a)(2) of this section, by posting a copy of the notice in a conspicuous place on the premises of the company base of operations in the county.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-5, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-5. - Appeals from decisions of public safety department and city manager.
(a)
Decisions of the public safety department that adversely affect or aggrieve any applicant or permittee may be appealed to the city manager or the city manager's designated representative. Such appeal shall be by written petition, filed in the city manager's office within 15 days after the public safety department's decision.
(b)
Decisions of the city manager that adversely affect or aggrieve any applicant or permittee may be appealed to the city commission. Such appeal shall be by written petition, filed in the city manager's office within 15 days after the city manager's decision.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-6, 4-6-92; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 102-6. - Audits; filing returns.
Each company and individual licensed under the provisions of this chapter shall be subject to an audit by the public safety department to determine compliance with all applicable provisions of this chapter. No licensed business or individual shall fail to comply with the audit requirements of the city.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-7, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-7. - Appearance and hygiene of drivers; responsibility of licensees for driver dress and conduct.
Drivers must practice good personal hygiene and wear proper dress while operating a vehicle for hire. Proper dress shall mean the wearing of shoes, ankle-length pants, a shirt or blouse with sleeves and collar. Clothing must be clean and not visibly soiled. Licensees under this chapter are responsible for driver dress and conduct.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-15, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-8. - Trip sheets or logs.
Drivers must maintain daily trip sheets or logs of all passengers, the time, place of entry, the destination of each passenger, the amount charged and an itemization of any personal property left in the vehicle for hire. Trip sheets must be maintained in the vehicle for 48 hours and, thereafter, transferred to and maintained at the licensed business premises for a period of time to be specified by the public safety department in that department's published rules and regulations for vehicles for hire.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-16, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-9. - Drivers smoking, playing radios, possessing alcohol or controlled substances.
(a)
A driver while operating a vehicle for hire is not to smoke or play a radio or tape player if objected to by a passenger.
(b)
No person operating a vehicle under the provisions of this chapter shall transport or have in said person's possession any alcoholic beverage or illegal drug or controlled substance.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-17, 4-6-92; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 102-10. - Cruising and use of vehicle stands.
Licensees under this chapter are responsible for ensuring that no driver participates in cruising. Cruising is defined as moving about the streets of the city for the purpose of picking up and transporting passengers who have not previously requested such service by telephone or by personal command. Licensees shall ensure that their drivers use open stands on a nonexclusive, first come, first served basis.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-18, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-11. - Call jumping.
Licensees under this chapter shall not participate in nor allow their drivers to practice call jumping or the act of intercepting a passenger who has requested service from another company.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-19, 4-6-92)
Secs. 102-12—102-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSES AND PERMITS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 102-36. - Transfer and term.
All company licenses, individual vehicle stickers and driver permits required by this chapter are nontransferable and shall expire on June 30 of each year. Licensees should contact the public safety department by May 1 of each year for renewal scheduling.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-12, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-37. - Suspension or revocation.
A business license may be suspended or revoked by the city commission, and a driver's permit may be suspended or revoked by the public safety department for the following reasons:
(1)
The applicant furnishes fraudulent or untruthful information or omits information requested in the application for a license or permit.
(2)
Failure to pay all fees, taxes or other charges.
(3)
Failure to maintain all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a license or driver's permit.
(4)
For violation of any part of this chapter.
(5)
Charging a fare in excess of those fares on file with the city manager's office.
(6)
Having four or more moving traffic violations in any 12-month period.
(7)
Refusing to accept a passenger solely on the basis of race, color, national origin, religious belief, sex or sexual orientation. Sexual orientation shall mean the state of being heterosexual, homosexual or bisexual. Operators shall not refuse to accept a passenger unless the passenger is obviously intoxicated or dangerous.
(8)
Allowing the required insurance coverage to lapse or allowing a vehicle to operate in the city without a city inspection sticker.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-13, 4-6-92)
Secs. 102-38—102-55. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 102-56. - Licensing generally.
(a)
Required for operation of business; county-based operation. No person shall conduct the business of operating vehicles for hire in the city without first having been issued a city business license. Such business shall have a company base of operations located in the geographical boundaries of the county. Limousine operations having no more than three vehicles for hire shall be exempt from the requirement to maintain a company base of operations located in the geographical boundaries of the county.
(b)
Posting of license at base of operations. The license must be posted in public view at the company base of operations in the county.
(c)
Qualifications of applicant; police clearance. Any applicant for a license is required to provide information showing qualifications on a form provided by the public safety department and to submit to a police clearance consisting of a background investigation or fingerprinting. If the applicant is other than a sole proprietor, all partners, officers, managers and stockholders holding a ten percent or more interest in the company shall be subject to the provisions of this section. An applicant must:
(1)
Be at least 21 years of age.
(2)
Be a citizen of the United States or an alien admitted for permanent residence or who has otherwise been granted employment authorization by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service.
(3)
Have not been convicted, been on probation, parole, or been imprisoned for a period of five years previous to the date of application, or for the violation of any of the following offenses of the state, of any other state, or of the United States: Homicide; rape; aggravated battery; burglary; aggravated assault; kidnapping; robbery; driving under the influence of drugs or alcohol; child molestation; criminal solicitation to commit any of these listed offenses; attempts to commit any of these offenses; any felony where a motor vehicle was used; any crime of violence or theft; any crime of possession, sale or distribution of illegal drugs; and moral turpitude.
(4)
Show on the application a company dispatch location or dispatch terminus located within the geographical boundaries of the county from which business will be conducted. Applicants for limousine operations having no more than three vehicles for hire shall be exempt from having a county-based business location. If located in the corporate limits of the city, the company location must be located in a C-2 general commercial or C-3 heavy commercial zoning district, meet all applicable city zoning or other ordinance requirements, must be staffed by company agents or employees, have a published telephone number and have sufficient parking for accommodating its vehicles for hire when not in use.
(5)
File applications for the initial licensing and inspections of vehicles along with a list of all drivers who will be scheduled for driver permitting and indicate whether drivers are company employees or contract drivers. A copy of each contract driver's insurance coverage must be included in the application.
(6)
Provide a copy of the rate schedule and the daily hours of operation.
(7)
Provide the name, address and telephone number of a responsible individual residing in the county who will be the registered agent for the purpose of serving of process.
(d)
Application fee. Each application for a business license shall be accompanied by a fee in the amount established by the action of the city commissioners, as set forth in the schedule of rates and charges, a copy of which is on file in the city manager's office.
(e)
Rate approval. Rates to be charged by vehicles for hire, as set forth in the schedule of rates and charges, shall be approved by the city commission.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-8, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-57. - Fee.
The license fee for the operation of any business regulated by this chapter shall be fixed by the board of commissioners. The license fee for such business is hereby fixed at an amount as set forth in the schedule of rates and charges per vehicle for the first two vehicles operated and for all vehicles over two vehicles operated by any one person the license fee is hereby fixed at an amount as set forth in the schedule of rates and charges per vehicle.
(Ord. No. O-94-17, § 5, 12-19-94)
Sec. 102-58. - Insurance.
(a)
Liability and property damage required. An applicant for a license required by the provisions of this chapter shall provide, with the application, proof of motor vehicle insurance covering public liability and property damage issued by an insurer approved by the state. Such insurance shall insure passengers and third persons against personal injury and property damage in amounts specified by this section or by the state financial responsibility law, whichever is greater.
(b)
Minimum coverage for taxicabs. Any applicant for a license to operate a taxicab service shall maintain minimum insurance coverage in the following amounts:
(1)
Fifteen thousand dollars bodily injury per person.
(2)
Thirty thousand dollars bodily injury per occurrence.
(3)
Ten thousand dollars property damage.
(4)
Five thousand dollars personal injury protection, as provided in no fault insurance coverage.
(c)
Minimum coverage for limousines. Any applicant for a license to operate a limousine service shall maintain minimum insurance coverage in the following amounts:
(1)
One hundred thousand dollars bodily injury per person.
(2)
Three hundred thousand dollars bodily injury per occurrence.
(3)
Fifty thousand dollars property damage.
(4)
Five thousand dollars personal injury protection, as provided in no fault insurance coverage.
(d)
Notice of cancellation. Before the policy is cancelled for nonpayment of premium or other cause, notice thereof shall be given in writing to the public safety department at least 30 days before the policy lapses. The policy shall further provide that it shall not be cancelled or rendered unenforceable because the insured failed to notify the insurer of an accident or injury, or any other condition upon which notice of claim is ordinarily required.
(e)
Proof of insurance. A licensee under this chapter shall provide to the public safety director or the public safety director's designated representative proof of insurance required under this chapter on or before July 1 each year for all of the vehicles for hire that are in operation on that date. Before any new or substitute vehicle may be operated in the city subsequent to that date, proof of insurance for such vehicle must be provided to the public safety director or the public safety director's designated representative.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-9, 4-6-92; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)

DIVISION 3. - PERMIT

Sec. 102-81. - Permitting generally.
(a)
Required before driver operates vehicle. No person shall drive a vehicle for hire without a driver's permit. No business licensed for operating vehicles for hire shall employ any driver who has not first met the requirements of this chapter and been issued a driver's permit.
(b)
Issuance to drivers of licensed companies. Drivers' permits shall not be issued to any driver not employed by or representing a licensed company.
(c)
Posting; inspection sticker. Driver permits must be posted on the dash or sunvisor of the vehicle being driven so that it is visible from the passenger area. Licensees under this chapter are responsible for checking to ensure that each driver has a current driver permit in the driver's possession and posted on the dash or sunvisor of the vehicle being operated along with a visible vehicle inspection sticker.
(d)
Application; requesting letter from licensed company; police clearance; qualifications. No request for a driver's permit will be processed unless the permit applicant presents a letter on company stationery to the public safety department from a licensed company requesting issuance of a driver's permit to the named individual. The driver's permit applicant will further furnish information requested on a form to be provided by the public safety department and submit to a police clearance consisting of a background investigation or fingerprinting. Driver permit applicants must meet the following requirements:
(1)
Be at least 21 years of age.
(2)
Be a citizen of the United States or an alien admitted for permanent residence or who has otherwise been granted employment authorization by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service.
(3)
Possess a current valid state driver's license. Such license must not be limited as defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-5-58 and O.C.G.A. § 40-5-64.
(4)
Exhibit a proficiency with the English language so as to be able to comprehend and interpret traffic signs, issue written receipts to passengers and obey lawful orders of police and others in lawful authority.
(5)
Have not been convicted, been on probation, parole, or been imprisoned for a period of five years previous to the date of application, or for the violation of any of the following offenses of the state, of any other state, or of the United States: Homicide; rape; aggravated battery; burglary; aggravated assault; kidnapping; robbery; driving under the influence of drugs or alcohol; child molestation; criminal solicitation to commit any of these listed offenses; attempts to commit any of these listed offenses; any felony where a motor vehicle was used; any crime of violence or theft; any crime of possession, sale or distribution of illegal drugs; and moral turpitude. Discharge without court adjudication of guilt pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 42-8-62 shall not disqualify an applicant.
(e)
Report of changes. Drivers are responsible for reporting any change in qualifications or other licensing or permitting information previously supplied to the public safety department within ten days of the change.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-10, 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-82. - Card; fee.
If the chief of police grants a permit to any applicant pursuant to the provisions of this chapter, the city clerk shall issue the applicant a permit card to which a photograph of the driver shall be affixed and which shall be kept in view of the passengers at all times when such person is operating a cab, taxi, or other vehicle. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate any such vehicle upon the streets of the city unless the driver has posted such permit card in plain view of the passengers of the vehicle the driver is driving. Upon the issuance of such permit the city clerk shall collect from the applicant a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Ord. No. O-94-17, § 5, 12-19-94; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 102-83. - Fees generally.
Fees for driver permits shall be established by the city commissioners and set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, are nonrefundable and are not prorated.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-11, 4-6-92)

ARTICLE III. - VEHICLE CONDITIONS AND EQUIPMENT; INSPECTION

Sec. 102-111. - Responsibility for vehicle maintenance.
Drivers and company licensees are responsible for maintaining each vehicle for hire in a clean and mechanically safe condition. The interior and exterior shall meet the requirements set out under inspection requirements outlined in sections 102-112 and 102-113.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-14(1), 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-112. - Operating requirements generally.
Drivers are not to drive and company licensees are not to allow drivers to operate a vehicle without the required markings, a taximeter, top light and inspection sticker as outlined in this article. Limousines are excepted from having markings, top lights, two-way radios or taximeters; however, limousines are required to have a plate attached to their front or rear bumper indicating the company business name.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-14(2), 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-113. - Annual inspection; sticker; vehicle requirements.
All vehicles for hire to be used by a licensee in the city shall be inspected annually and approved by the public safety department, such inspection to be made prior to the issuance or renewal of a company license. Once a vehicle meets the requirements of the inspection, a sticker will be affixed to the left side of the windshield. The requirements that each vehicle must meet are as follows:
(1)
Exterior inspection. Exterior inspection shall ensure that headlights, taillights, brake lights, directional signal lights, license plate lights, windshield wipers, all vehicle glass, window cranks or electric windows, doors and door locks, trunk lid, trunk, hood, door handles, exhaust system, bumpers, fenders, body, tires and other vehicle parts are in good condition and functioning properly. There shall be no tears or rust holes in the vehicle body and no loose pieces hanging from the vehicle body. There shall be no unrepaired body damage or any body condition which would create a safety problem or interfere with the operation of the vehicle.
(2)
Interior inspection. Interior inspection shall include the rearview mirror, steering wheel, foot brakes, parking brakes, air conditioning and heating systems to ensure each item is in good operating condition. The upholstery, floor mats, headlining, door panels and the trunk compartment shall be inspected to ensure there are no tears, that they are clean and have no offensive odors and that the trunk has sufficient space for passenger luggage. Vehicles for hire shall have posted therein in a place plainly visible to passengers hiring such vehicle a schedule of rates, the size, form and substance of which shall be approved by the public safety director.
(3)
Taximeter and other required features and equipment. The vehicle shall have a spare tire and jack, a functional two-way radio and a taximeter. The taximeter is to be positioned so that it is visible from the passenger compartment. Taximeter accuracy shall be verified according to the public safety department's published rules and regulations for vehicles for hire. Vehicles for hire shall have at least four doors and shall be either a sedan or station wagon. All vehicles for hire operated by a single company or licensee shall be painted a uniform color. Limousines are not required to have a two-way radio and a taximeter.
(4)
Random inspection; removal; sticker removal; additional requirements. Vehicles shall be subject to random inspections at any time. Vehicles found to be substandard shall be removed from service immediately and shall be subject to immediate vehicle inspection sticker removal by the public safety department. Additional inspection requirements will be outlined in the public safety department's vehicle rules and regulations governing passenger-carrying vehicles.
(5)
Sticker proof of meeting licensing and insurance and last inspection requirements. The inspection sticker is proof that the company met the licensing and insurance requirements at the time of license issuance and that the vehicle passed the last vehicle inspection. Each vehicle operator must have in the vehicle proof of current insurance coverage. Any company or vehicle letting insurance coverage lapse shall have the inspection sticker removed by the public safety department and be subject to suspension of its business license. Business operations shall not be resumed until proof of insurance is provided to the public safety department, the license reinstated and the vehicle or vehicles reinspected and new inspection stickers issued by the public safety department.
(6)
Vehicles passing county inspection approved for use. Vehicles passing a county inspection will be considered approved for use in the city.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-14(3), 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-114. - Inspection and sticker required for vehicle use.
No business licensed for operating vehicles for hire shall use any vehicle that has not been inspected and had the city inspection sticker affixed.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-14(5), 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-115. - Sticker restrictions.
Inspection stickers are not transferable from vehicle to vehicle and are nonrefundable if the vehicle is wrecked or taken out of service for any reason. The public safety department must be notified within ten days of any vehicle being taken out of service. Stickers from vehicles taken out of service must be turned in to the public safety department. Stickers for replacement vehicles or additional vehicles are issued under the same procedures as original inspection stickers.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-14(6), 4-6-92)
Sec. 102-116. - Inspection fees.
Fees for vehicle inspections shall be established by the city commission, are set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, are nonrefundable and not prorated.
(Ord. No. O-92-09, § 22-11, 4-6-92)
