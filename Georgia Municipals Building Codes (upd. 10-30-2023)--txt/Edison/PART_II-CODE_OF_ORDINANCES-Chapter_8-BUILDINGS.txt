ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Exemption from water saving plumbing device requirements.
(a)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 8-3-2(c) when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewerage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing buildings;
(2)
When such plumbing or sewerage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this chapter were installed;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by persons with disabilities;
b.
Specifically designed it withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(b)
The owner, or his agent, of a building undergoing new construction or repair or renovation, who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (a)(2), (3) or (4) of this section, shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the building inspector for the city. A fee in an amount as established from time to time by the city shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
(Ord. of 6-10-1991, § 5)
Sec. 8-2. - Dangerous buildings.
(a)
State law adopted. This section is adopted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 41-2-7(a) to enable the city to exercise its police power to repair, close or demolish such dwellings, buildings, or structures in the manner provided in O.C.G.A. §§ 41-2-8 through 41-2-17. The provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 41-2-7 through 41-2-17 are hereby adopted and incorporated by reference as if fully set forth in this chapter.
(b)
Police chief designated to enforce chapter. The city chief of police is hereby designated as the public officer authorized to exercise powers prescribed in the statutes adopted in this section.
(c)
Findings of fact. It is found that there exist in the city dwellings, buildings and structures which are unfit for human habitation or for commercial, industrial or business uses due to:
(1)
Dilapidation and defects increasing the hazards of fire, accident, or other calamities;
(2)
The lack of adequate ventilation, light or sanitary facilities; or
(3)
Other conditions rendering such dwellings, buildings or structures unsafe or unsanitary, or dangerous or detrimental to the health, safety or welfare, or otherwise inimical to the welfare of the residents of the city.
(Ord. of 12-10-1990(1), §§ 1, 2; Ord. of 12-10-1990(2), §§ 1, 2)

ARTICLE II. - BUILDING NUMBERING

Sec. 8-23. - Failure to comply; fine.
(a)
Failure to comply with this article is a misdemeanor. The police department or their designee may cause to be issued an arrest warrant, or summons to appear before the municipal court to answer such alleged violation. A summons to appear may be served upon an alleged violator either in person or by certified mail.
(b)
The minimum fine for a violation of this article shall be $25.00 plus court costs.
(Ord. of 3-10-2003)
Sec. 8-24. - Scheme.
(a)
The city has established a new address numbering system, which shall be administered by the Early-Calhoun 911 Department.
(b)
Each house, building, or other occupiable structure shall be assigned a separate number. A number or alphabetical letter shall be assigned for each separate and distinct occupant or division (i.e., apartment, company, etc.) within a building or other occupied structure.
(c)
Existing numbers shall be changed only as necessary to ensure the effectiveness or efficiency of the Enhanced 911 System of Early-Calhoun County, as determined by the judgment of the Early-Calhoun 911 Department.
(d)
All new buildings and structures must be assigned a numbered address by the Early-Calhoun 911 Department, prior to or simultaneous with the granting of: a building permit, septic tank permit, electrical permit, or connecting to any city gas, water or sewerage line.
(e)
Written notification of the address assigned under the terms of this article to each house, building, or structure shall be given or delivered to its respective owner/occupant.
(f)
Written notification of the address assigned under the terms of this article to each house, building or structure shall also be given to the U.S. Postal 911 Address Coordinator.
(Ord. of 3-10-2003)
Sec. 8-25. - Placement.
(a)
The owner, occupant, or agent of each house, building or business shall display the assigned number on the structure no later than March 31, 2003.
(b)
The Early-Calhoun 911 Department will supply the numbers for all structures one time, any subsequent numbers required will be the responsibility of the property owner. The property owner, occupant, or agent of each house, building, or other structure, shall be responsible for installation of the numbers.
(c)
Residential numbers shall be conspicuously placed immediately to the right side of the appropriate door, so that the number is clearly visible from the street, and/or the building number may be placed near the walk, driveway, or common entrance to the building, or placed on the mailbox, gatepost, or other appropriate place, so as to be clearly visible from the street.
(Ord. of 3-10-2003)
Sec. 8-26. - Defacing, altering, destroying, concealing or removing numbers.
(a)
Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to deface, alter, destroy, conceal or remove any 911 address numbers, except by the owner, occupant, or agent, as required for maintenance, upkeep and replacement as required, to maintain the numbers in good readable condition.
(b)
Fine. The minimum fine for a violation of this section shall be $100.00 plus court costs.
(Ord. of 3-10-2003)

Editor's note— A resolution adopted Dec. 10, 2012, added provisions designated as Ch. 8, §§ 8-1—8-6. Inasmuch as there were already provisions so designated, the provisions have been redesignated as Ch. 8, Art. III, §§ 8-51—8-56.
Sec. 8-51. - Purpose.
The purpose of this section is to provide for the administration and enforcement of the Georgia State minimum standard codes as allowed heretofore adopted. Hereinafter, all of the codes heretofore adopted shall be referred to as the "technical codes," as may be adopted by the state or local jurisdiction.
(Res. of 12-10-12, § 8-1)
Sec. 8-52. - Codes adopted.
The following Georgia State minimum standard codes, the latest editions as adopted and as amended, shall be enforced by the city:
(1)
International Building Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(2)
International Mechanical Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(3)
International Fuel Gas Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(5)
National Electrical Code, 2005 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(6)
International Energy Conservation Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(7)
International Residential Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(8)
International Fire Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments.
(Res. of 12-10-12, § 8-2)
Sec. 8-53. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(Res. of 12-10-12, § 8-3)
Sec. 8-54. - Code remedial.
(a)
General. These codes are hereby declared to be remedial, and shall be construed to secure the beneficial interest and purposes thereof - which are public safety, health, and general welfare - through structural strength, stability, sanitation, adequate light and ventilation, and safety to life and property from fire and other hazards attributed to the built environment including alteration, repair, removal, demolition, use and occupancy of buildings, structures, or premises, and by regulating the installation and maintenance of all electrical, gas, mechanical and plumbing systems, which may be referred to as service systems.
(b)
Quality control. Quality control of materials and workmanship is no within the purview of this code except as it relates to the purposes stated herein.
(c)
Permitting and inspection. The inspection or permitting of any building, system or plan, under the requirements of this code shall not be construed in any court as a warranty of the physical condition of such building, system or plan or their adequacy. The City of Edison, nor any employee thereof, shall be liable in tort for damages for any defect or hazardous or illegal condition or inadequacy in such building, system or plan, nor for any failure of any component of such, which may occur subsequent to such inspection or permitting.
(Res. of 12-10-12, § 8-4)
Sec. 8-55. - Applicability to repairs, renovations.
The requirements herein shall apply to any residential construction initiated after December 10, 2012, and to any commercial construction initiated after December 10, 2012, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building.
(Res. of 12-10-12, § 8-5)
Sec. 8-56. - Enforcement; penalty.
(a)
This article shall be enforced by the office of the director of public utilities of the city. Citations for violations maybe issued by the director of public utilities for the city.
(b)
Any person violating this article shall be tried before the municipal court. Any person violating the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided shall be punishable as a misdemeanor.
(Res. of 12-10-12, § 8-6)
