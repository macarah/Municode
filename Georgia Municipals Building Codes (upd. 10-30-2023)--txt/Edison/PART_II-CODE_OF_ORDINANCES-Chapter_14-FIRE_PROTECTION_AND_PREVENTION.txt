Chapter 14 - FIRE PROTECTION AND PREVENTION

FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Fire prevention and protection, O.C.G.A. § 25-1-1 et seq.
Sec. 14-1. - Election of fire chief; appointment of subordinates; responsibility for fire apparatus.
The chief of the fire department shall be elected annually by the council. The chief shall have the authority to appoint assistants and employees subject to the approval of the council. Each fire company shall have the care and management of its respective hoses, ladders, hooks, and apparatus for extinguishing fires.
(Code 1973, § 7-2)
State law reference— Local fire departments, O.C.G.A. § 25-3-1 et seq.; Georgia Firefighter Standards and Training Act, O.C.G.A. § 25-4-1 et seq.
Sec. 14-2. - Authority of chief or acting chief at scene.
The chief of the fire department shall have supreme and absolute control and command of the fire department at all fires, and in his absence the assistant chief shall be vested with his authority, and in the absence of both named officers, the same authority shall devolve upon the senior firefighter present.
(Code 1973, § 7-3)
Sec. 14-3. - Obstructing fire house or vehicle prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully put any trash or other obstructions in front of any fire house or other appurtenance of or belonging to the fire department.
(Code 1973, § 7-4)
Sec. 14-4. - Persons allowed at fire scene.
It shall be unlawful for any person, except firefighters, fire wardens, members of the police force, owners or agents of the property endangered by fire, and interested insurance company representatives, to be within the immediate limits of operation during the progress of any fire in the city, unless ordered there by the officers in command of the fire department or officers of the city.
(Code 1973, § 7-5)
Sec. 14-5. - Fires and combustible material to be used in safe manner.
No person shall use fire or combustible material without due precaution.
(Code 1973, § 10-2(14))
