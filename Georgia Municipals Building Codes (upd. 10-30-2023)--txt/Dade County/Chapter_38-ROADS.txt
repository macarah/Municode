ARTICLE II. - ACCEPTANCE OF ROADS

Sec. 38-31. - Subdivision development roads and rights-of-way.
Subdivision development roads and rights-of-way proposals shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
Proposals must be platted with base flood elevation data certified by a licensed surveyor.
(2)
The development must have a minimum six-inch water main available for all lots if public water is available. Fire hydrants must be installed within one thousand (1,000) feet of any planned development.
(3)
Drainage slopes must include adequate structures of concrete or metal culverts and drainage ditches which are a minimum of fifteen (15) inches deep.
(4)
Access of a minimum of three (3) lots or five (5) mobile home lots for every one-third (⅓) of a mile.
(5)
The development must have a dedicated sixty-foot right-of-way, thirty (30) feet each side of a road centerline; minimum twenty-four-foot roadbed with a five-foot shoulder on each side and thirteen (13) feet on each side available to ensure proper space for slopes, fills, drainage and utilities, for maintenance.
(6)
Curbs and guttering are required if the development is designed with a fifty-foot right-of-way. The roadbed must be twenty-eight (28) feet, including curb and guttering, with eleven (11) feet each side available for utilities, slopes and shoulders, for maintenance.
(7)
Eight (8) inches of compacted base which shall consist of six (6) inches of chert and two (2) inches of stone.
(8)
The roadbed shall be graded smooth with minimum cuts or outcroppings.
(9)
If the grade is less than six percent (6%), the roadbed shall be made of asphalt with two-inch E-mix. If the grade is higher than six percent (6%), a nonskid mix must be approved by the county road superintendent.
(10)
Entry to a public road should be at a ninety-degree angle, where possible; however, the angle shall not exceed sixty (60) degrees. A landing for sixty (60) feet is required at no more than a two percent (2%) grade.
(11)
A minimum of three (3) inspections must be completed by the county road superintendent at stages of development critical to excellent road construction. Final inspection will be after twelve (12) months.
(Res. No. R 1-95, § I, 1-5-1995)
Sec. 38-32. - Existing county road extensions (dead-end roads).
Existing county road extensions (dead-end roads) shall have the following requirements:
(1)
No utility relocations by the county.
(2)
A minimum of a sixty-foot clear right-of-way deeded to the county, fee simple, after the twelve-month final inspection, and subsequent approval.
(3)
The surface of the roadbed must conform to or exceed existing roadbed specifications as follows:
a.
Six (6) inches of chert plus two (2) inches of stone or six (6) inches of compacted aggregate base.
b.
Triple surface treatment according to county specifications.
c.
The county road superintendent shall inspect the roadbed a minimum of two (2) times during construction.
d.
The cul-de-sac shall be completed using roadbed and surface requirements as required by county specifications and shall have a minimum radius of fifty (50) feet. Additionally, the minimum radius of the right-of-way for the cul-de-sac shall be sixty-three (63) feet.
(Res. No. R 1-95, § II, 1-5-1995; Res. No. R-20-07, 8-2-2007)
Sec. 38-33. - Existing road extensions to another county road.
Existing road extensions to another county road shall have the following requirements:
(1)
The same requirements as set forth in section 38-32 but also must conform to the roadbed that has the higher specifications in place.
(2)
If asphalt (E-mix) is required, the inspection must be performed a minimum of three (3) times during construction by the county road superintendent.
(3)
A minimum of five (5) homes or eight (8) mobile homes must be in place for a minimum of five (5) years at the time of petition for acceptance.
(Res. No. R 1-95, § III, 1-5-1995)
Sec. 38-34. - State graded roads.
State graded roads are required to have eighty-foot rights-of-way. The roadbed will conform to the twenty-four-foot requirement with five-foot shoulders on each side.
(Res. No. R 1-95, § IV, 1-5-1995)
Sec. 38-35. - Generally.
(a)
The final inspection of any petitioned road must be completed by the county road superintendent a minimum of twelve (12) months after the last construction inspection. Drainage problems and poor quality construction will be considered prior to recommendation for county acceptance.
(b)
Petitioners are not allowed to promise that the county will accept the road. If such promise is made, the petitioner must pay damages to the purchaser in such amounts prescribed by statute.
(c)
The county will not accept any road that has a grade greater than ten percent (10%).
(d)
Crossfalls must be a minimum of one-eighth (1/8) inch per foot on all finished surfaces. Super-elevated sections must be determined by the county road superintendent.
(e)
The county may not desire to accrue a new road; therefore, even when the minimum standards have been met, the county may exercise its right not to accept a road into the system.
(f)
Unless the county specifically desires to obtain a road, all efforts to obtain rights-of-way will be the responsibility of the party desiring to turn the road over to the county.
(g)
When the county manager determines that a road qualifies for acceptance by the county, he/she will bring the matter before the board of commissioners. If the board of commissioners agrees that the road should be accepted, it will do so contingent on delivery of completed right-of-way deeds, within sixty (60) days.
(h)
Alterations in the roadbed on existing roads must be presented to the county road superintendent and the county manager. If the determination is made to alter the roadbed for good reason, the petitioner must present a deed as required within sixty (60) calendar days after the board of commissioners has agreed to make such alterations.
(Res. No. R 1-95, § V, 1-5-1995)

ARTICLE III. - ROAD ADDRESSING

Sec. 38-66. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Address program administrator means the official of the county charged with the administration of this article, including his authorized representative. The address program administrator shall be the 911 administrator of the county.
Building means a structure for residential or business use, having a roof supported by columns or walls for the shelter of persons. When separated by division walls from the ground up, without openings, each such enclosure may be deemed a separate building.
Concurrence means the board of commissioners shall have the right to disapprove any action of the address program administrator by a majority vote. If the board of commissioners shall fail to act within two (2) regular meetings after the action of the address program administrator, the board of commissioners shall be deemed to have concurred with such action of the address program administrator.
Driveway means any private way, beginning at the property line of a lot abutting a public road, private road, easement or private right-of-way, giving access from the public road, private road, easement or private right-of-way, and leading to a building, lot or physical location.
Private road means any one-way or two-way road for ingress and/or egress, not for use by the general public, that serves two (2) or more properties.
Public road means any road, street, highway, thoroughfare or other way of passage that has been irrevocably dedicated to the public, or in which the public has acquired rights by prescription, without regard to whether such road is open for travel.
Road address means the combination of numbers and road names assigned under the terms of this article which identifies a particular building, lot or other physical location.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 3, 12-7-1995)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 38-67. - Administration and application.
The address program administrator, with the concurrence of the board of commissioners, will be responsible for the interpretation and administration of this article, including:
(1)
Assigning all numbers for properties and buildings.
(2)
Compiling a database of addresses of each property and building.
(3)
Recommending change of existing addresses, when necessary, to facilitate sequential house numbers.
(4)
Designating individual unit addresses within multiple housing units in conformity with this article.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 4, 12-7-1995)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Sec. 38-68. - Display of road address numbers.
(a)
Every owner of improved property shall purchase and display official address numbers so that they are clearly visible from the street or road on which the property fronts. The following criteria shall be used to properly display such address number:
(1)
Address numbers must be a minimum of three (3) inches in height for residential uses so as to be seen easily from the road.
(2)
Numbers for multiple dwelling units and nonresidential buildings shall be at least six (6) inches in height and shall be placed on the front of the building facing the road or on the end of the building nearest the road. Individual units shall be required to display a unit number which shall be at least three (3) inches in height on the front door or immediately adjacent to the door.
(3)
Address numbers must be set on a background of a contrasting color.
(4)
On a corner lot, the house number should face the street named in the address.
(5)
If a building is more than seventy-five (75) feet from any road, the address number shall be displayed on a sign attached to a fence gate, lawn stake, mailbox, etc., adjacent to and clearly visible from the road right-of-way.
(6)
Address numbers shall be plain block numeric numbers and not in alpha or cursive style print.
(b)
The address program administrator, with the concurrence of the board of commissioners, will have the right to authorize and approve alternate methods of displaying numbers which meet the intent of this article, when strict adherence to this article cannot be met.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 5, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-69. - Types of numbers.
The following types of numbers are to be used under this article:
(1)
A primary number is to be assigned to each street frontage of each parcel of land, whether or not the parcel is occupied. The primary number is required to be posted only if the parcel is occupied by a dwelling unit, structure or active use and the owner, occupant or person in charge is notified as set forth in section 38-68. Other primary numbers are reserved for future development of the numbered parcels and will be assigned at the time of development.
(2)
Secondary numbers may be used when a number of units, structures and uses coexist on the same parcel of land. Examples of parcels requiring secondary numbers include apartment projects, mobile home parks, office parks, planned unit developments, recreational vehicle parks, recreational areas, shopping centers and other uses where the use of secondary numbers would clarify the location of a unit or use for public safety purposes. To provide secondary numbers, the address program administrator shall work with the owner, manager or person in charge of the project to determine a logical numbering system under the following guidelines:
a.
A building on a single parcel with more than one (1) and less than five (5) units may be given either letter (preferred) or number designations as requested by the owner of the property.
b.
A single building on a single parcel of land with five (5) or more units may be given numerical designations such as suite number, apartment number or similar designations, if so desired.
c.
Multiple buildings on the same parcel of land may be given secondary numbers consisting of number designation if the buildings are accessed from a main entrance to the project. The number designations should increase in a clockwise direction from the main entrance.
d.
Mobile home parks, recreational vehicle parks and similar uses shall be given letter designations for lots or section and number designations for individual lots or sites.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 6, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-70. - Exempt structures.
The intent of this section is to exclude from this article those structures that do not present a significant danger to human life if destroyed by fire or other events. The following structures shall be excluded from the provisions of this article:
(1)
Agricultural buildings not requiring a separate mailing address such as a barn, poultry house, outbuildings or equipment storage buildings. Buildings used as a dwelling unit, office or nominal work station of an employee shall not be exempt.
(2)
Storage and accessory buildings for the use of the occupant of another building on the property. Buildings used as dwelling units, offices or the nominal work station of an employee, or requiring a separate mailing address, shall not be exempt.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 7, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-71. - Existing road names.
The names of roads listed on the official county road map sheet as developed by 911 that are located within the unincorporated areas of the county, whether public or private, shall not be changed by any methods except those set forth in this article. A copy of this article, and a list of road names, shall be forwarded to the department of transportation. The official county 911 road registry shall cross reference the old road names.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 8, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-72. - New road names.
(a)
No new public roads in the unincorporated area of the county shall be named without approval of the address program administrator with the concurrence of the board of commissioners.
(b)
No new private road names shall be recognized by this article or added to the county's list of road names without prior approval of the address program administrator with the concurrence of the board of commissioners.
(c)
The name of any new road, public or private, shall not duplicate or be confusingly similar to any other road name already named by this article or any subsequent ordinances or amendments thereto. This subsection shall not apply to an extension of existing roads.
(d)
New subdivision roads, whether public or private, shall be named when created through the subdivision process which shall include submission of the road names to the address program administrator for approval.
(e)
Developers establishing new public roads shall pay the county for the actual cost of purchasing and erecting street signs which meet the standards of the department of transportation.
(f)
Developers desiring to use any type of sign, other than the style which meets standards set forth by the department of transportation, on a public road, shall have the style and design approved by the address program administrator with the concurrence of the board of commissioners.
(g)
The names of private roads, including mobile home roads, which require naming to facilitate postal delivery and emergency response, shall be recognized by the county at the request of the citizens who own property adjacent to the road. Such road must serve at least three (3) households. The name of the private road shall be commonly known to the community. If there are two (2) or more recommendations of names for such road, the county will recognize the name preferred by at least fifty-one percent (51%) of the property owners on the road; however, this recognition shall not constitute acceptance of dedication of the road by the county for maintenance or other purposes.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 9, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-73. - Numbers for future structures and lots.
All residential and business buildings erected after the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived shall be assigned a number prior to the issuance of a building permit. Owners shall purchase and display such number as provided in section 38-68.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 11, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-74. - Utility company compliance.
(a)
Following the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived, all utility services regulated by the state public service commission, or any utility cooperative service organization providing service in the unincorporated area of the county, shall withhold service from any building, dwelling, mobile home or other structure not exempt under this article, until the owner or other requesting party has furnished the utility with a valid assigned number.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the address program administrator to notify each affected utility of each implementation area by issuing maps and lists of official numbers assigned each structure or use.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 12, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-75. - Implementations by county departments.
Every department and official under the jurisdiction of the board of commissioners shall not render any nonemergency service until the occupant, owner or requesting individual has provided the department with a valid assigned number. Nonemergency service includes any service, except law enforcement, fire, rescue or emergency medical service.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 13, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-76. - Renaming roads.
The provisions of the Road Renaming Resolution adopted on July 6, 1993, shall be amended to provide as follows:
(1)
Property owners who want to have the name of a road changed shall submit a petition to the address program administrator. The address program administrator will make recommendations to the board of commissioners after the following criteria have been met:
a.
A nonrefundable fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk for each road shall accompany any petition requesting that an existing road name be changed.
b.
The petition shall be in substantial conformity with the provisions of such resolution.
c.
If the address program administrator approves the request, with the concurrence of the board of commissioners, the petitioners shall be required to pay the county for the cost of purchasing new signs and erecting such signs, including labor.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 10, 12-7-1995)
Sec. 38-77. - Enforcement; penalty.
Owners or occupants of buildings already constructed which do not comply with this article will be notified and requested to meet the requirements of this article within sixty (60) days from the date of notification. A warning notice will be issued after sixty (60) days if the requirements have not been met. The owner or occupant who does not voluntarily comply with this article within thirty (30) days after delivery of a warning notice by registered or certified mail shall be subject to criminal prosecution. Any person violating a provision of the county road addressing ordinance shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished by a fine as provided in section 1-19, and if such violation is continued, each day's violation shall be a separate offense. Violation of this article may also be enforced by seeking an order to compel compliance of civil remedies against the violator. Prosecutions for violation of the county road addressing ordinance shall be upon citation as provided in O.C.G.A. §§ 15-10-62 and 15-10-63 in the magistrate court of the county. The address program administrator is authorized to issue citations for the enforcement of the provisions of the county road naming and addressing ordinance in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.
(Ord. No. O-4-95, § 14, 12-7-1995)
Secs. 38-78—38-85. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - UTILITY ACCOMMODATIONS IN RIGHTS-OF-WAY

Sec. 38-86. - Title; definitions.
(a)
This article shall be known as the "Dade County Utility Accommodations in Rights-of-Way Ordinance".
(b)
As used in this article, unless the context clearly requires otherwise, the following words or phrases shall have the following meanings:
Applicant means a person submitting all required information necessary for the director of public works to consider issuance of a permit to conduct excavation or construction activity within the public right-of-way or county road.
Construction means the planning, location, surveying, designing, supervising, inspecting, paving, striping, modifying, grading, relocating, fixing, and the actual installation of a utility facility within the county road or county right-of-way.
County means the unincorporated areas of Dade County, Georgia, and any division, department, agency, authority, instrumentality, or branch thereof, or the county governing authority, its board of commissioners, director of public works, or other county officers in charge of the roads, bridges and revenues of the county.
County road means a highway, road, street, avenue, toll road, toll way, drive, detour, or other way open to the public, and intended for use for its enjoyment and for the passage of vehicles in the county, including but not limited to the following public rights, structures, sidewalks, facilities, and appurtenances incidental to the construction, maintenance, and enjoyment of such rights-of-way: surface, shoulders, and sides; bridges; causeways; viaducts; overpasses; underpasses; railroad grade crossings; tunnels; signs, signals, markings or other traffic control devices; way-side parks; parking facilities; drainage ditches; canals and culverts; and scenic easements and easements of light, air, view, and access.
Damage means any impact or exposure that results in the need to repair utility facilities due to the weakening or the partial or complete destruction of a facility, including, but not limited to, the protective coating, lateral support, cathodic protection or the housing for the line, device, or facility.
Permit means an authorization which grants permission to conduct specific regulated activities on, in, over, under or within the public right-of-way, in which a sub-condition set forth in this article and the permit document.
Utility means all privately, publicly, or cooperatively owned systems for producing, transmitting, or distributing communication, data, information, telecommunication, cable television, power, electricity, light, heat, gas, oil, crude products, water/sewer, steam, fire and police signals, traffic control devices, and street lighting systems, and housing or conduit for any of the foregoing, which directly or indirectly serve the public or any part thereof. The term utility may also be used to refer to the owner, operator, provider, servicer, or any agent thereof, of any above-described utility or utility facility.
Utility facilities means pipes, mains, conduits, cable wires, poles, towers, traffic and other signals, and other equipment, of any utility, in, on, along, over, or under the county roads or rights-of-way.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § I)
Sec. 38-87. - Standards for utility facilities in rights-of-way.
(a)
Adopted. All utilities maintaining or locating their facilities on or in county roads or rights-of-way shall maintain or locate their facilities in accordance with the state department of transportation's utility accommodation policy and standards, as they may be amended from time to time and which are hereby adopted for all county roads and rights-of-way. A copy of the manual shall be maintained at the offices of the director of public works or his designee and the clerk of the board of commissioners, and shall be open for public inspection.
(b)
Amendments. The 1988 Utility Accommodation Policy and Standards manual promulgated by the state department of transportation, as may be amended from time to time, adopted in subsection (a) of this section, is amended in order to equate state definitions and provisions with their appropriate and equivalent county counterparts, such that a policy shall be implemented to reflect the intent and effect of the state right-of- way policy as it would logically apply to county roads or rights-of-way, including, but not limited to the following:
(1)
Commissioner = county director of public works;
(2)
Department = county department (of public works);
(3)
District engineer = county director of public works or his or her designee;
(4)
Highway = any road that is part of the county road system;
(5)
State = county;
(6)
State bridge and structural design engineer = county director of public works or his or her designee;
(7)
State highway (chief) engineer = county director of roads or his or her designee;
(8)
State utilities engineer = county director of roads or his or her designee;
(9)
Telephone booth = shall include any booth or structure, to be used by the public at large, which houses a communications link for local or long distance communications, including but not limited to data communication ports, terminals, kiosks, structures or jacks for connection with local or long distance communications;
(10)
Utility = shall be defined as stated in § 38-86.
(c)
Administration. The director of public works shall have the authority to administer the state department of transportation's utility accommodation policy and standards, as amended from time to time by the state or county, on county roads and rights-of-way in accordance with any policies and procedures the director of public works may establish hereunder and as approved by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § II)
Sec. 38-88. - Permit required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to install, construct, maintain, renew, remove, or relocate utility facilities in, on, along, over, or under county roads or rights-of-way without having first complied with all county ordinances regulating the use of county roads and rights-of-way and without having first applied for and received a utility permit from the director of public works allowing such utility usage of the county roads and rights-of-way.
(b)
The director of public works shall adopt permitting policies and procedures, pursuant to approval by board of commissions, as deemed necessary to implement the requirements of this article.
(c)
The board of commissioners shall be authorized to charge permit application fees in accordance with the state department of transportation's utility accommodations policy and standards and any other applicable laws that exist now or may be enacted in the future. Fees shall be determined by the director of the public works and subject to the approval of and publicly disclosed by the board of commissioners. Any fee schedule shall be posted at the offices of the director of public works or his designee and the clerk of the board of commissioners and open for public inspection.
(d)
After work approved under this section has been completed, the utility shall repair the pavement, or portion of rights-of-way, that has been encroached upon. Repair work shall be completed in accordance with all of the work specifications on file with the director of public works and to the director's satisfaction.
(e)
The rights-of-way shall be kept clear of any form of unnecessary obstruction. Anyone not having written permission from the director of public works to place any type of structure or material, including junk or debris, on the rights-of-way shall be subject to legal action and/or prosecution as provided by law.
(f)
The issuance of a permit under this article is a license for a permissive use only and shall not be construed so as to give the grantee any permanent right or exclusive easement. A permit issued under this chapter shall only authorize that construction specifically shown in the application, and construction shall be in strict compliance with the plan submitted with the application, unless the county has granted an exception.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § III)
Sec. 38-89. - Facilities.
Each utility with facilities located on county roads or rights-of-way shall furnish the department of roads with a map or drawing showing the county roads or rights-of-way on which the utility's facilities are located, and shall furnish the map or drawing within sixty (60) days if the same is requested by the director of public works. Each utility furnishing such map to the director of public works shall be entitled to receive notice from the director of public works before the issuance of any permit to another utility for construction work that might, in the opinion of the director of public works, effect the operations or services of the utility so notified. The applicant for any permit, upon request of any utility so notified, shall meet with such utility at the proposed location of construction work and review the proposed construction work as set out in the application.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § IV)
Sec. 38-90. - Removal, relocation or repair.
(a)
The county shall have superior rights to the use of its public roads and public right-of-way.
(b)
The county reserves the right to subsequently require any utility hereunder to remove, relocate, or repair the utility facility at the sole cost of the utility so that the county may make future improvements or operations in the county road system or any road right-of-way of the county road system, and each utility shall remove, relocate, or repair same as so requested by the county within sixty (60) days after receiving written notice with reference to the same from the county. Should the utility fail to remove, relocate, or repair any such utility facility, the county may so remove, relocate or repair the same and submit a statement of the total costs for the same to the utility. Within thirty (30) days of receiving such statement, the utility shall pay to the county the full cost of such removal, relocation or repairs. By the placement or maintenance of any utility facility hereunder, the utility agrees that in the event the utility fails or refuses to pay such costs incurred by the county for such removal, relocation or repairs, and it becomes necessary for the county to commence an action or proceeding in any court of competent jurisdiction to recover such cost, the county shall be entitled to recover, in addition to the statutory court costs and disbursements, such additional sums as the court may adjudge reasonable for attorneys' fees to be allowed the county in any such action or proceeding.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § V)
Sec. 38-91. - Liability for damages.
(a)
Any and all damages to county roads or rights-of-way caused by a utility must be repaired immediately by the utility to the satisfaction of the director of public works. If satisfactory repairs are not made, any costs or expenses incurred by the county in repairing or replacing the structure which are not left in the original condition by the utility shall be chargeable to the utility, including reasonable attorneys' fees incurred in connection with such costs or expenses. The maintenance of a utility's facilities on county roads or rights-of-way shall be deemed as that utility's agreement to pay for such attorneys' fees as provided in this section.
(b)
The county, its officers, employees, contractors, or other agents, shall not be held responsible or liable for injury or damage that may occur to facilities covered by a permit issued under this chapter, or to any connection or connections thereto, by reasons of road maintenance or construction activities or road contractors or utilities operations. The county, its officers, employees, contractors, or other agents shall not be held liable for any damages that may occur to utility facilities if the utility has been notified of the construction conflict and given reasonable time to mark or relocate its facilities and has failed to do so.
(c)
The utility shall indemnify and hold harmless the county and all of the officers, employees, or other agents of the county, against any and all claims, damages, demands, actions, causes of death, costs and expenses, of whatever nature, which may result from any injury to or the death of any person(s) or from the loss of, or damage to, property of any kind or nature, if such injury, loss, or death arises out of the construction, operation, maintenance, repair, removal or relocation of facilities pursuant to a permit issued under this chapter.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § VI)
Sec. 38-92. - Violations; penalties; authority to stop work.
If any provision of this chapter is violated, the county may cause all work to be stopped, the utility facility under construction to be removed, and the roadway to be repaired to its original condition. In addition to other remedies or penalties set forth in this article, any person, firm, corporation, association, partnership violating any provision of this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereinafter be amended, or shall fail to do anything required by this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the county magistrate court, and shall be punished as provided in § 1-19 of the county code of ordinances. In the event any provision of this chapter has been violated or is being violated, in addition to other remedies, the county may institute injunction, mandamus, or other appropriate action or proceeding to correct or abate such violation.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § VII)
Sec. 38-93. - Enforcement of other laws, rules, or regulations not limited; severability.
This article shall not limit the authority of any state, local agency, or department of the county to enforce any other laws, rules, regulations or ordinances relating to utilities. In the event any section, subsection, sentence, clause, or phrase of this article is declared or adjudged to be unconstitutional, or otherwise contrary to law, such adjudication shall in no manner affect any other section, subsection, sentence, clause or phrase, and shall remain in full force and effect as if the section, subsection, sentence, clause, or phrase so declared or adjudged invalid or unconstitutional, or not originally a part hereof.
(Ord. of 7-7-2005, § VIII)

ARTICLE V. - DRIVEWAY PIPE INSTALLATION

Sec. 38-101. - Title; definitions.
(a)
This article shall be known as the "Dade County Driveway Pipe Installation Ordinance".
(b)
As used in this article, unless the context clearly requires otherwise, the following words or phrases shall have the following meanings:
County means the unincorporated areas of Dade County, Georgia, and any division, department, agency, authority, instrumentality, or branch thereof, its board of commissioners, director of public works, or other county officers in charge of the roads, bridges, or rights-of-way of the county.
Driveway includes any new ingress or egress to private or public property from a county road right-of-way, and shall include ingress or egress for temporary or permanent use.
Permit means authorization from the county to connect a driveway to a county road right-of-way and/or to install driveway pipe on the county road rights-of-way.
(Ord. No. 05-03-07, § I, 5-3-2007)
Sec. 38-102. - Permit required; actions prohibited.
(a)
A permit shall be required before connecting a driveway to county roads or before installing driveway pipe in the county road rights-of-way. This requirement shall be applicable to all driveways located in the county, whether the driveways be for residential, commercial, temporary, or permanent use, and shall include without limitation access driveways.
(b)
Drainage ditches along county road rights-of-way shall not be temporarily filled for access to building sites. No debris, excavation, building materials or other such things shall be placed on the county right-of-way at the construction sites.
(Ord. No. 05-03-07, § II, 5-3-2007)
Sec. 38-103. - Permit conditions.
A permit shall be issued under this article subject to the following terms:
(1)
Applications for a permit for a driveway and/or driveway pipe installation shall be made with the county building official or public works department.
(2)
The application shall include the name and telephone number of the applicant, the exact address or detailed description of the location for which access to the county road right-of-way is requested, and such other information as may be requested by for the public works director or county building official in the furtherance of the required duties under this article.
(3)
The applicant shall be advised that two (2) inspection phases, as required by this article, shall be as follows:
a.
Initial investigation of the site to determine the proper pipe size and installation procedures required; and
b.
Final inspection prior to pouring or laying the permanent driveway surface.
(Ord. No. 05-03-07, § III, 5-3-2007)
Sec. 38-104. - Specifications for pipe installation.
(a)
Prior to installation, all pipes to be installed on county road rights-of-way must be approved for use by the county.
(b)
Pipe to be installed under this article must meet the following minimum requirements and such other characteristics as required by the county:
(1)
All pipes shall be either class 3 reinforced concrete or corrugated metal. The public works department will determine which type pipe is to be installed on the initial inspection.
(2)
Metal driveway pipe shall be a minimum of twelve-gauge galvanized corrugated pipe.
(3)
The diameter of the pipe required is to be determined upon the initial site inspection by a representative from the public works department.
(4)
Pipe culverts for residential driveways must be a minimum of twenty (20) feet in length, plus any additional length requirements due to special conditions such as ditches over two (2) feet in depth, high fills or other safety considerations.
(5)
Arch pipe will be required when the depth of the ditch is not sufficient to handle standard round pipe.
(6)
Pipe culverts for commercial driveways shall be determined individually to suit conditions, use and safety.
(7)
The material used to cover pipes shall be required to be of a good grade of chert, sandy clay, or its equivalent, and shall be free from particles over two (2) inches in size. All cover materials shall be well compacted.
(8)
The pipe shall be laid on a well-compacted true-to-line grade with a uniform bearing throughout the line. The line must have a minimum of one-tenth foot of fall per ten (10) feet.
(Ord. No. 05-03-07, § IV, 5-3-2007)
Sec. 38-105. - Enforcement; penalties for violations.
(a)
If any provision of this article is violated, the county may cause all work to be stopped, the driveway pipe or other installation be removed from the county right-of-way, and the roadway be repaired to its' original condition. In addition to other remedies or penalties set forth in this article, any person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership violating any provision of this article as the same exists or as it may hereinafter be amended, or shall fail to do anything required by this article, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the county magistrate court, and shall be punished as provided in § 1-19 of the county code of ordinances. In the event any provision of this article has been violated or is being violated, in addition to other remedies, the county may institute an injunction, mandamus, or other appropriate action or proceeding to correct or date such violation.
(b)
Any and all damages to county roads rights-of-way caused by violation of this article must be repaired immediately to the satisfaction of the director of public works. If satisfactory repairs are not made, any costs or expenses incurred by the county in repairing the county right-of-way shall be chargeable to any individuals or entity violating this article, including reasonable attorneys fees incurred in connection with such costs or expense.
(Ord. No. 05-03-07, § V, 5-3-2007)
Sec. 38-106. - Enforcement of other laws not limited; severability.
This article shall not limit the authority of any state, local agency, or department of the county to enforce any other laws, rules, regulations, or ordinances. In the event any section, subsection, sentence, clause, or phrase of this article is declared or adjudged to be unconstitutional, or otherwise contrary to law, such adjudication shall in no manner affect any other section, subsection, sentence, clause or phrase, and shall remain in full force and effect as if the section, subsection, sentence, clause or phase so declared or adjudged invalid or unconstitutional was not originally a part hereof. This article shall become effective immediately upon its adoption, and all ordinances in conflict with this article are repealed to the extent of the conflict.
(Ord. No. 05-03-07, § VI, 5-3-2007)
