ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this chapter shall be budgeted and approached annually, and from time to time.
(Code 1998, § 2-101)
Sec. 30-2. - Contract for the conduct of city elections.
The Darien City Council may authorize McIntosh County or the Board of Elections and Registration of McIntosh County or both to conduct elections of the City of Darien pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45 upon such terms as the Darien City Council may deem appropriate.
(Ord. of 7-21-2009(2))
Secs. 30-3—30-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - REGISTRATION

Sec. 30-21. - Challenge of registration.
Any elector of the municipality shall have the right to challenge the registration of any person whose name appears on the registration list in the manner set forth below:
(1)
Appeal to registrar. Any challenge of a person's right to register shall be made in the first instance to the registrar, who shall upon hearing the evidence decide whether to affirm or reverse the original decision.
(2)
Appeal to superior court. Any person asserting a challenge under this section shall have the right to appeal from the decision of the registrar to the superior court. Said appeal must be filed in writing within ten days from the date of the decision complained. The decision of the registrar shall remain in force until such time as the appeal is decided.
(Code 1998, § 2-201)
Sec. 30-22. - Permanency of registration.
Registration of an elector will remain permanent if the elector votes in at least one election every three years. If such person does not vote in at least one general or special election or primary in a three-year period and does not specially request continuation of his registration, then the elector's name will be removed from the registration list and he shall be required to reregister in the manner provided for original registration.
(Code 1998, § 2-202)
Sec. 30-23. - Absentee registration.
Absentee registration shall not be allowed in any municipal primary or general or special election held in the city.
(Code 1998, § 2-203)
Secs. 30-24—30-49. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - CANDIDATES

Sec. 30-50. - Notice of candidacy.
Each candidate desiring to have his name placed on the ballot for an office to fill in a municipal general or special election shall file personally, or by his agent, notice of his candidacy in a manner and accompanied by the documents and information required by state law.
(Code 1998, § 2-301)
Sec. 30-51. - Qualification fees.
The qualification fee for candidates filing a notice of candidacy in any general or special election shall be set by the council. Such fee shall be paid to the municipal election superintendent at the time of filing such notice of candidacy.
(Code 1998, § 2-302)
Sec. 30-52. - Campaign financing disclosures.
All candidates for municipal public office shall keep all such records and file all such reports as are required by state law.
(Code 1998, § 2-303)
State Law reference— Financial disclosure statements for candidates generally, O.C.G.A. § 21-5-50 et seq.
Secs. 30-53—30-77. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - VOTING

Sec. 30-78. - Election officials.
(a)
Appointment. The following election officials shall be appointed by the mayor and council and shall receive such compensation as is provided by the same:
(1)
Municipal election superintendent;
(2)
Chief manager;
(3)
Two assistant managers;
(4)
Clerks, as necessary.
(b)
Qualifications and powers. The municipal election superintendent and all poll officers shall meet such qualifications and exercise all such powers and duties as are provided in Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.).
(Code 1998, § 2-401)
Sec. 30-79. - Election districts.
There shall be two election districts within the corporate limits of the city, to be designated District 1 (formerly known as "South Ward") and District 2 (formerly known as "North Ward").
(Code 1998, § 2-402)
Sec. 30-80. - Absentee ballots.
Absentee ballots shall be used in all municipal elections and such use shall be governed by state law.
(Code 1998, § 2-405)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
Secs. 30-81—30-105. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - IMPEACHMENT AND REMOVAL

Sec. 30-106. - Grounds for removal.
The mayor or any councilman shall be subject to removal from office for any one or more of the following causes:
(1)
Incompetence, misfeasance, or malfeasance in office;
(2)
Conviction of a crime involving moral turpitude;
(3)
Failure at any qualifications of law;
(4)
Knowingly violating any express prohibition of the municipal charter;
(5)
Abandonment of office or neglect to perform the duties thereof; or
(6)
Failure for any other cause to perform the duties of office as required by charter or by law.
(Code 1998, § 2-501)
Sec. 30-107. - Recall elections.
Any election to remove an elected official from city office shall be conducted according to state law.
(Code 1998, § 2-504)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 21-4-1 et seq.
