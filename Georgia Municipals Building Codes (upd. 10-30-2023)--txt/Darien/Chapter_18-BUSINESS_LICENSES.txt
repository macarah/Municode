ARTICLE II. - LICENSING STANDARDS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 18-19. - Application.
modified
(a)
Each and every person, firm, or corporation transacting or offering to transact any business or working at or offering to work at any trade, occupation, or profession within the limits of the city (except attorneys at law and others who are exempt by law) shall obtain a license to transact such business, trade, occupation or profession to be issued by the city manager in accordance with further provisions of this chapter, notwithstanding the provision of section 18-25(c).
(b)
No business license shall be issued or renewed by the city until the applicant or permittee demonstrates compliance with all provisions of this Code, municipal ordinances, and state and county laws, rules and/or regulations.
(c)
If a business license is denied, reapplication may not be made by the same person or entity for a period of 180 days.
(Code 1998, § 17-101; Ord. No. 2022-06, § I, 6-21-2022; Ord. No. 2022-10, § I, 8-16-2022)
Sec. 18-20. - Term of license.
Licenses to be issued shall authorize the carrying on of a particular business, trade, occupation, or profession for a period of a calendar year beginning January 1, and expiring at midnight, December 31 of the year for which granted. Any new business shall obtain a license before beginning business. Any person licensed under this article or any previous licensing ordinance shall renew their licenses on or before January 15 of each year.
(Code 1998, § 17-102)
Sec. 18-21. - Fees and procedures for issuance.
At the time of application for a license, the applicant shall pay to the clerk and treasurer of the city all license fees as hereinafter provided. License fees shall be set in November of each year by the mayor and council in regular meeting. License fees shall be fixed for each business, trade, occupation, or profession for the succeeding year, subject to review by the incoming mayor and council at any regular meeting held in January of the succeeding year, in those years in which an election for a mayor and council is held. Licenses issued during November of the year before the incoming mayor and council take office shall be deemed valid licenses, but the fee paid therefor may be reduced or increased by the mayor and council. Once a license fee has been set by the mayor and council in accordance with the provisions of this section, the same shall remain in effect for future years until changed by a valid action thereon by the mayor and council under the provisions of this section.
(Code 1998, § 17-103)
Sec. 18-22. - Fees for businesses, trades, occupations not included in the general schedule.
The mayor and council may, at any regular meeting, by a simple resolution fix a license fee for any business, trade, occupation or profession not included in the general schedule. Also, the mayor and council may, in exercise of sound discretion, fix special combination license fees for particular business establishments engaged in a variety of activities so long as the total fee bears a reasonable relationship to the individual license fees set for specific activities.
(Code 1998, § 17-104)
Sec. 18-23. - Transfers.
No license granted to any person for any particular business, trade, occupation or profession, as provided for in any of the preceding sections, shall authorize any such person to carry on any kind of business at his place of business other than that for which it is specifically granted; nor shall it authorize such person to carry on or conduct his said business at any place other than the one for which the license was granted. Licenses may be transferred in the event of the sale of a business, provided approval of the city manager is first obtained.
(Code 1998, § 17-105)
Sec. 18-24. - Exemptions.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to transact any business or work at or offer to work at any business, trade, occupation or profession within the city limits (except those exempt by law) without first having obtained a license therefor under the provisions of this chapter; except any person, firm, or corporation holding a license which expired on December 31 of a given year, shall not be considered to be violating this chapter by continuing to do business on or after January 1 of the succeeding year provided license for the succeeding year is properly obtained and the license fee paid on or before January 15 of such succeeding year, except for whiskey or other alcoholic beverage licenses which must be obtained and license fee paid on or before their expiration date of December 31 of each year.
(Code 1998, § 17-107)
Sec. 18-25. - Penalties.
modified
(a)
If any person, firm or corporation embraced in this article shall fail or refuse to obtain a license and pay the license fee therein required of them for the year for which the same is required within the time required, he, she or they shall be notified by the chief of police to take out and pay for a license instanter, and upon failure to comply, shall be guilty of a breach of this chapter, and upon conviction before the city court, shall be required to at once take out and pay said license tax, and shall be punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-8.
(b)
For all said sums, the clerk shall issue an execution against said delinquent and against his, her, or their property and place same in the hands of the chief of police for levy and sale under the same rules as govern the levy and sale under tax executions.
(c)
Any person, firm or corporation which conducts business without first obtaining a business license issued by the city manager, shall only be issued a business license by the mayor and council at a regular business meeting. This is in addition to any other penalties the person, firm or corporation may be subject to.
(Code 1998, § 17-109; Ord. No. 2022-10, § I, 8-16-2022)
Sec. 18-26. - Proration of fees/taxes.
Any new applications for an alcoholic beverage license received or occupational tax for a business beginning after July 1 of any year shall pay one-half of the annual fee or tax for a license or tax payment for the remaining portion of that year.
(Ord. of 11-17-2009)

DIVISION 2. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
Subdivision I. - In General

Sec. 18-54. - License required.
Each brewer, wholesale dealer, and retail dealer of malt beverages, as such terms are defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-5-42, each person engaged in the business of retailing wine, and each manufacturer, wholesale distributor and retail distributor of distilled spirits doing business within the city shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(a), 17-203(a), 17-204(a))
Sec. 18-55. - Annual license fees established by ordinance.
The annual business license fees for malt beverage brewers, retail dealers, and wholesale dealers, retail wine distributors, and distilled spirit manufacturers and distributors whose places of business are located within the city shall be set by ordinance or resolution of the city council and made available for public examination in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(b), 17-203(b), 17-204(b))
Sec. 18-56. - Publication of notice of application for license.
No application for a license for the sale or manufacture of malt beverages or distilled spirits or the retail sale of wine within the city shall be acted upon by the city council until after notice has been run in a newspaper of general circulation in the city at least ten days prior to the regular meeting at which such application is to be presented and considered. Such notice shall contain the name and the applicant and the location of the proposed business, and shall be run at the applicant's expense.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(c), 17-203(c), 17-204(c))
Sec. 18-57. - Factors for council consideration.
In deciding whether to grant or deny the application for a license to sell malt beverages, distilled spirits, or wine at retail, the mayor and council shall be guided by the same considerations as those applied in granting or denying a license for the sale or manufacture of alcoholic beverages.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(d), 17-203(d), 17-204(d))
Sec. 18-58. - No change of location.
A license issued under the provisions of this section shall permit the sale or manufacture of malt beverages or distilled spirits or the retail sale of wine only on the premises described in the application.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(e), 17-203(e), 17-204(e))
Sec. 18-59. - Fees.
The following license fees are hereby established:
EXPAND
(1) Beer and wine, package sales $750.00
(2) Beer and wine, consumption on premises $900.00
(3) Beer, wine and liquor, package sales $1,300.00
(4) Beer, wine and liquor, consumption on premises $1,560.00
(5) Hotels, consumption on premises $1,800.00
(6) Malt beverage brewers and dealers, not for consumption on premises $750.00
  As used herein, the term "beer" shall also include malt beverages. The term "package sales" means sales in unbroken containers or packages not for consumption on the premises.
Sec. 18-60. - Consumption on premises.
It shall be a violation for any licensee to permit any alcoholic beverage to be consumed in or on the licensed premises unless he has a license permitting such consumption. Notwithstanding the foregoing, it shall not be a violation of these ordinances for the holder of a package sales license to permit a customer or potential customer to taste wine at no cost to the customer or potential customer.
(Ord. of 5-19-2009(3))

Subdivision II. - Alcohol Regulations

Sec. 18-65. - Licenses for establishments selling alcoholic beverages.
(a)
All of the definitions set out in Sec. 3-1-2 of O.C.G.A. (O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2), as now contained in said section, and as the same may be amended from time to time, are hereby adopted as definitions for the same terms or similar terms used by this chapter.
(b)
Except as may be otherwise provided by ordinance or by state law, it shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the manufacture, distribution or sale, at wholesale or retail, of alcoholic beverages in the corporate limits of the city without first obtaining a license from the governing authority, and it shall be unlawful for any person to engage in manufacture, distribution or sale, at wholesale or retail of alcoholic beverages in the corporate limits of the city after his said license has been revoked or suspended under the further provisions of this Code.
(c)
No new license for the manufacture, distribution or sale of alcoholic beverages shall be issued until the governing authority has conducted a public hearing thereon.
(d)
In the granting, refusal, suspension, or revocation of licenses pertaining to alcoholic beverages, the governing authority shall act in accordance with the following guidelines and other specific rules and regulations as set out in this Code:
(1)
Each alcoholic beverage license shall be issued for conduct of business at a specific location and shall not be transferred to any other location except as may be hereinafter provided in this Code.
(2)
Each alcoholic beverage license shall be issued to a specific person and shall not be transferable to another person except as hereinafter provided.
(3)
No beverage license shall be issued for conduct of business at a location where conduct of the particular business at such location would be in violation of the state law as the same now exists or as hereafter adopted or would be a violation of the zoning regulations of the city.
(4)
All alcoholic beverage licensees shall abide by all state laws relating to alcoholic beverages, and failure to comply with such state laws or with any ordinance of the city pertaining to alcoholic beverages shall be grounds for refusal, suspension, or revocation of licenses in accordance with the further provisions of this Code.
(5)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be granted to any person who:
a.
Is under 21 years of age.
b.
Has been convicted of any felony or has been convicted of a violation of any law or ordinance pertaining to alcoholic beverages or any law or ordinance pertaining to dangerous drugs or controlled substances (as defined under law of the state) within a period of five years previous to the time of application for license or the time the license is issued, whichever is greater.
c.
Is not a resident of the state at the time the license is to be issued, except that if license is applied for in the name of a foreign corporation qualified with the secretary of state to do business in the state, the designation of a registered agent in the state by such corporation shall satisfy the requirement as to residence.
d.
Who does not maintain a telephone at the place licensed.
(e)
In deciding whether or not to issue an alcoholic beverage license in the first instance, the governing authority, in addition to the above rules, may consider the following factors and may refuse to issue a license in its discretion where issuance of the particular license for a particular location would be likely to cause traffic congestion, noise pollution, unsanitary conditions or unusual law enforcement problems.
(f)
If at any time while an alcoholic beverage license is in effect, the holder of such license shall be convicted of a felony or cited for any violation of the laws of this state pertaining to dangerous drugs or controlled substances, or of the provisions of this Code relating to alcoholic beverages, or if such license holder should place himself in such circumstances as would have been grounds for refusal of a license in the first instance, the license of such person shall be subject to suspension or revocation by action of the governing authority under the further provisions of this Code.
(g)
All decisions of the governing authority approving, denying, suspending, or revoking alcoholic beverage licenses shall be in writing, the reasons therefor stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the applicant or holder of the license.
(h)
Upon timely application, any applicant or license holder aggrieved by the decision of the governing authority shall be afforded a hearing with an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine opposing witnesses.
(Code 1998, § 17-106)

Sec. 18-67. - Sunday sales of alcoholic beverages.
(a)
It shall be lawful for any holder of an alcoholic beverage license with a Sunday sales endorsement to sell alcoholic beverages on Sunday from 12:30 p.m. to 12:00 midnight in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served and in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total gross income from rental of rooms for overnight lodging.
(b)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, the package sales by retailers of wine/beer/distilled spirits on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m., if such Sunday sales of malt beverages, wine and distilled spirits are approved by a majority of the voters of Darien in a referendum called for that purpose.
(Code 1998, § 17-110; Ord. of 8-21-2007(4); Ord. of 2-21-2012)
Sec. 18-68. - Sanitary conditions.
All premises used for the sale or manufacture of malt beverages or distilled spirits or the retail sale of wine shall be kept in a safe and sanitary condition as required by the ordinances of the city and the laws of the state.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(f), 17-203(f), 17-204(f))
Sec. 18-69. - Hours of sale.
It shall be unlawful to sell malt beverages, wine, or distilled spirits except during those hours authorized for the sale of alcoholic beverages. In the absence of an ordinance or resolution of the city council establishing authorized hours for sale of malt beverages, wine, or distilled spirits, such hours for sale shall be in accordance with those enumerated under state law.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(g), 17-203(g), 17-204(g))
Sec. 18-70. - Furnishing alcoholic beverages to minors, intoxicated persons and habitual drunkards.
It shall be unlawful for persons to furnish, or cause to be furnished, or to permit any person in his employ to furnish alcoholic beverages to any minor, to any person who is noticeably intoxicated, or to any habitual drunkard whose intemperate habits are known to such person.
(Code 1998, §§ 17-202(h), 17-203(h), 17-204(h))

DIVISION 3. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS AND LIFE INSURERS

Sec. 18-75. - Financial institutions business license tax.
(a)
There is hereby levied an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations, and state building and loan associations at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of said institutions. Gross receipts shall mean gross receipts as defined under state law. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this chapter shall be $1,000.00 per year.
(b)
Each depository financial institution within the city shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts were measured. Said returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the Commissioner of the Department of Banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth under state law. The tax levied pursuant to this chapter shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in said return. The due date of taxes levied by this chapter shall be April 1 of each year.
(Code 1998, § 17-205)
State Law reference— Municipalities authorized to levy and collect a business license tax from depository financial institutions, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
Sec. 18-76. - Life insurer license fees.
(a)
Basic fees. There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each life insurer doing business within the city in the amount to be set by council and which is consistent with state law. For each separate business location, in excess of one, not covered by subsection (b) of this section, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount to be set by council and which is consistent with state law. For the purposes of this section, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in the class of insurance designated under state law.
(b)
License fee for life insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations. For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales, offers, solicits, or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of a life insurer for life insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee to be set by council and which is consistent with state law, per location for each year.
(c)
Life insurance agency license fees; independent life insurance agencies, brokers, etc. not otherwise licensed. There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which a life insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by subsection (a) of this section, in the amount to be set by council and which is consistent with state law, for each such location within the city.
(d)
Due date for license fees. License fees imposed in sections 18-71 and 62-8 shall be due and payable on March 1 of each year.
(Code 1998, § 17-206)
State Law reference— Designated classes of insurance, O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5; municipalities authorized to impose and collect certain license fees upon insurance companies for the privilege of engaging in the business within municipal jurisdiction, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.

ARTICLE III. - LICENSE REGULATIONS, TAXES, AND FEES

Sec. 18-81. - Carnivals, tent shows, exhibitions, exhibits, circuses, and other public exhibitions.
(a)
Permit and license required. It shall be unlawful for any person to show, exhibit, or offer to show or exhibit to the public any carnival, circus, tent show, exhibit, stage show, minstrel show, moving picture, or other form of exhibition or amusement whereby the public is likely to congregate within the city until such person, firm, corporation, or combination of persons shall first obtain a license and permit to operate or offer to operate such attraction.
(b)
Health department permit; time frame. Before a license shall be issued to any person, for the showing of or offering to show any of the things or attractions stated in this section, such person shall exhibit to the clerk of council a permit duly signed, issued and delivered to such applicant for license by the county health department of which said permit shall be in writing and shall bear a date of less than ten days prior to the time application is made.
(c)
Council authorized to revoke license. The council is hereby authorized and empowered to prohibit any of the shows, circuses, exhibits and attractions that are herein mentioned in this article, when in their opinion it would be likely if such attractions, exhibits, carnivals, shows, and other exhibitions mentioned herein should continue to operate in said city, to spread any infectious or contagious disease, and upon their written opinion that such attraction of exhibition, carnival, circus, tent show, or other form of attraction is likely to spread such disease, the license issued shall be immediately revoked, without further notice than having the chief of police or his deputy to deliver to the person who is apparently in charge a written notice that such license is revoked.
(d)
License fee. A license fee shall be charged and such charge shall be set by council based upon the type of exhibition for which the license is issued. The amount of the charge for differing types of exhibitions shall be available for public examination in the office of the city clerk.
(e)
Duration of license. The licenses set forth in this section shall be issued at said stated sums for a period of one week. If the licensee desires an additional period of time for the exhibition, then at the end of the one week term of the original license, he may apply for an additional one week license by submitting a new application which shall conform in all respects to the original application and the requirements thereof.
(f)
Penalties for violation. Any person who shall operate, show, exhibit, or offer to do so, without complying with the terms, conditions, rules, and regulations herein set out, or in anywise violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall severally be guilty of a breach of the peace as well as doing business without a license, and shall for each offense be, upon conviction in the mayor's or police court, punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-8.
(Code 1998, § 17-201)
Sec. 18-82. - Handbills, pamphlets, circulars, advertisements.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to distribute any handbill, pamphlet, circular or advertisement on the public streets or sidewalks of the city without first obtaining a permit from the city clerk in accordance with the further provisions hereof.
(b)
Any person desiring to distribute writings as described in subsection (a) of this section shall apply to the clerk of the city during established hours for the keeping open of said office and shall state the time and places where the distribution is to be made and present the city clerk with a copy of the matter to be distributed.
(c)
The city clerk shall have no discretion as to the issuance of a permit but shall be required to issue the permit forthwith. Any person who shall distribute such writings as herein described without first obtaining the permit herein provided for shall, upon conviction in the municipal court for the city, be punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-8.
(Code 1998, § 16-111)
Sec. 18-83. - Solicitors, peddlers, itinerant merchants, and special event merchants.
modified
(a)
Definitions.
Itinerant merchant. An itinerant merchant under this ordinance shall be deemed and construed to mean and include any person or persons, firm or corporation, either principal or agent, employer or employee who engages in a temporary business in the city by selling or offering for sale goods, wares, merchandise or things or articles of value for a period of not more than 190 days and who, for the purpose of carrying on such business, hires, leases or occupies any room, building, structure, trailer, or stand on any real property or on adjoining any street or public place in the city. The person, firm or corporation so engaged shall not be relieved from the provisions of this section by reason of associating temporarily with any local dealer, trader, merchant or auctioneer, or by reason of conducting such temporary business in connection with or as a part of any local business or in the name of any local dealer, trader, merchant or auctioneer.
Peddlers shall be defined as any person who sells and makes immediate delivery or offers for sale and immediate delivery any goods, wares, merchandise or thing in the possession of the seller at any place in the city other than a fixed place of business, but shall not include salesmen or agents for wholesale houses or firms who sell to retail dealers for resale or sell to manufacturers for manufacturing purposes or to bidders for public works or supplies.
Solicitor shall be defined as any person who engages in the business of going from house to house, place to place, in or along the streets, within the city selling or taking orders for or offering to sell or take orders for goods, wares or merchandise or other things of value for future delivery or for services to be performed in the future.
Special event merchant. A special event merchant under this chapter shall be deemed and construed to mean an itinerant merchant who carries on business, within the city limits, for a prescribed event (i.e.: Blessing of the Fleet, Fall Fest, etc.) for a period not to exceed 72 hours in length.
(b)
Permit required. It shall be unlawful to conduct a business of soliciting, peddling and engaging in the business of an itinerant merchant or special event merchant within the city, unless a valid permit has been issued to the solicitor, peddler, special event merchant, and itinerant merchant of such an activity by the code enforcement officer of the city. Such permit shall be required prior to the issuance of any business license required by any other ordinance of the city. Violation of any provision of the article will result in the revocation of the business license and may result in a fine not to exceed $1,000.00. Consistent with the stated purpose of the CHT overlay zoning requirements, no solicitors, peddlers or itinerant merchant permit shall be valid for any activity in the Cultural and Heritage Tourism District (CHT) as defined and delineated by the official zoning map of the city.
(c)
Permit application. Any interested person desiring such a permit required by this chapter shall make application with the code enforcement officer at least 30 days prior to the proposed date of conducting the business activity. The application must set forth:
(1)
The name and address of the company or firm for which orders are to be secured;
(2)
The name and address of the nearest local or state manager;
(3)
The kind of goods, wares or merchandise to be delivered;
(4)
The place from which the same are to be shipped or forwarded;
(5)
The method of solicitation or of taking orders;
(6)
The location of any warehouse, factory or plant within the state and the method of delivery;
(7)
The name and location of the residence of the applicant;
(8)
Such other information as the code enforcement officer may deem necessary;
(9)
The applicant's Georgia Department of Revenue taxpayer account number for sales and use tax.
(10)
A statement certifying:
a.
The correctness of the information given on the application.
b.
Bond required. All solicitors, peddlers, and itinerant merchants requiring cash deposits or who require a contract of agreement to finance the sale of any goods, services or merchandise for future delivery or for services to be performed in the future shall furnish to the city a bond in the sum of $5,000.00 executed by the bound principal and any reliable surety company. Such bond shall be conditioned upon the making of final delivery of the goods ordered or services to be performed in accordance with the terms in such order or failing therein that the advance payment on such order be refunded within 60 days from the contract delivery date.
Any person aggrieved or damaged by the action of any such peddler, solicitor or itinerant merchant shall have the right of action on the bond for the recovery of money or damages or both. Such bond shall remain in full force and effect, for a period of 90 days after the expiration of such license, unless sooner released by action of the city council.
Special event merchants need not be bonded.
(d)
Permit fee and duration. Each initial application for a permit required by section 18-83(c) shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable fee designated by city council resolution. Initial permits shall be valid until January 31 of the year following issuance for solicitors, peddlers, and itinerant merchants. Applications for renewals shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable fee designated by city council resolution, and renewal permits shall be valid for a one-year period from January 31. Special event merchant permits will be valid for the period of the event or 72 hours, whichever is less.
Each holder of a solicitor's, peddler's or itinerant merchant permit shall submit to the code enforcement officer, by the fifth day of each month, a form designated by the city which affirms under oath the dates in which the solicitor, peddler, or itinerant merchant worked during that time frame. Each holder of a solicitor's, peddler's or itinerant merchant permit shall further submit to the code enforcement officer, by the fifth day of each month, all forms, electronically or by any means, submitted to the Georgia Department of Revenue for any and all sales and use taxes due. Failure to submit either of these forms shall result in a revocation of the permit held.
(e)
Exemptions.
(1)
Persons exempted from the payment of a business license shall be exempted from the payment of the permit fee.
(2)
Any person who claims to be entitled to an exemption from the payment of a permit provided for in section 18-83(d) upon the grounds that he or she is employed by a charitable or fraternal organization shall file a written affidavit disclosing the following information: A list of all officers of such organization, the name of the immediate supervisor, and any other information which the code enforcement officer may require to establish such claim of exemption.
(f)
Issuance or denial. If all requirements of this chapter are adhered to, the city manager shall issue such permit. If any or all requirements have not been met, the city manager shall not issue such permit.
(g)
Permit and identification and display. Any peddler or solicitor being duly licensed according to the provisions of this chapter of the City Code shall, carry on his person at all times while operating in the city a solicitor's permit as furnished by the code enforcement officer. This permit shall serve as a notice to the public that such solicitor is duly licensed by the city and has the necessary approval.

Sec. 18-84. - Short-term rentals.
(a)
Short title. This section shall be known and may be cited as the "Short-Term Rental Ordinance of City of Darien."
(b)
Purpose. It is the purpose of this section to regulate the use of and establish standards for privately owned residential property used as vacation homes and rented to transient occupants so as to ensure that hotel-motel taxes owed to City of Darien are properly collected and remitted to City of Darien, to minimize the adverse effects of short-term rental uses on surrounding residential properties and neighborhoods, and to preserve the character, integrity, and stability of residential neighborhoods in which short-term rentals are located. This section is not intended to regulate hotels, motels, inns, hospitals, or non-vacation type rental arrangements.
(c)
Definitions. The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning. When not inconsistent with the context, words in the plural number include the singular number, and words in the singular include the plural number.
(1)
Hotel-motel tax means the excise tax imposed and collected by City of Darien pursuant to O.C.G.A §§ 48-13-50 through 48-13-63 and Code section 62-9 for the furnishing for value to the public of any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations in the City of Darien.
(2)
Short-term rental business license or license means the document issued by the city manager to the property owner or the managing agency or managing agent of a short-term rental under this section containing an official short-term rental hotel-motel tax number for the purpose of verifying and enforcing compliance with the hotel-motel tax requirements of the City of Darien Code of Ordinances, as well the other provisions of this section pertaining to the operation of a short-term rental for transient occupants.
(3)
Advertisement or advertising means the listing or marketing of any real property as a short-term rental through any print, outdoor, digital, broadcast, or other advertising medium, such as magazines, brochures, newsletters, banners, signs, social media platforms, apps on a smartphone, electronic or online marketplaces or booking platforms, marketplace facilitators, websites, internet, computer or other electronic devices, television, or radio.
(4)
City council means the mayor and city council of Darien, Georgia.
(5)
Commercial event means an event or gathering of people, or the marketing or advertising thereof, where a fee is charged in exchange for allowing an event or gathering on the property.
(6)
City means City of Darien, Georgia.
(7)
Dwelling unit means one or more rooms, designed, occupied or intended for occupancy as separate living quarters for one party with cooking, sleeping and sanitary facilities provided within the dwelling unit for the exclusive use of the occupants thereof.
(8)
City manager means the city manager of Darien, Georgia.
(9)
Local point of contact or point of contact means a person who is at least 18 years of age and who, using reasonably prudent business practices, is responsible for ensuring that all occupants and/or guests of the short-term rental comply with all applicable laws, rules and regulations pertaining to the use and occupancy of the subject short-term rental and for responding to questions or concerns from occupants, guests, and the city regarding the operation of the short-term rental.
(10)
Managing agency or managing agent means a person, firm, agency, or company with a valid city occupation tax license that manages one or more short-term rentals under a management agreement on behalf of the owner of the short-term rental.
(11)
Maximum occupancy rate means no more than two persons per bedroom, plus two additional persons. A bedroom is a room or space within the short-term rental furnished with one or more beds and that is customarily used and intended primarily for sleeping. Children under 12 years of age shall not be included in determining the maximum occupancy of a short-term rental, but in no event shall occupancy exceed 15 persons total, including children.
(12)
Owner means a person that holds legal title to private property.
(13)
Party means one or more persons who as a single group rent a short-term rental pursuant to a single reservation and payment.
(14)
Person means an individual, firm, partnership, corporation, association, company, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary or any combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision whether public, private or quasi-public.
(15)
Property means a legal lot of record on which any rental unit(s) is being operated or offered as a short-term rental.
(16)
Rental unit means a residential dwelling unit that is offered for lease, rental, trade or barter. Multiple rental units may be located on the same property.
(17)
Short-term rental means an accommodation for transient occupants or guests where, in exchange for compensation of any type or amount, a residential rental unit is provided for lodging for a period of time not to exceed 30 consecutive days.
(18)
Any term used in this section that is also used in the zoning ordinance of City of Darien shall have the same meaning as defined in the zoning ordinance, unless otherwise specifically defined in this section.
(d)
Short-term rental business license.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any owner of any property within the City of Darien. (hereinafter "City of Darien" or "city"), to market, advertise, rent, or operate a short-term rental without a short-term rental business license, or to operate a short-term rental contrary to the procedures and regulations established in this section, other provisions of this Code, or any applicable state law.
(2)
A short-term rental business license must be obtained from the city manager prior to the use, operation, offering, or advertisement of any short-term rental in City of Darien. Any property being used, operated, offered, or advertised as a short-term rental in City of Darien must have a current short-term rental business license validly issued by the city manager reflecting compliance with the hotel-motel tax requirements of the City of Darien Code of Ordinances. It shall be a violation of this section to use, operate, or advertise a short-term rental without a valid short-term rental business license issued by City of Darien under this section.
(3)
A short-term rental business license shall only be issued to a person with an ownership interest in the short-term rental or to a managing agency or agent that manages the short-term rental. Renters of real property are not eligible to receive a short-term rental business license, unless the property owner has provided explicit written permission to the tenant.
(e)
Short-term rental standards. Short-term rentals, as defined in section 18-84(c), shall be subject to the following general requirements in addition to the zoning district regulations for the zoning district in which the short-term rental is located:
(1)
Short-term rentals shall meet all applicable building, health, fire, and related safety codes at all times. Each short-term rental shall also have:
a.
Working smoke and carbon monoxide detectors in every bedroom and on all habitable floors;
b.
A properly maintained and charged fire extinguisher in each short-term rental unit; and
c.
A solid waste collection (trash) service that removes waste at least once a week. All solid waste (trash) must be contained in secured containers when outside of the residence. Trash and refuse shall not be left or stored on the exterior of the property unless it is placed in a curbside container. The curbside container shall not be placed on the curb awaiting trash service pick up any sooner than 24 hours prior to the pickup day and must be removed no later than 24 hours after trash service pickup.
(2)
No exterior signage shall be permitted except in accordance with the regulations for the district in which the short-term rental is located.
(3)
Short-term rentals shall not be operated outdoors or in a non-habitable structure.
(4)
Parking shall be provided in accordance with any existing or future parking ordinances of the city.
(5)
Only one party of guests shall be permitted per short-term rental unit.
(f)
Parking.
(1)
Each short-term rental shall designate the maximum number of cars allowed on-site and, where applicable, instructions for off-site parking for cars exceeding the maximum allowed on-site.
(2)
Parking spaces within the grounds of the short-term rental unit must be clearly identified for guests use.
(3)
The property owner shall provide guests with parking instructions prior to arrival.
(4)
Exceeding the maximum number of vehicles allowed on-site of a short-term rental is a violation of this section.
(g)
Operation.
(1)
Occupancy of a short-term rental between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. shall not exceed the maximum occupancy rate, as defined in section 18-84(c) no person operating or occupying a short-term rental shall violate or exceed, or allow another to exceed, the maximum occupancy rate.
(2)
A short-term rental shall not be used for hosting any commercial events.
(3)
The short-term rental business license holder shall keep on file with the city at all times the name, cell phone number, and email address of a valid local point of contact who shall use reasonably prudent business practices to ensure that all occupants and/or guests of the short-term rental comply with all applicable laws, rules and regulations pertaining to the use and occupancy of the short-term rental, as well as for responding to questions or concerns from occupants, guests, and the city regarding the operation of the short-term rental. The local point of contact may, but does not necessarily need to be, the property owner or representative of a managing agency or agent.
(4)
The local point of contact shall be available 24 hours a day to accept telephone calls and respond to complaints regarding the condition, operation, or conduct of a short-term rental, including the behavior and conduct of the occupants and/or guests thereof. When the short-term rental is rented and occupied, the local point of contact must be available to respond to complaints in person at the short-term rental within a reasonable time period, not to exceed two hours after notification of the complaint.
(5)
Failure of the local point of contact to respond to calls or complaints in a timely and appropriate manner shall be a violation of this section. However, it is not intended that any property owner, local point of contact, and/or license holder act as a peace officer or place himself or herself in an at-risk situation.
(6)
A valid short-term rental short-term rental business license shall be posted in a conspicuous location inside the main entrance of the short-term rental on or adjacent to the front door. In addition to the short-term rental business license, a clearly visible and legible notice containing the following information shall be conspicuously posted within the short-term rental on or adjacent to the front door sign:
a.
The name of the local point of contact and an email address and telephone number at which the local point of contact may be reached on a 24-hour, seven days a week, basis. The notice must be updated within seven days of any change of the local point of contact and/or such person's telephone number;
b.
The name and address of the nearest hospital;
c.
The maximum number of overnight occupants (maximum occupancy rate) permitted in the short-term rental and a statement notifying the occupants that failure to conform to the occupancy requirements of the short-term rental is a violation of this section;
d.
The maximum number of vehicles allowed to be parked on the property and a statement notifying the occupants that exceeding the maximum number of vehicles allowed on-site is a violation of this section;
e.
The city's noise ordinance regulations and a statement notifying the occupant(s) that any failure to comply with the city's noise ordinance is a violation of this section;
f.
State and city emergency management website information; and
g.
Other relevant community restriction(s), if applicable.
(7)
All property specific digital, outdoor, or print advertising for any short-term rental, including electronic or digital advertising on short-term rental websites, shall include the short-term rental business license number, or license sub-number if applicable, issued by the city for the short-term rental. It is a violation of this section to advertise a short-term rental using an expired short-term rental business license number or a license number that was not assigned to the short-term rental by the city.
(8)
Short-term rentals, and the operation thereof, are subject to hotel-motel taxes and are liable for payment thereof as established by state law and the City of Darien Code of Ordinances. The short-term rental business license holder shall timely remit all applicable hotel-motel taxes owed in connection with the operation of short-term rental(s) as set forth in this section.
(9)
The short-term rental business license holder shall maintain records of all short-term rental booking dates, rental income, and taxes collected and remitted to City of Darien for three years and shall provide such records to the city upon request.
(10)
The short-term rental business license holder shall abide by any local, state, or federal rules during a declared disaster event, including, but not limited to evacuation and reentry orders and shall provide guests with the state and city emergency management websites and emergency contact phone numbers.
(h)
Application for license.
(1)
Any property owner or managing agency desiring to operate a short-term rental must submit an application for a short-term rental business license to the city manager on a form to be provided by the city. Upon submission of a properly completed application, an applicant meeting the conditions and requirements of this section for the operation of a short-term rental shall qualify for and be issued a short-term rental business license for such short-term rental. At the time of filing an application for an initial short-term rental business license, an applicant must:
a.
Pay and submit to City of Darien a license fee of $125.00 for each short-term rental. If the application is filed prior to July 1 of a particular year, the full $125.00 fee shall be paid. If the application is filed after July 1, the license fee shall be $62.50;
b.
Provide the name, address, email, and contact information of the property owner;
c.
Provide the address and parcel identification number of the property where the short-term rental is located;
d.
Provide the number of rental units on the property and the number of bedrooms in each rental unit;
e.
State whether the applicant is the property owner or a managing agency or agent operating the short-term rental on behalf of the property owner;
f.
Specify whether the short-term rental is or will be managed by a managing agency or agent and provide the name, address, email, phone number, and occupation tax number of such managing agency or agent;
g.
Provide the name, address, email address and cell phone number for a local point of contact, as such term is defined in subsection (c);
h.
Provide the number of parking spaces on the grounds of the short-term rental and affirm whether those spaces are clearly identified for occupant and guest use.
i.
Affirm the applicant's responsibility to collect and timely remit hotel-motel taxes in accordance with city ordinance and to pay occupation taxes in accordance with city ordinance;
j.
Affirm that the short-term rental is not in violation of any other land use restrictions, including covenants, deed restrictions, homeowner association rules and regulations, or other such restrictions;
k.
Affirm understanding that City of Darien strongly recommends that the property owner carry property and renter's liability insurance that specifically covers short-term rental use;
l.
Affirm that the short-term rental has an operable fire extinguisher in every rental unit and working smoke and carbon monoxide detectors in every bedroom and on all habitable floors;
m.
Affirm that the short-term rental has solid waste collection (trash) service that removes solid waste at least once a week;
n.
Affirm that the short-term rental has no past due hotel-motel taxes, short-term rental business license fees, ad valorem taxes, or fines for violations of this section; and
o.
Affirm whether the applicant or short-term rental has had a short-term rental business license suspended or revoked within the previous 12 months.
(2)
If the applicant is a managing agency or agent, the managing agency or agent must submit documentation evidencing that it is authorized to submit the application and otherwise manage and act on behalf of the property owner in relation to the operation and use of the short-term rental.
(3)
Should any property owner or managing agency or agent own or manage two or more short-term rentals, a single short-term rental business license may be issued for multiple properties under common ownership or professional management that otherwise meet the requirements for a license under this section, provided that the requisite payment, information, and confirmations are provided by the applicant for each short-term rental. In the event that a single short-term rental business license is issued for multiple short-term rentals hereunder, each short-term rental under the license shall be assigned separate license sub-numbers.
(4)
Any fraudulent or material misrepresentation, omission, or false or untruthful statement or information furnished by or made by the applicant in any application (or supporting materials) submitted to the city for a license or renewal thereof shall be grounds for denial, suspension, or revocation of a short-term rental business license, or any renewal thereof.
(5)
All requirements, acknowledgements, affirmations, and attestations necessary to obtain a short-term rental business license must be continuously maintained at all times throughout the duration of the initial short-term rental business license and any renewal thereof. All material changes in the information or supporting documents or materials provided to the city in any application (or renewal application), including any change in the local point of contact and/or such person's contact information shall be updated and reported in writing to the city manager within three business days of such change.
(6)
An accommodation excise license is not assignable or transferable. Upon sale of a property or when a managing agency or agent changes, there will be a 30-day grace period to operate as a short-term rental so long as an application for a short-term rental business license is submitted by the new owner or managing agency or agent within seven days of the sale or management change.
(i)
License expiration/renewal.
(1)
All short-term rental business licenses, and any renewals thereof shall expire on December 31 of each year. Applications for renewal of a license for the following calendar year must be filed on or before January 15 of the following year. However, nothing herein shall be construed as precluding the filing of an application for renewal after such date.
(2)
To renew a short-term rental business license for the ensuing calendar year, license holders must submit and file a renewal application with the city manager on a form to be provided by the city. In order to qualify for and be issued a renewed license, the applicant, at the time of filing, must:
a.
Pay and submit to City of Darien an annual renewal fee of $125.00 for each short-term rental;
b.
Affirm and update, as needed, all information, documentation, and affirmations, submitted to the city in the application for the initial license set forth in subsection (h) above;
c.
Affirm that all hotel-motel taxes for each short-term rental under the license being renewed have been collected and timely remitted to City of Darien; and
d.
Affirm that the renewal application is not for a short-term rental that has had its license revoked within the last 12 months, except as permitted under subsection (m)(4);
(3)
A renewed short-term rental business license shall be valid for one calendar year immediately following its issuance, beginning on January 1 and ending on December 31 of each year.
(4)
Fees paid for a short-term rental business license, or any renewal thereof, are not refundable once a license is issued.
(j)
Application review/denials. The city manager or his/her designee may deny an application for a short-term rental business license, or any renewal thereof, if:
(1)
The application is for a property that has had its license revoked within the preceding 12 months, except as permitted under subsection (d)(4);
(2)
The person applying lacks authority to represent the property owner for which the application is made;
(3)
The applicant does not qualify for a license or the application is filed by a person as a subterfuge for another person;
(4)
The application is incomplete, fails to meet the application requirements of this section, or does not contain the requisite documents, information, or affirmations under this section;
(5)
A short-term rental is not permitted on the property for which a license is sought;
(6)
Information or materials submitted by the applicant to the city in connection with the application contain any fraudulent or material misrepresentation, omission, or false or untruthful statement or information; or
(7)
The applicant fails to pay any application fee required by this section.
All decisions denying an application for a short-term rental business license, or any renewal thereof, shall be in writing and served upon the applicant by email or certified mail, or both, to the email address or physical address provided by the applicant, and shall specify the reasons for the denial. Decisions denying an application hereunder shall be appealable to the city council in accordance with subsection 2-31-14[(n)] of this section.
(k)
Violations and penalties.
(1)
Whenever in this section any act is prohibited (or not allowed) or is made or declared to be unlawful or an offense, or whenever herein the doing of an act is required or the failure to do any act is declared to be unlawful or an offense, the violation of such provision shall be an ordinance violation punishable as follows:
a.
Upon conviction of the first offense under this section within a consecutive 12-month period, the owner(s) of the property and/or the license holder shall be punished by a fine of $250.00;
b.
Upon conviction of the second offense under this section within a consecutive 12-month period, the owner(s) of the property and/or the license holder shall be punished by a fine of $500.00;
c.
Upon conviction of the third and any subsequent offense under this section within a consecutive 12-month period, the owner(s) of the property and/or the license holder shall be punished by a fine of $1,000.00; and
d.
Enforcement actions may also be brought against the occupants and/or guests of a short-term rental for violations of this section and/or such other ordinances of the city as may be applicable to the conduct of the occupants or guests, notwithstanding that this section may also make the property owner and/or the license holder responsible for the conduct constituting the violation. Violations of the provisions of this section by occupants and/or guests of the short-term rental shall be an ordinance violation punishable by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00;
(2)
Each day that a short-term rental is marketed, advertised, or rented for overnight accommodation without the necessary short-term rental license required under this section shall constitute a separate violation. Any person marketing, advertising, and/or operating a short-term rental without a valid short-term rental license in violation of this section may be prosecuted and, upon conviction, punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00;
(3)
Nonpayment of any taxes due from the operation of the short-term rental may subject the property owner and/or the license holder to revocation of a license and/or any additional penalties, enforcement, or consequences to the extent provided for by state law and city ordinances.
(4)
Should the City of Darien Police Department respond to any property being operated as a short-term rental, the police should forward any report to the city manager.
(5)
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary herein, the imposition of a fine, punishment, or other penalty under the provisions of this section shall not prevent the suspension or revocation of any short-term rental business license upon violation of this section.
(6)
Violations of this section may also subject the violator to any and all other remedies, legal or equitable, available to City of Darien to the extent provided for by law, including injunctive relief.
(l)
Temporary license suspension.
(1)
In addition to other remedies provided for in this section, the city manager (or designee) may temporarily suspend any short-term rental business license issued pursuant to this section upon a reasonable belief that the operation of the short-term rental poses an imminent serious health and safety threat and that the suspension is in the best interest of the public health and safety.
(2)
Decisions of the city manager to temporarily suspend a short-term rental business license under this section shall become effective and implemented immediately for a period not to exceed 14 days pending a hearing by the city manager to determine whether the conditions giving rise to the temporary suspension have been abated or whether the suspension should be extended due to public health and safety concerns. At the hearing, the license holder shall be afforded an opportunity to produce evidence and information relevant to the temporary suspension. If, after the hearing, the city manager extends the suspension beyond the initial 14-day period, such decision shall be appealable pursuant to subsection (n), except that an appeal of a temporary suspension under this section shall not stay enforcement of or delay implementation of the temporary suspension or continuation thereof.
(3)
If the conditions that gave rise to the temporary suspension are remedied in advance of any hearing, the license holder may request reinstatement by the city manager. If the city manager determines that the conditions giving rise to the temporary suspension no longer exist, the short-term rental business license shall be reinstated without the need for a hearing before the city manager.
(m)
License suspensions and revocations.
(1)
In addition to any other penalty, punishment, or remedy provided for under this section, a license may be suspended or revoked by the city manager (or designee) as provided for herein. The city manager, or his or her designee, may, upon investigation, suspend or revoke a short-term rental business license associated with a short-term rental:
a.
If the property or short-term rental corresponding to that license has been the subject of three or more violations of this section or the city's noise ordinance which have resulted in convictions of either the property owner, license holder, local point of contact, or the occupants/guests (or any combination thereof) in the immediately preceding 12-month period;
b.
If the short-term rental or property upon which it is located is being operated in a disorderly manner so as to constitute a public nuisance after: (i) the license holder and property owner (if different) have been advised in writing by the city of the unsatisfactory manner in which the short-term rental is being operated; and (ii) after the license holder and property owner (if different) have been given a reasonable opportunity to cure said deficiencies;
c.
Upon learning that an applicant furnished or made any fraudulent or material misrepresentation, omission, or false or untruthful statement or information in the application (or supporting materials) submitted to the city for a license or renewal thereof;
d.
If the license holder or property owner violates or fails to meet or comply with any provision or requirement of this section, or fails to timely pay or remit any license fees imposed under the provisions of this section or any hotel-motel taxes due to the city pursuant to section [62-9]; provided, however, prior to any such suspension or revocation, the license holder and property owner shall be advised in writing by the city of such violation or non-compliance and the fees or taxes believed to be past due, and the license holder and property owner shall be provided 14 calendar days to correct and cure such violation or non-compliance, or pay any such past due fee or excise tax; or
e.
If a local point of contact fails to respond to calls or complaints regarding the condition, operation, or conduct of a short-term rental, or the behavior or conduct of the occupants and/or guests thereof, in a timely and appropriate manner on three or more separate occasions within the immediately preceding 12 months, provided that the city provides the license holder and property owner (if different) with notice of each such failure and a 14-day opportunity to respond to the notice in writing. It is not intended that an owner, managing agency, or local point of contact act as a peace officer or place himself or herself in an at-risk situation.
(2)
If, upon investigation, the city manager (or designee) determines that sufficient grounds exist to suspend or revoke a license in accordance with this section, the city manager (or designee) shall issue written notice of the suspension or revocation to the license holder and property owner, if different. Suspensions shall specify the length of the suspension, not exceed 12 months. The written notice of suspension or revocation may be served upon the license holder and property owner by email or certified mail, or both, to the email address or physical address provided by the license holder in the application, and shall specify the facts which, in the opinion of the city manager (or designee) constitute grounds for the suspension or revocation of the license. The notice shall also specify that the suspension or revocation of the license shall become effective 15 calendar days from the date of the notice, unless the license holder appeals such decision to the city council in writing in accordance with subsection (n) of this section no later than 14 calendar days from the date of the receipt of the notice of suspension or revocation.
(3)
In the event that a single short-term rental business license is issued for multiple short-term rentals, the suspension or revocation notice shall specify the license sub-number(s) of the property(ies) whose license is being suspended or revoked. In such a case, suspension or revocation of a license(s) for the identified short-term rental(s) shall not affect the validity of the license as it relates to the remaining short-term rentals on that license.
(4)
Should a short-term rental business license for any short-term rental be revoked under this section, then no short-term rental business license shall issue for that property or to that property owner or license holder for that particular location for a period of 12 months after the effective date of the revocation. Notwithstanding the foregoing, a new license may be applied for and issued under an application that otherwise meets the application requirements of this section: (1) if the subject property is sold to a new owner as part of an arm's length transaction; or (2) the application is submitted by a managing agency or agent who will serve as the new license holder, provided that it has not been the managing agency or agent or license holder for that particular property within the immediately preceding 12 months.
(n)
Appeals.
(1)
Decisions denying, suspending, or revoking a short-term rental business license, or any renewal thereof, under this section may be appealed by the applicant or license holder, as applicable, to the city council.
(2)
Upon receipt of any decision or determination to deny, suspend, or revoke any short-term rental business license, or any renewal thereof, an applicant whose application was denied, or a license holder whose license was revoked, may appeal such decision to the city council. Any such appeal must be in writing and filed with the city manager within 14 calendar days of the applicant's or license holder's receipt of the decision being appealed. Appeals to the city council must be sent to and received by the city manager by certified U.S. mail or hand delivery. Appeals not physically received in writing by the city manager within 14 calendar days of the applicant's or license holder's receipt of the decision being appealed will be deemed untimely and disallowed.
(3)
The written appeal must state succinctly the grounds upon which it is asserted that the decision should be reversed and shall be accompanied by a copy of the decision from which the appeal is being made, along with any other documents deemed relevant to the appeal. The filing of a timely appeal to the city council will delay implementation of the decision being appealed until a decision is made on that appeal by the city council, except for appeals of temporary suspensions pursuant to [subsection] (l). If an appeal is not timely received, decisions denying, suspending, or revoking a license shall become effective and final on the 15th calendar day following the applicant's or license holder's receipt of such decision.
(4)
Upon receipt of a timely appeal under this section, the city manager shall schedule a hearing before the city council at a regular or special called meeting within 45 days of the receipt of the appeal. The city manager shall provide written notice to the appealing party of the time, place and date of the scheduled hearing by certified U.S. mail.
(5)
The city council shall have the duty of conducting hearings concerning the denial, revocation, or suspension of a license. The standard of proof on all issues in the hearing shall be a preponderance of the evidence and a determination will be made on the basis of the evidence presented at the hearing.
(6)
At the hearing, after presentation of the case against the applicant or license holder, the appealing party will have an opportunity to present his or her case, to present evidence and information relevant to the appeal, to rebut the allegations made against him or her, and to present whatever defenses he or she has. The appealing party shall have the right to be represented by an attorney at his or her own expense.
(7)
At the conclusion of the hearing, the city council shall affirm, modify, or reverse the decision being appealed. Written notice of the board's decision shall be sent to the appealing party by the city manager via email or certified U.S. mail, or both, and shall set forth the findings and conclusions of the board.
(8)
Decisions of the city council regarding denials, suspensions, and revocations shall be binding, subject to the right of certiorari to superior court as provided by Georgia law.
(o)
Right to audit. City of Darien shall have the right to audit and examine the books, papers, records, and financial reports of any license holder, in order to verify the accuracy of any hotel-motel tax return made, or if no return is made by the license holder, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid. The short-term rental business license holder shall keep and maintain such records, receipts, invoices, and other pertinent papers regarding the operation of a short-term rental and shall establish and maintain a reasonable accounting system to readily identify and calculate the amount of hotel-motel taxes due for each short-term rental.
(p)
Exceptions.
(1)
Real property that is occupied for a period of less than 30 consecutive days by a buyer or seller of real property prior to or after the closing of same shall not be considered a short-term rental or require a short-term rental business license under this section, provided that the property is not otherwise furnished or offered for value to any other person(s) during the same calendar year for 30 days or less at one time.
(2)
Real property that is occupied by or offered for occupancy to the same person for a period greater than 30 consecutive days shall not be considered a short-term rental or require a short-term rental business license under this section, provided that the property is not otherwise furnished or offered for value to any other person(s) during the same calendar year for 30 days or less at one time.
