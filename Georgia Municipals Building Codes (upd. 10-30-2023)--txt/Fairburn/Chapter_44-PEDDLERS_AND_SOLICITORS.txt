Chapter 44 - PEDDLERS AND SOLICITORS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 44-1. - Applicability.
The provisions of this chapter shall not apply to officers or employees of the city, county, state or federal government, or any subdivision thereof, when on official business.
Sec. 44-2. - Qualifications; criminal background check.
(a)
No license or permit shall be issued under this chapter to any person that has entered a plea of nolo contendere to or has been convicted, within a five-year period prior to the date of application for a license or permit, in any state or federal court, of any felony or of any crime opposed to decency and morality, or involving moral turpitude, or involving fraud or theft, or involving illegal drugs, or involving violation of the ordinances of the city.
(b)
Applicants shall provide to the city police chief such information as may be necessary for the chief or his designee to conduct a criminal background check to ensure compliance with this section.
(c)
If the applicant is a business entity, the provisions of this section shall apply to the owners and principals thereof.
Secs. 44-3—44-22. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - MERCHANTS

Sec. 44-23. - License required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the business of an itinerant merchant, peddler or hawker of any merchandise, article or thing in the city without first having obtained a license therefor.
Sec. 44-24. - Application; criminal background check.
Applications for licenses shall be made in writing to the city clerk and shall state thereon the following:
(1)
The description and number of vehicles, if any, intended to be operated;
(2)
The kind of merchandise intended to be peddled; and
(3)
The permanent address of the itinerant merchant, peddler or hawker.
Sec. 44-25. - Fee.
The annual license fee to be paid by itinerant merchants, peddlers or hawkers shall be as established by the city council. No license shall be issued to the terms of this article unless the license fee has been paid.
Sec. 44-26. - Evidence of payment of taxes.
No license shall be issued pursuant to the terms of this article unless the applicant therefor shall produce evidence satisfactory to the city clerk that said applicant is complying with the state law relating to the tax imposed upon persons engaged in the business of selling tangible, personal property at retail.
Sec. 44-27. - Term of license.
The annual license period shall be from January 1 to December 31 of the succeeding year. Semiannual, quarterly and monthly licenses shall automatically expire not later than December 31 of the year in which issued.
Sec. 44-28. - License certificates; vehicle tags.
Each licensee, upon the payment of the annual, semiannual or quarterly license fee, shall be issued a certificate authorizing such licensee to engage in and carry on the business of an itinerant merchant, peddler or hawker described in the application. Each licensee, upon the payment of the annual, semiannual or quarterly license fee, shall be issued a metal tag for each vehicle or cart in the city, and said tag shall be carried at all times in said vehicle and displayed when in the city and shall be produced upon request of any city inspector or official.
Sec. 44-29. - Street peddlers.
No peddler shall ply his vocation on any street, sidewalk, park, parkway or in any other public place, unless his peddler's license specifies that peddling in such public places is permitted thereunder.
Sec. 44-30. - Conduct of licensee.
No licensee under this article, or agent, employee or representative of such licensee shall:
(1)
Cheat or defraud any person in the city;
(2)
Make any false representations to any persons in the city;
(3)
Barter, sell or peddle any goods, wares or merchandise other than those specified in his application for a license;
(4)
Make or cause to be made, permit or allow, while upon the streets, sidewalks and public places, any excessive noise of any kind by crying, calling, shouting, or by means of any whistle, rattle, bell, horn or similar device, for the purpose of advertising or of attracting the attention or inviting the patronage of any person.
Secs. 44-31—44-48. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SOLICITORS

Sec. 44-49. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable means and includes the words patriotic, philanthropic, social service, welfare, benevolent, education, civic or fraternal, either actual or purported.
Contributions means donations of money, property or items of value, whether under the guise of a loan or a gift.
Promoter means any person who promotes, manages, supervises, organizes or attempts to promote, manage, supervise or organize a campaign of solicitation.
Religious and religion mean and include the term "charitable," but shall be given their commonly accepted definitions.
Solicit and solicitation mean the request directly or indirectly of money, credit, property, financial assistance or other things of value on the plea or representation that such money, credit, property, financial assistance or other thing of value will be used for a charitable or religious purpose, or going from place to place offering to deliver goods or merchandise or rendition of services in return for money, credit, property, financial assistance or other things of value.
Solicitor-canvasser shall be construed to include, but not be limited to, salesmen, representatives, counselors or anyone engaged in the same or similar trade or occupation.
Sec. 44-50. - Permits.
(a)
Contents. Permits issued under the provisions of this article shall bear the name and address of the organization by whom the solicitation is to be made, the date issued and the dates within which the solicitation will be conducted.
(b)
Duration. No permit issued under the provisions of this article shall grant the permittee the right to solicit for a period of time longer than 30 days from its date of issuance.
(c)
Nontransferable. Any permit issued under the provisions of this article shall not be transferable.
Sec. 44-51. - Solicitation for profit.
(a)
Permit required. No person shall engage in business as a canvasser or solicitor, calling at residences in the city without the previous consent of the occupant for the purpose of soliciting orders, sales, subscriptions or business of any kind or seeking information without first having obtained a permit therefor from the city clerk.
(b)
Application. Applications for permits to solicit for the sale of goods, merchandise or rendition of services or business of any kind shall be in writing on a form provided by the city clerk and shall contain, but not be limited to the following information:
(1)
The name, address and headquarters of the person applying for the permit;
(2)
The name, address and headquarters of the person he represents;
(3)
The nature of the products or services in which he is interested;
(4)
The names of the manufacturers of such products or of the organization he represents;
(5)
The name and address of the person who will be in direct charge of conducting the solicitations in the city;
(6)
The dates such solicitation will be conducted;
(7)
The proposed method of operation within the city;
(8)
Retailers' occupation tax registration number and such other information as may be deemed necessary by the city clerk;
(9)
Complete identification of the applicant;
(10)
The person making such application shall swear or affirm before a notary public as to the truthfulness of the information contained in such application.
(c)
Fee. The license fee to be paid by a canvasser or solicitor for profit shall be the amount established by the city council. No license shall be issued pursuant to this article until the license fee is paid.
(d)
Qualifications; investigation. No permit hereunder shall be issued until five days after the application is made to the city clerk. Upon receipt of such application, the city clerk shall refer the same to the chief of police, who shall cause an investigation of the applicant's criminal record, if any. If the applicant has been convicted of a felony, then, and in that event, no permit shall be issued; if the applicant has not been convicted of any felony, then the permit hereunder shall be issued at the expiration of five days after the application is made.
(e)
License to be carried. Each person shall, at all times, while soliciting or canvassing within the city, carry upon his person the permit, and the same shall be exhibited by said person whenever he is requested to do so by any officer or resident of the city.
Sec. 44-52. - Charitable or communal solicitation.
(a)
Permit required. No person shall solicit contributions within the city to any charity or community project, without having first obtained a permit therefor; provided, however, no permit need be obtained by any church or organization to conduct a solicitation solely among its own membership.
(b)
Application. Applications for permits to solicit funds shall be made in writing to the city clerk on forms provided by him. Such applications shall contain the following information, or in lieu thereof, a statement as to why such information cannot be furnished:
(1)
The name and address of the charity or organization applying for the permit;
(2)
The names and addresses of the residents of the city in charge of organizing and supervising the solicitation;
(3)
The name and address of any person who is to receive a percentage of the funds collected for organizing or promoting the solicitation;
(4)
The amount or percentage to be paid to such promoter or organizer;
(5)
The purpose for which the collected funds are to be used;
(6)
The name and address of the person or organization to which the funds are to be remitted when collected;
(7)
The method to be used in conducting the solicitation;
(8)
The time when such solicitation is to be made, giving dates preferred for beginning and ending the solicitation;
(9)
The amount of money collected by such solicitation in the last preceding year;
(10)
A statement to the effect that if a permit is granted, it will not be used or represented in any way as an endorsement by the city or any department or officer thereof;
(11)
Such other information as may be deemed necessary by the city clerk.
(c)
Verification. The person making such application shall swear or affirm before a notary public as to the truthfulness of the information contained in said application.
(d)
Fee. The license fee to be paid by charitable or communal solicitors or canvassers shall be in the amount established by the city council. No license shall be issued pursuant to this article until the license fee is paid.
(e)
Issuance. The city clerk shall cause the solicitation permit to be issued after finding:
(1)
That the statements in the application are true;
(2)
That control and supervision of the solicitation will be under responsible and reliable persons;
(3)
That the solicitation is solely for the purpose of financing the charitable cause described, except for the attendant expense relative thereto in the application;
(4)
That the license fee has been paid.
Sec. 44-53. - Report of solicitation.
Any person receiving more than $1.00 from any contribution by solicitation made pursuant to a permit granted under this article shall give to the contributor a written receipt, signed by the solicitor, showing the name of the organization to which the permit is issued and the amount received; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to contributions collected by means of a closed box or receptacle used in a solicitation.
