ARTICLE III. - MASSAGE[3]
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-48. - Right of inspection.
The city, through its city clerk or the city police department, shall have the right to inspect the place of business and records of any licensee under this article during the hours authorized under this article for the conduct of business, to ensure compliance with this article.
(Code 2002, § 10-201; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-12)
Sec. 10-49. - Authority to train personnel.
Any licensee under this article who has at least two years' practical experience in a regularly licensed massage or physical culture studio shall have the authority to train massage practitioners under their supervision at their place of business.
(Code 2002, § 10-202; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-6)
Sec. 10-50. - List of employees to be filed with city.
It shall be the duty of all licensees under this article to file with the city, through its city clerk, the names of all employees, their home addresses, home telephone numbers and places of employment within ten days of employment. Any changes in information furnished shall be filed within ten days of the change.
(Code 2002, § 10-203; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-7)
Sec. 10-51. - Record of treatments to be kept.
It shall be the duty of any person granted a license under this article to maintain correct and accurate records at the place of business of the names and addresses of the persons receiving treatment at such establishment, the type of treatment administered and the name of the person of the establishment administering such treatment. Records shall also be maintained as required under section 10-52. Such records shall be subject to inspection at any time by any city official.
(Code 2002, § 10-204; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-8)
Sec. 10-52. - Hours and place of operation.
(a)
No massage shall be performed by any massage practitioner or any of his employees, apprentices or trainees as a part of conduct of business, in the home of any client or in any place except for the place of business of a licensee under this article, unless a written memorandum is first made in a log or record book kept at the place of business for the purpose of recording consecutively the date, time of treatment, place of treatment, name and address of person to receive treatment, type of treatment to be received, the name of the person to render treatment, and the time the practitioner leaves to render treatment. The practitioner shall record the time each treatment was completed immediately upon his return to the place of business.
(b)
No business of a massage practitioner shall be located upon any realty unless it is zoned C-2 or I under the zoning chapter of the city.
(c)
No business of a massage practitioner shall be engaged in and no place of business shall be open for business except within and between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m., Eastern Standard Time or Daylight Saving Time, as the case may be at the time.
(Code 2002, § 10-205; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-10)
State Law reference— Giving massages in places used for lewdness, prostitution, etc., O.C.G.A. § 16-6-17.
Sec. 10-53. - Prohibited contact.
No massage practitioner, or any of his employees, apprentices or trainees shall manipulate, fondle or handle the sexual organs of any person.
(Code 2002, § 10-206; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-11)
Secs. 10-54—10-79. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 10-80. - Required.
No person shall engage in the business, trade or profession, or practice the business, trade or profession of a massage practitioner unless such person shall, before engaging in such business, trade or profession, obtain a license with the city through its city clerk.
(Code 2002, § 10-231; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-1)
Sec. 10-81. - Massages by unlicensed persons.
Massages may be given by persons not holding a license under this division only if such person is an apprentice or trainee of a license holder; only if such massages are given under the direct supervision of a licensee; and only if such licensee is present in the same room where the massage is being administered during the entire time during which the massage is given, and has an unobstructed view of both the person giving and receiving the massage.
(Code 2002, § 10-232; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-9)
Sec. 10-82. - Application contents.
An application for the license required by this division shall be in writing and shall set forth or show compliance with the following:
(1)
Name and address of applicant.
(2)
Name and address of any person having previously employed the applicant for a period of two years or longer in a massage therapy clinic.
(3)
Qualifications, plainly stated, with all documentary exhibits annexed.
(4)
Affidavit certifying as to the good moral character of the applicant, signed by three qualified electors of the county. No such affidavit shall be required for renewals under this division.
(5)
A copy of the diploma required under section 10-84(2) or affidavits proving facts required under section 10-84(3) must be attached.
(Code 2002, § 10-233; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-1)
Sec. 10-83. - Investigation.
The city police department shall investigate the background and make a character reference check of applicant and each employee of the applicant prior to the issuance of any license.
(Code 2002, § 10-234; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-2)
Sec. 10-84. - Qualifications of applicant.
An applicant, prior to making application for a license, must have the following qualifications:
(1)
The applicant must be of good moral character.
(2)
The applicant must be the holder of a diploma earned by the applicant from a school, representative of the fact that the applicant attended a course of study of not less than six months, consisting of a curriculum of physical culture, massage, hydrotherapy, electrotherapy, hygiene, health service management and other such subjects.
(3)
If the applicant does not have such diploma, then the applicant must have had an apprenticeship and practical experience for a period of not less than two years in a regularly licensed massage therapy clinic in which the applicant has received training and experience in massage therapy, hydrotherapy, electrotherapy, hygiene, health service management, and other subjects.
(Code 2002, § 10-235; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-3)
Sec. 10-85. - Issuance; fee; display.
(a)
If such application is submitted in proper form and is approved by the mayor and council, then the city clerk shall issue a business license to such applicant for the calendar year during which it is issued upon the payment of an annual license fee, based on a calendar year from January 1 through December 31 of each year, in an amount to be set by resolution of the mayor and council from time to time. A schedule of fees shall be maintained on file in the office of the city clerk.
(b)
Such license shall be prominently displayed in licensee's place of business so as to be capable of being viewed by clients and customers.
(c)
The applicant shall be required to sign an acknowledgement that he has received, read and understands the provisions of this division, prior to issuance of any license.
(Code 2002, § 10-236; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-4)
Sec. 10-86. - Transfers.
No license shall be transferable.
(Code 2002, § 10-237; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-13)
Sec. 10-87. - Denial, suspension or revocation for cause; hearing.
(a)
No application shall be denied except for due cause as defined in subsection (b) of this section. No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any person shall be suspended or revoked except for due cause as defined in subsection (b) of this section, and after a hearing upon written notice to the licensee of the time, place and purpose of such hearing, and a statement of the charge upon which such hearing shall be held. Three days' notice shall be deemed reasonable, but shorter or longer periods of notice may be authorized by the mayor and council as the mayor and council may deem the circumstances justify.
(b)
Due cause for the denial of an application or any renewal for the suspension or revocation of any license shall consist of one or more of the following grounds:
(1)
The applicant or licensee is or has been guilty of fraud in the practice of massage, or fraud or deceit in his being licensed to the practice of massage.
(2)
An applicant or licensee has committed a deceptive business practice in that he sells, offers or exposes for sale, or delivers less than the represented quality or quantity of goods or services or engages in any other fraudulent business transaction which is made punishable by the laws of this state.
(3)
An applicant or licensee has supplied false information to the city regardless of when the city may become aware of it.
(4)
An applicant or licensee has made or makes any misrepresentation of fact, whether through advertisement or through any form of direct communication, oral or written, which is intended to mislead the public or to mislead any party with whom the applicant or licensee has dealt or deals in pursuance of the licensed business. The term "misrepresentation of fact," as used in this subsection, shall embrace not only express misrepresentations, but also misrepresentations arising by virtue of the licensee's conduct, including acts and omissions.
(5)
The applicant or licensee is or has been engaged in the practice of massage under a false or assumed name, or is impersonating another practitioner of a like or different name.
(6)
The applicant or licensee is addicted to the habitual use of intoxicating liquors, narcotics or stimulants so as to incapacitate such person to the extent that he is unable to perform his professional duties.
(7)
The applicant or licensee has violated any of the provisions of this division.
(8)
The applicant or licensee has violated or is guilty of criminal attempt or conspiracy to violate any laws relating to racketeer-influenced and corrupt organizations as defined in O.C.G.A. title 16, ch. 14 (O.C.G.A. § 16-14-1 et seq.), crimes against the person as defined in O.C.G.A. title 16, ch. 5 (O.C.G.A. § 16-5-1 et seq.), sexual offenses as defined in O.C.G.A. title 16, ch. 6 (O.C.G.A. § 16-6-1 et seq.), gambling offenses as defined in O.C.G.A. title 16, ch. 12, art. 2 (O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20 et seq.), obscenity and related offenses as defined in O.C.G.A. title 16, ch. 12, art. 3 (O.C.G.A. § 16-12-80 et seq.), or contributing to the delinquency of a minor, as now or hereafter defined by state law or has been convicted of any felony or misdemeanor involving moral turpitude under the laws of this state or any other state or of the federal government.
(9)
The original application, or its renewal, contains materially false information, or the applicant has deliberately sought to falsify information contained therein.
(10)
There has been the occurrence of a fact that would have barred the issuance of the original license.
(11)
Failure of the applicant or licensee to have or maintain initial qualifications for obtaining the license.
(12)
The applicant or licensee is guilty of employing any person who is not a licensed massage practitioner and allowing or permitting such person to administer massage in such establishment except as may be allowed under this division.
(13)
The premises in which the business is located are in violation of any of the federal, state, county or municipal laws designed for the health, protection and safety of the occupants.
(14)
Failure of the applicant or licensee to actively supervise and monitor the conduct of the employees, customers and others on the premises in order to protect the health, safety and well-being of the general public and the customers.
(c)
Any applicant or licensee who has his license application denied or revoked shall be disqualified from reapplying for such a license for 12 months immediately following the revocation.
(d)
In all hearings pursuant to this section, the following procedures shall prevail, and the proceeding shall be as informal as compatible with justice:
(1)
The mayor shall read or cause to be read the charges and specifications against the licensee. He shall then read or cause to be read any response filed by the licensee.
(2)
The mayor and council shall hear the evidence upon the charges and specifications as filed against the licensee and shall not consider any additional evidence beyond the scope of the charges, and may exclude evidence that is purely cumulative.
(3)
The order of proof shall be as follows: The city representative shall present his evidence under oath in support of the charges; the licensee shall then present his evidence under oath. The evidence of each party may be supported by the submission of pertinent documents. The mayor and council will allow each party to present pertinent rebuttal evidence.
(4)
The licensee and the city may be represented by counsel and may present, examine and cross examine witnesses. In addition, the mayor and council may interrogate both parties and all witnesses to obtain necessary information.
(Code 2002, § 10-238; Ord. of 5-14-2001(1), § 10-6-5)
