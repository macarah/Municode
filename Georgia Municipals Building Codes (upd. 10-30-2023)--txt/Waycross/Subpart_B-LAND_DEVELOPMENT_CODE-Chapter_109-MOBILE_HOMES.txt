Chapter 109 - MOBILE HOMES

Sec. 109-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Mobile home means a single-family dwelling designed for transportation after fabrication in one or more sections not otherwise in compliance with the building codes of the city, on streets or highways, on its own wheels or on a flatbed or other trailers and arriving at the site where it is to be occupied as a dwelling complete and ready for occupancy except for minor and incidental unpacking and assembly operations, locations on jacks or permanent foundations, connections of utilities and the like. Mobile homes can consist of one or more components that can be retracted for towing purposes and subsequently expanded for additional capacity or of two or more units separately towable but designated to be joined into one integral unit. A home complying with the terms of the standard housing code (as the same may be amended from time to time) relating to the construction of single-family homes and placed on a foundation complying with all of the terms of the standard housing code (as the same may be amended from time to time) relating to a single-family home shall not be deemed to be a mobile home.
(Code 1977, § 19-6; Ord. No. 94-25, § 1, 12-20-1994)
Sec. 109-2. - Registration.
Every owner of a mobile home or relocatable home used as a residence and located within the corporate limits of the city shall register such mobile home or relocatable home with the building official, furnishing information as to the make, model number, length, width and location of such mobile home or relocatable home. A registration certificate, and a permit as hereinafter provided, shall be furnished to such owner, provided that the mobile home or relocatable home and its location shall meet the requirements of the zoning ordinance and other applicable codes and ordinances of the city.
(Code 1977, § 19-2; Ord. of 8-19-1969, § 2)
Sec. 109-3. - Permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning a mobile home or relocatable home intended to be used by such owner or any other person as a residence to locate such mobile home or relocatable home within the corporate limits of the city, or to relocate the same within such corporate limits, or to remove the same beyond such corporate limits, without having first applied for and obtained a permit from the city authorizing such location or relocation.
(Code 1977, § 19-1; Ord. of 8-19-1969, § 1)
Sec. 109-4. - Payment of taxes.
No permit for the location or relocation of any mobile home or relocatable home shall be issued by the building official unless all applicable taxes due the city on such mobile home or relocatable home have been paid.
(Code 1977, § 19-3; Ord. of 8-19-1969, § 2)
Sec. 109-5. - Fee.
No fee shall be charged by the city for any registration certificate required by this chapter. The fee for any required permit shall be as established by the city commission.
(Code 1977, § 19-4; Ord. of 8-19-1969, § 6; Ord. No. 85-12, § 1, 4-16-1985; Ord. No. 94-16, § 1, 7-19-1994)
Sec. 109-6. - Other provisions of law.
Nothing in this chapter shall in any way modify, alter, amend or affect the provisions of the zoning ordinance or other codes or ordinances of the city applicable to mobile homes or relocatable homes.
(Code 1977, § 19-5; Ord. of 8-19-1969, § 7)
