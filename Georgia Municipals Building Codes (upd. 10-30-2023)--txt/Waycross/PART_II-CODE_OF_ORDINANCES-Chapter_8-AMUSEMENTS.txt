Sec. 8-1. - Pool rooms, etc.; hours regulated.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Billiard room means any public place where a person is permitted to play the game of billiards or pool.
Billiards means any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and shall include all forms of the game known as "carom billiards," "pocket billiards" and "English billiards" or "pool."
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, corporation or entity to operate, run or carry on a billiard room within the city between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. on any day.
(Code 1952, ch. 15, § 37; Code 1977, § 5-1; Ord. No. 92-20, § 1, 6-22-1992; Ord. No. 98-15, § 1, 6-2-1998)
Sec. 8-2. - Dance halls; hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful and an offense for any person to keep open and to operate a dance hall or similar place of recreation within the corporate limits of the city between the hours of 12:00 midnight Saturday and 12:00 midnight Sunday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. on other days of the week.
(Code 1977, § 5-2; Ord. of 8-9-1952, § 1)
Sec. 8-3. - Payment of admission fee.
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter any building, tent or enclosure used for purposes of public or private amusement, entertainment or exhibition without the consent of those in control thereof or without having first paid the entrance or admission fee fixed by those in control thereof.
(Code 1952, ch. 15, § 4; Code 1977, § 5-3)
Sec. 8-4. - Disturbing, interfering with performances, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person, having entered any public building, tent or enclosure used for purposes of public or private amusement, entertainment or exhibition, with the intent to prevent or disrupt any lawful performance, entertainment, amusement or any other lawful undertaking in progress therein to substantially obstruct or interfere with the lawful performance, entertainment, amusement or any other lawful undertaking in progress by physical action or verbal utterance.
(Code 1952, ch. 15, § 5; Code 1977, § 5-4)
Sec. 8-5. - Obstructing entrances; boisterous, etc., conduct at entrances.
It shall be unlawful for any person to congregate or gather about the door or other entrance to any building, tent or enclosure used for public or private amusement, entertainment or exhibition in such a manner as to interrupt, interfere with, impede or obstruct the progress of any person entering or leaving such places or to engage in any boisterous, noisy or disorderly conduct or speak while so gathered or congregated about such a place.
(Code 1952, ch. 15, § 6; Code 1977, § 5-5)
