Sec. 30-1. - Municipal elections conducted by county.
Authority is hereby granted to the county to conduct any and all elections which would otherwise be held or conducted by the city in accordance with O.C.G.A. title 21, ch. 2 (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.) and in that regard the county shall perform all duties as superintendent of elections.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 1, 5-27-1997; Ord. No. 2001-02, § 1, 2-13-2001)
Sec. 30-2. - Reimbursement to county.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45(c), the city shall reimburse the county for all costs incurred in performing the functions hereby authorized.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 2, 5-27-1997; Ord. No. 2001-02, § 2, 2-13-2001)
Sec. 30-3. - Authority to contract.
The mayor of the city is hereby authorized to enter into such contracts with the county as may be necessary to carry out the intent of sections 30-1—30-3.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 3, 5-27-1997; Ord. No. 2001-02, § 3, 2-13-2001)
Sec. 30-4. - Election by plurality.
In any election held to fill the offices of mayor and/or councilmember, election shall be by plurality. The candidate, or in the case of councilmember, candidates, receiving the most votes shall be declared elected. A majority vote shall not be required.
(Ord. of 8-25-1976, § I; Ord. of 7-30-1984, § I)
Sec. 30-5. - Election of councilmembers.
A candidate for council shall not be required to designate the specific council position which he/she is seeking. In a general election the six candidates for council with the highest number of votes shall be declared elected.
(Ord. of 8-25-1976, § II; Ord. of 7-30-1984, § II)
Sec. 30-6. - Voter registration.
A person who is not registered and desires to vote at any special primary or special election, shall make application no later than the close of business on the fifth day after the date of the call for the special primary or special election, excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays of the state, except as provided in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-224.
(Ord. of 7-30-1984, § III)
Sec. 30-7. - Notice of candidacy; filing dates.
In the case of a general election, the municipal qualifying period shall commence no earlier than 8:30 a.m. on the last Monday in August immediately preceding the general election and shall end no later than 4:30 p.m. on the following Friday. In the case of a special election, the municipal qualifying period shall commence no earlier than the date of the call and shall end no later than 25 days prior to the election.
(Ord. of 7-30-1984, § IV)
Sec. 30-8. - Term of office.
Officers properly elected in the general election shall take office on the first day of January following and continue in office for four years or until their properly elected successors take office.
(Ord. of 8-25-1976, § IV; Ord. of 7-30-1984, § V)
Sec. 30-9. - Contesting elections.
The results of any election may be contested as provided by law.
(Ord. of 8-25-1976, § III; Ord. of 7-30-1984, § VI)
State Law reference— Contesting elections, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-520 et seq.
