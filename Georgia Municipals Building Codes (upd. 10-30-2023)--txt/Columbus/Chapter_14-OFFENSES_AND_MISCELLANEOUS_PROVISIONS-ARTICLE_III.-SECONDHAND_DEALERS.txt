Sec. 14-81. - Secondhand dealers defined.
Secondhand dealers covered by this article shall include for profit trade shops, swap shops, flea markets, and thrift shops, and any other person who buys or receives for the purpose of resale for profit used or new property which is still capable of being used for the purpose for which it was originally intended; but this article does not cover secondhand dealers, such as pawnbrokers and dealers in precious metals and gems, who are covered by other provisions of this Code.
(Ord. No. 80-101, § 1, 8-19-80; Ord. No. 84-11, § 2, 2-7-84; Ord. No. 90-92, 10-9-90)
Note— See the editor's footnote at the beginning of this article.
Sec. 14-82. - Registry; information required.
Every dealer in the articles described herein shall keep a registry which shall contain the following information in connection with the purchase by such dealer of such article or articles:
(1)
The name of the person or other entity from whom the article is purchased;
(2)
The residence or place of business of such person or entity;
(3)
Identification of such person or entity, including driver's license number or vehicle plate number of the seller;
(4)
The name and address of the person or other entity from whom the article was acquired by the person selling the article to the dealer;
(5)
A full description of each seller; and
(6)
A serial and/or identifying number where available shall be secured from the seller.
(Ord. No. 80-101, § 2, 8-19-80)
Sec. 14-83. - Retention and availability of registry.
(a)
The registry required by this article shall be retained by the dealer for a minimum of 90 days.
(b)
Each dealer shall make the registry required herein available for inspection by any officer of the Columbus Police Department.
(c)
In addition to the record-keeping requirements of this article, secondhand dealers will also be required to keep on their business premises, for a period of seven days, all items purchased or traded, and such items will not be altered in any manner and shall be open for inspection by law enforcement officers or the general public.
(Ord. No. 80-101, § 3, 8-19-80; Ord. No. 84-11, § 3, 2-7-84)
Note— See the editor's footnote at the beginning of this article.
Sec. 14-84. - Duty of seller to furnish accurate information.
Any seller of articles described herein shall give accurate, true and correct facts to the dealer, of all information required under this article.
(Ord. No. 80-101, § 4, 8-19-80)
Sec. 14-85. - Violations.
It shall be unlawful for secondhand dealers or their agents to purchase or trade secondhand items from any seller or trader without first obtaining the information required by this article and without keeping and signing the records and allowing inspection of the goods as required by this article; it shall be unlawful for sellers or traders to dispose of merchandise in violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 80-101, § 5, 8-19-80; Ord. No. 84-11, § 4, 2-7-84)
Note— See the editor's footnote at the beginning of this article.
