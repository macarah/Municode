ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 17-1. - Badge—Possession of official badge only.
It shall be unlawful for any person to have in his possession a police badge of the city except the official police badge.
(Ord. of 5-2-23, § 2)
Sec. 17-2. - Same—Possession by authorized personnel only.
It shall be unlawful for any person to have in his possession the police badge mentioned in the preceding section, except a police officer of the city or some officer authorized by law to exercise police power for the city.
(Ord. of 5-2-23, § 3)
Sec. 17-3. - Same—Sale, delivery, etc.; certificate, required, by whom signed.
It shall be unlawful, in the city or its police district, for any person to sell or deliver to any other person any badge containing the word "detective", "police", "sheriff", "deputy sheriff", "marshal", "deputy marshal", or other words descriptive of a law enforcement or peace officer, unless the person to whom such badge is sold or delivered first furnishes to the person so selling or delivering such badge a written certificate showing that the person to whom the badge is sold or delivered is legally the type of officer to which such badge applies. Such certificate is to be signed by the officer or legal authority charged with the appointment of such type of officer, except that if the wording on such badge is descriptive of any officer elected by vote of the people the certificate shall be signed by the Ordinary of Muscogee County.
(Ord. of 10-1-46, § 1)

Sec. 17-5. - Jurisdiction.
The jurisdiction of the police officers of Columbus, Georgia shall be the entire area of Columbus, Georgia. The police officers are hereby authorized to make cases for violations of the law, to issue summons, and to take other actions necessary to the fulfillment of their duties and responsibilities.
(Ord. of 9-5-23, § 2; Ord. No. 78-65, § 4, 5-23-78)
Sec. 17-6. - Stolen, abandoned or impounded property—Disposition by police.
All personal property (except as herein stated) now in the possession of the police department of the City of Columbus, or which may hereafter come into the possession of said department, where said property has been stolen, or where the ownership is unknown to the chief of police, or where such property has been impounded for violation of any law relating to traffic for a period of 60 days and has not been claimed or called for by the owner or other person having the right to possession of the impounded property, shall be handled and disposed of as follows:
All such property shall be safely kept, for a period of 60 days after being received by the department, in a room provided therefor at police headquarters, and if after the expiration of that time the same shall be unclaimed by a person making proof of ownership satisfactory to the chief of police, such property shall, by the chief of police, be advertised and sold.
(Ord. of 6-20-38, § 1; Ord. No. 62-133, § 1, 12-27-62)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 62-133 amended the ordinance of June 20, 1938, from which above § 17-6 is derived, to add the provision relating to impounded property in the first paragraph.
Sec. 17-7. - Same—Notice of sale, posting publication, information.
The advertisement of such property for sale shall, (except as hereinafter stated), be posted at the door of the County Court House in Muscogee County, Georgia, and published one time in a newspaper regularly published in the city, such posting and publication to be at least ten days before the date set by the chief of police for such sale. The advertisement shall give a description of the articles to be sold, and the time when and the place where the sale will take place.
(Ord. of 6-20-38, § 2)
Sec. 17-8. - Same—Time and place of sales.
All such sales shall be held in front of police headquarters in the city, between the hours of eleven o'clock a.m., Central Standard Time, and three o'clock p.m., Central Standard Time, and shall be at public outcry to the highest bidder for cash; provided, however, such a sale of personal property may be held at any place within the corporate limits of the city, which is specified in the advertisement of such sale.
(Ord. of 6-20-38, § 3; Ord. of 9-21-54, § 1)
Sec. 17-9. - Same—Perishable property.
Any unclaimed perishable property coming into the possession of the police department may, in the discretion of the chief of police, be sold by him at either public or private sale, either without advertisement or upon such advertisement, as he deems expedient.
(Ord. of 6-20-38, § 4)
Sec. 17-10. - Same—Destruction of weapons or claim by finder.
(a)
The preceding sections relating to disposition of stolen or abandoned property will not authorize the sale of any weapons that are illegal per se or that are prohibited by law, nor shall the sale of any weapons under the preceding sections be consummated unless all provisions regulating such sales imposed by local, state, or federal law are met. The police chief shall have the authority to conduct the sale of weapons in bulk and to receive bulk bids on any given number of weapons available for sale.
(b)
For the purposes of this section, the finder of the property, unless he is an employee of a law enforcement agency of Columbus, Georgia, or convicted of a felony, may claim such property after the expiration of the 60-day period required by this section, and provided such weapon is a legal weapon.
(Ord. of 6-20-38, § 7; Ord. No. 80-86, § 1, 7-22-80; Ord. No. 91-31, 5-7-91)
Sec. 17-11. - Same—Record of disposition, information.
The chief of police shall keep a record showing when such property came into the possession of the police department, where found or from whom taken, its description, when claimed and by whom, or when advertised and when sold, and the amount for which each article is sold. All funds derived from such sales shall be paid by the chief of police to the treasurer of the city.
(Ord. of 6-20-38, § 5)

Sec. 17-13. - Metro drug task force.
(a)
Authorization. The chief of the police department and the county sheriff are authorized to participate in the joint law enforcement activities of the metro drug task force to the extent permitted by applicable law, with such task force described in detail by documents entitled "Metro Narcotics Task Force" which are on file in the office of the clerk of council.
(b)
Forfeited money or currency; administration. Money and currency which is forfeited under the Georgia Controlled Substances Act (including money and currency from forfeited or condemned property) which vests in and which is paid into the county treasury as county funds shall be released from the county treasury and shall be jointly administered by the chief of the police department and the county sheriff and designated for use by the members of their respective departments in conjunction with law enforcement activities of the metro drug task force under the following conditions and within the following limits:
(1)
The amount so released, administered and designated shall be placed in a separate account which shall not exceed $100,000.00 at any one time.
(2)
All money and currency in said account shall be derived from cases involving members of the Columbus Police Department or members of the Muscogee County Sheriff's Department in conjunction with law enforcement activities of the Metro Drug Task Force.
(3)
No provision contained herein shall affect any accounts previously established for the use of the police department or the county sheriffs department pursuant to Ordinance Number 82-64 and the provisions of Ordinance Number 82-64 shall remain in full force and effect.
(c)
Use of funds. The money and currency contained in the account shall be used to defray nonbudgeted narcotic investigative costs as permitted under O.C.G.A. section 16-13-49, and these funds shall supplement any funds budgeted for such purposes.
(Ord. No. 89-48, §§ 1—3, 4-25-89)
Editor's note— Ordinance No. 89-48, adopted April 25, 1989, did not specifically amend this Code; hence, inclusion of 1—3 as § 17-13 was at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 17-14. - Duties of police chief.
(a)
The chief of police shall be the chief executive officer of the Columbus Police Department. The chief shall be responsible to the mayor for the overall operations of the department. The chief of police is the ranking officer of the department and shall have authority over all personnel, sworn and nonsworn, within the department.
(b)
The chief shall be responsible for the selection, training, assignment and promotion of all personnel. The chief shall have the authority to discipline, including the termination of, any departmental personnel.
(c)
The chief is authorized to promulgate rules and regulations and to establish policy and procedure governing the conduct of departmental employees and the activities and operations of the police department. The chief is responsible for the fiscal management of the department and shall prepare and submit an annual budget to the mayor and city manager.
(d)
The chief of police shall make a monthly written report to the mayor outlining the activities, accomplishments and condition of the department.
(e)
The chief may delegate any of the responsibilities of the office to any of the subordinates within the police department.
(Ord. No. 91-117, 12-13-91)
Editor's note— The provisions of Ord. No. 91-117, adopted Dec. 31, 1991, amending Ch. 17 by adding a new section, were included as § 17-14 at the editor's discretion.
Cross reference— Office of chief of police, § 17-21.2.
Sec. 17-15. - Forfeited money.
(a)
Money which is forfeited pursuant to any state or federal law, including money obtained from the sale of forfeited property, which is made available to the police department or the sheriff shall be accounted for and used under the following conditions:
(1)
All forfeited money made available to the police department shall be deposited in the police forfeiture fund, which is hereby established for the exclusive uses provided herein. All forfeited money made available to the sheriff shall be deposited in the sheriff's forfeiture fund, which is hereby established for the exclusive uses provided herein. The police forfeiture fund and the sheriff's forfeiture fund shall be maintained by the finance director as special revenue funds in a manner which conforms to all applicable state and federal laws or regulations governing forfeitures, and to all laws and regulations governing the accounting and use of public funds.
(2)
The money contained in the funds established herein shall be used for unbudgeted operating expenditure of the police department and sheriff, but in no event shall forfeited money be used for personnel expenses or for any purpose prohibited by state or federal law or to supplant funds which otherwise would be made available to the police department or sheriff through the annual budget. The mayor/director of public safety will receive quarterly audit reports of the funds activity.
(3)
Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary, the money contained in the funds established herein may be used as matching funds for grants if permitted by the state or federal laws under which the forfeiture are obtained.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to forfeitures made available to Metro Narcotics Task Force pursuant to section 17-13 of the Columbus Code of Ordinances.
(c)
Ordinance No. 82-64 is hereby repealed. The finance director shall take such actions as are necessary to place funds currently in the sheriff's account established therein in the sheriff's forfeiture fund and to place funds in the police department account established therein in the police forfeiture fund.
(Ord. No. 98-22, §§ 1—3, 3-10-98)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 98-22, §§ 1-3, adopted March 10, 1998, did not specifically amend this Code; hence, inclusion of its provisions as § 17-15 herein was at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 17-23. - Definitions.
The following definitions shall be used to administer and interpret Article III of Chapter 17:
(a)
Applicant: Employees who assert they are eligible for promotion to the rank of police sergeant, police lieutenant or police captain in the police department of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia.
(b)
Eligible: Any applicant certified in writing by the chief of police to participate in the applicable promotional procedure.
(c)
Participant: Any employee who, upon being certified as eligible by the chief of police and actually engages in any or all of the applicant procedures for the rank for which he has been certified as eligible.
(d)
Candidate: Any participant whose name appears on the promotional roster established for the rank for which he is competing.
(Ord. No. 20-058, § 2, 12-15-20)
Sec. 17-24. - General provisions.
(a)
It shall be the policy of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia Police Department to select only from among the best qualified employees for promotions to positions of greater responsibility. Concurrently, it shall be the policy of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia Police Department to administer promotional procedures which yield valid and reliable information on which to base promotional decisions and to insure rigorous and fair competition among all employees eligible for promotion.
(b)
Separate eligibility requirements, promotional procedures, promotional rosters, and other provisions relating to the ranks of police sergeant, police lieutenant and police captain are specified herein. Policy shall be promulgated by affected departments of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia to implement the provisions and intent of this or related enactments.
(c)
Each promotional rank, those being police sergeant, police lieutenant and police captain, shall have a separate promotional roster which shall have a duration or life of 24 months from the date on which it is established. A new promotional roster must be established within 90 calendar days of the lapse of the previous promotional roster for that rank.
(d)
The chief of police shall fill each promotional vacancy within 30 calendar days from a valid promotional roster. Provided, however, that if a vacancy occurs within the last 30 calendar days of the life of a promotional roster, such vacancy or vacancies must be filled before the promotional roster can lapse. If a vacancy occurs after the expiration of a roster and before the establishment of a new roster, that vacancy must be filled within 15 working days after the new roster is established.
(e)
With respect to written test scores, each participant may review his/her own examination and the applicable keyed examination. Examinations and keyed examinations will not be released, except as may be otherwise legally required. Each participant will be provided with their written test score and a rank ordered list of written test scores so as to allow the participant to determine where he/she fell in that ranking. There will be no identifying information connected with the list of rank ordered written test scores. Participants desiring to do so must make a written protest within five days of receiving their examination scores. The Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia, Chief of Police and Director of Personnel, or their designee, shall respond in writing within 15 days of the receipt of a written protest as to its disposition.
(f)
Prior to the assessment center, the outside entity will furnish the chief of police and the personnel officer with a listing of the predetermined range used to band the participants. The personnel officer will be responsible for posting the range standards so any participant may review them prior to the assessment center. With respect to the assessment center results, candidates who personally participated may review their own scores and narrative summaries and make protests and receive responses as provided for in section 17-24 (e) above.
(g)
All promotions shall be probationary and no officer shall be permanently appointed to a rank until a six-month probation period is satisfactorily completed. Such probationary period may be extended six months in accordance with the personnel rules and regulations of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia. During either the original term or an extension of a probationary period, the chief of police may return a candidate to his previous rank for cause.
(h)
Any candidate who declines a promotion shall do so in writing and shall be eliminated for further promotional consideration for the duration of the promotional roster on which his name appears.
(i)
No promotion shall be made under this promotional procedure when a vacancy occurs in a nonbudgeted position, except where the candidate for such position is occupying a police assignment level equal to or greater in salary than the permanent rank position that is vacant.
(j)
Other appeals: The right of an employee to challenge any aspect of the administration of the promotional procedure which has a direct impact upon his promotability is fundamental to any equitable system. Appeals not specifically provided for elsewhere in Article III of Chapter 17 may be made and shall be disposed of in the manner described in section 17-24(e); provided, however, the selection of an individual(s) for promotion shall not be subject to such appeal process.
(k)
Availability of source material: No announcement of the beginning of a police sergeant's or police lieutenant's promotional procedure can be made until sufficient source material from which written examination questions are to be drawn are available to immediately implement a reasonable system of loan to eligibles.
(l)
To be eligible for the appointed position of corporal, an officer must meet the following criteria at the time of application: 1) have two years of continuous service with the department as a police officer; and have a minimum of 63 semester hours or 90 quarter hours of college credit from an accredited institution, or 2) have a minimum of four years of continuous service with the department as a police officer and have completed Supervision Levels I, II, and III from a POST accredited academy or organization. Officers who have had two formal disciplinary actions or who have had two sustained personnel complaints within 12 months of the application date will be ineligible. Officers who Such appointed position shall be made at the discretion of the chief of police.
(m)
Employees appointed to the position of corporal are to carry the assigned title, paygrade and step for so long as they hold either position. Removal from the corporal position shall be at the discretion of the chief of police, based on one or more of the following: disciplinary reasons, poor job performance, or administrative or operational manpower allocations or adjustments. Employees being returned to a police officer status shall forfeit the title and the step increase they received as a result of their initial appointment and shall be placed in the appropriate paygrade and step of a police officer.
(Ord. No. 20-058, § 2, 12-15-20)
Sec. 17-25. - Eligibility for promotions.
(a)
For promotion to police sergeant. To be eligible to participate in the promotional procedure for police sergeant, the employee must hold the rank of police officer or its equivalent as determined by the chief of police and have a minimum of five years' continuous service, including the probationary period, with the Police Department of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia. For purposes of computation of service, all eligibles who will have completed the fifth year of such service on the date on which the written phase of the sergeant's promotional procedure is given, shall be deemed to have met the length of service requirement.
(b)
For promotion to police lieutenant. To be eligible to participate in the promotional procedure for police lieutenant, the employee must hold the rank of police sergeant and have a minimum of three years' continuous service, including the probationary period, as a police sergeant with the Police Department of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia. For purposes of computation of service, eligibles who will have completed the third year of such service on the date on which the written phase of the lieutenant's promotional procedure is given, shall be deemed to have met the length of service requirement.
(c)
For promotion to police captain. To be eligible to participate in the promotional procedure for police captain, the employee must hold the rank of police lieutenant and have a minimum of two years of continuous service, including the probationary period, as a police lieutenant with the Police Department of the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia. All eligibles who have completed the second year of service on the first day of the captain's assessment center, shall be deemed to have met the length of service requirement.
(d)
To be eligible to participate in any future promotional procedure for sergeant, the employee must possess a minimum of an associate degree in any field within the educational guidelines set forth by this Columbus Police Department. To be eligible to participate in any future promotional procedure for lieutenant and captain, the employee must possess a minimum of a baccalaureate degree in any field within the educational guidelines set forth by this Columbus Police Department.
(Ord. No. 20-058, § 2, 12-15-20)

Sec. 17-26.1. - Promotional procedure for police sergeant.
For promotion to the rank of police sergeant, the following sequence of phrases shall be followed:
(a)
Certification of eligibility. At least 90 calendar days prior to the date of the written examination, the chief of police shall announce in writing all pertinent facts relating to the police sergeant's promotional procedures, including source material from which the questions are to be drawn. At the end of the ten calendar days following such announcement, the application period shall be deemed closed and within another ten calendar days, the chief shall certify in writing the list of those eligible to participate.
(b)
Written test. A 100 question multiple-choice written examination, based on the results of a job-task analysis, shall be administered. A participant's preliminary test scores may be released to him/her when tests are graded onsite immediately following an examination. Final written examination scores will not be released to participants until all appeals and any appropriate adjustments have been made. Any adjustments arising out of any analysis of the test shall be specified in a written report and shall be made available for review by participants in the same manner as provided for by section 17-24(e). No participant shall have been deemed to have passed the written examination unless they score at least 70 or above on that examination. All participants scoring 70 or above shall advance to the assessment center phase.
(c)
Assessment center. The assessment center shall consist of a minimum of three exercises, such as oral presentation, role-playing, in-basket tasks, and other written problem analyses that are designed to evaluate job-related dimensions. Upon completion of the sergeant's assessment center phase, candidates must, based upon their performance on exercises derived from a job-task analysis, be assigned to one of two promotional priorities to be considered for promotion:
(1)
"Highly recommended for promotion," and
(2)
"Recommended for promotion."
All candidates that score below the recommended standard will not be considered during this promotional process.
(Ord. No. 20-058, § 2, 12-15-20)

Sec. 17-28. - Effect of disciplinary action upon participant whose name appears in the "highly recommended" or "recommended for promotion" portions of the promotional roster.
The chief of police shall as a result of one or more sustained disciplinary actions against a candidate do the following:

Freeze the candidate's promotional consideration when the participant accumulates a total of five days' suspension time (eight hours per suspension day) in any 12-month period.
(2)
Strike the participant's name from the promotional roster for the life of that roster when the participant accumulates a total of ten days' suspension time (eight hours per suspension day) in any 12-month period.
(3)
A participant affected by section 17-28(2) during the life cycle of a given promotional roster will be charged under section 17-28(1), only for suspension days in excess of the ten days for which he/she was removed from the roster, for the next promotional cycle.
(Ord. No. 20-058, § 2, 12-15-20)

All examinations, scores, files, records, administrative directives, and promotional results shall be stored in a secured area by the Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia, Police Department for four years following the publication of the promotional roster for each separate promotional procedure.
(Ord. No. 20-058, § 2, 12-15-20)

The Consolidated Government of Columbus, Georgia, Chief of Police, in conjunction with the Police Promotional Procedures Committee, shall annually review, evaluate and report to the mayor in writing as to the administration of Article III, Chapter 17, and recommend such changes as may be needed considering the totality of circumstances.

ARTICLE VII. - SPECIAL ENFORCEMENT OFFICERS[7]

Footnotes:
--- (7) ---
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 72-55, enacted April 11, 1972, amended Ch. 17 by adding thereto provisions designated as Art. IV, §§ 17-60—17-86. The designation of said provisions was changed to Art. VII by § 1 of Ord. No. 81-96, enacted Sept. 22, 1981.

Sec. 17-60. - Responsibility of chief of police to appoint; general powers, duties of officers; officers subject to police department rules, regulations and orders; badges.
(a)
The chief of police shall have the authority to appoint employees of the consolidated government of Columbus, Georgia, and retired public safety officers who are also certified peace officers pursuant to the requirements of the Georgia Peace Officers Standards and Training Council as special enforcement officers for the consolidated government will be selected by the department for whom they are employed and then referred to the Columbus Police Department for appointment. Such retired officers must make application directly with the personnel officer of the police department.
(b)
The chief of police may whenever expedient upon the application of any person, or corporation, showing the necessity, therefore, appoint and swear any number of special enforcement officers to do special duty in the consolidated city of Columbus, Georgia.
(c)
Special enforcement officers appointed pursuant of law while acting as special enforcement officers should possess the power, perform the duties, and be subject to the orders, rules and regulations of the Columbus Police Department in the same manner as regular sworn officers. Every such special enforcement officer shall wear a badge to be prescribed by the chief of police.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72; Ord. No. 72-79, § 1, 5-9-72; Ord. No. 72-80, 5-9-72; Ord. No. 91-118, 12-13-91)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 72-79, § 1, amended § 17-60(c) by requiring that the appointment of citizens as special enforcement officers during emergency, apprehension of riot, etc. be approved by the council by resolution in writing duly adopted after submission of the names of such citizens to the council. Ord. No. 72-80, § 1, repealed subsection (c) relative to the appointment of special enforcement officers during time of riot, etc. Former subsection (d) was redesignated (c) by the editors to maintain alphabetical sequences of subsections.
Sec. 17-61. - Waiver of claim.
Special enforcement officers, excluding those employed by the consolidated government of Columbus, in consideration of their appointment must sign an agreement releasing and waiving all claims whatever against the Columbus Police Department and the consolidated government of the City of Columbus, Georgia, for pay, salary, or other employee benefits, or compensation for their services and for all expenses connected therewith.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-62. - Powers and duties; "hot pursuit."
Special enforcement officers shall during the term of their holding appointment, possess all of the powers and discharge all of the duties of the force applicable to regular police officers only at the place of their employment or assignment unless engaged in hot pursuit continuously from their point of assignment through adjacent areas.
(a)
Such continuing pursuit with a view toward immediate apprehension is to be continued only in the absence of a regularly commissioned Columbus police officer.
(b)
In all other instances support and cooperation of Columbus police officers is to be requested prior to special enforcement officers attempting apprehension away from their point of employment or assignment.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-63. - Removal.
The special enforcement officer so appointed may be removed at any time by the chief of police, without assigning cause, therefore, and nothing in this section shall be construed to constitute such special enforcement officers as members of the force or entitle them to the privileges of the regular members of the force, or to receive any salary, pay, compensation, employee benefits, or monies whatever from the department or city, or to share in the police pension fund.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-64. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Former § 17-64, which pertained to residence requirements for special enforcement officers, was repealed by Ord. No. 86-31, enacted March 25, 1986. The repealed provisions derived from Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, enacted April 11, 1972.
Sec. 17-65. - Application; photographing, fingerprinting of applicants; search of arrests, records; processing fee.
Applicants must fill out an application in such detail as is required by the chief of police and must be photographed and fingerprinted. Where required, applicants must provide waivers permitting the police to search arrests and other records to develop their histories. A fee set by the chief of police will be paid by the applicant for the processing of an investigation of each application.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-66. - Investigation of applicants.
Applicants must undergo such investigation as deemed advisable by the Columbus Police Department.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-67. - Identification of officers; issuance of card; deposit.
A special enforcement officer will receive an identification card containing his commission which must be returned to the Columbus Police Department upon his death, resignation, termination of employment or revocation of the appointment as special enforcement officer. A deposit of $5.00 will be made with the city treasurer for each commissioned card issued and is returnable upon surrender of the receipt for the deposit and commissioned card.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-68. - Altering identification card.
Persons forfeiting, counterfeiting, or altering a special enforcement officer's identification card so that it may be used by a person other than the one it was originally issued shall be guilty of violating a city ordinance and punished as set out in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-69. - Loss of identification card.
Special enforcement officers are required to advise the chief of police of the loss of their commissioned card with full details concerning the circumstances and what amount of effort was made to recover the card within 48 hours of determining the loss of the document. Failure to cooperate with the chief of police in his inquiries will result in the permanent loss of special enforcement officer commission and forfeiture of the deposit [required by section 17-67.]
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-70. - Uniform.
The uniform of special enforcement officers will not be of similar design and color of that worn by members of the Columbus Police Department, Muscogee County Sheriff's Department and Muscogee County Marshal's Office, and shall be approved by the Chief of Police.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-71. - Shield and cap.
Special enforcement officers will wear a shield and cap device.
(a)
The shield should be made of metal and be easily distinguishable from those worn by members of the Columbus Police Department, Muscogee County Sheriff's Department and Muscogee County Marshal's Office. The clearly, legible words, "Special Enforcement Officer" are to be shown on the badge to the chief of police's satisfaction. Such other designs and insignia as desired by the employer may be placed upon the shield to give it character, but such additions are not to change the overall distinctions or obscure the clearly legible words "Special Enforcement Officer".
(b)
The cap should be made of metal easily distinguishable from those worn by members of the Columbus Police Department, Muscogee County Sheriff's Department and Muscogee County Marshal's Office and bear the clearly visible words "Special Enforcement Officer". Other insignias and identification data can be placed on the cap device by the employer as long as the words "Special Enforcement Officer" are clearly visible and the design meets the standards set by the chief of police.
(c)
Special enforcement officers when in uniform are to wear name badges showing their true names and printed clearly and legible at a minimum distance of six feet.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-72. - Firearms.
A special enforcement officer who is required to carry a revolver or other firearm during the course of his duties shall have his employer direct a letter to the chief of police indicating that the special enforcement officer will carry firearms and that he will conform to all firearm ordinances and regulations set forth in the Code of Ordinances for the consolidated government of Columbus, Georgia. The employer in his letter is to certify to the chief of police that he understands that full responsibility for the use of weapons must be borne by the special enforcement officer and the employer. Liability will not be accrued to the City of Columbus for special enforcement officers employed by private enterprises as a result of carrying or using weapons.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-73. - False representation.
It shall be unlawful for any person not a member of the Columbus Police Department to represent himself falsely as being a member with the fraudulent design upon persons or property, or to have, use, wear, or display without specific authority from the chief of police any uniform, shield, button, wreaths, numbers or other insignia or emblem in any way resembling that worn by members of the Columbus Police Department, Muscogee County Sheriff's Department or Muscogee County Marshal's Office. A violation of this section shall constitute an infraction of a city ordinance and be punishable in accordance with provisions of this Code.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-74. - Vehicles generally.
Special enforcement officers and their employers are not to operate vehicles of similar color and design or with insignias similar to that used by the Columbus Police Department and Muscogee County Sheriff's Department.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-75. - Emergency lights.
It shall be unlawful for special enforcement officers to drive a vehicle upon the streets of Columbus, Georgia displaying emergency lights designated under section 20-10.3 of the Columbus Code.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-76. - Orders.
A special enforcement officer will be subject to the orders of the chief of police and will cooperate in the performance of his duties with members of the Columbus Police Department.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-77. - Limitation on authority.
A special enforcement officer will not perform duty for any person or corporation other than that for which he is appointed.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-78. - Duties of police department.
Columbus police officers will observe special enforcement officers on duty and report to the chief of police any misconduct on the part of such officers or failure to comply with regulations.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-79. - Limitation on use of uniform.
A special enforcement officer will not carry while off duty, except while going to and from his place of assignment, his shield, cap device, or wear his uniform.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-80. - Arrest; making charges.
A special enforcement officer will forthwith upon making an arrest, bring the prisoner before the booking officer at the Columbus Police Department. Assistance from sworn members of the Columbus Police Department should be used in cases where suspects are to be formally arrested and charges lodged. Special enforcement officers should be familiar with existing requirements concerning rights of the criminally accused and should advise all persons taken into custody of these rights. Offense reports will be prepared by the special enforcement officer concerning arrests and other incidents in which they are involved and such reports will be turned in to the records division.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-81. - Testifying in court.
A special enforcement officer is to appear and testify in cases charged against arrested persons at the proper time and place. Failure to appear may cause the commission of the special enforcement officer to be revoked and subject him to contempt of court charges.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-82. - Issuance of summons.
When a special enforcement officer serves a summons, he shall deliver the original copy and one additional copy to Columbus Police Headquarters within eight hours of the time of issuance. The summons will be processed in the same manner as those issued by regular sworn personnel of the Columbus Police Department.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-83. - Change of residence.
A special enforcement officer will promptly notify the chief of police of a change of residence.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-84. - Revocation of appointment.
Noncompliance with any of the provisions of these rules will make the officer liable for revocation of his appointment.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-85. - Answer to mobilization.
Upon notification of a mobilization call for special enforcement officers, they will report to the chief of police.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-86. - Penalties.
Violations of any of the provisions of this article shall be punishable as set forth in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 72-55, § 1, 4-11-72)
Sec. 17-87. - Notice to council of appointments, removals.
All appointments of special enforcement officers made by the chief of police under this article shall be furnished to the Council of Columbus. The chief of police shall inform the council of all initial appointees and thereafter shall notify the Council by the first meeting after an appointment is made, the names of such appointees and shall also notify the council when any appointee is removed from the list of special enforcement officers. Such list shall include the name and address of employer.
(Ord. No. 72-69, § 1, 5-2-72)
