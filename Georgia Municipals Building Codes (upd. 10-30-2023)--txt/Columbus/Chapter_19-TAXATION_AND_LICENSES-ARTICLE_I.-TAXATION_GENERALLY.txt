Sec. 19-01. - Taxing districts established; services considered in establishing tax liability.
(a)
General Service District. The General Services District of Columbus, Georgia, consists of the total area of Muscogee County, and taxation for the General Services District shall be established based upon all services rendered by Columbus, Georgia, with the exception of fire protection, police protection and the operation of the public works department.
(b)
Urban Services District 1. Urban Services District 1 shall consist of the territory which is described as Urban Services District 1 and 2 as of December 1, 1985. Urban Services District 1 shall also consist of Land Lots 277, 259 and 240 in the Number 19 Nances 772 GM; Land Lots 48 and 19 in the Number 8 Nances 772 GM; Land Lots 87, 88 and 89 in the Number 8 Nances 772 GM that comprised the former Town of Bibb City; and the following Land Lots in the Number 9 Edwards 1128 GM that comprise the following areas; the Muscogee Technology Park, being lots 71, 88, 89, 90, 91, and 102; that portion of 69 and 70 southeast of the Central of Georgia Railroad; that portion of 92, 100, and 101 south of Chattsworth Road; that portion of 124 and 125 southwest of 10 th Armored Division Road; that portion of 103, 104, 105, and 123 west of the Fort Benning United States Military Reservation; that portion of 74 and 87 north of the Fort Benning United States Military Reservation; and that portion of 74 west and north of the Fort Benning United States Military Reservation.
(c)
Urban Services District 2. Urban Services District Number 2 consists of all the area of Columbus, Georgia, not described as Urban Services District 1, except the United States Military Reservation.

Sec. 19-10.1. - Exemption of certain solar energy heating and cooling systems and machinery directly used in their manufacture from taxation.
(a)
Pursuant to the authority granted by Article VII, Section 1, paragraph IV of the Constitution of 1976 of the State of Georgia, ad valorem tax exemptions are hereby granted to the following types of tangible property:
(1)
Tangible property used in a solar energy heating or cooling system.
(2)
Tangible property consisting of machinery and equipment directly used in the manufacture of solar energy heating or cooling systems.
(b)
The ad valorem tax exemption granted shall include all such taxes levied for state, local or school purposes.
(c)
(1)
For the purposes of this section, "solar energy heating or cooling systems" shall mean and include all controls, tanks, pumps, heat exchanges, and other equipment used directly and exclusively for the conversion of solar energy for heating or cooling. Such systems shall not include walls, roofs or equipment that would ordinarily be contained in a similar structure not designed or modified to use solar energy for heating or cooling.
(2)
For the purposes of this exemption, "heating" shall mean and include water heating and drying.
(d)
The ad valorem tax exemption herein granted shall begin with the tax year 1977 and shall expire on July 1, 1986.
(Ord. No. 77-62, §§ 1—5, 5-31-77)
Editor's note— Section 19-10.1 is derived from Ord. No. 77-62, §§ 1—5, which ordinance was nonamendatory of this Code.
Sec. 19-10.2. - When tax commissioner's books open for returns of tangible property.
Pursuant to the authority granted to Columbus, Georgia by state law, in 1979 and all future years, the tax commissioner's books shall be open for returns of tangible property, including descriptions of real estate, for the purpose of assessment for taxes, between the first day of January and the first day of March. Such returns shall be made on oath as required by law.
(Ord. No. 78-139, 12-19-78)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 78-139, being nonamendatory of this Code, has been included herein as § 19-10.2 at the discretion of the editors.
