Sec. 14-221. - Adult entertainment establishments prohibited where designated.
After the effective date of this article it shall be unlawful to locate any adult entertainment establishment on sites prohibited by this article.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 1, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 1, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-222. - Establishments existing at the time of enactment of this article.
It shall be unlawful for adult entertainment establishments either existing at the time of the enactment of this article or established thereafter to be operated in violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 2, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 2, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-223. - "Adult entertainment establishment" defined.
Adult entertainment establishments are defined as businesses in which, as the major activity, customers congregate primarily for the purpose of viewing or associating with employees who display anatomical areas designed to provide sexual stimulation or sexual gratification: human genitals, the immediate pubic region, or pubic hair; buttocks to the extent of exposing the immediate anal area; female breasts to points below the nipples; male genitals in a state of erection, even if covered with opaque clothing; all of the above anatomical areas when covered only by transparent or diaphanous clothing.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 3, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 3, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-224. - Identification when applying for license.
All businesses engaging in activities defined by the term "adult entertainment establishment" shall so identify themselves when applying for licenses or permits or certificates from any division or department of the consolidated government, and no such license or permit or certificate shall be issued unless the premises and the persons owning or operating the businesses comply with the criteria in this article; however, any such business located on a site that was not prohibited prior to the enactment of this article may renew but not transfer its license to operate.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 4, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 4, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-225. - Permits.
In addition to all other requirements for doing business in Columbus, Georgia, adult entertainment establishments and all employees thereof shall be required to apply for and obtain a permit from the chief of police certifying compliance with this article. A non-refundable fee in the amount of $6.00 shall be charged for the preparation of each permit. It shall be the responsibility of the employer to see that the employees are carrying with them an identification permit at all times they are working. Said identification permit will expire each year on the birth date of the applicant and must be renewed at such time, and said permit must at all times be in the possession of such person affected hereunder for him or her to be eligible to engage in such business. In making decisions to grant or deny permits, the chief of police shall only consider whether an applicant has strictly complied with this article and its requirements. Said permits shall be either issued or denied within thirty (30) days of application. If an applicant is dissatisfied with the decision of the chief of police, the applicant may appeal to the Superior Court of Muscogee County within thirty (30) days of the decision. If no decision is rendered by the chief of police within thirty (30) days of filing a completed application for a permit, said permit shall be deemed approved.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 5, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 5, 3-19-96; Ord. No. 00-28, 3-7-00; Ord. No. 02-24, § 1, 3-19-02)
Sec. 14-226. - Location.
No adult entertainment establishment shall be located within 600 feet of the property line of any church, school, community teen center, library, nursery school, day care center, playground, residential zone, or condominiums and multiple-family dwellings and townhouses located in A-O zones.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 6, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 6, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-227. - Persons convicted of felony or misdemeanor.
No person shall own, manage, operate in any manner or be employed in any capacity by an adult entertainment establishment who has been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to any federal or state felony within five years of applying for the permit from the chief of police necessary to do business or to be employed, nor shall any such person who has been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to any federal or state misdemeanor involving moral turpitude within two years of such application be issued such a permit.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 7, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 7, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-228. - Private modeling sessions prohibited.
No adult entertainment establishment shall allow and no employee thereof shall engage in private modeling sessions or other activities involving sexual displays in which one employee is placed in a one-on-one situation with one customer, and a regular show area with open seating shall be required for such sessions or displays; neither shall any adult entertainment establishment or any employee thereof allow personal physical contact or close mingling between customers and employees.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 8, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 8, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-229. - Admitting minors prohibited.
All adult entertainment establishments are prohibited during regular business hours from admitting minors as defined by O.C.G.A. § 39-1-1.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 9, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 9, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-230. - License revocation.
The chief of police is empowered to recommend the revocation of the license of any adult entertainment establishment in which the owner is convicted of or pleads guilty or nolo contendere to any provisions of this article or any federal or state felony or any federal or state misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, and the chief of police is empowered to recommend the revocation of the license of any adult entertainment establishment in which a manager or any employee (or employees) in two or more separate incidents on separate occasions is convicted of or enters pleas of guilty or nolo contendere to any such violation.
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 10, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 10, 3-19-96)
Sec. 14-231. - Procedures for revocation.
The procedures for revocation shall be the same as the procedure required by Columbus Code section 3-11(b).
(Ord. No. 93-79, § 11, 9-14-93; Ord. No. 96-21, § 11, 3-19-96)
