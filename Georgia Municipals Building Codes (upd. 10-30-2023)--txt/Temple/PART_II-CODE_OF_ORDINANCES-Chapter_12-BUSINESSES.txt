ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Penalty.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall, upon conviction thereof, be subject to a fine of not more than $500.00 for each offense.
(Ord. of 12-6-1982, art. II, § 8)

ARTICLE II. - INSURANCE INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 12-23. - Annual company license fee.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount of $25.00. For each separate business location in excess of one which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $25.00. For the purposes of this article, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. of 10-3-1983(01), § 1; Ord. of 12-6-1993, § 1)
Sec. 12-24. - Fee for company insuring risks in connection with businesses lending money at various locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under section 12-23, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and, in connection with such loans or sales, offers, solicits or takes applications for life insurance through a licensed agent of a life insurance company, such company shall pay an additional license fee of $10.00 per location.
(Ord. of 10-3-1983(01), § 2)
Sec. 12-25. - Annual fee for each company location; limit on number of insurance classes.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted, and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 12-23, in the amount of $35.00 for each such location within the city. Such license fee shall authorize the writing of only one class of insurance from such location, and the writing of more than one class of insurance from such location shall render such location liable for an additional license fee for each class of insurance written.
(Ord. of 10-3-1983(01), § 3)
Sec. 12-26. - Gross premiums tax.
(a)
Term defined. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-23
(b)
Life, accident and sickness insurance. There is hereby levied an annual tax upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the city in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1.
(c)
Risk insurance. There is hereby levied an annual tax upon each insurer insuring risks within the city, in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year, excluding premiums taxed under subsection (b) of this section, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2.
(Ord. of 10-3-1983(01), §§ 4, 5)
Secs. 12-27—12-52. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SOLICITORS AND CANVASSERS

Sec. 12-53. - Registration and fee required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in business as a canvasser or solicitor, calling at residences without the previous consent of the occupant, for the purpose of soliciting orders, sales, subscriptions, or business of any kind, or for seeking information or donations without first having registered in the city clerk's office. The registrant shall give his complete identification and signature, the name of his employer, the nature of the products or services in which he is interested, the names of the manufacturers of such products or the name of the organization which he is representing, and the proposed method of operation in the city.
(b)
Each registrant shall pay to the city clerk a registration fee of $10.00 for the period expiring 90 days after the date of such registration.
(Ord. of 12-6-1982, art. II, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 12-54. - Registration certificate to be carried at all times.
(a)
Each applicant who shows evidence of good character and who pays the fee provided for in section 12-53(b) shall be furnished a certificate indicating that he has registered and showing the dates covered by such registration.
(b)
Each person shall at all times while soliciting or canvassing in the city carry upon his person the registration certificate, and the same shall be exhibited by such registrant whenever he is required to do so by any police officer or by any person solicited.
(Ord. of 12-6-1982, art. II, § 3)
Sec. 12-55. - Exceptions for officers and employees of city, county, state or federal government.
The provisions of this article shall not apply to officers or employees of the city, county, state or federal government or any subdivision thereof when on official business.
(Ord. of 12-6-1982, art. II, § 4)
Sec. 12-56. - Mayor or chief of police may revoke registration for violations.
Any registration described in section 12-53 may be revoked by the mayor or chief of police because of any violation by the registrant of this article or of any ordinance of the city, or of any state or federal law, or whenever the registrant shall cease to possess the qualifications and character required in this article for the original registration.
(Ord. of 12-6-1982, art. II, § 5)
Sec. 12-57. - Declared nuisance when uninvited.
The practice of going in and upon private residences in the city by solicitors, peddlers, hawkers, itinerant merchants or transient vendors of merchandise, not having been requested or invited to do so by the owners or occupants of such private residences, for the purpose of soliciting orders for the sale of goods, wares and merchandise, and/or for the purpose of disposing of and/or peddling or hawking the same, is declared to be a nuisance.
(Ord. of 12-6-1982, art. II, § 6)
Sec. 12-58. - Limited to certain times and days.
It is hereby further declared to be unlawful and shall constitute a nuisance for any person, whether registered under this article or not, to go upon any premises and ring the doorbell upon or near any door of a residence located thereon, or rap or knock upon any door or create any sound in any other manner calculated to attract the attention of the occupant of such residence, for the purpose of securing an audience with the occupant thereof and engage in soliciting, as defined in section 12-53(a), prior to 9:00 a.m. or after 9:00 p.m. of any weekday or Saturday, or at any time on a Sunday or a state or national holiday.
(Ord. of 12-6-1982, art. II, § 7)
Secs. 12-59—12-84. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - MISCELLANEOUS

Sec. 12-85. - Commercial chicken houses.
(a)
In order to protect the health, well-being and general welfare of the citizens of the city, it shall be unlawful for any person to operate a commercial chicken house wherein chickens are raised within the city limits.
(b)
Excepted from the provisions of subsection (a) of this section are any commercial chicken houses in operation as of August 6, 1979, within the city limits, and the keeping of chickens for personal consumption on private property.
(Ord. No. 100-11, 8-6-1979)

ARTICLE V. - PAWNSHOPS
new

Sec. 12-111. - Definitions.
new
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicated a difference meaning:
Pawnbroker shall mean the owner of the pawnshop and also the manager, agent, clerk, and employees of the owner of such pawnshop.
Pawnshop shall mean the business of advancing money and goods, wares, merchandise or other personal property taken on pawn or in which used or secondhand goods, wares, merchandise or personal property are purchased in the normal course of business.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-71)
Sec. 12-112. - Punishment for violation.
new
Any person engaged in the business of pawnbroker or operating a pawnshop who shall fail or refuse to keep the books as required by this article, or who shall make false entries in the books, or who shall take any articles in pawn or purchase any articles without complying with the provisions of this article, or who shall fail or refuse to permit an inspection and examination by a police officer of the city of the books and of the property pledged with or sold to them, shall be guilty of an offense.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-72)
Sec. 12-113. - Special permit required; application.
new
Any person before beginning the business of operating a pawnshop or similar place of business shall file an application with the city clerk for a permit to conduct the business. The application for permit shall state the name and address of the applicant, the street, and number at which the applicant proposes to operate the business, and the names and addresses of all persons with a financial interest in the business.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-73)
Sec. 12-114. - Permit required for occupational tax certificate; issuance.
new
(a)
No license shall be issued to any person until the permit required by this article shall have been granted by the city council. After the permit has been issued by the city council, the clerk shall then issue the license as may be required for the conduct of business upon payment of a license fee in the amount as set for the in the schedule of fees and charges.
(b)
Any person licensed under subsection (a) of this section who sells goods in addition to goods pledged in pawn shall obtain an additional license thereof in accordance with the provisions of article I of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-74)
Sec. 12-115. - Revocation; suspension.
new
Any person failing to comply with the provisions of this article, or such other laws and regulations as may be adopted by the city council for the conduct of the business of a pawnshop, shall be subject to having the license and permit to conduct the business revoked or suspended for a period of time in the discretion of the city council. Such forfeiture and suspension shall result from conviction in the municipal court for a violation of any provision of this article or any other ordinance or regulation covering the conduct of the business for which the permit and license shall have been issued.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-75)
Sec. 12-116. - Records of transactions; inspection by police.
new
All pawnbrokers shall keep books wherein shall be entered an accurate description of all property pledged or sold to them. The description shall include the name and maker of the article, any identifying mark or number, and a description of the kind of material of which it is made so that the article can be reasonably and definitely identified. There shall also be entered in the book the name, address, and age of the person by whom the article was deposited or sold and the dated and hour the transaction was made. If the pawnbroker accepting the article is not personally acquainted with the person making the deposit or sale, the pawnbroker shall require identification sufficient to show that the identification information given is not fictitious of false. The book and articles pledged or sold shall at all times be subject to inspection and examination by the police.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-76)
Sec. 12-117. - Holding period for goods.
new
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnshop permit and license who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, the word goods being herein used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold the goods so taken in pawn or purchase for at least 30 days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-77)
Sec. 12-118. - Reports of transactions.
new
Every pawnbroker shall complete a report to the chief of police before 10:00 a.m. each Monday, in such form as may be prescribed by the chief of police, of all property pledged to or bought by him during the preceding week. The report shall show the name and address of the pawnbroker; the time of each transaction; the serial number on the pawn ticket; the amount paid or advanced; a full description of the article, including the kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry, and all identifying names, marks and numbers, and a description of person selling or pawning, including the name, address, color, weight and height, and such other information as may be required by the chief of police.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-78)
Sec. 12-119. - Prohibited purchases from minors.
new
It shall be unlawful for any pawnbroker, pawnshop operator, his agent or employee to receive in pawn or to purchase from minors goods, wares, merchandise or personal property of any character or description. Any pawnbroker, pawnshop operator, or his agents or employees shall have the burden of requiring identification sufficient to show that the person so making a pawn or sale is not less than 18 years of age.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-79)
Sec. 12-120. - License not transferable.
new
No license issued under the terms of this article shall be transferred from the person to whom it was issued.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-80)
Secs. 12-121—12-135. - Reserved.

ARTICLE VI. - PEDDLING, SOLICITING, AND CANVASSING
new

Sec. 12-136. - Registration required.
new
Any person peddling, soliciting or canvassing within the city shall be required to register and obtain an identification card as provided by this article.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-96)
Sec. 12-137. - Registration; fee; application for identification card.
new
Any person desiring to peddle, solicit, or canvass within the city shall pay to the city clerk a registration fee of $7.50 per day and shall make application with the chief of police for an identification card which shall show payment of the registration fee and the days that the registrant has registered to peddle, solicit or canvass within the city.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-97)
Sec. 12-138. - Investigation of applicants.
new
(a)
Each applicant for registration under this article shall show evidence of good character as required by the chief of police who is hereby given authority to require photographs and fingerprints.
(b)
If, after proper inquiry and investigation, the chief of police shall determine that the applicant is of good character and that the safety of the citizens and the property of the city are protected, each applicant shall be issued an identification card indicating that he has paid the registration fee and has been duly registered and showing thereon the days for which he has registered to peddle, canvass or solicit.
(c)
If the applicant is not a resident of the county, the chief of police may request a report from the county chamber of commerce as to the character and qualifications of the applicant.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-98)
Sec. 12-139. - Exhibition of registration card.
new
Each registrant under this article shall at all times while in the city have upon his person the registration card and shall exhibit the same when requested to do so by any law enforcement officer or by any municipal authority and by any person being solicited. Possession of this registration card shall not in any way represent an endorsement or approval of any product or project by the city.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-99)
Sec. 12-140. - Unlawful acts.
new
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle, canvass, or solicit after sundown.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle, canvass, or solicit within the fire zone of the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle, canvass, or solicit without having registered with the city clerk in accordance with this article or to peddle, solicit, or canvass without having on his person and in his possession an identification card issued in accordance with this article.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-100)

ARTICLE VII. - FLEA MARKETS
new

Sec. 12-161. - Definitions.
new
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Flea market means any business whereby there is operated a center for shopping among collected flea market vendors marketing merchandise to the public from booths, stalls, tables, benches and similar display and marketing configurations and arrangements for the sale of new and used merchandise. Flea markets shall not include antique stores, jewelry stores, coin shops, salvage operations, clothing stores, or other businesses of common merchandise with flea markets where the vendor therein is not operating among a collection of similar vendors. All businesses in the city which meet the definition of flea market shall be subject to the provisions and regulations of these businesses as are set forth in this article.
Flea market promoters means all persons, firms or corporations, partnerships or other forms of business entities operating, maintaining and promoting flea markets.
Flea market vendors means all person, businesses or individuals of every type of and character operating among collections of other flea market vendors for retail sales to the public of new and/or used merchandise in collected marketing centers for sales from stalls, booths, tables, benches and other similar displays or marketing configurations and arrangements.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-151)
Sec. 12-162. - Applications for certificates.
new
All flea market promoters and flea market vendors, prior to the commencement of business, shall first file an application for a occupational tax certificate with the coordinator of inspections or his authorized representative, for a license to conduct such business. Such application shall be on forms prescribed by the coordinator of inspections and contain such information as, in the opinion of the coordinator of inspections, is necessary or proper to regulate this business.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-152)
Sec. 12-163. - Revocation of license.
new
The coordinator of inspections may revoke the license of any flea market promoter when the promoter allows a vendor to operate on premises without a proper vendor's license; the promoter operates without a proper vendor's license; or wherever revocation is necessary to prevent vendors engaging in the purchase, sale, barter or trade of stolen property; or to prevent the purchase, sale, barter, or trade of property with minors provided the promoter has previously been warned of such activity and has not taken action necessary to prevent a recurrence. Such action may also be taken when the flea market promoter repeatedly fails to keep and provide accurate records as required in section 12-164.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-153)
Sec. 12-164. - Records of transactions required.
new
(a)
Records shall be made of all transactions whether purchase, sale, trade, or barter of any merchandise which transaction or amount is $10.00 or more. The records of such transaction shall be made on a form prescribed by the coordinator of inspections. The form shall be dated and require a description of the property and the amount of the transaction.
(b)
All sales of used or previously owned merchandise shall, in addition to the foregoing, require an accurate description of the property, including identifying marks and manufacturer's identification, numbers of the item sold. Additionally, as to the purchaser of such items, the flea market vendor shall obtain and verify from current valid identification presented by such person his name, current address, sex, race, height, weight, and date of birth. A current valid driver's license with be positive identification. The coordinator of inspections may, from time to time, prescribe other acceptable identification forms.
(c)
All forms required in subsection (a) of this section shall be held by vendors and furnished to the police department of the city or to the flea market promoter for delivery to the police department.
(d)
No flea market vendor shall engage in any transaction to purchase or trade for any merchandise or property of a minor.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-154)
Sec. 12-165. - Holding period for precious metals.
new
Any person operating under a flea market promoter's or flea market vendor's license, or any dealer in precious metal or gems who takes, purchases. barters, swaps, or otherwise acquires any gems, numismatic coins, or precious metals as defined in O.C.G.A. § 43-37-1 relating to dealers in precious metals and gems shall comply with all provisions thereof.
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-155)
Sec. 12-166. - Flea market license tax.
new
(a)
The occupation tax for flea market promoters shall be determined as are occupation taxes generally for the city.
(b)
The occupation tax for flea market vendors operating as a part of a flea market promotion consisting of two or more vendors will be as follows:
Weekends .....$5.00
Quarterly .....35.00
Semiannually .....50.00
Annually (based on gross receipts) Minimum .....70.00
(Ord. of 3-3-1997, § 6-156)
