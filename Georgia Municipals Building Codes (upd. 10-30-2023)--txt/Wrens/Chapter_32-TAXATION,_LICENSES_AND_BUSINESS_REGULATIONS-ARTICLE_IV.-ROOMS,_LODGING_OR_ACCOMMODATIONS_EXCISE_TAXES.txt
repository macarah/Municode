ARTICLE IV. - ROOMS, LODGING OR ACCOMMODATIONS EXCISE TAXES

Sec. 32-100. - Excise tax levied.
Pursuant to the provisions of and act of the general assembly authorizing each municipality in the state to impose, levy and collect an excise tax upon the furnishing for value to the public of any room or rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished by any person or legal entity licensed by or required to pay business or occupation taxes to the municipality for operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin or any other place in which rooms, lodgings or accommodations are regularly furnished for value, provided no such tax shall be levied upon the sale or charges for any rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished for periods of more than 30 consecutive days, or for the use of meeting rooms, there is hereby levied an excise tax in the amount of five percent of the charges to the public for such furnishing of any room or rooms, lodgings or accommodation within the city.
(Ord. No. 01-001-2009, § 1, 1-13-2009)
Sec. 32-101. - Tax returns required.
Each person, firm, or corporation collecting said tax shall on or before the first day of the month following the month in which this tax shall become effective and on or before the first day of each month thereafter transmit to the city clerk or a designated representative, upon forms as prescribed by the city, returns showing the total charges for the furnishing of such rooms, lodging or accommodations as provided for herein during the preceding calendar month and shall account for and remit the tax levied herein.
(Ord. No. 01-001-2009, § 2, 1-13-2009)
Sec. 32-102. - Tax deduction for return preparation.
Each person, firm or corporation collecting said tax as provided for herein shall be allowed three percent of the tax due and accounted for and remitted to the city in the form of a deduction in submitting the monthly return and paying the amount due provided said amount due is not delinquent at the time of payment.
(Ord. No. 01-001-2009, § 3, 1-13-2009)
Sec. 32-103. - Failure to pay tax; penalties; interest.
(a)
When any person, firm or corporation liable hereunder shall fail to make any return or pay the full amount of the tax required herein, there shall be imposed a specific penalty to be added to the tax in the amount of five percent or $5.00, whichever is greater, for each additional 30 days, or fraction thereof, during which the failure continues, not to exceed 25 percent or $25.00 in the aggregate, whichever is greater; provided, however, if such failure is due to providential cause shown to the satisfaction of the city administrator in affidavit form attached to the return and remittance within ten days of due date, such returns may be accepted exclusive of penalties and interest. In the case of a false or fraudulent return or the failure to file a return, where willful intent exists to defraud the city of any tax due herein, a specific penalty of 50 percent of the tax due shall be assessed.
(b)
When any person, firm or corporation fails to remit the tax, or any portion thereof, on or before the day when such tax shall be required by law to be paid, there shall be added to the amount due interest at the rate provided for executions from the date due until the date paid.
(c)
All penalties and interest imposed herein shall be payable and collectible by the city in the manner as if they were a part of the tax imposed.
(Ord. No. 01-001-2009, § 4, 1-13-2009)
Sec. 32-104. - Budgeting and application of excise tax.
The revenues raised pursuant to said excise tax shall be budgeted and applied for the promotion of the city and the county in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3)(A).
(Ord. No. 01-001-2009, § 5, 1-13-2009)
Sec. 32-105. - Survival of existing ordinances.
The ordinance from which this article is derived does not repeal or affect the force of any part of any ordinance passed prior to this article where excise taxes levied under such prior ordinance have not been paid in full. Any and all parts of prior ordinances passed that provided for the issuing and enforcing of any tax or assessment required by such ordinances, or that impose fines or penalties for the nonpayment of such taxes, provided for in such ordinance or ordinances, or for failure to comply with any other provisions thereof, shall continue and remain in force until such excise tax or assessment shall be fully paid.
(Ord. No. 01-001-2009, § 6, 1-13-2009)
