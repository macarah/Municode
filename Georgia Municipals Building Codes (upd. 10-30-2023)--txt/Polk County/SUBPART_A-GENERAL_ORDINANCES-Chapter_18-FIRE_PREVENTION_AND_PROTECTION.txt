Chapter 18 - FIRE PREVENTION AND PROTECTION

Sec. 18-1. - Restrictions on fires.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any individual, firm, association or corporation to light, ignite or otherwise set fire in or upon any mountainous brush or forest covered land or land covered with flammable material or upon any road or trail traversing any such land in the county, during a hazardous fire condition as declared by the board of commissioners, any burn ban set forth by the Georgia Forestry Commission, or any mandated burn ban set forth by the State of Georgia.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any individual, firm, association or corporation to light or ignite any bonfires or to burn any rubbish or other materials within the county during a hazardous fire condition as declared by the board of commissioners, mandated burn bans set forth by the State of Georgia, or Georgia Forestry Commission.
(c)
Any law enforcement officer, firefighter, or authorized employee of the county, shall be authorized to extinguish or cause to be extinguished any fire, bonfire or outdoor rubbish fires or other condition in violation of this chapter summarily and without notice in order to ensure the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the county.
(Ord. of 11-4-1986, §§ 1—5; Ord. of 10-9-2012(2))
