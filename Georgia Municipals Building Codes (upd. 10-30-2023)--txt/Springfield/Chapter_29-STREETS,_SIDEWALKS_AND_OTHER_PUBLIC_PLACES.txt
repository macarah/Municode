ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 29-1. - Penalty.
Unless stated otherwise, violations of this chapter are punishable as provided in section 1-7.
Secs. 29-2—29-19. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OBSTRUCTIONS AND ENCROACHMENTS

Sec. 29-20. - Violation of article deemed nuisance.
Any violation of this article and any material part thereof shall be deemed a nuisance per se, and the city council shall, upon ten days' written notice to any person found in violation thereof, proceed to abate said nuisance.
( Ord. No. 01-61, § V, 11-27-1961 )
Sec. 29-21. - Open air markets.
No person shall conduct, maintain or operate an open air market upon the sidewalks or streets in this city, and shall not use any part of the system of streets and sidewalks for the purpose of display of merchandise for sale, except with the written permission of the city council.
( Ord. No. 01-61, § I, 11-27-1961 )
Sec. 29-22. - Awnings, sheds or marquees.
No person shall erect, construct or maintain any shed, awning or marquee so that the same shall extend or project over the sidewalks of the city, except with the written permission of the city council, and then only in such instances where there is a clearance of at least seven feet between the sidewalk and such shed, awning or marquee, and provided the same is suspended from a building with adequate support to hold the same so that no structure, pillar, post or other device shall be required to be erected upon the sidewalk, within the grass plots between the sidewalks or within the streets for the purpose of supporting such structure.
( Ord. No. 01-61, § II, 11-27-1961 )
Sec. 29-23. - Temporary structures.
No person shall at any time erect any temporary or permanent structure within 20 feet of a fire plug and across the property line between the abutting landowner and the sidewalk.
( Ord. No. 01-61, § III, 11-27-1961 )
Sec. 29-24. - Curb service enterprises near fire hydrants.
No person shall conduct a curb service enterprise for delivery or servicing to customers where such service is within 20 feet of a fire hydrant. This section does not apply to normal customer delivery from stores to properly parked vehicles where such vehicles are parked in a legal parking area.
( Ord. No. 01-61, § IV, 11-27-1961 )
