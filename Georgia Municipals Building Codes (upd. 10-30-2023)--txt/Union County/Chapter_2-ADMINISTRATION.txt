Sec. 2-31. - Meetings.
(a)
The commissioner shall hold regular meetings on the third Thursday of each month at 6:00 p.m., unless otherwise ordered by the commissioner.
(b)
Special meetings may be held upon call by the commissioner. Due notice of such meetings shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).
(c)
Meetings shall be held at the Union County Courthouse, 114 Courthouse Street, Blairsville, Georgia.

Sec. 2-32. - Agenda.
There shall be a written agenda prepared prior to each commissioner meeting. Persons who desire to address the commissioner at a meeting shall notify the clerk of the office of commissioner by noon on the Wednesday preceding the next commissioner's meeting. The commissioner shall retain the discretion to recognize any person at a meeting who is not otherwise on the agenda.

DIVISION 2. - COUNTY ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-81. - Primary function and duties.
The primary function of the county attorney is to provide legal advice to, and to represent the county. The county attorney's duties shall include advising and representing the commissioner, sheriff, tax commissioner, clerk of superior court, probate court judge, officials and employees of the county. The county attorney's duties also include advising the commissioner and other county officers and employees of their powers and duties under the law, preparing ordinances and legal documents, reviewing proposed contracts and representing the county and its officers in legal proceedings.
(Res. of 1-25-1995)
Sec. 2-82. - Improvement of understanding.
The county wishes to improve the understanding of the proper role of the county attorney and, by the provisions set forth in this division, clarify the proper role and responsibilities of the county attorney in the representation of county officers.
(Res. of 1-25-1995)
Sec. 2-83. - Ensuring legal representation.
The county is committed to ensuring that each and every county official receives proper and adequate legal representation when appropriate, from the county attorney for actions taken pursuant to the public duties of such officials.
(Res. of 1-25-1995)
Sec. 2-84. - Legal conflict of interest.
It is the county's position that in cases where there is legal conflict of interest with the county attorney providing legal representation, or where the simultaneous or dual representation by the county attorney of any officer or employee of the county and the commissioner would constitute a legal conflict of interest, the county shall provide for independent legal representation of such county officer or employee.
(Res. of 1-25-1995)
Sec. 2-85. - Notification of decisions.
The county attorney, as a professional and a member of the state bar, is sworn to uphold the requirements of professionalism. The county attorney shall seek to fully inform the commissioner and any affected county officer of all decisions affecting the resolution of any dispute requiring representation.
(Res. of 1-25-1995)
Sec. 2-86. - Communications.
The county attorney shall clearly communicate with the commissioner and any affected county officers concerning the nature of representation and, particularly regarding settlements and discussions of proposed items.
(Res. of 1-25-1995)
Sec. 2-87. - Counseling on the value of cooperation.
The county attorney shall counsel the commissioner and affected county officers and employees on the value of cooperation as a means towards the productive resolution of disputes.
(Res. of 1-25-1995)
Secs. 2-88—2-110. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DRUG-FREE WORKPLACE POLICY

Sec. 2-111. - Signing.
All employees will be notified, and will sign a statement acknowledging such notification, that the unlawful manufacture, distribution, dispensing, possession or use of controlled substances (defined in Schedules I through V of Section 202 of the Controlled Substances Act, 21 USC 812, and further defined in Regulation 21 CFR 1308.11—1308.15) is prohibited in the workplace.
(Res. of 6-27-1989)
Sec. 2-112. - Condition of employment.
As a condition of employment, employees will:
(1)
Abide by the terms of this division;
(2)
Notify the commissioner of any criminal drug statute conviction for a violation occurring in the workplace no later than five days after such conviction.
(Res. of 6-27-1989)
Sec. 2-113. - Violation; penalties.
Penalties for drug abuse violations occurring in the workplace will include:
(1)
Taking appropriate personnel action up to and including termination of employment; or
(2)
Requiring the employee to participate satisfactorily in an appropriate drug abuse assistance or rehabilitation program.
(Res. of 6-27-1989)
Sec. 2-114. - Drug-free awareness program.
The county will provide information regarding a drug-free awareness program which is intended to inform employees of:
(1)
The dangers of drug abuse in the workplace;
(2)
The county's intent to maintain a drug-free workplace;
(3)
Any available drug counseling, rehabilitation and employee assistance programs (the county is not required by law to pay for such services); and
(4)
The penalties that may be imposed upon employees for drug abuse violations occurring in the workplace.
(Res. of 6-27-1989)
Secs. 2-115—2-135. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - JOINT DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-191. - Declaration of need; transaction of business.
It is hereby declared that there is a need for a joint development authority to function in Fannin County, Towns County and Union County. Such authority, to be known as the Joint Development Authority of Fannin County, Towns County and Union County and called the authority, is created and activated. Such authority shall transact business pursuant to, and exercise the powers provided by the provisions of the Development Authorities Law, codified in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Res. of 3-28-1995, § 1)
Sec. 2-192. - Creation and activation.
The joint development authority is created and activated by resolution of the governing bodies of Fannin County, Towns County, and Union County as a joint authority pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.1.
(Res. of 3-28-1995, § 2)
Sec. 2-193. - Membership.
(a)
The number of members of the joint development authority shall be nine. Three members shall be taxpayers residing in Fannin County and shall be appointed initially as provided in this division and, thereafter, by the board of commissioners of Fannin County as their terms expire. Three members shall be taxpayers residing in Towns County and shall be appointed initially as provided in this division and, thereafter, by the commissioner of Towns County as their terms expire. Three members shall be taxpayers residing in Union County and shall be appointed initially as provided in this division and, thereafter, by the commissioner of Union County as their terms expire. The members shall receive no compensation for their services, but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties. The initial members shall serve for terms of two and four years.
(b)
After the expiration of the initial terms, the terms of office of all members shall be for terms of four years. Successors to such members shall be appointed by the governing body of the county from which such member has been appointed. If at the end of any term of any member a successor to such member has not been appointed, the member whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is appointed. A majority of the members of the authority shall constitute a quorum, but no action may be taken by the authority without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the authority.
(Res. of 3-28-1995, § 3)
Sec. 2-194. - Officers.
The directors of the joint development authority shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice-chairman, and shall also elect a secretary and treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may, but need not be, a director.
(Res. of 3-28-1995, § 4)
Secs. 2-195—2-215. - Reserved.

The Hospital Authority of Union County, referred to in this division as the "hospital authority," is created and established.

The members of the board of trustees shall serve for staggered terms of six years.

(a)
The Union County Hospital Authority is enlarged from five members to nine members.

The terms of the new members of the hospital authority shall be for two years, four years and six years, and until their successors shall have been elected and qualified.
