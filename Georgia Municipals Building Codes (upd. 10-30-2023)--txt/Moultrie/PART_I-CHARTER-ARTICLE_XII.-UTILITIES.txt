ARTICLE XII. - UTILITIES

Sec. 113. - Collection of utility fees and charges.
Said Council shall have the power and authority by resolution to provide for the collection of fees and charges for services provided by the various utilities and to enforce the payment thereof by execution against the recipient which execution shall be issued like executions for taxes under existing ordinances or such as may hereinafter be made applicable to the subject. They shall have the power and authority to pass ordinances to provide for the manner of carrying the provisions of this section into effect.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1458, § 34; 1968 Ga. Laws, page 2130, § 8; Ord. No. 799, § 2, 2-2-1999)
Sec. 114. - Protection of city utilities.
The Council shall have the power to pass such ordinances, and enforce compliance therewith by suitable penalties, as may be necessary for the protection of all city utilities and their appurtenances, and for securing of purity and healthfulness in the water supply, and shall have full power and authority to abate and remove anything which may cause impurity or unhealthfulness in the water supply of the city.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1458, § 33; 1968 Ga. Laws, page 2130, § 7; Ord. No. 799, § 3, 2-2-1999)
Sec. 115. - Authority to establish charges and fees for collection of trash and garbage.
The mayor and council are hereby authorized to provide by ordinance for charges and fees for the collection of trash and garbage.
(1974 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1220), § 4; Ord. No. 799, § 4, 2-2-1999)
Sec. 116. - Powers regarding utility services.
The Council shall have full power and authority to make rules and regulations as allowed by law regulating the furnishing of water, gas, electricity, sewer service, cable television and any and all data transmission and telecommunication systems, whether by fiberoptic cable or otherwise, into any premises and from time to time to regulate the same and for the protection of the apparatus and other fixtures and appurtenances of said systems.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1458, § 35; 1968 Ga. Laws, page 2130, § 9; Ord. No. 799, § 4, 2-2-1999)
Sec. 117. - Reserved.
Amendment note—Ord. No. 799, § 4, adopted February 2, 1999, repealed 1943 Ga. Laws, page 1458, § 36, as amended by 1968 Ga. Laws, page 2130, § 10, codified as § 117, relating to electric and gas service.
Sec. 118. - Reserved.
Amendment note—1971 Ga. Laws (Act No. 761), § 1, repealed 1956 Ga. Laws, page 3473, § 1, codified as § 118, relating to operation of the city's utility systems outside the city.
Sec. 119. - Acquisition of utility systems in annexed territory.
Where the area of said city has been or may be expanded beyond that defined in its charter by annexation of territory, said city shall not interfere in any manner with the operation, in any part of such annexed territory, of a water, electric or gas system which was operating in any part of said newly annexed territory at the time of such annexation; nor shall said city deny to any such system the use of its streets, alleys or ways subject to reasonable regulation. Provided, however, that said City of Moultrie shall have, and is hereby granted, the power to acquire any portion of any such system which has been incorporated into the territorial limits of said city by the exercise of the right of eminent domain under and in accordance with the provisions of Chapter 36 of the Code of Georgia Annotated, 1933 [O.C.G.A. tit. 22].
(1956 Ga. Laws, page 3473, § 2)
Sec. 120. - Sewer construction.
The council is hereby authorized to construct and lay down sewers through property in said city; provided, that before doing so, any damages done to private property thereby shall be ascertained and paid. The manner of ascertaining the damages shall be the same made as in cases of opening streets and alleys, as set forth in Section 30 of this Act [Section 111 of this Charter]. The mayor and council are hereby authorized to provide for the assessment of costs for sewer improvements to or within their property in the manner provided for the assessment of costs for paving and improving sidewalks by Section 32 of this Act [Section 112 of this Charter], as amended.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1458, § 41; 1974 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1220), § 5)
Sec. 121. - Franchises.
The council shall have the authority to grant franchises, by proper ordinances, to any electric light company, telephone company, cable television company, street railroad company, sewerage company, waterworks company, or other like utilities, or to grant same to any person or corporation under such restrictions and for such times as they may see fit, not to exceed forty years from the date of the granting of the franchise.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1458, § 55; 1968 Ga. Laws, page 2130, § 13)
