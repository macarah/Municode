ARTICLE II. - ELECTION PROCEDURE

Sec. 14-19. - Election officials.
The mayor and council shall by resolution as needed appoint a municipal election superintendent, election managers, registrars, absentee ballot clerk and any other officials as are necessary, all of whom shall exercise those powers and duties set forth in the Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-2-1)
State Law reference— Superintendents of elections, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70 et seq.; powers and duties of municipal superintendents, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70.1; poll officers, election managers, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-90 et seq.; clerks, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-91; compensation of poll officers, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-98.
Sec. 14-20. - Polling place.
The polling place shall be as fixed from time to time by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-2-5)
State Law reference— Selection of polling places, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-265; public buildings to be used as polling places where practicable, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-266.
Sec. 14-21. - Voter registration deadline.
The deadline for registration of voters in city elections shall be at 5:00 p.m. on the day 15 days prior to the date of the election.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-2-2)
State Law reference— Registration of voters generally, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-210 et seq.; qualifications of electors, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-216; rules for determination of residency, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-217.

ARTICLE III. - CANDIDATES

Sec. 14-46. - Notice of candidacy; filing dates.
Notices of candidacy shall be filed by candidates for offices of the city council not sooner than 45 days prior to the election in the case of a general election and not sooner than 30 days prior to the election in the case of a special election. The deadline for filing the foregoing notices of candidacy shall be 22 days prior to the election in the case of a general election and 15 days prior to the election in the case of a special election. Notices of candidacy shall be filed in the office of the municipal election superintendent during normal business hours.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-2-3)
State Law reference— Filing notice of candidacy, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
Sec. 14-47. - Qualification fees.
The qualification fee for candidates filing a notice of candidacy in any general or special election for the office of mayor or city councilmember, shall be three percent of the annual compensation of the office sought, which fees shall be received by the municipal election superintendent at the time of filing the notice of candidacy and paid over to the city by the municipal election superintendent.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-2-4)
State Law reference— Qualification fees, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-131.
