DIVISION 5. - RECORDS AND REPORTS

Sec. 2-394. - Personnel transactions.
All appointments, separations, and other personnel transactions shall be made on forms designated by human resources. A separate file folder shall be prepared and maintained for each employee in a central file location and shall contain all pertinent documents.
(Ord. of 3-6-2020, ch. 5(A))
Sec. 2-395. - Public inspection.
Subject to the Open Records Act, which permits extensive disclosure of personnel records, all personnel records of employees and all records and materials relating to the administration of the human resources system shall be considered confidential and the property of the city. Information obtained in the course of official duties shall not be released by any employee other than by those officially charged with this responsibility. All requests for personnel information must be processed through human resources.
(Ord. of 3-6-2020, ch. 5(B))
Sec. 2-396. - Destruction of records.
(a)
Employee personnel file records shall generally be kept for no less than seven years after termination of employment (O.C.G.A. §§ 45-20-13, 47-2-94), including correspondence, application, retirement records and other personnel records, except as otherwise summarized below (as may be amended by legislature).
(b)
Records are further governed by the Georgia Records Management Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99 (maintain payroll records, 50 years; unemployment compensation, five years; employee handbooks and retirement plans and pay scales, permanent); also see O.C.G.A. § 9-3-25 (correspondence, five years); O.C.G.A. §§ 47-2-26 and 48-7-111 (employee retirement contributions, six years); 29 CFR 825.500 (FMLA, three years); 29 CFR 516.5 (garnishments, four years); 45 CFR 164 (HIPAA, four years); O.C.G.A. §§ 9-3-25 and 9-3-26 (payroll reports, four years); O.C.G.A. § 48-7-111 (wage and tax statements and withholding certificates, four years); 29 CFR 516.6 (work time schedules); O.C.G.A. § 9-3-33 (accident reports); 29 CFR 1602 (non-hired applicants, two years); 29 CFR 1027 (COBRA documentation, two years); O.C.G.A. § 45-20-13 and 49 CFR 382 (drug testing and EAP, five years); 29 CFR 1602 (Grievances, two years); 29 CFR 1910 (hazardous substance, 30 years from separation); and O.C.G.A. §§ 9-3-31 to 9-3-33 (insurance claims, five years).
(Ord. of 3-6-2020, ch. 5(C))
Sec. 2-397. - Attendance reports.
Each supervisor shall prepare and submit regular attendance reports on the form and schedule designated by the human resources office.
(Ord. of 3-6-2020, ch. 5(D))
