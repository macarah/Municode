ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Hours of operation of public parks.
All public parks located in the unincorporated areas of the county shall be open from 6:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. each day, unless otherwise posted at the entrances to such parks; provided, however, the hours when public parks are closed shall not apply to persons using the parks for purposes of launching or removing boats, fishing or camping in areas designated for camping within such parks.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-1)
Sec. 62-2. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited in public parks.
No person shall use, possess or consume any alcoholic beverage within any public park located in the unincorporated areas of the county. For the purposes of this section, the term "alcoholic beverage" means beer, wine, liquor or malt beverage as defined by section 6-2 of this Code.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-2)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 6.
Sec. 62-3. - Violation; penalties.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-3; Ord. No. 00-10, § 11, 10-3-2000)

ARTICLE II. - RECREATION BOARD[2]

--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Boards and commissioners, § 2-31 et seq.

Sec. 62-36. - Established.
There is established and created in and for the county, a recreation board which shall consist of seven members who shall serve without pay. The terms of the current members of the recreation board shall terminate on December 31, 2007. The members of the recreation board shall be appointed as provided for in the bylaws for the recreation board promulgated by resolution of the board of commissioners for the operation of the recreation board. The members of the recreation board appointed to terms beginning on January 1, 2008, or thereafter shall serve for the terms specified in the bylaws.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-11; Ord. No. 07-11, § 1, 11-6-2007)
Sec. 62-37. - Vacancies.
Vacancies on the recreation board shall be filled by appointment for the unexpired term as provided for in the bylaws of the recreation board.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-12; Ord. No. 07-11, § 2, 11-6-2007)
Sec. 62-38. - Recreation director.
The recreation director, employed by the board of commissioners, is made a member of the recreation board.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-13)
Cross reference— Officers and employees, § 2-186 et seq.
Sec. 62-39. - Officers.
The officers and their appointment shall be as provided for in the bylaws of the recreation board.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-14; Ord. No. 07-11, § 3, 11-6-2007)
Sec. 62-40. - Meetings.
Meetings of the recreation board shall be held and conducted as provided for in the bylaws of the recreation board.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-15; Ord. No. 07-11, § 4, 11-6-2007)

The recreating board shall make reports as provided for in the bylaws of the recreation board.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-16; Ord. No. 07-11, § 5, 11-6-2007)
Sec. 62-42. - Functions.
(a)
The recreation board shall provide advice and recommendations to the board of commissioners, the director of the department of county government which oversees the recreation programs in the county and the recreation director as to the improvement of the recreation services then being provided, the addition of new recreation services, the deletion of existing recreation services, ways to raise funds to support recreation services in the county, recreational facilities that are needed and any other matters that bear upon the scope and quality of the county's recreational program. If a vacancy occurs in the position of recreation director, the recreation board may provide advice and recommendations as to candidates to fill such position.
(b)
The recreation board may organize and conduct fundraising events and activities to raise funds for the support and improvement of the recreation program in the county. All funds raised by the recreation board shall be deposited in a special account of the county to be designated as the recreation program enhancement fund (hereinafter referred to as the "fund"). These funds shall not be used to offset funds that would otherwise be spent from the general fund and other sources of county revenues to pay for the operations and capital improvements necessary to conduct the county's recreation program. Money in the fund shall be used for training, equipment, capital projects and other expenses or costs designated to ensure that the recreation program in the county is first class and excels above those of other counties.
(c)
The recreation board shall annually prepare a report of its activities during the preceding year and a proposed budget for the fund, to be presented to the board of commissioners in the spring of each year as part of its budgetary process. Once the budget is approved by the board of commissioners, it shall guide the use of the money in the fund during the ensuing July 1 through June 30 fiscal year. During the fiscal year, the budget may be modified by the board of commissioners as circumstances dictate. The recreation board may submit recommendations to the board of commissioners as to modifications in the approved budget when the recreation board determines such modifications to be desirable.
(d)
The recreation board shall, throughout the fiscal year, submit requisitions to the county finance department for the disbursement of money in the fund to pay for the projects as designated in the approved budget or approved modifications to the budget. The expenditure of money in the fund shall be administered by the county's financial officials, as are other county funds, provided that requests by the recreation board for expenditures from the fund consistent with the approved budget shall require no further action on the part of the board of commissioners, unless shortfalls in the budgeted revenues shall result in insufficient money in the fund to meet the requested expenditure.
(e)
The recreation board shall formulate guidelines, policies, and procedures concerning the display of public art and monuments; evaluate proposals concerning the placement of public art and monuments; and make recommendations to the board of commissioners concerning the placement of art and monuments in public spaces throughout the county.
(f)
The recreation board shall review and approve applications for park bench dedications.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-17; Ord. No. 21-08 , § 2, 12-21-2021)
Sec. 62-43. - Status of recreation director.
Nothing contained in this article shall be construed or interpreted to change, alter or modify the status of the recreation director as an employee of and responsible to the county board of commissioners.
(Code 1979, § 2-10.5-18)

ARTICLE III. - PUBLIC SWIMMING POOLS, SPAS AND RECREATIONAL WATER PARKS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 02-7, § 1, adopted July 16, 2002, amended art. III in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former art. III pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Code 1979, §§ 2-7.1-1—2-7.1-35.
Cross reference— Zoning, ch. 90.

Sec. 62-76. - Applicability.
This article shall apply to all public swimming pools. The term "public swimming pool" shall be defined to mean any structure, chamber or tank containing an artificial body of water used by the public for swimming, diving, wading, recreation or therapy, together with buildings, appurtenances and equipment used in connection with the body of water, regardless of whether a fee is charged for its use. The term includes municipal, school, hotel or motel pools and any pool to which access is granted in exchange for payment of a daily fee. The term shall also include pools and spas operated by or serving camps, churches, day care centers, group home facilities of twelve or more clients, institutions, parks, state agencies, apartment complexes, subdivisions, condominiums, country clubs, mobile home parks, recreational vehicle parks, associations, health clubs, special purpose pools, and recreational water park attractions.
(Ord. No. 02-7, § 1, 7-16-2002
Sec. 62-77. - Rules and regulations.
The operation and maintenance of public swimming pools shall be according to the provisions set forth in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, Section 31-45-4 through Section 31-45-11 and regulations of the Georgia Department of Human Resources Public Health, Chapter 290-5-57, "Swimming Pools, Spas and Recreation Water Parks", as amended from time to time, such rules and regulations being incorporated into and made a part of this Code, subject to the enforcement provisions in this article.
(Ord. No. 02-7, § 1, 7-16-2002)
Sec. 62-78. - Enforcement.
(a)
The county board of health or its authorized agent shall be authorized to enforce this article, and to enter upon and inspect the premises of any public swimming pool covered under this article.
(b)
Each and every public swimming pool subject to inspection under the provisions of this article shall be required to pay to the county board of health an annual inspection fee as determined by the board of health and approved by the board of commissioners, due within 30 days of receipt of notification from the county board of health. Failure to pay such fee shall be considered in violation of the provisions of this article.
(c)
The county board of health or its authorized agent shall be authorized to issue citations for violations of the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 02-7, § 1, 7-16-2002)
Sec. 62-79. - Violation; penalties.
Any party who shall violate any provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties as set forth in section 1-9 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 02-7, § 1, 7-6-2002)
