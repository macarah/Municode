ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 54-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Combination park means any plot or tract of land on which both a mobile home park and a travel trailer park are located or are intended to be located.
Mobile home means a detached, single-family dwelling unit with all of the following characteristics:
(1)
Designed for longterm occupancy, and containing sleeping accommodations, a flush toilet, a tub or shower bath and kitchen facilities, with plumbing and electrical connections;
(2)
Prefabricated and designed to be transported, fully assembled into one unit, on its own wheels, or on a flatbed or other trailer or detachable wheels;
(3)
Arriving at the site where such mobile home is to be occupied as a complete dwelling, including major appliances and furniture, and ready for occupancy, except for minor and incidental unpacking and assembly operations, location on foundation supports, and connection to utilities, etc.; and
(4)
At least eight feet but not greater than 12 feet in body width, exclusive of any acceptable incidental appurtenances such as windows, porches, etc.
Mobile home park means any plot or tract of land on which two or more mobile homes are located, or intended to be located, for purposes of residential occupancy.
Mobile home subdivision means any plot or tract of land subdivided into lots with such lots sold or marketed, or intended to be sold or marketed, for the placement of mobile homes.
Mobile office means a prefabricated structure designed to be transported on its own wheels, flatbed, trailer or detachable wheels, and used or occupied, or intended to be used or occupied, for the transaction of business or the rendering of a professional service.
Tiny house means a tiny house as defined in section 90-9 of this Code of Ordinances.
Tiny house community means any parcel or tract of land on which four or more tiny houses are located, or are intended to be located.
Travel trailer park means any plot or tract of land on which two or more travel trailers are located, or are intended to be located.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 54-2. - Purposes.
This chapter is intended to provide policies and technical standards for the use of mobile homes and travel trailers in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-3. - Administrative officer.
The county's director of development services shall administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter, except as may be otherwise provided in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Cross reference— Officers and employees, § 2-186 et seq.
Sec. 54-4. - Health, sanitation and safety generally.
(a)
All mobile homes and travel trailers shall provide for a healthful environment, with living and service facilities arranged and equipped to assure such a healthful condition.
(b)
All mobile homes and travel trailers shall conform to all applicable building codes as may be in effect, particularly the Uniform Standards Code for Mobile Homes Act, O.C.G.A. ch. 84-48, as amended.
(c)
Utility services such as public water, gas, electricity, sewer, etc., shall not be provided to any mobile home, tiny house, or travel trailer until the provisions of this chapter are met.
(d)
No mobile home, tiny house or travel trailer shall be used for overnight occupancy in any place, public or private, except in accordance with this chapter.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-5. - Building permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to commence the excavation for or construction of a mobile home, travel trailer or combination park, or place a mobile home, mobile office, tiny house, or travel trailer on an individual lot, until a building permit authorizing such action has been properly issued.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-6. - Permit application.
Application for a building permit under this article shall be filed with the building standards department. Applications shall be in writing, accompanied by three copies of the required plans, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name and address of the applicant;
(2)
Location and legal description of the proposed site;
(3)
Plot plan at a scale of one inch equals not more than 100 feet, indicating the size and location of spaces or areas for the placement of mobile homes, travel trailers and park facilities;
(4)
Utilities plan at a scale of one inch equals not more than 100 feet, indicating the size and location of all gas, water, sewer, electrical and other features;
(5)
Site features plan at a scale of one inch equals not more than 100 feet, indicating the location of mobile home park drives and walks, parking bays or areas, tenant storage facilities, surface water drainage pattern, drainage structures, areas of disturbed soil and existing and proposed planting and screening;
(6)
Floor plans and specifications of any service buildings or other structures;
(7)
A scaled layout of typical mobile home and trailer sites or integrated grouping of sites; and
(8)
Such further information as may be required by the director to determine if the proposed use will comply with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-7. - Fees.
Fees for building permits under this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk, and such fees shall be paid by the developer or property owner.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-8. - Certificate of occupancy.
No mobile home, tiny house, office, or travel trailer on an individual lot and no mobile home, travel trailer or combination park shall be occupied or used until the director shall have issued a certificate of occupancy therefor. Such certificate of occupancy shall be evidence that the regulations of this chapter have been met.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-9. - Temporary use authorization.
(a)
The temporary use allowing a mobile home or modular building used as a construction office, storage buildings, outdoor storage, and/or employee parking areas to be placed on an individual lot for nonresidential use shall be submitted in conjunction with a building permit application, land disturbance permit application, or site plan review. The location of the mobile home or modular building used as a construction office, storage buildings, outdoor storage, and/or employee parking areas shall be shown on a site plan submitted with the permit or review that has been requested. The applicant shall show it is necessary to use the above use for the preparation and completion of a construction project. Said request shall be reviewed in conjunction with the application it was submitted to the appropriate county departments. Adequate facilities shall be available for electrical, water, and/or sewerage or the appropriate permits will be obtained from the development services division with subsequent inspections as required. Upon approval, a temporary use permit shall be obtained from the development services division and posted in a conspicuous place at the approved location. The temporary use permit shall expire one year from the date of approval of the temporary use permit or upon completion of such project, whichever first occurs, and such authorization may be renewed once following noticing procedures outlined in subsection (c) of this section.
(b)
The planning director or his designee shall have the authority to approve a temporary use allowing a mobile home or modular building to be placed on an individual residential lot for nonresidential use when the applicant for such can show that the request is related to the marketing of a development under construction. The property owner or their designated representative may make an application for a temporary use on forms provided by the director. Upon receipt of the application, a public hearing shall be scheduled before the planning commission. Notice of the public hearing shall follow the requirements set forth in section 90-180(5). The public hearing shall follow procedures as outlined in section 90-180(6) of Columbia County Code. Such temporary offices shall be authorized for three years or until the final lot in the development is sold or leased, whichever happens first. Such authorizations may be renewed once at the discretion of the director or his designee.
(c)
The planning commission may authorize the planning director or his designee to approve a temporary use allowing a mobile home or travel trailer to be placed on an individual lot for residential purposes. The property owner or their designated representative may make an application for a temporary use on forms provided by the director. Upon receipt of the application, a public hearing shall be scheduled before the planning commission Notice of the public hearing shall follow the requirements set forth in section 90-180(5). The public hearing shall follow procedures as outlined in section 90-180(6) of Columbia County Code. The request shall be reviewed using the following criteria:
(1)
Special circumstances or existing conditions are such that the denial of such authorization would deprive the applicant of any reasonable or beneficial use of his land (mere loss in value will not justify approval), or that the denial of such authorization would cause an undue hardship and burden on the applicant to the extent that a denial would be injurious to the applicant's physical wellbeing;
(2)
The granting of such authorization or use shall not be injurious to the neighborhood or detrimental to the public welfare;
(3)
That adequate facilities are available for electrical, water and/or sewerage or the appropriate permits will be obtained from the development service division with subsequent inspections.
(d)
Any authorization granted pursuant to subsection (c) of this section shall expire one year from the date of approval by the planning commission or the planning director or designee and may be renewed only once by following the procedures governing the original authorization.
(e)
Any mobile home, modular building, or travel trailer placed on a site in accordance with the above regulations must meet the stated building setbacks for the lot on which it is placed.
(f)
Upon written approval of the temporary use authorization by the planning commission or the planning director or designee, the approved applicant shall obtain a temporary use permit from the development services division to be posted in a conspicuous place at the approved location of the temporary use. All subsequent permits and inspections related specifically to the temporary use shall be input under this initial application.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019; Ord. No. 23-02 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-20-2023)
Sec. 54-10. - Conditions required for permit.
Prior to the beginning of the development of a mobile home park, travel trailer park or combination park, the developer shall comply with the provisions of this chapter and chapter 90 of this Code, as well as other applicable provisions of this Code.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-11. - Existing individual mobile homes and travel trailers.
(a)
After October 15, 1972, any existing mobile home or office on an individual lot, that shall have been placed or erected and approved prior to such date in accordance with the "Trailer Coach Regulations of the Unincorporated Areas of Columbia County, Georgia," adopted February 10, 1970, may be continued and maintained although the location and use of such existing mobile home or office may not conform to the regulations of this chapter.
(b)
No existing mobile home, office or travel trailer on an individual lot, for either temporary or permanent occupancy, shall be relocated to another site; and no mobile home, office or travel trailer shall be placed on a site vacated by an existing mobile home, office or travel trailer unless such relocation or placement complies with the regulations of this chapter.
(c)
Existing mobile homes, offices and travel trailers not in compliance with the regulations of this chapter shall be subject to the nonconforming use provisions of chapter 90 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-12. - Existing parks.
(a)
Mobile home, travel trailer and combination parks existing after October 15, 1972, and developed, occupied and approved prior to such date in accordance with the "Trailer Coach Regulations of the Unincorporated Areas of Columbia County, Georgia," adopted February 10, 1970, may be continued and maintained, except that such parks shall not be enlarged, expanded or altered unless such enlargement, expansion or alteration is in full compliance with the regulations of this chapter.
(b)
Existing mobile home, travel trailer and combination parks not in compliance with the regulations of this chapter shall be subject to the nonconforming use provisions of chapter 90 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-13. - Appeals.
The planning commission is authorized to hear and decide appeals arising from the enforcement and administration of this chapter, and shall have those powers granted to the planning commission by the chapter 90 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-14. - Amendments.
The board of county commissioners may from time to time amend, supplement or repeal any or all provisions of this chapter. Any proposed amendment or supplement shall first be submitted to the planning commission for its recommendation. The planning commission may also initiate recommendations for amendments or supplements to this chapter.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-15. - Violation; penalties.
A violation of any provision of this chapter is declared to be a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9. Each day such violation continues shall be punishable as a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-16. - Effective date.
This chapter shall be in full force and effect from and after its adoption and effective date of October 15, 1972.
(Code 1979, § 2-9-17)
Secs. 54-17—54-50. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - MOBILE HOME PARKS

Sec. 54-51. - Where allowed.
Mobile home parks subject to the requirements of this article may be allowed pursuant to section 90-50 and 90-147.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-52. - Minimum size.
All mobile home parks shall contain a minimum of eight acres of land; and shall be located, dimensioned and related as to facilitate efficient park design and management.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-53. - Minimum number of spaces.
Before a certificate of occupancy will be granted, all mobile home parks shall contain at least 25 mobile home spaces fully completed and ready for occupancy.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-54. - Access to site.
Direct vehicular access to a mobile home park shall be provided by means of an abutting, paved public street.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-55. - Site conditions and improvements.
(a)
A mobile home park site shall be such that the condition of the soil, groundwater level, drainage and topography do not create hazards to the property or to the health and safety of the occupants of such mobile home park.
(b)
A mobile home park, including mobile home stands, patios, convenience structures, streets and other features shall be developed:
(1)
In harmony with the topography, shape of the site and existing site features;
(2)
To protect the park and adjoining properties from objectionable views; and
(3)
To provide for the practical and efficient operation and maintenance of water, sewerage and refuse collection facilities.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-56. - Internal streets.
All mobile home parks shall contain a street system designed to provide convenient circulation within the park. The following requirements shall apply to the development of a mobile home park's street system:
(1)
All park streets shall be provided with a smooth, hard and dense surface which shall be durable and well-drained under normal use and weather conditions. The surface shall be maintained in a state of good repair at all times.
(2)
All streets shall be well-drained and have a minimum pavement width of 22 feet.
(3)
Pavement surface material shall be as required by the county's street and road department and the county engineer. Written approval of the street system and surface material by the street and road department and the county engineer shall be required before a building permit will be issued.
(4)
Street grades shall not exceed ten percent slope, and curb radii shall not be less than 20 feet.
(5)
On-street parking shall not be permitted on any mobile home park street.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-57. - Water supply system.
Every mobile home park shall provide an accessible, adequate potable water supply. The following requirements shall apply to the park's water system:
(1)
The design and size of the water system shall be based on the maximum number of mobile home spaces to be contained in the mobile home park.
(2)
The location, design and construction of a mobile home park's water supply and distribution system requires the approval of the county engineer and the health officer. Written approval of the water system by the county engineer and the health officer shall be required before a building permit will be issued.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-58. - Sewage disposal system.
A safe and sanitary method of sewage collection and disposal shall be provided in all mobile home parks. The location, design and construction of a mobile home park's sewerage system requires the approval of the county engineer and the health officer. Written approval of the sewerage system by the county engineer and the health officer shall be required before a building permit will be issued. The design and size of the sewerage system shall be based on the maximum number of mobile home spaces to be contained in the mobile home park.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Cross reference— Water and sewers, ch. 86.
Sec. 54-59. - Refuse collection system.
Every mobile home park shall be provided with a sanitary method of solid waste collection and disposal. The following requirements shall apply to a mobile home park's refuse collection system:
(1)
Every mobile home stand shall be provided with at least two watertight, covered refuse containers which shall have a capacity of 30 gallons each.
(2)
Refuse shall be collected at least twice weekly. Where twice weekly collection is not feasible, screened, centrally-located bulk storage containers shall be provided within the mobile home park.
(3)
Refuse collection service shall be provided by a qualified public or private agency, where available. In such instances the mobile home park operator shall not be directly responsible for the method of disposal. In mobile home parks where the owner or operator provides the refuse collection service, refuse shall be conveyed to the nearest sanitary landfill or buried in a manner and location approved by the county health officer.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-60. - Recreational area.
Every mobile home park shall contain an outdoor recreational area appropriately developed for both active and passive outdoor recreation. The following minimum requirements shall apply to a mobile home park's recreational area:
(1)
At least eight percent of the gross land area of a mobile home park shall be reserved, developed and maintained for recreational purposes, except that the total amount of land used for recreational purposes shall contain not less than 22,000 square feet.
(2)
No area in a mobile home park which is devoted to automobile parking, setback, service buildings or other such functions shall be counted as part of the required recreational area, except that swimming pools, recreational buildings and child play areas may be included as part of the required recreational area.
(3)
Recreational areas shall be located so as to be easily accessible by all mobile home park tenants, and the total acreage of such recreational areas may be comprised of two or more separate areas, provided that no single area shall contain less than 10,000 square feet.
(4)
For safety reasons, recreational areas shall be located where they are free of traffic hazards.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-61. - Service and convenience buildings.
Service buildings such as restrooms, management offices and storage facilities, and convenience buildings of a commercial nature, including coin-operated laundry, beauty shops and stores, may be permitted in mobile home parks subject to the following restrictions:
(1)
Such buildings and their parking areas shall not occupy more than ten percent of the gross land area of the mobile home park.
(2)
All service and convenience buildings shall be subordinate to the residential use and character of the mobile home park, and shall be located, designed and intended to serve only the trade or service needs of the mobile home park's residents.
(3)
No service or convenience building shall present visible evidence of its commercial character from any residential district outside of the mobile home park.
(4)
The planning commission shall have the authority to review and approve or disapprove the use and type of such service and convenience buildings that may be permitted within a mobile home park.
(5)
All mobile home park service and convenience buildings shall be required to conform to any applicable county building codes.
(6)
All mobile home park service and convenience buildings shall conform to any lot size, parking, setback and buffer requirements which may be imposed by the planning commission.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-62. - Lot size and improvements.
Every mobile home space within a mobile home park shall be of sufficient size to accommodate the mobile home and its accessory structures which will be placed on such mobile home space. The following minimum requirements shall apply to the development of each mobile home space:
(1)
Every mobile home space shall contain at least 4,500 square feet of area, with a minimum frontage on an interior park street of 40 feet.
(2)
Every mobile home space shall be adequately marked by permanent lot markers.
(3)
Every mobile home space shall contain a permanent mobile home stand which shall be constructed of at least four inches of concrete on a suitable subgrade material to support the mobile home. A solid pad measuring at least ten feet wide by 60 feet long, or tandem strips at least two feet wide by 60 feet long positioned parallel and eight feet on-center are considered acceptable forms of mobile home stands.
(4)
Every mobile home space shall be provided with a permanent concrete patio which is four inches thick, measuring at least eight feet by 12 feet, and which is located convenient to the entrance of the mobile home.
(5)
An accessway not less than 12 feet wide shall be provided from the fronting mobile home park street to each mobile home stand. Paving of the accessway is not required.
(6)
Every mobile home space shall be provided with at least two off-street parking spaces. Parking spaces may be either located on each mobile home space or in integrated groupings (bays, clusters, etc.), provided that no parking space shall be further than 50 feet from the mobile home space it serves.
(7)
Every mobile home space shall be provided with its own connection to the mobile home park's water, sewer and electrical systems.
(8)
Every mobile home space shall have two covered, waterproof 30-gallon refuse containers.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-63. - Placement on lot.
The following minimum requirements shall apply to the placement of a mobile home on a lot (space) within a mobile home park:
(1)
No mobile home shall be located closer than 30 feet from the centerline of any interior park street.
(2)
No mobile home shall be located closer than 25 feet to another mobile home.
(3)
No mobile home shall be located closer than 35 feet from the right-of-way line of any abutting public street, except that such minimum distance shall be increased to 50 feet for abutting major thoroughfares.
(4)
No mobile home shall be located closer than 20 feet from any mobile home park property line.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-64. - Supervision and maintenance.
The person to whom a building permit is issued for a mobile home park shall be responsible for providing adequate supervision of the mobile home park in order to maintain it in full compliance with this article, and to keep the mobile home park's facilities and equipment in good repair and in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-65. - Tenant register.
The manager of a mobile home park shall keep an up-to-date register of the mobile home park's residents by lot or street address, and maintain such register of departed occupants for a period of three years following their departure. Such register shall be made available to any authorized person inspecting the mobile home park, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The names of all occupants of the mobile home quartered on each designated space and the ages of minors;
(2)
The make, model and year of the occupied mobile home, and the serial number, state and license number or other designation or description of such mobile home;
(3)
The date of registration and departure of the mobile home and its occupants.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Secs. 54-66—54-95. - Reserved.

Sec. 54-96. - Where individual lot mobile homes and tiny houses allowed.
Mobile homes and tiny houses on separate, individual lots, subject to the requirements of this article, may be allowed pursuant to section 90-50 and 90-147.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-97. - Separate lot for each mobile home or tiny house.
Only one mobile home or tiny house shall be located on each individual lot, parcel or tract of land, except in an approved mobile home park or tiny house community. No mobile home or tiny house shall be located permanently on any plot, parcel or tract of land occupied by any other permanent dwelling or occupied building. (See section 54-9.)
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-98. - Compliance of individual lot mobile homes and tiny houses with district zoning.
Mobile homes and tiny houses on individual lots shall fully comply with the zoning district's requirements for lot size, setbacks and other restrictions for permanent single-family residential dwellings as well as the requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-99. - Minimum individual lot mobile home and tiny house site improvements.
Mobile homes and tiny houses on separate, individual lots shall be provided with the following minimum improvements:
(1)
The chassis of each mobile home or tiny house, shall be supported on an adequate masonry foundation, and the perimeter shall be fully and suitably enclosed with an acceptable skirting material. An adequate masonry foundation shall be as required by the director or other official having jurisdiction over such matters. Acceptable skirting material shall be of only those types and materials designed by the manufacturer expressly for such use on mobile homes or tiny houses.
(2)
A solid four-inch thick concrete pad at least ten feet wide by 60 feet long, or tandem strips at least two feet wide by 60 feet long positioned parallel and eight feet on-center. A tiny house shall use a solid four-inch thick concrete pad or tandem strips that provides the necessary coverage for the individual tiny house. Where either the solid pad or tandem strips are used, the base of the mobile home or tiny house shall be fully skirted to screen the supports or wheels from view. Acceptable skirting shall be of only those types and materials designed by the manufacturer expressly for such use on mobile homes or tiny houses.
(3)
Every mobile home or tiny house shall be served by an acceptable water supply and method of sewage disposal subject to the written approval of the county health officer having jurisdiction over such matters.
(4)
Every mobile home or tiny house shall have an individual connection to public electrical power.
(5)
Except for tiny houses built on trailers or permanent chassis, every mobile home or tiny house lot shall have a permanent patio, paved with a permanent, solid or mortar-bound material, and conveniently located to the entrance of the mobile home. Such patio shall measure not less than eight feet by 12 feet. A tiny house lot may use a smaller patio upon approval of the director of development services.
(6)
Every mobile home or tiny house lot shall be provided with two 30-gallon covered refuse containers. Refuse shall be stored only in such containers and collected and disposed of regularly in an approved disposal facility.
(7)
Every mobile home or tiny house lot shall contain at least two off-street parking spaces.
(8)
Any accessory structures or additions to the mobile home or tiny house or lot shall be of a similar design and material as the mobile home unit, and shall not be used as complete, independent housing units with permanent provisions for sleeping, cooking and sanitation.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-100. - Where mobile offices allowed.
Mobile homes equipped, designed and/or used on separate, individual lots as permanent offices or other commercial or industrial purposes may be allowed in zoning districts C-2, C-3, M-1 and M-2, subject to the requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-101. - Compliance of mobile offices with district zoning.
Mobile offices or sales areas shall fully comply with the zoning district's requirements for lot size, setbacks and other restrictions applicable to permanent commercial or industrial structures, as well as the provisions of this article. In all respects, mobile offices, except as provided for in section 54-9, shall be considered permanent structures, and as such are subject to any applicable building codes.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-102. - Minimum mobile office site improvements.
Mobile offices, other than those authorized for temporary use as provided in section 54-9, shall comply with the following minimum improvements:
(1)
The chassis of each mobile office shall be supported by and firmly anchored to a permanent concrete block or brick foundation, which is fully closed and vented.
(2)
Every mobile office shall be served by an acceptable water supply and method of sewage disposal subject to the written approval of the county health officer and other officers having jurisdiction over such matters.
(3)
Every mobile office shall have an individual connection to public electrical power.
(4)
Every mobile office lot shall contain parking spaces as required by chapter 90 for similar or like uses.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-103. - Minimum requirements for mobile home subdivision design.
Mobile home subdivisions shall be developed in full compliance with the applicable regulations regarding the subdivision of land in the county as set forth in chapter 74 of this Code. Individual lots and the placement of mobile homes on such lots shall fully comply with the zoning district requirements for lot size, setbacks and other restrictions for permanent single-family residential dwellings as set forth in chapter 90 of this Code, as well as the requirements of sections 54-97 and 54-99. No mobile home subdivision shall be approved unless it contains at least 25 lots platted for development, and which shall be recorded in the clerk of the superior court's office.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-104. - Private covenant on mobile home subdivision plat and deeds.
On the approved plat for a mobile home subdivision and in the deed executed upon the sale of each lot, the developer shall stipulate the types of residential structures permitted in the subdivision, i.e., mobile homes and similar types of manufactured housing as may be approved by the planning commission.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Secs. 54-105—54-135. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - TRAVEL TRAILER PARKS

Sec. 54-136. - Where allowed.
Travel trailer parks, subject to the requirements of this article, may be allowed pursuant to section 90-50, section 90-97, and section 90-147.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-137. - Minimum size.
All travel trailer parks shall contain a minimum of one acre of land, and shall be located, dimensioned and related so as to facilitate efficient park design, use and management. Travel trailer parks may be located jointly on a lot with another commercial use.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-138. - Minimum number of spaces.
Before a certificate of occupancy will be granted, all travel trailer parks shall contain at least ten travel trailer spaces, fully completed and ready for occupancy, before a certificate of occupancy will be granted.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-139. - Access to site.
Direct vehicular access to a travel trailer park site shall be provided from an abutting, paved public street. However, the entrance drive may be shared with another use to which the travel trailer park is accessory or secondary.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-140. - Site conditions and improvements.
(a)
A travel trailer park site shall be such that the condition of the soil, groundwater level, drainage and topography do not create hazards to the property or to the health and safety of the occupants of the travel trailer park.
(b)
The travel trailer park and its features shall be designed and developed in harmony with the topography, shape of the site and existing site features, to protect the travel trailer park and adjoining properties from objectionable views, and to provide for the practical and efficient operation and maintenance of the travel trailer park and its facilities.
(c)
Any travel trailer park developed jointly with another commercial use shall be designed, located and developed so as to assure the adequate separation of the travel trailer park functions from those of the other commercial enterprise.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-141. - Internal streets.
All travel trailer parks shall contain a street system, properly adapted to the site's topography, designed to provide convenient vehicular circulation within the travel trailer park and access to each travel trailer space. The following requirements shall apply to the development of a travel trailer park's street system:
(1)
All travel trailer park streets shall be provided with a smooth, hard and dense surface, which shall be durable and well-drained under normal use and weather conditions. Street surfaces and drainage facilities shall, at all times, be maintained in a state of good repair.
(2)
Travel trailer park streets shall have a surface width of 15 feet for one-way streets, and 22 feet for two-way streets. Where on-street parking is to be permitted on either one-way or two-way streets, the surfaced width shall be increased by eight feet to allow for parking on one side of the street only, and by 16 feet for parking on both sides of the street.
(3)
Street surface material shall be compacted gravel unless otherwise required by the county's street and road department. Written approval of the street system and surface material by the road department shall be required before a permit will be issued.
(4)
Street grades shall not exceed ten percent, and curb radii shall not be less than 20 feet.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-142. - Water supply system.
Every travel trailer park shall provide an accessible, adequate and potable water supply. The following requirements shall apply to a travel trailer park's water system:
(1)
The water system shall be such that it is capable of supplying at least 100 gallons of water, per day, to each travel trailer space.
(2)
The location, design and construction of a travel trailer park's water supply and distribution system requires the written approval of the county health officer before any permit will be issued.
(3)
The design and size of the water system shall be based on the maximum number of travel trailer spaces to be contained in the travel trailer park.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-143. - Sewage disposal system.
A safe and sanitary method of sewage collection and disposal shall be provided in all travel trailer parks. The location, design and construction of a travel trailer park's sewerage requires the written approval of the county health officer before any permit will be issued. Sewerage facilities shall be provided by one of the following methods:
(1)
A park-wide system may be employed and designed so that each travel trailer park space is provided with an individual connection to the sewerage system. In such cases, the system shall be of sufficient size to accommodate a sewage flow of at least 100 gallons, per trailer space, per day; or
(2)
Sanitary stations at the rate of one station per ten travel trailer spaces to be contained in the park may be used in lieu of individual sewerage connection. Where sanitary stations are used, they shall be centrally located among the travel trailer spaces they are to serve, and shall have an individual capacity to accommodate a sewage flow of 750 gallons, per day. Sanitary stations shall be well-screened, equipped with a self-closing hatch and connected to an approved sewage disposal system. Sanitary stations shall not be located closer than 50 feet to any travel trailer space.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-144. - Refuse collection system.
Every travel trailer park shall contain adequate facilities for the collection and disposal of refuse and other such solid waste. The following requirements shall apply to a travel trailer park's refuse collection system:
(1)
All refuse shall be stored in watertight, rodentproof containers, which shall be located not more than 150 feet from any travel trailer space. Containers shall be provided in sufficient number and capacity to properly store all refuse.
(2)
Refuse collection stands shall be provided for all refuse containers. Such container stands shall be designed so as to prevent containers from being tipped, to minimize spillage and container deterioration.
(3)
All refuse containing garbage shall be collected at least twice weekly. Where suitable collection service is not available from public or private agencies, the owner or operator of the travel trailer park shall provide such service.
(4)
Where public or private disposal service is not available, the owner or operator of the travel trailer park shall dispose of the refuse by transporting such refuse to a disposal site approved by the county health officer.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-145. - Recreational area.
Every travel trailer park shall contain a recreational area appropriately developed for both active and passive recreation. The following requirements shall apply to a travel trailer park's recreational area:
(1)
At least eight percent of the gross land area of a travel trailer park, or 8,000 square feet, whichever is larger, shall be reserved, developed and maintained for recreational purposes.
(2)
No areas of a travel trailer park which are used for automobile parking, setback, service buildings, travel trailer spaces or other such function shall be counted as part of the required recreational area, except that recreational buildings may be included as part of the required acreage.
(3)
Recreational areas shall be located so as to be easily accessible for all travel trailer park tenants, but shall not be located in areas where traffic hazards may exist.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-146. - Restroom and bathing facilities.
(a)
Every travel trailer park shall provide on-site toilet and bathing facilities for the exclusive use of the tenants of the travel trailer park.
(b)
Such toilet and bathing facilities shall consist of at least one lavatory, one water closet and one shower stall, one of each provided and distinctly marked for each sex.
(c)
Toilet and bathing facilities set forth in subsection (b) of this section shall be provided at the rate of one lavatory, one water closet and one shower stall for each sex for each ten travel trailer spaces available for occupancy in the travel trailer park.
(d)
Toilet and bathing facilities shall be connected to the travel trailer park's water and sewerage systems.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-147. - Other service buildings.
Service buildings such as management offices, storage facilities, coin-operated laundries and indoor recreational buildings may be permitted in travel trailer parks subject to the following restrictions:
(1)
Such buildings and their parking areas shall not occupy more than five percent of the gross land area of the travel trailer park.
(2)
All service buildings shall be subordinate to the character and function of the travel trailer park, and shall be located, designed and intended to serve the exclusive service needs of the travel trailer park's residents.
(3)
The planning commission shall have the authority to review and approve or disapprove the use and type of such service buildings that may be permitted within a travel trailer park.
(4)
All service buildings shall conform to any lot size, parking, setback and buffer requirements as may be imposed by the planning commission.
(5)
All service buildings shall conform to any applicable building codes of the county.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-148. - Lot size and improvements.
Every travel trailer space within a travel trailer park shall be of sufficient size to accommodate the travel trailer to be placed thereon. The following minimum requirements shall apply to the development of each travel trailer space:
(1)
Every travel trailer space shall measure at least 20 feet wide by 50 feet long.
(2)
Every travel trailer space shall be provided with a parking stand or space for the travel trailer, and shall be composed of not less than six inches of compacted gravel or other acceptable surface material.
(3)
Every travel trailer space shall have an accessway constructed of compacted gravel or other acceptable material connecting an interior travel trailer park street with the mobile home stand. Such accessway shall be at least ten feet wide and 20 feet long. Vehicular parking may be permitted in the accessway.
(4)
Every travel trailer space shall be provided with a weatherproof electrical connection supplying 110 volts.
(5)
Every travel trailer space shall be provided with an individual connection (or service hydrant) to the travel trailer park's water system; and, where centrally-located sanitary stations as provided in section 54-143(2) are not furnished, the travel trailer space shall be provided with an individual sewerage connection as provided in section 54-143(1).
(6)
Every travel trailer space shall be provided with ample room to assure that a typical travel trailer measuring eight feet wide by 30 feet long can be placed on the space and be located not closer than 20 feet to the centerline of any interior travel trailer park street; and closer than 35 feet to any travel trailer park service building, park property line or a public street right-of-way; and closer than 20 feet to any other travel trailer.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-149. - Supervision and maintenance.
The person to whom a permit for a travel trailer park is issued shall be responsible for providing adequate supervision of the travel trailer park to maintain the travel trailer park in full compliance with this article, and to keep the travel trailer park's facilities and equipment in good repair, and in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-150. - Tenant register.
The manager of a travel trailer park shall keep an up-to-date register of the travel trailer park's occupants, and shall maintain such register of departed occupants for a period of three years following their departure. The travel trailer park's register shall be made available to any authorized person inspecting the travel trailer park, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The names of all occupants quartered on each designated travel trailer space;
(2)
The description, make, model and year of all travel trailers and towing vehicles, and the state and license number of each; and
(3)
The dates of arrival and departure of occupants of each travel trailer coach space.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Secs. 54-151—54-180. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - COMBINATION PARKS

Sec. 54-181. - Use objectives.
Travel trailer parks, when combined with or developed jointly with a mobile home park or subdivision, or such are contiguous properties, shall be considered to be a mobile home park or subdivision and must comply with the governing requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-182. - Development requirements.
(a)
Combination mobile home and travel trailer parks shall be developed as though both the mobile home park portion and the travel trailer park portion were separate undertakings.
(b)
Both the mobile home park portion and travel trailer park portion shall fully comply with the regulations applicable to their particular development as provided in articles III and IV of this chapter, except that a travel trailer park developed in combination with a mobile home park shall not be located jointly with or share a common access to any public street with any use other than the mobile home park. Although both parks may share common facilities such as a park water and sewerage system, recreational areas and street system, the combined facilities shall not be less than required by each park if developed separately.
(c)
Incidental service or convenience buildings as may be provided for in articles III and IV of this chapter shall be subject to the approval or disapproval of the planning commission, and if approved, shall fully comply with the applicable provisions of this chapter regarding such buildings.
(d)
In all respects, travel trailer parks developed as part of a combination park shall be subordinate to the mobile home park portion; and in no case shall the ratio of travel trailer spaces to mobile home spaces exceed 2:5.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
ARTICLE VI. - TINY HOUSE COMMUNITIES

Sec. 54-183. - Where allowed.
Tiny house communities may be located pursuant to the requirements of section 90-97 and section 90-147.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-184. - Lot requirements and setbacks
•
The minimum lot area per dwelling unit shall be 1,200 square feet.
•
The minimum lot width shall be 18 feet.
•
The minimum lot depth shall be 50 feet.
•
The minimum front setback shall be 20 feet.
•
The minimum rear setback shall be five feet.
•
The sum of the side setbacks shall be no less than ten feet.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-185. - Centralized common area.
•
All lots shall be organized around a central common open space of no less than 400 square feet per unit. The open space area shall include usable public spaces such as lawn, gardens, patios, or plazas. Common tables, chairs, and benches are encouraged.
•
All units must be within five feet of each common open space. Setbacks cannot be counted toward the open space calculation.
•
The principal common open space must be located centrally to the project. Additional common open space can only account for 25 percent of the total requirement.
•
Community buildings or clubhouses can be counted toward the common open space calculation.
•
Common open space shall be located outside of stormwater/detention ponds, wetlands, streams, and lakes, and cannot be located on slopes greater than ten percent.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)

Sec. 54-216. - Mobile home defined.
As used in this article, the term "mobile home" means as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 84-4802(a) and (b) (Uniform Standards Code for Mobile Homes Act, O.C.G.A. ch. 84-48); provided, however, those mobile homes which qualify the taxpayer for homestead exemption under state law shall not be considered to be mobile homes and shall not be subject to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)

Sec. 54-217. - Permit required for location or relocation within the county.
If a mobile home is to be used as a residence by the owner of such mobile home or by others, or for any other purpose, before such person shall locate or relocate such mobile home within the limits of the county, he shall apply for and obtain from the county tax commissioner a permit authorizing such location or relocation.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-218. - Permit required for resident to relocate within the county or elsewhere.
A mobile home which is located within the limits of the county and which is used as the owner's residence, may not be relocated within or beyond the confines of the county without first obtaining a permit from the county tax commissioner authorizing such relocation.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-219. - Payment of tax required prior to issuance of permit.
It shall be a condition precedent to issuance of any permit required in this article that the owner of such mobile home shall submit to the tax commissioner proof that all state and county taxes accruing and payable with respect to such mobile home have, in fact, been paid; and if such mobile home which is to be located or relocated is then located within any portion of the county that lies within the limits of a municipality in the county, such owner shall likewise submit proof that all applicable municipal taxes have been paid before a permit may be issued.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-220. - Decal generally.
Any owner of a mobile home, who has made application for the location or relocation of such mobile home, and whose application meets the qualifications of this article, shall be issued a permit and a decal. The decal to be issued with a permit authorizing location of a mobile home within the county shall be the same color as prescribed by state law, and shall be affixed to the mobile home at all times that the mobile home is being used as a residence within the confines of the county. The decal issued with the permit authorizing relocation of a mobile home shall be red in color, and shall be affixed to the mobile home at all times such mobile home is being transported within the confines of the county. Such decal shall be designed in such manner and affixed to the mobile home in such a manner as to cause the decal to be easily visible for inspection.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-221. - Fee.
Every application for a permit required by this article shall be accompanied by a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk, which fee shall be paid to the tax commissioner before such permit shall be issued. All sums collected by the tax commissioner under the provisions of this section shall be paid into the county treasury.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-222. - Annual application; display of decal.
Every owner of a mobile home subject to taxation under the provisions of this article shall, on or before April 1 each year, make application to the tax commissioner for a mobile home location permit, and upon payment of the fee set forth in section 54-221, and upon proof of payment of taxes, shall be issued a location permit. The issuance of such permit by the tax commissioner shall be evidenced by the issuance of a green decal, which shall reflect the county of issuance and the calendar year for which such permit is issued. The decal shall be prominently attached and displayed on the mobile home by the owner.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-223. - Compliance required.
(a)
No person shall own or have any mobile home located within the limits of the county unless application for a permit shall have been made, and a permit received as provided by this article.
(b)
No person shall initially locate any mobile home or relocate any mobile home within the limits of the county without having made an application for a permit and having obtained such permit as provided by this article.
(c)
No person shall occupy any mobile home as a residence, nor permit any other person to occupy a mobile home as a residence unless a permit shall have been obtained as provided by this article.
(d)
No owner or operator of a mobile home park shall permit any mobile home to be located or relocated within the confines of such mobile home park for residential use unless the permit required by this article shall have been obtained. No owner or operator of any mobile home park shall permit any mobile home presently located within the limits of such mobile home park owned or operated by him to remain in such mobile home park unless a permit authorizing such continued location as required by this article shall have been obtained.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-224. - Notice of relocation to another county.
Whenever a permit under this article authorizing the relocation of a mobile home is issued and such mobile home is to be relocated within the confines of another county in this state, the tax commissioner of this county shall notify the tax commissioner of such other county of the date of the issuance of such permit.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-225. - Forms; information.
The tax commissioner is authorized and empowered, subject to the specific provisions of this article, to prescribe application, permit and decal forms, and to alter and change such forms, and he is empowered to require that applicants furnish such information in writing as may be reasonably required to effectuate the purposes of this article.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-226. - Annual notification by dealers.
All mobile home dealers shall furnish to the tax commissioner a list of all mobile homes in their possession, whether or not owned by them, on January 1 each year.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-227. - Zoning compliance.
The issuance of any permit under the provisions of this article shall not be construed as superseding the requirements for the location of mobile homes as location requirements may be contained within appropriate resolutions or other actions of the county planning commission. The issuance of a permit under the provisions of this article shall not prohibit the planning commission from requiring a relocation of the mobile home to a place where such mobile home is permitted by the regulations of the county planning commission; but in such case, the owner of such mobile home shall apply for and obtain a permit for such relocation as if such relocation was the result of his own initiative.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
Sec. 54-228. - Violation; penalty.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this article, or fails to comply with the requirements of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9. Each day such violation continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(b)
The owner or tenant of any building, structure, premises or part thereof, and any architect, builder, contractor, agent or other person who commits, participates in, assists in or maintains such violation may each be found guilty of a separate offense and suffer the penalties provided in subsection (a) of this section.
(Ord. No. 19-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-6-2019)
