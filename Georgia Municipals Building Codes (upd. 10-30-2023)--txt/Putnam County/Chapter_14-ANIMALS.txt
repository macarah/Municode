ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abuse or cruelty to animals or fowl means knowing and willful failure by an owner or any other person charged with an animal's or fowl's care to care for that animal or fowl in a humane manner; or, by act, omission or neglect, causing or inflicting unjustified physical suffering, pain, harm, or death to an animal or fowl.
Animal means any and all types of animals, domesticated, male and female, singular and plural.
Animal control officer means an employee of Putnam County under the direct supervision of the animal services director authorized by local law or by the governing authority of Putnam County to carry out the duties imposed by O.C.G.A. Title 4, Chapter 11 or imposed by local ordinance.
Animal establishment means any pet shop, grooming shop, animal auction, kennel, performing animal exhibition, or animal shelter, except this term shall not include veterinary medical facilities, licensed research facilities, facilities operated by government agencies, or licensed animal dealers regulated by the United States Department of Agriculture.
Animal services director means an individual selected by the governing authority of Putnam County to aid in the administration and enforcement of duties imposed by O.C.G.A. Title 4 or imposed by local ordinance.
Animal shelter means a facility designated or recognized by the board of commissioners for the purpose of impounding and/or caring for animals.
At large means off the premises, and not under the immediate control, of the owner or custodian of the animal.
Board of commissioners and board mean the Putnam County Board of Commissioners.
Confined means in an enclosure or secured by a leash which shall have a length not less than five times the length of the animal and which is free from obstructions. All shall be provided with protection from the elements and have food and water provided daily.
Dangerous animal means any animal which constitutes a physical threat to human beings or other domestic animals by virtue of a known propensity to endanger life by unprovoked assault or by biting so as to cause serious bodily harm or an animal trained, owned or harbored for the purpose, primarily or in part, of animal fighting. An animal shall not be deemed dangerous if:
1.
It bites, attacks or menaces:
a.
Anyone assaulting the owner
b.
A trespasser on the property of the owner
c.
Any person or other animal who has tormented or abused it.
2.
It is otherwise acting in defense of an attack from a person or other animal upon the owner or other person. (O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20)
Domestic animal means any dog, cat, or other legally owned animal not defined as wildlife, livestock or nontraditional livestock.
Domesticated fowl means any and all birds, male and female, singular and plural, kept as pets or kept, maintained, bred or harvested for agricultural purposes.
Enclosure means in a building, pen or fenced area or other structure to prevent intrusion or escape by an animal.
Feral animal means a domestic animal displaying aggressive and/or wild like behavior; not tame.
Fowl means any and all birds, domesticated and wild, male and female, singular and plural.
Guard dog or attack dog means any dog which has been trained to attack persons or other animals independently or upon verbal approval command and any dog which, while not so trained, is reasonably expected by the owner to perform as a guardian of the property upon and within which it is located.
Humane manner means care of an animal to include adequate protection from heat or cold, ventilation, sanitary shelter, wholesome food and clean water, consistent with the normal requirements and feeding habits of the animal's size, species and breed.
Kennel means an establishment kept for the purpose of breeding, selling, or boarding more than three dogs or three cats (except litters of animals of not more than six months of age) for commercial purposes or engaging in training of dogs or cats for commercial purposes.
Licensing authority means the county agency or designated representative thereof charged with administering the issuance and/or revocation of permits and licenses under the provisions of this chapter.
Livestock means cattle, swine, equines, poultry, sheep, goats, ratites, nontraditional livestock, and ruminants.
Neutered/spayed means rendered permanently incapable of reproduction.
Nuisance means a dog or cat which:
(1)
Damages, soils, or defecates on private property other than the owner's or on public walks and recreation areas unless such waste is immediately removed and properly disposed of by the owner or caregiver;
(2)
Causes a disturbance or annoyance to others by excessive barking or other noise making or offensive odors;
(3)
Chases vehicles or molests, attacks, or interferes with persons, domestic animals or domesticated fowl.
Owner means any person who owns, keeps, harbors, possesses or otherwise keeps any domestic, nontraditional livestock, or livestock animal for a period of seven days or any person who knowingly permits any domestic, nontraditional livestock, or livestock animal to be kept, harbored, possessed, or otherwise kept upon his premises for a period of seven days, or who has any animal under his or her care or responsibility, either temporarily or permanently, at any time, within the county.
Potentially dangerous animal means any animal which without provocation bites a human being, domesticated animal or domesticated fowl at any time after March 31, 1989. No animal shall be deemed to be a "dangerous animal" or "potentially dangerous animal" based solely on incidents in which the animal:
(1)
Inflicts an injury upon a person when the animal is being used by a law enforcement officer in connection with the law enforcement officer's official duties; and
(2)
Inflicts an injury if the injury was sustained by a person who, at the time, was attacking the animal's owner or owner's family member, committing a willful trespass or other tort, or was tormenting, abusing, or assaulting the animal or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused, or assaulted the animal or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous animal or potentially dangerous animal confined in a manner suitable to prevent the entry of young children and to prevent the animal from escaping. A proper enclosure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and a secure bottom. If the animal is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the animal's escape either from over or from under the fence. All gates shall be constructed so that they are self-closing and self-latching. All gates shall be locked or securely fastened at all times. A proper enclosure also shall provide protection from the elements for the animal.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal services agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state, or local court; or records of an animal control officer as provided in this chapter or by the laws of the state.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones, death, cosmetic surgery, or lacerations requiring medical treatment.
Stray animal means any animal not wearing a collar with a tag and/or license.
Under control means any animal that is controlled by a leash when off the property of the owner, or is within the passenger area of a vehicle driven or parked on the streets, or within the property limits of its owner and is confined or attended by its owner, or confined within the property limits of another with the permission of the person in control of the property.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-2. - Duties and prohibitions relating to the control of animals.
(a)
It is unlawful for any owner not to have his domestic animal under control at all times while in the county.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bring an animal into the confines of any park located in the county that is not on a leash or under the direct control of the person.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit a domestic animal under his ownership or possession to do the following:
(1)
Go upon the property other than [that of] its owner or keeper without the express consent of such owner or occupant.
(2)
Attack or act so as to menace pedestrians or other persons using public ways.
(3)
Disturb the peace of any person by loud, persistent, habitual, barking, howling, growling, yelping, whining or other noise making. Note: No one shall be charged with violating this subsection unless written warning has been previously issued by an animal control officer within 30 days of the violation. No citation for violation of this section shall be issued unless there are at least two complaining witnesses who shall have signed a written statement stating the animal owner's address, description of the animal, and the date(s), time(s) and location(s) of the offense.
(4)
Defecate or urinate upon the property of another.
(5)
Be in heat and not be confined in a secure enclosure on the property of the owner or keeper.
(6)
Animals under three months of age must be confined on the owner's property to prevent the spread of communicable diseases.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-3. - Penalties for violations.
Any person who does anything prohibited by this chapter as it exists or as it may hereafter be amended, or who fails to do anything required by this chapter as it exists or as it may hereafter be amended, upon conviction of a violation shall be subject to punishment as provided by law. Any violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punishable as a misdemeanor. Prosecution of violations of any provision of this chapter shall be pursuant to and in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-4. - Animal services director; animal services board.
(a)
Primary responsibility for enforcement of this chapter shall be vested in the animal services director.
(b)
The board of commissioners shall be authorized to create an animal services board which shall operate under the following bylaws:
(1)
Created. An animal services advisory board is hereby created and established in Putnam County. This animal services advisory board shall serve in an advisory capacity to the board of county commissioners on matters relating to the welfare and control of the domesticated animal population within the county.
(2)
Purpose. The purpose of the animal services advisory board is to provide information, advice and counsel to the board of county commissioners, county manager and the director of the animal services department with respect to the appropriate means and methods by which the county may adequately provide for the welfare of its domesticated animal population and for the protection of its citizens in relation to uncontrolled and/or dangerous dogs. The stated purpose of the advisory board shall not be construed as delegating to such animal services advisory board or any of its members any power or authority of a governmental, legislative or administrative nature, but merely is the establishment of an official instrument by which the board of county commissioners may receive the benefit of accurate, comprehensive information, advice and counsel concerning all matters relating to animal service programs which may be necessary for the appropriate action by the board of county commissioners.
(3)
Duties and functions. The animal services advisory board shall have the duties, functions and responsibilities to:
a.
Serve in an advisory capacity to the board of county commissioners, county manager and the director of the animal services department with respect to all matters pertaining to animal welfare and control policies and programs conducted within the county;
b.
Carry out continuing study of animal services facilities and services in the county and report to the board of county commissioners recommended improvements in such services as needed by the county in respect to animal welfare and control;
c.
Devise means and methods by which animal services facilities and services may be improved and more fully utilized to provide better service and availability to the general public;
d.
Make a continuing study and periodic reports and recommendations for sound, feasible programs for financing the cost of improving animal services facilities and services;
e.
Review all laws, ordinances, resolutions and regulations governing animal services programs and services and make recommendations concerning appropriate action that may be taken to improve same;
f.
Participate in hearings and in the disposition of dangerous and potentially dangerous dogs as required by O.C.G.A. title 4, chapter 8, article 2;
g.
Formulate plans and programs for the coordination of the activities of all the governmental entities and nongovernmental agencies, as well as corporations for profit or not for profit and individuals, relating to animal welfare and control programs, facilities and services within the county;
h.
Formulate comprehensive, feasible plans or programs for providing an adequate animal welfare and control service to the unincorporated areas of the county and to those municipalities who have entered into any local agreements with the county to provide animal control services; and
i.
Perform and carry out such other duties and functions of an advisory nature as may be assigned to the animal services advisory board by the board of county commissioners.
(4)
Membership.
a.
This board shall be composed of county citizens who are dedicated to the purpose of providing a sound animal welfare and control policy;
b.
The animal services advisory board shall be composed of six members, with five voting members appointed by the board of county commissioners and one ex-officio nonvoting member. All members shall serve on a voluntary basis without compensation. The board members shall be selected according to the following:
1.
One member shall be a member of a regional nonprofit animal welfare organization and shall be nominated by the chairperson of the board of county commissioners;
2.
Four members shall be nominated from the citizens of the county; their nomination shall reflect their manifested interest in animal welfare and control programs conducted by the county. Each district commissioner shall have the opportunity to nominate one member as outlined in section 2-53;
3.
The director of the animal services department shall serve as an ex-officio member of the board and as such will serve as a nonvoting member.
c.
Each voting member shall be appointed for a four-year term;
d.
The ex-officio nonvoting member of the advisory board shall serve consecutive terms without interruptions.
(5)
Meetings.
a.
The animal services advisory board shall meet at least every quarter. The meetings may be called by the chairperson or by written notice signed by three members of the said board in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d);
b.
Special meetings may be called in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d) by the director of the animal services department in order to conduct hearings needed to make disposition of dangerous and potential dangerous dogs;
c.
The presence of three voting members is required to constitute a quorum sufficient to the holding of a meeting or a taking of any action. A majority vote of those members present at any meeting shall be sufficient to enable the taking of any action;
d.
The committee shall keep adequate records of its meetings, including the minutes, attendance records and records of formal committee action regarding animal services program review and monitoring;
e.
The animal services advisory board shall select its chairperson and such other officers as may be deemed necessary or desirable and shall establish a time and procedure for the election of such officers on an annual basis;
f.
Members shall attend meetings regularly in order to remain a member of the animal services advisory board in good standing. Absences shall be authorized by the chair of said board;
g.
Additional procedures for the conduct of meetings, bylaws and regulations shall be developed by the animal services advisory board.
(6)
Limitation on powers. The animal services advisory board shall have no power or authority to commit the county government to any policies or to incur any financial obligation or to create any liability on the part of the county. No actions or recommendations of the advisory committee shall be binding on the county until approved or adopted by the board of county commissioners.
(7)
Amendments to bylaws. The provisions set forth herein may be amended by a majority vote of the voting members of the animal services advisory board. Any amendments shall be voted on at a regular meeting and all members shall receive a minimum of ten days' prior notice. The amendments approved by said board will require the concurrence of the Putnam County Board of Commissioners.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-18-2010(1) ; Amend. of 3-1-2013 ; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )

Sec. 14-5. - Obstruction of an animal control officer.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to hinder, interfere, harass or otherwise obstruct the performance of any officer of the Putnam County Animal Services Department in the performance of his or her duties as provided for in this chapter.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person to relocate, interfere with, or alter the operation of any device, equipment, or item of the Putnam County Animal Services Department.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place their own animal into or remove any animal confined within a trap or cage operated by the Putnam County Animal Services Department.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for any person to give a false name, address, or date of birth to any officer, employee, or agent of the Putnam County Animal Services Department in the lawful discharge of his/her official duties with the intent to mislead such officer, employee or agent in any way.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-6. - Identification and inoculation.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to own, possess or maintain any dog or cat that does not have a current rabies inoculation.
(b)
Any such dog or cat shall be microchipped or wear at all times a securely attached collar about its neck displaying such current rabies tag. Proof of a current vaccination shall rest with the owner.
(c)
No person shall vaccinate dogs/cats for rabies who is not licensed to practice veterinary medicine in the State of Georgia.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-7. - Animals at large.
A person owning or having animals in his custody and control shall at all times control such animals so as to prevent the animals from going at large to the injury, nuisance or endangerment to the safety of others. Failure to do so shall constitute a violation of this chapter. It shall be unlawful for an owner or keeper of any hog, cattle, mule, sheep, goat, fowl or any other livestock or nontraditional livestock animal to permit it to run at large in the county or to stray from the property of the owner or keeper or to go upon the premises of any other person.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-8. - Removal of animal wastes.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or keeper of any animal to refuse or fail to immediately remove any feces deposited by such animal upon public sidewalks, public streets, public parks, or other public property in the county. It shall be unlawful for any person to place, or deposit, or permit to be deposited in any unsanitary manner on private property within the county any animal excrement.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-9. - Impoundment by animal control officer.
The animal control officer shall be authorized to impound any domestic animal in violation of this chapter, whether on public or private property. In lieu of impoundment, the animal control officer may issue a citation for violation of this chapter.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-10. - Impoundment by property owner or injured party.
Any person finding any domestic animal upon his/her property may contact the animal control officer to impound such animal
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-11. - Impoundment; liability for costs.
The owner of an animal impounded pursuant to this chapter shall be liable for the cost of maintenance of such animal during such impoundment. Such costs shall be set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk and shall be payable upon redemption of such animal.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-12. - Impoundment; disposition of unredeemed animals.
(1)
All incoming animals shall be checked for a microchip pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-11-5.2.
(2)
Except for sick, injured, feral or owner surrender, animals shall be impounded for a minimum of seven days before disposition of the animal. At the end of seven days, if no owner has been found, the animal services director may:
(a)
Release for adoption.
(b)
Release the animal over to a humane society/rescue organization for adoption.
(c)
Euthanize the animal.
(3)
Owner surrender animals shall have no minimum impound requirements.
(4)
Any domestic animal displaying feral behavior shall be impounded for a minimum of three days.
(5)
Sick or injured animals. (See section 14-15.)
(6)
No animal in the possession of Putnam County Animal Services shall be released to any individual or institution for the purposes of medical and/or scientific research.
(7)
The animal services director may, at his or her sole discretion, extend the time for which an animal is retained at the shelter.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-13. - Redemption of impounded animals.
If an impounded animal can be identified (rabies tag, ID collar, etc.), it shall be the responsibility of the Putnam County Animal Services Director to attempt to notify the owner by phone, if possible, or by mail within two working days of impoundment. The owner must claim his/her animal within five days after notification. After seven days from impound, with no contact from the owner, the animal will be disposed of in accordance with section 14-12.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-14. - Fees and penalties.
(1)
Any person reclaiming a neutered/spayed animal shall pay a fee for the first impoundment, the second impoundment and for each subsequent impoundment. In addition a day boarding fee will be assessed. Any veterinary costs will be added to the fees. All dogs and cats must have a rabies shot before they will be released to their owner.
(2)
Any person reclaiming an unneutered/unspayed animal shall pay a fee for the first impoundment, the second impoundment, and for every subsequent impoundment. In addition a day boarding fee will be assessed. Any veterinary costs will be added to the fees. All dogs or cats must have a rabies shot before they are released to their owner.
(3)
Any person violating any section of this chapter shall be punished by a fine. Each violation shall be a separate offense.
(4)
All fees shall be set as per policy of the animal services department.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-15. - Disposition of a sick or injured animal.
When an animal does not bear a tag identifying the owner, the animal services director or licensed veterinarian shall have the authority to humanely dispose of diseased or injured animals, whenever it shall appear that any such animal has been severely injured and is suffering great pain. Whenever a sick or injured animal can be identified, the animal control officer will attempt to notify the owner and transport the animal to an appropriate veterinarian. All medical fees incurred are the sole responsibility of the owner of the animal.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-16. - Adoptions.
The animal services director may, at his or her sole discretion, deny adoption to any individual.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-17. - Abandonment or abuse of animals.
It shall be unlawful for anyone knowingly to abandon, abuse or fail to keep any domesticated animal or domesticated fowl in a humane manner within the county. Each animal so abandoned or abused, and each day the violation of this chapter exists, shall constitute a separate offense.
Putnam County Animal Services shall be authorized to enforce this provision of O.C.G.A. Title 4, Chapter 11, Article 1, titled "The Georgia Animal Protection Act" (O.C.G.A. § 4-11-2 et seq.)
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )

ARTICLE II. - DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 14-41. - Sections incorporated by reference.
O.C.G.A. §§ 4-8-22 through 4-8-30 are incorporated herein by reference thereto, except as set out in this article.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-42. - Notice to public of guard dogs and dangerous or potentially dangerous dogs.
It shall be the duty of each owner, tenant, or custodian of an enclosure, building or any residential or commercial property [at] which a guard or attack dog, a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is kept for security purposes to post a notice in a prominent and conspicuous location on such property. Such a notice shall comprise the words: "WARNING: GUARD DOG", "WARNING: ATTACK DOG", "WARNING: DANGEROUS DOG" or "WARNING: POTENTIALLY DANGEROUS DOG", or "BEWARE OF DOG". Such letters shall be not less than one inch in width and six inches in height. If a guard dog is confined within a fence area or in a building, such notice shall be conspicuously posted at each entrance and exit of the fence or building.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
Sec. 14-43. - Regulation of dangerous dogs.
The animal services director and any animal control officers under the direct supervision of the animal services director shall be the designated authority to carry out the duties of dog control officer imposed by O.C.G.A. Title 4, Chapter 8 or imposed by local ordinance. It shall be the duty of the animal services board to conduct hearings and make disposition of dangerous and potentially dangerous dogs as required by O.C.G.A. § 4-8-23.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-44. - Financial responsibility.
(a)
In addition to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25, the owner of a dangerous dog, guard dog or attack dog shall register such animals with the county clerk and present to the county clerk evidence of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $50,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state and insuring the owner of the dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of $50,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state and payable to any person or persons injured by the dangerous dog.
(b)
Dogs owned by or used by law enforcement agencies are exempted from the provisions of this article.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003; Amend. of 5-19-2015 )
Sec. 14-45. - Effective date, jurisdiction.
This chapter shall be effective immediately upon its enactment and passage by the board of commissioners. This chapter shall only apply to the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Amend. of 3-18-2003)
