ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Building inspector.
(a)
Appointment; powers and duties. The mayor and council shall appoint a building inspector, who shall have the following duties and authority:
(1)
Inspections. Inspect all work and improvements covered by this chapter where a permit has been issued upon the completion of the work, and, if deemed necessary, make inspections at any stage of the work in process.
(2)
Orders for removal and replacement of work. Demand that defective work and materials be removed and properly replaced.
(3)
Imposition of supplemental requirements. Make and enforce requirements to supplement those set forth in this chapter when, in such inspector's discretion, such are necessary to safeguard life or property.
(b)
Right of entry. The granting of a permit by the city shall give the inspector the right to enter any buildings or premises at any reasonable hour to ascertain whether or not there has been compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Code 1988, § 5-1)
Cross reference— Officers and employees, § 2-61 et seq.
Sec. 18-2. - Designation of additional building inspectors.
The mayor and council shall have the right to designate the city administrator or other employees of the city to act as building inspectors when deemed necessary or desirable, and when so designated such personnel shall have the same duties and authority as this chapter grants to the building inspector.
(Code 1988, § 5-2)
Sec. 18-3. - Fire district.
All of the territory embraced within the corporate limits of the city, as such limits now exist or may be hereafter enlarged, shall constitute and be the fire district.
(Code 1988, § 5-3)
Cross reference— Fire protection and prevention, ch. 50.
Sec. 18-4. - Licenses and permits.
(a)
Responsibility for obtaining license and permit. The person hiring or contracting with any person shall be jointly responsible with the contractor or person hired when either hiring or contracting for work covered by this chapter to be done, to ascertain and require that a license and permit be acquired by the party doing the improvement or construction prior to the commencement of any work.
(b)
Suspension or revocation of license or permit. In addition to the penalties imposed for violation of this Code, violation of any provision of this chapter shall constitute sufficient grounds for suspension or revocation of any license or permit held by a violator when deemed meet and proper for the welfare and protection of persons and property within the city, determination thereof to be made either by the municipal judge or by the mayor and council.
(c)
License and permit required. No person shall engage in or carry on the business of providing services or contracting to provide work, supplies, equipment, appliances or services for the installation of any plumbing, electrical work, brick masonry or natural gas, for the remodeling of present structures, or for the building or construction of any new structures within the city, until the following requirements have been fully complied with:
(1)
License. All builders, plumbers, electricians and brick masons, whether doing business as an individual, firm or corporation, before carrying on such work, must first obtain from the city clerk an annual license therefor, and pay the required license fee.
(2)
Permit. All builders, plumbers, electricians and brick masons, before beginning the remodeling of present structures or the building or construction of any new structures, must first obtain from the city clerk a permit for each individual job or contract for which the remodeling or new building and construction is to be started.
(d)
Permit fees. The schedules of permit fees shall be as prescribed from time to time by the mayor and council and filed in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 5-4)
Sec. 18-5. - Fencing of swimming pools.
(a)
Every outdoor residential swimming pool or family pool within the city shall be completely surrounded by a fence or wall not less than four feet in height, which shall be so constructed as not to have openings, holes, or gaps larger than four inches in any dimension except for doors and gates, and if a picket fence is erected or maintained, the horizontal dimension shall not exceed four inches. A dwelling house or accessory building may be used as part of such enclosure.
(b)
All gates or doors opening through such enclosures shall be equipped with a self-closing and self-latching device for keeping the gate or door securely closed at all times when not in actual use, except that the door of any dwelling which forms a part of the enclosure need not be so equipped.
(c)
This requirement shall be applicable to all new swimming pools or family pools hereafter constructed, other than indoor pools, and shall apply to all existing pools which have a minimum depth of 18 inches of water. No person in possession of land within the city, either as owner, purchaser, lessee, tenant or a licensee, upon which is situated a residential swimming pool or family pool having a minimum depth of 18 inches shall fail to provide and maintain such fence or wall as provided in this section.
(d)
The building inspector may make modifications in individual cases, upon a showing of good cause, with respect to the height, nature or location of the fence, wall, gates or latches, or the necessity therefor, provided the protection as sought under this section is not reduced thereby. The building inspector shall allow a reasonable period within which to comply with the requirements of this section.
(Code 1988, § 5-13)
Cross reference— Zoning, app. A.
Sec. 18-6. - Fencing of junkyards.
All junkyards (scrap piles, auto graveyards, etc.) of any kind whatsoever located within the city shall be enclosed by an eight-foot fence built of dressed lumber and painted.
(Code 1988, § 5-14)
Cross reference— Junk dealers and junkyards, § 22-311 et seq.

Sec. 18-32. - Amendments to gas code.
The gas code shall be amended by adding the following subsections:
(1)
It is required that either galvanized pipe or black pipe be used for the conduction of gas in a residential building.
(2)
If black pipe is used for the conduction of gas in a residential building, the pipe must be painted in order to minimize external corrosion.
(3)
Pipe used for the conduction of gas in commercial buildings must be painted yellow.
(Code 1988, § 5-10)
Sec. 18-33. - Water-conserving plumbing fixtures required.
(a)
Definitions. For purposes of this section, the following definitions shall apply:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable, or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet, or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling, but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(b)
Requirements for new buildings. After March 1, 1991, there shall not be initiated within the boundary limits of the city the construction of any residential or commercial building of any type which:
(1)
Employs a gravity tank type, flushometer valve, or flushometer tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.6 gallons of water per flush;
(2)
Employs a shower head that allows a flow of more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
Employs a urinal that uses more than an average of 1.0 gallon of water per flush;
(4)
Employs a lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Employs a kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute.
(c)
Repairs and renovations. The requirements of subsection (b) of this section shall also apply to any residential and commercial construction initiated after March 1, 1991, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes the replacement of toilets or showers or both; however, this section shall not apply to any construction of a residential or commercial building or repair or renovation of any such building the contract for which was entered into prior to March 1, 1991.
(d)
Conditions for exemptions and variances. Exemption and/or variance to the requirements of subsections (b) and (c) of this section may be granted by the building inspector of the city in accordance with the procedure hereinafter set out as to new construction and repair or renovation of existing buildings based upon the following conditions:
(1)
When the repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets, or shower heads within such existing building;
(2)
When such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation, would not function properly if the toilets, faucets, or shower heads required by this section were installed;
(3)
When such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
When the units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(e)
Procedure for granting exemption or variance. Any person entity desiring to obtain an exception and/or variance from the requirements set forth in subsections (b) and (c) of this section shall present the request to the building inspector for a decision of whether this building (commercial or residential) and the construction or renovation or repair is entitled to the exception and/or variance. The building inspector shall require the application for exception to be submitted in writing and on such forms as such inspector so designates with any attachments that such inspector so requires. The building inspector will have ten days from the date of receipt of the application for the exception in which to render a decision. Once rendered, the decision shall be submitted immediately to the applicant in writing from the building inspector. The applicant, if in disagreement with the decision of the building inspector, may appeal the decision to the mayor and council by filing a request for a de novo proceeding before the mayor and council within 30 days of receipt of the decision by the building inspector. The mayor and council will conduct their hearing within 30 days of receipt of the notice of appeal and will render their decision in writing to the applicant within ten days of the conclusion of the hearing. At the hearing, the applicant will be entitled to introduce evidence by testimony or documents, subpoena a witness, and cross examine any witness appearing on behalf of the building inspector, and may be represented by counsel of his choice, and the proceeding will be recorded in accordance with the administrative policies of the city. During the appeal process and until a final order is entered by the mayor and council, the request for appeal will operate as a supersedeas to the building inspector's decision. The mayor and council may affirm, deny, revise, amend, remand for additional proceeding and alter the decision of building inspector.
(f)
Violations. Any person who installs any toilet, faucet, urinal, or shower head in violation of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Code 1988, § 5-11)
Sec. 18-34. - Issuance of permits; record of permits.
(a)
All applications for permits under this article, describing the work to be done, shall be made to the city clerk by the person performing the work. The application shall be accompanied by such plans, specifications and schedules as may be necessary to determine whether the work will conform to the requirements of this chapter. If it shall be found that the work will conform to the requirements of this chapter, a permit therefor shall be issued.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the city clerk to keep a book in which shall be recorded all such permits, together with all the papers in connection therewith. Such book shall be properly indexed and numbered.
(Code 1988, § 5-12)

DIVISION 2. - ELECTRICAL STANDARDS

Sec. 18-61. - Duties of electrical inspector; personnel.
(a)
Administration and enforcement. It shall be the duty of the electrical inspector to enforce the provisions of this division and the city electrical code. The inspector shall, upon application, grant permits for the installation or alteration of electrical equipment, and shall make inspections of electrical installations, all as provided in this division, and shall keep complete records of all permits issued, inspections and reinspections made and other official work performed in accordance with the provisions of this division.
(b)
Personnel. The electrical inspector is empowered to employ, after receiving the approval of the director of utilities or the electric superintendent, any assistant inspectors and clerical assistants necessary for the proper conduct of the office and the inspection of electrical installations as provided for in this division.
(c)
Conflict of interest. It shall be unlawful for the electrical inspector or any of the inspector's assistants to engage in the sale, installation or maintenance of electrical equipment, either directly or indirectly, and they shall have no financial interest in any concern engaged in such business in the city at any time while holding the office provided for in this section.
(Code 1988, § 5-20)
Cross reference— Officers and employees, § 2-61 et seq.
Sec. 18-62. - Inspections; order to correct conditions; authority to disconnect service.
(a)
Inspections. The electrical inspector shall have the right during reasonable hours to enter any building or premises in the discharge of official duties, or for the purpose of making any inspection, reinspection or test of the electrical equipment contained therein or its installation.
(b)
Orders; effect of noncompliance. When any electrical equipment is found by the electrical inspector to be dangerous to persons or property because it is defective or defectively installed, the person responsible for the electrical equipment shall be notified in writing and shall make any changes or repairs required by the electrical inspector to place such equipment in safe condition. If such work is not completed within 15 days or any longer period that may be specified by the electrical inspector in such notice, the electrical inspector shall have the authority to discontinue or order the discontinuance of electric service to such equipment.
(c)
Emergency disconnection of service. In case of emergency, where necessary for safety to persons or property or where electrical equipment may interfere with the work of the fire department, the electrical inspector shall have the authority to immediately disconnect or cause the disconnection of any electrical equipment.
(d)
Delegation of authority by electrical inspector. The electrical inspector may delegate any of such inspector's powers or duties to any of such inspector's assistants.
(Code 1988, § 5-21)
Sec. 18-63. - Applicability of electrical standards and code.
(a)
The provisions of this division and the electrical code adopted in section 18-31 shall apply to all installations of electrical conductors, fittings, devices, appliances and fixtures, referred to in this division as "electrical equipment," within or on public and private buildings and premises, with exceptions as provided in this division and with the following general exceptions:
(1)
Installations in mines or railway cars, or automotive equipment.
(2)
Installations used by electricity supply or communication agencies in the generation, transmission or distribution of electricity or for the operation of signals or the transmission of intelligence, and located within or on buildings or premises used exclusively by such an agency or on public thoroughfares.
(b)
The provisions of this division shall apply to electrical equipment used for radio transmission in amateur radio transmitting stations and shall apply to all electrical equipment used for power supply to radio and television transmitting equipment, but shall not apply to other electrical equipment used for radio or television transmission.
(Code 1988, § 5-22)
Sec. 18-64. - Permit; standards for work; conference with electrical inspector before starting work; supervision and inspection of work.
(a)
Any person intending to perform any electrical work inside the city, excluding normal industrial plant maintenance, but including any major change or addition to an industrial plant, must obtain a permit to perform such electrical work from the city prior to commencing such work.
(b)
All electrical work performed inside the city shall be done by a licensed electrician or under the direct supervision of a licensed electrician.
(c)
All electrical work shall be performed by competent personnel and in a good and workmanlike manner.
(d)
A preconference on the job site with the electrician, builder, and city electrical inspector is required before any electrical work is begun.
(e)
The electrical meter location and the service entrance to the job site are to be established by the electrician and the city electrical inspector before electrical work is commenced.
(f)
The city electrical inspector will inspect the work after rough-in before any work is covered.
(g)
The city electrical inspector shall make a final inspection prior to the setting of the meter.
(h)
All electrical work must be in compliance with the standards set forth in the electrical code of the city.
(i)
The electrical permit shall be signed by the city electrical inspector at each stage of inspection and the meter shall not be set until the final inspection is completed.
(j)
In addition to the penalties specified in section 1-7, any violation of this section may result in revocation of the offender's license.
(Code 1988, § 5-23)
Sec. 18-65. - Power factor for gas tube lighting devices.
Gas tube lighting devices, including fluorescent lighting, shall have corrective devices incorporated in them so that the power factor of the whole device will not be less than 90 percent.
(Code 1988, § 5-24)
Sec. 18-66. - Connection to transmission system.
No connection shall be made to electric transmission wires by anyone except city electricians, and such electricians shall not make any connection until the wiring in the building to which such connection is to be made has been inspected and approved by such electrical inspector or the inspector's designee.
(Code 1988, § 5-25)
Sec. 18-67. - Responsibility for damages.
This chapter shall not be construed to relieve from or lessen the responsibility or liability of any party owning, operating, controlling or installing any electrical equipment for damages to persons or property caused by any defect therein, nor shall the city be held as assuming any such liability by reason of any inspection or reinspection or by issuance of any permit or certificate of approval of any equipment.
(Code 1988, § 5-26)

Sec. 18-91. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Building means a house, commercial building, industrial building, or any structure of a size great enough so that the moving thereof over a street would cause hazard or inconvenience to any person.
Interfering structure means any wire, cable, conduit, pipe, tree, pole, curb, sidewalk, pavement or other structure or device located in or about a street which must be temporarily relocated or otherwise altered in order to facilitate the passage of a building.
Over includes on, across, and upon.
Permittee means the person to whom the permit is issued pursuant to the provisions of this article.
Public conveyance means any motor vehicle designed, used or maintained primarily for the transportation of property.
Trailer means any vehicle, without motive power, designed for carrying property and for being drawn by a motor vehicle.
(Code 1988, § 5-30)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-92. - Permit required.
No person shall move a building over any street within the city without first obtaining a permit therefor. Permits issued under this article shall not be assignable or transferable and shall not be used by any person other than the permittee or the permittee's employees.
(Code 1988, § 5-31)
Sec. 18-93. - Application for permit; insurance requirements.
(a)
Contents of application. Any person desiring to obtain a permit authorizing the moving of a building over a street within the city shall make application therefor to the building inspector upon a form provided by the inspector. The application shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The location from which the building is to be moved.
(3)
The destination to which the building is to be moved.
(4)
The route proposed by the applicant over which the building is to be moved.
(5)
The dimensions of the building.
(6)
Type of construction (frame, brick, concrete, etc.).
(7)
The proposed date and time of movement of the building.
(8)
The applicant's signature.
(b)
Certificate of public convenience and necessity; department of transportation permit; proof of insurance.
(1)
The application shall also be accompanied by proof of a current valid certificate of public convenience and necessity issued to applicant by the state public service commission, a valid permit from the state department of transportation for the proposed building movement, and proof of public liability and property damage insurance in at least the following amounts: public liability, $300,000.00 each accident; $100,000.00 each person injured; and total property damage, $50,000.00.
(2)
Upon request, the person receiving the permit shall furnish the city the number of the policy of insurance required in this section as well as the address of the insurance company
(3)
If either the certificate of public convenience and necessity or the department of transportation permit referred to in this section shall not be required for the particular building movement for which the permit is requested, it shall be the duty of the applicant to demonstrate the lack of need therefor.
(Code 1988, § 5-32)
Sec. 18-94. - Deposit for costs of cutting, raising or replacing interfering structures.
If it appears to the building inspector that it may be necessary to cut, raise, or replace any interfering structure in order to facilitate the movement of a building, the permit shall not be issued unless the applicant first files with the building inspector a receipt given to the applicant by the owner or other lawful custodian of the interfering structure for a deposit equal to the amount of actual expense involved in cutting, raising, or replacing the structure.
(Code 1988, § 5-33)
Sec. 18-95. - Route.
The permit issued under this article shall designate the route over which the applicant shall move any building to which this article applies. The route shall not be changed unless the permit is amended. Should part of such route be over a state road, the required permit by the state must be exhibited to the building inspector.
(Code 1988, § 5-34)
Sec. 18-96. - Cutting or trimming trees.
No tree shall be cut or trimmed by a permittee under this article without prior written consent of the owner or other lawful custodian of such tree.
(Code 1988, § 5-35)
Sec. 18-97. - Dilapidated buildings.
No dilapidated, substandard, dangerous or unsafe building may be moved over any city street.
(Code 1988, § 5-36)
