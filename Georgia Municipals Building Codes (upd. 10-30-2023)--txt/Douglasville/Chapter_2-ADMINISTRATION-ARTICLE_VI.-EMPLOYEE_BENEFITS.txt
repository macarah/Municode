ARTICLE VI. - EMPLOYEE BENEFITS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-246. - Workers' compensation self insurance fund.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 34-9-150 et seq., the City of Douglasville hereby agrees to become a member of the Georgia Municipal Association Workers' Compensation Self Insurance Fund established pursuant to that code chapter.
(b)
The city hereby agrees to subscribe to and abide by the intrastate agreement, bylaws, rules and regulations of the fund and rules and regulations of the insurance commissioner of the state related to such fund.
(c)
The mayor of the city is hereby authorized to execute the application and any other documents necessary to enroll the city as a member in such fund.
(d)
The effective date of this section shall be March 1, 1983.
(Code 1977, § 2-133)
Sec. 2-247. - Insurance benefits for elected officials.
For elected officials who initially take office on or before January 8, 2016, the city shall pay for the cost of the group health and dental insurance premiums for all elected officials receiving retirement benefits from the city as it would for an active service employee, who meet all of the following prerequisites:
(1)
The retiree has at least ten years of service with the city.
(2)
The retiree has reached the age of 55 when he leaves active service with the city and elects to immediately begin receiving a retirement benefit, so that there is no lapse in health insurance coverage.
The retired elected official shall be responsible to pay the full cost of dependent coverage at the regular premium rate of a city employee.
Whenever city-administered insurance terminates for any elected official or former elected official or his dependents, the effective date of termination shall be the earlier of: (a) the first day of the calendar month after the date of the event causing cessation of benefits, or (b) the date of termination specified by the city's insurance contract with the benefits provider. The elected official or former elected official or other insured shall continue to pay the insured's portion of the premium through the effective date of termination.
The city reserves the right to amend, limit or significantly alter insurance coverage or premiums payments provided for elected officials and former elected officials and their dependents at the discretion of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. O-2019-37 , § 3, 8-5-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. O-2019-37 , § 3, adopted Aug. 5, 2019, amended § 2-247 in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former § 2-247 pertained to health and dental insurance benefits for elected employees and derived from Ord. No. O-2015-55, § 1, adopted Dec. 7, 2015.

The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
State agency means the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia.

Sec. 2-267. - Statement of purpose.
It is hereby declared to be the policy and purpose of the city to extend at the earliest date possible, under the agreement entered into by the state and the federal security administration, to the employees and officials thereof and its instrumentalities, not excluded by law or by this division, the benefits of the system of old-age and survivors insurance as authorized by the Federal Social Security Act and amendments thereto, including Public Law No. 734 of the 81st Congress, and as provided for in Act No. 295, Georgia Laws 1951, approved February 21, 1951. In pursuance of such policy, and for that purpose, the city shall take such action as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1977, § 2-147)
Sec. 2-268. - Execution of agreements.
The mayor is hereby authorized and directed to execute all necessary agreements and amendments thereto with the state agency to secure coverage of employees and officials as provided for in this division.
(Code 1977, § 2-148)
Sec. 2-269. - Withholdings authorized.
Withholdings from salaries or wages of employees and officials for the purpose provided by this division are hereby authorized to be made in the amounts and at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations and shall be paid over to the state agency.
(Code 1977, § 2-149)
Sec. 2-270. - Appropriations.
There shall be appropriated from the general fund of the city such amounts at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations for employer's contributions, administrative expense, and a guaranty fund to assure that the state will not incur any debt or loss as a result of the agreement referred to in this division. Such fund shall be paid over to the state agency in accordance with regulations established by such agency.
(Code 1977, § 2-150)
Sec. 2-271. - Records and reports.
The city shall keep such records and make such reports as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1977, § 2-151)
Sec. 2-272. - Exclusion from provisions.
There is hereby excluded from the provisions of this division any authority to make any agreement with respect to any position, or any employee or official not authorized to be covered by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1977, § 2-152)
