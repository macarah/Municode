Sec. 26-1. - Occupation tax required; occupation tax required for business dealings in the incorporated area of the City of Douglasville, Georgia.
For the year 1995 and succeeding years thereafter, each person engaged in any business, trade, profession, or occupation in the City of Douglasville, Georgia whether with a location in the city or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in Georgia but exerting substantial efforts within the city pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax for said business, trade, profession, or occupation; which tax and any applicable registration shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in the City of Douglasville, Georgia. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the City of Douglasville, Georgia, such business tax registration shall be shown to the Clerk's Office of the City of Douglasville or any duly authorized representative of the City of Douglasville, Georgia, upon request.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-2. - Construction of terms; definitions.
(a)
Wherever the term city is used herein, such term shall be construed to mean the incorporated area of the City of Douglasville, Georgia.
(b)
As used in this article, the term:
(1)
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
(1.1)
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form I.R.S. W-2 but not a form I.R.S. 1099.
(2)
a.
Gross receipts means the total revenue of the business or practitioner for the period, including without limitation to the following:
i.
Total income without deduction for the cost of goods or expenses incurred;
ii.
Gain from trading in stocks, bonds, capital assets, or instruments of indebtedness;
iii.
Proceeds from commissions on the sale of property, goods, or services;
iv.
Proceeds from fees for services rendered; and
v.
Proceeds from rent, interest, royalty or dividend income.
b.
Gross receipts shall not include the following:
i.
Sales, use, or excise tax;
ii.
Sales returns, allowances, and discounts;
iii.
Interorganizational sales or transfers between or among the units of a parent-subsidiary controlled group of corporations as defined by 26 U.S.C. § 1563(a)(1), or between or among the units of a brother-sister controlled group of corporations as defined by 26 U.S.C. § 1563(a)(2), or between or among wholly owned partnerships or other wholly owned entities;
iv.
Payments made to a subcontractor or an independent agent;
v.
Governmental and foundation grants, charitable contributions, or the interest income derived from such funds received by a nonprofit organization which employs salaried practitioners otherwise covered by this article, if such funds constitute 80 percent or more of the organization's receipts; and
vi.
Proceeds from sales to customers outside the state.
(3)
Location or office shall include any structure or vehicle where any business, profession or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession.
(4)
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession, or business for revenue raising purposes.
(5)
Regulatory fees means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, which are required by a local government as an exercise of its police power and as a part of or an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession, or business. The amount of a regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the city. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee. Development impact fees as defined by paragraph 8 of O.C.G.A. § 36-71-2 or other costs or conditions of zoning or land development are not regulatory fees.
(6)
Dominant line means the type of business, within a multiple-line business, that the greatest amount of income is derived from.
(7)
Person shall be held to include sole proprietors, corporations, partnerships, nonprofits, or any other form of business organization.
(8)
Practitioner of profession or occupation is one who by state law requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation.
(9)
Practitioner of profession and occupation shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-3. - Administrative and regulatory fee structure; occupation tax structure.
(a)
A nonprorated, nonrefundable administrative fee of $50.00 shall be required on all business and occupation tax accounts for the initial start-up, renewal, or reopening of those accounts.
(b)
An administrative fee of $10.00 shall be required on all business and occupation tax accounts for the transfer of the registration certificate to a new business location for the same owner to operate the same business under the same name at the new location for the remainder of the existing license period under the same terms and conditions contained herein.
(c)
In addition to the administrative fee, a non-prorated regulatory fee will be imposed as provided under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9 on those applicable businesses. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee and is non-refundable. The regulatory and administrative fees required by this section shall be paid in addition to any occupation tax required under section 26-4.

(d)
(1)
No business or practitioner shall be required to pay more than one occupation tax for each of its locations.
(2)
No occupation tax will be required upon more than 100 percent of a business' gross receipts.
(3)
No occupation tax will be required on receipts on which such tax has been levied in other localities or states.
(4)
An occupation tax shall be required from real estate brokers, agents, or companies whose offices are located outside the taxing jurisdiction and who sell property inside the taxing jurisdiction.
(5)
An occupation tax shall not be levied in any other manner except as described in this section.
(6)
Occupation taxes are limited to the gross receipts earned in the State of Georgia.
(7)
Out-of-state businesses with no location in Georgia shall be assessed occupation taxes based on the gross receipts of the business as defined in O.C.G.A. § 14-13-7 which are reasonably attributed to sales or services in the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-2013-53, § 7, 12-16-13)
Sec. 26-5. - Paying occupation tax of business with no location in Georgia.
Registration and assessment of an occupation tax is hereby imposed on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the State of Georgia if the business's largest dollar volume of business in Georgia is in the city and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of the city for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of the city.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-6. - Each line of business to be identified on business registration.
The business registration of each business operated in the local government's jurisdiction shall identify the line or lines of business that the business conducts. No business shall conduct any line of business without first having that line of business registered with the finance director office and that line of business being noted by the occupational tax office upon the business registration form which is to be displayed by the business owner.
(Ord. No. O-95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-2013-53, § 8, 12-16-13)
Sec. 26-7. - The number of businesses considered to be operating in city.
Where a person conducts business at more than one fixed location, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-8. - Professionals as classified in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c), Paragraphs 1 through 18.
(a)
Practitioners of professions as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18) shall elect as their entire occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax based on gross receipts combined with profitability ratios as set forth in section 26-4 of this article; or
(2)
A flat fee of $300.00 per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the practitioner's office or location; provided, however, that a practitioner paying according to this paragraph shall not be required to provide information to the local government relating to the gross receipts of the business or practitioner. The per-practitioner fee applies to each person in the business who qualifies as a practitioner under the state's regulatory guidelines and framework.
(b)
This election is to be made on an annual basis and must be done by October 15 of the preceding year. If the practitioner fails to make the election by October 15 the city will assess the flat fee of $300.00 against the practitioner.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-9. - Practitioners exclusively practicing for a government.
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, the state, a municipality or county of the state, instrumentalities of the United States, the state, or a municipality or county of the state shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that practice.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-10. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5 to 48-13-26. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-11. - When tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
Each such occupation tax shall be levied for a calendar year. Said registration and occupation tax shall be payable January 1 of each year and, if not paid by January 30 of each year, the taxpayer shall be subject to citation to the municipal court for violation of this article. If the tax is not paid by March 31, the taxpayer shall be subject to a ten percent penalty for delinquency, and interest at the rate of one and one-half percent per month on the amount of the unpaid tax accruing after January 30. On any new profession, trade, or calling begun in the city after January of any year, the registration and tax shall be delinquent if not obtained within 30 days after beginning business and a ten percent delinquency penalty imposed after 90 days, plus interest at the at the rate of one and one-half percent per month on the amount of the unpaid tax accruing after the thirtieth day of business. The tax registration herein provided for shall be issued by the city finance director and if any person, firm, or corporation whose duty it is to obtain a registration shall, after said registration or occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in the city, any of the kind of profession, trade, or calling subject to this article without having first obtained said registration, such offender shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided by this article and the ordinances of the city. Each day that the profession, trade or calling subject violates the aforesaid provision shall be deemed to be a separate offense.
(b)
In addition to the above remedies and any other remedies provided for by law, the officer charged with the collection of the tax or other official authorized pursuant to the law may proceed to collect in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-2016-52 , § 2, 11-7-16)
Sec. 26-12. - Allocation of gross receipts of business with multiple intra- or interstate locations.
For those businesses that have multiple locations inside and outside of the city where the gross receipts can be allocated to each location, the gross receipts used to determine the occupation tax assessed will be those gross receipts attributed to each city location. Where the dollar amount of gross receipts attributed locally cannot be determined in those businesses with multiple locations, the total gross receipts will be divided by the total number of locations in the city and elsewhere and allotted to those locations, Upon request, the business or practitioner with a location or office situated in more than one jurisdiction shall provide to the city the following:
(1)
Financial information necessary to allocate the gross receipts of the business or practitioner; and
(2)
Information relating to the allocation of the business's or practitioner's gross receipts by other local governments.
Where the business has locations outside of the city and taxation is levied for a criteria other than gross receipts in the other local governments, the city shall not assess more than the allotted share of gross receipts for the local operation.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-13. - Exemptions.
All state and local authorities and all nonprofit organizations shall be exempt from occupation tax, regulatory fees and administrative fees. There shall be an annual exemption against occupation tax in the amount of $7,500.00 and an annual exemption against from regulatory fees in the amount of $100.00 available for any business which is wholly owned by any individual who is:
(1)
One hundred percent medically disabled; or
(2)
A veteran of the United States armed service who is 50 percent or greater disabled.
The exemption shall apply only to one business normally taxed hereunder, and shall not apply to any additional businesses or locations as herein defined. Written proof of qualification for said exemptions shall be exhibited to the city clerk and all required applications shall be completed.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-14. - Evidence of state registration required if applicable; state registration to be displayed.
(a)
Each person who is licensed by the Secretary of State pursuant to Title 4.3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated shall provide evidence of proper and current state licensure before the city registration may be issued.
(b)
Each person who is licensed by the state shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the licensee's place of business and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-15. - Evidence of qualification required if applicable.
(a)
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency, or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of a city business registration, show evidence that such requirements have been met.
(b)
All persons registering for the occupation tax shall comply with all ordinances of the city, including but not limited to, all zoning ordinances, fire ordinances, building ordinances and regulations, and environmental ordinances and regulations or said occupation tax registration may denied.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-16. - Liability of officers and agents; registration required; failure to obtain.
All persons subject to the occupation tax levy pursuant to this article shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for said business as described in this article, and in default thereof the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a registration. Every person commencing business in the city after January 1 of each year shall likewise obtain the registration herein provided for before commencing the same; and any person transacting, or offering to transact in the city, any of the kinds of business, trade, profession, or occupation without first having so obtained said registration, shall be subject to penalties provided thereof.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-17. - Penalty of article violation.
Any person violating any provisions of this article may, upon conviction in any court of competent jurisdiction, be fined in an amount not exceeding $500.00 and/or imprisoned not exceeding 60 days for each offense in the discretion of the court.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-18. - Administration by finance director; subpoena powers.
The occupational tax ordinance shall be enforced by the office of the finance director and its duly designated employees and inspectors shall be classified as deputy marshal-business inspectors with the ability to seek full subpoena powers in conjunction with any violation pertaining to the occupation tax ordinance. The finance director may deny any application for registration due to (a) the applicant's failure to comply with the requirements of this chapter, or (b) any reason which would be a basis for revocation pursuant to section 26-42, had the applicant been licensed.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-01-81, § 4, 12-3-01; Ord. No. O-04-14, § 1, 4-19-04; Ord. No. O-2011-39, § 1, 10-3-11)

Sec. 26-20. - Occupation tax inapplicable where prohibited by law or provided for pursuant to other existing law.
An occupation tax shall not apply to the gross receipts of any part of a business where such levy is prohibited or exempted by the laws of Georgia or of the United States.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-21. - Occupation taxes levied on business to be transacted during calendar year; filing of initial returns showing gross receipts during preceding calendar year.
(a)
All occupation taxes levied under this article are levied on the amount of business to be transacted during the calendar year. However, for the convenience of both the city and the taxpayer, each business subject to the occupation tax levied in section 26-4 shall file an initial tax return prior to the subject tax year, based on the gross receipts for the business during the 12 months preceding filing. This initial return and any other relevant information deemed pertinent by the finance director shall be used as the basis for estimating the amount to be paid for the occupation tax for the calendar year of registration. The actual and final amount of tax levied for business transacted during the subject calendar year of registration shall be adjusted and paid in accordance with a final return to be made after the end of the subject year, in accordance with the procedures set forth in this chapter.
(b)
After July 31 and before October 16 in any year preceding a calendar of business registration in the city, the owner, proprietor, manager, or executive officer of any business subject to occupation tax shall file with the finance director's office, on a form provided by the finance director, a signed initial return setting forth the amount of gross receipts of such business for the 12 months immediately preceding the filing of the initial return. Concurrent with said filing, the taxpayer shall produce for the finance director such information as is required to substantiate any exemption or exclusion from taxes claimed by the taxpayer. The initial tax return and any other information deemed pertinent by the finance director will be used to determine an estimate of gross receipts for the subject calendar year of registration and the initial tax assessment to be paid.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-01-81, § 4, 12-3-01)
Sec. 26-22. - Filing of initial return showing actual gross receipts during calendar year; procedure where taxes overpaid or underpaid.
(a)
After January 1 and before March 2nd of any year, immediately following a calendar year wherein a taxpayer has been registered or transacted business subject to occupation tax in the city, the owner, proprietor, manager, or executive officer of a business; subject to occupation tax shall file with the finance director's office, on a form provided by the finance director, a signed return setting forth the actual amount of the gross receipts of such business during the preceding calendar year. Concurrent with said filing, the taxpayer shall produce for the finance director such information as is required to substantiate any exemption or exclusion from taxes claimed by the taxpayer. The final tax return and any other information deemed pertinent by the finance director will be used to determine the gross receipts for the subject calendar year of registration and the final tax assessment.
(b)
If the amount of the final tax assessment for a taxpayer exceeds the initial assessment paid, then the taxpayer shall remit payment of the difference in said assessments on or before March 15 immediately following the subject tax year.
(c)
If the amount of the final tax assessment for a taxpayer is less than the initial assessment paid, then the city shall refund the difference in said assessments to the taxpayer on or before May 15. At the option of the city, where any refund is due a taxpayer with a current business registration, the city may apply such refund as a credit to the taxpayer's occupation tax account for the current year. Any taxpayer due a refund or credit for tax overpayment shall notify the finance director in writing of the amount of the same, and provide any necessary supporting documentation, on or before March 15th immediately following the subject tax year.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-01-81, § 4, 12-3-01)
Sec. 26-23. - Initial return for newly established businesses; filing of final return before end of calendar year.
(a)
Where a business currently subject to occupation tax has been conducted for only a part of the preceding calendar year, then the amount of gross receipts for such part of the preceding year shall be reported for purposes of the initial return for the business. The initial return shall also show a figure putting the receipts for such part of a year on an annual basis, with the part-year receipts bearing the same ratio to the whole-year gross receipts as the part year bears to the whole year. Said extrapolated figure shall be used as the estimate of the gross receipts for the business for purposes of the initial return for the current tax year.
(b)
Where a business currently subject to occupation tax was not conducted for any part of the preceding calendar year, then the owner, proprietor, manager, or executive officer of the business subject to occupation tax shall estimate the gross revenue to be received by the business from the date of commencement of business through the end of the current calendar year, and report the same as estimated gross income for purposes of the initial tax return. The owner, proprietor, manager, or executive officer of any such newly established business subject to occupation tax shall file an initial tax return and an application for business registration, and pay the initial tax assessment on or before beginning to conduct business in the city.
(c)
Where any registered business has ceased to maintain an office or location inside the corporate limits of the city due to transfer of ownership or relocation of the business, then the owner, proprietor, manager or executive officer of the business subject to occupation tax may surrender to the finance director the original business registration or business license document previously issued for the business by the finance director for the current tax year, and file a final tax return for the business before the end of the current tax year. Concurrent with filing of the final return or within 60 days thereafter, the taxpayer shall notify the finance director in writing of the amount of any claim for refund and provide any necessary supporting documentation. Any refund determined to be due the taxpayer shall be paid by the city within 90 days of submission of a documented claim for refund.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-01-81, § 4, 12-3-01)
Sec. 26-24. - More than one place or line of business; consolidated licenses.
(a)
Where a business is operated at more than one place or where the business includes more than one line, said business shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for each location and line and pay an occupation tax in accordance with the prevailing taxing method and tax rate for each location and line.
(b)
Individual vendors of used goods, operating in a single commercial location where multiple vendors customarily operate, may, at their option, dispense with individual registration and be licensed jointly under the name of the main operator of the establishment, if the establishment is:
(1)
An indoor business located in not more than two suites of a single building where all customer payments are received at a central location for all vendors; or
(2)
An outdoor flea market, where the main operator elects and pays a double occupation tax for all vendors of goods; vendors of services at such location shall be licensed individually, and may not be included in the consolidated license.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-02-34, § 1, 9-16-02; Ord. No. O-03-02, § 1, 2-3-03; Ord. No. O-07-03, § 1, 1-16-07)
Sec. 26-25. - Returns confidential.
Except in the case of judicial proceedings or other proceedings necessary to collect the occupation tax hereby levied, it shall be unlawful for any officer, employee, agent, or clerk of the city, or any other person to divulge or make known in any manner the amount of gross receipts or any particulars set forth or disclosed in any occupation tax return required under this article. All contents of said return shall be confidential and open only to the officials, employees, agents, or clerks of the city using said returns for the purpose of this occupation tax levy and the collection of the tax. Independent auditors or bookkeepers employed by the city shall be classed as "employees" for the purposes of this paragraph and section 26-26. Nothing herein shall be construed to prohibit the publication by the city officials of statistics, so classified as to prevent the identification of particular reports or returns and items thereof; or the inspection of the records by duly qualified employees of the tax departments of the State of Georgia, the United States, and other local governments
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-26. - Inspections of books and records.
In any case the finance director, through its officers, agents, employees, or representatives, may inspect the books of the business or person for which the returns are made. The finance director shall have the right to inspect the books or records for the business of which the return was made in the City of Douglasville, Georgia, and upon demand of the finance director such books or records shall be submitted for inspection by a representative of the city within 30 days. Failure of submission of such books or records within 30 days shall be grounds for revocation of the tax registration currently existing to do business in the city. Adequate records shall be kept in the city for examination by the finance director at that official's discretion. If, after examination of the books or records, it is determined that a deficiency occurs as a result of underreporting, a penalty of ten percent will be assessed for the period delinquent. Said assessment and penalty shall be due and payable within ten calendar days of receiving written notice of any such deficiencies.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-01-81, § 4, 12-3-01)
Sec. 26-27. - Tax registration to be revoked for failure to pay tax, file returns, permit inspection of books.
Upon the failure of any business to pay said occupation tax or any part thereof before it becomes delinquent, or upon failure to make any of said returns within the time required herein, or upon failure to make a true return, or upon failure to amend a return to set forth the truth, or upon failure to permit inspection of its books as above provided, any business tax registration granted by the city under this article permitting the owner of said business to do business for the current year shall be, ipso facto, revoked. No new business tax registration shall be granted by the city for the operation of a business for which any part of the occupation tax herein provided for is at that time unpaid, or to an individual, firm, or corporation who has failed to submit adequate records as requested by the city clerk in accordance with provisions found in section 26-22. In the case of those practitioners where the local government cannot suspend the right of the practitioner to conduct business, the imposition of any lawful penalties permitted shall be pursued by the local government in the case of delinquent occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-28. - Effect of failure to comply with article provisions; continuing in business after tax registration revocation.
Any persons, their managers, agents, or employees, who do business in said city after the registration for said business has been revoked as above, hereby required to make occupation tax returns, and who fail to make said returns within the time and in the manner herein provided, who refuse to amend such returns so as to set forth the truth, or who shall make false returns; and any persons, their managers, agents, or employees who refuse to permit an inspection of books in their charge when the officers, agents, employees, or representatives of the city request such inspection, during business hours, for the purpose of determining the accuracy of the returns herein provided for, shall be subject to penalties provided herein. In the case of those practitioners where the local government cannot suspend the right of the practitioner to conduct business, the imposition of many lawful penalties shall be permitted and pursued by the local government in the case of delinquent occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-29. - Lien taken for delinquent occupation tax or fees.
In addition to the other remedies herein provided for the collection of assessments for the occupation tax, administrative fees and regulatory fees herein levied, upon any assessment becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid, the assessment shall bear interest at the rate of 1.5 percent per month from the date when such assessment or installment becomes delinquent, until execution issued. Additionally, upon any assessment becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid for more than 90 days, there shall be added a ten percent penalty. Upon any assessment becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid for more than 90 days, the city clerk shall issue execution for the correct amount of said assessment against the persons, partnership, or corporation liable for said assessment. The execution shall bear interest on the assessment, excluding any penalty and pre-execution interest, at the rate of 1.5 percent per month, and the lien shall cover the property in the city of persons, partnership, or corporation liable for said assessment, all as provided by the ordinances of said city and the laws of Georgia, and the defendant in said execution shall have rights of defense, by affidavit of illegality and otherwise, which are provided by the applicable laws in regard to tax executions. When a nulla bona entry has been entered by proper authority upon an execution issued by the appropriate officer or official against any person defaulting on the occupation tax, the person against whom the entry was made shall not be allowed or entitled to have or collect any fees or charges whatsoever for services rendered after the entry of the nulla bona. If, at any time after the entry of nulla bona has been made, the person against whom the execution issues pays the tax in full together with all interest and costs accrued on the tax, the person may collect any fees and charges due him or her as though he or she had never defaulted in the payment of the taxes.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-30. - Appeals and qualifications for board of adjustments and appeals.
(a)
Any person, firm or corporation who is of the opinion that the occupation tax has been incorrectly assessed or that the major line classification is incorrect or that the registration has been incorrectly denied shall have the right to appeal to the board of adjustments and appeals within ten calendar days of having been notified of the disputed decision by the city clerk's office. A written appeal stating the position of the appealing party to the board of adjustment and appeals must be made within ten calendar days of any adverse decision. A hearing by the board of adjustments and appeals on the dispute shall be held within ten calendar days of written appeal to the board of adjustment and appeals. If the appealing part is still aggrieved after the decision of the said board he/she may file a written appeal to the city council within ten calendar days of receiving said adverse decision.
(b)
Reference to the board of adjustments and appeals within this section shall refer to such board consisting of at least three members of the city council. Such members shall be selected by the mayor and approved by the city council by resolution, to serve for such term as the city council shall select and at the pleasure of the city council.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-31. - Posting of emergency information.
All businesses, including separate business establishments within one building, shall post in a conspicuous place the current name and telephone number or numbers of a person for contact in the event of an emergency. It shall be the duty of the applicant to comply with this requirement.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-32. - Advertisement.
Any person advertising by signs, cards, circulars, newspaper advertising, radio advertising or otherwise, that he/she is in business of any kind the city shall be held liable for the registration, if any, required of such business.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-33. - Miscellaneous registration provisions.
(a)
If there is any business not specifically and particularly mentioned or provided for by the terms of this article or any other ordinance now in effect, the finance director and/or the city manager are authorized and empowered to require that a registration be obtained for the conduct of such business, and to fix the amount thereof prior to the engaging in such business, and no person shall engage in any business without having paid such occupation tax, but the finance director and/or city manager shall be required to notify the city council of any such action within 30 days.
(b)
The maximum occupation tax set by the finance director and/or city manager shall be the maximum as set forth in this article for a business mentioned in this article and the minimum shall be the minimum as set forth in this article for a business mentioned in this article.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-01-81, § 5, 12-3-01)
Sec. 26-34. - Amendment, repeal of provision.
This article shall be subject to amendment or repeal, in whole or in part, at any time, and no such amendment or repeal shall be construed to deny the right of the city council to assess and collect any of the taxes or other charges prescribed. Said amendment may increase or lower the amounts and tax rates of any occupation and may change the classification thereof. The payment of any occupation tax provided for shall not be construed as prohibiting the levy or collection by the jurisdiction of additional occupation taxes upon the same person, property, or business.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-35. - Applications of provisions to prior ordinance.
This article does not repeal or affect the force of any part of any ordinance heretofore passed where taxes levied under such prior ordinance have not been paid in full. So much and such parts of ordinances heretofore and hereinafter passed as provided for the issuing and enforcing of execution for any tax or assessment required by such ordinances, or that imposed fines or penalties for the nonpayment of such tax, or for failure to pay regulatory fees provided for in said ordinance or ordinances, or failure to comply with any other provisions hereof, shall continue and remain in force until such tax, regulatory fee, or assessment shall be fully paid.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-36. - Enforcement of provisions.
It is hereby made the duty of the city clerk and other duly appointed persons to see that the provisions of this article relating to occupation taxes are observed; and to summon all violators of the same not exempt from criminal penalties to appear before the court. Notwithstanding any ordinance to the contrary, attorneys at law are hereby exempted from any and all criminal sanctions for failure to comply with this chapter, and are subject only to civil penalties for failure to comply. It is hereby made the further duty of the city clerk, police chief, members of the police department, building department, code compliance officer and their assistants to inspect all registrations issued by the city, as often as in their judgment it may seem necessary to determine whether the registration held is the proper one for the business sought to be transacted thereunder.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95; Ord. No. O-97-58, § 1, 9-2-97; Ord. No. O-2018-9 , § 2, 3-5-18)
Sec. 26-37. - Requirement of public hearing before tax increase.
After January 1, 1996, the city council shall conduct at least one public hearing before adopting any ordinance or resolution which will increase the rate of occupation tax as set forth in the article.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-38. - Option to establish exemption or reduction in occupation tax.
The city council may by subsequent ordinance or resolution provide for an exemption or reduction in occupation tax to one or more types of businesses or practitioners of occupations or professions as part of a plan for economic development or attracting or encouraging selected types of businesses or practitioners of selected occupations or professions. Such exemptions or reductions in occupation tax shall not be arbitrary or capricious, and the reasons shall be set forth in the minutes of the governing authority or a plan.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-39. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention hereof that the specific shall control.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-40. - Severability.
If any section, provision, or clause of any part of this article shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, or if the provisions of any part of this article as applied to any particular situation or set of circumstances shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, such individuality shall not be construed to affect the portions of this article not so held to be invalid, or the application of this article to other circumstances not so held to be invalid. It is hereby declared as the intent that this article would have been adopted had such invalid portion not been included herein.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-41. - Repeal of conflicting provisions.
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict with this article, and not preserved hereby, are hereby repealed.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-42. - Revocation of registration.
The city council, on the basis of citations issued by the city clerk or any duly authorized representative, or on the basis of citations issued by any law enforcement officer, shall have the right to suspend and/or revoke any registration issued under this chapter whenever the licensee shall do any of the following:
(1)
Fail to confine the business operations to those normally associated with the particular registered activity; or
(2)
Violate any city code, rule, regulation or ordinance pertaining directly or indirectly to the registered activity; or
(3)
Violate any state or federal code, rule, regulation, ordinance or statute pertaining directly or indirectly to the registered activity.
Any person alleged to have violated the provisions of this chapter shall be given written notice of such violation by a representative of the city and shall be entitled to have a hearing before the city council on any proposed suspension or revocation. At the hearing, the licensee shall be entitled to legal representation and shall have the right to present witnesses and to cross-examine any witnesses presented by the city. No hearing provided for herein shall be conducted sooner than ten days from the date of the issuance of the citation. The decision of the city council shall be rendered within ten days from the date of the hearing.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-43. - Taxes on alcoholic beverages additional.
Nothing herein shall affect any ordinance providing for the regulation of the sale of any form of alcoholic beverages and takes imposed thereon, such taxes being due and payable in addition to the occupational tax imposed hereby. Holders of licenses issued by the City of Douglasville for the sale of alcoholic beverages, both retail and wholesale, shall be in addition to this article be taxed in accordance with the applicable provisions of state law and the Alcoholic Beverage Code of the City of Douglasville.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-44. - Street sales restricted.
Generally, the streets, sidewalks and alleys of the city shall not be used for the sale of any merchandise. The city council shall have authority, upon written application to the council, to make exception to this section for periods of one day at a time for the purpose of sidewalk sales, street festivals and the like.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-45. - Unlawful display.
No person shall carry or display any city license or permit which has been revoked or has expired or which has not been lawfully acquired and issued.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
Sec. 26-46. - Inspection by building official.
The building official of the City of Douglasville or his duly authorized agent shall inspect every business address within the corporate limits of the City of Douglasville list[ed] on the business license application as a place of conducting business, of every person, firm or corporation to verify that the business location is properly zoned, the building structure is suitable for the type of business to be located in the structure, that no building code violations exist and that a certificate of occupancy has been issued for the structure, if applicable.
(Ord. No. 95-39, § 1, 12-18-95)
