ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 82-1. - Uniform Rules of the Road adopted.
(a)
Adoption by reference. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, the Uniform Rules of the Road (O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 through 40-6-395) and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the City of Douglasville, Georgia, with the effect as if recited in this chapter.
(b)
Reserved.
(Code 1977, § 24-1; Ord. No. O-05-50, § 5, 9-19-05)
Sec. 82-2. - Enforcement of chapter.
It shall be the duty of the officers of the police department or such officers as are assigned by the chief of police to enforce this chapter and all other traffic laws of the city and all of the state vehicle laws applicable to street traffic in the city.
(Code 1977, § 24-2)
Sec. 82-3. - Dockless mobility devices.
(a)
Definitions. As used in this section, unless specifically stated otherwise, the following terms shall mean and include:
Dockless mobility device means an electric or motorized device that permits an individual to move or be moved freely which shall include, but not be limited to, e-scooters and shall exclude bicycles, e-bicycles, and any motor vehicle required to be registered with the state in accordance with state law, or the operation of which is otherwise governed by state law.
Shareable dockless mobility device means an electric or motorized or human-powered device that permits an individual to move or be moved freely, is available for rent to the general public for short-term one-way trips without the installation of any infrastructure in the public right-of-way and shall include, but not be limited to, an e-bicycle or e-scooter and shall exclude any motor vehicle required to be registered with the state, in accordance with state law.
(b)
No dockless mobility device may be operated or deployed in the city except while being ridden or pushed on a public street. No dockless mobility device shall be abandoned, parked, pushed or operated on any sidewalk, public plaza or pedestrian walkway. No vendor or applicant shall be licensed to deploy shareable dockless mobility devices in the city.
(Ord. No. O-2019-46 , § 3, 9-3-19)
Editor's note— Ord. No. O-2019-46 , § 2, adopted Sep. 3, 2019, repealed the former § 82-3, and § 3 of the ord. enacted a new section as set out herein. The former § 82-3 pertained to emergency and experimental regulations and derived from Code 1977, § 24-3.
Sec. 82-4. - Direction of traffic by police, firemen.
Officers of the police department, or such officers as are assigned by the chief of police, are hereby authorized to direct all traffic by voice, hand or signal in conformity with the traffic laws; provided, that upon a fire or other emergency or to expedite traffic or to safeguard pedestrians, officers of the police department may direct traffic as conditions require notwithstanding the provisions of the traffic laws. Officers of the fire department, when at the scene of a fire, may direct or assist the police in directing traffic thereat or in the immediate vicinity.
(Code 1977, § 24-4)
Sec. 82-5. - Parades—Permit.
Except for funeral processions, no parade or procession containing five or more persons or two or more vehicles, shall occupy, march or proceed along any street or sidewalk so as to obstruct or slow the normal flow of traffic, except in accordance with a parade permit issued by the city council or the chief of police. The city council and the chief of police may impose reasonable conditions upon such permit regarding scope, date, time, location, and security requirements.
(Code 1977, § 24-5; Ord. No. O-01-83, § 1, 12-3-01)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 74.
Sec. 82-6. - Same—Revocation of permit.
Any permit issued under the provisions of section 82-5 may be revoked by the chief of police for the violation by the permittee of any applicable provision of this Code, state law or city ordinance.
(Code 1977, § 24-6)
Sec. 82-7. - Use of coasters, roller skates and similar devices restricted.
(a)
No person upon roller skates, or riding in or by means of any non-motorized coaster, toy vehicle or similar device shall go upon any roadway except while crossing a street or on a crosswalk and, when so crossing, such person shall be granted all the rights and shall be subject to all of the duties applicable to pedestrians. There shall be no such skating or riding on the sidewalks of and within the business district, but any person may so skate or ride upon the sidewalks outside the business district.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for persons so skating or riding to gather in crowds, or to create noise or be guilty of any disorderly conduct, or to take hold of or hang on to any automobile or other vehicle, or in any way to interfere with the natural progress of automobiles or other vehicles along and in the streets of the city, or for two or more persons to join hands while skating on the sidewalks.
(c)
Persons so skating or riding shall give the right-of-way to, and in no way interfere with, persons walking on the sidewalks.
(d)
The parent of any child and the guardian of any ward shall not authorize or knowingly permit any such child or ward to violate any of the provisions of this section.
(Code 1977, § 24-7; Ord. No. O-2019-46 , § 4, 9-3-19)
Sec. 82-8. - Riding on portion of vehicles not intended for passengers.
No person shall ride on any vehicle upon any portion thereof not designated or designed or intended for the use of passengers. This provision shall not apply to any employee engaged in the necessary discharge of a duty, or to persons riding within truck bodies in space intended for merchandise.
(Code 1977, § 24-8)

Sec. 82-10. - Dropping loads along roadway prohibited.
It shall be unlawful to operate any vehicle so loaded or constructed that any part of its load is dropped along or upon the roadway in the city.
(Ord. No. O-00-32, § 1, 3-20-00)
Sec. 82-11. - Speed limit, driving past persons working on streets.
The maximum rate of speed shall be 15 miles per hour when approaching or driving past persons working in the street who are city employees, public utility employees, or those granted permission of the city, provided such workers have erected a sign reading: "Men Working," or such sign as may be prescribed by the chief of police, and such sign is in accordance with the specifications of the chief of police.
(Code 1977, § 24-11)
Sec. 82-12. - Unlawful spinning of vehicle tires.
No person shall start a vehicle intentionally where the power of acceleration would cause the tires to spin on the surface of the roadway, driveway or parking lot.
(Code 1977, § 24-12)
Sec. 82-13. - Right-of-way of funeral procession.
(a)
Vehicles of a funeral procession shall have the right-of-way over all vehicles, except authorized emergency vehicles, provided each shall identify itself by burning regular headlights, shall keep in close formation, and shall display a windshield sticker carrying the word "funeral."
(b)
Vehicles of a funeral procession complying with the above provisions may proceed through intersections with traffic control signals without observing such traffic control signals.
(Code 1977, § 24-13)
Sec. 82-14. - Driving through processions prohibited.
No driver of a vehicle shall drive between the vehicles comprising a funeral or other procession while they are in motion and when such vehicles are conspicuously designated as required in this chapter.
(Code 1977, § 24-14)
Sec. 82-15. - Driving in a procession.
Each driver in a funeral or other procession shall drive as near to the right-hand edge of the roadway as practicable and shall follow the vehicle ahead as close as practicable and safe.
(Code 1977, § 24-15)
Sec. 82-16. - Reserved.

Sec. 82-17. - Stop before entering intersection when traffic obstructed.
No driver shall enter an intersection or a marked crosswalk unless there is sufficient space on the other side of the intersection or crosswalk to accommodate the vehicle he is operating without obstructing the passage of other vehicles or pedestrians notwithstanding any traffic control signal indication to proceed.
(Code 1977, § 24-17)
Sec. 82-18. - Unattended vehicles.
No person having control or charge of a motor vehicle shall allow such vehicle to stand on any street unattended without first setting the brakes thereon and stopping the motor of such vehicle and, when standing upon a perceptible grade, without turning the wheels of such vehicle to the curb or the side of the street or highway.
(Code 1977, § 24-18)
Sec. 82-19. - Parking for certain purposes prohibited.
No person shall park, stop, or stand a vehicle upon any street or roadway for the purpose of:
(1)
Displaying such vehicle for sale.
(2)
Washing, greasing, storing or repairing such vehicle except repairs necessitated by a sudden emergency, and in such emergency such vehicle shall be moved or towed away with all due haste.
(3)
Displaying advertising.
(4)
Selling or peddling of any goods, wares, or merchandise contained therein or thereon except in conjunction with a peddler's permit or a transient merchant permit.
(Code 1977, § 24-19; Ord. No. O-2019-46 , § 5, 9-3-19)

Sec. 82-24. - Parking restrictions.
The city council or the police chief shall designate all regulations or prohibitions concerning stopping, standing or parking of motor vehicles with respect to streets, roads and highways under its jurisdiction as allowed under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200 et seq., as may be amended. The city shall cause such regulations and prohibitions to be posted in clear view on the applicable street. Any vehicle found to be in violation of such regulations and prohibitions may be cited, impounded or immobilized.
(Ord. No. O-91-2, § 2(24-25), 2-18-91; Ord. No. O-2019-46 , § 6, 9-3-19)
Sec. 82-25. - Driving without proper equipment.
No person shall operate a motor vehicle upon the public roads, streets or highways within the city without the proper equipment thereon which is required by any city ordinance or any statute of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. O-2012-24, § 1, 6-4-12)
Sec. 82-26. - Driving too fast for conditions.
No person shall operate a motor vehicle upon the public roads, streets or highways within the city in a manner that is too fast for the conditions of that road, street or highway, as related to traffic, road conditions, weather, darkness or otherwise.
(Ord. No. O-2012-24, § 2, 6-4-12)
