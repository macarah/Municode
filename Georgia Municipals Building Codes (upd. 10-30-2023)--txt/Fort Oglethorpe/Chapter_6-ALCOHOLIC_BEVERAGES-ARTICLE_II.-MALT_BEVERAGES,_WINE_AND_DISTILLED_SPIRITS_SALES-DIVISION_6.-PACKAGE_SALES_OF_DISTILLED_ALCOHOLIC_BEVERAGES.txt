DIVISION 6. - PACKAGE SALES OF DISTILLED ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES

Sec. 6-135. - Definitions.
All definitions set forth in the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code (O.C.G.A. Title 3) and State of Georgia regulations, as amended are hereby adopted by this division. The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this division, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means the person, persons, or entity who files forms designated by the city requesting to be issued a privilege license to sell packaged distilled spirits by either wholesale or retail within the city.
Application means the forms an applicant is required to file in order apply to obtain a license to sell packaged distilled spirits in the city.
Bottle means any container, vessel, bottle, or other receptacle used for holding alcoholic beverages. "Unsealed bottle" means any bottle with the original seal cork, cap, or other enclosing device that is either broken, removed, or which the federal revenue strip stamp has been broken.
Church means a permanent building located in an area designated for such use by the zoning ordinances where persons regularly assemble for religious worship, that is publicly designated as a church, but does not include residences or place of businesses also used for religious purposes.
City means the City of Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia.
Enforcement officer means a certified City of Fort Oglethorpe Police Officer or other individual who, through specialized knowledge and training, is employed by the city to regulate and enforce compliance with city ordinances.
Distance means the distance established by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
Family member means any person related within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity to the licensee or applicant for a package distilled spirits license.
Federal statutes mean the statutes of the United States now in effect or as may hereinafter be modified.
License means the authorization by the mayor and council to engage in the sale of packaged distilled spirits in the city.
Licensee means a person, real or artificial, authorized by the mayor and council to engage in the sale of packaged distilled spirits in the city.
Person means any private individual, partnership, joint venture, corporation, limited liability company or any other business entity or association.
Person underage means an individual under the age of 21 years.
Packaged distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage with an alcohol volume greater than 24 percent sold or transferred at wholesale or retail in any container, vessel, bottle, or other receptacle used for holding alcoholic beverages.
Packaged distilled spirits retailer means a person licensed by the mayor and council to sell packaged distilled spirits in unbroken packages for consumption off premises.
Premises means the property owned, leased, or controlled by the licensee, including but not limited to the building and the parking areas surrounding it, where packaged distilled spirits are sold.
Quality cigars means the sale of handmade cigars rated 85 or above by Cigar Aficionado in the sizes of petite corona, corona, robusto, corona, toro, guordo, panatela, lonsdale, churchill, lancero, double Corona and grand corona.
Retail sale means selling or offering for sale packaged distilled spirits to any member of the public.
School means only such state, county, city, church, or other schools that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state.
State statutes, rules and regulations means all applicable statutes, rules, and regulations of the State of Georgia applicable to the sale of packaged distilled spirits as may now be in effect or which may be amended hereinafter.
Wholesale means the sale of packaged distilled spirits to any person for the purpose of resale.
Words importing the masculine gender shall include the feminine and neuter, and the singular shall include the plural.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-136. - Application and issuance of license.
Every person desiring to obtain a license for the retail or wholesale sale of packaged distilled spirits in the city shall be required under this division to make formal written application to the mayor and council for such privilege, which shall be signed by the applicant and/or agent, if applicable, upon forms to be prepared and provided by the city. All such applicants and agents shall furnish all reasonable data, information and records requested of them by the city. Failure to furnish such data, information, and records within 30 days from the date of a written request from the city for such information shall automatically serve to dismiss the application. Applicants, or those having an interest in the application, by filing an application, agree to produce any such data, information, and records for review by enforcement officers and the mayor and council. Applicants, or those having an interest in the application, by filing an application, agree to submit to an oral interrogation by enforcement officers or by the mayor and council if considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to an application. The failure to produce such applicant or person of interest within 30 days after being requested in writing to do so shall result in the automatic dismissal of the application. The application must identify the name of the person conducting the business at the premises address contained in the application.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-137. - False information.
Any material omission or untrue or misleading information contained in or left out of an original or renewal application for a license to sell packaged distilled spirits in the city shall be cause for the denial thereof. If any license has previously been granted based on such misleading statements or material omissions, such shall constitute cause for the revocation of the license.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-138. - Processing fee.
Upon filing an application for a license to sell packaged distilled spirits at wholesale or retail in the city with the city clerk, the city shall require the applicant to pay a non-refundable fee to cover the cost of processing the application in the amount of $100.00. Said fee may be amended by resolution of the mayor and council as shall be set from time to time. No application shall be processed without payment of fee.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-139. - Payment of taxes and other debts due to city.
The city shall cause an inquiry to be made into the city tax records to determine if an applicant for a license to sell packaged distilled spirits at wholesale or retail in the city, or other party with an interest in the application, has any outstanding taxes or special assessments that are delinquent against his property or any other monies owing to the city. No license shall be issued or renewed until such debts are paid in full.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-140. - Issuance date.
Upon an application for a license to sell packaged distilled spirits at wholesale or retail in the city being granted by the mayor and council, a license shall be issued to the applicant as of the date the applicant commences his alcoholic beverage business and upon the payment by the applicant of the license fee.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-141. - Notification of denial.
The denial of an application for a license to sell packaged distilled spirits at wholesale or retail in the city shall be in writing, with the reasons therefore stated, and it shall be mailed or delivered to the applicant at the address of the applicant contained on the application by first class mail. Any applicant aggrieved by the decision of the mayor and council regarding the denial of a license shall be afforded a hearing with an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine opposing witnesses as provided herein.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-142. - Acceptance and consideration of application after rejection or revocation.
When any application for a retail or wholesale package distilled spirits license is denied for cause, or any license is revoked for cause by the mayor and council, the city clerk shall not accept or consider any application by such applicant for a license to operate the same type of business for a period of 24 months from the date of a license denial and 60 months from the date of a revocation of license in the event of a revocation for cause.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-143. - Approval of applications for sites under construction.
An application for a proposed retail package distilled spirits license may be approved by the mayor and council prior to the applicant obtaining an occupancy permit for a premise if the premises is currently, or will be, under construction. If approved by the mayor and council, the city shall not issue the license until the applicant has obtained an occupancy permit and all other approvals required by the state and city for building code purposes, and the applicant has advised the city in writing of the date on which the business shall commence, which date shall not exceed 12 months from the date of application approval. The license shall be dated as of such commencement date and shall not be effective until that date. If the applicant fails to obtain an occupancy permit within 12 months of the date of approval of the application to sell packaged distilled spirits at retail in the city, the applicant shall forfeit his application which shall, by virtue of failure to obtain an occupancy permit, be rejected without the necessity of any further action of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-144. - Time limit for commencement of business.
Except for a license issued to a site under construction, or soon to be under construction, an applicant must open the retail package distilled spirits business in the establishment referred to in the application within six months after its approval by mayor and council. The city clerk shall not issue the license until presented with the applicant's notarized statement stating the applicant is in fact the owner of the business and will open for business on a date certain. The license shall be dated as of the date certain in the affidavit and shall not be effective until that date. Failure to open the licensed establishment within the six-month period or the date stated in the applicant's affidavit shall serve an automatic forfeiture and cancellation of the unused license, and no refund of fees shall be made.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-145. - Time limit for closed business.
Any holder of a license issued under this division, who shall begin the operation of the business authorized in the license, but who shall for a period of six consecutive months thereafter cease to operate the business as authorized in the license, shall, upon completion of the six-month period, automatically forfeit his license. Said license shall, by virtue of the failure to operate, be revoked without the necessity of any further action of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-146. - Interest in other licenses by wholesalers.
No person who has any direct financial interest in a license for the sale of package distilled spirits at wholesale shall hold any other license or an interest in any license under the terms of this division.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-147. - Licenses required for sale of alcoholic beverages at retail.
It shall be lawful for a licensee to sell packaged distilled spirits at retail at a designated premises location in the city provided that such sales are made in strict compliance with all federal statutes, all state laws, rules and regulations, and all provisions of this division, and provided that such licensee has a valid and duly issued privilege license from the city permitting him or her to sell same. Transfer of ownership or possession of any retail or wholesale packaged distilled spirits license is prohibited, void and invalid.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-148. - Licensee responsible for officers and agents.
Each licensee shall be responsible for all acts of such licensee as well as the acts of the licensee's officers, employees, agents, and representatives. Any violation of this division by any officer, employee, agent, or representative of a licensee shall constitute a violation of this division by such licensee.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-149. - Applicant citizenship, age, and residency requirements.
Citizenship, age, and residency requirements for an applicant for a license to engage in the sale of packaged distilled spirits shall be as follows:
(1)
If the applicant is a person, the person shall be a United States citizen, or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residency, a resident of the city, and at least 21 years of age; provided, however, the person applicant need not be a city resident if the person designates a resident of the city who is at least 21 years of age as his or her agent who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(2)
If the applicant is a partnership, corporation, limited liability company, or other legal entity, this section shall apply to all its partners, officers, managers, and principal owners. If a corporation or limited liability company, the license shall be issued to the corporation or limited liability company. Where the principal owner or member is not a resident of the city, the corporation or limited liability company shall designate an individual as its agent who must be a resident of the city and at least 21 years of age who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license. For a partnership, the license shall be issued to one of the partners. If a partner is not a resident of the city, the partnership must designate a city resident at least 21 years of age as the partnership's agent who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(3)
Any change in any relationship declared in this division must be filed, when said change is made, with the Fort Oglethorpe Police Chief, or his designee, and failure to do so within a period of 30 days after such change is made shall be grounds for revocation of a license granted under this division by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-150. - Applicant disqualification.
No application for any license required under this division shall be granted where the application, investigation or the evidence presented at a hearing before the mayor and council shows any of the following conditions to exist:
(1)
The applicant, principal owner, or his agent has a conviction, as defined in section 6-150(5), or does not have sufficient mental capacity to conduct the business for which application is made or who has been dishonorably discharged from the armed services of the United States.
(2)
The applicant or any agent, stockholder, partner, or member of the applicant has had any license issued by the city previously revoked within 36 months of the date of the application.
(3)
The applicant or his agent, as a previous holder of a license to sell any type of alcoholic beverages in any jurisdiction, has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business within a 36-month period immediately preceding the date of the application. In the event of a renewal application, offences prosecuted and resolved pursuant to this division shall not be considered.
(4)
The applicant's proposed business premise is not properly zoned for the license applied for.
(5)
Persons with prior felony criminal convictions.
a.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
1.
"Convictions" include an adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere, a guilty plea under a first offender statute, or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a felony;
2.
"Principal owner" refers to an individual owning, directly or indirectly, a five percent or more interest in a corporation, partnership, limited partnership, limited liability company or other entity.
b.
No license for the sale of packaged distilled spirits shall be issued to any individual, partnership, limited partnership, corporation or limited liability company where such individual or any individual who is a principal owner of any partnership, limited partnership, corporation, limited liability company or other entity, or who is the agent responsible for matters relating to the license shall have been convicted under any federal, state or local law of any misdemeanor involving sexual misconduct, dishonesty, possession or use of a controlled substance, or possession of drug paraphernalia within ten years prior to the filing of the application for such license; or, convicted under any federal state or local law of any felony within ten years prior to the filing of the application for such license.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-151. - Transferability.
(a)
No license for the sale of packaged distilled spirits shall be transferable, except as otherwise provided in this section.
(b)
If an individual licensee dies, the licensee's estate shall be allowed to continue to sell packaged distilled spirits for a period of 60 days from the date of death, or until expiration of the license, or until approval of a new licensee, whichever shall first occur. However, no sale of alcoholic beverages shall be allowed until such time as a personal representative of the estate, appointed by a probate court of competent jurisdiction, shall make application with the city clerk for authorization to continue to sell for such period.
(c)
If a licensee or responsible person, other than the owner, severs such licensees association with a licensed establishment, the establishment may continue to sell packaged distilled spirits for a period of 60 days from the date of such severance, provided a new application is filed with the city clerk within ten days of such severance, and indicates that there has been no change in the ownership of the licensed establishment. If approved by the mayor and council, the term of the new license shall be for the remaining term of the original license and the original license shall terminate as of the date of such approval. No additional license fees, other than administrative fee set by the mayor and council by resolution from time to time, shall be required from the applicant.
(d)
Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license to withdraw from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license and who meet the qualifications of a licensee under this article. This subsection shall not prohibit the transfer of stock between persons who held stock in the corporation at the time of issuance of the license, nor shall it prohibit transfers of stock which do not result in any person increasing such person's holdings by a total of ten percent or more of any class stock.
(e)
Except as provided in this section, any change in the ownership of any entity granted a license shall cancel and revoke any license issued automatically, without the necessity of any hearing.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-152. - Surrender of licenses.
(a)
Any licensee authorized to engage in the sale of packaged distilled spirits at specifically described premises shall be required to surrender the license held without demand, except as otherwise provided in this division, upon any of the following events: Any sale or transfer of an interest in the business of the licensee, whether by an actual sale of any of the licensee's stock or proprietorship or partnership interest to any unregistered person not identified in the licensee's application or upon a sale of all or substantially all of the licensee's assets to any unregistered person. For purposes of this subsection, the term "unregistered person" shall mean any individual, corporation, partnership, limited partnership, club, or association, not shown upon any current application for the specific premises involved. For purposes of this subsection, a sale of all or substantially all the licensee's assets shall include or be deemed to include any bulk sale of inventory or assets at any specifically described premises for which the license holder is licensed to engage in the sale of packaged distilled spirits in the city. For purposes of this subsection, any corporation or limited partnership or association whose stock is traded on any stock exchange recognized by the United States Securities and Exchange Commission shall be excepted from this requirement to the extent of any transfer of shareholding or partnership interests not exceeding five percent in any one trade or transaction.
(b)
Time limit. Surrender as provided in this chapter shall be made to the city clerk within 30 days of any event requiring surrender of the license.
(c)
Fines for failure to surrender. For failure to surrender the license within the period provided in subsection (b) of this section, the applicant shall be fined an amount as follows:
(1)
The sum of $50.00 for failure to surrender within 30 days of the date that any surrender of the alcoholic beverage license is required: and
(2)
An additional sum of $10.00 per day for each day thereafter from which the licensee fails or defaults in the surrender of the license after the date upon which the surrender is required.
(d)
The city clerk shall assess any penalty against any licensee for failure to surrender the license as required by this section. Notice of assessment shall be by certified letter to the licensee based upon information and belief of the city clerk stating the date that any required surrender should have been made by the licensee. The licensee shall have a period of ten days from the date of receipt of the certified letter from the city clerk of the notice of assessment of the penalty to protest the assessment to the city clerk by presenting evidence or information in writing to the city clerk as to the licensee's position. If the city clerk shall not agree with the licensee's position, the licensee shall have ten days from the date of the written notice of the city clerk's written decision disagreeing with the licensee's protest to request in writing a hearing before the mayor and council. The city clerk shall forward the protest to the mayor and council who shall at its next regular meeting accept, reject, or modify the assessment of the penalty.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-153. - Expiration upon delinquency.
A license issued under this article shall expire automatically and without notice upon the occurrence of a delinquency of 20 days in:
(1)
Filing any required monthly report under this chapter; or
(2)
Paying over any required excise tax levied under this chapter or any other indebtedness owed to the city.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-154. - Revocation of state license.
Whenever the state department of revenue shall revoke any permit or license to sell at wholesale or retail any packaged distilled spirits, any license issued under this division for the same licensed premises shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any action by the mayor and council, or any city officer, and the licensee shall not be entitled to any refund of any license fee theretofore paid to the city.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-155. - Suspension in emergency.
The mayor and the chief of police are each delegated the authority to suspend any license issued under this division for due cause in any emergency situation, and the suspension shall be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until a hearing before the mayor and council is held pursuant to section 6-157, section 6-158, and section 6-159.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-156. - Other licenses.
When the mayor or chief of police find a violation of any section of this division for which the licensee shall have his packaged distilled spirits license suspended or revoked, all alcoholic beverage licenses of the licensee under this article shall be so suspended or revoked.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-157. - Hearing process.
(a)
Due cause hearing; defined; cost of hearing.
(1)
No license which has been issued or which may be issued pursuant to this article shall be suspended or revoked, except as provided in section 6-155, except for due cause and after a hearing as provided in this division.
(b)
The term "due cause" for the purposes of this chapter of the Code shall include, but not be limited to:
(1)
Conviction of, or the entering of a plea of guilty, plea of first offender, or plea of nolo contendere by the licensee or any person holding an interest in the license for any felony, any law, administrative regulation, or ordinance involving alcoholic beverages, gambling or narcotics, or tax laws.
(2)
Conviction of, or the entering of a plea of guilty, plea of first offender, or plea of nolo contendere by the licensee or any person holding an interest in the license for any sex offense, or when the licensee permits on-premises consumption.
(3)
Material falsification of any fact given in an application for a license issued under this division or bearing upon the licensee's qualification therefore. Any act, which may be construed as a subterfuge to circumvent any of the qualifications for a license under this division.
(4)
Violation of any provision of this division, or any failure to meet or maintain any standard or regulation prescribed by this division as a condition or qualification for holding a license.
(5)
Any other factor known to or discovered by the city whereby it is objectively shown the licensee, any of the licensee's employees or any person holding an interest in a license, has engaged in conduct at or involving the licensed business or has permitted conduct on the licensed premises that constitutes a violation of federal or state law, local ordinance or administrative regulations involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, narcotics or sex offenses. Same shall create a rebuttable presumption that the violative act was done with the knowledge or consent of the licensee.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-158. - Notice of violation and hearing.
The city clerk shall notify the licensee of any charge of conduct or offense subject to penalty, suspension or revocation or any combination thereof. If the potential sanction is suspension or revocation the notice shall also state the place and time the mayor and council will hear the charges against the licensee. The notice may also contain such additional information as the city clerk may deem appropriate. The notice shall be delivered to the licensee at least five days prior to the hearing date, by personal delivery, or by first class mail addressed to the licensee at the address contained in licensee's application, or by posting the notice on the front door of the licensed premises. In the case of delivery by first class mail the notice shall be deemed delivered two days after being deposited into the U.S. mail properly addressed and with adequate postage.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-159. - Hearing.
(a)
The mayor and council shall conduct a hearing on the record on any revocation or suspension of a packaged distilled spirits license. The licensee shall be allowed to appear at said hearing and to present evidence and cross-examine witnesses. The mayor and council shall notify the licensee of its decision as to revocation or suspension of the license at such hearing or in writing no later than 30 days after such hearing. Upon a finding by the mayor and council that sufficient evidence exists to support the revocation or suspension, the mayor and council may revoke or suspend the license of the licensee for up to a period not to exceed 60 months. Failure of the mayor and council to notify the licensee of its determination at such hearing or in writing no later than 30 days after such hearing shall result in a dismissal of the charge.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-161. - No refund of fee.
When an alcoholic beverage license is revoked, suspended or expires, the city shall not be required to refund any portion of the license fee.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-162. - Removal of signs and alcoholic beverages.
When any license for selling packaged distilled spirits is revoked, suspended, or automatically expires, all signs indicating that such packaged distilled spirits may be sold or purchased at that premises shall be removed from the premises, both outside and inside.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-163. - Maximum number of licenses authorized.
(1)
The number of packaged distilled spirits licenses for the retail sale of packaged distilled spirits at designated locations in the city, issued under this division and outstanding within the City of Fort Oglethorpe, shall be limited to three licenses, with one such license issued for district A, one such license issued for district B, and one such license issued for district C.
(2)
With respect to the issuance of the first three retail sale of distilled spirits licenses, if more than one properly completed application is submitted to the city for a respective district and approved by the mayor and council at the time of the publicly announced deadline for applications has expired, the city will then have a lottery drawing for each respective district where more than one properly completed application is approved by the mayor and council. An applicant may submit an application for more than one district, but in the event the applicant's application is drawn in the lottery, all other applications of that applicant shall become null and void. In the event a district does not receive an application by the deadline mentioned above, the city will then accept additional properly completed applications for an additional 30 days in that respective district with a lottery to follow if there are more than one properly completed applications approved by the mayor and council for that district. If no applications are received during the second period for that district the license will then be awarded to the first qualified applicant. In the event of a surrender, suspension for more than 180 days, or the revocation of a retail distilled spirits license, the city will hold a lottery drawing for the vacant license with such lottery being held not less than 60 days from the effective date of such suspension, surrender, termination or revocation. With respect to such lottery, the city shall issue public notice for the submission of applications not less than 30 days prior to the date of the lottery drawing. For purposes of this section, the effective date shall be the date upon which all appeals, if any, are no longer available to a licensee or have otherwise been exhausted.
(3)
Any lottery conducted under this division, if more than one district is being licensed, shall be conducted in the following order: District A, then district B, then district C.
(4)
If an applicant that is selected by lottery fails to complete the process required by the city to obtain a license to sell packaged distilled spirits within the time limits set forth in this division, then the city will select by lottery another applicant from the previous pool of properly submitted applications.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-164. - License districts.
(a)
There is hereby created within the city three districts in which only one retail sale of packaged distilled spirits license may be issued per district.
(1)
District A shall be designated as containing those eligible properties that have road frontage access directly along Battlefield Parkway from Fort Town Drive to the City limits at Pine Grove Road.
(2)
District B shall be designated as containing those eligible properties that have road frontage access directly from Highway 41 at the state line to Cloud Springs Road and road frontage access along Cloud Springs Road to Roadway Drive.
(3)
District C shall be designated as containing those eligible properties that have road frontage access directly from Highway 27 at Cloud Springs Road to Gilbert Drive and road frontage access from Highway 27 along Battlefield Parkway to Fant Drive.
(4)
A map depicting said districts shall be maintained in the city clerk's office at city hall.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-165. - Approval of mayor and council.
(a)
No retail or wholesale packaged distilled spirits license shall be issued until it has been approved by the mayor and council.
(b)
No retail or wholesale packaged distilled spirits license shall be approved unless all sections of this division, state laws, rules and regulations have been met by the applicant.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-166. - Grant or privilege.
Every license issued under this article shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on such business during the term of the license, subject to all the terms and conditions imposed by this division and related laws, applicable sections of state law, rules and regulations and other city ordinances and resolutions relating to such business.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-167. - Holding more than one retail license.
A packaged distilled spirits licensee may hold more than one type of retail license, including but not limited to wine, malt beverage and pouring license, provided that each license must be approved by the mayor and council. Licensees seeking more than one licenses for an individual premise must meet the requirements of each license.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-168. - Restrictions of multiple class licensees.
It shall be unlawful for a licensee to engage in any activity pertaining to the sale of alcoholic beverages except as authorized by the type of license held.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-169. - Limitations on use.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person operating a premise licensed for the retail sale of wine and malt beverages to store or have on the premises any packaged distilled spirits for retail sales without a license therefore.
(b)
Holders of a pouring distilled spirits license shall not be permitted to sell packaged distilled spirits.
(c)
Packaged distilled spirits licensees shall not be permitted to sell packaged distilled spirits for home delivery.
(d)
Packaged distilled spirits licensees shall not be permitted to sell packaged distilled spirits through a drive through window, door, or other convenience.
(e)
Packaged distilled spirits licensees shall not be permitted to operate, store, or maintain power coin operated amusements machines in the business premises licensed to sell by retail packaged distilled spirits. No form of entertainment, including pinball machines, lottery, bingo, music machines or similar devices, shall be permitted in any premises used for packaged retail distilled spirits sales. No seating facilities, other than for employees, shall be permitted.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-170. - Display of license.
Each licensee for the sale of packaged distilled spirits shall have his license posted conspicuously in the place of business. The failure of a licensee to display the license conspicuously in his place of business shall be a violation of this division.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-171. - Premises for retail distilled spirits sales.
(a)
A license issued for the retail package sales of distilled spirits shall be valid only for the operation of said purpose upon the property proposed in the licensee's application and any change of location shall be cause for revocation of the license.
(b)
No retail package distilled spirits license shall be issued for the operation of same where such would be a prohibited use under the city's zoning ordinance in effect at time of issuance.
(c)
No license for the sale of packaged distilled spirits at retail shall be issued for a location where the proposed place of business is located on property the owner of which has any direct or indirect interest or association, including ownership, financial or governing, in any other property within the City of Fort Oglethorpe that operates another retail or wholesale packaged distilled spirits business. For purposes of this section, "owner" shall mean and include an individual, partner, corporation, company, association, or other business entity.
(d)
To assure that these requirements are satisfied, no original or renewal license shall be issued for any location until a majority of the members of the city council have approved the proposed location as being suitable for the retail sale of packaged distilled spirits after a consideration of this matter at a properly advertised public meeting of the city council.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-172. - Limitations on structure containing retail package distilled spirit businesses.
(a)
The physical locations designated by an applicant for use in the sale by retail of packaged distilled spirits shall be a newly constructed free-standing building. The front of the building must have a brick, stone, or stucco facade. The remaining exterior elevations must meet applicable City of Fort Oglethorpe UDC requirements. Retail packaged distilled spirits premises shall not be made from or out of a manufactured, movable or any prefabricated type of building.
(b)
The minimum square footage of the premises display area within a structure shall be 5,000 square feet. Excess storage, not including display areas, must be no less than 500 square feet within the structure but separate from the retail display floor. No separate modules or buildings are allowed, and no storage is permitted outside.
(c)
All premises in the city used for the retail sale of packaged distilled spirits shall be located on a ground floor level. Each premises shall have only one main entrance for use by the public as a means of ingress and egress for the purpose of purchasing packaged distilled spirits at retail. Notwithstanding, when a premise is located on the corner of two public streets such premises may maintain a door opening on each of the public streets.
(d)
All premises used by a licensee for the retail sale of packaged distilled spirits shall have night lights surrounding the premises and shall be equipped with a functioning security system, which includes a burglar alarm system on the inside of the premises and at least one indoor and one outdoor camera with video storage capability. Security bars or other types of window or door obstructions are prohibited.
(e)
The windows in such premises of licensee shall not be obstructed or obscured by any object, including, but not limited to signage, shelves, inventory, or displays and shall be arranged so that passersby and law enforcement personnel on the street can view the premises by looking through windows opening onto the streets. This requirement shall apply to all sides of structures facing streets. Proposed plans and drawings must be furnished to the mayor and council as a part of the application. All plans for such premises must receive approval from the mayor and council prior to the commencement of construction.
(f)
All premises utilized by a licensee for the purpose of retail packaged distilled spirits sales shall be subject to applicable zoning, building, and city land development regulations.
(g)
A license issued under this division for a retail package distilled spirits sales location shall be valid only for the operation of such business upon the property proposed in the application and any change of location shall be cause for revocation of the license.
(h)
No license shall be issued for the operation of a retail packaged distilled spirits business where such premises would be a prohibited use under the city's zoning ordinance in effect at time of issuance.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-173. - Inspection of licensed establishments.
Sworn officers of the police department and code enforcement officers shall have the authority to inspect establishments licensed under this division during the hours in which the premises are open for business. These inspections shall be made for the purpose of verifying compliance with the requirements of this division and state law. This section is not intended to limit the authority of any other city officer to conduct inspections authorized by other sections of the Fort Oglethorpe Code of Ordinances or state law.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-174. - Annual fee.
An annual retail and wholesale packaged distilled spirits license fee shall be paid in advance on or before January 15th of each year, in the sum of $5,000.00. Any new license granted during a calendar year shall not be prorated for the remainder of the calendar year.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-175. - Distilled spirits tasting.
(a)
A distilled spirit tasting permit for purposes of this section shall be limited to a person possessing a current license from the city for the retail sale of packaged distilled spirits and a valid current distilled spirits license from the state.
(b)
Any tasting occurring on the premises of a business possessing a tasting permit shall be limited to an area that is separated from the retail area of the premises by walls or other partitions that prohibit pedestrian traffic through the tasting area.
(c)
An eligible licensee may petition the city for a tasting permit provided the licensee holds a valid license for the sale of packaged distilled spirits issued by the city.
(d)
A tasting permit shall allow the licensee to offer or sell samples in connection with an instructional or educational promotion and the samples must be consumed in the presence of a representative of the licensed business.
(e)
A tasting permit shall allow the permittee to offer 26 tasting events per year, for no more than one time per day, for a maximum of four hours, and only during the hours the licensed premises may lawfully sell unbroken packages of distilled spirits.
(f)
A tasting event may only include one type of distilled spirit per event, may include more than one brand of each type of distilled spirit, and no more than four brands may be open at any one time during the tasting event.
(g)
A tasting permit shall be subject to all laws, rules and regulations of the city and state, including O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26 and Rule 560-2-5-05 of the state department of revenue, alcohol, and tobacco division, and shall be subject to suspension or revocation for violation thereof.
(h)
Said tasting permit need only be applied for once and may be automatically renew when said packaged distilled spirits license is renewed. Provided, however, that the city may revoke or suspend such tasting permit and/or impose such conditions on its operation at the city's discretion for violation of this division or in furtherance of the health, safety, and welfare of the city's inhabitants.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-176. - Availability of prices and penal sections.
Each retail licensee for the sale of distilled spirits shall conspicuously display within the interior of the licensed premises not less than four copies of a printed price list for the packaged distilled spirits offered for sale. However, a licensee, in lieu of having four copies of a printed list, may have the price placed on the bottles or on the bottom of the shelf where beverages are exhibited for sale.
No price or prices for the retail sale of distilled spirits shall be displayed in any show window or any place visible from the outside the premises where retail packaged distilled spirits are sold.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
State Law reference— See O.C.G.A. § 3-4-26.
Sec. 6-177. - Compliance with division by wholesalers.
All wholesalers licensed under this division shall file a certified statement with the city of their gross sales in the city for the preceding calendar month on or before the 20th of the following month.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-178. - Sales and deliveries by wholesalers.
Distilled spirits deliveries and sales by wholesalers under this division shall only be made to retailers that are properly licensed for the operation of retail packaged distilled spirits sales in the city. Deliveries shall be made in a conveyance owned and operated by the licensed wholesaler. Deliveries of inventory shall only occur only at the rear of the premises through the designated rear entrance to the premises.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-179. - Retailers to purchase from licensed wholesalers.
Retail licensees under this division shall not buy or accept deliveries of packaged distilled spirits from wholesalers not licensed under this division. Retail licensees under this division shall only accept deliveries of packaged distilled spirits to the premises for which the license is issued and by no means other than a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesaler licensed as required by this division. However, by written request to the mayor and council and upon the granting of permission by the mayor and council in writing, deliveries may, in special instances, be made otherwise upon the terms and conditions as prescribed by the mayor and council as to each such delivery.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-180. - Sale untaxed spirits.
It shall be a violation of this division for any person to sell at retail within the city any distilled spirits on which the taxes provided for in this division have not been paid.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-181. - Levied.
In addition to all other taxes or license fees imposed upon retail dealers engaged in the city in the business of selling distilled spirits, as defined in this chapter, there is imposed and levied upon all such licensees an excise tax, to be computed and collected as set forth in this division.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-182. - Duties of wholesaler.
Each wholesale dealer or distributor selling, shipping, or delivering distilled spirits to any retail packaged distilled spirits licensee in the city shall, as a condition of the privilege of carrying on such business in the city:
(1)
Keep true and correct records of all sales, shipments, or deliveries of such distilled spirits to each retail licensee in the city. Such records shall be preserved for a period of two years and shall be made available on request for the inspection of any duly authorized city representative.
(2)
Collect from each such retail licensee in the city, at the time of delivery of the distilled spirits, the amount of tax due under this division and hold the money in trust for the city until such amount is remitted to the city as provided in subsection (3) of this section.
(3)
On or before the 20th day of each calendar month, make a verified and comprehensive report to the city which shall correctly show all sales and deliveries of distilled spirits to or for each retail licensee in the city for the month immediately preceding the report. The report shall show the name and address of each retail licensee, quantities delivered to each retail licensee, the amount of tax collected under the terms of this division and such other information as may be called for by the city. The report shall be accompanied by remittance to the city for all taxes collected or due as shown on the report. There is assessed a ten percent late charge on the gross tax due for the month. Delinquent amounts shall bear interest at an annual rate equal to the bank prime loan rate as posted by the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System in statistical release H. 15 or any publication that may supersede it, plus three percent, to accrue monthly. Such annual interest rate shall be determined for each calendar year based on the first weekly posting of statistical release of H. 15 on or after January 1st of each calendar year.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-183. - Unlawful deliveries.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this division for any person to deliver any distilled spirits to any retail licensee in the city without collecting the taxes provided for in this division at the time of delivery.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-184. - Noncompliance by wholesale dealer, distributor.
If any wholesale dealer or distributor fails or refuses to make the reports provided for in this division, the city shall notify the licensee in writing. If the reports are not made and the taxes remitted within five days from the date of the notice, the city may withdraw from the wholesale dealer or distributor the privilege of doing business in the city by revoking the license.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-185. - Frequency of payment.
The tax imposed in this division shall be computable and payable monthly.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-186. - Rate.
The tax levied under this division shall be computed on the basis of $0.22 cents per liter, and a proportionate tax on all fractional parts of distilled spirits sold or delivered.
State Law reference— See O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-187. - False report.
The filing of a false or fraudulent report under this division shall render the wholesale dealer making such report liable for a penalty equal to ten percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report. In addition to other penalties provided in this division, the failure to make a timely report or remittance or the filing of a false or fraudulent report under this division shall also constitute grounds for the revocation of the business license issued by the city to a wholesale dealer.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-188. - Action for tax; tax credit; penalty or interest paid more than once or erroneously or illegally collected.
At any time within three years after any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected pursuant to this division becomes due and payable, and at any time within three years after the delinquency of any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected, the mayor and council may bring an action in the courts of this state, any other state, or of the United States in the name of the city to collect the amount delinquent, together with penalties and interest, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees, and other legal fees incident thereto. Whenever the amount of any tax, penalty or interest has been paid more than once or has been paid erroneously or illegally collected or received by the city under this division, it may be offset against any future liability for the tax. If the licensee determines that he has overpaid or paid more than once, which fact has not been determined by the city, he will have three years from the date of payment to file a claim in writing stating the specific ground upon which the claim is founded. The claim shall be audited. If the claim is approved by the mayor and council, the excess amount paid the city may be credited on any amounts then due and payable from the person by whom it was paid or from his administrators or executors.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-189. - Hours of operation for retail sales of packaged distilled beverages.
Unless state laws and regulations hereafter provide otherwise, packaged distilled spirits may be sold only between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 11:30 p.m., Monday through Saturday.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-190. - Delivery by retailer beyond licensed premises.
It shall be unlawful for any person issued a license under this division to make deliveries of any distilled spirits by the package beyond the boundaries of the premises covered by the license.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-191. - Drive-in.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or dispense distilled spirits from drive-in or service windows. Violation of this section will result in suspension or revocation for cause of the license.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-192. - Sales in connection with other commodities.
No retail license for the package sales of distilled spirits shall be allowed in or in connection with the operation of any restaurant, cafe or eating place, or in the same room where a bar is maintained for the dispensing and sale of malt beverages, wine by the drink, or distilled spirits by the drink, or any other business establishment, except as may be permitted herein by this division. Nothing in this section, however, shall be construed to limit or prohibit distilled sprit tasting events.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-193. - Misrepresentation of contents.
Under this division it shall be unlawful for licensees or their agents to add to the contents of a bottle or to refill empty bottles or in any other manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality, or brand name of any alcoholic beverage.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-194. - Sale to certain persons.
No person issued a license under this chapter or any other person on the licensed premises shall sell any alcoholic beverages to any person in an intoxicated condition.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-195. - Inventory.
(a)
There must be present on the premises an inventory of retail goods and merchandise available for sale having a retail value of not less than $750,000.00, at all times. For purposes of measuring whether a licensee's inventory of retail goods and merchandise has a value of at least $750,000.00, there must be an average monthly inventory based upon the inventory records for the specific premises of at least $750,000.00.
(b)
Each license holder at the premises shall have his monthly inventory records showing the retail value of all goods and merchandise on the premises at the location of the premises at all times during regular business hours for inspection by the chief of police, his designee or codes enforcement.
(c)
No licensee licensed to sell packaged distilled spirits shall sell, offer for sale, display, or keep in stock for sale or furnish at its licensed location where distilled spirits are offered for sale, any other products, or services, except the following:
(1)
Wines, if the licensee holds a valid and current license to sell wine at that premises;
(2)
Malt beverages, if the licensee holds a valid and current license to sell malt beverages at that premises;
(3)
Paper, styrofoam, plastic cups, gift bags, which are limited in size to accommodate one 750 ml size bottle of wine or spirits.
(4)
Bar supplies, corkscrews, openers, straws, swizzle stirrers, and bar-related containers, and wares made of glass, plastic, metal or ceramic materials, cocktail olives, onions, cherries, lemons, limes, and sugars or salts produced and marketed specifically for tie preparation of alcoholic beverage drinks, alcoholic beverage drink recipe booklets, bar guides and consumer-oriented alcoholic beverage publications.
(5)
Quality cigars.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-196. - Limitation on consumption sales.
It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly and intentionally sell any alcoholic beverage for consumption on premises where packaged distilled spirits are sold, except in connection with a tasting permit.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-197. - Proper identification—Definition.
Proper identification as used in this division shall mean any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and such person's date of birth. Proper identification includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or the identification card authorized under O.C.G.A. § 40-5-100 through 40-5-104. Proper identification shall not include a birth certificate and shall not include any traffic citation and complaint form.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-198. - Responsibility to examine proper identification.
It shall be the responsibility of the licensee to examine the proper identification of each patron desiring to purchase packaged distilled spirits to ascertain that such patron is 21 years of age or older. Packaged distilled spirits shall not be sold to anyone younger than 21 years of age.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
Sec. 6-199. - Premises distance requirements.
(a)
For a retail packaged distilled spirits license to be issued, the premises of the applicant must meet the following distance requirements:
(1)
The premises shall not be located in or within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state, the county, or the city.
(2)
The premises shall not be located in or within 100 yards of any church building, housing authority property, or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus.
(3)
No new retail package liquor licensed place of business or the relocation of an existing retail package liquor licensed place of business engaged in the retail package sale of distilled spirits shall be located within 1,000 yards of any other business licensed to engage in the retail sale of distilled spirits as measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(4)
The most direct route of pedestrian travel on the ground shall be along the right-of-way measured from the nearest building wall of any church or nearest property line of any school, public housing, college campus or public recreation area to the center of any door of customer entry of the proposed premises of the applicant.
(Ord. No. 2022-01, 2-8-2022)
