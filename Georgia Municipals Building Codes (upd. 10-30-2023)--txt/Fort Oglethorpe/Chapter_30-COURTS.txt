ARTICLE II. - MUNICIPAL COURT

Sec. 30-31 - Jurisdiction; powers.
(a)
There is hereby established for and in the City of Fort Oglethorpe, a court to be known as the Municipal Court of the City of Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia. Except as provided in paragraph (b) below, all warrants, summons, citations, subpoenas or other process issued requiring appearance or attendance in such municipal court shall be issued by the clerk of the court, directed to any sworn policeman of City of Fort Oglethorpe Police Department, and bear teste in the name of the municipal court judge.
(b)
Citations or summons, including but not limited to summons on forms developed by the state department of public safety under O.C.G.A. § 40-13-1, requiring appearance or attendance in municipal court may be issued by the municipal court judge, city court clerk, deputy clerk, any City of Fort Oglethorpe police officer, marshal or deputy marshal, code enforcement officer, animal control officer, fire marshal or deputy fire marshal, chief building official or building inspector, employee of the department of transportation, the City of Fort Oglethorpe solicitor, assistant solicitor or by any other person specifically authorized to issue such citations or summons by the Code of Ordinances for the City of Fort Oglethorpe or by state law.
(c)
The City of Fort Oglethorpe Prosecuting Attorney is authorized to issue and file accusations as charging instruments in municipal court.
(d)
The judge of municipal court shall have jurisdiction of first instance over all City of Fort Oglethorpe ordinances. The municipal court shall have jurisdiction over such traffic and misdemeanor offenses as authorized presently, or in the future, by state law.
(e)
Sessions of the court shall be on a regular basis as set by the judge for the City of Fort Oglethorpe.
(f)
The municipal court judge shall have authority in accordance with Georgia law and a sound construction of the laws and ordinances of the City of Fort Oglethorpe to make reasonable rules and regulations necessary and proper to secure the efficient and successful administration of the municipal court; as well as utilization of volunteer personnel for the efficient operation of the court; provided however, the rules and regulations shall be filed with the clerk of the court and available for public inspection.
(g)
The judge of the municipal court shall have authority to provide for bails and recognizance and for surety therefor to ensure the presence of those charged with violations before the court, and the court shall have authority to provide for forfeitures of the same.
(h)
The judge of the municipal court shall have authority to punish those in its presence for contempt in such fine amount as shall be provided by the court, or jail time, or both.
(i)
The municipal court may fix punishment for offenses within its jurisdiction in a fine amount for each charge not exceeding $ 1,000.00 or imprisonment for not more than 12 months, or both, as now or hereinafter provided by state law. The municipal court judge shall have the authority to establish a schedule of fines for punishment of said violation. The municipal court judge shall have the authority to establish a schedule of fees to defray the cost of operations.
(j)
The municipal court judge shall have the authority to establish bail and recognizance to ensure the appearance of those charged with violations before the court, and shall have discretionary authority to accept cash or personal or real property as surety for the appearance of persons charged. Whenever any person shall give bail for that person's appearance and shall fail to appear at the time fixed for trial, the bond may be forfeited by the judge. In the event that cash or property is accepted in lieu of bond for security for the appearance of a defendant at trial, and if such defendant fails to appear at the time and place fixed for trial, the cash so deposited shall be, on order of the judge, declared forfeited to the city, or the property so deposited shall have a lien against it for the value forfeited. Such lien shall be enforceable in the same manner and to the same extent as a lien for city property tax.
(k)
The municipal courts shall have the same authority as superior courts to compel the production of evidence in the possession of any person; to enforce obedience its orders, judgments, and sentences; and, to administer oaths as are necessary.
(l)
The municipal court judge shall have the authority to issue warrants for the arrest of persons charged with offenses against any ordinance of the city and shall have the same authority as a magistrate judge of the state to issue warrants.
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-32. - Judge generally.
(a)
In presiding over the municipal court, the judge shall have full power and authority, as provided by the Ordinances of the City of Fort Oglethorpe and state law, including, but not limited to, the full power and authority to compel the attendance of parties and witnesses in court.
(b)
The judge shall have the power to enforce all the court's orders and judgments, to preserve order and to punish for contempt to the fullest extent authorized by law.
(c)
The term of office of the municipal court judge shall be for a period of two years beginning on February 1 in the year of appointment, and ending January 31 in the second year following appointment. In the event of any vacancy in the office of a municipal court judge for any cause, whether by death, resignation, sickness, vacancy or removal, the vacancy shall be filled by the council for the remaining term of the vacant office. The municipal court judge shall be appointed by the council in January of the appointment year.
(d)
The terms "judge" or "municipal court judge" as used throughout this section and related law shall include any person authorized by this paragraph to hear cases in the municipal court.
(e)
The compensation of the municipal court judge shall be set by the mayor and council by ordinance.
(f)
The judge shall be readily available to sign warrants or perform other functions required.
(g)
The judge of the municipal court shall possess such qualifications as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-32-1.1.
(h)
The Judge may assess an administrative fee, also termed "court cost" in any case not to exceed $1,000.00 per citation, accusation, charge, violation or motion by the city requesting certain relief. The total for said charges of administrative fee shall be calculated upon a reasonable estimate of cost incurred in the matter before the court. In special circumstance where clear and convincing evidence demonstrates cost in excess of $1,000.00 the court may increase the amount commensurate with the proof submitted.
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-33. - Substitute municipal court judge.
Whenever, from any cause, the judge of municipal court cannot be in attendance to hold municipal court, or if it should appear that he or she is disqualified in any case, then the judge shall appoint a judge pro hac vice to preside in the court in his/her place. All actions of the appointee shall be binding as if performed by the municipal judge. Any substitute judge of the municipal court shall possess such qualifications as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-32-1.1.
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-34. - Prosecuting attorney.
(a)
There is hereby created the office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court. The term "prosecuting attorney" shall be substituted for the term "city solicitor' and for the word "solicitor" wherever they may appear in the Code of Ordinances or general state law.
(b)
The office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court shall be a part-time position. The compensation for the prosecuting attorney shall be as set by contract from time to time by the council.
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-35. - Qualifications; oaths.
(a)
The prosecuting attorney of the municipal court shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia and shall be admitted to practice before the appellate courts of the State of Georgia.
(b)
The term of office of the prosecuting attorney shall be for a period of three years beginning on February 1 in the year of appointment, and ending January 31 in the third year following appointment. In the event of any vacancy in the office of a prosecuting attorney, whether by death, resignation, sickness, vacancy or removal, the vacancy shall be filled by the council for the remaining term of the appointment. The prosecuting attorney shall be appointed by the council in January of the appointment year. In addition to the oaths prescribed by O.C.G.A. Tit. 45, Ch. 3, relating to official oaths, the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court shall take and subscribe to the following oath: "I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as Prosecuting Attorney of the City of Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia."
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-36. - Substitute prosecuting attorney.
The mayor and council may provide for a substitute prosecuting attorney if the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court is disqualified from engaging in the prosecution of a particular case or is unable to perform the duties of said office due to caseload, illness or incapacity. At any time in which a substitute prosecuting attorney is not available or an appointment has not been made, the city attorney, or his designee, may assist the prosecuting attorney or substitute prosecuting attorney as may be required to meet the court's case load. Any substitute prosecuting attorney shall meet the qualifications set forth in section 30-35. The compensation for the substitute prosecuting attorney shall be as set by the council by contract as is necessary.
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-37. - Duties.
The prosecuting attorney of the municipal court shall have the duties and the authority to represent the City of Fort Oglethorpe as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 15-18-96.
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-38. - Public defender.
(a)
There is hereby created the office of public defender of the municipal court.
(b)
The office of public defender of the municipal court shall be a part-time position. The term and compensation for the public defender shall be as set by contract from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
Sec. 30-39. - Qualifications; oaths.
(a)
The public defender of the municipal court shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia and shall be admitted to practice before the appellate courts of the State of Georgia.
(b)
The term of office of the public defender shall be for a period of one year beginning on February 1 in the year of appointment, and ending January 31 in the appointment year following appointment. In the event of any vacancy in the office of a public defender, whether by death, resignation, sickness, vacancy or removal, the vacancy shall be filled by the council for a term to expire on January 31 in the appointment year. The public defender shall be appointed by the council in January of the appointment year.
(c)
In addition to the oaths prescribed by O.C.G.A. Tit. 45, Ch. 3, relating to official oaths, the public defender of the municipal court shall take and subscribe to the following oath: "I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as public defender of the City of Fort Oglethorpe, Georgia."
(Ord. No. 2019-07, 3-25-2019)
