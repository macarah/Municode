ARTICLE II. - PUBLIC PARKS, PLAYGROUNDS AND RECREATION CENTERS

Sec. 20-19. - Development of municipal recreation programs.
The authority to develop programs of recreational activities and services designed to meet the leisure time interest and needs of all municipal citizens is granted to and vested in the park and recreation board, which board shall have the power to maintain and equip parks, playgrounds, recreation centers and the buildings associated therewith and to develop, maintain and operate all types of recreation facilities, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-64-1 et seq.
(Code 1979, § 23-101; Code 2003, § 93.01)
Sec. 20-20. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to provide rules and regulations for the use of and conduct in the parks and recreation areas of the city, which have become increasingly crowded and for which scheduling and protective measures are needed for public safety, and in order to coordinate maximum safe and non-disruptive usage.
(Code 2003, § 93.02; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-21. - Applicability.
This article shall apply in all parks and recreation areas under the jurisdiction of the city, unless expressly exempted. For the issuance of permits, temporary designations, authorizations, granting of approval and other actions, the approving governing agency shall be the mayor and city council (hereinafter, the recreation commissioner or the commissioner) or, as the commissioner's designee, any councilmember or city employee designated in writing by the mayor with the approval of the city council as the recreation commissioner with responsibility for city parks and recreation or the city clerk or deputy city clerk for ministerial functions, such as accepting applications for filing and collecting applications or permit fees.
(Code 2003, § 93.03; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-22. - Construction.
In the interpretation of this article and all succeeding park and recreation area ordinances, the provisions shall be construed as follows:
(1)
Any requirement or prohibitions of any act shall respectively extend to and include the causing and procuring, directly or indirectly, such act;
(2)
Any act otherwise prohibited by law or local ordinance shall be lawful if performed under, by virtue of and strictly within the provisions of a permit to do so and to the extent authorized thereby.
(Code 2003, § 93.04; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-23. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animals means and includes cats, dogs, horses, any fowl or birds and any living creatures within the jurisdiction of the park or recreation area.
Approving governing agency means the board of recreation commissioners or its designee.
Crossing means any crossing whether marked by a pavement or otherwise, the extension to any sidewalk space across any intersecting drive, street, highway.
Curb means any boundary of any street, road, avenue, boulevard or drive, whether or not marked by a curb.
Director, commissioner or recreation commissioner means the member of the city council charged with responsibility for parks and recreation, unless an employee position of recreation director is established by the city council.
Park attendant means any person employed by the city as a parks attendant to perform duties or tasks within the park and recreation areas.
Path means any footpath, walk or any path maintained for pedestrians.
Pedestrian means a person afoot.
Permit means any written license issued by or under the authority of the approving governing agency permitting a special event or activity on park facilities.
Person means any natural person, corporation, company, association, joint stock association, firm or co-partnership.
Soliciting means persons selling goods or services by sample or taking orders for future delivery with or without accepting advance payment for the goods; persons seeking any form of contributions.
Stopping orstanding, when prohibited, means any cessation of movement of a vehicle occupied or not, except when necessary to avoid conflict with pedestrians or other traffic, including horses and bicycles.
Traffic means pedestrians, ridden or herded animals and vehicles, either singly or together.
Vehicle means any conveyance (except baby carriages), including motor vehicles, trailers of all types, campers, tricycles, bicycles, motorized or not, sleds, sleighs, push carts or vehicles propelled by other than muscular power; also any horse or horse-drawn conveyance.
Vending means selling or trading any item of service.
(Code 2003, § 93.05; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-24. - Enforcement.
(a)
The city police department, the recreation commissioner and park and area director and attendants shall, in connection with their duties imposed by law, diligently enforce the provisions of this article.
(b)
The city police department, recreation commissioner, park and recreation area director and any attendant shall have the authority to order any person acting in violation of this article to leave any park or recreation area.
(c)
At any special event in a city park or recreation area, the sponsoring organization or entity is responsible for providing security by two law enforcement personnel, currently trained and designated by the state, per 500 people expected in attendance at such special event. In compliance with this requirement, such sponsoring organization or entity may arrange through the city chief of police to hire and pay off-duty city police officers at the rate of $60.00 per police officer per four-hour shift or any portion thereof or the sponsoring organization or entity can make its own arrangements to provide such state trained and designated law enforcement personnel for security.
(Code 2003, § 93.06; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-25. - Rules of conduct.
It shall be unlawful for any person in a public park or recreation area to:
(1)
Mark, deface, disfigure, injure, tamper with or displace or remove any gazebos, sheds, buildings, bridges, tables, benches, fireplaces, railings, paving or paving materials, water lines or other public utilities or parts or appurtenances thereof, signs, notices or placards, whether temporary or permanent, monuments, stakes, posts or other boundary markers or other structures or equipment, facilities or park property or appurtenances whatsoever, either real or personal;
(2)
Fail to cooperate in maintaining restrooms and washrooms in a neat and sanitary condition;
(3)
Dig or remove any soil, rock, sand, stones, trees, shrubs, plants or other wood or materials or make any excavation by tool, equipment, blasting or other means or agency;
(4)
Construct or erect any building or structure of whatever kind, whether permanent or temporary, or run or string any public service utility into, upon or across such lands, except on special written permit issued hereunder;
(5)
Damage, cut, carve, mark, transplant or remove any plant, injure the bark or pick flowers or seed of any tree or plant, dig in or otherwise disturb grass areas or in any other way injure the natural beauty or usefulness of any area;
(6)
Climb any tree or walk, climb, stand or sit upon monuments, vases, planters, fountains, railings, fences or upon any other property not designated or customarily used for such purposes;
(7)
Attach any rope or cable or other contrivance to any tree, fence, railing bridge, bench or other structure;
(8)
Throw, discharge or otherwise place or cause to be placed in the waters of any fountains, pond, lake, stream or other body of water in or adjacent to any park or tributary, stream, storm sewer or drain flowing into such water any substance, matter or thing, liquid or solid which will or may result in the pollution of the waters;
(9)
Take into, carry through or put into any park any rubbish, refuse, garbage or other material. The refuse and rubbish shall be deposited in receptacles so provided. Where receptacles are not provided, all such rubbish or waste shall be carried away from the park by the person responsible for its presence and properly disposed of elsewhere;
(10)
Bring any glass container into any park or recreation area;
(11)
Cause or permit to run loose any animal;
(12)
Tie or hitch an animal to any tree or plant;
(13)
Hunt, molest, harm, frighten, kill, trap, pursue, chase, tease, shoot or throw missiles at any animal, wildlife, reptile or bird; nor shall he remove or have in his possession the young of any wild animal or the eggs or nest or young of any reptile or bird. Exception to the foregoing is made in that snakes known to be deadly poisonous may be killed on sight;
(14)
Walk a dog without a leash, the leash to be no longer than six feet;
(15)
Ride a horse, except on designated bridle trails; horses shall be thoroughly broken and properly restrained and ridden with due care and shall not be allowed to graze or go unattended;
(16)
Walk a domestic animal without a leash, the leash to be no longer than six feet. Further, the owner or person having custody of the domestic animal shall be responsible for removal of any animal solid waste;
(17)
Without express permission of the city council, connect electrical appliances, lights, wiring or other electricity-using equipment to any electrical outlet, transformer, switch box, panel box or other electrical equipment or electrical distribution system located in a park or recreation area owned by the city or its electric utility provider.
(Code 2003, § 93.20; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-26. - Vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person in a public park or recreation area to:
(1)
Drive any vehicle on any area except the paved park roads or parking areas or such areas as may on occasion be specifically designated as temporary areas;
(2)
Park a vehicle anywhere except on a designated parking area;
(3)
Leave a vehicle standing or parked in established parking areas or elsewhere in the park and recreation areas during hours when the park and recreation area is closed. During a snowstorm and for three days thereafter established parking areas in parks and recreation areas may be utilized for parking, but not by trailers of any kind;
(4)
Leave a bicycle in a place other than a bicycle rack when such is provided and there is space available;
(5)
Ride a bicycle without reasonable regard to the safety of others;
(6)
Leave a bicycle lying on the ground or paving or set against trees or in any place or position where other persons may trip over or be injured by them;
(7)
Wash any vehicle;
(8)
Except for railroad operations on existing an right-of-way, drive or operate within the parks any bus or vehicle adapted for more than 12 passengers; any hearse, truck or tractor or vehicle used for advertising, provided that taxis, buses and trucks may be used to carry visitors to the parks and recreation areas;
(9)
Except for antique automobile shows issued permits by the commissioner as provided elsewhere herein, use the parks, park drives, parking places or parkways for the purpose of demonstrating any vehicles or for the purpose of instructing another to drive or operate any vehicle, nor shall any person use any park area, including parking places, for the repairing or cleaning of any vehicle, except in an emergency;
(10)
Except for concessionaires issued permits by the commissioner as provided elsewhere herein, cause or permit a vehicle in tow of another vehicle to enter the parks or proceed therein, except that, in case of a breakdown, a disabled vehicle may be towed to the nearest exit; or operate or drive a vehicle containing any person or object projecting or hanging outside of or beyond the side of the rear thereof.
(Code 2003, § 93.21; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-27. - Firearms, weapons, tools.
Unless specially permitted for veterans, Civil War re-enactments and similar memorial events utilizing blank ammunition, it shall be unlawful for any person to bring into or have in his possession in any park or recreation area:
(1)
Any pistol or revolver or objects upon which loaded or blank cartridges may be used. Official starters, at authorized track and field events, are excepted from this restriction;
(2)
Any paint gun, slingshots, bow and arrow, crossbow, blowgun or similar projectile repelling utensil;
(3)
Any burglar tools;
(4)
Any rifle, shotgun, BB gun, air gun, spring gun, sling-shot, bow or other weapon in which the propelling force is gunpowder, a spring or air.
(Code 2003, § 93.22; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-28. - Advertising, assemblages, entertainment.
(a)
No person shall post, paint, affix, distribute, deliver, place, cast or leave to persons not willing to accept the same any bill, billboard, placard, ticket, handbill, circular or advertisement.
(b)
No person shall do any of the following without a permit, provided that no permit shall be required for any action or event sponsored by the city or the commissioner:
(1)
Display any signs or other matter, provided that a sign attached to a vehicle to identify the vehicle or a sign lawfully on a taxi or bus is not prohibited;
(2)
Publicly operate for any musical instrument, soundtrack or drum;
(3)
Hold public assemblages;
(4)
Conduct exhibitions;
(5)
Hold a parade.
(Code 2003, § 93.24; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-29. - Merchandise, sale prohibited.
No person shall expose or offer for sale any article in any park or recreation areas, without a license as a concessionaire.
(Code 2003, § 93.25; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-30. - Ignitable and combustible materials.
No person shall kindle, build, maintain or use a fire except in places provided for such purposes. Any fire shall be continuously under the care and direction of a competent person from the time it is kindled until it is extinguished. No person shall throw away or discard any lighted match, cigar, cigarette, tobacco, paper or other material within or against any building, boat or vehicle or under any tree or in underbrush.
(Code 2003, § 93.26; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-31. - Alcoholic beverages, controlled dangerous substances, alms, gambling.
While in a public park or recreation area, all persons shall conduct themselves in a proper and orderly manner, and in particular, no person shall:
(1)
Bring alcoholic beverages, or controlled dangerous substances, drink or use the same at any time, nor shall any person be under the influence of intoxicating liquor or a controlled dangerous substance in a park or recreation area;
(2)
Solicit alms or contributions for any purpose, whether public or private;
(3)
Play any game of chance or have possession of any instrument or device for gambling;
(4)
Play, engage or take part in any game or competitive sport for money, or other valuable thing, without a written permit.
(Code 2003, § 93.27; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-32. - Unlawful conduct.
It shall be unlawful for any person in a park or recreation area to:
(1)
Camp or stay overnight anywhere, except in areas designated for camping or staying overnight in vehicles or trailers;
(2)
Take part in the playing of any games involving thrown or otherwise propelled objects, except in those areas designated for such forms of recreation;
(3)
Swim or play football, baseball, basketball, soccer or lacrosse, except in areas designated for such games;
(4)
Roller skate or use skateboards, except in those areas specifically designed for such pastimes;
(5)
Enter an area posted as closed to the public;
(6)
Engage in threatening, abusive, insulting or indecent language or engage in any disorderly conduct or behavior tending to breach into public peace;
(7)
Fail to produce and exhibit any permit he claims to have, upon request of any authorized person who shall desire to inspect the same for the purpose of enforcing compliance with any ordinance or rule;
(8)
Disturb or interfere unreasonably with any person or party occupying any area or participating in any activity under the authority of a permit;
(9)
Erect or occupy any tent, stand or other structure in any park or playground, sell or give away from any such tent, stand or other structure any food, drink or other thing, without a permit;
(10)
Deliberately deface or destroy any public property located therein.
(Code 2003, § 93.28; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-33. - Picnic areas.
It shall be unlawful for any person or group of persons to hold a picnic in any park, except in areas set aside or specifically designated as a picnic area. A permit must be secured for any picnic with more than 60 participants.
(Code 2003, § 93.29; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-34. - Swimming pools.
The commissioner shall hire as part-time life guards, from Memorial Day to Labor Day each year, persons, physically fit and deemed qualified by the commissioner, who provide the city with evidence of their satisfactory completion of a senior lifesaving course and certification as a lifeguard or senior lifesaver by the American Red Cross. The commissioner shall ensure that a minimum of two such lifeguards are always on duty at all times, and no less than one lifeguard for each 25 persons in each pool owned or operated by the city. As a prerequisite for any use of the city's pool by any governmental or private organization or entity, such using organization or entity shall utilize the city's lifeguards and shall reimburse the city at the hourly rate the city pays such lifeguards, over and above, and in addition to, any rental charges the city council may from time-to-time establish for use of its pools by governmental or private organization or entities.
(Code 2003, § 93.30; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-35. - Boating.
Any person desiring to use any kind of a boat in parks shall abide by the ordinances relating to boating in public places. No boats with motors shall be permitted, except in areas specifically designated for them.
(Code 2003, § 93.31; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-36. - Closing hours.
Except for designated camping areas, no person shall be in any park during the hours the park is closed. Closing hours will be posted at all parks, and different closing hours from those may be designated and posted by the commissioner. The commissioner may, at his discretion, erect gates at the entrances to any park or recreation area for the purpose of restricting or preventing vehicular or other traffic through any park or recreation area during the posted hours such park or recreation area is closed.
(Code 2003, § 93.32; Ord. of 4-15-2002)

Sec. 20-38. - Permits.
(a)
General. Permits for special events, and for the operation of a concession by a vendor at such a special event, in parks and recreation areas shall be obtained by application to the approving commissioner or the commissioner's designee in accordance with the following procedure:
(1)
Application. A person seeking issuance of a permit hereunder shall file an application (with an application fee in an amount as established by the mayor and city council from time to time) stating:
a.
The name and address of the applicant;
b.
The name and address of the person, corporation or association sponsoring the activity, if any;
c.
The day and hours for which the permit is desired;
d.
The park or portion thereof for which the permit is desired;
e.
The names and qualifications of the security company or individual law enforcement personnel whom the applicant intends to utilize to provide security for such event in accordance with section 20-24(c);
f.
The expected attendance at such special event, including the maximum total attendance for the event, the average daily attendance for the event and the expected highest attendance for each day;
g.
A copy of a rental agreement or other documentation evidencing:
1.
That portable toilet or restroom facilities will be available at any special event where more than 150 people are expected to attend at any given time; and
2.
That at least one such portable toilets will be provided at no cost to the city for each 150 people expected in attendance at any given time during such special event;
h.
Any other information reasonably necessary to a determination as to whether a permit should be issued hereunder;
i.
Variances required from park rules and regulations.
(2)
Standards for issuance of a use permit. Standards for issuance of a use permit shall include the following findings:
a.
That the proposed activity or use of the park will not unreasonably interfere with or detract from the general public's enjoyment of the park;
b.
That the proposed activity and use will not unreasonably interfere with or detract from the promotion of public health, welfare, safety and recreation;
c.
That the proposed activity or uses that are reasonably anticipated will not include violence, crime or disorderly conduct;
d.
That the proposed activity will not entail extraordinary or burdensome expense or police operation by the city;
e.
That the facilities desired have not been reserved for other use on the date and hour requested in the application.
(b)
Appeal.
(1)
Within ten days after the receipt of an application, the commissioner shall tell an applicant in writing of his decision to grant or deny a permit; in the event of a denial, the notification shall include the reason for the denial. Any aggrieved person shall have the right to appeal to the city council by serving written notice thereof on the city clerk within five working days of the refusal.
(2)
A copy of the notice shall also be served on the recreation commission within the same time and the commission shall immediately forward the application and the reasons for its refusal to the city council. The city council shall decide within ten days from the receipt of the appeal by the city clerk, or at its first meeting after the appeal, whichever is later. The decision of the city council shall be final.
(c)
Bound by park rules. A permittee shall be bound by all park rules and regulations and all applicable ordinances fully as though the same were inserted in the permits.
(d)
Liability insurance; exceptions. Except for wedding parties, pre-school, elementary and secondary school-sponsored activities and public library events, every applicant for a permit shall be required to submit evidence of liability insurance covering injuries to members of the general public arising out of such permitted activities in the amount of $1,000,000.00 or such higher amount as may be from time to time determined prior to the commencement of any activity or issuance of any permit.
(e)
Parade. Any group must have a permit for a parade.
(f)
Revocation. The city council shall have the authority to revoke a permit upon a finding of violation of any rule or ordinance or upon good cause shown.
(Code 2003, § 93.34; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-39. - Reservation of park areas.
The commissioner, or any employee designated by the commissioner, may make reservations of specific areas in public parks on a first come, first served basis, in accordance with the following regulations:
(1)
No areas shall be reserved, except picnic shelters and areas designated as playing fields, such as soccer fields and baseball fields.
(2)
No reservation shall be made for any organization known to the commissioner or his designees to discriminate against persons on account of gender, skin color, race or national origin, provided that this provision shall not prevent a reservation on behalf of a church league or other religious organization.
(3)
No area shall be reserved exclusively for any one league or organization for a period of more than three consecutive days, and the times of reservation of each area shall be divided among the leagues or organizations applying to use them. Each area shall be open at some times during each week for use by persons not participating in any organization, and if no such persons request a reservation, then time shall be scheduled as open time and a notice shall be posted in a conspicuous place at or near the area, designating the times when the area will be open for use on a first come, first served basis. In scheduling the times under this section, the commissioner will take into account the hours at which there is demand for the use of the area and divide the hours of greatest demand among various organizations and among persons not involved in organization.
(4)
Written public records shall be kept of all reservations. Public notices shall be posted of the scheduling policy and of the telephone number to call to make a reservation for any area.
(5)
No public area shall be reserved for any school or training program, except for programs of the public schools provided under any written park-school plan and except for governmental employee or volunteer training approved by the mayor and city council.
(6)
A clean-up fee, in the amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time, will be made with the application for any reservation under this section, at the discretion of the commissioner. If proper cleanup is done, this will be returned.
(7)
No organization or individual shall be refused permission to use any park facility or to make a reservation because of any religious beliefs or religious speech of the organization.
(Code 2003, § 93.35; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-40. - Concessions in parks and recreation areas.
(a)
Sale of food or any other product, without a concession, prohibited. No person, firm, corporation, association, club or organization shall sell any food, food or any other product, service or personal property items of any kind in any park or playground without a license as a concessionaire as provided in this section.
(b)
Application for license. Each person, firm, corporation, association, club or organization desiring to apply for a license as a concessionaire to sell food products or any other product, service or personal property items in any park or playground shall file an application therefor. The application shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant;
(2)
The location where the applicant proposes to sell such food, food product or other product, service or personal property items;
(3)
The types of food, food product or other product, service or personal property proposed to be sold;
(4)
A description of any park area or areas, buildings or equipment which are owned by the city that are desired to be used by the concessionaire;
(5)
The equipment which the concessionaire plans to use, including not only food preparation and food serving equipment, but also any booth, stall, cart or building proposed to be used in the operation of each concession;
(6)
The prices proposed to be charged by the concessionaire for the various items of food, food products and other products, services or personal property items.
(c)
Operation not to be changed. Any person receiving a concession license or permit under this section shall not change any part of the operation described in the application, including equipment, food, product or services to be sold by the concessionaire or any other factor not disclosed in the application, without applying for an approval of the change and receiving approval thereafter from the commissioner or the city council.
(d)
Fees. Each concessionaire shall pay fees for such concession space operated by such concessionaire.
(1)
An application fee for the processing of the concession application payable when such application is filed.
(2)
A concession fee per concession space per calendar week, or any portion of a calendar week of such concession operation, payable at the time such permit is issued.
(3)
The fees described in this section shall be in amounts as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(e)
Issuance of concession permit or license. Licenses or permits to concessionaires shall be issued by the commissioner or his designee in the first instance or on application for their denial by the city council. All actions by the city council on concessions shall be taken at a public meeting.
(f)
Duration of concession. Each license to each concessionaire shall expire on December 31 of each year, unless an earlier expiration date is specified in such or permit, such as a one-, two- or three-day permit or a weeklong permit which expires earlier by its terms. Applications for new concessions may be made at any time after November 1 of each, but no later than ten days prior to the time for which such concession is sought. Consideration in renewals will be given to persons having existing concessions, if all other factors are equal.
(g)
Temporary concessions. Temporary concessions may be given for special events to any person, firm or corporation or to any civic group. The temporary concession licenses or permits shall contain a duration or expiration date (for example, a three-day permit commencing July 18, 2002 and ending July 20, 2002). The provisions of this article will be followed in all matters, including the requirement of the payment of fees.
(h)
Clean-up of area used. At the expiration of any concession permit, the permitted concessionaire shall police the area utilized by such concessionaire and shall pay the city any cleanup or repair costs for such concession area incurred by the city over and above the per week charge assessed in subsection (d)(2) of this section, insofar as necessary to restore the area to its pre-concession state.
(Code 2003, § 93.36; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
Sec. 20-41. - Penalty.
Any person, firm, corporation, association, club or other organization violating any provision of this article shall be fined not less than $25.00 nor more than $1,000.00 for each offense, and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Code 2003, § 93.99; Ord. of 4-15-2002)
