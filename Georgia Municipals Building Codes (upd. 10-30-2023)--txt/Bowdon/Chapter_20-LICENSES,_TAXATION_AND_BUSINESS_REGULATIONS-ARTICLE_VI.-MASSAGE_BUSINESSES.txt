ARTICLE VI. - MASSAGE BUSINESSES

Sec. 20-380. - Purpose.
(a)
The purpose of this article is to provide for local licensing and regulation of locations where massage is offered, pursuant to the authority of the Charter of the City of Bowdon, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(b)(17), and O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-1 et seq. as amended.
(b)
A license issued pursuant to this article is a privilege, not a right.
(Code 1991, § 5-361)
Sec. 20-381. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Act means the Georgia Massage Therapy Practice Act, enacted by and defined in O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-1 et seq. as amended.
Board means the Georgia Board of Massage Therapy, enacted by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-4.
For hire means a reasonable expectation that the person to whom the massage is provided, or some third person on his behalf, will pay money, give other consideration, or provide any gratuity therefor.
Massage means the application of a system of structured touch, pressure, movement, and holding to the soft tissue of the body in which the primary intent is to enhance or restore health and well-being. The term "massage" includes complementary methods, including, without limitation, the external application of water, superficial heat, superficial cold, lubricants, salt scrubs, or other topical preparations and the use of commercially available electromechanical devices which do not require the use of transcutaneous electrodes and which mimic or enhance the actions possible by the hands; the term "massage" also includes determining whether massage therapy is appropriate or contraindicated, or whether referral to another health care provider is appropriate. Massage therapy shall not include the use of ultrasound, fluidotherapy, laser, and other methods of deep thermal modalities.
Massage business or massage parlor means a business with a location in the city at which any person engages in or offers massage, regardless of the name of the business or the words used to describe the business through signage or advertisement or in filings with the secretary of state. Massage parlors are subject to regulation by a local government, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(b)(17) and O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-22(a) as amended.
Massage therapist means:
(1)
A person who holds a valid, current, unrevoked, and unsuspended state license in the practice of massage therapy issued by the board pursuant to the Georgia Massage Therapy Practice Act.
(2)
A person who holds a valid, current, unrevoked, and unsuspended state provisional permit in the practice of massage therapy issued by the board pursuant to the Georgia Massage Therapy Practice Act, and working under the supervision of a licensed massage therapist.
(3)
A person pursuing a course of study leading to a degree or certificate as a massage therapist in an educational program recognized by the board, if such person is designated by title indicating student status and is fulfilling uncompensated work experiences required for the attainment of the degree or certificate, and working under the supervision of a licensed massage therapist.
(4)
A nonresident person rendering massage therapy up to 60 days during a 12-month period for treatment of a temporary sojourner only; provided that such nonresident massage therapist holds a license, registration or certification from another state, jurisdiction or country if the requirements as determined by the Georgia Board for Licensure are substantially equal to the requirements contained in this article; or provided that such nonresident massage therapist is currently nationally certified in therapeutic massage and bodywork.
(5)
A person duly licensed, registered, or certified in another jurisdiction, state, territory, or a foreign country when incidentally in this state to provide service as part of an emergency response team working in conjunction with disaster relief officials or as part of a charity event with which he comes into the state.
Masseuse, masseur, massage practitioner, or persons practicing massage means a person who performs or engages in the practice of massage.
State license means a license to practice massage therapy issued by the board, pursuant to the Act.
(Code 1991, § 5-362)
Sec. 20-382. - Requirements for licensure.
(a)
State license. No person other than a massage therapist shall perform or offer to perform massage in the City of Bowdon.
(b)
City license. No person or entity shall operate a massage parlor without a valid, current, unrevoked, and unsuspended license from the city, pursuant to this article. Every person who performs or offers to perform massage on the premises must be a massage therapist as defined herein, and it shall be unlawful to operate as a massage parlor unless all such persons are and remain licensed massage therapists. The city license of a massage parlor that fails to meet this requirement is subject to revocation.
(c)
Conviction of a crime. No license under this section shall be issued or renewed to any person or entity who shall have been convicted for any charge involving a felony, crime of moral turpitude, prostitution, masturbation for hire, or any sexual service crime, within ten years immediately prior to the filing of the application. The term "conviction" shall include an adjudication of guilty or plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond in part or in whole when charged with a crime. Licenses granted to persons who fail to meet this requirement at any time shall be subject to revocation.
(d)
Age of licensee; education. No person under the age of eighteen (18) shall be issued a license to operate a massage parlor.
(e)
Personal residency; agent. All applicants for licenses under this article and all actual owners of parlors for which licenses are sought shall be bona fide residents of Carroll County at the time of the filing of the applications and shall remain bona fide residents of such county during all times that the licenses and renewals thereof are in effect, or shall name one or more residents in the county as the agent or representative of the licensee, who shall be responsible for any matter relating to such license and who, at all such times, shall be a bona fide resident of Carroll County.
(f)
Corporate residency. All applications for licenses under this article by a corporation or other business entity shall name in the application one or more residents in the county as the agent and representative of the corporation to receive all communications, notices, services of process, or other papers or documents on behalf of the corporation in connection with any matter arising out of or connected with the issuance, holding, suspension, revocation, or other action with respect to any license issued pursuant to this article. The application shall give the mailing address of the person, and the mailing to any such person at such address of any notice required to be given under this article or any other law shall be sufficient notice to the corporation. If any such person shall cease to be a resident of the county, another person shall immediately be appointed, in writing, in his place, and written notice shall be given the city clerk, stating the name and address of the new agent.
(g)
Annual license fee. The annual license fee shall be determined from time to time by the mayor and council, based upon the reasonable costs of the actual regulatory activity, and listed in a schedule of fees and charges maintained by the city clerk.
(Code 1991, § 5-363)
Sec. 20-383. - Application for license.
(a)
To operate a massage parlor within the boundaries of the city, the applicant must first make application to the city clerk on a form provided, and that person, after having fully and truthfully completed such form and paying the prescribed fees and having received a license, shall maintain and operate such business pursuant to the ordinances of the city and the laws of the state.
(b)
The applicant for a license hereunder shall be the actual owner of the proposed licensed business and may be a natural person, a corporation or a limited liability company.
(1)
Where the applicant is a corporation, the "applicant" shall be the president and secretary, and each officer must provide those items required of applicants, and in addition the corporation shall present a letter of good standing from the secretary of state.
(2)
Where the applicant is a limited liability company, the "applicant" shall include all members and the manager, if the company is manager-managed, and each member and manager must provide those items required of applicants, and in addition shall present a letter of good standing from the secretary of state.
(3)
Partnerships shall not be issued licenses in a partnership name, but partners may be licensed individually, however if the partnership is only operating at a single location, only one licensing fee shall be charged.
(c)
Upon applying for a license with the finance officer of the city, each applicant shall provide a true and correct copy or original of the state license for each and every massage practitioner, performing or anticipated to perform massage at its location, and a list of all other employees to the finance officer for inspection and copying. Failure of any massage practitioner to provide his state license shall automatically result in the denial of the issuance of a city license to the applicant.
(d)
The city may request that the applicant provide or consent to the provision of a GCIC criminal history at the time of application.
(e)
Subsequent to the city license being issued, the licensee shall, in person, furnish to the finance officer a true and correct copy or original of the state license of each new massage practitioner at the licensee's massage parlor prior to such person's commencing to perform or offering to perform massage at such massage parlor. When any massage practitioner discontinues performing massage at a massage parlor, the licensee shall make the departure known in writing to the finance officer within 30 days of such departure.
(f)
Each applicant for a license shall make his affidavit before the finance officer, on a form provided by the finance officer, upon oath, swearing or affirming that all persons practicing massage on the premises of his massage parlor are licensed massage therapists and that all requirements of this article for a license have been met.
(Code 1991, § 5-364)
Sec. 20-384. - License renewal.
Licenses for massage parlors may be renewed on a calendar year basis, provided that the licensee continues to meet the requirements set out in this article and the requirements of the Act.
(Code 1991, § 5-365)
Sec. 20-385. - Registry.
A registry shall be kept of all persons practicing massage on the premises of such massage parlor, which shall be available for inspection by an agent of the city during normal hours of business, providing the following information:
(1)
Legal name and any other name(s) the massage practitioner has been or is known as or by;
(2)
Current address including street and city;
(3)
State or country of birth; date of birth;
(4)
Number and expiration date of state license; and
(5)
Position with the massage parlor or business entity.
(Code 1991, § 5-366)
Sec. 20-386. - Posting licenses and rates.
(a)
Each massage parlor shall post its license obtained pursuant to this article, along with the state license of each masseuse operating on the premises of such parlor, in a conspicuous place in the licensee's place of business and shall keep such licenses there at all times.
(b)
Price rates for all services shall be prominently posted in the reception area or other conspicuous location available for all prospective customers, no service shall be allowed or permitted that is not prominently posted with its fees in such location.
(Code 1991, § 5-367)
Sec. 20-387. - Escort services and adult entertainment prohibited.
No massage parlor shall act as an escort or dating service.
(Code 1991, § 5-368)
Sec. 20-388. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold, served, given, dispensed, provided, consumed, or caused or allowed to be sold, served, given, dispensed, provided, consumed by or to any person on the premises of a massage parlor or kept thereon. "Alcoholic beverages" means and includes all types and kinds of alcohol as defined in this Code.
(Code 1991, § 5-369)
Sec. 20-389. - Hours of operation; Inspection.
(a)
Massage parlors shall be open for business only between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m., inclusive.
(b)
Any business holding a license issued under this article shall, at any time that the premises are open for business, be open to inspection by an officer of the police department, the health department, license inspectors, and/or fire department inspectors.
(Code 1991, § 5-370)
Sec. 20-390. - Name; place of business; sale, transfer or change of location.
No person or business entity granted a location license pursuant to this article shall operate the business under a name not specified in the license, nor conduct business at any location or place not specified in the license. No license shall be transferable. Upon the sale or relocation of a massage parlor, the license thereof shall be null and void.
(Code 1991, § 5-371)
Sec. 20-391. - Operating requirements.
(a)
Every portion of the premises of, and all equipment and supplies of, the massage parlor shall be kept clean and shall be operated in a sanitary condition.
(b)
All employees and masseuses at each massage parlor shall wear clean, nontransparent outer garments, covering the breast, buttocks and genital areas.
(Code 1991, § 5-372)
Sec. 20-392. - Revocation, suspensions, violations and penalties.
(a)
The city may, upon notice and a hearing for good cause, revoke or suspend or place on probation the license of any massage parlor:
(1)
That commits or allows any violations of the provisions of this article;
(2)
That allows any masseuse, working on the massage parlor's premises, to commit or offer to commit a sexual crime under O.C.G.A. tit. 16, ch. 6;
(3)
That allows any person to perform or offer massage on the premises who is not a licensed massage therapist;
(4)
Where the applicant for the massage parlor's city license gave false information on the application, in his affidavit, or in the massage parlor's registry as required in this article, or fails to update information related to the license to the finance officer as required by this article; or
(5)
Where the licensee fails at any time to meet the requirements for licensure under this article.
(b)
Any violation of any section of this article shall be punishable as provided in section 1-6. Each day during which said offenses occur shall constitute a separate offense, as shall each separate act constitute a violation.
(Code 1991, § 5-373)
Sec. 20-393. - Exemptions.
The requirements of this article shall have no application to or effect upon the following persons acting within the scope of their professions:
(1)
Medical doctors and osteopaths, chiropractors, physical and occupational therapists, podiatrists, acupuncturists, registered or licensed practical nurses, barbers, beauty culturists, and cosmetologists duly licensed to practice in this state pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 43.
(2)
Employees of duly licensed nursing and convalescent homes and hospitals while working at their place of employment.
(3)
Athletic directors or trainers who are affiliated with an accredited educational institution or a bona fide sports team and whose work is limited to athletic team members.
(4)
Any other individual or entity expressly exempted from local regulation of massage therapy by the laws of the state.
(5)
A person who restricts his practice to the manipulation of the soft tissue of the human body to hands, feet, or ears who does not have the client disrobe and does not hold himself or herself out as a massage therapist.
(6)
Any person who desires to provide in the city only certain bodywork services which the person believes are of a modality that is not a massage as defined in this article may request from the planning commission a waiver of the licensing requirements of this article for conducting the business of and providing such bodywork services, Such request will be made in writing, filed with the city clerk at least 15 days before the regularly scheduled meeting of the planning commission, and shall fully explain why the modality is not massage therapy. The planning commission shall act upon the request at the next regularly scheduled meeting. If the request is granted, the licensing requirements of this chapter shall be waived for the person making the request. The decision of the planning commission may be appealed pursuant to this Code or state law.
(Code 1991, § 5-374)
Sec. 20-394. - Suspension and revocation.
Any license issued hereunder may be suspended or revoked, in the discretion of the city council, for material misrepresentations in the application therefor, for suspension or revocation of the licensee's state master license, and for any violation of this code or state law. The procedure for suspension or revocation and appeal therefrom shall be as provided in section 20-263 through 20-270.
(Code 1991, § 5-401)
Sec. 20-395. - Notice of appeal.
The licensee shall, within ten days after he has been notified of an adverse determination, submit a notice of appeal to the city clerk. The notice of appeal shall be addressed to the city council and shall specify the subject matter of the appeal, the date of any original and amended application or requests, the date of the adverse decision (or receipt of notice thereof), the basis of the appeal, the action requested of the council, and the name of the applicant. The clerk shall place the appeal on the agenda of the next regular city council meeting occurring not less than five and no more than 30 days after receipt of the application for the city council action.
(Code 1991, § 5-402)
Sec. 20-396. - Discretion of council regarding appeal procedure.
When an appeal is placed on the city council agenda, the city council may take either of the following actions:
(1)
Set a hearing date and instruct the city clerk to give the notice of hearing as may be required by law.
(2)
Appoint a hearing officer and fix the time and place for hearing. The hearing officer may or may not be a city employee and may be appointed for an extended period of time. If a hearing officer is appointed, the hearing shall be conducted in accordance with the procedures set out in this article.
(Code 1991, § 5-403)
Sec. 20-397. - Notice of hearing.
The clerk shall be responsible for the publication of notice of the hearing as may be required by law.
(Code 1991, § 5-404)
Sec. 20-398. - Conduct of hearing before city council.
Whenever the city clerk has scheduled an appeal before the city council, at the time and date set therefor, the city council shall receive all relevant testimony and evidence from the licensee, from interested parties, and from city staff. The city council may sustain, overrule or modify the action complained of. The action of the city council shall be final.
(Code 1991, § 5-405)
Sec. 20-399. - Conduct of hearing before hearing officer.
The hearing officer, appointed, pursuant to the procedure set out in this article, may receive and rule on admissibility of evidence, hear testimony under oath and call witnesses as he may deem advisable with respect to the conduct of the hearing.
(Code 1991, § 5-406)
Sec. 20-400. - Rules of evidence inapplicable.
The city council and the hearing officer shall not be bound by the traditional rules of evidence in hearings conducted under this article, Rules of evidence as applied in an administrative hearing shall apply.
(Code 1991, § 5-407)
Sec. 20-401. - Report of hearing officer; notice of hearing.
(a)
If a hearing officer has been appointed to hear the appeal, the hearing officer shall, within a reasonable time not to exceed 30 days from the date the hearing is terminated, submit a written report to the city council.
(b)
The report shall contain a brief summary of the evidence considered and state findings, conclusions and recommendation.
(c)
All reports shall be filed with the city clerk, and shall be considered public records. A copy of the report shall be forwarded by certified mail to the licensee the same day as it is filed with the city clerk, with additional copies furnished to city council and the chief of police.
(d)
The city clerk shall place the hearing officer's report on the agenda of the next regular city council meeting occurring not less than ten days after the report is filed and shall notify the licensee of the date of the meeting at least ten days prior to the meeting unless the licensee stipulates to a shorter notice period.
(Code 1991, § 5-408)
Sec. 20-402. - Review of report by city council; determination.
The city council may adopt or reject the hearing officer's decision in its entirety or may modify the proposed recommendation. If the city council does not adopt the hearing officer's recommendation, it may:
(1)
Refer the matter to the same or another hearing officer for a completely new hearing, or for the taking of additional evidence on specific points; in either of the cases the hearing officer shall proceed as provided in this article.
(2)
Decide the case upon a review of the entire record before the hearing officer with or without taking additional evidence.
(Code 1991, § 5-409)
Sec. 20-403. - Penalties.
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall be subject to penalties as set forth in section 1-6.
(Code 1991, § 5-410)
