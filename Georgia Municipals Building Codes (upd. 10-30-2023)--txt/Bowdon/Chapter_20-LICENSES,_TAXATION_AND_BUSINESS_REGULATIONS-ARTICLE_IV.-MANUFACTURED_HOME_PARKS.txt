Sec. 20-88. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Camper means any self-propelled vehicle designated and constructed so as to permit occupancy thereof as a temporary dwelling unit or sleeping place.
Lot means and includes the term "plot" or "parcel."
Manufactured home means a structure transportable in one or more sections and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities, and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein.
Manufactured home park means an area which consists of lots with appropriate and adequate community services, recreational facilities, utilities, streets and sidewalks provided by the developer. Usually the community resident pays a monthly lot-rental fee. Manufactured home communities were formerly known as "parks" and "courts."
Map or zoning map means the zoning map of the city.
Nonconforming use means any building or land lawfully occupied by a use on October 12, 1987, or the time of amendment of this article, but which does not conform, after such date with the use regulations of the district in which it is situated.
Owner means any person who is authorized to operate and maintain a manufactured home park or manufactured home subdivision under the provisions of this article.
Trailer camp means a parcel of land that has been planned and improved for the rental or lease of trailer spaces or lots upon which transient guests are permitted to park and occupy their own travel trailers or campers on a temporary basis.
Travel trailer means any vehicle used, or maintained for use, as a conveyance upon the highways or city streets, so designed and constructed as to permit occupancy thereof as a temporary dwelling unit or sleeping place for one or more persons. Travel trailers have no foundation other than wheels or jacks and are not self-propelled.
Used or occupied includes the words intended, arranged or designed to be used or occupied.
(Code 1991, § 5-76; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. III, §§ 301, 302)
State Law reference— Definitions relating to factory-built buildings and dwelling units, O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-111, 8-2-131.
Sec. 20-89. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to provide for the placement of manufactured homes, trailers and campers in an environment that will provide pleasant and satisfactory living conditions and to provide no adverse effects on neighboring properties.
(Code 1991, § 5-77; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. I)
Sec. 20-90. - Application.
The provisions of this article shall govern the operation of all manufactured home parks and trailer camps within the corporate limits of the city. No manufactured home, trailer or camper shall be used or occupied except in conformity with the regulations of this article.
(Code 1991, § 5-78; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. IV, § 401)
Sec. 20-91. - Location restricted.
The parking of a manufactured home in any district other than the M-H-P, mobile home park, shall be prohibited; however:
(1)
This section shall not prevent the storage of manufactured homes when the same are on commercial sales lots for manufactured homes when the same are unoccupied and are not used for any purpose other than inspection and display. The sales office for such a manufactured home sales lot may be located in a manufactured home.
(2)
Construction trailers and manufactured homes used as offices may be placed on construction sites while construction is in progress; however, the same shall not be used for living quarters. When not in active use, construction trailers may be stored at the contractor's commercial base of operations.
(3)
The placement of a manufactured home not meeting the minimum structure requirement as set forth in this article shall be permitted on a vacant lot for a period not to exceed 12 months, provided a permanent dwelling is under construction within the 12-month period. The single-family dwelling must meet all requirements of the city building code within a maximum period of 24 months.
(4)
All manufactured homes shall meet a minimum structure requirement of not less than 1,050 square feet, for each dwelling designed to be occupied by a single family.
(5)
Each dwelling shall be placed upon a permanent masonry foundation.
(Code 1991, § 5-79; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. IV, § 402)
Editor's note— The building-related codes are on file in the office of the city clerk.
Sec. 20-92. - Temporary parking of manufactured homes.
Temporary parking of not more than one manufactured home for a period not to exceed five years provided the manufactured home is located behind an existing single-family dwelling and that the manufactured home is occupied by the mother, father, son or daughter of the residents of the single-family dwelling; provided that the manufactured home shall be located not nearer than 20 feet to any property line or any structure. If the manufactured home ceases to be occupied by such family member, then it must be moved within 90 days.
(Code 1991, § 5-80; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. IV, § 403)
Sec. 20-93. - Parking of travel trailers.
The parking or storage of one travel trailer or camper will be permitted on any lot in any district; however, the travel trailer or camper shall not be used for living, sleeping, or commercial or utility purposes while the same is so parked or stored. Manufactured homes may not be parked or stored except in approved manufactured home parks or commercial sales lots.
(Code 1991, § 5-81; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. IV, § 404)
Sec. 20-94. - Park and camp permits.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to construct, enlarge, maintain, or operate a manufactured home park, or trailer camp within the city unless such a person shall first obtain a valid written permit from the building inspector. Such permits may not be issued until the proposed establishment conforms to the requirements set forth in this article and full compliance with these regulations will be required of all parks and camps; however, the maintenance of a park or camp that is in existence on October 12, 1987, may be continued as a nonconforming use for three years. Thereafter, full compliance with these regulations will be required of all parks and camps except that minimum lot sizes and minimum spacing requirements will not be imposed in cases where noncompliance existed on October 12, 1987. The mayor and council may, upon the recommendation of the planning commission, grant a variance in cases where noncompliance by a park or camp, which existed on October 12, 1987, is demonstrated to be beyond the control of the park owner.
(b)
Application for permits to construct a manufactured home park or trailer camp shall be made in duplicate upon forms provided by the building inspector. Such forms shall be completed in all details by the applicant, signed by him in his name in his own behalf or as authorized agent for the owner as the case may be. One approved copy shall be returned to the applicant for his own file and one copy shall be filed with the building inspector. Permits shall be void after six months from their date of issuance unless construction of the proposed facility has begun. Construction of the minimum facilities required by this article and shown on the approved plat for any one phase of development must be completed prior to occupancy of any portion of that specific phase.
(c)
Accompanying the application for a permit to construct and/or operate a manufactured home park or trailer camp shall be a plot plan prepared by a registered engineer or surveyor showing the following information:
(1)
All property lines with dimensions.
(2)
Adjoining street with right-of-way and paving widths.
(3)
Locations of buildings and other structures referenced to the centerline of adjoining streets and to the property lines of the tract.
(4)
Entrances and exits, streets, right-of-way lines, and off-street parking areas.
(5)
North arrow, district number and land lot, date, name of owner, etc.
(6)
Driveways, walkways, and manufactured home or camper sites.
(7)
Location and size of all utility lines.
(8)
Location of outdoor lighting.
(9)
Location of creeks, ponds, storm drainage facilities, etc.
(10)
Location of all easements.
(11)
Recreational areas with description of type and quantity of facilities to be provided.
(12)
Any additional information specifically requested by the building inspector.
(d)
All provisions of this article, including plat permitting requirements, must be met before occupancy of the structure.
(Code 1991, § 5-82; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. V, § 501)
Sec. 20-95. - Discontinuance of nonconforming uses.
In the event the use of any nonconforming manufactured home is discontinued for a period of six months or more, or is at least 50 percent destroyed, the same may not be continued as a nonconforming use or moved to any other conforming site. No nonconforming mobile home may be replaced with another nonconforming unit.
(Code 1991, § 5-83; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. V, § 502)
Sec. 20-96. - Area and space requirements.
A manufactured home park or trailer camp shall be no less than three acres in size, and shall be located on a well-drained site, properly graded to ensure positive drainage and freedom from stagnant pools of water. A minimum park width of 200 feet shall be required. Not more than 60 percent of the total land area of the park may be utilized for mobile home spaces. The remaining 40 percent of land area shall be used for streets, open spaces, buffer zones and recreational areas as approved by the building inspector.
(Code 1991, § 5-84; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VI, § 601)
Sec. 20-97. - Manufactured home park lots.
(a)
Each manufactured home lot shall be at least 50 feet in width and shall provide a minimum of 4,000 square feet in area.
(b)
No manufactured home shall be located within:
(1)
Ten feet of its individual lot line.
(2)
20 feet of another such unit side by side or end to end.
(3)
50 feet of the right-of-way line of any public street or highway.
(4)
20 feet of any exterior boundary of the park or subdivision excepting any public street or highway.
(5)
20 feet of any service building within the park or subdivision.
(Code 1991, § 5-85; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VI, § 602)
Sec. 20-98. - Travel trailer and camper spaces.
Each space provided for transient parking and camping at an approved trailer camp shall contain at least 3,000 square feet and shall be a minimum of 40 feet in width.
(Code 1991, § 5-86; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VI, § 603)
Sec. 20-99. - Streets; driveways.
All manufactured home spaces in manufactured home parks shall abut upon a paved internal street not less than 20 feet in width. Spaces at trailer camps may abut a graved surfaced driveway.
(Code 1991, § 5-87; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VI, § 604)
Sec. 20-100. - Automobile parking.
Two off-street paved parking spaces shall be provided for each manufactured home lot at a location convenient to the occupants of the manufactured home. One off-street automobile parking space shall be provided at each camp space at a travel trailer park.
(Code 1991, § 5-88; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VI, § 605)
Sec. 20-101. - Other minimum facilities for manufactured home parks.
Each manufactured home park or subdivision shall provide the following minimum facilities:
(1)
All-weather walkways to each trailer space and to all service buildings and public facilities.
(2)
A weatherproof electric outlet supplying 220-volt, 100-ampere power at each manufactured home lot. Overhead power lines shall not be less than 15 feet above the ground. The electrical system shall conform in every respect to the National Electrical Code.
(3)
Each manufactured home space shall be connected with the city water and sewerage system in a proper manner.
(4)
Each manufactured home lot shall be located within 500 feet of a fire hydrant. Existing nonconforming parks shall be exempted from this provision until adequate municipal water supply becomes available.
(5)
On the side and rear property lines of the park or subdivision (excepting only ingress and egress points) there shall be a planting easement of at least ten feet upon which manufactured homes or buildings shall not be permitted and which shall be planted and permanently maintained in trees and shrubbery.
(6)
Garbage and refuse collection facilities acceptable to the city.
(7)
Outdoor lighting for all streets, driveways, parking areas and walkways.
(Code 1991, § 5-89; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VI, § 606)
Sec. 20-102. - Enforcement.
The building inspector and the county board of health shall be among the officials charged with enforcing this article.
(Code 1991, § 5-90; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VII, § 701)
Sec. 20-103. - Violation and penalty.
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall upon conviction thereof be subject to fine or imprisonment, or both, as provided by the law. Any structure, use, or improvement established contrary to the provisions of this article is hereby declared to be a nuisance and may be abated, enjoined or otherwise regulated by all existing laws and regulations of the city. Upon violation of any provisions of this article, any license granted by the city for the operation of a manufactured home park, manufactured home subdivision, trailer camp, or for the occupancy of a manufactured home may, at the option of the mayor and council, be revoked or suspended. In providing for penalties under the law, each day that a violation occurs shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1991, § 5-91; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VII, § 702)
Sec. 20-104. - Conflict with other laws.
Whenever the regulations of this article require or impose more restrictive standards than are required in or under any statute or covenant, the requirements of this article shall govern. Whenever the provisions of any statute or covenant require more restrictive standards than are required by this article, the provision of such statute or covenant shall govern.
(Code 1991, § 5-92; Ord. of 10-12-1987, art. VIII, § 801)
