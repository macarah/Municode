ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Fiscal year.
The fiscal year of the city shall be from July 1 of each year through June 30 of the following year.
(Code 1974, § 2-1; Code 1991, § 2-1)
State Law reference— Fiscal year, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3.

ARTICLE II. - MAYOR AND COUNCIL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-21. - Filling vacancies.
In case of the suspension from office of any publicly elected municipal official, the remaining members of the mayor and council shall appoint an acting public official to fill such vacancy for the duration of the suspension.
(Ord. of 11-14-1988(1); Code 1991, § 2-26)
Sec. 2-22. - Appointments by mayor.
In case of any vacancy in any office which is filled by election of the mayor and council, the mayor shall appoint someone to fill such vacancy until the mayor and council elects a replacement to fill the unexpired term.
(Code 1974, § 2-3; Code 1991, § 2-28)
Secs. 2-23—2-47. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS

Sec. 2-48. - Call of special meetings.
Special meetings of the mayor and council may be called by the mayor, or in case of his absence from the city, by two councilmembers, in which event the members of the council within the city and the clerk shall be notified. Any business may be transacted at a called meeting that may be done at a regular meeting.
(Code 1974, § 2-39; Code 1991, § 2-47)
Sec. 2-49. - Presiding officer designated.
The mayor or, in his absence, the mayor pro tem, shall preside at all meetings of the mayor and council.
(Code 1974, § 2-40; Code 1991, § 2-48)
Sec. 2-50. - General duties of presiding officer; appeals from rulings.
The mayor shall preserve order and decorum at meetings of the mayor and council, appoint all committees, unless otherwise directed by the council, and perform such other duties and exercise such other powers as are usual and customary and authorized or required by parliamentary law. Any person interested and dissatisfied with the mayor's rulings may appeal to the council.
(Code 1974, § 2-41; Code 1991, § 2-49)
Sec. 2-51. - Quorum.
Any three members of the council shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business, or any two members and the mayor.
(Code 1974, § 2-43; Code 1991, § 2-50)
Sec. 2-52. - Votes necessary for elections.
A majority of the votes cast shall be necessary to an election by the council. In case of a tie, the mayor shall cast the deciding vote.
(Code 1974, § 2-44; Code 1991, § 2-51)
Sec. 2-53. - Rules of procedure and order of business.
The mayor and council shall prescribe the rules governing the procedure and order of business at its meetings; such rules shall not conflict with any provision of this division.
(Code 1974, § 2-45; Code 1991, § 2-52)
Sec. 2-54. - When motions to reconsider may be made.
Motions to reconsider may be made at a meeting of the mayor and council by anyone who voted with the majority at any time the subject is passed upon, in the order of business, or when the call of miscellaneous business is reached.
(Code 1974, § 2-47; Code 1991, § 2-53)
Sec. 2-55. - Address by nonmembers.
Persons not members of the council shall not address the same when in session, except by permission.
(Code 1974, § 2-48; Code 1991, § 2-54)
Sec. 2-56. - Minutes.
Minutes shall be kept of all the transactions of the mayor and council showing, among other things, the members present, how each member votes on all motions and where there is a tie and the mayor's vote decides the question.
(Code 1974, § 2-49; Code 1991, § 2-55)
Sec. 2-57. - Committee report required for consideration of accounts.
Accounts against the city shall not be considered by the mayor and council unless reported by the appropriate committee.
(Code 1974, § 2-50; Code 1991, § 2-56)
Secs. 2-58—2-87. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-88. - Official oaths.
The oath of office for city officers, unless specially prescribed, shall be for the faithful performance of official duties.
(Code 1974, § 2-4; Code 1991, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-89. - Official bonds—To whom payable; attestation and approval; condition.
(a)
The bonds of all officers of the city, and other bonds required to be given, where no other provision is made, shall be payable to the mayor and council.
(b)
Official bonds may be attested by any member of the council, the city clerk or by any officer of the state authorized to attest deeds, and they shall be approved by the mayor.
(c)
The condition of official bonds shall be for the faithful performance of the duties of the office, and such other conditions as may be specially prescribed.
(d)
The obligation of official bonds taken under this Code shall be obligatory upon the principal and sureties thereon:
(1)
For any breach of the condition during the time the officer continues in office or discharges any of the duties thereof.
(2)
For the faithful discharge of any duties which may be required of such officer by any law or ordinance, passed subsequently to the execution of such bond although no such condition is expressed therein.
(3)
For the use and benefit of every person who is injured, as well as by any wrongful act committed under color of his office as by his failure to perform, or by the improper or neglectful performance of those duties imposed by law.
(Code 1974, § 2-5; Code 1991, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-90. - Official bonds—Not discharged by single recovery; joint and several.
(a)
An official bond is not discharged by a single recovery, but proceedings may be had from time to time until the whole penalty is exhausted against the officer and his sureties, or either.
(b)
Official bonds are joint and several, whether so set forth or not.
(Code 1974, § 2-6; Code 1991, § 2-4)
Sec. 2-91. - Official bonds—Validity when not in conformity with law; release of sureties.
(a)
Official bonds are valid though not in conformity to law. When an officer acts under a bond which is not in the penalty, payable and conditioned or approved and filed as required by law, such bond shall stand the same as if it were made in conformity to law.
(b)
On good cause shown and on notice to the principal, the mayor and council may, in its discretion, relieve the sureties on any official bond, and cause the officer involved to give other security. Any such officer failing to give such other security shall be dismissed from his office.
(Code 1974, § 2-7; Code 1991, § 2-5)
State Law reference— Official bonds, O.C.G.A. § 45-4-1 et seq.
Secs. 2-92—2-110. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CITY MANAGER

Sec. 2-111. - Office created; term and compensation.
The office of city manager is hereby created. The city manager shall hold office at such time and at such compensation as the mayor and council may direct or prescribe.
(Code 1974, § 2-19; Code 1991, § 2-91)
Sec. 2-112. - General powers and duties.
The city manager shall supervise and control the affairs of the city and shall perform such duties as are prescribed by this Code and by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1974, § 2-20; Code 1991, § 2-92)
Sec. 2-113. - Investigation of complaints.
Complaints against the city shall be filed with the city manager and he shall make such investigations thereof as he may deem necessary.
(Code 1974, § 2-24; Code 1991, § 2-93)
Sec. 2-114. - To supervise police department.
The city manager shall supervise the police department.
(Code 1974, § 2-25; Code 1991, § 2-94)
Sec. 2-115. - Actions subject to supervision, approval of mayor and council.
All actions of the city manager shall be subject to the direction, supervision, control and approval of the mayor and council.
(Code 1974, § 2-26; Code 1991, § 2-95)
Sec. 2-116. - Reports.
The city manager shall make such reports of his actions as are required or requested by the mayor and council.
(Code 1974, § 2-27; Code 1991, § 2-96)
Sec. 2-117. - Taxes; tax returns.
The city manager shall be responsible for the collection of taxes and the receipt of tax returns for the city.
(Code 1991, § 2-97; Res. of 8-18-1986)
Secs. 2-118—2-147. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - CITY CLERK

Sec. 2-148. - Office created; time and compensation.
The office of city clerk is hereby created. The city clerk shall hold office at such time and at such compensation as the mayor, council and city manager shall prescribe.
(Code 1991, § 2-136; Res. of 8-18-1986)
Sec. 2-149. - General powers and duties.
The city clerk shall supervise and control the maintenance of records and documents for the mayor and council, including those of the water and sewerage departments, receive, account for and disburse all city funds, supervise other clerical and accounting personnel, and shall perform such duties as are prescribed by this Code and by the mayor, council and city manager from time to time.
(Code 1991, § 2-137; Res. of 8-18-1986)
Sec. 2-150. - Duties as ex officio treasurer.
The city clerk shall be the ex officio treasurer and shall perform the duties prescribed for the treasurer by the provisions of this Code, ordinances of the city and laws of the state.
(Code 1991, § 2-138; Res. of 8-18-1986)
Sec. 2-151. - Duties as ex officio tax assessor.
The city clerk shall be the ex officio tax assessor and shall perform the duties prescribed for the tax assessor by the provisions of this Code, ordinances of the city and laws of the state.
(Code 1991, § 2-139; Res. of 8-18-1986)
Sec. 2-152. - Actions subject to the supervision and approval of the mayor, council and city manager.
All actions of the city clerk shall be subject to the direction, supervision, control and approval of the mayor, council and city manager.
(Code 1991, § 2-140; Res. of 8-18-1986)
Sec. 2-153. - Reports to the mayor, council and city manager.
The city clerk shall make such reports of his actions as are required or requested by the mayor, council and city manager.
(Code 1991, § 2-141; Res. of 8-18-1986)
Secs. 2-154—2-174. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - SOCIAL SECURITY[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Social security coverage for city employees, O.C.G.A. § 47-18-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-202. - Extension of benefits.
It is hereby declared to be the policy and purpose of the city to extend, under the agreement entered into by the state and the Federal Social Security Administration, to the employees and officials thereof, and its instrumentalities, not excluded by law or by this division, the benefits of the system of old-age and survivors insurance, as authorized by the Federal Social Security Act, the State Enabling Act, and amendments thereto. In pursuance of such policy, and for that purpose, the city shall take such action as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1974, § 15-12; Code 1991, § 2-226)
Sec. 2-203. - Execution of agreements; employees included.
The mayor is hereby authorized and directed to execute all necessary agreements and amendments thereto with the state employees' retirement system for coverage of city employees and officials, as provided for in section 2-202, including police officers, firefighters and any employees in positions covered by a public retirement system.
(Code 1974, § 15-13; Code 1991, § 2-227)
State Law reference— Plans for coverage submitted to state, O.C.G.A. § 47-18-41.
Sec. 2-204. - Exclusions.
There is hereby excluded from this division any authority to make any agreement with respect to any position, employee or official not authorized to be covered by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1974, § 15-14; Code 1991, § 2-228)
Sec. 2-205. - Withholding employees' contributions.
Withholdings from salaries or wages of employees and officials for the purpose provided for in section 2-202 are hereby authorized to be made in the amounts and at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations, and shall be paid over to the state agency.
(Code 1974, § 15-15; Code 1991, § 2-229)
Sec. 2-206. - Appropriations by city.
There shall be appropriated from the general fund of the city such amounts at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations for employer's contributions, administrative expenses and a guaranty fund to ensure that the state will not incur any debt or loss as a result of the agreement referred to in section 2-203. Such funds shall be paid over to the state employees' retirement system in accordance with regulations established by such agency.
(Code 1974, § 15-16; Code 1991, § 2-230)
State Law reference— State agency, O.C.G.A. § 47-18-2(8).
Sec. 2-207. - Records and reports.
The city shall keep such records and make such reports as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations concerning the federal old-age and survivors insurance program.
(Code 1974, § 15-17; Code 1991, § 2-231)
Secs. 2-208—2-237. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Development authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-268. - Statement of need.
There is hereby determined and declared a pressing, existing and future need for a development authority (as more fully described and defined in the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.) to function in the city for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the city thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry and, in the furtherance of that purpose, to finance projects permitted thereby.
(Code 1974, § 2-92; Code 1991, § 2-206)
Sec. 2-269. - Activation of development authority.
There is hereby activated in the city a public body corporate and politic known as the "Development Authority of the City of Bowdon" which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.).
(Code 1974, § 2-93; Code 1991, § 2-207)
Sec. 2-270. - Members appointed.
There shall be appointed as members of the board of directors of the development authority of the city, seven persons, each of whom is a taxpayer of the city and none of whom is an officer or employee of the city.
(Code 1974, § 2-94; Code 1991, § 2-208)
Sec. 2-271. - Terms of directors.
The persons named as directors shall serve in such capacity for six-year staggered terms, and if at the end of any term of office of any director a successor thereto shall not have been appointed, then the director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so appointed.
(Code 1974, § 2-95; Code 1991, § 2-209)
Sec. 2-272. - Board to organize and carry out duties and responsibilities.
The board of directors hereinbefore elected shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Code 1974, § 2-96; Code 1991, § 2-210)
