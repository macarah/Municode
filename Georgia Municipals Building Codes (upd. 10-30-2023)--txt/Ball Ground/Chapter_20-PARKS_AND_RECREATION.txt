ARTICLE II. - PUBLIC CONDUCT IN PARKS AND RECREATION FACILITIES

Sec. 20-19. - Use of facilities; reservations.
(a)
All park properties are to be used on a first-come, first-served basis. Reservations will be taken for ball fields, tennis courts and pavilions.
(b)
The first priority for the use of park properties shall be given to the residents of the city and the county.
(c)
The practice time for the ball fields shall be on a first-come, first-served basis. Scheduled league and tournament play shall have first priority.
(d)
No person in a park shall use any portion of the picnic areas, or any of the buildings or structures therein, except validly reserved ball fields, tennis courts and pavilions for the purpose of holding picnics to the exclusion of other persons. No person shall use such areas and facilities for an unreasonable time if the facilities are crowded.
(e)
Each person using the public parks and grounds shall clean up all debris, extinguish all fires when such fires are permitted, and leave the premises in good order and the facilities in a neat and sanitary condition.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-1; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-20. - Hours of operation.
(a)
City parks shall be open to the public daily between the hours of 7:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. No person other than city personnel conducting city business, or engaging in a city sponsored event, shall occupy or be present in such parks during any other hours.
(b)
Any section or part of a park, or an entire park, may be declared closed to the public by the city manager or chief of police at any time and for any interval of time, either temporarily or at regular stated intervals.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-2; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-21. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
No person using the parks, grounds or facilities shall:
(1)
Hunt, molest, harm, frighten, kill, trap, chase, tease, shoot or throw missiles at any animal, reptile or bird; or remove or have in his possession the young of any wild animal, or the eggs, nest or young of any other animal.
(2)
Disturb the peace, or use any language reasonably calculated to provoke an immediate breach of the peace.
(3)
Endanger the safety of any person by any conduct or act.
(4)
Engage in fighting.
(5)
Carry, possess or drink any alcoholic beverages unless specifically approved as a public event, approved in writing by the city manager (wine tasting, wine and arts festival etc.).
(6)
Prevent any person from using any park, or any of its facilities, or interfere with such use.
(7)
Swim, bathe or wade in any waters or waterways in or adjacent to any park.
(8)
Use boats, canoes, or any other flotation device in any waters or waterways, except by city employees or contractors involved in parks maintenance activities.
(9)
Violate the noise ordinance as per city ordinance, with the exception of city sponsored events.
(10)
Skateboard in any area not specifically designed for that use.
(11)
Park overnight. Vehicles remaining in any city park parking area after 10:00 p.m. are subject to being towed.
(b)
Children over 12 years of age are prohibited from climbing on or playing on the children's playground structure at Calvin Farmer Park.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-3; Ord. of 6-26-2014; Ord. of 10-9-2014)
Sec. 20-22. - Motor vehicles.
No person shall drive or park any motorized vehicle except on streets, driveways or parking lots in the park, or park or leave such a vehicle in any place other than one established for public parking.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-4; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-23. - Limitations on games.
No person shall take part in or abet the playing of any games involving objects such as balls, stones, arrows, javelins or model airplanes except in areas set apart for such forms of recreation. The playing of rough or potentially dangerous games such as football, baseball and soccer is prohibited except on the fields, courts, or areas provided therefor.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-5; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-24. - Fires restricted.
No person shall build or attempt to build a fire except in areas and under regulations as may be designated by the recreation director. No person shall drop, throw or otherwise scatter lighted matches, burning cigarettes or cigars, tobacco paper of other inflammable material within any park area or on any contiguous highway, road or street.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-6; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-25. - Camping prohibited.
No person shall set up tents, shacks or any other temporary shelter for the purpose of overnight camping, nor shall any person leave in a park after closing hours any movable structure or special vehicle to be used, or that could be used, for such purposes. Exceptions to this rule may be granted by the city manager for city sanctioned events.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-7; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-26. - Unleashed dogs, animals prohibited.
No person shall bring any dangerous animal into any park. Except as provided in O.C.G.A. § 30-40-1, no dog may be in any park unless such dog is on a leash not more than ten feet long and under full control of the person holding the leash. No animals are permitted in the viewing areas of any baseball or softball field and no animals are permitted within 100 feet of any concession stand.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-8; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-27. - Sales prohibited.
No person shall vend, sell, peddle or offer for sale any commodity or article within any park, except at city sanctioned fairs and events.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-9; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-28. - Signs prohibited.
No person shall paste, glue, tack or otherwise post any sign, placard, advertisement or inscription whatever, nor shall any person erect or cause to be erected any sign whatever on any public lands or highways or roads adjacent to a park. This provision shall not apply to any properly authorized government official in pursuit of any official duty.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-10; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-29. - Outdoor tennis and basketball courts.
No person shall be permitted within the limits of the outdoor tennis and basketball courts unless they are actively engaged in the sport of tennis or basketball. Spectators shall remain outside the fence. No other activities such as running, jumping, bicycling, skateboarding, roller blading, etc., are allowed on the tennis and basketball court surfaces. Only soft soled shoes such as basketball shoes or tennis shoes are permitted on the basketball and tennis court surfaces.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-11; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
Sec. 20-30. - Ball Ground city gym.
Use of the city gym will be on a reservation basis only with priority given to basketball. Use of the facility will be scheduled through the parks and recreation committee with a rental fee of $25.00 per hour of use. This rate is subject to change from time to time and may be increased without notice except where a rental contract has already been entered. Only basketball shoes are permitted on the court surface. No animals are allowed in the gym.
(Code 2010, § 4-3-12; Ord. of 6-26-2014)
