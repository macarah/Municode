ARTICLE VIII. - RECORDS RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION

Sec. 2-444. - Document retention.
Pursuant to the Georgia Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq., the city adopts the records management plan and record retention schedules recommended by the secretary of state, as amended from time to time.
(Ord. of 2-15-2010; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, § 2.13.1, 7-18-2016)
Sec. 2-445. - Fees.
The city clerk shall charge the full amount permitted by state law to copy the records subject to O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq. If compilation and replication of such documents takes more than 15 minutes, the city clerk may charge an additional reasonable administrative charge.
(Ord. of 2-15-2010; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, § 2.13.2, 7-18-2016)
Sec. 2-446. - Records manager.
Except as otherwise provided, the city clerk shall be the records manager and shall coordinate all records management for the city including storage, archiving, and destruction of records, with the exception of the records of the police department and municipal court.
(Ord. of 2-15-2010; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, § 2.13.3, 7-18-2016)
Sec. 2-447. - Police records.
The support services commander of the city police department has been designated as the records manager for the police department as approved in the police department standard operating procedures manual.
(Ord. of 2-15-2010; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, § 2.13.4, 7-18-2016)
Sec. 2-448. - Municipal court records.
The municipal court clerk shall be designated as the records manager for the municipal court.
(Ord. of 2-15-2010; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, § 2.13.5, 7-18-2016)
Sec. 2-449. - Retention schedule and destruction.
Records shall be maintained according to the approved retention schedule, and no record shall be destroyed except as provided in the approved retention schedule. Any changes to the retention schedules shall be adopted by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 2-15-2010; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, § 2.13.6, 7-18-2016)
Sec. 2-450. - Open record request.
All requests made under the Georgia Open Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq., with the exception of routine requests of the police department and municipal court, shall be made to the city clerk, who shall be charged with compliance with said request in accordance with law and procedure mandated by the above-referenced sections of state law.
(Ord. of 2-15-2010; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, § 2.13.7, 7-18-2016)
Secs. 2-451—2-468. - Reserved.
