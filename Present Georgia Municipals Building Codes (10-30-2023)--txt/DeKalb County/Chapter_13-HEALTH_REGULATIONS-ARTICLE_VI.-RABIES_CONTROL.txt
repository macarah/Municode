Sec. 13-126. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal means any domesticated or undomesticated animal, and includes but is not limited to dogs and cats, wild carnivores, cattle and other livestock, bats, rodents and rabbits, and opossums.
Animal control center means the facility established by the board of commissioners and used by the director for the detention of animals.
Bite (bitten) means any injury caused by an animal which results in breakage of the skin.
County rabies control officer means the person responsible for assuring compliance with the provisions of this article in accordance with section 13-145.
Home quarantine means secure confinement and isolation of an animal from exposure to humans and other animals at the residence of the owner of the animal where access is provided to the animal control center for monitoring the condition of the animal.
Quarantine means the secure confinement and isolation of an animal from exposure to other animals in the animal control center or a veterinary hospital or other approved boarding kennel.
Vaccinated or inoculated means the injection of antirabies vaccine into the body of an animal by a veterinarian using a vaccine approved by the Georgia Department of Human Resources.
Veterinarian means a person licensed to practice veterinary medicine in accordance with O.C.G.A. tit. 43, ch. 50 [§ 43-50-1 et seq.]
(Code 1976, § 12-9001; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 13-127. - Notification when person bitten.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the person bitten by any animal to notify immediately the director, who in turn shall notify the animal control center. Parents or guardians are responsible for notification when a minor has been bitten.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the director to notify any person bitten by an animal which tests positive for rabies of such test results.
(Code 1976, § 12-9002; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-128. - Notification by owner of biting animal or animal exhibiting signs of rabies.
It shall be the duty of the owner, custodian or person having possession of, or knowledge of, any animal which has bitten any person or animal, or any animal which exhibits any signs of rabies, to notify the animal control center, which in turn shall notify the director.
(Code 1976, § 12-9003; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-129. - Quarantine of biting dogs and cats.
(a)
Any dog or cat known or suspected to have bitten any person or persons shall be ordered by the animal control center to be quarantined and observed for a period of not less than ten (10) days from the date of the biting. Under no circumstances shall the dog or cat be destroyed during this time. If the dog or cat is normal after ten (10) days, the animal control center may order its release or otherwise dispose of it as the occasion may demand. If at any time during the ten (10) days of quarantine, the dog or cat becomes rabid or exhibits symptoms of rabies, the animal control center shall humanely and safely destroy the dog or cat and submit its head for examination as required in section 13-132.
(b)
In the event no owner or harborer of a biting dog or cat can be found, the animal control center shall capture and quarantine the biting dog or cat for a period of not less than ten (10) days. After ten (10) days, the animal control center may release the dog or cat from quarantine, if normal. If at any time during the ten (10) days of quarantine the dog or cat becomes rabid or exhibits symptoms or rabies, the animal control center shall humanely and safely destroy the dog or cat and submit its head for examination as required in section 13-132.
(c)
The animal control center shall maintain records of the impounding or disposition of any dog or cat quarantined under subsection (a) or (b) of this section and shall provide such records to the director upon request.
(d)
Home quarantine may be permitted in lieu of quarantine except during declaration of an area of infection.
(e)
This section shall apply to any biting dog or cat and does not exempt any dog or cat which has been vaccinated or inoculated.
(f)
The owner of any animal ordered quarantined under this section shall not refuse to comply with such order or remove the animal from DeKalb County prior to termination of the quarantine order without the approval of the animal control center.
(Code 1976, § 12-9004; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-130. - Confinement of animal bitten by rabid animal.
(a)
Any unvaccinated dog or cat bitten by an animal known or suspected to be rabid may be destroyed by order of the animal control center, or ordered by the animal control center to be quarantined and observed for a period of six (6) months. If rabies has not developed in such animal by the end of six (6) months, the animal control center may order the animal released from quarantine. The director shall be notified by the animal control center of the confinement and disposition of the animal. The animal control center shall maintain records of the confinement and disposition of animals and shall provide such records to the director upon request. The animal shall be revaccinated one (1) month before release.
(b)
Any vaccinated dog or cat bitten by an animal known or suspected to be rabid shall be revaccinated and ordered quarantined by the animal control center for a period of ninety (90) days.
(c)
Home quarantine may be permitted in lieu of quarantine except during declaration of an area of infection.
(d)
In the event no owner of the bitten dog or cat can be found, the animal control center may, at any time during the period of confinement, humanely dispatch or otherwise dispose of the bitten animal.
(Code 1976, § 12-9005; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-131. - General quarantine of dogs or cats within area of infection; epidemic measures.
(a)
In the event rabies is known to exist within DeKalb County or a part of DeKalb County, the director shall determine an area of infection and may provide for immunization as shall be indicated for the prevention and control of the disease.
(b)
All dogs or cats within the area of infection may be ordered by the director to be quarantined or confined and not exposed to other animals for a period of not less than ninety (90) days from the date of the last occurrence. All dogs or cats found on public highways or streets, or anywhere off the premises of the owner except on a leash, shall be quarantined. Dogs or cats thus impounded, if not reclaimed by the owner within five (5) days, shall be humanely dispatched or otherwise disposed of at the discretion of the animal control center.
(c)
The director shall give public notice of such quarantine or confinement by publication in local newspapers, or by posting of notice in public places, or by both.
(d)
Such quarantine or confinement shall apply to all dogs or cats and does not exempt dogs or cats which have been vaccinated or inoculated.
(Code 1976, § 12-9006; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-132. - Examination of specimens.
The head of any dog or cat suspected of having rabies at the time of its death shall be submitted by the animal control center for examination to a public health laboratory operated by the Georgia Department of Human Resources.
(Code 1976, § 12-9007; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-133. - Laboratory results.
(a)
The animal control center shall furnish the director with a copy of any laboratory results obtained on specimens submitted under sections 13-132 and 13-134 through 13-136.
(b)
Animals known to transmit rabies which have escaped or are otherwise unavailable for examination shall be presumed rabid.
(Code 1976, § 12-9008; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-134. - Biting wild carnivores.
Any wild carnivore, which includes, but is not limited to, foxes, skunks, raccoons and bobcats, that has bitten a person or animal shall be destroyed by order of the animal control center, and its head shall be submitted for examination to a public health laboratory operated by the Georgia Department of Human Resources.
(Code 1976, § 12-9009; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-135. - Biting bats.
Any bat which bites a person or domestic animal shall be destroyed by order of the animal control center and the entire bat shall be submitted for examination to a public health laboratory operated by the Georgia Department of Human Resources.
(Code 1976, § 12-9010; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-136. - Unprovoked biting rodents and rabbits.
Only unprovoked rabbits, chipmunks, rats and mice which attack and bite a person or animal shall be destroyed by order of the animal control center and submitted for examination to a public health laboratory operated by the Georgia Department of Human Resources. A bite from a rodent or rabbit while it is being fed is not considered as an unprovoked attack.
(Code 1976, § 12-9011; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-137. - Disposition of bitten cattle and other livestock.
Any cattle or livestock known to have been bitten by a rabid animal shall be slaughtered immediately, or at the request of the owner, placed in strict confinement for six (6) months, after which the cattle or livestock may be released by order of the director, if normal, or slaughtered, if rabid.
(Code 1976, § 12-9012; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-138. - Inoculation of dogs, cats.
Any person who owns, keeps, maintains or harbors any dog or cat, three (3) months of age or over, at any place within DeKalb County shall have the dog or cat vaccinated at age three (3) months, and on or before the expiration date of the certificate of inoculation required in section 13-139.
(Code 1976, § 12-9013)
Cross reference— Rabies inoculation required, §§ 5-12, 5-14.

Sec. 13-139. - State-issued tag, certificate of inoculation.
(a)
Any person who owns, keeps, maintains or harbors any dog or cat, age three (3) months or over, shall obtain a Georgia Department of Human Resources issued numbered tag and certificate of inoculation in accordance with chapter 5 of this Code. The certificate shall include the tag number and shall be signed by a veterinarian. The numbered tag so issued shall be attached to a collar or harness which shall be worn by the dog or cat at all times.
(b)
It shall be the duty of any veterinarian issuing tags and certificates of inoculation to provide a copy of the registration certificate to the animal control center within forty-five (45) days of the date of inoculation.
(Code 1976, § 12-9014; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Cross reference— Rabies inoculation, §§ 5-12, 5-14.
State Law reference— Rabies tags, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-6.
Sec. 13-140. - Confinement of puppies.
Dogs under age three (3) months shall be confined to their owner's premises and not allowed to roam at large.
(Code 1976, § 12-9015; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-141. - Impoundment of dogs, cats not wearing tags or not vaccinated.
Any dog or cat, age three (3) months or over, not displaying a numbered tag or whose owner does not possess a certificate of inoculation for said dog or cat, and not confined on the premises of the owner, may be confined in the animal control center and disposed of as permitted in this article.
(Code 1976, § 12-9016; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-142. - Exemptions from tagging and inoculation.
Dogs entering DeKalb County for the purpose of performing or for a temporary stay of fourteen (14) days or less, and kept under direct control by their owners or handlers, are exempt from the requirements of section 13-139(a).
(Code 1976, § 12-9017; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-143. - When impoundment authorized; disclaimer of liability.
(a)
Any animal may be captured and confined by the animal control center for either of the following reasons:
(1)
Being vicious.
(2)
Having bitten a person or animal, or which has been bitten by an animal suspected of having rabies.
(b)
In addition to subsection (a) above, any dog or cat may be captured and contained for any of the following reasons:
(1)
Ownership being unknown.
(2)
Being off the premises of its owner.
(3)
Not having a numbered tag on a collar or harness, as required in section 13-139(a).
(4)
Whose owner has no certificate or an expired certificate of inoculation for the animal.
(c)
The director and other officials and employees of DeKalb County have no liability for any animal which incurs a disease or is injured while being handled, transported or impounded pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(Code 1976, § 12-9018; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-144. - Animal control center.
(a)
The animal control center established by the board of commissioners of DeKalb County shall be used by the director for the confinement of animals.
(b)
The animal control center shall be capable of holding in confinement dogs and other small animals for the purpose of observation and, if necessary, destruction.
(c)
The animal control center shall notify the owner or person having right of possession of any animal impounded. If said animal is not claimed by the owner or the person having right of possession within a period of time established by the animal control center, then it shall be the duty of the animal control center to order the animal to be painlessly destroyed, or otherwise disposed of at the discretion of the animal control center.
(d)
Animals under observation shall remain in the animal control center for the periods established in sections 13-129 through 13-131. However, if no owner of the animal can be found, the animal control center may humanely destroy the animal at any time during the period of confinement.
(Code 1976, § 12-9019; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 13-145. - County rabies control officer.
The director is designated as the county rabies control officer and shall be responsible for assuring compliance with this article. The director of the department in which the animal control center is established by the board of commissioners of DeKalb County may exercise the powers and duties of the county rabies control officer with respect to sections 13-128 through 13-130, and sections 13-132 through 13-144, and shall be responsible for the enforcement thereof, if authorized by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1976, § 12-9020; Ord. No. 92-05, § 1, 4-14-92)
State Law reference— County rabies control officer, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-7.
