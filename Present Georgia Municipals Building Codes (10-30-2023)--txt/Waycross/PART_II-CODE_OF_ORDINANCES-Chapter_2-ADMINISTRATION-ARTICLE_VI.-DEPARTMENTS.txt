ARTICLE VI. - DEPARTMENTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-263. - Donations, gifts and bequests.
(a)
Each department is authorized to receive any donation, gift or bequest of money, funds, personal or real property subject to any conditions as may be specified in the instrument giving, donating or bequeathing the property.
(b)
Any money or funds received for such purposes, unless otherwise provided by the terms of the gift or bequest, shall be deposited with the city finance director, or his/her designee, to a designated account so that the donation or gift may be withdrawn and paid out for the purpose as specified in the instrument giving or donating the funds.
(c)
If any donation or gift is specified in the instrument giving, donating or bequeathing real property for either temporary or permanent use for parks, playgrounds, other recreational purposes, animal control or any other city department, and will subject the city to additional expense for improvements, maintenance, or renewal, the acceptance of any grant or devise of real estate shall be subject to the approval of the city commission.
( Ord. No. O21-07 , § 1, 3-16-2021)
Secs. 2-264—2-287. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - COMMUNITY IMPROVEMENT DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-288. - Created.
There is hereby created a new department of the city to be known as the department of community improvement.
(Code 1977, § 2-134; Ord. of 8-6-1963, § 1)
Sec. 2-289. - Functions.
The department of community improvement is authorized and empowered, and it shall be the duty and function of such department, to plan, establish, and carry on a workable program of rehabilitation, redevelopment, and improvement of property within the city, and to perform such additional duties and functions as may be prescribed by the city commission.
(Code 1977, § 2-135; Ord. of 8-6-1963, § 2)
Sec. 2-290. - Director.
The administrative head of the department of community improvement shall be known as the director of community improvement.
(Code 1977, § 2-136; Ord. of 8-6-1963, § 3)
Sec. 2-291. - Enforcement of state minimum standard codes.
It is the policy of the city to enforce the mandatory minimum standard codes and amendments thereto pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-20(9)(B)(ii) and 8-2-25. The city shall enforce those permissive minimum standard codes and amendments thereto that are adopted by the city as set forth in section 103-47.
(Code 1977, § 2-137; Ord. No. 95-06, § 1, 2-7-1995)
Secs. 2-292—2-315. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - PURCHASING DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-316. - Created.
There is hereby created a department of the city to be known as the department of purchasing.
(Code 1977, § 2-191; Ord. No. 78-23, § 1, 10-3-1978)
Sec. 2-317. - Functions.
The purchasing department is authorized and empowered, and it shall be the duty and function of said department to purchase, acquire and secure all the materials, equipment, vehicles, supplies and contractual services required and requisitioned for the operations and functions of all city departments and agencies; also, to maintain records, logs and controls for inventory of equipment and vehicles; and dispose of surplus materials, equipment, vehicles and supplies declared as obsolete by the city commission.
(Code 1977, § 2-192; Ord. No. 78-23, § 2, 10-3-1978)
Sec. 2-318. - Director.
The administrative head of the department of purchasing shall be known as the director of purchasing and shall be appointed by the city manager, subject to the confirmation of the city commission, in like manner as are the heads of the other departments of the city.
(Code 1977, § 2-193; Ord. No. 78-23, § 3, 10-3-1978)
Sec. 2-319. - Staff.
The purchasing department shall consist of a director and such staff as may, in the judgment of the purchasing director and city manager, be necessary to the proper discharge of duties and operations of such department. All members of the purchasing department of the city shall be employees of the city.
(Code 1977, § 2-194; Ord. No. 78-23, § 4, 10-3-1978)
Sec. 2-320. - Operations of purchasing system.
(a)
The purchasing director shall procure and purchase all the materials, equipment, vehicles, supplies and contractual services for the city using competitive bidding from suppliers, to attain and buy from the "lowest and best" bidders meeting specifications. All purchases and contracts estimated to cost over $10,000.00 shall be based on: (1) Formal written bids, and shall have written notices, newspaper advertisements, specifications, sealed bids, public opening of bids, bid deposits or bonds as deemed necessary; (2) Formal request for proposals; or (3) With prior approval of the city manager, online auction services, including, but not limited to, reverse auction services; and shall be approved by the city commission. All purchases and contracts estimated to cost from $1,500.00 to $10,000.00 shall be based, wherever possible, on written competitive quotations, by letter inquiry or formal written bids, as deemed applicable by the purchasing director and the city manager, and shall be approved by the purchasing director and the city manager. All purchases and contracts estimated to cost under $1,500.00 shall be based, wherever possible, on competitive quotations, either from telephone or letter inquiry and shall be approved by the purchasing director. "Lowest and best bid" criteria shall include price, and in addition the purchasing director, city manager and city commission shall consider ability, capacity, skill, character, integrity, reputation, judgment, experience, efficiency, previous performance, compliance to law, service, maintenance ability and financial resources of bidders. Products shall be evaluated on quality, availability, adaptability, delivery time and future maintenance and service. The purchasing director or the city manager shall provide the city commission a full written report of all procurements, purchases and contracts for those purchases of services and goods costing from $5,000.00 to $10,000.00 at the next work session.
(b)
Notwithstanding any provisions of this Code to the contrary, the city commission may proclaim an emergency and may authorize the city manager or his designee to procure by purchase or lease such goods and services as are deemed necessary and appropriate for the city's emergency response effort. This emergency procurement may be made in the open market with or without advertisement. The city manager shall use his best efforts to obtain competitive pricing for such emergency goods and services, and shall, where possible require sealed bids for such goods and services, and advertise for the procurement for such goods and services. Such emergency procedures shall not be used in violation or contravention of state and federal laws or regulations. The city manager shall provide for the commission a full written report of all procurements, together with a requisition for the required goods and services during the emergency.
(Code 1977, § 2-195; Ord. No. 78-23, § 5, 10-3-1978; Ord. No. 04-03, §§ 1, 2, 2-3-2004; Ord. No. 05-02, § 1, 1-18-2005; Ord. No. O21-21 , § 1, 7-6-2021)
Sec. 2-321. - Surplus disposal.
The purchasing director shall offer to public sale by sealed bid procedures, public auction, or through a duly authorized online market, all city material, equipment, vehicles and supplies that have been declared unusable and/or obsolete. Any sealed bid shall be advertised, opened in public and the highest and best bid shall be considered as buyer unless specified otherwise by the city commission. Any public auction shall be conducted using a licensed auctioneer and following standard public auction procedures. For use of the online market, the property shall be posted and sold in accordance with the terms of the approved host site, with the property going to the highest and best bidder.
(Code 1977, § 2-196; Ord. No. 78-23, § 6, 10-3-1978; Ord. No. 01-21, § 1, 11-6-2001; Ord. No. 05-12, § 1, 5-17-2005)
Sec. 2-322. - Procedures and regulations.
The purchasing director is hereby authorized and directed to adopt such written regulations as may be necessary to the implementation of this division. Any regulations adopted by the purchasing and property control director shall become effective 30 days after submission to the mayor and city commission unless the regulations are disapproved by the mayor and city commission within 30 days. Copies of the regulation shall be filed in the office of the purchasing director and made available for public inspection during business hours.
(Code 1977, § 2-197; Ord. No. 78-23, § 7, 10-3-1978)
Sec. 2-323. - Awards to county bidders.
(a)
Notwithstanding the terms and provisions of section 2-320 and any other terms or provisions of any other section of this Code, the city shall procure or purchase materials, equipment, vehicles, supplies and contractorial services from the lowest and best county bidder (as defined in subsection (b) of this section); provided, however, that the lowest and best county bidder must submit a bid in accordance with the terms and provisions of sections 2-316 through 2-322, inclusive; further provided that the lowest and best county bidder submits a bid which does not exceed the lowest and best bid from a bidder who is not a county bidder by the lesser of five percent or $1,000.00; further provided that if no county bidder submits a bid or if all county bids exceed the lowest and best bid from a bidder who is not a county bidder by more than the lesser of five percent or $1,000.00, then the bid shall be awarded to the lowest and best bidder, irrespective of the location of the bidder.
(b)
The term "county bidder" means a bidder who, at the time the bid is submitted (and for a period of at least 30 days prior thereto), maintains an office or place of business within the county, and employs at least one employee who works at, from, or out of said office or place of business.
(c)
In determining the lowest and best county bidder, the purchasing director, city manager and commission shall consider ability, capacity, skill, character, integrity, reputation, judgment, experience, efficiency, previous performance, compliance to the law, service, maintenance ability, and financial resources of bidders. Products shall be evaluated on quality, availability, adaptability, delivery time, and future maintenance and service.
(Code 1977, § 2-199; Ord. No. 87-23, § 1, 7-7-1987)
Secs. 2-324—2-349. - Reserved.
