ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - Trespassing upon grass plots.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride, lead, operate or park any vehicle or animal upon or across any grass plot in any street, park, side park or other public property.
(Code 1975, § 17-3; Code 1985, § 18-3)
Sec. 42-2. - Animals restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any person to take any animal into property upon which any zoo is located without obtaining authority from the director of the zoological park.
(Code 1975, § 17-4; Code 1985, § 18-4)
Sec. 42-3. - Paper, rubbish, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw or place any paper, stones, bottles, broken glass or crockery or any offensive or injurious things, litter or rubbish of any kind, or any building material in or upon any park or other public property within the city.
(Code 1975, § 17-5; Code 1985, § 18-5)
Sec. 42-4. - Playing games prohibited; exception.
No football, baseball or other game shall be played in any park, square or grass plot, nor shall the same be played in any public playground, except at such times and under such conditions as may be permitted by the director of recreation which permit shall be granted as any other permit given by the director, unless otherwise provided by ordinance. This section shall not apply to designated athletic areas owned by the city and county.
(Code 1975, § 17-6; Code 1985, § 18-6)
Sec. 42-5. - Bonds to repair damage.
All persons to whom permits have been issued for any purpose which may change, deface, injure or destroy any portion of any park or other public property shall execute a bond with adequate security in double the sum of the cost to repair any damage which may be caused by any action taken under such permit. The amount of such cost shall be determined by the city.
(Code 1975, § 17-7; Code 1985, § 18-7)
Sec. 42-6. - Fees for use of facilities.
The fees and charges assessed for the use of any park or recreation facility owned or operated by the city shall be as determined from time to time by the board of city commissioners, which fees shall be on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1975, §§ 17-8, 17-9, 17-13; Code 1985, § 18-8)
Sec. 42-7. - Advertising upon city athletic and recreational facilities.
(a)
Specifications. Except as otherwise provided in this section, upon application to the director of recreation, and upon approval thereby, sponsors of events taking place on city athletic and recreational facilities may display a temporary banner or poster or other advertising device advertising the sponsor's business or product at the site of the event which is being sponsored. The type, size and style of the banner or poster or advertising device, its location and method of erection, and the content of the advertising material, must have the prior approval of the director of recreation, and such display shall be permitted only for the duration of the event sponsored.
(b)
Charge to outside agencies. Where outside agencies or individuals rent city athletic or other recreational facilities for the purpose of holding activities or events which are not a part of the operation of the city recreation department, the board of city commissioners reserves the right, upon the recommendation of the director of recreation, to establish a reasonable charge for displaying advertising for the particular event.
(c)
Team sponsors may not advertise. Subsections (a) and (b) of this section apply only to sponsors of events and activities, and does not apply to sponsors of teams. Sponsors of teams in events sponsored by the city in its general recreation program, such as adult softball games, are not permitted to display advertising as provided in subsections (a) and (b) of this section.
(d)
Erecting permanent facilities. Whenever prospective advertisers desire to place or erect permanent facilities on parks, golf courses, athletic areas or other athletic or recreational facilities, such as benches, tables or shelters, or other facilities lending themselves to advertising, the director of recreation may, if he/she deems it in the best interest of the city, permit the placement or erection of such facilities, cause specifications therefor to be prepared, and shall afford opportunity to the public for bidding on the placement or erection of such facilities, with advertising rights, in accordance with the city's regular bidding policy, and contracts will be let in accordance with such policy.
(e)
Little league exception. This section shall not apply to little league activities or to the athletic fields upon which such activities take place.
(Code 1985, § 18-9; Ord. No. 84-116, §§ 1—4, 3-13-1984)
Sec. 42-8. - Regulations for Chehaw Park adopted by reference.
(a)
Those certain regulations for Chehaw Park, a copy of which is incorporated into this chapter by reference as fully as if set forth verbatim, are hereby approved and adopted.
(b)
Any violation of any rules and regulations contained in such regulations shall constitute a violation of this section.
(Code 1975, § 17-12; Code 1985, § 18-10)
Secs. 42-9—42-38. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PORTABLE BLEACHER SEATS

Sec. 42-39. - Prerequisites.
The provisions of this article are hereby established as prerequisites for the use of city-owned portable bleacher seats by persons and organizations other than the city.
(Code 1975, § 17-21; Code 1985, § 18-21)
Sec. 42-40. - Use discretionary.
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this article, use of city-owned bleacher seats by persons or organizations shall be at the discretion of the director of recreation and parks, and compliance with the requirements of this article shall not entitle any person or organization to use the same as a matter of right.
(Code 1975, § 17-22; Code 1985, § 18-22)
Sec. 42-41. - Application.
All requests for the use of city-owned portable bleacher seats shall be in writing, addressed to the director of recreation and parks and delivered or mailed to him/her. Such requests shall show the time and date applicants desire to obtain the bleachers, and the time and date the bleachers may be picked up by the city.
(Code 1975, § 17-23; Code 1985, § 18-23)
Sec. 42-42. - Use outside county prohibited.
No applications for the use of city-owned bleacher seats outside the county will be granted, and it shall be unlawful for any person or organization using the bleachers to transport them outside the county.
(Code 1975, § 17-24; Code 1985, § 18-24)
Sec. 42-43. - User's responsibility.
Persons and organizations granted the use of city-owned bleacher seats shall be responsible for and shall pay the city for any damage to the bleachers sustained while in their possession from any cause whatsoever.
(Code 1975, § 17-25; Code 1985, § 18-25)
Sec. 42-44. - Insurance.
Permission to use city-owned bleacher seats shall be conditioned upon applicants' first furnishing the city with a policy or certificate of public liability insurance naming the city as an insured and insuring against all liability of applicants and the city for damages on account of injuries to persons or property. The policies shall be for a minimum coverage of $50,000.00 for any one person and $300,000.00 for any one accident or occurrence.
(Code 1975, § 17-26; Code 1985, § 18-26)
