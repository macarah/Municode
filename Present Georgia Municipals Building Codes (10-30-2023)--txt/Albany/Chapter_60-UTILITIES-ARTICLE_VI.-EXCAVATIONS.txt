ARTICLE VI. - EXCAVATIONS[3]

State Law reference— The Georgia Utility Facility Protection Act, O.C.G.A. § 25-9-1 et seq.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 60-909. - Permit—Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to make any excavation or opening in any street, alley or public highway of the city without first having obtained a permit from the director of engineering to do so. The city engineer shall coordinate with the water, gas and light commission prior to granting such permit.
(Code 1975, § 29-101; Code 1985, § 24-101)
Sec. 60-910. - Same—Application.
Any person desiring a permit required by this article shall make application therefor, such application to contain the following information:
(1)
Lot number, block number, addition or subdivision of the town and street number, if any, if none, then such data as will designate the exact location where the excavation is to be made;
(2)
Dimensions of the proposed excavation;
(3)
Purpose for which excavation will be used;
(4)
What precautions will be used to protect children and adults from falling into it (i.e. barricade open excavations and post warning lights for safety of persons; operate warning lights as necessary depending on light conditions, but as a minimum from dusk to dawn each day);
(5)
Indicate on the permit drawings precautions that will be implemented to prevent the excavation from becoming flooded with water as well as to protect structures, utilities, sidewalks, pavements and other facilities immediately adjacent to excavations from damage caused by settlement, lateral movement, undermining, washout and other hazards;
(6)
In case of underground storage, the kind of substance to be stored, and the capacity of the storage that will be installed;
(7)
Indicate the date that the work will start. Applicant shall submit drawings for review a minimum of 14 days prior to commencing the work. Work shall not commence until applicant has received an approved permit. The permit shall be posted at the site;
(8)
Name and address of the owner;
(9)
Distance from, direction from and generally its location with reference to all nearby streets, alleys and public places and grounds (show location of proposed excavation in relationship to street or alley right-of-way or easement);
(10)
In the case of underground storage for gasoline, a volatile of any kind or an explosive of any kind, the application shall obligate the applicant to sink the storage container to such depth that the top of it will be at least three feet below the ground surface level, and that the space above the top of the container will be filled with three feet of firm dirt, gravel or other compact filler material and well tamped up to ground surface level. Applicant must obtain permitting from any other governing authorities having jurisdiction regarding underground storage tanks and comply with applicable requirements where such requirements exceed those specified in this section;
(11)
Applicant shall indicate on drawings any and all traffic control measures necessary which will minimize inconvenience to vehicular and pedestrian traffic. When flaggers are utilized, individuals must meet local and state requirements. Contractor shall provide documentation that flaggers are certified. When trenching activities cross a roadway, applicant shall provide plans indicating all traffic control measures to be implemented to maintain traffic on all roads and streets which must be crossed by the trenching activities and must make two separate cuts so that at least one traffic lane is open at all times. All traffic controls must conform to pt. VI of the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices, ANSI D6.1.e;
(12)
Indicate on plans the limits of side slopes of excavation to comply with subpt. P of pt. 1926 of the Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA) as amended. If space restrictions or material stability issues exist, indicate on the plans provided any shoring or bracing to be used;
(13)
Exceptions. In case of emergency, a utility company or the utility companies contractor may proceed with the work in accordance with applicable requirements of all governing agencies. The utility company or the utility's contractor will obtain a permit not later than one working day after the work has begun.
(Code 1975, § 29-102; Code 1985, § 24-102)
Sec. 60-911. - Same—Fee.
Before any permit shall be issued under the provisions of this article, the applicant therefor shall pay a fee in an amount as has been established by the board of city commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 29-103; Code 1985, § 24-103)
Sec. 60-912. - Bond required.
The director of engineering shall estimate the probable cost of replacing the soil which may be removed from such public street or highway; and shall require the applicant to file a written bond in an amount sufficient to cover such cost, with two or more sufficient sureties, conditioned that the street shall be restored in as good condition as it was before the excavation or opening therein was made. Such bond shall be received and approved by the director of engineering. If the applicant elects to do so, the applicant may make a cash deposit in lieu of the written bond. The amount of the bond or cash deposit shall be established by ordinance or resolution of the board of city commissioners, from time to time, and made available for public examination in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1975, § 29-104; Code 1985, § 24-104)
Sec. 60-913. - Issuance of permit.
(a)
When the bond or deposit required in this article shall have been accepted by the director of engineering, the director of engineering shall then issue a permit for the excavation or opening named in the application, and the applicant may then proceed with the work in the exact location named in such applicant's application, and in no other place.
(b)
A permit issued pursuant to law authorizing excavation operations shall not be deemed to relieve a person from responsibility for complying with the provisions of sections 54-53 through 60-955 and the Georgia Utility Facility Protection Act (O.C.G.A. § 25-9-1 et seq.).
(Code 1975, § 29-105; Code 1985, § 24-105; Ord. No. 87-210, § 3, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-914. - Records of applications for permits.
The director of engineering shall keep an accurate record of all applications for permits under this article and the disposition made thereof.
(Code 1975, § 29-106; Code 1985, § 24-106)
Sec. 60-915. - Expiration of permits.
All permits issued under this article will expire at the end of 60 days from issuance if the work shall not have been started by then.
(Code 1975, § 29-107; Code 1985, § 24-107)
Sec. 60-916. - Authority of director of engineering.
The director of engineering shall have the authority to prescribe working rules and regulations governing work performed under the provisions of this article in order to insure traffic flow.
(Code 1975, § 29-109; Code 1985, § 24-109)
Sec. 60-917. - Protection of public.
It shall be the duty of any person making any excavation in the streets of the city to adequately and sufficiently protect all such excavations by barricades or other obstructions, including not less than one red light on each side of such excavation during the nighttime, so as to prevent accidents or injury to persons or property until such time as the excavation is resurfaced and approved by the director of engineering.
(Code 1975, § 29-110; Code 1985, § 24-110)
Sec. 60-918. - Supervision of work.
All the work of excavating or making any character of opening in any of the streets, alleys or highways of the city shall at all times be under the supervision of the director of engineering; and the person doing such work shall do the same in the manner directed by the director of engineering to the end that the streets and highways of the city shall not be unduly disturbed and traffic thereon obstructed.
(Code 1975, § 29-111; Code 1985, § 24-111)
Sec. 60-919. - Duties upon completion of work.
After the excavation or opening shall have been made, the person so making it shall backfill the excavation with such materials and in such manner as the director of engineering may require, and leave the street or highway in at least the same condition as it was before the opening therein was made.
(Code 1975, § 29-112; Code 1985, § 24-112)
Sec. 60-920. - Acceptance, inspection of completed work.
The conditions of the bond required by this article shall not be considered to have been complied with until the director of engineering shall have accepted the street or highway as being in good condition. In the event that the person making the opening shall fail to leave the highway in a good state of repair, satisfactory to the director of engineering, then the director of engineering shall call upon the bondsmen to pay for the cost of repairing such highway; provided, however, that if a cash deposit was given instead of a bond, the director of engineering may proceed to have the street or other highway put in good condition, paying for the same out of the deposit made with the director of engineering, and if there is any remainder of the deposit after paying for such work, such remainder shall be refunded to the person making the deposit.
(Code 1975, § 29-113; Code 1985, § 24-113)
Secs. 60-921—60-945. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - AVOIDANCE OF UNDERGROUND UTILITY FACILITIES

Sec. 60-946. - Statement of purpose.
(a)
Excavation or demolition operations that are performed without knowledge of the presence or location of underground utility facilities, or without reasonable precaution when such presence of location is known, has and continues to be a cause of damage to such facilities which can result in loss of life, health and property.
(b)
The purpose of this division is, among other things, to provide procedures designed to insure that persons performing excavation or demolition operations know, prior to commencing such operations, of the presence and location of underground utility facilities in the excavation or demolition area.
(c)
This division also provides that certain precautions shall be taken to avoid damage to underground utility facilities during excavation and demolition operations, for notices to affected parties when underground utility facilities are damaged, and for penalties for violations of the division.
(Code 1985, § 24-121; Ord. No. 87-210, § 1, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-947. - Definitions.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Underground utility facility includes all electrical, water, fiber optic, and/or gas lines owned by water, gas and light commission of the city, as well as sanitary and storm sewers owned by the city; whether located within or without the corporate limits of the city.
WGL means water, gas and light commission of the city.
(b)
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 25-9-3, shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this article. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this article, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 25-9-3, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 25-9-3.
(Code 1985, § 24-122; Ord. No. 87-210, § 2, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-948. - Participation in utilities protection center.
The water, gas and light commission and the city will at all times maintain its membership and participation in the Utilities Protection Center, which is a "one-call notification center" organized under and pursuant to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 25-9-1 et seq., as amended, and shall otherwise comply with the requirements of said chapter 9, as amended from time to time.
(Code 1985, § 24-123; Ord. No. 87-210, § 4, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-949. - Response to notice of intent to excavate.
Where notified in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 25-9-1 et seq., the WGL and the city shall, in addition to the duties imposed upon it by said chapter 9, furnish the following information and assistance to the person responsible for the excavation:
(1)
Furnish, in addition to the information required by O.C.G.A. § 25-9-1 et seq., any other information which would assist that person in locating and thereby avoiding damage to the underground utility facilities in locations where permanent markers do not exist. For purposes of this section the approximate location of underground utility facilities is defined as a strip of land at least four feet wide, but not wider than the width of the utility facility plus two feet on either side thereof.
(2)
Whenever the city and/or the WGL determines that the work may be done only in the presence of city personnel and WGL personnel, the city and WGL shall so notify the excavator, and it shall be unlawful for the excavator receiving such notification to perform any work unless city personnel and WGL personnel are present.
(3)
No excavation operation shall be commenced or continued until the provisions of this section and of this division have been complied with.
(Code 1985, § 24-124; Ord. No. 87-210, § 5, 12-8-1987; Ord. No. 09-141, § 11, 12-15-2009)
Sec. 60-950. - Precautions to avoid damages.
Each person responsible for any excavation activity shall:
(1)
Plan the excavation to avoid damage to and minimize interference with underground utility facilities in and near the construction area.
(2)
Maintain such a clearance between an underground utility facility and the cutting edge or point of any mechanized equipment that, taking into account the known limit of control of such cutting edge or point, may be reasonably necessary to avoid damage to such facility.
(3)
Provide such support for underground utility facilities in and near the construction area, including during backfill operations, as may be reasonably necessary for the protection of such facilities.
(4)
Prior to construction of underground facilities, proposed locations shall be marked with white flags and white paint before all other existing underground utilities have been marked in the work area covered by the proposed construction.
(Code 1985, § 24-125; Ord. No. 87-210, § 6, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-951. - Excavation damage.
(a)
Except as provided by subsection (b) of this section, each person responsible for any excavation activity that results in any damage to an underground utility facility shall, immediately upon discovery of such damage, notify the city and WGL of the location and nature of the damage and shall allow the city and WGL reasonable time to accomplish necessary repairs before completing the excavation in the immediate area of such utility.
(b)
Each person responsible for any excavation activity that results in damage to an underground utility facility permitting the escape of any flammable, toxic or corrosive gas or liquid shall, immediately upon discovery of such damage, notify the city and/or WGL and take any other action that may be reasonably necessary to protect persons and property and to minimize the hazards until arrival of the city personnel and/or WGL personnel or police and fire departments.
(Code 1985, § 24-126; Ord. No. 87-210, § 7, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-952. - List of excavators.
The city and WGL shall prepare and maintain, on a current basis, a list of persons who normally engage in excavation activities in the area in which underground utility facilities are located.
(Code 1985, § 24-127; Ord. No. 87-210, § 8, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-953. - General notification.
The city and WGL shall provide general notification to the public through media advertising, bill inserts, or similar means, and direct notification to the list of excavators identified in section 60-952 of the program's existence and purpose, and how to learn the location of underground utility facilities before the excavation activities begin.
(Code 1985, § 24-128; Ord. No. 87-210, § 9, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-954. - Inspections.
The city and WGL shall conduct regular inspections during and after the activities as frequently as may be necessary to verify the integrity of the utility facility. In the case of blasting, such inspections shall include leakage surveys.
(Code 1985, § 24-129; Ord. No. 87-210, § 10, 12-8-1987)
Sec. 60-955. - Penalties.
The failure to comply with the requirements of section 60-949(2) and (3), or with any of the requirements of sections 60-950 and 60-951, shall be an offense punishable under section 1-17. Each day any such failure or violation continues shall constitute a separate punishable offense.
(Code 1985, § 24-130; Ord. No. 87-210, § 11, 12-8-1987)
