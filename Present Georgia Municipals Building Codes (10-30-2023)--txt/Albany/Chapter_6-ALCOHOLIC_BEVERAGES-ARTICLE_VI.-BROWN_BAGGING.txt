ARTICLE VI. - BROWN BAGGING

Sec. 6-233. - Prohibited; exceptions.
Brown bagging is prohibited within the corporate limits of the city unless the business has met the requirements of subsections (1) and (2) of this section:
(1)
The business must purchase and maintain a license to sell beer and/or wine on the premises; and
(2)
The business must pay an annual "brown bagging permit fee" of $1,000.00 to the business license office of the department of finance at the time the license to sell beer is purchased or renewed.
(Code 1985, § 4-140; Ord. No. 04-111, § 3, 3-23-2004)
Sec. 6-234. - Provisions.
Brown bagging establishments will be subject to all provisions in article IV of this chapter, and including, but not limited to the provisions of section 6-5 (hours of operation), section 6-10 (telephone required), section 6-14 (certain attire and conduct prohibited upon premises licensed to sell alcoholic beverages), section 6-41 (unlawful for any person who has not attained legal age to be on licensed premises), section 6-74 (qualifications for person seeking alcoholic beverage license), and section 6-107 et seq. (procedure for suspension and revocation of license).
(Code 1985, § 4-141; Ord. No. 04-111, § 3, 3-23-2004)
Sec. 6-235. - Violations; penalties.
Every person, whether acting as an individual owner, employee of an owner or operator for an owner or whether acting as an agent or independent contractor for the owner, employee or operator, who participates in or allows, directly or indirectly, brown bagging in violation of this section shall be guilty of an offense hereunder. Upon conviction such person shall be punished pursuant to the provisions of section 1-17.
(Code 1985, § 4-142; Ord. No. 04-111, § 3, 3-23-2004)
