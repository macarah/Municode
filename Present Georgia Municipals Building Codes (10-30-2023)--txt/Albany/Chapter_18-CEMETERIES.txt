ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Interment in city cemeteries.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to inter any dead body in any other place within the city or its police jurisdiction than in a cemetery.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, other than the superintendent of parks and cemeteries, to inter any dead body in any part of the city cemeteries.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to inter other than human remains in any part of the city cemeteries.
(Code 1975, §§ 6-1—6-3; Code 1985, § 9-1)
Sec. 18-2. - Hebrew section.
That portion of either cemetery now or hereafter designated in the plan of the cemeteries in the office of the city engineer as the "Hebrew Section" is hereby set apart for the special use of persons of the Jewish faith in the city for cemetery purposes. They shall have power to enclose the same at their own charge and in any manner assented to by the city manager. The superintendent of parks and cemeteries shall dig all graves and keep a record of all interments made therein, in the manner and on the same terms as in other portions of the city cemetery. All ordinances applicable to the city cemetery shall apply to this portion thus set off.
(Code 1975, § 6-4; Code 1985, § 9-2)
Sec. 18-3. - Public section.
That portion of either cemetery now or hereafter designated in the plan of the cemeteries as the "Public Section" shall be used for the interment of the remains of nonresidents, persons buried at the public expense and persons not owning lots or who are unable to buy the same.
(Code 1975, § 6-5; Code 1985, § 9-3)
Sec. 18-4. - Lot and grave service fees.
The price of cemetery lots and grave services in any city cemetery shall be as determined by the board of city commissioners from time to time and on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1975, § 6-6; Code 1985, § 9-4)
Sec. 18-5. - Sale of lots—Payment of purchase money.
Purchasers of cemetery lots shall, on payment of the purchase money to the city treasurer, receive titles for the same. No deed shall be made or delivered until the purchase price shall have been paid.
(Code 1975, § 6-7; Code 1985, § 9-5)
Sec. 18-6. - Same—Form of deed; conditions.
All deeds shall be made and signed by the mayor and city manager and have affixed thereto the seal of the city. All deeds shall contain a condition that the lot conveyed shall not be used for any other than burial of human remains and cemetery purposes and shall be reconveyed in whole or in part only upon notice to and approval by the public administration and finance committee or committee of the whole of the board of city commissioners and shall also have inserted therein a clause declaring that the board of city commissioners or its proper authority shall always have the right and authority to regulate the manner of interments on the lots.
(Code 1975, § 6-8; Code 1985, § 9-6)
Sec. 18-7. - Recordation of deeds.
It shall be the duty of the city clerk to keep a well-bound book in which the city clerk shall record the city title to the city cemeteries and all titles granted pursuant to this chapter. Such titles shall be recorded within 30 days after they shall have been issued and the book shall have a numerical index as to lot numbers as well as a name index as to purchasers.
(Code 1975, § 6-9; Code 1985, § 9-7)
Sec. 18-8. - Depositing offensive matter prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit on any part of any cemetery or any land adjoining it any garbage, trash, rubbish or any putrid or offensive matter whatsoever.
(Code 1975, § 6-11; Code 1985, § 9-8)
Sec. 18-9. - Hours.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter any cemetery belonging to, owned or controlled by the city or walk upon or across any such cemetery between sunset and sunrise without the written consent of the superintendent of parks and cemeteries.
(b)
All persons shall be allowed access to the grounds of Riverside Cemetery between sunrise and sunset.
(Code 1975, §§ 6-12, 6-33; Code 1985, § 9-9)
Secs. 18-10—18-36. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OAKVIEW CEMETERY

Sec. 18-37. - Old plan adopted.
The plan of the cemetery heretofore known, and hereafter to be known as Oakview Cemetery, in the office of the city clerk is hereby adopted as the true plan thereof, subject to such changes as may be made necessary therein by the sale of lots not heretofore entered thereon, and all the ways, passages, avenues and corners thereon delineated shall forever be kept free and unobstructed for use of those who are now or may at any time hereafter become owners of lots in the cemetery and for the public; subject, however, at all times to such rules and regulations as may be made by the board of city commissioners for the government of the same.
(Code 1975, § 6-21; Code 1985, § 9-16)
Sec. 18-38. - New and revised plan.
The city engineer shall make a revision of the original plan of Oakview Cemetery, conforming as nearly as possible to the original plan, with respect to showing numbers of lots and subdivisions of the same. The city engineer shall also plot and lay off, under the direction of the superintendent of parks and cemeteries, the addition to the cemetery purchased from the Albany Brick Manufacturing company and make a plan or map thereof, with departments or subdivisions corresponding to those named in Oakview and after completion of the revised plan and the new plan and after they have been approved in writing thereon by the board of city commissioners, both shall be filed with the city clerk and by the city clerk safely kept at the city hall and shall be the official cemetery plans and from which all the titles shall be made.
(Code 1975, § 6-22; Code 1985, § 9-17)
Sec. 18-39. - Lots undisposed of.
All those lots in Oakview Cemetery which have been used for burial purposes and which have not been conveyed away by the board of city commissioners shall be considered public lots and used as such unless the friends of such persons as have been interred on any of such lots shall come forward and pay the regular valuation price for the same, after advertising in the city paper for a space of 60 days.
(Code 1975, § 6-23; Code 1985, § 9-18)
Secs. 18-40—18-66. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - RIVERSIDE CEMETERY

Sec. 18-67. - Application.
The provisions of this article shall govern the control and use of the Riverside Cemetery and the Hebrew Section.
(Code 1975, § 6-31; Code 1985, § 9-26)
Sec. 18-68. - Sale, transfer by owner.
Owners of lots shall not be permitted to sell or transfer lots without the written consent and approval of the city, and no interment upon the lot of an owner shall be permitted for a consideration.
(Code 1975, § 6-32; Code 1985, § 9-27)
Sec. 18-69. - Interment permit required.
No interments shall be made in Riverside Cemetery until a permit shall have been secured from the proper authority of the city.
(Code 1975, § 6-34; Code 1985, § 9-28)
Sec. 18-70. - Vaults required.
All interments shall be in approved metal or concrete vaults except those in Babyland or public sections. Upon approval of the superintendent of parks and cemeteries, vaults may be installed at ground level at no expense to the city. When interment is made, the usual grave opening fee shall apply.
(Code 1975, § 6-35; Code 1985, § 9-29; Ord. No. 81-149, § 1, 4-28-1981)
Sec. 18-71. - Disinterments.
All disinterments shall be made by authorities of the city and after all proper and requisite permits have been secured.
(Code 1975, § 6-36; Code 1985, § 9-30)
Sec. 18-72. - Enclosures, marks, etc.
No enclosure of lots shall be permitted, and all corner stones or other marks shall be set flush with the ground.
(Code 1975, § 6-37; Code 1985, § 9-31)
Sec. 18-73. - Erection of stones, monuments, etc.
Owners shall have the right to erect proper stones, monuments or sepulchral structures thereon, except that no slab shall be set other than in a horizontal position and flush with the ground. Headstones shall not exceed two feet in height above the ground and 18 inches in width and shall be set in a solid concrete foundation. Monuments of cut stone or marble shall not exceed ten feet in height and the erection of same must be authorized and supervised by the authorized officers of the city. Aboveground vaults or mausoleums shall be constructed only after due authorization of the city. All lots shall be sodded with grass and all markers, monuments, stones and slabs shall be set so as to interfere as little as possible with the mowing of the grass by the city.
(Code 1975, § 6-38; Code 1985, § 9-32)
Sec. 18-74. - Permits for improvements.
The erection of all monuments and the planting of flowers, shrubs or any other effort to improve or beautify any lot within the cemetery shall be done only after the plans for such work shall have been submitted to the proper city authority, approved thereby and a permit issued therefor.
(Code 1975, § 6-39; Code 1985, § 9-33)
Sec. 18-75. - Monument and masonry work.
All monumental work and masonry shall at all times be of the most permanent character. Defective workmanship will not be allowed.
(Code 1975, § 6-40; Code 1985, § 9-34)
Sec. 18-76. - Workmen.
All workmen engaged in any work being done in the cemetery shall at all times be subject to the direction and control of the authorities of the city in charge of the cemetery. No work shall be done without express written authority from the city. Where a permit has been secured for such work the same must be carried on under the direction and supervision of the authorities of the city in charge of the cemetery.
(Code 1975, § 6-41; Code 1985, § 9-35)
Sec. 18-77. - Park lot.
The plot just east of Jackson Street in the northwest corner of the Riverside Cemetery shall forever be maintained as a park.
(Code 1975, § 6-42; Code 1985, § 9-36)
Sec. 18-78. - Advertisements, signs, etc.
No advertisement, sign, erection of fences, trellises, planting of hedges or trees, or the solicitation of work by gardeners or others within the cemetery shall be permitted.
(Code 1975, § 6-43; Code 1985, § 9-37)
