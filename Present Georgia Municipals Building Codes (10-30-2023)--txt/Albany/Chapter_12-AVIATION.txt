ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Soliciting at airport restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any person, either for himself or as agent or employee of any other person, to solicit the sale, use, hiring or rental of goods, wares, merchandise or services of any kind in the lobby of the airport terminal or at any other place on the property of the Southwest Georgia Regional Airport, except at a location duly assigned and licensed by contract for that purpose, and by a person under contract with the city to sell or rent such goods or to render such services at such location.
(Code 1975, § 3-1; Code 1985, § 7-1; Ord. No. 88-153, § 1, 8-23-1988)
Sec. 12-2. - Vehicles for hire—Use generally.
Except as permitted by contract with the city, it shall be unlawful for any taxicab or other vehicle for rent or hire to park, linger or remain on the property of the Southwest Georgia Regional Airport, except for the purpose of discharging passengers, or of picking up passengers or delivering vehicles for rent or hire in response to an arrangement previously made with the customers and not pursuant to any solicitation of business on the airport premises.
(Code 1975, § 3-2; Code 1985, § 7-2; Ord. No. 88-153, § 1, 8-23-1988)
Sec. 12-3. - Same—Delivery.
All vehicles for hire delivered to or for a customer at the airport by or on behalf of persons not operating inside the airport terminal building under contract with the city shall be delivered to the customer at such point outside the airport terminal building as shall be designated by the city for that purpose. No person delivering such vehicles for hire shall enter the airport terminal building for any purpose whatsoever.
(Code 1975, § 3-3; Code 1985, § 7-3)
Sec. 12-4. - Same—Advertising.
It shall be unlawful for any person to display inside the airport terminal building any advertisement, insignia, badge or other device or article of clothing containing the name, trademark or other mark identifying any business offering vehicles for rent or hire, or other otherwise advertising the same, except as authorized by contract with the city.
(Code 1975, § 3-4; Code 1985, § 7-4)
Sec. 12-5. - Authority of airport director to establish traffic regulations; penalties; conflicts.
(a)
The airport director, or the chief safety officer of the airport when authorized by the airport director, shall determine and designate from time to time those areas of airport properties, including access roads and other roads, which shall be accessible to vehicular traffic and shall determine and establish the conditions and regulations under which such access shall be exercised. Suitable signs shall be posted by the airport director or the chief safety officer or under their direction giving notice of accessible areas and prohibited areas.
(b)
The airport director, or the chief safety officer of the airport when authorized by the airport director, shall determine from time to time the speed limits which shall be in effect upon the accessible airport properties and upon the public roads lying within the boundaries of the airport properties and shall determine what portions of areas and public roads may be used for parking and upon what conditions. Suitable signs shall be posted by the airport director or the chief safety officer giving notice of the speed limits and parking regulations.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any traffic regulations or parking regulations adopted pursuant to the provisions of subsections (a) and (b) of this section or to enter any areas not designated as accessible areas pursuant thereto.
(d)
Except to the extent that they may conflict with anything contained in this section, the rules and regulations of the Southwest Georgia Regional Airport, heretofore approved by the board of city commissioners by Resolution No. 76-R181, adopted August 10, 1976, are hereby reaffirmed, ratified, enacted and ordained, and shall remain in full force and effect.
(Code 1975, § 3-5; Code 1985, § 7-5; Ord. No. 88-153, § 1, 8-23-1988)

ARTICLE II. - AVIATION COMMISSION

Sec. 12-29. - Creation.
There is hereby created and established the Albany-Dougherty County Aviation Commission, hereinafter referred to in this article as the aviation commission.
(Code 1975, § 3-21; Code 1985, § 7-16)
Sec. 12-30. - Composition.
The aviation commission shall be composed of seven members, one of who shall be the mayor of the city, who shall serve for the duration of his/her term as such, or his/her designee who shall serve at the mayor's pleasure, one member of the board of city commissioners who shall be appointed for a term of one year, and five members at large.
(Code 1975, § 3-22; Code 1985, § 7-17; Ord. No. 12-116, § 1, 10-23-2012)
Sec. 12-31. - Qualification of members.
All appointees of the aviation commission shall be citizens of the county who have had at least five years of business or professional experience and have manifested some interest in the advancement of aviation.
(Code 1975, § 3-23; Code 1985, § 7-18)
Sec. 12-32. - Appointment, terms of at-large members.
The members at-large of the aviation commission shall be appointed to the commission by the board of city commissioners for terms of three years. All members of the aviation commission shall serve until their successors are appointed and qualified.
(Code 1975, § 3-24; Code 1985, § 7-19; Ord. No. 90-146, 9-11-1990; Ord. No. 00-140, § 1, 8-22-2000; Ord. No. 01-100, § 1, 1-8-2001)
Sec. 12-33. - Filling vacancies.
When a vacancy occurs on the aviation commission for any cause, it shall be the duty of the board of city commissioners to appoint a member to complete the unexpired term of the member whose absence created the vacancy. The aviation commission will submit nominations of members at large to the board of city commissioners for their consideration.
(Code 1975, § 3-25; Code 1985, § 7-20; Ord. No. 84-166, § 1, 8-14-1984; Ord. No. 00-140, § 1, 8-22-2000; Ord. No. 01-100, § 1, 1-8-2001)
Sec. 12-34. - Compensation.
All members of the aviation commission shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1975, § 3-26; Code 1985, § 7-21)
Sec. 12-35. - Election of chairman.
The chairman of the aviation commission shall be elected annually by the members of the commission.
(Code 1975, § 3-27; Code 1985, § 7-22)
Sec. 12-36. - General management of airport.
(a)
The aviation commission shall have the charge and management of all property incidental to the operation of the Southwest Georgia Regional Airport and the McAfee Air Terminal, and the aviation commission shall take all steps necessary, subject to the approval of the board of city commissioners where otherwise required, toward constructing, improving and maintaining such properties; including the construction, alteration and repair of airport buildings and the removal, lowering, marking and lighting of airport obstructions. The aviation commission shall make recommendations to the board of city commissioners for the acquisition of such lands or property interests therein as the commission shall deem necessary either for the construction or expansion of the airport, or to protect the airport and the approaches thereto, and for this purpose the aviation commission shall make surveys, prepare plans and specifications, and employ adequate personnel therefor.
(b)
The aviation commission shall, subject to approval of the board of city commissioners where required, be authorized and empowered to:
(1)
Enter into contracts, either as lessor or lessee, for the rental of buildings or portions of buildings, lands, office space, equipment and any other property now owned or hereafter acquired by the city located at or upon airport property;
(2)
Contract for concessions and other rights thereon;
(3)
Adopt rules and regulations for the operation of the aviation commission and of the airport;
(4)
Receive all revenues from leases of properties used in connection with the airport, from concessions, rentals, fees, grants, contributions and other sources, and to make payment out of such funds for salaries, improvements, repairs and other expenses;
(5)
In all respects to include hire and discharge all employees necessary to fulfill the duties of the aviation commission, and to fix the salaries and compensations of such employees, provided the airport's director shall be hired by and report to the city manager. Additionally, as with other department heads, the airport's director maybe terminated by the city manager. Further, the airport director shall receive such compensation as may be prescribed by the city manager.
(6)
Have exclusive control, custody and direction of all land, properties, and improvements, and the general direction of the same.
(c)
The aviation commission shall make no sales or conveyances of property, or agreements for the lease, rental or sale of any property, until such transactions have been approved by the board of city commissioners. All agreements or contracts for the expenditure of any sum in excess of $40,000.00 for any single project or purchase or undertaking shall be approved by the board of city commissioners. Expenditures in excess of $5,000.00 but less than $40,000.00 must receive approval of the city manager. Expenditures of $5,000.00 or less may be approved by the aviation commission.
(Code 1975, § 3-28; Code 1985, § 7-23; Ord. No. 88-153, § 1, 8-23-1988; Ord. No. 13-110, § 1, 3-26-2013)
Sec. 12-37. - Financial procedures.
(a)
The aviation commission shall operate on the same fiscal year as the city. The commission shall furnish to the board of city commissioners a detailed statement of its operations for the current fiscal year, and a proposed budget for the next fiscal year showing anticipated revenues and anticipated expenditures, which shall be filed with the city's finance department, and shall return to the city treasurer all funds that have not been expended by the end of the fiscal year and which are not contracted for or set aside to match federal or state funds or reserved for the payment of debts which have been made but not yet matured.
(b)
The board of city commissioners shall have the right of revision of the proposed budget, and upon approval of a budget for the aviation commission, the board of city commissioners shall appropriate for the coming fiscal year a sufficient amount to equal the deficiency between the estimated revenues of the aviation commission and the estimated expenditures commencing July 1 of the fiscal year.
(c)
The aviation commission shall have the right to apply for and to receive and utilize any federal or state funds which may be or become available for the acquisition, improvement, alteration or maintenance of airports or airport or terminal facilities, and to enter into any agreements with the federal government in connection therewith, particularly grant agreements necessary under the provisions of such federal or state laws in order to become the recipient of federal or state aid, under such terms and conditions as might be required by the federal or state government under grant or aid agreements, and also shall have the right to apply for and receive state aid or grants for such purposes under such conditions as the state government might prescribe. The aviation commission shall also have the right to receive contributions from any other source; provided, however, that the aviation commission shall not match or agree to match any funds for any purpose from any agency without the consent of the board of city commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 3-29; Code 1985, § 7-24)
Sec. 12-38. - Salaries, duties and airport personnel.
Employees of the aviation commission shall be eligible to receive the benefits of any present or future pension or retirement plans of the city, and any life insurance, hospitalization or other employee benefits in effect now or in the future by the city, or any successor form of government, except that for airport employees any matching contribution to any such plans or benefits shall be made by the aviation commission.
(Code 1975, § 3-30; Code 1985, § 7-25)
Sec. 12-39. - Rules, reports.
The aviation commission shall make its own rules with reference to meetings, and shall make periodic reports to the board of city commissioners. All minutes and documents of the aviation commission shall be available at all times to the board of city commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 3-31; Code 1985, § 7-26)
Sec. 12-40. - Termination.
If the aviation commission shall become ineffective, or shall function at the displeasure of the city, the commission can be dissolved at any time by a majority vote of the board of city commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 3-32; Code 1985, § 7-27)
Sec. 12-41. - Airport police and firefighting personnel.
(a)
The aviation commission is authorized to employ police and firefighting personnel for the city's airport facilities. Such personnel shall be under the control of the chief security and firefighting officer.
(b)
Persons employed as safety, police, and firefighting personnel are required to meet the qualifications and training requirements set forth in O.C.G.A. §§ 25-4-31 and 35-8-1 to 35-8-18 and be so certified. They shall be required to take the oath required of police officers by Charter section 23, and by section 2-130. The oath shall be administered by the chairman of the aviation commission.
(c)
Police and firefighting personnel employed pursuant to the provisions of this section shall have general powers of arrest for violations of federal or state law or ordinances of the city, committed upon the airport premises. Such officers shall also have the power and authority to issue citations and summons for violations of traffic laws and regulations or other ordinances of the city or the county committed upon the airport premises.
(Code 1975, § 3-33; Code 1985, § 7-28)
