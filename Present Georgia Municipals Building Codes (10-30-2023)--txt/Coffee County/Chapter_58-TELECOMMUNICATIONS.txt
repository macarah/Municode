Chapter 58 - TELECOMMUNICATIONS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 58-1. - E-911 Wireless phone charge subscriber fee.
A wireless enhanced 911 charge shall be imposed upon each wireless telecommunications connection subscribed to by subscribers whose billing address is within the jurisdiction of the county, at the rate of $1.50 per month per wireless connection provided to each telephone subscriber.
(Res. No. 02-08, 4-1-2002; Res. of 8-1-2003; Res. No. 2007-02, 3-19-2007)
Sec. 58-2. - Prepaid wireless service 9-1-1 charges.
(a)
9-1-1 Charge on prepaid wireless transactions. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charge as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4) upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public service answering point in the amount of $0.75.
(b)
Collection of 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions. Prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charges collected by sellers shall be remitted to the Commissioner of the Department of Revenue at the times and in the manner provided by Chapter 8 of Title 48 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated with respect to the sales and use tax imposed on prepaid wireless calling service.
(c)
Administrative provisions. The clerk of the county is hereby directed to file with the State Revenue Commissioner a certified copy of this ordinance and amendments thereto, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), within ten days of enactment of this ordinance.
(d)
Depositing of funds; use of funds. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(5), funds received by this county from charges imposed by this ordinance shall be deposited in the Emergency Telephone System Fund maintained by this county pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134 and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction; all such funds shall be used exclusively for the purpose authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(e).
(Ord. No. 11-3, §§ 1—4, 6-20-2011, eff. 1-1-2012)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 11-3, §§ 1—4, adopted June 20, 2011, effective Jan. 1, 2012, added provisions that were not specifically amendatory. At the editor's discretion, said provisions were included as § 58-2.
Secs. 58-3—58-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CABLE TELEVISION FRANCHISE[1]

State Law reference— Regulation of cable television systems by counties, O.C.G.A. § 36-18-1 et seq.; municipalities prohibited from granting franchise for cable television within unincorporated area of county, O.C.G.A. § 36-18-3; avoiding or attempting to avoid charges for use of cable television service, O.C.G.A. § 46-5-2.

Sec. 58-21. - Short title.
This legislation shall be known as the "Coffee County Cable Television Franchise Ordinance."
(Ord. of 12-18-1989, art. I)
Sec. 58-22. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board means the board of commissioners.
Cable television system means a nonbroadcast facility consisting of a set of transmission paths and associated generation, reception, transmission and control equipment, under common ownership and control, that distributes or is designed to distribute to subscribers the signals of one or more television broadcast stations and programs received in satellite, microwave, videotape or video disks, or produced by a franchised cable system.
Franchise means an agreement between a person desiring to operate a cable television system within the unincorporated areas of the county and the board which provides for the respective rights and obligations of the franchisee and the board with respect to the operation of such cable television system and the franchise fee to be paid to the county as compensation for the privilege of operating such cable television system.
Franchise fee means a fee, to be naturally agreed upon by the board and the franchisee, which the franchisee shall pay into the general revenues of the county, at least monthly, as compensation for the privilege of operating a cable television system within the unincorporated areas of the county.
Franchisee means a person who has been granted a franchise to operate a cable television system within the unincorporated areas of the county by the board.
(Code 2002, § 62-31; Ord. of 12-18-1989, § 302)
Sec. 58-23. - Operation unlawful without franchise.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a cable television system within the unincorporated areas of the county, without first having obtained a franchise for such cable television system as provided in this article.
(Code 2002, § 62-32; Ord. of 12-18-1989, art. IV)
Sec. 58-24. - Application for franchise.
Any person desiring to operate any cable television system within the unincorporated areas of the county shall first make application for a franchise to operate such cable television system on a form to be provided by the board, and shall furnish such information as may be required by the board.
(Code 2002, § 62-33; Ord. of 12-18-1989, § 501)
Sec. 58-25. - Appearance before board.
Each applicant for a cable television franchise shall cause a representative to appear before the board at a regularly scheduled meeting to be designated by the board and shall furnish to the board such additional information it may request. Following such meeting the board shall either grant or deny the franchise.
(Code 2002, § 62-34; Ord. of 12-18-1989, § 502)
Sec. 58-26. - Franchise fees.
Any person who is granted a franchise to operate a cable television system as provided in this article shall pay to the general treasury of the county, at least monthly, a fee which shall be at a rate to be mutually agreed upon by the board and the franchisee.
(Code 2002, § 62-35; Ord. of 12-18-1989, § 503)
Sec. 58-27. - Duration of franchise.
Each franchise granted pursuant to this article shall continue in force and effect until it is either abandoned by the franchisee or canceled by the board pursuant to terms and conditions specified in the franchise.
(Code 2002, § 62-36; Ord. of 12-18-1989, § 601)
Sec. 58-28. - Easements.
Each franchisee shall have the sole responsibility for obtaining from electrical utility companies and/or telephone companies operating within the unincorporated areas of the county such easements for the installation and maintenance of its cables and lines as it may deem appropriate. Each franchisee shall agree to indemnify and hold harmless the county from any and all claims which may be asserted against it by any entity as a result of the use of or damage to any property of such entity by the franchisee.
(Code 2002, § 62-37; Ord. of 12-18-1989, § 602)
Sec. 58-29. - Transferability.
Each franchise granted pursuant to this article shall be deemed and considered to be a privilege granted exclusively to the original franchisee. No such franchise shall be transferable by such franchisee to any other person without the prior written approval of the board.
(Code 2002, § 62-38; Ord. of 12-18-1989, § 603)
