ARTICLE III. - BUILDING NUMBERING

Sec. 18-71. - Display required.
It shall be the duty of all owners, lessees and/or occupants of buildings, including businesses and/or homes within the unincorporated areas of the county, to display the address number of the location on the structure.
(Code 2002, § 18-71; Ord. of 7-7-1997, § 1)
Sec. 18-72. - Application; assigning.
It shall be the duty of all owners, lessees and/or occupants of buildings in the county to make application to the director of E 911 for the county for building numbers, including all houses and/or all other structures, for all structures not having a current designated number, giving the director the name of the street, road and location of such structure; and it shall be the duty of the director to assign correct numbers to the applicant for such premises.
(Code 2002, § 18-72; Ord. of 7-7-1997, § 2)
Sec. 18-73. - Display.
It shall be the duty of all owners, lessees and/or occupants of buildings in the county to display the numbers, which shall be at least four inches high and at least one-half inch in width. The numbers shall be placed on the front of such dwelling or business structure by the owners, lessees and/or occupants at the expense of the owners, lessees and/or occupants.
(Code 2002, § 18-73; Ord. of 7-7-1997, § 3)
Sec. 18-74. - Unlawful actions.
It shall be unlawful for any property owner, lessee and/or occupant of structures located within the unincorporated areas of the county to fail or refuse to make application, or fail or refuse to place the numbers designated for such structures, as provided in this article, on such structures.
(Code 2002, § 18-74; Ord. of 7-7-1997, § 4)
Sec. 18-75. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any violation of this article shall be deemed a misdemeanor and shall be prosecuted in the magistrate court of the county, with punishment as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 2002, § 18-75; Ord. of 7-7-1997, § 5)

ARTICLE IV. - FLOW-RATE RESTRICTION ON PLUMBING FIXTURES

Sec. 18-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building, and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling, but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(Code 2002, § 18-111; Ord. of 9-16-1991, § 1)
Sec. 18-112. - Restricted residential construction.
No construction may be initiated within the unincorporated areas of the county for any residential building of any type which employs a:
(1)
Gravity tank-type, flushometer-valve, or flushometer-tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.6 gallons of water per flush;
(2)
Showerhead that allows a flow of more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
Urinal that uses more than an average of 1.0 gallon of water per flush;
(4)
Lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute.
(Code 2002, § 18-112; Ord. of 9-16-1991, § 2)
Sec. 18-113. - Restricted commercial construction.
There shall be no construction of any commercial building initiated within the unincorporated areas of the county for any commercial building of any type which does not meet the requirements of sections 18-112.
(Code 2002, § 18-113; Ord. of 9-16-1991, § 3)
Sec. 18-114. - Application of restrictions.
The requirements of section 18-112 shall apply to any residential construction and to any commercial construction which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement of toilets or showers or both.
(Code 2002, § 18-114; Ord. of 9-16-1991, § 4)
Sec. 18-115. - Exemptions.
(a)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of sections 18-112, 18-113 and 18-114 when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing buildings;
(2)
When such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design or installation, would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this article were installed;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped.
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution.
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(b)
The owner, or his agent, of a building undergoing new construction or repair or renovation who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (a)(2), (3) or (4) of this section shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the county building official. A fee, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and maintained in the office of the county clerk, shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
(Code 2002, § 18-115; Ord. of 9-16-1991, § 5)
Sec. 18-116. - Enforcement; penalty.
(a)
This article shall be enforced by the office of the building official of the county. Citations for violations may be issued by the chief building official of the county.
(b)
Any person violating this article shall be tried before the chief magistrate of the county. Upon conviction, a violation of this article may be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 2002, § 18-116; Ord. of 9-16-1991, § 6)

DIVISION 2. - SMOKING PROHIBITED

Sec. 18-149. - Smoking of tobacco prohibited.
Pursuant to provisions in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-2, the smoking of tobacco products shall be prohibited in all buildings and structures owned or operated, in whole or in part, by the county board of commissioners, including offices, elevators, garages, storages areas, restrooms, corridors, break rooms and stairwells, without exception.
(Ord. of 1-21-1992, § 1; Ord. No. 02-02, § 131, 3-4-2002)
Sec. 18-150. - Designated smoking areas; littering prohibited.
Outdoor smoking areas shall be designated for each aforementioned building and structure. All persons shall use available ashtrays and other conduits for disposal of collection waste, and also observe anti-litter laws of the city and county.
(Ord. No. 02-02, § 132, 3-4-2002)
Sec. 18-151. - Enforcement; penalty.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of the division, or any ordinance, rule or regulation adopted or issued in pursuance thereof shall be punished by a fine of not less than $10.00 nor more than $1,000.00 or imprisonment for not more than 60 days or both. Each act of violation and each day upon which any such violation shall occur shall constitute a separate offense. This article may be enforced either by law enforcement officers and code enforcement officers of the county and the prevailing jurisdiction.
(Ord. of 1-21-1992, § 2; Ord. No. 02-02, § 133, 3-4-2002)
