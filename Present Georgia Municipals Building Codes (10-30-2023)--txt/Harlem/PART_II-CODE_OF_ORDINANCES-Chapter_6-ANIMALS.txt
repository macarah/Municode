ARTICLE I. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply in the interpretation and enforcement of this chapter:
Animal control shall mean an individual that works for the Columbia County Animal Control Division.
Domestic animal means any dog, cat, or other legally owned animal not defined as wildlife, livestock, or nontraditional livestock.
Ear tipping means straight-line cutting of the tip of the left or right ear of a cat while the cat is anesthetized by a licensed veterinarian during sterilization and vaccination procedures. A feral cat that has been ear tipped is presumed to be sterile and to have been vaccinated at least once against rabies.
Electronic confinement system is a system designed to confine a domestic animal in a designated area within the property limits of the animal's owner or keeper using an electronic signal from a transmitter to a receiver worn by the animal that provides a shock, or other behavior correction method, to the confined animal when the animal approaches the perimeter of the confinement area. Such behavior correction method shall be of sufficient effect to prevent the animal from leaving the confinement area.
Feral cat means a cat, regardless of sex or age, which is unowned, free-roaming, and not interactive with humans.
Feral cat colony means an identified group of feral cats capable of being trapped, neutered or spayed, vaccinated, and released back into the area from which they were taken.
Feral cat colony caretaker means a person who is associated with an organization that meets the qualifications for an animal rescue group under standards set by animal control policies and procedures, who has registered the feral cat colony with animal control and who provides sustenance, water and care for a feral cat colony.
Guard dog means any dog which has been trained to attack persons or other animals independently or upon verbal command and any dog which, while not so trained, is reasonably expected to perform as a guardian of the property upon and within which he is located.
Livestock means cattle, swine, equines, poultry, sheep, goats, ratites, nontraditional livestock and ruminants.
Nontraditional livestock means the species of Artiodactyla (even-toed ungulates) listed as bison, water buffalo, farmed deer, llamas and alpacas that are held and possessed legally and in a manner, which is not in conflict with the provisions of O.C.G.A. ch. 5, tit. 27 dealing with wild animals.
Officer means an individual who is either a City of Harlem Police Officer, code enforcement officer or designated by the city manager to enforce this chapter.
Owner means any person who owns, keeps, harbors, possesses or otherwise keeps any domestic, nontraditional livestock or livestock animal for a period of at least 16 days, or any person who knowingly permits any domestic, nontraditional livestock or livestock animal to be kept, harbored, possessed, or otherwise kept upon his premises for a period of 16 days, or who has any animal under his or her care or responsibility, either temporarily or permanently, at any time, within City of Harlem. "Owner" does not include a feral cat colony caretaker or the owner of the premises where a registered feral cat colony is located.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, corporation, joint venture, association, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, syndicate or other group or combination acting as a unit.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that without provocation causes severe injury to another dog or cat, except that a dog that inflicts an injury upon another dog or cat when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties shall not be a potentially dangerous dog within the meaning of this definition. In addition, a dog shall not be a potentially dangerous dog within the meaning of this definition if the injury inflicted by the dog was sustained by another dog or cat that, at the time, was not under control as defined in this section.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, or, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Such enclosure must be approved by the animal control superintendent.
Severe injury to another dog or cat means any unprovoked physical injury that results in broken bones or severe lacerations requiring multiple sutures or surgery.
Wildlife means any vertebrate or invertebrate animal life indigenous to this state or any species introduced or specified by the Department of Natural Resources and includes fish, except domestic fish produced by an aquaculturist registered under O.C.G.A. § 27-6-255, mammals, birds, amphibians, reptiles, crustaceans and mollusks or any part thereof.
Zoonotic disease means those diseases transmittable to humans from animals, including parasitic, bacterial, fungal and viral diseases.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Secs. 6-2—6-10. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DUTIES AND PROHIBITIONS RELATING TO CONTROL OF ANIMALS

Sec. 6-11. - Keeping domestic animals under control.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any owner of a domestic animal not to control the animal at all times through one of the following means:
(1)
On a leash when off the property of the owner;
(2)
Within the passenger area of a vehicle driven or parked on the streets;
(3)
Within the property limits of its owner and attended by its owner; or
(4)
Confined within the property limits of its owner or of another with the permission of the person in control of the property.
Confined means kept within an electronic confinement system, building, pen or fenced area or other structure built to prevent escape by a domestic animal.
Electronic confinement systems shall not be considered an adequate means of confinement if the system is improperly installed, malfunctioning, in disrepair, damaged, switched off, or otherwise deactivated or nonoperational. Any domestic animal confined by means of electronic confinement system must wear at all times an appropriate and properly functioning receiving device while within the confines of the system. Signage indicating the presence of an electronic confinement system must be placed around the perimeter of the system at regular intervals in sufficient quantity and of sufficient size so as to be clearly visible to anyone approaching the property. No animal which has been classified or is currently being reviewed for classification as a dangerous, potentially dangerous, or vicious dog pursuant to state or local law may be confined by means of an electronic confinement system.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any owner of a domestic animal to chain, tie, fasten or otherwise tether the animal to dog houses, trees, fences, vehicles or other stationary objects as a means of confinement except that the animal may be temporarily confined by a tether while attended by its owner.
(c)
Exemptions. Feral cat colony caretakers are exempt from the requirements of this section.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-12. - Specific requirements for confinement
It shall be an offense for any owner of a domestic animal not to satisfy the following requirements:
(1)
Any tether used to temporarily confine an animal while attended by its owner must be attached to a collar or harness and shall not be wrapped directly around the animal's neck. Such tethers shall not be excessively heavy or weighted so as to inhibit the animal's movement.
(2)
All domestic animals shall be provided with sanitary shelter from the elements. Said shelter shall be designed, constructed and maintained to protect the animal from rain, snow, ice, excessive cold, excessive heat and excessive direct sunlight, and of a size to allow the animal sufficient space to stand, turn around, and lie down, and make all other normal body movements in a normal and comfortable position appropriate to the age, size, and health of the animal. Examples of inadequate shelter include, but are not limited to, lean-tos, metal or plastic drums, boxes, vehicles, or the area on or beneath exposed porches or decks. Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to prevent the transportation and temporary confinement of a domestic animal within the passenger area of a vehicle provided that the animal is provided adequate ventilation and temperature control in order to protect the animal from excessive heat or cold.
(3)
All domestic animals shall be provided with clean, potable water at all times.
(4)
An owner must promptly remove and dispose of animal excrement and food wastes and debris so as to minimize vermin infestation, contamination, odors, and disease hazards.
(5)
All domestic animals shall be provided with a safe and sanitary confinement area constructed to confine the animal, to protect the animal from injury, and of a sufficient space to allow for adequate exercise suitable to the age, size, species, and breed of animal. The area shall have a means to rapidly eliminate excess water and minimize mud.
(6)
Exemptions. Feral cat colony caretakers are exempt from the requirements of this section.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-13. - Prohibitions for domestic animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit a domestic animal under his or her ownership or possession to do the following:
(1)
Go upon the property of any person other than its owner or keeper without the express consent of such other owner or occupant;
(2)
Attack or act so as to menace pedestrians or other persons using public ways or on the property of another. Any person convicted of violating this subsection, in addition to penalties provided for in section 6-42, shall be required to maintain the dog in a proper enclosure, as defined in this chapter.
(3)
Disturb the peace of any other person by loud, persistent, habitual barking, howling, growling, yelping or whining.
No person shall be charged with violating this subsection unless written warning has previously been issued within 90 days of the charged violation.
No citation for violation of this subsection shall be issued unless there are at least two complaining witnesses who shall have signed a written statement stating the address of the dog owner, a description of the dog and of the offense and the date, time and location of the offense;
(4)
Reserved.
(5)
Be in heat and not be confined in an enclosure to the property of its owner or keeper.
(6)
Exemptions. Feral cat colony caretakers are exempt from the requirements of this section.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-14. - Livestock running at large.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or keeper of any hog, cattle, mule, sheep, goat, fowl or any other livestock or nontraditional livestock animal to permit it to run at large in the city limits of Harlem or to stray from the property of the owner or keeper or to go upon the premises of any other person.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-15. - Control and removal of animal wastes.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any animal to refuse or fail to immediately remove any feces deposited by such animal upon public sidewalks, public streets, public parks or other public property in the City of Harlem, or upon any private property within the City of Harlem not under the exclusive use and control of said owner or keeper.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any animal to permit such animal to defecate or urinate upon any private property except that upon which he or she resides or owns unless he or she has the permission of the owner of such other private property.
(c)
Exemptions. Feral cat colony caretakers are exempt from the requirements of this section.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-16. - Guard dogs.
It shall be the duty of the owner, tenant or custodian of any residential or commercial property on which a guard dog is kept for security purposes to post a notice in a prominent and conspicuous location on such property. Such notice shall comprise the words "warning guard dog" and shall be in clearly contrasting color. Such letters shall be not less than one inch in width and not less than five inches in height. If a guard dog is confined within a fenced area, such notice shall be conspicuously posted upon such fence at every entrance and exit of the fence.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-17. - Identification and inoculation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to own, possess or maintain any dog or cat that does not have a current rabies inoculation, and any such dog or cat shall wear at all times a securely attached collar about its neck displaying such current rabies tag. A feral cat that has been ear tipped is exempt from displaying collar and rabies tag.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-18. - Animal giveaway.
No person in the City of Harlem shall give away any live animal, fish, reptile or bird as a prize for, or as an inducement to enter, any contest, game, or other competition, or as an inducement to enter a place of amusement, or offer such animal as an incentive to enter into any business agreement whereby the offer was for the purpose of attracting trade unless part of a special event approved by the city.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-19. - Obstruction of officer.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to hinder, interfere, harass or otherwise obstruct the performance of any officer with the City of Harlem or the Columbia County Animal Control Division in the official performance of his or her duties as provided for in this chapter.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to relocate, interfere with, or alter the operation of any device, equipment, or item of the City of Harlem or Columbia County Animal Control Division.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place their own animal into or remove any animal confined within a trap or cage operated by the City of Harlem or Columbia County Animal Control Division.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to give a false name, address or date of birth to any officer, employee or agent of the animal control division in the lawful discharge of his or her official duties with the intent to mislead such officer, employee or agent in any way.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Secs. 6-20—6-30. - Reserved.

Sec. 6-31. - Abuse of animals.
The officers of the City of Harlem and Columbia County Animal Control Division shall be authorized to enforce the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-32. - Bird sanctuary.
The entire area embraced within the City of Harlem is hereby designated as a bird sanctuary. It shall be unlawful for any person to trap, hunt, shoot or attempt to shoot, or molest in any manner any bird or fowl or to rob a bird's nest or wild fowl's nest; provided, however, if starlings or similar birds are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality so as to constitute a nuisance or a menace to health or property in the opinion of the proper health authorities in the City of Harlem, such birds may be destroyed as humanely as possible in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by the health authorities under the supervision of the police department. However, any areas permitted as a hunting club are exempted from this section.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)

ARTICLE IV. - FEES AND PENALTIES

Sec. 6-41. - Fees.
(a)
Any person reclaiming a neutered male dog or cat, spayed female dog or cat, or any other animal shall pay an established fee to the Columbia County Animal Control Division.
(b)
Any person reclaiming an unneutered male or unspayed female dog or cat shall pay an established fee to the Columbia County Animal Control Division.
(c)
The fee for adoptions shall be established by the Columbia County Animal Control Division.
(d)
There shall be a registration fee of $100.00 for all dangerous and potentially dangerous dogs in the City of Harlem. Such registration is to be renewed on an annual basis.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-42. - Penalty.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in paragraph (b) below, any person violating this chapter shall be punished pursuant to the provisions of section 1-13 of the Code of the City of Harlem.
(b)
Anyone convicted of a violation of section 6-13(b) shall be prohibited from confining any domestic animal by means of an electronic confinement system for a period of six months after the date of conviction.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)

ARTICLE V. - OFFICIAL POWERS AND AUTHORITY OF ANIMAL CONTROL

Sec. 6-51. - Power to issue citation.
Officers of the City of Harlem, the Columbia County Animal Control Division, the city code enforcement division, the city police department, the sheriff's department or the marshals are hereby authorized to issue a summons or citation relative to the enforcement of this chapter. A citation or summons which has been prepared in accordance with this chapter and signed by an officer of the City of Harlem or Columbia County Animal Control Division may be personally served by anyone with either agency provided that they are not less than 18 years of age.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-52. - Injured animals.
The director of the Columbia County Animal Control Division shall have the authority humanely to dispose of impounded animals which are diseased or injured whenever it shall appear that any such animal is suffering great pain.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-53. - Impoundment of animals.
Except as otherwise provided for by state law, any domestic, nontraditional livestock or livestock animal that is in violation of this chapter, whether or not the animal is on public or private property, may be, but is not required to be, impounded by the Columbia County Animal Control Division in accordance with rules and regulations adopted pursuant to this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-54. - Disposition of impounded animals.
(a)
If an animal remains unclaimed within the prescribed amount of time, then the superintendent of the animal control division, or his designees, shall be authorized to dispose of such animal in as humane and painless a manner as possible, to offer the animal for adoption in accordance with section 6-58, or to donate such animal to a non-profit institution or agency for the purpose of humane placement or rescue.
(b)
No live animal in the possession of the City of Harlem or Columbia County Animal Control Division shall be released, sold or given to any individual, institution, or private firm for the purposes of medical and scientific research.
(c)
When calculating the prescribed amount of time for the execution of rights or responsibilities in this chapter, each day that the animal control facility is open shall be counted, except for the first day that an animal is impounded shall not be counted.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-55. - Enforcement.
This chapter shall be enforced by the Columbia County Animal Control Division and officers from the City of Harlem. Upon information known to, or a signed written statement lodged with, the City of Harlem or the Columbia County Animal Control Division that any owner of an animal is in violation of this chapter, a summons or citation may be served upon authority of the authorized officer requiring the owner of the animal to appear before the appropriate court on a day and time certain to stand trial for violation of this chapter. If such violation has not been witnessed by an officer, a subpoena may be issued to the person supplying the information on which a formal charge may be made to appear at said time and date to testify. The authorized officers are authorized to use necessary means to obtain warrants or subpoenas from municipal court or magistrate court for the purpose of enforcing this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-56. - Confinement of biting animals.
(a)
Any animal that is known to have bitten or scratched a person shall be confined for the purposes of rabies observation to the animal control facility, to a veterinary facility or to such other facility as may be directed by the county health department for a period of ten days after the date of the bite or scratch. All expenses incurred for such confinement and observation shall be paid by the owner of such animal.
(b)
The Columbia County Animal Control Division shall be authorized to order the owner of any dog that is believed to be subject to classification as potentially dangerous under this chapter or vicious to submit the dog to the animal control facility. All expenses incurred for such confinement shall be paid by the owner of such animal.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-57. - Redemption of impounded animals.
It shall be the responsibility of the Columbia County Animal Control Division to attempt to notify the owner or feral cat colony caretaker, if known, or can be reasonably ascertained, of every animal impounded, by telephone if possible, or by mail or by written notice at the residence of the owner.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-58. - Adoption of animals.
(a)
The Columbia County Animal Control Division may offer for adoption any animal deemed abandoned or any animal that has been donated to the division by the owner subject to state law.
(b)
Potential persons who want to adopt an animal must make application for adoption with the Columbia County Animal Control Division and meet requirements associated with the humane housing and care of the animal as determined by Columbia County Animal Control Division and by the payment of the required fees.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-59. - Escape, injury or theft.
Employees of the City of Harlem and the Columbia County Animal Control Division shall exercise reasonable care to prevent the injury, escape or theft of any animal which is in their custody or which they are attempting to bring into their custody under the provisions of this chapter, but they shall not be responsible for any such occurrence.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-60. - Records.
It shall be the duty of the Columbia County Animal Control Division to keep accurate written records showing the final disposition of all animals impounded thereby, the date of impoundment, the description and other identifying data of the animal, the fees collected, the disposition of the animal and the date thereof, which record shall be open and available for public inspection at a reasonable place during the regular business hours.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-61. - Regulation of dangerous, vicious and potentially dangerous dogs.
(a)
The Columbia County Animal Control Division shall be the designated authority to carry out the duties of dog control officer as provided for in this chapter.
(b)
It shall be the duty of a judge of the appropriate court to conduct hearings and make determinations concerning the regulation and disposition of potentially dangerous dogs as required in this chapter and dangerous and vicious dogs.
(c)
During the period while classification is pending and during the amount of time that such classification is eligible for or under appeal by the owner, the dog will be impounded at the Columbia Animal Shelter.
(d)
Whenever a dog that has been classified as dangerous or vicious or potentially dangerous pursuant to the provisions of this chapter (together, "classified dogs" and each a "classified dog") is present in the City of Harlem, the following apply:
(1)
Before the dog may be housed anywhere within the City of Harlem other than at the Columbia County Animal Control or at a veterinarian facility for the purpose of satisfying the procedures required in this section, the owner must meet all of the requirements of this section and be issued a certificate of registration.
(2)
All classified dogs must be spayed or neutered and microchipped. The owner must provide written proof of the spay or neuter and the identification number from the dog's microchip to the animal control superintendent before a certificate of registration may be issued or updated. The animal control superintendent shall be authorized to administer the microchip and, in any event, shall confirm the microchip placement and number by scanning the dog to the extent that such administration or confirmation can be accomplished without risk of physical harm to animal control personnel. The microchip number shall be included on the certificate of registration, and it shall be unlawful for any person to tamper with or remove such identification.
(3)
The owner of a classified dog shall confine the dog only in a proper enclosure, as set forth in this section, which has been inspected and approved by the animal control superintendent or officer of Harlem. Such inspection shall occur prior to issuance or renewal of a certificate of registration and must be updated if the location at which the animal is being kept is changed at any time, including if a previously classified dog is brought into the City of Harlem. The animal control superintendent or his designee is authorized to update it at other times in his discretion.
(4)
If the owner has not already met the requirements for issuance of a certificate of registration at the time the dog is classified or at the time a previously classified dog is brought into the City of Harlem, the dog shall be impounded. The owner must meet the requirements for keeping a potentially dangerous, dangerous or vicious dog, as applicable, no later than five days after the date the classification becomes effective or after a previously classified dog is brought into City of Harlem. If the requirements are not met or the dog is not retrieved from animal control within such time, then the dog shall be deemed unclaimed and subject to the provisions of section 6-54.
(5)
The owner of a classified dog must renew the certificate of registration annually. The animal control superintendent or his designee shall inspect and approve the proper enclosure for the dog prior to renewing a certificate of registration, re-scan and verify the dog's microchip identification number, and confirm that the dog has been spayed or neutered to the extent that such confirmation can be accomplished without risk of physical harm to animal control personnel.
(6)
The owner of a classified dog shall immediately orally notify the animal control division if the dog is not confined in the approved location or by the approved method, if the dog is stolen or missing, or if the dog is otherwise loose, if the dog is transferred, or if the dog is deceased. The owner thereafter must submit, within two business days, a notarized, sworn statement describing the circumstances of the dog's death or disappearance, or the name, address and telephone number of the person to whom the dog was transferred.
(7)
Whenever a classified dog is subject to confiscation, the owner of the dog must submit the dog to animal control without delay. A classified dog is subject to confiscation under this chapter if it is not validly registered, not maintained in a proper enclosure, or is outside the proper enclosure in violation of this chapter.
(8)
No classified dog in the custody of animal control may be offered for the purpose of adoption; provided, however, that dogs classified as potentially dangerous may be transferred to an animal shelter licensed by the Georgia Department of Agriculture if authorized by the animal control superintendent.
(e)
In addition to the requirements set forth above, whenever a dog that has been classified as potentially dangerous pursuant to the provisions of this chapter is present in the City of Harlem, the following apply:
(1)
A "proper enclosure" shall mean an enclosure designed to securely confine the potentially dangerous dog on the owner's property, indoors, or in a securely locked and enclosed pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the potentially dangerous dog from leaving such property.
(2)
The owner of the dog shall post on the premises where the dog is kept a clearly visible sign warning that there is a potentially dangerous dog on the property.
(3)
Whenever outside the proper enclosure, the potentially dangerous dog must be restrained by a leash not to exceed six feet in length and under the immediate physical control of a person capable of preventing the dog from engaging any other human or animal when necessary.
(f)
Investigations by animal control superintendent or an officer of Harlem; notice to owner; hearings; determinations by hearing authority.
(1)
Upon receiving a report of a dog believed to be subject to classification as a potentially dangerous dog as defined in this chapter, the animal control superintendent or Harlem Officer shall make such investigations as necessary to determine whether such dog is subject to classification as a potentially dangerous dog.
(2)
When the animal control superintendent determines that a dog is subject to classification as a potentially dangerous dog, the animal control superintendent shall send, by certified mail to the owner's last known address, a dated notice to the dog's owner. Such notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's determination and shall state that the owner has a right to request a hearing from the judge of the appropriate court on the animal determination within seven days after the date shown on the notice. The notice shall provide a form for requesting the hearing and shall state that if a hearing is not requested within the allotted time, the animal control superintendent's determination shall become effective for all purposes under this chapter. If an owner cannot be located within ten days of a dog control officer's determination that a dog is subject to classification as a potentially dangerous dog, the dog shall be deemed unclaimed and subject to the provisions of section 6-54.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Sec. 6-62. - Disposition of feral cats—Generally.
(a)
Animal control may impound feral cats in violation of this chapter and dispose of the cats by adoption or other disposition in accordance with this chapter, or release the cat to a feral cat colony caretaker who will maintain the cat as part of a registered feral cat colony.
(b)
The superintendent of the animal control division, or his designee, shall have the authority humanely to dispose of any cat believed to be feral whenever all of the following apply:
(1)
No tag, microchip, or other evidence (including without limitation the proximity of an ear-tipped cat at the time of recovery to a registered feral cat colony) which identifies or which could reasonably lead to the identity of an owner or feral colony caretaker is present;
(2)
A determination has been made that the cat is feral after an overnight observation period;
(3)
No owner or feral cat colony caretaker has been identified; and
(4)
The cat appears to be more than 16 weeks of age.
(Ord. No. 1909, 7-22-2019)
Secs. 6-63—6-80. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - KEEPING OF ANIMALS IN RESIDENTIAL ZONES

Sec. 6-81. - Definitions.
Chickens mean a member of that group of animals having the scientific name Gallus domesticus.
Ducks mean a member of that group of animals having the scientific name Ana Platyrhynchos.
Goats, dairy means a member of that group of animals having the scientific name Capra Aegagrus Hircus that produces milk for human consumption.
Goats, wool means a member of that group of animals having the scientific name Capra Aegagrus Hircus that produces wool for human use.
Horses mean a member of the Equus caballus species.
Sheep means a member of that group of animals having the scientific name Ovis Aries.
Turkeys mean a member of that group of animals having the scientific name Meleagris gallopavo.
(Ord. No. 2006, 10-26-2020)

The keeping of residential animals is restricted to parcels with a residential zoning and that parcels with an agricultural zoning are reserved for farming production.

Roosters are not permitted to be kept in residentially zoned parcels

All animals shall be kept in a clean manner with animal feed kept in a covered storage unit and the housing and exercise yard kept free of vermin.
(d)
Chickens and ducks can be kept in the same facilities.

The keeping of backyard animals is exclusionary in that the criteria for keeping backyard goats cannot be used for the keeping of sheep. A single fence meeting the requirements of all kept animals may be utilized to enclose the total area and requirements for each kept type of animal; and

Sec. 6-83. - Procedures.
(a)
Before animals may be placed upon residentially zoned property, an initial inspection of the animal keeping location and structures shall be scheduled by the community development director. Should the proposed animal keeping location and structures fully comply with the conditions of the chapter, an animal keeping permit may be submitted; and
(b)
An annual inspection of the animal keeping location and structures shall be a condition for the maintenance of the animal keeping permit. The community development director shall schedule and assure such annual inspection occurs; and
(c)
Failing to maintain the animal keeping location and structures in full compliance with the conditions of this chapter shall be grounds for the revocation of the animal keeping permit. Should an animal keeping location or animal keeping structures be found not in compliance with the conditions of this chapter, the property owner shall be permitted 30 days to become fully compliant. The Columbia County Animal Control Office shall be contacted at the earliest time possible as to the possible revocation of the animal keeping permit.
(d)
If any and all deficiencies of the animal keeping location and structures are not eliminated after 30 days of notice of such deficiencies, the community development director shall deliver to the holder of the animal keeping permit notice that the permit has been revoked and that all animals shall be removed immediately.
(e)
Appeals of any decision of the community development director shall follow that process as outlined in section 108-378, Appeals of administrative decisions.
