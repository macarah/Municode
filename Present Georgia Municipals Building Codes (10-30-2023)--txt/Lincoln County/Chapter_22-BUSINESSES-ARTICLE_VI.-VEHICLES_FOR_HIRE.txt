ARTICLE VI. - VEHICLES FOR HIRE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-181. - Declaration of policy.
The Board of Commissioners of Lincoln County, Georgia, hereby declares its concern with the public welfare and safety with regard to the usage and operation of taxicabs within the unincorporated area of the county and hereby declares its policy to be to allow the use of taxicabs on the public streets, and adopts this chapter with concern for the quality of service to be offered to the general public by taxicab company owners and drivers.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-182. - Occupation tax required.
No person shall operate or permit a taxicab or any other automobile owned or controlled by him to be operated as a vehicle for hire in Lincoln County without having first obtained a business license authorizing such operation.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-183. - Place of business required.
(a)
Where the business is located in the county, no taxicab or any other vehicle for hire shall operate in the county unless the owner thereof shall maintain a place of business from a building located on property within a commercially zoned district in which vehicles for hire is a permitted use in accordance with the most recently adopted zoning map and have first obtained certification of compliance from the department of planning and zoning.
(b)
Home occupation exception: A taxicab business may operate as a home occupation as provided for in section 34-623 of this Code. Such business shall fully comply with the all provisions of section 34-623 of this Code and shall be further restricted to the operation of not more than two taxicabs from any property located in a residential district.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-184. - Operation of vehicles for hire.
No person shall operate a taxicab or any other vehicle for hire unless such person possesses the proper driver's license as required under the laws of the state and is not less than 18 years of age and has obtained a permit as set forth herein.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Secs. 22-185—22-190. - Reserved.
Sec. 22-191. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means an individual registering a business authorized herein.
Application means a form supplied by the county for the purpose of registering a business authorized herein.
Business means the business of operating or owning a taxicab business.
Certificate means certificate issued or approved by the director authorizing the holder thereof to conduct a taxicab business within the county.
County means Lincoln County, Georgia.
Convicted or conviction shall include a plea of nolo contendere.
Director means the director of planning and zoning.
Driver, operator means any person in charge of or driving or operating a taxicab, as defined herein, whether as owner, agent, employee or otherwise.
Driver's permit means the permission granted by the governing body to a person to drive a taxicab within the county.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form IRS W-2 but not a form IRS 1099.
Highway means any of the public streets, alleys, lanes, boulevards, avenues, drives, circles, or roads of the county.
Individual means a natural person.
Interest in a business means an interest held by an owner or co-owner of a registered business; by a partner of a general or limited partnership which owns or co-owns a registered business; a stockholder of five percent or more shares in any closely held corporation which owns or co-owns a registered business; or anyone sharing in the income or corpus of any trust or estate having such an interest; or a person having subscriptions, options, and/or warranty for purchase of five percent or more of a closely held corporation's stock or 50 percent or more of any corporation's stock.
Memorandum certificate means the card issued to a taxicab owner to display within the taxicab indicating that such taxicab has been issued a certificate.
Non-emergency transport vehicles means a vehicle used to transport an individual for a non-urgent procedure or elective care where there is no need for immediate assistance.
Owner means a person having an interest in a business, including a stockholder and to whom a certificate for operating a taxicab has been or is to be issued.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business, trust, receiver, fiduciary or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic or political subdivision, whether public, private, or quasi-public, or any entity.
Taxicab means any motor vehicle seating eight or fewer persons and the driver, operating upon any street on call or demand, accepting or soliciting passengers indiscriminately for hire between such points along such streets as may be directed by the passengers being so transported. This definition shall include non-emergency transport vehicles.
Vehicles for hire means taxicab as defined herein.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-192. - Compliance with laws, due regard for safety required.
All taxicabs operating within the county shall comply with all laws of the United States of America, the state and the county regulating the speed, the operation of motor vehicles and the requirements for safety equipment and devices. All passengers, whether adult, children or infants shall use the required passenger restraint devices including child restraints as necessary. Drivers shall show due regard for the safety, comfort and convenience of passengers, for the safe and careful transportation of property and for the safety of the general public.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-193. - Identification of vehicles as taxicabs.
(a)
Information required. All motor vehicles operated as taxicabs in the county shall be marked on the outside with the following information with respect thereto:
(1)
The name of the owner of the taxicab.
(2)
The word "taxicab" or "taxi."
(3)
If a fleet of more than one taxicab is operated by one company or owner, a number shall be assigned to each vehicle, prominently displayed and identifying.
(4)
Any other information which may be deemed necessary to identify such vehicle as required by the director.
(b)
Affixing information. The above markings or identification shall be prominently displayed on both sides of the vehicles operated as taxicabs in letters of not less than three inches in height and must be printed so that the identification may be easily read at a distance of at least 50 feet. Such information may be displayed on vehicle with paint, by stencil, by decals or with magnetic signs.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-194. - Riding on exterior of vehicle.
No person shall be permitted to ride on the fender, hood, roof or any other exterior portion of a taxicab.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Secs. 22-195—22-200. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - OPERATION OF BUSINESS

Sec. 22-201. - Business license—Application.
All persons applying for a certificate to conduct a taxicab business shall do so in writing to the director on the form provided by county. Such applicant shall furnish all information required by such form in complete detail and shall certify under oath to the correctness of the information.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-202. - Action upon application and issuance.
The director shall investigate the application provided for in section 23-201 and shall grant the business license if the application meets all requirements of this article and payment of the required fee. The director shall also issue a memorandum certificate for each taxicab authorized by the county.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-203. - Expiration, reinstatement.
All business licenses issued under this article shall expire on December 31 of each year, including the year of issue, but may be reinstated by the director for the following year, provided that there has been no revocation thereof and further provided that all license fees required to be paid have been paid.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-204. - Registration of vehicle—Required.
(a)
Duty of operator. Prior to placing a taxicab in service, and prior to annually renewing the permit, any operator shall register that vehicle with the director, providing a complete and true description of such vehicle to include the serial number, make, model and color; and a statement from an automotive service excellency (ASE) certified mechanic concerning the safety of the vehicle.
(b)
Duty of the director. The director shall maintain a file on each operator, including the description of each vehicle registered as required by this section, and he may issue identification numbers for each registered vehicle. The director may make inspections at any time to assure that the taxicabs in operation are registered and in full compliance with this article.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-205. - Identification numbers to be affixed to vehicle.
Should the director issue identification numbers, these numbers shall be affixed by decal or similar means in the rear window of the vehicle on the driver's side.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-206. - Insurance of vehicle; required.
(a)
Every person operating a taxicab in the county or the owner of any such taxicab shall provide the director with a copy of the insurance certificate showing that such vehicle is properly insured in accordance with the laws of the state, and such insurance company shall be properly licensed to do business in the state and the county.
(b)
Before using or operating any taxicab subject to the provisions of this article for transporting passengers, and before allowing any other person to do so within the county upon the roads, streets and highways, the owner shall provide for each vehicle to be so used, and for the benefit of any and all persons having claims against the owner for damage by way of loss of life, damage to property, and/or for injury to the person, minimum liability insurance coverage as follows:
(1)
Minimum personal injury coverage for injuries .....$100,000.00
(2)
Minimum property damage coverage .....200,000.00
(c)
Insurance coverage required by this division shall be subject to approval by the director and shall be with a company and in a form satisfactory to the board of commissioners.
(d)
It shall be the duty of the owner to deposit such policies with the director, where they shall remain while in force.
(e)
It shall be the duty of the insurer to give reasonable notice to the county before canceling any coverage. Any company failing to do so will not be considered an acceptable company for the purpose of furnishing such coverage.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-207. - Cancellation of insurance, notice required.
The county shall be given a minimum of ten days' written notice of cancellation of vehicular insurance covering the operation of a taxicab.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-208. - Suspension of unsafe vehicle from service.
The director may suspend from operation any taxicab found to be unsafe, and any vehicle so suspended shall not be permitted to again operate until repaired and put in proper condition for use. Prior to returning to service, such taxicab shall be inspected by an ASE certified mechanic and a statement from the mechanic shall be submitted to the director.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-209. - Sale, trade, exchange of vehicle.
(a)
Notice required. If any owner or operator of a taxicab in the county shall sell, trade or exchange any taxicab being operated in the county and substitute another car therefore, it shall be the duty of the owner or operator to notify the director within 24 hours of the sale, trade or exchange of the vehicle, and such owner or operator shall provide the director with the identification number of such vehicle under which the vehicle is identified on the insurance policy on file.
(b)
Transfer of insurance required. It shall be the duty of such owner or operator of the car substituted to furnish director written endorsement of the insurance company issuing the vehicular insurance policy showing that the additional vehicle is properly insured under the terms of the policy.
(c)
Any person receiving a license under this article shall be required to amend the license application, originally filed for the license, to include new taxicabs or exclude old taxicabs promptly upon adding or deleting such vehicles from the taxicab business so that an up-to-date list of operating taxicabs may be maintained.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-210. - Rates and charges—Contents of schedule, posting required.
Each taxicab operator licensed in the county shall post, on both sides of his taxicabs, a schedule of rates, such schedule clearly showing distances and prices therefore. Such posting shall be clearly visible to any passenger entering the vehicle from either side in contrasting letters not less than one inch in height. Each such operator shall furnish a similar copy of such rates so posted in his taxicabs to the director.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-211. - Penalties and violations.
It shall be unlawful for any taxicab owner or operator to violate any provision of this article. Violations of this article shall be tried upon citation and summons to the Magistrate Court of Lincoln County and shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $200.00 or more than $1,000.00 or up to 60 days confinement or both. Not withstanding any other provision of this article, upon conviction of violation of any provision of this article the director may suspend or revoke such permit as set forth in section 22-304.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-212. - Maintenance of taxicabs, records by owners.
(a)
Every owner of a taxicab or taxicab company shall comply with the following regulations:
(1)
Condition of taxicab. All taxicabs shall be maintained in a clean and safe condition at all times.
(2)
Record of calls. The owner or taxicab company shall keep a record of all telephone calls and fares for taxicab service for a minimum of 30 days.
(b)
No taxicab licensed by Lincoln County shall be older than ten model-years.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Secs. 22-213—22-300. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DRIVERS

Sec. 22-301. - Permit—Required, application fee.
(a)
Every person desiring to operate a taxicab within the county shall be required to have an operator's permit issued by the director under this Code. This subsection shall not apply to a taxicab licensed in another jurisdiction which delivers a passenger to the unincorporated area of Lincoln County from another city or county or picks up a passenger in the unincorporated area of Lincoln County for transport to another city or county.
(b)
Applicants for a taxicab operator's permit shall register with the director, furnishing name, address, a photo copy of a valid driver's license issued by the state, a set of three photographs, passport or similar type, which clearly and accurately depict the applicant's face from full front view. The applicant shall make application to the Lincoln County Sheriffs Department for traffic and criminal records background check, any cost or fees associated with such records check shall be paid to the sheriffs department by the applicant. The results of the traffic and criminal background check shall be attached to the application by the director.
(c)
Notwithstanding the date of conviction, plea of guilty or nolo contendere to any offense described in subsection (d) of this section or the fact that an applicant is on probation or parole as a result of any such offense may be a factor considered by the director in determining whether to issue a taxicab operator's permit.
(d)
No person shall be issued a permit to operate a taxicab in the county if the records check reveals any of the following conditions:
(1)
A conviction, plea of guilty or nolo contendere to any offense constituting a felony against the person of another as defined in O.C.G.A. Title 16, Chapter 5 within the five years immediately preceding the application for such permit.
(2)
A conviction, plea of guilty, or nolo contendere to any other felony offense within three years immediately preceding the application for such permit.
(3)
A conviction, plea of guilty, or nolo contendere to an offense involving prostitution, solicitation for prostitution, or the illegal manufacture, sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages or drugs during the three years immediately preceding the date of application for such permit.
(4)
A conviction, plea of guilty or plea of nolo contendere to DUI (driving under the influence) during the year immediately preceding the date of application for such permit.
(5)
An applicant having two or more convictions, pleas of guilty or pleas of nolo contendere to DUI during a three-year period of time shall not be eligible to receive to taxicab operator's permit for a period of five years following the date of the most recent DUI conviction or plea.
(6)
Any person who has been convicted of, pled guilty to or forfeited bonds as being in violation of traffic offenses totaling more than 20 points, as provided by state law within the five years immediately prior to his application for a license, shall not be entitled to a permit to operate a taxicab or vehicle for hire within the county.
(7)
A conviction, plea of guilty or nolo contendere to any offenses involving sexual behavior, whether felony or misdemeanor, as set out in O.C.G.A. Title 16, Chapter 6, Sexual Offenses.
(e)
Applicants for a taxicab operator's permit shall be required to sign an agreement whereby they obligate themselves to meet minimum dress code requirements and standards of conduct and appearance as set out below:
(1)
Drivers shall be clean and neat in appearance, a shirt must be worn; no sleeveless or collarless shirts or blouses; no cut-off pants.
(2)
Drivers shall be clean-shaven or shall maintain neatly trimmed mustaches or beards.
(f)
Fees. An investigative fee of $30.00 shall be paid to county by each applicant for a taxicab driver's permit at the time his or her original application is filed and upon any application for renewal or for replacement of a taxicab driver's permit. A regulatory annual fee shall be due at the time of issuance or renewal of permits; the annual permit fee for a taxi company shall be $200.00 and the annual permit fee for a taxicab driver shall be $50.00.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-302. - Action upon application.
Issuance of permit; contents. After receipt of the application for permit provided for in section 22-201, the director shall consider the same, and if satisfied from such application and other evidence that applicant is a fit and proper person to safely drive a taxicab in the county, and that application complies with the provisions of this article and the traffic ordinances of the county, the permit shall be granted the applicant and signed by the director. The permit shall contain the name and description and photo of the applicant and such other data as the director deems proper.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-303. - Posting inside taxicab.
The name and picture of each taxicab driver, shall be attached to the permit issued to such driver, and such permit, picture and signature shall be prominently displayed inside each taxicab such driver operates.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-304. - Suspension, revocation.
Each permit granted for the privilege of driving a taxicab upon the roads of the county is subject to suspension or revocation, or both, by the director whenever the holder of such permit violates or fails to comply with a provision of this article, whether or not there has been a trial of such driver before any court. The director shall notify such driver in writing of the suspension or revocation.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-305. - Appeal from denial, suspension, revocation.
Any person to whom the director has denied a permit to drive a taxicab, and any person whose permit has been suspended or revoked shall have the right to appeal to the board of commissioners, which may, by resolution, grant or deny relief as may appear proper to the board of commissioners and in accordance with the terms and intent of this article.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-306. - Extent of authority of director.
Because of the necessity of providing for the public safety and the public health, and because the driving of taxicabs upon the roads of the county is a mere privilege and not a right, a broad discretion shall be vested in the director in granting or refusing permits, suspending and revoking such permits and carrying out the provisions and intent of this article.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-307. - Extent of authority of board of commissioners.
The board of commissioners shall not be limited in the control over permits to operate taxicabs to cases appealed from the action of the director, nor to cases arising because of violations of the terms of this article. The board of commissioners shall have the power to suspend or revoke any permit granted for the driving of a taxicab on county roads whenever it finds that such suspension or revocation would be in the public interest.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-308. - Health generally.
(a)
All persons applying for permits to drive or already driving taxicabs on county roads shall be in good health and be capable of physically performing the work as a driver.
(b)
No person shall drive a taxicab when on account of poor eyesight not corrected by glasses, or on account of bodily or mental infirmity, or being afflicted by infectious or contagious disease or for other reasons, he is unable safely to drive a taxicab upon such roads.
(c)
Should the director have concerns about the capability of any applicant to comply with the section, the director may require that such applicant, at his own expense, undergo a physical examination by a licensed physician. Refusal of the applicant to undergo the examination shall constitute grounds for denial or suspension of the permit.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-309. - Fingerprinting.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the roads of the county until he has had his fingerprints taken by the sheriff department.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Secs. 22-310—22-320. - Reserved.
Sec. 22-321. - Familiarity with ordinances required.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the roads of the county until he has shown, upon examination by the director, a familiarity with the provisions of this article, the same to be furnished by the director to each driver.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-322. - Obedience to law enforcement.
A person driving a taxicab upon the roads of the county shall bring the vehicle to a full stop immediately upon command of a member of the sheriff department and any other law enforcement officer, and shall proceed only as lawfully directed by such officer.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-323. - Driving under the influence of alcoholic beverages and/or narcotics.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the roads of the county while under the influence of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or who habitually uses alcoholic beverages or narcotics.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-324. - Transporting alcoholic beverages, narcotics.
(a)
Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person operating a licensed taxicab service in the county or any driver operating a taxicab in the county under such license, knowingly to haul any form of untaxed or open alcoholic beverages, narcotics, or other illegal substances in such taxicab.
(b)
Revocation of operator's license. If the driver of any taxicab in the county knowingly allows or permits untaxed or open alcoholic beverages or narcotics to be hauled or consumed in the taxicab operated by him, the holder or owner of the license issued by the county, under which such taxicab is being operated, shall forfeit his license and the license shall be automatically revoked. If any license is forfeited and revoked because a driver is guilty of hauling alcoholic beverages or narcotics, the licensee shall have the right of a hearing before the board of commissioners at such time and place as the board may designate. Pending such hearing before the board of commissioners, the owner or holder of such license shall not operate the taxicab service under and by virtue of any such license.
(c)
Forfeiture of driver's rights. It shall be unlawful for any driver guilty of knowingly hauling alcoholic beverages or narcotics in any taxicab driven by him to again drive a taxicab in the county.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-325. - Transporting for illegal purposes.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the roads of the county transporting prostitutes or soliciting or transporting persons for the purposes of illegal actions.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-326. - Number of passengers restricted.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the roads of the county when such vehicle contains passengers in excess of the maximum number for which the vehicle was designed.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-327. - Receipt, discharge of passengers.
Drivers of taxicabs shall not receive or discharge passengers in the roadway.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-328. - Inspection of taxicab, driver.
Every person applying for a permit to drive or who drives a taxicab upon the roads of the county must submit himself and the taxicab driven by him to the inspection of the director whenever so directed by such director.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-329. - Permit not transferable.
No permit shall be transferred and any permit to drive a motor vehicle issued under this article shall be valid only when the person issued the permit is driving for the company shown on the permit.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-330. - Smoking.
The driver of any taxicab operated upon the streets of Lincoln County shall not smoke in the taxicab while the taxicab is occupied.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-331. - Property left in taxicab.
(a)
Any taxicab driver or operator discovering in any taxicab under his or her control personal property which was lost or left therein by a passenger shall report the loss and deliver all such property to the office of the taxicab company within 12 hours after the discovery of the property and the company shall retain the property on behalf of the owner for at least 90 days. In lieu of such procedure, the taxicab driver or operator may deposit such personal property with the Lincoln County Sheriffs Department within 12 hours after the discovery of it.
(b)
The company shall make all reasonable efforts to contact the owner, if the identity of the owner can reasonably be ascertained.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
Sec. 22-332. - Additional rules authorized.
(a)
The director, upon approval of the board of commissioners, is fully authorized and empowered to adopt additional policies or regulations respecting taxicabs, operators and drivers consistent with this Code and state law, and to designate, and from time to time change or eliminate, taxicab stands or parking areas.
(b)
These additional regulations shall become effective after approval by the board of commissioners and after being filed with the clerk of the board of commissioners unless disapproved within 15 days of such filing by motion of the board.
(c)
The board of commissioners may, by resolution, amend these regulations at any time.
(Ord. of 3-8-2007)
