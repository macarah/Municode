ARTICLE II. - WATER AND SEWER IMPROVEMENT PROJECT
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 70-31. - Title.
This article shall be known as the "Lincoln County Water and Sewer Improvement Project Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 43, § 201, 5-10-2002)
Sec. 70-32. - Definitions.
The following, words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Director means the director of the water department.
(Ord. No. 43, § 202, 5-10-2002)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-33. - Administration and enforcement.
The director of the water department shall administer and enforce the connections to the sewage system and the assessment and collection of fees provided for in this article.
(Ord. No. 43, § 202, 5-10-2002)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Secs. 70-34—70-50. - Reserved.

Sec. 70-51. - Requirement to connect.
(a)
Each residential, business and industrial user of county water within 250 feet of the county sewage line must tap onto and use the sewage system belonging to the county, unless such connection is waived by the board of commissioners.
(b)
All new construction within 250 feet of the county sewage line must connect to and utilize the county water system and the county sewage system, unless such connection is waived by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 43, § 301, 5-10-2002)
Sec. 70-52. - Cost to connect.
(a)
All existing residences, businesses and industries who are county water users connecting to the county water and sewage system during the initial construction period of the system in the Old Petersburg Road Area shall be connected without cost to the property owner, including installation of the individual service line from the dwelling or commercial property through the property of the individual to the point of the tap into the county line, and the county shall further bear the expense of the tap into the county line.
(b)
After the initial construction period of the system in the Old Petersburg Road Area, each property owner who is required to connect onto any expansion of the sewage system belonging to the county shall pay the cost of installation of the individual service line to connect his property to the county system and shall further pay a tap fee to connect the individual service line to the county system, unless such costs and tap fees are waived by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 43, § 302, 5-10-2002)
Secs. 70-53—70-70. - Reserved.

Sec. 70-71. - Tap fees.
No tap fees shall be paid by residences, businesses, or industries connecting to the county sewage system during the initial construction period in the Old Petersburg Road Area. After the initial construction period and initial operation of the sewage system, all future connections to the county sewage system shall pay tap fees authorized and set from time to time by resolution of the board of commissioners unless waived by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 43, § 401, 5-10-2002)
Sec. 70-72. - Sewage usage fees and/or sewage rates.
(a)
Each residential, commercial and industrial water user who taps onto and connects to the system shall pay to the county a monthly sewage user fee based on the number of gallons of water usage according to the rates as authorized and set from time to time by resolution of the board of commissioners of the county.
(b)
All usage fees and/or sewer rates for the use of the county water and sewage system shall be set from time to time by resolution of the board of commissioners, where necessary in conjunction with the City of Lincolnton and as agreed upon by the county and the city.
(Ord. No. 43, § 402, 5-10-2002)
