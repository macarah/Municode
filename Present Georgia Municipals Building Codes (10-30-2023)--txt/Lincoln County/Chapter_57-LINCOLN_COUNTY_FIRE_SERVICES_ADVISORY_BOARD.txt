ARTICLE II. - FIRE SERVICES ADVISORY BOARD

Sec. 57-21. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Fire services advisory board means the designated representatives of the four volunteer fire departments located in Lincoln County, Georgia, and the listed representatives designated by the Lincoln County board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
Sec. 57-22. - Created.
There is hereby created and established a fire services advisory board for the county which shall be known as the "Lincoln County fire services advisory board."
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
Sec. 57-23. - Membership.
The fire services advisory board created in this article shall consist of two members from each of the four fire districts located in Lincoln County. Additionally, the director of the Lincoln County office of emergency services shall serve as a member.
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
Sec. 57-24. - Eligibility; terms of office; vacancy and removal.
(a)
To be eligible for appointment to the fire services advisory board from a fire district, a member must meet the following requirements: Each member elected to the fire services advisory board from a fire district board shall be a resident of that same fire district. Of the two members from each fire district, one shall be the current fire chief of that fire department. the other member shall be a current member of the board of directors from that same fire district. The member from the district fire board shall be appointed by a majority vote of the current members of that fire district board.
(b)
The chairman of the fire services advisory board shall be the director of the Lincoln County office of emergency services.
(c)
The terms of the members of the fire services advisory board shall continue until a member is no longer a resident of the fire district, no longer serves on the local district board, or the member is removed from the fire services advisory board by a majority vote of the local fire district board of directors.
(d)
Any member of the fire services advisor board may be removed for cause by a majority vote of the Lincoln County, Georgia board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
Sec. 57-25. - Oath of office.
No member under this article shall begin to discharge his duties as a member of the fire services advisory board without first having taken the oath of office.
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
Sec. 57-26. - Organization.
Immediately after appointment, the membership of the fire services advisory board shall meet and organize by electing a vice-chair and secretary. The fire services advisory board may prepare and adopt by-laws, rules, and regulations regarding the conduct of its affairs.
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
Sec. 57-27. - Meetings.
(a)
The fire services advisory board shall meet the first Thursday of each month in the months of January, March, May, July, September, and November.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit special meetings being called by the chair of the board or by a request of four board members.
(c)
The membership of any member of the fire services advisory board missing two meetings during a calendar year shall be automatically terminated under this section.
(d)
The fire services advisory board shall keep official written minutes of each meeting. The minutes of each meeting shall be provided to the board of commissioners within 15 days of the date of the meeting.
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
Sec. 57-28. - Reports.
The fire services advisory board shall submit a written report to the board of commissioners of its transactions, findings, proceedings, and recommendations at least once per year.
(Ord. of 3-8-2018)
