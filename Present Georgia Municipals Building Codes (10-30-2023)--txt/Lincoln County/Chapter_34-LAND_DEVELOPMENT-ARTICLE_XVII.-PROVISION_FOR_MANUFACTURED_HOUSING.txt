Sec. 34-551. - Purpose and definitions.
(a)
Purpose. The purpose of this article is to provide standards and regulations to protect the health, safety and general welfare of the residents of the county. It is the intent of the county to encourage the provision of affordable housing in a general residential environment.
(b)
Definitions. For the purpose of this article the following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section.
Manufactured home means any structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air-conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein; except that such term shall include any structure which meets all the requirements of this paragraph except the size requirements and with respect to which the manufacturer voluntarily files a certification required by the secretary of housing and urban development and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401, et seq.
New manufactured home means any manufactured home that has not been previously used as a residence, has not been titled, and complies with the current standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401, et seq. Compliance shall be evidenced by presence of HUD Certification Label and Manufactures Data Plate in accordance with CFR Title 24 Chapter XX Part 3280 - Manufactured Homes Construction and Safety Standards.
Pre-owned/used manufactured home means any manufactured home that has been previously used as a residential dwelling and has been titled and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401, et seq. Compliance shall be evidenced by inspection by the building official or his/her designated representative.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)
Sec. 34-552. - Utilities.
Electricity shall not be provided to any manufactured home until the building official determines that the requirements of this chapter have been met, by conducting a final inspection and by granting final approval. The building official shall then issue a release for power for electricity to the manufactured home.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)

Sec. 34-553. - Location disclosure and decal requirement.
(a)
On or before May 1 of each year, each and every owner of a manufactured home subject to taxation as required by state law shall obtain from the tax commissioner of the county, or the county of taxation of the manufactured home, a manufactured home location permit.
(b)
On or before May 1 of each year, every owner of a manufactured home located in the county on January 1 which is not subject to taxation under state laws, shall obtain a manufactured home location permit from the tax commissioner of the county.
(c)
The issuance of the manufactured home location permit by the tax commissioner of the county shall be evidenced by a decal provided by the state revenue commissioner and be in such shape, color, size, and contain such information as prescribed by the state revenue commissioner each calendar year. Each decal shall reflect the county of issuance and the calendar year for which the permit is issued.
(d)
The decal shall be prominently attached and displayed on the manufactured home by the owner.
(e)
This chapter shall be enforced by the office of planning and zoning. Citations for violations of this chapter may be issued by the director, department of planning and zoning of the county; the code enforcement officer of the county; the sheriff or any of his lawful deputies.
(1)
Any owner who fails to attach and display the decal on the manufactured home as required in this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished by a fine of not less than $25.00 nor more than $200.00, except that upon receipt of proof of purchase of a decal prior to the date of the issuance of a summons, the fine shall be $25.00.
(2)
Any owner or person who moves, assists in moving, or transports into the county or moves a manufactured home from one location in the county to another location in the county and which manufactured home is required to have and which does not have attached and displayed thereon the decal as provided in this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished by a fine of not less than $200.00 nor more than $1,000.00 or by imprisonment for not more than 12 months, or both.
(f)
The magistrate court of the county shall have criminal jurisdiction over violations of the provisions of this chapter as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60.
(g)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-5-490—48-5-495 governing manufactured homes are hereby incorporated in this article and made a part of this chapter to the same extent as if set out in their entirety.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)
Sec. 34-554. - Manufactured home permit required.
(a)
Any owner of a manufactured home, whether locating or relocating the manufactured home within the county, shall file an application and fee, as established by the county, for a manufactured home permit with the building official.
(b)
Application procedure for new manufactured home. No new manufactured home shall be moved into or within the county until the following conditions have been met:
(1)
Septic tank permit. A septic tank permit must be obtained from the county health department, a copy of which must be forwarded to the building official.
(2)
Location decal. A valid location decal must be obtained from the tax commissioner's office, a copy of which must be forwarded to the building official.
(3)
Site plan. A site plan for the proposed location of the new manufactured home must be completed, a copy of which must be forwarded to the building official.
(4)
Application. An application for a new manufactured home permit shall be made on forms developed for that purpose and shall be submitted to the building official for review and approval in accordance with this chapter.
Upon completion of the preceding conditions, the building official shall review and determine whether the proposed new manufactured home is in compliance with the requirements established by this chapter. The building official shall issue a new manufactured home permit or deny the request after the application has been fully reviewed.
(c)
Application procedure for pre-owned/used manufactured home. No pre-owned/used manufactured home shall be moved into or within the county until the following conditions have been met:
(1)
Septic tank permit. A septic tank permit must be obtained from the county health department, a copy of which must be forwarded to the building official.
(2)
Location decal. A valid location decal must be obtained from the tax commissioner's office, a copy of which must be forwarded to the building official.
(3)
Site plan. A site plan for the proposed location of the pre-owned/used manufactured home must be completed, a copy of which must be forwarded to the building official.
(4)
Transport permit. A transport permit must be obtained from the building inspector. To obtain a transport permit, an applicant shall meet the following conditions:
a.
The applicant must provide a notarized affidavit signed by the applicant that the pre-owned/used manufactured home meets health and safety standards required by the Land Development Code and subsection (e) below. The affidavit shall be made on forms developed and provided for by the county for that purpose;
b.
The applicant must provide a $1,000.00 ($500.00 refundable) guarantee of condition bond; bond shall be by cash, certified check, bank check or money order.
1.
Upon completion of setup, all required repairs and issuance of a "certificate of occupancy", $500.00 of the $1,000.00 guarantee of condition bond will be refunded to the applicant/owner.
2.
Enforcement. For the purpose of this chapter the guarantee of condition bond shall be forfeited in its entirety ($1,600.00) should the applicant/owner:
a.
Fail to complete the necessary corrections required to bring the home into compliance with the requirements of subsection (e) within 90 days of issuance of a manufactured home permit, or
b.
Fail to demolish or remove from Lincoln County within 14 days any pre-owned/used manufactured home which has been declared unsafe for human occupation by the building official.
(5)
Pre-installation inspection. Upon issue of transport permit the applicant/owner shall transport the pre-owned/used manufactured home, to a pre-determined and designated inspection location within Lincoln County. The cost of moving the home to or from the inspection lot shall be the sole responsibility of the applicant/owner. The transporter and applicant/owner shall hold Lincoln County harmless for any and all costs, fees or damages, either physical or monetary resulting from moving the manufactured home.
a.
Inspection. Upon relocation of the manufactured home to the inspection lot, an inspection will be completed by the building official to ensure the manufactured home meets the standards established by the Land Development Code. If the manufactured home does not meet these standard[s], in accordance with section 34-552, the building official shall approve or disapprove the manufactured home for installation by written report. The report shall list defects or code violations found during the pre-installation inspection with corrective action required to obtain compliance with subsection 34-553(e).
b.
Exception: Manufactured homes installed or setup on lots or parcels within the confines of Lincoln County may be inspected on or at the original site prior to relocation to a new lot or parcel. In no case shall any pre-owned /used manufactured home be relocated within the county prior to the completion of the pre-installation inspection.
(6)
Application. An application for a pre-owned/used manufactured home permit shall be made on forms developed for that purpose and shall be submitted to the building official for review and approval in accordance with this chapter. The application for a pre-owned/used manufactured home permit shall be made on forms developed for that purpose and shall be submitted to the building official for review and approval in accordance with this chapter.
(d)
Pre-owned/used manufactured home permit. Upon determination by the building official that the pre-owned/used manufactured home will meet the minimum manufactured home standards as set forth in subsection (e), by condition or corrective action to be performed, a manufactured home permit will be issued for the home.
(e)
Manufactured home minimum standards. All manufactured homes shall comply with the following standards:
(1)
[HUD Code.] Every pre-owned/used manufactured home located or relocated in Lincoln County shall be in accordance with the Federal Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act, 42 U.S.C. 5401-5445 (the HUD code) and shall not have been altered in such a way that the home no longer meets HUD code.
(2)
Interior condition. Every floor, interior wall, and ceiling of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in sound condition. Doors and windows shall be operable, watertight and in good working condition. The floor system shall be in sound condition and free of warping, holes, water damage, or deterioration.
(3)
Exterior condition. The exterior of all pre-owned manufactured homes shall be free of loose or rotting boards or timbers and any other conditions that might admit rain or moisture to the interior portions of the walls or to occupied spaces. The exterior siding shall be free of rot and rust. Roofs shall be structurally sound and have no obvious defects that might admit rain or cause moisture to collect on the interior portion of the home. The bottom board or under board shall be continuous and free of defects. All plumbing, heating-ventilating-air conditioning, and electrical connections shall be sealed to the bottom board/under board to prevent rodent and insect infestation.
(4)
Sanitary facilities. Every plumbing fixture, water, and waste pipe of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in a sanitary working condition when properly connected, and shall be free from leaks and obstructions. Each home shall contain a kitchen sink. Each bathroom shall contain a lavatory and water closet. At least one bathroom shall contain a tub and/or shower facilities. Each of these fixtures shall be checked upon being connected to ensure they are in good working condition.
(5)
[Heating systems.] Heating systems shall be safe and in working condition. Un-vented fuel fired space heaters shall be prohibited.
(6)
[Electrical systems.] Electrical systems (switches, receptacles, fixtures, etc.) shall be properly installed and wired and shall be in working condition. Distribution panels shall be in compliance with the approved listing, complete with required breakers, with all unused openings covered with solid covers approved and listed for that purpose. The home shall be subject to an electrical continuity test to assure that all metallic parts are properly bonded. Aluminum wiring smaller than #6 American Wire Gauge shall be prohibited.
(7)
Hot water supply. Each home shall contain a water heater in safe and working condition.
(8)
Egress windows. Each bedroom of a manufactured home shall have at least one operable window of sufficient size to allow egress if necessary.
(9)
Ventilation. The kitchen in the home shall have at least a working domestic hood system.
(10)
Smoke detectors. Each pre-owned manufactured home shall contain one operable battery-powered smoke detector in each bedroom and in the kitchen, which must be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations.
(f)
Penalties. Notwithstanding any other provision of the Code of Ordinances of Lincoln County, any owner or person who moves, assists in moving, or transports into the county or moves a manufactured home from one location in the county to another location in the county without a county manufactured home permit shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished by a fine of not less than $200.00 nor more than $1,000.00 or by imprisonment for not more than 12 months, or both. Any applicant/owner who shall willfully violate any other provision of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor punishable by a fine of not less than $100.00 per day nor more than $1,000.00 per day for each day that such violation has continued or shall continue.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)
Sec. 34-555. - Administrative procedures.
(a)
HUD code or DCA approval required. Only HUD code or DCA approved manufactured homes will be permitted to locate in the county for residential purposes.
(b)
Permit procedure.
(1)
All applications for manufactured permits under this chapter shall be submitted to the building official. The building official shall make an investigation of all applicants and shall make a determination regarding the applicant/owner's compliance with the provisions of this chapter. The building official shall render a decision regarding approval or disapproval of the manufactured home permit within 14 days of the building official's receipt of the application and any other documents required by section 34-554.
(2)
Any applicant/owner who alleges there is an error in, or who is aggrieved by a decision of the building official in the administration, enforcement, and/or interpretation of this chapter, may file a written appeal with the director of planning and zoning which states the grounds for such appeal. The director of planning and zoning is hereby authorized to hear and decide said appeal. The director of planning and zoning reserves the right to establish the process for such an appeal, including creation of forms and other documents.
(c)
Recreational vehicles. The above requirements do not apply to recreational vehicles because they are not acceptable for permanent occupancy and may be used for a maximum of 90 consecutive days as a temporary residence. All recreational vehicles must display a current state license plate sticker before movement. A temporary permit for recreational vehicles must be obtained from the director of planning and zoning.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)
Sec. 34-556. - Manufactured home installation requirements.
Prior to final approval by the building official and issuance of a release for power for electricity, all manufactured homes must undergo installation inspection. Installation of manufactured homes must comply with the following requirements:
(a)
Utility connections. All manufactured homes must be permanently connected to water, sewerage and electrical service.
(1)
Water. Water must be either county water, a community water system, or a well located on the site. Connection procedures must meet the requirements of the most recent plumbing code adopted by the state and must be freeze proof above and below ground. Water connection must be inspected and approved by the director.
(2)
Sewer. Plumbing of the septic system must meet the most current plumbing code adopted by the state and any county health department requirements. The sewerage system must be inspected and approved by the county health department.
(3)
Electrical. Electrical service must meet requirements of the most recent electrical code adopted by the state. Electrical connection must be inspected and approved by the building official prior to authorization of a release for power to the servicing power company to install permanent power service.
(b)
Foundation. All manufactured homes must be installed on a pier system, installed with permanent skirting, secured with tie down devices and provided with steps and landings. The installation of all required foundation requirements must be inspected and approved by the building official.
(c)
Pier system. Piers or load bearing supports or devices shall be designed and constructed to evenly distribute the loads. Piers shall be securely attached to the frame of the manufactured home or shall extend at least six inches from the centerline of the frame member. Manufactured load bearing supports or devices shall be listed or approved for the use intended, or piers shall be constructed as follows:
a.
Piers less than 40 inches in height shall be constructed of open or closed cell, eight-inch by eight-inch by 16-inch concrete blocks (with open cells vertically placed upon the footer). Single-stacked block piers shall be installed with the 16-inch dimensions perpendicular to the main (I-beam) frame. The piers shall be covered with a two-inch by eight-inch by 16-inch pressure treated wood or concrete.
b.
Piers between 40 and 80 inches in height and all corner piers over three blocks high shall be double blocked with blocks interlocked and capped with a four-inch by 16-inch by 16-inch solid concrete block, or equivalent cap.
c.
Piers over 80 inches in height shall be constructed in accordance with subsection (b)(1)b. of this section and they shall be laid in concrete mortar and steel reinforcing bars inserted in block cells with the block cells filled with concrete.
d.
All piers shall be placed on footings of solid concrete not less than 16 inches by 16 inches by six inches deep on compacted or undisturbed earth.
(d)
Skirting. Installation of skirting shall be required. Installation shall be in accordance with the manufacturer's installation instructions. Acceptable materials as set forth in sections 34-557 and 34-558.
(e)
Tie down. Each manufactured home shall have tie downs or other devices securing the stability of the manufactured home as per installation of manufactured homes and mobile homes rules and regulations established and published by state safety fire commissioner (O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160 et seq.).
a.
The requirements for units 16 feet and less in width require two types of anchorage:
i.
Over the top tie downs to restrict overturning; and
ii.
Frame tie downs to restrict the unit from being pushed from its piers.
b.
The requirements for units over 16 feet in width (class A) require frame tie downs, only.
(f)
Steps and landings. Steps and landings are required for all homes and shall be constructed to the standards set forth in the most recent building code adopted by the state.
(g)
Permanent connection and installation of recreational vehicles prohibited. Permanent connection of recreational vehicles to water or electrical service is prohibited. Permanent installation of recreational vehicles on piers and jacks is also prohibited.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)
Sec. 34-557. - Class A manufactured homes.
(a)
Permitted locations. Manufactured homes qualifying as class A manufactured homes shall be allowed in A-1, A-2, R-2 and R-3 residential zoning districts and shall be regulated uniformly with other housing constructed on site, subject to requirements and limitations set forth in this chapter.
(b)
Compatibility standards. Compatibility standards for class A manufactured homes are as follows:
(1)
The home has a minimum width in excess of 16 feet.
(2)
The pitch of the home's roof has a minimum vertical rise of three feet for each 12 feet of horizontal run (nominal 2.8/12 or greater pitch permitted), and the roof is finished with a type of shingle that is commonly used in conventional residential construction.
(3)
The exterior siding consists of wood, hardboard, vinyl, brick, masonry, or aluminum (vinyl-covered or painted, but in no case exceeding the reflectivity of gloss white paint) comparable in composition, appearance, and durability to the exterior siding commonly used in conventional residential construction.
(4)
A masonry, mortar joint curtain wall (skirting), unpierced except for required ventilation and access, and installed so that it encloses the area located under the home to the ground level. The wall must have a minimum thickness of four inches, and must be constructed of commonly used standard clay brick or standard concrete block.
(5)
The tongue, axles, transporting lights and towing apparatus are removed after placement on the lot and before occupancy.
(6)
Landings shall be installed prior to occupancy, and of the requisite composition and size as per the most recent building code adopted by the state.
(7)
The manufactured home must be installed in accordance with O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-130 et seq. and 8-2-160 et seq. to include poured concrete footers.
(8)
Reserved.
(9)
Manufactured homes may not be used for any other purpose or use other than the residential use originally designed and approved by HUD.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)

A masonry, mortar joint curtain wall (skirting), unpierced except for required ventilation and access, installed so that it encloses the area located under the home to the ground level. Such wall must have a minimum thickness of four inches and must be constructed of commonly used standard clay brick or standard concrete block.
(3)
Landings shall be installed prior to occupancy, and of the requisite composition and size as per the most recent building code adopted by the state.
(4)
The manufactured home must be installed in accordance with O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-130 et seq. and 8-2-160 et seq. Poured concrete footers may not be required.
(5)
The tongue, axles, transporting lights, and towing apparatus are removed after placement on the lot and before occupancy.
(6)
Manufactured homes may not be used for any other purpose or use other than the residential use originally designed and approved by HUD.
(7)
Reserved.
(8)
The pitch of the home's roof has a minimum vertical rise of three feet for each 12 feet of horizontal run, and the roof is finished with a type of shingle that is commonly used in conventional residential construction.
(Amend. of 9-1-2010)

Manufactured homes may not be used for any other purpose or use other than the residential use originally designed and approved by HUD.
(6)
The tongue, axles, transporting lights and towing apparatus are removed after placement on the lot and before occupancy.
