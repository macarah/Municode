Chapter 18 - PARKS AND RECREATION[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Power to provide parks, recreation areas, programs and facilities, Ga. Const. art. IX, § II, ¶ III(a)(5); establishment of recreation systems, O.C.G.A. § 36-64-1 et seq.

Sec. 18-1. - Areas covered by chapter.
The general recreational areas covered by this chapter are:
(1)
The County Recreation Park;
(2)
The Deuce Lloyd Field;
(3)
The Burntfort Boatramp and parking area;
(4)
The Traders Hill River Park and Boatramp;
(5)
The Camp Pinkney Boatramp, parking and picnic area;
(6)
The Chatman-Mobley Recreation Park;
(7)
Future developed recreational areas.
(Code 1999, § 14-1; Res. of 3-15-1984, § II)
Sec. 18-2. - Responsible use of areas.
Every person authorized by the recreation director to use recreational areas will be held responsible and accountable for the proper use and protection of all property which comes under his control, and he shall not cause damage or waste to recreational property.
(Code 1999, § 14-2; Res. of 3-15-1984, § III(4))
Sec. 18-3. - Nature of authorized activities.
All activities in recreational areas or units under the authority granted to the recreation director shall be wholesome, carried out with proper supervision and be designed to meet the needs of the various leisure interests of the citizens of the county.
(Code 1999, § 14-3; Res. of 3-15-1984, § III(1))
Sec. 18-4. - Injuries at events to be reported.
Any accident to an individual on the property of a recreational facility during any event authorized by the recreation director shall be reported, in writing, to the clerk of the board of commissions or the county administrator within 24 hours after the injury.
(Code 1999, § 14-4; Res. of 3-15-1984, § III(2))
Sec. 18-5. - Alcoholic beverages.
The use of alcoholic beverages in any form on the premises of a recreational area or unit shall not be permitted.
(Code 1999, § 14-5; Res. of 3-15-1984, § III(3))
Sec. 18-6. - Limit use of county lands for camping.
(a)
The county provides camping facilities with potable water and sanitation facilities for a modest fee.
(b)
Long-term campers on public lands of the county, especially on or near playgrounds, boat ramps, docks, swimming areas and picnic areas, pose a public health and safety risk.
(c)
The county must limit camping or residing on the public lands of the county to seven days at a time and no more than three separate occasions in any calendar year. The designated camping areas at Traders Hill Recreation Area is excepted from this chapter.
(d)
Persons in violation of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and subject to a fine not to exceed $250.00 or ten days in jail, or both. Upon being found guilty, the offender and his personal property will be removed from the public county property immediately.
(Ord. No. 2008-1, 3-6-2008)
