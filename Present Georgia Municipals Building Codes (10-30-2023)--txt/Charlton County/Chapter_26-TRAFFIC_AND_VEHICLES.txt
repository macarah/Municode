Sec. 26-1. - Uniform Rules of the Road adopted.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-2 through 40-6-395 (except for O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-393 and 40-6-394), and O.C.G.A. §§ 40-2-20 and 40-5-20, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted by reference as the traffic regulations of the county as if set forth in full in this section.
(Code 1999, § 18-1)
Sec. 26-2. - Traffic regulations generally.
The county compiles and publishes a traffic schedule specifying regulations pertaining to the categories enumerated in this section. An official copy of the traffic schedule is on file in the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners and is hereby adopted by reference as if set out in full in this section.
(1)
The location of traffic-control devices;
(2)
Areas or zones where regulations are applied to parking and loading;
(3)
Speed limits;
(4)
Restricting or regulating traffic at certain times on certain streets or to certain types, weights or sizes of vehicles;
(5)
Location of through streets, stop intersections, yield right-of-way intersections, waiting lanes, one-way streets or truck traffic routes; and
(6)
Establishing regulations upon vehicle turns at designated locations.
(Code 1999, § 18-2; Ord. of 3-3-2011; Ord. of 10-6-2011)
Sec. 26-3. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of sections 1-10 through 1-14.
(Code 1999, § 18-3)
Sec. 26-4. - Maximum posted speed limits.
The Board of Commissioners of Charlton County hereby resolves that the maximum posted speed limit on the following specified county roads shall be as follows:
(1)
The maximum posted speed limit on Arbor Road shall be 15 miles per hour;
(2)
The maximum posted speed limit on Buck Road, in the Stokes Lake Subdivision, shall be 20 miles per hour; and
(3)
The maximum posted speed limit on Buckhorn Road, in the Stokes Lake Subdivision, shall be 20 miles per hour; and
(4)
The maximum posted speed limit on Colonial Way, in the Settlers Village Subdivision, shall be 25 miles per hour.
(5)
The maximum posted speed limit on Eleventh Street, in St. George, shall be 15 miles per hour.
(6)
The maximum posted speed limit on Farm Road shall be 25 miles per hour;
(7)
The maximum posted speed limit on Harvester Way shall be 15 miles per hour;
(8)
The maximum posted speed limit on Lakeview Drive, in the Stokes Lake Subdivision, shall be 20 miles per hour;
(9)
The maximum posted speed limit on Pine Acres Road shall be 25 miles per hour;
(10)
The maximum posted speed limit on Oak Ridge Road shall be 25 miles per hour;
(11)
The maximum posted speed limit on Settlers Way, in the Settlers Village Subdivision, shall be 25 miles per hour; and
(12)
The maximum posted speed limit on Stokes Lake Road, in the Stokes Lake Subdivision, shall be 20 miles per hour;
(13)
The maximum posted speed limit on Tiger Branch Road shall be 25 miles per hour;
(14)
The maximum posted speed limit on Turkey Ridge Road shall be 25 miles per hour.
(15)
The maximum posted speed limit on Wheeler Street, in St. George, shall be 30 miles per hour; and
Driving upon the said roads at a rate in excess of the posted speed limit shall be punishable as any misdemeanor in the State of Georgia.
The Charlton County Road Department is herewith directed to post appropriate signs consistent with the maximum speed limits set forth above.
(3-20-2014; Ord. No. 1 of 2014, 7-17-2014; Ord. No. 2 of 2014, 7-17-2014)
