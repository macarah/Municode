ARTICLE I. - ANIMALS IN GENERAL[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 1041, adopted June 1, 2010, amended art. I in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former art. I, §§ 14-1—14-7, pertained to similar subject matter, and derived from Ord. No. 987, adopted Oct. 18, 2005.

Sec. 14-1. - Keeping domestic animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or keeping any horses, mules, or any other domestic animals, chickens, ducks, geese, or other fowl, with the exception of cats, to allow or permit them to run at large within the city. Any such animal or fowl, other than dogs or cats, so found running at large may be impounded by the animal control officer, who shall sell those impounded after giving three days' notice of the time and place thereof posted at the police department, unless the owner shall within that time redeem the same by paying the fines imposed, together with all costs incurred by the city. After selling impounded animals, the proceeds of the sale shall be turned over to the treasurer of the county.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large or straying, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.
Sec. 14-2. - Limitation on keeping horses in the city.
The keeping of a horse on any lot that contains less than two acres is unlawful. One horse may be kept on any lot in the city that contains at least two contiguous acres, and one additional horse may be kept on any one lot for each additional two contiguous acres contained in one such lot. However, this shall only be allowed in ES estate subdivision—class two. The stable located on any such lot must be located at least 100 feet from the nearest property line and at least 100 feet from the nearest point on the nearest residential structure. The keeping of horses in the city other than as permitted by this section is prohibited and is unlawful.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10; Ord. No. 1180 , 11-15-22)
Sec. 14-3. - Keeping swine, cows and goats.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep hogs, pigs or other swine within the city. It shall be unlawful for any person to keep any cows or goats on a lot that is less than five acres. Cows and goats shall be permitted at a rate of one cow and two goats for each acre in excess of five, provided that the land is zoned ES estate subdivision—class two. The keeping of cows and goats in the city other than as permitted by this section is prohibited and unlawful.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10; Ord. No. 1180 , 11-15-22)
Sec. 14-4. - Limitation on number of dogs allowed to be kept in the city.
(a)
No more than three dogs may be kept on any lot in the city which contains three contiguous acres or less. One additional dog may be kept on any one lot for each additional two contiguous acres contained in one such lot. In addition, if a female dog located in the city in compliance with this chapter bears a litter of puppies, the puppies in the litter may be retained in the city on the lot where delivery occurred for a period of time not to exceed 12 weeks. The keeping of dogs in the city in excess of the number permitted by this section is prohibited, and is unlawful.
(b)
No more than three cats may be kept on any lot in the city which contains three contiguous acres or less. One additional cat may be kept on any one lot for each additional two contiguous acres contained in one such lot. In addition, if a female cat located in the city in compliance with this chapter bears a litter of kittens, the kittens in the litter may be retained in the city on the lot where delivery occurred for a period of time not to exceed 12 weeks. The keeping of cats in the city in excess of the number permitted by this section is prohibited, and is unlawful.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10; Ord. No. 1113, 6-18-19)
Sec. 14-5. - Sanitation.
(a)
Animal owners must keep their yard, property, porches, balconies, decks, etc., reasonably free of animal feces. Animal waste will be removed in a timely manner by placing said material in a closed or sealed container and thereafter disposing of it in the animal owner's trash receptacle, sanitary disposal unit, or other closed or sealed refuse container.
(b)
Persons who permit their animals to defecate on public or private property other than their own property are required to immediately pick up the feces, bag the feces and properly dispose of the feces. Animal waste shall be removed by placing said matter in a closed or sealed container and thereafter disposing of it in the animal owner's or public trash receptacle, sanitary disposal unit or other closed or sealed refuse container.
(c)
Any person found in violation of subsection (a) or (b) of this section shall be in violation of this chapter, subject to the following exceptions:
(1)
Persons using guide dogs or assistance dogs are exempt from removing feces; or
(2)
Law enforcement officers working police trained dogs.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10)
Sec. 14-6. - Penalty for violations.
Any person who violates the provisions of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11. Each day that a violation of either sections 14-2, 14-4, or 14-56 continues, after the initial conviction for a violation of such section, shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10)
Sec. 14-7. - Designation of animal control officer and enforcement personnel.
The city manager shall appoint an animal control officer and other animal control personnel to assist him, all of whom, as well as any law enforcement officer of the city, shall be empowered to carry out the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10)
Sec. 14-8. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal control officer shall mean the person appointed as such by the city manager under this chapter, and shall include any assistants of such officer in the employ of the city.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm or trustee owning, possessing, harboring, keeping or having custody or control of an animal or fowl that is subject to the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1041, 6-1-10)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.

Sec. 14-27. - Rabies inoculation required; tag; owner to maintain proof.
The owner of every cat located in the city shall have such animal inoculated against rabies in the same manner and at the same intervals as is required relative to the inoculation of dogs under the laws, rules and regulations relative to dogs. Any dog found in the city not wearing a tag evidencing inoculation as required by the laws, rules and regulations applicable thereto shall be impounded, held, redeemed, or disposed of in accordance with section 14-31, except that no animal shall be redeemed unless and until the same has been properly inoculated at the expense of the claimant, or documentary proof of a current inoculation is submitted by such claimant. The owner of any cat shall, upon demand of any law enforcement officer of the city or animal control officer of the city, produce documentary evidence of the inoculation of such cat. The failure to so do shall constitute prima facie evidence that such animal is uninoculated, and such cat shall also be subjected to the provisions of section 14-31.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-28. - Duties of dog owner in respect to control.
It shall be the duty of each owner of every dog within the city to see that his dog is, at all times, either:
(1)
Confined within a fenced area from which it cannot escape on property either owned or controlled by the owner of such dog or on the property of a person with whom the owner of such dog has an agreement relative to the keeping of such dog thereon;
(2)
Tethered, in such a fashion that such dog cannot escape therefrom, on property either owned or controlled by the owner of such dog or on the property of a person with whom the owner of such dog has an agreement relative to the tethering of such dog thereon, and the tethering of such dog shall be accomplished in a manner that will prevent such dog from being able to reach the property of any adjoining owner or any public street, public way or right-of-way;
(3)
Accompanied by the owner of such dog while restrained on a leash at all times from which such dog cannot escape when such dog is not confined in the manner required by either subsection (1) or (2) of this section of this article; or
(4)
Confined within an area controlled by an electric fence.
(5)
Within his immediate voice control; provided, however, that this subsection shall apply only if such dog has fully completed an obedience training school and documentary evidence from such school of such training has, prior to the alleged offense, been filed with the city clerk. Whether or not a dog thus trained is, at the time of the alleged offense, actually within the immediate voice control of the owner shall be a question of fact to be determined by the trier of fact in the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-29. - Duty of owners of cats and dogs to prevent nuisance.
The owner of any dog or cat within the city shall keep, maintain and control the same in such fashion as to prevent such animal, or the keeping thereof in the city, from becoming a nuisance either from odor, noise or otherwise. The failure to so do prior to any notice to abate the same shall constitute a violation of this article. Such owner shall be proceeded against as is provided in this article. If any such person continues a nuisance after having been cited and found to have created, or to be maintaining, a nuisance such person shall be proceeded against for contempt of court. If such nuisance is a nuisance which tends to annoy the community, injure the health of the citizens in general, or corrupt the public morals as is referred to in O.C.G.A. § 41-1-6 such person shall be proceeded against under O.C.G.A. § 41-1-6; if such nuisance is not one that tends to annoy the community, injure the health of the citizens in general, or corrupt the public morals, then the continuance thereof shall constitute a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-30. - Citations for violations; written complaints.
Citations for violations of this article may issue upon the written complaint, under oath, of any resident of the city or upon information submitted by any law enforcement officer of the city, as well as by the animal control officer. The written complaint of any resident under this article shall contain a description of the animal complained against, the name and address of the owner if known, as well as the person keeping the animal, if other than the owner, together with a complete description of the facts which constitute the alleged violation including the date, time and place thereof, together with the names and addresses of all other known witnesses.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-31. - Impoundment of dogs or cats found in violation; notification of owner; claiming impounded animal; disposition of animals unclaimed.
Any dog or cat found in the city, the condition or location of which animal fails to comply strictly with the terms and provisions of this chapter applicable to such animal, shall be impounded by the animal control officer.
(1)
Upon impounding any animal, the animal control officer or his authorized representative shall deliver the animal to the county animal shelter, and cause to be made a prompt and reasonable effort to locate the animal's owner, and provide to such owner notice of the impoundment.
(2)
Upon delivery of the animal to the county animal control shelter all proceedings thereafter relative to such animal, such as further notices, reclamation by the owner, adoption and destruction of the animal, shall be done by the county personnel at the shelter in accordance with the ordinances, rules and regulation of the county governing animals delivered to the shelter by the county animal control personnel.
(3)
A dangerous dog, as defined and classified in article III of this chapter, shall not be eligible for adoption by any person.
(4)
In no event shall any animal be redeemed by its lawful owner until the fees established for boarding the animal, and for inoculation, as necessary, are paid, and no animal shall be adopted until the fees established for such adoption are paid.
(5)
If for any reason as herein above described any animal is impounded, or if any owner of any animal violates any of the duties imposed by this chapter, in addition to the penalties otherwise imposed, such owner shall be conclusively presumed to have given his or her consent to the adoption of the impounded animal or to have given his or her consent to the disposal of the animal in a humane fashion in accordance with this chapter and the laws of the state, unless the animal has been claimed by its lawful owner in accordance with the terms of this chapter.
(6)
Any animal confined for suspicion of rabies, and any animal which has bitten a person or another animal, shall, notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, be kept confined for a period of time as prescribed by the county health department, but in no event shall such time be less than ten days. Disposal of an animal determined to be rabid shall be in accordance with the rules and regulations of the health department.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)

ARTICLE III. - DANGEROUS DOG CONTROL

Sec. 14-51. - Short title.
This article is enacted under the authority of the police power of the city and O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq., and this article shall be known and may be cited as the dangerous dog control code.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-52. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dangerous dog means any dog the actions and conduct of which is such that a reasonable man would conclude that if such dog were able to reach a human being it would attack, and inflict injury upon, such human being without being provoked to do so by the person attacked.
Injury means physical harm or damage to a human.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 14-53. - Duties of animal control officer in regard to dangerous dogs.
(a)
Upon receiving a report of, or upon observing, a dog that is claimed, or appears to be dangerous, the animal control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such dog as he deems necessary in order to determine whether, in his judgment, such dog meets the definition of a dangerous dog.
(b)
If, upon the completion of such investigation, the animal control officer classifies a dog as a dangerous dog, he shall give the owner a written notice of such determination, which notice shall instruct such person to remove such dog from the corporate limits of the city in not less than ten days, failing which such person will be charged with a violation of this article. If the animal control officer cannot locate the owner of the dog, he shall nail a copy of the notice to the front door of the main building on the premises where the dog is kept, if a building is located thereon, otherwise, the notice shall be posted on a sign on a stake placed by the animal control officer in the ground of the premises.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-54. - Violations of this article.
It shall be unlawful, and a violation of this article, to keep in the city any dog that has been determined by the animal control officer to be a dangerous dog after the tenth day from the date of the notice required by section 14-53. A dog that is used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties shall not be a "dangerous dog" within the meaning of this article.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-55. - Citation for violations.
In the event that a person fails to comply with the notice required by section 14-53 the animal control officer shall issue a citation charging such person with a violation of this article, and such person, in addition to any other defenses to which such person may be entitled, may contest before the municipal court the issue of whether the dog is, in fact, a dangerous dog.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
Sec. 14-56. - Confiscation of dogs; grounds; disposition.
The owner of the dog shall remove the dog from the corporate limits of the city within 24 hours of a final judicial determination that the dog is a dangerous dog. A failure to do so shall be unlawful and a violation of this article, and upon such failure, the dog shall be immediately confiscated by the animal control officer or by a law enforcement officer or by another person authorized by the animal control officer and shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(Ord. No. 987, § 1, 10-18-05)
