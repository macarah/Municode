DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-11. - Registration and occupation tax required for business dealings in the city.
For the year 1996 and succeeding years thereafter, each person with a location in the city, and each out-of-state business with no location in Georgia which exerts substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A., § 48-13-7, which is engaged in any business, trade, profession, or occupation in the city, shall register with, and shall pay an occupation tax to, the city for said business, trade, profession, or occupation. Evidence of the payment of tax, and any applicable registration, shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in Thomaston, Georgia. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in Thomaston, Georgia, such business tax registration shall be exhibited to any employee of the city upon request.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-12. - Construction of terms; definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms or phrases shall mean:
City shall mean the City of Thomaston, Georgia.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation, a form I.R.S W-2 but not a form I.R.S. 1099.
Location of office shall include any structure of vehicle where a business, profession, or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, trade, business or profession, for revenue raising purposes.
Person shall be held to include sole proprietors, corporations, partnerships, or any other form of business organization.
Practitioner(s) of profession(s) or occupation(s) is one who by state law requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation, but "practitioner of professions or occupations" shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)

An occupation tax is hereby levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices in the corporate limits of the city, and also upon those out-of-state businesses with no location or office in Georgia which out of state businesses are subject to municipal occupation tax pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, and section 22-14 hereinbelow. Sec. 22-14. - Paying occupation tax of business with no location in Georgia.
Registration of, and the assessment of an occupation tax, is hereby imposed on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the State of Georgia if the business's largest dollar volume of business in Georgia is in Thomaston and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of the city for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of the city.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-15. - Each line of business to be identified on business registration.
The business registration of each business operated in the city's jurisdiction shall identify the line or lines of business that the business conducts. No business shall conduct any line of business without first having that line of business registered with the city and that line of business being noted by the city upon the business registration form which is to be displayed by the business owner.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-16. - More that one place or line of business.
Where a person conducts business at more than one fixed location in the city, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of the occupation tax imposed by this article. Where a business has more than one location or includes more than one line, said business shall be required to register each location separately, and describe each line of business on the applicable registration.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-17. - Taxation of professionals as classified in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c).
Practitioners of professions as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c), as the same now exist, or as it may be hereafter amended, shall elect as their entire occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax based on number of employees as hereinabove levied; or
(2)
A fee of $400.00 per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the practitioner's office or location; provided, however, that a practitioner paying according to this paragraph shall not be required to provide information to the local government relating to the gross receipts of the business or practitioner. The per-practitioner fee applies to each person in the business who qualifies as a practitioner under the state's regulatory guidelines and framework.
(3)
An election, once made by a practitioner, shall continue until changed by the practitioner.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-18. - Practitioners and entities exempt from this article.
(a)
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by, and who is employed in practice exclusively by, the United States, the state, a municipality or county of the state, instrumentalities of the United States, the state, or a municipality or county of the state shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that practice.
(b)
All state and local authorities and nonprofit organizations are exempt from any occupation tax, regulatory fee, or administrative fee imposed by this article.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-19. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses, trades, occupations or professions which are covered by the provisions of Article 1, Chapter 13 of Title 48 of the Official Code of Georgia. All other businesses and occupations taxed by the city are taxed pursuant to the provisions of other general and local laws and ordinances.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-20. - When tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
The occupation tax levied by this article shall be for the calendar year 1996 and succeeding calendar years unless otherwise specifically provided. Each business shall register and pay the occupation tax hereby levied on January 1 of each year, or on the date of commencing to do business as to businesses begun during the year, and shall be delinquent 30 days thereafter. If not paid within 90 days of its due date, a penalty equal to ten percent of the tax shall be due, and is hereby levied.
(b)
Delinquent occupation taxes shall bear interest at the rate of 1.5 percent per month, which shall be paid at the time of registration.
(c)
Each person or business subject to the requirements of this article beginning business after January 1 in any year shall register and pay teh tax required by this article prior to beginning business, and shall be subject to the penalties and interest hereby imposed.
(d)
The city clerk shall issue written evidence to each registrant upon registration and payment of the appropriate tax.
(e)
If any person, firm, or corporation whose duty it is to register and pay a tax shall, after said registration or occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in the city, any of the professions, trades, occupations or professions subject to this article without having first registered and paid the tax required by this article such offender shall, upon conviction, be punished by a fine not to exceed $500.00, or imprisonment not to exceed 180 days, either or both, in the discretion of the court.
(f)
In addition to the above remedies, the marshal may proceed to collect the same in the manner provided by law for tax executions.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-21. - Evidence of state registration required if applicable; state registration to be displayed.
(a)
Each person who is licensed by the Secretary of State pursuant to Title 43 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated shall provide evidence of proper and current state licensure before the city registration may be issued.
(b)
Each person who is licensed by the state shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the business and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-22. - Evidence of qualification required if applicable.
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency, or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of evidence of city business registration, show evidence that such requirements have been met.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-23. - Liability of officers and agents; registration required; failure to obtain.
All persons subject to the occupation tax levied by this article shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for said business as described in this article, and in default thereof, the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a registration.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-24. - Effective term of registration; collection procedures as to penalties and interest.
(a)
Each such registration shall be for the calendar year in which the registration was obtained unless otherwise specifically provided. Any person transacting or offering to transact in the city any business, trade, occupation or profession without first having registered and paid the tax required by this article shall be subject to the penalties and interest provided in this article. Said penalties and interest may be collected in the manner herein provided for collection of the occupation tax, and shall have the same lien and priority as the principal portion of the occupation tax.
(b)
The evidence of registration herein provided for shall be issued by the city, and if any person, firm, or corporation whose duty it is to register and pay an occupation tax and obtain a registration shall, after said occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in the city, any business, trade, occupation or profession subject to this article without having first obtained said registration, such offender shall be subject to the criminal and financial penalties provided therefor in this article.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-25. - Penalty for violation.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, be fined in an amount not exceeding $500.00 or imprisoned not exceeding 180 days, either or both, in the discretion of the court.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-26. - Business not subject to this article.
The following businesses and entities are not subject to the provisions of this article but may be assessed an occupation tax or other type of tax pursuant to the provisions of other applicable general or local laws:
(1)
Those businesses regulated by the Georgia Public Service Commission.
(2)
Those electrical service businesses organized under Article 3 of Title 46 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
(3)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including agribusiness.
(4)
Cooperative marketing associations governed by O.C.G.A. § 20-10-105.
(5)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8, et seq.
(6)
Motor common carriers governed by O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15.
(7)
Those businesses governed by O.C.G.A. §48-5-355 (businesses that purchase carload lots of guano, meats, meal, flour, bran, cottonseed, or cottonseed meal and hulls).
(8)
Agricultural products and livestock raised in the State of Georgia governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356.
(9)
Depository financial institutions governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(10)
Facilities operated by a charitable trust governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-55.
(11)
State and local authorities and nonprofit organizations.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-27. - Occupation tax inapplicable where prohibited by law or provided for pursuant to other existing law.
An occupation tax shall not apply to any part of a business where such levy is prohibited or exempted by the laws of Georgia or of the United States.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-28. - When occupation tax determined, due and payable.
(a)
The amount of occupation tax of businesses existing on the first day of the calendar year shall be determined as of that date, and the tax due shall be due and payable to the city, at the office of the city clerk, on January 1 each year.
(b)
Each person or business subject to the requirements of this article beginning business after January 1 in any year shall register and pay the tax required by this article prior to beginning business.
(c)
The amount of the occupation tax applicable to a business not in existence on January 1 shall be determined by the number of persons the owner plans to have employed on the date the business first commences full operation.
(d)
When any business is begun after July 1 in any year, the business or occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax imposed for the entire year.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-29. - Returns confidential.
Except in the case of judicial proceedings or other proceedings necessary to collect the occupation tax hereby levied, it shall be unlawful for any officer, employee, agent, or clerk of the city, or any other person to divulge or make known in any manner any information provided to a local government by a business or practitioner of any occupation or profession for the purpose of determining the amount of occupation tax for the business or practitioner. All contents of said return shall be confidential and open only to the officials, employees, agents, attorneys, or clerks of the city using said returns for the purpose of this occupation tax levy and the collection of the tax. Independent auditors or bookkeepers employed by the city shall be classed as "employees." Nothing herein shall be construed to prohibit the publication by city officials of statistics, so classified as to prevent the identification of particular reports or returns and items thereof; or the inspection of the records by duly qualified employees of the tax departments of the State of Georgia, the United States, and other local governments.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-30. - Inspections of books and records.
The city clerk and the employees in his office shall have the right to inspect the books or records of any business for which a registration is required under this article, and upon demand of the city clerk such books or records shall be submitted for inspection by a representative of the city within 30 days. Failure of submission of such books or records within 30 days shall be grounds for revocation of the tax registration of such business or profession currently existing to do business in the city. Adequate records shall be kept by each business in the city for examination by the city clerk at that officer's discretion. If, after examination of the books or records of any business, it is determined that an insufficient amount of tax has been paid as a result of inaccurate reporting, a penalty of ten (10) percent of the unpaid tax plus interest thereon at 1.5 percent per month from the date it became delinquent, plus the deficiency, will be assessed for the period delinquent.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-31. - Tax registration to be revoked for violations.
Upon the failure of any business to pay said occupation tax or any part thereof before it becomes delinquent, or upon failure to make any of said returns within the time required herein or upon failure to make a true return, or upon failure to amend a return to set forth the truth, or upon failure to permit inspection of its books as above provided, any business tax registration granted by the city under this article permitting the owner of said business to do business for the current year shall be, ipso facto, revoked. No new business tax registration shall be granted by the city for the operation of a business for which any part of the occupation tax herein provided for is at that time unpaid, or to an individual, firm, or corporation who has failed to submit adequate records as required by the city clerk in accordance with the provisions of this article. In the case of those practitioners where the local government cannot suspend the right of the practitioner to conduct business, the imposition of civil and criminal penalties provided in this article shall be pursued by the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-32. - Effect of failure to comply with the provisions of this article; continuing in business after tax registration revocation.
Any person, who, on their own behalf, or as agent for another either:
(1)
Does business in the city after the registration for said business has been revoked as above provided; or
(2)
Who fails to make said returns within the time and in the manner herein provided; or
(3)
Who refuses to amend such returns so as to set forth the truth; or
(4)
Who shall make a false return; or
(5)
Who refuses to permit an inspection of the books and records in their charge when the officers, agents, employees, or representatives of the city request such inspection during business hours, for the purpose of determining the accuracy of the returns herein provided for,
shall be guilty of violating this article, and, upon conviction, subject to the penalties provided herein.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-33. - Lien taken for delinquent occupation tax.
In addition to the other remedies herein provided for the collection of the occupation tax herein levied, the city clerk, upon any tax or portion thereof becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid for 90 days, shall issue execution for the correct amount of said tax, plus any penalties and interest applicable thereto, against the persons, partnership, or corporations liable for said tax, and the lien shall cover the property of the person, partnership, or corporation liable for said tax, all as provided by this Code, the Charter of the City and the laws of Georgia. The lien of said occupation tax shall become fixed on the date and from the time when such tax or any installment thereof becomes delinquent. The execution shall be levied by the marshal or chief of police of the city or such other officer as is by law authorized to so do, upon the property of the tax payer, and sufficient property shall be advertised and sold to pay the amount of said execution, with interest, penalties and costs. All other proceedings in relation thereto shall be had as is provided by the applicable provisions of law, and the defendant in said execution shall have rights of defense, by affidavit of illegality and otherwise, which are provided by the applicable laws in regard to tax executions. When a nulla bona entry has been entered by proper authority upon an execution issued by the city clerk against any person defaulting on the occupation tax, the person against whom the entry was made shall not be allowed or entitled to have or collect any fees or charges whatsoever for services rendered after the entry of the nulla bona. If, at any time after the entry of nulla bona has been made, the person against whom the execution issues pays the tax in full together with all interest and costs accrued on the tax, the person may collect any fees and charges due as though such person had never defaulted in the payment of the taxes.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-34. - Continuation of liability for taxes due under prior provisions of this Code.
Notwithstanding anything herein to the contrary, any taxes due the city under any prior ordinances or provisions of this Code shall continue to be a liability of the tax payer to the city and shall be collectible in accordance with, and in the manner provided in, this article.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-35. - Enforcement of provisions.
It is hereby made the duty of the city clerk, the chief of police and the members of the police force of the city to see that the provisions of this article relating to occupation taxes are observed; and to summon all violators of the same to appear before the court. It is hereby made the further duty of the city clerk, to inspect all registrations issued by the city, as often as in his judgment it may seem necessary to determine whether the registration held is the proper one for the business sought to be transacted thereunder.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-36. - Requirement of public hearings.
The mayor and council shall hold at least one public hearing:
(1)
Prior to the adoption of any ordinance or resolution regarding the occupation tax; and
(2)
As a part of the process of determining how to use the additional revenue in any year when revenue from occupation taxes is greater than revenue from occupation taxes for the preceding year.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-37. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention hereof that the specific shall control.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)
Sec. 22-38. - Severability.
If any section, provision, or clause, or any part of this article shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, or if the provisions of any part of this article as applied to any particular situation or set of circumstances shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional such invalidity shall not be construed to affect the portions of this article not so held to be invalid, or the application of this article to other circumstances not so held to be invalid. It is hereby declared as the intent of the council that this article would have been adopted had such invalid portion not been included herein.
(Ord. No. 849, § 1, 12-5-95)

Sec. 22-39. - Definitions.
Whenever any of the following words, or terms are used in this article, they shall have the following meaning:
City shall mean the City of Thomaston.
Lodgings shall mean any room or rooms, lodgings, or accommodations furnished by any person or legal entity licensed by, or required to pay business or occupation taxes to, the city for operation a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin, or any other place in which rooms, lodgings, or accommodations are regularly furnished for value for use by the public. The term "lodgings" does not include an accommodation which a person occupies, or has the right to occupy, as his domicile and permanent residence.
Operator shall mean each person or legal entity furnishing lodgings.
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96)
Sec. 22-40. - Tax imposed and exemptions.
(a)
There is hereby imposed an excise tax upon each operator, which tax shall be at the rate of five percent of the charge to the public for the furnishing of lodgings within the city, and shall be due from, and remitted by, each operator regardless of whether the same is collected from the lodging public.
(b)
The tax hereby levied shall not be imposed upon any lodgings exempted from taxation by the provisions of Title 48, Chapter 13, Article 3 of the Official Code of Georgia.
(c)
Each operator shall be allowed to retain three percent of the amount due as an allowance for reporting and paying the tax by its due date; no allowance shall be retained on any taxes that are delinquent.
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96)
Sec. 22-41. - Collection.
The tax herein levied may, at the option of the operator, be charged to the person using the lodgings, and added to the other charges made therefor, and if the same is charged to the user of the lodgings, each such person shall be given a bill, invoice, receipt of other statement or memorandum of the price, charge or rent payable upon which the lodgings tax shall be stated, charged and shown separately. When said tax is collected from the person using the lodgings, the operator shall hold it as trustee for and on behalf of the city until it is paid to the city as required by this article.
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96)
Sec. 22-42. - Forms to be filed, payment of, and penalty for nonpayment.
(a)
Every operator shall file with the city a sworn tax return not later than the fifteenth day of each month, on a form so prescribed by the city, which return shall set forth the gross receipts from lodgings during the immediately preceding month, the amount thereof claimed to be exempt from the tax levied by this article, and the amount of tax due the city, the amount of allowance claimed by the operator for reporting and paying the tax, and the net tax due. Each operator shall pay to the city, on the date such return is due, the amount of tax then due under this article.
(b)
There is hereby levied a penalty, at the rate of two percent per month, or fraction thereof, on any portion of said tax that is not paid when due, and, in addition, the allowance to the operator for collecting the tax shall be forfeited as is provided in the O.C.G.A. § 48-13-52.
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96)
Sec. 22-43. - Records required.
Every operator shall keep books and records showing the gross receipts from lodgings taxable under this article, including full information demonstrating any exemptions claimed on the returns made to the city. The city clerk and the employees in his office shall have the right to inspect the books and records of any operator, and upon demand of the city clerk such books or records, specifically including a copy of the sales tax return filed by the operator with the State of Georgia, shall be submitted for inspection by a representative of the city within 30 days. Failure of submission of such books or records within 30 days shall constitute a violation of this article, shall be grounds for revocation of the tax registration and license of such business to do business in the city, and shall authorize the city to institute legal actions against the operator for the discovery of the amount, and the recovery of, the tax due under this article. Records shall be kept by each operator for examination by the city clerk, at that officer's discretion, to determine the amount of tax due hereunder.
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96)
Sec. 22-44. - Procedures in the event of failure to pay tax.
In the event that any operator fails to pay the tax, or the penalties, levied by this article, the city clerk shall give written notice, by certified mail with return receipt, to the operator of such failure, which notice shall set forth the time periods for which the taxes levied by this article have not been paid, and shall inform the operator of a time at which the operator shall appear before the city clerk to respond to such notice. In the event that the issue is not thereby resolved by the payment of any taxes and penalties determined to be due, the city clerk shall refer the matter to judge of the city court for a trial of the disputed issues. The court shall issue a summons directed to the operator giving the operator notice of the contentions of the city, and directing the operator to be and appear before the court at a time certain, to answer such contentions, failure to respond to which shall be punished as a contempt of court, and the court may proceed, in the absence of the operator, to a trial of the issues. In the event that the judge finds, and enters a judgment, that the operator has failed to pay the taxes and penalties due under this article, the court may suspend, or revoke the license issued to such operator to conduct a lodgings business in the city, and the court shall issue execution for the correct amount of said tax, plus any penalties applicable thereto, against the persons, partnership, or corporations liable for said tax, and the lien shall cover the property of the person, partnership, or corporation liable for said tax, all as provided by this Code, the Charter of the city and the laws of Georgia. The lien of said lodgings tax shall become fixed on the date and from the time when such tax or any installment thereof becomes delinquent. The execution shall be levied by the marshall or chief of police of the city or such other officer as is by law authorized to so do, upon the property of the tax payer, and sufficient property shall be advertised and sold to pay the amount of said execution, with interest, penalties, and costs. All other proceeding in relation thereto shall be had as is provided by the applicable provisions of law, and the defendant in said execution shall have rights of defense, by affidavit of illegality and otherwise, which are provided by the applicable laws in regard to tax executions.
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96)
Sec. 22-45. - Proceeds.
The proceeds resulting from the imposition of the tax under this article, including penalties, shall be expended in accordance with the applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3).
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96; Ord. No. 1012, § 1, 11-20-07)
Sec. 22-45.1. - Penalties.
Any operator, or agent of an operator, who fails to file the return of taxes due hereunder, or who knowingly files a false return, or who collects any tax due hereunder from its patrons and fails to pay the same to the city, shall, upon conviction, be punished in accordance with the provisions of law applicable to the city court.
(Ord. No. 854, § 1, 4-16-96)

Sec. 22-46. - Persons acting as security on six or more bail bonds.
There is hereby created a rebuttable presumption that any person who acts as security on six separate and distinct criminal bail bonds posted with the city in connection with violations or alleged violation of this Code or the ordinances of the city during any one calendar year is engaged in the business of that of a bail bondsman and such person shall obtain from the city the license required therefor by the occupation license and tax ordinance of the city and shall pay the fee therefor as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Code 1965, § 17-14)
Sec. 22-47. - Officials not to accept persons as security without license.
No official or officer of the city shall accept any person as security on more than five criminal bail bonds in any one calendar year unless such person shall then have in effect at such time a valid business license from the city to engage in the business of a bail bondsman.
(Code 1965, § 17-15)

Sec. 22-71. - License fee; license fee for additional locations, generally.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges upon every insurance company engaging in the business of insurance within the corporate limits of the city, and there is hereby levied an additional annual license fee in such amount for each separate business location in excess of one operated and maintained by any such company within the corporate limits.

There is hereby levied an additional annual license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on each insurance company for each separate business location not otherwise subject to a license fee under section 22-71, which company is operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and which, in connection with the loans or sales involving term financing, offers, solicits, or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of the insurance company for insurance.

Sec. 22-73. - License fee for additional locations of independent agencies and brokers.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which a life insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by either section 22-71 or section 22-72, in the amount set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for each such location within the city.
(Code 1965, § 15D-3)
Sec. 22-74. - Gross direct premiums tax.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the city in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The expression "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by sections 22-71 and 22-72.
(Code 1965, § 15D-4)
Sec. 22-75. - Gross direct premiums tax on classes of insurance other than life, accident, sickness.
There is hereby levied an annual tax upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1) doing business as provided in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2 in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The expression "gross direct premiums" as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a).
(Code 1965, § 15D-5)
Sec. 22-76. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 22-71, 22-72, and 22-73 shall be due and payable January 1 of each year.
(Code 1965, § 15D-6)

State Law reference— Georgia Charitable Solicitations Act of 1988, O.C.G.A. § 43-17-1 et seq.; peddlers and itinerant traders, O.C.G.A. § 43-32-1 et seq.; Transient Merchant Act of Georgia, O.C.G.A. § 43-46-1 et seq.

Sec. 22-96. - Requirements.
It shall be unlawful for any person who is domiciled outside of the county directly or indirectly to solicit property or financial assistance of any kind, to sell or offer to sell any article, tag, service emblem, publication, ticket, advertisement, subscription, or anything of value, on the plea or representation that such sale or solicitation is being made for or on behalf of a religious purpose in any place in the city, other than in a regular meeting of a religious organization, unless such person shall have first established, in the manner set forth in this section, his identity and his authority to act for the cause which he purports to represent. Every such person shall, prior to endeavoring to solicit or sell for a religious purpose, furnish, in writing, his full name, age, address and social security number, and shall, simultaneously therewith, tender an identifying document, such as a driver's license, birth certificate, selective service registration card, or any other identifying document issued by the United States government or by any state, and such person shall also furnish therewith a statement, bearing an issue date not more than six months prior thereto, from the organization on behalf of which such solicitor purports to act stating that such solicitor is authorized to solicit funds on behalf of such organization. Such information shall be furnished to the city clerk or, in his absence, to his designee. Upon compliance herewith, the city clerk shall return to the solicitor a photocopy of the writing containing the name, age, address and social security number of such solicitor upon which photocopy the city clerk shall place the words, "identification requirements complied with," shall date the same and shall sign his name thereto. Identification of any such person shall lapse six months from the date shown on the statement of authority from the religious organization required to be submitted in this section, and it shall thereafter be unlawful for any person who continues to solicit funds, or offers to sell items, for a religious purpose without furnishing to the city a new statement, bearing a date not more than six months prior thereto, from the organization on whose behalf he purports to act stating that his authority to solicit or sell on its behalf remains in effect.
(Code 1965, § 8-10)

Sec. 22-116. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Gross receipts has the same meaning as is ascribed to the term "gross receipts" for each such depository financial institution in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(Ord. No. 689, § 2, 12-20-83)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-117. - Tax levied.
(a)
Pursuant to the authority contained in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93 there is levied for each year, upon each state and national bank, each federal savings and loan association, and each state building and loan association located in the city, not exempted by state law, an annual business license tax at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of each such institution, or $1,000.00, per anum, whichever amount is greater.
(b)
The taxes levied by subsection (a) of this section for each year shall be levied on the amount of gross receipts received by each such depository financial institution during the immediately preceding calendar year.
(c)
Each depository financial institution subject to the tax levied by subsection (a) of this section above shall file with the city not later than March 1 of each year a return of its gross receipts received by it during the immediately preceding calendar year. The returns shall be made in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this article shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in the return.
(d)
The taxes levied by this article shall be due and payable on April 1 of each year.
(Ord. No. 689, §§ 1, 3—5, 12-20-83)
