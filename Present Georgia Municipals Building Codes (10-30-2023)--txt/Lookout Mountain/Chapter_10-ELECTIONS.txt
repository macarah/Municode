Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
Whenever the term "election code" appears herein, it shall refer to the Georgia Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.), and all words and phrases used herein shall have the same meaning as set forth in said election code, unless otherwise clearly apparent from the context hereof.
(Ord. No. 18, § 1, 10-16-1969)
Sec. 10-2. - Election officials.
modified
(a)
At a regular or properly noticed special meeting of the council, the council shall appoint, by resolution, a municipal superintendent who shall perform the duties specified by the election code. The municipal superintendent shall be a judicious, intelligent and upright citizen of the United States and shall be impartial and unbiased. The municipal superintendent shall appoint poll officers, in accordance with the election code and in consultation with the mayor, to conduct elections and primaries in the city. Such poll officers shall include a chief manager, two assistant managers, and as many clerks as are necessary to aid and assist in the conduct of the elections and primaries. Such poll officers shall have the qualifications specified by the election code and, before entering upon their duties, shall take an oath of office as required by the election code which shall be substantially as follows:
"I do solemnly swear that I will faithfully and impartially discharge, to the best of my ability, the duties imposed upon me by law as _____ of the City of Lookout Mountain, Georgia."
(b)
Said oath shall be filed with the city clerk and entered upon the minutes book. All such officials shall serve at a rate of compensation, if any, to be set by the council by resolution.
(Ord. No. 18, § 3, 10-16-1969; Ord. No. 324 , § 1, 11-14-2019)
Sec. 10-3. - Single election district; no precincts.
The entire city shall constitute a single election district, and there shall be no precincts within the city.
(Ord. No. 18, § 4, 10-16-1969)
Sec. 10-4. - Polling place.
The polling place within the election district shall be City Hall, located at 1214 Lula Lake Road, Lookout Mountain, Georgia, or, in the event of the unavailability or unusability thereof, the polling place shall be the Lookout Mountain United Methodist Church.
(Ord. No. 18, § 5, 10-16-1969; Ord. No. 164, § 1, 2-17-1994)
Sec. 10-5. - Closing date for filing notice of candidacy.
The closing date for filing notice of candidacy for any candidate seeking public office in the city in either a general election or a special election shall be 30 days prior to the election date.
(Ord. No. 18, § 6, 10-16-1969; Ord. No. 164, § 1, 2-17-1994)
Sec. 10-6. - Councilmember not to specify duty.
In the case of a candidate seeking the office of councilmember, there shall be no necessity for such candidate to specify the particular duty, such as police councilmember or fire councilmember, in his notice, the duties of councilmember being readily re-allocable among the various councilmembers.
(Ord. No. 18, § 7, 10-16-1969)
Sec. 10-7. - Nominating petitions.
Nominating petitions, as provided for under the city Charter, shall contain the signature of at least 40 persons who shall be upright and intelligent electors of said city, entitled to register and vote in the next ensuing election to be held in said city.
(Ord. No. 18, § 8, 10-16-1969)
Sec. 10-8. - Absentee ballot clerk.
The municipal registrar is herewith given the additional duty of serving as absentee ballot clerk.
(Ord. No. 18, § 9, 10-16-1969)
State Law reference— Absentee voting, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-380 et seq.
Sec. 10-9. - Plurality elects.
A candidate nominated by primary election may be nominated by a plurality of the votes cast to fill such nomination. A candidate may be elected for public office by a plurality of the votes cast to fill such public office, and in the case of candidates for two or more public offices with the same title (such as, but not limited to, councilmember), the candidates receiving the highest number of votes shall be elected.
(Ord. No. 18, § 10, 10-16-1969)
