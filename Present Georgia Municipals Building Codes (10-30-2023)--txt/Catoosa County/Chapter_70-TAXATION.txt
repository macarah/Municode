Chapter 70 - TAXATION[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 70-1. - Sales tax imposed.
The local option sales tax is imposed pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-8-83.
(Res. of 12-17-79)
Sec. 70-2. - Certain tax executions ineligible for transfer.
(a)
The tax executions on the following properties shall be excluded from transfer:
(1)
All tax executions less than 121 days past due.
(2)
All tax executions on property carrying an exemption granted to an individual who is over age 62.
(3)
All tax executions on property carrying an exemption granted to an individual as a veteran of the United States Armed Forces.
(4)
All tax executions on property owned, in whole or in part, by a disabled person.
(5)
All tax executions on property owned, in whole or in part, by a taxpayer who has entered into a written payment agreement with the county for the orderly payment of such taxes.
(b)
No tax execution on property shall be sold to third parties for less than full value.
(Res. of 7-2-96, § 5)
Sec. 70-3. - Waiver, assessment of penalties.
The board of commissioners delegates the authority to the tax commissioner of the county to make the final determination on a case-by-case basis following sound business guidelines, and the tax commissioner is vested with the authority to make the final decision as to whether or not to waive or assess penalties on ad valorem taxes.
(Res. of 7-18-95)
Secs. 70-4—70-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BOARD OF TAX ASSESSORS[2]

Sec. 70-36. - Creation of board; duties; personnel.
(a)
Pursuant to the authority contained in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-290, there is established in the county a board of tax assessors, which shall be known as the Catoosa County Board of Tax Assessors.
(b)
The board of tax assessors shall have the duties and responsibilities provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-299 and such other duties as may be imposed by general or special law.
(c)
The employees of the board of tax assessors shall be under the jurisdiction and control of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 12-30-96, § I)
Sec. 70-37. - Qualifications; certain relationships prohibited.
(a)
Under O.C.G.A. § 48-5-290(d), no individual shall be appointed or reappointed to a county board of tax assessors when the individual is related to a member of the board of commissioners in one of the following degrees:
(1)
Mother or mother-in-law;
(2)
Father or father-in-law;
(3)
Sister or sister-in-law;
(4)
Brother or brother-in-law;
(5)
Grandmother or grandmother by marriage;
(6)
Grandfather or grandfather by marriage;
(7)
Son or son-in-law; or
(8)
Daughter or daughter-in-law.
(b)
No individual shall serve as a member of the board of tax assessors:
(1)
Who is less than 21 years of age.
(2)
Who fails to make his residence within the county within six months after taking the oath of office as a member of the board.
(3)
Who does not hold a high school diploma or its equivalent (an individual who has held an equivalent responsible position of employment for a period of five years shall not be required to meet the high school education requirement provided in this subsection).
(4)
Who does not have at least one year's experience in appraisal-related work (the successful completion of 40 hours of approved appraisal courses provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-291(c) taken either prior to appointment or within one year after appointment, may be substituted for this experience requirement).
(5)
Who has not successfully completed an assessor examination to be administered by the commissioner or who has not successfully completed at least 40 hours of approved appraisal courses as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-291(c).
(c)
In addition, no member of the board of tax assessors shall be eligible to hold any state, county or municipal office during the time he holds office as a member of the board of tax assessors.
(Res. of 12-30-96, § II)
Sec. 70-38. - Number on board; posts; term of office; initial terms.
(a)
The board of tax assessors shall consist of five members, who shall be appointed for staggered terms to a post on the board of tax assessors.
(b)
The term of office, after the initial terms, of the members of the board of tax assessors shall be four years.
(c)
Each member of the board of tax assessors shall serve until their successors are duly appointed and take their oath of office.
(d)
A member of the board of tax assessors may be reappointed to succeed himself as a member.
(e)
The chief appraiser or a member of the county appraisal staff may serve as a member of the board of tax assessors. Any vacancy created on the board of tax assessors under this subsection shall be filled in the manner provided under O.C.G.A. § 48-5-295(a). If the member ceases to be employed as chief appraiser or a member of the county appraisal staff, the member shall cease to be a member of the board of tax assessors.
(f)
The appointment by the board of commissioners shall constitute the order of appointment and shall be regularly entered on the record of the superior court of the county in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-5-290. The clerk of the superior court shall administer the oath of office as required by law.
(Res. of 12-30-96, § III)
Sec. 70-39. - Removal from office.
Under O.C.G.A. § 48-5-295(b), a member of the board of tax assessors may be removed by the board of commissioners only for cause shown for the failure to perform the duties or meet the qualifications imposed upon the board member by law. No member of the county board of tax assessors who is also employed by the county as a staff appraiser may be removed by the board of commissioners during his term of appointment until the member has been afforded an opportunity for hearing before the judge of the superior court of the county for recommendations by the judge of the superior court to the board of commissioners regarding such removal.
(Res. of 12-30-96, § V)
Secs. 70-40—70-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - INSURERS

Sec. 70-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Insurer means a company authorized to transact business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-72. - Life insurers license fees.
There is levied an annual license fee upon each life insurer doing business within the county as provided in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 70-73, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the county, there is levied a license fee as provided in such schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk.
Sec. 70-73. - License fee for life insurers insuring certain risks at additional business location.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under this article, operated and maintained by a business organization engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales, an insurer who offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of a life insurer for life insurance shall pay an additional annual license fee as provided in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk.
Sec. 70-74. - Life insurance agency license fees; independent life insurance agencies, brokers not otherwise licensed.
There is levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which a life insurance business is conducted and is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 70-72 as provided in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk.
Sec. 70-75. - Gross premiums tax—Life insurers.
There is levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the county in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. Gross direct premiums as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 70-72.
Sec. 70-76. - All other insurers.
There is levied an annual tax upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the county in an amount equal to two and one-half percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. Gross direct premiums as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a).
(Res. of 9-26-83)
Sec. 70-77. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in this article shall be due and payable on January 1 of each year.
Sec. 70-78. - License fees, all insurers doing business in the county.
There is levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the county as provided in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk. For each separate business location in excess of one operating on behalf of such insurers within the county, there is levied a license fee as provided in such schedule of fees and charges.
Secs. 70-79—70-110. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 70-111. - Business license tax.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is levied an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations, and state building and loan associations a business license tax at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of such institutions. Gross receipts shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this article, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this article shall be $1,000.00 per year.
Sec. 70-112. - Filing of return.
Each depository financial institution within the county shall file a return of its gross receipts with the county on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts were measured. Such returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this article shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in such return.
Sec. 70-113. - Due date.
The due date of taxes levied by this article shall be April 1 of each year.

DIVISION 2. - FREEPORT EXEMPTION

Sec. 70-151. - Applicability.
The board of commissioners hereby exempts from taxation, as hereinafter provided, the following classes of tangible personal property:
(1)
An exemption for inventory of goods in the process of manufacture or production which shall include all partly finished goods and raw materials held for direct use or consumption in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business in the state. Provided, however, that the exemption provided for with respect to this class of tangible personal property shall apply only to tangible personal property which is substantially modified, altered, or changed in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing, processing, or production operations in the state.
(2)
An exemption for inventory of finished goods manufactured or produced within the state in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business when held by the original manufacturer or producer of such finished goods. Provided, however, that such exemption shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is manufactured or produced.
(3)
An exemption for inventory of finished goods which, on January 1 of a given tax year, are stored in a warehouse, dock, or wharf, whether public or private, and which are destined for shipment outside the state and the inventory of finished goods which are shipped into the state from outside this state and which are stored for transshipment to a final destination outside this state. Provided, however, that such exemption shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is stored in this state.
(Ord. of 9-1-98, § I)
Sec. 70-152. - Implementation.
The exemption from taxation provided in section 70-151 above shall be implemented as follows:
(1)
For the tax year commencing on January 1, 1999, the exemption from taxation shall be an amount equal to 20 percent of the value of such tangible personal property described in section 70-151 above.
(2)
For the tax year commencing on January 1, 2000, the exemption from taxation shall be an amount equal to 40 percent of the value of such tangible personal property described in section 70-151 above.
(3)
For the tax year commencing on January 1, 2001, the exemption from taxation shall be an amount equal to 60 percent of the value of such tangible personal property described in section 70-151 above.
(4)
For the tax year commencing on January 1, 2002, the exemption from taxation shall be an amount equal to 100 percent of the value of such tangible personal property described in section 70-151 above.
(Ord. of 9-1-98, § II)
Sec. 70-153. - Application for tangible personal property inventory exemption.
(a)
Any person, firm, or corporation seeking an exemption from ad valorem taxation of the classes of tangible personal property provided in section 70-151 above shall file a written application and schedule of property with the board of tax assessors on forms to be furnished by the board of tax assessors. Such application shall be filed in the year in which the exemption from taxation is sought and no later than the date on which the board of tax assessors or tax commissioner close their books for the return of taxes. The application for tangible personal property inventory exemption shall provide for:
(1)
A schedule of the inventory of goods in the process of manufacture or production which shall include all partially finished goods and raw materials held for direct use or consumption in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business in the state;
(2)
A schedule of the inventory of finished goods manufactured or produced within the state in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business when held by the original manufacturer or producer of such finished goods;
(3)
A schedule of the inventory of finished goods which on January 1 are stored in a warehouse, dock, or wharf, whether public or private, and which are destined for shipment outside the state and the inventory or finished goods which are shipped into the state from outside this state and which are stored for transshipment to a final destination outside this state. The information required by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2 to be contained in the official books and records of the warehouse, dock, or wharf where such property is being stored, which official books and records are required to be open to the inspection of the taxing authorities of this state and the county, shall not be required to be included as part of or to accompany the application for such exemption.
(b)
For purposes of this section the term "file properly" shall mean and include the timely filing of the application and complete schedule of the inventory for which the exemption is sought on or before the date the board of tax assessors closes its books for the return of taxes.
(c)
The failure to properly file the application and schedule shall constitute a waiver of the exemption on the part of the person, firm, or corporation failing to make the application for such exemption for that year as follows:
(1)
The failure to report any inventory for which such exemption is sought and the schedule provided for in the application shall constitute a waiver of the exemption on the part of the person, firm, or corporation failing to so report for that taxable year in an amount equal to the difference between the fair market value of the inventory as reported and the fair market value finally determined to be applicable to the inventory for which the exemption is sought.
(2)
The failure to file timely such application and schedule shall constitute a waiver of the exemption until the first day of the month following the month such application and schedule are filed properly with the tax assessor; provided, however, that unless the application and schedule are filed on or before June 1, of such year, the exemption shall be waived for that entire year.
(d)
Upon receiving the application and schedule, the board of tax assessors shall determine the eligibility of all types of tangible personal property listed on the application. If any property has been listed which the board of tax assessors believes is not eligible for the exemption, the board of tax assessors shall issue a letter notifying the applicant that all or a portion of the application has been denied. The denial letter shall list the type and total fair market value of all property listed on the application for which the exemption has been approved and the type and total fair market value of all property listed on the application for which the exemption has been denied. The applicant shall have the right to appeal from the denial of the exemption for any property listed and such appeal shall proceed as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-311.
(Ord. of 9-1-98, § III)

ARTICLE VI. - OCCUPATION TAX

Sec. 70-171. - Purpose of ordinance and findings.
(1)
Purposes. The purposes of this article are to:
(a)
Protect the public health, safety and well being of the citizens of the county;
(b)
Collect and maintain information concerning the number, types and locations of businesses, professions, practitioners and occupations operating within the unincorporated areas of the county;
(c)
Classify and categorize businesses, professions, practitioners and occupations operating within the unincorporated areas of the county according to the number of employees employed by each;
(d)
Impose and collect an occupation tax on each business, profession, practitioner and occupation operating within the unincorporated areas of the county as authorized by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-6 et seq.;
(e)
Impose and collect a regulatory fee on certain businesses, professions, practitioners and occupations operating within the unincorporated areas of the county as authorized by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-8 et seq.; and
(f)
Provide for uniform rules, regulations, methods and exemptions for the collection and administration of the occupation tax and regulatory fees authorized hereunder.
(2)
Findings. In connection with the adoption of this article, it is hereby ascertained, determined and declared by the board that:
(a)
To ensure that the health, safety and welfare of the citizens of the county are protected, information must be collected and maintained concerning the number, types and locations of businesses, professions, practitioners and occupations operating within the unincorporated areas of the county;
(b)
Uniform rules and regulations concerning the imposition, collection and administration of occupation taxes and regulatory fees are needed to carry out the purposes of this article; and
(c)
The board has determined that categorizing businesses, professions, practitioners and occupations operating within the unincorporated areas of the county based upon number of employees and collection and imposition [of] an occupation tax and/or regulatory fee based upon said categorization is the most efficient, economic and beneficial means to carry out the purposes of this article.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § I, 7-7-09)
Sec. 70-172. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
Board means the board of commissioners of Catoosa County, Georgia, or the governing authority of Catoosa County, Georgia, if other than a board of commissioners.
Business includes, but is not necessarily limited to, an individual, practitioner, partnership, corporation or other entity engaged in an occupation, profession or other venture within the unincorporated areas of the county for the purpose of generating revenue.
County means the County of Catoosa, a political subdivision of the State of Georgia.
Employee shall have the definition and meaning described in the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5(1.1), which said provisions are expressly incorporated by reference herein.
Location or office shall include any structure or vehicle where a business, profession, or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office. The renter's or lessee's location which is the site of personal property which is rented or leased from another does not constitute a location or office for the personal property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor. The site of real property which is rented or leased to another does not constitute a location or office for the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor unless the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor, in addition to showing the property to prospective lessees or tenants and performing maintenance or repair of the property, otherwise conducts the business of renting or leasing the real property at such site or otherwise conducts any other business, profession, or occupation at such site.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on businesses, professions, practitioners, persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession, or business for revenue raising purposes within the unincorporated areas of the county.
Practitioners of professions and occupations shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
Regulatory fees means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, which are required by the county as an exercise of its police power and as a part of or as an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession, or business. The amount of a regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the county. Any regulatory fee imposed by the county shall not include an administrative fee or registration fee. Any regulatory fees imposed by the county shall not be considered as or include development impact fees as defined by paragraph (8) of O.C.G.A. § 36-71-2 or other costs or conditions of zoning or land development.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § II, 7-7-09)

The categories of each business, profession, practitioner and occupation by number of employees are more particularly set forth on Table 1.1 which is attached hereto and made a part hereof.

Sec. 70-174. - Occupation tax provisions.
(1)
Businesses located within the county. Each and every business, practitioner, profession or occupation with a location or office lying solely within the unincorporated areas of the county shall be subject to and shall pay an occupation tax according to the number of full time or full time equivalent employees employed by the business, practitioner, profession or occupation. In order to establish the number of full time or full time equivalent employees, the county shall utilize the criteria set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(a). The amount of the occupation tax imposed on each category of business, practitioner, profession or occupation shall be the amount described and shown on Table 1.1 which is attached hereto and made a part hereof.
(2)
Businesses located outside the county. Pursuant to the authority granted by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, each and every business, practitioner, profession or occupation which has no location or office within the boundaries of the county or the State of Georgia shall pay an occupation tax hereunder provided the business, practitioner, profession or occupation:
(a)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the unincorporated areas of the county for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(b)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located in the unincorporated areas of the county.
The occupation tax imposed upon businesses, practitioners, professions or occupations pursuant to this section shall be based upon the number of employees and shall be in the amount described and shown on Table 1.1 which is attached hereto and made a part hereof.
(3)
Purpose and scope of occupational tax. The occupation tax imposed hereunder shall be applicable only to those businesses, practitioners, professions and occupations described and authorized by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5 through O.C.G.A. § 48-13-26. The occupation tax is imposed by the county for revenue purposes and not for regulatory purposes. The payment of the occupation tax imposed hereunder shall not be deemed as a condition precedent to the payment of any particular trade, profession or occupation.
(4)
Registration requirements.
(a)
Each and every business, profession, practitioner or occupation subject to payment of the occupation tax hereunder shall be required to obtain an annual registration and/or license from the county evidencing payment of the required tax. The registration shall be issued by the county manager or such other office of the county as may be designated by the board from time to time.
(b)
Prior to obtaining the required annual registration and paying the occupation tax, each business, profession, practitioner or occupation shall complete an application identifying the primary or dominant line of business being conducted and the number of employees employed by the business, profession, practitioner or occupation. If a business, profession, practitioner or occupation includes more than one line of business or is operated at more than one location or office, the number of employees employed at each location or office shall be entered on a separate application and the number of employees employed in each different line of business identified shall be disclosed and provided on the application.
(c)
Only employees of a business, profession, practitioner or occupation who actually work within the boundaries of the county shall be counted for occupation tax purposes. Upon request by the county, a business, profession, practitioner or occupation with a location or office situated in more than one jurisdiction shall provide information concerning the names and numbers of employees who work within and outside the county.
(d)
The required license/registration shall be displayed in a conspicuous place if the business, profession, practitioner or occupation has a permanent location or office within the county. If the business, profession, practitioner or occupation does not have a permanent location or office within the county, said registration shall be displayed to the county manager or any county codes enforcement officer upon request.
(5)
Administrative fee. The occupation tax collected hereunder includes an administrative fee in order to offset the cost and expenses incurred by the county in administering this article and handling and processing the occupation tax collected hereunder.
(6)
Collection and payment of occupation tax.
(a)
The occupation tax due hereunder (including the necessary application for said tax) shall be paid each calendar year. The application shall be provided and occupation tax paid on an annual basis on January 1 of each year and shall be delinquent if not paid by March 1 of each year.
(b)
Any new business, profession, practitioner or occupation commencing business in the county during a calendar year shall file the application and pay the full amount of the occupation tax within ten days of beginning to transact business within the county. Provided, however, if a business, profession, practitioner or occupation commences business in the county after July 1 of any given year, the amount of occupation tax due shall be 50 percent of the actual occupation tax amount for the calendar year in which business was commenced. However, any business, profession, practitioner or occupation commencing business after July 1 shall not receive a reduction in the administrative fee and any profession or practitioner electing to pay the occupation tax described in subsection (7) hereunder shall not receive a reduction in said tax if business is commenced after July 1 of any given calendar year.
(7)
Occupation tax for certain professions. All practitioners of those professions enumerated in the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1)—(18) shall make an election to pay the required occupation tax using either of the following criteria:
(a)
Based upon the number of full time and/or full time employees employed in the business, profession, practitioner or occupation as more particularly set forth on Table 1.1 which is attached hereto and made a part hereof; or
(b)
Based upon a fee/tax imposed per practitioner licensed to perform or provide the service provided by the business or profession. Said fee/tax shall be paid at the practitioner's location or office and said per-practitioner fee/tax applies to each person in the business or profession who qualifies as a practitioner under applicable state regulatory guidelines and laws. A practitioner electing to pay the fee/tax under this subsection shall not be required to furnish the county with information or documents concerning the gross receipts of the business or profession. The fee/tax paid pursuant to this subsection is set forth on Table 1.2 which is attached hereto and made a part hereof.
The election required hereunder shall be made on an annual basis and must be made on or before January 1 of each calendar year. This subsection shall not apply to practitioners whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the government of the United States, State of Georgia, or any county or any municipality of the State of Georgia. Said individuals shall not be required to pay the occupational tax or file any applications required hereunder.
(8)
Multiple locations within the county. When a business, profession, practitioner or occupation conducts and/or transacts business at more than one fixed location or office within the unincorporated areas of the county, each location or office shall be considered a separate and distinct business, profession, practitioner or occupation for the purposes of payment of the occupation tax required hereunder.
(9)
State registration and licensure.
(a)
Any business, profession, practitioner or occupation which is required to be licensed by the Georgia Secretary of State pursuant to the provisions of Title 43 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated shall provide the county with evidence of the required state licensure prior to commencing business within the county and prior to paying the occupation tax required hereunder and being issued any registration by the county.
(b)
Any business, profession, practitioner or occupation required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency or other regulatory permits, registrations qualifications or licenses shall provide the county with evidence that all qualifications are current and met prior to commencing business within the county and prior to paying the occupation tax required hereunder and being issued any registration by the county.
(c)
Each business, profession, practitioner or occupation required to maintain any license, permit, bond, certificate or registration referenced in this section shall conspicuously post the same at the location or office of the business, profession, practitioner or occupation and it shall continue to be posted as long as the license, permit, bond, certificate or registration remains valid.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § IV, 7-7-09)
Editor's note— The above-referenced Table 1.1 can be found in section 70-180 of this Code.

(2)
Purpose and scope of regulatory fee. The regulatory fee collected pursuant to this section is collected from businesses, professions, practitioners and occupations for which the county customarily performs investigation or inspection in order to protect the public health, safety, or welfare of the citizens of the county or in the course of enforcing a state or local building, health, or safety code. The regulatory fee is not collected or used as a means of raising revenue for general purposes but is collected to offset the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the county.
(3)
Collection and payment of regulatory fees.
(a)
The regulatory fee required hereunder shall be due in the amount and according to the schedule set forth on Table 1.3 which is attached hereto and made a part hereof. No portion of the regulatory fee shall include a separate administration fee.
(b)
The regulatory fee due hereunder shall be paid each calendar year. The regulatory fee shall be paid on an annual basis on January 1 of each year and shall be delinquent if not paid by March 1 of each year.
(c)
Any new business, profession, practitioner or occupation required to pay a regulatory fee and which commences business in the county during a calendar year shall pay the full amount of the regulatory fee within ten days of beginning to transact business within the county.
(4)
Multiple locations within the county. When a business, profession, practitioner or occupation conducts and/or transacts business at more than one fixed location or office within the unincorporated areas of the county, each location or office shall be considered a separate and distinct business, profession, practitioner or occupation for the purposes of payment of the regulatory fee required hereunder.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § V, 7-7-09)

Sec. 70-176. - Exemptions.
(1)
General exemptions. The following businesses, professions, practitioners and occupations are exempt from payment of the occupation tax and regulatory fees required by this article:
(a)
Those businesses regulated by the Georgia Public Service Commission;
(b)
Those electrical service businesses organized under Chapter 3 of Title 46 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated;
(c)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including any agribusiness;
(d)
Any disabled veteran of any war or armed conflict in which any branch of the armed forces of the United States engaged, whether under United States command or otherwise;
(e)
Any blind person;
(f)
Any veteran of peace-time service in the United States armed forces who has a physical disability incurred during the period of such service;
(g)
Practitioners whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the government of the United States, State of Georgia, or any county or any municipality of the State of Georgia;
(h)
Any business operated solely for a charitable purpose. Provided, however, to qualify for said exemption a business must devote at least 50 percent of its gross receipts to charitable purposes and must furnish the county with written documentation confirming that at least 50 percent of its gross receipts were devoted to charitable purposes;
(i)
Cooperative marketing associations governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105;
(j)
Insurance companies governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.;
(k)
Motor common carriers governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15;
(l)
Those businesses described in and governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355;
(m)
Agricultural products and livestock raised in the state governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356;
(n)
Depository financial institutions governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93;
(o)
Facilities operated by a charitable trust governed by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-55; and
(p)
Any business, profession, practitioner or occupation upon which levy of an occupation tax or regulatory fee is prohibited by state or federal law.
(2)
Exemption applicable to small businesses. Pursuant to the authority contained in the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(f), and in order to promote and encourage economic development and to further encourage, attract, develop and maintain small businesses within the county, including providing jobs and economic livelihood for many, all small businesses of whatever kind and nature located within the unincorporated areas of the county are exempt from paying the occupation tax imposed by this article. As utilized herein, the term "small business" shall be defined as a business or occupation which employs five or less employees. However, the term "small business" shall not include those professions or occupations listed on Table 1.2 hereof, regardless of the number of employees.
(3)
Exemption applicable to real estate brokers. Pursuant to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-17(a), no occupation tax shall be levied upon or collected from real estate brokers or agents who transact business within the county but whose offices are located outside the county. In the event the real estate agent or broker has a location or office within the county, he/she shall be subject to the occupation tax imposed hereunder.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § VI, 7-7-09)

Sec. 70-177. - Penalties and enforcement.
(1)
Inspection of books and records. All businesses, professions, practitioners or occupations subject to the requirements of this article shall keep and maintain current business records concerning the operations of said business. The county manager, through his officers, agents, employees and representatives, may inspect the books or records of any business, profession, practitioner or occupation which is subject to the requirements of this article and said books or records shall be submitted for inspection within 30 days of the date a request for inspection is made. Any records, books or information provided to or reviewed by the county shall, pursuant to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-15 and shall not be subject to disclosure under the provisions of the Georgia Open Records Act.
(2)
Delinquent penalties. In the event any occupation tax, administrative fee or regulatory fee remains unpaid for a period of 90 days after its due date, a delinquent penalty equal to ten percent of the occupation tax, administrative fee or regulatory fee shall be due and payable. In addition, interest shall accrue on unpaid occupation taxes, administrative fees or regulatory fees at the rate of one and one-half percent per month until paid.
(3)
Enforcement. In addition to all civil and criminal remedies authorized by this article, the Code of Catoosa County and Georgia Law, the officer charged with the collection of the occupation tax, administrative fee or regulatory fee hereunder shall be permitted to issue executions against any business, profession, practitioner or occupation for the amounts authorized by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-26. In addition, a civil fine may be imposed by a court of competent jurisdiction in an amount up to the maximum permitted by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-26.
(4)
Transaction of business. No business, profession, practitioner or occupation shall transact business or collect fees or monies from the transaction of business in the county unless and until all requirements of the ordinance are met and all occupation taxes, administrative fees and regulatory fees are paid current. Any business, profession, practitioner or occupation (which includes the person or persons soliciting for or representing said entities) which transacts business in the county in violation of this article or while any fees or taxes due hereunder are delinquent shall be subject to any and all remedies and penalties available under this article, the Code of Catoosa County or Georgia law.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § VIII, 7-7-09)
Sec. 70-178. - Court proceedings.
(1)
Jurisdiction. Violations of this article shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county.
(2)
Available remedies. Nothing in this section shall prevent the county from bringing any civil action for injunction, mandamus or other proceedings to present, correct or abate any violation of this article. No sanction, penalty or remedy described herein shall be considered exclusive of any other remedy, but shall be available in addition to any other sanction, penalty or remedy at equity or law.
(3)
Separate violations. Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(4)
Public records. Records of convictions for violation of this article shall be a matter of public record.
(5)
Bench warrants. No person accused of violating this ordinance shall be arrested prior to trial, but any defendant who fails to appear for trial shall be arrested thereafter on the warrant of the magistrate and required to post a bond for his future appearances.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § VIII, 7-7-09)
Sec. 70-179. - General provisions.
(1)
Public hearings. A public hearing shall be held by the board prior to any increase in the occupation tax, administrative fee or regulatory fee due hereunder. In addition, if the revenues received from the taxes and fees under this article exceed the revenues from the preceding year, the board shall hold a public hearing as required under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-28 to determine the use of said additional revenue.
(2)
Effective date. This article shall become effective on the date of its adoption and shall remain in full force and effect until its appeal.
(3)
Repeal of existing ordinances. Any and all prior or existing ordinances of the county which conflict with the provisions of this article are hereby repealed.
(Ord. No. 09-006, § IX, 7-7-09)
