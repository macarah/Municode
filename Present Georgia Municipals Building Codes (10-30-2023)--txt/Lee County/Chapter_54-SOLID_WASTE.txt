ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 54-1. - Purpose.
It is the principal objective of this chapter to attain a clean and litter-free community through education and voluntary compliance. Enforcement procedures shall be utilized only where other means fail.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-2. - Jurisdiction.
The provisions of this chapter shall be applicable in the unincorporated areas of the county. This chapter shall govern the accumulation, handling and disposal of all solid waste in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-3. - Administration and interpretations.
(a)
It is the responsibility of the county manager to administer the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
The county manager, upon written requests, shall provide interpretations concerning provisions of this chapter which are unclear or in dispute.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008; Ord. of 5-28-2013)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Sec. 54-4. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement officer to enforce this chapter.
(b)
Violations of the provisions of this chapter shall be deemed a misdemeanor (with the exception of failure to pay the monthly garbage bill, which shall be handled through a civil action) punishable as provided by in state law, with each day such violations continue constituting a separate offense. Appropriate actions and proceedings may be taken by law or in equity to prevent any violations of this chapter; to recover damages; and to restrain, correct or abate a violation. These remedies shall be in addition to the other penalties described in this subsection.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-5. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Annual means a calendar year commencing January 1 of each year and concluding December 31 of each year.
Ashes means the residue from the burning of wood, coal or other combustible materials.
Bags means plastic sacks designed for refuse with sufficient wall strength to maintain physical integrity when lifted by the top with no support beneath, securely tied at the top for collection, with a capacity not to exceed 30 gallons and a loaded weight not to exceed 35 pounds.
Brown goods means bulky waste such as sofas, chairs, mattresses and the like.
Bulky waste means a large appliance, piece of furniture or waste material from a residential source other than construction debris or hazardous waste, with a weight or volume greater than that allowed for containers.
Code enforcement officer means the person designated by the county manager as responsible for enforcing violations of this chapter.
Commercial establishment means any person or entity required by the county ordinance to pay an occupation tax, plus any person who is exempt from the county occupation tax pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-12-1. For the purposes of this chapter, such establishments are divided into the following classifications:
(1)
Individual commercial establishment means a commercial establishment not part of a group commercial establishment.
(2)
Group commercial establishment means a building or tract of land with more than one place of business or space for more than one commercial establishment.
Construction and demolition material means any material such as but not limited to lumber, brick, block, stone, carpet, plumbing materials, plaster, concrete, roofing, floor tile, gutters or other substances accumulated as a result of the construction of or repairs or additions to structures or accessory structures or the demolition of such.
Container means a receptacle (not including front-loading containers) made of plastic, metal or fiberglass with a capacity not to exceed 100 gallons, a loaded weight of not more than 75 pounds, a tight fitting lid, and handles of adequate strength for lifting.
Disposal site means a refuse depository for the processing or disposal of refuse, including but not limited to sanitary landfills, transfer stations, incinerators, and waste processing separation centers, licensed, permitted or approved by all governmental bodies and agencies having jurisdiction.
Front-loading container means a metal receptacle that can be mechanically lifted into the county's or its designee's collection vehicles for disposal of the refuse contained in the receptacle.
Garbage means putrescible animal or vegetable wastes resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking, serving or consumption of food, including food containers.
Group residential development means a building or development with more than eight individual rental dwellings or lots, attached or detached, on one parcel of land. Examples include apartments and manufactured home parks.
Hazardous refuse means waste designated as hazardous by the United States Environmental Protection Agency or appropriate state agency, to include but not be limited to motor oil, fuels, antifreeze, batteries and lead-based paint products.
Household trash means waste accumulation usually attendant upon housekeeping, such as paper, sweepings, dust, rags, bottles, cans or other matter of any kind other than garbage.
Individual residential unit means a residential unit that is not part of a group residential development.
Industrial establishment means any establishment involved in the production of goods or any part of goods.
Industrial waste means all waste, including solids, semisolids, sludge and liquids, created by or resulting from the operation of industrial establishments.
Institutional establishments are considered to be commercial establishments (q.v.).
Litter means all discarded materials, including but not limited to garbage, household trash, commercial trash, industrial waste, building materials, appliances or portions of appliances, refuse, waste materials, sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, debris, dead animals, or any other discarded or used substance not handled in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
Medical waste means any biomedical hazard material.
Non-owner occupied residential unit means a residential unit that is not occupied by the owner of the residential unit, but is rented, leased, or available for rent or lease.
Refuse means discarded waste materials in a solid or semi-liquid state, consisting of garbage, rubbish, or a combination of garbage and rubbish.
Residential unit means a dwelling or living space, and includes single-family residential units, duplexes, quadriplexes, apartments, mobile homes and modular homes.
Rubbish means nonputrescible solid wastes consisting of combustible and noncombustible materials.
Scavenging means the unauthorized retrieval of discarded refuse items.
Solid waste means the same as "refuse."
Utility services department director means the supervisor of the utility services department, responsible for administering this chapter.
White goods means bulky wastes such as refrigerators, freezers, washers, dryers and other large appliances.
Yard waste means waste accumulation of tree branches, tree limbs, parts of trees, bushes, shrubbery cuttings or clippings, grass clippings and all other similar wastes.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012; Ord. of 5-28-2013)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.

ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION[2]

Sec. 54-36. - Authority of the county manager.
The county manager shall have charge of the administration of this chapter. The county manager is authorized to adopt and promulgate rules and regulations respecting the size, type and specifications, number and location of all containers for collection. Such rules and regulations shall have the force of law, but shall not be contrary to anything in this chapter but supplementary to this chapter.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008; Ord. of 5-28-2013)
Sec. 54-37. - Ownership of refuse.
All refuse shall be and shall remain the property of the owner or occupant, jointly and severally, until it has been placed in vehicles of the county or its designee or taken to a licensed landfill or otherwise deposed of in compliance with this chapter. The county has no responsibility or liability for any injuries or damage resulting from the refuse.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-38. - Collection, hauling and disposal services.
(a)
All residential, commercial and industrial establishments in the unincorporated portions of the county shall utilize the solid waste collection service of the county for the disposition of refuse. Refuse shall be neatly stored in containers approved by the utility billing department director and shall be collected on a schedule set by the utility billing department director.
(b)
In group residential developments, the owner of the development shall be responsible, for choosing for tenants to be billed individually or collectively. Such fee shall be established in the annual budget. The utility billing department director, in consultation with the development owner, shall determine the appropriate size, number and type of container to serve the development.
(c)
Owner or lessee of any residential unit, other than a group residential development, shall be responsible for the fee assessed by the county for solid waste collection for the residential unit. Such fees shall be established in the annual budget.
(d)
In group commercial establishments, the owners of the building or real property on which the establishment lies shall be responsible, jointly and severally, for the fee assessed for solid waste collection for the establishment. Such fees shall be established in the annual budget. The utility billing department director, in consultation with the owner of the group commercial establishment, shall determine what size and how many front-loading containers shall be needed to adequately serve the group commercial establishment.
(e)
In individual commercial establishments, the owners or operators of the individual business shall be responsible, jointly and severally, for the fee assessed for solid waste collection for the establishment. Such fees shall be established in the annual budget. The utility billing department director, in consultation with the establishment owner, shall determine if the establishment in question will be best served by a 90-gallon cart or a front-loading container. A cart shall not be used if the refuse the establishment generates overflows the cart.
(f)
It shall be the responsibility of the person identified in this section as responsible for the solid waste collection fee to notify the utility billing department when moving, to discontinue service. At the time service is discontinued, payment in full of all outstanding balances is required.
(g)
The county or its designee may decline to collect any:
(1)
Container not properly placed or not placed prior to 7:00 a.m. on the designated collection day;
(2)
Container not approved by the utility billing department director;
(3)
Containers that contain sharp objects or liquids or any refuse not properly contained;
(4)
Yard waste;
(5)
Hazardous waste; or
(6)
Medical waste.
Where the county or its designee has reason to leave solid waste uncollected, the county or its agents shall inform the customer within two days by written notice, mailing, or telephone as to why the solid waste was not collected.
(h)
If a regularly scheduled collection is missed and a complaint is received by the utility billing department, and where no fault is found on the customer's part, a special collection of refuse will be required of the county or its designee within 48 hours.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-39. - Monthly payment of waste collection service fees, late fees, and returned check fees for residential and non-owner occupied residential units.
(a)
Bills for solid waste collection services provided to residential units and non-owner occupied residential units shall be billed to the person or entity responsible for the payment of such bills on a monthly basis.
(b)
Monthly bills for solid waste collection service fees for residential units and non-owner occupied residential units levied by this chapter shall be paid within 15 calendar days of the date of the monthly bill for such services. The amount of each such monthly bill shall be the amount established in the schedule of fees and charges for solid waste collection services.
(c)
Such monthly bills shall be mailed or otherwise delivered to the person or entity responsible for the payment of such monthly bill by the Lee County Utility Services Department, or such other department of the county as may be designated by the county manager. Payment of such utility bills shall be remitted directly to the Lee County Utility Services Department, or such other department as may be designated by the county manager for receipt of such payments.
(d)
If solid waste collection service fees are not paid by the person or entity responsible therefor in a timely manner, the person or entity responsible therefor shall pay an additional ten percent of the balance due compounded monthly from the due date of the bill as a late payment penalty. In addition, solid waste collection service provided to the residential units or the non-owner occupied residential units where such solid waste collection service fees are not paid in a timely manner may be discontinued in the event that the bill owed for such services is more than 60 calendar days past due. Any solid waste that accumulates as a result of discontinued service for non-payment shall be considered a violation of this chapter and shall subject the owner to such penalties as may be provided in this Code.
(e)
All returned checks remitted to the county in payment of solid waste collection service fees shall be subject to a processing fee in the amount set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012; Ord. of 5-28-2013)

Sec. 54-40.1. - Partial payments of solid waste collection service fees prohibited.
Partial payments of solid waste collection service fees shall not be authorized.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012)
Sec. 54-41. - Receptacles and containers required.
(a)
Every occupied building or premises upon which refuse is accumulated or produced shall be required by this chapter to provide and keep in a suitable place, readily accessible to the solid waste collection crews of the county or its designees, receptacles and containers approved by the utility billing department director as suitable for the storage of all such refuse that will normally accumulate between the established collection dates.
(b)
The utility billing department director shall determine the type, size, specifications, number and location of the receptacles and shall determine whether the receptacles and containers are in a serviceable condition.
(c)
Should any additional individual residential unit containers be needed, either on the determination of the customer or the utility billing department director, they may be obtained from the utility billing department for an additional fee equal to that fee assessed for the first cart. These fees shall be established in the annual budget.
(d)
Unless approved by the utility billing department director, containers furnished by the county or its designees shall not be removed from the premises to which delivered.
(e)
Any spillage or overflow from the refuse container shall be cleaned up immediately by the customer.
(f)
Those customers with curbside pickup shall have their refuse container placed curbside by 7:00 a.m. on the designated collection day. Curbside refers to that portion of the right-of-way adjacent to and within ten feet of paved or traveled roadways (including alleys).
(g)
Those customers with curbside pickup shall return their refuse container to the side or rear of their building no later than 7:00 p.m. on the designated garbage pickup day. Where the collection point is located more than 100 yards from a building, the container may be stored at another location approved by the utility billing department director.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-42. - Receptacles and containers to be safe.
(a)
All receptacles and containers shall be maintained in good service condition at all times. Any receptacle or container that does not conform to the requirements of this chapter or to the rules and regulations of the utility billing department director, or which is likely to hamper or injure the person collecting the contents or the public generally, shall be replaced promptly by the user.
(b)
If any such container, after request by the utility billing department director, has not been replaced, this shall be considered a violation of this chapter and shall be treated accordingly.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-43. - Garbage collection service for disabled persons.
The county shall provide collection service for disabled persons who are in fact unable by reason, age, or infirmity to place their containers at the roadside and live at a residence where no able bodied person resides. Persons with disabilities certified to the utility services department director by a licensed physician will be exempt from placing their refuse roadside. This exemption is for one year. It is renewable if the person's doctor certifies annually, to the utility services department director, the presence of an ongoing disability. The county or its designee agrees to collect the waste of such disabled persons in any mutually convenient place on the premises of such disabled persons, except the county or its designee shall not be required to go into houses or other dwellings.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012)
Secs. 54-44—54-75. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PRECOLLECTION PRACTICES

Sec. 54-76. - Garbage.
(a)
Garbage placed in containers for collection shall be enclosed in bags as defined in section 54-5. Household trash shall be combined with garbage.
(b)
No yard waste shall be placed in any container to be picked up by the county or its designee.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-77. - Injurious trash items.
Waste material with potential for inflicting injury, such as needles, broken glass, light bulbs, sharp pieces of metal, fluorescent tubes and television tubes shall be securely packaged or wrapped in such a manner as will prevent injury to the collection crews or other persons. The provisions of this subsection shall not apply to material placed in mechanically handled containers.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-78. - Hazardous waste.
Hazardous refuse shall not be collected by the county or its designee. No person shall place any items of hazardous waste in any refuse receptacle or other container for collection by the county. Federal and state hazardous waste laws shall be observed by all persons, and any violations will be reported to the appropriate authorities by the code enforcement officer.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-79. - Construction and demolition materials.
(a)
Construction and demolition materials will not be collected by the county or its designee. The owner of the premises or the contractor, builder or person doing the repairs, remodeling, construction or demolition shall be required to legally dispose of all such debris. Such debris may be disposed of at the county inert and recyclable holding facility in accordance with the schedule of fees and charges or by a state licensed private contractor. The utility billing department will provide a list of such contractors upon request.
(b)
Construction and demolition materials that may accumulate during the construction or demolition of a development or structure shall be contained in a trash bin, drop-off trailer, or similar container. It shall be a violation of this chapter for any developer, builder, contractor or landowner to allow construction and demolition materials or other debris to accumulate other than in a container from which such items cannot escape (except by human force) and from which such items may be easily gathered for disposal. Violations will be handled as described in section 54-4. Any person cited for violating this section shall have his building permit revoked until the violation is corrected to the satisfaction of the code enforcement officer.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-80. - Yard waste.
(a)
In all zoning districts, yard waste accumulated by the owner or occupant shall be composed on the site or taken by the owner or occupant to the county inert and recyclable holding facility or picked up by the county in accordance as established in the annual budget. Any owner or occupant wishing to burn yard waste shall comply with O.C.G.A. § 12-6-90 as administered by the local forestry department. Failure to comply with this section shall constitute a violation of this chapter.
(b)
When a tree or other yard waste is cut from private property by anyone other than the owner, such as a private contractor, such party shall legally dispose of all such material. The owner, tenant or other persons in possession of the property shall be responsible to see that such party complies with this provision.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-81. - Ashes.
It shall be unlawful to deposit hot ashes in garbage containers. Ashes shall not be placed or deposited upon streets, sidewalks or other publicly owned land.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-82. - Pet litter.
Pet litter shall be placed in securely tied plastic bags and placed in the refuse container for collection by the county or its designee.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-83. - Household furniture and furnishings (brown goods).
(a)
A collection area will be provided at the county inert and recyclable holding facility where brown goods may be accumulated. Cost of disposal is based on weight determined at the facility in accordance with the schedule of fees and charges.
(b)
It is the responsibility of the owner of the waste to use the facility or legally dispose of the brown goods in another manner.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-84. - Appliances (white goods).
A collection area will be provided at the county inert and recyclable holding facility where white goods may be accumulated. Cost of disposal is based on weight determined at the facility in accordance with the schedule of fees and charges. It is the responsibility of the owner of the waste to use the facility or legally dispose of the white goods in another manner.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-85. - Tires.
(a)
A collection area will be provided at the county inert and recyclable holding facility where tires may be accumulated. Cost of disposal shall be in accordance with the fee schedule.
(b)
It is the responsibility of the owner of the tires to use the facility or legally dispose of the tires in another manner.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-86. - Disposable diapers.
Disposable diapers shall be placed and tied securely in plastic bags and placed in the refuse container for collection by the county or its designee. No other type of human waste shall be placed in any refuse receptacle for collection by the county or its designee.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-87. - Twice yearly, no-cost disposal of yard waste and appliances/white goods.
Notwithstanding the provisions of section 54-80 of this Code (relating to the disposal of yard waste), and notwithstanding the provisions of section 54-84 of this Code (relating to the disposal of appliances/white goods), the residents of each residential dwelling in Lee County shall be authorized to deliver yard waste and appliances/white goods to the county landfill on two Saturdays per calendar year without the payment of tipping fees, disposal fees or other normal and customary landfill charges, as long as the following requirements are met:
(1)
The total amount to be delivered and disposed of at the landfill under this subsection shall not exceed 500 pounds during any calendar month per residence.
(2)
The person delivering the yard waste and appliances/white goods to the landfill shall have each delivery weighed at the landfill.
(3)
In the event that the debris or other items to be disposed of at the landfill under this section for any one residence exceed a total weight of 500 pounds for any calendar month, then the usual and customary tipping fees, disposal fees, and other normal customary landfill charges shall be paid by the person disposing of such waste with respect to the weight of the waste in excess of 500 pounds.
(4)
Any waste or other debris that does not meet the definition of yard waste or appliances/white goods shall be subject to the payment of tipping fees, disposal fees, and other usual and customary landfill charges thereon at regular rates as established from time to time.
(5)
No disposal shall occur under the authority of this section unless the landfill is attended by county employees assigned to work at the landfill.
(6)
The Lee County Public Works Department shall keep accurate records of the category (yard waste or appliances/white goods) and weight of each delivery to the landfill under this subsection.
The twice-annual disposal of yard waste and appliances/white goods under this section shall only occur on the Saturday each calendar year designated for the Rivers Alive Cleanup Program in Lee County and on the Saturday each calendar year designated for the Great American Clean-Up in Lee County. In the event that one or both of such events are cancelled or terminated, or are no longer held for whatever reason, the board of commissioners shall be authorized, by resolution, to establish other Saturdays during each calendar year for the disposal of yard waste and appliances/white goods without the payment of fees under this section, but in no event shall there be more than two such Saturdays established in each calendar year.
The Lee County Public Works Department, with approval of the county manager, shall be authorized to establish such rules and regulations consistent with this section as such department deem necessary in order to effectively administer the provisions of this section.
( Ord. of 6-23-2015(1) )
Editor's note— Ord. of 6-23-2015(1) repealed the former section and enacted a new section as set out herein. The former section pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Ord. of 11-12-2013(2).
Sec. 54-88. - Penalties for failure to dispose of items in county landfill in designated location.
(a)
Notwithstanding other provisions of this chapter, any person who fails or refuses to deposit materials in the county landfill at the designated disposal site for such materials shall be guilty of a violation of this chapter.
(b)
Any person who violates the provisions of this section shall, upon the first offense, receive a written warning from the public works director, or his or her designee. Upon the second offense, such person shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall receive a citation and to be subject to a fine not to exceed $100.00 in the Magistrate Court of Lee County; and upon the third or subsequent offense, such person shall be prohibited by the public works director, or his designee, from using the landfill or the services provided at the landfill for a period not to exceed 12 calendar months.
( Ord. of 7-12-2016 )
Secs. 54-89—54-115. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

Sec. 54-116. - Public and private property.
No person shall place any litter, refuse, garbage or trash in any street, median strip, alley or other public place of travel, nor upon any other public property, nor upon any private property except with the written consent of the owner or occupant, and then only in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-117. - Drainageways.
No person shall impede or cause to be impeded any drainage way, public or private, by placing any litter, refuse, trash, trees, yard waste, or any other item in, on or over such public or private drainageways.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-118. - Vacant units, undeveloped lots and rental units.
The county or its designee will not remove garbage, trash, construction and demolition material, household trash or yard waste or any other debris that is accumulated on or in vacant units, undeveloped lots and rental units.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-119. - Scavenging.
No person other than the owner shall disturb or interfere with any container used for the purpose of storing refuse pending its collection, or remove any contents from the containers, or remove such container from its location.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-120. - Transportation of debris.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle transporting loose materials on any of the roads of the county to transport such materials without suitable covers securely fastened to the vehicle in such a manner as to prevent the loose material from being deposited on the streets or adjoining public and/or private lands.
(Ord. of 9-30-2008)
Sec. 54-121. - Penalty for violation.
The violation of any provision of this chapter, or the failure to do any act or meet any standard required by this chapter, or the failure to comply in any manner with any requirement of this chapter, shall constitute an offense triable in the Magistrate Court of Lee County, and such offense shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment for 60 days, or both, as authorized pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012)
Sec. 54-122. - Prima facie evidence.
Whenever litter, garbage, or debris is thrown, deposited, dropped, or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane, wagon, wheelbarrow, or other conveyance in violation of this chapter, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance from which such litter or garbage is thrown, deposited, dropped, or dumped has violated this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012)
Sec. 54-123. - Rebuttable presumption.
Whenever any litter or garbage is dumped, deposited, thrown, or left on public or private property in violation of this chapter is discovered to contain any article or articles, including, but not limited to, letters, bills, publications, or other writings that display the name of the person thereon in such a manner so as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person dumped, deposited, threw, or left such litter or garbage illegally in violation of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012)
Sec. 54-124. - Specific penalties—Cumulative.
(a)
Where any section of this chapter contains specific remedies, penalties, or punishments, the specific remedies, penalties, or punishments shall not be exclusive, but shall be in addition to all other remedies, penalties, and punishments provided in this chapter or otherwise provided by law for the violation of or non-compliance with this chapter.
(b)
In addition to all other punishments and penalties provided in this chapter or otherwise by law, the Magistrate Court of Lee County shall be authorized to impose the following or additional alternative sentences, either as a direct sentence, or as a condition of probation:
(1)
The court may direct the convicted person to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the date of the execution of the sentence; or
(2)
The court may direct the convicted person to pick up and remove from any public park, private right-of-way, or, with the permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by any competent evidence that the person has deposited litter or garbage prior to the date of the execution of the sentence.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012)
Sec. 54-125. - Action in emergencies.
Nothing contained in this chapter shall require intermediate measures where the violation of this chapter is of such nature or degree so as to constitute an immediate clear and present danger to the health and safety of the public. Any failure by the county to take the steps provided in this chapter shall not constitute a defense to any proceeding to enforce the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012)
Sec. 54-126. - Appeals.
Any person aggrieved by a decision of the county under this chapter with respect to the amount owed by such person as a result of the fees imposed by this chapter may file an appeal of such action or decision with the county manager within 45 calendar days of receipt of such person's bill for such solid waste collection service fees. Such appeal must be in writing on an approved appeal form which may be obtained from the county manager's office. The appeal must set forth in detail the reason the property owner believes that the solid waste collection service fee is not owed or has been incorrectly billed to the property owner. If the property owner wishes to present oral argument in support of his or her appeal, the property owner, in his or her appeal, must request such oral argument before the county manager. In the event of such a request, the county manager will set a date to hear such argument within 30 days of the date of filing of the appeal. The appeal shall be deemed to have been filed on the date the appeal is actually received by the county manager. The county manager will have 30 calendar days from the date of such hearing to determine if the relief sought in the appeal should be granted. In the event that no oral argument is requested, the county manager shall determine if the relief sought in the appeal should be granted within 30 calendar days of the date of the filing of the appeal. A written response providing notification to the property owner of the decision shall be provided to the property owner within ten calendar days after the date of such decision. The county manager shall be authorized to designate a hearing officer to hear and decide such appeals. The county manager shall also be authorized to establish an advisory committee to assist in hearing such appeals and making recommendations to the county manager regarding the disposition of such appeals.
(Ord. of 3-27-2012; Ord. of 5-28-2013)
