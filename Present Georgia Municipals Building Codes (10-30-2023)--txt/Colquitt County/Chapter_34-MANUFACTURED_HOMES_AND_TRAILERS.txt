ARTICLE II. - MOBILE HOME DECALS

Sec. 34-31. - Applicability.
This article shall apply to all prosecutions in the county arising under or pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-493.
(Ord. of 6-20-94, § 2)
Sec. 34-32. - Citations.
Violations of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-493 may be tried upon citations with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations. Each citation shall provide the information required by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63(b). Prosecutions for violations of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-493 may be commenced by the completion, signing and service of a citation by either of the county tax assessors. The original of the citation shall be personally served upon the accused by either of the tax assessors or by any employee or staff member of the county board of tax assessors. The prosecution of said violations of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-493 shall in all other respects follow the procedures set forth in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.
(Ord. of 6-20-94, § 3)

Sec. 34-51. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following definitions shall apply:
Manufactured home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein; except that such term shall include any structure which meets all the requirements of this paragraph except the size requirements and with respect to which the manufacturer voluntarily files a certification required by the Secretary of Housing and Urban Development and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. § 5401, et seq.
Mobile home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein and manufactured prior to June 15, 1976.
Person includes a natural person, partnership, limited partnership (domestic or foreign), trust, estate, association, or corporation.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 3-17-03)
Sec. 34-52. - Relocation permit required—Moving home in county.
No person shall move a mobile home or manufactured home in the county without first obtaining a permit from the county compliance officer authorizing such relocation. No relocation permit shall be issued unless the person applying for the permit specifies the new location to which the mobile home or manufactured home is to be moved. The permit shall remain on the mobile home or manufactured home until a certificate of occupancy is issued and said permit is voided by the county compliance officer.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 2, 3-17-03)
Sec. 34-53. - Same—Moving home into or out of county.
No person shall move a mobile home or manufactured home into the county for location within the county or move a mobile home or manufactured home out of the county whose location has been in the county without first obtaining a permit from the county compliance officer authorizing such relocation. No relocation permit shall be issued unless the person applying for the permit specifies the new location to which the mobile home or manufactured home is to be moved.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 3, 3-17-03)
Sec. 34-54. - Same—Proof of payment of taxes required.
No permit required by this article shall be issued until the person applying for the relocation permit submits to the county compliance officer proof that all state and county taxes, including the county residential solid waste tax, theretofore accruing and payable with respect to such mobile home or manufactured home have been paid; and if the mobile home or manufactured home to be relocated is then located within any portion of the county that lies within the limits of a municipality therein, such person shall likewise submit proof that all applicable municipal taxes have been paid.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 4, 3-17-03)
Sec. 34-55. - Decal required.
Any person who has made application for the relocation of a mobile home or manufactured home and whose application meets the requirements of this article shall be issued a permit and a decal upon payment of a fee to be established from time to time by resolution of the board of commissioners of the county. The decal issued with the permit authorizing the relocation of a mobile home or manufactured home shall be affixed to the mobile home or manufactured home at all times such mobile home or manufactured home is being transported within the confines of the county. Such decal shall be designed in such manner and affixed to the mobile home or manufactured home in such manner as to cause it to be easily visible for inspection and shall stay affixed on the mobile home or manufactured home until same is inspected.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 5, 3-17-03; Ord. No. 2005-2, § 1, 7-18-05)
Sec. 34-56. - Notification of other counties.
Whenever a permit authorizing the relocation of a mobile home or manufactured home is used and such mobile home or manufactured home is to be relocated within the confines of another county in this state, the county tax commissioner shall notify the tax commissioner of such other county of the date of the issuance of such permit and the location of the mobile home or manufactured home after being moved.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 6, 3-17-03)
Sec. 34-57. - Application, permit and decal forms.
The county compliance officer is hereby authorized and empowered, subject to the specific provisions of this article, to prescribe application, permit, and decal forms, and thereafter to alter and change the same, and said compliance officer is empowered to require that applicants furnish such information in writing as may be reasonably required to effectuate the purposes hereof.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 7, 3-17-03)
Sec. 34-58. - List of mobile homes or manufactured homes in county.
Every person owning or operating a mobile home park or manufactured home park in the county or engaged in the business of renting lots upon which to place mobile homes or manufactured homes in the county shall furnish the county tax assessor on the first working day of each calendar year with a complete list of all mobile homes and manufactured homes parked, rented, or otherwise located upon the property of the person making the return.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 8, 3-17-03)
Sec. 34-59. - Enforcement.
It shall be the duty of the county tax commissioner, the county sheriff, the county tax assessors, the county administrator, and the county compliance officer to enforce the provisions of this article. Upon receiving information constituting probable cause made known to any person authorized to enforce the provisions of this article that a violation has occurred, a citation and summons may be issued to the alleged violator requiring him or her to appear before the county magistrate court on a day and time certain, to answer the charge or charges contained therein.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 9, 3-17-03)
