Sec. 1-12-1. - Athens-Clarke County Marshal.
(a)
Established. There is hereby created the position of Athens-Clarke County Marshal.
(b)
Recognition and certification. The Athens-Clarke County Marshal shall do all things necessary in order to be recognized as a law enforcement officer of the state and certified within the standards of the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Act (POST Act), as provided in O.C.G.A. § 35-8-1 et seq. In the event the Athens-Clarke County Marshal is not POST certified, then he shall have 12 months from his hiring date in which to become POST certified.
(c)
Power of arrest. The Athens-Clarke County Marshal shall have the power of arrest in the enforcement of all ordinances of Athens-Clarke County, Georgia, and the laws of the state.
(d)
Duties and responsibilities. The primary duties and responsibilities of the Athens-Clarke County Marshal shall be as follows:
(1)
To enforce all ordinances enacted by the mayor and commission of Athens-Clarke County;
(2)
To issue warnings, notices of ordinance violation, and citations for court appearances for the violation of ordinances;
(3)
To investigate and collect evidence of these ordinance violations;
(4)
To respond to complaints of alleged ordinance violations;
(5)
To appear and testify in court;
(6)
To do any follow-up work that may be necessary;
(7)
To ensure proper collection of fines;
(8)
To coordinate enforcement efforts with the building inspection office, planning department office, public works department and any other department or division of Athens-Clarke County as may be deemed necessary.
(e)
Placement of position. All employees in the Athens-Clarke County Marshal's Service shall be under the supervision of the Manager.
(Ord. of 12-2-2003, § 1)
Sec. 1-12-2. - Athens-Clarke County Code Enforcement Officer.
(a)
Established. There is hereby created the position of Athens-Clarke County Code Enforcement Officer, to be designated by the Manager.
(b)
Duties and responsibilities. The primary duties and responsibilities of the Athens-Clarke County Code Enforcement Officer shall be as follows:
(1)
To enforce all ordinances enacted by the mayor and commission of Athens-Clarke County;
(2)
To issue warnings, notices of ordinance violations, and citations for court appearances for the violation of ordinances;
(3)
To investigate and collect evidence of these ordinance violations;
(4)
To respond to complaints of alleged ordinance violations;
(5)
To appear and testify in court;
(6)
To do any follow-up work that may be necessary;
(7)
To ensure proper collection of fines;
(8)
To coordinate enforcement efforts with the building inspection office, planning department office, public works department and any other department or division of Athens-Clarke County as may be deemed necessary.
(c)
Placement of position. Except as otherwise designated in other departments by the manager at his discretion, all code enforcement officers shall be in the Athens-Clarke County Code Enforcement Division under the supervision of the department of building inspection.
(d)
In addition to all of the duties and responsibilities set forth above, where any provisions of this Code specifically authorize the Athens-Clarke County Marshal to issue summons or citations, the code enforcement officer shall also specifically be authorized to issue said summons or citation.
(Ord. of 12-2-2003, § 1; Ord. of 12-2-2016(1), § 1 )
