ARTICLE III. - WATER CONSERVATION

Sec. 82-56. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Water emergency means that the municipal water supply is critically short and unable to provide more than domestic needs of its customers.
Water shortage means that the available water supply of the municipal system is inadequate to meet peek daily demand of water customers and/or the state environmental protection division has issued a state-wide notice of watering restrictions.
(Ord. of 10-20-86(5), § 1; Ord. of 6-18-01)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 82-57. - Enforcement of article.
(a)
To enforce the provisions of the water shortage and water emergency requirements of this article, the following enforcement actions are established:
(1)
First violation. Any person found to be violating such provisions shall be served written notice stating the nature of the violation and shall be issued a warning citation for this violation by the city administrator.
(2)
Second violation. Upon a second violation, as determined by the city council, water service will be terminated and a reconnection fee of $50.00 will be charged.
(3)
Subsequent violations. For all subsequent violations, as determined by the city council, water service will be terminated and there shall be charged a reconnection fee of $100.00 for each violation.
(b)
Duly authorized officers bearing proper credentials and identification shall be permitted to enter all properties for the purpose of inspection, observation, measurement and testing in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 10-20-86(5), § 5)
Sec. 82-58. - Conservation generally.
No water is to be wasted in the city. All utility customers will be urged to use water conservation measures whenever possible.
(Ord. of 10-20-86(5), § 2)
Sec. 82-59. - Use of water during period of water shortage.
During periods of water shortage as declared by the mayor and council, there are hereby adopted the following uniform requirements for the public water supply:
(1)
Outside watering, including watering of grass, lawns and gardens and the washing of cars, will be allowed on alternate days only, at even house numbers on evennumbered days and at odd house numbers on oddnumbered days, or will be allowed on fewer than alternate days, if necessary, as set by the council; and
(2)
No street or water main flushing is permitted.
(Ord. of 10-20-86(5), § 3; Ord. of 7-20-92, § 1)
Sec. 82-60. - Use of water during period of water emergency.
During periods of water emergency as declared by the mayor and council, there are hereby adopted the following uniform requirements for the public water supply:
(1)
No outside watering of any kind, including watering of lawns, gardens, shrubbery, parks, etc., is permitted.
(2)
No street or water main flushing is permitted.
(3)
Commercial laundries may be open from 7:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m. only.
(4)
No makeup water shall be used for home swimming pools.
(Ord. of 10-20-86(5), § 4)
Sec. 82-61. - Outdoor water use.
(a)
Definitions. When used in this Code section:
Address means the "house number" (a numeric or alphanumeric designation) that, together with the street name, describes a physical location of a specific property. "Even numbered address" means a house number ending with the number 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, or no house number. "Odd numbered address" means a house number ending with the number 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
Declared drought response level means one of four levels of drought that can be declared based on the severity of drought conditions, with one being the least severe and four being the most severe.
(b)
Applicability of Code section. This section applies to water consumption from the city's public water system.
(c)
Outdoor water use schedule during non-drought periods.
(1)
During non-drought periods, outdoor water use other than activities exempted in Code Section 5-1032(e), shall occur only within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. to 12:00 midnight on the following scheduled days:
a.
Scheduled days for odd-numbered addresses are Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
b.
Scheduled days for even-numbered addresses are Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
Use of hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing is prohibited.
(d)
Outdoor water use schedule during declared drought response levels.
(1)
The mayor and council are authorized to make drought declarations.
(2)
During declared drought conditions, outdoor water use other than activities exempted in subsection (e) below, shall occur only during scheduled hours on the scheduled days.
(3)
Declared drought response level one—Outdoor water use may occur on scheduled days within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. to 12:00 midnight.
a.
Scheduled days for odd-numbered addresses are Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
b.
Scheduled days for even-numbered addresses are Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
c.
Use of hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing is prohibited.
(4)
Declared drought response level two—Outdoor water use may occur on scheduled days within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m.
a.
Scheduled days for odd-numbered addresses are Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
b.
Scheduled days for even-numbered addresses and golf course fairways are Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
c.
The following uses are prohibited:
1.
Using hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing.
2.
Washing hard surfaces, such as streets, gutters, sidewalks and driveways except when necessary for public health and safety.
(5)
Declared drought response level three—Outdoor water use may occur on the scheduled day within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m.
a.
The scheduled day for odd-numbered addresses is Sunday.
b.
The scheduled days for even-numbered addresses and golf course fairways is Saturday.
c.
The following uses are prohibited:
1.
Using hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing.
2.
Washing hard surfaces, such as streets, gutters, sidewalks, driveways, except when necessary for public health and safety.
3.
Filling installed swimming pools except when necessary for health care or structural integrity.
4.
Washing vehicles, such as cars, boats, trailers, motorbikes, airplanes, golf carts.
5.
Washing buildings or structures except for immediate fire protection.
6.
Non-commercial fund raisers, such as car washes.
7.
Using water for ornamental purposes, such as fountains, reflecting pools, and waterfalls except when necessary to support aquatic life.
(6)
Declared drought response level four—No outdoor water use is allowed, other than for activities exempted in subsection (e) below, or as the mayor and council may allow.
(e)
Exemptions.
(1)
This rule shall not apply to the following outdoor water uses:
a.
Capture and re-use of cooling system condensate or storm water in compliance with applicable City Code provisions.
b.
Re-use of gray water in compliance with applicable City Code provisions.
(2)
The following established landscape water uses are exempt from the outdoor water use schedules of this section:
a.
Use of reclaimed wastewater by a designated user from a system permitted by EPD to provide reclaimed wastewater.
b.
Irrigation of personal food gardens.
(3)
Newly (in place less than 30 days) installed landscapes are subject to the following:
a.
Irrigation of newly installed landscapes is allowed any day of the week, but only during allowed hours for the drought response level in effect, for a period of 30 days following installation. No watering is allowed during drought response level four.
b.
For new landscapes installed by certified or licensed professionals, commercial exemptions apply.
(4)
The following golf course outdoor water uses are exempt from the outdoor water use schedules of this rule:
a.
Use of reclaimed wastewater by a designated user from a system permitted by EPD to provide reclaimed wastewater.
b.
Irrigation of fairways during times of non-drought and declared drought response level one.
c.
Irrigation of tees during times of non-drought and declared drought response levels one, two and three.
d.
Irrigation of greens.
(5)
The following commercial outdoor water uses are exempt from the outdoor water use schedules of this section:
a.
Professionally certified or licensed landscapers, golf course contractors, and sports turf landscapers: during installation and 30 days following installation only. Professional landscapers must be certified or licensed for commercial exemptions to apply.
b.
Irrigation contractors: during installation and as needed for proper maintenance and adjustments only.
c.
Sod producers.
d.
Ornamental growers.
e.
Fruit and vegetable growers.
f.
Retail garden centers.
g.
Hydro-seeding.
h.
Power-washing.
i.
Construction sites.
j.
Producers of food and fiber.
k.
Car washes.
l.
Other activities essential to daily business.
m.
Watering-in of pesticides and herbicides on turf.
(f)
Rain sensing devises for automatic irrigation systems.
(1)
New automatic irrigation systems. Any new irrigation system installed within the city on or after January 1, 2011, must be equipped with rain sensing devices approved as to type by the city manager.
(2)
Existing automatic irrigation systems as of December 31, 2010.
Any irrigation system installed before January 1, 2002, may not be operated after January 1, 2011, without being equipped with rain sensing devises and freeze gauges approved as to number and type by the city manager.
(g)
Variances.
(1)
Conditions for variance. The city manager may, in special cases, grant variances from certain provisions of Code Sections 5-1032(c) and 5-1032(d) to persons demonstrating extreme hardship and need. The city manager may grant variances only under all of the following circumstances and conditions:
a.
Granting of a variance must not cause an immediate significant reduction in the city's water supply.
b.
The extreme hardship or need requiring the variance must relate to the health, safety, and welfare of the person requesting it.
c.
The health, safety, and welfare of other persons must not be adversely affected by granting the variance.
(2)
Process to receive variance. The applicant must sign a compliance agreement on forms provided by the city manager, and approved by the city attorney, agreeing to irrigate or water a lawn or landscape only in the amount and manner permitted by the variance.
(h)
Enforcement.
(1)
Notice of violation. Whenever the city finds that a person has violated a prohibition or failed to meet a requirement of this Code Section, the city may order compliance by written notice of the violation to the responsible party. Such notice will include the nature of the violation, a notice to immediately cease actions in violation of this Code Section, and a schedule to address the violation, if necessary.
(2)
Appeal of notice of violation. Any person receiving a notice of violation may appeal the determination of the city to the Municipal Court of Garden City, Georgia. The notice of appeal must be received by the clerk of said court within 15 days from the date of the notice of violation. Hearing on the appeal before the appropriate authority shall take place within 15 days from the date of receipt of the notice of appeal. The decision of the reviewing authority shall be final.
(3)
Injunctive relief. It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any provision or fail to comply with any of the requirements of this Code Section. If a person has violated or continues to violate the provisions of this Code Section, the city may petition for a preliminary or permanent injunction restraining the person from activities which would create further violations, or compelling the person to perform abatement or remediation of the violation.
(4)
Criminal prosecution. Any person that has violated or continues to violate this Code Section shall be liable to criminal prosecution to the fullest extent of the law, and shall be subject to a criminal penalty of $100.00 per violation per day. The City of Garden City may recover all attorneys' fees, court costs and other expenses associated with enforcement of this Code Section, including sampling and monitoring expenses.
(Ord. No. 2004-15, § 1, 8-16-04; Ord. No. 2010-8, §§ 1—4, 5-17-10)
