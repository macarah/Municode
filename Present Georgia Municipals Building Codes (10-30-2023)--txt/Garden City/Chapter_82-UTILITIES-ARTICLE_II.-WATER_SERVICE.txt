ARTICLE II. - WATER SERVICE

Sec. 82-26. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person convicted of violating any section of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-13.
(Code 1976, § 5-1014)
Sec. 82-27. - Application required; contents.
Every person wishing to become a user of water from the city water system must make application at the office of the water department or such other place as may be designated by the mayor and council. Such application shall be made in writing upon forms to be furnished to the applicant. The applicant shall give the street number and lot number and describe the location where the water connection is to be made, and shall further state the purposes for which the water is to be used. If the owner of the property being served is applying for such service, a copy of proof of property ownership (e.g., closing statement, warranty deed, etc.) shall be produced. If the applicant for water service is a tenant of the property to be served, a copy of the written lease shall be provided. In addition, the applicant's drivers' license or other valid form of identification must be submitted. At the time such application is made, the applicant shall pay any security deposit or other costs set forth in the schedule of fees approved by the city for water service.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001(1); Ord. No. 2018-16, § 1, 12-3-18)
Sec. 82-28. - Signing of application.
An application for water service must be signed by the applicant before any connection shall be made to the water system or prior to the furnishing of water to the premises. The application shall provide that the applicant shall be liable for the payment of any charges for water and sewer service to the premises.
(Code 1976, § 5-1002; Ord. No. 2018-16, § 2, 12-3-18)
Sec. 82-29. - Connections.
(a)
Stopcock required. Every connection to the city water system shall have a stopcock installed. The cost of the stopcock shall be paid by the owner of the property. The stopcock and cover therefor shall be maintained in good condition by the owner.
(b)
Persons authorized to make connection. No connection shall be made to the water system except by duly authorized employees of the city or by a plumber approved by the council.
(c)
Payment of costs. Any applicant receiving approval of his application to use water from the system shall pay to the water department such sum as the council may require to cover the expense of making the connection and installing the service cock.
(d)
Materials. All materials used by a user or owner in making a connection to a water main shall be approved by the council or its authorized agent.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001(3)—(5), (12))
Sec. 82-30. - Permit for work affecting system.
No user, after connection to the city water system, shall make or cause to be made any connection, extension or alteration without a written permit from the council.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001(2))
Sec. 82-31. - Compliance with regulations.
The turning on of city water service at any premises shall mean that compliance with the ordinances, rules and regulations of the city has been agreed to by the applicant for service.
(Code 1976, § 5-1007)
Sec. 82-32. - Wasting water; repair of leaks.
(a)
No user of the city water system shall allow any water to be unnecessarily wasted on his or another's premises. The council, upon investigation of any waste, may cut off the supply of water to the user.
(b)
Whenever it comes to the attention of the water department that any service connection upon the property of any owner is leaking, the council shall serve written notice upon the property owner requiring the owner to repair the condition. If the property owner does not repair the leak within ten days after receiving the notice, the city, through its agents, may enter upon the property of the owner and repair the condition at the expense of the owner. If the property owner fails to pay the cost of the repair by the tenth day of the month succeeding the month in which the repairs are made, the council may order that the water connection be disconnected until all charges are paid.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001(6), (7))
Sec. 82-33. - Authority to disconnect service.
A water connection to the city water system may be disconnected for the following reasons:
(1)
Non-payment of utility bills;
(2)
Fraud committed by the user in applying for or using the water service;
(3)
The user's willful disregard of the city's rules or regulations adopted by Council;
(4)
Emergency repairs;
(5)
Insufficiency of water supply due to circumstances beyond the City's control;
(6)
Direction of public authority; and,
(7)
Strike, riot, fire, flood, or unavoidable accident.
Should any disconnection be made for any non-payment of bills or a violation of city's rules and regulations, no reconnection shall be made until all accrued costs have been paid in full, plus a charge for reconnection for the water service. No person shall make any unauthorized connection to the water system.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001(8), (9); Ord. No. 2003-3, § 1, 5-5-03; Ord. No. 2008-1, § 2, 1-22-08; Ord. No. 2018-16, § 3, 12-3-18)
Sec. 82-34. - Authority to shutoff water at mains.
The council reserves the right at any time, without notice, to shutoff water at its mains for the purpose of making repairs or extensions or for any other purpose.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001(11))
Sec. 82-35. - Payment of costs for lowering service line.
Should it become necessary for any reason to lower the depth of the city water mains, the user or owner shall pay the cost of lowering the connecting water line which carries water to such user or owner. If the user or owner fails to pay such cost, the city may pay it. If paid by the city, such cost shall become a part of the water rent, and failure of the user or owner to pay such cost shall give the city the right to take such action provided in this chapter for collection of delinquent water rent, or such other penalty or remedy as provided by law.
(Code 1976, § 5-1001(13))
Sec. 82-36. - Unauthorized connection; tampering with water system.
(a)
All meters, curb cocks, valves and meter boxes connected with the city water mains and service pipes shall remain under the direct control of the water department, and it shall be unlawful for persons other than those authorized by the director of such department to connect, disconnect or tamper with any such meter, or to turn off the water at the curb cock, valve or meter, or to open, move or tamper with any meter box.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, other than an employee of the city in the performance of his duties as such, to tap into or make any opening in connection with the water mains or water lines of the city.
(Code 1976, § 5-1013)
Secs. 82-37—82-55. - Reserved.
