ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Purpose of chapter and applicability of article.
This chapter is enacted in furtherance of the police powers of the governing authority of the county to promote the health and general welfare of its citizens; to regulate and control the licensing and sale by the package and by the drink of beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits; to establish reasonable regulations and ascertainable standards for licensees which will ensure the public peace; to protect schools, churches, public housing, and residential areas from the negative secondary effects attributable to establishments that sell beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits; and to ensure that only qualified persons obtain licenses for the manufacture, sale or distribution of beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits. Further, unless otherwise provided, this article shall apply to all types of alcoholic beverage licenses issued by the governing authority.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-2. - Definitions.
Except as otherwise set forth in this chapter and except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning, the following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Applicant means the person or entity applying for any license authorized by this chapter.
Application means the written form approved and/or generated by the governing authority which must be completed and executed by any applicant for any license authorized by this chapter.
Beer or malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Brown bag establishment means any restaurant, music hall, theater, or other establishment providing food or entertainment in the normal course of business, and in which the owners or their agents knowingly allow patrons to bring in and consume the patron's own alcoholic beverages.
Church building means as a free-standing structure, originally built for use as a church, but does not mean a commercial or retail building or space that has been converted for occupancy by a church or for religious purposes.
Code means the Code of Ordinances of Tattnall County, Georgia.
County means Tattnall County, Georgia, and the land within the boundaries and territory of Tattnall County, Georgia.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Governing authority means the board of commissioners of Tattnall County, Georgia.
Hotel means any lodge, inn, or similar establishment which offers overnight sleeping accommodations to registered guests for hire, and at which prepared meals or food are regularly served daily and consumed in one or more dining rooms, having an adequate and sanitary kitchen, such sleeping accommodations and dining rooms being conducted on the same premises. Hotels may grant franchises for the operation of a restaurant and lounge on their premises, and the holder of such franchise shall be included in this definition of a hotel. Lounges may be located separately from dining facilities in hotels provided there is a commonality of ownership of the restaurant and lounge.
License means the authorization granted by the governing authority of the county to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages in a specified manner for a calendar year or the remaining portion of a calendar year.
Licensee means the person to whom a license is issued or, in the case of any type of partnership, limited liability company or corporation, all partners, officers, managers and directors of the partnership, limited liability company or corporation, and the use of a masculine, feminine or neutral pronoun to describe licensee will contemplate the correct licensee even if the incorrect pronoun is used.
Liter means metric measurement, currently used by the United States.
Manager means the person who supervises any operations, business, activity or location of any person licensed under this chapter.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The word "manufacturer" also means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits.
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, any brewer.
Minor means any person under the age of 21 years.
Owner means any person, partner, stockholder or member owning any percentage of an activity, business or operation.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, limited liability company, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private or quasi-public.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased or controlled by the licensee and used by the licensee for the purpose of operating under the license including any public sidewalk or roadway adjacent to the premises which is used by the licensee to sell or distribute alcoholic beverages.
Public club means an establishment, other than a restaurant as defined in this chapter, having the principal purpose of offering live music, dancing, theatrical productions or comedians, and at which the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises only is an attendant purpose. A public club shall not be operated or conducted in conjunction with any other type of business, trade or profession and may offer and sale only those items commonly associated with such establishments.
Public place means any place that the public is invited or permitted to go or congregate.
Purchaser means any individual, partnership, corporation, limited liability company, trust or association purchasing alcoholic beverages from a licensee.
Restaurant means any public place used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen, where the principal business is the sale of food, and that:
(1)
Fills 75 percent of the usable square footage of its primary premises, including decks and patios which are immediately adjacent to and a part of its primary premises, with tables and chairs intended for dining,
(2)
Is staffed at all times while open to the public with a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook, and serve edible food to its guests,
(3)
Is open to the public at least four hours a day, serving at least one meal per day,
(4)
Serves meals at least five days a week with the exception of weeks including holidays, vacations and periods of maintenance, construction or remodeling,
(5)
Does not have a common table or counter area that comprises more than 25 percent of its dining area, and
(6)
Derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals and food, and in calculating annual gross sales, it shall use all of its receipts, including those of persons laboring on the premises, such as all employees, independent contractors, performers, servers and entertainers.
Retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only, to consumers and not for resale.
School means any school building, school grounds, college campus, educational facility or educational building owned or run by or affiliated with a state, county, city or private or religious entity, any church school, day care, or kindergarten and to any building at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state. It shall not apply to home schools.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-3. - License application and fees.
(a)
Any person seeking any license permitted by this chapter shall file a written application with the county clerk, on the forms prescribed by the governing authority. Each application shall state:
(1)
The applicant's full name, birth date, present address and addresses for the past five years, business address and business addresses of employers and their addresses for the last five years, name of spouse, complete record of all arrests, convictions and guilty pleas ("guilty plea", whenever used in this chapter, shall include any plea entered under any local, state or federal ordinance or law relating to nolo contendere, first offender or conditional discharge, regardless of the nomenclature used by the local, state or federal government) for violations of any county or local ordinance and of any state or federal law and the disposition of all arrests and charges within the last five years prior to the application. As well, the applicant shall present to the county clerk originals of the following documents for copying (such copies shall be kept by the county clerk):
a.
Current state driver's license,
b.
Birth or naturalization certificate, and
c.
Two forms of valid picture identification, including but not limited to current state driver's license, current passport, current state identification card or military identification;
(2)
Exact location of the applicant's proposed premises, including street address, zoning district, if any, and tax map and parcel number;
(3)
Name and address of the owner or landlord and/or resident manager of the applicant's proposed premises, whether or not there is a building already erected thereon;
(4)
Trade name (or proposed trade name) of the applicant's proposed business and/or premises; and
(5)
Names and addresses of all persons having any financial or beneficial interest in the applicant's proposed business or premises.
(b)
As to the manager of the applicant's proposed premises (if not the same as the applicant), the same information as required of the applicant in this section.
(c)
Each application for a license under this chapter shall be accompanied by a certified bank check for the full amount of the license fee, together with a separate certified bank check in the amount of $200.00 to defray investigative and administrative costs. If the applicant withdraws his or her application prior to its being submitted to the governing authority, or if the application is denied by the governing authority, the deposit representing the license fee shall be refunded, but the $200.00 paid for investigation and administrative costs shall not be refunded.
(d)
The application shall be sworn to or affirmed under oath.
(e)
Surveyor's certificate. Unless otherwise permitted by law, all new applications for each premises shall be accompanied by a current certificate from a registered surveyor and shall contain the following information about the applicant's premises:
(1)
A scale drawing of the premises, with its location identified by street address and tax map and parcel number, and showing any structures on, or to be built on, the land;
(2)
Any proposed off-street parking facilities and all outdoor lighting;
(3)
Current zoning classification of the premises, if any; and
(4)
The distance in yards as measured by the most direct route of travel from the nearest property line of the premises to nearest property line of each of the following: any school building, school grounds, college campus, alcoholic treatment center or public housing which is owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government.
(f)
An application under this chapter containing known false information may result in the denial of the license applied for and prosecution as a felony.
(g)
As a prerequisite to the issuance of any initial license only, the applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints to be forwarded to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation (hereinafter referred to as "GBI"), which shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for any instance of criminal activity during the two years immediately preceding the date of the application. The GBI shall also submit the fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under the rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification of records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the governing authority submitting the fingerprints.
(h)
Applications must be filed on or before November 30 of each year; otherwise, a penalty equal to 20 percent of the annual license fee shall be imposed.
(i)
Except for temporary licenses or permits that may be granted pursuant to this chapter, all licenses shall be for the full calendar year or for the number of months (or portion thereof) remaining in the calendar year. License fees shall not be prorated for any reason, and license fees shall not be refundable for any reason.
(j)
Failure to furnish complete information in the application will delay the issuance of the license. The failure to furnish complete information within 30 days after being requested to do so by the county clerk may be grounds to deny the application.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-4. - Moral character of license applicants.
No person shall be issued a license unless it appears to the satisfaction of the governing authority that the license applicant is of good moral character. At the time an application is filed, the applicant shall, by affidavit duly sworn or affirmed, certify that the applicant has not been convicted of any felony offense within five years of the filing of the application. No license shall be issued where any applicant has been convicted of any felony offense within five years of the filing of the application.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-5. - General licensing qualifications.
(a)
No applicant for a license shall be issued to any person who is not either a citizen of the United States or a permanent legal resident of the United States. An applicant for any license for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages, as permitted by this chapter, must be a resident of the county, unless the applicant designates in writing a resident of the county (such resident must an individual and the applicant must provide a valid name, address and telephone number for such resident) who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, or possess for the purpose of sale, or offer to sell any alcoholic beverages as permitted by this chapter in the unincorporated area of the county, except pursuant to a license as permitted by this chapter.
(c)
A retail premises or business shall not have alcoholic beverages on its sales floor or in its cooler unless such premises or business has a license as permitted by this chapter.
(d)
A license issued under this chapter shall be a mere grant of privilege—not a right to carry on the business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this chapter and state law.
(e)
(1)
A person shall apply for a license in the name of the person who owns and/or operates the licensed business, and upon approval, the license will be issued in the name of the person.
(2)
Where an entity (e.g., corporations, limited liability companies, limited partnerships, limited liability partnerships, trusts, etc.), which is duly formed and in good standing with the Georgia Secretary of State, applies for a license, the entity shall apply in the legal name of the entity, and upon approval, the license shall be issued in the name of the entity; provided that, such applicant shall attach to the application a certificate showing compliance with the annual registration requirements from the Georgia Secretary of State, corporate division, showing the name and address of a registered agent for service within the state, and providing the identity of an agent, who is involved in the active management of the business to be licensed, who shall meet the requirements of individual applicants under this chapter at the time application is made and at all times during which the license is in effect.
(3)
Where a partnership applies for a license, the partnership shall apply in the name of one of the general partners, who is a resident of this state, and upon approval, the license will be issued in the name of all partners of the partnership; if the applicant is a partner in a partnership, the requirements of this chapter shall apply to all partners at the time of application, and at all times during which the license is in effect.
(f)
No license shall be issued to any person that has not attained the age of 21 years of age.
(g)
The governing authority may decline to grant a license to any person who has had any license issued by the County revoked within the two years prior to the filing of an application as permitted by this chapter.
(h)
The governing authority may decline to issue a license to any person that has violated any part of this chapter within one year prior to the filing of the application for such license.
(i)
The governing authority may decline to grant a license when, after reasonable effort, the county sheriff, or any of his or her deputies, is unable to verify any statement or information required to be disclosed in the application.
(j)
Any person or entity who is issued a license shall have and continuously maintain a registered agent, upon whom any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter may be served upon. The registered agent shall be a resident of the state, and the licensee shall maintain with the county clerk the current name, address and telephone number of the registered agent.
(k)
No license shall be issued to any person until such person provides the county clerk with proof of current commercial general liability insurance, covering the business and/or premises seeking the license, in the form of a true and correct insurance declarations page. Such proof must be provided annually to the county clerk.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-6. - Each location to have a separate license; transferability of license; fee required to change business owner, manager, partner, shareholder or business name.
(a)
An applicant who will operate more than one premises shall complete a separate application for each premises and shall pay all applicable license fees for each premises. Each application shall be accompanied by a survey from a registered land surveyor showing that the premises is not within the prohibited distance from any school, school ground, church building, alcoholic treatment center or public housing.
(b)
Each license shall be personal to the licensee and the licensed premises. No license shall be transferable, unless specifically authorized by this chapter.
(c)
In the case of the death of a licensee, the deceased licensee's license shall be the property of his, her, or its heirs or personal representatives, as the case may be, and the business or premises may continue to sell alcoholic beverages for a period of 45 days from the date of death of deceased licensee or until expiration of deceased licensee's license or until issuance of a new licensee, whichever occurs first, and upon such occurrence authorization to sell under the deceased licensee's license shall terminate by operation of law.
(d)
If 50 percent or more of a licensee is to be sold or transferred to a person other than any existing partner, shareholder or member, then such licensed entity shall apply for a new license as set forth herein and pay all license costs and fees, if such licensee desires to be authorized, or continue to be authorized, to sell alcoholic beverages as permitted by this chapter.
(e)
Any licensee who proposes to change the location of the licensed premises within the unincorporated area of the county shall so notify the county clerk in writing at least 30 days prior to any proposed change of location. The notice of proposed change of location shall be accompanied by a survey as required by subsection (a) of this section. A licensee's business shall not change location unless the new location complies with all provisions of this chapter. The governing authority shall have the authority to approve or deny the change of location in the same manner that it may approve or deny the issuance of a new license. An investigative fee of $200.00 shall be paid by the licensee at the time that a notice of change of location is made. If a change of location is approved, with no change of ownership of the business, the license fee paid for the old location shall be applied to the new location.
(f)
Any licensee who desires to change the manager or name of the licensed business or premises shall so notify the county clerk in writing at least 30 days prior to any change of manager or name, and the county clerk shall direct the issuance of a corrected license in replacement of the original license, or, in the case of a change of manager, such licensee shall ftle with the county clerk such information about the new manager as the county clerk may require. A fee of $25.00 shall be paid by the licensee at the time that such notice is given for the change of manager or name of such business or premises.
(g)
Except as provided in this section, any change in the ownership or control of any licensee shall be cause to revoke any license issued under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-7. - Issuance of licenses subject to state law and code.
(a)
No license shall be issued to any elected official of the county or any employee of the county, nor shall any such person have any whole, partial or beneficial interest in any license.
(b)
No license shall be issued in connection with any premises that fails to meet:
(1)
All applicable fire, life safety, building and health laws, rules and regulations under state law or the Code, and
(2)
Any applicable zoning ordinances and regulations of the Code.
(c)
No license shall be issued to any applicant who is delinquent in the payment of county taxes or fees, including, but not limited to property taxes, alcoholic beverage excise taxes, business license fees, occupational taxes, or any other tax or fee which is owed to the county by the applicant.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-8. - License may issue; sales prohibited in incomplete premises.
Where a person applies for a license in connection with any premises that is not yet in existence or completed, a license may be issued for such premises; provided that, the plans for such premises comply with this chapter and the Code. No sales shall be allowed in such premises until it has been completed in accordance with such plans, a certificate of occupancy issued, and it has been inspected by the proper state and/or county officials, including but not limited to the county manager, county administrator, county building inspector, county code enforcement officer or the county sheriff, or any of his or her deputies, to ensure compliance with this chapter and the Code.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-9. - Expiration of license for failure to commence or operate business.
(a)
All licensed premises must open for business within six months after issuance of the license. Failure to do so shall serve as a ground for revocation of such license by the governing authority, unless an extension of time is granted by the county manager or administrator before the expiration of the six-month period, for good cause shown by the licensee. No refund of the license fee shall be made in the event of a revocation hereunder.
(b)
Any licensee who begins operating the business or premises authorized in the license, but who thereafter ceases operation for six months within any calendar year, shall be subject to revocation of his license by the governing authority, unless an extension of time is granted by the county manager or administrator before the expiration of the six-month period, for good cause shown by the licensee. No refund of license fee shall be made in the event of a revocation hereunder.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-10. - Expiration and renewal.
(a)
All licenses shall expire on December 31 of each year. A licensee who desires to renew the license shall file an application, with the requisite fees as set forth in this chapter, with the county clerk on the form provided for renewal of the license for the ensuing year. All applications for renewal will be reviewed by the county clerk. A licensee does not have a right to automatic renewal and must be compliant with this chapter when applying for the renewal of his or her license. Applications for renewal must be filed on or before November 30 of each year; otherwise, a penalty equal to 20 percent of the annual license fee shall be imposed. No renewal license shall be granted after December 31, but such application shall be treated as an initial application, and the applicant shall be required to comply with all rules and regulations for the granting of licenses as if no previous license had been issued to licensee.
(b)
Except for temporary licenses or permits that may be granted pursuant to this chapter, all licenses granted under this chapter shall be for the full calendar year or for the number of months (or portion thereof) remaining in the calendar year. License fees shall not be prorated for any reason, and license fees shall not be refundable for any reason.
(c)
Failure to furnish complete information in the renewal application will delay the issuance of the license. The failure to furnish complete information within 30 days after being requested to do so by the county clerk may be grounds to deny the renewal application.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-11. - Distance requirement.
(a)
No license shall be issued for the sale of any distilled spirits in or within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus.
(b)
No license shall be issued for the sale of malt beverage(s) and/or wine by the package for a location that is within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds or college campus (the preceding regulation shall not apply to any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 1981, nor to the renewal of such license, nor shall the preceding regulation apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of malt beverage(s) and/or wine was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application); provided that, nothing in the preceding regulation shall prohibit a grocery store licensed for the retail sale of only malt beverages and/or wine for consumption off the premises from selling malt beverage(s) and/or wine within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus, where so permitted by resolution or ordinance of the county or municipality (as used in this subparagraph, the term "grocery store" means a retail establishment which has a total retail floor space of at least 10,000 square feet of which at least 85 percent is reserved for the sale of food and other nonalcoholic items, conducts all of its sales inside the building containing its retail floor space, and meets such other criteria as may be required by the governing authority.
(c)
No license shall be issued for the sale of malt beverage(s) and/or wine by the package for a location that is within 100 yards of any alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or by any county or municipal government (the preceding regulation shall not apply to any business having a license in effect on July 1, 1981).
(d)
No license shall be issued for the sale any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property (the preceding regulation shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 2000, nor to the renewal of such license, nor shall the preceding regulation apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately prior to such application; and as used in the preceding regulation, the term "housing authority property" means any property containing 300 housing units or fewer owned or operated by a housing authority created by Article 1 of Chapter 3 of Title 8, the "Housing Authorities Law," O.C.G.A.).
(e)
For purposes of this chapter, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-12. - Grant of license.
(a)
A license shall be issued for a calendar year and shall expire on December 31 of the year for which issued regardless of the date issued, and there shall be no rebate, proration or refund of any annual license fee.
(b)
The fee(s) for any license issued pursuant to this chapter shall be in the amount as determined and set by the governing authority pursuant to resolution or ordinance, and any and all such fees shall be tendered to the county clerk upon submission of the application, in the form of certified funds or cash.
(c)
A license may be issued only on written application, and the county clerk shall provide the application form required by this chapter, which shall include but not be limited to the applicant's full name, address, telephone number. The fee for a license shall be tendered upon submission of the application to the county clerk.
(d)
Each application for a license may be issued a license only by the governing authority while in session. Each application for a license shall not be considered until:
(1)
Notice of the application has been posted by the county clerk at the courthouse door of Tattnall County for at least ten days prior to the governing authority's consideration of the application, and
(2)
The county sheriff, or any of his or her deputies, has reviewed the application and given a report to the governing authority regarding the applicant and location proposed by the application.
Only the initial application for a new license shall be required to post the notice required by this chapter. Yearly renewals of a license, which include the same licensee and location, do not require the posting of notice.
(e)
Where an application is issued and a license issued in connection therewith, such license shall be issued for only one licensee and location, and such license shall not be transferrable either as to the location or licensee, except as may be otherwise permitted by this chapter.
(f)
A licensee shall not have any vested rights in a license, and a license shall not be renewable as a matter of right. Applications must be made annually for the governing authority's consideration if a licensee desires to continue operation from year to year. Each application shall be considered as a new application, except insofar as the requirements of posting a notice may be concerned, even though the applicant may have a license for the same location for the previous year.
(g)
The standards to be applied by the governing authority, acting in its judicial capacity to grant or deny a license, shall include whether, in the best judgment of the governing authority, the applicant, based on all information obtained in the application process, possesses the qualities of sound judgment and discretion necessary for one who dispenses alcoholic beverages to the public generally.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-13. - License and certain code provisions to be posted.
Each licensee shall display all licenses issued to such licensee prominently and at all times on the premises for which all such licenses are issued.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-14. - Inspection of licensed business or premises by the sheriff.
Any code enforcement officer of the county or the county sheriff, or any of his or her deputies shall have the authority to inspect any business or premises licensed under this chapter during the hours in which the business or premises is open for business. These inspections shall be made for the purpose of verifying compliance with the requirements of this chapter and state law.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-15. - Waiting period before re-application for license.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, an unsuccessful applicant for a license shall not be authorized to re-apply for a license until 180 days have elapsed from the date that the governing authority's decision to deny such license becomes final. Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, a licensee whose license is revoked by governing authority shall not be eligible to re-apply for a license until one year has elapsed from the date that the governing authority's decision to revoke such license becomes final.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-16. - Application of chapter provisions to minors generally.
(a)
No person under 18 years of age shall be employed in or about the premises of any person licensed to sell alcoholic beverages.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a person under 21 years of age to attempt to purchase an alcoholic beverage or for any person to purchase an alcoholic beverage for a person under 21 years of age.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for a person under 21 years of age to exhibit faked, forged, or borrowed credentials in an effort to obtain an alcoholic beverage to which he is not legally entitled.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for a person under 21 years of age, other than an employee employed by a licensed person, to have an open container of any alcoholic beverage in his possession.
(e)
It shall be a violation for any licensee not to require and properly analyze the identification of a person to ensure that any person under 21 years of age is not sold, served, or has in his possession any alcoholic beverage while in a licensed business or premises; provided that, a licensed employee working in a licensed business or premises may have in his possession any alcoholic beverage for the purpose of selling or serving such alcoholic beverages. The term "identification" as used in this section, shall mean any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, a photograph, and giving such person's date of birth, and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license or state identification card.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-17. - Acts prohibited by licensee generally.
No licensee shall do any of the following upon the licensed business or premises:
(1)
Sell or distribute any alcoholic beverage to a person under 21 years of age;
(2)
Sell or distribute any alcoholic beverage to any person while such person is in an intoxicated condition;
(3)
Sell or distribute any alcoholic beverage on any day or at any time when such sale is prohibited by law; or
(4)
Allow any person to consume any alcoholic beverage on the premises of a person not licensed for the retail sale of any alcoholic beverage by drink for consumption on the premises only.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-18. - Purchases from and sales to person other than licensees.
No licensed retail dealer shall purchase any alcoholic beverage from any person other than a licensed wholesale dealer. No licensed wholesale dealer shall sell any alcoholic beverages to any person other than a licensed retail dealer.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-19. - Unlawful conduct on licensed premises.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for a licensee, his agent or employee to permit any disturbance of the peace, any obscenity, or any lewd, immoral or improper behavior, conduct or practices in the licensed business or premises, or to operate the licensed business or premises in such a manner as to create a nuisance.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any licensee, his agent or employee to fail to report to the county sheriff any disturbance which occurs immediately outside his business or premises, or in the parking facilities of his business or premises, of which he has knowledge.
(c)
It shall be the duty and responsibility of the licensee, his agent, or employee to report to the county sheriff any usage or possession of illegal drugs or narcotics and to report any unlawful conduct of any nature which occurs on the licensed premises, of which he has knowledge.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any licensee, his agent or employee, to fail to assist any law enforcement officer in the performance of the officer's duties while the officer is in or on a licensed business or premises.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-20. - Open area and patio sales.
Consumption or sale of alcoholic beverages in open areas or on decks or patios is not permitted unless a licensee's business or premises is licensed for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises and such licensee complies with the following regulations:
(1)
The open area, patio or deck must be enclosed in a manner that directs ingress and egress by the public through a single entrance only through the licensed business or premises (the purpose of this requirement is to prevent a customer from leaving the open area, patio or deck with an alcoholic beverage without the licensee's knowledge).
(2)
The open area, patio or deck shall be enclosed by a structure at least 42 inches in height as measured from the floor of the open area, patio or deck, and such structure must meet all building code requirements of the county.
(3)
Any licensee desiring to obtain approval for open area, patio or deck sales must submit to the county a complete set of plans that will be reviewed and approved on an individual basis by appropriate county officials and/or employee(s), and all plans and construction pursuant to such plans shall meet the requirements of the Code, as well as all applicable state health, life safety and fire code. Only upon satisfactory completion of a final inspection will the county approve of any open area, patio or deck sales.
(4)
Any structure or area set aside for open area, patio or deck sales shall not contain a number of square feet that represents an amount greater than 40 percent of the existing seating area of the licensed business or premises.
(5)
Loudspeakers or other amplifiers of sound shall not be permitted upon the open areas, patios or decks of any licensed business or premises.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-21. - Open containers and brown bag establishments prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink any alcoholic beverage or other intoxicating beverage at any public place within the unincorporated area of county, or for any person to have in his or her possession any unsealed or opened container with such intoxicating beverage in it at any public place within the unincorporated area of the county, except as may be otherwise permitted by this chapter or the Code.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or possess any alcoholic beverage upon real property of the county that is used as a public park, playground or school.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink any alcoholic beverage in any automobile on the roads or streets of the county, whether such automobile is moving or parked.
(d)
Brown bag establishments shall be prohibited within the unincorporated area of the county.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-22. - Signs and advertising.
(a)
No outdoor advertising promoting the sale or consumption of alcoholic beverages shall be permitted on the exterior of any business or premises that is licensed for wholesale or retail sale of alcoholic beverages or any business or premises that is licensed for the sale of alcoholic beverages for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises.
(b)
Any indoor sign or display promoting the sale or consumption of alcoholic beverages may be visible from the exterior of any business or premises that is licensed for wholesale or retail sale of alcoholic beverages or any business or premises that is licensed for the sale of alcoholic beverages for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises, subject to the limitations as otherwise set forth in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-23. - Happy hour promotions.
No
licensee, or his or her agents or employees, shall engage in any of the following practices in connection with the sale or other disposition of alcoholic beverages for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises:
(1)
Advertising "Happy Hour" through any form of media, including, but not limited to, newspapers, magazines, electronics, radio, television and internet, or
(2)
Outdoor advertising or signs promoting "Happy Hour" on the exterior of any licensed premises or on the windows or elsewhere on the licensed business or premises that may be viewed from the outside.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-24. - Visibility.
(a)
The business or premises of each licensee shall have a clearly marked point(s) of entry.
(b)
The business or premises of each licensee shall have point(s) of entry that are clearly visible from a public street; however, this requirement shall not apply to hotels or where the point of entry of the licensed business or premises is in a shopping center or business building.
(c)
The business or premises of each licensee shall ensure that, at all points of entry, there are clear windows and doors. Further, the business or premises of each licensee must maintain clear windows and doors for any cashier's cage within the premises, or other internally enclosed space.
(d)
The business or premises of each licensee, including those entirely enclosed in another building, such as a restaurant located in a hotel, must keep all windows and doors free of visual obstructions so that the interior of such business or premises is clearly visible from the exterior. No tinting, shades, curtains, blinds or other devices shall be used to obstruct the view into such business or premises, but tinting, shades, curtains, blinds, and other devices may be used to reduce sun glare so long as such measures do not impede visual access to the business or premises.
(e)
The business or premises of each licensee shall operate with an adequate level of internal lighting, so as to ensure visual access from the exterior and safety to law enforcement or other emergency responders, 24 hours a day, seven days a week. External lighting cannot be solely used to meet this requirement.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-25. - Completeness, denial and re-submittal of application.
(a)
No license application shall be considered until the county clerk deems it complete as set forth in this chapter.
(b)
If a license application is denied, the applicant for such license cannot apply for another license for a period of 180 days from the date of the denial.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)

All licensees shall maintain the following records for a period of three years from the issuance of any license and make available the original records, or certified copies thereof, to the county manager or administrator for the purpose of auditing:

Monthly income or operating statements;
(2)
Daily sales receipts and invoices showing liquor, beer, malt beverages and wine separately;
(3)
Daily cash register receipts;
(4)
Monthly Georgia Sales and Use Tax Reports; and
(5)
Federal Income Tax Returns with all Form 1099s.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)

Tuesday through Saturday: from 2:00 a.m. until 6:00 a.m.; and

Sunday: 12:01 a.m. until 10:59 a.m.

A licensed retail dealer of beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits by the drink for beverages purposes for consumption on the premises only shall not sell, give away or otherwise distribute any of the foregoing alcoholic beverages during the following time periods:

Monday: from 12:01 a.m. until 10:00 a.m.;

Tuesday through Saturday: from 2:00 a.m. until 10:00 a.m.; and

(a)
A retail dealer of beer, malt beverages and/or wine by the package may apply for and obtain a Sunday sales license for the retail sale of beer, malt beverages and/or wine by the package upon due qualification for and payment for such license, as set forth in this chapter.
(b)
A retail dealer of beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises may apply for and obtain a Sunday sales license for the retail sale of beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises, upon the occurrence of the following:
(1)
Due qualification for and payment for such license, as set forth in this chapter; and
(2)
Showing to the satisfaction of the county manager or administrator, or his or her designee, that such retail dealer derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishments where food is served and in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-29. - Denial of application and suspension or revocation of license.
(a)
Unless otherwise permitted by law or this chapter, no license which has been applied for shall be denied, and no license which has been issued shall be suspended or revoked, except for any ground, cause, good cause or due cause as hereinafter defined.
(b)
The terms "ground", "cause", "good cause" or "due cause" (hereafter these terms may be collectively referred to as "ground") for the purposes of this chapter shall include, but not be limited to:
(1)
Violation of any provision of this chapter;
(2)
Conviction of any felony by the licensee or any employee of licensee or any person holding an interest in the license;
(3)
Knowing falsification of any fact given in an application for a license or bearing upon the applicant's or licensee's qualification for a license;
(4)
Failure or refusal to meet or maintain any requirement or standard prescribed by this chapter as a condition to or qualification for the granting of a license;
(5)
Violation of the specified days or hours of operation or alcohol sales as set out in this chapter;
(6)
Selling or dispensing alcoholic beverages during a period of suspension;
(7)
Where the licensee's business or premises does not meet the licensing requirements of this chapter;
(8)
Littering, loitering or public consumption of alcohol in the area of the licensed business or premises;
(9)
Any other factor known to or discovered by the governing authority whereby it is shown that the licensee, any employee of licensee or any person holding any interest in a license, has engaged in conduct at or involving the licensed business or premises, or has permitted conduct on the licensed premises, that constitutes a violation of federal or state law, local ordinance or administrative regulations involving alcoholic beverages, gambling or narcotics. With respect to this subsection, it may be inferred that the violation was done with the knowledge or consent of the licensee; provided that such inference may be rebutted; and
(10)
A licensed premises fails to open for operation or business within six months after the granting of the license.
(c)
The governing authority shall provide a written statement of ground(s) to the applicant or licensee, as the case may be, and the governing authority shall provide such statement by personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the applicant or licensee, or the registered agent of the licensee. Within ten days of the giving of such statement, the applicant or licensee shall have the right to request a hearing on the statement, and the applicant or licensee shall do so by filing a written hearing request with the county clerk. Upon receipt by the county clerk of the hearing request, a hearing shall be scheduled within 20 days, and the applicant or licensee, or the registered agent of the licensee, shall be notified in writing, at least five days before such hearing, of the date, time, and place of the hearing (the governing authority's notice of hearing of hearing shall be served by personal service or certified mail, return receipt requested, to the applicant or license, of the registered agent of the licensee). The hearing shall be before the governing authority, and at the hearing the applicant or licensee shall have the right to appear in person and by attorney and to have witnesses appear on the applicant's or licensee's behalf and to cross examine opposing witnesses and present evidence. The witnesses shall testify under oath, but rules of evidence shall not apply.
(d)
In the event that the governing authority determines that there exists any ground for the denial of an application, the governing authority shall deny such application.
(e)
In the event that the governing authority determines that there exists any ground for the suspension of a license, a period of license suspension shall be imposed by the governing authority for a term not to exceed 30 days.
(f)
In the event that the governing authority determines that there exists any ground for the revocation of a license, the revocation of a license by the governing authority shall be for the remaining calendar year of the year in which the revocation is imposed.
(g)
The governing authority's decision regarding the denial of an application or the suspension or revocation of a license shall be personally served or mailed by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the applicant or licensee, or the registered agent of the licensee, within five business days of the close of the hearing. The decision of the governing authority shall constitute final action by the governing authority, subject to review on appeal pursuant to applicable Georgia law.
(h)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, upon the licensee's receipt of any statement of grounds, the licensee may waive the right to a hearing and consent to any sanction permitted herein, or as otherwise may be permitted by the Code. Any consent entered under this chapter shall be in the form of a written decree, executed by the licensee and the governing authority, and such decree shall be considered final action and non-appealable.
(i)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, upon the revocation of a licensee's state alcohol or alcoholic beverage license, the licensee's license issued under this chapter shall be immediately revoked by operation of law. The sheriff of Tattnall County or his deputies, upon notice of such revocation, shall take the necessary steps to ensure the immediate cessation of the licensee's sale of alcoholic beverages.
(j)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, the governing authority may exercise any other remedy at law or equity to enjoin a license or licensee, temporarily or permanently, in connection with any exigency that arises involving the licensed premises or location, where the governing authority finds that action is required for the protection of the public health, safety, or welfare of citizens of Tattnall County.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-30. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter shall be punishable by a fine not exceed a fine of $1,000.00, or six months' imprisonment, or both; provided that, the judge shall probate not less than 120 days of any sentence imposed, except as otherwise provided by general law, and shall not exceed the maximum punishment specified by the this chapter. In the event a sentence is revoked, a defendant shall not serve more than 60 days in the county jail.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-31. - Additional penalty for violation.
(a)
In addition to any other punishment or penalty set forth in this chapter, unless prohibited by law, any person who violates any provision of this chapter may be cited for violating any part of this chapter and tried by the court of competent jurisdiction in the county, and upon conviction for any such violation, be punished as authorized by or provided for in the Code.
(b)
Notwithstanding any other penalty or remedy authorized by this chapter or law, continuing violations of this chapter are hereby further declared to be a public nuisance, harmful to the public health, safety, and welfare, and are subject to abatement or injunction by the court of competent jurisdiction in the county.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)

ARTICLE II. - SALE OF BEER, MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE

Sec. 10-66. - Authority to sell; license required.
A license for a retail dealer or wholesale dealer for the sale of beer, malt beverages and/or wine may be granted by the governing authority upon the terms and conditions as provided in this chapter. Further, a license for the retail sale of beer, malt beverages and/or wine for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises may be granted by the governing authority upon the terms and conditions as provided in this chapter. No person will be issued a license for the sale of beer, malt beverages and/or wine, where the same is sold in the original packages, and a license for the sale of beer, malt beverages and/or wine for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises at the same location.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-67. - License fees.
Before any license permitted under this article is granted by the governing authority, the applicant shall comply with all regulations adopted by the governing authority applicable to such licenses and pay the applicable license fee by certified bank check in an amount set by resolution or ordinance by the governing authority and maintained as a public document by the county clerk.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-68. - Excise taxes on beer and malt beverages.
(a)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon the sale of beer and malt beverages within the unincorporated area of the county a specific excise tax as follows:
(1)
where beer and malt beverages are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons; and
(2)
where beer and malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(b)
The excise taxes provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the licensed wholesale dealer in beer and malt beverages. Each wholesale dealer selling beer and malt beverages within the unincorporated area of the county shall file a report with the county by the 10th day of each month showing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of beer and malt beverages, by size and type of container, sold during the month within the unincorporated area of the county. Each wholesale dealer shall remit to the county clerk on the 10th day of the month after the calendar month in which such sales were made the amount of excise tax due in accordance with this section.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-69. - Excise taxes on wine.
(a)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon the sale of wine within the unincorporated area of the county, a specific excise tax in the amount of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(b)
The taxes imposed by this section shall not be levied with respect to:
(1)
Wine sold to and used by established and recognized churches and synagogues for use in sacramental services only;
(2)
Any sale of wine which is exempt from taxation by the state under the Constitution of the United States;
(3)
Wine sold to persons outside the state for resale or consumption outside the state; or
(4)
Wine which contains less than one-half of one percent alcohol.
(c)
The excise taxes as provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and paid by the licensed wholesale dealer in wine in the unincorporated area of the county, and each wholesale dealer of wine within the unincorporated area of the county shall file a report with the county by the 10th day of each month showing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of wine, by size and type of container, sold during the month within the unincorporated area of the county. Each wholesale dealer shall remit to the county on the 10th day of the month after the calendar month in which such sales were made the amount of excise tax due in accordance with the manner or procedure described in section 10-68.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-70. - Sale without license; separate application and separate license required for each premises.
Beer, malt beverages and/or wine shall not be distributed, sold or possessed for the purposes of sale without a license granted by the governing authority. A separate application must be made, and a separate license must be obtained to sell beer, malt beverages and/or wine for each premises where beer, malt beverages and/or wine is sold at retail or wholesale, or where beer, malt beverages and/or wine is sold at retail for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-71. - Limitation regarding licenses for retail sale for consumption on the premises only.
A license for the retail sale of beer, malt beverages and/or wine by the drink for beverage purposes for consumption on the premises only shall be issued only in conjunction with a restaurant, hotel or motel, or a publicly owned facility, as defined in Chapter 8 of Title 3, O.C.G.A.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-72. - Reporting requirements for restaurants.
(a)
Where a license is issued for the retail sale of beer, malt beverages and/or wine by the drink for beverage purposes for consumption on the premises only, in conjunction with a restaurant, the licensee of such license shall submit quarterly written reports to the county clerk, as more particularly set forth herein.
(b)
Every report shall be post marked on or before the last day of the calendar month after the calendar quarter.
(c)
Every report shall be in a form recognized and accepted by certified public accountants and shall utilize common and generally recognized accounting principles and shall be submitted under oath. False information in any report may result in the prosecution under O.C.G.A. § 16-10-20 by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(d)
Every report shall clearly indicate all sales receipts of the restaurant for food and alcoholic beverages, and for reporting purposes, food shall be defined as all edible substances appropriate for human consumption as detennined by the health department inspecting restaurants as sold or provided to the public in a restaurant in the unincorporated area of the county. Food shall not be construed to include olives, cherries, limes, lemons, salt, pepper, pineapples, celery, tomato juice, or any other fruit or vegetable, or any dairy product such as ice cream, cream, yogurt or milk, or any soft drink, water, tonic water or non-alcoholic carbonated or non-carbonated beverages, when such items are used in any alcoholic beverage or mixed drink as an essential part of the beverage, mixed drink, or as a garnish thereon.
(e)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, if any report of a restaurant shows that its sales receipts for food comprise less than 50 percent of a restaurant's total gross sales receipts in any quarter of a calendar year, then, thereafter, the governing authority may consider such event as grounds for suspension or revocation.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-73. - Wholesaler's delivery to licensed retail dealer; marking of vehicles.
No beer, malt beverages and/or wine shall be delivered to any licensed retail dealer except by a licensed wholesale dealer, who shall make such deliveries by a vehicle owned by the wholesaler, with wholesaler's name clearly marked on the outside of the vehicle.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-74. - Machines operated for amusement purposes in licensed retail businesses.
No person with a license for the retail sale of beer, malt beverages or wine shall permit on his premises any coin operated amusement machine, including but not limited to a slot machine, mechanical music box or pinball machine, or any form of electronic or mechanical game machine or coin-operated device, which might be used for entertainment or amusement purposes, except pursuant to applicable state law.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)

Sec. 10-113. - Application for license to be accompanied by sworn statement regarding additional information.
(a)
All applications for licenses under this article, both original and renewal, must be accompanied by a full and complete statement under oath of information describing any and all interests, as defined by state law, in the person licensed or to be licensed. This information shall include the names and addresses of any person who has any ownership interest in the person licensed or to be licensed and the size of such interest; the specific street address of any other premises which is authorized for retail sale of alcoholic beverages by the package in which the person licensed or to be licensed has any ownership interest; detailed description of the ownership or leasehold of premises where the person licensed or to be licensed is or will be operating, including but not limited to an executed copy of the vesting deed or lease; and the name and address of any owner and lessor of the premises in which the person licensed or to be licensed is or will be operating.
(b)
Any change in the information required in subsection (a) must be filed with the county clerk when the change occurs, and failure to do so within a period of 30 days of such change shall be grounds for denial, suspension or revocation, as applicable under the circumstances, by the governing authority.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-114. - Places where retail sales permitted.
(a)
No distilled spirits by the package shall be sold at retail except in:
(1)
Retail businesses or premises in which space has been set aside and devoted substantially to the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package, with ingress and egress provided directly to, and only to, the exterior of the buildings in which the business or premises is located and not any other enclosed part of the building in which such business or premises is located, except as provided hereafter; and
(2)
Hotels, motels and office buildings where:
a.
Every public entrance to such business or premises shall be from a lobby, hallway or other interior portion of the primary use structure; and
b.
No show window, advertising or display shall be visible from the exterior of the primary use structure.
(b)
Each license for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package shall have conspicuously displayed within the interior of his business or premises not less than four copies of a printed price list of the distilled spirits offered for sale, provided that a licensee, in lieu of having four copies of a printed price list, may have the prices placed on the bottles or on the front of the shelf where distilled spirits are exhibited for sale.
(c)
No exterior signs may be used to advertise the price of any distilled spirits offered for sale at the business or premises of a person licensed for retail sale of distilled spirits by the package.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-115. - Machines operated for amusement purposes in licensed retail businesses.
No person with a license for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package shall permit on his premises any coin operated amusement machine, including but not limited to a slot machine, mechanical music box or pinball machine, or any form of electronic or mechanical game machine or coin-operated device, which might be used for entertainment or amusement purposes, except pursuant to applicable state law.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-116. - Signs and display of license.
(a)
Signs advertising or promoting the sale of distilled spirits by the package are permitted within the unincorporated area of the County in accordance with this chapter and as otherwise permitted in the Code.
(b)
The license for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package shall be conspicuously displayed on a front window or entrance of the licensed premises, with the name of the licensee, together with the inscription "Tattnall County License for Retail Sale of Distilled Spirits by the Package, No _____.
(c)
The license shall at all times be kept in plain view within the licensed premises or business.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-117. - Persons with financial interest in wholesale dealer license; wholesaler's delivery to licensed retail dealer; marking of vehicles.
(a)
No person who has any financial interest in a wholesale dealer license for the sale of distilled spirits shall be issued any license for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package.
(b)
No distilled spirits shall be delivered to any licensed retail dealer in the unincorporated area of the county except by a licensed wholesale dealer, who shall make such deliveries by a vehicle owned by the wholesaler, with the wholesaler's name clearly marked on the outside of the vehicle.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Secs. 10-118—10-145. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - SALE OF DISTILLED SPIRITS FOR BEVERAGE PURPOSES BY DRINK FOR CONSUMPTION ONLY ON PREMISES

Sec. 10-146. - Authority to sell; license required.
A license for the retail sale of distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises may be granted by the governing authority upon the terms and conditions as provided in this article.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-147. - License fees.
Before any license permitted under this article is granted by the governing authority, the applicant shall comply with all regulations adopted by the governing authority applicable to such license and pay the applicable license fee by certified bank check in an amount set by resolution or ordinance by the governing authority and maintained as a public document by the county clerk.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-148. - Separate application and separate license required for each premises.
A separate application must be made, and a separate license must be obtained, for the retail sale of distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises for each premises where distilled spirits are sold at retail for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-149. - Consumption sales only.
Persons issued a license for retail sale of distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises shall not be licensed to sell distilled spirits by the package or bottle.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-150. - Limitation regarding licenses for retail sale of distilled spirits by the drink for beverage purposes for consumption on premises only.
A license for the retail sale of distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises shall be issued only in conjunction with a restaurant, hotel or motel, or a publicly owned facility, as defined by Chapter 8 of Title 3, O.C.G.A.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-151. - Reporting requirements for restaurants.
(a)
Where a license is issued for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the drink for beverage purposes for consumption on the premises only, in conjunction with a restaurant, the licensee of such license shall submit quarterly written reports to the county clerk, as more particularly set forth herein.
(b)
Every report shall be post marked on or before the last day of the calendar month after the calendar quarter.
(c)
Every report shall be in a form recognized and accepted by certified public accountants and shall utilize common and generally recognized accounting principles and shall be submitted under oath. False information in any report may result in the prosecution under O.C.G.A. § 16-10-20 by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(d)
Every report shall clearly indicate all sales receipts of the restaurant for food and alcoholic beverages, and for reporting purposes, food shall be defined as all edible substances appropriate for human consumption as determined by the health department inspecting restaurants as sold or provided to the public in a restaurant in the unincorporated area of the county. Food shall not be construed to include olives, cherries, limes, lemons, salt, pepper, pineapples, celery, tomato juice, or any other fruit or vegetable, or any dairy product such as ice cream, cream, yogurt or milk, or any soft drink, water, tonic water or non-alcoholic carbonated or non-carbonated beverages, when such items are used in any alcoholic beverage or mixed drink as an essential part of the beverage, mixed drink, or as a garnish thereon.
(e)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, if any report of a restaurant shows that its sales receipts for food comprised less than 50 percent of a restaurant's total gross sales receipts in any quarter of a calendar year, then, thereafter, the governing authority may consider such event as grounds for suspension or revocation.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-152. - Machines operated for amusement purposes in licensed retail businesses.
No person with a license for the retail sale of distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises shall permit on his or her premises any coin operated amusement machine, including but not limited to a slot machine, mechanical music box or pinball machine, or any form of electronic or mechanical game machine or coin-operated device, which might be used for entertainment or amusement purposes, except pursuant to applicable state law.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-153. - Wholesaler's delivery to licensed retail dealer; marking of vehicles.
No distilled spirits shall be delivered to any licensed retail dealer of distilled spirits for beverage purposes by the drink for consumption only on the premises, except by a licensed wholesale dealer of distilled spirits, who shall make such deliveries by a vehicle owned by the wholesaler, with wholesaler's name clearly marked on the outside of the vehicle.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Secs. 10-154—10-185. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - FARM WINERIES AND TASTING ROOMS

Sec. 10-186. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where otherwise defined or the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Farm winery and tasting room shall be defined according to O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1, as amended.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-187. - Regulations.
(a)
Farm winery licenses are subject to proper licensing from the state, and farm wineries must be in compliance with all applicable federal and state rules and regulations, including O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1, as amended, and Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. 560-2-10-.01., as amended.
(b)
Unless otherwise specified in this Code or required by law, a farm winery and tasting room is subject to all rules and regulations of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
Sec. 10-188. - Taxes.
(a)
Farm winery excise tax paid to the county will be $0.22 per liter or $0.83 per gallon. The excise taxes shall be paid on or before the 20th of each month along with a copy of the completed ATT-62 form required by the state.
(b)
Farm wineries are exempt from the requirement that wine possessed, sold, or held for sale at retail in the county be purchased from wholesalers licensed to make sales in the county.
(Ord. No. 2022-3, § I, 7-11-2022; Ord. No. 23-1, § I, 1-9-2023)
State Law reference— License for manufacture and sale by farm wineries, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1; authorization to levy tax on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60.
Secs. 10-189—10-225. - Reserved.
