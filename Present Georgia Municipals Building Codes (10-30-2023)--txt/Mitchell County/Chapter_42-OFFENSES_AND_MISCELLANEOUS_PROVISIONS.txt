ARTICLE II. - ADDITIONAL PENALTIES

Sec. 42-19. - Jail Construction and Staffing Act.
The board of commissioners places into effect O.C.G.A. § 15-21-90 et seq., known as the Jail Construction and Staffing Act.
(Code 2004, § 46-31; Ord. of 11-13-1990, § 1)
Sec. 42-20. - Imposition.
(a)
Fines; bail or bond.
(1)
In every case in which the superior court of the county, state court of the county, probate court of the county or magistrate court of the county shall impose a fine, which shall be construed to include costs, for any offense against a criminal or traffic law of the state or any provision of this Code, there shall be imposed as an additional penalty a sum equal to ten percent of the original fine.
(2)
At the time of posting bail or bond in any case involving a violation of a criminal or traffic law of the state or ordinance of the county, an additional sum equal to ten percent of the original amount of the bail or bond shall be posted. In every case in which the superior court of the county, state court of the county, probate court of the county, or magistrate court of the county shall order the forfeiture of bail or bond, the additional sum equal to ten percent of the original bail or bond shall be paid over as provided in section 42-21.
(b)
Sums required by subsection (a) of this section shall be in addition to that amount required by O.C.G.A. § 47-17-60 to be paid into the peace officers annuity and benefit fund or O.C.G.A. § 47-11-51 concerning the judges of the probate court's retirement fund of the state.
(Code 2004, § 46-32; Ord. of 11-13-1990, § 2)
Sec. 42-21. - Collection.
(a)
The sums provided for in section 42-20 shall be assessed and collected by the clerk or court officer charged with the duty of collecting monies arising from fines and forfeited bonds and shall be paid over to the board of commissioners of the county by the tenth day of the month following the month in which such sums are collected. Such sums paid over to the board of commissioners shall be deposited by the board of commissioners into a special account to be known as the county jail fund.
(b)
Any person whose duty it is to collect and remit the sums provided for in section 42-20 who fails and refuses to remit such sums by the date required by this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished as provided by law.
(Code 2004, § 46-33; Ord. of 11-13-1990, § 3)
Sec. 42-22. - Use of monies collected.
Monies collected pursuant to this article and placed in the county jail fund shall be expended by the board of commissioners of the county, solely and exclusively for constructing, operating, and staffing county jails, county correctional institutions and detention facilities of the county, or for the purpose of contracting for such facilities with other counties, the state, municipalities or other political subdivisions as authorized by Ga. Const. art. IX, § III, ¶ I. The county jail fund and monies collected pursuant to this article to be placed in the county jail fund may be pledged as security for the payment of bonds issued for the construction of county jails, county correctional institutions and detention facilities of the county, or such other counties with whom it may have contracted.
(Code 2004, § 46-34; Ord. of 11-13-1990, § 4)
