ARTICLE II. - RECREATION AUTHORITY[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Establishment of park and recreation authority, O.C.G.A. §§ 36-64-3, 36-64-5.

Sec. 28-19. - Created; appointment; terms of directors; quorum.
(a)
There is created in and for the city a public body corporate and politic to be known as the recreation authority of the city, which shall consist of five directors to be appointed by the commission. At the time of the appointment of the first directors, the city commission shall appoint three directors for two years and two directors for one year and thereafter the terms of all directors shall be two years. If, at the end of any term of office of any director, a successor thereto has not been appointed, the director whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is so appointed.
(b)
A majority of the directors shall constitute a quorum. No action may be taken by the recreation authority without the affirmative vote of three directors of the recreation authority.
(Code 2000, § 54-31; Ord. No. 1227, § 3, 5-7-1990)
Sec. 28-20. - Directors; officers; compensation.
(a)
The directors of the recreation authority shall be taxpayers residing within the corporate limits of the city, and be appointed as provided in section 28-19. No director shall be an officer or employee of the city.
(b)
The directors shall elect one director as chairman and another as vice-chairman and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer.
(c)
The directors shall receive no compensation for their services but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(Code 2000, § 54-32(a)—(c); Ord. No. 1227, § 4, 5-7-1990)
Sec. 28-21. - Adoption of bylaws; delegation of powers and duties; perpetual existence of authority unless abolished.
The authority may make bylaws and regulations for its governance and may delegate to one or more of its officers, agents, and employees such powers and duties as may be deemed necessary and proper. The authority shall have perpetual existence until and unless abolished by proper resolution of the city commission.
(Code 2000, § 54-32(d); Ord. No. 1227, § 4, 5-7-1990)
Sec. 28-22. - Powers of recreation authority generally.
The recreation authority of the city shall have all of the powers necessary or convenient to carry out and effectuate the purposes and provisions of this article, including, but without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the power to:
(1)
Bring and defend actions.
(2)
Adopt and amend a corporate seal.
(3)
Provide, maintain, and conduct parks, playgrounds, recreation centers, and other recreational activities and facilities.
(4)
Maintain and equip parks, playgrounds, recreation centers, and the buildings thereon.
(5)
Develop, maintain and operate all types of recreation facilities.
(6)
Operate and conduct facilities on properties controlled by other authorities.
(7)
Employ playleaders, playground or community center directors, supervisors, recreation superintendents, or such other officers or employees as it deems are needed.
(8)
Develop a program of recreational activities and services designated to meet the various leisure time interests of all people.
(9)
Accept any grant or devise of real estate or any gift or bequest of money or other personal property or any donation, the principal or income of which is to be applied for either temporary or permanent use for playgrounds or recreation purposes. If the acceptance thereof for such purposes will subject the city to additional expense for improvements, maintenance, or renewal, the acceptance of any grant or devise of real estate shall be subject to the approval of the city commission. Money received for such purposes, unless otherwise provided by the terms of the gift or bequest, shall be deposited with the city clerk to the account of the recreation authority, and it shall be withdrawn and paid out by such body in the same manner as money appropriated for recreation purposes.
(10)
Appoint officers and retain agents, engineers, attorneys, fiscal agents, accountants, and employees and to provide for their compensation and duties.
(Code 2000, § 54-33; Ord. No. 1227, § 5, 5-7-1990)
State Law reference— Acceptance of grants, gifts, or donations; disbursement of moneys so received, O.C.G.A. § 36-64-6.

ARTICLE III. - PARK RULES AND REGULATIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 28-48. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Park means any parkland or open space (including buildings and property therein) owned by and/or under the jurisdiction and direction of the city.
Parking means the standing of any vehicle, whether occupied or not, upon any park road other than temporarily or while actively engaged in loading or unloading, or while standing in obedience to traffic signals or traffic regulators. Parking areas in the park are established for the convenience of patrons while using the facilities of the park.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, corporation or association of persons, and the singular number shall include the plural.
Recreation department means the governing city department for parks and recreation and individuals appointed by the department as its authorized agents.
Vehicle means every device in, upon, or by which a person or property is or may be transported.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-21)
Sec. 28-49. - Hours of operation.
(a)
City parks are open from 6:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m. No person shall be permitted to remain, stop, or park within the confines of any park between 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m., except in an emergency situation or with special written permission in their possession from the recreation department.
(b)
In emergency situations or when the public interest demands it, any portion of a park may be closed to the public or to designated persons until it is deemed prudent to allow readmission. The only exception to this regulation is the allowance for persons actively engaged in authorized sporting events utilizing artificial illumination to complete such events on tennis courts or sporting fields.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-22)
Sec. 28-50. - Permits.
(a)
A permit or written permission must be obtained from the recreation department for the following:
(1)
The use of playfields, tennis courts, or open areas for special or private use.
(2)
The vending of food and drink.
(b)
Requests and permits shall be processed and issued by the recreation department at Buford City Hall, 2300 Buford Highway, Buford, Georgia 30518. Participates shall indemnify the city for any injury or damages.
(c)
Permit applicants must be at least 18 years of age. All applications for permits must be received by the recreation department at least ten days prior to the date of the intended use. The signer of the permit shall be responsible for all damages to park facilities, grounds, or contents thereof and shall agree to indemnify the city for such damage.
(d)
All permits issued shall be in writing by the recreation director and shall be subject to park rules and regulations. A permit to do any act shall authorize the same insofar as it may be performed in strict accordance with the written terms and conditions thereof, and not in violation of any law, ordinance, rule, or regulation governing parks. Any violation of any term or condition thereof of any law, ordinance, rule, or regulation governing parks shall constitute grounds for revocation of any said permit by the recreation department, whose action therefor shall be final.
(e)
No person shall fail to produce and exhibit any permit which he/she claims to have, upon request of the recreation department or its authorized agent, or any official law enforcement officer representing the city, who shall inspect the same for the purpose of enforcing compliance with any ordinance or regulation. Failure to produce any permit upon request will nullify existence of said permit.
(f)
The applicant shall pay any associated fees at the time the permit is obtained. Such fees may vary according to the activity permitted and the facilities used.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-23)
Sec. 28-51. - Unlawful conduct or activities.
(a)
Preservation of property and natural features. No person shall injure, deface, or disturb any part of a park or any building, sign, equipment, or other property found therein; nor shall any tree, flower, rock, or other natural element be removed, injured, or destroyed.
(b)
No pets or animals of any kind. No animals or pets of any kind shall be permitted, except service animals that are specifically trained to aid a person with a disability as defined by O.C.G.A. § 30-4-2 and the Americans with Disabilities Act. (Official certified law enforcement service canines are exempt from this regulation.)
(c)
Hunting, trapping, fishing, or molesting wildlife. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 27-1-30, no person shall hunt, trap, fish, or molest any wildlife in the confines of any city park.
(d)
No firearms permitted. City park property has been designated as a school safety zone, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-11-127.1 et seq. by the city school district. Any person possessing a weapon in, on, or within 1,000 feet of this property shall be guilty of a misdemeanor or felony, as applicable.
(e)
No projectiles permitted. BB guns, pellet guns, air- or gas-powered guns, including paintball guns, crossbows or bows, or other projectile, missile-throwing devices are strictly prohibited in the confines of any city park.
(f)
Soliciting and unlawful sales. Commercial activities, including soliciting, and soliciting and/or selling merchandise without proper permit is prohibited. No person shall beg, hawk, or peddle within the confines of any city park without a proper permit or authorized written permission issued from an official agent or representative of the city.
(g)
Special events. No individual or organization shall conduct, sponsor, or promote any special event or other public activity in a city park without prior authorization of the city manager or his/her designee. Events or activities which, in the opinion of the recreation director, constitute hazards or dangers to the personal safety of participants or park visitors, endanger peace and good order in the parks, interfere with regular and normal use of the facilities and activities by park visitors, or are inconsistent with the use of the park or park purposes shall not be permitted. Erecting of structures, goals, nets, event tents, or similar on city park facilities is expressly prohibited without prior written consent of the director of recreation or his/her designee.
(h)
Noise. No person shall disturb other park users by causing excessive levels of noise.
(i)
Littering.
(1)
Littering in city parks is strictly prohibited. No person shall litter which includes, but is not limited to, any organic or inorganic waste, any animal or human excrement or feces, refuse, garbage, trash, hulls, peelings, debris, grass, weeds, ashes, sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, metal, plastic, glass container, broken glass, dead animals, or intentionally or unintentionally discarded material of every kind and description which is "hazardous waste" as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-51(6). All refuse shall be disposed in the proper trash receptacles provided for such purposes.
(2)
All park patrons shall use the proper designated restroom facilities, as provided in the park, and shall cooperate in maintaining the restrooms in a neat and sanitary condition.
(j)
Alcoholic beverages. The possession or consumption of alcoholic liquors or beverages in a city park shall not be permitted.
(k)
Tobacco products. The use of tobacco products in the confines of a city park is prohibited.
(l)
Bicycles, motorbikes and all-terrain vehicles.
(1)
Go-carts, all-terrain vehicles, skateboards, hover boards, and roller skates are prohibited in all areas of city parks.
(2)
No person shall operate a bicycle, motorcycle, motorbike or any other similar type vehicle upon sidewalks, grass, ball fields, walkways, tennis courts, playgrounds or picnic areas in any city park.
(3)
No unlicensed motorcycle, motorbike or other unlicensed vehicles shall be permitted in any area of city parks.
(4)
Riders of bicycles shall comply with all regulations and rules while operating in city parks. (Reference title 40, Uniform Rules of the Road, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-55 and 40-6-56.)
(5)
Bicycles shall be parked in places designated for such purposes.
(m)
Traffic.
(1)
No person shall drive any automobile, motorcycle, motorbike, or other vehicle in any area of any park except on the proper drives and parking areas or permit the same to stand upon the drives or any part thereof so as to congest traffic or obstruct the drive. Paths established as foot or bicycle paths shall not be used for vehicular traffic.
(2)
No vehicle shall be operated on any road or drive in any park at a speed exceeding 15 miles per hour unless otherwise posted.
(3)
All stop signs and or other traffic control devices shall be obeyed.
(4)
No person shall fail to obey any law enforcement officer, traffic officer or authorized city personnel charged with the duty of directing traffic in any city park.
(5)
No person shall operate a vehicle along or over any road or drive within a park in a reckless manner or without due regard for the safety and rights of pedestrians and drivers and occupants of all vehicles, so as to endanger the life, limb, or property of any person while in lawful use of said park drive or roads.
(n)
Parking.
(1)
Overnight parking is strictly prohibited, and no parking of any motor vehicle or trailer or other vehicle shall be allowed within a city park including on any of the park roads or parking lots while the park is officially closed between 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m., except in an emergency situation or with the express written consent of the recreation director.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park, stop, or leave standing any vehicle in any parking area in any park except during the time he/she remains in the park.
(3)
Any vehicle left within the park boundaries after closing hours without proper permission shall be removed at the owner's expense.
(4)
Repairing or servicing of vehicles is prohibited within the confines of the park except in emergency situations and upon the immediate notification of the recreation director.
(5)
Official city vehicles are permitted to park in city parks after hours for the purpose of engaging in authorized activity or events.
(o)
Advertising and signs. No person shall post or display any sign, banner, advertisement, circular, notice, or statement within any city park without prior permission of the recreation director.
(p)
Picnicking and cooking fires.
(1)
No person shall picnic or cook in any area not designated for that purpose by the recreation department, nor make nor kindle any fire except in places provided therefor. Each person must be sure that any fire he/she started is completely out before leaving the park.
(2)
No person shall move or carry tables or equipment found within a park for park use to a different area of the park.
(3)
It shall be the responsibility of park patrons to contact the Gwinnett County fire marshal's office either by phone or the Gwinnett County fire and emergency services website to ensure that the day of the intended picnic and cookout is not an officially designated "no burn day" due to high fire danger conditions prevalent in the area.
(q)
Disorderly conduct and loitering.
(1)
No person shall disturb the peace and good order in any park by fighting or disorderly conduct; using threatening, abusive, indecent, or obscene language; or threatening violence to the person or property of others.
(2)
No person shall refuse to comply with the lawful order of a law enforcement officer or authorized personnel to disperse and leave the park.
(3)
No person shall loiter in or near toilet buildings or parking areas.
(4)
No person shall write, paint, carve, tattoo, graffiti, or otherwise mark or deface public park property.
(5)
No person shall be in the park under the influence of drugs or alcohol.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-24)
Sec. 28-52. - Parental responsibility.
(a)
All minor children shall be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian while in a city park.
(b)
No parent, guardian, or custodian of any minor shall permit or allow him or her to do any act which would constitute a violation of this article.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-25)

DIVISION 2. - TENNIS COURTS

Sec. 28-78. - Rules and operating standards.
(a)
Soft-soled designated tennis shoes or rubber-soled nonmarking shoes shall be worn at all times while on the court. All shoes shall be close-toed.
(b)
Flip-flops, Crocs, sandals, or open-toed shoes shall not be permitted on the courts.
(c)
No glass containers, animals, bikes, skateboards, hover boards, roller skates, or like motorized vehicles shall be allowed on the courts.
(d)
All minor children shall be accompanied by a parent, legal guardian, or custodian while using tennis facilities. Parents, guardians, and custodians are responsible for supervision and actions of their minor children while on city property.
(e)
Players shall avoid interfering with play on adjoining courts.
(f)
If individuals are waiting for courts, court time shall be limited to 90 minutes to allow others to play.
(g)
Players wishing to utilize the courts shall first check the court schedule located in the front right window of the tennis building to find an open court for play.
(h)
The city manager or tennis director or their designees shall be authorized to draft league rules and establish associated fee schedules; and establish such forms fees as he/she deems necessary and appropriate.
(i)
Compensated instructions/lessons shall be strictly prohibited on any courts without the express written approval of the city's tennis director or his/her designee. Any instructor desiring to give individual or group lessons outside of city league programs shall first complete the appropriate application and remit the respective fee, providing same is approved by the tennis director. The tennis director shall be authorized to produce such forms and establish such fees as he/she deems necessary and appropriate, subject to approval of the city manager.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-26)
Sec. 28-79. - Hours of operation.
Tennis courts shall be open for play from 6:00 a.m. until 11:00 p.m. The tennis courts shall be closed from 11:00 p.m. until 6:00 a.m.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-27)
Sec. 28-80. - Enforcement.
The tennis director, or his/her designee, shall be authorized to remove any individual from the courts whose conduct is detrimental or contrary to the rules and regulations in this division. The tennis director shall have the authority to suspend or permanently ban any individual from future play for violating these rules and regulations.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-28)

DIVISION 3. - COMPLIANCE AND PENALTIES

Sec. 28-104. - Compliance required; no liability for city officials.
(a)
No person shall fail or refuse to comply with any reasonable order relating to the regulation, direction, or control of traffic or to any order lawfully given by any law enforcement officer, city marshal, code enforcement officer, or any city official in the execution of his or her office; or willfully resist, obstruct, or abuse any law enforcement officer, city marshal, code enforcement officer, or city official in the execution of his or her office or duties.
(b)
No official or employee of the city shall, while acting pursuant to the provisions of this article, be personally liable for any damages that may accrue to persons or property as the result of any act required or permitted in the discharge of his or her official duties, provided that such acts are performed in good faith and without gross negligence.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-29)
Sec. 28-105. - Penalties for offenses.
(a)
Any person convicted of a violation of this article shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $500.00.
(b)
Any person damaging park property shall be liable for any and all related damages.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-30)
Sec. 28-106. - No assumption of liability.
(a)
Parking in any city park is at the vehicle owner's own risk. The city accepts no responsibility or liability for damages incurred as a result of foul balls or objects striking vehicles.
(b)
The city and its agents and employees assume no responsibility or obligation for any injury that may occur on park premises or while using park facilities.
(Ord. of 4-12-2016(2), § 54-31)
