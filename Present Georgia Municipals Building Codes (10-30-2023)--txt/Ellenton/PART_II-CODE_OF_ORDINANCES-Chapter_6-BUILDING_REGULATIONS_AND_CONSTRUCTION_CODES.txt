Chapter 6 - BUILDING REGULATIONS AND CONSTRUCTION CODES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Building official or building inspector means the qualified building official or qualified building inspector as designated by the city.
Sec. 6-2. - Enforcement.
(a)
The building official is hereby authorized to enforce within the city the provisions of this chapter and the state minimum codes adopted herein.
(b)
The building official is further authorized to render interpretations of this Code and the adopted state codes and to adopt policies and procedures in order to clarify the application of their provisions. Such interpretations, policies and procedures shall be in compliance with the intent and purpose of this chapter. Such policies and procedures shall not have the force and effect of law and shall not have the effect of waiving requirements specifically provided for in this Code or in the adopted state codes.
Sec. 6-3. - References to officials in adopted technical codes.
Where reference is made to the duties of certain officials named within the technical codes which are adopted by reference in this chapter, then that designated official of the city who has duties corresponding to those of the named official in said technical code shall be deemed to be the responsible official insofar as enforcing the provisions of that technical code are concerned.
Sec. 6-4. - State minimum technical codes.
(a)
The following technical and building codes, as adopted and amended by the state department of community affairs, are adopted by reference and incorporated into this Code as if set out fully herein, including the administration and appendix chapters:
(1)
International Building Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007.
(2)
International Residential Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(3)
International Fire Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2008.
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(5)
International Mechanical Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(6)
International Fuel Gas Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(7)
National Electrical Code, 2005 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2006.
(8)
International Energy Conservation Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Supplements and Amendments 2008.
(9)
International Property Maintenance Code, 2003 edition, as amended.
(10)
International Existing Building Code, 2006 edition.
(11)
CABO One- and Two-Family Dwelling Code, appendix G, as the Standard Swimming Pool Code.
(b)
If any of the publications listed in subsection (a) of this section are adopted as a state minimum code pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., then the edition adopted by the state shall control. If any of the publications listed in subsection (a) of this section are less stringent than the state minimum codes promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., then the state minimum code shall control.
(c)
A copy of each of the publications listed in subsection (a) of this section, as adopted by the state, is filed in the office of the city clerk and available for review by the public. The same is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein, and from the date on which this section shall take effect, the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the corporate limits of the city.
Sec. 6-5. - Statewide application.
(a)
The city shall enforce those state minimum standard codes which have statewide application.
(b)
The provisions of this chapter shall apply to the construction, erection, installation, alteration, demolition, repair, relocation, replacement, addition to, use or maintenance of buildings or structures, plumbing, mechanical, gas, and electrical systems within the city. Any and all requirements of this chapter shall expressly include any and all technical codes as amended by the city pursuant to this chapter.
Secs. 6-6—6-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - MANUFACTURED HOMES AND MOBILE HOMES[2]

Sec. 6-29. - Manufactured/mobile home regulations.
(a)
Requirements. Each manufactured home and mobile home in the city shall meet the standards established by the county health authorities, as approved by the mayor and city council.
(b)
Relocation permit/fee. Every manufactured home or mobile home must first obtain a city and county relocation permit before relocating to the city. Before setting up or relocating a mobile home within the corporate limits of the city, for any purpose, the owner of said home shall first obtain a relocation permit and pay to the city clerk a non-refundable relocation fee. The amount of the fee shall be as established by the mayor and city council, from time to time.
(c)
Registration. Every mobile home located within the city must have a valid county registration sticker attached to it before city utilities shall be connected.
(d)
Specifications. All mobile homes manufactured after 1976 shall meet all HUD House Specifications. No mobile home manufactured previous to 1976 and not meeting the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing and Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 USC 5401 et. seq. will be allowed to be located within the city. Mobile camping trailers shall not be classified as a single family home and therefore will not be considered as such.
(e)
Lot size and approval. Only one single-family home shall be allowed per lot. Each single family home must meet the minimum lot set-back requirements of ten feet side-yard, 15 feet back-yard and 35 feet front-yard set-back from the public right-of-way. Each lot shall be approved by the city building inspector and the county health officer as meeting the lot size and specifications.
(f)
In city home relocation inspections. Each home proposed for relocating within the city shall first be inspected and receive approval of the city building inspector before locating within the city as well as all other requirements as specified in this section.
(g)
Installation. Each home shall be set-up and installed by a state-licensed contractor as approved by the city's building inspector. Each home shall have appropriate skirting installed before an official certificate of occupancy is issued and before permanent municipal utilities are connected.
(h)
Storage. No manufactured home or mobile home shall be moved into the city for the purpose of storage on any private lot.
(i)
Posting of address. Each home being allowed to locate in the city must have an approved address posted so as to be visible from the public right-of-way.
(j)
Offense; penalty. It is hereby declared to be an offense for any person, owner, agent, or tenant to violate any provision of this section. Each day a violation is continued shall constitute a separate offense. Upon conviction, unless otherwise provided by state law, the judge may impose a fine not exceeding $1,000.00 and imprisonment in the county jail not exceeding six months, and work on the city streets and public works not exceeding 30 days.
(Prior Code, § 31-107; Ord. No. 2017-2, 3-13-2017)
Sec. 6-30. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Manufactured home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein; except that the term "manufactured home" includes any structure which meets all the requirements of this definition except the size requirements and with respect to which the manufacturer voluntarily files a certification required by the secretary of housing and urban development and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 USC 5401 et seq.
Mobile home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein and manufactured prior to June 15, 1976.
Sec. 6-31. - Septic tank.
The design and/or construction of a septic tank for anyone setting up a new manufactured home or mobile home or house with septic tank shall equal or exceed minimum design and construction criteria established by the department of human resources of the state, as published in the current manual for on-site sewage management systems, and the rules, regulations, and standards as established by the county board of health. If not, the city cannot give them any water.
(Mo. of 7-6-1988)
Sec. 6-32. - Permit required.
Moving manufactured homes or mobile homes in the city requires that the mover obtain a permit as issued by the city. The cost for a permit to move a manufactured home or mobile home in the city shall be in an amount as established by the city council, from time to time.
(Mo. of 6-3-2002)
Sec. 6-33. - Emergencies.
Whenever the code enforcement officer finds that an emergency exists which requires immediate action to protect the public health, including violations of any of the applicable rules and regulations of the city, county or state health authorities, such officer may without notice or hearing issue an order reciting the existence of such an emergency and requiring that such action be taken as such officer may deem necessary to meet the emergency, including the suspension of the permit. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this article, such order shall be effective immediately. Any person to whom such an order is directed shall comply therewith immediately, but upon petition to the city council, such person shall be afforded a hearing as soon as possible.
Secs. 6-34—6-55. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PROPERTY NUMBERING

Sec. 6-56. - Uniform numbering system.
A uniform system of numbering properties and buildings as described by a map and material filed in the office of the city clerk is hereby adopted. The map and system description as filed is hereby adopted and made a part of this article.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(1), § 1)
Sec. 6-57. - Assignment of numbers.
(a)
All properties, lots and parcels of land within the corporate limits of the city shall hereafter be identified by reference to the uniform numbering system adopted herein, except that all existing number identification of buildings or lots or parcels not in conformity with the system adopted herein shall be changed in not more than six months from the adoption of this article so as to conform.
(b)
Each principal building shall bear the number assigned to it by the administrator of the system as herein provided and where more than one business or family dwelling unit occupies a single structure shall bear, in addition to the basic number assigned to the structure, the supplementary numerals or letters assigned to them.
(c)
Numerals and letters indicating the official property number designation shall be posted at the entrance for which the designated number has been assigned in such a manner as to provide an unobstructed sighting from the street on which the building is located.
(d)
No person, agency or business shall adopt, assign, display or cite any number other than that assigned as provided herein for the purpose of property identification or for the purpose of designating location of property.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(1), § 2)
Sec. 6-58. - Administration.
(a)
The compliance officer shall be responsible for implementing and maintaining the numbering system as adopted by this article including the supplementary documents referred to herein.
(b)
The compliance officer or his designee shall assign and maintain a record of all property numbers for the city.
(c)
The compliance officer or his designee may make available to public agencies such as those responsible for emergency services, tax officials, and post office such part of the records of property number assignments as may be required to accomplish a public purpose.
(d)
The compliance officer shall issue one set of numerals for each property number assigned, including any future legally accomplished subdivision such as the establishment of apartments. The maintenance of the numbers is the responsibility of the property owner, except that, at the discretion of the city clerk, numerals may be replaced if they have been destroyed by an act of God.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(1), § 4)
Secs. 6-59—6-76. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - ESTABLISHING STREET NAMES

Sec. 6-77. - Established.
There is hereby established an official system of street names in the city as shown on the map entitled Base Map—City of Ellenton, as produced by the Southwest Georgia Regional Development Center, which is made a part of this article.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(2), § 1)
Sec. 6-78. - Existing street names unchanged.
Names of streets in the city shall remain as shown on said map unless officially changed by ordinance.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(2), § 2)
Sec. 6-79. - New street names.
No new streets shall be accepted by the city and no municipal improvements made therein until such streets have been named. If they are extensions of existing streets, the existing names shall be continued if not an extension. Names recorded may not duplicate or closely approximate street names already assigned.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(2), § 3)
Sec. 6-80. - Copy of street name map.
The city clerk shall be responsible for the maintenance of the system of street names. He or his designee shall maintain a copy of the map adopted by this article and shall record thereon all changes made by ordinance or new assignments accepted by subdivision platting or other legal means. From time to time he shall cause to be made revisions to the official map to include all official changes made up to the date of revision, which date is also to be clearly recorded thereon.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(2), § 4)
Sec. 6-81. - Street naming.
(a)
Berrien Street is the dividing line north and south while Baker Street is the east-west dividing line for a system that separates the city for street designation purposes into quadrants.
(b)
Baker Street north of Berrien Street will be known as North Baker Street, south of Berrien Street will be known as South Baker Street.
(c)
Street designations will require these directional prefixes to be complete.
(d)
Streets east of North Baker and north of Berrien Street will be known by a basic name plus the designation northeast or N.E.
(e)
Streets west of North Baker Street and north of Berrien Street will be known by a basic name plus the designation of northwest or N.W.
(f)
Streets south of Berrien Street and east of South Baker Street will carry a basic name plus the designation southeast or S.E.
(g)
Streets west of South Baker and south of Berrien Street will carry a basic name, plus the designation southwest or S.W.
(h)
A map with all complete street names reflecting the official system described in subsections (a) through (g) of this section carrying the date on which it and the implementing ordinance were adopted is to be kept in the custody of the city clerk or his designee as the official street naming map of the city.
(i)
A copy of said map is to be available to the city clerk or his designee to be used as a work sheet on which to record street additions and street name changes. At the city clerk's discretion, he should have the original, official map revised periodically to record all marked up additions and changes and this revised map and copies of it then used in lieu of those previously in use. Care should be taken to ensure that the date of revision is recorded on the face of any official revision.
(j)
Streets that change from one predominant direction (such as east-west) to another predominant direction (north-south) will be assigned a different name for that segment that deviates in directions. This provision is necessary so that the numbering system can be used without confusion.
(Ord. of 7-12-2004(3))
Secs. 6-82—6-107. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - HIGH-EFFICIENCY PLUMBING FIXTURES[3]

Sec. 6-108. - Definitions.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(b)
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-3 (high-efficiency plumbing fixtures) shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this article. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this article, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-3, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-3.
Sec. 6-109. - Residential building construction.
(a)
No plumbing fixture shall be installed which does not meet the standards listed in this section or the state minimum plumbing code, whichever is stricter. This includes all plumbing fixtures installed in newly constructed buildings or when replacing plumbing fixtures during remodeling or renovation of existing buildings.
(b)
The standards are as follows:
(1)
A water closet or toilet that:
a.
Is a dual flush water closet that meets the following standards:
1.
The average flush volume of two reduced flushes and one full flush may not exceed 1.28 gallons;
2.
The toilet meets the performance, testing, and labeling requirements prescribed by the following standards, as applicable:
(i)
American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.2-2008; and
(ii)
American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.14-2006 Six-Liter Water Closets Equipped with a Dual Flushing Device; and
3.
The toilet is listed to the WaterSense™ Tank-Type High Efficiency Toilet Specification; or
b.
Is a single flush water closet, including gravity, pressure assisted, and electro-hydraulic tank types, that meets the following standards:
1.
The average flush volume may not exceed 1.28 gallons;
2.
The toilet must meet the performance, testing, and labeling requirements prescribed by the American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.192/CSA B45.1 or A112.19.14; and
3.
The toilet must be listed to the WaterSense™ Tank-Type High Efficiency Toilet Specification;
(2)
A shower head that allows a flow of no more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
A urinal and associated flush valve that:
a.
Uses no more than 0.5 gallons of water per flush;
b.
Meets the performance, testing, and labeling requirements prescribed by the American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.2/CSA B45.1;
c.
For flushing urinals, meets all WaterSense™ specifications for flushing urinals; and
d.
Where non-water urinals are employed, complies with American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.3/CSA B45.4 or American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.19/CSA B45.4. Non-water urinals shall be cleaned and maintained in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions after installation. Where non-water urinals are installed, they shall have a water distribution line roughed-in to the urinal location at a minimum height of 56 inches (1,422 mm) to allow for the installation of an approved backflow prevention device in the event of a retrofit. Such water distribution lines shall be installed with shutoff valves located as close as possible to the distributing main to prevent the creation of dead ends. Where non-water urinals are installed, a minimum of one water-supplied fixture rated at a minimum of one water supply fixture unit shall be installed upstream on the same drain line to facilitate drain line flow and rinsing;
(4)
A lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of no more than 1.5 gallons of water per minute at a pressure of 60 pounds per square inch in accordance with American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.18.1/CSA B.125.1 and listed to the WaterSense™ High-Efficiency Lavatory Faucet Specification; and
(5)
A kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of no more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute.
Sec. 6-110. - Commercial building construction.
There shall be no construction of any commercial building initiated within the city for any commercial building of any type which does not meet the requirements of this article.
Sec. 6-111. - Exemptions.
(a)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of this article when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing buildings;
(2)
Such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation, would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this chapter were installed;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Specifically designed as toilets for juveniles.
(b)
The owner, or his agent, of a building undergoing new construction or repair or renovation who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (a)(2), (3) or (4) of this section shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the building official. A fee, as determined by the city council and shown on the city fee schedule, shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
