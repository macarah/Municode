ARTICLE IX. - BROWN BAG ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 6-216. - Permitted locations.
A bring-your-own-bottle establishment or brown bag establishment must have a full-service kitchen and be prepared to serve food prepared on the premises every hour it is open. Except for restaurants and hotels, an establishment that seeks to be a brown bag establishment may not hold a license to sell and may not sell or serve any alcoholic beverages. It must be in a zoning district that allows a restaurant as a conforming use.
(Code 1982, § 9-2-121; Ord. No. 741 , pt. VII, 8-15-17; Ord. No. 754 , pt. XXVII, 10-16-18)
Sec. 6-217. - License required; compliance with applicable regulations.
It shall be unlawful to operate an establishment governed under the provisions of this article without first obtaining a license from the finance director and paying such fees and taxes as may be required by the ordinances, rules and regulations of the city. A bring-your-own-bottle or brown bag establishment shall be subject to all the general licensing requirements and regulations as well as the licensing requirements and regulations for consumption on the premises establishments.
(Code 1982, § 9-2-122; Ord. No. 801 , pt. XXXII, 9-21-21)
