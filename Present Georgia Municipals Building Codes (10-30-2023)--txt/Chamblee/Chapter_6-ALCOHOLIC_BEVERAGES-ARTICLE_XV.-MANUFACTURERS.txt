ARTICLE XV. - MANUFACTURERS

Sec. 6-301. - Manufacturer license required; compliance with applicable regulations.
It shall be unlawful to operate an establishment governed under the provisions of this article without first obtaining a license from the finance director and paying such fees and taxes as may be required by the ordinances, rules and regulations of the city.
(Ord. No. 703 , pt. II, 4-19-16; Ord. No. 759 , pt. II, 12-18-18; Ord. No. 801 , pt. XLIII, 9-21-21)
Sec. 6-302. - Investigative and administrative fee.
Each application for a license under this article shall be accompanied by payment for the full amount of the license fee, together with payment in an amount as may be required by the ordinances, rules and regulations of the city to defray investigative and administrative costs. If the application is denied and the license refused, or if the applicant withdraws the application prior to its being issued, the license fee shall be refunded, but the investigative and administrative fees shall be retained. Notwithstanding the foregoing sentence, the license fee shall not be refunded to any applicant that sold, or offered to sell, alcoholic beverages during the license year at issue.
(Ord. No. 703 , pt. II, 4-19-16; Ord. No. 801 , pt. XLIV, 9-21-21)
Sec. 6-303. - Sales to individuals; hours of sale.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for a manufacturer to sell to an individual any alcoholic beverage that was not produced at the manufacturer's licensed premises.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a craft brewery to sell malt beverages to an individual for consumption off the premises unless:
(1)
The malt beverages were produced on the licensed premises of the craft brewery;
(2)
The sales do not exceed 288 ounces of malt beverages per consumer per day; and
(3)
The sales occur only during the hours allowed under section 6-123 of this chapter.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for a craft distillery to sell distilled spirits to an individual for consumption off the premises unless:
(1)
The distilled spirits were produced on the licensed premises of the craft distillery;
(2)
The sales do not exceed 2,250 milliliters of distilled spirits per consumer per day; and
(3)
The sales occur only during the hours allowed under section 6-100 of this chapter.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for a craft brewery to sell malt beverages for consumption on the premises unless:
(1)
The malt beverages were produced on the licensed premises of the craft brewery;
(2)
The sales do not exceed 3,000 barrels of malt beverages per year;
(3)
The sales and consumption occur only during the hours allowed under section 6-152 of this chapter; and
(4)
The sales and consumption occur only within the establishment's licensed manufacturer's tasting room.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for a craft distillery to sell distilled spirits for consumption on the premises unless:
(1)
The distilled spirits were produced on the licensed premises of the craft distillery;
(2)
The sales do not exceed 500 barrels of distilled spirits per year;
(3)
The sales and consumption occur only during the hours allowed under section 6-152 of this chapter; and
(4)
The sales and consumption occur only within the establishment's licensed manufacturer's tasting room.
(f)
Craft breweries and craft distilleries that possess a valid license to allow customers to consume alcoholic beverages in a manufacturer's tasting room are authorized to sell alcoholic beverages produced on the premises, for consumption on the premises, during the Sunday hours allowed under section 6-152.
(Ord. No. 759 , pt. III, 12-18-18)
Editor's note— Formerly § 6-303 was entitled "Tastings," which was deleted by Ord. No. 741 , pt. I, adopted Aug. 15, 2017, and derived from Ord. No. 703 , pt. II, adopted Apr. 19, 2016.
