Editor's note— Section 4 of Ord. No. 95-17, adopted Sept. 5, 1995, added provisions designated as a new Ch. 15, Art. VIII, §§ 15-141—15-146. Inasmuch as there existed a Ch. 15, Art. VIII, such provisions have been redesignated as a new Ch. 15, Art. IX, §§ 15-171—15-176.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 15-171. - Definitions.
This article shall apply to any person, firm or corporation, as principal or agent, or both, which is not a regular retail or wholesale merchant with a permanent place of business in the city, but rather one who displays harvested but unprocessed agricultural or forestry products or processed or unprocessed nuts in or upon any lot, building, room or structure of any kind whether fixed or mobile for the purpose of selling to the general public.
(Ord. No. 95-17, § 4, 9-5-95; Ord. No. 2018-24, § 4, 11-20-18)
Sec. 15-172. - Occupational tax.
It shall be unlawful for any merchant meeting the terms of this article to engage in business without first paying the occupational tax required under article II of this chapter. Provided, however, a merchant selling from a pick-up truck that does not exceed three-fourths (¾) ton does not need but one license annually to sell in multiple permitted locations.
(Ord. No. 95-17, § 4, 9-5-95; Ord. No. 2004-22, § 1, 5-4-04)
Sec. 15-173. - Restrictions; permitted locations; size of operation; etc.
No merchant meeting the terms of this article shall operate such business without strict compliance with the following:
(1)
Permitted locations. Such merchants shall be allowed only in the following described areas:
a.
Sam Nunn Boulevard beginning at the junction of Ball Street and Washington Street and extending along both sides of Sam Nunn Boulevard in a northwest direction to the intersection of Mason Terrace and Sam Nunn Boulevard.
b.
Courtney Hodges Boulevard beginning at the junction of Commerce and Carroll Street and extending the full length of General Courtney Hodges Boulevard along both sides of said street.
c.
Larry Walker Parkway beginning at the intersection of Martin Luther King, Jr. Drive and Larry Walker Parkway and extending along Larry Walker Parkway on both sides of said street to the intersection of Larry Walker Parkway and General Courtney Hodges Boulevard.
d.
Marshallville Highway beginning at the intersection of General Courtney Hodges Boulevard and Marshallville Highway and extending along both sides of Marshallville Highway to the intersection of Marshallville Highway and U.S. Interstate 75.
e.
Macon Road beginning at the intersection of Macon Road and Main Street and extending north along both sides of Macon Road to the intersection of Macon Road and Perimeter Road. From the intersection of Macon Road and Main Street and extending north on both sides of Macon Road to the intersection of Macon Road and Northside Drive, such business shall operate only between the hours of 7:00 am and 10:00 pm. All structures, signs, display cases, and other business equipment shall be removed at the end of each business day's operation.
(2)
Size, safety of operations, surfaces, etc. No such merchant shall occupy more than four hundred (400) square feet of area on any lot or erect or set up any structures or apparatus which in total exceeds two hundred (200) square feet with the exception of property used for the sale of Christmas trees.
Any and all structures and apparatus used in conjunction with such a business shall be of substantial construction and material as determined by the building official so as not to constitute a nuisance or hazard to the general public. Exposed wood and/or unpainted surfaces are prohibited. The structures and apparatus shall be painted a neutral or earthtone color. Building trim and accent areas may feature brighter colors, including primary colors. Such structures and apparatus shall not be vacated nor remain on any property unattended for more than seventy-two (72) hours and shall be permanently removed upon the expiration date specified in the application.
(3)
Any and all structures, apparatus and signage must be set back ten (10) feet from established rights-of-way.
(4)
Signage shall not exceed a total of ten (10) square feet per business. Said signage shall be stenciled. Hand-painted or spray painted signage is specifically prohibited.
(5)
Applicant must obtain a temporary use permit from the community development department.
(Ord. No. 95-17, § 4, 9-5-95; Ord. No. 2011-20, § 1, 2, 8-2-11; Ord. No. 2012-21, §§ 1, 2, 9-18-12; Ord. No. 2013-18, 7-16-13; Ord. No. 2018-24, §§ 5—7, 11-20-18)
DIVISION 2. - APPLICATION

Sec. 15-174. - Application.
(a)
Any merchant desiring to transact business within the city shall file with the city clerk an application prior to the transaction of any such business. Such application shall include the following information:
(1)
The name and permanent address of the merchant;
(2)
The type of business to be conducted;
(3)
The number of days during which the sale is to be conducted;
(4)
Evidence of either ownership of the property by the merchant or written lease or written permission from the owner of the property to the merchant regarding the property upon which the sale is to be conducted;
(5)
The location where the sale is to be conducted.
(b)
No person shall make any false statements in any application in order to conduct a merchant's business.
(Ord. No. 95-17, § 4, 9-5-95)
Sec. 15-175. - Regulatory fee; change in location.
(a)
There shall be a regulatory fee payable in conjunction with the occupational tax upon submittal of an approved application. Such fee shall be established by the mayor and council.
(b)
Change in locations or renewals of existing locations will require new application subject to the established regulatory fee. Provided, however, a merchant selling from a pick-up truck that does not exceed three-fourths (¾) ton shall be required to pay only one (1) regulatory fee annually for multiple permitted locations and make only one (1) application.
(Ord. No. 95-17, § 4, 9-5-95; Ord. No. 2004-22, § 2, 5-4-04)
Sec. 15-176. - Issuance; term; etc.
After the applicant has complied with all the provisions of this division, a business application shall be approved. Such approval is not transferable, is valid only within the territorial limits of the city, is valid only for the business stated on the application, and shall expire on the last day named therein on which the sale is to be conducted.
(Ord. No. 95-17, § 4, 9-5-95)
