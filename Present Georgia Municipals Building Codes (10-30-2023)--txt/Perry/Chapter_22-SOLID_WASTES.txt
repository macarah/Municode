Sec. 22-1. - Purpose and intent.
(a)
To control the costs of solid waste management to the residents of the City of Perry.
(b)
To protect the health, safety and general well-being of the citizens of the City of Perry.
(c)
To enhance and maintain the quality of the environment, conserve natural resources and prevent water and air pollution by providing a comprehensive, rational and effective means of regulating the collection and disposal of solid waste in the city in accordance with the provisions of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management as amended.
(d)
To control solid waste material in the city by establishing limitations, prohibiting certain acts causing solid waste disposal problems and to provide for enforcement for violations of the provisions of this chapter.
(e)
To govern the storage, collection and disposal of solid waste produced or otherwise created within the city.
(f)
To encourage and expand solid waste recycling and waste reduction.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-2. - City to be exclusive collector of solid waste; mandatory solid waste service required.
(a)
The city shall have the exclusive right to collect and dispose of all residential garbage, trash, recycling, yard waste, bulk waste and other solid waste defined as acceptable for city collection by the remaining portions of this chapter.
(b)
It shall be mandatory for all owners, occupants or persons in possession, charge or control of all dwellings, buildings, places and premises in the City of Perry in and from which solid waste is created, accumulated or produced to use the solid waste collection system of the city and pay for refuse collection service rendered to such dwelling, building, place or premises by a collector or permittee.
(c)
The mayor and council shall have the power and authority to contract for and provide a waste collection system employing the use of standardized front-loaded containers to facilitate the collection and hauling of commercial and business garbage and solid waste in the city.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-3. - Refuse disposal.
All disposal of solid waste shall be by a method or methods in accordance with the requirements of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, as amended and local law.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-4. - Unlawful disposal of solid waste.
(a)
Reserved.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to leave, place, throw or deposit within the city's service area, or cause or permit any other person to leave, place, throw or deposit within the city's service area, solid waste of any kind for the purpose of collection and disposal by the city when the solid waste is generated at a location outside the city's service area or from a location where the city does not collect solid waste.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for an owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of a building or dwelling or any other person to bury or submerse in water any solid waste material that is not permitted by the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management as amended.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for an owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of a building or dwelling or any other person to burn solid waste except as permitted by the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management as amended.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for an owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of a building or dwelling or any other person to allow an accumulation of solid waste which creates a fire, health or safety hazard or harborage for rodents or insects.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for an owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of a building or dwelling or any other person to deposit in a solid waste container or otherwise set out for solid waste collection and disposal by the city or its authorized representative any waste that is considered unacceptable as defined in section 22-13.

Sec. 22-10. - Proper storage.
(a)
No owner, occupant, tenant or lessee of any property may deposit, store or permit to accumulate any solid waste upon his property that is not stored or disposed of in a manner prescribed by this chapter. Furthermore, it shall be unlawful for any person to allow an accumulation of solid waste which creates a fire, health or safety hazard or harborage for rodents or insects.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the owner or occupant of any premises within the city to comply with the applicable provisions of this chapter, except where the property is unoccupied or where the occupant or owner has by deed, contract, lease, negotiation, agreement, arrangement, express or implied, or custom, determined otherwise; and further except in the case of mobile toter replacement in which case the duly shall be as provided in section 22-20. In case the property is unoccupied, the responsibility of the owner shall be as set forth in section 19-12.
(c)
In case there is some arrangement between the owner and occupant, upon proof thereof by the occupant, the terms of such deed, contract, lease, regulations, agreement, arrangement, express or implied, or custom shall control the responsibility.
(d)
The owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of any premises upon which garbage is stored shall remove, or cause to be removed all garbage from said premises at least once a week (7 day period). The work shall be done in a clean orderly manner and any refuse that is spilled shall be cleaned up and the premises left in a clean and sanitary condition.
(e)
No owner, occupant, tenant or lessee of a building or dwelling may leave outside the building or dwelling, in a place accessible to children, any abandoned or unattended icebox, refrigerator or other receptacle that has an airtight door, without first removing the door.
(f)
No person shall store solid waste in such a manner that it may be carried or deposited by the elements upon any public or private premises.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)

Sec. 22-12. - Refuse acceptable for collection by city.
The following refuse shall be considered to be acceptable for collection by the city:
(1)
Garbage. Putrescible animal and vegetable wastes resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking or consumption of foods.
(2)
Ashes. The residue from the burning of wood, coal, coke or other combustible materials.
(3)
Rubbish. Nonputrescible solid waste, excluding ashes, consisting of paper, cardboard, tin cans, yard clippings, grass clippings, wood, glass, bedding, crockery, metal and similar materials. All boxes and packing crates regardless of construction shall be dismantled and/or flattened.
(4)
Dead animals. Small dead animals, not exceeding seventy-five (75) pounds in weight, which die in the normal course of community activity, excluding condemned animals from a slaughterhouse or other animals normally considered industrial refuse, shall be removed by the city upon request.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-13. - Refuse not acceptable for collection by city.
(a)
Unless otherwise determined by the city, the following waste is determined inappropriate for collection and conveyance by the city: incinerator ash; foundry sand; explosives; hospital, pathological and biological waste; chemicals and radioactive materials; oil sludge; asbestos in identifiable quantities; septic tank or other human waste; sewage and other highly diluted, water-carried materials or substances; materials in gaseous form; human remains; street sweepings; ash mining waste; sludge; and hazardous refuse of any kind, such as cleaning fluids, crank case oils, cutting oils, paints, acids, caustics, poisons, drugs. The city may issue regulations adding or removing items from this list.
(b)
Hazardous waste means any refuse, sludge or other waste material or combinations of refuse, sludge or other waste material in solid, semi-solid, liquid or contained gaseous form which, because of its quantity, concentration or chemical, physical or infectious characteristics may cause or significantly contribute to an increase in mortality or an increase in serious irreversible, or incapacitating reversible illness, or pose a substantial present or potential hazard to human health or the environment when improperly treated, stored, transported or disposed of or otherwise managed. Categories of hazardous waste materials include, but are not limited to: explosives, flammables, oxidizers, poisons, irritants and corrosives. The city may issue regulations adding or removing items from this list.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-14. - Prohibition of tires in garbage dumpsters and mobile toters.
No tires are allowed in garbage dumpsters or mobile toters. Tire tubes are allowed and may be placed in toters or dumpsters.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-15. - Duty of occupant.
(a)
The occupant of any premises within the city, whether business, commercial, industrial, institutional or residential shall maintain the premises in a clean, orderly, safe and sanitary condition, free from any accumulation of solid waste which creates a fire, health or safety hazard or harborage for rodents or insects.
(b)
The occupant of any premises within the city shall ensure that all solid waste is stored on the premises in containers complying with the requirements of this chapter.
(c)
The occupant of any premises within the city shall cover and/or secure all solid waste containers, keeping the storage area and area surrounding any containers in a clean, orderly and sanitary manner and preventing the waste from being scattered by animals, wind or other means. In the event that any solid waste stored in a plastic bag or other container becomes torn, spilled or otherwise scattered, the occupant shall be responsible for clean-up of the area.
(d)
The occupant of any premises within the city shall not store any solid waste, including yard waste and bulk waste, outside for more than seven (7) days.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-16. - Duty of owner.
(a)
The owner of any multi-family dwelling shall provide the premises with a sufficient number of covered solid waste containers complying with the requirements of section 22-24 to allow the occupants to adequately store the solid waste generated by the occupants of the premises between collection days.
(b)
The owner of any premises shall not allow the accumulation of solid waste on the premises to such an extent that the solid waste creates a fire, health or safety hazard or harborage for rodents or insects.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)

(a)
All fees and charges for the cost of collecting, removing and disposing of garbage and other solid waste in the city shall be determined by the mayor and council and kept on file in the office of the city clerk and the utility business office. Such fees and charges are hereby incorporated in this section as if set out in length therein and shall include, but shall not be limited to, fees and charges for:
(1)
Single family dwellings.
(2)
Duplex dwellings.
(3)
Multi-family housing facilities.
(4)
Commercial, governmental, institutional, business or industrial establishments using mobile toters and containerized trash served by the city.
(5)
Commercial, institutional, business or governmental establishments using dumpsters leased from the city's authorized representative.
(6)
Businesses and residential areas which generate trash in excess of normal household waste.
(b)
The service charges, specified above, are levied against both the owner and occupant of the premises served, jointly and severally. The owner of the property shall be billed by the city for the service charges, but collection from one (1) shall relieve the other. The service charges shall be collected in the manner and at such intervals as may be prescribed by the mayor and council and shall be paid into the city treasury. All uncollected service charges shall constitute a continuing lien against the property served to the same and as full an extent and subject to addition of interest and costs as provided by law with respect to city ad valorem taxes, and may be enforced in the same manner. The rates of such service charges may be fixed and changed from time to time; users of said services may be classified and reclassified; and, different rates for different classes of users may be prescribed by mayor and council.
Changes may be made without adoption of an amendment to existing.
(c)
Each resident of the city, who is the head of the household of the residential dwelling unit in which such person resides and upon whom is imposed a residential garbage and sanitation services charge for the residential dwelling unit in which such person resides by the provision of this article, and who is sixty-five (65) years of age or older, or who is then totally disabled according to a current ruling of any agency of this state or the federal government, and who has a gross annual income from all sources, including the income of all members of such person's family residing within said residential dwelling unit, not in excess of seventeen thousand nine hundred dollars ($17,900.00), shall not have to pay the monthly residential charge for garbage and sanitation services.
(1)
The exemption provided in this section shall not, however, be granted unless an affidavit of the person claiming the exemption prepared upon forms prescribed and provided by the city manager for that purpose, shall be filed with said city manager or his or her designee which affidavit shall be filed annually on or before January 15 and the exemption from such payment shall apply to each billing cycle beginning more than thirty (30) days thereafter and shall show the age of the person claiming such exemption on the date of the filing of such affidavit or shall have attached a true copy of a determination of total disability issued by either a state or federal agency. If disability is claimed as the ground for exemption, the total amount of income received by such person from all sources during the immediately preceding calendar year, the total amount of income received during the immediately preceding calendar year from all sources by each family member residing in such residential dwelling unit, together with a statement that such claimant's income, when added to the income of each other family member residing in said residential dwelling unit will not be in excess of the income level of seventeen thousand nine hundred dollars ($17,900.00) in the calendar year in which such affidavit is filed, together with the exact address of the residence of such claimant, and including such other information as may be required by the city manager or his or her designee.
(2)
It shall be unlawful to willfully furnish any false information to the city manager to obtain the exemption of the monthly garbage service charge as provided herein.
(3)
In the event that any person has once qualified hereunder for an exemption of the monthly residential garbage pickup service charge and, subsequent thereto, such person becomes disqualified therefore, it shall be the duty of such person to immediately so notify the city manager who shall remove such person's name from the list of those receiving said exemption; in the event that any such person fails to so notify the city manager and continues to receive such exemptions after being no longer qualified, therefore, such person shall be immediately liable to the city for the amount that would have been required to be paid by such person without such exemption and upon the refusal of such person to pay the amount due, the city manager or his or her designee shall cause all city utility services to be discontinued as to such person's residence until such time as such person has paid the amount due in full.

Sec. 22-19. - Mobile toters generally.
(a)
A mobile toter is a plastic, wheeled refuse receptacle having a tight-fitting attached lid, carrying identification of the city and/or the city's solid waste collection contractor, with a designated serial number/barcode and whose design, construction and capacity shall be determined by the city.
(b)
Toters shall be required for curb side collection service. The responsibility for obtaining such toter shall be upon the owner or occupant of the premises desiring and otherwise eligible, by the terms of this chapter, for curb side collection service. The owner or occupant of the premises is required to contact the department of public works and request the delivery of a toter.
(c)
Additional toters may be obtained from the city by the owner or occupant for an additional fee and will be serviced according to the provisions of this chapter subject to this section.
(d)
All refuse, ashes, garbage and trash shall be placed in the toter for collection. The toter shall be placed at curb side for collection purposes and the collection schedule shall be announced by the department of public works. If the toter is not near the curb side at collection time, this will indicate no service is needed.
(e)
Any person physically incapacitated so as to prevent such person from placing the toter at curb side for collection purposes, may obtain non curb side collection by obtaining the approval of the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-20. - Maintenance of mobile toters.
(a)
The city shall own the mobile toter and residents are granted exclusive use of the toter at their address only. All toters issued by the city for use shall remain the city's property and shall only be used for storage of solid waste materials collected by the city. Each person subject to the provisions of this chapter shall be responsible for the toter assigned and must not abuse, remove or destroy the toter. If service is discontinued for any reason, the toter must be left on the premises vacated, and if the toter is not left on the vacated premises the cost of the toter may be charged to the utility deposit refund.
(b)
Toters shall be secured by the resident, between collection days, in such a manner that they are not readily susceptible to theft or vandalism.
(c)
Use shall be for small businesses, single-family dwellings and multifamily dwellings with four (4) or fewer units.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any other person to willfully damage or destroy any toter. Anyone found to have intentionally or negligently damaged a city-owned toter shall be liable for the replacement or repair cost. The city shall have sole determination of willful damage or destruction of a toter. In cases of hardship, the department of public works shall have the ability to waive the fee for replacement. Any toter that is stolen shall be replaced by the city, provided that a police report is submitted to the city.
(e)
If the toter requires physical maintenance, including replacement of wheels, lid or repairs of other wearing or damage, the customer shall call the city to request such service. The damaged toter shall be emptied and washed out and left in front of the garage or next to the building/house where it can readily be seen from the street.
(f)
Customers shall keep the inside and the outside of the toter clean. There shall be no markings, such as graffiti or stickers, on the outside of the toter that are not placed there by the city or by the city's authorized representative. Any markings that appear on the outside of the toter that are not placed there by the city's authorized representative or the city shall be promptly removed by the customer.
(g)
Customers shall routinely wash or rinse out the interior of the toter so that offensive odors are not detected outside of the empty toter.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-21. - Containers for trash and cold ash, generally.
Trash or cold ashes may be separately placed in disposable containers. All such containers shall be removed with the contents. Trash and ashes shall not be placed in any disposable containers that are so worn or defective that the contents leak or spill out.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-22. - Frequency of collections.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided and except in the case of an emergency arising from an act of God or under circumstances over which the city has or had no control, the city shall collect, remove and dispose of all household garbage, recycling, yard waste, bulk waste and other solid waste in the city's service area at least once a week. If a holiday should fall during the week in which an area is due a regular collection, such collection shall not be made until the next regularly scheduled collection day for that area, unless otherwise announced by the city.
(b)
Solid waste pickup shall be any time from 6:00 a.m. until 6:00 p.m. on the day of collection. Changes in weather conditions, crew assignment and equipment problems can cause the pickup time to be earlier or later in the day. Solid waste for collection should always be out by 6:00 a.m. on collection day.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-23. - Solid waste transportation and vehicle requirements.
(a)
No solid waste collector shall transport solid waste in a conveyance that has not been approved by the city manager. All vehicles, except those owned by individuals hauling their own solid waste, shall display an identifying tag or decal.
(b)
All haulers under contract to collect residential and commercial waste in the city shall use a compactor or roll-off trucks. Vehicles or containers used for the collection and transportation of garbage or refuse containing garbage shall be covered, leak-proof, durable and of sturdy construction. They shall be cleaned as often as necessary to prevent a nuisance, pollution or insect breeding and shall be maintained in good repair. In cases when the compactor or roll-off trucks cannot proceed on a certain road due to physical barriers or safety concerns, trucks with permanent bed covers shall be acceptable.
(c)
All vehicles, both private and commercial, used for the transportation of solid waste shall provide that all solid waste or other items to be disposed of be covered or loads secured by some effective means such to prevent the spillage or loss of waste while being transported. "Effective means" shall mean durable, heavy plastic, canvas or tarpaulin tied down or secured to cover the entire load. Loads consisting of building rubbish, limbs and bulk items shall be loaded and secured with rope or tie downs to assure spillage does not occur. Where spillage does occur, the material shall be picked up immediately by the solid waste collector and transporter and returned to the vehicle or container and the area properly cleaned.
(d)
If a residential or commercial hauler allows leachate to escape onto the public roadway or ditch line, said leachate shall be cleaned up immediately by the hauler.
(e)
Any person collecting and transporting solid waste generated on their property for disposal at an approved disposal site shall comply with this chapter in all respects.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-24. - Collection and disposal of solid waste.
Trash and refuse collectors are not required to collect from premises where the provisions of this chapter are violated. The failure to collect any trash or garbage from the premises because of a violation of the provisions of this chapter shall not relieve the person responsible for such violation from prosecution and penalty therefore.
(1)
Residential solid waste collection and disposal. The city, or its authorized representative, shall provide residential solid waste collection and disposal services from eligible residential buildings. The city, or its authorized representative, is not required to collect any residential solid waste that does not comply with the requirements of this chapter.
a.
The occupant shall place all residential solid waste curbside for collection and disposal by the city, or its authorized representative, no later than 6:00 a.m. on the day of collection and no earlier than 3:00 p.m. on the day before the scheduled collection day. The mobile-toter shall be removed from curbside no later than 8:00 a.m. on the day after collection. Violations of this section should be reported to the department of public works. Upon receiving a complaint, the department of public works shall investigate such complaint. Upon finding a violation, the offending party shall be notified in writing to remove the toter. The offending party shall have forty-eight (48) hours after receiving the notice to remove the toter. Any party failing to remove the toter from curbside within the specified time after receiving the notice shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
b.
All toters shall be stored on the property of the owner or occupant of the premises at a site not in public view.
c.
The toter shall not be filled to overflowing. When the toter is fully loaded, extra trash, garbage, refuse or ashes shall be placed in a metal or plastic, insect proof, rodent proof container with a tight-fitting, waterproof cover and placed next to the toter for collection. If bags are used, they must be of sufficient strength and tightly secured to contain all solid waste generated from the location without tearing or breaking when lifted. Bags may not be stored outside a building for extended periods unless placed in solid waste containers. Bags, however, may be placed curbside without a container the day of collection.
d.
The occupant shall place all residential solid waste for collection by the city, or its authorized representative, within five (5) feet of the edge of the traveled portion of the street or at such other place as approved or designated by the city.
e.
All toters shall be maintained in a clean and sanitary condition and in good repair.
f.
Any container that does not conform to the provisions of this section, or that may have ragged or sharp edges or any other defect that will hamper or injure the person collecting the contents thereof, or is likely to cause bags to tear, must be promptly removed and replaced by a proper container upon receipt of notice to that effect from the city.
g.
Use of unapproved containers, such as wooden containers, screen/wire containers or fifty-five (55) gallon drums for the storage and collection of solid waste are prohibited.
(2)
Commercial solid waste collection and disposal.
a.
The occupant of any commercial building shall be responsible for the lawful collection and disposal of all commercial solid waste generated from the location, except that the owner of a building containing five (5) or more rental dwelling units shall also be responsible for the lawful collection and disposal of all solid waste generated from the location. The owner shall supply the building with containers of sufficient number and size to contain all solid waste generated from the location.
b.
In the case of multiple housing units of ten (10) or more separate units, and in the case of commercial establishments when the limit imposed by section 22-19 is exceeded, dumpsters shall be used. In the case of multiple housing units and trailer courts, of ten (10) or more units, when dumpsters are required, there shall be one (1) six-cubic yard dumpster for every twenty (20) units.
c.
All containers in which solid waste is placed shall be made of metal or plastic, insect-proof, rodent-proof and have a tight-fitting, watertight cover.
d.
All containers shall be maintained in a clean and sanitary condition and in good repair.
e.
Any container that does not conform to the provisions of this section must be promptly removed and replaced by a proper container upon receipt of notice to that effect from the city.
(3)
Dumpsters.
a.
Each dumpster or roll-off container shall have posted thereon the name, address and phone number of the owner of said dumpster or roll-off container.
b.
Each dumpster or refuse container shall be equipped with reflectorized tape or other reflector devices adequate to warn others of its presence during night hours.
c.
No dumpster or refuse container shall be placed in a moving lane of traffic.
d.
The director of public works shall be notified of the location of all dumpsters or refuse containers placed in any streets, alleys, highways, sidewalks or other public ways within the city.
e.
The dumpster or refuse container shall be required to be moved if the placement or location of the dumpster or refuse container constitute a safety hazard.
f.
No dumpster or refuse container shall be placed so as to interfere with public works construction being performed by the city.
g.
No dumpster or refuse container shall be placed within twenty-five (25) feet of any intersection.
h.
Dumpsters or refuse containers used in commercial areas shall be maintained and cleaned to prevent the harboring or breeding of vectors including rats, rodents, flies or harmful insects.
(4)
Dumpster and recycling bin screening.
a.
All dumpsters and recycling bins shall be enclosed within a wall of equal or greater height but at a minimum of eight (8) feet in height, on three (3) sides, accessed by an opaque or solid gate The material of the wall enclosure and access gate shall be similar and compatible to the material on the outside of the principal building and shall comply with the design standards of that particular land district
b.
Dumpsters and recycling bins shall be placed on concrete pads of sufficient size and strength to support the weight of service vehicles.
c.
Restaurants and other food establishments shall place dumpsters and recycling bins on concrete pads that are designed to slope into a drain that is equipped with a grease trap.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15; Ord. No. 2017-23, § 1, 12-5-17)
Sec. 22-25. - Yard waste.
modified
(a)
The city's yard waste collection service was established for the benefit of its residential customers who have the mobile toter. Yard waste includes organic waste generated during regular household landscaping and gardening activities. Examples include: leaves, straw, hedges, shrubs, tree trimmings, grass and other inert materials resulting from general residential yard cleaning. Rocks, dirt and gravel are not considered yard waste.
(b)
Commercial, multi-family, industrial, institutional or other similar facilities are required to make private arrangements to dispose of their yard waste. Yard waste from these facilities is prohibited from staying on the curb more than five (5) days. Any such materials found in the right-of-way after five (5) days shall be immediately removed by the city, and the costs of such removal will be billed to the property owner.
(c)
Preparation of yard waste.
(1)
Yard waste shall not be mixed with household waste or other solid waste.
(2)
Yard waste shall be placed within five (5) feet of the curb and at least three (3) feet away from other collection items and obstacles such as mailboxes, phone poles, vehicles, etc. Do not block the sidewalk. If the department of public works is unable to reach the yard waste due to an obstruction, it will not be collected.
(3)
Divide yard waste into piles that are no larger than five (5) feet wide and five (5) feet high. Piles larger than these dimensions shall result in an additional collection charge.
(4)
Should the weight of any yard waste exceed the limits that the city collection equipment can handle, the property owner will be notified to make other arrangements for disposal.
(5)
Sod is accepted if the dirt is removed.
(6)
Leaves, straw, hedges, shrubs, tree trimmings, grass and other inert materials resulting from general residential yard cleaning should not be placed in ditches or along the curbs. When ditches and storm drains get clogged with leaves and debris, water no longer drains from the street, it ponds along streets and can flood intersections and homes.
(d)
Yard waste that will not be collected.
(1)
Yard waste generated by private contractors performing tree removal, mowing, trimming or other landscaping activities at residences. All nurseries, landscape gardening firms, clearing, grading and building contractors and logging and pulpwood related operations shall remove all solid waste which they generate through their work.
(2)
Trimmings where trees are trimmed or cut for compensation whether the compensation is in cash or in the wood from the trees trimmed.
(3)
Trimmings from the original development of a lot.
(4)
Tree stumps and root balls weighing in excess of the city's equipment capacity.
(5)
Yard debris on vacant lots.
(6)
Yard waste that is not properly prepared or is mixed with garbage or other solid waste.
Any contractor working on the property of another person or owner shall, upon completion of the work undertaken or upon termination of the undertaking for any other reason, remove from the work site property all yard rubbish or trash that is on such property as a result of the contractor, its subcontractor, agent or employee working thereon, including, but not limited to, yard rubbish or trash resulting from trimming trees, clearing land, excavating, bulldozing, cutting trees and site preparation for building. Sec. 22-26. - Bulk waste.
modified
(a)
The city's bulk waste collection service was established for the benefit of its residential customers who have the mobile toter. Acceptable bulk waste items include furniture, carpeting, mattresses and debris from residential remodeling and repair operations (if the work is performed by the resident and the proper building permit is displayed), household appliances or "white goods," including items such as refrigerators, washing machines or dryers.
(b)
Commercial, multi-family, industrial, institutional or other similar facilities are required to make private arrangements to dispose of their bulk waste. Bulk waste from these facilities is prohibited from staying on the curb more than five (5) days. Any such materials found in the right-of-way after five (5) days shall be immediately removed by the city, and the costs of such removal will be billed to the property owner.
(c)
Preparation of bulk waste:
(1)
Bulk waste shall be placed within five (5) feet of the curb and at least three (3) feet away from other collection items and obstacles such as mailboxes, phone poles, vehicles, etc. Do not block the sidewalk. If the department of public works is unable to reach the bulk waste due to an obstruction, it will not be collected.
(2)
Bulk waste shall be neatly slacked in a pile and not scattered.
(3)
Carpet, padding and rugs shall be rolled in five-foot sections and tied or taped.
(4)
Any refrigerator, freezer, icebox or other similar receptacle that has an airtight door and is accessible to children shall have the door removed prior to being placed curbside for collection.
(5)
For items that contain glass, the glass shall be extensively taped and cross taped to prevent it from shattering.
(6)
Bulk waste should not be placed in ditches or along the curbs. When ditches and storm drains get clogged with debris, water no longer drains from the street, it ponds along streets and can flood intersections and homes.
(d)
Bulk waste that will not be collected:
(1)
Auto parts, dirt, shingles, pallets, concrete blocks, concrete, bricks, fuel/oil tanks, manufacturing process debris, lose or dangerous refuse.
(2)
Debris from work performed by anyone other than the resident.
(3)
Building materials from a contractor.
(4)
Materials from evictions, foreclosures, unoccupied properties, home renovation/remodeling debris from contractors, commercial properties or apartment complexes.
(5)
Debris from vacant property that has been empty for more than sixty (60) days.
(6)
Debris of any type on vacant lots.
(7)
Bulk waste that is mixed with other types of solid waste.
Any contractor working on the property of another person or owner shall, upon completion of the work undertaken or upon termination of the undertaking for any other reason, remove from the work site property these items including but not limited to brick, stone, asphalt, plumbing materials, renovation/remodeling debris, materials from evictions, foreclosures, unoccupied/vacant properties, roofing, floor coverings, gutters and shingles that is on such property as a result of the contractor, its subcontractor, agent or employee working thereon. Any such materials found in the right-of-way shall be immediately removed by the city, and costs of such removal will be billed to the property owner.
(f)
Bulk waste that is not properly prepared or is mixed with garbage or other solid waste shall not be collected.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15; Ord. No. 2023-27, § 3, 8-1-23)
Sec. 22-27. - Disposition of industrial waste.
Industrial waste shall be collected, removed and disposed of by the operator of the factory, plant or enterprise creating or causing the same in accordance with regulations promulgated by the city council.

Sec. 22-29. - Confinement of animals which interfere with collectors.
Any occupant owning or possessing an animal on the premises which may bite or attempt to bite a collector while engaged in collecting refuse shall be required to secure the animal(s) or place the receptacles in such manner that such animal shall not constitute a hazard or endanger a solid waste collector.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-30. - Fire prevention.
It shall be unlawful for any person to set or cause to be set any fire in a solid waste container. No person shall place embers, ashes or other materials into a solid waste container which would create a fire hazard.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
Sec. 22-31. - Penalty.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be subject to the maximum punishment as provided in section 4.13 of the City Charter.
(Ord. No. 2015-26, 10-20-15)
