Sec. 54-1. - Public drunkenness.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be and appear in an intoxicated condition on any street, park or any other place of public gathering or assembly within the city limits.
(Code 1986, § 11-1-1)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 6.
Sec. 54-2. - Disorderly conduct.
It shall be unlawful and disorderly conduct for any person to:
(1)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby a reasonable person would be placed in fear of safety for life or limb;
(2)
Place the property of another in serious danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Use fighting words directed toward another, who becomes outraged and thus creates a turmoil;
(4)
Violently interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation; or
(5)
Congregate with others to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuse to clear the way when ordered by lawful authority to do so.
(Code 1986, § 11-1-2)
Sec. 54-3. - Discharge of firearms.
It will be unlawful for any person to discharge any firearms, slingshots, air rifles, or any other device discharging shot or other pellets within the corporate limits of the city unless it is absolutely necessary to discharge the firearms for the protection of person or property; provided, that the firing of such weapons at the area known as the rifle range and in the direction where targets were formerly placed may be authorized upon special permission from the city manager, the city manager being given discretionary power to grant such permission for days other than Sunday upon condition that the person desiring such permission will assume responsibility for the exercise of the privilege in a safe and proper manner in compliance with city ordinances.
(Code 1986, § 11-1-3)
State Law reference— Discharge of firearm near public highway or street, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-103; discharge of firearm on property of another, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-104; discharge of firearm on Sunday, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-105; Georgia Firearms and Weapons Act, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-120 et seq.
Sec. 54-4. - Operation of public amusements.
(a)
No business or commercial establishment which operates pool and/or billiard tables, arcade, pinballs, video arcade, video poker or other similar self-contained game or gaming equipment shall remain open to the public between the hours of 1:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m.
(b)
In the event a business or commercial establishment which operates pool and/or billiard tables, arcade, pinballs, video arcade, video poker or other similar self-contained game or gaming equipment also operates businesses or activities other than described herein during the hours prescribed in subsection (a) of this section, then the business may continue to operate the activities so allowed if the portions of the business or establishment containing pool and/or billiard tables, arcade, pinballs, video arcade, video poker or other similar self-contained game or game equipment is closed to public access or the equipment and devices described herein are rendered inoperative.
(c)
Penalty. Any person and/or company violating the provisions of this section shall for each offense be punished as provided in section 4.13 of the Charter of the city.
(d)
This article shall not be construed to amend or repeal any provision of the malt beverage and wine ordinance.
(Ord. No. 99-73, 2-28-00)
Cross reference— Amusements and entertainment, ch. 10.
Sec. 54-5. - Alcoholic beverages.
No alcoholic beverages, including beer and wine, shall be consumed or possessed within any business in the city or in any public place.
(Code 1986, § 11-1-5)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages generally, ch. 6.
Sec. 54-6. - Obstruction of easements and rights-of-way.
No person shall erect a fence or other permanent structure on a drainage, sewer, or other city easement or on any city right-of-way property.
(Code 1986, § 11-1-6)
Sec. 54-7. - Keeping of junk, abandoned appliances, inoperative vehicles and other equipment on property.
(a)
In order to eliminate unsightly, unhealthy or dangerous situations, and in order to protect property values and to enhance the beauty of the city, it shall be unlawful for any person to own, rent, lease or be in possession of any premises, dwelling, dwelling unit or other structure, place or vacant lot wherein are kept dilapidated furniture, appliances, machinery or equipment, including automobiles, which are either in a wholly or partially wrecked, junked, dismantled or inoperative condition and which are not completely enclosed within a building.
(b)
Should the chief of police determine that any person is violating the terms of this section, the city manager shall give the offending party ten days' notice within which to eliminate the unsightly, unhealthy or dangerous situation. This notice shall be in writing and shall be a prerequisite to the bringing of charges against an offender. Should the person to whom the notice is directed fail to comply with the requests made within the ten-day period, the law enforcement officers of the city shall be authorized to proceed with the bringing of charges as for the violation of any city ordinance. Each day the unsightly, unhealthy or dangerous situation exists shall be deemed a separate offense.
(c)
Furniture, appliances, machinery or equipment, including automobiles, which remain on the same property for a period of 30 days after either a plea or a finding of guilty shall be presumed to be abandoned and subject to being removed from the property by the chief of police or his duly authorized agent or employee.
(Code 1986, § 11-1-10)
Cross reference— Environment, ch. 34.
State Law reference— Authority to provide by ordinance for removal and disposal of junked vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-4; abandoned motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1 et seq.; when police officers may remove vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-206; removal of improperly parked cars or trespassing personal property, O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13.
Sec. 54-8. - Noise; creating unnecessary noise.
(a)
The creating of any unreasonably loud, disturbing and unnecessary noise within the limits of the city is prohibited. Noise of such character, intensity or duration as to be detrimental to the life or health of any individual, or in disturbance of the public peace and welfare, is prohibited.
(b)
The following acts, among others, are declared to be loud, disturbing and unnecessary noises and noises in violation of this section; but this enumeration shall not be deemed to be exclusive:
(1)
Horns. The sounding of any horn or signal device on any automobile, motorcycle, bus or other vehicle while not in motion except as a danger signal if another vehicle is approaching apparently out of control, or, if in motion, only as a danger signal; the creation by means of any such signal device of any unreasonably loud or harsh sound or the sounding of that device for an unnecessary or unreasonable period of time.
(2)
Musical instruments. The playing of any radio, phonograph or any musical instrument in such a manner or with such volume, particularly during the hours between 12:00 midnight and 7:00 a.m., as to annoy or disturb the quiet, comfort or repose of persons in any office, hospital or in any dwelling, hotel or other type of residence, or any persons in the vicinity; except this section shall not apply to schools of music between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m.
(3)
Voices. Yelling, shouting, hooting, whistling or singing on the public streets, particularly between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 7:00 a.m., or at any time or place so as to annoy or disturb the quiet, comfort or repose of persons in any hospital, dwelling, hotel or other type of residence, or any persons in the vicinity.
(4)
Steam whistles. The blowing of any steam whistle attached to any stationary boiler, except to give notice of the time to begin or stop work, or as a warning of fire or danger, or upon request of proper city authority.
(5)
Construction work. The erection (including excavating), demolition, alteration or repair of any building in any residential district or section, the excavation of streets and highways in any residential district or section, other than between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. on weekdays except in cases of urgent necessity, and then only with a permit from the city, which permit may be granted for a period not to exceed 60 days while the emergency continues.
(6)
On streets of institutions requiring quiet. The creation of any excessive noise on any street and adjacent to any school, institution of learning, church or court while they are in session, or adjacent to any hospital, which unreasonably interferes with the workings or sessions of such institutions.
(7)
Loudspeakers on vehicles. The use of mechanical loudspeakers or amplifiers on trucks or other moving or standing vehicles for advertising or other purposes.
(8)
Stationary automobiles. The use of automobiles, either street legal or otherwise, in a stationary manner as to annoy or disturb the quiet, comfort or repose of persons in any office, hospital, or any dwelling, hotel or other type of residence, or any persons in the vicinity.
(c)
None of the foregoing terms or prohibitions shall apply to or be enforced against:
(1)
Any vehicle of the city while engaged upon necessary public business;
(2)
Excavations or repairs of bridges, streets or highways, by or on behalf of the city, county or state during the night, when the public welfare and convenience renders it impossible to perform that work during the day; nor shall this section apply to work performed by public utility companies under like conditions and circumstances, or when there is urgent necessity for such work; and
(3)
The reasonable use of amplifiers or loudspeakers in the course of public addresses which are noncommercial in character.
(d)
The prohibitions shall not be applicable to any parade, celebration or performance for which a written permit has been previously obtained from the city manager.
(e)
It shall be unlawful to use, maintain or operate loudspeakers, sound trucks, amplifiers or other mechanical or electrical devices for increasing the volume of sound, upon the streets, sidewalks, parks or other public places of the city; provided that nothing in this section shall apply to the United States of America, the state, the county nor the city, nor to public agencies.
(Code 1986, § 11-1-8; Ord. No. 95-12, 8-28-1995)
Cross reference— Environment, ch. 34.
State Law reference— Limits on sound volume produced by radio, tape player or other mechanical sound-making device or instrument from within motor vehicle, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-14; sale of muffler which causes excessive noise prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 40-8-71(c).
Sec. 54-9. - Regulation of false alarms.
(a)
Purpose. To regulate responsibilities of those persons who purchase, rent, or use alarm systems, devices or services. This section is also intended to encourage the improvement and reliability of these systems, devices and services and to insure that police and fire department personnel will not be unduly diverted from responding from actual criminal activity as a result of responding to false alarms. This section specifically encompasses fire alarms, burglar alarms, robbery alarms, hold-up alarms, medical alarms, and panic alarms, both audible and inaudible (silent). The provisions of this section shall not apply to alarm systems which are owned or maintained by the city with the exception of those alarms which are installed by a tenant on premises in which the city merely has a property interest as a landlord.
(b)
Definitions.
(1)
Alarm shall mean and include any fire, robbery, burglary, hold-up, medical or panic alarm that transmit any signal (audible, visual or silent) to the city, indicating a pre-determined emergency condition, whether the alarm is received either:
a.
By a telephone line to a designated position on an alarm panel;
b.
By a private alarm service company relayed to the city communications center telephone;
c.
By an automated telephone alarm system, playing a recorded message when received on the city communication center telephone; or
d.
By a signal relayed to the city communication center by a third party.
(2)
False alarms shall mean the activation of an alarm system where none of the emergencies for which the alarm system has been installed, have in fact occurred.
(3)
Response shall mean the dispatch of any police or fire unit to the premises where an alarm has been activated or the arrival of an officer to the location by any means of notification.
(d)
Automatic dialers; automatic response alarm systems.
It shall be unlawful to key automatic response alarm systems to any city police department telephone number. Such alarm shall only be keyed to a private answering service, who can then notify the city or the city police department.
State Law reference— O.C.G.A. § 36-60-12.
(d)
False alarms.
(1)
Fees for response per police/fire response to any false alarm of an automatic alarm response system or independent alarm system, the chief of police/fire chief or their designees shall charge and collect from the person, business or legal entity using or maintaining the automatic response alarm system or independent alarm system, fees as follows:
a.
For a first or second response to premises to which no other false alarm has occurred within a calendar year, no fee shall be charged.
b.
For a third response to a false alarm within a calendar year, no fee shall be charged, but notice shall be given to the personal business using or maintaining the alarms system of the false alarms and the requirements of this section, including the fee requirement for any subsequent false alarm responses by the police/fire departments within the calendar year.
c.
Upon a fourth response to a false alarm that premises within a calendar year, a fee of $50.00 shall be charged to the person occupying the premises. Upon a fifth response within a calendar year, a fee of $75.00 shall be charged; and for all succeeding responses within a calendar year, a fee of $100.00 shall be charged per response.
d.
All charges when there has been a response to a false alarm at a premises within a calendar year greater than three responses shall be made through the Fort Oglethorpe Municipal Court by way of a citation.
(e)
Automatic cut-off system.
All audible alarms, sirens, bells or horns shall have an automatic cut-off so that the alarm will not operate for more than 30 continuous minutes after activation.
(f)
Penalties.
Any person, firm, or corporation violating the provisions of subparagraphs (c) or (e) of this section shall be fined not less than $100.00 nor more than $500.00 by the municipal court of the city for each offense.
(Ord. No. 2000-57, § 1, 1-16-01)
