ARTICLE XVI. - TEMPORARY STRUCTURE AND USE PERMIT

Sec. 5-249. - Definitions.
Adjacent property: A lot or parcel of land that shares all or part of a common lot line with another lot or parcel of land. For purposes of this article, it shall also include lots or parcels directly across street rights-of-way.
Temporary structure: A building without any permanent foundation or footing and that is removed when the designated time period, activity, or use for which the temporary structure was erected has ceased.
Temporary use: A use established for a limited duration with the intent to discontinue such use upon the expiration of the time period.
(Ord. No. 02-15, 1-23-03)
Sec. 5-250. - Purpose and intent.
The purpose of the temporary structure and use permit is to allow certain necessary structures and uses that normally would not be allowed in a particular zoning district on a temporary basis. No structure to be used as a residence shall be permitted under this article.
(Ord. No. 02-15, 1-23-03)
Sec. 5-251. - General.
(a)
The city administrator may issue temporary permits, valid for a period of not more than one (1) year from the date of issuance, for the following structures and uses that would not normally conform to the zoning code:
(1)
Storage of equipment during the building of roads or developments.
(2)
Real estate office used for the sale of lots or housing in a subdivision.
(3)
Temporary office structures.
(4)
Temporary storage of structures or equipment.
(5)
Sheds used in conjunction with the building of a structure.
(6)
Other nonresidential structures or uses of a temporary nature.
(Ord. No. 02-15, 1-23-03)
Sec. 5-252. - Restrictions.
(a)
The city administrator may require, as he deems appropriate, conditions to mitigate negative impacts.
(b)
A bond, letter of credit, or other acceptable security in the amount of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) is posted to insure removal of the temporary structure or use.
(c)
Upon the expiration of the temporary permit, the applicant shall have thirty (30) days within which to remove and/or discontinue such temporary structure or use. If at the end of this time period such temporary use or structure is not removed or discontinued, said cash or surety bond shall be forfeited.
(d)
Temporary permits may be renewed by city council approval provided that proof of additional time is needed.
(1)
A public hearing shall be held in conjunction with requests for renewal.
(2)
Notice shall be provided to adjacent property owners for all petitions for renewal.
(Ord. No. 02-15, 1-23-03)
Sec. 5-253. - Application procedure.
(a)
The applicant will complete an application form to be provided at city hall. The applicant shall pay a permit fee in the amount provided in section 5-26.
(b)
Building, electrical, and plumbing inspections shall be required as necessary.
(Ord. No. 02-15, 1-23-03)
Sec. 5-254. - Appeals.
(a)
The applicant or adjacent property owners have the right to appeal the decision of the city administrator.
(b)
Notice of the city administrator's findings and decision, and information describing the appeals process, shall be mailed by first class mail to the applicant and the record owners of all adjacent property owners.
(c)
An appeal must be filed within ten (10) days of the decision. The board of zoning appeals shall make a recommendation to city council. City council shall make final decision on approval or denial of an appealed permit.
(d)
Persons filing an appeal shall pay the prevailing fee for filing appeals to the board of zoning appeals.
(Ord. No. 02-15, 1-23-03)
