Sec. 5-16. - Adopted.
(a)
There is hereby adopted by reference that publication known as the Standard Building Code, latest edition; including Appendices A, D, H and M, recommended by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any of the provisions of the code adopted in subsection (a).
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90; Ord. No. 91-8, 9-26-91; Ord. No. 96-05, 3-28-96; Ord. No. 97-3, 2-13-97)
Sec. 5-17. - "Municipality" defined.
As used in the code adopted by the provisions of this article, the term "municipality" means the City of Port Wentworth.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-18. - Fee for building permits required.
On all buildings, structures or alterations requiring a building permit, a fee for each building permit shall be paid as required at the time of filing application, in accordance with the schedule outlined in section 5-26 herein.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-19. - Previous fee schedules repealed.
All previous fee schedules have been repealed.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-20. - Fees mandatory.
(a)
Payment due before issuance. No permit shall be issued until the fees prescribed in this section shall have been paid, nor shall an amendment to a permit be approved until the additional fee, if any, due to an increase in the estimated cost of the building or structure, has been paid.
(b)
Exception: Nonprofit organizations. Nonprofit organizations shall be exempt from fees when they meet the following requirements:
(1)
Be a nonprofit organization and, if a corporation of the State of Georgia, be qualified and chartered as a nonprofit organization under the laws of the State of Georgia.
(2)
Be qualified with the Internal Revenue Service of the United States and hold the written qualification as such from the Internal Revenue Service.
(3)
Be qualified with the Internal Revenue Service to receive donations over and above any fee for services rendered, to the extent that such donations are tax deductible to the donor.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-21. - Failure to obtain a permit.
(a)
If any person commences work on a building or structure before obtaining the necessary permit from the applicable governing body, he or she shall be subject to the penalties prescribed herein.
(b)
Reserved.
(c)
A separate permit will be required for all buildings. A single permit will not be allowed for multiple buildings.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-22. - Plan check fees.
For new commercial and multifamily construction, a plan checking fee shall be paid to the City of Port Wentworth. When the value of construction exceeds one thousand dollars ($1,000.00), said plan checking fee shall be equal to one-half (½) of the building permit fee. These fees are non-refundable. There will be no plan check fees assessed on new residential construction.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90; Ord. No. 00-17, 12-14-00; Ord. No. 05-19, 11-10-05; Ord. No. 07-24, 7-26-07)
Sec. 5-23. - Procedure for permit refunds.
(a)
Permit holder must return all applicable permit forms and receipts.
(b)
Permit refund will be for total amount paid minus plan checking fee as set forth in section 5-22.
(c)
No refunds will be given for permits where construction has not started within six (6) months of issuance, provided the building official has not issued or granted any extensions of time as provided for in Section A103.6 of the Standard Building Code.
(d)
No refunds will be given for work started and which is subsequently discontinued.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-24. - Reinspections.
If the building inspector or his duly authorized representative shall, upon his inspection after the completion of the work or apparatus, find the same does not conform to and comply with the provisions of this code, he shall notify the contractor, indicating the corrections to be made, and then he shall again inspect the work or apparatus without further charge; but when extra inspections are required for either of the following reasons, a charge of twenty dollars ($20.00) shall be levied for each reinspection. This requirement applies to all the disciplines contained in this Code:
(1)
Repairs or corrections not made when inspections are called.
(2)
Work not ready for inspection when called.
All reinspection fees shall be paid before any further inspections are made.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-25. - Building permit valuations.
If in the opinion of the building inspector or city administrator, the valuation of the building, alteration or structure appears to be underestimated on the application, the city will utilize the Marshall and Swift's Residential Cost Handbook and Marshall Valuation Service to determine the valuation. The valuation from Marshall and Swift will be the basis for permit issuance. Valuation from Marshall and Swift is determined by final cost to the buyer/owner of the structure.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90; Ord. No. 00-17, 12-14-00)
Sec. 5-26. - Permit fees.
modified
On all new buildings, structures or alternations requiring a building permit, as set forth in Section A1-3.1 of the Standard Building Code, the fee shall be paid as required at the time of filing the application in accordance with the following schedule. The city administrator of Port Wentworth shall have full authority to lower or give free inspections for schools or civic groups, etc. Each building permit shall have an administrative fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) in addition to the valuation fee. Permits issued for construction that was started and not completed by the original permit holder shall be charged twenty-five dollars ($25.00) per remaining inspection and a one-time fifty-dollar ($50.00) administrative fee.

Sec. 5-27. - City permits—Time limits.
(a)
Building permits.
(b)
Sign permits.
(c)
Mobile homes permits.
(d)
Excavation permits.
(e)
Fence permits.
(f)
Land disturbing activity permits.
