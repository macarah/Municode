5.40.010 - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this Chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dealer in precious metals or gems means a person, either owner or manager, whose business, whether known as a redemption center, gold and silver dealer, or any other name, involves receiving by buying or trading of, for, or in precious metals such as gold or silver or platinum, including coins, and the receiving by buying or trading jewelry, gems, or precious or semiprecious stones; however it shall not include a business that involves purchases from licensed wholesalers in the normal course of business. The term also shall include any definition under State law.
A dealer in precious metals and gems shall be classified as either Class A or Class B. A Class B dealer in precious metals and gems shall be a dealer in precious metals and gems for which the business of purchasing precious metals and gems from non-wholesale sources shall be incidental to the conduct of the operation of a retail jewelry store. For the purposes of this section, "Incidental" shall mean not more than 25 transactions per month, nor more than 5 transactions per day, and no more than one transaction per person, per month. A Class A dealer in precious metals and gems shall be any such license holder that does not qualify as Class B.
Employee means any person employed or allowed to perform duties by a pawnbroker or dealer in precious metals or gems, whether or not such person receives a salary, wages or other compensation or consideration, but not including the person covered on the license or renewal application.
Goods carries its normal meaning and further it should be construed in the broadest sense allowable and should include all forms of personal property.
Interest in a business means an interest held by an owner or co-owner of a registered business; by a partner of a general or limited partnership which owns or co-owns a registered business; a stockholder of five percent or more shares in any closely held corporation which owns or co-owns a registered business; an owner, co-owner, lessor, sublessor, partner in a partnership, stockholder of five percent or more stock of a closely held corporation, or stockholder of 50 percent or more of any corporation owning or leasing any real estate upon which is located a registered business; or anyone sharing in the income or corpus of any trust or estate having such an interest; or a person having subscriptions, options, and/or warranty for purchase of five percent or more of a closely held corporation's stock or 50 percent or more of any corporation's stock.
Owner means any person or partner or stockholder owning at least a 25 percent share of a business.
Pawnbroker means any person who shall in any manner lend or advance money or other things for profit on the pledge or possession of personal property, or other valuable things other than securities or written evidences of indebtedness or who deals in the purchasing of personal property or other valuable things on condition of selling the same, back to the seller at a stipulated price. The term also shall include any definition under State law.
Person means an individual, partnership, corporation, joint venture, trust, association, or any other legal entity however organized.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.020 - License Required.
A.
Every person establishing a business in the City as a pawnbroker and/or dealer in precious metals or gems after the effective date of this Chapter, or who takes purchases, acquires, or in any manner assumes control of a pawn shop after the effective date of this Chapter, prior to commencing business, shall make proper application for and receive the appropriate business license as set forth in this Chapter. Such license shall be in addition to the registration for and payment of the occupational tax license.
B.
No application for license to do business as a pawnbroker and/or Class A dealer in precious metals or gems shall be considered or approved or a license issued where the total number of pawnbroker and/or Class A dealer in precious metals or gems licenses issued and in effect at the time of such application numbers at least one for each 5,000 residents according to the latest officially and finally published annual population estimates by the Atlanta Regional Commission. There shall be no numerical limitation on issuance of licenses for Class B dealers in precious metals or gems. In making this computation, fractions of each 5,000 residents shall not be considered. For purposes of this section, non-conforming pawnshops shall be included for purposes of calculating the number of outstanding licenses.
Whenever it shall be recognized by the Community Development Director, or suggested by any person, that the release of population projections by the Atlanta Regional Commission results in the availability of one or more additional licenses, and it is determined by the Community Development Director that one or more additional licenses are available, the City shall publish a notice, one time, advising the public of such, and that applications will be received for a period of 45 days subsequent to the publication.
If, during this period, applications are received which exceed the number of newly available licenses, there shall be held a lottery, administered by the Community Development Director, to determine the order in which applications shall be processed. Thereafter, the applications shall be processed in the order reflected in the results of the lottery. Once the processing of applications has resulted in the issuance of licenses up to the limit contained in this Chapter, the remaining applications shall be returned to the applicants, unprocessed.
If no applications are received during the forty-five-day period after publication, applications shall thereafter be processed in the order in which they may thereafter be received.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.030 - Application for license and permit.
A.
Qualifications.
(a)
The applicant and each individual with an interest in the business shall be a citizen of the United States.
(b)
The applicant must be a resident of the county for a period of one year preceding the date of filing application. The time requirement as to residency shall not apply to honorably discharged members of the nation's armed forces who have returned to the City to reside.
(c)
Corporations must either be incorporated in the State of Georgia or must have registered to do business in this state, and only the applying officer must meet the residency requirements.
(d)
No applicant, general manager, or person having an interest in the business shall have been convicted nor entered a plea of nolo contendere in any state, the United States, or any other country, within ten years immediately preceding the date of application for any felony, or crime involving moral turpitude, or any violation of the laws of any state, county, or municipality relating to the sale, manufacture, or distribution of alcoholic beverages, or soliciting for prostitution, pandering, letting premises for prostitution, keeping a disorderly place, illegal dealing in narcotics, sex offenses, any theft or burglary offense, receiving or possession of stolen property, or any statute or ordinance regulating the business of pawnbroker. In those instances involving corporations, this prohibition shall also apply to the corporation's president, vice-president, secretary, treasurer. In those instances involving corporations, this prohibition shall also apply to (1) any closely held corporation's president, vice-president, secretary, treasurer, and all stockholders owning five percent or more of the corporation's stock, or a person having subscriptions, options, and/or warrants for purchase of a closely held corporation's stock that when fully executed would give that person five percent or more of the corporation's stock, and (2) any shareholder owning 50 percent or more of any corporation, whether closely held or not, or a person having subscriptions, options and/or warrants for purchase of a corporation's (whether closely held or not) stock that when fully executed would give that person 50 percent or more of the corporation's stock.
(e)
Applicants shall be the owners of the business premises or the holder of any lease thereon or the general agent of the owner.
(f)
The applicant (individual in case of proprietorship, applying officer in case of corporation, and one of the partners in case of partnership) shall be responsible for the management and operation of the business if approval is granted.
(g)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement of the qualifications to the above and shall place same on file with the City before approval of the business is granted.
(h)
If the applicant is a partnership, all members of the partnership must approve of this application but only one member must meet the requirements for residency. However, all members of the partnership must meet the other qualifications. If approval of the business is granted, the approval shall apply to the partners in the name of the partnership.
(i)
No person shall be granted approval to engage in the business of pawning unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the governing body that the person is of good moral character.
(j)
An application shall not be granted, or if granted shall be revoked, should the City manager or his designee find any of the following:
(1)
The applicant and/or person with an interest in the business has conducted any business within the City which has required the necessity for unusual police observation or inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance by the applicant or person with an interest in the business, or it was necessary for the City to take action to compel the adherence to any law, regulation, or ordinance; or
(2)
The applicant and/or person with an interest in the business has more than one such license within the City.
(3)
The applicant and/or person with an interest in the business has ever had a pawn business suspended or closed by any governing entity.
B.
Applicants for a license or permit under this Chapter shall file a written, sworn application on a form provided by the City, signed by the applicant if an individual, or if the applicant is a business entity, executed so as to contractually bind that particular business entity under Georgia law, with the City showing:
1.
The name or names of the principals, agents, and employees of the applicant's business during the time that it is proposed that it will be carried on in the City, the local address or addresses of such person or persons while engaged in such business, the permanent address or addresses of such person or persons, the capacity or capacities in which such person or persons will act (as proprietor, agent, employee or otherwise), the name and the address of the person for whose account the business will be carried on, if any, and if a corporation or other business entity, under the laws of what state it is incorporated or otherwise created or organized;
2.
Except for applications for Class B dealers in precious metals or gems, the fingerprints of the principals, agents and employees of the applicant's business;
3.
The place in the City where the applicant proposes to carry on the applicant's business, the zoning district at such place or places, and the length of time during which it is proposed that such business shall be conducted, and, except for applications for Class B dealers in precious metals or gems, a map, prepared by a registered surveyor reflecting the location of the proposed place of business and a certification that the proposed location meets all of the location restrictions contained in this Chapter;
4.
The place or places, other than the permanent place of business of the applicant, where the applicant within the six months next preceding the date of such application conducted any itinerant business, and a statement of the nature thereof and the appropriate post office(s) and street address(es) or any building(s) or office(s) in which such business was conducted;
5.
Except for applications for Class B dealers in precious metals or gems, whether or not the person or persons having the management or supervision of the applicant's business have been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to a crime, misdemeanor or the violation of any municipal ordinance, and the nature of such offense and the punishment assessed therefore;
6.
If the applicant is a corporation or other business entity:
a.
Whether or not any shares in the corporation have been sold, and if so, to whom the shares were sold; and
b.
Whether or not majority control of the corporation has been transferred in the preceding year.
C.
Except for applications for Class B dealers in precious metals or gems, every applicant for a license shall post with the City Clerk a surety bond in favor of the City in the amount of $8,000.00, which bond shall not be accepted unless it be with a corporate surety licensed to do business in the state and unless it is conditioned that the applicant shall comply fully with all applicable provisions of federal, state and local laws, rules and regulations concerning the activity for which the applicant is to be licensed, and that the property purchased is guaranteed to be in accordance with the specifications, standards and conditions represented to the purchaser by the applicant. A blanket bond otherwise meeting the requirements of this section shall be accepted.
D.
If any person shall be aggrieved by the conduct of any such licensee, except for applications for Class B dealers in precious metals or gems, may receive judgment against him therefore, such person may, after the return unsatisfied, either in whole or in part, of any execution issued upon such judgment, maintain an action in his own name upon the bond of this license in any court having jurisdiction of this amount of such judgment remaining unsatisfied.
E.
In lieu of posting such bond, the licensee may post cash.
F.
The application shall be under oath and shall state thereon that "It is unlawful for any person to make a false statement on this application." Any false statement made on the form will be cause to revoke the license issued thereunder.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.040 - Exclusions/exceptions.
Businesses which limit the purchase of used valuables to the acceptance of trade-ins for credit for new goods or the dispensing of refunds in regard to valuables previously purchased from such business shall not be governed by the provisions of this Chapter.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.050 - Employee permit.
Each employee of a pawnbroker or dealer in precious metals or gems must have an approved employee permit. Any co-owner, agent, employee or any other person authorized to transact business other than licensee shall be required to have the above employee permit for each license under which he/she operates or transacts business. This permit must be displayed along with the business license in accordance with license display requirements.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.060 - Business license and permit fees.
A.
Designated: All businesses licensed under this Chapter shall pay the following designated license and permit fees per calendar year:
1.
Pawnshops or Class A dealers in precious metals or gems. This fee shall include the initial registration of named applicant, $1,500.00.
2.
Class B dealers in precious metals or gems. This fee shall include the initial registration of named applicant, $250.00
3.
Employee permits, per application, $20.00.
There shall be no proration or refund of any license fee.
B.
Transfer of license or permit.
(a)
When approval of a pawn business is granted pursuant to an application to the City, that approval shall not be transferable, either by location or among persons, except as hereinafter provided.
(b)
In the case of a death of any person having an interest in a business, approval of the business may be transferred to the estate of the deceased person; however, the duly appointed administrator or executor of the estate shall be named as responsible for such interest and said individual shall be subject to the qualifications of an applicant. Thereafter, the estate may transfer the license as directed; however, such transferee shall be subject to the qualifications of an applicant.
(c)
One or more partners in a partnership having an approved pawn business may withdraw in favor of one or more of the existing partners. In addition, partners or additional stockholders may be added where it is determined that the additional capital furnished is to be exclusively for additional inventory or the expansion of facilities and that the original partners are receiving none of the additional capital invested. All such additional partners or new principal stockholders must be approved by the City, and shall be subject to the qualifications of an applicant. Otherwise, no new ownership may be allowed.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.070 - Renewal of license.
(a)
The renewal of an approved application is required annually. Such renewal shall be dependent upon proof that the renewal applicant meets all requirements upon which the original application was granted.
(b)
The applicant must provide at the time of application for renewal an affidavit substantiating compliance with the requirements set forth in this article.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.080 - Location of businesses.
All businesses licensed under this Chapter shall be located in strict compliance with the City's zoning ordinance and at a location with an identifiable street address, which shall be stated on the license or renewal application along with other required information; and no business licensed under this Chapter shall change its address without giving advance notice to the City Clerk, the building official and the police Department, and receiving a written approval of the change, such approval to be granted if the new location complies with this Chapter and the City's zoning ordinance.
In addition to the provisions of the Zoning Ordinance, the following restrictions on locations for licensees shall apply:
1)
No license shall be issued for any location lying within the boundaries reflected on a map entitled "Pawn Shop Ordinance Boundary," attached hereto and made a part hereof, and kept in the office of the City Clerk.
2)
No license shall be issued for any location within an absolute radius of 4,000 feet from the location of an existing licensee.
3)
No license shall be issued for any proposed location on any road or street upon which there is an existing licensee.
4)
No licensed shall be issued pursuant to this Chapter to operate in or from a motel or hotel.
These restrictions shall not prohibit the renewal of any license issued on or before March 1, 2011 which does not meet these limitations. These location restrictions shall not apply to Class B dealers in precious metals or gems.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.090 - Records to be kept.
All pawnbrokers and dealers of precious metals or gems, in addition to other records, shall keep books wherein shall be entered an accurate description of all property received by or pledged or sold to them. Such description shall include the brand name of the article, any identifying mark, number, initial, social security number or name of any person or company, and a statement of the kind of material of which it is made. In such books there shall be entered also the name, age, address, signature and identification number from a driver's license or similar identification of the person by whom same was delivered, deposited or sold, the name of the broker, agent or employee receiving the item, the time when such transaction occurred, the price paid for the goods purchased and the check number issued if applicable. These entries shall be made no later than one hour after the transaction. A permanent bound set of the pawn tickets shall constitute the book required by this Chapter. All books and records required by this Chapter and items pledged or sold under this section shall at all times be subject to inspection and examination by any law enforcement officer of the City or the state. It shall be unlawful for any person engaged in any business licensed under this Chapter to fail or refuse to keep the records as provided in this Chapter, or to make false entries concerning the transaction or to fail or refuse to permit an inspection by any law enforcement officer of the City or the state of such records and of the property pledged with or sold to them.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.100 - Standard forms required.
All businesses licensed pursuant to this Chapter shall use a standard form for pawn and a standard form for purchase, and no ticket may be used for a dual purpose. Standard forms may differ only by the name of the business and business address. The design of this form shall be provided by the police department and the forms shall be numbered and chronologically used and sent to the police department. Each number missing from the chronological sequence shall be considered a separate violation of this Chapter.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.110 - Conformance with sections 5.40.080 and 5.40.090.
No business licensed under this Chapter shall take any item in pawn or purchase without entering each transaction of such goods on his books and on the standard forms as provided for herein in section 5.40.080 and section 5.40.090.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.120 - Report to police.
1.
Every pawnbroker and Class A dealers in precious metals or gems business licensed under this Chapter shall make a weekly report electronically or in writing to the police department of all property pledged or purchased during the week ending at 10:00 p.m. on Friday of each week of the date of the report for the prior seven days. Such reports shall be legibly written or typed in English and shall show:
A.
The name and address of the business;
B.
Time and date of transaction;
C.
Sequel number of the pawn or purchase tickets;
D.
Amount paid or advanced;
E.
Full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry and all identifying names, marks, numbers, and initials; and
F.
A description of the person selling or pawning such item including name, address, race and/or apparent ethnicity, weight and height, and social security number.
2.
Every Class B dealers in precious metals or gems business licensed under this Chapter shall make a weekly report in writing to the police department of all precious metals or gems purchased during the week ending at 10:00 p.m. on Friday of each week of the date of the report for the prior 7 days. Such report shall be legibly written or typed in English and shall show:
A.
The name and address of the business;
B.
Time and date of transaction;
C
Amount paid or advanced;
D.
Full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design. Kind and number of stones in jewelry and all identifying names, marks, numbers, and initials; and
E.
A description of the person selling or pawning such item including name, address, race and/or apparent ethnicity, weight and height, and social security number.
In addition, every such dealer shall, on a quarterly basis beginning on March 31 of each calendar year, tender a sworn affidavit that such purchases from non-wholesale sources are Incidental, as that term is defined herein. In the event that such quarterly report is not filed, or if filed reflects that such purchases are not Incidental, upon written notice from the City, the license to conduct the business of Class B dealer in precious metals and gems shall be automatically revoked.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.130 - Identification required.
All businesses licensed under this Chapter shall, when receiving in pawn or in purchasing goods, require a proper identification, such as a driver's license or military I.D. card or state approved I.D. card or company employee I.D. card containing a picture showing positive identification of the person who is pawning, selling or bartering and verify the identification.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.140 - Fingerprint required.
Every business licensed under this Chapter shall require every person from whom it accepts goods on pawn or purchase to place the fingerprint of his right index finger (if right index finger is missing, the next finger in line will be used) in ink on the front of the copy of the pawn or purchase ticket which is to be sent to the police department.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.150 - Retention of purchased articles.
Every business licensed under this Chapter shall hold, on the premises designated in the license for doing business, every item required to be listed under section 5.40.090 for at least 15 days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment, or otherwise. The provisions of this section shall not apply to purchases from licensed wholesalers in the normal course of business.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.160 - Retention of pawned articles.
Every business licensed under this Chapter which takes goods on pawn shall hold such goods for at least 30 days, unless redeemed by the owner or on authority of the owner, before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.170 - Display of goods pledged or purchased.
For a period of 15 days from the date of purchase and 30 days from the date of pledge, unless redeemed by or by authority of the person who pledged the goods, all goods purchased or pawned and required to be listed under section 5.40.090 shall be kept on the premises designated in the license for doing business. Every item required to be kept on the premises under this section shall have attached to it either a copy of the form required in section 5.40.090 or the number of such form. Any person desiring to examine any merchandise shall first obtain a copy of a police department incident or stolen property report. Upon presentation of a copy of such report to any licensee or Employee and upon giving in writing to such licensee or Employee a full description of the stolen items sought, including all identifying characteristics, his name, address, signature, identification, and incident or stolen property report number and issuing department, such individual shall [b]e allowed to examine any merchandise, of the type described on the premises. Such written information shall be kept in sequential, dated order in a permanent file or book. This record shall be kept in addition to all other records required under this Chapter.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.180 - Alteration.
Any item required to be kept or displayed under the provisions of sections 5.40.140 through 5.40.160 shall be kept in their original form as they existed when first purchased and their physical characteristics shall not be altered in any manner whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.190 - Erasures, obliterations, write-overs and type-overs prohibited.
No person licensed under this Chapter shall erase, obliterate, type over, write over or in any way cause the original entry on the records required under this Chapter to become illegible. All pawn or purchase tickets filled out in error shall be sent to the police department in their numbered order along with daily reports and shall have the word 'VOID' typed or written across the front. Such entry shall not be used to cause the original entry to be illegible. If an error is made and the ticket is still to be used, then a single line shall be drawn through the incorrect entry. This line shall not cause the original entry to be illegible, and the correct information entry shall be made on the next available area.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.200 - Retention of records.
The record of each pawn or purchase transaction regulated by this Chapter shall be maintained for a period of not less than four years from date of transaction.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.210 - Hours of operation.
All businesses licensed under this Chapter shall close their establishment and cease transacting any business no later than 10:00 p.m. each day and begin their operation and commence transacting business at no time before 7:00 a.m. each day.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.220 - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed under this Chapter to receive in pawn or in purchase any goods of any character or of any description from any person who has not attained the age of 21 years.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.230 - Location of business for transactions.
No business transactions by any business licensed under this Chapter shall be conducted outside the building designated as the normal place to conduct transactions unless such business does not have a building. For the purposes of this Section, the term "business transaction" shall mean any part of business, including inspection or appraisal of any goods when it is possible for such goods to be taken inside the building, where transactions normally are made (the term "possible" being here used in the broadest sense); and no person other than those processing a license or employee permit shall be allowed to conduct any form of business transactions either inside the establishment or on any part of outside land area covered under address of the license.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.240 - Identification numbers required.
No business licensed under this Chapter shall accept any articles which have had the serial number altered, obliterated or removed from such article or which the person who is making the business transaction knows, or should know, should have a serial number. Licensees shall immediately report any attempted business transactions of this nature to the police department.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.250 - Violations; penalties.
Any person licensed under this Chapter who shall:
A.
Fail to make any entry of any required item in the permanent record book;
B.
Make any false entry therein;
C.
Falsify, obliterate, record upon so that information is illegible, destroy or remove from his place of business, such permanent record book;
D.
Refuse to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer to inspect such permanent record book, or any goods in his possession, during the ordinary hours of business or any reasonable time, or refuse to allow any member of the general public to inspect goods in his possession being held under pawn or purchase, within the 15-day purchase and 30-day pawn dates, pursuant to section 5.40.160, during ordinary hours of business;
E.
Fail to maintain a record of each pawn or purchase transaction for at least four (4) years; or
F.
Violate any other provision of this Chapter.
Operation of any business under this Chapter in violation of any provision of this Chapter shall constitute a separate offense for each day of operation.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.260 - Inspection.
As a condition of the granting of the license under this Chapter, every licensee agrees to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer, pursuant to his official duties, to be and remain on or about his premises without the formality of a warrant, provided the officer does not unduly interfere with the licensee's legitimate business operation.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.270 - Nonformed precious metal.
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed under this Chapter to purchase precious metals in a melted, smelted or nonformed state unless the purchase is from a registered dealer in precious metals or gems.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.280 - Stolen items.
Every licensee under this Chapter and every employee of a licensee shall, immediately upon request, turn over to the police department any item believed by the police department to be stolen. The police department shall furnish a property receipt including a case number of recovery to the licensee or Employee surrendering such items. Such items shall be returned to the licensee only after the police department determines that such item is not stolen. If it is determined that such item is stolen, it shall be returned to its lawful owner.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.290 - Separate business establishment.
No business licensed under this Chapter shall be designed or operated so as to allow access to such business immediately from any other business without first exiting the other business into a common or open area to which the public at large has access.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.300 - State law.
This Chapter is to be construed as consistent with state law. However, these provisions are to be applied in lieu of state law and state fee and registration requirements whenever possible. It is the intent of the City council that all duties delegated to the chief law enforcement officer of the City shall be exercised as provided under this Chapter.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
5.40.310 - Revocation.
1)
Upon a determination by the City Administrator, or his designee, that a license holder may be in violation of this Chapter, the City Administrator will prepare an appropriate notice to the license holder advising him/it of his conclusions and that with that Notice, following an appropriate grace period for appeal, the license is suspended for a set period of time, or revoked, and that further conduct of such business not consistent with such order, after the deadline, and without appeal, will be unlawful.
2)
The Notice from the City Administrator will be self-executing. After the period for appeal, the decision of the City Administrator, unless modified by the hearing officer as provided below, will be final;
3)
The license holder shall have a 5 calendar day period to file an appeal. His period shall run from the date the notice is received by the license holder. If an appeal is filed, the decision of the City Administrator will be stayed pending the appeal;
4)
The appeal shall be heard by a hearing officer, to be selected by the City;
5)
At the appeal hearing, both sides may be represented by counsel, present evidence and/or witnesses, and cross-examine witnesses for the opposing side; and
6)
The decision of the hearing officer shall be in writing, shall detail his findings and his conclusions, and shall be final. The decision of the hearing officer may be appealed to the Superior Court of Henry County by writ of certiorari.
(Ord. No. 11-05-16(B), § I, 5-16-2011)
