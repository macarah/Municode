Sections:
15.06.010 - Water mains.
A.
In commercial or industrial areas, to include motels, hotels, nursing homes, hospitals, educational buildings and office buildings, a minimum of eight-inch (8") pipe or larger water line shall be installed.
B.
In multi-family residential subdivisions or complexes, a minimum of eight-inch (8") pipe or larger water line shall be installed.
(Ord. No. 99-08-09A, 8-9-1999)
15.06.020 - Fire hydrants, residual pressures.
A.
Fire hydrants shall be three-way hydrants and shall be spaced not to exceed five hundred (500) feet with additional fire hydrants located as determined necessary to permit all portions of buildings to be reached by hose lays of not more than four hundred (400) feet by road travel.
B.
Fire hydrants shall meet the requirements of NFPA No. 24, "Fire Hydrants for Outside Protection."
C.
In all cases, residual pressures shall not be less than twenty (20) pounds per square inch. Water mains and fire hydrants shall be installed and ready for fire-fighting before any sheathing may be installed on walls and roofs of buildings, unless the sheathing is of fire-resistive construction and permission is granted by the city Fire Marshall.
(Ord. No. 99-08-09A, 8-9-1999)
15.06.030 - Construction of water mains and fire hydrants.
A.
All design standards must be preapproved before construction begins by the Fire Marshall.
B.
All materials and workmanship shall meet city standards.
(Ord. No. 99-08-09A, 8-9-1999)
