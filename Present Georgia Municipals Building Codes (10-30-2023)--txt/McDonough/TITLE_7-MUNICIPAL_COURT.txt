TITLE 7 - MUNICIPAL COURT
CHAPTER 7.02 - MUNICIPAL COURT

7.02.010 - Establishment of the court; jurisdiction.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-32-1 and the City Charter, the City hereby establishes a municipal court for the city, to be known as "The Municipal Court for the City of McDonough, Georgia," having jurisdiction over violations of municipal ordinances, as well as all other matters made subject to its jurisdiction by general law.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.020 - Continuation of existing court.
The municipal court created herein shall be a continuation of the court, by whatever name or style given, created and functioning under prior City laws or ordinances.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.030 - Terms of court.
Regular terms of court shall be convened in the City of McDonough municipal courtroom, at a day and time certain as, from time to time, set by order of the court; special sessions may be convened, as needed, by order of the court.
Whenever necessity requires, the municipal court may hold sessions at a site outside the corporate limits of the City of McDonough, so long as the location is within Henry County.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.040 - Maximum punishment established by general law.
Unless otherwise limited by a general or local law, or by ordinance, the municipal court shall be authorized to impose any punishment up to the maximums specified by general law, including the maximums specified in O.C.G.A. § 36-35-6(a)(2)(B) and (C).
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.050 - Right to representation.
The municipal court shall not impose any punishment of confinement, probation (including community service work), or other loss of liberty, or impose any fine, fee, or cost enforceable by confinement, probation, or other loss of liberty, as authorized by general or local law, unless the court provides to the accused the right to representation by a lawyer, or provides to those accused who are indigent the right to counsel at no cost to the accused. Such representation shall be subject to all applicable standards adopted by the Georgia Public Defender Standards Council for representation of indigent persons in this state.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.060 - Contracts with the public defender's office.
The municipal court may contract with the Flint Circuit Public Defender's office as a means of complying with its legal obligation to provide defense counsel at no cost to indigent persons appearing before the court in relation to violations of violations of municipal ordinances, county ordinances, or state laws.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.070 - Trial upon citation or accusation; bench trial, appeals.
Notwithstanding any other contrary provision of law, misdemeanor offenses or ordinance violations may be tried upon a uniform traffic citation, summons, citation, or an accusation. All trials shall be bench trials at which the judge serves as finder of fact. Unless a direct appeal is provided by law, alleged errors of law shall be brought before the superior court on petition for writ of certiorari within 30 days of a final adjudication.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.080 - Evidence and procedure.
The municipal court shall operate in accordance with the current uniform municipal court rules, as adopted by the state supreme court, and as may hereafter be amended. The rules of criminal and civil procedure, and rules of evidence, shall conform as nearly as practicable to the rules of procedure and evidence in the superior and state trial courts; provided, however, there shall be no trials by jury and no civil discovery unless allowed by the judge, by order, to prevent manifest injustice. Subpoenas for the attendance of witnesses and production of evidence at trial shall be issued by the municipal court clerk, upon timely application of a party or defendant.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.02.090 - Alternative sentences.
The municipal court shall have the authority and the power to impose fines upon persons convicted of violations of City ordinances, with the alternative of other punishment allowed by law, in the even that such fines are not paid; or to sentence such person to community service work; or to impose a sentence consisting of any combination of the foregoing penalties.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
CHAPTER 7.04 - BONDS AND RECOGNIZANCES

7.04.010 - Bail bonds.
As used in this chapter, the term "bail" shall include the releasing of a person on such person's own recognizance.
Except for those offenses bondable only before a judge of the superior court of this state, the judge of the municipal court shall have all the power and authority of a magistrate in the matter of and pertaining to criminal cases of whatever nature, including the authority to set and accept bail as provided in O.C.G.A. tit. 17, ch. 6.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.04.020 - Acceptance of driver's license in lieu of bond.
For misdemeanors and ordinance violations returnable to the municipal court for trial, the arresting officer shall give due consideration to release on the person's own recognizance bond or the acceptance of the person's driver's license in lieu of bail; in all other cases, a property, surety, or cash bond may be accepted in an amount sufficient to assure the person's appearance in court. The judge shall prepare and, from time-to-time revise, by order, a schedule of cash bonds for all offenses and penal or traffic ordinance violations triable before the municipal court. Such order shall designate the chief of police or municipal court clerk to accept cash bond payments. The officer assessing and accepting a cash bond shall issue a written receipt for the bond to the person charged; if accepted by an officer other than the municipal court clerk, bond proceeds shall be immediately transmitted to the municipal court clerk, who shall enter payment upon a docket of cash bond payments. Proceeds shall be deposited by the clerk into a separate bank account from other funds and receivables of the city.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.04.030 - Failure to appear; bench warrants.
Any person charged with a violation of a misdemeanor offense, or ordinance violation returnable to the municipal court, who gives a cash bond for his personal appearance in court at a designated date, time and place, and who fails to appear on said date and at such time and place shall forfeit the cash bond upon the call of the case for trial. It shall not be necessary for the city to take any further action to forfeit the cash bond. Entry of a cash bond forfeiture shall be made by the municipal court clerk on the cash bond docket; periodically, funds representing forfeited cash bonds shall be transferred from the cash bond account to the general fund of the city. In the discretion of the court a bench warrant may issue for the arrest of the accused. Forfeiture of a cash bond shall not be a bar to a subsequent prosecution of the accused for the violation.
Where a driver's license has been accepted in lieu of bail and the person fails to appear and answer to the charges against him or her, the municipal court clerk shall immediately forward to the state Department of Motor Vehicle Safety the name and driver's license number for suspension of the driver's license and driving privileges of the accused, until the person appears and fully disposes of the charges against him or her. In addition thereto, the court may issue a bench warrant for arrest of the accused.
Upon the failure of a person released upon his own recognizance to appear for trial, if the release is not otherwise conditioned by the court, the court may summarily issue a bench warrant for arrest of the accused which shall be enforced as in cases of forfeited bonds.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.04.040 - Bond forfeitures.
Where a property or surety bond has been posted, bond forfeiture shall follow the procedures set forth in O.C.G.A. § 17-6-70 et seq., which by reference is made a part hereof as if fully set out herein. It shall be the duty of the municipal court clerk to issue notice to sureties as provided in O.C.G.A. § 17-6-70(b).
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
CHAPTER 7.06 - MUNICIPAL COURT JUDGE

7.06.010 - Chief judge; appointment; term of office; oath.
The Mayor and Council shall appoint, by resolution, a chief judge to hold and preside over the municipal court. The term of office shall be four years; provided, however, an appointee shall hold over in office until his or her successor has been duly qualified and appointed.
Before assuming the duties of office, the person appointed as chief judge shall subscribe to the following oath:
"I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as chief judge of the municipal court for the City of McDonough, Georgia."
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.06.020 - Qualifications; compensation.
The municipal court judge shall be licensed to practice law in the state of Georgia, and have served at least seven years as an active member in good standing of the state bar. The judge shall meet all requirements and conform to all policies and procedures of the judicial qualifications commission of the state. Compensation shall be fixed by resolution of the Mayor and Council.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.06.030 - Removal from office.
A person appointed to the office of municipal court judge may only be removed, during his or her term, for cause, as that term is defined at O.C.G.A. § 36-32-2.1, or as hereafter may be amended, and pursuant to the removal proceedings described therein.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.06.040 - Associate judge.
The Mayor and Council may appoint one or more associate, stand-by judges, to hold and preside over the municipal court in the absence, disability, or disqualification of the chief judge, and shall set the term and compensation thereof by resolution. Associate judges shall possess all qualifications and satisfy all training requirements for the office of chief judge, and, while presiding, shall have all powers and duties of the chief judge of the municipal court as granted by law.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.06.050 - Duties and authority.
In addition to the powers and duties granted by law or City Charter, judges of the municipal court shall have and may exercise the same powers and authorities as magistrates in the matter of and pertaining to criminal cases of whatsoever nature returnable to the several courts of this state.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)

7.08.010 - Establishment of office.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 15-18-91, the Office of Prosecuting Attorney of the Municipal Court for the City of McDonough is hereby established for the purpose of representing the City in all matters pertaining to quasi-criminal ordinance violations, misdemeanor offenses triable in municipal court, and such other duties as set forth under law, particularly in O.C.G.A. § 15-18-96.
The prosecuting attorney shall be a part-time position and the person appointed shall not practice law in the municipal court or appear in any matter in which he or she appeared before the court, or in which the underlying accusation arises from the investigation and/or arrest by law enforcement officers of the City of McDonough Police Department.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.08.020 - Appointment; term of office; compensation.
The Mayor and Council shall appoint and set the compensation for the prosecuting attorney, by resolution. The term of office shall be four years; provided, however, an appointee shall hold over in office until his or her successor has been duly qualified and appointed.
The City Clerk shall provide a copy of any resolution of Mayor and Council, which appoints a prosecuting attorney of the municipal court, to the Prosecuting Attorneys' Council of the State of Georgia, within 30 days of the adoption of such resolution.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.08.030 - Qualifications; oath.
The prosecuting attorney shall be licensed to practice law in the state, and shall have served a minimum of three years as an active member in good standing of the state bar. The prosecuting attorney shall satisfactorily complete all required training for such office.
Before assuming the duties of office, the person appointed shall subscribe to the following oath:
"I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially, and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as prosecuting attorney of the City of McDonough."
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.08.040 - Removal from office.
A person appointed to the office of prosecuting attorney may only be removed, during his or her term, for cause, after written notice and opportunity to be heard before the Mayor and Council.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.08.050 - Assistant prosecuting attorney.
Subject to the approval of Mayor and Council, the prosecuting attorney may employ one or more assistant prosecuting attorneys, and such other employees or independent contractors, as he or she deems necessary, in order to carry out his or her duties official duties.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.08.060 - Disqualification in certain cases; substitute prosecuting attorney.
If the prosecuting attorney is disqualified from engaging in the prosecution of a particular case, or is unable to perform the duties of said office due to illness or incapacity, the city attorney, or his or her designee, may serve as substitute prosecuting attorney until such time as the prosecuting attorney is available, or a permanent, substitute prosecuting attorney is appointed by resolution of Mayor and Council.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
7.08.080 - Ratification.
This section shall ratify all actions that have been taken by the persons acting in the positions of prosecuting attorney for the municipal court, to date, as authorized by the Mayor and Council.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
CHAPTER 7.10 - TRAINING

7.10.010 - Costs of mandatory training.
Whenever an officer of the municipal court is required by law to complete and/or maintain training from the Georgia Municipal Courts Training Council or Prosecuting Attorneys Council of Georgia, the cost of training (registration, travel, lodging and meals) shall be paid from city funds as an expense and shall not be considered additional compensation paid to the officer.
( Ord. No. 16-12-1(A), § 1, 12-1-2016)
