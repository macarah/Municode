Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(13).
Open container means any container which is immediately capable of being consumed from, or the seal of which has been broken.
Package store license means a license issued to a retail dealer for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine, in sealed containers, for consumption off the premises, generally known and designated as sale of malt beverages or wine "to go."
Pouring license means a license issued to the operator of an eating establishment for the sale of malt beverages or wine for consumption on the premises of the establishment.
Prohibited locations means it shall be unlawful for any person to consume alcoholic beverages or be in possession of an open container of alcoholic beverages while on the streets, alleys, sidewalks, parking lots, parks, other public property of the city or other public places (duly licensed establishments excepted) or while on private property without the express consent of the owner.
Retail dealer means any person selling malt beverages or wine, in unbroken packages, directly to the consumer.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Code 2002, §§ 4-1, 4-2; Ord. of 3-15-1999, § 14-2; Ord. of 7-10-2000, §§ 32-201, 32-215)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(24).
Sec. 4-2. - State law provisions to govern matters not covered by chapter.
All other matters pertaining to the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages not specifically covered in this chapter shall be governed by O.C.G.A. title 3, ch. 3 (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1 et seq.).
(Code 2002, § 4-3; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-218)
Secs. 4-3—4-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - WINE AND BEER[2]
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-25. - Excise tax; levy; rate.
In accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80, the city does hereby set and levy upon the sale of malt beverages within the city an excise tax as follows:
(1)
On containers consisting of barrels or bulk containers and containing what is commonly known as tap or draft beer, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold, containing not more than 15½ gallons, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons.
(2)
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans or other containers, other than barrels or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(Code 2002, § 4-41; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-216)
State Law reference— Local excise tax on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80 et seq.
Sec. 4-26. - Sanitary conditions of premises used for sales.
All premises used for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be kept in a safe and sanitary condition as required by the ordinances of the city and the laws of this state.
(Code 2002, § 4-42; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-207)
Sec. 4-27. - Required distances of premises from churches and schools; measurement.
(a)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell any alcoholic beverages within 100 yards of any church or within 200 yards of any school or playground. Such distances shall be measured as follows:
(1)
From the property line of the tract on which is located the business regulated under this article;
(2)
To the property line of the tract on which is located the church, school, or playground; and
(3)
Along a straight line which describes the shortest distance between the two property lines.
(b)
This section shall not apply to any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 1981, nor to the renewal of such license. This section shall not apply to any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of wine and beer was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding the license application.
(Code 2002, § 4-43; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-208)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages near churches, schools or college campuses, O.C.G.A § 3-3-21.
Sec. 4-28. - Hours for sale of alcoholic beverages.
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold in the city between the hours of 12:00 midnight Saturday and 5:00 a.m. Monday.
(Code 2002, § 4-44; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-212)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on election day, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-29. - Closing times for sales for consumption establishments.
All business establishments which sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within the city limits are required to close to the public no later than 12:00 midnight, and such establishments are required to remove all patrons from the premises no later than 1:00 a.m.
(Code 2002, § 4-45; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-213)
Sec. 4-30. - Sales near polling places.
No person shall sell alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place during such time as the polls are open for voting during an election.
(Code 2002, § 4-46; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-214)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20(b)(3).
Sec. 4-31. - Sales to persons under the age of 21 years.
No licensee shall furnish or cause to be furnished or permit any person in his employ to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person under the age of 21 years. The licensee shall be held responsible for any such sale made by his employees or others in the licensed business premises. The prohibition of this section includes the sale, gift or any other furnishing of alcoholic beverages to a person under the age of 21 years.
(Code 2002, § 4-47; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-209)
State Law reference— Sales to underage persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23 et seq.
Sec. 4-32. - Posting of notice to persons under the age of 21 years.
The holder of any license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall post in a conspicuous place in his place of business a notice with letters not less than one inch in height which shall be clearly visible to all customers entering the establishment as follows: "Sale of Alcoholic Beverages to Persons Under 21 is Strictly Prohibited."
(Code 2002, § 4-48; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-210)
State Law reference— Posting of laws regarding sale of alcoholic beverages to underage persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.2.
Sec. 4-33. - Employment of persons under the age of 21 years.
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow or require a person in his employment under the age of 21 years to dispense, serve, sell, or take orders for wine or malt beverages; provided, however, that the provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under the age of 21 years who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores or drugstores from selling or handling wine or malt beverages which is sold for consumption off the premises, so long as such person under the age of 21 years is under the supervision of at least one adult, whether such adult is the licensee or another employee.
(Code 2002, § 4-49; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-211)
State Law reference— Restrictions on employment of persons under age 18 years, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
Secs. 4-34—4-54. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - RETAIL LICENSES[3]

Sec. 4-55. - Required.
(a)
Each applicant for a pouring license shall have his fingerprints taken by an authorized official, and shall be subject to a criminal background check. Such background check shall be received only by the chief of police, and the city council shall seek his recommendation for or against approval per section 4-58(10) and, if necessary, in closed session.
(b)
No application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages within this city shall be acted upon by the mayor and council until after notice shall have been run in a newspaper of general circulation in the city for at least ten days prior to the regular meeting of the mayor and council at which such application is to be presented and considered. Such notice shall contain the name of the applicant, the location of the proposed business, and shall be run at the applicant's expense.
(Code 2002, § 4-71; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-202; Ord. of 8-13-2007)
State Law reference— Fingerprints of applicants, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2; license required, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-5-40, 3-6-40.
Sec. 4-56. - Pouring license; additional requirements.
(a)
Each applicant for a pouring license shall have his fingerprints taken by an authorized official, and shall be subject to a criminal background check. Such background check shall be received only by the chief of police, and the city council shall seek his recommendation for or against approval per section 4-58(10) and, if necessary, in closed session.
(b)
No pouring license shall be issued to any establishment that does not maintain a minimum of 50 percent of its gross revenues from the sale of food.
(c)
Such licensee shall submit a report, on a form proscribed by the city, to the city clerk within 15 days following the end of each calendar quarter, which form shall set forth the gross dollar sales of food and alcoholic beverages for the preceding three months.
(d)
The books and records of a licensed establishment selling alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall be subject to audit by the city clerk upon reasonable notice to the person designated as the keeper of such records.
(Code 2002, § 4-72; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-203; Ord. of 8-13-2007)
Sec. 4-57. - Fees.
The annual license fees for retail dealers and consumption on the premises of alcoholic beverages shall be as determined from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Code 2002, § 4-73; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-204)
State Law reference— Local malt beverage license fee, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-42.
Sec. 4-58. - Factors for mayor and council consideration.
The mayor and council, in passing upon an initial application for a license, shall be guided by the following factors as to whether to grant or deny such application:
(1)
The proximity of other establishments selling alcoholic beverages to the proposed location.
(2)
The character of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location.
(3)
The proximity of churches, schools and playgrounds to the proposed location.
(4)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking facilities or other parking available for its patrons.
(5)
Whether the location would tend to increase or promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom.
(6)
The feelings and attitudes of the citizens residing in the general vicinity of the proposed location.
(7)
The information set forth in the application.
(8)
Whether any license previously issued for such location was ever revoked for cause by the mayor and council.
(9)
Whether the applicant has ever sold alcoholic beverages in the municipality.
(10)
The results of any criminal background check.
(Code 2002, § 4-74; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-205)
Sec. 4-59. - Location restrictions.
A license issued under the provisions of this division shall permit the sale of alcoholic beverages only on the premises described in the application.
(Code 2002, § 4-75; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 32-206)
Secs. 4-60—4-76. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - FARM WINERIES[4]

Sec. 4-77. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Farm winery and tasting room shall be defined according to O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1, as presently constituted and as may be hereinafter amended.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-100))
Sec. 4-78. - Farm winery operations.
(a)
Farm wineries licensed under this article shall be authorized to sell the wine of such licensed farm winery and the wine of any other state farm winery licensee in the state at retail in a tasting room located within the municipality of the city for consumption on the premises and in closed packages for consumption off the premises, including the sale of wine by the glass, whether by drink or for tasting.
(b)
No person shall operate a farm winery or tasting room within the city until an appropriate license has been issued by the city, and further until such time as the license has been granted by the state department of revenue.
(c)
The license created in accordance with this article shall be limited to farm winery tasting rooms licensed by the state in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1 et seq., and the licensee shall be permitted to perform only acts allowed in accordance with such statutes. No license is hereby created authorizing any other use.
(d)
Any license under this article must meet all of the requirements under this article for an alcoholic beverages license in the city, including, but not limited to, premises location, distance, hours of sale and applicant qualifications.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-101))
Sec. 4-79. - Regulations.
No holder of a license under this article, nor an agent or employee of the licensee shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell or serve wine to a minor.
(2)
Sell wine in the city between the hours of 12:00 midnight Saturday and 5:00 a.m. Monday.
(3)
Permit on the licensed premises any disorderly conduct, breach of peace, or any lewd, immoral conduct or practices.
(4)
Maintain any type of amusement music machine or video game or utilize any device which amplifies music or voice beyond the confines of the tasting room facility.
(5)
Allow any purchase made on the licensed premises be taken therefrom until put in a bag or otherwise securely wrapped in paper by the proprietor or clerk operating the licensed business.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-102))
Sec. 4-80. - Enforcement.
(a)
Any violation of the provisions of this article shall be grounds for revocation of a license.
(b)
A license shall be a mere grant of privilege to carry on business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this article, any future resolution pertaining thereto, or regulations adopted by this body.
(c)
The license may be suspended or revoked by the city council for any violation of this article; for any violation of state laws and regulations relating to alcoholic beverages; for any material misrepresentation or omission in the application for the license; or if the licensee or the licensed business ceased to meet the eligibility requirements for licensure.
(d)
Prior to the suspension or revocation of the license by the city council, the city shall give written notice to the licensee of the time, place and purpose of the hearing, and a statement of the charges upon which the hearing before the city council shall be held. Service of such notice shall be by personal service or certified mail. The licensee shall be afforded an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine witnesses at any such hearing. The city council shall make a decision regarding revocation or suspension within five days from the date of the hearing, which decision shall be in writing. Any appeal of said decision shall be by writ of certiorari to the superior court of the county.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-103))
Sec. 4-81. - Licenses, fees, and taxes.
(a)
The applicant for a domestic winery or farm winery license shall pay the annual license fee of $325.00.
(b)
The applicant for a farm winery tasting room shall pay the annual license fee $325.00 for each tasting room location.
(c)
The applicant for a license under this article shall pay to the clerk of the city the sum of $325.00 as an annual license fee for the operation of one year beginning on or after January 1 and ending on December 31 of such year.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-104))
Sec. 4-82. - Authority to issue licenses.
Licenses shall be issued only upon the approval of the city. Applications for said licenses shall be presented to the governing authority, and all fees provided for shall be paid to the clerk of said body in the amount and at the time hereinbefore specified.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-105))
Sec. 4-83. - Regulations, future ordinances, and revocation of licenses.
All the provisions of this article are and shall be subject to such regulations which this body may deem advisable for the proper control and regulation of the sale and consumption of malt beverages and wine. This article and such regulations may be from time to time amended. All such licenses issued pursuant to this article and any such regulations shall be expressly subject to the provisions of the regulations, as may be then or thereafter in force, and subsequent ordinances. All such licenses shall be nontransferable and shall convey to the licensee no property right, but only a privilege, which may be revoked by this body.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-106))
Sec. 4-84. - Limited license.
The farm winery license hereunder is limited to farm winery tasting rooms licensed by the state and allows the licensee to perform all acts as allowed by such state legislation in such tasting rooms. No license is created by the adoption of this article authorizing any other uses.
(Ord. of 5-12-2014, § 1(4-107))
