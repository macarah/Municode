Sec. 12-47. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Insurer means any company authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5 as Class (1).
(Code 1994, § 22-51; Ord. No. 95-05, § 1(22-51), 12-11-1995)
Sec. 12-48. - Insurer license fees.
There is levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 12-49, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is levied a license fee. The annual license fees shall be in amounts as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1972, § 16-27; Code 1994, § 22-52; Ord. of 9-13-1983(2), § 16-27; Ord. of 4-9-1984, § 16-27; Ord. of 7-10-1984; Ord. No. 95-05, § 1(22-52), 12-11-1995)
Sec. 12-49. - License fee for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under this article, operated and maintained by a business organization engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and, in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits, or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee per location for each year. The additional license fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1994, § 22-53; Ord. of 9-13-1983(2), § 16-28; Ord. of 4-9-1984, § 16-28; Ord. of 7-10-1984; Ord. No. 95-05, § 1(22-53), 12-11-1995)
Sec. 12-50. - License fee for independent insurance agencies, brokers not otherwise licensed.
There is levied an annual license fee on independent insurance agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 12-48. The annual license fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1972, § 16-28; Code 1994, § 22-54; Ord. of 9-13-1983(2), § 16-29; Ord. of 4-9-1984, § 16-29; Ord. of 7-10-1984; Ord. No. 95-05, § 1(22-54), 12-11-1995)
Sec. 12-51. - Gross premiums tax on insurers writing life, accident and health insurance.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident, and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. As used in this section, the term "gross direct premiums" means the same as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-48.
(Code 1972, § 16-29; Code 1994, § 22-55; Ord. of 9-13-1983(2), § 16-30; Ord. of 4-9-1984, § 16-30; Ord. No. 95-05, § 1(22-55), 12-11-1995)
Sec. 12-52. - Gross premiums tax on all other insurers.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. As used in this section, the term "gross premiums" means the same as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a). The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-48.
(Code 1972, § 16-30; Code 1994, § 22-56; Ord. of 9-13-1983(2), § 16-31; Ord. of 4-9-1984, § 16-31; Ord. No. 95-05, § 1(22-56), 12-11-1995)
Sec. 12-53. - Administration.
(a)
Due date for licensees. License fees imposed in sections 12-48, 12-49 and 12-50 shall be due and payable on January 1 of each year.
(b)
Copy of article sent to state insurance commissioner. The city clerk is directed to forward a duly certified copy of the ordinance from which this article is derived to the state insurance commissioner within 45 days of its enactment.
(c)
Reports.
(1)
The person responsible for each insurance business location subject to a license fee under section 12-50 shall file with the city clerk a report, on forms prescribed by him, showing the address of the business location; the class of insurance written; the names of the persons writing insurance at such location; the names of the companies represented; and such other reasonable information as may be required.
(2)
All reports required to be filed under this section shall be confidential, and the information contained therein shall be solely for the use of the officers of the city responsible for administering this article.
(Code 1972, §§ 16-31, 16-32; Code 1994, § 22-57; Ord. of 9-13-1983(2), § 16-32; Ord. of 4-9-1984, § 16-32; Ord. No. 95-05, § 1(22-57), 12-11-1995)
