ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Bar means any premises at which a retailer licensed pursuant to this title to sell alcoholic beverages derives 75 percent or more total annual gross revenue from the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
Bouncer means an individual primarily performing duties related to verifying age for admittance, security, maintaining order, or safety, or a combination thereof.
Conviction means an adjudication of guilt or a plea of guilty, or a plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of bond by a person charged with a crime.
Disciplinary action means any citation or arrest arising out of the violation of any law, rule, regulation, resolution, or ordinance of a governmental entity relating to the manufacture, distribution, sale, or possession of alcoholic beverages against a licensee, an employee of a licensee, or any person holding a financial interest in the license of the licensee on the premises or place of business of any licensee.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverages obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Distance means measurement in a straight line in linear feet from the nearest wall enclosing the proposed premises of licensee to the nearest property line of any school, college, or church.
License means the authorization by the city council to engage in the sale of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine.
Governmental entity means the United States government, any state governmental, any local government, and any department, agency, or instrumentality thereof.
License means the authorization by the city council to engage in the sale of malt beverages and/or wine.
Licensee means any person issued a license pursuant to this title by a governmental entity to operate a bar.
Malt beverages and wine mean those beverages defined by the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, § 3-1-2.
Minor means any person to whom the sale of alcoholic beverages is prohibited by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Person means any individual.
Powdered alcohol means a powdered or crystalline substance that contains any amount of alcohol for direct use or reconstitution.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned-in locality, whether a room, shop, or building, wherein malt beverages and/or wine are sold and any and all surrounding and adjacent areas, whether enclosed or not, which are owned by or under the control of the person to whom the license is issued.
Retail sale means selling or offering for sale any malt beverage and/or wine to any member of the public.
School or college mean such state, county, city, church or other school that teaches the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state, to include licensed preschool and/or kindergarten facilities and private schools, except that the terms shall not include private schools where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations, and other special subjects, are taught.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015; Ord. No. 2016-01, § 1, 6-20-2016)

Sec. 10-2.1. - Public possession or consumption; open containers prohibited; exceptions.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (d) herein or in the downtown containment district per section 10-2.2, it shall be unlawful for any person to drink or to attempt to drink any alcoholic beverage or to have in his or her hand any open container used, or which has been used for alcoholic beverages, upon any streets, sidewalks, parks, plazas, rights-of-way, city properties, other public places in the city, or any open premises adjacent to any business in the city, whether or not such person is located in a vehicle.
(b)
Except as provided in the downtown containment district per section 10-2.2, it shall be unlawful for any establishment or entity licensed under this chapter to sell alcoholic beverages to allow any person to leave such licensed premises with any alcoholic beverage in an open cup, bottle, can, or other open container.
(c)
This section shall not be applicable to the parking lot or other premises of a duly licensed business selling alcoholic beverages where curb service is regularly provided by employees of such business; however, this section is expressly applicable to such businesses where customers service themselves, which is to say, where the customer makes his purchase and returns to a vehicle or the outside premises of such business.
(d)
The prohibitions of subsection (a) herein shall not apply to designated city property when the city, by a majority vote of the city council, authorizes the service or consumption of alcohol at an event held or sponsored by the city on such property.
( Ord. No. 2019-02 , § 1, 5-20-2019)
Sec. 10-2.2. - Downtown containment district.
(a)
The purpose of this section is to establish an exception to section 10-2.1 for a downtown containment district. This exception shall apply only to licensees whose establishments:
(1)
Are located within the downtown containment district, as defined herein; and
(2)
Possess a valid license to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises; and
(3)
Derive a minimum of 50 percent of their total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food.
(b)
Except as set forth in this section, all such licensees shall remain subject to all other provisions of the city's alcoholic beverages ordinance.
(c)
The downtown containment district is defined as follows: The area generally bounded on the east by North Main Street, on the north by East Kelly Street, on the west by North Isabella Street, and on the south by what was formerly East Railroad Street and is now incorporated into the city's pedestrian mall at the depot, including all streets, avenues, sidewalks, plazas, parks, and rights-of-way within said boundaries. For purposes of this definition, any licensed establishment fronting a street within the described downtown containment district shall be considered to be located within the defined area.
(d)
Between 5:00 p.m. and 10:00 p.m., any establishment or entity that satisfies the foregoing requirements of this section is authorized to dispense an alcoholic beverage for removal from the premises within the downtown containment district subject to the following conditions:
(1)
Alcoholic beverages consumed pursuant to this provision must be purchased from a licensed vendor within the downtown containment district.
(2)
Alcoholic beverages consumed pursuant to this provision shall be dispensed and removed from the licensed premises in a container that does not exceed 16 fluid ounces in size.
(3)
Alcoholic beverages consumed pursuant to this provision shall be in a disposable cup that is not a can, bottle, or glass.
(4)
Alcoholic beverages consumed pursuant to this provision must be disposed of before leaving the downtown containment district and may not be consumed outside said district.
(5)
No alcoholic beverage purchased pursuant to this provision may be consumed upon any private property without the consent of the property owner.
(6)
No person shall remove more than one such alcoholic beverage per person at a time from the licensed premises.
(7)
Any establishment that dispenses alcoholic beverages pursuant to this provision shall post a sign in public view near its primary exit which shall be no less than a square foot in area and shall read as follows: "All patrons leaving this establishment with an alcoholic beverage may only consume such alcoholic beverage in the immediate downtown containment district, and any individual that leaves the permitted area with an alcoholic beverage is in violation of the City of Sylvester's Code of Ordinances and may be subject to a citation and/or fine."
(e)
The provisions of this section shall not be deemed to abrogate or otherwise impact any state law or local ordinance pertaining to public drunkenness, disorderly conduct, driving with an open container or under the influence of alcohol or similar laws.
( Ord. No. 2019-02 , § 1, 5-20-2019)
Sec. 10-3. - Possession, etc., by minors.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for a minor to attempt to purchase or purchase, or for any other person to attempt to purchase or purchase for a minor, any alcoholic beverage.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a minor to exhibit any fake, forged, or borrowed credentials in an effort to obtain alcoholic beverages to which he is not legally entitled.
(c)
It shall be illegal for a person under the age of 18 years to enter or remain upon any establishment selling alcoholic beverages at retail for on-premises consumption. Provided, however, this subsection shall not prohibit a person under the age of 18 years from entering or remaining on the premises of any establishment with more than 50 percent of its gross sales from food.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for a minor to have alcoholic beverages in his possession.
(e)
A violation of any provision of this section shall constitute an offense.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
State Law reference— Possession of alcoholic beverages by persons under 21 years of age, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 10-4. - General operational regulations.
No holder of a license authorizing the retail sale of malt beverages and/or wine in the city, or any agent or employee of the licensee, shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell malt beverages and/or wine to a minor.
(2)
Knowingly sell malt beverages and/or wine to any person while such person is in an intoxicated condition.
(3)
Sell malt beverages and/or wine upon the licensed premises, or permit the consumption of malt beverages and/or wine upon the licensed premises, on any day or at any time when such sale or consumption is prohibited by law.
(4)
Permit on the licensed premises any disorderly conduct or breach of peace.
A license holder who knowingly permits an employee or agent to do any of the prohibited acts of this section shall be guilty of the offense as if said license holder committed the offense in person.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-5. - Applicant and employee affidavits required.
(a)
Prior to the issuance or renewal of any license authorizing the retail sale of malt beverages and/or wine, an applicant shall provide the city a sworn affidavit that the applicant has established a training program for any employee and/or agent of the applicant who will be engaged in the retail sale of malt beverages and/or wine. The training program shall at a minimum contain a review of all ordinances and regulations established and enacted by the city and all laws, rules, and regulations of the state with regard to the sale of alcohol. A training program shall not be required of any establishment with an on-premises consumption license and derives 50 percent of its income from the sale of food.
(b)
Prior to any employee or agent of any holder of a license for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine engages in the sale of these beverages, the employee shall execute a sworn affidavit that they have undergone the training as prescribed in subsection (a) of this section. Employee affidavits shall remain on file on the premises and shall be available for immediate inspection by the city.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)

modified

Alcoholic beverages shall be received at or delivered to the premises of the retail licensee by no other means than by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a permit from the State of Georgia and a license from the city to make deliveries in the city. The receipt of any such beverages transported by any other means shall be grounds for revocation of the retail license or wholesale license.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)

All state laws and regulations pertaining to the sale of alcoholic beverages are incorporated herein and considered a part of this article.

Sec. 10-9. - Security required for establishment providing on-premises consumption of alcohol.
Any establishment granting a license for on-premises consumption that derives 50 percent or more of its gross income from the sale of alcoholic beverages shall provide security at all times and upon all occasions between the hours of 8:00 p.m. and closing when such establishment shall have more than 25 patrons present in such establishment. However, any establishment granting a license for on-premises consumption that derives 50 percent or more of its gross income from the sale of alcoholic beverages shall provide security from 8:00 p.m. and until the time of closing on any Thursday, any Friday, any Saturday, any federal holiday, any state holiday, and on the day immediately preceding any state or federal holiday, notwithstanding the fact that there may be less than 26 patrons in such establishment during such time. Security personnel shall be POST certified law enforcement officers. When any such establishment shall have more than 100 patrons present between the hours of 8:00 p.m. and closing time, the establishment shall provide an additional security officer for every 100 patrons, or any part thereof, in addition to the first 100 patrons. [For example, should there be 101 patrons present or 200 patrons present at any establishment between the hours of 8:00 p.m. and closing time, the establishment would be required to have at least two security officers present. However, should there be 201 patrons present of 300 patrons present at any such establishment between the hours of 8:00 p.m. and closing time, the establishment would be required to have three security officers present.] Notwithstanding the fact that the security personnel shall be compensated by the establishment, the security officer(s) shall file a written report of any violation on the licensed premises whether such violation be by a patron, an employee of the establishment, or the licensee; and the security officer shall take such action as may be necessary to enforce or seek enforcement of this or any other applicable local, state or federal law or ordinance.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-10. - Handguns and long guns in bars and/or restaurants.
(a)
Definitions.
(1)
The term "bar" as used in this section is defined as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-11-127(a)(1) as an establishment that is devoted to the serving of alcoholic beverages for consumption by guests on the premises and in which the serving of alcoholic beverages for consumption by guests on the premises and in which the serving of food is only incidental to the consumption of those beverages, including, but not limited to, taverns, nightclubs, cocktail lounges, and cabarets.
(2)
The term "handgun" as used in this section is defined to mean a firearm of any description, loaded or unloaded, from which any shot, bullet, or other missile can be discharged by an action of an explosive where the length of the barrel, not including any revolving, detachable, or magazine breech, does not exceed 12 inches.
(3)
The term "long gun" as used in this section is defined to mean a firearm with a barrel length in excess of 12 inches designed or made and intended to be fired from the shoulder.
(b)
Possession on certain premises forbidden.
(1)
The owners and operators of bars, as that term is herein defined, holding alcoholic beverage licenses permitting consumption on the premises, shall not permit the carrying of handguns or long guns on the licensed premises, irrespective of whether the possessor holds a valid Georgia license permitting such possession. Such owners and operators shall post in a prominent place or places on the premises one or more notices adequate to inform customers of this prohibition.
(2)
The violation of any of the provisions of subsection (b)(1) shall be a ground for the suspension or revocation of the license under section 10-79 of the Code of Ordinances of the city (the Code), and shall be a ground for the denial of future applications for alcoholic beverage licenses of any kind on any premises in the city by the holder under section 10-71 of the Code.
(3)
Owners and operators of premises upon which alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises, other than bars, shall forbid possession on the premises of handguns and long guns, irrespective of whether the possessor thereof holds a valid Georgia license permitting such possession. Such owners and operators of [premises] shall post in a prominent place or places on the premises one or more notices adequate to inform customers of this prohibition.
(4)
The violation of any of the provisions of subsection (b)(3) shall be a ground for the suspension or revocation of the license under section 10-79 of the Code, and shall be a ground for the denial of future application for alcoholic beverage licenses of any kind on any premises in the city by the holder under section 10-71 of the Code.
(5)
It shall be unlawful and punishable as an offense under section 1-8 of the Code for any owner or operator to fail to comply with the provisions of subsection (b)(2) and (b)(3) above.
(6)
It shall be unlawful and punishable as an offense under section 1-8 of the Code for any person to possess upon the licensed premises any handgun or long gun, irrespective of whether the possessor holds a valid Georgia license permitting such possession.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)

Sec. 10-41. - Sale permitted; conditions and restrictions.
The sale of malt beverages and/or wine shall be legal in the city, subject to the following terms, conditions, and restrictions, and upon full compliance therewith by all persons engaged in business under the provisions of this article:
(1)
The license granted under this article shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on a business during the terms of the license, subject to all the terms and conditions imposed in this article and by related laws located in the applicable provisions of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated and other city ordinances relating to business licenses.
(2)
Licenses may be granted for the purpose of package sales of malt beverages and/or wine.
(3)
Licenses may be granted for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine to be consumed on the premises.
(4)
There shall be absolutely no licenses granted for the package sale of distilled spirits.
(5)
There shall be licenses issued for the sale of distilled spirits on premises to those establishments who derive 50 percent or more of their gross revenue from the sale of food.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-42. - Offenses; punishment.
Any license holder, any employee of the license holder, and any customer of the license holder who violates any provision of this article, or who fails to comply with any provision of this article applicable to them, shall be guilty of an offense punishable as provided in this Code, and each day such violation or failure to comply continues shall be a separate punishable offense.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-43. - [Persons under the age of 21.]
Subject to the exceptions set forth below, no business or establishment which sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on its business premises shall permit any person under the age of 21 years to enter or remain upon any such of its premises unless such businesses or establishment derives more than 50 percent of its gross sales at such business premises from food.
(1)
Alcohol stamp exception. A business or establishment which sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on its business premises and derives 50 percent or less of its gross sale at such business premises from food, may nevertheless permit persons under the age of 21 years, who are 18 years of age or older, to enter or remain upon such of its premises provided it provides to all persons and requires all person upon such premises who are 21 years of age or older to wear a unique bright colored wrist band with the word "ALCOHOL" printed or emblazed thereupon or a unique stamp denoted "ALCOHOL: affixed to the hand of such person and does not provide nor permit any person under the age of 21 years to wear or possess any such "ALCOHOL" wrist band or stamp upon such premises.
(2)
Employment exception. If such conduct is not otherwise prohibited pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24, nothing contained in this section shall be construed to prohibit any person under 21 years of age from:
a.
Dispensing, serving, selling, or handling alcoholic beverages as a part of employment in any licensed establishment;
b.
Being employed in any establishment in which alcoholic beverages are distilled or manufactured; or
c.
Taking orders for and having possession of alcoholic beverages as a part of employment in a licensed establishment.
(3)
No person shall allow or require an individual under the age of 21 to serve as a bouncer on a premises or in an establishment where alcoholic beverages are dispensed, served, or sold pursuant to a license issued under Title III of the Georgia Code.
(4)
No individual under the age of 21 shall enter or be allowed to enter a bar unless he or she is accompanied by his or her parent, guardian, or spouse who is 21 years of age or older. This subsection shall not apply to an individual while he or she is attending a live musical concert or live presentation of the performing arts for which he or she has paid an admission charge.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015; Ord. No. 2016-01, § 2, 6-20-2016)

All applicants shall be investigated by the city police department or by such other person or entity as may be designated by the city council. The report of such investigation shall be presented to the city council for consideration with the application.

No alcoholic beverage license required by this chapter shall be issued:
(1)
To any corporation, partnership, or other business organization. A license shall be issued only to one or more individuals.
(2)
Reserved.

To a person who, in the opinion and judgment of the city council, is not of good moral character and reputation in the community in which such person resides.

(5)
To any person who is the previous holder of an alcoholic beverage license and is, in the judgment and discretion of the city council, an unfit person to have a license because of the manner in which such person conducted business thereunder as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any laws or regulations relating to such business.
(6)
For a location not suitable in the judgment and discretion of the city council because of traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood, or by reason of the effect which such establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding properties.

To a person who has had a license revoked within the last five years.

For a location within 100 yards of a school or school house or within 100 yards of any church. (14)
For a location which does not have adequate off-street facilities or other parking available for its patrons.

Sec. 10-72. - Malt beverages—License required.
Each brewer, wholesale dealer, and retail dealer of malt beverages, as such terms are defined in O.C.G.A. §§ 3-1-2 and 3-5-1, who does business within the city shall be required to obtain an annual license from the city clerk. Each brewer, wholesale dealer, and retail dealer shall make application in the manner prescribed by the city clerk prior to January 1 of each year. Each application shall be approved by the city council no later than the first regularly scheduled meeting in January. No applicant for a new alcohol license may engage in the sale of alcohol until approval of the new application by the city council. Applicants for renewal may continue the sale of alcohol beverages until the license application is denied by the council.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-73. - Same—Fees.
The annual license fee for malt beverage brewers, retail dealers, and wholesale dealers shall be $500.00.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-74. - Wine—License required.
Each retail or wholesale dealer of wine, as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. §§ 3-1-2 and 3-5-1, who does business within the city shall be required to obtain an annual license from the city clerk. Each such dealer shall make application in the manner prescribed by the city clerk prior to January 1 of each year. Each application shall be approved by the city council no later than the first regularly scheduled meeting in January. No new applicant for an alcohol license may engage in the sale of alcohol without approval by the city council. Applicants for renewal may continue the sale of alcohol beverages until the license application is denied by the council.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-75. - Same—Fees.
The annual license fee for each place of retail wine distribution in the city shall be $500.00. The annual license fee for each wholesale dealer of wine shall be $500.00.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-76. - Same—Factors for council consideration.
In deciding whether to grant or deny the application for a license to sell wine at retail or wholesale, the city council shall be guided by the same considerations as those applied in granting or denying a license for the sale or manufacture of alcoholic beverages.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-77. - On-premises consumption.
In addition to the license fees required by sections 10-73 and 10-75 of this chapter, there shall be an additional fee of $100.00 for on premises consumption of malt beverages and wine. The license fee for on-premises consumption of distilled spirits shall be $1,000.00.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-78. - Change of location and/or licensee.
A license issued under the provisions of this article shall permit the sale or manufacture of alcoholic beverages only on the premises described in the application under the supervision of the named licensee. Any license transfer to a location other than the premises described in the original application or any transfer from one licensee to another must be approved by the city council. There shall be an application fee of $100.00 for the transfer of an alcohol license.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)
Sec. 10-79. - Suspension and revocation—Grounds for suspension or revocation.
(a)
The city council may suspend or revoke any alcohol license. Should the council revoke a license, such license may not be reinstated for five years. Should the council suspend any license, the license shall be suspended for a specific period of time and shall be reinstated at the end of the suspension period upon payment of a $500.00 reinstatement fee without further action by the city council; provided, however, should an alcohol license be suspended beyond the end of the calendar year, the city council shall approve or deny the license at the conclusion of the suspension period as provided in sections 10-72 and 10-74. Upon such approval, the applicant shall pay a $500.00 reinstatement fee and the annual license fees prescribed in this division.
(b)
The following shall be grounds for the suspension or revocation of an alcoholic beverage license; whether or not such grounds exist shall be determined by the governing body in its sole discretion:
(1)
The violation by the license holder of any federal, state, or local law or ordinance relating to the use and sale of drugs or alcoholic beverages or involving the use of firearms, theft, violence, or a crime of moral turpitude.
(2)
The failure of the license holder to report promptly to the police department any violation of law or municipal ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance, or altercation occurring inside the premises.
(3)
Any conduct on the part of the license holder or license holder's employee on or off premises contrary to the public health, safety, or morals of the citizens of the city.
(4)
Operating or conducting the business on the licensed premises in a manner contrary to the public welfare, safety, health, or morals of the citizens of the city or in such manner as to constitute a nuisance.
(5)
Upon the business or the operation or location thereof, or the owner or any person connected therewith, ceasing to meet any of the qualifications for the issuance of a license as stated under section 10-72.
(6)
The failure of the license holder or license holder's employees to keep the licensed premises free and clear of any disturbances of the peace; loud or boisterous conduct; lewd, obscene, or immoral entertainment; violation of any federal, state, or local law or ordinance relating to the use and sale of drugs or alcoholic beverages or involving the use of firearms, theft, violence, or a crime of moral turpitude.
(7)
The failure of the license holder to provide a training program as required by section 10-5.
(8)
The failure of the license holder to maintain a sworn affidavit of each employee or agent that they have undergone a training program as required by section 10-5.
(c)
Should the city council determine that the license holder or the license holder's employee has violated this chapter within a period of two years, the following actions shall be taken:
(1)
First offense; Suspension for no more than 14 consecutive days.
(2)
Second offense; Suspension for no more than 30 consecutive days.
(3)
Third offense; Revocation.
Provided, however, if the second or third offense do not occur within two years of the first offense, the subsequent offenses shall be treated as if they were the first offense. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the city council may revoke any license or suspend a license for any period for knowingly selling malt beverages and/or wine to a minor.
(d)
The Chief of Police of the City of Sylvester, or his or her designated, shall report any violation of this section or any state or federal law by any license holder or any disturbance or violation of law of any kind at any location alcoholic beverages are sold to the city clerk. The report shall be placed on the agenda at the next regularly scheduled meeting of the city council.
(e)
The determination of whether the license holder or license holder's employee has violated this section shall be made by the city council. An actual conviction in a court for any offense shall not be necessary in order to suspend or revoke the license.
(f)
In the event a license holder fails to submit proof to the city that the license holder has obtained an alcoholic beverage license from the State of Georgia within 90 days after the date that the alcoholic beverage is issued by the city to the license holder, the alcoholic beverage license issued by the city to the license holder shall be automatically revoked with no refund of the license fees paid by the license holder to the city. In the event the license holder subsequently applies for an alcoholic beverage license, proof that the applicant has an alcoholic beverage license issued by the State of Georgia must be submitted with a renewal application.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015; Ord. No. 2016-07, § 1, 11-21-2016)
Sec. 10-80. - Same—Procedure; appeal.
(a)
After the city council shall deny any application for a license or the transfer of any license or shall suspend or revoke any existing license, the applicant or license holder, as the case may be, shall be given notice in writing with the reasons therefor stated.
(b)
Any applicant or license holder aggrieved by the decision of the city council shall have the right to request, within ten days after receipt of notice, a hearing on such decision. Upon receipt by the city council of a request for such hearing, a hearing shall be scheduled within 20 working days; and the applicant or license holder, as the case may be, shall be notified of the date, time, and place of such hearing. At such hearing, the licensee or applicant shall have the right to appear in person and by attorney and to have witnesses appear on his behalf and to cross examine opposing witnesses. The witnesses shall testify under oath, and the technical rules of evidence shall not apply.
( Ord. No. 2015-08 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-16-2015)

Sec. 10-101. - Malt beverages.
(a)
Rate of levy. There is hereby levied and imposed upon all wholesale dealers selling malt beverages within the city a specific excise tax in the amount of $.05 per 12-ounce container or any portion thereof so as to graduate the tax on bottles, cans, and containers of various sizes of malt beverages sold by each wholesale dealer within the city, and an excise tax on tap or draft beer in the amount of $6.00 for each container sold, as hereinafter specified, containing not more than 15½ gallons and at a like rate for fractional parts, where the beverage is sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, such beverage being commonly known as tap or draft beer.

Against whom levied. The malt beverage excise tax is levied against and shall be paid by each licensed wholesale dealer in malt beverages in the city.

The malt beverage excise tax shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of, and payment shall be accompanied by a report itemizing the exact quantities of malt beverages sold for the preceding calendar month by size and type of container.
