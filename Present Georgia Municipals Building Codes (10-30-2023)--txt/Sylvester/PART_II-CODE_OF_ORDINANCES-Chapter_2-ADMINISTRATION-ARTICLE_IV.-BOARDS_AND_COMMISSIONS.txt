DIVISION 2. - PLANNING COMMISSION[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2003-02, section 3, adopted Jan. 1, 2003, designated the "Worth County Planning Commission" as the planning commission for the City of Sylvester.
State Constitution reference— Home rule for municipalities, Ga. Const. art. IX, § II, ¶ IV.
State Law reference— Municipality coordinated and comprehensive planning, O.C.G.A. § 36-70-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-271. - Created.
In order to guide and accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the city which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote public health, safety, morals, convenience, order, prosperity, and the general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development, the Sylvester Planning Commission, referred to in this article as the planning commission, is created and established as authorized by the Zoning Procedures Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-66-1, et seq.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-272. - Membership.
The planning commission shall consist of five members. Each council member shall appoint one member who shall be a resident of the council member's election district. The mayor shall appoint an at-large member who shall be a resident of the city.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-273. - Terms.
The terms of the members of the planning commission shall be four years, except that in the appointment of the first planning commission, the members from Districts 1 and 2 shall be appointed for a term of one year, the members from Districts 3 and 4 shall be appointed for a term of two years, and the at-large member shall be appointed for a term of three years.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-274. - Vacancies; removal.
Any vacancy in membership of the planning commission shall be filled for the unexpired term by the city council, which shall have the authority to remove any member for cause on written charges, after written notice and a public hearing.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-275. - Compensation; expenses.
The members of the planning commission shall receive such compensation as determined by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-276. - Organization.
The planning commission shall elect its chairman and vice chairman from among its appointed members. The term of chairman and vice chairman shall be one year with eligibility for reelection.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-277. - Rules of procedure.
The planning commission rules of procedure shall be in accordance with the Zoning Procedures Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-66-1, et seq.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-278. - Meetings; when held; records public.
(a)
The planning commission shall meet on the first Thursday of each month, or at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the chairman or the board may determine.
(b)
Except for lawful executive sessions, all meetings of the planning commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public, and all records of the planning commission shall be public record.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-279. - Expenditures.
The expenditures of the planning commission shall be within the amounts appropriated by the city council.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Sec. 2-280. - Powers and duties.
From and after the time when the planning commission shall have organized and selected its officers and shall have adopted its rules of procedure, then the planning commission shall have all the powers, duties, and responsibilities set forth in the Zoning Procedures Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-66-1, et seq.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, § 1, 4-17-2006; Ord. No. 2006-07, § 1, 5-15-2006)
Secs. 2-281—2-285. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - COMMUNITY RELATIONS COMMISSION

Sec. 2-286. - Established.
There is hereby established in and for the City of Sylvester the Sylvester Community Relations Commission to be composed of eleven members who shall be residents of the city. Each council member shall appoint two members to the commission and the mayor shall appoint three members. Emphasis in the selection of members shall be upon obtaining a group diverse with respect to race, religion, economic status, type of employment, age, national origin, sex, geographic location, and political party affiliation. Each of the eight members appointed by the city council members shall be appointed to an initial term of two years and until a successor is appointed and qualified. The three members appointed by the mayor shall each be appointed for an initial term of one year and until a successor is appointed and qualified. After initial appointments have been served, terms shall be two years each in duration and until successors are appointed and qualified.
(Ord. No. 2004-15, § 1, 12-20-2004)
Sec. 2-287. - Officers appointed.
The chairman of the Sylvester Community Relations Commission shall be appointed by the mayor of the City of Sylvester annually from the members of the community relations commission, said chairman to serve for one calendar year or until a successor has been appointed and qualified. The Sylvester Community Relations Commission shall appoint such additional officers as it shall deem appropriate and necessary.
(Ord. No. 2004-15, § 2, 12-20-2004)
Sec. 2-288. - Duties, powers, and functions.
The Sylvester Community Relations Commission shall have the following duties, powers, and functions:
(1)
To encourage, promote, and develop fair and equal treatment and opportunity for all persons regardless of race, color, creed, sex, or national origin;
(2)
To coordinate and assist local governmental agencies on human relations in their efforts to promote better relations;
(3)
To cooperate with community, professional, civic, and religious organizations and federal, state, county, and municipal agencies in the development of public information programs, leadership, and activities in the interest of equal opportunity and treatment of all racial, religious, and ethnic groups;
(4)
To conduct research projects and studies and to make reports on race relations in the city;
(5)
To annually and periodically report to the mayor and city council of the city on the activities of the Sylvester Community Relations Commission;
(6)
To adopt such rules and regulations as are necessary to govern the proceedings of the Sylvester Community Relations Commission and to conduct all meetings in accordance with said rules and regulations;
(7)
To investigate, study, and inform the public as to any complaints received concerning the above areas;
(8)
To provide a forum for airing community problems and grievances.
(Ord. No. 2004-15, § 3, 12-20-2004)
Sec. 2-289. - Compensation.
No member of the Sylvester Community Relations Commission shall receive any compensation for his or her activities as a member of said body. The Sylvester Community Relations Commission shall submit an annual budgetary request to the governing authority of the city. The governing authority of the city is hereby authorized to appropriate funds for use by the Sylvester Community Relations Commission, as provided herein
(Ord. No. 2004-15, § 4, 12-20-2004)
Sec. 2-290. - Attendance at meetings.
The absence of any member from three consecutive meetings of the Sylvester Community Relations Commission shall automatically create a vacancy, which shall be filled by each official or his/her successor who originally made the appointment for the unexpired term of the member whose absences created the vacancy.
(Ord. No. 2004-15, § 5, 12-20-2004)
Secs. 2-291—2-340. - Reserved.
