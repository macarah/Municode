Sec. 22-1. - Air pollution control; adoption of regulation.
The county board of health regulation number 24 entitled "Air Pollution Control," attached to the ordinance from which this section is derived, is hereby adopted by the city by reference, and incorporated herein as fully as if set out at length herein. One or more copies thereof are on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1989, § 10-1; Ord. No. 110, 11-29-1969)
Sec. 22-2. - Clean community system.
(a)
The city council hereby endorses the clean community system, and supports the participation of the city in the system.
(b)
In keeping with the design of the clean community system, the city council hereby recognizes the city clean and beautiful commission. Such commission shall be an official committee of the city and accountable to the mayor. The members and chairperson of the clean and beautiful commission shall be appointed by the city council.
(Code 1989, § 10-2; Res. of 2-10-1986)
Sec. 22-3. - Burial of materials on property under development.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury, or cause or permit any person to bury, glass, cans, tires, appliances, and similar manmade materials on any property under development.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury, or cause or permit any person to bury, tree stumps, limbs and similar natural materials on any property under development, except in compliance with the following conditions:
(1)
No burial shall be permitted on any lot having an area of 20,000 square feet or less, exclusive of the right-of-way of any road;
(2)
No portion of any burial site shall lie within 50 feet of any roadbed, driveway, building or other structure;
(3)
Prior to any burial, the city director of planning and community development shall receive and approve a detailed site plan which shall set forth, in addition to other information required by ordinances of the city, the location of the proposed burial site;
(4)
In the event that a burial is permitted in accordance with the foregoing provisions, the location of the burial site shall be shown on the final plat submitted for recording.
(Code 1989, § 10-3; Ord. No. 215, § 2, 10-14-1985)
Sec. 22-4. - Regulation of smoking in certain public places.
(a)
Areas where prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to smoke in any facility owned or leased by the city, and in any private facility where a public meeting is called by the city, or an agency thereof, during the time such meeting is being held. For the purposes of this section, the term "smoking" or "to smoke" means the act of smoking or carrying a lighted or smoldering cigar, cigarette or pipe of any kind or lighting a cigar, cigarette or pipe of any kind.
(b)
Posting of signs. Signs prohibiting smoking shall be conspicuously posted in those areas in which smoking is prohibited by this section.
(c)
Enforcement. This section may be enforced by any member of the city police department or fire department.
(d)
Penalties. Any person violating any provision of this section shall be fined according to the penalties described in section 1-8 for each offense, and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Code 1989, § 10-4; Ord. No. 329, §§ 1—4, 1-24-1994)
Sec. 22-5. - Spill containment kits required.
(a)
The city shall acquire and equip all city vehicles with appropriate spill containment kits as selected and designated by the city's director of environmental services.
(b)
All employees operating city vehicles shall be appropriately trained in the safe and effective use of spill containment kits.
(Code 1989, § 10-5; Res. No. 800, 10-18-1999)
