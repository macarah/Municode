DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 42-120. - Ad valorem tax due and delinquency dates; penalties.
modified
Citizens of the city are hereby given the following deadline for filing and paying their property taxes:
(1)
All property owners, both real and personal, shall declare their taxable properties in accordance with the rules and regulations provided for by the county tax assessor and the state property tax division. The city shall levy and collect ad valorem taxes based upon tax records provided by the county. These records shall include, but not be limited to, the owner's name, address, property assessment, valuation, and exemptions (if any).
(2)
Ad valorem taxes will be billed on or about October 1 of each year for that tax year, and shall be payable within 60 days of the bill date. Any ad valorem taxes due under the provisions of this section shall bear interest at the maximum rate allowed by state law. Additionally, a penalty shall be imposed on any unpaid principal in accordance with state law.
(3)
Unpaid accounts shall be delinquent 30 days after the due date and shall be subject to late notices, publication of owners' names within the legal organ for the city, and execution of fi. fas.
(4)
The ad valorem tax rate for the City of Alpharetta, Georgia, for the 2021 calendar year, on property subject to ad valorem taxation by the city is hereby fixed at $5.750 on 40 percent of each $1,000.00 of property subject to ad valorem tax by the city.
(5)
Said rate of $5.750 on 40 percent of each $1,000.00 of taxable property is hereby levied as follows:
a.
For general government purposes, $4.870 on 40 percent of each $1,000.00 of taxable property.
b.
For the purpose of retiring outstanding general obligation bonds, $0.880 on 40 percent of each $1,000.00 of taxable property.
(Code 1989, § 8-51; Ord. No. 101, 11-8-1966; Ord. No. 457, § 1, 12-18-2000; Ord. No. 706, §§ I, II, 7-20-2015; Ord. No. 828 , §§ I, II, 6-21-2021; Ord. No. 834 , §§ I, II, 8-2-2021; Ord. No. 858 , § II, 6-19-2023)

DIVISION 2. - TAX EXECUTIONS AND SALES

Sec. 42-139. - Municipal tax collector.
(a)
Creation of office. There is hereby created in the city the office of municipal tax collector. This office shall be held by the city administrator who shall be responsible for carrying out the provisions of this division. The municipal tax collector shall also undertake such duties as prescribed, from time to time, by the mayor and council.
(b)
Additional powers and authority. The municipal tax collector shall have such further powers and authority as is necessary to carry out the requirements of this division.
(Code 1989, § 8-61; Ord. No. 263, §§ 1, 22, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-140. - Records to be kept by municipal tax collector.
The municipal tax collector or his designee shall maintain the following records in carrying out the provisions of this division:
(1)
Defaulters book. The municipal tax collector shall prepare and maintain a book listing all defaulters (delinquent taxpayers) and the amount and value of the property in default.
(2)
Execution docket. The municipal tax collector shall maintain an execution docket listing the executions against all delinquent taxpayers by entering their names in the docket along with an itemized statement of the taxes covered by the executions.
(Code 1989, § 8-62; Ord. No. 263, § 2, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-141. - Issuance, direction and recording of tax executions.
Where any property owner who has property subject to taxation within the limits of the city shall fail, refuse or neglect to pay the taxes imposed according to law or the ordinances of the city, the municipal tax collector is hereby authorized to issue an execution against the property owner for the tax, which execution shall bear interest in the name of the city, be entered on the records of the municipal tax collector, and be directed to the chief of police, commanding him to levy on the personal and real property of the property owner, or so much thereof as shall be sufficient to satisfy the demand and costs. Upon issuance of the execution, the municipal tax collector shall also be authorized to record the execution on the general execution docket in the office of the clerk of the superior court of the county.
(Code 1989, § 8-63; Ord. No. 263, § 3, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-142. - Date of issuance; enforcement of executions.
At any time after the last day for the payment of taxes has arrived, the municipal tax collector may notify, in writing, each delinquent taxpayer of the fact that the taxes owing have not been paid, and that, unless paid, an execution shall be issued. The municipal tax collector may issue executions for nonpayment of taxes collectable at any time after 30 days have elapsed since giving the foregoing notice. The execution shall include the owner of the property to which the execution is directed, the year for which the execution is to be issued, a description of the property sufficient to put interested parties on notice as to where the subject property can be located, the amount due including the tax, penalties, execution costs, and interest, the name of the officer conducting the levy and his address, the date the execution was issued, and the official who issued the tax execution. Such tax executions shall be levied and collected as provided by this division and state law.
(Code 1989, § 8-64; Ord. No. 263, § 4, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-143. - City's costs and fees on executions.
The costs and fees of the city on executions shall be as follows: the costs and fees associated with the collection of delinquent tax accounts including the execution of fi. fas, settling fi. fas, advertising, and any reasonable collection costs shall be added to the delinquent tax account at the actual proportionate cost paid by the city, with relation to each delinquent tax account.
(Code 1989, § 8-65; Ord. No. 263, § 5, 10-23-1989; Ord. No. 457, § 2, 12-18-2000)
Sec. 42-144. - Alias tax executions.
When a properly issued tax execution is lost or destroyed, an alias tax execution may be issued by the municipal tax collector, and such alias tax execution shall have the same force and effect as the lost or destroyed original execution.
(Code 1989, § 8-66; Ord. No. 263, § 6, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-145. - Interest on executions.
All taxes, from the date the same become delinquent and in default, and all penalties and other sums owing under executions, from the date of imposition, shall bear interest from such dates as provided by state law.
(Code 1989, § 8-67; Ord. No. 263, § 7, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-146. - Procedures for sales under tax executions.
The property which is the subject of the tax execution shall be advertised and sold as provided by state law for fi. fas and judicial sales, with the exception that the place of sale shall be held in the council chambers of the city. In addition to the notice required under state law, at any sale under tax execution under this division, the defendant in fi. fa shall be given 20 days' written notice of such sale by registered mail to the last-known address as listed in the records of the municipal tax collector.
(Code 1989, § 8-68; Ord. No. 263, § 8, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-147. - Duty of levying officers.
The municipal tax collector shall place issued executions in the hands of the city police chief or his designee, who shall be authorized to collect or levy the executions as provided herein. The levying officer shall proceed promptly to enforce by levy and sale the collection of the executions. The levying or collecting officer shall make prompt settlements with the municipal tax collector and return to the municipal tax collector the tax collected and the uncollected executions with proper entries on the executions.
(Code 1989, § 8-69; Ord. No. 263, § 9, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-148. - Failure to find property on which to levy.
When the levying officer can find no property belonging to the taxpayer on which to levy, the officer shall place such notation (nulla bona) on the execution and return it to the municipal tax collector. Upon the return of the execution, the city may proceed with the issuance of a summons of garnishment as well as such other remedies as provided under state law.
(Code 1989, § 8-70; Ord. No. 263, § 10, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-149. - Payment of excess.
Any excess remaining after the payment of taxes, costs and all expenses of a sale shall be immediately paid to the person authorized to receive the excess.
(Code 1989, § 8-71; Ord. No. 263, § 11, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-150. - Authority of levying officer to put purchaser in possession of land.
The officer selling property at a tax sale shall have the authority, subject to requirements of state law, to put purchasers in possession of land sold under tax executions.
(Code 1989, § 8-72; Ord. No. 263, § 12, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-151. - Purchase of property by city.
The city may purchase and hold in its official capacity any real or personal property offered for sale by virtue of tax executions, except that the city may bid on such property only when other bids do not cover the amount of the tax execution and related costs. The city shall not bid more for the property than the amount of taxes, interest, penalties and costs due.
(Code 1989, § 8-73; Ord. No. 263, § 13, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-152. - Register to be kept showing land sold for taxes and redemption.
The municipal tax collector shall keep a register of all sales of real estate, whether vacant or improved, in a book provided for that purpose, in which the municipal tax collector shall enter each tract or lot of land sold, the precise quantity sold, a copy of the advertisement, the amount of taxes and costs the property was sold for and the purchaser's name. Space shall also be provided to insert the name of the person who may redeem such lot or tract of land, the date of redemption and the amount of redemption money paid.
(Code 1989, § 8-74; Ord. No. 263, § 14, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-153. - Persons entitled to redeem land sold under tax execution; payment; time.
Whenever any real property is sold under or by virtue of an execution issued for the collection of ad valorem taxes, the defendant in fi. fa or any person having any right, title or interest in or lien upon such property may redeem the property from the sale by the payment of the redemption price as provided in section 42-154:
(1)
At any time within 12 months from the date of the sale; and
(2)
At any time after the sale until the right to redeem is foreclosed by the giving of the notice provided for in section 42-157.
(Code 1989, § 8-75; Ord. No. 263, § 15, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-154. - Amount payable for redemption.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in subsection (b) of this section, the amount required to be paid for redemption of property from any sale for taxes as provided in this division (the "redemption price") shall be the amount paid for the property at the tax sale, as shown by the recitals in the tax deed, plus any taxes paid on the property by the purchaser after the sale for taxes, plus a premium of ten percent of the amount for each year or fraction of a year which has elapsed between the date of the sale and the date on which the redemption payment is made. If redemption is not made until after the required notice of foreclosure of right to redeem has been given, there shall be added to the redemption price the cost of serving the notice, the cost of publication of the notice, if any, and the sum of ten percent of the amount paid for the property at the sale to cover the cost of making the necessary examinations to determine the person upon whom notice should be served. All amounts required to be paid hereunder shall be paid to the purchaser at the tax sale or to his successors.
(b)
In cases where the city purchases property at a tax sale, the redemption price shall be the amount paid for the property at the tax sale, as shown by the recitals in the tax deed, plus a premium of 20 percent of the amount for each year or fraction of a year which has elapsed between the date of the sale and the date on which the redemption payment is made. If redemption is not made until after the required notice of foreclosure of right to redeem has been given, there shall be added to the redemption price the cost in connection with serving the notice, the cost of publication of the notice, if any, and the further sum of 20 percent of the amount paid for the property of the sale to cover the cost of making the necessary examination to determine the person upon whom notice should be served. All of the amounts required to be paid hereunder shall be paid to the purchaser at the tax sale or to his successors.
(Code 1989, § 8-76; Ord. No. 263, § 16, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-155. - Effect of redemption.
When property has been redeemed, the effect of the redemption shall be to put the title conveyed by the tax sale back into the defendant in fi. fa, subject to all liens existing at the time of the tax sale. If the redemption has been made by any creditor of the defendant or by any person having any interest in the property, the amount expended by the creditor or person interested shall constitute a first lien on the property and, if the quit-claim deed provided for in section 42-156 is recorded as required by law, shall be repaid prior to any other claims upon the property.
(Code 1989, § 8-77; Ord. No. 263, § 17, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-156. - Quit-claim deed by purchaser.
In all cases where property is redeemed, the purchaser at the tax sale shall make a quit-claim deed to the defendant in fi. fa, which deed shall recite the name of the person who has paid the redemption money and the capacity in which, or the claim of right or interest pursuant to which, the redemption money was paid.
(Code 1989, § 8-78; Ord. No. 263, § 18, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-157. - Notice of foreclosure of right to redeem; time.
After 12 months from the date of a tax sale, the purchaser at the sale or his heirs, successors or assigns may terminate, foreclose, divest and forever bar the right to redeem the property from the sale by causing a notice of the foreclosure as prescribed by state law to be served as provided by state law.
(Code 1989, § 8-79; Ord. No. 263, § 19, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-158. - Sale of property bought by city at tax sale and not redeemed.
Where property purchased by the city at a tax sale by the municipal tax collector has not been redeemed as provided by law, it may be sold by the city in accordance with state law.
(Code 1989, § 8-80; Ord. No. 263, § 20, 10-23-1989)
Sec. 42-159. - Execution of deeds.
It shall be the duty of the municipal tax collector to execute deeds to purchasers at tax sales under executions.
(Code 1989, § 8-81; Ord. No. 263, § 21, 10-23-1989)
