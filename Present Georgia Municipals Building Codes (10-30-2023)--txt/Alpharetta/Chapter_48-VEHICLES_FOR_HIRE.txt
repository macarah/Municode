Sec. 48-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Taxicab means a motor vehicle used in the business of transporting passengers for compensation in the city and not operated on a fixed route. The term "taxicab" shall not include limousines regulated by the state public service commission.
(Code 1989, § 13-146; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-20. - Permits required.
No permit is required if the operation of the taxicab in the city is limited solely to passing through the city without picking up a passenger or dropping off a passenger whose trip originated outside the city.
(Code 1989, § 13-147; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 1, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-21. - Classes of permits.
There shall be three classes of permits required under this article:
(1)
Company permit. This permit applies to persons, firms, partnerships, corporations or other entities engaged in the business of operating a taxicab in the city, and which are subject to an occupation tax under chapter 42.
(2)
Resident operator's permit. This permit applies to any person operating a taxicab on the streets of the city and who is operating under the auspices of a company permit.
(3)
Nonresident operator's permit. This permit applies to any person operating a taxicab on the streets of the city and who is not operating under the auspices of a company subject to an occupation tax in the city.
(Code 1989, § 13-148; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-22. - Company permit.
In order to secure a company permit, an applicant must provide information showing its qualifications on a form provided by the city, and must submit to a background investigation and fingerprinting. An applicant must:
(1)
Be at least 21 years of age;
(2)
Be a citizen of the United States or an alien admitted for permanent residence, or a person who has otherwise been granted employment authorized by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service;
(3)
Hold an occupation tax certificate issued by the city;
(4)
Maintain an office within the city staffed at all times during hours of operation as outlined in this article by company employees, including a dispatcher, from which all dispatching of vehicles will be performed;
(5)
Provide proof of insurance as required in this article.
(Code 1989, § 13-149; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-23. - Convictions disqualifying applicant for a company permit.
No permit shall be issued if the applicant has been convicted of any of the following offenses:
(1)
Manslaughter, or negligent homicide, resulting from the operation of a motor vehicle.
(2)
Driving a motor vehicle while under the influence of intoxicating liquor or drugs, in the last five years.
(3)
Any felony in the commission of which a motor vehicle is used.
(4)
Failure to stop and render aid or leaving the scene of an accident, as required under the laws of the state.
(5)
Perjury or false swearing in making any statements under oath in connection with this application for permit.
(6)
Perjury or the making of a false affidavit or statement under oath relating to the ownership or operation of motor vehicles.
(7)
Convictions or forfeiture of bail on three charges of a violation of the state motor vehicle laws within a 12-month period.
(8)
The violation of any law involving moral turpitude.
(9)
Repeated violation of the city ordinances, county laws, or state laws which affect the safety of human life or limb on the streets of the city.
(Code 1989, § 13-150; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 2, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-24. - Place of operation.
No person to whom an occupation tax certificate to operate or engage in a taxicab business has been granted by lawful authority shall operate or carry on the business from any place in the city except the established place of business referred to and described in the application for an occupation tax certificate to operate or engage in the taxicab business.
(Code 1989, § 13-151; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-25. - Hours of operation.
The standard hours of operations for any holder of a company permit to operate or engage in a taxicab business within the city shall be Monday through Sunday, 24 hours per day.
(Code 1989, § 13-152; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-26. - Insurance required.
(a)
No owner of a taxicab shall operate or authorize any other person to operate a taxicab in the city unless it is covered by a liability insurance policy issued by an insurance company authorized to do business in this state, which provides the following minimum coverage:
(1)
$25,000.00 for injury to or death of one person in one accident;
(2)
$50,000.00 for injury to or death of two or more persons in any one accident; and
(3)
$25,000.00 for injury or destruction of property of others in any one accident.
(b)
Each taxicab operated within the city limits shall be required to carry and maintain in effect this minimum insurance coverage and provide current certificates of insurance evidencing such coverage, the name of the insurance company supplying the coverage and the policy number. The insurance must provide specific coverage for a vehicle used in the business of transporting passengers.
(c)
If the term of said insurance coverage is less than a period of 12 months, on the first Monday of each month, the holder of the company permit shall provide to the chief of police or his designee either a current insurance identification card for each vehicle intended to be operated in the coming month or a verified statement from a licensed insurance agent or licensed insurance company within this state setting forth the identity of the vehicles for which coverage has been procured, by vehicle identification number, make and model, the amount of coverage afforded to each such vehicle and the term for which such coverage has been prepared by the taxicab company or taxicab driver.
(d)
If the term of such insurance coverage is at least a period of 12 months, proof of such insurance coverage, together with a receipted premium from the insurer, or its duly authorized agent, showing payment of the premium on the policy for and during the period covered by the life of the occupational tax certificate, shall be provided to the city before a permit shall be issued. The insurance policy must provide and stipulate therein that the insurer will give notice to the city a minimum of 30 days before its termination or cancellation, for any reason, of the policy on the person insured.
(e)
Failure to maintain such coverage shall constitute cause for revocation of a city permit.
(f)
Nothing in this section shall be construed to waive applicable state or federal laws, if any, which require a higher amount of minimum coverage.
(Code 1989, § 13-153; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 3, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-27. - Drivers' qualifications.
Before operating a taxicab within the city, a driver shall:
(1)
Possess a Georgia class C driver's license, with a for-hire endorsement or a private background check certification, through the taxicab service or ride share network service that employs them;
(2)
Be competent to operate a motor vehicle;
(3)
Be familiar with the traffic laws and ordinances of the city;
(4)
Not have been convicted of a felony unless ten years have elapsed since the date of completion of the felony sentence;
(5)
Not have been convicted of a crime of moral turpitude within past ten years;
(6)
Not have been classified as an habitual offender by the department of public safety for the previous five years;
(7)
Not have had a driver's license revoked by the department of public safety for violations for the previous five years;
(8)
Provide authorization and any information necessary to enable the director of public safety or his designee to investigate the driver's background, criminal history, and traffic record;
(9)
Be of good character as determined by the aforesaid investigation; and
(10)
Possess an operator's permit issued by the city.
(Code 1989, § 13-154; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 4, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-28. - Taxicab equipment.
All taxicabs operating in the city shall:
(1)
Be equipped with a taximeter maintained in good working order at all times. Such taximeter shall be permanently affixed in front of the passenger's seat and be visible to them at all times. After sundown, the face of the taximeter shall be illuminated. It shall be operated mechanically by a mechanism of standard design and construction, driven either from the transmission or from one of the front wheels by a flexible and permanently attached driving mechanism. It shall be sealed at all points and connections that, if manipulated, would affect its correct reading and recording.
(2)
Be affixed with a vehicle permit sticker issued by the director of public safety or his designee. Such stickers must at all times be displayed in the upper right corner of the rear window. Each vehicle will be assigned a numbered sticker, and that sticker shall not be used on any other vehicle.
(3)
Such marking shall be affixed only by means of permanent vinyl lettering or paint, and shall be a minimum of four inches in height. The four-inch lettering requirement can be met if the logo is a minimum of four inches in height and the lettering within the logo is a minimum of two inches in height.
(4)
Be marked with rates on the side of the vehicle so as to be plainly visible to persons seeking to use the taxicab.
(5)
Maintain signage in the passenger compartment stating, in clearly legible lettering of a minimum of one inch in height, "The public is invited to contact the city department of public safety by calling (678)297-6306 to report any instance of a taxicab being operated while not in a clean and safe operating condition."
(6)
Be equipped with a roof light permanently mounted on the roof of the vehicle and maintained in good working order at all times.
(7)
Be maintained in good, safe and serviceable mechanical condition as verified in a statement signed by a technician at a location properly licensed to do business within the city, and be in compliance with all county and state licensing and emission standards.
(8)
Be maintained in a clean condition, free from foreign matter and offensive odors.
(9)
Transport as passengers only the number of persons specified by the vehicle manufacturer.
(10)
The minimum weight of a taxicab shall be 2,000 pounds, as determined by the manufacturer's specifications.
(11)
No vehicle operated as a taxicab shall be more than eight years old.
(12)
No taxicab shall have less than three doors for loading passengers, and each taxicab must be a station wagon, a sedan, a minivan or a sports utility vehicle. All such vehicles shall meet the emission standards as set by federal guidelines for their particular class.
(Code 1989, § 13-155; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 5, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-29. - Log books.
(a)
Daily trip sheets shall be maintained for all taxicabs operated within the city. The driver shall retain the trip sheets in the vehicle for a period of 72 hours from the time of the recorded trip, after which they will be maintained in the office of the company permit holder for a period of no less than 120 days. All trip sheets shall be subject to the inspection of the police chief or his designee.
(b)
As used in this subsection, the term "trip sheet" means a daily log of all passengers, the time, place of entry and the destination of each passenger, the amount charged and an itemization of any personal property left in the vehicle for hire.
(Code 1989, § 13-156; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-30. - Transferability.
Any vehicle permit issued in accordance with this article shall not be transferable.
(Code 1989, § 13-157; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-31. - Denial, suspension and revocation of permit.
(a)
An application for a permit or a permit issued under this article may be denied, suspended or revoked for due cause as defined in subsection (c) of this section.
(b)
The municipal court shall furnish a permit holder a notice setting forth the time, place, and purpose of a hearing at which it intends to consider the revocation or suspension of a permit. After a hearing, if the municipal court determines due cause exists, it may suspend, revoke or place on probation for a maximum of 12 months, with or without conditions, the permit.
(c)
A permit may be denied, suspended or revoked for any of the following reasons:
(1)
Where the applicant furnishes fraudulent or untruthful information or omits information requested in the application for a permit.
(2)
For failure to pay all fees, taxes, penalties or other charges imposed by the provisions of this article, and of this Code.
(3)
For failure to maintain all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a permit.
(4)
For violation of any part of this article by the permit holder, his agents, partners, officers, employees or contractors.
(5)
Allowing the required insurance coverage to lapse shall result in the automatic suspension of the permit.
(6)
For unauthorized transfer or other misuse of the permit.
(d)
An operator's permit may be suspended or revoked for having three or more moving violations in any 12-month period.
(e)
Permit holders under this article are responsible for violations of this article by their vehicle operators whether such operators are direct employees or independent contractors.
(f)
Permits issued under this article are not transferable.
(Code 1989, § 13-158; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 6, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-32. - Regulatory fee required.
A regulatory fee of $300.00 shall be required annually for a company permit and the resident operator's permits for drivers operating under the auspices of a company permit. A regulatory fee of $50.00 shall be required annually for a nonresident operator's permit.
(Code 1989, § 13-159; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 7, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-33. - Notification of new drivers.
It shall be the duty of a company permit holder to notify the director of public safety whenever a new driver is hired or terminated from employment. Failure to so notify can result in revocation of the permit.
(Code 1989, § 13-160; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-34. - Violations.
(a)
Repeated violations of traffic laws or ordinances, or of any ordinance provision regulating the conduct of taxicab drivers, shall be cause for revocation of said permit to operate in the city.
(b)
It is a violation and grounds for suspension or revocation of an operator permit for any operator to:
(1)
Commit more than three traffic infractions within any 12-month period;
(2)
Be found guilty of a felony;
(3)
Be guilty of reckless driving or driving or being in physical control of a vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs;
(4)
Fail to deliver to the city police department within 24 hours any property of value left in a vehicle;
(5)
Allow a person to alight from the vehicle while it is in motion, or discharge a passenger at any place other than the curb or pavement edge;
(6)
Allow a passenger to consume alcohol, intoxicating substances, or other controlled substances in the vehicle;
(7)
Transport a passenger to a destination by a route that is not the safest and most direct, unless the passenger specifically authorizes such alternate or indirect route;
(8)
Pick up any additional passengers without the express consent of the original passenger;
(9)
Refuse to pick up as a passenger any person of proper deportment when the vehicle is available, or discriminate against any person because of race, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, or mental or sensory handicap;
(10)
Activate the meter when the vehicle is not engaged or fail to activate the meter at the beginning of each trip, unless the trip is made under contract;
(11)
Activate equipment indicating the vehicle is engaged when it is not, or fail to activate such equipment when the vehicle is engaged;
(12)
Engage in any unfair or deceptive acts or practices.
(Code 1989, § 13-161; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002; Ord. No. 523, § 8, 3-3-2003)
Sec. 48-35. - Enforcement and penalty.
(a)
The police department and the code enforcement office shall enforce the regulations contained in this article.
(b)
Violations of the provisions of this article shall be punished according to the penalties described in section 1-8. Each separate occurrence shall be deemed a separate offense.
(c)
The violation of the provisions of this article by any person, corporation, partnership or other entity may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings for injunction in the courts of competent jurisdiction in this state. Such actions may be maintained notwithstanding that other adequate remedies at law exist. Such actions may be instituted in the name of the city.
(d)
Violations of any provision of this article by any permit holder shall be due cause for revocation of any city permit or certificate, after notice and hearing before the city municipal court.
(Code 1989, § 13-162; Ord. No. 497, § I, 6-17-2002)
Sec. 48-36. - Miscellaneous.
Nothing in this article shall be deemed to prohibit the charging of a flat rate for trips to and from Hartsfield International Airport, provided that such flat rates are conspicuously posted on the taxicab or set forth on a rate card provided to the customer prior to the trip.
(Code 1989, § 13-163; Ord. No. 523, § 9, 3-3-2003)
