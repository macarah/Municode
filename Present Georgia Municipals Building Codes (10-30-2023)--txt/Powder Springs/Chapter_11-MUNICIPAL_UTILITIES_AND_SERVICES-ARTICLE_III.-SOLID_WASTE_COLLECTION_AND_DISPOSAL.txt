ARTICLE III. - SOLID WASTE COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL[3]

Sec. 11-40. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Garbage: All vegetable matter, food scraps, newspapers, ice cream cartons, all tissue paper and other waste material from kitchens, grocery stores, butcher shops, restaurants, cafes, drugstores, hotels, roominghouses and boardinghouses.
Recyclable materials means plastic beverage bottles and jugs, glass bottles and jars, corrugated cardboard, newspaper, bimetal and aluminum cans, magazines, telephone books, automobile batteries, used motor oil, dry paint cans, metal, and large appliances.
Rubbish: All plastics, glass, metal cans, throw-away baby diapers, dog and cat litter, bundled newspapers, razor blades, wire, and all throw-away bottles.
Trash: Paint cans, boxes, old rags, yard cleanings, grass clippings, leaves, pine straw, broken toys, tree trimmings, grills, and old garbage cans.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 1, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-41. - Refuse not acceptable for collection by city.
The following refuse shall be considered to be not acceptable for collection by the city:
(1)
Dangerous materials or substances, such as poisons, acids, caustics, infected materials and explosives.
(2)
Unusual quantities of materials resulting from the repair, excavation or construction of buildings or structures such as earth, plaster, mortar and roofing materials.
(3)
Materials which have not been prepared for collection in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(4)
The solid wastes resulting from industrial processes.
(5)
Tree trimmings where trees are trimmed or cut for compensation whether the compensation be in cash or in the wood from the trees trimmed or cut except in the case of a natural disaster.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-42. - Inspection.
It is hereby made the duty of the director of the utilities and public works department or designated representative, to make inspection trips at regular intervals to determine whether or not garbage, trash, recyclable materials and rubbish are being properly collected, removed and disposed of as required by this Code or any applicable health ordinance. In the event it is found that such ordinance or regulations are being violated, appropriate and timely action shall be taken to insure full compliance with the provisions.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 2, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-43. - Service required; exceptions; penalties; fee increases.
(a)
Every household in the city shall subscribe to the sanitation service provided or contracted by the city for the collection of garbage, trash, recyclable materials and rubbish. A household may be exempted by providing a certified statement as to the method of disposal of the household wastes, and by applying for the temporary suspension of service as provided below. The disposal must be in accordance with the state law requiring safe, sanitary and adequate disposal.
(b)
Those persons found guilty of improper disposal of their household waste shall be fined the maximum allowed by law per occurrence. Improper disposal shall include placing waste in commercial dumpsters, along roadways, vacant lots, another person's cart, and similar situations.
(c)
The monthly sanitation fee shall be established by resolution, which may be amended from time to time, and shall be maintained in the office of the city clerk. The fee shall be adjusted automatically to account for any increases in dumping fees.
(d)
Temporary suspension of service and of monthly sanitation fee for unoccupied residences.
(1)
A city resident whose residential unit will be unoccupied for more than thirty (30) consecutive days may make application with city hall for a temporary suspension of the service and fee. Such application may be made by the resident on a form made available by city hall. The length of suspension of the service and fee shall not exceed six (6) months unless authorized by the public works director.
(2)
Within fourteen (14) days of the decision by city hall, any resident aggrieved by a decision to refuse to suspend may appeal that decision to the public works director for a final administrative hearing and determination. The public works director shall conduct an informal administrative hearing within ten (10) business days of the resident appealing the original decision to not suspend. The resident and the city shall have the right to present sworn testimony and evidence. Upon the final administrative decision of the public works director to approve or deny the suspension under this chapter, such decision being in writing and including a notice of the decision and reasons therefore to the resident, the applicant may appeal the decision to the Cobb County Superior Court by writ of certiorari. There shall be no suspension of the service or fee during the appeal notification period or while an appeal is pending before the public works director.
(Ord. No. 87-7, §§ 1—3, 2-16-87; Ord. No. 2010-52, § 2, 11-15-10; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 3, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-44. - Collection to be carried on in systematic and efficient manner.
The collection, removal and disposal of all garbage, trash, recyclable materials and rubbish shall be carried on in a systematic and efficient manner, keeping the entire city in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 4, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-45. - Plastic carts—Provided by city.
(a)
Plastic wheeled carts shall be required for street-side collection service. The responsibility for obtaining such cart shall be upon the owner or occupant of the premises desiring and otherwise eligible, by the terms of this article, for street-side collection service. The owner or occupant of the premises is required to contact the department of public works and request the delivery of a cart.
(b)
Additional carts may be purchased from the city by the owner or occupant and will be serviced according to the provision of this article subject to the following limit, which is hereby imposed upon the number of carts that will be serviced by the city:
Number of Carts
(1)
Residences .....2
(2)
Commercial establishments .....2
(3)
Multiple housing units and trailer courts less than ten (10) separate units, per unit .....2
(4)
Multiple housing units and trailer courts of ten (10) or more housing units shall be required to use dumpster boxes.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-45.1. - Recycling.
In order to protect the environment by conserving natural resources and preserving rapidly dwindling landfill space as well as minimizing waste disposal costs, the city encourages recycling for all residential occupants within the city limits to recycle all recyclable materials.
(1)
Collection and container requirements for recyclable materials will be the same as those outlined in sections 11-45, 11-46, and 11-48 of this chapter.
( Ord. No. 2014-033, § 5, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-46. - Same—Placement for collection.
All refuse, ashes, garbage, trash and recyclable materials shall be placed in carts for collection. The carts shall be placed at street-side for collection purposes. The collection schedule shall be announced by the department of utilities and public works. If the carts are not near the street-side at collection time, this will indicate no service is needed.
(Ord. No 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 6, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-47. - Same—Non-street-side collection.
Non-street-side collection will be provided for households within the city limits if all persons abiding in the household meet the following conditions:
(1)
Physically unable to walk a cart to the curb, due to being confined to a bed or confined to a wheelchair, or,
(2)
Physically unable to roll a garbage cart to street side, as certified by a medical doctor, or,
(3)
Older than sixty-five (65) years of age,
(4)
For those persons who are head of household and age seventy (70) years or older, backyard service will be provided at a rate of fifty (50) percent of the normal residential customer rate.
Each household desiring to receive backyard service must apply to city hall with the appropriate information. The household information will be verified and a decision provided. If the applicant is denied, the decision may be appealed to the governing body.
The exception will be reevaluated each twelve (12) months.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 87-9, §§ 1, 2, 3-2-87; Ord. No. 89-15, §§ 1, 2, 5-15-89)
Sec. 11-48. - Same—Time limit at street-side.
Carts shall be placed at street-side no earlier than 8:00 p.m. on the day before the scheduled collection day and carts shall be removed from street-side no later than 9:00 p.m. on the day of collection. Violations of this section shall be reported to the department of utilities and public works. Upon receiving a complaint, the department shall investigate such complaint. Upon finding a violation, the offending party shall have forty-eight (48) hours after receiving the notice to remove carts. Any party failing to remove carts from street-side within the specified time after receiving the notice shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 7, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-49. - Same—Replacement.
(a)
The department of utilities and public works shall have the authority to require the replacement of any cart that is so worn or damaged as to hinder the effective street-side collection program.
(b)
Any cart that is damaged or destroyed through the negligence or misuse of the city shall be replaced at the expense of the city. The department of utilities and public works shall have the authority to determine in each case of damage whether the damage is sufficient to require replacement.
(c)
In all other cases in which a cart is rendered unserviceable, the city may replace the cart and charge the property owner for all replacement costs incurred in connection with the replacement.
(d)
The property owner shall be responsible for replacement of any lost or stolen cart.
(e)
Deposits for city garbage carts being returned will be based upon the condition of the cart returned, ordinary wear and tear excepted.
(f)
Subject to availability, the city may allow those residents utilizing city garbage service to obtain a used garbage cart for a deposit of thirty-five dollars ($35.00) per cart. All other provisions of city's waste collection and disposal ordinance applicable to new carts shall continue to apply to any used carts obtained under this provision.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 93-23, §§ 1, 2, 9-7-93; Ord. No. 95-11, 10-2-95)
Sec. 11-50. - Same—Not to be over-filled; placement of extra trash in disposable bags or boxes.
Carts shall not be filled to overflowing. When carts are fully loaded, extra trash, garbage, refuse or ashes may be placed in disposable containers and placed next to carts for collection. The owner/occupant may place tree limbs, branches, tree cuttings and vines beside the curb for collection by request to city hall and payment of fee. Such tree limbs, branches, tree cuttings and vines shall be no longer than four (4) feet and no more than seventy-five (75) pounds in weight. The city shall not collect trimmings where trees are trimmed or cut for compensation whether the compensation be in cash or in the wood from the trees trimmed or cut except in the case of a natural disaster.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 8, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-51. - Containers for trash and cold ashes, generally.
Trash or cold ashes may be separately placed in disposable containers. All such receptacles shall be removed with the contents. Trash and ashes shall not be placed in any disposable containers that are so worn or defective that the contents leak or spill out.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-52. - Material not to be collected from premises where violation exists.
Trash and refuse collectors are not required to collect from premises where the provisions of this article are violated. The failure to collect any trash or garbage from the premises because of a violation of the provisions of this article shall not relieve the persons responsible for such violation from prosecution and penalty therefor.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-53. - Confinement of animals which interfere with collectors.
Occupants of premises shall facilitate collections of garbage, trash, recyclable materials and refuse by confining animals which might interfere with collectors.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 9, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-54. - Frequency of collections.
Except as otherwise in this article provided and except in the case of emergency arising from an act of God or under circumstances over which the director has no control, the department shall collect, remove and dispose of all household refuse and trash in the city once per week.
(1)
If a holiday should fall during the week in which an area is due a regular collection, such collection shall be made on the following work day, unless otherwise announced by the department of public works.
(2)
Refrigerators, stoves, washing machines, furniture and similar items will be collected only by appointment made with the department. Collections shall be made at the property line and such items shall be placed at the property line no sooner than twenty-four (24) hours prior to the appointed time of collection. The occupant of the premises shall be responsible to see that locks or doors of refrigerators, ice boxes or containers are removed.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-55. - Dumpster boxes.
In the case of multiple housing units of ten (10) or more individual units, trailer courts of ten (10) or more separate units and in the case of commercial establishments when the limit imposed by section 11-45 is exceeded, dumpster boxes shall be used. In the case of multiple housing units and trailer courts, of ten (10) or more units, when dumpster boxes are required, there shall be one (1) six-cubic-yard box for every ten (10) units.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-55.1. - Disposition of industrial waste.
Industrial waste shall be collected, removed and disposed of by the operator of the factory, plant or enterprise creating or causing the same in accordance with regulations promulgated by the governing body.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-56. - Disposition of building rubbish.
All trash, debris, tree limbs, stumps, brush, logs, building scraps, leaves, and other such debris and rubbish shall be removed from the construction site or burned in accordance with the county fire safety code after a burning permit is issued in writing by the county fire department.
(a)
No debris of any kind shall be buried at the site of any construction or development.
(b)
A grading permit must be issued by the city public works department prior to any grading, as provided in the unified development code.
(c)
No fill shall be made until after inspection by the public works department in accordance with the provisions of the unified development code.
(d)
Those persons found guilty of improper disposal of building waste shall be fined the maximum allowed by law per occurrence and be required to promptly remove rubbish, trash, debris or other waste.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 88-25, § 1, 8-15-88; Ord. No. 88-34, §§ 1—10, 12-5-88; Ord. No. 91-20, 7-15-91; Ord. No. 2010-52, § 3, 11-15-10)
Sec. 11-57. - Disposition so as to avoid pick-up service.
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit any garbage, dirt, trash, dead animals or other refuse in any container provided or maintained by any owner, occupant, or tenant or lessee using or occupying any building, house or structure within the city for residences, schools, churches, colleges, lodges, commercial businesses in any quantity to avoid providing or subscribing to a service for the pick-up of garbage, trash, dirt, dead animals or other refuse at the residence or the site of the operations of the business of such persons.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-58. - Depositing refuse in container of another.
It shall be unlawful for any person to wilfully or intentionally deposit garbage, trash, recyclable materials or rubbish into the garbage container of another person.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-22-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 10, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-59. - Garbage and trash to be drained or wrapped before placing therein.
(a)
All garbage or trash that is mixed with water or other liquids shall be drained before being put in the garbage or trash container.
(b)
All animal matter that is subject to decomposition shall be well-wrapped in paper or other material before being deposited in such container.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-60. - Tampering with contents.
It shall be unlawful for any person to uncover, pilfer, dig into, turn over or in any manner tamper with any refuse container belonging to another. This section shall not be construed to prohibit the use of public refuse containers for their intended purpose.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-61. - Collection procedures; service deposit.
(a)
To avoid duplication in billing and as a convenience to the public, the fee for collection service shall be included on the monthly water bill. Should this service be rendered to any owner, occupant, tenant or lessee not being served by the city public water system, such charges for this service shall be rendered separately on a monthly basis.
(b)
Any person desiring to receive collection service shall pay a deposit, the deposit to be held by the city until after the rendering of the final bill due the city for services. The amount of the deposit will be determined by the service rate of the customer.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 87-7, § 1, 2-16-87)
Sec. 11-62. - Service charges.
The fee charged by the city for deposit and collection of garbage, trash and recyclable materials shall be determined by the city. Rates shall be as posted at city hall and shall be reviewed at least annually.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 11, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-63. - Private collectors prohibited.
No private collector of garbage shall operate in the city. The collection of garbage shall be exclusively a municipal function. The only private collector of recyclable materials operating in the city shall be the private collector authorized by the city to collect recyclable materials.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86; Ord. No. 2014-033, § 12, 10-6-14 )
Sec. 11-64. - Enforcement.
It shall be the duty of the director of the utilities public works department to enforce these rules and regulations, and in performance of this duty the director or such director's authorized representative is hereby authorized to enter, at any reasonable hour, any premises as may be necessary for such enforcement. The chief of police or his representative shall issue actual citations upon the request of the superintendent of public works.
(Ord. No. 86-24, 9-2-86)
Sec. 11-65. - Packaging of garbage, rubbish and trash within Central Business District.
In addition to any and all other requirements of law, including any and all other requirements of this article, all commercial establishments (including, without limitation, all office, institutional, retail, commercial and industrial uses) located in any CBD zoning district which occupy or control single buildings containing ten thousand (10,000) square feet or more of floor space, or multiple buildings aggregating ten thousand (10,000) square feet or more floor space, shall compact, to the smallest practicable volume, all garbage, trash and rubbish to be collected by the city.
Rubbish may be stored for collection in paperboard boxes or other disposable containers sufficiently strong to contain the rubbish.
Disposable paper or plastic bags of a type, size and material designed for solid waste disposal may be used to contain garbage and/or refuse for storage and collection, provided they are unbroken, sealed, and loaded in such a manner that normal handling cannot cause the bag to rupture or open.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-15-90)
Secs. 11-66—11-69. - Reserved.
