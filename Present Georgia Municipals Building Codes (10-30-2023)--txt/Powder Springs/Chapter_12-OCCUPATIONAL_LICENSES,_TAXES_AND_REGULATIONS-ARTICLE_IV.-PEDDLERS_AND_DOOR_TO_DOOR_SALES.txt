Sec. 12-80. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable organization. Any benevolent, philanthropic, patriotic, or eleemosynary (of, relating to, or supported by charity or alms) corporation, partnership, limited liability company, association, joint-stock company, trust, unincorporated organization or individual who solicits or obtains contributions solicited from the general public, any part of which contributions is used for charitable purposes.
Charitable purpose. Any charitable, benevolent, philanthropic, patriotic, or eleemosynary purpose for religion, health, education, social welfare, arts and humanities, environment, civic or public interest.
Door-to-door salesperson. Any person who shall solicit orders on behalf of a firm, corporation, company, association, partnership or individual of any goods, wares or merchandise, or other things of value from house to house shall be deemed a door-to-door salesperson. Any person who obtains orders for merchandise or other things of value shall be deemed a door-to-door salesperson. Excluded from this definition are persons who solicit orders for goods, wares or merchandise, or other things of value for charitable organizations or purposes or on behalf of religious organizations, political organizations and political candidates.
Peddler. Any person who sells or solicits for sale in the city limits of Powder Springs any new or used goods, wares, merchandise, produce or other things of value and goes about from place to place within the city selling or offering for sale any of such things to either merchants or customers shall be deemed a peddler. Excluded from this definition are persons who sell or solicit for sale goods, wares or merchandise, or other things of value for charitable organizations or purposes or on behalf of religious organizations, political organizations and political candidates.
Vehicle. Motorized conveyance fully functioning and moving under its own power with valid vehicle tag, and registered in the name of the owner of the business. A vehicle shall not include a trailer or anything pulled by the vehicle. Vehicles shall not be in excess of twenty-one (21) feet in length, seven (7) feet in width, and eight (8) feet in height.
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09)
Sec. 12-81. - Peddlers.
(a)
Every peddler shall be required to obtain a license.
(b)
Peddlers shall park the vehicle associated with the business only on asphalt or concrete out of the right-of-way and conduct business only in locations zoned CRC, Community Retail Commercial.
(c)
Peddlers shall maintain all merchandise on a vehicle, and the peddler's business license shall be void if the peddler does not have in his possession at the location the business license issued by the city and a written notarized affidavit from the property owner authorizing the peddler to be engaged in business at the property owner's location.
(d)
All vehicles must be removed from the peddler's location each day.
(e)
No tents, tables, boxes, chairs, or any accessory to the business shall be allowed. Anything associated with the business, with exception of personnel, that is not on the vehicle shall be in violation of this article.
(f)
Only one (1) sign, not exceeding six (6) square feet in size, is allowed and shall be on the vehicle.
(g)
Only one (1) vehicle is allowed for each location.
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09; Ord. No. 2017-010 , § 1, 5-1-17)
Sec. 12-82. - Door-to-door salespersons.
(a)
No door to door salesperson may conduct business without first being issued a permit from the city. The door-to-door salesperson shall present the permit to each household he visits. The permit shall include a photograph of the permittee and be issued through the police department.
(b)
The door-to-door salesperson is not to enter a home except at the express invitation of the occupant.
(c)
The occupant has the right to refuse entry or to ask the door-to-door salesperson to leave the premises, and, if the salesperson should fail to leave the premises after such instructions, he shall be in violation of the laws of this state and ordinances of the city.
(d)
If a person purchases the product of the door-to-door salesperson, the person has the right to cancel a home solicitation service agreement any time until midnight on the business day after the day on which he signs the agreement, provided he gives notice of the cancellation to the seller at the place of business as set forth in the agreement or by certified mail, return receipt requested, which shall be posted not later than midnight on the day following execution of the agreement.
(e)
At all times, the door-to-door salesperson shall wear the permit so that it can be readily seen by the owner or occupant of the premises.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for a door-to-door salesperson to fail to wear the permit readily visible to the resident or occupant of a home at which the door-to-door salesperson solicits business.
At each house, the door-to-door salesperson shall announce to the owner or occupant of the home his name and that he has been issued a permit by the city as a home door-to-door salesperson, and shall announce the purpose of his appearance at the residence.
(g)
No door-to-door salesperson shall enter private property without the express invitation of the occupant or owner. If a "no soliciting" sign is posted at or on a residence, no soliciting shall be made and the door-to-door salesperson shall vacate the premises. If a "no soliciting" sign is posted at the entrance of a residential subdivision, no soliciting shall be made at any residence in such subdivision, and the door-to-door salesperson shall vacate such subdivision.
(h)
A door-to-door salesperson shall at all times maintain a courteous decorum and shall not use opprobrious words to any homeowner or occupant. No door-to-door salesperson shall remain upon the premises of an occupant or owner after such occupant or owner has requested the door-to-door salesperson to leave or indicated that he is not interested in purchasing the door-to-door salesperson's product.
(i)
It shall be unlawful for any door-to-door salesperson to represent by word, action or deed that the issuance of a permit by the city in any way represents approval or condonation by the city of either the action of the door-to-door salesperson or his product or organization.
(j)
If a door-to-door salesperson shall secure an order from an owner or occupant, such door-to-door salesperson shall inform the owner or occupant as to his rights under the Retail Installment and Home Solicitation Act of the state. This may be done by the door-to-door salesperson leaving a written card or document specifying the rights of a homeowner or occupant under the Retail Installment and Home Solicitation Sales Act (O.C.G.A. § 10-1-1 et seq.).
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09; Ord. No. 2012-46, 11-19-12; Ord. No. 2017-010 , § 1, 5-1-17)
Sec. 12-83. - Records of license office; complaints.
(a)
The city police department shall maintain true and accurate records of the name and identification number of each door-to-door salesperson, together with other requirements of this division, and shall maintain a log of all complaints for each peddler and door-to-door salesperson or organization represented by such person. If the police department shall receive any ordinance violation complaints concerning a particular peddler or door-to-door salesperson, or three (3) violation complaints concerning an organization, the police department shall notify the peddler and door-to-door salesperson and organization such person represents of the complaints, and the city manager or his/her designee may suspend the license of the peddler or permit of the door-to-door salesperson until such time as the peddler or door-to-door salesperson or organization can show cause that he or it is in compliance with the rules and regulations of this article.
(b)
The city police department shall maintain a true and accurate log of each complaint showing the name of the complainant, the address of the complainant, and the substance of the complaint. Such records shall be available to the peddler, door-to-door salesperson, or his company or organization, and shall be deemed a public record. A hearing shall be set before the city manager or his/her designee within five (5) business days after notification to the peddler and door-to-door salesperson, at which time the police department shall investigate the nature of the complaints. The peddler and the door-to-door salesperson may use witnesses or other evidence to show his compliance with this chapter. If the city manager or his/her designee shall determine a violation on the part of the peddler or door-to-door salesperson of the ordinances of the city, he shall revoke the license of the peddler or permit of the door-to-door salesperson.
(c)
Any violations of the provisions in this article shall subject the license or permit to revocation in addition to any other penalties provided by city ordinances.
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09; Ord. No. 2017-010 , § 1, 5-1-17)
Sec. 12-84. - Appeals.
(a)
A peddler and door-to-door salesperson shall have the right to file an appeal from the ruling of the city manager or his/her designee to the mayor and council.
(b)
If any peddler and door-to-door salesperson or his company shall be dissatisfied with the decision of the mayor and council, he shall have his right to appeal as provided by the laws of the state.
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09; Ord. No. 2017-010 , § 1, 5-1-17)
Sec. 12-85. - Application.
(a)
No peddler shall be authorized to solicit until he files an application and obtains a license from the business registration division. No door-to-door salesperson shall be authorized to solicit until the door-to-door salesperson files an application and obtains a permit from the city police department. The police department shall make an investigation, including but not limited to an investigation of the police record and prior ordinance violations regulating business activities of the applicant. A copy of the application shall be furnished to the police department and business registration division. The police department, within ten (10) days, shall furnish a report to the business registration division of the police record, if any, of the applicant. No license for a peddler or permit for a door-to-door salesperson shall be issued to any applicant who shall have either a pending charge or a conviction for a crime of moral turpitude or an offense involving the elements of assault and battery, or any civil judgments involving unethical and improper business actions, including but not limited to actions which would constitute fraud and deceit under the laws of the state.
(b)
No application for a peddler license or door-to-door salesperson permit under this article shall be received or acted upon if the applicant has submitted the same or a similar application within the preceding twelve (12) months, which prior application has been dismissed, denied or abandoned. No license shall be granted if the applicant is in violation of this article or has within twelve (12) months preceding the application been in violation of this article or any other law or ordinance regulating the activities for which such license is sought.
(c)
An applicant shall pay the fee set in the schedule of fees under this chapter as cost for the investigation and regulation. This fee shall be used for the cost of such registration, investigations and regulation of persons subject to this article.
(d)
A license or permit issued under this article shall be valid for thirty (30) days from the date the license is issued. However, the license or permit may be renewed by filing a renewal application with the business registration division for the peddler's license and from the city police department for the door-to-door permit. A renewal fee for the actual expense of updating the investigative report shall be required.
(e)
All door-to-door salespersons shall wear a permit issued by the police department. The door-to-door salesperson permit issued by the police department shall be uniform as to all door-to-door salespersons, providing the following information: a photograph and the name of the door-to-door salesperson, the name of the company, and an identification number, in large enough type to be read and seen by persons with normal vision at a distance of ten (10) feet. At all times, the door-to-door salesperson shall wear on either the left or right shoulder of his or her garment the permit so that it can be readily seen by the owner or occupant of the premises.
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09; Ord. No. 2017-010 , § 1, 5-1-17)
Sec. 12-86. - Selling on streets.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale any goods, wares, merchandise, farm product or any other product or to solicit contributions or anything else of value for a purpose on the public streets in the city or upon any sidewalk of the city without first obtaining written permission from the governing body.
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09)
Sec. 12-87. - Uninvited peddlers and door to door sales.
The practice of being in and upon private residences in the city by solicitors, peddlers, hawkers, itinerant merchants, door to door salesperson, and transient vendors of merchandise, not having been requested or invited to do so by the owner or occupant of the private residence, for the purpose of soliciting orders for the sale of goods, wares and merchandise or for the purpose of disposing of and peddling or hawking the same, except in accordance with the provisions of this article, is hereby declared to be a nuisance. The police officers of the city are hereby required and directed to suppress the same and to abate any such nuisance as described in this section. Any person convicted of perpetrating a nuisance, as described and prohibited in this section, upon conviction thereof shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. No. 2009-14, 4-6-09)
