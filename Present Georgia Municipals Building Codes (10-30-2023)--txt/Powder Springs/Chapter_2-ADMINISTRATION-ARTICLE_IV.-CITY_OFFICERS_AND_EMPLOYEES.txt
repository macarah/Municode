ARTICLE IV. - CITY OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES

Sec. 2-60. - Appointment; compensation as fixed; civil service system created.
(a)
Appointment. All officers and employees who are deemed necessary by the council and who are not otherwise provided for by law or ordinance shall be appointed as provided by the terms of the Personnel Policy Manual and the Position Classification and Pay Plan for the city adopted by the mayor and council. Hiring, discipline, and dismissal shall be carried out as described in said Personnel Policy Manual. The mayor and council shall also have authority to appoint on an interim basis, persons to fill vacancies or anticipated vacancies in the follow positions: city manager, assistant city manager, directors or heads of any city department, or other positions, and to specify compensation for such interim positions as may be commensurate with the scope of duties thereof.
(b)
Compensation. The mayor and council shall have sole final authority for determining and establishing salaries and pay raises for all city employees.
(Code 1972, § 3-201; Ord. No. 87-21 § 1, 2, 7-6-87; Ord. No. 2014-023, § 5, 11-3-14 )
Editor's note— Ord. No. 87-21, adopted July 6, 1987, did not specifically amend this Code; hence, inclusion of §§ 1, 2 as § 2-60(b), with the existing provisions being designated subsection (a), was at the discretion of the editor.

  Sec. 2-61. - Surety bonds.
Except as otherwise provided by law, the council may require any department head, city official or employee, before entering upon discharge of official duties, to give good and sufficient bond in amounts decided by the council. Said bonds shall be payable to the city, and shall be conditioned on the faithful performance of the respective duties of said persons and to secure against corruption, malfeasance, misappropriations or unlawful expenditures. Said surety bonds shall be obtained from a surety company licensed to do business in the state and approved by the council. The premiums thereon shall be paid by the city. When one (1) person holds two (2) positions, only one (1) bond shall be required.
(Code 1972, §§ 3-102, 3-202)
Sec. 2-62. - Performance of duties.
It shall be a violation of this Code for any department head, official or employee to fail to perform the duties enumerated herein.
(Code 1972, § 3-103)
Sec. 2-63. - Social security—Coverage.
It is hereby declared to be the policy and purpose of this municipality to provide for all eligible employees and officials of the municipality, whether employed in connection with a governmental or proprietary function, the benefits of the system of federal old age and survivors' insurance. In pursuance of said policy, and for that purpose, the municipality shall take such action as may be required by applicable state and federal laws and regulations.
(Code 1972, § 3-203)
Sec. 2-64. - Same—Necessary agreements to be executed.
The mayor is hereby authorized and directed to execute all the necessary agreements and amendments thereto with the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia, as agent or agency, to secure coverage of employees and officials as provided in section 2-63.
(Code 1972, § 3-204)
Sec. 2-65. - Same—Withholdings from salaries or wages.
Withholdings from the salaries or wages of employees and officials for the purpose provided in section 2-63 are hereby authorized to be made in the amounts and at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws and regulations, and shall be paid over to the state or federal agency designated by said laws and regulations.
(Code 1972, § 3-205)
Sec. 2-66. - Same—Appropriations for employer's contributions.
There shall be appropriated from available funds such amounts at such times as may be required by applicable state and federal laws and regulations for employer's contributions, and the same shall be paid over to the state or federal agency designated by said laws and regulations.
(Code 1972, § 3-206)
Sec. 2-67. - Same—Records and reports.
The municipality shall keep such records and make such reports as may be required by applicable state and federal laws and regulations.
(Code 1972, § 3-207)
Sec. 2-68. - Travel and expense reimbursement policy.
The city hereby adopts a travel and expense reimbursement policy for the city.
(Ord. No. 88-31, § 1, 11-7-88)
