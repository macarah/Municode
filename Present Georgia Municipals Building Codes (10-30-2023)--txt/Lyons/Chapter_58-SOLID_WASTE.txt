ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 58-1. - Multijurisdictional solid waste management resolution adopted.
The resolution regarding the county multijurisdictional solid waste management plan, performed under the county board of commissioners, is hereby adopted by reference. At least one copy thereof is on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1977, § 18-1)
State Law reference— Local, multijurisdictional and regional solid waste plans, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-31.1.

ARTICLE II. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Sec. 58-31. - Dumping at landfill by residents only.
There shall be no dumping at the city sanitary landfill other than by city residents.
(Code 1977, § 18-2)
Sec. 58-32. - Garbage pickup fees.
The garbage pickup fees shall be as prescribed from time to time by the city council.
(Code 1977, § 18-3)
Sec. 58-33. - Placement of trash receptacles by businesses.
Receptacles for trash shall be placed at the back entrance of business houses.
(Code 1977, § 18-4)
Sec. 58-34. - Disposal of dead animals.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury, dispose of, or burn any carcass of any dead animal, such as dogs, hogs, cows, mules, or of other description, within the city upon any private property, street, alley, lane, lot or other property except at the city sanitary landfill, unless such burning is personally supervised by the chief of police or a member of the police department.
(b)
Any animal described in subsection (a) of this section that is taken, hauled or carried to the city sanitary landfill shall be buried not less than four feet deep in the ground, and the grave or hole in which such animal is buried must be covered level with the ground with earth, sand or cement. The sanitary landfill shall be the only place in which any dead animal may be taken, carried or dumped.
(Code 1977, § 18-5)
Cross reference— Animals, ch. 10.
State Law reference— Dead Animal Disposal Act, O.C.G.A. § 4-5-1 et seq.
Sec. 58-35. - Fee for debris removal from city right-of-ways.
Effective January 1, 2005, a fee of $1.50 per month will be assessed every household and business within the city. The purpose of this fee is to help defray the cost of maintaining the city's rights-of-way and the inert landfill in a neat, orderly and sanitary manner. The fee will be included in the monthly water bill as designated as a tipping fee.
(Res. of 12-7-2004)
Editor's note— A resolution of Dec. 7, 2004, added provisions to the Code, but did not specify manner of inclusion. Therefore, at the discretion of the editor, said provisions have been included herein as § 58-35.
