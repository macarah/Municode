ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Purpose of chapter and applicability of article.
modified
This chapter is enacted in furtherance of the police powers of the city council ("governing authority) of the City of Lyons, Georgia, ("city") to promote the health and general welfare of its citizens; to regulate and control the licensing and sale by the package and by the drink of beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits, and the manner of same; to establish reasonable regulations and ascertainable standards for licensees which will ensure the public peace; to protect schools, churches and residential areas from the negative secondary effects attributable to establishments that sell beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits; and to ensure that only qualified persons obtain licenses for the manufacture, sale, distribution or distribution of beer, malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits. Further, unless otherwise provided, this chapter shall apply to all types of alcoholic beverage licenses issued by the governing authority of the city.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-2. - Definitions.
modified
Except as otherwise set forth in this chapter and except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning, the following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Beer or malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Brown bag establishment means any restaurant, music hall, theater, or other establishment providing food or entertainment in the normal course of business, and in which the owners or their agents knowingly allow patrons to bring in and consume the patron's own alcoholic beverages.
Church building means as a free-standing structure, originally built for use as a church, but does not mean a commercial or retail building or space that has been converted for occupancy by a church or for religious purposes.
Code means the Code of Ordinances of the City of Lyons, Georgia.
City means the City of Lyons, Georgia, and the land within the corporate limits of Lyons, Georgia, Toombs County, Georgia.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Governing authority means the city council of the City of Lyons, Georgia.
Hotel means any lodge, inn, or similar establishment which offers overnight sleeping accommodations to registered guests for hire, and at which prepared meals or food are regularly served daily and consumed in one or more dining rooms, having an adequate and sanitary kitchen, such sleeping accommodations and dining rooms being conducted on the same premises. Hotels may grant franchises for the operation of a restaurant and lounge on their premises, and the holder of such franchise shall be included in this definition of a hotel. Lounges may be located separately from dining facilities in hotels provided there is a commonality of ownership of the restaurant and lounge.
License means the authorization granted by the governing authority of the city, Georgia to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages in a specified manner for a calendar year or the remaining portion of a calendar year.
Licensed alcoholic beverage caterer means any retail dealer who has been licensed pursuant to Article 2 of Chapter 4, Article 2 of Chapter 5, or Article 2 of Chapter 6 of Title 3, of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
Licensee means the person to whom a license is issued or, in the case of any type of partnership, limited liability company or corporation, all partners, officers, managers and directors of the partnership, limited liability company or corporation, and the use of a masculine, feminine or neutral pronoun to describe licensee will contemplate the correct licensee even if the incorrect pronoun is used.
Liter means metric measurement, currently used by the United States.
Manager means the person who supervises any operations, business, activity or location of any person licensed under this chapter.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The word "manufacturer" also means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits.
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, any brewer.
Minor means any person under the age of 21 years.
Owner means any person, partner, stockholder or member owning any percentage of an activity, business or operation.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Packaged goods retailer shall have the same meaning as provided in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-10.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, limited liability company, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private or quasi-public.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased or controlled by the licensee and used by the licensee for the purpose of operating under the license including any public sidewalk or roadway adjacent to the premises which is used by the licensee to sell or distribute alcoholic beverages.
Public club means an establishment, other than a restaurant as defined in this chapter, having the principal purpose of offering live music, dancing, theatrical productions or comedians, and at which the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises only is an attendant purpose. A public club shall not be operated or conducted in conjunction with any other type of business, trade or profession and may offer and sale only those items commonly associated with such establishments.
Public place means any place that the public is invited or permitted to go or congregate.
Purchaser means any individual, partnership, corporation, limited liability company, trust or association purchasing alcoholic beverages from a licensee.
Restaurant means any public place used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen, where the principal business is the sale of food, and that: (i) fills 75 percent of the usable square footage of its primary premises, including decks and patios which are immediately adjacent to and a part of its primary premises, with tables and chairs intended for dining, (ii) is staffed at all times while open to the public with a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook, and serve edible food to its guests, (iii) is open to the public at least four hours a day, serving at least one meal per day, (iv) serves meals at least five days a week with the exception of weeks including holidays, vacations and periods of maintenance, construction or remodeling, (v) does not have a common table or counter area that comprises more than 25 percent of its dining area, and (vi) derives at least 51 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals and food, and in calculating annual gross sales, it shall use all of its receipts, including those of persons laboring on the premises, such as all employees independent contractors, performers, servers and entertainers.
Retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only, to consumers and not for resale.
School means any school building, school grounds, college campus, educational facility or educational building owned or run by or affiliated with a state, county, city or private or religious entity, any church school, day care, or kindergarten and to any building at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state. It shall not apply to home schools.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-3. - License application and fees.
modified
(a)
Any person seeking any license permitted by this chapter shall file a written application with the city clerk, on the forms prescribed by the governing authority. Each application shall state:
(1)
The applicant's full name, birth date, present address and addresses for the past five years, business address and business addresses of employers and their addresses for the last five years, name of spouse, complete record of all arrests, convictions and guilty pleas ("guilty plea", whenever used in this chapter, shall include any plea entered under any local, state or federal ordinance or law relating to nolo contendere, first offender or conditional discharge, regardless of the nomenclature used by the local, state or federal government) for violations of any city or local ordinance and of any state or federal law and the disposition of all arrests and charges within the last five years prior to the application. As well, the applicant shall present to the city clerk originals of the following documents for copying (such copies shall be kept by the city clerk): (i) current state driver's license, (ii) birth or naturalization certificate, and (iii) two forms of valid picture identification, including, but not limited to current state driver's license, current passport, current state identification card or military identification;
(2)
Exact location of the applicant's proposed premises, including street address, zoning district and tax map and parcel number;
(3)
Name and address of the owner or landlord and/or resident manager of the applicant's proposed premises, whether or not there is a building already erected thereon;
(4)
Trade name (or proposed trade name) of the applicant's proposed business and/or premises; and
(5)
Names and addresses of all persons having any financial or beneficial interest in the applicant's proposed business or premises.
(b)
As to the manager of the applicant's proposed premises (if not the same as the applicant), the same information as required of the applicant in this section.
(c)
Each application for a license under this chapter shall be accompanied by a certified bank check for the full amount of the license fee, together with a separate certified bank check in the amount of $200.00 to defray investigative and administrative costs. If the applicant withdraws his application prior to its being submitted to the governing authority, or if the application is denied by the governing authority, the deposit representing the license fee shall be refunded, but the $200.00 paid for investigation and administrative costs shall not be refunded.
(d)
The application shall be sworn to or affirmed under oath.
(e)
Surveyor's certificate. Unless otherwise permitted by law, all new applications for each premises shall be accompanied by a current certificate from a registered surveyor and shall contain the following information about the applicant's premises:
(1)
A scale drawing of the premises, with its location identified by street address and tax map and parcel number, and showing any structures on, or to be built on, the land;
(2)
Any proposed off-street parking facilities and all outdoor lighting;
(3)
Current zoning classification of the premises; and
(4)
The distance in yards as measured by the most direct route of travel from the nearest property line of the premises to nearest property line of each of the following: any school building, school grounds, public housing authority, college campus or alcoholic treatment center which is owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government.
(f)
An application under this chapter containing known false information may result in the denial of the license applied for and prosecution as a felony.
(g)
As a prerequisite to the issuance of any initial license only, the applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints to be forwarded to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation, which shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for any instance of criminal activity during the two years immediately preceding the date of the application. The Georgia Bureau of Investigation shall also submit the fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under the rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification of records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the governing authority submitting the fingerprints.
(h)
Applications must be filed on or before November 30 of each year; otherwise, a penalty equal to 20 percent of the annual license fee shall be imposed.
(i)
Except for temporary licenses or permits that may be granted pursuant to this chapter, all licenses shall be for the full calendar year or for the number of months (or portion thereof) remaining in the calendar year. License fees shall not be prorated for any reason, and license fees shall not be refundable for any reason.
(j)
Failure to furnish complete information in the application will delay the issuance of the license. The failure to furnish complete information within 30 days after being requested to do so by the city clerk may be grounds to deny the application.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-4. - Moral character of license applicants.
modified
A license shall not be issued to any person unless it appears to the satisfaction of the governing authority that the license applicant is of good moral character. At the time an application is filed, the applicant shall, by affidavit duly sworn or affirmed, certify that he has not been convicted of or entered a guilty plea to any misdemeanor or felony involving moral turpitude, any lottery or gambling offense, or any offense related to illegal possession, distribution or sale of any illegal drug or substance (including, but not limited to marijuana or any narcotic) or liquor within five years of the filing of the application. A license shall not be issued to any applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law or entered a guilty plea to any misdemeanor or felony involving moral turpitude, any lottery or gambling offense, or any offense related to illegal possession, distribution or sale of any illegal drug or substance (including, but not limited to marijuana or any narcotic) or liquor within five years of the filing of the application.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-5. - General licensing qualifications.
modified
(a)
No applicant for a license shall be issued to any person who is not a citizen of the United States. No license for the retail sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages shall be granted to any person who is not a resident of Toombs County, Georgia, unless the applicant designates a Toombs County resident (such resident shall be an individual) who consents in writing to be responsible for any matter related to the license.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, or possess for the purpose of sale, or offer to sell any alcoholic beverage within the city, except pursuant to a license as permitted by this chapter.
(c)
A retail premises or business shall not have alcoholic beverages on its sales floor or in its cooler unless such premises or business has a license as permitted by this chapter.
(d)
Any license issued under this chapter shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on the business during the term of the license subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this chapter and state law.
(e)
(1)
A person shall apply for a license in the name of the person who owns and/or operates the licensed business, and upon approval, the license will be issued in the name of the person.
(2)
Where an entity (e.g., corporations, limited liability companies, limited partnerships, limited liability partnerships, trusts, etc.), which is duly formed and in good standing with the Secretary of State, applies for a license, the entity shall apply in the legal name of the entity, and upon approval, the license shall be issued in the name of the entity; provided that, such applicant shall attach to the application a certificate showing compliance with the annual registration requirements from the Secretary of State, corporate division. showing the name and address of a registered agent for service within the state, and providing the identity of an agent, who is involved in the active management of the business to be licensed, who shall meet the requirements of individual applicants under this chapter at the time application is made and at all times during which the license is in effect.
(3)
Where a partnership applies for a license, the partnership shall apply in the name of one of the general partners, who is a resident of this state, and upon approval, the license will be issued in the name of all partners of the partnership; if the applicant is a partner in a partnership, the requirements of this chapter shall apply to all partners at the time of application, and at all times during which the license is in effect.
(f)
No license shall be issued to any person that has not attained the age of 21 years.
(g)
No license shall be issued to any person who has had any license issued by the city revoked within the two years prior to the filing of an application as permitted by this chapter.
(h)
The governing authority may decline to issue a license to any person that has violated any part of this chapter within one year prior to the filing of the application for such license.
(i)
The governing authority may decline to issue a license where any person having any interest in or control over the licensee or licensed business or premises does not meet the same moral character requirements applicable to licensee, as set forth in this chapter.
(j)
The governing authority may decline to issue a license when after reasonable effort the city clerk or chief of police is unable to verify any statement or information required to be disclosed in the application.
(k)
Any person or entity who is issued a license shall have and continuously maintain a registered agent upon whom any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter may be served upon. The registered agent shall be a resident of Toombs County. The licensee shall file the name, address and telephone number of the registered agent, along with the written consent of such agent to receive any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter, with the city on such form as prescribed by the city clerk.
(l)
No license shall be issued to any person until he provides the city clerk with proof of current commercial general liability insurance, covering the business and/or premises seeking the license, in the form of a true and correct insurance declarations page. Such proof must be provided annually to the city clerk.
(m)
No license shall be issued to any person unless he personally appears before the governing authority at one regularly scheduled council meeting.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-6. - Each premises to have a separate license; transferability of license; fee required to change business owner, manager, partner, shareholder or business name.
modified
(a)
An applicant who will operate more than one premises shall complete a separate application for each premises and shall pay all applicable license fees for each premises.
(b)
Each license shall be personal to the licensee, and no license is transferable except as may otherwise be provided in this chapter.
(c)
In the case of the death of a licensee, the deceased licensee's license shall be the property of his heirs or personal representatives, as the case may be, and the business or premises may continue to sell alcoholic beverages for a period of 45 days from the date of death of deceased licensee or until expiration of deceased licensee's license or until issuance of a new licensee, whichever occurs first, and upon such occurrence authorization to sell under the deceased licensee's license shall terminate by operation of law.
(d)
If 50 percent or more of a licensed entity is to be sold or transferred to a person other than any existing partner, shareholder or member, then such licensed entity shall apply for a new license as set forth herein and pay all license costs and fees, if such licensed entity desires to be authorized, or continue to be authorized, to sell alcoholic beverages.
(e)
Any licensee who desires to change the location of the licensed premises within the city shall so notify the city clerk in writing 30 business days prior to any such change of location. The notice of change of location shall be accompanied by a survey as required by this chapter. A licensee's business shall not change location unless the new location complies with all provisions of this chapter. The governing authority shall have the authority to approve or deny the change of location in the same manner that it may approve or deny the issuance of a new license. An investigative fee of $200.00 shall be paid by the licensee at the time that a notice of change of location is made. If a change of location is approved, with no change of ownership of the business, the license fee paid for the old location shall be applied to the new location.
(f)
Any licensee who desires to change the name of his business or premises shall so notify the city clerk in writing at least 30 business days prior to any change of name, and the city clerk shall direct the issuance of a corrected license in replacement of the original license. A fee of $25.00 shall be paid by the licensee at the time that such notice given for the change of name of such business or premises.
(g)
Any licensee who changes the manager of his business or premises shall so notify the city clerk in writing at least three days of the effective date of notice of the change of manager and shall file with the city clerk such information about the new manager as the city clerk may require. A fee of $25.00 shall be paid at the time that the notice for the change of manager is made to defer the costs of investigation and administration.
(h)
Except as provided in this section, any change in the ownership or control of any licensed entity shall be grounds to revoke a license issued to such entity.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)

Sec. 6-8. - License may issue; sales prohibited in incomplete premises.
modified
Where a person applies for a license in connection with any premises that is not yet in existence or completed, a license may be issued for such premises; provided that, the plans for such premises comply with this chapter and the Code. No sales shall be allowed in such premises until it has been completed in accordance with such plans, a certificate of occupancy issued, and it has been inspected by the proper state and/or local officials, including, but not limited to the city manager, city building inspector, city code enforcement officer or any sworn police officer of the city, to ensure compliance with this chapter and the Code.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-9. - Expiration of license for failure to commence or operate business.
modified
(a)
All licensed premises must open for business within six months after issuance of the license. Failure to do so shall serve as a ground for revocation of such license by the governing authority, unless an extension of time is granted by the governing authority before the expiration of the six-month period, for good cause shown by the licensee. No refund of the license fee shall be made in the event of a revocation hereunder.
(b)
Any licensee who begins operating the business or premises authorized in the license, but who thereafter ceases operation for six months within any calendar year, shall be subject to revocation of his license by the governing authority, unless an extension of time is granted by the governing authority before the expiration of the six-month period, for good cause shown by the licensee. No refund of license fee shall be made in the event of a revocation hereunder.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-10. - Expiration and renewal.
modified
(a)
All licenses shall expire on December 31 of each year. A licensee who desires to renew the license shall file an application, with the requisite fees as set forth in this chapter, with the city clerk on the form provided for renewal of the license for the ensuing year. All applications for renewal will be reviewed by the governing authority or its designee. A licensee does not have a right to automatic renewal and must be compliant with this chapter when applying for the renewal of his license. Applications for renewal must be filed on or before November 30 of each year; otherwise, a penalty equal to 20 percent of the annual license fee shall be imposed. No renewal license shall be granted after December 31, but such application shall be treated as an initial application, and the applicant shall be required to comply with all rules and regulations for the granting of licenses as if no previous license had been issued to licensee.
(b)
Except for temporary permits that may be granted pursuant to this chapter, all licenses granted under this chapter shall be for the full calendar year or for the number of months (or portion thereof) remaining in the calendar year. License fees shall not be prorated for any reason, and license fees shall not be refundable for any reason.
(c)
Failure to furnish complete information in the renewal application will delay the issuance of the license. The failure to furnish complete information within 30 days after being requested to do so by the city clerk may be grounds to deny the renewal application.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-11. - Denial of application of license and suspension and revocation of license.
modified
(a)
Unless otherwise permitted by law or this chapter, no license which has been applied for shall be denied, and no license which has been issued shall be suspended or revoked, except for any ground, cause, good cause or due cause as hereinafter defined.
(b)
The terms "ground", "cause", "good cause" or "due cause" (hereafter these terms may be collectively referred to as "ground") for the purposes of this chapter shall include, but not be limited to:
(1)
Violation of any provision of this chapter;
(2)
Conviction of any felony by the licensee or any employee of licensee or any person holding an interest in the license;
(3)
Knowing falsification of any fact given in an application for a license or bearing upon the applicant's or licensee's qualification for a license;
(4)
Failure or refusal to meet or maintain any requirement or standard prescribed by this chapter as a condition to or qualification for the granting of a license;
(5)
Violation of the specified days or hours of operation or alcohol sales as set out in this chapter;
(6)
Selling or dispensing alcoholic beverages during a period of suspension;
(7)
Where the licensee's business or premises does not meet the licensing requirements of this chapter;
(8)
Littering, loitering or public consumption of alcohol in the area of the licensed business or premises;
(9)
Any other factor known to or discovered by the governing authority whereby it is shown that the licensee, any employee of licensee or any person holding any interest in a license, has engaged in conduct at or involving the licensed business or premises, or has permitted conduct on the licensed premises, that constitutes a violation of federal or state law, local ordinance or administrative regulations involving alcoholic beverages, gambling or narcotics. With respect to this subsection, it may be inferred that the violation was done with the knowledge or consent of the licensee; provided that such inference may be rebutted; and
(10)
A licensed premises fails to open for operation or business within six months after the granting of the license.
(c)
The governing authority shall provide a written statement of ground(s) to the applicant or licensee, as the case may be, and the governing authority shall provide such statement by personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the applicant or licensee, or the registered agent of the licensee. Within ten days of the giving of such statement, the applicant or licensee shall have the right to request a hearing on the statement, and the applicant or licensee shall do so by filing a written hearing request with the city clerk. Upon receipt by the city clerk of the hearing request, a hearing shall be scheduled within 20 days, and the applicant or licensee, or the registered agent of the licensee, shall be notified in writing, at least five days before such hearing, of the date, time, and place of the hearing (the governing authority's notice of hearing of hearing shall be served by personal service or certified mail, return receipt requested, to the applicant or license, of the registered agent of the licensee). The hearing shall be before the governing authority, and at the hearing the applicant or licensee shall have the right to appear in person and by attorney and to have witnesses appear on the applicant's or licensee's behalf and to cross examine opposing witnesses and present evidence. The witnesses shall testify under oath, but rules of evidence shall not apply.
(d)
In the event that the governing authority determines that there exists any ground for the denial of an application, the governing authority shall deny such application.
(e)
In the event that the governing authority determines that there exists any ground for the suspension of a license, a period of license suspension shall be imposed by the governing authority for a term not to exceed 30 days.
(f)
In the event that the governing authority determines that there exists any ground for the revocation of a license, the revocation of a license by the governing authority shall be for the remaining calendar year of the year in which the revocation is imposed.
(g)
The governing authority's decision regarding the denial of an application or the suspension or revocation of a license shall be personally served or mailed by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the applicant or licensee, or the registered agent of the licensee, within five business days of the close of the hearing. The decision of the governing authority shall constitute final action by the governing authority, subject to review upon petition for certiorari to the Superior Court of Toombs County. Georgia, unless another manner of appeal is required by Georgia law.
(h)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, upon the licensee's receipt of any statement of grounds, the licensee may waive the right to a hearing and consent to any sanction permitted herein, or as otherwise may be permitted by the Code. Any consent entered under this chapter shall be in the form of a written decree, executed by the licensee and the governing authority, and such decree shall be considered final action and non-appealable.
(i)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, upon the revocation of a licensee's state alcohol or alcoholic beverage license, the licensee's license issued under this chapter shall be immediately revoked by operation of law. The chief of police of the city or his sworn officers, upon notice of such revocation, shall take the necessary steps to ensure the immediate cessation of the licensee's sale of alcoholic beverages.
(j)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, the governing authority may exercise any other remedy at law or equity to enjoin a license or licensee, temporarily or permanently, in connection with any exigency that arises involving the licensed premises or location, where the governing authority finds that action is required for the protection of the public health, safety, or welfare of citizens of the city.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-12. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
modified
In addition to any other punishment or penalty set forth in this chapter, unless prohibited by law, any person who violates any provision of this chapter may be cited for violating same and tried in the municipal court of the city, and upon conviction for any such violation, said court shall be authorized to punish such person by a fine not exceeding $300.00 or imprisonment for a term not exceeding 30 days or any combination thereof. Further, with respect to violations of this chapter that are continuous with respect to time, each day the violation continues is a separate offense, and with respect to violations of this chapter that are not continuous with respect to time, each act constitutes a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-13. - Inspection of licensed business or premises by the police department.
modified
The city manager, code enforcement officers, building inspectors and sworn police officers of the city shall have the authority to inspect any business or premises licensed under this chapter during the hours in which the business or premises is open for business. These inspections shall be made for the purpose of verifying compliance with the requirements of this chapter and state law. This section is not intended to limit the authority of any other city officer to conduct inspections authorized by other provisions of this Code or state law.
(Ord. No. 1698, § I, 5-3-2022)
Sec. 6-14. - License and certain code provisions to be posted.
modified
(a)

new
(a)
Any person desiring to operate as an alcoholic beverage caterer, engaging in the retail sale and/or serving of alcoholic beverages for consumption by the drink, at a location within the city and in connection with a specific, catering event permit (as hereinafter provided), shall, prior to engaging in such activity, apply with the governing authority or its designee for an alcoholic beverage caterer license and pay all fees and/or costs associated therewith.
