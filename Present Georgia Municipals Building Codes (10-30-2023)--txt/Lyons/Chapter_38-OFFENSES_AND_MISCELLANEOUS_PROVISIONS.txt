ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Drinking in public.
No person shall drink any alcoholic beverage in streets, alleys, parks, motor vehicles, or any public place or place where the public is invited, except such places as are licensed to pour alcoholic beverages.
(Code 1977, § 14-10)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 6.
Sec. 38-2. - Possession of marijuana.
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess one ounce or less of marijuana within the corporate limits of the City of Lyons. Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $300.00, or where the court finds that a such person is without the financial means to pay a fine, the court shall direct such person to perform a term of community service, not to exceed 40 hours, which must be completed within 60 days of the imposition of sentence.
(Code 1977, § 14-6; Ord. No. 1681, § I, 3-2-2021)
State Law reference— Drug offenses, O.C.G.A. § 16-13-2 et seq.; jurisdiction of municipal court over marijuana cases, O.C.G.A. § 36-32-6.

ARTICLE II. - OFFENSES INVOLVING PUBLIC SAFETY

Sec. 38-31. - Turkey shoots.
Turkey shoots are prohibited within the city.
(Code 1977, § 14-3)
Sec. 38-32. - Loitering.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be found within any public or private building, after being requested by the owner or person in charge thereof to leave.
(Code 1977, § 14-5)
Secs. 38-33—38-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - OFFENSES INVOLVING PUBLIC PEACE AND ORDER

Sec. 38-61. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
Scope of violation. Any person who shall use loud, boisterous or obscene language or who shall fight, quarrel or make any unnecessary noise or do any other thing or act within the corporate city limits calculated to disturb the peace, quiet and good order of the city or of any person within the city limits shall be guilty of the offense of disorderly conduct.
(b)
Defense to charge. It shall be a full defense to any charge under this section that the offense charged was committed in the reasonable defense of person, family, habitation or property against unprovoked or unlawful attack, assault or trespass; but for a mutual consent to fight, both persons shall be guilty of the offense.
(c)
Fine for disorderly fighting. The fine for disorderly fighting is $200.00 for the first offense.
(Code 1977, § 14-9)
Sec. 38-62. - Congregating to obstruct streets.
It shall be unlawful for any person to occupy the streets or sidewalks or for a number of persons to congregate in such numbers on the city sidewalks or streets so as to obstruct the sidewalks or streets.
(Code 1977, § 14-2)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 62.
State Law reference— Obstructing highways, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-43; unlawful assembly, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-33.
Sec. 38-63. - Hunting inside city limits.
No hunting or discharging of firearms shall be allowed within the city limits.
(Code 1977, § 14-11)
Sec. 38-64. - Indecent exposure.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to appear in any public place or in view of the public wearing pants or shorts below the waist, which exposes the skin or undergarments.
(b)
Any person violating the provision of this section shall upon conviction thereof be fined not less than $50.00 and not more than $250.00 and be ordered to perform not less than 20 hours or more than 80 hours of community service.
(Mo. of 8-2-2011)
Secs. 38-65—38-80. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - OFFENSES INVOLVING PUBLIC HEALTH

Sec. 38-81. - Use of tobacco products on city property prohibited.
(a)
Unlawful use of tobacco products. It shall be unlawful for any person to use any form of tobacco, including, but not limited to cigarettes, E-cigarettes, cigars, tobacco pipes, chewing tobacco, and any form of smokeless tobacco at, in or on any indoor or outdoor facilities owned or operated by the City of Lyons, including, but not limited to all public buildings, city-owned vehicles and equipment, real property, parking lots, restrooms, parks, athletic fields, and walking/hiking trails leased, owned or maintained by the city.
(b)
Signage. The city shall cause appropriate signs to be posted sufficient to inform the public and all city employees of the prohibition on the use of tobacco products as set forth in subsection (a).
(c)
Enforcement. City staff[,] city police and code enforcement officers shall make periodic inspection of all city property and facilities to monitor for compliance with this section of the Code.
(1)
Employees of the city that knowingly use tobacco products in violation of this section shall be subject to disciplinary action. Repeated violations of this section by employees of the city may result in disciplinary action, including, but not limited to written reprimand, suspension from employment without pay, or dismissal for cause.
(2)
Any other person(s) found in violation of the prohibition on the use of tobacco products set forth in this section shall be entitled to one verbal warning by a city police or code enforcement officer a staff member of the city prior to enforcement of this section by issuance of a citation by a law enforcement officer[,] city police or code enforcement officer.
(3)
The minimum fine for the first conviction of violating this section within 12 months shall be $50.00. Subsequent violations within the same 12-month period by the same individual shall double for each violation up to a maximum of $800.00.
(4)
The city recommends the Georgia Tobacco Quit Line (1-800-270-7867), a toll free resource operated by the Georgia Cancer Coalition, for those members of the public and employees of the city that are attempting to quit tobacco use. More information is available at www.georgiacancer.org/prev-quitline.php.
(Ord. of 3-4-2014)
