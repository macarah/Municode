Chapter 50 - MANUFACTURED HOMES AND TRAILERS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Recreational vehicles at nursing homes or convalescent centers.
The provisions of this chapter shall not apply to the parking of recreational vehicles on property owned and controlled by a nursing home or convalescent home where the following conditions are met:
(1)
The area where such recreational vehicles are to be parked is paved with asphalt or other suitable paving material and is not larger than 15,000 square feet.
(2)
The area where the recreational vehicles are to be parked is not located adjacent to a city street or state highway but is located at least 500 feet from the right-of-way of any city street or state highway.
(3)
No occupant of any recreational vehicle may reside at such location for more than 30 consecutive days and that there are no more than ten recreational vehicles parked at such location at any one time.
(4)
The owner of the nursing home or convalescent home shall be authorized to establish electrical connections and running water connections to the area set aside for the parking of such recreational vehicles, but such electrical connections and water connections shall, in all respects comply with the other provisions of this Code.
(5)
Under no circumstances shall any sewage from such recreational vehicles be disposed of in the city's sewerage system, but the owner of the nursing home or convalescent home shall be responsible for ensuring that all such sewage is disposed of properly in appropriate locations.
(6)
The owner or operator of any recreational vehicle parked in accord with the terms of this section shall not be required to pay any rent or lease for the space utilized by such vehicle; nor shall such owner or operator be charged for the use of any electricity or water at such location.
(7)
Notwithstanding the foregoing, nothing herein shall prohibit the owner of a recreational vehicle who resides at a nursing home or convalescent center from parking his recreational vehicle at such location during such times and for such lengths of time as he or she may deem appropriate, and the nursing home or convalescent center shall be authorized to permit the owners of such recreational vehicles to connect to the electrical and water connections at such parking area.
(Code 1986, § 21-16; Ord. No. O-97-02-03, 2-20-1997)

ARTICLE II. - TRAILER CAMPS

Sec. 50-21. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Automobile trailer, trailer coach, or trailer means any vehicle or structure so designed and constructed in such manner as will permit occupancy thereof as sleeping quarters for one or more persons, for the conduct of any business or profession, occupancy or tract, or use as a selling of advertising device, and so designed that it is or may be mounted on wheels and used as a conveyance on highways or city streets, propelled or drawn by its own or other motor power, excepting a device used exclusively upon stationary rails or tracks.
Trailer camp means any park, trailer park, trailer court, court, camp, site, lot, parcel, or tract of land designed, maintained or intended for the purposes of supplying a location or accommodations for any trailer coach or trailer coaches and upon which any trailer coach or trailer coaches are parked and shall include all buildings used or intended for use a part of the equipment thereof whether a charge is made for the use of the trailer camp and its facilities or not. The term "trailer camp" shall not include automobile or trailer sales lots on which unoccupied trailers are parked for purposes of inspection and sale.
Unit means a section of ground in a trailer camp of not less than 1,000 square feet of unoccupied space in an area designated as the location for only one automobile and one trailer.
(Code 1986, § 21-1; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-22. - License.
(a)
Required. It shall be unlawful for any person to establish, operate or maintain, or permit to be established, operated or maintained upon any property owned or controlled by him, a trailer camp within the limits of the city without having first secured a license therefor. Such license shall expire one year from the date of issuance but may be renewed under the provisions of this chapter for additional periods of one year.
(b)
Application. The application for such license or the renewal thereof shall be filed with the finance director, there will be no such fee charged for this license, for each unit in the existing or proposed camp. The application for a license or renewal thereof shall be made on such printed forms or other documents as may be furnished by the finance director and shall include the name and address of the owner of the tract and such a legal description of the premises, upon which the trailer camp is or will be located as will readily identify and definitely locate the premises. The application shall be accompanied by four copies of the camp plan showing the following, either existing or as proposed:
(1)
The extent and area to be used for camp purposes.
(2)
Roadways and driveways.
(3)
Location of sites or units for trailer coaches.
(4)
Location and number of sanitary conveniences, including toilets, washrooms, laundries and utility rooms to be used by occupants of units.
(5)
Method and plan for sewage disposal.
(6)
Method and plan for garbage removal.
(7)
Plan for water supply.
(8)
Plan for electrical lighting of the units.
(c)
Recommendation; procedure. Before such license may be issued, there must be a favorable recommendation by the majority of the planning commission and the plan and premises must be inspected and approved by the chief of the fire department and the county health department. Upon obtaining the recommendation of the planning commission, the chief of the fire department, and the county health department, the license application shall be transmitted to the city council for public hearing. Such public hearing shall be conducted in the same manner as public hearings are conducted for rezoning or amendments to the zoning chapter, with the additional requirement that the city council shall consider and determine whether or not the proposed camp complies with the requirements contained in this chapter. After initial approval of the application and the granting of the license by the city council, the city council shall not be required to have a public hearing on the renewal of such license in subsequent years unless so requested by the planning commission, the chief of the fire department, or the county health department, or where a license previously issued shall have expired prior to the renewal thereof.
(d)
Determination. Upon the conclusion of the public hearing, the city council shall determine whether or not to grant the license to the applicant.
(e)
Limitations. Licenses issued under the terms of this chapter convey no right to erect any building, to do any plumbing work or to do any electrical work.
(Code 1986, § 21-2; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-23. - Zoning.
(a)
No trailer camp shall be located in any zone within the city limits other than an R-4 MH residential district, as amended.
(b)
No trailer or tourist camp shall be located within the limits of the city unless city water and sewer connections and fire protection facilities are available.
(c)
No occupied trailer or any other building shall be located within the city limits within the recognized setback line for the zoning district in which such trailer is located nor less than ten feet from any other building or vehicle or the boundary line of the tract on which located.
(d)
No trailer camp shall be licensed or operated in conjunction with or as part of a mobile home park or as a part of a mobile home subdivision.
(Code 1986, § 21-3; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-24. - Plan for stormwater, driveways, electricity, etc., required.
(a)
Every trailer camp shall be located on a well-drained area, and the premises shall be properly graded so as to prevent the accumulation of stormwater or other waters.
(b)
Units shall be clearly designated and the camp so arranged so that all units shall face or abut on a driveway of not less than 16 feet in width, giving easy access from all units to a public street. Such driveway shall be maintained in good condition having natural drainage into a city street catchbasin, be well lighted at night, and shall not be obstructed.
(c)
The camp shall be laid out so that no trailer shall be located farther than 200 feet from the toilets and service buildings provided for herein, and walkways to such buildings shall be well lighted at night.
(d)
Every trailer unit shall be furnished with an electric service outlet. Such outlet shall be equipped with an externally operated switch or fuse of not less than 30 amperes capacity, and heavy duty outlet receptacle.
(Code 1986, § 21-4; Ord. No. O-90-07-18, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-25. - City water required; adequate faucets and hot water.
(a)
An adequate supply of pure water, furnished through a pipe distribution system connected directly with the city water main, with supply faucets located not more than 200 feet from any trailer shall be furnished for drinking and domestic purposes.
(b)
No common drinking vessels shall be permitted, nor shall any drinking water faucets be placed in any toilet room.
(c)
An abundant supply of hot water shall be provided at all times for bathing, washing and laundry facilities.
(Code 1986, § 21-5; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-26. - Service building requirements; accommodations based on plan.
(a)
Every trailer camp shall have erected thereon, at a distance not greater than 200 feet from any unit it is designed to serve, a suitable building for housing toilets, showers, and laundry facilities as required by this article.
(b)
There shall be provided separate toilet rooms for each sex. Flush toilets provided with an adequate water supply shall be enclosed in separate compartments and shall be provided for each sex in the ratio of one toilet for each eight units.
(c)
Separate bathing facilities for each sex shall be provided with one shower for each eight units.
(d)
Laundry facilities shall be provided.
(e)
The accommodations, specified in this section, shall be based on the total camp capacity according to its accepted plans.
(Code 1986, § 21-6; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-27. - Connection to city sewer system required; garbage collection by city services.
(a)
All waste from showers, toilets, laundries, faucets and lavatories shall be wasted into a sewer system extended from and connected with the city sewer system.
(b)
All sanitary facilities in any trailer that are not connected with the city sewer system by means of rigid pipe connections shall be sealed and their use is hereby declared unlawful.
(c)
Each faucet site shall be equipped with facilities for drainage of waste and excess water into the sewer. In no case shall any wastewater be thrown or discharged upon the surface of the ground or disposed of by means other than is herein provided.
(d)
Every unit shall be provided with a wheeled container supplied by the city for garbage depository from which the contents shall be removed by the city garbage collection services.
(Code 1986, § 21-7; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-28. - Length of stay; number of occupants.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to remain or live in any trailer or tourist camp for more than 90 days in each 12-month period, unless an exception to this requirement is approved by the city manager of the city, or his designee, and such exception may be approved for good cause only.
(b)
No trailer may be inhabited by a greater number of occupants than that for which it was designed.
(Code 1986, § 21-8; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 50-29. - Management.
At all times, the management (licensee) shall:
(1)
Keep a register of all guests, showing for such guests:
a.
Names and addresses.
b.
Dates of entrance and departure.
c.
License numbers of all trailers and towing or other automobiles.
d.
States issuing such licenses.
e.
Place of last location and length of stay.
(2)
Maintain the camp in a clean, orderly and sanitary condition at all times.
(3)
See that the provisions of this chapter are complied with and enforced and report promptly to the proper authorities any violations of this chapter or any other violations of law which may come to their attention.
(4)
Prevent the running loose of dogs, cats or other animals or pets.
(5)
Maintain in convenient places, approved by the chief of the fire department, hand fire extinguishers in the ratio of one to each eight units.
(6)
Prohibit the lighting of open fires on the premises.
(7)
Prohibit the use of any trailer by a greater number of occupants than that which it is designed to accommodate.
(8)
Report to the county health department all cases of persons or animals infected or suspected of being infected with any communicable disease.
(Code 1986, § 21-9; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-30. - Compliance with plumbing, electrical and building ordinances.
All plumbing, electrical, building and other work on or at any camp licensed under this chapter shall be in accord with the ordinances of the city regulating such work unless such ordinances are specifically made inapplicable under the terms of this chapter.
(Code 1986, § 21-10; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-31. - Parking restricted.
(a)
It shall be unlawful within the city limits for any person to park any trailer on any street, alley or highway, or other public place, or on any tract of land owned by any person, occupied or unoccupied, within the city, except as provided in this chapter.
(b)
Emergency or temporary stopping or parking is permitted on any street, alley or highway for not longer than one hour, subject to any other and further prohibitions, regulations, or limitations imposed by the traffic and parking regulations or ordinances of that street, alley or highway.
(c)
No person shall park or occupy any trailer on the premises or any occupied dwelling or on any lot which is not part of the premises of any occupied dwelling, either of which is situated outside an approved trailer camp; except, the parking of only one unoccupied trailer in an accessory private garage building, or in a rear yard in any district, is permitted, provided no living quarters shall be maintained or any business practiced in said trailer while such trailer is so parked or stored.
(Code 1986, § 21-11; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
Sec. 50-32. - Revocation.
The planning commission is hereby authorized to revoke any license issued pursuant to the terms of this article if after due investigation they determine that the holder thereof has violated any of the provisions of this article or that any trailer or trailer camp is being maintained in an unsanitary or unsafe manner or is a nuisance.
(Code 1986, § 21-12; Ord. No. O-90-07-19, 7-23-1990)
