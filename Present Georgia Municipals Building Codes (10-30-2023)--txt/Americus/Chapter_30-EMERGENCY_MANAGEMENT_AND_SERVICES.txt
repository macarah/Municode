Chapter 30 - EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT AND SERVICES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - False alarms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to turn in an alarm of fire except in the case of actual or threatened fire.
(b)
The term "accidental false alarm" shall be defined as the transmission of an audible or electronic message or signal by a residence or business or is caused by accidental activation by the owner, resident, manager, or employee of a residence or business, is caused by a malfunction or other reason where the responding public safety personnel find no evidence of entry, attempted entry, or indication of smoke or a fire. Personal security devices activated manually, weather-related activations due to severe weather, and power outages resulting in the transmission of false alarms shall not be considered accidental false alarms for the purpose of this section.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for a person or business to allow, cause, or fail to repair or maintain any alarm which transmits a false alarm more than three times in a single calendar month. Such false alarms shall be deemed a nuisance alarm.
(d)
A penalty of $25.00 shall be assessed for each nuisance alarm transmitted after the third nuisance alarm in any calendar month. A penalty of $25.00 shall be assessed for each accidental false alarm after the third such accidental false alarm in any calendar month. Such fines may be assessed by the municipal court upon the issuance of a citation therefor and a finding of guilt by said court.
(Code 1962, § 9-6; Code 1986, § 11-3; Ord. No. O-02-03-08, 3-21-2002)
State Law reference— False alarms, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-27.

Sec. 30-21. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Agreement means the emergency and disaster mutual aid agreement with Sumter County Georgia.
Assisting city or county means the participating city or county furnishing equipment, services, personnel, or other aid pursuant to this chapter.
Curfew means a regulation requiring the withdrawal from any person not otherwise exempt from this section from appearing in certain public areas during specified hours.
Disaster means any natural, technological, civil emergency or threat thereof that causes damage or has the potential to cause damage of sufficient severity and magnitude to result in a declaration of a state of emergency declared by a county, the governor, or the President of the United States.
EMA means emergency management agency.
Emergency means any occurrence, or threat thereof, whether natural, or caused by man, in war or in peace, which results or may result in substantial injury or harm to the population or substantial damage to or loss of property.
Emergency management means the preparation for the carrying out of all emergency functions other than functions for which military forces are primarily responsible to prevent, minimize, and repair injury and damage resulting from emergencies, energy emergencies, disasters, or the imminent threat thereof, of manmade or natural origin caused by enemy attack, sabotage, acts of domestic or international terrorism, civil disturbance, fire, flood, earthquake, wind, storm, wave action, oil spill or other water contamination requiring emergency action to avert danger or damage, epidemic, pandemic, wide-spread health emergencies, air contamination, blight, drought, infestation, explosion, riot or other hostile action, radiological action, or other causes. These functions include, without limitation, firefighting services, police services, emergency medical services, rescue, engineering, warning services, communications, defense from radiological, chemical, biological, and other special weapons to include weapons of mass destruction, evacuation of persons from stricken areas, emergency welfare services, consequence management functions to include victim services, emergency transportation, plant protection, temporary restoration of public utility services, and other functions related to civilian protection, together with all other activities necessary or incidental to the preparation for and carrying out of the foregoing functions.
Emergency management director (EMA director) means the person appointed by Sumter County to implement the emergency plan of Sumter County and the municipalities within Sumter County.
Employees means paid, volunteer, and auxiliary employees and emergency management workers.
Exempt individuals, unless otherwise specified in the resolution implementing the curfew, means those individuals engaged in the provision of designated essential services, such as fire, law enforcement, emergency medical services and hospital services, military services, and utility emergency repairs. The resolution may, in the discretion of the board of commissioners, also exempt regular employees of local industries traveling to and from their jobs with appropriate identification, news media employees, building and repair contractors performing activities related to construction, repair, renovation or improvement of buildings and other structures damaged during the disaster or emergency.
Participating city or county means a city or county that has duly enacted this chapter.
Receiving city or county means the participating city or county requesting equipment, services, personnel, or other aid pursuant to this chapter.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-22. - Mutual aid agreement and emergency management plan.
The city has entered into a mutual aid agreement with the county.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-23. - Creation; membership; office.
An emergency management agency is created in and for the city. The office of the emergency management agency shall be at city hall.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-24. - Emergency management director.
The mayor and city council shall appoint a director of emergency management for the city, who shall be the liaison between the city and the county director of emergency management. The director of emergency management for the city shall have the following duties:
(1)
To represent the governing officials of the city on matters pertaining to emergency management;
(2)
To assist city officials in organizing city departments for emergency operations;
(3)
To assist in developing in conjunction with the county emergency management director, the plan for emergency functions for the county emergency organization;
(4)
To render reports as required by governing officials;
(5)
Maintaining, with authority of city's governing officials, the facility used as the city as an emergency operations center;
(6)
Coordinate the activities of the city emergency operating center staff during periods of an emergency, and under the supervision of the mayor.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-25. - Emergency powers.
Upon a manmade or natural disaster, actual enemy attack upon the United States, or any other emergency as defined in this article that may affect the lives and property of the citizens of the city, the mayor and city council may determine that an emergency or disaster exists and thereafter shall have and may exercise for such period as such emergency or disaster exists or continues, the following powers:
(1)
To enforce all rules, laws and regulations relating to emergency management and to assume direct operational control over all emergency management forces operating at the direction of the city;
(2)
To seize, take for temporary use of, or condemn any property for the protection of the public;
(3)
To sell, lend, give, or distribute all or any such property or supplies among the inhabitants of the city, to maintain a strict accounting of property or supplies distributed and for funds received for such property or supplies;
(4)
To perform and exercise such other functions and duties, and take such emergency actions as may be necessary to promote and secure the safety, protection and well-being of the inhabitants of the city.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-26. - Volunteers.
All persons, other than officers and employees of the city, performing emergency functions pursuant to this article, who are volunteers providing assistance during the course of an emergency shall serve with or without compensation. While engaged in such emergency functions, volunteers shall have the same immunities as city officers and employees.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-27. - Overcharging prohibited.
(a)
Prohibition. In order to preserve, protect, or sustain the life, health, or safety of persons or their property, it shall be unlawful, during the duration of a state of emergency or subsequent recovery period in which any portion of the city has been designated as a disaster area by the governor of the state or by the governing body of the city, for any person, firm, or corporation located or doing business in the city to overcharge for any goods, material, services, or housing sold within the geographic limits of the city.
(b)
Definitions.
(1)
Overcharging. The term "overcharging" is defined as charging prices for goods, materials, services, or housing which are substantially in excess of the customary charges or in applicable cases substantially in excess of the supplier's or provider's costs for such goods, materials, services, or housing. The existence of overcharging shall be presumed from a substantial increase in the price at which the goods, materials, services, or housing was offered in the usual course of business immediately prior to the onset of the emergency, but shall not include increases in costs to the supplier directly attributable to higher costs of materials, supplies, and labor costs resulting from the emergency.
(2)
State of emergency. The term "state of emergency" is defined, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 38-3-3(5), as a condition declared by the governor when, in his judgment, the threat or actual occurrence of a disaster or emergency is of sufficient severity and magnitude as to warrant extraordinary efforts in preventing or alleviating the damage, loss, hardship, or suffering threatened or caused thereby. Such "state of emergency" may also be declared by the governing body of the city whenever a disaster or emergency is of sufficient severity and magnitude as to warrant extraordinary efforts in preventing or alleviating the damage, loss, hardship, or suffering threatened or caused thereby.
(3)
Subsequent recovery period. The term "subsequent recovery period" is defined as that period during which the disaster continues to cause disruptions in the disaster area, but shall not exceed six months after the emergency declaration has been terminated by the governor unless extended by official action of the governing authority of the city.
(c)
Penalties. Violation of the provisions of this section upon conviction shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 per violation or imprisonment not to exceed 60 days, or both such fine and imprisonment. Each sale shall constitute a separate offense.
(d)
Severability. If any section, paragraph, sentence, clause, phrase, or word of this section is for any reason held by the court to be unconstitutional, inoperative, or void, such holding shall not affect the remainder of this section.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020; Ord. No. O-2021-14, 8-19-2021)
Sec. 30-28. - Unlawful acts during emergencies.
It shall be unlawful for any person to commit any of the following acts during an emergency:
(1)
To willfully obstruct, hinder or delay any member of the emergency management corps in the enforcement of any lawful rule or regulation issued pursuant to this article or in the performance of any duty imposed by virtue of this article;
(2)
To do any act forbidden by any lawful rules or regulations issued pursuant to this article, if such act is of such a nature as to give assistance to the enemy, imperil the life or property of any inhabitant of the city, or prevent, hinder or delay the defense or protection of the city; and
(3)
To wear, carry or display, without authority, any mark or identification specified by the emergency management agency.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-29. - Meetings of mayor and city council.
(a)
Upon proclamation by the appropriate federal or state official of an emergency or disaster of manmade or natural causes or enemy attack impending on or affecting the state or the United States, the affairs and business of the city may be conducted at places other than the regular or usual place thereof, within or outside of the city, when it is not prudent, expedient or possible to conduct business at the regular location. When such meetings of the mayor and city council, or any committee thereof, occur outside of the city, all actions taken by the mayor and city council shall be as valid and binding as if performed within the city. The powers and functions of the governing body of the city may be exercised in the light of the exigencies of the emergency situation without regard to or compliance with time-consuming procedures and formalities otherwise required by law.
(b)
Such emergencies shall be considered to be "special circumstances" if so declared by the mayor and city council, and meetings of the mayor and city council, or any committee thereof, maybe be held with less than 24 hours' notice if such meeting is called and held in accord with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d)(3), relating to the holding of meetings under special circumstances so declared by the mayor and city council.
(c)
Any such meeting of the mayor and city council, or any committee, held during such emergency may be held be teleconference or other similar procedure in compliance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(g).
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-30. - Purchasing and public works contracts.
Upon the declaration of a state of emergency by the governor, or upon the determination by the mayor and city council, or its designee, of the existence of an emergency or disaster, the mayor and city council, its designee or the emergency interim successor, may contract for public works without letting such contract out to the lowest, responsible bidder and without advertising and posting notification of such contract for four weeks; however, any public works contract entered into pursuant to this section shall be entered on the official minutes of the city as soon as practical and the nature of the emergency described therein.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-31. - Code enforcement.
Upon the declaration of a state of emergency by the governor, or upon the determination by the mayor and city council, or its designee, of the existence of an emergency or disaster, the mayor and city council, its designee or the emergency interim successor, may temporarily suspend the enforcement of this Code, or any portion thereof, if:
(1)
The emergency or disaster is of such nature that immediate action outside the Code is required;
(2)
The suspension is consistent with the protection of the public health, safety and welfare; and
(3)
The suspension is not inconsistent with any federal or state statutes or regulations.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-32. - Curfew.
(a)
Upon the declaration of a state of emergency by the governor, or upon the determination by the mayor and city council, or its designee, of the existence of an emergency or disaster, the mayor and city council, its designee or the emergency interim successor, may adopt a resolution instituting a curfew when it is determined necessary to protect and safeguard persons and the property within the corporate limits of the city.
(b)
All of the territory of the incorporated city shall be subject to the terms of the curfew, unless otherwise specified in the resolution.
(c)
The resolution instituting the curfew shall include the dates and hours that the curfew shall be in effect.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-33. - Prohibition of appearance in public places.
It shall be prohibited for any person, other than exempt individuals, to appear in public in the area of the city subject to the curfew including, but not limited to, streets, highways, alleys, sidewalks, vacant lots, parks, public buildings or any other public places in all or a delineated part of the incorporated city during the stated hours of the curfew.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
Sec. 30-34. - City operations.
To promote civil stability and to provide basic services to the city in the case of emergencies or hazardous inclement weather, it is essential that the city be able to maintain an adequate workforce. The following provisions shall guide the conduct of city employees in the event of an emergency or a hazardous inclement weather situation.
(1)
Essential employees. All essential employees engaged in the protection of citizens and provision of emergency services shall be required to report to work at their regular shift unless called in early by their immediate supervisor, the mayor, the city manager or the emergency management director. Essential officers and employees are defined as the city manager, EMA director, all police personnel, all fire and EMS personnel, dispatchers, emergency medical service providers, director of public works, building inspector, and all code enforcement personnel.
(2)
Nonessential persons. The mayor and the city manager, in consultation with the EMA director, the police chief, the fire chief, and such other personnel as the mayor and city council may designate, shall decide whether to open city hall and other city work facilities for business. It shall be their responsibility to notify city officers and employees if the city's offices are to be closed. If the city's offices are open, all employees in all offices are expected to report for their assigned shift. If the city's offices are not open for business during the regular hours of operation, all employees are required to check in with city hall periodically as determined by each employee's department head for updates. Employees will not be paid for regular hours that the city's offices are not in operation.
(3)
Employee pay. Any employee not able to report for his assigned shift may take annual leave for those hours. If an employee reports for work and is then sent home by the closing of the city's offices, the employee will be paid for the remainder of the day. The mayor and the city manager shall make the decision to close the city's offices.
(Ord. No. O-2020-6, 3-19-2020)
