DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 90-365. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ashes means and includes the waste product from coal, wood or other fuels used for cooking and heating.
Brown goods includes such items as chairs, mattresses, sofas, tables and other furniture items.
Building refuse means solid waste generated from construction or demolition activities undertaken on property located within the corporate limits of the city. The term "building refuse" includes, but is not limited to, lumber, brick, block, stone, carpet, plumbing materials, plaster, concrete, roofing, floor tile, gutters, and other substances accumulated as a result of construction of or repairs or additions to structures or accessory structures or the demolition thereof.
Bulk waste means appliances, furniture, bedding material, automobile parts, mechanical equipment, mechanical parts and carpet.
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses, and other nonmanufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes.
Composting means the controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable, odor-free humus.
Disposable trash containers means plastic bags and cardboard, wooden and corrugated boxes which shall be nonreturnable and shall be collected and packed into the garbage truck collecting such items.
Disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of solid waste occurs and includes, but is not limited to, landfilling and solid waste thermal treatment technology facilities.
Dump means to throw, discard, place, deposit, discharge, bury, burn or dispose of a substance.
Front-loading container means a heavy-duty steel container designed for use in commercial, industrial, and apartment units for storage of garbage, miscellaneous rubbish, trash and other waste.
Garbage means all vegetable and animal matter, food and other refuse from a kitchen, market, store, or elsewhere, including all matter that decays, attracts flies, and creates disagreeable odors.
Hazardous waste means solid or liquid waste material resulting from the manufacture or use of chemicals, pesticides and drugs, other than normal household use, pathological waste, highly flammable, explosive waste, toxic waste, sewer sludge, insecticides, poisons, corrosives, caustics, acids, infectious materials, compressed gases, biological materials, radioactive materials, petroleum products and other waste material that is determined to be a hazard to public health, safety or environment.
Industrial waste means solid waste from generated by factories, processing plants, wholesale establishments, assembling plants, food processing wastes, cinders and ashes, lumber scraps, sawdust, shavings, including solids, semisolids, sludges, and liquids floor sweepings, metal scrap and shavings, and any other waste products from industrial and manufacturing processes.
Leachate collection system means a system at a landfill for collection of the leachate, which may percolate through the waste and into the soils surrounding the landfill.
Litter means all discarded manmade materials, including, but not limited to, garbage, lottery tickets, household trash, industrial waste, building materials, appliances, or portions thereof, refuse, waste materials, sand, gravel, slag, brick bats, rubbish, tin cans, cigarette and cigar butts, other tobacco products, rags, cloths, newspapers, aluminum cans, trash, debris, dead animals, or any other discarded, used or unconsumed substance which is not handled in accordance with the provisions of this article.
Multiple residential unit means any duplex, dwelling place or apartment, or group of apartments under a single roof, or in a single apartment complex, used for a dwelling place for more than a one-family unit and which is served by a single water meter.
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks and includes solid waste for single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste, but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural or silvacultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
Municipal solid waste disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of any amount of municipal solid waste occurs, whether or not mixed or including commercial or industrial solid waste, including, but not limited to municipal solid waste landfills.
Municipal solid waste landfill means a disposal facility where any amount of municipal solid waste, whether or not mixed with or including commercial waste, industrial waste, nonhazardous sludges or small quantity generator hazardous waste, is disposed of by means of placing an approved cover thereon.
Owner means any person, firm, corporation owning, leasing, renting, occupying or managing any premises.
Plastic bag means a polyethylene or other heavy-duty plastic bag meeting the National Sanitation Foundation Standard of 1.1 mills and not exceeding a 30-gallon capacity, with securing twist tie. An untied plastic bag shall not be an approved container.
Retail tire dealer means a person actively engaged in the business of selling new and used replacement tires.
Rubbish means a variety of nonrecyclable household waste derived from places of residence and shall include paper, rags, plastics, cartons, boxes, broken glass bottles and jars, crockery, rubber, discarded clothing, and similar materials.
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage or defect.
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires not otherwise exempted in the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user or disposal facility.
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires. The term "scrap tire generator" may include, but is not limited to, retail tire dealers, retreaders, scrap tire processors, automobile dealers, private company vehicle maintenance shops, garages, service stations, and city, county and state governments.
Scrap tire processor means any person who is approved by the environmental protection division to receive scrap tires from scrap tire generators or scrap tire carriers for the purpose of scrap tire processing.
Scrap tire sorter means any person, other than the original scrap tire generator, who handles mixed tires by separating used tires and retreadable casings from scrap tires.
Single residential unit means any dwelling place occupied by one-family unit and which is served by a single water meter.
Solid waste means any garage or refuse; sludge from wastewater treatment plants; solid, liquid, semisolid, or contained gaseous material resulting from operations; mineral matter; metal and wooden furniture; appliances; large metal scraps and wires; wooden crates, boxes or pallets; dead animals; and other waste in a solid or semisolid state not otherwise defined in this section.
Waste means discarded material including, but not limited to, ashes, building refuse, garbage, hazardous waste, industrial refuse, recyclable waste, rubbish, solid waste, yard debris, tires, sand, gravel, slag, brick bats, trash, debris, dead animals, bottles, cans, boxes, containers, papers, tobacco products, appliances, mechanical equipment or parts, building or construction materials, tools, machinery, wood, motor vehicles or parts, vessels, aircraft equipment, waste oil, batteries, antifreeze, sludge from wastewater treatment plants or air pollution control facilities, and any other discarded material or substance of every kind and description resulting from domestic, industrial, commercial, governmental, or other operations.
Wheeled container means a covered plastic or rubber container on wheels designed primarily for use by residential units for storage of garbage, miscellaneous refuse, trash and other waste.
White goods means appliances such as stoves, refrigerators and other bulky household items primarily constructed of metal, parts of appliances, metal parts and miscellaneous metal.
Yard debris means tree limbs, branches, stumps and logs, twigs, grass and shrub trimmings and clippings, bushes, weeds, leaves, and general yard and garden waste materials, and includes stone and dirt rakings and similar waste materials resulting from landscaping activities.
Yard trimmings means leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvacultural operations.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-110, 11-16-2006)
Sec. 90-366. - Penalties for violation of article.
Except as otherwise specifically provided in this article, any violation of this article shall be subject to punishment in the municipal court of the city, upon conviction of such violation, as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1986, § 13-130)
Sec. 90-367. - Litter.
(a)
Scattering refuse and litter prohibited. No person shall throw or deposit any rubbish on any public or private street or scatter such refuse or litter on any public or private property, or throw or deposit in any stream or drainage area.
(b)
Use of streets and roads. It shall be unlawful for any vehicle transporting loose materials to transport the same without a suitable cover securely fastened to the vehicle in such a manner as to prevent said loose material from being deposited on the streets and adjoining areas.
(c)
Unlawful dumping. It shall be unlawful for a person to deposit, throw, drop, dump or otherwise leave any litter on the premises of another without the consent of such other person and without complying with the applicable laws including, but not limited to this section.
(d)
Prima facie case. Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane, or other conveyance in violation of this section, the trier of fact may in its discretion and in consideration of the totality of the circumstances infer that the operator of the conveyance has violated this section.
(e)
Presumption. Whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown, or left on public or private property in violation of this section is discovered to contain any articles, including, but not limited to, letters, bills, publications, or other writings which display the name of the person thereon in such manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be an rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this section.
(f)
Emergencies. Nothing contained in this section shall require the intermediate measures where the violation is of such nature or degree as to constitute an immediate, clear, and present danger to the health and safety of the public.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-125, 11-16-2006)
State Law reference— Litter control, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-40.
Sec. 90-368. - Administration and enforcement of article.
(a)
The city manager or his designee shall be responsible for the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this article. He may call upon the police department for assistance in its enforcement. All regulatory actions of the city manager or his designee shall be subject to review and approval of city council or a committee of city council designated for such purpose by the mayor.
(b)
Upon determining that a violation of this article exists, the city manager or his designee shall cause written notice of such violation to be issued to the owner or occupier of the subject premises, setting forth the violation of the chapter and granting such owner or resident a reasonable time, not to exceed ten days, for the owner to correct said condition.
(c)
Upon determining that the owner has failed or refused to correct the condition within the time prescribed in the notice, the city manager or his designee shall cause a notice to be issued directing the owner or occupier of said premises to appear in the municipal court to answer the charge of violation of this article.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-128, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 90-369. - Offal defined; removal to designated area; time limitation.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Offal means the inedible parts of a butchered animal and waste parts of the animal.
(b)
All offal and byproducts which result from the slaughter of an animal within the city shall be removed to the area designated by the county commissioners by the person responsible for slaughtering said animal within 12 hours after the slaughter of the animal.

DIVISION 2. - COLLECTION

Sec. 90-397. - Waste collection responsibility of city.
(a)
The city shall be responsible for the collection and disposal of all garbage, solid waste, yard debris and rubbish within the city, and no person, unless otherwise authorized by a contract with the city, shall collect and dispose of or transport over the streets of the city any of such waste without first obtaining a written permit from the city to do so. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the city shall be authorized to enter into a contract with a private corporation or a public authority to provide for the collection and disposal of all garbage, solid waste, yard debris, and rubbish within the city. Such contract shall contain such terms and conditions as authorized by the city council and shall be for such length of time as may be determined by the city council and its citizens, but in no event shall such contract extend a length of time not otherwise authorized by the Constitution and laws of the state.
(b)
The city shall not be responsible for the collection of hazardous waste and industrial refuse except as otherwise provided herein. The city shall not be responsible for the collection of building refuse generated by a contractor or other person who does not own or occupy the premises where such building refuse is generated. The city shall not be responsible for the collection of more than one cubic yard of residential building refuse generated in any one-week collection period by a person who owns or occupies the premises where such building refuse is generated. The disposal of all such waste, which is not subject to collection by the city, shall be the responsibility of the person generating such waste. Such waste shall be disposed of in accord with local, state and federal statutes, ordinances and regulations. One cubic yard shall be the equivalent of the size of two-wheeled garbage containers used for residential garbage collections.
(c)
No person who owns or occupies any premises in the city shall permit trash, garbage, or other waste or rubbish to collect or remain on such premises, except during such periods between waste pickup; and no person who owns or occupies any premises in the city shall permit garbage, trash or other waste to be scattered from such premises into the public streets of the city.
(d)
Any owner of property who permits garbage, solid waste, building refuse, yard debris, recyclable waste and rubbish, hazardous waste or industrial refuse to become in a condition which is hazardous or dangerous to the public health, safety and welfare shall be notified in writing by the city manager or his designee to abate such condition within ten days of the date of such notice. In the event that such condition is not abated within the time provided in such notice, then the city shall be authorized, either directly or through a private contractor, to abate such condition, and the cost of such abatement shall be charged against the property and shall be collected in the same manner as a lien for taxes is collected.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-111, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-09-08-27, 8-20-2009; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 90-398. - Refuse collection by city.
(a)
The city, or any entity with which the city has contracted for such purposes, shall make collections of waste from residential premises which have wheeled containers at least once weekly. The particular day or days of each week during which such collection shall occur shall be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent.
(b)
The city, or any entity with which the city has contracted for such purposes, shall make collections of waste from commercial and industrial premises which have wheeled containers at least weekly. The particular day or days of each week during which such collection shall occur shall be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent.
(c)
The city, or any entity with which the city has contracted for such purposes, shall make collections of residential building refuse not exceeding one cubic yard in size on a schedule and at such times as may be determined by the city manager or his designee. One cubic yard shall be the equivalent of the size of two, wheeled garbage containers used for residential garbage collection.
(d)
Yard debris and yard rubbish shall be collected by the city, or by any entity with which the city has contracted for such purposes, at least once a week on a schedule at such times as may be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent of the city. Such waste shall only be collected by the city when placed curbside. Wheeled containers shall not be utilized for disposal of such waste.
(e)
Industrial refuse shall be collected from business and commercial premises a minimum of twice weekly and a maximum of five times weekly, based upon the need of the customer, according to such schedule as may be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent of the city. Such collection shall be made from a front-loading container.
(f)
For purposes of this section, any resident of any multiple residential or commercial unit authorized to dispose of its waste by use of a front-loading container shall not be required to comply with the recyclable waste disposal provisions of this division.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-112, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-09-08-27, 8-20-2009; Ord. No. O-10-05-17, 5-20-2010; Ord. No. O-10-08-27, 8-19-2010; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 90-399. - Garbage containers.
(a)
It shall be the duty of any owner or occupant of any single or multiple residential or commercial unit to place the wheeled container provided for such purpose on the assigned collection day at a front curbside location in such a manner as not to obstruct pedestrian passage, except that the city manager or his designee or agent may require the wheeled container to be placed on any side of a parcel of property for the reasons of topography or efficiency.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the owner or occupant of a single or multiple residential or commercial unit to place the wheeled container at an appropriate curbside location at or before 7:00 a.m. or prior to the arrival of the sanitation truck.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the owner or occupant of any single or multiple residential or commercial unit to remove the wheeled container from the curbside location to a storage location, which storage location shall be nearer to the unit located on the premises than to any street abutting such premises; provided, however, that said removal shall be accomplished not later than 7:00 p.m. on the day the contents thereof are collected.
(d)
Garbage containers must be placed at least three feet away from vehicles, poles, fire hydrants, fences, overhead wires, low-hanging trees, branches, limbs, or other types of cover.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to set fire to the contents of, indiscriminately scatter or disperse the contents of, or otherwise vandalize any container provided by the municipality or its agent for the dumping of garbage.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-113, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 90-400. - Precollection practices.
(a)
Front-loading containers. The city manager or his designee or agent shall determine the type, size, specifications, number and location of the garbage containers and shall determine the condition.
(b)
Secure containers. All containers shall be maintained at all times with tight-fitting lids or covers, and secured in such a manner as to prevent the overturning by the elements or by dogs or other animals.
(c)
Garbage. Garbage placed in containers for collection shall be enclosed in disposable watertight plastic bags with the top secured and placed in the container.
(d)
Liquid. Garbage shall be drained of all liquids prior to its being placed in the container.
(e)
Injurious garbage or trash items. Garbage placed for pickup with the potential for injury, such as broken glass, lightbulbs, sharp pieces of metal, fluorescent tubes, etc., shall be securely packaged or wrapped in such a manner to prevent injury to collection crews or other persons.
(f)
Pet litter. Pet litter must be placed in securely tied plastic bags not to exceed ten pounds in weight and placed in the container.
(g)
Clean container. It shall be the responsibility of the owner or lessee of the property to keep the container clean and free of odor.
(h)
Scrap tires. No person shall deposit a scrap tire in any garbage container or on any public or private property.
(i)
Dead animals. No dead animals shall be deposited in any garbage container.
(j)
Burying or burning litter or waste; written consent. No owner or occupant of any such property within the corporate limits shall bury or burn litter or waste without prior authorization and written consent from the applicable regulatory agency, including, but not limited to, the United States Environmental Protection Division and/or the state forestry commission. Nothing in this provision shall authorize or be construed to permit the burial or burning of any material which is otherwise prohibited by state and/or federal law.
(k)
Accumulation. No owner or occupant of any property shall cause, suffer, or allow the accumulation on his premises, of garbage, litter or waste where such material creates or causes a health hazard to neighbors or other citizens, or which is unsightly or emits foul or obnoxious odors.
(l)
Violations. The conduct described in subsections (h), (j) and (k) of this section shall constitute a separate violation of this article for each day the garbage, litter or waste material remains or continues to unlawfully pollute, contaminate or burn on such premises.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-114, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 90-401. - Garbage collection for disabled persons.
Any person who is a full-time resident of a residential unit and who is disabled to the extent that such person is incapable of moving the wheeled container and who shall obtain a physician's certificate as to such disability, shall not be required to place the wheeled container at curbside as required in section 90-399. This section shall not apply unless all of the persons residing in a residential unit are disabled and obtain such physician's certificate. Provision for the collection of garbage from disabled residents shall be included in any contract approved by the city council under section 90-397.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-115, 11-16-2006)
Sec. 90-402. - Sorting, storing, composting and collecting yard trimmings.
(a)
Yard trimmings, and other small debris generated from the maintenance of a yard shall be collected and placed at the curb of the residence for collection. Said items are to be bagged or placed inside a container with a lid before being placed at the curb for pickup.
(b)
The on-site composting of yard trimmings and other yard debris is encouraged. Further, the development of low-maintenance landscaping is encouraged for all commercial and industrial developments.
(c)
Storing of yard trimmings and other debris generated on-site for a use other than in a composting or landscaping effort is hereby prohibited.
(d)
Limbs and yard trimmings which cannot be bagged as required under subsection (a) hereof, shall be placed at the curb for pickup and shall be subject to the following limitations:
(1)
Limbs and yard trimmings no more than five inches in diameter must be cut to no more than five feet long;
(2)
Limbs and yard trimmings measuring from five inches to 12 inches in diameter must be cut to no more than one foot long.
(3)
No limbs or yard trimmings larger than 12 inches in diameter shall be picked up by the city or by any entity with which the city has contracted.
(e)
Yard trimmings and limbs shall be picked up on vacant lots, but only when such debris is generated from the vacant lot.
(f)
Yard waste bags or containers must be placed at least six feet away from vehicles, trees, poles, fences, mailboxes, fire hydrants, low-hanging trees, limbs, branches, power lines, guy wires, or other types of obstructions.
(g)
Lawn services and contractors are responsible for bagging the yard waste and other waste that they generate and disposing of it in a suitable location as identified by this division.
(h)
Grass clippings, weeds, leaves, and other small yard waste materials must be bagged or placed in a container with a lid prior to being placed at the curb for pickup.
(i)
Yard waste bags may be placed in the right-of-way, the street pavement or in roadside ditches as long as the flow of water or the flow of traffic is not inhibited or changed.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-116, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-09-08-27, 8-20-2009; Ord. No. O-10-05-17, 5-20-2010; Ord. No. O-10-08-27, 8-19-2010)
Sec. 90-403. - Prohibitions.
(a)
Yard trimmings. It shall be unlawful to place or mix yard trimmings with municipal solid waste within the city.
(b)
Yard trimmings disposal. Yard trimmings shall not be disposed of at any municipal solid waste disposal facility having a liner and leachate collection system or requiring vertical expansion located within the city or the county.
(c)
Stormwater collection system. It shall be unlawful for any person to dump any solid waste on any property, in any waters or in any stormwater collection system, except as authorized by applicable permits it shall be the burden of the alleged violator to show proof of any applicable permits.
(d)
Storm drains. No person shall place any litter, refuse, trash, trees, grass, or other rubbish over or within five feet of a storm drain, drop inlet, culvert, or manhole.
(e)
Appliances. No person shall leave outside in any building or in a place accessible to children any appliance, refrigerator, freezer, or other container which has an airtight snap lock or similar device without first removing therefrom the door. This section shall not apply to any appliance, refrigerator, or container which has been placed on or adjacent to the rear of the building and which has been crated, strapped, or locked so that it will be impossible for a child to obtain access to any compartment thereof.
(f)
Dumping. It shall be unlawful to dump, open dump, or permit the dumping of litter, waste, scrap tires, municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste or recyclables at any place within the corporate city limits including and without limitations, in or on any public highway, road, street, alley, or thoroughfare, including any portion of the right-of-way, any public or private property in the city or any waters in the city unless such litter or waste originates within the corporate limits of the city and the property is designated by the city council or its duly designated agent for the disposal of the above, and the material is placed into a receptacle or container designed for such property; and the property has a valid solid waste handling permit issued by the state environmental protection division (EPD) when required.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-117, 11-16-2006)
Sec. 90-404. - Damaging, etc., containers; enforcement of regulations.
(a)
No person shall overhaul, damage, or interfere with the contents of any wheeled container or adjacent containerized trash set out for collection for emptying or removal by the city, unless by permission of the city manager or his designee or agent.
(b)
The city manager or his designee or agent is assigned the responsibility for enforcement of waste collection procedures enumerated herein.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-120, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 90-405. - Scrap tires.
(a)
The owner or occupant of any premises shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposal of all scrap tires on the premises used or occupied by such person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to cause, suffer or allow the dumping of scrap tires at any place including, and without limitations, in or on any public highway, road, street, alley, or thoroughfare, including any portion of the right-of-way thereof, any public or private property or any waters unless the property is designated by the city council and the state environmental protection division for the collection or disposal of scrap tires and the person is authorized to use such property. The scrap tires must be placed into a receptacle or container installed specifically for such property and the property has a valid solid waste handling permit or other applicable identification numbers, and approvals and/or permits issued by the state environmental protection division (EPD) when required.
(c)
All persons defined as scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers, scrap tire processors, including scrap tire sorters, and retail tire dealers shall be subject to rules as defined in chapter 391-3-4 et seq. of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, as amended, and handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq. and the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, chapter 391-3-4 et seq., as amended, applicable to solid waste, except where provisions of this division are more stringent, in which case the provisions of this division shall be applicable unless preempted by state law.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person in a residential zone to accumulate any amount of scrap tires on or around property, which they own or occupy.
(e)
A retail tire dealer may hold up to 100 scrap tires per garage bay in storage, up to a maximum of 1,000 scrap tires.
(f)
Tires must be stored in covered or enclosed areas to prevent the accumulation of water.
(g)
Any person, firm, or corporation violating any portion of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor:
(1)
The court may order the publication of the names and photographs of persons convicted of violating this division;
(2)
The court may order the person convicted hereunder to repair or restore property damaged, or pay damages resulting from such violations, or perform public service related to the repair or restoration of property damaged by the violation;
(3)
In case of a tire dump, the property owner, contractor, developer, builder or other person responsible for the property shall, upon conviction, cause the property to be cleaned and to come into full compliance with this division. The city council shall not be responsible for any costs of cleanup or remediation;
(4)
The expenses incurred by the city council for cleanup, enforcement of violations and penalties shall be chargeable to the person convicted hereunder, such expenses including, but not limited to: court costs, filing fees, special investigations, mutual aid assistance from other agencies and other costs necessary for the reasonable enforcement of this division;
(5)
In addition to actions filed by the city council for violations of this division, any state or federal agency may independently file separate or concurrent charges within their respective applicable authority and seek conviction within a court of competent jurisdiction;
(6)
Enforcement of this division shall be the responsibility of the city manager or his designee; and
(7)
Any person authorized to enforce this division shall be empowered to enter any property, upon reasonable cause, at reasonable or necessary times in order to properly inspect for violations of this division, subject to the condition that to allow entry onto private property for inspection, the alleged violation of this division must be visible from a public road or right-of-way, or upon said person having received a valid complaint alleging a violation of this division, or by a judge's order upon said person having received information/allegations that constitute reasonable suspicion that a serious unlawful act or threat to the health and safety of the community and/or the environment has occurred or is about to occur.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-124, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 90-406. - Waste not subject to pickup by the city.
(a)
Manufacturers. All processors of raw material or manufactured items, manufactured and/or assembly plants, are hereby exempted from payment of the fees provided for in this division; and no pickup service is to be furnished to such establishments except upon request, oral or written, which pick up shall be made by the city upon an estimated cost basis to be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent and to be billed by the finance director to such business making the request.
(b)
Automotive parts. Automotive parts will not be collected by the city or it's agent.
(c)
Dangerous materials. Dangerous materials or substances such as poisons, acids, caustics, infectious materials, explosives, paints, solvents, petroleum products, and other dangerous materials will not be collected by the city or its agent.
(d)
Dead animals. The collection of heavy dead animals such as cows, horses, deer, and mules will not be included in the services provided by the city or its agent.
(e)
Land clearing. Waste from land clearing operations such as rocks, dirt, sand, brick, and concrete will not be collected by the city or its agent.
(f)
Tires. No automobile or other motor vehicle tires will be collected by the city or it's agent.
(g)
Separation required. No comingled rubbish will be collected by the city or it's agent. All white goods, brown goods, yard trimmings, and garbage shall be separated into separate piles prior to collection.
(h)
Building materials. The contractor and/or builder shall be required to haul and transfer the debris to an authorized and permitted transfer station or landfill.
(i)
Batteries. Automotive, boat, and any other type of battery will not be collected by the city or it's agent.
(j)
Eviction or rental property debris. Trash, rubbish, white goods, brown goods, will not be collected by the city or its agent if occurs as a result of an eviction or as a result of the property owner cleaning up after a tenant has moved out. Sec. 90-407. - Enforcement of this division.
(a)
In the event that any person violates any provision of this division, the city council or other appropriate authority may, in addition to other remedies, institute an action for injunction, cleanup or stop work orders, mandamus, irreversible damage fines, liens on property or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful acts or to correct or abate any such violation. In addition, the city may immediately revoke or suspend any and all business, building, development or any and all other city issued permits related to the property or properties involved with the violation until such time that compliance is met, or until the ruling of a court of competent jurisdiction is obtained, at which time respective permits may be reissued.
(b)
Upon finding evidence, a written notice of violation may be issued at the discretion of the enforcing officer, in lieu of a citation. In the absence of corrective action or in the event that a second violation occurs, the evidence constituting the notice of violation may be submitted as evidence for consideration as a first offense before a court with competent jurisdiction and the pending case treated as a second offense by the court.
(c)
If a person is found guilty of a violation of the provisions of this division, the court may cause written notice to be incorporated into the court order to the violator instructing that person to properly address any provision still remaining in violation of this division for which said violator is convicted. Such notice shall be by personal service and in the event the violator cannot be so served, then by registered mail sent to the violator's last known address.
(d)
Upon failure, neglect or refusal of any person so notified to properly address said provisions within ten days after receipt of notice as provided in this section, the city council is hereby authorized and empowered to cause the cleanup, removal or disposal of scrap tires, dumped, deposited, thrown, or left on public or private property in violation of this division on behalf of the city. The expenses incurred by the city shall be chargeable to the violator and the city council and/or the court shall send a statement of the amount due for said expenses by registered mail.
(e)
When the full amount of such charges are not paid by the violator within 30 days after receipt of said statement as provided for in this section, the city council shall cause to be recorded in the execution docket a sworn statement showing the cost and expense incurred by the city, the dates of the action, the location of the property for which action was taken, and the name of the person to be charged for the expenses incurred. The recording of such statement shall constitute a lien on the personal and real property of the person to be charged and shall remain in full force and effect until final payment is received in full, including accrued interest from the date of recording and any and all costs. Such amount as shall constitute final payment shall be subject to collection in the manner fixed by law for the collection of taxes.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-129, 11-16-2006)

DIVISION 3. - RATES AND CHARGES

Sec. 90-428. - Charge—Authorized.
(a)
A monthly refuse disposal charge is hereby fixed and assessed against the owner or occupant of every improved premises within the city for which the occupant shall be liable for payment, but if for any reason an occupant of improved premises shall fail to pay said monthly charge to the city, then the owner thereof shall be liable for the payment thereof.
(b)
The owner or occupant of every improved premises within the city which is furnished a wheeled container by the city, or by any entity with which the city has contracted for garbage collection, shall be liable for any damage to such container and shall be liable for the expense of repairs to such container which shall be required, except for normal wear and tear, which repairs shall be made by the owner of the container. Such repair for which an owner or occupant shall be liable shall be billed to the occupant. If for any reason an occupant shall fail to pay said charges, then the owner of said improved premises shall be liable for the payment thereof. The charges shall be the actual cost of the necessary repairs.
(c)
The owner or occupant of any improved premises within the city who intends to vacate such premises must notify the city in order that the wheeled container may be removed from the premises if the owner of the wheeled container deems it appropriate to do so.
(d)
Where multiple residential or commercial premises are served through a master water meter, the owner of the premises shall be required to pay the appropriate charges as established in section 90-429 at the same time and in the same manner as the billing for water and other related services.
(Ord. No. O-97-05-16, 5-22-1997; Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-118, 11-16-2006)
Sec. 90-429. - Same—Schedule.
The monthly charge, provided for in section 90-428, within the city shall be established by separate ordinance.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-119, 11-16-2006)
Sec. 90-430. - Billing.
(a)
The charges provided for in section 90-429 within the city shall be billed monthly by the city.
(b)
In all instances where a residential premises is occupied by more than one single-family unit and is served by a single water meter, the refuse collection charges that are applicable to the residential premises at such location shall all be billed to the person in whose name the water meter is registered at the finance director's office, the charge to be included thereon for each family unit on the premises; provided, however, that with respect to any such multi-family premises where dumpster service is provided at such location, the owner of the premises shall be billed and shall pay the applicable monthly dumpster charges as provided in the then-current dumpster fee schedule approved by the local governing body from time to time. In the event of failure to pay the bill in accordance with ordinances applicable thereto, the city, by and through its proper officials, may proceed to enforce collection of same as provided by ordinance, and in addition to such remedies, failure to pay the bill within 30 days from the date of issue will constitute adequate cause to have such water service disconnected and discontinued at those premises.
(c)
Charges for repairs to wheeled containers shall be billed and collected with the following billing of refuse collection charges. Subsections (a) and (b) of this section and sections 90-431 and 90-432 are applicable to wheeled container repair charges.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-121, 11-16-2006; Ord. No. O-2017-12, 10-19-2017)
Sec. 90-431. - Execution, levy, and sale authorized.
Should any person liable for payment of the fees and charges hereby fixed and imposed fail to pay the same within 30 days after having been billed therefor, the finance director is authorized to issue an execution therefor against such person in the same manner provided in cases of default in payment of utility bills.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-122, 11-16-2006)
Sec. 90-432. - Discontinuance of services to premises when charges remain unpaid.
Any person failing for any reason or refusing to pay monthly the charge provided for in this division shall be subject to the discontinuance of city services as provided in section 90-1.
(Ord. No. O-06-11-41, exh. A, § 13-123, 11-16-2006)
