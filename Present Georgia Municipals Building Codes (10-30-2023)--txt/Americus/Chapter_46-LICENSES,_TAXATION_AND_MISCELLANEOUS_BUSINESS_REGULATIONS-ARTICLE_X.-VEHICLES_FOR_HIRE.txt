ARTICLE X. - VEHICLES FOR HIRE

Sec. 46-367. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Vehicle for hire means and shall refer to any vehicle utilized by a person has been issued a permit to transport persons for a fee by the chief of police consistent with the provisions of this article. Such a vehicle of hire shall be owned only by a person who has been issued a business license by the finance director for the operation of a vehicle for hire business within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1986, § 22-1; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004)
Sec. 46-368. - Business license required; cost.
In order to operate a vehicle for hire business within the corporate limits of the city, the owner of such business must obtain a business license from the finance director of the city for such business. The cost of such business license, which shall be paid at the time of application and which shall be nonrefundable, shall be in an amount to be determined from time to time by the city council. The issuance of such business license for a vehicle for hire business shall not authorize the owner of the business, or any other person employed by such owner, to engage in the vehicle for hire business until such owner complies with all of the other provisions of this article.
(Code 1986, § 22-2; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004)
Sec. 46-369. - Documentation required; notification of changes.
The owner of such business shall keep on file with the chief of police documentation providing the name of the owner of the business, the owner's business address, all business telephone numbers listed in the owner's name, the names of all drivers of vehicles for hire employed by such owner, the permit number for each such driver, the home address of each such driver, and the total number of vehicles for hire that such owner operates as part of such business. Any changes in such information shall be reported by the owner in writing to the chief of police within 48 hours of such change.
(Code 1986, § 22-3; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-370. - Regulations; compliance.
Every owner of a vehicle for hire business shall comply with the following regulations:
(1)
Include in the application for such owner's business license the hours and service periods during which the vehicles for hire will be available to the public;
(2)
Provide a telephone call system for vehicle for hire customers and sufficient vehicles to provide the public with the service as contemplated in the license application;
(3)
Require vehicle for hire drivers to comply with all city, county, state, and federal statutes, laws, ordinances, and regulations;
(4)
Submit all vehicles used as vehicle for hire to inspection when requested by the chief of police or his designee for the purpose of maintaining all such vehicles in a safe, sanitary, and clean condition at all times;
(5)
Require that each vehicle for hire display the business name, the business telephone number or numbers, and the vehicle for hire permit number appropriately;
(6)
Maintain a record of all telephone calls requesting vehicle for hire service for a minimum of 90 calendar days from the date of such call for use by the police department if necessary; and
(7)
Require that each driver of a vehicle for hire maintain a daily written trip log or manifest.
(Code 1986, § 22-4; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-371. - Serial numbers; locations; decals; size.
Upon the issuance of a business license by the finance director to the owner of the vehicle for hire business, the finance director shall also provide to such owner a distinct serial number that shall be painted or stenciled on each vehicle for hire. One distinct serial number shall be issued to each vehicle for hire, and no two vehicles shall be authorized to use the same serial number. Such number shall be at least six inches in height and shall be placed on a conspicuous part of the vehicle for hire so that it may be visible. In addition, the name and address of the vehicle for hire business shall be displayed in a conspicuous place on all of the vehicles for hire at all times. The name of the driver shall be prominently displayed within all vehicles for hire. No vehicle for hire shall be authorized to operate within the corporate limits of the city as a vehicle for hire until compliance with the provisions of this section is completed.
(Code 1986, § 22-5; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004)
Sec. 46-372. - Business location in city.
No vehicle for hire business shall operate in the city unless the owner thereof shall maintain a place of business within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1986, § 22-6; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-373. - Drivers' permits—Required; applications.
(a)
Any person, including any person who owns and operates a vehicle for hire business, who intends to drive a vehicle for hire within the corporate limits of the city, shall apply for a permit to do so from the police department. Such permit shall be applied for in writing upon such application forms as are deemed appropriate by the chief of police. Such application shall require such information as may be deemed necessary by the chief of police. Such permit shall be in addition to the vehicle for hire business license that the owner of such vehicle for hire business is required to obtain from the finance director as required by this chapter.
(b)
Such permit application shall be signed by at least two individuals who are personally familiar with the applicant and can testify to the applicant's good character. No person may sign such application as a reference who either owns a vehicle for hire business or who has, or is in the process of applying for, a permit to operate a vehicle for hire as a driver. Such application shall also be signed by the applicant.
(c)
No permit to operate a vehicle for hire shall be issued to any person who has not exhibited a familiarity with this article and the traffic laws, statutes, ordinances, and regulations of the state, who has been convicted by the state or any other state of a felony within the past five years, or who has been convicted of three or more moving traffic offenses during the five years immediately preceding the date of the application; provided, however, that any applicant who has been convicted of a felony within the prescribed time may be issued a permit if the chief of police has determined that the sentence imposed for such felony conviction has been served, that the service of such sentence has been completed for at least three calendar years from the date of the application, and the nature of the felony for which such applicant was convicted is determined by the chief of police not to be such an offense that would adversely impair the applicant's ability to operate a vehicle for hire and would not endanger or be reasonably likely to expose any vehicle for hire passengers to an unacceptable risk of bodily injury, property damage or loss.
(Code 1986, § 22-7; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004)
Sec. 46-374. - Same—Valid state driver's license.
In order for a person to receive a permit to operate a vehicle for hire within the city, such person must have attained the age of at least 18 years and must possess a valid state driver's license issued by the state department of motor vehicles of the appropriate class under O.C.G.A. title 40 (O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 et seq.) that would authorize the applicant to drive persons in a vehicle for hire. Any loss of the privilege to drive a motor vehicle arising out of or in connection with the permit holder's arrest for or violation of any of the traffic or drug statutes or ordinances of this state, any other state, or any county or municipality located in this or any other state, shall immediately invalidate any permit to operate a vehicle for hire issued under this article.
(Code 1986, § 22-8; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-375. - Health certificate; vision certificate.
Each person applying for a permit to drive a vehicle for hire shall furnish a health certificate and a vision certificate to the chief of police with such permit application. The health certificate shall be signed by a practicing physician in good standing and the vision certificate shall be signed by a practicing ophthalmologist or optometrist in good standing. The form of the certificate must be approved by the chief of police.
(Code 1986, § 22-9; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-376. - Drivers permit—Examination; familiarity with this article and traffic laws.
No person shall be authorized to drive a vehicle for hire carrying passengers until such person has exhibited, upon examination by the chief of police or his designee, a familiarity with the provisions of this article and the traffic statutes, laws, ordinances, and regulations in force in the city. The chief of police shall provide at least one copy of the vehicle for hire article to each vehicle for hire driver.
(Code 1986, § 22-10; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-377. - Same—Expiration date.
Each permit issued to a driver of a vehicle for hire shall expire on December 31 of the year in which such permit was issued. Applications for renewal of such permit shall be accepted by the police department between December 1 and December 31 of each calendar year, but no person shall be authorized to operate a vehicle for hire beyond December 31 of any calendar year until such person's application for a new or renewal permit is approved in accord with this article.
(Code 1986, § 22-11; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-378. - Same—Application fee; permit display.
(a)
The application fee required to be paid at the time of the submission of the application for a permit as a driver of a vehicle for hire, shall not be prorated.
(b)
The permit issued to a successful applicant shall be numbered, dated as of the date of issue, and shall have such other information displayed thereon as may be deemed appropriate by the chief of police.
(c)
The permit shall, at all times when the permittee is engaged in driving a vehicle for hire within the city, be displayed in a conspicuous place by said permittee in the vehicle so that the permit can be easily seen by passengers riding in said vehicle for hire.
(Code 1986, § 22-12; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-379. - Liability insurance.
(a)
Each applicant for a vehicle for hire business license shall, before the license is granted, provide evidence of a policy or certificate of motor vehicle liability insurance, with respect to each vehicle to be operated as a vehicle for hire, with coverage in the following amounts:
(1)
Ten thousand dollars for injury to one person;
(2)
Twenty-five thousand dollars for injury to more than one person in any one accident; and
(3)
Five thousand dollars for property loss or damage.
(b)
Such insurance shall be issued by a company authorized to do business in the state. The policy or certificate of insurance shall identify all vehicles insured under such policy and shall contain a provision obligating the insurance carrier to provide notice of cancellation or modification of the policy to the chief of police within 15 calendar days after such a cancellation or modification. In the event of lapse, cancellation, or termination of such policy or certificate of insurance, or in the event that such coverage as required above are no longer afforded under such policy or certificate of insurance, then the license for the vehicle for hire business shall be deemed to have immediately expired.
(c)
In lieu of such liability insurance requirement, the licensee shall be authorized to operate as a "self-insurer" if such operation is otherwise authorized under O.C.G.A. § 33-34-5.1.
(Code 1986, § 22-13; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-380. - Rates posted in vehicle, visible to passengers.
The rates charged by vehicles for hire must be posted in the vehicle, for inspection by passengers at all times.
(Code 1986, § 22-14; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-381. - Inspection by police department; driver and vehicle.
Each person who drives a vehicle for hire upon the streets of the city shall submit to inspection of both the vehicle and the driver by a certified police officer of the city police department whenever directed to do so.
(Code 1986, § 22-15; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003)
Sec. 46-382. - Suspension or revocation of license or permit.
(a)
In the event that the owner of the vehicle for hire business fails to comply with any of the provisions of this article, the finance director shall be authorized to suspend or revoke the business license previously issued to such owner. In the event of such a suspension or revocation, notice of such decision shall be communicated to the owner in writing by personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested. Such notice is deemed to be effective upon personal service or upon mailing. Any suspension hereunder shall be for such duration as the finance director deems appropriate, but any such suspension shall not exceed 30 calendar days. Violation of a written notice of suspension by the owner shall be grounds for revocation of the owner's license to operate such vehicle for hire business.
(b)
In the event that a person issued a permit to drive a vehicle for hire violates or fails to comply with any of the provisions of this article, then the chief of police shall be authorized to suspend or revoke such permit. In the event of such a suspension or revocation, notice of such decision shall be communicated to the permittee in writing by personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested. Such notice is deemed to be effective upon personal service or upon mailing. Any suspension hereunder shall be for such duration as the chief of police deems appropriate, but any such suspension shall not exceed 30 calendar days. Violation of a written notice of suspension by the permittee shall be grounds for revocation of the permittee's permit to drive a vehicle for hire.
(c)
Any person whose business license to operate a vehicle for hire business, or whose permit to drive a vehicle for hire in the city has been suspended or revoked, has the right to appeal such suspension or revocation to the city council within ten calendar days of the effective date of such suspension or revocation. Such appeal must be in writing, must be submitted to the city official who ordered such suspension or revocation, and must set out the grounds upon which the appellant contends that the suspension or revocation was erroneously issued. The city manager of the city shall be notified of the filing of such an appeal and shall schedule a hearing thereon by the city council within 30 calendar days from the date of filing such appeal. In considering such appeal, the city council shall affirm the action or decision of the finance director or the chief of police, as the case may be, if the city council finds that such decision was supported by any evidence.
(Code 1986, § 22-16; Ord. No. O-03-12-41, 12-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
