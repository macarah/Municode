ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Bird sanctuary; constituted.
The city as now constituted or as may hereafter be constituted, together with all property belonging to it or under its jurisdiction, is hereby established as a bird sanctuary.
(Code 1962, § 4-1; Code 1986, § 4-1)
Sec. 10-2. - Injuring, molesting or killing wild fowl and other birds.
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to trap, hunt, kill, shoot, or attempt to shoot, wound, injure, abuse or molest any wild fowl or other bird or to rob bird nests; provided, however, if starlings, English sparrows or any other kind of bird are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or menace to the health or property of the citizens of the city in the opinion of the public health authorities, it shall be so declared by such authorities. Then such birds may be destroyed in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by said health authorities.
(Code 1962, § 4-6; Code 1986, § 4-2)
Sec. 10-3. - Cruelty to animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to overload, cruelly treat, maim, bruise, abuse, mistreat, torture, deprive of necessary sustenance, ill use or otherwise wrongfully deal with any domestic animal or fowl or otherwise treat such domestic creatures in a harsh, inhumane or unreasonable manner. Shelter for horses, cattle or other domestic animals shall be provided to protect such creatures against inclement weather and extreme temperatures.
(Code 1962, § 4-4; Code 1986, § 4-3)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4.
Sec. 10-4. - Poisoning animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to put out or cause to be put out any poison within the city for the killing of any domestic animal.
(Code 1962, § 4-5; Code 1986, § 4-4)
Sec. 10-5. - Livestock running at large; impounding; sale; redemption by owner; fees.
(a)
No horse, mule, cow, hog, goat, or other animal used or fit either for food or labor, shall be permitted to run at large upon the streets, sidewalks, alleys or public grounds in the city. Any such animal found at large within the city shall be taken up and impounded by the impounding officer, or other officer of the city.
(b)
Each animal so impounded shall be sold by the finance director or his designee, at public outcry, after an advertisement of at least five days in the newspaper in which the city printing is published, giving the time and place of sale. The proceeds of said sale, after paying all costs and expenses thereof, and of taking up and keeping such animal, shall be deposited in the city treasury for six months to await the claim of the owner; at the expiration of which time, if there is no claimant therefor, it shall be used for general city purposes.
(c)
The owner of such animal may, at any time before the sale, reclaim such stock by paying all costs and expenses of impounding and keeping the same. The fee for taking up and impounding any such animal shall be as established by ordinance, and for keeping the same shall be as established by ordinance.
(Code 1962, § 4-2; Code 1986, § 4-5)
Sec. 10-6. - Swine prohibited.
No person shall keep any swine within the city.
(Code 1962, § 4-3; Code 1986, § 4-6)
Sec. 10-7. - Manner of keeping of animals and fowl—Sanitation of enclosures; nuisance.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, maintain or possess any animal or fowl in the city in such manner as to create an unsanitary or obnoxious condition, or to become noisy, offensive or a nuisance to the neighbors or citizens of the city, or to disturb the peace of the city.
(b)
Should a complaint arise from the causes referred to in subsection (a) of this section, the person involved will be properly notified and given adequate time to either dispose of the animal or fowl or eliminate the cause of the complaint.
(Code 1962, § 4-7; Code 1986, § 4-7)
Sec. 10-8. - Same—Pens or enclosures within 300 feet of building.
It shall be unlawful for any person to build any pen, coop or enclosure for animals or fowl, or to permit any animal or fowl to be kept, maintained or grazed, within 300 feet of any house or other building where people reside or work. This section shall not apply to any house or other building on the same premises where such animal or fowl is kept or maintained, nor shall it apply to dogs or cats kept as household pets, but it shall apply to dog kennels, where more than one dog is kept.
(Code 1962, § 4-11; Code 1986, § 4-8)
Sec. 10-9. - Vicious animals.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or other person to maintain or harbor within the city a manifestly vicious animal unless the same is securely, but humanely, confined or restrained in such manner as to prevent such animal from attacking or biting a person or another animal. It shall be prima facie evidence of viciousness if an animal, without provocation, fiercely attacks or bites a person or animal not on the owner's premises.
(Code 1962, § 4-8; Code 1986, § 4-9)
Sec. 10-10. - Dead animals—Duty of owner to remove from public ways.
The owner of dead animals upon the public streets and ways shall remove the same so as not to constitute a nuisance. If the owner fails to do so the city shall remove and dispose of such carcass, and charge the cost of such removal and disposition to the owner of the carcass, if known.
(Code 1962, § 4-9; Code 1986, § 4-10)
Sec. 10-11. - Same—Removal from private property.
When any dead animal is found on private property, the owner of the dead animal or the owner of the premises upon which such animal is located shall remove the same immediately at such owner's own cost. Upon failure to do so within a reasonable time the city shall remove same, the cost to be collected from the offending party.
(Code 1962, § 4-10; Code 1986, § 4-11)
Sec. 10-12. - Nuisances prohibited and defined.
(a)
For the purposes of this section, the term "nuisance" means an act or situation caused by or contributed to by an animal with or without the permission of the animal's owner or keeper, which act or situation substantially interferes with the rights of citizens to enjoyment of life or property or unreasonably annoys humans or endangers the life or health of persons or other animals. Such a nuisance shall include, but shall not be limited to the following acts by an animal:
(1)
Molesting or threatening to molest pedestrians;
(2)
Chasing vehicles;
(3)
Causing loss or damage to the person or property of someone other than the owner or keeper of the animal; or
(4)
Continually and repeatedly howling, barking, whining or otherwise making noises which cause unreasonable annoyance, disturbance or discomfort to neighbors or others.
(b)
Where a nuisance is a public nuisance, an action or a proceeding to abate same, may be brought in the municipal court for the city by the appropriate city official or officials.
(c)
Where such nuisance is a private nuisance, a proceeding to abate such nuisance may be brought in the municipal court for the city by the party or parties aggrieved thereby. Such court shall have all authority granted pursuant to city ordinances and state law to order abatement of such nuisance.
(Code 1962, § 4-22; Code 1986, § 4-30; Ord. No. O-90-09-22, 9-24-1990)
Sec. 10-13. - Control and removal of animal waste.
(a)
The owner or custodian of any animal shall immediately remove any feces deposited by such animal upon the public sidewalks, public streets, public parks, or other public property in the City of Americus, or upon any private property within the City of Americus not under the use and control of said owner or custodian. The owner of any such animal, or the custodian of any such animal, shall be in possession of materials to remove feces left by such animal at any time during which the animal is not on property under the use and control of said owner or custodian.
(b)
Violation of this section shall be punishable upon conviction by a fine of not less than $75.00, nor more than $500.00. Each act in violation of this section shall be considered a separate offense.
(Ord. No. O-10-05-18, 5-20-2010)
Secs. 10-14—10-42. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL CONTROL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-43. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal control officer means an employee of the police department of the city whose duties specifically include enforcement of the provisions of this article.
Dangerous dog means any dog or breed of dog that, according to records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation, on public or private property; or
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification; or
(3)
Any dog breed which, notwithstanding the records of an appropriate authority with respect to any particular dog, has been determined, by proper resolution of the governing body of the city, to be a dangerous dog breed in accord with the provisions of section 10-67.1 of the City Code.
Dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breeds means a breed or breeds of dogs which the governing body of the City of Americus has determined, by resolution, to be a breed which consists primarily of dangerous dogs or potentially dangerous dogs.
Dog control officer means the chief of police of the city, and such employees of the animal control division of the police department acting under his supervision and control.
Owner means any natural persons or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust, owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dog or cat within the territorial boundaries of the city.
Potentially dangerous dog means either: (1) any dog that has, without provocation, bitten a human being while on public or private property; or (2) any dog that has been determined, by proper resolution of the governing body of the city, to be a member of a dangerous dog breed or a potentially dangerous dog breed in accord with the provisions of section 10-67.1 of the City Code.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, while on the owner's property, securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Rabies control officer means an employee of the county board of health whose specific duties include enforcement of the rabies control rules and regulations of the county.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county or municipal law enforcement agency, records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state or local court; or records maintained by the dog control officer pursuant to this article.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(Code 1986, § 4-20; Ord. No. O-90-02-06, 2-26-1990; Ord. No. O-09-07-22, 7-23-2009)
Sec. 10-44. - Restraint of dogs and prohibition against nuisances.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner or keeper of any dog to allow the same to run upon the streets, sidewalks or alleys of the city, unless said dog is accompanied by its owner or other person having charge of said dog, in which event said dog must be tied with a cord or chain of sufficient strength to hold such dog.
(b)
No person owning or having custody of any female dog shall permit such female dog off the premises of the owner or person having control of such dog when dog shall be in heat, unless the same is under control and attached to a leash, as herein provided.
(c)
Whenever a dog is allowed to run free, in violation of this section, impoundment of the animal is authorized. Upon identification of the owner, citation shall be issued for violation of this section.
(d)
The owner and keeper of each animal within the city shall keep it from becoming a nuisance and from endangering or injuring any person. As a consequence thereof, it shall be the duty of every owner or keeper of any animal to exercise reasonable care with respect to said animal, and to take all reasonable steps and precautions necessary to protect other persons, property and animals from injury, loss or damage which might be caused by or arise from such animal's behavior, regardless of whether such behavior is motivated by mischievousness, playfulness or ferocity. If the owner or keeper of any animal is a minor, the parent or guardian of such minor shall be responsible for ensuring compliance with this section.
(e)
Whenever an animal is kept in such a manner as constitutes a nuisance as defined under either city ordinances or state law, a proceeding to abate the nuisance may be brought by the party aggrieved, pursuant to section 10-12. Such an action may also be brought by the city pursuant to said section. An animal found by the court to constitute a nuisance may be impounded by the city if the owner, after reasonable opportunity to do so, fails to abate the nuisance as ordered by the court.
(Code 1986, § 4-21; Ord. No. O-90-02-06, 2-26-1990; Ord. No. O-90-09-22, 9-24-1990)
Sec. 10-45. - Inoculation; registration; tagging of animals.
(a)
All dogs and cats shall be inoculated against rabies in accordance with the procedures set forth in the rabies control rules and regulations of the county, as promulgated and from time to time amended by the county board of health, which by reference are expressly incorporated as part of this section as if fully set out herein. Violation of said rules and regulations may be enforced by the rabies control officer in the municipal court of the city, upon citation, as an offense against the city.
(b)
The numbered vaccination tag, issued by a licensed veterinarian upon inoculation, shall be attached to a collar or harness and worn by the animal at all times. Every dog found roaming or running free, whether tagged or untagged, shall be impounded; every tagged or untagged cat found roaming or running free shall be impounded. If a tagged animal is impounded, and it is found not to be registered or its current registration has expired, the owner may be cited for violation of this section.
(Code 1986, § 4-22; Ord. No. O-90-02-06, 2-26-1990)
Sec. 10-46. - Impoundment and actions related thereto.
All animals impounded by the city shall be held in a humane manner and in accord with an agreement between the city and the Sumter Humane Society, Inc., or some other entity as may from time to time be determined and established by action of the city council.
(Code 1986, § 4-23; Ord. No. O-90-02-06, 2-26-1990; Ord. No. O-90-09-22, 9-24-1990)

Sec. 10-66. - Penalties.
Violations of this division shall be punished by the issuance of a state warrant pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28.
(Code 1986, § 4-26; Ord. No. O-90-02-06, 2-26-1990)
Sec. 10-67. - Dangerous dogs or potentially dangerous dogs.
(a)
Upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the dog control officer's jurisdiction from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, or county board of health, the dog control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this article. Whenever the dog control officer classifies a dog as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, notice shall be given the dog's owner in writing by certified mail to the owner's last known address of such classification or reclassification. Such notices shall be deemed complete upon mailing.
(b)
The notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address.
(2)
The notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's finding that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog.
(3)
The notice shall be dated and shall state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog before the governing authority.
(4)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes under this section on the day after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing.
(c)
When the governing authority receives a request for a hearing it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The governing authority shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time and place of the hearing. At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence and in addition thereto the governing authority shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the governing authority may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog. Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the governing authority shall notify the owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify it is effective on the third day following the date of receipt by the owner.
(d)
Issuance of a certificate of registration shall be made pursuant to the following:
(1)
Subject to the requirements of this section, the dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog if the owner presents to the dog control officer or if the dog control officer finds sufficient evidence of:
a.
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog.
b.
The posting of the premises where the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the premises. The sign required to be posted must substantially conform to the design provided by the department of natural resources.
c.
Payment to the city of a fee in the amount of $50.00, to defray the cost of administering and enforcing this section.
(2)
In addition, the owner of a dangerous dog shall present evidence to the dog control officer of:
a.
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $15,000.00, issued by an insurer authorized to transact business within the state insuring the owner of the dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
b.
A surety bond in the amount of $15,000.00, or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person or persons injured by the dangerous dog.
(e)
Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of an annual renewal of a certificate of registration, a dog control officer shall require evidence from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with other provisions of this article.
(f)
Issuance of a certificate of registration or the renewal of a certificate of registration by the local government does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements specified in subsection (d) of this section are maintained by the owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog on a continuous basis following the date of the issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate.
(g)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, if unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the name, address and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(h)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer if the owner is moving from the dog control officer's jurisdiction.
(i)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the state shall register the dog in the manner provided by this article within 30 days after becoming a resident.
(j)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who moves from one jurisdiction to another within the state shall register the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog in the manner provided by this article within 30 days after becoming a resident of the new jurisdiction.
(k)
The fee to be paid to the city for a certificate of registration for a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25 shall be as established by ordinance.
(Code 1986, § 4-24; Ord. No. O-90-02-06, 2-26-1990)
Sec. 10-67.1. - Determination by city council of dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breeds.
(a)
In making a determination that a particular dog breed is a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breed, the governing body shall undertake the following steps:
(1)
In the event that the dog control officer receives a report or obtains other information which leads the dog control officer to believe that a particular dog breed is a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breed, such information shall be provided to the city manager of the city.
(2)
The city manager shall review all evidence and other information submitted by the dog control officer regarding the particular dog breed involved and, in the event that the city manager determines that a particular dog breed is a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breed, the city manager shall report such fact, and the information and evidence supporting such fact, to the governing body of the city.
(3)
Upon receipt of such report from the city manager, the governing body of the city shall, upon recommendation of the public safety committee, hold a public hearing on the issue of whether or not the particular dog breed is a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breed. Notice of such public hearing shall be published at least once per week for the two calendar weeks immediately prior to the date of the public hearing. Such published notice shall appear in the legal organ of the city, and the cost of such publication shall be paid by the city.
(4)
At the public hearing, the findings and evidence that the dog breed is a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breed shall be presented to the governing body by the city manager or his or her designee. Upon presentation of such evidence, persons desiring to speak in favor of declaring the particular dog breed a dangerous or potentially dangerous breed shall have ten minutes to make their presentations to the governing body as to why such dog breed is a dangerous or potentially dangerous breed. At the conclusion of such presentations, persons opposed to declaring the particular dog breed a dangerous or potentially dangerous breed shall have ten minutes to present evidence as to why such dog breed is not a dangerous or potentially dangerous breed. At the conclusion of the evidence, the governing body shall consider all evidence presented and shall determine whether the particular dog breed is a dangerous or potentially dangerous breed based upon the evidence and argument presented at the public hearing. Such determination shall be made by the governing body in the form of a resolution declaring that such breed is either a dangerous or a potentially dangerous dog breed.
(5)
For purposes of such a determination, the governing body of the city shall be required to establish that a particular breed is dangerous or potentially dangerous by reviewing evidence presented to the governing body regarding the dog breed in question, including the dog breed's propensity for inflicting severe injuries on humans without provocation or aggressively biting, attacking or endangering the safety of humans without provocation, or that the breed is likely, based upon the characteristics of the breed, to do so. The final determination as to whether a particular breed is a potentially dangerous dog breed or a dangerous dog breed shall be made by the governing body of the city based upon the evidence presented to the governing body at the public hearing required in accord with the Code section.
(b)
In the event that the governing body of the city adopts a resolution declaring a particular dog breed as a dangerous dog breed, the provisions of section 10-67 and section 10-68 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Americus which are applicable to dangerous dogs shall be applicable to that particular dog breed.
(c)
In the event that the governing body of the city adopts a resolution declaring a particular dog breed as a potentially dangerous dog breed, the provisions of section 10-67 and section 10-68 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Americus which are applicable to potentially dangerous dogs shall be applicable to that particular dog breed.
(Ord. No. O-09-07-22, 7-23-2009; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 10-68. - Possession without certification; keeping dog in violation of certification; failure to post clearly visible warning sign; confiscation and impoundment; destruction of animal.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner to have or possess within the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with this article.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for a potentially dangerous dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog to fail to post a warning sign on the premises where a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is housed or kept by its owner in accordance with this article. The warning sign shall give notice to the public of the housing or keeping of a dog or a breed of dog which has been declared to be a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog.
(e)
It shall be the duty of the dog control officer, and any person under his employ, to impound the dog if:
(1)
The owner does not secure a certificate of registration in accordance with this article.
(2)
The owner does not secure liability insurance or meet the bond requirement of this article.
(3)
The owner does not keep the dog within a proper enclosure or permits the dog to roam or run free.
(f)
Any dog impounded under the provisions of this section shall be returned to the owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon payment of reasonable impoundment costs, as fixed by a schedule approved by the governing authority, as from time to time amended. Notice of impoundment under this section shall be given the owner, in writing, by certified mail, at his last known address. This notice shall state that if the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within 20 days from receipt of such notice, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner; provided, however, no dog shall have been destroyed under this section unless it has been held a full 20 days, exclusive of the date of giving notice.
(g)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused such dog.
(h)
Under no circumstances shall the city or any employee or official of the city which enforces or fails to enforce the provisions of this article be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog:
(1)
That has been identified as being a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
Which the city has failed to identify as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
Which has been identified as being a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog but has not been kept or restrained in the manner described in this section; or
(4)
Whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required in this section.
(Code 1986, § 4-25; Ord. No. O-90-02-06, 2-26-1990; Ord. No. O-09-07-22, 7-23-2009)
Sec. 10-69. - Penalty for violation of city ordinances related to dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breeds.
Notwithstanding the provisions of section 10-66 of the City Code, any violation of the provisions of the City Code, related to dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breeds, shall be punished in accord with the provisions of section 1-8 of the City Code. In addition to the penalty provisions of section 1-8 of the City Code, any person owning a dog breed which has been declared as a dangerous or a potentially dangerous dog breed who violates the provisions of the City Code relative to dangerous dog breeds or potentially dangerous dog breeds shall have his or her dangerous or potentially dangerous dog breed immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer or another person authorized by the dog control officer and placed in quarantine for the proper length of time as determined by the county board of health and, thereafter, the dog which is a member of the dangerous dog breed or a member of the potentially dangerous dog breed may be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(Ord. No. O-09-07-22, 7-23-2009)
