Sec. 46-229. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Month means that period of time from one date in a calendar month to the corresponding date in the following calendar month, but if there is no such corresponding date, then the last day of such following month.
Pawn transaction means any loan on the security of pledged goods or any purchase of pledged goods on the condition that the pledged goods may be redeemed or repurchased by the pledger or seller for a fixed price during a fixed period of time.
Pawnbroker means any person engaged in whole or in part in the business of lending money on the security of pledged goods, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property on the condition that it may be redeemed or repurchased by the seller during a fixed period of time, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property from persons or sources other than manufacturers or licensed dealers as part of or in conjunction with the business activities described in this definition.
Pawnshop means any business, a substantial portion of which is to take or receive, by way of pledge, pawn, or exchange, any goods, wares, merchandise, or any kind of personal property whatsoever, as security for the repayment of money lent thereon.
(Code 1986, § 14-202; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-230. - Purpose.
This article is enacted in accordance with a plan designed for the purpose of promoting the health and general welfare of the community, to provide, establish, and ascertain standards for the regulation and control of pawnshops or similar places where money is advanced on goods and other effects, merchandise, or personal property.
(Code 1986, § 14-200; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-231. - Compliance required.
All persons, before beginning the business of operating a pawnshop, shall first file an application with the city and receive a business license for the operation of a pawnshop in order to conduct such business from the finance director. The application for such business license shall be made upon such forms and in such manner as the finance director may establish from time to time. Such business license shall be obtained in accordance with regular business license application procedure, which is required under article IV of this chapter.
(Code 1986, § 14-201; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004)
Sec. 46-232. - General qualifications for business license.
(a)
No license shall be issued if the application or other evidence available to the city reveals that any one or more of the following conditions to exist:
(1)
The applicant is of bad moral character or does not have the mental capacity to conduct the business for which the application is made.
(2)
The applicant has had any business license or permit issued under the police powers of this or any other city or other governmental subdivision previously suspended or revoked.
(3)
The applicant, while a holder of a license to operate a pawnshop has been convicted of, or pled guilty or nolo contendere to, a violation of any law, regulation, or ordinance relating to the pawnshop business within a ten-year period immediately preceding the date of application.
(4)
The applicant has failed to produce any paper or document necessary in the operation of its business as may be required by any officer, agency, or department of a municipality, county, state, or the United States under authority of any law, ordinance, or resolution of such municipality, county, state, or the United States.
(5)
The applicant has provided false information either upon his application or in any communication with any official of the city relative to his application.
(6)
The applicant has failed to pay any fee required under this article or has otherwise failed to comply with the provisions of this article.
(b)
In addition to the criteria in subsection (a) of this section, in determining whether or not any license applied for shall be issued, the following criteria shall be considered as being relevant to the city's obligation to protect the health, safety, and welfare of the public, and in such connection, a negative finding by the city as to one or more of the following criteria shall authorize the denial of a licensure application:
(1)
Whether the applicant is a previous holder of a pawnshop business license, the manner in which he conducted the business thereunder as to the necessity for unusual or enhanced police effort, observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business;
(2)
The number of licenses already granted for similar businesses operating in the same general vicinity or neighborhood.
(Code 1986, § 14-203; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-233. - Nontransferable.
No business license granted under this article shall be transferable.
(Code 1986, § 14-204; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-234. - Revocations and suspensions of licenses.
(a)
In the event that the owner of the pawnshop business or any employee of such owner fails to comply with any of the provisions of this article, the finance director shall be authorized to suspend or revoke the pawnshop business license previously issued to such owner. In the event of such a suspension or revocation, notice of such decision shall be communicated to the owner in writing by personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested. Such notice is deemed to be effective upon personal service or upon mailing; provided, however, that such suspension or revocation shall be effective no sooner than ten calendar days from the effective date of such service or mailing. Any suspension hereunder shall be for such duration as the finance director deems appropriate, but any such suspension shall not exceed 30 calendar days. Violation of a written notice of suspension by the owner or any employee of the owner shall be grounds for revocation of the owner's pawnshop business license.
(b)
Any person whose pawnshop business license is suspended or revoked has the right to appeal such suspension or revocation to the city council within ten calendar days of the effective date of such suspension or revocation. Such appeal must be in writing, must be filed with the finance director, and must set out the grounds upon which the appellant contends that the suspension or revocation was erroneously issued. The filing of such an appeal shall constitute an automatic stay of the suspension or revocation, and such automatic stay shall continue in effect until such time as the appeal is finally adjudicated by the city council. The city manager of the city shall be notified of the filing of such an appeal and shall schedule a hearing thereon by the city council within 30 calendar days from the date of filing such appeal. In considering such appeal, the city council shall affirm the action or decision of the finance director if the city council finds that such decision was supported by any evidence.
(Code 1986, § 14-205; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004; Ord. No. O-2015-15, 7-23-2015)
Sec. 46-235. - Daily report to police required.
(a)
Every licensee shall prepare a daily report for the city police department, in such form as may be prescribed by the police department, of all property pledged to or bought by the licensee during the preceding 24 hours ending at 8:00 p.m. on the effective date of the report. In addition to any other information required by the police department, the report shall disclose:
(1)
The name and address of the licensee;
(2)
Time of transaction;
(3)
Serial numbers of pawn tickets;
(4)
Full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry, and all identifying names, marks, and numbers;
(5)
A description of persons pledging, selling, or pawning; including name, address, information from such person's driver's license or government-issued identification, including weight, height, and eye color; and
(6)
Serial numbers on items must be included in the report.
Insufficient or incomplete reports shall be rejected.
(b)
Any licensee or employee who fails or refuses to prepare and keep reports as provided in this section or makes false entries concerning a transaction or who fails or refuses to permit an inspection or examination of the report by any police officer, designated by the chief of police to conduct such inspections, shall constitute a violation of this Code, which shall be punishable as provided in this article.
(c)
Each licensee shall require any person pawning, pledging, or selling an article or articles to display government-issued identification which at a minimum contains a photograph of the person, the person's date of birth, race, and sex as well as the identification number, drivers license number and/or social security number of the person, the type of identification and the name of the city council issuing such identification. The licensee shall record all of the above information from such identification.
(d)
The failure of any licensee or employee to comply with the provisions of this section shall constitute a violation of this Code, which shall be punishable as provided in this article.
(Code 1986, § 14-206; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-236. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction or entering a plea of guilty or nolo contendere in the city municipal court, be subject to:
(1)
First offense: by a fine of not more than $600.00 or by confinement in the common jail of the county for a period of not more than 60 days or by both fine and confinement.
(2)
Second offense: by a fine of not more than $900.00 or by confinement in the common jail of the county for a period of not more than 90 days or by both fine and confinement.
(b)
Each violation of this article shall be deemed a separate offense.
(c)
Any violation of the provisions of this article may also be enjoined by the Superior Court of Sumter County in an action filed for such purpose by the city. Such injunction may be granted by said court notwithstanding that other adequate remedies at law may exist. Remedies contained in this article shall be cumulative in nature.
(d)
Any violation of the provisions of this article by a licensee hereunder, and any failure to pay any taxes or fees owed to the city by a licensee hereunder, shall also subject the business owner of the pawnshop to suspension or revocation of such owner's business license under section 46-234.
(Code 1986, § 14-207; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-237. - Disposal period for pawned, purchased articles.
Any licensee who takes goods on pawn or buys goods in its capacity as a pawnshop, taking full title thereto, shall hold such goods for at least 40 calendar days in the case of pawned goods and 15 calendar days in the case of purchased goods before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment, or otherwise unless such goods are redeemed by the owner thereof. Failure to comply with this section shall constitute a violation of this Code and shall be punishable as provided in this article.
(Code 1986, § 14-208; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-238. - Dealing with minors prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee, his agents or employees, to receive in pawn, pledge, or sale goods of any character or description from a minor. For the purpose of this article, a minor is any person who is 17 years of age or younger. Any licensee or employee thereof violating this section shall constitute a violation of this Code and shall be punishable as provided in this article.
(Code 1986, § 14-209; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-239. - Sale of prohibited weapons.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee to sell, offer for sale, or expose for sale any weapons, the sale of which are prohibited by city ordinance, state law, or federal law or regulation. Any licensee or employee thereof violating this section shall constitute a violation of this Code and shall be punishable as provided in this article.
(Code 1986, § 14-210; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-240. - Security cameras required.
(a)
Any establishment which is licensed by the city to engage in the business of operating as a pawnshop and which is open to the public for business any day of the week is required to install security cameras and recording mechanisms of a type and number capable of recording evidentiary quality images for the prosecution of criminal cases, or for such other uses as may be deemed authorized by law.
(b)
Cameras and recording mechanisms meeting the requirements of this section must be capable of producing a retrievable image on video tape or other medium that can be made a permanent record and can be enlarged through projection or other means. Cameras and recording mechanisms meeting the requirements of this section shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to inspection by the city police department.
(c)
Security videos must have a shelf life of 15 calendar days from time of daily taping. The security video must be maintained at the licensee's place of business in an unaltered state and be available for inspection by the city police department.
(d)
In the event that the security cameras and recording mechanisms become inoperative, any licensee or employee thereof shall not be in violation of this section for conducting business if the licensee is diligently pursuing repairs of such system; provided that such repairs must be completed within ten days after such system becomes inoperative.
(e)
Any licensee or employee thereof violating this section shall constitute a violation of this Code and shall be punishable as provided in this article.
(Code 1986, § 14-211; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 46-241. - Licenses constitute grant of privilege; fees; duration of licenses.
(a)
All licenses issued hereunder shall be a mere grant of a privilege to carry on a pawnshop business during the term authorized in the license subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this article and by any other ordinance, resolution, or law of the county, state, or United States relating to such business.
(b)
The cost of such pawnshop business license shall be as established from time to time by the city council and shall be paid as a regulatory fee under article IV of this chapter.
(c)
Each pawnshop business license issued pursuant to this article shall expire on December 31 of the year in which such pawnshop license was issued. Applications for renewal of such pawnshop business license shall be accepted by the finance director between January 1 and March 15 of each calendar year, but no person shall be authorized to engage in the pawnshop business beyond March 15 of any calendar year until such person's application for a new pawnshop business license and city business license is approved in accord with this article.
(Code 1986, § 14-212; Ord. No. O-03-09-25, 9-18-2003; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004)
