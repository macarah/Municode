Chapter 38 - FIRE PREVENTION AND PROTECTION
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Interference with equipment.
It shall be unlawful for any person willfully to damage, destroy or in any way interfere with any cistern, engine, hose, hose cart, truck, hooks, ladders or other apparatus, implement, fire hydrant or material of any kind connected with or belonging to the fire department for the extinguishment of fires.
(Code 1962, § 9-5; Code 1986, § 11-2)
State Law reference— Criminal trespass and damage to property, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-20 et seq.
Sec. 38-2. - Fire—In public areas.
No person shall kindle or build a fire upon any sidewalk, or in any street or public square of this city without permission of the fire department.
(Code 1962, § 9-8; Code 1986, § 11-4)
Sec. 38-3. - Same—On private lots.
It shall be unlawful for any person to burn trash, lumber, leaves, straw or any other combustible material on any private lot within the city without permission of the fire department.
(Code 1962, § 9-8; Code 1986, § 11-5)
Sec. 38-4. - Motor vehicles parking in fire lanes.
(a)
The marking of fire lanes on private property, devoted to public use, shall be approved by the fire chief and the police chief.
(b)
Parking of motor vehicles or otherwise obstructing fire lanes on private property, devoted to public use, so marked and approved, is prohibited at all times.
(c)
Any owner or user of a motor vehicle in violation of this section who pleads guilty, is found guilty, or pleads nolo contendere in the municipal court of the city shall be subject to punishment in accord with the provisions of section 1-8 of the city Code.
(Code 1962, § 9-10; Code 1986, § 11-6; Ord. No. O-10-07-23, 7-22-2010)

Sec. 38-6. - Consumer fireworks; permits.
(a)
"Consumer fireworks" means small firework devices as more particularly defined in O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1(a)(1). The term "consumer fireworks" shall not include the following:
(1)
Model rockets and model rocket engines designed, sold, and used for the purpose of preparing recoverable aero models; toy pistol paper caps in which the explosive contents average 0.25 grains or less of explosive mixture per paper cap or toy pistols; toy canons, toy canes, toy guns, or other devices using such paper caps; nor shall the term "consumer fireworks" include ammunition consumed by weapons used for sporting and hunting purposes; and
(2)
Wire or wood sparklers of 100 grams or less mixture per item; other sparkling items which are nonexplosive and nonaerial and contains 75 grams or less of chemical components per tube for a total of 500 grams or less for multiple tubes; snake and glow worms; smoke devices; or trick noise makers which include paper streamers, party poppers, string poppers, snappers, and drop pops each consisting of 0.25 grains or less of explosive mixtures.
(b)
Any person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership which desires to undertake the use or ignition of consumer fireworks within the corporate limits of the city shall be required to obtain a special use permit to do so from the city council. The cost of such permit shall be established by the city council, but such cost shall not exceed $100.00 per day for each such permit. No such permit shall be required for use or ignition of consumer fireworks on January 1, the last Saturday and Sunday in May, July 3, July 4, the first Monday in September, and December 31 of each year after the time of 10:00 a.m. and up to and including the time of 11:59 p.m., and on January 1 of each year beginning at the time of 12:00 midnight and up to and including the ending time of 1:00 a.m. The use of consumer fireworks shall be subject to the noise provisions of chapter 62, section 62-110, provided however that the decibel measurement limitations shall not be applicable to consumer fireworks used or ignited during the dates and times specified in subparagraph (b) of this section.
(c)
Such special use permits shall designate the time or times and location that such person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership may use or ignite or cause to be ignited such consumer fireworks. Each person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership and each location shall require a separate special use permit.
(d)
No such special use permit shall be issued for the use or ignition of consumer fireworks at any location where such consumer fireworks are prohibited as established by O.C.G.A. § 25-10-2(b)(3)(C) or by any other federal or state law or regulation.
(e)
No person younger than 18 years shall be authorized to use or ignite or cause to be ignited or possess, manufacture, transport, or store consumer fireworks.
(f)
Any person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership which uses, ignites, or causes to be ignited consumer fireworks at a time during which a special permit to do so is required for the location where such fireworks are used or ignited, shall be deemed to be guilty of a misdemeanor in violation of this division and, upon conviction therefor, shall be subject to punishment in accord with the provisions of chapter 1, section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2021-15, 9-23-2021)

Sec. 38-49. - Authorized.
The fire department is hereby permitted to answer calls to property lying outside the corporate limits of the city only in the event that there is in existence, at the time of the call, either: (i) an intergovernmental agreement between the governing body of the city and the governing body of the jurisdiction from which such fire call originates providing for the city fire department to answer such calls in such jurisdiction; or (ii) a mutual aid agreement between the governing body of the city and the governing body of the jurisdiction from which such fire call originates.
(Ord. No. O-10-07-23, 7-22-2010)
Sec. 38-50. - Discretion of fire chief preserved.
This article shall not impair the discretion of the chief of the fire department to answer calls on account of fire to the property of citizens adjacent to the corporate limits where such calls are necessary for the protection of property lying within the city.
(Ord. No. O-10-07-23, 7-22-2010)
Sec. 38-51. - Right of refusal.
Except where the city fire department is obligated by contract as provided in section 38-49, the permission given to the fire department to answer calls outside the city shall not be considered as obligatory or mandatory, nor shall any person have any right to compel the fire department to answer such fire call, but whenever, in the opinion of the chief of the fire department, the use of equipment and personnel to answer such calls would jeopardize the safety or welfare of property within the corporate limits, the fire department may refuse to make such calls.
(Ord. No. O-10-07-23, 7-22-2010)
Sec. 38-52. - Exception.
None of the provisions of this article shall affect contracts that the city has, or may hereafter have, with industrial and commercial plants whereby such property owners pay certain stipulated amounts to cover sprinkler connections, extensions or water mains, fire protection, etc.
(Ord. No. O-10-07-23, 7-22-2010)
