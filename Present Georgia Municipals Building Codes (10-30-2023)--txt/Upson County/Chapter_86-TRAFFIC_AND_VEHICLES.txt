Sec. 86-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Junked motor vehicle means any motor vehicle in an inoperative state by means of having been discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, or junked or any parts thereof and which does not have a current state motor vehicle tag.
Motor vehicle means a machine designed to be propelled by an internal combustion gasoline or diesel engine and intended as a means of transporting persons or property and includes without limitation automobiles, trucks, motorcycles, and tractors.
Property means any real property located within the territorial limits of the county, but not within the municipal limits of any city or town, which does not comprise a street or highway or which does not comprise the premises of any junkyard complying with the laws of this state relating to the licensing and regulating of motor vehicle junkyards.
(Ord. No. 97, § 1, 5-6-1997)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 86-32. - Unlawful conduct; nuisance.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person in charge or control of any property located in the county, whether as owner, tenant, occupant, lessee, or otherwise, to cause or allow a junked motor vehicle to remain on such property for a period of time in excess of three days. However, this article shall not apply to any vehicle stored in an enclosed building.
(b)
Any person who violates subsection (a) of this section shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(c)
For so long as a junked motor vehicle remains on such property in violation of subsection (a) of this section it shall constitute a continuing nuisance. Each 24-hour period which passes subsequent to the issuance of a citation for the violation of subsection (a) of this section without the removal of the junked motor vehicle from such property shall constitute a separate and independent violation of this article. The failure to abate such continuing nuisance shall authorize the county to take such actions as provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-60-4.
(Ord. No. 97, § 2, 5-6-1997)
Cross reference— Nuisances, § 38-61 et seq.
Sec. 86-33. - Inspections; citation for violation.
It shall be the duty of the building and zoning office to inspect or to cause to be inspected property located within the limits of the county to determine whether or not there are any violations of section 86-32. Upon discovery of any such violation, the building and zoning office shall promptly issue a citation to the offending person.
(Ord. No. 97, § 3, 5-6-1997)
Secs. 86-34—86-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PARKING, STOPPING AND STANDING

Sec. 86-61. - Parking in prohibited places.
It shall be unlawful to park a motor vehicle along the following designated sides of the following streets and roads:
(1)
The east side of Raines Street.
(2)
Either side of Zorn Street.
(3)
Either side of Knight Trail from State Route 36 to State Route 74. This prohibition on parking includes the median of the roadway.
(Ord. No. 98, § 2, 5-6-1997)

ARTICLE IV. - ONE-WAY TRAVEL

Sec. 86-91. - Lincoln Park Subdivision.
Pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371, the following one-way traffic rules shall apply to Lincoln Park Subdivision:

(2)

(Ord. No. 98, § 3, 5-6-1997)
