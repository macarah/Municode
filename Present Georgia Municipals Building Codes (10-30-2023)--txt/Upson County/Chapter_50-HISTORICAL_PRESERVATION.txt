Sec. 50-26. - Findings; established.
(a)
For the purposes of this article, the board of commissioners finds as follows:
(1)
The county is in possession of properties of historical, cultural and esthetic value;
(2)
The board of commissioners is desirous of protecting, enhancing, and preserving such properties;
(3)
The state general assembly has found that the historical, cultural, and esthetic heritage of this state is among its most valued and important assets and that the preservation of this heritage is essential to the promotion of the health, prosperity, and general welfare of the people, and has pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 44-10-20—44-10-28 provided for the establishing of a historic preservation commission; and
(4)
O.C.G.A. § 44-10-24 provides that the local governing body of the county shall determine the number of members of the commission, which shall be at least three, and the length of their terms, which shall be no greater than four years, and a majority of the members of any such commission shall have demonstrated special interest, experience, or education in history or architecture and all shall serve without compensation.
(b)
Therefore, a commission known as the Upson County Historic Preservation Commission is established, subject to this article.
(Ord. No. 47, 1-27-1989)
Sec. 50-27. - Members.
The historic preservation commission shall consist of four members who are residents of the county and have demonstrated special interest in the preservation of the county's historic properties.
(Ord. No. 47, § 1, 1-27-1989)
Sec. 50-28. - Term of office.
Members of the historic preservation commission shall be appointed by the board of commissioners to serve staggered terms of four years.
(Ord. No. 47, § 2, 1-27-1989)
Sec. 50-29. - Powers and duties.
Members of the historic preservation commission shall be authorized to:
(1)
Prepare an inventory of all property within its respective historic preservation jurisdiction having the potential for designation as historic property.
(2)
Recommend to the board of commissioners specific places, districts, sites, buildings, structures or works of art to be designated by ordinance as historic properties or historic districts.
(3)
Review applications for certificates of appropriateness and grant or deny the certificates in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 44-10-28.
(4)
Recommend to the board of commissioners that the designation of any place, district, site, building, structure, or work of art as a historic property or as a historic district be revoked or removed.
(5)
Restore or preserve any historic properties acquired by the county.
(6)
Promote the acquisition by the board of commissioners of facade easements and conservation easements in accordance with O.C.G.A. §§ 44-10-1—44-10-5.
(7)
Conduct an educational program on historic properties located within its historic preservation jurisdiction.
(8)
Make such investigations and studies of matters relating to historic preservation as the board of commissioners or the historic preservation commission itself may from time to time deem necessary or appropriate for the purposes of this article.
(9)
Seek out state and federal funds for historic preservation and make recommendations to the board of commissioners concerning the most appropriate use of any funds acquired.
(10)
Consult with historic preservation experts in the historic preservation section of the department of natural resources or its successor and the Georgia Trust for Historic Preservation, Inc.
(11)
Submit to the historic preservation section of the department of natural resources or its successor a list of historic properties or historic districts designated as such pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 44-10-26.
(12)
Adopt such rules, regulations and procedures as may be deemed necessary or desirable for the conduct of the business of the historic preservation commission.
(13)
Hold as trustee for the county such leases, agreements, titles, interest and easements to historic properties that are owned or acquired by the county.
(14)
Establish such reasonable rules, regulations and procedures as may be necessary for the enjoyment of historic properties by the general public.
(Ord. No. 47, § 3, 1-27-1989)
