ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 54-1. - Provisions saved from repeal.
Ordinance No. 45, adopted January 22, 1993, on the subject of manufactured housing regulations, is not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect pending a revision of the subject matter by the county.
Secs. 54-2—54-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - MANUFACTURED HOME DECALS AND REPORTS BY MANUFACTURED HOME PARK OPERATORS

Sec. 54-21. - Decal required.
(a)
All persons owning a manufactured home based in Upson County on January 1 of each year shall obtain from the tax commissioner a decal before May 1, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-492. This requirement pertains to all manufactured homes, including those exempt from taxation by homestead exemption or other provisions of state law.
(b)
Such decals shall be affixed to the mobile homes in such a manner as to cause them to be easily visible for inspection.
(c)
Owners of manufactured homes locating in the county after January 1 of any year shall be required to report their mobile homes to the office of the tax commissioner within ten working days.
(Ord. No. 176, § 1, 8-23-2005)
Sec. 54-22. - Reports by manufactured home park operators.
(a)
Every owner and operator of a manufactured home park is required to give the tax assessors an inventory of all manufactured homes based in the park as of October 1 of each year. This shall be done no later than October 15 of each year. The tax assessors shall make available an inventory of records as of October 1.
(b)
Every owner, manager, and operator of a manufactured home park is required to report to the tax assessors any manufactured home removal from the park prior to said home being removed, if known to the park manager, operator or owner.
(c)
Every owner, manager and operator of a manufactured home park shall furnish to the tax assessors an updated lot map of each park, and any changes shall be reported by January 1 of each year.
(d)
Every owner, manager and operator of a manufactured home park is required to notify the tax assessors of any change in ownership or of name of any manufactured home park within 30 days of such change.
(Ord. No. 176, § 2, 8-23-2005)
Sec. 54-23. - Penalties.
Any person who violates one or more of the provisions of this article shall be punished in accordance with section 1-11 of the Code of Upson County, Georgia. Generally, this provides for the punishment by a sentence not to exceed 60 days imprisonment or a $1,000.00 fine, or both.
(Ord. No. 176, § 3, 8-23-2005)
Sec. 54-24. - Administration.
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement officer of Upson County to inspect, or cause to be inspected, property located in the county to determine whether or not there are any violations of this article. Upon discovery of any such violations, the code enforcement officer may issue a citation to the offending person.
(Ord. No. 176, § 1, 8-23-2005)
