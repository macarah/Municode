ARTICLE II. - EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 12-19. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the term "emergency management" means the preparation for the carrying out of all emergency functions other than functions for which military forces are primarily responsible to prevent, minimize and repair injury and damage resulting from emergencies, energy emergencies, disasters or the imminent threat thereof, of manmade or natural origin caused by enemy attack, sabotage, acts of domestic or international terrorism, civil disturbance, fire, flood, earthquake, wind, storm, wave action, oil spill or other water contamination requiring emergency action to avert danger or damage, epidemic, air contamination, blight, drought, infestation, explosion, riot or other hostile action, radiological action, or other causes. These functions include, without limitation:
(1)
Firefighting services;
(2)
Police services;
(3)
Emergency medical services;
(4)
Rescue;
(5)
Engineering;
(6)
Warning services;
(7)
Communications;
(8)
Defense from radiological, chemical, biological and other special weapons, including weapons of mass destruction;
(9)
Evacuation of persons from stricken areas;
(10)
Emergency welfare services;
(11)
Consequence management functions, including victim services;
(12)
Emergency transportation;
(13)
Plant protection;
(14)
Temporary restoration of public utility services; and
(15)
Other functions related to civilian protection, together with all other activities necessary or incidental to the preparation for and carrying out of the foregoing functions.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(1); Code 1989, § 6-1)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 38-3-3(1).
Sec. 12-20. - Prohibited acts.
It shall be a misdemeanor punishable as provided in general penalty section 1-9, during an emergency, to:
(1)
Willfully obstruct, hinder or delay any member of the emergency management corps, or any enforcement of any lawful rule or regulation issued pursuant to this chapter or in the performance of any duty imposed by virtue of this chapter.
(2)
Do any act forbidden by any lawful rules or regulations issued pursuant to this chapter if such act is of such a nature as to give assistance to the enemy or to imperil the life or property of any inhabitants of this city or to prevent, hinder or delay the defense or protection thereof.
(3)
Wear, carry or display without authority any mark or identification specified by the emergency management agency.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(8); Code 1989, § 6-8)
Sec. 12-21. - Volunteers.
(a)
Composition. All persons other than officers and employees of the city volunteering in service pursuant to emergency management shall serve without compensation.
(b)
Immunity. While engaged in such service they shall have the same immunities as persons and employees of the city performing similar duties.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(7); Code 1989, § 6-7)
Sec. 12-22. - Assisting neighboring city.
(a)
City official determination. In the event that the city is called upon to furnish supplies, equipment, machinery and personnel to a neighboring city in distress, the general manager of the city utilities or in such manager's absence, the mayor shall be authorized to furnish said requested equipment, machinery, supplies and personnel in such official's discretion without further approval of the city council.
(b)
Liability insurance. The city is hereby requested to contact the liability insurance carrier of the city and to work out the necessary details to be sure that such liability insurance coverage will be in force on any equipment and machinery which may be furnished under this plan by the city, and carrier is further requested to take such other action as is necessary to protect any key personnel who may be furnished to a requesting city under such emergency plan.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101; Code 1989, § 6-9; Res. of 5-6-1974)

DIVISION 2. - EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT AGENCY

Sec. 12-48. - Created; composition.
The city emergency management agency is hereby created and shall include the following persons:
(1)
Mayor.
(2)
Director of emergency management, as appointed.
(3)
Police chief.
(4)
County health officer.
(5)
American Red Cross representative.
(6)
Others, including representatives of business, labor and patriotic agencies as appointed by the city council.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(2); Code 1989, § 6-2)
State Law reference— Authority, O.C.G.A. § 38-3-27.
Sec. 12-49. - Powers and duties.
It shall be the duty of the emergency management agency and it is hereby empowered:
(1)
To develop a city emergency management plan. This plan shall provide for the effective mobilization of all city resources, both private and public.
(2)
To prepare and recommend for consideration by the city council ordinances necessary to implement the emergency management plan.
(3)
To consider and recommend to the city council for approval mutual aid plans and agreements.
(4)
To perform any other function as determined locally.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(3); Code 1989, § 6-3)
Sec. 12-50. - Director's office established; duties.
There is hereby created the office of city emergency management director. The mayor shall appoint the director. The director is hereby appointed:
(1)
To represent the mayor on all matters pertaining to emergency management.
(2)
To require and direct the cooperation of city officials in the preparation and implementation of emergency management plans.
(3)
During periods of emergency, to direct the services of any city officers or employees.
(4)
During periods of emergency, to obtain vital supplies and equipment lacking, needed for the protection of life and property of people and bind the city for the value thereof and if required immediately, requisition it.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(4); Code 1989, § 6-4)
Secs. 12-51—12-73. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT CORPS

Sec. 12-74. - Composition.
Officers and employees of the city with volunteer forces enrolled to aid them during an emergency and all groups, agencies and persons who make the agreements on operation of law to be charged with the duties necessary for the protection of the life and property in the city during such an emergency shall constitute the emergency management corps.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(5); Code 1989, § 6-5)
Sec. 12-75. - Divisions established.
The functions and duties of the city emergency management corps shall be distributed among the divisions as listed in subsections (1) through (8) of this section. Each division shall be under the direction of a chief and deputy chief appointed by the emergency management agency. The chief of the divisions will include public officials, such as sheriffs, chiefs of police and fire departments, and heads of agencies dealing with transportation, communications and other sundry services as well as private business persons who through their profession, knowledge and experience are particularly qualified to head divisions requiring specialized knowledge and experience. The divisions are established as follows:
(1)
Law enforcement.
(2)
Fire services.
(3)
Transportation.
(4)
Medical and health.
(5)
Communications.
(6)
Welfare.
(7)
Information and education.
(8)
Others as determined locally.
(Comp. 1976, § 22-101(6); Code 1989, § 6-6)
