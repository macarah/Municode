DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-382. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Amusement game room means any location as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(b), (c) or (d) where one or more bona fide coin operated amusement machines are operated that permit non-cash redemption as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(d)(l)(B), (C), or a combination thereof.
Bona fide coin operated amusement machine means the same as this term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 50-27-70(b)(2)(A) and (B) and any applicable regulations of the State of Georgia. Examples of bona fide coin operated amusement machines include, but are expressly not limited to, the following:
(1)
Pinball machines;
(2)
Console machines;
(3)
Video games;
(4)
Crane machines;
(5)
Claw machines;
(6)
Pusher machines;
(7)
Bowling machines;
(8)
Novelty arcade games;
(9)
Foosball or table soccer machines;
(10)
Miniature racetrack, football or golf machines;
(11)
Target or shooting gallery machines;
12)
Basketball machines;
(13)
Shuffleboard machines;
(14)
Kiddie ride games;
(15)
Skee-Ball® machines;
(16)
Air hockey machines;
(17)
Roll down machines;
(18)
Coin operated pool table or coin operated billiard table as defined in paragraph (3) of O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1; and
(19)
Any other similar amusement machine which can be legally operated in Georgia.
The term "coin operated amusement machine" does not include the following:
(1)
Coin operated washing machines or dryers;
(2)
Vending machines which for payment of money dispense products or services;
(3)
Gas and electric meters;
(4)
Pay telephones;
(5)
Pay toilets;
(6)
Cigarette vending machines;
(7)
Coin operated vending machines;
(8)
Coin operated scales;
(9)
Coin operated gumball machines;
(10)
Coin operated television sets which provide cable or network programming;
(11)
Coin operated massage beds; and
(12)
Machines which are not legally permitted to be operated in Georgia.
(c)
Location means a business within the city that has complied with the provisions of the ordinances of the city relating to occupation taxes and business licenses.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-383. - Enforcing officer.
The chief of police or his designee is hereby designated as the enforcement officer and shall execute all requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
DIVISION 2. - PROHIBITION OF GAMBLING DEVICES AND PLACES

Sec. 10-384. - Gambling devices prohibited.
Gambling devices, as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20(2), are prohibited in the city, and the ownership, use, or transport thereof shall be a misdemeanor pursuant to state law, except as exempted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(a) through (k).
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-385. - Gambling places prohibited.
Gambling places, as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20(3), are prohibited in the city, and the operation thereof shall be a misdemeanor pursuant to state law.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
DIVISION 3. - LICENSES

Sec. 10-386. - License required.
No person, firm or corporation shall engage in the business of an owner or proprietor of an amusement game room, as the term is herein defined, without first having obtained a license under this article and complying with the requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-387. - Issuance of license.
(a)
Application for a license for operating an amusement game room within the corporate limits of the city shall be made to the city clerk upon a form to be supplied by the city clerk for this purpose. The license application shall include the following information, which shall be made under oath of a natural person submitting the application:
(1)
If the applicant is a natural person, the name, address, phone number, and age of the applicant and the date of the application;
(2)
If the applicant is a partnership, cooperative, joint venture, association, corporation or nonresident, the application shall be in the name of the resident officer, partner or associate who owns a substantial interest in the applicant or in the name of the principal resident managing officer or managing agent and the application shall show that the license is for the use of the applicant and the application shall include the name and address of the applicant, as well as the name, address, phone number, and age of the resident officer, partner or associate, managing officer, or managing agent, and the date of the application;
(3)
Address or place where the bona fide coin operated amusement machine or machines are to be offered to the public for play and the other business or businesses operated at that place or places;
(4)
Name and address of the owner of the machine or machines and a copy of the owner's master license;
(5)
Copy of the applicant's location license for the place where the bona fide coin operated amusement machine or machines are to be offered to the public for play;
(6)
Name and address of any other business owned or operated by applicant within the corporate limits of the city; and
(7)
List of any other licenses or permits from the city held by the applicant.
(b)
Upon request of a the holder of a license for an amusement game room, the city official or employee shall provide such licensee with a copy of this article.
(c)
The city shall not require a fee for licensure or registration of an amusement game room.
(d)
A license issued in accordance with this article shall be valid until December 31 of the year in which the license was issued.
(e)
An application for the renewal of a license must be made to the city by December 31 of each year.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-388. - Payment of occupation tax required.
No person, firm or corporation shall engage in the business of an owner or proprietor of amusement game room, as the term is herein defined, or shall be entitled to a license under this article, without first having completed the occupation tax certificate form and paid the required occupational tax in accordance with article II of chapter 10 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-389. - Refusal to issue or renew license.
The city shall be entitled to refuse to issue or renew a license under this article under any of the following circumstances:
(a)
The licensee or applicant has failed or refused to comply with any provision of this article;
(b)
The licensee or applicant has intentionally violated a provision of this article;
(c)
Any license or permit that is material to the operation of the amusement game room and that is issued by the Georgia Lottery Commission or any other governmental agency is revoked, suspended or no properly maintained in good standing; or
(d)
The licensee or applicant has intentionally failed to provide requested information or answer a question, intentionally made a false statement in or in connection with his or her application or renewal, or omitted any material or requested information.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-390. - Revocation of license.
In the event that it comes to the attention of the city manager that an amusement game room has created a nuisance to the surrounding community or the operator or proprietor has violated any provision of this article, the following procedure will be followed:
(a)
The city manager shall issue a notice of objection which shall include the address of the location and the specific reasons why the amusement game room in question is alleged to be a nuisance or alleged to be in violation of any provision of this section.
(b)
The city manager will then notify the proprietor that a notice of objection has been filed and will set a date for a hearing before the mayor and council. Extensions and necessary investigations will be granted and conducted at the discretion of the city clerk.
(c)
At the time of the hearing, any resident, the operator, the proprietor and the city manager or other witnesses may be heard.
(d)
After the hearing, the mayor and council shall issue a notice of decision which will call for the dismissal of the objection, removal of the machines, or such other remedy as they deem appropriate under the circumstances that are consistent with the purpose of this section.
(e)
The decision of the mayor and council shall be in writing, shall state the reasons for their decision, and shall be based upon the merits of the case.
(f)
The decision of the mayor and council shall be binding upon the proprietor and operator of the amusement game room under consideration, subject to appeal to the Superior Court of Hart County, Georgia, by petition for writ of certiorari.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-391. - No property right; licenses nontransferable.
(a)
A license issued pursuant to this article:
(1)
Shall vest no property or right in the holder of the license except to conduct the licensed or permitted business during the period the license or permit is in effect, subject to the conditions and limitations of this article and applicable law;
(2)
Shall be nontransferable and nonassignable;
(3)
Shall not be transferred or assigned to any other person, including, but not limited to, another owner at the same site within the city; and
(4)
Expires upon the death of an individual holder of a license or permit or upon the dissolution of any other holder of a license or permit.
(b)
A new owner or proprietor of an amusement game room must first obtain a new license if such owner or proprietor desires to operate an amusement game room in the same or different location in the city.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-392. - Display of license.
All businesses that have bona fide coin operated amusement machines on the premises shall display, in plain view, the current license issued by the city.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
DIVISION 4. - SUBSTANTIVE REGULATIONS

Sec. 10-393. - Distance.
(a)
Every amusement game room in the city shall comply with the proximity provision for a business licensed to sell alcohol set out in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21. After a hearing, the mayor and council may waive the application of this provision to an individual location if no alcohol is served or sold at such location.
(b)
Every amusement game room in the city shall comply with the proximity provision for businesses licensed to sell alcohol as set out in sections 4-130 and 4-247 of this Code. After a hearing, the mayor and council may waive the applicability of these provisions to an individual location if no alcohol is served at such location.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-394. - Number of bona fide coin operated amusement machines at a location.
No amusement game room in the city shall offer to the public more than nine bona fide coin operated amusement machines offering non-cash redemption in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(c) and (d)(1)(2), or both, at the same location. After a hearing, the mayor and council may, after consideration of the nature and character of the business, waive the applicability of this provision to an individual location.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-395. - Gross receipts from bona fide coin operated amusement machines and from business.
(a)
Every amusement game room shall keep records available for inspection by city officials that set out separately annual gross receipts for the amusement games and the other products and services sold at the location.
(b)
Income from the amusement game or games shall not constitute more than 50 percent of the income from the location.
(c)
Compliance with this section requires both the availability of records for inspection and compliance with the 50 percent of income requirement.
(d)
Every owner or operator of an amusement game room who is subject to O.C.G.A. § 50-27-84(b)(l) must provide to the city a copy of each verified monthly report prepared in accordance with such Code section.
(e)
Every owner or operator of an amusement game room who is subject to O.C.G.A. § 50-27-84(b)(l) shall allow the local government annually to audit the reports from the owner or operator to the Georgia Lottery Corporation.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-396. - Notice requirements.
(a)
Every amusement game room shall post a conspicuous sign with the following or similar words:
GEORGIA LAW PROHIBITS GIVING OR RECEIPT OF ANY MONEY FOR WINNING A GAME OR GAMES ON AN AMUSEMENT MACHINE; GIVING OR RECEIPT OF MONEY FOR FREE REPLAYS WON ON AN AMUSEMENT MACHINE; GIVING OR RECEIPT OF MONEY FOR ANY MERCHANDISE, PRIZE, TOY, GIFT CERTIFICATE, OR NOVELTY WON ON ANY AMUSEMENT MACHINE; OR AWARDING ANY MERCHANDISE, PRIZE, TOY, GIFT CERTIFICATE, OR NOVELTY OF A VALUE EXCEEDING $5.00 FOR A SINGLE PLAY OF AN AMUSEMENT MACHINE.
(b)
Every amusement game room shall conspicuously and permanently post the license issued by the city pursuant to this article.
(c)
The owner or proprietor of each amusement game room shall inform every employee of the acts and omissions prohibited by O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 and by this article, and of the penalties for violation of O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 and this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-397. - Compliance with O.C.G.A. provisions relating to master licenses, location licenses, and stickers for individual machines.
(a)
Bona fide coin operated amusement machines may be used in an amusement game room within the city only if:
(1)
The machines are owned by a person who holds a valid master license in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-27-71;
(2)
Each machine offered to the public for play has a valid permit sticker in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-27-78; and
(3)
The business owner where the machines are available for play by the public has paid a location license fee in order to obtain a valid location license in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-27-71(a.l) and (b).
(b)
The city official in charge of issuing business licenses shall notify the state commissioner of revenue of any observed violation of O.C.G.A. § 50-27-71 or § 50-27-78.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-398. - Operating regulations.
All businesses operating as an amusement game room hereunder shall be subject to the following regulations:
(a)
Devices to be kept in plain view; gambling devices prohibited. All machines shall at all times be kept and placed in plain view of and open and accessible to any person(s) who may frequent or be in any place of business where such machines are kept or used. Bona fide coin operated amusement machines shall be placed so that the decal issued to show payment of the permit fee is in plain view. Nothing in this article shall be construed to authorize, permit or license any gambling device of any nature whatsoever.
(b)
Inspection. The licensee shall permit the chief of police or his designee to inspect or cause the inspection of any place or building in which any bona fide coin operated amusement machines are operated or set up for operating, and to inspect, investigate and test such bona fide coin operated amusement machines and inspect any records concerning such bona fide coin operated amusement machines as needed.
(c)
Attendant required. It shall be unlawful for any proprietor to open his business to the public unless an attendant is present. Said attendant shall be of sufficient mental and physical capacity so as to be able to provide aid to patrons if needed or desired. Said attendant shall not be less than 18 years of age.
(d)
Loitering. As used in this section, "loitering" shall mean remaining idle in essentially one location and shall include the concepts of spending time idly, loafing or walking about aimlessly. It shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation licensed to operate an amusement game room to permit loitering on or in the immediate vicinity of any machine or business premises regulated hereunder in such a manner as to:
(1)
Create or cause to be created a danger of a breach of the peace;
(2)
Create or cause to be created any disturbance or annoyance to the comfort and repose of any person;
(3)
Obstruct the free passage of pedestrians or vehicles; or
(4)
Obstruct, molest or interfere with any person lawfully in a public place.
(e)
Shirt and shoes required. All proprietors shall require shirts and shoes to be worn at all times by any person frequenting their premises.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-399. - Payment of city taxes required.
No person, firm or corporation shall engage in the business of an owner or proprietor of amusement game room, as the term is herein defined, or shall be entitled to a license under this article, unless such person, firm or corporation shall pay any and all taxes owed to the city in a timely manner.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-400. - Compliance with state law.
(a)
A licensee shall comply with any and all laws and regulations of the State of Georgia applicable to bona fide coin operated amusement machines, including the provisions of Article 3 of Chapter 27 of Title 50 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, and any and all regulations promulgated thereunder, and any and all directives and orders of the Georgia Lottery Commission and any other governmental agency.
(b)
A licensee shall maintain all licenses required by the State of Georgia for the operation of an amusement game room.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
DIVISION 5. - PENALTIES

Sec. 10-400A. - Penalties for violations by owners or operators of amusement game rooms.
(a)
In addition to penalties set out in the O.C.G.A. for failure to comply with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(a) through (k), the owner or operator of an amusement game room, after a hearing before the mayor and council, may be subject to the following penalties:
(1)
First Offense: Suspension for not more than six months of the owner or proprietor's license for offering any amusement game at the location where the violation occurred.
(2)
Second Offense: Suspension for not more than 12 months of the owner or proprietor's license for offering any amusement game at the Location where the violation occurred.
(3)
Third Offense: Suspension or permanent revocation of the owner or proprietor/operator's license for offering any amusement game at the location where the violation occurred, and suspension of other permits and licenses granted by the city for not more than six months.
(b)
Penalties for violation of any of the provisions of this article by the owner or operator of an amusement game room, after conviction in the municipal court of the city, are as follows:
(1)
First Offense: Fine not to exceed $500.00 for each violation.
(2)
Second Offense: Fine not to exceed $750.00 for each violation, suspension of the owner or operator's license for offering any amusement game at the location for not more than three months, or both.
(3)
Third offense: Fine not to exceed $1,000.00 for each violation, suspension or permanent revocation of the owner or operator's license for offering any amusement game at the location, or suspension of other permits and licenses granted by the city for not more than six months, or any combination of these penalties.
(c)
The fines listed in the penalties for violation of this article may be imposed by the judge of the municipal court of the city.
(d)
Suspension or revocation of the owner or operator's license for offering any amusement game at the location where the violation occurred, and suspension of other permits and licenses granted by the city, may be imposed by the mayor and council after a hearing before the mayor and council. The mayor and council shall be authorized to make their own findings concerning any violations of this article and to suspend or revoke any license issued by the city, as the mayor and council deem appropriate, in the discretion of the mayor and council, consistent with this article, without a conviction in municipal court, and it shall not be necessary for the municipal court or any court to make any finding concerning any alleged violation for the mayor and city council to make a finding concerning such alleged violation and to suspend or revoke any license issued by the city.
(e)
Offering one or more bona fide coin operated amusement machine games in violation of an order suspending or revoking the license for the offering of any amusement game at the location is punishable, after conviction in the municipal court of the city, by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, imprisonment not to exceed 30 days, or both such fine and imprisonment.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
Sec. 10-400B. - Penalties for violations by those who play bona fide coin operated machines in violation of law or ordinance.
The municipal court of the city is authorized to impose the following penalties on any person convicted of receiving money as a reward for the successful play or winning of any bona fide coin operated amusement machine from any person owning, possessing, controlling or overseeing such bona fide coin operated amusement machine or any person employed by or acting on behalf of a person owning, possessing, controlling or overseeing a bona fide coin operated amusement machines:
(1)
First offense: Fine not to exceed $250.00 for each violation.
(2)
Second and subsequent offense: Fine not to exceed $500.00 for each violation.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 1-6-2014)
