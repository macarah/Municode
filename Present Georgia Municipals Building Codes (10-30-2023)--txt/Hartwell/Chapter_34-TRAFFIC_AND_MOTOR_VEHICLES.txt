ARTICLE II. - OPERATION OF VEHICLES

Sec. 34-19. - State uniform rules of road adopted; penalty.
(a)
The Uniform Rules of the Road (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1) and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby incorporated and adopted by reference as the city's traffic regulations as if fully set forth in this section.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished as provided in general penalty section 1-9.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-120; Code 1989, § 16-1; Ord. of 10-4-1982; Ord. of 11-6-1989)
Sec. 34-20. - Reckless driving.
Any person who drives or causes to be driven any motor vehicle upon the streets, highways, private streets, public parks, drive-ins, parking lots, schoolyards or other places of public gathering within the city carelessly and heedlessly, and in such a manner as to be in willful or wanton disregard of the safety of persons or property, shall be deemed guilty of the offense of reckless driving.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-122; Code 1989, § 16-41; Ord. of 6-6-1955(1), § 1)

ARTICLE III. - TRAFFIC CONTROL

Sec. 34-44. - One-way streets during specified hours.
Each day that the Hart County Middle School and Hart County High School are open for student attendance, the following streets located in the city shall be open for traffic in only one direction between the hours of 7:45 a.m. to 8:30 a.m. and between the hours of 3:00 p.m. to 3:30 p.m., as set forth in subsections (1) through (3) of this section:
(1)
Powell Road between Fairview Avenue and Campbell Drive shall be one-way, and traffic shall proceed in a westerly direction from Fairview Avenue to Campbell Drive.
(2)
Campbell Drive between East Franklin Street and Fifth Street shall be one-way, and traffic shall proceed in a northerly direction from Fifth Street to East Franklin Street.
(3)
Traffic on Fifth Street and First Street shall be two-way at all times.
(Code 1989, § 16-42; Ord. of 8-31-1990; Ord. No. 98-006, 8-3-1998)
Sec. 34-45. - Left turns prohibited at certain intersections.
Traffic, except pedestrians, facing a steady circular red signal may not make a left turn from the lefthand lane of a one-way street onto a one-way street onto which traffic moves toward the driver's left at the following intersections:
(1)
Forest Avenue and Franklin Street.
(2)
Franklin Street and Carolina Avenue.
(3)
Carolina Avenue and Howell Street.
(4)
Howell Street and Forest Avenue.
(Code 1989, § 16-43; Ord. of 12-3-1990)

ARTICLE V. - PARKING RESTRICTIONS

Sec. 34-93. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person violating any provision of this article shall appear before the municipal court and upon conviction shall be subject to a fine of not less than $10.00 or more than $25.00, subject to the provisions of section 34-94.
(b)
Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (a) of this section, any person cited for the violation of any such provision may, in lieu of court appearance, post a cash bond of $5.00 within 48 hours of receipt of citation and summons. Such cash bond will increase to $10.00 48 hours after receipt of citation and summons. Failure to pay such cash bond prior to the appearance date of such summons will require attendance in municipal court as set out in this section.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-124-B; Code 1989, § 16-25; Ord. of 5-4-1987; Ord. of 8-7-1989; Ord. No. 2001-006, § 3, 8-6-2001)
Sec. 34-94. - Increased bond amount.
(a)
When the records of the police department or records of the state crime information center or other appropriate agency disclose that any person has been cited ten or more times in a continuous 12-month period for violations of this article, the cash bond provided in section 34-93(a) shall be $25.00.
(b)
For each subsequent violation by the same violator, the cash bond for violations of this article shall remain at $25.00 per violation until such time as the violator has had no violations of this article for a continuous period of 90 days.
(c)
Notice of application of the increased cash bond amount shall be provided to the violator by the police department through certified mail.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-124-B; Code 1989, § 16-25; Ord. of 5-4-1987; Ord. of 8-7-1989; Ord. No. 2001-006, § 3, 8-6-2001)
Sec. 34-95. - Immobilization of vehicle.
(a)
If the owner of a vehicle that has been cited as illegally parking ten times within a 12-month period fails to pay the parking citations, within five days of receiving the tenth parking citation, the person shall satisfy all delinquent parking tickets or be subject to an additional penalty or fine of $50.00. In addition, the police department shall immobilize the vehicle through the use of a wheel lock or similar device.
(b)
The wheel lock or similar device shall be removed upon payment of all previous parking fines for that person and a fee of $50.00 for the removal of the device and posting of bond as provided in this article.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-124-B; Code 1989, § 16-25; Ord. of 5-4-1987; Ord. of 8-7-1989; Ord. No. 2001-006, § 3, 8-6-2001)
Sec. 34-96. - Parking so as to block alley.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle in front of any alley in the city so that the parking blocks normal traffic in and out of the alley.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-116; Code 1989, § 16-20; Ord. of 11-6-1950(2), § 1)
Sec. 34-97. - Parking within painted lines or markings.
Each vehicle parking along side of or next to the curb on either side of a street shall be parked within the lines or markings so established. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section to park any vehicle in such a position that the same shall not be entirely within the area so designated by such lines and markings.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-103(3); Code 1989, § 16-26)
Sec. 34-98. - Fifteen minute parking zones.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle in front of the United States Post Office in the city for a continuous period in excess of 15 minutes:
(1)
In any of the parking spaces on the north side of Franklin Street, between the midpoint of the block between Forest Avenue and Carolina Street and the intersection of Carolina Street and Franklin Street; or
(2)
In any of the parking spaces on the east side of Carolina Street between the intersection of Carolina Street and Franklin Street and the rear of the United States post office. The city manager and chief of police shall cause the spaces that shall be included in this zone to be marked. The selection of spaces that fall within the zone as described in this section, and the manner of marking of such spaces, shall be in the judgment and discretion of the city manager and police chief.
(Ord. No. 2001-006, § 2, 8-6-2001)
Sec. 34-99. - Two-hour parking zones.
The following two-hour parking zones shall be in effect between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. on Monday through Friday, on the following streets in the city:
(1)
On the east and west sides of North and South Forest Avenue, beginning at East Johnson Street and running south to Railroad Street.
(2)
On the north and south sides of Depot Street, beginning at South Forest Avenue and extending west to Jackson Street.
(3)
On the north and south sides of West Howell and East Howell Street, beginning at Jackson Street and extending east to Carter Street.
(4)
On the north and south sides of West Franklin and East Franklin Street, beginning at the midpoint of the block between Jackson Street and Carolina Street and extending to Carter Street.
(5)
On the east and west sides of Carolina Street, beginning at Depot Street and extending to Johnson Street.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-124-B; Code 1989, § 16-21; Ord. of 5-4-1987; Ord. No. 2001-006, § 1, 8-6-2001)
Sec. 34-100. - Parking time limits; offenses.
No vehicle shall be parked for a continuous period of over two hours at or in the same parking space in any area designated as a two-hour parking zone by the city, except vehicles of the physically handicapped. The exceeding of each segment of time or fraction thereof beyond the initial lawful unit of time shall constitute a separate violation under this section.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-124-B; Code 1989, § 16-24; Ord. of 5-4-1987)
Sec. 34-101. - Removal of illegally parked vehicles.
If any such vehicle remains continuously for a period of over 48 hours at or in the same parking space in any such area designated as a two-hour parking zone by the city, police chief or members of the police department shall cause the same to be moved and placed in storage at such place as may be designated by the police chief. The owner or the operator of the vehicle shall bear all costs of moving the vehicle and the storage costs, if any, thereof.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-124-B; Code 1989, § 16-24; Ord. of 5-4-1987)
Sec. 34-102. - Altering tire markings prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove, erase or alter any tire markings, chalk or otherwise, placed by the police department on any vehicle parked in any area designated as a two-hour parking zone with the intent to prevent the enforcement of two-hour parking within such zones.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-124-B; Code 1989, § 16-24; Ord. of 5-4-1987)
