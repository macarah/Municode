ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 28-1. - Garbage service and payment of fees required.
All occupied residences will receive garbage service and pay garbage fees as charged on the utility bill.
(Comp. 1976, § 8-102; Code 1989, § 10-140; Ord. of 3-14-1985, § 1)
Sec. 28-2. - Service terminated for nonpayment.
Failure to pay the charge on the utility bill will result in termination of all utility services.
(Comp. 1976, § 8-102; Code 1989, § 10-141; Ord. of 3-15-1985, § 2)
Sec. 28-3. - Receptacle use and restrictions.
(a)
The Poly Kart is to be used only for normal household garbage.
(b)
The load limit is 200 pounds.
(c)
Wastes not acceptable for the Poly Kart are:
(1)
Poisons, acids, caustics, explosives and volatile liquids, such as gasoline, oil and paint;
(2)
Hot coals and ashes;
(3)
Building rubbish or materials and furniture;
(4)
Soil, rocks and automobile and vehicle tires.
(d)
The Poly Kart shall be placed near the street on the scheduled collection day by 8:00 a.m.
(e)
Exemption from placing the Poly Kart near the street will be made by the city only when an attending physician certifies that all persons occupying the site are unable to perform this act.
(f)
The Poly Kart may not be emptied for the following reasons:
(1)
The kart is too heavy;
(2)
Unlawful materials were found in kart; and
(3)
The kart was not at the curb when truck arrived.
(Comp. 1976, § 8-102; Code 1989, § 10-145; Ord. of 3-15-1985, § 6)
Sec. 28-4. - Nuisance created.
It shall be unlawful and constitute a nuisance for any person to create or cause an unhealthy accumulation of garbage by willfully failing to place garbage for collection as set out in section 28-3.
(Comp. 1976, § 8-102; Code 1989, § 10-146; Ord. of 3-15-1985, § 7)
Sec. 28-5. - Collection of commercial and industrial waste.
(a)
All occupied commercial and industrial businesses located within the city limits will receive solid waste disposal services and pay fees as charged on the utility bill except those commercial and industrial businesses which are not now being serviced because of the inability of the city to serve said commercial or industrial businesses due to equipment limitations. For purposes of this section, the term "commercial businesses" includes public housing and mobile home parks.
(b)
The city will set fees based on the following:
(1)
A base fee.
(2)
A pickup fee.
(3)
A landfill fee.
(c)
Failure to pay the charges for solid waste disposal on the utility bill will result in the termination of all utility services.
(d)
Unacceptable waste:
(1)
Dangerous materials or substances such as poisons, acids, caustics, explosives and volatile liquids such as gasoline, oil and paint.
(2)
Hot coals and ashes.
(3)
Building rubbish or materials.
(4)
Furniture and appliances.
(e)
If unacceptable waste is found in the solid waste collected at any site the city will not pick up waste at such site until it is assured that future collection at said site will not contain unacceptable waste.
(Code 1989, § 10-147; Ord. of 12-7-1992)
Secs. 28-6—28-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - GARBAGE COLLECTION CONTAINERS

Sec. 28-29. - Maintenance; damage; replacement costs.
(a)
The Poly Kart will be furnished to all residents at no charge, and will be maintained by the city unless damaged by improper use or negligence. When the Poly Kart no longer functions properly due to normal wear and use, the city will replace it at no charge.
(b)
Any cost of Poly Kart repair or replacement not caused by normal and proper usage, including being lost or stolen, will be charged to the responsible resident on such resident's utility bill. The cost may be prorated over a period of time upon request of the resident if it exceeds $10.00, but in no circumstance will such period exceed 12 months.
(Comp. 1976, § 8-102; Code 1989, §§ 10-142, 10-143; Ord. of 3-15-1985, §§ 3, 4)
Sec. 28-30. - Resident to notify utility office when changing addresses.
The resident shall not move the Poly Kart when changing his address. The resident must notify the city utility office of the move and the sanitation department will pick up the kart.
(Comp. 1976, § 8-102; Code 1989, § 10-144; Ord. of 3-15-1985, § 5)
