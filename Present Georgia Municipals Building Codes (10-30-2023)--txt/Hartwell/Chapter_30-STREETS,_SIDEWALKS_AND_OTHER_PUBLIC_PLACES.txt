ARTICLE II. - STREET USE AND OBSTRUCTIONS

Sec. 30-19. - Obstructing street prohibited; nuisance; abatement.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct or maintain an obstruction in a street or public way in the city by a fence or otherwise in violation of state law, the city Charter or city ordinances. Such action is adjudged a nuisance and its abatement may be made by the city at the owner's or other person's expense upon failure of such owner or other person to abate the same within ten days after written notice from the city to do so.
(Comp. 1976, § 19-105; Code 1989, § 15-10; Ord. of 11-1-1982)
State Law reference— Obstructing streets, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-43.
Sec. 30-20. - Goods, wares and merchandise on sidewalks prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person engaged in business in the city, or otherwise, to store any type of goods, wares or merchandise on the city's sidewalks that would extend more than 24 inches from the building on such sidewalk.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-107; Code 1989, § 15-11)
Sec. 30-21. - Unlawful to tack, attach, etc., posters to poles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tack, attach, paste or place a poster on any pole located on the public streets and sidewalks of the city.
(Comp. 1976, § 7-107; Code 1989, § 15-12)
State Law reference— Damaging utility property, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25.
Sec. 30-22. - Private driveway damaged during repair of street or utility line.
Private driveways located on public rights-of-way shall be permitted and shall be constructed and maintained solely at the property owners' expense. If it becomes necessary for the city to repair any street or utility line that damages any private driveway, the city will, at its expense replace only that part of the driveway that is damaged by the city.
(Code 1989, § 15-13; Ord. of 12-7-1992)

ARTICLE III. - STREET DEDICATION

Sec. 30-48. - Rules and regulations promulgated by planning commission.
The planning commission shall be authorized to set up rules and regulations with regard to the procedure to be practiced in submitting any proposed streets, roads or alleys to the planning commission, subject to the provisions of this article.
(Comp. 1976, § 11-106; Code 1989, § 15-6; Ord. of 7-2-1956, § 2)
Sec. 30-49. - Plan to be submitted.
Before any street or road is opened or extended by private authority, or before any new street, road or alley is laid out in any subdivision for residential, business or industrial uses, and before the city council shall consider acceptance of any new street, road or alley, the person proposing to open or extend the street, road or alley shall submit a detailed plan of the same with such pertinent information or data as may be required by the planning commission to the commission. The commission shall carefully examine the plan with regard to its nature and the purpose, width, character and location of the street, road or alley.
(Comp. 1976, § 11-106; Code 1989, § 15-5; Ord. of 7-2-1956, § 1)
Sec. 30-50. - Report.
The planning commission shall make a report on each application for a proposed street, road or alley to the city council with its recommendation of approval or disapproval in writing, and the city council shall either approve or disapprove such plan.
(Comp. 1976, § 11-106; Code 1989, § 15-7; Ord. of 7-2-1956, § 3)
Sec. 30-51. - City council approval required.
No street, road or alley shall be accepted by the city or joined onto any existing public street or road without the city council's approval.
(Comp. 1976, § 11-106; Code 1989, § 15-8; Ord. of 7-2-1956, § 4)

ARTICLE IV. - EXCAVATIONS

Sec. 30-76. - Permit required.
Whenever it shall be necessary for any property owner or other person to cut into any street, sidewalk, curb or gutter for any reason, it shall be necessary to first obtain a permit from the city council. It shall be the duty of the permittee to replace the surface in as good condition as it was before the excavation was made.
(Comp. 1976, § 11-104; Code 1989, § 15-9; Ord. of 5-23-1927)
Sec. 30-77. - Duty to repair surface.
It shall be the duty of the permittee to replace the surface in as good condition as it was before the excavation was made.
(Comp. 1976, § 11-104; Code 1989, § 15-9; Ord. of 5-23-1927)

ARTICLE V. - PARADES

Sec. 30-98. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the term "parade," "procession" or "other public demonstration" does not include a funeral, wedding or city-sponsored procession provided with a police escort.
(Comp. 1976, § 18-105-A; Code 1989, § 12-20; Ord. of 6-24-1986, § 1(a))
Sec. 30-99. - Permit—Required.
It shall be unlawful to organize or hold, or to assist in organizing or holding, or to take part or participate in, any parade, procession or other public demonstration on the streets, sidewalks or other public ways of the city, unless otherwise allowed by state or federal law without first obtaining a written permit for the allowance of the same from the city.
(Comp. 1976, § 18-105-A; Code 1989, § 12-21; Ord. of 6-24-1986, § 1(a))
Sec. 30-100. - Same—Application.
A written application for such permit shall be made to the city manager at least 45 days prior to the date of the parade, procession or other public demonstration, indicating:
(1)
The hour, date, proposed route over which it is desired to have the parade, procession or other public demonstration pass;
(2)
The probable number of persons and/or vehicles engaged in such parade, procession or other public demonstration; and
(3)
Specify each street, sidewalk or other public way over, along or in which it is desired to have or hold such parade, procession or other public demonstration.
(Comp. 1976, § 18-105-A; Code 1989, § 12-22; Ord. of 6-24-1986, § 1(b))
Sec. 30-101. - Same—Issuance, contents.
(a)
The city council shall act upon the application no later than 45 days after the application is submitted to the city manager. In acting on the permit, the city council shall either deny the permit or issue a permit allowing such parade, procession or other public demonstration during daylight hours only (unless the application specifically requested other than daylight hours, and the permit specifically grants the request of other than daylight hours).
(b)
Such permit shall prescribe the route of such parade, procession or other public demonstration by listing the streets, sidewalks or other public ways over which the parade, procession or other public demonstration shall pass.
(c)
The written permit shall set forth the basis for any modification of the requested route. Any modification of a requested route shall be based on a consideration of the public welfare, peace, safety, health, good order and convenience of the general public necessitating regulations of pedestrian and vehicular traffic along the proposed route.
(Comp. 1976, § 18-105-A; Code 1989, § 12-23; Ord. of 6-24-1986, § 1(b))
Sec. 30-102. - Unlawful to deviate from prescribed route.
It shall be unlawful to use for such purposes any street, sidewalk or public way other than those set out in such permit.
(Comp. 1976, § 18-105-A; Code 1989, § 12-24; Ord. of 6-24-1986, § 1(b))
Sec. 30-103. - Appeal from route modification order.
(a)
Where the written permit issued modifies the proposed route of the parade, procession or other public demonstration as originally set forth in the application, the applicant may appeal such modification to the city council by filing, within ten days of the issuance of the permit modifying the proposed route, a written notice with the city clerk setting forth objections to the modification. The city council shall review such notice and issue a decision within ten days.
(b)
The city council shall have the power to amend or modify the written permit in any particular.
(Comp. 1976, § 18-105-A; Code 1989, § 12-25; Ord. of 6-24-1986, § 1(b))
