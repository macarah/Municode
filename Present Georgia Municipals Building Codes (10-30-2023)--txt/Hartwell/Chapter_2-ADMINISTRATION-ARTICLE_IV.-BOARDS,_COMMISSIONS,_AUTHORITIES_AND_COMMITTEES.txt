ARTICLE IV. - BOARDS, COMMISSIONS, AUTHORITIES AND COMMITTEES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-68. - Standing committees.
There shall be such standing committees as may be established from time to time by the city council. All committees, committee chairpersons and officers of the city council shall be appointed by the mayor and shall serve at the mayor's pleasure. The mayor shall have the power to appoint new members to any committee at any time.

Sec. 2-93. - Established; purpose.
In order to guide and accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the city which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote the public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity and the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development, the municipal planning commission is hereby created and established as authorized by law. Such commission shall be organized and empowered as provided in this division.
(Comp. 1976, § 3-703; Code 1989, § 14-1; Ord. of 5-5-1958)
Sec. 2-94. - Composition; terms; vacancy; compensation.
(a)
The city planning commission shall have not less than five or more than 12 members appointed by the city council.
(b)
A majority of the members of the planning commission shall be citizens who hold no other public office or position in the city or county except that one member may be a member of the board of zoning appeals.
(c)
Citizen members shall be appointed for overlapping terms of not less than three or more than five years or thereafter until their successors are appointed.
(d)
Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by city council, which shall have the authority to remove any member for cause on written charges after a public hearing.
(e)
All members shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for the actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Comp. 1976, § 3-703; Code 1989, § 14-2; Ord. of 5-5-1958, § 1)
Sec. 2-95. - Election of officers; rules; staff; finances.
(a)
The city planning commission shall elect its chairperson from among its members. The term of the chairperson shall be one year with eligibility for reelection.
(b)
The city planning commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the city.
(c)
The commission shall adopt rules for transactions, findings and determinations, which record shall be a public record, and shall determine its time of meeting.
(d)
The commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require.
(e)
The expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the city council.
(Comp. 1976, § 3-703; Code 1989, § 14-3; Ord. of 5-5-1958, § 2)
Sec. 2-96. - Powers.
From and after the time the city planning commission shall have organized and selected its officers together with the adoption of its rules or procedure, it shall have all the powers, duties and responsibilities as provided by law.
(Comp. 1976, § 3-703; Code 1989, § 14-4; Ord. of 5-5-1958, § 3)

DIVISION 3. - STREET TREE COMMITTEE

Sec. 2-121. - Established; composition.
An administrative committee called the "Street Tree Committee" is hereby established. The committee shall consist of nine members. Three of the members shall be nominated by the city, three members shall be nominated by the Hartwell Garden Club Council, and three members shall be nominated by the Hartwell/Hart Clean and Beautiful Board. The Hartwell/Hart Clean and Beautiful Board shall always choose as one of its nominees the Hart County Extension Service County Agent. All nominees except the Hart County Extension Service County Agent are subject to the city council's approval. The street tree committee shall elect a chairperson and any other officers it may desire to create.
(Comp. 1976, § 17-125; Code 1989, § 15-1; Ord. of 10-1-1983, § 1; Ord. of 11-6-1995)
Sec. 2-122. - Terms.
The nine-member committee shall be have terms as follows:
(1)
Five members shall be appointed for a term of two years (two members from the city, two members from the Hartwell Garden Club Council, and one member from Hartwell/Hart Clean and Beautiful Board);
(2)
Three members shall be appointed for a term of one year (one from the city, one from the Hartwell Garden Club Council, and one from Hartwell/Hart Clean and Beautiful Board);
(3)
One member, the Hart County Extension Service County Agent, shall be a permanent member.
(Comp. 1976, § 17-125; Code 1989, § 15-1; Ord. of 10-1-1983, § 1; Ord. of 11-6-1995)
Sec. 2-123. - Authority; rules and regulations.
The street tree committee shall have the authority to adopt rules and regulations as may be necessary for the purpose of carrying out its functions. Regulations for the planting, care, pruning and removal of trees shall not only be aimed at the elimination of economic waste by reason of damage to public property and the property of others in the interest of public health, safety and welfare, but also for the aesthetic appearance of streets, highways, parks and other public areas in the city.
(Comp. 1976, § 17-125; Code 1989, § 15-2; Ord. of 10-1-1983, § 2)
Sec. 2-124. - Duties.
The street tree committee shall have the right and duty to care for and protect all trees and shrubs now or hereafter existing on public property. Permission must be obtained from the street tree committee to remove or plant any trees or shrubs on public property. Private property owners will be notified by the committee if trees or shrubs on their property constitute a public nuisance or are affecting the public health, safety or welfare.
(Comp. 1976, § 17-125; Code 1989, § 15-3; Ord. of 10-1-1983, § 3; Ord. of 11-6-1995)

DIVISION 4. - ETHICS COMMITTEE

Sec. 2-147. - Composition.
The ethics committee shall consist of three persons, one appointed by the mayor, one appointed by the city council, and the third appointed by the two appointed members subject to approval of a majority of the city council. The third member shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2001-008, § 1(13-80), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 2-148. - Qualifications; compensation; term.
All members shall be residents of the city and shall serve, without compensation, for a two-year term, or until their successors are appointed.
(Ord. No. 2001-008, § 1(13-80), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 2-149. - Complaint procedure.
(a)
All complaints against city councilmembers shall be filed with the ethics committee. The ethics committee may require that oral complaints, and complaints illegibly or informally drawn, be reduced to a memorandum of complaint in such form as may be prescribed by the city council.
(b)
Upon receipt of a complaint in proper form, the ethics committee shall review it to determine whether the complaint is unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or fails to state facts sufficient to invoke the city council's disciplinary jurisdiction.
(c)
The ethics committee shall be empowered to collect evidence and information concerning any complaint and to add the findings and results of its investigation to the file containing such complaint. The ethics committee shall be empowered to conduct probable cause investigations, take evidence and hold hearings where provided in the rules.
(d)
Upon completion of its investigation of a complaint, the ethics committee shall be empowered to dismiss those complaints which are unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded, or which fail to state facts sufficient to invoke the city council's disciplinary jurisdiction; provided, however, that a rejection of such complaint by the ethics committee shall not deprive the complaining party of any action he might otherwise have at law or in equity against the respondent government servant.
(e)
The ethics committee shall be empowered to adopt forms for formal complaints, subpoenas, notices, applications for reinstatement and any other written instrument necessary or desirable under these rules.
(Ord. No. 2001-008, § 1(13-81), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 2-150. - Additional regulations.
This section shall be cumulative to any other ordinance, resolution or act now existing. Specifically, this section is in addition to section 2.14 of the Charter.
(Ord. No. 2001-008, § 1(13-82), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 2-151. - Appeals.
Any final decision by the city council pursuant to this code of ethics for city councilmembers shall be reviewable by the county superior court. The review by the superior court shall be limited to an inquiry of whether there was any evidence before the city council, which supported the council's decision. Notwithstanding the foregoing, however, no city council action refusing or failing to take action pursuant to this code of ethics shall be reviewable by the superior court.
(Ord. No. 2001-008, § 1(13-83), 8-6-2001)
Secs. 2-152—2-170. - Reserved.
