Chapter 15.48 - RECREATIONAL VEHICLE PARKS
Sections:

15.48.010 - Purpose and intent.
This chapter is enacted for the following purposes:
(a)
Provide adequate sites for temporary parking of recreational vehicles whose occupants are visiting or passing through the area of Metter, Georgia.
(b)
Minimize the adverse impacts between a recreational vehicle park and surrounding land uses.
(c)
Provide health and safety standards to protect both the users of the park and the community.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.020 - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Building inspector acts as the zoning administrator for the City of Metter. This person shall be responsible for administering this chapter.
Recreation vehicle is a vehicular portable structure built on a chassis, designed to be either pulled by another vehicle or self-propelled, to be used as a temporary dwelling for travel and recreation purposes. The term "recreational vehicle" includes the terms "RV," "motor home," "travel trailer" and related wheeled recreational equipment not designed for year-around occupancy.
Recreational vehicle park is any property where one or more lots are rented to users of recreational vehicles and which occupied for temporary purpose.
Recreational vehicle site is a plot of ground within a recreational vehicle park for one recreational vehicle, automobile and camping party.
Travel trailer, self contained is a trailer, which can operate independently of connection to sewer, water and electric systems. It contains a water-flushed toilet, lavatory, shower or bath and kitchen sink, all of which are connected to water storage and sewage holding tanks located within the trailer.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.030 - Administration.
The building inspector shall be responsible for the general administration of this chapter.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.040 - Permitted land use districts.
Recreational vehicle parks are permitted in the HOC, HOC-1A Districts. They may be permitted in the PEH, CR, and L-1 Districts with a conditional use permit, if granted from the zoning board of appeals.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.050 - Review process.
All new recreational vehicle parks or developments on any recreational vehicle park, new or pre-existing, must submit all plans and specifications in detail for such development to the City of Metter Building Official. No construction or development shall be commenced until a building permit is issued upon approval by the building official and planning commission verifying that said plans and specifications are in compliance with this title.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.060 - Site plan requirements.
A.
Plan submission. Before any permit is issued for construction and/or operation of any recreation vehicle park, a site plan and required documentation shall be submitted to and approved by the building inspector and the planning commission. The plan shall be prepared by a registered land surveyor or a registered professional engineer, shall be drawn to a scale of no less than 1" = 100', and shall include as a minimum the following:
1.
Name, address and telephone number of applicant.
2.
Location, address and legal description of the entire proposed recreation vehicle park site.
3.
Existing zone of subject property and all adjacent properties.
4.
Names and addresses of adjacent property owners.
5.
Complete engineering plans and specifications of the proposed recreational vehicle park showing:
a.
The area and dimensions of the entire tract of land.
b.
The land uses occupying the adjacent properties.
c.
The number, size and location of the proposed vehicle sites and other parking areas.
d.
The location, right-of-way and surfaced roadway width, surfacing material of roadways and walkways.
e.
The location of service buildings, sanitary stations, and any other existing or proposed structure.
f.
The location of water and sewer lines pipes.
g.
Plans and specifications of the water supply, sewage disposal and refuse facilities.
h.
The locations and details of lighting and electric systems.
i.
Plans for drainage, flood control, and landscaping.
j.
Plans and specifications of all buildings constructed or to be constructed within the recreation vehicle park.
k.
Letters of review from utility agencies stating whether they can provide services to the recreational vehicle park.
l.
Preliminary plan shall be drawn on twenty-four (24) inch by thirty-six (36) inch sheet size in blue or black ink.
m.
An Application fee per the City of Metter fee schedule.
B.
Phased approached. Where a recreational vehicle park development is proposed for construction in a series of stages, a master plan for the development of the entire tract shall be submitted along with the detailed plans and specifications for the initial stage, as well as any subsequent stages.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.070 - Site restrictions.
A.
Floodplain. No RV park or any structures in any RV park shall be located within a one hundred (100) year flood plain area as defined by FEMA or identified by the City of Metter Flood Plain Map, unless in compliance with all applicable local ordinances.
B.
Illumination. All RV parks shall be furnished with lighting standards so spaced and equipped with luminaries placed at such mounting heights as will provide adequate levels of illumination for the safe movement of pedestrians and vehicles at night.
C.
Site conditions. Conditions of soil, groundwater level, drainage and topography shall not create hazards to the property or the health or safety of the occupants. The site shall not be exposed to objectionable smoke, noise, odors or other adverse influences.
D.
Signage. All signage for the site will comply with the City of Metter's Sign Ordinance.
E.
Soil and groundcover. Exposed ground surfaces in all parts of the recreational vehicle park shall be paved, or covered with stone screening or other solid materials, or protected with a vegetative growth (grass) that is capable of preventing soil erosion and of eliminating objectionable dust.
F.
Stormwater/drainage. A storm drainage plan shall be developed for the RV park and approved by the city. These plans shall insure that all surface water is drained in a safe and efficient manner away from recreation vehicles sites.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.080 - Park size and density.
A.
Park size. The minimum area for a recreation vehicle park is five acres. The maximum area allowed is ten (10) acres.
B.
Park density. The maximum density shall not exceed twelve (12) RV's per acre.
C.
Minimum site size. Each RV site shall contain a minimum of one thousand two hundred (1,200) square feet and shall have a minimum width of twenty-five feet (25).
D.
Site pads. Each site shall contain a vehicle-parking pad of concrete or asphalt paving. Minimum length of the parking pad shall be thirty-five (35) feet. No part of the RV or other unit placed on the lot pad shall be closer than five to the edge of the lot.
E.
Required separation between RV vehicles. RV's shall be separated from each other and from other structures by at least ten (10) feet. Any accessory structure such as attached awnings for the purpose of this separation requirement shall be considered to be part of the RV.
F.
Site identification. Each site for the parking of the RV's shall be identified by numbers, a minimum of four inches in height, posted in a conspicuous place at the front of the site. This shall be done in a logical sequence.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.090 - Roadways and parking.
A.
Interior roads. All interior two-way roads shall be twenty-eight (28) feet minimum width in right-of-way. The pave area on all two-way roads shall be a minimum of twenty-two (22) feet wide, with eleven-foot lanes. All interior one-way roads shall be twenty (20) feet minimum width. The pave area on all one-way roads shall be a minimum of twelve (12) feet in width. All roads shall be paved with asphalt and crowned to facilitate drainage. Roadways shall be designed for the safe and convenient movement of vehicles.
B.
Parking requirements. At least one off-road parking space shall be provided in the park per each recreation vehicle site. Off-street common parking areas for additional vehicles of park occupants and guest shall be provided at a rate of not less than one and one-half spaces per five sites.
1.
No on-street parking will be permitted on roads or drives within the RV park.
2.
Sites and parking spaces shall not front or have access directly to public roads or streets or to private roads.
3.
No part of any RV park shall be used for the parking or storage of any heavy equipment or trucks exceeding one-ton capacity.
4.
No RV shall be parked anywhere but in an RV space, and only one RV shall be located within any RV space.
C.
Vehicular circulation. One-way drive aisles must originate and terminate at two-way drive aisles. Individual sites and parking spaces shall have direct access to the interior park street system.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.100 - Entrances and exits.
A.
Locations and access. No entrance or exit from a RV park shall be permitted through a residential district nor require movement of traffic from the park through residential district.
B.
Design of access to park. All RV parks shall have separate entrance and exit roadway, each of which shall be not less than twenty-eight (28) feet wide, from flow line to flow line and shall be hard surfaced with asphalt or concrete.
C.
Distances from intersections. Entrance driveways shall be located not closer than one hundred fifty (150) feet from the intersection of public streets.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.110 - Accessory uses.
A.
Service building and other uses. Management headquarters, toilets, showers, coin-operated laundry facilities and other uses and structures customarily incident to operation of a recreational vehicle park and campground are permitted as accessory uses to the park.
B.
Dumping stations. Trailer sanitation stations designed to receive the discharge of sewage holding tanks for self-contained vehicles shall be installed in an accessible location in every RV park in which there are sites not provided with drain inlets designed to receive the discharge of toilets.
C.
Recreational facilities. A recreational area of at least five thousand (5,000) square feet shall be provided in addition to the required landscaping. Recreation equipment, such as a pool, playground, and picnic tables and a service building, including toilets, showers, and laundry, can be located within the recreation area.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.120 - Buffering, setbacks, screening and landscaping.
A.
Yards and setbacks. Within an RV park, minimum setbacks shall be as follows:
1.
Front yard setback. The minimum front yard setback is fifty feet (50).
2.
Side yard setback. The minimum side yard setback when abutting residential districts is fifty feet (50); when abutting a dedicated public right-of-way, it shall be fifty feet (50); and when abutting any other zoning district, the side yard setback is twenty-five feet (25).
3.
Rear yard setback. The minimum rear yard setback is twenty-five (25) feet; unless it abuts a residential district, then the rear yard setback is fifty (50) feet.
B.
Landscaping. A landscaping plan illustrating the placement and type of trees and shrubs must be submitted as part of the park development plan. The design of the landscaping must mitigate the visual impact of the RV park on the surrounding area.
1.
No building or structure may be erected or placed within a buffer area, except a fence or wall.
2.
No RV may be located within a buffer area.
C.
Boundary fencing. Except for the front boundary, each recreation vehicle park shall be enclosed by attractive fencing.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.130 - Occupancy/length of stay.
A.
Length of stay. Length of occupancy for any recreational vehicle shall not exceed ninety (90) continuous days or one hundred eighty (180) days in any one-year period.
B.
Home occupation. No home occupation or business shall be operated from an RV park.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.140 - Water and sewer services.
All RV parks will be served by the City of Metter's water and sewer system if available.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.150 - Other utilities and public services.
A.
Underground utilities. All utilities, including electrical power and telephone lines, shall be installed underground.
B.
Refuse disposal and collection. The storage, collection and disposal of refuse shall be performed so as to minimize accidents, fire hazards, air pollution, odors, insects, rodents or other nuisance conditions.
1.
The City of Metter's Sanitation Department shall serve RV parks.
2.
Polycarts will be provided by the City of Metter at the current monthly rate as set by city council for businesses. No refuse-disposal area shall be located within a buffer area.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.160 - Fire prevention and protection.
A.
Hand fire extinguishers of type approved by the Metter Fire Department shall be maintained ineffective working order and located in convenient places in the ratio of one to eight vehicle sites.
B.
No outdoor fires will be allowed except in grills, ovens, stoves or park-provided fireboxes.
C.
Fire hydrants shall be so that every site within the park can be reached within one thousand (1,000) feet of the hose.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.170 - Service building.
A service building shall be constructed of easily cleanable, nonabsorbent materials, maintained in good repair and in a clean and sanitary condition.
1.
Restroom facilities. A minimum of one toilet and one lavatory for each sex shall be provided for the exclusive use of the park occupants. An additional toilet and lavatory for each sex shall be provided for each fifteen (15) sites or fraction thereof which is not provided with a water connection and a three-inch drain inlet for connection to a vehicle equipped with a toilet.
2.
Showers. A minimum of one shower for each sex is required.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.180 - Licensing and inspection.
A.
Licensed required. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate any recreational park within the city limits unless they hold a valid occupational tax certificate (business license) issued annually by the City of Metter in the name of such person for the specific RV park. The license certification shall be conspicuously posted in the office of the RV park at all times.
B.
Registration. All recreational vehicles in the park must have a current valid registration.
C.
Inspection. The building official shall inspect the RV park at least once annually to determine compliance with the provisions of this chapter and all other applicable ordinances, rules, regulations or codes. Such official shall have the authority to enter upon the premises for the purpose of such inspections at any reasonable time without notice to the owner or manager.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
15.48.190 - Responsibilities of management.
A.
Maintenance and enforcement of regulations. A responsible caretaker, owner, or manager shall be placed in charge of any RV park to keep all grounds, facilities and equipment in a clean, orderly and sanitary condition, and shall be answerable to the owner for any violation of the provisions of this or any ordinance. The facility shall have an on-site manager.
B.
Office. In every recreational vehicle park, there shall be a designated office building in which shall be located the office of the manager of said park.
(Ord. of 6-11-07, § 1)
