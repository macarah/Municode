Sec. 18-146. - Nature and scope of article; adoption of Standard Amusement Device Code.
(a)
Pursuant to the power and authority vested in the city council, and in order to promote the general health, welfare, and safety, a plan has been devised in order to regulate and control the operation of carnivals, circuses, road shows, tent shows, amusement rides, and migratory entertainment of like kind. Such operations are referred to in this article as an "enterprise."
(b)
This article shall apply to those firms, businesses, corporations, partnerships and sole proprietorships who derive their income by placing various forms of entertainment upon the property of another for the purpose of amusing or entertaining the general public in the form of enterprises, and charging a fee for their use and enjoyment.
(c)
The city does adopt hereby the Standard Amusement Device Code, 1985 edition, with any subsequent editions thereof, including any fee schedule included therein, as published by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc. Said code is adopted, referred to, and made a part of this article as if fully set out at length in this section. A copy of this code shall be maintained on file in the office of the city clerk. In the event of any conflict between the provisions of the Standard Amusement Device Code, and the provisions of this article, state law or ordinances, rules or regulations, the provisions of this article, state law or ordinances, shall prevail and be controlling.
(Ord. of 10-12-1992, § 1(9-7-1); Ord. No. 04-11, § 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-147. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person, firm, partnership or corporation properly cited with a violation of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as set forth in section 1-12.
(Ord. of 10-12-1992, § 1(9-7-6); Ord. No. 04-11, § 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-148. - Liability insurance and permit required for operators of certain amusement devices.
(a)
All persons shall, prior to operating an amusement device (as such term is defined in the Standard Amusement Device Code) where human beings are conveyed, obtain from the city a permit. Upon requesting a permit the applicant must show evidence of personal liability insurance in an amount as fixed from time to time by the city council, but in no event less than $1,000,000.00 for each person and $1,000,000.00 for each accident, plus $50,000.00 property damages, and name an agent for service domiciled in the county.
(b)
All traveling motion picture shows, carnivals, and circuses shall obtain a fire prevention regulatory license from the state fire marshal, as set forth in rules and regulations promulgated by the safety fire commissioner. The fee for the license shall be $100.00 for each calendar year or part thereof, payable to the state fire marshal.
(Ord. of 10-12-1992, § 1(9-7-2); Ord. No. 04-11, § 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-149. - Licensing procedure and requirements.
(a)
Any enterprise desiring to conduct business shall apply for a business license at city hall during regular business hours. The fee for the license shall be in an amount as listed in the schedule of fees and charges per day, which is payable at the time of issuance.
(b)
Such application shall be accompanied by a cash deposit in such an amount as the city council may require.
(1)
The deposit shall secure the removal of all litter and refuse as may be generated by the enterprise.
(2)
After the removal of all equipment, and upon inspection by a code enforcement officer, the deposit shall be refunded to the enterprise. If the city must expend funds to remove litter and refuse, such funds may be drawn from the deposit.
(c)
The application shall be accompanied by a certification of the owner of the property as set forth in subsection (d) below.
(d)
The application shall contain a certification by the enterprise of the total number of seats available on each and every amusement ride.
(Ord. of 10-12-1992, § 1(9-7-3); Ord. No. 04-11, § 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-150. - Parking facilities required; site restrictions.
(a)
Any enterprise which engages in the operation of various amusement rides, i.e., Ferris wheel, merry go rounds and rides or devices of a similar nature, must meet a minimum requirement of parking spaces for use by its patrons.
(b)
Such minimum parking facility shall be as follows:
(1)
For every 15 seats that are available for use by the public on each and every amusement ride, there must be one parking space available for use by the patrons;
(2)
However, a minimum number of 25 parking spaces shall be required at all times for each and every enterprise regulated under the terms of this article; and
(3)
No parking shall be allowed on public rights-of-way. It shall be the duty of the license holder to monitor rights-of-way adjacent to the enterprise to ensure compliance with this subsection.
(c)
The enterprise shall obtain and supply the city, upon application, with a certification by the owner of the property upon whose premises the enterprise will operate, or upon whose premises the automobiles are to be parked, that there exists on such property sufficient parking space for a given number of vehicles, and further, that the minimum number of parking spaces is equal to, or greater than, one ride for every 15 seats available on such amusement rides, and that there exist 25 or such greater number as required of parking spaces available to the public on the leased premises. Any property owner who gives false certification shall be deemed to be in violation of the terms of this article and shall be cited according to law.
(d)
Enterprises shall be allowable only in C-1, C-2 and M-1 zoning districts.
(Ord. of 10-12-1992, § 1(9-7-4); Ord. No. 04-11, § 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-151. - Term of license.
(a)
A license issued under this article shall be valid for a period not to exceed 15 days.
(b)
At the expiration of 15 days, no such license shall be renewed or reissued to the same enterprise until such time as a period of 30 days has elapsed after the expiration date of the original license term.
(Ord. of 10-12-1992, § 1(9-7-5); Ord. No. 04-11, § 2, 5-17-2004)
