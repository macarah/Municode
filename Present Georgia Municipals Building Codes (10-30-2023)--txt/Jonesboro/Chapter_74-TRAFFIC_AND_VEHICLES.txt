ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 74-1. - Vehicle cover required.
No person shall operate or load any vehicle on the public streets and roads of this city unless the vehicle is constructed, loaded and securely covered so as to prevent any of its load from dropping, escaping or shifting in such a manner as to create a safety hazard or in such a manner so as to litter the streets and roads of the city.
(Code 1987, § 10-1-4)
Sec. 74-2. - Vehicles to be parked within marked spaces.
Whenever a space is marked off on any highway for the parking of an individual vehicle, every vehicle there parked shall be parked within the lines bounding that space.
(Code 1987, § 10-1-5)
Sec. 74-3. - Storage and parking of trailers and commercial vehicles.
Commercial vehicles and trailers of all types, including travel, boat, camping and hauling trailers, shall not be parked or stored on any lot occupied by a dwelling or on any lot in any residential district except in accordance with the following provisions:
(1)
Not more than one commercial vehicle per family living on the premises, which does not exceed 1½ tons rated capacity, shall be permitted; and in no case shall a commercial vehicle used for hauling explosives, gasoline or liquefied petroleum products be permitted.
(2)
Not more than one camping or travel trailer, one hauling trailer, and one boat trailer per family living on the premises shall be permitted and the trailer shall not exceed 33 feet in length, or eight feet in width; and further provided that the trailer shall not be parked or stored for more than 48 hours unless it is located behind the front yard building line. A camping or travel trailer shall not be occupied either temporarily or permanently while it is parked or stored in any area except in a travel trailer court authorized under this chapter.
(3)
Not more than one tractor cab per family living on the premises, which cab does not exceed 2½ tons rated capacity, shall be permitted on any lot; and further provided that such tractor cab shall not be parked on a city street or a private driveway, but shall be parked only on a paved pad, constructed to accommodate the weight of the cab, as approved by the city building inspector. For the purposes of this section, a tractor cab shall mean a motor vehicle designed and used primarily for drawing trailers or semitrailers, and not designed to normally operate independently as a means of transportation without the presence of such trailer or semitrailer.
(4)
It shall be unlawful to park a tractor trailer with or without tractor cab on any street between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m., except where zoning specifically provides for such parking in an area zoned M-1.
(5)
It shall be unlawful to use a tractor trailer for the purpose of storage except where zoning specifically provides for such parking in areas zoned C-1, C-2 and M-1. In such instances, no more than two such trailers may be used for each lot and only for the purposes of storing seasonal horticultural perishables.
(Code 1987, § 10-1-6; Ord. of 5-10-1993(2), § 1)
Cross reference— Businesses, ch. 18.
Sec. 74-4. - Parking of cars for sale.
Only city personnel on official business may park a car on public property for the purpose of utilizing the same for signage.
(Code 1987, § 10-1-7; Ord. No. 02-04, § 10, 6-10-2002)
Sec. 74-5. - Riding bicycle upon sidewalk; Main Street.
It shall be unlawful to ride a bicycle on the sidewalks on Main Street from the Methodist Church to Smith Street.
(Code 1987, § 10-1-8)
Sec. 74-6. - Railroads; street blocking restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any railroad company, engineer, firefighter, conductor or the employees of any railroad company to stop or cause to be stopped, any train or portion of train, car or locomotive, on or across any street crossing in the city for a longer period of time than five minutes at any one time.
(Code 1987, § 10-1-9)
Sec. 74-7. - Overnight parking in residential districts.
(a)
On all public streets, except where parking spaces have been officially designated by the city, in areas of the city zoned R-1, R-2, R-2D, R-3, R-4 and HD no stopping, standing or parking of vehicles shall be allowed in any public street from 5:00 p.m. until 9:00 a.m.
(b)
Traffic control devices shall be placed in areas designated as residential above to regulate stopping, standing and parking of vehicles.
(c)
The definitions found in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 shall be and are made applicable to this section.
(d)
Vehicles found in violation of this section shall be towed to a city authorized impound facility or lot. All costs for towing and storage of vehicles shall be paid for by the owners of the vehicle.
(e)
All impounded vehicles shall be deemed to be abandoned pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1(C) after impound for 30 days.
(f)
The procedures regarding notice, foreclosure, abandonments, liens and sales as set forth in O.C.G.A. §§ 40-11-2 through 40-11-9 shall be used by parties removing vehicles from public property.
(g)
Penalties for violations of this section shall be punishable by a fine of not more than $100.00.
(Ord. of 4-9-2001, § 1)
Sec. 74-8. - Downtown Parking District.
(a)
Designated. The on-street parking spaces located on both sides of South Main Street, beginning at the intersection of West Mill Street and continuing on South Main Street to the intersection of Church Street, is hereby designated as the Downtown Parking District.
(b)
Parking regulations. The following regulations shall only be enforceable between the hours of 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to stop, park, leave standing, or store any motorized vehicle in the Downtown Parking District for a continuous period in excess of two hours.
(2)
The movement of a motorized vehicle to another on-street parking space in the district shall not extend the time limits for parking.
(3)
The regulations of this section shall not relieve any person from the duty to comply with other, more restrictive, regulations in this article and state law governing the stopping, standing, or parking of vehicles in specified places or at specified times.
(c)
Off-street parking. The regulations provided in this section shall not be applicable to designated off-street parking areas and lots. The city has two designated parking lots adjacent to the Downtown Parking District, located at 114 Broad Street and 138 West Mill Street, which are available to the public between the hours of 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday.
(d)
Penalties. Any person found in violation of this section shall be subject to the following fines:
(1)
For the first offense, a civil penalty not less than $50.00.
(2)
For the second offense, a civil penalty not less than $100.00.
(3)
For a third offense committed within one year of the imposition of a civil penalty for a second offense, the minimum fine shall be $300.00 and the vehicle shall be towed at owner's expense.
(Ord. No. 2019-014 , § 1, 11-11-2019)

ARTICLE IV. - TRUCK ROUTES

Sec. 74-91. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Street means the entire width between the boundary lines of every publicly maintained way when any part thereof is open to the use of the public for purposes of vehicular travel.
Temporary delivery means the nonregular, nonpermanent transport of materials or property to or from any location on a residential highway associated with an activity of limited duration. This term does not include the use of a truck as defined in this section to deliver a driver or passengers.
Traffic control device means any signal or sign of any kind used to direct, permit, prohibit, or regulate in any way the use of any public roadway or highway.
Truck means every motor vehicle with more than six wheels designed, used, or maintained primarily for the transportation of property where such motor vehicle is not the property of, not used by or not operating with the express written consent of the city in the pursuit of city business or is not engaged in a temporary delivery as defined in this section.
(Ord. of 9-27-1999, § 1(b))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 74-92. - Construction.
(a)
This article shall not be construed to prohibit truck traffic on state routes.
(b)
This article shall not be construed to generally prohibit the transportation and flow of goods and services in the ordinary flow of commerce or persons through the city, except as reasonably regulated in this article.
(c)
This article shall not be construed to generally prevent the pickup and delivery of goods and services and persons in the ordinary course of business except as reasonably regulated in this article.
(Ord. of 9-27-1999, § 1(k)—(m))
Sec. 74-93. - Violations.
Jurisdiction over a violation of this article shall be in any court having jurisdiction to try and dispose of traffic offenses.
(Ord. of 9-27-1999, § 1(i))
Sec. 74-94. - Established.
A truck route for trucks with over six wheels is established. The purpose of this route is to redirect truck traffic away from city streets. The truck route shall be as designated in figure 1.
PU MP 226.
(Ord. of 9-27-1999, § 1(j))
Sec. 74-95. - Traffic control devices prohibiting certain truck traffic.
(a)
Traffic control devices prohibiting trucks with over six wheels on city streets shall be erected in that such truck traffic is incompatible with the normal and safe movement of traffic on city streets.
(b)
Where these traffic control devices are in place no person shall disobey the restrictions stated on such traffic control devices.
(c)
The driver of any vehicle shall obey the instruction of the official traffic control device in place in accordance with this article, unless otherwise directed by a law enforcement officer or authorized emergency official acting in an official capacity, and subject to the exceptions granted the driver of an authorized emergency vehicle in accordance with local ordinances and the laws of the state.
(d)
The traffic control devices described in this section shall be placed in a position so as to be sufficiently legible to be seen by an ordinarily observant person.
(e)
The traffic control device shall conform to the lawful requirements pertaining to such traffic control devices.
(f)
The disregard or disobedience of the instructions of the traffic control devices set out and placed in accordance with the provisions of this article by a driver of a vehicle shall constitute a misdemeanor offense.
(g)
Destruction or damage to a traffic control device shall constitute a misdemeanor offense.
(Ord. of 9-27-1999, § 1(a), (c)—(h))

ARTICLE V. - SPEED MONITORING SYSTEMS

Sec. 74-121. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, the following terms, phrases and their derivatives shall have the meanings given herein:
Automated traffic enforcement safety device means a speed detection device that:
(1)
Is capable of producing photographically recorded still or video images, or both, of the rear of a motor vehicle or of the rear of a motor vehicle being towed by another vehicle, including an image of such vehicle's rear license plate;
(2)
Is capable of monitoring the speed of a vehicle as photographically recorded pursuant to this paragraph; and
(3)
Indicates on each photographically recorded still or video image produced the date, time, location, and speed of a photographically recorded vehicle traveling at a speed above the posted speed limit within a marked school zone.
City shall mean the City of Jonesboro.
Police or city police shall mean the City of Jonesboro Police Department.
School zone means the area within 1,000 feet of the boundary of any public or private elementary or secondary school.
(Ord. No. 2019-002 , § 1, 2-11-2019)
Sec. 74-122. - Authorized.
Speed camera enforcement, using automated traffic enforcement safety device that is fixed is authorized within school zones in the city between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday.
(Ord. No. 2019-002 , § 1, 2-11-2019)
Sec. 74-123. - Administration.
(a)
The city police, or agent on behalf of the city police, operating an automated traffic enforcement safety device provided for under Code Section 40-14-18 shall maintain a log for the automated traffic enforcement safety device attesting to the performance of such device's self-test at least once every 30 days and the results of such self-test pertaining to the accuracy of the automated traffic enforcement safety device. Such log shall be admissible in any court proceeding for a violation issued pursuant to Code Section 40-14-18.
(b)
The city police, or agent on behalf of the city police shall perform an independent calibration test on the automated traffic enforcement safety device at least once every 12 months. The results of such calibration test shall be admissible in any court proceeding for a violation issued pursuant to Code Section 40-14-18.
(c)
Prior to the placement of a device within a school zone, each school within whose school zone such automated traffic enforcement safety device is to be placed shall first apply for and secure a permit from the department of transportation for the use of such automated traffic enforcement safety device. Such permit shall be awarded based upon need. The department of transportation shall promulgate rules and regulations for the implementation of this paragraph.
(d)
If an automated traffic enforcement safety device is moved to or placed in a location where an automated traffic enforcement safety device had not previously been moved to or placed in, no citation shall be issued for a violation recorded by that automated traffic enforcement safety device until:
(1)
The city erects signs warning of the use of a stationary speed detection device within the approaching school zone. Such signs shall be:
a.
At least 24 by 30 inches in area;
b.
Shall be visible plainly from every lane of traffic;
c.
Shall be viewable in all traffic conditions; and
d.
Shall not be placed in such a manner that the view of such sign is subject to being obstructed by any other vehicle on such highway. Such signs shall be placed within 500 feet prior to the warning sign announcing the reduction of the speed limit for the school speed zone;
(2)
There shall be a rebuttable presumption that the warning signs described in subsection (1)(a) are properly installed pursuant to this subsection at the time of any alleged violation under this article; and
(3)
That no citation shall be issued for the first 30 days after such signage is installed for the initial automated traffic enforcement safety device authorized by this article.
(Ord. No. 2019-002 , § 1, 2-11-2019)
Sec. 74-124. - Violations and penalties.
Any person who shall violate any provision of this article shall be subject to the penalties set forth in O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18(b)(1), as amended, including a fine in the amount of $75.00 for a first violation and $125.00 for a second or any subsequent violation, in addition to fees associated with the electronic processing of such civil monetary penalty which shall not exceed $25.00.
(Ord. No. 2019-002 , § 1, 2-11-2019)

ARTICLE VI. - MOTORIZED CARTS[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2018-012 , § 1, adopted Oct. 8, 2018, amended Art. VI in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former Art. VI, §§ 74-175—74-181, pertained to golf carts, and derived from Ord. No. 2008-004, § 1, adopted Sept. 8, 2008; and Ord. No. 2010-002, § 1, 2-8-10.

Sec. 74-175. - Findings, purpose and definitions.
(a)
The mayor and city council find that all public streets and paved recreational paths located within the geographic boundaries of the city are designed and constructed as to safely permit their use.
(b)
It shall be the intent of this article to regulate the use of motorized carts by residents within the city and provide for the regulated use of motorized carts by non-city residents within the city limits.
(c)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the content clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the City of Jonesboro.
City resident means a citizen living within the incorporated limits of the City of Jonesboro.
Driver is defined as the person driving and having physical control over the motorized cart and being the licensee.
Motorized carts are defined as those electric and gasoline-powered pleasure carts, commonly referred as to "golf carts", and further defined by reference to O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1(17.3). These are the only personal transportation vehicles, as that term is defined by O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1(43.1), authorized for use under this article.
Non-city resident means a citizen living outside of the incorporated limits of the city with personal business within the incorporated limits of the city.
Recreational paths are defined as areas designated for multi-use and having widths in greater than seven feet.
(Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 10-8-2018)
Sec. 74-176. - Registration/inspection/certification.
(a)
(1)
General. Before any motorized cart is operated over any recreational paths, authorized streets or any other public property pursuant to this article, it shall be the duty of the owner thereof to register the cart with and obtain a permit from the city. Only those persons 18 years of age or older may register a motorized cart. Motorized cart registration may be in one person's name only, and the registration form must be signed by that person. A record of each motorized cart number with corresponding owner information shall be maintained by the city.
(2)
Application. Every application for a permit shall be made on a form supplied by the city and shall contain the following minimum information:
a.
Name and current address (street and mailing) of applicant.
b.
Model, make, name and cart identification number.
c.
Copy of current Georgia driver's license number.
d.
Evidence of current motor vehicle insurance as required by law.
e.
For gasoline powered motorized carts, a current dealer certification that the vehicle meets the emission standards required herein.
f.
Such other information which the city may require in order to effectuate the purpose and intent of this article.
(3)
Fee. An annual registration fee for each newly-purchased motorized cart shall be $25.00 for city residents and $30.00 for non-city residents. All citizens aged 65 and over shall be entitled to a 50 percent reduction in the annual registration fee amount. The registration shall be effective until the ownership of the motorized cart is transferred to another person or entity. The registration fees shall be due by January 31st each year and shall be paid annually until such time as the cart is transferred or otherwise disposed of.
(4)
Transfer. Upon any transfer of ownership of a previously registered motorized cart, the new owner shall, before he or she shall operate the motorized cart in a manner regulated hereunder, apply for a new permit and registration. The new owner shall pay the registration and permit fee as required herein.
(5)
Decals. Two numerical decals shall be issued upon completion of registration. The decals must be affixed to the sides of the cart in such a manner as to be fully visible at all times. Decals for city residents and non-city residents shall be different, distinct colors. Decals for city residents and non-city residents shall change color annually, in a revolving schedule determined by the city.
(6)
Revocation of permit. Permits may be revoked if it is found that:
a.
The permit holder no longer holds a valid Georgia driver's license;
b.
The permit holder, or any operator of the registered vehicle, shall repeatedly fail to abide by the rules and regulations of this article, including, but not limited to, failing to have a current registration and properly displaying current decals.
c.
The permit holder shall fail to abide by traffic laws in the use of a motorized cart on authorized streets;
d.
The permit holder shall fail to grant right-of-way to pedestrians and bicyclists; or
e.
The permit holder cannot safely operate the motorized cart on a designated roadway or pose a threat to the safety of others.
(7)
Homeowner relocation. In the event that a registered motorized cart owner changes his/her address or contact information after the motorized cart is registered, that owner shall have 60 days to provide their new contact information to the city. If the ownership information is not updated within 60 days of relocation, a $20.00 penalty will be applied and the cart shall be considered an unregistered cart after the 60-day period.
(8)
Temporary permits. For short-term visitors or guests to the city using a motorized cart within the city, which is registered in another municipality other than the city or Clayton County, Georgia, a temporary permit card is required by the city and shall be placed in a conspicuous place on the subject cart. No affixed decals are required for temporary permits.
(b)
Gasoline carts.
(1)
Every motorized cart powered by gasoline shall at all times be equipped with an exhaust system in good working order and in constant operation, meeting the following specifications:
a.
The exhaust system shall include the piping leading from the flange of the exhaust manifold to and including the muffler and exhaust pipes or include any and all parts specified by the manufacturer;
b.
The exhaust system and its elements shall be securely fastened, with brackets or hangers which are designed for the particular purpose of fastening the motorized cart exhaust systems; and
c.
The engine and powered mechanism of every motorized cart shall be so equipped, adjusted and tuned so that the exhaust is in good working order.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any motorized cart to operate or permit the operation of such motorized cart on which any device controlling or abating atmospheric emissions, which is placed on a cart by the manufacturer, is rendered unserviceable by removal, alteration or other acts which interfere with its operation.
(3)
Every two years, running with the time of registration, every gasoline motorized cart must be inspected by a dealer holding a valid business license for the inspection and repair of motorized carts, and the dealer must certify that the exhaust system meets the standards required by this article. This certification must be presented to the city in order to obtain a renewal permit.
(c)
Rental carts. Motorized cart dealers and distributors, as well as other commercial establishments, may rent carts to the public for use on authorized streets and those areas accessible by the public of the city. Each such establishment renting carts shall be required to register each such rental cart in accordance with subsection (a) of this section and shall maintain a written record of each person who rents each cart. Operators of rental vehicles shall be required to furnish positive identification, shall be provided a copy of this article, shall carry a copy of the current registration in the vehicle, and must be at least 18 years of age. The registration fee and transfer fees and regulations shall be the same as those in subsections (a)(4) and (5) of this section. Motorized carts for temporary use by the city during special events are exempt from the requirement of this section.
(Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 10-8-2018)
Sec. 74-177. - Operation regulations.
(a)
Only those persons who hold a valid driver's license may drive a motorized cart on authorized streets and those areas accessible by the public of the city.
(b)
Motorized carts shall only be operated over those authorized streets and those areas accessible by the public during daylight hours, unless such motorized carts are equipped with functional headlights and taillights. All carts must have functional brake lights and turn signals.
(c)
Children (passengers) under the age of 12 must wear a certified bicycle helmet.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any motorized cart or any other person operating, employing, permitting the use of or otherwise directing the use of such cart to operate or permit the operator of any such cart to drive over the authorized streets or those areas accessible by the public in the city in violation of this article.
(e)
All laws and ordinances relative to alcohol and its use, including open container laws, which apply to traffic on streets of the city, shall also apply to motorized carts.
(f)
Sidewalks and recreational paths. Motorized carts shall not be driven on any city sidewalks or on unauthorized recreational paths.
(g)
Designated parking areas. Except where specifically authorized, motorized carts shall not be parked on streets, street shoulders, or within rights-of-way. Motorized carts at residences shall only be parked in driveways or in carports/garages and shall be provided adequate off-street parking space as other types of vehicles. The city shall reserve the right to create motorized cart parking areas in certain districts.
(h)
All motorized cart operators shall abide by all traffic regulations applicable to vehicular traffic when using the streets and those areas accessible by the public in the city. Sec. 74-178. - Authorized streets and other areas.
(a)
Motorized carts may be operated on all city streets and areas, except as provided below:
(1)
Streets and areas north of Georgia State Route 138 Spur.
(2)
Turner Road.
(3)
Stockbridge Road.
(4)
Tara Boulevard.
(5)
Public sidewalks and unauthorized recreational paths.
(Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 10-8-2018)
Sec. 74-179. - Liability.
Nothing in this article shall be construed as an assumption of liability by the city for any injuries to persons or property which may result from the operation of a motorized cart by a license holder or the failure by the city to revoke said license.
(Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 10-8-2018)
Sec. 74-180. - Penalties.
(a)
All operators shall abide by all traffic regulations applicable to vehicular traffic when using the streets and those areas accessible by the public. Violations shall be punished as provided herein.
(b)
In addition to any fine as may be imposed by operation of subsection (a) above, the registered owner of the cart, shall, upon any violation of this article by any operator of the registered vehicle, be charged a civil penalty as follows:
(1)
For the first offense, a civil penalty of not less than $100.00.
(2)
For the second offense, a civil penalty of not less than $250.00.
(3)
For a third offense committed within one year of the imposition of a civil penalty for a second offense, the minimum fine shall be $500.00, and the registered owner's motorized cart registration shall be revoked. The registered owner may not thereafter operate a motorized cart in the city for a period of two years after imposition of the civil penalty.
(Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 10-8-2018)
Sec. 74-181. - Signs.
The city shall post signs in conjunction with all existing city speed limit signs (on relevant streets) currently erected by the city, informing drivers of which city streets are authorized for motorized carts.
(Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 10-8-2018)
