Sec. 34-81. - Acts constituting nuisances designated.
(a)
A "nuisance" is anything that works hurt, inconvenience or damage to another, and the fact that the act done may otherwise be lawful shall not keep it from being a nuisance. The inconvenience complained of shall not be fanciful, or such as would affect only one of fastidious taste, but it shall be such as would affect an ordinarily reasonable person.
(b)
The following conditions, among others, may be declared to be nuisances:
(1)
Stagnant water on premises;
(2)
Any dead or decaying matter, weeds, brush, vegetation, or any fruit, vegetable, animal or rodent, or any other objectionable, unsightly, or unsanitary matter upon a premises which is odorous or capable of causing disease or annoyance to the inhabitants of the city. For the purpose of this article, "weeds" means any brush, grass, vegetation, or any plant that is not regularly cultivated, that exceeds a height of ten inches; provided, however, that weeds shall not include plants in a maintained wildflower garden. Any plant that is not regularly cultivated exceeding ten inches in height shall be presumed to be a weed. "Regularly cultivated" means any plant which is routinely cared for by any person to foster its growth, including ornamental grasses, bushes, and trees;
(3)
The pollution of public water or the injection of matter into the sewage system which would be damaging thereto;
(4)
Maintaining a dangerous or diseased animal or fowl;
(5)
Obstruction of a public street, highway or sidewalk without a permit;
(6)
Loud or unnecessary noises as defined in section 34-121 et seq.;
(7)
All walls, trees and buildings that may endanger persons or property;
(8)
Any unsafe or unhealthful premises as defined in section 16-72 et seq.;
(9)
Unused or inoperable appliances of any size or type, including, but not limited to, iceboxes, refrigerators, washers, dryers, water heaters, televisions, stereos, or any parts thereof;
(10)
Any trees, shrubbery or other plants or parts thereof, which obstruct clear, safe vision on roadways and intersections of the city;
(11)
Junked or abandoned motor vehicles as defined in section 34-150 et seq.;
(12)
The generation of smoke or fumes in sufficient amount to cause odor or annoyance to the inhabitants of the city;
(13)
Toilets or sanitary facilities, plumbing, or sewers in bad repair; and
(14)
Violation of the Jonesboro Social Hosting Ordinance, as set forth in section 6-211 et seq.
(15)
Any other condition constituting a nuisance under state law.
(Ord. No. 04-20, § 2, 8-9-2004; Ord. No. 2015-002, § 1, 4-13-2015)
Sec. 34-82. - Abatement of nuisances—Municipal court authority.
The city municipal court shall have full jurisdiction to try and dispose of all questions of nuisance affecting the public health, safety or welfare. Any person who pleads guilty, nolo contendere, or is convicted of violating the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided in section 34-86 or such other general law as may be enacted in the future to provide for punishment and penalties that may be imposed by the municipal courts of the state.
(Ord. No. 04-20, § 2, 8-9-2004)
Sec. 34-83. - Complaints of nuisance—Investigation and abatement order.
(a)
Any official or inhabitant of the city may direct a complaint of nuisance to the code enforcement officer who shall investigate and may place the complaint on the municipal court docket for a hearing upon the basis of the investigation. The municipal court, after a ten-day notice to the party involved, shall hold a hearing thereon and, upon finding that a nuisance does exist, shall issue an order to the owner, agent in control of, or tenant in possession, stating that a nuisance has been found to exist and that the nuisance must be abated within so many hours or days as the court shall deem reasonable, having consideration for the nature of the nuisance and its effect on the public.
(b)
Animal control officers, license and building inspectors, the city police department, and the Clayton County Sheriff may also receive complaints, investigate the same, and place on the court docket such complaints in the same manner as the code enforcement officer.
(Ord. No. 04-20, § 2, 8-9-2004)
Sec. 34-84. - Abatement by city—Billing for costs.
(a)
In any case where the owner, agent or tenant fails to abate the nuisance in the time specified, or where the owner, agent or tenant cannot be served with notice, or where the nature of the nuisance is such, in the opinion of the court, that it must be immediately abated, the judge may issue an order to the appropriate officials directing the nuisance to be abated. In such case, the appropriate officials shall keep a record of the expenses and cost of abating same, and the costs shall be billed against the owner, agent or tenant for collection as for city revenues, and these shall become a lien against the property and the same shall be filed of record with the office of the clerk of superior court.
(b)
Other city departments shall assist the police department as is necessary in abating nuisances hereunder.
(Ord. No. 04-20, § 2, 8-9-2004)
Sec. 34-85. - Summary abatement authorized when.
Nothing contained in this article shall prevent the mayor and city council from summarily and without notice ordering the abatement of or abating any nuisance that is a nuisance per se in the law or where the case is an urgent one and the health and safety of the public or a portion thereof is in imminent danger.
(Ord. No. 04-20, § 2, 8-9-2004)
Sec. 34-86. - Penalties for violation.
Each day a nuisance is continued shall constitute a separate offense. Anyone committing a nuisance shall be guilty of a petty offense and, upon conviction, punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, imprisonment for no more than six months, or a combination thereof. In addition to the fine and/or jail sentence, the judge may impose community service and require that the defendant abate or cure the nuisance.
(Ord. No. 04-20, § 2, 8-9-2004)
