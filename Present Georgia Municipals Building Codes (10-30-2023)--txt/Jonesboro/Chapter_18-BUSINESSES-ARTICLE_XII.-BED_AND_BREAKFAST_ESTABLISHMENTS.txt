ARTICLE XII. - BED AND BREAKFAST ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 18-460. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bed and breakfast establishment means a structure occupied and operated by all of its owners, wherein lodging or lodging and breakfast only are provided to guests for compensation for a period no longer than 14 consecutive days. The dwelling unit in which the bed and breakfast takes place shall be the principal residence of the owner, and the owner shall live in the structure when the bed and breakfast establishment is in operation.
Guest means a person who pays a fee to the owner of a bed and breakfast establishment for the purpose of lodging at that establishment.
Lodging room means a room that is used for temporary occupancy for a fee.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-461. - Exceptions.
This article shall not apply to hotels, inns, motels, motor lodges, boardinghouses, roominghouses, or other establishments providing rooms to guests for compensation. None of these establishments shall be permitted in the city except as otherwise allowed by ordinances of the city. No bed and breakfast establishment shall be operated except as allowed in this article.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-462. - Temporary establishments.
This article shall not apply to bed and breakfast establishments being operated on a temporary basis for charitable or civic organizations. These establishments shall be permitted at the discretion of the mayor and city council upon application for no more than four consecutive days and for no more than eight days per calendar year.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-463. - License required and regulatory fee.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a bed and breakfast establishment without having first obtained a license. The annual regulatory fee for operation of a bed and breakfast establishment in the city shall be set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for the whole or any part of one year, and shall be paid at city hall not later than December 15 of the preceding year or upon the filing of an application for the license.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-464. - Guest register.
The owner shall keep a register of all guests of the bed and breakfast establishment. Such list shall be available for inspection by the city and/or its designee at all reasonable times.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-465. - Materials needed for license application.
An applicant for a bed and breakfast establishment license shall submit the following materials for review:
(1)
A floor plan for the single-family dwelling unit, illustrating that the proposed operation will comply with all ordinances and regulations of the city, and all other governmental agencies or bodies;
(2)
A completed and signed application form;
(3)
A plat or survey of the subject real estate signed and sealed by a state registered land surveyor which shows all improvements located on the real estate; and
(4)
A nonrefundable administrative fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for processing the application. This fee is in addition to the regulatory fee which must be paid at the same time as the administrative fee but is refundable if the license application is not approved.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-466. - Inspection of premises.
No bed and breakfast establishment license shall be granted to any applicant until the bed and breakfast premises has been inspected by the building inspector, who finds that the electrical and plumbing systems of the premises are in safe and satisfactory condition. All establishments shall be constructed and maintained in accordance with the provisions of Chapter 16, Article II of this Code regarding construction codes as adopted and from time to time amended.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Cross reference— Construction codes, §§ 16-26—16-45.
Sec. 18-467. - License period.
All licenses granted under this article shall be issued for a period of one year or until December 31 of the year the application is made, whichever date is earlier.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-468. - Financial records.
All license holders shall maintain separate records which show all activities of their bed and breakfast establishment. All of such documents and related papers shall be available to the city and/or its designee at all reasonable times.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-469. - Council consideration of license.
In deciding whether or not to grant, renew, suspend or revoke a license under this article, the mayor and council shall consider the following:
(1)
Compliance by the applicant or license holder with the provisions of this article and all other relevant laws or ordinances, regulations and resolutions of the city and any other governmental body or agency.
(2)
The documented conduct of the business of the applicant or license holder.
(3)
The actual or proposed effect of the bed and breakfast on nearby properties, including but not limited to noise, pollution, dirt, property values and all other relevant considerations.
(4)
The actual or proposed effect of the bed and breakfast on the city including but not limited to the services and utilities of the city.
(5)
All other relevant issues, including but not limited to the public health, safety, morals, interest and convenience.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-470. - Zoning classification.
Bed and breakfast establishments shall be allowed in areas zoned "RS" (single-family residence district), areas zoned "RS" found within the historic district, and areas that were previously zoned "R3" immediately prior to their current zoning classification.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Cross reference— Zoning, §§ 86-6, 86-81, 86-156.
Sec. 18-471. - Conditions for operation.
No premises shall be utilized for a bed and breakfast establishment unless all of the following conditions have been met:
(1)
There shall be no more than eight lodging rooms and no less than three lodging rooms per dwelling unit used for a bed and breakfast establishment;
(2)
Each guest room must have its own private indoor lavatory and bathing facility. Where one guest room in a bed and breakfast establishment does not meet the requirements of this paragraph, the mayor and council may nevertheless issue a license under this chapter;
(3)
Adequate off-street parking must be available for the bed and breakfast establishment. No on-street parking for guests or owners of the bed and breakfast establishments shall be permitted;
(4)
The owner shall furnish clean bed linens, unused by any other person since the last laundering thereof, on all beds assigned to the use of any quest of the establishment;
(5)
The owner shall keep the closets and restrooms used in connection with the bed and breakfast establishment in a clean and sanitary condition;
(6)
No cooking by guests shall be allowed;
(7)
The entire dwelling unit shall contain at least 2,500 square feet of heated living area, and shall be detached from any other dwelling;
(8)
There shall be no outside evidence of a bed and breakfast establishment, and no changes within six months of the date of application by the applicant shall be made in the bed and breakfast structure that are visible from the exterior except the posting of one non-illuminated identification sign. The size of such sign shall not exceed the signable area of eight square feet and such shall be made of natural materials which are substantially similar to the facade materials of the principal structure. Such sign shall comply with the provisions of Chapter 86, Article VIII of this Code;
(9)
The bed and breakfast shall be totally operated in the principal existing structure and not to any degree in any accessory structure, unless the accessory structure was under the same ownership as the principal structure as of the effective date of this article and was in existence prior to the effective date of this article;
(10)
No more than three non-family workers shall be employed to work inside the premises;
(11)
Food shall be prepared in a sanitary manner. Storage of food and the temperature at which food must be stored shall be in accordance with applicable laws, rules and regulations of the state, county and city. No meals except breakfast may be served to any guest.
(12)
Bed and breakfast establishments shall be available for occupancy on a continuous basis except for repairs, renovations, or the absence of the owner.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Cross reference— Signs, §§ 86-278—86-300.
Sec. 18-472. - Activities restricted.
(a)
All bed and breakfast establishments located in an area zoned "RS" (single-family residence district), "RS" within a historic district, and areas that were previously zoned "R3" immediately prior to their current zoning classification, shall be used exclusively for residential and for bed and breakfast purposes. No type of home occupation or commercial activity shall be conducted on any portion of the subject real estate, except for the sale of memorabilia of a guest's stay at the bed and breakfast establishment provided such merchandise does not occupy more than five percent of the total heated floor space in the primary structure.
(b)
Provisions for receptions. Bed and breakfast establishments may utilize its premises for the purpose of hosting receptions. No reception shall be allowed on the premises unless all of the following conditions have been met:
(1)
The owner of the bed and breakfast establishment shall submit an application to and obtain a permit from the city and/or its designee prior to the reception event. The application for a permit shall contain the following information:
a.
The name and address of the establishment;
b.
The date and time of the reception event;
c.
Verification that the owner has obtained all applicable certificates of occupancy from the local fire marshal prior to the event; and
d.
Verification that the owner shall comply with the city's noise ordinance found at Chapter 34, Article IV of this Code.
(2)
Parking of vehicles shall be in compliance with all applicable laws, ordinances and regulations. The police department may enforce such temporary controls as may be necessary to alleviate congestion;
(3)
The reception event can only be held between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 9:00 p.m.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Cross reference— Noise, §§ 34-121—34-123.
Sec. 18-473. - License voided upon sale.
A bed and breakfast establishment license shall be immediately voided upon the sale or transfer of the subject real estate or the sale of the business.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-474. - Discrimination prohibited.
No owner/operator of a bed and breakfast establishment shall discriminate regarding his guests on the basis of race, color, sex, religion, creed or natural origin.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
Sec. 18-475. - Applicability of hotel/motel tax.
All bed and breakfast establishments shall be subject to the hotel/motel tax of the city.
(Ord. No. 04-10, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-2004)
