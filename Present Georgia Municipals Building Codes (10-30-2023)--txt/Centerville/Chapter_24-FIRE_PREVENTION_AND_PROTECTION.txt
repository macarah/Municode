Sec. 24-52. - Driving over fire hose.
No person shall drive any vehicle over fire hose except upon specific orders from the fire chief or other officer in charge where the hose is used.

No person shall park any vehicle or otherwise cause any obstruction to be placed within 20 feet of the entrance to any fire station or other place where fire apparatus is stored, or within 15 feet of any fire hydrant or cistern.
(Code 1992, § 7-3; Ord. of 11-5-1985, art. 5, § 6.5)

All motor equipment and all personal cars of fire department personnel shall have right-of-way over all traffic when responding to an alarm.

DIVISION 2. - OUTDOOR BURNING

Sec. 24-84. - Burning banned; burning permitted; other burning; permit required; penalties and fines.
(a)
Burning banned. Open burning is banned from May 1 - September 30 of each year. Air curtain destructors may not be used for land clearing from May 1 - September 30 of each year.
(b)
Burning permitted. Open burning is allowed from October 1 - April 30 of each year. Limited burning of yard debris such as leaves, pine straw and small limbs as well as the use of air curtain destructors for land clearing are allowed from October 1 - April 30 of each year subject to the follow restrictions:
(1)
Burning is allowed between the hours of 10:00 a.m. and one hour before sunset. No smoldering or hot coals should be present after one hour prior to sunset.
(2)
No burning is allowed on windy days (winds of ten mph or greater) or on days when atmospheric conditions (cloudy, overcast or raining) would cause smoke to remain low to the ground.
(3)
Burning must, at all times, be attended by an adult over the age of 21. Fires must never be left unattended.
(4)
A water hose, long enough to reach the fire, must be on hand and ready to use if needed.
(5)
Fires may not be started with petroleum-based products.
(6)
All open burning fires must be at least 25 feet from: all structures, including but not limited to, fences made of combustible material and limbs no more than six inches in diameter.
(7)
All open burning fires must be limited to a pile of not more than six feet × six feet and at least 25 feet from roads with speed limits greater than 35 miles per hour.
(8)
Burning in barrels is prohibited.
(9)
Garbage may not be burned. Garbage includes, but is not limited to: any waste material such as plastic, styrofoam, fiberglass, covered wire, tires, clothing, furniture, mattresses, boxes, papers, stumps, root balls and kudzu vines.
(10)
Material may not be moved from one property to another to be burned.
(11)
Logs and other clean wood may be burned. Clean wood is natural wood which has not been painted, varnished or coated with any similar material; has not been pressure treated with preservatives; and does not contain resins or glues such as may be found in plywood or other composite wood products.
(12)
Persons carrying out open burning under this division may be required to extinguish their fire if it adversely affects another's enjoyment of life, health or use of their own property.
(13)
During the allowable period for burning, local fire officials may prohibit burning at times when atmospheric conditions or local circumstances make burning hazardous.
(c)
Other burning. Certain types of fires/burning are allowed all year without regard to burn bans as follows:
(1)
Recreational burning. Fires in which only clean wood or logs with a total area of three feet or less in diameter and two feet or less in height are allowed for recreational; religious ceremony; cooking or outdoor warmth and not for the purposes of burning garbage or yard waste are allowed.
(2)
Recreational burning times. Recreational fires are allowed between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
(3)
Windy or adverse conditions. No recreational burning is allowed on windy days (ten miles per hour or more) or on days when adverse atmospheric conditions (cloudy, overcast or raining) would cause smoke to remain low to the ground.
(4)
Adult supervision required. Recreational fires/burning must be attended at all times by an adult at least 21 years of age or older.
(5)
Water supply or fire extinguisher required. A water hose or fire extinguisher must, at all times, be on hand that can reach the fire and can be readily used if the need arises.
(6)
No petroleum-based starters or accelerants. Recreational fires may not be started or accelerated with petroleum-based products.
(7)
Fires burned for the purpose of cooking food for immediate human consumption, such as on a barbeque grill or pit are allowed.
(8)
Fires in an outdoor fireplace, chimney, fire bowls or other similar device are allowed if burning clean wood or logs only.
(d)
Permit required.
(1)
Burn permits are required for any burning of yard debris allowable under this division. Residents must first contact the Georgia Forestry Commission at 1-800-428-7337, or by going online at the Georgia Forestry Commission's website to obtain a burn permit. After obtaining a permit from the Georgia Forestry Commission, residents must contact the Centerville Fire Department at 478-953-4050 and provide the following permit information: name, address, phone number and Georgia Forestry Commission burn permit number.
(2)
Special permits are required by the Centerville Fire Department for all of the following:
a.
Bonfires must be 50 feet from structures; 100 feet from property lines; and completely out by 11:00 p.m.
b.
Large yard waste burns must be 100 feet from property lines; 300 feet from structures; limbs of not more than 12 inches in diameter; and a pile not more than 12 feet × 12 feet, (may be subject to the GA EPD land clearing burning rules).
c.
Land clearing when using an air curtain destructor (may be subject to the GA EPD land clearing burning rules).
(e)
Penalties and fines.
(1)
Unauthorized outdoor burning:
a.
First violation - advised of corrective actions and burn laws - warning; no fine.
b.
Second violation - $50.00 fine.
c.
Third violation - $100.00 fine.
d.
Fourth and subsequent violations - Minimum fine $150.00.
(2)
Burns requiring permit:
a.
First violation - advised of corrective actions and burn laws - warning; no fine.
b.
Second violation - $50.00 fine.
c.
Third violation - $100.00 fine.
d.
Fourth and subsequent violations - Minimum fine $150.00.
(Code 1992, § 7-10(2); Ord. No. 2004-23, 3-2-2004; Ord. No. 2016-3 , 6-7-2016)

ARTICLE V. - FIREWORKS

Sec. 24-201. - Purpose of regulations.
The purpose of this article is to protect and safeguard the public health and welfare, and to prevent fires, and to eliminate unnecessary noises, explosions and nuisances.
Sec. 24-202. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Fireworks.
(1)
The term "fireworks" means any combustible or explosive composition or any substance or combination of substances or article prepared for the purpose of producing a visible or audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration, or detonation, including blank cartridges, balloons requiring fire underneath to propel them, firecrackers, torpedoes, skyrockets, Roman candles, bombs, sparklers, and other combustibles and explosives of like construction, as well as articles containing any explosive or flammable compound and tablets and other devices containing an explosive substance.
(2)
The term "fireworks" does not include model rockets and model rocket engines, designed, sold, and used for the purpose of propelling recoverable aero models, toy pistol paper caps in which the explosive content does not average more than one-fourth grain of explosive mixture per paper cap nor toy pistols, toy cannons, toy canes, toy guns, or other devices using such paper caps; nor shall the term "fireworks" include ammunition consumed by weapons used for sporting and hunting purposes; nor shall the term "fireworks" include inflatable balloons in which human beings are transported.
Sec. 24-203. - Compliance with state law and permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to offer for sale, expose to sale, sell, use, possess or explode within the city any fireworks, except as permitted by state law and pursuant to a permit issued by the city clerk's office. Applications for permit shall be made to the city clerk's office on city forms, and accompanied by a permit fee in the amount provided in the city fee schedule in chapter 22. The fire chief mayplace conditions on the permit as he deems necessary or advisable for public safety purposes. Anyone aggrieved by the fire chief's denial of a fireworks permit may appeal the decision to the city administrator.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 25-10-2.
Sec. 24-204. - Seizure.
The police chief or other official authorized by the city are authorized and directed, at any time, at the expense of the owner, to seize, take, or cause to be removed to a place of safety, any stock of fireworks or combustibles possessed, used, displayed, sold, offered or exposed for sale, or held in any manner in violation of this article.
State Law reference— Authority, O.C.G.A. § 25-10-6.

DIVISION 2. - KNOX BOX

Sec. 24-291. - Purpose and intent.
The City of Centerville Fire Department recognizes the importance and need of providing rapid entry into locked buildings. Delays in the fire department gaining rapid entry reduces the occupant's chance of survival in a fire, the chance for substantial property damage increases because of delays in gaining entry to the structure and firefighter safety and survival may be compromised because of said delays. Additionally, delays in providing prompt pre-hospital care to the sick and injured as a result of locked/secured structure may/will have a negative outcome on patient recovery. To assist in gaining entry into locked/secured facilities/structures, the City of Centerville Fire Department hereby adopts a Rapid Entry System utilizing the "Knox Company" Knox-Box and/or 1.5″ to 2.5″ Locking FDC Plug.
( Ord. No. 2018-3, 6-19-2018 )
Sec. 24-292. - For commercial, residential, and other locations with restricted access through locked doors and gates.
(a)
New structures not completed or otherwise in existence as of June 19, 2018 shall be equipped with a key lockbox at or near the main entrance or other such location required by the fire marshal before a certificate of occupancy or business license shall be issued.
(b)
Existing structures that are classified as any of the following shall be equipped with a key lockbox at or near the main entrance or such other location required by the fire marshal on or before January 1, 2019:
(1)
Commercial or industrial structures protected by an automatic fire alarm system and/or automatic suppression system that is secured in a manner that restricts access during an emergency;
(2)
Multifamily residential structures comprised of three or more units in which access to the building or common areas or mechanical or electrical rooms within the building is denied through locked doors;
(3)
Commercial structures comprised of three or more units;
(4)
Any building or facility containing any quantity of hazardous materials, which would require compliance with Title III of SARA (Superfund Amendment Reauthorization Act);
(5)
Nursing care facilities;
(6)
Governmental structures;
(7)
Communities in which access to the community is denied through locked gates;
(8)
Educational and daycare facilities.
(c)
All newly constructed structures, communities, or complexes subject to this section shall have the Knox-Box, a 1.5″ to 2.5″ Locking FDC Plug, and/or means to access community gates, operational prior to the issuance of a certificate of occupancy.
(d)
All structures, communities, or complexes in existence shall have the Knox-Box, a 1.5" to 2.5" Locking FDC Plug, and/or means to access community gates, installed and operational within 90 days of being notified of the ordinance from which this section is derived.
(e)
The fire marshal shall designate the type of Knox-Box, a 1.5″ to 2.5″ Locking FDC Plug, and/or means to access community gates, to be implemented within the city and shall have the authority to require all structures to use the designated system.
(f)
The owner or operator of a structure required to have a KNOX key lockbox shall, at all times, keep keys in the lockbox that will allow for access to the following:
(1)
Keys to locked points of egress, whether on the interior or exterior of such buildings;
(2)
Keys to locked mechanical equipment rooms;
(3)
Keys to locked electrical rooms;
(4)
Key to elevator controls;
(5)
Keys to the other areas as directed by the City of Centerville Fire Department.
(g)
The fire marshal shall be authorized to implement rules and regulations for the use of the Knox-Box, a 1.5″ to 2.5″ Locking FDC Plug, and/or means to access community gates.
(h)
Any person who owns or operates a structure, community, or complex subject to this section shall be subject to the penalties set forth in all sections of this Code for any violation of this section; provided that the maximum fine for a conviction for a violation of this section shall be $500.00.
(i)
All requests for the designated Knox-Box, a 1.5" to 2.5" Locking FDC Plug, and/or means to access community gates, shall be coordinated through the fire marshal's office and have the location approved on each site.
(j)
This article shall take effect immediately upon its passage.
( Ord. No. 2018-3, 6-19-2018 )
Sec. 24-293. - Installation and location.
(a)
All "Knox Boxes" (and/or Knox Locking Vaults) for new construction shall be recess mounted into the building/structure at a height of not less than six feet above the ground (surface grade) nor more than eight feet above the ground (surface grade). The "face plate" of the "Knox" box shall be flush with the exterior façade of the structure/building. All "Knox" boxes (and/or Knox Locking Vaults) shall be located next to the main entrance (or as close as reasonably possible to) on the address side of the structure/building or at such other location as required by the fire marshal.
(b)
All "Knox Boxes" (and/or Knox Locking Vaults) for existing structures shall be mounted on the building/structure at a height of not less than six feet above the ground (surface grade) nor more than eight feet above the ground (surface grade). All "Knox" boxes (and/or Knox Locking Vaults) shall be located next to the main entrance (or as close as reasonably possible to) on the address side of the structure/building or at such other location as required by the fire marshal.
( Ord. No. 2018-3, 6-19-2018 )
