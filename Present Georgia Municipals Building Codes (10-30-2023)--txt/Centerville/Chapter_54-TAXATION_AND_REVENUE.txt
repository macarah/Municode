ARTICLE II. - AD VALOREM TAXATION

Sec. 54-19. - Amount of assessment.
All real and personal property shall be assessed for the purpose of ad valorem taxation at 40 percent of the fair market value.
(Code 1992, § 16-26; Ord. No. 90-8, § 1, 7-3-1990; Ord. No. 2006-16, § 1, 11-7-2006; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 1, 11-10-2008)
Sec. 54-20. - Millage rate levied.
The millage rate applicable to the assessed value of the property shall be as established from time to time by the city council.
(Code 1992, § 16-27; Ord. No. 90-8, § 2, 7-3-1990; Ord. No. 93-11, 12-7-1993; Ord. No. 2006-16, § 2, 11-7-2006; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 2, 11-10-2008)
Sec. 54-21. - Determination of fair market value.
The fair market value of property as finally determined for county ad valorem property taxation will be the fair market value of property for the city's ad valorem tax purposes.
(Code 1992, § 16-28; Ord. No. 90-8, § 3, 7-3-1990; Ord. No. 2006-16, § 3, 11-7-2006; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 3, 11-10-2008)
Sec. 54-22. - Making of returns.
All persons owning property subject to ad valorem tax shall make return thereof as required by the tax assessors of the county and such persons shall not be required to make another return to the city.
(Code 1992, § 16-29; Ord. No. 90-8, § 4, 7-3-1990; Ord. No. 2006-16, § 4, 11-7-2006; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 4, 11-10-2008)
Sec. 54-23. - Penalty for failure to timely pay ad valorem tax.
In any instance in which any person willfully fails, on or after July 1, 1981, to pay, within 120 days of the date when due, any ad valorem tax owed the City of Centerville, such person shall pay, in the absence of a specific statutory civil penalty for the failure, a penalty of five percent of the amount of tax due and not paid at the time such penalty is assessed, together with interest as specified by law. After 120 days from the imposition of the initial penalty, an additional penalty of five percent of any tax amount remaining due shall be imposed, together with interest as specified by law. If any tax amount remains due after 120 days from the imposition of such additional penalty, a penalty of five percent shall be imposed, together with interest as specified by law. Should any tax amount remain due 120 days after such date, a penalty of five percent shall be imposed, together with interest as specified by law. The aggregate amount of penalties imposed pursuant to this section shall not exceed an amount equal to 20 percent of the principal amount of the tax originally due.
(Code 1992, § 16-30; Ord. No. 90-8, § 5, 7-3-1990; Ord. No. 2006-16, § 5, 11-7-2006; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 5, 11-10-2008; Ord. No. 2017-2 , 2-21-2017)
Sec. 54-24. - Due date.
All ad valorem taxes shall be payable on or before December 20 of each year.
(Code 1992, § 16-31; Ord. No. 90-8, § 6, 7-3-1990; Ord. No. 2006-16, § 6, 11-7-2006; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 6, 11-10-2008)
Sec. 54-25. - Interest rate.
Except as otherwise expressly provided by law, taxes owed to the City of Centerville shall bear interest at an annual rate equal to the bank prime loan rate as posted by the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System in statistical release H. 15 or any publication that may supersede it, plus three percent, to accrue monthly. Such annual interest rate shall be determined for each calendar year based on the first weekly posting of statistical release H. 15 on or after January 1 of each calendar year. Interest shall begin to accrue from the date the tax is due until the date the tax is paid. For the purposes of this Code section, any period of less than one month shall be considered to be one month. This Code section shall also apply to alcoholic beverage faxes.
(Code 1992, § 16-32; Ord. No. 90-8, § 7, 7-3-1990; Ord. No. 2006-16, § 7, 11-7-2006; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 7, 11-10-2008; Ord. No. 2017-2 , 2-21-2017)
Sec. 54-26. - Homestead exemption for persons aged 70 years or over.
(a)
Granted. Each resident of the city who is 70 years of age or over is granted an exemption on that person's homestead from all city ad valorem taxes for any city purposes, including, but not limited to, taxes to retire bonded indebtedness in the amount of the assessed value of that resident's homestead as defined and qualified in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-40, except that land which is included in that homestead and which exceeds one acre shall not have the value thereof exempt under O.C.G.A. § 48-5-40 et seq. (herein after referred to as "the Act").
(b)
Application and affidavit requirement. The governing authority of the city or its designee shall provide certificate and application forms for the exemption granted by the Act and shall require with the initial application an affidavit by the owner as to the age of the owner and such other information as may be necessary to determine the eligibility of the owner for the exemption.
(c)
Application due date. The exemption granted by the Act shall be claimed and returned as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-50.1, except that any applicant seeking an exemption under the Act shall file a written application on or before June 30 of the year in which such exemption is sought.
(d)
Continuation without reapplication; notice of ineligibility. After any such owner has filed the proper affidavit as provided in the Act, and has been allowed the exemption provided in the Act, it shall not be necessary that he make an application and file such affidavit for any year thereafter, and such exemption shall continue to be allowed to such owner. It shall be the duty of any resident of the city who has claimed the homestead exemption provided for in the Act to notify the governing authority of the city or its designee in the event he becomes ineligible for any reason to receive such homestead exemption.
(e)
Exclusive. The exemption granted by the Act shall be in lieu of and not in addition to any other homestead exemption from city ad valorem taxes.
(f)
Nonapplicable to certain taxes. The exemption granted by the Act shall not apply to nor affect any county taxes for county purposes, county school district taxes for educational purposes or state taxes.
(g)
Exemption not proratable; runs with land for each calendar year. Any homestead exemption granted under this section shall be for the entire calendar year beginning January 1 through December 31 regardless of any change in ownership or status of the property that makes it ineligible for exemption in subsequent years. There will be no prorated taxes due for or during the calendar year that the city receives notice of ineligibility as contemplated in subsection (d) of this section.
(Code 1992, § 16-33; Ord. No. 94-14, § VIII(1—7), 9-12-1994; Ord. No. 2008-13, 12-16-2008)
