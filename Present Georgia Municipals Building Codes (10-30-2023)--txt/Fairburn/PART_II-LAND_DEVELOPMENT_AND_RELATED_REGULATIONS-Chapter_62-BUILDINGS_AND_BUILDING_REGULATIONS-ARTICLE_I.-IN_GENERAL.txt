ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Adoption of standard codes.
(a)
Adoption by reference. The following codes, as adopted and amended by the state department of community affairs, along with the referenced appendices, are adopted by reference as though they were set forth fully herein:
International Building Code, 2018 Edition.
International Residential Code, 2018 Edition.
International Plumbing Code, 2018 Edition.
International Mechanical Code, 2018 Edition.
International Fuel Gas Code, 2018 Edition.
International Energy Conservation Code, 2015 Edition.
National Electrical Code, 2017 Edition.
International Property Maintenance Code, 2012 Edition.
International Existing Building Code, 2012 Edition.
National Green Building Standard, 2008 Edition.
International Swimming Pool and Spa Code, 2018 Edition.
(b)
Administration and enforcement. The procedures set forth in chapter 1 of the Standard Building Code adopted in this section, as amended in "Standard Building Code—Attachment A", on file at the city clerk's office, shall be the procedures that govern the administration, enforcement and penalties for the codes referenced in subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
Fees. The fees for permitting and inspection of the various code related activities shall be as shown on the city fee schedule as amended from time to time.
(Code 1966, § 5-1; Ord. No. 96-29, 11-12-1996; Ord. No. 2013-24, § 1, 10-14-2013; Ord. No. 2014-06, 2-24-2014; Ord. No. 2015-21, § 1, 8-10-2015; Ord. No. 2-4-2020(1) )
Note— The city adopted the 2012 Edition of the Minimum Standard Codes in 2013 along with Georgia Amendments; these codes were in existence until December 31, 2019. On January 1, 2020 the D.C.A. adopted the 2018 Editions, leaving the National Electrical Code with the 2017 Edition and the International Energy Code upgraded to the 2015 Edition.
Sec. 62-2. - Permits and inspections.
(a)
Permit or license required. Except as provided herein, no persons shall begin building construction without a permit and/or a business license issued by the city as provided herein.
(b)
Building additions. Any addition to a dwelling in the city shall require a building permit. The building inspector will make as many inspections as he feels necessary to ensure a satisfactory job.
(c)
Building improvements. A building permit shall be required for any improvement on a dwelling other than painting, except an improvement costing $200.00 which shall not require a permit. The building inspector will make one inspection.
(d)
Conversion to multiple-family building. If an existing single-family dwelling is converted to a multiple-family building, a building permit shall be required. The alteration shall carry the same requirements as new construction.
(e)
Time for inspections. The city building inspector shall make inspections from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. (unless a special time is agreed upon) Monday through Friday. In the event the inspector cannot be reached within 48 hours of the time called, then inspection can be made by someone designated by the mayor.
(f)
Failure to procure inspection. Anyone who fails to call for an inspection at the proper stage of construction and continues on with the construction shall be subject to a fine.
(g)
Verification of business licenses. The building inspector shall have the responsibility and authority to check that anyone doing business in the construction field within the city limits possesses a city business license.
(Code 1966, § 5-1.1; Ord. No. 67-20, 11-27-1967)
