Chapter 23 - ELECTIONS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 23-1. - Rules and regulations adopted.
All elections, special elections, referendums, and other types of elections in the city, except political party primaries, shall be held in accordance with the Georgia Elections Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-1-1 et seq., except as may be provided in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-1, 7-11-2005)
Sec. 23-2. - Management of elections.
All elections held in the city shall be held under the management of an election superintendent appointed by the mayor and council who shall have all of the powers and duties as set forth in the Georgia Elections Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-1-1 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-27, 7-11-2005)
Sec. 23-3. - Day of general election for mayor and council.
The general election for mayor and council of the city shall be held on the first Tuesday next following the first Monday in November of each odd-numbered year.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-4, 7-11-2005)
Sec. 23-4. - Polling place; times open.
(a)
Location. The polling place for municipal elections shall be the Campbell Elementary School, 91 Elder Street, Fairburn, Georgia 30213.
(b)
Hours. The polling place shall be open from 7:00 a.m. until 7:00 p.m. in the day of a general or a special election.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-6, 7-11-2005; Ord. No. 2009-6, § I, 6-8-2009)
Sec. 23-5. - In person voting equipment to be used; paper ballot.
In all elections by the people the electors voting in person shall use the Diebold AccuVote-Touchscreen Ballot Station or paper ballot, hand tabulated as provided in the Georgia Elections Code.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-7, 7-11-2005; Ord. No. 2009-6, § II, 6-8-2009)
Sec. 23-6. - Absentee voting equipment to be used; paper ballot.
In all elections by the people the electors voting by absentee ballot shall use the Dievold AccuVote-Optical Scan Tabulator or paper ballot, had tabulated, as provided in the Georgia Elections Code.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-8, 7-11-2005; Ord. No. 2009-6, § III, 6-8-2009)
Secs. 23-7—23-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - VOTERS

Sec. 23-31. - Residence requirement; registration.
All residents of the city who are qualified electors as set forth in the Georgia Elections Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-1-1 et seq., shall be eligible to register as voters in the city by registering with the city clerk or with the county registrar or as otherwise provided by the Georgia Elections Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-1-1 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-14, 7-11-2005)
Sec. 23-32. - Registration of electors, county system.
The city shall use the county voter registration list. Any person who is a resident of the city and who is registered as an elector with the county board of registrars shall be eligible to vote in a primary or general election or referendum in the city. For all other purposes, the city clerk is designated as the municipal registrar of the city.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-17, 7-11-2005)
Sec. 23-33. - Registration of voters by county.
The city clerk is hereby authorized to cooperate with the county registrar in the registration of voters both for county elections and city elections, and to that extent the city clerk is authorized to accept an assignment from the county registrar to register voters in county and state elections.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-18, 7-11-2005)
Secs. 23-34—23-54. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - CANDIDATES

Sec. 23-55. - Qualifications of candidates for office.
All candidates for an elective office in the city shall have been residents of the city for a period of not less than 12 months immediately preceding the day such candidate would assume the duties of the office he seeks, shall not be less than 21 years of age, of good moral character, shall have committed no crime involving moral turpitude, and meet all other qualifications set forth in the Georgia Elections Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-1-1 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-22, 7-11-2005)
Sec. 23-56. - Closing date for qualifying and for filing notice of candidacy.
(a)
The time for qualifying and for filing notice of candidacy for the offices of mayor and members of the city council shall be in accordance with O.G.C.A. § 21-3-91 et seq.
(b)
Candidates shall file their notice of candidacy in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 2005-16, § 8-23, 7-11-2005)
