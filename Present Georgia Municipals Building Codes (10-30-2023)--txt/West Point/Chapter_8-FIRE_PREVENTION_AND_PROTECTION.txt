ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Rules and regulations of state safety fire commissioner adopted.
There is hereby adopted by reference the 2000 Georgia State Minimum Standard Fire Prevention Code with the 2000 amendments, published as the 2000 Standard Fire Prevention Code with 20002 amendments by the State of Georgia Office of Insurance and Fire Safety Commissioner.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-2. - Fire department established.
The fire department of West Point, Georgia is hereby established and the department shall be organized to consist of a fire chief, one (1) supervisor per shift, and not less than three (3) additional persons per shift with the supervisor. The fire department shall be named the West Point Fire Department.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-3. - General powers of the fire department.
The West Point Fire Department shall have the authority to:
(1)
Protect life and property against fire, explosions, and hazardous materials;
(2)
Detect and prevent arson;
(3)
Administer and enforce the laws of this state; the rules, regulations, and codes adopted by the mayor and council of West Point, Georgia related to the prevention and suppression of fires, explosion, or injuries from hazardous materials and explosions and the protection of life and property from such hazardous;
(4)
Conduct programs of public education in fire prevention and safety;
(5)
Conduct emergency medical services and rescue assistance, subject to Chapter 11 of Title 31 (O.C.G.A.) and subject to the approval of the city;
(6)
Control and regulate the flow of traffic in areas of existing emergencies, including rail, highway, water and air traffic; and
(7)
Perform all such services of a fire department as may be provided by law or which necessarily appertain thereto.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-4. - Fire equipment; emergency right-of-way and use.
(a)
All fire apparatus and equipment is hereby given and granted the exclusive right-of-way over and through all streets, avenues, alleys, and public thoroughfares in the city while enroute to emergency alarms, and it shall be unlawful for any person or persons to in any manner obstruct or hinder the apparatus or equipment.
(b)
All emergency vehicles of the fire department, while proceeding on official business, shall be operated in strict accordance with the requirements of the State of Georgia regarding the operation of emergency vehicles, and each departmental member assigned to the operation of emergency vehicles shall familiarize himself or herself with the requirements of the law and govern himself or herself accordingly. Any operator violating the provisions of the state law shall be liable for disciplinary action.
(c)
All apparatus of the fire department shall be operated by personnel who have proper state diver's license. All personnel shall be seat belted into position while the apparatus is in motion.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate motorized or non-motorized vehicles through the streets where the fire department is assembled for the purpose of conducting business.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any persons to follow by automobile, bicycle, motorcycle or any other vehicle in the city within three hundred (300) feet of any fire truck or similar apparatus when such fire apparatus is responding to an emergency.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to use a gong, siren, whistle, or signal device similar in kind to the siren, gong, whistle or signal device used by the fire department of the city, to signal the approach of fire apparatus; provided, however, that nothing contained in this section shall be construed to apply to such gongs, or devices on any locomotive, engine, police vehicle or ambulance.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-5. - Fire equipment; fire hose.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to drive any vehicle over any fire hose laid on any street, avenue, alley, and public thoroughfare or lot.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
State Law reference— Crossing fire hose, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-248.
Sec. 8-6. - Fire equipment; fire hydrants.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use water from any fire hydrant, whether public or private, for any purpose other than for the extinguishment of fires, or lawful city purposes.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to tamper or interfere with any hydrant of the city, except by authority of the fire department or the water department.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or cause to be placed upon or about fire hydrant any rubbish, building materials, fence or other obstruction of any character, or in any manner obstruct, hinder, or delay the fire department in the performance of its duties in case of fire.
(d)
No person or persons shall fasten to any fire hydrant any guy rope or braces, nor stand any vehicle or solid obstruction within fifteen (15) feet in all direction of any such hydrant.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
State Law reference— Stopping, standing, parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-203.
Sec. 8-7. - Fire equipment; damages.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully and knowingly injure or damage in any way any of the automotive fire apparatus or other equipment or paraphernalia of the fire department.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct in any manner any fire station.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-8. - False alarms.
It shall be unlawful for any person to give or cause to be given or aid or abet in the giving of any false alarm of fire with intent to deceive the department, or to pull the hook of any call station or call box with such intent. The city shall maintain the right to impose fines and fees associated with false alarms causing a fire department emergency response.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
State Law reference— Transmitting false report of fire, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-27.
Sec. 8-9. - Private use of fire equipment.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to take away or use any fire apparatus or equipment for any private purpose or for any person willfully and without proper authority to remove, take away, keep or conceal any tool, appliance, equipment or other ordinance used in any way by the fire department.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-10. - Parking of trucks carring hazardous materials.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any truck upon which there is a container containing or used for carrying hazardous materials on any street in the city, except that such truck may be parked temporarily for business purposes, and except in cases of emergency. When parked in emergency cases, it shall be the duty of the driver to place a danger signal to protect persons using such streets. It shall be the driver/owner to remove the said container in a timely manner as to not allow the container become permanent.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-11. - Hazardous materials; charges for cost of clean-up to responsible party.
(a)
As used in this section, the term:
(1)
Discharge means leakage, seepage, or other release of hazardous materials on land or into a river, stream, lake, pond, sewer, or into the air.
(2)
Hazardous materials mean any material which because of its quantity, concentration, or physical, chemical, or infectious characteristics may:
a.
Cause or significantly contribute to an increased mortality or an increase in serious irreversible or incapacitating reversible illness; or
b.
Pose a substantial threat or potential hazard to human health or to the environment when improperly treated, stored, transported, disposed or, or otherwise managed.
(b)
In the event of any discharge of any hazardous materials which results from the transportation, storage; holding, detention, delivery for transportation, or acceptance of hazardous materials in the city, the person, firm, or corporation responsible for the discharge of hazardous materials shall remedy the effects of such discharge at no cost to the city. When deemed necessary by the fire chief, the city may take action necessary to prevent, abate, or remove such hazardous materials. In such an event, the city may recover from any shipper, carrier, bailor, bailee, or any person responsible for such storage, transportation, holding, detention, delivery, or acceptance all cost incurred by the city in the prevention, abatement, or removal of any such discharge or threatened discharge, including reasonable attorneys' fees incurred with respect to recovery.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Secs. 8-12—8-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - FIRE PREVENTION

Sec. 8-41. - Fire prevention; enforcement.
The code hereby shall be enforced by the chief of the department or his/her designee.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-42. - Fire prevention; enforcement—Duty.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the fire chief, or his designee, to inspect or cause to be inspected as often as may be necessary all building, particularly all mercantile buildings, manufacturing plants, warehouse, garages, hotels, boarding house, rooming houses, theaters, auditoriums and all places of public assemblage, for the purpose of discovering the violation of any fire preventive law or any fire hazard and ascertaining and causing to be corrected any conditions liable to cause fires and to see that all places of public assemblage, hotels and rooming houses have sufficient and unobstructed facilities for escape therefrom in case of fire.
(b)
Open burning in the city limits shall only be allowed upon receipt of a City of West Point, Georgia burn permit.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow to accumulate or to keep in any part of any building or in any alley, sidewalk, street or premises within thirty (30) feet of any building any rubbish, trash, waste paper, excelsior, empty boxes, barrels or other combustibles which shall constitute a fire hazard. Noncombustible dumpsters may be allowed with approval of city planners office and fire chief.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit, stack or store greater than twenty-five (25) square bales (weighing approximately thirty-five (35) pounds each) or equivalent of hay or straw within one hundred (100) feet of any building located inside the City of West Point. Temporary decorations are not considered storing.
(e)
It shall be unlawful to keep excelsior or other packing material in any other than metal or wood metal lined boxes or bins having self closing or automatic covers. All refuse and trash from rooms where packing or unpacking is done shall be removed daily.
(f)
It is unlawful for any person to cause or create anywhere within the city, or to permit on any premises under his or her control, any situation or condition that is conducive to or likely to cause or permit the outbreak of fire or the spreading of fire. Any situation or condition conducive to the outbreak of or spreading of fire is declared to be a fire hazard. The violation of or failure to comply with any law pertaining to the storage, handling or use of inflammable oils, explosives, liquefied petroleum gases, or fertilizers and all wires and other conductors charged with electricity, is declared to be a fire hazard. The placing of stools, chairs or any other obstruction in the aisles, hallways, doorway, or exit of any theater, public hall, auditorium, church or other place of public assemblage with sufficient, accessible and unobstructed fire exits and escapes is also declared to be a fire hazard. The obstruction of any street, avenue, alley, fire hydrant or any other condition that might delay the fire department in fighting fire is declared to be unlawful.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-43. - Abatement of fire hazards; issuing order.
Whenever
any officer or member of the fire department shall find or discover any fire hazard or shall find in any building or upon any premises combustible or explosive material or dangerous accumulation or rubbish or unnecessary accumulation of paper, boxes, shavings or any other inflammable material, so situated as to endanger property by the probability of fire, or shall find or discover any violation of this chapter or any other law hazardous to public safety from fire, the fire chief, or his/her designee, shall order the fire hazard or danger from the fire forthwith abated and remedied and such order shall be complied with immediately by the owner or occupant of such buildings or premises. If the hazard or condition ordered abated and remedied is a violation of, or a failure to comply with any law, the fire chief shall report the matter to the city code enforcement officer for further actions as necessary.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-44. - Abatement of fire hazards; service of order; records.
Any order made under section 8-43 shall be in writing and may be served personally upon the owner or occupant of the premises or by leaving it with any person in charge of the premises or if the premises are unoccupied and the owner is a nonresident of the city, then by mailing a copy to the owner's last known post office address. One (1) notice to either the occupant or owner shall be sufficient The fire chief, or designee, shall keep a record of and copies of all such orders and notices and shall follow up such notices at the expiration of the time for compliance therewith and when complied with make proper entry, and if not complied with, file complaint with the city code enforcement officer for further actions as necessary.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-45. - Rapid entry system.
Each business, commercial property, church, school, and industrial building are required to provide the fire department with rapid entry. Such device as the "knox box" can be purchased from the fire department. This box is to contain entry key, emergency contact information and a diagram of the facility to highlight critical points of concern such as: utilities disconnect hazardous materials storage, etc. Such rapid entry systems must be approved by the fire department to include brand, key and installation location.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-46. - Address postings.
Each building shall have the numerical address posted on the front of the building. The numbers shall be visible from the center line of the street of which the address is given. The numbers shall be a contrasting color of the background of the building.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-47. - Smoke detectors.
All structures shall have a minimum of one (1) working smoke detector. The smoke detector shall be one hundred twenty (120) volt wired into the structures electrical system with a battery back up.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
State Law reference— Fire Protection and Safety, O.C.G.A. § 25-2-40 and installed per NFPA 70, National Electric Code.
Secs. 8-48—8-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - FIRE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 8-71. - Supervision of department.
The chief of the department shall be under the supervision of the city manager and shall have immediate superintendence and control over and be responsible for the care and condition of the fire apparatus and equipment. It shall be the chief's duty to see that all apparatus and equipment are ready at all times for immediate use.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-72. - Fire chief; powers.
(a)
The fire chief shall be responsible for the discipline of the members and is hereby so given authority to suspend or expel any member for refusal to obey orders or for misconduct or failure to do his or her duty at a fire.
(b)
The chief shall also have the right to summon any and all persons present to aid in extinguishing a fire or to aid in removing personal property from any building on fire or in danger thereof and in guarding the same.
(c)
At fires the chief shall have full power, control and command of all persons present and shall direct the scene of the fire apparatus and equipment, and command the firefighters in the discharge of their duties. The chief shall take such measures as deemed necessary in the preservation and protection of property and extinguishing of fires.
(d)
The fire chief shall be and is hereby designated local fire marshal for the city or the chief may appoint a fire marshal.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-73. - Fire chief; records.
The fire chief or his/her designee shall keep in convenient form a complete record of all fires. Such information shall include the time and location, construction of building, owner, occupancy, how extinguished, value of building and contents, members responding to the alarm, and any other information deemed advisable.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
Sec. 8-74. - Shift officers.
In the absence of the chief, the shift officers shall perform all the duties and have all the authority and responsibility of the chief as conferred by this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2011-1, 1-25-11)
