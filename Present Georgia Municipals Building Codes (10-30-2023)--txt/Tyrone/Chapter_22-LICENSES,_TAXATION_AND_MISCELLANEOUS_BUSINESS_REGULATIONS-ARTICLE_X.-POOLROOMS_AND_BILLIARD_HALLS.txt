ARTICLE X. - POOLROOMS AND BILLIARD HALLS

Sec. 22-541. - Scope of provisions.
Nothing contained in this article shall apply to any billiard or pool table kept for amusement at private residences.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-6-1), 11-18-2004)
Sec. 22-542. - Windows not to be obscured.
Windows of pool or billiard parlors shall not be covered or painted to obstruct the view from the street.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-6-2), 11-18-2004)
Sec. 22-543. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a billiard room between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m., or to permit any person to remain in such billiard room between such prohibited hours, except regular employees performing necessary labor on the premises.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-6-3), 11-18-2004)
Sec. 22-544. - Admission of persons under 18 years of age.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 18 years to play billiards in, or for any other purpose to enter or remain in, a billiard room or any establishment in which billiard tables are located and in which beer, wine or alcoholic beverages are offered for sale for consumption on the premises. This section shall not apply to a person under 18 years of age who is accompanied by a parent or guardian or who possess a written permit from a parent or guardian, which permit is witnessed by a notary public with seal, and the billiard room or the establishment in which the billiard tables are located derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or serves other than alcoholic beverages.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator, or manager of a billiard room or any establishment in which billiard tables are located in which beer, wine or alcoholic beverages are offered for consumption on the premises to permit any person under the age of 18 years to play billiards in, or for any purpose to enter or remain in such room or establishment unless the person under 18 is accompanied by a parent or guardian or who possess a written permit from a parent or guardian, which permit is witnessed by a notary public with seal, and the billiard room or the establishment in which the billiard tables are located derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or serves other than alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-6-4), 11-18-2004)
Sec. 22-545. - Disorderly conduct to be reported.
It shall be the duty of every person owning or managing a pool or billiard room in the town to report to the police of the town each and every person guilty of disorderly conduct in the pool or billiard room immediately after the same comes to the knowledge of such owner or manager.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-6-5), 11-18-2004)
Sec. 22-546. - Miniature pool or billiard tables.
Miniature pool tables or pocket billiard tables shall be operated subject to all of the provisions governing pool or billiard tables.
(Ord. No. 467, § 1(7-6-6), 11-18-2004)
