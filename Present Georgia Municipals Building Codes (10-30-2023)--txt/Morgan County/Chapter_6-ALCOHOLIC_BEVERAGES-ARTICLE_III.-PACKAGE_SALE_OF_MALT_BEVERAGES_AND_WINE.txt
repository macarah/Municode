Sec. 6-323. - Off-premises consumption.
Malt beverages and/or wine sold pursuant to a package license granted under this chapter will be for consumption only off the premises of the licensee.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(3); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(3))
Sec. 6-324. - Bond requirement.
Before any package license is granted, the applicant must post with the board of commissioners, along with the application, a cash bond or performance bond with an insurance company as surety. Said bond shall require the faithful observance and performance by the licensee of the rules and regulations contained in this section. Upon the violation of this section, or any part thereof, the amount of the bond to be forfeited will be determined by the seriousness of the violation as determined by the board. Said bond is to be approved by the board and shall be properly executed. Said bond shall be in the amount of $2,500.00 for a wholesale permit and $500.00 for a retail license.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(5)(d); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(5)(d))
Sec. 6-325. - Annual license fee.
The annual fee for a license shall be $500.00 per annum and shall be paid after acceptance and approval of the application by the board of commissioners, but prior to the issuance of such license. The fee shall be paid either in cash or by bank certified check. All licenses shall expire on December 31 of the year for which they were granted. No license fee shall be prorated.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(6); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(6))
Sec. 6-326. - Regulations.
(a)
No licensee shall sell or deliver any malt beverages and/or wine to any person except in the licensee's place of business. A licensee shall be permitted to load purchased goods in a customer's vehicle when the sale physically takes place and monies have been exchanged inside the place of business. A licensee may have a drive-in window and may deliver packaged malt beverages and/or wine through such window. Said drive-in window must be visible from the nearest street. "Curb service" type sales and sales to customers in vehicles parked in the parking area of the place of business are prohibited.
(b)
No licensee shall furnish, sell or offer for sale any malt beverages and/or wine at any times except:
(1)
Between 6:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m. on Mondays through Saturday; and
(2)
Between 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sundays.
(c)
No person shall sell or possess for the purpose of sale, any malt beverages and/or wine where such person does not have a license from the board of commissioners to sell or possess for sale such beverages and/or wine.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(7), (32); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(7), (32); Ord. No. 2012-003, § 1(exh. A), 10-2-2012)
Sec. 6-327. - Location and relocation.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-4-49, no license shall be granted to any person authorizing the location of a new retail package liquor licensed place of business or the relocation of an existing retail package liquor licensed place of business engaged in the retail package sales of distilled spirits within 500 yards of any other business licensed to sell package liquor at retail, as measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(4)(C); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(4)(C))
Sec. 6-328. - Wholesale permits.
Any wholesale distributor in malt beverages and wine licensed by the state or the agent of such licensed wholesale distributor may be granted a permit to distribute such malt beverages and wine in the county, upon application for such permit to the clerk, and upon the presentation of evidence satisfactory to said county that the applicant understands and will comply with all terms and conditions and provisions of the chapter governing the package sale of malt beverages and wine in the county, as said chapter may be amended, including, but not limited to, those under which malt beverages and wine licenses are issued, and upon payment of an annual fee of $100.00 for malt beverage wholesale distributors pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-43, a fee of $500.00 for wine wholesale distributors and a fee of $600.00 for wholesale distributors of both beer and wine; provided, however, that malt beverage wholesale distributors who have their principal place of business in the county shall pay an annual fee of $2,500.00.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(9); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(9))
Sec. 6-329. - Malt beverage taxes; distributor reports.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80 and O.C.G.A. § 3-5-81, there is hereby levied upon wholesale malt beverages distributors a tax on malt beverages as follows:
(1)
Malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, shall be at a rate of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15 1/2 gallons, and a proportional tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15 1/2 gallons.
(2)
When malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
Each wholesale distributor shall furnish to the board of commissioners a report itemizing all purchase invoices for malt beverages sold to each licensee in the county, on or before the tenth day of the month following such purchase. Said invoices shall show the amount of excise tax paid.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(10); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(10))
Sec. 6-330. - Wine taxes; exemptions; reports.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60, there is hereby levied on the first sale or use of wine by the package a tax of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the time rate on all fractional parts of a liter. Each month the first purchaser or user shall furnish to the board of commissioners a report itemizing each first purchase or use during the month. The report shall be furnished before the tenth day of the month following the month reported. The report shall show the amount of excise tax paid during the reported month. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-6-70 and O.C.G.A. § 3-6-71, no tax shall be levied by the board with respect to:
(1)
Wine sold to and used by established and recognized churches and synagogues for use in sacramental services only;
(2)
Any sale of wine which is exempt from taxation by the state or under the Constitution of the United States;
(3)
Wine sold to persons outside this state for resale or consumption outside this state; or
(4)
Wines which contain less than one-half of one percent alcohol by volume.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(11); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(11))
Sec. 6-331. - Financial responsibility.
Any applicant for a license must show financial responsibility to the satisfaction of the board of commissioners. Financial statements must be submitted with each completed application as prescribed by the board. Any applicant who becomes licensed must maintain a minimum of $1,000.00 stock of goods while said license is held by said licensee. The stock shall be subject to inventory audit at the discretion of the board.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(12); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(12))
Sec. 6-332. - Transportation.
(a)
All malt beverages transported or delivered to a licensee shall be packaged in the following manner, to-wit: seven-ounce, eight-ounce or 12-ounce sealed containers packaged four, six or 12 per package; 16-ounce sealed containers packaged six per package; 24-ounce sealed containers packaged only by the full case; 32-ounce sealed containers packaged only by the case; and draft beer in sealed containers which contain one-half barrel (15 1/2 gallons), one-quarter barrel (7 3/4 gallons) or any other size as draft beer is normally sold, transported and delivered in sealed containers within the state.
(b)
All wine transported or delivered to a licensee shall be packaged in containers specified in the Standards of Fill for Wine prescribed by the Department of the Treasury of the United States for wines shipped in interstate commerce. The federal regulations relating to Standards of Fill for Wine are adopted and incorporated by reference into this subsection. Wines manufactured and produced in the state shall be exempt from the provisions of this subsection to the extent that they are exempted from the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-6-28, as amended from time to time.
(Code 2000, § 32-204(14); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-204(14))
