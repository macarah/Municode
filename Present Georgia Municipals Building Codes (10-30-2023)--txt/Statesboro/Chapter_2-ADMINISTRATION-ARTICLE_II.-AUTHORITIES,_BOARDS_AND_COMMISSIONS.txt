ARTICLE II. - AUTHORITIES, BOARDS AND COMMISSIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-36. - Appointment.
(a)
This section shall apply to all authorities, boards, and commissions established pursuant to this chapter and to any and all bodies created pursuant to intergovernmental agreements and all other partnerships or other collaborations with public and/or private entities.
(b)
Should the establishment documents of any such body vest the general power to appoint member(s) in the city or mayor and city council the method described in this section shall be complied with in the order set out.
(c)
Body chairperson shall notify the city clerk of any vacancy or pending vacancy of a seat over which the city has appointment powers.
(d)
At next regularly scheduled council meeting city clerk shall notify mayor and council of the vacancy during department head reports. If public applications are required or requested such announcement shall be made at this time.
(e)
Mayor and council shall send any nominations to fill vacancy(ies) to city clerk via email prior to the next regular meeting of mayor and council.
(f)
City clerk shall produce a list of nominees and/or applications to be presented to mayor and council for consideration during executive session.
(g)
Vacancy shall be discussed in executive session upon motion to do so.
(h)
Any action taken by mayor and council as a result of the executive discussion, including but not limited to appointment of body member(s), shall be made in open session of the meeting.
(i)
This section shall not apply in the event the establishment documents vest appointment powers in particular elected or non-elected persons or positions.
(Ord. No. 2019-05, 5-21-19)

There is created within the city a beautification commission.

The beautification commission shall consist of a chairman and not more than ten members to be appointed by the mayor or presiding officer of the city, and four ex officio members, one member from the board of commissioners of roads and revenues of the county, one member from the board of education of the county, one member from the city council and one member to be appointed by the president of Georgia Southern University.

The term of office of each appointed member of the beautification commission shall be five years or until his successor is appointed and has qualified, except that members of the commission first appointed shall be appointed for such terms that the term of two members shall expire annually thereafter.
(Code 1987, § 2-73)

Immediately after their appointment, the members of the beautification commission shall meet and organize by electing one of their members as chairman and such other officers as may be necessary.
(Code 1987, § 2-73)
Sec. 2-50. - Vacancies.
Vacancies on the beautification commission occurring otherwise than by expiration of term shall be filled by the mayor or presiding officer of the council only for the unexpired term.
(Code 1987, § 2-74)
Sec. 2-51. - Finances.
The beautification commission, in the performance of its duties, may, with the approval of the mayor and council, cooperate with and accept funds from federal, state or local public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, and may expend such funds for the purposes of the beautification commission with the approval of the mayor and council.
(Code 1987, § 2-72)
Sec. 2-52. - Powers and duties.
(a)
The beautification commission shall have authority to establish, conduct and maintain the parks and trees of the city with the goal of maintaining and beautifying the city and preserving its trees, shrubs and flowers.
(b)
The beautification commission will also encourage action by local governments, property owners and others to beautify private and public properties, streets and passageways, and will publicly recognize the efforts of any person who makes an effort to help the city and the county be a more desirable place to live through the improvement of the ecology.
(c)
The beautification commission is also empowered to cooperate with any garden or civic clubs or other persons with the goal of the beautification, maintenance and preservation of the trees and parks of the city needing the services.
(Code 1987, § 2-71)

(b)
The One Boro Commission shall have the authority to prepare studies and reports strategies, education efforts and programs for the purpose of informing the governing body on policy matters related to promoting an inclusive workforce where job seekers are able to maximize their career potential by developing skills and educational credentials to earn a living wage and businesses with the talent they need to prosper.

Sec. 2-59. - Violence prevention.
The One Boro Commission will be responsible for convening with stakeholders at least once per calendar year, with additional meetings as needed, to develop action items deemed necessary to respond to and prevent upticks in violence, which shall be reported directly to mayor and council. Stakeholders may include members of city council, community members from districts disproportionately impacted by violence, clergy, members from violence prevention-related organizations, at least one youth representative, and representatives from the city police department, the county sheriff's office, Federal Bureau of Investigation, Drug Enforcement Administration, Georgia State Patrol, GSU Police Department, family violence task force, mayor's office, and the county school district.
(Ord. No. 2020-09, 11-17-20)

A member may be removed from the planning commission by a majority vote of the governing body for cause, for absenteeism at three successive called or regular meetings, or four or more meetings within any 12-month timeframe, for failure to attend required training, or for other reasons the governing body may deem appropriate.

The planning commission shall select one of its appointed members to serve as chairperson, who shall serve for a term of two years or until s/he is reelected or her/his successor is elected. The director of community development or her/his designee shall serve as secretary to the planning commission. (Ord. No. 2010-10, § 1, 3-2-10)
Sec. 2-74. - Meetings.
In order to conduct its business, the planning commission shall meet at a regular meeting every month, with the date, time and place being established by the planning commission and approved by the governing body. Special or called meetings of the planning commission may be held at the call of the chair, or director of community development if there is business to be brought before it; or at such other times as the planning commission may determine. The planning commission may adopt such by-laws, rules or procedures as appropriate and are not in conflict with this Code, the Zoning Procedures Act, or policies adopted by the governing body.

(Ord. No. 2010-10, § 1, 3-2-10)
Sec. 2-76. - Quorum.
A total of three planning commission members shall be present to constitute a quorum. A majority vote of the quorum shall be necessary to approve any decision, motion or recommendation.
(Ord. No. 2010-10, § 1, 3-2-10)
Sec. 2-77. - Voting.
Planning commission members attending a meeting shall vote on each matter placed before the planning commission. A member may abstain from voting only in the instance of a conflict of interest. Where a member has abstained from voting on a matter due to a conflict of interest, the nature of the conflict must be stated for the record, and the member shall have abstained from any form of participation in the matter.
(Ord. No. 2010-10, § 1, 3-2-10)
Sec. 2-78. - Powers and duties.
The planning commission shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
Recommendations on the comprehensive plan. Review the provisions of the city's comprehensive plan from time to time, and make such recommendations to the governing body as it deems appropriate concerning its adoption or amendment.
(2)
Recommendations on city planning documents, in general. Review the provisions of all other long-range or strategic planning documents, and make such recommendations to the governing body as it deems appropriate concerning adoption or amendment.
(3)
Recommendations on the adoption of land development ordinances. Provide the governing body with recommendations related to the adoption of ordinances whose principal purpose is to promote the orderly growth and development of the City of Statesboro.
(4)
Preparation and disbursement of studies and policy papers. Initiate and/or prepare studies, reports and policy recommendations for the purpose of informing or providing guidance to the governing body on matters related to orderly growth and development in accordance with the comprehensive plan.
(5)
Other powers. The planning commission shall also have such other powers, duties or responsibilities assigned to it by the governing body, explicitly assigned by this Code, or reasonably implied by this Code.
(Ord. No. 2010-10, § 1, 3-2-10)

Sec. 2-91. - Created.
In cooperation with the governing authorities of other area municipalities and counties, in order to guide and accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the municipality and the southeastern Georgia area which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity and the general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development, the Altamaha-Georgia Southern Area Regional Development Center, hereinafter referred to as the development center, is hereby created and established as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 50-8-30 et seq. The development center shall be organized and empowered as provided in this division.
(Code 1987, § 2-59)
Sec. 2-92. - Membership.
(a)
The commission shall consist of four members from each of the counties participating in the commission. Representation shall be on the following basis:
(1)
One delegate shall be appointed by the county commissioners.
(2)
One delegate shall be appointed by the mayor and city council of the county seat municipality.
(3)
One delegate of the minority race from each member county government shall be appointed jointly by the member governments within that county.
(4)
One delegate shall be named in one of the following ways: Any city, other than the county seat municipality with 2,000 population. If there is not a city that qualifies under the 2,000 population, then the county government, together with all other municipalities that participate will name the fourth member. If there are no other municipalities participating in the program, then the county government, together with the county seat municipality will together name the fourth member.
(b)
Appointments shall be made so that at least two representatives from each county are elected officials; one representing county government and one representing city government.
(Code 1987, § 2-60)
Sec. 2-93. - Term of office.
The terms of office for all members shall be three years, except that elected officials shall remain on the commission only as long as they hold the elected office that they held at the time of their appointment. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment which has become vacant. The appointing authority shall also have the power to remove any member it may appoint. All members shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1987, § 2-61)
Sec. 2-94. - Organization, rules and staff.
The commission shall elect a chairman and vice-chairman from among its appointive members. The term of office of the chairman and vice-chairman shall be for the one year or until reelected or their successors are elected. The commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the commission. The commission shall meet at least once a month at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the chairman or board may determine, shall adopt rules for the transaction of business and shall keep a record of its resolutions, transactions, findings and determinations, which record shall be a public record. The commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work.
(Code 1987, § 2-62)
Sec. 2-95. - Finances.
In the performance of its duties, the commission may cooperate with, contract with or accept funds from federal, state or local, public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, may spend such funds, and may carry out such cooperative undertakings and contracts. It may make expenditures for the purchase of required equipment and supplies. The expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts to the commission and contract receipts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the participating governing authorities. The contribution shall be at the rate of $0.40 per capita, based on the most recent U.S. Census of population, to be paid by arrangement between the county government and the participating municipalities.
(Code 1987, § 2-63)
Sec. 2-96. - Powers and duties.
It shall be the function and duty of the commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the entire southeastern Georgia area and each political subdivision therein. In general, the commission shall have such powers and duties and responsibilities as set forth in the General Planning Enabling Act of 1957, as amended, and Georgia Act 1066, 1970.
(Code 1987, § 2-64)

Vacancies on the Greener Boro Commission occurring other than by expiration of term shall be filled by an interim appointment for the unexpired term by the mayor and city council. The mayor and city council shall have the authority to remove any member of the Greener Boro Commission by a majority vote for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing. The mayor and city council in consultation with the Greener Boro Commission may appoint nonvoting ex officio members to said commission to provide administrative support and/or subject matter expertise. All members shall serve without compensation.
(Ord. No. 2022-02, 4-19-22)
Sec. 2-100.6. - Chair; rules of procedure and bylaws; meetings; records.
(a)
The Greener Boro Commission shall elect a chair from among its members who shall serve for a term of one year and who shall be eligible for re-election as chair.
(b)
The Greener Boro Commission shall make its own bylaws and rules of procedure and shall determine its time and place of meetings.
(c)
The transactions of the Greener Boro Commission are governed by the Georgia Open Meetings Act O.C.G.A. §§ 50-14-1 et seq., and it shall be the joint and several responsibility of the members to comply with the Georgia Open Meetings Act.
(Ord. No. 2022-02, 4-19-22)
Sec. 2-100.7. - Scope of authority.
The Greener Boro Commission shall have the authority to prepare studies, reports, strategies, education efforts and/or programs for the purpose of informing and advising the governing body on policy matters related to best municipal practices for environmental sustainability.
(Ord. No. 2022-02, 4-19-22)
Sec. 2-100.8. - Finances.
The Greener Boro Commission, in the performance of its duties, may, with the approval of the mayor and council, cooperate with and accept funds from federal, state or local public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, and may expend such funds for the purposes of the commission with the approval of the mayor and council. Any grants received will be done so pursuant to Resolution 2012-19 or any other subsequently adopted grant policy of the city. All purchases shall be made in compliance with the city's purchasing policy as set out in chapter 5 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 2022-02, 4-19-22)
