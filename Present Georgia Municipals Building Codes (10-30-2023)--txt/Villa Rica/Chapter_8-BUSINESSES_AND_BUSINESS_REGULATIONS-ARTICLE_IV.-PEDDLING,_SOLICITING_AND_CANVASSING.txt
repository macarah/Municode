Sec. 8-116. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable organization means any person, group or organization which complies with the Georgia Charitable Solicitations Act of 1988 (O.C.G.A. § 43-17-1 et seq.).
Educational institution means an entity organized and operated exclusively for educational purposes and which either:
(1)
Maintains a regular faculty and curriculum and has a regularly enrolled body of students in attendance at the place where its educational activities are regularly carried on; or
(2)
Is accredited by a nationally recognized, independent higher education accreditation body.
Peddler means any person who engages in the business of selling any goods, wares, merchandise or services or who solicits orders for such goods, wares, merchandise or services by going from house to house or place to place and who does not have a permanent place of business in the city and who is not employed by any person having a permanent place of business in the city.
Religious organization means an entity which:
(1)
Conducts regular worship services; or
(2)
Is qualified as a religious organization under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as now or hereafter amended, that is not required to file IRS Form 990, Return of Organization Exempt From Income Tax, under any circumstances.
Soliciting means any action which compels or attempts to compel another to purchase, donate or otherwise do business with the person doing the soliciting.
(Ord. No. 05-13-ADM, 1-3-2006; Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 8-117. - Registration required.
Any person 18 years of age or older who is peddling, soliciting or canvassing within the city, shall be required to register and obtain an identification card as provided by this article.
(Code 1979, 9-3-1; Code 1993, § 6-96; Ord. No. 05-13-ADM, 1-3-2006; Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 8-118. - Registration; fee; application for identification card.
(a)
Any person 18 years of age or older desiring to peddle, solicit or canvass within the city shall make application with the police department and pay a nonrefundable registration fee of $25.00 per day. The application shall provide the following information:
(1)
Name and description of applicant.
(2)
Address, both legal and local.
(3)
A brief description of the nature of the business and the goods to be sold.
(4)
The name and address of the applicant's employer, if other than the applicant, together with credentials establishing the exact relationship between the applicant and the employer (i.e., date of hire, position applicant holds).
(5)
If a vehicle is used, a description of the vehicle and tag number, as well as a copy of the driver's license of the designated driver.
(6)
Information on any convictions or pending cases of any crime, misdemeanor or violation of any municipal ordinance, the nature of the offense and the punishment or penalty assessed therefore.
(7)
The dates and times which the applicant proposes to peddle, solicit or canvass within the city.
(8)
Anticipated location of peddling or solicitation.
(b)
After completion of a favorable investigation as specified in section 8-119, the applicant shall:
(1)
Apply to the police department for an identification card which shall have a picture of the applicant and the day or days that the registrant is allowed to peddle, solicit or canvass in the city;
(2)
Obtain a police department issued safety vest which shall require a $25.00 refundable deposit.
(Code 1979, § 9-3-2; Code 1993, § 6-97; Ord. of 12-20-1994; Ord. No. 05-13-ADM, 1-3-2006; Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 8-119. - Investigation of applicants; consideration of approval.
(a)
Eligibility of an applicant is contingent upon an investigation which reveals the applicant's history is free from convictions, charges or accusations of a nature which, in the judgment of a reasonable person, could jeopardize the safety and property of the citizens and of the city in general. Such convictions, charges and accusations include, but are not limited to:
(1)
Felonies.
(2)
Misdemeanors of a high and aggravated nature.
(3)
Misdemeanors which would constitute moral turpitude.
(b)
After proper inquiry and investigation, the police department shall determine whether the applicant is eligible to be permitted. Each eligible applicant shall be issued an identification card indicating that he has been duly registered and showing thereon a photo ID and the days for which he has registered to peddle, canvass or solicit.
(c)
If, after proper inquiry and investigation, the police department shall determine that the applicant is not eligible, the police department shall deny the application and:
(1)
File the application, investigation documentation and written recommendation to deny the applicant at the police department where it will be maintained for two years; and
(2)
Notify the applicant by personal services or by first class mail to the address shown on the application.
It shall be conclusively presumed that notice by first class mail was received by the applicant and the failure of the applicant to receive such notice shall not be invalidate the action taken.
(Code 1979, § 9-3-3; Code 1993, § 6-98; Ord. No. 05-13-ADM, 1-3-2006; Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 8-120. - Exhibition of registration/identification card/safety vest.
(a)
Each registrant under this article shall at all times while in the city visibly display the registration card and shall exhibit the same when requested to do so by any law enforcement officer or by any municipal authority and by any person being solicited. Possession of this registration card shall not in any way represent an endorsement or approval of any product or project by the city.
(b)
No registration card shall be used at any time by any person other than the one to whom it is issued.
(c)
Each registrant under this article shall, at all times while peddling, soliciting or canvassing in the city, wear the issued safety vest as required in section 8-118(b)(2) and shall not allow a person who is not approved to wear the vest.
(Code 1979, § 9-3-4; Code 1993, § 6-99; Ord. No. 05-13-ADM, 1-3-2006; Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 8-121. - Unlawful acts.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle, canvass or solicit after sundown.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle, canvass or solicit within the fire zone of the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle, canvass or solicit without having registered with the police department in accordance with this article or to peddle, solicit or canvass without having on his person and in his possession an identification card issued in accordance with this article.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to alter or deface any permit or registration card issued under this article.
(e)
Violation of this article shall result in citation and punishment as set forth in section 1-8.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle, canvass or solicit who is under the age of 18 years of age.
(g)
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle food items prepared on site or which are otherwise prepared outside of a state licensed facility, except for the following conditions:
(1)
City-sponsored or -approved festivals,
(2)
Charitable fundraisers on private property provided the property owner has given written consent.
(Code 1979, § 9-3-5; Code 1993, § 6-100; Ord. No. 05-13-ADM, 1-3-2006; Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-2009; Ord. No. 10-04-CCO, 7-6-2010)
Sec. 8-122. - Exceptions.
(a)
The fee set forth in section 8-118 shall not apply to solicitations, sales or distributions made by educational, civic, religious or charitable organizations which have their principal place of activity in the city or which are otherwise approved by the mayor and council.
(b)
The fee set forth in section 8-118 shall not apply to persons selling, distributing or soliciting orders for farm and garden produce as raised or prepared by the vendor or members of his family, and homegrown so far as the sale of such commodities is authorized by law.
(c)
The fee set forth in section 8-118 shall not apply to persons selling, distributing or soliciting orders for local or area newspapers.
(d)
All persons and organizations as described in subsections (a), (b), and (c) of this section shall register at the police department and receive a permit at no charge before any solicitations, sales or distributions commence. This permit shall be presented upon the demand of any city, county or state official.
(e)
All persons and organizations as described in subsections (a), (b), and (c) of this section shall obtain a safety vest issued by the police department after having provided a refundable deposit of $25.00.
(Ord. No. 05-13-ADM, 1-3-2006; Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-09; Ord. No. 10-04-CCO, 7-6-2010)
Sec. 8-123. - Standards and guidelines for granting or denying approval.
In addition to all other requirements stated in this article, the city shall consider the following standards and guidelines in reaching its decision on whether to grant or deny an application to peddle, solicit, or canvass:
(1)
The report of the police investigation as required by this article, and any other reports that may be required;
(2)
The number of approvals already granted for similar entities within the city limits and in the trading area of the location for which an approval is sought;
(3)
The number of approvals should be limited to one week within a four-week period;
(4)
The criminal record, general good character and reputation of the applicant and/or the affiliated organization;
(5)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom;
(6)
The nature of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location, that is, whether the neighborhood is predominantly residential, industrial or business;
(7)
Whether the applicant has satisfied all requirements pertaining to the application process;
(8)
Consideration of the health, safety, location, parking and welfare of the citizens and preservation of surrounding neighborhoods, with reference to such factors as noise, traffic and loitering;
(9)
Consideration of the health, safety, and welfare of the participants to the peddling, soliciting and canvassing, including but not limited to age of participants, level of adult supervision, likelihood of subjection of participants to life threatening occurrences; and
(10)
The history or reputation of the location for criminal activity, including but not limited to prostitution or other sex offenses, crimes of violence, gambling, and illegal dealing in alcoholic beverages or drugs.
(Ord. No. 09-12-CCO, 12-1-2009; Ord. No. 10-04-CCO, 7-6-2010)
