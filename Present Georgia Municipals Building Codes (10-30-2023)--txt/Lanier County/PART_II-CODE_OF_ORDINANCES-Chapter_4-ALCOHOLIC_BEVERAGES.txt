Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
modified
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2 ("Alcoholic Beverages—Definitions") shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this chapter. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this chapter, shall have the same respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-2. - Qualifications.
modified
No retail alcoholic beverage license shall be granted to any person, unless such person is of legal age and has been a resident of the county for a minimum of one year prior to filing an application for such license; same requirements for a corporation.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-3. - Location of premises.
modified
No alcoholic beverage license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premise of the proposed location is situated beyond 100 yards from the property line of any church and beyond 200 yards from the property line of any school building, educational building, school ground or college campus, the same to be measured by way of the nearest traveled road, street, or highway, as provided by law. Each application for retail alcoholic beverage license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church or school or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this chapter with reference to this section.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-4. - Separate retail alcoholic beverage license.
modified
A separate retail alcoholic beverage license shall be required for each place of business.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-5. - Building specifications.
modified
(a)
No retail alcoholic beverage license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of said building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with the ordinances of the county and regulations of the state revenue commissioner and laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the building inspector. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the front of the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building.
(b)
Security camera requirements:
(1)
Any establishment licensed by the county to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages for off-premises consumption is required to install security camera systems of a type and number enumerated in this section within 90 days from the date of licensure.
(2)
Camera systems meeting the requirements of this section must be capable of producing retrievable images on a compact disc, digital versatile disc, or other digital memory device that can be made a permanent record. Camera systems meeting the requirements of this section shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to periodic inspection by the sheriff or the sheriff's designee or the code enforcement officer/marshal or the code enforcement officer/marshal's designee.
(3)
All camera systems shall consist of, at a minimum, the following:
a.
Video cameras with a minimum resolution of 640 × 480 lines (1,040 preferred) will be located at each exit door of the establishment with additional cameras located at each point of customer transaction (e.g., cash registers, teller machines, drive-thrus, etc.) and pointed at the customer. All video cameras must have signal-to-noise ratio of at least 48 dB.
b.
A recorder with retrieval capability. The recorder must record at a minimum resolution of 640 × 480 lines per frame (1,040 preferred). The recorder shall record at no less than 30 frames per second per camera.
c.
A monitor for playback review connected to the output of the recording device.
d.
If a switcher or multiplexer is used (either internal or external), video images shall not be recorded in multi-image display mode.
e.
All components must be compatible to produce sharp, detailed, video images. The line loss between the camera and the switcher, multiplexer or recorder shall not cause the signal to fall below 45 dB.
(4)
Analog video cassette recorders are not permitted as an acceptable recording medium unless the system is pre-existing prior to this ordinance and download to approved media in approved format is available.
(5)
Any digital video recorder must be capable of the following:
a.
The recorder must have a BNC, RCA, S-video (monitor output), FireWire or USB type output connector.
b.
The recorder must be able to produce retrievable video on a CD, DVD or other removable memory storage device in its native file format with the proprietary player.
c.
The recorder must also be able to produce retrievable video in an open file format such as AVI, DV, or MPEG format on a CD, DVD or other removable memory storage device. Any codec file that is required to play the open file format must be included in the CD, DVD or memory device.
d.
If alarm activation is incorporated into the recorder, the recorder shall record at the highest number of frames per second, the highest resolution, and the least compression available when the alarm is activated. The recorder should have a buffer capable of retaining five minutes of video prior to alarm activation.
1.
Responsibilities of owner. The business owner or the business owner's designee will be responsible for the maintenance and periodic checks of camera systems. In the event of an actual crime, the business will be responsible for producing a recording of the incident within 24 hours. The tape, CD or DVD will become the property of the Sheriff's Office to be used as evidence.
e.
Violation and penalties. Violation of any provisions of this section shall constitute an offense hereunder and be punished as follows;
1.
First offense. A fine of $200.00 and the court may impose a suspension of all alcoholic beverage licenses from the county for a period not to exceed 30 day.
2.
Second offense. An offense will be considered a second offense if it occurs within 365 calendar days from the date of the occurrence of the first offense. A second offense will incur a fine of $350.00 and all alcoholic beverage licenses at the establishment issued by the county will be suspended for a minimum period of 60 days.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-6. - Retailers packaging.
modified
All licensed retailers shall sell or offer to sell in the original unbroken package only and shall sell no package containing less than 50 milliliters each.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-7. - Other products or commodities.
modified
(a)
No retailer shall sell or offer for sale or display or keep in stock, at their place of business where distilled spirits are offered for sale, any other product or commodity .except the following;
(1)
Wines and beers, when properly licensed; and
(2)
Beverages containing no alcohol commonly used to dilute distilled spirits.
(b)
No beverages of any kind may be opened or consumed in said place of business.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-8. - No sale or delivery outside of the retailer's place of business.
modified
No retailer shall sell or deliver any alcoholic beverage to any person, except in said retailer's place of business.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-9. - When sales are prohibited.
modified
No retailer shall furnish, sell or offer for sale any alcoholic beverage at any of the following times:
(1)
At any time on Thanksgiving Day, Christmas Day or on Sundays;
(2)
At any time in violation of a local ordinance or regulation or of a special order of the governing authority; or
(3)
On any day before 6:00 a.m. or after 11:45 p.m.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-10. - Underage drinking.
modified
No retailer shall give, sell or offer to sell any alcoholic beverage to any person who is under the age of 21 years, or who is noticeably intoxicated, or who is of unsound mind, or is a habitual drunkard whose intemperate habits are known to said retailer. At any time, proper identification shall be considered the only legal proof of age which would relieve a person making a sale from responsibility thereof. The term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or an identification card authorized under O.C.G.A. §§ 40-5-100 through 40-5-104. The term "proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate and shall not include any traffic citation and complaint form.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-11. - Notice prohibiting underage sales.
modified
The holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages shall post in the most conspicuous place in their establishment a sign which shall contain the provisions of the laws of this state which deal with the unlawful sale of such items to underage persons and the penalties for violating such laws. The notices shall contain those provisions of the law of this state which the state department of revenue determines will best inform the citizens of this state of the relevant provisions of the law regarding the sale of alcoholic beverages to underage persons.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-12. - Stored alcoholic beverages.
modified
No licensed retailer shall keep any alcoholic beverage stored in any bonded or other type of warehouse in the county, nor shall be entered into any type of arrangement whereby alcohol ordered by them is stored for them by any licensed wholesaler. A retailer shall keep no inventory or stock of alcohol at any place except their licensed place of business, and within their licensed place of business their storage space for alcohol shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which they are licensed to do business.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-13. - Applications.
modified
Applications for a retail alcohol license shall be made on forms furnished by the governing authority as follows:
(1)
A retail alcohol license shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and no such license may be renewed. A licensee who desires to continue in the business during the next or subsequent calendar year must make a new application for such year on or before November 1st of the preceding year.
(2)
In addition to the completion and submission of application forms as required by the board of county commissioners, each applicant shall attach thereto a completed application form, an investigative consent form and a complete set of fingerprints, along with all attachments and requirements for a state license.
(3)
Before any retail alcohol license is granted, applicant must post with the governing authority, along with his application, a personal performance bond; said bond to be conditioned requiring the faithful observance and performance by the licensee of the rules and regulations contained in this chapter. Upon the violation of this chapter or any part thereof, the amount of the bond to be forfeited will be determined by the seriousness of the violations as determined by the governing authority after a hearing is held by the governing authority after three days' written notice is given to the violator. Said bond is to be payable to the county in the amount of $2,500.00 and said bond is to be approved by the governing authority and shall be executed in a form to be furnished by the county.
(4)
A notice of each application shall be advertised in the official gazette of the county once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the governing authority by which the application is to be acted upon.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-14. - Annual license fee and excise tax.
modified
The annual fee for a retail alcohol license shall be in an amount as established by the board of commissioners from time to time, and shall be paid prior to the issuance of any license, said fee to accompany the application and shall be either in cash or a bank certified check,
(1)
In addition to the annual retail alcohol license fee required, there is hereby levied an excise tax on the sale of distilled spirits by the package at either the wholesale or retail level, of which tax shall not exceed $0.22 per liter of distilled spirits, excluding fortified wine, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter, which shall be paid to the governing authority on all distilled spirits sold, displayed or stored in said county. Said tax shall be paid to the governing authority by the wholesale distributor on all distilled spirits sold to retailers in the county as follows: Each wholesale distributor selling, shipping, or in any way delivering distilled spirits to any retailer, shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery and shall remit the same together with a summary of all deliveries to each retailer on or before the tenth day of the month following. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this chapter for any wholesale distributor to sell, ship or deliver in any manner any distilled spirits to a retailer without having collected said tax. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this chapter for any retailer to possess, own, hold, store, display or sell any distilled spirits on which such tax has not been paid.
(2)
Each wholesale distributor shall furnish to the governing authority a summary of all purchase invoices for distilled spirits sold to each retailer in the county on or before the tenth of each month following such purchases. Said invoices shall show the amount of excise tax paid.
(3)
No county excise tax shall be imposed, levied, or collected in any portion of the county in which a municipality within the county is imposing the same tax on distilled spirits sold by the package.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-15. - Financial responsibility.
modified
Any applicant for a retail alcohol license must show, to the satisfaction of the governing authority, financial responsibility. Financial statements must be submitted with each completed application as prescribed by the governing authority. Forms of the same are to be furnished by the governing authority along with or included in the application forms for license.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-16. - Qualifications affecting licensing determination.
modified
(a)
Any applicant for a retail alcohol license must be of good character, and all managers, clerks, or other employees shall be of like character or application will not be considered. Corporate applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(b)
The standards of good character upon which the board of commissioners may base a determination of the applicant's qualifications for a license may include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
All applicants, managers, clerks and other employees of applicant are required to undergo a background check, with the results of said background check to be submitted to the governing authority of the county.
(2)
All persons selling and/or serving alcoholic beverages in a licensee's place of business shall have a server's permit as described in section 4-19 of this chapter.
(3)
a.
A person who has been convicted of the violation of any federal or state law or regulation respecting the manufacture, possession or sale of any alcoholic beverage or who shall have forfeited his bond to appear in court to answer charges for any such violation;
b.
Any person convicted of a felony who served any part of a criminal sentence, including probation within the ten years immediately preceding the date of receipt of submission of the application.
Any person who has been convicted of a misdemeanor who served any part of a criminal sentence, including probation within the five years immediately preceding the date of receipt of submission of the application.
Any person, regardless of the date of the conviction, who has been charged with a crime involving moral turpitude, any sexually related crime or any criminal offense related to taxes, gambling or alcoholic beverages; provided however, for the purposes of this section any first conviction for driving under the influence shall not count to disqualify the applicant/licensee from consideration.
(4)
A person who is the previous holder of an alcoholic beverage license and is, in the judgment and discretion of the board of commissioners, an unfit person to have a license because of the manner in which he conducted business thereunder as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law or regulation relating to such business.
(5)
Where any alcohol license has been revoked or suspended, no further alcohol license shall be issued to the holder of such revoked or suspended license for a period of 12 months from the date of the revocation or suspension.
(6)
For a location at which a previous alcoholic beverage license has been revoked or suspended, no further alcohol license shall be issued at such location for a period of 12 months from the date of the suspension or revocation.
(7)
For a partnership, if any member of the partnership would be disqualified under this section.
(8)
For a corporation, if any officer, manager or director thereof would be disqualified under this section.
(9)
The governing authority, in their discretion, may consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application or the proposed location of the business. If in their judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the application.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-17. - License applications presented in person.
modified
All applications for a retail alcoholic beverage license shall be presented in person.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-18. - Transferability.
modified
No retail alcoholic beverage license shall be transferable or assignable to any person or other location, and in the even that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender his license to the governing authority.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-19. - Servers' permits.
modified
(a)
Obtaining server's permits:
(1)
Any person who serves or distributes alcoholic beverages by virtue of any license or on behalf of any licensee holding a license issued pursuant to the terms of this chapter (excluding wholesale dealers and distributors) including, without limitation, server, bartender, cashier, clerk, checkout clerk, waiter, waitress, manager, owner, proprietor, volunteer, employee and otherwise, must, before serving or distributing alcoholic beverages, obtain a retail alcoholic beverage server's permit (i.e. server's permit), pursuant to the terms of this section. This does not apply to those persons who work in a licensed facility who do not sell or serve alcoholic beverages.
(2)
Each person who wishes to obtain a server's permit as described above must apply for such permit, must be fingerprinted, photographed and be issued a server's permit by the Lanier County Board of Commissioners prior to selling or serving alcoholic beverages.
(3)
Server's permits are valid only until the last day of the month of the licensee's license for the sale of alcoholic beverages issued by the county. The holder of the permit may apply for renewal within 30 days prior to the expiration date of the permit. The procedure for application for initial registration or renewal registration shall be established by the county.
(b)
No holder of an alcoholic beverages license issued under this chapter nor any agent of the licensee, whether a person, firm or corporation, shall allow any person to sell or serve alcoholic beverages in the business of the licensee unless that person shall have first been fingerprinted, photographed, and issued a retail alcohol beverage server's permit by the sheriff's department as provided herein.
(c)
Applicants for an alcoholic beverage server's permit shall provide all personal information required by the sheriff's department and county to establish the true identify and criminal history, if any, of the applicant. Applications for a server's permit shall be filed with the county on such forms as may be provided by the sheriff's department or county. Each applicant shall provide such information as may be required by the application and shall pay an application fee, to be established from time to time by the Lanier County, Georgia Board of Commissioners, at the time of filing the application. No application will be processed or considered until the applicant files the application and pays the application fee.
(d)
Persons holding a server's permit pursuant to this section are required to have the server's permit in their immediate possession and prominently displayed on their person during the hours they sell or serve alcoholic beverages. The permit shall contain the server's name, picture, and license number.
(e)
(1)
Initial retail alcoholic beverage server's permits shall not be issued to any person initially who has any felony conviction or felony bond forfeiture for any violation of law, regulation tantamount to law or ordinance regarding controlled substances and/or alcoholic beverages within five years of the date of application. Initial retail alcoholic beverage server's permits shall not be issued to any person initially who has any misdemeanor conviction or misdemeanor bond forfeiture for any violation of law, regulation tantamount to law or ordinance regarding controlled substances and/or alcoholic beverages within one year of the date of application. Further, no permit to serve shall be valid or issued to a person on parole or probation for the purpose of employment in a bar. This section shall not apply to violations of state and local traffic codes.
(2)
Any person previously issued a server's permit under the provisions of this code section shall be deemed qualified to hold a permit under this code section and no violations shall affect this holder except those occurring from the date of issuance.
(f)
Any person who has been issued a server's permit who is convicted of, pleads guilty to, pleads nolo contendere to, or forfeits bond for any violation of law, regulation tantamount to law or ordinance regarding controlled substances and/or alcoholic beverages shall immediately surrender said server's permit to the sheriffs department of Lanier County, Georgia as an invalid permit and shall not serve or distribute alcoholic beverages in the county. No person shall be allowed to continue to hold or exercise the privileges of and granted by a server's permit or shall have a server's permit renewed if convicted of, pleads guilty to, pleads nolo contendere to, or forfeits bond for any violation of law, regulation tantamount to law or ordinance of the status of a felony regarding controlled substances and/or alcoholic beverages for a period of five years. No person shall be allowed to continue to hold or exercise the privileges of and. granted by a server's permit or shall have a server's permit renewed if convicted of, pleads guilty to, pleads nolo contendere to, or forfeits bond for any violation of law, regulation tantamount to law or ordinance for the status of a misdemeanor regarding controlled substances and/or alcoholic beverages for a period of one year. No renewal permit shall be issued to a person on parole or probation for the purpose of employment in a bar. This section shall not apply to violations of state and local traffic codes.
(g)
(1)
Any server's permit issued pursuant to the terms of this chapter shall be subject to suspension or revocation for violation of this chapter.
(2)
When a person who has been issued a server's permit loses their permit for a violation of Title 3, O.C.G.A. or chapter 4 of the Lanier County Code, said person shall no longer be eligible to receive a permit to sell or serve alcoholic beverages.
(h)
(1)
Any person who has been issued a server's permit pursuant to the provisions of this chapter shall be entitled to a due process hearing prior to final action, suspension or revocation of a server's permit.
(2)
A server's permit may be temporarily suspended by the County Administrator or his designee, pending a hearing, in the event of special circumstances and conditions. When the person permitted to sell or serve alcoholic beverages makes a sale to an underaged person, the officer may immediately take possession of the permit issued hereunder at the making of the charge and the person so charged may not sell or serve any alcoholic beverage to anyone pending a hearing.
(3)
The holder of a server's permit shall be served with notice of violation of this section and shall be offered a hearing before the county administrator or his designee.
(i)
The terms retail alcoholic beverage server's permit and server's permit as used in this chapter shall be synonymous.
(j)
Violations of this section shall result in the following consequences;
(1)
The first violation shall result in a written warning being issued to applicant;
(2)
The second violation shall result in a fine to applicant as determined by the board of commissioners;
(3)
The third violation shall result in automatic loss of alcohol license.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)

modified

The licensee shall be held responsible for any acts of their employees in any violation of this chapter or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner.

modified
The business premises of the holder of a retail alcohol license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-22. - False statements.
modified
The making of any statement on an application for license to sell alcohol which shall be later found to be false, shall constitute grounds for revocation of said license.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-23. - Employment of persons under 21 years of age.
modified
No person under 21 years of age shall be employed in any capacity in any place of business licensed under this chapter.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-24. - Prohibited activities on premises.
modified
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punch-boards, vending machines, slot machines, pin-ball machines, lotteries or tickets or chances therein or the operation of any schemes for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any licensed retailer's place of business or in any room adjoining the same, owned, leased or controlled by licensee and any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of license.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-25. - Display of licenses.
modified
Licenses under this chapter shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which same was issued.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-26. - Compliance.
modified
No person shall engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages in the county without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this chapter. Any person violating the rules and regulations set out in this chapter shall be subject to revocation of their license and punishment as prescribed by the governing authority. In addition to the rules and regulations hereinabove set out, each licensee doing business in the county under this chapter shall comply with all laws of the state, federal laws, and rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner relating to the sale and distribution of alcoholic beverages in the state, and any violation of same shall subject said licensee to immediate suspension or revocation of their retail alcohol license and also shall subject said licensee to criminal prosecution by the proper authority as provided by law and the ordinances of the county.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-27. - Expiration of licenses.
modified
(a)
All licenses hencefore issued by the county board of commissioners will expire on December 31st of the year in which the license is issued.
(b)
All licenses to be renewed for the following year shall be applied for on or before November 1st and all renewals shall be passed upon at the regular December board of commissioners meeting each year.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-28. - Sale of alcoholic beverages on election days.
modified
(a)
The county board of commissioners hereby authorizes the sale of alcoholic beverages on any election day.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of any polling place or of the outer edge of any building within which such polling place is established on primary election days.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-29. - Carry-out of alcoholic beverage unlawful.
new
(a)
All alcoholic beverages sold or otherwise dispensed for consumption on the premises licensees shall be consumed only on the licensed premises. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove from the licensed premises any alcoholic beverages sold for consumption on the premises, and it shall be unlawful for the licensee to permit any person to remove from the licensed premises any alcoholic beverages sold for consumption on the premises. The licensee shall be responsible for ensuring that no person so removes any alcoholic beverages from the premises in any type of container.
(b)
Each consumption on the premises licensee shall post in a prominent place at each exit from the licensed premises a sign in substantially the following form: "It is a violation of the Code of Ordinances of Lanier County, Georgia to take any type of alcoholic beverage out of this establishment." Such sign shall be in uniform letters not less than one inch in height.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person purchasing alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises to leave the premises without paying for such alcoholic beverages.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for customers to gather outside the establishment of a consumption on the premises licensee and consume alcoholic beverages.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for the manager or any employee of a consumption on the premises licensee to allow persons to gather outside the establishment and consume alcoholic beverages.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
Sec. 4-30. - Brown bagging; when BYOB is allowed.
new
It is prohibited for any person to bring his own alcoholic beverage into any retail establishment without regard to whether such establishment is licensed to serve alcoholic beverages except for the following specific exceptions:
(1)
This section shall not prohibit any person dining at a retail establishment licensed to sell wine for consumption on the premises from bringing an unopened bottle of wine into said establishment for consumption where the establishment has a policy permitting same.
(Res. of 7-13-2015)
