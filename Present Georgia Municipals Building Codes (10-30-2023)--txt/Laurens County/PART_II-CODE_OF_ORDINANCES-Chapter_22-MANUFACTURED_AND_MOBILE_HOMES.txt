Chapter 22 - MANUFACTURED AND MOBILE HOMES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Decal required.
(a)
All persons owning a mobile home based in the county on January 1 of each year shall obtain from the tax commissioner a decal before May 1 as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-492. This requirement pertains to all mobile homes, including those exempt from taxation by homestead exemption or other provisions of state law.
(b)
Such decals shall be affixed to the mobile homes in such a manner as to cause them to be easily visible for inspection.
(c)
Owners of mobile homes located in the county after January 1 of any year shall be required to report their mobile homes to the office of the tax commissioner within ten working days.
(d)
Any person who violates this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and subject to be summoned to the magistrate court by any employee of the tax commissioner's office or any deputy sheriff and, upon conviction thereof, be subject to a fine of not less than $25.00 nor more than $200.00, except that upon receipt of proof of purchase of a decal prior to the issuance of a summons, the fine shall be in the maximum amount permitted by state law. In addition, any person convicted of a violation of this section shall be subject to payment of all collection costs, including, but not limited to, court costs.
(Code 1994, §§ 54-26, 54-27; Ord. of 3-20-1990, §§ 1, 3)
Secs. 22-2—22-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PARKS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-21. - Purpose.
The public health, safety, economy, good order, appearance, convenience, morals and general welfare require the harmonious, orderly and progressive development of land within the county. In keeping with the general intent of this article, it is the specific intent of this article to encourage the development of safe, sound, and stable mobile home parks within the county.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 1.2)
Sec. 22-22. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Mobile home means a structure, transportable on its own chassis, permanently equipped to travel on the public highways, that is used either temporarily or permanently as a residence or living quarters. Such unit shall be considered a mobile home whether or not the wheels have been removed and whether it is set on jacks, skirts, masonry blocks, or other foundation.
Mobile home lot means that portion of a mobile home park reserved for occupancy by a single mobile home unit and its accessory building, structures and uses.
Mobile home park means a parcel of land owned by an individual, partnership, or corporation which is used for or set apart for the purpose of supplying rental of three or more mobile home spaces or actual mobile homes.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), §§ 2.1—2.3)
Sec. 22-23. - Administration.
The county administrator is appointed by the board of commissioners to administer this article, approve or disapprove of preliminary plats and construction plans.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 1.3)
Sec. 22-24. - Liability.
This article shall not be construed as imposing upon the county any liability or responsibility for damages to any person or property caused by defect in any piping or appliance or installation of any products, nor shall the county or any official employee be held as assuming any such liability or responsibility by means of the inspection authorized under this article.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 1.4)
Sec. 22-25. - Nonconforming uses.
(a)
The lawful use of land existing at the time of the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived, although such use does not conform to the provisions herein, may be continued. However, if such nonconforming use is discontinued for a period of 90 days, the existing mobile home park must then meet the provisions of this article.
(b)
This article does not require anyone to relocate a mobile home due to lot line requirements.
(c)
This article does not prevent anyone from replacing an existing mobile home with another mobile home in the same location as long as the replacement conforms with subsection (a) of this section.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 1.5)
Sec. 22-26. - Variance.
When due to a particular reason experienced by a developer it is impractical to comply with the interpretations of this article, the commissioners shall have the authority to vary such requirements provided the intent and purposes of this article are not violated. Such variances and reason for granting them must be entered into the minutes of the commissioner's meeting in which the variance was granted.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 8.1)
Sec. 22-27. - Violations of article.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be subject to such penalties as are provided by law.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 9.1)
Sec. 22-28. - Court of jurisdiction.
The county board of commissioners may bring violations of this article before the magistrate of the county.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 9.2)
Sec. 22-29. - Reports by mobile home park operators.
(a)
Inventory of mobile homes. Every owner and operator of a mobile home park is required to give to the tax commissioner an inventory of all mobile homes based in the park as of January 1 of each year. This shall be done no later than January 20 of each year. The tax commissioner will make available an inventory of records as of January 1.
(b)
Removal of homes. Every owner, manager and operator of a mobile home park is required to report any mobile home removal from the park prior to the home being removed, if known to the park manager, operator or owner.
(c)
Map of mobile home park. Every owner, manager and operator of a mobile home park shall furnish to the tax commissioner an updated lot map of each park, and any changes shall be reported by January 1 of each year.
(d)
Notice of change in park ownership. Every owner, manager and operator of a mobile home park is required to notify the county of any change of ownership or of the name of any mobile home park within 30 days of such change.
(Code 1994, § 54-28; Ord. of 3-20-1990, § 2)
Secs. 22-30—22-46. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - DEVELOPMENT PLAN

Sec. 22-47. - Plan approval.
In order to construct, alter, or expand a mobile home park, a developer must secure approval of a development plan from the county board of commissioners. Such plans must be submitted at least 30 days prior to the time construction is to begin. the board of commissioners will then have 30 days to approve, disapprove, or request additional information on the development plan.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 3.1)
Sec. 22-48. - Plan requirements.
The mobile home park development plan shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name and address of applicant.
(2)
Name and address of proposed mobile home park, location, and legal description of the property.
(3)
A copy of the plan that is legible and showing the following:
a.
The total park area including the shape and dimensions of the site.
b.
All existing and/or proposed structures, facilities and lots.
c.
Location, size, and number of existing and proposed mobile home lots.
d.
Location and width of all existing and proposed roads, including entrances, exits and walkways.
e.
Provisions for providing water and sewer.
f.
Other information as may be required by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 3.2)
Secs. 22-49—22-69. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DESIGN AND CONSTRUCTION

Sec. 22-70. - Setbacks.
To allow for adequate lanes for fire protection, all mobile homes and all buildings and structures within a mobile park shall have a front setback of at least 25 feet from the right-of-way of any public street or highway and a minimum setback of ten feet from any road within the park area.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.1)
Sec. 22-71. - Drainage.
The ground surface in all parts of the mobile home park shall be graded and equipped to drain all surface water or stormwater in a safe and efficient manner.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.2)
Sec. 22-72. - Erosion control.
Exposed ground surfaces in all parts of every mobile home park shall be protected with a cover that is capable of preventing soil erosion and eliminating objectionable dust.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.3)
Sec. 22-73. - Numbering.
Every mobile home within the park shall be numbered by a means to provide ready visibility from an interior roadway.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.4)
Sec. 22-74. - Entrances and exits.
The mobile home park community entrance and exit streets shall be designed to provide safe access between the public street and the park.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.5)
Sec. 22-75. - Roads.
All roads, including private roads, within mobile home parks, excluding a private driveway, shall be designed and constructed in accordance with county road specifications and shall have a minimum right-of-way of at least 60 feet with a paved road bed of not less than 20 feet. All rights-of-way must be cleared of all obstructions, structures, and timbers and be grassed.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.6)
Sec. 22-76. - Cul-de-sac.
A cul-de-sac with a diameter of not less than 50 feet shall be constructed at the end of all deadend streets within the mobile home park.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.7)
Sec. 22-77. - Sanitary conditions.
The owner of the mobile home park shall ensure the mobile home park, its facilities, equipment, grounds, roads, buffers, fences, and mobile home lots are maintained in a clean, orderly, safe, and sanitary condition.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.9)
Sec. 22-78. - Health, safety, and welfare.
In approving a mobile home park, the county board of commissioners may attach additional conditions to protect the health, safety and welfare of both the occupants of the mobile home parks and the occupants of surrounding property.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.10)
Sec. 22-79. - Green space/buffer zone.
The perimeter boundaries of all mobile home parks shall provide for a 50-foot green space or buffer zone between the exterior property lines of the park and the adjoining lot, tract, or parcel of land; provided, however, that such green space or buffer zone shall not be required where a state/county road or highway is the division line between the mobile home park and the adjoining property, or where the mobile home park road is the division line.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.11)
Sec. 22-80. - Green space/buffer zone restrictions.
There shall be no manmade improvements allowed in the green space or buffer zone.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 4.12)
Sec. 22-81. - Lot size.
Mobile home lot/sites shall have an area not less than that required by the county health department.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 5.1)
Sec. 22-82. - Floodplain area.
No lots shall be located in an area designated by the Federal Emergency Management Agency as a floodplain.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 5.2)
Sec. 22-83. - Lot location.
All mobile home lots must abut an interior road which has direct access to a public road.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 5.7)
Sec. 22-84. - Tie downs.
Each mobile home shall have tie downs or other services securing the stability of the mobile home.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 6.1)
Sec. 22-85. - Skirting.
Each mobile home shall be skirted on all sides.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 6.2)
Sec. 22-86. - Steps.
Steps and/or landings are required for mobile homes at all doors.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 6.3)
Sec. 22-87. - Water supply.
An adequate, safe, potable supply of water shall be provided for each mobile home. The source of the supply shall be through a public water system with each mobile home connecting to the water lines or, when such a system is not available, the mobile home community must be serviced by a supply approved by the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division or the health inspector.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 7.1)
Sec. 22-88. - Sewage.
An adequate and safe sewage disposal system shall be provided for each mobile home in accordance with county on-site sewage management regulations. Collection systems, sewage treatment facilities, or individual septic tanks shall be approved by the health inspector prior to installation.
(Ord. of 4-3-2007(1), § 7.2)
