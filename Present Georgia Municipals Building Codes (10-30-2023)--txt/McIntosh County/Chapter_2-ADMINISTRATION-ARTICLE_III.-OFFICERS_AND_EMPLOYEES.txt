ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-55. - Use of equipment.
Usage of county-owned equipment by employees of the county in off-duty employment positions is hereby prohibited. Each department head and elected official is directed to inform all employees of his department or elected office to cease and desist from such activities.
(Res. of 1-14-1997)
Sec. 2-56. - Outside employment.
An employee of the county subject to the personnel policy shall have the consent of the county manager before accepting outside employment.
(Res. of 1-14-1997, amd. 8)
Secs. 2-57—2-85. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - OFFICERS

Sec. 2-86. - County officers.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall appoint qualified persons to each of the following positions for the county:
(1)
County manager.
(2)
County attorney.
(3)
County clerk.
(4)
County auditor. 1
1 Nominees are to be assembled and presented to the board by the county manager.
(b)
The duties of these officials shall be as enumerated under state law and by resolution of the board of commissioners. The board of commissioners may change such duties and responsibilities at any time, in accordance with state law, and the officials shall serve at the will and pleasure of the board, subject to the right to contract.
(c)
Each of the appointed positions listed in subsection (a) of this section have job descriptions which are on file in the county clerk's office. Nothing contained within those job descriptions shall serve to prevent the assignment of additional duties.
(Ord. of 5-12-2020(1))
Sec. 2-87. - Consolidation.
Nothing contained in this article shall prevent the consolidation, at the discretion of the board of commissioners, of one or more of the county official positions. Nothing contained herein shall prevent one individual from acting as a county official in one or more county positions subject to the direction of the board of commissioners.
Sec. 2-88. - County manager.
(a)
Position created; purpose. There is created the position of county manager to provide administrative assistance to the board in the performance of its duties and responsibilities.
(b)
Powers and duties. The county manager as the chief executive officer of the government of the county shall be responsible to the board of commissioners for the proper and efficient administration of the affairs of the county, and shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
To direct and supervise the administration of all county offices and departments for which the board is responsible, or may hereafter be responsible, and specifically the construction, maintenance and operation of all county roads, bridges, drains, buildings and other public works, and the care and maintenance of all real and personal property owned by the county. The county manager shall be the chief official in directing the county's economic development strategies and plans;
(2)
To appoint and, when in his discretion the welfare of the county requires it, suspend, discharge, transfer or remove all employees for whom the board is responsible, excepting the county clerk and county attorney, who are appointed directly by the board. The county manager shall not fire a department head without the consent of the commission but shall be empowered to discipline department heads in any other manner without the commission's consent. He may delegate to any department head such powers with respect to subordinates within the officer's department or office;
(3)
To see that all ordinances, resolutions and orders of the board and all laws of the state subject to enforcement by him or by officers or department heads subject under this section to his direction and supervision are faithfully executed;
(4)
To prepare and submit the annual budget to the board and to execute the budget adopted by the board, approving all disbursements and expenditures as budgeted and/or authorized by the board;
(5)
To confer with and act as a liaison between the commission and all boards, such as the county health department, leisure services, planning and zoning, board of elections and registration, industrial development authority, tax assessors board, and board of equalization, whose missions are not under the direct control and supervision of the board, but who are dependent upon appropriations of the board for their continued operation;
(6)
To confer with and advise all elected or appointed officials of the county who are not under the immediate control of the board, but who receive financial support from the board, such as the sheriff, clerk of court, tax commissioner, state court judge, state court solicitor, probate and magistrate court judge;
(7)
To examine regularly at periods fixed by the board the accounts, records and operations of every county board, commission, department, office and agency which receives appropriations from the board; to make regular monthly reports to the board on county fiscal and other affairs; to keep the board fully advised on the financial condition of the future needs of the county; and to make such recommendations on county affairs as he deems necessary for the efficient operation of the county;
(8)
To supervise the keeping of all records, books of account, bookkeeping, etc., of the county;
(9)
To supervise and approve the issuance of all purchase orders of the county;
(10)
To submit to the board at the end of each fiscal year a complete report on the finances and administrative activities of the county for the preceding year;
(11)
To carry into execution such other powers and duties as may be prescribed from time to time by the board.
All reports by the manager shall be made to the full board in either a regular or called meeting of the board.
Sec. 2-89. - County clerk.
(a)
Position created; purpose. There is created the position of county clerk to provide administrative assistance to the board in the performance of its duties and responsibilities.
(b)
Powers and duties. The board of commissioners shall appoint a clerk, whose salary shall be determined by the board, to be paid weekly out of the county treasury, and it shall be the duty of said clerk to attend all meetings of the board of commissioners and keep complete and orderly minutes of all such meeting.
(1)
The clerk shall file and keep in the order of their date all original orders and papers, petitions, applications, and other papers addressed to the board of commissioners concerning county business.
(2)
The clerk shall also keep on record and in a separate book the payment of all money out of the county treasury or county depository by order of the chairperson of the board of commissioners, giving the amount and date of said such payments and the persons to whom paid and for what purposes paid.
(3)
Said clerk shall also keep a book showing a full and detailed statement of all accounts and other indebtedness contracted by the board of commissioners. All the books, files, and records required to be used or kept in the office of the board of commissioners shall always be ready and open to inspection of any taxpayer of the county during normal working hours.
(4)
Said clerk before entering on the discharge of his duties shall be required to give bond in any amount to be determined by the board for the faithful performance of his duties and shall take the same oath as required by commissioner.
(5)
Said clerk shall hold said office at the pleasure of the board of commissioners. The clerk shall work in conjunction with the county manager and shall be subject to the manager's oversight; however, the county manager may not discharge the clerk of his duties but shall be given the authority of any disciplinary actions provided in the personal policy except for termination.
Sec. 2-90. - County attorney.
(a)
Position created; purpose. There is created the position of county attorney to provide legal assistance to the board in the performance of its duties and responsibilities.
(b)
Powers and duties. The board of commissioners shall appoint a county attorney whom shall serve at the pleasure of the board. The county attorney may be discharged upon a vote of a majority of the board. The county attorney shall:
(1)
Act as a legal advisor, attorney, and counsel to the board of commissioners and all of its officers in matters relating to their official duties;
(2)
Prepare or supervise preparation of all contracts, bonds, and other instruments in writing in which the county government is concerned;
(3)
Perform such other duties as may be required by law, ordinance, or resolution of the board of commissioners. The county attorney may not represent any county official or employee if the representation of the county official or employee conflicts with the interest of the county government. In such cases, the board of commissioners may employ separate counsel to represent the county official or employee in which the county government is obligated to provide representation for such county official or employee. If needed, the board of commissioners may appoint other attorneys to represent the board or to assist the county attorney upon such terms and conditions as the board may consider appropriate. No person who serves as county attorney or deputy county attorney may represent any client before the board of commissioners. No person who is a member of the same law firm as the county attorney may represent any client before the board of commissioners. The compensation of the county attorney shall be fixed by the board of commissioners upon such terms and conditions as maybe approved by the board; and
(4)
The county attorney shall work in conjunction with the county manager but is not subject to any oversight from the county manager. The county manager is authorized to engage the services of the county attorney on behalf of the board of commissioners and provide such supervision as may be required to accomplish said duties.
