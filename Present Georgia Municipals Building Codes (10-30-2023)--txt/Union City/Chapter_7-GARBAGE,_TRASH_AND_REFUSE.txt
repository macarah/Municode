ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 7-1. - Purpose.
It is declared to be the policy of Union City, Georgia, in furtherance of its responsibility to protect the public health, safety, and well-being of its citizens and to protect and enhance the quality of its environment, to institute and maintain a comprehensive city-wide program for all solid waste management which will assure that solid waste facilities, whether publicly or privately operated, do not adversely affect the health, safety, and well-being of the public and do not degrade the quality of the environment by reason of location, design, method of operation or other means and which, to the extent feasible and practical, makes maximum utilization of the resources contained in solid waste referred to in this chapter as recovered material.
It is further declared to be the policy of Union City to educate and encourage generators and handlers of all solid waste to reduce and minimize to the greatest extent possible the amount of solid waste which requires collection, treatment, or disposal through source reduction, reuse, composting, recycling, and other methods to promote markets for and engage in the purchase of goods made from recovered materials and goods which are recyclable. The mayor and council of the City of Union City does hereby ordain and enact into law the following:
(Code 1976, § 10-101; Ord. No. 99-20, § 1, 8-17-99)
Sec. 7-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other nonmanufacturing activities as defined in section 12-8-22(4.1) of O.C.G.A.
Disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of solid waste occurs and includes but is not limited to landfilling and solid waste thermal treatment technology facilities as defined in section 12-8-22(8) of O.C.G.A.
Industrial solid waste means waste generated by manufacturing or industrial processes or operations. Such waste includes but is not limited to fertilizer, agricultural chemicals, food and food related products and by-products as defined in section 12-8-22(12.1) of O.C.G.A.
Inert waste means wastes that will not or are not likely to cause production of leachate of environmental concern. Such wastes are limited to earth and earth-like products, concrete, cured asphalt, rock, bricks, yard trimmings, stumps, limbs, and leaves. This definition excludes industrial and demolition waste not specifically listed above (Section 391-3-4-.06 of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management).
Litter means discarded materials of every kind as defined in section 16-7-42(1) of O.C.G.A.
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash, and sanitary waste in septic tanks and means solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds; and day use recreation areas. The term includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste, but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural, or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations as defined in section 12-8-22(18) of O.C.G.A.
Open dump means a disposal facility at which solid waste from one or more sources is left to decompose, burn or to otherwise create a threat to human health or the environment as defined in Section 391-3-4-.01(44) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Public or private property means the right of way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any park, playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area; and residential or farm properties, timberlands, or forests, as defined in section 16-7-42(2) of O.C.G.A.
Recovered materials means those materials which have known use, reuse, or recycling potential and have been diverted or removed from the solid waste stream for sale, use, reuse or recycling as defined in section 12-8-22(25) of O.C.G.A.
Recovered materials processing facility means a facility engaged solely in the storage, processing, and resale or reuse of recovered materials as defined in section 12-8-22(26) of O.C.G.A.
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage, or defect as defined in section 12-8-22(31) of O.C.G.A.
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires as defined in Section 391-3-4-.19(2.i) of the Georgia Rules of Solid Waste Management.
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires not otherwise exempted under Section 391-3-4-.19(5.g) [of the Georgia Rules of Solid Waste Management] for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user, or disposal facility as defined in section 12-8-22(32) of O.C.G.A.
Solid waste means any garbage or refuse; sludge from a wastewater treatment plant, water supply treatment plant, or air pollution contract facility; and other discarded material including solid, liquid, semisolid, or contained gaseous material resulting from industrial, commercial, mining, and agricultural operations and from community activities, but does not include recovered materials; solid or dissolved materials in domestic sewage; solid or dissolved materials in irrigation return flows or industrial discharges that are point sources subject to permit under 33 U.S.C. Section 1342; or source, special nuclear, or byproduct material as defined by the federal Atomic Energy Act of 1954, as amended (68 Stat. 923) as defined in section 12-8-22(33) of O.C.G.A.
Solid waste handling means the storage, collection, transportation, treatment, utilization, processing or disposal of solid waste, or any combination of such activities as defined in section 12-8-22(34) of O.C.G.A.
Tire retailer means any person engaged in the business of selling new replacement tires as defined in section 12-8-22(39) of O.C.G.A.
Transfer station means a facility used to transfer solid wastes from one transportation vehicle to another for transportation to a disposal facility.
(Code 1976, § 10-102; Ord. No. 92-8, § 1, 8-4-92; Ord. No. 99-20, § 2, 8-17-99)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 7-3. - Enforcement.
(a)
In general. The superintendent of public works shall be responsible for the administration and enforcement of this chapter. In the exercise of his solid waste management duties, the superintendent shall:
(1)
Supervise the collection of solid waste, including the operation and maintenance of equipment and the supervision of personnel;
(2)
Establish collection schedules in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
Limitations of authority. All regulatory actions of the superintendent of public works shall be subject to the review of the mayor and council.
(c)
Appeal. Any person aggrieved by a requirement of, or a fee charged by, the superintendent of public works shall have the right to appeal to the mayor and council, which body may, upon a hearing, confirm, modify or revoke such requirement or fee.
(Code 1976, § 10-103)
Sec. 7-4. - Precollection.
(a)
In general. No person shall keep or store solid waste outside of any residence or building within the city, except in proper containers for collection or otherwise prepared, as set forth in this chapter or under the express prior approval of the director of public works. Any unauthorized accumulation of solid waste is hereby declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. The director of public works by written notice shall notify the owner or occupant of any premises with such solid waste accumulation to remove same. Failure to remove such accumulation within two (2) weeks of the date of such written notice to do so shall be deemed a violation of these regulations and subject to penalties set forth in section 7-13. A separate violation shall be deemed committed each day during or upon which such unlawful accumulation continues.
(b)
Containers. A waste wheeler cart shall be provided by the city to each single-family residence to accommodate solid waste generated by the residents. The safekeeping of the cart shall be the responsibility of the occupant of the residence. The cart shall at all times remain the property of the city.
(c)
Placement. Waste containers must be placed at the street for the garbage to be collected. Containers shall be placed at the street no sooner than 12:00 noon the day immediately preceding the collection day and must be removed no later than 10:00 a.m. on the day immediately following the collection day unless other arrangements have been made with the superintendent. When waste containers are not placed at the street for collection purposes, they shall be screened from public view.
(d)
Scavenging. No person other than the owner or authorized collection personnel shall sift the contents of or remove anything from containers, boxes or bundles containing garbage or rubbish.
(e)
Maintenance of containers. All solid waste or litter containers or receptacles shall be maintained in as sanitary a manner as is reasonably possible consistent with its use for solid waste and litter disposal.
(f)
Depositing of authorized materials. Persons using solid waste and litter containers or receptacles shall deposit only authorized solid waste and refuse in the container or receptacle.
(g)
Scrap tires. No person shall deposit a scrap tire in any container or receptacle unless authorized by the owner of the receptacle or the mayor and council or their designee.
(h)
Burning or smoldering material. No person shall deposit any burning or smoldering material in such container or receptacle.
(i)
Setting fire to contents. No person shall set fire to the contents of any such container or receptacle.
(j)
Noncompatible materials. No person shall deposit large noncompatible articles in containers or receptacles such as, but not limited to, stoves, refrigerators, bed springs, large tree limbs or air conditioning units, except containers or receptacles designated for that purpose only.
(k)
Flammable or explosive materials. No person shall deposit any flammable or explosive materials in any such container or receptacle.
(l)
Dead animals. No dead animals shall be deposited in any such container or receptacle.
(m)
Willful damage or altering of location. No person shall willfully damage or alter the location of any such container or receptacle without the written consent of the mayor and council.
(n)
Salvage or scavenging. No salvage or scavenging operations shall be conducted in or around such containers or receptacles except by written consent of the mayor and council.
(o)
Depositing at solid waste collection and recycling center. No person shall deposit any solid waste at a city solid waste collection and recycling center unless such solid waste is contained in a manner that it can be handled by the attendant and shall be deposited only during the official hours of operation.
(p)
Depositing of authorized materials. No person shall deposit solid waste, water or litter of any kind at any city solid waste collection and recycling center into city-owned receptacles or containers designated for the collection of recovered materials. Only authorized materials such as a glass, aluminum, newspaper, cardboard, plastic, and tin or other accepted material may be deposited in the appropriate container designated for said material.
(Code 1976, § 10-104; Ord. No. 98-15, § 1, 9-15-98; Ord. No. 99-20, § 5, 8-17-99)
Sec. 7-5. - Collection.
(a)
Frequency. Garbage shall be collected from residential premises once weekly. Garbage shall be collected from commercial and business establishments in accord with need and convenience, as determined by the superintendent. Rubbish shall be collected upon request made to the city.
(b)
Limitations. Industrial waste, large animals over thirty (30) pounds or waste from construction, demolition, landscaping or processing operations will not be collected, transported or disposed of by public facilities, as all such waste collection, transportation and disposal shall be made by the owners or generators of such waste or animals.
(c)
Hazardous materials. It shall be a violation of these regulations to place or to cause to be placed in any container, box, bundle or otherwise for collection any hazardous waste of any kind, except upon specific prior arrangement with the director of public works.
(d)
It shall be unlawful to drive or operate a vehicle in Union City hauling municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste that leaks, flows freely or spills from said vehicle.
(e)
Any litter or municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste hauled on a moving vehicle shall be covered or secured in such a manner that litter will not blow or escape from said vehicle while moving or parked on public or private property in Union City.
(Code 1976, § 10-105; Ord. No. 99-20, § 4, 8-17-99)
Sec. 7-6. - Waste disposal.
(a)
The owner or occupant of any premises shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposing of all litter, scrap tires, and municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste on the premises used or occupied by such person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to dump, open dump, or permit the dumping of litter, scrap tires, municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste or recyclables at any place in this city including, and without limitations, any public or private property in the city or any waters in this city unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the mayor and council or its duly designated agent for the disposal of litter, municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste, scrap tires or recovered materials and the person is authorized to use such property.
(2)
The litter, municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste, recyclables or scrap tires is placed into a receptacle or container installed specifically for such property; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare and not otherwise in violation of law.
(4)
The property has a valid solid waste handling permit issued by EPD.
(c)
All persons defined as scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers, tire retailers shall be subject to rules as defined in section 391-3-4-.19 [of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management,] and handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of section 12-8-20 et seq. of O.C.G.A. and the Rules for Solid Waste Management, Chapter 391-3-4 applicable to solid waste.
(Code 1976, § 10-106; Ord. No. 99-20, § 3, 8-17-99)
Sec. 7-7. - Garbage removal tax.
(a)
Levy. A monthly garbage removal tax is hereby levied and assessed against all of the different classes of persons, and against all of the various classes, kinds and sizes of improved building lots of land and improvements thereon to be served or benefited by the city's removal of garbage therefrom, and against the owner or owners thereof, and against each and all of the occupants and tenants thereof, and against each water customer of the city, and against the head of each family within the city; provided, however, that the maximum monthly garbage removal tax shall be eleven dollars and twenty-five cents ($11.25), and provided further, that a building lot shall consist of any lot of land with a building thereon, and no building lot shall be considered improved unless there is a building thereon.
For collection of such garbage removal tax, the city shall have a lien against such building lot and improvements thereon, and the owner thereof, which are delinquent in payment, which lien shall be prior and superior to any and all other liens except state and county tax liens, and except that it shall be equal to any liens said city may have against such building lot for city taxes.
(b)
Amount. The amount of tax or charge for removal of garbage from a residence is hereby fixed at eleven dollars and twenty-five cents ($11.25) per month. The amount of tax or charge for removal of garbage from a lot with a business, commercial, professional, institutional building, or factory thereon, shall be twelve dollars and five cents ($12.05) per cubic yard provided the minimum charge per month for garbage removal services from business establishments shall not be less than twenty-four dollars and ten cents ($24.10) whether the amount removed is more or less than one (1) cubic yard.
(c)
Payment. The garbage removal service charge shall be due and payable by each person liable therefor at the same time that water bills are due and payable on the premises. If the premises are not served by water, the bill shall be payable by the fifteenth of the month for the previous month's service.
(d)
Water/sewer and garbage removal tax penalty. If payment for water service, sewer service, and/or the garbage removal tax is not made on or before the due date stated on the applicable bill rendered (said due dates fall on either the first or the fifteenth of each month), an additional service charge of ten (10) percent of the outstanding debt shall be added to the amount due.
(Ord. No. 87-9, §§ 1—3, 2-3-87; Ord.No. 00-10, § 10-107, 9-9-00; Ord. No. 2004-03, § 1, 3-16-04; Ord. No. 2004-16, § 1, 8-17-04)
Cross reference— Taxation and finance, Ch. 13.
Sec. 7-8. - Extra trash pickup.
The amount of tax or charge for removal of extra trash from a residential lot shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such charges is on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 83-4, 3-15-83)
Sec. 7-9. - Dumpsters.
The amount of tax or charge for a change out of dumpster sizes at a customer's request from a lot with a business, commercial, professional, institutional building or factory thereon shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such charges is on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 83-4, 3-15-83)
Sec. 7-10. - Franchises.
(a)
Authority. The mayor and council shall have the authority to grant the right, privilege and franchise to remove and dispose of solid waste within all zoning districts. The franchise, authority or permit granted shall extend for a period not to exceed three (3) calendar years.
(b)
Fees. The grantee of a franchise to remove and dispose of solid waste shall pay to the city a fee in the amount of ten (10) percent of the total monthly billing collected. This fee shall be paid on or before the fifteenth day of each month for the collection of solid waste the preceding month. If the franchise fee shall be in arrears in whole or in part for fifteen (15) or more days, the mayor and council shall have the option to cancel the franchise.
(c)
Records. All books and records of a grantee concerning its operations within the city and the collection of bills shall be made available for inspection and audit by the administrative officer or his designee within thirty (30) days after any request for such inspection or audit.
(Ord. No. 80-5, 4-15-80)
Charter reference— Authority to grant franchises for public services, § 1-103(10).
Cross reference— Public utility franchise tax, § 13-2.
Sec. 7-11. - Records and reports.
(a)
All contractors and private vendors providing solid waste handling services within the city shall keep records relevant to the provision of such services. All required information shall be carefully documented by the service provider, and shall be reported to the city on a monthly basis. Required information which shall be compiled and reported to the city includes the following:
(1)
Amount of solid waste collected in tons.
(2)
Origin of solid waste collected.
(3)
Type of solid waste collected.
(4)
Disposal site location and tipping fee.
(5)
Number of customers served by collection service.
(6)
Types of solid waste handling services provided.
(7)
Recycling quantities and materials recycled from private organizations.
(8)
Amount of solid waste recycled in tons.
(9)
Amount of solid waste landfilled in tons.
(b)
Failure to compile such information and to provide the city with such reports on the dates due, as scheduled by the city administrator, shall constitute a violation of the Code of Ordinances, and shall subject such violator to a penalty up to, but no more than, the maximum fine and/or jail term allowed in the city court. For purposes of this section, each week beyond the scheduled due date in which the service provider continues to withhold the necessary information from the city shall constitute a separate violation.
(Ord. No. 92-8, § 2, 8-4-92)
Sec. 7-12. - Regulation of landfills, solid waste collection and recycling centers.
(a)
No landfill shall be operated in Union City, Georgia, other than a landfill designated by the mayor and council as the city landfill, and no private municipal solid waste landfill shall be operated in Union City, Georgia, without a solid waste handling permit issued by the Georgia Environmental Protection Division.
(b)
No person shall deposit solid waste of any kind outside the gate of a solid waste collection and recycling center.
(c)
No scavenging operation of any kind shall be allowed at a solid waste collection and recycling center.
(d)
No person shall move, remove, or cross any fence, gate, barrier, or sign at a solid waste collection recycling center.
(e)
Price on solid waste brought to said center will be set to defray total cost of solid waste and recycling operations.
(f)
Hours of operation of said centers will be set to maximize convenient use of residents of that area.
(g)
All recovered materials (recyclables) brought to collection centers will be accepted free of charge, provided, it is separated under current market specifications and likewise uncontaminated.
(h)
Attendant on duty will have full authority of said center consistent with this chapter.
(i)
All rules listed above shall apply to all public and/or private property in said city, all waters and/or waterways of said city, and all city solid waste collection and recycling centers.
(Ord. No. 99-20, § 6, 8-17-99)
Sec. 7-13. - Violation.
Any person violating any portion of this chapter shall be punished by a fine of not less than one hundred dollars ($100.00) and not more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and/or punishment within the sound discretion of the Judge of the Municipal Court of Union City. The offender(s) may also be directed to pickup and remove litter from public or private areas as provided in section 16-7-43(b) of O.C.G.A.
(Ord. No. 99-20, § 7, 8-17-99)

ARTICLE II. - RECYCLING

Sec. 7-21. - Separation of recyclable trash for curbside pickup.
(a)
Newspapers;
(b)
Aluminum cans and aluminum products (which shall have been emptied of all contents, both liquid and solid);
(c)
Glass products (which shall have been emptied of all contents, both liquid and solid); and
(d)
Plastics (which shall have been emptied of all contents, both liquid and solid).
(1)
For the purpose of this article, condominium units and fee-simple townhomes will be treated like single-family residences, and multifamily dwelling complexes shall mean those complexes consisting of more than one (1) single-family dwelling unit under one (1) ownership.
(2)
All residents of multifamily complexes and condominium units shall separate the above-listed recyclable items of trash for placement into a designated container, which shall be located in an area established by the multifamily complex owner or the condominium complex manager as the pickup area.
(3)
Residents of Union City who fail to separate recyclable trash and/or garbage from other trash and/or garbage shall be fined a penalty of no more than three hundred dollars ($300.00) in the Municipal Court of Union City, for failing to comply with the terms and conditions of this ordinance.
(4)
Current fee schedules and pickup schedules will be kept on file in city hall, and are subject to change at the discretion of the city.
