ARTICLE II. - LITTER CONTROL

Sec. 38-19. - Allowing litter, foul odors, etc., to remain on or emanate from property or causing such on the property of another.
It shall be unlawful for any person to trespass on the rights of others through the neglect of property by causing or allowing unsightly litter, foul odor or potentially dangerous devices to remain on or emanate from the property, or to discard, abandon or cause such on public property or other's private property. Provided however, odors generated by legitimate, commercial agricultural activities shall not be deemed to constitute a violation of this article.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-20. - Scattering of refuse and littering prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, deposit, throw or leave or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving of litter on any public or private property in the city or any waters in the city, unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the state or by the county for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container installed on such property; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consisting with the public welfare and consistent with other provisions of this article, as well as other applicable laws, ordinances and resolutions.
(b)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of subsection (a) of this section, it shall establish a rebuttable presumption that the operator of the conveyance has violated this article.
(c)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of this section which is discovered to contain any articles, including, but not limited to, letters, bills, publications or other writings which display the name of a person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs to such person, there shall be established a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this article.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw or deposit any refuse, building material, building material waste, cement sludge, asphalt sludge or other substances accumulated as a result of construction, repairs to existing facilities, demolition of existing structures on or into any public or private road, shoulder or any public or private road or public or private ditch.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-21. - Maintenance of property.
(a)
Property. All owners or occupants of property shall maintain their property in a litter-free condition.
(b)
Sweeping into sidewalks or streets. No person shall sweep into or deposit in any street or sidewalk the accumulation of litter from any building or property.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Secs. 38-22—38-39. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 38-40. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this chapter the following definitions apply:
All other refuse includes tin cans and such other materials as may not be classified under other heads.
Ashes means the refuse from fires in houses, schools, stores, etc. They include at times shall quantities of glass, crockery, sweepings, dust, brick, metal and other inorganic materials.
Carts shall mean trash receptacles provided by the city.
Commercial solid waste shall mean all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other non-manufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes.
Composting shall mean the controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable, odor-free humus.
Garbage means the organic waste both animal and vegetable from house, kitchen, restaurants, hotels, hospitals, etc. It is comprised chiefly of waste food and shall not include liquid that may be drained into the sewer.
Leachate collection system shall mean a system at a landfill for collection of the leachate which may percolate through the waste and into the soils surrounding the landfill.
Municipal solid waste shall mean any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks and includes solid waste from single-family and multi-family residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
Municipal solid waste disposal facility shall mean any facility or location where the final deposition of any amount of municipal solid waste occurs, whether or not mixed with or including commercial or industrial solid waste, including, but not limited to municipal solid waste landfills.
Municipal solid waste landfill shall mean a disposal facility where any amount of municipal solid waste, whether or not mixed with or including commercial waste, industrial waste, non-hazardous sludges or small quantity generator hazardous waste, is disposed of by means of placing an approved cover thereon.
Occupant shall mean persons over the age of 18 years residing in a residence within the city; owners of businesses in the city, including, and without limitation, sole proprietors, shareholders in a corporation, and general partners in a partnership; and onsite managers of businesses within the city.
Yard trimmings shall mean leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvicultural operations.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-41. - Fees for collection.
Garbage collection fees and fees for collecting yard trimmings, trees and other collection, shall be as fixed and established from time to time by the mayor and council, and the schedule of fees shall be on file in the office of the city clerk. Every residence, business establishment or service agency within the limits of the city and to which garbage and solid waste collection and disposal services are available, and for which collection and disposal services are available for yard trimmings, trees and other collection, shall pay a fee for the availability of such service, regardless of whether the residence, business or agency use the service, and the fee shall be based upon said fee schedule. All collection and disposal charges to a complex of apartments, houses, mobile homes or businesses will be billed to the landlord of the property, and the landlord shall be responsible to the city for the payment of assessed charges.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-42. - Garbage required to be promptly removed.
No garbage that has become decayed or that shall otherwise be a menace to health or cleanliness shall be allowed to remain in any dwelling house, hotel, boardinghouse, café, restaurant, lunch stand, fruit stand, meat market, store or other building or on any premises a longer time than shall be reasonably necessary to remove and deposit it in a receptacle or receptacles as provided in this chapter.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-43. - Collection, transportation of garbage by private persons.
No person shall collect, handle, haul or transport, on any of the streets, alleys, public ways or places of the city, any garbage without first having procured a permit therefor from the city clerk, and only upon approval of the city council, and from the state department of natural resources. Any vehicle to be used for the purpose of hauling or transporting garbage shall first be approved by the city council o its designee, and shall be at all times subject to inspection by the city as to condition and cleanliness. All garbage hauled in these vehicles shall be hauled in closed containers. Persons having a permit for hauling garbage under the provisions of this section shall place daily at each hotel, café, cafeteria or restaurant or other place from which this garbage is to be hauled, a clean empty can furnished by this person or by the operator of this hotel, café, cafeteria or restaurant, for the garbage; and each can shall be removed not later than the following date, Sundays excepted, without changing the contents thereof.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-44. - Removal of garbage, ashes and refuse by city.
Garbage, ashes and refuse will be collected and removed on the days fixed therefor by the city council. Public notice shall be given of any change in collection days.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Secs. 38-45—38-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL
DIVISION 1. - GARBAGE COLLECTION

Sec. 38-61. - Cart system.
Placement and removal of carts. Occupants shall place garbage for curbside pick up in carts provided by the city. On the evening before each day assigned by the city for collection in the occupant's area, the cart will be placed on the curb, in front of the premises from which it is to be collected or on the curb at the side of the premises where the premises are adjacent to more than one street, as close to the curb line or edge of roadway as practicable. The cart should be placed no earlier than 5:00 p.m. for pick up the next day or later than 8:00 a.m. on the day of pick up. The cart will be removed from the curbside point by the occupant no later than 8:00 a.m. on the day after the assigned collection date. At other times, carts will be stored by the occupant where possible in locations not visible from the street or road on which the garbage is collected.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-62. - Cost of carts.
The city shall initially provide and bear the cost of the carts for each residence and business. There shall be a $50.00 deposit required for the first cart to cover any damages, theft, or if the cart is lost. If the city uses all or part of the deposit to cover any damages, theft, or if the cart is lost, the occupant shall be responsible for immediately replacing such deposit up to the full $50.00 to cover any future damages, theft, or if cart is lost. In the event the first cart is insufficient to contain the garbage for a residence or business, the city will provide an additional cart or carts, provided the occupant pays a one-time $10.00 administrative fee for each additional cart. No additional deposit shall be required for additional carts, but $50.00 deposit shall stand good for any such additional carts. In the event the $50.00 deposit is insufficient to cover the actual cost of replacing any stolen, damaged or lost carts, the occupant shall immediately pay unto the city the difference. Upon the occupant's return of all carts to the city, the $50.00 deposit shall be refunded.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-63. - Removal by city.
In the event the cart is not used for a period of three weeks, the city shall have the right to remove the cart from the residence or business.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-64. - Physical handicaps.
If there is no occupant of the residence or business who is physically capable of complying with the requirements of Section 38-61 above, regarding the placement and removal of carts at curbside, the occupant or occupants of such residence or business shall be exempted from such requirements upon written approval of the city clerk. The city clerk may require such proof of disability as he/she, in his/her sole discretion, deems sufficient, including, but not limited to, a signed statement from a medical doctor.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-65. - Schedules.
Assigned collection dates, fees and costs for carts, and fees and costs for collection and disposal service shall be contained on a schedule approved by the city council and kept on file with the city clerk.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-66. - Damage or destruction of carts.
It shall be unlawful for any person to damage or destroy any cart provided by the city pursuant to this section.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-67. - Yard debris.
No tree trimmings, grass, or other bulky materials shall be placed in carts.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-68. - Sidewalks and streets.
No householder or business operator, by himself/herself or agent, shall sweep from any house, yard, store or elsewhere any dirt or refuse in or upon the sidewalk or public street, but all refuse shall be placed in carts as herein provided. No person shall place any tree trimmings or other material in any sidewalk area or in the vehicular portion of any street or alley.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-69. - Building materials.
All refuse resulting from building, repairing, remodeling or any type of construction or demolition should be removed by the contractor, owner of the property, or person having such work done. The city shall not remove refuse of this nature. Examples are lumber, shingles, carpet, tile and brick.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-70. - Tires.
It shall be the responsibility of the owner or lessee of the property to properly dispose of tires in accordance with state and federal law. The city shall not remove or dispose of tires.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Secs. 38-71—38-80. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - YARD TRIMMINGS AND TREES

Sec. 38-81. - Tree removal.
(a)
For compensation. It shall be unlawful for any firm, partnership, proprietorship or corporation to cut trees having a trunk diameter exceeding six inches for compensation within the city unless the byproduct from those services is immediately removed from the premises and placed or deposited by the contractor in the city designated landfill or hauling station. Compensation shall be interpreted to include receiving as compensation the wood, limbs, and/or other residue from such trimming and cutting. Any form of compensation shall place the burden of disposal directly upon the individual or contractor performing the task. Provided, however, for any tree having a diameter of no greater than six inches, the same may be placed for street pickup by the city if the tree and limbs from such tree or cut into sections having a length of no greater than five feet.
(b)
By occupant. For any trees having a trunk diameter no greater than 24 inches, which are cut by the occupant and/or not for compensation as set forth in subsection (a) above, the tree and limbs from such tree may be placed for street pickup by the city if the same are cut into sections having a length of no greater than five feet. Provided, however, for any tree having a trunk diameter of greater than 24 inches, it shall be the responsibility of the occupant to properly dispose of the tree and limbs, and any byproducts from such cuttings, and shall not be picked up by the city.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-82. - Placement and disposal of yard trimmings; prohibitions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place or mix yard trimmings with municipal solid waste within the city.
(b)
Yard trimmings shall not be disposed at any municipal solid waste disposal facility having a liner and leachate collection system or requiring vertical expansion located within the city.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-83. - Sorting, storing, composting and collecting yard trimmings.
Yard trimmings shall be placed on the street curb in order that it may be removed without the collector going into the yard or place of business, and any exceptions to this subjection shall be determined at the discretion of the city clerk or his/her designee. The city council shall establish fixed days from the removal of this material and so publish the schedule. Yard trash for street pick up should be separated as follows:
(1)
Leaves, straw, grass clippings;
(2)
Limbs with a maximum of five inches in diameter, placed with butts toward street;
(3)
Shrubbery trimmings and vines.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-84. - City shall not pick up household furniture and appliances.
The city shall not collect household furniture and appliances, including sofas, chairs, beds, refrigerators, washers, dryers, hot water heaters, furnaces, and similar items. It shall be a violation of this chapter to place such items in the streets or rights-of-way of the city for curbside collection or for any other reason. It shall be the responsibility of the occupant to properly dispose of such items.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-85. - Prohibited activities.
The following activities are hereby declared to be unlawful and in violation of this chapter:
(1)
Public streets and private property. No person shall place any accumulations of refuse and trash in any street, median strip, alley or other public place of travel, nor upon any private property, except with the written consent thereof and then only in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(2)
Blockage of drainage. At no time shall trash be placed in a manner as to obstruct drains, utility meters, walkways or streets. It shall be unlawful to use brooms, rakes, blowers or other sweeping, raking or debris removal tools or equipment so as to sweep, rake, blow or otherwise deposit or accumulate trash, garbage, gardening residue, clippings, limbs, ashes, alluvium, sediment or debris of any kind in or on city streets, walkways, drains, pipes or gutters. It shall be unlawful to place, accumulate or otherwise deposit any type of the aforementioned debris or material into a city drain, pipe, gutter or other draining conduit or receptacle.
(3)
Unauthorized storage. Any accumulation of refuse or trash items on any lot, property, premises, public streets, alley or other public or private place not permitted by this article, is hereby declared to be a nuisance. Failure of owner or occupant to remove and correct any such accumulation of refuse after appropriate notice from the city clerk shall raise the presumption that such person intended to violate this article.
(4)
Junk. It shall be unlawful for any person to place or leave outside any building or dwelling, any dilapidated furniture, appliance, machinery equipment, building material, junked motor vehicle, tires or other items which are either in a partially used, wrecked, junked, dismantled or inoperative condition, and which are not completely enclosed within a building or dwelling.
(5)
Sweeping and blowing. It shall be unlawful to sweep or blow with a blower, grass clippings, leaves and other debris onto a city sidewalk or street.
(6)
Dumping. It shall be unlawful and in violation of this section to dump, place or leave bricks, blocks, rocks, asphalt, cement, concrete, concrete products or waste, roofing, sheetrock, leaves, limbs, bushes, trees, roots, dead animals, appliances, equipment, bed springs, mattresses, furniture, fixtures, motors, engines' chemical wastes, industrial or commercial waste or rubbish, scrap metal, fence wire, vehicles or parts thereof, tires, building materials or supplies, and inorganic solid material of any type or kind except as authorized herein.
(7)
Fires. It shall be unlawful and in violation of this chapter to set any fire, to allow a fire to continue to burn, or in any other way attempt to damage or destroy the garbage and solid waste collection containers provided by the city.
(8)
Container of another. It shall be unlawful and in violation of this chapter for any person to dump, place or leave any item at a garbage and solid waste collection area or in any collection container unless such person has permission of the owner and/or occupant of the premises on which the area or container is located.
(9)
Billboards.
a.
It shall be unlawful for owners to fail to maintain billboards in a manner that prevents loose paper, bills and other litter and debris resulting from the use of such signs and billboards. Any such debris, loose papers, bills and other litter shall be removed by the owners or their agents immediately upon accumulation.
b.
Any such item or items which remain on the property of the occupant for a period of 30 days after notice of violation of this article, shall be presumed to be abandoned and subject to being removed from the property by the city without further notice. The city may charge the owner or occupant a fee for the cost to removing such item or items. This section shall not apply to licensed junk dealers or currently licensed establishments engaged in the repair, rebuilding, reconditioning, or salvaging of equipment or furniture.
(10)
Scavenging. No person other than the owner thereof or an agent or employee of the director shall disturb or interfere with any container used for the purpose of storing refuse pending its collection, remove any contents therefrom, or remove such container from its collection.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-86. - Abandonment.
Any such item or items which remain on the property of the occupant for a period of 30 days after noticed of violation of this article, shall be presumed to be abandoned and subject to being removed from the property by the city without further notice. The city may charge the owner or occupant a fee for the cost of removing such item or items. In the event the owner is also the occupant of the premises, any unpaid charge for removal may be collected by the city clerk in the same manner as delinquent taxes. This section shall not apply to licensed junk dealers or currently licensed establishments engaged in the repair, rebuilding, reconditioning, or salvaging of equipment or furniture.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Secs. 38-87—38-89. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - COMMERCIAL ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 38-90. - Generally.
All commercial establishments shall store their refuse in containers, as specified in this article, so as to eliminate wind-driven debris and unsightly litter in and about their establishments. Approved methods of containerization shall include refuse receptacles, bulk containers and detachable containers. Any spillage or overflow shall be immediately cleaned up by such establishment.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-91. - Loading and unloading areas.
All loading and unloading areas shall be provided with refuse receptacles for loose debris, paper, packaging materials and other trash. The number of containers necessary for each area shall be as required to maintain clean, neat and sanitary premises. The owner of the premises and the occupant thereof shall jointly and severally maintain surveillance to ensure that all litter is placed in the proper container and the area is kept clean.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-92. - Construction sites and demolition sites.
(a)
On-site refuse receptacles required; number. All construction and demolition contractors shall provide on-site refuse receptacles, bulk containers or detachable containers for loose debris, paper, building material waste, scrap building materials and other trash procured by those working on the site. The site shall be kept in a litter-free condition. The number of refuse receptacles, bulk containers or detachable containers shall be determined by the size of the job.
(b)
Removal of dirt, mud, construction materials, etc. Dirt, mud, construction materials or other debris deposited upon any public or private property as a result of construction or demolition shall be removed by the contractor upon completion of the construction.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-93. - Parking lot receptacles.
(a)
Required; number and type; collection. All parking lots and establishments with parking lots shall provide refuse receptacles distributed within the parking area. The city council or its designee shall have authority to determine the number and types of receptacles necessary to provide proper containerization. Such receptacles shall be weighted or attached to the ground as necessary to prevent spillage. It shall be the responsibility of the owner or the manager of the parking lots to collect the refuse and trash deposited in such containers, and to have the same properly disposed on in accordance with this chapter.
(b)
Obligation to use. It shall be the obligation of all persons using the parking areas to use such refuse receptacles or containers for the purpose intended, and it shall be unlawful for any person to dump, scatter or throw upon any such parking lot, any refuse, garbage or trash of any kind.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-94. - Use of dumpsters.
The provisions of this chapter regarding use of carts shall not apply to any businesses, industries, or apartment buildings or structures within the city that have been approved by the city council or its designee to utilize dumpsters.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Secs. 38-95—38-99. - Reserved
ARTICLE VI. - SPECIAL REFUSE DISPOSAL

Sec. 38-100. - Industrial waste.
Industrial waste shall be collected, removed and disposed of by any factory, plant or enterprise creating or causing such waste.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-101. - Hazardous refuse.
No hazardous refuse shall be placed in any receptacle used for collection of refuse by the city.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-102. - Dead animals.
Owners of dead animals shall be responsible for their removal and disposal.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-103. - Building materials.
The city shall not be responsible for the collecting preliminary to, during or subsequent to the construction of a new building, alteration or additions to an existing building or of any kind of construction of any and all refuse, trash, debris resulting therefrom as well as from demolition of existing structures. Such material shall be removed by the owner of the property or by the contractor.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-104. - Contagious disease refuse.
The removal of clothing, bedding or other refuse from homes or other places where highly infectious diseases have prevailed shall be decontaminated prior to removal under the supervision and direction of the county health department. Such refuse shall not be placed in receptacles or bulk container for the city collection until decontaminated.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-105. - Hypodermic instruments.
No person shall dispose of or discard any hypodermic needle or any instrument or device for making hypodermic injections before first breaking, disassembling, destroying or otherwise rendering inoperable and incapable of re-use, such hypodermic syringe, needle, instrument or device, and without safeguarding the disposal thereof, by securely placing in a plastic container with lid so as to avoid the possibility of causing injury to the collection personnel.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-106. - Cardboard boxes and cartons.
Prior to depositing refuse for collection in authorized containers or receptacles or in commercial containers, the person disposing of any such boxes or cartons or the person in charge of the premises shall collapse all cardboard boxes and cartons.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Secs. 38-107—38-119. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VII. - ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 38-120. - Enforcement.
The city clerk or his/her designee shall investigate complaints or violations of any provisions of this article. They shall determine if there has been a violation, issue warnings, and initiate an attempt to obtain compliance with this article by the person in violation thereof.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-121. - Initiation of complaint.
The city clerk or his/her designee shall be authorized to sign a complaint or issue a summons against a violator of this article.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-122. - Civil remedies.
In case of any violation of this article, the city clerk or his/her designee and the city attorney, in addition to any other remedies, may institute injunction, mandates, or other appropriate proceedings to prevent such violation, or to correct or abate such violation.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
Sec. 38-123. - Suspension of service.
Upon appropriate notice to the person violating this chapter, the city clerk or his/her designee may suspend any service or the use of any facility maintained by the city during the period such violation continues to exist.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
DIVISION 1. - CIVIL PENALTY

Sec. 38-124. - Penalty for violation.
Any person violating this article may be held liable for damages, and the recovery shall be in a sum of not less than $110.00, nor more than $550.00 for each violation. The city attorney may institute a proceeding in any court having jurisdiction thereof to collect such civil penalty. If the violation has been committed by the owner of the property on which the violation occurs, the city clerk may further proceed to collect the costs to the city of eliminating, removing or cleaning the premises in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
DIVISION 2. - CRIMINAL PENALTY

Sec. 38-125. - Penalty.
Any person who shall violate any provision of this chapter shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 upon conviction. Provided, however, the foregoing shall not apply to any occupant who fails to remove a cart by 8:00 a.m. on the day after the assigned collection date for garbage pickup. Rather, such occupant shall be subject to a fine of $10.00 per day until the cart is removed. Provided further, the provisions regarding carts shall not apply to any businesses within the city that have been approved by the city council or its designee to utilize dumpsters.
(Ord. of 4-15-2021(1))
