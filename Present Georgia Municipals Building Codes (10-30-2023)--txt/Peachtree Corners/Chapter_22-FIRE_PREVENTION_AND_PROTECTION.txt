ARTICLE II. - FIRE CODES

Sec. 22-19. - Adoption of fire safety law.
There is hereby adopted by the city for the purpose of prescribing regulations governing conditions hazardous to life and property from fire or explosion those certain codes known as the National Fire Codes, volumes 1 through 11, as amended by Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. ch. 120-3-3 of the rules and regulations of the state, under authority of O.C.G.A. §§ 25-2-4, 25-2-12, and 50-13-21. There shall be not less than one copy of such codes filed in the office of the city and the codes are hereby adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out completely in this article. From the date on which the ordinance from which this article is derived shall take effect, the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the limits of the city.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-26)
Sec. 22-20. - Construction of codes.
All codes, ordinances, standards and pamphlets adopted in this article shall be construed as the standard for fire codes' compliance among buildings within the city and shall prevail in matters of life safety over all other local or city codes, ordinances or standards which apply to the same building components or systems covered by this article. Final approving authority for fire codes' compliance covered in this section shall be the county fire marshal's office.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-27)
Sec. 22-21. - Enforcement of article.
The provisions of this article shall be enforced by authorized personnel of the county fire and emergency services, or by authorized officers of the county division of police services. In addition, the provisions of this article may be enforced by authorized officers of the police department of any municipality within the county.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-28)
Sec. 22-22. - Penalties for violation of article.
(a)
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of this article or the codes adopted by reference in this article or fail to comply therewith or who shall violate or fail to comply with any order made pursuant to this article, or who shall build in violation of any detailed statement of specifications or plans submitted and approved thereunder and from which no appeal has been taken, or who shall fail to comply with such an order as affirmed or modified by the board of commissioners within the time set forth in this article, shall constitute a violation of a county ordinance. Any person violating this article or any of the codes and/or laws incorporated in this article shall be deemed guilty of violating a county ordinance and shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $500.00 and by imprisonment in the common jail of the county not to exceed 60 days, or by both fine and imprisonment. The imposition of one penalty shall not excuse the violation nor permit it to continue, and such persons shall be required to correct or remedy such violations or defects within a specified time. When not otherwise specified, each day that prohibited conditions are maintained shall constitute a separate violation of this article.
(b)
The application of the penalty in subsection (a) of this section shall not be held to prevent the enforced removal of the prohibited conditions.
(c)
Upon written notice from the fire marshal or his designee, work on any project that is being performed contrary to the provisions of this article and the codes adopted in this article, or otherwise being done in a dangerous or unsafe manner, shall cease immediately. Such notice shall be given to the owner of the property, his agent, or the person doing the work and shall state the conditions under which the work may be resumed.
(d)
Permits or approvals may be revoked by the fire marshal or his designee when there has been any false statement or misrepresentation as to any materials or facts contained in plans or other information on which the permit or approval has been based.
(e)
Whenever any of the officers or inspectors of the department of fire and emergency services find conditions which are unsafe and/or in violation of the provisions or intent of the codes or ordinances enforced by the fire marshal's office, written notice, which has been approved by the fire marshal, shall be given to the owner, the owner's agent or the occupant or occupant's representative requiring that any activities impacted by the conditions shall cease until the condition is corrected.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-29)
Sec. 22-23. - Establishment and duties of the fire prevention section.
(a)
The fire prevention codes shall be enforced by the fire prevention section in the department of fire and emergency services of the county. The fire prevention section shall also be known as the office of the fire marshal.
(b)
The fire marshal in charge of the fire prevention section shall be appointed by the chief of the department of fire and emergency services in accordance with the county merit system rules and regulations.
(c)
The chief of the department of fire and emergency services may detail such members of the fire service as inspectors as shall from time to time be necessary. The chief of the department of fire and emergency services shall recommend to the board of commissioners the employment of technical inspectors, who shall be selected in accordance with the county merit system rules and regulations.
(d)
A report of the fire prevention section shall be made annually and transmitted to the board of commissioners. It shall contain a report of all activities and inspections undertaken pursuant to this article, with such statistics as the chief of the department of fire and emergency services may wish to include therein. The chief of the department of fire and emergency services shall also recommend any amendments to the code which, in his judgment, shall be desirable.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-30)
Sec. 22-24. - Establishment of district limits in which storage of explosives and blasting agents is prohibited.
Locations used for the storage of explosives and blasting agents shall comply with the rules promulgated by the fire safety commissioner of the state entitled, "Rules and Regulations for Explosives for Blasting Agents."
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-31)
Sec. 22-25. - Establishment of district limits in which storage of flammable liquids in outside aboveground tanks is provided.
Permanent installations for the storage of flammable liquids in outside aboveground tanks shall comply with section 1311 of the county zoning resolution, as amended, as well as NFPA Pamphlet 30 titled, "Flammable and Combustible Liquids Code." A fee will be charged when a final inspection is made of all flammable liquid storage tanks conducted by the department of fire and emergency services. Fees shall be payable, by check only, to the county fire marshal. The final inspection fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-32)
Sec. 22-26. - Establishment of limits in which bulk storage of liquefied petroleum gases is restricted.
Bulk storage of liquefied petroleum gas shall comply with section 1311 of the county zoning resolution, as amended, as well as NFPA Pamphlet 58 titled, "Standard for the Storage and Handling of Liquefied Petroleum Gases." A fee will be charged when a final inspection is made of all liquefied petroleum gas installations conducted by the department of fire and emergency services. Fees shall be payable, by check only, to the county fire marshal. The final inspection fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-33)
Sec. 22-27. - Modifications of code.
The fire marshal, or his assistant, shall have the power to modify any of the provisions of this article and any codes enforced by the fire marshal's office, upon written application by the owner or leasee of property, or his duly authorized agent, when there are practical difficulties in carrying out the provisions of the article, provided that the spirit of this article shall be observed, public safety secured, and substantial justice done. The details of such modifications, when granted or allowed, and the decision shall be entered upon the records of the department of fire and emergency services, and a signed copy shall be furnished to the applicant.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-34)
Sec. 22-28. - Variances.
A committee comprised of the fire marshal, plan review supervisor and the field inspection supervisor shall have the power to implement systems, methods, or devices of equivalent or superior quality, strength, fire resistance, effectiveness, durability, and safety as alternatives to those prescribed by the codes, provided technical documentation is submitted to the committee to demonstrate equivalency, and the system, method, or device is approved for the intended purpose. The decision of the committee or chief of the department of fire and emergency services may be appealed to the county board of construction adjustments and appeals.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-34.5)
Sec. 22-29. - Waivers.
The chief of the department of fire and emergency services or the fire marshal are authorized to grant exemptions from the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 30-3-1 et seq., with regard to the access and use of facilities by persons with disabilities. The decision of the chief of the department of fire and emergency services or the fire marshal on such exemption may be appealed to the board of construction adjustments and appeals.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-35)
Sec. 22-30. - Appeals.
Any appeal or request for a variance from the provisions of the codes set out in this article shall be submitted in writing to the fire marshal's office with the explanation and reason why the variance should be granted. In response thereto, the fire marshal's office shall furnish a statement as to the purpose of the requirement along with a response which approves or denies the request. Should the affected party wish to appeal the decision of the fire marshal or the chief of the department of fire and emergency services, the appeal shall be submitted in writing on forms provided by the department of fire and emergency services and shall be accompanied by a fee to be submitted to the board of construction adjustments and appeals. The appeal fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-36)
Sec. 22-31. - Standards for minimum size water mains and spacing of fire hydrants for all new construction and additions and renovations on private property.
(a)
Subdivisions of freestanding single-family homes shall have eight-inch water mains with three-way fire hydrants spaced no more than 450 feet apart.
(b)
Approved subdivisions of multifamily residential units shall have eight-inch or larger water mains installed. Three-way type fire hydrants shall be located to permit all portions of all buildings to be reached with hose lays not to exceed 400 feet by road travel. All hydrants shall be capable of providing a minimum flow of 1,000 gallons per minute (GPM). Verification of water flow GPM shall be conducted as set forth in NFPA #291. Exception: Hose lays may be extended to 500 feet in fire sprinklered projects.
(c)
Industrial and commercial developments shall have 12-inch or larger mains installed with three-way fire hydrants spaced no more than 300 feet apart along dedicated roadways.
(1)
Exception: This shall not apply to private driveways giving access from a public way to three or less buildings totaling a maximum of 500,000 square feet.
(2)
Exception: Fire hydrants and water mains of a minimum of eight inches with a flow of 1,250 gallons per minute shall be placed so that all portions of all buildings shall be reached with hose lays not to exceed 400 feet by road travel.
(d)
Existing six-inch or larger water mains and fire hydrants may be continued in use that meet the following criteria:
(1)
The existing fire hydrant is in compliance with specified hose lay requirements.
(2)
Any additional water mains and fire hydrants to be installed to meet hose lay requirements shall be supplied by a minimum of an eight-inch or larger water main.
(3)
The existing fire hydrant shall provide a minimum of 1,000 gallons of water per minute. Verification of water flow shall be in accordance with NFPA #291.
(e)
Commercial or industrial buildings provided with automatic fire sprinkler or standpipe systems shall have a three-way type fire hydrant placed within 150 feet of the fire department Siamese connection.
(f)
Residential multifamily occupancies provided with automatic fire sprinkler protection or standpipe systems shall have a three-way fire hydrant placed within 250 feet of the fire department Siamese connection.
(g)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct the approach or visibility of any fire hydrant or fire department connection, closer than five feet in any direction, parallel with street access.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-37)
Sec. 22-32. - Location of hydrants and hydrant specifications.
(a)
Water mains and fire hydrants shall be installed, be under water pressure, and ready for fire service use prior to beginning construction with combustible materials. Exception: Materials used in forming for footings and foundations on grade.
(b)
Hydrants, fittings, valves, and fire department connections shall be approved by the department of fire and emergency services. Connections shall not be less than 18 inches nor more than 36 inches above the level of the adjoining finished grade. The thread of such connections shall be uniform with that used by the department of fire and emergency services.
(c)
Hydrants shall meet the requirements of NFPA #24 "Fire Hydrants for Outside Protection," and have not less than a six-inch connection with the mains. The number, size, and arrangement of the outlets, the size of the main valve opening and size of barrel shall be suitable for the protection to be provided and shall be approved by the department of water resources and the department of fire and emergency services.
(d)
The size and the shape of the operating nut as well as the direction the hydrant stem operates shall be uniform with hydrants as provided in the city and county water distribution system specifications.
(e)
The department of fire and emergency services shall be allowed to conduct periodic checks of fire hydrants to verify their operable condition and to apply paint to those fire hydrants.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-38)
Sec. 22-33. - Obstruction of approach or visibility of fire hydrants and connections.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct the approach or visibility of any fire hydrant or fire department connection, closer than five feet in any direction, parallel with street access.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-39)
Sec. 22-34. - Requirements pertaining to fire protection systems.
(a)
Buildings classified as office occupancies, with four or more floor levels, including basements and/or parking levels, shall be equipped with the approved NFPA Class I wet standpipe systems installed in accordance with NFPA standard #14.
(b)
Hotels, motels, roominghouses or lodginghouses and health care facilities of combustible construction shall have complete automatic fire sprinkler systems installed in accordance with NFPA standard #13. Exception: Where each suite or room has direct access to the exterior at grade level.
(c)
Hotels, motels, roominghouses or lodginghouses and health care facilities with three or more floor levels, regardless of type of construction, shall have complete, automatic fire sprinkler systems installed in accordance with NFPA standard #13.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-40)
Sec. 22-35. - Requirements for tenant separations and attic fire barrier—Within multi-tenanted commercial and industrial buildings.
(a)
Tenant separations shall have a minimum fire resistance rating of one hour. Rated walls shall extend from the floor of the lowest level to the underside of the roof deck. All rated assemblies, both walls and floor/ceiling assemblies, shall have been tested by an approved, accredited testing laboratory and shall be listed as such. All openings and/or penetrations within rated assemblies shall be tightly sealed using an approved, listed material complying with ASTM-E814.
(b)
Attic fire barriers shall be constructed of wood or metal studs with one layer of fire rated sheetrock on each side to divide combustible attic spaces into areas of 3,000 square feet or less. Access panels, 24 inches by 36 inches in size, shall be located leading into each area from the floor level immediately below the compartmentalized area.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-41)
Sec. 22-36. - Same—Within multifamily residential buildings.
(a)
All tenant separations shall have a minimum fire resistance rating of one hour. All tenant separation walls shall extend from the floor of the lowest level to the underside of the roof deck, unless the top floor ceiling has a one-hour fire resistance rating consisting of two layers of fire resistant sheetrock, in which event attic draft stops shall be installed at 3,000 square foot intervals or at every other tenant line, whichever is less. Attic fire barriers shall run with the roof trusses and be placed to create compartments covering no more than two units. All rated assemblies, both walls and floor/ceiling assemblies, shall have been tested by an approved, accredited testing laboratory and shall be listed as such. All openings and/or penetrations within or through rated assemblies shall be tightly sealed using an approved, listed material complying with ASTM-E814. Tenant separations may be constructed of not less than one-half-inch gypsum wallboard, provided the building is equipped with an automatic fire suppression system designed and installed in compliance with the county fire marshal's criteria. All openings and penetrations within or through tenant separations shall be tightly sealed to resist the passage of smoke. Attic areas shall be draft stopped at 3,000 square foot intervals or every other tenant line, whichever is less.
(b)
Access panels, 24 inches by 36 inches in size, shall be located leading into each area from the floor level immediately below the compartmentalized area.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-42)
Sec. 22-37. - Accessibility for firefighting equipment and personnel.
(a)
When access to or within a structure or project is unduly difficult because of secured openings, the owner and/or tenant shall be responsible for ensuring quick access into the area for fire services' use.
(b)
An all-weather access drive shall be provided to or around all buildings, if so specified by fire services in the permit process.
(c)
Accessibility shall be maintained throughout all stages of construction.
(d)
Minimum widths of a development's interior access driveways, excluding parking, shall be 20 feet. Dead-end roadways more than 150 feet in length shall be provided at the closed end with a cul-de-sac having a 50-foot radius at the outside curbline, or other approved means for turning around.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-43)

Changes to the number of exits.

Changes to features necessary for accessibility for the disabled.
(4)
Changes to fire alarm systems, except as stated in the above subsection.
(5)
Changes to sprinkler systems to include site plans except as stated in the above subsection.

Exception: Projects consisting of multifamily residential projects mandate 50 percent inspections in order to inspect the methods of sealing penetrations within and through walls.

Upon a satisfactory final inspection, the necessary certificate of occupancy shall be issued.

No persons except firefighters, police officers, members of the governing body of the city, the owners of the subject property or their agents, and the agents of insuring companies shall be allowed within the immediate vicinity of any fire without being requested there by the fire chief or the officer in charge at the time. Any person refusing to obey the orders and directions of the officer in charge of a fire may be arrested for violation of this section and, on conviction thereof, be punished for the violation of a city ordinance.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-51)

Any person refusing to obey the orders and directions of the officer in charge of a fire may be arrested for violation of this section and, on conviction thereof, be punished as provided by general law.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-52)
Sec. 22-47. - Crossing fire hose prohibited.
No vehicle shall be driven over any unprotected hose of the department of fire and emergency services when laid down on any street, roadway, or private driveway without the consent of the fire service official in command. Any person refusing to obey the orders and directions of the officer in charge of a fire may be arrested for violation of this section and, on conviction thereof, be punished for the violation of a city ordinance.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-53)
Sec. 22-48. - Rules and regulations for outdoor and open burning.
(a)
Permits and fees.
(1)
Permits for opening burning at or on development sites for the purpose of clearing land for new construction shall be obtained from the county department of fire and emergency services, office of the fire marshal. Permits shall be registered with the fire services communications center by the fire marshal's office.
(2)
The applicant shall complete an application for an open burning permit issued by the fire marshal's office. The applicant shall submit a plat or site plan showing the proposed location of the pit and information relating to the proximity of any occupied or unoccupied structure.
(3)
The application, if approved, shall be accompanied by a check. The check shall be made payable to the county department of fire and emergency services. The application fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(4)
No burning shall commence prior to an inspection of the site and the pit by a representative of the county fire marshal's office. Upon approval of the pit, the permit shall be valid for a period of 30 days. Permits may be extended for increments of 15-day periods without additional fees, maximum of two extensions.
(b)
Fires requiring written permits.
(1)
The only type of fires permitted for the purpose of land clearing are pit fires. Non-pit fires are not permitted for land clearing in the city. All pit fires shall be conducted in strict accordance with the "Air Curtain Destructor Operating Guide and Procedures," as published by the North Georgia Region of the Environmental Protection Division, attached hereto as Exhibit A (not set out herein; Exhibit A is on file with the county).
(2)
Pit fires shall comply with the following stipulations:
a.
The pit shall be at least 300 feet from any occupied or habitable structure. This distance may be increased if need is assessed by the fire marshal's office and validity exists.
b.
Where a site cannot be excavated to the recommended depth due to high water table and/or subsurface rock, the pit may be constructed above ground level, subject to the same state standards and procedures for a pit below ground level.
c.
Only wood wastes consisting of trees, logs, large brush, stumps, leaves, and untreated lumber may be burned in the pit.
d.
Leaves, sawdust or other densely packed wood wastes, paper (any type), chemically treated, coated or impregnated wood, or non-wood construction materials shall not be burned. The amount of dirt/soil mixture shall be kept to a minimum.
e.
No more than one air curtain destructor is allowed to be operated within a ten-acre permitted site at one time. The air curtain destructor must be under the supervision of a trained operator at all times during operation. In the event there is a need for multiple burning pits, there shall be a minimum of 1,000 feet between each burning pit.
f.
Air curtain destructors shall be able to pass an even, non-turbulent flow of air across the top of the pit. Air curtain destructors with an air duct or manifold that has been altered by bends, dents, holes, etc., and are incapable of providing an even and non-turbulent flow of air across the top of the pit shall not be approved for use.
g.
The air curtain destructor shall be operated in a manner to prevent air, land or water pollution, safety/health hazards, or nuisances.
h.
Tires or other rubber products, plastics, heavy oils or asphaltic base or impregnated materials shall not be used to start or maintain air curtain destructor operations.
i.
No smoke emissions exceeding 40 percent opacity may be produced during operation except for a reasonable period during ignition and charging of the pit.
j.
When the pit is cleared of ash, airborne particulate is to be minimized by wetting or mixing the ashes with dirt.
k.
Diesel fuel or kerosene may be used to start pit fires.
(c)
Types of burning allowed without a written permit. The following types of burning are allowed without a written permit:
(1)
Burning of leaves and limbs and natural vegetation that may fall on one's property.
(2)
Fires for the purpose of keeping warm. Warming fires shall be contained within a metal barrel of 55-gallon capacity or less. Untreated wood or lumber shall be the only material or substance permitted to be burned. The container for the warming fire shall not be less than 50 feet from any structure. No on-ground warming fires shall be permitted. (Note: The outside temperature shall be 50 degrees Fahrenheit or less.)
(3)
Fires for the purpose of cooking food for human consumption.
(4)
Bonfires. Permit required; no fee.
a.
No person shall kindle or maintain any bonfire or authorize any such fire to be kindled or maintained on any private or public land unless the location is no less than 50 feet from a structure with a size not to exceed four cubic feet of combustible materials and 100 feet from a structure with a size not to exceed five cubic feet of combustible materials.
b.
For purposes of clarification, bonfires shall be limited to activities sponsored by civic, educational, religious or other groups for purpose of celebration, etc. An example would be a high school pep rally.
c.
Bonfires shall be constantly attended by a competent person until such fire is extinguished. Such person shall have a garden hose connected to a water supply, or other fire extinguishing equipment readily available for use. Access to communications to the fire department shall be maintained within 500 feet of the bonfire.
(d)
Burning restrictions. The following rules govern all burning in the county, whether or not a written permit is required:
(1)
The fire marshal's office may temporarily prohibit any and/or all outdoor fires when atmospheric conditions or local circumstances make such fires hazardous.
(2)
No burning is permitted on Sundays or nights. Burning at construction sites for the clearing of land for new development shall not be conducted before or after daylight hours.
(3)
No burning is permitted when prevailing winds are in excess of 15 miles per hour in the direction of populated areas immediately adjacent to the permitted site.
(4)
No burning is permitted during an air pollution episode, such as an air pollution alert/warning/emergency declared by proper authorities.
(5)
No burning is permitted when there is a fog, rain, or cloud bases that are diffused, or ill defined, or for at least one day following the passage of a cold front.
(6)
No burning of solid waste or household garbage is allowed.
(7)
No burning of any materials that emit a heavy dark smoke, such as rubber products, oils, roofing material, petroleum-based products, etc.
(8)
No burning when smoke presents a health hazard to persons in the vicinity of the fire.
(9)
No burning shall be conducted within 20 feet of flammable, combustible or explosive materials unless otherwise specified by NFPA or standard fire prevention codes.
(10)
Burning of leaves, limbs, etc., that fall on a person's own property can only be conducted during daylight hours.
(11)
Prior notification shall be given to the state forestry commission before any agricultural burning is conducted. The term "agricultural" refers to land zoned RA-200, as defined by the county zoning ordinance.
(12)
All fires shall be constantly attended by a competent person until extinguished.
(13)
There shall be a garden hose connected to a water supply or other fire extinguishing equipment readily available for use.
(e)
Summary abatement. The staff of the fire marshal's office and personnel of the county department of fire and emergency services shall have the authority to summarily abate any conditions which are in violation of any of the provisions of this section, and which may constitute an immediate threat to life and property. In no event shall a pit fire or other open burning continue after the fire marshal's office has revoked the permit.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-54)
Sec. 22-49. - Setting fire to motor vehicle; notice required.
No person shall set fire to an automobile, truck, or any other type of motor vehicle without first obtaining written permission from the chief of the department of fire and emergency services.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-55)
Sec. 22-50. - Burning of certain materials prohibited.
No material or substance which emits noxious or poisonous gases such as nitrogen dioxide, hydrogen cyanide or chlorine, or other poisonous gases that will not readily dissipate in the atmosphere, may be burned within the city limits.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-56)
Sec. 22-51. - Certain officers vested with police authority.
The chief of the department of fire and emergency services or other authorized officers acting pursuant to his authority shall be sworn in as police officers with the powers to make arrests pertaining to arson within the city.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-57)
Sec. 22-52. - Authority to cut electric wires.
The chief of the department of fire and emergency services or other authorized officers acting pursuant to his authority are hereby authorized to cut any electrical wire which shall be deemed necessary on the occurrence of any fire, without expense to the county for so doing, provided that nothing contained in this section shall imply that the county has authorized any person to undertake the risk of cutting any such wires or renders itself liable for any injury to the person so doing.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-58)
Sec. 22-53. - Obstructing a fire station.
It shall be unlawful for any person to put any obstruction of any nature whatsoever in front of any fire station or location where fire service equipment is located.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-59)
Sec. 22-54. - False alarms.
No person shall intentionally make, turn in, or report a false alarm of fire or false report for police or ambulance assistance, or aid or abet in the commission of such an act.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-60)
Sec. 22-55. - Response to malfunctioning fire alarms.
When emergency response to a malfunctioning fire alarm system exceeds two in a 30-day period, a fee shall be levied for each additional incident. Fees shall be payable, by check, to the fire marshal's office. The additional incident fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-61)
Sec. 22-56. - Fire extinguishing equipment installation in restaurants or other public facilities having cooking equipment.
(a)
Approved fire extinguishing equipment shall be installed in restaurants or other public food facilities having cooking appliances, such as ranges, deep fat fryers, grills or broilers.
(b)
The standards for approval of such facilities shall be based on NFPA #96 or an equivalent measure deemed acceptable to the fire marshal or assistant fire marshal.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-62)
Sec. 22-57. - Combustible roof construction.
Plans for the construction of all roofs shall comply with NFPA pamphlets 203M, 220, 251 and 256.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-63)
Sec. 22-58. - Portable buildings.
(a)
All portable buildings open to the public shall provide adequate measures to prevent the superstructure from sliding, uplift, rotation and overturning.
(b)
Such portable buildings shall be provided with anchors and tiedowns, such as cast-in-place, concrete dead-men eyelets embedded in concrete slabs, runways, screwaugers, arrowhead anchors, or other devices to be used to stabilize the portable building.
(c)
Anchors and tiedowns shall be placed at each corner of the portable building and shall be able to sustain a minimum load of 4,800 pounds.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-64)
Sec. 22-59. - Designated fire lanes.
(a)
Streets or drives adjacent to the following locations shall have designated fire lanes:
(1)
Any building 22,500 square feet or more with storage facilities, except for storage warehouse occupancies which are equal to or greater than 80 percent of the total building area and which comply with subsection (b) of this section.
(2)
Places of assembly with occupant load of 300 or more.
(3)
The area five feet on each side of fire hydrants and associated appliances used for fire protection.
(4)
All points of exit from nursing homes, hospitals, multistoried office buildings and places of assembly.
(b)
Buildings not required to have fire lanes shall maintain fire service access to at least one side.
(c)
Where designated fire lanes are required, they shall meet the following criteria:
(1)
The curbing shall be painted traffic yellow. Where curbing is not present, there shall be designated a 20-foot-wide, cross hatched, traffic yellow way of access kept free of obstructions.
(2)
Signs meeting the specifications described in this article shall be spaced no less than 30 feet apart and legible from both directions of travel.
Specifications for fire lane signs shall be as indicated on the official diagram maintained in the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners entitled "Specifications for Fire Lane Signs."
(d)
Building driveways shall be kept clear of any obstruction which would hinder access by fire services equipment.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-65)
Sec. 22-60. - Unlawful ignition of combustible materials.
Any persons smoking or attempting to light or smoke a cigarette, cigar, pipe or tobacco in any form for which lighters, matches, flammable liquids or chemicals are used, and who sets fire to any bedding, furniture, curtains, drapes, house or household furnishings, in any hotel, roominghouse or multifamily house, as a result of such smoking or attempting to smoke, shall be in violation of this article.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-66)
Sec. 22-61. - Reporting of fires.
Any damage by fire having a total dollar value of $200.00 or more shall be reported to the fire prevention section of the department of fire and emergency services within 24 hours.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-67)

Sec. 22-85. - Solution guidelines.
(a)
Plans review. Plans and specifications as submitted by an architect, engineer, owner, developer, or contractor shall be reviewed by staff members of the fire marshal's office of the department of fire and emergency services and the development division of the department of planning and development. Each plan review unit will apply their respective codes and ordinances, as defined by Part A, Items No. 1, 2 and 3, of the ordinance from which this section is derived. During the review process, whenever differing code requirements or interpretations surface and cannot be resolved by the plan review staff, the following shall take place:
(1)
The differing requirements and/or opinions shall be referred to the manager of the building construction plan review unit and the fire marshal for disposition.
(2)
If the manager of the building construction plan review unit and the fire marshal are unable to resolve the problem, it shall be referred to the development division director of the department of planning and development and the assistant chief of technical services.
(3)
If the development division director and the assistant chief of technical services are not able to come to agreement, it shall be referred to the chief of the department of fire and emergency services and the director of the department of planning and development.
(4)
If the director of the department of planning and development and the chief of the department of fire and emergency services are unable to reach an accord, the quandary shall be referred to the attention of the board of construction adjustments and appeals for final disposition.
(b)
Inspections. Once the facility is permitted and under construction, it will be inspected by members of the fire marshal's office of the department of fire and emergency services and the department of planning and development. Each unit applies their respective regulations pursuant to the approved plans and specifications relating to the construction project. During the inspection process, whenever differing code requirements or interpretations surface and cannot be resolved by the field inspections staff, the following shall take place:
(1)
The differing requirements or opinions shall be referred to the appropriate chief inspector and the field inspections supervisor of the fire marshal's office of the department of fire and emergency services for solution.
(2)
If the appropriate chief inspector and the field inspections supervisor of the fire marshal's office of the department of fire and emergency services are not successful in reaching a solution, the situation shall be forwarded to the manager of the building inspections unit and the fire marshal for disposition.
(3)
If the manager of the building inspections unit and the fire marshal are unable to reach a compromise, the predicament will be directed to the attention of the development division director of the department of planning and development and the assistant chief of technical services.
(4)
If the development division director of the department of planning and development and the assistant chief of technical services are not able to come to agreement, it shall be referred to the chief of the department of fire and emergency services and the director of the department of planning and development.
(5)
If the director of the department of planning and development and the chief of the department of fire and emergency services are unable to reach an accord, the matter shall be referred to the board of construction adjustments and appeals for final disposition.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 46-93)
