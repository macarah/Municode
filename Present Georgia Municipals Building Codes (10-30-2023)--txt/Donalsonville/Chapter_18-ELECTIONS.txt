Chapter 18 - ELECTIONS[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Georgia Municipal Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.

Sec. 18-1. - Compliance with state law.
All elections and challenges thereto shall be in compliance with the Georgia Election Code codified at O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
Sec. 18-2. - General elections.
In accordance with section 2.02 of the Charter, general municipal elections shall be held on the Tuesday next following the first Monday in November of each odd-numbered year.
Sec. 18-3. - Special elections.
Special elections shall be held as needed according to section 2.03 of the Charter and shall be conducted in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
Sec. 18-4. - Superintendents.
The city clerk shall serve as the municipal superintendent in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70.1. The council shall provide for the superintendent in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-71.
Sec. 18-5. - Conducting elections.
The city shall coordinate all elections with the Seminole County Commission and Seminole County Probate Court. The city may contract or otherwise decide by agreement with the Seminole County Probate Court to serve as municipal superintendent and to coordinate and conduct all elections in the city.
Sec. 18-6. - Notice of candidacy.
Filing of notice of candidacy for municipal office shall be conducted in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132 as amended.
Sec. 18-7. - Fees.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, each candidate shall pay a fee of three percent of the total salary of the office sought.
(b)
A pauper's affidavit may be filed in lieu of the qualifying fee in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
Sec. 18-8. - Electors to be registered by county.
The city council shall provide for the use of the county voter registration list. Any person who is a resident of the city and who is registered as an elector with the county shall be eligible to vote in any municipal primary or election. Only residents of a particular council district within the city who are registered as electors with the county shall be eligible to vote in the municipal primary, general or special election for a councilmember of that particular district. Any resident of the city who is registered as an elector with the county shall be eligible to vote in at-large elections.
Sec. 18-9. - Polling place.
The polling places shall be as fixed from time to time by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 2-2-5; Code 1996, § 34-5)
