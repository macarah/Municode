DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1; due notice requirements for other than regular meetings, requirement to prescribe the time, place and dates of regular meetings of governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).

Sec. 2-40. - Regular meetings.
Regular meetings of the mayor and council are held at the city hall, or at such other place as may be designated, on the first Tuesday of each month at a time as established by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 2-1-1; Code 1996, § 2-46)
Sec. 2-41. - Adjourned meetings.
If a quorum shall fail to attend any regular or special meeting of the mayor and council or if, for any reason, any meeting shall fail to complete transaction of the business before the end of the meeting, the meeting may be adjourned to any date prior to the next regular meeting, agreed upon by a majority of the members present, so long as any meeting is scheduled and noticed consistent with the requirements of the Charter.
(Code 1984, § 2-1-2; Code 1996, § 2-47)
Sec. 2-42. - Order of business.
(a)
The order of business at all regular meetings of the mayor and council shall be as follows:
(1)
Approval or correction and approval of minutes of last meeting;
(2)
Appearances;
(3)
Permit and license applications;
(4)
Internal matters and reports;
(5)
Old business;
(6)
New business;
(7)
Other business; and
(8)
Adjournment.
(b)
If the mayor and council directs any matter to be the special business of a future meeting, that matter shall have precedence over all other business at that future meeting.
(c)
No proposition shall be entertained by the mayor and council until it has been seconded, and every proposition shall, when required by the mayor or any member, be reduced to writing.
(Code 1984, § 2-1-3; Code 1996, § 2-48)
Sec. 2-43. - Rules of procedure.
Except as otherwise provided by ordinance, the procedures of the mayor and council shall be governed by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised.
(Code 1984, § 2-1-4; Code 1996, § 2-49)
Sec. 2-44. - Previous questions.
The previous questions may be called at any time by a majority of the members present. The ayes and nays may be called for by any member.
(Code 1984, § 2-1-5; Code 1996, § 2-50)
Sec. 2-45. - Motions having precedence.
(a)
When a question is under consideration, no motion shall be received except as follows:
(1)
Lay on the table;
(2)
Postpone to a time certain;
(3)
Postpone indefinitely;
(4)
Refer to a committee;
(5)
Amend;
(6)
Strike out or insert; or
(7)
Divide.
(b)
Motions for any of these purposes shall have precedence in the order named.
(Code 1984, § 2-1-6; Code 1996, § 2-51)
Sec. 2-46. - Motion to adjourn.
A motion to adjourn shall always be in order and shall be decided without debate.
(Code 1984, § 2-1-7; Code 1996, § 2-52)
Sec. 2-47. - Executive session minutes.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(e)(2)(C), when the city holds an executive session, they shall ensure that:
(1)
Minutes of executive sessions shall be recorded but shall not be open to the public;
(2)
Such minutes shall specify each issue discussed in executive session and that if matters subject to the attorney-client privilege are discussed, then the fact that an attorney-client privilege discussion occurred and its subject matter shall be identified, but the substance of the discussion need not be recorded and shall not be identified in the minutes;
(3)
Such minutes shall be kept and preserved for in camera and in chambers for inspection by an appropriate court, should a dispute arise as to the propriety of any executive session; and
(4)
Minutes may be made and records kept by the city clerk or a person temporarily appointed to take such minutes so long as the minutes accurately reflect the record of the meeting, subject to the limitations provided herein.
(b)
The mayor or the mayor's designee, which may include the clerk, shall record minutes of executive sessions of the mayor and city council.
(c)
Prior to the conclusion of the executive session, all members of the mayor and city council attending the executive session shall review the minutes recorded.
(d)
Following the conclusion of the executive session, and upon approval of such confidential minutes by a majority of the members in an open meeting, the minutes of the executive session recorded and approved, in accord with the terms hereof and as noted on the minutes, shall be transmitted to the city clerk to be placed in proper form and kept in a confidential file and preserved for in camera and in chambers for inspection in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(e)(2)(C).
(Res. of 9-1-2015(1))

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-68. - City clerk; duties.
In addition to the duties of the city clerk under the Charter, the city clerk shall perform the following duties:
(1)
Preserve the codes, records and documents belonging to the city and maintain a proper index to all records and documents;
(2)
Receive all money due the city, including taxes, licenses and fees, and pay out the money only upon orders of the mayor and council; and
(3)
Perform any other duties as may be required by the city manager.
(Code 1984, § 2-4-1; Code 1996, § 2-71)
Sec. 2-69. - City attorney; duties.
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the city and in that capacity shall:
(1)
Prepare ordinances when so requested by the mayor and council;
(2)
Prepare for execution all contracts and instruments to which the city is a party when so requested and approve, as to form, all ordinances, bonds and city contracts; and
(3)
Render any other legal services as may be required by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 2-4-2; Code 1996, § 2-72)

DIVISION 2. - CITY MANAGER

Sec. 2-97. - General responsibilities.
The city manager is responsible for the overall administration of city government, including personnel, budgets, purchasing, grants, insurance and pensions and maintenance.
(Code 1996, § 2-91)
Sec. 2-98. - Purpose of position; scope and affect.
(a)
The purpose of the position of city manager is to administer all phases of city government. The work is made complex by the multitude and variety of tasks to be completed and by the necessity for dealing with public input, which can be conflicting or critical.
(b)
The purpose of the position of city manager is to assist the city council in seeing that the city's government operates according to state law and city ordinances. Successful performance in this position facilitates work of all city departments, ensures that grant monies are obtained and used properly, ensures that city services are delivered, and ensures that city records are properly retained and enhances the image of the city.
(Code 1996, § 2-92)
Sec. 2-99. - Use of guidelines.
Guidelines to be used by the city manager consist of all city ordinances, applicable state and federal laws, grant application instructions, city policies and procedures, and directives from the city council. These guidelines require selection, judgment and interpretation in application.
(Code 1996, § 2-93)
Sec. 2-100. - Qualifications.
Minimum qualifications for the position of city manager are as follows:
(1)
Knowledge and level of competency commonly associated with the completion of a baccalaureate degree in a course of study related to this occupational field, or a combination of college education, training and experience sufficient to adequately prepare an individual to serve in this position.
(2)
Experience sufficient to thoroughly understand the diverse objectives and functions of the subunits in the division/department in order to direct and coordinate work within the division/department, usually interpreted to require three to five years of related experience.
(3)
Knowledge of the principles and practices of public administration.
(4)
Knowledge of the techniques used in the grant application process.
(5)
Knowledge of the city codes and ordinances and other regulations, policies and procedures.
(6)
Knowledge of budget preparation and finance.
(7)
Knowledge of the functions, organization and operations of all city departments.
(8)
Knowledge of the city's demographic and economic profile, including the industrial base.
(9)
Knowledge of the principles and practices of effective public relations.
(10)
Knowledge of state and federal laws in all areas applicable to city government, including unemployment, wage and hour, EEOC, taxes, worker's compensation, pensions and others.
(11)
Skill in written and oral communications.
(Code 1996, § 2-94)
Sec. 2-101. - Major duties.
The city manager shall have the following duties:
(1)
Administer all operations of city government.
(2)
Serve as personnel officer by appointing, supervising and evaluating employees, with the exception of the city attorney, in conformity to the city personnel rules and regulations.
(3)
See that all laws and ordinances of the city are enforced.
(4)
Supervise and direct daily operations of all city departments.
(5)
Develop materials and reports for meetings of the city council and implement decisions and directives of the council.
(6)
Prepare the city budget by consulting with department heads, elected officials and the city council, make and implement recommendations and control income and expenditures.
(7)
Keep the city council fully advised as to the financial condition of the city by monthly financial and administrative reports. Special reports from the city manager may be required.
(8)
Supervise the performance of all street and other contracts made for the city and assist the city council in acquiring rights-of-way for street projects.
(9)
Develop applications for and administer grants such as, but not limited to, community development block grants, department of community affairs grants, Environment Protection Agency grants and state department of transportation grants.
(10)
Act as liaison between the city council and the public and media by responding to inquiries and resolving conflicts.
(11)
Provide reports and information and any other related duties for the city council.
(Code 1996, § 2-95)
Sec. 2-102. - Supervisory control.
The mayor and council assigns work on the basis of standing goals, objectives and priorities. The city manager must use much judgment in deciding how to accomplish goals. The supervisor provides guidance for problematic situations and spot checks work for the nature and propriety of final results.
(Code 1996, § 2-96)
Sec. 2-103. - Personal contacts.
(a)
Contacts are typically with employees in all city departments, members of the city council, employees of the state, representatives of organizations and the public at large.
(b)
Contacts are typically for exchange of information relevant to city administrative activities, provision of guidance to city employees, response to inquiries from the public, and provision of information about the county to the state and others.
(Code 1996, § 2-97)

ARTICLE IV. - DEPARTMENTS

Sec. 2-135. - Established.
The following departments of the city are hereby established:
(1)
Administration;
(2)
Police;
(3)
Fire;
(4)
Utilities;
(5)
Public works.
(Code 1984, § 2-4-3; Code 1996, § 2-116)
Sec. 2-136. - Records management.
(a)
The general government department is responsible for records management activities, and this department shall designate a person or officer to act for and on behalf of the mayor and council in directing and coordinating all records management matters, including maintenance and security of records.
(b)
No record shall be destroyed except in accordance with approved retention schedules.
(c)
Whenever any records are destroyed, a certificate of destruction shall be prepared and forwarded to the mayor and council which shall show the type of records destroyed, the approved retention schedule number, the dates covered, and the volume destroyed.
(Code 1984, § 2-4-4; Code 1996, § 2-117)

Sec. 2-181. - Purchases.
(a)
Emergency or routine. All departments of the city are authorized to make emergency or routine purchases not in excess of $250.00, provided a purchase order in triplicate has been issued and signed by the head of the department making the purchase.
(b)
Purchase order between $250.00 and $5,000.00. Whenever a purchase order is issued by a department head in excess of $250.00, but not exceeding $5,000.00, the department head shall obtain the signature of the city manager as a second signature on the purchase order. The city manager is authorized to issue purchase orders, either individually or as second signature, on purchases up to $5,000.00.
(c)
Purchase order over $5,000.00. When a purchase order is issued for a purchase in excess of $5,000.00, approval to do so shall be obtained from the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 3-1-1; Code 1996, § 2-176; Ord. of 5-7-1991)
State Law reference— Multiyear lease, purchase or lease-purchase contracts, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-13.
Sec. 2-182. - Duty of clerk to present financial statement.
It shall be the duty of the city clerk to present a financial statement at each regular monthly meeting of the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 3-1-2; Code 1996, § 2-177)
