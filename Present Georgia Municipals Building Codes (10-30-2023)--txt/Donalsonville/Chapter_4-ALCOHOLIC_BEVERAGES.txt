Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverages means all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Gallon or wine gallon means a United States gallon of liquid measure equivalent to the volume of 231 cubic inches or the nearest equivalent metric measurement.
Importer means any person who imports an alcoholic beverage into this state from a foreign country and sells the alcoholic beverage to another importer, broker, or wholesaler and who maintains a stock of alcoholic beverages.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The term "manufacturer" also means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits;
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, any brewer; and
(3)
In the case of wine, any vintner.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Retail consumption dealer means any person who sells distilled spirits for consumption on the premises at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Retailer or retail dealer means, except as to distilled spirits, any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale. With respect to distilled spirits, the term "retailer" or "retail dealer" means any person who sells distilled spirits in unbroken packages at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Shipper means any person who ships an alcoholic beverage from outside this state.
Standard case means six containers of 1.75 liters, 12 containers of 750 milliliters, 12 containers of one liter, 24 containers of 500 milliliters, 24 containers of 375 milliliters, 48 containers of 200 milliliters, or 120 containers of 50 milliliters.
Tax stamp means the official mark, stamp or indicium of the department used to indicate the payment of taxes imposed by this chapter.
Taxpayer means any person made liable by law to file a return or to pay tax.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-1)
Sec. 4-2. - Hearing procedure.
(a)
Generally. Whenever the city clerk has scheduled an appeal under this chapter before the city council, at the time and date set therefor, the council shall receive all relevant testimony and evidence from the permittee, from interested parties and from city staff. The city council may sustain, overrule or modify the action complained of. The action of the city council shall be final.
(b)
Powers of hearing officer. The hearing officer, appointed pursuant to the procedure set out in this chapter, may receive and rule on admissibility of evidence, hear testimony under oath and call witnesses as he may deem advisable with respect to the conduct of the hearing.
(c)
Rules of evidence inapplicable. The city council and the hearing officer shall not be bound by the traditional rules of evidence in hearings conducted under this chapter. Rules of evidence as applied in an administrative hearing shall apply.
(d)
Hearing officer's report. The hearing officer shall, within a reasonable time not to exceed 30 days from the date such hearing is terminated, submit a written report to the council. Such report shall contain a brief summary of the evidence considered and state findings, conclusions and recommendation. All such reports shall be filed with the city clerk, and shall be considered public records. A copy of such report shall be forwarded by certified mail to the permittee/appellant the same day it is filed with the city clerk.
(e)
Meeting. The city clerk shall place the hearing officer's report on the agenda of the next regular city council meeting occurring not less than ten days after the report is filed and shall notify the permittee/appellant of the date of such meeting at least ten days prior to the meeting, unless the permittee/appellant stipulates to a shorter notice period.
(f)
Action by council. The city council may adopt or reject the hearing officer's decision in its entirety or may modify the proposed recommendation. If the city council does not adopt the hearing officer's recommendation, it may:
(1)
Refer the matter to the same or another hearing officer for a completely new hearing, or for the taking of additional evidence on specified points. In either of such cases, the hearing officer shall proceed as provided in this chapter.
(2)
Decide the case upon a review of the entire record before the hearing officer with or without taking additional evidence.
Sec. 4-3. - Appeal procedure.
(a)
The licensee or applicant, as applicable, under this chapter shall, within ten days after he has been notified of an adverse determination, submit a notice of appeal to the city clerk.
(b)
The notice of appeal shall be addressed to the mayor and council and shall specify the subject matter of the appeal, the date of any original and amended application or requests, the date of the adverse decision or receipt of notice thereof, the basis of the appeal, the action requested of the council and the name and address of the applicant.
(c)
The clerk shall place the appeal on the agenda of the next regular city council meeting occurring not less than five nor more than 30 days after receipt of the application for council action.
(d)
When an appeal is placed on the council agenda, the council may take either of the following actions:
(1)
Set a hearing date and instruct the city clerk to give such notice of hearing as may be required by law.
(2)
Appoint a hearing officer and fix the time and place for hearing. The hearing officer may or may not be a city employee, and may be appointed for an extended period of time. The clerk shall assume responsibility for such publication of notice of the hearing as may be required by law. If a hearing officer is appointed, the hearing shall be conducted in accordance with the procedures set out in this chapter.
(Code 1996, § 10-87)

ARTICLE II. - LICENSES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-25. - Compliance required.
(a)
The sale of alcoholic beverages shall be legal in the city, subject to the following terms, conditions, restrictions set forth herein and upon full compliance by all persons engaged in business under the auspices of any license granted under this article.
(b)
The license granted under this article shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on the licensed business during the term of the license, subject to all the terms and conditions imposed by local, state, and federal law.
(c)
The following licenses may be issued by the mayor and city council as provided herein:
(1)
Off-premises retail package malt beverage license.
(2)
Off-premises retail package wine license.
(3)
Off-premises retail package malt beverage and wine license.
(4)
Off-premises retail package liquor/distilled spirits license.
(5)
On-premises consumption of liquor/distilled spirits license.
(6)
On-premises consumption malt beverages and wine license.
(7)
On-premises consumption malt beverages, wine, and liquor/distilled spirits license.
(8)
Special event malt beverage and wine consumption license, as a license which may be available to qualified applicants.
(9)
Alcoholic beverage amenity license, as a license which may be available to qualified applicants.
(d)
On-premises consumption licenses shall derive at least 50 percent of the establishment's total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals and food in all of the combined retail outlets. Sales of food products other than prepared meals will be considered in determining the 50 percent ratio so long as bona fide artistic or cultural activities are conducted on the premises simultaneous with the consumption of alcohol. The applicant shall submit such business records as may be requested by the city to verify that the foregoing percentages are being met.
(Code 1996, § 6-75(a); Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-31; Ord. No. 12-06-22, § 1, 1-3-2023; Ord. No. 05-02-23, § I, 6-6-2023; Ord. No. 12-01-22, 1, 4-17-2023)
Sec. 4-26. - Renewal, fee.
Each such license and license fee shall be for the calendar year. The fee shall be due and payable in advance by January 1 of each year. Any person desiring to begin an alcoholic beverage business in the city shall pay the prescribed fee and obtain a license before commencing business.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-33)
Sec. 4-27. - Applications for licenses.
(a)
The application for a permit shall be submitted in writing to the mayor and council, or their designee, and shall set forth therein the following information:
(1)
The type of license for which application is made and whether the applicable requirements stated in this article are met;
(2)
The location of the place of business wherein beer and/or wine and/or liquor is to be sold, and whether the specific requirements related to the location and premises, including, but not limited to, occupancy of structure, land use, distance restriction and all other requirements of this Code are met;
(3)
Whether the premises are owned or leased with proof of ownership or lawful possession by a current lease shown by the applicant;
(4)
Names and address of all persons, firms or corporations having a financial interest in profits or losses;
(5)
A copy of the birth certificate of the applicant showing the applicant to be a U.S. citizen or proof of legal permanent residency in the United States or proof that the applicant is a naturalized citizen of the United States;
(6)
If the applicant is a corporation, proof by a certificate from the state secretary of state showing that the corporation is in good standing to conduct business in the state, either as a state corporation or a corporation organized in another state lawfully registered to do business in the state;
(7)
Proof of liquor liability insurance, as required by this article;
(8)
A signed consent to conduct an NCIC and GCIC background check and fingerprint check to be conducted by the city police department or the county sheriff's office, as authorized in this article;
(9)
Payment of all applicable application fees, including all cost of background checks and/or fingerprinting and cost of publishing notice of the application, as applicable, in addition to the applicable license fee;
(10)
And such other information that the mayor and council may from time to time require.
(b)
The application shall be signed by the applicant and must be verified by oath of the applicant; and if the applicant is a corporation, by the oath of one of its corporate officers or managers. Such application and oath must be on a form provided by the city.
(c)
All applicants shall furnish all data, information, and records pertinent to the application requested of them in writing by the city, and failure to furnish such data, information, and records within 30 day from the date of such request shall automatically serve to dismiss, with prejudice, the application. The applicants, by filling an application, agree to produce for oral interrogation any person requested by the police department or the mayor and council as considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to such application and/or license under the terms of this article. The failure to produce such person within 30 days after being requested to do so shall result in the automatic dismissal of any application pending under this article.
(d)
Any establishment making applications for a license to sell malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits for on-premises consumption shall secure and maintain proof necessary, to the city, of general liability insurance for said establishment for liquor liability, injury to persons, and damage to property related to the consumption of alcohol from said establishment in limits of liability with a minimum coverage of $300,000.00 per occurrence.
(e)
Upon filing an application, all applicants must pay an application fee and license fee in the required amounts, as established by the mayor and council, the city manager shall issue a receipt and attach a copy of said receipt to the application.
(f)
The city manager shall first review the application for completeness and compliance with this article, then the application, along with the city manager's recommendation will be considered by the mayor and council not less than 30 nor more than 60 days from the submission of a completed application for processing any application for a license under the terms of this article.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-34)
Sec. 4-28. - Notice.
(a)
Unless otherwise provided, notice of all applications for any license under this article shall, within ten days after the completion of the required investigation, be published in the official newspaper of the city wherein all legal advertisements are published to the effect that such application has been made, listing the name of the applicant, the complete address of the place of business where such application for the sale of alcoholic beverages is proposed, and the date of the hearing thereon. The advertisement shall be published once per week for two weeks preceding the next regular meeting of the mayor and council. Such advertisement shall be filed with the proper officer or agent of such newspaper within ten days following the date of application.
(b)
The applicant shall pay in advance to the city clerk an application fee, as established by the mayor and council, which covers the cost of advertisement of the notice. The city clerk will then place the required notice in the legal organ of the city.
(c)
No application for a license under this article shall be acted upon on issued by the mayor and council until after advertisement and public hearing thereof, as required herein.
(d)
A public hearing upon all applications for a license under this article shall be held before the mayor and council at its regular meeting not less than 30 nor more than 60 days after the filing of the application.
(e)
Advertisement is required for all license applications, except for renewal of licenses.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-36)
Sec. 4-29. - Investigation of applicant by police.
All applicants shall be investigated by the police department or by such other person as may be designated by the mayor and council. The report of such investigation shall be presented to the mayor and council for consideration with the application.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-37)
Sec. 4-30. - Tax investigation of applicant.
The mayor and council shall cause an inquiry to be made into the tax records to determine if any applicant or other parties interested in an application have outstanding taxes or special assessments that are delinquent or any other monies owing to the city. No license shall be issued, nor shall a license be renewed, until all such debts are paid in full.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-38)
Sec. 4-31. - Issuance prohibited to certain persons.
It shall be unlawful to issue a license to:
(1)
A person who has been convicted of a felony under the state statutes or the statutes of any other state or of the United States within five years of the date of application.
(2)
A person whose place of business in conducted by an employee, manager or agent, unless the manger or agent or employee possesses the same qualifications as the licensee.
(3)
A person who is not of good moral character.
(4)
A person who is deemed to have insufficient mental capacity.
(5)
A person who has been dishonorably discharged from the armed services of the United States.
(6)
Any person who, as a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages, has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business within a three-year period preceding the date of the application under this article.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-39)
Sec. 4-32. - Qualifications of applicant and employees.
(a)
No person shall be granted an alcoholic beverage license unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the mayor and council that such person, partners in the firm, and officers and directors of the corporation shall be of good moral character, and in no event shall any license be granted to any person or office or director of any such corporation who has been convicted or has pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving moral turpitude, lottery, or illegal possession or sale of narcotics or liquors within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such applications; or who has been convicted or has pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to the crime of DUI or public drunkenness within a period of two years immediately prior to the filing of such application; nor shall any such person sell or dispense alcoholic beverages. At the time an application is submitted for a retail or wholesale alcoholic beverage license, the applicant shall, by a duly sworn affidavit, certify that neither he, nor any of the other owners of the retail or wholesale establishment, nor the manager of such establishment, has been convicted or has pled quality or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving moral turpitude, lottery or illegal possession or sale of narcotics or liquors within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application, or has been convicted or has pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to the crime of DUI or public drunkenness within a period of two years immediately prior to the filing of such application. Should any applicant, partner or officer, after a license has been granted, be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to a crime involving moral turpitude or the violation of any laws regulating the sale of narcotics, alcoholic beverages or the lottery laws of this state, such license shall be immediately revoked and cancelled.
(b)
Should any employee involved in the sale or dispensing of alcoholic beverages after a license has been granted be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to a crime involving moral turpitude or the violation of any law regarding the sale of narcotics, alcoholic beverages or the lottery laws of this state, and the crime was committed in the course of the employee's employment, on or off the premises of the licensee, and the licensee knew or should have known that the crime had been committed and did not immediately report the crime to the proper police authorities, such license shall be subject to be revoked and cancelled.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-41)
Sec. 4-33. - Distance requirements.
(a)
Regulations regarding sales within certain distances from churches, schools, etc. As provided in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21, no person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell:
(1)
Any distilled spirits in or within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus;
(2)
Any wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus. This subsection shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 1981, nor to the renewal of such license. Nor shall this subsection apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of wine and beer was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application. Nothing in this subsection shall prohibit a grocery store licensed for the retail sale of only wine and malt beverages for consumption off the premises from selling wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus, where, as used in this subsection, the term "grocery store" means a retail establishment which has a total retail floor space of at least 10,000 square feet, of which at least 85 percent is reserved for the sale of food and other non-alcoholic items, conducts all of its sales inside the building containing its retail floor space;
(3)
Any distilled spirits, wine, or malt beverages within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by this state or any county or municipal government therein. This subsection shall not apply to any business having a license in effect on July 1, 1981;
(4)
As used in this subsection, the term "school building" or "educational building" shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b).
(b)
Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the licensing of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages by:
(1)
Hotels of 50 rooms or more which have been in continuous operation for a period of at least five years preceding July 1, 1981;
(2)
Bona fide private clubs, owning their own homes, subject to licensing under O.C.G.A. § 3-7-1 et seq.; and
(3)
Licensees for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises only who shall be subject to regulation as to distances from churches, schools, and colleges.
(c)
For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(d)
Housing authority property.
(1)
As used in this subsection, the term "housing authority property" shall have the same meaning as provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21(e).
(2)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property. This subsection shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 2000, nor to the renewal of such license. Nor shall this subsection apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-42; Res. No. 06-19-2017, 6-19-2017)
Sec. 4-34. - Effect of revocation or denial.
If the mayor and council revokes or denies any license for the sale of alcoholic beverages at any location within the city, no new license shall be granted by the city for the sale of any alcoholic beverages for the period of six months after the date of revocation or denial at the same location. After notice to the mayor and council that a person holding a license, or his employee or agent, is under investigation of prohibited acts, no new license shall be issued for such location until after the disposition of the complaint.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-42(h); Res. No. 06-19-2017, 6-19-2017)
Sec. 4-35. - Transferability.
Licenses issued under the authority of this article shall not be transferable from one person to another, nor from one location to another, except with the express written approval of the mayor and council after completion of a new application by the transferee of said license.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-43)
Sec. 4-36. - Display of licenses.
The license certificate shall be posted conspicuously in the licensee's place of business. Before operating under such city license, the licensee must secure the state license and comply with all provisions of applicable state law. It shall be the duty of the city clerk to furnish to the licensee such certificate, copy of the city license, or other evidence as may be required by the state revenue commission in order to show payment of the city license fee and approval of the license application by the mayor and council, all as provided herein.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-44)
Sec. 4-37. - Duration; renewals.
(a)
All licenses granted pursuant to this article shall expire on December 31 of the year in which the license shall be issued.
(b)
Licensees who desire to renew their license shall file applications with the requisite fee as established by the mayor and council, with the city manager on the form provided for renewal of the license for the ensuing year. Renewal applications for any establishment currently in compliance with applicable local, state and federal regulations are not required to submit to criminal background investigations, provide public notice, or to obtain mayor and council approval, as required for initial licenses.
(c)
Applications for the renewal must be filed before December 1 of each year, otherwise no renewal license shall be granted. An application for renewal shall be treated as an initial application when filed after the December 1 deadline and the application shall be required to comply with all licensing requirements outlined in this article as if no previous license has been held.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-45)
Sec. 4-38. - Suspension; revocation.
(a)
Any license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any licensee may be suspended or revoked for due cause as defined in this section, and after a hearing held by the mayor and council for the purpose of considering any such suspension or revocation. At least five days' prior written notice of a time, place and purpose of such hearing, and a statement of the charges upon which such hearing shall be held, shall be given to the holder of such license which is sought to be suspended or revoked.
(b)
The term "due cause" for the suspension or revocation of any license shall consist of a violation of any laws or ordinances regulating such business, or violation of regulations made pursuant to the authority granted for the purpose of regulating such businesses, or for the violation of any state, federal, municipal or city ordinance that would prevent one from obtaining a license, or for the violation of any provision of this article.
(c)
The mayor is hereby authorized to suspend any license issued under this article in any emergency situation, such as civil disorders or natural disasters, or in any situation that the mayor deems such immediate suspension necessary for the protection of the health and welfare of the citizens of the city, and the suspension may be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until the mayor determines the emergency is over or until the next regular meeting of the mayor and council.
(d)
When a license is suspended or revoked, the city shall not be required to refund any portion of the license fee to the holder of such suspended or revoked license. When a license is revoked, the licensee may not reapply for a license under this article for a period of three years from such revocation.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-46)
Sec. 4-39. - Sales under suspended license and hearings.
No person whose license has been suspended or revoked pursuant to any provision of this article shall sell any beverages covered by the suspended or revoked license during the period of such suspension or after such revocation. No license shall be suspended or revoked without the mayor and council first giving the licensee written notice of the reason therefor, and, within five days thereafter, the licensee shall have the right to a hearing before the mayor and council and may present any evidence or give any reason why the license should not be suspended or revoked. Any person or corporation denied issuance of a license may request in writing a hearing before the mayor and council within five days of written notice of the denial of the application.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-47)
Sec. 4-40. - Rehearing.
Any person aggrieved by any act or omission of the mayor and council with respect to its proceedings under this article, including the suspension or revocation of a license, shall have the right to move for a rehearing. Such motion for omission complained of shall be in writing, and shall outline the manner in which such aggrieved party believes that the mayor and council erred. If the motion relates to a matter as to which evidence has already been heard, no additional evidence or argument shall be permitted on the motion for rehearing except after a grant of rehearing by the mayor and council. If the matter of which the complaint is made was not the subject of a prior hearing at which the aggrieved party was permitted to be present and heard, the mayor and council shall afford a hearing within ten days after the filing of the motion, of which reasonable notice shall be given to all parties known to the mayor and council to be interested, at which hearing persons interested in protesting or supporting the act or omission of the mayor and council shall be allowed to appear, be represented, introduce oral and documentary evidence, and examine and cross-examine witnesses.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-48)
Sec. 4-41. - Off-premises consumption district.
There is hereby established an off-premises consumption district within the city as defined by the attached aerial photograph, incorporated herein by reference. The following rules and regulations shall apply to this district (and subject to state law to the extent the same would govern):
(a)
A licensee located within the off-premises consumption district who holds a valid on-premises consumption of malt beverage and wine license is authorized to serve alcoholic beverages to patrons who opt to be seated in an outdoor dining area as part of the operation of the premises. The licensee may only serve alcoholic beverages to persons seated at a table or in a chair provided by the licensee. In addition, the alcoholic beverages being served shall only be transported into the outdoor dining area by a licensee's working employees as part of their work duties.
(b)
All alcoholic beverages sold only for consumption on the premises shall be consumed only at the licensed premises including the outdoor dining area as above described. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove, subject to paragraph (c) below, from the licensed premises any alcoholic beverage sold for consumption on the premises. Each licensee shall be responsible for ensuring that no person removes any on-premise alcoholic beverage from the premises in any type container other than as allowed by paragraph (c)(3) below.
(c)
As an exception to the foregoing subsections (a) and (b), a licensee which is located within the off-premise consumption district who is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages as permitted by section 4-25 of the Code of Ordinances may be permitted to sell wine and beer for outside consumption under the following terms and conditions:
(1)
Beer and wine, but not distilled spirits, purchased from licensees within the off-premises consumption district may be consumed on public streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, city parks or parking lots located in the said district, and according to the terms, conditions and regulations as enumerated in paragraphs (2) through (5) below.
(2)
Any establishment within the off-premises consumption district licensed to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on-premises is authorized to dispense an alcoholic beverage in a city approved paper or plastic cup, or other city approved container other than a can, bottle, or glass, for removal from the premises; provided however, that no establishment shall dispense to any person more than one such alcoholic beverage at a time for removal from the premises, and no person shall remove at one time more than one such alcoholic beverage from the licensed premises.
(3)
No open container in which an alcoholic beverage is dispensed and removed from the licensed premises shall exceed 16 fluid ounces in size. No person shall hold in possession in any public place within the district any open alcoholic beverage container which exceeds 16 fluid ounces in size, and which has not been purchased from a licensee within the district. Nothing set forth in this sub-paragraph shall be construed to permit the possession of open alcoholic beverages in any public place within the district except as otherwise expressly authorized herein.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess, drink or attempt to drink any alcoholic beverage from a can, bottle, glass or other such container on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, parks or parking lots of the city.
(5)
No alcoholic beverage purchased pursuant to the provisions of this chapter shall be consumed except within the authorized hours of sale of the establishment where purchased.
(Ord. No. 01-04-2022, § 1, 2-1-2022)
Sec. 4-42. - Special event malt beverage and wine permit.
(a)
A special event malt beverage and wine permit license is required for all special events that involve the sale and consumption of malt beverages and wine. Said license shall not be given without the prior approval of the city council. The term "special event" or "event' shall mean any organized activity, whether for profit or not, having as its primary purpose entertainment, recreation and/or education, which provides a substantial public and community benefit, such as a street festival, celebration, parade, rodeo, grand openings, concerts, rally or assembly, which takes place in whole or in part on public property public street or sidewalk or right-of-way, or any organized activity that occurs on private property and impacts government services on public right of way.
(b)
General rules; number of permits annually.
(1)
The special event malt beverage and wine permit shall be valid only for the place specified in the permit and the area where food and alcohol are to be served shall be specifically delineated on the application. The outermost boundaries of the food and alcohol area shall be fully and clearly delineated on a map, which may be created from an aerial photo of the area to be used for the special event, which shall be no smaller than 8½x11 inches in size and attached to the application for a permit. In no event shall patrons of the licensee be allowed to go beyond the said area as delineated on the application.
(2)
Food must be available to be served during any period of time that alcoholic beverages are served.
(3)
All alcohol sold must be in non glass containers.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for licensee's employees or patrons of licensee to bring into or take outside the delineated area any alcoholic beverage or to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person outside the delineated area.
(5)
No more than six such permits may be issued to the applicant in any one calendar year.
(c)
Period of license; business hours; distances.
(1)
A special event malt beverage and wine permit license shall authorize the licensee to sell malt beverages and wine for consumption only in the designated area for a period not to exceed three days.
(2)
The special event malt beverage and wine permit shall be subject to all laws and ordinances regulating the time for selling such beverages.
(3)
The special event malt beverage and wine permit shall be subject to all laws and ordinances regulating distance requirements as found in the above and foregoing section 4-33.
(d)
Licensee fee.
Upon the filing of an application with the required attachment, and payment of a non-refundable filing fee of $25.00 and a permit fee of $50.00 per day, and after investigation by the city staff, and upon favorable recommendation of the city manager, the city council may issue a permit to an individual or organization for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine for consumption on the premises only during a special event.
(e)
Application for permit.
(1)
Special event alcohol beverage permit license applications must be submitted at least 40 days prior to the planned special event.
(2)
At least ten days prior to the event, the applicant shall meet with the appropriate staff members of the city to formalize final plans and to determine any necessary involvement by the city to ensure compliance with the terms and provisions of the permit. The applicant shall sign a statement of understanding of an agreement to the terms and conditions imposed on the event.
(3)
The application for a special event permit shall set forth the following information.
A.
The name, address and telephone number of the individual seeking to conduct the special event;
B.
If the special event is proposed to be conducted for, or on behalf of or by an organization, the name, address and telephone number of the headquarters of the organization, and of the authorized and responsible heads of this organization;
C.
The name address and telephone number of the individual who will be the chair of the event for the organization and who will be responsible for its conduct;
D.
The dates when the special event will be conducted;
E.
The hours when the special event will begin and terminate;
F.
The estimated number of people who will attend the event;
G.
A reasonably retailed description of the event, together with the location at which alcohol will be consumed; and
H.
Any additional information which the city staff shall determine to be reasonably necessary to enable a fair determination as to whether a permit should issue.
(f)
General license eligibility requirements.
(1)
No alcoholic beverage permit license shall be granted to any person who is not at least 21 years of age.
(2)
Any individual who is a convicted felon, or has been convicted of a crime of moral turpitude, or who has been convicted a violation of the provisions of the chapter of this code regarding alcoholic beverages, shall not be eligible to receive a permit for a special event as contemplated herein.
(3)
All special event alcoholic beverage permit licensees shall be currently licensees by the city for the sale of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises.
(g)
Security.
The special event alcoholic beverage permit licensee shall provide, at their own expense, at least one security officer, approved by the chief of police, or designee.
(h)
Posting of license and notice; proof of license.
(1)
All special event alcohol beverage permit licenses shall be posted at greeting areas or main entrances to the event.
(2)
Every area where alcoholic beverages are consumed shall be conspicuously posted at all times at each point of entrance/exit with signs stating the following: "The possession of alcoholic beverages beyond this point is prohibited." The letters of such sign shall not be less than three inches in height and one-half inch in width and shall be in black letters on a contrasting light background.
(3)
City staff may ask licensee or business owners to show proof of license during the event.
(Ord. No. 12-01-22, § 2, 4-17-2023)

Sec. 4-43 - Alcoholic beverage amenity license.
A non-eating establishment that offers alcoholic beverages as an act of hospitality, where such activity is clearly an ancillary function of the business, shall be eligible to apply for an alcoholic beverage amenity license. Eating establishments shall not be eligible for an alcoholic beverage amenity license.
(a)
An amenity license shall allow the licensee to offer complimentary alcoholic beverages as an act of hospitality and such offer shall not be part of the core operation of the establishment.
(b)
Any alcoholic beverage served on the premises shall only be served by an employee of the business holding a valid pouring permit.
(c)
Application shall be made pursuant to the terms and requirements of the Donalsonville Code of Ordinances section 4-27 applications for licenses.
(d)
The entity applying for an alcoholic beverage amenity license shall comply with, and be governed by, all applicable provisions and restrictions, and shall meet all requirements of, article II, of chapter 4 of the Code of Ordinances except those in conflict with this section.
(Ord. No. 5-2-23, § II, 6-6-2023)

DIVISION 2. - PROHIBITED ACTS

Sec. 4-69. - Sales to minors and intoxicated persons.
No licensee shall by himself or another sell, furnish or cause to be sold or furnished, or permit any person in his employ to furnish alcoholic beverages to any person under the legal age for consumption of alcoholic beverages in the state, to any person who is noticeably intoxicated or to any habitual drunk who intemperate habits are known to such persons.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-66)
Sec. 4-70. - Effect of payment of tax.
The payment of any tax for the privilege of engaging in the business of vending alcoholic beverages shall not prevent the city from increasing at any time, before or after the grant of a license, the amount of tax, nor prevent the city from providing for and collecting any other form of taxation of such business.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-67)
Sec. 4-71. - Responsibility for acts of employees.
The licensee is responsible for the conduct and actions of his employees while in his employment.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-68)
Sec. 4-72. - Minors as employees.
(a)
Persons under 18 years of age are not to be allowed or required to serve, sell or take orders for alcoholic beverages.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under 18 years of age who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, or drugstores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-69)
Sec. 4-73. - Offenses involving minors.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the legal age for consumption of alcoholic beverages in the state to purchase, drink or possess any alcoholic beverages.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep or maintain a place wherein those under the legal age for consumption of alcoholic beverages in the state are permitted to come and bring or purchase any alcoholic beverages.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to buy alcoholic beverages or furnish alcoholic beverages to anyone under the legal age for consumption of alcoholic beverages in the state.
(d)
It shall be the responsibility of the licensee to examine the identification of patrons to be certain that such patrons are of legal age. A state driver's license, which is apparently valid upon its face, may be accepted by the licensee or his employee as evidence of age.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-70)
Sec. 4-74. - Age misrepresentation.
It shall be unlawful for any minor to misrepresent his age in any manner whatsoever for the purpose of evading state or local law.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-71)
Sec. 4-75. - Minors loitering on premises.
No person who holds a license to sell alcoholic beverages shall allow any minor to loiter about the premises of the licensee, unless he is present for the purpose of purchasing goods other than alcoholic beverages.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-72)
Sec. 4-76. - Package stores; standards.
(a)
Each licensee shall have printed on the front window of the licensed premises the inscription "City Retail License No. _____" in uniform letters not less than four inches in height.
(b)
No screen, blind, curtain, partition, article or thing which shall prevent a clear view into the interior shall be permitted in the window or upon the doors of any retail package store, and no booth, screen, partition or other obstruction shall be permitted within the interior of any store. Each retail store shall be so lighted that the interior of the store is visible day and night.
(c)
All premises shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition and in full compliance with the provisions and regulations governing the conditions of premises used for the storage and sale of food for human consumption. The fire department shall, upon request of the mayor and council, inspect such premises and report its findings to the mayor and council. All premises licensed under this article shall conform at all times with all fire regulations of the city.
(d)
No retailer of distilled spirits shall permit on his premises any slot machines or mechanical music boxes or pinball machines of any kind or character or any coin-operated machines or any machines operated for amusement purposes.
(e)
Any licensee under this article who shall begin the operation of the business and sale of the products, as authorized in the license, but who shall, for a period of six consecutive months thereafter, cease to operate the business and sale of the products authorized in the license shall, upon completion of the six-month period, automatically forfeit his license, which license shall, by virtue of the failure to operate, be cancelled without the necessity of any further action of the mayor and council.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-73)
Sec. 4-77. - Curb services; signs.
(a)
Curb service or other sales of alcoholic beverages or wine outside the building licensed to sell is hereby expressly prohibited.
(b)
No sign shall be erected anywhere within the city advertising or promoting the sale of alcoholic beverages except as may be authorized by state law and regulations of the state department of revenue or other agency having jurisdiction.
(c)
No lighted signs advertising alcoholic beverages for sale visible from outside the premises shall be permitted.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-74)
Sec. 4-78. - Hours of sale, food service and other requirements.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided herein, it shall be unlawful for any licensee licensed for on-premises consumption of malt beverages, wine, or distilled spirits hereunder to permit the sale or engage in the sale of such malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits, from 12:01 a.m. Sunday to 12:30 p.m. Sunday and from 12:01 a.m. Monday to 6:00 a.m. Monday.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any licensee licensed to sell packaged malt beverages, packaged wine or packaged distilled spirits hereunder to permit the sale or engage in the sale of such malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits from 12:01 a.m. Sunday to 12:30 p.m. Sunday and from 11:30 p.m. Sunday to 6:00 a.m. Monday.
(c)
All owners, managers, operators, representatives, agents, employees, corporate officer, or directors of a license shall cease operations of on the premises business for which a license has been issued by the city as of 2:01 a.m. Tuesday through Saturday, and 11:30 p.m. Sunday, and shall require all patrons of parties to vacate the premises from which license has been issued by 2:30 a.m. Tuesday through Saturday, by 12:01 a.m. Sunday and Monday, which shall include parking lots, buildings, and curtilages.
(d)
Hotels, motels, grocery stores, service stations and restaurants shall not be required to close functions and activities unrelated to alcohol sales and services not licensed under this article, and must ensure any alcohol inventory be and remain in the custody and control of the license or his employees and may not be dispensed in any manner.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-75; Res. of 9-1-2015(2); Res. of 9-4-2015)
Sec. 4-79. - Sales on election day.
Pursuant to the delegation of authority granted to the mayor and council by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20(b), the sale of malt beverages, wine and distilled spirits shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; provided, however, nothing herein shall authorize the sale of malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits within 250 feet of a polling place during such time as the polls are open.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-75(a))
Sec. 4-80. - Brown bagging.
(a)
Brown bagging prohibited. Except as permitted by this section, brown bagging and allowing, facilitating, permitting, or participating in brown bagging is unlawful and prohibited.
(b)
Operation of a brown bagging establishment prohibited. Except as permitted by this section, operating, allowing, facilitating, permitting, or participating in the operation of a brown bagging establishment is unlawful and prohibited.
(c)
Brown bagging at nonprofit events. Brown bagging shall not be prohibited at events of a bona fide nonprofit organization, which, for purposes of this section, shall include only those which are charitable, civic, religious, educational, artistic, theatrical, fraternal, or veterans' organizations.
(d)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bona fide nonprofit civic organization means an entity which is exempt from federal income tax pursuant to the provisions of 26 USC 501(c), (d) or (e).
Brown bagging means bringing onto and/or consuming upon any premises alcoholic beverages not lawfully purchased upon such premises.
Brown bagging establishment means any business, commercial establishment, or other facility or location upon which the owner, lessee, operator or his respective employees, agents, or representative thereof allow, facilitate, permit, or participate in brown bagging upon its premises.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016, § 6-76)

State Law reference— Excise tax on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80; excise tax on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60; excise tax on distilled liquor, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80.

Sec. 4-104. - Malt beverage excise tax; imposition and collection.
(a)
There is imposed and levied a specific tax upon all retail dealers in malt beverages within the city limits as follows:
(1)
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional part of 15½ gallons.
(2)
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional part of 12 ounces.
(b)
Each distributor, wholesale dealer or manufacturer selling, shipping or delivering malt beverages to any retail dealer in the city shall collect the excise tax as set forth in subsection (a) of this section and shall remit the tax to the city not later than the tenth of the month following collection. Each monthly remittance will be accompanied by invoice copies of all sales or deliveries to all retailers in the city for resale in the city, together with the sworn affidavit of such distributor, wholesale dealer or manufacturer, that the attached invoices are true, correct and complete.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-91)
Sec. 4-105. - Wine excise tax; imposition and collection.
(a)
In addition to the annual retail wine license fee, there is hereby levied an excise tax computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax on all fractional parts of a liter which shall be paid to the mayor and council on all wine sold, displayed or stored in the incorporated area of the city. The tax shall be collected by wholesale distributors and paid to the mayor and council by the wholesale distributors on all wine sold to retailers in the incorporate area of the city as follows: Each wholesale distributor selling, shipping, or in any way delivering wines to any retailer shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery and shall remit the tax together with a summary of all deliveries to each retailer on or before the tenth day of the following month.
(b)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any retailer to possess, own, hold store, display or sell any wine on which the tax has not been paid.
(c)
Each wholesale distributor shall furnish to the mayor and council a summary of all purchase invoices for wine sold to each retailer in the incorporated area of the city on or before the 15th day of each month following such purchases. The invoices shall show the amount of excise tax paid.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-92)
Sec. 4-106. - Retailers' records.
All retail dealers in malt beverages and/or wine, and all persons selling malt beverages and/or wine at retail, shall keep a complete and correct record of all purchases of malt beverage and wine, and shall require all persons from whom they purchase malt beverages or wine to furnish and deliver to them, at the time of delivery of such beverages, a correct invoice of purchase, which invoice shall be kept and preserved by the retailer at his place of business for a period of six months from the date of purchase. Such invoices and all records of retailers of malt beverages and/or wine shall be subject to inspection by any authorized representative of the city. All records of purchase shall be kept for at least three years from the date of such purchase or sale before being destroyed.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-93)
Sec. 4-107. - Distilled spirits excise tax.
(a)
In addition to the annual liquor license fee, there is hereby levied an excise tax computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax on all fractional part of a liter which shall be paid to the mayor and council on all distilled spirits displayed and stored in the city. The tax shall be collected by the wholesale distributor and paid to the mayor and council by the wholesale distributor on all distilled spirits sold to retailers in the city as follows: Each wholesale distributor selling, shipping, or in any way delivering distilled spirits to any retailer shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery and shall remit the tax, together with a summary of all deliveries to each retailer, on or before the tenth day of the following month.
(b)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any retailer to possess, own, hold, store, display or sell any distilled spirits on which the tax has not been paid.
(c)
Each wholesale distributor shall furnish to the mayor and council a summary of all purchase invoices for distilled spirits to each retailer in the city on or before the 15th day of each month following such purchases. The invoice shall show the amount of excise tax paid.
(Res. of 3-3-2015, § 6-94)
