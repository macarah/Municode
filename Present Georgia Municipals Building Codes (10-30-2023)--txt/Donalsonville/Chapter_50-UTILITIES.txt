ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Destruction or damage to utility apparatus; diversion of services.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person intentionally and without authority to destroy, damage or tamper with any meter, pipe, conduit, wire, line, post, lamp or other apparatus owned by the city or by any company engaged in the manufacture, distribution, or sale of electricity, gas, water, telephone, cable television or other public service.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person intentionally and without authority to prevent a meter from properly registering the quantity of such service supplied, or in any way to interfere with the proper action of the city, or of a company engaged in the manufacture, distribution, or sale of electricity, gas, water, telephone, cable television or other public service, or intentionally to divert any of the services of the city, or such company, or otherwise intentionally and without authority to use or cause to be used, without the consent of the city or such company, any service manufactured, sold or distributed by the city or such company.
(c)
No person shall knowingly receive the benefit of acts of diversion of, or tampering with, electricity, gas, water, telephone, cable television or other public service without the proper charge.
(d)
Proof that any of the acts specified in this section was done on premises in possession of an accused, or that the accused received the benefit of any such act, shall be prima facie evidence that the accused committed such act or aided and abetted in the commission of such act.
(Code 1984, § 6-1-1; Code 1996, § 90-1)
Sec. 50-2. - Mandatory use of city's water system.
No water service, aside from that provided through the city, may be constructed, reconstructed, maintained or otherwise established within the city so long as connection to the city water system is available.
(Ord. No. 05-07-19, 6-4-2019)
Sec. 50-3. - Water and sewer rates and charges.
Water and sewer rates and charges shall be as established by the mayor and council. Failure to pay such rates and charges shall constitute a lien upon the property to which water and sewer service is rendered by the city.
(Code 1984, § 6-1-2; Code 1996, § 90-2)
Sec. 50-4. - Resale of water.
The resale of water purchased from the city is expressly prohibited.
(Code 1984, § 6-1-3; Code 1996, § 90-3)
Secs. 50-5—50-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - WATER CONSERVATION

Sec. 50-25. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable, or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling, but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(Code 1996, § 90-31; Ord. of 1-8-1991, § 1)
Sec. 50-26. - Enforcement of article.
This article shall be enforced by the office of the city building official. Citations for violations may be issued by the city building official.
(Code 1996, § 90-32; Ord. of 1-8-1991, § 6(a))
Sec. 50-27. - Prohibited plumbing fixtures.
No construction may be initiated within the city for any residential building of any type which employs a:
(1)
Gravity tank-type, flushometer valve, or flushometer tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1⅗ gallons of water per flush;
(2)
Showerhead that allows a flow of more than an average of 2½ gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
Urinal that uses more than an average of one gallon of water per flush;
(4)
Lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than two gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2½ gallons of water per minute.
(Code 1996, § 90-34; Ord. of 1-8-1991, § 1)
Sec. 50-28. - Application of section 50-27 to residential and commercial construction.
The requirements of section 50-27 shall apply to any residential construction and to any commercial construction which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement of toilets or showers, or both.
(Code 1996, § 90-36; Ord. of 1-8-1991, § 4)
Sec. 50-29. - Exemptions.
(a)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of this article when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing buildings;
(2)
Such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design or installation, would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this article were installed;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(b)
The owner or his agent of a building undergoing new construction, repair or renovation who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (a)(2), (a)(3) or (a)(4) of this section shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the city building official. A fee, as established by the mayor and council, shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
(Code 1996, § 90-37; Ord. of 1-8-1991, § 5)
Sec. 50-30. - Restriction on outdoor watering of landscape.
Outdoor watering for purposes of planting, growing, managing, or maintaining ground cover, trees, shrubs, or other plants may occur only between the hours of 4:00 p.m. and 10:00 a.m.; provided, however, that this limitation shall not create any limitation upon the following outdoor water uses:
(1)
Commercial raising, harvesting, or storing of crops; feeding, breeding, or managing livestock or poultry; commercial production or storing of feed for use in the production of livestock, including, but not limited to, cattle, calves, swine, hogs, goats, sheep, and rabbits, or for use in the production of poultry, including, but not limited to, chickens, hens, ratites, and turkeys; producing plants, trees, fowl, or animals; or commercial production of aquacultural, horticultural, dairy, livestock, poultry, eggs, and apiarian products or as otherwise defined in O.C.G.A. § 1-3-3;
(2)
Capture and reuse of cooling system condensate or stormwater in compliance with applicable city ordinances and state guidelines;
(3)
Reuse of gray water in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 31-3-5.2 and applicable local board of health regulations;
(4)
Use of reclaimed waste water by a designated user from a system permitted by the environmental protection division of the state department of natural resources to provide reclaimed waste water;
(5)
Watering personal food gardens;
(6)
Watering new and replanted plant, seed, or turf in landscapes, golf courses, or sports turf fields during installation and for a period of 30 days immediately following the date of installation;
(7)
Drip irrigation or irrigation using soaker hoses;
(8)
Hand watering with a hose with automatic cutoff or handheld container;
(9)
Use of water withdrawn from private water wells or surface water by an owner or operator of property if such well or surface water is on said property;
(10)
Watering horticultural crops held for sale, resale, or installation;
(11)
Watering athletic fields, golf courses, or public turf grass recreational areas;
(12)
Installation, maintenance, or calibration of irrigation systems; or
(13)
Hydroseeding.
(Ord. of 11-2-2010, § 90-38)
Sec. 50-31. - Enforcement.
The chief of police shall be the enforcement authority for this article. The county/city manager may also authorize other departments as may be deemed necessary to support enforcement.
(Ord. of 11-2-2010, § 90-39)
Secs. 50-32—50-50. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SEWER USE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 50-51. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Biochemical oxygen demand (BOD) means the calculated pounds of oxygen required to satisfy the five-day oxygen demand of 1,000,000 pounds of waste when tested in accordance with the procedure given in the latest edition of Standard Methods, published by the American Public Health Association.
Building sewer means that part of the horizontal piping of a building drainage system extending from the outside of the building or end of the inside plumbing system to, and including, the connection with the city sewer system.
City sewer system means such system composed of sanitary sewers, storm sewers, combined sewers, and sewage treatment works owned by a city.
Combined sewer means a sewer intended to receive domestic sewage, treated or weak industrial wastes, surface water and stormwater.
Domestic sewage means liquid from residences, business buildings or institutions which has a daily average of not more than 400 parts per million of BOD and a like amount of suspended solids.
Industrial wastes means the liquid wastes resulting from processes employed in industrial establishments. An industrial waste will be considered weak when it contains less than 600 parts per million of BOD and suspended solids with no biologically toxic substances in it.
pH means a measure of acidity or alkalinity which has a range of zero to 14.
Sanitary sewer means a sewer intended to receive domestic sewage and treated or weak industrial waste without the admixture of surface water, stormwater, or unpolluted industrial process water.
Storm sewer means a sewer intended to receive stormwater and surface water, street wash, or drainage and sewage and industrial wastes that have been completely purified.
Suspended solids means the amount of material in suspension expressed in parts per million as determined by the Gooch crucible method given in the latest edition of Standard Methods, published by the American Public Health Association.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-1; Code 1996, § 90-66)
Sec. 50-52. - Notice of violation.
Whenever it is found that any person is violating any of the provisions or requirements set out in this article, a written notice, stating the offense and setting a time limit for the correction, shall be served upon the offender by the chief of police. The offender shall, within the time limit stated in the notice served upon him, forever cease all violations.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-9(a); Code 1996, § 90-67)
Sec. 50-53. - Unsanitary deposits.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or lessee to place, deposit, or permit to be deposited, any human excrement, garbage or any other organic waste upon the premises in an unsanitary manner.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-2; Code 1996, § 90-69)
Sec. 50-54. - Discharge of polluted waters to streams, ditches or other underground water source.
It shall be unlawful to discharge any untreated domestic sewage, industrial wastes or other polluted waters into any streams or ditches or any abandoned water well, or in any way to discharge sewage or industrial waste directly into underground waters within the area controlled by the city.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-3; Code 1996, § 90-70)
Sec. 50-55. - Connection required where property within 200 feet of city sewer system.
It shall be unlawful to construct or maintain any privy, privy vault, cesspool or septic tank intended for, or subject to, receiving organic wastes wherever a connection can be made to the city sewer system within 200 feet of any property.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-4; Code 1996, § 90-71)
Sec. 50-56. - Connection to be at owner's expense; time within which to connect.
The owner of all houses, buildings or properties situated within the city, abutting on any streets, alleys or parkways, in which there is now located or may hereafter be located any sewer pipeline of the sanitary sewer system of the city, is hereby required, at his own cost and expense, to install suitable toilet facilities and to connect those houses or buildings erected upon the property directly with the sanitary sewer system within three months after notice to do so.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-5; Code 1996, § 90-72)
Sec. 50-57. - Connection of properties beyond city limits.
Any person owning or controlling premises located beyond the corporate limits of the city and desiring to install a plumbing system on the premises and connect the system with the sanitary sewers of the city may do so by complying with the requirements of this article and by paying a monthly sewer rental charge.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-6; Code 1996, § 90-73)
Sec. 50-58. - Method of disposal where sewer unavailable or connection cannot be made.
Where city sewers are not available, or where a connection, as determined by the mayor and council, cannot be made, a septic tank or another method of private sewage disposal must be constructed according to the requirements of the state department of natural resources.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-7; Code 1996, § 90-74)
Sec. 50-59. - City's right of entry.
Authority is hereby granted to the duly authorized city employees to enter upon all properties to make all necessary inspections, tests, and measurements to ensure complete compliance with all of the regulations of this article.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-8; Code 1996, § 90-75)
Secs. 50-60—50-76. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - BUILDING SEWERS

Sec. 50-77. - Separate sewers required; exception.
A separate building sewer is required for each individual building or house, whether constructed as a detached unit or as one or a pair or row, but a single building sewer will be permitted to serve an apartment house or other multiple unit structure or industrial plant whose individual apartments or units are not subject to separate ownership.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-21; Code 1996, § 90-91)
Sec. 50-78. - Connection to be at owner's expense; permit to connect required.
Building sewers from the point of entry of the city sewer to the building shall be laid by the owner at his expense, but only after such connections have been authorized by a permit issued by the superintendent of utilities upon the submission of evidence by the owner that the discharge from the building sewer will comply with all of the regulations of this article.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-22; Code 1996, § 90-92)
Sec. 50-79. - Use of old building sewers and drains.
Old building sewers and drains may be used in connection with new buildings or new plumbing only when they are approved through issue of a permit as for new construction of building sewers.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-23; Code 1996, § 90-93)
Sec. 50-80. - Diameter and fall.
All building sewers shall be not less than six inches in diameter and shall have a fall of not less than one-half inch per foot for six-inch pipe and not less than one-quarter inch per foot for pipe eight inches or over in diameter.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-24; Code 1996, § 90-94)
Sec. 50-81. - Laying of pipes.
All pipe shall be laid on a uniform grade and, where possible, on a straight line. Where the building sewer cannot be laid on a straight line, curved pipe shall be used for every deflection from a straight line of more than three inches per foot.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-25; Code 1996, § 90-95)
Sec. 50-82. - Recommendation where sewer near large tree trunks.
Where building sewers are within 20 feet of large tree trunks, it is recommended that cast iron pipe with leaded joints be used. All joints must be filled, made smooth and absolutely watertight.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-26; Code 1996, § 90-96)
Sec. 50-83. - Manner of connecting building sewer to city sewer.
The connection between the building sewer and the city sewer shall be made at the Y branch provided. If no Y branch was left in the city sewer for the building sewer and the city sewer is not over 12 inches in diameter, a Y branch shall be installed in the city sewer at the desired location by the owner of the building sewer. Where the city sewer is over 12 inches in diameter and where no Y branch was left for the building sewer, a hole may be cut in the city sewer large enough to receive the end of the building sewer and the connection made so that the building sewer enters the city sewer at an angle of 45 degrees. A 45-degree ell can be used to make this connection if the spigot end is cut to fit so that it does not extend past the inside surface of the city sewer. City sewers shall not be tapped below the centerline of the city sewer.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-27; Code 1996, § 90-97)
Sec. 50-84. - Backfilling.
In backfilling, the material shall be carefully placed and packed around the pipe to provide a uniform bearing and to keep the pipe in proper position. No stones, bricks or like material shall be used in the backfill until there has been a depth of at least 18 inches of fine earth or gravel placed over the pipe.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-28; Code 1996, § 90-98)
Sec. 50-85. - Maintenance of barricades and lights.
Proper barricades and lights must be maintained on the banks of the trenches to guard the public against accidents during construction or connection of the building sewer.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-29; Code 1996, § 90-99)
Secs. 50-86—50-113. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DISCHARGE OF DELETERIOUS WASTES

Sec. 50-114. - Prohibited discharges to sanitary sewers.
No stormwater from pavements, areaways, roofs or other sources shall be admitted to the sanitary sewers which are designed to be used exclusively as carriers of domestic sewage and suitable industrial wastes. The same shall apply to unpolluted process or cooling waters.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-41; Code 1996, § 90-116)
Sec. 50-115. - Discharge of stormwater.
Stormwater shall be admitted only to such sewers as are specifically designed as combined sewers or storm sewers.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-42; Code 1996, § 90-117)
Sec. 50-116. - Discharge of liquids and gases above certain temperatures.
No person shall discharge into any sewer any liquid or gas having a temperature greater than 100 degrees Fahrenheit.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-43; Code 1996, § 90-118)
Sec. 50-117. - Prohibited discharges to public sewers.
No person shall discharge or deposit any of the following described waste materials into any sanitary, storm or combined sewer:
(1)
Liquid waste materials which contain more than 100 parts of oil or grease per million parts, determined by weight;
(2)
Liquid waste material in which the suspended solids exceed 600 parts per million, determined by weight;
(3)
Any petroleum product or other product which, by reason of its nature or quality, may cause a fire or explosion, or in any way be injurious to persons or to the sewer system;
(4)
Any industrial waste with a pH of less than 5.5 or greater than 9.0;
(5)
Any industrial waste with a five-day biochemical oxygen demand of more than 600 parts per million, by weight;
(6)
Any industrial waste containing any toxic or poisonous substance in a sufficient quantity to interfere with the biologic processes used in the sewage treatment process or that will pass through the sewage treatment plant and harm persons or aquatic life in the receiving stream;
(7)
Any unshredded garbage, scraps, or vegetables; or
(8)
Any straw, ashes, cinders, shavings, butchers' offal, rags, feathers or any other material capable of causing obstructions, nuisance, or injury to or interference with the sewer system.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-44; Code 1996, § 90-119)
Sec. 50-118. - Sampling manholes.
(a)
Required in certain instances. Every building sewer discharging industrial wastes from any plant or establishment into the city sewer system may be required, at the discretion of the approving authorities, to have a sampling manhole for the purpose of ascertaining the quality and quantity of the waste flowing in the building sewer.
(b)
Location; construction. The manhole shall be located at a suitable and satisfactory location and it shall be not less than three feet in inside diameter and built in a manner designated by the superintendent of utilities.
(c)
Cost. The cost of the manhole shall be borne by the applicant, but the manhole shall become the property of the city government.
(d)
Maintenance. The responsibility for maintaining the manhole in a safe condition shall be assumed and remain with the owner of the property at all times.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-45; Code 1996, § 90-120)
Sec. 50-119. - Pretreatment of wastes.
Whenever the industrial wastes do not meet the requirements of this article, or the quantity of biochemical oxygen demand is one-tenth of the total of the entire wastes of the city, the industry will be required to pretreat its waste or enter into a one-year agreement with the city to treat the waste as discharged.
(Code 1984, § 6-2-46; Code 1996, § 90-121)

ARTICLE IV. - UNDERGROUND UTILITY INSTALLATIONS

Sec. 50-137. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Damage means the substantial weakening of structural or lateral support of an underground utility, penetration or destruction of any protective coating, housing or other protective device of an underground utility, and the partial or complete severance of an underground utility.
Demolish or demolition means any operation by which a structure or mass of material is wrecked, razed, rendered, moved or removed by means of any tool, equipment or discharge of explosives.
Excavate or excavation means an operation for the purpose of the movement or removal of earth, rock or other materials in or on the ground by use of mechanized equipment or by discharge of explosives, and including augering, backfilling, digging, drilling, grading, plowing-in pulling-in, ripping, scraping, trenching and tunneling, but not including the tilling of soil for agricultural purposes.
Mechanized equipment means equipment operated by means of mechanical power, including trenchers, bulldozers, power shovels, augers, backhoes, scrapers, drills, cable and pipe plows and other equipment used for plowing in or pulling in cable or pipe.
Operator means any city or utility commission who owns or operates a utility.
Utility means any line, system or facility used for producing, storing, conveying, transmitting or distributing communication, electricity, gas, petroleum, petroleum products, hazardous liquids, water, steam or sewage.
Working day means every day, except Saturdays, Sundays and national and legal state holidays.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-1; Code 1996, § 90-141)
Sec. 50-138. - Excavation and demolition permits; effect.
A permit issued pursuant to the law authorizing excavation or demolition operations shall not be deemed to relieve a person from the responsibility for complying with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-2; Code 1996, § 90-143)
Sec. 50-139. - Prohibited acts.
Except as provided in section 50-142, no person may excavate in a street, highway, public space, or a private easement of an operator, or near the location of a utility installed on the premises of a customer served by such utility, or demolish a building, without having first ascertained, in the manner prescribed in sections 50-140 and 50-141, the location of all underground utilities in the area that would be affected by the proposed excavation or demolition. Each operator shall maintain a list of all underground utilities and plants or maps showing their location.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-3; Code 1996, § 90-144)
Sec. 50-140. - Notice of intent.
(a)
Except as provided in section 50-142, before commencing any excavation or demolition operation designated in section 50-139, each person responsible for such excavation or demolition shall serve written or telephone notice of intent to excavate or demolish at least three but not more than ten full working days to the state utility protection center prior to any such excavation or demolition.
(b)
The written or telephonic notice required by subsection (a) of this section must contain the name, address and telephone number of the person filing the notice of intent, and, if different, the person responsible for the excavation or demolition, the starting date, anticipated duration and type of excavation or demolition operation to be conducted, the location of the proposed excavation or demolition, and whether or not explosives are to be used.
(c)
If notification required by this section is made by telephone, an adequate record of such notification shall be maintained by the superintendent at the city hall.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-4; Code 1996, § 90-145)
Sec. 50-141. - Response to notice of intent.
Each operator or designated representative notified in accordance with section 50-140 shall, not less than two working days in advance of the proposed excavation or demolition, unless a shorter period is provided by agreement between the person responsible for the excavation or demolition and the operator or designated representative, supply, by use of maps when appropriate, the following information to the person responsible for the excavation or demolition:
(1)
The approximate location and description of all of its underground utilities which may be damaged as a result of the excavation or demolition;
(2)
The location and description of all utility markers indicating the approximate location of the underground utilities; and
(3)
Any other information that would assist that person in locating and thereby avoiding damage to the underground utilities, including providing adequate temporary marking indicating the approximate location of the underground utility in locations where permanent utility markers do not exist.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-5; Code 1996, § 90-146)
Sec. 50-142. - Emergency.
Compliance with the notice requirements of section 50-140 is not required of persons responsible for emergency excavation or demolition to ameliorate an imminent danger to life, health or property; provided, however, that such persons give, as soon as practicable, oral notice of the emergency excavation or demolition to each operator having underground utilities located in the area where such excavation or demolition is to be performed and requests emergency assistance from each operator so identified in locating and providing immediate protection to its underground utilities. An imminent danger to life, health, or property exists whenever there is a substantial likelihood that loss of life, health, or property will result before the procedures under sections 50-140 and 50-141 can be fully complied with.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-6; Code 1996, § 90-147)
Sec. 50-143. - Damage.
(a)
Except as provided by subsection (b) of this section, each person responsible for any excavation or demolition operation designated in section 50-139 that results in any damage to an underground utility shall, immediately upon discovery of such damage, notify the operator of such utility of the location and nature of the damage and shall allow the operator reasonable time to accomplish necessary repairs before completing the excavation or demolition in the immediate area of such utility.
(b)
Each person responsible for any excavation or demolition operation designated in section 50-139 that results in damage to an underground utility permitting the escape of any flammable, toxic or corrosive gas or liquid shall, immediately upon discovery of such damage, notify the operator, police and fire departments, and take any other action as may be reasonably necessary to protect persons and property and to minimize the hazards until the arrival of the operator's personnel or police and fire departments.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-8; Code 1996, § 90-148)
Sec. 50-144. - Precautions to avoid damage.
In addition to the notification requirements of section 50-140, each person responsible for any excavation or demolition operation designated in section 50-139 shall:
(1)
Plan the excavation or demolition to avoid damage to or minimize interference with underground utilities in and near the construction area;
(2)
Maintain a clearance between an underground utility and the cutting edge or point of any mechanized equipment, taking into account the known limit of control of such cutting edge or point, as may be reasonably necessary to avoid damage to such utility; and
(3)
Provide such support for underground utilities in and near the construction area, including during backfill operations, as may be reasonably necessary for the protection of such utilities.
(Code 1984, § 6-3-7; Code 1996, § 90-149)
Secs. 50-145—50-171. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - CATV FRANCHISE

Sec. 50-172. - Fee per Consumer Choice for Television Act.
The city shall collect a fee of five percent of the operator's gross revenues as provided by law, for any cable or video state franchise issued in the corporate boundaries by the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-76-1 et seq., known as the "Consumer Choice for Television Act" of 2007.
(Res. of 7-1-2008)
