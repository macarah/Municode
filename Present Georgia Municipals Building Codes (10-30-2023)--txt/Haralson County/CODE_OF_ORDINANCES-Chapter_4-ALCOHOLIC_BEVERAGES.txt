ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, malt beverage, malt beverage, wine, or fortified wine as defined in this section.
Applicant means any one or more persons applying for a county license or renewal thereof.
Application means those forms required by the county to be completed by persons applying for an original license or a renewal license under this chapter.
Beer means "malt beverage" as that term is defined herein.
Church means any place of permanent public religious worship and shall exist if a building permit therefore has been obtained and construction of the church building has commenced or substantial materials or supplies for its construction have been moved to the site where it is to be built.
Convicted includes an adjudication of guilt on a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Employed on the licensed premises and work on any licensed premises mean work done or services performed on the physical location of the licensed premises as well as work done or services performed while in the scope of employment elsewhere than on the licensed premises.
License means the formal approval granted or issued by the county to a licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages within the county.
Licensee means the individual or business entity to which the license is issued.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion of decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product or any combination of the products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager, malt beverage, small malt beverage, and strong malt beverage. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Minor means any person under the age of 21 years.
Parcel of land means any quantity of land capable of being described by location and boundary, designated and used or to be used as a unit.
Premises means, not only the structure wherein any premises for sale of alcoholic beverages is operated under a county license, but includes all of the lot whereon the premises is located, except where the premises is located in a hotel or shopping center.
Prepared food means any food item or meal that is assembled, created, or cooked by a chef or cooks in a full service kitchen on the premises and served to the customer who customarily eats it with eating utensils. Pre-packaged food items (hot or cold) are not recognized as prepared meals because they are not made or assembled on premises.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised, and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly prepared and served, without sleeping accommodations; the place seating a minimum of 50 people and holding a certificate of approval from the county health department. Stools for serving at a counter or bar shall not be considered as seating facilities as contemplated herein.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by fermentation or natural fermentation with brandy added. Wine includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of the beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
State Law reference— Similar definitions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.

ARTICLE II. - LICENSES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-21. - Privilege; revocation.
The sale of alcoholic beverages with the county is a mere privilege and not a right, and the county board of commissioners reserves the right to limit the total number of license issues and to revoke the license of any wholesale or retail dealer at any time, with or without cause, with or without notice, and notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1.
Sec. 4-22. - License classification.
The county issues, subject to the limitations and regulations of, and upon the conditions set forth in this article, the following alcoholic beverage licenses:
(1)
Class A licenses, which authorize the retail sale of malt beverages only for consumption off the premises;
(2)
Class B licenses, which authorize the retail sale of wine only for consumption off the premises;
(3)
Class C licenses, which authorize the retail package sales of distilled liquor only for consumption off the premises;
(4)
Class D licenses, which authorize the retail sale of beer for consumption on the premises;
(5)
Class E licenses, which authorize the retail sale of wine for consumption on the premises;
(6)
Class F licenses, which authorize the retail sale of distilled liquor by the drink for consumption on the premises;
(7)
Class T licenses, which authorize the temporary sale of malt beverages, wine, distilled liquor, or any combination thereof, as specified in the permit; and
(8)
Class W licenses, which authorize the wholesale sale of alcoholic beverages.
Sec. 4-23. - Limitations on total number of licenses issued.
The total number of licenses within each class that may be operative simultaneously may be limited by the board of commissioners. Should there be a sufficient number of current licenses to meet the needs and desires of the inhabitants of the county, no new licenses shall be issues. In determining the needs and desires of the inhabitants, the county commissioners shall consider the best interests of the county's citizens, based on factors affecting the local peace, health, and welfare.

Wholesale licenses shall be issued only to those parties who are licensed by the state to sell and distribute alcoholic beverages at wholesale. Application shall be made with the county clerk.

All applications for licenses shall be presented to the board of county commissioners for approval and, if approved, the licenses shall be issued by the county clerk upon the payment of the required fee.

Sec. 4-53. - Licensee qualifications.
(a)
An applicant for licensure under this article must be a person of good moral character, a citizen of the United States, and a resident of the county for a period of one year next preceding the date of filing application. This shall not apply to the honorably discharged members of the federal armed forces who have returned to the county within the prescribed limit.
(b)
The licensee shall not have been convicted within two years of the date of his application of any felony or any violation of the laws of this state, or any other state, relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages, or convicted in any federal or state court of a felony, misdemeanor, or other offense involving moral turpitude.
(c)
The licensee shall be responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(d)
The applicant must be at least 21 years of age.
(e)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement of his qualifications according to subsections (a) through (d) of this section and shall place same on file with the county clerk before any license is issued.
(f)
If the application covers a partnership, all members of the partnership must be qualified to obtain a license and must make sworn statements of these qualifications.
(g)
No person shall be granted a license to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages at retail unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the board of county commissioners that such person shall meet the qualifications of this section.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § III; Res. of 5-3-1988, § II)
State Law reference— Discretion and due process regarding grant or suspension of local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 4-54. - Applications for licenses.
(a)
An applicant for license to sell alcoholic beverages shall file a written application with the county clerk. The application shall contain such information as may be required by the board of commissioners in order to enforce the provisions of this article.
(b)
The applicant shall publish the application in a local newspaper once a week for four consecutive weeks to coincide with the sheriff's investigation as specified in subsection (c) of this section.
(c)
Before a license is issued, the application shall be referred by the clerk to the county sheriff. All applicants must be photographed, fingerprinted, and registered with the sheriff's department. The sheriff shall make an investigation and report back to the clerk within 30 days the following information:
(1)
Whether the person has ever been convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude or ever been convicted of violating malt beverage, wine, or liquor laws of any city, county, state, or federal government.
(2)
Whether the applicant has ever had an alcoholic beverage license revoked for cause.
Sec. 4-55. - Additional requirements and restrictions for licenses for consumption on the premises.
(a)
In addition to all other requirements for licensure as set forth in this article, applicants for licensure for sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises must have, prior to license issuance, a current county license and health department certificate for a restaurant as defined in section 4-1.
(b)
Additionally, the sale of malt beverage to be consumed on the premises shall be ancillary to the primary business of serving prepared foods.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § IV; Res. of 5-3-1988, § III)
Sec. 4-56. - Nontransferability.
Licenses issued pursuant to this chapter are not transferable without the consent of the county board of commissioners.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § VIII; Res. of 5-3-1988, § VII)
Sec. 4-57. - Business license, regulatory, and investigative fees.
(a)
License fees. The annual license fees for all licenses issued pursuant to this chapter shall be as established from time to time by the board of commissioners. Renewal applications and license fees, which shall be the same as fees for original issuance, shall be due no later than December 1 of each year.
(b)
Regulatory fee. In addition, each licensee under this chapter shall pay the regulatory fee as required in chapter 12, article II pertaining to business regulatory fees.
(c)
Investigative fees. In addition to license fees, every applicant for a license under this chapter shall pay, at the time of making application for original issuance or renewal of the annual license, a nonrefundable investigative fee in the amount established by the board of commissioners, to defray the cost to the county of inspections, fingerprinting, and criminal background investigations.
State Law reference— Limitations on local alcohol licensing fees, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-43.
Sec. 4-58. - Fingerprinting.
(a)
As a prerequisite to the issuance of any license, the applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints for all persons required to sign the application to be forwarded to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation and to the Federal Bureau of Investigation, as specified under state law.
(b)
Each person required to sign the application for an original license or renewal license authorizes the county or its designated representatives to secure from any state, county, municipal, or federal court, or any sheriff's department or law enforcement agency, the applicant's criminal history and civil history and further authorizes the county, and its officers and employees, to use the information in determining whether an alcoholic beverage license will be issued to the applicant.
(c)
The applicant, by submitting the application, authorizes the county, and its officers and employees, to use the information in a public hearing if necessary, to determine whether the applicant's license should be issued, denied, voided, cancelled, or revoked.
(d)
Each applicant waives any rights the applicant may have under state or federal law or statute or court ruling to preclude the county from securing the criminal or civil history from any source and waives any right the applicant may have to preclude the county from using the information publicly in determining whether the license will be issued to the applicant.
Sec. 4-59. - Report of county sheriff or his designee; appeal.
(a)
Once an application, the accompanying documents, and the required investigative and license fees are filed with the county clerk, a copy of the application and all accompanying documents shall be referred to the county sheriff or his designee for purposes of the investigation required pursuant to this article.
(b)
The county sheriff or his designee shall make a written report regarding the qualifications of the applicant to the board of commissioners.
(c)
If the report of the county sheriff or his designee is favorable and the board of commissioners finds that the applicant meets all other qualifications provided in this article, then the board of commissioners shall schedule the application for a hearing at the next board of commissioners meeting and shall so inform the applicant before the meeting.
(d)
The board of commissioners or its designee shall instruct the applicant, with the exception of applicants for wholesale permits, to publish, prior to the meeting, notice in the form herein provided in the official county newspaper of the county, at least once each week for four consecutive weeks, the applicant's intent to secure a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package or by the drink, the exact location of the place of business for which a license is sought, the names and addresses of each owner of the business, and, if the applicant is a corporation, the names and titles of all corporate officers.
(e)
If the report of the county sheriff or his designee is unfavorable, or if the board of commissioners finds that the applicant fails to meet all of the qualifications provided in this article, then the board of commissioners shall inform the applicant, in writing, that his application has been denied. The notice shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for the denial and shall notify the applicant of his right to appeal as provided in division 3 of this article.
Sec. 4-60. - Review of application by board of commissioners.
(a)
Any application that the board of commissioners determines satisfies all the qualifications outlined in this article, including absence of prohibited criminal activity as disclosed in the report of the county sheriff or his designee, shall be scheduled for review at the next meeting of the board of commissioners.
(b)
At that meeting, the applicant and any person opposed to the application has the right to present to the board of commissioners any information that the board of commissioners determines is relevant to the licensing decision.
(c)
In making its determination with regard to approval or denial of the application, the board of commissioners shall look to the public interest and welfare and shall have the sole discretion to grant or deny the application based on the information presented.
(d)
A decision by the board of commissioners shall be made within 30 days from the date of the board of commissioners meeting, and notice of that decision shall be mailed to the applicant within ten days thereafter. In the event the application is denied, the written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the reason for the denial and shall notify the applicant of his right to appeal; the appeal shall be in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
Sec. 4-61. - Issuance of license.
Upon approval by the board of commissioners of the application for a license, the county clerk shall issue a license in the following name, as appropriate according to the approved application:
(1)
When the applicant is applying for himself and a corporation or club, the license shall be issued in the name of the approved applicant and the corporation or club.
(2)
If the applicant is a partnership, the license shall be issued in the name of one or more partners in the partnership.
(3)
If the applicant is an individual, the license shall be issued in the name of the individual.
Sec. 4-62. - Display of license; separate license required for each licensed premises.
Each licensee of the county shall display the license issued hereunder prominently at all times at the premises for which the license is issued. A separate license must be issued for each licensed premises.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-3.

DIVISION 3. - DENIAL OF ISSUANCE OR RENEWAL, SUSPENSION, AND REVOCATION

Sec. 4-83. - Reapplication after denial.
In all instances in which an application is denied under the provisions of this article, the applicant may not reapply for the same type of license for at least one year from the final date of the denial.
Sec. 4-84. - Grounds for denial, suspension, or revocation of license.
The following shall be considered just cause for denial to issue, suspension, or revocation of a license hereunder:
(1)
Performance of any act prohibited by this chapter or the failure to perform any act required by this chapter, as well as the violation of any law, state or federal, relating to the business of the licensee. If such act, omission, or violation is done by an agent, servant, employee, or officer of the licensee, lack of knowledge on the part of the licensee or the lack of authorization for such act, omission, or violation shall be no defense;
(2)
Any misstatement or concealment of fact in the application that shall make the applicant liable to prosecution for perjury or false swearing under the law;
(3)
Conviction of or entry of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere by any applicant, licensee, owner, or partner, or any officer, director, or stockholder holding the controlling interest of a licensee or applicant corporation with respect to a charge of violation of any of the federal or state laws relating to the business of the applicant or licensee, or any crime, felony, or misdemeanor involving moral turpitude;
(4)
Occurrence on two or more occasions within any 12-month period any instance of fighting, disorderly conduct, drunkenness, breach of the peace, and other similar conduct on the licensee's premises, whether the same was committed by the licensee, employees, agents, customers, or others;
(5)
Any other act or omission with respect to the operation of a business licensed or proposed license hereunder that the board of commissioners finds to be contrary to the public interest or that renders the applicant, the licensee, or the business location unsuitable for the continued operation of the business.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § II; Res. of 5-3-1988, § I; Ord. of 7-28-1993)
State Law reference— Discretion and due process regarding local licensure, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 4-85. - Procedure for reconsideration of refusal to grant or renew license.
(a)
In the event the board of commissioners should refuse to grant any license or to renew any license previously granted, the applicant shall have the right to request the board of commissioners to advise in writing the reason for refusal.
(b)
The applicant shall further have the right, within ten days of receipt of the written reasons for refusal, to request a hearing for reconsideration before the board of commissioners and shall be entitled to present evidence in his behalf as to why the license should be issued. Evidence shall also be admissible to show why the license should not be issued.
(c)
After the hearing, the board of commissioners shall make a finding as to whether the license or license renewal should be granted or denied and, if denied, shall state the reason for denial.
Sec. 4-86. - Procedure for license suspension or revocation.
(a)
In the event that any license is temporarily suspended by any authorized officer of the county, including the sheriff, the license shall be turned over to the board of commissioners' office immediately, and the licensee shall appear before the board of commissioners at a regular meeting for consideration of continued suspension, permanent revocation, or reinstatement.
(b)
Prior to the suspension or revocation of any license, a hearing shall be held before the board of commissioners. The licensee shall be given ten days written notice of the hearing. The notice shall contain the date, place, and time of the hearing and the alleged reasons being considered for the suspension or revocation. The licensee shall have the right at the hearing, individually or through a representative, to examine and cross examine witnesses and to introduce oral and documentary evidence.
(c)
The board of commissioners shall determine whether a license shall be suspended or revoked and, if suspended, for what period of time, after consideration of the evidence in the cause and in accordance with the findings as to the severity of the offense, if any, found to have been committed.
Sec. 4-87. - Rehearing.
(a)
Any person aggrieved by the act of the county with respect to licensure suspension or revocation shall have the right to move for a rehearing. Motion for a rehearing shall be filed within five days after the act or omission complained of, shall be in writing, and shall outline the manner in which such aggrieved party believes that the county erred.
(b)
If the motion relates to a matter as to which evidence has already been heard, no additional evidence or argument shall be permitted in the motion for rehearing except after a grant of permission therefor by the board of commissioners.
(c)
If the subject matter that forms the basis of the complaint of the aggrieved party is not the subject of a prior hearing at which the aggrieved party was permitted to be present and heard, the board of commissioners shall afford a hearing within ten days after the filing of the motion, of which reasonable notice shall be given to all parties known to the board of commissioners to be interested and at which persons interested in protesting or supporting the act or omission of the county shall be allowed to appear, be represented, introduce oral and documentary evidence, and examine and cross examine witnesses.

ARTICLE III. - EMPLOYEE PERMITS

Sec. 4-118. - Employment permit required; application; fingerprints.
Before any person may work on a licensed premises, he shall file a notice with the licensing officer of his intended employment and an application for a permit therefor on forms supplied by the licensing officer and shall, upon approval, receive a permit evidencing approval of such employment from the licensing officer. The prospective employee shall supply such information as the licensing officer requires, including a set of fingerprints, on regular county or federal forms. Upon approval, the employee may begin working on the licensed premises.
(Ord. of 6-22-1993, § 1(10))
Sec. 4-119. - Permit period.
Employee permits shall expire annually on November 30, and application for renewal thereof, together with fees as provided herein, is due on December 1 of each year.
Sec. 4-120. - Nonrefundable investigation fee; permit fee.
(a)
Investigation fee. A nonrefundable investigation fee shall accompany the notice of intended employment and application for employment permit. The investigation fee shall be in the amount established from time to time by the board of commissioners as shown in the county's fee schedule.
(b)
Permit fee. In addition to the investigation fee, each application for original issuance and renewal of an employee permit shall be accompanied by an annual fee in the amount established by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 6-22-1993, § 1(10))
Sec. 4-121. - Qualifications.
Employees of an adult entertainment establishment shall be not less than 18 years of age. Every employee must be of good moral character as defined in this chapter.
(Ord. of 6-22-1993, § 1(10))
Sec. 4-122. - Effect of criminal convictions.
Any employee who is convicted of a crime constituting a felony or a crime not a felony involving moral turpitude while employed as an adult entertainment establishment employee shall not thereafter be eligible for a permit hereunder or work on any licensed premises for a period of five years from the date of such conviction, unless a longer time is ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(Ord. of 6-22-1993, § 1(10))
Sec. 4-123. - Denial of employment permit.
If the application for permit for employment is denied, the prospective employee may, within ten days of said denial, apply to the licensing officer for a hearing. The decision of the licensing officer after hearing may be appealed to the board of county commissioners or its designee who may issue such order as is proper in the premises.
(Ord. of 6-22-1993, § 1(10))
Sec. 4-124. - Suspension or revocation of approval of employment.
Violation of the provisions of this Code, other county ordinances, or county or state laws and regulations shall subject an employee to suspension or revocation of his employment under this article.
(Ord. of 6-22-1993, § 1(10))
Sec. 4-125. - Independent contractors considered employees.
For the purpose of this article, independent contractors shall be considered as employees and shall be subject to the permit requirements of this article as employees, regardless of the business relationship with the owner or licensee of any adult entertainment establishment.
(Ord. of 6-22-1993, § 1(10))

ARTICLE V. - REGULATIONS

Sec. 4-169. - Location restrictions.
(a)
No license shall be issued for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages nor for the on-premises consumption of alcoholic beverages in any location that is prohibited by state law.
(b)
In addition, no license shall be issued for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages nor for the sale for on-premises consumption of alcoholic beverages for any business that is located on a parcel of land, the property line of that is nearer than 400 feet to the property line of any school grounds or church grounds, unless a more stringent state law applies.
(c)
For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by airline measurement from property line to property line, using the closest property lines of the parcels of land involved.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § III; Res. of 5-3-1988, § II)
Sec. 4-170. - Alcoholic beverage sales and purchase records.
Every licensee shall keep and preserve records of all alcoholic beverages purchased and sold by the licensee. The records shall be kept for a period of three years from the date of purchase and sale and should at all times be open to audit or inspection by the board of commissioners, any designated county employee, any outside agent for the county, upon approval of the outside agent by the county.
State Law reference— Record of sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-6.

Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation, or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law;
(2)
The touching, caressing, or fondling of the breast, buttock, anus, or genitals; or
(3)
The displaying of the pubic hair, anus, vulva, or genitals.
(e)
The holding, promotion, or allowance of any contest, promotion, special night, or any other sexual activity is prohibited in any licensed premises.
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-40 et seq.
Sec. 4-172. - Soliciting purchases prohibited.
No person employed or working in any capacity at any licensed premises shall solicit or encourage patrons to purchase drinks to be consumed by or otherwise disposed of by the person so employed or working.
Sec. 4-173. - Adulteration of product.
No licensee shall add to the contents of a bottle or refill an empty bottle or in any other manner misrepresent the quantity, quality, or brand name of any alcoholic beverage.
State Law reference— Adulteration or misbranding of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 51-1-24.
Sec. 4-174. - Sanitary condition required for premises.
The licensee shall keep the premises clean, and in proper sanitary condition, and in full compliance with provisions and regulations governing the conditions of premises used for the storage and sale of food for human consumption and as provided by the rules and regulations of the county board of health.
Sec. 4-175. - Inspections.
Any officials that may be authorized by the board of commissioners including, but not by way of limitation, the county's sheriff and deputy sheriffs, other law enforcement officers, county code enforcement officers, and the county's fire chief or his designees, shall have the authority to inspect the premises and premises licensed under this chapter during the hours when the premises is open for business. These inspections shall be made for the purpose of verifying compliance with the requirements of this chapter and state law. This section is not intended to limit the authority of any other county, state, or federal officials to conduct inspections authorized by other provisions of law.
Sec. 4-176. - Responsibility of employer.
The licensee is responsible for the conduct or actions of his employees while in his employment. It shall be the duty of the licensee hereunder to maintain a copy of this chapter at the premises and to instruct each and every employee engaged in the sale or handling of alcoholic beverages of the terms hereof.
Sec. 4-177. - Sales contrary to law prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee hereunder to sell alcoholic beverages upon the licensed premises, or permit alcoholic beverages to be consumed thereon, on any day or at any time when such sale or consumption is prohibited by law.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § VI; Res. of 5-3-1988, § V)
Sec. 4-178. - Delivery of alcoholic beverages restricted.
Alcoholic beverages shall be received at or delivered to the premises of the retail licensee by no other means than by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a permit from the county to make deliveries in the county. Transportation of malt beverages by any other means shall be grounds for revocation of a wholesale permit.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § VI; Res. of 5-3-1988, § V)
Sec. 4-179. - Advertising restricted.
No retail or wholesale dealer licensed by the county shall advertise the sale of alcoholic beverages through the utilization of exterior signs of any type or through the use of promotional products or displays which can be seen from the exterior of the business establishment, except as may otherwise be permitted by state law. Such signs or markings as are customarily put upon containers, cases, or cartons containing said beverages by the distributors thereof shall not be considered as advertising under the terms and provisions of this section.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § VI; Res. of 5-3-1988, § V)
Sec. 4-180. - Hours and days of sale; election day and Sunday sales.
(a)
The hours of operation of retail licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be all hours and days during which the sale of alcoholic beverages is permitted pursuant to state law. The hours of operation of a wholesale licensee for the distribution of malt beverages within the county shall be between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m., except on Sunday.
(b)
There shall be no alcoholic beverages sold or distributed within the county on Sundays, or any other days prohibited by state law.
(c)
It shall be lawful to sell wine and malt beverages in the unincorporated areas of the county during the polling hours of any election; provided, however, that no alcoholic beverages may be sold within 250 feet of a polling place during such a time as the polls are opened.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § IX; Res. of 5-3-1988, § VIII; Ord. of 10-10-1988, § I)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sales on election days, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-181. - Additional regulations for premises licensed for consumption on the premises.
In addition to all other requirements of this chapter, premises licensed for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall comply with the following restrictions:
(1)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold from 12:00 midnight to 7:00 a.m. and no malt beverage may be consumed on the premises from 1:00 a.m. to 6:00 a.m.
(2)
The licensee may hold said license only so long as at least one meal per day shall be served to the general public from the premises at least six days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations, and periods of redecorating, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the serving of malt beverage and wine to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto.
(3)
The sale and service of malt beverage and wine for consumption on the premises shall not be in any back room or side room that is not open to general public use, except that private parties or groups which have been scheduled in advance and as may be served in private dining rooms or meeting rooms, or to registered guests of any hotel or motel in their designated room.
(Res. of 4-25-1985, § IV; Res. of 5-3-1988, § III)

Sec. 4-201. - Sales prohibited.
Except as otherwise authorized by law, no licensee shall sell or permit to be sold alcoholic beverages to any person under the legal drinking age as prescribed by state law.
Sec. 4-202. - Presence on licensed premises restricted.
No licensee hereunder shall permit any person under the legal drinking age as prescribed by state law to be in, frequent, or loiter about the licensed premises unless this person is accompanied by a parent, legal guardian, or custodian; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to persons who are employees of the licensee.
Sec. 4-203. - Proof of age required.
All licensees shall require and properly check identification to ensure an underage person is not sold, served, or has in his possession alcoholic beverages while in a licensed establishment. Identification in this section shall mean any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, or the person's photograph, and giving the person's date of birth, and including, but without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or state department of public safety identification card. Proper identification shall not include a birth certificate.
