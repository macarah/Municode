ARTICLE II. - SALE OF MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE

Sec. 6-31. - Licenses for the sale of malt beverages and wine.
Retail licenses for the sale of malt beverages and wine in the county and permits for the wholesale distribution of malt beverages and wine in the county will be granted in the manner set out in this article.
(Res. of 1-13-1998, § I)
Sec. 6-32. - Type of sale.
modified
Malt beverages and wine sold in the county may be consumed on the premises of the retailer if said retailer has an on-premises license. Malt beverages and wine will be sold by package only in retail businesses with an off-premises license. Malt beverages and wine may be refrigerated and cold prior to retail sale.
Editor's note— In the referendum on May 24, 2016, [it was] voted in favor of distilled spirits being sold as mixed drinks by the drink on the premises.
(Res. of 1-13-1998, § II; Res. of 5-24-2016)
Sec. 6-33. - Retail licensee qualifications.
The retail licensee under this article must be a person of good moral character and a citizen of the United States. This shall not apply to the honorably discharged members of the nations' armed forces who have returned to the county within the prescribed limit.
(1)
The licensee shall not have been convicted within five years of the date of his application of a felony or any violation of the laws of this state, or any other state, relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(2)
The licensee shall be the owner of the premises for which the license is held or the holder of any lease thereon.
(3)
The licensee shall be active in, and solely responsible for, the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(4)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement of his qualifications according to the requirements set forth in this section and shall place same on file with the county clerk before any license is issued.
(5)
If the application covers a partnership, all members of the partnership must be qualified to obtain a license and must make sworn statements of these qualifications.
(6)
No person shall be granted a license to engage in the sale of malt or vinous beverages at retail unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the board of commissioners that such person, or the officers and directors of any corporation, shall be of good character and shall have a law-abiding reputation in the county.
(7)
Any misstatement or concealment of fact in the application shall be grounds for revocation of the license issued and shall make the applicant liable to prosecution for perjury under law.
(8)
No person shall be granted a license who has been convicted of violating any state law or ordinance concerning alcoholic beverages within ten years.
(9)
A fingerprint card must accompany all new applications.
(Res. of 1-13-1998, § III)
Sec. 6-34. - Wholesale permits.
Any wholesale dealer in malt beverages licensed by the state or the agent of such wholesale dealer shall be granted a permit to distribute such beverages in the county upon application for such permit to the county clerk and the presentation of evidence that he understands this article and the conditions under which retail licenses are issued.
(Res. of 1-13-1998, § IV)
Sec. 6-35. - Regulations.
(a)
No holder of a license authorizing the sale of malt beverages or wine at retail in the county, nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell beer or wine to a minor.
(2)
Knowingly sell beer or wine to any person while such person is in an intoxicated condition.
(3)
Sell beer or wine upon the licensed premises or permit beer or wine to be consumed thereon, on any day or at any time when such sale or consumption is prohibited by law.
(4)
Permit on the licensed premises any disorderly conduct, breach of peace, or any lewd, immoral, or improper entertainment, conduct or practices.
(b)
In addition:
(1)
It shall be the duty of the licensee to check the ID of all persons attempting to purchase malt beverages or wine.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any minor to purchase beer or wine in the county. It shall be unlawful for any person or any parent to buy beer or wine and furnish it to a minor for consumption.
(3)
No licensee shall sell any beer or wine to any person under the age of 21 years.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell, or possess, for the purpose of sale, any malt beverages or wine where such person does not have a license from the county to sell or possess for sale such beverages.
(5)
Malt beverages or wine shall be received at or delivered to the premises of the retail licensee by no other means than by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer. Transportation of malt beverages or wine by any other means shall be grounds for revocation of the retail license.
(6)
Dealers in malt beverages or wine with an off-premises license shall not engage in the sale of such beverages except between the hours of 12:00 midnight Monday through 12:00 midnight Saturday night, and shall not permit their places of business to be opened for the sale of such beverages on Sunday.
(c)
Application.
(1)
Before a person may file an application for a malt beverage and wine license, he must have first have applied to the state for a sales tax number.
(2)
The application for a license to sell malt beverages and or wine shall include the following question: "Have you applied for a Georgia Sales Tax Number? If not, you must first apply for a number before you are eligible to apply for a license." Each applicant must answer the question in this subsection.
(3)
On the first application for a license to sell malt beverages and or wine, the applicant must file with the county clerk a copy of his state license within days after the same is issued by the state, and on subsequent applications, the applicant must attach a copy of his application for a state license to his application for a county license.
(4)
When a federal license is issued to an applicant, the applicant must file a copy of same with the county clerk within ten days of when it is issued.
(5)
An applicant who fails to file such copy of his state license or state application and/or the federal license shall subject his license to being revoked by the board of commissioners.
(d)
Any nightclub or place of business licensed to sell beer or wine for consumption on the premises of the business and located within the unincorporated area of the county shall:
(1)
Not sell or offer for sale or allow the consumption of alcohol on the business premises of such premises from 12:00 midnight Saturday until 12:00 midnight Sunday night each and every weekend.
(2)
Close the business at 12:00 midnight Saturday until 12:00 midnight Sunday night each and every weekend unless authorized to stay open later and at other times by other provisions of this article.
(3)
If such nightclub and/or business which is licensed to sell beer and wine for consumption on the premises also serves prepared food for consumption on the premises, then, in that event, the nightclub or business shall be authorized to be open from 12:00 midnight Saturday until 1:00 a.m. Sunday morning and from 11:00 a.m. Sunday morning until 12:00 midnight Sunday for the sale of food and soft drinks, provided the beer or beer and wine is locked up at 12:00 midnight Saturday night, and provided no beer or beer and wine is sold or consumed on the premises after 12:00 midnight Saturday until 12:00 midnight, the following Sunday.
(4)
Any person operating such business as enumerated above, who violates any of the terms of this article shall be guilty of a violation of this article, and, upon arrest and conviction in the magistrate court of the county, be imprisoned in the county jail for a period not to exceed 60 days and/or a fine not to exceed $500.00 for each offense. Each time that any person operating a business fails to close as aforesaid, it shall be a new and distinct crime and may be charged with a new crime each time they fail to close as aforesaid.
(5)
Any licensee operating such business or businesses as enumerated above, and who violates any of the terms of this article, may be charged with the violation and upon a hearing before the board of commissioners, and upon evidence of such violation, may have his license to sell beer or beer and wine revoked.
(e)
A license shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this and subject to revocation with or without cause, and with or without notice or hearing. In case of revocation, the licensee shall not be entitled to a return of the unearned license fee. All such licenses shall have printed on the front these words: "This license is a mere privilege subject to be revoked and annulled by the Marion County Commissioners, with notice of hearing."
(f)
Any violation of the provisions of this article shall be grounds for immediate revocation of a license by the board of commissioners without delay.
(Res. of 1-13-1998, § V)

Sec. 6-38. - Distance restrictions.
(a)
Distance requirements. No license under this article shall be issued to any dealer where the place of business of the license is located within 50 yards of any public library or branch thereof, which is on the same side of the street as the establishment, or within 100 yards of any church, or within 200 yards of a school ground or college campus, except that the restrictions of the section shall not apply to private social clubs owning their own homes which have been chartered and in continuous operation for a period of 25 years prior to the date of the resolution from which this article is derived. The schools or colleges referred to herein shall include only such state, county, city, church or other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools or colleges of this state, and shall not include private schools or colleges wherein only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations or other special subjects are taught.
(b)
Measurement. The distances set out in subsection (a) of this section shall be ascertained by measuring the distance along the route that a person ordinarily walks going directly from the entrance of the building of the licensee to the nearest library, church, school ground or college campus.
(Res. of 1-13-1998, § VIII)
Sec. 6-39. - Administration.
(a)
If the board of commissioners approves the application under this article, the county clerk shall issue an annual retail license to the applicant upon the payment of the license fee.
(b)
A license to engage in the business of selling malt beverages or wine at retail shall not be transferable, except that in the case of the death of any person holding such a license, or any interest therein, in the discretion of the board of commissioners, the license may be transferred to the administrator, executor, or lawful heirs of the deceased person.
(c)
It shall not be necessary for any dealer in malt beverages to make application for a permit for the renewal of an existing license. It shall be necessary, however, when there is any change of any kind in the ownership of any interest in the business to comply with the terms and provisions of this article.
(d)
A copy of this article shall be given to all applicants for license upon their filing their applications with the county clerk. Each of those applicants who have been approved by the board of commissioners shall be required to comply with the provisions of this article upon beginning operation of the establishment.
(Res. of 1-13-1998, § IX)
Sec. 6-40. - Mandatory training.
new
(a)
New licenses. Effective August 9, 2016, any applicant seeking to obtain an alcoholic beverage license in Marion County must provide proof of attendance at, and completion of, a responsible alcohol sales and services (RASS) workshop that has been approved by the county manager. Such workshop shall be attended by the applicant prior to the issuance of any alcohol license and shall be at the applicant's expense. If, at the time the application is considered by the board of commissioners, the applicant has not provided evidence to the board that the applicant has successfully completed an approved RASS workshop, the license application shall be denied. Once the license is granted, the licensee will be required to meet the remaining requirements set forth in this section.
(b)
Existing licenses. All licensees currently holding a valid county alcoholic beverage license at the time of this amendment, who has not attended an approved responsible alcohol sales and service (RASS) workshop, are required to attend and complete a RASS workshop that has been approved by the county manager. Such workshop shall be attended by the licensee at the licensee's expense. All licensees must provide proof of attendance and completion of such RASS workshop to the board of commissioners. Failure to abide by any of the provisions in this subsection may result in suspension or revocation of the licensee's alcohol beverage license. Any change of ownership or licensee will require a new license and will require compliance with the requirements of subsections (a), (b), (c), (d), (e) and (f).
(c)
All alcohol beverage licensees shall be required to attend and complete an approved responsible alcohol sales and service (RASS) workshop every two years. Proof of attendance at, and completion of, an approved RASS workshop within the previous two years must be attached to any renewal application. No alcoholic license will be renewed or issued unless this requirement has been satisfied.
(d)
Effective August 9, 2016, within 60 days of the issuance of any new license, or prior to any license renewal, the licensee must provide proof that all employees and managers involved in the sale or service of alcoholic beverages have, within the previous year, satisfactorily completed a comprehensive alcohol compliance training course approved by the county manager. Employees and managers must complete such yearly training within six months of commencing work or being hired, whichever is shorter. Documentation of each employee's and manager's date of hire and proof of training within the previous year must be attached to all renewal applications and maintaining on the premises, posted in a prominent location and available for public inspection at all times during which the location is open for business and engaged in alcohol sales for off-premises consumption. Employees and managers who directly serve alcoholic beverages to patrons for on-premises consumption shall be required to keep on their person while at work documentation verifying that they have completed their annual RASS/alcohol compliance training as required under this article. All employees and managers subject to this subsection must attend an approved training course at least once per year. Such training shall be at the expense of the licensee or the licensed establishment.
(e)
If, at the time an alcohol beverage license application or renewal is considered by Marion County Board of Commissioners, the licensee has not provided evidence to the county manager that the licensee has satisfied the requirements of subsections (a), (b), (c), and (d) above, the application or renewal shall be denied.
(f)
If, at the time an alcoholic beverage license renewal is being considered, or at any other time more than 60 days after the license has been issued, the license has been issued, the licensee is unable to provide evidence to the county manager and the board of commissioners that the employees and management of the licensed establishment have complied with the training requirements as set forth in subsection (a), (b), (c), and (d), the license may be suspended or revoked.
(Ord. of 8-9-2016(1))
