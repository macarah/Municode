ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 5-1. - City designated bird sanctuary.
(a)
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city is hereby designated as a bird sanctuary.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, shoot or attempt to shoot or molest in any manner any bird or wild fowl or to rob bird nests or wild fowl nests; provided, however, if vandal birds are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or menace to health or property in the opinion of the proper health authorities of the city, such authority is authorized to approve the disposal of such birds.
(c)
Any individual may prevent any such vandal birds from destroying crops of fruit, however, any shooting of vandal birds may only be done after first having obtained permission from the city council.
(Code 1981, § 4-1)
Sec. 5-2. - Animal and fowl—Running at large prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, keeper or person having control of any wild or domestic animal or fowl to permit the same to run at large upon any street or public ground of the city or upon the property of any other person.
(Code 1981, § 4-2)
Sec. 5-3. - Same—Impounding required.
The police are required to seize and impound any wild or domestic animal or fowl found running at large in violation of the preceding section, and to safely keep the same until it shall be redeemed by its owner, upon the payment of a fee for each animal or fowl impounded, and all costs associated with the caring and feeding of the same while impounded, said fees and costs to be established from time to time by resolution adopted by the city council.
(Code 1981, § 4-3)
Sec. 5-4. - Same—Redemption or sale.
In case said impounded animals or fowl shall not be redeemed within five days from the date of their seizure, the police shall cause the same to be advertised and sold as an estray, as provided by the laws of Georgia, if the owner is unknown. But if the owner is known, and he shall fail to redeem such animals or fowl within five days after notice of their impoundment, it shall be a violation of this chapter and, upon conviction, he shall be subject to the provisions of section 1-13.
(Code 1981, § 4-4)
Sec. 5-5. - Cleanliness of premises for animals, fowl.
All places and premises in the city where horses, mules, cattle, rabbits, dogs, pigs, hogs, chickens, or other animals or fowl are harbored or confined shall be kept clean, sanitary and free of noxious odors and shall be maintained in such a manner as will effectively prevent the propagation of flies in or about such premises. The maintaining or ownership of a place or premises in violation of this Code shall be deemed a nuisance and may be abated in accordance with law.
(Code 1981, § 4-6)
Sec. 5-6. - Riding of horses, etc., prohibited on public streets, etc.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride, drive or pasture any horse, mule, donkey, goat, cow or other domestic animal over and upon any street, way, alley, curb or other public property or thoroughfare belonging to the city without first obtaining permission of the mayor and city council.
(b)
Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of section 1-13.
(Code 1981, § 4-8)
Secs. 5-7—5-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DOGS

Sec. 5-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dog means and includes only members of the domestic canine family, three months or more of age. All other members of the canine family, including, but not limited to, foxes, wolves, coyotes, etc., are excluded herefrom.
Inoculation against rabies means the injection, subcutaneously or otherwise as approved by the state department of public health, of canine anti-rabic vaccine approved by the state department of public health.
Owner means and includes any person having a right or property in the dog, or who keeps or harbors a dog, has it in its care, acts as its custodian, or permits a dog to remain on or about any premises occupied by him.
(Code 1981, § 4-13)
Sec. 5-32. - Inoculation and tag required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, maintain, or permit the keeping or maintaining on their premises or elsewhere within the corporate limits of the city any dog which has not been inoculated and tagged and the owner of any dog found within the corporate limits of the city which has not been tagged and inoculated as herein prescribed shall be guilty of a violation of this section.
(b)
All dogs within the corporate limits of the city shall be inoculated and tagged in the following manner:
(1)
They shall be inoculated with a standard and effective anti-rabies serum or virus by a licensed and practicing veterinarian or physician.
(2)
Upon such dog being inoculated, the person so administering the inoculation shall issue his certificate to that effect and shall deliver to the owner of the dog a tag to be worn by such dog.
(3)
Said tag shall be not less than one inch in diameter and shall be of metal and shall have impressed thereon the respective serial number and year, in which issued, and shall be so constructed as to permit its being hung securely around the neck of said dog.
(4)
Said tag shall be worn by the dog around its neck at all times during the year for which the same is issued.
(5)
Inoculations shall be done prior to May 1 of each year, and the inoculation shall be effective for a calendar year from May 1.
(Code 1981, § 4-14)
Sec. 5-33. - Actions constituting nuisance.
No person shall allow a dog of which he has custody or control to remain on his property or premises if the same shall constitute a nuisance as the term is defined by the law of this state. For purposes of this section, it shall constitute a nuisance per se for any such animal to bark, howl, whine or emit any noises whatsoever in a continuous manner for a period of 30 minutes or longer so as to disturb any occupant of any adjacent or adjoining property within hearing distance of such animal.
(Code 1981, § 4-15)
Sec. 5-34. - Female dogs in heat.
No person owning or having custody of any female dog shall permit such female dog off the premises of the owner or person having control of such dog when such dog shall be in heat unless the same is under control and attached to a leash as hereinabove provided.
(Code 1981, § 4-16)
Sec. 5-35. - Rabies.
If a dog is believed to have rabies or has been bitten by an animal suspected of having rabies, such dog shall promptly be confined by a chain to the owner's premises. The owner shall then immediately notify the city clerk of the fact that his dog has been exposed to rabies and at the discretion of the city clerk or other official designated by the governing body, the dog shall be removed from the owner's premises to a veterinary hospital and there placed under observation for a period of two weeks.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person knowing or suspecting a dog to have rabies to allow such dog to be taken off his premises or beyond the limits of the city without first notifying the county health department and obtaining permission from the governing body.
(2)
Whenever a dog is ascertained to be rabid, notification of same shall be made to any police officer of the city, who shall cause such dog to be removed from the city and immediately disposed of.
(Code 1981, § 4-18)
State Law reference— Rabies Control, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1 et seq.; inoculation of dogs and cats against rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-5.
Sec. 5-36. - Running at large prohibited; lease required; impounding; exception.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any dog to allow the same to run upon the streets, sidewalks or alleys of the city, unless said dog is accompanied by its owner or other person having charge of said dog, in which event said dog must be tied with a cord or chain of sufficient strength to hold it.
(b)
Any dog found running at large shall be caught and impounded by any policeman, marshal or other officer of the city as the city may direct and kept for a period of five days, unless claimed by its owner, in which event said owner shall pay a fee for each dog impounded, and all costs associated with the caring and feeding of the same while impounded, said fees and costs to be established from time to time by the city council by appropriate resolution. The city may contract with private or public animal shelters or other facilities of impoundment licensed by the state and pass on any fees and costs from these shelters or facilities to the owner. Should any dog so sheltered or impounded not be claimed by an owner within five days it may be disposed of or destroyed under the normal rules and regulations of the shelter or facility. Any dog so impounded found not to be inoculated for rabies may be immediately inoculated for same and any such inoculation fee shall be chargeable to the owner of the dog.
(Code 1981, § 4-19)

ARTICLE III. - DANGEROUS DOGS[2]

Sec. 5-61. - Authority; exemptions; definitions.
(a)
This article is passed under the authority of and in compliance with Georgia's Responsible Dog Ownership Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq. The purpose of this article is to clearly define local government responsibility, as well as to limit the administrative and liability burdens of the city with regard to dangerous dogs and vicious dogs.
(b)
No dog shall be classified as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog for actions that occur while the dog is being used by a law enforcement or military officer to carry out the law enforcement or military officer's official duties. No dog shall be classified as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog if the person injured by such dog is a person who, at the time, was committing a trespass, was abusing the dog, or was committing or attempting to commit an offense under O.C.G.A. title 16, chapter 5.
(c)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal control board means a board consisting of a veterinarian, a city employee and a citizen of the city as hereinafter appointed by the city council.
Animal control officer means the person charged by the city council with enforcing this article.
Sec. 5-62. - Designation of dog control officer.
The chief animal control officer shall be designated as a dog control officer for the purposes of this article. It shall be the duty of the dog control officer to investigate reports of dogs believed to be subject to classification as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog within the city. Upon receipt of such a report, it shall be the duty of the dog control officer to make such investigations as necessary to determine whether such dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog. The chief animal control officer shall be appointed by the city council.
Sec. 5-63. - Determination by dog control officer; notice; right to hearing; decision by animal control board.
(a)
After concluding the investigation of a report regarding a dog believed to be subject to classification as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog as required by section 5-62, the dog control officer shall mail a dated notice to the dog' s owner within 72 hours. Such notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's determination and shall state that the owner has a right to request a hearing from the animal control board on the dog control officer's determination. Such hearing must be requested within 15 days after the effective date of the notice as shown thereon. Such notice shall also provide a form for requesting such hearing and shall state that if the hearing is not requested within said 15-day period, the dog control officer's determination shall become effective for all purposes under this article.
(b)
When a hearing is requested by a dog owner in accordance with subsection (a) of this section, such hearing shall be scheduled within 30 days after the request is received by the animal control board; provided, however, that such hearing may be continued from time to time by the animal control board for good cause shown by the party requesting the continuance. At least ten days prior to the hearing, the animal control board shall mail to the dog owner written notice of the date, time, and place of the hearing. At the hearing, the dog owner shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence, and the animal control board shall receive other evidence and testimony as may be reasonably necessary to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's determination. The formal rules of evidence applicable in the courts of record of this state shall not apply during such hearing.
(c)
Within ten days after the hearing before the animal control board, the animal control board shall mail written notice to the dog owner of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, the classification shall specify the date upon which that determination shall be effective. If the determination is that the dog is to be euthanized, as authorized under state law, the notice shall specify the date by which such euthanasia shall occur.
(d)
A judicial review of the final decision of the animal control board may be had in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 50-13-19.
(e)
All notices submitted by mail under this section shall be by certified mail or by statutory overnight delivery to the recipient's last known address.
Sec. 5-64. - Issuance of certificate of registration.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner to have or possess a classified dog without a certificate of registration issued in accord with the provisions of state law. Certificates of registration shall be non-transferable and shall only be issued to a person 18 years of age or older. No more than one certificate of registration shall be issued per domicile.
(b)
Unless otherwise specified in this section, or by state law, a certificate of registration for a dangerous dog shall be issued if the dog control officer determines that the following requirements have been met:
(1)
The owner has maintained an enclosure designed to securely confine the dangerous dog on the owner's property, indoors, or in a securely locked and enclosed pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the dangerous dog from leaving such property; and
(2)
Clearly visible warning signs have been posted at all entrances to the premises where the dog resides.
(c)
Except as provided in subsections (e) and (f) of this section, a certificate of registration for a vicious dog shall be issued if the dog control officer determines that the following requirements have been met:
(1)
The owner has maintained an enclosure designed to securely confine the vicious dog on the owner's property, indoors, or in a securely locked and enclosed pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the vicious dog from leaving such property;
(2)
Clearly visible warning signs have been posted at all entrances to the premises where the dog resides;
(3)
A microchip containing an identification number and capable of being scanned has been injected under the skin between the shoulder blades of the dog; and
(4)
The owner maintains and can provide proof of general or specific liability insurance in the amount of at least $50,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the State of Georgia insuring the owner of the vicious dog against liability for any bodily injury or property damage caused by the dog.
(d)
No certificate of registration shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of two or more violations of O.C.G.A. title 4, chapter 8, article 2.
(e)
No person shall be the owner of more than one vicious dog.
(f)
No certificate of registration for a vicious dog shall be issued to any person who has convicted of:
(1)
A felony as defined in O.C.G.A. § 17-10-6.1;
(2)
The felony of dog fighting as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-37, or the felony of aggravated cruelty to animals as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4; or
(3)
A felony involving trafficking and cocaine, illegal drugs, marijuana, methamphetamine, or ecstasy as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-13-31 and in O.C.G.A. § 16-13-31.1;
from the time of conviction until two years after completion of his sentence, nor to any person residing with such person.
(g)
Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of renewal of a certificate of registration for a vicious dog, a dog control officer shall verify that the owner is continuing to comply with the provisions of this article. Failure to renew a certificate of registration within ten days of the renewal date or initial classification date shall constitute a violation of state law and this article.
Sec. 5-65. - Confiscation of dog; grounds; disposition.
(a)
A dangerous or vicious dog shall be immediately confiscated by any dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer in the case of any violation of state law or this article.
(b)
The owner of any dog that has been confiscated pursuant to state law or this article shall recover such dog upon payment of reasonable confiscation and housing costs and proof of compliance with the provisions of Georgia law and this article. All fines and all charges for services performed by a law enforcement or dog control officer shall be paid prior to the owner's recovery of the dog. Criminal prosecution shall not be stayed due to the owner's recovery of the dog or euthanasia of the dog.
(c)
In the event that the owner of the dog has not complied with the provisions of state law or this article within 20 days of the date the dog was confiscated, such dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner, and the owner may be required to pay the costs of housing and euthanasia.
Sec. 5-66. - Limits of city liability.
It is the intent of this article that the owner of a dangerous dog or a vicious dog shall be solely liable to any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog. Under no circumstances shall the city, or any of its employees or officials, who enforce or fail to enforce the provisions of this article be held liable for any damage to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog that has been classified as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, or by a dog that has been reported to the proper authorities as being a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, or by a dog that the city has failed to identify as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, or by a dog which has been identified as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, but has not been kept or restrained in the manner required by this article, or by a dangerous dog or a vicious dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage as required by Georgia law and this article.
Sec. 5-67. - Violations of article.
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-29 relating to unlawful acts of the owner of a dangerous dog are hereby incorporated into the provisions of this article. Any person charged with committing such unlawful acts shall be prosecuted in a court of competent jurisdiction that has the authority to impose the penalties provided in said statute or this Code.
Sec. 5-68. - Duties and prohibitions of owner of a classified dog.
(a)
The owner of a classified dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose or has attacked a human being and shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog has died or has been euthanized.
(b)
A vicious dog shall not be transferred, sold, or donated to any other person unless it is relinquished by a government facility or veterinarian to be euthanized.
(c)
The owner of a classified dog who moves into the city shall register the classified dog in the city within ten days of becoming a resident of the city and shall notify the dog control officer of the jurisdiction from whence he moved.
Sec. 5-69. - Euthanasia for causing serious injury on more than one occasion.
In accord with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-26, a dog that is found, after notice and opportunity for hearing provided by section 5-63, to have caused a serious injury to a human on more than one occasion shall be euthanized; provided, however, that no injury occurring before July 1, 2012, shall count for purposes of this section.
Sec. 5-70. - Previous classified dangerous or vicious dogs.
(a)
Any dog classified prior to July 1, 2012, as a potentially dangerous dog, shall on or after that date be classified as a dangerous dog under this article.
(b)
Any dog classified prior to July 1, 2012, as a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, shall on or after that date be classified as a vicious dog under this article.
(c)
The owner of any dog referred to in subsection (a) or (b) of this section shall come into compliance with all current provisions of this article by January 1, 2014.
Sec. 5-71. - Authority of superior court judge.
Nothing in this article shall be deemed or construed to limit the authority of a judge of the superior court of the county to order euthanasia of a dog in accord with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25.
Sec. 5-72. - Applicability of state law.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, in the event that there is a conflict between the provisions of this article and the provisions of O.C.G.A. title 4, chapter 8, article 2, as now or hereafter existing, the provisions of O.C.G.A. title 4, chapter 8, article 2, as now or hereafter existing, shall in all respects control.
