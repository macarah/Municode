Sec. 26-1. - Management and supervision.
(a)
The responsibility for the management, operation, maintenance, and general supervision of the municipal cemeteries shall be vested in the city clerk/treasurer, subject to the authority and power of the municipal cemetery committee of the city council.
(b)
The city clerk/treasurer, in supervising the affairs of the municipal cemeteries, shall have the following duties:
(1)
To look after and take proper care of all municipal cemeteries and see that no lot in any cemetery is used or occupied in violation of this chapter or any rule or regulation promulgated by the municipal cemetery committee;
(2)
To keep full and detailed accounts of the receipts and expenditures on account of the municipal cemeteries; and
(3)
To see to the digging of graves and to the proper interring of the dead when called upon to do so.
(Code 1982, § 14-101)
Sec. 26-2. - Purchase and conveyance of lots.
(a)
Lot valuation. The municipal cemetery committee of the city council shall place a value upon all unsold lots in the Greenlawn Cemetery and shall record such valuations on an official plan or map of such cemetery. Such map or plan of the Greenlawn Cemetery shall be kept on file in the city clerk/treasurer's office.
(b)
Method of purchase. Any person desiring to purchase an easement in particular lots, for the purpose for which they are intended, shall be permitted to do so upon payment of the fixed price for such lots according to the rules and regulations that may be established by the municipal cemetery committee. Burial lots shall be sold only in multiples of two. There shall be no discrimination in the sale of lots on any basis.
(c)
Manner of conveying. Conveyances of burial lots shall be executed in the name of the city by the mayor and have affixed thereto the seal of the city. Such conveyance shall not convey fee simple title, but shall convey to the purchaser of the burial site an easement for the exclusive right of interment and sepulcher in such lots; and, by reference therein made, shall convey such lots subject to all of the provisions of this chapter as fully as if set out in each such conveyance.
(d)
Recordation of deed. At the time the city conveys any burial lots, the city clerk/treasurer, in addition to collecting the purchase price thereof shall collect from the purchaser an amount sufficient to have such conveyance recorded in the clerk/treasurer's office, county superior court, and shall have such conveyance so recorded before delivery thereof to the purchaser. No sale or transfer of any such burial lot or lots or any right therein shall be valid unless approved in writing by the municipal cemetery committee or until the deed of transfer and such written approval are recorded in the clerk/treasurer's office, county superior court.
(e)
Records of ownership. The city clerk/treasurer shall keep full and complete records of the ownership of all lot easements in the cemetery; of the location of each grave; of the names and ages and other vital statistics of the persons buried in each grave that has been or shall hereafter be used; the date of burial of each; and the names of persons responsible for maintenance of such lot, if other than the record owner.
(f)
Sale or transfer by owner. Owners of lots shall not be permitted to sell or transfer lots without the written consent of the municipal cemetery committee.
(g)
Rules for interment. No person shall be interred in any municipal cemetery until a permit has been issued by the city clerk/treasurer. Such permit shall be issued upon application providing proof that:
(1)
The lot in which burial is to be made has been fully paid for;
(2)
The person arranging for such burial has the right to such use;
(3)
Such lot is not used beyond its capacity; and
(4)
Proper record is made of the deceased, the location of the grave, and the owner of the lot or those otherwise responsible for maintenance, in accordance with subsection (e) of this section.
(h)
Annual maintenance fee. Owners of lots, their successors in interest, or their designee, shall be responsible for the payment of an annual maintenance fee to the municipal cemetery committee in accordance with such rules and regulations as may be set by such committee.
(Code 1982, § 14-102)
Sec. 26-3. - Restrictions on use.
(a)
Enclosures. No curbing, fencing, or enclosure of any sort shall be erected on, in, or around any burial lot in the cemetery, but the city reserves the right to erect curbing along the walks and driveways, if it so desires.
(b)
Plantings. The planting of flowers and shrubs or any other effort to improve or beautify any lot within the municipal cemetery shall be done only after the plans for such work have been submitted to the municipal cemetery committee and approved by that committee. Nothing contained in this section shall be construed to prohibit the decoration or adornment of any grave site with potted or cut flowers or plants.
(c)
Stones and monuments. Owners of cemetery lot easements numbers 1 through 262 shall have the right to erect proper stones or monuments marking the burial site, except that no slab shall be set except in a horizontal position and flush with the ground.
(1)
Headstones shall not exceed two feet in height above the ground and 18 inches in width and shall be set in a solid concrete foundation.
(2)
Monuments of cut stone or marble shall not exceed ten feet in height and the erection of the monuments must be authorized and supervised by the municipal cemetery committee.
(Code 1982, § 14-103; Ord. of 4-9-1991, § 1)
Sec. 26-4. - General regulations.
(a)
Trees and shrubs. No tree or shrub shall be planted, removed, cut down, or destroyed within the borders of any burial lot, walks, or lawn spaces, without the consent of the municipal cemetery committee.
(b)
Injury to monuments. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove, deface, or in any manner injure any monument, headstone, or ornament thereof within the municipal cemetery.
(c)
Discharging firearms. It shall be unlawful for any person to shoot any gun, pistol, or other firearm within the municipal cemetery except at a funeral ceremony.
(d)
Depositing offensive matter. It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit on any part of the cemetery or any land adjoining it any dead carcass or any putrid or offensive matter whatever.
(e)
Disorderly conduct. It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct himself in a boisterous or disorderly manner within the perimeter of the municipal cemetery.
(f)
Entering cemeteries at night. It shall be unlawful for any person to enter the municipal cemetery or walk upon or across the cemetery between sunset and sunrise without the written consent of the municipal cemetery committee.
(g)
Advertisements. It shall be unlawful for any person to post any advertisement or sign, or to solicit any work in the municipal cemetery.
(h)
Unauthorized interments. It shall be unlawful for any person to inter any dead body in any part of the municipal cemetery without the express authorization of the municipal cemetery committee.
(i)
Disinterment. Disinterments shall be made only when authorized by the municipal cemetery committee and the county board of health.
(j)
Maintenance. Notwithstanding section 26-2 (h) above, the owner of each burial plot shall have the ultimate obligation to maintain it in a neat and attractive condition at the owner's sole expense, to include without limitation: flower beds weeded, old flowers removed, and grass clippings, gravel and any other debris removed from the grave plot.
(Code 1982, § 14-104; Amend. of 6-12-2007, § 1)
Sec. 26-5. - Authority of the city to regulate.
(a)
The city council reserves to themselves and their successors in office the right to alter, amend, modify, or add to the rules, regulations, conditions, and restrictions set forth in this chapter at any time it is deemed advisable to do so in order to carry out the purposes of this chapter.
(b)
There shall be no liability whatsoever, either tort or contractual, on the part of the city, or its officials or officers (or their successors in office), or its agents or employees, to any purchases of any lots in the municipal cemetery, or to any person holding under them, or to the family or relatives of any person buried in the cemetery, or to any person or the family of such person who has erected any monument, marker, or mausoleum therein, by reason of any act or acts, omission, negligence, or otherwise relating to the municipal cemetery. In accepting any conveyance of any burial lot, each purchaser agrees that all provisions of this chapter are valid, and that he and his heirs and assigns shall hold such lot subject to all the provisions of this chapter and subject to all amendments hereto made by the city council.
(Code 1982, § 14-105)
Sec. 26-6. - Depth of burial; general regulations.
It shall be unlawful for any individual, partnership, or corporation to bury any dead body within the city limits the city in violation of the following stipulated requirements:
(1)
An excavation or grave within the city in which a dead body is buried, must be covered with soil of not less than 18 inches on top of vault or burial casing in which body is placed and up to the ground level around such excavation or grave.
(2)
This section is a health measure and will be strictly enforced.
(3)
Violators of this section shall be subject to fine or punishment as prescribed in section 1-12.
(Code 1982, § 14-106)
Sec. 26-7. - Grave liners.
(a)
Any excavation or grave within the city, in which a dead body is buried, must also include a steel or concrete grave liner (vault).
(b)
Violators of this section shall be subject to fine or punishment as prescribed in section 1-12.
(Ord. of 5-11-1999)
