Chapter 68 - SOLID WASTE, GARBAGE COLLECTION AND TRANSFER STATION
ARTICLE I. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 68-1. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Garbage means any spoiled or waste food, whether the same be for animal or human consumption, which results from either the handling, preparation, cooking, consumption or disposal of such food.
Litter means any garbage, trash, refuse, rubbish, sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, waste material, tin cans, trash, debris, dead animals, dead trees, weeds, junk vehicles or parts from junk vehicles, or discarded items or materials of any kind of description which have been set in a disorderly state which would tend to create a danger to the public health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of this county
Person means any person, firm, association, corporation, company, or other entity whether legally constituted or simply associated in fact.
Refuse means anything that is thrown away or rejected as worthless or useless such as trash, rubbish, garbage, and includes any solid waste and any property held for salvage purposes.
Rubbish means any solid waste which is not susceptible to becoming putrid and consists of such things as paper, wrappings, cigarettes, cardboard, tin cans, yard clippings, leaves, wood, glass, bedding, crockery and similar materials which are rejected or thrown away as worthless.
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003)
Sec. 68-2. - Enforcing officers.
Primary enforcement of this chapter shall be made by citation from the marshal's office of Paulding County. In addition, this chapter may be enforced by citation issued by any zoning enforcement officer, sheriff or deputy sheriff.
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003)
Sec. 68-3—68-9. - Reserved.
Sec. 68-10. - Billing of and payment by commercial haulers.
All commercial haulers will be billed monthly by the county for disposal services and such billing shall be payable not later than the fifteenth of the month following the month for which the bill was rendered. Failure to remit by the due date shall constitute grounds for suspension of the permit. Accounts delinquent for a period of 15 days shall result in revocation of all permits, and forfeiture of surety bond. In the event of revocation, the privilege of again using the transfer station shall not be restored until a new permit is granted, a new bond is posted, and all outstanding debt is paid in full.
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003)
Sec. 68-11—-68-15. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TRANSFER STATION

Sec. 68-16. - Transfer station hours.
The operating hours of the county transfer station may vary from season to season and from year to year, as the need arises in the judgment of the board of commissioners. A schedule of the current operating hours of the transfer station is available from the office of the board of commissioners. An attendant shall be on duty during all hours of operation. No entry onto the transfer station will be permitted except during the hours of operation as specified by the board of commissioners. (Monday—Saturday 8:00 A.M. to 4:30 P.M.)
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003)
Sec. 68-17. - Non-acceptable items at transfer station.
The following waste is not acceptable:
(1)
Paint.
(2)
Oil.
(3)
Petroleum products or by-products.
(4)
Asbestos.
(5)
Tires.
(6)
Batteries.
(7)
Bio-medical products.
(8)
Liquid waste sludge.
(9)
Hazardous waste as defined by Georgia O.C.G.A. § 12-8-60, Department of Natural Resources and Environmental Protection Department 391-3-11.
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003)
Sec. 68-18. - Special waste.
The following items can be processed as follows:
Recycled items.
(1)
Refrigerator.
(2)
All air conditioners (require an additional $10.00 fee for freon removal).
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003)
Sec. 68-19. - Scavenging prohibited.
No scavenging will be permitted at the transfer station.
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003)
Secs. 68-20—68-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PENALTIES

Sec. 68-36. - Punishment of violators.
Failure to comply with the provisions of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor which, upon conviction, shall be punishable as provided in section 1-19.
(1)
Each day any violation of this Code shall constitute a separate offense.
(2)
In addition to the penalties hereinabove provided, any condition caused or permitted to exist in violation of any provisions of this Code or any ordinance or resolution shall be deemed a public nuisance and may be abated by the county as provided by law, and each day that such condition continues shall be regarded as a new separate offense.
(Res. No. 03-23 §§ 2, 3, 8-26-2003; Ord. No. 20-08, § 2, 7-28-2020)
