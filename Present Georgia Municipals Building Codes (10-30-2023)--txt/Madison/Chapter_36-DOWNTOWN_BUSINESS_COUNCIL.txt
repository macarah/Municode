Chapter 36 - DOWNTOWN BUSINESS COUNCIL

Sec. 36-1. - Creation.
It has been determined by the mayor and council that there is a need for revitalization and redevelopment of Downtown Madison to develop and promote trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities, and to promote the general welfare of the community and the state by creating a climate favorable to the location of new industry, trade and commerce, and the further development of existing industry, trade and commerce within the city. The creation of a downtown business council will benefit the public good and general welfare of the community. It is in the public interest and is vital to the public welfare of the citizens of the city to revitalize and redevelop the downtown area of the city. Accordingly, after thorough investigation, the mayor and council have determined that it is desirable and necessary that the downtown business council be reactivated immediately, in order to fulfill the needs expressed above. Therefore, be it ordained by the mayor and council, and it is hereby resolved, that there is hereby determined and declared to be a present and future need for a downtown business council to function in the city. There is hereby activated in the city a public body corporate and politic known as the "Madison Downtown Business Council."
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-2. - Reconstituted council; membership.
The mayor and council acknowledge that pursuant to the ordinance of July 14, 1986, there has been in operation over the years a downtown business council. Membership of the council shall consist of all persons owning property or operating businesses within the downtown area as outlined in section 36-9. It is the purpose of the amendment to reconfirm and reconstitute the Madison downtown business council. The downtown business council shall support the continued prosperity of Downtown Madison as both a strong economic engine for the community and as a vital core of the surrounding residential environs.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-3. - Board; composition; qualifications.
The leadership of the downtown business council shall be known as the "Main Street Advisory Board." The board shall be composed of between seven and 11 members, all of whom have a demonstrated interest in the support, promotion and continued prosperity of Downtown Madison as a vital community center. A majority of the board must be registered voters in the city, and must be members of the downtown business council. Board members shall be appointed by the mayor and council of the city, who shall consider during selection the following; business or professional background, including a knowledge of finance and/or personnel matters; commitment to spending the time necessary to be a properly oriented, educated, and participating board member, with an emphasis on board members growing in their service; recognition of both sexes and significant racial or ethnic population; and commitment to the principles of this chapter and a desire to have Downtown Madison succeed and improve.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-4. - Board terms and initial appointments.
Board members shall serve two-year terms. However, to insure staggered terms, the following appointments will be from one year only, from June, 2003 to June, 2004: (1) Kemp Crawford, (2) Rob Hanselman, (3) Joe Houston, and (4) Weyman Hunt. The following persons shall serve a full two-year term, from June, 2003 to June, 2005; (5) Mark Argo, (6) Davis Bell, (7) Faye Craft, (8) Terrell Gerrard, (9) June Royal, and (10) Barbara Voyles.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-5. - Board member's oath.
Each board member, before assuming the duties of his/her office, shall sign the following oath, which shall be retained with the records of the board: "I, _______, citizen of Morgan County, Georgia, do solemnly swear that I will, to the best of my ability, without favor or affection to any person and without any unauthorized financial gain or compensation to myself, faithfully and fairly discharge all of the duties and responsibilities that devolve upon me as a member of Main Street Advisory Board, during the term of my service as such member."
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-6. - Compensation.
Board members shall receive no compensation, but may be reimbursed by resolution of the board for actual expenses in performing their duties.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-7. - Conflict of interest.
Even the appearance of impropriety is to be avoided. No board member shall accept a gift, payment, or other benefit in exchange for any consideration on any matter before the board.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-8. - Role of the Main Street advisory board.
The Main Street advisory board serves at the pleasure of the mayor and council. The Main Street office is responsible for administration of the activities and functions of the Main Street advisory board. The Main Street advisory board and its staff shall be financed and insured by the city. The functions of the Main Street advisory board shall be performed in harmony with the responsibilities and activities of the various municipal board and committees. The board is advisory in nature, and shall make no decisions, nor shall it order any directives. Instead, it shall make recommendations, and provide advice, consistent with the needs of the city. The board shall organize itself, carry out its responsibilities, and exercise its powers, in strict accordance with the requirements of this chapter.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-9. - Jurisdiction.
The downtown area over which the board shall exercise jurisdiction shall be that geographical area described in exhibit "B" attached to the ordinance from which this chapter derives and made a part hereof by reference. Said area shall be concurrent with the boundaries established by resolution of the downtown development authority on March 12, 1984, as amended, and generally reflect the boundaries of the special tax district-central business district created by the mayor and council on November 13, 1995, as amended.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-10. - General goals.
As part of its general mission of promoting trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities for the public good and the continued prosperity of Downtown Madison, the downtown business council and the Main Street advisory board shall also strive to accomplish the following goals:
(1)
Advocate the maintenance of the downtown commercial area ("downtown") as the center of the city business community.
(2)
Provide leadership and coordination for various initiatives located in downtown.
(3)
Assist the promotion of downtown as a destination for residents and visitors.
(4)
Support the preservation of downtown's historic integrity and small town character.
(5)
Comment on downtown revitalization and redevelopment efforts, including studies and reports, and recommendations to the mayor and council.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-11. - Specific functions of the board.
In order to meet the above stated goals of the board, it shall perform the following specific functions:
(1)
Work with the Main Street director and other municipal offices to accomplish the above stated goals.
(2)
Serve as a sounding board for downtown initiatives, including verbal and written feedback and reports to the mayor and council in regard to proposed downtown initiatives.
(3)
Serve as a conduit between the city council and the downtown business council for information pertinent to downtown.
(4)
Serve as a conduit for information between Main Street staff and various committees associated with the downtown business council and with the board.
(5)
Provide a forum for discussion of issues in regard to downtown.
(6)
Gather information regarding downtown issues and initiatives, including discussion thereof at board meetings and presentation thereof to the mayor and city council.
(7)
Host workshops or lectures advantageous to the downtown community.
(8)
Serve in an advisory capacity to the mayor and city council regarding downtown issues, including provision of recommendations to the city council and to the various committees and staff associated with downtown.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-12. - Regular meetings and records of the board.
(a)
Regular meetings shall be conducted in accordance with the requirements of state law, including without limitation the Open Meetings Act. Notice of the meetings shall be via posting of the agenda at a public location at city hall, and via verbal or written notification to the local press.
(b)
Minutes shall be kept of board meetings in accordance with this chapter and state law.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-13. - Rules of procedure.
The commission shall adopt rules for the transaction of its business, including provision for regular meetings, called meetings, election of officers, rules of attendance, appointment of committees, etc. These rules of procedure, or subsequent amendments thereto, shall become effective after review and approval by the mayor and council of the city.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-14. - Ethics.
Board members shall put loyalty to the highest moral principles and to country above loyalty to persons, party; uphold the federal and state constitutions, laws, and legal regulations and never be a party to their evasion; never discriminate unfairly by the dispensing of special favors or privileges to anyone, and never accept, for self or family, favors or benefits under circumstances which might be reasonably construed as influencing performance of duties; make no private promises of any kind binding upon the duties of office, since a member has no private word which can be binding on public duty; engage in no business, directly or indirectly, inconsistent with the best interest of the city; never use information obtained confidentially in the performance of duties as a means for making private profit; expose corruption wherever discovered; and uphold these principles, ever conscious that public office is a public trust. Staff for the board shall at all times adhere to the city's ethics policy.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-15. - No adverse affect.
The action taken by the mayor and council herein is not intended in any way to adversely affect, replace or supplant any public corporation or any industrial development, downtown development or other authority previously created.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
Sec. 36-16. - Effectiveness.
This chapter shall be effective immediately upon its adoption by the mayor and council. From and after such adoption the downtown business council and the Main Street advisory board shall be deemed reconstituted and reactivated.
(Amd. of 5-12-03, § 1)
