ARTICLE II. - RECREATION COMMISSION[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Authorities, boards, commissions and committees, § 2-141 et seq.

Sec. 50-31. - Established; term of members.
There is established a recreation commission, to be known as the Madison-Morgan County Recreation Commission. Four members of this commission shall be appointed by the mayor and city council, and four members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of the county; the commission shall consist of eight persons serving without pay. The term of office shall be six years or until their successors are appointed and qualified, except that the members of such commission first appointed shall expire annually thereafter. Vacancies in such commission occurring otherwise than by expiration of term shall be filled by another appointment for the unexpired term by the mayor and city council or the board of commissioners, respectively.
(Code 1991, § 18-101)
Sec. 50-32. - Officers.
Immediately after their appointment, the members shall meet and organize by electing one of their members president and such other officers as may be necessary.
(Code 1991, § 18-101)
Sec. 50-33. - Powers.
(a)
The recreation commission shall have power to adopt bylaws, rules and regulations for the proper conduct of recreation for the city and county.
(b)
The recreation commission shall provide, conduct and supervise public playgrounds, playfields, indoor recreation centers, and other recreation facilities owned or controlled by the city and county. It shall have the power to conduct any form of recreation or cultural activity that will employ the leisure time of the people in a wholesome or constructive manner. It may conduct such activities on properties under its own control, on public properties with the consent of the authorities of such properties, and on private properties with the consent of the owners.
(Code 1991, § 18-101)
Sec. 50-34. - Superintendent, other personnel.
The recreation commission shall have the power to appoint or designate someone as superintendent who is trained or properly qualified for the work, and such other personnel as the commission deems proper.
(Code 1991, § 18-101)
Sec. 50-35. - Budget; gifts; title to real property.
The recreation commission shall submit an annual budget to the mayor and council and to the county commissioners for their approval. The commission may also solicit or receive any gifts or bequests of money or other personal property or any donation to be applied, principal or income, for either temporary or permanent use for playgrounds or other recreational purposes. The commission may also receive and hold title to any real property as authorized by the city or county.
(Code 1991, § 18-101)
Sec. 50-36. - Reports.
County commissioners' reports and such other reports may be requested from time to time.
(Code 1991, § 18-101)

ARTICLE III. - PUBLIC PARKS AND FACILITIES REGULATIONS

Sec. 50-66. - Hours of operation.
(a)
Any public park in the city administered by the city, any public recreation commission or any similar governing authority, and all facilities on the premises, with the exception of lighted tennis courts and the parking lot adjacent to such tennis courts, shall be closed each day at 7:00 p.m. until local sunrise, commencing September 1 each year and continuing through March 31 of the following year, and from 9:00 p.m. until local sunrise, commencing on April 1 through August 31 each year; however, the mayor and council may issue permits upon application granting the use of the facilities to individuals or groups, and issuing permits for such usage beyond the hours herein stated in this section.
(b)
The hours of operation of the tennis courts, the swimming pool and bathhouse and restroom facilities located in any such park shall be open as regulated by the mayor and city council or at the direction of the mayor and city council by the recreation department.
(c)
The use of any public park in the city or any of its facilities after the hours of closing and before local sunrise without permission of the mayor and city council, or the presence of anyone in the park after the hours of closing and before local sunrise, without such permission, shall constitute a misdemeanor; and upon conviction, anyone violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1991, § 18-102)
Sec. 50-67. - Possession of alcoholic beverages; penalty.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for anyone to use or possess alcoholic beverages any time in public parks in the city or any of the facilities in such a park.
(b)
The use or possession of any alcoholic beverage in such park or its facilities at any time shall constitute a misdemeanor, and upon conviction anyone violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1991, § 18-102)
