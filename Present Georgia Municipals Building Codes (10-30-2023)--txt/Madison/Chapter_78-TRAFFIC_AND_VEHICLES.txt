ARTICLE III. - STOPPING, STANDING AND PARKING[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200 et seq.; authority to regulate or prohibit stopping, standing or parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(1).

Sec. 78-66. - Parking on town square.
(a)
Parking in and around certain areas of the town square, namely, those areas in which parking time limits are posted in certain city blocks, shall be governed by the following terms and conditions:
(1)
Parking times shall be limited to the times posted in a particular block in the city.
(2)
Should a vehicle remain in a parking spot for longer than the time posted, a violation shall be imposed, and a ticket shall be given indicating the violation, and a fine of $10.00 shall be imposed.
(3)
Automobiles of nonresidents of the county may be provided with courtesy tickets, in a form designated by the mayor, so as to provide visitors with a courteous welcome in the city.
(4)
Any persons who shall attempt to evade or who does evade the parking rules and regulations of the city by erasing any marking placed on any portion of any motor vehicle for the purpose of hindering or preventing city authorities from ascertaining the length of time the vehicle has been parked shall be guilty of a violation of this section and upon conviction shall be fined up to $50.00.
(5)
Any person who shall remove his vehicle from one public parking place to another for the purpose of hindering or preventing the proper city authorities from enforcing the city's rules and regulations shall be guilty of a violation of this section and upon conviction shall be fined up to $50.00.
(6)
The parking areas referred to in this section are declared to be all parking spaces in the city to which time limits apply.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person who is an employee, owner or operator of any trade, business or profession, or a resident to park in any timed parking zone in the city during the hours set forth in this section, if the trade, business or profession, or residence of which the person is an employee, owner or operator, or resident is located along a street in the city which abuts a timed parking zone. An exception will be made to allow business owners and employees, or residents a loading and unloading period. This period is not to exceed 30 minutes continuously and shall not exceed two such uses per day. The business or resident so engaged in loading or unloading shall notify the city by calling the police department prior to each such use and reporting the intended use of the privilege herein granted. Any business having access to off-street loading shall not be entitled to use the provisions of this subsection. All persons violating this subsection (b) will be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, may be punished by a fine of $15.00 for the first three violations in a calendar year, and $30.00 for any subsequent violations in a calendar year. Any citation received for violating subsection (b) must be paid within five calendar days or additional fees will be added as follows: $10.00 late fee for a $15.00 citation and $25.00 late fee for a $30.00 citation.
(c)
A timed parking zone is an area of public property which is marked as a parking place, and which is located on a block for which a sign is erected designating the time for which parking is permitted in that zone, e.g. "One-Hour Parking" etc.
(d)
The hours of application of this section shall be as follows: 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday.
(e)
This section shall not apply to any person who is parked in a timed parking zone while conducting business with any tradesworker, business person or professional person whose business is not affiliated with the business of the person so parked. It is the intent of this subsection to allow any person to use the timed parking area while doing business with the downtown merchants, tradesworkers and professionals. It is the intent of this section to prohibit use of the timed parking areas as a parking place for anyone in the pursuit of their own or their employer's trade, business or profession.

Sec. 78-101. - Definitions.
Junked motor vehicle means any motor vehicle which does not have lawfully affixed an unexpired license plate, and which is discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined or junked.
Motor vehicle means any vehicle which is designed to travel along the ground and shall include but is not limited to automobiles, buses, motorbikes, motorcycles, trucks, tractors, go-carts, campers and trailers.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-102. - Public nuisance.
No person shall have a junked motor vehicle within the city limits for more than 72 hours. Violation is declared a public nuisance which may be abated as such in accordance with the provisions of this article. This section shall not apply to any vehicle totally enclosed within a building on private property or to any vehicle held in connection with a business enterprise, lawfully licensed by the city, and properly operated in the appropriate condition specifically adopted or designed for operation on drag strips or raceways, or any vehicle retained for legitimate antique collection purposes.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-103. - Notice to owner.
If a violation exists, notice in writing shall be served upon the violator or his/her agent requesting removal of the junked motor vehicle within seven days of receipt of notice. The notice shall advise that upon failure to remove, the city or its designee shall undertake such removal, with the cost of removal to be levied against the violator.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-104. - Responsibility for removal.
Upon proper notice and opportunity to be heard, the violator shall remove the junked motor vehicle. In the event of removal and disposition by the city, the violator shall be liable for the expenses incurred.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-105. - Hearing.
A violator who disagrees with the notice may within six days of receipt thereof file a written request for hearing before the mayor and council. The hearing shall be held within 90 days after the filing of the request. The violator shall be advised of the time and place of the hearing at least five days in advance. At any such hearing the city and the violator may call and examine witnesses and present evidence, after which the mayor and council shall make a decision regarding the notice of violation.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-106. - Removal of vehicle.
If the violation has not been remedied within the seven-day period for compliance, or if the notice of violation is affirmed by the mayor and council, the city may cause the junked motor vehicle to be removed from the premises. It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with such removal. Within 48 hours of the removal of such vehicle, written notice shall be given to the violator and anyone else known to the city who has an ownership interest in the junked motor vehicle, that the vehicle has been impounded and stored. The notice shall give the location of the vehicle and the costs incurred by the city for removal.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-107. - Sale of vehicle.
Upon removing a vehicle the city may cause the junked motor vehicle to be sold in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 9-13-140 et seq. The notice of the sale shall state:
(1)
That the sale is of a junked motor vehicle in the possession of the city.
(2)
A description of the vehicle, including make, model and license number.
(3)
The terms of the sale.
(4)
The date, time and place of the sale.
(5)
Any other information required by state law.
The vehicle shall be sold to the highest and best bidder. At the time of payment, the city shall cause to be executed a certificate of sale in duplicate, the original for the purchaser, with the copy for the tax commissioner. Should the sale for any reason be invalid, the city's liability shall be limited to the return of the purchase price.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-108. - Redemption of vehicle.
The owner of any vehicle seized hereunder may redeem such vehicle prior to its sale or destruction upon proof of ownership and payment to the city of expenses of removal plus any advertising, all of such sums not to exceed $500.00 plus ten dollars per day storage.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-109. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction shall be subject to a fine of not more than $200.00. Each day of such violation shall be a separate offense.
(Amd. of 10-13-03, § 1)
Sec. 78-110. - Trucks over six wheels prohibited on roads designated: "No Through Trucks".
(a)
Trucks over six wheels are prohibited from using roads designated by the city manager as "No Through Trucks", which designation shall be ratified by vote of the mayor and council at the next council meeting after said designation by the manager. Such designation is permissible when such trucks are expressly found to be incompatible with the normal and safe movement of traffic in that area. The purpose of this restriction is to protect the public safety in the use of the city's streets, to facilitate and expedite traffic, and to protect the streets from unusual wear and damage. The mayor and council have control and supervision over its roads and streets. This prohibition is based on a traffic engineering investigation and recommendation.

The signage shall be in proper position and sufficiently legible to be seen by an ordinarily observant person. Any law enforcement officer or code enforcement officer to whom law enforcement authority has been designated who observes a motor vehicle being operated upon a public road of the state and who has reason to believe that any provision hereof is being violated is authorized to stop such vehicle and make investigation of such.
(c)
Exemption. ARTICLE VI. - UNLICENSED VEHICLES

Sec. 78-150. - Definitions.
Unlicensed vehicle means the following devices with the following definitions per the listed section of the O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1:
(15.5)
Electric assisted bicycle means a device with two or three wheels, which has a saddle and fully operative pedals for human propulsion and also has an electric motor. For such a device to be considered an electric assisted bicycle, it shall meet the requirements of the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards, as set forth in 49 C.F.R. section 571, et seq., and shall operate in such a manner that the electric motor disengages or ceases to function when the brakes are applied. The electric motor in an electric assisted bicycle shall:
(A)
Have a power output of not more than 1,000 watts;
(B)
Be incapable of propelling the device at a speed of more than 20 miles per hour on level ground; and
(C)
Be incapable of further increasing the speed of the device when human power alone is used to propel the device at or more than 20 miles per hour.
(25.1)
Low-speed vehicle means any four-wheeled electric vehicle whose top speed attainable in one mile is greater than 20 miles per hour but not greater than 25 miles per hour on a paved level surface and which is manufactured in compliance with those federal motor vehicle safety standards for low-speed vehicles set forth in 49 C.F.R. section 571.500 and in effect on January 1, 2001.
(28)
Moped means a motor driven cycle equipped with two or three wheels, with or without foot pedals to permit muscular propulsion, and an independent power source providing a maximum of two brake horsepower. If a combustion engine is used, the maximum piston or rotor displacement shall be 3.05 cubic inches (50 cubic centimeters) regardless of the number of chambers in such power source. The power source shall be capable of propelling the vehicle, unassisted, at a speed not to exceed 30 miles per hour (48.28 kilometers per hour) on level road surface and shall be equipped with a power drive system that functions directly or automatically only, not requiring clutching or shifting by the operator after the drive system is engaged.
(30)
Motor driven cycle means every motorcycle, including every motor scooter, with a motor which produces not to exceed five brake horsepower, every bicycle with a motor attached, and every moped.
Motor vehicle means every vehicle which is self-propelled other than the vehicles defined above.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-151. - Applicability.
The provisions hereof shall apply whenever an unlicensed vehicle is operated upon a highway, road or street or upon any path set aside for the use of unlicensed vehicles, subject to those exceptions stated in this part.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-152. - Traffic laws.
The provisions of this article that apply to motor vehicles, shall apply to unlicensed vehicles, except that the penalties prescribed in O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-390(b), 40-6-391(c) and 40-6-393(a) shall not apply to persons operating unlicensed vehicles.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-153. - Proper operation.
A person operating an unlicensed vehicle shall not ride other than upon or astride a permanent and regular seat attached thereto and shall allow no person to ride upon the handlebars. No unlicensed vehicle shall be used to carry more persons at one time than the number for which it is designed and equipped. No child under four years shall ride as a passenger on an unlicensed vehicle unless the child is securely seated according to the manufacturer's instructions and the seat is properly affixed according to the manufacturer's instructions.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-154. - Manner of operation.
Every person operating an unlicensed vehicle upon a road shall ride as near to the right side of the road as practicable, except when turning left or avoiding hazards, when the lane is too narrow to share safely with a motor vehicle, when traveling at the same speed as traffic, or while exercising due care when passing a standing vehicle or one proceeding in the same direction; provided, however, that every person operating an unlicensed vehicle shall exercise reasonable care and shall give due consideration to the other applicable rules of the road.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-155. - Carrying.
No person operating an unlicensed vehicle shall carry any package, bundle, or other article that prevents him from keeping both hands upon the handlebars or steering wheel.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-156. - Lights, brakes, handlebars and helmets.
(a)
Every unlicensed vehicle when in use at nighttime shall be equipped with a light on the front which shall emit a white light visible from a distance of 300 feet to the front and with a red reflector on the rear of a type approved by the department of motor vehicle safety which shall be visible from a distance of 300 feet to the rear when directly in front of lawful upper beams of headlights on a motor vehicle.
(b)
Every unlicensed vehicle sold or operated shall be equipped with a brake which will enable the operator to make the braked wheels skid on dry, level pavement.
(c)
No unlicensed vehicle shall be equipped or operated while equipped with a set of handlebars so raised that the operator must elevate his hands above his shoulders in order to grasp the normal steering grip area.
(d)
No person shall operate a two-wheeled unlicensed vehicle on a road, path, or sidewalk under without wearing a bicycle helmet which meets or exceeds the impact standards for bicycle helmets set by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) or the Snell Memorial Foundation. A person shall be deemed to wear a helmet only if a helmet of good fit is fastened securely upon the head with the straps of the helmet.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-157. - Reflectors.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or use an unlicensed vehicle unless equipped with reflectors of a type approved by the department of public safety. The reflectors shall be so designed and situated as to be visible from the front and rear of the unlicensed vehicle during darkness from a distance of 200 feet.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-158. - Limits on operation.
(a)
No person shall operate any unlicensed vehicle on a road unless the maximum speed limit of the roadway is 35 miles per hour or less.
(b)
When traveling on any road, a person operating an unlicensed vehicle shall travel in the same direction authorized for motor vehicle traffic on such road.
(c)
Any person operating an unlicensed vehicle shall exercise due care to avoid colliding with, and shall yield the right of way to, persons traveling on foot.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-159. - Sidewalks.
Unlicensed vehicles shall not be operated on any sidewalks.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-160. - Parking.
(a)
An unlicensed vehicle may not be parked on a sidewalk.
(b)
An unlicensed vehicle shall not be parked on any road as to prevent the movement of a legally parked motor vehicle.
(c)
Except as otherwise provided herein, any person operating an unlicensed vehicle shall be subject to the same parking restrictions as provided for motor vehicles. All violations of parking restrictions shall be deemed the responsibility of the owner of such device; and, for purposes of parking restrictions, the owner shall be deemed to be in control of the device at the time of a parking violation involving such device, and no evidence of actual control by such owner need be proven as an element of the offense.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-161. - DUI.
Any person who is under the influence of any intoxicating liquor or any drug to a degree which renders him or her a hazard shall not operate any unlicensed vehicle on any highway, road or street. Violation of this Code section shall be a misdemeanor, punishable upon conviction by a fine not to exceed $500.00.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-162. - Age.
No person under 15 years shall operate an unlicensed vehicle on any highway, road or street. No person shall operate such vehicle upon a highway, road or street unless he or she shall have in his or her possession a valid driver's license, instructional permit, or limited permit. No license or permit shall be required for the operation of an electric assisted bicycle.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-163. - Penalties.
Any person violating any of the provisions hereof shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction shall be subject to a fine of not more than $300.00, except as specifically enumerated otherwise.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Secs. 78-164—78-169. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VII. - PROHIBITED VEHICLES

Sec. 78-170. - Definition.
Prohibited vehicle means golf carts, similar motorized carts, all terrain vehicles (ATV's), Kawasaki Mules and similar, unlicensed motorcycles, scooters that do not meet Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards, miniature motorcycles, and go-karts.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-171. - Prohibition.
Prohibited vehicles shall not be operated on any public: streets, roads, highways, alleys, parking lots, sidewalks, parks or school grounds.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-172. - Penalties.
Any person violating any of the provisions hereof shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction shall be subject to a fine of not more than $300.00, except as specifically enumerated otherwise.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-173. - Juveniles.
Juveniles who violate 78-171 shall have their prohibited vehicles seized by law enforcement. The adult responsible for the juvenile may pick up the seized vehicle upon payment of costs actually accrued by law enforcement.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-174. - Liability.
The adult responsible for the juvenile shall be liable for any damages and/or injuries resulting from the violation of 78-171.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Secs. 78-175—78-179. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VIII. - SKATEBOARDS

Sec. 78-180. - Riding roller skates, roller blades or skateboards in the downtown tax district.
It shall be unlawful for any person to rollerskate, rollerblade or ride a skateboard on any public: streets, roads, highways, alleys, parking lots, sidewalks, parks or school grounds within the area designated as the downtown tax district.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-181. - Other prohibited uses.
(a)
Bicycles, skates, skateboards, roller blades and other similar devices must never be ridden on public property steps, stairways, access ramps, retaining walls, benches, railings or on similar architectural features.
(b)
The riding of bicycles and use of skates, skateboards, and roller blades in a reckless, disruptive, or unsafe manner is prohibited. A reckless, disruptive or unsafe manner is defined as riding at excessive speed and/or performing or practicing stunts; causing noise which disrupts people; riding with undue regard for the safety of others; or riding in a manner that causes damage to public property and landscaping.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
Sec. 78-182. - Punishment.
Any violation of this section may be punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00.
(Amd. of 3-14-05(2), § 1)
