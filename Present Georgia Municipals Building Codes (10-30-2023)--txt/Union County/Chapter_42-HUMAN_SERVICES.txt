ARTICLE II. - FUNERAL ASSISTANCE FOR INDIGENT RESIDENTS

Sec. 42-31. - Determination of eligibility.
(a)
A deceased person must have been a resident of the county at the time of his death to be eligible for a funeral at the county's expense under the provisions of this article.
(b)
An application for a funeral as an indigent at the county's expense must be made by a relative of the deceased person, and must be certified by the state department of family and children's services prior to burial of such indigent person.
(c)
If a person owns any property and/or personal possessions at the time of his death, the total value of which is $200.00 or more, such person shall be ineligible for a funeral at the county's expense.
(d)
If a person owns any property and/or personal possessions at the time of his death, the total value of which is less than $200.00, such person shall be eligible for assistance under this article if such person meets the other qualifications set forth in this article.
(e)
If a deceased's spouse or parent (if the deceased was a minor child) owns property and/or personal possessions, the total value of which exceeds $200.00, the person is ineligible for assistance under this article.
(Ord. No. O-2000-005, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 42-32. - Amount of funds available.
If a deceased person died owning property and/or personal possessions, the property and/or personal possessions shall first be applied to the cost of the burial of such person. Funds will be provided pursuant to this article in an amount so that, when the funds are added to the property and/or personal possessions owned by the deceased at the time of his death, a total not to exceed $300.00 in the case of an adult or infant will be available for a regular burial or $175.00 for a cremation.
(Ord. No. O-2000-005, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 42-33. - Administration.
(a)
The state department of family and children's services shall make arrangements with a funeral director to furnish a suitable casket, embalming services, hearse and funeral services at a cost of $300.00 for indigent persons buried at the county's expense.
(b)
The funeral director shall agree that he shall receive no funds from any of the deceased's family, estate, friends or other sources, except funds applied to the cost of the funeral in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(c)
If any additional funds are obtained by the funeral director, he shall deduct the amount of such funds received from the expense of the funeral or turn the funds over to the county.
(Ord. No. O-2000-005, 4-25-2000)
