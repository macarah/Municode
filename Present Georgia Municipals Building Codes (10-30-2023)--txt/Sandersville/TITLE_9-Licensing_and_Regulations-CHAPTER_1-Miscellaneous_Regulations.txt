ARTICLE A - Motor Fuel Dispensing Pumps[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Authority of City to license self-service motor fuel dispensing pumps, O.C.G.A., Sec. 36-60-1.

Sec. 9-1-1 - License required; fee.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation to construct, operate, or maintain within the City's corporate limits a motor fuel dispensing station with self-service motor fuel dispensing pumps without first obtaining a license.
(b)
The annual license fee for operating self-service motor fuel dispensing pumps shall be as fixed from time to time by the Mayor and Council.
(c)
This article does not apply to motor fuel dispensing units which are not open to the public.
(Ord. of 5/3/76, Secs. 1, 5)
Sec. 9-1-2 - Attendant required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to operate or maintain a self-service motor fuel dispensing pump unless an attendant is present at the business location.
(b)
The attendant shall be of sufficient mental and physical capacity so as to be able to provide aid to patrons of such self-service stations if needed or desired.
(Ord. of 5/3/76, Sec. 2)
Sec. 9-1-3 - Penalty provided.
Any person, firm or corporation violating the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in Section 1-1-8 of this Code. Each day such violation is committed or permitted to continue shall constitute a separate offense and shall be punishable individually.
(Ord. of 5/3/76, Sec. 3)
