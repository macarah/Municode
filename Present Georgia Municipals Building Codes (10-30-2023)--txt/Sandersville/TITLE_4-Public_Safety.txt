TITLE 4 - Public Safety
CHAPTER 1 - Law Enforcement
State Law reference— Powers of city concerning police protection, Ga. Const. 1983, Art. IX, Sec. II, Para. III; peace officers' standards and training, O.C.G.A., Sec. 35-8-1 et seq.; compensation of officers restricted, O.C.G.A., Sec. 36-30-9; tort liability of officers, O.C.G.A., Sec. 36-33-3; obstructing or hindering law enforcement officers, O.C.G.A., Sec. 16-10-24; subsistence allowance, O.C.G.A., Sec. 35-1-3.
Cross reference— Appointment of special police officers, Charter, Sec. 29; duty of police officers to preserve peace, Charter, Sec. 37; duties of police chief, Charter, Sec. 33; authority of mayor and council to regulate police force, Charter, Sec. 38; duty of police to enforce state law, Charter, Sec. 39.

Sec. 4-1-1 - Composition.
(a)
The police department shall consist of the chief of police and such other officers and personnel as the mayor and council shall prescribe.
(b)
The mayor and council shall elect one chief of police, one assistant chief of police, and as many additional police officers as in the judgment of mayor and council is proper and necessary. The salaries of these various officers shall be such as is fixed by the mayor and council by ordinance. All police officers shall be uniformed as the city council may, by committee or otherwise, direct.
Sec. 4-1-2 - Duties.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the police officers to preserve order in the city; to suppress riots, to arrest all drunk and disorderly persons and commit them to the city or county jail, or bring them before the municipal court to be dealt with as the evidence warrants; to arrest, with or without a warrant, all persons who have committed breaches of the criminal law of the city while in the city limits; to arrest persons guilty of offenses against the criminal laws of the city who are charged by warrant with criminal infractions; to patrol the city; to disperse illegal assemblages; and generally to obey all commands issued to them by the mayor and council through the laws, ordinances and charter of the city.
(b)
The chief of police and police officers of the city shall serve all notices, summons, subpoenas, orders, processes of any kind and execute all warrants issued by city authorities.
(c)
The police chief or assistant police chief shall attend all meetings of the mayor and council and all sessions of municipal court, to preserve order.
(Code 1941, Secs. 150, 152, 156)
Sec. 4-1-3 - Absence of police chief.
In the absence, disability, or disqualification of the police chief, the assistant police chief shall perform his duties; in case of absence, disability or disqualification of both the police chief and assistant police chief, any police officer of the city designated by the mayor shall perform his duties.
(Code 1941, Sec. 157)
Sec. 4-1-4 - Conduct generally.
(a)
All police officers shall be polite and attentive to members of the public and one another.
(b)
No police officer shall use vulgar, obscene or profane language while on duty.
(c)
No police officer shall report for or be on duty while under the influence of drugs or alcohol.
(Code 1941, Sec. 309)
Sec. 4-1-5 - Uniform.
All police officers shall be in full uniform while on duty, unless the officer's supervisor otherwise directs.
(Code 1941, Sec. 309)
Sec. 4-1-6 - Off duty officers; weapons.
Police officers may carry their badges and weapons while off duty or on suspension.
(Code 1941, Sec. 309)
Sec. 4-1-7 - Attendance.
All police officers must report for duty on time. Tardiness of 30 minutes will constitute failure to report. Failure to report may be punishable by suspension. Repeated offenses will be cause for dismissal.
(Code 1941, Sec. 309)
Sec. 4-1-8 - Acceptance of rewards and gifts.
Police officers shall not, directly or indirectly, accept from any person either liable to arrest, or complaint, or in custody, or after he has been discharged, or from any family members or friends of such person, any reward or gift.
Sec. 4-1-9 - Leaving post while on duty; sleeping on duty.
(a)
Police officers shall not leave their beat or post except for some police or personal necessity, and in such case shall notify the officer in charge of their whereabouts, giving the reason for leaving, and shall return as soon as possible.
(b)
Police officers shall not sleep on duty.
(Code 1941, Secs. 309, 310)
CHAPTER 2 - Fire Prevention and Protection
State Law reference— Powers concerning fire protection, Ga. Const. 1983, Art. IX, Sec. II, Para. III; powers of local fire departments generally, O.C.G.A., Sec. 25-3-1 et seq.; fire fighters' standards and training, O.C.G.A., Sec. 25-4-1 et seq.
Cross reference— Authority of mayor and council to establish fire department, Charter, Sec. 66; powers of mayor and council concerning fire prevention, Charter, Sec. 65.
ARTICLE A - Fire Department

Sec. 4-2-1 - Establishment.
A volunteer fire department, which shall be known as the Sandersville Fire Department, is hereby established.
(Code 1941, Sec. 230)
Sec. 4-2-2 - Composition.
The fire department shall be composed of such number of teams as deemed necessary from time to time by the mayor and council. Each team shall be headed by a lieutenant who shall be subject to the control and direction of the fire chief.
Sec. 4-2-3 - Fire chief; selection; duties.
(a)
The chief of the department shall be appointed by the mayor and council.
(b)
The chief shall have full authority and power to control and manage all three (3) companies; and each and every member thereof in case of fire or other cause of the department as may seem best to him; it shall be his duty to personally direct the movements of the department during fires or whenever the department is called out. He shall see that the companies keep all hose and reels entrusted to them in proper condition. It shall be his duty to do all things imposed on him by state law, local ordinance or directive of the mayor and council.
Sec. 4-2-4 - Firefighters; selection.
(a)
The members of the fire department shall be appointed by the mayor and council upon written application to city council; when a member is elected he shall be assigned to one of the three companies. The members shall receive only such compensation as the mayor and council grants.
(b)
The captain and foreperson of each company shall be appointed by the mayor and council upon nomination of the chief of the department.
Historical Note: Subsec. (b) is derived from Code 1941, Sec. 234.
Sec. 4-2-5 - Establishment of rules.
The rules and by-laws of the department shall be drawn up by the ordinance committee and approved by the mayor and council.
Sec. 4-2-6 - Fire department property; storage.
All hose, reels, and other property shall be kept in a building known as the fire department building.
Sec. 4-2-7 - Obstructing fire department.
Any person who shall be guilty of willfully interfering with, or in any way, obstructing or hindering any member when on duty or called out for drills shall be punished as prescribed in the code.
(Code 1941, Sec. 291)
Sec. 4-2-8 - Workers' compensation coverage.
All volunteer firefighters performing volunteer firefighter services rendered to and on behalf of the city shall be included under the definition of "employee" for workers' compensation purposes and will be classed as municipal employees to the extent of any accidents which occur to any such firefighters while engaged in firefighting activities on behalf of the city.
Secs. 4-2-9—4-2-20 - Reserved.
ARTICLE B - Fire Prevention Code and Safety Standards

Sec. 4-2-21 - Fire prevention code; adoption.
(a)
There is hereby adopted by the city for the purpose of prescribing regulations governing conditions hazardous to life and property from fire or explosion, that certain code known as the Standard Fire Prevention Code, as recommended by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc., being particularly the 1988 edition thereof, with all subsequent revisions and updated editions thereto, save and except such portions as are hereinafter deleted, modified or amended, a copy of which code is filed in the office of the city clerk, and the same is hereby adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein, and from the date on which this section shall take effect, the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the limits of the city; provided, however, that should any provisions of the fire prevention code as adopted hereby be in conflict with any other provisions of this code of ordinances, the provisions of this code of ordinances shall prevail and be controlling.
(b)
Wherever the word "municipality" is used in the code hereby adopted, it shall be held to mean the City of Sandersville.
(Ord. of 6/6/88)
Sec. 4-2-22 - Enforcement of code; modification of code.
(a)
The code hereby adopted shall be enforced by the chief of the fire department.
(b)
The chief of the fire department shall have power to modify any of the provisions of the code hereby adopted upon application in writing by the owner or lessee, or his duly authorized agent. In any modification, the spirit of the code shall be observed, public safety secured, and substantial justice done. The particulars of such modification when granted or allowed and the decision of the chief of the fire department thereon shall be entered upon the records of the department and a signed copy shall be furnished the applicant.
(c)
Whenever the chief of the fire department shall disapprove an application or refuse to grant a permit applied for, or when it is claimed that the provisions of the code do not apply or that the true intent and meaning of the code have been misconstrued or wrongly interpreted, the applicant may appeal from the decision of the chief of the fire department to the city council within 30 days from the date of the decision appealed.
(Ord. of 5/20/69, Secs. II, IV, V)
Sec. 4-2-23 - Violation of fire code; penalty.
(a)
Any person who:
(1)
violates any of the provisions of the code or fails to comply with the code;
(2)
violates or fails to comply with any order made pursuant to the code;
(3)
builds in violation of any detailed statement of specifications or plans submitted and approved or in violation of a certificate or permit issued where an appeal was not taken; or
(4)
fails to comply with an order as affirmed or modified by the city or by a court of competent jurisdiction;
shall be guilty of an offense.
(b)
The offense is punishable as provided in section 1-1-8 of this code. The imposition of one penalty for any violation shall not excuse the violation or permit it to continue; and all such persons shall be required to correct or remedy such violations or defects within a reasonable time; and when not otherwise specified, each 10 days that prohibited conditions are maintained shall constitute a separate offense.
(c)
The application of the above penalty shall not be held to prevent the enforced removal of prohibited conditions.
Sec. 4-2-24 - Removal of fire hazards.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to put in, install or maintain in any building in the city, electrical works, electrical wiring, or electrical apparatus of any other kind that is not approved by the fire prevention code adopted in section 4-2-21 or to make such installation in a different manner from that approved by the code.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the building inspector to inspect all such work and wiring before the current is turned on, and to make a report to the mayor of any defective work, apparatus or fittings, stating wherein they are defective and deficient.
(c)
The mayor shall cause to be served upon the owner of a defective building or apparatus a notice to be at the next meeting of city council, to which notice a copy of the building inspector's report shall be attached, requiring such person to show cause why the defective condition or apparatus or wiring should not be removed.
(d)
If no good cause is shown to the contrary, it shall be the duty of the police of the city to remove defective work, wires or fittings under direction of the building inspector at cost of the owner to be collected as provided in the laws of the city for removing obstructions from the streets.
CHAPTER 3 - Emergency Management
State Law reference— Emergency management responsibilities of local governments, O.C.G.A., Sec. 38-3-37.

Sec. 4-3-1 - Definition; participation in county-wide system.
(a)
As used in this chapter the term "emergency management" shall mean the preparation for and the carrying out of all emergency functions, other than the functions for which the state and federal governments are primarily responsible, to prevent, minimize, and repair injury and damage resulting from disasters caused by enemy nuclear attack, sabotage, or other hostile causes. These functions include, without limitations, fire-fighting services, law enforcement services, medical and health services, rescue services, engineering services, warning services, communications, defense from radiological fallout, welfare services, evacuation when and where necessary, emergency transportation, plant and utility protection, restoration of public utility services, and other functions relative to the protection and well-being of the population of the city, together with all other activities necessary or incidental to preparation for and the carrying out of the above functions.
(b)
The city hereby elects to participate in the county emergency management system and plan.
Sec. 4-3-2 - Liaison to county director.
The fire chief shall serve as liaison between the city and the county emergency management organization.
Sec. 4-3-3 - Emergency powers.
In the event of an actual enemy attach upon the United States or any other disaster which may affect the lives and property of the citizens of Sandersville, the mayor, or in his absence, his legally appointed successor, may declare that a state of emergency exists and thereafter shall have and may exercise for such period as such state of emergency exists or continues, the following emergency powers:
(1)
To enforce all rules, laws and regulations relating to emergency management and to assume direct operational control over all emergency management forces.
(2)
To seize, take for temporary use, or condemn any property for the protection of the public.
(3)
To sell, lend, give or distribute all or any such property or supplies among the inhabitants of the city, to maintain a strict accounting of property or supplies distributed and for funds received for such property or supplies.
(4)
To perform and exercise such other functions and duties, and take such emergency actions as may be necessary to promote and secure the safety, protection and well-being of the inhabitants of the city.
(Ord. of 8/9/71, Sec. III)
Sec. 4-3-4 - Volunteers.
All persons, other than officers and employees of the city, performing emergency functions pursuant to this chapter, shall serve with or without compensation. While engaged in such emergency functions, volunteers shall have the same immunities as city officers and employees.
(Ord. of 8/9/71, Sec. IV)
Sec. 4-3-5 - Penalties.
Any person violating any provision of this chapter, or any rule, order, or regulation made pursuant to this chapter, shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished as provided in section 1-1-8.
(Ord. of 8/9/71, Sec. V)
Sec. 4-3-6 - Liberality of construction.
This chapter shall be construed liberally in order to effectuate its purposes.
(Ord. of 8/9/71, Sec. VI)
CHAPTER 4 - National Incident Management System (NIMS)

Sec. 4-4-1. - Adopted.
The City of Sandersville hereby adopts the National Incident Management System (NIMS) as established under HSPD 5 and the Unified Command System as established under O.C.G.A. 38-3-57 as its system of preparing for and responding to disaster incidents and directs all incident managers and response organizations in the City of Sandersville train and exercise and use these systems in their response operations.
(Ord. of 10-4-2005)
CHAPTER 5 - Washington County Joint Pre-Disaster Mitigation Plan

Sec. 4-5-1. - Adopted.
The Washington County Joint Pre-Disaster Mitigation Plan is hereby adopted as the official plan of the City of Sandersville.
The respective officials identified in the strategy of the plan are hereby directed to implement the recommended actions assigned to them in the plan. These officials will report as directed in this plan on their activities.
The Washington County Joint Pre-Disaster Mitigation Planning Committee will provide progress reports (as directed in the plan) on the status of the implementation of the plan to the mayor and city council.
(Res. of 12-5-2005)
