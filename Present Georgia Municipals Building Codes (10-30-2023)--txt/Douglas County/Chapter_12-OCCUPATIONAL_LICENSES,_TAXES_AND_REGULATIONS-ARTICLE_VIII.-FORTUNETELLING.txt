ARTICLE VIII. - FORTUNETELLING

Sec. 12-120. - License required.
Any person desiring to engage in the business, trade or profession of fortunetelling, phrenology, astrology, clairvoyance, palmistry or other kindred practice where a charge is made or a donation accepted for such services within the unincorporated area of the county, before engaging in such business, trade or profession, shall obtain a license therefor and pay all required fees. Such applicant shall be accompanied by a certificate certifying to the good moral character of the applicant, signed by three (3) currently qualified and registered voters of good moral character in the county. The letters shall not be required for annual renewals of licenses issued hereunder.
Sec. 12-121. - Investigation.
Applicant must be fingerprinted by the county sheriff's department and a character reference check obtained on the applicant and all persons who operate the business. Fingerprints must be made at least ten (10) days prior to issuance of license to allow for investigation of applicant and employees. No fortunetelling license shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of any form of theft, robbery, burglary, deceptive practices or a crime involving moral turpitude.
Sec. 12-122. - Fee as established.
The applicant shall pay the business license fee and application fee established by the board of commissioners and on file in the business license office.
Sec. 12-123. - Procedure.
In addition to the foregoing, and where not inconsistent with the provisions hereof, each applicant shall comply with the provisions of Article I of this chapter.
