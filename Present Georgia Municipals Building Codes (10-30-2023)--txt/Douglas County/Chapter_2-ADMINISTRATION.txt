ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Exercise of governmental authority.
Except as otherwise provided in this Code or by state law, the governmental powers of the county shall be exercised by the board of commissioners in the manner provided by state law and by the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-15-77, §§ 3-101, 4-101)
Sec. 2-2. - Meetings of board of commissioners—Time; calling special meetings; open to public.
(a)
Regular meetings. The board of commissioners shall hold regular meetings on the first and third Tuesdays of each month, at 10:00 a.m., unless otherwise provided by resolution.
(b)
Special meetings. The chairman or any two (2) members of the board of commissioners may convene the board whenever the chairman or the two (2) members deem that the public business demands it.
(c)
Open to public. All meetings at which official actions are to be taken shall be open to the public.
(d)
Work sessions. The board of commissioners may conduct work sessions at its pleasure for the purpose of reviewing matters brought to its attention, which shall be open to the public.
(Ord. of 1-18-77(3), § 4-102)
State Law reference— Meetings to the public, O.C.G.A. § 38-80-1; "Sunshine" law, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.; Ga. Laws 1952, p. 2705, requires a meeting on the first Tuesday in each month.
Sec. 2-3. - Same—Rules of procedure.
Except as otherwise provided in this section, Roberts' Rules of Order shall govern the conduct of the board meetings.
(1)
Call to order. All meetings of the board of commissioners shall be open to the public. The chairman, or in the chairman's absence, the member designated by the chairman, or if not so designated, the member selected by the members present, or any other party designated by the board, shall take the chair at the hour appointed for any regular, temporarily adjourned, special or called meeting, and shall immediately call the board to order.
(2)
Attendance record. The county clerk shall enter in the minutes the names of commissioners who are present and those who are absent.
(3)
Quorum. A majority of all the members elected to the board of commissioners shall constitute a quorum at any regular or special meeting, and an affirmative vote of a majority of such number shall be sufficient to permit the conduct of all business.
(4)
Approval of minutes. Unless a reading of the minutes of a meeting is requested by a commissioner, such minutes may be approved without a reading if the county clerk has previously furnished each member with a copy not later than the Friday preceding the next meeting.
(5)
Reports by committees. Any business coming before the board may be referred to a committee of one (1) or more commissioners or to a department head for further study. It shall be the duty of each such committee to make a timely report on matters referred to it.
(6)
Citizen input.
a.
At voting meetings of the board of commissioners, any person who desires to address the board regarding an item germane to the agenda (meaning on the meeting agenda) during citizen's input, shall first register to do so at an area designated for such purpose at the meeting location, completing and submitting a speaker form to the county clerk at least ten (10) minutes prior to the announced starting time of the meeting. Upon recognition by the presiding officer to speak at the meeting, such person shall appear at the location designated by the presiding officer and provide their name and address for the record. Thereafter, the person so recognized to address the board shall have up to three (3) minutes to provide input on a matter germane to the agenda. Citizens desiring to provide public input to the board of commissioners on issues not germane to the agenda shall be directed to the board's work session for citizen's input. If such person addressing the board has printed materials to distribute to members of the board, such materials shall be submitted to the county clerk prior to the start of said meeting in order that distribution to the board of commissioners will not unduly delay the meeting. If such person wishes to distribute printed material to the general audience in attendance at the public meeting, such person shall place the materials in the area designated by the presiding officer in order to provide public access to the materials. Without the express permission of the presiding officer during the conduct of the meeting, all presentations will be closed after three (3) minutes. The presiding officer may establish time limits on public comments regarding an issue, provided time limits for differing opinions are equivalent. In the event of multiple people wishing to speak on a topic, it is suggested that people with similar views to express select one (1) representative to speak for all of them. No campaigning of any kind shall be permitted as public input under this procedure, and no campaign materials may be distributed at meetings of the board of commissioners.
b.
At non-voting work sessions of the board of commissioners, any person who desires to address the board regarding any item of public interest during citizen's input, shall first register to do so at an area designated for such purpose at the meeting location, completing and submitting a speaker form to the county clerk at least ten (10) minutes prior to the announced starting time of the meeting. Upon recognition by the presiding officer to speak at the meeting, such person shall appear at the location designated by the presiding officer and provide their name and address for the record. Thereafter, the person so recognized to address the board shall have up to three (3) minutes to provide input on any matter of public interest, subject to the limitations herein described. If such person addressing the board has printed materials to distribute to members of the board, such materials shall be submitted to the county clerk prior to the start of said meeting in order that distribution to the board of commissioners will not unduly delay the meeting. If such person wishes to distribute printed material to the general audience in attendance at the public work session, such person shall place the materials in the area designated by the presiding officer in order to provide public access to the materials. Without the express permission of the presiding officer during the conduct of the meeting, all presentations will be closed after three (3) minutes. The presiding officer may establish time limits on public comments regarding an issue, provided time limits for differing opinions are equivalent. In the event of multiple people wishing to speak on a topic, it is suggested that people with similar views to express select one (1) representative to speak for all of them. No campaigning of any kind shall be permitted as public input under this procedure, and no campaign materials may be distributed at work sessions of the board of commissioners.
(7)
Recording vote. The yeas and nays of the commissioners present shall be recorded on the minutes on any vote taken.
(Ord. of 1-18-77(3), §§ 4-103(a)—(c), 4-104(e)—(h); Ord. of 7-9-92; Ord. of 6-2-09)
Sec. 2-4. - Same—Adoption of ordinances, resolutions, contracts and agreements.
Unless otherwise provided in this Code, all ordinances, resolutions, contracts and agreements of the county shall be prepared, approved, introduced and adopted in the following manner:
(1)
Preparation. All ordinances shall be prepared by the county attorney. No ordinance shall be prepared for presentation to the board of commissioners unless ordered by a majority vote of the board, or requested in writing by the chairman, or by the county attorney on such attorney's own initiative.
(2)
Administrative staff approval. All ordinances, resolutions, contract documents and agreements, before presentation to the board, shall have been approved as to form and legality by the county attorney.
(3)
Introduction and adoption. Ordinances, resolutions and other matters or subjects requiring action by the board of commissioners must be introduced and sponsored by a member of the board, except that the county attorney may present ordinances, resolutions and other matters or subjects to the board, and any commissioner may assume sponsorship thereof by moving that such ordinances, resolutions, matters or subjects be adopted.
(Ord. of 1-18-77(3), § 4-104(a)—(c))
Sec. 2-5. - Composition of administrative services.
Any department or person under the control and supervision of the board of commissioners shall constitute the administrative service of the county.
(Ord. of 3-15-77, § 3-102)
Sec. 2-6. - Administrative policy and procedures.
(a)
Officers. Each officer shall perform all duties required of the office by state law and this Code, and such other duties not in conflict therewith as may be required by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Department heads. All department heads shall:
(1)
Be immediately responsible to the board of commissioners for the effective administration of their respective departments and all activities assigned thereto;
(2)
Keep informed as to the latest practices in their particular field and initiate, with the approval of the board of commissioners, such new practices as appear to be of benefit to the county and to the public;
(3)
Submit reports quarterly or as otherwise required by the board of commissioners of the activities of their respective departments to the board of commissioners;
(4)
Establish and maintain a system of filing and indexing records and reports in sufficient detail to furnish all information necessary for proper control of departmental activities and to form a basis for the periodic reports to the board of commissioners;
(5)
Prepare budgets annually, or as otherwise directed, as required by the board of commissioners;
(6)
Have power, when authorized by the board of commissioners, to appoint and remove, subject to personnel regulations, all subordinate employees; and
(7)
Be responsible for the proper maintenance of all county property and equipment used in their respective departments.
(c)
Departmental cooperation. Each department shall cooperate with every other department and shall furnish, upon the direction of the board of commissioners, any other department such service, labor and materials as may be requisitioned by the head of the department.
(d)
Open records. All county records, except those which by this Code, state law or court order are prohibited from being open to public inspection, shall be open for personal inspection by any citizen of the state during the hours of operation of the administrative service hereinbelow prescribed.
(e)
Operation of administrative service. All units in the administrative service shall:
(1)
Be open between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. on weekdays and shall be closed on Saturday, Sunday and legal holidays, except as otherwise directed by the board of commissioners; provided that any elected county official may extend the hours of operation of offices under such official's direct supervision when such official deems it reasonably necessary for the convenience of the public.
(2)
Make a daily deposit with the county depository designated by the board of commissioners of any monies received from the public.
(Ord. of 3-15-77, § 3-103)
Sec. 2-7. - Bonds.
Except as otherwise provided by law, the board of commissioners may require any department head, county official or employee, before entering upon the discharge of official duties, to give good and sufficient bond in any amount decided by the board of commissioners. Said bond shall be payable to the county for the faithful performance of said duties and to secure against corruption, malfeasance, misappropriation or unlawful expenditures in office. Said surety bonds shall be obtained from a surety company licensed to do business in the state and approved by the board of commissioners. The premiums thereon shall be paid by the county.
(Ord. of 3-15-77, § 3-104)
Sec. 2-8. - Open records policy.
The document attached to an ordinance of March 20, 2001, marked as Exhibit A is adopted as the county open records requests policy. The policy is not set out herein, but is on file and available for inspection in the office of the county clerk.
(Ord. of 3-20-01)
Sec. 2-9. - Official newspaper.
(a)
No journal or newspaper published in the county shall be declared, made, or maintained as the official organ of the county for the publication of sheriff's sales, citations of probate court judges, or any other advertising commonly known in terms of "official or legal advertising" and required by law to be published in such county official newspaper unless the newspaper shall meet and maintain the following qualifications:
(1)
"Newspaper" as used in this section means a printed product of multiple pages containing not greater than seventy-five (75) percent advertising content in no more than one-half (½) of its issues during the previous twelve (12) months, excluding separate advertising supplements inserted into but separately identifiable from any regular issue or issues of the newspaper;
(2)
The newspaper shall be published within the county and continuously at least weekly for a period of two (2) years or is the direct successor of such a newspaper. Failure to publish for not more than two (2) weeks in any calendar year shall not disqualify a newspaper otherwise qualified;
(3)
For a period of two (2) years prior to designation and thereafter, the newspaper shall have and maintain at least seventy-five (75) percent paid circulation as established by an independent audit. Paid circulation shall not include newspapers that are distributed free or in connection with a service or promotion at no additional charge to the ultimate recipient. For circulation to be considered paid, the recipient of the newspaper or such recipient's employer or household must pay reasonable and adequate consideration for the newspaper. No rules of circulation of audit companies, the United States Postal Service, or accounting principles may be considered in determining paid circulation if they are inconsistent with the provisions of this subsection;
(4)
Based on the published results of the 1990 United States decennial census or any future such census, the newspaper shall have and maintain at least the following paid circulation within the county to be designated the legal organ newspaper:
a.
Five hundred (500) copies per issue if the county population is less than twenty thousand (20,000);
b.
Seven hundred fifty (750) copies per issue if the county has a population of at least twenty thousand (20,000) but less than one hundred thousand (100,000); or
c.
One thousand five hundred (1,500) copies per issue if the county has a population of one hundred thousand (100,000) or greater.
(5)
For purposes of this section, paid circulation shall include home or mail delivery subscription sales, counter, vendor and news rack sales, and sales to independent newspaper contract carriers for resale. Paid circulation shall not include multiple copies purchased by one (1) entity unless the multiple copies are purchased for and distributed to the purchaser's officers, employees, or agents, or within the purchaser's household.
(b)
However, if the county has no journal or newspaper that meets the qualifications set forth in subsection (a) of this section, the official organ may be designated by the judge of the probate court, the sheriff, and the clerk of the superior court, a majority of these officers governing from among newspapers otherwise qualified to be a legal organ that meet the minimum circulation in the preceding subsection for the county, or if there is no such newspaper, then the newspaper having the greatest general paid circulation in the county.
(c)
Any selection or change in the official organ of the county shall be made upon the concurrent action of the judge of the probate court, the sheriff, and the clerk of the superior court of the county or a majority of the officers. No change in the official legal organ shall be effective without the publication for four (4) weeks of notice of the decision to make a change in the newspaper in which legal advertisements have previously been published. All changes in the official legal organ shall be made effective on January 1 unless a change has to be made where there is no other qualified newspaper.
(d)
Notwithstanding the other provisions of this section, an official organ of the county meeting the qualifications under the statute in force at the time of its appointment and which was appointed prior to July 1, 1999, may remain the official organ of the county until a majority of the judge of the probate court, the sheriff, and the clerk of the superior court determine to appoint a new official organ for the county.
(e)
During the month of December in each year, the judge of the probate court of the county shall notify the secretary of state, on a form supplied by the secretary of state, of the name and mailing address of the journal or newspaper currently serving as the official organ of the county. The judge of the probate court shall also likewise notify the secretary of state of any change in the official organ of the county at the time that such change is made. The secretary of state shall maintain at all times a current listing of the names and addresses of all county organs and shall make such list available to any person upon request.
(Ord. of 8-17-21, § 1)

ARTICLE II. - EMPLOYEE USE OF COUNTY-OWNED VEHICLES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---

Sec. 2-21. - Vehicles assigned to full-time, permanent employees only.
No vehicle may be assigned to any employee who is not a full-time, permanent employee of the county. A full-time, permanent employee of the county is one who is scheduled to work forty (40) hours per week, has been certified by the county personnel director as an employee.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 1)
Sec. 2-22. - License requirements; driving record; assignment to employee residing outside county.
Each employee assigned the use of a county vehicle must have a valid Georgia State drivers license without restriction and must demonstrate to the satisfaction of the department head that such employee is a competent operator of the vehicle of the type and description to be used by such employee. The employee must also have a driving record which, though, perhaps, including instances of vehicular collisions in the past, nonetheless, shows the ability to responsibly and capably handle the vehicle of the type and description to be assigned to such employee. No vehicles may be assigned to an employee who resides outside of Douglas County, Georgia, unless it is shown that such employee is subject to be called to duty outside of normal operating hours. For purposes of this article, normal working hours are 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday of each week, excluding holidays.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 2)
Sec. 2-23. - Return of vehicle; exception.
All vehicles assigned to employees shall be returned to a designated location of county property at the conclusion of each normal working day, unless the employee is authorized to take the vehicle to such employee's private residence.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 3)
Sec. 2-24. - Taking vehicle to employee's private residence outside of normal working hours.
No employee shall be authorized to take any county vehicles to such employee's private residence outside of normal working hours unless such employee is subject to be called to return to perform functions in the course of such employee's employment for the county outside of regular working hours.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 4)
Sec. 2-25. - Use of county vehicles for personal purposes.
No county vehicles may be used by any employee for personal purposes at any time. Transportation to and from place of employment to the residence of employee shall not be considered personal use.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 5)
Sec. 2-26. - Responsibility for vehicle.
Any vehicle assigned to an employee shall be the responsibility of that employee, and the employee shall assure that the vehicle receives routine maintenance, is properly fueled, and is operated in a safe and responsible manner. Maintenance on such vehicle shall be performed by county personnel at the county maintenance facility.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 6)
Sec. 2-27. - Abuse of privileges.
The abuse of this article by any employee may result in the termination of the privileges of using such vehicle and may further subject the employee to disciplinary action pursuant to the personnel regulations and merit system of the county.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 7)
Cross reference— Personnel, Ch. 13; civil service system, § 13-14 et seq.
Sec. 2-28. - Use of vehicle constituting income for federal income tax purposes.
To the extent that the use of county-owned vehicles by any employee constitutes income for federal income tax purposes, each employee shall solely bear full and complete responsibility for reporting such income and paying any and all taxes due in connection therewith.
(Ord. of 5-16-89, § 8)
Secs. 2-29—2-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - COUNTY MANAGER[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— A resolution of March 2, 1993 repealed former §§ 2-41—2-46 in their entirety and added a new §§ 2-41—2-46. Former §§ 2-41—2-46 pertained to similar subject matter and derived from ordinances of June 6, 1989; November 21, 1989; and July 21, 1992.

Sec. 2-41. - Office created; appointed.
There is hereby created the office of county manager of Douglas County, hereinafter referred to as "manager".
(Res. of 3-2-93)
Sec. 2-42. - Qualifications.
The manager shall be appointed or may be removed by majority vote of the entire board. The manager shall receive such compensation as prescribed annually by the board. The manager shall serve at the pleasure of the board. The board may designate a person or persons to perform on a temporary basis the functions and duties of the manager during his absence, disability, or suspension. No member of the board of commissioners shall be appointed county manager during the term of office for which he or she is elected or for a period of one (1) year after leaving office as a commissioner.
(Res. of 3-2-93)
Sec. 2-43. - Experience.
The manager shall be chosen by the board, upon the basis of his executive and administrative qualifications with special reference to his actual experience in and knowledge of the duties of office as set forth in this article.
(Ord. of 2-21-17, § 1)
Editor's note— An ordinance adopted February 21, 2017, § 1, repealed former § 2-43, and enacted a new § 2-43. Former § 2-43 pertained to similar subject matter and derived from a resolution of March 2, 1993.
Sec. 2-44. - Duties.
The board of commissioners of the county shall continue to be the chief executive entity of the county, and it shall be the duty of the board to set the overall executive policies and procedures of the county. The manager shall be the chief administrative officer of the county, fully responsible to the chairman and other members of the board for the proper and efficient administration of all the affairs of the county, including all of the day to day operations of the county except as otherwise provided by law. Specifically, it shall be the duty of the manager:
(1)
To appoint and remove all department heads in the administrative service of Douglas County government with the express advice and consent of the board, as provided for by this article and in compliance with the Douglas County merit system or personnel rules adopted by the county, except the county attorney and county clerk; who shall be appointed and removed only by the board;
(2)
To supervise the administration of all departments, offices and agencies of the county or any that may be created except as otherwise provided by law or ordinance with the advice of the board;
(3)
To submit to the board the annual operating budget for the county, specifically including a capital improvement budget, which has been prepared by the budget/fiance officer. Also, the manager shall obtain from all department heads and local constitutional officers their proposed annual budgets;
(4)
To submit to the board and make available to the public a complete report on the finances and administrative activities of the county as of the end of each fiscal year and at such other times as the board may direct;
(5)
To prepare and make such other reports concerning the operations of departments, offices and agencies of the government as may be required by the board;
(6)
To keep all commissioners fully advised as to the financial condition and needs of the county and make such recommendations to the board concerning the affairs of the county as he deems desirable;
(7)
To confer with and assist the directors of all departments, such as the department of health and department of family and children services, whose responsibilities and activities are not under direct control and jurisdiction of the commissioners, but who are dependent upon appropriations therefrom, and provide general liaison between the commissioners and all departments, boards, commissions and employees of the county;
(8)
To confer with and advise all elected and appointed officials of the government who are not under the immediate control or jurisdiction of the commissioners, but who receive financial support therefrom, including but not limited to the clerk of the superior court, judge of the probate court, sheriff and tax commissioner;
(9)
To examine regularly accounts, records and operations of every board, commission, authority, department, office and agency which receive appropriations from the Commissioners;
(10)
To see that the ordinances, resolutions and regulations of the board and the laws of the state are forth fully executed and enforced;
(11)
The manager shall not engage in or be concerned with local partisan politics or any local political campaign. He shall not contribute to any local campaign fund or solicit funds for local political purposes from any other person. He shall not be eligible for election as a member of the board of commissioners of Douglas County for a period of one (1) year after termination of his service as county manager.
(Res. of 3-2-93)
Sec. 2-45. - Supervision of all departments by board of commissioners; additional duties of manager.
The board of commissioners shall supervise and direct the administration of all departments, offices and agencies of the county, solely through the chairman of the board. The chairman may delegate or designate the manager to act in his stead in such administrative matters as the chairman determines are necessary.
The manager shall attend all meetings of the board and such other meetings as directed by the chairman. The manager shall perform such other duties as may from time to time be required by the chairman.
The manager shall further have the responsibility of coordinating with department heads after consultation with the board rules for regulating purchases by the county and setting forth specific limitations on authority for other individuals and providing for the securing of bonds for performance of services.
The manager shall with the concurrence of the chairman have the authority to transfer funds within any department budget among personnel operations or capital improvement categories up to a maximum of five thousand dollars ($5,000.00). All proposed transfers in excess of that amount shall require the consent of a majority of the board.
(Res. of 3-2-93)
Sec. 2-46. - Bond.
The manager shall be required to execute and deliver a good and sufficient bond payable to the county to be approved by the board and conditioned upon the faithful performance of his duties. The amount of said bond shall be determined by the board. Said bond shall be delivered to the board within five (5) business days after the manager assumes his duties, and upon receipt, it shall be filed by the chairman of the board in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Douglas County, Georgia.
(Res. of 3-2-93)
Secs. 2-47—2-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - JUDGE OF THE JUVENILE COURT[4]

Editor's note— The provisions of a resolution, adopted Dec. 19, 1989, have been included herein at the discretion of the editor as Art. VI, §§ 2-61—2-64.

Sec. 2-61. - Office created; appointment, term.
There is hereby created the office of judge of the juvenile court. Such office shall be effective January 1, 1990. The office of judge of juvenile court of Douglas County shall be a full-time judicial position. The judge of said court shall be selected in the manner and for the term provided by statutory law as set forth in the Official Code of Georgia.
(Res. of 12-19-89)
Sec. 2-62. - Duties.
(a)
The said judge shall have such duties and authority as is set forth in the Juvenile Court Code of Georgia.
(b)
The judge shall assist the Superior Court of the Douglas Judicial Circuit by presiding in such matters as may be assigned by the judges thereof pursuant to the Official Code of Georgia Annotated Section 15-1-9.1 or other lawful authority.
(c)
The judge shall be prohibited from engaging in the private practice of law except as the same shall involve carrying out the duties as judge or in defending any action brought against him/her.
(Res. of 12-19-89)
Sec. 2-63. - Compensation.
(a)
The initial annual compensation of the judge shall be as provided in the budget of the county governing authority for the year 1990.
(b)
Compensation shall be increased at such times as increases incur in the compensation of Douglas County Merit System employees and in the same percentage amount as the increases for such employees, provided that such increases are approved by written order of the judge or judges of the Superior Court as are by law responsible for setting the salary of the judge of the juvenile court at the time of such increase.
(Res. of 12-19-89)
Sec. 2-64. - Effective date.
This article shall take effect concurrent with an order of the judges of the Superior Court relating to the provisions hereof and evidencing the consent of the sitting judge of the juvenile court to the restrictions and duties provided in this section succeeding judges of the juvenile court of the county shall likewise consent in writing to restrictions and duties provided for in this article prior to assuming the office created herein.
(Res. of 12-19-89)
