Sec. 6-211. - Adoption of plumbing code.
A certain document, one (1) copy of which is on file in the office of the city clerk, being marked and designated as the 2012 International Plumbing Code, adopted by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc., is hereby adopted as the plumbing code of the city; all of the regulations, provisions, conditions and terms of this Standard Plumbing Code are hereby referred to, adopted and made a pert of this article, as though fully set out at length herein, with the additions, insertions, deletions and changes as are prescribed in section 6-212 of this article.
(Code 1977, § 9-1151; Ord. No. 43-89, § 3, 8-21-89; Ord. No. 45-91, § 4, 8-19-91; Ord. No. 52-93, § 1, 10-4-93; Ord. No. 62-04, 12-20-04; Memo. of 7-12-11; Ord. No. 07-14, § 1, 3-17-14)
Editor's note— The user's attention is directed to the editor's note at the title of this chapter wherein the city's intention of enforcing the latest edition of the International Plumbing Code is set forth.
State Law reference— Buildings and housing, § 8-2-20 et seq.
Sec. 6-212. - Additions, insertions, deletions and changes.
(a)
Section 102 of the Standard Plumbing Code is deleted and wherever the words "plumbing official" are used in the code they shall mean building inspection department.
(b)
The words "administrative authority" and the words "local governing body" as used in this code shall mean the mayor and council.
(c)
The words "department of law" as used in the code shall mean the city attorney.
(d)
The words "plumbing inspection department" shall mean the building inspection department.
(e)
Plastic pipe is hereby approved for water mains and service lines.
(f)
Backwater connection valves shall be required on all residential building structures constructed within the city and connected to the city's sanitary sewer system. These shall contain forty-five (45) degree long sweeps with clean-out valves on either side of the backwater valve.
(Code 1977, § 9-1152; Ord. No. 35-96, § 1, 5-20-96)
Sec. 6-213. - Inspection fees.
Inspection fees shall be charged for the installation and/or removal and relocation of fixtures, according to the following schedule:
(1)
Commercial: Five dollars ($5.00) per fixture for commercial projects (including floor drains).
(2)
Residential:
Five dollars ($5.00) per fixture for an addition (not to exceed twenty dollars ($20.00)).
Fifty dollars ($50.00) per living unit (new single-family dwellings).
Fifty dollars ($50.00) per living unit (multi-family dwellings).
(3)
Rehab projects: Four dollars ($4.00) per fixture.
(4)
Additions: Five dollars ($5.00) per fixture (not to exceed fifty dollars ($50.00)).
(5)
Heating and air conditioning:
Commercial and industrial:
a.
One (1) to fifteen (15) tons: Nine dollars and seventy-five cents ($9.75) per ton (15 × $9.75 = $146.25).
b.
Sixteen (16) tons or more: Five dollars and twenty cents ($5.20) per ton (16 × $4.00 = $83.20).
New single-family dwellings: Fifty dollars ($50.00).
Add to existing home: Thirty dollars ($30.00).
Change out old system: No charge.
(Code 1977, § 9-1153; Ord. No. 37-90, § 1, 6-18-90; Ord. No. 37-92, § 1, 8-3-92; Ord. No. 56-93, § 2, 10-18-93; Ord. No. 12-97, § 1, 1-21-97; Ord. No. 54-01, 8-20-01; Ord. No. 76-02, §§ 5, 6, 11-18-02; Ord. No. 27-04, §§ 5, 6, 6-21-04; Ord. No. 24-11, § 1, 7-18-11)
Sec. 6-214. - Pipe required to be used.
All outside building sewer lines within the city shall be required to use Schedule 40 ASTM D2 665 plastic pipe and fittings or equivalent after June 18, 1990.
(Ord. No. 37-90, § 5, 6-18-90; Ord. No. 46-91, § 1, 8-19-91)
