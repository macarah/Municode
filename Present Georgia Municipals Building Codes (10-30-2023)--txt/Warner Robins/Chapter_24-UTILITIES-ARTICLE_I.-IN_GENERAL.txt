ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Definition.
In this article "utility service" or "utility" means the city's gas, water, sewage collection and disposal or solid waste collection and disposal systems or services, or any combination of same.
Sec. 24-2. - Services outside the city; conditions.
(a)
Gas, water, sewer. No water, sewer or gas service shall be furnished to property outside the city until the following items have been complied with and approved by the mayor and council:
(1)
Approval of preliminary plans. Approval of preliminary plans for subdivision by the appropriate governmental authority which plan shall include the following:
a.
Water system including size of main;
b.
Sanitary sewer system including all trunk lines and lateral lines;
c.
Proposed street names;
d.
Type of street paving;
e.
Provision for adequate curbs and gutters;
f.
Installation of storm sewers where needed; and
g.
Proposed playground or park area; if any.
(2)
Contract required. Before any water, sewage or gas utilities will be furnished to properties outside the city, it shall be necessary that the city and contractor enter into a contract relative to the accomplishment of the items heretofore outlined.
(b)
Utilities.
(1)
When a property owner requests utility services to property not within the corporate limits of the city, at the discretion of the city, the owner will be required to:
a.
Petition for annexation (if the property is contiguous to the existing limits of the city) prior to commencement of construction of utility lines; or
b.
Execute a restrictive covenant consenting to annexation of such property into the city when the property becomes contiguous.
(2)
When such property is not contiguous and execution of the covenant is required, the owner will also be required to submit a certificate of title to the city attorney's office to verify ownership of the property. The covenant will be recorded in the property records at the county courthouse and will stipulate that if the property becomes contiguous in the future and the property owner refuses to petition for annexation, the city will have the authority to terminate utility services.
(3)
Construction of utility lines will not begin until the requirements of this policy are met, which shall be indicated by authorization of the mayor.
Any connection of utility services to a property outside the corporate limits of the city must be approved in advance by a formal vote of the mayor and council.
(Code 1977, §§ 5-1002, 5-1002.1; Ord. No. 2-13, § 1, 2-19-13; Ord. No. 17-16, § 1, 8-1-16; Ord. No. 59-17, § 1, 12-14-17)
Sec. 24-3. - Rules, regulations for extension of water, sewer service.[2]
The city shall furnish water and sewer service to all users within economical reach of its water and sewer system in accordance with the following rules and regulations:
(1)
Connection fee. All applications or contracts for service must be accompanied by the required connection fee before the connection will be made.
(2)
Extensions to one hundred (100) feet.
a.
To properties lying within the city limits. The city shall make necessary extensions of supply mains or sewers up to but not in excess of one hundred (100) feet, and will provide the required service line to property lines of customers. Exceptions to this provision may be made by the mayor in his/her discretion provided:
1.
i.
The affected properties are within the city limits; or
ii.
The affected properties are planned for annexation in accordance with a binding agreement between a developer/owner and the city; and
2.
The total cost of the extension does not exceed two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500.00) for the sewerage lines and two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500.00) for water lines. Any other exception to this policy must be evidenced by a written contract between the city and the developer/owner and approved by the mayor and council as is required by Charter for approval of all contracts.
b.
To properties lying outside the city limits. The developer/owner shall be responsible for the full costs of any such extension.
(3)
Service lines. Service lines on customers' property must be furnished by customers.
(4)
Extensions in excess of one hundred feet; expenses; refunds. Extensions for water or sewer service in excess of one hundred (100) feet will be made for builders, developers or individuals only at their expense. The cost of these extensions must be paid at the time application or agreement for service is executed. Amounts advanced for these extensions may be refunded in accordance with the terms of the agreement but not in excess of fifty dollars ($50.00) for each customer connected to the line provided for the service. Refunds will be due and payable one (1) year from the date of agreement and annually thereafter until the amount advanced has been refunded, but not for a period in excess of five (5) years from the date of the agreement.
(5)
Service to builders, subdividers, developers, conditions. The city will supply water and sewer service to builders, subdividers or developers when the facilities, exclusive of water meters, are constructed in accordance with plans and specifications approved by the city and paid for by the developers. Ownership of these facilities must be transferred to the city free and clear of all liens before connections are made and the city will operate and maintain the facilities thereafter as a part of the municipal water and sewer systems.
(6)
Adjusting minimum charges. In cases where extensions are requested to service existing residences, but immediate revenues are less than twenty (20) percent of the cost of the extensions, the minimum charge for water or sewer service may be increased to justify the investment in the extension. These increased rates shall be reduced annually in direct proportion to the number of new customers added to the extended main but not less than the minimum rate established by the applicable schedule of rates then in effect.
(7)
Sale of water for resale. The city shall not sell water to any customer for resale to others, except to other governmental agencies.
(8)
Other extensions. Extensions not covered specifically by the foregoing regulations shall be made in general conformity to the overall policies established herein to the end that all transactions are fair and equitable to all customers of the city water and sewer department.
(Code 1977, § 5-2002; Ord. No. 45-93, § 1, 8-2-93; Ord. No. 23-94, § 1, 4-18-94; Ord. No. 32-94, § 6, 7-18-94)

Sec. 24-4. - Unlawful use of city utilities; penalty.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to connect to the water, sewer or gas utilities systems of the city without first complying with all the rules, regulations and ordinances now applicable to tap-in, connection or service fees, and by placing the proper deposit for each of the services.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to reconnect to the public utility system of the city when the city has disconnected the utilities for nonpayment for services or utilities rendered.
(c)
A violation of the above shall constitute a continuing daily crime against the city and shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) or by imprisonment in the city jail for a period of not more than one (1) year, or both, for each offense.
(d)
The city reserves the right to disconnect any and all utilities furnished by the city if the applicant is in a delinquent status with the city for utilities furnished.
(Code 1977, § 5-1004)
Sec. 24-5. - Connecting or tampering with water and sewer mains.
(a)
All meters, curb cocks, valves and meter boxes and other apparatus connected with the city's water and sewer mains and service pipes shall remain under the direct control of the utilities department and it shall be unlawful for persons other than those authorized by the utilities director to connect, disconnect or tamper with any such meter, or to turn off the water at the curb cock, valve or meter, or to open, move or tamper with any meter box or other apparatus connected with the city's water and sewer mains and lines.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, other than an employee of the utilities department in the performance of his duties as such, to tap into or make any opening in any street in connection with the water or sewer mains or lines of the city, and any such taps or connections that require cutting in a public street shall comply with the provisions of chapter 23, article II.
(Code 1977, § 5-1005)
Sec. 24-6. - Utility service charges.
(a)
When city employees disconnect gas and/or water meters at the request of the customer, service charges of thirty-five dollars ($35.00) shall be required for disconnection of the service and thirty-five dollars ($35.00) for the reconnection. In the discretion of the mayor or his designee, these charges may be waived for emergency or system repairs. Repeat trips to connect utility services, for the same work order, shall require a twenty-dollar service charge per trip.
(b)
When city employees make a service trip to disconnect gas and/or water meters for delinquent bills a thirty-five-dollar service charge shall be applied.
(c)
A service charge of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) shall be required for a return trip, by city employees, to reread a utility meter when done at the customers request or due to inaccessibility to the meter because of a locked gate, shrubberies blocking meters and unfriendly pets, etc.
(d)
Any check in payment for utility services returned because of insufficient funds, closed accounts, etc., shall be subject to a thirty-dollar handling fee.
(e)
Transfer of gas and/or water utility service from one (1) location to another shall be subject to a twenty-five-dollar transfer fee.
(f)
A service charge of sixty-five dollars ($65.00) shall be charged for the connection of service between the hours of 7:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m., Monday through Sunday, including holidays.
(Code 1977, §§ 5-1001(c), 5-1006, 5-1008; Ord. No. 42-90, § 1, 7-2-90; Ord. No. 21-93, § 1, 3-15-93; Ord. No. 55-93, § 1, 10-18-93; Ord. No. 24-95, § 1, 4-17-95; Ord. No. 20-98, § 1, 3-2-98; Ord. No. 37-10, § 1, 8-16-10)
Sec. 24-7. - Bills; penalty; disconnection.
(a)
Bills to customers for utility services shall be mailed out on such day or days of each month as may be determined to be desirable and the amount due shall be payable in ten (10) days from the date thereof, and if not paid within fifteen (15) days of the date bills are mailed, a penalty of ten (10) percent of the amount of the bill shall be added thereto and collected. If the amount of the bill is not paid within twenty (20) days after the date the bill is rendered, all utility services shall be disconnected unless a prior agreement has been reached between the city and the utility customer.
(b)
The mayor, at his discretion, may authorize the utility department to continue service for new owners of property where a lien exists if the new owners agree to an installment payment schedule with a period not to exceed twelve (12) months. Where the responsible party does not meet the agreed payment schedule services shall be discontinued.
(Code 1977, § 5-1001(a), (b); Ord. No. 23-96, § 1, 4-1-96; Ord. No. 05-17, § 1, 2-21-17)
Sec. 24-8. - Accessibility of meters.
(a)
Utility customers shall maintain the area immediately surrounding meters free of debris, shrubbery, weeds, or tall grass so as to make such meter readily accessible to utility service personnel. Customers who do not meet the above requirements shall be subject to a thirty dollar ($30.00) administrative charge for each meter reading where utility service personnel find a violation, to compensate for additional efforts to read the meter. The customer shall be notified by first class mail that the requirements of this section have not been met, and such notice shall provide a minimum of ten (10) days' notice to the customer of the department's intention to impose the administrative charge at the next meter reading if the obstruction has not been removed.
(b)
(1)
It shall be unlawful to obstruct, damage, or hide any water meter, or other water recording device, in such a manner that the meter reader or other employee of the utility department is unable to gain access. If the obstruction remains in place for two (2) days following notice of the violation, the owner, or person in legal control of the property, will be responsible for the administrative charge set forth in (a) above and any cost incurred by the department for the purpose of removing the obstruction.
(2)
Repeated violations of this article will subject the customer to the maximum penalties set forth in section 1-7 of the City Code.
(Code 1977, § 5-1001(d); Ord. No. 26-18, § 1, 8-6-18)
Sec. 24-9. - Collection of delinquent water bills.
(a)
The mayor and council may enforce the collection of delinquent charges for natural gas sales, water, sewerage, and sanitation services, by execution to be issued by the city clerk against the owners of the premises and such other persons as may be liable therefor, when such rents and charges are due and remain unpaid for a period of twenty (20) days. The execution shall become a lien upon the premises when recorded in the general execution docket of the county and shall be collected as a lien for city taxes.
(b)
The mayor may, at his discretion, cause services, to present or subsequent owners, to be discontinued until the unpaid rents or charges are paid, or take other steps that he considers necessary to collect the delinquent charges or rents.
(Code 1977, § 5-1011; Ord. No. 16-04, § 1, 4-5-04)
Sec. 24-10. - [Activation fee.]
(a)
For each new account established for city provided water and/or sewer service for residential customers, a non-refundable activation fee shall be charged on a one-time basis. This activation fee shall be set at sixty-five dollars ($65.00) per account. For each new account established for city provided water and/or sewer utility service for commercial customers, a non-refundable activation fee shall be charged on a one-time basis. This activation fee shall be set at eighty dollars ($80.00) per account.
This activation fee will not be charged if one (1) or more of the below conditions exist:
(1)
The customer is a local governmental entity, State of Georgia entity or federal agency.
(2)
The customer is relocating from another city service location and no service/payment issue(s) have occurred within the prior twenty-four (24) months.
(3)
The customer is a landlord/rental agent providing service on an account for no more than thirty (30) days.
(b)
For mobile home parks, apartment complexes and multihousing units, an additional thirty-five-dollar activation fee shall be charged for each mobile home or apartment unit.
(c)
Notwithstanding any of the above provisions, the owner of any multiple-family dwelling which is served by a single water meter shall be responsible for the payment of any and all activation fees required under this section and for payment of the water bill for the entire dwelling. In the alternative, at the election of the property owner, such owner shall be entitled to install, at such owner's sole expense, separate water meters to any or each of the units contained in such dwelling. For purposes of this section, multiple-family dwelling shall be defined as any single structure which contains separate and distinct living areas for two (2) or more family units.
(d)
In the event any utility account becomes delinquent, the city shall, in the sole discretion of the mayor, or his/her designee, be authorized to require a security deposit of an amount equal to the sum of the total utility bills for the three (3) highest months within the year immediately preceding such delinquency. For purposes of this section only, an account shall be delinquent when it has, on two (2) or more occasions, remained unpaid on the twentieth day after billing and penalties have been assessed.
(e)
For each new account established for city provided natural gas utility service for residential customers, a non-refundable activation fee shall be charged on a one-time basis. This activation fee shall be set at eighty-five dollars ($85.00) per account. For each new account established for city provided natural gas utility service for commercial customers, a non-refundable activation fee shall be charged on a one-time basis. This activation fee shall be set at one hundred dollars ($100.00) per account.
(f)
Pilot lights.
(1)
Except as provided below, a twenty-five-dollar fee shall be charged for the initial lighting of a pilot light by city employees. Any lighting of additional pilot lights on the same service call shall require the payment of a ten-dollar service fee per lighting.
(2)
The fees for lighting a pilot light shall not be required when gas service has been interrupted by the city due to emergency or normal gas line maintenance.
(3)
The fees for lighting pilot(s) shall not be required for new gas account customers.
This activation fee will not be charged if one (1) or more of the below conditions exist:
a.
The customer is a local governmental entity, State of Georgia entity or federal agency.
b.
The customer is relocating from another city service location and no service/payment issue(s) have occurred within the prior twenty-four (24) months.
(g)
For each new account established for a combination of city provided water, sewer and natural gas utility service for a single residential location/customer, a non-refundable activation fee shall be set at one hundred twenty dollars ($120.00). For each new account established for a combination of city provided water, sewer and natural gas utility service for a single commercial location/customer, a non-refundable activation fee shall be set at one hundred forty-four dollars ($144.00).
(Ord. No. 37-10, § 1, 8-16-10)
Sec. 24-11. - Tampering with utility equipment and theft of utility services.
(a)
The following practices or acts are hereby declared to be illegal and are prohibited by the mayor and council of the City of Warner Robins.
(1)
The cutting, removing or in any manner making ineffective any seal, locking band or lock on an electrical or natural gas meter.
(2)
Restoration of service by any means after service has been terminated for nonpayment, or obtaining electricity or natural gas, without making the proper deposit with a utility company or receiving proper authorization from the respective utility company.
(3)
Obtaining electricity or natural gas by use of jumper wires, or by any other means which bypass a metering device either partially or completely.
(4)
Damaging or tampering in any manner with any part of a metering device belonging to a utility company.
(5)
Changing or altering the normal installed position of a metering device in any fashion which causes the normal accurate recording of utility service received to be altered and inaccurate.
(6)
Obtaining electricity or natural gas by use of a metering device which is not authorized by a utility company.
(7)
Interfering with the automatic registration, recording and transmission of electricity or natural gas consumption when readings are recorded and/or transmitted electronically.
(8)
Fraudulently obtaining, or attempting to obtain service from a utility company by using a false name or identification.
(9)
Fraudulently obtaining, or attempting to obtain service from a utility company by placing the account in the name of someone else after the service has been disconnected for nonpayment or theft of service while the person in whose name the service was in when disconnected is still living at the location of the service; the utility company may refuse or discontinue service to an applicant in this case.
(10)
Any other reception of electrical power or natural gas service without proper authorization from the respective utility company.
(b)
Alleged violations of this section shall be tried in the Municipal Court of the City of Warner Robins. Any person found guilty of violating any section or part of this section shall be fined a sum not less than two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) nor more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) on each count. Each day that the violation continues is a separate count.
(c)
Any person found violating any section or part of this section will be required to make full restitution to the utility company for the cost of the services obtained in violation of the section, for damage to equipment of a utility company and for all associated costs of the investigation. In calculating the amount of restitution to be paid, the court is authorized to consider the following evidence.
(1)
The cost of the service received as a result of violating this section shall be computed by the utility company.
(2)
The cost of any damage to utility equipment shall be based on the actual costs. Costs may be computed and updated annually.
(3)
Investigative and any associated costs will be computed and updated annually by the utility company.
(d)
Any person in possession of any premises as owner, occupant, or tenant who is found to have an electric or natural gas meter which has been tapered with or altered in violation of this section or found to be receiving electricity or natural gas as a result of any of the methods described above shall be presumed to have knowingly violated the terms of this section. Such receipt of electricity of natural gas shall be deemed prima facie evidence of intent to defraud or deprive the utility company of recovering proper charges for payment for such service.
(e)
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby repealed to the extent of said conflict.
(f)
That the provisions of this section are hereby declared to be severable, and if any section, phrase or provision shall be declared or held invalid, such invalidity shall not affect the remainder of the sections, phrases or provisions.
(g)
The mayor and council find that there is a continuing problem by individuals' illegally obtaining electricity and natural gas service by various means and that this results in a loss of revenue to utility companies, increased costs to other consumers and creates a dangerous situation that can result in the loss of life or property.
(h)
This section shall be cumulative to O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25, and is enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25(c) which states:
O.C.G.A. 16-7-25 Damaging, injuring, or interfering with property of public utility companies, municipalities, or political subdivisions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful or any person intentionally and without authority to injure or destroy any meter, pipe, conduit, wire, line, post, lamp, or other apparatus belonging to a company, municipality, or political subdivision engaged in the manufacture or sale of electricity, gas, water, telephone, or other public services; intentionally and without authority to prevent a meter from properly registering the quantity of such service supplied; in any way to interfere with the proper action of such company, municipality, or political subdivision; intentionally to divert any services of such company, municipality, or political subdivision; or otherwise intentionally and without authority to use or cause to be used, without the consent of the company, municipality, or political subdivision, any service manufactured, sold or distributed by the company, municipality, or political subdivision.
(b)
Where there is no evidence to the contrary, the person performing any of the illegal acts set forth in subsection (a) of this Code section and the person who with knowledge of such violation receives the benefit of such service without proper charge as a result of the improper action shall be presumed to be responsible for the act of tampering or diversion.
(c)
This Code section shall be cumulative to and shall not prohibit the enactment of any other general and local laws, rules, and regulations of state or local authorities or agencies and local ordinances prohibiting such activities which are more restrictive than this Code section.
(d)
Any person who violates this Code section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ga. L. 1897, p. 69, 1; Penal Code 1910, 783; Ga. L. 1916, p. 153, 1; Code 1933, 26-3801, 26-3802; Ga. L. 1957, p. 490, 1-5; Code 1933, 26-1507, enacted by Ga. L. 1976, p. 773, 1; Ga. L. 1978, p. 1658, 1.)
(i)
For purposes of this section solely, the term utility company shall be defined as: Any company, electrical membership corporation, municipality, or political subdivision engaged in the manufacturer or sale of electricity or provision of natural gas service.
(Ord. No. 44-10, 9-20-10)
