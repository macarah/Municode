ARTICLE I. - AD VALOREM TAXATION

Sec. 10-1. - Manner, time of issuing execution.
Executions for nonpayment of ad valorem taxes and other indebtedness due the city shall be issued by the city clerk or tax clerk of the city when delinquent.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-2. - Receipt to be attached to execution; record.
When executions for taxes have been issued, it shall be the duty of the tax clerk to attach the unsigned tax receipt of the taxpayer to the execution and place the execution in the hands of the one of the officers designated in section 10-1. The tax clerk shall keep an accurate record of the execution delivered to each officer, including the name of the officer and the date of delivery of the execution to him.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-3. - Interest on past-due taxes and assessments; penalty, and fifa cost.
All taxes and assessments past due shall bear interest at the maximum rate allowed under state law. In addition, the maximum penalty allowed by state law and a fifa cost of twelve dollars ($12.00) on any past due taxes and assessments shall be charged and collected. The tax clerk shall collect the interest, penalty, and fifa cost on past due items and make a separate record thereof.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94; Ord. No. 64-02, § 1, 8-5-02; Ord. No. 44-08, 12-15-08; Ord. No. 19-16, § 1, 8-1-16)
Sec. 10-4. - City marshal designated; fee for tax levy; tax sale.
The mayor and council may designate a city marshal or code enforcement officer whose name shall be entered in the minutes of the mayor and council and who may be removed by mayor and council with or without cause at any time. The mayor and council may also designate [an] additional city marshal, if, in their judgement, the need exists and such appointment may be made (on) a temporary basis. Once a levy is made or posted on the property of a delinquent or defaulting taxpayer, the marshal, code enforcement officer or an appointee shall collect, in addition to any other costs, interest, and penalties, the actual expenses incurred by the city in issuing the execution and administering the levy by imposing a levy administration fee which shall be five (5) percent of the delinquent tax or two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) whichever is the lesser. Regardless of any other provision of this section, however, no such levy administration fee shall be less than fifty dollars ($50.00).
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94; Ord. No. 28-97, §§ 1, 2, 4-7-97; Ord. No. 64-02, § 2, 8-5-02)
Sec. 10-5. - Property tax; levied; rate.
A tax on the taxable real and personal property located within the city is hereby levied and shall be collected for the purpose of operating the city, the maintaining of its government and for other lawful uses in connection with the furtherance of the city's welfare. The city's annual millage rate shall be as adopted by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94; Ord. No. 74-96, § 1, 10-7-96)
Sec. 10-6. - Homestead exemption for persons sixty-five and over.
A homestead exemption from all City of Warner Robins ad valorem taxes for any city purposes in the amount of twenty-four thousand dollars ($24,000.00) of the assessed value of the homestead of certain persons who, for at least the immediately preceding five (5) years and who are sixty-five (65) years of age or older, are residents of the city. Such exemption will be lost if not claimed in person with the city clerk's office by April 1 of any year. The homestead exemption provided under this section shall be extended to a residential property annexed into the corporate limits of the city, provided (a) the owner is sixty-five (65) years of age or older, and (b) the property has been the principal residence of the owner for the five (5) years immediately preceding the annexation.
(Mo. of 11-7-95; Amend. 11-6-01; Ord. No. 36-10, § 1, 7-21-10; Ord. No. 20-20, § 1, 7-6-20)
Editor's note— At the request of the city, the above text was added to the Code as § 10-6.
Sec. 10-7. - Exemption of certain personal property from ad valorem taxes.
The mayor and council hereby exempts from ad valorem taxes, beginning with the year 1995, one hundred (100) percent of the value of the three (3) classes of tangible personal property defined in Official Code of Georgia Annotated Sec. 48-5-48.2(b).
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94; Ord. No. 49-94, § 1, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-8. - Due date for returns and payment of taxes.
All ad valorem taxes shall be payable on or before December 20 of the year in which levied and assessed.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-9. - Financial institutions business license tax.
(a)
Imposed. There is hereby levied an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations and state building and loan associations a business license tax at the rate of twenty-five-hundredths of one percent of the gross receipts of such institutions. "Gross receipts" shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. 48-6-93. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this section, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this section shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) per year.
(b)
Due date. The due date of taxes levied by this section shall be April 1 of each year.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-10. - City authorized to collect expenses at tax sale.
The city shall be authorized at tax sale (as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-359) to collect any and all expenses associated with the collection of ad valorem taxes on such real property, including any expenses incurred in the collection of such taxes.
(Ord. No. 26-98, 4-6-98)
Sec. 10-11. - Payment by credit card.
Beginning on January 1, 2007, a convenience fee will be charged to credit card users who desire to pay ad valorem taxes with a credit card, and the convenience fee schedule will be updated and amended, [from time to time,] so as to cover the cost of interchange fees charged to the city.
(Ord. No. 72-06, 12-18-06)
Secs. 10-12—10-24. - Reserved.
