ARTICLE V. - POOL, BILLIARD TABLES[4]

State Law reference— Authority to license, tax and regulate billiard rooms, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-291. - Hours of operation.
The holder of a permit issued under this article is prohibited from operation of pool or billiard tables for those hours that the establishment is closed or when beer, wine or alcoholic beverages are prohibited from being sold.
(Ord. No. 1062, § 2(d))
Sec. 10-292. - Restrictions.
The number of pool or billiard tables in any one establishment shall not exceed three in number. Food and beverages must be served and available for purchase at such location. Provided, however, any bona fide family entertainment center may have pool or billiard tables greater than three in number but not exceeding a total of six upon approval of the city council.
For purposes of this section, a "bona fide family entertainment center" shall be defined as a for-profit retail business consisting of an improved indoor facility exceeding 10,000 square feet in heated floor space with its core function and majority of facilities being dedicated and accessible to children and family entertainment including one or more of the following: bowling lanes, games, laser tag, and batting cages. A bona fide family activity center shall also be required to have a full-service kitchen with sit-down dining that includes tableside wait-staff service.
(Ord. No. 1062, § 2(e); Ord. of 9-25-2007)
Secs. 10-293—10-315. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 10-316. - Compliance.
Any person, firm, corporation or other business entity desiring to operate and/or charge for the use of any pool or billiard table shall first, before engaging in such business, submit an application and obtain a permit from the city.
(Ord. No. 1062, § 2(a))
Sec. 10-317. - Business occupation tax license required.
In addition to the permit required by this division, any person coming under the provisions of this article shall obtain a business occupation tax license for any activity coming under the provisions of section 50-161 et seq.
Sec. 10-318. - Application.
The application for a permit shall provide the name and location of the business, the name and address of the owner and the name and address of the individual present on the premises to oversee the operation of the pool or billiard tables.
(Ord. No. 1062, § 2(a))
Sec. 10-319. - Rate.
Upon approval of the application, the permit shall be issued at the rate as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 1062, § 2(b))
Sec. 10-320. - Discretion of mayor and city council.
The mayor and city council, at their discretion, may refuse to grant such permit at any location which they deem unsuitable or undesirable for the location thereof, or to deny such application based on the individual applying for such permit, and may request a criminal background search on such person.
(Ord. No. 1062, § 2(c))
Secs. 10-321—10-399. - Reserved.
