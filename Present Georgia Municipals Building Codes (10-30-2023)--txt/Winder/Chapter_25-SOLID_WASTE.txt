ARTICLE I. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Secs. 25-1—25-9. - Reserved.
Sec. 25-10. - Municipal jurisdiction.
It shall be mandatory that all owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of residential buildings, offices or businesses and commercial building and premises and the same shall be required to accept such collection and removal services as may be provided by the city relating to the disposal of garbage, trash, rubbish and other debris within the municipal jurisdiction of the city.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-11. - Commercial notification for collection.
All commercial businesses that require cart service shall notify the city that collection of garbage from such places is needed. For dumpster or roll-off containers see section 25-103.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-12. - Businesses and industries within the city.
(a)
All businesses and industries located in the city shall place all the garbage and rubbish generated by them in containers. All commercial containers must have closefitting lids. All lids and side doors shall be kept closed by the users so the contents are covered at all times.
(b)
All containers shall be stored on private property and may not interfere with vehicular or pedestrian traffic. In the event that a container is placed on city property a lease agreement shall be first entered into with the city.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-13. - Reserved.
Sec. 25-14. - Pre-collection.
(a)
All garbage and refuse shall be placed in plastic or paper bags securely closed and stored in solid waste containers authorized by the city.
(b)
Any solid waste that may injure or hamper a collector, such as, but not limited to, broken glass; light bulbs; razor blades; or other potentially sharp or dangerous objects shall be securely wrapped so as to prevent puncture to the wrapping or solid waste container.
(c)
Private streets and other roads which are not dedicated city rights-of-way shall be fully accessible to the city's provider and equipment in order for collections to be made in an unobstructed fashion by the city. The city reserves the right to select an alternative location for collection when private roads are not suitable.
(d)
If an authorized garbage container is obstructed in such a manner as to prohibit the automated collection vehicle from dumping the container, the container's contents will not be collected and/or an additional charge may be imposed for any additional effort to collect such waste.
(e)
Waste placed in containers other than authorized containers will not be collected and the customer will be subject to fines.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-15. - Authorized containers.
Each residential dwelling unit or commercial business will be provided one (1) standard authorized rollaway cart/garbage container by the city or its contractor. Upon request additional garbage containers may be delivered to a residential dwelling unit at an additional monthly charge to the resident.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-16. - Loss or damage to authorized containers.
(a)
Authorized containers assigned to residential or commercial customers by the city are and shall remain property of the city's provider.
(b)
Damage to, loss, or removal of an authorized container assigned to a residential or commercial unit shall be the responsibility of the holder of the utility account for the unit at the time such damage, loss or removal is discovered by the city/the city's provider, and such holder of the utility account or property owner (if no water account is noted) shall be liable for all costs associated with replacing the container.
(c)
Persons moving from one (1) address in the city to another address in the city, or from the city to another locality shall leave all authorized containers at the location to which the containers have been assigned.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-17. - Maintenance of authorized containers.
(a)
Authorized containers assigned to a customer shall be the responsibility of and kept and maintained by the customer in a clean and sanitary manner so as to prevent the breeding of insects, vermin, vectors, and/or the release of odors.
(b)
Dumpsters, roll-off containers, and compactors shall be the responsibility of and maintained by the owner of such property in a clean, disinfected, and sanitary manner so as to prevent the breeding of insects, vermin, or vectors, and/or the release of odors.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-18. - Time of placement of residential solid waste for collection.
(a)
Authorized carts shall be placed by the owner of such property in a location that is readily accessible to collection equipment, not to exceed three (3) feet from the curb or edge of the traveled portion of the street or road and not to be located in a manner that will block the driveway or interfere with vehicular or pedestrian traffic. Such placement shall not restrict access to public sidewalks, pathways and bicycle paths. Where there is no curb, authorized carts shall be placed next to the edge of the road pavement in the same manner as if there were a curb.
(b)
Household garbage shall be set out for collection entirely within fully closed authorized garbage carts and placed by the owner of such property on the street abutting the residential or commercial unit for which the container was assigned.
(c)
For proper automated collection, the authorized garbage container shall be placed by the owner of such property in such a manner as to have sufficient clearance on each side so as to be accessible to the automated collections vehicle with the container handle facing away from the street or right-of-way. Containers shall be placed at a minimum of three (3) feet from vehicles, utility poles and boxes, trees, mailboxes, and other objects.
(d)
Authorized containers shall be removed by the owner of such property from the street and stored on the side or rear of the dwelling to which it is assigned as soon as possible after collection. Containers shall not be permitted to be placed before 12:01 a.m. the day before the scheduled pickup or remain on the street after midnight of the day following the scheduled collection day. Each resident, property owner, agent, manager and/or occupant of a property is responsible for compliance with this provision. Exception: For nonresidential property, carts may be placed curbside after 2:00 p.m. on Friday if the day for collection is Monday. The fine for a violation of this subsection shall be twenty-five dollars ($25.00) per citation after the first warning is provided.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-19. - Collection assistance due to physical limitations.
(a)
A written verification by a medical doctor that the resident is unable to physically place carts at the curbside may be required to receive non-curbside cart assistance. Handicapped or disabled residents must live alone or all persons living in household must be disabled to receive non-curbside cart assistance.
(b)
Residences receiving assistance with the non-curbside service will utilize a collection point designated by the city's provider.
(c)
Assistance for nonresidential property non-curbside service will charged an additional monthly rate of twelve dollars ($12.00) per cart.
(d)
A disability form may be required by the contracted provider and shall be available at City Hall.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-20. - Special service.
Any person requesting special service, that is the collection of any items not otherwise provided for in this chapter, may inquire with the city for such service. Special services provided by the city shall be billed in addition to the standard monthly cost and payment may be required in advance. Special service may be provided through the city's provider or a third party contractor.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-21. - Scavenging.
No person other than the occupants of the property/owners, city code enforcement officials, or authorized collection personnel shall sift the contents of or remove anything from carts, bags, or boxes containing items placed for disposal and/or recycling.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-22. - Move-outs and evictions.
(a)
For rental properties, garbage, trash, or other solid waste originating from the property during the clean up between tenants shall be the responsibility of the landlord and/or the property owner. Following an eviction, any property, trash, belongings, or other items shall be placed adjacent to some portion of the property, immediately behind the sidewalk or if there is no sidewalk, a minimum of three (3) feet behind the curb or off the pavement and shall not be located as to impede or obstruct or divert either pedestrian or vehicular traffic or the flow of surface water or block drains.
(b)
The city and/or the selected provider are not responsible for and will not return any items collected curbside once the item has been removed. It is the property's owner or tenant's responsibility to ensure that items not intended for collection are not placed at and/or are timely removed from the curbside.
(c)
Items place on city right-of-way or near the edge of a public road will be removed by the city and the utility customer or property owner will be billed seventy-five dollars ($75.00) per cubic yard.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-23. - Reserved.
Sec. 25-24. - Building materials.
The city shall not be responsible for collecting or hauling discarded building materials, dirt, broken concrete, bricks, rock or debris resulting from repairs, renovations, remodeling, or construction waste (plumbing fixtures, including, but not limited to, sinks, bath tubs, shower stalls, toilets, cabinets, doors, windows, roofing debris, lumber, siding, carpet and/or floor coverings). Such material must be disposed of by the builder, property owner, tenant, or their authorized contractor.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-25. - Prohibited materials.
(a)
Dead animals, tires, sewage, poisons, commercial medical waste, explosives, dangerous or corrosive chemicals, motor oil, bulky items and/or cylinders containing gas under pressure, air conditioners, vehicle batteries, paint cans (unless all paint is dry), or lumber shall not be placed in carts used for regular collection service.
(b)
No person shall dispose of any hypodermic syringe, needle, instrument or any device used for injections except when such disposal is in compliance with state and federal regulations and, at a minimum, making this material completely incapable of reuse; prior to disposal, such material shall be placed in an appropriate container and secured so as to prevent injury to collectors.
(c)
Cardboard boxes. All cardboard boxes must be flattened before these items will be acceptable for collection. When available, cardboard should be placed in the recycling cart.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-26. - Drainage of refuse.
All refuse capable of holding liquids must be drained before being deposited in the city's containers.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-27. - Transportation.
It is unlawful for any person to transport any solid waste or recovered material over any city road unless the solid waste or recovered material is securely tied, placed in a secure container, or covered when necessary so as to prevent leakage or spillage. It is unlawful for any person to allow solid waste or recovered materials to spill, blow or drop from any vehicle on any road.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-28. - Illegal disposal of garbage, etc.
(a)
No person shall deposit garbage, trash or refuse of any kind on or bury garbage, trash or refuse of any kind in any public easements or right-of-way, street, alley, sidewalk, or other public land, vacant or unoccupied lot.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any owner, occupant or lessee of any building, yard or lot of ground within the city to allow garbage, trash or refuse of any kind to accumulate or remain in the building, or upon the yard or lot, unless placed in a container as required by this article.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place garbage, yard debris, or refuse for collection in such a manner as to obstruct gutters, drains, stormwater ditch, or any natural waterway.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-29. - Nuisance.
(a)
The keeping of garbage in containers other than containers prescribed by this chapter or the keeping upon premises of garbage or yard waste which is allowed to become a public health hazard, cause odor, or blight, shall constitute a public nuisance and may be abated in the manner provided by city ordinance and/or state law.
(b)
A separate violation shall be deemed committed each day during or upon which such unlawful accumulation continues.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 22-30. - Recycling.
(a)
In order to promote recycling, the city shall provide each residence with one (1) recycle cart. Customers not wishing to participate are required to contact the waste provider for non-delivery or removal of the recycling cart.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place any material not designated for recycling in the recyclable material cart.
(c)
Recycling carts shall be removed from customers not complying with the established recycling program.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-31. - Removal of nonconforming solid waste or other debris.
If a customer has not removed extra or nonconforming solid waste, or refuse within forty-eight (48) hours after notification by the city, the city administrator or designee may order such removal and all actual costs incurred plus a fifty dollar ($50.00) administrative fee shall be billed to the customer's utility account.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-32. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person violating this article shall be punished by a fine not less than one hundred dollars ($100.00) or more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) per violation, by imprisonment for a term not to exceed thirty (30) days and/or a combination of such fine and imprisonment.
(b)
Upon conviction, the court may also order the convicted person to remove any solid waste that has been accumulated in violation of this article from the property.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Secs. 25-33—25-44. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - YARD TRIMMINGS AND BULK WASTE DISPOSAL REGULATIONS

Sec. 25-45. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed in this section:
(a)
Bulky waste. Those items whose large size or weight precludes or complicates their handling by normal collection, processing and disposal methods. Common examples are furniture, carpet, and appliances.
(b)
Commercial solid waste. All types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other nonmanufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes.
(c)
Composting. The controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable, odor-free humus.
(d)
Cubic yard. Three (3) feet high, by three (3) feet deep, by three (3) feet wide.
(e)
Last week of the month. The last week having a Monday through Friday of the month.
(f)
Yard debris. Leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree pruning, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvicultural operations.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-46. - Leaves and grass clippings.
(a)
Yard debris such as leaves, grass clippings and pine straw shall be bagged and placed adjacent to the street for collection. Yard debris will be picked up by the city. Each single-family or duplex residential property may place up to eight (8) bags weighing less than thirty (30) pounds by the street each week for no additional charge.
(b)
Residential property owners with more than eight (8) bags should contact the city's public works department to make arrangements for an additional charge.
(c)
Yard debris not bagged will be picked up upon request, but will be subject to a fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) per cubic yard. All fees shall be added to the monthly utility bill. Any delinquent or unpaid fees shall be subject to the enforcement, delinquency and execution provisions as set forth in this chapter.
(d)
The city shall conduct loose leaf roadside collection, from October 1 to January 31. Residents shall rake or blow their leaves to edge of the road during this time frame, without blocking traffic or drainage ditches, and the city will pick them up. During this time frame leaves will be collected twice per month.
(e)
The loose leaf roadside collection only applies to property zoned residential that have an active solid waste account with the city.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-47. - Time of placement of yard debris for collection.
(a)
All residents utilizing city collection of yard debris shall place such items on their premises behind the curb line, located so as to be easily accessible to street pickup, not to be placed over or next to fixed items such as fire hydrants, telephone poles or electrical boxes, water meters, mail boxes or anything that could be damaged by equipment or personnel picking up said items. The City of Winder and its collection contractor will not be responsible for repairing or replacing items removed or damaged which were placed curbside in such a manner that it appeared to be intended for removal or was not visible because it was mixed with other items placed curbside.
(b)
The total allowable volume of yard debris to be collected at curbside per residential property per week is a maximum of one (1) pile approximately four (4) feet in length by four (4) feet in height by a width of four (4) feet. This includes the combined total of both bulk and vegetative waste. Limbs shall not be greater than four (4) inches in diameter or longer than four (4) feet. Yard debris shall be stacked and bundled with string, twine, or other similar materials.
(c)
Loose yard debris not complying with section 25-47(b) will be collected at a fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) per cubic yard and billed to the property's utility bill. Properties that do not have an active utility bill will be subject to collections as provided in this chapter.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-48. - Tree and landscaping service disposal.
(a)
All holders of a tree service license or any person engaging in the cutting of trees/vegetation for hire or compensation shall be required to remove all debris, brush and limbs. It shall be unlawful for any person to trim or remove trees within the city unless the byproduct from those services is immediately removed from the premises and properly disposed of.
(b)
This section shall apply to all debris which exceeds the definition of yard debris acceptable for monthly collection by the city noted in section 25-47(b).
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-49. - Bulk waste.
(a)
Bulk waste from residences may be placed at curbside for collection the last week of the month, provided that such waste is in piles separate from yard waste and all other solid waste. One (1) cubic yard will be collected per month for each active residential account at no additional charge.
(b)
All residents utilizing city collection of bulk waste shall place such items on their premises behind the curb line, located so as to be easily accessible to street pickup, not to be placed over or next to fixed items such as fire hydrants, telephone poles or electrical boxes, water meters, mail boxes or anything that could be damaged by equipment or personnel picking up said items. The city and its collection contractor will not be responsible for repairing or replacing items removed or damaged which were placed curbside in such a manner that it appeared to be intended for removal or was not visible because it was mixed with other items placed curbside.
(c)
Bulk waste placed curbside outside of the last week of the month and/or exceeding the one (1) cubic yard per month shall be picked up at a cost of fifty dollars ($50.00) per cubic yard and billed to the property's utility account.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-50. - Penalties.
Any person violating this article shall be subject to a fine not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) per violation, by imprisonment for a term not to exceed thirty (30) days and/or a combination of such fine and imprisonment.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Secs. 25-51—25-59. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - LITTER

Sec. 25-60. - Litter control—Definitions.
As used in this article, the following definitions shall apply:
(a)
Litter means all sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste material, tin cans, refuse, garbage, paper, trash, debris or discarded materials of every kind and description;
(b)
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any park, playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area; and residential or farm properties, timberlands or forests within the city limits of Winder or any other privately or publicly owned property within the city limits of Winder.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-61. - Same—Unlawful activity enumerated; penalty.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to dump, deposit, throw or leave, or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving of, any litter, garbage, rubbish or refuse on any public or private property in this municipality or any waters in this municipality, unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the federal government, State of Georgia, or by any of its agencies or political subdivisions, for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper government authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter, garbage, rubbish or refuse is placed into a litter, garbage, rubbish or refuse receptacle or container installed on such property and the person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession, or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(b)
Any person who violates subsection (a) of this Code section shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished as follows:
(1)
As provided in section 1-8 of this Code and shall be cited to appear before the municipal court for hearing and determination of the applicable fine. Each and every violation of the provisions of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(2)
In the sound discretion of the judge, when conviction is obtained, the person also may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way, located within the city limits, for a distance not to exceed one (1) mile, any litter he has deposited and any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the date of execution of sentence; or
(3)
In the sound discretion of the judge, should conviction be obtained, the person also may be directed to pick up and remove from any public beach, public park, private right-of-way or, with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that he has deposited litter, located within the city limits, any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(c)
The judge may publish the names of persons convicted of violating subsection (a) of this section.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-62. - Same—Prima facie evidence.
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of section 25-61, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated section 25-61.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-63. - Litter receptacles to be provided by city.
The public works director shall establish and maintain receptacles for the deposit of litter at appropriate locations within the city where the property is frequented by the public.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Secs. 25-64—25-89. - Reserved.

When applicable, collection schedules will be placed via a sticker on the individual containers.

ARTICLE V. - SOLID WASTE PROVIDER

Sec. 25-100. - Contracts with independent solid waste providers.
In order for the city to provide adequate service to the citizens of the city for the collection, removal and disposal of refuse accumulated within the city, the city may enter into a contract with an independent contractor, either private or another municipal corporate entity, for such services.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-101. - Contract price.
The city council may negotiate with an independent contractor or another municipal corporate entity the terms, conditions and consideration to be paid for the services sought under such a contract; and it shall be presumed that any consideration agreed upon between the city and such independent contractor or municipal corporate entity shall be in the best interest and welfare of the citizens of this city.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-102. - Minimum vehicle standards.
(a)
An adequate number of vehicles shall be provided by the commercial contractor to service its city customers. All such vehicles shall be licensed in the State of Georgia and shall operate in compliance with all applicable state, federal and municipal regulations.
(b)
All vehicles and other equipment shall be kept in proper repair and sanitary condition. Each vehicle shall have at least one (1) broom and shovel to clean up solid waste that may be spilled or otherwise scattered during the contractor's process of collection.
(c)
All vehicles shall be sufficiently secure so as to prevent any littering of solid waste and/or leakage of fluid. No vehicle shall be willfully overloaded. Each vehicle shall be operated by a qualified driver holding a valid license of the category required to operate such vehicles.
(d)
All vehicles shall be clearly marked with the company's name and telephone number in letters not less than two (2) inches in height.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-103. - Commercial and industrial refuse collection to be provided on a competitive fair market value.
It is the policy and intent of the City of Winder that commercial and industrial refuse collection services be provided within the city on a competitive, free market basis for dumpsters and roll-offs. In order to promote this policy and intent, commercial and industrial customers within the city are encouraged to solicit multiple bids from qualified licensed providers and may contract for such services.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-104. - Exclusive right of collection; ownership of materials collected or dumped.
(a)
The city council shall have the exclusive right to award the collection, removal and disposal of garbage with the city limits. No private individual, business, agent or other operation shall be permitted to haul garbage produced by owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of residential buildings and premises for residential property and premises without first having obtained permission from the council therefore.
(b)
Any person or company desiring such permission shall bid on the service during times specified, giving the details of operation and any charges to be made, all as may be required by the council and, where allowed, may negotiate such additional terms and conditions. Once a contract is issued by the council for such operations, the contract shall be issued in the name of the applicant and such permit shall not be transferable. The council shall have full authority to make any other reasonable regulations relating to the hauling of garbage over city streets and thoroughfares by outside collectors.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Secs. 25-105—25-119. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - RATES AND CHARGES[2]

Sec. 25-120. - Refuse collection rates and charges not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance relating to rates and charges for refuse collection or disposal, and all such ordinances are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-121. - Rates.
(a)
Fees for collection of solid waste, garbage and yard debris shall be those as established by resolution of the city council; and shall be published in the office of the city clerk and where utility bills may be paid.
(b)
Rates for service to residential and light commercial service will be subject to periodic review and adjusted by city council by separate resolution as necessary to ensure adequate cost recovery for operation of the sanitary municipal solid waste system.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-122. - Billing and assessment.
(a)
Levy, amount. For the promotion of public health and safety, there shall be charged against each family occupying a mobile home trailer, house, apartment or other dwelling and against each person, partnership, corporation or other entity owning or operating a business an amount fixed from time to time by the mayor and council, which charge may be included with the monthly water and gas bill, provided said charge is separately stated.
(b)
The city will not split bills or charge for portions of use. The price shall be billed in full to one (1) single owner.
(c)
Exemptions. Businesses requiring dumpster or industrial, medical, or hazardous wastes removal services are exempt from city solid waste service and assessed fees provided other arrangements are made for disposal through a licensed provider.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
Sec. 25-123. - Nonpayment of fees.
(a)
Should any owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of residential buildings and premises or business and commercial buildings and premises fail or refuse to pay the charges fixed against such owners, occupants, tenants and lessees as provided for in this article when due, the city may discontinue garbage collection services to the residential buildings and premises or business and commercial buildings and premises of such owners, occupants, tenants and lessees against such sanitary service charge or fee and charge for the collection, removal and disposal of garbage services until such fees have been paid in full.
(b)
If the tenant of any property has no city utility meter and the sums due under this article are not paid when due, the landlord or lessor shall be responsible for such charges and shall be billed for the charges.
(c)
Upon delinquent notification, if a delinquent account is not paid within five (5) days, the city or the contracted provider may cease all refuse collection for that account or individual address. Service shall be resumed thereafter only upon payment of the entire balance on the account plus all penalties, and all applicable reconnection or container resetting charges.
(d)
Any person failing or refusing to pay the monthly solid waste or other related charges provided for in this chapter, after having been duly billed by the city, shall be guilty of a violation of this chapter, and all solid waste services and water service provided by the city to the premises for which the monthly charges are past due shall be terminated by the city.
(e)
Enforcement. In the event charges remain unpaid for fifteen (15) days after the bill or account for service has been rendered to the customer, execution shall be issued in favor of the city against such delinquent user for the amount of such unpaid charges, which execution shall be issued and enforced, levied and collected in the same manner as other executions running in favor of the city.
(f)
If the account or bill remains unpaid after said past delinquent/due notice is mailed, then the public water supply will be turned off and will not be turned back on until said bill is paid.
(g)
In addition to all other rates, charges, penalties, fees, expenses and cost otherwise provided for in this chapter, the city may also collect all costs incurred for the collection of delinquent utility accounts, including, but not limited to, postage expenses, late fees as established by resolution or ordinance of the council from time to time, court filing fees, attorney's fees incurred by the city, and fees paid to third-party collection agencies. The city is authorized to use third-party collection agencies to collect delinquent utility accounts and all cost, fees and expenses of such third-party agencies shall be the responsibility of the delinquent customer.
(Ord. (W-2-13) of 1-8-13)
