Chapter 20 - OFFENSES AND NUISANCES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 20-1. - Alcoholic beverage possession on county property.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to consume, use, sell or have in his possession any alcoholic beverage while such person is upon or in any county property or facility, whether or not such person is located in a vehicle.
(b)
It shall be unlawful, upon or in any county property or facilities, for any person to have in his hand, or upon his person, or in any vehicle, any open container containing an alcoholic beverage.
(c)
The restrictions in this section shall not apply to persons who consume alcoholic beverages at a closed private function that has been approved by the chairman of the board of commissioners or his designee, and is limited to a prescribed area.
(Code 2008, § 31-103)
Sec. 20-2. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Meeting shall have the meaning set forth in O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(a)(3).
(b)
Provisions. It shall be unlawful for any person to commit any of the following acts within the county:
(1)
Any person who shall act in a violent, tumultuous, or disorderly manner toward another, whereby any person is placed in reasonable fear of the safety of his life, limb or health.
(2)
Any person who shall act in a violent, tumultuous, or disorderly manner toward another, whereby public property or the property of any other person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged.
(3)
Any person who shall endanger lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence or threats of imminent bodily harm.
(4)
Any person who shall cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another or public property.
(5)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or others and cause, provoke, or engage in any fight or brawl.
(6)
Any person who shall collect in bodies or crowds and engage in unlawful activities.
(7)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or others and engage or attempt to engage in unlawful gaming.
(8)
Any person who shall frequent any public place and obtain money from another by an illegal or fraudulent scheme, trick, artifice or device, or attempt to do so.
(9)
Any person who shall use fighting words directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates turmoil.
(10)
Any person who, with the intent to prevent or disrupt a lawful meeting, procession or gathering, substantially obstructs or interferes with the meeting, procession or gathering by physical action or verbal utterance.
(Code 2008, § 31-106)
Sec. 20-3. - Discharge of firearm in a platted subdivision.
(a)
Definitions. A platted subdivision shall be any subdivision in the county that is on record in the real property records of the county.
(b)
Provisions. It shall be unlawful to shoot or discharge a firearm within 200 yards of any residential structure of another landowner within the platted limits of a subdivision. This section does not apply to property adjacent to a platted subdivision that is not a part of the subdivision. This section shall not prohibit an individual from protecting himself or his property from imminent danger from an individual or an animal.
(Code 2008, § 31-108)
Sec. 20-4. - Penalty and enforcement.
(a)
Any person who violates any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction before the county magistrate court, be fined or imprisoned as prescribed by section 1-9.
(b)
Citations for violations of this article will be issued by the county sheriff or his deputies, or any law enforcement officer certified by the peace officer standards and training council.

Sec. 20-6. - Criminal trespass.
new
(a)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when, in unincorporated Oglethorpe County, he intentionally enters on the property of another without the consent of that other person or knowingly and maliciously interferes with the possession or use of the property of another person without the consent of that person.
(b)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when, in unincorporated Oglethorpe County, he knowingly and without authority:
(1)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person for an unlawful purpose;
(2)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving, prior to such entry, notice from the owner or rightful occupant which may include a posted sign, or, upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant that such entry is forbidden; or
(3)
Remains upon the land or premises of another person or within the vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving notice from the owner, rightful occupant, or upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant to depart.
(c)
Any person violating the ordinance from which this section derived shall, upon conviction before the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court, by fined an amount not exceeding $1,000.00 or imprisoned for a period not exceeding 60 days, or both, at the discretion of Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court.
(d)
Citations for violations of this section may be issued by a certified law enforcement officer.
( Ord. No. 2023-02 , § 2, 3-6-2023)
Sec. 20-7. - Criminal interference with government property.
new
(a)
A person commits the offence of criminal interference with government property when he or she destroys, defaces, tampers with, injuries, or removes moveable or personal property belonging to or under supervision or control of Oglethorpe County.
(b)
A person commits the offense of interference with government property when he or she moves, disturbs, or takes, any earth, stone or material from any public property in unincorporated Oglethorpe County.
(c)
A person commits the offense of interference with government property when he or she cuts, defaces, or destroys a tree on Oglethorpe County property unless authorized.
(d)
Any person violating the ordinance from which this section derived shall, upon conviction before the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court, by fined an amount not exceeding $1,000.00 or imprisoned for a period not exceeding 60 days, or both, at the discretion of Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court.
(e)
Citations for violations of the ordinance from which this section derived may be issued by a certified law enforcement officer.
(f)
If there is an apparent conflict in this section between a specific and a general provision, the specific provision shall control.
( Ord. No. 2023-02 , § 3, 3-6-2023)
Sec. 20-8. - Burning of woodland, brush, fields, or other lands.
new
(a)
Provisions. It shall be unlawful, in unincorporated Oglethorpe County:
(1)
To start, cause, or procure another to start a fire in any woodlands, brush, field, or any other lands not one's own or leased and without the permission of the owner;
(2)
To burn any brush, field, forest land, campfire, or debris whether on one's own land or the lands of another, without taking the necessary precautions before, during and after the fire to prevent the escape of such fire onto the lands of another. The escape of such fire shall be prima-facie evidence that necessary precautions were not taken;
(3)
For a person to cause a fire by discarding any lighted cigarette, cigar, debris, or any other flaming or smoldering material that may cause a forest fire; or
(4)
To destroy or damage any material or device used in the detection or suppression of wildfires.
(b)
Exclusion. This section shall not apply to fire resulting from the operation of transportation machinery or equipment used in its normal or customary manner.
(c)
Penalty. Any person violating the ordinance from which this section derived shall, upon conviction before the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court, be fined an amount not exceeding $1,000.00 or imprisoned for period not exceeding 60 days, or both, at the discretion of the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court.
(d)
Enforcement. Citations for violations of this section may be issued by a certified law enforcement officer, code enforcement officer, or Georgia Forestry Commission Agent.
(e)
Conflicts. When there is an apparent conflict in this section between specific and general provisions, the specific provision shall control.
( Ord. No. 2023-02 , § 4, 3-6-2023)
Sec. 20-9. - Uncontrolled fire as constituting a public nuisance; duties and liabilities of person or entity responsible.
new
(a)
Provisions. In unincorporated Oglethorpe County:
(1)
Any fire burning uncontrolled on any forested or cut-over brush land or grassland is declared to be a public nuisance by reason of its menace to life and property.
(2)
Any person, firm, or corporation responsible either for the starting or for the existence of such fire is required to control or extinguish it immediately. If such person, firm, or corporation shall refuse or neglect to do so, any organized fire suppression force may suppress the nuisance thus constituted by controlling and extinguishing the fire; and the cost thereof may be recovered from the responsible person, firm or corporation.
(b)
Penalty. Any person violating the ordinance from which this section derived shall, upon conviction before the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court, be fined an amount not exceeding $1,000.00 or imprisoned for a period not exceeding 60 days, or both, at the discretion of the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court and may, at the direction of the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court, be ordered to pay restitution to the fire suppression force controlling and extinguishing the fire.
(c)
Enforcement. Citations for violations of this section may be issued by a certified law enforcement officer, code enforcement officer or Georgia Forestry Commission Agent.
( Ord. No. 2023-02 , § 5, 3-6-2023)
Sec. 20-10. - Burning permit.
new
(a)
Provisions. Any person, firm, corporation, or association lawfully entitled to burn any woods, lands, marshes, or any other flammable vegetation, whether in cultivated or uncultivated areas, shall prior such burning in unincorporated Oglethorpe County obtain a permit from the Forest Ranger of Oglethorpe County or from another employee of the forestry unit serving Oglethorpe County who is authorized by the chief forester of such unit to grant such permits. An applicant for such a permit shall provide the Oglethorpe County Forest Ranger or other authorized employee of the forestry unit serving Oglethorpe County with the location and estimated time of the proposed burn. Such information may be provided, and the permit may be obtained by a telephone call to the Oglethorpe County Forest Ranger or to another authorized employee of the forestry unit serving Oglethorpe County. The permit shall be given by providing the applicant thereof with a permit number which will grant permission for a controlled burn to take place at the location specified by the applicant at a time approved by the Oglethorpe County Forest Ranger or by the other authorized employee of the forestry unit serving Oglethorpe County.
(b)
Exclusions.
(1)
It shall not be necessary to obtain a permit otherwise required if a sudden emergency requires a firing in order to render one's premises safe. In any prosecution under this section, a necessary firing in a sudden emergency shall constitute an affirmative defense.
(2)
It shall not be necessary to obtain a permit otherwise required to burn improved pastures, residue on cultivated crop land, or leaf piles if the person, firm, corporation or association intending to burn such residue shall, prior to such burning, give notice of the approximate time and location thereof to the Oglethorpe County Forest Ranger or to an employee of the forestry unit serving Oglethorpe County who is authorized to grant such permits.
(3)
The notice or permit required shall be in addition to any other notice or permit or other requirement for burning provided for by state law or by ordinance, resolution or regulation of Oglethorpe County provided, however, that nothing herein shall prohibit burning the understory for the health of the forest and wildlife or prohibit the landowner's ability to reduce fuel loads on the forest floor for the safety of the community; provided, further, that the foregoing exception shall not apply to the burning of leaf or brush piles not necessary to accomplish the purposes of prescribed burning.
(c)
Penalty. Any person violating the ordinance from which this section derived in unincorporated Oglethorpe County shall, upon conviction before the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court, be fined an amount not exceeding $1,000.00 or imprisoned for a period not exceeding 60 days, or both, at the discretion of the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court.
(d)
Enforcement. Citations for violations of the ordinance from which this section derived may be issued by a certified law enforcement officer, code enforcement officer or Georgia Forestry Commission Agent.
(e)
Conflicts. When there is an apparent conflict in this section between specific and general provisions, the specific provision shall control.
(f)
Severability. If any section, provision or clause of any part of this section shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, or if the provisions of this section as applied to any particular situation or set of circumstances shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, the same shall not be construed to affect the portions of this section not held to be invalid or the application of this section to other circumstances not held to be invalid.
( Ord. No. 2023-02 , § 6, 3-6-2023)
Sec. 20-11. - Ordinance to regulate the use of public rights-of-way for festivals, recreation purposes and sporting events.
new
(a)
This section shall apply to any recreational or sporting event conducted wholly or in part within the unincorporated area of Oglethorpe County. If the event is conducted wholly within the unincorporated area of Oglethorpe County, this section shall apply to the entire event and its full length. If the event is conducted partly within the unincorporated area and partly outside the unincorporated area, this section shall apply to the portion of the event conducted in the unincorporated area.
(b)
Public rights-of-way of Oglethorpe County shall not be used for the sporting or recreational contests or competitions except as provided in this section. The contests regulated by this section shall include all sporting or recreational contests or competitions on foot, bicycle, vehicle, or any other method of locomotion.
(c)
No such event shall be conducted on the public rights-of-way of Oglethorpe County unless the event is sponsored by a non-profit charitable institution such as a local school, religious institution, youth organization, fraternal organization, or chamber of commerce and is approved by the board of commissioners. The sponsoring organization must be actively involved in the planning, scheduling, and staging of the event and is subject to approval under the following conditions:
(1)
The sponsor of the event shall, at least 30 days prior to the scheduled date of the event, make application to the Oglethorpe County Board of Commissioners or its designee to conduct the event. Applications shall be in such form as the Oglethorpe County Board of Commissioners prescribes and shall at least provide the name and address of the local sponsor or event organizer (or in event of an organization which is not local, the name and address of a responsible county resident who is involved in planning the event); the date and times of the proposed events; the expected number of participants; names and qualification of medical or paramedical personnel that will be available at the event; the location of such events including all public rights-of-way which would be used or otherwise impacted; the duration of disruption of any vehicular traffic; specific arrangements for the crowd control and accommodations for security, traffic, safety, restroom facilities, and waste control; certification that the event will be handled as part of a festival or other ongoing event; and that the length of the competition or race shall then be no more than one-half mile from the start to the finish line.
(2)
The board of commissioners reserves the right to reject any al all applications based on the public health, safety, and welfare including the failure to provide for adequate measures to ensure a safe event. Special consideration shall be given to the impact of the event on more heavily traveled roads and on citizens not involved in the event.
(d)
If the board of commissioners shall determine, upon investigation, that the proposed route is hazardous to participants or others, the board of commissioners or its designee may alter the proposed route and specify another or different route in the interest of public safety.
(e)
Any person violating any provision of the ordinance from which this section derived shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished, upon conviction, by the Oglethorpe County Magistrate Court by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by imprisonment up to 60 days, or both, in the discretion of the magistrate count.
(f)
Should any section, subsection, or portion of this section by declared invalid or unconstitutional in any count of competent jurisdiction, such declaration shall not affect the validity of this section as a whole or any part thereof that is not specifically held to be invalid.
( Ord. No. 2023-02 , § 7, 3-6-2023)

ARTICLE II. - NOISE CONTROL

Sec. 20-25. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Excessively loud and disturbing means a state which occurs when a person of normal hearing who is inside a residence or other building that is not the location of the noise source can:
(1)
Clearly hear the noise;
(2)
Distinguish the noise from background ambient noise; and
(3)
Ascertain and identify the nature and source of the noise.
(Code 2008, § 14-101)
Sec. 20-26. - General prohibition.
No person shall create or cause to be created an excessively loud or disturbing noise within the unincorporated area of the county between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m., daily, and also on Sundays between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 5:30 p.m.
(Code 2008, § 14-102)
Sec. 20-27. - Investigation upon complaint.
Upon a complaint, by a resident of the unincorporated area of the county, an officer of the county sheriff's office shall respond and enter the residence or other building of the complainant to determine whether the noise is in violation of this article.
(Code 2008, § 14-103)
Sec. 20-28. - Issuance of citation.
If the officer determines the noise is excessively loud and disturbing, the officer shall issue a citation to the person violating this article.
(Code 2008, § 14-104)
Sec. 20-29. - Penalty.
Any person who violates this article shall, upon conviction before the county magistrate court, be fined or imprisoned as provided by section 1-9.
(Code 2008, § 14-105)
Sec. 20-30. - Impoundment of instrument, device, etc.
If the person to whom a citation is issued under this article neglects or refuses to abate the noise upon the direction of the officer, such officer may impound the instrument, device, object, mechanism or animal causing the noise if he determines that such action is necessary to abate the noise. Any such impounded property shall be stored in a safe place by the county sheriff's office or, in the case of an animal, in an appropriate animal control facility. Upon the impoundment of such property, the owner thereof shall be issued a receipt describing the same, the location where it will be stored, and when it will be available to be returned. Such property will be made available for return to its owner by no later than one hour after the conclusion of the period, as described in section 20-26, when such property was impounded.
(Code 2008, § 14-106)
