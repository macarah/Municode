ARTICLE II. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 8-23. - Definitions.
The definitions of O.C.G.A. title 3 are incorporated by reference herein as though fully set forth in this chapter.
Sec. 8-24. - Products for sale.
(a)
No retail dealer shall keep in stock, display, sell, or offer to sell, at any place of business licensed under this article, any other product or commodity except the following:
(1)
Wines, when properly licensed;
(2)
Malt beverages, when properly licensed;
(3)
Distilled spirits, when properly licensed;
(4)
Beverages containing no alcohol commonly used to dilute distilled spirits;
(5)
Tobacco products;
(6)
Ice to be sold in sealed containers only and not to be opened on the premises; and
(7)
Paper, Styrofoam or plastic cups.
(b)
Beverages containing no alcohol and commonly used to dilute distilled spirits may be dispensed through the use of vending machines, but no alcoholic beverages shall be dispensed through such vending machines.
(Code 2008, § 3-201)
Secs. 8-25—8-50. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - DISTILLED SPIRITS
Subdivision I. - In General

Sec. 8-51. - Place of sale or delivery of goods.
No retail dealer shall sell or deliver any distilled spirits to any person except in such retail dealer's place of business. A retail dealer shall be permitted to load purchased goods into a customer's vehicle when the sale physically takes place and monies have been exchanged inside the place of business. A retail dealer licensee under this article shall be permitted to have a drive-in window and it shall be permissible for the licensee or licensee's employees to deliver distilled spirits through such window. Curb service type sales and sales to customers in vehicles parked in the parking area of the place of business are prohibited. Retail dealers licensed under this division may only sell distilled spirits for consumption off premises.
(Code 2008, § 3-202)
Sec. 8-52. - Hours of operation.
(a)
No retail dealer shall be in a place of business, or open a place of business, or furnish, sell or offer for sale any distilled spirits at any of the following times:
(1)
At any time on Sunday, except as provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7;
(2)
At any time in violation of a local ordinance or regulation or of a special order of the board of commissioners; or
(3)
On any day before 7:00 a.m. or after 12:00 midnight.
(b)
The wholesale or retail sales of distilled spirits shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; provided however, the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place is unlawful during such time that the polls are open.
(Code 2008, § 3-203)
Sec. 8-53. - Retail dealers to store inventory only on-premises.
A licensed retail dealer shall keep no inventory or stock of distilled spirits at any place except the licensed place of business, and within the licensed place of business the storage area for distilled spirits shall be immediately adjacent to the retail area. No licensed retail dealer shall enter into any type of arrangement whereby distilled spirits ordered by the retail dealer are stored by a licensed wholesaler.
(Code 2008, § 3-204)
Sec. 8-54. - Gambling on-premises prohibited.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punchboard, slot machines, pinball machines, or the operation of any scheme for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any place of business licensed under this article, or in any room adjoining such business, owned, leased or controlled by a licensee. This prohibition shall not apply to the applicant's service as a retailer for the state lottery under O.C.G.A. § 50-27-17 or as otherwise permitted under the laws of the state. Any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of a license.
(Code 2008, § 3-205)
Sec. 8-55. - Unbroken packages.
A retail dealer may only sell distilled spirits in the original unbroken packages.
Secs. 8-56—8-81. - Reserved.
Subdivision II. - Licensure

Sec. 8-82. - Retail dealer license; applications; notice; fingerprints; bonds; restrictions; standards; hearing.
(a)
The business of manufacturing, distributing, selling, handling, and otherwise dealing in or possessing distilled spirits and alcoholic beverages are declared to be privileges and not rights; and such privileges shall not be exercised in the county except as licensed under the terms of this article.
(b)
Application for a retail dealer's license for the sale of distilled spirits for off-premises consumption shall be made on forms furnished by the county.
(c)
Each applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints which shall be forwarded to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation which shall search the files of the Georgia crime information center for a period of three years immediately preceding the date of such application for any instance of criminal activity. The Georgia Bureau of Investigation shall also submit such fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under rules established by the Federal Department of Justice for processing and identification records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the board of commissioners for use in considering such application.
(d)
Applicants must post with the board of commissioners along with their application, a personal performance bond; such bond to be conditioned upon the faithful observance and performance by the licensee of the rules and regulations contained in this article. Upon the violation of this article, or any part thereof, the amount of the bond to be forfeited will be determined by the board of commissioners in accordance with the nature of the violation after a hearing is held by the board of commissioners. Written notice of such hearing shall be given to the alleged violator not less than 15 days prior thereto. The determination by the board of commissioners to forfeit such bond may be in addition to any action taken by the board of commissioners under section 8-92. The bond shall be payable to the county in the amount of $10,000.00 and must be approved by the board of commissioners and shall be executed in the form specified by the board of commissioners. However, nothing in this article shall preclude the board of commissioners from waiving such bond requirement whenever a licensee has established sufficient financial responsibility or a record of faithful compliance with federal, state and county law.
(e)
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person, unless such person is of legal age to consume alcohol.
(f)
Only two retail dealer licenses under this division shall be issued to any one person in the county, and no person who holds more than two retail dealer licenses or has a beneficial interest in more than two retail dealer licenses in any other county or municipality in the state shall be eligible to receive a retail dealers license. The terms "person" and "beneficial interest" shall have the same meaning as used in O.C.G.A. § 3-4-21.
(g)
No employee of the county shall be eligible to receive a retail dealer's license.
(h)
The following standards shall be applied to all decisions pertaining to the issuance or denial of licenses under this division:
(1)
All applicants for a retail dealer's license must show financial responsibility. The board of commissioners may require all applicants to provide financial statements and other evidence of financial responsibility in conjunction with the application.
(2)
All applicants for a retail dealer's license must be of good character, and all operators, managers, clerks, and other employees shall be of like character. Corporate or firm applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(3)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state or local law for a criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which a license is being sought in conformity with federal, state or county laws.
(4)
A license application may be denied to any applicant for a retail dealer's license where it appears that the applicant would not have adequate financial participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or where it appears that the applicant is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever.
(5)
The board of commissioners, in its discretion, may consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application or the proposed location of the business. If in its judgment circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the applicant.
(i)
All decisions of the board of commissioners either approving or denying a license application shall be stated in writing and a copy of such statement shall be provided to the applicant either personally or by certified mail.
(j)
Whenever the board of commissioners shall deny an application for a retail dealer's license, the applicant shall have 15 days following notification of denial to request a hearing before the board of commissioners.
(k)
A corporate licensee applicant shall appoint and designate to the board of commissioners an agent authorized to receive service of process under the laws of the state.
(l)
Licensees shall be active and solely responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(Code 2008, § 3-206)
Sec. 8-83. - Premises.
No retail dealer's license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of such building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit for a proposed building to be built or renovated are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the county, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval by the building inspector when completed. Each such building shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all the outside premises of such building. Each applicant for a retail dealer's license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. A separate retail dealer's license shall be required for each place of business.
(Code 2008, § 3-207)
Sec. 8-84. - Distance requirements.
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location is situated not less than 100 yards from any church building, not less than 200 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, and not less than 100 yards from any building used as an alcohol rehabilitation center, the distance to be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground. Each application for a retail dealer's license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church building, school ground or campus, or alcohol rehabilitation center, or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this article with reference to this section.
(Code 2008, § 3-208)
Sec. 8-85. - Term of license; renewal.
A retail dealer's license shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and no such license may be renewed except upon approval of the board of commissioners.
(Code 2008, § 3-209)
Sec. 8-86. - License fee.
The annual fee for a retail dealer's license shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk and shall be paid prior to the issuance of such license. The fee shall accompany the application and shall be either in cash or a bank certified check. The annual fee shall be prorated if such license is issued after February 28 of any calendar year.
(Code 2008, § 3-210)
Sec. 8-87. - Excise tax.
There is levied an excise tax computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter which shall be paid to the board of commissioners on all distilled spirits sold in the county as permitted under O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80. Such tax shall be paid to the board of commissioners by the wholesaler on all distilled spirits sold to retail dealers in the county, as follows. Each wholesaler selling, shipping, or in any way delivering distilled spirits to any retail dealer, shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery to each retail dealer on or before the tenth day of the month following. The $0.22 per liter rate shall be prorated so that all containers of distilled spirits shall be taxed on the basis of $0.22 per liter. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any wholesaler to sell, ship or deliver in any manner any distilled spirits to a retail dealer without collecting such tax. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any retail dealer to possess, own, hold, store, display, or sell any distilled spirits on which such tax has not been paid. Each wholesaler shall be paid three percent of the amount of taxes collected as reimbursement for collection of such taxes.
(Code 2008, § 3-211)
Sec. 8-88. - Licenses not transferable.
No retail dealer's license issued under this article shall be transferable or assignable to any other person or location, and in the event that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender the license to the board of commissioners.
(Code 2008, § 3-212)
Sec. 8-89. - Display of license.
Licenses issued under this article shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which the license was issued.
(Code 2008, § 3-213)
Sec. 8-90. - Licensees to maintain a copy of the article; employees to be familiar with terms; responsibility of licensee for violations.
Each licensed retail dealer under this article shall keep a copy of this article in the licensed premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms of this article. Each licensee, the licensee's agents and employees selling distilled spirits shall at all times be familiar with the terms of this article. The licensee shall be responsible for any acts of agents or employees that are in violation of this article or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner.
(Code 2008, § 3-214)
Sec. 8-91. - Premises open for inspection.
The business premises of a licensed retail dealer under this article shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials representing the board of commissioners authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Code 2008, § 3-215)
Sec. 8-92. - Grounds for suspension or revocation of license; hearing.
(a)
No person shall engage in the sale of distilled spirits in the county without first complying with the rules and requirements set out in this article. Any license issued under this article shall be subject to a suspension or revocation upon any of the following grounds:
(1)
Making any statement in an application for a license issued under this chapter which shall be later found to be false;
(2)
Failure to faithfully comply with, or violating any of, the provisions of this chapter, and the state and federal laws and regulations;
(3)
Failure to maintain adequate financial responsibility upon which issuance of the license was conditioned or defaulting on any obligation, of any kind whatsoever, lawfully owing to the county;
(4)
Suspension or revocation of a state retail dealers license shall automatically operate to suspend or revoke any license issued under this article;
(5)
Any other legal and sufficient cause.
(b)
Any action taken by the board of commissioners to suspend or revoke a license issued under this article shall not preclude and may be in addition to, any criminal prosecution by a proper authority as provided by the laws and ordinances of the county, the state, or the United States. Whenever any action is taken by the board of commissioners to suspend or revoke any license issued under this article, the board of commissioners shall provide written notice to the licensee of the action taken and stating the reasons therefor. The licensee shall have 15 days following notification of such action to request a hearing before the board of commissioners. The licensee shall be entitled at such hearing to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses. The board of commissioners shall have the authority to appoint a hearing officer to conduct such hearing and, in such case, shall base its decision on its review of the record generated at such hearing; in such case the licensee shall have the right to address the board of commissioners when it reviews such record.
(Code 2008, § 3-216)

Subdivision III. - Sale of Distilled Spirits by the Drink for Consumption on the Premises Where Sold

Sec. 8-96. - Definitions.
As used in this subdivision the following terms are defined as follows:
Consumption on-premises means to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption only on the premises where sold.
License or license for consumption on-premises means a license issued pursuant to this section to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption only on the premises where sold. Such licenses shall be issued pursuant to the standards and procedures set forth in this section.
Licensee refers to the holder of a license to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption only on the premises where sold. A licensee shall also be authorized to sell malt beverages and wine for consumption only on such premises, subject to any other applicable requirements of state law and the Code of Oglethorpe County, 2007.
(Ord. of 2-4-2019)
Sec. 8-97. - Establishments eligible for licenses.
Licenses for consumption on-premises shall only be issued to persons who manage and operate a: (i) restaurant; or (ii) nonprofit club.
(1)
Restaurant. A restaurant means an established place of business actively in operation within Oglethorpe County where meals with substantial entrees selected by the customer from a full menu are served during all hours of operation with:
a.
A full-service kitchen;
b.
The serving of such meals being the principal business conducted, with the serving of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto; and
c.
A minimum serving schedule of at least two meals a day for at least five days a week, with the exception of recognized holidays, reasonable vacation time, and periods of actual redecoration.
(2)
Nonprofit club. A nonprofit club means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state which:
a.
Has been in existence and actively in operation within Oglethorpe County, Georgia, for at least one year immediately prior to the application for a license under this subdivision;
b.
Is organized and exists primarily for civic, social, or charitable purposes;
c.
Has members that participate and further the organization's purposes;
d.
Owns or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members; and
e.
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
(Ord. of 2-4-2019)
Sec. 8-98. - Consumption off-premises prohibited.
It is prohibited for consumers to leave the licensee's premises with open alcoholic beverages and it is the licensee's responsibility to ensure that no open alcoholic beverages are carried out.

Subdivision IV. - Off-premises Sale of Distilled Spirits by the Drink at an Authorized Catered Function

Sec. 8-112.1. - Definitions.
(a)
Qualifying licensee means a person who has been issued and holds: (1) a license for the sale of distilled spirits by the package for off-premises consumption; or (2) a license for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for on premises consumption, issued pursuant to this Code.
(b)
Licensed alcoholic beverage caterer, for purposes of this section, means any retail dealer of distilled spirits who is so licensed pursuant to Article 2, of Chapter 4, of Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia (O.C.G.A. § 3-4-2).
(c)
Authorized catered function shall mean an event for which an event permit has been issued pursuant to this section and O.C.G.A. § 3-11-3.
( Ord. No. 2021-02 , 9-7-2021)
Sec. 8-112.2. - Off-premises sale of distilled spirits by the drink at an authorized catered function.
The off-premises sale of distilled spirits by the drink at an authorized catered function by the holder of an off-premises license, as provided for herein, is hereby authorized, subject to the provisions of this section and applicable state law.
( Ord. No. 2021-02 , 9-7-2021)
Sec. 8-112.3. - Off-premises license.
(a)
A qualifying licensee which is also a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer shall be eligible to apply to the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County for an off-premises license authorizing the off-premises sale of distilled spirits by the drink at an authorized catered function, as provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-11-2(e).
(b)
An Off-premises license shall not be issued to any applicant which has been finally determined within the preceding five years to have violated any federal, state or county laws, ordinances, or regulations governing the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages.
(c)
The Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County shall establish an annual fee for an off-premises license which shall not exceed $5,000.00.
(d)
An off-premises license issued pursuant to this section shall be subject to revocation if the holder of such license ceases to be a qualifying licensee or licensed alcoholic beverage caterer, or if the holder violates or allows the violation of any federal, state, or local laws, ordinances, or regulations regarding the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages.
(e)
Any concealment or misstatement on an application for an off-premises license shall be grounds for its denial or revocation.
( Ord. No. 2021-02 , 9-7-2021)
Sec. 8-112.4. - Authorized catered function/event permit.
(a)
For an off-premises event where distilled spirits are to be sold to be deemed an authorized catered function as referenced in this section, the holder of an off-premises license must also apply for, and be issued, an event permit by the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County, as prescribed by O.C.G.A. § 3-11-3.
(b)
An application for an event permit, as referenced in this section, shall include the name and the state and local alcohol license numbers of the applicant; and the date, address, and time of the event.
(c)
The applicant for an event permit under this section must establish that it holds the licenses and permits required by this Code and by state law to sell distilled spirts, and that it is the holder of an off-premises license as referenced in this section, and is otherwise eligible under state law to be issued an event permit.
(d)
The holder of an event permit must comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, ordinances, and regulations in connection with the authorized catered function.
(e)
The holder of an event permit must, at its own expense, provide at least one security officer, approved by the sheriff or his designee, at each vehicle access point at the authorized catered function, or such other quantity and placement of security officers as is directed by the board of commissioners.
(f)
An event permit may only be issued, even if all the other requirements provided for herein are complied with, if the board of commissioners determines that the issuance of such a permit is consistent with the best interests of the public and is consistent with any applicable requirements of the Unified Development Code of Oglethorpe County.
(g)
The board of commissioners may impose conditions upon the issuance of an event permit to mitigate any negative impact of the authorized catered function as are consistent with the public interest.
(h)
Food must be available to attendees of the authorized catered function for which the event permit is issued.
(i)
No alcoholic beverage shall be sold, bartered, exchanged, given, provided, or furnished to any person at an authorized catered function who is in state of noticeable intoxication, as provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22.
(j)
An event permit shall be valid for a designated period of time not to exceed three consecutive days.
(k)
The board of commissioners may issue an event permit for an authorized catered function to a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer holding an off-premises license issued by a jurisdiction other than Oglethorpe County, subject to the requirements of this section. In such a circumstance, the board of commissioners may charge an event fee of $50.00 and levy local excise taxes on the total quantity of alcohol brought into Oglethorpe County by said caterer.
(l)
An event permit shall be subject to revocation if its holder violates any of the conditions or limitations thereof or violates any federal, state, or local laws, ordinances, or regulations in connection with the authorized catered function for which it was issued.
(m)
Any concealment or misstatement on an application for an event permit shall be grounds for its denial or revocation.
(n)
The holder of the event permit must provide Oglethorpe County with a copy of all reports submitted to the Georgia Department of Revenue regarding the quantity of any and all alcoholic beverages transported into Oglethorpe County to the location of the authorized catered function, and any other reports submitted to the Georgia Department of Revenue in connection with said function.
(o)
Any person dispensing alcoholic beverages pursuant to an event permit issued hereunder, and all persons consuming alcohol at an authorized catered function, shall comply with all applicable laws, ordinances, and regulations pursuant to the sale, distribution, consumption, and possession of alcoholic beverages.
( Ord. No. 2021-02 , 9-7-2021)
Sec. 8-112.5. - Violation and penalties.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell distilled spirits by the drink off-premises, except at an authorized catered function by the holder of an off-premises license who has been issued an event permit hereunder for said function or who has been issued, and is acting within the scope of, a special use permit for the sale of alcoholic beverages issued by the Georgia Revenue Commissioner pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-14-1.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the holder of an event permit hereunder to violate the terms or limitations of said permit.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any of the provisions of this subdivision.
(d)
Any person charged with violating this subdivision shall be tried in the Magistrate Court of Oglethorpe County in accordance with the provisions of this Code. Prosecution for such violations shall be upon citation issued and served upon the accused violator by the Oglethorpe County Sheriff or his deputy, or by an Oglethorpe County Code Enforcement Officer.
(e)
Any person convicted of violating this subdivision shall be punished by a fine of not more than $1,000.00 per offense or by imprisonment in the county jail for a period not to exceed six months, or by both fine and imprisonment, as provided by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60. Each act in violation of this subdivision shall constitute a separate offense.

DIVISION 3. - MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE
Subdivision I. - In General

Sec. 8-113. - Sales in unbroken package only; minimum package size.
All licensed retail dealers shall sell malt beverage and wine in the original unbroken package only and shall sell no package containing less than 200 milliliters.
(Code 2008, § 3-301)
Sec. 8-114. - Place of sale or delivery of goods.
No retail dealer shall sell or deliver any malt beverages or wine to any person except in such retail dealer's place of business. A retail dealer shall be permitted to load purchased goods in a customer's vehicle when the sale physically takes place and monies have been exchanged inside the place of business. A retail dealer licensee under this article shall be permitted to have a drive-in window and it shall be permissible for the licensee or licensee's employees to deliver malt beverages or wine through such window. Curb service type sales and sales to customers in vehicles parked in the parking area of the place of business are prohibited. Retail dealers licensed under this division may only sell malt beverages and wine for consumption off-premises.
(Code 2008, § 3-302)
Sec. 8-115. - Hours of operation.
(a)
No retail dealer shall furnish, sell or offer for sale any malt beverages or wine at any of the following times:
(1)
At any time on Sunday, except as provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7;
(2)
At any time in violation of a local ordinance or regulation or of a special order of the board of commissioners; or
(3)
On any day before 7:00 a.m. or after 12:00 midnight.
(b)
The wholesale or retail sales of alcohol or malt beverages and wine shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; provided, however, the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place is unlawful during such time that the polls are open.
(Code 2008, § 3-303)
Sec. 8-116. - Retail dealers to buy only from licensed wholesalers.
No licensed retail dealer shall buy or arrange to buy or in any way effect the transfer of any malt beverages or wine except from a licensed wholesaler.
(Code 2008, § 3-304)
Sec. 8-117. - Retail dealers to store inventory only on-premises.
A licensed retail dealer shall keep no inventory or stock of malt beverages or wine at any place except the licensed place of business, and within the licensed place of business the storage area for malt beverages or wine shall be immediately adjacent to the retail area. No licensed retail dealer shall enter into any type of arrangement whereby malt beverages or wine ordered by the retail dealer are stored by a licensed wholesaler.
(Code 2008, § 3-305)
Sec. 8-118. - Gambling on-premises prohibited.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punchboard, slot machines, or the operation of any scheme for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any place of business licensed under this article, or in any room adjoining such business, owned, leased or controlled by a licensee. This prohibition shall not apply to the applicant's service as a retailer for the state lottery under O.C.G.A. § 50-27-17 or as otherwise permitted under the laws of the state. Any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of a license.
(Code 2008, § 3-306)
Secs. 8-119—8-149. - Reserved.
Subdivision II. - Licensure

Sec. 8-150. - Retail dealer license; applications; notice; fingerprints; bonds; restrictions; standards; hearing.
(a)
The business of manufacturing, distributing, selling, handling, and otherwise dealing in or possessing malt beverages or wine and alcoholic beverages are declared to be privileges and not rights; and such privileges shall not be exercised in the county except as licensed under the terms of this article.
(b)
Application for a retail dealer's license for the sale of malt beverages and wine for off-premises consumption shall be made on forms furnished by the county. A notice of each application shall be advertised in the official newspaper of the county once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the board of commissioners in which the application is to be acted upon.
(c)
Each applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints which shall be forwarded to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation (GBI) who shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for a period of three years immediately preceding the date of such application for any instance of criminal activity. The Georgia Bureau of Investigation shall also submit such fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the board of commissioners considering such application.
(d)
Applicants must post with the board of commissioners, along with their application, a personal performance bond; such bond to be conditioned upon the faithful observance and performance by the licensee of the rules and regulations contained in this article. Upon the violation of this article, or any part thereof, the amount of the bond to be forfeited will be determined by the board of commissioners in accordance with the nature of the violation after a hearing is held by the board of commissioners. Written notice of such hearing shall be given to the alleged violator not less than 15 days prior thereto. The determination by the board of commissioners to forfeit such bond may be in addition to any action taken by the board of commissioners under section 8-159. The bond shall be payable to the county in the amount of $10,000.00 and must be approved by the board of commissioners and shall be executed in the form specified by the board of commissioners. However, nothing in this article shall preclude the board of commissioners from waiving such bond requirement whenever a licensee has established sufficient financial responsibility or a record of faithful compliance with the laws and regulations of the United States of America, the state and the county.
(e)
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person, unless such person is of legal age to consume alcohol.
(f)
Only two retail dealer licenses under this article shall be issued to any one person in the county, and no person who holds more than two retail dealer licenses or has a beneficial interest in more than two retail dealer licenses in any other county or municipality in the state shall be eligible to receive a retail dealer's license. The terms "person" and "beneficial interest" shall have the same meaning as used in O.C.G.A. § 3-4-21.
(g)
No employee of the county shall be eligible to receive a retail dealer's license.
(h)
The following standards shall be applied to all decisions pertaining to the issuance or denial of licenses under this article:
(1)
All applicants for a retail dealers license must show financial responsibility. The board of commissioners may require all applicants to provide financial statements and other evidence of financial responsibility in conjunction with the application.
(2)
All applicants for a retail dealer's license must be of good character, and all operators, managers, clerks, and other employees shall be of like character. Corporate or firm applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(3)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state or local law for a criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which a license is being sought in conformity with federal, state or county laws.
(4)
A license application may be denied to any applicant for a retail dealer's license where it appears that the applicant would not have adequate financial participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or where it appears that the applicant is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever.
(5)
The board of commissioners, in its discretion, may consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application or the proposed location of the business. If, in its judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the applicant.
(i)
All decisions of the board of commissioners either approving or denying a license application shall be stated in writing and a copy of such statement shall be provided to the applicant either personally or by certified mail.
(j)
Whenever the board of commissioners shall deny an application for a retail dealer's license, the applicant shall have 15 days following notification of denial to request a hearing before the board of commissioners.
(k)
A corporate licensee applicant shall appoint and designate to the board of commissioners an agent authorized to receive service of process under the laws of the state.
(l)
Licensees shall be active and solely responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(Code 2008, § 3-307)
Sec. 8-151. - Premises.
No retail dealer's license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of such building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit for a proposed building to be built or renovated are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the county, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval by the building inspector when completed. Each such building shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all the outside premises of such building. Each applicant for a retail dealer's license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. A separate retail dealer's license shall be required for each place of business.
(Code 2008, § 3-308)
Sec. 8-152. - Distance requirements.
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location is situated not less than 100 yards from any church building, not less than 200 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, and not less than 100 yards from any building used as an alcohol rehabilitation center, such distance is to be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground. Each application for a retail dealer's license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church building, school ground or campus, or alcohol rehabilitation center, or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this article with reference to this section.
(Code 2008, § 3-309)
Sec. 8-153. - Term of license; renewal.
A retail dealer's license shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and no such license may be renewed except upon approval of the board of commissioners. A licensee who desires to continue in business during a subsequent calendar year must renew the license for such year prior the expiration of the current license.
(Code 2008, § 3-310)
Sec. 8-154. - License fee.
The annual fee for a retail dealer's license shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk and shall be paid prior to the issuance of such license. The fee shall accompany the application and shall be either in cash or a bank certified check. The annual fee shall be prorated if such license is issued after February 28 of any calendar year.
(Code 2008, § 3-311)
Sec. 8-155. - Licenses not transferable.
No retail dealer's license issued under this article shall be transferable or assignable to any other person or location, and in the event that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender the license to the board of commissioners.
(Code 2008, § 3-312)
Sec. 8-156. - Display of license.
Licenses issued under this article shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which the license was issued.
(Code 2008, § 3-313)
Sec. 8-157. - Licensees to maintain a copy of the article; employees to be familiar with terms; responsibility of licensee for violations.
Each licensed retail dealer under this article shall keep a copy of this article in the licensed premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms of this article and each licensee, the licensee's agents and employees selling malt beverages or wine shall at all times be familiar with the terms of this article. The licensee shall be responsible for any acts of agents or employees that are in violation of this article or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner.
(Code 2008, § 3-314)
Sec. 8-158. - Premises open for inspection.
The business premises of a licensed retail dealer under this article shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials representing the board of commissioners authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Code 2008, § 3-315)
Sec. 8-159. - Grounds for suspension or revocation of license.
(a)
No person shall engage in the sale of malt beverages or wine in the county without first complying with the rules and requirements set out in this article. Any license issued under this article shall be subject to a suspension or revocation upon any of the following grounds:
(1)
Making any statement in an application for a license issued under this chapter which shall be later found to be false;
(2)
Failure to faithfully comply with, or violating any of, the provisions of this chapter, and the laws and regulations of the United States and the State of Georgia;
(3)
Failure to maintain adequate financial responsibility upon which issuance of the license was conditioned or defaulting on any obligation, of any kind whatsoever, lawfully owing to the county;
(4)
Suspension or revocation of a state retail dealers license shall automatically operate to suspend or revoke any license issued under this article;
(5)
Any other legal and sufficient cause.
(b)
Any action taken by the board of commissioners to suspend or revoke a license issued under this article shall not preclude, and may be in addition to, any criminal prosecution by a proper authority as provided by the laws and ordinances of the county, the state, or the United States. Whenever any action is taken by the board of commissioners to suspend or revoke any license issued under this article, the board of commissioners shall provide written notice to the licensee of the action taken and stating the reasons therefor. The licensee shall have 15 days following notification of such action to request a hearing before the board of commissioners. The licensee shall be entitled at such hearing to present evidence and cross examine opposing witnesses. The board of commissioners shall have the authority to appoint a hearing officer to conduct such hearing and, in such case, shall base its decision on its review of the record generated at such hearing; in such case the licensee shall have the right to address the board of commissioners when it reviews such record.
(Code 2008, § 3-316)

DIVISION 4. - SPECIAL EVENT PERMITS

Sec. 8-187. - Intent.
The intent of this division is to address public safety and nuisance issues related to alcohol consumption at special events in the county by regulating the sale of malt beverages and wine at authorized special events. Uncontrolled alcohol consumption at special events has also caused public expenditures which may be reduced by enforcing this division.
(Code 2008, § 3-401)
Sec. 8-188. - Issuance of special events permits authorized.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2, the county board of commissioners authorizes the issuance of a permit to qualified applicants, upon approval by the board of commissioners, to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink for consumption only on the premises and only in connection with an authorized special event within the scope of the event permit. The board of commissioners maintains discretion concerning the conditions of a special event, including, but not limited to, privileges related to alcoholic beverages.
(b)
All decisions approving, denying, suspending, or revoking a special event permit shall be in writing, with the reasons therefor stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the applicant.
(c)
Upon timely application, any applicant aggrieved by the decision of the board of commissioners regarding a permit shall be afforded a hearing.
(d)
The county is authorized to charge a special event permit fee in the amount provided in the county fee schedule.
(Code 2008, § 3-402)
Sec. 8-189. - Requirements and conditions for issuance of a special events permit.
(a)
To be eligible to be issued a special event permit, an applicant must:
(1)
Hold a state special event alcoholic beverage permit, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-14-1; or
(2)
Be a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer as defined by O.C.G.A. § 3-11-1.
(b)
A special event permit shall be issued only as an expressed condition of a special event which has been approved by the board of commissioners.
(c)
At the event for which the special event permit is issued:
(1)
Food must be available to event attendees.
(2)
The producer of the special event shall provide, at their expense, at least one security officer, approved by the sheriff, or his designee, at each vehicle access point.
(d)
It shall be otherwise unlawful for a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to distribute or sell distilled spirits in the county without a special events permit or outside the scope of the permit. The county does not permit the sale or distribution of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(e)
At the conclusion of the event for which the special event permit was issued, the applicant must provide the county with a copy of reports submitted to the state department of revenue stating the quantity of any and all alcoholic beverages transported from the applicant's primary premises to the location of the authorized event and such other information as required by the department of revenue.
(f)
Any misstatement or concealment of fact in the application shall be grounds for denial or revocation of the permit issued and shall subject the applicant to prosecution.
(g)
The granting of such a permit is a privilege subject to be annulled and revoked if the applicant is deemed not to conduct the event in accordance with the approved conditions of the application, ordinances of the county, or the laws of the state.
(h)
Notwithstanding the provisions of this chapter, any person dispensing or consuming beer or wine in accordance with this chapter shall comply with all other laws and ordinances pertaining to the distribution, sale, possession and consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(i)
The applicant must maintain original local event permits and documents required by the department of revenue in the vehicle transporting the alcoholic beverages to the catered function at all times.
(j)
It shall be unlawful for an alcoholic beverage caterer or the holder of a special event to employ any person less than 21 years of age who, in the course of such employment, would dispense, serve, sell, or handle alcoholic beverages.
(Code 2008, § 3-403)
Sec. 8-190. - Excise tax.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60, there is levied a local excise tax on the total quantity of wine sold or used in the county by the special event holder which shall be paid to the board of commissioners by the special event holder, as follows:
(1)
On the first sale or use of wine by the package, $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(2)
The taxes shall be paid within ten days of the date of the special event in which the beverages are sold or disposed of within the county.
(3)
Each permittee responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report itemizing the exact quantities of malt beverages and wines, by size and type of container, sold during the special event within the county. The permittee shall file the report with the county board of commissioners.
(Code 2008, § 3-404)
Secs. 8-191—8-218. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - CONSUMPTION ON PREMISES
Subdivision I. - In General

Sec. 8-219. - Consumption on premises.
As used in this division, the term "consumption on premises" means to sell, serve, or furnish malt beverages and wine for consumption by the drink on the premises at retail only to consumers and not for resale. Licenses for consumption on premises shall be issued by the board of commissioners pursuant to the rules and regulations set forth in this article. Nothing in this article shall be construed to permit any licensee to sell, serve, or furnish distilled spirits to consumers for consumption on its premises.
(Code 2008, § 3-501)
Sec. 8-220. - Restaurants.
Licenses for consumption on-premises only shall only be issued to persons who manage and operate a restaurant or nonprofit club.
(1)
The term "restaurant" means an established place of business actively in operation within the county where meals with substantial entrees selected by the customer from a full menu are served during all hours of operation with:
a.
A full-service kitchen;
b.
The serving of such meals being the principal business conducted, with the serving of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto; and
c.
A minimum serving schedule of at least two meals a day for at least five days a week, with the exception of recognized holidays, reasonable vacation time and periods of actual redecoration.
(2)
The term "nonprofit club" means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of the state which:
a.
Has been in existence and actively in operation in the county for at least one year immediately prior to the application for a license hereunder;
b.
Is organized and exists primarily for civic, social or charitable purposes;
c,
Has members that participate and further the organization's purpose;
d.
Owns or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members; and
e.
Has no member, officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages, other than a fixed salary.
(Code 2008, § 3-502)
Sec. 8-221. - Consumption off premises prohibited.
It is prohibited for consumers to the leave the licensed business premises with open alcoholic beverages and it is the licensee's responsibility to ensure that no open alcoholic beverages are carried out.
(Code 2008, § 3-503)
Sec. 8-222. - Hours of operation.
(a)
No licensee under this article shall sell, offer for sale, serve, or otherwise furnish any malt beverages or wine at any of the following times:
(1)
At any time on Sunday, except as provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7;
(2)
At any time in violation of a local ordinance or regulation or of a special order of the board of commissioners; or
(3)
On any day before 12:00 noon or after 10:00 p.m.
(b)
The sale of malt beverages and wine for consumption on premises shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; provided, however, the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place is unlawful during such time that the polls are open.
(Code 2008, § 3-504)
Sec. 8-223. - Licensees to buy only from licensed wholesalers.
No licensee under this article shall buy or arrange to buy or in any way effect the transfer of any malt beverages or wine except from a licensed wholesaler.
(Code 2008, § 3-505)
Sec. 8-224. - Inventory stored only on premises.
A licensee under this article shall keep no inventory or stock of malt beverages or wine at any place except the licensed place of business. No licensee under this article shall enter into any type of arrangement whereby malt beverages or wine ordered by the licensee are stored by a licensed wholesaler.
(Code 2008, § 3-506)
Sec. 8-225. - Employment of minors.
No licensee shall allow or require any employee less than 19 years of age, or the minimum age designated by state law, whichever is greater, to dispense, serve, sell, or take orders for any malt beverage or wine.
(Code 2008, § 3-507)
Sec. 8-226. - Gambling on-premises prohibited.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punchboard, slot machines, or the operation of any scheme for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any place of business licensed under this article, or in any room adjoining such business, owned, leased or controlled by a licensee. This prohibition shall not apply to:
(1)
Nonprofit bingo if the applicant is licensed under O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20 et seq.; or
(2)
Georgia Lottery sales if the licensee is issued under O.C.G.A. § 50-27-1 et seq.
Any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of a license.
(Code 2008, § 3-508)

If, in its judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the application.
(i)
All decisions of the board of commissioners either approving or denying a license application shall be stated in writing and a copy of such statement shall be provided to the applicant either personally or by certified mail.
(j)
Whenever the board of commissioners shall deny an application for a license under this article, the applicant shall have 15 days following notification of denial to request a hearing before the board of commissioners. The applicant shall be entitled to such hearing to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
(k)
A corporate licensee applicant shall appoint and designate to the board of commissioners an agent authorized to receive service of process under the laws of the state.
(l)
Licensees shall be active and solely responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.

Sec. 8-247. - Premises.
No license shall be issued under this article to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of such building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit for a proposed building to be built or renovated are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the county, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval by the building inspector when completed. Each such building shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside dining area of the building and so as to reveal all the outside premises of such building. Each applicant for a license under this article shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. A separate license shall be required for each place of business.
(Code 2008, § 32-510)
Sec. 8-248. - Term of license; renewal.
A license issued under this article shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and no such license may be renewed except upon approval of the board of commissioners. A licensee who desires to continue in business during a subsequent calendar year must renew its license for such year prior the expiration of the current license.
(Code 2008, § 32-511)
Sec. 8-249. - License fee.
The annual fee for a license issued under this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk and shall be paid prior to the issuance of such license. The fee shall accompany the application and shall be either in cash or a bank certified check. The annual fee shall be prorated if such license is issued after February 28 of any calendar year.
(Code 2008, § 32-512)
Sec. 8-250. - Licenses not transferable.
No license issued under this article shall be transferable or assignable to any other person or location, and in the event that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender the license to the board of commissioners.
(Code 2008, § 32-513)
Sec. 8-251. - Display of license.
Licenses issued under this article shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which the license was issued.
(Code 2008, § 32-514)
Sec. 8-252. - Licensees to maintain a copy of article; employees to be familiar with terms; responsibility of licensee for violations.
Each licensee under this article shall keep a copy of this article in the licensed premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms of this article and each licensee, the licensee's agents and employees selling malt beverages or wine, shall at all times be familiar with the terms of this article. The licensee shall be responsible for any acts of agents or employees that are in violation of this article or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner.
(Code 2008, § 32-515)
Sec. 8-253. - Premises open for inspection.
The business premises of a licensee under this article shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials representing the board of commissioners authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Code 2008, § 32-516)
Sec. 8-254. - Grounds for suspension or revocation of license.
(a)
No person shall engage in the sale of malt beverages or wine for consumption on-premises in the county without first complying with the rules and requirements set out in this article. Any license issued under this article shall be subject to a suspension or revocation upon any of the following grounds:
(1)
Making any statement in an application for a license issued under this chapter which shall be later found to be false;
(2)
Failure to faithfully comply with, or violating any of, the provisions of this chapter, and federal and state laws and regulations;
(3)
Failure to maintain adequate financial responsibility upon which issuance of the license was conditioned or defaulting on any obligation, of any kind whatsoever, lawfully owing to the county;
(4)
Suspension or revocation of a state retail consumption dealers license shall automatically operate to suspend or revoke any license issued under this chapter;
(5)
Any other legal and sufficient cause.
(b)
Any action taken by the board of commissioners to suspend or revoke a license issued under this article shall not preclude, and may be in addition to, any criminal prosecution by a proper authority as provided by the laws and ordinances of the county, the state, or the United States. Whenever any action is taken by the board of commissioners to suspend or revoke any license issued under this article, the board of commissioners shall provide written notice to the licensee of the action taken and stating the reasons therefor. The licensee shall have 15 days following notification of such action to request a hearing before the board of commissioners. The licensee shall be entitled at such hearing to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses. The board of commissioners shall have the authority to appoint a hearing officer to conduct such hearing and, in such case, shall base its decision on its review of the record generated at such hearing; in such case the licensee shall have the right to address the board of commissioners when it reviews such record.
(Code 2008, § 34-207)
