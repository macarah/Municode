ARTICLE II. - PARKS

Sec. 54-36. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any persons violating any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-13, and, in addition, shall pay the cost of such action.
(Ord. of 5-2-85, § 1)
Sec. 54-37. - Hours of operation.
All public parks located in the unincorporated areas of the county operated by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers shall be opened from 6:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m. each day, unless otherwise posted at the entrance to such parks; provided, however, the hours when public parks are closed shall not apply to persons using the parks for purposes of launching or removing boats, fishing, or camping in areas designated for camping within such parks.
(Ord. of 5-2-85, § 1)
Sec. 54-38. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
No person shall use, possess or consume any alcoholic beverage in public within any public park located in the unincorporated area of the county. For the purposes of this section, alcoholic beverage shall mean beer, wine, liquor or malt beverage.
(Ord. of 5-2-85, § 1)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 6.

Footnotes:

Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.

Subdivision I. - General Provisions

Sec. 54-81. - Creation.
There is hereby created the county department of recreation and leisure services, referred to in this division as D.R.L.S.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 2-102)
Sec. 54-82. - Authority of county board of commissioners.
The D.R.L.S. shall be under the direct auspices of the county board of commissioners and shall abide by all rules, regulations, directives, and instructions of and by the board of commissioners of the county.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 2-103)
Sec. 54-83. - Supervision, responsibility and authority of D.R.L.S.
The D.R.L.S. shall have full supervision, responsibility, and authority over all activities heretofore conducted by the Thomson/McDuffie County recreation and parks department, rural public transportation department and the council on aging. It shall be the sole and exclusive responsibility of D.R.L.S. to promulgate programs, policies and procedures in order to carry out a full and thorough recreation program to satisfy the recreation and park needs of the county, to establish a dynamic and cost-effective recreation and parks program, to ensure full utilization of all recreational resources within the City of Thomson, the City of Dearing, and the county, and to improve those resources where necessary, and to unite and supervise all three communities' recreation and parks resources.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 2-104)
Sec. 54-84. - Creation of position of director.
There is hereby created the position of director of the county department of recreation and leisure services. The director shall report directly to the chairman of the county board of commissioners. However, the director shall also work with an advisory board created in subdivision II of this division.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 3-101)
Sec. 54-85. - Responsibility of director.
It shall be the responsibility of the director to plan, direct, organize, supervise, and implement all parks and recreation and other D.R.L.S. activities within the county.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 3-102)
Sec. 54-86. - Statement of qualifications of director prepared by advisory board.
The advisory board created in subdivision II of this division shall prepare a statement of qualifications and a job description for the director which shall be submitted to the county board of commissioners and the City of Thomson for their approval.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 3-103)
Sec. 54-87. - Salary of director.
The salary of the director shall be paid out of the operating budget of the D.R.L.S., with the advice and recommendations of the advisory board.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 3-104)
Sec. 54-88. - Budget recommendations of director.
(a)
The director will require that each D.R.L.S. division estimate their budget. With that information, the director will then put together a total department operational budget for consideration by each of the contributing local governments.
(b)
The director shall recommend a separate budget for capital improvements.
(Res. of 6-6-90, §§ 3-105, 3-106)
Sec. 54-89. - Funding.
(a)
Although the D.R.L.S. falls administratively under the auspices of the county board of commissioners, funding will be provided in accordance with the following formula:
(1)
McDuffie County: 60 percent.
(2)
City of Thomson: 39 percent.
(3)
City of Dearing: One percent.
(b)
The D.R.L.S. budget must be approved separately by each city and county government for each budget year.
(c)
Each government's contribution to capital improvements will be determined outside of the formula plan.
(d)
Each local government's contribution shall be reviewed annually with revisions made to maintain an equitable share in the operations of D.R.L.S.
(Res. of 6-6-90, §§ 6-102—6-105)
Secs. 54-90—54-115. - Reserved.
Subdivision II. - Advisory Board[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
Cross reference— Authorities, boards and commissions, § 2-201 et seq.

Sec. 54-116. - Creation; composition.
There is hereby created the advisory board to the department of recreation and leisure services, consisting of 12 members representing governmental entities as follows:
(1)
Five residents living in the unincorporated county.
(2)
Four residents living within the incorporated City of Thomson.
(3)
Two residents living within the incorporated City of Dearing.
(4)
One representative from the county board of education without regard to residence.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 4-101)
Sec. 54-117. - Community interests represented.
The 12-member recreation and leisure services advisory board shall represent the following interests:
(1)
Two members from the Thomson City Council.
(2)
One member from the Dearing City Council.
(3)
Two members from the county board of commissioners.
(4)
One member of the county board of education.
(5)
Three members who represent the community's recreation interests, two to be appointed by the county board of commissioners and one by the City of Thomson.
(6)
Three members who represent the community's aging interests, two to be appointed by the county board of commissioners and one by the City of Thomson.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 4-102)
Sec. 54-118. - Duties and responsibilities.
The recreation and leisure services advisory board shall have the following duties and responsibilities:
(1)
Prescribe and set forth the qualifications and job description of the director of D.R.L.S.
(2)
Make recommendations to the county board of commissioners as to salary, benefits, and budget of the D.R.L.S.
(3)
Observe and listen, take the pulse of the community and bring that back as advice to the director.
(4)
Perform strategic planning and present it to the county board of commissioners for approval.
(5)
Establish a forum for discussion and advise the director as to solutions to problems within the D.R.L.S.
(6)
Interface with other recreation agencies such as 4-H, scouts, hospital wellness programs, the school board, etc.
(7)
Elect a chairman and secretary, and such other officers and committees as it deems necessary to properly carry out the recreation and parks and other D.R.L.S. programs of the county.
(8)
Hold and conduct meetings at such times and places as it deems appropriate for the carrying out of its duties.
(9)
Solicit and receive input, recommendations, and advice from any and all organizations or agencies within the county which have any connection with recreation, to conclude by way of specification but not limitation, 4-H, school coaches, hospital, board of education, church youth groups and scouts.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 4-103)
Sec. 54-119. - Appointment; terms.
Members of the recreation and leisure services advisory board shall be appointed by the appropriate governmental agency for the initial term expiring December 31, 1992, and thereafter for two-year terms expiring December 31 of each even-numbered year. Members may succeed themselves.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 4-104)

DIVISION 3. - INTER-AGENCY RECREATION COUNCIL[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
Cross reference— Authorities, boards and commissions, § 2-201 et seq.

Sec. 54-136. - Creation; composition.
There is hereby created the inter-agency recreation council, which shall consist of a group of citizens appointed by the county with the advice of the City of Thomson and the City of Dearing, which council shall consist of a number of not less than ten nor more than 25 for terms prescribed in the sole discretion of the county board of commissioners.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 5-101)
Sec. 54-137. - Meetings.
The inter-agency recreation council shall meet as often as they deem necessary but in no event less than quarterly, and shall organize themselves with officers and committees as they deem necessary and appropriate.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 5-102)
Sec. 54-138. - Interests represented.
The inter-agency recreation council shall represent the interests of groups involved and interested in recreation and leisure, to include but not be limited to 4-H and other programs of the state extension service, Thomson Junior Women's Club, county schools and the athletic team coaches, the county hospital, boy scouts, girl scouts, and local church youth and aging groups. The appointing authority shall ensure that the interests of all ages and backgrounds are represented on the council.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 5-103)
Sec. 54-139. - Responsibility to serve as forum for represented groups.
It shall be the responsibility of the county inter-agency recreation council to serve as a forum for discussion of common recreation and leisure service needs and opportunities and facilitate cooperation and joint effort between D.R.L.S. and other represented groups.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 5-104)
Sec. 54-140. - Recommendations made to advisory board.
Any and all recommendations of the inter-agency recreation council shall be made to the advisory board through its chairman.
(Res. of 6-6-90, § 5-105)
