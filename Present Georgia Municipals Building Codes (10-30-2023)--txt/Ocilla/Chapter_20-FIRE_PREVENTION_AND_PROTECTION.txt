ARTICLE II. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Local fire departments generally, O.C.G.A. § 25-3-1 et seq.; Georgia Firefighter Standards and Training Act, O.C.G.A. § 25-4-1 et seq.

Sec. 20-19. - Composition.
The Fire Department shall consist of a volunteer fire company, including a fire chief and one or more assistant fire chiefs, appointed by the Council. The Fire Chief shall have the control, direction and management of the company when on duty.
(Code 2003, § 3-2001)
Sec. 20-20. - Regulations.
The Fire Chief may make and enforce such regulations as the Chief shall deem necessary to the efficient training of the company, provided, any such regulation shall not in any way conflict with the City Charter or City ordinances.
(Code 2003, § 3-2002)
Sec. 20-21. - Apparatus.
The reels, fire hose and all fire apparatus shall be under the management and control of the Fire Chief, who shall be responsible for their care, protection and safe keeping.
(Code 2003, § 3-2003)
Sec. 20-22. - Reports by Fire Chief.
The Fire Chief shall keep informed as to the condition of the Fire Department and of the apparatus and shall report to the Council any repairs that need to be made in the apparatus and when replacements are necessary.
(Code 2003, § 3-2004)
Sec. 20-23. - Tampering with equipment.
It shall be unlawful for any person willfully to interfere with any cistern, hose, hose cart, truck, hook, ladder or other apparatus, implement or material of any kind connected with or belonging to the Fire Department.
(Code 2003, § 3-2005)
Sec. 20-24. - Removing apparatus.
It shall be unlawful for any person to take from the place of storage any instrument, tool, ladder or other apparatus belonging to the Fire Department, without the consent of the Mayor, except in case of fire, or when a fire alarm is given.
(Code 2003, § 3-2006)
Sec. 20-25. - Interfering with Fire Department.
No person shall interfere with the work or impede the progress of the Fire Department.
(Code 2003, § 3-2007)
State Law reference— False fire alarm, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-27.
Sec. 20-26. - Notification of fire.
Upon notification of a fire, a member of the Fire Department shall immediately telephone the suppliers of the City of water, gas and electricity.
(Code 2003, § 3-2008; Ord. of 9-5-1967, ¶ 1)
Sec. 20-27. - Voluntary firefighters covered under workers compensation insurance.
(a)
All voluntary firefighters of the City, as are presently listed and as may be hereafter listed of record in the office of the City Clerk, are deemed to be covered by the policy of workers compensation insurance presently in force and effect for City employees.
(b)
This policy of including all voluntary firefighters listed in the files of the City Clerk's office under coverage of workers compensation insurance shall be deemed continuing and of full force and effect, regardless of whether the present insurance policy is continued or another insurance policy secured, until such time as the City, by majority action, reversed or discontinues this policy of covering voluntary firefighters.
(Code 2003, § 3-2009; Res. of 9-6-1983)
