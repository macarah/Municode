Sec. 20-1. - Penalty.
Unless stated otherwise, violations of this chapter are punishable as provided in section 1-7.
Sec. 20-2. - Nuisance abatement.
(a)
The city is authorized pursuant to the police powers granted to it and its Charter to repair, close, or demolish buildings or structures in the manner provided in this section.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall also be applied to private property, when an accumulation of weeds, trash, junk, filth, and other unsanitary or unsafe conditions shall create a public health hazard or general nuisance to those persons residing in the vicinity. A finding by any governmental health department, health officer, or building inspector that such property is a health or safety hazard shall constitute prima facie evidence that said property is in violation of this section.
(c)
For purposes of this section, the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 41-2-8 shall have the same meaning for such words used herein.
(d)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 41-2-9(a) through (f) are hereby incorporated herein and made a part of this section.
(e)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 41-2-10 and 41-2-11 insofar as such provisions are not inconsistent with O.C.G.A. § 41-2-9 as amended are hereby adopted and made a part hereof and the powers granted in O.C.G.A. § 41-2-11 to the public officer are hereby expressly adopted for the purpose of the public officer carrying out the duties pursuant to this section.
(f)
This section shall be construed and is intended to be read in a manner consistent with the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 41-2-7 through 41-2-16.
