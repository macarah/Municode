Sec. 6-108. - Adoption of National Electrical Code.
There is hereby adopted by the mayor and city council, for the purpose of establishing standards and procedures affecting the installation of electrical systems and electrical materials, the National Electrical Code, 1984 edition, being particularly that code published by the National Fire Protection Association, of which code not less than one (1) copy has been and now is filed in the office of the city clerk, and the same is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein, and the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the corporate limits of the city. All subsequent changes or edition improvements shall be adopted and incorporated for the city upon publication by the National Fire Protection Association.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-109. - Requirements for equipment and appliances.
No electrical materials, devices or appliances shall be sold, used or installed in the city unless they are in conformity with the provisions of this article, the statutes of the state, the rules and regulations of the city, and the rules and regulations of the National Board of Fire Underwriters and are in conformity with the approved methods of construction for safety to life and property.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-110. - Equipment, appliances to be labeled.
The maker's name, trademark or other identification symbol, along with the label of the Board of Underwriter's Laboratory, shall be placed on all electrical equipment, devices and appliances used or installed under this article. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove any said labels.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-111. - Temporary wiring and service.
(a)
Permission required; purposes. Temporary wiring may be installed when written permission is secured from the building official for the following purposes:
(1)
To be used to supply power for the purpose of hoisting materials used in buildings under construction or being remodeled, and for the purpose of lighting said building or structure used in connection with the construction of such buildings.
(2)
For the purpose of lighting tents or buildings used for religious gatherings or shows where said tents or buildings will only be used for a period of fourteen (14) days, or for decorative purposes where wiring will only be used for a period of fourteen (14) days.
(b)
Safety required. Temporary approvals may be used covering work as outlined in this section, when such work is installed in such manner as not to endanger life or property.
(c)
Authorized for testing or to permit use of completed installations. Temporary approvals may be issued for the purpose of allowing current to be turned on certain parts of wiring installations which are safe to the satisfaction of the building official in order to allow the testing of certain electrical equipment and in order to allow tenants, lessees or owners the use of certain completed parts of buildings before the entire job is completed.
(d)
Duration. No temporary approval shall be issued for a period of more than ninety (90) days. If it is necessary for temporary work to remain, request for an extension of the temporary approval shall be made in writing by the person holding the permit.
(e)
Discontinuance upon expiration. Upon expiration of temporary approval such service shall be immediately discontinued, unless approval for an extension of time is obtained from the building official.
(f)
Contents of application. The application for temporary service shall state the period of time the service is required and the necessity for same.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)
Sec. 6-112. - Alterations in wiring or load prohibited.
No alterations shall be made in wiring of any building or structure for light, heat, or power, or increase in the rated load as fixed by the building official.
(Ord. No. 89-6, 3-16-89)

Sec. 6-114. - Permit fees.
[Permit fees for work performed under this article shall be as follows:]
For issuing each permit .....$10.00
(a)
Residential. Electrical, plumbing, heating and air conditioning values shall be based on one dollar and fifty cents ($1.50) per square foot. Permit cost shall be determined on two (2) per cent of the total value.
(b)
Commercial. Electrical, plumbing, heating and air conditioning permit fees shall be based on per centages, as follows:
(1)
Up to one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00), the total permit fee shall be based on two (2) per cent of the total cost.
(2)
More than one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) but not exceeding five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00), the total permit fee shall be based on one and three-fourths (1¾) per cent of the total cost.
(3)
More than five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00) but not exceeding one million dollars ($1,000,000.00), the total permit fee shall be based on one and one-half (1½) per cent of the total cost.
(4)
More than one million dollars ($1,000,000.00), the total permit fee shall be based on one (1) per cent of the total cost.
(Ord. No. 91-1, 1-3-91)
