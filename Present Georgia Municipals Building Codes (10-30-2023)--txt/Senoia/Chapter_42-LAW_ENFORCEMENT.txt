ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - Obstructing, hindering officers.
A person who knowingly and willfully obstructs or hinders any law enforcement officer in the lawful discharge of his official duties is guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. of 2-4-85, § 9-1-12)
Sec. 42-2. - Giving false information.
It shall be unlawful for any person to give a police officer a false name, address or date of birth with the intent of misleading the officer as to his identity.
(Ord. of 2-4-85, § 9-1-14)
Secs. 42-3—42-25. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 42-26. - Composition.
The police department shall consist of those officers and employees of those ranks and positions as approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1980, § 3-1-1)
Sec. 42-27. - Responsibilities.
The police department shall be responsible for patrol, traffic control, investigation of accidents, investigation of crimes, apprehension of offenders, court appearances, security of business establishments and for any other matters of public safety and law enforcement as directed by the mayor and council.
(Code 1980, § 3-1-2)
Sec. 42-28. - Volunteer police division.
The police department is authorized to create a volunteer police division. The officers of the volunteer division shall abide by the rules and regulations established for the volunteer division. They shall be unsworn, noncertified officers, whose function is to supplement the activities of the police department and whose authority does not possess the power of arrest.
(Code 1980, § 3-1-3)
