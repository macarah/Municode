ARTICLE II. - WATER
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 70-26. - Application for service; deposit.
The consumer shall make application for water service either in person at city hall, or online at www.Senoia.com. At the time of applying for water service, applicant shall make a cash security deposit for water service in an amount to be determined by the mayor and council and can be found on the Senoia fee schedule found at Senoia.com. Said deposit shall remain on deposit in the city treasury until such time as the account is closed out by resident.
(Code 1980, § 5-1-3; Ord. of 10-6-80, § 2; Ord. of 4-17-96; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-27. - Connection to water required before occupying building; authorized person to make connection.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to occupy any building of any kind unless the same is connected with the water system of the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person except an authorized employee of the city to connect with or tap any water main of the city, except with the specific authorization of the employee and under this supervision.
(Code 1980, § 5-1-4; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-28. - Connection fee.
Each consumer subscribing to use the water service of the city shall pay a connection fee in an amount determined by the mayor and council. The city fee schedule can be found online at www.Senoia.com.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 3; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-29. - Minimum charges; use at authorized location only.
The minimum charge, as provided in the rate of schedule, shall be made for the water connection subscribed for. Water furnished for a given lot shall be used on that lot only and, except for the fire protection, the city shall not under any condition furnish water free of charge to anyone.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 4; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-30. - City's responsibility and liability.
(a)
The city shall run a service line from its distribution line to the property line where the distribution line exists or is to be constructed, and runs immediately adjacent and parallel to the property to be served. No service charge, other than the connection fee referred to in section 70-28 will be made for a meter five-eighths inch by three-quarters inch in size. A proportionately greater charge than the connection fee will be made for a meter with larger dimensions.
(b)
The city may make connections to service other properties not adjacent to its lines upon payment of reasonable costs for the extensions of its distribution lines as may be required to render such service.
(c)
The city may install its meter at or near the property line, or at the city's option, on the consumer's property within three feet of the property line.
(d)
The city reserves the right to refuse service unless the consumer's lines or piping is installed in such a manner as to prevent cross connections or backflow.
(e)
Under normal conditions the consumer will be notified of any anticipated interruptions of service by the city.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 5; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-31. - Meters.
modified
(a)
As used in this article II, the term "consumer" means the one who uses the water supplied by the department.
(b)
Every regular consumer of water shall be supplied with a meter by the city, which meter shall be under the exclusive control of the city, for which the consumer shall deposit an amount as determined by the mayor and council and filed in the city clerk's office. This amount shall be refunded by the city if any consumer discontinues the use of city water, less all charges which may stand against that consumer.
(c)
Water meters shall be located at the property line adjacent to a street or alleyway or at any other location as determined by the city. The person in whose name the service is rendered shall be responsible for water rents.
(d)
Supply from one service. No more than one house or building shall be supplied from one service connection except by special permission of the council. Whenever two or more parties, or two or more residential, office, commercial, or industrial users or consumers are supplied from one pipe connecting with a service main, each such user or consumer shall have a separate meter.
(Code 1980, § 5-1-1; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021; Ord. No. 23-02 , § 1, 4-17-2023)
Sec. 70-32. - Schedule of water rates.
The schedule of water rates and connection fees, as set from time to time by the mayor and council, shall be kept current and maintained in the office of the city clerk. The fee schedule can also be found on the city website at www.senoia.com.
(Code 1980, § 5-1-2; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-33. - Consumer's responsibility and liability.
(a)
Water furnished by the city shall be used for consumption by the consumer, members of his household and employees only. The consumer shall not sell water to any other person or permit any other person to use the water. Water shall not be used for irrigation, fire protection nor other purposes except when water is available in sufficient quantity without interfering with the regular domestic consumption in the area served. Disregard for this rule shall be sufficient cause for refusal and/or discontinuance of service.
(b)
Where the meter or meter box is placed on the premises of a consumer, a suitable place shall be provided by the consumer therefore, unobstructed and accessible at all times to the meter reader.
(c)
The consumer shall furnish and maintain a private cutoff valve on the consumer's side of the meter.
(d)
The consumer's piping and apparatus shall be installed and maintained by the consumer at the consumer's expense, in a safe and efficient manner, and in accordance with the sanitary regulations of the state department of human resources.
(e)
In order to be received as a consumer and entitled to receive water from the city's water system, all applicants must offer proof that any private wells located on their property are not physically connected to the lines of the city's water system and all applicants by becoming consumers of the city covenant and agree that so long as they continue to be consumers of the city they will not permit the connection of any private wells on their property to the city's water system.
(f)
If damage to water meter, water meter box or water meter antennae occurs and damage is not directly caused by a city employee, it is the financial responsibility of the consumer, tenant, or homeowner to replace or repair the water meter, water meter box or water meter antennae located on said property. Fees for replacement or repair are approved by mayor and council. The fee schedule can be found at www.senoia.com.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 6; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-34. - Access to premises, extensions of system.
(a)
Duly authorized agents of the city shall have access at all reasonable hours to the premises of the consumer for the purpose of installing or removing city property, inspecting piping, reading and testing meters, or for any other purpose in connection with the water service and its facilities.
(b)
Extensions to the system shall be made only when the consumer shall grant or convey, or shall cause to be granted or conveyed, to the city as permanent easement of right-of-way across any property traversed by the water lines.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 7; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-35. - Meter reading; billing; collection.
(a)
Bills to customers for water service shall be mailed out on such day of each month as may be determined as desirable by the city. The failure of water users to pay charges duly imposed shall result in the automatic imposition of the following penalties:
(1)
Nonpayment within 15 days from the due date will be subject to a penalty of ten percent of the delinquent account.
(2)
Nonpayment by the 25 th day of the month will result in the water being shut off from the water user's property.
(3)
Nonpayment for 30 days after original date will allow the city, in addition to all other rights and remedies, to terminate the agreement, and in such event the water user shall not be entitled to receive, nor the city obligated to supply, any water under the agreement. The city, at its discretion, reserves the right to remove water meter from said premises for non-payment or theft of services. Before water services can be reconnected, consumer must lease a water meter from the city. Fee for leasing said meter are approved by mayor and council and can be found at www.senoia.com, as outlined in the fee schedule.
(b)
Water service shall not be reconnected until the customer's delinquent bill, including penalty and disconnection charges, have been paid in full. Should such customer thereafter desire to be reconnected to the water system, a reconnection charge as set by mayor and council shall be paid online or in person at the city hall. Failure to receive bills or notices shall not prevent the bills from becoming delinquent nor relieve the consumer from payment of the bills.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 9; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)

Sec. 70-37. - Change of occupancy; notice to city.
Not less than three days' notice must be given, in person or in writing, at the city hall to discontinue water service or to change occupancy. The departing person shall be responsible for all water consumed up to the time of departure or the time specified for departure, whichever period is longer. The new occupant shall apply for water service within 48 hours after occupying the premises and failure to do so will make him liable for paying for the water consumed since the last meter reading.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 8; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-38. - Suspension of service.
(a)
When water service is discontinued and all bills paid, the security deposit shall be refunded to the consumer by the city.
(b)
Upon discontinuance of service for nonpayment of bills, the security deposit will be applied by the city toward settlement of the account. Any balance will be refunded to the consumer; however, if the security deposit is insufficient to cover the bill, the city may proceed to collect the balance in the usual way provided by law for the collection of debts.
(c)
Service disconnected for nonpayment of bills will be restored only after bills are paid in full, such security deposit as may be required by the mayor and council is made, and a reconnection fee paid for each meter reconnected.
(d)
The city reserves the right to discontinue its service without notice for the following additional reasons:
(1)
To prevent fraud or abuse.
(2)
Consumer's willful disregard of the city's rules.
(3)
Emergency repairs.
(4)
Insufficiency of water supply due to circumstances beyond the city's control.
(5)
Legal processes.
(6)
Direction of public authorities.
(7)
Strike, riot, fire, flood, unavoidable accident.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 10; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-39. - Complaints, adjustments.
(a)
If the consumer believes his bill to be in error, he shall present his claim, in person, or submit claim online at www.senoia.com before the bill becomes delinquent. Such claim, if made after the bill has become delinquent, shall not be effective in preventing discontinuance of service as provided in this article. The consumer may pay such bill under protest and the payment shall not prejudice his claim.
(b)
If the meter or antennae is broken by other than the city's representative or if the meter fails to register correctly or is stopped for any cause, the consumer shall pay an amount estimated from the record of his previous bills and/or from other proper data.
(Ord. of 10-6-80, § 11; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-40. - Tampering with, removing meter.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove, tamper or in any way meddle with any water meter box, rim cover thereto or any part of any water meter box.
(Code 1980, § 5-1-5; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)
Sec. 70-41. - Taking water from fire hydrants, city-owned mains.
It shall be unlawful for any person to turn on and/or take water from any fire hydrant or water mains and lines owned by the city except pursuant to lawful permission from an authorized firefighter or city employee.
(Code 1980, § 5-1-6; Ord. No. 21-03 , § 1, 6-7-2021)

DIVISION 2. - RAW WATER PROTECTION

Sec. 70-51. - Declaration of policy.
It is unlawful to spray, discharge or dump any substance or commit any other act which contaminates or pollutes the raw water of the city water system. Items which would be included as contaminants or pollutants would include but not be limited to those items recognized by the state environmental protection division as being harmful to consumers if contained in potable water, those items listed on EPD's toxic list, insecticides and herbicides.
(Ord. of 8-1-88, § I)
Sec. 70-52. - Authorization.
The city council is hereby authorized and directed to implement the applicable provisions of this division for the public welfare and safety. The municipal court of the city will be responsible for determining whether or not a certain act constitutes a violation of this division.
(Ord. of 8-1-88, § II)
Sec. 70-53. - Enforcement.
(a)
Any police officer or other city employee designated by the city council may issue a citation to a person he reasonably believes to be in violation of this division. The citation shall be prepared in duplicate and shall contain the name and address of the alleged violator, if known, and the offense charged, and shall direct him to appear in the municipal court within five days of service of the citation. The alleged violator shall be requested to sign the citation and shall be served a copy of the citation. Service of the citation shall be complete upon the attempt to give it to the alleged violator, to an agent or employee of a violator, or to a person over 15 years of age who is a resident at the violator's residence. The alleged violator shall appear in municipal court to make his plea within five days of service of the citation, and failure to so appear shall be a violation of this division and subject the alleged violation to being held in contempt of court. A police officer may arrest for any offense under this division where permitted by state arrest law. Such cases shall be expedited and given preferential setting in municipal court before all other cases.
(b)
A person in apparent control of the property where the violation occurs or originates shall be presumed to be the violator, and proof of facts showing apparent control by such person of the premises and proof that the violation occurred on the premises shall constitute prima facie evidence that the person committed the violation, but the person shall have the right to show that he did not commit the violation. Any person whose name is on file as owner of the property where the violation occurs or originates shall be presumed to be the violator, and proof that the violation occurred on the premises shall constitute prima facie evidence that the owner committed the violation, but the customer shall have the right to show that he did not commit the violation. Parents shall be presumed to be responsible for violations of their minor children, and proof that a child committed a violation on property within the parent's control shall constitute prima facie evidence that the parent committed the violation, but the parent may be excused if he proves that he had previously directed the child with regard to this division and that the parent could not have reasonably known of the violation.
(Ord. of 8-1-88, § III)
DIVISION 3. - WATER CONSERVATION AND DROUGHT MANAGEMENT[2]

Sec. 70-54. - Purpose and policy.
The general welfare and public interest require that the water resources of Georgia be put to beneficial use to the fullest extent to which they are capable, subject to reasonable regulation in order to conserve such water resources, to protect natural systems, and to provide and maintain conditions that are conducive to the development and use of water resources. The General Assembly of Georgia has found and concluded that effective water resource management protects public health and the safety and welfare of Georgia's citizens. Water resources are to be managed in a sustainable manner so that current and future generations have access to adequate supplies of quality water that support both human needs and natural systems. All citizens have a stewardship responsibility to conserve and protect the water resources of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 07-07, § 1(1), 12-3-2007)
Sec. 70-55. - Goals and objectives.
In furtherance of the state's policy to conserve water resources and demand management goals, the City of Senoia, as a permitted water system operator, adopts the following goals and objectives:
(a)
To assure that first priority is given to providing water for human consumption and sanitation; all other purposes shall be secondary;
(b)
To control the rate of growth and development so as to minimize the demand on existing and planned water supply sources available to the City of Senoia and its customers in order to maximize overall utilization of this limited resource;
(c)
To reduce the upward trend of seasonal peak day demands that drive the costly expansion of water treatment, storage, and transmission facilities;
(d)
To improve system efficiency and operations by reducing unbillable or unaccounted for water loss on the system; and
(e)
To provide an orderly process for reducing system-wide demands during periods of drought or other emergency water shortages.
(Ord. No. 07-06, § 1(2), 12-3-2007)
Sec. 70-56. - Year-round water conservation practices.
(a)
All City of Senoia Water System (the "system") users are encouraged to exercise voluntary water conservation practices at all times of the year, regardless of drought conditions, water shortage, or emergency condition. The city may periodically provide informational guidance and conservation tips to its users.
(b)
All newly installed or substantially improved irrigation systems, which use City of Senoia Water System water shall be equipped with automatic timers with rain or soil moisture sensors that activate to prevent operation of those systems while rain is falling and/or when soil moisture is adequate.
(c)
Outdoor watering and non-essential outdoor water usage from the City of Senoia Water System for all homes and businesses shall follow restrictions imposed by the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, as from time to time amended, consistent with the level of drought; Rules and Regulations of the State of Georgia, Department of Natural Resources, Environmental Protection Division, Chapter 391-3-30, by express reference, is incorporated herein, together with the exemptions set forth therein.
(Ord. No. 07-06, § 1(3), 12-3-2007)
Sec. 70-57. - Drought or other water use restrictions.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 12-5-102, the Director of the Environmental Protection Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources may, from time to time, impose by permit, administrative or emergency order use restrictions on the City of Senoia Water System.
(b)
In addition thereto and supplemental thereof, water use restrictions may be imposed whenever the City of Senoia Water System cannot supply its customers with sufficient water to protect the public health and safety without substantial reductions in water usage. Whenever it is determined, in writing, by the system's consulting engineer that the system is unable or reasonably unlikely to continue meeting its customer demand without a substantial reduction in consumption, the mayor and council, after consultation with the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, Environmental Protection Division, shall issue an administrative order imposing water use restrictions using such criteria, if any, set forth by the Georgia Environmental Protection Division.
(Ord. No. 07-06, § 1(4), 12-3-2007)
Sec. 70-58. - Water use emergency.
Any other circumstances, including service losses caused by failure of equipment or facilities, human error, weather, or natural disaster, which constrain the system's water production capacity to less than the current level of customer demand may constitute a water use emergency. In the event the city administrator determines reasonable cause exists to declare a water use emergency, authority is hereby delegated to the city administrator, under the criteria set forth above, to issue an administrative order imposing immediate use restrictions on all users of the city water system. Orders issued by the administrator under this section shall only restrict such non-essential uses as necessary to achieve system objectives, and may be modified, as needed, during a water use emergency to achieve the system's highest priority of providing water for human consumption and sanitation over all other water uses.
(Ord. No. 07-06, § 1(4), 12-3-2007)
Sec. 70-59. - Notice of mandatory water conservation practices, water use restrictions, and/or water use emergency.
Customers and users of the City of Senoia Water System shall be given notice of water use restrictions by the best means available, including without limitation, inclusion of a notice in monthly utility bills and/or publication in a newspaper of general circulation within the system's service area. Public service announcements through television, radio and posting on the City of Senoia's website, may also be utilized.
(Ord. No. 07-06, § 1(5), 12-3-2007)
Sec. 70-60. - Enforcement of mandatory water conservation practices, water use restrictions, and water supply emergency orders.
(a)
It shall be a violation of this division for any person, firm, or corporation to use or permit the use of potable water from the City of Senoia Water System for any purpose or use restricted or prohibited by mandatory water conservation practices established by the Department of Natural Resources, by a permit, administrative or emergency order imposed by the Director of the Environmental Protection Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, by an administrative order of the mayor and council imposing water use restrictions, or in a water use emergency as declared by this division.
(b)
Violators shall be cited to appear before the Municipal Court of the City of Senoia, or other court of appropriate jurisdiction within the system's customer service area, and upon conviction shall be subject to a fine as set forth in section 1-5 of this Code, or, if applicable, charged upon state warrant with committing a misdemeanor as defined by general law.
(c)
In addition thereto, in the sole discretion of the city administrator, a water use surcharge, in an amount double the customer's most recent monthly water bill, may be imposed on any customer for a first violation of this division; and a water use surcharge, in an amount triple the customer's most recent monthly water bill, may be imposed for a second or subsequent violation of this division. For any three or more violations within any 12 month period, water service shall be discontinued to any customer of the City of Senoia Water System that willfully or systematically violates restrictions or prohibitions on water usage after written notice from the city administrator of the facts establishing such violation. When service has been discontinued under this section, service shall not be reinstated unless the customer posts a surety bond in the amount of $5,000.00, payable to the City of Senoia, conditioned on faithful adherence to all water use policies and restrictions of the system.
(d)
The city administrator is hereby authorized, as necessary, to seek from any court of appropriate equitable jurisdiction, injunctive relief against any user of the City of Senoia Water System for summary abatement or remedying of appropriate conditions dangerous or prejudicial to the public health and safety, together with recovery of the costs and legal expenses thereof.
(Ord. No. 07-06, § 1(6), 12-3-2007)

DIVISION 4. - CROSS-CONNECTION AUXILIARY INTAKES, BY-PASSES, AND INTER-CONNECTIONS TO THE CITY WATER SYSTEM

Sec. 70-63. - Definitions.
The following definitions and terms shall apply in the interpretation of this ordinance:
Auxiliary intake. Any piping connection or other device whereby water may be secured from a source other than that normally used,
By-pass. Any system of piping or other arrangement whereby the water may be diverted around any part or portion of a water purification plant.
Cross-connection. Any physical connection whereby the public water supply is connected with any other water supply, whether public or private, either inside or outside of any building or buildings, in such a manner that a flow of water into the public water supply is possible either through the manipulation of vales or because of ineffective check or back pressure valves, or because of any other arrangement.
Director. References to the director, as used in this division, shall mean the director of the water and wastewater department of the City of Senoia.
Inter-connection. Any system of piping or other arrangement whereby the public water supply is connected directly with a sewer, drain, conduit, pool, storage reservoir, or other device which does or may contain sewerage or other waste or liquid which would be capable of imparting contamination to the public water supply.
Person. Any person, firm, corporation, association, organization, or entity, including governmental bodies, existing under the laws of this or any other state or country.
Public water supply. The system owned, operated and maintained by the City of Senoia, consisting of supply, collection, treatment, storage and distribution facilities, furnishing piped water to the public for human consumption and general use operating under permit issued by the Georgia Department of Natural Resources Environmental Protection Division.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-64. - Statutory compliance.
The City of Senoia Public Water System operates in compliance with O.C.G.A. 12-5-170, et seq., 'The Georgia Safe Drinking Water Act", and Chapter 391-3-S-.13 of the Georgia Rules and Administrative Regulations, which pertain to safe drinking water standards. As required by its permit, it is the purpose of this division to establish an effective, ongoing program to control undesirable water uses.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-65. - Cross-connections prohibited
That it shall be unlawful for any person to cause a cross-connection, auxiliary intake, bypass, or inter-connection to be made to the City of Senoia Public Water System or allow one to exist for any purpose whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-66. - Use of private water supply or unapproved water supply.
Any person whose premises are supplied with water from the public water supply, and who also has on the same premises a separate source of water supply or stores water in a storage reservoir from which the water stored therein is circulated through a piping system, shall file with the director a sworn statement of the nonexistence of unapproved or unauthorized cross-connections, auxiliary intakes, by-passes, or inter-connections with the public water supply. Such statement shall also contain an agreement that no cross-connection will be permitted upon the premises.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-67. - Inspections of premises with secondary water supplies.
It shall be the duty of the director of the City of Senoia Public Water System to cause inspections to be made of all properties served by the public water supply where cross-connections with the public water supply are known to exist or reasonably deemed probable. The frequency of inspections and re-inspections based on potential health hazards involved shall be established by the director. The director or his authorized representative shall have the right to enter at any reasonable time, upon any property served by a connection to the City of Senoia Public Water System for the purpose of inspecting the piping system or systems thereof for cross-connections, auxiliary intakes, by-passes, or inter-connections. Advance notice to the owner or occupant of a property shall be given, if feasible, in the manner best calculated to apprise the owner/occupant of the director's intent. On request, the owner or occupant of any property so served shall furnish to the director or inspection agent any pertinent information regarding the piping system or systems on such property. The refusal of such information or refusal of access when requested, shall create rebuttable presumption of the presence of unapproved cross- connections and shall constitute grounds for discontinuing public water service.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-68. - Installation of approved protective devices.
The director, or his designated representative, shall require the installation and use of an approved protective device(s) on any public water supply service line serving premises on which a secondary water supply is used or available for use to assure that any pollutant or contaminant that may originate on the premises is contained therein. The protective device(s) shall be a reduced pressure zone type backflow preventer or a double check type backflow preventer approved by the director of water and wastewater as to manufacturer, model, and size. The method of installation of backflow protective devices shall be approved by the director prior to installation and shall comply with the criteria set forth by the director who shall oversee the installation. When the installation is performed by city forces, the owner/occupant of the premises is responsible for the expense of the installation labor and materials, and shall be billed for the actual expense. The owner, or occupant, has the option to have the installation performed by a private contractor, subject to the director's specifications.
The department shall have the right to inspect and test the device or devices on an annual basis or whenever deemed necessary by the director. Water service shall not be disrupted to test the device without the knowledge of the owner/occupant of the premises. Where the use of water is critical to the continuance of normal operations or protection of life, property, or equipment, duplicate units shall be provided to avoid the necessity of discontinuing water service to test or repair the protective device or devices. Where only one unit is installed and the continuance of service is critical, the director shall notify, in writing, the owner/occupant of the premises of plans to discontinue water service and arrange for a mutually acceptable time to test and/or repair the device. The director shall require the owner/occupant of the promises to make all repairs indicated promptly, and the expense of such repairs shall be borne by the owner/occupant of the premises. Those repairs shall be made by qualified personnel certified and licensed, acceptable to the director.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-69. - Notice to disconnect unapproved water supply.
Any person who now has cross-connections, auxiliary intakes, by-passes, or interconnections in violation of the provisions of this division shall be allowed a reasonable time within which to comply with the provisions of this division. After a thorough investigation of existing conditions and an appraisal of the time required to complete the work, the amount of time shall be designated by the director and written notice given outlining specified time limits.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-70. - Required use of additional protective device(s) in certain installations.
Where the nature of use of the water supplied to a premises by the public water supply is such that it is deemed:
a.
Impractical to provide an effective air-gap separation;
b.
The owner/occupant of the premises cannot or is not willing demonstrate to the director that the water use and protective features of the plumbing are such as to pose no threat to the safety of the public water supply;
c.
The nature and mode of operation within a premises are such that frequent alterations are made to the plumbing; or
d.
There is a substantial likelihood that existing protective measures may be subverted, altered, or disconnected;
e.
Maximum contaminate containment protection shall be provided by installation of a double detector check backflow prevention device(s), approved by the director.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-71. - Notice on secondary sources.
The drinking water supply made available to properties served by the public water supply shall be protected from possible contamination as specified herein. Any water outlet which could be used for human consumption or drinking water purposes and which is not supplied by the public water system shall be labeled in a conspicuous manner as:
WATER UNSAFE FOR DRINKING
By a sign or decal affixed at or near the water outlet. The minimum acceptable sign or decal shall have black letters one inch high located on a red background.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Sec. 70-72. - Violations; enforcement penalties.
Any person who willfully refuses to comply with the provisions of this division shall be cited to appear before the municipal court and, upon conviction, shall be fined not less than $100.00; each day of continued violation, after citation, shall constitute a separate offense. In addition to the foregoing fines, upon conviction, the director shall discontinue the public water supply service at any premises upon which there is found to be a cross-connection, auxiliary intake, by-pass, or inter-connection, and service shall not be restored until such cross-connection, auxiliary, by-pass, or inter-connection has been discontinued.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 1, 8-18-2003)
Secs. 70-73—70-75. - Reserved.
