Sec. 54-1. - Noise prohibitions.
(a)
General prohibition. It shall be unlawful for any person to make, continue, or cause to be made or continued any loud, unnecessary or unusual noise or any noise which either annoys, disturbs, injures or endangers the comfort, repose, health, peace or safety of others, within the city.
(b)
Acts enumerated. The following acts, among others, unless occurring at a duly permitted parade, event or festival, are declared to be loud, disturbing and unnecessary noises in violation of this section, but the enumeration shall not be deemed to be exclusive:
(1)
Racing motors. The unnecessary racing of the motor of any truck tractor, motor vehicle, motorcycle, farm tractor, road tractor and other motor-driven vehicle in such a manner as to emit loud noises.
(2)
Horns, signaling devices, etc. The sounding of any horn or signaling device on any automobile, motorcycle, truck tractor, or other vehicle on any street or public place of the city, except as a danger warning; the creation by means of any such signaling device of any unreasonably loud or harsh sound; the sounding of any such device for an unnecessary and unreasonable period of time; the use of any signaling device except one operated by hand or electricity; the use of any horn, whistle or other device operated by engine exhaust; and the use of such signaling device when traffic is for any reason held up.
(3)
Yelling, shouting, etc. Yelling, shouting, hooting, whistling or singing on the public streets, particularly between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. or at any time or place so as to annoy or disturb the quiet, comfort, or repose of persons in any office, or in any dwelling, hotel or other type of residence, or of any persons in the vicinity.
(4)
Animals, birds, etc. The keeping of any animal or bird which by causing frequent or long continued noise shall disturb the comfort or repose of any persons in the vicinity.
(5)
Steam whistles. The blowing of any locomotive steam whistle or steam whistle attached to any stationary boiler except to give notice of the time to begin or stop work or as a warning of fire or danger, or upon request of proper city authorities.
(6)
Exhausts. The discharge into the open air of the exhaust of any steam engine, stationary internal combustion engine, or motor vehicle (except tractors) except through a muffler or other device which will effectively prevent loud or explosive noises therefrom.
(7)
Defect in vehicle or load. The use of any automobile, motorcycle, or other vehicle so out of repair, so loaded or in such manner as to create loud and unnecessary grating, grinding, rattling or other noise.
(8)
Loading, unloading, opening boxes. The creation of a loud and excessive noise in connection with loading or unloading any vehicle or the opening and destruction of bales, boxes, crates and containers.
(9)
Schools, courts, churches, hospitals. The creation of any excessive noise on any street adjacent to any school, institution of learning, or church while the school, institution of learning, or church are in use, or adjacent to any hospital, which unreasonably interferes with the workings of such institution, or which disturbs or unduly annoys patients in the hospital, provided conspicuous signs are displayed in such streets indicating that the street is a school or hospital street.
(10)
Hawkers, peddlers. The shouting and crying of peddlers, hawkers and vendors which disturbs the peace and quiet of the neighborhood.
(11)
Drums. The use of any drum or other instrument or device for the purpose of attracting attention by creation of noise to any performance, show or sale.
(c)
Loudspeakers. It shall be unlawful for any person, either as principal, agent or employee, to play, use or operate for advertising purposes, or for any other purpose whatever, on or upon the public streets, sidewalks, or thoroughfares in the city, any device known as a sound truck, loudspeaker or sound amplifier, or radio or phonograph with a loudspeaker or amplifier, or any kind of instrument known as a calliope or any instrument of any kind or character which emits therefrom loud and raucous noises and is attached to and upon any vehicle operated or standing upon the streets or public places mentioned in this subsection.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-2. - Discharging and carrying firearms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to shoot or discharge any firearm within the city limits except in conjunction with military funerals or other events or functions as permitted by the city manager.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to shoot any airgun, air pistol, or like instrument within the city limits except as provided in this section:
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person at any time within the city limits to shoot any airgun, air pistol or like weapon except upon and within the confines of the person's own private property, and then in such a manner as not to injure or endanger another or the property of another.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 16 years to shoot any airgun, air pistol or like weapon except under the supervision of an adult.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-3. - Criminal trespass.
Any person who enters upon the premises of another after receiving notice that such entry is forbidden or who remains upon the premises of another after receiving notice to depart said premises shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-4. - Criminal damage to property.
Any person who intentionally damages any property of another without consent of said other person, or knowingly and maliciously interferes with the possession or use of the property of another person without the consent of said person, and if the value of said damage does not exceed $500.00, commits the offense of criminal damage to property. Such person shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-5. - Criminal damage to city property.
Any person who intentionally destroys, damages or defaces property of the city, to property in which the city has a leasehold interest or which it otherwise lawfully possesses or controls, and the damage does not exceed $500.00, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-6. - Loitering or prowling.
(a)
A person commits the offense of loitering or prowling when he is in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for law-abiding individuals under circumstances that warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity.
(b)
Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether alarm is warranted is the fact that the person takes flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer, refuses to identify himself, or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself or any object. Unless flight by the person or other circumstances make it impracticable, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest for an offense under this section, afford the person an opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern which would otherwise be warranted by requesting the person to identify himself and explain his presence and conduct. No person shall be convicted of an offense under this section if the law enforcement officer fails to comply with the foregoing procedure or if it appears at trial that the explanation given by the person was true and would have dispelled the alarm or immediate concern.
(c)
A person committing the offense of loitering or prowling shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
State Law reference— Authority to proscribe loitering, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-36; littering, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-48.
Sec. 54-7. - Disorderly conduct.
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to engage in any conduct described in the following subsections; provided, however, that no person shall be convicted of any of the following sections upon a showing that the predominant intent of such conduct was to exercise a constitutional right:
(1)
To act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby any person is placed in fear of the safety of his life, limb or health;
(2)
To act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being damaged or destroyed;
(3)
To cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger life, limb, health or property of another;
(4)
To be in or about any place, where illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed, or tolerated, for the purpose of or intent to engage in the purchase, use, possession or consumption of such illegal drugs, narcotics or alcoholic beverages;
(5)
To direct "fighting words" toward another, that is, words which by their very nature tend to incite a breach of the peace;
(6)
To interfere with, by acts of physical obstruction, another's pursuit of a lawful occupation;
(7)
To congregate with another or others on or about any public way so as to halt the flow or vehicular or pedestrian traffic, and to fail to clear that public way after being ordered to so do by a police officer or other lawful authority;
(8)
To disrupt, by actions which tend to incite a breach of the peace, the undisturbed activities of any house of worship, hospital or home for the elderly;
(9)
To throw bottles, paper, cans, glass, sticks, stones, missiles or any other debris on public property; or
(10)
To defecate or urinate on the streets, sidewalks or public areas of the city or in the halls, elevators, public areas of any public or commercial buildings or elsewhere on any other property open to public view.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
State Law reference— Authority to punish disorderly conduct, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-34.
Sec. 54-8. - Public intoxication.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be and appear on the streets of the city or in any public place or place of business patronized by the public in a state of intoxication or incapacitation caused by illicit drugs, alcohol, concentrated vapors, or inhalants. The condition of intoxication or incapacitation shall be manifested by boisterousness, public indecency, indecent acts, vulgar, profane, or loud language, unconsciousness, disorientation or the inability to care for his own needs or recognize obvious dangers.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
State Law reference— Authority to proscribe public drunkenness, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-41.
Sec. 54-9. - Curfew for minors.
(a)
Evening curfew. It shall be unlawful for any person 16 years of age or younger to idle, loiter or congregate on or in the public streets, sidewalks or other public places within the city between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m.
Provisions of this section shall not apply to a minor accompanied by his parent, legal guardian or other adult person having legal care and custody of the minor or where the minor is on an emergency errand or legitimate business, such as, but not limited to, returning home from work, from school sponsored activities, or travelling through the city and/or as directed by his parent, guardian or other adult person having the legal care and custody of the minor.
(b)
Daytime curfew. It shall be unlawful for any person 16 years of age or younger to idle, loiter, or congregate on or in the public streets, sidewalks or other public places within the city during "school hours," which shall, for the purposes of this section, be deemed to be those hours during which schools are in session in the Troup County School System.
Provisions of this section shall not apply to a minor on an emergency errand or legitimate business, such as, but not limited to traveling directly from home to school or returning directly from school to home, is enrolled in a legitimate home study program as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690 and has written consent from a parent or legal guardian to be outside the home, has graduated from high school, has written proof from school authorities that he/she is excused from school at that particular time or is participating in an approved school activity which requires the minor to be off school property.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-10. - Parental responsibility; violation; penalty.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the parent, guardian or other person having custody or control of any child 16 years of age or younger to permit, or by insufficient control or supervision, to allow any such child to be in or upon the public streets or any other place listed in section 54-10 within the city between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on any day and 6:00 a.m. the succeeding day except under the circumstances set forth in section 54-10. Upon conviction of violation of this section for the first time, in the discretion of the judge of the municipal court, such person may be given a warning citation. Upon further conviction, a person shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $500.00 and costs, imprisonment for not more than 60 days, work on the public streets or on the public works of the city for not more than 60 days or to any one or more of such punishments. Each violation of this section shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
As an alternative to the penalty provision provided in subsection (a) of this section, the judge of the municipal court of the city may, in his discretion, order any person in violation of the provision of such subsection to attend and participate in, at their expense, an alternative treatment or intervention program recommended and approved by the court.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-11. - Disorderly homes.
(a)
Maintaining. It shall be unlawful for any person, either personally or as agent of another, to permit noisy or riotous persons, or persons of disorderly character to assemble in or around any house located in the city owned, occupied or controlled by such person in such a manner that the occupants of the house disturb the peace and enjoyment of the surrounding neighborhood.
(b)
Assembly in disorderly manner. It shall be unlawful for persons to assemble in a disorderly manner in or around any house within the city to the disturbance of the citizens residing in the surrounding neighborhood.
(c)
Surrounding neighborhood defined. Noise emanating from disorderly persons within or around such a house sufficient to constitute a violation of the city noise ordinance as provided in section 54-1, shall be sufficient to constitute an offense under this section.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-12. - Obstruction and interference.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to intentionally interfere or hinder a city official, employee, or agent when such official, employee or agent has properly identified either himself or is otherwise identifiable as such and is engaged in the lawful performance of his official duties.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to give a false name, address or date of birth, or any other false information, to any city official, employee, or agent in the lawful discharge of his official duties with the intent to mislead such official, employee or agent in any way.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to provide identification, address or date of birth to a city police officer while said officer is conducting an investigation and the officer has reasonable belief that said individual committed a crime, is committing a crime or is about to commit a crime. However, said person shall not be compelled to answer any other inquiry.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-13. - False representations to city departments.
It shall be unlawful for any person, knowingly and willfully and with intent thereby to mislead, either in such person's own behalf or in behalf of others, as principal or as agent, to make or file orally or in writing any false representations of fact to any police officer of the city or to any department of the city government.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-14. - Reckless operation of motor vehicle on private property.
No person shall operate a motor vehicle upon any parking facility, public or private, vehicle-access or pedestrian walkway of any parking facility by sudden starting, stopping or turning so as to endanger the person or property of another.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-15. - Police officers generally.
(a)
Resisting, obstructing, etc. It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly and willfully obstruct, oppose or hinder any police officer in the lawful discharge of his official duties, including but not limited to the making or attempting to make a legal arrest of any person or the serving or attempting service of any legal process or order issued by authorities of the city, county or state.
(b)
Escape; attempted escape. It shall be unlawful for any person to escape or attempt to escape from any city police officer, or from the city jail or from any officer or other person in charge of a work gang.
(c)
Impersonating police officer. It shall be unlawful for any person to appear on the streets wearing clothes or uniforms or badges which closely resemble the uniforms or badges adopted by the police force or to do any other similar act or to in any way impersonate a police officer.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)
Sec. 54-16. - Responsibility of parents, legal guardians or persons having legal custody of minors to supervise and control.
(a)
No parent, legal guardian or person legally in loco parentis or other person having legal custody or control of or responsibility for a minor child between six and through and including 16 years of age shall at any time, through insufficient control or supervision, allow any such child to engage in any status offense or delinquent action as defined within O.C.G.A. § 15-11-2. This prohibition shall apply to such acts at those locations identified within section 54-9. Responsibility for the prevention of such status offenses and/or delinquent acts by any minor child shall be attached to the parent or other such person having legal custody or control of the child upon proof of prior notification of said child's potential to so offend. Such prior notification shall be established through confirmation by local law enforcement agency records or other evidence that the parent or other such person having legal custody or control of the child was notified, within the immediately preceding 12 months, of at least two previous warnings or juvenile petition initiations against said child for a status offense or delinquent act at any location identified within section 54-9.
(b)
Any person who shall violate the provisions of this section shall, after trial and upon conviction, be penalized as provided in section 1-7. The municipal court judge may, in his discretion, consider alternate sentencing, including parenting classes, for first offenders or in other appropriate circumstances.
(Ord. No. 18-0319 , § 1, 3-19-2018)
Sec. 54-17. - Marijuana possession.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or have under his or her control within the city one ounce or less of marijuana.
(b)
For the purposes of this section, the term "marijuana" means all parts of the plant of the genus cannabis, whether growing or not, the seeds thereof, the resin extracted from any part of such plant, and every compound, manufacture, salt, derivative, mixture, or preparation of such plant, its seeds, or resin; but shall not include samples as described in O.C.G.A. § 16-13-25(3)(P) and shall not include the completely defoliated mature stalks of such plant, fiber produced from such stalks, oil, or cake, or the completely sterilized samples of seeds of the plant which are incapable of germination.
(c)
Any person charged with a violation of this section shall be entitled, upon request, to have the case against him or her transferred to the State Court of Troup County, to be prosecuted and tried as a misdemeanor in that court.
(Ord. No. 18-0319 , § 1, 3-19-2018)
Sec. 54-18. - Shoplifting.
The municipal court of the city is authorized to prosecute and dispose of shoplifting cases in accordance with state law. The municipal court shall have jurisdiction over cases in which a person is charged with a first, second or third offense of theft by shoplifting when the property which was the subject of the theft was valued at $500.00 or less and the offense occurs within the city limits. Any person charged in municipal court under this section shall be entitled to have the case transferred to the State Court of Troup County upon request.
(Ord. No. 18-0319 , § 1, 3-19-2018)
Sec. 54-19. - Penalties for violation.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction in the municipal court of the city, be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 16-0620 , § 1, 6-20-2016)

Sec. 54-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ashes means the residue from the burning of wood, coal, coke or other combustible material.
Garbage means putrescible animal and vegetable waste resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking and consumption of food.
Refuse means all putrescible or nonputrescible waste (except body waste), including, but not limited to, garbage, rubbish, ashes, street cleanings, dead animals, abandoned automobiles and solid market and industrial waste.
Rubbish means nonputrescible waste (including ashes), consisting of both combustible and noncombustible waste, including, but not limited to, paper, cardboard, tin cans, yard clippings, wood, glass, bedding, crockery and similar materials.
Unauthorized accumulation of refuse means refuse which is stored or accumulated on any premises in the city in such manner as to create unsanitary conditions, or to create fire hazards; or to cause offensive and obnoxious odors; or to provide a breeding place for insects or a harboring place for vermin; or to invite plundering; or to endanger the safety of children as well as adults; or to reduce property values; or in any way to adversely affect the health, safety, welfare and well-being of the public.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 54-32. - Containers.
(a)
Refuse containers shall be provided by the owner, tenant, lessee or occupant of the premises and shall be maintained in good condition. Any container that does not conform to the provisions of this section or that may have ragged or sharp edges or any other defect liable to hamper or injure the person collecting the contents thereof shall be promptly replaced upon notice.
(b)
Garbage containers shall be made of metal or plastic, equipped with suitable handles and tightfitting covers, and shall be watertight. Garbage containers shall have a capacity of not more than 40 gallons. Paper or plastic bags manufactured specifically for the storage of garbage may be used provided the bags are attached to a bag holder which is equipped with a watertight lid made of durable metal or plastic. Garbage containers shall be kept clean, neat and in a sanitary condition at all times and shall be located on the premises at a place easily accessible to the collectors. Each owner, tenant, lessee or occupant of the premises shall provide an adequate number of garbage containers to hold the amount of garbage accumulated between collections.
(c)
Rubbish containers shall be of the kind suitable for collection purposes and the location and type shall be approved by the authorized representative of the city.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-2)
Sec. 54-33. - Scattering refuse.
(a)
No person shall place refuse in any street, alley or other public place, or upon any private property, whether owned by such person or not within the city except as prescribed in this article.
(b)
No person shall throw or deposit any refuse in any stream or other body of water.
(c)
No person shall cast, place, sweep or deposit anywhere within the city any refuse in such a manner that it may be carried or deposited by the elements upon any street, sidewalk, alley, sewer, parkway or other public place, or into any occupied premises within the city.
(d)
Scattering refuse as provided in this section is declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-3)
State Law reference— Littering, O.C.G.A. §§ 16-7-43, 16-7-48.
Sec. 54-34. - Unauthorized accumulations generally; abandoned motor vehicles; motor vehicles parts.
(a)
Any unauthorized accumulation of refuse on any premises is declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. Such nuisance shall be abated as prescribed in section 54-39.
(b)
The accumulation of one or more motor vehicles or any major part thereof which is in any one of the following conditions:
(1)
Resting on blocks;
(2)
Without wheels;
(3)
With major parts missing;
(4)
Without tires; or
(5)
In a state of repair which precludes further use;
is declared to be an unauthorized accumulation of refuse, except where vehicles are stored in an enclosed building or located on the premises of a licensed business enterprise operated in a lawful place and manner when necessary to the operation of such licensed business enterprise; provided, however, where any such motor vehicle or major part thereof is not located in an enclosed building, such premises shall be screened on all sides by fence and shrubbery so as to shield such accumulation from sight.
(c)
In addition to the prohibitions stated in subsections (a) and (b) of this section, no vehicles, parts of vehicles, or former vehicles (including, but not limited to, vehicle tires and truck trailers) shall be parked or stored openly. This prohibition shall not apply to vehicles which bear an up-to-date license and for which all ad valorem taxes have been paid.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-4; Ord. of 11-18-2002; Ord. No. 13-04-15C, § 1, 4-15-2013)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 13-04-15C, § 1, adopted Apr. 15, 2013, retitled the catchline of § 54-34 from "Unauthorized accumulations generally; abandoned motor vehicles" to read as herein set out.
Sec. 54-35. - Unauthorized disposal areas.
It shall be unlawful to dump, deposit or dispose of any refuse on any lot of record, as defined by section 102-2, public street, alley or roadway within the city, unless an application for the designation of a disposal area showing such use of the area has been authorized by the city. Failure to comply with this section is declared to be a nuisance. The location and boundaries of such disposal areas must be filed in the office of the city manager and must be approved by the city manager prior to use as a disposal area. The city manager shall have the authority to impose restrictions on type of disposal.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-5)
Sec. 54-36. - Burning leaves; rubbish.
Leaves, rubbish or refuse of any kind shall not be set on fire or burned on any paved street or sidewalk.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-6)
Sec. 54-37. - Unsightly or unhealthful growth prohibited.
No person shall allow or permit weeds, grass and other such vegetable matter to grow and be upon his lot of record, as defined in section 102-2, uncut so as to render the premises unsightly or unhealthful, or so as to constitute a fire hazard, a breeding place for mosquitoes, or cause other peril from the growth and accumulation of such weeds, grass or other vegetable matter. Any such growth in excess of one foot in height shall be deemed to constitute such prohibited condition.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-7)
Sec. 54-38. - Declaration of nuisance.
To permit the growth and accumulation of weeds, grass or other vegetable matter so as to create any condition mentioned in section 54-37 is declared to be a nuisance.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-8)
Sec. 54-39. - Complaint; trial; order to abate nuisance.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to create a nuisance on his/her premises or premises occupied by him/her, or to permit a nuisance to remain six hours after notice. The following, among others, are declared to be nuisances: failure to comply with the requirements of sections 54-33 through 54-38; failure to comply with section 54-43; stagnant water, decayed vegetables, decayed fruits and fruit refuse, dead animals or tainted meat, filthy privies, filthy water or excrement, broken or ruptured sewer lines, raw sewage or anything that causes offensive odors or that works hurt, damage or inconvenience to another; the conducting by pipes, sewers or otherwise into any street, public alley or on the lot of another, of any filthy water or excrement of any kind, such as comes from kitchens, bathtubs, laundry places, privies, stables and other places.
(b)
All nuisances and dangerous objects in and about the streets and other public places may be removed at once by the city, without any notice, and the expense incurred in the removal shall be paid by the owner of the dangerous objects or of the person maintaining the nuisance.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for the person creating or maintaining any nuisance within the city to fail or refuse to abate and remove same after having been ordered to do so in accordance with the provisions of the charter and ordinances of the city.
(d)
Any nuisance on private property may be removed on the written complaint of any officer or inhabitant of the city, which complaint must set forth particularly the nature and location of same and the person complained against. On such complaint being filed with the municipal court judge, he shall issue a summons to the person charged with maintaining such nuisance, requiring him to show cause before the municipal court judge at some time therein specified, within not more than 15 days, why said nuisance complained of should not be abated. Such notice shall be served personally by any law enforcement officer at least one day before the hearing. At the trial, the court shall hear evidence as to the facts and pass an order as may be just. If the thing complained of is a nuisance, the order shall specify within what time it is to be abated by the defendant. If it is not abated within the time specified in the order, the court shall issue a writ directed to the chief of police directing and requiring the abatement of the nuisance.
(e)
Whenever the person creating or maintaining any nuisance within the city shall fail to abate after notice to do so, and it shall become necessary to issue a writ, directed to the chief of police, requiring him to remove same, it shall be the duty of the chief of police to keep an account of the necessary expenses incurred and to render to the court a statement of same. The court shall issue an execution against such order for such amount, the same to be levied upon and collected in the same manner as is provided for the levy in collection of executions for taxes.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-9; Ord. No. 18-0827A , § 1, 8-27-2018)
Sec. 54-40. - Collection of abatement expense.
Whenever the person creating or maintaining any nuisance within the city shall fail to abate after notice to do so, and it shall become necessary to issue a writ, directed to the chief of police, requiring him to remove the nuisance, it shall be the duty of the chief of police to keep an account of the necessary expenses incurred and to render to the clerk a statement of those expenses. The clerk shall issue execution against the owner for that amount, which execution shall be levied upon and collected in the same manner as is provided for the levy and collection of executions for taxes.
(Code 1988, § 11-2-10)
Sec. 54-41. - Unsightly conditions in residential areas.
(a)
No person shall maintain residential property in which such person is residing in an unsightly condition. The term "unsightly condition" shall include, but is not limited to, unkept yards, porches, steps or stoops and the cluttering of yards, porches, steps or stoops with any items which would detract from or have any adverse effect on the aesthetics of the neighborhood.
(b)
Any person convicted of violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. of 9-18-95)
Sec. 54-42. - Penalties for violation.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction in the municipal court of the city, be punished as provided in section 1-7.
Sec. 54-43. - Dangerous trees; required removal; city authority.
(a)
Any dead or diseased tree or limb located on private property within the city which is likely to fall on or across a public way and thus damage persons or property shall be removed by the owner. Removal shall be accomplished by the owner within 30 days after receiving notice of the dangerous tree or limb condition as defined herein.
(b)
Any owner who willfully violates this section shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor punishable in accord with section 1-7.
(c)
Upon failure of the owner to remove the dangerous tree or limb as required herein, the city shall have the right, power and authority, but not the obligation, to remove such tree or limb which presents a clear and immediate danger of falling on or across any public way. This city may initiate action to have such tree or limb removed under article II of chapter 54 which allows for the removal of nuisances. In such event, and upon order of the municipal court requiring such removal by the city, the court shall issue an execution for the costs incurred, the same to be levied upon and collected in the same manner as provided for the levy, collection, and execution of taxes.
(Ord. No. 18-0827A , § 2, 8-27-2018)

ARTICLE III. - COMMERCIAL SOLICITATION

Sec. 54-50. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Commercial solicitation or to commercially solicit means any request made in person without a permit, asking for an immediate donation of money or other thing of value, including the purchase of an item or service for an amount far exceeding its value, under circumstances in which a reasonable person would understand that the purchase is a donation. "Commercial solicitation" shall include passively standing or sitting with a sign or other indication that one is seeking donations without addressing the request to any specific person.
Downtown development area means the area as designated by the City of Hogansville in conjunction with its activation of the Downtown Development Authority of the City of Hogansville, which area is specifically described within "Exhibit A," attached to Ordinance No. 13-04-15D, and is generally described as follows:
That area of the City of Hogansville bounded on the south by East Boyd Road and Boyd Road; bounded on the west by Pine Street, Ware Street and the railroad right of way; bounded on the north by Green Avenue and Lincoln Street; and bounded on the east by Industrial Drive.
Highway 54 commercial corridor means that area of Highway 54 from the downtown development area running east to the termination of the city limits, as well as 500 feet on both sides of Highway 54.
(Ord. No. 13-04-15D, § 1, 4-15-2013)
Sec. 54-51. - Certain commercial solicitation prohibited; area of restriction.
It shall be unlawful for any person to commercially solicit when the person solicited is in any of the following places within the City of Hogansville, unless otherwise provided for in section 54-52 below:
(1)
On private property if the owner, tenant or lawful occupant has asked the person not to solicit on the property, or has posted a sign clearly indicating that solicitations are not welcome on the property;
(2)
Within the streets, sidewalks or other public areas of the Downtown development area and Highway 54 commercial corridor as defined in section 54-50 above.
(Ord. No. 13-04-15D, § 1, 4-15-2013)
Sec. 54-52. - Exceptions.
The following persons are exempt from the provisions of this article which ban commercial solicitation:
(1)
Educational institutions and those organizations, foundations, associations, corporations, charities and agencies operated, supervised or controlled by or in connection with a nonprofit educational institution, provided that any such institutional organization is qualified under Section 501(c) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended;
(2)
Fraternal, civic, benevolent, patriotic and social organizations, when solicitation of contributions is carried on by persons without any form of compensation and which solicitation is confined to their membership;
(3)
Any charitable organization whose total gross revenue has been less than $25,000.00 for both the immediately preceding and current calendar years;
(4)
Religious organizations; and
(5)
Political parties, candidates for federal or state office, and political action committees required to file financial information with federal or state elections commissions.
(Ord. No. 13-04-15D, § 1, 4-15-2013)
Sec. 54-53. - Permit required.
(a)
No person shall engage in commercial solicitation pursuant to this article unless a permit shall have been obtained from the city manager or his designee. An application for a commercial solicitation permit shall be made to the city manager at least two weeks prior to the proposed date(s) of the commercial solicitation and shall contain all necessary information relating to the same including but in no way limited to:
(1)
The name of person sponsoring and/or conducting the commercial solicitation;
(2)
The purpose of the commercial solicitation;
(3)
The date and beginning and ending hours of the commercial solicitation;
(4)
The location(s) within the City of Hogansville of the proposed commercial solicitation;
(5)
The estimated number of participants expected to engage in the commercial solicitation; and
(6)
The name, address and telephone number of a contact person with the sponsor of the commercial solicitation who will be familiar with all aspects of same.
(b)
Written approval or denial of the application for commercial solicitation permits shall be issued by the city manager within seven days of the filing of the application. Any person denied a permit or who disagrees with the restrictions placed on the permit may appeal such decision to the next regular meeting of the mayor and council. There shall be no cost for the permit.
(c)
The city manager shall receive and investigate all applications for commercial solicitation permits and is authorized to issue such permits based on the information contained in the application, provided the commercial solicitation is otherwise lawful and when, in the discretion of the city manager, the proposed solicitation will allow for proper traffic control and pedestrian safety. The city manager shall have the right to impose reasonable restrictions on the solicitation including the date, hours, location and number of participants when, in his judgment, such reasonable restrictions are necessary or desirable for the safety, protection and welfare of the public.
(Ord. No. 13-04-15D, § 1, 4-15-2013)
Sec. 54-54. - Penalties.
Any person who shall violate the provisions of this ordinance shall upon conviction in the municipal court of the city be punished as provided in Code section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 13-04-15D, § 1, 4-15-2013)
