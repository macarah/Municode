Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - License required.
Each brewer, wholesale dealer, and retail dealer of malt beverages or wine as such terms are defined in chapters 5 and 6, title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. §§ 3-5-1 et seq. and 3-6-1 et seq.) who does business within this municipality shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk/treasurer in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(1); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-2. - Fee established.
The annual business license fee for wine retailers, malt beverage brewers, retail dealers, and wholesale dealers whose place of business is located within the municipality, and the annual license fee for other wholesale dealers doing business in the city shall be as established by the city council, from time to time. The fee shall be reduced one-half if the license is issued during the last six months of the calendar year.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(2); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-3. - Publication of notice of application for license.
No application for a license for the sale or manufacture of malt beverages or wine within this municipality shall be acted upon by the city council until after notice shall have been run in a newspaper of general circulation in the city for at least ten days prior to the regular meeting at which such application is to be presented and considered. Such notice shall contain the name of the applicant and the location of the proposed business, and shall be run at the applicant's expense.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(3); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-4. - Retail licensee qualifications.
The retail licensee must be a citizen of the United States.
(1)
The licensee shall not have been convicted within five years of the date of his application of felony, or any violation of the laws of this state, or any other state, relating to the sale of alcoholic liquor.
(2)
The licensee shall be the owner of the premises for which the license is held or the holder of a written lease thereon. No license shall be issued for any premises in which a portion thereof is used as a residence.
(3)
The licensee shall be active in, and solely responsible for, the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(4)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement of his qualifications according to this section and shall place same on file with the city clerk before any license is issued.
(5)
If the application covers a partnership, all members of the partnership must be qualified to obtain a license and must make sworn statements of these qualifications.
(6)
No person shall be granted a license to engage in the sale of malt beverages at retail unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the city council that such person, or the officers and directors of any corporation, shall be of good moral character.
(7)
Any misstatement or concealment of fact in the application shall be grounds for revocation of the license issued and shall make the applicant liable to prosecution for perjury under the law of the state.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(4); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-5. - Factors for council consideration.
The full council, in passing upon an initial application for a malt beverage and wine license at the final meeting thereon, shall be guided by the following factors as to whether to grant or deny such application:
(1)
The proximity of other establishments selling malt or alcoholic beverages to the proposed location;
(2)
The character of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location;
(3)
The proximity of churches, schools, and playgrounds to the proposed location;
(4)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking facilities or other parking available for its patrons;
(5)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom;
(6)
The feelings and attitudes of the citizens residing in the area adjacent to the proposed location;
(7)
The information set forth in the application;
(8)
Whether any malt or alcoholic beverage license previously issued for such location was ever revoked for cause by the city council;
(9)
Whether the applicant has ever sold malt or alcoholic beverages illegally in the municipality;
(10)
Fingerprint report.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(5); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-6. - Time period for applicant to act upon grant of license.
An applicant who is granted a license shall pay all necessary fees and meet all administrative requirements to activate such license within 45 days after the action taken by the council to grant the license. This time period may only be extended by the full council.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(5.5); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-7. - Change of location or ownership.
A license issued under the provisions of this section shall permit the sale or manufacture of malt or alcoholic beverages only on the premises described in the application. The license is not transferrable from one person to another or from one location to another without the express approval of the licensing body of the city.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(6); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-8. - Sanitary conditions.
All premises used for the sale or manufacture of malt beverages and wine shall be kept in a safe and sanitary condition as required by the ordinances of the city and the laws of this state.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(7); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-9. - Regulations.
(a)
No holder of a license authorizing the sale of malt beverages and wine at retail in the city nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell beer or wine to a person under the legal age for the purchase of beer or wine as established by the state;
(2)
Knowingly sell beer or wine to any person while such person is in an intoxicated state;
(3)
Sell beer or wine upon the licensed premises or permit beer or wine to be consumed thereon, on any day or at any time when such sale or consumption is prohibited by law; or
(4)
Permit on the licensed premises any disorderly conduct, breach of peace, or any lewd, immoral, or improper entertainment, conduct or practices.
(b)
In addition:
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell, or possess for the purpose of sale, any malt beverages or wine where such person does not have a license from the city to sell or possess for sale such beverages.
(2)
No licensee, agent or employee shall consume malt beverages or wine while operating an establishment.
(3)
No licensee, agent or employee shall permit on the premises any disorderly conduct, or any notice or conduct which will interfere with contiguous property owners.
(4)
No licensee, agent or employee for a premises on which malt beverages or wine may be consumed shall allow a person under the legal age for the purchase of malt beverages on the premises.
(5)
No licensee, agent or employee for a premises on which malt beverages or wine may be consumed shall allow persons to congregate outside the building on such premises.
(6)
Malt beverages and wine shall be received at or delivered to the premises of the retail licensee by no other means than by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a permit from the state to make deliveries in said state.
(7)
Transportation of malt beverages or wine by any other means shall be grounds for revocation of retail license or wholesale permit.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(8); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-10. - Hours of sale.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to sell or offer for sale at retail, in or upon any licensed premises, any malt or alcoholic beverages for transport away from such premises except between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and midnight on weekdays and Saturdays.
(b)
Any retail licensee of malt beverages to be consumed on the premises which derives less than 50 percent of its gross sales from the sale of food to be consumed on the premises, shall be closed to the general public at all times other than between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and midnight on weekdays and Saturdays and 30 minutes preceding or following such hours.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to sell or offer for sale at wholesale, in or upon any licensed premises, any malt or alcoholic beverages, except between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. on weekdays and Saturdays.
(d)
It shall be unlawful to sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail, in or upon any licensed premises, any malt beverages on Sundays.
(e)
It shall be lawful to sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail, in or upon any licensed premises, any malt beverages on election days, provided that such sales of alcoholic beverages shall not be within 250 feet of a polling place or of the outer edge of any building within which such polling place is established on primary or election days.
(f)
It shall be lawful to sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail, in or upon any licensed premises, any malt beverages on Christmas days, provided that Christmas day is not on Sunday.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(9); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-11. - Furnishing malt beverages to minors and intoxicated persons; possession by minors.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to furnish, or cause to be furnished, or to permit any person in his employ to furnish malt beverages to any person who is noticeably intoxicated.
(b)
Possession by minors.
(1)
The term "minor," as used in this section, means any person under the age of 21 years.
(2)
No person under 21 years of age shall purchase, attempt to purchase, or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverage.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for a minor to exhibit faked, forged or borrowed credentials in an effort to obtain malt or alcoholic beverages which he is not legally entitled to purchase.
(4)
It shall be illegal for a minor to loiter in an establishment selling alcoholic beverages at retail for consumption on the premises.
(5)
No person knowingly or intentionally shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of a person under 21 years of age.
(6)
No person engaged in the business regulated hereunder shall make or permit to be made any sales to minors.
(7)
Any person found guilty by the city court of violating the provisions of this section shall be punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-8; except that any person convicted of violating subsection (b)(2) of this section shall, upon the first conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished by not more than six months' imprisonment or a fine of not more than $300.00, or both and except that any person convicted of violating subsection (b)(4) of this section shall, upon the first conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature. Fines imposed therefor may be collected by execution. Each day any violation continues shall be a separate offense.
a.
Any person convicted of violating any prohibition contained in subsection (b) of this section shall, upon the second or subsequent conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature, except that any person convicted of violating subsection (b)(2) of this section shall, upon the second or subsequent conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor.
b.
In addition to any other penalty provided for in this subsection, the driver's license of any person convicted of attempting to purchase an alcoholic beverage in violation of subsection (b)(2) of this section upon the first conviction shall be suspended for six months and upon the second or subsequent conviction shall be suspended for one year.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(10); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-12. - Terms of the license.
A license shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by the city council, or any future city council, and subject to revocation with or without cause, and with or without notice or hearing. In case of revocation, the licensee shall not be entitled to a return of the unearned license fee. All such licenses shall have printed on the front these words:
"This license is a mere privilege subject to be revoked and annulled by the city, with or without cause, and with or without notice or hearing."
(Code 1998, § 32-201(11); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
Sec. 4-13. - Grounds for revocation of license.
Any violation of the provisions of this chapter shall be grounds for immediate revocation of a license by the city council.
(Code 1998, § 32-201(12); Ord. of 8-17-1998; Ord. of 11-4-1999; Ord. of 2-3-2000)
State Law reference— Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.

ARTICLE II. - ON-PREMISES CONSUMPTION
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-45. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverages means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverages or wine.
Consumption on the premises license means a license issued to a sole proprietorship, partnership or corporation allowing the licensee to engage in sales of nonpackaged alcoholic beverages to the general public for their use on the premises and within the structure occupied by the licensee. On the premises (indoors) sales of alcoholic beverages for consumption shall include also porch areas which are defined as covered (roofed) areas surrounded entirely by banisters or other barriers, although generally open to the passage of air and light.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume including, but not limited to, fortified wine, i.e. brandy.
Malt beverage (beer) means any fermented beverage made in whole or in part from malt, or any similar fermented beverage, containing less than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, port, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer.
Premises means the definite closed or petitioned locality whether a room, shop or building, wherein alcoholic beverages are sold, any and all surrounding and adjacent areas, whether enclosed or not, which are owned by or under the control of the person to whom the license is issued.
Restaurant means an establishment that is used and held out to the public as a place where meals prepared on the premises are regularly served to the public for adequate pay each day the establishment is open for business, has kitchen facilities and staff to prepare, cook, and serve food, and has at least 50 percent of its total sales comprised of the sale of food prepared on the premises and nonalcoholic beverages consumed on the premises.
Retail sale or sale means selling or offering for sale any alcoholic beverage to any member of the public.
School or college means only such state, county, city, or other school that teaches the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state to included licensed preschool and/or kindergarten facilities and does not include private schools where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subject are taught.
Wine means any vinous liquors or other fermented fruits, fortified or unfortified, containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 1; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)

modified
(a)
Required. No person shall engage in the business of providing alcoholic beverages of any kind in the city limits without first obtaining a license under this section.

Types of licenses. The only authorized licenses under this section shall be for:

Distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages pouring license; retail sale of distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages by the drink;

modified
(a)
Applicants. All applicants shall meet the following qualifications:

A licensee shall be at least 21 years of age, of good moral character, and a citizen of the United States and the state;

A licensee shall not have been convicted within the past ten years of a felony, any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, any other misdemeanor or violation of a city ordinance within the last four years, or at any time of any criminal offense relating to alcoholic beverages, taxes, or gambling;

The premises shall have a well-maintained parking lot to accommodate as many patrons as are allowed inside the premises. The lot shall at all times have adequate lighting, sufficient to light up all areas of the lot.

Prior alcohol violations. If the location, even under prior ownership, has ever received a prior citation for a violation of an alcohol ordinance, the new applicant shall be required to demonstrate how the establishment will avoid reoccurrence of the problem. It shall be grounds for the denial of a license that a new applicant has a past association with a prior license holder whose license was revoked at the same location.

modified

Written denial. The city clerk shall provide written notice to the applicant of the denial, the reasonable grounds therefor, and provide the applicant an opportunity to appeal as set forth in section 4-71.

Sec. 4-69. - Term; transfer; renewal.
modified
(a)
Term. The alcoholic beverage license issued shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated on the license and shall expire on December 31 of that year.
(b)
Transfers. No city license under this section may be transferred from one person to another or from one location to another without permission and approval of the city council upon written application made. Approvals as to any change shall be at the sole discretion of the city council. Any licensee desiring to discontinue business at one location and commence business at some other location must make a complete new application for such location.
(c)
Renewals.
(1)
A licensee that desires to continue in business during the next or subsequent calendar year must file a renewal application for such year on or before November 15 of the preceding year. Payment of license fees for renewal shall be made at the time of application; and upon approval of renewal by the city council at a regular meeting, the license may be issued.
(2)
All applications for a renewal of licenses for the ensuing year shall be treated as applications for new licenses, except that no advertising, investigations, or waiting period shall be required, except in the discretion of city council.
(3)
With the application for renewal, the licensee shall furnish to the city clerk for the months of August, September and October of the then current calendar year an affidavit showing the gross sale of food products, excluding sale of alcoholic beverages, the gross sales of alcoholic beverages and the gross sale of other items. This affidavit shall be on a form furnished by the clerk, attached to this affidavit shall be a copy of the state sales and use report for the same period. These reports shall remain confidential.
(4)
The city clerk shall provide written notice to the applicant of a denial of a renewal, the reasonable grounds therefor, and provide the applicant an opportunity to appeal as set forth in section 4-71.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 7; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)

modified
(a)
Revocation or suspension. Licenses may be revoked or suspended for the following:

Any violation of this and/or another chapter, for any violation of state or federal law;

The licensee or the licensed business ceases to meet the eligibility requirements for licensure;

(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 8; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)
Sec. 4-71. - License appeals and hearings.
modified
(a)
No license shall be denied, suspended, or revoked without the opportunity for a hearing as hereinafter provided. The chief of police or his designee may temporarily suspend any license for a period of up to 72 hours without hearing if the licensee is in violation of this article and the safety of the public necessitates the suspension.
(b)
The city clerk shall provide written notice to the applicant or licensee of his order to deny, suspend, or revoke the license. Such written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for such action and shall notify the applicant or licensee of the right to appeal under the provisions of this article. Any applicant or licensee who is aggrieved or adversely affected by a final action of the city clerk may have a review thereof by appeal to the city council. Such appeal shall be by written petition, filed in the office of the city clerk within 15 days after the final order or action of the city clerk. In order to defray administrative costs, said written petition must be accompanied by a filing fee of $200.
(c)
A hearing shall be conducted on each appeal within 30 days of the date of filing with the city clerk, unless a continuance of such date is agreed to by the appellant and the city clerk. The appellant at such hearing shall have the right to be represented by an attorney, at the expense of the appellant, and to present evidence and cross examine witnesses.
(d)
The findings of the city council shall be forwarded to the city clerk within 15 days after the conclusion of the hearing and it shall be the duty of the city clerk to notify the appellant of the action of the city council.
(e)
The findings of the city council shall not be set aside unless found to be:
(1)
Contrary to law or ordinances; or
(2)
There is no evidence in the record to support the findings of the city council.
(f)
The findings of the city council shall be final unless appealed within 30 days of the date of said findings by certiorari to the superior court of the county.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 9; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)

modified
Any person who engages in the business of selling alcoholic beverages, whether by a single act or more often, in the city, without first obtaining the city license required in division 2 of this article for the calendar year in which such business is conducted; any person engaging in such business in the city after such license has expired or has been revoked; and any person violating any other provision of this article, upon conviction for each offense, shall be punished up to the maximum allowed by the city Charter.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 10; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)
Sec. 4-102. - Minors; identification check.
modified
(a)
Minors. No licensee nor any agent or employee of the licensee shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell or furnish alcoholic beverages to any person under 21 years of age;
(2)
Permit persons under 18 years of age to serve, sell or take orders for alcoholic beverages.
(b)
Identification check. It shall be the responsibility of the licensee to examine the identification of patrons to be certain that such patrons are of legal age. Identification in this section shall mean any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person and such person's photograph; giving such person's date of birth; and including, without being limited to, a passport, military ID card, driver's license or state department of public safety ID card.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 11; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)
Sec. 4-103. - Time of sale.
modified
(a)
The hours of sale of alcoholic beverages shall be Mondays through Saturdays, 11:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight.
(b)
No licensee nor any agent or employee of the licensee shall sell alcoholic beverages upon the licensed premises on any day or at any time when such sale or consumption is prohibited.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 12; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)
Sec. 4-104. - Consumption only on the premises.
modified
(a)
Except as provided in subsections (d) and (e) below, all alcoholic beverages sold by consumption on the premises licensees shall be consumed only on the licensed premises. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove from the licensed premises any alcoholic beverages sold for consumption on the premises. Each licensee shall be responsible for ensuring that no person so removes any alcoholic beverages from the premises in any type of container.
(b)
Except for consumption on the premises licensees located within the central business district of the city (i.e., that portion of the city generally described as City of Franklin Square and surrounding areas), each consumption on the premises licensee shall post in a prominent place at each exit from the licensed premises a sign in substantially the following form:
"It is a violation of city ordinance to take any type alcoholic beverage from this outlet." Such sign shall be uniform letters not less than one inch in height.
(c)
Except as provided in subsections (d) and (e) below, it shall be unlawful for any person to drink or have in his possession an open container of any alcoholic beverage:
(1)
On any public street, sidewalk, park or other public place within the city, or upon or within any motor vehicle on the streets, sidewalks, parks and public places of the city; or
(2)
While on private property, open to public view, without the express permission of the owner, agent or person in lawful possession thereof.
(d)
However, a licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink or for the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the licensee's premises shall have the right to serve alcoholic beverages to patrons seated in an outdoor dining area as part of the operation of the premises. The licensee shall not have the right to serve alcoholic beverages to any person who is not seated at a table or in a chair provided by the licensee. In addition, alcoholic beverages shall only be transported into outdoor dining areas and/or opened in outdoor dining areas by a licensee's working employees as part of their work duties.
(e)
Outside consumption of alcoholic beverages permitted in certain areas during special events; number and size limited. Within the central business district of the city (i.e., that portion of the city generally described as City of Franklin Square and surrounding areas), the following regulations shall apply during special events approved by the mayor and council:
(1)
The mayor and council may designate the days and hours of special events within the city where the provisions of this section shall apply (i.e., Fourth of July, musical events on the square, etc.). The city shalt post signs around the square when these special event rules are in effect.
(2)
Two drink on-street limit. Any establishment licensed to dispense alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises is authorized to dispense alcoholic beverages in paper or plastic cups or containers other than a can, bottle, or glass, for removal from the premises; provided, however, that no establishment shall dispense to any person more than two such alcoholic beverages at a time for removal from the premises, and no person shall remove at one time more than two such alcoholic beverages from the licensed premises.
(3)
Size limited to 16 ounces. No container in which an alcoholic beverage is dispensed and removed from the licensed premises shall exceed 16 fluid ounces in size. No person shall hold in possession on the streets and sidewalks, in parks and squares, or in other public places within the defined area any open alcoholic beverage container which exceeds 16 fluid ounces in size.
(4)
Drinking from can, bottle, or glass prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or attempt to drink any alcoholic beverage from a can, bottle, or glass or to possess in an open can, bottle, or glass any alcoholic beverage on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, and parking lots, whether public or private.
(5)
During all designated special events all city ordinances prohibiting public drunkenness will be strictly enforced.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 13; Ord. of 5-7-2015(1), §§ 1, 2; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)
Sec. 4-105. - Additional prohibitions.
modified
No licensee nor any agent or employee of the licensee shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell alcoholic beverages to any person while such person is in a noticeably intoxicated state or to any habitual drunk and whose intemperate habits are known to such person;
(2)
Permit any disorderly conduct or breach of the peace on the licensed premises;
(3)
Sell, offer for sale, possess or permit the consumption on the licensed premises of any kind of alcoholic beverages the sale or possession of which is not authorized under the license;
(4)
Sell alcoholic beverages of any kind in any back room or side room which is not open to the general public;
(5)
Permit smoking, inhaling, exhaling, burning or carrying any lighted tobacco or other plant material product including cigarettes, cigars, and pipe tobacco or other plant material within the licensed premises;
(6)
Offer either free or at a reduced price alcoholic beverages in conjunction with the sale of any food item or sale of any other malt beverage or wine;
(7)
Sponsor, conduct, or allow contests or promotion which have as their primary purpose the increasing of the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the licensed premises;
(8)
Allow any person to bring in his own alcoholic beverage of any kind (brown bag) on the licensed premises;
(9)
Permit gambling, betting, or any games of chance on the licensed premises;
(10)
Permit live entertainment involving any person who is unclothed or in such attire, costume, or clothing as to expose to view any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola or of any portion of the male or female pubic hair, anus, cleft of buttocks, vulva or genitals within the licensed premises.
(Ord. of 7-7-2012, § 14; Ord. of 11-16-2017, § 1)

modified

Sec. 4-109. - Violations.
modified
(a)
Any licensee or any agent or employee of the licensee who violates this article shall, upon conviction for each offense, be punished by a fine and license suspension as follows:
(1)
First offense: A fine of $500;
(2)
Second offense, if occurring within 12 months of the first offense: A fine of $500 and suspension of the licensee's license for a period of 30 days from the date of the conviction;
(3)
Third offense, if occurring within 24 months of the first offense: A fine of $500 and suspension of the licensee's license for a period of 12 months from date of the conviction;
(4)
Fourth offense, if occurring within 36 months of first offense: A fine of $500 and permanent suspension of the licensee's license.
(b)
Each day a violation occurs shall be considered a separate and distinct violation.
(c)
Any person whose license is suspended pursuant to this section shall be entitled to petition the city council for a hearing pursuant to section 4-71.
