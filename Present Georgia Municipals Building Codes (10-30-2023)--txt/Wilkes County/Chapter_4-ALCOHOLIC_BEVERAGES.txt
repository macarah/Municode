ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Short title.
This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Wilkes County Alcoholic Beverage Control Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 01232012, § I, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § I, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, malt beverage, wine, or fortified wine as defined in this section, and beer.
Bona fide private club means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state which:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(2)
Has at least 75 regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment; and
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests; and
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Calendar year means the period of time from the date of issuance of a license on or after each January 1 to the date of December 31 of the year of issuance of said license.
Clerk or county administrator means the clerk or county administrator of the board of county commissioners.
Designated county official means the person designated by the board of commissioners as inspectors for the purpose of inspections as cited in the definition of the term "inspection" in this section.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume.
Election day means any general election, primary election, run-off election, special election, or referendum within the unincorporated areas of the county.
First public notice means the date of the posting of the sign required in section 4-25(a) on the premises.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Inspection means a health and building inspection will be conducted each year prior to renewal of retail license and prior to issuance of a new license.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product or any combination of such products in water containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Restaurant means any establishment which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages and which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the sale of on-site prepared meals or food and which has a seating capacity of not less than 30 persons.
School means state or county school or such other schools (public or private) as teach subjects commonly taught in common schools and colleges of this state, and inclusive of day care centers that may supervise five or more children at one time.
Special event means an event that is to be held in a place other than the normal establishment licensed for the sale of beer and wine in the unincorporated area of the county. Such event shall be organized with provisions for security (including traffic control), fire protection, medical services, and assurances of compliance with all federal, state and local laws, rules and regulations. Said event shall also include provisions for any un-named items that may be required by the board of commissioners considered necessary for the safety and protection of persons and property.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combination of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § II, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § II, 6-12-2014)

ARTICLE II. - LICENSING

Sec. 4-23. - License required.
No person, firm or corporation shall offer for sale or sell any alcoholic beverage within the unincorporated areas of the county without first obtaining the appropriate license herein required.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § III, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § III, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-24. - Application.
(a)
Each applicant shall apply to the clerk or county administrator for a license by written application on forms approved by the board. All questions and information requested on the application form shall be filled in and subscribed to by all applicants.
(b)
All applications must be filed at least 30 days prior to the date considered by the board. This may be waived in the case of a special event.
(c)
Applications will be considered and licenses granted only at a regular meeting of the board. This may be waived in the case of a special event.
(d)
No license shall be granted to any person who is not a citizen of the state or to any person who is less than 21 years of age.
(e)
If the owner of the business is an individual, the application shall be made by such individual unless he is not a citizen of the state, in such event, the application shall be made by a person who is a citizen of the state and who is actively engaged in the management of the business for which such license is sought. This may be waived in the case of a special event.
(f)
If the ownership of the business for which a license is sought is a partnership or corporation, the application shall be made by one of the partners or an officer of the corporation who is actively engaged in the management of the business. In the event that no partner or officer is a citizen of the state, then the application shall be made by a designated agent of the business who is a resident of the state and who is actively engaged in management of the business.
(g)
In the event that a firm, partnership or corporation owns or operates more than one place of business in the county, all applications for licenses for each place of business made pursuant to this article shall be made independently. In the case of a special event, a permit may be issued for each location where beer and wine will be sold during the time of the event, through a locally approved license holder.
(h)
If the application is being made on behalf of a private club, the application shall be made by an officer of such private club.
(i)
No person shall be granted a license unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the board that such person is of good moral character and the business or private club which he represents enjoys a good reputation; and in no event shall any such license be granted to any person who has been convicted or plead guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any felony, crime of moral turpitude, or misdemeanor of a high and aggravated degree within a period of five years immediately prior to the filing of the application.
(j)
If an application is refused or withdrawn after being placed on the board's agenda for consideration, no application for the same location will be accepted for one year from the date of the filing of the application when reapplied for by the same person or business.
(k)
The building in which the sale of beer and wine will occur, must be inspected prior to the issuance of a new license. This may be waived in the case of a special event. Said inspection will be to assure that the building and contents are considered safe and no health hazards are evident. Applicant must obtain a statement from the designated county official attesting to the health and safety of the building and contents.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § IV, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § IV, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-25. - Advertisement.
(a)
Except in the case of a special event for which the board of commissioners has allowed a waiver, all persons desiring to engage in the sale of malt beverages or wine shall give notice of their intention to make such application by advertisement in the form prescribed by the board.
(b)
Advertising by the applicant for original license shall mean that there shall be a sign posted in a prominent position on the building, if there be one (it may be on or in the front window where it can be read from the road), at least 30 days prior to consideration of the application by the board. In the event there is no building on the premises, the notice shall be posted on the lot where said building is to be erected.
(c)
The wording and specifications for the sign which must be posted when applying for a new alcoholic beverage license may be updated from time to time by the county commission and kept with the county clerk.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § V, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § V, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-26. - Application fee.
Every application, excluding renewals, shall be accompanied by a non-refundable application fee in the amount to be established by the board, from time to time. (Except in the case of a special event, see section 4-29.) Said fee shall be paid to the board and shall be credited toward the license fee if a license is granted.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § VI, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § VI, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-27. - Investigation; recommendations.
The applicant shall be directed to the sheriff's department, who shall have the applicant investigated. All fees required to conduct said investigation shall be paid by the applicant to the sheriff's department at the time of request. The sheriff's department shall cause the character and reputation of the applicant and suitability of the location (inclusive of any required measurement of distances) of the establishment for which the license is sought to be fully investigated. The applicant shall be finger-printed and photographed for record and investigated for past criminal history. The applicant shall sign a release form to allow information gathered by the sheriff's department to be summarized and reported to the board. Only after all of the foregoing requirements have been fulfilled will the application for a license be considered by the board.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § VII, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § VII, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-28. - Hearing; consideration; denial of license.
(a)
All applications presented shall be noted as to the date of filing and, after filing, such application shall be presented and considered by the board in the order of their filing at the next regularly scheduled meeting of the board, provided 30 days have elapsed after the date of filing. This may be waived in the case of a special event.
(b)
In determining whether or not any license applied for hereunder shall be granted, in addition to all the other provisions of this article, in the public interest and welfare, the following will be considered:
(1)
The applicant's reputation, character, mental, and physical capacity to conduct this business.
(2)
Whether the person has previously had an alcoholic beverage license suspended or revoked.
(c)
No license shall be granted to any existing building or establishment having a history of or reputation for prostitution or other sex offenses; fighting; shooting; stabbing or other violence; gambling; illegal dealing in alcoholic beverages or drugs; or other violations of the law. The term "history or reputation" means within the one year next proceeding the filing of said application and provided said business is now owned, operated, managed, or in any way connected to the person operating said business during the times these offenses occurred.
(d)
No license shall be granted for the sale of malt beverages or wine from a building occupied as a dwelling or any building attached to a residential dwelling or within 50 feet of any residential dwelling.
(e)
No license shall be granted for the sale of malt beverages or wine from a building inspected by a designated county official and sited for any health violations or safety violations as to the building or contents.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § VIII, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § VIII, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-29. - Annual license fees.
(a)
The following annual license fees shall apply for the sale of alcoholic beverages. Such license fees shall be established by the board, from time to time:
(1)
Retail: beer, wine, and/or distilled spirits.
(2)
Wholesale: beer and wine.
(3)
Alcohol beverage caterer's permit.
(4)
Special event.
(b)
When a license has been approved and granted, the fee for same shall be paid to the board in the full amount of the license fee (less any credit due).
(c)
The annual license fee shall cover the period from January 1, or date of issuance if after January 1, through December 31 of each year. (Except in the case of a special event). There will be no pro-rating of any license fee.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § IX, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § IX, 6-12-2014; Ord. No. 09092021 , 9-9-2021)
Sec. 4-30. - License period; lapse for nonuse.
(a)
All licenses shall be issued for a calendar year. In case of the revocation or surrender of a license before the expiration of such calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive a refund whatsoever.
(b)
All licenses must be obtained no later than 90 days from the date of approval of the application, and, if not so obtained, said license shall be void.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § X, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § X, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-31. - Renewal; denial.
(a)
All licenses granted hereunder shall expire on December 31 of each year and shall be renewable at the option of the board. Licensees who desire to renew their licenses shall pay the required fee for such renewal on or before December 31 of each year. Failure to secure the renewal by December 31 shall result in a loss of license and the owner will be required to file for a new license at the new license fee.
(b)
The board may grant or deny, for cause, the renewal of any license.
(c)
Each November, a designated county official shall visit each licensed site to make an inspection to ensure no health or safety problems exist and issue a statement to the board recommending approval or denial of renewal. If denial is recommended, the county official shall state the reason and immediately notify the license holder. The official will return for a follow-up inspection two weeks after the notice is written. The applicant will be assessed a fee for any follow-up inspection to be determined by the board, from time to time. Failure to correct the sited violations within the two-week period will be just cause to deny renewal of the license until all violations are corrected.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XI, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XI, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-32. - Sale of business.
(a)
In the event that an individual, corporation, firm, or partnership sells the business at which a license issued hereunder authorizes the sale of malt beverages and wine, such license shall automatically terminate; provided, however, if an application is filed by the new owner within 15 days from the date of purchase pursuant to the provisions of this article, the clerk or county administrator or is hereby authorized to issue a temporary permit allowing such business to continue the sale of malt beverages and wines until such new application can be processed in accordance with the terms of this article.
(b)
Said temporary permit allowing such business to continue may be issued upon receipt of a non-refundable fee paid to the county by the applicant and to be determined by the board, from time to time.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XIII, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XIII, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-33. - Transfers.
(a)
A license may be transferred between immediate family members, when limited to the members having interest in the same business (spouse to spouse; spouse to son or daughter and vice-versa; brother or sister to brother or sister).
(b)
In the event that the holder of license on behalf of a corporation or private club ceases to be an officer of such corporation or is no longer associated with such private club, such corporation or private club shall apply within 15 days after such event has occurred for a new license to be issued to the person holding the same office as the previous licensee. In such event, a fee to be determined by the board, from time to time, shall be paid to the board.
(c)
No license shall be transferred from one location to another unless the new location meets all of the standards required by this article and such transfer is approved by the board.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XIV, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XIV, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-34. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
Any false or misleading information contained or material omissions in the application for a license shall be cause for the denial thereof, and if any license has been granted under such circumstances, same shall be subject to revocation.
(b)
Should any licensee be convicted, plead guilty or plead nolo contendere to any felony, crime of moral turpitude, or misdemeanor of a high and aggravated degree, such license shall be and stand immediately and automatically revoked and canceled.
(c)
The board shall have full power and authority to revoke, refuse to renew, suspend or place on probation any license to sell malt beverages and wine for violation of this article or any other county ordinance or violation of any state or federal laws.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XV, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XV, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-35. - Display or posting on premises.
The county license shall at all times be posted in a conspicuous place within the building, and if said license is not so posted, said business shall not be authorized to sell alcoholic beverages until same shall be posted.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XVI, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XVI, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-36. - Hours and days of sale and operation.
(a)
No license holder shall engage in the sale of malt beverages or wine except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m. on weekdays (Monday through Friday) and 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on Saturdays; and 12:30 p.m. and 12:00 midnight on Sundays; and shall not permit the sale of malt beverages or wine on Christmas Day or any other day on which the state law prohibits the sale of malt beverages and wine.
(b)
All establishments must close by no later than 2:00 a.m., Tuesday through Saturday, and shall be completely vacated of patrons and shall not reopen prior to 6:00 a.m. In the instance of Saturday night, all establishments must close no later than 1:00 a.m. on Sunday morning and shall be completely vacated of patrons and shall not reopen prior to 6:00 a.m. on the following Monday morning.
(c)
Grocery or convenience stores where the principal business is the sale of groceries and other like items, and restaurants where the principal business is the sale of prepared food, and the sale of malt beverages and wine is incidental thereto, may remain open during other hours but shall not sell or allow to be consumed any malt beverages or wine except during the times and hours specified in subsection (a) of this section.
(d)
The business hours of wholesale dealers shall be the same hours and days as retail dealers are allowed to engage in the sale of malt beverages and wine by the terms of subsection (a) of this section.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XVII, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XVII, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-37. - Location.
No license shall be issued hereunder where the place of business is located within 200 yards of any school or church. All distances herein referred to shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the front door of the structure from which alcoholic beverages or wine is sold or offered for sale, in a straight line to the nearest property line of the church or school.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XVIII, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XVIII, 6-12-2014; Ord. No. 09092021 , 9-9-2021)
Sec. 4-38. - Sale to minors, incompetents, and inebriates.
(a)
No malt beverage or wine shall be sold to a minor under any conditions whatsoever. A minor, for the purpose of this article, shall be considered to be a person under the legal age as provided by state law.
(b)
No malt beverage or wine shall be sold to any intoxicated person, nor shall any intoxicated person be allowed to remain upon the premises of any place of business selling malt beverages or wine.
(c)
No employees of any business selling malt beverages or wine shall work in said place of business while intoxicated and no employees shall drink malt beverages or wine while working and employed in such business.
(d)
No malt beverages or wine shall be sold to any person who is mentally incompetent.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XIX, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XIX, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-39. - Compliance with health rules.
All retail licenses under this article are hereby required to comply with all rules and regulations and requirements of the county board of health, state board of health and any and all other state, federal and local public health authorities.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XX, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XX, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-40. - Penalty for violation.
(a)
Violation of any provisions of this article for failure to comply with any of its requirements shall constitute a misdemeanor. Any person who violates this article or fails to comply with any of its requirements shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished according to section 1-9, general penalty. Each day such violation continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(b)
In addition thereto and totally and separately distinct there from, any violation of any of the provisions of this article or failure to comply with any of its requirements shall be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of any and all licenses, regardless of the number of locations involved, and regardless of whether or not the holder of the license knew or contributed to such violation. It is the intent of this subsection without limiting the generality of the foregoing to grant the board the right to suspend or revoke any or all licenses held by a licensee when any violation of this article occurs at any place of business where alcoholic beverages or wine are sold pursuant to a license issued hereunder.
(c)
Any violation of the general statute law of the state pertaining to alcoholic beverages or wine shall constitute a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XXI, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XXI, 6-12-2014)
Sec. 4-41. - Due process.
(a)
Under this article, the board has the discretionary power within the guidelines of due process as hereinafter defined to grant, refuse, and to suspend or revoke any license in accordance with the standards set forth in this article.
(b)
All decisions by the board denying, suspending, or revoking such licenses shall be in writing with the reasons there for stated and mailed or delivered to the applicant personally within ten days from the date of the decision by the board.
(c)
Upon application within ten days of the receipt of the notice of denial, suspension, or revocation, the applicant shall be afforded a hearing with an opportunity to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XXII, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XXII, 6-12-2014)
Secs. 4-42—4-61. - Reserved.

Sec. 4-62. - Imposed on malt beverages and wines; audit, report and remittance of sales.
There is an excise tax hereby imposed on malt beverages and wines in the maximum amount allowed by law, as now exists or hereafter amended.
(1)
Said tax shall be paid to the board by each wholesale dealer on all malt beverages and wine sold within the unincorporated areas of the county not later than the tenth of each month, based upon the units of beer and wine sold during the previous calendar month of said wholesalers to retailers in said county.
(2)
The wholesale dealer shall keep true and correct records of all sales and shipments and shall render a sworn statement of the same accompanying the monthly report and payment to the board and said report shall show the exact quantities of malt beverages or wine, by size and type of container and the amount of excise tax collected.
(3)
The board shall have the right to audit, and to require production of records from each retailer so supplied.
(4)
Failure to make a timely report and remittance of aforesaid taxes shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to ten percent of the total amount due, in addition to the amount due; and additionally, if said report is not filed or if said taxes are not remitted and paid within 30 days from the date that the same are due, the board shall have the right to suspend and/or revoke any wholesale license, and further to prohibit said wholesaler from making any deliveries of any type whatsoever within the unincorporated areas of the county and to report such action to the state.
(5)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell retail or otherwise within the unincorporated areas of the county any malt beverage or wine on which the tax required in this article has not been paid to the wholesaler or distributor or the board of the county.
(6)
All holders of alcohol beverage caterer's licenses shall be liable for collection and timely payment of all excise taxes due on sales at special event. Additionally, special event organizers should ensure that said tax is paid on all alcoholic beverages in such a way that the county receives benefit from taxes paid. In addition, special event organizers should ensure that all applicable sales tax is collected and remitted in such a way that the county benefits from taxes paid.
(Ord. No. 01232012, § XII, 1-23-2012; Ord. No. 06122014, § XII, 6-12-2014)
