Chapter 28 - TAXES

Sec. 28-1. - Freeport Tax.
The Board amended the Freeport Tax from 20 percent to 100 percent as an incentive for new industry to come into the County. An application for exemption of the Freeport Tax has to be applied for in the Tax Appraiser's Office.
(Code 2003, ch. 24, art. I; Mot. of 8-4-1992)
Sec. 28-2. - Board of Tax Assessors.
[(a)
Membership.] Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-290 there is an established Montgomery County Board of Tax Assessors. The Board of Tax Assessors shall consist of not less than three nor more than five members to be appointed by the Montgomery County Board of Commissioners. The appointment up to a five member board is authorized but not mandated.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-292 the Montgomery County Chief Appraiser is eligible to serve on the Board of Tax Assessors if authorized in a meeting of the Board of Commissioners.
[(b)
Compensation.] Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-294 each member of the Board of Tax Assessors shall be paid as compensation for his services an amount to be determined from time to time by the Board of Commissioners. The compensation to be paid to a member of the Board of Tax Assessors shall not be less than $20.00 per day for the time he is actually discharging the duties required of him. Attendance at required approved appraisal courses shall be part of the official duties of a member of the Board of Tax Assessors and he shall be paid for each day in attendance at such courses and shall be allowed reasonable expenses necessarily incurred in connection with the courses. The compensation of the members of the Board of Tax Assessors and other expenses as may necessarily be incurred in the performance of the duties of the Board shall be paid from the county treasury in the same manner as other payments by the County are made.
[(c)
Term.] Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-295 appointment of members to the Board of Tax Assessors will be for a term of six years and until a successor is duly appointed and qualified.
[(d)
Certification of appointment.] Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-290(c) the order making an appointment to the Board of Tax Assessors shall be regularly entered upon the record of the Superior Court of Montgomery County. A certificate from the Clerk of the Superior Court reciting the order and stating that the person appointed has taken the oath required by law shall constitute the commission of a member. No other commission shall be required. The Clerk of the Superior Court shall transmit a copy of the certificate to the State Revenue Commissioner within five days of the date the oath is administered.
(Res. No. 16-08-04, 8-8-2016)
Editor's note— Res. No. 16-08-04, adopted Aug. 8, 2016, repealed the former § 28-2 and enacted a new § 28-2 as set out herein. The former § 28-2 pertained to Tax Assessor's term of office and derived from the Code of 2003, ch. 24, art. I; and a resolution adopted Aug. 5, 1996.
Sec. 28-3. - Taxpayer's Bill of Rights.
The Board of Commissioners adopts and incorporates by reference the procedures required by the most recently published version of the Taxpayers' Bill of Rights as promulgated by the State Department of Revenue.
(Code 2003, ch. 24, art. I; Mot. of 7-2-1999)
