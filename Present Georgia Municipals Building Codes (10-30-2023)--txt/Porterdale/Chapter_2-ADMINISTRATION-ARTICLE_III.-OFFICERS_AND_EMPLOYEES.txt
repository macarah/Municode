DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-122. - Code of ethics.
(a)
Prohibited conduct. Public officials and employees of the city shall treat all citizens with courtesy, impartiality, fairness, and equality under the law, and shall avoid both actual potential conflicts between their private self-interest and the public interest. Prohibited conduct of each such official and employee shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
(1)
Granting or making available to any person any special consideration, treatment, advantage, or favor beyond that which it is the general practice to grant or make available to the public large;
(2)
Requesting, using, or permitting the use of any publicly owned or publicly supported property, vehicle, equipment, labor, or service for the personal convenience or the private advantage of himself or any other person, except as otherwise allowed by law;
(3)
Participating in the deliberation of or voting on any matter involving his financial or personal interest;
(4)
Engaging in private employment with, or rendering services for, any private person who has business transactions with the city, unless he has made full public disclosure of the nature and extent of such employment or services;
(5)
Appearing on behalf of any private persons, other that himself, before any public body in the city;
(6)
Accepting any gift, whether in the form of money, thing, favor, loan, or promise, that would not be offered or given to him if he were not an official or employee;
(7)
Disclosing any confidential information concerning any official or employee, or any other person, or any property or governmental affairs of the city, without prior formal authorization of the governing body;
(8)
Using or permitting the use of confidential information to advance the financial or personal interest of himself or any other person; or
(9)
Appointing or voting for the appointment of any person related to him by blood or marriage to fill an office, position, employment, or duty, when the salary, wages, pay, or compensation is to be paid out of public funds.
(b)
Hearings and determinations. Upon the sworn complaint of any person alleging facts which if true would constitute a violation of this section, the city council shall conduct a public hearing at which the accused shall be given an opportunity to be heard, either personally or through city council. At the conclusion of said hearing, the city council shall, in written findings of fact and conclusions based thereon, make a determination concerning the propriety of the conduct of the official or employee in question.
(Code 2003, § 3-102)
State Law reference— Code of ethics, O.C.G.A. § 45-10-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-123. - Bonds.
Except as otherwise provided by law, the mayor and city council may require any department head, city official, or employee, before entering upon the discharge of his duties, to give good and sufficient bond in any amount decided by the mayor and city council. Said bond shall be payable to the City of Porterdale for the faithful performance of said duties and to secure against corruption, malfeasance, misappropriation, or unlawful expenditures in office. Said surety bonds shall be obtained from a surety company licensed to do business in the State of Georgia and approved by the mayor and city council. The premiums thereon shall be paid by the city.
(Code 2003, § 3-105)
Sec. 2-124. - Compensation.
Each officer and employee of the city shall receive such compensation as is provided from time to time by resolution.
(Code 2003, § 3-106(1); Ord. of 6-2-2014)
Sec. 2-125. - Procedure for removal from office.
(a)
Resolution for investigative hearing. Upon receipt and review of complaints and/or allegations of misconduct or neglect of duty, the city council shall adopt a resolution for investigative hearing by majority vote.
(b)
Service of charges. A hand-delivered document shall notify the defendant of the charges filed, date of hearing, list of witnesses and rights of the defendant to present his case. A return of service shall be filed with the city attorney stating that this document was delivered. This document shall be delivered no sooner than ten days after the resolution has been signed by city council.
(c)
Service of subpoenas/calling of witnesses. The city council and city attorney will determine witnesses to be called and direct subpoenas to be served. A return of service will be filed with the city attorney stating that subpoenas have been served. Any person who willfully refuses to answer a subpoena by the city council is subject to fines and/or penalties in a court of law.
(d)
Seating of an impartial panel.
(1)
An impartial panel consisting of three members will constitute the judging body of the hearing. These members will hear testimony and be presented with evidence supporting the charges and shall determine the guilt or innocence of the defendant based on the hearing. A vote will be taken on each charge presented and guilt or innocence will be determined by majority vote.
(2)
This panel shall be seated from populations residing outside the city limits of Porterdale. No elected official, appointed officer or employee of the city or any agency or entity to which the city Charter applies shall be seated on this panel.
(3)
Each member of the city council shall provide four names to be considered for the panel. After notification of persons whose names have been submitted, a panel of three shall be appointed by the city council.
(e)
Conduct of hearing.
(1)
The city council shall schedule the investigative hearing. If the hearing cannot be concluded on the date set, the council will continue the hearing to the following week or to another date as it may determine to be appropriate. The hearing will be conducted by the city attorney who shall present the charges, call witnesses and present evidence on the behalf of the city. The defendant or his counsel will be allowed to cross examine any witness who may testify. The defendant will be allowed to testify on his own behalf and may offer evidence at the hearing.
(2)
After each charge is concluded, a vote will be taken by the panel as to the guilt or innocence of the defendant as applies to that specific charge. The verdict on each charge is determined by majority vote.
(f)
Verdicts and subsequent actions.
(1)
A verdict of guilty on any charge shall result in immediate removal from office as outlined in O.C.G.A. title 45 and section 5.16 of the city Charter as follows:
a.
Incompetence, misfeasance, or malfeasance in office.
b.
Conviction of a crime involving moral turpitude.
c.
Failure at any time to possess any of the qualifications of office as provided by Charter or law.
d.
Knowingly violating any express prohibition of the Charter.
e.
Abandonment of office or neglect to perform the duties thereof.
f.
Failure for any other cause to perform the duties of office as required by the Charter or by state law.
(2)
The right of appeal to superior court shall not delay removal from office.
(Ord. of 1-6-2003)

DIVISION 2. - CITY MANAGER

Sec. 2-149. - Appointment and qualifications.
The city manager shall be appointed and serve at the pleasure of the city council who shall fix his compensation. Such person shall be selected solely on the basis of his executive and administrative qualifications.
(Code 2003, § 3-501)
Sec. 2-150. - Bond and oath.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city manager shall take an oath or affirmation for the faithful performance of his duties and shall execute a bond payable to the city in the sum as determined by the council for the faithful accounting of all funds belonging to the city that come into his hands. The premium on such bond shall be paid by the city.
(Code 2003, § 3-502)
Sec. 2-151. - Powers and duties.
The city manager shall be the chief administrative officer of the city. He shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
To keep current accounts showing at all times the fiscal condition of the city, including the current and anticipated expenses, appropriations, cash on hand, and anticipated revenue of all municipal funds and accounts;
(2)
To keep the members of the city council fully advised as to the financial condition and needs of the city;
(3)
To make reports as the city council may require concerning the operations of city departments, offices, and agencies subject to his direction and supervision;
(4)
To fix all salaries and compensation of the city employees lawfully employed by him, subject to the approval of the city council;
(5)
To examine all proposed contracts to which the city may be a party, and sign on behalf of the city any contract authorized by the city council, except where the city council directs that some other officer shall do so;
(6)
To supervise the performance of all contracts made for work done for the city;
(7)
To make all purchases of supplies, materials, and equipment for the city and see that the same are received as contracted for, in accordance with the purchasing policy;
(8)
To conduct all sales which the city council may authorize of personal property of the city which has become unnecessary or unfit for the city to use;
(9)
To keep current inventory showing all real and personal property of the city and its location;
(10)
To see to the publication of all notices, ordinances, or other documents required by law to be published;
(11)
To prepare for the election officials all notices, ballots, and election supplies necessary in connection with municipal elections;
(12)
To prepare and have custody of all municipal records required by law to be kept by the city and not by a specific officer.
(Code 2003, § 3-503)

DIVISION 3. - CITY CLERK/TREASURER

Sec. 2-171. - Election.
The city council shall at its first regular meeting after election and qualification elect/appoint a city clerk/treasurer.
(Code 2003, § 3-401)
Sec. 2-172. - Term of office.
The city clerk/treasurer shall serve at the pleasures of the council, subject to removal for cause, and until his successor is elected and qualified.
(Code 2003, § 3-402)
Sec. 2-173. - Bond.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city clerk/treasurer shall give a good and sufficient bond, payable to the city council, such bond to be fixed and approved by the city council.
(Code 2003, § 3-403)
Sec. 2-174. - Duties of city clerk/treasurer as clerk.
The city clerk/treasurer shall have the following duties in his capacity as city clerk:
(1)
To attend all meetings of the city council;
(2)
To keep correct and full minutes of the proceedings of city council, together with all ordinances and resolutions passed by it, in a properly indexed book or register kept for that purpose;
(3)
To receive all applications or petitions made to the city and to place them before the mayor and city council at the meeting of the city council next succeeding the receipt thereof;
(4)
To issue all licenses, and keep a record thereof, and all badges and permits authorized by the city council;
(5)
To keep an execution docket, in which he shall enter the names of all persons tried and fined by the municipal court, the nature of the offense, date of trial, amount of fine, and return of the police officer thereon;
(6)
To issue all summonses, processes, and subpoenas to witnesses that may be necessary in the enforcement of this Code or other rules, regulations, and ordinances of the city council;
(7)
To be the custodian of the city seal and affix its impression on documents whenever required;
(8)
To carefully preserve the records and documents belonging to the city which are not assigned to the custody of some other office, and to maintain a proper index to all such records and documents so that ready access thereto and use thereof may be had; and
(9)
On a day-to-day basis, report to and be subject to the direction, supervision and oversight of the city manager; provided, however, that, in the event of any conflict of interest, concern for fiduciary responsibility, or other matter in which such direction, supervision, and oversight may be contrary to the public interest, the city clerk/treasurer is obligated to bring such information to the attention of the city council.
(Code 2003, § 3-404; Ord. of 4-10-2012; Ord. of 4-26-2012; Ord. of 5-7-2012(1))
Sec. 2-175. - Duties of city clerk/treasurer as treasurer.
The city clerk/treasurer shall have the following duties in his capacity as city treasurer:
(1)
To receive all money due the city council, including taxes, licenses, fees, and other monies belonging to the city and pay out the same only upon orders passed by the city council and signed by the mayor, or, in his absence, the mayor pro tempore;
(2)
To keep a book of accounts showing all money received on behalf of the city and the source and disposition thereof, which book shall be open for inspection by the public and members of the city council;
(3)
To maintain a uniform system of accounts and keep such other records and accounts as may be required by statute or ordinance;
(4)
To furnish the city council with quarterly statements detailing all receipts and payments of funds for the quarter;
(5)
To enforce all laws of Georgia relating to the collection of delinquent taxes and sale or foreclosure for nonpayment of taxes to the city; and
(6)
On a day-to-day basis, report to and be subject to the direction, supervision and oversight of the city manager; provided, however, that, in the event of any conflict of interest, concern for fiduciary responsibility, or other matter in which such direction, supervision, and oversight may be contrary to the public interest, the city treasurer is obligated to bring such information to the attention of the city council.
(Code 2003, § 3-405; Ord. of 4-10-2012; Ord. of 4-26-2012; Ord. of 5-7-2012(1))
Sec. 2-176. - Compensation.
The city council shall provide for the compensation of the city clerk/treasurer.
(Code 2003, § 3-406)

DIVISION 4. - CITY ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-206. - Appointment and qualification.
The city attorney serves until a successor is appointed and has qualified.
(Code 2003, § 3-601(1))
Sec. 2-207. - Oath.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city attorney shall take the oath prescribed by this Code for city officers.
(Code 2003, § 3-601(2))
Sec. 2-208. - Compensation.
The city attorney shall submit to the city council a monthly bill for his services, itemizing the type of work performed for the city and the number of hours engaged in each type of work during the month.
(Code 2003, § 3-601(4))
Sec. 2-209. - Powers and duties.
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the city and in such capacity shall:
(1)
Prepare or revise ordinances when so requested by the city council or any committee thereof, and keep the Code of Ordinances of the city up to date and properly indexed.
(2)
Give his opinion upon any legal matter or question submitted to him by the city council, or any of its committees, or by any city officer.
(3)
Attend all meetings of the city council as directed for the purpose of giving the city council any legal advice requested by its members.
(4)
Prepare for execution all contracts and instruments to which the city is a party and approve, as to form, all bonds required to be submitted to the city.
(5)
Prepare, when authorized by the city council, all charges and complaints against, and appear in the municipal court in the prosecution of, every person charged with a violation of this Code or city Charter, and see to the full enforcement of all judgments or decrees rendered or entered in favor of the city.
(6)
Make immediate reports to the mayor and city council of the outcome of any litigation in which the city has an interest.
(7)
Make an annual report to the mayor and city council of all pending litigation in which the city has an interest and the status thereof.
(8)
Have the power to adjust, settle, compromise or submit to arbitration, any action, causes of action, accounts, debts, claims, demands, disputes, and matters in favor of or against the city or in which the city is concerned as debtor or creditor, now existing or which may hereafter arise not involving or requiring payment to exceed an amount determined by council.
(9)
Keep complete and accurate records of the following, which records shall forever remain the property of the city:
a.
All suits in which the city had or has an interest, giving the names of the parties, the nature of the action, the disposition of the case or its status, if pending, and the briefs of counsel; and
b.
All written opinions prepared by the city attorney and all certificates or abstracts of titles furnished by him to the city, or any department or official thereof.
(10)
Render such other legal services as may be required by the mayor or city council.
(Code 2003, § 3-601(3))
