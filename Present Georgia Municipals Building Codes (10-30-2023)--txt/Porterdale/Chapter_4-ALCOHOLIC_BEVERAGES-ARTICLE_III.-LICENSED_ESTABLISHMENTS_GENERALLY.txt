ARTICLE III. - LICENSED ESTABLISHMENTS GENERALLY

Sec. 4-53. - Compliance with chapter.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages within the city without having first complied with the provisions of this chapter.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(2))
Sec. 4-54. - Police supervision.
Anyone holding a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall, at all times, permit the police department to have complete supervision over the conduct of those people patronizing the establishment, and any dealer or licensee refusing to permit the police department to have full supervision, or interfering with such activities or hindering the proper policing of such establishments, will be subject to having the license suspended or revoked in the manner provided herein.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(22))
Sec. 4-55. - Employment of certain persons prohibited.
(a)
It is unlawful to knowingly employ in any premises used for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink hereunder, any person in any capacity whatsoever, including performers, entertainers and musicians, who have been convicted in this or in any other state or by the United States or any other country of soliciting for prostitution, pandering, letting premises for prostitution, keeping a disorderly place, illegally dealing in narcotics, sex offenses, or any charge relating to the manufacture or sale of alcoholic beverages. A conviction occurring more than ten years prior to the period of employment shall not be considered a disqualification. The term "conviction" includes an adjudication of guilt, a plea of nolo contendere or a forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime specified in this subsection.
(b)
Where a violation is for a misdemeanor, forfeiture of a bond, or violation of a municipal ordinance, the city council, one year after the date of disposition of the violation by the court, may waive same as a disqualification of employment.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(5); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-56. - Drinking of alcoholic beverages by employees at outlets.
It shall be unlawful for the licensee or any employee of a licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages to drink any alcoholic beverages while at such licensed location or to be present at said location in an intoxicated condition. Permitted violations of this section by the licensee, or repeated violations, shall constitute grounds for suspension, revocation and refusal to renew said license or other action with regard to said license.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(6))
Sec. 4-57. - Consumption on premises.
Except as provided herein for premises licensed to sell a type of alcoholic beverage for consumption on the premises and wine cafes, it shall be unlawful for anyone to open or consume any alcoholic beverage upon any premises for which a license to sell any retail package alcoholic beverage is issued.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(19); Ord. of 8-3-2009)
Sec. 4-58. - Removal of alcoholic beverages in open container from licenses premises prohibited.
No person, firm or corporation licensed to sell alcoholic beverages by package sale or for consumption on the premises, or any employees or agents of said licensee, shall permit any person to remove from such premises, any alcoholic beverage in any open container; provided, however, that any restaurant which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises may permit a patron to remove one unsealed bottle of wine in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-6-4.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(A)(20))
Sec. 4-59. - Hours of operation.
(a)
No sale of alcoholic beverages by the package shall be allowed except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on weekdays and Saturdays.
(b)
No sale of alcoholic beverage for consumption on the premises shall be allowed except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on Mondays through Thursdays. On Fridays and Saturdays, these hours shall be extended to 1:45 a.m. on the succeeding day (that is, sale for consumption on the premises shall be allowed between the hours of 12:01 a.m. and 1:45 a.m. on Saturdays, and between the hours of 12:01 a.m. and 1:45 a.m. on Sundays).
(c)
All establishments holding a license for sale of any alcoholic beverage for consumption on the premises where sold shall remove all alcoholic beverages which have been served to customers from tables, bars, and all other service areas within 30 minutes of the cessation of the sales hours established in subsection (b) of this section 4-59.
(d)
Sunday sales for consumption on the premises where sold. In addition to the provisions of subsection (b) of this section, the sale of alcoholic beverages on the premises shall be permitted on Sundays during the hours of 12:30 p.m. to 12:00 midnight, in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served, and in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging.
(e)
Sunday sales of malt beverages and wine by the package. Retailers holding a valid license from the city for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine by the package (i.e., "package stores" as defined in section 4-22) shall be authorized to sell malt beverages and wine by the package on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m., and 11:30 p.m.
