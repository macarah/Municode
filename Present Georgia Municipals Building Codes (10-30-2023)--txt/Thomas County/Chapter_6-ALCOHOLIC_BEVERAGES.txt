ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverages, wine or fortified wine.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Retailer or retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 6-2. - Consumption on certain public lands prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to consume any alcoholic beverage while on any of the following public lands or properties of the county:
(1)
That tract of land known as the Thomas County boat ramp property, and the access road thereto, both located in Land Lots 14 and 15 of the 13th Land District of Thomas County, Georgia.
(2)
That tract of land known as the Thomas County fish pond property, and the access road thereto, both located in Land Lot 144 of the 13th Land District of Thomas County, Georgia.
The term alcoholic beverage shall include all malt beverages, wine and distilled spirits.
(b)
Possession of an open container of any alcoholic beverage while on any of the above public lands or properties of the county shall constitute prima facie evidence of violation of this section.
(c)
Prosecution for violation of this section shall be instituted and conducted pursuant to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. in the magistrate court of the county.
(d)
Penalty.
(1)
First offense. Any person convicted for a first offense under this section shall be assessed a fine of not more than $100.00.
(2)
Second offense. Any person convicted of a second offense under this section shall be assessed a fine of not more than $250.00, imprisoned for not more than 20 days, or both.
(3)
Third or subsequent offense. Any person convicted of a third or subsequent offense under this section shall be fined an amount not to exceed $500.00, imprisoned for not more than 60 days, or both. The court may suspend sentence if the interest of justice so requires, upon such conditions as it may direct. No offense committed more than five years prior to an adjudication of guilt shall be deemed a previous conviction for the purpose of computation of penalty.
(Ord. of 1-13-87)
State Law reference— Possession of open container of alcoholic beverage while operating vehicle, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-253.
Sec. 6-3. - Security cameras required.
(a)
Any establishment licensed by the county to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package for off-premises consumption is required to install security cameras of a type and number approved by the sheriff of the county.
(b)
Cameras meeting the requirements of this section must be capable of producing a retrievable image on film or tape that can be made a permanent record and can be enlarged through projection or other means. Cameras meeting the requirements of this section shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to periodic inspection by the sheriff or his designee.
(c)
Violation of any provision of this section shall constitute an offense and be punishable as follows:
(1)
First offense. A fine of $200.00 and the judge of the state court may impose a suspension of all alcoholic beverage licenses from the county for a period not to exceed 30 days.
(2)
Second offense. An offense will be considered a second offense if it occurs within 365 calendar days from the date of the occurrence of the first offense. A second offense will incur a fine of $350.00 and all alcoholic beverage licenses at the establishment issued by the county will be suspended for a minimum period of 60 days.
(Res. of 2-12-91, §§ 1—4; Res. of 9-14-93, §§ 1—3)

ARTICLE II. - PACKAGE SALES OF MALT BEVERAGES

Sec. 6-31. - Sale authorized.
Authority is hereby given for the sale of malt beverages at wholesale or retail within the unincorporated areas of the county, subject to the provisions and conditions of this article.
(Code 1977, § 7-2001)
Sec. 6-32. - License required; display; minimum inventory of other goods; package sales only.
(a)
No person shall sell malt beverages, at either wholesale or retail, within the unincorporated area of the county unless a license for carrying on the business of selling beer, wholesale or retail as the case may be, is first obtained from the board of commissioners, upon written application made to the board of commissioners, and payment of the business tax required in this Code for the carrying on of that business.
(b)
The license shall be prominently displayed in the person's place of business.
(c)
Only those places of business which carry in stock or inventory an amount of goods in excess of $5,000.00 exclusive of malt beverages and wine shall be eligible for a license to sell malt beverages. Such stock or inventory shall be defined as and include any two of the following: foodstuffs, dry goods or gasoline products. The license shall be restricted to the sale of packaged malt beverages not to be consumed in the place of business, except as otherwise provided in section 6-61.
(Code 1977, § 7-2002; Res. of 11-13-90, § 2(a))
Sec. 6-33. - Application.
(a)
The application for a license as required in this article shall be in writing and addressed to the board of commissioners, and shall set forth therein the following information:
(1)
Whether he desires to sell malt beverages at wholesale or retail.
(2)
The location of the place of business wherein malt beverages are to be sold.
(3)
The age of the applicant.
(4)
If a corporation, where incorporated.
(5)
The present occupations of the applicants.
(6)
The names and ages of the persons who shall be employed in the carrying on of such business.
(b)
The application shall be signed by the applicant, and must be verified by oath of the applicant, and if the applicant is a corporation, by the oath of one of its executive officers or managers. Such application and oath shall be on forms provided by the county clerk.
(Code 1977, § 7-2003)
Sec. 6-34. - Fingerprinting of certain applicants and certain employees.
(a)
Each applicant for a license to sell malt beverages in the county who has not heretofore been licensed by the board of commissioners to sell malt beverages at retail, shall be required to submit to a complete fingerprinting record and test, pursuant to rules and regulations established by the board of commissioners from time to time, the such fingerprinting record and test being used by the county to supplement the information contained on the application and to make available to the county any records of criminal offenses, either federal or state from other jurisdictions.
(b)
The board of commissioners, within its own discretion, from time to time may require employees working in any establishment dispensing malt beverages to submit to the same complete fingerprinting records and test and for the same purposes of supplementing any information available to the county with respect to the employees.
(Code 1977, § 7-2004)
Sec. 6-35. - Consideration and approval; issuance.
Each application provided for in this article shall be considered and passed upon by the board of commissioners in regular or special session, legally assembled. No permit shall be granted to any person unless it be made to appear to the board of commissioners that the applicant is a suitable and fit person to carry on such business, and that the business will be conducted in an orderly manner and in compliance with all laws, municipal, state and federal. Upon approval of the application and payment of the required business tax, the applicant shall be entitled to the issuance of the license. The license shall be issued with the original to the licensee and a copy retained by the board of commissioners by the county clerk.
(Code 1977, § 7-2005)
Sec. 6-36. - Revocation for violation; hearing and determination.
(a)
If, after issuance of the license provided for in this article, the licensee should be fined or sentenced in any court for violation of any law of the county, state or United States relative to the sale of malt beverages or other alcoholic beverage, he shall be given at least three days' notice of a hearing before the board of commissioners and ordered to show cause why his privilege license should not be placed on probation, or withdrawn temporarily or permanently. The board of commissioners may, in its discretion, revoke the license permanently for cause, or may place the licensee on probation for two years on the first fine or sentence as above.
(b)
If the license has previously been placed on probation for any violation as above written within a two-year period, upon the first such fine or sentence thereafter for another violation his license shall be immediately withdrawn for a period of 30 days.
(c)
If the license has previously been withdrawn for any violation as above within a two-year period, upon the first such fine or sentence thereafter for another violation his license shall be immediately withdrawn for a period of three months.
(d)
If the license has previously been withdrawn the second time, under the provisions of subsection (c), and another violation as above occurs within a two-year period thereafter for which fine or sentence imposed, the license shall be withdrawn immediately pending hearing before the board of commissioners, which may in its discretion revoke the license permanently for cause, or impose such other terms, conditions or suspension as it may decide upon at the conclusion of the hearing.
(Code 1977, § 7-2006)
Sec. 6-37. - Sales to minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, give, provide or furnish any minor with malt beverages, wine or alcoholic spirituous liquors of any nature, and any offender under the provisions of this section shall be penalized by the revocation of any county license.
(Code 1977, § 7-2007)
Sec. 6-38. - Proximity of sales to schools and churches.
No license shall be issued for the sale of malt beverages to any new business unless the new business is located 200 yards or more from any school ground or college campus and 200 yards or more from any established church building. For purposes of measurement for schools or colleges, the measurement shall be from the property line nearest the retail establishment to the closest wall of the retail establishment provided, however, that if malt beverages are sold for consumption in areas adjacent to the building, the measurement shall be from the nearest property line of the school or college to the property line of the retail business. For purposes of measurement for churches, the measurement shall be in a straight line from the point on the wall of the church building closest to the retail establishment to the wall of the retail establishment closest to the church.
(Code 1977, § 7-2008; Ord. of 5-11-99; Ord. of 5-24-99)
Sec. 6-39. - Business hours restricted.
(a)
No malt beverages shall be kept for sale or offered for sale in any building, dwelling house, storehouse or other place on Sundays.
(b)
No malt beverages shall be sold or given away in the county before 5:00 a.m. or after 12:00 midnight on days during the week other than Sunday. No place of business where alcoholic beverages, provided for in this chapter, are sold exclusively shall be open for business before 5:00 a.m. and such places of business shall be closed and kept closed after 12:00 midnight.
(Code 1977, § 7-2009)
Sec. 6-40. - Location restricted to licensed premises.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell malt beverages within the county except in his place of business described in the permit and license issued to him.
(Code 1977, § 7-2010)
Sec. 6-41. - Sheriff, deputies to report violations.
It shall be the duty of the sheriff and each deputy of the county to keep strict surveillance over the places of business in the county where malt beverages are permitted to be sold, and to report to the board of commissioners any violations of any provisions of this Code, or other law of the county or any state or federal law, in reference to the selling of malt beverages or other intoxicants within the county.
(Code 1977, § 7-2012)

(Code 1977, § 7-2023(a))

Sec. 6-109. - Proximity of sales to schools and churches.
No wine shall be sold for consumption on the premises in any building, storehouse, place of business or vehicle located within 100 yards of any school ground or college campus or within 300 yards of any established church, the place of business being herein defined as any building or structure in which wine is sold, or if wine is sold at such location for consumption on the premises other than in a building or structure, then, in that case, the place of business is defined as being any portion of the lot occupied by such business. In measuring from the school grounds to a building or structure, the measurement shall be made directly from the part of the school grounds nearest to the part of the lot that fronts on a public street, then directly to the nearest part of the building in which wine are to be sold for consumption on the premises.
(Amd. of 10-14-97)
Sec. 6-110. - Business hours restricted.
(a)
No wine shall be kept for sale or offered for sale in any building, dwelling house, storehouse or other place on Sundays.
(b)
No wine shall be sold or given away in the county before 5:00 a.m. or after 12:00 midnight on days during the week other than Sunday. No place of business where alcoholic beverages, provided for in this chapter, are sold exclusively shall be open for business before 5:00 a.m. and such places of business shall be closed and kept closed after 12:00 midnight.
(Amd. of 10-14-97)
Sec. 6-111. - Location; restricted to licensed premises.
It shall be unlawful for any persons to sell wine for consumption on premises within the county except in his place of business described in the permit and license issued to him.
(Amd. of 10-14-97)
Sec. 6-112. - Sheriff, deputies to report violations.
It shall be the duty of the sheriff and each deputy of the county to keep surveillance over the places of business in the county where wine is permitted to be sold, and to report to the board of commissioners any violations of any of the provisions of this Code, or other law of the county or any state or federal law, in reference to the selling of wine or other intoxicants within the county.
(Amd. of 10-14-97)

ARTICLE VI. - EXCISE TAXES

Sec. 6-116. - Levied on wholesale dealers in malt beverages.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, there is hereby levied and imposed upon all wholesale dealers selling malt beverages within the unincorporated areas of the county a specific excise tax in the amount of .4166 cents per ounce of malt beverages sold by each wholesale dealer within the unincorporated limits of the county.
(b)
All malt beverages sold in or from a barrel or bulk container and being commonly known as tap or draft beer, shall not be subject to the excise tax provided for in subsection (a) of this section, but in lieu thereof there is hereby levied and imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling such malt beverages within the unincorporated areas of the county an excise tax of $6.00 for each barrel or bulk container having a capacity of 15½ gallons sold by such wholesale dealer within the unincorporated areas of the county, and at a like rate for fractional parts thereof.
(c)
Each wholesale dealer taxed under this section shall file a report with the county clerk by the tenth day of each month showing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type of container, sold by the dealer within the county. Each such wholesale dealer shall remit to the county not later than the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made, the amount of excise tax due.
(d)
The excise tax provided for in this section shall be in addition to any license fee, tax or charge which may now or in the future be imposed upon the business of selling malt beverages at wholesale within the corporate limits of the county.
(e)
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to ten percent of the total amount due, plus interest accrued at the rate of 12 percent per annum. The county clerk shall issue an execution against the wholesale dealer, firm or corporation so delinquent on the dealer's property for the amount of the excise tax due and delinquent, plus penalty and interest accrued, and shall proceed to collect that amount in the same manner as is done in the case of executions issued for collection of county ad valorem property taxes.
(f)
In addition to the grounds for revocation of business licenses to sell malt beverages in the county provided in this Code, additional grounds for revocation of license of wholesale dealers shall exist where at any time it is found that false and fraudulent reports have been made by the wholesale dealer, his agents, officers or employees, and upon which payment of malt beverages excise tax was reported or paid to the county; or where at any time the monthly report and payment called for in this section is past due over 30 days. There shall be the same notice of hearing before the board of commissioners, hearing and action taken on revocation of the license as is provided in section 6-36.
(Code 1977, § 3-2022)
Sec. 6-117. - Levied on retail dealers of wine.
There is imposed and levied upon all retail dealers in wine within the unincorporated areas of the county an excise tax on the first sale or use of wine by the package at a rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(Code 1977, § 3-2023(a))
Sec. 6-118. - Collection; enforcement.
The board of commissioners is hereby empowered to implement the collection of the tax levied under this article by the examination and verification of such invoices and reports hereinafter called for as in the board's discretion may be necessary to accomplish and enforce the foregoing, and specifically, is authorized to require the examination of the books and invoices of wholesalers, distributors or manufacturers selling wine to retail dealers in the county, and to require the same of retail dealers in wine located in the county.
(Code 1977, § 3-2023(b))
Sec. 6-119. - Manner of payment.
The tax levied under this article shall be in addition to all other taxes or license fees imposed upon the retail dealers, and may be added by the retail dealer and collected from each customer as if the same were a sales tax, but shall be paid, nevertheless, to the county by the retail dealer in the following manner:
(1)
Invoices; preparation, disposition. Each distributor, wholesale dealer or manufacturer selling, shipping or delivering wine to any retail dealer or to any establishment having a wine license in the county by rail, truck or otherwise, whether delivered to the dealer's place of business in the county or elsewhere for resale in the county, shall make three true and correct copies of invoices of all sales and deliveries made to or for retail dealers in the county. The invoices must show the name of each retail dealer, the location of the place of business of each retailer or the place where each delivery was made, the method of delivery, the number and size of each container, the amount collected as taxes, and any other information as may be called for by the board of commissioners. One copy of the invoice shall be delivered to the retail dealer at the time of delivery of the wine. One copy shall be mailed to the board of commissioners on or before the 15th of the month following delivery and one copy shall be retained and preserved by the wholesaler, distributor, or manufacturer, for a period of 12 months from date of the sale to the retail dealer. The copy so retained shall be subject to inspection by any duly authorized representative of the county at all reasonable times.
(2)
Collection, custody of taxes. At any time of delivery of wine the wholesale dealer, distributor or manufacturer shall collect from the retail dealer the taxes imposed by this article and hold the same in trust for the county until the tax is remitted to the county as hereinafter provided.
(3)
Monthly report; remittance of taxes; service fee. On or before the 15th day of each calendar month each wholesale dealer, distributor or manufacturer delivering wine to or for any retail dealer or to an establishment having a wine license shall make a true and correct report to the county of all sales and deliveries made to or for retail dealers in the county for the current month immediately preceding the report. The report shall show, by attaching copies of invoices required under subsection (1) of this section, the name of each retail dealer, the location of the place of business of the retailer or the place where each delivery was made, the method of delivery, the number and size of each container, the amount collected as taxes, and any other information as may be called for by the board of commissioners. Each report shall be accompanied by remittance to the county of all taxes collected or due as shown on the report, and shall also be accompanied by a sworn statement that the report is a true and correct report of all sales and shipments made within the county. For the service rendered by the wholesaler in collecting from the retailer and remitting to the board of commissioners the sums collected, the wholesaler shall be entitled to receive and may deduct from the remittance five percent of the amount collected by them.
(Code 1977, § 3-2023(c))
Sec. 6-120. - Records of retailers.
All retail dealers of wine and all persons selling wine at retail within the county shall keep a correct record of all purchases of wine and shall demand of and require all persons from whom they purchase wine to furnish and deliver to them with the wine a correct invoice of each purchase, this invoice to be kept and preserved by the retailer at the place of business for a period of 12 months from the date of the purchase and the invoices shall be open and subject to inspection by any authorized representative of the county at all reasonable times.
(Code 1977, § 3-2023(d))
Sec. 6-121. - Delinquent taxes.
If a wholesaler, distributor or manufacturer fails and refuses to make the reports as provided in this article and at the time specified, the board of commissioners shall notify that party in writing by mail, or otherwise. If the reports are not made and the taxes paid within five days from date of the notice, the board of commissioners shall proceed to assess the amount of taxes due by the dealer from the best information available, together with ten percent thereof as a penalty and proceed to collect the taxes and penalty, as provided for the collection of delinquent taxes.
(Code 1977, § 3-2023(e))
Sec. 6-122. - Violations.
It shall be a violation of this Code for any person to sell at retail or otherwise within the county, any wine on which the tax as set out in this article has not been paid to the wholesaler or distributor for the county as provided in this article. The violation of this article shall be deemed a sufficient ground for the revocation of the license of any retail or wholesale dealer holding a license to sell wine within the county.
(Code 1977, § 3-2023(f))
