ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 20-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Permanent trailer means any trailer from which the wheels have been removed except for temporary purposes or repair, and which is attached to the ground by means of posts, piers or a foundation of any type.
Trailer means any vehicle, house car, motor home, fifth wheel, movable structure or any portable or movable vehicle on wheels, skids, rollers or blocks, either self-propelled or propelled by any other means, which is designed to be used for residential living, sleeping, commercial or utility purposes.
(Code 1969, § 18-1)
Sec. 20-2. - Temporary construction trailer.
A temporary construction trailer may be used while a permanent structure is being built provided a permit has been issued by the building official, but for not more than ninety (90) days unless the permit is extended. For purposes of this section, a temporary construction trailer means a movable structure located at a construction site wherein the various phases and operations of a construction project are coordinated and administered.

ARTICLE II. - TRAILER CAMPS

Sec. 20-26. - General requirements.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or maintain any trailer used for human habitation or to use any trailer for living, sleeping or business purposes on any premises in the City.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park, for use or occupancy, any trailer overnight on any street, alley, highway or other public place.
(c)
One (1) trailer owned by the holder of fee simple title to the land or the occupant may be parked or stored on any lot or plot, provided that no living quarters shall be maintained nor any business conducted therein while such trailer is so parked or stored.
(Code 1969, § 18-19)
Sec. 20-27. - Required lot area.
(a)
No trailer camp shall be constructed or maintained on a lot which has an average width of less than two hundred (200) feet or total area of less than sixty thousand (60,000) square feet.
(b)
Each trailer space shall be at least twenty-five (25) feet in width and shall provide a minimum of one thousand (1,000) square feet in area exclusive of access driveways, off-street parking and other required service areas; provided, that existing trailer spaces not less than eight hundred (800) square feet may be accepted.
(c)
Each trailer space shall be defined by a marker at each corner.
(Code 1969, § 18-20)
Sec. 20-28. - Yard requirements.
No trailer shall be located within:
(1)
Five (5) feet of its individual lot line.
(2)
Fifteen (15) feet of another such unit side-by-side or end-to-end.
(3)
Fifty (50) feet of the right-of-way line of any public street or way.
(4)
Twenty (20) feet of any exterior boundary, excepting any public street or way, of the trailer court.
(Code 1969, § 18-21)
Sec. 20-29. - Automobile parking requirements.
(a)
One (1) off-street automobile parking space for each trailer space shall be provided in a trailer camp.
(b)
Such off-street parking area shall be situated in a location convenient to the occupants of the trailers and shall have ingress and egress by means of a public way. Such off-street parking area shall be paved in accordance with specifications of the public works department of the county.
(c)
Ingress and egress to parking areas adjacent to a public street shall be through driveways or openings not exceeding twenty-five (25) feet in width in the curb line of the street.
(d)
Automobile parking shall be prohibited within a trailer camp except in the specified off-street parking areas.
(e)
Each trailer space shall abut upon a driveway of not less than twenty (20) feet in width. Such driveways shall be of all-weather type, well defined, lighted at night, and shall have clear access to a public thoroughfare.
(Code 1969, § 18-22)
Sec. 20-30. - Minimum facilities.
Each trailer camp shall provide the following minimum facilities on the site for the common use of all trailer occupants:
(1)
Walkways. Well marked serviceable walkways from each trailer space to toilets, laundry, or other service buildings.
(2)
Electric outlets. A weatherproof electric outlet supplying one hundred ten (110) volts shall be provided at each trailer space. Power lines over the trailer camp shall be not less than fifteen (15) feet above the ground or in acceptable underground conduits, and no power line shall be permitted to lie on the ground.
(3)
Refuse collection facilities. One (1) refuse collection station shall be provided for each twelve (12) families or fraction thereof, conveniently located to serve tenants, not more than one hundred (100) feet from any trailer served and shall be conveniently located for collection. Such station shall be constructed with a concrete floor, protected with a roof, properly screened, have a water supply, and shall have sanitary drainage facilities.
(4)
Water and sewerage. Each trailer shall be connected with water and sanitary facilities in a manner approved by the county health department and in compliance with the plumbing code of the county.
(5)
Laundry facilities. Laundry facilities shall be connected to approved sanitary facilities and shall be provided at the rate of four (4) laundry trays, two (2) washing machines, and eight (8) ironing boards for each fifty (50) trailer units or fraction thereof. Such facilities shall be located not more than three hundred fifty (350) feet from the trailer units served.
(6)
Laundry drying yards. Each trailer camp shall provide a fenced laundry drying yard adjacent to laundry facilities. Such drying yards shall provide an aggregate of fifteen (15) feet of clothesline per trailer unit. In lieu of a fenced laundry yard automatic drying equipment consisting of one (1) dryer unit for each ten (10) trailer units or fraction thereof may be substituted.
(7)
Public telephones. Public telephones shall be provided at convenient locations in separate structures or booths in the proportion of one (1) phone per thirty-five (35) trailer units or fraction thereof.
(8)
Toilet and bathing facilities. Separate and plainly marked central toilet facilities for each sex shall be provided within two hundred (200) feet of trailer units without private or connecting baths.
a.
Central facilities shall provide as a minimum:
1.
For men: One (1) commode, one (1) lavatory, one (1) urinal, and one (1) tub or showerhead for each ten (10) trailer units or fraction thereof.
2.
For women: One (1) commode, one (1) lavatory, one (1) tub, and one (1) showerhead for each ten (10) trailer units, or fraction thereof.
b.
Sufficient artificial light shall be provided to illuminate adequately each central toilet or bathing facility.
(9)
Fuel storage. Individual containers and outdoor storage facilities for liquid cooking and heating fuel shall be provided for each trailer unit. Bulk liquid fuel containers shall be provided when necessary to comply with regulations set forth by the fire department of the county. Such containers shall be located conveniently for delivery by fuel trucks.
(10)
Fire protection. Each trailer unit shall be equipped with a fire extinguisher approved by the fire department of the county. In addition, each trailer camp shall furnish one (1) hose cabinet connected with the county water supply together with fire hose and nozzle, for each twenty-five (25) trailer units or fraction thereof.
(11)
Landscaping. Each trailer camp shall be landscaped with shade trees and exterior screen planting to provide and assure the reasonable amenities of living and privacy characteristic of more permanent living quarters.
(Code 1969, § 18-23)
Sec. 20-31. - Improvements to trailers.
(a)
No permanent additions of any kind shall be built onto or become a part of any transient trailer unit.
(b)
Permanent additions may be constructed upon, adjacent to, or over permanent trailer units; provided that a building permit is obtained for such additions and that the additions comply with the building, electrical, and plumbing codes and other applicable ordinances; and further provided that no addition encroaches upon the required open spaces as required herein.
(Code 1969, § 18-24)

ARTICLE III. - TOURIST COURTS

Sec. 20-51. - General requirements.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or maintain a tourist court as herein defined on any premises in the incorporated area of the City except in a commercial or industrial district, and then only when duly permitted and maintained pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(Code 1969, § 18-35)
Sec. 20-52. - Required lot area.
(a)
No tourist court shall be constructed or maintained on a lot which has an average width of less than two hundred (200) feet or total area of less than sixty thousand (60,000) square feet.
(b)
The minimum aggregate gross land area requirement per sleeping unit shall be one thousand (1,000) square feet.
(c)
The minimum area of premises used or occupied as a living unit shall be one hundred seventy (170) square feet. Each structure shall contain not less than two (2) units.
(Code 1969, § 18-36)
Sec. 20-53. - Yard requirements.
No building shall be located within:
(1)
Twenty (20) feet from any sideline.
(2)
Ten (10) feet from any other structure.
(3)
Twenty-five (25) feet of the rear lot line.
(4)
One hundred twenty (120) feet from the centerline of any public county road or any state highway situated within the City or any other highway which may be designated as an arterial highway and in no instance less than forty-five (45) feet from the property line abutting such public road or highway.
(5)
Seventy-five (75) feet from the centerline of any minor street and in no instance less than forty-five (45) feet from the property line abutting such public road or highway.
(Code 1969, § 18-37)
Sec. 20-54. - Automobile parking requirements.
(a)
One (1) off-street automobile parking space for each living unit shall be provided in a tourist court.
(b)
Such off-street parking area shall be situated in a location convenient to the occupants of the living units and shall have ingress and egress by means of a public way. Such off-street parking area shall be paved in accordance with specifications of the public works department of the county.
(c)
Ingress and egress to parking areas adjacent to a public street shall be through driveways or openings not exceeding twenty-five (25) feet in width in the curbline of such street.
(d)
Automobile parking shall be prohibited within a tourist court except in the specified off-street parking areas.
(Code 1969, § 18-38)
Sec. 20-55. - Minimum facilities.
Each tourist court shall provide the following minimum facilities on the site for the common use of all occupants:
(1)
Water and sewerage. All plumbing in the tourist camps shall be connected with water and sanitary facilities in a manner approved by the county health department in compliance with the plumbing code of the county.
(2)
Public telephone. As specified for trailer camps.
(3)
Toilet and bathing facilities. Each unit shall have a lavatory, commode and shower or tub, and shall be supplied with hot and cold running water.
(4)
Fire protection. Each tourist camp shall furnish one (1) fire hose cabinet connected with the county water supply, together with fire hose and nozzle, for each twenty-five (25) units or fraction thereof and any other facilities required by the county fire department.
(5)
Landscaping. As specified for trailer camps.
(Code 1969, § 18-39)
