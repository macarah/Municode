ARTICLE II. - PRELIMINARY PLAT

Sec. 17.5-21. - Preparation of preliminary plat and application for approval.
The developer shall have a registered civil engineer, surveyor or landscape architect prepare the preliminary plat of the subdivision. Eight (8) copies shall be filed with the City Superintendent if the development is to be served by a public sewer, or ten (10) copies if the development is to be served by septic tanks, along with three (3) copies of the preliminary subdivision application. Applications may be obtained at the Doraville City Hall or the county planning department offices.
(Ord. No. 133, § 4, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-22. - Review of preliminary plat; tentative approval; disapproval.
The preliminary plat will be transmitted to the county subdivision administrator for processing through the various county departments by the subdivision administrator for tentative approval. Comments, recommendations and changes, if any, will be marked on the preliminary plat in each department and returned to the subdivision administrator, who shall combine all data and return the preliminary plat to the City Superintendent for submission to the Planning Commission for approval or disapproval.
Three (3) copies of the preliminary plat with the action of the Planning Commission and reasons for disapproval, if disapproved, shown thereon shall be certified by the secretary of the Planning Commission.
If the preliminary plat is approved by the Planning Commission, the City Superintendent shall immediately transmit said plat to the City Council for review and concurrence in the dedication of streets and other public lands shown thereon. If any changes in said plat are desired by the City Council, the plat and the requested changes shall immediately be transmitted back to the Planning Commission for further review and conference with the developer. Further processing of the plat shall follow the same procedures as for initial processing of a preliminary plat. After action by the Planning Commission and the City Council, one (1) copy of the preliminary plat, whether approved or disapproved, shall be filed in the Doraville City Hall, the second copy in the county planning department, and the third copy shall be returned to the developer. Upon receiving an approved preliminary plat, the developer shall have prepared grading and drainage plans which shall be submitted to the county road engineer for approval prior to grading and staking the subdivision.
(Ord. No. 133, § 5, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-23. - What approval of preliminary plat constitutes.
Approval of the preliminary plat shall not constitute approval of the final plat. Rather it shall be deemed an expression of approval of the layout submitted on the preliminary plat as a guide to the preparation of the final plat which will be submitted for approval of the Planning Commission, the City Council, and the county commissioners and recorded upon fulfillment of the requirements of these regulations and the conditions of the approval of the preliminary plat, if any. If a final plat for all of the subdivision, or a portion of the subdivision is not submitted is not submitted within twelve (12) months of the preliminary plat approval, the preliminary approval shall expire. If final plats are not submitted for the remaining portions of the subdivision within three (3) years of preliminary plat approval, the preliminary plat approval shall expire, unless an extension of time is granted by the Planning Commission.
(Ord. No. 133, § 6, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-24. - Scale of preliminary plat.
Preliminary plats shall be prepared at a scale of fifty (50), one hundred (100) or two hundred (200) feet to one (1) inch.
(Ord. No. 133, § 7, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-25. - Information required to be shown on preliminary plat.
The following existing conditions shall be shown on the preliminary plat:
(1)
Boundary lines. Perimeter boundary of overall tract, bearings, if available, distances referred to a land lot corner or known street intersection, and total area of the tract.
(2)
Easements. Location, width and purpose.
(3)
Streets on and adjacent to the tract. Name, right-of-way width and location.
(4)
Contour data. Significant contour data at a maximum of not more than five-foot elevation intervals to determine preliminary design of the proposed improvements and other dependent features. The source of this data shall be written on the plat.
(5)
Other conditions on the tract. Watercourses with total drainage area served, existing structures and other significant features.
(6)
Title and certificates. Title under which proposed subdivision is to be recorded, if known, with name of owner and name of designer.
(7)
Geographical data. Numerical and graphic scales, north arrow, date of survey, date of plat, land lot and district numbers and lines, City and county names and limit lines.
(8)
Prior subdivisions. Name and reference of any former subdivision crossing any of the land shown on the plat.
(9)
Proposed or existing covenants.
(Ord. No. 133, § 8, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-26. - Physical layout required to be shown.
The following proposed conditions shall be shown on the preliminary plat:
(1)
Street names; right-of-way widths including necessary right-of-way required for present or future widening of major, minor, collector or other streets as shown on the thoroughfare plan.
(2)
Lot lines, lot numbers and block numbers.
(3)
Sites, if any, to be dedicated or reserved for parks, schools, playgrounds or other public uses, together with the purpose and the conditions or limitations of such dedication, if any.
(4)
Sites, if any, reserved for use other than single-family dwellings, with purpose indicated.
(5)
Minimum building setback lines as required under yard requirements in the zoning ordinance.
(Ord. No. 133, § 9, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-27. - Restrictions on names.
The proposed name of the subdivision and proposed street names shall not duplicate or too closely approximate, phonetically, the name of any other subdivision or street in the Metropolitan Atlanta area. Information will be furnished by the property numbering engineer in the county department of planning for duplication.
(Ord. No. 133, § 10, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-28. - Compliance with zoning.
The proposed land development shall comply with all requirements as stipulated in the zoning chapter.
(Ord. No. 133, § 11, 10-2-67)
