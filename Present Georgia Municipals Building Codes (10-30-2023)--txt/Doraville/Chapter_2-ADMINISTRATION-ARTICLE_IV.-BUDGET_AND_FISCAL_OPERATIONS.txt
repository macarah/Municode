ARTICLE IV. - BUDGET AND FISCAL OPERATIONS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Charter reference— Fiscal administration, § 4.01 et seq.

Sec. 2-81. - Budget required.
The City shall be operated on a budget, duly adopted in accordance with the procedures as are set forth in this article.
(Ord. No. 185, § I, 2-10-76)
Sec. 2-82. - Fiscal year.
modified
The City shall be operated on a fiscal year basis.
(1)
For Fiscal Year 2022 and all prior fiscal years, the fiscal year shall commence on July 1 of each year and end on June 30 of the following calendar year.
(2)
The City shall adopt a three-month budget for the fiscal period running from July 1, 2022, through September 30, 2022.
(3)
For Fiscal Year 2023 and all subsequent fiscal years, the fiscal year shall commence on October 1 of each year and end on September 30 of the following calendar year.
(Ord. No. 185, § II, 2-10-76; Ord. No. 2022-17 , § 1, 3-14-22)
Sec. 2-83. - Preparation by City Manager or designee.
The City Manager or designee shall, after evaluation of the fiscal condition of the City, prepare and submit to the Council a budget which shall set forth categories of projected planned expenditures and detailed plans for revenue collection of the City for the next fiscal year. The City Manager or designee shall have the power to hire or associate such financial experts or other persons as he deems necessary for the proper analysis, presentation and drafting of the budget.
(Ord. No. 185, § III, 2-10-76; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 2013-11, adopted June 17, 2013, changed the title of § 2-83 from "Preparation by Mayor" to "Preparation by City Manager or designee."
Sec. 2-84. - Review by Council.
The Council shall, after receipt of the City Manager or designee's proposed budget, analyze and review such budget. The Council shall have the power to amend the City Manager or designee's proposed budget by procedures which the Council may itself adopt.
(Ord. No. 185, § IV, 2-10-76; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Sec. 2-85. - When to be adopted.
modified
The City Manager or designee's proposed budget, as amended by the Council, shall be presented and adopted by a majority vote of the City Council prior to the commencement of each fiscal year at a regularly scheduled or specially called meeting of the Mayor and City Council.
(Ord. No. 185, § V, 2-10-76; Ord. No. 04-14, § 1, 6-7-04; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13; Ord. No. 2022-17 , § 2, 3-14-22)
Sec. 2-86. - Manner of adoption.
The City Manager or designee's proposed budget and the adopted budget shall not be deemed to be an ordinance or resolution of the Mayor and City Council, but rather a financial projection and plan for the City based upon facts existing at the date of adoption. As such, the budget may be adopted by the Council in accordance with the procedures outlined herein without necessity of multiple readings as required by the City charter for ordinances and resolutions.
(Ord. No. 185, § VIII, 2-10-76; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Sec. 2-87. - Modification during fiscal year.
If, upon review of the City's current financial condition at any time during the fiscal year, it appears to the City Manager or designee that amendment or revision of the budget is necessary to reflect current financial information regarding City collections or expenditures, then such amendment is authorized to be made by the City Manager or designee; provided, however, that any change in any scheduled expenditure in the budget in excess of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) shall require majority approval by the Council.
(Ord. No. 185, § VI, 2-10-76; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Sec. 2-88. - City Manager or designee to apprise Council of City's financial condition.
It shall be the responsibility of the City Manager or designee to keep the Council apprised of the current financial condition of the City throughout the fiscal year.
(Ord. No. 185, § VII, 2-10-76; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 2013-11, adopted June 17, 2013, changed the title of § 2-88 from "Mayor to apprise Council of City's financial condition" to "City Manager or designee to apprise Council of City's financial condition."
Sec. 2-89. - Reserved.
Sec. 2-90. - Purchasing.
The City's purchasing and bidding requirements shall be governed by purchasing policies as adopted by Mayor and City Council and as may be amended in the future. A copy of said purchasing policies shall be kept on file with the City Clerk and Finance Director, and available for inspection upon request.
(Ord. No. 93-16, § 1, 10-4-93; Ord. No. 94-4, § 1, 4-4-94; Ord. No. 04-19, § 1, 8-2-04; Ord. No. 2011-26, § 1, 6-20-11; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13; Ord. No. 2013-15, § 1, 7-15-13)
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 2013-15, adopted July 15, 2013, changed the title of § 2-90 from "Bids required; conditions; exceptions" to "Purchasing."
Sec. 2-91. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Section 2 of Ord. No. 2013-15, adopted July 15, 2013, repealed § 2-91 which pertained to purchasing authority of the Mayor, and derived from Ord. No. 2012-24, § 1, adopted Dec. 3, 2012; Res. No. 2013-03, adopted June 17, 2013; and Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, adopted June 17, 2013.
Secs. 2-92—2-105. - Reserved.
