Sec. 7-1. - Enforcement by county fire officials.
Any fire official of the county, as well as the City Building Official or any member of the police department, may enforce the provisions of this chapter and make charges against any person for violations thereof.
(Code 1969, § 7-1)
Sec. 7-2. - Fire districts.
There are hereby adopted the fire districts of DeKalb County as may be now in effect, as set out on the map of the DeKalb County fire prevention system dated November 12, 1963, prepared by the county planning department, a copy of which is on file in the City Clerk's office, and by reference is made a part hereof.
(Code 1969, § 7-2)
Sec. 7-3. - Code adopted.
Chapter 7, entitled "Fire Prevention," of Part II of the Code of DeKalb County, Georgia, as amended by the action of the board of commissioners dated April 9, 1974, is hereby adopted as the fire prevention code of the City.
(Ord. No. 175, § 1, 10-7-74)
Editor's note— Pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25, on and after October 1, 1991, the Standard Fire Prevention Code (SBCCI) shall have statewide application and shall not require adoption by a municipality.
Sec. 7-4. - Enforcement of fire code.
The Bureau of Fire Prevention of the DeKalb County Fire Department operated under the supervision of the Chief of the DeKalb County Fire Department shall enforce the fire prevention code as established by previously agreed to terms between the City of Doraville, Georgia, and DeKalb County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 175, § 2, 10-7-74)
Sec. 7-5. - Appointment of fire marshals.
The City reserves the right to appoint such fire marshals as it may deem necessary. The person so appointed shall be approved by the chief of the county fire department and shall be subject to the control and direction of the fire marshal and the chief of the county fire department.
(Ord. No. 175, § 2, 10-7-74)
Sec. 7-6. - Application of fire code in City limits.
Wherever the word "municipality" is used in the fire prevention code, it shall mean the incorporated area of the City of Doraville, Georgia, as well as the unincorporated area of DeKalb County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 175, § 3, 10-7-74)
Sec. 7-7. - Penalties for violation.
Any person who shall violate the provisions of this chapter or fail to comply therewith or who shall violate or fail to comply with any order thereunder or who shall build in violation of any detailed statement, specifications or plans submitted and approved thereunder or who shall build in violation of any certificate or permits issued thereunder and from which no appeal has been taken, or who shall fail to comply with any order as affirmed or modified by the City Clerk or of a court of competent jurisdiction shall be charged in the municipal court for each and every such violation and noncompliance, and upon conviction shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Ord. No. 175, § 4, 10-7-74)
Sec. 7-8. - Adoption of other standards and regulations.
The minimum fire safety standards of the state, and the fire prevention ordinances of DeKalb County, Georgia, together with any and all future amendments or additions thereto, and any and all rules and regulations promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-2-2, are hereby incorporated by reference into this chapter and adopted as the ordinances of the City.
(Ord. No. 222, § I, 9-6-83)
Sec. 7-9. - Extension of county fire services.
DeKalb County, Georgia, and the governing authority thereof is hereby requested and designated to extend the services of its fire prevention bureau to the City. The personnel and representatives of the county fire bureau are hereby authorized to enforce such ordinances within the City limits and are hereby made deputy marshals of the City.
(Ord. No. 222, § II, 9-6-83)
Sec. 7-10. - Adoption of amendments.
This chapter is adopted with the understanding that there may be amendments and changes in both the state minimum fire safety standards, the fire prevention ordinances of the county, and rules and regulations promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-2-2, and those changes shall be incorporated automatically by reference into this chapter upon their passage and shall become part hereof.
(Ord. No. 222, § III, 9-6-83)
Sec. 7-11. - Incorporation.
All amendments, resolutions, and modifications to the minimum fire safety standards of the state and the fire prevention ordinances of the county, and any and all rules and regulations promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-2-2 made since the date of the adoption of this ordinance, September 6, 1983, to date, are hereby incorporated by reference into this chapter.
(Ord. No. 222-1, § I, 11-4-85)
Sec. 7-12. - Noise.
All owners of premises or lessees or tenants thereof of any facility which is operated within the City, shall, in addition to all other obligations and responsibilities, be required to assure that no more than the maximum number of persons authorized by the current certificate of occupancy issued by the DeKalb County Fire Department, DeKalb County, Georgia are permitted on such premises. Appropriate records and methods of counting shall be maintained and made available to representatives of the City or DeKalb County Fire Department. However, at all facilities where more than two thousand (2,000) patrons are anticipated to be in attendance, a representative of the DeKalb County Fire Marshal Office shall be in attendance at the sole expense of the owner, tenant or lessee to assure that the number of patrons in attendance do not exceed those authorized by the then current certificate of occupancy. In addition, all owners of such premises or lessees or tenants thereof shall jointly and severally be responsible for the availability of adequate off-street authorized parking for such persons.
(Ord. No. 96-6, § 1, 4-15-96)
Sec. 7-13. - Carbon monoxide detection.
new
All owners of buildings designated as group R or I occupancies, as defined by the carbon monoxide detection section in the International Building Code (IBC), shall install carbon monoxide alarms as amended by January 1, 2022, and shall execute an affidavit swearing that said installation has occurred as a part of their occupational tax certificate application process for the 2021 calendar year. The City shall provide the affidavit as part of the occupational tax certificate application. Failure to provide said carbon monoxide alarms or falsification of the affidavit shall subject the building owner to a fine in accordance with section 1-12 and/or denial of their occupational tax certificate application.
(Ord. No. 2021-10 , § 2, 2-8-21)
