ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 11-1. - Fines and bonds.
(a)
Bond amounts for offenses shall be in such amounts as shall be established from time to time by the Mayor and Council in consultation with the municipal court clerk, the chief of police, and the municipal court judges, and shall be listed in a bond schedule on file and available for reference and review in the City Clerk's office, the police department and the municipal court clerk's office. The municipal court judge shall have the discretion to set the amount of the bond within the limits provided in the bond schedule. The following fine and bond schedule is adopted until such time as the same is amended or repealed. Other fines for local ordinance violations may be assessed pursuant to duly adopted ordinances and such fines may not necessarily be reflected in this schedule. Adoption of this schedule is not intended to prevent the creation of other fines as may be determined by the Mayor and Council.
(b)
Enhanced penalties.
(1)
At any time after the filing of an indictment or accusation for a violation of Doraville Municipal Code sections 11-7, 11-9, 11-10, 11-160, and/or 11-161, but not later than the arraignment, the City shall notify the defendant of its intention to seek the enhanced penalty or penalties authorized by this section. The notice shall be in writing and shall allege the specific factor or factors authorizing an enhanced sentence in the case.
(2)
Subject to the notice requirement provided in subsection (b)(1) above and in enhancement of the penalty imposed, if the trier of fact determines beyond a reasonable doubt that the defendant accused of violating any of the Code sections listed in subsection (b)(1) above intentionally selected any victim or group of victims or any property as the object of the offense in whole or in part because of the actual or perceived race, color, creed, religion, ancestry, gender, sexual orientation, gender identity, physical or mental disability, or national origin of such victim or group of victims, the judge imposing the sentence shall impose an increased sentence of imprisonment and/or fine up to and including the maximums permitted under Doraville Municipal Code section 1-12 and applicable state law. In addition, or in lieu thereof, the court may also impose community service or participation in an educational program.
(3)
The judge shall state when he or she imposes the sentence the amount of the increase of the sentence based on the application of subsections (b)(1) and (2) of this Code section.
(c)
Fines imposed for concurrent or contemporaneous violation of more than one (1) of the Code sections in subsection (b)(1) above may be assessed an enhanced penalty under this section for each violation.
(d)
As used in this section, "gender identity" means gender-related identity, appearance, or behavior, regardless of whether such gender-related identity, appearance, or behavior is different from that traditionally associated with the person's physiology or assigned sex at birth.
(Code 1969, § 10-1; Ord. No. 2008-28, § 1, 11-3-08; Ord. No. 2020-019 , § 1, 8-10-20)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2008-28, § 1, adopted Nov. 3, 2008, changed the title of § 11-1 from "Cash bond schedule" to "Fines and bonds."

It shall be unlawful for any person to wrongfully, fraudulently or privately take, steal or carry away any article, commodity, merchandise or ware being the property of, or on the premises of and within the custody and control of any merchant duly licensed to do business within the City.

(Ord. No. 180, § III, 12-2-74)
Sec. 11-5. - Posting of name and telephone number of person to contact in emergency.
(a)
Any person owning, conducting or maintaining a place of business, office, shop, store, factory, warehouse, plant, terminal or any other place of whatever nature from which business is conducted, which place is located within the corporate limits of the City, shall, in addition to complying with all other applicable ordinances, rules, laws, statutes and regulations, both state and local, comply with and shall be subject to the terms, rules, regulations and conditions herein contained.
(b)
Every person who conducts, maintains or owns a place of business, office, shop, store, factory, warehouse, plant, terminal or other place or location of whatever nature from which its business is conducted or transacted, which is located within the City, shall cause to be displayed in a conspicuous location, visible and legible from the outside of such place of business, a card containing the name, address and telephone number of a responsible representative of such entity, with authority to act on behalf of such entity, at which telephone number such representative can be reached in the event of any emergency occurring on or about such premises at times and periods when same are not occupied or open for the transaction of business.
(c)
Any person subject to the terms of this section who fails to comply with this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12 of this Code for each such offense, and upon a second or subsequent offense shall be subject to the suspension of his occupation tax certificate as issued by the City for a period not to exceed thirty (30) days, pursuant to the provisions of chapter 6, article IX and section 1-15 of this Code.

Sec. 11-9. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
Prohibited acts enumerated. It shall be unlawful for any person within the City to violate any of the following:
(1)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby any person is placed in fear of safety of his life, limb or health.
(2)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged.
(3)
Endanger the lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence, angry threats and abusive conduct.
(4)
Cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another.
(5)
Assemble or congregate with another or others for the purpose of causing, provoking or engaging in any fight or brawl.
(6)
Be found jostling or roughly crowding or pushing any person in any public place.
(7)
Collect in bodies or in crowds for unlawful purposes as defined by the current ordinances of the City.
(8)
Assemble or congregate with another or others for the purpose or with the intent to engage in gaming.
(9)
Frequent any public place with intent to obtain money from other persons by illegal and fraudulent schemes, tricks, artifices or devices.
(10)
Assemble for the purpose of engaging in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person in the City or aid or abet therein.

(12)
Utter while in a state of anger in the presence of another any bawdy, lewd or obscene words or epithets.
(13)
Frequent any place where gaming or illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed or tolerated.
(14)
Act in a dangerous manner toward others.
(15)
Use "fighting words" directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates a turmoil.
(16)
Assemble or congregate for the purpose of trouncing upon another.
(17)
By acts of violence, interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation.
(18)
Congregate with another or others in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuse to clear such public way when ordered by the City police or other lawful authority.

Possess outside a persons home or place of business any firearm, knife, bludgeon, metal knuckles, explosive compound, weapon or any other device or instrument of like character designed for the purpose of offense or defense unless otherwise permitted by state or federal law.

Designation of offense. Any person who shall violate any subsection of this section may be charged with the offense of "disorderly conduct," and on conviction thereof, may be found guilty of disorderly conduct.

Sec. 11-12. - Glue, other adhesives; ownership, possession, purchase, sale.
(a)
Prohibited; exceptions. It shall be unlawful for any person to own, possess, buy or sell more than one (1) unit container of cement, glue or other adhesive material containing any of the following ingredients: acetone, amyl acetate, butyl acetate, ethyl acetate, benzene butyl alcohol, hexane, isopropyl acetate, isopropyl alcohol, toluene and zylene, except under the following conditions:
(1)
The possession is held by a person licensed to engage in the business of assembling those products requiring such adhesive and it is not for resale or disposal to others in any manner.
(2)
The possession, if by an individual, shall be in the original container and for his own use in a hobby or craft, but only one (1) container shall be open at any time and no more than one (1) replacement shall be in possession.
(3)
The possession by any organized club, group or other organization shall be under the strict supervision of an adult person who shall be personally responsible for the use of such adhesives.
(4)
Possession by any of the above persons shall be maintained in locked containers at all times except when access thereto is necessary for its use.
(b)
Violations by minors. It shall also be unlawful for any person under the age of eighteen (18) years to purchase any cement, glue or other adhesive material containing any of the following ingredients: acetone, amyl acetate, butyl acetate, ethyl acetate, benzene butyl alcohol, hexane, isopropyl acetate, isopropyl alcohol, toluene and zylene.
(c)
Violations and penalties. Any persons apprehended who is under the influence of the vapor fumes from any of the ingredients of the cement, glue or other adhesives covered by this section, or found in possession of such adhesives in other than the original container, or who shall be apprehended in the process of the preparation to, or in the act of inhaling the vapor fumes from such adhesives or its ingredients, shall be guilty of a violation and shall be punished as provided in section 1-12 of this Code.
(d)
Violations by licensees. Any person licensed to possess such cements, glues and other adhesives for their own use or resale who violates any of the provisions of this section shall, if found guilty by a court of competent jurisdiction, be punished as the law provides, and his business license shall be subject to revocation by the City Manager or designee.
(Code 1969, § 10-16.1; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 3, 6-17-13)
State Law reference— Sale and possession of model glue, O.C.G.A. § 16-13-90 et seq.

The possession, if by an individual, shall be in the original container and for the individual's own use or hobby or craft.

Possession by any of the above persons shall be maintained in locked containers except when access thereto is necessary for use.

This subsection shall not apply to dealers or businesses which sell, in the ordinary course of business, model glue and paint as defined herein. However, all the other provisions of this section set forth in paragraphs (a) through (d) shall apply to said dealers.

Any person violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12 of this Code.

A violation of this section by any such licensee may be the bases for revocation of any business license issued by the City.

Sec. 11-13. - Idling, loitering, loafing; noise.
(a)
Prohibited; exceptions. It shall be disorderly conduct and unlawful for any person, except the duly employed and authorized employees and agents of the City, or of its commissions or agencies, in the regular and necessary conduct and management of their employment by same, to be upon or to appear within any of the public parks, public areas and public buildings, owned or controlled by the City, or by any of its subsidiary agencies, commissions or boards in an idling or loitering manner between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m., except and unless such person has a right to be upon and within such designated public places by virtue of a lawful admission, invitation or ticket to some activity or entertainment as authorized and being carried on by the City, or by such subsidiary agency, commission or board of the City, as may have control over such public park, area, grounds, and buildings. This subsection does not cover or apply to public streets outside such designated places.
(b)
Aiding, abetting as idler, loiter. It shall be disorderly conduct and unlawful for any person to go to, congregate at, or assemble in or upon the public places, public streets and public sidewalks of the City to aid, induce, encourage or abet, as an idler or loiterer, or as an obviously interested spectator, or otherwise, any person in the violation of any of this Code or of any other ordinances of the City, or of the laws of the state.
(c)
Presumption of aiding, abetting. If a person should see, hear or otherwise know or understand that a violation of such ordinance or such law is being committed or violated, or has been violated, or is planned to be violated, and should fail or refuse to use ordinary means to disassociate himself from such place or such violation, or to notify the City police officers of such violation, when it should appear to the ordinary person that the officers should be notified thereof, such person, presumably, aids or abets the violator, and upon conviction, shall be equally guilty with such actual violator of the ordinance or law.
(d)
Noise. It shall be unlawful for any person to make, continue or cause to be made or continued any loud, unnecessary or unusual noise or any noise which either annoys, disturbs, injures or endangers the comfort, repose, health, peace or safety of others in the City.
(Code 1969, § 10-17)

Sec. 11-16. - Vagrancy.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to congregate or appear in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for law abiding individuals under circumstances that warrant justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity, or that a criminal act may be occurring. Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether alarm is warranted, is the fact that the person or persons take flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer, refusal to identify oneself, manifest attempts to conceal the individual or any object or property. Other circumstances which may be considered are a refusal to disperse when a group of more than two (2) persons are blocking public or private sidewalks or ways, obstructing or interfering with the ingress or egress to businesses, shopping centers, or other private ways, or other obstruction of the orderly flow of traffic, pedestrian, vehicular, or otherwise. Unless flight by the person or persons or other circumstances make it impractical, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest under this section, afford the person a reasonable opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern which would otherwise be warranted by requesting the person to identify himself and explain his presence and conduct. No person shall be convicted of an offense under this section if the law enforcement officer fails to comply with the foregoing procedure or if it appears at trial that the explanation given by the person was true and would have dispelled the alarm or immediate concern.
(Code 1969, § 10-25; Ord. No. 00-2, § 1, 1-3-00)
Sec. 11-16.1. - Urban camping and improper use of public places.
(a)
City parks. It shall be unlawful to sleep, lie down, to reside or to store personal property in any park owned by the City.
(b)
Sidewalks. It shall be unlawful to sleep, to reside, to store personal property, or to sit or lie down on any sidewalk.
(c)
Other public property—Blocking ingress and egress. It shall be unlawful to sleep, to reside, to store personal property, or to sit or lie down on any public property so as to interfere with the ingress or egress from buildings.
(d)
Same—Urban camping. It shall be unlawful to use any public place, including City parks and sidewalks, for living accommodations purposes or camping, except in areas specifically designed for such use or specifically authorized by permit.
(e)
Private property—Urban camping without owner's permission. It shall be unlawful for anyone other than the owner, a leaseholder, an employee, or other rightful occupant to camp, sleep, reside, store personal property, or lie down, outside on any private property without the owner's or leaseholder's permission.
(f)
Exceptions. Notwithstanding anything in this Code section to the contrary, this Code section shall not apply to nor be construed to prohibit the following behavior:
(1)
Persons sitting or lying down as a result of a medical emergency;
(2)
Persons sitting in wheelchairs while using parks, sidewalks, or other public places;
(3)
Persons sitting down while attending parades for which valid permits have been issued;
(4)
Persons sitting down while patronizing outdoor cafes;
(5)
Persons sitting down, lying down or napping while attending performances, festivals and concerts taking place in any park, street or sidewalk closed by permit for such purpose;
(6)
Persons sitting on chairs or benches supplied by a public agency or abutting private property owner;
(7)
Persons sitting on seats in bus zones occupied by people waiting for the bus;
(8)
Persons sitting or lying down while waiting in an orderly line outside a box office to purchase tickets to any sporting event, concert, performance, or other special event; or
(9)
Children under the age of thirteen (13) years sleeping in parks, or sleeping anywhere else while being carried by an accompanying person or while sitting or lying in a stroller or baby carriage.
(g)
Unless flight or an immediate threat to the safety of any person makes it impractical, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest for a violation of this section, afford the person a reasonable opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern which would otherwise be warranted by requesting the person to identify himself and explain his presence and conduct. No person may be arrested for violating this Code section 11-16.1 until he or she has received an oral or written warning from the law enforcement official to cease the unlawful conduct. If the violator fails to comply with the warning issued within five (5) minutes, then he or she may be subject to arrest for urban camping.
(Ord. No. 98-14, § 1, 9-8-98; Ord. No. 00-1, § 1, 1-3-00)

It shall be unlawful for any person to play and bet for money or other thing of value at any game played with cards, dice or balls or to play and bet for money or other thing of value at any table of whatever name, kind or description, for gaming, or to bet at any game of ninepins or any other number of pins, or at any billiard or pool table.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any gambling device to be operated, maintained or played on any place of business within the City. For purposes of this subsection, "gambling device" shall refer to any contrivance which for a consideration affords the player an opportunity to obtain money, or other thing of value, the award of which is determined by chance, even though accompanied by some skill, and whether or not the prize is automatically paid by the contrivance.
(Code 1969, § 10-16; Ord. No. 169, § I, 2-18-74)
State Law reference— Gambling and related offenses, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20 et seq.
Sec. 11-19. - Marijuana possession.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or have under his control within the City one (1) ounce or less of marijuana.
(b)
For purposes of this section the term "marijuana" shall mean all parts of the plant of the genus cannabis, whether growing or not, the seeds thereof, the resin extracted from any part of such plant, and every compound, manufacture, salt, derivative, mixture or preparation of such plant, its seeds or resin; and shall not include the mature stalks of such plant, fiber produced from such stalks, oil or cake, or the completely sterilized samples of seeds of the plant which are incapable of germination.
(c)
Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be punished by:
(1)
Minor—Under the age of eighteen (18): First offense: seventy-five-dollar ($75.00) fine, court date required with parent or guardian present. Second offenses within three hundred sixty-five (365) days of original offense, one hundred fifty-dollar ($150.00) fine. Third offense within three hundred sixty-five (365) days, three hundred-dollar ($300.00) fine. Any additional offenses shall be punished as provided in section 1-12 of this Code. The offender may pay said fines) by performing community service, per the court's reasonable discretion.
(2)
Adult—Over the age of eighteen (18): First offense: seventy-five-dollar ($75.00) fine, payable online, no court date required. Second within three hundred sixty-five (365) days of original offense, one hundred fifty-dollar ($150.00) fine. Third offense within three hundred sixty-five (365) days, three hundred-dollar ($300.00) fine. Additional offenses shall be punished as provided in section 1-12 of this Code. The offender may pay said fine(s) by performing community service, per the court's reasonable discretion.
(3)
The civil fines in this subsection are not available if the circumstances of possession involve consumption of marijuana in a public place, which includes a vehicle, or any place outside the confines of a private residence. If a person is found guilty of using marijuana while in possession, such person shall be subject to all available punishment under section 1-12 of the City Code.
(Ord. No. 208, §§ I—III, 2-16-81; Ord. No. 2020-020 , § 1, 8-10-20)
State Law reference— Unlawful to possess or distribute controlled substance, O.C.G.A. § 16-13-30; jurisdiction of municipal courts in marijuana possession cases, O.C.G.A. § 36-32-6; use of marijuana for treatment of cancer and glaucoma, O.C.G.A. § 43-34-120 et seq.
Sec. 11-20. - Open container is prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess an open container of any alcoholic beverage upon the public streets or highways of the City. It shall also be unlawful for any person to possess an open container of any alcoholic beverage upon private property which is open to the public except those facilities having a license from the City to sell alcoholic beverages or at portions of such facility which may be outside but adjacent to such facility.
(b)
Open container means any container which is immediately capable of being consumed from or the seal of which has been broken.
(Ord. No. 92-9, § 1, 11-2-92)
Sec. 11-20.1. - Exception for special events where the sale of alcohol has been authorized.
Notwithstanding the provisions contained in section 11-20, concerning consumption and possession of alcoholic beverages in public streets, highways, and private property open to the public, there shall be an exception within the bounded area of a permitted special event where the sale of alcohol is authorized pursuant [to section] 3-45(a). Except for the provisions concerning consumption and possession of alcohol as set forth in this exception, any such sale, consumption, and/or possession of alcoholic beverages in the City shall be in compliance with the provisions of chapter 3, "alcoholic beverages," of this Code of Ordinances and the statutes and regulations of the State of Georgia with respect to sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 2018-017 , § 4, 10-15-18)
Sec. 11-21. - Smoking and drinking prohibited on MARTA buses, trains, and rail cars and paid areas; prohibiting evasion of payment of fare for transportation service.
(a)
Smoking and drinking prohibited on MARTA buses, trains, rail cars and paid areas. It shall be unlawful for occupants of Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority (MARTA) buses, trains or rail cars to smoke tobacco or consume alcoholic beverages while such buses, trains or rail cars are transporting passengers within the corporate limits of the City or within paid areas adjacent thereto. Charter bus service is excluded from these restrictions.
(b)
Evading payment of fare for transportation service. It shall be unlawful, within the corporate limits of the City, for any person to evade or attempt to evade payment of the fare for transportation service upon the Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority rapid rail system or public transit bus. As used in this section, "evasion of payment of fare" shall include the following acts, activities or conduct:
(1)
Entry into a paid area or upon a rapid rail car of public transit bus without payment of the fare charged for transportation service;
(2)
Misuse of a transfer, TransCard, pass, ticket, token, or other evidence of payment of or prepayment of a fare issued by the Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority ("MARTA") with the intent to avoid or to assist another in avoiding payment of the fare charged for transportation service;
(3)
Use or attempted use of a signed MARTA TransCard for the payment of the fare charged for transportation service by a person who is not the person whose signature appears on the TransCard.
(Ord. No. 92-10, § 1, 12-7-92)
Sec. 11-22. - Sale or possession of goods containing or using the unauthorized name or seal of other persons, firms, corporations or associations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any firm, person, corporation, or association to use the name, mark or design which is identical to, substantially indistinguishable from, or an imitation of a trademark, service mark, or copyrighted or registered design which is registered for those types of goods or services of any other person, firm, corporation or association incidental to the sale, display or use of goods which has not been authorized by the lawful owner thereof, for the purpose of deceiving the public, who knows, or should have known, that such use is unauthorized.
(b)
Upon conviction of the violation set out in [subsection] (a) above, the City of Doraville Occupation License of the business shall be revoked.
(c)
Any violation of this section shall be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-12 of the Municipal Code of the City of Doraville, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 94-10, § 1, 8-1-94; Ord. No. 08-06, § 1, 5-5-08)
Sec. 11-23. - Certain acts prohibited in transit.
(a)
A person who commits or attempts to commit any of the following acts in a public transit bus, a rapid rail car, or a rapid rail station or intermodal bus station shall be guilty of an offense:
(1)
Spits, defecates, or urinates;
(2)
Discards litter, except into receptacles designated for that purpose;
(3)
Smokes tobacco in any form;
(4)
Consumes food or beverage or possesses any open food or beverage container, provided that this paragraph shall not apply to an operator of a public transit bus at an authorized layover point or to a person providing food or beverage to any child under age five (5);
(5)
Plays any radio; cassette, cartridge, or tape player; or similar device unless such device is connected to an earphone that limits the sound to the hearing of the individual user;
(6)
Carries or possesses any explosives, acids, other dangerous articles, or live animal, except for the following:
a.
A guide dog or service dog as described in O.C.G.A. § 30-4-1, provided that such guide dog or service dog is accompanied by a physically disabled person, blind person, person with visual disabilities, deaf person, or a person who is responsible for training a guide dog or service dog; and
b.
Small pets confined to rigid pet carriers with locks or latches;
(7)
Obstructs, hinders, interferes with, or otherwise disrupts or disturbs the operation, operator, or passengers of a public transit bus or rapid rail car; pushes other persons, uses loud or profane language, or otherwise disturbs any other person on such public transit facilities, or
(8)
Boards any public transit bus through the rear exit door, unless so directed by an employee or agent of the carrier;
(9)
Remains aboard any public transit bus or rapid rail car after such vehicle has completed its scheduled route and passengers have been advised to exit the vehicle or remains aboard any public transit bus or rapid rail car after been warned and after such vehicle has entered a garage or other restricted area not open to the public;
(10)
Enters, exits, or passes through any emergency door of any rapid rail car or public transit bus in the absence of a bona fide emergency; or
(11)
Enters the operator's cab or driver's seat of any rapid rail car or public transit bus in the absence of a bona fide emergency.
(b)
Employees of a public transportation authority or carrier while at work performing the duties of their employment shall be exempted from the restriction of paragraphs (8), (9), (10), and (11) of subsection (a).
(c)
A person convicted of a first offense of violating subsection (a) or this Code section, or any portion thereof, shall be punished by a fine of not less than fifty dollars ($50.00) and not more than one hundred dollars ($100.00) for the first offense and a jail sentence of not more than ten (10) days. Upon a second or subsequent conviction, a person shall be punished by a fine or not less than one hundred dollars ($100.00) and not more than two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) or by imprisonment for not more than thirty (30) days, or both.
(Ord. No. 98-15, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 11-24. - Fare evasion.
A person who commits or attempts or commit any of the following acts shall be guilty of an offense:
(1)
Sells, makes, or possesses any coin, note, token, transfer, transaction card, or similar article which has been altered from its original condition contrary to its intended use to enter or gain entry into or on any bus, rail vehicle, or station;
(2)
Sells or exchanges any token, transfer, transaction card, ticket, fare medium, or similar article used or to be used as payment for entry into or on any bus, rail vehicle, or terminal without the express consent of the public transit agency owning or operating such vehicles or stations;
(3)
Offers entry or provides entry into or on any bus, rapid rail car, or station to any person without the payment of the proper fare to the public transit agency owning or operating such vehicles or stations;
(4)
Gains entry into or on any bus, rapid rail car, or station without payment of the proper fare to the public transit agency owning or operating such vehicles or stations; or
(5)
Gains entry into or on any bus, rapid rail car, or station through the use of a transcard, transaction card, or other similar fare media which is the property of another person.
(Ord. No. 98-16, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 11-25. - Parking on public transit property.
(a)
In all parking lots, parking decks, and other such facilities owned or operated by any public transit authority or established for the exclusive purpose of providing parking for passengers of rapid rail or public transit buses, it shall be unlawful to:
(1)
Stop, stand, or park a vehicle other than in marked spaces designed for that purpose;
(2)
Stop, stand, or park a vehicle on any yellow curb;
(3)
Stop, stand, or park a vehicle in any location which results in impeding ingress and egress to said facility or which results in impeding the free passage of any other vehicle;
(4)
Leave any vehicle unattended in areas designated as "kiss-ride" or designated as "attended vehicles only";
(5)
Stop, stand, or park any taxicab or other vehicle for hire, whether attended or unattended, in any area not specifically designated for such vehicles;
(6)
Stop, stand, or park any vehicle for the purpose of loading or unloading passengers, except in areas specifically designated for that use, such as "kiss-ride" or "passenger drop-off" areas;
(7)
Stop, stand, or park any vehicle for a period in excess of twenty-four (24) hours unless such parking area is designated "overnight" or "long-term" parking;
(8)
Stop, stand, or park any vehicle for any purpose other than to board the rapid rail car or public transit bus serving such parking lot; or
(9)
Create or maintain any fire or flame, including fires or flames for cooking or grilling, or to fail to extinguish said fire or flame and to remove from such public transit parking facility all debris and residue associated with the creation and maintenance of said fire or flame.
(b)
Any person violating subsection (a) of this Code section shall be subject to a fine of not less than twenty-five dollars ($25.00) and not more than two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00). Such offense may be cited by the issuance of a written citation which shall be left on the violator's vehicle and which shall contain, at a minimum:
(1)
The nature of the violation;
(2)
The amount of the fine which will be levied for such violation;
(3)
That the cited individual has the right to contest the citation and be given an opportunity to be heard;
(4)
The location of the court in which the cited individual must appear to contest same; and
(5)
The location at which fines may be paid; and
(c)
The operator of said vehicle shall also be subject to citation by personal delivery of said citation.
(d)
Nothing in this section shall be construed to limit the enforcement of any other provision of state or municipal law which may be applicable.
(Ord. No. 98-17, § 1, 9-8-98)
