DIVISION 2. - TAX COMMISSIONER[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— County tax officials and administration, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-100 et seq .

Sec. 2-121. - Office created.
There is hereby created the office of tax commissioner of Rockdale County, with the same rights, duties and liabilities as were heretofore incumbent on the tax collector, and tax receiver of Rockdale County. All general laws now governing the office of tax receiver and tax collector shall govern the tax commissioner insofar as the same are applicable, and except as hereinafter provided.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 1)
Editor's note— The county tax collector's and tax receiver's offices were created by 1870 Ga. Laws, page 16 and abolished by 1943 Ga. Laws, pages 1106 and 1109, respectively.
Sec. 2-122. - Fees, commissions and fines.
(a)
All fees, commissions, costs, fines, emoluments and perquisites of whatever kind received and collected by the tax commissioner shall be received, collected and held by him as public funds belonging to Rockdale County. Once each month the tax commissioner shall turn over to the fiscal authority of said county all funds collected by him with a detailed itemized statement showing the sources from which such funds were collected. It is specifically provided that the salary provided herein for the tax commissioner shall be in lieu of all fees, commissions, costs, fines, emoluments and perquisites of whatever kind, including those commissions allowed by an act approved March 9, 1955 (1955 Ga. Laws, page 659), as amended [ O.C.G.A. § 40-2-33 ], relating to the sale of motor vehicle license plates by local tax officials, notwithstanding the fact that such services for which the commissions are derived may have been performed in the capacity of an agent for the state revenue department and an act relating to the commission on taxes collected in excess of a certain percentage of the taxes due according to the tax net digest, approved January 17, 1938.
(b)
The tax commissioner is hereby authorized and directed to remit to the board of education of Rockdale County once each month the proceeds from all education taxes collected by him; except one (1) percent of the education taxes collected shall be retained and turned over, to the, fiscal, authority of Rockdale County, as provides by subsection (a) hereof, as reimbursement to the county for the cost of collecting such education taxes.
(1937-38 Ga. Laws, Ex. Sess., page 297; 1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 3; 1967 Ga. Laws, page 3149; 1978 Ga. Laws, page 3832)
State Law reference— Rate of commissions, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-180 .
Sec. 2-123. - Salary; deputies.
The tax commissioner of Rockdale County shall receive an annual salary equal to 56 percent of the base salary of a superior court judge as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 45-7-4 , as now or hereafter amended. The base salary of a superior court judge shall mean that salary paid from state funds and shall not include any county supplement or expense reimbursements. The salary shall be payable in equal monthly installments from the funds of Rockdale County. The tax commissioner shall be authorized to appoint a chief deputy and four full-time deputies to assist him in the discharge of his official duties. The tax commissioner shall also be authorized to participate in the group retirement program and the group hospitalization benefit program on the same basis as other officers and employees of Rockdale County if he meets the normal eligibility requirements of the programs.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 4; 1977 Ga. Laws, page 2837; 1979 Ga. Laws, page 3168; 1980 Ga. Laws, page 3492, § 1; 1990 Ga. Laws (Act No. 865), page 4183, § 1)
State Law reference— Salary, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-183 .
Sec. 2-124. - Salaries of deputies of tax commissioners fixed for counties of 18,100 to 18,250 in population (general law).
In all counties of this state having a population of not less than 18,100 and not more than 18,250 according to the United States Decennial Census of 1970 or any future such census, the deputies of the tax commissioner shall each receive a salary [as provided by law], payable from the funds of such counties. Beginning January 1, 1976, such deputies shall each receive a salary of not more than $600.00 per month, payable from the funds of such counties.
(1975 Ga. Laws, page 2912)
Sec. 2-125. - Office location.
That said tax commissioner shall have his office in the courthouse of said county at Conyers, Georgia, and he shall not be required to make any rounds to receive tax returns or tax payments.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 5)
Sec. 2-126. - Term of office.
The term of office of such commissioner shall be for a period of four (4) years, and the successor of Mrs. Fannie S. Freeman is to be elected in the same manner and time as the Ordinary of Rockdale County [judge of the probate court] is now elected. Her successor to be named in the general election of 1948.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 7)
Sec. 2-127. - Vacancy.
That in the event said office becomes vacant by death or otherwise, the said vacancy to be filled for any unexpired term by appointment of the governor.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 8)
Sec. 2-128. - Commissioning.
That said commissioner shall be commissioned as the clerk of superior courts are [sic].
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 9)
Sec. 2-129. - Oath.
That before entering upon the duties of his office, said tax commissioner shall take the oath now prescribed by law for the tax collector and tax receiver, and shall at the same time give bond and security as prescribed by section 92-4801 of the Code of Georgia of 1933 [now O.C.G.A. § 48-5-122 ]. Said bond premium to be paid by the Commissioner of Roads and Revenues [Board of Commissioners] of Rockdale County from county funds.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 10)
Sec. 2-130. - Tax to pay salary.
That the Commissioner of Roads and Revenue [Board of Commissioners] of Rockdale County is hereby authorized and empowered to levy and collect a tax on all taxable property in Rockdale County, sufficient to pay the salary of said commissioner and his expenses as herein provided.
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 12)
Sec. 2-131. - Equipment and supplies.
The Commissioner of Roads and Revenue [Board of Commissioners] of Rockdale County shall furnish the necessary equipment and supplies for said office [of the tax commissioner].
(1943 Ga. Laws, page 1106, § 13)
Secs. 2-132—2-150. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - SHERIFF[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Sheriffs generally, O.C.G.A. § 15-16-1 et seq. ; sheriffs authorized to contract with municipalities for law enforcement, O.C.G.A. § 15-16-13 ; jails, O.C.G.A. § 42-4-1 et seq .

Sec. 2-151. - Sheriff on salary.
The present mode of compensating the sheriff of Rockdale County, known as the fee system, is hereby abolished; and in lieu thereof an annual salary for such officer is prescribed as hereinafter provided.
(1966 Ga. Laws, page 2039, § 1)
Sec. 2-152. - Compensation, sheriff.
The sheriff shall receive an annual salary equal to 62.5 percent of the base salary of a superior court judge as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 45-7-4 , as now or hereafter amended. The base salary of a superior court judge shall mean that salary paid from state funds and shall not include any county supplement or expense reimbursements. The salary shall be payable in equal monthly installments from the funds of Rockdale County. The sheriff shall also be authorized to participate in the group retirement program and the group hospitalization benefit program on the same basis as other officers and employees of Rockdale County if he meets the normal eligibility requirements of the programs.
(1966 Ga. Laws, page 2039, § 2; 1977 Ga. Laws, page 2848, § 1; 1979 Ga. Laws, page 3302; 1980 Ga. Laws, page 3494, § 1; 1990 Ga. Laws (Act No. 863), page 4179, § 1)
Sec. 2-153. - Collection of fees, fines and the like.
Said officer shall diligently and faithfully undertake to collect all fees, fines, forfeitures, commissions, costs, allowances, penalties, funds, monies and all other emoluments and perquisites formerly allowed him as compensation for services in any capacity, and shall receive and hold the same in trust for said county as public monies and shall pay the same into the county treasury on or before the fifteenth day of each month next following the month in which they were collected or received. At the time of each such monthly payment into the county treasury, the sheriff shall furnish the governing authority of the county a detailed, itemized statement, under oath, of all such funds received during the preceding month by such officer and paid into the county treasury. The statement shall show the respective amounts of money collected and the source thereof.
(1966 Ga. Laws, page 2039, § 3)
Sec. 2-154. - Personnel.
The sheriff shall have the authority to appoint such deputies, clerks, investigators and other employees as he shall deem necessary to discharge the official duties of his office efficiently and effectively; and in no event shall the sheriff appoint fewer deputies, clerks, investigators, and other employees than that number so employed on January 1, 1985. He shall, from time to time, recommend to the governing authority of said county the number of such personnel needed by his office, together with the suggested compensation to be paid each employee. However, it shall be within the sole discretion of the governing authority of said county to fix the compensation to be received by each employee in said office. It shall be within the sole power and authority of the sheriff, during his term of office, to designate and name the person or persons who shall be employed as such deputies, clerks, assistants or other employees, and to prescribe their duties and assignments, and to remove or replace any of such employees at will and within his sole discretion.
(1966 Ga. Laws, page 2039, § 4; 1977 Ga. Laws, page 2848, § 2; 1985 Ga. Laws, page 4001, § 1)
Sec. 2-155. - Operating expenses.
The necessary operating expenses of the sheriff's office, expressly including the compensation of all personnel and employees, shall be paid from any funds of the county available for such purpose. All supplies, materials, furnishings, furniture, utilities, uniforms, vehicles and equipment, and the repair, replacement and maintenance thereof, as may be reasonably required in discharging the official duties of said office, shall be furnished by the county and shall be paid from any funds of the county available for such purpose. The determination of such requirements shall be at the sole discretion of the governing authority of Rockdale County.
(1966 Ga. Laws, page 2039, § 6)
Sec. 2-156. - Administrative and technology fees.
Pursuant to the authority granted by the State of Georgia as outlined in O.C.G.A. §§ 15-21-93 and 15-21-94, the following fees shall be imposed on all citations upon which the accused is either convicted or enters a plea which resolves the citation again the interest of the accused:
(a)
An administrative fee of $3.00 per qualifying citation;
(b)
A technology fee of $5.00 per qualifying citation.
(Ord. No. 0-2020-19 , § 1, 12-8-20)
Secs. 2-157—2-170. - Reserved.

Sec. 2-171. - Coroner on salary.

The coroner shall receive an annual salary equal to 10.5 percent of the base salary of a superior court judge as set forth in Code O.C.G.A. § 45-7-4 , as now or hereafter amended. (1967 Ga. Laws, page 2505, § 2; 1977 Ga. Laws, page 2839; 1979 Ga. Laws, page 3159; 1980 Ga. Laws, page 3490, § 1; 1990 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1016), page 4724, § 1)

The statement shall show the respective amounts of money collected and the source thereof.
(1967 Ga. Laws, page 2505, § 3)
Sec. 2-174. - Operating expenses.

All supplies, material, furnishings, furniture, utilities and equipment, and the repair, replacement and maintenance thereof, as may be reasonably required in discharging the official duties of said office, shall be furnished by the county and shall be paid from any funds of the county available for such purpose.
