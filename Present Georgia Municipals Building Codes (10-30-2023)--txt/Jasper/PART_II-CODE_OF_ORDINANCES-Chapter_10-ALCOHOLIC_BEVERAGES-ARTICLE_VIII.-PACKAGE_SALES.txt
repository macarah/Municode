DIVISION 2. - MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Local licensing of the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-42; local licensing of the sale of wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-10-40.

Sec. 10-253. - License fees.
The license fee for a license to sell malt beverages or wine by the package shall be paid to the city manager in the amount established by the city council by certified or cashier's check at the time of the filing of the original application or any renewal thereof.
(Code 1979, § 37-2.1(B))
State Law reference— Maximum fees for municipal alcoholic beverage licenses, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-50.
Sec. 10-254. - Type of retail outlet.
Beer and wine may be sold at retail, by the package, only in outlets duly licensed to sell beer or wine by the package or outlets maintaining 75 percent of the floor space and storage area in a manner which is devoted principally to the retail sale of other foods and groceries.
(Code 1979, § 37-2.4)
Sec. 10-255. - Hours of sale.
Retail package outlets shall engage in the sale of beer or wine only between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m. Monday through Saturday. No package beer or wine may be sold on Sunday.
(Code 1979, § 37-2.5)
State Law reference— Sunday sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale on Sunday and election days, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 10-256. - Consumption on premises prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to consume any alcoholic beverage on the premises licensed for the sale of malt beverages or wine by the package and it shall be unlawful for any licensee by the package to open for, or break the package for, a purchaser or permit the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises.
(Code 1979, § 37-2.6)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26.

DIVISION 3. - DISTILLED SPIRITS[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Local licensing of the sale of distilled spirits, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-40 et seq.

Sec. 10-276. - License fees.
A license fee for the sale of distilled spirits in the original package in the amount established by the city council shall be paid by certified or cashier's check to the city manager by the licensee at the time of the application for the license or renewal thereof.
(Code 1979, § 37-3.1)
State Law reference— Maximum fees for municipal alcoholic beverage licenses, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-50.
Sec. 10-277. - Type of retail outlet.
(a)
Distilled spirits by the package may be sold at retail, only in outlets duly licensed to sell distilled spirits by the package and outlets which are devoted exclusively to the retail sale of distilled spirits, beer and wine by the package with ingress and egress provided directly to and only to the exterior of the building in which the facility is located and not to any other enclosed part of the building or adjoining building, except in hotels, motels, and high-rise office buildings where every public entrance to this outlet shall be from a lobby, hallway, or other interior portion of the primary use structure.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit the retail sale within these outlets of liquid commodities and mixes normally used in the preparation and serving of distilled spirits. It is the intention of this section to allow the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package only in outlets devoted exclusively to the sale of distilled spirits, beer and wine and to prohibit the sales in outlets that sell groceries, food, gasoline and other such commodities.
(Code 1979, § 37-3.3)
Sec. 10-278. - Hours of sale.
The sale of distilled spirits by the package shall occur only between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m. Monday through Saturday. No sale of distilled spirits by the package shall occur on Sunday.
(Code 1979, § 37-3.4)
State Law reference— Sunday sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale on Sunday and election days, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 10-279. - On-premises consumption prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to consume any alcoholic beverage on premises licensed for the sale of distilled spirits by the package and it shall be unlawful for any licensee authorized to sell distilled spirits by the package to open for, or break the package for a purchaser, or permit the consumption of alcoholic beverages on premises.
(Code 1979, § 37-3.5)
Sec. 10-280. - Listing of prices.
The licensee shall indicate plainly by tag or labels on the bottles or containers, or on the shelf immediately below where the bottles or containers are placed, the prices of all distilled spirits exposed for sale. The licensee shall not display prices or brand names in such a way as to be visible from the outside of the establishment.
(Code 1979, § 37-3.6)
State Law reference— Advertisement by alcoholic beverage retailers, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-3; advertisement of distilled spirits, O.C.G.A. § 3-2-15; advertisement of wine prices, O.C.G.A. § 3-10-25.1.
