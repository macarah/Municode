DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-123. - Definitions.
Unless the context demands otherwise, the words and terms used in this article shall have the same definition and meaning as state law where used in O.C.G.A. title 3 (O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.), known as the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, as now or hereafter amended. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business location and location of the business mean the location or proposed location of a retail or wholesale liquor outlet.
Distillery means a place where distilled spirits are manufactured.
Liquor means spirituous liquor or distilled spirits.
Measured means measured by way of the nearest travelled road, street, or highway from the front entrance of an outlet to the nearest travelled road, street, or highway, to the front entrance of an involved institution.
Outlet means a store for the sale of liquor.
Outlet manager means the person who does or will actually manage and operate the liquor store on a day-to-day basis.
Private residence means a dwelling for one or more persons or families.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-136))
Sec. 4-124. - General regulations.
(a)
The building or proposed building to house a retail outlet shall meet all requirements of the building inspector, and plans, specifications, and/or photographs for the building or proposed building must be approved by city council.
(b)
No advertising sign on the exterior of the retail outlet shall be permitted.
(c)
Each retail outlet shall post the following signs and notices conspicuously within the place of business:
(1)
A sign printed in letters at least two inches high, reading as follows: "Sale of liquor to persons under 21 years of age strictly prohibited."
(2)
One printed copy of the penal sections of this chapter.
(d)
No screen, partition or thing which prevents a clear view into the interior of a retail outlet from the street, nor any booth within, shall be permitted.
(e)
No sale of liquor shall be allowed except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 1:30 a.m. of the following day on weekdays, except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on Saturday, and except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:00 p.m. on Sunday, nor on other days prohibited by state law.
(f)
No advertising of prices outside a retail outlet in any manner whatsoever, including signs on the outlet or outlet window, shall be permitted.
(g)
No drive-in windows shall be permitted.
(h)
The state regulations relating to the sale and distribution of distilled spirits, as revised, promulgated by the state department of revenue, are hereby incorporated into and made a part of this article as if fully set out in this section.
(i)
The applicant or license holder shall make an immediate report to, and receive approval by, the city council of any change in the interests in or ownership of the liquor outlet or liquor business and/or any change in the information stated in the original application for license.
(j)
The open parking area of any building or proposed building to house a retail outlet shall have an average maintained foot-candle intensity of at least one foot-candle with a minimum allowable intensity of three-tenths of a foot-candle. The covered parking area of any building or proposed building to house a retail outlet shall have an average maintained foot-candle intensity of at least two foot-candles with a minimum allowable intensity of five-tenths of a foot-candle.
(k)
Each outlet for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package shall install and maintain security cameras in the outlet of a type and number approved by the chief of police. Such cameras shall be:
(1)
Placed in the outlet to record activities in the check out or cash register area.
(2)
Capable of producing a retrievable image on film or tape that can be made a permanent record and enlarged through projection or other means.
Such cameras shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to periodic inspection by the chief of police or his designee. If a robbery or other felony occurs in such outlet, the film or tape recording such event shall immediately be made available to the chief of police or his designee. Provided, however, any outlet shall be exempt from the provisions of this subsection if it has two or more employees on duty and on the premises at all times. All films, tapes, or images required by this section shall be retained by the outlet, in its original, viewable format, for a period of no less than 30 days after recording.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-137))
Secs. 4-125—4-146. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 4-147. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell or offer to sell any spirituous liquors or distilled spirits at wholesale or retail within the corporate limits of the city without having the appropriate license for such manufacture or sale, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or this article.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-166))
State Law reference— Local liquor license, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-40 et seq.
Sec. 4-148. - Separate license for each outlet; transfer; surrender.
(a)
A separate license shall be required for each liquor outlet, and a separate application shall be made for each.
(b)
Licenses for approved outlets under this division shall be transferable upon the prior approval of the city clerk and the police chief and upon payment of a transfer fee of the amount set forth in the fee schedule kept and maintained by the city clerk.
(c)
Immediately upon the sale or closing of an outlet, it shall be the duty of the licensee to surrender his license to the city council.
(d)
Any licensee who is an employee of a corporation, who is separated from his employment, whether he resigns, retires or is fired, shall immediately notify the office of the city clerk of such separation. Should the city clerk's office not be open at such time, the police department shall be notified instead with notice given to the city clerk as soon as possible on the next day of normal business. If such employee removes the liquor license from the establishment, he shall immediately surrender such license to the city clerk, or to the police department. Failure to surrender such license, if removed from the place of business, will be a violation and shall be punished as provided by section 6.20 of the city Charter.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-167))
Sec. 4-149. - Classification of licenses; fees.
(a)
Type of licenses. Licenses under this article shall be classified as follows:
(1)
Class D. Distilleries, which shall permit the operation of distilleries, as contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 3-4-24.2 et seq.
(2)
Class M. Manufacturers, which shall permit only the manufacture, bottling, and packaging of distilled spirits.
(3)
Class R. Retail liquor package store, which shall permit only the sale of liquor in packages at retail.
(4)
Class W. Wholesale liquor distributor, which shall permit only the sale of liquor at wholesale.
(b)
Fee established. The amount of the license fee for each class shall be as set forth in the license fee schedule of the city as kept and maintained by the city clerk.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-168))
Sec. 4-150. - Initial application for license.
(a)
An application for a Class M license shall be made to the city council.
(b)
Application for a Class D, Class R, or Class W license shall be as follows:
(1)
Each initial applicant shall make a written application to the city council for the privilege of engaging in the sale (specifying distillery, wholesale, or retail) of liquor, such applicant using forms approved by the city clerk.
(2)
The application shall be filed with the city clerk with a fee for investigation of the applicant and of the proposed location and processing of the application, in the amount provided for in the license fee schedule of the city as kept and maintained by the city clerk.
(3)
The application shall state:
a.
As to the applicants: Full name, birth date, present address and addresses for the past five years (including compliance with O.C.G.A. § 3-4-23) business address and business addresses or employers and their addresses for the last five years, names of spouse and all minor children, complete record of all arrests, convictions and guilty pleas for violations of all laws, city, state and federal, of both applicant and spouse, and the disposition of all charges.
b.
As to the applicant's outlet manager (if not the same as the applicant), the same information as required of the applicant in this subsection.
c.
Exact location (street address and otherwise) of proposed business.
d.
Name and address of owner or landlord and/or resident manager of property proposed for location and whether or not there is a building proposed to be used already erected thereon.
e.
Trade name, or proposed trade name, of proposed business.
f.
Names and addresses of all persons having any financial interest in the outlet by way of ownership of building or stock, receipt of income from the business or otherwise.
g.
Such other information as may be required by the city council, the chief of police, the city attorney, or the city clerk through interrogatories or otherwise.
(4)
The application shall contain evidence of the following persons' approval of the licensee and of the owner and the manager of the liquor establishment:
a.
Building inspector (i.e., building permit, certificate of occupancy, or other evidence).
b.
Chief of police.
(5)
The application shall be sworn to.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-169))
Sec. 4-151. - Sham applicant; de facto applicant or outlet manager.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to file, or permit to be filed, an application for license under this division wherein a sham applicant or a sham outlet manager is named.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to file an application for license containing his name as applicant, or the name of an outlet manager, when such person is not the de facto applicant or the de facto outlet manager, as the case may be.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit his name to be used on an application for license where such person will not be the de facto owner of the license or the de facto outlet manager, as the case may be.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to file an application for license, or permit his name to be used in such application, where the application contains a nominal applicant or nominal outlet manager for the purpose of avoiding the provisions of this division restricting applicants or managers to persons of good character and without criminal records, or restricting economic interests in outlets, or for any other elusive purpose.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-170))
Sec. 4-152. - Application containing false information.
An application under this division containing known false information may result in the disapproval, revocation, suspension or failure to renew the license for which applied.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-171))
Sec. 4-153. - Incomplete application.
An application under this division filed and submitted before containing substantially all the information required may be rejected by the city council, and an applicant may not refile for a period of 90 days.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-172))
Sec. 4-154. - Advertisement, proof thereof.
(a)
Each applicant under this division shall advertise his application for license in a local daily newspaper once a week for two weeks, which advertisement shall appear after filing application and before the public hearing provided for in this division. The advertisement shall contain the name of the applicant, the purpose of the application, the proposed location of the business and its owners, and the name of the proposed outlet manager, if known.
(b)
The applicant shall also, within three days after filing the application, identify the proposed location by posting a sign not less than 24 inches by 36 inches in size, with letters of at least 2½ inches on the front door of the building proposed to be used (if facing the right-of-way of the city street upon which the building fronts; otherwise, within three feet of the right-of-way), or upon a prominent place on the lot where the building is proposed to be constructed, facing the street. The sign shall be captioned "Liquor license application pending" and shall designate the name and address of the applicant, the type of license and the date of the application, and no other advertisement or wording. The sign shall remain posted until final action by the city council on the application.
(c)
The cost of the advertising, both newspaper and sign, shall be borne by the applicant.
(d)
The applicant, before consideration will be given to his application, shall file with the city clerk:
(1)
A copy of the advertisement together with affidavits to the effect that it has been printed as required.
(2)
A photograph of the sign erected pursuant to this section, together with affidavits to the effect that it has been erected as required.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-174))
Sec. 4-155. - Investigation of applicant; recommendations; grant, denial of application.
Within seven days from the time proof of advertisement is filed under this division, the city clerk shall notify the chief of police of the application, who shall investigate the character of the applicant and report to the city council. The city clerk shall also refer the application, together with any objections filed, to the city council. The city council shall afford the applicant a hearing prior to rendering its decision. The city council, after investigation, shall direct the city clerk to issue or refuse the license for which application is made, upon payment of the appropriate fee.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-175))
Sec. 4-156. - Conditional approval of application; suspension of license.
The city council may approve an application under this division subject to supplying documentation and/or meeting required formalities, and withhold issuance of a license until such requirements are complete.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-176))
Sec. 4-157. - Approval of application; expiration in 90 days; extension for cause.
Approval of an application under this division by the city council may be valid for up to 180 days, at the discretion of the city council. The city council may extend the approval for cause and within its discretion, provided the request therefor is made within the original approval period.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-177))
Sec. 4-158. - Application not to be resubmitted or entertained within six months of previous application; deferral of action on application subject matter of which is being litigated.
(a)
No applicant under this division may submit, and the city council shall not act upon, an application which is substantially the same as an application submitted within the previous six months, unless the council specifically approves a request for early resubmission.
(b)
The city council may defer action upon any application, the subject matter of which is substantially the same as the subject matter of litigation then pending in the state or federal courts until such time as the litigation is resolved.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-178))
Sec. 4-159. - Considerations and guidelines for granting or denying license.
The considerations and guidelines for granting or denying a liquor license shall be the same as for malt beverages, as set out in section 4-92. An application for a retail package liquor license shall be the same as, and in accordance with, the requirements applicable to the sale of malt beverages by the package at retail, and all provisions of this chapter pertaining to the application and qualification for a retail package license, including sections 4-89 through 4-94 and 4-97, shall be enforced in the sale of liquor, except to the extent any of those provisions conflicts with any provision in this article; in the case of such a conflict the provision of this article shall control.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-179))
Sec. 4-160. - Grounds for denial of license.
(a)
Prohibited locations. The requirements pertaining to prohibited locations of liquor establishments shall be the same as for malt beverages in section 4-93(a) except that:
(1)
No Class R license shall be issued unless the distances from school grounds or college campuses shall be 200 yards;
(2)
No Class R license shall be issued unless the location of the proposed retail facility is at least one-half miles from the southwest corner of the intersection of Cave Spring Road and Alabama Street;
(3)
No new Class R license nor the relocation of any existing Class R establishment shall be located within 500 yards of any other business licensed to sell package liquor at retail, as measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground; and
(4)
No Class R license shall be issued for any location within a measured 150 feet of a private residence.
(b)
Prohibited persons. It shall be unlawful for any person to grant a license for the sale of liquor to:
(1)
A person who does not meet the state law residency requirements set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-4-23.
(2)
A person who is not of good character and reputation in the community in which he resides.
(3)
A person who has been convicted of a felony, or of a crime opposed to decency and morality, or who has been convicted of a crime involving violation of the ordinances of the city relating to the use, sale, taxability or possession of malt beverages, wine or liquor, or violations of the laws of the state or federal government pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation or sale of malt beverages, wine or intoxicating liquors, or taxability thereof.
(4)
A corporation.
(5)
A partnership, unless all partners qualify.
(6)
A person whose license under this chapter has been revoked for cause.
(7)
A person who is under the age of 21 years or who is not of sound mind and memory.
(8)
An elected official, officer, or employee of the city, or members of his immediate family, provided however that the city council shall have the authority to review applications to determine if appropriate and sufficient grounds exist to grant a variance to this prohibition.
(9)
A person who already holds a Class W license or a license in the classification applied for.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-180))
Sec. 4-161. - Becoming a prohibited person.
Any licensee under this division who becomes a prohibited person as defined in section 4-160(b), shall, within ten days of the event, make the fact known to the city council and shall surrender his license upon demand by the city council in such case.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-181))
Sec. 4-162. - Renewal; denial of renewal.
(a)
All licenses under this division shall be issued on a calendar-year basis and shall be renewable as a matter of course upon payment of the appropriate fee, unless adverse information shall have come to the knowledge of the city council or unless the chief of police makes a report as is set out in subsection (d) of this section.
(b)
Each licensee shall make written application for renewal of his license on or before November 15 of each year on forms provided by the city clerk, and the license fee shall be paid in full on or before December 31 of each year.
(c)
The application shall be referred to the chief of police, who shall report on the licensee's activity, if any, and upon activity at the location of the licensed business, if any, during the year.
(d)
If the chief of police reports any activity which constitutes probable cause for not renewing a license, the renewal shall not be made, and the matter shall be referred to the city council for its consideration.
(e)
The city council shall afford the licensee a public hearing and make findings of fact. In passing upon the application for renewal, the city council shall be guided by factors applicable to an initial application set forth in section 4-92, and by section 4-161. The city council may grant, or deny for cause, the renewal of any license issued under this division.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-182))
Sec. 4-163. - Suspension, revocation, and monetary sanction—Generally.
(a)
Summary action. There shall be no summary seizure of licenses under this division, unless there exists in the city a state of emergency duly declared; after any such seizure, a hearing shall be held as soon as practicable.
(b)
Action by city council. The city council shall have full power and authority to suspend, refuse to renew, place on probation, or revoke a license to sell liquor, and to impose a monetary sanction not to exceed $2,000.00 under the conditions set forth in this section. Before taking any such action except suspending a license for failure to pay an imposed monetary sanction when due, the city council shall conduct a hearing, at least five days' notice of which hearing shall have been given to the affected licensee. Sufficient grounds for the suspension, refusal to renew, probation, revocation of a license, or imposition of a monetary sanction include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
A licensee has failed to open a business within six months after approval of the license.
(2)
A licensee has ceased to operate the business for a period of three consecutive months.
(3)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has sold alcoholic beverages during a period of suspension.
(4)
A licensee gave false or misleading information in the original application or renewal process.
(5)
A licensee has failed to pay any fee, license fee, or other amount of money due to the city under this chapter or any other city licensing ordinance.
(6)
A licensee has failed to maintain any and all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a license as set forth in this article.
(7)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person that the licensee or the licensee's employee or agent, knew or should have known to be in a state of intoxication.
(8)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person under the age of legal consumption in the state.
(9)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other law, ordinance, or regulation governing the operation of an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages or which is reasonably related to the operation of such establishment.
(10)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other section of this chapter.
(11)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has been convicted or pled guilty or no contest to any violation of its license to sell or distribute alcoholic beverages issued by the state department of revenue, the city, the county and/or the county sheriff's office.
(12)
A licensee has failed to satisfy the criteria for obtaining a license to sell liquor.
(13)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any federal, state, or local laws or ordinances relating to the use and sale of drugs and alcoholic beverages.
(14)
When the continued operation of the licensed business is detrimental to the health, safety, or welfare of the public.
(c)
Suspension. The failure of a licensee to pay when due any monetary sanction assessed pursuant to the terms of this chapter shall result in the immediate suspension of all alcohol licenses issued by the city to such licensee. A suspension under this subsection shall continue until all monetary sanctions and other sums due the city by the licensee are paid in full.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-183))
Sec. 4-164. - Suspension, revocation, and monetary sanction—Changes of circumstances.
The approval of an application by the city council, and/or the granting of a license by the city council, may be suspended or revoked if any change in the information supplied, or in the physical layout represented in the plat submitted, occurs without the approval of the city council. An applicant anticipating any such change shall submit all details thereof to the city council.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-184))
Secs. 4-165—4-181. - Reserved.
