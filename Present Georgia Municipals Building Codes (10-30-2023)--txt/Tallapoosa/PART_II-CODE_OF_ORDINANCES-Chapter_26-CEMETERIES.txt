Sec. 26-1. - Purchase of lots.
All persons desiring to purchase a lot in the city cemetery shall apply to the city clerk, who, upon payment of the fixed price for each lot, shall cause a deed or other instrument of receipt therefor to be given to the purchaser.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-1; Code 2005, § 26-1)
Sec. 26-2. - Lot charges.
The cost of lots in the cemetery shall be as from time to time established by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-2; Code 2005, § 26-2)
Sec. 26-3. - Burial permit.
No person shall be buried in the cemetery without there first having been obtained from the city a burial permit for the person who is to be buried in the cemetery.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-3; Code 2005, § 26-3)
Sec. 26-4. - Digging of graves.
All graves shall be dug in accordance with regulations approved by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-4; Code 2005, § 26-4)
Sec. 26-5. - Approval required for planting of trees or other plants.
No person shall plant or set any tree, shrub, flower, grass, or other plant of any kind in the cemetery except with the approval of the city.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-5; Code 2005, § 26-5)
Sec. 26-6. - Cutting of trees and shrubs.
The pruning or cutting of all trees and shrubbery in the cemetery is hereby prohibited unless done under the immediate supervision of a city employee.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-6; Code 2005, § 26-6)
Sec. 26-7. - Work to be done by city.
All grading, landscaping and improvements of every kind shall be made or done by the city only.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-7; Code 2005, § 26-7)
Sec. 26-8. - Conduct in cemetery; damaging trees or other plants; trespassing.
No person shall disturb the quiet, repose and good order of the cemetery, nor shall any person deface, remove, disturb, injure or destroy any tree, plant, or shrub therein except in the manner provided in this chapter. Trespassing within the cemetery is hereby prohibited, and no person shall commit any immoral act therein.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-8; Code 2005, § 26-8)
Sec. 26-9. - Vehicles.
Vehicles shall be driven only upon the public roadways within the cemetery and at a rate of speed not in excess of 15 miles per hour. No vehicle shall enter the cemetery except for the purpose of attending funerals, visiting graves, or other lawful mission.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-9; Code 2005, § 26-9)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 98.
Sec. 26-10. - Deposit of waste.
No person shall deposit any rubbish, filth, waste, or other unclean or unsightly substance in the cemetery.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-10; Code 2005, § 26-10)
Cross reference— Solid waste, ch. 82.
Sec. 26-11. - Lot regulations.
All lots within the cemetery of the city, and within any extension of the cemetery at any time hereafter made, shall be subject to and regulated and controlled by the provisions of this chapter and any rules adopted by the city.
(Code 1990, § 5-4-11; Code 2005, § 26-11)
Sec. 26-12. - Rules and regulations pertaining to all city cemeteries.
(a)
Permits.
(1)
A no-cost notification permit must be obtained from the city hall before any construction can be commenced. A fee shall be charged for subsection (a)(1)d of this section.
a.
A grave to be dug.
b.
A grave to be reopened.
c.
A grave to be disinterred.
d.
A grave or lot to be owner transferred.
e.
A monument (headstone) to be installed in a cemetery grave. This permit will require a footing inspection to be performed prior to the monument (headstone) being installed.
(2)
Such permits should be obtained 24 hours prior to the planned activity, if possible.
(b)
Corner markers required. All purchasers of municipal cemetery grave spaces shall be required to place corner markers on said spaces. These markers shall be designated by the city, on lot locations and at various areas within the city cemetery, so as to more accurately and clearly designate the lot spaces, sections, or other areas within the city cemetery.
(c)
Grave digging or maintenance.
(1)
All graves shall be dug and prepared under the direction of the city.
(2)
Only approved (authorized) persons, or firms with a state license, upon receipt of a permit from the family, may dig graves in the city cemeteries.
(3)
General maintenance may be done by lot owners on their lots at any time except while a burial is in progress.
(4)
The lot owner is responsible for ensuring that all excess dirt is removed from a city cemetery, leaving at least two feet covering the vault and shall level off and place, or make a permanent grave site covering, with grass seed. At the end of 30 days, or a reasonable time, if the lot owner has not done this, the city will remove the excess dirt to level off the grave site, make the permanent grave site covering and bill the lot owner or family $100.00 for the service. The city will contact the owner prior to the work being started.
(5)
No grave shall be less than four feet to the bottom of the vault. No wooden boxes shall be used for burial. Vaults are required for all burials having a length of five feet. Vaults must be made of concrete, fiberglass, steel, or equal.
(6)
Markers must be removed and replaced by the company digging the grave, and that charge, if there is an additional fee, added to the grave opening fee, as established by the grave digging company and billed to the customer by the grave digging company. That company is responsible and accountable for this action, and failure of the company to do so may subject that company to additional fees assessed by the city, and perhaps loss of authorization to work in the city cemeteries.
(d)
Trees and shrubs.
(1)
Trees and shrubs are not permitted.
(2)
If trees or plants currently exist on a grave lot, the city has the authority to remove them if they become unsightly, or interfere with the maintenance or orderly operation of other plots.
(3)
The city will notify the lot owner of maintenance needs, which shall be done by the owner.
(4)
If the city maintenance crew is required to perform necessary lot maintenance, the owner will be billed for the work at $25.00 per hour, with at least one hour billed. However, the city will contact the owner prior to the work being started.
(e)
Lot flowers, wreaths, etc.
(1)
Lot owners may place flowers, wreaths, etc., on their plots at any time.
(2)
The owner shall remove live flowers within a reasonable length of time of their placement.
(3)
Plastic or silk arrangements must be removed when worn and/or faded.
(4)
Items not removed by the owner may be removed by the city upon being determined they are unsightly or faded.
(5)
Following a burial, the owner shall remove all original arrangements, after a reasonable length of time.
(6)
If lot arrangements are vandalized, the city may immediately clean the lot without notifying the owner.
(f)
Erection of monuments, gravestones, and markers.
(1)
Grave markers (headstones) will carry very limited restrictions, as described below. However, they must be inside the boundaries of the owner's lot by a minimum of six inches on all sides. Damage to markers shall be repaired within two weeks of a city notification to the owner of the damage.
(2)
Each headstone and footstone shall have a six-inch concrete base, or "footing." All footings will be inspected by the city prior to the stone being set.
(3)
Plot boundary markers shall be only ground level, flush with the ground, such that a lawn mower can cut over the marker. The permitted marker shall be constructed out of bronze, granite or marble. Markers must be maintained within the boundaries of the owner's lot.
(4)
No monument shall be more than six feet (72 inches) in height above the ground, and must be at least four inches thick (Pollard section only).
(5)
The plan, design and location of all monuments and markers must be submitted to, and approved by, the city before work thereon is commenced.
(6)
Only flat markers shall be permitted in the First Addition (lower Hollywood section). No walls or masonry markers will be allowed. No flowers can be planted on or around lots. All flower arrangements, including vases and potted plants, must comply with subsection (e) of this section and should be removed once they become faded, unsightly or wilted.
(g)
Above-ground vaults, crypts, or mausoleums. No above-ground vaults, crypts, or mausoleums shall be placed in the city cemetery, except in certain sections. Mausoleums may be a maximum of nine feet in height, designed to accommodate two vaults (bodies).
(h)
Burial of paupers. The mayor and city council, or their assigned representatives, must approve the burial of paupers. At the request of the assigned representative, the party requesting burial shall disclose pertinent information that is related to the burial.
(i)
Burials.
(1)
The city cemetery will accommodate the burial of human remains only.
(2)
Cremation burial policy:
a.
The city must pre-approve any grave opening in the city cemetery.
b.
A licensed grave digger must dig all grave sites.
c.
Cremation vaults are required.
d.
Cremation vaults must have a minimum of 24 inches of dirt covering the top.
e.
Burial of cremated remains are limited to two per grave.
f.
All rules regarding standard grave opening shall apply, except depth.
(j)
Disinterment. No disinterment shall be allowed, except as provided by state law. The city shall make arrangements with the owner or person obtaining the permit for disinterment for such work.
(k)
Vandalism.
(1)
No person shall damage, injure, obliterate or desecrate any grave or lot.
(2)
No person shall trespass upon any grave.
(3)
No person shall cut, deface, mutilate or otherwise damage or injure any monument or other memorial or marker.
(4)
No person shall damage, injure, destroy, or remove any flower, shrub, tree, or other plant or any structure or other object in the cemetery.
(l)
Cemetery hours. The city cemetery is closed every day from sundown to sunup.
(m)
Burial notification. The owner or his assigned representative shall notify the city 24 hours prior to a burial.
(n)
Fees and charges. A burial lot in a city cemetery consists of four grave spaces. Fees and costs for burial lots (which includes all permit fees and costs), transfer of ownership records, maintenance work and mausoleums, in the city cemetery, shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, as follows below:
(1)
Current fees and charges (these may be amended by the mayor and council by resolution):
a.
Lower section Hollywood Cemetery, lot: $1,200.00 (includes all fees and permits).
b.
New addition, Pollard section, lot: $1,600.00 (includes all fees and permits).
c.
Transfer of ownership records: $25.00 per lot.
d.
Commercial mausoleums (per vault space): $300.00.
(o)
Payment arrangements for burial lot purchase. At the request of the burial lot purchaser, the city may make arrangements for the purchaser to acquire a burial lot by paying the fee in monthly installments. The maximum number of months that either burial lot, lower section Hollywood Cemetery or new addition, Pollard section, may take to fully pay for the burial lot is 12 months. Such an arrangements' details shall be agreed upon with the city manager. Normally, the payments would be either $100.00 per month or $133.00 ($137.00 first payment), respectively. The first month's payment must be paid at the signing of the burial lot deed, and continue in consecutive months until the debt retirement is completed. The burial lot deed shall be held by the city until that time that the entire cost of the burial lot has been paid. However, if events require the purchaser to use the burial lot prior to the debt being completely paid, purchaser must pay the entire unpaid balance to the city, prior to the city providing permission to have one of the grave sites opened.
(p)
Abandoned cemetery lots. Any grave lot or space, or portions thereof, within the city cemetery, which have been abandoned or the rights of the owner of said lot have been terminated or forfeited, for any reason, shall be addressed, pursuant to the provisions of the O.C.G.A. § 44-5-211, as presently enacted, or as may from time to time be subsequently amended or revised.
(q)
Miscellaneous rules and regulations.
(1)
The grades of all lots shall be fixed at the time of the preparation of the lot for sale; and no change will be allowed when that grade has been established.
(2)
No coping, fence, sidewalk, wall or any other kind of enclosure of a grave, lot or burial space will be allowed.
(3)
No benches, fences, seats, boxes or other articles or fixtures shall be allowed on any lot.
(4)
All prior sales of lots shall be grandfathered, in as much that no further fees regarding grave opening shall be levied, as of the date of adoption of the ordinance from which this amendment is derived.
(r)
Penalty for violation of article. All violations of this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7, upon conviction.
