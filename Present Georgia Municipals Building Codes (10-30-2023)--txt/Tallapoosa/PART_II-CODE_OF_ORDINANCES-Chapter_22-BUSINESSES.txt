ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Junk dealers.
(a)
No person shall collect junk of any kind on private property within the city without first obtaining a junk dealer's license. No junk dealer shall operate his business on public property or on any property not allowing such a business under chapter 106, pertaining to zoning.
(b)
All junk dealers shall enclose the property used for their business with a solid, opaque fence six feet high. No junk may be maintained on such property in such a manner as to be visible from passersby on a public right-of-way or by persons on neighboring property.
(c)
It is required to obtain and maintain a junk yard cleanup bond equal to a value of $25,000.00 per acre of the lot where the junk yard is to be located. Proof of the issuance of the bond must be shown prior to the new or subsequent license being issued.
(Code 1990, § 9-4-1; Code 2005, § 22-1)
Sec. 22-2. - Closing hours for pool rooms.
No pool room or billiard hall shall remain open in the city later than 12:00 midnight.
(Code 1990, § 9-4-2; Code 2005, § 22-2)
State Law reference— Regulation of pool rooms, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.
Sec. 22-3. - Peddling on streets.
No person shall peddle or sell from any vehicle on city streets or rights-of-way.
(Code 1990, § 9-4-3; Code 2005, § 22-3)

ARTICLE II. - TATTOO STUDIOS

Sec. 22-25. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Moral character.
(1)
Good moral character means the propensity of the person to serve the public in the licensed area in a fair, honest and open manner.
(2)
A judgment of guilt in a criminal prosecution or a judgment in a civil action shall not be used in and of itself as proof of a person's lack of good moral character. It may be used as evidence in the determination and when so used the person shall be notified and shall be permitted to rebut the evidence by showing that:
a.
At the current time he has the ability to, and is likely to, serve the public in a fair, honest and open manner; and
b.
He is rehabilitated, or that the substance of the former offense is not reasonably related to the occupation or profession for which he seeks a business license or business permit.
(3)
The following criminal records shall not be used, examined, or requested by the city in a determination of good moral character when used as a requirement to obtain a business license or business permit:
a.
Records of an arrest not followed by a conviction.
b.
Records of a conviction which has been reversed or vacated, including the arrest records relevant to that conviction.
c.
Records of an arrest or conviction for a misdemeanor or a felony unrelated to the person's likelihood to serve the public in a fair, honest, and open manner.
d.
Records of an arrest or conviction for a misdemeanor for the conviction of which a person may not be incarcerated in a jail or prison.
Tattoo, tattoo artist and tattoo studio mean the same as found in the definitions section of O.C.G.A. § 31-40-1. Further, should there be additional defined terms under state law which apply to tattoo establishments, these terms are likewise incorporated into this article by reference.
(Code 2005, § 22-31; Ord. No. 01216, § 9-5-1, 3-12-2001)
Cross reference— Definitions, generally, § 1-2.
State Law reference— Terms defined, O.C.G.A. § 31-40-1.
Sec. 22-26. - Adoption of state law.
The city hereby adopts the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 31-40-1 et seq., as if set out fully in this section, with additions as further noted in this article.
(Code 2005, § 22-32; Ord. No. 01216, 3-12-2001)
State Law reference— Tattoo studio regulations, O.C.G.A. § 31-40-1 et seq.
Sec. 22-27. - Business license required; insurance.
(a)
A normal business license shall be required to operate a tattoo studio pursuant to this Code.
(b)
Any person operating a tattoo studio within the corporate limits of the city shall maintain a general public liability policy covering any acts, errors or omissions in connection with the operation of the studio in an amount to be set by the mayor and city council from time to time. Further, medical payments coverage, also to be set by the mayor and city council from time to time, shall be obtained, so as to adequately protect the public against injury, death, medical complications, sickness or other matters which might occur as a result of negligent failure of operations, or similar tortuous conduct, of any tattoo studio. Evidence of the certificate of insurance meeting the minimum requirements of this section shall be provided to the city prior to the issuance of a license.
(Code 2005, § 22-33; Ord. No. 01216, § 9-5-2, 3-12-2001)
Sec. 22-28. - Prohibited locations.
It shall be unlawful for a tattoo studio to operate in any location which is presently a prohibited location for the operation of malt beverage establishments in the city pursuant to this Code.
(Code 2005, § 22-34; Ord. No. 01216, § 9-5-3, 3-12-2001)
Sec. 22-29. - State permit; compliance with state regulations.
No person shall be authorized to operate a tattoo studio within the corporate limits of the city without first having obtained a valid permit for such operations by the duly authorized representative of the state department of human resources, through the county board of health or such other agency as might review the permit procedure. The provisions concerning the issuance of permits, suspensions and revocations and rules and regulations concerning tattoo studios as contained in O.C.G.A. § 31-40-2 et seq., are incorporated in this section by reference and made a part of this article. Further, the rules of the state department of human resources governing tattoo studios and artists, presently promulgated in chapter 290-5, as currently enacted or as hereafter amended, are incorporated in this section by reference and made a part of this article. Any applicant and/or license holder shall comply with all terms, provisions and conditions of both state law and state regulatory authority concerning the operation of a tattoo studio, and first obtain a state permit prior to applying for a city license.
(Code 2005, § 22-35; Ord. No. 01216, § 9-5-4, 3-12-2001)
Sec. 22-30. - Persons to whom license may be issued; transfer of license.
A license may only be granted to a person (or if a partnership to a managing partner, and if a corporation a local outlet manager of the corporation) for any tattoo studio. Such license is not transferable.
(Code 2005, § 22-36; Ord. No. 01216, § 9-5-5, 3-12-2001)
Sec. 22-31. - Revocation of license.
Any person who shall conduct a tattoo studio or otherwise engage in the business of tattooing without first securing a state permit license and city license, or operating in violation of the permit and/or license, shall be subject to revocation of the license upon appropriate notice and hearing by the city. Where public health and emergency exist, the license may be forthwith (in only exigent circumstances) suspended, provided that a hearing is conducted within ten days of the exigent suspension concerning the reason for emergency suspension and for a decision affecting partial or permanent revocation of the license. Grounds for revocation of a license shall be violations of the provisions of this article or the provisions and regulations of state law, or similar violations of federal, state or local laws governing tattoo studios and these establishments in general.
(Code 2005, § 22-37; Ord. No. 01216, § 9-5-6, 3-12-2001)
Secs. 22-32—22-50. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - WRECKER AND TOWING SERVICES[2]

Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 98.

Sec. 22-51. - Prerequisites for placement on rotation list; rotation list procedures.
(a)
No person engaged in the wrecker/towing service business within the city shall be called upon by city police officers or other authorized employees of the city to furnish the service of towing any vehicle upon city streets unless the person's firm or corporation shall have been placed on the approved scheduled rotation (month-to-month) call list after having complied with the following conditions:
(1)
Such person must complete the full on-call wrecker service application shown in section 22-57.
(2)
Such person must possess a valid/current occupational license.
(3)
Such person must possess a valid state public service commission certificate and meet all federal motor carrier safety regulations and other applicable state public service commission requirements for operating a wrecker/towing service.
(4)
Such person must have an established business location (as defined in this article) within the three-mile trade area of the city.
(5)
The service must be available 24 hours a day, seven days per week, and be able to respond within 20 minutes on at least 90 percent of calls made by the city police department.
(6)
The wrecker/towing service shall have qualified employees skilled in performing their duties and possessing a valid Georgia driver license of the proper class.
(7)
The wrecker/towing service shall have available at least two wreckers, one of which must be a roll-back wrecker in good operating condition. This means meeting requirements of local rules/regulations, local ordinances, federal motor carrier regulations, state law, and state public service commission requirements.
(8)
All wreckers shall be equipped with an amber flashing light meeting the most current regulatory requirements of the Georgia State Patrol, and also sufficient red flares, lanterns, or warning lights and reflectors (as required by section 393.95 of the Federal Motor Carrier Regulations) so as to be able to mark the scene of a wreck on both sides of the wreck, so as to warn all traffic on the highway of the emergency existing while the wreckers are in operation. The wreckers shall also carry brooms, shovels, and a bucket (pail), and fire extinguishers, suitable for extinguishing fires in wrecked vehicles and for cleaning up the debris and glass from the area where the wreck has occurred. The fire extinguisher shall meet the requirements of section 393.95 of the Federal Motor Carrier Regulations.
(9)
There shall be painted in a conspicuous place, on both the right and left side of the wrecker, the name of the company, the address where the wrecker is based, and a telephone number.
(10)
All persons engaged in the wrecker/towing business shall carry insurance, including liability insurance in the form of comprehensive, general, and automobile liability, and the policy shall protect it from claims for bodily injury, including death, to its employees and all others, and from claims for property damage, all of which may arise out of the result of the wrecker's operation under this article. This insurance shall be sufficient to reasonably ensure the contractor's solvency in the event of any injuries, deaths, or property damage, with the following minimum limits:
a.
Bodily injury: $100,000.00 per person, $300,000.00 per accident.
b.
Property damage in the amount of $50,000.00 or single limit of $300,000.00.
A valid certificate of insurance must be maintained. Expiration of insurance will result in immediate removal from the list.
(11)
The wrecker/towing service shall provide a storage area located within the three-mile trade area of the city for the storage and safekeeping of vehicles towed under this article and enclosed with a solid wall or substantial wire fence not less than six feet in height. The storage area shall be staffed weekly during regular business hours. Garage liability insurance in the same amount as required in subsection (a)(10) of this section shall be required.
(12)
All wrecker/towing services agree to permit regular inspection of wrecker/towing vehicles and storage premises by representatives of the city police department. These inspections may be conducted by the state public service commission.
(b)
The police officer or lawfully authorized officer shall determine whether or not a wrecker is needed, and if a wrecker is needed shall ascertain if the owner or operator is able to make his preference known as to what wrecker service the owner or operator desires called and have the dispatcher call the preferred service desired by the owner or operator of the vehicle. No police officer or other authorized officer may recommend to any person the wrecker service company to be called or employed.
(c)
If the owner or operator is unable (due to injury or physical or mental impairment) to make a choice, or does not have a preference, the city police department will call the wrecker on the scheduled call list.
(d)
If the city police are unable to reach the wrecker service on call, or for any reason the wrecker service on call is unable to furnish the service, the backup wrecker service (which will be the service next on call) shall be called. A wrecker/towing service shall not be able to designate its own backup service.
(e)
Except in extreme emergencies, the police officer or other authorized officer shall await the arrival of the wrecker and shall, in the meantime, try to make the scene of the accident safe, giving warnings and directing traffic.
(f)
The police officer or other authorized officer shall make out a tow-in report, which shall contain the information and details prescribed by the city.
(g)
If an officer requests that a vehicle be towed to the police department, the wrecker operator shall have the shift sergeant sign the tow-in bill. At this point, the shift sergeant or designee will assume the responsibility for the vehicle and indicate where the vehicle is to be placed.
(h)
Additional wrecker/towing services meeting the requirements of this section may be added to the rotation list. The first scheduled on-call rotation for additional service shall occur after all services on the list have completed one regular month rotation.
(Code 2005, § 22-61; Ord. No. 01191, § I, 5-12-1997)
Sec. 22-52. - Responsibilities of operators.
(a)
Wrecker firms shall be responsible to the owner for all damage done during towing and storage of any vehicles entrusted to them under this article.
(b)
Wrecker services shall cooperate with all duly authorized law enforcement officers in the protection of any evidence or in any investigation arising out of a wreck or seizure of a vehicle by any official.
(c)
It shall be the responsibility of the wrecker service (prior to leaving the scene of any collision) to clean the pavement of all debris, including all glass.
(d)
The wrecker service shall not release any vehicle placed on hold from storage unless approved by an authorized representative of the city police department with issuance of a signed release form.
(e)
The wrecker service shall maintain all wrecker units and all related equipment adequately, and be able to perform towing services in a responsible, workmanlike, and safe manner.
(f)
When responding to a wrecker call the service shall use due regard and shall not be equipped or operated as an emergency vehicle. A wrecker service shall not follow or attempt to overtake, remain close to, or use authorized emergency vehicles to assist in violating state traffic laws or ordinances of the city unless authorized by officers on the scene as relayed by dispatch.
(Code 2005, § 22-62; Ord. No. 01191, § II, 5-12-1997)
Sec. 22-53. - Established business location required; business license, insurance and state certification; sharing wreckers.
(a)
Office facilities and storage area. Each wrecker service must have a separate office and garage/storage area located within the three-mile trade area of the city. The garage/storage area cannot be shared with any other services on the approved scheduled call list.
(b)
Telephone. Each service must have a separate telephone listing. This telephone number must be manned on a 24-hour-a-day basis during the on-call status.
(c)
Occupational license. Each service must have a separate valid occupational license in the name of the service.
(d)
Insurance. Each service must have separate insurance coverage issued in the name of the service, meeting all limits required in this article.
(e)
State public service commission certificate. Each service must have a separate state public service commission certificate as required by law or by regulation.
(f)
Sharing wreckers. Wrecker/towing units and equipment required under this article must be assigned and allocated to a specific service. Wreckers/towing units may not be assigned or allocated to more than one service or shared between or among services for the purposes of meeting the requirements of this article.
(Code 2005, § 22-63; Ord. No. 01191, § III, 5-12-1997)
Sec. 22-54. - Suspension or removal from rotation list.
(a)
Upon written notice, the chief of police, or his designee, may suspend or remove any wrecker service assigned to the scheduled rotation call list for, but not limited to, violations of this article, violations of local ordinance, state law, or state public service commission regulations, or other just cause.
(b)
Removal by the chief of police, or his designee, may be appealed in writing to the city manager, or his designee, within ten working days following the effective date of the notice.
(c)
The city manager, or his designee, shall act as a hearing officer to conduct an appeal hearing and render an impartial decision based on the testimony and/or evidence presented.
(d)
No later than five working days following the completion of the hearing, the city manager, or his designee, shall render an impartial decision on the appeal. The decision of the city manager will be final.
(e)
Any grievance or complaint against the action of a city employee shall be rendered in writing. This grievance shall be returned to the sergeant within five working days of the date of the incident.
(f)
The sergeant shall review the complaint for merit and, upon his recommendation, shall forward the complaint through the chain of command for review.
(g)
Upon the request of the wrecker/towing service, any decision rendered by the sergeant may be appealed to the chief of police. The decision of the chief of police shall be final.
(h)
If a complaint against an employee is filed in conjunction with a disciplinary action against a wrecker/towing service, both will be handled concurrently.
(Code 2005, § 22-64; Ord. No. 01191, § IV, 5-12-1997)
Sec. 22-55. - Fees for wrecker services.
Fees for services rendered under this article shall be established by the respective wrecker/towing service. A listing of such fees shall be clearly posted at the garage/storage site and a written copy available for distribution on each wrecker unit.
(Code 2005, § 22-65; Ord. No. 01191, § V, 5-12-1997)
Sec. 22-56. - Use of rotation call list.
A schedule shall be set up on a one-week rotating basis of all qualified wrecker/towing services under this article, which service shall be used as provided in this article except in extreme emergency, where persons are trapped or pinned in or under a vehicle, or in burning vehicles, or in danger of drowning by submersion, or where traffic is blocked to prevent the normal flow of vehicles, in which event and only in which event the police officer shall be authorized to call the nearest, most qualified service, to prevent serious injury or death.
(Code 2005, § 22-66; Ord. No. 01191, § VI, 5-12-1997)

ARTICLE VI. - YARD SALES

Sec. 22-146. - Permitted; regulations.
The city hereby proclaims that the occurrence of yard sales is permitted without any necessary paperwork being completed, and no permit, with a permit number, being issued by the city, provided that the following regulations are complied with:
(1)
Yard sales are restricted to land either zoned residential and occupied with a residential building, or land zoned for another land use but occupied by a residential structure, and occupied by people as their place of residence.
(2)
Yard sales are strictly prohibited on property which is vacant.
(3)
The person conducting the yard sale must be a citizen of the city and the yard sale which is occurring must be conducted on the residential property which the citizen owns. The citizen may, at their discretion, allow nonresidents to conduct a yard sale simultaneously while they are conducting a yard sale on their own property, provided that the non-citizen sets up their yard sale on the same property as the sponsoring citizen.
(4)
A citizen can conduct no more than three yard sales per year, with a maximum of three consecutive days of operation (such as Friday, Saturday and Sunday). Yard sales on consecutive weekends are prohibited.
(5)
Any individual setting up a yard sale or yard sale type display on land zoned as either commercial (any district) or manufacturing (any district), whether occupied by another building utilized for commercial/manufacturing activity or vacant land, shall be construed to be a business operating for pecuniary benefit. Therefore, that entity must acquire a occupational tax permit (business license) from the city, the fees for which are established in Ordinance Nos. 01183, 01198 and 01225, and in chapter 94, article III.
(6)
Any individual or entity occupying a commercially zoned or manufacturing zoned parcel of land for a minimum of two hours shall be required to purchase a occupational tax permit from the city, as though they have opened a business up and located an office in Tallapoosa, the fees for which are established in Ordinance Nos. 01183, 11198 and 01225, and in chapter 94, article III.
(7)
Failure to comply with any aspect of these regulations shall result in a citation to appear in the municipal court of the city being issued by the city police department. Punishment is in accordance with section 1-7, with a maximum penalty of $1,000.00 and/or 30 days' incarceration. All activity in violation of this section shall be ordered halted, and a cease and desist order issued by the chief of police or an officer designated to do so by the chief of police.
(8)
No activity currently occurring or previously having been conducted shall be allowed to continue. (No grandfather clause shall be extended.)
(9)
Exemptions. The above-enumerated regulations shall be suspended for the weekend of the city-wide yard sale, which currently occurs during on the first weekend of October, and shall include the Friday immediately prior to that Saturday, and the immediately proceeding Sunday. Such exemption shall be provided for whatever weekend city-wide yard sales occur on in the future.
(Ord. No. 01250, exh. 2005-D, 9-12-2005)
