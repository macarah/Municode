CHAPTER VI. - ELECTIONS

Sec. 6.10. - Regular elections; time for holding.
The regular election for Mayor and Councilmen, or Councilmen, as the case may be, shall be held on the first Tuesday in November every two years. All regular elections [are held in odd years. Officials elected at any regular election shall [take] office at the [first] regular meeting of the Mayor and Council of [in] January next following such election.
Editor's note— The regular elections are held pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
State Law reference— Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
Sec. 6.11. - Applicability of general laws.
The procedures and requirements for election of all elected officials of the City of Tallapoosa as to primary, special or general elections shall be in conformity with the provisions of the Georgia Municipal Election Code (1968 Ga. Laws, page 885), [Repealed—see now O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.] as now or hereafter amended.
Sec. 6.12. - Election by plurality.
All elective City officers must be elected by a plurality of the votes cast for the particular office in the regular or special election.
Sec. 6.13. - Special elections.
In the event that the office of Mayor or any Councilman shall become vacant as provided in section 2.12 of this Charter, the remaining members of the Mayor and Council shall order a special election to fill the balance of the unexpired term of such office; provided, however, if the vacancy occurs within six (6) months of the expiration of the term of office of such Mayor or Councilman, the vacancy may, at the discretion of the remaining members of the Mayor and Council, be filled by appointment of such remaining members, rather than by special election.
