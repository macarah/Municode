ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Exercise of governmental authority.
The corporate governmental powers of the city shall be exercised by the mayor and council in the manner provided by the Charter and by the provisions of this chapter.
(Code 1980, § 2-101; Code 2003, § 2-1)
Sec. 2-2. - Personnel provisions.
Nothing in this Code or in the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance or resolution adopting, establishing or amending any job classification and compensation plan for the employees of the city or any other personnel rules, regulations, policies or procedures, which has been adopted by the mayor and council. All such ordinances are recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out in this Code.
(Code 2003, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-3. - Nepotism.
No person shall be hired as a full-time or part-time employee of the city if such person is related as close as the fifth degree to another employee of the city or an elected official of the city, provided that, if a person related to an employee of the city within the fifth degree shall be elected to an elected office of the city, such election shall not be grounds for the termination of the related city employee.
(Code 2003, § 2-3; Ord. of 3-7-2000, § 1)
Sec. 2-4. - Fiscal year.
The city operates on a fiscal year which shall begin July 1 and end on June 30.
(Code 1980, § 19-301; Code 2003, § 50-213)
