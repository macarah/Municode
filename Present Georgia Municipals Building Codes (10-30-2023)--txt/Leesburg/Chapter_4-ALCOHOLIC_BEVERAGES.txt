Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Purposes.
(a)
The purposes of this chapter shall include, without necessarily being limited to, the following:
(1)
Compliance with and effectuation of the general state law;
(2)
Prevention and control of the sale of alcoholic beverages by unfit persons;
(3)
Ensuring that any licenses issued for the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises are issued only to a legitimate restaurant or private club;
(4)
The protection of schools, homes, churches, parks, and other institutions;
(5)
Protection of the public health, safety, and welfare.
(b)
To the maximum extent permissible under state and federal law, the business of selling alcoholic beverages shall, under this chapter, be considered to be a privilege to be accorded in conformity with the foregoing and other public policies of the city, rather than a right.
(Code 2003, § 6-1; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-101))
Sec. 4-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Alcoholic beverage means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Malt beverage means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Manufacturer means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Package means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Person means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Premises means on the property owned, leased or controlled by the holder of a license issued under this chapter and so connected with the business described in the license as to form a component or integral part of it.
Retail means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Retail dealer means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Wholesale dealer means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Wholesaler means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
(Code 2003, § 6-2; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-102))
Sec. 4-3. - Hours of sale.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale, at wholesale or retail, in or upon any licensed premises, any alcoholic beverages, except between the hours of 8:00 a.m. Monday and 11:59 p.m. Saturday of each week; provided, however, that in any licensed establishment which derives at least 60 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served and in any licensed establishment which derives at least 60 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging, alcoholic beverages for on-premises consumption may also be sold or offered for sale between 12:30 p.m. on Sundays until 12:00 midnight on Sundays.
(Code 2003, § 6-3; Ord. of 3-7-1989, § 1; Ord. of 1-4-2011, § 1)
Sec. 4-4. - Sale to persons under age.
It shall be unlawful for any person to furnish or cause to be furnished or to permit any such person in his employ to furnish any alcoholic beverage to a person in violation of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23, as amended.
(Code 2003, § 6-6; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-105))
Sec. 4-5. - Advertising restricted.
It shall be unlawful for the holder of any license issued under this chapter to advertise in any news media or by other means the fact that alcoholic beverages may be purchased at such establishment except as permitted by state law and by regulations promulgated thereunder.
(Code 2003, § 6-7; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-218))
Secs. 4-6—4-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSES

Sec. 4-29. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell or offer to sell any alcoholic beverages within the city except under a valid license issued under this chapter and in compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Code 2003, § 6-41; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-201); Ord. of 1-4-2011, § 3)
Sec. 4-30. - Qualifications.
No license required by section 4-29 shall be issued to:
(1)
Any person unless the person or his manager or the person actually in charge of the operation of the business is a resident of the county.
(2)
A person who, in the opinion and judgment of the city council, is not of good moral character in the community in which he resides.
(3)
A person who, by reason of reputation, character or mental or physical capacity, is not, in the judgment of the city council, a fit or qualified person to conduct such business.
(4)
A person found by the city council to have been convicted of any felony under federal or state law or any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude. A plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond shall be considered a conviction for purposes of this subsection.
(5)
A partnership, if any member of the partnership is disqualified under this section.
(6)
A corporation, if any officer, manager or director thereof would not be eligible to receive a license under this article for any reason other than nonresidence in the county.
(7)
A person whose business is conducted by a manager or agent, unless the manager or agent possesses the same qualifications required of the license holder.
(8)
A person who has been convicted of the violation of any federal or state law or regulation respecting the manufacturing, possessing or sale of any alcoholic beverage.
(Code 2003, § 6-42; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-202))
Sec. 4-31. - Types of licenses.
Licenses may be issued for the following activities:
(1)
Manufacturer of malt beverages.
(2)
Manufacturer of wine.
(3)
Wholesaler of malt beverages.
(4)
Wholesaler of wine.
(5)
Retail sale of wine, consumption on premises. This license permits the holder to allow the consumption on the premises of the holder of wine sold by the license holder.
(6)
Retail sale of wine, consumption off premises. This license permits the sale of wine only in unbroken packages and does not allow consumption on the premises.
(7)
Retail sale of malt beverages, consumption on premises. This license permits the holder to allow the consumption on the premises of the holder of malt beverages sold by the license holder.
(8)
Retail sale of malt beverages, consumption off premises. This license permits the sale of malt beverages only in unbroken packages and does not allow consumption on the premises.
(9)
Retail sale of distilled spirits, consumption on premises. This license permits the holder to allow the consumption on the premises of the holder of distilled spirits sold by the license holder.
(Code 2003, § 6-43; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-203); Ord. of 1-4-2011, § 4)
Sec. 4-32. - Fees.
The annual fee for all licenses required by this article shall be established from time to time by the city council by appropriate resolution.
(Code 2003, § 6-44; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-204))
Sec. 4-33. - Application procedure.
(a)
As a prerequisite to the issuance of any license under this article, the applicant shall furnish to the city clerk a complete set of fingerprints to be forwarded to the state bureau of investigation for disposition required by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2(c).
(b)
The applicant for a license under this article shall complete and return to the city clerk an application in a form approved by the city council.
(c)
No application for a license under this article shall be considered by the city council until the applicant shall have caused to be published, at such applicant's expense, a notice in the legal organ of the county one time at least ten days and not more than 21 days prior to the regular meeting of the city council at which such application is to be considered. Such notice shall contain the name of the applicant, the location of the proposed business, the type of license applied for and the date, time and location of the meeting of the city council at which the application will be considered.
(Code 2003, § 6-45; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-205))
Sec. 4-34. - Due process guidelines.
The granting or refusal of any license under this article shall be based upon the following factors:
(1)
The proximity of any other establishments engaged in the selling, distributing or manufacturing of alcoholic beverages.
(2)
The character of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location.
(3)
The proximity of churches, schools and playgrounds to the proposed location.
(4)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking for its patrons.
(5)
Whether the proposed location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom.
(6)
The information contained in the application.
(7)
The qualifications contained in section 4-30.
(8)
Whether any alcoholic beverage license previously issued for such location or to the applicant was ever revoked for cause by the city or state.
(9)
The number of existing alcoholic beverage licenses already issued in the city.
(10)
Compliance with zoning regulations.
(Code 2003, § 6-46; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-206))
Sec. 4-35. - Final decision and appeal.
All decisions approving or denying any license under this article shall be in writing with the reasons therefor stated and shall be mailed or delivered to the applicant. Upon written application delivered to the city clerk no less than one week after the decision of the council, any applicant aggrieved by such decision shall be afforded a hearing before the council within 30 days of such written application with an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine witnesses. After close of evidence, the council shall deliberate and render a written decision within ten days.
(Code 2003, § 6-47; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-207))
Sec. 4-36. - Closing for emergencies.
(a)
The police chief shall have the authority to close any location holding any license under this article for a period not in excess of 24 hours in the event there shall occur on the premises any violation of law, this chapter or any other ordinance of the city, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence, or other occurrence which is contrary to the public health, welfare or safety when, in the judgment of the police chief, such action is necessary to protect the public health, welfare or safety.
(b)
The police chief, with the concurrence of the city council, may close any location holding a license under this article in the event of disorders which threaten the public health, welfare or safety for the duration of the emergency.
(Code 2003, § 6-48; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-208))
Sec. 4-37. - Suspension by mayor.
The mayor may, upon recommendation by the police chief, suspend any license issued under this article pending the hearing provided for in this article, where, in the judgment of the mayor, such action is necessary to protect the public health, welfare and safety. Any such decision shall be in writing and delivered to the license holder.
(Code 2003, § 6-49; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-209))
Sec. 4-38. - Suspension and revocation.
Any license issued under this article may be suspended for a period not to exceed one year or revoked in its entirety by the city council.
(Code 2003, § 6-50; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-210))
Sec. 4-39. - Grounds for suspension and revocation.
The following shall be grounds for the suspension and revocation of a license issued under this article:
(1)
The violation by the license holder or his manager of any state, federal or city law or regulation involving moral turpitude or pertaining to alcoholic beverages. The determination of whether or not such violation has occurred shall be made by the city council and an actual conviction in a court shall not be necessary in order to suspend or revoke the license.
(2)
The failure of the license holder or his manager to report with reasonable promptness to the police department any violation of law, this chapter or any other ordinance of the city, breach of peace, disturbance or altercation, occurring on the premises, or the removal of any customer from the premises by the license holder or his manager.
(3)
Any conduct on the part of the license holder or his manager contrary to the public welfare, safety or health or in a manner constituting a nuisance.
(4)
If subsequent investigation reveals false statements on the application for such license.
(5)
Upon failure of the business or the operation or location thereof, or the owner or any person connected therewith, to continue meeting the qualifications for the issuance of such license as set forth in section 4-30.
(6)
Where, in the judgment of the city council, the operation of the business by the license holder constitutes a nuisance, or poses problems of law enforcement and the maintenance of public order because of the number of fights and/or being frequented by known or reputed drug dealers and/or illegal sales or use of controlled substances and/or breaches of peace and/or violations of the law by patrons of the license holder in or about the premises.
(Code 2003, § 6-51; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-211))
Sec. 4-40. - Procedures.
(a)
Prior to the revocation or suspension of a license other than the suspension provided for in sections 4-36 and 4-37, the license holder shall be given notice in writing to show cause, before the city council at a time and place specified therein but not less than two days nor more than 14 days from the date of the service of the notice, why the license should not be revoked or suspended, as the case may be, stating the grounds therefor.
(b)
At the appointed time and place the license holder shall have an opportunity to show cause, if any exists, why the license should not be suspended or revoked, after which the city council shall take such action as it, in its judgment and discretion, shall deem appropriate under the facts. The decision of the city council shall be in writing and mailed or delivered to the license holder.
(Code 2003, § 6-52; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-212))
Sec. 4-41. - Final decision and appeal.
All decisions suspending or revoking any license under this article shall be in writing with the reasons therefor stated and shall be mailed or delivered to the license holder. Upon written application delivered to the city clerk no less than one week after the decision of the council, any license holder aggrieved by such decision shall be afforded a hearing before the city council within 30 days of such written application with an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine witnesses. After close of the evidence, the council shall deliberate and render a written decision within ten days.
(Code 2003, § 6-53; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-213))
Sec. 4-42. - Transfer of license.
All licenses issued under this article shall be personal to the licensee to whom issued. Upon proper application, the city council may permit the license to be transferred upon payment of a transfer fee equal to one-half the original license fee, provided the transferee meets all of the qualifications which would have been required had the transferee been an applicant for a license under this article. Upon the death of any license holder, his personal representatives or his heirs may continue to operate under the license for the balance of the year with the payment of any fee, if otherwise qualified as license holders, or the license may be transferred to a qualified transferee within 30 days after the date of death of the license holder upon payment of the transfer fee set forth in this section.
(Code 2003, § 6-54; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-214))
Sec. 4-43. - Sale of interest by license holder.
Where the license holder under this article is a partnership or a corporation, one or more partners or stockholders may withdraw from the partnership or corporation in favor of one or more of the partners or stockholders who were partners or stockholders at the time of the issuance of the license. The sale by a partner or a stockholder of his interest in the partnership or corporation to a person who is not at the time of the sale already a partner or stockholder shall constitute grounds for revocation of the license unless the new partner or stockholder is a person qualified to hold a license, and unless the transaction is approved by the city council.
(Code 2003, § 6-55; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-215))
Sec. 4-44. - Change of location.
The location named in a license issued under this article shall not be changed. Any request for a change in location shall be considered as a new application and is subject to the provisions of this article regarding applications for licenses under this article.
(Code 2003, § 6-56; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-216))
Sec. 4-45. - Duration.
All licenses issued under this article shall expire at 12:00 midnight on December 31 of the year in which issued. All applications for renewal of the license for the ensuing year shall be treated as applications for new licenses, except that they need not be advertised as required by this article.
(Code 2003, § 6-57; Ord. of 2-4-1986, § 1(3-217))
Secs. 4-46—4-63. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - CONSUMPTION ON THE PREMISES

Sec. 4-64. - Grant of license for sale of alcoholic beverages.
A license for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises where sold may be granted only to a restaurant, as defined under section 4-65, or a bona fide private club, as defined under section 4-66.
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-91))
Sec. 4-65. - Restaurant.
In order to be eligible for a pouring or limited pouring license for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises, a restaurant shall:
(1)
Be used and held out to the public as a place where meals prepared on the premises are regularly served to the public for adequate pay each day the establishment is open for business;
(2)
Contain one or more public dining rooms, with adequate and sanitary kitchen facilities and staff to prepare, cook, and serve suitable food for its guests;
(3)
Have employees available to serve meals prepared on the premises during any time the establishment is open for business;
(4)
Have a valid county health department food service permit and any other applicable local, state, or federal permits, licenses, etc., required for food service establishments;
(5)
On an annual basis, have at least 60 percent of its total sales comprised of the sale of food prepared on the premises and nonalcoholic beverages consumed on the premises; and for this purpose if a restaurant makes a minimum charge, cover charge, or admission charge, or any other nonfood or nonalcoholic beverage charge, the amount so charged shall not be counted in computing total sales and shall not be counted as a food or beverage sale; and
(6)
Have a permanent seating capacity at tables, not counting bar stools, of at least 50 persons.
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-92))
Sec. 4-66. - Bona fide private club.
(a)
In order to be eligible for a pouring or limited pouring license for the sale of mixed drinks for consumption on the premises, a bona fide private club must be a veterans organization, or a country club (whether established for profit or nonprofit), which meets all of the requirements of this section, and all of such organizations must be incorporated under state law, and:
(1)
The local chapter or entity must have been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license.
(2)
Have at least 75 regular dues-paying members.
(3)
Be organized and operated exclusively for pleasure, recreation, and other such purposes.
(4)
Own, hire, or lease a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining space and equipment;
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests; and
c.
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving in the form of salary or other compensation any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
(b)
For purposes of subsection (a)(4)c of this section, the term "fixed salary" means the amount of compensation paid any member, officer, agent, or employee of a private club as may be fixed for revenue of the club and shall not include any commission or any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(c)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be granted to a bona fide private club organized or operated primarily for the selling or serving of alcoholic beverages.
(d)
Bona fide private clubs licensed under the provisions of this section shall not be required to maintain the percentage sale of food/nonalcoholic beverages as compared to alcoholic beverages; however, any such organization shall be subject to all regulations of this chapter dealing with general licensing and consumption on the premises.
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-93))
Sec. 4-67. - Ratio of alcoholic/nonalcoholic sales.
Except as otherwise provided in this article, should the total sales from food and nonalcoholic beverages reported by any licensee for the sale of mixed drinks for consumption on the premises where sold not comply with the requirements of section 4-65(5) for any 12-month period, the license may be suspended or revoked, or, if an application for renewal is pending, renewal may be denied.
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-94))
Sec. 4-68. - Illumination of premises; location of sales restricted.
(a)
All restaurants, including all tables, booths, and other areas where customers are served, and including all passageways for customers, shall be sufficiently well-illuminated so that they may be viewed by those inside the premises.
(b)
The sale of alcoholic beverages in any back room or side room which is not open to the general public is prohibited, except that this prohibition shall not apply with respect to:
(1)
Private parties which have been scheduled in advance;
(2)
Room service to hotel guests in their hotel rooms, provided a properly licensed restaurant is located on the premises; and
(3)
Private clubs.
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-95))
Sec. 4-69. - Hours of sale.
Consumption on the premises licensees, including, but not limited to, licensees selling mixed drinks for consumption on the premises where sold, shall only be authorized to sell alcoholic beverages for on-premises consumption during the hours that the restaurant or private club is open for business, but in no event shall any alcoholic beverages for on-premises consumption be sold or offered for sale between 12:00 midnight on Sundays and 8:00 a.m. on Mondays; provided, however, that in any licensed establishment which derives at least 60 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served and in any licensed establishment which derives at least 60 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging, alcoholic beverages for on-premises consumption may also be sold or offered for between 12:30 p.m. on Sundays until 12:00 midnight on Sundays.
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-97))
Sec. 4-70. - Removal of beverages prohibited.
(a)
All alcoholic beverages sold by consumption on the premises licensees, including, but not limited to, the sale of mixed drinks for consumption on the premises where sold, shall be consumed only on the licensed premises. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove from the licensed premises any alcoholic beverages, including mixed drinks, sold for consumption on the premises. Each licensee shall be responsible for ensuring that no person removes any alcoholic beverages, including mixed drinks, from the premises in any type of container, except as otherwise provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-6-4 (removal of partially consumed bottle of wine from premises).
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or have in his possession an open container of any alcoholic beverage on any public street, sidewalk, park, or other public place within the city, or upon or within any motor vehicle on the streets, sidewalks, parks, and public places of the city, except as otherwise provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-6-4 (removal of partially consumed bottle of wine from premises).
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-98))
Sec. 4-71. - Sexually oriented entertainment.
(a)
No license holder under this article shall conduct, stage, arrange, or permit upon premises on which alcoholic beverages are sold or dispensed for consumption the following:
(1)
Any live performances which constitute or simulate:
a.
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation, or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law;
b.
The touching, caressing, or fondling of the breast, buttocks, anus, or genitals; or
c.
The displaying of any portion of the female breasts below the top of the areola or the displaying of any portion of any person's pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva, or genitals.
(2)
The use of artificial devices or inanimate objects to perform, simulate, or depict any of the prohibited conduct or activities described in subsection (a) (1) of this section.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall be interpreted to prohibit performances in mainstream performance establishments, museums, or theaters.
(Ord. of 1-4-2011, exh. A(6-99))
