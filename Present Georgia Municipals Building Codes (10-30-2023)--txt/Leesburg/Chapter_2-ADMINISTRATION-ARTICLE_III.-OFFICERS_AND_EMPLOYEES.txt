ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES[4]
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-176. - Duties.
Each officer shall perform all duties required of his office by state law, the Charter, and this Code, and such other duties not in conflict therewith as may be required by the city manager.
(Code 1980, § 2-102(1); Code 2003, § 2-171)
Sec. 2-177. - Oaths.
All officers and employees required by the Charter or some other provision of law to take an oath shall, before entering upon the discharge of their respective duties, take and subscribe the following oath before an officer authorized by law to administer oaths:
"I do solemnly swear that I will well and truly perform the duties of ____________ of the City of Leesburg, Georgia, and that I will support and defend the Charter thereof as well as the Constitution and laws of the State of Georgia and of the United States of America."
(Code 1980, § 2-103; Code 2003, § 2-172)
Sec. 2-178. - Bonds.
Except as otherwise provided by law, the council may require any department head, city official or employee, before entering upon the discharge of his duties, to give good and sufficient bond in any amount decided by the council. Such bond shall be payable to the city for the faithful performance of such duties and to secure against corruption, malfeasance, misappropriations or unlawful expenditures in office. Surety bonds shall be obtained from a surety company licensed to do business in the state and approved by the council. The premiums thereon shall be paid by the city.
(Code 1980, § 2-104; Code 2003, § 2-173)
Sec. 2-179. - Compensation.
Each officer and employee of the city shall receive such compensation as may be provided from time to time by resolution.
(Code 1980, § 2-105; Code 2003, § 2-174)
Sec. 2-180. - Personnel provisions.
Nothing in this Code or in the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance or resolution adopting, establishing or amending any job classification and compensation plan for the employees of the city or any other personnel rules, regulations, policies or procedures, which have been adopted by the council. All such ordinances are recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out in this Code.
(Code 2003, § 2-175)
Sec. 2-181. - Employment of personnel.
Each department head, subject to the approval of the city manager, shall have the authority to employ the necessary personnel, as authorized by council action, to perform the duties of his department, and it shall be the responsibility of each such department head to determine by the physician's report and the applicant's history whether such applicant has the necessary qualifications for the particular position applied for.
(Code 1980, § 2-603(1); Code 2003, § 2-176)
Sec. 2-182. - Physician's examination.
Applicants seeking employment shall complete a standard application form as provided by the city and shall pass a complete physical examination to be given by a licensed physician and paid for by the prospective employee. The physician shall enter his findings on a standard form to be provided by the city. This provision shall not apply to those employees hired in an emergency and for a period of less than ten working days.
(Code 1980, § 2-603(2); Code 2003, § 2-177)
Sec. 2-183. - Accidents.
Employees involved in, or having any knowledge of, any accident involving any other person employed by the city or any property or equipment owned or operated by the city shall immediately report the accident and pertinent information to their department head, who shall forward such information to the office of the city clerk. The city clerk shall record the information on three copies of the applicable accident form, one copy to be forwarded to the insurance carrier, one copy to the city attorney, and one copy retained on file in the city clerk's office.
(Code 1980, § 2-603(4); Code 2003, § 2-179)
Sec. 2-184. - Outside employment.
No municipal employee shall engage in any outside employment during any period in which he is being compensated to do work for the city.
(Code 1980, § 2-603(6); Code 2003, § 2-180)
Sec. 2-185. - Political activity.
Municipal employees may individually exercise their right to vote and privately express their political views as citizens, but no municipal employee shall solicit political campaign contributions or engage in or actively participate in any political campaign to the neglect of his official duties.
(Code 1980, § 2-603(7); Code 2003, § 2-181)
Sec. 2-186. - Strikes.
No municipal employee shall participate in or encourage any form of sit down, slow down, work stoppage, or strike against the city.
(Code 1980, § 2-603(8); Code 2003, § 2-182)
Sec. 2-187. - Smoking ban for city property.
(a)
Smoking will not be permitted indoors on property owned, operated or leased by the city, including, but not limited to, the following areas:
(1)
Employee work areas.
(2)
Employee break rooms, lounges, snack bars and restrooms.
(3)
Conference and meeting rooms, offices, hallways, courtrooms and other public assembly chambers.
(4)
Any vehicle or equipment owned, leased, or operated by the city.
(b)
"No Smoking" signs shall be placed conspicuously in city facilities to serve as a directive to employees and the general public.
(c)
In all cases the rights of nonsmokers to protect their health will take precedence over persons who desire to smoke. Voluntary compliance with these policies is desired. However, violation of these policies by employees may subject them to disciplinary action under provisions of the city personnel policies. Members of the public who smoke in restricted areas will be asked to comply with these policies by extinguishing all smoking materials. Persons refusing to extinguish smoking materials will be escorted from the building by appropriate law enforcement officials, and may be charged with a violation of this section or state law.
(Res. of 6-3-2003, § 1(2-183))
State Law reference— Georgia Smokefree Air Act of 2005, O.C.G.A. § 31-12A-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-188. - Duties of finance director/tax collector.
The finance director/tax collector's duties shall be assigned by and, unless otherwise dictated by the city Charter and other law, shall be subject to the discretion of the city manager.
(Code 1980, § 2-405; Code 2003, § 2-205)

DIVISION 2. - CLERK

Sec. 2-213. - Appointment.
The city clerk shall be appointed by the council.
(Code 1980, § 2-401; Code 2003, § 2-201)
Sec. 2-214. - Term of office.
The city clerk shall hold office at the pleasure of the council.
(Code 1980, § 2-40; Code 2003, § 2-202)
Sec. 2-215. - Bond.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city clerk shall give a good and sufficient bond, payable to the city council, such bond to be fixed and approved by the city council.
(Code 1980, § 2-40; Code 2003, § 2-203)
Sec. 2-216. - Duties.
The city clerk's duties shall be assigned by and, unless otherwise dictated by the city Charter and other law, shall be subject to the discretion of the city manager. The city manager shall serve at all times as ex officio city clerk.
(Code 1980, § 2-404; Code 2003, § 2-204)
Secs. 2-217—2-242. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - CITY ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-243. - Appointment and qualifications.
The city attorney shall be appointed by the city council, and shall serve until a successor is appointed and has qualified. No person shall be so appointed unless he is a member in good standing of the Georgia Bar and has been actively engaged in the practice of law for at least three years preceding his appointment.
(Code 1980, § 2-601(1); Code 2003, § 2-231)
Sec. 2-244. - Oath.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city attorney shall take the oath prescribed by this article for city officers.
(Code 1980, § 2-601(2); Code 2003, § 2-232)
Sec. 2-245. - Powers and duties.
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the city and in such capacity shall:
(1)
Advise the council or its committees or any city officer, when thereto requested, upon all legal questions arising in the conduct of city business.
(2)
Prepare or revise ordinances when so requested by the council or any committee thereof, and keep the Code up to date and properly indexed.
(3)
Give his opinion upon any legal matter or question submitted to him by the council, or any of its committees, or by any city officer.
(4)
Attend all council meetings in their entirety for the purpose of giving the council any legal advice requested by its members.
(5)
Prepare for execution all contracts and instruments to which the city is a party and approve, as to form, all bonds required to be submitted to the city.
(6)
Prepare, when authorized by the council, all charges and complaints against, and appear in the municipal court in the prosecution of every person charged with a violation of this Code or of a city ordinance or of any regulations adopted under authority of the Charter, and see to the full enforcement of all judgments or decrees rendered or entered in favor of the city.
(7)
Defend any and all suits and actions at law or equity brought against the city, unless otherwise directed by the city council.
(8)
Make an immediate report to the council of the outcome of any litigation in which the city has an interest.
(9)
Make an annual report to the council of all pending litigation in which the city has an interest and the status thereof.
(10)
Keep complete and accurate records of the following, which records shall forever remain the property of the city:
a.
All suits in which the city had or has an interest, giving the names of the parties, the nature of the action, the disposition of the case or its status, if pending, and the briefs of counsel.
b.
All written opinions prepared by the city attorney and all certificates or abstracts of titles furnished by him to the city, or any department or official thereof.
(11)
Render such other legal services as may be required by the city manager or council.
(Code 1980, § 2-601(3); Code 2003, § 2-233)
Sec. 2-246. - Compensation.
The city attorney shall submit to the council a monthly bill for his services, itemizing the type of work performed for the city and the number of hours engaged in each type of work during the month.
(Code 1980, § 2-601(4); Code 2003, § 2-234)

DIVISION 4. - CITY ENGINEER

Sec. 2-271. - Appointment.
The city engineer may be appointed by the city manager, by and with the advice and consent of the city council, and shall serve until a successor is appointed and has qualified.
(Code 1980, § 2-602(1); Code 2003, § 2-261)
Sec. 2-272. - Oath.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city engineer shall take the oath prescribed by this article for city officers.
(Code 1980, § 2-602(2); Code 2003, § 2-262)
Sec. 2-273. - Duties.
The city engineer shall advise the council and city officials on all engineering matters referred to him and shall, from time to time as required by the city manager or council, make reports regarding public improvements, repairs of streets, bridges, and sidewalks, and prepare such other reports as the city manager or council may request. He shall keep accurate maps, plats, and records of all public works, lands, or property owned by the city, and perform such other duties as may be imposed upon him from time to time by the city manager or council.
(Code 1980, § 2-602(3); Code 2003, § 2-263)
Sec. 2-274. - Compensation.
The city engineer shall submit to the council a monthly bill for his services, itemizing the type of work performed for the city and the number of hours engaged in each type of work during the month.
(Code 1980, § 2-602(4); Code 2003, § 2-264)
Secs. 2-275—2-296. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - DISCIPLINE, APPEALS AND GRIEVANCES

Sec. 2-297. - Employee discipline, appeals and grievances.
Discipline, appeals and grievances shall be governed under the provisions as published in the city personnel handbook and, where applicable, in accordance with the standard operating procedures of the police department, both of which are on file in the city manager's office and available for public inspection.
