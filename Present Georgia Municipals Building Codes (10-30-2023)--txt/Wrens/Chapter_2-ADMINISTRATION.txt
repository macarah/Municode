ARTICLE II. - LEGISLATIVE BODY

Sec. 2-19. - Compensation.
(a)
The mayor shall be compensated for services to the city in the amount of $4,800.00 annually and shall receive no expense allowance. However, the mayor may receive his actual and necessary expenses incurred in the performance of his duties of office.
(b)
Each councilmember shall be compensated for his services to the city in the amount of $2,400.00 annually and shall receive no expense allowance. However, each councilmember may receive his actual and necessary expenses incurred in the performance of his duties of office.
(Code 2002, § 2-1-1; Ord. No. 02-007-2004, § 1, 7-13-2004)
Sec. 2-20. - Regular meetings, special meetings.
(a)
All regular meetings of the mayor and council shall be held at the city hall annex building located at 223 Broad Street on the third Tuesday of each month at 6:00 p.m.
(b)
Special or called meetings may be had at any time upon the call of the mayor or any two councilmembers; provided, however, that not less than 24 hours' notice shall be given to the mayor and councilmembers orally or in writing and that notice of the time, place and purpose of the called meeting shall be posted in a conspicuous place on the bulletin board in city hall not less than 24 hours in advance of the special or called meeting.
(c)
Any business may be transacted at a regular meeting or called meeting if all members of the council are present and such action is unanimous, but if any member of council is absent, then only such business as was stated in the agenda can be transacted.
(Code 2002, § 2-1-11; Ord. No. 01-002-2004, § 3, 2-13-2004; Ord. No. 01-008-2007, § 3, 8-14-2007; Ord. No. 2017-11-02 , § 2, 11-7-2017)
Sec. 2-21. - Quorum.
A quorum must be present for conducting meetings of the city council. A quorum is three members of the city council.
Sec. 2-22. - Adjourned meetings.
If a quorum shall fail to attend any regular or special meeting of the mayor and council or if for any reason any meeting shall fail to complete the transaction of business scheduled for the meeting, the meeting may be adjourned to any date prior to the next regular meeting. Said date shall be agreed upon by a majority of the members present.
(Code 2002, § 2-1-12)
Sec. 2-23. - Order of business.
(a)
The order of business at all regular meetings shall be as follows:
(1)
Approval of minutes of last meeting;
(2)
Approval of the financial statement;
(3)
Unfinished/old business;
(4)
New business;
(5)
Councilmember reports;
(6)
Staff reports;
(7)
Ordinance readings; and
(8)
Executive session.
(b)
If the mayor and council direct any matter to be the special business of a future meeting, that matter shall have precedence over all other business at that future meeting.
(c)
No proposition shall be entertained by the mayor and council until it has been seconded, and every proposition shall, when required by the mayor or any councilmember, be reduced to writing.
(Code 2002, § 2-1-13)
Sec. 2-24. - Rules of procedure.
Except as otherwise provided by ordinance, the parliamentary procedures of the mayor and council shall be governed by the current edition of Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised (11th edition).
(Code 2002, § 2-1-14)
Sec. 2-25. - Previous questions.
Previous questions may be called at any time by a majority of the councilmembers present. The ayes and nays may be called for by any councilmember.
(Code 2002, § 2-1-15)
Sec. 2-26. - Motions having precedence.
(a)
When a question is under consideration no motion shall be received except as follows:
(1)
To lay on the table;
(2)
To postpone to a time certain;
(3)
To postpone indefinitely;
(4)
To refer to a committee;
(5)
To amend;
(6)
To strike out or insert; or
(7)
To divide.
(b)
Motions for any of these purposes shall have precedence in the order named.
(Code 2002, § 2-1-16)
Sec. 2-27. - Motion to adjourn.
A motion to adjourn shall always be in order and shall be decided without debate.
(Code 2002, § 2-1-17)

ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES

Sec. 2-58. - City clerk; duties.
In addition to the duties of the city clerk under the city Charter, the city clerk shall perform the following duties:
(1)
Be the custodian of the city seal and affix its impression on documents whenever required;
(2)
Preserve the codes, records and documents belonging to the city and maintain a proper index to all records and documents;
(3)
Receive all money due the city, including taxes, licenses and fees, and payout the same only upon orders of the mayor and council; and
(4)
Perform any other duties as may be required by the mayor and council.
(Code 2002, § 2-4-1)
Sec. 2-59. - City attorney; duties.
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the city and in that capacity shall:
(1)
Prepare ordinances when so requested by the mayor and council;
(2)
Prepare for execution all contracts and instruments to which the city is a party when so requested and approve, as to form, all ordinances, bonds and city contracts; and
(3)
Render any other legal services as may be required by the mayor and council.
(Code 2002, § 2-4-2)
Sec. 2-60. - City administrator; qualifications; duties; removal.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the city administrator to execute and administer the policies of the city government fixed by the mayor and council, and to have authority over all administrative matters of the city and to be accountable only to the mayor and council. The city administrator shall be selected by the mayor and council on the basis of ability, experience in or his knowledge of practice in respect to the duties of his office and experience and familiarity in dealing with state and federal agencies in city-related matters. During his tenure of office, the city administrator shall reside within the city, normal moving and transfer of residence time excepted, and shall devote his entire time to the duties of his office and shall not engage in any other business or profession.
(b)
The city administrator shall:
(1)
See that the laws of the state and the ordinances, resolutions, rules and regulations of the city are faithfully executed and enforced;
(2)
Prepare the agenda and attend meetings of the mayor and council and recommend for adoption any measure deemed proper and suitable for the city;
(3)
Make reports to the mayor and council from time to time upon the affairs of the city and keep the mayor and council fully advised of the city's financial condition and its future needs;
(4)
Correspond with state and federal agencies and departments, regarding state or federal qualifications or rules for the city, or grants or loans available to the city, and report such correspondence to the mayor and council;
(5)
Upon the direction and advice of the mayor and council, appoint and suspend or remove all city employees, except the municipal court judge, associate municipal court judge, city attorney, city treasurer, or city clerk, in accordance with such general rules, regulations, policies or ordinances as the mayor and council may adopt;
(6)
Prepare and submit the annual budget and capital program to the mayor and council;
(7)
Direct and supervise the administration of all departments, properties, and operations of the city, subject to the general direction and control of the mayor and council;
(8)
Investigate the affairs, records, accounts, and expenditures of the various commissions and boards created by the city and report thereon at least once a year to the mayor and council;
(9)
Have the right to request the council, advice or opinion of the city attorney concerning any matter affecting the interest of the city;
(10)
Act in a liaison capacity on behalf of the mayor and council with boards, offices, agencies, and commissions of the city;
(11)
Attest contracts, deeds, licenses, and other public documents on behalf of the city with the city clerk as an alternate attesting signatory, after approval of the mayor and council;
(12)
Exercise any other powers and perform any other duties required or authorized by the mayor and council and not inconsistent with the Charter, code or ordinances of the city.
(c)
Removal of city administrator.
(1)
The mayor and council may remove the city administrator by a majority vote of all of its members by adopting a resolution to that effect. In the event the administrator is sought to be removed by the action of the mayor and council, he shall be entitled to a written notice specifying the ground or grounds for removal, with termination to become effective not sooner than 30 days from receipt of said notice. The city administrator may, within ten days after notice of adoption of such resolution, reply in writing and may request a public hearing, which shall be held not earlier than 15 or later than 25 days after the filing of such request. Following a public hearing, if requested, the decision of the mayor and council in removing the city administrator shall be final.
(2)
When there shall be a vacancy in the office of the city administrator, the duties and functions of the city administrator shall be discharged by the mayor and council.
(Code 2002, § 2-4-3)

DIVISION 2. - PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 2-131. - Planning commission: creation; composition; terms.
The city planning and zoning commission, hereinafter the "planning commission," is hereby created and established. The planning commission shall consist of seven members who shall be appointed by the mayor and council. The term of the members shall be for five years, except that in the appointment of the first planning commission pursuant to this article, the members shall be appointed for varying terms in order that future terms shall be staggered.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-1; Ord. No. 001-007-2014, § 3, 7-1-2014)
Sec. 2-132. - Planning commission vacancies; compensation.
Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the mayor and council who shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause. All members shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-2)
Sec. 2-133. - Removal of planning commission members for nonattendance.
Any member who fails to attend five consecutive meetings of the planning commission shall be conclusively presumed to have resigned and the post may be declared vacant by the city council.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-3)
Sec. 2-134. - Officers; staff; expenditures; meetings; records of the planning commission.
The planning commission shall elect a chairperson from its members. The term of the chairperson shall be for one year, and he shall be eligible for re-election as chairperson. The planning commission shall be entitled to receive such assistance from the employees of the city as it shall request and shall be authorized to secure such professional assistance on a full-time or part-time basis as it may in its discretion deem necessary or appropriate, provided that the expenditures therefore shall be not in excess of such amounts as may be appropriated therefore by the mayor and council. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and shall determine its time of meeting. All meetings of the planning commission at which official action shall be taken or discussed shall be open to the public and all records of the planning commission shall be public records and shall be kept in the custody of the city clerk.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-4)
Secs. 2-135—2-151. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DOWNTOWN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-152. - Declaration of necessity for a Downtown Development Authority.
There is hereby determined and declared an urgent existing and future need for a development authority (as more fully described and defined in the Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.) to function in the city for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in said city thereby promoting the general welfare of its citizenry.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-11)
Sec. 2-153. - Activation of the Downtown Development Authority.
There is hereby activated in the city the public body corporate and politic known as the "Downtown Development Authority of the City of Wrens" which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Downtown Development Authorities Law.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-12)
Sec. 2-154. - Downtown development area defined.
The "Downtown Development Area," as defined in O.C.G.A. § 36-42-3(3), shall consist of all areas within the city limits which are zoned commercial as provided by the City of Wrens' Unified Development Ordinance, as amended. The city may modify the designated Downtown Development Area by subsequent resolution.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-13; Ord. No. 01-007-2004, § 1(6-1-13), 7-13-2004)
Sec. 2-155. - Composition of authority; qualifications; officers; training.
(a)
The Downtown Development Authority of the city shall consist of a board of seven directors. Directors shall be:
(1)
Taxpayers residing in the city;
(2)
Owners or operators of businesses located within the Downtown Development Area who are taxpayers residing in the county; or
(3)
Persons having a combination of the qualifications specified in subsection (a)(1) or (2) of this section. Not less than four of the directors having the qualifications provided herein shall be persons who, in the judgment of the city council, either have or represent a party who has an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization of the Downtown Development Area.
(b)
Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section, one director appointed to the board may reside outside the county; provided, however, that such appointed director owns a business within the Downtown Development Area and is a resident of the state. If subsequently to his appointment to the board pursuant to this subsection, the director ceases to own a business within the Downtown Development Area or reside in the state, such director shall relinquish his seat on the board.
(c)
The city council may appoint one of its elected members as a director of the Downtown Development Authority.
(d)
The directors shall elect one of their members as chairperson and another as vice-chairperson and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may but need not be a director. The directors shall receive no compensation for their services but shall be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred by them in the performance of their duties.
(e)
Directors shall attend and complete training on downtown development and redevelopment as provided by O.C.G.A. § 36-42-7, as amended.
(f)
A majority of the board of directors shall constitute a quorum.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-14; Ord. No. 01-007-2004, § 1(6-1-14), 7-13-2004)
Sec. 2-156. - Terms of Downtown Development Authority board of directors.
Except for a director who is a member of the city council, the terms of all directors shall be four years. Successors to the directors shall be appointed by the city council. The term of a director who is also a councilmember shall end when such director is no longer a member of the city council. If at the end of any term of office of any director a successor to such director has not been elected, the director whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until a successor is elected.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-15; Ord. No. 01-007-2004, § 1(6-1-15), 7-13-2004)
Sec. 2-157. - Organization; powers; duties of the Downtown Development Authority.
The board of directors hereinbefore elected shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Downtown Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Code 2002, § 6-1-16)

Sec. 2-185. - Social security coverage extended.
It is hereby declared to be the policy and purpose of the city to extend at the earliest date possible under the agreement entered into by the state and the federal Social Security Administration, to the employees and officials thereof, and its instrumentalities, not excluded by law or by this section and sections 2-186 and 2-187, the benefits of the System of Old Age and Survivors Insurance as authorized by the federal Social Security Act, the State Enabling Act, and amendments thereto. The mayor, or other chief executive officer, of the city is hereby authorized and directed to execute all necessary agreements and amendments thereto with the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia for coverage of said city employees and officials as provided for in section 2-185, to include any employees that are in positions covered by a public retirement system, in the manner provided by said law. Specifically, employees covered by the Georgia Firemen's Pension Fund are included in the city's social security program.
(Code 2002, § 2-4-12)
Sec. 2-187. - Social security appropriations by city.
There shall be appropriated from general funds such amounts at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations for employer's contributions and administrative expenses, and such funds shall be paid over to the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia in accordance with regulations established by said agency.
(Code 2002, § 2-4-14)

ARTICLE VII. - FINANCE

Sec. 2-213. - Fiscal year.
The fiscal year of the city shall begin on January 1 and end on December 31 of each year, and all normal and usual budgetary planning and projections shall coincide with such fiscal year.
(Code 2002, § 2-5-1)
Sec. 2-214. - Annual budget; appropriations.
An annual budget and an appropriations ordinance shall be adopted by the mayor and council prior to the first day of the fiscal year. However, if for good and sufficient reasons the budget cannot be adopted by the first day of the fiscal year, the budget shall be adopted not later than 45 days subsequent to the beginning of the fiscal year. If the budget and the appropriations ordinance are not adopted prior to the beginning of the fiscal year, this section automatically authorizes the continuation of necessary and essential expenditures to operate the city. The budget as adopted shall be a balanced budget with anticipated revenues (including appropriated unencumbered surplus) equal to appropriated expenditures. All funds within the budget shall also be balanced.
(Code 2002, § 2-5-2)
Sec. 2-215. - Purchasing.
The city administrator and city clerk shall be the purchasing agents of the city and shall:
(1)
Arrange and negotiate the purchase or contract for all equipment, supplies and contractual services for the city, and sell or otherwise dispose of all surplus city equipment and supplies with authorization by the mayor and council; and
(2)
Maintain an inventory of all materials, supplies or equipment owned by the city.
(Code 2002, § 2-5-3)
