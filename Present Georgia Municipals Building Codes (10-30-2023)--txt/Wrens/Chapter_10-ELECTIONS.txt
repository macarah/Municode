Chapter 10 - ELECTIONS

Sec. 10-1. - Election officials.
The city clerk is hereby appointed chief registrar and superintendent of elections, to serve in both capacities at the will of the mayor and council. The clerk may receive additional compensation for his services in either capacity at the direction of the mayor and city council. The clerk's powers and duties shall be all of those set forth for those positions in the Georgia Election Code.
(Code 2002, § 2-2-1)
Sec. 10-2. - Voter registration.
Voter registration shall be as set forth in the Georgia Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.).
(Code 2002, § 2-2-2)
State Law reference— Voter registration, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-224.
Sec. 10-3. - Notice of candidacy; filing dates.
Notices of candidacy shall be filed as set forth in the Georgia Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.).
(Code 2002, § 2-2-3)
Sec. 10-4. - Qualification fees.
The qualification fee for candidates filing a notice of candidacy in any general or special election shall be $144.00 for the office of mayor and $72.00 for the office of city councilmember, which fees shall be received by the municipal election superintendent at the time of filing the notice of candidacy and paid over to the city.
(Code 2002, § 2-2-4)
State Law reference— Qualification fees, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-131.
Sec. 10-5. - Election district; polling place.
The city shall constitute one, and only one, election district. The polling place shall be the community house at 101 McNair Street in the City of Wrens.
(Code 2002, § 2-2-5)
Sec. 10-6. - Plurality determines.
All candidates shall be elected by a plurality of the votes cast.
(Code 2002, § 2-2-6)
