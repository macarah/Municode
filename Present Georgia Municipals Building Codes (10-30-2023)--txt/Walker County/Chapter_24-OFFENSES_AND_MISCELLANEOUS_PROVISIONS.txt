Sec. 24-1. - Public indecency.
(a)
Purpose. The ordinance from which this section is derived has been enacted for the purpose, among others, of:
(1)
Protecting and preserving the health, safety, welfare and morals of the citizens of the County;
(2)
Protecting societal order;
(3)
Reflecting moral disapproval of people appearing in the nude among strangers in public places; and
(4)
Proscribing public nudity across the board.
(b)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Accessible to the public means any area or place, including, but not limited to, any building, structure or business, where the public is invited and is free to go upon special or implied invitation, including, but not limited to, such an area or place available to all or a certain segment of the public.
Nudity means:
(1)
The showing of the human male or female genitals, pubic area, or buttocks with less than a fully opaque covering;
(2)
The showing of the female breast with less than a fully opaque covering of any part of the nipple; or
(3)
The showing of covered male genitals in a discernibly turgid state.
Public place means any area or place, including, but not limited to, any building, structure or business, accessible to the public, regardless of whether privately or publicly owned.
Showing means pointing out or drawing attention to.
(c)
Prohibited acts. A person shall be prohibited, while in a public place, from knowingly or intentionally:
(1)
Engaging in sexual intercourse;
(2)
Engaging in deviate sexual conduct;
(3)
Appearing in a state of nudity; or
(4)
Fondling the genitals or himself or another person.
(d)
Violations; penalties. It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in any of the prohibited conduct prescribed in this section. Any person who violates this section shall be subject to section 1-13.
(Code 2005, § 38-1; Ord. of 4-13-1993, §§ I—III)
Sec. 24-2. - Disorderly conduct.
Any person who shall do or engage in any of the following in the unincorporated area of the County shall be guilty of disorderly conduct:
(1)
Any person who shall endanger the lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence or threats of bodily harm.
(2)
Any person who shall cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another or public property.
(3)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or others and cause, provoke or engage in any fight or brawl.
(4)
Any person who shall collect in bodies or in crowds and engage in unlawful activities.
(5)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or others and engage or attempt to engage in gaming.
(Code 2005, § 38-2; Ord. of 12-10-1991, § 1)
Sec. 24-3. - Sale and distribution of methadone.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to establish, own or operate a business which has as its purpose the sale or distribution of the controlled substance methadone within the unincorporated areas of the County.
(b)
Whenever any provision of this section has been or is being violated, the appropriate County authority, in addition to other remedies, may institute injunction or other appropriate actions or proceedings and prosecute the same to final judgment to correct or abate such violation.
(Code 2005, § 38-3; Ord. of 5-27-1990, §§ II, III)
Sec. 24-4. - Adult bookstores and movie houses.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Adult bookstore means any commercial establishment which derives at least 10 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of books, publications, films or other media which depict nudity or sexual conduct.
Adult movie house means any movie theater which, on a regular continuing basis, shows films rated X, XX, XXX or any combination of Xs by the Motion Picture Association or any movie theater which presents for public viewing on a regular continuing basis so-called "adult films" depicting sexual conduct.
Nudity means the showing of the human male or female genitals or pubic area with less than a fully opaque covering or the depiction of covered male genitals in a discernibly turgid state.
Sexual conduct means acts of masturbation, homosexuality, sodomy, sexual intercourse or physical contact with a person's clothed or unclothed genitals, pubic area, buttocks or, if such person is female, breast, which, to the average person, applying contemporary community standards, taken as a whole, lacks serious literary, artistic, political, or scientific value and predominantly appeals to the prurient interest, that is, shameful or morbid interest in nudity or sex.
(b)
Unlawful activity. It shall be unlawful for any person to own, establish, operate or maintain an adult bookstore or adult movie house in the unincorporated areas of the County except in compliance with section 22-327.
(c)
Violations; penalties.
(1)
Any person who violates this section shall be guilty of an offense and, upon conviction in the Magistrate Court of the County, shall be punished according to section 1-13.
(2)
Whenever any provision of this section has been or is being violated, the appropriate County authority, in addition to other remedies, may institute injunction or other appropriate actions or proceedings and prosecute the same to final judgment to correct or abate such violation.
(Code 2005, § 38-4; Ord. of 2-17-1987, §§ 2—4)
Sec. 24-5. - Pawnbrokers.
(a)
Any pawnbroker operating a business in the unincorporated areas of the County shall keep a book in which shall be legibly written, in ink or type, in the English language, at the time of each loan, purchase, sale, or receipt of personal property:
(1)
The name of the person conducting the transaction;
(2)
The number of the receipt or pawn ticket;
(3)
If the payment is made by check, the number of the check issued for the purchase price or loan;
(4)
The maturity date of the transaction;
(5)
The signature of the customer;
(6)
An accurate account and description of the goods, articles or things pawned, purchased or received;
(7)
The amount of monies loaned or advanced thereon or paid therefor;
(8)
The dates, and the times, including both days and hours of pawning, purchasing or receiving such goods, articles or things; and
(9)
The names, age, residences and descriptions of the general appearance of the persons pawning, selling or delivering such goods, articles or things, and the distinctive number from the customer's driver's license or similar identification card.
No entry made in such book shall be erased, obliterated or defaced. The book required herein shall be kept and used for the sole and limited purpose of complying with this section.
(b)
Every pawnbroker shall have a correct copy of all entries made in the book required by the preceding subsection during the immediately preceding 7-day period available for inspection at the place of business by the Sheriff's Department at 1:00 p.m. on Thursday of each week.
(c)
The book required by subsection (a) of this section, as well as every article or thing pawned, pledged, sold or deposited, shall be open to inspection by the Sheriff's Department during normal business hours when the pawn shop is open to the public.
(d)
No pawnbroker shall take in pawn, pledge or as security, or purchase, any article from any person under 18 years of age.
(e)
If any person is found to be the owner of stolen property which has been pawned, pledged or sold to a pawnbroker, such property shall be returned by the pawnbroker to the owner thereof without the payment of the amount advanced by the pawnbroker or any costs or charges of any kind which the pawnbroker may have placed upon the same. Stolen property shall be surrendered to any Law Enforcement Officer only upon presentation of a police incident report which contains an accurate description of the stolen property sufficient to identify the property which has allegedly been stolen.
(Code 2005, § 38-5; Ord. of 4-12-1988, §§ 1—6)
