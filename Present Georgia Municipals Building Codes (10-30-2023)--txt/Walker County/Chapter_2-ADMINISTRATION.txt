ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - License denied.
A license shall be denied whenever it can be shown that a licensee under this Code no longer maintains adequate financial responsibility upon which issuance of the license was conditioned or whenever the licensee has defaulted in any obligation, of any kind whatsoever, lawfully owing to the County.
(Code 2005, § 2-1; Ord. of 7-27-1993, § 6.1(c))
Sec. 2-2. - Monies on deposit.
(a)
All monies of the County on deposit in any bank or banking institution shall be placed in a general fund, except that monies for County payroll purposes shall be placed in a payroll fund, and monies for the payment and compensation of jurors shall be placed in a jury fund.
(b)
All books and records showing the deposit and expenditures of monies of the County shall be kept in accordance with the foregoing provisions of this section.
(Code 1983, § 2-1; Code 2005, § 2-2)
State Law reference— County depositories, O.C.G.A. § 36-6-16 et seq.
Sec. 2-3. - Land acquisitions; relocations; easements.
(a)
The County will make every reasonable effort to acquire real property by negotiated purchase before instituting eminent domain proceedings.
(b)
The County will not require any owner to surrender possession of real property until the applicant pays, or causes to be paid, to the owner:
(1)
The agreed purchase price arrived at by negotiation; or
(2)
In any case where only the amount of the payment to the owner is in dispute, not less than 75 percent of the appraised fair value as approved by the applicant and concurred in by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), on all projects which are funded either in whole or in part by HUD.
(c)
The County will not require any person lawfully occupying property to surrender possession without at least 90 days' written notice from the applicant of the date on which possession will be required.
(d)
Land and real property referred to in this section shall include easements.
(Code 1983, § 2-2; Code 2005, § 2-3)
State Constitution reference— Eminent domain, Ga. Const. art. IX, § 2, ¶ 5.
State Law reference— Eminent domain, O.C.G.A. § 22-1-1 et seq.; acquisition and loss of property, O.C.G.A. § 44-5-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-4. - Method of hospitalization of the mentally ill.
The medical admission provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 37-3-40 through 37-3-44, 37-3-6 through 37-3-65, 37-3-8 through 37-3-82 and 37-3-84 shall be the only applicable method of hospitalization of the mentally ill in the County.
(Code 1983, § 2-3; Code 2005, § 2-4)
State Law reference— Regional housing authorities, O.C.G.A. § 8-3-100 et seq.
Sec. 2-5. - Council of governments.
The Board of Commissioners is authorized to become a member of the Chattanooga Area Regional Council of Governments, a general welfare corporation, and to subscribe on behalf of the County to its purposes and objectives, including the collection of data and preparation of plans and programs for land use, transportation, housing, economic development, natural resource development, community facilities, sewer systems, garbage disposal, public health, safety and morals, in cooperation with the membership of the Chattanooga Area Regional Council of Governments.
(Code 1983, § 2-5; Code 2005, § 2-6)
Sec. 2-6. - Limestone Valley Resource Conservation and Development Council.
The Board of Commissioners, acting for and on behalf of the County, establishes and authorizes the County to become a sponsoring member of the Limestone Valley Resource Conservation and Development Council.
(Code 1983, § 2-6; Code 2005, § 2-7)

DIVISION 2. - HISTORIC PRESERVATION COMMISSION

Sec. 2-76. - Historic Preservation Commission.
(a)
Created. There is created a Commission whose title shall be the Walker County Historic Preservation Commission (hereinafter referred to as the "Commission").
(b)
Position within County government. The Commission shall be part of the planning functions of the County.
(c)
Membership; compensation.
(1)
The Commission shall consist of 7 members appointed by the Chairperson with approval by the Board of Commissioners pursuant to ch. 00, § 10, (2017 Ga. Laws (Act No. 165), § 10). All members shall be residents of the County and shall be persons who have demonstrated special interest, experience or education in history, architecture or the preservation of historic resources.
(2)
To the extent available in the County, at least 3 members shall be appointed from among professionals in the disciplines of architecture, history, architectural history, planning, archaeology or related professions.
(3)
Members shall serve 3-year terms. Members may not serve more than 2 consecutive terms. In order to achieve staggered terms, initial appointments shall be 1 member for 1 year; 3 members for 2 years; and 3 members for 3 years.
(4)
Members shall not receive a salary, although they may be reimbursed for expenses.
(d)
Powers. The Commission shall be authorized to:
(1)
Prepare and maintain an inventory of all property within the County having the potential for designation as an historic property.
(2)
Recommend to the Board of Commissioners specific districts, sites, buildings, structures or objects to be designated by ordinance as historic properties or historic districts.
(3)
Review applications for Certificates of Appropriateness, and grant or deny the same in accordance with the provisions of this division.
(4)
Recommend to the Board of Commissioners that the designation of any district, site, building, structure or object as an historic property or as an historic district be revoked or removed.
(5)
Restore or preserve any historic property acquired by the County.
(6)
Promote the acquisition by the County of facade easements and conservation easements, as appropriate, in accordance with the provisions of the Georgia Uniform Conservation Easement Act (O.C.G.A. §§ 44-10-1 through 44-10-5).
(7)
Conduct educational programs on historic properties located within the County and on general historic preservation activities.
(8)
Make such investigations and studies of matters relating to historic preservation, including consultation with historic preservation experts, the Board of Commissioners or the Commission itself as it may, from time to time, deem necessary or appropriate for the purposes of preserving historic resources.
(9)
Seek out local, State, Federal or private funds for historic preservation, and make recommendations to the Board of Commissioners concerning the most appropriate uses of any funds acquired.
(10)
Submit to the Historic Preservation Division of the Department of Natural Resources a list of historic properties or historic districts designated.
(11)
Perform historic preservation activities as the official agency of the County Historic Preservation Program.
(12)
Employ persons, if necessary, to carry out the responsibilities of the Board of Commissioners.
(13)
Receive donations, grants, funds, or gifts of historic property and acquire and sell historic properties. The Historic Preservation Commission shall not obligate the County without prior consent.
(14)
Review and make comments to the Historic Preservation Division of the Department of Natural Resources concerning the nomination of properties within its jurisdiction to the National Register of Historic Places.
(15)
Participate in private, State and Federal historic preservation programs and, with the consent of the Board of Commissioners, enter into agreements to do the same.
(e)
Adoption of rules and standards. The Board of Commissioners shall adopt rules and standards for the transaction of its business and for consideration of applications for designation and Certificates of Appropriateness, such as bylaws, membership provisions, and design guidelines and criteria. The Board of Commissioners shall have the flexibility to adopt rules and standards without amendment to this division. The Board of Commissioners shall provide for the time and place of regular meetings and a method for the calling of special meetings. The Chairperson, with the approval of the Board of Commissioners, shall select such officers as it deems appropriate from among its members. A quorum shall consist of a majority of the members.
(f)
Conflicts of interest. The Historic Preservation Commission shall be subject to all conflict-of-interest laws set forth in statutes and in the County Code.
(g)
Authority to receive funding from various sources. The Historic Preservation Commission shall have the authority to accept donations and shall ensure that these funds do not displace appropriated governmental funds.
(h)
Records. A public record shall be kept of the Board of Commissioners' resolutions, proceedings and actions.
(Code 2005, § 34-143; Ord. of 8-21-2002, § III)

DIVISION 3. - PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 2-96. - Created.
There is hereby established the County Planning Commission, to be governed by this division.
(Code 1983, § 12-13; Code 2005, § 34-31; Ord. of 10-18-2012(2), § 34-31)
Sec. 2-97. - Terms of office; vacancies; compensation.
(a)
Members. The County Planning Commission shall consist of 12 members, appointed by the Chairperson with approval by the Board of Commissioners pursuant to ch. 00, § 10 (2017 Ga. Laws (Act No. 165), § 10).
(b)
Terms. The terms of the members shall be for 4 years, except that in the appointment of the first Planning Commission under the terms of this division, the first 3 members shall be appointed for a term of 1 year, the second 3 members shall be appointed for a term of 2 years, the third 3 members shall be appointed for a term of 3 years and the remaining 3 members shall be appointed for a term of 4 years.
(c)
Vacancies. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the Chairperson, with approval by the Board of Commissioners, who shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing if requested by the member, including, but not limited to:
(1)
Failure to attend at least 8 regular voting meetings of the Planning Commission in a calendar year, without justification. To be excused, members of the Planning Commission shall notify the Planning and Development Department or Planning Commission Chair when they intend to be absent from a meeting;
(2)
Failure to maintain permanent residence within the jurisdiction of Walker County; or
(3)
Violation of Planning Commission rules of procedure or bylaws or Walker County's land development regulations by a member.
(d)
Payment to the Planning Commission members. All members shall be paid $50.00 per meeting attended. Members of the Planning Commission shall be reimbursed for travel expenses associated with their duties at a rate per meeting attended as set by the Board of Commissioners.
(Code 1983, § 12-14; Code 2005, § 34-32; Ord. of 10-18-2012(2), § 34-32)
Sec. 2-98. - Organization; rules; staff; finances.
(a)
The Planning Commission Chair shall be appointed by the Chairperson, with approval of the Board of Commissioners. The term of the Chair shall be 1 year, with eligibility for reappointment.
(b)
The Planning Director shall serve as the Planning Commission's Secretary and may delegate the performance of the tasks required of the Secretary to his subordinates.
(c)
The Planning Commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting.
(d)
The Planning Commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with the State Planning Agency, city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require. The expenditures of the Planning Commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the Board of Commissioners.
(Code 1983, § 12-15; Code 2005, § 34-33; Ord. of 10-18-2012(2), § 34-33)
State Law reference— Conduct of meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.; state office of planning and budget, O.C.G.A. § 45-12-72 et seq.; state department of community affairs, O.C.G.A. § 50-8-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-99. - Powers and duties.
From and after the time when the Planning Commission shall have organized and selected its officers and shall have adopted its rules of procedure, said Commission shall have all the powers, duties and responsibilities set forth in the General Planning and Zoning Enabling Act, 1957 Ga. Laws (Act. No. 358), page 420, as amended, notwithstanding the repeal of said Act as general law.
(Code 1983, § 12-16; Code 2005, § 34-34; Ord. of 10-18-2012(2), § 34-34)
Sec. 2-100. - Flood insurance.
(a)
The Board of Commissioners ensures the Federal Insurance Administration that it takes the following legislative action:
(1)
Appoints the County Planning Commission with the responsibility, authority and means to:
a.
Delineate or assist the Planning Director, at his request, in delineating the limits of the areas having special flood hazards on available local maps of sufficient scale to identify the locale of building sites.
b.
Provide such information as the Planning Director may request concerning present uses and occupancy of the floodplain area.
c.
Maintain for public inspection and furnishing, upon request, with respect to each area having special flood hazards, information on elevations (in relation to mean sea level) of the lowest floors of all new or substantially improved structures.
d.
Cooperate with Federal, State and local agencies and private firms which undertake to study, survey, map and identify floodplain areas, and cooperate with neighboring communities with respect to management of adjoining flood areas in order to prevent aggravation of existing hazards.
e.
Submit, on the anniversary date of the community's initial eligibility, an annual report to the Planning Director on the progress made during the past year within the community in the development and implementation of floodplain area management measures.
(2)
Takes such other official action as may be reasonably necessary to carry out the objectives of the program.
(b)
The Board of Commissioners appoints the County Planning Commission with the overall responsibility, authority and means to implement all commitments made in this section.
(Code 1983, § 12-17; Code 2005, § 34-35)
Sec. 2-101. - Official name.
The official name of the Planning Commission shall be the Walker County Planning Commission.
(Code 1983, § 12-24; Code 2005, § 34-61; Ord. of 10-18-2012(3), § 34-61)
Sec. 2-102. - Officers.
The Chair of the Planning Commission is appointed by the Chairperson of the Board of Commissioners, with the approval of the Board of Commissioners. Annually, at the regular meeting of the Planning Commission held in the month of January, the Planning Commission shall elect a Vice-Chair. The officers may succeed themselves. The Chair shall decide all points of order and procedure, unless directed otherwise by a majority of the Planning Commission subject to these bylaws. The Chair shall have the authority to determine the need to call for a roll call vote when a voice vote is inconclusive. The Chair shall direct the Planning and Development Department to prepare the agenda for each meeting, to transmit reports, plans and recommendations of the Planning Commission to the appropriate Governing Authority and, in general, to act as spokesman for the Planning Commission.

Have the custody of all funds coming into the possession of the Commission and shall submit all funds collected to the Board of Commissioner's Office; and

Prepare the placement of appropriate legal notices as required by the resolutions and ordinances of Walker County.
(Code 1983, § 12-28; Code 2005, § 34-65; Ord. of 10-18-2012(3), § 34-65)

DIVISION 2. - FIRE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-204. - Creation.
A Department to be known as Walker County Fire Rescue is created. The objective of the Department shall be the prevention of fire and the protection of life and property within the authorized fire protection limits of the County.
(Ord. of 11-16-2006, § 23-2.1)
Sec. 2-205. - Officers.
(a)
The Fire Department shall consist of a Chief, Deputy Chief, Assistant Chief, and others as the Chief, and Board of Commissioners may deem necessary for effective operation of the Department.
(b)
The Chief shall be appointed by the Chairperson of the Board of Commissioners for an indefinite period of time, and his tenure of office shall depend on his good conduct and efficiency. The Chief shall be technically qualified by training and experience and shall have the ability to command personnel and to hold their respect and confidence. He shall be removed from office only for poor work performance and may request a public hearing during the Board of Commissioners' meeting. The Fire Chief is additionally considered "career staff" and is therefore subject to section 2-208.
(c)
The Chief shall be held accountable for the efficient operation of the Department and shall make written and verbal reports to the Chairperson of the Board of Commissioners as may be required. All other Department and company officers shall be accountable to the Chief only.
(d)
The Deputy and Assistant Chiefs and all other Department and company officers shall be appointed by the Chief. Such officers shall be accountable only to the Chief and subject to removal by him, to be approved by the Chairperson.
(Ord. of 11-16-2006, § 23-2.2)
Sec. 2-206. - Duties of the Chief.
(a)
The Fire Chief shall formulate a set of rules and regulations to govern the Department and shall be responsible to the Chairperson for the personnel, morals, and general efficiency of the Department.
(b)
The Chief shall determine the number and type of companies of which the Department is to be composed and shall determine the response of such companies to alarms.
(c)
The Chief shall, at least once a month, conduct or cause to be conducted suitable drills or instruction in the operation and handling of equipment, fire aid and rescue work, salvage, a study of buildings in the authorized fire protection limits, fire prevention, water supplies, and all other matters generally considered essential to good firefighting and safety of life and property from fire.
(d)
The Chief is required to assist the proper authorities in suppressing the crime of arson by investigating or causing to be investigated the cause, origin and circumstances of all fires.
(e)
The Chief or his authorized representative is empowered to enter any and all buildings and premises at any reasonable hour for the purpose of making inspections and to serve notice upon the owner of occupant to abate, within a specified time, any and all fire hazards that may be found.
(f)
Any person so served with a notice to abate any fire hazard shall comply with such notice and promptly notify the Chief.
(g)
The Chief shall see that complete records are kept of all fires, inspections, apparatus and minor equipment, personnel and other information about the work of the Department.
(h)
The Chief shall report monthly to the Chairperson the condition of the apparatus and equipment; the number of fires during the month, their location and cause, and date and loss occasioned by the fire; the number and purpose of all other runs made; the number of members responding to each fire or other run; and any changes in membership.
(i)
The Chief shall make a complete official annual report to the Chairperson within 60 days after the close of the fiscal year, such report to include the information specified in subsection (g) of this section, together with comparative data for previous years and recommendations for improving the operation of the Department.
(Ord. of 11-16-2006, § 23-2.3)
Sec. 2-207. - Chief of Fire Department (Fire Marshal); police powers.
The Fire Chief or Fire Marshal shall have authority to exercise police powers in the enforcement of any provision of this Code or any other ordinance pertaining to the Fire Department, right-of-way, inspection of buildings, and fire escapes, or other provisions or ordinances pertaining to fire protection.
(Ord. of 11-16-2006, § 23-2.4)
Sec. 2-208. - Career staff and volunteers.
(a)
Career staff. In order to provide proper fire protection to the citizens of Walker County and to ensure there is at least 1 unit available for incident response, a career staff has been established. All career personnel are considered full-time and, after serving a probationary period, are permanent employees. These employees can only be removed by just cause or for poor work performance. Poor work performance must be documented through the Department evaluation form.
(1)
Status of present full-time employees and new hires. Regular employees having served in their current positions for 6 months or more prior to adoption of the personnel policies shall have permanent status. Employees with less than 6 months' continuous employment in their current positions shall serve a probationary period as defined in the policies allowing for time served previously in probationary status for the position. Firefighter probation periods will be extended until State certification is received in Georgia Firefighter Standards and Training and Emergency Medical Technician—Intermediate.
(2)
Objective. The probationary period shall be regarded as an integral part of the selection process and shall be utilized for closely observing the employee's work, for securing the most effective adjustment of a new employee to his position and for rejecting any employee whose performance is not satisfactory.
(3)
Duration. For appointments and promotions, the probationary period shall normally be 1 year in duration. If the Fire Chief or supervisor requests an extension of the probationary period within 15 days prior to expiration, the Chairperson may extend the duration of such period and notify the employee.
(4)
Evaluation of performance. During the probationary period, the immediate supervisor and/or Fire Chief will frequently confer with the employee as to job-related goals and objectives for successful job performance and as to progress made and acceptability of work performance. At least 15 days prior to the expiration of the probationary period, the employee shall be notified whether he has satisfactorily completed the probationary period. If the probationary period has been extended, circumstances surrounding the decision will also be provided.
(b)
Volunteers. The volunteer membership of the Fire Department shall consist of such persons as may be appointed by the Fire Chief and shall be able-bodied citizens residing within the fire protection limits, or close proximity as to allow for a reasonable response time when summoned, preferably property owners whose business activities are normally within the confines of the fire protection limits and who preferably have telephones in their homes. Determination of whether candidates for appointment are able-bodied shall be made by the Chief after a medical and physical examination has been made in a manner prescribed by the Chief.
(c)
Suspension or discharge. Any member of the Department may be suspended or discharged from the Department by the Chief at any time he may deem such action necessary for the good of the Department.
(Ord. of 11-16-2006, § 23-2.5)
Sec. 2-209. - Special officers.
(a)
The Fire Department may elect a President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer, or any other officers necessary in any volunteer firefighter's club that they choose to organize. Such officers may be elected in any manner for any term the membership may decide upon, and their duties shall be to arrange for and manage any or all social or other functions sponsored by the Department.
(b)
The functions and duties of the special officers shall in no way interfere with those of the regular Department officers who are charged with the responsibility for all fire service activities of the Department.
(Ord. of 11-16-2006, § 23-2.6)

DIVISION 3. - POLICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-240. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this division, the terms, phrases, words and their derivatives, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. §§ 36-8-1 through 36-8-7. When not consistent with the context, the words used in the present tense shall include the future tense; words used in the plural number shall include the singular number; words used in the singular number shall constitute the plural number; and words in the masculine gender shall include the feminine gender. The term "shall" is always mandatory and not merely directory.
(Ord. of 7-22-1986, § 12.5-2)
Sec. 2-241. - Formation of County Police Force.
The Board of Commissioners hereby establishes the Walker County Police Force, to be governed by the provisions of this division and the applicable provisions of Georgia law set out in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated. The Chairperson shall appoint such number of County Police as in its discretion it deems proper and necessary. Any person appointed to the County Police Force shall possess the qualifications described in O.C.G.A. § 35-8-8. Any person so appointed by the Chairperson may be removed from office at any time at the will of the Board of Commissioners, with or without cause, at a salary fixed by the Chairperson.
(Ord. of 7-22-1986, § 12.5-3)
Sec. 2-242. - Powers of County Police, generally.
Under the direction and control of the Board of Commissioners, the County Police shall have:
(1)
The same power to make arrests and to execute and return criminal warrants and processes in Walker County as the Sheriff of Walker County has;
(2)
All the powers that the Sheriff of Walker County has as a Peace Officer as set out under the laws of the State of Georgia; and
(3)
The power to enforce ordinances of Walker County, Georgia.
(Ord. of 7-22-1986, § 12.5-4)
Sec. 2-243. - Rules and regulations for the management of County Police.
Each member of the County Police shall be subject to the rules and regulations governing all Walker County employees in addition to those statutory requirements governing law enforcement personnel as set out in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated. The Board of Commissioners may make such additional rules and regulations for the conduct, management, and control of such County Police.
(Ord. of 7-22-1986, § 12.5-5)
Sec. 2-244. - Appointment of Officers.
Each person appointed by the Chairperson to serve as a Police Officer for Walker County shall, before entering upon the duties of his office, take and subscribe to the oath prescribed by the laws of the State of Georgia and file it in said Board of Commissioners' Office. The Chairperson shall issue a Certificate of Appointment to each such person appointed as a County Police Officer and such Certificate of Appointment shall constitute the appointee's authority for exercising the powers and carrying out the duties conferred and imposed upon him by this division.
(Ord. of 7-22-1986, § 12.5-6)
Sec. 2-245. - Bond.
Each person appointed as a County Police Officer shall enter into a good and solvent bond in a sum of $1,000.00 conditioned upon the faithful performance of all his duties.
(Ord. of 7-22-1986, § 12.5-7)
