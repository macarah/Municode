Division 1 - GENERALLY

Sec. 30-5-111. - Licenses; taxes.
(a)
Nothing contained in this article shall exempt any person from payment of licenses or taxes as otherwise provided from time to time.
(b)
Other than as provided in section 30-5-116 of this article V, it shall be unlawful for any person to peddle or to engage in the business of peddler without first obtaining in force a license therefor as required by section 30-1-7 of this title and paying the appropriate fee therefor.
(Code 1972, § 15-121; Ord. No. 90-44, § 2, 9-11-90; Ord. No. 94-18, § 1, 6-14-94)
State Law reference— Peddlers and itinerant traders licensed by probate court, O.C.G.A. § 3-32-1 et seq.
Sec. 30-5-112. - Vehicles to keep moving.
Except as authorized by section 30-5-116 of this article V, all peddlers in the city, when using a vehicle of any sort to transport the goods, wares or merchandise they are offering for sale, shall keep said vehicle moving from place to place; and it shall be unlawful for any such peddler to park said vehicle and make sale from the parked vehicle on the streets, sidewalks or public ways of the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-122; Ord. No. 94-18, § 2, 6-14-94)
State Law reference— Stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200 et seq.
Sec. 30-5-113. - Stands prohibited on streets, etc.
Except as provided in section 30-5-116 of this article V, it shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation to peddle, sell or otherwise do or conduct business of any kind or maintain stands for vending goods or other articles on the streets, sidewalks or other public ways of the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-123; Ord. No. 94-18, § 3, 6-14-94)
Sec. 30-5-114. - Hours of operation.
Peddlers operating under this article shall do so only from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday.
(Ord. No. 03-17, § 1, 4-22-03)

Division 2 - CONCESSION STANDS

Sec. 30-5-116. - Definitions.
(a)
For the purpose of this article, a concession stand is defined as being a stand containing not more than twenty-five (25) square feet, designed and to be used for the purpose of selling and delivering sandwiches, hot dogs, hamburgers, non-alcoholic soft drinks and beverages, desserts and related food snacks at the fixed location defined in the license issued pursuant to the provisions hereof. Any such location must be within and be limited to that area bounded on the south by the north right-of-way of Broome Street; on the north by the south right-of-way of Haralson Street; on the west by the west property lines of Bull Street, West LaFayette Square and Church Street; and on the east by the east property lines of Main Street, East LaFayette Square and Ridley Avenue, each such location to meet the criteria hereinafter specified. Such license, when issued, shall be effective only insofar as the city is authorized to license or permit the same as applies to state roads, highways and rights-of-way under the jurisdiction of the state department of transportation. Any other licenses or permits required shall be the sole responsibility of the applicant/licensee.
(b)
A concession stand and any signage on or attached thereto must conform with design guidelines issued by the LaGrange Downtown Development Authority.
(Ord. No. 94-18, § 4, 6-14-94)
Sec. 30-5-117. - Regulations for licensees.
Each concession stand authorized and licensed pursuant to the provisions of this division shall be operated in accordance with and shall at all times meet the following criteria:
(1)
Each license sold shall be for a designated area.
(2)
Each concession stand must be of a cart design approved by the director of community and economic development of the city and must be capable of and will be removed from the public property or right-of-way daily at the close of business.
(3)
Such cart may sell only those items specified and named in its license and must have, prior to operation, and maintain during the entire time of operation, all appropriate certifications and approvals required by the health department or any agencies or departments of the state.
(4)
The concession stand authorized hereby shall:
a.
Be open and shall be operated only between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m. and shall not be motorized but can only be hand-pushed.
b.
The stand must not have any noise-making equipment.
c.
The neatness and cleanliness of the operator and the concession stand must at all times meet the reasonable criteria established by the department of community and economic development of the city and must be available for inspection at any time requested.
d.
The license required hereby may be annually renewed, contingent upon inspection and compliance with the provisions of this division.
e.
There shall be no trash, garbage, waste, etc., deposited or disposed of in the area around the concession stand except in approved and authorized receptacles.
f.
The owner must agree and must execute an agreement to indemnify, save and hold the city, its officers, agents or employees harmless of and from any claim, damages, costs expenses or other liability that may be imposed upon the city, its officers, agents or employees, resulting from or in any way connected with or reasonably related to the licensing or operation of the concession stand business contemplated hereby and will maintain liability insurance coverage for such purpose, with a company or companies acceptable to the city in the minimum amount of one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) per occurrence, which policy of insurance shall show the city, its officers, agents or employees, as additional insured and shall provide to and maintain with the city appropriate certification of insurance.
(5)
The business authorized by and the license to be issued pursuant to the provisions of this division shall not be operated less than two hundred fifty (250) feet, as measured along the most reasonable traveled route, of any other licensed food concession stand or within one hundred (100) feet, as measured along the most reasonable traveled route, of any licensed food service establishment in existence at the time the license contemplated hereby is to be granted. Additionally, any such concession stand will not be operated within a minimum distance of fifteen (15) feet of any existing or future authorized curb cuts, driveways or intersections; and no such concession stand shall obstruct any entrance to driveways and must at all times be self-sufficient for operating purposes. Under no circumstances will any connecting water lines, electrical lines or other utility connections be made to any such concession stand.
(Ord. No. 94-18, § 5, 6-14-94)

Division 3 - PERMIT

Sec. 30-5-126. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to go in or upon any private residence, apartment or premises in the city for the purpose of soliciting from the occupants thereof, or to canvass for orders of goods, wares, merchandise or services of any character or description, or for the purpose of offering to give or to furnish, or to give or furnish any goods, wares, merchandise or services to any such occupants to induce or invite such orders, without having first applied for and received from the chief of police a permit to do so.
(Code 1972, § 15-116)
Sec. 30-5-127. - Application.
Any person desiring to secure a permit under this division shall apply therefor in writing over his signature to the chief of police on forms provided by the city, and such application shall state the:
(1)
Name and address of the applicant;
(2)
Name and address of the person by whom employed;
(3)
Length of service of such applicant with such employer;
(4)
Place of residence and nature of the employment of the applicant during the preceding twelve (12) months;
(5)
Nature or character of the goods, wares, merchandise or services to be offered by the applicant; and
(6)
Personal description of the applicant.
Such application shall be accompanied by such credentials and other evidence of the good moral character and identity of the applicant as may be reasonably required by the chief of police.
(Code 1972, § 15-117)
Sec. 30-5-128. - Issuance; expiration.
If the chief of police shall determine after reasonable investigation that the applicant is of good moral character and proposes to engage in a lawful and legitimate commercial or professional enterprise, he shall issue the permit applied for, which permit shall expire on the first day of January next after the permit has been issued.
(Code 1972, § 15-118)
Sec. 30-5-129. - Carrying, exhibiting.
A permit issued under this division shall be carried at all times by the person to whom issued, when soliciting or canvassing in the city, and shall be exhibited by any such person whenever he shall be required to do so by any police officer or any person solicited.
(Code 1972, § 15-119)
Sec. 30-5-130. - Revocation.
Any permit issued under this division may be revoked by the chief of police for violations by the holder thereof of any of the ordinances of the city or of any state or federal law, or whenever the holder of such permit in the judgment of the chief of police ceases to possess the character and qualifications required by this article for the issuance of such permit.
(Code 1972, § 15-120)
