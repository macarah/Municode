ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Adoption of fire prevention code; enforcement.
(a)
In accordance with the Official Code of Georgia Annotated 25-2-4, the Georgia State Minimum Fire Safety Standards, as set forth in the Rules and Regulations of the State of Georgia, Chapter 120-3-3, as now or hereafter amended are applicable in Columbia County and are therefore incorporated herein by this reference as if fully set forth.
(b)
The Georgia State Minimum Fire Safety Standards, as implemented and enforced in Columbia County shall be known and may be referred to as the Columbia County Fire Prevention Code.
(c)
Copies of the Columbia County Fire Prevention Code shall be available through the office of the Columbia County Fire Marshal.
(d)
The Columbia County Fire Prevention Code shall be enforced by the Columbia County Fire Marshal, his or her designees, and any other duly authorized agents of Columbia County who may be empowered to enforce laws or issue citations.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Sec. 38-2. - Inspections required.
(a)
A representative of the office of the fire marshal shall inspect all work governed by the Columbia County Fire Prevention Code at such intervals that the fire marshal may deem appropriate. A final inspection shall be made of every building, structure, site or facility prior to its opening for use, business, or occupation.
(b)
The owner of any property upon which work governed by the Columbia County Fire Prevention Code will be conducted shall submit, or cause to be submitted on their behalf, plans and specifications for the work to be performed to the office of the fire marshal as a condition precedent to obtaining the necessary construction permits.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Sec. 38-3. - Stop work orders.
(a)
The fire marshal shall be authorized to issue written notice to immediately cease work on any project which the fire marshal determines, in his or her sole discretion, is not being performed in accordance with the provisions of this article, or is otherwise being performed in a dangerous or unsafe manner. The written notice may be delivered to the owner of the property, the owner's agent, or the party or parties performing work upon the property. The written notice shall state the conditions under which work may be resumed.
(b)
Emergency stop work orders. Where in the opinion of the fire marshal an emergency exists, an oral instruction from the fire marshal, or his or her designee, to cease work shall be followed by any party receiving such instruction. An emergency stop work order under this section may be lifted by the fire marshal, or his or her designee, upon correction of the emergency condition or extended by a written stop work order.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Sec. 38-4. - Fees.
Fees for plan review, inspection, reinspections, final inspections, and certifications or approvals for use or occupancy shall be as established from time to time by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Secs. 38-5—38-31. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OPEN BURNING

Sec. 38-32. - Open burning defined.
Open-burning means any outdoor fire from which the products of combustion are emitted directly into the open air without passing through a stack, chimney or duct.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Sec. 38-33. - Requirements and restrictions.
All open burning activities shall be conducted in strict accordance with the following standards, which are incorporated herein by this reference as if fully set forth:
(1)
The Rules and Regulations for Air Quality Control, chapter 391-3-1, as now or hereafter amended by the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, Environmental Protection Division, including, but not limited to the specifications and restrictions for open burning of Rule 391-3-1-.02(5).
(2)
The Georgia Forest Fire Protection Act, Official Code of Georgia, Annotated § 12-6-80, et seq. as now or hereafter amended including, but not limited to, the permit requirements of § 12-6-90.
(3)
The Georgia Prescribed Burning Act, Official Code of Georgia Annotated § 12-6-145, et seq. as now or hereafter amended.
The foregoing standards as implemented and enforced in Columbia County shall be known and may be referred to as the Columbia County Open Burning Code.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Sec. 38-34. - Enforcement.
The Columbia County Open Burning Code shall be enforced by the Columbia County Fire Marshal, his or her designees, and any other duly authorized agents of Columbia County who may be empowered to enforce laws or issue citations.
Any notices or permits that may be required under the state laws or regulations adopted by reference in the Open Burning Code must be given to or obtained from the appropriate state agent, as specified in the applicable state law or regulation.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Sec. 38-35. - Violations; penalties.
Any person who violates any provision of this article, or fails to comply with any requirements of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9.
(Ord. No. 03-4, § 1, 5-20-2003)
Secs. 38-36—38-39. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SPECIAL SERVICE DISTRICT

Sec. 38-40. - Created.
Pursuant to Article 9, Section 2, Paragraph 6 of the Constitution of the State of Georgia there is hereby created within the unincorporated portion of Columbia County, excluding that portion of such area within the boundaries of Fort Gordon, a special service district within which fire protection services and the construction, acquisition and maintenance of equipment and facilities therefore will be provided by Columbia County, Georgia with the cost thereof to be paid by an ad valorem tax to be levied and collected, beginning with calendar year 2004, within the special service district on all property located therein which is subject to ad valorem taxation by Columbia County. Columbia County may provide such fire protection services, equipment and facilities by contracting with one or more third party providers to provide the services, equipment or facilities or by providing such services with county owned equipment, facilities and/or employees or through any combination of these arrangements.
(Ord. No. 04-06, § 1, 7-9-2004)
