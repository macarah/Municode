Sec. 58-1. - Diving, etc., from bridges.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bridge means any structure crossing over a lake, stream, creek or pond or other impoundment of water, which structure is sufficient to support vehicular traffic, and is part of a state, county or federal road.
Diving or swimming means entering a lake, stream, creek or other impoundment of water from a bridge in any manner whatsoever.
Fishing means standing on or under, or in any way supporting one's self on, a bridge, while attempting to take fish from a lake, stream, creek or other impoundment of water.
(b)
Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to dive, fish or swim from any bridge in the county.
(c)
Sign. It shall be the duty of the county engineer to see that a sign is placed on either end of each bridge in the county which shall carry the words: "NO FISHING, SWIMMING OR DIVING FROM THIS BRIDGE," or similar words stating such prohibition.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-1; Ord. of 2-7-1978; Ord. of 7-16-1985)
Sec. 58-2. - Preventing or disrupting lawful meetings, gatherings or processions.
A person who recklessly or knowingly commits any act which may reasonably be expected to prevent or disrupt a lawful meeting, gathering or procession, is guilty of the offense of preventing or disrupting lawful meetings, gatherings or processions.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-2)
Sec. 58-3. - Loitering or prowling.
A person commits the offense of loitering or prowling when he is in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for law-abiding persons under circumstances that warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-3)
Sec. 58-4. - Public drunkenness.
A person who shall be and appear in an intoxicated condition in a public place or within the curtilage of any private residence not his own, other than by invitation of the owner or lawful occupant, which condition is made manifest by boisterousness, indecent conduct or act, or vulgar, profane, loud or unbecoming language, is guilty of the offense of public drunkenness.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-4)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 6.
Sec. 58-5. - Violation; penalties.
Any person who fails to comply with the provisions of this chapter, or the doing of any act prohibited by this chapter, shall be guilty of an offense and, upon conviction in the county magistrate court, shall be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-5; Ord. No. 00-10, § 8, 10-3-2000)
Sec. 58-6. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Artifice means an artful device or strategy, or trickery.
Assemble means to bring or gather into a group.
Befoul means to soil or make dirty.
Brawl means a noisy argument or fight.
Congregate means to bring or come together in a crowd.
Damage means to impair or injure the usefulness or value of a person or property.
Device means a plan or scheme.
Endanger means to expose to danger or harm.
Epithets means a derogatory or abusive word or phrase used as a descriptive substitute for the name or title of a person or thing.
Fighting word means a word used in an argument or quarrel which is assertive or combative.
Fraudulent scheme means a deliberate plan, deception or trick practiced so as to secure an unfair or unlawful gain over another person.
Gaming means gambling or betting money on the outcome of a game or contest, or to play a game of chance for stakes.
Hazardous means dangerous.
Lawful occupation means any regular employment which is allowed, established, sanctioned or recognized by law.
Lawful pursuit means any activity regularly engaged in which is allowed, established, sanctioned or recognized by law.
Obscene means offensive to the accepted standards of decency, or offensive or repulsive to the senses.
Physically offensive means disagreeable to the senses.
Provoke means to cause someone or something to take action.
Public property means any property which is maintained for or used by the people or community.
Riotous conduct means unrestrained, disorderly or uncontrolled behavior which causes a disturbance of the public peace.
Trick means an indirect, often deceptive or fraudulent means of achieving an end.
Tumultuous means confusedly or violently agitated so as to cause commotion, noise or a great disturbance.
Turmoil means utter confusion or agitation.
Unlawful activity means an activity engaged in which is not allowed, established, sanctioned or recognized by law.
Utter means to express by means of speech.
Violent means characterized or caused by great physical force or rough action.
(b)
Unlawful actions. Any person who shall do or engage in any of the following in the unincorporated areas of the county shall be guilty of disorderly conduct:
(1)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby any person is placed in danger of safety of his life, limb or health.
(2)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner towards another, whereby public property or property of another person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged.
(3)
Endanger lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence or threats of bodily harm.
(4)
Cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another or public property.
(5)
Assemble or congregate with another and cause, provoke or engage in any fight or brawl.
(6)
Collect in bodies or in crowds, and engage in unlawful activities.
(7)
Assemble or congregate with another and engage in or attempt to engage in gaming.
(8)
Frequent any public place and obtain money from another by an illegal and fraudulent scheme, trick, artifice or device, or attempt to do so.
(9)
Assemble with another and engage in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick to obtain a valuable thing in any place or from any person, or attempt to do so.
(10)
Utter any obscene word or epithet in a public place or any place open to the public.
(11)
Frequent any place where gaming or the illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages, narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed or tolerated.
(12)
Use fighting words directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates turmoil.
(13)
Assemble or congregate with another and do bodily harm to another.
(14)
By act of violence, interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation.
(15)
Congregate with another in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic, and refuse to clear such public way when ordered to do so by a peace officer or other person having authority.
(16)
Damage, befoul or disturb public property or the property of another so as to create a hazardous, unhealthy or physically offensive condition.
(17)
Assemble or congregate with others in a group or a crowd of more than five people in the parking lot or on the premises of a business establishment which is open and available to the public, not for the purpose of transacting business at the establishment, but for the purpose of socializing, hanging out or consuming products purchased inside the business establishment, so as to interfere with or hinder the operation of the business activities conducted on the property or in the establishment served by such parking lot or premises by taking up parking spaces which are intended to be available for customers of the business, hampering access to the business establishment or by the existence of a group or crowd in the parking lot or on such premises discouraging potential customers from entering the premises of the business establishment; provided that the owner, manager or operator of the business establishment has complained about the situation to the sheriff's department and the sheriff or his deputy has advised the people in the group or crowd of the complaint of the business establishment, requested all of those in the group or crowd to disperse and move out of the parking lot or on such premises and, after being given the opportunity to comply with the request, the person fails to remove himself from the parking lot or on such premises, or, in the alternative, the owner, manager or operator of the business establishment has posted and maintains, at points clearly visible from the place where the people are assembled or congregated, a sign containing the following information in easy to read print:
a.
Notice of this section;
b.
Identification of the property reserved for customer use only;
c.
Identification of the owner, manager or operator of the business establishment;
d.
Warning that violators will be prosecuted.
For purposes of determining whether a group or crowd of more than five people have assembled or congregated in a parking lot or on the premises, separate groups of less than five people engaging in the activity set forth in this subsection (b)(17) may be aggregated to determine the total number of people in the parking lot or on the premises participating in such activity.
(18)
Assemble or congregate with others in a group or a crowd of more than five people in the parking lot or on the premises of a business establishment which at the time is closed to the public, for the purpose of socializing, hanging out or other similar activities against the wishes of the owner, manager or operator of such business establishment; provided that the owner, manager or operator of the business establishment or his representative has complained about the situation to the sheriff's department and the sheriff or his deputy has advised the people in the group or crowd of the complaint of the business establishment, requested all those in the group or crowd to disperse and move out of the parking lot and, after being given the opportunity to comply with the request, the person fails to remove himself from the parking lot or, in the alternative, the owner, manager or operator of the business establishment or his representative has posted and maintained, at points clearly visible from the place where the people are assembled or congregated, a sign containing the following information in easy to read print:
a.
Notice of this section;
b.
Identification of the property on which assembling or congregating is not permitted;
c.
Identification of the owner of the business establishment;
d.
Warning that violators will be prosecuted.
For purposes of determining whether a group or crowd of more than five people have assembled or congregated in a parking lot or on the premises, separate groups of less than five people engaging in the activity set forth in this subsection (b)(18) may be aggregated to determine the total number of people in the parking lot or on the premises participating in such activity.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-6; Ord. of 4-11-1989; Ord. of 7-20-1994)
Sec. 58-7. - Obscene clothing.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Epithets means a derogatory or abusive word or phrase used as a descriptive substitute for the name or title of a person or thing.
Lewd means obscene; sexually unchaste.
Obscene means offensive to accepted standards of decency, or offensive or repulsive to the senses.
Outerwear clothing means shirts, tee shirts, blouses, pants, skirts, jackets, coats and any other article of clothing which is worn as an outer garment.
Profane means irreverent; serving to debase or defile what is holy; impure.
(b)
Unlawful wear. No person shall knowingly affix or attach to any piece of outwear clothing, or wear any piece of outwear clothing, which has affixed or attached to it any sticker, decal, emblem, painting, needlework or other device containing profane or lewd words or epithets describing sexual acts, excretory functions or parts of the human body.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-7; Ord. of 8-15-1989)
Sec. 58-8. - Use of tobacco products on property owned, leased or controlled by Columbia County, Georgia.
(a)
The following definitions shall apply to the following terms as they are used in this section:
(1)
Columbia County, Georgia Property, shall mean and include, but is not limited to, all buildings, land, parking lots, parking decks, greenspace, lawns, parks and any other improvements situated or located thereon owned, leased or controlled by Columbia County, Georgia with the exception of County owned roads which are part of the public road system, Wildwood Park, and existing designated smoking areas located at the Columbia County Detention Center, 2273 County Camp Road and located at County owned fire stations, which designated areas shall not be subject to the provisions of this section.
(2)
Tobacco product shall mean any product derived from cultivated tobacco, including but not limited to, cigarettes, cigars, bidis, kreteks and pipes.
(b)
Commissioners, officers, administrators, staff, employees, visitors, vendors, contractors and all others are prohibited from smoking of any tobacco product while on Columbia County, Georgia Property. Use of tobacco products in vehicles that are being operated on or are parked on Columbia County, Georgia Property is prohibited.
(c)
Any person who violates this section, shall, upon conviction be subject to the penalties set forth in section 1-9 of this Code.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.1-8; Ord. of 4-7-1993; Ord. No. 11-14, § 1, 6-22-2011)
Cross reference— Buildings and building construction, ch. 18.
Sec. 58-9. - Trespassing upon; tampering with any railroad switch; damaging or obstructing railroad property, equipment or machinery or getting on or off any locomotive engine, railroad car, trailer, container, equipment or machinery.
(a)
No person shall trespass upon the tracks, rights-of-way, yards, piggy-back facilities, buildings, trestles or other properties located in the unincorporated area of Columbia County and belonging to or in the possession of any railroad company operating in the unincorporated area of Columbia County.
(b)
No person shall tamper with or damage any railroad switch, wire, line, signal or other property located in the unincorporated area of Columbia County or place any object on railroad tracks which obstruct the movements or operation of a train or which could cause a train to be damaged or derailed while operating in the unincorporated area of Columbia County.
(c)
No person shall get on or off any locomotive engine, railroad car, trailer, container or other machinery or equipment located in the unincorporated area of Columbia County belonging to or in the possession of a railroad company operating in Columbia County.
(d)
This section shall not apply to any person acting with the authority or permission of the railroad company which owns or is in possession of the property in question.
(Ord. No. 04-09, § 1, 10-19-2004)
Sec. 58-10. - Panhandling.
(a)
Purpose. This ordinance regulates the time, place, and manner of solicitations and panhandling in the unincorporated areas of the county and shall not apply to anyone exercising their rights to constitutionally-protected activity.
(b)
Definitions. The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
(1)
Solicit or panhandle shall mean to request an immediate donation of money or other thing of value from another person, regardless of the solicitor's purpose or intended use of the money or other thing of value, including a request for employment, business, or contributions, or to request the sale of goods or services. This definition shall include all methods of solicitation including, but not limited to, spoken, written, or printed words, or by other means of communication including, but not limited to, bodily gestures.
(2)
Public area shall mean an area to which the public or a substantial group of persons has access, including, but not limited to, streets, highways, roadways (including shoulders and medians), bridges, the area above and below any bridge, sidewalks, alleys, parking lots and decks, plazas, parks, public greenspace areas, playgrounds, schools, transportation facilities, within a public-transportation vehicle, an area owned in whole or in part by, operated by, for, or under the custody and control of Columbia County, Georgia, and any other property where public gatherings occur on a regular basis, and any other property where public meetings are conducted.
(3)
Outdoor dining area shall mean an outdoor dining area of a restaurant or other establishment serving food or drink for immediate consumption.
(4)
Aggressive panhandling shall mean panhandling, as defined in this section, in any one or more of the following manners in a public area or on private property without having first obtained the permission of the owner or other person legally in possession of the property:
a.
Intentionally or recklessly making any physical contact with another person or vehicle in the course of soliciting without that person's express consent;
b.
Continuing to solicit from a person after that person has given a negative response to an initial solicitation;
c.
Approaching, speaking to, or following the person being solicited, if that manner of conduct is intended to, or is reasonably likely to cause a reasonable person to fear imminent bodily harm to that person or others in the area or the commission of a criminal act upon property in the person's possession, or is intended to, or is reasonably likely to intimidate the person being solicited into responding affirmatively to the solicitation;
d.
Intentionally or recklessly blocking the safe or free passage of the person being solicited, or requiring the person, or the driver of a vehicle, to take evasive action to avoid physical contact with the person making the solicitation;
e.
Intentionally or recklessly using violent, threatening, obscene, or abusive language or gestures that is intended to, or is reasonably likely to cause a reasonable person to fear imminent bodily harm to that person or others in the area or the commission of a criminal act upon property in the person's possession, or is intended to, or is reasonably likely to intimidate the person being solicited into responding affirmatively to the solicitation; and
f.
Soliciting a person at night.
(5)
At night shall mean the time period between 30 minutes after sunset to 30 minutes before sunrise.
(c)
Prohibited acts. It shall be unlawful for any person within the unincorporated areas of the county to commit any of the following acts.
(1)
No person shall engage in aggressive panhandling within the unincorporated areas of the county;
(2)
No person shall solicit or panhandle on private property without first having obtained the permission of the owner or other person legally in possession of the property.
(3)
No person shall solicit or panhandle on private property if the owner or other person legally in possession of the property has notified the person, by signage or otherwise, that they are not allowed to solicit on the property.
(4)
No person shall solicit or panhandle within 30 feet of any automated teller machine or any bank, financial institution, or check-cashing facility.
(5)
No person shall solicit or panhandle while under the influence of alcohol or a controlled substance, as defined by Georgia or federal law.
(6)
No person shall solicit or panhandle within ten feet of the entrance or exit of a building.
(7)
No person shall solicit or panhandle within 30 feet of an outdoor dining area.
(8)
No person shall solicit an operator or passenger of a motor vehicle while such person is operating or occupying a motor vehicle; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to services rendered in connection with the provision of emergency repairs requested by the operator or passengers of such vehicle.
( Ord. No. 23-01 MEETING , § 1, 3-7-2023)
