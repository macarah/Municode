ARTICLE VIII. - RETENTION OF RECORDS[8]

State Law reference— Records management plans for local governments, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90; records management plan required, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99(e).

Sec. 2-486. - Generally.
(a)
The clerk of the board of commissioners shall be responsible for retaining the records of the board of commissioners and the governmental offices and officers under the board of commissioners which maintain or are responsible for records. The board of commissioners shall establish and thereafter periodically review and modify as necessary or desirable records retention schedules. Establishment and review of records retention schedules will be based upon legal, fiscal, administrative and historical retention requirements and needs.
(b)
Each officer and official of the county and each division director and department head employed by the board of commissioners shall comply with the retention schedules adopted by the board of commissioners or established by state law, as the case may be. Such schedule shall include an inventory of the type of records maintained and the length of time each type of record will be maintained in the office or in a records holding area. Records retention schedules recommended to local government by the secretary of state through the department of archives and history shall be followed until modified or changed by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-47; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-61, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-487. - Maintenance.
All records required for day-to-day county office operation shall be maintained at the regular place of business of the office. All other records, until disposed of in accordance with the approved retention schedules, shall be maintained in such places as designated by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-48; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-62, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-488. - Historical records.
The board of commissioners shall designate an archival depository to store records determined to have permanent historical value in accordance with state laws.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-49; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-63, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-489. - Destruction, alienation, etc., of public records.
The destruction of records of the board of commissioners and the governmental offices and officers under the board of commissioners which maintain or are responsible for records shall occur only through the operation of an applicable retention schedule. The alienation, alteration, theft or destruction of any such records by any person in a manner not authorized by an applicable retention schedule is a misdemeanor.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-50; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-64, 12-16-1999)
