ARTICLE VI. - CABARETS

Sec. 10-260. - Scope of article; cabaret defined; license required.
(a)
In addition to all other license requirements of this Code, the following sections shall apply to the licensing and operation of a cabaret, which is defined as any establishment, indoor or outdoor, which provides entertainment (live or otherwise), or a place for entertainment, or a dance floor, or any facility for participatory entertainment, regardless of whether or not alcoholic beverages are served therein, provided this shall not apply to a motion picture or live theater unless alcoholic beverages are served therein.
(b)
A separate business license shall be required for the operation of a cabaret according to the business license schedule adopted from time to time by the city, provided a separate business license is required for the operation of the business initially. Should any section of this article conflict with other provisions of this Code, then the most restrictive provisions shall control.
(Code 1976, § 7-2091; Code 1985, § 6-150; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 9-8-1986)
Sec. 10-261. - Entertainment; hours.
Entertainment at a cabaret shall be permitted only from 8:00 a.m. until the following 2:00 a.m. each day, Monday through Saturday.
(Code 1976, § 7-2092; Code 1985, § 6-151; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 6-2-1986; Ord. of 9-8-1986)
Sec. 10-262. - Private guards.
Private security must be provided by the holder of a cabaret license at the holder's own expense as follows:
(1)
All establishments operating under a cabaret license shall have at least one private duty guard on the premises who shall be a person other than an owner, bartender, cook or cashier.
(2)
Any person so employed as a guard shall first submit to a records and identification check by the police department in the discretion of the police chief based on the results of the records and identification examination.
(3)
The cost of a records and identification examination and the issuance of a city identification card shall be $10.00 and shall be borne by the licensee.
(4)
Any person so employed as a guard shall not be allowed to carry any firearm in the performance of such person's duties unless such guard is an employee of a licensed security agency and is individually licensed by the state as a security guard.
(5)
All guards shall wear prominently displayed on their person an identification card or badge identifying them as guards and shall at all times carry on their persons the city identification card.
(6)
No person shall be employed as a guard unless such person shall be age 21 years or older.
(7)
Private security guards shall be on duty from 5:00 p.m. until the following 2:00 a.m. both Friday and Saturday nights.
(8)
Private security guards shall be given a copy of the relevant portions of this Code. It shall be their duty to enforce such ordinances, provided they shall not have the power to arrest or detain any offender but shall call for police assistance to affect any arrest or citation for violation of this Code.
(Code 1976, § 7-2093; Code 1985, § 6-152; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 6-2-1986; Ord. of 9-8-1986; Ord. of 3-6-1989)
Sec. 10-263. - Loitering.
(a)
For the purposes of a cabaret license, the term "loitering" means remaining outside the place of business of any cabaret license holder for a period of more than ten minutes; or, if a crowd of more than ten people has assembled, for any period of time longer than necessary to walk to one's automobile and drive directly from the premises.
(b)
Loitering outside the place of business of any cabaret license holder is prohibited at any time.
(c)
Violations of this section by customers of the cabaret license holder or members of the public not customers shall subject the customers or members of the public to punishment as for a misdemeanor.
(d)
The cabaret license holder shall be in violation of this section by permitting loitering or upon being notified that loitering is taking place outside such holder's place of business failing to take all reasonable steps to cause the same to cease.
(Code 1976, § 7-2094; Code 1985, § 6-153; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 9-8-1986)
