ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Chief appointing authority means the City of Austell, Georgia.
Board of adjustments and appeals means the city council.
Building, heating, air conditioning, electrical, swimming pool, and plumbing official, and flood control administrator means those officials appointed by the city council to administer such codes.
(Code 1976, § 10-1002; Code 1985, § 5-1; Ord. of 6-3-1991; Ord. of 12-18-1991; Ord. of 7-12-1993, § 11)
Sec. 8-2. - Condemnation board.
The city has created a condemnation board consisting of the fire chief or his designee, the building inspector, one councilmember and two citizens the electrical inspector, the plumbing inspector, the mayor and one citizen appointed by the city council for a two-year term beginning on January 1 of odd-numbered years. This board shall act as the building official for the purposes of enforcement of city unsafe building regulations the Unsafe Building Abatement Code. All decisions of the board shall be by majority vote of all its members and its recommendations shall be sent to the city for action.
(Code 1985, § 5-2; Ord. of 6-5-1989; Ord. of 8-6-1990, § 1; Ord. of 7-12-1993, § 12)

Sec. 8-4. - Codes remedial in nature.
The construction codes adopted herein are remedial in nature, and shall be construed to secure the beneficial interests and purposes thereof, which are public safety, health, and general welfare through structural strength, stability, sanitation, adequate light and ventilation, and safety to life and property from fire and other hazards attributed to the built environment including alteration, repair, removal, demolition, use and occupancy of buildings, structures, or premises, and by regulating the installation and maintenance of all electrical, gas, mechanical and plumbing systems.
Sec. 8-5. - Requirements not covered by adopted codes.
Any requirements necessary for the strength, stability or proper operation of an existing or proposed building, structure, electrical, gas, mechanical or plumbing system, or for the public safety, health and general welfare, not specifically covered by this chapter or the adopted construction codes, shall be determined by the building inspector.
Sec. 8-6. - Alternate materials and methods.
The provisions of the construction codes are not intended to prevent the use of any material or method of construction not specifically prescribed by them, provided any such alternate has been reviewed by the building inspector. The building inspector shall approve any such alternate, provided the building inspector finds that the alternate, for the purpose intended, is at least the equivalent of that prescribed in the construction codes, in quality, strength, effectiveness, fire resistance, durability and safety. The building inspector shall require that sufficient evidence or proof be submitted to substantiate any claim made regarding the alternate.
Sec. 8-7. - Liability of city.
The inspection or permitting of any building, system or plan, under the requirements of this chapter or the adopted construction codes shall not be construed in any court as a warranty of the physical condition of such building, system or plan or their adequacy. Neither the city nor any employee thereof shall be liable in tort for damages for any defect or hazardous or illegal condition or inadequacy in such building, system or plan, nor for any failure of any component of such, that may occur subsequent to such inspection or permitting.
Sec. 8-8. - Property fronting on a state highway; state highway department permit required.
No city building permit shall be issued for property fronting on a state highway unless a valid state highway department permit has been issued so that right-of-way control may be maintained.
(Code 1976, § 10-1006; Code 1985, § 5-6)
Sec. 8-9. - Structure moving.
(a)
Public service certificate and license. Only persons having a state public service commission certificate and public service license may move in the city houses or other buildings having a width greater than 12 feet and only in accordance with the restrictions provided in this section.
(b)
Notice; permit. Any person desiring to move in the city a house or other building of a width greater than 12 feet shall first give 24 hours' notice to the police department and obtain a moving permit therefor from the police chief.
(c)
Time limited. Houses or other buildings may be moved through the city only between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 3:00 p.m. and only on Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday.
(d)
Escort. Any person moving houses or other buildings through the city shall have at least two qualified escorts, one in front and one following the house or other building; city police will be available to act as escorts at such mover's expense, provided the duties of the police are not required elsewhere. The city police department may act as escorts for a fee in the amount provided in the city fee schedule. If the services of any other city department is required, the department shall be compensated in an amount determined the mayor.
(e)
Warning lights and signs. The escort preceding the moving structure must have a flashing light and a wide load sign on the front of the vehicle. The escort following must have a flashing light and a wide load sign on the rear of the vehicle.
(f)
Damages. Any damages caused by the moving of houses or other buildings, including damages to or the moving of traffic signals, wires, signs, and similar devices may be required to be paid by the mover prior to the time the house or other building leaves the city limits.
(g)
Violations. Violations of this section are punishable as provided in section 1-9, and any house or other building moved in violation of this section shall be immediately moved off the public right-of-way to the nearest space available, at the violator's expense, and held until this section is fully complied with.
(Code 1976, §§ 8-4001, 8-4007; Code 1985, § 5-8; Ord. of 3-6-1989)

ARTICLE II. - BUILDING PERMITS

Sec. 8-35. - Building permit fees.
Building permit fees shall be as provided in the fee schedule of the issuing department.
(Code 1976, § 10-1005; Code 1985, § 5-5; Ord. of 3-6-1989; Ord. of 6-5-2000, § 2, exh. A; 7-10-2000)
Sec. 8-36. - Purpose and intent; building official to enforce; building permits required.
(a)
Purpose and intent. The purpose and intent of this chapter is to:
(1)
Promote the general health, safety and welfare and to maintain required local uniformity with the administrative and technical requirements of the construction and maintenance codes adopted by the city;
(2)
Acquire information on the type, size, and location of all buildings and structures constructed, deconstructed, remodeled, removed, or demolished;
(3)
Exercise jurisdiction over the construction and inspection of new buildings, as well as additions that increase the physical dimensions of dwellings and commercial buildings, alterations to building structure or alterations to a building's heating, ventilating air conditioning, electrical or plumbing system;
(4)
Provide plan review and on-site inspection performed by persons designated by the building official; and
(5)
Establish and collect fees to defray the cost thereof.
(b)
Building official. There is hereby created the position of building official, who shall administer and enforce this chapter. The building official also has the following duties:
(1)
Issuing of building permits and certificates of occupancy in accordance with all provisions of this article.
(2)
Making field inspections to determine that the building or structure being constructed, reconstructed or structurally altered or used is being pursued in accordance with the site plan for which a building permit has been issued.
(3)
Making field inspections to ensure compliance of existing buildings, uses, and structures to provisions of this article.
(4)
Ensuring that all construction has been completed in accordance with all applicable Code requirements prior to allowing occupancy.
(5)
Enforcing provisions of this article through legal or other appropriate means to ensure compliance.
(6)
Administering consent variances with minor setback changes.
(7)
Issue temporary land use permits for construction or education mobile units. The temporary land use permit shall not exceed the time limits as set forth in this article.
(c)
Building permits required. Prior to commencing any of the following work, unless exempted by the city's adopted building code, the owner or agent shall obtain a valid permit from the city for the following:
(1)
Construction of new building, including one- and two-family dwellings, garages, and other structures.
(2)
Additions that increase the physical dimensions of a building, including decks.
(3)
Alterations to the building structure, or an alteration to the building's heating, electrical or plumbing systems, except where exempted below. Restoration or repair of an installation to its previous code-compliant condition as determined by the building official.
(4)
Water heater replacements shall require a permit if the plumbing, venting, electrical or gas supply systems are altered.
(5)
Electrical service modifications.
(d)
Exemptions from permit requirements. The following buildings, structures and activities are exempt from the permit requirements of this section:
(1)
Buildings or structures owned by the city;
(2)
The restoration or repair of building equipment, such as furnaces, central air conditions, water heaters, and similar mechanical equipment (except electric service) without the alteration of, or addition to, the building or structure.
(3)
Minor repairs performed for maintenance or replacement purposes in an existing building which does not involve the structural portions of the building or structure, or which does not affect room arrangement, light and ventilation, access to or efficiency of any exit stairways or exits, fire protections and which does not increase a given occupancy or use, for example window and door replacement if it does not affect structure.
(4)
Re-siding, re-roofing and finishing interior surfaces, and installation of cabinetry.
(5)
Fences not over seven feet (2,134 mm) high or other similar like enclosures.
(6)
Portable buildings, or buildings less than 144 square feet, as long as they are not placed upon a permanent slab or foundation.
(e)
Application for building permit. All applications for building permits shall be made to the building official. Any permit for new construction applied for shall be accompanied by five sets of complete plans, drawn to scale, which shall have been prepared by an architect or engineer whose state registration is current and valid, and whose seal shall be affixed to the plan submitted. Such plans shall include the following:
(1)
The actual shape and dimensions of the lot to be built upon.
(2)
The sizes and locations on the lot of any existing buildings.
(3)
The shape, size, height, use and the location on the lot of the building or structure proposed to be built.
(4)
Such other information as may be necessary to provide for the enforcement of the provisions of this article.
(f)
Building permit fees. The building permit fee shall be determined from time to time pursuant to resolution adopted by the mayor and council.
(g)
Duration of permit validity. A building permit for the purpose of remodeling or an addition to an existing structure shall be valid for a period of six months. A building permit for the purpose of new construction shall be valid for a period of one year. The building permits will expire at the end of the validity date whether work has been completed or not at which time the permit will be required to be renewed for work to continue.
(h)
Certificate of occupancy required.
(1)
A certificate of occupancy issued by the building official is required in advance of the use or occupancy of the following:
a.
Any lot or a change or extension in the use thereof.
b.
A building hereafter erected or a change in the use of an existing building.
c.
Any nonconforming use that is existing at the time of the enactment of this article or an amendment thereto or that is changed, extended, altered or rebuilt thereafter, the certificate of occupancy shall state specifically herein the nonconforming use fails to meet the provision of this article.
d.
Nothing contained herein shall require a user to comply with minimum setback or side yard requirements for use of an existing building.
(2)
A certificate of occupancy, either for the whole or a part of a building, shall be applied for coincident with the application for a building permit and shall be issued within ten days after the erection or structural alterations of such building or part thereof, and shall have been completed in conformity with the provisions of this article.
(3)
A certificate of occupancy shall not be issued unless the lot, building, or structure, or use thereof complies with all the provisions of this article. If the certificate of occupancy is denied, the building official shall state in writing the reasons for refusal and the applicant shall be notified of the refusal. A record of all certificates shall be kept on file in the office of the building official and copies shall be furnished on request to any person upon request. .
(i)
Penalties for violation. Any person found in violation of subsection (c) of this section shall be charged an increased permit fee of twice the original permit cost. Any person who is violation of any part of this section may also be subject to the penalties as set forth in section 1-9.
(j)
Remedies. In any case in which any building or structure is or is proposed to be erected, constructed, reconstructed, altered, maintained or used, or any land that is or is proposed to be used in violation of the provisions of this article or amendments thereto, the building official or other appropriate city authority or any adjacent or other property owner who would be damaged by such violation may, in addition to other remedies and after due notice to the owner of the violation, institute injunction, abatement, or any other appropriate action or actions, proceeding or proceedings to prevent, enjoin, abate, or remove such unlawful erection, construction, reconstruction, alteration, maintenance or use.
(Code 1985, § 22-161)
Sec. 8-37. - Daycare facility permit and certificate procedure.
(a)
Generally. For construction of daycare facilities, a commercial building permit form shall be completed and submitted to the city community affairs office for approval. In addition, when plans are required by this section, four sets of construction plans shall be submitted to the fire marshal's office for review and approval.
(b)
Sprinkler systems and fire alarm systems required. All daycare facilities must have an approved fire alarm system. In addition, daycare facilities over one story and all commercial daycare facilities must have an approved sprinkler system. Fire alarm or sprinkler system work will require separate plans to be submitted by a licensed subcontractor after issuance of the building permit.
(c)
Plans for facilities for fewer than seven occupants. Plans for facilities designed to have fewer than seven occupants may be rendered on eight-inch by 11-inch paper and shall include the following:
(1)
Dimensions for the overall space.
(2)
Dimensions for each room.
(3)
All doors and windows including dimensions of each.
(4)
Emergency lighting and exit sign locations.
(5)
Fire extinguisher number and location.
(6)
Fire alarm device number, type and location.
(d)
Plans for facilities for seven or more occupants. Facilities designed to have seven or more occupants are subject to the following additional requirements:
(1)
Plans must be stamped with the seal and state registration number of the drafting architect or engineer, per O.C.G.A. § 25-2-14(a).
(2)
A site plan shall be submitted with the plans, showing locations of fire hydrants and the distance from the structure to the hydrant.
(3)
A water flow test result of the closest hydrant showing a minimum flow 1,500 gallons per minute at 20 psi must also be submitted with the plans.
(e)
Filing approved plans. Plans stamped as approved by the fire marshal's office shall be filed with the city community affairs office.
(f)
Field inspection. The applicant must request appropriate field inspections by the city building inspector and the fire marshal's office.
(g)
Certificate of occupancy. When the facility has been approved by and passed all inspections by the building inspector and fire marshal's office, a certificate of occupancy shall be issued.

ARTICLE III. - EXCAVATING AND TRENCHING

Sec. 8-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Certificate means the certificate issued by Cobb County upon satisfactory completion of a mandatory training/educational program regarding excavation and trench safety practices, such program to be provided by the county.
Certificate holder means the holder of the certificate identified in the definition of the term "certificate" in this section.
Excavation means any manmade cavity of depression in the earth's surface, including its sides, walls, or faces, formed by earth removal and producing unsupported earth conditions by reasons of the excavation. If installed forms or similar structures reduce the depth-to-width relationship, an excavation may become a trench.
OSHA means the Federal Department of Labor, Occupational Safety and Health Administration, or successor agency.
Supervisory personnel means any person who has the responsibility for layout, oversight, superintending, directing or controlling an excavation or trenching site.
Trench is a narrow excavation made below the surface of the ground. In general, the depth is greater than the width, but the width of a trench is not greater than 15 feet.
(Code 1985, § 5-61; Ord. of 11-7-1988, § 1)
Sec. 8-62. - Compliance with regulations required.
No individual, partnership, corporation, or other entity of any kind whatsoever shall engage in any excavation or trenching except in compliance with the provisions of this article and in compliance with any applicable state and federal laws and rules.
(Code 1985, § 5-62; Ord. of 11-7-1988, § 2)
Sec. 8-63. - Adoption of OSHA safety and health regulations.
All safety and health regulations adopted by OSHA with regard to excavating and trenching operations, particularly 1926 CFR 1926.650—1926.653 (subpart P—excavations, trenching and shoring), as the same now exist or may be amended, are adopted as a part of this article the same as if quoted verbatim herein.
(Code 1985, § 5-63; Ord. of 11-7-1988, § 3)
Sec. 8-64. - County certificate required.
(a)
No equipment operator or supervisory personnel shall participate in any excavation or trenching or in any way work in an excavation or trench unless such person holds a valid certificate from the county evidencing satisfactory completion of a required educational program on safe trench/excavation practices provided by the county. No other person shall participate in or work in any excavation or trenching site unless a certificate holder is present at the excavation or trench site where work is being performed.
(b)
In the event any person who holds a valid certificate is found to be in violation of the safety standards and requirements on any job site, the offense shall be promptly reported to the county.
(Code 1985, § 5-64; Ord. of 11-7-1988, § 4)
Sec. 8-65. - City permit required.
(a)
No excavating or trenching shall be performed until a permit or authorization for the same has been obtained from the city. Any applicant will be required to acknowledge receipt and understanding of safety requirements before any permit will be issued. All such permits shall be conspicuously posted upon the job site. No such permit shall be suspended or revoked except for due cause and after hearing before the mayor and council after notice to the permit holder of the date, time and place of hearing, along with notice of the charges, except a permit may be suspended prior to such hearing in the event of a cave-in of an excavation or trench resulting in human injury or death. Due cause shall consist of the violating of any laws or ordinances regulating excavating or trenching. Three days' notice shall be deemed reasonable, but shorter or longer periods of notice shall be authorized as the mayor and council may deem the circumstances to justify. At such hearing, the permit holder shall be entitled to be represented by an attorney, to cross examine any witnesses who may appear against him, and to present evidence in his own behalf.
(b)
After July 1, 1988, no permit or authorization shall be issued unless an authorized agent of the applicant holds a certificate.
(Code 1985, § 5-65; Ord. of 11-7-1988, § 5)
Sec. 8-66. - Inspection by the city.
City personnel shall periodically inspect trench/excavation sites. Such inspectors shall, among other things, verify the presence of the required permit, verify existence of required certificates, and verify compliance to OSHA safety standards hereinabove adopted.
(Code 1985, § 5-66; Ord. of 11-7-1988, § 6)
Sec. 8-67. - Violations.
(a)
Violations of this chapter may result in revocation or suspension of any permit issued hereunder as hereinabove set out.
(b)
A notice of violation shall be issued for any deficiency. Upon receipt of such notice, the deficiency shall be corrected immediately.
(c)
All excavation and trenching work upon any job site shall cease immediately when there is trench activity by any person not a certificate holder or when the deficiencies cannot be corrected immediately or when there is a refusal or failure to correct immediately or when such work is being done without a permit.
(d)
If deficiencies identified in a notice of violation are not corrected, the building permit, land disturbance permit or any other permit issued by the city may be suspended or revoked for due cause upon the conditions herein identified for revocation of permit.
(e)
Flagrant or repeated violations shall be reported by the city to OSHA and Cobb County for action.
(f)
Any person, firm, partnership, corporation, or entity of any kind whatsoever, violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be punished by a fine of not more than $500.00 or six-month imprisonment or both for each violation.
(g)
In addition, every violation of the terms of this chapter by any person, firm, partnership, corporation, or entity of any kind whatsoever, shall be deemed a nuisance and a continuing nuisance so long as such violation may be continued; and such violation is subject to abatement as a nuisance as provided by the laws of this state.
(h)
Any violation of this article shall be subject to injunction.
(i)
The above remedies are cumulative. Pursuance of any one remedy shall not be deemed an election of remedies and shall not prohibit pursuance of more than one remedy simultaneously.
(Code 1985, § 5-67; Ord. of 11-7-1988, § 7)
