ARTICLE IX. - ALCOHOL IN PUBLIC PLACES, BROWN-BAGGING

Sec. 6-120. - Alcoholic beverages in public places.
(a)
No person, firm or corporation licensed to sell alcoholic beverages shall permit any person to remove from such premises any alcoholic beverage in any open container.
(b)
This section shall not apply to nor shall it affect the sale of or possession of a partially consumed bottle of wine purchased with a meal from a restaurant and resealed as permitted by O.C.G.A. §§ 3-6-4 and 40-6-253(2).
(c)
This section shall not apply in the following instances:
(1)
For events that are sponsored or organized by the city, but only for malt beverage and wine and where alcohol is obtained from a participating business in the downtown entertainment district, and is contained in and consumed from an approved clear plastic container, and where the person consuming or possessing such alcohol is wearing an approved wristband when designated an open container event.
a.
For the purposes of this chapter only, the downtown entertainment district is defined as follows: The center of downtown Washington bordered by Robert Toombs Avenue on the south, by North Jefferson Street on the east, by Court Street on the north, and by Allison Street on the west. This is a specifically authorized and pedestrian oriented area of the city that allows those establishments with valid consumption on premises licenses within such area to dispense and/or serve an alcoholic beverage for "carry out" purposes, provided all other laws, rules, and ordinances are followed.
b.
One drink per person on street limit. Within the downtown entertainment district any establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises is authorized to dispense an alcoholic beverage in a clear plastic cup for removal from the premises; provided however that no person shall remove more than one such alcoholic from the licensed premises at a time.
c.
Size limited to a maximum of 16 ounces. Within the downtown entertainment district, no container in which an alcoholic beverage is dispensed and removed from the licensed premises shall exceed 16 fluid ounces in size. No person shall hold in their possession on the streets and sidewalks, in parks and squares, or in other public places within a carry district any open alcoholic beverage container which exceeds 16 fluid ounces in size.
d.
Drinking from can, bottle, or glass prohibited. It shall be unlawful within the downtown entertainment district for any person to drink or attempt to drink any alcoholic beverage from any can, bottle, or glass, or to possess in an open can, bottle or glass any alcoholic beverage on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, or parking lots whether public or private.
e.
Consumption limited to the downtown entertainment district. No alcoholic beverage purchased pursuant to this provision may be consumed outside of the downtown entertainment district, upon any sidewalk, adjacent to a church, school or park (unless specifically authorized by the city) or upon any private property without the express written consent of the property owners.
f.
Consumption limited to lawful hours of operation. Alcoholic beverage purchased within the downtown entertainment district shall not be consumed within the downtown entertainment district on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, and/or parking lots, whether public or private, prior to 11:00 a.m., or later than 11 p.m.
(2)
For events sponsored or organized by the city and that are open to the general public on city owned property, when designated a BYOB event, attendees may have in their possession no more than 72 ounces of malt beverages and/or 50 ounces of wine not otherwise purchased pursuant to subsection (1) above. Any such malt beverages and/ or wine may be consumed on city property only. No glass containers shall be allowed, and coolers shall be limited to no more than 24 inches along any dimension. Any such coolers must remain in the possession of an adult at all times. All empty containers shall be removed and/or properly disposed of by the person consuming its contents. These provisions notwithstanding, the city may elect not to allow attendees to have in their possession malt beverages and/or wine not otherwise purchased pursuant to subsection (1) above.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-121. - Bring your own bottle "brown-bagging" prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bring any alcoholic beverage (brown bag) onto any property unless such person is the lawful owner of said property, unless the occupational tax on said property has been paid in full and is current, and unless the alcoholic beverage is for the owner's personal use. This restriction shall not be applicable to residential or government-owned property.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any corporation, partnership, individual or other entity who maintains a business which is subject to any of the occupational tax provisions of the city or is specifically exempted from occupational taxes as provided for in the city occupational tax ordinance to knowingly allow a person to violate the provisions of subsection (a) on their property.
(c)
This section shall not apply to, nor shall it affect any business in possession of a bottle house license in accordance with section 6-82.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-122. - Permitted locations and special events.
(a)
No alcoholic beverages shall be dispensed, distributed, poured or served at any special event location, premises, or building in the city except as set out in this chapter unless otherwise provided for under this chapter;
(b)
The owner or lessee of a location at which alcoholic beverages are to be furnished and served at a private function or special event by a person at a location not otherwise required to be licensed pursuant to this chapter shall first obtain a qualifying location permit designating such location as a "permitted location." Such qualifying location permit shall be good for the calendar year in which it is issued and may be renewed annually as set forth in this chapter. Thereafter, the person organizing the private function or special event to be held at a permitted location must apply for and obtain a "special event permit" as set forth in section 6-123 of this chapter. The cost of such permits shall be set by the city council as set out in section 6-36. The requirements of section 6-122 shall be met by an applicant for a permitted location license.
(c)
To qualify as a "permitted location," the location, premises or building shall not fail to meet all the minimum distance requirements set out in section 6-122, and shall not fail to meet all requirements of the building inspector, the fire marshal, and the planning and zoning department for an on-premises pouring outlet, and shall possess a permit as a "permitted location" pursuant to this chapter. The applicant for a qualifying location permit shall submit a plat of survey prepared by a state registered land surveyor showing the distances set forth in this chapter with the application for the qualifying location permit.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to furnish and serve alcoholic beverages at a private function or special event at a permitted location unless food is also served at the private function or special event.
(e)
Alcoholic beverages furnished and served at a private function or special event may be consumed only on the premises; and may not be removed from the premises in an open container.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any alcoholic beverages to be sold at a private function or special event, i.e. "no cash bar" sales will be permitted unless the permitted location also holds an on premises consumption license for alcoholic beverages.
(g)
Locations, premises or buildings for which an on-premises consumption license has been issued pursuant to this chapter shall be deemed a "permitted location" under this section without further application; however, all rules, laws, and ordinances must be followed.
(h)
The "permitted location" licensee shall be responsible for any violation of this chapter which occurs at the "permitted location" whether by the licensee's employees or agents, a caterer or their employees or agents, or a lessee or invitee of the licensee, their lessee or invitees or any of their employees or agents.
(i)
The person holding the private function or special event, and not otherwise required to be licensed pursuant to this chapter must purchase and furnish the alcoholic beverages.
(j)
The serving, without sale, of alcoholic beverages at a special event in a permitted location for a special event not otherwise required to be licensed or licensed hereunder for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall be only permitted during the times for sales of alcoholic beverages as set forth in section 6-13 of this chapter for premises licensed for consumption on the premises only.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-123. - Special event permits.
(a)
In order to serve wine, and/or beer and malt beverages, and/or distilled spirits at a private function or special event, at a permitted location, the person organizing the private function or special event shall be required to:
(1)
Apply to the city for a "special event permit." The application shall include the name and business address of any caterer providing food service for the private function or special event, the date, time and location of the event, which location must be a permitted location as set forth in section 6-122 or otherwise exempt as set forth under this chapter;
(2)
Pay the cost of such permit as set forth in section 6-36 of this chapter.
If the application meets all of the requirements of this chapter, the city clerk shall issue the special event permit. If the city clerk finds that the application does not meet the requirements of this chapter and denies the special event permit, the applicant may appeal to the city council within five days of the date of the denial.
(b)
The "special event permit" shall be maintained at the site of the private function or special event during the hours alcoholic beverages are served.
(c)
The person holding the private function or special event, and not otherwise required to be licensed pursuant to this chapter, must purchase the alcoholic beverages.
(d)
The "special event permit" licensee shall be responsible for any violation of this chapter which occurs at the event whether by the licensee's employees or agents, invitee of the licensee or invitee of the invitee of the licensee.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-124. - Exceptions.
(a)
The requirements of this chapter shall not apply to an event held for business development, or charitable purposes, by a bona fide nonprofit organization, at which alcoholic beverages are served, by a person or organization not otherwise required to be licensed pursuant to this chapter, to members and invited guests of such persons or organizations holding the event at a permitted location or at a location not otherwise required to be permitted pursuant to this chapter, provided that the person or organization purchased and served the alcoholic beverages.
(b)
The requirements of this chapter shall not apply to an event held at a private residence at which alcoholic beverages are purchased and served by a person not otherwise required to be licensed pursuant to this chapter to invited guests of such person, provided that the person holding the event purchases the alcoholic beverages.
(c)
Notwithstanding the times for sales of alcoholic beverages set forth in section 6-13 herein, shall apply to the exceptions set forth in paragraphs (a) and (b) of this section, except that serving without sale of alcoholic beverages at an event described in paragraph (a) or (b) at a location not otherwise required to be licensed, or licensed hereunder for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises only shall be shall be permitted on Sunday between the hours of 1:00 p.m. and 12:00 midnight.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-125. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person or persons found by the building official to be in violation of the above sections having to do with special event permits will be subject to the following penalties:
(1)
The first offense will result in a written warning and request for immediate corrective action.
(2)
The second offense will result in a suspension of the qualified location permit for up to 90 days as determined in the sole discretion of the building official.
(3)
The third offense will result in permanent revocation of the qualified location permit.
(4)
The aforementioned penalties are determined in the sole discretion of the building official, but, can be appealed to the city administrator within ten days of the violation.
(5)
Any violation of state law will be reported to the proper authorities.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
