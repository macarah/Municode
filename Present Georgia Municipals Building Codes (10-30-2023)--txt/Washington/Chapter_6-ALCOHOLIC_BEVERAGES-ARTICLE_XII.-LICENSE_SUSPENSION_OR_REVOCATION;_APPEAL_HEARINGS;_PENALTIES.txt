ARTICLE XII. - LICENSE SUSPENSION OR REVOCATION; APPEAL HEARINGS; PENALTIES

Sec. 6-150. - Emergency suspension situations.
The city clerk or his/her designee is hereby authorized to suspend any license issued hereunder for any emergency situation such as civil disorders or natural disasters or in any situation that the city clerk deems such immediate suspension necessary for the protection of the health and welfare of the citizens of the city, and such suspension may be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until the license is reinstated by the city clerk or his/her designee.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-151. - Removal of signs.
When any license for the sale of alcoholic beverages is revoked, all signs indicating that such beverages may be sold or purchased shall immediately be removed from both inside and outside the place of business.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-152. - Appeal hearings.
No license or permit shall be denied, suspended, or revoked without the opportunity for a hearing as hereinafter provided.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-154. - Denial of alcoholic beverage license; appeals.
(a)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall provide written notice to an alcoholic beverage license applicant of the decision to deny said alcoholic beverage license application. Such written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for such action and shall notify the applicant of the right to appeal under the provisions of this article.
(b)
An applicant may appeal the denial of an alcoholic beverage license application by providing written notification to the city administrator at city hall within ten calendar days of the denial of the alcoholic beverage license application. The written notification shall include the legal and factual basis upon which the applicant's appeal is based.
(c)
In order to defray administrative costs, the appeal must be accompanied by a filing fee of $500.00. If, following the administrative hearing, the city clerk or his/her designee issues an alcoholic beverage license to the applicant, then the $500.00 filing fee shall be applied towards the cost of the alcoholic beverage license.
(d)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall set an administrative hearing date that shall occur within 30 calendar days of the filing of the appeal request. Notice of said administrative hearing shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage license applicant at the address shown on the alcoholic beverage license applicant's application.
(e)
At the administrative hearing, the alcoholic beverage license applicant is entitled to be heard and present evidence on his/her behalf. The city administrator or his/her designee shall determine whether the denial of the alcoholic beverage license application is justified using a preponderance of the evidence standard.
(f)
The city administrator or his/her designee shall consider the following factors in determining whether to uphold the denial of an application:
(1)
Compliance with the criminal history requirements;
(2)
Compliance with the requirements of this chapter;
(3)
The history of and facts relating to previous violations of this chapter;
(4)
Non-alcohol related violations of this Code;
(5)
Violations of Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the alcohol and tobacco tax division of the Georgia Department of Revenue;
(6)
Documented efforts at avoiding violations of this chapter, this Code or state law;
(7)
Community involvement; and
(8)
Any other objective and relevant facts.
(g)
The city administrator or his/her designee, after hearing evidence, shall have the authority to uphold, modify or overturn the previous decision. The city administrator or his/her designee shall issue a written decision on the appeal within 30 calendar days of the date of the administrative hearing. All decisions by the city administrator or his/her designee shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage license applicant at the address set forth in the license application.
(h)
Any applicant may appeal the city administrator or his/her designee's decision to the mayor and city council within ten calendar days of the date that the adverse decision is issued by the city administrator. Notice of the appeal shall be delivered in writing to the clerk of the city. The written notification shall include the legal and factual basis upon which the applicant's appeal is based.
(i)
In order to defray administrative costs, the appeal must be accompanied by an additional filing fee of $500.00. If, following the administrative hearing, the mayor and city council issue an alcoholic beverage license to the applicant, then the $1,000.00 in total filing fees shall be applied towards the cost of the alcoholic beverage license.
(j)
The mayor and city council shall set a hearing date within 30 calendar days of receiving the appeal request. Notice of said hearing shall be mailed to the applicant at the address shown on the alcoholic beverage license applicant's application. At the hearing, the applicant is entitled to be heard and present evidence on his/her behalf. The city administrator or his/her representative may similarly be heard and present evidence in support of the denial. The decision of the city administrator or his/her designee shall not be set aside unless the mayor and city council finds it to be:
(1)
Contrary to law or ordinances;
(2)
Unsupported by substantial evidence on the record as a whole; or
(3)
Unreasonable.
(k)
All decisions of the mayor and city council shall be final. The mayor and city council shall issue a written decision on the appeal within 30 calendar days of the date of the hearing. All decisions by the city council shall be in writing and shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage license applicant at the address set forth in the applicable license application.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-155. - Administrative penalties and appeal hearings for alcoholic beverage handling permit holders.
(a)
Upon notice of violation of these ordinances, the city clerk or his/her designee shall provide written notice to an alcoholic beverage handling permit holder of the intention to impose one or more of the following administrative penalties:
(1)
Suspend their permit;
(2)
Revoke their permit;
(3)
Impose an administrative monetary penalty not to exceed $1,000.00; or
(4)
Convey a warning for any violation of this chapter, of Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the Alcohol and tobacco tax division of the Georgia Department of Revenue.
Such written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the facts supporting such violation, shall set forth the proposed administrative penalty, and shall notify the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder of their right to appeal under the provisions of this article. Said written notification shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address set forth in the applicable application, and a copy of said written notification shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.
(b)
An alcoholic beverage handling permit holder may appeal the proposed administrative penalty by providing written notification to the city clerk within ten calendar days of the date that said written notification was hand delivered to the premises. The written notification shall include the legal and factual basis upon which the applicant's appeal is based.
(c)
In the event that the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder does not timely appeal said proposed administrative penalty(ies), then said alcoholic beverage handling permit holder shall be deemed to agree with said proposed administrative penalty(ies). Further, in the event that said proposed administrative penalty include the imposition of an administrative monetary penalty, said administrative monetary penalty shall be due and owing to the city within five calendar days of the date of hand delivery of the written notification. The failure to timely pay any administrative monetary penalties shall subject the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder to further administrative penalties.
(d)
In the event that the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder does timely appeal said proposed administrative penalty, then the city clerk shall set an administrative hearing date that shall occur within 30 calendar days of the filing of the appeal request. Notice of said administrative hearing shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address shown on the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said written notice shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.
(e)
At the administrative hearing, the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder is entitled to be heard and present evidence on his/her behalf. The city clerk shall determine whether the proposed administrative penalty of the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder as set forth in the written notification is justified using a preponderance of the evidence standard.
(f)
The city clerk shall consider the following factors in determining whether the proposed administrative penalty of the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder as set forth in the written notification is justified:
(1)
Compliance with the criminal history requirements;
(2)
The history of and facts relating to previous violations of this chapter;
(3)
Non-alcohol related violations of this Code;
(4)
Violations of Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the alcohol and tobacco tax division of the Georgia Department of Revenue;
(5)
Documented efforts at avoiding violations of this chapter, of Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the alcohol and tobacco tax division of the Georgia Department of Revenue;
(6)
Community involvement; and
(7)
Any other objective and relevant facts.
(g)
The city clerk, after hearing evidence, shall have the authority to uphold, modify or overturn the proposed administrative penalty. The city clerk shall issue a written decision on the appeal within 30 calendar days of the date of the hearing. All decisions by the city clerk shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address set forth in the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said written decision shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.
(h)
Any alcoholic beverage handling permit holder may appeal the city clerk's decision to the city administrator within ten calendar days of the date that the adverse decision is hand delivered to the premises. Notice of the appeal shall be delivered in writing to the clerk of the city. The written notification shall include the legal and factual basis upon which the applicant's appeal is based.
(i)
The city administrator shall set a hearing date within 30 calendar days of receiving the appeal request. Notice of said hearing shall be mailed to the applicant at the address shown on the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said notice shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred. At the hearing, the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder is entitled to be heard and present evidence on his/her behalf. The city clerk or his/her representative may similarly be heard and present evidence in support of the imposition of the administrative penalty. The decision of the city clerk shall not be set aside unless the city administrator finds it to be:
(1)
Contrary to law or ordinances;
(2)
Unsupported by substantial evidence on the record as a whole; or
(3)
Unreasonable.
(j)
All decisions of the city administrator shall be final. The city administrator shall issue a written decision on the appeal within 30 days of the date of delivery of the hearing. All decisions by the city administrator shall be in writing and shall be mailed to the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder at the address set forth in the alcoholic beverage handling permit holder's application and a copy of said written decision shall be hand delivered to the premises where said violation(s) are alleged to have occurred.

Sec. 6-157. - Ordinance violation penalties.
(a)
Upon conviction in the municipal court of any violation of this chapter, the person so convicted shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment for a period of not more than six months, or both. Each act in violation and each day upon which any violation shall occur shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
In the event of a suspension of a license is imposed upon any licensed establishment where a violation of O.C.G.A. ยง 3-3-23 has occurred by employees, managers, or owners, the licensed establishment shall be required to post signs, to be provided by the city clerk or his/her designee, on the exterior doors of the establishment which contains the following language: "An employee or owner of this store has violated state law by selling alcoholic beverages to a minor" for the period of the suspension imposed by the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
