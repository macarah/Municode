ARTICLE III. - CONSIDERATION OF LICENSE APPLICATIONS

Sec. 6-50. - Considerations for granting a license permitting the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(a)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall have the discretion to grant or refuse to grant any application for a license hereunder; however, the city clerk or his/her designee shall consider the following matters in connection with each application for a license hereunder, which considerations are deemed to be in the public interest and welfare, as follows:
(1)
Whether the applicant, or any person having a direct or indirect beneficial interest in the issuance of the license, has had any license issued by the city or by any other city in the state, or by any other licensing authority in the state relating to the manufacture, distribution or sale of alcoholic beverages suspended within the past five years or revoked within the past ten years;
(2)
Whether any applicant for a license to sell distilled spirits or package beer and wine, or any person having a direct or indirect beneficial interest in the issuance of the license, has had any license issued by the city or by any other city in the state, or by any other licensing authority in the state relating to the manufacture, distribution or sale of alcoholic beverages suspended within the past five years or revoked within the past ten years;
(3)
Whether the applicant or any person owning a direct or indirect beneficial interest in the license for which application is made has sufficient mental capacity to conduct the business for which the application is made;
(4)
Whether the applicant or any person owning a direct or indirect beneficial interest in the license for which application is made has been dishonorably discharged from the armed services of the United States;
(5)
Whether the applicant or any person owning a direct or indirect beneficial interest in the license for which application is made fails to demonstrate financial stability;
(6)
Whether the applicant is 21 years of age or older;
(7)
Whether the applicant is a citizen of the United States or a resident alien lawfully permitted for permanent residence;
(8)
Whether the location of the premises from which the proposed sale of alcoholic beverages shall be conducted meets the distance restrictions set forth by state law and local ordinance;
(9)
Whether the applicant, owner, and manager have obtained a valid alcoholic beverage handling permit as required by this chapter;
(10)
The applicant's reputation, character, criminal history, and mental and physical capacity to conduct the business proposed to be conducted;
(11)
Whether the applicant has been convicted of, pled guilty to, or entered a plea of nolo contendere to, or is presently charged with any felony, any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, any criminal offense related to lottery rules or regulations, any offense related to illegal gambling, soliciting for prostitution, pandering, letting of premises for prostitution, keeping a disorderly place, any offense related to the illegal possession or sale of illegal drugs or alcoholic beverages, any sex offense found in O.C.G.A. Title 16, Chapter 6 or any similar violation found in any other state or federal statute, any charges related to the manufacture or sale of alcoholic beverages, or a second offense of driving under the influence of alcohol within the last ten years;
(12)
Whether the applicant, or any person who shall have a direct or indirect beneficial interest in the license, as a previous holder of a license to sell any type of alcoholic beverages has been found to have violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business within a ten-year period, immediately preceding the date of the application for license hereunder;
(13)
If applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell any type of alcoholic beverages, the manner in which he or she conducted the business under such license and the necessity for any unusual police observation and inspection of such prior business in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business;
(14)
If applicant has had a prior license, whether any such prior license for the sale of any type of alcoholic beverages has been suspended or revoked or whether or not hearings have been held for the revocation or suspension of any such license regardless of whether or not any such license was revoked or suspended as a result of such hearing;
(16)
Whether the applicant, or any person who shall have a direct or indirect beneficial interest in the license, has had any city license revoked within two years prior to the filing of the application;
(17)
Whether the facility where the applicant proposes to sell alcoholic beverages meets city zoning requirements;
(18)
Whether it appears that the applicant would have adequate financial participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or whether it appears that the applicant is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever; and
(19)
Whether the applicant has failed to meet any other criteria specified by this chapter or state law.
(b)
The city clerk or his/her designee has discretionary authority to consider any other objective criteria that is rationally related to the application including any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application, or the proposed location of the business when making a decision to approve or deny a license requested hereunder. If, in the judgment of the city clerk or his/her designee, circumstances are such that granting the license would not be in the best interest of the general health, safety, and public welfare, such circumstances shall be grounds for denying the application.
(c)
The city clerk or his/her designee may issue any city license provided for in this chapter only after a motion to that effect is adopted by the city council. The city clerk or his/her designee may issue a license for the off-premises sale of alcoholic beverages for catered functions without the necessity of city council approval.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-51. - Continuing duty to report criminal convictions.
All license holders shall have a continuing duty to notify the city in writing in the event that the license holder or any shareholder or partner that holds a ten percent or greater interest in the entity to whom the license is issued is charged with, is convicted of or pleads guilty or nolo contendre to any criminal offense under the laws of this state or any other state or other jurisdiction after a license has been granted.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-52. - Payment of taxes and other debts to the city.
The city clerk or his/her designee shall cause an inquiry to be made into the tax records of the city to determine if any applicant or any shareholder or partner that holds a ten percent or greater interest in the entity to whom the license is issued have any outstanding taxes or special assessments that are delinquent or any other monies owing to the city. No license shall be issued, nor shall a license be renewed, until all such debts are paid in full.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-53. - Change in relationships and/or ownership interest.
Any change in any of the ownership interest contained on the original application for a license or for an application for the renewal of an existing license must be filed in writing with the city clerk or his/her designee within 30 calendar days after such change is made. The failure to do so shall be grounds for the imposition of administrative penalties or for the immediate denial of any pending application for such a license or for the renewal of such a license.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-54. - Completion of proposed licensed premises.
Where a building in which an applicant proposes to operate under a license permitting the sale of alcoholic beverages is at the time of the application for such license not in existence, or not yet completed, a license may be issued for such location providing the plans and specifications for the proposed building are filed with the city clerk or his/her designee and show clear compliance with the other provisions of this chapter and other applicable ordinances of the city. No sales shall be allowed in such location until the location has been completed in accordance with said plans and specifications and is in conformity with all other provisions of this chapter, all building codes, and other applicable ordinances of the city and until a certificate of occupancy has been issued.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-55. - Application for new license in existing license location.
An application for a new license in an existing license location shall be required to comply with all requirements of this chapter for the granting of a new license except that an application for an annual renewal license shall not be subject to the notice requirements of section 6-27.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
