ARTICLE II. - LICENSING

Sec. 10-19. - License fees.
Business license fees will be established from time to time by action of the city council and set out in the consolidated fee schedule.
(Ord. No. 0910-005, § 1, 7-13-2010)
Sec. 10-20. - Additional penalty.
In addition to any other authorized penalty, businesses that do not purchase their license until after March 15 of the year in which the license is due will also pay a five percent penalty.
(Ord. No. 0910-005, § 2, 7-13-2010)
Sec. 10-21. - Penalty.
Any person violating this article shall, upon conviction, be punished in accordance with section 1-13.
(Ord. No. 04-05-003, 1-11-2005)

ARTICLE III. - INSURANCE COMPANIES

Sec. 10-46. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Blind person means a person whose vision, with correcting glasses, is so defective as to prevent the performance of activities for which eyesight is essential.
Business location means any location maintained by the insurance company, broker or agent where any business of the insurance company, broker or agent is performed.
Engaging in business means purposefully doing some act or consummating some transaction within the corporate limits of the city.
Veteran means any disabled veteran of any war or armed conflict in which any branch of the armed forces of the United States engaged, whether under United States command or otherwise; or, any veteran of peace time service in the United States armed forces who has a physical disability incurred during the period of such service, provided that such person receives a certificate of exemption issued by the commissioner of veterans service.
(Ord. of 11-15-2001, § 1)
Sec. 10-47. - When license required; fee for license; persons exempt from license fee; effective dates of license.
(a)
Before engaging in any business in the city, all insurance companies, brokers, and agents must obtain a license from the city.
(b)
(1)
License fees for insurance companies, brokers, and agents engaging in business within the municipal corporate limits of the city shall be established from time to time by action of the city council and set out in the consolidated fee schedule.
(2)
An additional annual license fee in the same amount shall be paid for each separate business location in excess of one operated and maintained by such company within the municipal corporate limits of the city.
(3)
There shall be an additional annual license fee set out in the consolidated fee schedule on each insurance company for each separate business location not otherwise subject to a license fee under this section, which company is operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and which, in connection with the loans or sales involving term financing, offers, solicits, or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of the insurance company for insurance.
(c)
Notwithstanding the other provisions of this article, there shall be no license fee for:
(1)
A veteran; or
(2)
A blind person.
(d)
The effective date of each license is from January 1 through December 31 of each year.
(Ord. of 11-15-2001, § 2)
Sec. 10-48. - Penalty for violation.
Any insurance company, broker, or agent who transacts any business in the city in violation of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished in accordance with section 1-13.
(Ord. of 11-15-2001, § 3)
