ARTICLE III. - NO PARKING AREAS AND ZONES

Sec. 30-44. - Authority to designate no parking areas.
(a)
The city clerk, police chief or other designated official of the city is hereby authorized to designate, with approval of the city council, those portions of streets, alleys or other rights-of-way within the city where parking shall be prohibited. Further, these authorities shall designate any areas where parking time limits may be established, handicapped parking established or similar parking restrictions are deemed appropriate within the city. All parking zones will be marked with signs specific to the type and limitations of the zone.
(b)
Any person who violates the provisions of this section shall, upon conviction, be punished in accordance with section 1-13.
(c)
The provisions of this section are meant to be in addition to, and to the extent there is a conflict, shall supersede any provisions of other ordinances, including the zoning ordinance.
(Ord. No. 07-08-012, 7-8-2008)

Sec. 30-61. - Title.
An ordinance of the city regulating parades, marches, protests, picketing, and public gatherings within the incorporated boundaries of the city, the use of city streets, parks, and public buildings during the course of aforementioned activities, the procedure for the permitting of parades and marches, enforcement of this article, and penalties for the violation of its provisions.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
Sec. 30-62. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
March is any procession involving five or more persons or five or more vehicles utilizing city streets, sidewalks, parks, etc., as a route of travel and areas of observation for the general public. Marches may be identified by their goal of advancing a political agenda, advocating a social change, correcting a perceived injustice, or other purposes not considered to be motivated for the purpose of family entertainment and appropriate for young children. The determination of gathering's qualification as a march rests with the chief of the Mt. Zion Police Department and/or the mayor and city council.
Parade is any procession involving ten or more persons or five or more vehicles utilizing city streets, sidewalks, parks, etc., as a route of travel and areas of observation for the general public. A parade will be identified by its characteristic of appealing to family entertainment and its being appropriate for young children. The determination of a gathering's qualification as a parade rests with the chief of the Mt. Zion Police Department and/or the mayor and city council.
Parade permit is a permit issued by the city as required by this article.
Picketing is a gathering of five or more individuals at a single or multiple locations on either public or private property for the furtherance of a lawful purpose.
Protest is a gathering of five or more persons utilizing city streets, sidewalks, parks, etc., as a route of travel and areas of observation for the general public. A protest may have the same identifying characteristics as a march and may be eligible to wear the same moniker. The determination of a gathering's qualification as a protest rests with the chief of Mt. Zion Police department and/or the mayor and city council.
Public gathering/assembly shall be identified as ten or more individuals together as a group for the purpose of entertainment - family oriented and suitable for young children, the attainment of a political objective, the correction of a perceived social injustice, campaigning for public office, attendance at an organized or unorganized sporting event with its related pep rallies and tailgating, or other congregations of individuals not in the norm of daily city activities. The determination of whether or not a gathering falls under the prevue of the ordinance rests the chief of the Mt. Zion Police Department and/or the mayor and city council.
Vehicle includes any device which moves on wheels or tracks whether under its own power or otherwise.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
Sec. 30-63. - Exceptions.
This article shall not apply to:
(1)
Funeral processions, provided the police department is notified of each procession's time and route.
(2)
Students going to and from school classes or participation in educational or school sponsored activities, providing such conduct is under the immediate direction and supervision of the proper school authorities.
(3)
A government agency acting within the scope of its functions.
(4)
Gatherings or congregations of people deemed by the chief of the Mt. Zion Police Department and/or the mayor and city council as being outside the scope and purpose of this article.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
Sec. 30-64. - Public conduct at parades, marches, protest, gatherings, etc.
Regardless of whether an assembly permit is required, or has been obtained, the following conduct will not be permitted during any assembly. For the purpose of this section, the term assembly will also pertain to parades, marches, protests, picketing and gatherings.
(1)
No person shall unreasonably hamper, obstruct, impede, or interfere with any assembly, or with any person, vehicle, or animal participating in or used in an assembly.
(2)
No driver or vehicle shall drive between the vehicles or persons comprising an assembly when the vehicles or persons are in motion and are conspicuously designated as an assembly, unless specifically authorized to do so by Mt. Zion Police Department personnel.
(3)
No person shall carry or possess glass bottles, glass jars, glass containers of any kind, bricks, stones, rocks, pieces of asphalt or concrete, or any other object that can be used as a weapon, including but not limited to any air rifle, air pistol, paintball gun, paintball rifle, explosive, blasting cap, knife, hatchet, ax, slingshot, blackjack, slapjack, metal knuckles, mace, iron buckle, ax handle, chains, crowbar, hammer, any club, bludgeon, revolver, pistol, rifle, shotgun, self-propelled rocket or missile, any other instrumentality used or intended to be used as a weapon.
(4)
No person shall carry, possess, or assemble, any sign, display placard, o r banner larger than three feet by three feet, or composed, in whole or in part, of wood, metal, or a hard plastic material, unless said sign, display, flag, placard, or banner has been approved by the Mt. Zion Police Department prior to it being used during an assembly. No sign, display, flag, placard, or banner may be draped or affixed to any city property, including but not limited to, streets, sidewalks, trees, buildings, or any other property of the city or of a utility company.
(5)
No electronic sound amplification equipment shall be used or employed during an assembly (except for a radio or recorder or other device which is possessed and used by an individual for his/her own enjoyment and is operated in such a manner so as to not interfere with the use and enjoyment of any other person) unless prior authorization from the chief of the Mt. Zion Police Department.
(6)
No vehicles shall be used in an assembly unless prior authorization is obtained from the chief of the Mt. Zion Police Department.
(7)
No persons shall violate the terms of their assembly permit or deviate from the approved information submitted in the application for the permit, if the assembly is such that a permit is requested.
(8)
No person engaged in, participating in, conducting, attending, or observing an assembly shall violate any federal, state, or local law or ordinance.
(9)
Nothing in this article shall prohibit a disabled person from carrying, possessing, or using a wheelchair, cane, walker, or similar device necessary for providing mobility so that person may participate in an assembly.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
Sec. 30-65. - Types of unlawful assembly, picketing, demonstrations, parades, marches, and protests enumerated.
(a)
The assembly of two or more persons for the purposes of committing a n unlawful act
(b)
The failure of two or more persons to withdraw or disperse from any assembly, picketing, demonstration, parade, march, or protest on being lawfully commanded to do so by peace officer and before any member of the assembly has inflicted injury to the person or property of another.
(c)
The gathering of two or more persons, without authority of law or without a permit pursuant to the provisions contained in this article for the issuance of the permit, for the purpose of doing violence to the person or property of one supposed by the accused to have been guilty of a violation of law.
(d)
The assembly of two or more persons, without authority of law or without a permit pursuant to the provisions contained in this article, for the purpose of exercising correctional or regulative powers over any person by violence, threat, or intimidation.
(e)
Any person who shall assemble, loiter, or remain in sufficient numbers or in such a manner as to be an obstruction to free passage on the streets, sidewalks, parks, or within any public place within the corporate limits of the city.
(f)
Any person who, upon being assembled, picketing, parading, or otherwise engaging in any acts covered by this article, engages in any acts, statements, or other public utterance by which they attempt to incite a riot, revolt, a general disturbance of the peace, or otherwise engage in any conduct which tends to incite or provoke riots, public disturbances, affrays, or other similar conduct prohibited by law.
(g)
Any persons who have previously gathered within the corporate limits of the city, upon proper authority and with a permit, and have, on that occasion, committed any unlawful acts, engaged in any conduct which incited a public disturbance, created an affray, resulted in a breach of the peace, or otherwise disrupt the good order and peace of citizens within the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
Sec. 30-66. - Permits and registration.
Any person who wishes to organize, form, or conduct any of the gatherings or assemblies regulated by this article shall be required to register with the chief of police a least four days in advance of the event, on forms to be provided by the chief of police, to obtain a permit therefor. An application for a permit to conduct the acts set forth in this article shall be made to the chief of police in writing, shall be signed by the person responsible for the conduct of the event, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The date and time proposed for the event.
(2)
The route, place, or other area of the proposed event.
(3)
The number of vehicles and the approximate number of persons who would participate in the proposed event.
(4)
The name and address of the person sponsoring or promoting the proposed event.
(5)
The name and address of the person making the application for the permit, and proper identification of that person.
(6)
The name and address of at least five principal parties who will also be responsible for, or who may participate in, the event, including proper identification of these parties.
(7)
The name, address, telephone number, and authorization of the sponsoring organization and its leaders if the activity is designed to be held by, for, or on behalf of any person, group, or organization other than the applicant.
(8)
A statement as to whether the parade, assembly, or other activity will occupy all or only a portion of the width of the streets, the location of such streets, and any assembly areas for the group.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
Sec. 30-67. - Public safety.
The safety of Mt. Zion citizens and the protection of private property will be the determining factor in all decisions by the city involving the issuance of permits as described in this article. The city does not assume responsibility for the safety of march organizers, participants' vehicles, or equipment when an assembly conflicts with the peace and good order of the city once a n event is underway. Event organizers, persons requesting the permit, principal parties, and individua ls identified by law enforcement as agitators or facilitators, as well as those individuals actually committing a criminal act, will be prosecuted to the fullest possible extent allowed by state law and city ordinances. Marches, parades, protests, pickets, groups, or assemblies that stage outside the corporate limits of the city in an attempt to avoid permitting requirements and attempt to proceed into the city shall be stopped at the city limits by the Mt. Zion Police Department and other assisting agencies. The city also reserves its right to recover both compensatory and punitive damages through civil litigation. The city serves notice that it will stand with any Mt. Zion citizen involved in civil litigation as a result of any permitted or unpermitted assembly to include law enforcement testimony, video recordings, photographs, or physical evidence. It is the expectation of the city that all assemblies and events discussed in this article will be peaceful and conform to the guidelines that have been set forth. To ensure this end, the following requirements may be imposed by the city as a requirement for the issuance of a permit:
(1)
An event policy from an accredited insurance company in an amount to be determined by the city.
(2)
Escrowed funds considered adequate by the city to cover the cost of law enforcement personnel, expenses involved in trash cleanup, expenses associated with mutual aid agreements with county, state, or other municipalities, etc. Escrowed funds amount will be determined by the chief of police and in the possession of the city prior to permitting. A minimum amount of $100,000.00 is mandatory and any unused portion, if any, will be returned to the permit holder 60 days after the event.
(3)
Portable restroom facilities shall be supplied by the event organizer in a quantity and manner acceptable to the chief of the Mt. Zion Police Department.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
Sec. 30-68. - Penalties.
Individuals charged with a violation of this article and any of its subsections shall appear before the Mt. Zion Municipal Court and be subject to the appropriate sentencing guidelines set forth by the city court judge and state law.
(Ord. No. 2020-001, 11-10-2020)
