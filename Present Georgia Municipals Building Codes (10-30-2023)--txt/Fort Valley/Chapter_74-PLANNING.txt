ARTICLE II. - PLANNING COMMISSION[2]

Sec. 74-31. - Created and established.
The city planning commission is hereby created and established as authorized by the City Charter, Ga. Const. art. IX, § II, ¶ IV and the General Planning and Zoning Enabling Act of 1957 (1957 Ga. Laws, page 420), as amended.
(Code 1982, § 18-1)
Editor's note— The statute cited above, though repealed, is referred to in O.C.G.A. § 34-9-4.
Sec. 74-32. - Membership; terms; compensation.
The planning commission shall consist of six members, who shall be residents of the city, appointed by the mayor and city council. The terms of the members shall be for five years. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the mayor and city council, which shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1982, § 18-2)
Sec. 74-33. - Officers; rules; meetings; expenditures.
(a)
The planning commission shall elect its chairperson from among its members. The term of the chairperson shall be one year, with eligibility for reelection. The planning commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the municipality. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its meeting time.
(b)
All meetings of the planning commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public as provided by law, and all records of the planning commission shall be a public record.
(c)
The planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work, and may contract with the state planning agency, city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require.
(d)
The expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for such purpose by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 18-3)
Sec. 74-34. - Powers and duties.
The planning commission shall review all proposed zoning decisions, and shall make such recommendations to the mayor and city council regarding any proposed zoning decision as it may deem appropriate. Such recommendations shall have advisory effect only and shall not be binding upon the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 18-4)
Sec. 74-35. - Standards for recommendations.
The planning commission shall utilize the same standards as those set forth in appendix B, section 126 to this Code in making its recommendations.
(Code 1982, § 18-5)

ARTICLE III. - MIDDLE GEORGIA REGIONAL DEVELOPMENT CENTER

Sec. 74-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Center means the Middle Georgia Regional Development Center.
(Code 1982, § 18-20)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 74-72. - Created and established.
The mayor and city council, jointly with the governing authorities of other area municipalities and counties, in order to guide the planning for the total development of the city and the middle Georgia area, which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote the public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity and general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development, hereby creates and establishes the Middle Georgia Regional Development Center, as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 58-8-30 et seq. The center shall be organized and empowered as provided in this article.
(Code 1982, § 18-20)
Sec. 74-73. - Membership.
The center, by bylaw, shall provide for its membership and for the selection of center representatives by the member governments, subject to the requirements and limitations of this article and applicable state law. Center members shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1982, § 18-21)
Sec. 74-74. - Organization.
The center is hereby authorized to elect its officers and manage its affairs in accordance with its bylaws, subject to the requirements and limitations of this article and applicable state law.
(Code 1982, § 18-22)
Sec. 74-75. - Powers and duties.
The center shall be a public agency as defined, and with all of the powers, duties and obligations set forth in O.C.G.A. § 50-8-30 et seq., and the center shall also have all the powers, duties and obligations of a joint planning commission set forth in any law of general application pertaining to joint planning commissions and regional development centers.
(Code 1982, § 18-23)
Sec. 74-76. - Advisory body.
The center shall act in an advisory capacity, and any plan, or part thereof, or amendment, extension or addition thereto, adopted by the center shall constitute a recommendation.
(Code 1982, § 18-24)
Sec. 74-77. - Effect on other planning bodies.
The passage of the ordinance from which this article is derived shall in no way affect the present or future powers and duties granted to any existing local planning and zoning commission and shall in no way affect the future creation of any local planning and zoning commission and any future amendments related thereto.
(Code 1982, § 18-25)
Sec. 74-78. - Bylaws.
The initial bylaws of the center are attached to an ordinance enacted on February 15, 1973, and the bylaws of the center, as they exist on the effective date of this Code, are hereby approved and consented to.
(Code 1982, § 18-26)
