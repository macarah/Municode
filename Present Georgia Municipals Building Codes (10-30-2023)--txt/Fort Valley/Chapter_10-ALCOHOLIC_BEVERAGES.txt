Chapter 10 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverages, wines or fortified wines.
Building code means and includes all building, plumbing and electrical codes and any other similar technical codes of the city.
Church means any permanent building and grounds where persons regularly assemble for religious worship and shall not include any church land or property not regularly used as an assembly area for religious worship services.
College means only the state, county, city, church or other colleges that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common colleges of the state and shall not include private colleges where only specialized subjects, such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects, are taught.
Distance means the measurement in linear feet by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the front door of the structure from which an alcoholic beverage is sold or offered for sale;
(2)
In a straight line to the nearest public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway;
(3)
Along such public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway by the nearest route;
(4)
To the front door of the building, or to the nearest portion of the grounds, whichever is applicable under the appropriate statute.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Premises means a definite closed or partitioned establishment, whether room, shop or building, wherein alcoholic beverages are sold or consumed.
Retail means retail sales packaged to go and not for consumption on the premises.
School means only the state, county, city, church or other schools that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of the state and shall include the adjacent open land used for school purposes, but shall not include any portion of a private school where only specialized subjects, such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects, are taught.
(Code 1982, § 3-50)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Sec. 10-2. - Scope.
The requirements of this chapter shall be in addition to any other requirements for business licenses under this Code. If other provisions of this Code conflict with this chapter, then this chapter shall control.
(Code 1982, § 3-51(b))
Sec. 10-3. - Time limits for opening and continuing business.
(a)
All licensed businesses issued a license under this chapter must open for business within 90 days after the issuance of a city license. Failure to open for business shall constitute a forfeiture and cancellation of the issued licenses and no refund of license fees or business taxes shall be made. Any applicant unable to comply with the time limit of this subsection may make written request to the clerk for an extension of time not to exceed 90 days, and the clerk, at his discretion, may grant or deny the request in accordance with a form approved by the mayor and city council.
(b)
Any license issued under this chapter shall automatically be null and void where the licensed business has not operated or been open to the public for six consecutive months.
(Code 1982, § 3-52)
Cross reference— Businesses, ch. 22.
Sec. 10-4. - Prohibited interests.
(a)
No person who has any direct financial interest in a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages at wholesale shall hold any other license or any interest in any other license under the terms of this chapter.
(b)
No person, related within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity under civil law to any wholesale licensee or employee thereof or any person with any interest therein, shall hold any other license or an interest in any other license issued under this chapter.
(c)
No elected or full-time appointed official of the city or any person, related within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity under civil law to such an official of the city, shall own any interest in any license issued under this chapter unless the interest was obtained prior to election or appointment to office.
(d)
No applicant under this chapter, nor any member of the immediate family of any applicant under this chapter, shall own, control or have a financial interest in more than two liquor stores, liquor store licenses or liquor businesses within the state and shall not have any interest in any liquor distillery or wholesale liquor distributorship or business any place.
(Code 1982, § 3-53)
Sec. 10-5. - Applications for new licenses.
(a)
[Generally.] The provisions of this Code for general business licensing shall apply to applications for new licenses under this chapter, in addition to the following:
(1)
Fee. All applications for new licenses under this chapter shall be accompanied by a payment, in cash or by certified check, for the first year's license fee in the amount as set by the mayor and city council and business taxes provided for in this Code for the classification into which the applicant falls;
(2)
Additional information. The city application shall be a photocopy or other copy of all the appropriate state applications and forms, with a city supplemental application form provided by the city, as well as the following:
a.
A legal description of the property upon which the proposed premises are located, together with a plat of the property by a registered land surveyor;
b.
A certificate by a registered land surveyor that the proposed premises are not within 300 feet of any church or within 600 feet of any school, schoolground or college campus;
c.
A current photograph of the applicant;
d.
Fingerprints of the applicant, to be taken by the police department;
e.
A certificate from the building inspector as to the zoning classification of the proposed premises.
(b)
Oath. The application shall also contain a form of oath providing that the information disclosed in the application is true and correct and, providing further, that the applicant will abide by, observe and conduct his business according to the rules and regulations prescribed by the city, the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code (O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.), as amended, and the rules and regulations of the state department of revenue in respect thereto. The oath shall be taken by the applicant and the agent in charge of the establishment, if different from, or additional to, the applicant, and the oath shall be taken by the true owners.
(c)
False statement; misrepresentation. Any false statement or material misrepresentation in any application under this section shall be grounds for the revocation of any license granted under this section.
(Code 1982, § 3-54)
Sec. 10-6. - Agents.
Any application by any person for a license under this chapter shall name in the application one or more residents in the county as the agent and representative of the person to receive all communications, notices, services of process or other papers or documents on behalf of the person in connection with any matter arising out of, or connected with, the issuance, holding, suspension, revocation or other action with respect to any city license. The application shall give the mailing address of such persons, and the mailing to any such person at that address of any notice required to be given under this chapter or any other law shall be sufficient notice to the applicant. If any agent shall cease to be a resident of the county, another person shall immediately be appointed in writing in his place, and written notice shall be given to the clerk for stating the name and address of the new agent.
(Code 1982, § 3-55)
Sec. 10-7. - Tax records inquiries; preliminary approval required; furnishing data; additional requirements; period of processing.
(a)
The clerk shall cause an inquiry to be made into the city tax records to determine if the applicant or other party with interest in the application has any outstanding taxes or special assessments that are delinquent against his property or any other moneys owing to the city. No license shall be issued or renewed until such debts are paid in full.
(b)
All applications shall be approved or disapproved by the chief of police as a condition to action thereon and issuance of a license by the clerk. If the chief of police determines that the application is not in order, in view of the requirements of federal or state law, or this Code and any police department investigation, the clerk shall then notify the applicant of the reasons for not issuing the license to the applicant.
(c)
All applicants shall furnish all reasonable data, information and records requested of them by the clerk or chief of police and failure to furnish such data, information, contracts, documents, financial statements and other records within 30 days of the request shall automatically serve to dismiss the application.
(d)
The chief of police may require, in addition to the licensee, fingerprinting and a background investigation of any person interested in the application, including any person with a financial interest, such as stockholders, partners or employees, and any spouse, brother, sister, child or parent of any person with a financial interest. Should the applicant fail to produce such persons within 30 days of the request, the application may be denied by the clerk.
(e)
By filing an application, applicants agree to produce for oral interrogation any person requested by the chief of police and considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to such license. The failure to produce such person within 30 days after being requested to do so may result in a denial of the application by the clerk.
(f)
A period of 45 days shall be allowed for processing any application for a license required under this chapter. For investigative purposes, the chief of police may extend the 45-day processing period according to the licensee's compliance, should the chief of police deem it necessary.
(Code 1982, § 3-56)
Cross reference— Taxation, ch. 82.
Sec. 10-8. - Publishing of advertisements; signs.
(a)
At least once a week for two weeks immediately preceding city council approval of any license application under this chapter, the applicant shall cause advertisements to be placed in the newspaper for sheriff's advertisements in the county. The advertisements shall be in a form prescribed by the clerk and shall include the name and address of the applicant, the type of license applied for, the business location applied for and the time, date and location of the city council meeting on the application.
(b)
The applicant shall also cause to be placed upon the location of the proposed premises to be licensed, signs of a size, number and form prescribed by the clerk, stating the type of license applied for and the time, date and location of the city council meeting on the application.
(Code 1982, § 3-57)
Cross reference— Advertising, ch. 6.
Sec. 10-9. - Completion of premises proposed to be licensed.
Where a building in which any person intends to operate under the provisions of this chapter is, at the time of the application for the license, not in existence or not yet complete, a license may be issued for the location, provided the plans for the proposed building clearly show a compliance with the other provisions of this chapter. No sales shall be allowed in the establishment until it has been completed in accordance with the plans and is in conformity with all of the other provisions of this chapter and this Code.
(Code 1982, § 3-58)
Sec. 10-10. - Issuance of license to persons with prior convictions.
(a)
No license under this chapter shall be issued, renewed or transferred to any person where any individual having an interest either as owner, partner or stockholder, directly or indirectly, beneficial or absolute, shall have been convicted of any felony within five years immediately prior to the filing of the application, or convicted of any misdemeanor relating to any alcoholic beverage business or any municipal ordinance violation relating to any alcoholic beverage business within two years immediately prior to the filing of the application.
(b)
For the purposes of this section, the term "conviction" includes an adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond, in part or in whole, when charged with a crime, or the suspension or revocation of any alcoholic beverage license owned, in whole or in part, by persons listed in subsection (a) of this section.
(Code 1982, § 3-59)
Sec. 10-11. - Issuance or denial of license; resubmission of application.
(a)
The clerk shall deny a city license under this chapter on any of the following grounds:
(1)
Failure to meet state requirements for a state license;
(2)
Failure to pay required fees and taxes;
(3)
Failure to provide valid information, documents, etc., required by this chapter;
(4)
False information in the application or attached documents;
(5)
Improper residency of registered agents;
(6)
Failure to pass review by the chief of police;
(7)
Failure to post and maintain proper signs and advertisements required in this chapter;
(8)
Failure to meet distance or location requirements;
(9)
Prior convictions, as provided in section 10-10; or
(10)
Failure to meet any other requirements in this chapter for a license of the class applied for.
(b)
An application which has been denied shall not be resubmitted for a period of 12 months after the denial, unless there is a substantial change therein which would affect the reason for the original denial.
(c)
The city clerk may otherwise issue any city license provided for in this chapter after approval by the city council.
(Code 1982, § 3-60)
Sec. 10-12. - State regulations.
All licenses issued under this chapter shall be subject to the rules and regulations adopted by the state, as amended, and prescribed by the state department of revenue, and violation of such statutes and regulations shall be violations of this Code. In addition, all businesses, the applicant for the license, the agent in charge of the business and the owners thereof shall observe and obey the regulations provided in this article and any other applicable provisions of this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 3-61)
Sec. 10-13. - Books and records; open to inspection.
All persons holding wholesale or retail alcoholic beverage licenses from the city shall maintain accurate and complete records of all alcoholic beverages sold or delivered within the city and the collection and remittance of all taxes due thereon. Any establishment holding a license issued under this chapter, and the books and records pertaining thereto, shall, at all times during the period allowable by law for operation of the business, be open to inspection and audit by any officer of the police department, any license inspector of the city or any person designated by the chief of police or the city marshal. In addition, if the premises is being used after hours by employees, the owners or their agents, the premises may be inspected at such time by the persons designated in this section.
(Code 1982, §§ 3-44, 3-62)
Sec. 10-14. - Open containers prohibited; exception.
No bottle or other container of alcoholic beverage shall be opened or consumed by any person on the premises, or in any adjacent parking area, upon which the place of business is conducted and licensed under this chapter, whether the bottle or other container so opened or consumed was bought or obtained at that place of business or elsewhere, unless the premises is licensed under this chapter for consumption on the premises.
(Code 1982, § 3-63)
Sec. 10-15. - Transfer of license.
(a)
Scope. Licenses under this chapter shall not be transferrable, except as otherwise provided in this section.
(b)
Death of licensee. In case of the death of any person holding such a license, or any interest therein, the license may be transferred to the administrator, executor or the lawful heirs of the deceased person, in the discretion of the city council.
(c)
Withdrawal of partner. Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license from withdrawing from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of such license. Such a withdrawal shall not, however, serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership.
(d)
Transfer of location. Should a transfer of a location be approved, there shall be no pro rata return or refund of any license fee, and the new location shall require a new license under this chapter.
(e)
Addition of partners and stockholders. A licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined by the city council that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business, or for building new facilities and where it appears that neither the licensee himself, nor any partner or stockholder therein, receives, directly or indirectly, any of the additional capital invested. Under this subsection, an additional partner or new principal stockholder must be approved by the city council.
(f)
Transfer from agent to agent; name change. Where licenses are held by agents, upon the resignation or removal of an agent holding the alcoholic beverage license for a particular location, the partners, owners or stockholders of the business entity shall immediately appoint a new agent to hold the alcoholic beverage license, which agent shall apply to the chief of police of the city for the reissuance of the alcoholic beverage license in his name for the stated location. Upon a completion of the police background check and investigation as required in this article, the chief of police shall make a recommendation to the city council for the approval or disapproval of the transfer of agent. In such a transfer, it will be required that a new investigation fee be paid for the investigation of the new agent, but no new fees for the issuance of the alcoholic beverage license will be collected until the normal expiration date of the existing license. In such instances in which only the name of the establishment holding an alcoholic beverage license is to change, with no change in the ownership or agent holding the license, the partners, owners or stockholders shall notify the chief of police of the change of name, which notation shall be made on the records of the city and forwarded to the license inspector's office for a change on the records of such office. No charge will be made for the name change.
(Code 1982, § 3-64)
Sec. 10-16. - Furnishing to, purchase of or possession by persons under 21 years of age of alcoholic beverages.
(a)
Except as otherwise authorized by law:
(1)
No person knowingly, directly or through another person, shall furnish, cause to be furnished or permit any person in such person's employ to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person under 21 years of age;
(2)
No person under 21 years of age shall purchase, attempt to purchase or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverage;
(3)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent such person's age in any manner whatsoever for the purpose of illegally obtaining any alcoholic beverage;
(4)
No person, knowingly or intentionally, shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for, or on behalf of, a person under 21 years of age; and
(5)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent his identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(b)
The provisions contained in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23(b), (c), (d) and (e) are hereby adopted with like effect as if recited in this subsection, and any future amendment or repeal of such provisions shall amend or repeal the pertinent provisions of this section.
(Code 1982, § 3-67)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 10-17. - Duration and renewal of license.
Except as otherwise provided, duration and renewal of licenses under this chapter shall be from January 1 through December 31 of each year; and no licensee shall have any vested right to the renewal of any city license. No license under this chapter may be renewed if the license could be denied under this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 3-69(a))
Sec. 10-18. - Location of retail businesses.
No retail business licensed pursuant to this chapter shall be located within the boundaries of the R-1 Residential, R-2 Residential, I-N Institutional and I Industrial zoning districts.
(Code 1982, § 3-70; Res. No. 14-0515-23, 5-15-2014)
Cross reference— Businesses, ch. 22; zoning, app. B.
Sec. 10-19. - Collection of taxes from distributors, wholesale dealers and manufacturers.
At the time of delivery of any alcoholic beverages as set forth in this chapter, such wholesale dealer, distributor or manufacturer shall collect from the retail dealer the taxes imposed by this chapter, giving a correct and proper receipt therefor, and hold such taxes in trust for the city until the tax is remitted to the city as provided in this chapter. It shall be unlawful for any wholesaler, distributor, manufacturer or person to sell or deliver any alcoholic beverages as set forth in this chapter to any retail dealer in the city, or for any such alcoholic beverages to be transported into the city for resale by any means whatsoever, except to a licensed wholesale distributor, without collecting the taxes thereon, as set forth in this section, at the time of such sale or delivery.
(Code 1982, § 3-71(a))
Sec. 10-20. - Duties of retail dealers generally.
All retail dealers in any alcoholic beverages as set forth in this chapter, and all persons selling any such alcoholic beverages at retail within the city, shall keep a correct record of all purchases of each of such alcoholic beverages, and shall demand of and require all persons from whom they purchase such alcoholic beverages to furnish and deliver to them with such alcoholic beverages a correct invoice of each purchase. Such invoice shall be kept and preserved by such retailer at his place of business for a period of one year from the date of such purchase, and such invoice shall be open and subject to inspection by any authorized representative of the city, at all reasonable times. Such retailer shall, on or before the close of business on Monday of each week, make a report to the city clerk showing the number of containers of each and all such alcoholic beverages purchased during the previous week, and up to the close of business on Saturday of the week, showing the size and contents of each container, the name and address of all dealers and persons from whom purchases were made, and the dates of delivery of such containers. Such report shall be accompanied by a remittance of all taxes due under this chapter for all purchases made, except purchases on which taxes have been paid to the manufacturers, distributors or wholesalers at the time of delivery as provided in this chapter and, for all such taxes paid to such manufacturers, distributors or wholesalers at the time of delivery as provided in this chapter, such retailers shall furnish the city with the proper original receipts.
(Code 1982, § 3-72)
Sec. 10-21. - Refusal to make reports or remittances.
If any wholesaler or distributor fails or refuses to timely make the reports or remittances as provided in this chapter, the clerk shall notify such party, in writing, by mail or otherwise and, if such reports are not made and the taxes paid within five days from the date of such notice, the clerk shall proceed to assess the amount of taxes due by such wholesaler or distributor from the best information available, plus ten percent thereof as a penalty, and cause an execution for the assessment to be issued at once, and proceed to collect such taxes, penalty and costs as provided by ordinance for the collection of any other delinquent taxes, and in addition thereto, prosecute such wholesaler or distributor in the municipal court for such offense.
(Code 1982, § 3-73)
Sec. 10-22. - Processing and investigation fee.
In addition to the license application requirements of this chapter, a processing and investigation fee in the amount as set by the mayor and city council, in addition to other fees and taxes required in this chapter, shall be deposited with the application to cover the expense of advertising, investigating and processing of the application and the fee shall be nonrefundable.
(Code 1982, § 3-74)
Sec. 10-23. - Policies for suspension and revocation of licenses generally.
The following policies relating to suspension or revocation of all licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages and naturally fermented wines in the city shall be impartially enforced:
(1)
Whenever there is clear and convincing evidence, to the satisfaction of the chief of police, of any violation of state law or municipal ordinance by any license holder or employee thereof within the city, at any time after service of warrant, the chief of police shall send written notice of any violation to the license holder and the license committee chairperson, who shall call a meeting of the license committee within ten days from the date of such notification for consideration of any alleged violation. The license holder shall be notified, in writing, of the time and place of such meeting and may appear and be heard.
(2)
All of the following provisions shall apply to the 12-month period from April 1 until license renewal the following year:
a.
First violation. For a first violation, there shall be an immediate 15-day suspension of privilege and reinstatement fee thereafter in the amount as set by the mayor and city council.
b.
Second violation. For a second violation, there shall be a suspension of privilege for a 30-day period and a reinstatement fee thereafter in the amount as set by the mayor and city council.
c.
Third violation. For a third violation, there shall be a suspension of privilege for a period of 60 days and a reinstatement fee thereafter in the amount as set by the mayor and city council.
d.
Fourth violation. For a fourth violation, the licensee shall be notified to show cause before the mayor and city council why the license should not be revoked or, in the alternative, why there should not be a suspension of privilege for a 90-day period and a reinstatement fee thereafter in the amount as set by the mayor and city council.
(3)
All decisions suspending or revoking any license shall be in writing, with the reasons stated for such suspension or revocation, and shall be mailed or delivered to the licensee.
(4)
Upon timely application within ten days after mailing or delivery of the decision of the license committee, any licensee shall be afforded a hearing before the mayor and city council, with an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine opposing witnesses.
(Code 1982, § 3-1)
Sec. 10-24. - Copies of state licenses required.
(a)
All applicants for licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall file with the city clerk a copy of any state license for such sales issued to such applicant within 30 days after issuance thereof by state authorities.
(b)
All applicants for the renewal of any license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall file with the renewal application a copy of any state license for such sales currently held by the applicant.
(Code 1982, § 3-2)
Sec. 10-25. - Establishments providing on-premises consumption; underage persons prohibited, exceptions.
(a)
Any person holding an over-the-counter beer license or wine pouring license who does business in an establishment wherein the consumption of alcoholic beverages is allowed or permitted within such establishment or on any adjoining premises is prohibited, during regular business hours, from admitting to such establishment or to the adjoining premises persons under the minimum legal drinking age as established in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not be construed to prohibit persons under the legal drinking age from entering a restaurant, which is defined as any public place maintained and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment and seating capacity for at least 50 people, and having sufficient employees to prepare and serve suitable food to its guests. Such restaurant shall serve at least one meal a day for at least six days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating.
(c)
The purpose of this section is to prohibit the presence of persons under the legal drinking age in establishments which derive a major portion of their revenues from the sale of alcoholic beverages, but this section shall not apply to any employee of the licensee.
(Code 1982, § 3-3)
Sec. 10-26. - Video recording systems.
(a)
Any licensee who sells any alcoholic beverage packaged to go is hereby required to install a continuous video recording system, dedicated to each register area, with cameras and lenses of a type, number and location approved by the chief of the police department. Such cameras must be capable of producing a retrievable and identifiable image on film or tape that can be made a permanent record and can be enlarged through projection or other means.
(b)
The retrievable image from the security camera must be preserved for a period of at least 21 days from the date the image is retrieved and shall be made available for inspection by the police department, upon request.
(Amd. of 2-18-1999; Res. No. 17-0721-55, 7-20-2017)
Sec. 10-27. - Sunday sales.
(a)
Establishments holding a license for the sale of malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits are permitted to sell and serve only between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sundays.
(b)
Any person, wholesaler or retailer who shall violate the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as prescribed by law.
(Res. No. 13-1121-60, § 10-80, 11-21-2013)
Secs. 10-28—10-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - MALT BEVERAGES

Sec. 10-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Container means the can, bottle or barrel in which any malt beverage is placed and shall not be deemed to mean the case or carton in which such cans, bottles or barrels are packed.
(Code 1982, § 3-20)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
State Law reference— Definitions, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-1-2, 3-5-1.
Sec. 10-62. - License application; fingerprinting of applicant; processing.
A printed application, to be supplied by the city clerk, shall be completed and filed by any applicant for a malt beverage license within the city. When completed, the application shall be returned to the city clerk's office at city hall, at which time the applicant shall be fingerprinted by the police department. The applicant's fingerprints shall be submitted to the appropriate law enforcement agencies in accordance with state law. The application will then be processed by the police department, fire department, building inspector and county board of health, and returned to the city clerk, who, in turn, will forward the application to the chairperson of the license committee, who will bring the application before the mayor and city council for approval or disapproval.
(Code 1982, § 3-21)
State Law reference— Authority to license malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 10-63. - Rules and regulations.
All rules and regulations governing and controlling the sale of malt beverages shall be posted, for information and notice to the public, beside the licensee's license. Such rules and regulations shall be published and distributed to the various malt beverage licensees, and a written receipt, signed by the licensee, acknowledging receipt of such rules and regulations shall be obtained from each such licensee.
(Code 1982, § 3-22)
Sec. 10-64. - Excise tax levied and imposed; reports; payment.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, there is hereby levied and imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling malt beverages within the city an excise tax in the amount as set by the mayor and city council per 12-ounce container, or fraction thereof, of malt beverages sold by such wholesale dealer within the city.
(b)
All malt beverages sold in, or from, a barrel or bulk container, and being commonly known as tap or draft beer, shall not be subject to the excise tax provided for in subsection (a) of this section, but, in lieu thereof, there is hereby imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling such malt beverages within the city an excise tax in the amount as set by the mayor and city council for each barrel or bulk container having a capacity of 15½ gallons sold by such wholesale dealer within the city and at a similar rate for fractional parts thereof.
(c)
Each wholesale dealer who sells malt beverages within the city shall file a report by the tenth day of each month, itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of all malt beverages, by size and type of container, for the month sold or disposed of within the city. Each such wholesale dealer shall remit to the city, on the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made, the amount of excise tax due in accordance with this section.
(d)
No decal, stamp or other identifying mark shall be required on malt beverages sold within the city.
(Code 1982, § 3-23)
Cross reference— Taxation, ch. 82.
State Law reference— Municipal malt beverage excise tax, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80 et seq.
Sec. 10-65. - Excise tax in addition to other taxes.
The excise taxes provided for in section 10-64 shall be in addition to any license fee, tax or charge which may be imposed upon the business of selling malt beverages at retail or wholesale within the city.
(Code 1982, § 3-24)
Cross reference— Taxation, ch. 82.
Sec. 10-66. - Records required.
(a)
Each wholesaler selling, shipping or delivering malt beverages in the city shall keep accurate invoices of all sales of malt beverages and shall make the invoices available for inspection by the city. Such records shall be maintained for a period of at least one year.
(b)
All retail dealers who sell malt beverages at retail within the city shall maintain a record of their purchases from all wholesalers and from any other person from whom they may purchase malt beverages, showing the quantities purchased and the dates thereof, and shall maintain such records for a period of at least one year.
(Code 1982, § 3-25)
Sec. 10-67. - Failure to make timely reports and remittances; filing false or fraudulent reports; penalties.
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to two percent of the total amount due during the first 30-day period following the date such report and remittance were due and a further penalty of two percent of the amount of such remittance for each successive 30-day period, or any portion thereof, during which such report and remittance are not filed. The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the wholesale dealer making such report liable for a penalty equal to two percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report. Such failure to make a timely report or remittance, or the filing of a false or fraudulent report, shall also constitute grounds for the revocation of the business license issued by the city to such wholesale dealer.
(Code 1982, § 3-26)
Sec. 10-68. - Hours of sales.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, or permit to be sold, any malt beverages, other than between the hours of 7:00 a.m. to 2:00 a.m., Monday through Friday, inclusive, and 7:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight on Saturday.
(Code 1982, § 3-27)
Sec. 10-69. - Reserved.
Sec. 10-70. - Holders of over-the-counter beer and wine pouring licenses to observe closing regulations; exception.
No establishment wherein the holder of any over-the-counter beer license or wine pouring license conducts business as a licensee shall be or remain open or in operation for any purpose on Sunday, or on any day on which the sale of alcoholic beverages is prohibited by state law or municipal ordinance.
(Code 1982, § 3-28.1; Res. No. 08-1120-38, 11-20-2008)
Sec. 10-71. - Sale near churches and schools.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell any wine or malt beverage at a place within 300 feet of any church or school within the city. Any person who shall violate this section shall, if licensed, be liable to the suspension or revocation of said license. This section shall not apply not any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 1981, nor to the renewal of such license; nor shall this section apply at any location for which a new license is applied for, if the sale of the wine and beer was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application. For the purposes of this section, the term "school" does not include college or university.
(b)
Distance shall be measured in linear feet by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the front door of the structure from which malt beverages are sold or offered for sale;
(2)
In a straight line to the nearest public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway;
(3)
Along such public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway by the nearest route;
(4)
To the front door of the church building, or to the nearest portion of the schoolgrounds, whichever is applicable.
(Code 1982, § 3-29; Res. No. 10-0415-19, 4-15-2010; Res. No. 10-0415-20, 4-15-2010)
Cross reference— Distance of distilled spirits from churches and schools, § 10-138; zoning, app. B.

ARTICLE III. - WINE

Sec. 10-101. - Applicability of chapter provisions.
All restrictions against sales and all requirements for granting of wholesale or retail licenses pertaining to the sale of malt beverages within the city shall be applicable to wine sales and licenses made or granted under this article.
(Code 1982, § 3-40)
State Law reference— Municipal wine license, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-40.
Sec. 10-102. - Tax imposed and levied.
There is imposed and levied upon every dealer at retail of naturally fermented wines a tax in the amount as set by the mayor and city council per wine gallon and a pro rata tax upon fractional gallons on all such wines sold or distributed in the city.
(Code 1982, § 3-41)
Cross reference— Taxation, ch. 82.
State Law reference— Municipal excise tax on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60.
Sec. 10-103. - Payment of tax.
The tax levied in section 10-102 shall be paid by retailers to wholesalers at the time of delivery of such wines to the retailer. It shall be the duty of each such retailer to pay, and it shall be the duty of each wholesaler of each case delivered by such wholesaler to such retailer to receive, the proper amount of the tax levied in section 10-102. It shall further be the duty of such wholesaler to furnish the city clerk with duplicate invoices of all sales of wine to local retail dealers with the monthly case tax remittance report and to remit such taxes to the clerk by the tenth day of the succeeding calendar month, less a five percent discount.
(Code 1982, § 3-42)
Cross reference— Taxation, ch. 82.
Sec. 10-104. - Failure to pay or remit taxes.
It shall be unlawful for any retailer to possess, display or sell any wine delivered in any case on which such taxes have not been paid, and for any wholesaler to fail to remit to the city clerk all such taxes collected as prescribed by this article.
(Code 1982, § 3-43)
Cross reference— Taxation, ch. 82.
Secs. 10-105—10-130. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - DISTILLED SPIRITS[2]

Sec. 10-131. - License required.
(a)
No distilled spirits shall be stored, delivered, sold or manufactured in the city, except under a license issued pursuant to this article.
(b)
Licenses may be issued under this article for the manufacturing, wholesaling and retailing of distilled spirits.
(Code 1982, § 3-51(a), (c))
Sec. 10-132. - License fee for liquor dispensed and sold by the drink.
(a)
The governing authority of the city hereby sets the fee for the privilege to sell distilled spirits at $5,000.00 which shall not be subject to proration.
(b)
A license holder shall, at all times, maintain a policy of general liability insurance of at least $500,000.00, proof of which must accompany the license application and shall be subject to verification at all times during which the license holder's license is valid.
(Res. No. 12-0315-17, 3-15-2012; Res. No. 12-0419-23, 4-19-2012)
Sec. 10-133. - Billiard tables, pinball machines, music boxes, etc.
No retail licensee for any distilled spirits within the city shall permit or maintain on the premises any billiard tables, pinball machines, music boxes or jukeboxes, or any other video or electronic amusement games of any type.
(Code 1982, § 3-68)
Sec. 10-134. - Renewal of license.
In any given year, applicants for renewal of their licenses under this article for the sale of distilled spirits for the next year shall file their application with the city administrator on or before December 10. Upon payment of such fees and license taxes to the city administrator as are applicable, on or before December 31 and no later than January 1, the city administrator shall issue the new license to the applicant.
(Code 1982, § 3-69(b))
Sec. 10-135. - Monthly reports.
Each wholesaler or distributor who has sold distilled spirits within the city shall file a report by the tenth day of each month, itemizing, for the preceding calendar month, the exact quantities of all distilled spirits, by size and type of container, for the month, sold within the city. Each such wholesaler or distributor shall remit to the city, on the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made, the amount of excise taxies due, in accordance with the provisions of section 10-140.
(Code 1982, § 3-71(b))
Sec. 10-136. - Limitation on number of licenses within a family or corporation.
(a)
No application for a license to sell distilled spirits at retail shall be granted where the person applying for the license or members of such person's family or any corporation in which the person owns an interest already holds any interest in two or more licenses to sell distilled spirits by the package at retail within the state. For the purposes of this section, the term "family" means any person related to the applicant within the first degree of consanguinity and affinity under civil law.
(b)
As used in this section, an interest in a license shall be deemed to exist if the person involved is the outright owner of the license; a co-owner of the license; a partner in a partnership which owns all, or any part, of a license; or a stockholder in any corporation organized for pecuniary gain which owns all, or any part, of a license. Notwithstanding any of the other provisions of this section, no one person shall be deemed to have more than one interest in any one license.
(c)
All applications for a license under this article, both original and renewal, must be accompanied by a full and complete statement, under oath, of information relative to any and all interest, as defined in subsection (b) of this section, in retail liquor stores. This shall include the names and addresses of all persons interested in the ownership of the business of selling at retail package liquor, together with any interest each person or any member of his family has in any other retail liquor store and any other similar information called for by the chief of police.
(Code 1982, § 3-75)
Sec. 10-136.1. - Number and location of licensed premises limited.
(a)
Notwithstanding any other provision herein, the total number of licenses issued for sale of distilled spirits shall not exceed one for every 3,500 residents as determined by the most recent decennial census or such other population number determined and provided by any authorized agency of this state.
(b)
In order to receive or maintain a license to sell distilled spirits, any such establishment must be located and front on either Highway 49, Highway 96 or Highway 341 (or such other name assigned to said highways as they pass within the city).
(Res. No. 05-1020-62, 10-20-2005)
Sec. 10-137. - Restrictions on other merchandise.
A licensee of a retail establishment under this article shall not operate the business in connection with any other mercantile establishment, provided this shall not prohibit a licensee from operating in the same building complex with other businesses. No retailer under this article shall sell, offer for sale, display or keep any other commodity in stock at his place of business where packaged distilled spirits are offered for sale, except for the following, all of which may be sold by the retailer at his option:
(1)
Beverages containing no alcohol and commonly used to dilute distilled spirits;
(2)
Wines and malt beverages, when properly licensed;
(3)
Glasses, stirring sticks and similar paraphernalia commonly used in the consumption of alcoholic beverages;
(4)
Tobacco products; and
(5)
Packaged ice.
(Code 1982, § 3-76)
Sec. 10-138. - Distance from churches and schools.
No person shall be licensed by the city under this article when the proposed place of business is within 300 feet of any church, or within 600 feet of any school building, schoolground or college campus.
(Code 1982, § 3-77)
Cross reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages near churches and schools, § 10-71.
Sec. 10-139. - Wholesale and manufacturing licenses.
Wholesale and manufacturing licenses shall be issued only to such applicants who are licensed by the state to distribute distilled spirits. Application for wholesale distribution and manufacturing licenses shall be made as set forth in this chapter, and applicants are subject to such qualifications and restrictions as set forth in this chapter. All applications shall be accompanied by the appropriate license fee and investigation fee as provided by this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 3-78)
Sec. 10-140. - Schedule of license fees and excise taxes.
In addition to any other business license fees that may be applicable under this Code, the annual license fees and excise taxes shall be paid for the type of license being acquired as set by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 3-79)
Secs. 10-141—10-160. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - ON-PREMISES CONSUMPTION OF BEER, WINE AND DISTILLED SPIRITS

Sec. 10-161. - Applicability.
All restrictions against sales and all requirements for granting licenses contained in this chapter shall apply for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink for on-premises consumption.
(Res. No. 12-0216-06, § 10-140, 2-16-2012)
Sec. 10-162. - Locations where allowed; exception.
(a)
For the purposes of this article, the term "premises" shall mean the enclosed, under roof area of a single building and shall include such outdoor patios and open areas directly adjacent to and contiguous with the licensed structure.
(b)
The provision of this section shall not apply to special event permit licenses.
(Res. No. 12-0216-06, § 10-140, 2-16-2012)
