Chapter 2 - ADMINISTRATION[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Oaths.
All official oaths, unless otherwise specifically prescribed, shall be for the faithful performance of official duties.
(Code 1982, § 2-1)
Charter reference— Oath of office of mayor and aldermen, § 8.
Sec. 2-2. - Bonds to be payable to city.
The bonds of all city officers, and other bonds required to be given, when no provision is made, shall be payable to the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-3. - Attestation and approval of bonds.
Official bonds may be attested by the mayor, mayor pro tem, aldermen or city clerk, or by any officer of the state authorized to attest deeds, and such bonds shall be approved by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-4. - Conditions of bonds.
The conditions of official bonds shall be for the faithful performance of the duties of the office, and such other conditions as may be specially prescribed.
(Code 1982, § 2-4)
Sec. 2-5. - Bond conditions of liability.
Every official bond executed by and for a city officer or employee shall obligate the principal and sureties thereon as provided in O.C.G.A. § 45-4-24, which section is hereby adopted by reference, as fully as if set out at length in this section.
(Code 1982, § 2-5)
State Law reference— Liability of officers, O.C.G.A. § 36-33-4.
Sec. 2-6. - Validity of nonconforming bonds.
Whenever any officer required by law to give an official bond shall act under a bond which is not payable and conditioned or not approved and filed as prescribed by law, such bond shall not be void, but shall stand in the place of the official bond, subject, on its condition being broken, to all the remedies, including actions, which the persons aggrieved might have maintained on the official bond.
(Code 1982, § 2-6)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 45-4-20.
Sec. 2-7. - Relief of sureties; insufficiency of sureties.
When the surety on any bond, given by any officer for the performance of any public duty, shall give notice, in writing, to the mayor of such surety's desire to be relieved from future liability, for good cause therein stated and sworn to, or when any such surety shall, in the opinion of the mayor, formed upon satisfactory evidence, become insufficient, it shall be the mayor's duty to require of such officer a new bond and surety.
(Code 1982, § 2-7)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 45-4-9.
Sec. 2-8. - Records management program.
Pursuant to the provisions of section 11 of the Georgia Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99), the city establishes a records management program, and does hereby adopt the state rules and regulations as a basis for establishing such program.
(Code 1982, § 2-8)
Sec. 2-9. - Challenge of applicant for registration to vote by other electors; notice and hearing; right of appeal.
(a)
Any elector of the city may challenge the qualifications of any person applying to register to vote in the city and may challenge the qualifications of any elector of the city whose name appears on the list of electors. Such challenges shall be in writing and shall distinctly specify the grounds of the challenge.
(b)
Upon such challenge being filed with the board of registrars, the registrars shall set a hearing on such challenge. Notice of the date, time and place of the hearing shall be served upon the person whose qualifications are being challenged, along with a copy of such challenge and upon the elector making the challenge. The person being challenged shall receive at least three days' notice of the date, time and place of the hearing. Such notice shall be served either by first class mail, addressed to the mailing address shown on the person's voter registration records or in the manner provided in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-228(c).
(c)
The burden shall be on the elector making the challenge to prove that the person being challenged is not qualified to remain on the list of electors. The board of registrars shall have the authority to issue subpoenas for the attendance of witnesses and the production of books, papers and other material upon application by the person whose qualifications are being challenged or the elector making the challenge. The party requesting such subpoenas shall be responsible to serve such subpoenas and, if necessary, to enforce the subpoenas by application to the superior court. Any witness so subpoenaed, and after attending, shall be allowed and paid the same mileage and fee as allowed and paid witnesses in civil actions in the superior court.
(d)
After the hearing provided for in this section, the registrars shall determine such challenge and shall notify the parties of their decision. If the registrars uphold the challenge, the person's application for registration shall be rejected or the person's name removed from the list of electors, as appropriate. The elector shall be notified of such decision, in writing, either by first class mail addressed to the mailing address shown on the person's voter registration records or in the manner provided in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-228(c) for other notices.
(e)
Either party shall have a right of appeal from the decision of the registrars to the superior court by filing a petition with the clerk of the superior court within ten days after the date of the decision of the registrars. A copy of such petition shall be served upon the other parties and the registrars. Unless and until the decision of the registrars is reversed by the court, the decision of the registrars shall stand.
(Code 1982, § 2-9)
Charter reference— Elections generally, §§ 3, 5—7, 28A.
State Law reference— Elections, O.C.G.A. tit. 21; challenge of applicant for registration, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-229.

ARTICLE II. - MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL[2]

Sec. 2-41. - Chief executive officer.
The mayor shall be the chief executive officer of the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-20)
Sec. 2-42. - Duties of mayor.
It shall be the duty of the mayor to:
(1)
Preside over the deliberations of the mayor and city council.
(2)
Have the laws and ordinances of the city put in full force.
(3)
Supervise all subordinate officers and cause them to perform the duties connected with their respective offices.
(4)
Adopt such measures as may be deemed proper to ensure the enforcement of this Code and the ordinances of the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-21)
Charter reference— General duties and powers of mayor, § 14.
Sec. 2-43. - Presiding officer.
The mayor shall preside at all meetings of the mayor and city council, and in his absence, the mayor pro tem shall preside. If the mayor and mayor pro tem are absent, the city council shall elect a presiding officer or chairperson pro tem who shall preside.
(Code 1982, § 2-22)
Sec. 2-44. - Mayor pro tem.
A mayor pro tem shall be elected by the mayor and city council at its first meeting after the members' election at the time of election of other officers. The mayor pro tem shall serve during the sickness, absence or disability of the mayor and shall be clothed with all rights and privileges of the mayor and perform the mayor's duties.
(Code 1982, § 2-23)
Charter reference— Selection of mayor pro tem, § 3.
Sec. 2-45. - Councilmembers' duties.
The duties of the aldermen shall be to:
(1)
Legislate for the city.
(2)
Act on such committees and attend to the executive part of the government of the city as the mayor and city council may determine from time to time.
(Code 1982, § 2-24)
Sec. 2-46. - Compensation.
The mayor and councilmembers shall receive salaries as shall be fixed as provided in the Charter of the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-25)
Charter reference— Compensation of mayor and councilmen, § 13.
Sec. 2-47. - Regular meetings.
The regular meetings of the mayor and city council shall be held at the council chamber on the third Thursday night in each month.
(Code 1982, § 2-26)
Sec. 2-48. - Called meetings.
Extra meetings of the mayor and city council may be called by the mayor in case of necessity or emergency, in which event, the members of the city council not out of the city shall be notified. Any business may be transacted at a called meeting that may be done at a regular meeting.
(Code 1982, § 2-27)
Sec. 2-49. - Minutes of meetings.
Minutes are to be kept of all the transactions of the mayor and city council, showing, among other things, which members are present, and how each member voted on all motions, where an aye and nay vote is called for. All contracts shall be spread on the minutes. No paid claim shall be placed on the minutes, except when ordered by the mayor and city council, but the city clerk shall keep a record of claims paid in a book kept for such purpose.
(Code 1982, § 2-28)
Sec. 2-50. - Parliamentary procedure at meetings.
The proceedings of the mayor and city council shall be governed by the general rules of parliamentary law and practice for other deliberative bodies, so far as applicable and not inconsistent with the special rules of the mayor and city council, the Charter and the ordinances of the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-29)
Sec. 2-51. - Duties upon passage of ordinances; publication discretionary.
After the passage of an ordinance, the ordinance shall be signed by the mayor and transcribed by the city clerk in the book of ordinances, and shall be published in a newspaper of the city, at the discretion of the mayor.
(Code 1982, § 2-30)
Sec. 2-52. - Effective date of ordinances.
Ordinances which do not provide for their taking effect at a different time shall take effect immediately after their passage, without reference to their publication in a newspaper.
(Code 1982, § 2-31)
Sec. 2-53. - Appointment of committees.
At the first regular meeting following each election, the mayor shall appoint from the city council, by order entered on the minutes, such committees as may be necessary for the proper government of the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-32)
Sec. 2-54. - Community development committee.
The duties of the community development committee shall be to:
(1)
Perform and coordinate any action or activity required on behalf of the city in the implementation and execution of any federal or state grant.
(2)
Coordinate such actions and activities with the mayor and city council, the Middle Georgia Regional Development Center and other state or federal agencies.
(3)
Perform such other duties as may be required.
(Code 1982, § 2-33)

DIVISION 2. - CODE OF ETHICS

Sec. 2-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City official means the mayor, all members of the city council and any and all other persons appointed to represent the city on any board or commission unless specifically exempted from this division by the mayor and city council.
Complaint means a written statement containing specific allegations of misconduct by a city official; provided, however, such allegations must be filed within six months of the discovery of the alleged misconduct.
Interest means any direct pecuniary benefit, which is not a remote interest held by, or accruing to, a city official as a result of a contract or transaction that is, or may be, the subject of an official act or action by, or with, the city.
(Amd. of 1-20-2000)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 2-112. - Findings.
The city is governed by a representative democracy. Persons who are elected, appointed and hired to serve the public are required to fulfill their public duties in a manner that is fair, faithful and honest in order to preserve the public trust. This requires that the government be conducted in an open manner, when appropriate, so that the public will have access to information about the decisions that are made that affect them. Public trust also requires that acts which are contrary to the public interest be defined and prohibited; ethical behavior be encouraged; and unethical behavior be discouraged in a process that is fundamentally fair.
(Ord. of 11-18-1999, intro. ¶)
Sec. 2-113. - Purpose; adoption.
(a)
It is essential to the proper administration and operation of the city that the public servants be, and give the appearance of being, independent and impartial; public office not be used for private gain; and there be public confidence in the integrity of the mayor and city council. In recognition of this purpose, a code of ethics for all city officials is hereby adopted.
(b)
The purpose of the code of ethics is to:
(1)
Encourage high ethical standards in official conduct by city officials.
(2)
Establish guidelines for ethical standards of conduct for all such officials by setting forth the acts or actions that are incompatible with the best interests of the city.
(3)
Require disclosure by such officials of private financial or other interest in manners affecting such interest.
(4)
Serve as a basis for disciplining persons who refuse to abide by its terms.
(Ord. of 11-18-1999, art. I)
Sec. 2-114. - Applicability.
The provisions of the code of ethics shall be applicable to the mayor and all members of the city council and any and all other persons appointed to represent the city on any board or commission unless specifically exempted from this division by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of 11-18-1999, art. II)
Sec. 2-115. - Standards of conduct.
(a)
No city official shall:
(1)
Use such position to secure special privileges or exemptions for such person or others, or secure confidential information for any purpose other than official responsibilities;
(2)
In any matter before the city council or board or commission in which he has a substantial interest, fail to disclose such interest prior to any discussion or vote;
(3)
Directly or indirectly receive, or agree to receive, any compensation, gift, reward or gratuity in any matter or proceeding connected with, or related to, the duties of his office, except as may be provided by law;
(4)
Enter into any contract with the city, except as specifically authorized by state statute. Any city official who has a proprietary interest in an agency doing business with the city shall make that interest known, in writing, to the city council;
(5)
Use public funds in any manner, except in a manner which is for the general welfare of the people and not for personal economic gain;
(6)
Grant or make available to any person any consideration, treatment, advantage or favor beyond that which it is the general practice to grant or make available to the public at large;
(7)
By conduct, give a reasonable basis for the impression that any person can improperly influence him or unduly enjoy his favor in the performance of official acts.
(b)
This section shall in no way prohibit:
(1)
A nonpecuniary gift of insignificant value;
(2)
An award publicly presented in recognition of public service;
(3)
A commercially reasonable loan or other financial transaction made in the ordinary course of business by an institution or individual authorized by the laws of the state to engage in the making of such loan or financial transaction;
(4)
Campaign contributions made and reported in accordance with state law.
(Ord. of 11-18-1999, art. III)
Sec. 2-116. - Board of ethics created; composition.
(a)
There is hereby created a board of ethics, consisting of five residents of the city to be appointed as provided in subsections (b) and (c) of this section. Each member of the board of ethics shall have been a resident of the city for at least one year immediately preceding the date of taking office and shall remain a resident of the city while serving as a member of the board of ethics. No person shall serve as a member of the board of ethics if the person has, or has had within the preceding one-year period, any interest in any contract, transaction or official action of the city.
(b)
The mayor and councilmembers shall each designate two qualified citizens to provide a pool of 14 individuals who have consented to serve as members of the board of ethics and who will be available for a period of two years to be called upon to serve if a board of ethics is appointed.
(c)
The city administrator shall maintain a listing of the 14 qualified citizens. Should it be determined a complaint warrants a hearing before the board of ethics, the mayor and city council, at the first public meeting after such determination, shall draw names randomly from the listing of qualified citizens until the specified five members of the board of ethics have been appointed. Such board of ethics will elect one of its members to serve as chairperson.
(d)
The members of the board of ethics shall serve without compensation. The mayor and city council shall provide space for the board of ethics. Subject to budgetary procedures and requirements of the city, the city shall provide the board of ethics with such supplies and equipment as may be reasonably necessary for the board of ethics to perform its duties and responsibilities.
(Ord. of 11-18-1999, art. IV)
Sec. 2-117. - Duties and powers of board of ethics.
(a)
The constituted board of ethics shall have the following duties and powers:
(1)
Establish procedures, rules and regulations governing its internal organization and conduct of its affairs;
(2)
Hold a hearing within 60 days after the receipt of a complaint. Failure to hold a hearing within the specified time shall result in the complaint being deferred to mayor and city council for disposition;
(3)
Receive and hear complaints of violations of the standards required in this division;
(4)
Make such investigation and response to a complaint as it deems necessary to determine whether any person has violated any provision of this division;
(5)
Hold such hearings and make such inquiries as deemed necessary to investigate and rule upon complaints;
(6)
Report its findings to the mayor and city council within five days of the completion of the hearing for such action as the mayor and city council deem appropriate.
(Ord. of 11-18-1999, art. V)
Sec. 2-118. - Violations; disciplinary action.
Violations of the code of ethics shall subject the offender to disciplinary action by the mayor and city council that may include censure, public or private reprimand and/or request for resignation.
(Ord. of 11-18-1999, art. VI)

DIVISION 3. - CITY CLERK AND TREASURER[4]

Sec. 2-141. - Appointment.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City clerk and city treasurer shall refer to the city clerk and treasurer.
(b)
There shall be an officer known as the city clerk and treasurer, who shall also be the ex officio tax receiver and tax collector, who shall be appointed and hold office as provided in the Charter.
(Code 1982, § 2-40)
Charter reference— Appointment, duties, §§ 18, 19, 38.
Sec. 2-142. - Filling vacancies in office.
If any vacancy in the office of the city clerk shall occur, the mayor shall fill the office by appointment until the next regular meeting of the mayor and city council after such vacancy occurs, at which time, the mayor and city council shall elect an officer to fill the unexpired term.
(Code 1982, § 2-41)
Sec. 2-143. - Oath and bond.
The city clerk and treasurer, before entering on the discharge of his official duties, shall take and subscribe to an oath and give bond to faithfully perform the duties as clerk and treasurer, tax collector and tax receiver. The bond shall be in such sum as the mayor and city council may prescribe and shall have good and solvent security to be approved by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 2-42)
Charter reference— Required bond, § 19(f).
State Law reference— Indemnification of public officers and employees, O.C.G.A. § 45-9-1 et seq.; personal liability of municipal officers, O.C.G.A. § 36-33-4.
Sec. 2-144. - Duties.
The duties of the city clerk and treasurer, as such, and as ex officio tax collector and tax receiver, shall be to:
(1)
Attend every meeting of the mayor and city council and keep a fair record of the proceedings thereof in a book to be provided for such purpose.
(2)
Issue all summonses, writs, processes and other papers necessary for the enforcement of the laws, ordinances and regulations of the city, or which may be required of such officer.
(3)
Issue all licenses to persons entitled, upon their compliance with the requirements in relation thereto, and keep a record of the issuance of such licenses in a separate book to be kept for such purpose, in which such officer shall state the name of the person applying for the license, the nature of the license and the amount received.
(4)
Keep a supply of such other official forms, as may be necessary.
(5)
Preserve and keep all records and files of the mayor and city council.
(6)
Transcribe from the minutes in a separate book, known as an ordinance book, all ordinances passed by the mayor and city council from time to time.
(7)
Publish such notices as are required to be published.
(8)
Be receiver of tax returns, make up the digest, collect property taxes and do such other things in reference to taxes as may be required by ordinance.
(9)
Collect all moneys from every other source due the city not otherwise provided for in this section.
(10)
Keep a written record of all money collected from property tax, fines and forfeitures, sale of city property or other sources. The record shall be kept so as to show each separate item collected under its own proper head and shall be balanced by payments to the general fund.
(11)
Be the custodian of the funds of the city, deposit all the funds at a solvent bank in the city as designated by the mayor and city council, and receive and pay out all moneys in the manner prescribed by law.
(12)
Keep a written record of each account paid by the city, item by item, giving the date of payment.
(13)
Keep his office open during the business hours of each day.
(14)
Perform such other acts as may be required of such officer in the Charter or by the mayor and city council pertaining to such office.
(Code 1982, § 2-43)
Charter reference— Duties of clerk and treasurer, §§ 19, 38.
Sec. 2-145. - Reports.
(a)
At the end of each month, the clerk and treasurer shall make a detailed report of transactions for the month, bringing forward the balance and showing the amount on hand at the close of the month. Such reports shall be referred to the finance committee, which shall examine it and report as to its correctness at the next meeting.
(b)
At the close of each year, the clerk and treasurer shall make a consolidated report of financial transactions during the year and shall submit the report to the mayor and city council for approval.
(Code 1982, § 2-44)
Sec. 2-146. - Duties upon leaving office.
At the expiration of the term of office, the clerk and treasurer shall turn over to his successor the balance of the city's money in such officer's hands for which such officer may be liable, take a receipt for such money, and make a final report to the mayor and city council at its next meeting.
(Code 1982, § 2-45)
Sec. 2-147. - Paying out money from treasury.
The clerk and treasurer shall pay out no money, except as provided in this Code and in the ordinances and Charter of the city. Payments in an unauthorized manner will not be allowed to such officer as credits.
(Code 1982, § 2-46)

DIVISION 4. - CITY ATTORNEY[5]

Sec. 2-171. - Appointment.
There may be appointed a city attorney. If a city attorney is appointed, such attorney shall be appointed by the mayor and city council at the same time the city clerk is appointed, and shall hold office at the pleasure of the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 2-50)
Charter reference— Authority to appoint attorneys, § 20.
Sec. 2-172. - Duties.
The city attorney shall act as legal advisor to the mayor and city council and shall represent the city in all legal matters before any court. At the request of the mayor and city council, the city attorney shall defend all officers or employees of the city in any suit or prosecution for any act in the discharge of their official duties. When required by the mayor and city council, the city attorney shall also act as attorney and counsel for any officer of the city in any prosecution or proceeding which may be instituted by order of the mayor and city council or by such officer in any court other than the municipal court. The city attorney shall do every professional act incidental to such office which may be required of such attorney by the mayor and city council and shall furnish, upon request, any city officer or employee with advice touching the duties of such officer or employee. The city attorney shall prosecute such offenders before the municipal court as may be requested by the mayor and city council. At the end of his term, the city attorney shall make a report to the mayor and city council of the condition and status of all city litigation. The city attorney shall attend the meetings of the mayor and city council, when required.
(Code 1982, § 2-51)
Sec. 2-173. - Compensation.
The city attorney shall receive as a retainer, for advice and other work performed for the city, such compensation as may be determined, each year, by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 2-52)

DIVISION 2. - COMMITTEE ON THE HANDICAPPED[7]

Sec. 2-221. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Appeal means a request to the mayor and city council to reconsider the decision and procedures of the committee on the handicapped.
Committee on the handicapped means a committee that consists of five members of the community, selected by the mayor and city council, knowledgeable on the problems of the handicapped, or representing a handicapped group or organization, each member of which will have demonstrated sound judgment and civic pride and will serve a two-year term.
Evidentiary hearing means a hearing before the committee on the handicapped where a grievant and management may present testimony and evidence in support of their respective positions.
Grievance form means the standard form promulgated by the city for the purpose of reporting and processing grievances.
Handicapped coordinator means the management official designated to be responsible for coordination of efforts to comply with the handicapped discrimination regulations.
(Code 1982, § 17-50)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 2-222. - Procedure for filing and handling grievances.
(a)
Within 90 days of the effective date of an alleged discriminatory act or condition, or 90 days from the date that the grievant could reasonably be expected to have knowledge of the existence of the act or condition, the grievant must request and submit a grievance form with the city clerk's office. Complaints arising from actions and conditions which are ongoing or repetitive in nature shall be considered timely.
(b)
Upon receiving the grievance form, the city clerk shall forward the grievance form to the management official designated as the handicapped coordinator.
(c)
Within ten business days, the handicapped coordinator shall determine the merit of the complaint, meet and confer with the grieving party and attempt to resolve the complaint.
(d)
If the problem remains unresolved, the city clerk shall, upon receiving the grievance form from the handicapped coordinator, notify and convene the committee on the handicapped.
(e)
Within ten business days of such notification, the committee on the handicapped shall conduct an evidentiary hearing on the subject grievance and prepare a written recommendation to resolve the problem. The grievance form and recommendation shall be returned to the city clerk for administrative review by the mayor and city council.
(f)
The city clerk shall present the recommendation of the committee on the handicapped and the grievance form to the mayor or the chairperson of the committee on the handicapped. Within ten business days, the mayor and city council shall review and approve or modify the recommendation.
(g)
The city clerk shall notify the grievant regarding the disposition of the grievance.
(h)
If the grievance remains unresolved by the committee on the handicapped's recommendation, the grievant may, in writing, direct the city clerk to prepare his grievance form for appeal by the mayor and city council at the next regularly scheduled meeting.
(i)
Upon submission of an appeal, the mayor and city council shall conduct an independent investigation. The mayor and city council may elect to delegate such finding of fact to counsel. A resolution of the problem shall be rendered at the following regularly scheduled meeting. The decision of the mayor and city council shall be final.
(Code 1982, § 17-51)

DIVISION 3. - CODE ENFORCEMENT BOARD

Sec. 2-231. - Short title; authority.
This division shall be known and may be cited as the City of Fort Valley Code Enforcement Board Ordinance. This division is authorized by O.C.G.A. § 36-74-20, et seq.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 1, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-232. - Intent.
It is the intent of this division to promote, protect and improve the health, safety and welfare of the citizens of Fort Valley by the creation of an administrative board with authority to impose administrative fines and other noncriminal penalties to provide an equitable, expeditious, effective and inexpensive method of enforcing any codes and ordinances in force in the city where a pending or repeated violation continues to exist.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 2, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-233. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Code inspector means any authorized agent or employee of the city whose duty it is to ensure code compliance.
City means the City of Fort Valley, Georgia.
City of Fort Valley codes and ordinances means City of Fort Valley zoning ordinances and resolutions, ordinances and resolutions enacting subdivision regulations, environmental ordinances and resolutions, state minimum standard codes provided for in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25, ordinances and resolutions enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25, other ordinances and resolutions regulating the development of real property, and ordinances and regulations providing for control of litter and debris, control of junked or abandoned vehicles, and control of overgrown vegetation. Notwithstanding the above, the term "City of Fort Valley codes and ordinances" shall not include:
(1)
Those codes and ordinances requiring a permit, unless the alleged violator has failed to secure all necessary valid permits under said codes and ordinances; or
(2)
Any local amendments to the state minimum standard codes provided for in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25 that have not been adopted in conformity with the requirements of subsection (c) of O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25.
Enforcement board means the City of Fort Valley Code Enforcement Board.
Mayor and council means the mayor and council of the City of Fort Valley.
Violation involving the health or safety of a third party means a violation that creates a legitimate concern for the health and safety of a third party occupant of a dwelling place or that creates an immediate and substantial danger to the environment.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 3, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-234. - Organization of the code enforcement board.
(a)
The enforcement board shall consist of seven members appointed by resolution of the city. In making appointments to the enforcement board, the mayor and council shall make good faith efforts to appoint up to two alternate members for the enforcement board to serve on the board in the absence of board members.
(b)
Members of the enforcement board shall be residents of the city. In making appointments to the enforcement board, the mayor and council shall make good faith efforts to appoint one or more individuals who have experience or expertise relevant to one or more of the municipal codes that are within the subject matter jurisdiction of the enforcement board, including individuals with property management and litter control experience; provided, however, that the authority and jurisdiction of the enforcement board shall not in any way be limited due to the absence from its membership of one or more individuals with such experience or expertise.
(c)
Appointment; terms.
(1)
The initial appointments to the enforcement board shall be as follows: Three members appointed for a term of two years each; and four members appointed for a term of four years each.
(2)
Upon the expiration of the initial terms specified above, all terms shall be for three years.
(3)
A member may be reappointed by resolution of the mayor and council.
(4)
An appointment to fill any vacancy on the enforcement board shall be for the remainder of the unexpired term of office. If any member fails to attend two of three successive meetings without cause and without prior approval of the chairperson, the enforcement board shall declare the member's office vacant, and the mayor and council shall promptly fill such vacancy.
(5)
The members shall serve in accordance with the policies and procedures of the city and may be suspended and removed for cause. The mayor and council may, with or without cause, refuse to reappoint any member of the enforcement board at the expiration of his term of office.
(d)
The members of the enforcement board shall elect a chairperson, who shall be a voting member, from among the members of the board. The presence of four or more members shall constitute a quorum of the enforcement board. Members shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for such travel, mileage, and per diem expenses as may be authorized by the mayor and council or as otherwise provided by law.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 4, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-235. - Enforcement procedures.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the code inspector to initiate enforcement proceedings pursuant to the various codes; however, no member of the enforcement board shall have the power to initiate such enforcement proceedings.
(b)
Except as provided in subsections (c) and (d) of this section, if a violation of any code or ordinance is found, the code inspector shall notify the violator and give him a reasonable time to correct the violation. Should the violation continue beyond the time specified for correction, the code inspector shall proceed with enforcement through the enforcement board. If the code inspector proceeds through the enforcement board, the code inspector shall notify the enforcement board and request a hearing. The enforcement board shall schedule a hearing, and written notice of such hearing shall be hand delivered or made as provided in section 2-240 to said violator. At the option of the enforcement board, notice may additionally be served by publication as provided in section 2-240. If the violation is corrected and then recurs or if the violation is not corrected by the time specified for correction by the code inspector, the case may be presented to the enforcement board even if the violation has been corrected prior to the hearing, and the notice shall so state.
(c)
If a repeat violation is found, the code inspector shall notify the violator but is not required to give the violator a reasonable time to correct the violation. The code inspector, upon notifying the violator of a repeat violation, shall notify the enforcement board and request a hearing. The enforcement board shall schedule a hearing and shall provide written notice pursuant to section 2-240. The case may be presented to the enforcement board even if the repeat violation has been corrected prior to the hearing, and the notice shall so state.
(d)
If the code inspector has substantial reason to believe a violation presents a serious threat to the public health, safety and welfare or if the violation is irreparable or irreversible in nature, the code inspector shall make a reasonable effort to notify the violator and may immediately notify the enforcement board and request a hearing.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 5, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-236. - Conduct of hearing.
(a)
Upon request of the code inspector, or at such other times as may be necessary, the chairperson of the enforcement board may call a hearing by the enforcement board. Minutes shall be kept of all hearings by the enforcement board, and all hearings and proceedings shall be open to the public. The mayor and council may provide or assign clerical and administrative personnel to assist the enforcement board in the proper performance of its duties.
(b)
Each case before the enforcement board shall be presented by the city attorney or by the code inspector or other member of the administrative staff.
(c)
The enforcement board shall proceed to hear the cases on the agenda for that day. All testimony shall be under oath and shall be recorded. The enforcement board shall take testimony from the code inspector and alleged violator. Formal rules of evidence shall not apply, but fundamental due process shall be observed and shall govern the proceedings.
(d)
At the conclusion of the hearing, the enforcement board shall issue findings of fact, based on evidence of record and conclusions of law, and shall issue an order affording the proper relief consistent with owners granted in this division. The findings and conclusions shall be motion-approved by a majority of those members present and voting, except that at least four members of the enforcement board must vote in order for the action to be official. The order may include a notice that it must be complied with by a specified date and a fine may be imposed if the order is not complied with by said date. A certified copy of such order may be recorded in the public records of the city and Peach County and shall constitute notice to any subsequent purchasers, successors in interest, or assigns if the violation concerns real property, and the findings therein shall be binding upon the violator and, if the violation concerns real property, subsequent purchasers, successors in interest, or assigns. If an order is recorded in the public records pursuant to this subsection and the order is complied with by the date specified in the order, the enforcement board shall issue an order acknowledging compliance that shall be recorded in the public records. A hearing is not required to issue such an order acknowledging compliance.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 6, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-237. - Powers.
The enforcement board shall have the power to:
(1)
Adopt rules for the conduct of its hearings, which rules shall, at a minimum, ensure that each side has an equal opportunity to present evidence and argument in support of its case;
(2)
Subpoena alleged violators and witnesses to its hearings, with the approval of the court with jurisdiction over a criminal violator of the municipal code or ordinance. Subpoenas may be served by an officer of the police department or by any other individual not less than 18 years of age;
(3)
Subpoena evidence to its hearings in the same way as provided in subsection (2) of this section, with the approval of the court with jurisdiction over a criminal violator of the municipal code or ordinance;
(4)
Take testimony under oath; and
(5)
Issue orders having the force of law to command whatever steps are necessary to bring a violation into compliance.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 7, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-238. - Administrative fines and liens.
(a)
The enforcement board, upon notification by the code inspector that an order of the enforcement board has not been complied with by the set time, may order the violator to pay an administrative fine in an amount specified in this section.
(b)
Fines.
(1)
An administrative fine imposed pursuant to this section for a violation involving the health or safety of a third party shall not exceed $1,000.00 per day.
(2)
An administrative fine imposed pursuant to this section for a violation that is not a violation involving the health or safety of a third party shall not exceed a total of $1,000.00.
(3)
In determining the amount of the fine, if any, the enforcement board shall consider the following factors:
a.
The gravity of the violation;
b.
Any actions taken by the violator to correct the violation; and
c.
Any previous violations committed by the violator.
(4)
The enforcement board may reduce a fine imposed pursuant to this section.
(c)
A certified copy of an order imposing an administrative fine maybe recorded in the public records of any county and thereafter shall constitute a lien against the property on which the violation exists and upon any real or personal property owned by the violator. Upon petition to the superior court, such order may be enforced in the same manner as a court judgment by the sheriffs of this state, including levy against the personal property, but such order shall not be deemed to be a court judgment except for enforcement purposes. After three months after the filing of any such lien which remains unpaid, the enforcement board may request the city attorney to foreclose on the lien.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 8, 9-18-2003)
Sec. 2-239. - Appeals.
An aggrieved party, including the city, may appeal a final administrative order of the enforcement board to the superior court. Such an appeal shall be a hearing de novo. An appeal shall be filed within 30 days of the execution of the order to be appealed.
(Ord. No. 03-0918-65, § 9, 9-18-2003)

(a)
All notices required by this division shall be provided to the alleged violator by certified mail, return receipt requested; by hand delivery by any law enforcement officer, code inspector, or other person designated by the city; or by leaving the notice at the violator's usual place of residence with any person residing therein who is above 15 years of age and informing such person of the contents of the notice.

Sec. 2-241. - Provisions are supplemental.
It is the intent of this division to provide an additional or supplemental means of obtaining compliance with local codes. Nothing contained herein shall prohibit the city, through its code inspector, from enforcing its des by any other lawful means including criminal and civil proceedings; provided, however, that the city shall not pursue a specific instance of an alleged violation of an ordinance against one violator before both the enforcement board and a magistrate, municipal or other court authorized to hear ordinance violations.

ARTICLE V. - FINANCE[8]

Sec. 2-251. - Purchases.
All purchases for the city shall be made by and through the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1982, § 9-1)
Charter reference— City administrator designated purchasing agent, § 19(e)(8).
Sec. 2-252. - Requisitions; purchase orders.
(a)
Before issuing a purchase order for any work to be performed or any supplies for the city, the purchasing agent shall require the official or employee of the city applying for the purchase order to furnish a requisition therefor, showing the supplies or work wanted and the name of the department to which it is chargeable.
(b)
The city shall not be liable for any purchase made, work done or service rendered for which a purchase order has not first been issued and signed by the purchasing agent.
(c)
When a purchase requisition is received by the purchasing agent and it is determined that sufficient funds to cover the cost of the purchase do not exist in the identified line item of the budget, no purchase order will be issued. If funds are to be transferred from one line item to another within the department's budget, such information must be reflected on the requisition form and authorized by the signature of the appropriate committee chairperson.
(d)
If the transfer of funds, as outlined in subsection (c) of this section, cannot be accomplished or the purchase will cause the requesting department to exceed its total appropriated budget, the request will be transferred to the mayor and city council through the finance committee for its approval.
(e)
Any purchase classified as an emergency purchase must be identified as such on the requisition form and bear the signatures of the appropriate department head and committee chairperson at the time the requisition is submitted for a purchase order.
(f)
Under no circumstances will the purchasing agent be authorized to issue a city purchase order for a purchase made that predates the purchase requisition form unless authorized by the finance committee.
(Code 1982, § 9-2)
Sec. 2-253. - Incurring indebtedness without purchase order.
No official or employee of the city shall have the authority to incur any indebtedness against the city without first securing a purchase order from the purchasing agent as provided in section 2-252. Any such official or employee found violating this section shall be held strictly accountable for such violation and liable to removal from office within the discretion of the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 9-3)
Sec. 2-254. - Approval of bills against city.
All bills against the city shall first be approved by the chairperson of the committee under whose jurisdiction they fall and then approved by the chairperson of the finance committee, or in case of such chairperson's absence, by a member of the finance committee, before payment thereof.
(Code 1982, § 9-4)
Sec. 2-255. - Drawing and signing of vouchers and checks.
All vouchers and checks shall be drawn by the clerk and treasurer, and shall be signed by two of the following:
(1)
The mayor.
(2)
The city administrator.
(3)
A councilmember.
(Code 1982, § 9-5; Res. of 4-21-1994)
Sec. 2-256. - Payment of moneys collected by officers and employees.
All moneys collected for or on behalf of the city by any city officer or employee shall be promptly paid over to the finance director.
(Code 1982, § 9-6)
Sec. 2-257. - Guidelines for travel expenses generally.
(a)
Requests for requisitions for travel expenses shall be presented to the finance committee for preapproval no later than ten days prior to the scheduled trip.
(b)
Registration forms and meeting agendas shall be attached to the expense requisition form, along with an explanation of how the trip constitutes or qualifies as official business.
(c)
When a requisition form is submitted at least ten days prior to the scheduled trip, the finance committee shall notify the requester, in writing, whether the request is approved, and if so, the amount of expenses approved for payment.
(d)
If the requester disagrees with all or part of a decision from the finance committee, the matter shall be brought before the entire mayor and city council for resolution.
(e)
Emergency trips can be approved by the city administrator and/or the mayor and two councilmembers.
(Res. of 8-17-1995)
Sec. 2-258. - Per diem and travel expenses for elected officials.
(a)
If any city elected official desires to attend an event out of the city, but within the state, which is related to his position and responsibilities with the city, he shall be reimbursed for the expenses of attending the meeting as provided by the mayor and city council.
(b)
The extent of reimbursement for expenses incurred for travel outside of the state shall be determined preceding the meeting, provided that the need for attendance has been preapproved by the mayor and city council.
(Res. of 8-17-1995; Res. of 3-18-1999)
