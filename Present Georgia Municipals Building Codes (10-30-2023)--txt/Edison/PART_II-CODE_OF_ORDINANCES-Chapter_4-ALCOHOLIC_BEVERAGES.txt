Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.; local regulation of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1 et seq.
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The definitions in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2 apply to this chapter.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 1)
Sec. 4-2. - Malt beverages tax.
(a)
Imposed. There is hereby imposed upon all wholesale dealers selling malt beverages within the city a specific excise tax as follows:
(1)
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons.
(2)
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(b)
Tax in addition to other charges. The excise tax provided for in subsection (a) of this section shall be in addition to any license fee, tax or charge which may now or in the future be imposed upon the business of selling malt beverages at retail or wholesale within the corporate limits of the city.
(c)
Penalty. The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to five percent of the total amount due during the first 30-day period following the date such report and remittance were due and a further penalty of five percent of the amount of such remittance for each successive 30-day period, or any portion thereof, during which such report and remittance are not filed. The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the wholesale dealer making such report liable for a penalty equal to 50 percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report.
(Code 1973, §§ 3-24, 3-27, 3-28)
State law reference— Tax on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80 et seq.
Sec. 4-3. - Wine tax.
In addition to the annual retail license fee required, there is hereby levied an excise tax on the first sale or use of wine by the package computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter sold, displayed or stored in the city. Said tax shall be paid to the city by the wholesale distributor on all wines sold to retailers in the city, as follows: Each wholesale distributor selling, shipping, or in any way delivering wine to any retailer, shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery and shall remit the same together with a summary of all deliveries to each retailer on or before the 20th day of the month following. It shall be unlawful and a violation of the ordinance from which this section is derived for any wholesale distributor to sell, ship or delivery in any manner any wine to a retailer without having collected said tax. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any retailer to possess, own, hold, store, display or sell any wine on which such tax has not been paid. Each wholesaler shall be paid three percent of the amount of taxes collected as reimbursement of collection of said tax. Every wine distributor shall deliver a copy of each delivery ticket to the city clerk after making a delivery, said delivery ticket to show the exact quantity delivered and the amount of tax collected thereon
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 15; Ord. of 2-13-1984)
State law reference— Wine tax, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60.
Sec. 4-4. - Persons eligible for licenses; financial responsibility required.
(a)
No license required by the provisions of this chapter shall be issued to:
(1)
Any applicant who is not of good moral character. Corporation applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(2)
An applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state, local law or ordinance of a criminal offense involving moral turpitude, or has been convicted under any federal, state or local law of any criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations, if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with law or ordinance.
(3)
A person whose place of business is conducted by an employee, manager or agent unless the manager, agent or employee possesses the same qualifications as the licensee. Corporation applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(4)
A person who does not possess financial responsibility. Financial statements must be submitted with each completed application as prescribed by the council. Evidence of financial responsibility shall include evidence that the person can obtain adequate liability insurance for the business and premises to be licensed.
(b)
No license shall be issued for any location or premises where the sale of any alcoholic beverages to be sold under the license is prohibited by state law or city ordinance.
(c)
No license shall be granted to any person who is not at least 21 years of age unless the business is managed by a person who is at least 21 years of age.
(d)
No employee of the city shall be issued an alcoholic beverage license.
(e)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued if such issuance is prohibited by this chapter.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, §§ 2, 3, 16, 17; Ord. of 2-13-1984; Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-6)
State law reference— Standards for issuance of alcoholic beverages required, O.C.G.A. § 3-2-2; location restrictions for sale of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 4-5. - Toilet facility only to be accessible from inside building.
All toilets or restroom facilities maintained in the premises of any business engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be entered only from the inside of the building in which any such business is conducted, and shall not be accessible from the outside.
(Code 1973, § 3-1)

ARTICLE II. - MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE PACKAGE STORES

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-31. - Penalties.
Any person violating this article shall be punished as provided in general penalty section 1-7, and such conviction shall automatically revoke the permit and license of the one so convicted.
(Code 1973, § 3-34)
Sec. 4-32. - Right of entry.
The mayor or city police shall have the right to enter upon the premises where malt beverages or wine is sold at retail in the city, at any reasonable hour when such business is open, for the purpose of checking on the amount of malt beverages or wine on hand and to make such other reasonable investigation of such retail sales of malt beverages or wine as will furnish information on the number of sales of such retail malt beverages or wine dealer.
(Code 1973, § 3-29)
Sec. 4-33. - Signs warning no liquor drinking on premises required.
Every person engaged in the sale of malt beverages or wine shall display prominently in such place of business a sign in bold letters: "DRINKING OF WHISKEY OR ANY SPIRITUOUS LIQUOR PROHIBITED ON THESE PREMISES."
(Code 1973, § 3-2)
Sec. 4-34. - Wholesaling to unlicensed retailers prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any wholesale dealer in malt beverages or wine to sell, dispense or deliver any malt beverages or wine to any person who is not duly authorized and licensed to sell malt beverages or wine at retail within the city.
(Code 1973, § 3-33)
Sec. 4-35. - Closing hours.
(a)
Enumerated. Any business selling malt beverages or wine in the city shall close said place of business not later than 12:00 midnight on each day said business is open for operation, and remain closed until 7:00 a.m. following.
(b)
Revocation of license for violation. Should any person keeping any business wherein is sold or offered for sale malt beverages or wine, sell or offer the same for sale after 12:00 midnight and before 7:00 a.m. on any day, or otherwise violate this chapter or applicable state law, the license to sell beer or wine shall be revoked by the city, and no new license for such sales shall be issued or allowed to such person for the sale of malt beverages or wine in such business location for a period of one year from the date of said revocation; and no license for such sales of malt beverages or wine shall be issued to the person holding such revoked license, or to who such license which was revoked had previously been issued for a period of one year from the date of such revocation.
(Code 1973, §§ 3-5, 3-6)
State law reference— Hours of sale of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Secs. 4-36—4-55. - Reserved.

Sec. 4-56. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell any malt beverages or wine within the limits of the city without first obtaining from the council a license to sell the same.
(Code 1973, § 3-30)
Sec. 4-57. - Application required; investigation and report; action by council.
(a)
Persons desiring to sell malt beverages or wine shall file a license application with the city clerk, who shall refer the same to the chief of police or mayor or his designee for investigation. Said officer shall investigate:
(1)
The moral fitness and reputation of the applicant;
(2)
The applicant's reputation as a peaceful citizen or law violator;
(3)
The general character and reputation of the applicant's place of business; and
(4)
Any other facts that the council should know in considering the granting of a permit and license to the applicant.
(b)
It shall be the duty of said officer to report his findings to the council at the next regular meeting or as soon thereafter as possible, at which the applicant shall personally appear, which body shall either grant or refuse the permit and license.
(Code 1973, § 3-31)
Sec. 4-58. - Conditions required for lawful establishment.
Sales of such malt beverages or wine shall be lawful within the limits of the city by licensed persons under the following conditions:
(1)
Must hold a permit and license paid for in full. The permit and license shall be issued only in the name of the operator of said business. No permit and license shall be transferable and in the event of a change in operator, a new permit and license must be obtained in the name of the new operator. No one-half year license or any portion of a year license will be issued.
(2)
No sales shall be made by any persons outside the place of business of the license.
(3)
The city council and city police shall have the full authority and privilege of making inspections at any time to see that laws are not violated.
(4)
Every person shall file with his application the names and ages of every person employed by him in said business. Changes of employees after a permit and license has been granted shall be immediately reported to the chief of police or city clerk.
(Code 1973, § 3-32)

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-75. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Church means a regular house of worship.
Conviction means an adjudication of guilt or a plea of guilty, or a plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of bond by a person charged with a crime.
Distance means the measurement in a straight line in linear feet from the nearest wall enclosing the proposed premises of the licensee to the nearest property line of any school, college or church.
Licensee means the authorization by the city council to engage in the sale of malt beverages for on-premises consumption.
Premises means the definite closed area wherein malt beverages are sold, which is owned by or under control of the person to whom the license is issued.
Retail sale means the selling or offering for sale any malt beverage or wine to any member of the public.
School or college means only such state, county, city, church or other schools as teach subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state.
(1)
The term "school or college" includes licensed preschool and/or kindergarten facilities.
(2)
The term "school or college" does not include private schools where only specialized subjects are taught.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-1)
Sec. 4-76. - Hours of sale.
It shall be unlawful to sell any malt beverage or wine for on-premises consumption between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 8:00 a.m.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-17)
State law reference— Hours of sale of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.

Sec. 4-93. - Required.
(a)
Term; prohibited acts. No person shall sell malt beverages or wine for on-premises consumption without first obtaining a city license for the calendar year in which such business in the city is conducted. No person shall sell malt beverages for on-premises consumption after such license has expired or been revoked.
(b)
Class of license. This license shall be for the sale of malt beverages or wine for on-premises consumption. No malt beverage shall be sold at retail for consumption on the premises except at a:
(1)
Restaurant, described as any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually regularly served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment and having employed there in sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its guest.
(2)
Tavern, which means a place of entertainment for the accommodation of the public where malt beverages or wine is sold at retail for on-premises consumption.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, §§ 3A-2, 3A-18)
Sec. 4-94. - Application
(a)
Required; contents. The application for license shall be in writing and addressed to the council, and shall set forth therein the following information:
(1)
The location of the place of business wherein malt beverages or wine are to be sold;
(2)
The age, name and present occupation of applicant; and
(3)
The names and ages of persons who shall be employed in the carrying on of such business.
The application shall be signed by the applicant and must be verified by oath of such applicant. Such application and oath must be on a form provided by the city clerk.
(b)
Fee. All applicants for license shall pay a fee in an amount as established from time to time by the city at the time such application is filed; and $100.00 of such fee shall be retained by the city in the event the application is withdrawn or not approved. The city shall issue a receipt to the applicant and attach a copy of the receipt to the application.
(c)
Duty to provided information. All applicants shall furnish all data, information and records pertinent to the application requested of them in writing to the council and the failure to furnish such date, information and records within 30 days from the date of such request shall automatically serve to dismiss, with prejudice, the application. The applicants, by filing an application, agree to produce for oral interrogation any person requested by the city police department or council, and considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to such application and/or license issued under the terms of this chapter. The failure to produce such person within 30 days after being requested to do so shall result in the automatic dismissal of any application pending under this chapter.
(d)
Deadline for processing. The city shall require not less than 30 days or more than 60 days from the date of completing the application for processing any application for license under the terms of this chapter.
(e)
State license; display of local certificate. Application for license shall be made to the clerk and accompanied by the amount of the license fee. Licenses shall be posted conspicuously in the licensee's place of business. Before operating under such city license, the licensee must secure the state license and comply with all provisions of O.C.G.A. title 3; and it shall be the duty of the city clerk to furnish to the licensee such certificate, copy of the city license, or other evidence as may be required by the state revenue commission in order to show payment of the city license.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, §§ 3A-10, 3A-13)
Sec. 4-95. - Notice of application.
(a)
All applicants for a license shall, within ten days after filing of such application, publish an advertisement in the official newspaper of the city wherein all legal advertisements are published to the effect that such application has been made, listing the name of the applicant, the complete address of the place of business where such application for the sale of malt beverages is proposed, and the date of the hearing thereon. This advertisement shall run for a period of two weeks preceding the next regular meeting of the council. Such advertisement shall be filed with the proper officer or agent of such paper within ten days following the date of application. The cost of such advertisement shall be paid by the applicant and a copy of the published notice shall be filed with the city clerk.
(b)
The advertisement required by subsection (a) of this section shall be and read substantially as follows:
Notice of Application for Retail License to sell malt beverages or wine. The undersigned has made application to the Council of Edison, Georgia for a retail license to sell malt beverages or wine for on premises consumption at ____________.
This application will be heard by the Council at its regular meeting to be held at ____________ o'clock p.m. on the ____________ day of ____________, ____________.
(c)
No application for a license under this division shall be acted upon or issued by the council until after advertisement and public hearing thereon, as required therein.
(d)
A public hearing upon all applications for a license under this division shall be held before the council at its regular meeting not less than 30 days or more than 60 days after the filing of the application.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-4)
Sec. 4-96. - Police investigation and report required.
All applicants shall be investigated by the police department or such other person as may be designated by the council. The report of such investigation shall be presented to the council for consideration with the application.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-5)
Sec. 4-97. - Fingerprints.
(a)
Each applicant for a license to sell malt beverages or wine in this division who has not heretofore been licensed by the city to sell alcoholic beverages, at retail, shall be required to submit to a complete fingerprinting record and test, pursuant to the rules and regulations established by the city from time to time, the same being used by the city to supplement the information contained on the application and to make available to the city any records of criminal offenses, either federal or state from other jurisdictions.
(b)
The council, within its own discretion, from time to time may require employees, male and female, working in any establishment dispensing malt beverages or wine, to submit to the same complete fingerprinting record and test and for the same purposes of supplementing any information available to the city with respect to employees.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-7)
Sec. 4-98. - Distance requirements; reissue after denial or revocation.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to issue any license under this article within 300 feet of any church.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to issue any license under this article within 300 feet of the grounds of any school or school building.
(c)
In the event the council revokes or denies any license at any location within the city, no new license shall be granted by the city for the period of six months after the date of revocation or denial, at the same location. After notice to the council that a person holding a license or his employee or agent, has been accused of prohibited acts, no new license shall be issued for such location until after the disposition of the complaint.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-8)
Sec. 4-99. - Duration; renewals.
(a)
All licenses granted pursuant to this article shall expire on December 31 of the year in which the same shall be issued. The fee shall be due and payable in advance by January 1 of each year.
(b)
Licensees who desire to renew their license shall file applications with the requisite fees heretofore provided with the city clerk on the form provided for renewal of the license for the ensuing year. Applications for renewal must be filed December 1 of each year, otherwise no renewal license shall be granted; but an application for renewal shall be required and shall be treated as an initial application, and the applicant shall be required to comply with all rules and regulations for the granting of licenses as if no previous license has been held. All licenses granted hereunder shall be for the calendar year and the full license fee must be paid for a license issued at any time during the license year.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, §§ 3A-11, 3A-21)
Sec. 4-100. - Nontransferable.
Licenses issued under authority of this article shall not be transferable from one person to another nor from one location to another except with the express written approval of the council.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-9)
Sec. 4-101. - Suspension; revocation.
(a)
Any license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any licensee may be suspended or revoked for the due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing held for the purpose of considering any such suspension or revocation. At least five days prior written notice of a time, place and purpose of such hearing, a statement of the charge or charges upon which such hearing shall be held shall be given to the holder of such license which is sought to be suspended or revoked.
(b)
Due cause for the suspension or revocation of any license shall consist of a violation of any laws or ordinances regulating such business, or violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such business, or for the violation of any state, federal, municipal ordinance that would prevent one from obtaining a license, or for violation of any provision of this chapter.
(c)
The mayor is hereby authorized to suspend any license issued under this article in any emergency situation such as civil disorders or natural disasters or in any situation that he deems some immediate suspension necessary for the protection of the health and welfare of the citizens of the city, and the suspension may be effective immediately and shall remain in force until the mayor determines the emergency is over or until the next regular meeting of the council.
(d)
When a license is suspended or revoked, the city shall not be required to refund any portion of the license fee to the holder of such suspended or revoked license. When a license is revoked, the licensee may not reapply for a license under this article for a period of three years from such revocation.
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-13)
Sec. 4-102. - Sales under suspended license prohibited.
No person whose license has been suspended or revoked pursuant to any provisions of this chapter shall sell any beverages covered by the suspended or revoked license during the period of such suspension or after revocation. No license shall be suspended or revoked without the council first giving to said licensee written notice of the reason thereof, and within five days thereafter the licensee shall have the right of a hearing before the council and may present any evidence or give any reason why the license should not be suspended or revoked, except for the suspension provided for in section 4-101(c).
(Ord. of 7-17-2001, § 3A-14)

ARTICLE IV. - PACKAGE SALES OF DISTILLED SPIRITS

State Law reference— Distilled spirits, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-1 et seq.
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-133. - Copy of article to be kept on licensed premises; licensee responsible for acts of employees.
All holders of a license for the sale of distilled spirits shall keep a copy of the ordinance from which this article is derived in his premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms of this article and each licensee or his agents selling distilled spirits shall at all times be familiar with the terms of this article. The licensee shall be held responsible for any acts of his employees in any violation of this article or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 20)
Sec. 4-134. - Authority to inspect.
The business premises of the holder of a retail distilled spirits license for the sale of distilled spirits shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 21)
Sec. 4-135. - Package sales only.
All retailers licensed under this article shall sell or offer to sell in the original unbroken package only and shall sell no package containing less than one-half pint.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 7)
State law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26.
Sec. 4-136. - Tax.
In addition to the annual retail distilled spirits license fee, there is hereby levied an excise tax computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter of distilled spirits, excluding fortified wine, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter which shall be paid to the city on all distilled spirits sold, displayed or stored in said city. Said tax shall be due and payable to the city by the wholesale distributor on all distilled spirits sold to retailers in the city at the time of delivery and remitted the together with a summary of all deliveries to each retailer on or before the 20th day of the month following. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any wholesale distributor to sell, ship or delivery in any manner any distilled spirits to a retailer without having collected said tax. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any retailer to possess, own, hold, store, display or sell any distilled spirits on which such tax has not been paid. Each wholesaler shall be paid three percent of the amount of taxes collected as reimbursement of collection of said tax. Every distilled spirits distributor shall deliver a copy of each delivery ticket to the city clerk after making a delivery, said delivery ticket to show the exact quantity delivered and the amount of tax collected thereon
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 15; Ord. of 2-13-1984)
State law reference— Local tax authorized, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80.
Sec. 4-137. - Sales and deliveries to take place on business premises.
No retailer shall sell or deliver any distilled spirits to any person except in said retailer's place of business.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 9)
Sec. 4-138. - Sale of other products.
(a)
No retailer shall sell or offer for sale or display or keep in stock, at his place of business where distilled spirits are offered for sale, any other product or commodity, except the following:
(1)
Wines, when properly licensed;
(2)
Beverages containing no alcohol commonly used to dilute distilled spirits;
(3)
Malt beverages or beer, when properly licensed;
(4)
Tobacco products; and
(5)
Packaged ice.
(b)
No beverages or packages of ice may be opened or consumed in said place of business.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 8)
Sec. 4-139. - Days and hours of sale.
No retailer shall be in his place of business or open his place of business, or furnish, sell or offer for sale any distilled spirits or other alcoholic beverages, with the exemptions set forth in this article, at any of the following times:
(1)
At any time Thanksgiving Day or Christmas Day;
(2)
At any time in violation of a local ordinance or regulation or of a special order of the council; or
(3)
On any day before 8:00 a.m. or after 11:45 p.m.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 10; Ord. of 8-11-1986)
State law reference— Hours of sale of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-140. - Storage.
No licensed retailer shall keep any distilled spirits stored in any bonded or other type warehouse in the city, nor shall he enter into any type of arrangement whereby distilled spirits ordered by him is stored for him by any licensed wholesaler. A retailer shall keep no inventory or stock of distilled spirits at any place except his licensed place of business, and within his licensed place of business his storage space for distilled spirits shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which he is licensed to do business.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 13)
Sec. 4-141. - Gambling prohibited; exceptions.
Except as authorized by law, there shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punchboards, vending machines, slot machines, pin ball machines, pool tables, lotteries or tickets or chances therein or the operation of any schemes for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any licensed retailer's place of business or in any room adjoining the same, owned, leased or controlled by licensee and any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of license.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 24)

All applications for a retail distilled spirits license shall be presented in person.
(g)
The making of any statement on an application for a license to sell liquor distilled spirits which shall be later found to be false shall constitute grounds for revocation of said license.

Sec. 4-168. - Maximum number of licenses issued to one person.
Only two retail distilled spirits licenses shall be issued to any one person in the city, and no person who holds a retail distilled spirits license in any other county or municipality of the state, shall be eligible to receive a retail distilled spirits license. As used in this section, the term "person" includes all members of such licensee's family and the term "family" includes any person related to the holder of such license within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity as computed according to the civil law.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 3)
Sec. 4-169. - Restrictions—Location.
Each application for a retail distilled spirits license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church or school or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 4)
Sec. 4-170. - Same—Building.
No retail distilled spirits license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of said building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless the a building permit and plans and specifications of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the city, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the building inspector. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premise on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the front of the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building. Each applicant for a retail distilled spirits license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 6)
Sec. 4-171. - Fee.
The annual fee for a retail distilled spirits license shall be paid prior to the issuance of any license, said fee to accompany the application and shall be either in cash or a bank certified check. Such fee shall be in an amount as established from time to time by the city.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 15; Ord. of 2-13-1984)
Sec. 4-172. - Nontransferable and nonassignable.
No retail distilled spirits license shall be transferable or assignable to any person or other location, and in the event that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender his license to the city.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 19)
Sec. 4-173. - Display.
Licenses under this article shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which same was issued.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 25)
Sec. 4-174. - Revocation or suspension.
In addition to any penalty contained in section 1-7 or otherwise:
(1)
A violation of this article by a licensee is grounds for the revocation of a license issued under this article.
(2)
A violation of state or federal laws and regulations of the state revenue commissioner relating to the sale and distribution of distilled spirits by a licensee shall subject the licensee to the immediate suspension or revocation of his license.
(Ord. of 2-14-1977, § 26)
