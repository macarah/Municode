ARTICLE I. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
In this chapter, the following words shall have the indicated meanings:
Alcoholic beverages means and includes, but is not limited to, malt beverages or beer, vinous liquor or wine and distilled spirits as defined herein.
Building code means and includes all building, plumbing and electrical codes and any other similar technical code of the city.
Church means any permanent, standalone building that was originally constructed or built for the regular assembly of religious worship upon real property and land that was, at the time of such building or construction, zoned or classified for the use as a place of religious worship.
College means only such state, county, city, church or other colleges that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common colleges of this state and shall not include private colleges where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
Courtyard means an area open or partially open to the sky and enclosed or surrounded on all sides by buildings, walls, fences or other such structures, or a combination thereof.
Distance means the measurement in lineal feet as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21. For purposes of this entire chapter, alcoholic beverages, distances shall be measured by the most direct route from property line to property line.
Distilled spirits means all beverages containing alcohol, obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including fortified wines.
Hotel means a building or other structure kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public to be a place where sleeping accommodations are offered for adequate pay to travelers and guests whether transient, permanent or residential, in which 25 or more rooms are used for the sleeping accommodations of guests. Motels meeting the qualifications set out herein for hotels shall be classified in the same category as hotels. Hotels shall have the privilege of granting franchises for the operation of a lounge, restaurant or nightclub in their premises and the holder of a franchise shall be included in the definition of hotel thereunder.
Lounge means a separate room connected with a part of and adjacent to a restaurant or located in hotels as defined herein with all booths, stools and tables being unobstructed and open to view. All lounges shall be air conditioned and have a seating capacity of at least 20 persons.
Malt beverages means fermented beverages such as beer made in whole or in part from malt, or any similar fermented beverage, and containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume.
Moral character of licensee shall mean the combination of the following: (i) any licensee who has possession of all other licenses or permits required by any other applicable federal or state governmental or other regulatory agency for the purchase, sale or distribution of alcohol or alcoholic beverages; and (ii) who has not been convicted of a felony or aggravated misdemeanor in the last five years (computed from the date of the licensee's application); and (iii) who has not had a business license, alcohol license or occupation tax certificate revoked or suspended for the alleged violation of penal or criminal laws governing the purchase, sale or distribution of alcohol or alcoholic beverages; and (iv) who has not been convicted upon a citation or charge for the alleged violation of penal or criminal laws governing the purchase, sale or distribution of alcohol or alcoholic beverages in the last five years (computed from the date of the licensee's application). For purposes of this chapter, alcoholic beverages, a plea of guilty, no contest or nolo contendere shall constitute a conviction. This definition shall apply to any similar references to the good character and reputation of the applicant or licensee as contained in this chapter.
Nightclub means an establishment having inside service capacity of at least 25 persons.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned establishment, whether room, shop or building wherein alcoholic beverages are sold or consumed and includes a courtyard or patio as herein defined.
Private club means a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the state actively in operation within the corporate limits of the city, and having regularly paying monthly, quarterly or semiannual dues paying members.
Retail means retail sales packaged to go and not for consumption on the premises.
School means only such state, county, city, church or other schools that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state and shall not include private schools where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
Vinous liquors means any and all wines, fortified and unfortified, produced either within or outside the state and containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume.
No retail license for distilled spirits shall be issued so that the number of licenses exceed one license for every 3,000 residents within the City of Jesup as shown by the most recent official U.S. decennial census.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006; Ord. of 8-6-2019(3), Pt. 1)
Sec. 6-2. - License a privilege.
(a)
All licenses issued under this chapter shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on the business during the term of the license subject to all terms and conditions imposed by the city and state law.
(b)
All licenses issued hereunder shall have printed on the front these words:
"THIS LICENSE IS A MERE PRIVILEGE SUBJECT TO REVOCATION AND ANNULMENT, AND IS SUBJECT TO ANY AND ALL ORDINANCES NOW IN EFFECT OR WHICH MAY BE ENACTED."
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)

No malt beverage, vinous liquors, distilled spirits or any other alcoholic beverage shall be sold or manufactured in the city except under a license issued under this chapter by the city manager or his designee.

(c)
Licenses which may be issued under this chapter:
(1)
Manufacturing alcoholic beverages;
(2)
Wholesale:
a.
Malt beverages and vinous liquors;
b.
Distilled spirits;
(3)
Retail:
a.
Malt beverages and vinous liquors;
b.
Distilled spirits;
(4)
Consumption on the premises:
a.
Malt beverages and vinous liquors;
b.
Distilled spirits;
(5)
Any combination under subsection (2);
(6)
Any combination under subsection (3); or
(7)
Any combination under subsection (4).

All licenses issued under this chapter must, within 90 days after the issuance of a city license, open for business. (a)
No application for a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be acted upon until after the applicant has published in the newspaper which publishes the legal advertisements of the city a notice of his intention to secure a license for sale of alcoholic beverages. The type of license for which application has been filed;
(2)
The exact location of the place of business for which a license is sought;
(3)
The names and addresses of each owner of the business; and
(4)
If the applicant is a corporation, the names and titles of all corporate officers.
(b)
Proof of publication of the notice required by this section shall be attached to an application for a license for sale of alcoholic beverages.
(c)
An applicant for a renewal license shall not be subject to the notice requirements of this code section.

(13)
Each application shall be verified and acknowledged under oath to be true and correct:

If the applicant is an individual, the individual;
b.
If by a partnership, by the manager or general partnership;
c.
If a corporation, by the president of the corporation or other registered officer;

If any other organization of association, by the chief administrative official.

A separate processing fee of $50.00 to cover the expense of an investigation and processing the application, which fee shall not be refundable.

Proof that the location of the proposed premises complies with all zoning and planning requirements.

(f)
All applications shall be sworn to by the applicant before a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths.
(g)
The city shall require no more than 60 days for processing any application for a license under the terms of this chapter, alcoholic beverages, provided that the 60 days may be extended by agreement of the applicant and the city.
(h)
In all instances in which an application is denied under the provisions of this chapter the applicant may not reapply for a license for at least one year from the final date of such denial.
(i)
The city manager or his designee shall provide written notice to any applicant whose application is denied under the provisions of this chapter. Such written notification shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for such denial and shall advise the applicant of the right to appeal under the provisions of this chapter.
(j)
In the event the applicant is denied a license by the state, any business tax paid shall be refunded, but all other fees shall be retained by the city.

Sec. 6-7. - Separate application and separate license for each location of sale.
A separate application must be made for each location, and a separate license must be issued.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-8. - Preliminary approval required.
All applications shall be approved or disapproved by the chief of police as a condition to action thereon and issuance of a license by the city manager or his designee. Before the chief of police approves or disapproves an application, he shall make or cause to be made a complete and exhaustive investigation of the police record of the applicant and the character and reputation of the applicant. If the police chief determines that the applicant has no record that is in violation of this chapter and is of good character and reputation, he shall approve the application.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-9. - Denial of license application; granting.
(a)
The city manager, or his designee, may deny a city license under this chapter on any of the following grounds:
(1)
Failure to meet state requirements for state license;
(2)
Failure to pay required fees and taxes;
(3)
Failure to provide required valid information, documents and the like;
(4)
False documentation in the application or attached documents;
(5)
Failure to pass review by the chief of police;
(6)
Improper residency of applicant, owner or registered agents;
(7)
Prior convictions as herein provided; or
(8)
Failure to meet any other requirements in this chapter for a license of the class applied for
(b)
Otherwise, the city manager or his designee may issue any city license provided for in this chapter.
(c)
In the event the city manager or his designee denies a license application, such denial shall be in written form, addressed to the applicant at the application address, and shall state the grounds upon which the denial is based. Within ten days of the date of issuance of such notice, the applicant may appeal the denial by submitting a written notice of appeal to the city council. The council shall schedule a hearing on the appeal within 30 days of receipt of the notice of appeal and shall provide written notice thereof to the appellant. At the hearing, the appellant and the city manager or his designee may each present evidence relating to the grounds of denial. The appellant may be represented by counsel at such hearing or may appear in his own behalf.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006; Ord. of 8-6-2019(3), Pt. 4)
Sec. 6-10. - Residence requirements.
No license shall be granted under this chapter to any applicant who has not been a resident of the state for one year prior to the filing of an application for the license. Where the applicant is a corporation, partnership or association, the license shall be issued jointly to the corporation, partnership or association and an officer or agent of the corporation, partnership or association who meets the above requirement.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-11. - Moral character of licensee.
No person, firm, partnership or corporation shall be granted a license under this chapter unless it appears to the satisfaction of the city council that such person, firm, partnership or corporation, and the officials and directors thereof, shall be of good moral character. In no event shall a license be granted to any person, officials or directors of any corporation who have been convicted, pled guilty to or have entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving moral turpitude, lottery or other offense involving narcotics or liquor within a period of ten years prior to the filing of the application.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-12. - Completion of proposed licensed premises.
If the building in which an applicant for a license proposes to operate under the provisions of this article is not, at the time of application for the license, in existence, or not yet completed, a license may be issued for the location provided the plans and specifications for the proposed building are filed with the city clerk and show clearly a compliance with the provisions of this article and other applicable provisions of this Code. No sales of alcoholic beverages shall be allowed in the establishment until it has been completed in accordance with the plans and specifications and in conformity with all other provisions of this Code.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-13. - State regulations.
All licenses issued under this chapter shall be subject to the rules and regulations set forth in Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated and those prescribed by the state department of revenue. Violations thereof shall be additional grounds for revocation or suspension of any license hereunder.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-14. - Minors; prohibited practices.
It is unlawful for any person to allow or require a person in his employment under 18 years of age to dispense, serve, sell or take orders for any alcoholic beverage.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-15. - Duration and renewal of license.
(a)
Any license issued in this chapter shall automatically expire on December 31 of the year of its issuance. A license may be automatically renewed upon the payment of the annual license fee in full plus the approval by the city manager or his designee.
(b)
All fees for renewal of licenses shall be due and payable prior to December 1 of the year of expiration.
(c)
Any person holding an alcoholic beverage license who is found guilty of selling alcoholic beverages to a minor by the state revenue commissioner may have his license revoked for thirty days for the first offence and one year for the second and all further offenses.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006; Ord. of 8-6-2019(3), Pt. 5)
Sec. 6-16. - Transfer of licenses prohibited; change of location.
No license may be transferred to another entity or moved to another location without first obtaining the approval of the city council.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-17. - Sale of alcohol on election days permitted.
Pursuant to the delegation of authority granted to the city by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20(b)(2)(b), the sale by licensees of alcoholic beverages, distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; provided, however, nothing herein shall authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place during such time as the polls are open.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
State Law reference— Election day sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 6-18. - Hearings; appeal.
All hearings and appeals shall be heard by the city council.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-19. - Penalties.
In addition to possible suspension or revocation of a license, any person violating any provisions shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided by city ordinance in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)

ARTICLE II. - LICENSING AND OPERATION OF RETAIL AND WHOLESALE DISTILLED SPIRITS ESTABLISHMENTS AND THE MANUFACTURING OF DISTILLED SPIRITS

Sec. 6-31. - Scope of article.
In addition to the general business license requirements of this Code and Article I of this chapter, the following sections shall apply to the licensing and operation of retail and wholesale distilled spirits establishments and the manufacturing of distilled spirits in the city; and should any section of this article conflict with other provisions in this chapter or the general business license requirements of this code, then this article shall control.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-32. - Restrictions on other mercantile establishments.
A licensee of a retail establishment under this article shall not operate the business in connection with any other mercantile establishment. No retailer hereunder shall sell, offer for sale, display or keep in stock at his place of business where packaged distilled spirits are offered for sale any other commodity except the following, all of which may be sold by the retailer at his option:
(1)
Beverages containing no alcohol and commonly used to dilute distilled spirits, but no alcoholic beverages of any kind may be opened or consumed in the place of business;
(2)
Wines, malt beverages or beer, when properly licensed;
(3)
Tobacco products; and
(4)
Packaged ice.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-33. - Distance requirements.
(a)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell any distilled spirits for off-premises consumption:
(1)
Within 200 yards of any school building, school grounds or college campus; or
(2)
In or within 100 yards of any church building, alcoholic treatment center or licensed child care center.
(b)
For purposes of this article, distances shall be measured by the most direct route from property line to property line.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Secs. 6-34—6-39. - Reserved.

ARTICLE V. - LICENSING AND OPERATIONS FOR SALES OFF-PREMISES FOR CATERED FUNCTIONS AND SALES FOR TEMPORARY FUNCTIONS HELD BY NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS

Sec. 6-60. - Scope of article.
In addition to the general business license requirements of this Code and Article I of this chapter, the following sections shall apply to the licensing and operations for sales off-premises for catered functions and sales for temporary functions held by nonprofit organizations. Should any section of this article conflict with other provisions of the chapter or the general business license requirements of this Code, then this article shall control.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-61. - Eligibility for off-premises license.
(a)
Any applicant who holds a license issued by the city under the terms and conditions set forth in this chapter to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink for consumption on the premises may be issued an off-premises license which authorizes such applicant to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink off-premises and in connection with an authorized catered function. For purposes of this section an "authorized catered function" may include a function held inside a publicly or privately owned building or structure or a function held outside such a building or structure.
(b)
Any applicant who holds a license issued by the city under the terms and conditions set forth in this chapter which authorizes the applicant to sell malt beverages or wine by the package for consumption off the premises may be issued an off-premises license which authorizes such applicant to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink and in connection with an authorized catered function. For purposes of this section an "authorized catered function" may include a function held inside a publicly or privately owned building or structure or a function held outside such a building or structure.
(c)
Any applicant who holds a license issued by the city under the terms and conditions set forth in this chapter which authorizes the applicant to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises may be issued an off-premises license which authorizes such applicant to sell distilled spirits by the drink off-premises and in connection with an authorized catered function. For purposes of this section an "authorized catered function" may include a function held inside a publicly or privately owned building or structure or a function held outside such a building or structure.
(d)
Any applicant who holds a license issued by the city under the terms and conditions set forth in this chapter which authorizes the applicant to sell distilled spirits by the package for consumption off the premises may be issued an off-premise license which authorizes the applicant to sell distilled spirits by the drink off-premises and in connection with an authorized catered function. For purposes of this section an "authorized catered function" may include a function held inside a publicly or privately owned building or structure or a function held outside such a building or structure.
(e)
Any applicant validly existing and operating pursuant to the laws of the State of Georgia as a nonprofit organization may be issued a temporary license to sell distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine by the drink in connection with an authorized temporary nonprofit function. For purposes of this section an "authorized temporary nonprofit function" may include a function held inside a publicly or privately owned building or structure or a function held outside such a building or structure for the benefit of a nonprofit organization.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
Sec. 6-62. - Event permits.
In order to distribute or sell distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine at an authorized catered function or a temporary nonprofit function held by a nonprofit organization, an applicant shall be required to:
(1)
Apply to the city for an event permit. The application shall include the name of the applicant; the date, address and time of the event; and the applicant's alcoholic beverage license number if the applicant is applying for an authorized catered function.
(2)
If applicable, provide satisfactory reports as required to the State of Georgia on forms provided by the State of Georgia, stating the quantity of any and all alcoholic beverages transported from the applicant's primary premises to the location of the authorized catered function and such other information as may be required by the state.
(3)
If applicable, maintain original event permits and documents in the vehicle transporting the alcoholic beverage to the catered function at all times.
(4)
For nonprofit organizations, providing documents evidencing the organization's proof of existence and good standing as a nonprofit organization with approval by the organization of the applicant's authority to act on the organization's behalf.
(Ord. of 3-21-2006)
ARTICLE VI. - SUNDAY PACKAGE SALES

Sec. 6-63. - Days when sales allowed.
(a)

Sec. 6-64. - Hours of operation.
Package licensees shall not engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages except between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight Monday through Saturday and between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sunday. Package licensees shall not permit their places of business to be open except between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight, Monday through Saturday, or between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sundays, except that where the primary business of a malt beverage package licensee or wine package licensee is other than the sale of alcoholic beverages, such restrictive hours shall apply only with respect to the sale of malt beverages or wine.
(Ord. of 12-18-2018(1), § 1)
ARTICLE VII. - SUNDAY BEVERAGE SALES

Sec. 6-65. - Conditions for Sunday beverage sales.
(a)
The following definitions shall apply in the interpretation and enforcement of this article:
(1)
Hotel means a building or other structure kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public to be a place where sleeping accommodations are offered for adequate pay to travelers and guests, whether transient, permanent or residential, in which 50 or more rooms are used for the sleeping accommodations of such guests, such sleeping accommodations being conducted in the same building or in separate buildings or structures used in connection therewith that are on the same premises and are a part of the hotel operation. Motels meeting the qualifications set out in this definition for hotels shall be classified in the same category as hotels.
(2)
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, and advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment, having employed a sufficient number of cooks and kinds of employees to prepare, cook, and serve suitable food at tables with seating, and holding a certificate of inspection and approval from the county health department. At least one meal per day shall be served at least five days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations, and periods of remodeling, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the serving of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto. A restaurant shall have 50 percent or more of its total annual gross sales of food and alcoholic beverages from the sale of prepared meals or food.
(b)
No licensee shall permit the sale of alcoholic beverages on any day or during any time of day when such sales are prohibited by state law. Any licensee desiring to sell alcoholic beverages on Sunday as contemplated in this article shall obtain a "Sunday-Sales License" or "Sunday-Sales Permit" from the City of Jesup, Georgia as required by this article.
(c)
The licensee of any restaurant, as defined in this article, desiring the privilege to sell, furnish or otherwise dispense distilled spirits, malt beverages, or wine on Sunday in accordance with this article shall, before dispensing such beverages on Sunday, file an affidavit with the City of Jesup, Georgia, certifying under oath that: (i) such establishment holds or has applied for a current occupation tax certificate for a restaurant/eating establishment; and (ii) such restaurant, if an existing business, derived at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales income from the sale of prepared meals or food during the preceding calendar year and qualifies as a restaurant as defined in this article; or if a new business, fully intends and expects to derive at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales income from the sale of prepared meals or food during the remainder of the current calendar year and to qualify as a restaurant as defined in this article. Such an affidavit may be submitted at any time the above stated conditions are met, and must be submitted annually with such establishment's alcoholic beverage license renewal application if Sunday sales authorization is to be continued.
(d)
The licensee of any hotel, as defined in this article, desiring the privilege to sell, furnish or otherwise dispense distilled spirits, malt beverages, or wine on Sunday in accordance with this article shall, before dispensing such beverages on Sunday, file an affidavit with the City of Jesup, Georgia, certifying under oath that: (i) such establishment holds or has applied for a current occupation tax certificate for a hotel, motel or other establishment for rental of rooms for overnight lodging; and (ii) such hotel, motel, or other establishment for rental of rooms for overnight lodging, if an existing business, derived at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging during the preceding calendar year and qualifies for a Sunday sales permit; or if a new business, fully intends and expects to derive at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging during the remainder of the current calendar year and to qualify for Sunday sales under this second. Such an affidavit may be submitted at any time the above-stated conditions are met, and must be submitted annually with such establishment's alcoholic beverage license renewal application if Sunday sales authorization is to be continued.
(e)
No new application for Sunday sales permit for the same location will be allowed unless filed by an applicant with 100 percent change in ownership and control. For purposes of this subparagraph, 100 percent change in ownership and control shall mean a 100-percent change in all individuals, partners, officers, directors, shareholders, members, managers and/or all persons having any whole, partial, beneficial or other interest in the business where the alcoholic beverage license is located. One hundred percent change in ownership and control shall not include a transfer in ownership and control to any person in the immediate family of any individuals, partners, officers, directors, shareholders, members, managers and/or any persons having any interest in the business where the alcoholic beverage license is located. The application for the alcoholic beverage license must be applied for and approved prior to the sale of the business. An alcoholic beverage license may not be issued until the new owner has established that the sale of the business is bona fide and concluded. The applicant for the new license must provide documentation concerning the sale, including, but not limited to, documentation concerning ownership of and all business interests in the old license in addition to documentation concerning the ownership of and all business interests in the new license, as requested by the City of Jesup, Georgia.
(f)
No licensee shall be permitted to sell distilled spirits or alcoholic beverages on Sundays for on premises consumption, by the drink, and for beverage purposes except between the hours of 11:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight.
(g)
Failure to comply with the provisions of this article may result in revocation of the licensee's Sunday sales permit.
(Ord. of 12-18-2018(2), § 1)
