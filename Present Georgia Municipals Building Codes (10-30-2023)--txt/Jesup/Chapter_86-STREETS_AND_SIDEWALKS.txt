ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alley means a minor way to be used primarily for vehicular access to the back or side of properties otherwise abutting on a street. No alley, whether called an alley, lane or other designation, shall measure less or more than 20 feet in width.
Street means a way for vehicular traffic, whether designated as a street, highway, thoroughfare, parkway, road, avenue, boulevard, lane, place or however otherwise designated.
(1)
Arterial street means a fast or heavy traffic street of considerable continuity, and used primarily as a traffic street artery for intercommunication among other areas, and shall not measure less than 80 feet in width.
(2)
Collector street means a street which carries traffic from minor streets to arterial streets, including the principal entrance streets of a residential development and streets for circulation within such a development, and shall not measure less than 60 feet in width.
(3)
Marginal access street means a minor street which is parallel and adjacent to arterial streets, and which provides access to abutting properties and protection from through traffic, and shall not measure less than 50 feet in width.
(4)
Minor street means a street used primarily for access to the abutting properties, and shall not measure less than 50 feet in width.
(Code 1976, § 31-1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 86-2. - Approval of dedication.
No dedication to the city by any owner of property to be used as a street or alley shall be valid until such property shall have been accepted in writing by the city engineer or other duly designated official. It shall be the duty of the city engineer to examine all applications for dedications of streets and alleys, and determine if they meet the requirements of this section and the other ordinances and rules adopted by the board of commissioners. The city engineer shall determine whether a right-of-way shall be designated as an alley, arterial, collector, minor or marginal access street.
(Code 1976, § 31-2)
Sec. 86-3. - Dumping on city property.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place, or cause to be placed, in any street or alley, any dirt, trash or any other material so as to interfere with the drainage or use of such street or alley, without first obtaining a permit to do so from the city engineer.
(Code 1976, § 31-3)
Sec. 86-4. - Removing soil from street.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove soil from any street, or make any excavation in any street or alley without first obtaining a permit to do so from the city engineer.
(Code 1976, § 31-4)
Sec. 86-5. - Restrictions on wheels; exceptions.
(a)
No vehicle load, any portion of which drags or slides on the surface of the roadway, shall be used or transported on the streets.
(b)
No vehicle shall be used or transported on the streets either from construction or otherwise, the wheels of which cause pounding on the road surface.
(c)
No vehicle equipped with solid tires shall be used or transported on the streets, unless every tire on such vehicle shall have rubber on its entire traction surface of at least one inch thickness above the edge of the flange of the entire periphery.
(d)
No vehicle shall be used or transported on the streets, the wheels of which have any wooden or metal cleats, spikes, corrugations or other irregularities on the surface which tend to damage the surface of the street.
(e)
Nothing in this section shall be construed to apply to unimproved streets.
(Code 1976, § 31-5)
Sec. 86-6. - Interfering with cleaning crews.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle of any nature on the paved streets of the city between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 5:00 a.m. on each Sunday morning, or to park any automobile on any street at any time in such a manner as to obstruct the cleaning crews. Any automobile found parked in violation of this provision shall be impounded by the police department and shall be moved or towed away. Such automobile shall be returned to the owner upon payment of any and all towing and storing charges incurred by the city, together with any penalty or fine that may be imposed for such violation.
(Code 1976, § 31-6)
Sec. 86-7. - Street specifications.
The specifications and standards for the construction and repair of the public streets of the city shall be as adopted by the board of commissioners from time to time.
(Code 1976, § 31-7)
Sec. 86-8. - E 911 director to approve new street names.
The names of all new streets, roads and rights-of-way shall be approved by the E 911 director before such names are voted upon and approved by the board of commissioners, provided, the decision of the E 911 director shall be based solely on whether the name of a new street, road or right-of-way is the same or so similar to an existing name as to cause confusion to E 911 personnel. The E 911 director shall provide the board of commissioners in writing with his specific reasons for the disapproval of the name of any new street, road or right-of-way.
(Code 1976, § 31-8)
Sec. 86-9. - Numbering of buildings.
(a)
Required. All houses, buildings, structures and lots within the city shall be numbered in accordance with a plan designated by the city.
(b)
Records. The fire chief shall keep records showing the proper street number of every lot within the city, which record shall be open for inspection by any interested person.
(c)
Legibility. It shall be the duty of the owners and occupants of every house, building and structure within the city to have placed thereon, in a place visible from the street, figures at least 3½ inches high, and colored differently from the material on which such figures are mounted, showing the number of the house, building, structure or lot.
(d)
Violation; penalty. Any person failing to number any house, building, structure or lot owned or occupied by him, after receiving notice to do so from the clerk, shall be subject to a fine of $5.00 for each day during or on which a failure to number such house, building, structure or lot continues.
(Code 1976, § 6-1)
Sec. 86-10. - Permanent structures.
No permanent structure shall be constructed within 75 feet of either side of the present right-of-way of that section Sunset Boulevard extending from US 301 South to GA 84 West, with the exception of asphalt or concrete paving.
(Ord. of 7-17-2007)

ARTICLE II. - ENCROACHMENTS, OBSTRUCTIONS

Sec. 86-41. - Prohibited; nuisance declared.
It shall be unlawful for any person to encroach by fence, or in any other way, upon any part of a street, lane, alley or other thoroughfare, and it shall be unlawful to maintain any building, steps or any other obstructions in any street, lane, alley or other thoroughfare, and every such encroachment is hereby declared to be a public nuisance.
(Code 1976, § 31-19)
Sec. 86-42. - Removal.
If, at any time, it shall appear that any person is encroaching upon or maintaining any obstruction in any part of a street, lane, alley or other thoroughfare, it shall be the duty of the city marshal or designee to give notice to such party, ordering the removal of such encroachment or obstruction; and if such encroachment or obstruction is not removed within 24 hours after receipt of such notice, or such other reasonable time as may be designated by the board of commissioners, it shall be the duty of the city marshal or designee to cause any such encroachment or obstruction to be summarily removed, and the cost and expense thereof shall be charged to the person maintaining the encroachment or obstruction.
(Code 1976, § 31-20)
Sec. 86-43. - Building materials in streets.
No person shall put any lumber, wood, boxes, bricks or other such obstructions in any street, alley or sidewalk without the permission of the building inspector.
(Code 1976, § 31-21)
Sec. 86-44. - Warning signals required at obstructions.
Any person who shall cause any obstruction to be placed in any street, alley or sidewalk of the city shall display a sufficient danger signal or light at such obstruction to warn the traveling public of such obstruction and to enable them to avoid injury from such obstruction.
(Code 1976, § 31-22)
Sec. 86-45. - Right of city to remove or trim trees, shrubs or growths.
The city shall have the right to cut down, remove or trim any tree, shrub or growth, or portion thereof, standing on the streets or sidewalks of the city or upon the private property of any person within the city, whenever, by order of the mayor and board of commissioners, it shall be necessary to protect any person or the property of any individual in the city from injury or damage. It shall be the duty of the city to notify such owner, or the agent of the owner or the tenant in possession of such property, to remove such tree, shrub or growth within five days; and if, at the expiration of such time, such tree, shrub or growth is not removed, the person so offending shall be deemed to be in violation of this section. If at the expiration of the time expressed in such notice the tree, shrub or growth has not been removed, the city shall have the right to remove the tree, shrub or growth, and to issue execution against the owner of such property for the amount of expenses incurred by reason of such removal, and enforce the execution in the same manner as other executions of the city are enforced.
(Code 1976, § 31-23)
Sec. 86-46. - Traffic obstruction.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct the streets or sidewalks of the city, except as permitted by the ordinances of the city.
(b)
All persons responsible for altering or having altered the conditions of city sidewalks, roads, and streets through such actions as utility cuts, excavations, access modifications, and other such actions which disrupt the smooth and uninterrupted flow of traffic shall obtain a permit from the city and provide immediate relief in the form of temporary, all weather improvements, approved by the city engineer, that shall restore the smooth and uninterrupted flow of traffic within 24 hours of having begun such disruptions. Permanent restoration of the sidewalk, street, or roadway shall be completed within seven days of the date of having begun such disruption.
(c)
Any building or construction permit associated with activities that require disturbance of city sidewalks, roads, and streets shall designate such activities within the permit. Building permits for activities not in compliance with these provisions of issuance and restoration shall be stayed until the permit and worksite are found to be in compliance with this section. No work may continue at a worksite where the building permit has been stayed except such work permitted by the city engineer or building inspector required to correct those conditions for which the permit was stayed.
(d)
The city manager may grant written relief from strict compliance with these requirements provided such relief be supported by a finding of the city engineer or building inspector stating that strict compliance is unreasonable or impractical. Such finding must also contain an alternate plan and schedule for restoration of smooth and uninterrupted traffic.
(e)
Each 24-hour period that sidewalk and street traffic remains interrupted as provided in this section shall constitute a separate offense.
(f)
The penalty for the violation of this section shall be as provided in section 1-11 of this Code.
(Ord. of 12-2-2008)

ARTICLE III. - ABANDONMENT OF STREETS, ALLEYS

Sec. 86-81. - Petition required.
Any person desiring that any street or alley, or portion thereof, be abandoned by the city, shall file a written petition to the board of commissioners requesting such abandonment and containing the following:
(1)
A legal description of the street or alley, or portion thereof, sought to be abandoned.
(2)
Name and address of the petitioner.
(3)
Names and addresses of the owners of all property abutting the property sought to be abandoned.
(4)
Written consent of all of the owners of the property abutting the property sought to be abandoned.
(Code 1976, § 31-35)
Sec. 86-82. - Application fee.
Any person filing a petition for the abandonment of any street or alley, or portion thereof, shall pay to the city, at the time such petition is filed, an application fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1976, § 31-36)
Sec. 86-83. - Notice of petition.
Upon receipt of a petition filed under this article, it shall be the duty of the city clerk to cause an advertisement to be published in the official newspaper of the city once a week for three weeks immediately preceding the meeting at which such application shall be considered by the board of commissioners, and such advertisement shall be in substantially the following form:
Notice is hereby given that a petition has been filed with the board of commissioners for the City of Jesup requesting the abandonment of the following street or alley, or portion thereof, described as follows:
(description)
A public hearing will be held on such application at the regular meeting of the board of commissioners for the City of Jesup, to be held on the _____ day of ________, 20___. All persons are cited to be and appear at such hearing to show cause why such application should not be granted.
This _____ day of ________, 20___.
EXPAND
_____
Clerk, City of Jesup
  (Code 1976, § 31-37)
Sec. 86-84. - Inspection; report.
The city clerk shall refer a copy of the petition filed under this article to the city manager. It shall be the duty of the city manager to inspect the street or alley, or portion thereof, sought to be abandoned and to determine if the street or alley, or portion thereof, is open and in use, and if it is needed, or likely to be needed, by the city in the future for right-of-way or other purposes, and it shall also be the duty of the city manager to determine the value of such property. The city manager shall make a written report of his findings to the board of commissioners.
(Code 1976, § 31-38)
Sec. 86-85. - Hearing; vacation procedure.
At a public hearing, the board of commissioners shall hear all parties favoring or opposing the granting of an application under this article for the abandonment of a street or alley, or portion thereof, and determine if such street or alley, or portion thereof, shall be abandoned. If the board of commissioners shall determine that such application shall be granted, they shall also determine the value of such property. After such determination, the applicant shall be notified of the determination of such amount and shall deposit the amount with the city clerk, whereupon the board of commissioners shall proceed, by ordinance, to abandon such property, and to authorize the execution and delivery of a quit claim deed to the owners of the adjoining property.
(Code 1976, § 31-39)

Sec. 86-101. - Article name.
This article shall be known and may be cited as "The Modification, Permanent Closure, Alteration, Abandonment and Disposal of Streets, Alleys, Lanes, Roadways, Easements, Rights-of-Way, and Surplus Real Property and to Review Requests for Deeds and Similar Instruments Ordinance."
(Ord. of 2-4-2020, § 1)
Sec. 86-102. - Purpose, authorization, and findings of fact.
(a)
The intent and purpose of this article is to set and establish procedures for the modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment and disposal of streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, rights-of-way and surplus real property that are located within the City of Jesup.
(b)
This article is further enacted for the purpose of assisting the city in avoiding or mitigating the costs of maintaining streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, rights-of-way and surplus real property that no longer serve a substantial public purpose or that are no longer in the best public interest of the city to retain or continue.
(c)
This article is likewise enacted for the purposes of assisting the city in its consideration of the modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment or the disposal of streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, rights-of-way and surplus real property which would serve a substantial public purpose, or which would be in the best public interest.
(d)
Similarly, this article is also enacted for the purpose of assisting the city in its consideration and assessment of various requests from the public for various deeds to real property, quit-claim deeds, warranty deeds and similar articles of conveyance from the city to such persons.
(e)
This article will assist the city in promoting business development, residential development and overall positive growth of the city by providing procedures for those persons seeking the modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment or disposal of streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, rights-of-way and surplus real property within the City of Jesup for such growth and development purposes.
(f)
This article will promote the public interest by providing clearly-articulated procedures for those persons seeking the modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment or disposal of streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, rights-of-way and surplus real property within the City of Jesup and similar clearly articulated procedures for the consideration and assessment of various requests from the public for various deeds to real property, quit-claim deeds, warranty deeds and similar articles of conveyance from the city to such persons.
(g)
This article is intended to assist the City of Jesup in its abandonment of streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, and rights-of-way in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 32-7-2, as amended.
(h)
This article is intended to assist the City of Jesup in its disposal or disposition of abandoned streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, rights-of-way and surplus real property in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 32-7-4, as amended, and in the consideration and assessment of various requests from the public for various deeds to real property, quit-claim deeds, warranty deeds and similar articles of conveyance from the city to such persons.
(i)
This article is adopted pursuant to the police power and authority given to cities and municipalities by the Constitution of the State of Georgia, the codes and general laws of the State of Georgia, and the Amended and Restated Charter of the City of Jesup.
(j)
This article is adopted pursuant to and in accordance with O.C.G.A. §§ 32-7-2 and 32-7-4, and other provisions of statewide law of general jurisdiction.
(k)
The findings and recitations set out in this article are found to be true and correct, and they are hereby adopted by the board of commissioners and are made a part hereof for all purposes.
(Ord. of 2-4-2020, § 2)
Sec. 86-103. - Scope.
(a)
All persons, other than the City of Jesup and except as otherwise expressly provided herein, desiring or requesting that the City of Jesup modify, permanently close, alter, abandon or dispose of any street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way within the City of Jesup are required to comply with this article.
(b)
All persons, other than the City of Jesup and except as otherwise expressly provided herein, desiring or requesting any form of a deed to real property, quit-claim deed, easement, license, warranty deed or similar article of conveyance from the city are required to comply with this article.
(c)
All persons, other than the City of Jesup and except as otherwise expressly provided herein, desiring or requesting the disposal of surplus real property of the city are required to comply with this article.
(d)
This article shall not limit, restrict, prevent or prohibit the City of Jesup, itself, from modifying, closing, altering, abandoning or disposing of any street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way within the City of Jesup that, in accordance with the general operations of the city or as otherwise determined to be necessary by the city, the city, itself, deems should be so modified, closed, altered, abandoned or disposed of. The purpose of this article is to provide uniform procedures to address requests from third parties for the modification, closing, alteration, abandonment or disposal of the city's streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, or rights-of-way.
(e)
This article shall not limit, restrict, prevent or prohibit the City of Jesup, itself, from disposing of surplus real property in accordance with the general operations of the city or as otherwise determined to be beneficial to the city. The further purpose of this article is to provide uniform procedures to address requests from third parties for the disposal and disposition of surplus real property.
(f)
This article shall not apply to requests for the modification, closing, alteration, abandonment or disposal of the city's streets, alleys, lanes, roadways, easements, or rights-of-way from any department, agency or arm of any federal, state or local government.
(g)
This article shall not apply to requests from any department, agency or arm of any federal, state or local government for the disposal and disposition of surplus real property.
(h)
This article shall not create or constitute any right, entitlement, property interest, property right, title, vested interest or other claim that the city must actually take or effectuate any action to modify, permanently close, alter, abandon or dispose of any street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way that is located within the City of Jesup.
(i)
This article shall not create or constitute any right, entitlement, property interest, property right, title, vested interest or other claim that the city must actually give, grant, execute or provide any form of a deed to real property, quit-claim deed, easement, license, warranty deed or similar article of conveyance from the city.
(j)
This article shall not create or constitute any right, entitlement, property interest, property right, title, vested interest or other claim that the city must actually dispose of, sell, convey, or assign surplus real property.
(Ord. of 2-4-2020, § 3)
Sec. 86-104. - Application data.
(a)
Any person desiring that the city modify, permanently close, alter, abandon or dispose of any street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way within the City of Jesup shall submit an application to the city on application forms provided by the city for such purposes and submit any required fee for such application. Any such application and all other supporting documents or information required under this article shall be submitted to the city manager or to his or her designee.
(b)
Any person desiring that the city dispose of, sell, convey, or assign surplus real property within the City of Jesup shall submit an application to the city on application forms provided by the city for such purposes and submit any required fee for such application. Any such application and all other supporting documents or information required under this article shall be submitted to the city manager or to his or her designee.
(c)
Any person desiring or requesting any form of a deed to real property, quit-claim deed, easement, license, warranty deed or similar article of conveyance from the city shall submit an application to the city on application forms provided by the city for such purposes and submit any required fee for such application. Any such application and all other supporting documents or information required under this article shall be submitted to the city manager or to his or her designee.
(d)
Any application under this article shall be accompanied by a non-refundable application fee in the sum and amount of $500.00. The city may adjust this fee at any time by resolution or ordinance of the board of commissioners.
(e)
In addition to submitting the completed application form provided by the city, the applicant shall also provide the following, along with the application:
(1)
A written description and a photograph or sketch of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way subject to the application or request, or a similar written description and photograph or sketch of any surplus real property within the City of Jesup subject to the application or request;
(2)
A written statement of the nature of the applicant's request, including whether the request is for: (a) the modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment, disposal or a combination thereof of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way; or (b) for the disposal, conveyance or assignment of surplus real property within the City of Jesup; or (c) for a deed to real property, quit-claim deed, easement, license, warranty deed or similar article of conveyance from the city, along with a copy (or draft) of the proposed deed, quit-claim deed, easement, license, or similar instrument sought by the applicant.
(3)
A written statement as to: (a) the applicant's need for the modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment, disposal or a combination thereof of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way and the applicant's proposed usage of such; or (b) the applicant's need for the surplus real property and the applicant's proposed usage of such surplus real property; or (c) the applicant's need for any proposed deed, quit-claim deed, easement, license, or similar instrument sought by the applicant.
(4)
The applicant's anticipated use of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way, or surplus real property, or any proposed deed, quit-claim deed, easement, license, or similar instrument sought by the applicant.
(5)
Any other information or data that may be requested by the city manager or his or her designee related to: (a) the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the proposed modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment, disposal or a combination thereof; or (b) the surplus real property; or (c) the proposed deed, quit-claim deed, easement, license, or similar instrument sought by the applicant. Although not initially required with the application, the city manager may further require the applicant to submit, at the applicant's own, sole cost and expense the following prior to the city's manager's recommending approval or denial or such an application: (a) a plat or survey of the affected street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the surplus real property or any property affected by or subject to any proposed deed, quit-claim deed, easement, license, or similar instrument sought by the applicant; and (b) a proposed engineering drawing of the proposed modification, permanent closure, alteration, abandonment, disposal or a combination thereof; and (c) a metes and bound legal description of the affected street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the portion, part, segment, or stretch thereof to be modified, closed, altered, abandoned or disposed of or any surplus real property to be disposed of; and (d) a written certification or affirmation of the underlying title from an attorney at law admitted to practice law in the State of Georgia or a title insurance company that is authorized to provide title-related services in the State of Georgia related to: (d)(i) such affected street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way, or (d)(ii) of any affected surplus real property or (d)(iii) any property affected by any proposed deed, quit-claim deed, easement, license, or similar instrument sought by the applicant.
(Ord. of 2-4-2020, § 4)
Sec. 86-105. - Application review and standard of review.
(a)
The city manager may first give his or her recommendation for approval or denial of any application submitted under this article. If the city manager first preliminarily recommends in writing to the board of commissioners the approval of the application, such application shall be then forwarded to the board of commissioners for ultimate disposition, with such consideration by the board to occur at an official meeting of the commissioners.
(b)
The city manager and board of commissioners may solicit input or comments upon the application from any source that he, she, they or it deem appropriate, including from the street department, sanitation department, public works department, city engineer, city planner, zoning official, code enforcement officer, city police department, city fire department, city attorney, federal, state, county or city government officials or departments, community development authorities, utility providers (including water and sewer, electrical, telephone, cable and natural gas providers), adjacent or otherwise-affected landowners and any member of the public.
(c)
For purposes of this paragraph, both substantial public purpose and best public interest shall mean any tangible or objectively verifiable purpose, benefit, advantage, assistance, usefulness, helpfulness, or effectiveness to the City of Jesup in connection with any of the following activities or functions: (a) the city's provision of utilities or city-related services; (b) ingress into and egress from the city; (c) travel and egress throughout the city; (d) the construction, maintenance or future construction or extension of water-services infrastructure, sewerage infrastructure, cellular utility infrastructure, communications infrastructure, roads, streets, or methods of ingress into, egress out of, or travel through the city; (e) the provision or expansion of sanitation or waste-management services; (f) the promotion of traffic control, parking or crowd control within the City of Jesup; (g) the provision of lighting throughout the city; (h) crime prevention; (i) the provision of police and public-safety services; (j) the provision of fire prevention and control services; (k) the future expansion of city services; (1) drainage; (m) stormwater discharge; (n) the elimination or eradication of blighted or dilapidated properties; (o) the promotion of the growth and development of existing or future businesses in the city; (p) the promotion of the growth and development of existing or future residential areas in the city; or (q) any other conditions, facts, factors, or requirements contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 32-7-2, as amended from time-to-time.
(d)
The city manager and the board of commissioners (if approval is recommended in writing by the city manager) shall review any applications submitted to them under this article for the following:
(1)
Whether a subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or subject surplus real property is then in actual usage by the city or otherwise serves any substantial public purpose for the City of Jesup or may in the future be useful or beneficial to the city in the city's provision of city services (inclusive of fire, police protection or refuse removal services), potable water or sewerage utilities; or for general ingress and egress purposes; or
(2)
Whether the continuation or retention of such street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or surplus real property is in the best economic interest of the city; and
(3)
Whether the then-current configuration, arrangement, alignment, structure or location of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or surplus real property provides for or may facilitate the expansion and further development, extension, expansion or renewal of the city's then-existing infrastructure; and
(4)
Whether the then-current configuration, arrangement, alignment, structure or location of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or surplus real property provides for or may facilitate the expansion and further development, extension, expansion or renewal of the city's then-existing infrastructure; and
(5)
Whether the then-current configuration, arrangement, alignment, structure or location of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or surplus real property provides for or may facilitate the further growth, development or renewal of the city's downtown corridor; and
(6)
Whether the then-current configuration, arrangement, alignment, structure or location of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or surplus real property facilitates the implementation of the city's comprehensive plan as such plan is modified from time-to-time; and
(7)

Sec. 86-106. - Additional procedures applicable for permanent closure, abandonment or disposal of a street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or disposal or disposition of surplus real property.
(a)
In the event of a proposed permanent closure, abandonment or disposal of a street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way, if the city manager first preliminarily recommends in writing to the board of commissioners the approval of the application, the following additional procedures shall apply:
(1)
Within 90 days of the city manager's recommendation in writing of the approval of the application for permanent closure, abandonment or disposal of a street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or for the disposition or disposal of the subject surplus real property, the applicant shall provide to the city manager, at the applicant's own, sole cost the following:
a.
An official and recordable plat or survey by a registered land surveyor (licensed in the State of Georgia) of the affected street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the subject surplus real property; and
b.
A metes and bound legal description of the affected street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the portion, part, segment, or stretch thereof to be modified, closed, altered, abandoned or disposed of or of the subject surplus real property; and
c.
A written certification or affirmation of the underlying title of such affected street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way, or of the subject surplus real property by an attorney at law admitted to practice law in the State of Georgia or a title insurance company that is authorized to provided title-related services in the State of Georgia; and
d.
A complete list of all property owners, including their names, addresses and telephone numbers, within 1,500 feet of the affected part or portion of street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the subject surplus real property;
(2)
A public hearing before the board of commissioners upon the issue of the proposed permanent closure, abandonment or disposal of a street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the disposition or disposal of surplus real property shall be scheduled following the applicant's provision of the additional materials required under this section. Such hearing may occur during or at a regularly-scheduled or specially-called meeting of the board of commissioners.
(3)
The city manager will direct the city clerk to run an advertisement in the local newspaper once a week for two weeks advising that a public hearing will be held upon the proposed permanent closure, abandonment or disposal of such street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the disposition or disposal of surplus real property.
(4)
The city clerk will notify by mail the applicant and all effected property owners (as indicated in city or county tax records and the applicant's list of such persons) within 1,500 of the affected part or portion of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, right-of-way or the surplus real property as to the purpose, time and date of the public hearing.
(5)
The time, date and proposed request shall also be posted on the affected part or portion of the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, right-of-way or the surplus real property by the city at least ten calendar days prior to the public hearing.
(b)
If the board of commissioners approves a proposed permanent closure, abandonment or disposal, the city shall then dispose of such the subject street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way or the surplus real property (or any portion thereof) in accordance with applicable statewide law of general jurisdiction, including, O.C.G.A. § 32-7-4, as amended.
(Ord. of 2-4-2020, § 6)
Sec. 86-107. - Requests for various deeds to real property, quit-claim deeds, warranty deeds and similar articles of conveyance from the city to such persons.
(a)
The City of Jesup and its various departments, officials and employees shall avoid and refrain from the issuance, distribution, provision or delivery of deeds to real property, quit-claim deeds, warranty deeds, assignment and similar articles of conveyance from the city to members of the public absent compliance with this article.
(b)
No street, alley, lane, roadway, easement, or right-of-way of the City of Jesup or any surplus real property may be formally disposed of by deed, quit-claim deeds, warranty deeds, assignment and similar articles of conveyance apart from compliance with applicable provisions of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, the Amended and Restated Charter of the City of Jesup and this (and other applicable) ordinances of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 2-4-2020, § 7)
Sec. 86-108. - Miscellaneous provisions.
(a)
Any and all ordinances, resolutions, motions or parts thereof in conflict herewith are hereby repealed and superseded. If any sentence, clause, phrase or paragraph of this article is declared to be unconstitutional or void by any court of competent jurisdiction, such holding will not affect any other portion of this article.
(b)
This article, being an exercise of a power specifically delegated to the City of Jesup, Georgia's legislative body, which is its duly-elected board of commissioners, is not subject to referendum.
(c)
This article shall become effective immediately upon its adoption by the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup, Georgia.
(Ord. of 2-4-2020, § 8)
