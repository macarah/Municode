Sec. 14-3. - Arrest of person accused; summons.
The chief of police may arrest any person charged with violating any city ordinance or any state offense enforceable in the municipal court and bring such person before the judge, releasing such person upon such person's giving bond in compliance with section 14-5, or he or she may summon such offender to appear before the municipal court, notifying the accused of the time and place at which such person shall appear. For either arrest or summons the chief of police shall advise the accused of the charges against such person. If the accused demands it, such person shall be furnished with a written statement of the charges against such person, and also upon demand the accused shall have compulsory process for obtaining witnesses.
(Code 1935, § 69; Code 1987, § 7-3; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-4. - Issuance of arrest warrants.
Warrants for the arrest of the accused and those persons failing to appear before the court when summoned may be issued by the judge for violation of the city ordinances and any state offense enforceable in the municipal court on affidavits similar to affidavits required to obtain state warrants, except in such affidavits the words "City of Byron" shall be used in lieu of the words "County of __________." Such warrants shall be directed to the chief of police, and it shall be the duty of the chief of police to exercise reasonable diligence in effecting the arrest and trial of such accused person.
(Code 1935, § 70; Code 1987, § 7-4; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-5. - Making bail.
Any person arrested by the chief of police for the violation of the city ordinances or any state offense enforceable in the municipal court may give the chief of police a bond with good security to be approved by the judge and given in accordance with the city Charter and this Code.
(Code 1935, § 71; Code 1987, § 7-5; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-6. - Forfeiture of bond.
(a)
Upon default or failure of any person under bond to appear at the time and place as provided, such bond shall be forfeited in the manner in which bonds are forfeited in superior court, and the judge, chief of police and clerk shall perform the duties incident to such forfeiture, the issuance of scire facias, and the collection of such bond. After ten days' notice in writing has been served on the principal and surety, by personal service if such principal and surety are in the city; if either or both are out of the city, by written notice published in a public place in the city. Unless good cause is shown to the contrary, judgment may be entered against such principal and surety or such of them as have been served.
(b)
The costs of such forfeiture shall be one-half of that allowed by law to officers of the superior court in like cases. Bail may surrender the principal at any time before the rendition of judgment on the scire facias, but shall be liable for the costs accruing up to the time of surrender. Forfeiture of a bond does not abate the proceedings against a defendant.
(Code 1935, § 73; Code 1987, § 7-6; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-7. - Criminal docket.
The clerk shall keep a criminal docket whereon such officer shall docket all cases of persons charged with violations of the city ordinances and any state offense enforceable in the municipal court, showing the name of the accused, the offense charged, the date, the final judgment, and all other entries showing the progress of the case.
(Code 1935, § 74; Code 1987, § 7-7; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-8. - Speedy trial.
The accused shall have a speedy trial, and continuances shall be granted only on proper showing in the discretion of the judge.
(Code 1935, § 75; Code 1987, § 7-8; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-9. - Practice.
The practice in the municipal court shall be the same as in superior court so far as is practicable, under the ordinances and Charter of the city, and the accused shall have the right of counsel. Any defendant that is unable to afford their own counsel that is charged with a violation of a city ordinance or any state offense enforceable in the municipal court for which the possibility of incarceration exists as a penalty shall be provided counsel at no cost by the city as provided by law and according to the rules governing indigent defense qualifications in Georgia.
(Code 1935, § 76; Code 1987, § 7-9; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-10. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2021-7 , adopted May 10, 2021, repealed § 14-10, which pertained to appeal, and derived from the Code of 1987, § 7-10.
Sec. 14-11. - Disposition of convicted persons.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police or other officer in attendance on the municipal court to take all persons who have been convicted and fined or sentenced by the municipal court, or the appellate court on disposition of appeal, into such officer's custody and in no case to release such person so convicted and fined or sentenced, until the fine is paid or sentence is completed. It shall be the duty of the chief of police to confine such convicted persons in the jail, when the sentence is a jail sentence; to supervise their community service when that is the sentence; to collect the fine when a fine is assessed; or to execute the sentence of the court, whatever it may be. The city may use the common jail of the county as the city prison under such arrangement as the city authorities may make with the county authorities. The chief of police shall not maltreat a prisoner or convict committed to such officer's custody, but shall use all reasonable measures and means to carry out the city ordinances and the sentences and orders of the court.
(Code 1935, § 78; Code 1987, § 7-11; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-12. - Technology surcharge.
There shall be imposed by the municipal court a technology surcharge in the amount of $25.00 per offense for all offenses except O.C.G.A. §§ 40-8-76 and 40-8-76.1, said technology surcharge to be in addition to all other fines and fees imposed by the municipal court. All revenue derived from the technology surcharge shall be utilized by the city to provide technology support for the police department and municipal court functions.
(Ord. No. 2010-10, 12-30-2010; Ord. No. 2012-6, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)

ARTICLE II. - PROSECUTING ATTORNEY OF THE MUNICIPAL COURT

Sec. 14-20. - Short title.
This article shall be known as the "Byron Office of Prosecuting Attorney of the Municipal Court Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-21. - Findings and intent.
This article is adopted to address requirements made under Georgia law for the city to pass an ordinance or resolution creating the office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court should the city choose to hire or have a prosecuting attorney of the municipal court. Under state law, in order to have a municipal court prosecutor, the city must pass the ordinance or resolution codified in this article, and provide to the Prosecuting Attorneys' Council of the State of Georgia a copy of the same. The city is also required to submit the name of the person appointed to be the prosecuting attorney of the municipal court within 30 days of such appointment in order to maintain the office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court. It is therefore the intent of the city to comply with Georgia law, particularly O.C.G.A. § 15-18-5, and to enact this article.
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-22. - Establishment of office.
The office of prosecuting attorney of the municipal court is hereby established for the purpose of providing representation of the City of Byron in matters pertaining to ordinance violations of the city and state offenses enforceable in the municipal court as allowed by Georgia law. The prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall be a part-time position.
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-23. - Qualifications.
Any person appointed as the prosecuting attorney for the Municipal Court of the City of Byron shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia and admitted to practice before the trial and appellate courts of this state. Nothing in this article shall prevent the city from appointing the city attorney to be the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court so long as all other requirements under the law are met.
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-24. - Oath.
The prosecuting attorney of the Municipal Court for the City of Byron shall take and subscribe to the following oath:
"I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as prosecuting attorney of the Municipal Court for the City of Byron."
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-25. - Term of office.
The prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall serve at the pleasure of the city council unless a term of office is otherwise provided for by written agreement of the parties.
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-26. - Jurisdiction, duties and authority.
The office of prosecuting attorney of the Municipal Court for the City of Byron shall have the duty and authority to represent the city as defined by the city Charter and by state law, particularly as described in O.C.G.A. § 15-18-96.
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-27. - Assistant prosecuting attorney.
One or more assistant prosecuting attorneys for the municipal court may be recommended by the prosecuting attorney and appointed by the city council in its discretion. Such appointments shall be subject to budgetary funding. Such assistant prosecuting attorney shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia or satisfy the provisions of the Third Year Practice Act, found in O.C.G.A. § 15-18-22.
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
Sec. 14-28. - Ratification.
This article shall ratify all actions that have been taken by the persons acting in the positions of the office of the prosecuting attorney for the municipal court to date as authorized by the city council.
(Ord. No. 2012-7, 7-9-2012; Ord. No. 2021-7 , 5-10-2021)
