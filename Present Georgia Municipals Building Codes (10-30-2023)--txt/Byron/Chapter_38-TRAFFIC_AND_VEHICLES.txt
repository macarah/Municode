Sec. 38-3. - Obstruction of traffic.
modified
It shall be unlawful for any person to place any obstructions in the streets or sidewalks tending to prevent free passage, provided that vehicles may be parked and left on the streets a reasonable length of time.
(Code 1935, § 87; Code 1987, § 19-3; Ord. No. 2022-3 , 2-14-2022)
State Law reference— Obstructing streets, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-43.
Sec. 38-4. - Truck routes—Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section and in sections 38-5 and 38-6, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Deviating truck means a truck which leaves and departs from a truck route while traveling within the city limits as permitted.
Truck means any vehicle designed or operated for the transportation of property, including raw materials, and the body weight or combined body and load weight of which exceeds three tons.
Truck route means any street, or portion thereof, within the city, over which trucks are not prohibited to operate by the provisions of sections 38-5 and 38-6.
(Ord. of 10-13-1981, § 1; Code 1987, § 19-4; Ord. of 6-1-1998; Ord. No. 2022-3 , 2-14-2022)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.

(b)
Exceptions to subsection (a) of this section are as follows:
(1)
Any truck may be deviated from a truck route and operated over a prohibited street listed in subsection (a) of this section where necessary to reach a destination point within the city for the conduct of business, provided streets upon which truck traffic is permitted are used until reaching the intersection of the prohibited street nearest such destination. An adequate surety bond for persons using the prohibited street more than casual is required.
(2)
Any truck may be operated over a prohibited street listed in subsection (a) of this section where necessary to leave a point of origin within the city for the conduct of business, provided it leaves such prohibited street at the nearest intersection of a truck route to such point of origin.
(3)
Any truck may be operated over a prohibited street fisted in subsection (a) of this section if such truck is owned or operated by the city, public utilities, any contractor or material man, while engaged in the repair, maintenance or construction of streets, street improvements, or street utilities within the city.
(c)
All streets within the city except those listed in subsection (a) of this section are open to truck traffic.
(Ord. of 10-13-1981, § 2; Code 1987, § 19-5; Ord. of 6-1-1998; Ord. No. 2010-1; 2-8-2010; Ord. No. 2022-3 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 38-6. - Same—Enforcement.
modified
(a)
The clerk shall keep and maintain accurate maps setting out truck routes and streets upon which truck traffic is permitted. The maps shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk and shall be available to the public.
(b)
The chief of police shall cause those streets upon which truck traffic is prohibited to be clearly signposted to give notice that the prohibitions are in effect.
(c)
The chief of police shall have the authority to require any person driving or in control of any commercial vehicle not proceeding over a truck route or street over which truck traffic is permitted to proceed to any public or private scale available for the purpose of weighing and determining whether sections 38-4 and 38-5 have been complied with.
(Ord. of 10-13-1981, § 3; Ord. of 6-1-1998; Code 1987, § 19-6; Ord. No. 2022-3 , 2-14-2022)

Sec. 38-10. - Driving across private property to avoid an intersection.
modified
It shall be unlawful for the driver of any vehicle to drive the same upon or through the private property of another for the purpose of cutting through or taking a short-cut without the permission of the person in possession, or entitled to possession, of the premises so as to avoid entering any intersection.
(Ord. No. 2017-1 , 4-10-2017; Ord. No. 2022-3 , 2-14-2022)
