ARTICLE XIII. - FILM PERMITS

Sec. 12-500. - Purpose.
The City of Hiram invites and encourages film production utilizing city property provided: the City of Hiram's primary responsibility to provide service and protection to the general public is not impaired; City of Hiram is compensated for the time, labor, and other costs associated with permitting the utilization of City of Hiram property and facilities; and, City of Hiram is indemnified from any responsibility arising from any injury, accident, destruction of property or other occurrence associated with the project. City of Hiram reserves the right to refuse access to city property on the grounds of prior reference examination, and portrayal of the city in the content of the project.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-501. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Chief of police shall mean the City of Hiram Police Chief or his designee.
Filming activity shall mean the staging, shooting, filming, videotaping, photographing, or other similar process conducted for the making of still photographs, motion pictures, television programs, music videos, commercials, and other commercial and non-theatrical productions.
Film permit shall mean written authorization from the City of Hiram to conduct the filming activity outlined in the permit.
High impact film activities, in general, are those that fall outside two or more of the criteria for a low impact activity. Additional criteria that classify a film activity as high impact include:
(1)
High speed chases or crashes;
(2)
Use of pyrotechnics or explosives; or
(3)
Use of aircraft.
Low impact film activities are defined as those which:
(1)
Allow uninterrupted flow of pedestrian traffic;
(2)
Hold vehicular traffic for no more than one minute intervals;
(3)
Utilize no more than four on-street parking spaces in any linear block containing businesses dependent on on-street parking;
(4)
Have minimal impact on normal business activities;
(5)
Take place in the downtown business district outside normal business hours;
(6)
Utilize no more than 50 percent of the on-street parking spaces in any linear block containing business not dependent on on-street parking;
(7)
Utilize no on-street parking where there are no marked on-street parking spaces;
(8)
Utilize no parking in alleys without prior approval by the abutting businesses and the fire department;
(9)
Provide parking for cast, crew, extras and other nonessential vehicles in off-street lots, as well as alternate parking for those displaced from these lots;
(10)
Provide a private holding area for extras; and
(11)
Conduct prep and wrap activities in accordance with the above.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-502. - Permit required.
(a)
A film permit is required for any commercial film activity which involves the use of public property, the public right-of-way in any one location for longer than five consecutive minutes or impacts the public right-of-way beyond normal traffic. (Examples: on-street parking; interruption of vehicular or pedestrian traffic flow; placement of flats, cameras, lights or other equipment on public property).
(b)
Application will be made on the city's standard form and presented to the permits clerk or his/her duly appointed representative for review no later than ten days prior to start of filming. Exceptions may be made in special situations. Early application is recommended as permits are issued on a first come first served basis, and time may be needed to work out any difficulties associated with the planned activity. Requirements may vary depending on the impact of the activity.
(c)
Film permits issued pursuant to this article shall be effective for the stated purpose from the date of issuance through the date specified in the permit.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-503. - Applications and issuance of permit.
(a)
Applications shall be made on a form prescribed by the city and shall include the following information:
(1)
Address of filming location;
(2)
The specific location at such address or place (include drawing);
(3)
The inclusive hours and dates such activity will occur;
(4)
A general statement of the character or nature of the proposed filming activity;
(5)
The name, address, email address, and telephone number of the person or persons in charge of such filming activity, including the name of the production company;
(6)
The anticipated number of personnel to be involved;
(7)
Activity that may cause public alarm such as the use of any animals, gunfire or pyrotechnics and low flying aircraft;
(8)
The anticipated amount/type of vehicles/equipment to be employed along with a parking plan;
(9)
Clean-up plan;
(10)
Property owner permission including contact information (i.e. phone number and email address).
(b)
Applications must be submitted a minimum of ten days in advance.
(c)
Upon the request of the applicant, the city shall have the power, upon a showing of good cause, to change the date for which a film permit has been issued, provided established limitations are complied with in regard to time, location, and notification.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-504. - Notification of affected businesses and residents.
(a)
The production company will notify all businesses and residents affected by the activity. Notification should take place utilizing the city's acknowledgement and consent for filming form turned in with application. The area to be covered by notification may vary depending on the impact of the planned activity but will be a minimum of all businesses or residents within a 500-foot radius of the area in which the activity is to occur. If filming in a residential area, also see the "residential areas" section.
(b)
Affected businesses and residents shall have ten business days after submittal of the application for a permit in which to notify the city of any concerns/objections in reference to the proposed film activities.
(c)
The city representative shall attempt to resolve concerns or objections reported in consultation with the business, and the production company.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-505. - Traffic control and street closures.
(a)
Permit approval must be obtained prior to any film activity which would disrupt the normal flow of traffic. Traffic control, if needed, must be handled by off-duty police officers hired by the production company through the city's police department. The city retains the right to determine the number of officers needed. Intermittent holding of traffic shall not exceed three-minute intervals except when specifically approved by permit.
(b)
Street closures are discouraged in most instances. Requests for street closures or diverting of traffic should be made well in advance to allow for planning of alternate routes and proper notification.
(c)
Interruption of traffic on state routes or closure of state routes requires prior approval from the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT), as well as the city. The city will provide the production company with the appropriate contact at the GDOT.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-506. - Special effects, explosives and similar devices.
No film activity which involves the use of explosives, pyrotechnics, fire, smoke-making machines or other special effects may be undertaken unless specifically approved by Paulding Fire and Emergency Services. The production company will be responsible for acquiring the required permissions.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-507. - Removal of vegetation.
Removal, cutting or trimming of vegetation in the public right-of-way is prohibited unless specifically approved by permit.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-508. - Residential areas.
(a)
In areas zoned residential, film activities will not begin prior to 7:00 a.m., and will end by 11:00 p.m.
(b)
Affected residents in an area not less than a 500-foot radius of the proposed filming location must be notified. Notification should include information concerning the location and duration of the filming activity in question, as well as specifics regarding lights, noise, or any special effects, and be in the form of the acknowledgment and consent form.
(c)
All equipment and support vehicles not in immediate use shall be parked so as not to cause any interference to pedestrians or vehicular traffic.
(d)
When filming on residential streets which experience traffic congestion as a result of schools opening and closing or the morning and evening "rush" hours, neither pedestrian nor vehicular traffic may be held during these "rush" times, which will be agreed upon in advance by the locations' manager.
(e)
Production vehicles must not block fire hydrants, driveways, or other access ramps unless authorized by the city or property owner. Ten feet of clearance should be left on either side of affected driveways, unless otherwise authorized.
(f)
Production vehicles must be parked in such a way as to not impede safe lines of vision at intersections.
(g)
Lighting for filming, both during the day and night, should be oriented away from neighboring residences wherever possible, and should not interfere with the safe movement of traffic.
(h)
Production companies are responsible for cleaning and restoring locations used, including public and private lands, with a minimum amount of noise and disruption.
(i)
Production companies blocking off or holding up traffic on streets that are scheduled for garbage pick-up is prohibited.
(j)
Residents may not charge production companies for use of the public right-of-way in front of their homes, nor prohibit use without justifiable cause, but may reasonably expect the right-of-way to be restored to its' original condition by the production company.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-509. - Insurance.
As a condition for the issuance of its permit, each applicant will at all times during the life of its permit and at its own cost and expense procure and continue in force: (1) workers' compensation insurance as required by Georgia law; and (2) commercial general liability insurance providing coverage on an occurrence basis with limits of not less than $1,000,000.00 each occurrence for bodily injury and property damage combined, $5,000,000.00 products and completed operations annual aggregate. The applicant's liability insurance policy or policies will: (i) include premises and operations liability coverage, products and completed operations liability coverage, broad form property damage coverage including completed operations, blanket contractual liability coverage including, to the maximum extent possible, coverage for the indemnification obligations of the applicant under its permit, and personal and advertising injury coverage; (ii) provide that the insurance company has the duty to defend all insureds under the policy; (iii) provide that defense costs are paid in addition to and do not deplete any of the policy limits; and (iv) cover liabilities arising out of or incurred in connection with the applicant's and its agents', employees', contractors', licensees', affiliates', customers', invitees' and/or other representatives' use of the permit. The limits of such insurance will not limit the liability of the applicant. All of the insurance required under this section will name the city as an additional insured thereunder. The applicant will deliver to the city certificates of insurance evidencing the existence and amounts of such insurance with loss payable clauses satisfactory to issuer provided that in the event the applicant fails to procure and maintain such insurance, the applicant's permit shall be automatically and immediately revoked. No such policy will be cancelable or subject to reduction of coverage or other modification except after 30 days' prior written notice to the city by the insurer. The applicant will, within 20 days prior to the expiration of such policies furnish the city with renewals or binders. All such policies will be written as primary policies, not contributing with and not in excess of coverage, which the city may have. The applicant will have the right to provide such insurance coverage pursuant to blanket policies obtained by the applicant provided such blanket policies expressly afford coverage to the activities subject to the permit, city, and applicant as required by this article.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-510. - Indemnification.
Prior to issuance of a permit to any applicant, the applicant shall sign and record and indemnification agreement in a form approved by the city in which the applicant agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the city and its officials, employees, agents, and insurers from any liability arising from the issuance of the applicant's permit except for liability caused by the sole negligence of city and its officials, employees, agents, and insurers. The applicant shall further require in a form approved by the city that its users fully release the city and its officials, employees, agents, and insurers from any liability arising from the use of the applicant's permit.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-511. - Permit denial, revocation, or suspension.
(a)
A film production permit may be denied, revoked or suspended for any of the following reasons:
(1)
A materially false statement contained in the application;
(2)
Failure to comply with federal, state or municipal laws and regulations;
(3)
Failure to comply with any conditions imposed by the city on the issuance of the film production permit;
(4)
Failure to operate the film production in accordance with such ordinances, laws, orders, rules and regulations as may be applicable;
(5)
Conducting the film production business or activity in a fraudulent or disorderly manner;
(6)
The permit seeks to use a location or is in the vicinity of a location the undisturbed functioning of which is essential to public safety or is a component of critical infrastructure; or
(7)
Conducting the film production business or activity in a manner which endangers the public health, safety, or welfare of the city and/or its residents.
(b)
Notice of denial of a permit application and the reasons therefor shall be immediately delivered to the applicant. Notice of suspension or revocation of an existing film permit shall be delivered on site to the permit holder and effective immediately.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-512. - Appeal process.
The denial, suspension, or revocation of a film permit by the city staff may be appealed to the city council by filing a notice of appeal with the city clerk within ten days of the date of decision denying, suspending, or revoking a film permit. The city council will hear the matter at its next available meeting.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-513. - Penalties for violation.
A violation of this chapter is punishable as provided for in section 1-12 of the city's Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
Sec. 12-514. - Permit fees.
(a)
Each applicant will pay the required fees as established by resolution of the city council. Where set fees are established, production companies pay the same fee as the general public for the service or facility. Services for which a fee has not been established will be charged on the basis of time, equipment, and material. All fees are subject to change.
(b)
There are charges associated with use of certain city services or facilities, in addition to the ones listed below.
(1)
Police officer……………………………$35.00/hour* minimum with a five days' notice
*If less than five-day notice the fee increases to $50.00/hour.
(2)
Low impact permit fee .....$100.00/day
(3)
High impact permit fee .....$200.00/day
(Ord. No. 2021-05, § 1(Exh. A), 4-6-2021; Ord. No. 2021-06, § 1(Exh. A), 5-4-2021)
