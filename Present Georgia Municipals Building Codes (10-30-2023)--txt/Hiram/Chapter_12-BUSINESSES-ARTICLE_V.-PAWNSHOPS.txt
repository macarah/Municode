DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 12-111. - Purpose and authority of article.
(a)
This article is enacted in accordance with the plan designed for the purposes, among others, of promoting the health and general welfare of the community, to establish reasonable and ascertainable standards for the regulation and control of pawnshops or similar places where money is advanced on goods or other effects, or merchandise of any kind is taken in pawn.
(b)
This article is enacted pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-112. - License required.
(a)
License required. No pawnshop or any other business engaging in pawn transactions or otherwise regulated by this article shall be commenced or continue to be operated except as provided by and in compliance with this article, including, but not limited to, obtaining a lawfully issued license.
(b)
Any pawnshop or other business engaging in pawn transactions that is operated, conducted or maintained contrary to the provisions of this article shall be declared to be unlawful and a public nuisance. The city may, in addition to, or in lieu of prosecuting a criminal violation hereunder, commence an action or actions, proceeding or proceedings for abatement, removal or enjoinment thereof, in the manner provided by law. It shall take such other steps and shall apply to such court or courts as may have jurisdiction to grant such relief as will abate or remove such pawnshop and restrain and enjoin any person from operating, conducting or maintaining a pawnshop contrary to the provisions of this chapter. In addition, violation of the provisions of this article shall be per se grounds for suspension or revocation of any related license and occupation tax registration granted hereunder.
(c)
Rules governing those engaged in business prior to article; deadline for application. All persons already engaging in a business regulated by this article shall file an application in accordance with the terms of this article no later than December 31 of the year in which it is enacted. If the city council shall determine that any such application filed pursuant to this subsection should be denied, the applicant may continue to operate the business during any appeal process if pursued; provided, however, that at all times during which such applicant continues to operate his business he shall be subject to the regulatory provisions of this article.
(d)
The provisions of this article are supplementary of the state law statutes and regulations governing pawnshops and pawn transaction. Compliance with all such state law requirements is required in addition to compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-113. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Accepted identification means an official document, most commonly in the form of a plastic-coated/sealed card, issued for purposes of identification or driver's license. These documents must be issued by one of the 50 states or a branch of the U.S. military, i.e., Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines, Coast Guard, or current State of Georgia and counties of Georgia probation and parole cards. The identification must, at a minimum, bear a true photograph of the person presenting it, date of birth, description of the person, and an address for the person.
Deceptive business practice means:
(1)
Any use or possession of a false weight or measurement, or any other device for falsely determining or recording any quality or quantity in connection with any scale or service;
(2)
Any sale of, offer to sell or delivery of less than the represented quality or quantity;
(3)
Any attempt to take more than the represented quantity of any commodity, when as buyer or broker, he furnishes the weight or measure;
(4)
Any service which is of an unreasonably lesser quality than the service offered or represented;
(5)
Any other practice designated as unlawful by O.C.G.A. § 10-1-390 et seq.;
(6)
Any other fraudulent business transaction which is made punishable by the laws of the State of Georgia.
Interest in the license means exists if the person is the outright owner of the pawnshop; the applicant and/or licensee; a co-owner of the pawnshop; a partner in a partnership which owns all or part of the pawnshop; or a stockholder in any corporation organized for pecuniary gain which owns all or any part of the pawnshop.
Licensee means the person or entity authorized to conduct and transact business in accordance with this article.
Minor means any person who has not attained the age of 18 years.
Pawn or pledge means a bailment of personal property or title (including, but not limited to, a motor vehicle title) as security for any debt or engagement, redeemable upon certain terms and with the express or implied power of sale on default.
Pawn transaction means any loan on the security of pledged goods or any purchase of pledged goods on the condition that the pledged goods may be redeemed or repurchased by the pledger or seller for a fixed price within a fixed period of time.
Pawnbroker means any person engaged in whole or in part in the business of lending money on the security of pledged goods, personal property or titles, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property or title on the condition that it may be redeemed or repurchased by the seller for a fixed price within a fixed period of time, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property or title from persons or sources other than the manufacturers or regulated dealers as a part of or in conjunction with the business activities described herein.
Pawnshop means any business wherein a well-defined part of such business is to take or receive, by way of pledge, pawn, or exchange, any goods, wares, merchandise, motor vehicles, or any kind of personal property or title whatsoever, as security for the repayment of money lent thereon.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-114. - Violation of article.
(a)
In addition to the licensee, any person violating any of the provisions of this article may be cited and shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in the general penalties described in section 1-12.
(b)
The licensee(s) shall be responsible for violations of this article.
(c)
The violation of the provisions of this article by any person may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings for injunction in the courts of competent jurisdiction of this state. Such actions may be maintained, notwithstanding that other adequate remedies at law may exist. Such actions may be instituted in the name of the city council.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 12-115. - Applications; contents; deadline for submission; fees.
(a)
Any person(s) desiring to obtain a license required under this article shall make written application to the city manager through the city clerk for such privilege upon forms to be prepared and approved by the city. Such application shall require the name, address, telephone number, and email address of the applicant and the proposed licensee, if different than the applicant and a description of the nature and character of the business to be conducted. If the applicant or the proposed licensee is a partnership, the applicant must provide with the application the names, addresses, telephone numbers, and email addresses of all partners. If the applicant or the proposed licensee is a corporation, the applicant must provide with the application the names, addresses, telephone numbers, and email addresses of the officers and stockholders with 20 percent or more ownership.
(b)
At the time application is made, the applicant, on behalf of the proposed licensee, shall designate the person to be responsible for acceptance of service of any process, notice, violation, or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter.
(c)
In addition to the foregoing, the city may require additional information to assist it in evaluating the request for a license. No application shall be completed and submitted by anyone under the age of 21.
(d)
Applicants shall furnish all data, information, and records reasonably requested of them by the city and failure to furnish such data, information and records within 30 days from the date of such request shall be considered abandonment of the application.
(e)
At the time of application, the applicant shall certify for himself/herself and as the representative of the proposed licensee that they have read this article and agree to comply with its requirements. If the license is granted, each licensee shall maintain a copy of this article on the premises.
(f)
The application shall be made on an annual basis.
(g)
Licensees shall immediately notify the city in writing through the city clerk of any change in any information, material or data furnished in connection with an application for a license, or of any material change in the type of business, ownership or qualifications of any licensee, applicant, or employee subsequent to license issuance.
(h)
The initial license application shall be accompanied by an application fee in the amount of $100.00. Subsequent renewal applications shall be accompanied by a $50.00 renewal application fee. Application fees are not subject to refund.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-116. - Investigation of applicant; report by police department.
Accompanying submission of all applications to the city as required by this article shall be executed consent forms authorizing the city to conduct background investigations, including criminal background investigations on each individual having an interest in the license. For the purpose of this section, in reference to corporations, consent forms will be initially required for stockholders that have at least 20 percent ownership in stock shares. All applications shall be investigated, and the police department shall provide its findings to the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-117. - Issuance to persons with prior convictions prohibited; exception; pending.
No license under this article shall be issued where any individual having an interest in the license shall have been convicted or shall have taken a plea of nolo contendere within ten years immediately prior to the filing of the application for any felony, misdemeanor or offense of any state or of the United States or any municipal or county ordinance related to moral turpitude, illegal gambling, theft, deception or fraudulent practices. For purposes of this article, a conviction or plea of guilty or nolo contendere entered under the Georgia First Offender Act, O.C.G.A. § 42-8-60 et seq., shall not be considered. Provided, however, that any such offense shall be considered and be deemed disqualifying where the applicant violated any term of probation imposed by the court granting first offender status or committed another crime and the sentencing court entered an adjudication of guilt as to the crime for which the defendant had previously been sentenced as a first offender. The term "conviction" shall include an adjudication of guilt or plea of guilty or nolo contendere, or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime. Where the violation is for a misdemeanor, forfeiture of bond, or violation of a municipal or county ordinance, the city council may, upon denial on such a basis and upon appeal, consider waiving such violation as a disqualification. No license shall be issued so long as there are outstanding criminal warrants, criminal charges, accusations or indictments for any of the crimes enumerated in this section on which there has been no final disposition or adjudication, and any application involving such pending charges shall be held for final decision until final disposition or adjudication of the charges. No license to operate a pawnshop that deals in firearms shall be issued to any convicted felon, unless that person's right to possess firearms has been verifiably reinstated in the jurisdiction where the conviction occurred.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-118. - Disqualification of applicant(s); licensees.
No license under this article shall be granted where the application or the evidence shows any of the following conditions to exist:
(1)
An applicant or proposed licensee has had any pawnbroker license issued by a municipality, county, or other governmental entity previously suspended or revoked.
(2)
An applicant or proposed licensee, while a holder of a previous license to operate a pawnshop in any jurisdiction, has been convicted of violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business, within a ten-year period immediately preceding the date of application, or, within the same time period, has any disqualifying conviction under section 12-117.
(3)
An applicant or proposed licensee has failed to comply with any other requirements of any local, state, or federal law prescribed for the operation of the business contemplated by the license sought.
(4)
An applicant or proposed licensee has supplied false information either upon his application or in any communication with any official of the city relative to his application.
(5)
An applicant or proposed licensee has failed to pay any fee due to the city or county or has otherwise failed to comply with the provisions of this article.
(6)
An applicant or proposed licensee, during the 12-month period immediately preceding the filing of his application, has been determined by a competent court or administrative agency of having engaged in a deceptive business practice.
(7)
In addition to subsections (1) through (6) of this section and in determining whether or not any license applied for shall be granted, the following shall be considered in the public interest and welfare:
a.
If any applicant or proposed licensee is a previous holder of a license, the manner in which he conducted the business under such license as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
b.
The location for which the license is sought as to traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood and the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values.
c.
The number of licenses already granted for similar businesses in the trading area of and the place for which that license is sought.
d.
Whether and the extent to which the applicant or proposed licensee is presented as or engaged in a ruse or scheme to procure a license for a person or entity not otherwise eligible for a license.
(8)
No more than five such business establishments are permissible within the city; provided, however, that if such a business establishment ceases operation, the maximum number of such permissible business establishments within the city is reduced correspondingly, and no additional business establishment is permissible if the number of such business establishments then in operation exceeds a threshold of one such licensed business establishment for each 2,000 citizens in the city plus one additional license for any fractional figure exceeding the 2,000 citizen population threshold.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-119. - Consideration by city manager.
(a)
If the background investigation, including the criminal investigation, do not disclose disqualifying information or any other information requiring denial of the license and all other requirements for licensing are satisfied, then the city manager shall issue the license.
(b)
If the city manager determines that the license should be denied, then the city manager shall inform the proposed licensee, in writing, that the application is denied, providing a description in reasonable detail of the reasons for the denial and notifying the proposed licensee of the right to appeal.
(c)
Such appeal shall be before the city council in accordance with section 12-120 of this article. An appeal must be requested in writing and must be filed with the city clerk within ten business days of the date of the denial notice. An appeal shall be scheduled for a hearing before the city council at its next regularly scheduled meeting, provided that such meeting is no less than 15 days from the date the written request for an appeal is filed with the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-120. - Appeal hearings.
(a)
Written notice of date, time, and place of the hearing on an appeal of denial of a license shall be provided to the proposed licensee, provided that the proposed licensee shall be provided no less than 15 days' notice of the hearing.
(b)
At the hearing, the proposed licensee shall be afforded the opportunity to be heard and to present evidence, including witness testimony, in support of any contention that the denial is unwarranted or is otherwise inconsistent with the requirements of this article or any other provision of law. The proposed licensee is entitled to be represented by counsel at the hearing who may argue for and present the case of the proposed licensee.
(c)
At the conclusion of the hearing, the city council shall determine whether to sustain the decision of the city manager, reverse the decision of the city manager, or reverse the decision of the city manager but imposing conditions to be satisfied by the proposed licensee. The decision of the city council is final, subject to appeal to the Superior Court of Paulding County by way of a petition for a writ of certiorari as provided in O.C.G.A. § 5-4-1 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-121. - Revocation and suspension of licenses—Generally.
(a)
Subject to the provisions of this section, the city manager may, for due cause, suspend, revoke, or place on probation, with or without conditions, any license issued under this article.
(b)
Due cause for the revocation, suspension, or imposition of probationary status shall consist of any of the following:
(1)
A licensee commits a deceptive business practice that consists of:
a.
Using or possessing a false weight or measure, or any other device for falsely determining or recording any quality or quantity in connection with any sale or service;
b.
Selling, offering, exposing for sale, or delivering less than the represented quality or quantity;
c.
Taking or attempting to take more than the represented quantity of any commodity when as buyer or broker the licensee furnishes the weight or measure;
d.
Providing a service which is of any unreasonably lesser quality than the service offered or represented;
e.
Engaging in any other fraudulent business transaction which is made punishable by the laws of the state;
f.
Commission of any of the foregoing by any person employed or otherwise utilized in transacting the business of the licensee; or
g.
Any misrepresentation of fact, whether through advertisement or through any form of direct communication, oral or written, which is intended to mislead the public or to mislead any party with whom the licensee deals in pursuance of the licensee's business. The term "misrepresentation of fact," as used in this subsection, shall embrace not only express misrepresentations but also misrepresentations arising by virtue of the licensee's conduct, including acts and omissions.
(2)
Violation of any laws, ordinances, or resolutions regulating such business, or violation of regulations made pursuant to the authority granted for the purpose of regulating such business.
(3)
Providing false information to the city in support of the application for a license or any renewal thereof or in response to any of the reporting requirements imposed by this article, regardless of when the city shall have become aware of such false information.
(4)
Failure to comply with any other requirements of any local, state, or federal law prescribed for the operation of the business contemplated by the license sought as well as any other local, state, or federal law applicable to the business operation, including, but not limited to, the acts or practices declared unlawful by the State Fair Business Practices Act of 1975 (O.C.G.A. § 10-1-390 et seq.), as it may be amended from time to time or declared unlawful by the administrator of the Act pursuant to regulations issued under Section 4 of the Act, all subject to the exemptions in Section 6 of the Act.
(5)
Failing to comply with any provisions of this article.
(6)
The licensee has lost qualifications required by this article.
(c)
Upon determining that a license issued under this article should be suspended, revoked or placed on probation with or without conditions, the city manager shall inform the licensee in writing of that tentative decision and provide a description of the reason(s) why the action should be taken. The licensee shall be given seven business days to meet with the city manager to review and consider the situation, including the action proposed by the city manager. The licensee will be informed in writing of the city manager's final action within three business days of such meeting. The writing shall describe the reason(s) for the city manager's action(s) and notify the licensee of a right to appeal the action to the city council. A licensee's failure to meet with the city manager within the seven-day window constitutes a waiver of the right to so meet and any rights attendant to such a meeting and the notice of the city manager's tentative decision shall serve as the city manager's final action.
(d)
An appeal must be requested in writing and must be filed with the city clerk within ten business days of the date of the written notice of the city manager's final action. An appeal shall be scheduled for a hearing before the city council at its next regularly scheduled meeting, provided that such meeting is no less than 15 days from the date the written request for an appeal is filed with the city clerk. All appeal hearings are governed by and subject to the provisions of section 12-120.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-122. - Transfers.
Licenses issued for operation of a business establishment subject to this article are not transferable. Any person or business entity acquiring ownership or control otherwise of an already existing and operating business establishment may not operate the business without first complying with all of the requirements of this article as if a new licensee and receiving a license from the city except that if the business establishment was not required by law to have a special use permit, no special use permit shall be required of the new licensee unless the new licensee intends to relocate the business operation to a new location in the city. If at any time the business establishment licensed by the city ceases operation, the provisions of subsection 12-118(8) may preclude re-establishing the business operation.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-123. - To be obtained within two weeks of approval of application.
(a)
All licenses must be retrieved in person at city hall at which time all regulatory fees shall be paid. No license shall be effective until all regulatory fees have been paid. If retrieval and payment of the regulatory fees is not accomplished within two weeks from the date of the approval of the application, the application and any license otherwise to be issued in response to the application shall be deemed abandoned and void.
(b)
When a license has been approved and the applicant has deposited with the city clerk's office the required fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, the license shall be issued by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-124. - Renewal.
All licenses issued pursuant to this article or any predecessor provisions of a city ordinance shall be renewed annually. Renewal requires compliance with all provisions of this article. All licensees shall apply for the renewal of any existing license and pay the renewal application fee and annual regulatory fee no later than February 1 of each calendar year in which business is conducted.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-125. - Annual fee to be set by city council; refunds.
(a)
Prior to issuance of any license under this article, payment of an annual regulatory fee shall be required.
(b)
The annual regulatory fee shall be $250.00.
(c)
All licenses shall be issued for the calendar year beginning January 1, and the fees prescribed by the city council shall be paid in full by each person prior to issuance of a license.
(d)
Any license issued under this article shall expire on December 31 of the calendar year for which it is issued.
(e)
In case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of such calendar year period the holder of the license shall not be entitled to receive any refund whatsoever.
(f)
The regulatory fee shall be prorated by 50 percent for any business commenced after June 30 of any calendar year.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-126. - Authorizes conduct of one place of business.
Each license under this article shall authorize the conduct of one place of business only, and persons doing business in more than one place within the city shall obtain a separate license, paying a separate fee, for each such place of business.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-127. - Change of location.
(a)
The business authorized by a license may be relocated to another address subject to the following requirements:
(1)
The licensee shall advise the city clerk in writing of the proposed relocation and the new address at which business is proposed to be transacted;

If a special use permit is required for the proposed new location of the business, the special use permit must be obtained prior to any relocation and transaction of business at the new address. If a special use permit is required and not obtained, then the business may not relocate; and

Operation of the business at the new location is otherwise compliant with the city's zoning regulations.

If relocation is permitted, a new business license for the new business location shall be issued upon payment of a fee in an amount to be set by the city council.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-128. - Display.
All licenses issued under this article shall be posted conspicuously in the place of business authorized by the license.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)

All holders of licenses under this article must, within 30 days after the issuance of the license, open for business the establishment referred to in the license. No refund of application or regulatory license fees shall be provided.

(a)
All licenses issued under this article are a mere grant or privilege to carry on such business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this article and any other ordinance, resolution or law of the city, county, state or United States relating to such business.

All licenses issued under this article shall have printed on the front these words: "This license is a mere privilege subject to be revoked or suspended by the city in accordance with such procedures as adopted by the city."

(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-132. - Collection of unpaid fees.
Upon the failure to pay any regulatory fee as required by this article, any such fee is subject to collection in the manner as provided for the collection of taxes, regardless of the provisions of this article for penalties.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-133. - Void licenses.
Any license issued in violation of any provision or requirement of this article or any other applicable legal requirement is void.

DIVISION 3. - BUSINESS OPERATION

Sec. 12-162. - Employee regulations.
(a)
Every person who works in a pawnshop is required to have a permit issued by the city. No permit shall be issued unless the person satisfies the requirements in this section or as otherwise required by the article. The permit required by this section expires annually on the date established generally for the business license of the pawnshop, and each person is required to renew their permit by that date. The permit required by this section must be in the possession of the person while performing services at the pawnshop or elsewhere on behalf of the licensee and must be available for inspection. For purposes of this section "person" includes, but is not limited to, employees, the licensee and, if a corporation, its officers, directors, and shareholders and, if a partnership, its partners, as well as the designated responsible person. It is the duty of the licensee to ensure compliance with the provisions of this section.
(1)
No licensee shall allow any person to work on the premises or undertake work on behalf of the licensee unless the person has in his or her possession a current, valid city permit, and licensees are required to inspect and verify that each such person has in his or her possession a valid, current pawnshop permit.
(b)
The following must be provided in connection with seeking a permit:
(1)
A completed and signed application filed with the chief of police or his designee. The application shall, at a minimum, require the applicant's name, residential address, telephone number, email address, social security number, date of birth, and a description of the applicant's prior arrest record that shall only be used for investigative purposes and shall not give rise to a presumption or inference of guilt. The applicant must also provide proof of identify at the time of submitting the application, and only official, government-issued, photo identification is acceptable (e.g., driver's license, passport, military card, or state identification card). The city may from time to time require additional information of applicants;
(2)
A SAVE affidavit, a secure and verifiable document and immigration documents (if applicable) indicating the applicant is a U.S. citizen or otherwise qualified alien;
(3)
A signed consent form authorizing the chief of police or his designee to conduct a criminal background check on the applicant. The chief of police or his designee shall conduct a complete and exhaustive search relative to any police record of the applicant; and
(4)
Payment of an administrative fee in an amount of $25.00.
(c)
Persons are ineligible for a permit if:
(1)
They have been convicted of or pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving moral turpitude, illegal gambling, any felonies, or any misdemeanor crimes involving theft, deception or fraudulent practices within ten years preceding the date of application. For purposes of this article, a conviction or plea of guilty or nolo contendere entered under the Georgia First Offender Act, O.C.G.A. § 42-8-60 et seq., shall not be considered. Provided, however, that any such offense shall be considered and be deemed disqualifying where the applicant violated any term of probation imposed by the court granting first offender status or committed another crime and the sentencing court entered an adjudication of guilt as to the crime for which the defendant had previously been sentenced as a first offender;
(2)
They are subject to outstanding criminal warrants, criminal charges, accusations or indictments for any of the crimes enumerated in this section on which there has been no final disposition or adjudication whereupon any application involving such pending charges shall be held for a final decision until final disposition or adjudication of the charges;
(3)
For purposes of working in or on behalf of a pawnshop that deals in firearms, a person must be eligible under then prevailing state law to possess a firearm, and the person must retain eligibility throughout employment in accordance with state law as it may be amended from time to time;
(4)
If it is determined that they falsified, concealed, or misrepresented any material fact by any device, trick, or scheme while making application to the City for a permit under this section. No permit shall be deemed effective absent compliance with this provision, and a failure to comply constitutes abandonment of and voids the application and permit sought;
(5)
They have previously violated any provision of this article; or
(6)
They are a city employee who is directly involved in the licensing, permitting, and regulation of pawnshops.
(d)
In the event the applicant satisfies the criteria established in this section and may otherwise be applicable in this article, the chief of police or his designee shall notify the applicant that a permit has been issued. The applicant must make himself or herself available, within one week of notification, to the chief of police or his designee for photographing for the purpose of issuing the permit.
(e)
If the permit is denied, the chief of police or his designee shall notify the applicant in writing with a description of the reason(s) for the denial and informing the applicant of the right to appeal the denial to the city manager. Any appeal to the city manager must be presented in writing to the city clerk within ten days of the date of the written denial, and the document filed with the city clerk must provide an explanation by the applicant of the reason for the appeal. Within ten days the city manager shall inform the applicant in writing whether the denial is upheld or reversed. The decision of the city manager is final, subject to review in the Superior Court of Paulding County by way of a petition for a writ of certiorari in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 5-4-1 et seq.
(f)
Any permit issued in contravention of this section, article, or any other applicable provision of law is void and may be revoked and seized by the chief of police or his designee, subject to appeal rights as provided in subsection 12-162(e) of this article.
(g)
All permits issued hereunder remain the property of the police department and shall be produced for inspection upon the demand of any officer or designee of the city police department.
(h)
Replacement permits will be issued within 30 days of the original date, upon paying one-half of the fee charged for pawnshop permits. After 30 days of the original application date, a new application and fee must be submitted.

Sec. 12-163. - Reporting requirement for employee information.
Twice annually, during the month of June and again during the month of December, the licensee must file with the chief of police or his designee, a document identifying the name of the establishment and its address, the occupation tax certificate number, and all persons conducting or transacting business on behalf of the licensee along with each such person's date of birth, Social Security Number, residential address and telephone number, and email address.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-164. - Adding merchandise to stock.
(a)
A 51 percent majority of pawned or pledged merchandise must be maintained in inventory for sale at all times if a licensee is also selling new merchandise.
(b)
A licensee must maintain a documented, objective, and verifiable record of the retail value of all new merchandise made available for sale as well as for the pawned or pledged merchandise made available for sale. The city reserves the right to inspect these records for purposes of determining compliance.
(c)
A licensee is prohibited from operating its business on the same premises as a business that sells, deals in, exchanges, markets, or handles new goods, wares, or merchandise, and a licensee is prohibited from permitting or otherwise allowing any other business operation on the licensee's premises.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-165. - Articles not to be disposed of for 30 days after acquisition.
Any licensee who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, the word "goods" being here used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold such goods so taken in pawn or purchased for at least 30 days before disposing of such goods by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. Failure to comply with this section shall constitute an offense, punishable according to section 12-114.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-166. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful to receive in pawn, pledge or sale goods of any character or description from a minor.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-167. - Sale of knives, blackjacks and other weapons.
It shall be unlawful to sell, offer for sale or expose for sale any kind of metal knucks, dirks, sword-in-canes, spears, Bowie knives, switchblade knives, or any blackjacks or similar weapons. Violation of this section shall be punished as provided for in section 12-114.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-168. - Records and information to be maintained; display of pawnshop transaction number; identification; digital photographs; fingerprints; records storage.
(a)
In the public interest and as set forth herein, licensees shall maintain records documenting all pawnshop transactions. The records shall provide accurate descriptions of all property pledged, traded, pawned, exchanged, or sold to the pawnbroker. Such descriptions shall include, to the extent possible, the manufacturer, model, serial number, style, material, kind, color, design, number of stones if jewelry, and all other identifying names, marks, and numbers. The licensee shall assign a pawnshop transaction number documenting each transaction, and ensure each item received is tagged with the pawnshop transaction number.
(b)
The tag bearing the pawnshop transaction number must remain attached to the item until the property is disposed of by sale, trade, or other lawful means. This subsection does not apply to the purchase of property from licensed wholesale or distributor businesses for the purpose of retail sales; however the licensee shall be required to maintain all purchasing records for property exempted from this subsection.
(c)
The licensee shall require all persons pledging, trading, pawning, exchanging, or selling property to show proper identification prior to conducting a transaction. Proper identification is defined as a government issued photo identification card such as a driver's license, military identification card, state identification card, or passport.
(d)
The licensee shall also document the name, address, telephone number, race, sex, height, weight, driver's license number, date of birth, and social security number of the person pledging, trading, pawning, exchanging, or selling the property, along with the date and time of transaction, including the amount loaned/paid and any check number written for payment. This documentation shall be made at the time of the transaction.
(e)
There shall be no erasures or blanks on any of the documentation required by this section. Corrections shall be marked through with a solid line and signed by the employee or other person acting on behalf of the licensee making the correction.
(f)
The licensee is required to photograph the person pledging, trading, pawning, exchanging, or selling the property. The photograph shall be taken with a digital camera at the time of the transaction. The photograph shall clearly show a frontal view of the subject's face along with the pawnshop transaction number. The digital photograph shall be labeled and stored in such a manner that it is safe from corruption, readily identifiable, and readily available for review.
(g)
The licensee shall obtain from each person pledging, trading, pawning, exchanging, or selling any property, the fingerprint of the right hand index finger, unless such finger is missing, in which event the print of the next finger in existence on the right hand shall be obtained with a notation as to the exact fingerprinted. The fingerprint shall be imprinted onto the pawn transaction form in the designated area along with the signature of the person pawning, trading, pledging, exchanging, or selling the property. The fingerprint must be clear and legible. In the event that more than one pawn transaction form is required, a fingerprint and signature should be obtained for each form. Fingerprints and the information required herein shall be obtained each time such person pledges, trades, pawns, exchanges, or sells any property.
(h)
Items of property that appear to be new, unused, and in their original packaging cannot be accepted unless the customer can supply to the licensee at the time of the transaction a copy of the original sales receipt or other proof of purchase from the place of purchase. In lieu of the original receipt or proof of purchase, a confirmation of ownership form may be accepted at the licensee's risk. The licensee shall retain the receipt, proof of purchase, or confirmation of ownership on file.
(i)
The licensee shall store the records required in this section, including all digital images and fingerprints, for a period of four years and make them available to law enforcement personnel upon request.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
Sec. 12-169. - Daily report to police.
(a)
Contents of daily report.
(1)
Every pawnshop shall prepare a daily report in writing for the police department, in such form as may be prescribed by the police department or any designated agent, of all property that has been the subject of a pawn transaction during the 24 hours ending at 8:00 p.m. on the date of the report. Such report shall be computer generated, typewritten or printed legibly. In addition to any other information required by the police department, the report shall show:
a.
The name and address of the licensee;
b.
Time of transaction;
c.
Serial number of pawn tickets;
d.
Amount paid or advanced;
e.
Full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry, and all identifying names, marks and numbers; and
f.
A description of persons pledging, selling or pawning, including name, address, driver's license number, weight and height.
(2)
Serial numbers on items with serial numbers must be included in this report. Insufficient reports shall be rejected, and any licensee or representative of licensee making such report shall be deemed guilty of an offense punishable according to the provisions of section 1-12. The daily report shall be in such form and contain such information as may be required by the police chief or by his designated agent, and sufficient information to identify each of such articles. Any person subject to this article, any licensee or employee of a licensee, who shall fail or refuse to prepare and keep reports as provided in this section or shall make false entries concerning the transactions named, or who shall fail or refuse to permit an inspection or examination by any police officer of the reports and of the property pledged or sold to licensee shall be punished as provided for in section 1-12.
(b)
The report shall be signed and dated by the licensee, if an individual, or by the responsible person, if a corporate or other business entity. The licensee, as well as the person signing the daily report, is responsible for ensuring the completeness and accuracy of the report.
(c)
The failure of any licensee or representative of the licensee to comply with the provisions of this section shall constitute an offense punishable as provided in section 1-12.
(Ord. No. 2013-12, § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2013)
