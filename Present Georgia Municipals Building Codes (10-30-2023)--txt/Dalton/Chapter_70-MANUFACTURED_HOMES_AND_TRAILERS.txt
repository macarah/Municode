ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 70-1. - Definitions.
Terms not defined in this section shall have their customary dictionary definitions where not inconsistent with the context. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Building inspector means the duly appointed building inspector or his authorized representative of the city.
Health officer means the legally designated health authority or his authorized representative of the city-county health department.
Mobile home means a transportable, single-family dwelling unit suitable for yearround occupancy and containing the same water supply, waste disposal and electrical convenience as immobile housing.
Mobile home lot means a parcel of land designed for the exclusive use of the occupants of a single mobile home.
Mobile home park means a parcel of land which has been planned and improved for the placement of mobile homes for nontransient use.
Modular home means a factory-fabricated dwelling over 32 feet in length and at least 24 feet wide designed and constructed without carriage or hitch collar as stationary house construction for placement upon a permanent foundation, to be permanently connected to utilities, for yearround occupancy. It can consist of two or more components that can be separated when transported but designed to be jointed into one integral unit.
Permit means a written permit issued by the building inspector with the approval of the health officer, permitting the mobile home park to operate under this chapter.
Permittee means any person issued a permit to operate and maintain a mobile home park under this chapter.
Service building means a building or structure located within a mobile home park for the welfare and convenience of occupants of such mobile home park.
(Code 1983, § 13-2)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-2. - Violations.
At the discretion of the municipal judge, any person convicted of a violation of this chapter may be given a reasonable length of time to rectify or correct the violation.
(Code 1983, § 13-1)
Sec. 70-3. - Conflict with other regulations.
(a)
Whenever the sections of this chapter require a greater width or size of yards, courts or other open space or impose other restrictive standards than are required in or under any other statutes, the requirements of this chapter shall govern.
(b)
Whenever the provisions of any state statute require more restrictive standards than are required by this chapter, the provisions of such statute shall govern. However, if a subdivision for mobile homes meets other sections of this chapter, title with the restrictive covenants of this chapter shall be permitted.
(Code 1983, § 13-6)
Sec. 70-4. - Modular homes.
Modular homes shall meet the minimum standards for house construction as specified in the Standard Building Code, the FHA minimum property standards and the state factory-built housing rules and shall have a roof with at least a 3:12 pitch.
(Code 1983, § 13-5)
State Law reference— Factory-built buildings and dwelling units, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-110 et seq.
Secs. 70-5—70-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - SINGLE MOBILE HOMES

Sec. 70-31. - Conditions for permit issuance.
A permit may be issued for the occupancy of a single mobile home when the following conditions are complied with:
(1)
The home conforms with the Standard Housing Code.
(2)
The person owns the property on which the home will be located.
(3)
The home is to be used as a family unit—not to be rented.
(4)
The home is situated on its own foundation on blocks and underpinned.
(5)
It complies with the same general restrictions for other local housing.
(Code 1983, § 13-3)
Sec. 70-32. - Standards in article III.
The requirements of article III of this chapter shall apply to single mobile homes, except for any section which, by its nature, could have no such application.
(Code 1983, § 13-4)
Secs. 70-33—70-60. - Reserved.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 70-61. - Approval of plans.
No person shall construct, maintain, alter or operate a mobile home park in the city unless the plans and specifications for such work have been submitted and approved by the city-county planning commission. An existing court shall be considered as a new park under this article and upon request for a permit.
(Code 1983, § 13-21)
Sec. 70-62. - Location generally.
The minimum requirements for the location of all mobile home parks shall be that the location is:
(1)
Free from adverse influence by swamps, marshes, garbage or rubbish disposal areas, or other potential breeding places for insects or rodents.
(2)
Not subject to any source of pollution, such as drainage from garbage disposal areas.
(3)
Not subject to flooding from adjacent bodies of water or waterways.
(Code 1983, § 13-22)
Sec. 70-63. - Supervision.
The person to whom a permit for a mobile home park is issued shall at all times operate the park in compliance with this chapter and shall provide adequate supervision to maintain the park, its facilities and equipment in good repair and in a clean and sanitary condition at all times.
(Code 1983, § 13-23)
Sec. 70-64. - Registration of occupants.
Every mobile home park owner or operator shall maintain a register containing a record of all mobile homes and occupants using the mobile home park.
(Code 1983, § 13-24)
Sec. 70-65. - Compliance with technical codes.
Every mobile home in a mobile home park will be maintained in compliance with the city's zoning ordinance and with the provisions of the city's plumbing, gas, electrical and housing codes as applicable to other local housing.
(Code 1983, § 13-70)
Sec. 70-66. - Pets.
No owner or person in charge of a dog, cat or other pet animal shall permit it to commit any nuisance within the limits of any mobile home park.
(Code 1983, § 13-25)
State Law reference— Nuisances, O.C.G.A. tit. 41; nuisances in cities, O.C.G.A. § 41-2-5.
Sec. 70-67. - Suspected disease.
Every owner, operator, attendant or other person operating a mobile home park shall notify the health officer immediately of any suspected communicable or contagious disease within the mobile home park.
(Code 1983, § 13-26)
Secs. 70-68—70-95. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 70-96. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to construct, maintain or operate any mobile home park or mobile home lot within the city limits unless he holds a valid permit issued annually by the building inspector in the name of such person for the specific mobile home park or mobile home lot.
(Code 1983, § 13-66)
Sec. 70-97. - Application and issuance generally.
All applications for permits required under this division shall be made to the building inspector, who shall issue a permit upon compliance by the applicant with this chapter and the provisions of any other applicable legal requirements and upon approval of the health officer.
(Code 1983, § 13-67)
Sec. 70-98. - Contents of application.
An application for an original permit required under this division shall be in writing, signed by the applicant, and shall contain the following:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The interest of the applicant in and the location and legal description of the mobile home park.
(3)
A complete plan of the mobile home park, showing compliance with all applicable sections of this chapter.
(4)
Such further information as may be requested by the building inspector and the health officer to enable them to determine that the proposed mobile home park will comply with legal requirements.
(Code 1983, § 13-68)
Sec. 70-99. - Plan.
A complete plan, for the purpose of obtaining a permit required under this division, to be issued by the building inspector with the approval of the health officer, shall show the following:
(1)
The area and dimensions of the tract of land.
(2)
The number, location and size of all mobile home lots.
(3)
The location of service buildings and other improvements constructed or to be constructed with the mobile home park.
(Code 1983, § 13-69)
Sec. 70-100. - Transferability.
Mobile home park and mobile home lot permits are not transferable.
(Code 1983, § 13-71)
Sec. 70-101. - Renewal.
Permits issued under this division will automatically be renewed each year, provided the park management has met all requirements set forth under this chapter.
(Code 1983, § 13-72)
Secs. 70-102—70-130. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - GENERAL STANDARDS

Sec. 70-131. - Buffers.
Every mobile home park located adjacent to a residential area shall be provided with a screening buffer strip, at least five feet wide, along the property boundary line separating the park and such adjacent nonresidential use. Where effective visual barriers do not already exist along such property lines, the buffer strip shall be furnished with screening, consisting of fences, or natural growth.
(Code 1983, § 13-41)
Sec. 70-132. - Minimum area.
(a)
Park. Each mobile home park shall have an overall area to devote to park purposes of not less than 80,000 square feet. Parks in existence on the effective date of the ordinance from which this chapter derives shall be exempted from complying with this subsection.
(b)
Spaces. Each space within a mobile home park shall have an area of not less than 4,000 square feet, and lot lines shall be clearly defined or delineated.
(Code 1983, §§ 13-42, 13-43)
Sec. 70-133. - Site drainage.
(a)
The ground surface in all parts of every mobile home park shall be graded and equipped to drain all surface water in a safe, efficient manner.
(b)
Surface water collectors and other bodies of standing water capable of breeding mosquitoes and other vectors shall be treated in an approved manner.
(Code 1983, § 13-44)
Sec. 70-134. - Soil and ground cover.
Mobile home park grounds, except roadways, shall be maintained with vegetable growth, such as grass lawn, that is capable of preventing soil erosion and the emanation of dust during dry weather.
(Code 1983, § 13-45)
Sec. 70-135. - Access from public ways.
All mobile home parks shall provide safe, continuous and convenient vehicular access from abutting public streets or roads to each mobile home space. For purposes of this article, all streets or roads providing such vehicular access shall be referred to as the "park street system."
(Code 1983, § 13-46)
Sec. 70-136. - Grades of streets.
Grades of all streets in a mobile home park shall be sufficient to ensure adequate surface drainage.
(Code 1983, § 13-47)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 98.
Sec. 70-137. - Surface of streets.
All streets in mobile home parks shall be provided with a smooth, hard surface which shall be durable and well-drained under normal use and weather conditions. Street surfaces shall be maintained free of holes and other hazards.
(Code 1983, § 13-48)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 98.
Sec. 70-138. - Interior drives.
(a)
All mobile home spaces in a mobile home park shall abut upon an interior driveway. All interior driveways shall be not less than 20 feet in width and shall have unobstructed access to a public street or highway. They shall be adequately maintained and lighted by the owner.
(b)
In mobile home parks where parking is permitted on interior drives, such drives shall be in excess of 20 feet, sufficient to allow for parking cars.
(Code 1983, § 13-49)
Sec. 70-139. - Illumination.
All mobile home parks shall be furnished with sufficient electrical systems and lighting units so spaced and equipped as will provide for the safe movement of pedestrians and vehicles at night.
(Code 1983, § 13-50)
Sec. 70-140. - Pedestrian access.
All mobile home parks shall provide safe, convenient, all-season pedestrian access between individual mobile homes and all community facilities provided for park residents.
(Code 1983, § 13-51)
Sec. 70-141. - Off-street parking.
Off-street parking shall be provided in all mobile home parks where street parking is prohibited for the use of park occupants and guests. Parking bays shall be so located as to provide convenient access to mobile home spaces.
(Code 1983, § 13-52)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 98.
Sec. 70-142. - Setbacks.
All mobile homes shall be located at least 25 feet from any mobile home park property boundary line abutting upon a public street or highway and at least ten feet from other park property lines. There shall be a minimum distance of eight feet between an individual mobile home, including accessory structures attached thereto, and the adjoining pavement of a park street or common parking area or other common areas accessible to park residents.
(Code 1983, § 13-53)
Sec. 70-143. - Foundation.
Each mobile home in a mobile home park shall be placed on a masonry foundation. If the floor level of the home does not exceed 18 inches above the ground line, the foundation may consist of piers at least four inches thick and spaced no more than 48 inches on center around the perimeter of the mobile home. However, if the mobile home foundation is done in this manner, the foundation must have an exterior screen of sheetmetal, sheet plastic, pickets or some other comparable material to be approved by the building inspector. If the floor level exceeds 18 inches above the ground line, the foundation must be at least six inches thick and continuous around the perimeter of the home. Each mobile home shall be securely anchored to the masonry foundation.
(Code 1983, § 13-54)
Sec. 70-144. - Minimum distance between units.
Mobile homes in a mobile home park shall be separated from each other and from other buildings and structures by at least 20 feet. Mobile homes placed end to end may have a clearance of 15 feet, provided rear walls are staggered.
(Code 1983, § 13-55)
Sec. 70-145. - Additions.
In a mobile home park, no additions shall be permitted to mobile homes except awnings, open patios and open carports. Patios, carports and awnings shall be disregarded in determining separation between mobile homes.
(Code 1983, § 13-56)
Sec. 70-146. - Portable fire extinguishers.
Portable fire extinguishers of a type approved by the fire department shall be kept in mobile home park service buildings and at all other locations designated by the fire department and shall be maintained in good operation condition.
(Code 1983, § 13-57)
Sec. 70-147. - Recreation areas.
In all mobile home parks accommodating or designed to accommodate 25 or more mobile homes, there shall be one or more recreation areas which shall be easily accessible to all park residents. The size of such recreation areas shall be based upon a minimum of 100 square feet for each space in the park that is designed to accommodate a mobile home. No outdoor recreation area shall contain less than 2,500 square feet. Recreation areas shall be so located as to be free of traffic hazards and shall, where topography permits, be centrally located.
(Code 1983, § 13-58)
Sec. 70-148. - Community service facilities.
The requirements of this section shall apply to accessory structures and community service facilities in all mobile home parks which are required for park management and for the servicing and well-being of park residents. Such accessory structures may include but are not limited to service buildings or other community structures containing one or more of the following uses:
(1)
Park management offices, repair shops and storage.
(2)
Community sanitary facilities.
(3)
Community laundry facilities.
(4)
Indoor community recreation areas.
(5)
Commercial uses supplying essential goods or services for the exclusive use of park residents.
(Code 1983, § 13-59)
Secs. 70-149—70-175. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - INSECT AND RODENT CONTROL GENERALLY

Sec. 70-176. - Vermin or rodent harborage and infestation.
Grounds, buildings and structures of mobile home parks shall be maintained free of vermin and rodent harborage and infestation. Extermination methods and other measures to control vermin and rodents shall conform with the requirements of the health authority having jurisdiction.
(Code 1983, § 13-81)
Sec. 70-177. - Accumulation of debris.
Mobile home parks shall be maintained free of accumulations of debris which may provide rodent harborage or breeding places for flies, mosquitoes and other pests.
(Code 1983, § 13-82)
Sec. 70-178. - Storage areas.
Storage areas in mobile home parks shall be so maintained as to prevent rodent harborage; lumber, pipe and other building material shall be stored at least four inches above the ground.
(Code 1983, § 13-83)
Sec. 70-179. - Exterior openings.
Where the potential for rodent or vermin infestation exists in a mobile home park, all exterior openings in or beneath any structure shall be appropriately screened with wire mesh or other suitable materials.
(Code 1983, § 13-84)
Sec. 70-180. - Weeds, brush and grass.
The growth of brush, weeds and grass in mobile home parks shall be controlled. Parks shall be so maintained as to prevent the growth of noxious weeds considered detrimental to health. Open areas shall be maintained free of heavy undergrowth of any description.
(Code 1983, § 13-85)
Cross reference— Vegetation, ch. 122.
Secs. 70-181—70-205. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - REFUSE DISPOSAL

Sec. 70-206. - Management generally.
The storage, collection and disposal of refuse in a mobile home park shall be so managed as to create no health hazards, rodent harborage, insect breeding areas, accident or fire hazards or air pollution.
(Code 1983, § 13-94)
Sec. 70-207. - Containers.
All refuse in mobile home parks shall be stored in flytight, watertight, rodentproof containers, which shall be in a location convenient to any mobile home space. Containers shall be provided in sufficient number and capacity to properly store all refuse.
(Code 1983, § 13-95)
Sec. 70-208. - Stands.
Refuse collection stands shall be provided for all refuse containers in mobile home parks. Such container stands shall be so designed as to prevent containers from being tipped, to minimize spillage and container deterioration to facilitate cleaning around them.
(Code 1983, § 13-96)
Sec. 70-209. - Collection.
All refuse in mobile home parks shall be collected at least twice weekly. Where suitable collection service is not available from city or private agencies, the mobile home park operator shall provide this service. All refuse shall be collected and transported in covered vehicles or covered containers.
(Code 1983, § 13-97)
Sec. 70-210. - Methods of disposal.
Where city or private refuse disposal service is not available, the mobile home park operator shall dispose of the refuse by incineration, burial or transporting to an approved disposal site.
(Code 1983, § 13-98)
Sec. 70-211. - Burial.
Refuse from mobile home parks shall be buried only at locations and by methods approved by the health officer.
(Code 1983, § 13-99)
Sec. 70-212. - Incinerators.
(a)
Construction. Refuse incinerators for mobile home parks shall be constructed only with the approval of the health authority. Such approval shall be based on a review of the plans and specifications for such incinerators and approval of the site where they will be located. Such approval shall specify the type of material which may be placed in the incinerator.
(b)
Operation generally. Incinerators shall be operated only when attended by some person specifically authorized by the owner or operator of the mobile home park.
(c)
Burning of garbage. Garbage shall be burned on the premises of a mobile home park only in an approved incinerator.
(Code 1983, §§ 13-100—13-102)
Secs. 70-213—70-240. - Reserved.
DIVISION 6. - INSPECTIONS

Sec. 70-241. - Authorized generally.
The health officer and the building inspector are authorized and directed to make inspections to determine the condition of mobile home parks located within the city in order that they may perform their duty of safeguarding the health and safety of occupants of mobile home parks and of the general public.
(Code 1983, § 13-116)
Sec. 70-242. - Right of entry.
The health officer and/or building inspector shall have the power to enter at reasonable times upon any private or public property for the purpose of inspecting and investigating conditions relating to the enforcement of this chapter.
(Code 1983, § 13-117)
Sec. 70-243. - Inspection of register.
At reasonable times, the health officer and/or building inspector shall have the power to inspect the register containing a record of all mobile homes and occupants using the mobile home park.
(Code 1983, § 13-118)
Sec. 70-244. - Duty to give access to health officer and building inspector.
It shall be the duty of the owner or occupants of a mobile home park or of the person in charge thereof to give the health officer and/or building inspector free access to such premises at reasonable times for the purpose of inspection.
(Code 1983, § 13-119)
Sec. 70-245. - Duty of occupants to give access to owner.
It shall be the duty of every occupant of a mobile home park to give the owner thereof or his agent or employee access to any part of such mobile home park or its premises at reasonable times for the purpose of making such repairs or alterations as are necessary to effect compliance with this chapter or with any lawful order issued pursuant to this chapter.
(Code 1983, § 13-120)
