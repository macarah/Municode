ARTICLE II. - DOOR-TO-DOOR SOLICITATIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 82-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Solicitation activities means the practices of solicitors as listed in this section.
Solicitor means any person, corporation, partnership, association, joint stock company, trust or unincorporated organization which, through door-to-door solicitation, engages in the practice of:
(1)
Selling or taking orders for or offering to sell or take orders for goods, merchandise, wares, or other items of value for further delivery, or services to be performed in the future, for commercial purposes; or
(2)
Requesting contributions of funds, property, or anything of value or the pledge of any type of future donation or selling or offering for sale any type or property, including but not limited to goods, tickets, books, and pamphlets, for political, charitable, religious, or other noncommercial purposes.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-160))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 82-27. - Penalties.
In addition to the suspension and revocation penalties provided in section 82-62, any solicitor or individual engaged in door-to-door solicitation who violates any section of this article shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by imprisonment for a period not to exceed 20 days or both or to labor on the streets, sidewalks, or other public works for a period not exceeding 20 days.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-171))
Sec. 82-28. - Exemptions.
The following solicitors are exempt from the sections of this article pertaining to obtaining a permit, paying a fee, obtaining an identification badge, and posting a bond:
(1)
Minors under the age of 18 engaged in the solicitation of donations or the sale of any type of property for the purpose of financing extracurricular social, athletic, artistic, scientific or cultural programs, provided such solicitation activities shall have been approved by the principal of the school sponsoring such social, athletic, artistic, scientific or cultural programs.
(2)
Political candidates engaged in the solicitation of campaign contributions, provided that at the time of such solicitation each such candidate has formally qualified and is seeking election to a local, state or national elective office.
(3)
Nonprofit, tax exempt, charitable organization and churches.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-162))
Sec. 82-29. - Solicitation activities.
The following shall apply to solicitors engaged in solicitation activities in the city:
(1)
Solicitation activities shall be conducted only between the hours of 10:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m.
(2)
Solicitation activities shall not be conducted in defiance of any notice exhibited by a residence or business indicating that solicitors are not welcome or not invited. In addition, the police department shall maintain a list of addresses of residents and businesses which have notified the city that solicitors are not welcome or not invited to their residences or businesses. Solicitation activities shall not be conducted at these residences or businesses. Any solicitor may obtain a copy of this list upon reimbursing the city for photocopying expenses.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-168))
Secs. 82-30—82-55. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 82-56. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any solicitor to engage in solicitation activities in the city without first obtaining a permit from the city.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-161))
Sec. 82-57. - Application.
Application for a solicitor's permit shall be made to the city clerk one day each week during hours posted by the city clerk on an application provided by the city, which shall contain the following information:
(1)
The solicitor's name, address and telephone number. If the solicitor is a chapter or other affiliate of an organization having its principal office outside the city, the name, address, and telephone number of the parent organization;
(2)
The name, address, telephone number, date of birth, and social security number of the person who will be in charge of conducting the solicitation activities;
(3)
The name, address, telephone number, date of birth, and social security number of each individual engaging in door-to-door solicitation on behalf of the solicitor;
(4)
An outline of the methods to be used in conducting the solicitation activities;
(5)
The period within which such soliciting activity shall be conducted, including the proposed dates and times for such solicitation activities; and
(6)
A brief description of the nature, character and quality of the goods or merchandise to be offered for sale, if any.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-163))
Sec. 82-58. - Fees; duration.
Each solicitor applying for a permit under this division shall pay the city clerk a permit fee of $25.00, plus a fee of $15.00 for each individual engaging in door-to-door solicitation on behalf of the solicitor to cover the cost of the identification badge and administration. A solicitor's permit shall be valid for the period of time requested in the solicitor's application, up to 20 days.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-164))
Sec. 82-59. - Bond.
All solicitors requiring cash deposits or taking orders for cash on delivery (COD) purchases or who require a contract or agreement to finance the sale of any goods, services or merchandise for future delivery or for services to be performed in the future shall furnish to the city a cash bond in the amount of $500.00. The cash bond shall be refunded to the solicitor not later than 30 days after the expiration of the permit, provided that no claims against the solicitor have been filed prior to the date of such refund.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-165))
Sec. 82-60. - Damages.
Any person aggrieved or damaged by the action of a solicitor shall have a right of action on the bond for recovery of money deposited or damages or both.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-166))
Sec. 82-61. - Issuance; transferability; identification badge.
(a)
Upon the applicant's fulfillment of the requirements of this division and subject to compliance with this article, the police department shall issue to the applicant a solicitor's permit. No permit shall be issued to any solicitor convicted of a violation of any section of this article or whose solicitor's permit has previously been suspended or revoked. No individual convicted of a violation of any section of this article shall thereafter be permitted to engage in door-to-door solicitation within the city.
(b)
No permit shall be issued to any applicant who provides any false or misleading information in connection with the application required by this section.
(c)
No permit or badge shall be issued to any applicant for solicitation under the age of 18 or who shall have been convicted, plead guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere for any federal, state, or local law of any crime of moral turpitude, or has pending a charge involving the elements of assault, battery, theft, fraud or any civil judgment involving unethical or improper business actions, including, but not limited to, actions which would constitute fraud and deceit under the laws of this state, illegal gambling, any felony, criminal trespass, public indecency, misdemeanor involving any type of sexual related crime, any theft or violence against a person or property, any crime of possession, sale, or distribution of illegal drugs, distribution of material depicting nudity or sexual conduct as defined under state law, criminal solicitation to commit any of these listed offenses or attempts to commit any of these listed offenses.
(d)
Any permit issued under this division shall be personal to the applicant and shall not be assigned or transferred to any other person or association. Any such attempt at assignment or transfer shall render the permit void.
(e)
In addition to the permit, the city shall issue an identification badge to every individual engaging in door-to-door solicitation on behalf of the solicitor. Such person shall wear the badge in such a way that the badge may be easily read while doing business. If a badge becomes damaged or obscured, the solicitor shall return it to the city and receive another badge upon payment of the applicable fee. An identification badge shall only be used by the person to whom it is issued and may not be transferred to any other person.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-167); Ord. No. 15-15, § 1, 10-5-2015)
Sec. 82-62. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
Any permit issued under this division may be suspended or revoked for any of the following reasons:
(1)
Fraud or misrepresentation in the application for a permit;
(2)
Fraud or misrepresentation in the course of conducting solicitation activities;
(3)
Conducting solicitation activities contrary to the conditions of the permit; or
(4)
Conducting solicitation activities in such a manner as to create a public nuisance or constitute a danger to the public health, safety or welfare.
(b)
Upon suspension or revocation, the city shall deliver written notice to the permit holder stating the action taken and the reason supporting such action. The written notice shall be delivered to the address set forth in the permit holder's application.
(Ord. of 7-19-1999(1), § 1(12-169))

ARTICLE III. - STREET SOLICITATIONS[2]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 15-11, § 1, adopted Aug. 17, 2015, repealed former Art. III, §§ 82-64—82-66, in its entirety and enacted new provisions as herein set out. Former Art. III pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Ord. No. 03-23, § 1, 9-2-2003; Ord. No. 04-09, § 1, 3-15-2004.

Sec. 82-64. - Purpose and findings.
The City of Dalton has a governmental interest in public safety. The solicitation of employment, business or money in the public right-of-way from occupants of vehicles poses a significant hazard to solicitors and to motorists. The mayor and council recognize that solicitation is a form of free speech, but also find that other and safer avenues of solicitation are available to the public within the city. The mayor and council find that the hazards posed by street solicitations can only extinguished by prohibiting all such solicitation within the city.
(Ord. No. 15-11, § 1, 8-17-2015)
Sec. 82-65. - Street solicitations prohibited.
No person shall stand on any street, road, or highway within the city limits for the purpose of soliciting employment, business or contributions from any occupant of any vehicle.
(Ord. No. 15-11, § 1, 8-17-2015)
Sec. 82-66. - Penalty.
Any person who sells any goods or services or solicits for the contribution of money in violation of this Article shall upon the first violation be issued a warning. Upon the second violation and each violation thereafter, the person in violation of this article shall be fined up to $250.00 for each offense upon conviction in the Municipal Court of the City of Dalton.
(Ord. No. 15-11, § 1, 8-17-2015)

ARTICLE IV. - TRANSIENT MERCHANTS

Sec. 82-70. - Definition.
Transient merchant means any person, firm, or corporation, as principal or agent, or both, which is not a regular retail or wholesale merchant with a fixed and permanent retail place of business in the city, but rather one who displays samples and/or sells, goods, wares, merchandise or food in any temporary location, whether fixed or mobile, for immediate or future delivery. The term "transient merchant" shall not include any person, firm, or corporation which:
(a)
Sells agriculture products in their natural state which are grown or raised by the seller;
(b)
Makes house-to-house or personal calls for the purpose of displaying samples or taking orders for shipment directly from a manufacturer;
(c)
Conducts business at any industry or association trade show; or
(d)
Sells items at a rummage, garage, antique, or similar sale when such person, firm, or corporation is a resident of the city or has a permanent place of business within the city and such sale is not part of the regular business activity of such person, firm, or corporation or when the net proceeds of such sale are to be used for charitable purposes.
(e)
Sells goods, wares, merchandise or food in the downtown business district under the authority of a special events permit issued pursuant to the Code.
(Ord. No. 07-28, § 1, 10-15-2007)
Sec. 82-71. - Registration required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in business as a transient merchant without first having registered at the police department with the police chief or his or her designated representative. Each registrant shall be required to fill out a registration form giving complete identification, signature, name of employer, products sold, name of manufacturer of products, name of organization the transient merchant is representing, time of operation in the city and proposed method of operation within the city and to provide such other identification of information as may be required. Any transient merchant, before being issued a permit, shall file with the police chief proof that the applicant or the business proposed to be conducted pursuant to the license or permit, possesses, if required, a valid state sales tax number issued by the commissioner of revenue of the state and has filed with the city clerk an occupational tax return.
(Ord. No. 07-28, § 1, 10-15-2007)
Sec. 82-72. - Registration fee.
Each registrant shall pay to the police chief of the city a registration fee of $25.00 or such other amount as the mayor and council may set from time to time. Each registrant shall make application with the police chief for authorization to operate within the city as a transient merchant and, if qualified, shall be issued a permit that shall state the days that such applicant has registered to operate within the city as a transient merchant, which in any event shall not exceed seven consecutive days.
(Ord. No. 07-28, § 1, 10-15-2007)
Sec. 82-73. - Transient merchant; operating requirements.
(a)
A transient merchant shall not locate and conduct a business from a mobile vehicle or upon the public sidewalks, streets, alleys, walkways, parks, parking lots, rights-of-way, or other public lands of the city or upon state rights-of-way within the city limits.
(b)
Upon the request of the city clerk, it shall be the duty of each transient merchant, after having engaged in business in the city, to report the amount of gross sales made within the city during the period that such transient merchant was licensed and permitted to do business in the city and to furnish proof at such time that all sales taxes due through the commissioner of revenue of the state upon such sales, including the local option sales tax, have been filed and remitted to the commissioner of revenue as required by state law.
(c)
No transient merchant authorized to engage in any business in the city shall, in carrying on such business, obstruct the whole or any part of any sidewalk, street or highway in the city.
(d)
Notwithstanding anything else contained herein to the contrary, transient merchants shall comply with the following:
(1)
No merchandise, goods, or wares shall be sold within 50 feet of any right-of-way, including, but not limited to, any street or sidewalk;
(2)
In the event that a transient merchant attempts to sell on the premises of a business within the city, the transient merchant must furnish written proof of the property owner's consent for the transient merchant to engage in such sales upon the property;
(3)
If a transient merchant is going to sell merchandise, goods, foods, or wares on the premises of a business within the city, the property owner must have sufficient parking on said property to accommodate not only the existing businesses on said property, pursuant to the requirements of the zoning ordinance, but must also demonstrate that it has eight available parking spaces for the sales of the transient merchant;
(4)
The location of the transient merchant must not impede the ingress and egress of emergency vehicles onto or out of the premises on which the transient merchant's business is sited.
(5)
If the business of the transient merchant involves commercial cooking, then the transient merchant shall comply with all applicable, health, fire and environmental codes and regulations.
(6)
Any electrical power use by the transient merchant must be self-contained and not drawn from the power supply of the owner of the premises upon which the transient merchant is located.
(7)
Any signs utilized by a transient merchant shall comply with the city sign regulations;
(8)
The hours of operation for any transient merchant on the premises of a business shall be between the hours of 10:00 a.m. until 6:00 p.m.
(Ord. No. 07-28, § 1, 10-15-2007)
Sec. 82-74. - Revocation of permit.
Any permit may be revoked by the police chief or his/her designated representative because of any violation by the registrant (or any one of a group of registrants) of this article, or of any ordinance of the city, or of any state or federal law, or whenever the registrant shall cease to possess the qualifications required in this article for the original registration.
(Ord. No. 07-28, § 1, 10-15-2007)
Sec. 82-75. - Violation, penalty.
Any transient merchant who violates any provision of this article shall, in addition to a revocation of permit, shall be subject to a civil fine of not less than $200.00 and not more than $1,000.00 per violation.
(Ord. No. 07-28, § 1, 10-15-2007)
Sec. 82-76. - Enforcement.
The police chief, fire chief and building inspector, and their respective designees, are hereby authorized, empowered and directed to enforce compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 07-28, § 1, 10-15-2007)

ARTICLE V. - APPEALS

Sec. 82-80. - Appeal to public safety commission.
Decisions of the city police chief or his designee that adversely affect or aggrieve any applicant or permittee under this chapter may be appealed to the public safety commission. Any such appeal shall be by written petition filed in the office of the city clerk within seven days after the final decision by the city police chief or his designee.
(Ord. No. 11-03, § 2, 2-21-2011)
Sec. 82-81. - Hearing.
(a)
A hearing shall be conducted on each appeal no later than 45 days of the date of filing the written petition, unless a continuance of such hearing is agreed to by the appellant and the public safety commission. The appellant at such hearing shall have the right to be represented by an attorney, at the expense of the appellant, and to present evidence and cross examine witnesses.
(b)
The failure of the public safety commission to conduct a hearing as of said 45-day period or upon any continuance date shall be deemed an approval of the petitioner's application or a reinstatement of the petitioner's permit, as the case may be.
(c)
No later than 30 days of the hearing date the public safety commission shall either uphold the decision of the city police chief or his designee or reinstate the operating permit. The failure of the public safety commission to render a written decision on the petition as of 30 days of the hearing date shall be deemed an approval of the petitioner's application or a reinstatement of the petitioner's permit, as the case may be. The findings of the public safety commission shall be final unless within seven days of the date of such findings the appellant files a written petition with the city clerk that the findings of the public safety commission be reviewed by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 11-03, § 2, 2-21-2011)
Sec. 82-82. - Review by mayor and council.
The mayor and council shall perform a review of the issue no later than 30 days of the date the written petition for review was filed with the city clerk. No additional information may be submitted on the issue unless it specifically requested by the mayor and council. The mayor and council shall uphold the decision of the public safety commission, approve the application or reinstate the permit, as the case may be. The failure of the mayor and council to render a written decision on the petition as of said 30-day period shall be deemed an approval of the petitioner's application or a reinstatement of the petitioner's permit, as the case may be.
(Ord. No. 11-03, § 2, 2-21-2011)
