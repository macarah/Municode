Sec. 22-1. - Lots—Sale.
(a)
The sales of lots in a city cemetery shall be for the price and upon the terms and conditions prescribed from time to time by ordinance or resolution of the city council. Conveyance of lots shall be by special cemetery deeds signed by the city clerk. The city shall retain the right of first refusal to repurchase the lot for the current value of the lot and this right of first refusal shall be stated on the cemetery deed. The form of the cemetery deed shall be as prescribed by the city clerk from time to time. The city manager shall have the authority to exercise the city's right of first refusal to repurchase cemetery lots for the current value of the lot and the city manager is authorized to expend city funds for said repurchase and to take all actions necessary to effectuate said repurchase.
(b)
No transfer of title shall be recognized by the city unless a copy of the special cemetery transfer deed is filed with the city clerk. In instances where the lot title holder is deceased or legally incapacitated transfer must be in accordance with the laws of the state relative to transfer of title to real property. Such transfers made by heirs, devisees, guardians, executors or other legal representatives must be accompanied by an opinion of an attorney authorized to practice law in this state which states that such grantor has the legal right to convey title to the subject lot.
(Code 1987, § 15-101; Ord. No. 2002.01, 1-14-02; Ord. No. 2013-003, 2-11-13)
Sec. 22-2. - Same—Maintenance.
It shall be the duty of each owner of a burial plot or lot in a city cemetery to care for and maintain the same in a manner and conditions suitable to and approved by the city council or by the officer charged by the council with the duty of inspecting such cemetery lots.
(Code 1987, § 15-102)
Sec. 22-3. - Human burials only.
The remains of human beings only shall be buried in any cemetery maintained by the city.
(Code 1987, § 15-103)
Sec. 22-4. - Place of burials.
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury, or cause or allow to be buried, any dead person in any other place in the city than a public cemetery or a licensed private cemetery.
(Code 1987, § 15-104)
Sec. 22-5. - Obstructing walkways.
It shall be unlawful for any person to close up or obstruct any walkway between lots in any city cemetery, by erection or monuments in or across the walkway, or by putting up a wall or any other obstruction that would prohibit full passage between lots.
(Code 1987, § 15-105)
Sec. 22-6. - Removal of prohibited obstructions.
Should any walkway in a city cemetery be closed or obstructed as prohibited in section 22-5, condemnation proceedings shall be commenced against the person offending, and the monument or other obstruction shall be confiscated and removed from the walkway.
(Code 1987, § 15-106)
Sec. 22-7. - Streets; walkways.
It shall be unlawful for any person to travel upon any of the grounds of a city cemetery, except upon a street, walkway or path established and designated for the purpose.
(Code 1987, § 15-107)
Sec. 22-8. - Malicious mischief.
It shall be unlawful for any person to injure or disturb any grave or to deface, pollute or remove anything placed to mark a grave, or to destroy any wall placed around any grave or to remove any plan, post or timber or to mutilate, deface or remove any tomb, monument or gravestone, or any other structure placed in a city cemetery or willfully to disturb, destroy, cut, break, remove or injure any tree, shrub or plant that may be growing in the cemetery.
(Code 1987, § 15-108)
Sec. 22-9. - Frequenting cemetery after dark.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be on the premises of a public cemetery between the hours of sunset and the following sunrise.
(Code 1987, § 15-109)
Sec. 22-10. - Disinterments.
No person without a permit shall disinter a human body within the city limits.
(Code 1987, § 15-110)
Sec. 22-11. - Charges for lots and grave openings.
The prices for grave sites and grave openings in city cemeteries shall be fixed from time to time by city council. Nothing in this Code shall affect any ordinance fixing such prices or otherwise governing the sale of cemetery lots, and all such ordinances are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
(Code 1987, § 15-111)
Sec. 22-12. - Permit—Placing objects, landscaping, etc.
No marker, slab, coping or shrubbery shall be erected or planted, nor shall any landscaping be performed in a city cemetery, without first obtaining a permit from the city, nor shall there be erected or planted anything of any character that will in any manner create an obstruction to the general maintenance thereof, without the express written authority of the city manager.
(Code 1987, § 15-112)
Sec. 22-13. - Same—Enclosures.
It shall be unlawful for any person to enclose any lot in a cemetery without first obtaining written permission of the city, in which permit shall provide that the lot has been measured and the corners established prior to the issuing of the same.
(Code 1987, § 15-113)
Sec. 22-14. - Same—Restrictions.
Any permit issued for any of the purposes enumerated in this chapter shall be subject to and shall set forth such restrictions as the city council shall deem necessary and proper.
(Code 1987, § 15-114)
Sec. 22-15. - Coffin and vaults required.
Any remains of human beings being buried in any cemetery maintained by the city shall be placed in a coffin and the coffin shall be placed in a sealed vault.
(Ord. No. 2003.004, 5-12-03)
