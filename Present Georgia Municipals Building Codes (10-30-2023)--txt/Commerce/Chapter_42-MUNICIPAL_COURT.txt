Sec. 42-1. - Convening of municipal court.
The chief municipal court judge shall designate the date, time and the location for court to be held.
(Code 1987, § 5-102; Ord. No. 2020-009, § I, 5-18-20)
Sec. 42-2. - Compensation.
The mayor and council shall determine the compensation to be paid the judge of the municipal court.
(Code 1987, § 5-103)
Sec. 42-3. - Creation of part-time prosecuting attorney's office.
There is hereby created the office of part-time prosecuting attorney for the City of Commerce Municipal Court. The city council shall appoint such prosecuting attorneys on an as-needed basis and said attorneys shall serve at the pleasure of the city council. Such attorneys shall have the authority and duty to represent the city as more fully set out in O.C.G.A. § 15-18-96. Said attorney shall serve terms of two years commencing and ending with the first meeting of the mayor and council held in January in even-numbered years.
(Ord. No. 2012-009, 7-9-12; Ord. No. 2020-009, § II, 5-18-20)
Sec. 42-4. - Qualifications.
Attorneys appointed as prosecuting attorneys for the municipal court shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia and shall be admitted to practice before the appellate courts of this state. The prosecuting attorney may engage in the private practice of law, but shall not practice in the Commerce municipal court or appear in any matter in which the prosecuting attorney has exercised jurisdiction. At any time in which a substitute prosecuting attorney is not available or an appointment has not been made, the city attorney may serve as the substitute prosecuting attorney until such time as a prosecuting attorney or substitute prosecuting attorney is available or appointed.
(Ord. No. 2012-009, 7-9-12)
Sec. 42-5. - Compensation.
The prosecuting attorney shall be compensated as determined from time to time by the city council. The city council must approve of any employment of assistant prosecuting attorneys, employees or independent contractors by the prosecuting attorney.
(Ord. No. 2012-009, 7-9-12)
Sec. 42-6. - Oath.
The prosecuting attorney shall swear to the following oath:
I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as prosecuting attorney of the City of Commerce.
(Ord. No. 2012-009, 7-9-12)
Sec. 42-7. - Notification.
The city clerk shall forward a copy of the ordinance from which sections 42-4 through 42-7 derive to the Prosecuting Attorney's Council of the State of Georgia. The clerk of the municipal court shall notify the prosecuting attorney's council of the State of Georgia of the name of any person appointed to be the prosecuting attorney within 30 days of appointment.
(Ord. No. 2012-009, 7-9-12)
Sec. 42-8. - Creation of part-time public defender's office.
There is hereby created the office of part-time public defender for the City of Commerce Municipal Court. The city council shall appoint such public defender and said attorney shall serve at the pleasure of the city council. Said attorney shall serve terms of two years commencing and ending with the first meeting of the mayor and council held in January in even-numbered years.
(Ord. No. 2020-009, § III, 5-18-20)
Sec. 42-9. - Qualifications.
Attorneys appointed as public defender for the municipal court shall be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia. The public defender may engage in the private practice of law, but shall not practice privately in the commerce municipal court or appear in any matter in which the public defender has exercised jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 2020-009, § III, 5-18-20)
Sec. 42-10. - Compensation.
The public defender shall be compensated as determined from time to time by the city council. The city council must approve of any employment of assistant public defender, employees or independent contractors by the public defender attorney.
(Ord. No. 2020-009, § III, 5-18-20)
Sec. 42-11. - Oath.
The public defender attorney shall swear to the following oath:
"I swear (or affirm) that I will well, faithfully, and impartially and without fear, favor, or affection discharge my duties as public defender of the City of Commerce Municipal Court."
(Ord. No. 2020-009, § III, 5-18-20)
