ARTICLE V. - COMMITTEES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Secs. 2-160—2-178. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - STANDING COMMITTEES

Sec. 2-179. - Established.
The city council shall have the following standing committees:
Fire and Emergency Services;
Personnel, Finance, and Budget;
Police;
Public Properties;
Streets and Sanitation;
Utilities.
(Code 1990, § 2-146; Ord. No. 250, § 1, 1-28-1992)
Sec. 2-180. - Mayor to appoint chairperson, members.
At the first meeting of each year, the mayor shall appoint a chairperson together with such other members, if any, that the mayor desires to appoint to each standing committee, said appointments being from the members of council. Said appointment shall be for that calendar year.
(Code 1990, § 2-147; Ord. No. 250, § 2, 1-28-1992)
Sec. 2-181. - Mayor to establish other standing committees.
The mayor shall have the authority to establish such other committees and to appoint chairpersons and members as the mayor deems needed for the administration of the city.
(Code 1990, § 2-148; Ord. No. 250, § 3, 1-28-1992)
Secs. 2-182—2-200. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - ETHICS COMMITTEE

Sec. 2-201. - Composition; appointment.
The ethics committee shall consist of three persons, one appointed by the mayor, one appointed by the council, and the third appointed by the two above-named members subject to approval by a majority of the city council. The third member of the ethics committee shall be a member in good standing of the state bar of Georgia.
(Code 1990, app. D, § 30; Ord. No. 314, 11-9-1999)
Sec. 2-202. - Qualifications; terms.
All members shall be residents of the county and shall serve a two-year term.
(Code 1990, app. D, § 30; Ord. No. 314, 11-9-1999)
Secs. 2-203—2-211. - Reserved.
