ARTICLE II. - PAWNBROKERS[2]
Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Municipal authority to license and regulate pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 78-31. - Daily report to police.
Every pawnbroker shall make a daily report in writing to the police department, on such forms as may be prescribed by the chief of police, of all property pledged, traded, or bought by him during the previous 24 hours ending at 9:00 p.m. on the date of the report. Such reports shall be typewritten. In addition to any other information required by the chief of police, the reports will show:
(1)
The name and address of the pawnbroker;
(2)
Date and time of transaction;
(3)
Serial number of the pawn ticket;
(4)
Amount paid or advanced;
(5)
Full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, as well as the number of stones in jewelry, and whether the jewelry is commonly worn by a male or a female;
(6)
All identifying names, marks, and numbers; and
(7)
A description of persons selling or pawning, including name, address, date of birth, drivers license number, social security number, race, weight and height.
Insufficient reports shall be rejected, and any pawnbroker making them shall be deemed guilty of being in violation of this section.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-5(a))
Sec. 78-32. - Fingerprints, etc., required.
In addition to other records and information called for in section 78-31, the pawnbroker shall obtain, from each person pawning any article or articles, the fingerprint of the right hand index finger, unless such finger is missing, in which event the print of the next finger in existence on the right hand of the person pawning the article or articles shall be obtained with a notation as to the exact finger printed. All prints shall be made on forms furnished by the police department, and the pawnbroker shall obtain all other information called for on the form furnished. Fingerprints and the information required herein shall be obtained from all persons each time such persons pawn any article with a pawnbroker regardless of whether or not that person may have previously pawned an article with such pawnbroker and had been fingerprinted.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-5(b))
Sec. 78-33. - Photograph required.
In addition to the other records and information as called for, the pawnbroker shall photograph with 35mm color film each customer with the photograph showing the pawnbroker's ticket and/or transaction number. This photograph shall be reduced to a negative form and maintained by the pawnbroker as a permanent record for a period of four years.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-5(c))
Sec. 78-34. - Weapons.
No pawnbroker shall receive as a pledge or purchase any pistol, revolver or other firearm or weapon prohibited under state law or city ordinance. No pawnbroker shall display in the window or shop any such firearms or weapons for sale.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-6)
Sec. 78-35. - Minors.
No pawnbroker shall have any business dealings as a pawnbroker with a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker's license be issued to a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker employ a minor to assist him in his business.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-7)
Sec. 78-36. - Stolen goods.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to report to the police any article pledged with him or which is sought to be pledged with him, if he shall have reason to believe the article was stolen, or lost and found by the person attempting to pledge it in the case of a lost article.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-8)
Sec. 78-37. - Retention period.
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnbroker's license who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto. The word goods being here used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold such goods taken in pawn or purchased for at least 30 days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment, or otherwise.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-8)
Secs. 78-38—78-60. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - OCCUPATIONAL TAX CERTIFICATE

Sec. 78-61. - Required.
No person shall conduct business as a pawnbroker, or maintain an establishment for the purposes of conducting business as a pawnbroker, until an occupational tax certificate has been obtained. Such application shall be made to the city clerk and an occupational tax certificate issued only upon approval by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-1)
Sec. 78-62. - Review of application.
No action on any application under this division shall be taken until the chief of police has reviewed such application and forwarded his recommendation thereon to the council for their consideration, approval or denial.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-2)
Sec. 78-63. - Limitation on issuance.
No occupational tax certificate shall be issued under this division to any person who has been convicted of any crime involving as an element thereof the theft of property.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-3)
Sec. 78-64. - Fee.
The annual fee for each pawnbroker doing business within the city or for each individual establishment maintained for the purpose of doing business as a pawnbroker within the city shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-4)
Sec. 78-65. - Suspension, revocation.
Any person failing to comply with any provision of this article, or such other laws, ordinances and regulations as may be passed by the city for the conduct of the business of a pawnbroker, shall have his occupational tax certificate revoked. Such revocation shall result from conviction in any court for a violation of any provision of this article or any other ordinance or regulation, covering the conduct of the business.
(Code 1988, § 9-4-9)

ARTICLE III. - YARD SALES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 78-96. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Yard sale means the sale or trading of clothing, furniture, household items, food, dishes, antiques or similar goods or merchandise, other than in the normal course of business, or the sale or trading of such goods as outlined above by a person not regularly engaged in such business. Such goods or merchandise do not need to be attended for a sale to be deemed to be in existence. The term "yard sale" shall include garage sales, carport sales and similar types of sales or events.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 78-97. - Traffic and parking.
The individual in charge of the premises upon which a yard sale is conducted shall control the parking of vehicles of patrons so as to avoid congestion and hazardous conditions. Failure to do so shall be sufficient cause for the sale to be immediately terminated by officials of the city.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-4)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 94.
Sec. 78-98. - Display of merchandise.
(a)
All displays of yard sale merchandise shall be limited to that portion of the front yard area which is at least five feet back from the property line on all sides, and ten feet back from any curb or road edge.
(b)
All items displayed and/or placed for sale at a yard sale shall be removed from the sale area and placed indoors within two hours of the closing of the sale. Failure to so remove sale items shall be deemed an offense.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-6)
Sec. 78-99. - Commercial promotion of sale activity.
No person shall be permitted to engage in the business of promoting or conducting yard sales for others for a fee or other consideration. No merchandise shall be purchased in anticipation of resale at a yard sale.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-7)
Sec. 78-100. - Inspection for violation.
For the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this article, the city, its proper representatives and employees shall have the right to enter upon any premises showing evidence of a yard sale and may close the premises to such a sale where any provisions of this article are being violated.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-8)
Secs. 78-101—78-120. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 78-121. - Permit required.
At least 24 hours prior to conducting a yard sale, a permit for the yard sale shall be secured from the city clerk. Upon application for a permit, an applicant shall be given the opportunity to review the provisions of this article.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-2(a))
Sec. 78-122. - Application.
Information presented by the applicant for a yard sale permit shall include the following:
(1)
The name and address of the person conducting, operating, sponsoring, and/or promoting a sale regulated herein;
(2)
Location at which the sale is to be conducted;
(3)
The date or dates on which the sale is to be conducted; and
(4)
The time period or periods of the day during which it is proposed to carry on the sale.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-2(b))
Sec. 78-123. - Issuance, denial.
If information submitted by the applicant indicates that the proposed yard sale activity will be in compliance with the provisions of this article, a permit for the yard sale shall be issued without charge by the city. If it is determined by the clerk that a permit should not be issued, that determination shall be appealable to the mayor and city council.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-2(c))
Sec. 78-124. - Inspection.
All permits issued under this division shall be available for official inspection at any and all times during the permitted sale.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-2(c))
Sec. 78-125. - Duration, location.
Each permit issued under this division shall be valid only for the time and place specified in the application for such permit.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-2(c))
Sec. 78-126. - Frequency of sales.
Yard sales shall be allowed for not more than a total of eight days in any calendar year. Permits shall be issued in two consecutive day increments only; however, no sale shall be conducted on Sunday.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-3)
Sec. 78-127. - Hours of sales.
Yard sales shall be limited to the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m. on Mondays through Saturdays only.
(Code 1988, § 9-5-3)
