ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2; departmental organization, § 2-4.

Sec. 58-31. - Composition.
The police department shall consist of those officers and employees of those ranks and positions as approved by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1988, § 4-1-1)
Sec. 58-32. - Responsibilities.
The police department shall be responsible for patrol, traffic control, investigation of accidents, investigation of crimes, apprehension of offenders, court appearances, security of business establishments and for any other matters of public safety and law enforcement as directed by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1988, § 4-1-2)
Sec. 58-33. - Chief of police; duties generally.
The chief of police or his sworn, certified designees of the city police shall be in charge of all prisoners convicted in the municipal court. It shall be the duty of the chief of police, or the chief's designees to turn all moneys received by them over to the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 2008-03, art. I, 6-10-2008)
Sec. 58-34. - Assistance to police officers.
Any person, resident of the state, summoned, either in writing or verbally, by a police officer to aid in arresting or imprisoning any person shall be subject to the same protection and consequences as official acts.
ARTICLE III. - CITY MARSHAL

Sec. 58-35. - City marshal designated.
There shall be created the position of city marshal. The city marshal shall be appointed from time to time by the code enforcement officer serving as outlined in chapter 18, article I, section 18-1. The city marshal shall at all times report directly to the code enforcement officer. Any person serving as city marshal shall possess the following minimum qualifications:
(1)
A high school diploma or GED;
(2)
Possess and maintain a valid Georgia driver's license;
(3)
Be over the age of 21;
(4)
Have no felony convictions or sufficient misdemeanors to establish a pattern of disregard for the law;
(5)
Have no felony or misdemeanor convictions while employed by the city;
(6)
Successfully met and completed Georgia Peace Officers Standards and Training;
(7)
Obtained and continue to maintain P.O.S.T. Certification;
(8)
[Have the] ability to meet and maintain compliance with present and future Georgia P.O.S.T.; and
(9)
Possess any other additional minimum qualifications as designated by the code enforcement officer as he or she deems appropriate for the position of city marshal.
(Ord. No. 2008-03, art. I, 6-10-2008)
Sec. 58-36. - City marshal; scope of authority and duties.
The city marshal shall be vested with authority to enforce all provisions of this Code of Ordinances. Specifically, the city marshal shall possess the powers to issue citations, investigate violations, arrest violators, and serve all orders, citations, summonses, subpoenas, or other process issued by the authority of the city, and such other provisions as may be delegated by the code enforcement officer for the violation of, or failure to comply, with this Code of Ordinances, the requirements thereof or any order or direction made pursuant to the provisions contained in any section of this Code of Ordinances which shall not have been complied with. The powers vested in the city marshal hereunder shall in no way limit the powers of the chief of police or his or her designees as listed in this chapter, article II.
(Ord. No. 2008-03, art. I, 6-10-2008)
