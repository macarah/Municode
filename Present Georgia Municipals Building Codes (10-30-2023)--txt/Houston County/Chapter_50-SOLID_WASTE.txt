ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning. The word "shall" is mandatory, and the word "may" is permissive.
Building material means any material such as lumber, roofing material, brick, concrete block, plaster, gutters, sand, gravel, carpet, padding, pallets, or other substances used in repairs or alteration of existing buildings, construction of new buildings, or demolition of existing structures.
Bulk container means a metal container not less than four cubic yards in capacity, and not larger than eight cubic yards in capacity, with a lid opening on top and constructed so that it can be emptied mechanically by specially equipped trucks used by the county landfill, placed in county-designated areas, for the disposal of household trash only.
Compactor site means a container of approximately 42 cubic yards in capacity, constructed by the county, for the disposal and compaction of county household trash only.
Director means the county engineer or his designee, who shall be the principal officer responsible for the enforcement of this chapter.
Garbage means the byproducts of animal or vegetable matter resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking and consumption of food, or other matter which is subject to decomposition, decay, putrefaction or the generation of noxious or offensive gases or odors, or which during or after decay may serve as a breeding area or feeding material for flies, insects or animals. Garbage shall also include putrescible wastes which are capable of being quickly decomposed by microorganisms. Examples of putrescible wastes include but are not necessarily limited to kitchen waste, animal manure, offal, and hatchery and poultry processing plant wastes.
Hazardous refuse means materials such as poisons, acids, caustic matter or solutions, chemicals, infected materials, offal, fecal matter, explosives, sewage sludge, radioactive materials and highly flammable substances.
Household trash means every waste accumulation of paper, sweepings, dust, rags, bottles, cans or other matter of any kind, other than garbage, which is usually attendant to housekeeping.
Industrial waste means all waste, including solids, semisolids, sludges and liquids, created by factories, processing plants or other manufacturing enterprises.
Junked vehicle includes any wrecked or inoperable automobile, truck or other vehicle, or any vehicle which does not bear a current license plate or a current inspection sticker.
Landfill means a disposal site where solid waste, other than putrescible waste or hazardous waste, is disposed of using landfilling techniques.
Landfilling means a method of disposing of solid waste, except putrescible waste or hazardous waste, on land, by placing an earth cover thereon.
Litter means any garbage, refuse, waste materials, sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, tin cans, trash, debris, dead animals or other discarded, used or unconsumed substance which is not handled in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
Loading and unloading area means a space or area used by any moving vehicle for the purpose of receiving, shipping and transporting goods, wares, commodities and persons.
Noncompliance bulk waste means construction debris, carpet and padding, shingles, gutters, siding, pallets, concrete blocks, concrete, bricks, dead animals, hazardous waste, fuel, oil tanks, or car parts. Materials from evictions, foreclosures, unoccupied properties, home renovations/remodeling. Any such materials found in the right-of-way and is removed by county, will be billed to the property owner.
Noncompliance yard waste means any yard waste which includes: (1) Plastic planting tubs, lumber or landscape timbers, wooden fencing, pallets, or posts. (2) Tree limbs not cut smaller than five feet in length, larger than eight inches in diameter and weighs heavier than 40 pounds. (3) Loose leaves, grass clippings, pine straw, pinecones, small twigs, sticks, shrubbery clippings and other small items not in a bag. (4) Any shrubbery pulled out with the root ball still attached to the shrubbery. (5) Rocks, dirt, and gravel. (6) Waste from commercial tree removal and commercial landscaping (multi-family, industrial, institutional, or other similar facilities are required to make private arrangements to dispose of the yard waste). (7) Waste from land clearing resulting from excavating, bulldozing, cutting trees, and site preparation for building.
Any such materials found in the right-of-way and removed by county, will be billed to the property owner.
Reclamation means a controlled method of sorting and storing solid waste for future use in accordance with an approved procedure.
Recycling means the process by which reclaimed resources are transformed into new products in such a manner that the original products may lose their identity.
Rubbish includes wastepaper, cartons, boxes, wood, tree branches, yard trimmings, furniture, appliances, metal, cans, glass, packing material and similar material.
Sanitary landfill means a disposal site where solid wastes are disposed of using sanitary landfilling techniques.
Scavenge and scavenging mean unauthorized or uncontrolled retrieval of discarded refuse materials.
Small dead animals means cats, dogs, small household pets and other animals of similar size having a weight of less than 50 pounds.
Solid waste means putrescible waste, except water-carried body waste, and shall include garbage, rubbish (paper, cartons, boxes, wood, tree branches, yard trimmings, furniture, appliances, metal, tin cans, glass, crockery and dunnage), ashes, street refuse, dead animals, sewage sludges, animal manures, industrial waste (waste materials from industrial operations), residue from incineration, food processing wastes, demolition wastes, abandoned automobiles, dredging wastes, construction waste and any other waste material in a solid or semisolid state not otherwise defined in this section.
Tree and shrubbery trimmings means waste accumulations of tree branches, tree limbs, tree stumps, parts of trees, bushes, shrubbery cuttings or clippings or other natural waste.
(Code 1976, § 4-2001; Amd. of 10-20-20)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 50-2. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Any person violating this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Code 1976, § 4-2023(e))
Sec. 50-3. - Enforcement of chapter.
(a)
The county sanitarian, the county marshal, or the director or inspectors designated by him shall investigate complaints or violations of any provisions of this chapter. They shall determine if there has been a violation and initiate an attempt to obtain compliance with this chapter by the person in violation thereof.
(b)
The director or his designated inspectors, the county sanitarian or the county marshal shall be authorized to sign a complaint or issue a summons against the violator.
(c)
In case of any violation of this chapter, the director, with the approval of the county attorney, in addition to any other remedies, may institute injunction, mandamus or other appropriate proceedings to prevent such violation, or to correct or abate such violation.
(d)
Upon appropriate notice to the person violating this chapter, the director may suspend any service or the use of any facility maintained by the board of commissioners during the period such violation continues to exist.
(Code 1976, § 4-2023(a)—(d))
Sec. 50-4. - Authority of director of county landfill.
The collection, transportation and disposal of solid waste shall be under the supervision and control of the director of the county landfill. In the performance of his duties, the director shall have the authority to establish routes and days of collection and such other matters as may pertain to the collection, transportation and disposal of solid waste as he may deem necessary and reasonable.
(Code 1976, § 4-2002)
Sec. 50-5. - Operating hours for county landfill.
The operating hours of the county landfill shall be from 7:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday to Saturday. The landfill shall be closed on Sundays.
(Code 1976, § 4-2004; Min. of 12-7-93; Amd. of 10-20-20)
Sec. 50-6. - Use of public disposal sites.
(a)
Use required; exceptions. All domestic and other acceptable solid waste shall be delivered and deposited at authorized public disposal sites in accordance with the directions and orders of the director or his designee in charge of operations; except, however, privately operated sites may be used for the sanitary disposal of industrial waste or other specified waste upon the written approval of the director and the board of commissioners, upon the issuance of appropriate disposal permits as required by the regulations of the state department of natural resources and the federal Environmental Protection Agency.
(b)
Designation of sites; use regulations.
(1)
Public sites approved for the disposal of refuse shall be identified by appropriate directional signs posted near the roadside and at the location of the county sanitary landfill.
(2)
Such sites shall be maintained in use until permanently closed, at which time additional authorized sites shall be opened and publicized by posting and through public advertisements.
(3)
The board of commissioners may restrict certain sites or portions thereof to specific types of refuse. Only household trash shall be disposed of in compactor sites and bulk container sites.
(4)
Commercial collectors shall only use the county landfill.
(5)
Residents of the county, other than commercial collectors, may use public compactor sites and bulk container sites without charge, in accordance with the regulations for the particular site and under the instruction of the site attendant.
(6)
No refuse generated outside the county will be accepted at any disposal site.
(c)
Ownership of waste. All materials delivered and deposited for disposal in a disposal site shall immediately become the property of the county.
(Code 1976, §§ 4-2015—4-2017)
Sec. 50-7. - Disposal of certain materials at county landfill prohibited.
Hazardous or liquid waste or highly combustible industrial waste shall not be disposed of in the county landfill.
(Code 1976, § 4-2009)
Sec. 50-8. - Private disposal sites.
(a)
Location, design and operation.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit or permit to be deposited, on land under his ownership and control, any refuse without first having obtained a disposal area permit as required by the regulations of the state department of natural resources, the Federal Environmental Protection Agency and the board of commissioners.
(2)
Any person operating a private landfill site of any kind shall be required to comply with the following provisions:
a.
These facilities shall not be permitted within:
1.
Five hundred feet of the nearest residence.
2.
One-half mile of a private well.
3.
Two miles of a public well or water supply.
4.
Ten thousand feet of an airport.
b.
Traffic routes, entrances and exits to the facility shall be approved by the county engineer.
c.
All facilities shall have an operator in attendance at all times when the landfill is in use, and the landfill must be barricaded when closed to the public.
d.
All facilities permitted after January 1, 1987, shall be enclosed with a security fence at least six feet in height, consisting of wire of not more than a two-inch mesh, or some other similar fencing material or device approved by the county engineer.
e.
Any changes in the normal drainage of the property upon which the facility is located shall be accommodated by storm sewers or other appropriate drainage structures as necessary to properly care for drainage. These stormwater structures shall be approved by the county engineer prior to their installation and shall be installed at the expense of the user. Design of these structures shall be by a registered professional engineer.
f.
These facilities shall be approved or disapproved by the board of commissioners. Certification by a registered engineer that the site meets state standards shall be submitted to the board of commissioners prior to the approval of the permit. A state permit, a county license and written approval of the county engineer shall be required before any fill operation begins.
g.
The operator shall permit to be placed in the facility only those materials which are authorized by appropriate permits. Specifically, and without limiting the provisions of this subsection, there shall be no disposition in any facility of solid waste or nonsolid waste which would be classified as hazardous material.
h.
Nonsolid waste shall not be disposed of in the facility.
i.
All waste deposited or disposed of in nonputrescible landfills must be covered with a minimum six-inch thickness of soil once per week.
j.
The operator of the facility shall obtain and maintain in force a performance bond, payable to the county, conditioned upon the operation of the facility in accordance with the terms of this Code and all state and federal regulations applicable thereto. The bond shall be set at $10,000.00 per acre of property zoned for a landfill, with a minimum bond of $250,000.00. Evidence of compliance with the bond requirement shall be submitted to the county attorney prior to approval by the board of commissioners.
k.
The operator of the facility shall obtain and maintain records which will reflect, as a minimum, the date and hour of the deposit of each load of solid waste, the name and address of the owner of the truck in which the waste was delivered, and a general description of the waste which is deposited.
l.
Every operator shall make the facility and the records maintained in connection therewith available for inspection at reasonable times by the county engineer or other person designated by the governing authority of the county so as to enable the county to determine whether the facility is being operated properly.
(3)
In addition to other remedies, if the county shall determine that the facility is being operated in violation of provisions of this section, and the violations are not corrected in a reasonable time thereof, the county may order the facility closed or its operation suspended. In default of compliance with such order, the county may seek an injunction to restrain further operations either on a permanent basis or until violations of this section are corrected.
(b)
Maintenance. It shall be the duty and responsibility of the owner of any private disposal site to keep the site in an orderly condition and maintained so as not to be a public nuisance or a menace to public health.
(c)
Inspection. The director or his designated representative shall have the right to enter a private disposal site at any time during normal business hours for the purpose of inspecting the site to determine whether or not the site is in compliance with this chapter and all other pertinent laws and regulations of the county. The inspections shall not in any way constitute approval of the operation therein, and shall not relieve or release the owner or operator of the liability for operating the facility in accordance with applicable county, state or federal regulations.
(Code 1976, §§ 4-2018—4-2020)
Sec. 50-9. - Permits and licenses for refuse collectors.
It shall be unlawful for any person to collect refuse within the unincorporated area of the county, except from his own residence, business or industrial plant, without first having obtained a proper permit from the state department of natural resources and the county and without first having obtained a county business license.
(Code 1976, § 4-2003)
Sec. 50-10. - Cover for refuse collection vehicles.
All persons who desire to haul refuse over roads which are a part of the county road system shall use a vehicle provided with a cover and operate it so as to prevent refuse from being dropped, blown or spilled therefrom.
(Code 1976, § 4-2013)
Sec. 50-11. - Property maintenance generally.
All owners or occupants of property shall maintain their property in a litter-free condition.
(Code 1976, § 4-2014(a))
Sec. 50-12. - Storage of refuse or junk.
Refrigerators and other airtight containers. It shall be unlawful for any person to leave outside any building in a place accessible to children any appliance, refrigerator or other container which has an airtight snap lock or similar device without first removing therefrom the lock or door. This subsection shall not apply to any appliance, refrigerator or container which has been placed on or adjacent to the rear of the building and which has been crated, strapped or locked so that it will be impossible for a child to obtain access to any compartment thereof.
(Code 1976, § 4-2005(2)—(4); Ord. of 8-20-96)
Sec. 50-13. - Blocking storm drain or drainage ditch.
No person shall place any refuse, trash or refuse receptacle or container on, over or near any storm drain or drainage ditch, or so close thereto as to cause such material to interfere in any way with such drainage.
(Code 1976, § 4-2005(1))
Sec. 50-14. - Sweeping litter into street or sidewalk.
No person shall sweep into or deposit in any street or sidewalk the accumulation of litter from any building or property.
(Code 1976, § 4-2014(b))
Sec. 50-15. - Scavenging.
No person other than the owner thereof, or an agent or employee of the director, shall disturb or interfere with any container used for the purpose of storing refuse pending its collection, or remove any contents therefrom or remove such container from its location.
(Code 1976, § 4-2005(5))
Sec. 50-16. - Littering.
It shall be unlawful for any person to:
(1)
Throw or deposit any solid waste on any public or private street or to scatter such solid waste or litter on any public or private property.
(2)
Throw or deposit any solid waste, trash or debris in any marsh area, stream, drainage ditch, body of water or beach area.
(Code 1976, § 4-2005(6))
Sec. 50-17. - Transport of loose materials.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle transporting loose materials on any county roadway to transport the materials without suitable covers securely fastened to the vehicle. This section shall not apply to the transportation of poultry, livestock, or silage or other feed grain used in the feeding of poultry or livestock.
(Code 1976, § 4-2005(7))
Sec. 50-18. - Disposal of industrial waste.
In accordance with the regulations of the state department of natural resources and the federal Environmental Protection Agency, industrial waste shall be collected, removed and disposed of by any factory, plant or enterprise creating or causing such waste.
(Code 1976, § 4-2006)
Sec. 50-19. - Disposal of hazardous waste.
No hazardous waste shall be placed in any receptacle used for collection of solid waste by the county landfill. Any hazardous waste should be disposed of in a manner consistent with the regulations of the state department of natural resources and the federal Environmental Protection Agency.
(Code 1976, § 4-2007)
Sec. 50-20. - Disposal of waste building material—Generally.
(a)
The director shall not be responsible for the collecting or hauling of building material originating from private property preliminary to, during or subsequent to the construction of a new building or alternations or additions to an existing building, of whatever type, or from demolition of existing structures. All construction site operators and/or owners are required to control waste such as discarded building materials, concrete truck washout, chemicals, litter and sanitary waste at construction sites that may cause adverse impacts to water quality. Such material shall be removed by the owner of the property or by the contractor, and disposed of only at the county landfill.
(b)
Building materials as described in subsection (a) of this section shall be dumped only in the county landfill at the same rate charged for commercial haulers.
(c)
The dumping of asbestos and asbestos-containing debris is allowed in the county landfill under the following circumstances:
(1)
All deliveries of asbestos debris must be scheduled a minimum of one day in advance.
(2)
All deliveries of asbestos debris must be double-bagged in heavy duty polyurethane bags in accordance with the requirements of the Environmental Protection Division.
(3)
The director of the county landfill has the authority to cut off the dumping of asbestos debris at any time should he feel the quantity is not manageable.
(4)
The fee required for handling asbestos debris shall be $30.00 per ton for in-county asbestos and $40.00 per ton for out-of-county asbestos.
(Code 1976, § 4-2008; Amd. of 9-16-14(1))
Sec. 50-21. - Same—Burial or burning.
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury any accumulation of building materials on property other than that located within the county landfill. Burning excess and scrap materials is permissible, provided that any and all residue therefrom is gathered and properly disposed of at the county landfill.
(Code 1976, § 4-2005(8))
Sec. 50-22. - Disposal of waste contaminated by contagious disease.
The clothing, bedding or other refuse from places where highly infectious diseases have prevailed shall be decontaminated prior to removal under the supervision and direction of the county health department. Such refuse shall not be placed in compactor sites. Such materials shall only be disposed of at the county landfill and only after they have been decontaminated.
(Code 1976, § 4-2010)
Sec. 50-23. - Disposal of hypodermic needles.
No person shall dispose of or discard any hypodermic syringe or hypodermic needle or any other instrument or device for making hypodermic injections before first breaking, disassembling, destroying or otherwise rendering inoperable and incapable of reuse such hypodermic syringe, needle, instrument or device and without safeguarding the disposal thereof by wrapping or securing the device in a suitable manner so as to avoid the possibility of causing injury to the collection personnel.
(Code 1976, § 4-2011)
Sec. 50-24. - Compaction of cardboard boxes and cartons.
Prior to depositing refuse for collection in authorized containers or receptacles or in commercial containers, the person disposing of any such boxes or cartons or the person in charge of the premises shall collapse all cardboard boxes and cartons.
(Code 1976, § 4-2012)
Sec. 50-25. - Disposal of personal property after the issuance of a writ of possession.
Upon the issuance of a writ of possession pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 44-7-55(c), the owner of the premises subject to the writ shall cause any personal property of the tenant removed from the premises to be placed in a container for pick up. The owner shall not violate any other provisions of this Code as to the location of the container.
If the owner does not have a utility account at the premises subject to the writ with the county utility department, it shall be the responsibility of the owner to transport or have transported to the county landfill or some other permitted landfill any personal property containerized after the execution of the write, within seven days of being put out for pick up.
The county or its agent will not transport any items it would not transport pursuant to other provisions of this Code, including, but not limited to, construction and demolition materials.
(Amd. of 10-20-09; Amd. of 9-7-10)
Secs. 50-26—50-35. - Reserved.

Sec. 50-36. - Application.
This article applies to the preparation, storage, collection, transportation, and disposal of all residential solid waste in the area under the jurisdiction of the county within the unincorporated areas of the county. It prescribes rules and regulations therefore, regulates the private collection of solid waste, and prohibits the dumping of solid waste within the district.

Sec. 50-37. - Definitions.
For purposes of this article, the following terms shall be defined as follows:
Agreement. This agreement, including exhibits and any amendments thereto, agreed to by the county and the contractor during the term of the agreement.
Bags. Plastic sacks furnished by customer designed to store refuse with sufficient wall strength to maintain physical integrity when lifted by top. Total weight of bag and its contents shall not exceed 50 lbs.
Bulky waste. White goods (stoves, freezers, dishwashers, trash compactors, refrigerators, water tanks, washing machines, dryers, water heaters, air conditioners), furniture and other waste materials not included: (construction debris, dead animals, hazardous waste or stable matter) with weights or volumes greater than those allowed for containers. (This item does not include yard waste.)
Bundle. Newspapers and magazines securely tied together forming an easily handled package not exceeding 50 lbs. in weight. (May be bagged or otherwise containerized in lieu of being tied together.)
Collection equipment. Equipment used on this contract which shall not exceed seven years in age.
Commodity. Material that can be sold in a spot or future market for processing and use or reuse. Each commodity shall retain its own identity and be kept separate.
Commodity buyer. A buyer or processor of material described in this section.
Construction debris. Waste building materials resulting from construction, remodeling, repair, or demolition operations. Waste building materials include but are not limited to carpet, carpet padding, lumber, fencing, pallets, shingles, gutters, siding, concrete blocks, concrete, bricks.
Container—Solid waste (or roll-out solid waste container). A roll-out receptacle with a capacity of 90 to 100 gallons constructed of plastic, metal, or fiberglass, having handles of adequate strength for lifting and having a tight fitting lid capable of preventing entrance into the container by vectors. The mouth of a container shall have an opening greater than or equal to that of the base. The containers shall be dark green in color bearing the county's name.
Container—Recyclable materials. A receptacle with a capacity of 18 gallons designed for the purpose of curbside collection of recyclable materials. The receptacle shall be one made of hard plastic and shall be dark green in color.
Contract documents. The request for proposals, instructions to contractors, contractor's proposal, general specifications, the contract performance bond and any addenda or changes to the foregoing documents agreed to by the county and the contractor.
Contractor. The person, corporation, or partnership performing or proposing to perform refuse collection and disposal under contract with the county.
County. County of Houston, Georgia or Houston County, Georgia.
Dead animals. Animals or portions thereof that have expired from any cause, except those slaughtered or killed for commercial or human use.
Disposal site. A refuse depository including but not limited to municipal solid waste landfills, construction/demolition landfills, transfer stations, incinerators, and waste processing/separation centers licensed, permitted or approved by all governmental bodies and agencies having jurisdiction and requiring such licenses, permits, or approvals to receive for processing or final disposal of refuse and dead animals. The disposal site is the Houston County MSW Landfill or Houston County Construction/Demolition Landfill near Kathleen, Georgia. All waste shall be disposed of at this facility by the contractor.
Garbage or putrescible waste. Any and all dead animals of less than 20 lbs. in weight, except those slaughtered for human consumption; every accumulation of waste (animal, vegetable, and/or other matter) that results from the preparation, processing, consumption, dealing in, handling, packing, canning, storage, transportation, decay or decomposition of meats, fish, fowl, birds, fruits, grains, or other animal or vegetable matter (including, but not by way of limitation, used tin cans, and other food containers; and all putrescible or easily decomposable waste animal or vegetable matter which is likely to attract flies or rodents); except (in all cases) any matter included in the definition of bulky waste, construction debris, dead animals, hazardous waste, rubbish, yard waste, or stable matter.
Hazardous waste. Waste, in any amount, which is defined, characterized or listed as hazardous by the United States Environmental Protection Agency or appropriate state agency by or pursuant to federal or state law, or waste, in any amount, which is regulated under federal or state law. The term "hazardous waste" shall also include lead acid batteries, acid, fertilizer, bulk insecticides, motor oil, gasoline, and liquid paint.
Producer. An occupant of a residential unit who generates refuse.
Recyclable material(s). Newsprint; aluminum and bi-metal cans; HDPE clear plastic milk jugs, and PET plastic soft drink containers.
Refuse or residential refuse. This term shall refer to residential refuse, rubbish, and bulky waste, construction debris and stable matter generated at a residential unit unless the context otherwise required.
Residential unit. A dwelling within the unincorporated area of Houston County occupied by a person or group of persons comprising not more than four families. A residential unit may be deemed occupied by the county when either water or domestic light and power services are being supplied thereto. A condominium dwelling, whether of single or multi-level construction, consisting of four or less contiguous or separate single-family dwelling units, shall be treated as a residential unit, except that each single-family dwelling within any such residential unit shall be billed separately as a residential unit.
Rubbish. All waste wood, wood products, chips, shavings, sawdust, printed matter, paper, pasteboard, rags, straw, used and discarded mattresses, used and discarded clothing, used and discarded shoes and boots, combustible waste pulp and other products such as are used for packaging or wrapping crockery and glass, ashes, cinders, floor sweepings, glass, mineral or metallic substances, and any and all other waste materials not included in the definition of bulky waste, construction debris, dead animals, garbage, hazardous waste or stable matter.
Stable matter. All manure and other waste matter normally accumulated in or about a stable, or any animal, livestock, or poultry enclosure, and resulting from the keeping of animals, poultry, or livestock.
Yard waste. Yard waste means leaves, brush, grass, clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping maintenance other than mining, agricultural, and silva cultural operations. Yard waste may be bundled, stacked, or bagged. The maximum size on yard waste will be limbs less than eight inches in diameter and five feet long. This item does not include land clearing debris.
White goods. White goods (freezers, dishwashers, trash compactors, refrigerator, stoves, water tanks, water heaters, washers, dryers, and similar items).
(Amd. of 4-5-05; Amd. of 10-20-20)
Sec. 50-38. - Findings.
The board of commissioners hereby finds that the public health of the citizens of district described in section 54-4 would be adversely affected should individual citizens be allowed to dispose of solid waste upon their own private property. The board of commissioners finds that it would be in the public interest for the board to pass an ordinance further regulating garbage and solid waste collection and disposal in the district.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-39. - Boundaries, qualified private company.
The boundaries of the district shall be all of the property situate, lying and being in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-40. - Use of receipts.
All fees imposed herein below within the district shall be used and appropriated to pay the cost of collection, storage an/or disposal service within the district, and the necessary related expenses associated therewith by the county, franchisee, contractor collector, or county's designee, including, but not limited to, the purchase of solid waste collection, storage, and/or disposal vehicles, trailers and other equipment; the construction and operation of solid waste collection, storage, and/or disposal collection and/or transfer stations; transportation costs to the disposal site and/or transfer site; tipping fees and disposal charges; and such other costs and expenses as may be deemed needed from time to time by the board of commissioners in order to collect, store and/or dispose of solid waste generated within the district.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-41. - Preparation and storage generally.
It shall be the duty of both the occupant and owner of every residential unit located in the district to keep all solid waste generated by each such residential unit, pending collection and disposal by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee, as follows:
(1)
Solid waste shall be free from excessive liquid and placed in plastic bags, containers, or bundles or roll-out solid waste container. One 90—100 gallon roll-out solid waste container shall be provided to each residential unit by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee for use by that residential unit only, for solid waste storage and disposal purposes.
(2)
Solid waste shall be placed in the roll-out solid waste container provided. All other waste must be in bags, bundles, or other containers. Debris placed in bags should be tightly cinched so items do not fall out. Such containers must be kept clean by the residential unit. All materials other than the roll-out solid waste container shall be placed alongside that container.
(3)
Solid waste shall be kept in the roll-out container and shall not be removed or scattered by anyone on public or private property.
(4)
Bulk waste to be disposed of shall also be placed neatly alongside the wheeled container and must be segregated and not mingled with other items placed for removal. All items shall be placed at the curbside by 7:00 a.m. on the regular collection day or on the next collection day if the regular collection day falls on a holiday. Exceptions to collection hours shall be effective if the county or the contract collector, or county's designee, determines that an exception is necessary in order to complete collection on an existing collection route due to inclement weather or unusual circumstances.
(5)
Solid waste shall be free from all noncompliance bulk waste items.
(6)
Solid waste shall be free from all noncompliant solid waste items. Any noncompliant solid waste items placed in the right-of-way and removed by the county or the contract collector, or county's designee, the county shall have the right to charge a removal fee, plus landfill cost to the solid waste account holder or the property owner a disposal fee for removal of debris.
(7)
Yard waste for collection must be cut smaller than five feet in length, no more than eight inches in diameter and must not weigh more than 40 lbs. Loose leaves, grass clippings, pine straw, pinecones, small twigs, sticks, shrubbery clippings and other small items shall be placed in containers, bags, and bundles. Debris placed in bags should be tightly cinched so items do not fall out and must be segregated not mingling with other items placed for removal. A pile less than ten cubic yards qualifies for curbside collection. All items shall be placed at the curbside by 7:00 a.m. on the regular collection day or on the next collection day if the regular collection day falls on a holiday. Exceptions to collection hours shall be effective if the county or the contract collector, or a county designee, determines that an exception is necessary in order to complete collection on an existing collection route due to inclement weather or unusual circumstances.
(8)
Solid waste shall be free from all noncompliant yard waste. Any work performed or hired by a for hire professional contractor will not be collected under residential collection service. Material under agriculture practices and or farming will not be considered for residential collection. A pile over ten cubic yards does not qualify for curbside collection.
(Amd. of 4-5-05; Amd. of 10-20-20)
Sec. 50-42. - Collection of refuse generally.
(a)
The county shall have the exclusive right to collect and dispose of all residential solid waste within the district either through employees of the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee issued to a person engaged in the business of residential solid waste collection and disposal. No person shall collect or dispose of any residential solid waste within the district except as provided for herein and except with the express permission of the county. Any waste carried directly to the Houston County Landfill must be tarped or otherwise covered to prevent litter and debris from leaving the vehicle.
(b)
The county, franchisee, contractor collector, or county's designee will collect solid waste within the district under the following conditions:
(1)
Garbage, yard waste, and bulky waste will be collected once each week from residential unit. Recyclable materials shall be collected from each residential unit two times per month on regularly scheduled days.
(2)
The county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee may decline to collect:
a.
Garbage and rubbish contained in containers larger than 100 gallons;
b.
Any containers that contain excessive liquid; or
c.
Any garbage or rubbish not properly placed on the collection day.
(3)
The county shall not be responsible for collection of:
a.
Construction debris; or
b.
Commercial solid waste.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-43. - Fees generally.
(a)
The fees for collection of residential solid waste levied pursuant to the terms of this article, and all late charges accruing thereon are hereby levied severally against the owner and occupant of each residential unit, including all residential units located in mobile home courts and/or trailer courts.
(b)
The fees and late charges, prescribed in this section shall constitute a continuing lien against the real property upon which each residential unit is located; on the personal property composing a residential unit, and upon the personal property of the occupant of each residential unit, until paid.
(c)
The fees and late charges, prescribed in this section shall be billed in the following manner: (i) each residential unit or premises having service from the Houston County Water System shall be billed for the sanitation services hereunder on the aforesaid county water bill. Failure to pay the fees and late charges, for sanitation services shall result in the termination of both services. This shall be in addition to other remedies available to the county, including legal remedies. (ii) each residential unit or premises not having service from the Houston County Water System shall be billed by separate bill. Failure to pay the aforesaid sanitation services shall result in the collection of the aforesaid fees by any and all legal remedies available to the county.
(d)
The monthly fees for solid waste collection hereby levied shall be due and payable as instructed on the bills, and if the same are not paid by the due date, there is hereby levied a late charge equal to ten percent of the amount due or $2.00, whichever is greater.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-44. - Fee rates.
The fees for the collection and disposal of residential solid waste within the district shall be established from time to time by action of the board of commissioners. These fee rates are kept on file at the utility business office. If refuse is removed by the county from a residential unit and is found to be noncompliant yard waste or bulk waste an additional fee will be added to the bill. The rates of such service charges may be fixed and changed from time to time by the county board of commissioners. Changes may be made without adoption of an amendment to existing sections. If refuse is removed by the county and the property owner does not have a utility bill the fee will be billed to the property owner.
(Amd. of 4-5-05; Amd. of 10-20-20)
Sec. 50-45. - Fee exemptions.
(a)
Senior citizen exemption. Any senior citizen, age 65 or older, who is the head of the household and, who can provide proof they can dispose of their garbage in a neighbor's or family member's roll-out solid waste container will be exempted from the county's solid waste collection fee in its entirety.
(b)
Special exemption. Any person with a condition that constitutes a hardship, in the sole opinion of the board of commissioners, may petition the Board for an exemption of all or a portion of the county's solid waste collection fee.
(c)
[Requirements]. Each petitioner shall be required to provide adequate information on forms provided by the county to assist the county commissioners in making a determination as to the exemption. All exemption requests approved by the county commissioners shall be effective for a 12-month period of time following the granting of the exemption.
(d)
It shall be unlawful to willfully furnish any false information to the utilities business office for service or exemption of service charge.
(Amd. of 4-5-05; Amd. of 10-20-20)
Sec. 50-46. - Payment.
The county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee shall prepare and mail to each residential unit in the district, a bill showing the amounts of the monthly installments due for residential solid waste collection service. These statements shall be prepared on a monthly basis and each monthly payment shall be paid by the owner or occupant of each residential unit. A late payment penalty shall be imposed upon each account for which payment is not made within the time limits prescribed thereon.
(Amd. of 4-5-05); Amd. of 10-20-20)
Sec. 50-47. - Non-liability.
(a)
Whenever the owner or occupant of a residential unit from which solid waste is collected by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee authorizes or permits the use of private driveways or other portions of private property for such collection, such authorization or permission, whether express or implied, shall be deemed to constitute a waiver on the part of the owner or occupant of the residential unit of any claim for damages against the county, its officers, agent, employees, franchisees, and contractors which result from the use of such private property in the collection process.
(b)
The county or any person or entity acting as its franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee pursuant to franchise or contract shall be deemed to have been invited and authorized to enter upon private property for the purpose of collection of solid waste in those cases where other than curbside collection has been requested by the owner or occupant of a residential unit and approved by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-48. - Use of facilities required.
The owner and occupant of each residential unit, including residential units located in mobile home courts and trailer courts, shall dispose of solid waste only by means of a collection and disposal service provided by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-49. - Termination of service.
Whenever a residential unit becomes vacant, destroyed by fire or other casualty, or is demolished or for some other similar reason no longer generates solid waste, it is incumbent severally upon the owner and former occupant of such residential unit to notify the county of the same. The county may cause such investigation of the report as it deems appropriate and if the county finds the same to be consistent with the provisions hereof, the residential unit in question shall be removed from the solid waste collection list and the fee charge shall thereupon cease. Until such notice shall have been received by the county, however, the fee shall continue to be charged and shall continue to be collectible, notwithstanding the fact that service is not in fact rendered to the residential unit. Similarly, if service has been discontinued pursuant to a notice as provided in this section, but is thereafter resumed, or if land which previously vacant has been improved with a residential unit and begins to receive service, it shall be incumbent on the owner and occupant, severally, to notify the county of such commencement of or resumption of service. The fee prescribed in this article shall be payable for such property from and after the date of resumption of service, whether or not notice has been received.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-50. - Resumption of service.
The county may charge a fee for resumption of service to the owner and/or occupant of a residential unit where service has been previously discontinued. The amount of such fee shall be that as set from time to time by the board of commissioners.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-51. - Unlawful disposal of refuse, garbage, or rubbish.
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump or bury or cause to be dumped or buried any garbage, refuse, solid waste, or rubbish anywhere in the area of the district not previously approved by the appropriate state authorities.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-52. - Violations of article generally.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully and intentionally violate any of the provisions of this article.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person willfully and with intent to defraud the county, to evade or defeat or attempt to evade or defraud any fee, penalty, or interest due the county or the payment thereof, imposed by this article.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-53. - Unlawful burning.
It shall be unlawful for any person to burn or attempt to burn or cause to be burned any material of any nature in any container belonging to or used by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee for residential solid waste collection purposes.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-54. - Penalty for violations.
Each violation of any of the provisions of this article shall be punishable as provided in Section 1-11 of the County Code Ordinances. Violations may be tried upon citations with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-6, et seq. Citations shall be issued by the sheriff's department or its designee. Service upon corporations, partnerships, or other entities and organizations shall be affected by serving any managing agent located in the county, or by serving the registered agent, or any officer or partner of the corporation, partnership, organization or entity. The provisions of this article may also be enforced through the use of other remedies, such as injunctive relief, provided by the laws of this state.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-55. - Violations by entities.
Whenever a corporation, partnership, organization or other entity shall violate any of the provisions of this article such violations shall be deemed to be also that of the individual directors, officers, or agents of such corporation, partnership, organization or other artificial entity who shall have authorized, ordered or done any of the acts constituting in whole or in part such violation. In such cases, service shall also be made upon the individual director, officer, or other agent accused of authorizing, ordering, or doing acts constituting in whole or in part violations of this article.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-56. - Placement.
The owner, occupant, tenant, or lessee of each residential unit shall, no later than 7:00 a.m. on the normal pickup days designated by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee, place the containers at the location established by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee for pickup. Containers shall be removed from the location to the premises of the owner, occupant, tenant or lessee of each such residential unit no later than 24 hours on the normal day of pickup, subject only to the exceptions enumerated in section 50-57.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-57. - Same—Exceptions.
The following are excepted from section 50-56.
(1)
Any person with documented disabilities, because of age or physical condition, living in a residential unit where there is no resident or occupant thereof physically capable of placing the container at the designated pickup location shall notify the county and if the county shall find that there is no one in or upon the residential unit on a regular basis physically capable of placing the container at the designated pickup location, then the county may arrange for the solid waste to be picked up at the place on the premises of the residential unit where the container is maintained by the occupant.
(2)
The county may establish exceptions for designated residential unit which are determined to be unsuitable for curbside pickup, taking into consideration the terrain, features or other physical impediments, and at such premises, pickup service shall be provided to residential units as determined by the county; or in the alternative, the occupant may be instructed to place only plastic; securely tied bags at a designated location on a scheduled pickup days.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-58. - Removal from premises.
The roll-out solid waste container furnished by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee shall not be removed from the premises of the residential unit to which they are delivered and it shall be the duty of the occupant of the residential unit, if the dwelling is to be vacated by the occupant, to notify the county seven days prior to the vacancy. No person shall remove any roll-out cart from the residential unit, except with permission of the county.
(Amd. of 4-5-05; Amd. of 10-20-20)
Sec. 50-59. - Replacement.
If a container previously furnished by the county, franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee to any residential unit is damaged, destroyed, or stolen, it shall be the duty of the owner or occupant of the residential unit to immediately notify the county and to cooperate with the board of commissioners and the county sheriff in investigating the cause of the damage, destruction, or theft. Further, the owner or occupant shall, if requested to do so, appear in the appropriate court as a witness should a prosecution result from the investigation. A replacement container shall be furnished to the residential unit and such unit may be charged a replacement fee as determined by the county.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-60. - Additional containers.
Additional containers may be obtained by each residential unit upon payment of an additional monthly fee to the county, as may be set from time to time by the board of commissioners.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
Sec. 50-61. - Damaging containers.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully and intentionally damage or destroy or to allow or permit damage to or destruction of any container provided by the county, its franchisee, contract collector, or county's designee.
(Amd. of 4-5-05)
