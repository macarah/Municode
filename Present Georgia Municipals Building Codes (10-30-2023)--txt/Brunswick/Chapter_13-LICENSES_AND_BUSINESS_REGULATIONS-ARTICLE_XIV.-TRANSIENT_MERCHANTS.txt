ARTICLE XIV. - TRANSIENT MERCHANTS[8]

State Law reference— Peddlers and itinerant trades, O.C.G.A. § 43-32-1 et seq.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 13-506. - Definition.
(a)
For the purpose of this article, a "transient merchant," "itinerant merchant" or "itinerant vendor" is defined as any person, whether as owner, agent, consignee or employee, and whether a resident of the city or not, who engages in a business of selling goods, wares, merchandise or services temporarily within the city and who, in furtherance of such purpose, hires, leases, uses or occupies any building, structure, shop, apartment or room in a hotel, motel or lodging house, tent, motor vehicle or railroad car, or any street, alley or other place within the city, for the exhibition for sale of such goods, wares and merchandise or performance of such services, either privately, publicly or at auction. The person so engaged shall not be relieved from complying with the provisions of this article by reason of associating temporarily with any local dealer, trader, merchant or auctioneer, or by conducting such transient business in connection with or in the name of any local dealer, trader, merchant or auctioneer.
(b)
The definition set forth in this section shall not be deemed to include any person who, while occupying such temporary location, merely takes orders for the sale of goods for future delivery or exhibits samples for the purpose of securing orders only for future delivery. Such definition shall be deemed to include any person engaged in the performance of any service such as fitting, measuring, planning, designing, photographing or any other service of similar kind, notwithstanding that such service may contemplate the future delivery of tangible property.
(Ord. No. 735½, §§ 2, 3, 6-21-1978)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 13-507. - Use of streets, parks, etc.
(a)
No transient merchant shall conduct, in whole or part, a business upon any public sidewalk, street, alley, park, right-of-way or other public lands of the city without first obtaining the consent of the city commission after furnishing proof satisfactory to the commission that the location and conduct of such business with neither obstruct such public ways and places, nor interfere with the orderly regulation of traffic, nor interfere with the right of the general public to the use of such public ways and places; provided, however, that the market at Mary Ross Waterfront Park and such other places as the commission may from time to time designate may be used for transient merchant business subject to any terms, fees and conditions as may be provided for by the city manager or such other person or persons as the commission may place in charge of management of the facility.
(b)
No transient merchant shall conduct a business upon any privately owned property without first providing the deputy city marshal with written authorization for such use of the property, identifying the owner thereof and signed by the owner.
(c)
No transient merchant shall locate any business in any residential-zoned area.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 9, 6-21-1978; Ord. No. 947, § 2, 1-16-2002)
Sec. 13-508. - Report of sales subject to sales tax.
It shall be the duty of each transient merchant, itinerant merchant, or itinerant vendor, after having engaged in business within the city, to report to the deputy city marshal the amount of gross sales made within the city during the period while the license was in effect and to furnish proof that all taxes due to the commissioner of revenue of the state upon such sales, including the local option sales tax, have been remitted to the commissioner of revenue as required by state law. Such report of gross sales and proof of tax payment thereon shall be filed with the deputy city marshal at the time payment is due to the commissioner of revenue.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 12, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-509. - Violations.
Failure to comply with the licensing, application and other requirements of this article XIV shall be deemed a violation of the City Code punishable as provided for in chapter 15 of the City Code.
(Ord. No. 947, § 4, 1-16-2002)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 13-521. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for a transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor to engage in such business within the city without first obtaining a license therefor in compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 1, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-522. - Application.
Applicants for a license under this division shall file with the deputy city marshal a written application under oath, signed by the applicant if an individual, by all partners if a partnership, and by the president if a corporation, and showing as follows:
(1)
The name of the person having the management or supervision of the applicant's business during the time it is proposed that it will be carried on within the city; the local address of such person while engaged in such business; the permanent address of such person; the capacity in which such person will act (that is, whether as proprietor, agent or otherwise); the name and address of the person for whose account the business will be carried on, if any; and if a corporation, the state of the incorporation.
(2)
The place or places within the city where it is proposed to carry on the applicant's business and the length of time during which it is proposed that such business will be conducted at each location. Where the transient business will be conducted upon privately owned property, the signed, written documentation of owner permission as required by section 13-507(b) shall be appended to the application.
(3)
The place or places other than the permanent place of business of the applicant where the applicant conducted a transient business within the six-month period next preceding the date of application, stating the nature of each such business and giving the post office and street address of the location at which the business was conducted and identifying the local licensing entity for each.
(4)
A statement of the nature, character, quality and quantity of the goods, produce, wares, merchandise or services to be sold or offered by the applicant within the city; whether the same are proposed to be sold from stock in possession, by sample, at auction, by direct sale, by taking orders for future delivery or any combination of the foregoing; where the goods or products to be sold are manufactured or produced and where the goods or products are located at the time of application. Where the applicant proposes to sell goods, wares and merchandise from stock in possession, there shall be appended to the application a complete and itemized list of the articles to be sold, which shall be signed by the applicant and verified under oath.
(5)
Written documentation or credentials from the person or company for which the applicant proposes to do business, authorizing the applicant to act as a representative.
(6)
A list of the full and correct names of all persons who will or may be employed in the transient business.
(7)
A listing of any prior periods when the applicant has carried on a transient business with the city.
(8)
An acknowledgement that applicant has been furnished with a copy of article XIV, chapter 13 of the City Code and will abide by all city ordinances in conducting the proposed transient business.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 4, 6-21-1978; Ord. No. 947, § 3, 1-16-2002)
Sec. 13-523. - Sales tax number required.
Before any license shall be issued pursuant to this division, the applicant shall file with the deputy city marshal proof satisfactory to the deputy city marshal that the applicant or the business proposed to be conducted pursuant to the license possesses a valid state sales tax number issued by the commissioner of revenue of the state.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 5, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-524. - Bond.
Before any license shall be issued pursuant to this division, the applicant shall file with the deputy city marshal a bond binding the applicant to the city in the full sum of $10,000.00, executed by the applicant as principal and by a surety approved by the deputy city marshal, conditioned that the applicant shall comply fully with the ordinances of the city and the statutes of the state governing the conduct of such business and will pay all judgments rendered against the applicant for any violation of such ordinances or statutes and all judgments that may be recovered against the applicant by any person or persons for damages growing out of any misrepresentation, deception or breach of warranty in the conduct of such business. Action on the bond may be brought in the name of the city for the use of any aggrieved person.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 6, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-525. - Agent for service of process.
Before any license shall be issued pursuant to this division, the applicant shall file with the deputy city marshal an instrument appointing the secretary of the city commission as his true and lawful agent for service of process upon the applicant in any suit or legal proceeding arising out of the business transacted pursuant to the license applied for or for the performance of the conditions of the aforesaid bond or for any breach thereof. Immediately upon service of process upon the secretary of the city commission, the secretary shall send to the licensee at his last-known address, by registered mail, a copy of such process.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 7, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-526. - Display; separate licenses for separate places of business.
The license issued pursuant to this division shall be posted conspicuously in the place of business named therein. In the event that the applicant shall desire to do business in more than one place within the city, separate licenses shall be purchased and issued for each place of business, and shall be posted conspicuously in each place of business.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 8, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-527. - Activity restricted.
No license issued pursuant to this division shall carry with it the privilege of making a house-to-house canvass of residences and offering for sale and delivery goods or services of any character if such canvass shall be otherwise regulated by the city.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 8, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-528. - Fee, occupational tax.
(a)
Licensees under this division shall pay an initial minimum license fee at the time of issuance of the license, equal to $25.00 for each day upon which business will be conducted pursuant to the license.
(b)
At the time of filing the report of gross sales required by section 13-508, the licensee shall pay a gross receipts license tax equivalent to the amount which the licensee would have paid pursuant to article II of this chapter if licensed under article II of this chapter less a credit for the initial minimum license fee paid at the time of issuance of the license.
(c)
In computing the taxes to be paid pursuant to subsection (b) of this section, all gross receipts derived in the city during a calendar year shall be cumulative, it being the intent of this section to require transient or itinerant merchants and vendors to pay license fees and occupational taxes not less than those paid by businesses having permanent locations within the city.
(d)
Failure to comply with section 13-508 or with subsections (b) and (c) of this section shall constitute ground to refuse the issuance of a subsequent license to a transient or itinerant merchant or vendor.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 10, 6-21-1978; Ord. No. 823, § 1, 9-14-1985)
Sec. 13-529. - Revocation.
(a)
The licenses issued pursuant to this division may be revoked by the board of city commissioners, after notice and hearing, for any of the following causes:
(1)
Any fraud, misrepresentation or false statement contained in the application for license.
(2)
Any fraud, misrepresentation or false statement made in connection with the selling of goods, wares, merchandise or services.
(3)
Any violation of this article.
(4)
Conducting the business licensed under this division in an unlawful manner or in such a manner as to constitute a breach of the peace or to constitute a menace to the health, safety or general welfare of the public.
(b)
Notice of hearing for revocation of a license shall be given in writing, setting forth specifically the grounds of the complaint and the time and place of the hearing. The notice shall be served on the licensee by handing the same personally to the person in charge of the license's business, or by mailing the same, postage prepaid, to the licensee at his last-known address at least five days prior to the date set for the hearing.
(c)
The giving of such notice shall suspend the license pending the outcome of the hearing, and business operations under the license shall cease.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 11, 6-21-1978)
Sec. 13-530. - Liability for payment of taxes due.
No license shall be issued to a transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor pursuant to this division unless the records on file in the office of the deputy city marshal show that the applicant has paid all taxes due upon any prior sales made within the city.
(Ord. No. 735½, § 13, 6-21-1978)
