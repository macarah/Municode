ARTICLE XIII. - TELEPHONE COMPANIES

Sec. 13-481. - Authority for tax.
The Georgia Public Service Commission, through an order dated February 18, 1971, provides for local service revenues as a base for occupational tax as it relates to telephone companies doing business within the city.
(Ord. No. 616, § 1, 7-7-1971)
Sec. 13-482. - Tax levied.
The telephone company doing business in the city is to remit to the city finance director payment equal to three percent of the local service revenues in two installments, one on or before May 1, and the other on or before September 1, in each given year.
(Ord. No. 616, § 2, 7-7-1971)
Sec. 13-483. - Application to franchisee.
The franchise agreement now in effect with Southern Bell Telephone Company is in no way affected by this change in rate method or manner of the payment of this occupational tax, and any franchise fees are to be paid along with this occupational tax at the time of remittance.
(Ord. No. 616, § 3, 7-7-1971)
