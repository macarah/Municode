ARTICLE XII. - PUBLIC DANCE HALLS

Sec. 13-456. - Definition.
The term "public dance hall," as used in this article, shall include any dance hall where a dance is held to which admission may be gained by the general public, where an orchestra furnishes the music, but shall not include any dance hall wherein a dance is held to which admission is restricted to invited guests.
(Code 1953, § 13.1)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 13-457. - License required.
No person shall conduct any public dance hall without first obtaining the license required by article II of this chapter, and complying with all of the terms of such article.
(Code 1953, § 13.2)
Sec. 13-458. - Prerequisite to issuance of license.
No license required by section 13-457 shall be issued where the dance hall is situated within the first fire district unless a petition shall be first presented to the city commission duly approved and signed by at least three-fourths of the residents living within 450 feet of the location of such hall.
(Code 1953, § 13.3)
Sec. 13-459. - Revocation of license.
The city commission shall have the right to revoke the license granted to any person for the operation of a public dance hall if there has been any boisterous, riotous or disorderly conduct or if the maintenance of such public dance hall or place for dancing shall disturb the public peace. Before revoking any such license, the commission shall allow the holder of such license a hearing. Five days' notice of such hearing shall be given to the holder in writing, together with a brief statement of the grounds upon which the commission proposes to revoke such license. If at such hearing the holder fails to appear or fails to show any good and sufficient reason in the judgment of the commission why his license should not be revoked, the commission shall revoke and cancel such license. It shall thereafter be unlawful for the holder of any such license to operate such public dance hall.
(Code 1953, § 13.4)
