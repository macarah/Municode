ARTICLE II. - CITY COMMISSION

Sec. 2-26. - Compensation.
Each member of the city commission shall receive compensation for their official duties. The amount of compensation shall be set from time to time by ordinance and shall be effective in accordance with state law.
(Ord. No. 879, 8-21-1989)
State Law reference— Authority to fix compensation for members of the governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-4.
Sec. 2-27. - Employment of city commissioners and mayor.
A commissioner or mayor shall not be eligible to apply for an open position of employment with the city. However, nothing herein shall prevent a mayor or commissioner from running for the office of commissioner or mayor pursuant to the City Charter.
(Ord. No. 1030, § 1, 4-17-2013)
Sec. 2-28. - Vacancy in office.
In the event the office of any commissioner or mayor shall become temporarily vacant the office shall be filled as follows:
(a)
A vacancy in the board shall be filled for the unexpired term by a member appointed by the remaining members of the board by a majority vote.
Upon the vacation from office, the city clerk shall post in the official organ of the county and on the city's website an advertisement, approved by the board, seeking applications for nominations to fill the unexpired term of the vacating member. The advertisement shall run once a week for two weeks following the vacation from office. The advertisement shall state a deadline for submitting applications. Each remaining member of the board shall nominate a person from the applications received for consideration by the board as a whole. The board as a whole shall appoint a person to fill the vacancy from those nominations.
(b)
If the vacancy becomes permanent, it shall be filled as provided for in the Charter of the City of Brunswick, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 1032, § 1, 8-21-2013)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 1032, § 1, adopted Aug. 21, 2013, set out provisions intended for use as § 2-27. For purposes of classification, and at the editor's discretion, these provisions have been included as § 2-28.
