ARTICLE II. - MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-19. - Regulations; prohibited acts.
No holder of a license authorizing the sale of malt beverages and wine at retail in the county or any agent or employee of the licensee, shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell beer or wine to a person under the age established by state law.
(2)
Knowingly sell beer or wine to any person when such person is in an intoxicated condition.
(3)
Sell or possess for the purpose of sale, any malt beverages or wine where such person does not have a license from the county commissioner to sell or possess for sale such beverages.
(4)
License malt beverages or wine to be received at or delivered to the premises of the retail licensee by any other means than by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a license from the county commissioner to make deliveries in the county. Transportation of malt beverages and wine by any other means shall be grounds for revocation of wholesale license.
(5)
Engage in the sale of such beverages on Sunday, Christmas Day, or any other days prohibited by state law.
(6)
Any violation of the provisions of this section shall be grounds for revocation of a license by the county commissioner.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § VI; Res. of 12-9-2002, § VI)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays, election days, and Christmas Day generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; local regulation of Sunday sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale of alcoholic beverage to intoxicated person, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22; furnishing to, possession of, or purchase of alcoholic beverages by persons under 21 years of age, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-20. - Qualifications for licensure.
Qualifications for licensure are as follows:
(1)
The retail licensee must be a person over the age of 21 years and a citizen of the United States and the State of Georgia.
(2)
Excepting vintner licensees who operate wine tasting rooms on premises, said licensee must have owned or operated the business within the county, from which malt beverages and wine will be sold, for a period of 30 days or more prior to application for said retail license and at least 50 percent of the gross weekly sales generated by licensee's business for which a retail malt beverage and wine license is granted must originate from the sale of products other than malt beverages or wine, as measured by said business's gross sales tax receipts.
(3)
The retail licensee may be a corporation organized under the laws of the state, provided that the corporation is in good standing as evidenced by a current certificate of status issued by the secretary of state, and, further, with the exception of vintner licensees organized under the corporate form who operate wine tasting rooms on premises, the corporation must have owned or operated the place of business within the county, from which malt beverages and wine will be sold, for a period of 30 days or more prior to filing an application for license and at least 50 percent of the gross weekly sales generated by the corporate licensee's business for which a retail malt beverage and wine license is granted must originate from the sale of all products other than malt beverages or wine, as measured by said business's gross sales tax receipts.
(4)
The licensee shall not have been convicted within seven years of the date of application of any felony, or any other violation of the laws of the state, or any other state, relating to the sale or use of alcoholic beverages.
(5)
The licensee shall be responsible for the daily management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(6)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement of his qualifications according to subsections (1) through (5) of this section and shall place same on file with the clerk of the county commissioner before any license is issued.
(7)
If the application is for a partnership or submitted on behalf of a partnership, all members of the partnership who own or operate the retail establishment in which malt beverages are to be sold must be qualified to obtain a license and must make sworn statements of these qualifications in the manner cited in subsection (6) of this section.
(8)
No person or entity shall be granted a license unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the county commissioner that such person or entity has a qualified location for the retail sale of malt beverages and wine.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § II; Res. of 12-9-2002, § II)
Sec. 4-21. - Effect of false statement or concealed facts in application.
Any misstatement or concealment of facts in the application shall be grounds for revocation of the license issued and shall make the applicant liable for prosecution for perjury under the laws of the state.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § II; Res. of 12-9-2002, § II)
Sec. 4-22. - Statement of gross sales tax receipts.
(a)
At the time of application for renewal of a license issued pursuant to this article, the commissioner may require all licensees, except vintners operating wine tasting rooms on their licensed premises, to provide the county with the licensee's gross sales tax receipts for the preceding period of licensure. Should the licensee's gross sales tax receipts not reflect conformity with the requirements of this article, renewal of the license will be denied.
(b)
Upon the request of the county commissioner, all licensees under this article, except vintners operating wine tasting rooms on premises, must provide, within a reasonable amount of time, records of their sales tax receipts to the county commissioner for inspection. Should said sales tax receipts not reflect conformity with this article, the retail license of such licensee may be suspended or revoked by the county commissioner.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § II; Res. of 12-9-2002, § II)
Sec. 4-23. - Wholesale licenses.
Wholesale licenses shall be issued only to those parties who are licensed by the state to sell and distribute malt beverages and wine at wholesale. Application shall be made with the clerk of the county commissioner.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § V; Res. of 12-9-2002, § V)
Sec. 4-24. - Applications, renewals, notice and hearing.
(a)
All applications for an original license or any renewal thereof shall be submitted in writing to the county commissioner or the clerk of the county commissioner.
(b)
Each applicant shall state, under oath, that he has read this chapter, that he qualifies in all respects for a license issued hereunder, and that he shall abide by the rules and regulations of this chapter.
(c)
The application shall be accompanied with a detailed description of the proposed location of the business regulated hereunder.
(d)
In all cases, except renewals, the application shall be accompanied with a complete set of fingerprints as provided by law. The license shall not be issued if a record check reveals a violation prohibited by the qualification requirements of this chapter.
(e)
The application shall be signed by the applicant under oath before an officer authorized to administer oaths in the state.
(f)
All corporate applications shall be submitted by the officers authorized by the corporation as the applicant on behalf of such corporation and the authorized officer shall comply with all application requirements provided herein.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § VII; Res. of 12-9-2002, § VII)
Sec. 4-25. - Issuance or denial of license.
(a)
All decisions approving, denying, suspending or revoking a license shall be in writing, with the reasons therefor stated and mailed or personally delivered to the applicant.
(b)
If approved, the license shall be issued by the clerk of the county commissioner upon payment of the required license fee.
(c)
Any applicant aggrieved by the decision of the county commissioner regarding a license may, within ten days from the date of such decision, request a hearing with an opportunity to present evidence and cross-examine witnesses. Upon a timely request, a hearing shall be held within a reasonable time not to exceed 30 days from the date of the request for a hearing. Based on such evidence as may be presented upon a hearing, the county commissioner may affirm, reverse, or modify the prior decision.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § VII; Res. of 12-9-2002, § VII)
Sec. 4-26. - Term of licenses.
(a)
Licenses issued under the terms of this article for sale of malt beverages and wine off the premises shall be renewed annually on or before November 15 of each year and shall expire on November 15 of each year.
(b)
Licenses issued under the terms of this article for the sale of malt beverages and wine on the premises, except licenses issued to nonprofit corporations, shall be renewed annually on or before November 15 of each year and shall expire on November 15 of each year.
(c)
Licenses issued to nonprofit corporations under the terms of this article for the sale of malt beverages and wine on the premises issued, shall be renewed annually on or before November 15 of each year and shall expire on November 15 of each year.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § VIII; Res. of 12-9-2002, § VIII)
Sec. 4-27. - License fees.
(a)
License fees shall be as established from time to time by the county commissioner and set forth in the county's fee schedule.
(b)
Should a license be revoked for a violation of any provision of this article or federal or state law, the unearned portion of such license fee shall be forfeited by the licensee.
(c)
If an applicant seeks and obtains a license under this chapter after the renewal date of November 15, the commissioner may pro-rate license fee for that portion of the year remaining until the renewal date.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § VIII; Res. of 12-9-2002, § VIII; Res. of 8-15-2017(1) , § 3)

No retail licensees of the county shall accept delivery of malt beverages and wine from any wholesale dealer unless the proper stamps are affixed to all containers as required by applicable ordinance or law.

Sec. 4-30. - Hours of operation.
(a)
The hours of operation of retail licensees for the sale of malt beverages and wine shall be between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight.
(b)
The hours of operation of wholesale licensees for the distribution of malt beverages and wine within the county shall be between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m. There shall be no malt beverages or wine sold or distributed in the county on Sundays, Christmas Day, or any other days prohibited by state law.

DIVISION 2. - SALE FOR CONSUMPTION OFF PREMISES

Sec. 4-49. - Type of sale.
Malt beverages and wine sold pursuant to this division will be for off-premises consumption only.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § I; Res. of 12-9-2002, § I)
State Law reference— Allowing or permitting of breaking of packages or drinking of contents thereof on premises, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26.
Sec. 4-50. - Qualified location.
(a)
A licensee may conduct the retail sale of malt beverages and wine only at a qualified location within the county. In order to be qualified, no location may be within 500 feet of any church building, funeral home, school building, educational building on a college campus.
(b)
For the purpose of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground, and shall measure from the property line of the tract on which is located the business regulated under this division to the property line of the tract on which is located the church building, funeral home, school building, or an educational building on a college campus.
(c)
The measurement of distance as contemplated in this section shall be the closest point of the property line a qualified business is located on to the closest point of the property line of any church building, funeral home, school building, or an educational building on a college campus.
(Res. of 5-6-1985, § III; Res. of 12-9-2002, § III)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.

Sec. 4-74. - Purpose.
The commissioner of Towns County, Georgia desires to set out fair and comprehensive rules and regulations establishing reasonable and ascertainable standards for the regulation and control of the licensing and sales of alcoholic beverages:
(1)
To promote the tourism trade, prosperity, economic well-being and the general welfare of the county;
(2)
To protect and preserve the community, including its schools and churches;
(3)
To maintain and promote desirable living conditions, and sustain stability of neighborhoods and property values;
(4)
To prevent undesirable persons from engaging in or having any interest in the sale of alcoholic beverages; and
(5)
For the public welfare of the citizens of the county.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-75. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or sprits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverages, wine, or fortified wine.
Alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises means all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, spirituous liquors, malt beverages, wine or fortified wine that are poured and consumed on the premises, including bottles of beer or malt liquor and bottles of wine that are opened only for consumption on the premises.
Church means a tax exempt organization in a building including the main structure together with any auxiliary structures where persons regularly assemble for religious worship.
County clerk means the official certified county clerk, or other duly designated person appointed by the Commissioner to handle certain county clerk duties.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume.
Eating establishment means any public place, including a place available for rental by the public, with seating for 35 or more persons, or other number as specified in this chapter, selling prepared food for consumption by the public on the premises, and including the service of chips, pretzels, snacks of various kinds, with a full-service kitchen which must be approved by the health and fire departments, and sanitary restroom facilities and which derives no less than 50 percent of its income from the sale of food and nonalcoholic beverages.
Family means and includes any person related to the holder of a license within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity, as determined according to civil law.
Front means any person or entity that is operating under authority or agency of another.
Licensee means a person holding any class of license issued under this chapter.
Malt beverage, commonly referred to as beer, means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, hard cider, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Nonprofit organization means an entity which is exempt from federal income tax pursuant to the provisions of 26 USC 501(c), (d), (e).
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, box or other original consumer container.
Premises means the entire building, including patios, porches and decks. In some instances, where indicated, the term "premises" may also include the entrance area, parking lot and the entire lot or parcel wherein the licensed establishment is located.
Registered agent means a resident of the county designated to receive all communications, notices, service of process or other papers or documents with respect to any alcohol license and who will be responsible for any matters relating to the alcohol license.
Retail consumption dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises, at retail, only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail package dealer means any person or store for the retail package sale of beer, malt beverages or wine, or any combination thereof, by unbroken packages in sealed containers to consumers at retail only and not for resale.
Single serving generally means 12 ounces of beer or five ounces of wine; however, in this chapter, single servings of beer shall include a container of up to 34 ounces in a single serving factory package, and for wine up to nine ounces, in a single serving factory package.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means an entity that sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, retail package dealers, or retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-76. - Compliance required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, provide, manufacture, or offer for sale alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises without having first complied with this chapter.
(b)
It shall be the duty of each licensee to maintain a copy of this chapter on the licensed premises, and to instruct each employee as to the terms of this chapter. The licensee and each employee shall, at all times, be familiar with this chapter and any amendments thereto.
(c)
A licensee is charged with the responsibility for compliance with this chapter by its officers, agents, servants and employees. A licensee is responsible for acts in violation of this chapter committed by others, other than those listed in this chapter, if within the knowledge of the licensee.
(d)
The state laws and regulations relating to the sale and distribution of alcoholic beverages, malt beverages and wine in the state are incorporated into and made a part of this chapter as if fully set out herein. Any violation of such state laws or regulations shall be grounds for suspension or revocation of any license issued under this chapter.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )

Alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises may be sold in the unincorporated areas of Towns County under licenses granted by the commissioner upon the terms and conditions provided in this section.
(b)
All licenses in this chapter shall be a mere grant of privilege to carry on the business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this chapter and state law.

Malt beverages and wine for consumption on the premises;

Malt beverages, wine, and distilled spirits for sale for consumption only on the premises;

Sec. 4-79. - General regulations for the sale of alcoholic beverages on premises.
(a)
All signs used to advertise a business shall comply with the county sign ordinance. All signage shall also comply with state law.
(b)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located shall comply with this chapter and all applicable state laws and regulations, including, but not limited to, the regulations of the state revenue commissioner.
(c)
Each building in which a business will be located shall be complete and in good repair, constructed of permanent exterior materials, and be neat, clean and well maintained not only at the time of application, but continuously maintained at all times a premises is licensed.
(d)
Each applicant for an alcoholic beverage license shall attach to the application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building, or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. If the applicant is a franchisee, then such applicant shall attach a copy of the franchise agreement or contract with the application. No percentage leases of buildings or realty shall be permitted.
(e)
If the county or its designee deems it necessary to conduct an audit of the records and books of the licensee, it shall notify the licensee of the date, time and place of the audit. The licensee shall cooperate with the audit or forfeit any license issued under this chapter.
(f)
All licensed establishments must maintain the following records for a three-year period and make them available for audit at the licensed premises or other office or headquarters located in the county:
(1)
Monthly income or operating statements.
(2)
Daily sales receipts showing distilled spirits, beer, wine, food, and other items sold separately, according to the type of license.
(3)
Daily cash register receipts such as Z tapes or guest tickets.
(4)
Monthly state sales and use tax reports.
(5)
Federal income tax return, with all form 1099s.
(6)
All invoices or other statements reflecting a record of alcohol purchases by the licensee.
(7)
Electronic equivalents of records may be accepted, if needed, and if the county or its designee agrees.
(g)
All licensed establishments shall submit to the county at least semi-annually, on or before November 14 and May 15 of each year, or at such other times as requested, summaries of financial records showing alcohol sales for the purpose of substantiating amounts due or paid to the county from alcohol sales, together with copies of back-up documentation, or the electronic equivalent if the county agrees.
(h)
The licensee is responsible to make sure all persons who are working/employed at the establishment and who are dispensing, selling and/or serving alcoholic beverages shall not be disqualified from said activity in accordance with the laws, statutes, rules and regulations of the State of Georgia.
(i)
The licensee shall verify that all persons (workers or employees) are not in violation of this section, and it shall be the duty and responsibility of the licensee to investigate and ensure the qualification of the employees before allowing any sale or serving of alcohol. Failure to verify and allow a person to serve alcohol on the premises shall be a violation of this chapter.
(j)
A person who is allowed to serve alcohol at the premises shall be at least 21 years of age, and if the person is at least 18 but not 21 years of age, that person may not mix or pour alcoholic beverages unless there is also present a manager or person in charge who is at least 21 years old.
(k)
All persons who mix, pour, or serve alcohol must be certified by Rserving.com. Proof of the employee's certification must be maintained by the employee and by the licensee.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-80. - Regulations for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
(a)
Alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises must be sold in an eating establishment regularly serving prepared food, with a full service kitchen which must be approved by the health and fire departments, having a full service kitchen approved by the county health and fire departments. An eating establishment must meet the requirement that at least 50 percent of its sales are from non-alcohol sales (sale of any goods other than food, as defined herein, to include, but not be limited to merchandise shall not be credited as a part of sales to meet the minimum sales requirement for the ratio of 50 percent of non-alcohol sales).
(b)
Such eating establishment will regularly serve food every hour they are open. Alcoholic beverages may be served to customers who do not purchase food; however, service of food shall be available at all times. Alcohol may be served up to 11:00 p.m.
(c)
Eating establishments licensed to sell alcohol for consumption on the premises are required to have seating for food service of at least 35 seats.
(1)
Alcohol may be served on an outside patio; however, all patios must be walled with a wall that is at least three feet high, with no access other than through the eating establishment. Any wall on an outside porch or patio shall meet all county building codes and requirements. Access to the outside patio shall be through the establishment; however, the owner must ensure, for the protection of the patrons, that there is a manner to leave the patio in the event of a fire and that such manner is in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any business establishments licensed to sell malt beverages, wine, distilled spirits, or other alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises only to sell, dispense or deliver alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises except between the hours of 11:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday, provided, however, that on New Year's Eve, alcohol may be served until 12:00 midnight. All establishments shall make service of "last call" no less than 15 minutes prior to closing, and alcohol consumption must be finished by no later than closing.
(e)
Persons holding a license to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall not be permitted to sell any alcoholic beverage by the package or to allow anyone to take alcoholic beverages, except as allowed by state law, away from the premises.
(f)
The name, brand or type of alcoholic beverage served and the price per serving may be provided to customers on a regular printed food menu.
(g)
Any person serving but not pouring alcoholic beverages in an eating establishment shall be at least 18 years old.
(h)
Happy hours. As to any retail consumption dealer, happy hours, or similar promotions, shall be permitted, and licensees in connection with the sale or service of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises may:
(1)
Serve any free alcoholic beverage (wine and beer) to any person or group of persons;
(2)
Not serve more than one alcoholic beverage to one person at a time but may serve a pitcher of beer or carafe of wine to a table of more than one person;
(3)
Sell, offer to sell, or serve to any person or group of persons any alcoholic beverage at a price less than the price regularly charged for such alcoholic beverage during the same calendar week;
(4)
Sell, offer to sell, or serve to any person or group of persons an unlimited number of alcoholic beverages during any set period of time for a fixed price;
(5)
Sell, offer to sell, or serve alcoholic beverages to any person or group of persons on any one day at prices less than those charged the general public on that day;
(6)
Vary the schedule of prices from day to day or from hour to hour within a single day.
(7)
Brown bagging shall be prohibited except that eating establishments may allow patrons to bring wine in a bottle with the original seal (cork or screw top) in place. The wine must be opened by a certified server and the rules and regulations pertaining to serving customers shall apply. This provision shall also apply to the food service area of hotels, motels and resorts.
(8)
Partially-consumed bottles of wine, whether brought by the patron or purchased at the eating establishment, may be securely resealed by an employee for removal by the patron.

Sec. 4-81. - Prohibited activities.
(a)
The activities prohibited in this section are to be construed as no more stringent than state law in regard to distance requirements for consumption on the premises. No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell:
(1)
Any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises in or within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus. This restriction shall not apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of wine and beer was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application;
(2)
Any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises in or within 100 yards of any church or funeral home;
(4)
As used in this subsection, the term "school building" or "educational building" shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such colleges of this state and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b).
(b)
For purposes of this section, distance shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground to the nearest portion of the church building, funeral home building or to the nearest portion of the school grounds.
(c)
As to any location licensed in the future, if the distance requirements in this section are met at the time of issuance of any license, the subsequent opening and operation of a church, funeral home or school within the distance prohibited in this section shall not prevent the continuance of an existing license or the renewal thereof or the issuance of a new license to any subsequent owner of such property.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to open or to consume all or any part of any type of alcoholic beverage outside in the parking lot or entrance areas or outside of the premises of any establishment selling alcohol for consumption on the premises.
(e)
It shall be unlawful to knowingly sell alcoholic beverages to a person who is intoxicated or to allow intoxicated persons to congregate on the premises, either inside, on any patio, or in any outside area or parking lot, and it shall be the responsibility of the owner of the business and the license holder and their agents and employees to strictly enforce this provision.
(f)
No person knowingly, directly or through another person, shall sell, cause to be sold, or permit any person in such person's employ to sell, serve, or provide any alcoholic beverage to any person under 21 years of age. Licensees and their agents and employees are responsible for checking identification to ensure that a purchaser is at least 21 years of age, and it shall be a violation of this chapter to reasonably fail to check such identification. For the purposes of this subsection, proper identification means any document issued by a government agency that has a photograph of the person and indicates the person's date of birth. These documents shall include, but not be limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or identification card authorized under an act to require the department of public safety to issue identification cards to persons who do not have a motor vehicle driver's license. Proper identification shall not include a birth certificate.
(g)
No person who is under 18 years of age may take orders for, sell, or serve any alcoholic beverage and further subject to the restrictions on preparation and pouring as set forth above.
(h)
There shall be no casino style video gambling or gaming devices, no gambling, betting, games of chance, slot machines or the operation of any scheme for hazarding money or any other thing of value, excluding arcade games, in any place of business licensed under this chapter, or in any room adjoining the same, owned, leased or controlled by a licensee.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-82. - Licenses; application; investigation.
(a)
Any holder of a license issued pursuant to this chapter is required to apply for and obtain an alcoholic beverage license from the state before any sales. County licensees are required to be familiar with and to abide by all applicable state regulations and laws.
(b)
The amount of the license shall be set initially and from time to time amended by resolution of the commissioner and set out in the consolidated fee schedule:
(1)
If the applicant is denied a state license, any license fee shall be refunded, but the cost paid for the investigation and administrative costs shall be retained.
(2)
Any applicant for a license under this chapter who has in existence, at the time of making the new application, an existing license under this chapter shall pay an administrative fee of one-half the regular administrative fee, if same is established since the time of the original issuance of the license, but shall pay a separate full initial license fee for each license.
(3)
When an applicant is making applications for more than one license at the same time, the applicant shall pay only one administrative fee of 125 percent of a normal administrative fee but shall pay a separate fee license fee for each license.
(4)
There shall be an annual license fee for each license payable in advance for the entire year, beginning November 15 and ending November 14 of the following year.
(5)
Licenses shall expire on November 14 of each year. Any licensee must annually meet the requirements set forth in this chapter in order to obtain a renewal of any license. Renewal applications received after November 14 shall incur a late fee of 20 percent of the renewal fee as a late charge penalty.
(6)
The payment of all license fees in full shall be a prerequisite to the issuance of a license unless otherwise provided by resolution of the county.
(7)
The suspension or revocation of any license granted pursuant to this chapter shall not entitle the licensee to a return of any portion of the license fees.
(8)
For new applications submitted after May 15, the license fee shall be reduced by one-half.
(c)
A person doing business at more than one establishment shall take out and pay for a separate license for each place of business.
(d)
Any establishment holding a license issued under this chapter shall at all times during the hours that the establishment is open to the public, be open to inspection by any law enforcement official, any officer of the sheriffs department or any license inspector of the county or to any person designated by the commissioner.
(e)
Alcoholic beverage licenses issued under this chapter shall not be transferable or assignable to new owners, but where there is a change in the ownership of a business, the new owner shall file an application for a new license within 30 days as provided by this chapter and shall pay a new initial license fee. Changes of business interests from one party in interest named in the original application to another party in interest named in the original application shall not be deemed a transfer of a license. However, any change of business interest shall be fully disclosed in writing to the county within 30 days of the change in business interest.
(f)
The application shall be in such form as shall be from time to time prescribed by the commissioner. The application form shall be obtained from the county clerk and filed with the clerk. It shall be subscribed by the applicant under oath, and shall be fully and completely executed. There shall also be attached to the application all required fees in full.
(g)
Any material omission, or untrue or misleading information contained in or left out of an original, or renewal application for any license issued under this chapter shall be unlawful, shall be just cause for a denial thereof, and shall be punishable as a violation of a county ordinance, if any such license has previously been granted under the above circumstances.
(h)
In addition to the provisions of this chapter, a licensee is also charged with responsibility for strictly adhering to any ordinance, resolution or provision of this Code, as well as the statutes and laws of the state and of the United States relating to the operation of the licensed business.
(i)
Any license issued under this chapter shall automatically be null and void where the licensed business has not begun operation or has not operated or been open to the public for four consecutive months.
(j)
An applicant, by filing an application for an alcoholic beverage license, submits to all of the terms of this chapter, and further agrees by the filing of such application, that if a license is issued thereon, he will comply with the terms of this chapter.
(k)
All licenses issued under this chapter shall be subject to the rules and regulations set forth in the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq., as now or hereafter amended, to legalize and control alcoholic beverages, and those prescribed by the state department of revenue. Violation of these statutes shall be violations of this chapter.
(l)
The license issued pursuant to this chapter shall be in such a form as shall be adopted by the commissioner.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-83. - Enforcement.
(a)
The commissioner shall be responsible for the enforcement of this section.
(b)
The commissioner shall be authorized to suspend a license previously granted under this chapter. If the commissioner seeks to suspend a license, it shall give written notification to the applicant of such action and such notice shall contain the specifics of the violation and shall be served upon the licensee at least ten days prior to a hearing. The licensee shall be given at least ten days' written notice of the time and place of the hearing.
(c)
The commissioner, if he finds cause, shall be authorized to suspend a license in the event of any one or more of the following:
(1)
Any licensee gave false or misleading information in the original application process;
(2)
Any licensee has knowingly allowed consumption, use, or sale of controlled substances, as defined under state law, on the premises to any adult or minor;
(3)
Any licensee has knowingly allowed the violation of this Code or a violation of any criminal law of the state to occur on the premises;
(4)
Violation of any of the provisions of this chapter by the holder of a license issued lander this chapter or his agents or employees, whether compensated or not;
(5)
Any licensee has been convicted of any drug-, alcohol- or firearms-related crime by the state or the county regarding an offense which was committed on the premises or which would otherwise violate the provisions of this chapter;
(6)
Any licensee fails to pay any fee, tax, fine or other amount of money due to the county under this chapter or any other ordinance provision of the county;
(7)
The performance of any act prohibited by this chapter or the failure to perform any act required by this chapter, as well as the violation of any law, state or federal, relating to the business of the licensee. If such act, omission or violation is done by an agent, servant, employee, or officer of the licensee, whether paid or unpaid, the lack of knowledge on the part of the licensee or the lack of authorization for such act or omission or violation shall be no defense;
(8)
The entry of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere, or the conviction of any licensee with respect to a charge of violation of any of the laws of the United States or any state relating to alcohol, drugs, firearms, or any crime, whether a felony or not, involving moral turpitude;
(9)
The occurrence on two or more occasions within any 12-month period of fights, disorderly conduct, drunkenness, breach of the peace, and other similar conduct on the premises, whether such conduct is committed by the licensee or by customers or others;
(10)
Sales of alcoholic beverages to any person under 21 years of age; or
(11)
Any other act or omission with respect to the operation of a business licensed under this chapter which the commissioner shall find to be contrary to the public interest, or which shall render the licensee or the business location unfit for the continued operation of the business.
(d)
In the event the commissioner shall suspend any license under this chapter, the suspension shall be for a period of not less than one day, nor more than 45 days, within the discretion of the county. The Commissioner shall forward to the county clerk the establishment's license, which shall be retained as revoked by the clerk until final adjudication of the suspension of the license by the commissioner.
(e)
Except for sales to persons under 21 years of age, the licensee shall be authorized to continue its business operations until the date of the hearing scheduled in accordance with this chapter.
(1)
Law enforcement may immediately revoke a license if it is determined by law enforcement that underage persons are being provided alcohol or if there is a disturbance of the peace or any other danger to the health, safety or welfare of the public, and the license shall be retrieved by the Sheriff and the alcoholic beverages on the premises inventoried and secured by the law enforcement officials to prevent their sale until a hearing is conducted by the commissioner and all state, federal, and county court charges, if any, have been resolved. The secured alcoholic beverages shall be dealt with in accordance with state law.
(2)
No applicant may apply for a license during any period of suspension or revocation.
(3)
The commissioner shall have the right to make such inquiry or investigation as it may find to be reasonably necessary to determine compliance with this chapter. Such investigation may consist, among other actions, of calling licensees for examination under oath, obtaining evidence under oath from other persons; their procurement of documents and records, including records of the licensee, and inspection and examination of records and documents from whatever source obtainable.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-84. - Hearing; basis of determination.
Whenever any alcoholic beverage license issued under this chapter has been revoked or suspended for cause shown, there shall be a hearing before the commissioner on the matter of which at least ten days' written notice shall be given to the licensee in person, by certified mail or by service by county law enforcement personnel.
(1)
Such notice shall specify the time, place and purpose of the hearing, and a statement of the charges upon which such hearing shall be held. At such hearing, the licensee shall have the right to appear in person and by attorney, and both the county attorney or his designee, and the licensee shall have the right to present evidence under oath, introduce documentary evidence, cross-examine witnesses and generally present evidence of violation of this chapter or absence thereof. The hearing shall be conducted before the commissioner. However, the commissioner reserves the right to appointment of a hearing officer to conduct the hearing. Said hearing officer shall preside over the proceedings and make rulings on issues of evidence and/or procedure and hear evidence. The commissioner may attend the hearing and hear all evidence and shall be the party to make the final determinations required.
(2)
Whether a license shall be suspended or revoked, and if suspended, for what period of time, shall be determined by the commissioner after consideration of the evidence in the case and in accordance with the commissioner finding as to the severity of the offense. Upon a determination of violation of this chapter by the commissioner, the commissioner may, in his discretion:
a.
Suspension of the license for a period of time up to one year; and/or
b.
Revocation of the license;
The decision of the commissioner shall be conclusive.
(3)
Any person aggrieved by act or omission of the commissioner with respect to its proceedings under this chapter including the suspension or revocation of a license shall have the right to appeal from the decision of the commissioner. Appeals shall be to the superior court of the county, filed within 30 days from the final action of the commissioner. If the applicant/licensee does not file an appeal from any decision of the commissioner, as provided in this section, the decision of the commissioner shall be final.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-85. - No new license to be issued after revocation.
When a license has been revoked under the provisions of this chapter, no application for a new alcoholic beverage license for the same location will be received for a period of 12 months, and no application for a new license from the licensee involved shall be received for a period of 24 months.
( Ord. of 3-19-2019(1) )
Sec. 4-86. - Excise taxes.
(a)
Every purchaser of distilled spirits by the drink shall be liable for a tax thereon at the rate of three percent of the retail price or charge for such drink. Such taxes shall be collected by the licensee licensed under this article, and such licensee shall remit the same to the county on or before the twentieth day of the succeeding month along with a summary of the licensee's gross sales derived from the sale of distilled spirits by the drink. Gross sales shall include all credit card sales and shall be reported, and taxes collected thereon shall be submitted to the county to the same extent as required of cash sales. Each licensee shall be allowed a deduction equal to that rate authorized for deductions from state tax under part V of the Georgia Retailer's and Consumer's Sales and Use Tax Act, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-50, as now written or hereafter amended provided that the tax is not delinquent at the time of payment. It shall be the duty of every such licensee required to make a report and pay any tax levied pursuant to this article, to keep and preserve suitable records of the sales taxable pursuant to this article, and such other books or accounts as may be necessary to determine the amount of tax due. It shall be the duty of every licensee to keep and preserve such records for a period of three years.
(b)
In addition to all other taxes or license fees imposed on the sale of malt beverages or wine in the unincorporated areas of the county, there is hereby levied and imposed the following excise taxes:
(1)
Upon the sale of any beer or malt beverages there is imposed an excise tax of:
1.
$0.0333 per 8-ounce container;
2.
$0.05 per 12-ounce container;
3.
$.0667 per 16-ounce container;
4.
$0.1333 per 32-ounce container.
(2)
Upon the sale of any wine there is imposed an excise tax of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(c)
No county excise tax shall be imposed, levied, or collected in any portion of a county in which a municipality within the county is imposing the same tax.
(d)
The excise taxes imposed may be paid by the wholesale dealers to the county by the twentieth day of each month based upon the units of beer and wine sold during the previous month. The wholesale dealers shall keep true and correct records based upon units of beer and wine sold to each licensed retail dealer within the unincorporated areas of the county shall render a sworn statement of the records accompanying the monthly report to the county. Any tax remaining unpaid at the expiration of 15 days from the due date shall be delinquent. The collections and accounting of excise taxes by wholesale dealers on behalf of licensee does not relieve the licensee of their duties and responsibilities under this division.
(e)
Wholesalers collecting excise taxes shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and paying the amount due, if the amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate authorized for deductions from state tax under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-50.
(f)
Determination of deficiencies.
(1)
If the county has cause to believe that the return or returns of the tax or the amount of the tax required to be paid to the county by any person is not proper, it may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information that is within or may come into its possession or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more monthly period(s).
(2)
The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of 0.75 percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the due date of taxes.
(3)
The county shall give to the licensee or wholesaler written notice of its determination regarding a deficiency. The notice may be served personally or by mail, if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the licensee at his address as it appears in the records of the county. Service by mail is complete when mailed with a certificate of service stamped thereon and a copy retained in county files or when delivered by certified mail with a receipt signed by the addressee.
(4)
Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within 30 days after the tenth day of the month following the calendar month for which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within 30 days after the return is filed, whichever period should last expire.
(g)
Determination if no return made.
(1)
If any licensee or wholesaler fails to make a return, the county shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the licensee, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total sales in this county which are subject to the tax. The estimate shall be made for the period or periods in respect to which the licensee failed to make the return and shall be based upon any information which is in possession of or may come into the possession of the county official. Written notice shall be given in the manner prescribed above.
(2)
The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of 0.75 percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the 20th day of the month following the calendar month for which the amount or any portion thereof would have been returned, until the date of payment.
(h)
Any licensee or wholesaler which fails to pay the tax herein imposed or fails to pay any amount of the tax required to be collected and paid to the county within the time required shall pay a penalty of 25 percent of the tax owed in addition to interest as set forth above.
(i)
The tax levied by this section may be enforced by execution in the same manner as other taxes of the county, and in addition, any failure of payment of such tax shall be grounds for revocation or refusal of the license of the delinquent taxpayer. The governing authority shall administer and enforce the provisions of this section for the collection of the tax herein imposed, and in so doing shall have the power to:
(1)
Examine, or authorize the examination of, books, papers, records financial reports, equipment, and other facilities of any person subject to the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any report made, or if no report is made by the operator, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid;
(2)
Require the filing of reports by any person or persons having possession or custody of information relating to the tax herein levied; and
(3)
Allow a credit on any amount due and payable from persons who paid the tax herein levied but who were erroneously or illegally subjected thereto.

DIVISION 4. - SALE FOR CONSUMPTION ON PREMISES BY NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS

Sec. 4-100. - Type of sale.
Malt beverages and wine sold under a license issued by the authority hereof shall be for on-the-premises consumption only.
(Res. of 2-14-1985, § I)
Sec. 4-101. - Licensee qualifications.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Fixed salary means the amount of compensation paid any member, officer, agent, or employee of the organization as may be fixed for him by its members at a prior annual meeting or by the governing body out of the general revenue of the organization and shall not include any commission on any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
The licensee must be a bona fide nonprofit organization, the same being defined as any nonprofit organization organized under the laws of the state that:
(1)
Has been in existence for at least 30 days prior to the filling of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this division;
(2)
Has at least 50 regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment and is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing and serving meals for its members and guests; and
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
(Res. of 2-14-1985, § II)
Sec. 4-102. - Designation of management personnel required.
The licensee shall designate no less than two members who shall be responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted, and who shall not have been convicted of a felony within four years of the date of the application, or any violation of the laws of the state, or any other state, relating to the sale or use of alcoholic beverages.
(Res. of 2-14-1985, § II)
Sec. 4-103. - Sworn statement of qualifications.
The applicant shall make a sworn statement of its qualifications according to sections 4-101 and 4-102 and shall place the same on file with the county commissioner before any license shall be issued. In addition, the county commissioner may require the filing by the applicant of a copy of its Articles of Incorporation issued by the Secretary of State; IRS nonprofit determination letter; charter, or other documentary evidence showing that such organization is a nonprofit organization organized under the laws of the state.
(Res. of 2-14-1985, § II)
Sec. 4-104. - Qualified location.
(a)
A licensee may conduct the business of retail sale of malt beverages and wine for consumption on the premises only at a qualified location within the unincorporated limits of the county. In order to be qualified, such location shall not be within 100 yards of any church building, funeral home, school building, school grounds, or college campus.
(b)
For the purpose of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall measure from the property line of the tract on which is located the business regulated under this article to the property line of the tract on which is located a church building, funeral home, school building, school ground or college campus.
(Res. of 2-14-1985, § III)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 4-105. - No alcoholic beverages permitted outside licensed premises.
No nonprofit establishment licensed under this division authorizing the sale of malt beverages and wine at retail and for consumption on the premises or any agent or employee of the licensee, shall allow beer or wine to be taken outdoors or off the licensed premises.
(Res. of 2-14-1985, § V)
Sec. 4-106. - Restriction on sale of certain merchandise.
No nonprofit establishment licensed under this division authorizing the sale of malt beverages and wine at retail and for consumption on the premises or any agent or employee of the licensee, shall allow beer or wine to be taken outdoors or off the licensed premises.
(Res. of 2-14-1985, § V)
