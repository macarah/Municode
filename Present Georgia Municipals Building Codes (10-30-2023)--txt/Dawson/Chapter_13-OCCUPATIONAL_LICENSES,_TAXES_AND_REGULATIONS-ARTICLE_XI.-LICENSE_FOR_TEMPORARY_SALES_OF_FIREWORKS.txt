ARTICLE XI. - LICENSE FOR TEMPORARY SALES OF FIREWORKS

Sec. 13-212. - Definitions.
Consumer fireworks: Any small fireworks devices containing restricted amounts of pyrotechnic composition, designed primarily to produce visible or audible effects by combustion, that comply with the construction, chemical composition, and labeling regulations of the United States Consumer Product Safety Commission as provided for in Parts 1500 and 1507 of Title 16 of the Code of Federal Regulations, the United States Department of Transportation as provided for in Part 172 of Title 49 of the Code of Federal Regulations, and the American Pyrotechnics Association as provided for in the 2001 American Pyrotechnics Association Standard 87-1, and additionally shall mean Roman candles.
Distributor: Any person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership which sells consumer fireworks.
Fireworks: Any combustible or explosive composition or any substance or combination of substances or article prepared for the purpose of producing a visible or audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration, or detonation, including blank cartridges, firecrackers, torpedos, skyrockets, bombs, sparklers, and other combustibles and explosives of like construction, as well as articles containing any explosive or flammable compound and tablets and other devices containing an explosive substance.
Pyrotechnics: Fireworks.
Proper identification: Any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person or such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth and includes without being limited to a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or other identification card authorized by the State of Georgia.
Temporary shall be a period of time not exceeding two (2) months in a calendar year and not exceeding thirty (30) days in a row in any one (1) period.
(Ord. No. 2015-10, § 1(Exh. A), 11-12-15)
Sec. 13-213. - License required.
Any business selling consumer fireworks from a temporary consumer fireworks retail sales stand must apply for a license from the City of Dawson. The license fee for a retail fireworks temporary stand license shall be five hundred dollars ($500.00) per each stand within the city.
(a)
Requirements for license.
(1)
An applicant for a fireworks retail license from the City of Dawson shall offer proof that the applicant maintains public liability and product liability insurance with minimum coverage limits of two million dollars ($2,000,000.00) to cover any loses, damages or injuries that might ensue to persons and property as a result of the sale of consumer fireworks.
(2)
The applicant must demonstrate that they have obtained a license from the Georgia Safety Fire Commissioner. The license fee for a retail fireworks license shall be five hundred dollars ($500.00) per location within the city.
(3)
A license will only be granted to applicants who agree to operate their stand in accordance with the National Fire Protection Act Provision 1124.
(b)
Locations. A stand shall only be permitted to be located within one thousand (1,000) feet of either a fire hydrant or a fire department connection belonging to the City of Dawson unless the fire chief of the City of Dawson provides in writing that the stand may operate further than one thousand (1,000) feet from a fire hydrant or other fire department connection.
(c)
Number of stands permitted.
(1)
When a distributor is a retail chain license by the Safety Fire Commission pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-10-5.1(d), the retailer may operate two (2) temporary consumer firework stands for every retail chain store located in the county.
(2)
If at the time of the application for a stand, no distributors licensed pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-10-5.1(b) or O.C.G.A. § 25-10-5.1(d) are doing business in Terrell County, any distributor licensed pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-10-5.1(b) or O.C.G.A. § 25-10-5.1(d) that maintains a retail sales facility within seventy-five (75) miles of the border of Terrell County, may choose to place one (1) of their two (2) retail stands in Terrell County.
(3)
A fireworks distributor who is not a retailer may operate two (2) retail stands for each license obtained from the safety fire commissioner.
(4)
Any licensee shall notify the city clerk and the fire chief of the location of the temporary retail stand no later than ten (10) days prior to the opening of the stand so that the fire chief can inspect the hydrant or fire connection located near or closest to the location of the stand in ensure its proper functioning.
(d)
The sale of fireworks may only be conducted through in person face to face sales. Any licensee selling fireworks within the City of Dawson shall ensure that all customers purchasing fireworks from this stand show proper identification verifying that they are over the age of eighteen (18).
(Ord. No. 2015-10, § 1(Exh. A), 11-12-15)
