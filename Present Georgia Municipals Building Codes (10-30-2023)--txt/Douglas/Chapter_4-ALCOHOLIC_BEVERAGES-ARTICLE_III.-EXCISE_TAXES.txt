ARTICLE III. - EXCISE TAXES[3]

Sec. 4-263. - License expiration upon inability or failure to pay.
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, every license and renewal thereof issued hereunder shall expire and be of no further force or effect in case of bankruptcy, receivership, levy of process, or failure to promptly account for and pay the excise tax levied on sales of alcoholic beverages within the city, state or United States.
(Code 1978, § 5-156; Code 1993, pt. II, § 6-191)
Sec. 4-264. - Excise tax on malt beverages.
For each license year commencing January 1 and ending December 31, there is hereby levied and imposed upon each retail and wholesale malt beverage dealer an excise tax in an amount equaling the total of $0.05 per 12 ounces. The excise tax on tap or draft malt beverages shall be in the sum of $6.00 for each container sold as hereinafter specified, containing not more than 15½ gallons and at a like rate for fractional parts where the beverage is sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, such beverage being commonly known as tap or draft beer.
(Code 1978, § 5-157; Code 1993, pt. II, § 6-192)
Sec. 4-265. - Excise tax on wine.
In addition to any license fees contained within this chapter or other laws and ordinances of the city, there is hereby levied an excise tax computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter which shall be paid to the city on all wine sold, displayed or stored in the city. Such tax shall be paid to the city by the wholesale distributor on all wine sold to licensees for the sale of wine in the city as follows: Each wholesale distributor selling, shipping or in any way delivering wine to any such licensee shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery and shall remit the same, together with a summary of all deliveries, to each licensee on or before the 20th day of the month following. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any such licensee to possess, own, hold, store, display or sell any wine on which such tax has not been paid. The wholesaler shall make the reports as required in this section.
(Code 1978, § 5-158; Code 1993, pt. II, § 6-193)
Sec. 4-266. - Excise tax on distilled spirits.
(a)
Levied. In addition to the annual license fee charged for the issuance of a Class II or III license, there is hereby assessed an excise tax in the amount of $0.22 per liter of distilled spirits and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter of distilled spirits sold to retail distilled spirits (Class II and Class III) licensees in the city by the wholesale distributor or held for sale by Class II and III retailers and remitted monthly by the wholesale (Class I) licensee. This subsection does not apply to fortified wines.
(b)
Class I licensee's reports; payment required. Every Class I licensee (wholesaler) selling packaged distilled spirits to businesses (Class II and Class III licensees) within the city shall file with the city manager a copy of each monthly report and a copy of invoices reflecting sales of distilled spirits by such wholesale distributors within the city.
(c)
Payment. The wholesale distributor (Class I licensee) shall file the report required in subsection (b) of this section on or before the 15th day of each month after the last day of each month for which report is made, and the tax due shall be paid at the time the report is filed.
(Code 1978, § 5-159; Code 1993, pt. II, § 6-194)
Sec. 4-267. - Failure to report, pay excise tax.
The failure of any Class I, II or III licensee to timely report and pay the excise tax enumerated in this article shall result, after hearing before the board of commissioners, in the suspension for up to 90 days of the licensee's alcoholic beverage license or revocation of same in the discretion of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 5-160; Code 1993, pt. II, § 6-195)
Sec. 4-268. - Storage of alcoholic beverages.
No Class I, II or III licensee shall keep any alcoholic beverages stored in any bonded or other type warehouse in the city, nor shall he enter into any type of arrangement whereby alcoholic beverages ordered by him are stored for him by any licensed wholesaler. A licensee shall keep no inventory or stock of alcoholic beverages at any place except his licensed place of business, and within his licensed place of business, his storage space for alcoholic beverages shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which he is licensed to do business.
(Code 1978, § 5-161; Code 1993, pt. II, § 6-196)
Sec. 4-269. - Minimum container size, sale in unbroken packages, Class III licensee.
No Class III licensee may purchase distilled spirits in containers smaller than one-fifth gallon. The sale of alcoholic beverages for any consumption on the premises of the licensee in unbroken packages or in any quantity for other than consumption on the premises is expressly prohibited.
(Code 1978, § 5-162; Code 1993, pt. II, § 6-197)
