ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Interfering with property of systems.
It shall be unlawful for any unauthorized person to interfere with or have anything to do with the city wells, reservoirs, treatment plants, mains or other equipment related to the city water supply, or any equipment, pipes, wires, conduits, installations, plants or property of the city gas, electrical or sewer systems.
(Code 1978, § 23-2; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-1)
State Law reference— Damaging, injuring or interfering with property of public utility companies, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25.
Sec. 38-2. - Diverting electricity, gas or water.
(a)
No person shall make any connection to any unmetered pipe, main or wire containing water, gas or electricity from the city system.
(b)
Proof that any appliance, tools, devices or instruments of any kind have been used to improperly pass current, gas or water around the meter so as to prevent same from duly registering shall constitute prima facie evidence of the committing or the intent to commit the acts described in this section.
(Code 1978, § 23-3; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-2)
State Law reference— Damaging, injuring or interfering with property of public utility companies, municipalities or political subdivisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25.
Sec. 38-3. - Laying conduits, etc., near center of street.
It shall be unlawful for any person to lay new mains for water, gas or sewerage, or conduit for any purpose, in any street, alley or public way, within four feet on each side of the centerline of any street, unless by and with the permission of the city manager.
(Code 1978, § 21-23; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-3)
Sec. 38-4. - Supervision of extensions to systems.
The appropriate city departments will make, or supervise, all extensions of mains, wires, conduits, etc., in the public streets and roads, both inside and outside the city, of any city utility.
(Code 1978, § 23-4; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-4)
