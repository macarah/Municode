Sec. 34-1. - County marshal.
(a)
There is hereby created the position of county marshal.
(b)
The county marshal, personally or by and through designees of the county marshal, is charged with authority to enforce all ordinances, resolutions, rules and regulations of the county, by the use of county citations, and to return such violations to the magistrate's court of the county for proper trial and sentencing, and to otherwise enforce county ordinances and perform the duties of marshals and constables as otherwise provided by law.
(Ord. No. 91-4, §§ 1—3, 6-28-91; Ord. No. 96-12, 8-13-96; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, exh. A, 8-2-16)
Editor's note— Ordinance No. 96-12, adopted August 13, 1996, amended § 34-1 to read as herein follows. Formerly, such section pertained to county police force.
Sec. 34-2. - Duties and responsibilities of the county marshal.
(a)
Duties and responsibilities. The primary duties and responsibilities of the county marshal shall be as follows:
(1)
To enforce all ordinances enacted by the Cherokee County Board of Commissioners;
(2)
To issue warnings, notices of ordinance violation, and citations for court appearances for the violation of ordinances;
(3)
To investigate and collect evidence of these ordinance violations;
(4)
To respond to complaints of alleged ordinance violations;
(5)
To appear and testify in court;
(6)
To do any follow-up work that may be necessary;
(7)
To ensure proper collection of fines;
(8)
To coordinate enforcement efforts with the agencies, departments and divisions of Cherokee County as may be deemed necessary; and
(9)
Perform any other lawful duties required by the county manager and/or the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 2016-O-005, exh. A, 8-2-16)
