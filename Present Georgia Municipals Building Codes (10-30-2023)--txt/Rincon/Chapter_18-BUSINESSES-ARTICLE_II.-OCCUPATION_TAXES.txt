ARTICLE II. - OCCUPATION TAXES[2]

Sec. 18-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
Dominant line means the type of business, within a multiple-line business, from which the greatest amount of income is derived.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form IRS W-2 but not a form IRS 1099.
Location or office means any structure or vehicle where a business, profession or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project, or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession or business for revenue raising purposes.
Occupation tax certificate may be referred to as a certificate, tax, permit, or license.
Practitioner of profession or practitioner of occupation means one who by state law requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation. Practitioners of professions and occupations shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-32. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied in this article is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5—48-13-28. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-33. - Penalty for violation of article.
(a)
Any violation of this article shall be unlawful and shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 and/or imprisonment in the city prison or in the county jail and/or work and labor on the streets or public works of the city, whether within or without the corporate limits, not exceeding six months, or both a fine and sentence of imprisonment and labor and all sentences may be in the alternative and fines may be imposed with the alternative of sentence to imprisonment and labor if the fines are not paid. Each day any violation of this Code or of any ordinance shall continue shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
The judge of the municipal court shall have the power and authority to:
(1)
Impose upon persons convicted in the municipal court the fines provided for in this article, or as otherwise provided by law, with the alternative of other punishment allowed by law, if such fines are not paid;
(2)
Sentence such person to community service work; or
(3)
Impose a sentence consisting of any combination of the penalties provided for in this section.
(c)
The judge of the municipal court shall have full power and authority to declare the forfeiture of bonds given by offenders for their appearance before the municipal court upon the offender's failure to appear as provided for in such bond. The procedure for the forfeiture of such bonds shall be as is provided for the forfeiture of bonds and recognizance set forth in O.C.G.A. § 17-6-70 et seq.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-34. - Occupation tax required for business dealings in the city.
For the year 1996 and succeeding years thereafter, each person engaged in any business, trade, profession or occupation in the city, whether with a location in the city, or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in the state exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax for such business, trade, profession or occupation. Such tax, or evidence of payment of such tax, and any applicable registration, shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in the city. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the city, such business tax, or evidence of payment of such business tax, and any applicable registration, shall be shown to the revenue collection officer or this officer's deputies or to any police officer of the city upon request.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-35. - Administrative fee structure; occupation tax structure.
(a)
Administrative fee. A non-prorated, nonrefundable administrative fee as provided in the schedule of fees and charges shall be required on all business and occupation tax accounts for the initial startup, renewal, or reopening of those accounts, notwithstanding the date of application.
(b)
Payment. Any business applying or registering under this article for initial startup, renewal, or reopening on or after July 1 of any tax year shall pay the prorated amount of one-half of the amount prescribed as the annual amount to be paid as occupation tax under the terms of this article. The administrative fee is not prorated.
(c)
Commencement of business activities. Any person or entity that commences business on or after Feb 1 of each year shall pay the occupation tax within 30 days following the commencement of the business. After 90 days, all fees, penalties, and interest for late payments shall apply.
(d)
Information required. Any business or practitioner of profession or occupation applying, registering or paying under this article shall provide to the city such information as may be required by the city regarding the site of any location or office of, and payment of any occupation taxes or other fees to other local governments by, such business or practitioner of a profession or occupation.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-36. - Levied; restrictions.
(a)
Levy. An occupation tax shall be levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices within the corporate limits of the city and/or upon the applicable out-of-state businesses with no location or office in the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, based upon the number of employees of the business or practitioner, according to the occupation tax table per employee in subsection (b) of this section.
(b)
Rate and amount. The tax rate and amount determined by number of employees for each business, trade, profession or occupation is provided in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(c)
Computation of employee numbers. The number of employees of the business or practitioner shall be computed on a full-time position basis or full-time position equivalent basis; provided, however, that for purposes of this computation an employee who works 40 hours or more weekly shall be considered a full-time employee, and that the average weekly hours of employees who work less than 40 hours weekly shall be added, and such sum shall be divided by 40 to produce full-time position equivalents; and provided further that the occupation tax table per employee shall apply to the number of full-time basis employees and full-time equivalent basis employees only.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-37. - Paying tax of business with no location in the state.
Registration and assessment of an occupation tax is hereby imposed on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the state if the business' largest dollar volume of business in the state is in the city and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of the city for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of the city.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-38. - Each line of business to be identified on business registration.
The business registration of each business operated in the city shall identify the dominant line of business that the business conducts.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-39. - Number of businesses considered to be operating in the city.
Where a person conducts business at more than one fixed location, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of occupation tax.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-40. - Prohibitions; restrictions; exemptions.
(a)
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, the state, a municipality or county of the state, instrumentalities of the United States, the state, or a municipality or county of the state, shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that practice.
(b)
No payment of any kind or by whatever name shall be required of a business or practitioner for the cost of ascertaining whether such a business or practitioner has paid any occupation tax to another local government.
(c)
No occupation tax or administrative fee shall be levied upon any state or local authority or nonprofit organization, which has been exempted by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS).
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-41. - Due date of tax; penalties; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
Such registration and occupation tax and administrative fee, if any, shall be payable January 1 of each year and shall, if not paid by January 31 of each year, be subject to penalties for delinquency as prescribed in this article.
(b)
On any new profession, trade or calling begun in the city in 1996 or succeeding years thereafter, the registration and tax and administrative fee, if any, shall be delinquent if not obtained immediately upon beginning the business, and, as to any such delinquency, a flat fee penalty as provided in the schedule of fees and charges shall be added thereto and imposed upon the applicant.
(c)
Any such occupation tax due and payable January 1 of any year under this article, except for a new business or practitioner beginning business on that date, and not paid by January 31 of such year, shall be delinquent and shall, at the close of business on January 31 of such year, be subject to an administrative fee, if any. A ten percent penalty shall be assessed when the occupation tax remains due and unpaid for 90 days from the due date of the tax.
(d)
Interest on any delinquency of any occupation tax, administrative fee, and penalty under this article shall be imposed at the rate of 1.5 percent per month.
(e)
If any person whose duty it is to obtain a registration shall, after such registration or occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in the city, any of the kind of profession, trade or calling subject to this article without having first obtained the registration, such person shall be in violation of this article and, upon conviction, be sentenced to pay the city all amounts due under this article and be punished as provided in section 18-33. The mayor and council must authorize the issuance of a summons for any violation of this article.
(f)
In addition to other remedies provided in this section, the city manager, or other appropriate official performing the functions of a marshal, may proceed to collect in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions, after mayor and council authorizes the collection of past due license fees.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-42. - Taxation of employees within jurisdiction.
Only employees who work within the jurisdiction of the city will be counted for taxing purposes.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-43. - Exemption on grounds that business is operated for charitable purpose.
No business on which a business registration or occupation tax is levied by this article shall be exempt from the registration or tax on the grounds that such business is operated for a charitable purpose (including all expenditures required for the function of the organization), unless 100 percent of the entire proceeds from the business is devoted to such purpose.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-44. - Evidence of state registration required if applicable; display.
(a)
Each person who is licensed by the secretary of state pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 43 shall provide evidence of proper and current state licensure before the city registration may be issued.
(b)
Each person who is licensed by the state shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the licensee's place of business and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-45. - Evidence of qualification required, if applicable.
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of a city business registration, show evidence that such requirements have been met.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-46. - Liability of officers and agents; registration required; failure to obtain.
All persons subject to the occupation tax levied pursuant to this article shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for such business as described in this article, and in default thereof all such persons and the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as are persons who fail to obtain a registration under this article. Any person transacting, or offering to transact in the city, any such business, trade, profession or occupation, or the officer or the agent of such person, without first having so obtained such registration, shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 18-33. A summons can be issued once authorized by mayor and council.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-47. - Businesses not covered by this article.
The following businesses are not covered by the provisions of this article but may be assessed an occupation tax or other type of tax pursuant to the provisions of other general laws of the state or by local law:
(1)
Those businesses regulated by the state public service commission.
(2)
Those electrical service businesses organized under O.C.G.A. tit. 46, ch. 3.
(3)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including agribusiness.
(4)
Cooperative marketing associations governed by O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105.
(5)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.
(6)
Motor common carriers governed by O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15.
(7)
Those businesses governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355, that purchase carload lots of guano, meats, meal, flour, bran, cottonseed, or cottonseed meal and hulls.
(8)
Those businesses involving agricultural products and livestock raised in the state governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356.
(9)
Depository financial institutions governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(10)
Facilities operated by a charitable trust governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-55.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-48. - Inspection of business records.
(a)
City staff shall send a certified letter to the business owner as notification to the business owner to appear before mayor and council for a determination of what business records shall be inspected. Mayor and council will hear the request made by the city official, and the business owner, or his representative or agent, will have an opportunity to offer comments.
(b)
Once authorized by mayor and council, the city manager or other duly authorized collection official of the city, through its officers, agents, employees or representatives, may inspect the books of the business or person for which the returns are made. Such inspecting officer shall have the right to inspect the books or records for the business for which the return was made in the city, and, upon demand of the inspecting officer, such books or records shall be submitted for inspection by a representative of the city within 30 days. Failure of submission of such books or records within 30 days shall be grounds for revocation of the tax registration currently existing to do business within the city. If, after examination of the books or records, it is determined that a deficiency occurs as a result of under reporting, a penalty of one percent per month times the amount deficient will be assessed for the period of delinquency.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-49. - Requirement of public hearing.
(a)
The city council shall conduct at least one public hearing before adopting any ordinance or resolution regarding the occupation tax.
(b)
In any year when revenue from occupation taxes is greater than revenue from occupation taxes for the preceding year, one or more public hearings shall be held as a part of the process of determining how to use the additional revenue.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)

Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention of this article that the specific shall control. In addition, thereto, Georgia Statutory law shall control over all general and specific provisions of the Rincon Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-52. - Home occupations and home business offices.
(a)
Purpose and intent. If permitted in a zoning district, a home occupation must comply with the following requirements:
(1)
Is carried on by the owner, or with the owner's permission;
(2)
Shall be clearly incidental and secondary to the use of the dwelling unit for residential purposes;
(3)
Utilizes not more than 20 percent of the total floor area of the total building area on parcel;
(4)
Involves the sale or offering for sale of articles produced or assembled on the premises or products not produced on site, but, related and accessory to the service provided;
(5)
Creates no disturbing or offensive noise, vibration, smoke, dust, odor, heat, glare, traffic hazard, unhealthy or unsightly condition;
(6)
No material, other than business supplies, may be stored on site;
(7)
No outdoor storage allowed at the residence of materials, goods, articles, or items related to the home occupation;
(8)
No work, repairing, assembly, or manufacture of merchandise, vehicles, motorcycles, or carts shall be conducted outside the residential dwelling;
(9)
No goods, materials, supplies, or items of any kind, can be delivered whether to or from the premises in connection with the home occupation except in a passenger automobile or by a residential express mail company (UPS, FedEx, etc.). No deliveries to or from the premises shall be made by tractor trailer or semi-truck, or as allowed by other City of Rincon ordinances; and
(10)
No hazardous material, whatsoever, shall be stored at the location.
(b)
Parking.
(1)
Shall not generate pedestrian or vehicular traffic or demand for parking.
(2)
No more than one commercial vehicle used in the conduction of the business may be parked at the home location.
(3)
There shall be no use of parking or storage of tractor trailers, vehicles over 10,000 pounds, heavy equipment, or pull-type trailers, greater than 18 feet in length, related to the home occupation.
(c)
Appearance and character of neighborhood. Produces no alteration or change in the character or exterior or change in the principal building from that of a residential dwelling;
(d)
Signage. Displays no sign or external indication of the home occupation.
(e)
Filing procedure.
(1)
Application for a home business office or home occupation shall be filed with the building and zoning department upon forms furnished by the City of Rincon. Home occupations require an annual fee and inspection to obtain an occupation tax certificate for a home occupation. Home occupation permits may be renewed as long as the operation follows the home occupation requirements.
(2)
By submitting an application for a home occupation tax certificate or renewal, the applicant hereby agrees to allow the building and zoning department access to the applicant's residence to conduct the required inspection upon 48 hours notification of the intended inspection date and time. The applicant also agrees to allow building and zoning department access to their residence to investigate alleged violations of this article upon 48 hours notification of the intended inspection date and time.
(f)
Permit and fees.
(1)
The home occupation applicant shall pay the annual City of Rincon Occupation Tax.
(2)
All occupational taxes shall be valid from January 1 until December 31.
(g)
Existing legal, nonconforming home occupation.
(1)
Existing, nonconforming home occupations, which existed prior to January 1, 2015, shall be subject to renewal every year and shall not be expanded or the use, as stated in the permit, changed.
(2)
A change in the use, ownership, or type of home occupation will conform to all of the requirements in this chapter.
(3)
If a nonconforming home occupation fails to obtain a tax certificate, then in that event, the nonconforming home occupation must meet the requirements of this article before a tax certificate will be granted.
(h)
Exceptions.
(1)
This chapter shall not apply to residential "garage sales" or "yard sales" not related to the home occupation.
(2)
This chapter shall not apply to in-home purchasing clubs or organizations (e.g., Amway, Watkins, Tupperware, etc.).
(3)
This chapter shall not apply to the occasional sale of personal property.
(i)
Prohibited uses. The following uses are not permitted as home occupations:
(1)
Vehicle and/or body and fender repair.
(2)
Greenhouse or commercial nursery.
(3)
Food handling, processing or packing of food, or production of food items.
(4)
Medical or dental lab.
(5)
Day care, for more than six minors.
(6)
Adult daycare centers.
(7)
Restaurants.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
Sec. 18-53. - Enforcement.
(a)
The Mayor and Council of the City of Rincon, or their designee, shall have the authority to enforce this chapter article herein.
(b)
Mayor and council shall be authorized to suspend an occupation tax certificate if any provision of the Rincon Code of Ordinances, or the purpose and intent of this article, has been violated for a period of five business days after being notified of the violation.
(c)
Upon a suspension of an occupation tax certificate, a revocation hearing before city council shall be scheduled within 30 days of the date of the suspension. The licensee shall be notified of the hearing date by mailing a notice by first class U.S. mail to the address listed on the license application. An advertisement in the legal organ of the county shall be placed notifying the licensee, any interested parties, and the public of the revocation hearing, date, time, and place. The hearing shall be informal in nature, such that the rules of evidence shall be relaxed and that hearsay evidence shall be allowed at the discretion of the city council.
(d)
Deviation from the requirements of a permitted occupation tax certificate will result in the revocation of all applicable permits associated with the use and its operation and be punished as provided in section 18-33 of the City of Rincon Code of Ordinances once approved by mayor and council.
(e)
It shall be unlawful to operate a business without first obtaining an occupation tax certificate. Violation of this code section shall be punished as provided in section 18-33 of the City of Rincon Code of Ordinances once approved by mayor and council. Each day of operation without a tax certificate shall be a separate violation.
(Ord. of 10-27-14)
