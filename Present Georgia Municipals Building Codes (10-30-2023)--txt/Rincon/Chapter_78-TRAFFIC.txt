Chapter 78 - TRAFFIC[1]

Sec. 78-1. - Adoption of uniform rules of the road.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-370, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-395, known as the uniform rules of the road, are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited in this chapter.
(Code 1984, §§ 6-101, 6-103)
State Law reference— Adoption of uniform rules of the road by local authorities, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372.
Sec. 78-2. - Impounding of vehicles—Conditions under which authorized.
Vehicles, may be impounded by the city when an officer:
(1)
Finds a vehicle unattended and such vehicle constitutes an obstruction to traffic.
(2)
Finds a vehicle parked at any place within the city where parking is prohibited by any existing ordinance of the city, or by approved traffic signs, and any such motor vehicle which has been tagged for exceeding the parking limit as provided by ordinance of the city and which motor vehicle remains at the place where tagged for more than 24 hours after so being tagged.
(3)
Has made an arrest of an offender against any section of this Code or any other ordinance of the city, and such offender has in his possession any motor vehicle, including motor scooters, bicycles or any other type of vehicle.
(Code 1984, § 6-107)
Sec. 78-3. - Same—Removal of vehicles; charges.
Whenever a vehicle is impounded as authorized in this section, the vehicle shall be removed from the streets or highways to a garage designated by the city marshal or arresting officer, and such vehicle shall be released upon the payment of a service charge for towing and storage. The payment of such service fee shall not release the owner, chauffeur or driver of such vehicle from any penalty imposed for violation of the laws, ordinances or rules of the city.
(Code 1984, § 6-108)
Sec. 78-4. - Blocking or obstruction of streets by railroad trains.
It shall be unlawful for any railroad company to permit the train, cars or engine of the company to remain stationary or standing for longer than a space of five minutes on any street or highway crossed by the railroad track of such company within the corporate limits of the city. It shall also be unlawful for any railroad company to permit any train or cars of the company to obstruct in any way any street crossing or highway for a longer period of time than ten minutes.
(Code 1984, § 6-109(1))

Sec. 78-7. - Operation of tractor or tractor trailer combination.
(a)
It shall be lawful for owners-operators to drive their truck tractors home (tractors only) by the shortest route to and from state and/or federal highways.
(b)
It shall be lawful for owners-operators to perform preventative maintenance at their residence, on their property (not on the street right-of-way), this being done during daylight hours. All used fluids removed from the vehicle must be disposed of by environmentally acceptable means. Any disposal in ditches, outfalls, streams or on land will be punishable by law. It shall be unlawful to allow the prolonged idling of such trucks or to cause to be made any loud or unusual noise which unreasonably or unnecessarily either annoys, disturbs, injures or endangers the comfort, repose, health, peace or safety of others in the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any tractor or tractor trailer combination or loaded trucks in excess of 6,000 pounds to use the city streets except for the delivery or receiving of goods or on designated truck routes.
(d)
Only two tractors (no trailers) will be permitted to park at any one residence.
(e)
Any person who violates the provisions of this section shall be punished in the police court of the city as for violation of misdemeanor.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any tractor or tractor trailer combination or loaded trucks to park on any city property or in the right-of-way. If a tractor or tractor trailer combo is found to be in violation of paragraph (e) or paragraph (f) of this section the tractor or tractor trailer combo will be tagged to be removed within 24 hours. If the tractor or tractor trailer combo is not moved within the 24-hour period the tractor or tractor trailer combo will be towed as per the Rincon Police Departments' list of towing company services. All repeat offenders will be towed immediately without the tractor or tractor trailer being tagged for 24-hour removal.
(Ord. No. 97-011, 11-24-97; Ord. No. 2003-006, 5-19-03)
Sec. 78-8. - No parking of trucks in the street.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park or stand any truck or other freight carrying vehicle on any street or right-of-way except for purposes of delivering or receiving of goods, longer than one hour at any time during the day or night. This section shall not apply to law enforcement officers engaged in official duties, public works employees (engaged in the care and maintenance of streets or rights-of-way), utility companies, or emergency vehicles.
(Ord. No. 97-011, 11-24-97; Ord. No. 2003-006, 5-19-03)
Sec. 78-9. - Trucks carrying hazardous substances.
It shall be unlawful for any tank or tanker or other like truck used for the transportation of hazardous or highly flammable substances, be they solid, liquid or gas, to be parked or stopped at any time on any street or right-of-way thereof in the city unless such vehicle is in the charge of an awake and immediately present attendant at all times.
Ie: Substances presenting such a hazard include gasoline, kerosene, and other flammable liquids, liquefied petroleum gases; welding and other gases; dry cleaning fluids; anhydrous ammonia; and other gases, liquids, or solids of a highly flammable or hazardous nature. "Hazardous material" means and includes radioactive materials, liquefied natural gas (LNG) and polychlorinated biphenyl (PCB), and any other material which, because of its quantity, concentration, or physical, chemical or infectious characteristics, may cause or significantly contribute to an increase in mortality or an increase in serious, irreversible or incapacitating reversible illness, and pose a substantial present or potential hazard to human health or to the environment when improperly treated, stored, transported, disposed of or otherwise managed.
(Ord. No. 97-011, 11-24-97; Ord. No. 2003-006, 5-19-03)
