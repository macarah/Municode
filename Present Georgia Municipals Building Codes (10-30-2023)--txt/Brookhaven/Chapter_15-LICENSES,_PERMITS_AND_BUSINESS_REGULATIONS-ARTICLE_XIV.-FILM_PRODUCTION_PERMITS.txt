ARTICLE XIV. - FILM PRODUCTION PERMITS

Sec. 15-560. - Purpose.
This article is adopted to promote film production in the City of Brookhaven while protecting the public health, safety, and welfare of the city's inhabitants and to safeguard the interests of the City of Brookhaven's residents, businesses and property. This article allows the city's departments and staff to work with permit applicants to determine any impact film production may have on the daily operations and functioning of the city, its parks, public streets, rights-of-way, sidewalks or property and to make the necessary recommendations and allowances.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-561. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means the individual applying for a permit who is legally authorized to bind the producer.
Application means the documents created by the department that must be completed and submitted to the department to request a film production permit.
Department means the community development department.
Director means the director of the community development department or designee.
Filming means creating motion picture images and all activity attendant to staging, shooting, and operations associated with the production of commercial motion pictures, independent filmography, documentaries, music videos, television shows, commercials, etc. Filming also means creating still photography images for commercial use and all activity attendant to staging or shooting such images.
Film production means filming as defined above and may be used interchangeably with that term.
Producer means an individual, organization, corporation or any other entity responsible for filming in the City of Brookhaven.
Public property means real property owned by the city, or of which the city is a tenant, including, without limitation, parks, streets, sidewalks, other rights-of-way, and buildings. Public property shall not include real property which is being leased by the city to a tenant.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-562. - Rules and regulations.
The director may promulgate such rules and regulations consistent with the provisions of this Code and necessary for the administration and enforcement of this article.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-563. - Permit required.
(a)
A permit is required if filming or any portion of filming is to take place on public property.
(b)
A permit is required if filming is to take place on private property, more specifically, property not owned by the city or any other governmental entity, and one of the following elements exists:
(1)
Filming outdoors between 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. within a 300-foot radius of a dwelling unit, residence, place of worship, or business;
(2)
Filming which would be disruptive to the normal flow of traffic;
(3)
Filming involving the closure of a street, lane and/or sidewalk;
(4)
Occupation or interference of a right-of-way;
(5)
Use of pyrotechnics or other explosives, smoke effects, water effects, flame effects, or other potentially dangerous instrumentalities;
(6)
Display or use of real or artificial firearms, grenades, or other weaponry;
(7)
Vehicle chases and/or vehicle crashes;
(8)
Dangerous stunts that have a reasonable likelihood of causing substantial personal injury;
(9)
Use of equipment that has a reasonable likelihood of causing damage to public property;
(10)
Filming that includes noise as defined and regulated by the city's noise ordinance, article VII of this Code, now and as it may be amended hereafter; and
(11)
Use or presence of any animal which is naturally inclined to do harm or capable of inflicting serious harm upon human beings, or any animal whose possession or sale is prohibited by federal, state, or local law or the Charter or Code of the City of Brookhaven.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-564. - When a film production permit is not required.
(a)
The provisions of this article shall not apply to:
(1)
Current news productions, including reporters, photographers or cameramen in the employment of a newspaper, news service, broadcasting station or similar entity engaged in the broadcasting of news events;
(2)
Productions that are conducted by the DeKalb County School System or other educational or governmental organizations, including but not limited to DeKalb County Television;
(3)
Filming associated with any rally, protest or demonstration, except when such rally or demonstration is staged for the sole purpose of being included in filming; and
(4)
Student filming by a student or students duly enrolled in an educational institution.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-565. - Generally.
(a)
Permits shall be issued by the department and shall be issued to a producer.
(b)
All permits issued pursuant to this article shall be temporary and shall not vest in the holder any permanent property rights in the permit.
(c)
The film production location must include sufficient parking, which may be reduced when, for instance, filming is located near public transit.
(d)
An application for a permit shall be subject to review by the director to determine compliance with this Code. Any conditions issued by the director shall become a requirement of the permit.
(e)
Unless specifically provided otherwise in this section, film production is subject to and must comply with all other applicable City ordinances.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-566. - Penalties for violation.
Any producer who performs filming without receiving a permit, or performs filming that violates the material terms of a permit, or is otherwise in violation of this article, upon citation or summons by the police chief, code enforcement officer or other authorized city employee, and judgment or conviction by the Municipal Court of the City of Brookhaven or any other court of competent jurisdiction, shall be subject to the penalties set forth in section 1-11 of this Code now and as it may be amended in the future. Each day a violation continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-567. - Application.
(a)
The producer of a film production shall make application for a permit for the film production on a form prescribed by the city.
(b)
An application for a film production permit shall be filed at least seven days prior to the date the film production is scheduled to begin in a residential area and 48 hours in advance of film production for all other locations; provided, however, no application shall be accepted earlier than one year prior to the date film production is scheduled to begin.
(c)
The director may waive the deadline for filing an application if there is sufficient time to process the application and for the department to prepare for the requested filming activity. The director will also determine any additional fees incurred due to any costs associated with expedited processing of the permit application.
(d)
All producers of a film production shall be properly identified on the application; provided, however, a film production permit shall be issued only to an individual person. Therefore, if a group, organization, association, or other entity is producing the film production, a designated agent of the producer shall be named for purposes of the permit, and this individual shall be solely and fully responsible for compliance with all provisions, including all financial requirements of this article and other applicable laws and ordinances.
(e)
Each city department and/or agency whose services would be impacted by the film production shall review the application and recommend in writing any conditions or restrictions deemed necessary. The city or any of its departments may require any other information deemed reasonably necessary to determine that the permit meets the requirements of this article. All special conditions or restrictions recommended by any of the city departments, city manager, or their designees, shall become a condition of the permit.
(f)
The application for a film production permit shall include the following information:
(1)
Project title;
(2)
Type of production;
(3)
Event details and description;
(4)
Name, address, e-mail address, and telephone number of the producer and the sponsoring entity or person in addition to the person named in subsection (d) of this section;
(5)
Dates, locations, and hours of filming;
(6)
Business license;
(7)
Payment of all fees;
(8)
Detailed description of each element of filming including but not limited to the elements listed in section 15-563 (b) regardless of whether the film location is on public or private property;
(9)
All applicable insurance information and certificates;
(10)
Overall site plan of the filming location. Plan must be drawn to scale and must include:
a.
All property boundaries and setbacks for proposed location of the film production;
b.
All existing buildings, structures, parking, and curb cuts permanently located on site; and
c.
Any proposed temporary structures including buildings, structures, and parking;
(11)
Projected attendance during film production;
(12)
Plans for parking, restroom facilities, and sanitation concerns;
(13)
First aid/medical support plan;
(14)
Plan for crowd and traffic control;
(15)
Plan for clean-up and close out of all locations used for filming;
(16)
List of all vehicles used as part of or incidental to filming and all dates and times of use;
(17)
Total number of personnel involved in filming including but not limited to crew, case, models and extras;
(18)
Staging area plan;
(19)
Proposed street/parking plan;
(20)
Proposed street/parking lot closure plan;
(21)
Proposed traffic plan (if necessary);
(22)
Proposed equipment and sound amplification plan;
(23)
Producer shall provide proof that all affected residents/businesses both on and adjacent to a proposed street closure are to be notified seven days prior to the proposed filming as required in the application form provided by the department. The department shall provide the list of affected residents/businesses to the producer. The event producer shall provide residents/businesses a copy of the proposed street closure map. Additionally, notification signs may be required at the producer's expense in the neighborhood during the street closure for traffic routing purposes.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-568. - Fee schedule.
The applicant shall pay all such applicable fees, including the following:
(1)
Each application must be accompanied by the nonrefundable application fee according to the provisions of section 2-177 and as set forth in the city's fee schedule.
(2)
Producer shall attach payment of the permit fee to the application.
(3)
Each city department and/or agency whose services would be impacted by the film production shall itemize the departmental activity required for the film production, showing the hourly rate and the actual and reasonable total cost. The "total costs to the city" shall be the sum of each department's costs. The city manager shall determine and calculate a reasonable fee to reimburse the city for its services.
(4)
Where filming requires the presence of law enforcement, fire rescue services, or any other services provided by the city, such services shall be approved by the department and will be paid for by producer according to the city's fee schedule.
(5)
A cash bond may be determined to be appropriate by the department and if such a bond is required, the department shall advise the producer of the amount. The bond shall be remitted to the city before the film production permit is issued.
(6)
The permit fee shall be paid in full prior to the issuance of the permit.
(7)
The fees required in this article shall be in addition to any other fees which may be required by any other applicable ordinances or regulations.
(8)
No producer of any film production, except as may otherwise be provided herein, shall be exempt from the payment of the appropriate fees and charges required under this article.
(9)
Should a producer desire to have use of any equipment owned by the city, such as barriers, traffic cones, and the like, an additional fee, as may be established from time to time by resolution of the city council, shall be paid so as to cover the reasonable cost for use of the equipment, as well as the delivery and return of the items by city employees.
(10)
Notwithstanding any provision to the contrary, the producer of any film production associated with and benefiting the cause of a charitable organization, recognized as such by the Internal Revenue Service under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, may request a 50 percent reduction of the film production permit fee.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-569. - Pre-permit meeting required.
If film production occurs at a residential location and contains any of the four listed items below, a meeting with the City of Brookhaven is required a minimum of one week in advance before the application for the film production permit can be accepted and processed.
(1)
Film preparation, filming and clean up lasting a total of three days or more;
(2)
Outdoor filming;
(3)
Full and/or partial lane and street closures; or
(4)
Requested variances to any city code.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-570. - Additional requirements.
(a)
In the event a film production requires the employment of off-duty, officers, uniformed Brookhaven Police Officer(s) shall be utilized. If Brookhaven Police Officer(s) are not available, then a DeKalb County police officer(s) may be utilized.
(b)
A film production permit may be issued only after an adequate plan for fire inspection/prevention and/or fire code enforcement and, when deemed necessary, employment of off-duty, uniformed fire personnel have been verified by the city and obtained by the producer.
(c)
A film production permit may be issued only after an adequate EMS plan and, when deemed necessary, employment of off-duty medics who are state-certified EMT or paramedics has been verified by the city and obtained by the producer.
(d)
A film production permit may be issued only after adequate waste disposal facilities have been determined by the city and obtained by the producer. The producer shall be required to clean the right-of-way and public/private property of rubbish and debris, returning it to its pre-film production condition, within 24 hours of the conclusion of the film production. If the producer fails to clean up such refuse, cleanup shall be arranged by the city, and the costs incurred for this service shall be charged to the producer.
(e)
A film production permit granted by the city may provide for the city to close designated streets and intersections to allow use of the public right-of-way for the film production during designated hours and days.
(f)
The sound level of any film production must comply with the city noise ordinance, found at article VII of this Code, now and as amended.
(g)
After all of the requested information pertaining to the film production has been submitted, reviewed, and approved, a permit may be issued upon payment of all applicable fees and costs. The film production permit, as well as any other permits required in conjunction with the film production, shall be posted on site during the film production.
(h)
Should a permit be denied, the producer shall be notified in writing of the denial.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-571. - Insurance and liability.
(a)
At the discretion of the department, prior to issuance of a permit, the producer shall provide to the city proof of comprehensive liability insurance naming the city as an additional insured. The producer's comprehensive liability insurance shall be primary over any other policy of the city.
(b)
At the discretion of the department, the producer, at all times during filming, shall maintain comprehensive general liability insurance combined single limits coverage including bodily injury and property damage with limits of $1,000,000.00 for each occurrence, personal and advertising injury with a limit of $1,000,000.00 per occurrence and $2,000,000.00 general and auto liability insurance including owner, hired, and non-owned vehicles with combined single limits including bodily injury and property damage of $1,000,000.00 for each occurrence and workers' compensation as required by law. The City of Brookhaven shall be named as an additional insured on all policies and said policies shall be primary to any insurance maintained by the city.
(c)
The producer of any film production shall provide a written agreement in a form satisfactory to the city providing the producer shall defend, pay, and save harmless the city, its officers, employees, and agents from liability of all personal injury or property damages arising from any acts or omissions emanating from a film production and from any and all claims, attorney fees or lawsuits for personal injury or property damage arising from or in any way connected to the film production. The agreement shall be filed with, and made a part of, the application form.
(d)
The city, its officials, employees, or agents shall not incur any liability or responsibility for any injury or damage to any person or any property in any way connected to the use for which the permit has been issued. The city, its officials, employees, or agents shall not be deemed to have assumed any liability or responsibility by reasons of inspections performed, the issuance of any permit, or the approval of any use of the right-of-way or other public property.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-572. - Other permits.
(a)
A permit allowing fireworks shall be approved and permitted by the city fire marshal. Further, the person to be performing the firework display shall be pyrotechnics licensed and qualified by the state.
(b)
Any other permit required by this Code.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-573. - Denial or revocation of a film production permit.
(a)
Reasons for denial of a film production permit:
(1)
The film production will unnecessarily disrupt traffic within the city beyond practical solution;
(2)
The film production will interfere with access to fire stations and fire hydrants;
(3)
The location of the film production will cause undue hardship to adjacent businesses or residents;
(4)
The film production will cause unnecessary disruption of public services which would unreasonably impact the remainder of the city;
(5)
The film production or producer is in violation of federal, state or local law or any provisions of the city's Charter or this Code;
(6)
The department, city manager, chief of the police department or the fire marshal, or their designees, determines that the health, welfare, or safety of the public may be endangered;
(7)
The application contains incomplete or false information or a misrepresentation; and
(8)
The producer fails to comply with any terms required by this article and/or this Code;
(9)
Filming poses a risk of injury or damage to persons or property not associated with the filming;
(10)
Filming poses an unreasonable risk of irreversible damage to public property;
(11)
The date and time requested conflicts with other filming or other scheduled events or activities;
(12)
Filming unreasonably interferes with city functions or operations;
(13)
Producer owes an outstanding debt to the city;
(14)
Producer cannot procure insurance; or
(15)
Producer previously violated this article on two occasions within the last three years.
(b)
Reasons for suspension or revocation of a film production permit are:
(1)
False or incomplete information on the application;
(2)
Failure to comply with all terms and conditions of the permit;
(3)
The film production or producer or anyone who works for the producer is in violation of federal, state or local law or any provisions of the city's Charter or this Code;
(4)
The department, city manager, chief of the police department or the fire marshal, or their designees, determines that the health, welfare, or safety of the public may be endangered;
(5)
Failure to arrange for or adequately remit all fees and bonds required by the city and to maintain all required insurance;
(6)
The department determines that the producer has intentionally or recklessly violated material terms of the permit;
(7)
The department determines that the producer does not have or has lost insurance coverage;
(8)
Existence of disaster, public calamity, riot or other emergency as the city determines, in its sole discretion, to be an impact upon the public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-574. - Responsibilities after permit issuance.
(a)
The producer shall conduct operations in an orderly fashion with continuous attention to the storage of equipment not in use and the removal of trash and debris. Upon completion of filming, the premises shall be left in a clean condition. It is the responsibility of the producer to exercise reasonable care in inspecting and immediately repairing any damage to any public property which occurs as a result of filming.
(b)
After filming concludes, the producer shall leave all public property in the same or better condition as it was prior to filming.
(c)
The producer must publicly display the approved permit on site at all times during filming, set up, break down and related activities.
(d)
The producer, and the producer's employees, contractors and subcontractors are responsible for knowing and complying with all City of Brookhaven ordinances and other applicable federal, state and local laws.
(e)
The producer must give written notification of filming activities to all residents and businesses impacted by the filming and post notices. The residents and businesses to be notified shall be determined by the department. the notification should include the date, time, location, and a general description of the filming activities authorized by the permit.
(f)
Where filming includes advertising signs or other displays of commercial speech, all signs and/or displays must be removed upon expiration of the permit.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
Sec. 15-575. - Appeals.
(a)
Any producer whose film production permit application has been denied, suspended or revoked may request in writing a review of this decision by the city manager. This request must be in writing and received by the city manager within five days of the date of the permit denial or revocation.
(b)
The city manager shall review the application and reasons for the denial or revocation of the film production permit and shall issue a decision, within five days, whether to uphold or reverse the previous decision and grant or reinstate the permit with such additional conditions as the city manager may deem justified. The decision of the city manager shall be final.
(Ord. No. 2018-10-06 , 10-23-2018)
