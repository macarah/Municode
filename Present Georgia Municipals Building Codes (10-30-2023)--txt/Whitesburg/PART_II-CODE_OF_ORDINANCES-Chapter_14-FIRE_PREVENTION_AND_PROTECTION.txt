Sec. 14-1. - Permit required.
Any person, firm, corporation, or association lawfully entitled to burn any woods, lands, marshes, or any other flammable vegetation, whether cultivated or uncultivated areas within the city, shall, prior to such burning, obtain a permit from the state forestry commission who shall issue a permit to the applicant prior to any such burn. An applicant for the permit shall provide the state forestry commission with the location and recommended time of the proposed burn. The state forestry commission shall provide the applicant with a permit which will grant permission for a controlled burn to take place at the location specified at the approximate time approved by the state forestry commission.
(Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 1)
Sec. 14-2. - Chapter cumulative.
This chapter shall be in addition to any other notice or permit or other requirement for burning provided for by law or by any statute or regulation of the state, specifically including O.C.G.A. § 12-6-90.
(Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 2)
Sec. 14-3. - Denial of permits.
The state forestry commission, in consultation with the mayor and the appropriate fire officials who have jurisdiction within the city, shall have the right to deny permits for controlled burning within the city at any time that they, in their sole discretion, may determine it to be hazardous to the health and welfare of persons or property in the city to allow such burn.
(Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 3)
Sec. 14-4. - Jurisdiction of violations.
The jurisdiction of violations of this chapter shall be in the municipal city court. Violations may be enforced by citations issued by codes enforcement officers, the police department, or the state forestry commission.
(Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 4)
Sec. 14-5. - Violations.
Any person who fails to give notice of obtain a permit required by this chapter without obtaining the permit required by the chapter shall be guilty of a violation of this chapter. Any such violation may be punished by the maximum penalties, including fines and imprisonment allowed by the Charter and this Code.
(Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 5)
