Chapter 38 - ELECTIONS[1]

Sec. 38-1. - Adoption of state rules, regulations.
The rules and regulations promulgated by the state election board that pertain to municipal elections, together with the provisions of the state municipal election code contained in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq., are adopted as the rules, regulations and provisions that shall govern the conduct of municipal general and special elections in the city.
Sec. 38-2. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this chapter shall be budgeted and appropriated annually, and at other times when required.
Sec. 38-3. - Polling places, voting precincts.
The polling places and voting precincts of the city shall be as shown on the official map on file in the office of the city clerk.
State Law reference— Duty of superintendent to fix location of polling places, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-265.
Sec. 38-4. - Superintendent.
The council shall by ordinance as needed appoint a municipal election superintendent who shall exercise those powers and duties set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
(Code 1976, § 2-2001)
Sec. 38-5. - Filing notice of candidacy; date.
The closing date for filing notice of candidacy by candidates or their agents shall be at least 15 days, but not more than 45 days prior to general elections and at least ten but not more than 30 days prior to special elections.
(Code 1976, § 2-2002)
Sec. 38-6. - Oath of office following special election.
Persons elected to the office of mayor or councilmember at a special election shall take the oath of office prescribed in subsection 2.4(b) of the Charter at the first regular meeting of the mayor and council following such election.
(Code 1976, § 2-2004)
Sec. 38-7. - Soliciting votes near voting place.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this Code for any person to solicit votes in any manner or by any means or method or to distribute or display any campaign literature, booklet, pamphlet, card, sign or written or printed matter of any kind in support of any person, party or proposition in any election or primary held in and for the city within 250 feet of any voting place or building within which such voting place is located.
(Code 1976, § 2-3005; Ord. No. 76-19, § 1, 5-5-1976)
