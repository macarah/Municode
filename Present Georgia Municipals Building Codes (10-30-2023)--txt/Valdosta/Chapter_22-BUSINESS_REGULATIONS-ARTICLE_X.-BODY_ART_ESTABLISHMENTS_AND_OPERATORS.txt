The legal authority of this article is O.C.G.A. §§ 16-5-71, 16-5-71.1, 16-12-5, 48-13-9 and Chapter 40 of Title 31. Authority O.C.G.A. §§ 16-5-71, 16-5-71.1, 16-12-5, 31-40-1, 31-40-2, 31-40-3, 31-40-4, 31-40-5, 31-40-6, 31-40-7, 31-40-8, 31-40-9, 31-40-10, 31-5 and 48-13-9.

Sec. 22-392. - Purpose.
The purpose of these rules and regulations is to establish reasonable standards for individuals performing body art procedures and for the facilities from which the procedures are provided. Such standards should insure the health and safety of all individuals performing and receiving these services. Any and all body art procedures not explicitly mentioned in these rules and regulations must be approved by the Lowndes County Board of Health prior to being performed in a body art facility.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-393. - Exemptions.
These regulations do not apply to a physician or osteopath licensed under O.C.G.A. tit. 43, ch. 34, or a technician acting under the direct supervision of such licensed physician or osteopath.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-394. - Definitions.
Unless otherwise defined by specific sections as used in these rules and regulations the term:
Antibacterial solution means any solution used to retard the growth of bacteria approved for application to human skin and includes all products labeled accordingly.
Body art means the practice of physical body adornment by permitted establishments and operators utilizing, but not limited to the following techniques: Body piercing, tattooing and cosmetic tattooing. This definition does not include implants under the skin, tattoo removal or any other practices that are considered medical procedures by a state medical board.
Body art establishment means any place or premises, whether public or private, temporary or permanent in nature or location, where the practices of body art, whether or not for profit, are performed.
Body piercing means any method of piercing the skin or mucosa, except the entire ear, in order to place any object including, but not limited to, rings, studs, bars or other forms of jewelry through the skin or mucosa.
Body piercing artist means any person who performs piercing.
City means the City of Valdosta, Georgia or its duly authorized representatives.
Currently licensed health care professional includes physicians, physician assistant (PA), nurse practitioner, registered nurses (RN), licensed practical nurses (LPN), and licensed emergency medical technicians (I) or (P), holding current licenses with the State of Georgia within their respective fields.
Department means the Department of Human Resources of the State of Georgia.
FDA means the United States Food and Drug Administration.
Governing body means the partnership, the corporation, the association or the person or group of persons who maintain and control the body art establishment and who are legally responsible for the operation of the establishment.
Instruments used for body art means any object or instrument used in the performance of body art procedures, which may come into contact with a client's bodily fluid during the performance of a body art procedure, and includes, but is not limited to, hand pieces, needles, needle bars and other instruments that may come in contact with a client's body fluid during body art procedures.
Operator means any person who controls, operates, manages, conducts or practices body art activities at a body art establishment and who is responsible for compliance with these regulations, regardless whether actually performing body art procedures or not. The term includes technicians who work under the operator and perform body art procedures.
OSHA means the United States Occupational Safety and Health Administration.
Permit means the authorization granted by the city to the governing body to operate a body art establishment.
Sanitary means clean and free of agents of infection or disease.
Sanitized means effective antibacterial treatment by a process that provides sufficient concentration of chemicals for enough time to reduce the bacteria count, including pathogens, to a safe level on equipment.
Spore means a small, usually single-celled, asexually- or sexually-reproductive body that is highly resistant to desiccation and heat and is capable of growing into a new organism, produced especially by certain bacteria, fungi, algae and nonflowering plants.
Standard precautions means a set of guidelines and controls, published by the Centers for Disease Control (CDC), which includes specific recommendations for the use of gloves, masks, protective eye wear and/or other protective equipment when contact with blood or body fluids containing blood is anticipated. These guidelines and controls may be found in "Recommendations for Prevention of HIV Transmission in Health Care Settings", Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report (MMWR), August 21, 1987, Vol. 36, No. (SU02); 001; "Universal Precautions for Prevention of Transmission of Human Immunodeficiency Virus, Hepatitis B Virus, and other Blood-borne Pathogens in Health-Care Settings", MMWR, June 24,1988, Vol. 37, No. 24; "Guidelines for Prevention and Transmission of Human Immunodeficiency Virus and Hepatitis B Virus to Health Care and Public Safety Workers", MMWR, June 23, 1989, Vol. 38, No.S-6; and MMWR; and "Recommendations for Preventing Transmission of Human Immunodeficiency Virus and Hepatitis B Virus to Patients During Exposure-Prone Invasive Procedures", July 12, 1991, Vol. 40, No. (RR08); 1-9. These guidelines are hereby incorporated by reference into this rule.
Sterilization means holding an instrument in an autoclave for 15 minutes at 15 pounds pressure at a temperature of 273 degrees Fahrenheit or 121 degrees Celsius.
Tattoo artist means any person who performs tattooing.
Tattooing means any method of placing ink or other pigment into or under the skin or mucosa by the aid of needles or any other instruments used to puncture the skin, resulting in permanent coloration of the skin or mucosa. This includes all forms of cosmetic tattooing. It shall be unlawful for any person to tattoo the body of any person within any area within one inch of the nearest part of the eye socket of such person.
Universal precautions means a set of guidelines and controls, published by the United State Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA), which includes specific recommendations for the use of gloves, masks, protective eye wear and/or other protective equipment when contact with blood or body fluids containing blood is anticipated. These guidelines and controls may be found at 29 C.F.R. 1910.1030 and are hereby incorporated by reference into this rule.

Sec. 22-395. - Governing body.
(a)
The governing body shall be responsible for compliance with the requirements of this chapter, with the requirements in O.C.G.A. tit. 31, ch. 40, and with applicable administrative rules and regulations of the Lowndes County Board of Health, including, but not limited to, all applicable statutes, rules and regulations regarding disclosure of ownership.
(b)
Prior to being granted a permit each body art establishment shall develop a written statement of policies and procedures outlining the responsibilities of management.
(1)
No person under the age of 18 shall be tattooed except that a physician or osteopath licensed under O.C.G.A. tit. 43, ch. 34, or a technician acting under the direct supervision of such licensed physician or osteopath shall be authorized to do.
(2)
No person shall pierce the body, with the exception of the ear lobes, of any person under the age of 18 years without the presence, consent, and proper identification of a parent, legal custodial parent or legal guardian unless under the direct supervision of a physician.
(3)
Body art operators shall not be under the influence of alcohol and/or drugs while performing body art procedures.
(4)
No person shall receive a body art procedure that appears to be under the influence of alcohol and/or drugs.
(5)
No animals, except for guide animals accompanying disabled persons, shall be allowed in the body art establishment.
(6)
Written report of any infection of allergic reaction resulting from the application of a tattoo or body piercing shall be forwarded to the city within five working days of its occurrence or knowledge thereof.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-396. - Minimum floor plan requirements.
(a)
The body art establishment shall be constructed, arranged and maintained as to provide adequately for the health and safety of its customers.
(b)
The establishment shall be constructed in a manner to allow the customer receiving the body art procedure adequate privacy from observers. The work area shall be separate from the waiting area.
(c)
Body art establishments must have adequate ventilation.
(d)
Walls and ceilings must be painted or covered in a manner which would allow for easy and effective cleaning. Paint or covering should be light in color in the work area.
(e)
Floors of the work area must not be carpeted. Surfaces shall be of a nonabsorbent material which would allow for effective cleaning by conventional methods.
(f)
A clean and sanitary toilet and hand washing facility shall be made accessible to customers; however, it shall be separate from the work area.
(g)
At least one sink and basin providing hot and cold running water for the use of the operator for washing their hands and preparing customers for body art procedures shall be easily accessible to each work station. This station shall be provided with soap, an antibacterial solution, single-use towels, and individual hand brushes for each artist.
(h)
The work area shall be provided with adequate lighting.
(i)
The work area shall not be used as a corridor for access to other rooms.
(j)
Enough potable water for the needs of the body art establishment shall be provided from an approved source that is a public water system; or a nonpublic water system that is constructed, maintained and operated according to applicable state or local codes and shall meet state drinking water quality standards.
(k)
Sewage disposal shall be disposed through an approved facility that is:
(1)
A public sewage treatment plant; or
(2)
An individual sewage disposal system that is sized, constructed, maintained and operated according to law.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-397. - Furnishings and fixtures.
(a)
Furnishings of the body art establishment shall be maintained in good condition, intact and functional. The establishment shall be kept clean, neat and free of litter and rubbish.
(b)
Facilities for the storage of instruments, dyes, pigments, single-use articles, carbon and stencils shall be provided for each operator and shall be maintained in a sanitary manner which protects them from contamination.
(c)
Work tables and chairs shall be provided for each operator.
(1)
Surfaces of all work tables and chairs shall be constructed of material which is smooth, nonabsorbent, corrosion-resistant, and easily sanitized. Work tables and chairs should be light in color.
(2)
Work tables and chairs shall be sanitized with an antibacterial solution after each application.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-398. - Supplies.
(a)
Bulk single-use articles shall be commercially packaged and handled to protect them from contamination. These articles shall be stored in an area separate from the toilet facilities.
(b)
All materials applied to the human skin shall be from single-use containers, applied only to a single individual, and shall be disposed of after each use.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-399. - Dyes and pigments.
(a)
All dyes and pigments used in tattooing shall be obtained from professional suppliers specifically providing dyes and/or pigments for the tattooing of human skin.
(b)
In preparing dyes or pigments to be used by the operator, only nontoxic sterile materials shall be used. Single-use or individual portions of dyes or pigments in clean, sterilized individual containers or single-use containers shall be used for each client.
(c)
After tattooing, the remaining unused dye or pigment in the single-use or individual containers shall be discarded along with the container.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-400. - Piercing jewelry.
(a)
Client and body piercing artist should have appropriate size and quality jewelry chosen before the procedure beings.
(b)
Only appropriate jewelry should be used in piercing. Appropriate jewelry is made of implant grade, high quality surgical stainless steel (316LVM series), titanium or platinum. Appropriate jewelry has no nicks, scratches or irregular surfaces that might endanger the tissues.
(c)
Ear studs or other jewelry designed for earlobe piercing are not appropriate jewelry for other body parts and must not be used by body piercing artists.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-401. - Sanitation.
(a)
Prior to the start of the body art procedure the operator should inspect his/her hands for hangnails, small cuts, sores and abrasions. If a cut, sore or abrasion is detected, a bandage should be applied for added protection before gloving. Trim fingernails to insure that gloves are not punctured.
(b)
All jewelry such as watches, rings, etc., should be removed prior to the start of the body art procedure.
(c)
Before working on each client, the fingernails and hands of the operator shall be thoroughly washed and scrubbed with warm water, and antibacterial soap, using an individual hand brush that is clean and in good repair. The hands shall then be dried by single-use towels prior to beginning work on each client or when interrupted in the process.
(d)
Single-use, disposable, FDA-approved examination gloves shall be worn during the body art procedure. Gloves shall be changed and properly disposed of each time there is an interruption in the body art procedure, the gloves become torn or punctured, or whenever their ability to function is compromised.
(e)
Each operator shall follow the standard precautions as published by the Centers for Disease Control.
(f)
Tattoo/body piercing artists who are experiencing symptoms of diarrhea, vomiting, fever, rash or skin infections shall refrain from tattooing/body piercing activities.
(g)
Smoking and consumption of food or drink by the artist is prohibited in the workstation.
(h)
Universal precautions are to be followed per OSHA standards and guidelines.
(i)
Only single-use disposable needles shall be used in the tattooing process.
(j)
Adequate numbers of sterilized needles and tubes must be on hand for each artist for the daily operating hours.
(k)
Only single-use disposable razors shall be used to shave the area to receive body art procedures.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-402. - Sterilization.
(a)
An autoclave shall be provided in each body art establishment.
(b)
Used nondisposable instruments shall be kept in a separate puncture resistant container until brush scrubbed in hot water and soap, and then sterilized by autoclaving.
(c)
The following procedures shall be followed during the sterilization process:
(1)
Prior to being placed in the autoclave all equipment shall be bagged, dated and sealed.
(2)
Each autoclave bag may hold no more than one liner and shader tube with needle and bar inserted into tube.
(3)
Instruments shall remain in the autoclave 55 minutes from a cold start.
(4)
If the process begins with a hot start then instruments should be processed for 35 minutes.
(5)
To achieve proper sterilization, temperature must reach 273 degrees Fahrenheit and 15-30 psi.
(6)
There must be a sterilization indicator in each autoclaving to monitor the sterilization procedure. The indicator must indicate exposure to steam and 250 degrees Fahrenheit.
(7)
Body art operations shall demonstrate, using spore destruction tests, that their autoclaves are capable of attaining the minimum operating standards recommended by the manufacturer of the autoclave. These tests shall be performed every 30 days and verified through an independent laboratory. Spore destruction tests shall be maintained on file at the body art establishment. Spore tests shall be provided to the city upon request.
(8)
Autoclaves shall be cleaned at the frequency recommended by the manufacturer. Autoclaves shall be serviced at the frequency by the manufacturer, but not less than once a year. A copy of the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and servicing must be maintained on file in the body art establishment.
(9)
All clean or packaged sterilized instruments and supplies shall be stored in clean, dry closed cabinets or tightly covered containers.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-403. - Temporary, special event and mobile body art requirements.
All provisions of these regulations shall apply with the following exceptions:
(1)
Hand wash facilities shall be easily accessible to each procedure area and designated for use by the operator only. Hand wash facilities shall comply with the requirements of subsection 22-396(g) of this article or, temporary hand wash facilities shall consist of soap, single-use paper towels and an adequate supply of potable water dispensed through a continuous flow spout. Wastewater shall be collected and disposed of in a sanitary manner.
(2)
Body art operators may bring presterilized instruments, or instruments which have been sterilized at another location with documentation showing a negative spore test result within the previous seven days. On-site sterilization units may be used and shall comply with the requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-404. - Tattoo preparation.
(a)
Before placing the tattoo design on the client's skin, the operator shall treat the skin area with an antibacterial solution.
(b)
If an acetate stencil is used by an operator for transferring the design to the skin, it shall be thoroughly cleaned and rinsed in a germicidal solution for at least 20 minutes and then dried with sterile gauze or dried in the air on a sanitized surface after each use.
(c)
If a paper stencil is used by a tattoo artist for transferring the design to the skin, it shall be single-use and disposable.
(d)
If the design is drawn directly onto the skin, it shall be applied with a single-use article only.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-405. - Body piercing preparation.
(a)
Set up equipment in front of client.
(b)
Open sealed autoclave bags containing sterile equipment in front of client, and discard the autoclave bag.
(c)
Disinfect all jewelry contaminated with only airborne pathogens (not previously worn or contaminated) with a nonhazardous hard surface disinfectant approved by the EPA. Any jewelry contaminated with blood-borne pathogens (previously worn by another person) shall not be reused.
(d)
Presterilize all needles used in piercing, use only on one person, at one sitting, for a single piercing, and immediately dispose of in a medical sharps container.
(e)
Presterilize all forceps, tubes, etc., in sealed, dated, sterile indicator bags. These items are to be used on one person, in one sitting. After one such use, they must be autoclaved and stored in sterile indicator bags, sealed and dated.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-406. - After tattoo/body piercing application.
(a)
The completed tattoo shall be washed with a single-use towel saturated with an antibacterial solution.
(b)
After the area has dried, apply a layer of antibacterial ointment from a collapsible or plastic tube.
(c)
A bandage shall then be applied to the tattoo using sealed nonsticking pads.
(d)
The client shall then be given written instructions on caring for the tattoo/piercing during the healing process.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-407. - Disinfection of workplace.
Each body art establishment must be kept clean and sanitary. The owner must develop and implement a cleaning schedule that includes appropriate methods of decontamination and tasks or procedures to be performed. This written schedule must be based on the location within the establishment, the type of surfaces to be cleaned, type of possible contamination present, the tasks or procedures to be performed, and their location within the establishment. A copy of the schedule shall be submitted to the city along with the application for permit. The following procedures should be adhered to:
(1)
Clean and sanitize all equipment and work surfaces with an appropriate disinfectant after completion of body art procedures and at the end of work shift when surfaces have become contaminated since the last cleaning.
(2)
Remove and replace protective coverings such as plastic wrap and aluminum foil after each body art procedure.
(3)
Inspect and decontaminate, on a regular basis, reusable receptacles such as bins, pails and cans that have likelihood for becoming contaminated. When contaminations are visible, clean and decontaminate receptacles immediately or as soon as feasible.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-408. - Disposal of waste.
(a)
Needles, razors or other sharp instruments used during body art procedures, shall be placed in puncture-resistant, closed containers immediately after use.
(b)
Needles shall not be purposely bent or broken, or otherwise manipulated by hand.
(c)
Containers of sharp waste shall be sent to an approved facility where they are either incinerated or otherwise rendered nonhazardous.
(d)
Disposable waste shall be placed in easily cleanable, closed containers provided with tight fitting lids, to prevent leakage or spillage.
(e)
Waste containers shall be kept closed when not in use.
(f)
Disposable waste shall be handled, stored and disposed of to minimize direct exposure of personnel to waste materials.
(g)
Each workstation shall be supplied with a container for sharp waste and a container for disposable waste. These containers shall not be overfilled.
(h)
All waste must be disposed of in an approved manner.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-409. - Personnel.
(a)
Unless the individual is a currently licensed health care professional, persons performing the body art procedures shall obtain current certification in first-aid/CPR, and proof of attendance at a blood-borne pathogen training program (or equivalent), given or approved by the Lowndes County Health Department. Training/courses provided by professional body art organizations/ associations or by equipment manufacturers may also be submitted for consideration.
(b)
Proof shall be provided upon request that all operators have completed the Hepatitis B vaccination series; that antibody testing has revealed that the employee is immune to Hepatitis B; or that the vaccine is contraindicated for medical reasons. Contraindication requires a dated and signed physician's statement specifying the name of the employee and that the vaccine cannot be given.
(c)
The governing body must maintain a file on all employees who perform body art procedures, which will be available for inspection. This file must include a copy of the current operator's permit, required medical records and documentation of the required training.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-410. - Client files.
(a)
For each client, proper records of body art administered shall be maintained by the studio.
(b)
Records of each client shall be prepared prior to the procedure being performed and shall reflect the client's name and signature, address, proof of age, date of body art, design, its location and name of the operator.
(c)
A statement of informed consent by the individual receiving the body art must be maintained on file.
(d)
A statement by the client attesting that he/she is not under the influence of alcohol and/or drugs shall be on file.
(e)
A copy of procedures signed by the client advising them of proper subsequent care of the body art procedure shall be maintained on file.
(f)
A copy of instructions signed by the client informing them on the risks involved and possible complications that might result from the body art procedure must be maintained on file.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-411. - Application for permit.
(a)
The governing body of each body art establishment shall submit to the city a written application for a permit to operate under the rules and regulations. No body art establishment shall be operated and no body art performed without such a permit which is current under these rules and regulations.
(b)
The application for permit shall be made on forms provided by the city, and shall be submitted to the city manager's designee.
(c)
Each application for a permit shall be accompanied by a floor sketch of the body art establishment showing windows, doors, room measurements, chairs, tables and equipment placement for clients and/or staff.
(d)
A listing of the names of all staff, including the owner who will be working in the establishment, shall be included with the application for a permit. This listing shall include the full name of each staff person.
(e)
The ownership of the establishment disclosed in its application for a permit.
(f)
Zoning and other local requirements (if applicable) regarding proper location and establishment of body art establishment must be approved prior to the issuance of any permit pursuant to this article by the city.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-412. - Operator/artist permit.
(a)
No person shall practice body art procedures without first obtaining an operator/artist permit from the city.
(b)
Persons performing the tattoo or body piercing operation shall be 18 years of age or older.
(c)
The operator/artist permit shall be valid from the date of issuance and shall automatically expire in one year and must be renewed annually unless revoked sooner by the city.
(d)
Application for the operator/artist permit shall include:
(1)
Name;
(2)
Date of birth;
(3)
Sex;
(4)
Residence address;
(5)
Mailing address;
(6)
Phone number;
(7)
Place(s) of employment as an operator/artist;
(8)
Training and/or experience;
(9)
Required medical information;
(10)
Photo identification;
(11)
Proof of attendance at a blood-borne pathogen training program and CPR/first aid course given or approved by the city.
(e)
All operator/artist permits shall be posted in a prominent and conspicuous area where they may be readily observed by clients.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-413. - Permit.
(a)
The governing body of each body art establishment shall obtain a valid permit from the city prior to beginning operation. To be eligible for a permit the studio must be in compliance with these rules and regulations.
(b)
The permit shall be displayed in a conspicuous place on the premises.
(c)
Permits are not transferable from one establishment to another.
(d)
A permit shall no longer be valid and shall be returned to the city when the establishment ceases to operate, is moved to another location, the ownership changes or the permit is suspended or revoked.
(e)
An establishment which fails to comply with these rules and regulations shall be subject to the sanctions available to the city pursuant to this article including, but not limited to denial or revocation of its permit by the city.
(f)
Any permit granted pursuant to this article shall be automatically suspended upon the suspension or revocation of any similar permit granted to the establishment by any other county, state, federal or regulatory authority.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-414. - Inspections.
(a)
The establishment and its records shall be available for review and examination by properly identified representatives of the city.
(b)
A copy of the inspection report shall be displayed in a conspicuous place on the premises and also shall be available for public inspection at the appropriate city wherein the establishment is located.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
Sec. 22-415. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
Enforcement of these rules and regulations shall be in accordance with the authority vested in the city pursuant to its Charter.
(b)
No person shall engage in body art except in a permitted establishment.
(c)
The city may refuse to grant a permit for the operation of any body art establishment which does not fulfill the minimum requirements of these rules and regulations and may revoke a permit which has been issued and invoke other sanctions if an establishment violates any of these rules and regulations. Before any order is entered refusing a permit applied for or revoking a permit the applicant or permit holder shall be afforded an opportunity for a hearing before the city board. The notice of refusal or revocation shall contain directions on how a hearing may be requested.
(d)
The city is empowered to institute appropriate proceedings in a court of competent jurisdiction for the purpose of enjoining violation of any applicable provision of this article.
(Ord. No. 2009-31, 7-9-2009)
