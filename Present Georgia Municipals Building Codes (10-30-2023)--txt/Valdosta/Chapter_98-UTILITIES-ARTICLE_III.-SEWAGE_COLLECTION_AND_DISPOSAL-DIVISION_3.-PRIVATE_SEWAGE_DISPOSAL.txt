DIVISION 3. - PRIVATE SEWAGE DISPOSAL

Sec. 98-211. - When public sanitary sewer available.
Where a public sanitary sewer is not available under the provisions of section 98-184, the building sewer shall be connected to a private sewage disposal system complying with the provisions of this division, provided the lot consists of a minimum of at least one-half acre with a minimum dimension of 100 feet.
(Code 1976, § 5-2031; Ord. No. 79-8, 3-7-1979; Ord. No. 83-29, 9-8-1983)
Sec. 98-212. - Permit required.
Before commencement of construction of a private sewage disposal system, the owner shall first obtain a written permit signed by the county health officer.
(Code 1976, § 5-2032; Ord. No. 79-8, 3-7-1979)
Sec. 98-213. - Inspection.
A permit for a private sewage disposal system shall not become effective until the installation is completed to the satisfaction of the county health officer. He shall be allowed to inspect the work at any stage of construction, and in any event the applicant for the permit shall notify the county health officer when the work is ready for final inspection and before any underground portions are covered. The inspection shall be made within 48 hours of the receipt of notice by county health officer.
(Code 1976, § 5-2033; Ord. No. 79-8, 3-7-1979)
Sec. 98-214. - Compliance with health department regulations.
The type, capacities, location and layout of a private sewage disposal system shall comply with all recommendations of the state department of human resources. No septic tank or cesspool shall be permitted to discharge to any natural outlet.
(Code 1976, § 5-2034; Ord. No. 79-8, 3-7-1979)
Sec. 98-215. - Connection of public sewer when available.
At such time as a public sewer becomes available to a property served by a private sewage disposal system, as provided in section 98-184, a direct connection shall be made to the public sewer in compliance with this article; and any septic tanks, cesspools and similar private sewage disposal facilities shall be abandoned and filled with suitable material.
(Code 1976, § 5-2035; Ord. No. 79-8, 3-7-1979)
Sec. 98-216. - Owner to maintain facilities in sanitary manner.
The owner shall operate and maintain the private sewage disposal facilities in a sanitary manner at all times, at no expense to the city.
(Code 1976, § 5-2036; Ord. No. 79-8, 3-7-1979)
Sec. 98-217. - Division not to interfere with requirements of health officer.
No statement contained in this division shall be construed to interfere with any additional requirements that may be imposed by the health officer.
(Code 1976, § 5-2037; Ord. No. 79-8, 3-7-1979)
Secs. 98-218—98-240. - Reserved.
