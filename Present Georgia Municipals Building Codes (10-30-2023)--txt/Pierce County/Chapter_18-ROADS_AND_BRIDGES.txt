Chapter 18 - ROADS AND BRIDGES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Utility facilities.
The regulations of the state department of transportation with respect to utilities on the state highway system, entitled "Utility Accommodation Policy and Standards Manual," dated 2016, as promulgated, is hereby adopted by reference as the governing regulation of the county, with the following modifications:
(1)
All references to the state department of transportation, or the department, shall be read as referring to the board of commissioners.
(2)
All references to state highway, roads or rights-of-way shall be read as referring to county roads or rights-of-way within the county road system lying outside the corporate limits of any municipality.
(3)
The authority under which the regulations are adopted shall be amended to those applicable to county regulations of roads and rights-of-way, specifically including the authority, under O.C.G.A. § 32-4-42(6), to promulgate regulations consistent with those of the department of transportation.
(4)
All references to the department's district engineer shall be read to refer to the county road superintendent, as designated by the board of commissioners from time to time; the county clerk shall, on request, provide the name, telephone number, and address of the person or firm designated the county road superintendent for the purpose of enforcing the utility regulations hereby adopted.
(5)
Such minor language adjustments as are required to read, interpret, and enforce the regulations on behalf of and in connection with county roads and rights-of-way by the county, but in no event shall any such adjustment change or effect any substantive requirement or result in any regulation more restrictive than as written for enforcement by the state department of transportation.
(Code 2005, § 38-1; Ord. of 3-5-2002)
Secs. 18-2—18-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ADDRESS AND STREET NUMBERING

Sec. 18-21. - Purpose.
(a)
The purpose of this article is to establish a system for the orderly naming of streets within the county and the standards for numbering and posting of numbers on all dwelling units and commercial establishments thereon. Further, this article empowers the board of commissioners to enforce the assignment of street numbering of all such dwelling and commercial units constructed thereon.
(b)
The enactment of this article should also facilitate the location of residences and businesses by the police, fire, sheriff, district attorney, animal control, business permits and emergency management departments. It will further facilitate the enhanced 911 system by ensuring that every location with telephone service will have a defined location.
(Code 2005, § 38-31; Ord. of 8-10-1992)
Sec. 18-22. - Designation of street numbers.
Street numbers for dwelling units and places of business on all public streets, and street numbers and/or building numbers for dwelling units and places of business within apartments/condominiums and nonresidential developments located on private streets, shall be assigned by the board of commissioners in accordance with its administrative procedures.
(Code 2005, § 38-32; Ord. of 8-10-1992, § 1)
Sec. 18-23. - Designation of street names.
Street names for all public streets, as well as street names for private streets that are opened for public access on a continuing basis, shall be assigned by the board of commissioners, in accordance with its administrative procedures in order to avoid duplication. A written petition for the change of an approved street name may be made to the board of commissioners by 70 percent or more of the residents and/or property owners residing on such street. Consideration will be given to such a petition, provided that the requested name does not conflict with other approved street names as to duplication, similar sounds and/or similar spellings.
(Code 2005, § 38-33; Ord. of 8-10-1992, § 2)
Sec. 18-24. - Posting of designated street addresses.
Each dwelling unit and place of business shall have posted and maintained, in a prominent place on the property, street numbers visible for a distance of 150 feet from the street providing general public access.
(1)
Residential. The address of the residential dwelling unit shall be in figures at least three inches high on a contrasting background of a material that will allow 24-hour visibility, and be placed within 15 feet of the back of the street curbing or edge of the street surface. If the numbers are placed beyond the 15-foot requirement, the figures will be at least four inches high.
(2)
Nonresidential. The address of the nonresidential establishments shall be in figures at least four inches high on a contrasting background of a material that will allow 24-hour visibility, and be placed within 15 feet of the back of the street curbing or edge of the street surface. If the figures are placed beyond the 15-foot limit, the letters will be at least six inches high.
(Code 2005, § 38-34; Ord. of 8-10-1992, § 3)
Sec. 18-25. - Special considerations.
(a)
All street numbering will be assigned in such a manner that, when traveling from the lower numbers to the higher numbers, the odd numbers will be to the right and the even numbers to the left.
(b)
Any site where a telephone line (circuit) with dial tone exists, regardless of the terminating equipment, shall be assigned a street address for E911 purposes.
(Code 2005, § 38-35; Ord. of 8-10-1992, § 4)
Sec. 18-26. - Exceptions.
Sites for utility services which are normally unmanned, but have been assigned a street number for E911 purposes, are exempt from any required posting (display) of such an address. Such sites would be traffic control points, water and radio tower sites, utility power substations, etc.
(Code 2005, § 38-36; Ord. of 8-10-1992, § 5)
Sec. 18-27. - Penalties.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished in accordance with section 1-9.
(Code 2005, § 38-37; Ord. of 8-10-1992, § 7)
Secs. 18-28—18-57. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - CONSTRUCTION OF PRIVATE WAYS

Sec. 18-58. - Culvert required.
Any person attempting to construct a private way from a county road over a ditch located beside said road shall install a culvert in such ditch prior to the creation or installation of such driveway.
(Code 2005, § 38-61; Ord. of 5-7-1990, § I)
Sec. 18-59. - Placing in and filling up of ditch prohibited.
The placing in and filling up of any ditch located beside any county road for the purpose of installing or creating a private way from the county road over such ditch to a home or residence by any person is hereby prohibited.
(Code 2005, § 38-62; Ord. of 5-7-1990, § II)
Sec. 18-60. - Costs.
Any person attempting to construct five or more private ways from a county road over a ditch located beside said road shall be responsible for the costs of installing culverts in such ditch prior to the creation or installation of such driveways.
(Code 2005, § 38-63; Ord. of 5-7-1990, § III)
Sec. 18-61. - Violation.
Any person violating the terms of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished in accordance with section 1-9.
(Code 2005, § 38-64; Ord. of 5-7-1990, § IV)
