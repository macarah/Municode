ARTICLE II. - DISORDERLY CONDUCT

Sec. 16-19. - Purpose.
There is hereby created and established within the unincorporated areas of the county, an ordinance to prohibit conduct which is offensive and adverse to public safety, which conduct is hereby classified as disorderly.
(Code 2005, § 30-31; Ord. of 8-5-1991, § 1)
Sec. 16-20. - Disorderly conduct prohibited.
No person shall engage in or exhibit disorderly conduct within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Code 2005, § 30-32; Ord. of 8-5-1991, § 2)
Sec. 16-21. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Disorderly conduct means conduct which shall be deemed to be offensive and adverse to public safety and thereby classified as disorderly conduct. The term "disorderly conduct" is exhibited or occurs when any person:
(1)
Acts in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another person whereby any person is placed in reasonable fear of safety of his life, limb or health;
(2)
Acts in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another person whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Endangers the lawful pursuits of another person by acts of violence, angry threats and abusive conduct;
(4)
Causes, provokes or engages in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another person;
(5)
Assembles or congregates with another person for the purpose of causing, provoking or engaging in any fight or brawl;
(6)
Jostles or roughly crowds or pushes any person in any public place;
(7)
Collects in bodies or in crowds for unlawful purposes as defined by the current ordinances of the county;
(8)
Frequents any public place with intent to obtain money from other persons by illegal and fraudulent schemes, tricks, artifices or devices;
(9)
Assembles, for the purpose of engaging in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick, to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person in the county, or who shall aid or abet therein;
(10)
Accosts or attempts to force his company upon any person;
(11)
Utters, while in a state of anger in the presence of another, any obscene, vulgar or profane words or epithets;
(12)
Who is knowingly present at any place where illegal gaming or illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed or tolerated;
(13)
Acts in a dangerous manner towards others;
(14)
Uses fighting words, as defined by state law, directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates a turmoil;
(15)
Assembles or congregates for the purpose of trouncing upon another;
(16)
By acts of violence, interferes with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation;
(17)
Congregates with another in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuses to clear any such public way when ordered by the law enforcement officers or other lawful authority;
(18)
Enters upon the tracks, rights-of-way, yards, piggyback facilities, agencies, building or other properties of any railroad company operating in the county, provided that this subsection shall not apply to on-duty officers, agents or employees of any railroad company operating in the county, or those persons conducting bona fide business with the railroad company;
(19)
Tampers with any railroad switch, wire or line or signal or places any object on the track that would otherwise obstruct the movement or operation of a train, provided that this subsection shall not apply to on-duty officers, agents or employees of any railroad company operating in the county or those persons conducting bona fide business with the railroad company;
(20)
Gets on or off any locomotive engine, railroad car, trailer or container, provided that this subsection shall not apply to on-duty officers, agents or employees of any railroad company operating in the county or those persons conducting bona fide business with the railroad company.
(Code 2005, § 30-33; Ord. of 8-5-1991, § 3)
Sec. 16-22. - Enforcement.
Any magistrate of the county, the sheriff of the county, the deputies thereof, and all other law enforcement officers, to specifically include railroad police, shall be authorized to issue citations to violators of any provisions of this article. Such citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is charged, shall have an identifying number by which it shall be filed with the court, shall indicate the identity of the accused and the date of service, and shall be signed by the officer who completes and services it.
(Code 2005, § 30-34; Ord. of 8-5-1991, § 4)
Sec. 16-23. - Penalty.
Any person violating this article, or any provision hereof, upon conviction, shall be punished in accordance with section 1-9.
(Code 2005, § 30-35; Ord. of 8-5-1991, § 5)
Sec. 16-24. - Court proceedings.
(a)
Violations of this article shall be tried upon citation as described in section 16-22 and may be tried with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations. The county attorney or the solicitor of the state court of the county shall serve as prosecuting attorney.
(b)
Violations of this article shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county, and shall be tried in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 15-10-1 et seq.
(c)
Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 2005, § 30-36; Ord. of 8-5-1991, § 6)

ARTICLE III. - INDIGENT DEFENSE ORDINANCE

Sec. 16-52. - Application for indigent defense services.
(a)
Each person lacking the financial ability to provide for themselves representation in their defense in criminal proceedings in the state court or magistrate court of the county, and desiring that the county provide such services to them, shall submit an application to the county requesting indigent defense representation and setting forth the specifics supporting their need for such indigent representation.
(b)
The application shall be submitted to the court clerk (upon intake at the jail, upon first appearance, and at any pre-trail hearing) on a form approved by the governing authority of the county.
(Ord. No. 2005-4, § 1, 3-8-2005)
Sec. 16-53. - Application fee.
Each application for indigent defense services shall pay an application fee of $50.00 to the clerk of the court in which indigent representation is being sought. Such fee must be paid either at the time the application is submitted or at the time the representation is provided.
(Ord. No. 2005-4, § 2, 3-8-2005)
Sec. 16-54. - Wavier.
The fee required by section 16-53 may be waived only upon order of the presiding judge scheduled to hear the case in which the applicant is seeking a waiver of the application fee. The judge may order a waiver of the fee only upon a finding by the court that the defendant is unable to pay the fee or that paying the fee would cause hardship to the defendant.
(Ord. No. 2005-4, § 3, 3-8-2005)
Sec. 16-55. - Transfer of funds.
Within ten days after the end of each month, the clerk of the court, in receipt of application fees required by section 16-53, shall remit the total of all application fees collected during the previous month to the governing authority of the county.
(Ord. No. 2005-4, § 4, 3-8-2005)
Sec. 16-56. - Separate account.
The funds remitted to the governing authority pursuant to section 16-55, shall be maintained in a separate fund for indigent defense services and shall be expended only for the purpose of providing legal representation to indigent defendants in the state court and magistrate court of the county.
(Ord. No. 2005-4, § 5, 3-8-2005)
