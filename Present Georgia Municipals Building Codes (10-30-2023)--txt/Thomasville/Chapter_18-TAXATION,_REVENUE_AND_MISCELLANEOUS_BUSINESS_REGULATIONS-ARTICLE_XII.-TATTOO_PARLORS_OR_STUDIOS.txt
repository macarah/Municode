ARTICLE XII. - TATTOO PARLORS OR STUDIOS

Sec. 18-421. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Tattoo means to mark or color the skin by pricking in, piercing, or implanting indelible pigments or dyes under the skin.
Tattoo artist means any person who performs tattooing, except that the term tattoo artist shall not include in its meaning any physician or osteopath licensed under O.C.G.A. title 43, ch. 34 (O.C.G.A. § 43-34-1 et seq.), nor shall it include any technician acting under the direct supervision of such licensed physician or osteopath.
Tattoo parlor shall have the same meaning as the term "tattoo studio."
Tattoo studio means any facility or building on a fixed foundation wherein a tattoo artist performs tattooing.
(Code 1991, § 18-270; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-270), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-422. - Application for permit; annual permit renewal.
(a)
Any person desiring to operate a tattoo parlor or studio or desiring to operate as a tattoo artist shall make application for a permit at the office of the tax and licensing official. The applicant, in addition to the payment of the regulatory fee as required by section 18-54, shall also be charged a one-time application and background investigation fee of $60.00 or such fee as shall be set from time to time by the city manager, to defray the cost of processing the application. The minimum age of each applicant shall be 18 years of age. Applications shall furnish two photographs showing a front and side picture of the full face of the applicant, size 2½ inches by 2¾ inches. The applicant shall present a medical certificate from a medical doctor licensed to practice in the state, certifying that the person is sound physically and mentally, has good eyesight and is not affected with a disease which can be communicated through openings in the human skin. The applicant shall also submit an approved permit as issued by the county health department pursuant to the provisions of section 18-423, and shall comply with all other requirements of this article.
(b)
Applicants who are issued a permit under the provisions of this article shall apply for renewal of the permit to the office of the tax and licensing official, and pay the regulatory fee by December 31 of each year preceding the year for which the license is issued, or shall cease to operate a tattoo parlor or studio or engage in the practice or business of tattooing until such permit is renewed.
(Code 1991, § 18-271; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-271), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-423. - Permit from county health department required.
(a)
A condition precedent to making an application for a permit or for the renewal of a permit under section 18-422 shall be satisfactory proof that the owner or operator of the tattoo parlor or studio has secured from the county health department a permit to operate the establishment.
(b)
The county health department, through its designated representatives, may conduct periodic unannounced inspections of any tattoo parlor or studio for the purpose of determining whether or not the establishment and the person performing the art of tattooing therein are in compliance with all applicable rules and regulations of the county health department.
(c)
Any tattoo parlor or studio operated pursuant to this article shall have an inspection performed by the county health department during each calendar year that a permit issued pursuant to this article is in effect. A copy of the report issued by the county health department shall be maintained at all times on the premises of the tattoo parlor or studio and shall be produced upon request of any city code enforcement officer.
(Code 1991, § 18-272; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-272), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-424. - Prohibited practices.
(a)
No person shall operate a tattoo parlor or studio or engage in the practice or business of tattooing human skin as a tattoo artist unless the person has first secured a permit to do so from the county health department; has complied with all ordinances of the city, including, but not limited to, its zoning ordinance; has obtained the permit required by section 18-422; and has paid the regulatory fee set forth in section 18-54.
(b)
No tattoo artist shall tattoo any person while the tattoo artist is under the influence of alcohol, drugs or other stimulants or depressants; nor shall the tattoo artist tattoo any person who is under the influence of either alcohol, drugs or other stimulants or depressants; or who is of unsound mind.
(Code 1991, § 18-273; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-273), 2-13-2006)
State Law reference— Tattooing minors, O.C.G.A. § 16-5-71.
Sec. 18-425. - Enforcement; right of entry.
Any designated representative of the county health department or any city code enforcement officer, in order to enforce compliance with this article, shall be permitted to enter upon and inspect the premises of a tattoo parlor or studio at any reasonable time and in a reasonable manner.
(Code 1991, § 18-274; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-274), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-426. - Authority of county health department to suspend, revoke, or deny permit.
The county health department may suspend or revoke a permit issued pursuant to this article where the health and safety of the public require such action. When, in the judgment of such a duly authorized agent of the county health department, it is necessary and proper that a permit previously granted be suspended or revoked, the holder of the permit shall be so notified in writing and shall be afforded an opportunity for hearing pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 31-5-2. In the event that such permit is finally suspended or revoked, the holder of such permit shall be given notice in writing, which notice shall specifically state the reasons why the permit has been suspended or revoked.
(Code 1991, § 18-275; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-275), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-427. - Appeal of suspension or revocation of permit.
Any person substantially affected by any final order of the county health department, suspending, revoking or refusing to renew any permit provided under this article may secure review thereof by appeal to the state department of public health as provided at O.C.G.A. § 31-5-3.
(Code 1991, § 18-276; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-276), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-428. - Permit issued by the board of health not in lieu of city permit.
Any permit issued for the operation of a tattoo parlor or studio by the county health department shall be cumulative and are not in lieu of any permit issued by the city.
(Code 1991, § 18-277; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-277), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-429. - Penalty.
Any person violating any provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to the penalty set by the municipal court of the city, under the provisions of section 4.13 of the Charter.
(Code 1991, § 18-278; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-278), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-430. - Zoning restriction.
Any tattoo parlor or studio operated pursuant to this article shall be permitted only in a district zoned C-1 or lower, except as hereafter provided. No tattoo parlor or studio shall be allowed in districts zoned as A, R1A, R1B, R1, R2A, R2B, C1A, MH, RTH, RCD, or the city historic districts.
(Code 1991, § 18-279; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-279), 2-13-2006)
Sec. 18-431. - Incorporation of state law.
The provisions of this article are adopted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 31-40-9. All provisions of O.C.G.A. title 31, ch. 40 (O.C.G.A. § 31-40-1 et seq.) not inconsistent with the provisions of this article are hereby incorporated by reference.
(Code 1991, § 18-280; Ord. No. 02132006-2 , § I(18-280), 2-13-2006)
