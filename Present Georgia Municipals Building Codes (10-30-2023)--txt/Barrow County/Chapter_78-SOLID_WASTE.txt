Sec. 78-31. - Title of article.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the Barrow County Debris and Waste Control Environmental Ordinance.
(Code 1993, § 21-26)
Sec. 78-32. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dump: To throw, discard, chuck, place, deposit, discharge, burn or dispose of a substance.
Handbill: Any printed or written matter, any sample or device, any dodger, circular, leaflet, pamphlet, paper or booklet or any other printed or otherwise reproduced original of copies of any matter of literature which:
(1)
Advertises for sale any merchandise, product, commodity, service or thing;
(2)
Directs attention to any business or mercantile or commercial establishment, or other activity, for the purpose of either directly or indirectly promoting the interest thereof by sales;
(3)
Directs attention to or advertises any meeting, theatrical performance, exhibition or event of any kind, for which an admission fee is charged for the purpose of private gain or profit; but the terms of this subsection shall not apply where an admission fee is charged, collected or taken up for the purpose of defraying the expenses incident to such meeting, theatrical performance, exhibition or event of any kind, when any such event is held, given or takes place in connection with the dissemination of information which is not restricted under the ordinary laws of obscenity, public peace, safety and good order; provided that nothing contained in this subsection shall be deemed to authorize the holding, giving or taking place of any meeting, theatrical performance, exhibition or event of any kind without a license where such license is or may be required by any law of this state or under any ordinance of the county; or
(4)
While containing reading matter other than advertising matter, is predominantly and essentially an advertisement and is distributed or circulated for advertising purposes or for the private benefit and gain of any person so engaged as advertiser or distributor.
Hazardous waste: Any solid waste which has been defined as a hazardous waste in regulations promulgated by the administrator of the United States Environmental Protection Agency pursuant to the federal act which are in force and effect on February 1, 1996, codified as 40 CFR 261.3, and any designated hazardous waste.
Litter: All discarded substances and materials, including without limitation all sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste materials, tin cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals, building or construction materials, demolition debris, mechanical equipment or parts, or scrap tires or any and all other materials or substances of any kind and description resulting from domestic, industrial, commercial or mining operations.
Litter receptacle: Those containers acceptable to the county and which may be standardized as to size, shape, capacity and color, as well as any other receptacles suitable for the depositing of litter.
Person: Any natural person, corporation, partnership, association, firm, receiver, guardian, trustee, executor, administrator, fiduciary or representative or group of persons or entities of any kind.
Private property: Any dwelling, house, building or other structure, whether inhabited or used permanently or temporarily, or continuously uninhabited or vacant, which is 100 percent privately owned and shall include any yard, grounds, walk, parking area, driveway, porch, steps, vestibule or mailbox belonging or pertinent to such dwelling, house, building or other structure.
Public place: Any area that is used or held out for use by the public, whether owned or operated by public or private interests, including but not limited to streets, parking lots, parking garages, sidewalks, rights-of-way, parks, campgrounds, highways, alleys and other public ways.
Vehicle: Includes every device capable of being moved upon a public highway and in, upon or by which any person or property may be transported or drawn upon a public highway, including devices moved by human power or used exclusively upon stationary rails or tracks.
(Code 1993, § 21-27; Ord. of 7-14-1998(1))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 78-33. - Purpose of article.
This article is adopted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20 and the authority and need to protect and preserve the public health, safety, morality, order, property values and welfare of the citizens of the county, as well as to maintain a healthful, clean and beautiful environment. This article is intended to supplement and to coordinate existing litter control and removal efforts, and not to terminate existing efforts, nor, except as specifically stated, to repeal or affect any other ordinance governing or prohibiting litter or the control and disposition of waste.
(Code 1993, § 21-28)
Sec. 78-34. - Penalty; restitution.
The punishment imposed for any violation of this article shall be the maximum fine allowed by state law for violations of county ordinances, or 60 days' imprisonment, or both; however, the minimum punishment imposed for any violation of this article shall be a fine of $500.00 for the first offense, and $1,000.00 for the second and subsequent offenses. In addition to the fines and/or imprisonment imposed, the offender shall be required to pick up and remove and any and all litter and debris from any public right-of-way or public park for a distance not to exceed one mile. Notwithstanding any penalties imposed for violations of any part of this article, the judge shall order restitution from the violator, charging the violator for actual damages incurred by any grieved party, including the county, the proceeds of which shall be dispersed to the damaged party. Each day such violation is committed or permitted shall be punishable as a separate offense.
(Code 1993, § 21-29; Ord. of 7-14-1998(1))
Sec. 78-35. - Enforcement; collection of costs incurred by county.
(a)
Notice of violation. Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, unless the nature of the violation of this article is such that an immediate threat to safety or health is evident, the phrase "reasonable notice," as used in this article, shall mean five days from such time as a written notice of the violation is posted on the property on which the violation has occurred and/or mailed to the owner of the property as reflected by the current property tax records of the county. Unless the nature of the violation of this article is such that an immediate threat to safety or health is evident, with respect to the second or subsequent violations of this article by the same property owner within a 24-month period of time, the phrase "reasonable notice" as used in this article shall mean three days from such time as a written notice of the violation is posted on the property and subsequently mailed to the owner of the property as reflected by the current property tax records of the county.
(b)
Failure to comply with notice. In addition to authorizing the removal of litter or other refuse by county employees at the property owner's expense, the failure to cure or eliminate a violation of this article as to which notice has been provided within the time period set forth in subsection (a) of this section shall result in the issuance of a citation, returnable to the magistrate court of the county, to the property owner or his agent, for a violation thereof.
(c)
Collection of costs incurred by county. To the extent that any property owner in the county becomes obligated to repay or pay to the county any of the costs imposed in this article, the county shall have the right to collect such costs by way of all available legal process, including but not limited to suit in magistrate, state or superior court and the right to pursue all available and lawful post-judgment remedies. A judgment obtained against a person under this article becomes a lien against the person's property in the manner provided by law. Interest on any such costs owed by any person to the county shall run at the rate of six percent per year from the date of completion of the work by the county until judgment or collection, whichever shall first occur and at the legal rate of interest per year from judgment until collection.
(d)
Citations. Nothing in this section shall preclude the county from enforcing the terms of this article by way of immediate issuance of a citation for any violation thereof.
(e)
Injunctive relief. Nothing in this section shall preclude the county from seeking appropriate injunctive relief to halt continuing violations of this article or to halt a nuisance.
(f)
Inspection and enforcement officers. Inspection and enforcement shall be by the code enforcement officers of the county and any other law enforcement officials responsible for carrying out and enforcing the laws of the county and the state.
(Code 1993, § 21-30; Ord. of 7-14-1998(1); Ord. of 8-9-2005, § 1)
Sec. 78-36. - Deposit of litter on public property.
No person shall throw, drop or in any way deposit litter on any public property except in authorized and identified litter receptacles.
(Code 1993, § 21-31)
Sec. 78-37. - Property maintenance; storage of refuse generally.
(a)
Duties of property owner. Each owner of private or public property in the county shall keep the property free and clear of any litter or other refuse which may constitute a threat or detriment to the public safety, welfare, property values, morality or good order.
(b)
Removal of litter and other refuse. Each person who owns private property in the county shall, at such time as the county may prescribe, remove to a prescribed and approved location or permit county employees to enter and remove from such property any and all litter and other substances which might endanger the health, safety, morality, order, property values or welfare of residents of the county. A failure to remove such litter or other substances may result, whenever the board of commissioners deems necessary and after reasonable notice, in the removal of such litter or other substances which might endanger the health, safety or welfare of residents of the county by the board of commissioners, in which event the costs or expenses thereof shall be chargeable to and paid by the owners of such property and may be collected by the county in the manner provided in this article.
(c)
(1)
Cutting of grass and weeds. Any corporation, entity, or person owning, claiming, occupying, or having supervision or control of any real property located within Barrow County shall maintain all vegetation, including, but not limited to, the grass, weeds, and other vegetative growth on such property. Maintenance required pursuant to this section shall include the following:
a.
The height of grass and other vegetation, excluding trees, shrubs, or other similar plant forms, shall not exceed 12 inches at any given time of the year. Grass and other vegetation, whether located on the front, rear or sides of property, should be routinely mowed, trimmed, and cut.
b.
All grass and other vegetation surrounding buildings, poles, electrical box covers, vehicles, homes, and other similar structures shall be mowed, trimmed, and cut so that the height does not exceed 12 inches at any given time of the year.
c.
Any invasive vines or shrubs shall be maintained in such a manner so as to prevent encroachment of such vegetation onto roads, signs, or other structures.
(2)
Exceptions: Any property zoned agriculture shall be exempt from the requirements set forth in subsection (c)(1) above.
(d)
Adequate receptacles required. It shall be the duty of every tenant, lessee or occupant of any residence or establishment where people reside, congregate or are employed to provide sufficient garbage containers or receptacles to effectively handle the accumulation of garbage, waste, litter and debris during the intervals between collections and to maintain the property free and clear from litter and debris which may constitute a threat or detriment to the public safety, welfare, property values, morality or good order of the area.
(e)
Removal of furniture, appliances and other oversized items. In reference to any discarded furniture, appliances, machinery or other oversized litter or trash, arrangements must be made by the property owner, at his expense, for removal and disposal. Under no circumstances shall a discarded refrigerator, icebox, freezer, washer, dryer or stove or other item capable of entrapping a child be left in an area accessible to children while awaiting removal. Any such items left outside on private property for a period of seven days or more, unless part of an authorized business operation, will be presumed to be discarded and after reasonable notice, be removed by the county at the property owner's expense.
(f)
Standards for receptacles and other containers. All litter receptacles, plastic bags or single-use paper or cardboard boxes shall be kept clean and free of accumulated waste and shall be treated with an effective insecticide if necessary to prevent nuisance. The capacity of the individual refuse receptacle, if used, shall not exceed 90 pounds total weight; provided that special receptacles of other capacities may be used with the written permission of the county sanitarian for multifamily residences or businesses.
(g)
Maintenance of nuisance or hazard; burial of waste. Under no circumstances shall any private property owner be permitted to store, accumulate or otherwise maintain any litter, debris or other refuse on the premises in such quantities as to constitute a nuisance or be harmful to the health or safety of the public; nor shall the burial of any litter, debris or other refuse be allowed on any private property, except where a special use permit has been issued for use as an inert landfill or sanitary landfill in compliance with the county zoning resolution and all applicable state, federal and county laws.
(Code 1993, § 21-32; Ord. of 7-14-1998(1)); Ord. of 3-9-1999; Ord. of 8-9-2005, § 2)
State Law reference— Abandonment, discarding, etc., of containers which lock or fasten automatically, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-100.
Sec. 78-38. - Dumping; use of private refuse container without permission; placement of unsorted waste in recycling container.
(a)
No person shall dump or otherwise dispose of litter, any waste containing hazardous substances, or other refuse or unsightly matter on any public or private property unless the property is specifically approved by the county or the state for dumping.
(b)
It is a violation of this article to dump any domestic, industrial, commercial or mining waste or litter, or refuse, into any private dumpster, container or private collection system without permission from the owner.
(c)
The dumping of any litter or waste materials into any container specifically designated as a recycling container is prohibited unless the materials discarded are properly separated and sorted into categories which are deposited into the containers specifically approved for the same type of recyclable waste and not mixed with any other debris or refuse.
(Code 1993, § 21-33; Ord. of 7-14-1998(1))
Sec. 78-39. - Throwing litter from vehicle.
It shall be unlawful to throw, drop or otherwise deposit or dispose of litter or any other refuse from any moving vehicle.
(Code 1993, § 21-34)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 86.
Sec. 78-40. - Allowing load to drop or leak from vehicle.
No vehicle shall be driven or moved on any street, highway or other public road within the county unless such vehicle is constructed or loaded to prevent any of its load from dropping, sifting, leaking or otherwise escaping therefrom; provided, however, that sand or any substance for increasing traction, or water or other substance, may be sprinkled on a roadway and the cleaning or maintaining of such roadway may be undertaken by the governmental agency having that responsibility. Any person operating a vehicle from which any glass, litter or other object has fallen or escaped which constitutes an obstruction or could damage a vehicle or otherwise endanger travel upon such street, highway or other public road shall immediately cause the street, highway or public road to be cleaned of all glass or objects and shall pay any costs for such removal. No provision of this section shall apply to any motor vehicle which is used exclusively for agricultural purposes and which is not operated on or over any public highway for any other purpose than the purpose of operating it across the highway or along the highway from one point of the owner's land to another part thereof, irrespective of whether or not the tracts adjoin.
(Code 1993, § 21-35)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 86.
Sec. 78-41. - Refuse collection and transport; removal of hazardous waste and dead animals.
(a)
Generally. Proper collection and disposal of accumulated litter and other refuse is required, and such collection and disposal may not be conducted in a manner contrary to the public health, order, morality, safety or welfare.
(b)
Equipment standards. All equipment used for the collection and transportation of litter and other refuse shall be so designed as to prevent its escape therefrom in accordance with section 78-40. This equipment shall be provided with a watertight body designed so as to prevent the escape of any fluid. All surfaces of collection and transportation equipment coming in contact with garbage or organic commercial waste shall be smooth, nonabsorbent and in good repair. Only equipment approved by the county sanitarian may be used in the collection and transportation of litter and other refuse.
(c)
Cleaning of equipment. All equipment used in the collection and transportation of litter and other refuse shall be thoroughly washed and treated with an approved insecticide as often as the health officer shall deem necessary to prevent fly breeding and nuisances.
(d)
Removal of spilled waste. All litter and other refuse spilled during collection or transportation shall be promptly removed by the refuse collector.
(e)
Emptying of receptacles. At each collection, all litter and other refuse in the refuse receptacle shall be removed and the receptacle left covered.
(f)
Removal of hazardous waste and dead animals. Hazardous waste and dead animals shall be removed in a manner approved by the county health department and subject to any other federal, state or local ordinances, laws, rules, statutes or regulations as may apply. The fee for burying dead animals shall be a minimum charge of $50.00 for the first hour and $50.00 per hour thereafter.
(g)
Collection service. The county has arranged for the collection of litter and other refuse from private or public property. Property owners choosing to subscribe shall be responsible for payment to the contractor of such reasonable charges as may from time to time be set for the regular removal of litter and other refuse.
(Code 1993, § 21-36)
Cross reference— Animals, ch. 14; traffic and vehicles, ch. 86.
State Law reference— "Hazardous constituent" defined, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(12); "hazardous substance" defined, O.C.G.A. § 12-14-1(4).
Sec. 78-42. - Litter receptacles for parking lots.
All parking lots and establishments with parking lots, public or private, shall provide litter receptacles distributed within the parking area. Such receptacles shall be weighted or attached to the ground as necessary to prevent spillage. It shall be the responsibility of the owner or manager of the property to collect the refuse and trash deposited and to provide for collection or disposal of such refuse and trash.
(Code 1993, § 21-37)
Sec. 78-43. - Storage of junk vehicles; removal by county.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, except within a fully enclosed building or structure or a building or structure otherwise shielded or screened from public view, on any property, unless properly zoned or permitted, any motor vehicle, trailer or semitrailer which is inoperative. As used in this section, the term "inoperative motor vehicle" means any motor vehicle which is not in operating condition; or which for a period of 90 days or longer has been partially or totally disassembled by the removal of tires, wheels, engine or other essential parts required for the operation of the vehicle.
(b)
The provisions of subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to a licensed business which regularly is engaged as an automobile dealer, salvage dealer or scrap processor.
(c)
If any person fails to remove from private property such inoperative motor vehicle, trailer or semitrailer when in violation of this article, then, after reasonable notice, the county, through its own agents or employees, may remove any such inoperative motor vehicle, trailer or semitrailer and dispose of it, after giving additional notice to the owner of the vehicle, and the cost of any such removal and disposal shall be chargeable to the owner of the vehicle or premises and may be collected by the county as taxes and levies are collected.
(Code 1993, § 21-38; Ord. of 3-9-1999)
Cross reference— Storage of junked and abandoned motor vehicles, §§ 86-121 et seq.
State Law reference— Removal of junked motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-4; abandoned motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-11-1 et seq.
Sec. 78-44. - Construction and demolition sites.
(a)
Applicability. All construction and demolition contractors, building permit holders and/or property owners shall adhere to all provisions of this section.
(b)
Receptacles required; accumulations of waste prohibited. On-site refuse receptacles to effectively and securely handle the accumulation of all debris, paper, building material waste, scrap building materials, concrete truck washout, chemicals, sanitary waste, and other trash and litter produced by those working on the site shall be installed and maintained. All construction and demolition sites shall be kept in a litter-free condition. Under no circumstances shall the accumulation of litter become a threat to the public safety or to the public health, welfare, property values, morality or good order of the county or portion thereof.
(c)
Removal of dirt or other materials deposited on streets. All dirt, mud, silt or any other earth deposited upon any public or private property, including streets and roads, as a result of construction and demolition shall be immediately removed by the permit holder so as to prevent any detectable accumulation which may tend to cause a threat to the public safety, property values both real and personal, or good order of the county or environment. Any construction supplies or materials, landscaping supplies or materials or other material deposited on a public road or right-of-way shall be removed on a daily basis or immediately if the presence of such materials constitutes a threat to the public health or safety. In addition to all of the remedies and relief provided in section 78-34 for violations of this article, the county marshal or building official or those agents as either may designate, employ or assign, may immediately cause to cease all work being performed at the site by the issuance of a stop work order. No further work shall resume at the site until the violation is corrected and inspected by the authority which issued the stop work order and all reinspection fees that are assessed are paid in full.
(d)
Burial of waste. Under no circumstances shall any litter, waste or debris, including but not limited to brush, limbs, stumps, roots and other byproducts of the construction or destruction process, be buried on any property not expressly and properly permitted for such activity.
(e)
Burning of waste. No burning of brush, trash, waste or debris may be conducted on any property unless the proper permit is secured from the state and/or the county.
(Code 1993, § 21-39; Ord. of 7-14-1998(1); Ord. of 3-9-1999; Ord. of 6-10-2014, § 1)
Sec. 78-45. - Distribution of handbills and advertising matter.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place in or on any automobile in the county any handbill, circular, pamphlet, poster, postcard or other literature, except with the express permission of the owner of the automobile.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or drop on any private property any handbill, circular, pamphlet, poster, postcard or other literature, except with the express permission of the owner of the property or intended recipient. The provisions of this section shall not apply to the distribution of mail by the United States Postal Service or to newspapers, except that newspapers shall be placed on private property in such a manner as to prevent their being carried or deposited by the elements upon any street, sidewalk or other public or private property.
(Code 1993, § 21-40)
Sec. 78-46. - Posting of notices on utility poles or other public structures.
No person shall post or affix any notice, poster or other paper or device calculated to attract the attention of the public to any public utility pole or other public lamppost or structure or within a county right-of-way unless expressly authorized.
(Code 1993, § 21-41; Ord. of 8-14-01(1))
Cross reference— Utilities, ch. 90.
Sec. 78-47. - Scavenging.
No person other than the owner thereof, or an agent or an employee of the county, shall interfere with any container placed for the purpose of storing refuse pending collection, or remove or take any of the contents thereof, or remove any such container from the location where the container has been placed by the owner thereof.
(Code 1993, § 21-42)

Sec. 78-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Scrap tire: A tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage or defect. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(31))
Scrap tire carrier: Any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires, not otherwise exempted under the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, section 391-3- 4-.19(5.g), for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user or disposal facility. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(32))
Scrap tire generator: Any person who generates scrap tires. Generators may include but are not limited to retail tire dealers, retreaders, scrap tire processors, automobile dealers, private company vehicle maintenance shops, garages, service stations and city, county and state governments.
Tire: A continuous solid or pneumatic rubber covering designed for encircling the wheel of a motor vehicle and which is neither attached to the motor vehicle nor a part of the motor vehicle as original equipment. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(38))
Tire retailer: Any person engaged in the business of selling new or replacement tires. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(39))
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(1), § 2(21-51))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 78-72. - Compliance with state regulations; depositing tires in refuse container without permission.
(a)
All persons defined as scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers, tire retailers and/or scrap tire processors shall be subject to rules as defined in the Rules for Solid Waste Management, section 391-3-4-.19, and handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 12-8-20 et seq., and the Rules for Solid Waste Management, chapter 391-3-4, applicable to solid waste.
(b)
No person shall deposit a scrap tire in any container or receptacle unless authorized by the owner of the receptacle or the board of commissioners or its designee.
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(1), § 2(21-52))
Sec. 78-73. - Enforcement; penalty.
(a)
Any person violating any portion of this article shall be punished by a fine of not less that $250.00 and not more than $1,000.00 and/or punishment within the sound discretion of the magistrate court of the county.
(b)
Inspection and enforcement shall be by the code enforcement officer of the county and any other law enforcement officials responsible for carrying out and enforcing the laws of the county and the state.
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(1), § 2(21-53))

ARTICLE IV. - HOST FEE SURCHARGE

Sec. 78-101. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Landfill: An area of land on which, or an excavation in which, solid waste is placed for permanent disposal and which is not a land application unit, surface impoundment, injection well or compost pile. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(14))
Private: That property as belongs absolutely to an individual and of which he has the exclusive right of disposition. For purposes of this article, the term "individual" shall encompass all companies not wholly owned by a political subdivision of the state, by any state of the United States or by the United States Government.
Solid waste: Any garbage or refuse, sludge from a wastewater treatment plant, water supply treatment plant or air pollution control facility and other discarded material, including solid, semisolid or contained gaseous material resulting from industrial, commercial, mining and agricultural operations and community activities, but does not include recovered materials, solid or dissolved materials in irrigation return flows or industrial discharges that are point sources subject to permit under 33 USC 1342 or source, special nuclear or byproduct material as defined by the federal Atomic Energy Act of 1954, as amended (68 Stat. 923). (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(33))
Solid waste handling: The storage, collection, transportation, treatment, utilization, processing or disposal of solid waste or any combination of such activities. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(34))
Solid waste handling facility: Any facility the primary purpose of which is the storage, collection, transportation, treatment, utilization, processing or disposal or any combination thereof, of solid waste. (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(35))
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(2), § 2(21-76))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 78-102. - Imposition of surcharge.
(a)
All private solid waste handling facilities located partially or entirely within the political boundaries of the county shall be subject to host fees to be paid to the county.
(b)
The surcharges shall be imposed pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 12-8-39(d), as amended, on a per ton of solid waste basis for each ton of solid waste disposed of in a solid waste handling facility in the county each month.
(c)
The county, as the host local government, may negotiate for and obtain, by contract, surcharges above and in addition to the statutory minimum per ton surcharge mandated by O.C.G.A. § 12-8-39(d).
(d)
The surcharge shall be imposed upon all private solid waste handling facilities and all solid waste disposed of in the county and shall be imposed equally and without regard for the place of origin, whether the place of origin of the solid waste is inside of or outside of the county or inside of or outside of the state.
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(2), § 2(21-77); Ord. of 3-10-2020, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 78-103. - Payment.
The amount of the host fee to be paid by solid waste handling facilities to the county under this article shall be calculated each calendar month and shall be paid on or before the 20th day of the following month. Should the 20th day of the month fall upon a Saturday, Sunday or legal holiday, the amount of the host fee to be paid by the solid waste handling facility shall be paid on or before the last business day prior to the 20th day of the month.
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(2), § 2(21-78))
Sec. 78-104. - Enforcement generally; inspection of records.
(a)
The county may inspect all books and records of solid waste handling facilities relating to the volume of solid waste disposed of in the landfill or other type of solid waste handling facility in order to verify the facility's calculations as to the volume of waste subject to the host fee provided for in this article.
(b)
These inspections shall be programmatic and random. No solid waste handling facility shall have its books and records inspected more than one time every six calendar months.
(c)
Should it become necessary to obtain a warrant to perform an inspection, probable cause to undertake such an inspection shall be established by showing a court of competent jurisdiction the existence of a random inspection program and establishing that the particular facility has been selected for inspection in that particular month.
(d)
Each inspection shall take place during the solid waste handling facility's normal operating hours upon 24 hours' prior notification from the county to the solid waste handling facility.
(e)
The county shall be responsible for paying all costs and expenses which it incurs in connection with these inspections.
(f)
These inspections shall be conducted in a manner as to not unreasonably interfere with the operation of the solid waste handling facility.
(g)
All information provided by the solid waste handling facility to the county with respect to the tonnage figures and any information collected by the county during any inspection of the solid waste handling facility's books and records shall remain strictly confidential, except to the extent the county may be required by law to disclose such information. It is acknowledged that this information is proprietary in nature and constitutes trade secrets belonging to the solid waste handling facility.
(h)
This article shall be enforced by the duly designated agent or appointed officer of the board of commissioners. He shall be vested with all such powers as are necessary to carry out the provisions in this article; provided that such powers shall not extend beyond such restraints as are placed upon this exercise of power by other federal, state or local law.
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(2), § 2(21-79))
Sec. 78-105. - Interest on unpaid fees.
For each month that a solid waste facility does not pay the previous month's host fee pursuant to this article, an additional interest charge of one percent per month or 12 percent per annum, shall be imposed upon all moneys owed. The minimum amount of interest to be charged for late payment for all periods for which payment is less than 30 days late shall be one percent of the amount owed for the host fee.
(Ord. of 1-13-1998(2), § 2(21-80))
