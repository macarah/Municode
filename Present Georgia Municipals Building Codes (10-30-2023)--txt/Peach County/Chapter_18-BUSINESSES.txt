Sec. 18-26. - Penalties for violation.
(a)
Nonpayment of license fees. All persons entitled to have a license issued to them under the provisions of this article who do not pay the fees by February 1 of each year, shall be guilty of doing business without a license, and shall, upon conviction, be punished as prescribed by section 1-11.
(b)
Violation of any provision of article. It shall be unlawful for any person to whom a license shall be granted under the provisions of this article to do any of the acts forbidden by this article, or to fail to do any of the things required to be done by the terms of this article. It shall be unlawful for any persons to whom a license shall be granted to violate the terms of this article, and violators shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(c)
Penalty for failure to timely obtain a license. In addition to all other penalties provided in this section, any person failing to obtain a license within 30 days from the time he first engages in such business during the calendar year shall pay a penalty of 25 percent of the scheduled amount of the license fee, which shall be added to the scheduled amount of the license fee.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-27. - Registration.
(a)
Required annually. Every person who exercises or may exercise within the unincorporated area of the county any profession, trade, calling, or business, of any nature whatever, shall annually register such profession, trade, calling or business with the county commissioners' office. This registration must be done by January 30 in each year. Any person who begins business for a trade or a profession in the unincorporated area of the county after January 30 in any year shall register their name and business as provided in this section.
(b)
Requirement for persons with no fixed places of business; tax. Whenever any person having no fixed place of business within the unincorporated area of the county shall nevertheless exercise within the unincorporated area of the county any business of any nature whatever, such person is required to register his name and business and be subjected to the same or like taxation on his business as is levied by the board of commissioners upon persons following a like business and having a fixed place of business.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-28. - Tax imposed on all businesses by board of commissioners.
Any business, trade, profession or calling upon which no license or tax is specifically imposed by the provisions of this article shall pay a license or tax to be fixed by the board of commissioners in conformity with the terms of this article.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-29. - Business in which license fees based on inventory; affidavit; intentional inaccuracy cause for revocation of license.
Each person operating a business in which their license fee is based on stock or inventory shall furnish the commissioners' office on or before January 30 in each year, an affidavit stating the average cost value of stock carried during the preceding year and estimate of the average cost value of stock to be carried during the current year. If no business was operated during the preceding year, only an estimate to the current year shall be included. The affidavit shall further list such additional lines of business to be engaged in by the affidavit in connection with the business described in this classification. The intentional inaccuracy of the information contained in such affidavit is cause for the revocation of the license or the assessment of 25 percent of the regular license fee attaching to the classification in which the particular business falls, whichever may be determined by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-30. - Advertisement of business creates liability for license; license issued to person applying; due date of business license tax; expiry of license; clearance of zoning office.
(a)
Any person advertising by signs, cards, circulars, newspaper advertising, or otherwise that he is in a business of any kind shall be held liable for the license required for such business.
(b)
Each license shall be in the name of the person to which the license is issued, and each tax levied by this article shall be due and payable on January 30 of each year, except as otherwise provided, and the license issued under this article shall expire on December 31 of each year.
(c)
All business license applicants must clear the zoning office prior to the issuance of the license.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-31. - Posting of license.
Each license shall be posted conspicuously at or in the place of business or the person taking out such license.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-32. - Determination of graduated tax based on stock value.
Whenever in this article a license, business or other tax is a graduated one based upon the value of stock, the word stock shall be deemed to mean the average stock on hand for the entire calendar year preceding for which the tax is levied. If the taxpayer was not in business during the entire preceding year, then the average shall be computed for the time the taxpayer was in business. If a business is begun after January 1 of the year for which the tax was levied, the value of the stock shall be determined as of the date of commencing business.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-33. - Classification of licensed applicant's business.
(a)
Oath required. In all cases the person applying for a license shall make oath, when required to do so, that the business conducted by the applicant falls within the class to which the ordinance fixing the tax specifically applies. Such licenses are imposed upon each separate business and apply only to that particular business and do not include any other business for which a specific license is required.
(b)
Number of employees. In determining the number of employees connected with a business, the owners, officers, or proprietors giving their personal attention to the business shall be considered employees for the purpose of this article.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-34. - Examination of taxpayer's books to determine compliance.
Whenever in order to ensure the proper collection of the licenses and taxes imposed and levied by this article, it is necessary to examine the books of the taxpayer to determine his compliance with the article, it shall be lawful for any authorized representative of the county to examine the books and records of any taxpayer whose books and records are necessary to such determination. Each taxpayer affected hereby shall allow reasonable access to such books and records, upon demand, and, upon failure to do so, shall be subject to the penal provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-35. - Payment of business license taxes.
(a)
License, occupation, and specific taxes are levied annually, unless otherwise provided for, except that when a business begins operation after January 1, the tax shall be due and payable when the business commences. Fees for licenses issued after July 1 shall be one-half the annual prescribed rate.
(b)
Licenses and taxes levied and collected under any of the sections of this article shall be payable in United States currency or coupons of bonds, as provided in the act authorizing the issue of the bonds.
(c)
If any license or tax, or any part thereof imposed in this article, shall be judged invalid for any reason, or any person engaging in the business or occupation falling under such invalid license, or tax or part thereof, who has not paid his license or tax imposed in this article, shall be liable and shall pay to the county the same license or tax paid for the preceding year.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-36. - Transferability of license.
No license is transferable for the person to whom it is issued, except by the payment of a fee amounting to ten percent of the cost of such license. No business shall change location without the approval of the county commissioners' office. This shall not include an alcoholic beverage license.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-37. - Separate license and fees for each business location.
Where a person conducts a business at more than one store, location or place, each store, location or place shall be considered a separate business under the terms of this article, and a separate business license fee or occupation tax shall be levied and paid. Should more than one business on which a business license fee or occupation tax is levied by this article be conducted in or in conjunction with one place or kind of business, each business shall be subject to a separate business license fee or occupation tax.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-38. - Disabled veteran's exemption.
As provided by O.C.G.A. § 43-12-1, an application for an exemption from the license fee must include a certificate of exemption issued by the commissioner of veterans service establishing the applicant's disabled veteran status.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-39. - Issuance of license conditioned on payment of past license fees.
A condition to the issuance of any license shall be payment by the applicant of all past due licenses.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)
Sec. 18-40. - Repeal of conflicting ordinances.
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict with this article are hereby repealed.
(Ord. of 1-1-93)

Sec. 18-81. - Operational requirements.
The following operational requirements relating to junkyards and auto salvage yards shall be observed:
(1)
All junkyard and salvage operations shall be completely enclosed by a buffer or wall which limits visibility into the area to ten percent, having a minimum height of six feet, but in no case less than such a height as will effectively screen all operations from view. Driveways shall be enclosed with gates which screen from view all storage of junk in the area.
(2)
The number of vehicular driveways permitted on any single street frontage shall be limited to one per 500 feet with a minimum of 30 feet driveway width.
(3)
All salvageable parts stored on dirt shall be racked at least 18 inches above ground in neat racks, to prevent rats from harboring under scrap. The area under the racks shall be cleaned by mowing or raking.
(4)
Any scrap left after salvaging parts shall be removed from the premises and not allowed to accumulate and rust away.
(5)
Any building used to store parts or dismantle parts shall be completely enclosed if in front of the screen. No wrecks shall be allowed to be parked outside the screened area.
(6)
Used cars held for sale must be in running order and moved out within six months. Any such used car lot shall be fenced with a six-inch woven wire fence, and screened from any adjoining residential district.
(7)
Only one caretaker's residence or mobile home shall be allowed on any one salvage yard.
(8)
Any application for a certificate of occupancy for a salvage business shall contain the requirements of section 112.1 of the county comprehensive land development resolution and a plan showing screening and the effective dates that the screening shall be such that all operations will be hid from view, and the type of screening proposed, together with a topographical survey of the land so that the drainage capabilities may be determined.
(9)
No junkyard or auto salvage yard shall be permitted to locate closer than 300 feet to a residence and no closer than 50 feet to any one or rear line, or closer than 200 feet to any county road, except for completely enclosed buildings.
(10)
Junkyards and auto salvage operations may be located only in M-2, RAG and RR-1 areas as defined in the county comprehensive land development resolution.
(Ord. of 5-10-77)
