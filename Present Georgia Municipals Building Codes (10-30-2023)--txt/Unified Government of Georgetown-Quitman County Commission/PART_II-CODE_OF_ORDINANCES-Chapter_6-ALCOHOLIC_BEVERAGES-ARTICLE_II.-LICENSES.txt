ARTICLE II. - LICENSES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-46. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell or possess for the purpose of sale any alcoholic beverages where such person does not have a license from Georgetown-Quitman County to sell or possess for sale such beverages.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-47. - Compliance with all applicable provisions.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued to the premises of an applicant that does not comply with all codes, ordinances and laws of the state and of Georgetown-Quitman County, whether it is an initial license, renewal or transfer of applicant or location.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
State Law reference— Permit or license from county or municipality required for wholesale or retail sales of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2; powers of local governing authorities as to the granting, refusal, suspension or revocation of alcoholic beverage permits or licenses, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 6-48. - Classification.
(a)
All alcoholic beverage licenses shall henceforth be classified as follows:
(1)
Class I license means that license granted to any person by Georgetown-Quitman County to an applicant for the sale and distribution of alcoholic beverages sold at wholesale to any valid alcoholic beverage licensee.
(2)
Class II license means that license granted to any persons by Georgetown-Quitman County to an applicant for package sales of wine and malt beverages sold at retail to the public for consumption off the premises.
(3)
Class III license means that license granted to any persons by Georgetown-Quitman County to an applicant for package sales of distilled spirits sold at retail to the public for consumption off the premises.
(4)
Class IV license means that license granted to any person by Georgetown-Quitman County to an applicant for an on-premises beer and wine consumption license at a location that meets the definition of a restaurant under this chapter.
(5)
Class V license means that license granted to any person by Georgetown-Quitman County to an applicant for an on-premises distilled spirits consumption license at a location that meets the definition of a restaurant under this chapter.
(b)
All applications and licenses issued hereafter shall be in keeping with the above classifications.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-49. - Separate licenses required.
A separate alcoholic beverage license is hereby required for each place of business when such application includes various classes of licenses (Class I, II, III, IV, V).
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-50. - Georgetown-Quitman County to maintain files.
Georgetown-Quitman County shall endeavor to accumulate and maintain a complete file of licenses granted under the terms and conditions of this chapter. Such file may contain any information deemed appropriate by the Georgetown-Quitman County, and may include applications, investigative reports, licenses issued, incidents involving licensee and/or place of business, code and/or building violations, hearings/results, premises inspections, photographs and other such information and data that may assist Georgetown-Quitman County in the determination of the decision to grant, renew, transfer, name change or other license actions required and/or applied for within Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Secs. 6-51—6-65. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - APPLICATION

Sec. 6-66. - General prerequisites for approval.
No pending or future application for a Georgetown-Quitman County alcoholic beverage license shall be approved by Georgetown-Quitman County unless it appears that all material and valid requirements of this chapter have been met by the applicant and that the applicant is qualified to hold a license, should it be issued, under this chapter. In addition, all applicants for a Georgetown-Quitman County alcoholic beverage license in the county must have all local taxes paid in full at the time said application is submitted, (with the exception of any taxes which are in arbitration) and must have first paid the county all money, taxes or otherwise, due and owed as of the date the application is submitted.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14; Ord. No. O2-2020 , 3-9-20)
Sec. 6-67. - New license, approval required.
No new alcoholic beverage license shall be issued until first having received approval of the license application by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-68. - Procedure.
All applicants for an alcoholic beverage license shall comply with the following application procedure:
(1)
All applications for an alcoholic beverage license shall be submitted on forms provided for that purpose by Georgetown-Quitman County Clerk and shall be approved by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County prior to the issuance of the license applied for.
(2)
The application shall include but shall not be limited to: the name and address of the applicant; the proposed business to be carried on; if a partnership, the names and residence address of the partners; if a limited liability company, L.L.C., the name and address of the manager(s) and the name of any person or legal entity owning any percentage of the L.L.C.; if a corporation, the names of the officers, the name and address of the registered agent for service of process, the name of the officer(s), and the name of all shareholders of any class of corporate stock, or any other entity having a financial interest in each entity which is to own or operate the establishment for which a license is sought. If the manager changes, the applicant must furnish the commission the name and address of the new manager and other information as requested within ten days of such change.
(3)
All applicants shall furnish data, fingerprints, financial responsibility and other records as required by the licensing and revenue manager and to ensure compliance with the provisions of this article. Failure to furnish data, pursuant to such request shall automatically serve to dismiss the application with prejudice.
(4)
All applications shall be sworn to by the applicant before a notary public or other officer empowered by law to administer oaths as to the information contained in the application and to any other information required or contained within the application and accompanying information and by this chapter.
(5)
In all instances in which an application is denied under the provisions of this chapter the applicant may not reapply for a license for at least one year from the final date of such denial.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-69. - Minimum applicant qualifications.
Any applicant for an alcoholic beverage license shall meet the minimum qualifications upon the submission of an application for same, as follows:
(1)
Be 21 years of age.
(2)
Meet all minimum requirements for such license imposed by the state.
(3)
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be granted to any person who is not a citizen of the United States or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence.
(4)
Where the applicant is a partnership, limited liability company or corporation, the provisions of this section shall apply to all its partners, officers, manager(s) and stockholders. In the case of a corporation the license shall be issued jointly to the corporation and a stockholder, if an individual. Where the majority stockholder is not an individual, the license shall be issued jointly to the corporation and its agent registered under the provisions of this chapter. In the case of a partnership the license will be issued to all the partners. In the case of a limited liability company, the license will be issued jointly to the limited liability company and manager(s).
(5)
The license applicant and all employees thereof shall not have been convicted within ten years of the date of application of any felony, misdemeanor, excepting minor traffic violations, or any violations of the laws or ordinances of Georgetown-Quitman County, state or United States relating to the distribution, sale or dispensing of alcoholic beverages or any crime involving moral turpitude. A plea of nolo contendere for any felony or misdemeanor of any city, state or federal court, or a forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime is hereby considered a conviction under this chapter; provided, however, where the violation is for a misdemeanor or the forfeiture of bond involves a municipal ordinance, Georgetown-Quitman County in its discretion may waive such disqualification.
(6)
No person, firm limited liability company or corporation shall be granted any alcoholic beverage license unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the commission, or its designee, that such person, manager(s), partners in the firm, or officers and directors of the corporation have not been convicted or plead guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to, and have been released from parole or probation concerning any crime involving moral turpitude, illegal gambling or illegal possession or sale of controlled substances or the illegal possession or sale of alcoholic beverages, including the sale or transfer of alcoholic beverages to minors in a manner contrary to law, keeping a place of prostitution, pandering, pimping, public indecency, prostitution, solicitation of sodomy, or any sexual related crime within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application. At the time an application is submitted for any alcoholic beverage license, the applicant shall, by a duly sworn affidavit, certify that neither the applicant, nor any of the other owners of the establishment has been convicted or has pleaded guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving moral turpitude, illegal gambling or illegal possession or sale of controlled substances or the illegal possession or sale of alcoholic beverages, including the sale or transfer of alcoholic beverages to minors in a manner contrary to law, keeping a place of prostitution, pandering, pimping, public indecency, prostitution, solicitation of sodomy, or any sexual-related crime within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application. Should any applicant, partner, or officer instrumental in the sale or dispensing of any alcoholic beverage, after a license has been granted, be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to a crime involving moral turpitude, illegal gambling or illegal possession or sale of controlled substances or the illegal possession or sale of alcoholic beverages, including the sale or transfer of alcoholic beverages to minors in a manner contrary to law, keeping a place of prostitution, pandering, pimping, public indecency, prostitution, solicitation of sodomy, or any sexual-related crime, the license shall be immediately revoked and cancelled.
(7)
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be granted to any person convicted under any federal, state or local law of any felony, within ten years prior to the filing of application for such license.
(8)
It shall be unlawful for any county employee directly involved in the issuance of alcoholic beverage licenses under this chapter, to have any whole, partial or beneficial interest in any license to sell alcoholic beverages in the county.
(9)
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be granted to any person who has had any license issued under the police powers of the county previously revoked within two years prior to the filing of the application.
(10)
The commission may decline to issue a license when any person having any ownership interest in the operation of such place of business or control over such place of business does not meet the same character requirements as herein set forth for the licensee.
(11)
All licensed establishments must have and continuously maintain in the county a registered agent upon whom any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter to be served upon the licensee or owner may be served. This person must be a resident of Georgetown-Quitman County. The licensee shall file the name of such agent, along with the written consent of such agent with the commission and shall be in such form as may be prescribed by the commission.
(12)
All applicants for any alcoholic beverage license must be of good character, and all operators, managers, clerks, or other employees shall be of like character. Corporate or firm applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(13)
A license application may be denied to any applicant for any alcoholic beverage license where it appears that the applicant would not have adequate financial participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or where it appears that the application is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-70. - Statement of qualifications by applicant.
The applicant shall make a sworn statement of his qualifications according to the terms and conditions of this chapter, and shall file same with the manager of Georgetown-Quitman County prior to any approval of application or license issuance.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-71. - Misstatement, concealment of fact.
Any misstatement or concealment of fact by the applicant shall be grounds for revocation of license issued and shall make the applicant liable to prosecution for perjury under the laws or ordinances of Georgetown-Quitman County, state and/or United States Government.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-72. - Reserved.
Sec. 6-73. - Delegation of management.
If the applicant/licensee for an alcoholic beverage license shall assign, allow, direct or permit the management, operations or conduct of the alcoholic beverage business by a party or parties other than himself, the party and/or parties managing, directing, conducting or operating such business shall be deemed equally responsible and subject to all conditions of this chapter in every respect in the same manner and degree as the owner.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-74. - Duties of manager, operator or designee.
(a)
The manager, operator or designee shall be that person designated by the licensee as responsible for the management and/or conduct of business connected with the operation of a licensed alcoholic beverage establishment, Class I, Class II, Class III, Class IV or Class V. The manager, operator or designee shall devote and be on the premises of the licensee a minimum of 30 hours per work week and shall be subject to the terms and conditions of this chapter.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the alcoholic beverage licensee to notify the Georgetown-Quitman County Manager within 48 hours in writing of any change made by the licensee in the manager, operator or designee for the operation of the licensed premises.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-75. - Georgetown-Quitman County's right of rejection of manager, designee; investigation; notice.
Georgetown-Quitman County shall have the right to accept or reject such manager, operator and/or designee based upon the results of an investigation of the record of the individual. The licensee shall be notified, in writing, by the Georgetown-Quitman County Manager as to the results of such investigation within 72 hours of such determination.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-76. - Fingerprinting and investigation—Applicants.
All applicants for an alcoholic beverage license within Georgetown-Quitman County shall be fingerprinted in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2 which same shall be submitted to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation in accordance with said law, who in turn shall submit same to the FBI and a record shall be returned to Georgetown-Quitman County as provided for by said statute. Georgetown-Quitman County shall have the right to issue or reject such application based solely upon the results of the investigation. No license shall be issued without said report.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-77. - Additional information.
The applicant shall furnish such other information as the Georgetown-Quitman County Manager may, upon direction of the commission, from time to time require.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-78. - Agreement to pay taxes.
The applicant shall sign an agreement that, in consideration of the privilege of approval of the application/license, the applicant shall pay all taxes, fees, levies, etc., imposed upon the licensee by this chapter and will sell or hold for sale no alcoholic beverage upon which such taxes, fees, levies, etc., have not been paid.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-79. - Public hearing required.
All applicants for a new alcoholic beverage license within Georgetown-Quitman County shall be required to have a public hearing conducted by the commission prior to the issuance of such license.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Secs. 6-80—6-95. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - FEES

Sec. 6-96. - Original investigative fee.
All applicants for an initial alcoholic beverage license shall, at the time of application therefore, deposit with the Manager of Georgetown-Quitman County a nonrefundable investigation fee as set by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-97. - License fees.
All applicants for an initial alcohol beverage license shall deposit with the Manager of Georgetown-Quitman County a license fee for such license as set by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County. Generally, no such fees shall be prorated for any portion of a year, nor shall any such fees be refundable, unless said fees are refundable under section 6-119. Provided however, the commission may prorate fees for a portion of the year if the circumstances call for such proration as determined in the sole discretion of the commission.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-98. - Renewal.
(a)
The annual license fee for the renewal of an alcoholic beverage license once granted shall be as set by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County.
(b)
All licenses for the upcoming year must be purchased prior to December 31 of each year. Licenses expire at midnight December 31. Renewing applicants are required to be fingerprinted and pay an administration fee of $100.00 annually at renewal time.
(c)
Upon application for a renewal of an alcoholic beverage license for a restaurant (Class IV and Class V licenses), a sales statement must be submitted by the license holder and the management of the establishment showing the total sales of food and alcoholic beverages and the percentage of food sales. In addition, the license holder shall submit a statement from each wholesaler from whom the licensee has purchased any type of alcohol which said statement must show the total dollar amount of alcohol beverages sold to the license holder for the license year. Georgetown-Quitman County may cause an audit of the books of a licensee to be made at any time. Each license holder, by virtue of having applied for a license, shall furnish to Georgetown-Quitman County all books and records when requested by Georgetown-Quitman County. Failure of a licensee to furnish the books and records and to cooperate in the execution of the audit shall be a violation of this chapter. An applicant shall, with the application for a Class IV and Class V licenses license, submit a projected sales statement showing the percentage of food and alcohol sales anticipated.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14; Amend. of 10-13-16 )
Sec. 6-99. - Beer and wine background check administration fee.
(a)
All first time alcoholic beverage license applicants are required to be fingerprinted and pay $255.00 for the administration fee.
(b)
All alcoholic beverage applicants that are renewing are required to be fingerprinted and pay an administration fee of $100.00 at renewal time.
(Ord. No. 05-2016, 10-13-16; Amend. of 10-13-16 ; Ord. No. O1-2019 , 1-22-19)
Secs. 6-100—6-110. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - LOCATION AND BUILDING REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 6-111. - Minimum specifications for retail outlets.
All retail sales locations must meet the following minimum specifications:
(1)
Must meet the building codes adopted by Georgetown-Quitman County.
(2)
The interior of the building must be clearly visible from the exterior.
(3)
The interior must contain lighting of such strength that the interior of the building can be clearly seen from the exterior.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-112. - Drawing of premises to accompany application.
No application for an alcoholic beverage license shall be approved which does not include or have attached thereto a drawing of the premises and interior and the location at which applicant desires to operate.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-113. - Distance to churches, schools, libraries, etc.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be granted or issued to any applicant for such license nor shall alcoholic beverages be sold, offered for sale, given or distributed from the premises of the licensee within the following distance of:
(1)
Churches: within 100 yards of any church building. The term "church building" as used herein shall mean the main structure used by any religious organization for purposes of worship.
(2)
Schools:
a.
Distilled spirits: within 200 yards of any school building, school grounds or college campus.
b.
Wine and malt beverages: within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds or college campus.
c.
As used in this section, the term "school building", "school", "school grounds" or educational building" shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools, universities and colleges of this state and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b). The term "school building" and "educational building" includes only those structures in which instruction is offered. The term "school grounds" and "college campus" shall apply only to the parcel or parcels of land on which a school, school building or educational building is located. The term "college" shall mean an educational institution authorized by the state to award associate, baccalaureate or higher degrees.
(3)
Libraries and/or branch libraries: within 100 yards feet of any library or branch premises thereof.
(4)
Alcohol or drug treatment or counseling facility: within 100 yards.
(5)
Government buildings (except as provided in section 6-21 for the central business district), public parks, recreation centers, sports or athletic facilities: within 100 yards.
(6)
Any area where minors congregate: within 100 yards.
(7)
Any private dwelling: within 100 feet.
(8)
Class III license applications only: within 500 yards of any other business licensed to sell package liquor at retail.
(9)
No location which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages on the effective date of the ordinance from which this chapter derives shall be denied continued operation under an existing license, or denied any renewal of such license, nor shall any new owner of said location be denied a new license based upon the measurements set forth in this section.
(10)
As to any location licensed in the future, if the distance requirements herein are met at the time of issuance of any license, the subsequent opening and operation of a church, school, or college within the distance prohibited herein shall not prevent the continuance of an existing license or the renewal thereof or the issuance of a new license to any subsequent owner of such property.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-114. - Distance measurements.
(a)
Unless otherwise provided by law, those distance measurement prohibitions stated in section 6-113 shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
In a straight line from the front door of the structure from which beverage alcohol is sold or offered for sale;
(2)
To the front door of the building of a church, government owned treatment center or a Class III retail package store; or
(3)
To the nearest property line of the real property being used for school or educational purposes.
(b)
All applications shall include a drawing showing that the premises is not in violation of distances under this chapter and describing the way said distances were measured. At their sole discretion, the commission may require that the applicant furnish a survey conducted by a certified surveyor showing that the premises is in compliance with the distances under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
State Law reference— Sales of alcoholic beverages near churches, schools or college campuses, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 6-115. - Issuance during construction.
When the license applied for under this article is for a license to be located in a building not in existence when such license application is filed or construction of such building has been commenced, but is not yet completed, a license may, if otherwise authorized hereunder, be issued for such location if the application for such license and the plans for the proposed building clearly show that such building and the distance requirements will, when completed, meet all requirements of this chapter, Georgetown-Quitman County law and the state law. The annual license fee shall not be discounted or reduced for time consumed in building operations after the license is approved, but no sales shall be made at such location until the building has been completed in accordance with the plans and conforms with all provisions of the law and this chapter.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-116. - Distance exception.
The measurements indicated in section 6-113 as measured under section 6-114 shall not apply to any person who now holds a license if the measurement was made, at the time the license was originally issued, utilizing some other measuring method. Distance requirements shall also not apply to a Class II license where the business is a grocery store or supermarket. As used in this paragraph, the term "grocery store" or "supermarket" means a retail establishment which has at least 85 percent of its total retail floor space reserved for the sale of food and other nonalcoholic items, conducts all of its sales inside the building containing its retail floor space, where groceries and food are purchased for off the premises consumption and the total sales area of such business exceeds 15,000 square feet.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-117. - Requirements, Class IV and Class V licenses.
In addition to all other requirements, no Class IV and Class V licenses shall be issued to any applicant whose business does not meet the definition of a restaurant as herein defined.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-118. - Priority of applicants.
(a)
Applications must be filed and license fees and administration fees paid in person at the commission offices. Applications will be given priority by the date of filling, provided however, if the number of applicants is more than or equal to the number of licenses available (e.g., when two or more Class III license applications are for premises within 500 yards of another) applications filed within five business days of the initial application will proceed to approval under this chapter.
(b)
A substantially incomplete or inaccurate application may not be accepted and if accepted and subsequently determined to be substantially incomplete or an inaccurate will likely make the applicant ineligible for being given priority.
(c)
If there are more approved applicants than licenses available, a public drawing will be held; the time, date and place of any drawing will be posted at the commission offices and the approved applicants will be notified. The drawing will be conducted in a manner the commission deems appropriate.
(d)
The license fee, will be automatically refunded to each unsuccessful drawing participant. Refund checks will be mailed within 45 days of the priority drawing. The administration fee is nonrefundable.
(e)
Unsuccessful drawing participants will maintain the priority position assigned at the priority drawing. In the event a successful applicant is disqualified for a license by the State of Georgia or withdraws, the next drawing participant in order of priority will be contacted and will have 30 days to repay the previously refunded license fee, and certify that the information on the original application has not changed.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Secs. 6-119—6-130. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - PUBLIC HEARING

Sec. 6-131. - Required; when.
(a)
A public hearing shall be conducted by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County upon the application(s) for any initial alcoholic beverage license.
(b)
No application for an alcoholic beverage license, except application for renewal thereof, shall be approved or granted by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County until after a public hearing has been held thereon by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County after due publication of notice.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-132. - Publication of notice of application and hearing.
Prior notice of the public hearing shall be given for three consecutive weeks in the legal organ. Publication expense shall be paid by the applicant in advance and is nonrefundable. The published notice shall include the notice of application; time, date and place of public hearing before the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County as well as any other information required by Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-133. - Publication requirements.
The publication of a notice of public hearing contained in section 6-132 above shall be made as follows:
(1)
For the three consecutive weeks immediately prior to the hearing date.
(2)
No such notice of publication required herein shall be less than ten-point capital and lower case lettering with a minimum of one-inch, two-column size.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-134. - License grant or denial, appeal rights.
Each application on which the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County conducts a public hearing shall be granted or denied by it within a reasonable period of time from the date on which such public hearing is concluded.
(1)
In determining whether or not it is in the public interest and welfare for any license applied for hereunder to be granted or denied, in addition to all the other provisions of and qualifications required by this chapter, the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County shall be guided by the following:
a.
Reputation, character. The applicant's reputation, character, trade and business associations or past business ventures, mental and physical capacity to conduct this business.
b.
Previous violations of liquor laws. If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages whether or not he has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
c.
Manner of conducting prior liquor business. If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell any type of alcohol, the manner in which he conducted the business thereunder, especially as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
d.
Location. The location for which the license is sought, as to traffic congestion, available parking, general character of the neighborhood, pedestrian traffic, proximity to those establishments or entities listed in section 6-113 that are not within the prohibited distances set forth therein, and the feelings and attitudes of the citizens residing in the area as to the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values and on their quality of life.
e.
Number of licenses in the central business district. The number of licenses already granted for similar business in the central business district, as hereinbefore defined, and the need for an additional retail outlet of alcoholic beverages in the central business district.
f.
Dancing. Whether dancing will take place; whether live bands will be used; whether there will music on the inside or outside of the premises; if dancing, live bands or music is to be permitted upon the premises for which the license is sought and the applicant has previously permitted dancing upon any premises controlled or supervised by him, the manner in which he controlled and supervised such dancing to prevent any violation of any law, regulation or ordinance.
g.
Previous revocation of license. If the applicant is a person whose license issued under the police powers of a governing authority which has been previously suspended or revoked or who has previously had an alcoholic beverages suspended or revoked.
h.
Payment of taxes. If the applicant and business are not delinquent in the payment of any local taxes or have been chronically late in the payment of taxes or assessments.
i.
Congregation of minors. Any circumstances which may cause minors to congregate at or in the vicinity of the proposed location.
j.
Prior incidents. Evidence that a substantial number of incidents requiring police intervention have occurred (within two square city blocks of the proposed location, during the 24 months immediately preceding the date of application).
k.
Previous denial or revocation. The denial of an application, or the revocation of a license, occurring within the preceding 24 months, concerning the proposed location.
(2)
If the application is denied, Georgetown-Quitman County shall notify the applicant in writing setting forth the reason or reasons for the denial within a reasonable period of time. The applicant may appeal the denial of the application by filing a written request with the Manager of Georgetown-Quitman County asking the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County to reconsider the application along with a filing fee of $100.00. All publication costs must also be paid in advance and no costs are refundable. The applicant shall file said request within five days of the receipt of the denial of the application. Any resident of Georgetown-Quitman County may appeal the grant of an application by filing a request for reconsideration within five days of the grant of an application along with the payment of said filing fee.
(3)
If an appeal is timely filed, a notice of the filing of the request for reconsideration shall be sent to the applicant and to any other person who has requested reconsideration, and said notice shall be published in the legal organ of Quitman County for three weeks prior to the time of the hearing on reconsideration. The expense of the publication shall be paid, in advance of the hearing, by the appellant. The notice shall contain the date, time and location of the hearing. The Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County shall, at the appointed time, hear any evidence offered by the applicant and from anyone opposing the issuance of the license. The applicant and appellant may be represented by counsel, may offer testimony by witnesses or any other evidence and may question any opposing witnesses. Formal evidentiary rules shall not apply and the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County may hear and consider any evidence which would be helpful to them in making a decision. At the close of the evidence, the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County shall either deny or approve the issuance of a license.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-135. - License renewal, objections to renewal.
Applications for the renewal of alcoholic beverage licenses shall not require a public hearing thereon unless written objections thereto are filed in writing with the Manager of Georgetown-Quitman County a minimum of 15 working days prior to such renewal. A hearing of such objection shall be conducted by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County after publication of notice thereof as set forth in section 6-171.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-136. - Notice of objection to license or renewal.
Applicants for an alcoholic beverage license renewal shall be notified in writing within 72 hours of receipt of any objection received by Georgetown-Quitman County to such application. The notice shall be by registered or certified mail addressed to the applicant at the address shown on the application form or to the place of business listed under the current Georgetown-Quitman County license and shall include a copy of the objection.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Secs. 6-137—6-145. - Reserved.
DIVISION 6. - TERM, RENEWAL, TRANSFER

Sec. 6-146. - Term; fee nonrefundable.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued for less than one calendar year. In case of a license revocation or surrender or failure to utilize a license issued hereunder prior to the expiration date, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive a refund of the license fees.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-147. - Deadline for filing renewal application.
Applicants in any given license year shall file their application for license renewal under this article for the next succeeding year prior to December 31 of the current license year.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14; Amend. of 10-13-16 ; Ord. No. O1-2019 , 1-22-19)
Sec. 6-148. - Objections to application; extended expiration date.
A Georgetown-Quitman County alcoholic beverage license shall not be issued hereunder until the application therefor is approved or granted by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County; however, when timely objections to an application for renewal are filed, the life of the license is hereby extended to and through the date on which the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County grants or denies such application, but in no event shall such extension exceed 30 calendar days. If renewal is denied after a public hearing, the license holder or objector shall be entitled to an appeal as set for in subsection 6-134(2).
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-149. - License nontransferable, non-assignable.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be transferable or assignable to any person or entity. If a licensed business is sold or closed or the license is revoked for any reason, it shall be the duty of the licensee to surrender such license to Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-150. - Withdrawal or substitution of partners.
If there is a withdrawal, death of or addition of partners in a partnership, or a transfer or sale of any stock or equity rights in a corporation or other entity holding a license, the license shall be void 60 days after such event. The owners must submit a new application for a license.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Secs. 6-151—6-165. - Reserved.
DIVISION 7. - SUSPENSION, REVOCATION, CANCELLATION

Sec. 6-166. - Grounds for suspension, denial of renewal or revocation.
"Due cause" as defined in this chapter shall be grounds for suspension, denial of renewal or revocation of a license by the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-167. - Automatic revocation.
When any state permit or license to sell alcoholic beverages in Georgetown-Quitman County is revoked, the Georgetown-Quitman County license granted hereunder shall thereupon stand automatically revoked without any action by the board of commissioners or any municipal officer.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-168. - Forfeiture of approval; causes, exception.
After approval of an application for a license or renewal thereof, failure of the applicant to pay the annual fee and obtain the license within two weeks, or to exercise within 60 days from the date of approval of issuance of the license the privilege conferred to the licensee, said license shall be void and Georgetown-Quitman County shall cancel such unused license without refund of any fee paid.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-169. - Cancellation of license for failure to continue business.
Every licensee who shall bring to exercise the privilege conferred by a license under this article, but who shall thereafter, for a period of 90 consecutive days, fail to exercise such privilege, shall have the license, by virtue of such failure, cancelled without further notice or action from the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-170. - Cancellation in case of insolvency or failure to pay tax.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, every license and renewal thereof issued hereunder shall expire and be of no further force or effect in case of bankruptcy, receivership, levy of process or failure to promptly account for and pay the excise tax levied on sales of alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-171. - Public hearing on suspensions or revocation.
(a)
Upon findings by Georgetown-Quitman County of the probability of "due cause" as defined in section 6-172, the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County shall at any regular or special public hearing called for that purpose establish a date, time and place for the conduct of a public hearing at which Georgetown-Quitman County shall determine if the alcoholic beverage license should be suspended, not renewed or revoked.
(b)
A notice of such public hearing shall be delivered to the licensee or manager in charge of the place of business (or to another authorized agent of the licensee), either personally by the inspector or by the Sheriff's Department of Quitman County; or by registered or certified mail to the address shown on the alcoholic beverage license. The notice shall be given a minimum of ten days prior to the conduct of the public hearing. If notice is given by registered or certified mail, notice shall be deemed given upon mailing.
(c)
The notice referred to in subsection (b) shall contain the following minimum information:
(1)
Name and address of license holder.
(2)
Location for which the license is issued.
(3)
A copy of Georgetown-Quitman County's resolution charging the licensee, on information and belief, with "due cause".
(4)
The date, time and place at which a public hearing will be held.
(d)
Upon conclusion of the public hearing, the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County, by majority vote, shall determine whether there is "due cause" and whether there should be a revocation or suspension of the license or whether additional information is needed, in which case the hearing shall be adjourned to a date certain.
(e)
If there is a decision, as a result of a public hearing as hereinbefore provided, that the alcoholic beverage license of any licensee is suspended, non-renewed or revoked, such suspension or revocation shall become effective within 48 hours of such action by Georgetown-Quitman County.
(f)
An appeal may taken as provided for in subsection 6-134(2).
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-172. - Revocation, suspension, refusal to renew; grounds; notice; hearing; emergency situations.
(a)
Upon a finding of due cause, as hereinafter defined, Georgetown-Quitman County shall have the authority to refuse to renew any license (with or without an objection being filed) or to revoke or suspend any license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by Georgetown-Quitman County to any licensee hereunder.
(b)
"Due cause" shall include, but shall not be limited to the following:
(1)
The solicitation on the licensed premises by a licensee or by any employee of the licensee for herself, or himself or for any person, the purchase by a patron of any alcoholic drink, or money with which to purchase the same; the paying by a licensee, or employee of the licensee, of a commission or any other compensation to any person frequenting the establishment of the licensee or to the agent, employee or manager of the licensee, to solicit for herself, himself or for others the purchase by the patron of any alcoholic drink.
(2)
The selling to or serving of any person below the age of 21 years.
(3)
The conviction of any felony reasonably related to the ability of the licensee to operate and maintain the premises in a proper manner.
(4)
The violation of any existing state law or regulation governing the manufacture, sale, distribution or transportation of alcoholic beverages.
(5)
The violation of any provisions of the Uniform Deceptive Trade Practices Act (Ga. Laws 1968, p. 337 et seq., as amended) reasonably related to the operation of licensed establishments.
(6)
Permitting the solicitation of patrons on the licensed premises for prostitution or any other illegal act where the licensee, his employee or agent knew or should have known of such conduct.
(7)
Permitting the sale or solicitation of patrons on the licensed premises for the sale or use of drugs or any other controlled substance where the licensee, his employees or agents knew or should have known of such conduct.
(8)
The selling or serving of any alcoholic beverage to any person that the licensee, his employee or agent knew or should have known to be in a state of intoxication.
(9)
The failure to comply with any and all federal, state or municipal tax laws and regulations applicable to the operation of establishments licensed to sell alcoholic beverages.
(10)
The failure to furnish any and all data, information and records related to the operation of licensed establishments, when the same has been requested by the Georgetown-Quitman County.
(11)
The failure to maintain any and all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a license as set forth in this chapter.
(12)
The failure to obtain prior approval of Georgetown-Quitman County for changing the type of establishment operated pursuant to this chapter.
(13)
The death or serious bodily injury of any person on the licensed premises by any act of violence, including, but not limited to, injury by firearms or knives or any other instrument ordinarily used for bodily injury.
(14)
Failure by the licensee to adequately supervise and monitor the conduct of the employees, patrons and other on the licensed premises, or on any property owned or leased by the licensee, including but not limited to, parking lots used by patrons of a licensed establishment, in order to protect the safety and parking areas, or on any parking lots or areas which may be lawfully well-being of the general public and of those utilizing the premises.
(15)
Willful violation of this chapter.
(c)
In addition to those enumerations listed in subsection (b) above, "due cause" for the for revocation, suspension or refusal to renew an existing license shall also consist of the violation of any other law, ordinance or regulation governing the operation of establishments licensed to sell alcoholic beverages or which are reasonably related to the operation of such establishments.
(d)
No application for renewal shall be denied, and no license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by Georgetown-Quitman County or any licensee hereunder shall be revoked or suspended except upon a finding of due cause as defined in subsections (b) and (c) of this section, and after notice and a public hearing as set forth in section 6-171.
(e)
The Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County shall conduct the hearings herein. In lieu of suspension, revocation or the failure to renew hereunder, Georgetown-Quitman County may impose a fine upon any licensee, such fine not to exceed $1,000.00 for each violation occurring on the licensed premises and/or may place the licensee on probation on such terms and conditions as Georgetown-Quitman County decides.
(f)
After denial of renewal, revocation or suspension, Georgetown-Quitman County may refuse to accept or consider any application for a license to operate at the subject location for the sale of alcoholic beverages for a period of 12 months from the time of revocation, suspension or failure to renew such license; however, any new application for license hereunder at such location shall ask of the new applicant whether the subject location has been revoked, suspended or not renewed under this chapter.
(g)
For due cause, in the event of a violation of this chapter which results in an emergency situation in which continued operation of the premises by the licensee endangers the health, welfare or safety of the public, Georgetown-Quitman County may suspend any license hereunder. Any such suspension may be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until the next regular or called meeting of the Commission of Georgetown-Quitman County.
(h)
If any part of this section is for any reason declared invalid, such adjudication shall in no manner affect the other portions of the section, which shall remain in full force and effect.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Secs. 6-173—6-190. - Reserved.
DIVISION 8. - EXCISE TAXES

Sec. 6-191. - License expiration upon inability or failure to pay.
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter every license and renewal thereof issued hereunder shall expire and be of no further force or effect in case of bankruptcy, receivership, levy of process, or failure to promptly account for and pay the excise tax levied on sales of alcoholic beverages within Georgetown-Quitman County or state or United States.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-192. - Excise tax on malt beverages.
(a)
In addition to any license fees contained within this chapter or other laws and ordinances of Georgetown-Quitman County, the Commission pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80(l) does hereby levy an excise tax on the sale of malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, which is sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, at the maximum rate permitted by state law for local excise taxation on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons on a proportionate tax at the same rate of all fractional parts of 15½ gallons.
(b)
In addition to any license fees contained within this chapter or other laws and ordinances of Georgetown-Quitman County, the Commission pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80(2) does hereby levy an excise tax on the sale of malt beverages, sold in bottles, cans and other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, at the maximum rate permitted by state law for local excise taxation per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(c)
Stamps, decals, etc., prohibited. No decal, stamp or other marking may be required on malt beverages, designating Georgetown-Quitman County wherein a sale of malt beverages is made, or wherein resides a licensed retailer to whom such beverages are delivered.
(d)
Report of sales; remitting taxes:
(1)
Each licensee responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type of container, for the month sold within each municipality and/or county.
(2)
The wholesaler shall remit to Georgetown-Quitman County on the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made the tax imposed by Georgetown-Quitman County.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
State Law reference— Imposition of excise tax on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80.
Sec. 6-193. - Excise tax on wine.
In addition to any license fees contained within this chapter or other laws and ordinances of Georgetown-Quitman County, there is hereby levied and imposed upon all wholesale and retail dealers selling wine in Georgetown-Quitman County a specific excise tax in an amount equal to the maximum amount allowed under Georgia law as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60, as amended from time to time. Such tax shall be paid to Georgetown-Quitman County by the wholesale distributor on all wine sold to licensees for the sale of wine in Georgetown-Quitman County as follows:
(1)
Each wholesale distributor selling, shipping or in any way delivering wine to any such licensee shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery and shall remit the same together with a summary of all deliveries to each licensee on or before the tenth day of the month following.
(2)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any such licensee to possess, own, hold, store, display or sell any wine on which such tax has not been paid.
(3)
The wholesaler shall make the reports as required for malt beverages.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
State Law reference— Authorization to levy tax on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60.
Sec. 6-194. - Excise tax on wholesale distilled spirits.
(a)
In addition to any license fees contained within this chapter or other laws and ordinances of Georgetown-Quitman County, the commission by this chapter pursuant to O.C.G.A § 3-4-80, does hereby levy an excise tax on the sale of alcoholic beverages by licensed wholesalers at the maximum amount prescribed by state law per liter on a proportionate tax at like rates on any fractional part of a liter on distilled spirits, excluding fortified wine.
(b)
Every licensed wholesaler shall file a monthly report with the commission manager on a form prescribed by the commission manager, which form shall set forth the total quantity of malt beverage, wine and distilled spirits sold during such month and shall have attached to it legible copies of all invoices covering said sales. Such monthly report shall be filed not later than the tenth day of the next month following the month of sale. The report shall be accompanied by a local check or cashier's check in the full amount of the tax due.
(c)
There is hereby imposed a penalty equal to ten percent of the taxes due upon each licensed wholesaler who fails to file a timely report and pay the tax due. If the commission determines that the tax deficiency or any part thereof is due to fraud with intent to evade the tax, the council may assess a penalty equal to 50 percent of the tax assessed. The failure to pay the tax provided for in this section shall constitute sufficient cause for the revocation of any license issued under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-195. - Excise tax on distilled spirits by the drink.
(a)
In addition to any license fees contained within this chapter or other laws and ordinances of Georgetown-Quitman County, the commission pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-4-131, does hereby levy an excise tax on the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink at the maximum percentage rate prescribed by state law of the charge made by the licensed dealer to the public for the beverage sold. The tax levied by this section shall not apply to the sale of fermented beverages made in whole or in part from malt or any similar fermented beverage.
(b)
The excise tax provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and paid by the licensed retail dealer of distilled spirits by the drink. The tax shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of. Each licensee responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report with the commission finance department containing such information as may be required by the commission finance department in order to calculate the tax due.
(c)
The failure to file the report or pay the tax shall subject licensed consumption dealer to a penalty comparable to that set out in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-196. - Failure to report, failure to collect taxes at delivery; failure to remit.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any wholesale dealer or distributor, person, firm or corporation to deliver any alcoholic beverages to any retail dealer within Georgetown-Quitman County without collecting the taxes provided for in this division at the time of delivery.
(b)
If any wholesale dealer or distributor fails or refuses to make the reports or pay the excise tax provided for in this division, the commission shall notify the party in writing, and if the reports are not made and the taxes remitted within five days from the date of the notice, the council may withdraw from the wholesale dealer or distributor the privilege of doing business in the Georgetown-Quitman County by notifying the state revenue commissioner of the failure and by notifying the retail dealer that further acceptance of deliveries from the wholesaler, until the taxes are paid, may subject the retail dealer to penalty for violation of this section.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-197. - Unpaid taxes, sales are violation.
It shall be a violation of this chapter for any person, firm or corporation to sell at retail within the incorporated areas of the city any alcoholic beverages on which the taxes provided for in this division have not been paid.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-198. - Suspension, revocation or prohibition of retailer's license.
A violation of this division or other sections of this chapter shall authorize the commission to suspend, revoke or place on probation, with or without conditions, the license of any retail party or holder in accordance with the procedures and standards set forth herein.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-199. - Storage of alcoholic beverages.
No Class I, II, III, IV or V licensee shall keep any alcoholic beverages stored in any bonded or other type warehouse in Georgetown-Quitman County, nor shall he enter into any type of arrangement whereby alcoholic beverages ordered by him are stored for him by any licensed wholesaler. A licensee shall keep no inventory or stock of alcoholic beverages at any place except his licensed place of business, and within his licensed place of business; his storage space for alcoholic beverages shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which he is licensed to do business.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-200. - Severability.
If any section, provision, or clause of any part of this chapter shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, or if the provisions of any part of this chapter as applied to any particular situation or set of circumstances shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, such individuality shall not be construed to affect the portions of this chapter not so held to be invalid, or the application of this chapter to other circumstances not so held to be invalid. It is hereby declared as the intent that this chapter would have been adopted had such invalid portion not been included therein.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-201. - Repeal.
This chapter shall, upon its passage, repeal all other ordinances or parts of ordinances which are in conflict with this chapter or any part hereof. However, the repeal of such ordinances shall not revoke any license that has been issued under such ordinances and any such license shall continue, unless otherwise subject to revocation or suspension, until its expiration.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Sec. 6-202. - Effective date.
The ordinance codified in this chapter shall become effective upon its passage.
(Ord. No. 02-14, 10-14-14)
Secs. 6-203—6-225. - Reserved.
