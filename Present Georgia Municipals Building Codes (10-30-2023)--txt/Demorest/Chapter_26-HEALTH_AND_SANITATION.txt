Chapter 26 - HEALTH AND SANITATION

Sec. 26-1. - Sanitary provisions.
All persons keeping house in the city shall provide themselves and their families with suitable toilet facilities, and in all cases where possible said toilets shall be either connected with the city sewerage system or be served by individual septic tanks. It shall be unlawful to maintain or keep a surface or outdoor privy.
(Code 1955, § 68)
Sec. 26-2. - Depositing filth in streets.
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw or deposit on the streets, sidewalks, or in any other exposed place in the city any slops, foul water, dead fowls, or carcasses of any sort, melon rinds, vegetable matter, or filth of any description.
(Code 1955, § 70)
State Law reference— Litter control, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-60 et seq.
Sec. 26-3. - Dead animals.
Whenever any domestic animal shall die within the limits of the city, it shall be the duty of the owner thereof to cause such animal to be removed beyond the limits of the territory of said city, or to dispose of them in such a manner as to prevent such animal becoming offensive to the public or a menace to the public health.
(Code 1955, § 71)
Sec. 26-4. - Hogs—Generally.
No person shall keep or maintain any hogs except in a low performance zoning district in which agriculture is an outright use.
(Code 1955, §§ 72, 72A)
Sec. 26-5. - Same—Kept in unsanitary manner.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to keep, or maintain any pig or hog within the incorporate limits of the city in such a manner that its pen, lot stable, or bed shall become filthy, a breeding place for nauseous insects, or a public nuisance.
(Code 1955, § 72B)
