DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-363. - Fiscal year.
The fiscal year of the county shall begin on July 1 and end on June 30 of the succeeding year.
State Law reference— Fiscal year required to be established by ordinance, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3.
Sec. 2-364. - Indigent funeral assistance policy.
The county shall assist with the expenses of indigent funerals within the county to the extent set forth in this article and only if the following requirements are met:
(1)
The deceased must have been a resident of the county at the time of death and have no property or personal possessions in his name at the time of death valued over $1,000.00.
(2)
The spouse of the deceased, if any, must have no property or personal possessions in his name valued over $1,000.00.
(3)
Before payment by the county can be made, written certification must be made by the surviving spouse or relative verifying that the deceased, and the deceased's surviving spouse, if any, meet the requirements of this section. If there is no surviving spouse or relative, the funeral home director in charge of burial may certify to compliance with this section.
(4)
The county will pay an indigent funeral expenses up to $750.00 or the amount provided by governing statute, whichever is less, upon certification as required herein and receipt of proper invoice from the funeral home director.
State Law reference— County appropriations for burial of paupers, O.C.G.A. § 36-12-5.
Sec. 2-365. - Budget.
The procedures for budget preparation, submission to the board of commissioners, review by the board of commissioners, public review, notice, and hearings shall be provided in O.C.G.A. 36-81-1 et seq.
State Law reference— Annual budget generally, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3 et seq.; adoption of budget, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-6; duty to adopt and to operate under annual balance budget, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3.
Sec. 2-366. - Annual audit.
The board of commissioners shall have a complete and independent audit in compliance with generally accepted audit standards performed annually by a qualified external auditor in accordance O.C.G.A. § 36-81-7. The audit will be accepted by the board at a regular or special meeting and made available for inspection by any interested person during regular business hours.

DIVISION 2. - INDIGENT DEFENSE SERVICES

Sec. 2-391. - Application; form.
Any person lacking financial ability to provide for himself legal representation in his defense in any criminal proceeding in any court in the county may submit an application to the county requesting indigent defense representation. The application shall be in the form prescribed by the board of commissioners, shall set forth specific facts supporting the request and need for indigent representation, and shall be filed with the clerk of the court in which representation is sought.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, § 1)
Sec. 2-392. - Application fee; waiver.
(a)
Each applicant for indigent defense services shall pay an application fee in the amount provided in the county fee schedule to the clerk of the court in which indigent representation is being sought. Such fee must be paid either at the time the application is submitted or at the time the representation is provided.
(b)
The application fee provided for in this section may be waived only upon order of the presiding judge scheduled to hear the case in which the applicant seeks waiver of the application fee. The presiding judge may order a waiver of the fee only upon a finding that the defendant is unable to pay the fee or that paying the fee would cause undue hardship to the defendant.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, §§ 2, 3)
Sec. 2-393. - Collection and disposition of funds.
Within ten days after the end of each month, the clerks of the courts in receipt of application fees required by this division shall remit the total of all application fees collected during the previous month to the county. All such funds shall be maintained by the county in a separate fund for indigent defense services and shall be expended only for the purpose of providing legal representation to indigent defendants pursuant to this division.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, §§ 4, 5)
