DIVISION 3. - DRUG TESTING FOR CMV OPERATORS[4]
modified

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Random drug testing of state employees in high-risk jobs, O.C.G.A. § 45-20-90 et seq.; Drug-Free Public Work Force Act of 1990, O.C.G.A. § 45-23-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-95. - Findings.
(a)
The board of commissioners places a high priority on ensuring the safe operation of commercial motor vehicles (CMV) by county employees. The board of commissioners recognizes that unimpaired judgment on the part of its drivers is essential to providing safe operation of commercial motor vehicles (CMV), and that alcohol misuse and controlled substance use may impair the judgment of drivers.
(b)
In order to prevent accidents and injuries resulting from alcohol misuse and controlled substance use, the board of commissioners hereby implements all federal and state laws and regulations regarding individuals who drive or may drive a commercial motor vehicle for the county at any time, including, but not limited to, full-time employees, part-time employees and others.
(Code 2000, § 2-101; Ord. of 12-19-1995)
Sec. 2-96. - Required.
All county employees who drive or may drive a commercial motor vehicle (CMV) for the county must participate in the controlled substance and alcohol testing program as more fully described in applicable federal and state law and regulations, specifically: Federal Highway Administration, DOT - Part 382 - Controlled Substances And Alcohol Use And Testing, and Part 40 - Procedures For Transportation Workplace Drug Testing Programs.
(Code 2000, § 2-102; Ord. of 12-19-1995)
Sec. 2-97. - Termination.
Individuals who have positive controlled substance test results shall be terminated. Individuals whose test results reveal blood alcohol concentrations 0.04 and above and individuals who refuse to submit to a required alcohol or controlled substance test shall be terminated. Individuals whose test results reveal blood alcohol concentrations of at least 0.02, but less than 0.04, and individuals who engage in other conduct prohibited by the regulations may, in the board of commissioners' discretion, be terminated.
(Code 2000, § 2-103; Ord. of 12-19-1995)
Sec. 2-98. - Prescription, over-the-counter medication.
Where applicable, drivers must inform their immediate supervisor of any therapeutic drug use, whether by prescription or over-the-counter, and must provide a statement from their treating physician that the substance does not adversely affect the driver's ability to operate a commercial motor vehicle.
(Code 2000, § 2-104; Ord. of 12-19-1995)

State Law reference— County manager authorized, O.C.G.A. § 36-5-22.

Sec. 2-125. - Appointment and qualifications.
The county manager may be appointed as the full-time administrative officer of the board of commissioners. No person holding an elective office in the county shall be eligible for appointment until one year after leaving elective office. Such appointment shall be by a majority vote of the entire board. The county manager shall be prohibited from engaging in any political activity, and the county manager shall not be eligible to qualify as a candidate for an elective office in the county for one year after leaving office. The county manager shall be appointed for a term to be determined by the board of commissioners; may succeed himself; and at any time may be removed from office for cause after written notice and hearing by a majority vote of the entire board. The county manager need not be a resident of the county at the time of his appointment but shall establish residence therein within a reasonable time as may be determined by the board of commissioners following such appointment and continue to reside therein throughout such appointment.
(Ord. No. 0060403-01, § 1(2-1), 10-5-2009)
Sec. 2-126. - Powers and duties.
(a)
The county manager shall be the administrative head of the county government. He shall be responsible to the board of commissioners for the proper and efficient administration of all affairs of the county except as otherwise provided by law. The county manager shall:
(1)
Assist with the preparation of the agenda of subjects to be acted on for all meetings of the county board of commissioners.
(2)
Attend all meetings of the board with the right to take part in the discussion, provided that he shall have no vote before the board. The county manager shall be entitled to notice of all special meetings.
(3)
Act as liaison between the board and the department heads and employees of the county.
(4)
Submit to the board of commissioners the names of final candidates prior to making the appointment of future heads of all county departments; and remove department heads and employees, with the exception of the county attorney and county auditor, pursuant to the personnel policies as may be adopted from time to time by the board of commissioners.
(5)
Prepare and submit to the board the proposed annual county budget.
(6)
Keep the board at all times fully advised as to the financial condition and needs of the county.
(7)
Ensure that all legislation, programs, regulations or plans promulgated and required by the board, the state, and federal governments are duly carried out and complied with in accordance with such legislation, programs, regulations and plans.
(8)
Conduct studies and investigations and make reports thereon to the board concerning the operations of the departments, offices, and agencies of the county.
(9)
Require any department, board, commission, or agency under the county manager's jurisdiction to submit written reports and to provide other information as deemed necessary.
(10)
Prescribe, require, publish, and implement standards of administrative, management, and operating practices and procedures to be followed and adhered to by all offices, departments, boards, commissions, authorities, and other agencies of the county which are subject to the county manager's supervision and jurisdiction.
(11)
Direct and supervise the performance of all contracts made by any person for work done for the county and to supervise and regulate all purchases of materials and supplies for the county within such limitations and under such rules and regulations as may be imposed by the board.
(12)
Perform such other duties as may be required by law, ordinance or resolution of the board of commissioners.
(b)
Except for the purpose of inquiry and investigation, the board of commissioners shall deal with county employees who are subject to appointment and removal by the county manager solely through the county manager and shall not give orders or directions to any such employee, either publicly or privately, directly or indirectly.
(Ord. No. 0060403-01, § 1(2-2), 10-5-2009)

DIVISION 2. - 9-1-1 JOINT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-169. - Created.
The Jasper County, Monticello and Shady Dale Joint 9-1-1 Authority is created, and is referred to in this division as the 9-1-1 joint authority.
(Code 2000, § 2-151; Res. of 11-7-1996)
Sec. 2-170. - Membership.
(a)
The 9-1-1 joint authority shall be a joint body representative of Jasper County, the City of Monticello and the City of Shady Dale. It shall consist of 11 members, each of whom shall be residents of the county and who shall serve without pay.
(b)
The county shall be entitled to appoint five members, the City of Monticello shall appoint three members and the City of Shady Dale shall appoint one member. In addition to these appointments, the sheriff of the county shall serve as a member and the director of emergency medical service shall serve as a member.
(Code 2000, § 2-152; Res. of 11-7-1996, §§ 1, 3)
Sec. 2-171. - Terms of office; vacancies.
The terms of office shall be four years or until their successors are appointed. Vacancies occurring other than by expiration of term shall be filled by another appointment for the unexpired term.
(Code 2000, § 2-153; Res. of 11-7-1996, § 2)
Sec. 2-172. - Contracts.
The 9-1-1 joint authority shall be authorized to contract with the counties or municipalities which formed the authority to operate an emergency 9-1-1 system for such local governments throughout the corporate boundaries of such local governments. Pursuant to such contracts, the local governments shall be authorized to provide funding to the authority from the emergency telephone system fund maintained by each local government. No authority shall be formed until each local government forming the authority has imposed a monthly 9-1-1 charge.
(Code 2000, § 2-154; Res. of 11-7-1996, § 5)
Sec. 2-173. - Powers.
The 9-1-1 joint authority shall have all of the powers necessary or convenient to carry out and effectuate the purposes and provisions of this division, including, but without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the power to:
(1)
Bring and defend actions;
(2)
Adopt and amend a corporate seal;
(3)
Make and execute contracts and other instruments necessary to exercise the power of the authority;
(4)
Receive and administer gifts, grants, and devises of any property;
(5)
Operate emergency call answering services for law enforcement, emergency management, fire, and emergency medical service agencies 24 hours a day, seven days a week, 365 days a year;
(6)
Acquire, by purchase, gift, or construction, any real or personal property desired to be acquired to operate the emergency 9-1-1 system;
(7)
Sell, lease, exchange, transfer, assign, pledge, mortgage, dispose of, or grant options for any real or personal property or interest therein for any such purposes; and
(8)
Mortgage, convey, pledge, or assign any properties, revenues, income, tolls, charges, or fees owned or received by the authority.
(Code 2000, § 2-155; Res. of 11-7-1996, § 6)
Sec. 2-174. - Election of officers; hiring and compensation of employees; annual budget.
The 9-1-1 joint authority shall elect a chairperson and such other officers as deemed necessary by the authority. The authority shall select a director who shall be responsible for establishing operating standards and procedures and overseeing the operations of the emergency 9-1-1 system. The director may be an employee working in the operation of the emergency 9-1-1 system. The authority shall be responsible for hiring, training, supervising, and disciplining employees working in the operation of the emergency 9-1-1 system. An appropriate number of full-time and part-time employees shall be hired to operate the emergency 9-1-1 system. The authority shall determine the compensation of such employees and shall be authorized to provide other employee benefits. The authority shall submit its annual budget and a report of its financial records to the local governments which created the authority.
(Code 2000, § 2-156; Res. of 11-7-1996, § 7)
Sec. 2-175. - Authorized to contract with service suppliers.
The 9-1-1 joint authority may contract with a service supplier in the same manner that local governments are so authorized under the provisions of this division.
(Code 2000, § 2-157; Res. of 11-7-1996, § 8)
Secs. 2-176—2-203. - Reserved.

Sec. 2-204. - Activation of the authority.
It is declared that there is a need for a joint development authority to function in Jasper County, Morgan County, Newton County and Walton County. Such authority, to be known as the Joint Development Authority of Jasper County, Morgan County, Newton County and Walton County, is hereby created and activated. The authority shall transfer business pursuant to, and exercise the powers provided by the provisions of, the Development Authority Law, codified in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Code 2000, § 2-181; Ord. of 3-1-1999, § 1)
Sec. 2-205. - Joint authority.
The authority is created and activated in section 2-204 as a joint authority pursuant to the provisions of 1981 Ga. Laws (Act No. 760), p. 1419, as amended, and O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.1.
(Code 2000, § 2-182; Ord. of 3-1-1999, § 2)
Sec. 2-206. - Board of directors.
(a)
The authority shall be controlled by a board of directors (the board) consisting of eight members. Two members of the board shall be appointed by the board of commissioners for each county. Each member appointed to the board shall be a taxpayer residing within the county making the appointment to the board. One member of the board from each county shall be an ex officio member of the board of commissioners of that county. The other member of the board from each county may be any taxpayer residing within that county who is not a member of the board of commissioners.
(b)
The initial eight members of the authority are to be appointed by the board of commissioners of each participating county at the first open meeting of such body following its adoption of the ordinance from which this division is derived, for terms beginning on the date of the first meeting of the authority following the adoption of the ordinance from which this division is derived and extending for terms of two years and four years. After the initial term, the terms of all directors shall be for four years. If at the end of any term of any member, a successor to such member has not been appointed, the member whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is appointed.
(c)
The member shall receive no compensation for his services, but shall be reimbursed for his actual expenses incurred in the performance of his duties. A majority of the members of the authority shall constitute a quorum, but no action may be taken by the authority without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the authority. The members of the authority shall meet at least quarterly and shall develop an operational business plan for the authority.
(Code 2000, § 2-183; Ord. of 3-1-1999, § 3)
Sec. 2-207. - Officers.
The directors of the board shall elect one of their members as chairperson and another as vice-chairperson and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may, but need not, be a director.
(Code 2000, § 2-184; Ord. of 3-1-1999, § 4)
Sec. 2-208. - Audited financial statements.
The authority shall provide to the fiscal officer of a participating county an audited financial statement if such audit has been required by the participating county within six months of the end of the previous fiscal year.
(Code 2000, § 2-185; Ord. of 3-1-1999, § 5)

Sec. 2-240. - Findings.
(a)
The board of commissioners is the governing authority of the county, a political subdivision created and existing under the laws of the state, and is charged with the duties of levying taxes, contracting debts, and managing the affairs of the county.
(b)
It has been determined by the board of commissioners that there exists an urgent need to develop and promote for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry, and employment opportunities in the county.
(c)
Ga. Const. art. IX, § VI, ¶ III, which permits the general assembly of the state to create development authorities for certain purposes, and an act of the general assembly of the state entitled the "Development Authorities Law," as amended, and codified as O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq. (the Development Authorities Law), which was enacted by the general assembly of the state pursuant to authority granted in said article IX, section VI, paragraph III, authorize each county and each municipal corporation in the state to activate a development authority within each such county and municipal corporation, such development authorities having been created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law.
(d)
The board of commissioners has determined that it is desirable and necessary that such development authority of the county be activated immediately in order to fulfill the present needs expressed in this section.
(e)
There is determined and declared to be a pressing, existing, and future need for a development authority, as more fully described and defined in the Development Authorities Law, to function in the county for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry, and employment opportunities in the county thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry.
(f)
There is activated in the county the public body corporate and politic known as the Development Authority of Jasper County which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law.
(g)
Each of the persons named as directors of the development authority shall serve in such capacity for terms of two years, four years, and six years unless they cease to be eligible prior to the expiration of such term and if at the end of any term or office of director, or upon becoming ineligible, a successor thereto shall be elected by the board of commissioners. A director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so elected.
(h)
The board of directors shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Code 2000, § 2-201; Ord. of 5-4-1998)
