Sec. 2-5-1. - Sessions of court; when held, generally; opening announcement.
A regular session of the recorder's court shall be held at 9:00 a.m. on the third Monday of each month and may continue from hour to hour or day to day as the business of the court may necessitate, provided any session may be dispensed with when there is no business pending in the court or no cases are ready for trial.
Sec. 2-5-2. - Special sessions of court.
The recorder may hold special sessions when, in his judgment, it is necessary or there is urgent cause for the trial of accused persons, before the next regular sitting of the court. The necessity or urgency of the cause shall be presumed from the fact of a session being held. All defendants shall have at least six hours notice of the session.
Sec. 2-5-3. - Police attendance.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police or his deputy, or some member of the police force designated by the recorder, to attend each session of the court to act as bailiff, and the duty of each policeman making the arrest or having knowledge of the case for or against the defendant, to attend the trial of any person brought before the recorder.
Sec. 2-5-4. - Clerk of court; subpoenas; dockets.
It shall be the duty of the city clerk, or an appropriate designee, to attend each session of the recorder's court and serve as clerk of court. The clerk of court shall:
(1)
Prepare all subpoenas and warrants and maintain a docket thereof that will show the name of the person for whom the subpoena or warrant was issued, its date, at whose instance it was issued, and to whom delivered.
(2)
The recorder may require that the clerk maintain a duplicate court docket and use the signed duplicate copy of the docket to make necessary entries in the master docket after each session of court, the master or original to be retained by the recorder and the duplicate to be examined for completeness and signed by the recorder.
Sec. 2-5-5. - Jury trial; bind over.
(a)
All charges cognizable as violations of state laws shall be handled by a commitment hearing, provided however, that they may be adjudicated in the recorder's court whenever the court would have jurisdiction over the offense, and the defendant does not demand a jury trial.
(b)
When a defendant is bound over to the appropriate state court, he or she may be released under any of the following methods:
(1)
If the defendant has made bond to the city he or she may be released on the same bond;
(2)
If the defendant has not made bond before being bound over, he or she shall make bond at the county jail book-in and an arraignment notice will then be given to the defendant and the defendant must be delivered, in person, by the city police, to the county authorities.
(c)
There shall be no jury trials in the recorder's court, except where one is required by law or where one is rightfully demanded, said case shall be bound over to the appropriate court by the recorder.
Sec. 2-5-6. - Appearance bond; procedure for accepting.
Offenses against the ordinances of the city shall be bailable as follows:
(1)
The arresting officer, chief of police, recorder or any other officer designated by the chief of police, may accept bond of the accused payable to the city conditioned upon the appearance of the accused at the next session or any subsequent session of the recorder's court as stated in the bond, to answer the charge.
(2)
The bond shall be in writing in an amount established in a schedule approved by the recorder.
(3)
A person arrested for violation of a city ordinance may be released on his own recognizance, may be released on bond without security or in lieu thereof may deposit cash or his driver's license with the arresting officer.
(4)
Any person released under subsection (3), above, shall receive a receipt for any driver's license deposited or, if released on the person's owns recognizance, shall sign and receive a copy of the citation or ticket describing the violation for which the person was arrested.
(5)
All persons arrested or notified by citation or ticket of parking violations shall be released on their own recognizance.
Sec. 2-5-7. - Continuance of cases.
The recorder may refuse to continue a case, except for good cause shown.
Sec. 2-5-8. - Forfeiture of appearance bonds.
(a)
The recorder or his designee shall call the case in its regular order, and, if the accused is not in court and does not answer the call, and, if no good and sufficient reason is given for nonappearance, the recorder shall enter on the docket where the case is stated "Bond forfeited," or similar words.
(b)
Upon forfeiture of any bond, the cash deposited as security shall be paid to the city clerk, and it shall become the property of the city.
(c)
Upon the forfeiture of any bond signed by a person as surety, the city clerk shall issue an execution against the principal and surety on the bond for the amount thereof which shall be collected as tax executions are collected.
State Law reference— Offenses against municipalities, forfeiture of bond as no bar to prosecution, Ga. Code Ann., § 27-910.
Sec. 2-5-9. - Pleas.
(a)
Upon a case being called, the recorder shall inform the defendant of the charge against him and shall inquire as to how he desires to plead thereon. Thereupon, the defendant shall enter one of the following pleas:
(1)
Guilty;
(2)
Not guilty;
(3)
Nolo contendere (with the permission of the court); or
(4)
A former judgment of conviction or acquittal of the offense charged.
(b)
If the defendant refuses to enter a plea, the court shall enter a plea of not guilty and set the case down for trial.
Sec. 2-5-10. - Fines; enforcement by levy and execution; payment in advance of court date.
(a)
For fines that may be due by any defendant, execution may be issued by the city clerk and collected as tax executions are collected.
(b)
Persons charged with traffic violations may, prior to the time for their court appearance, plead guilty in writing and pay to the city as their fine the amount set as the appearance bond for the offense charged; except that those persons charged with driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs and those charged with an offense in connection with an automobile collision shall be required in all cases to appear before the court for disposition of the case.
Sec. 2-5-11. - Appearance of complaining witnesses.
A material or complaining witness in any contested prosecution shall appear at the session at which the prosecution is docketed. The prosecution may be dismissed if the material witness fails to appear.
Sec. 2-5-12. - Rules of practice.
Practice in the recorder's court shall be governed by the rules of the superior courts of this state as far as practicable, under the ordinances and Charter of the city, and, where not provided otherwise by the Charter and ordinances of the city, the court may prescribe additional rules of practice where there are no rules applicable.
Sec. 2-5-13. - Judgment and sentences.
Upon a judgment or plea of guilty the court shall impose sentence. The court may:
(1)
Impose a fine, with or without a commitment for confinement, compulsory work or both;
(2)
Commit the defendant to confinement, compulsory work or both;
(3)
Suspend the execution of the sentence in whole or in part;
(4)
Defer the execution of the sentence or any portion or portions thereof to one or more fixed dates in the future; or
(5)
Impose such other form of judgment or sentence as the court deems appropriate, including but not limited to emotional maturity training, driver education, and to probate or suspend any of the foregoing sentences upon such conditions as the court shall deem appropriate.
Cross reference— General penalty, § 1-1-8.
Sec. 2-5-14. - Contempt of court.
Failure to appear pursuant to any summons or subpoena, disrespectful conduct or failure to comply with any other order or judgment of the court shall constitute contempt of court and be punishable as provided by the City Charter.
Sec. 2-5-15. - Court dates; contempt warrants; appeals.
(a)
Court date will be made known to the defendant at the time of the arrest process and shall be properly indicated on the copy of charges given to the defendant by the arresting officer.
(b)
Warrants for contempt of court charges may be issued following each session of court.
(c)
Appeals may be taken from the recorder's court in accordance with applicable state law.
