ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 28-19. - Composition of department.
The police department shall consist of those officers and employees of those ranks and positions as approved by the city council.
Sec. 28-20. - Responsibilities.
The department shall be responsible for patrol, traffic control, investigation of accidents, investigation of crimes, apprehension of offenders, court appearances, security of business establishments and for any other matters of public safety and law enforcement.
Sec. 28-21. - Oath.
Prior to entering upon the duties of their respective offices, all officers of the police department shall take and subscribe the oath which shall be administered by the city police chief.
Sec. 28-22. - Police chief.
(a)
The police chief shall be nominated by the city manager for confirmation by the city council and shall be the chief executive officer of the police department, subject always to the directives of the city manager.
(b)
In the exercise of his duties, the police chief shall have the power to establish and enforce rules and regulations for the government of the members and employees of the department, which rules shall, however, be consistent with this Code, all other resolutions and ordinances of the city, and the laws of the state. All police department rules, policies and procedures must be approved by the city manager and city attorney.
Sec. 28-23. - Police operations manual.
The chief of police shall be responsible for compilation and maintenance of a city police department operations manual, a copy of which shall be available in the office of city clerk and the city police chief.
