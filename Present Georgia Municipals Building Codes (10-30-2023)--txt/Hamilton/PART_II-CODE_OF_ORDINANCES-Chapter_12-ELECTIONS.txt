ARTICLE II. - CITY COUNCIL

Sec. 12-19. - Municipal superintendent; duties.
The city clerk is hereby appointed as the municipal superintendent. The municipal superintendent shall exercise all the powers granted under state law, including, but not limited to, the appointment and compensation of the absentee ballot clerk, poll officers, and other employees required to conduct an election; printing ballots and other election supplies required, furnishing all of the supplies, blank forms, including badges, tally sheets, and return sheets; and exercising supervisory control of registrars, poll officers, and other employees, in the performance of their duties; and performing all other duties prescribed by law in relation to the election.
Sec. 12-20. - Polling place.
The city hall or county commission office are designated as the polling places, as deemed appropriate.
Sec. 12-21. - Chief registrar.
The city clerk is hereby designated as the chief registrar with the authority to appoint such deputy registrars as are required.
Sec. 12-22. - Voter criteria.
No person shall vote unless he shall be:
(1)
Registered as an elector in the manner prescribed by law;
(2)
A citizen of this state and of the United States;
(3)
At least 18 years of age;
(4)
Domiciled in this city; and
(5)
Possessed of all other qualifications prescribed by state law.
Sec. 12-23. - Polls.
Polls are to be open from 7:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m. on election day.
