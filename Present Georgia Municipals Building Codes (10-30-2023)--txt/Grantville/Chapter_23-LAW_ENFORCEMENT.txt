Chapter 23 - LAW ENFORCEMENT
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 23-1. - Chief of police; authority to adopt rules and regulations.
The chief of police of the city is directed to promulgate formal rules and regulations regarding the conduct of police officers in the execution of their duties, and that such rules and regulations shall take effect on the date they are filed by the chief of police with the city clerk who shall file and preserve the original copies in his office.
(Prior Code, § 2.12.020; Ord. No. 599, 1991)
Secs. 23-2—23-21. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ARREST WARRANTS

Sec. 23-22. - Allegation; form.
The information to obtain the warrant shall describe the offense substantially in terms of the ordinance and only with such particularity as is required to put the accused on notice of the nature of the charge. The allegation may be substantially in the following forms:
STATE OF GEORGIA
TOWN OF GRANTVILLE
Personally before me comes the undersigned, who on oath says that to the best of his knowledge and belief, ___________ in said Town on the _____ day of _______, _____, did commit the offense of ___________, contrary to the Ordinances of said Town.
Sworn to and subscribed before me this _____ day of _______, _____.
___________
City Clerk
(Prior Code, § 1.12.020; Ord. No. 544, § 2, 1-14-1980)
Sec. 23-23. - Warrant; form.
The warrant shall be attached to the information, and shall be in the form substantially as follows:
STATE OF GEORGIA
TOWN OF GRANTVILLE
To the Marshal and Police Officers of said Town:
The foregoing affidavit having been made before me, you are therefore commanded to arrest said accused person and to bring same before the Recorder's Court to be dealt with as the law directs. Herein fail not.
Witness my hand and official signature this _____ day of _______, _____
___________
City Clerk
(Prior Code, § 1.12.030; Ord. No. 544, § 3, 1-14-1980)
Sec. 23-24. - Duty to execute.
The marshal and police officers are bound to execute the penal warrant placed in their hands.
(Prior Code, § 1.12.040; Ord. No. 544, § 4, 1-14-1980)
Sec. 23-25. - Arrest without warrant.
Generally, an arrest must be made by authority of a warrant; however, an arrest may be made without warrant:
(1)
If the offense is committed in the presence of the officer;
(2)
If the offender is endeavoring to escape; or
(3)
For other cause there is likely to be failure of justice for want of an officer to issue a warrant.
(Prior Code, § 1.12.050; Ord. No. 544, § 5, 1-14-1980)
