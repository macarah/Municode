Sec. 32-1. - Definitions.
When used in this chapter, the following words and phrases have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, unless the context indicates a different meaning:
Bulk waste means items which are too large for approved containers and typically consists of, but is not limited to, household items such as lawn equipment, furniture, mattresses and box springs, large pieces of glass and mirrors, flooring materials and other similar items.
Hazardous waste means solid or liquid waste material resulting from the manufacture or use of pesticides and drugs (other than normal household use); pathological wastes; highly flammable or explosive wastes; caustic wastes; toxic wastes; sewage sludges; and other similar waste material.
Landfill means a disposal site where solid wastes, other than putrescible wastes or hazardous wastes, are disposed of using landfilling techniques.
Landfilling means a method of disposing of solid wastes, except putrescible wastes or hazardous wastes, on land, by placing an earth cover thereon.
Open dump means a disposal site at which solid waste from one or more sources is consolidated and left to decompose, burn or to otherwise create environmental pollution or a nuisance.
Permit means written authorization granted to a person by the city to dispose of rubbish, as defined in this section.
Putrescible wastes means wastes that are capable of being decomposed by microorganisms to cause nuisances. The term "putrescible wastes" includes kitchen wastes, offal, hatchery and poultry processing plant wastes, and dead animals.
Rubbish means waste paper, cartons, boxes, wood, tree branches, yard trimmings, furniture, appliances, metals, cans, glass, crockery and/or similar materials.
Scavenge means the uncontrolled picking from discarded solid waste materials.
Solid waste (waste) means putrescible and nonputrescible wastes, except human body waste, and shall include garbage, rubbish (paper, cartons, boxes, wood, tree branches, yard trimmings, furniture and appliances, metals, tin cans, glass, crockery, or dunnage), ashes, street refuse, dead animals, industrial wastes (waste materials generated in industrial operations), residue from incineration, food processing wastes, demolition wastes, construction wastes, and any other waste materials in a solid or semisolid state, not otherwise defined in this chapter.
Yard waste means tree branches, stumps, twigs, bushes, weeds, and general yard and garden waste materials and includes stone and dirt rakings, grass clippings, leaves, tree prunings and yard trimmings.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.020; Ord. No. 519, § 2, 9-9-1975; Ord. No. 06-2014, § 1, 6-23-2014 )
Sec. 32-2. - Prohibited acts.
No person shall engage in solid waste handling in a manner which will:
(1)
Likely create a nuisance;
(2)
Be conducive to insect and rodent infestation or the harboring and feeding of wild dogs or other animals;
(3)
Impair the air quality;
(4)
Impair the quality of the groundwater or surface water;
(5)
Impair the quality of the environment; or
(6)
Likely create other hazards to the public health, safety or well-being as may be determined by the director.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.030; Ord. No. 519, § 3, 9-9-1975)
Sec. 32-3. - Open dump.
No solid waste shall be disposed of by any person in an open dump, nor shall any person cause, suffer, allow or permit open dumping on his property.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.040; Ord. No. 519, § 4, 9-9-1975)
Sec. 32-4. - Solid waste containers.
(a)
All containers used by any firm, corporation, person or party for the storage and/or collection of solid waste must have lids which will keep the contents free of rodents, flies or other insects. All solid waste not placed or stored in containers shall be kept free of rodents, flies and other insects. No waste may be stored, collected or disposed of in a condition or manner which creates a health hazard, a public nuisance, or is conducive to the breeding and/or existence of rodents or insects.
(b)
All residential solid waste stored outside the residence pending collection shall be placed inside approved containers with lids tightly affixed. Approved containers shall be those issued by the city or its designated contractor to residential customers pursuant to this article, designed to be rolled to curbside for periodic collection. All waste must be bagged and placed inside the container. Each container shall bear a serial number and shall be registered to the assigned customer. All containers shall remain the property of the city or its designated contractor.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.050; Ord. No. 504, § 1, 5-3-1971; Ord. No. 519, § 5, 9-9-1975; Ord. No. 06-2014, § 1, 6-23-2014 )
Sec. 32-5. - Collection of solid waste.
All households in the city limits must have curbside garbage collection.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.060; Ord. No. 519, § 6, 9-9-1975; Ord. No. 709, 12-27-1999)
Sec. 32-6. - Garbage rates.
The rate paid by residential customers of the city for garbage collection services shall be 15 percent more than that price or rate stated or contracted for in the city's vending contract for garbage collection.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.070; Ord. No. 665, 1996; Ord. No. 11-2011, § 1, 8-22-2011)
Sec. 32-7. - Placement for collection.
(a)
Customers shall place their container at curbside in front of the customer's residence not earlier than 12:00 noon on the day prior to the scheduled service.
(b)
All containers shall be removed from the curbside and returned to a rear or side yard of the residence no later than 12:00 noon on the day following the scheduled service.
(c)
Bulk waste, yard waste and other acceptable waste items shall be placed on the street curb no earlier than the day before pickup day.
(d)
Fallen limbs, trees, cut limbs, cut trees and trash of this category shall be cut by the property owner into lengths of not more than four feet that will be of a size that may be handled by one person or of a size to be consumed by a chipper machine. Other trash which shall include yard wastes, weeds, grass, trimmings, leaves, brush and pine straw must be neatly piled at the curb. All trash and cuttings shall be placed as near as possible to the street or sidewalk right-of-way adjacent to the property owner's property but not so as to get into the street or block the sidewalk. At no time shall trash be placed in gutters, drains, walkways or alleys or streets of the city.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.080; Ord. No. 686, 11-24-1997; Ord. No. 06-2014, § 1, 6-23-2014 ; Res. No. 2021-13 )
Sec. 32-8. - Prohibited dumping.
No person, party or firm shall dump human, solid or other waste in any stream or body of water, public or private, in the city without approval and/or permission of the county board of health and the city.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.120; Ord. No. 519, § 10, 9-9-1975)
Sec. 32-9. - Curbside removal fee.
(a)
All items not contained in a commercially sold garbage or trash bag shall be removed by the city at curb with fees being assessed to the owner or occupier of such residence:
(1)
Per appliance (metal construction);
(2)
Per furniture item (wood construction).
(b)
In addition, any yard clippings, ground debris or natural yard disposal (in excess of one cubic yard) shall be assessed a per hour fee equal to the cost of removal.
(c)
The rates of the fees for curbside removal shall be as established by the city council, from time to time.
(Prior Code, § 8.20.140; Ord. No. 693, 4-27-1998)
Sec. 32-10. - Waste unacceptable for collection.
(a)
The following items constitute waste unacceptable for collection by the city:
(1)
Dangerous materials or substances such as poisons, acids, caustics, batteries, paints and explosives;
(2)
Building rubbish;
(3)
Ashes;
(4)
Automobile and motor vehicle tires;
(5)
All other solid waste not specifically authorized in this chapter.
(b)
Any person responsible for waste not acceptable for collection by the city or its designated contractor shall make any arrangements as may be necessary for the collection and disposal of the unacceptable waste.
( Ord. No. 06-2014, § 2, 6-23-2014 ; Res. No. 2021-13 )
