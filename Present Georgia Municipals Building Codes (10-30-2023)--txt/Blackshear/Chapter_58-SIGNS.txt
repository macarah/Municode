Sec. 58-1. - General provisions.
(a)
All types of signs, except outdoor advertising signs, shall be allowed in all zoning districts without a permit.
(b)
All outdoor advertising signs shall be subject to the same yard and height limitations imposed upon other buildings or structures in the use district in which such sign is located, except as otherwise provided herein.
(c)
For the purpose of computing sign area, only one side of a "V-Type" or double-faced outdoor advertising sign shall be considered.
(d)
Rooftop outdoor advertising signs or sign structures shall not extend more than 30 feet above the roof line. Rooftop outdoor advertising signs or sign structures shall not extend beyond or overhang any exterior wall of the building upon which secured.
(e)
No outdoor advertising sign or sign structure shall be placed upon any street, sidewalk, or highway right-of-way. All signs must have a setback of three feet from property line.
(f)
No outdoor advertising sign shall be erected, relocated or maintained so as to prevent free ingress to or egress from any door, window or fire escape.
(g)
No portion of any outdoor advertising sign shall be less than ten feet above the level of a sidewalk or other pedestrian thoroughfare, nor be less than 16 feet above the level of a public driveway, alley or street.
(h)
Any sign not particularly designated above shall be subject to the control and supervision of the city council.
(Code 1987, § 5-70)
Sec. 58-2. - Outdoor advertising signs as permitted uses.
(a)
Residential districts. No outdoor advertising signs are permitted in residential districts in the city.
(b)
Commercial districts. One outdoor advertising sign for each lot of 100 feet or less or lot frontage with one additional sign for each additional 100 feet or less of lot frontage under single ownership at the time of the passage of the ordinance from which this chapter was derived, and provided such sign shall not exceed 800 square feet in area per structure facing. Where two signs, each not exceeding 600 square feet in area per structure facing are supported by the same sign structure, one above the other, they shall be considered as a single outdoor advertising sign.
(c)
Wholesale and industrial districts. One outdoor advertising sign for each lot of 75 feet or less of lot frontage with one additional sign for each additional 75 feet or less of lot frontage under single ownership at the time of the passage of the ordinance from which this chapter was derived, and provided such sign shall not exceed 800 square feet in area per structure facing. Where signs, each not exceeding 600 feet in area per structure facing are supported by the same sign structure, one above the other, they shall be considered as a single outdoor advertising sign.
(d)
Mobile business signs. Class 8 mobile business signs, single- or double-faced provided that they:
(1)
Shall have a maximum size of 15 feet for each side;
(2)
There is only one sign for each zoning lot on which the sign is located;
(3)
Shall be set back at least five feet from property line with flashing light; bulbs not to exceed 60 watt, or three feet setback without flashing lights;
(4)
All electrical hook-ups shall be approved by city building inspector.
(Code 1987, § 5-71)
Sec. 58-3. - Signs prohibited in all districts.
The following signs are prohibited in all use districts:
(1)
Any sign erected or painted upon a sloping roof, fence, tree, stand-pipe, fire escape or utility pole.
(2)
Any sign which uses the word "Stop" or "Danger" prominently displayed or which is a copy or imitation of an official traffic control sign.
(Code 1987, § 5-72)
Sec. 58-4. - Maintenance and removal of outdoor advertising signs.
(a)
All outdoor advertising signs and sign structures shall be kept in repair and in proper state preservation. All unsafe signs must adhere to the provisions of the city building code.
(b)
Outdoor advertising signs which are no longer functional or are abandoned, shall be removed, or relocated, at the owner's expense, in compliance with the provisions of this chapter within 30 days following malfunction.
(c)
Any legally established nonconforming outdoor advertising signs or sign structure shall be permitted without alteration in size or location, provided that the requirements of this chapter are adhered to and provided that nothing herein shall prevent maintenance, repairing or posting of legally established nonconforming signs.
(d)
Upon destruction, partial or complete, of an outdoor advertising sign, the owner thereof shall have the right to reconstruct, rebuild, renovate, or repair such sign substantially to the same condition as before such destruction, provided the provisions of this chapter are adhered to.
(Code 1987, § 5-73)
Sec. 58-5. - Temporary signs.
(a)
A temporary sign is a sign or sign structure which is not constructed for permanent use and advertises an event or activity occurring at a specific time or period or time. Examples of temporary signs are political advertisements, open house advertisements, and yard sale advertisements. Temporary signs do not include portable signs which are constructed for permanent use.
(b)
A political advertisement shall not be permitted for a period longer than 60 days, except that when a candidate wins a primary election, such political advertisement may remain until the general election, but in no case shall the additional time be greater than 90 days.
(c)
A temporary sign shall be removed by the owner or owner's designee within three days after the final date of the event or activity to which the sign makes reference. Failure of owner or owner's designee to remove a temporary sign within the time period prescribed in this Code section may result in the collection and destruction of the temporary sign by a representative of the city. Any cost associated with such removal and destruction may be administratively assessed by the city against the owner of the sign.
(d)
All vehicular signs shall adhere to the requirements set forth herein except for those signs not exceeding 40 square feet and attached to vehicles which are used primarily for other purposes than displaying an outdoor advertising sign.
(e)
Signs which display general advertisements or commercial promotions are prohibited.
(Code 1987, § 5-74; Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 4-9-13)
