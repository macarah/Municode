ARTICLE XXII. - COSMETOLOGISTS AND BARBERS

Sec. 11-350. - State law.
This article shall be supplemental to the provisions contained in Title 43, Chapters 7 and 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, and, whenever applicable, terms used herein shall be defined as described therein.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, § 1, 1-2-01)
Sec. 11-351. - Area requirements.
Each beauty shop and barbershop shall have an unpartitioned (i.e., no interior walls) area of at least one thousand (1,000) square feet of floor space, with no more than one (1) barber, barber apprentice, cosmetologist, esthetician, nail technician or assistant cosmetologist as defined in subsection (d), for every sixteen (16) square feet of floor space in the beauty shop or barbershop.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, § 1, 1-2-01)
Sec. 11-352. - Safety requirements.
Each beauty shop and barbershop, for every one thousand (1000) square feet of floor space, shall have at least one (1) functioning sink and one (1) bathroom facility that complies with the Americans with Disabilities Act. Each beauty shop and barbershop shall have an unobstructed means of access to a front entryway that provides a means of ingress to and egress from the beauty shop or barbershop. Additionally, each beauty shop and barbershop located in the downtown business district shall have an unobstructed means of access to a rear entryway that provides a means of ingress and egress from the beauty shop or barbershop.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, § 1, 1-2-01; Ord. No. 2001-28, § 1, 9-4-01)
Sec. 11-353. - Information and license requirements.
(a)
The owner(s) and/or operator(s) of each beauty shop and barbershop shall provide to the city a current list of all barbers, barber apprentices, cosmetologists, estheticians, nail technicians and assistant cosmetologists, as defined in subsection (d), who are employed by the owner(s) and/or operator(s) of the beauty shop or barbershop.
(b)
Each beauty shop and barbershop shall have and those doing business inside the beauty shop or barbershop shall provide to the city a current list of all barbers, barber apprentices, cosmetologists, estheticians, nail technicians and assistant cosmetologists, as defined in subsection (d), who engage in the occupation of cosmetology or barbering or otherwise work or are employed at the beauty shop or barbershop. The list shall also specify by whom each barber, barber apprentice, cosmetologist, esthetician, nail technician and assistant cosmetologist, as defined in subsection (d), who engages in the occupation of cosmetology or barbering, or otherwise works or is employed at the beauty shop or barbershop is employed. Compliance with this subsection shall be the joint and several responsibility of all barbers, barber apprentices, cosmetologists, estheticians, nail technicians and assistant cosmetologists, as defined in subsection (d), who engage in the occupation of cosmetology or barbering or otherwise work or are employed at the beauty shop or barbershop;
(c)
All barbers, barber apprentices, cosmetologists, estheticians, and nail technicians engaged in the occupation of cosmetology or barbering in the city shall have and make available immediately upon the request of any representative of the city that particular barber's barber apprentice's, cosmetologist's, esthetician's, or nail technician's license or certificate(s) of registration as required by Title 43, Chapters 7 or 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
(d)
Any person working in a Beauty Shop or Barbershop, but not required by Title 43, Chapters 7 or 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated to possess a license or certificate, who comes into contact with customers' bodies, nails or hair in any capacity while working in the Beauty Shop or Barbershop (hereinafter "Assistant Cosmetologist") must obtain from the City an Assistant Cosmetologist License and shall have and make available immediately upon the request of any representative of the City that particular Assistant Cosmetologist's License. The regulatory fee for this license shall be fifteen dollars ($15.00).
(Ord. No. 2001-01, § 1, 1-2-01)
Sec. 11-354. - Currently operating beauty shops and barbershops.
Beauty shops and barbershops that are operating as of the effective date of the ordinance [Ord. No. 2001-01, effective Jan. 2, 2001] establishing this article shall not be subject to the provisions of sections 11-351 and 11-352, provided that such beauty shops and barbershops shall continue to operate subject to the provisions of the zoning ordinance of the City of College Park.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, § 1, 1-2-01)
Sec. 11-355. - Penalty.
The penalty for violation of any provision of this Article shall be that penalty provided for by Section 1-8 of the City Code.
(Ord. No. 2001-01, § 1, 1-2-01)
Secs. 11-356—11-369. - Reserved.
