ARTICLE XIII. - DEPOSITORY FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 11-220. - Business license tax—Imposed.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-90, there is hereby levied, for each year, an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the city at a rate of twenty-five hundredths (0.25) per cent of the gross receipts of said depository financial institutions. "Gross receipts" shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-90. "Depository financial institutions," shall mean state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
(Code 1963, § 14-401)
Sec. 11-221. - Same—Minimum tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 11-220 shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
Sec. 11-222. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to this tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the governing body on March first of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Said return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in subsection (d) of O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. The governing body shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this article based upon the information provided in said return.
(Code 1963, § 14-403)
Sec. 11-223. - Due date of taxes.
Taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than thirty (30) days after filing of the return prescribed by section 11-222, unless extended by the governing body.
(Code 1963, § 14-404)
