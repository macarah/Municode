ARTICLE II. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Other boards and commissions, § 2-61 et seq.

Sec. 26-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Development Authorities Law means O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-3.
Sec. 26-32. - Determination of need.
There is hereby determined and declared to be a present and future need for a development authority (as more fully described and defined in the Development Authorities Law) to function in the county, for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the county thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-33. - Activation of development authority.
There is hereby activated in the county the public body corporate and politic known as the "Development Authority of Gilmer County" which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law and particularly O.C.G.A. § 36-62-4.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-34. - Board of directors; terms.
The board of directors of the development authority of the county shall consist of seven directors, who shall serve for four-year staggered terms.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Cross reference— Other boards and commissions, § 2-61 et seq.
Sec. 26-35. - Director to hold office until successor elected.
If at the end of any term of office of any director of the development authority, a successor thereto shall not have been elected then the director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so elected.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-36. - Organization; duties.
The board of directors of the development authority shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-37. - Right of municipalities to activate development authority not affected.
The action taken by the board of commissioners as specified in this article is not intended in any way to affect the right of any municipal corporation located within the county to activate a development authority as authorized by the Development Authorities Law.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-38. - Issuance of obligations; filing of statements.
In connection with any issue of obligations of the development authority of the county, the development authority is authorized and empowered on behalf of the county to file certain statements with the state department of community affairs, as required by law.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-39. - Filing of applications for allocations.
In connection with any allocation permitted by O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq., the development authority of the county is authorized and empowered on behalf of the county to file certain applications with the state department of community affairs, as required by law.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-40. - Notice of public hearing.
In connection with any issue of obligations of the development authority of the county, the development authority is authorized and empowered on behalf of the county to give notice as required by law, and to conduct, any public hearing required by under the Tax Equity and Fiscal Responsibility Act or any successor or similar provision of law now or hereafter in effect.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-41. - Conduct of public hearing.
No further action either official or unofficial, by or on behalf of the county shall be necessary for the development authority of the county to conduct a public hearing with regard to any particular issue of obligations of the development authority.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-42. - Article not to affect previous legislation.
The action taken by the board of commissioners as specified in this article is not intended in any way to affect any public corporation, downtown, industrial development, water and sewerage or payroll authority previously created by legislative act or constitutional amendment including, without limitation, its existence, purpose, organization, powers or function.
(Ord. of 10-9-1990)
Sec. 26-43—26-74. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - BROADBAND READY COMMUNITY

Sec. 26-75. - Definitions.
As used in this article:
Applicant means a person applying for a permit for a broadband network project.
Broadband network project means any deployment of broadband services.
Political subdivision means a county, municipal corporation, consolidated government, or local authority.
Permit means any local permit, license, certificate approval, registration, or similar form of approval required by policy, administrative rule, regulation, ordinance, or resolution with respect to a broadband network project.
(Res. No. 19-174, 11-14-2019)
Sec. 26-76. - Single point of contact.
Gilmer County, Georgia, shall appoint a single point of contact for all matters related to a broadband network project:
(1)
The single point of contact documentation shall include:
a.
Position and title;
b.
Name (first, last);
c.
Organization (municipality, county, or authority with participating entities involved in the Gilmer County request designation);
d.
Phone number(s) (work and mobile if applicable);
e.
Email(s) (preferred email alias that can be directed to point of contact or acceptable official work email);
f.
Website URL to contacts page (required if applicable/available).
(2)
The single point of contact shall be available for matters related to a broadband network project or a related liaison who may direct such inquiry in real time, with general scope and responsibilities to include permitting and right-of-way; and
(3)
The single point of contact information must be current to maintain designation, by updating with such change in contact information on web pages and associated sources, within 15 calendar days of change.
(Res. No. 19-174, 11-14-2019)
Sec. 26-77. - Application completeness review.
(a)
Gilmer County shall determine whether an application is incomplete and notify the applicant, by email, of the determination by Gilmer County within ten calendar days of receiving an application.
(b)
If Gilmer County does not respond to the applicant on whether the application is incomplete, within ten calendar days, the application shall be assumed to be complete on the 11th day.
(Res. No. 19-174, 11-14-2019)
Sec. 26-78. - Notification of incomplete application.
(a)
If Gilmer County determines that an application is not complete, the notification by email to the applicant shall specify all required components of the submitted application that were considered "incomplete".
(b)
Gilmer County's response shall include a checklist of sequenced items that resulted in the application being deemed "incomplete" and the review timeline shall be as follows:
(1)
The applicant has up to 40 calendar days from the date of notification of incompleteness to respond back with corrections; and
(2)
If the applicant does not respond back within 40 calendar days, the application is deemed canceled.
(c)
If within ten calendar days Gilmer County does not respond to the applicant on whether the corrected application is incomplete, the application shall be assumed to be complete on the 11th day; and
(d)
Gilmer County shall require a new submission and reset the process and application fees, should an application be deemed incomplete a second time.
(Res. No. 19-174, 11-14-2019)
Sec. 26-79. - Approval or denial notification.
If, on or before the 11th day as described in subsection 26-77(b), an application is deemed complete, Gilmer County shall approve or deny an application within ten calendar days unless a joint meeting between the applicant and Gilmer County is deemed as necessary.
(1)
If a joint meeting is deemed necessary, the joint meeting must occur within 15 calendar days of notification of completion and the joint meeting shall include:
a.
Where applicant is going to conduct work;
b.
When the work will be conducted;
c.
What type of work will be done;
d.
Who Gilmer County can contact for specific details or related questions; and
e.
Any permit seeking approval under application.
(2)
Following a joint meeting between the applicant and Gilmer County, Gilmer County shall deny or approve the application within ten days.
(3)
Upon final approval, any required permit permitted shall be deemed issued.
(Res. No. 19-174, 11-14-2019)
Sec. 26-80. - Related fees.
(a)
Any fee imposed by Gilmer County to review an application, issue a permit, or perform any other activity related to a broadband network project shall be reasonable, cost based, and nondiscriminatory to all applicants.
(b)
Any application fee that exceeds $100.00 shall be considered unreasonable unless Gilmer County can provide documentation justifying such fee based on a specific cost.
(Res. No. 19-174, 11-14-2019)
Sec. 26-81. - Other information.
(a)
Double fee. No city or county shall require an application or permit(s) when already approved by an authorized state or federal jurisdiction. Provided shall notify and provide a copy of the approved permit to the single point of contact at the city or county prior to access of right-of-way within the city or county jurisdiction.
(b)
Application validity timeline. Any approved application shall be valid for six months from the date of approval. Should a provider not commence the service request qualified in the approved application within six months, the application shall expire, and it shall require a new permit approval and any associated fees, as applicable.
(c)
Single service drop. A city or county shall not require a permit for a broadband service provider to perform an installation of broadband service at an individual customer's service address as long as the facility being utilized only transverses a deminimis portion of the public right-of-way to reach the customer's property. The provider must still comply with the provisions of Chapter 9 of Title 25 of the O.C.G.A.
(d)
Acknowledgment.
(1)
A Georgia certified broadband ready community has an affirmative duty to notify the Georgia Department of Community Affairs of any changes to the information submitted as part of its application; and
(2)
Failure to notify the Georgia Department of Community Affairs of changes may result in revocation of Gilmer County's Broadband Ready Certification, should the certification be granted.
(Res. No. 19-174, 11-14+2019)
