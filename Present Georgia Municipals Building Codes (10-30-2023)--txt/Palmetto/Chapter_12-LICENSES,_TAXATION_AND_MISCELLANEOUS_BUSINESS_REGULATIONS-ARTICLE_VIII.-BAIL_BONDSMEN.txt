Sec. 12-221. - Annual licensing.
(a)
Any person wishing to engage in the business of a bail bondsman must first obtain a county license, complete the initial application, submit any required paperwork or documentation, pay the license fee, and then be approved for the initial license by the mayor and council prior to conducting business or operating within the city. Initial licenses shall expire on December 31 of the year in which they are granted.
(b)
All licensees operating under this article shall be required to renew their licenses annually during the month of January of each year, following the same procedure for the renewal application as was required for the original application. Prior to renewal, all license holders shall obtain the required county license and shall display such county license to the city administrator at the time the application for renewal is submitted.
(c)
The annual license fee for licenses issued under this article shall be set from time to time by the mayor and council as they deem appropriate, and a schedule of such fees shall be kept current and maintained on file in the city administrator's office.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93)
Sec. 12-222. - Application.
(a)
Application under this article shall be completed in writing, verified, and filed with the city administrator, on a form to be provided by the city, and shall contain the following:
(1)
Name and home address of the applicant.
(2)
If the business is to be owned or operated by a corporation, partnership, or association, the names and home addresses of the owners, partners, shareholders, officers and directors.
(3)
The social security numbers, driver's license numbers, and dates of birth of the applicant and all owners, partners, shareholders, officers and directors.
(4)
Address and telephone number of the premises where the proposed business is to be located.
(b)
The following documents must be attached as exhibits to the application:
(1)
Certified documentation evidencing the county sheriff's approval of the applicant to conduct business as a bail bondsman, or a certified copy of the county license to conduct business as a bail bondsman.
(2)
The surety bond, or proof of an approved and accepted alternative form of security as a substitution thereof, as required under section 12-224.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93; Ord. No. 93-04, § 1, 6-7-93)
Sec. 12-223. - Investigation of applicant, owner, etc.
All applicants, owners, partners, shareholders, officers and directors applying for a license under this article shall be investigated by the chief of police within ten (10) business days following the applicant's submission of the application to the city administrator. The chief of police will then forward the application along with his recommendation to the mayor and council for consideration and action at their next regularly-scheduled public meeting. No license shall be granted prior to approval by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93)
Sec. 12-224. - Surety required.
(a)
Applicants for a license under this article shall file a surety bond with the city administrator binding the surety to the city in an amount up to twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) issued by a surety company that is acceptable to and approved by the city administrator. No license shall be granted under this article prior to submission of the surety bond and approval of the surety company by the city administrator.
(b)
A liability insurance policy or a letter of credit payable to the city in the amount of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00), which has been issued by an insurance company or a financial institution, respectively, authorized to do business in the state and approved by the city administrator, may be permitted by the mayor and council in their discretion in lieu of the surety bond herein required. Alternatively, real property with a fair market value of fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00), free and clear of any liens, and remaining lien-free throughout the existence of the business, may be posted to the city as security, if acceptable by the mayor and council in their discretion, in lieu of the surety bond, liability insurance policy, or letter of credit.
(c)
In addition, the city shall have the right to demand additional surety depending on the circumstances and in the discretion of the city administrator and/or the municipal court judge. The surety amount may also be raised from time to time, as the city administrator and/or the municipal court judge deem appropriate, based upon the number and amount of outstanding bonds issued by any particular business licensed hereunder.
(d)
Any security submitted pursuant to this section, whether in the form of surety bonds, insurance policies, letters of credit, or real property, shall be held by the city for a minimum period of twelve (12) months after the business is terminated.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93; Ord. No. 93-04, §§ 2—4, 6-7-93)
Sec. 12-225. - Suspension and revocation; authorized.
All licenses granted under this article are subject to revocation and/or suspension by either the mayor and council or by the municipal court, subject to constitutional due process.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93)
Sec. 12-226. - Transfer of license.
No license granted under this article shall be transferred.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93)
Sec. 12-227. - Automatic revocation upon revocation of county license.
Whenever the county shall revoke any county bondsman license the city bondsman license is thereby revoked. It shall be necessary to have all required county licenses in order to hold a valid city license.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93)
Sec. 12-228. - Penalty for noncompliance with article.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate, control or own any bail bondsman business without having complied with the provisions of this article. Any noncompliance shall subject the license holder to revocation of the business license, and/or fines and/or imprisonment.
(Ord. No. 93-03, 2-1-93)
