ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 23-1. - Statutes adopted.
Pursuant to chapter 6 of title 40 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, Code Sections 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, title 40, chapter 6 [O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq.] (all known as the Uniform Rules of the Road) and the definitions contained in Code Section 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city with like effect as if recited herein.
(Code 1977, § 16-1)
Sec. 23-2. - Temporary regulations.
The chief of police may establish temporary traffic regulations which the public must obey after being warned. Verbal warning by an authorized police officer of the city, or by printed signs erected in the area affected, shall constitute sufficient notice to the public of such temporary traffic regulations.
(Code 1977, § 16-4)
Sec. 23-3. - Failure to have vehicle under control.
No driver of any vehicle shall operate that vehicle in such a manner so as to collide with any object legally placed upon or adjacent to any street or highway nor shall a driver operate a vehicle so that such vehicle leaves or enters any street or highway except at an intersection or driveway.
(Ord. No. 85-1, § 16-30, 5-14-85)
Sec. 23-4. - Collision with a parked vehicle.
No driver of any vehicle shall operate that vehicle in a manner so as to collide with a parked vehicle.
(Ord. No. 85-1, § 16-29, 5-14-85)
Sec. 23-5. - Not to impede traffic; minimum.
No person shall retard or impede traffic between street intersections.
(Code 1977, § 16-3)
Sec. 23-6. - Blocking railroad grade crossings.
It shall be unlawful to block a railroad crossing for more than five (5) minutes.
(Code 1977, § 16-12)
Sec. 23-7. - Truck routes—Definition of truck; use of truck routes required; exceptions.
(a)
For purposes of this section, a "truck" is defined as any motor vehicle having more than two (2) axles; provided, however, that the restrictions contained in this section shall not apply to the following motor vehicles which may have more than two (2) axles:
(1)
Recreational vehicles;
(2)
Municipal emergency vehicles;
(3)
Municipal service vehicles; or
(4)
Buses.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a person or corporation to operate or park a truck on any street, road, drive or avenue in Palmetto which is not designated as a truck route in section 23-8 of this article, except under the following circumstances:
(1)
Any truck may travel via the most direct route possible from a truck route location in the city for the exclusive purpose of local pick up, delivery, repair, or other necessary business, subject to verification of such purpose by destination papers.
(2)
Any truck owned or operated by a business may travel the most direct route possible from a truck route to the place of said business in the city, provided that this exception shall not apply in cases where businesses are located in districts zoned for residential use.
( Ord. No. 2017-01, § 1, 3-6-2017 )
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2017-01 , § 1, adopted Mar. 6, 2017, repealed the former section and enacted a new section as set out herein. The former section pertained to similar subject matter. For prior history, see Code Comparative Table.
Sec. 23-8. - Truck routes designated.
The city streets, roads, drives and/or avenues, or portions thereof, as designated below, shall constitute the exclusive truck routes in Palmetto:
(1)
Roosevelt Highway (U.S. Highway 29) from the northern limits of the city to the southern limits of the city;
(2)
Cascade Palmetto Highway (State Route 154) from Roosevelt Highway (U.S. Highway 29) to the northern limits of the city; and
(3)
Weldon Road from Palmetto Tyrone Road to Roosevelt Highway (U.S. Highway 29).
These designated routes shall be shown on a map entitled "Official Truck Route Map—City of Palmetto, Georgia." This map is hereby made a part of this section. The city clerk shall retain a copy of the map and all amendments thereto.
( Ord. No. 2017-01, § 2, 3-6-2017 )
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2017-01 , § 2, adopted Mar. 6, 2017, repealed the former section and enacted a new section as set out herein. The former section pertained to similar subject matter. For prior history, see Code Comparative Table.
State Law reference— Authority to establish truck routes, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(13).
Sec. 23-9. - Bicycles, skateboards, etc., prohibited on certain sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride any bicycle, skateboard, roller skates or other such vehicle or apparatus upon the sidewalks of the business section of the city along U.S. Highway 29 beginning at a point where Church Street intersects U.S. Highway 29 and ending at a point where Menneffee Street intersects U.S. Highway 29.
(Code 1977, § 15-5)
State Law reference— Authority to regulate toy vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(18).
Sec. 23-10. - Shrubbery, other hazards at street corners; duty of property owner.
It shall be the duty of every property owner to keep weeds on their respective lots, as well as shrubbery thereon, in such condition as not to create traffic hazards at street corners. Shrubbery, vines, trees or other things which would obstruct the view of a person passing a street corner or street intersection shall be kept in such condition as not to obstruct the view of motorists using the streets.
(Code 1977, § 16-14)
Sec. 23-11. - Railroad trains.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a railroad train or locomotive within the city at a speed greater than twenty-five (25) miles per hour.
(Code 1977, § 16-5)

Sec. 23-36. - Traffic, speed, truck, parking and other zones; signs; traffic-control devices; schedule
(a)
Designation; maintenance. The police chief or his designee, upon approval by resolution of the mayor and council, is authorized to designate and maintain with appropriate control signs, markings and devices after engineering and traffic investigations:
(1)
Speed zones;
(2)
Truck zones and streets to be designated specifically to prohibit various classes of trucks;
(3)
One-way streets and other directional control devices;
(4)
Freight-loading zones and regulations;
(5)
Parking and no-parking zones and regulations thereon;
(6)
Stop, yield and other right-of-way signs;
(7)
Stop signals and other traffic signals; and
(8)
No-passing zones.
(b)
Written orders. The police chief or his designee is authorized to issue written orders designated by appropriate traffic-control signs, markings and devices after engineering and traffic investigations:
(1)
Pedestrian crosswalks;
(2)
Other safety zones for pedestrians;
(3)
Traffic lanes; and
(4)
Any other sign, marking or zone necessary for orderly and safe conditions on the streets of the city.
(c)
Schedule. The police chief shall maintain or cause to be recorded a current schedule of all traffic rules, regulations and orders under this section, which records shall be available for inspection by the public. For items listed in subsection (a) above, this schedule and any amendments thereto shall become effective only upon approval thereof by ordinance of the mayor and council; and this schedule is hereby incorporated herein, and copies thereof shall be available for public inspection in the office of the city clerk.
(d)
Specifications. All traffic-control signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the Manual on Uniform Traffic-Control Devices, adopted by the state transportation board. All signs and signals required hereunder for a particular purpose shall, so far as practicable, be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic-control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this Code shall be official traffic-control devices of the city.
(e)
Violations. Any violation of any traffic zone, marking, sign or any other traffic-control device established hereunder shall be a violation of this Code.
(Ord. No. 90-13, 1-7-91)
