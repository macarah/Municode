PART I. - BASIC PROVISIONS
ARTICLE I. - PREAMBLE AND ENACTMENT CLAUSE
Pursuant to the authority conferred by the Constitution of the State of Georgia and laws enacted pursuant thereto, and more specifically the Georgia General Planning Enabling Act of 1957 (Act No. 358, as amended), the mayor and council of the city, do hereby resolve and enact into law the following articles and sections, for the following purposes:
(1)
To encourage economically sound and orderly land development in accordance with the comprehensive plan and other policies and objectives of the city;
(2)
To assure the provision of required streets, utilities, fire protection and other facilities and services in new land developments;
(3)
To provide a system for the subdividing of lands and the accurate recording of land titles.
(4)
To assure equitable review and approval of all land subdivisions by providing uniform procedures and standards for the subdivider.
(5)
To assure the adequate provision of safe and convenient traffic access and circulation, both vehicular and pedestrian, in new developments;
(6)
To assure the provision of needed public open spaces and public facility sites in new subdivisions through the dedication or reservation of land for recreation, education and other public purposes; and
(7)
To assure, in general, the wise development of new areas in harmony with the development plans of the city.
(8)
To protect and promote the health, safety, and general welfare.
ARTICLE II. - SHORT TITLE
This ordinance shall be known and may be cited as "The Land Subdivision Regulations of the City of Palmetto, Georgia."
ARTICLE III. - INTENT AND APPLICATION
It is the intent of the mayor and council that this ordinance will apply to and provide guidance for the subdivision of lands within the incorporated limits of the city. Any land subdivision development activity must first comply with this ordinance.
ARTICLE IV. - DEFINITIONS
Except as specifically defined herein, all words in this ordinance have their customary dictionary definitions. For the purposes of this ordinance, certain words or terms used herein are defined as follows:

Sec. A. - Use of words and interpretation.
(1)
Words in the present tense include the future tense. Words in the singular include the plural, and words in the plural include the singular.
(2)
The word "shall" is always mandatory and never discretionary.
(3)
The word "may" is permissive.
(4)
The word "person" includes individuals, firms, partnerships, corporations, associations, governmental bodies and all other legal entities.
(5)
The word "street" includes streets, avenues, boulevards, roads, highways, expressways, lanes, alleys and other vehicular ways.
(6)
Use of the word "and" is inclusive and requires that all of the component phrases so connected must be present or fulfilled for sufficiency.
(7)
Use of the word "or" is not exclusive and requires that at least one (1) of the component phrases so connected must be present or fulfilled for sufficiency. The word "or" may allow more than one (1) component phrase to be present or fulfilled, as in the term "and/or."
Sec. B. - Specific definitions.
(1)
Alley or service drive. A minor, permanent, public service way which is used primarily for vehicular service to the back or the side for properties otherwise abutting a street.
(2)
Arterial. An arterial street as defined and/or designed in the Zoning Ordinance or comprehensive plan of the city.
(3)
City. The City of Palmetto, Georgia.
(4)
City engineer. The term "city engineer" as used herein includes the office of the city engineer, and as such, may include the building inspector, city clerk, city administrator, or other persons designated by the mayor and council to act on behalf of the city.
(5)
Cul-de-sac. A minor local street having one (1) end open to traffic and being permanently terminated by a vehicular turnaround.
(6)
Electrical distribution, overhead. All electrical conductors and devices attached above ground to poles or other structures of sufficient size and strength.
(7)
Electrical distribution, underground. All electrical conductors and devices with the exception of transformers, switching cubicles and meters installed underground.
(8)
Electrical system, primary. That portion of the electrical distribution system operating at 7200/12,470 volts. The primary electrical distribution system includes the transformers necessary to supply the secondary electrical system.
(9)
Electrical system, secondary. That portion of the electrical distribution system operating at the customer utilization voltage.
(10)
Final plat. A finished drawing of a subdivision showing completely and accurately all legal and boundary information and certifications required by these regulations and conforming to the state Plat Act.
(11)
Lot. A developed or undeveloped tract of land in one (1) ownership, legally transferable as a single unit of land.
(12)
Lot, corner. A lot abutting upon the intersection of two (2) or more streets.
(13)
Lot of record. A lot which is part of a subdivision, the plat for which has been recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Fulton/Coweta County, or a parcel of land, the deed for which has been recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Fulton/Coweta County.
(14)
Lot, through or double-frontage. A lot having frontage on two (2) streets that are approximately parallel.
(15)
Pedestrian way. A right-of-way within a block dedicated to public use, intended primarily for pedestrians and from which motor propelled vehicles are excluded.
(16)
Planning and zoning board. The city planning and zoning board.
(17)
Plat. A map or drawing indicating the subdivision, resubdivision, or recombination of land.
(18)
Preliminary plat. A drawing which shows the perimeter boundary, topography, lot layout arrangement, street layout, and other features of a proposed subdivision, as specified in these regulations.
(19)
Street, private. An access way, other than a driveway, similar to and having the same function as a public street, providing access to more than one (1) property, but held in private ownership.
(20)
Street, public. Any publicly maintained way for vehicular traffic which has been accepted for maintenance as a street by the city. For the purposes of this ordinance, streets are divided into the following categories:
(a)
Major thoroughfare: A principal arterial highway carrying through traffic, designated as such on the zoning map of the city.
(b)
Collector street: A street that carried traffic from local streets to major thoroughfares, including such streets in new subdivisions as required by the planning and zoning board.
(c)
Local street: A street used primarily for access to abutting properties.
(d)
Marginal access street: A local street parallel and adjacent to a major thoroughfare, expressway, limited access highway or railroad right-of-way, which provides access to abutting properties.
(21)
Subdivider. The person having sufficient proprietary interest in the land to be subdivided as will authorize the maintenance of proceedings to subdivide such land under these regulations, or the authorized agent of that person for the purpose of such proceeding.
(22)
Subdivision. All divisions of a tract or parcel of land into two (2) or more lots, building sites or other divisions for the purpose, whether immediate or future, of legacy or building development; including all divisions of land involving a new street to which the public has access, or a change in an existing street, and including resubdivision and, where appropriate to the context, relates to the process of subdividing or to the land or area subdivided.
ARTICLE V. - PLATTING AUTHORITY AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. A. - Platting authority.
From and after the passage of this ordinance, the mayor and council shall be the platting authority. No land dedicated as a public street or other public purpose shall be opened or accepted as a public street or for any other purpose, and no subdivision of land shall be made, nor subdivision plat, nor part thereof, shall be recorded before obtaining final approval from the mayor and council. Said final approval shall be entered in writing on the final plat by the city clerk. A recorded final plat shall be presented to the city clerk prior to any building permits being issued for the subdivision.
Sec. B. - Plat approval required prior to initiation of construction.
No construction work nor associated clearing work shall be undertaken on a proposed land subdivision within the incorporated area of the city except as permitted by these subdivision regulations.
Sec. C. - Land subdivision plat recording.
No plat of a land subdivision within the incorporated area of the city may be recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Fulton/Coweta County except the land subdivision plat of record on which is inscribed the approval of the planning and zoning board and the mayor and council.
The filing or recording of a plat of a subdivision which is not a land subdivision plat of record approved by the mayor and council is hereby declared a misdemeanor, and, upon conviction, the person(s) filing or recording such plat shall be punished as provided by law.
Sec. D. - Use of plat.
No person shall transfer title or attempt to record title to any land in the city, and no building permit may be issued on said land unless:
(1)
Said land existed as a single parcel, recorded as such with the clerk of superior court of Fulton County or Coweta County, as determined by the location of the land, prior to the 1966 subdivision regulations of the city;
(2)
Said land is shown on a final plat as approved (under these or any previous applicable regulations) and duly recorded with the clerk of superior court of Fulton County or Coweta County, as determined by the location of the land;
(3)
Said land is shown on a plat recorded with the clerk of superior court of Fulton County or Coweta County, as determined by the location of the land, pursuant to the regulations governing subdivision exemptions contained herein; or
(4)
Said land is an aggregation of properties for land assembly purposes, and no building permit will be requested prior to the compliance with these regulations and the zoning ordinance.
The owner or the agent of the owner of any land to be subdivided in the incorporated area of the city who transfers or sells or agrees to sell or negotiates to sell land by reference to or exhibition or other use of a plat of a subdivision that is not the land subdivision plat of record that has been approved by the mayor and council and has been recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Fulton/Coweta County shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided by law; and the description by metes and bounds in the instrument of transfer or other document used in the selling or transfer shall not exempt the transaction from such penalties. The city attorney of the city may cause such transfer or agreement to be enjoined by appropriate action.
Sec. E. - Acceptance of and improvements in unapproved streets.
The mayor and council shall not accept, lay out, open, improve, grade, pave or light any street, or authorize the laying of any water mains, sewers, connections or other facilities or utilities in any street within the incorporated area of the city unless such street is shown on a land subdivision plat of record approved by the mayor and council and recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Fulton/Coweta County, or unless the street had attained the legal status of a public street prior to the effective date of this or a prior subdivision ordinance; provided, however, that the mayor and council may locate, construct or accept any street if the resolution for such street is first submitted to the planning and zoning board for its review and comment.
Sec. F. - Erection of buildings.
No building permit shall be issued for, and no building or other structure shall be erected on, any lot within the incorporated area of the city unless permitted by these regulations; unless the street on which the lot fronts corresponds in its location and lines with a street shown on the subdivision plat of record of a subdivision that has been approved by the mayor and council and has been recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court of Fulton/Coweta County or unless the specific building lot existed and the street had attained the legal status of a public street prior to the effective date of this or a prior subdivision ordinance or had been accepted as a public street by the mayor and council.
Sec. G. - Effect of plat approval on dedications.
The tentative approval of preliminary plat by the planning and zoning board or the approval of a final plat by the mayor and council does not affect the status of dedication of any streets or other areas shown on the plat reserved for public use.
Sec. H. - Subdivision exemptions.
For the purpose of these regulations, the types of activities contained in this section shall be considered subdivisions but exempt from the procedures and required public improvements portions of these regulations, except as noted:
(1)
Combinations. The combination or recombination of two (2) or more previously platted lots of record, where the total number of lots is not increased and the resultant lots or parcels are in compliance with the zoning ordinance.
(2)
Minor subdivisions. The division of land into two (2) lots, provided:
(a)
Each lot complies with all requirements of the ordinance and is limited to single-family detached residential use.
(b)
Each proposed lot fronts on an existing public street having a right-of-way not less than the city minimum for the street category given to the existing public street.
(c)
Each proposed lot complies with requirements of the Fulton/Coweta County health department.
(d)
Each lot thus created may not be resubdivided pursuant to the provisions of this subparagraph. Such resubdivisions shall be accomplished only through the subdivision procedures contained in these regulations.
(3)
Estate subdivisions. The division of land in any single-family detached residential zoning district into lots having a minimum lot area of five (5) acres, provided:
(a)
Each proposed lot fronts on an existing public street having a right-of-way not less than the city minimum for the street category given to the existing public street.
(b)
Each proposed lot shall provide at least one hundred (100) feet of frontage upon the existing public street and shall meet or exceed all other minimum requirements of the applicable single-family detached residential zoning district.
(c)
Each proposed lot complies with requirements of the Fulton/Coweta health department.
(d)
No lot thus created may be rescheduled to less than five (5) acres as an exemption to these regulations.
For the purpose of these regulations, the types of activities contained in this section shall be considered subdivisions but exempt from the procedures and required public improvements portions of these regulations, provided the property owner notifies the city in writing of such division and provides a copy of a plat of the land subdivision prepared and sealed by a land surveyor currently registered in Georgia. Such notification shall be submitted to the city clerk.
ARTICLE VI. - PRE-APPLICATION CONFERENCE
Before proceeding with a proposed land subdivision, subdividers are invited and encouraged to consult early and informally with the city engineer. The subdivider may submit sketch plans and data showing existing conditions within the site and in its vicinity and the proposed layout of the subdivision. No fee shall be charged for this review, and no formal application is necessary. The purpose of the pre-application conference is to afford the subdivider an opportunity to avail himself of the advice and assistance of the city engineer in order to facilitate the subsequent preparation and approval of subdivision plans.
