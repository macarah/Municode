ARTICLE III. - BILLIARD ROOMS[3]

State Law reference— Billiard rooms generally, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-1 et seq.; municipal regulation of billiard rooms, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-6.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 12-46. - Purpose of article.
(a)
This article has been enacted in accordance with a plan designed for the purpose, among others, of promoting the health and general welfare of the city, to establish reasonable controls and ascertainable standards for the operation, regulation and control of billiard rooms, to give effect to existing land use, and with a general view of promoting desirable living conditions and sustaining neighborhood and property values.
(b)
This article shall not apply to those establishments maintaining no more than one (1) coin-operated pool table which has been previously licensed as a coin-operated device under Article IV, Coin-Operated Devices.
(Code 1977, § 6-20; Ord. No. 90-12, 10-1-90)
Sec. 12-47. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, definitions shall be the same as those utilized and contained within O.C.G.A. Section 48-8-1 except as may be contained and defined elsewhere in this article.
(Code 1977, § 6-21)
Sec. 12-48. - Compliance with article.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate, control, own, frequent or enter any billiard room without having complied with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1977, § 6-22)
Sec. 12-49. - Employees.
(a)
No person shall be employed by any establishment holding any license under this article until such person has been issued a certificate of eligibility by the police department.
(b)
All persons subject to the provisions of this article shall, within seventy-two (72) hours after the date of their first work in an establishment, report to the police department for fingerprinting.
(c)
All persons reporting to the police department may be required to fill out a personal history statement for the purposes of an investigation into their eligibility for employment.
(d)
The police department shall make a search relative to the police record of the person fingerprinted. The commission of a felony, and the conviction thereon shall act as a complete bar to employment in an establishment licensed under this article. The conviction for misdemeanors shall likewise act as a bar if they are of such numbers or of such types as to indicate that the individual has a bad reputation.
(e)
The police department shall either issue a certificate or shall inform the license holder and the prospective employee why the certificate cannot be issued.
(f)
The licensee may appeal an adverse finding to the municipal court.
(g)
The police department may, from time to time, and as additional information becomes available, review issued certificates and may, as circumstances dictate, withdraw and/or suspend certificates for cause. Adverse findings may likewise be appealed to the municipal court.
(h)
The licensee shall pay a twenty-five dollar ($25.00) fee to the police department before employment of any individual. Such fee shall be utilized to pay the costs of the police investigation.
(Code 1977, § 6-23)
Sec. 12-50. - Alcoholic beverages.
It shall be unlawful to sell, serve or allow the use of alcoholic beverages in or on the premises of any business licensed under this article unless such premises or establishment derives at least fifty-one (51) percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages.
(Code 1977, § 6-24; Ord. No. 92-04, § 1, 7-6-92)
Sec. 12-51. - Hours for operation of billiard rooms.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a billiard room between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. or to permit any person to remain in such billiard room between such prohibited hours, except regular employees performing necessary labor on the premises.
(Code 1977, § 6-25)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-9.
Sec. 12-52. - Sanitary regulations; immoral conduct, betting, etc.
(a)
All licensed premises shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to permit, participate in, and/or encourage any disturbance of the peace, or immoral conduct, or betting or wagering on any licensed premises.
(Code 1977, § 6-26)
State Law reference— Sanitation generally, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-7; gambling on premises, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-12.
Sec. 12-53. - Frequenting by minors.
It shall be unlawful for any operator, owner or employee of any business licensed under or operating under this article to allow or permit any person under the age of eighteen (18) years to enter or remain within the confines of said business unless that person is either accompanied by a parent or legal guardian or shall have obtained a notarized authorization, upon forms to be supplied by the city police department, from either a parent or legal guardian.
(Code 1977, § 6-27)
Sec. 12-54. - Ordering temporary closing.
Temporary closings and/or suspensions of license for reasons of public safety may be ordered by the mayor, city recorder, or chief of police. Such order shall have a duration of not more than five (5) days and shall be issued for good cause only.
(Code 1977, § 6-28)
Secs. 12-55—12-70. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 12-71. - Required.
No billiard room shall be opened or operated except under a license granted by the city.
(Code 1977, § 6-35)
Sec. 12-72. - Status of license; subject to laws, etc.
All licenses granted under this division shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on such business during the term of the license and subject to all the terms and conditions imposed by the city charter, and related laws, applicable provisions of this Code, other ordinances, and state, county and federal laws and regulations.
(Code 1977, § 6-36)
Sec. 12-73. - Division subject to change.
(a)
This division may be modified in whole or in part, and at any time, and all persons seeking or holding licenses are hereby notified that they may be affected by changes to this division that might in the future occur.
(b)
All persons are expressly placed on notice that any license granted under this division shall be and is a mere grant or privilege to carry on such business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this article, and that this article is subject to modification and that they are subject to any modification thereof.
(Code 1977, § 6-37)
Sec. 12-74. - Application form, content.
All persons desiring to obtain a license under this division shall make a written application to the city for such privilege upon forms to be prepared and approved by the governing body. Such application shall state the name and address of the applicant, the proposed location of the business, the nature and character of the business to be carried on, proof of compliance with state license laws, proof of ownership of county license, and such other information as may be required by the governing body.
(Code 1977, § 6-38)
Sec. 12-75. - Investigation of application, report.
All applications required under this division shall be investigated by the police department. The police department shall file its report with the police committee.
(Code 1977, § 6-39)
Sec. 12-76. - Disqualifying circumstances—General.
No license shall be granted under this division where the application, investigation by the police department, or the evidence on a hearing shows any of the following conditions to exist:
(1)
That the applicant is of bad moral character or has a bad reputation in the community, or does not have sufficient mental capacity to conduct the business for which the application is made.
(2)
That the applicant has had a license for a similar establishment suspended or revoked within the five-year period next preceding the date of application unless such applicant can show that such suspension was lifted or unless such revocation was lifted and the license later restored.
(3)
That the applicant has been convicted of more than one (1) misdemeanor within the five-year period next preceding the date of application. Minor traffic violations shall not be considered in the determination of the aforementioned misdemeanors. A conviction of a misdemeanor shall include the adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty or the forfeiture of a bond of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) or more. Violations of city ordinances shall be considered misdemeanors for the purposes of this section.
(Code 1977, § 6-40; Ord. No. 92-04, § 2, 7-6-92)
Sec. 12-77. - Same—Felony conviction; dishonorable military discharge.
No license shall be issued under this division to any person who shall have been convicted of any felony, nor to any person who shall have received a dishonorable discharge from the armed forces of the United States.
(Code 1977, § 6-41)
Sec. 12-78. - Same—Denial for false information in application.
Any material omission, or any untrue statement contained in an original or renewal application for a license under this division shall furnish grounds for denial thereof or sufficient grounds for revocation thereof as the case may be.
(Code 1977, § 6-42)
Sec. 12-79. - Issuance of license limited to designated areas.
No license shall be issued to any person for the operation of a business except in properly zoned areas.
(Code 1977, § 6-43)
Sec. 12-80. - Fee—Setting annually.
The annual license fee for licenses issued under this division shall be set by the governing body during the month of December of each year. Such fee shall be applied during the following year. Should the governing body not set a rate for any year then the fee to be charged shall continue at the rate set for the preceding year.
(Code 1977, § 6-44)
Sec. 12-81. - Same—Payment.
All annual license fees under this division shall be paid on or before February 15th of each year. Any new license granted hereunder shall require the full fee.
(Code 1977, § 6-45; Ord. No. 92-04, § 3, 7-6-92)
Sec. 12-82. - Same—No refund in event of revocation.
In the event of revocation, no refund of any kind, or of any part of any license fee issued under this division shall be made.
(Code 1977, § 6-46)
Sec. 12-83. - Investigation, report and approval prerequisite to issuance.
All applications for a license under this division shall be investigated by the police department, shall be reported to the police committee, and favorably acted upon by the governing body before the city administrator shall be authorized to issue any license under this division.
(Code 1977, § 6-47)
Sec. 12-84. - Time for obtaining license after approval.
All licenses required by this division must be obtained and all fees paid within thirty (30) days of approval of application by the governing body.
(Code 1977, § 6-48)
Sec. 12-85. - Transfer of license.
No license granted under this division shall be transferred.
(Code 1977, § 6-49)
Sec. 12-86. - Annual renewal.
(a)
All businesses operating under this division shall be required to renew their licenses annually, by February 15th of each year, upon the forms prescribed by the governing body.
(b)
Prior to renewal, all persons holding licenses and requesting renewal thereof shall obtain the required county license and shall display such county license to the city administrator at the time the application for renewal is submitted.
(Code 1977, § 6-50; Ord. No. 92-04, § 4, 7-6-92)
Sec. 12-87. - Suspension and revocation—Authorized.
All licenses granted under this division are subject to revocation and/or suspension by either the governing body or by the municipal court.
(Code 1977, § 6-51)
Sec. 12-88. - Same—Conditions; grounds.
(a)
No license issued by the city under this division shall be suspended or revoked except for due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing and upon five (5) days' written notice to the holder of such license of the time, place and purpose of such hearing and a statement of the charge or charges upon which the hearing shall be held.
(b)
"Due cause" for the suspension or for the revocation of any license issued under this division shall consist of the violation of any law or ordinance regulating such business, or the violation of any state or federal law, or the violation of any city ordinance, other than traffic ordinances, or the falsification, by either material omission or untrue statement, of any application, or information in obtaining any license or the renewal thereof.
(c)
The hearing for determination of grounds for suspension or revocation shall be held by the municipal court with appeal therefrom to the governing body.
(Code 1977, § 6-52)
Sec. 12-89. - Same—Automatic revocation upon revocation of county license.
Whenever the county shall revoke any county license the comparable city license is thereby revoked. It shall be necessary to have all required county licenses in order to hold a valid city license.
(Code 1977, § 6-53)
Secs. 12-90—12-105. - Reserved.
