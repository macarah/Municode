ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Licensee means the person to whom the license to sell malt beverages and wine is granted.
Malt beverages means beer or ale made in whole or in part from malt.
Measured means distance measured by way of the nearest traveled road, street or highway from the front entrance of an outlet to the nearest property line of any involved school, church or other institution.
Outlet means any place where malt beverages or wines are offered for sale.
Outlet manager means that person who manages or operates the outlet selling malt beverages or wines.
Wines means and includes any wines or other beverages made by fermentation and in which the alcoholic content is less than 21 percent by volume.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 2, 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-2. - Privilege; revocation.
The sale of beer and wine is a mere privilege and not a right, and the board of commissioners of the county reserves the right to revoke the license of any wholesale or retail dealer at any time, with or without cause, with or without notice, and notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1.

ARTICLE II. - LICENSES AND IDENTIFICATION BADGES

Sec. 4-23. - Package sales of malt beverages and wines authorized.
The sale of malt beverages and foreign or domestic wines, wholesale or retail, by the package only, it hereby authorized.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 1, 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-24. - License required.
(a)
No outlet shall engage in the sale of malt beverages or the sale of foreign or domestic wines without first having been issued a license for that outlet by the chairperson of the board of commissioners.
(b)
No individual shall engage in the sale of malt beverages or the sale of foreign or domestic wines without first having registered with the county sheriff's department and displaying an identification badge issued by the sheriff or the county commissioners' office.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 3, 1-1-1999)
State Law reference— Dealing in malt beverages at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-40; county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-41; restriction on county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-43; dealing in wine at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-8-40.
Sec. 4-25. - Applications for licenses.
(a)
An applicant for license to sell malt beverages or wines shall file written application with the county clerk. The application shall contain such information as may be required by the board of commissioners in order to enforce the provisions of this article.
(b)
The applicant shall publish the application in a local newspaper once a week for four consecutive weeks to coincide with the sheriff's investigation as specified herein.
(c)
Before a license is issued, the application shall be referred by the clerk to the county sheriff. All applicants must be photographed, fingerprinted, and registered with the sheriff's department. The sheriff shall make an investigation and report back to the clerk within 30 days the following information:
(1)
Whether the person has ever been convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude or ever been convicted of violating beer, wine or liquor laws of any city, county, state or federal government.
(2)
Whether the person is of good character and reputation in the community in which he resides.
(3)
Whether the applicant has ever had an alcoholic beverage license revoked for cause.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 4(a), 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-26. - Applications for identification badges.
(a)
All individuals, whether owners, employers, servers, or other persons selling malt beverages and/or wines, shall apply at the county commissioners' office for an identification badge. The applicant shall fill out an application giving any information required by the commissioners' office and/or the county sheriff's office. The commissioners' office and/or the county sheriff's office shall make a check of:
(1)
Whether the person has ever been convicted of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude or whether, to his knowledge, the person applying for such license has ever been convicted of violating beer, wine, or liquor laws of any city, county, state, or federal government.
(2)
Whether the person is of good character and reputation in the community in which he resides.
(3)
Whether the applicant has ever had an alcoholic beverage license revoked for cause.
(b)
The applicant shall be photographed and sign a consent for the background check. A criminal background check on the applicant shall be performed. Within 30 days of the application, the applicant shall be issued an identification badge containing the name of the entity, the applicant's name, the applicant's photograph, the store license number, and the date of the license. Upon the applicant leaving employment, the badge shall be turned in to the county commissioners' office.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 4(b), 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-27. - Prohibitions to licensure.
No license for the sale of malt beverages or wines shall be issued to:
(1)
Any person who cannot provide at least five years of confirmed addresses where the applicant has resided.
(2)
Any person who is not the owner or a fulltime manager of the outlet applying for the license.
(3)
Any person who, at the time of his application, has not reached the age of 21 years.
(4)
A person who is not of good character and reputation in the community in which he resides.
(5)
A person who has been convicted of a felony or of any crime involving moral turpitude or to a person who has been convicted of violating any of the laws of any city, county, state or federal government pertaining to alcoholic beverages.
(6)
A person whose license or badge to sell alcoholic beverages has been revoked for cause.
(7)
Any law enforcement officer of any municipality or county of the state.
(8)
Any person who is not eligible for an alcoholic beverage license from the state.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 5, 1-1-1999)
State Law reference— Discretion and due process regarding grant or suspension of local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 4-28. - Renewal of licenses and badges.
All licenses and badges shall be issued on a calendar year basis and shall be renewable as a matter of course upon payment of the appropriate fee, except where the licensee, badge holder, outlet manager, agent or employee of the licensee has violated any of the terms of this Code, or any state or federal law involving moral turpitude or involving alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 8, 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-29. - Fees.
Fees for all applicable licenses shall be set out in a separate fee schedule adopted by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 8, 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-30. - Reconsideration of refusal to grant or renew license.
(a)
In the event the board of commissioners should refuse to grant any license or the renewal of a license, the applicant shall have the right to request the board of commissioners to advise in writing the reason for refusal.
(b)
The applicant shall further have the right, within ten days of receipt of the written reasons for refusal, to request a hearing for reconsideration before the board of commissioners and shall be entitled to present evidence in his behalf as to why the license should be issued. Evidence shall also be admissible to show why the license should not be issued.
(c)
After hearing, the board of commissioners shall make a finding as to whether the license or license renewal should be granted or denied and, if denied, shall state the reason for denial.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 6, 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-31. - Transfers of license.
(a)
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages issued hereunder shall be transferable, except upon the death of a licensee, when the license may be transferred to the administrator, executor, or lawful adult heir of the deceased person. If the legal representatives of the deceased licensee cannot meet all the requirements of this article when the time arrives to renew the license, it shall not be renewed.
(b)
Except as provided herein, any change in the ownership of any entity owning a licensed outlet shall cancel and revoke any license issued hereunder automatically, without the necessity of any hearing.
(c)
A new license application must be applied for whenever a partnership holding a license admits a new partner to the partnership, not a partner at the time of the original license application.
(d)
When a partnership holding a license loses the license as a result of the provisions of this section, upon filing an application for a new license, the partnership may continue to operate under the terms of the prior license until the new application is approved or denied by the board of commissioners; provided, however, that no continued operation shall be authorized until the new application for license is made and the required fees paid to the county.
Sec. 4-32. - Change in status.
When a change occurs:
(1)
In the relationship of any person;
(2)
In the status of any property or license;
(3)
In payment of rents, ownership of the lease, or building or land on which the outlet is located;
(4)
In ownership or management;
(5)
In any loss or damage to goods which results in a claim against an insurance policy;
(6)
In the division of profits;
(7)
In any division of net or gross sales for any purpose whatsoever; and
(8)
In facts stated or claimed in any application or report herein required;
a sworn statement of the change in all material facts relating thereto shall be filed with the county clerk and failure to do so within five days after the change shall, unless the time limit is extended for good cause, be reason for cancellation of a license granted pursuant to the provisions of this article.
Sec. 4-33. - Display of license; separate outlet licenses.
Each outlet licensed by the county hereunder shall display the license prominently at all times at the outlet for which the license is issued. A separate license must be issued for each outlet of sale and a separate application must be made for each outlet.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-3.
Sec. 4-34. - Suspension and revocation.
modified
(a)
Violation and seizure. The county sheriff may seize and retain the license or badge of a licensee, outlet manager, agent, or employee of the licensee or holder of an identification badge if:
(1)
At any time it should appear that the licensee, outlet manager, agent or employee of the licensee or the holder of an identification badge has violated this article or any other applicable provisions of this Code, or any state or federal law involving moral turpitude or pertaining to alcoholic beverages;
(2)
For any reason the outlet should become a public nuisance;
(3)
It should appear that the application contained known false information; or
(4)
It appears necessary for the public safety, health and welfare.
(b)
Request for hearing. Any person whose license or badge, or both, is seized by the sheriff shall have the right, within ten days from seizure, to file a written request to the board of commissioners requesting a hearing before the board and reinstatement of the license or badge.
(c)
Representation by counsel. At such hearing it shall be the burden of the county to introduce evidence showing the grounds upon which the license or badge was seized. The licensee or badge holder shall also have the right to be represented by counsel and to present evidence in his behalf.
(d)
Outcome of hearing. Following the hearing, the board of commissioners shall reinstate the badge or license, if the board deems it appropriate, or suspend the license or badge for 30 days for the first offense, suspend the license or badge for 60 days for the second offense, or permanently revoke the license or badge for the third violation. If the license or identification badge is suspended or revoked permanently, the finding shall include the reason therefor.
(e)
Suspension. During suspension or revocation of a license, there shall be no alcoholic beverages on the premises unless padlocked.
(f)
Fines and penalties. Any action by the board of commissioners as a result of violation of this article, including issuance of citations, assessment of fines and penalties, nuisance abatement, and license and badge suspensions or revocations shall not be construed as impeding or preventing state prosecution if the violation is also prohibited by and a violation of state law.
(Ord. No. 10-125, §§ 7, 8, 1-1-1999)

ARTICLE III. - REGULATIONS

Sec. 4-57. - Sanitary condition required for premises.
The licensee shall keep the premises clean, and in proper sanitary condition, and in full compliance with provisions and regulations governing the conditions of premises used for the storage and sale of food for human consumption and as provided by the rules and regulations of the county board of health.
Sec. 4-58. - Inspections.
Any officials that may be authorized by the board of commissioners including, but not by way of limitation, the county's sheriff and deputy sheriffs, other law enforcement officers, county code enforcement officers, and the county's fire chief or his designees shall have the authority to inspect the outlet and premises licensed under this article during the hours when the outlet is open for business. These inspections shall be made for the purpose of verifying compliance with the requirements of this article and state law. This section is not intended to limit the authority of any other city, county, state, or federal officials to conduct inspections authorized by other provisions of law.
Sec. 4-59. - Responsibility of employer.
The licensee is responsible for the conduct or actions of his employees while in his employment. It shall be the duty of the licensee hereunder to maintain a copy of this article at the outlet and to instruct each and every employee engaged in the sale or handling of alcoholic beverages of the terms hereof.
Sec. 4-60. - Prohibited sales and consumption.
(a)
No holder of a license or identification badge, authorizing the sale of malt beverages or wines in the county, nor any outlet manager, agent or employee of the licensee shall sell any malt beverage or wine except in the original container in which said malt beverage or wine was received from the distributor or sell, offer for sale, possess or license the consumption on the licensed premises of any liquor or other alcoholic beverage or drugs, the sale or possession of which is not authorized by law.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to consume any alcoholic beverage on the premises licensed for the sale of malt beverages or wine by the package and it shall be unlawful for any licensee by the package to open for, or break the package for, a purchaser or permit the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 6(a), (h), 1-1-1999)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26.
Sec. 4-61. - Conduct on licensed premises restricted.
No holder of a license or identification badge, authorizing the sale of malt beverages or wines in the county, nor any outlet manager, agent or employee of the licensee shall allow any disorderly conduct, gambling, or breach of the peace on the premises of the outlet.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 6(a), (g), 1-1-1999)
Sec. 4-62. - Time of sale restricted.
No holder of a license or identification badge, authorizing the sale of malt beverages or wines in the county, nor any outlet manager, agent or employee of the licensee shall sell or permit sale of any malt beverage or wine after 12:00 a.m., midnight or until 7:00 a.m. of the following day during the week and from 12:00 a.m., midnight on Saturday until 7:00 a.m. on the following Monday. No alcoholic beverage may be present on the premises unless padlocked during the hours this establishment is open for regular business.
(Ord. No. 10-125, § 6(a), (i), 1-1-1999)
State Law reference— Sunday sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale on Sunday and election days, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-63. - Location restrictions.
(a)
No business licensed hereunder shall be operated within 100 yards of any church or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus; and no distilled spirits, beer, or wine shall be sold or delivered, or offered for sale or delivery, within such limits.
(b)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold or delivered, or offered for sale or delivery within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center located in the unincorporated area of the county and owned or operated by the state, the county, or any municipal government.
(c)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(d)
Malt beverages and wine shall be sold exclusively in a building or storehouse that is licensed hereunder and separate and distinct from any residence or living quarters.
(e)
For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 4-64. - Sale to intoxicated persons.
No licensee shall permit on the licensed premises the sale, barter, exchange, giving, providing or furnishing alcoholic beverages to any person who is in a state of noticeable intoxication.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22.
Sec. 4-65. - Adulteration of product.
No licensee shall add to the contents of a bottle or refill an empty bottle or in any other manner misrepresent the quantity, quality, or brand name of any alcoholic beverage.
State Law reference— Adulteration or misbranding of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 51-1-24.

Sec. 4-89. - Sales prohibited.
Except as otherwise authorized by law, no licensee shall sell or permit to be sold alcoholic beverages to any person under the legal drinking age as prescribed by state law.
Sec. 4-90. - Presence on licenses premises restricted.
No licensee hereunder shall permit any person under the legal drinking age as prescribed by state law to be in, frequent, or loiter about the licensed premises unless this person is accompanied by a parent, legal guardian, or custodian; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to persons who are employees of the licensee.
Sec. 4-91. - Proof of age required.
All licensees shall require and properly check identification to ensure an underage person is not sold, served, or has in his possession alcoholic beverages while in a licensed establishment. The term "identification," in this section, means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, or the person's photograph, and giving the person's date of birth, and including, but without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or state department public safety identification card. Proper identification shall not include a birth certificate.

ARTICLE V. - PRIVATE CLUBS

Sec. 4-111. - Definitions; permitted activities.
modified
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Private club is defined as a business or social organization catering to and allowing access only to persons who have been granted membership in said business or organization in accordance with the rules established by said business or organization.
(b)
Permitted activities. It shall be legal for private clubs for there to be music, dancing and live entertainment.
(Ord. No. 10-300, §§ 1, 3, 12-14-1982)
Sec. 4-112. - Criminal background history required.
Before any private club shall operate in the county it shall obtain a business registration as required in chapter 10 of this Code. Before issuing any such business registration, the sheriff's department will, within 30 days of the date of the application for registration, investigate the background of all applicants and provide a report back to the chairperson of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 10-300, § 2, 12-14-1982)
Sec. 4-113. - Registration holder responsible for the club.
The person in whose name the business registration is issued will be responsible for the operation of the private club and shall be deemed the manager and operator of the registered private club.
(Ord. No. 10-300, § 2, 12-14-1982)
Sec. 4-114. - Authority and duty regarding alcoholic beverages.
(a)
Private clubs registered within the unincorporated areas of the county shall be bound by and abide by all county zoning ordinances and restrictions. They shall be governed by applicable state, federal and local laws, including, but not by way of limitation, regulations regarding the sale, dispensing, and delivery of any alcoholic beverages.
(b)
Private clubs shall be allowed to sell beer, malt beverages and wine for consumption on the premises, but no beer, malt beverages or wine shall be sold or in any manner dispensed or delivered between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. on Monday through Friday nights and between Saturday, 12:00 a.m., midnight and 7:00 a.m. on the following Monday.
(c)
The tables, counters, or other places from which beer, malt beverages and wine are usually served within the registered private club must be cleared of all glasses, bottles, cans or containers by 2:30 a.m. each Monday through Friday and by 12:00 a.m., midnight on Saturday night.
(Ord. No. 10-300, § 4, 12-14-1982)
Sec. 4-115. - Inspections.
Private clubs shall be subject to inspection by all county and state law enforcement officers. The county sheriff's department shall have access to the premises of the private club at any time and, in the event key cards are issued to the members of the private club, the sheriff and deputy sheriffs shall be provided current key cards as they may require to gain access to the private club for the purpose of inspecting the club, enforcing all applicable provisions of this Code, and enforcing all applicable state and federal laws.
(Ord. No. 10-300, § 5, 12-14-1982)
Sec. 4-116. - Telephone required.
All private clubs shall have on the premises a telephone available to its members. The number of the telephone, required by this section shall be provided to the county sheriff's department. The telephone shall be kept operational so long as the private club continues to operate.
(Ord. No. 10-300, § 6, 12-14-1982)

Sec. 4-146. - Taxes levied; payment.
(a)
No beer shall be sold in the county by any wholesaler or retailer unless and until a tax of $0.05 for each can or bottle containing 12 ounces, or proportionately thereof, so as to graduate the tax on bottles, cans, kegs and barrels or various sizes, has been paid.
(b)
No wine shall be sold in the county by any wholesaler or retailer unless and until a tax of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter has been paid.
(c)
Every wholesaler is charged with the duty of paying the excise taxes on beer and wine provided for in this section. The taxes shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of within the county by the wholesale dealer.
(d)
Each licensee shall file a report with the county clerk monthly itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact qualities of malt beverages, by size and type of container, sold during the month within the county.
