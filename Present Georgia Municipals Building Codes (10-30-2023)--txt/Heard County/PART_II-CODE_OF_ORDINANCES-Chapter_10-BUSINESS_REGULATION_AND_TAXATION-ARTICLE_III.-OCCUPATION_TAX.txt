DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-53. - Purpose; scope of tax; exemptions.
(a)
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling.
(b)
The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5 to 48-13-26. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general or local law and ordinance.
(c)
This article does not apply to businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations excluded from county occupational taxes by O.C.G.A. § 43-12-1 or by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-6.
(d)
This article does not apply to those real estate brokers, real estate agents or real estate companies whose offices are located outside the jurisdiction and who sell property inside the jurisdiction.
Sec. 10-54. - Option to establish exemption or reduction.
The board of commissioners may by subsequent ordinance or resolution amending this article provide for an exemption or reduction in occupation tax to one or more types of businesses or practitioners of occupations or professions as part of a plan for economic development or attracting or encouraging selected types of businesses or practitioners of selected occupations or professions. Such exemptions or reductions in occupation tax shall not be arbitrary or capricious, and the reasons shall be set forth in the minutes of the board of commissioners.
Secs. 10-55—10-81. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - IMPOSITION AND PAYMENT OF TAXES

Sec. 10-82. - Tax levied; registration required; administrative fees.
(a)
Each person engaged in any business, trade, profession, or occupation in the county, whether with a location in the county or, in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in the state, exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax as provided herein.
(b)
All persons subject to the occupation tax levied pursuant to this article shall be required to obtain the necessary business registration as described in this article.
(c)
In addition to the business registration fees and occupational taxes as provided in this article, all businesses subject to occupational taxes pursuant to this article shall pay annually, with their occupational taxes, an administrative fee in the amount established by resolution of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 30-100-01, §§ 1, 9, 15, 10-25-1994)
Sec. 10-83. - Due date; penalties.
Occupation taxes hereunder shall be payable January 1 of each year and shall, if not paid by February 1 of each year, be subject to penalty of $200.00, in additional to all other fees and penalties provided herein.
(Ord. No. 30-100-01, § 6, 10-25-1994)
Sec. 10-84. - Calculation of taxes.
(a)
An occupation tax shall be levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices in the unincorporated part of the county and upon the applicable out-of-state businesses with no location or office in the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, based upon the number of employees of the business or practitioner. The schedule of occupation taxes based on the number of employees shall be as provided by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Practitioners of professions as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18) shall elect as their entire occupation tax either the occupation tax based on number of employees, as provided in subsection (a) of this section, or a flat rate fee in the amount established by the board of commissioners.
(c)
For those business that have multiple locations inside and outside the county, only employees who work within the unincorporated limits of the county will be counted for taxing purposes.
(d)
No business or practitioner shall be required to pay more than one occupation tax for each of its locations.
(Ord. No. 30-100-01, §§ 4, 5, 7, 10-25-1994)
Sec. 10-85. - Inspection of books and records.
With regard to practitioners of professions as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18) that elect as their entire occupation tax a tax based on number of employees, the chairperson or his designees shall have authority to inspect the books and records of the practitioner at reasonable times and places.
(Ord. No. 30-100-01, § 12, 10-25-1994)
