ARTICLE II. - BICYCLE HELMETS

Sec. 20-19. - Established.
This article will be known as the "City of Hiawassee Bicycle Helmet Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 0060, § I, 11-1-1997)
Sec. 20-20. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bicycle helmet means a piece of protective headgear which meets or exceeds the impact standards for bicycle helmets set by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI), the Snell Memorial Foundation or the federal government. For the purpose of this article, a person shall be deemed to be wearing a bicycle helmet only if a helmet of good fit is fastened securely upon the head with the straps of the helmet.
(Ord. No. 0060, § II, 11-1-1997)
Sec. 20-21. - Purpose.
(a)
Safety. To ensure that the use of a bicycle without a helmet does not create a safety hazard by subjecting members of the public under the age of 12 to risk of injury from the negligent operation of bicycles.
(b)
Welfare. To ensure that the improper operation of bicycles does not subject members of the public under the age of 12 to serious injury from accidents whether the rider is at fault or injured through the negligence of others.
(Ord. No. 0060, § III, 11-1-1997)
Sec. 20-22. - Prohibited acts.
Except as may be specifically permitted herein, it shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 12 to ride a bicycle on a highway, bicycle path, or sidewalk within the city limits without wearing a bicycle helmet.
(Ord. No. 0060, § IV, 11-1-1997)
Sec. 20-23. - Violations; penalty.
A person under the age of 12 failing to comply with this article shall have their bicycle impounded by the police. The parent or the caregiver of the offender may then retrieve the bicycle by going to the police department and showing proof that the person now has a bicycle helmet or by indicating that the person shall not be riding the bicycle further. Proof of ownership of a helmet may be a receipt documenting purchase or the helmet itself.
(Ord. No. 0060, § V, 11-1-1997)
