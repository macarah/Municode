Chapter 22 - MUNICIPAL COURT

Sec. 22-1. - Municipal court.
Municipal court shall try and punish violations of the city Charter and ordinances, and other provisions as provided by law. It shall be presided over by a chief judge, or any part-time, full-time or standby judges as the city council shall provide, from time to time, by ordinance.
Sec. 22-2. - Qualifications of judge.
In addition to the qualification and eligibility requirements of Charter section 4.11, any municipal court judge shall be licensed to practice law in the state and an active member of the State Bar of Georgia.
Sec. 22-3. - Clerk of court.
The city council shall appoint a clerk of court to assist the municipal judge in carrying out the functions and duties of the court. The council shall determine the term of office and compensation of the clerk of court. The clerk shall be subject to the direct supervision of the municipal judge during court proceedings, and subject to removal at the discretion of the city council.
Sec. 22-4. - Date and place of convening.
The municipal court shall convene the second Monday of each month at city hall between the hours of 11:30 a.m. and 1:00 p.m. The time and place of the convening of the municipal court may be changed, from time to time, by action of the city council.
Sec. 22-5. - Fee and fine schedule.
A fee and fine schedule may be adopted from time to time by action of the city council. Any such schedule adopted shall be on file in the office of the clerk of court and available for public review during normal business hours.
