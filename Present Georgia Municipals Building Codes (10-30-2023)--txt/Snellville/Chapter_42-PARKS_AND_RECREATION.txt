Sec. 42-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Recreation director means the director of the parks and recreation department appointed by the city manager.
Recreation facility means all city property designated for use as recreational areas, including, but not limited to, athletic fields, fitness or nature trails, parks (including passive parks), swimming pools, nature facilities, senior centers and any facilities incidental thereto, including pavilions, buildings and parking lots.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 42-2. - Violation of rules, regulations.
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate the provisions of this chapter and any other rules or regulations relating to the use of a recreation facility as established by the recreation director where such rules and regulations have been approved and adopted by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-3. - Enforcement.
(a)
The provisions of this chapter regulating the use of recreation facilities in the city shall be enforced by the recreation director, the recreation director's authorized representative(s) and any authorized law enforcement officer of the city, Gwinnett County, the federal government or the State of Georgia, and it shall be their duty and responsibilities to enforce such provisions. Where there has been a violation of any provision of this chapter, the law enforcement officer, in his discretion, may issue a verbal warning to or issue a citation to the person violating such provision rather than arresting the violator. Any such law enforcement officer, the recreation director or the recreation director's duly appointed representative shall also have the authority to eject from any recreation facility any person or persons acting in violation of this chapter.
(b)
Penalties. Violation of any provision of this chapter may be punishable by any penalty or penalties authorized by section 1-11 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-4. - Use generally.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use or attempt to use any part of a recreation facility, except during the hours and in accordance with the rules and regulations established by the recreation director for the time and method of use.
(b)
The established rules and regulations shall be posted and/or included in all rental and special event agreements.
(c)
Failure to observe posted rules and regulations shall be deemed to be a violation of this chapter.
(d)
A person using recreation facilities by reservation or agreement may be required to protect and save the city, its elected and appointed officials and employees while acting within the scope of their duties as such, harmless from and against all claims, demands, and causes of action of any kind or character, including the cost of defense thereof, arising in favor of the persons, employees, or third parties due to personal injuries, death, or damage to property arising out of the premises, or in any way arising out of the acts or omissions of the person and/or their agents, employees, or representatives. Users of any and all recreation facilities or areas do so at their own risk. The city assumes no liability or responsibility due to accidents or injury through authorized or unauthorized use of recreation facilities.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-5. - Litter control.
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw or deposit litter in or on any part of a recreation facility, except within public garbage receptacles and in such a manner that the litter will be prevented from being carried or deposited by the elements upon any part of the recreation facility. Where public garbage receptacles are not provided, all litter shall be carried away from the recreation facility by the person responsible for the presence of the litter, and such litter shall be properly disposed of elsewhere. It shall be unlawful to take into, carry through, or put into any recreation facility, any litter generated outside the recreation facility.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Cross reference— Solid wastes, ch. 46.
Sec. 42-6. - Weapons.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use or discharge any firearm weapon or similar device in a recreation facility.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to take on a recreational facility any firearm or weapon, or to use, carry or employ any firearm, weapon or similar device in a recreational facility, unless authorized to do so by the laws of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Cross reference— Prohibited weapons, § 38-3.
Sec. 42-7. - Special prohibitions.
(a)
Paint. It shall be unlawful for any unauthorized person to possess paint, in any form, in or on any park or other area owned or operated by the city for recreation purposes.
(b)
Glass containers. No person shall possess a glass container in or on any recreation facility except for inside a building or at a pavilion for the purpose of storing prepared food.
(c)
Bicycles on athletic fields. No person shall ride nor walk a bicycle or other non-motorized vehicle in or on any athletic field owned or operated by the city for recreation purposes.
(d)
Throwing objects. It shall be unlawful for any person to throw any stone, missile or object at any person or at any building or recreation facility while at a recreation facility if such act results in the physical injury to a person or physical damage to any building or recreation facility. This section shall not apply to any act done in the pursuit of any authorized or sanctioned recreational activity conducted with the approval of the department of parks and recreation.
(e)
Smoking. It shall be unlawful to smoke or use any tobacco products within 100 feet of (a) any recreational activity sanctioned or carried out by the city, (b) any playground, (c) any walking trail, (d) any athletic field or (d) any other area where a "No Smoking" sign is posted.
(f)
Fireworks. It shall be unlawful to possess or cause the combustion, explosion, deflagration, or detonation of any pyrotechnics within any recreational facility except in compliance with applicable state law and with the express written approval of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-8. - Disturbing conduct.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in any violent, abusive, loud, boisterous, vulgar, wanton, obscene or otherwise disorderly conduct, disturb or annoy others, or engage in any activity that could cause injury to other persons while on or in connection with a recreation facility.
(b)
No person shall upon or in connection with a recreation facility by act or speech willfully or unreasonably hinder, interrupt or interfere with any duly permitted activity or unreasonably or willfully intrude on any areas or into the structures designated for the use of a certain person or persons to the exclusion of others by written permit of the director.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to whistle or yell at another person in a boisterous, unbecoming or erring manner or make any loud, unnecessary or unusual noise or any noise or display, play or operate any sound amplification device including radios, television sets, public address systems, musical instruments, CD players and the like in such a way which either annoys, disturbs, injures or endangers the comfort, repose, peace or safety of other persons in a recreation facility.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deface, graffiti, harm or damage any park buildings, wildlife, property, equipment or signs.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-9. - Profanity.
It shall be unlawful for any person to use profanity or engage in any profane behavior while in a recreation facility.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-10. - Fire control.
It shall be unlawful for a person to build or maintain a fire except in areas designated for such purposes by clearly marked signs or defined with grills placed by parks and recreation employees. The use of propane tanks is strictly prohibited unless included in a written agreement between a contracted vendor and the parks and recreation department.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-11. - Protection of vegetation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cut trees, tree limbs, shrubbery, flowers or other vegetation in a recreation facility without the written approval of the director of parks and recreation.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Cross reference— Unified development ordinance, app. A
Sec. 42-12. - Illegal drugs, alcoholic beverages.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess, use, or consume any illegal drug in a recreation facility or on park property.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess, use, or consume any alcoholic beverage in a recreation facility or on park property except as permitted under section 6-196 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Snellville, Georgia.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to be under the influence of any alcoholic beverage or illegal drug in a recreation facility or on park property.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009; Ord. No. 2022-01, § 1, 2-14-2022)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 6.
Sec. 42-13. - Use of vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive any vehicle in a recreation facility, except upon roadways and paths constructed, designated and maintained for vehicular traffic. Motorized vehicles that are not registered and licensed by a governmental agency, or that may not lawfully be operated upon public roads are prohibited. Emergency personnel and recreation facility employees whose duties require them to drive maintenance vehicles and equipment shall be exempt from the limitation set forth in this section. The physically disabled that utilize a motorized device as a primary form of transport shall be exempt from this provision.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 58.
Sec. 42-14. - Parking of vehicles.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle in a recreation facility, except in those areas designated by appropriate signs as vehicle parking areas. Emergency personnel and recreation facility employees whose duties require them to drive maintenance vehicles and equipment shall be exempt from the limitation set forth in this subsection.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle in a designated parking area in a recreation facility if the owner and/or driver of the vehicle is not utilizing the recreation facility.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 58.
Sec. 42-15. - Commercial activities, solicitation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale any merchandise, solicit charitable contributions, advertise or solicit business, place upon any car or windshield any flyers or stickers, operate a concession or engage in any commercial activity in a recreation facility or on park property without the written authorization of the director of parks and recreation or the director's designee.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-16. - Closure of premises.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, other than authorized recreation facility personnel, to enter or be within a recreation facility when the facility is closed.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, other than authorized recreation facility personnel, to drive a vehicle in or on a recreation facility, attempt to park or park a vehicle on any of the streets, avenues or parking areas, or at any location within a recreation facility when the facility is closed.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-17. - Control of swimming areas.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to refuse to leave a recreation facility swimming area or to refuse to cease any activity that would be hazardous to others when directed to do so by a lifeguard. For purposes of this section, ignoring the directions of a lifeguard to leave or cease a hazardous activity will amount to a refusal.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in any activity within a recreation facility swimming pool that could cause injury or damage to such person or to other persons using such recreation facility.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any posted rules for any swimming area.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-18. - Recreational lighting.
No outdoor recreational facility, public or private, shall be illuminated from 10:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m., except to conclude a specific activity which is in progress under such illumination prior to 9:30 p.m.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-19. - Use of model and toy vehicles on city property.
It shall be unlawful for any person to start, fly or use any fuel-powered engine, jet-type, or electric powered model aircraft, boat, rocket or similar powered toy or model on city property except in such areas as designated by the director of parks and recreation for use of such models or toys, and in accordance with such rules, regulations and restrictions as promulgated by the director or the director's designee.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-20. - Animals/pets.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to have or allow an animal or pet on park property without written permission from the director of parks and recreation or the director's designee, unless the animal is confined or controlled on a leash or harness per the Gwinnett County Animal Control Ordinance, unless in designated dog park areas where off leash is permitted or approved.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person who possesses, harbors or is in charge of any animal not to immediately remove excrement deposited by the animal while at a recreation facility. All persons possessing any animal or pet shall be responsible for insuring that any animal excrement is removed and disposed of in a sanitary manner. It shall be the duty of any person having custody or control of any animal in the public park to have in such person's possession a device or equipment for the picking up and removal of animal excrement.
(c)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to an animal aiding the handicapped (e.g., guide dog) or to an animal when in police or rescue activities.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-21. - Camping.
Camping on park property is prohibited except in the case of a City of Snellville special event where a vendor must remain in the park overnight to monitor personal property. Overnight vendors are required to display a pass distributed by the director of parks and recreation or the director's designee.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
Sec. 42-22. - Usage of lake.
(a)
All lake fishing requirements are based on Georgia Department of Natural Resources Fishing Regulations, O.C.G.A. §§ 27-2-1, 27-4-5, 27-4-8, 27-4-10.
(b)
Fishing is restricted to a catch-and-release method from January 1 until the end of the annual Children's Fishing Derby, but in no event later than June 30 each year.
(c)
Swimming, wading or bathing is prohibited for park patrons and pets.
(d)
Feeding of any and all waterfowl is prohibited for the maintenance of lake water quality.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to launch or travel in any boat or watercraft.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 10-12-2009)
