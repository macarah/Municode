Chapter 10 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine, or fortified wine.
Beer means malt beverage as defined herein.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Retailer or retail dealer means, except as to distilled spirits, any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale. With respect to distilled spirits, the term means any person who sells distilled spirits in unbroken packages at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this Code section.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. 3-1-2.

ARTICLE II. - BEER AND WINE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-19. - Hours of operation.
(a)
All establishments licensed to sell alcoholic beverages in the county must close by 2:00 a.m. Tuesday through Friday.
(b)
No beer or wine shall be sold after midnight on Saturday nights. No open containers are to be allowed on the business premises past 12:30 a.m. Sunday morning, and the premises must be vacated by 1:00 a.m. Sunday morning for the duration of the day.
(c)
Any public or private premises leased or loaned to an individual, group of individuals, or organization for the purpose of a private or public gathering and upon which premises alcoholic beverages are consumed shall be subject to the same time constraints as the retail dealers of beer and wine.
(d)
Violation of this provision is prima facie evidence that a nuisance exists, and the sheriff of the county shall use any means necessary to abate the nuisance.
(Res. of 3-4-1960; Res. of 2-9-1993)
Sec. 10-20. - Compliance with state law, regulations; restrictions on delivery.
(a)
No beer or wine shall be sold in the county during any year by any retailer or wholesaler unless the licensee is and remains in compliance with all state laws and rules.
(b)
No wholesaler shall sell or deliver any beer or wine to any retailer who is not complying with these regulations.
(Res. of 3-4-1960)
Sec. 10-21. - Location restrictions.
(a)
No business licensed hereunder shall be operated within 100 yards of any church or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus, and no distilled spirits, beer, or wine shall be sold or delivered, or offered for sale or delivery, within such limits.
(b)
No distilled spirits, wine, or malt beverages shall be sold or delivered, or offered for sale or delivery within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center located in the unincorporated area of the county and owned or operated by the state, the county, or any municipal government.
(c)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(d)
Beer and wine shall be sold exclusively in a building or storehouse which is separate and distinct from any residence or living quarters; and no place of business shall hereafter be licensed which is not separate and distinct from a residence of living quarters.
(e)
For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(Res. of 3-4-1960)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 10-22. - Posting of regulations.
Every retail dealer shall keep posted in his place of business, in a conspicuous place, a copy of these regulations.
(Res. of 3-4-1960)
Sec. 10-23. - Bond; forfeiture.
(a)
No wholesale or retail dealer shall be granted a license to sell beer and/or wine until such dealer shall have posted with the administrator of the county board of commissioners, in addition to the license fee, a cash bond, to be conditioned for the faithful compliance with these regulations, in an amount established from time to time by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Upon violation of any law, or any of these regulations, or the revocation of the dealer's license for such cause, said bond shall be automatically forfeited to the county.
(Res. of 3-4-1960)
Sec. 10-24. - Taxes.
(a)
No beer shall be sold in the county by any wholesaler or retailer unless and until a tax of $0.05 for each can or bottle containing 12 ounces, or proportionately thereof, so as to graduate the tax on bottles, cans, kegs and barrels or various sizes, has been paid.
(b)
No wine shall be sold in the county by any wholesaler or retailer unless and until a tax of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter has been paid.
(c)
Every wholesaler is charged with the duty of paying the excise taxes on beer and wine provided for in this section. The taxes shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of within the county by the wholesale dealer.
(Res. of 3-4-1960; Res. of 2-19-1973; Amd. of 11-11-2002)
State Law reference— Local excise taxes on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80; excise taxes on malt beverages to be paid by wholesale dealer, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-81; prohibition on requirement for markings on beverage containers regarding excise taxes, O.C.G.A. § 35-5-82; local excise taxes on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60.

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 10-41. - Privilege; revocation.
The sale or beer and wine is a mere privilege and not a right, and the board of commissioners of the county, reserves the right to revoke the license of any wholesale or retail dealer at any time, with or without cause, with or without notice, notwithstanding any provision in the foregoing regulations.
(Res. of 3-4-1960)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1.
Sec. 10-42. - Required.
(a)
Every wholesale and retail dealer in beer or wine doing any business in the county shall obtain a license to carry on such business in said county and shall pay the annual license fee on or before January 1 of each year in advance. The amount of the license fee shall be as determined from time to time by the county board of commissioners.
(b)
No beer or wine shall be sold in any club, public or private, except under these regulations, which shall apply to all clubs and the same as to private businesses.
(Res. of 3-4-1960; Res. of 11-11-1988)
State Law reference— Dealing in malt beverages at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-40; county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-41; restriction on county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-43; dealing in wine at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-40;
Sec. 10-43. - Application.
(a)
Any new applicant requesting a license for beer and wine shall submit a request to the office of the county board of commissioners in writing. Such request will be placed on the agenda of the next commissioner's regular meeting session, at which the commissioners will determine, by majority vote, whether applicant may proceed with the application process.
(b)
If by majority vote the commissioners allow the applicant to proceed further with the application process, a notice will be placed in the legal organ showing the name and address of the applicant, the location for which the license is sought, the type of license applied for and the time and place that the commission will act on the application.
(c)
All applications shall be approved or disapproved by the county sheriff or a designee thereof as a condition to action thereon and issuance of a license by the commissioner's office. If the sheriff determines that the application is not in order, in view of the requirements of federal or state law or this Code and any sheriff's department investigation, the commissioner's office shall then notify the applicant of the reasons for not issuing the license to the applicant.
(Res. of 11-11-1988, §§ 2, 3, 5)
Sec. 10-44. - Qualifications.
(a)
All applicants for licenses under this article shall be bona fide residents of the county or shall designate a bona fide resident of the county who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license. Applicants may be owners or managers of the business. Corporations may apply for the license to be issued in their corporate names.
(b)
No license shall be issued, renewed or transferred to any person where any individual having an interest either as owner, partner, principal stockholder, directly or indirectly, beneficial or absolute, shall, in any state or federal court, have been convicted:
(1)
Within ten years immediately prior to the filing of the application for any felony involving moral turpitude or otherwise reasonably relating to the conduct of the business for which the license is issued; or
(2)
Within two years immediately prior to the filing of the application of any two or more misdemeanors, other than traffic offenses, involving moral turpitude or otherwise reasonably relating to the conduct of the business for which the license is issued.
(c)
The term "conviction," as used in this section, shall include an adjudication of guilty or a plea of guilt or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond in part or in whole when charged with a crime.
(Res. of 11-11-1988, §§ 4, 6)
State Law reference— Discretion and due process regarding grant or suspension of local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2
Sec. 10-45. - Fees.
All approved applications for new beer and wine licenses shall be accompanied by payment in cash or by certified check for all applicable license fees and taxes. A schedule of license fees is on file in the county commissioner's office and will be made available to all applicants upon request.
(Res. of 3-4-1960; Res. of 11-11-1988, § 1)
Sec. 10-46. - Revocation.
(a)
Upon sufficient evidence produced to satisfy the board that any wholesaler or retailer has violated any of the provisions of these regulations, the license of any wholesaler or retailer shall be immediately revoked; and the bond of the wholesaler or retailer shall be forfeited, either with or without notice to the holder of such license.
(b)
Any license issued under this article or past articles may be suspended for a definite time or may be revoked if the licensee or any employee of licensee has been convicted of the violation of any act prohibited by this article, of failure to perform any act required by this article, or is convicted of the violation of any other ordinance of the county or any state or federal law relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages. The forfeiture of bond or collateral or a plea of nolo contendere shall be considered a conviction for the purposes of this subsection.
(Res. of 3-4-1960; Res. of 11-11-1988, § 7)
