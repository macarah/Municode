Chapter 6 - ADULT ENTERTAINMENT

Sec. 6-1. - Prohibited entertainment, attire and conduct.
(a)
Within the unincorporated area of the county, the following types of entertainment, attire, and conduct are prohibited upon any premises open to the public or licensed to sell, serve, or dispense alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
(b)
The employment or use of any person, in any capacity, in the sale or service of any food or beverage including, but not limited to, alcoholic beverages while such person in unclothed or in such attire, costume or clothing as to expose to view any portion of the female breast below the top of areola or of any portion of the male or female pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva, or genitals.
(c)
Live entertainment where any person appears in the manner described in subsection (b) of this section or where such person performs acts of or acts that simulate any of the following:
(1)
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation, or any sexual act that is prohibited by law;
(2)
The touching, caressing, or fondling of the breasts, buttocks, anus, or genitals; or
(3)
The displaying of any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola or the displaying of any portion of any person's pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva, or genitals.
(d)
The holding, promotion, sponsoring or allowance of any contest, promotion, special night, event or any other activity where patrons of the establishment are encouraged or allowed to engage in any of the conduct described in subsection (b) or (c) of this section.
(Ord. of 7-12-1999, § 1.2)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-41.
Sec. 6-2. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Any person violating the provisions of this chapter shall be subject to penalties as set forth in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. of 2-16-1993, § 3; Ord. of 7-12-1999, § 1.1)
