ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - County ordinance adopted.
(a)
The hereby adopts the animal control ordinance of Gwinnett County in effect on the date of this article, and any subsequent revisions thereto, exclusive of section 10-10, livestock. A copy of the county ordinance is attached hereto and incorporated by reference.
(b)
The Gwinnett County Animal Control Unit is authorized to enforce this section and all fees charged by the Gwinnett County Animal Control Unit related to such enforcement activities within the City of Berkeley Lake shall be retained by the county.
(Ord. No. O-188-15, § 1, 9-17-2015; Ord. No. O-23-247, § I(a), 6-15-2023)
Sec. 10-2. - Wildlife.
(a)
Definition. Wildlife means any vertebrate animal life, other than domestic species, indigenous to this state or introduced, and includes mammals, birds, fish, reptiles and amphibians, crustaceans and mollusks, the taking and possession of which is controlled by state law; for example alligator, bat, bear, beaver, bobcat, coyote, deer, fox, opossum, raccoon, turtle, squirrel, tortoise, waterfowl, game and non-game birds and raptors such as eagle, hawk, kite, and vulture.
(b)
Feeding. It shall be unlawful for any person to provide feed in any manner to wildlife on public or private property within the corporate limits of Berkeley Lake.
(c)
Exceptions. The following are exceptions to subsection (b):
(1)
Feeding songbirds and other backyard birds, other than waterfowl and raptors, utilizing a feeder that can be accessed only by songbirds, other backyard birds and squirrels. Feeders that are accessed by other wildlife are in violation;
(2)
Feeding of fish or the lawful taking of fish;
(3)
Feeding of wildlife confined in properly licensed or permitted zoos, parks, rehabilitation facilities, or environmental education centers;
(4)
The providing of feed in the lawful exercise of trapping by any person properly permitted by the Georgia Department of Natural Resources to conduct nuisance wildlife abatement or by properly licensed trappers;
(5)
The providing of feed in the lawful exercise of falconry by any person properly permitted by the Georgia Department of Natural Resources.
(d)
Enforcement. This section shall be enforced by the ordinance enforcement officer. Citizen complaints shall be directed in writing.
(e)
Penalties. For the first violation of the provisions of this section, the ordinance enforcement officer shall issue a written warning to the violator ordering him or her to cease the feeding activity. For a second and each subsequent violation of the provisions of this section, the violator shall be issued a citation to appear in municipal court and shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00. Each day a violation occurs shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. O-188-15, § 1, 9-17-2015; Ord. No. O-19-228, § 1, 9-19-2019)
Sec. 10-3. - Exotic pet animals.
(a)
Definition. Exotic pet means an animal not a domestic dog or cat, not within the scope of the terms "livestock" or "wildlife" as defined in this article, which is bred and held in captivity for pleasure and which is allowed to be held as a pet by state law without the requirement for a permit or license issued by the Department of Natural Resources; for example: fish; non-venomous snakes, lizards and other reptiles; turtles; birds such as parrot, cockatiel, parakeet, canary, finch, and budgerigar; normally domesticated rodents such as mouse, rat, hamster, gerbil and guinea pig; ferrets; skunks; hedgehogs; hermit crabs; sugar gliders, normally domesticated rabbits; pot-bellied pigs; etc.
(b)
Housing. Except for large fish such as koi kept in a self-contained outdoor pond, exotic pets shall be permanently domiciled within the residence of the owner. Exotic pets shall not be kept in outdoor enclosures, aviaries or accessory structures except for periods of exercise, training or recreation during daylight hours.
(c)
Enforcement. This section shall be enforced by the ordinance enforcement officer. Citizen complaints shall be directed in writing.
(d)
Penalties. For the first violation of the provisions of this section, the ordinance enforcement officer shall issue a written warning to the violator ordering him or her to domicile the animal within their residence. For a second and each subsequent violation of the provisions of this section, the violator shall be issued a citation to appear in municipal court and shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00. Each day a violation occurs shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. O-188-15, § 1, 9-17-2015)
Sec. 10-4. - Livestock.
(a)
Definition. Livestock means and includes horses, cows, goats, pigs, or any other hoofed animal used for pleasure or profit. Fowl and rabbits are expressly included in this definition.
(b)
Prohibition. Livestock raising or keeping shall not be permitted within the City of Berkeley Lake.
(c)
Enforcement. This section shall be enforced by the ordinance enforcement officer. Citizen complaints shall be directed in writing.
(d)
Penalties. For the first violation of this section, the ordinance enforcement officer shall issue a written warning to the violator ordering him or her to re-home the livestock. For a second and each subsequent violation of the provisions of this section, the violator shall be issued a citation to appear in municipal court and shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00. Each day a violation occurs shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. O-23-247, § I(b), 6-15-2023)
