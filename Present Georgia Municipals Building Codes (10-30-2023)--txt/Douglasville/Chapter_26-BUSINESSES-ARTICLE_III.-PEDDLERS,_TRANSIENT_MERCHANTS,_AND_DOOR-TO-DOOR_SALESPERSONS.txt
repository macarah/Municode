Sec. 26-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable organization means any benevolent, philanthropic, patriotic, or eleemosynary (of, relating to, or supported by charity or alms) corporation, partnership, limited liability company, association, joint-stock company, trust, unincorporated organization or individual who solicits or obtains contributions solicited from the general public, any part of which contributions is used for charitable purposes.
Charitable purpose means any charitable, benevolent, philanthropic, patriotic, or eleemosynary purpose for religion, health, education, social welfare, arts and humanities, environment, civic or public interest.
Door-to-door salesperson means any person who solicits orders on behalf of a firm, corporation, company, association, partnership or individual of any goods, wares or merchandise, or other things of value by traveling from house to house.
Handicapped means any physical, mental, or neurological impairment which severely restricts a person's mobility, manual dexterity, or ability to climb stairs; substantial loss of sight or hearing; loss of one or more limbs or use thereof; or significantly diminished reasoning capacity.
Peddler means any person who sells or solicits for sale any new or used goods, wares, merchandise, produce, service or other things of value, who does not possess a certificate of occupancy, selling or offering for sale any of such things to either merchants or customers.
Transient merchant means any person, firm or corporation, as principal or agent, or both, which is not a regular retail or wholesale merchant with a permanent place of business in this state, but rather one who displays samples, goods, wares, or merchandise in any lot, building, room, structure or vehicle, for the purpose of securing orders for the sale of such item or items of like kind or quality for future or immediate delivery, or one who sells food, drink, produce or like products from a temporary site or structure.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-112. - Exclusions.
The terms "transient merchant" and "peddler" and "door-to-door salesperson" shall not include any person, firm or corporation which:
(1)
Sells directly to consumers only such produce or other direct farm products, as the seller has produced himself.
(2)
Solicits sales for charitable organizations, charitable purposes, or on behalf of religious organizations, political organizations or political candidates.
(3)
Conducts business at any industry or association trade show, or at any craft show or festival of not more than three days in duration.
(4)
Sells items at a rummage, garage, flea market, antique or similar sale where such person, firm or corporation is a resident of this state or has a permanent place of business within this city and such sale is not part of the regular business activity of such person, firm or corporation.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-113. - Registration required; duration of registration.
Each peddler, transient merchant and door-to-door sales person who does business within this city shall be required to obtain a registration from the finance director, in the manner specified in this article. Every such registration shall be valid for a continuous period of 15 days.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-114. - Requirements for corporations; registered agents.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any peddler or transient merchant or door-to-door salesperson which is a corporation to engage in business within the city unless such corporation shall comply with all applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. section 14-2-101 et. seq., relating to corporations and obtain a registration.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any peddler or transient merchant or door-to-door salesperson which is not a corporation to engage in business within the city unless such person shall have and maintain a registered agent or agents as required by section 26-116 and shall obtain a registration.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-115. - Applications.
(a)
All peddlers and transient merchants and door-to-door salespersons shall file an application for a registration with the office of the finance director. Such application shall include but not be limited to, the following:
(1)
The name and permanent address of the individual peddler or transient merchant or door-to-door salesperson, and if applicable the name and address of the corporate peddler's or transient merchant's or door-to-door salesperson's registered agent or office.
(2)
The type of business to be conducted.
(3)
The date and place of incorporation, when such peddler or transient merchant is a corporation.
(4)
The address, location or general area where business is to be conducted.
(5)
An administrative fee of $65.00 per registered individual, in addition to the base administrative fee required by section 26-3.
(b)
At the time the registration application is filed, the applicant shall pay all regulatory fees due under section 26-3. Registrations under this division are not transferable.
(c)
As a part of the registration application for transient merchants, each applicant designating any one or more stationary addresses or locations where business is to be conducted shall, prior to filing of such application with the office of the finance director, submit said application to the building official for approval. The building official shall approve applications designating addresses and locations for business where traffic will not be unduly impeded, and where any stationary non-motorized structures used to store inventory, samples, or other business materials is sufficiently stable to withstand inclement weather.
(d)
As a part of the registration application, each transient merchant applicant designating any one or more stationary addresses or locations owned by another where business is to be conducted, shall submit with such application a written statement showing the consent of the property owner (and lessee, if any) for the applicant to transact business thereon.
(e)
As a part of the registration application, each individual applicant and each representative for a corporate applicant shall submit to a criminal background check by the police department, and obtain a photo identification card bearing a current photograph of the applicant.
(f)
An individual applicant who is 65 years old or older, or one who is handicapped, shall be exempt from all regulatory fees, but shall still be required to make application for a registration with the office of the finance director and obtain and pay the administrative fee for a photo identification card from the police department. Any handicapped applicant will be required to provide proof of handicap either by providing state handicap identification or a letter of handicap from a licensed physician. Any applicant, who is 65 years old or older, shall provide proof of age either by birth certificate or state identification.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-116. - Denial of applications; revocation of registration.
(a)
Applications for registration for individual peddlers and transient merchants and door-to-door salespersons shall be denied by the police department for:
(1)
Any person who has been convicted within the past seven years of any felony offense; or
(2)
Any person who has been convicted within the past seven years of any misdemeanor offense involving theft, fraud or physical violence; or
(3)
Any person who has, within the past seven years, had revoked his business license or any license issued by a local government.
(b)
Denial of registration may be appealed by the applicant to the mayor and council, by filing of a written appeal with the city clerk not later than seven days after dispatch of written notice to the applicant. On appeal, the mayor and council may correct any errors of law or fact, upon appeal, but may not grant a registration to any individual whose background is in violation of the requirements of this section.
(c)
Registrations may be revoked otherwise sanctioned in the same manner and upon the same basis as for the revocation of business licenses and occupation tax registrations.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-117. - Display of photo identification.
Photo identification cards shall be displayed at all times while the applicant or representative conducts business in the city, and each shall exhibit such registration at the request of any person.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-118. - Loud noise and amplification devices prohibited.
No registration holder under this article shall shout, ring bells, blow horns, make loud noises or use any radio or amplifying system upon any of the streets, alleys, parks or other public places within the city for the purpose of attracting attention to the goods, wares or merchandise which such registrant intends to sell.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-119. - Reserved.
Sec. 26-120. - Additional regulations for transient merchants.
(a)
Transient merchants shall conduct business only in locations zoned general commercial and in accordance with Appendix A—Zoning of the City Code.
(b)
All vehicles must be removed from the transient merchant's location at the end of each workday. Only one vehicle, other than any vehicle of a customer present at the sales location, is allowed for each location during business hours.
(c)
No transient merchant shall transact business between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-121. - Additional regulations for peddlers.
(a)
Peddlers shall park the vehicle associated with the business on asphalt or concrete and out of the right-of-way.
(b)
No tents, tables, boxes, chairs, or any accessory to the business shall be allowed in connection with sales.
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
Sec. 26-122. - Additional regulations for door-to-door salespersons.
(a)
The police department shall provide to each registered door-to-door salesperson an information card, and the door-to-door salesperson shall present this card to each household he visits. It is the responsibility of the door-to-door sales person to make sufficient copies of the information card to furnish to each household he visits. Such card shall contain information as required by the police department.
(b)
At any home where a "no soliciting" sign is posted, no soliciting shall be made.
(c)
No door-to-door salesperson shall enter a home except at the express invitation of the occupant.
(d)
Any residential occupant may refuse entry or to ask the door-to-door salesperson to leave the premises, after allowing entry, and, if the door-to-door salesperson should fail to leave the premises after such instructions, he shall be in violation of the laws of this state and ordinances of the city.
(e)
Any purchaser of the product of the door-to-door salesperson has the right to cancel a home solicitation service agreement any time until midnight on the business day after the day on which the purchaser signs the agreement, provided the purchaser gives notice of the cancellation to the seller at the place of business as set forth in the agreement or by certified mail, return receipt requested, which shall be posted not later than midnight on the day following execution of the agreement.
(f)
At each house where a door-to-door salesperson calls for sales, the door-to-door salesperson shall announce to the owner or occupant of the home his name and that he has been issued a permit by or has registered with the City of Douglasville as a door-to-door salesperson, and shall announce the purpose of his call and present the card provided for by this division and shall allow time for the occupant or owner to read the card.
(g)
A door-to-door salesperson shall at all times maintain a courteous decorum and shall not use opprobrious words to any homeowner or occupant. No door-to-door salesperson shall remain upon the premises of an occupant or owner after such occupant or owner has requested the door-to-door salesperson to leave or has indicated that he is not interested in purchasing the door-to-door salesperson's product.
(h)
It shall be unlawful for any door-to-door salesperson to represent by word, action or deed that the issuance of a permit or registration by the city in any way represents approval of either the action of the door-to-door salesperson or his product or organization.
(i)
If a door-to-door salesperson shall secure an order from an owner or occupant, such door-to-door salesperson shall inform the owner or occupant as to his rights under the home solicitation act of the state. This may be done by the door-to-door salesperson leaving a written card or document specifying the rights of a homeowner or occupant under the Retail Installment and Home Solicitation Sales Act (O.C.G.A. § 10-1-1 et seq.).
(Ord. No. O-2013-52, § 1, Exh. A, 12-4-13)
