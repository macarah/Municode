DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-131. - City clerk, duties.
In addition to all other duties prescribed in the City Charter and this Code, the city clerk shall serve as the chief clerical employee of the city. The city clerk shall attend all meetings of the mayor and council and make such reports to them as they may direct and deem necessary, and shall perform such other and additional duties as may from time to time be assigned to the city clerk by the mayor and council. The city may employ one or more assistant city clerks who shall assist the city clerk as needed in all official functions, and shall have all the powers and duties of the city clerk. The city clerk is authorized and appointed to receive, accept and acknowledge civil process for lawsuits and other legal proceedings and to verify pleadings for purposes of lawsuits on behalf of the city. The city clerk and the police department records supervisor are authorized to certify copies of official city records.
(Code 1977, § 2-66; Ord. No. O-96-40B, § 1, 8-19-96; Ord. No. O-99-19, § 5, 6-21-99; Ord. No. O-01-41, § 1, 6-18-01; Ord. No. O-2015-4, § 1 , 1-20-15; Ord. No. O-2017-74, § 1 , 10-2-17)
Charter reference— City clerk generally, § 2.10.
Sec. 2-132. - Assistant city attorneys.
(a)
Generally. Under the authority of and by virtue of article III, section 3.12 of the Charter of the city, there is hereby created the office of chief assistant city attorney and such assistant city attorneys as shall be employed from time to time.
(b)
Chief assistant city attorney. The chief assistant city attorney shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council. Other assistant city attorneys shall be non-appointed, regular service employees or independent contractors. The chief assistant city attorney shall assist the city attorney and other counsel for the city in representing and defending the city in all litigation in which the city is a party; shall advise the council, mayor, and other officers and employees of the city concerning legal aspects of the city's affairs; and shall perform such other duties as provided by the mayor and council. The chief assistant city attorney is authorized and appointed to receive, accept and acknowledge civil process for lawsuits and other legal proceedings and to verify pleadings for purposes of lawsuits on behalf of the city. All assistant city attorneys, including the municipal court solicitor, are authorized to settle bond forfeiture matters with bonding companies in municipal court for not less than five percent of the face amount of any bond, plus court costs.
(c)
Municipal court solicitor. The municipal court solicitor shall be a contract appointee, serving at the pleasure of the mayor and council, and shall have the duties and powers authorized by O.C.G.A. title 15, chapter 18, article 5 for a prosecuting attorney. The city attorney and the chief assistant city attorney are authorized to assist or to substitute for the municipal court solicitor in the performance of those duties.
(Code 1977, § 2-67; Ord. No. O-01-41, § 2, 6-18-01; Ord. No. O-04-26, § 1, 6-21-04; Ord. No. O-08-007, § 1, 2-4-08; Ord. No. O-2012-37, § 1, 8-6-12; Ord. No. O-2015-45, § 1 , 9-21-15)
Sec. 2-133. - City attorney.
(a)
Office created. Under the authority of and by virtue of article III, section 3.12 of the Charter of the city, there is hereby created the office of city attorney.
(b)
Term of office. The city attorney shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council.
(c)
Duties. The city attorney shall represent and defend the city in all litigation in which the city is a party; shall advise the council, mayor, and other officers and employees of the city concerning legal aspects of the city's affairs; and shall perform such other duties as provided by the mayor and council. The city attorney is authorized and appointed to receive, accept and acknowledge civil process for lawsuits and other legal proceedings and to verify pleadings for purposes of lawsuits on behalf of the city. The city attorney is authorized to settle bond forfeiture matters with bonding companies in municipal court for not less than five percent of the face amount of any bond, plus court costs.
(Ord. No. O-01-41, § 3, 6-18-01; Ord. No. O-08-007, § 1, 2-4-08)

Sec. 2-151. - Office created.
Under the authority of and by virtue of article III, section 2.28 of the Charter of the city, there is hereby created the office of city manager.
(Code 1977, § 2-76; Ord. No. O-01-41, § 4, 6-18-01)
Sec. 2-152. - Term of office.
The city manager shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council.
(Code 1977, § 2-77)
Sec. 2-153. - Responsibility.
The city manager shall devote his time and attention to the affairs of the city and shall be responsible to the mayor and council for the efficient administration of all the affairs of the city over which he has jurisdiction.
(Code 1977, § 2-78)
Sec. 2-154. - Powers and duties.
Subject to the control, direction and supervision of the mayor and council, the city manager shall have the power and it shall be his duty to:
(1)
See that all laws and ordinances and the provisions of the city charter are enforced.
(2)
See that the business and affairs of the city are efficiently organized and handled.
(3)
Exercise administrative control over all regular departments and divisions of the city, provided that the city manager shall not have or exercise any control over the planning and zoning commission, or other special board, commission, committee, authority or body set up under any general law or any ordinance or agreement of the mayor and council.
(4)
Be the personnel officer and responsible for enforcing the city personnel ordinance. He shall with the advice and consent of the mayor and council hire and all employees within the bounds allowed by the Charter and the Personnel Policies and Procedures Ordinance. Nothing in this subsection shall prevent the mayor and council from creating, abolishing, consolidating, altering or modifying departments.
(5)
Attend the meetings of the mayor and council, with the right to take part in all discussions, but have no vote.
(6)
Recommend to the mayor and council for adoption such measures as he may deem necessary or expedient.
(7)
Prepare and submit to the mayor and council an annual budget.
(8)
Keep the mayor and council fully advised as to the financial condition and needs of the city.
(9)
Make and execute on behalf of the city appropriate contracts, without further authorization from the mayor and council, where:
a.
The city's obligations are limited to the payment of money, the furnishing of routine city services, and/or the furnishing of city property or facilities; and
b.
The value of the city's obligation does not exceed $50,000.00.
(10)
Submit to the mayor and council such reports as he may deem necessary or as may be required of him by the mayor and council.
(11)
Perform such other duties as may be required by this Code, or ordinance or resolution of the mayor and council.
(12)
Give bond in the penal sum fixed by the mayor and council, payable to the city and its successors and assigns, for the benefit of the city, and for the use and benefit of the public to secure and indemnify the city and each and all of the public, by reason of his default, misfeasance, malfeasance and/or nonfeasance in the performance of his duties. The premium of such bond shall be paid by the city.
(13)
Receive, accept and acknowledge civil process for lawsuits and other legal proceedings and verify pleadings for purposes of lawsuits on behalf of the city.

Sec. 2-155. - Assistant city manager.
Under the authority of and by virtue of article III, section 3.10 of the Charter of the city, there is hereby created the office of assistant city manager. The assistant city manager shall be an appointed employee. The assistant city manager shall assist the city manager and deputy city manager and shall have all the powers and duties of the city manager and deputy city manager, including those authorized and imposed by section 2.28 of the Charter of the city and section 2-154 of this division.
( Ord. No. O-2015-47, § 1 , 10-5-15; Ord. No. O-2023-30 , § 1, 7-3-23)
Sec. 2-156. - Deputy city manager.
Under the authority of and by virtue of article III, section 3.10 of the Charter of the city, there is hereby created the office of deputy city manager. The deputy city manager shall be an appointed employee. The deputy city manager shall assist the city manager and shall have all the powers and duties of the city manager, including those authorized and imposed by section 2.28 of the Charter of the city and section 2-154 of this division.
( Ord. No. O-2023-30 , § 3, 7-3-23)
