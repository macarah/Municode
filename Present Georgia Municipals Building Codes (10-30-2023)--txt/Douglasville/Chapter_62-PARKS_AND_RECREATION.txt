Sec. 62-1. - Douglasville Parks and Recreation Advisory Board—Created, terms.
There is hereby created the Douglasville Parks and Recreation Advisory Board. This board shall consist of 13 adult members over the age of 18, appointed by the mayor and city council. The post one member shall be a city resident of city election ward one at the time of his appointment. The post two member shall be a city resident of city election ward two at the time of his appointment. The post three member shall be a city resident of city election ward three at the time of his appointment. The post four member shall be a city resident of city election ward four at the time of his appointment. The post Five member shall be a city resident of city election ward five at the time of his appointment. The post six member shall be a city resident of city election ward two at the time of his appointment. The post seven member shall be a city resident of city election ward three at the time of his appointment. The post eight member shall be a Douglas County resident recommended by or chosen to represent the Cultural Art Council of Douglasville/ Douglas County Inc. The post nine through 13 members shall be Douglas County residents who are participants, parents of a minor participant, coaches, umpires or administrators for athletic teams which use city facilities as their home location. No appointed member shall lose eligibility to hold his post by reason of moving his residence during his term, but each individual must meet eligibility standards at the time of appointment and any reappointment.
The members for posts one, four, seven, ten and 13 shall serve an initial term ending on February 15, 2022, with subsequent terms ending on the three-year anniversary of that date. The members for posts two, five, eight and 11 shall serve an initial term ending on February 15, 2021, with subsequent terms ending on the three-year anniversary of that date. The members for posts three, six, nine and 12 shall serve an initial term ending on February 15, 2020, with subsequent terms ending on the three-year anniversary of that date.

Sec. 62-3. - Same—Duties.
The duties of the advisory board shall be to:
(1)
Assist in the preparation of a periodic appraisal of the city's parks and recreation department's programs, services and facilities.
(2)
Monitor and identify the community's needs for parks and recreation services and facilities.
(3)
Make recommendations to the city council regarding the planning and functioning of the parks and recreation department through the parks and recreation department director and the city manager.
(4)
Resolve any disputes within the youth athletic associations brought before the advisory board.
(5)
Support the recreation department in the enforcement of youth coaches certification and criminal background checks.

ARTICLE II. - USE OF PUBLIC PARKS AND FACILITIES GENERALLY[3]

Footnotes:

Sec. 62-36. - Violation, penalty.
Any violation of this article shall be deemed a violation punishable as prescribed by the charter of the City of Douglasville.
(Code 1977, § 17½-22)
Charter reference— General penalty, § 6.03.
Sec. 62-37. - Use of grounds and facilities in general.
Each person, firm or corporation using the public parks and grounds shall clean up all debris, extinguish all fires when such fires are permitted, and leave the premises in good order and the facilities in a neat and sanitary condition.
(Code 1977, § 17½-16)
Sec. 62-38. - Prohibited acts.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation using public parks, grounds or facilities to either perform or permit to be performed any of the following acts:
(1)
Willfully mark, deface, disfigure, injure, tamper with or displace or remove any buildings; bridges; tables; benches; fireplaces; railings; paving or paving material; waterlines or other public utilities or parts or appurtenances thereof; signs, notices or placards, whether temporary or permanent; monuments; stakes, posts or other boundary markers; or other structures or equipment, facilities or park property or appurtenances whatsoever, either real or personal.
(2)
Throw, discharge or otherwise place or cause to be placed in the waters of any fountain, pond, lake, stream, bay or other body of water in or adjacent to any park or any tributary, stream, storm sewer or drain flowing into such waters any substance, matter or thing, liquid or solid, which will or may result in the pollution of such waters.
(3)
Damage, cut, carve, transplant or remove any tree or plant or any part thereof.
(4)
Hunt, molest, harm, frighten, kill, trap, chase, tease, shoot or throw missiles at any animal, reptile or bird; or remove or have in his possession the young of any wild animal, or the eggs, nest or young of any other animal.
(5)
Bring in or dump, deposit or leave any bottles, broken glass, ashes, paper, boxes, cans, dirt, rubbish, waste, garbage, refuse or other trash. No such refuse or trash shall be placed in any waters in or contiguous to any park or left anywhere on the grounds thereof but shall be placed in the proper receptacles where these are provided; and where receptacles are not so provided, all such rubbish or waste shall be carried away from the park by the person responsible for the presence of same and properly disposed of elsewhere.
(6)
Disturb the peace or use any profane or obscene language.
(7)
Endanger the safety of any person by any conduct or act.
(8)
Engage in fighting.
(9)
Carry, possess or drink any alcoholic beverage, except at the West Pines Golf Course.
(10)
Violate any rule for the use of a park made or approved by the parks and recreation board.
(11)
Prevent any person from using any park, or any of its facilities, or interfere with such use in compliance with this chapter and with the rules applicable to such use.
(12)
Swim, bathe or wade in any waters or waterways in or adjacent to any park except in such waters and at such places as are provided therefor and in compliance with such regulations as may be adopted therefor.
(13)
Dress or undress on any beach or in any vehicle, toilet or other place except in such bathing houses or structures as may be provided for that purpose.
(14)
Smoking, as defined in section 38-83, except that smoking of cigar, cigarette, or pipe may be permitted outdoors at the West Pines Golf Course.
(Code 1977, § 17½-17; Ord. No. O-05-25, § 2, 6-6-05; Ord. No. O-2012-33, §§ 1, 2, 7-2-12; Ord. No. O-2022-12 , § 1, 3-21-22)
Cross reference— Disorderly conduct, § 58-6.
Sec. 62-39. - Hours of operation.
The public parks shall be open daily to the public during the hours of 8:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m., unless otherwise authorized by the director of parks and recreation; and it shall be unlawful for any person or persons other than city or county personnel conducting city or county business therein to occupy or be present in such parks during any other hours. Any section or part of a park, or an entire park, may be declared closed to the public by the director of parks and recreation at any time and for any interval of time, either temporarily or at regular or stated intervals.
(Code 1977, § 17½-18; Ord. No. O-2022-12 , § 2, 3-21-22)
Sec. 62-40. - Group activity.
(a)
Whenever more than ten members of any group, association or organization desire to use any park facilities for a particular purpose, such as picnics, parties or theatrical or other entertainment performances, a representative of such group, association or organization shall first obtain a permit from the director of parks and recreation for such purpose.
(b)
The director of parks and recreation shall grant the application for a permit if it appears that the group, association or organization will not interfere with the general use of the park by individual members of the public and if the group, association or organization meets all other reasonable conditions which may be imposed by the director of parks and recreation.
(c)
Such application may contain a requirement for an indemnity bond to protect the city or county from any liability of any kind or character and to protect city or county property from damage.
(Code 1977, § 17½-19)
Sec. 62-41. - Use of picnic areas.
No person in a park shall use any portion of the picnic areas, or of any of the buildings or structures therein, for the purpose of holding picnics to the exclusion of other persons, nor shall any person use such areas and facilities for an unreasonable time if the facilities are crowded unless permitted by the director of parks and recreation.
(Code 1977, § 17½-20)
Sec. 62-42. - General regulations.
(a)
Games. It shall be unlawful for any person to take part in or abet the playing of any games involving thrown or otherwise propelled objects such as balls, stones, arrows, javelins, drones or model airplanes except in areas set apart for such forms of recreation. The playing of rough or potentially dangerous games such as football, baseball and soccer is prohibited except on the fields, courts or areas provided therefore.
(b)
Camping. It shall be unlawful for any person to set up tents, shacks or any other temporary shelter for the purpose of overnight camping, nor shall any person leave in a park after closing hours any movable structure or special vehicle to be used, or that could be used, for such purpose, such as a house trailer, camp trailer, camp wagon or the like unless permitted by the director of parks and recreation.
(c)
Fires. It shall be unlawful for any person to build or attempt to build a fire except in such areas and under such regulations as may be designated by the director of parks and recreation. No person shall drop, throw or otherwise scatter lighted matches, burning cigarettes or cigars, tobacco paper or other flammable material within any park area or on a highway, road or street abutting or contiguous thereto.
(d)
Animals. It shall be unlawful to bring any animal into any park, except dogs as expressly allowed by this paragraph (d). Dogs may be brought to parks and kept there by their owners in accordance with all of the following:
(1)
No dogs, except for service animals, as defined by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), shall be allowed: (i) indoors, (ii) inside any fence other than a fence surrounding the entire park, (iii) within 30 feet of any athletic field, playground equipment, or swimming pool;
(2)
No dangerous dog or vicious dog as defined by the Georgia Responsible Dog Ownership Law, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-8-1 through 4-8-33, shall be allowed;
(3)
No dog shall remain in a park after having exhibited aggressive behavior toward any person or animal at the park or after having damaged personal property at the park;
(4)
No dog shall be brought to or remain in a park without wearing a visible dog license; the dog handler shall produce vaccination documents upon request by city staff or security personnel;
(5)
Dogs in parks must be leashed, under the control of their handler and in view of their handler at all times; no leash shall be longer than six feet while in use; no dog shall be tethered while in any park;
(6)
No dog handler shall be younger than 16 years of age; no handler shall supervise more than two dogs at one time;
(7)
Every dog handler shall remove and properly dispose of his dog's waste;
(8)
Every dog handler shall prevent his dogs from digging holes, and shall fill any holes dug;
(9)
Every dog handler shall be liable for any damage or injury inflicted by any dog in his possession.
(e)
Motor vehicles. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle within any area of a public park, playground or recreational facility if operation within such an area is prohibited by any clearly posted sign or notice in such area erected under the authority of the director of parks and recreation or the mayor and council.
(f)
Sales. It shall be unlawful for any person to vend, sell, peddle or offer for sale any commodity or article within any park unless permitted by the director of parks and recreation.
(g)
Alcoholic beverages. It shall be unlawful for any person within any park to have in his possession, custody or control any alcoholic beverage of any kind whatsoever, except at the West Pines Golf Course.
(h)
Signs. It shall be unlawful for anyone to paste, glue, tack or otherwise post any sign, placard, advertisement or inscription whatever upon and within public parks or playgrounds and recreational facilities unless same is permitted by the director of parks and recreation.
(Code 1977, § 17½-21; Ord. No. O-96-50, §§ 1, 2, 10-21-96; Ord. No. O-05-40, § 1, 7-5-05; Ord. No. O-2012-33, § 3, 7-2-12; Ord. No. O-2019-72 , § 1, 12-16-19; Ord. No. O-2022-12 , §§ 3, 4, 3-21-22)

ARTICLE III. - USE OF ATHLETIC FIELDS

Sec. 62-51. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Affiliated sports association means a sports association which is a member and participant in a national, state-wide or regional sports competition league.
Athletic field means any of the fields used for softball, baseball, soccer or football at any city park.
Coach means a teacher or manager for a sports team, working with a team for an entire athletic season or other indefinite period of time, with authority to interact with players or to come onto the athletic field or sidelines before, during or after a game, practice or tournament.
Director means the City of Douglasville Director of Parks and Recreation or his designee.
Health care provider means a licensed physician or another licensed individual under the supervision of a licensed physician, such as a nurse practitioner, physician assistant, or certified athletic trainer who has received training in concussion evaluation and management.
Instructor means a teacher for a sports team, working with a team for a limited period of time less than an entire athletic season, with authority to interact with players or to come onto the athletic field or sidelines before, during or after a game, practice or tournament.
Sports association means any individual or legally constituted entity which organizes and operates one or more competitive amateur sports teams for which the home team members are primarily local residents, and having a usual predetermined competition season and competition schedule.
Youth means a person or persons under the age of 19 years.
Youth athletic activity means an organized athletic activity in which the majority of the participants are youth athletes and are engaging in an organized athletic game or competition against another team, club, or entity or in practice or preparation for an organized game or competition against another team, club, or entity, and renting an athletic field or using an athletic field as the home team.
Youth sports association means any sports association having a majority of participants who are youths.
(Ord. No. O-2013-11, § 2, 3-4-13; Ord. No. O-2014-24, §§ 1, 2, 4-21-14; Ord. No. O-2015-40, §§ 1, 2 , 9-8-15; Ord. No. O-2017-23 , § 1, 5-15-17)
Sec. 62-52. - Regulations.
(a)
Athletic fields may be reserved on a reasonable schedule for use by sports associations for competition, practice and tournaments. Any youth sports association which is a non-profit corporation shall apply for reservation at least 60 days in advance of the first proposed date for use of the athletic field, to allow adequate time for processing, and to assure proper consideration of the preference criteria stated in subsection (d); provided however, that the director may waive compliance with this requirement for good cause shown, where the subject field has not already been reserved by another applicant for the requested time period.
(b)
Whenever an athletic field is not in use pursuant to reservation by a sports association, the director may in his discretion make that athletic field available for use by the public.
(c)
All reservations of athletic fields shall be initiated by written application filed with the director on forms prescribed by the director. At a minimum, such application shall disclose the following:
(1)
The legal name of the applicant, including the names of the sports association's principals, their addresses, and their telephone numbers;
(2)
A proposed schedule for games and practices and tournaments, including make-up days, and a schedule showing times when field lights will be needed;
(3)
Age groups for each team;
(4)
Program registration information including registration dates, times, locations, fees, and the length of the season;
(5)
A copy of the sports association's registration procedures, and try-out procedures, including a nondiscrimination statement substantially as follows:
This association agrees that all persons shall be offered the opportunity to participate in all association programs regardless of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, handicap, or other non-merit factors.
(6)
A financial statement for a minimum 12-month period concluding after the end of the last previous sports season, showing the name, address and phone number for the sports association's treasurer, along with one or more bank statements showing the funds on hand;
(7)
A copy of the sports association's insurance binder showing not less than $1,000,000.00 in liability coverage with the city as an additional loss payee, and showing a provision requiring notification to the city if the policy is cancelled prior to expiration; and
(8)
The names and completed consent forms for background check by the director and the City of Douglasville.
(9)
If the sports association is a youth sports association, a full statement of all fees and charges to be paid to the youth sports association by any youth athlete member or his parents or legal guardian for participation in the youth sports association during the athletic season for which the athletic field will be rented, including but not limited to fees for uniforms, equipment, field rental, concessions, food and drinks, umpire fees, coaching fees, transportation, vending, advertising, copy costs and any other administrative charges. This statement may be amended in writing after submission to add further fees and charges, but new fees and charges shall not be implemented until filed with the director.
(d)
Reserved.
(e)
For pre-season practices, reservations shall not exceed three 90-minute practice sessions in any one calendar week; for practices during the playing season, reservations shall not exceed two 90-minute practice sessions in any one calendar week. A sports association shall have adults present to supervise all youth activities, from the time the first youth arrives until the last one departs.
(f)
City parks close at 9:00 p.m., unless otherwise authorized by the director of parks and recreation. No one shall use an athletic field without an approved reservation, unless the gate is unlocked by the city indicating that the athletic field is open for public use.
(g)
All field and facility rental fees shall be updated and approved by the city manager. Such fee schedule shall be maintained by the director of parks and recreation, and a copy shall be available in the office of the city clerk and in each park of facility for inspection by the general public, and shall be accessible through the city's website. Such fees may be waived for partner organizations of city initiatives upon the mayor and city council's adoption of a resolution finding that such partnership confers a substantial benefit to the city.
(h)
No one shall operate or enter the concession stands or storage buildings at an athletic field without express prior permission of the director. Storage of concession items at times when not open sales requires the written permission of the director.
(i)
No one shall host or participate in a sports tournament at a city park without an approved reservation including a schedule for a tournament.
(j)
No person or sports association shall restrict or interfere with any other activity at the park or the facility taking place off the boundaries of the athletic field reserved.
(k)
The director may cancel any scheduled activity on any reserved athletic field due to severe weather when the director determines that such use would cause harm to the participants or damage to the athletic field.
(l)
After each reserved use of an athletic field, the reserving sports association shall remove any litter and garbage from the athletic field and the surrounding area, including any dugouts or team areas, any concession areas used, and any bleachers adjacent to the athletic field. The director is authorized to withhold any clean-up deposit due to failure of a sports association to comply with this provision.
(m)
No one shall construct any new fixed component, or repair, renovate, change, or relocate any fixed component of any city park without express written permission from the director. No one shall leave a privately owned storage vessel, trailer or building in a city park after same-day open hours for that park, without the prior written permission of the director.
(n)
No one shall place a lock upon any city building, concession stand, gate or facility without the prior written permission of the director. When such permission is granted, the association shall furnish to the director a tagged key for each lock, prior to use of the lock.
(o)
No individual shall serve as a coach, instructor or team parent for a sports association reserving an athletic field for youth sports who is not approved by the director. A criminal background check by the city and approval of the director is required before any individual may serve as a youth coach, instructor, or team parent for an affiliated sports association in connection with use of an athletic field. This requirement shall not apply to any coach, instructor or team parent for an event where a sports promoter has reserved the athletic field.
(p)
Every sports association reserving an athletic field shall supply to the director, in advance of the first reserve date for any sports season, a complete roster of players, including the name, age and residence address for each player. This roster shall be updated and supplied to the director within 30 days of the addition of any new player.
(q)
Every sports association reserving an athletic field shall supply to the director, in advance of the first reserve date for any sports season, a complete roster of coaches and instructors for the sports association. This roster shall be updated and supplied to the director prior to the use of any new coach or instructor in connection with an athletic field.
(r)
After approval of a reservation, the director may revoke or terminate the reservation based any of the following:
(1)
Persistent damage to city park facilities while in use by the sports association;
(2)
Disorderly behavior and unbecoming conduct by any player, coach or instructor of the sports association occurring in connection with use of an athletic field;
(3)
Noncompliance with or disobedience of any city ordinance or rule concerning city parks and recreation; or
(4)
Failure to use the athletic field as approved or for significant periods of the reserved time, where failure is not due to inclement weather.
(s)
Youth coaches, instructors and team parents requiring the criminal background check must wear a current city issued identification card, that indicates both criteria have been met, at all times while serving either of those roles at an athletic field. The minimum fine for violation of this provision shall be $50.00.
(t)
Youth sports associations, their coaches, personnel and youths shall comply with the following protocol:
(1)
Prior to the beginning of each athletic season of a youth athletic activity, every youth athletic association shall provide an information sheet to all youth athletes' parents or legal guardians which informs them of the nature and risk of concussion and head injury;
(2)
If a youth athlete participating in a youth athletic activity exhibits symptoms of having a concussion, the youth sports association shall remove that athlete from the game, competition, tryout, or practice, and advise the youth's athletes' parents or legal guardians that the youth athlete must be evaluated by a health care provider for concussion before returning to play; and
(3)
If a youth athlete is deemed by a health care provider to have sustained a concussion, the coach and youth sports association shall not permit the youth athlete to return to play until the youth athlete receives clearance from a health care provider for a full or graduated return to play; and
(4)
This subsection (t) shall not create any liability for, or create a cause of action against, a the city or its insurers, agents, officers, employees, volunteers, other city personnel for any act or omission to act related to the removal or non-removal of a youth athlete from a game, competition, tryout, or practice pursuant to this subsection. This subsection (t) shall not create any liability for, or create a cause of action against any health care providers acting in a volunteer capacity for any act or omission to act related to the removal or non-removal of a youth athlete from a game, competition, tryout, or practice pursuant to this subsection.
(u)
No youth sports association shall request or require any youth member or his parents or legal guardians to pay fees or charges other than those shown in the statement of fees or an amended statement of fees required by paragraph (c)(9) above.
(Ord. No. O-2013-11, §§ 2, 4, 3-4-13; Ord. No. O-2013-39, § 1, 9-16-13; Ord. No. O-2014-24, §§ 3—5, 4-21-14; Ord. No. O-2015-40, §§ 3, 4 , 9-8-15; Ord. No. O-2017-23 , §§ 2, 3, 5-15-17; Ord. No. O-2022-12 , §§ 5—7, 3-21-22)
