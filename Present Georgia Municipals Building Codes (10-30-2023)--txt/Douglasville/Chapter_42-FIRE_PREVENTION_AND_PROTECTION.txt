ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - False fire alarms.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully give a false alarm of fire within the city.
(Code 1977, § 11-1)
Cross reference— Offenses and miscellaneous provisions, ch. 58.
Sec. 42-2. - Damage to apparatus.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully or carelessly break, destroy or carry away any fire engine or apparatus belonging thereto, or used by the fire department.
(Code 1977, § 11-2)
Sec. 42-3. - Hindering fire fighting.
It shall be unlawful for any person to hinder, obstruct, delay or interfere with the personnel or apparatus of the fire department in the fighting of any fire or the discharge of the duties of the fire department in any manner whatsoever.
(Code 1977, § 11-3)
Sec. 42-4. - Tampering with fire hydrant.
It shall be unlawful for any person, without written authority from the fire chief, to tamper with or take water from or release water from a public fire hydrant.
(Code 1977, § 11-4)
Cross reference— Utilities, ch. 86.
Sec. 42-5. - Obstructing fire hydrants or fire lanes.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct with building materials or in any other manner whatsoever within 15 feet of any cistern or fire hydrant which would obstruct approaches to the same by any member of the fire department. No person shall put any post or installation of any nature whatsoever within 18 inches of any fire hydrant without first obtaining written permission from the city council.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct with any vehicle or other object any designated fire lane.
(c)
A person who shall violate a provision of this section or fail to comply therewith or with any requirement thereof, upon conviction by a court of competent jurisdiction, shall be punished as provided in Section 4.13 of the Charter of the City of Douglasville, Georgia. Where any offense continues from day to day, each day's continuance shall be deemed a separate offense. Citation for violation may be issued by any police officer of the city, or by any officer or official of the county fire department.
(Code 1977, § 11-5; Ord. No. O-01-41, § 8, 6-18-01; Ord. No. O-2022-10 , § 1, 3-7-22)
Secs. 42-6—42-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - FIRE PREVENTION CODE[2]

Sec. 42-36. - Code adopted.
The Georgia State Minimum Standard Fire Code (International Fire Code with Georgia State Amendments), of which code not less than one copy has been and now is filed in the office of the building official, is hereby adopted and incorporated as fully as if set forth at length in this section, and except as hereinafter amended, the provisions therein shall be controlling within the limits of the city.
(Code 1977, § 11-16; Ord. No. O-91-32, 10-7-91; Ord. No. O-04-43, § 1, 10-4-04; Ord. No. O-2022-10 , § 2, 3-7-22)
Sec. 42-37. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in the code adopted by this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Fire authority means the City of Douglasville.
Misdemeanor or violation means a violation which is punishable as a violation of the city charter.
(Code 1977, § 11-18)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 42-38. - Enforcement.
(a)
The fire prevention code adopted by this article shall be enforced by the Douglas County Fire Department. The fire department of the city shall also have authority to enforce the provisions of this code if such is necessary for the protection of the citizens.
(b)
The bureau of fire prevention shall be under the direction of the fire official. In the absence of the fire official the chief of the fire department shall perform any duties required of the fire official unless there is a duly appointed deputy to the fire official available to carry out the provisions of this code.
(c)
The fire official may from time to time, as necessary, and with the consent of the chief of the fire department, detail members of the fire department as his authorized agents to assist in the enforcement of the provisions of the fire prevention code adopted by this article.
(Code 1977, § 11-17; Ord. No. O-2022-10 , § 3, 3-7-22)
Sec. 42-39. - Violations.
(a)
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of the fire prevention code adopted by this article, or fail to comply therewith, or who shall violate or fail to comply with any order made thereunder, who shall build in violation of any detailed statement of specifications or plans submitted and approved thereunder, or any certificate or permit issued thereunder, and from which no appeal has been taken within the time affixed in this article, shall severally for each and every such violation and noncompliance respectively, be guilty of a violation of this Code. Each such person shall be deemed guilty of a separate offense for each and every day or portion thereof during which any violation of any of the provisions of the fire prevention code is committed or continued. Upon conviction of any such violation such person shall be punished within the limits of and as provided by section 1-3 of this Code.
(b)
The application of the above penalty shall not be held to prevent the enforced removal of prohibited conditions, or the requirement that each person shall be required to correct or remedy such violation or defects within a reasonable time.
(Code 1977, § 11-22)
Sec. 42-40. - Life Safety Code—Adopted.
The Life Safety Code, published by the National Fire Protection Association, as modified by the Rules and Regulations of the Safety Fire Commissioner, Rule 120-3-3, Rules and Regulations for the State Minimum Fire Safety Standards, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-2-4, of which code not less than one copy has been and now is filed in the office of the building official, is hereby adopted and incorporated as fully as if set forth at length in this section, and except as hereafter amended, the provisions therein shall be controlling within the limits of the city.
(Code 1977, § 11-23; Ord. No. O-04-43, § 2, 10-4-04; Ord. No. O-2022-10 , § 4, 3-7-22)
Sec. 42-41. - Same—Administration.
The life safety code adopted by the provisions of this article shall be administered and enforced jointly and/or severally by the building official and the Douglas County fire chief or their duly appointed deputies.
(Code 1977, § 11-24; Ord. No. O-2022-10 , § 5, 3-7-22)
Sec. 42-42. - Same—Penalty for violation.
Any person who shall violate a provision of the life safety code adopted by the provisions of this article or fail to comply therewith or with any of the provisions thereof, or violate a detailed statement or plans submitted and approved thereunder, shall be guilty of a violation of this Code. Each such person shall be deemed guilty of a separate offense for each and every day or portion thereof during which any violation of any of the provisions of the life safety code is committed and continued, and upon conviction of any such violation, such person shall be punished within the limits of and as provided by section 1-3 of this Code.
(Code 1977, § 11-25)
Sec. 42-43. - Conflicts.
If any conflict exists between the provisions of the codes adopted by the provisions of this article and applicable provisions of this Code of Ordinances, state law or city ordinances, rules or regulations, the more stringent provisions shall prevail and be controlling.
(Code 1977, § 11-26)
Sec. 42-44. - Automatic sprinkler systems and fire alarms.
(a)
Buildings of any type construction or occupancy of 5,000 square feet or more per floor, or buildings of two or more stories in height shall be protected throughout by an approved supervised automatic sprinkler system installed in accordance with the current State of Georgia adopted edition of the NFPA 13, as modified, Standards for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems. A sprinkler control valve and water flow device shall be provided for each floor. The building official or the Douglas County Fire Department may additionally require the installation of a water-based fire protection system and/or smoke detection system, where the public interest, the health and welfare of the fire fighters, or the protection of human life, would be protected by the same, due to the type of construction, hazard of content, limited access to the structure, or other safety factors.
(b)
All hotels, motels, dormitories, apartments, lodging facilities, townhouses, rooming houses, all residential board and care facilities, and all day care facilities, and all personal care facilities with more than seven clients as defined by NFPA 101, Life Safety Code shall be protected by an approved supervised automatic sprinkler system installed in accordance with the current State of Georgia adopted edition with amendments for NFPA 13 or 13R, as modified or amended, Standards for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems in Residential Occupancies up to and including three stories in height. Based on the type of construction and the tenants' ability to evacuate, the building official or his designee shall designate which system is best suited in each situation. Residential occupancies exceeding three stories in height shall be protected by an approved automatic sprinkler system installed in accordance with NFPA 13.
(c)
Existing buildings, when required to have automatic sprinkler protection by subsections (a) or (b) above, shall be retrofitted for automatic sprinklers as required by the building official or his designee in accordance with NFPA 13 or 13R, when:
(1)
A building is renovated, and the cost of renovation exceeds 50 percent of the latest approved tax assessed value of the building; or
(2)
There is a change in occupancy classification.
Whenever such retrofitting is required by this section, the buildings shall be required to upgrade to meet all current codes, standards, and ordinances for fire protection and life safety systems, and the owner shall have the system inspected and tested in accordance with NFPA 25, as modified, Standards for the Inspection, Testing and Maintenance of Water-based Fire Protection Systems.
Exceptionally, existing buildings located in the historic district or on any historic site may use standards set forth by chapter 48 of the City Code, as the city may amend from time to time. As a further exception, for existing buildings located within the historic district and lying south of Broad Street, north of Spring Street, east of Club Drive, and west of Duncan Street, installation of sprinklers shall not be required.
(d)
Automatic fire sprinkler systems shall be installed in all newly constructed multifamily dwellings, regardless of the type of construction or square feet. In addition, automatic fire sprinkler systems shall be installed in all new single-family and duplex dwellings, regardless of the type of construction, whenever side yard setbacks are to be 7.5 linear feet or less; said systems shall conform to the NFPA standard 13R, current edition, Standard for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems in One- and Two-Family Dwellings and Manufactured Homes. "New", for the purpose of this section, includes any addition to existing buildings, whether vertical or horizontal; any existing building or structure shall be subject to this requirement where there is substantial renovation due to fire or other hazard of serious consequence. For purposes of this paragraph, "substantial" means any construction project involving exits or internal/external features of such buildings or structure costing more than or equal to 50 percent of the building's or structure's gross tax assessed value at the time of such renovation, repair, remodeling, or replacement.
(e)
Where required, post indicator valves (PIV) and fire department connections (FDC) shall be located at or near the main entry to the site but no closer than one and one-half times the height of the building. Appropriate signage shall be provided to designate PIV and FDC connections. Minimum diameter of FDC coupling shall be two and one-half-inch.
(f)
All water-based fire protection systems shall have electronically supervised monitoring; all fire alarm systems installed in commercial buildings shall be supervised at all times, and shall include installation of horns and strobes; and pull stations shall be provided at all required exits as designated by the Douglas County Fire Department.
(g)
No person shall shut off or disable any automatic fire sprinkler system or fire alarm system, and no owner, occupant, or resident of any building shall disarm or allow disarming of such systems. Exceptionally, such systems may be shut off in order to perform maintenance work on the systems during the time that qualified maintenance personnel are on the premises performing necessary maintenance work. Such maintenance work shall be conducted after notice to and approval by the Douglas County Fire Department and Douglas County Emergency E-911. A fire watch may be required during the time the water-based fire protection system or fire alarm system is removed from service, and shall remain in place until such time as the systems are returned to full functioning capacity.
(Ord. No. O-04-41, § 1, 10-4-04; Ord. No. O-07-44, § 2, 8-2-07; Ord. No. O-07-64, § 4, 11-19-07; Ord. No. O-2010-17, § 2, 4-19-10; Ord. No. O-2022-10 , §§ 6—10, 3-7-22)
Sec. 42-45. - Fire-fighting accessibility; standards for hydrants, mains and pipes.
(a)
Fire-fighting accessibility.
(1)
Accessibility of buildings for fire-fighting equipment:
a.
Reasonable accessibility shall be provided to and around all buildings for firefighting equipment, including ladder trucks on multiple-story projects. Accessibility shall be approved by the fire chief or his designee.
b.
Accessibility for firefighter's equipment shall be maintained throughout all stages of construction.
c.
Minimum widths of access roads, excluding parking, shall be 20 feet. Minimum turning radius shall be 32 feet. All access roads and fire lanes shall be capable of supporting the loads of fire apparatus and shall have a minimum of 13) feet, six inches of vertical clearance.
d.
All access roads in excess of 150 feet in length shall be provided with an approved area for turning around fire apparatus.
e.
Gates securing fire apparatus access shall be provided with an automatic gate-opening device if the gates are controlled by electronic means. An approved siren operated sensor shall activate the gate-opening device.
f.
Gates securing fire apparatus access shall be a minimum 14 feet in clear width for divided lanes and 20 feet for a single lane. Gate shall be swing or sliding type. Fire access lanes shall be straight and have no turns for 50 feet on both sides of security gate.
g.
Additional fire apparatus access may be required based on the fire chief or his designee's opinion.
(2)
Aerial fire apparatus access road:
a.
Buildings or portions of buildings or facilities exceeding 30 feet in height above the lowest level of fire department vehicle access shall be provided with approved fire apparatus roads capable of accommodating fire department aerial apparatus. Overhead utility and power lines shall not be located within the aerial fire apparatus access roadway.
b.
Fire apparatus access roads shall have a minimum unobstructed width of 26 feet in the immediate vicinity of any building or portion of building more than 30 feet in height.
c.
At least one of the required access roads meeting this condition shall be located within a minimum of 15 feet and a maximum of 30 feet from the building, and shall be positioned parallel to one entire side of the building.
d.
Commercial and industrial buildings exceeding 30 feet or three stories in height shall have at least three means of fire apparatus access for each structure.
(b)
Fire hydrant installation prior to construction.
(1)
Water mains and fire hydrants, shall be installed, under water pressure and ready for fire fighting, before any combustible materials are brought onto construction sites.
(2)
All fire hydrants, fittings, valves and fire department connections shall be approved by the Douglasville-Douglas County Water and Sewer Authority and the Douglas County Fire Department. Fire department connections shall not be less than 18 inches nor more than 36 inches above the level of the adjoining ground or paving. The thread of such connections shall be uniform with that used by the fire department.
(3)
The size and shape of the operation nut, as well as the direction the hydrant stem operates, shall be uniform the hydrant stem operates, shall be uniform with construction standards and specifications of the Douglasville-Douglas County Water and Sewer Authority for hydrants and for water distribution systems.
(c)
Fire hydrant installation standards for minimum size water mains and spacing of fire hydrants for all new construction.
(1)
In single-family residential subdivisions, at least eight-inch ductile iron pipe shall be installed. Fire hydrants shall be spaced not to exceed 400 feet. Three-way fire hydrants shall be used.
(2)
In multi-family residential subdivisions, multi-family apartment complexes, industrial areas and commercial areas, (including but not limited to motels, hotels, nursing homes, hospitals, education buildings, all office buildings and churches) at least eight-inch ductile iron pipe water mains shall be installed to provide a minimum water flow of 1,000 gallons per minute. Larger mains and flows may be required by the building official or his designee. Fire hydrants shall be three-way hydrants and shall be spaced not to exceed 400 feet, with additional hydrants located as necessary, to permit all portions of the exterior buildings and combustible storage to be reached by hose lays of not more than 400 feet. Fire hydrant locations shall be approved by the building official or his designee to allow fire fighting equipment to be placed within 15 feet of said hydrants.
(3)
In mobile home parks, fire hydrants shall be spaced not to exceed 400 feet, with a minimum of an eight-inch ductile iron pipe waterline, which shall be installed to provide a water flow of at least 700 gallons per minute.
(4)
Prior to any preliminary subdivision plat submission for approval, the applicant shall obtain approval of the building official or his designee for the spacing of all fire hydrants, main size and water flows.
(5)
No pipe smaller than a standard eight inch in diameter shall be used to supply more than one hydrant or one hydrant on dead-end mains over 500 feet but not exceeding 999 feet, and no smaller than a ten inch in diameter for dead-end main for 1,000 feet or more.
(6)
No pipe smaller than a standard eight inch in diameter shall be used to supply one hydrant and automatic extinguishing systems.
(7)
No pipe smaller than a standard eight inch in diameter shall be used to supply more than one hydrant and automatic extinguishing systems on looped mains of 1,000 feet.
(d)
[Deviation.] In all of the above requirements in Section 42-45, in the event any deviation is desired to be made, a request for such deviation shall be submitted to and approved by the Douglas County Fire Department in writing. The fire department chief and/or his or her designee, said designee including but not limited to the fire marshal, shall have the authority to make such approval on behalf of the Douglas County Fire Department.
(Ord. No. O-04-42, § 1, 10-4-04; Ord. No. O-2022-10 , § 12, 3-7-22; Ord. No. O-2023-11 , § 1, 3-6-23)
Sec. 42-46. - Open burning.
(a)
In general. Except as hereinafter provided in subsection (b), no person shall kindle an open fire in any public or private place outside any building. Fires started in violation of this section shall be promptly extinguished by the persons responsible for same upon notice by the Douglas County fire chief or the chief's duly designated agent.
(b)
Exceptions.
(1)
Between October 1 and April 30 open burning of leaves on the premises on which they fall by the person in control of the premises shall require a burning permit issued by the fire department.
(2)
Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for production or harvesting of crops shall require a burning permit issued by the fire department.
(3)
Between October 1 and April 30, the "prescribed burning" of any land by the owners or the owner's designee shall require a burning permit issued by the fire department. Prescribed burning of land is subject to authorization by the state forestry commission.
(4)
For recreation purposes or cooking food for immediate human consumption. Recreational burning shall not exceed an area of three feet in diameter without the prior approval of the fire department. Recreational burning approved by the fire department in excess of three feet in diameter shall require a burning permit issued by the fire department.
(5)
Fires set for purposes of training fire-fighting personnel when authorized by the fire chief or the chief's duly designated agent.
(6)
Operation of devices using open flames such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters and other flame-making equipment.
(7)
Disposal of all packing materials previously containing explosives, in accordance with U.S. Department of Labor Safety Regulations, shall require a burning permit issued by the fire department.
(8)
Between October 1 and April 30 open burning of vegetative material for the purpose of land clearing using an air curtain destructor provided the following conditions are met:
a.
Authorization for such open burning is approved by and a burning permit has been issued by the fire department.
b.
The location of the air curtain destructor is at least 300 feet from any occupied structure or public road. Air curtain destructors used solely for utility line clearing or road clearing may be located at a lesser distance upon approval of the fire department.
c.
No more than one air curtain destructor is operated within a ten-acre area at one time or there must be at least 1,000 feet between any two air curtain destructors.
d.
Only wood waste consisting of trees, logs, large brush and stumps which are relatively free of soil are burned in the air curtain destructor.
e.
Tires or other rubber products, plastics, heavy oils or asphaltic based or impregnated materials are not used to start or maintain the operation of the air curtain destructor.
f.
The air curtain destructor is constructed, installed and operated in a manner consistent with good air pollution control practice for minimizing emissions of fly ash and smoke.
g.
The cleaning out of the air curtain destructor pit is performed in a manner to prevent fugitive dust: and
h.
The air curtain destructor cannot be fired before 10:00 a.m. and the fire must be completely extinguished, using water or by covering with dirt, at least one hour before sunset.
(c)
Open burning on premises heretofore described shall require a person in charge of such open burning to be physically present, with an effective means of controlling the fire immediately available. No fire shall be started or allowed to burn nearer than 30 feet from a property line or any structure. All such open burning shall be attended until the last embers of the fire have been extinguished; provided further that all such burning will be in conformity with state air quality control rules and regulations chapter 391-3-1.
(d)
Open burning, whenever feasible, shall be conducted between 10:00 a.m. and one hour before sunset.
(e)
Burning permits shall not be issued when the winds are greater than ten miles per hour or during dry weather conditions. At such times all exceptions are void and no open fires shall be permitted.
(f)
Burning permits may be cancelled at any time without advance notice due to changes in wind speed and weather conditions.
(g)
Definitions.
(1)
Prescribed burning means the controlled application of fire to existing vegetative fuels under specified environmental conditions and following appropriate precautionary measures, which causes the fire to be confined to a predetermined area and accomplishes one or more planned land management objectives as specified in paragraphs 12-6-146(3), (4), and (7) of the Georgia Prescribed Burning Act or to mitigate catastrophic wildfires. Burning to facilitate land use changes (such as a change from forest land to residential, commercial, or industrial development or a different agricultural use) is not considered prescribed burning and should therefore be considered a land clearing operation subject to the provisions of subparagraph 391-3-1-.02(5)(a)11. (Note: This definition includes what was previously defined as prescribed burning and as slash burning.)
(2)
Recreational burning means the burning of materials other than rubbish where fuel being burned is not contained in an incinerator, outdoor fireplace, barbecue grill or barbecue pit and with a total fuel area of three feet or less in diameter and two feet (610 mm) or less in height for pleasure, religious, ceremonial, cooking or similar purposes.
(h)
Burning of certain materials prohibited. No material or substance which emits noxious or poisonous gases such as nitrogen dioxide, hydrogen cyanide or chlorine or other poisonous gases that will not readily dissipate in the atmosphere may be burned within the city.
( Ord. No. O-2022-10 , § 11, 3-7-22)
