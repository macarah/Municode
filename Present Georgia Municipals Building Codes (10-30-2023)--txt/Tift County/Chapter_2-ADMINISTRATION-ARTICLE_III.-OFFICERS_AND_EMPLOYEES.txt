DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-76. - Employee ethics and conduct.
(a)
Generally. Because public employees are in positions created for the public and funded by the public, the public has the right to expect that the incumbents in these positions will not abuse the trust placed in them by the public. Tift County employees are expected to observe and honor all federal, state and local laws.
(b)
Political activity. Every county employee is entitled to exercise his/her right to vote. Employees may join or affiliate with civic organizations of a partisan or political nature, attend political meetings, advocate and support the principles and policies of civic or political organizations in accordance with federal, state, and local laws; however, neither an employee's position nor county time shall be used for political purposes, nor should any employee actively campaign for any candidate or issue.
(c)
Prohibited activity. No employee shall:
(1)
Engage in any political or partisan activity during working hours;
(2)
Use official authority or influence for the purpose of interfering with or affecting the result of an election or a nomination for office;
(3)
Be required as a duty of employment or as a condition of employment, promotion, or tenure in office to contribute funds for political or partisan purposes, to solicit funds, or act as a custodian of funds for political purposes;
(4)
Coerce or compel contributions for political or partisan purposes by another employee of the county;
(5)
Use any supplies, materials or equipment of the county for political or partisan purposes or to aid a political candidate, party, or cause;
(6)
Wear his/her county uniform for political purposes or to aid a political candidate, party or cause; or
(7)
Campaign on county premises, which means that employees shall not give out information or attempt to coerce or influence other county employees or the public. Employees shall not wear partisan political badges or buttons while on duty. Employees may display a political sticker on their private automobile, subject to work related instances.
Any violation of the provisions of this section shall be deemed improper conduct and such employee shall be subject to disciplinary action.
(d)
Running for local political office prohibited. County employees may not campaign nor run for local political office in the county. For purposes of this section, "local political office" shall mean any elective office in the county government, any City [of Tift] elective office, and the county board of education.
Any county employee intending to become a candidate for any political office shall, upon qualifying as a candidate for nomination or election, resign from his or her position with the county, or shall be placed on leave without pay until such time as the election for which he/she is a candidate is finally decided; provided, however, that the employee may elect to use annual leave until such leave is depleted, at which time the employee would be placed on leave without pay until such time as the election for which he/she is a candidate is finally decided.
Failure or refusal of an employee to resign or be placed on leave without pay or to use annual leave as described above upon qualifying as a candidate for nomination or election shall result in the dismissal of the employee.
(Ord. of 2-12-96)
Sec. 2-77. - Purchasing card and credit card use by elected officials.
(a)
Intent and scope. This section is intended to comply with the policy requirements of O.C.G.A. § 36-80-24 regarding the use of county issued government purchasing cards and credit cards.
(b)
Definitions.
Authorized elected official means an elected official designated by public vote of the board of commissioners to receive a county issued government purchasing card or credit card.
Card administrator means the purchasing card and credit card administrator designated by the county board of commissioners as required by O.C.G.A. § 36-80-24(c)(6).
County means Tift County and/or the board of commissioners.
County purchase card, county p-card or county credit card means a financial transaction card issued by any business organization, financial institution, or any duly authorized agent of such organization or institution, used by a county official to purchase goods, services and other things of value on behalf of the county.
Financial transaction card means an instrument or device as the term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-9-30(5).
User agreement means the required agreement between the board of commissioners and the authorized elected officials which restricts the use of a county purchasing card or credit card.
(c)
Designated elected officials. The county board of commissioners ("county"), in its discretion, may authorize specific county elected officials to use a county purchasing card or credit card by adoption of a resolution in a public meeting.
No authorized elected official may use a county purchasing card or credit card until and unless he or she has executed the county's purchasing card and credit card user agreement.
The county will not make payments to any business organization, financial institution, or any duly authorized agent of such organization or institution, for amounts charged by an elected official to any purchasing cards or credit cards that are not issued pursuant to this section or for any purchases that are not authorized by this section.
(d)
Card administrator.
The board of commissioners designates the county manager or his designee as purchasing card and credit card administrator. The responsibilities of the card administrator include:
(1)
Manage county issued purchasing cards and credit cards.
(2)
Serve as the main point of contact for all county purchasing card and credit card issues.
(3)
Serve as liaison to the elected officials authorized to use a purchasing card or credit card and their staff, as well as to the issuer of the purchasing card or credit card.
(4)
Provide training on card policies and procedures to the elected officials authorized to use a purchasing card or credit card and their staff.
(5)
Develop internal procedures to ensure timely payment of cards.
(6)
Assist authorized elected officials to dispute transactions when necessary.
(7)
Establish internal procedures to ensure compliance with this section, county procurement ordinances and policies, county purchasing card and credit card user agreements, applicable agreements with the business organization, financial institution, or any duly authorized agent of such organization or institution, issuing card, and state law, specifically, O.C.G.A. §§ 16-9-37 and 36-80-24.
(8)
Document internal controls, audits and other measures to prevent and detect misuse or abuse of the cards.
(9)
Audit and reconcile transactions monthly.
(10)
Maintain records for at least seven years or as otherwise provided by the county's record retention policy.
(e)
Use of cards.
(1)
Authorized purchases. County purchase cards and credit cards may be used to purchase goods and services directly related to the public duties of the authorized elected official only. All purchases are subject to the terms of this section, the county purchasing card and credit card user agreement, county procurement policies and ordinances, and the adopted budget.
Only authorized elected officials may use a county purchase card or credit card for purchases or payments. The cards, and use of the cards, are not transferrable to employees. The authorized elected official shall use care to ensure that others do not have access to the card account number, expiration date and security code.
Unless otherwise approved by the governing authority or established in the county purchasing card and credit card user agreement, the transaction limits shall be those card limits set at the time of issuance.
(2)
Unauthorized purchases. County purchasing cards and credit cards shall not be used for goods and services not directly related to the official responsibilities of the authorized elected official. Additionally, cards shall not be used to avoid compliance with the county's purchasing ordinances and procedures, to purchase goods and services that are not approved in the county's budget, to purchase goods and services exceeding the per transaction or per month limit, or to make purchases not in compliance with the county purchasing card and credit card user agreement.
(3)
Receipts and documentation. Receipts, invoices and other supporting documentation of all purchases made with a county purchasing card or credit card shall be obtained and maintained by the authorized county elected official for five years or as otherwise provided by the county's record retention policy. If an original or duplicate cannot be produced, a sworn affidavit of the authorized elected official may be substituted. The documentation must include the supplier or merchant information (i.e., name and location), quantity, description, unit price, total price, price paid without sales tax and an explanation of the purchase sufficient to show that the expense was in the performance of official county duties.
(4)
Public records. All receipt and other documentation of purchases are public records and subject to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq.
(f)
Review of purchases and audit. Proper documentation of purchases, internal controls and other measures prevent and allow detection to misuse or abuse of county issued purchase cards and credit cards. All authorized elected officials and their appointed staff that process payments under this program shall cooperate and comply with the procedures established by the county.
(1)
Review of purchases. All purchases shall be reviewed according to the following procedures internally set to ensure adequate check and balance of card use.
(2)
Audits. The card administrator shall perform an annual review of the card program to ensure adequacy of internal policies and procedures, cardholder spending limits, monthly reconciliation procedures and documentation for transactions. Elected officials and staff shall cooperate with such review.
(g)
Violations.
(1)
An elected official shall reimburse the county for any purchases made with a county issued purchase card or credit card in violation of this section or the user agreement.
(2)
In the discretion of the county governing authority, failure to comply with the procedures outlined in this section may result in:
a.
A warning;
b.
Suspension of the elected official's authority to use a county purchase card or credit card; or
c.
Revocation of the elected official's authority to use a county purchase card or credit card.
(3)
Nothing in this section shall preclude the county governing authority from referring misuse of a purchase card or credit card for prosecution to the appropriate authorities.
(Ord. No. 2015-01, §§ I—VI, 12-14-15)

Sec. 2-91. - Creation of office; appointment; removal; ineligibility.
There is hereby created the office of county manager of Tift County. The county manager shall be appointed by a majority vote of the board of commissioners for a period of one year or on such other terms as the board of commissioners may adopt by contract; however, he/she may be removed at the pleasure of the board of commissioners as outlined in this article or as provided by contract. No member of the board of commissioners shall be appointed county manager during the term of office for which he/she is elected. No person related by blood or marriage within the third degree to any member of the board of commissioners shall be eligible for appointment as county manager.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 2, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-92. - Qualifications.
The county manager shall have the following qualifications:
(a)
A degree in business administration, public administration or other related fields, together with at least three years experience as a city or county manager or assistant city or county manager; or
(b)
Comparable training, educational and professional experience which the board of commissioners deems to be appropriate so as to qualify the county manager for the responsibility and duties set forth in section 2-93 below. The commission in making this determination shall determine that the county manager has a knowledge and level of competency commonly associated with the completion of a degree in business or public administration, together with experience sufficiently to thoroughly understand the diverse objectives and functions of the subunits of county government in order to direct and coordinate work within the county government.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 3, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-93. - Job description.
The county manager shall be the administrative head of the county government. The manager shall be responsible to the board for the proper and efficient administration of all affairs of the county, except as otherwise provided by law.
(a)
Duties.
(1)
Direct and supervise all appointed department heads of Tift County in the performance of their assigned duties: to appoint, with the consent of the board of commissioners and when necessary suspend, discharge, transfer or remove, with the consent of the board of commissioners, all appointed department heads with the exception of the county attorney, the auditor, and the clerk, who shall be appointed and discharged directly by the board of commissioners.
(2)
Require reports from the heads of departments and other employees trusted with administrative duties or exercising discretion.
(3)
Assist all county elected officials, as well as, those department heads of the county whose duties and responsibilities are not under the direct control and supervision of the board, but who are dependent upon appropriations, in whole or in part, of the board for their continued operation.
(4)
Be responsible for the preparation and administration of the county budget.
(5)
Attend all meetings of the board with the right to take part in the discussion, provided the manager shall have no vote before the board.
(6)
Submit to the board of commissioners, as often as required, a complete report of the finances of the county and make such recommendations as he/she may deem necessary or desirable to meet present and future financial needs of the county.
(7)
Recommend to the board of commissioners such personnel policies regarding work schedules, pay schedules, vacation and sick leave, as may be consistent with the law and basic policies of the board.
(8)
Recommend to the board of commissioners adoption of such policies as may be deemed necessary to protect the health, safety and welfare of the community.
(9)
Submit the salaries of all officers and employees of the county, whom he/she has the right to appoint; subject, however, to supervision, control or disapproval by the board.
(10)
Submit to the board at the end of each fiscal year a complete report on the finances and administrative activities of the county for the preceding year.
(11)
See that all ordinances, resolutions and mandates adopted by the board of commissioners are enforced and duly carried out and complied with in accordance with such legislation, programs, regulations or plans.
(12)
See that legislation, programs, regulations or plans promulgated and required by the state or federal government are duly carried out in accordance with the direction of the board.
(13)
Supervise the performance of all contracts made by any person for work done for the county and to supervise and regulate all purchases of materials and supplies for the county within such limitations and under such rules and regulations as may be imposed by the board of commissioners.
(14)
Have care and custody of all building and of all real and personal property of the county government.
(15)
Coordinate with business and development community to increase industrial development in the county.
(b)
Knowledge, skills, and abilities.
(1)
Knowledge of the principles and practices of public administration.
(2)
Knowledge of the county code of ordinances and other regulations, policies, and procedures.
(3)
Knowledge of budget preparation and finance.
(4)
Knowledge of the functions, organization, and operations of all county departments.
(5)
Knowledge of the county's demographic and economic profile, including its industrial base.
(6)
Knowledge of the principles and practices of effective public relations.
(7)
Knowledge of the state and federal laws in all areas applicable to county government including unemployment, wages, hours, EEOC, taxes, workers compensation, pensions, and others.
(8)
Skill in written and oral communications.
(9)
Ability to establish and maintain effective working relationships with the county's elected officers and appointed department heads, other county employees and the general public.
(c)
Supervisory controls. The chairperson of the board of commissioners, assigns work on the basis of standing goals, objectives, and priorities. The manager must use much judgment in deciding how to accomplish goals. The supervisor provides guidance for problematic situations and spot checks work for the nature and propriety of final results.
(d)
Guidelines. Guidelines consist of all county ordinances, applicable state and federal laws, grant application instructions, county policies and procedures, and directives from the board of commissioners. These guidelines require selection, judgment, and interpretation in application.
(e)
Complexity. The purpose of this position is to manage all phases of county government. The work is made complex by the multitude and variety of tasks to be completed and by the necessity for dealing with public input which can be conflicting or critical.
(f)
Scope and effect. The purpose of this position is to assist the county commissioners in seeing that the county's government operates according to state laws and county ordinances. Successful performance in this position facilities work of all county departments, assures that grant monies are obtained and spent properly, assures that county services are delivered efficiently, and that county records are properly retained.
(g)
Personal contacts. Contacts are typically with co-workers, employees in all county departments, member of the board of commissioners, employees of the State of Georgia, representatives of organizations, and the public at large.
(h)
Physical demands. Work is performed with employee intermittently sitting, standing or stooping.
(i)
Work environment. Work is performed in an office and the field.
(j)
Supervisory and management responsibility. The position has direct supervision over all administrative personnel and all department heads.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 4, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-94. - Reports.
The board of commissioners shall have the right to require the county manager to appear before it at any time and make such reports, oral or written, as it deems necessary.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 5, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-95. - Bond.
At the election of the board of commissioners, the county manager may be required to execute and deliver a good and sufficient bond conditioned upon the faithful performance of his duties. The bond shall be payable to the county and shall be in such amount and on such terms as may be prescribed by the board. Should a bond be required by the board of commissioners, it shall be filed by the county clerk in the office of the clerk of the Superior Court of Tift County. Any premium due on such bond shall be paid by the county fiscal officer out of county funds.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 6, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-96. - Oath.
Before entering upon his duties, the county manager shall take and subscribe an oath for the faithful performance of his duties under this article, which oath shall be duly entered upon the minutes of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 7, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-97. - Suspension or removal.
The county manager may be suspended or removed at the pleasure of the county board of commissioners by a majority vote of such board, subject to the terms of any applicable contract of employment. In case of suspension or removal, the county manager shall be given a written statement of the reasons for such action. He may, within five days from receipt of such statement, request a public hearing thereon before the county board of commissioners. Upon receipt of such request, a hearing shall be set not earlier than ten days nor later than 15 days from the date of such request. Pending such hearing, and until final action has been taken thereon, the county manager may be suspended from office and all of the duties thereof performed by some other person designated by the county board of commissioners to perform such duties. After such hearing the board of commissioners, by majority vote, may reinstate or terminate the employment of the county manager. The action of the board of commissioners in suspending or removing the county manager shall be final.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 8, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-98. - Political activities.
The county manager shall not engage in or be concerned with any local partisan politics or any local political campaign. He shall not contribute to or solicit funds for any local political purposes from any other person. He shall not appoint any relative as an employee of the county. He shall not be eligible for election as a member of the county board of commissioners for a period of four years after termination of his service as county manager.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 9, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-99. - Compensation.
The county manager shall be paid an annual salary to be fixed by the board of commissioners of the county and there shall be no reduction of such salary during his term of office.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 10, 10-13-03)
Sec. 2-100. - Authority to deal with securities.
The county manager with regard to any SPLOST (special purpose local option sales tax) authorized by voters of Tift County is authorized and empowered for or on behalf of the board of commissioners of the county to establish and maintain one or more accounts, which may be margin accounts, with Nations Bank Capital Markets, Inc. (NCMI) for the purposes of purchasing, investing in or otherwise acquiring, selling (including short sales), possessing, transferring, exchanging, pledging or otherwise disposing of, or turning to the account of or realizing upon and generally dealing in and with any and all forms of securities including, but not by way of limitation, shares, stocks, bonds, debentures, notes, scrip, participation certificates, rights to subscribed, option, warrants, certificates of deposit, mortgages, evidences of indebtedness, commercial paper, foreign exchange contracts, commodities, community futures, certificates of indebtedness and certificates of interest of any and every kind and nature whatsoever, secured or unsecured, whether represented by trust, participating and/or other certificates otherwise, on margin or otherwise. This agreement shall also insure to the benefit of NCMI's successors, by merger, consolidation or otherwise, and assigns, and NCMI may transfer our account to any such successors or assigns.
(Ord. No. 2003-05, § 11, 10-13-03)

DIVISION 3. - COUNTY ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-121. - Roles and responsibilities.
(a)
The primary function of the county attorney is to provide legal advice and representations of the county. As a part of that function, the county attorney's duties include advising and representing the county board of commissioners as the governing authority of the county, the county sheriff's office, the county commissioners' office, clerk of superior court, probate court judge and other officials and employees of the county. The county attorney's duties also include advising the county commissioners and other county officers and employees of their powers and duties under the laws of the state, to prepare ordinances and legal documents, to review proposed contracts, and to represent the county and its officers in legal proceedings.
(b)
The county desires to improve and clarify the proper role and duties of the county attorney in the representation of county officers and employees of the county.
(c)
The county is committed to providing proper and adequate legal representation to each and every official and employee of the county for actions taken pursuant to their official duties.
(d)
Where there is a legal conflict of interest which would prevent the county attorney from providing legal representation or where simultaneous or dual representation by the county attorney or any official or employee of the county and the county commission creates a legal conflict of interest, the county should provide for separate and independent legal representation of the county official or employee.
(e)
The county attorney is a member of the State Bar of Georgia and is therefore sworn to uphold mandated requirements of ethics and professionalism. As such, the county attorney should seek to fully inform the county commission and any affected official or employee of the county of all decisions which effect the resolution of disputes requiring resolution.
(f)
The county attorney is required to fully inform the county commission and effected officials or employees of the county concerning the need of any representation, and most particularly communicate the terms or the proposed terms of the settlement of any dispute.
(g)
The county attorney should counsel and advise the county commission and any effected county official or employee on the value of cooperation and flexibility as a means towards the judicious resolution of disputes.
(Res. of 1-9-95)
