ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 106-1. - Uniform Rules of the Road adopted.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-2—40-6-395, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the county with like effect as if recited in this section.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by state law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-11.
State Law reference— Uniform Rules of the Road, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq.; adoption of uniform rules of the road by local authorities, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372.
Sec. 106-2. - Use of speed detection devices.
The board of commissioners of the county desires and approves the use of speed detection devices by its law enforcement officers.
(Res. of 1-4-88)
Sec. 106-3. - Interference with traffic control or railroad sign or device.
(a)
Generally. No person shall, without lawful authority, attempt to or in fact alter, deface, injure, knock down or remove any official traffic control sign or traffic control device or any railroad sign or signal or any inscription, shield or insignia thereon or any other part thereof.
(b)
Ignoring such signs. No person shall, without lawful authority, drive around or through or ignore any official traffic control device or sign so as to go onto an officially closed highway or road or onto a section of highway or road before it has been officially opened to the public. This subsection shall not apply to law enforcement officers in the performance of their duties, to individuals domiciled or making their livelihood within the affected area or to any person authorized to be in the affected area by the appropriate county or state officer.
(c)
Enforcement. The sheriff of the county and his designated deputies and the county administrator or his designee shall have authority to conduct such investigation as deemed necessary for the enforcement of this section. All formal charges brought under this section shall be initiated by citation and heard before the magistrate court of the county.
(d)
Violation and remedies. Any person convicted of violating any provision of this section shall be deemed to be in violation herewith and shall be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-11. The judge may in his sole discretion suspend, stay or probate any type of punishment imposed under this subsection.
(Ord. of 9-11-95, §§ 2—4)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-26.
Sec. 106-4. - Abandoned motor vehicles.
It shall be unlawful to abandon any motor vehicle as provided in O.C.G.A. Title 40, Chapter 11. Any motor vehicle abandoned within the county shall be disposed of by the proper authorities as provided in O.C.G.A. Title 40, Chapter 11.
(Mo. of 5-13-96)
Secs. 106-5—106-30. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - COUNTY ADMINISTRATIVE BUILDING

Sec. 106-76. - Applicability of division.
This division shall be applicable to all parking spaces and parking lots located between Tift Avenue and Chesnutt Avenue on property owned by Tift County, Georgia, adjacent to the county administrative building and to the parking lot located on the north side of Library Lane and the west side of Tift Avenue.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(2), § 1)
Sec. 106-77. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Parking space means any space marked with traffic paint and designated for parking of a vehicle.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(2), § 2)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 106-78. - Regulations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this division to park longer than 30 minutes in any parking spaces and any open area in the parking lot immediately west of the county administrative building.
(b)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this division to park in any parking space designated for the handicapped unless the car so parking has attached thereto a valid handicapped decal.
(c)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this division to park anywhere except in a marked parking space in the parking areas north and east of the county administrative building and in the parking lot north of Library Lane and west of Tift Avenue.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(2), § 3)
Sec. 106-79. - Enforcement of division.
The county magistrate's court shall have jurisdiction over violators of this division, and all procedures for enforcement of such division shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. Complaints of violations of any provision of this division shall be brought before the magistrate's court by the county sheriff, any deputy county sheriff, the chairman of the board of commissioners or a designated representative of the chairman of the board of commissioners and shall be prosecuted through that court.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(2), § 5)
Sec. 106-80. - Penalty for violation of division.
Any person convicted of violating this division shall be subject to the imposition of a fine not to exceed $50.00.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(2), § 4)
