ARTICLE I. IN GENERAL

Sec. 92-1. - Purpose.
It is the purpose of this article to establish a set of standards for the fabrication, placement and use of signs within the county. These standards are designed to protect and promote the health and safety of persons within the county and to aid and assist in the promotion of business and industry by providing regulations which allow and encourage creativity, effectiveness, and flexibility in the design and use of signs, while avoiding an environment that encourages visual blight.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. I, § 1, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-2. - Definitions.
Abandoned sign means a sign which no longer serves the purpose for which it was intended and has been in such a state for at least six months. It does not include a change of advertiser.
Advertising device means any structure or device erected or intended for the purpose of displaying advertising situated upon or attached to real property.
Area of sign means the area within a continuous perimeter enclosing the limits of writing, representation, emblem or any figure of similar character, together with any frame, other material, open space or color forming an integral part of the display or used to differentiate such sign from the background against which it is placed. The sign area of painted or affixed wall signs when composed of letters only is the sum of the areas of the smallest contiguous rectangles, each capable of containing one such letter. For double-faced signs, only the largest display face shall be measured in computing the sign area.
Banner means a sign, with or without characters, letters, illustrations or ornamentation, applied to cloth, paper or fabric of any kind, with only such material for a backing.
Bench sign means any sign painted on or otherwise attached to a bench or other seat placed in a public right-of-way or meant to be seen by the public and including bus shelter signs as defined in O.C.G.A. § 32-6-51.
Billboard means an outdoor, freestanding sign with a sign area of at least 480 square feet.
Bulletin board means any device which displays announcements of upcoming events excluding direct advertisement.
Bunting means a long colored strip of cloth or other pliable material used for festive decorations and containing no commercial or noncommercial message, logo, or emblem and must be attached to the structure.
County means the County of Tift, a political subdivision of the State of Georgia.
Construction sign means a sign erected and maintained on premises announcing the proposed or existing construction of a building or project.
Elevated sign means a freestanding sign that has at least ten feet of clearance from ground level up to the bottom edge of the message area of the sign.
Facade means any face of a building. The dominant facade of the building is where its principal entrance is located and which may not face the street upon which its legal address is located.
Freestanding sign means a single sign that stands unconnected to any permanent structures.
Ground sign means a freestanding sign that does not have at least ten feet of clearance from ground level up to the bottom edge of the message area of the sign.
Illuminated sign, direct, means a sign illuminated by an internal light source.
Illuminated sign, indirect, means a sign illuminated by an external light source directed primarily toward such sign.
Lumen means a quantitative unit measuring the amount of light emitted by a light source.
Nonconforming sign means any sign that does not conform to the provisions of this chapter.
Normal maintenance and repair means painting and cleaning. This does not include significant structural alteration, modification that requires a building permit or modification that costs in excess of 50 percent of the value of the sign prior to any damage necessitating repair. The value of the sign shall be determined by the replacement cost of the sign, which shall be based on the value shown by the Tift County Tax Assessor. If the tax assessor shows no value, then the value shall be based on the value of the sign as stated on the original permit application.
Off-premises sign means a sign that is not located upon the premises of the business or entity indicated or advertised. This includes products advertised in conjunction with a business or entity. However, off-premises signs may display noncommercial messages or noncommercial speech that is permitted by the First Amendment to the United States Constitution or the free speech clause of the Georgia Constitution.
On-premises sign means a sign, other than a temporary sign, which may identify, advertise or promote a product or service, a person, place, activity, event or idea or any other thing located or obtainable on or at the location where the sign is located; and, in addition, on-premises signs may display noncommercial messages or noncommercial speech that is permitted by the First Amendment to the United States Constitution or the free speech clause of the Georgia Constitution.
Political sign means a sign identifying or urging voter support for a particular election issue, political party, or candidate for public office.
Portable sign means any sign:
(1)
That is capable of being moved or intended to be moved from one location to another; removal of wheels does not indicate permanent;
(2)
That is considered a portable sign in the normal course of commerce, even though the sign may be temporarily or permanently affixed to the ground;
(3)
The design of which indicates it is capable of being moved or intended to be moved from one location to another;
(4)
That is not permanently affixed to a building or the ground;
(5)
That is used in such a manner as to be portable;
(6)
That is attached to, mounted on, painted or drawn on any vehicles, whether motorized or drawn, or any moveable sign structure which may be designed for transport, which is placed, parked on or maintained at one particular location for the express purpose and intent of promotion or for conveying an advertising message for a limited time.
Principal identification sign means a permanent sign with the purpose of identifying the business premises or marketing the product or service obtainable on the premises where the sign is located.
Public art means paintings and works of art on buildings which convey a message; such are considered signs and are governed by this chapter and by other applicable ordinances of the county addressing the need for and the regulation of art in public spaces.
Real estate sign means a temporary sign erected by the owner or his agent, advertising the real property upon which the sign is located for rent, lease or sale.
Sign means a display of a message openly visible to the public attached to a fixed or temporary object as described herein.
Sign face means the part of a sign that is or can be used for advertising purposes.
Snipe sign means a temporary sign or poster affixed to a tree, fence, telephone pole, public benches, streetlights, public property, or public right-of-way, except government and political signs.
Temporary sign means a sign of a nonpermanent design or construction used to announce the occurrence of a limited duration event or activity.
Window sign means a sign installed inside a window and intended to be viewed from outside premises.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. I, § 2, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-3. - Signs not requiring permits.
The following other types of signs are exempted from formal permitting but must be in conformance with all other requirements of this chapter and all other applicable codes and regulations:
(1)
One separate identification sign for each establishment when affixed to a building wall or window and limited to a maximum size of ten square feet. The purpose of the sign is to identify the establishment's street address, suite or unit number, etc.
(2)
Informational bulletin boards for public, charitable, educational or religious institutions when located on the premises of said institution. Bulletin boards may not exceed 32 square feet in area and shall be located at least ten feet from any property line when said institution is located in a residential district.
(3)
Legal notices and official instruments.
(4)
Flags, cumulatively subject to following provisions:
a.
Limited to official flags of each of the following: the United States or other recognized sovereign nation, Georgia, City of Tifton, Tift County, flags which depict affiliation or membership in an off-site nonprofit organization (such as FDIC) and do not convey a commercial message, flags which advertise the business upon which property the flag flies, or any other flag sanctioned by the City of Tifton.
b.
Flags must be flown in accordance with standard protocol and flag etiquette.
c.
Government owned parcels are exempt from total flag area limitations.
d.
In residential districts, flags which do not convey a commercial message and may be attached to a building that is occupied. These typically include family crests and symbols of hospitality or yearly seasons.
(5)
Decorative flags, banners, bunting and temporary directional signs for county-sanctioned celebrations, events, conventions, and commemorations when specifically authorized by the county for a prescribed period of time.
(6)
Holiday lights and decorations.
(7)
Directional signs or symbols (e.g., entrance, exit, caution, slow, or no trespassing) located on and pertaining to a parcel of private property not to exceed three square feet in area.
(8)
Identification signs at the entrance drive of residences, estates and farms which do not exceed three square feet in area, except identification signs for agricultural operations located within AU zoning districts may not exceed 32 square feet in area.
(9)
One nonilluminated real estate sign per street frontage, each containing a maximum of two sign faces back to back, and provided as follows:
a.
Maximum sign area (per face) shall be limited to six square feet in all residential districts, and 32 square feet in all nonresidential districts.
b.
Multiple listing strips, sale pending, and sold signs may be allowed when attached to a real estate sign and are subject to the same maximum area requirements.
(10)
One on-premises open house or open for inspection sign, not exceeding six square feet in area, may be allowed in addition to the above area requirements.
(11)
All real estate signs shall be removed when ownership or occupancy has changed and the property is no longer for sale, rent, or lease.
(12)
Signs for temporary garage sales, yard sales, and the like, located in residential districts and subject to the following provisions:
a.
On-premises signs shall be limited to one per parcel and a maximum area of three square feet.
b.
Similar off-premises signs for directional purposes, not exceeding three square feet in area, may be allowed near street intersections on private property with the consent of the property owner. These signs may not be erected in a public right-of-way. Snipe signs shall be expressly prohibited.
c.
All signs must be removed at the close of the sale.
(13)
One single-sided construction sign per street frontage located on property where building is actually in progress under a current building permit. This shall be a freestanding sign not to exceed eight feet in height, 16 square feet in all residential districts, 32 square feet in all nonresidential districts, and shall be set back at least ten feet from all property lines. The sign may include the names of persons and firms performing services or labor, or supplying materials to the premises. Such sign must be removed when a certificate of occupancy for any building or structure on the premise is issued.
(14)
Window signs which advertise temporary sales and special promotions for activities, services, goods, or products available within the building.
(15)
Signs incorporated on machinery and equipment at the manufacturer's or distributor's level, and which only identify or advertise the product or service dispensed by the machine or equipment. This includes signs customarily affixed to vending machines, newspaper racks, telephone booths and gasoline pumps.
(16)
One directory sign per building, which contains no commercial messages of any kind, which is attached to the building wall and limited to a maximum of 24 square feet.
(17)
Traffic control signs erected on public or private property which meet department of transportation standards and contain no commercial message of any kind. Signs located on private property shall not exceed three square feet.
(18)
Municipal and other governmental signs.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. I, § 3, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-4. - Prohibited signs.
(1)
Any sign, permanent or temporary, located on public right-of-way except as otherwise authorized in this chapter.
(2)
Abandoned signs, which advertise an activity, business, product or service no longer conducted or available. This does not include a change in business and/or owner that may utilize the same structure.
(3)
Bench signs.
(4)
No sign or illumination shall be used, constructed, maintained or located at any location where it may interfere with or obstruct the view of an authorized traffic control device. Said signs shall not be used, constructed, maintained, or located where, by reason of position, shape, wording or color, they may be confused with any authorized traffic control device or emergency vehicle device or markings.
(5)
No sign that interferes with such utilities as water mains and hydrants, sanitary sewerage, gas, electricity, and communications equipment or lines, nor interfere with natural or manmade stormwater drainage facilities.
(6)
Off-premises signs unless otherwise specifically authorized in this ordinance.
(7)
Signs which produce noise or sounds capable of being heard, even though the sounds produced are not understandable sounds.
(8)
Signs not in good repair, specifically including any sign which is in a state of disassembly or any sign which has its internal lighting exposed to view.
(9)
Snipe signs.
(10)
Signs similar to or mimicking official governmental traffic control signs, signals, or emergency vehicle devices.
(11)
Signs that interfere with safe and free ingress and egress of any door, emergency exit, driveway, street, or roadway.
(12)
Signs which conflict with the maximum height requirements imposed by the Federal Aviation Administration or other applicable federal agency charged with imposing maximum height limits in the interest of aviation safety.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. I, § 4, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-5. - Special provisions.
(a)
Political signs.
(1)
All political signs must meet all requirements of O.C.G.A. § 21-2-3, together with all other applicable state law, as amended from time to time, as well as the other provisions of this subsection, whichever are more restrictive.
(2)
The sign shall not be located, in whole or in part, on any public lands or public right-of-way, and shall not be attached to any sign or utility pole or structure, rock, or any form of vegetation.
(3)
Except for signs used for off-premises advertising (billboards), signs shall not exceed six square feet in area in all residential districts and 24 square feet in all nonresidential districts.
(4)
The county shall, without notice, immediately remove or cause to be removed any and all political signs in violation of this section. Signs may be reclaimed within three business days of removal. Unclaimed signs may be disposed of after three business days without liability to the county or its employees, agents or officers.
(5)
All political signs must also meet corresponding sign type regulations for the zoning district in which the sign is located.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. I, § 5, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-6. - Nonconforming signs.
(a)
Nonconforming signs shall not be replaced, expanded or enlarged; although activities associated with normal maintenance and repair may be performed without violating this chapter.
(b)
A nonconforming sign may not be removed and replaced by another nonconforming sign. No changes in the size or shape of a nonconforming sign shall be permitted except to make the sign comply with the requirements of this chapter.
(c)
Signs erected after the adoption of this chapter which do not conform to the requirements set forth within shall be removed.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. I, § 6, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-7. - Variances.
There shall be no deviation from the terms of this chapter unless a variance is granted. A variance may be granted by the Tift County Zoning Appeals Board. If a variance is not granted, the applicant shall be notified in writing. Upon receipt of the written denial, such applicant may appeal the denial to county board of commissioners consistent with the provisions of section 92-63, appeals, of this chapter. For a variance to be granted from this chapter, each of the following must be shown.
(1)
No characteristics contrary to the public interest or this chapter will be promoted.
(2)
No resulting variance shall create an effect or condition contrary to the public safety and welfare.
(3)
Relief shall not impair the purposes of this chapter.
(4)
A unique hardship exists because of an extraordinary and exceptional condition(s) pertaining to the particular piece of property in question because of a characteristic of its size, shape, and topography.
(5)
The condition causing the hardship is unique and is not common to the region in general.
Note: Economic or financial hardship alone may not be sufficient to support the grant of a variance. Signs that are nonconforming as of the effective date of this ordinance shall be granted automatic variance but must be registered in accordance with section 92-6 of this article I. Variance procedures shall apply to new signs erected thereafter.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. I, § 1, 7-10-06)

ARTICLE II. - PERMANENT SIGNS

Sec. 92-21. - General policy.
(1)
In general, commercial and industrial owners are encouraged to develop the public identity of their operations through high-quality and innovative structural design. Signs should adhere to the architectural motifs set forth by the building but should not be relied upon as the sole means of identification.
(2)
Unless exempted by this chapter, all signs shall be constructed of wood, masonry, glass, vinyl, plastic and/or high-grade metal.
(3)
Signs shall follow specific guidelines concerning height, materials, color, and size.
(4)
Signs, except off-premises signs, shall serve only to identify and market the business, structure, activity, or parcel at its location of business.
(5)
Freestanding monument or pedestal signs shall not extend over public property or right-of-way.
(6)
The signing for new businesses within existing projects shall comply with the provisions of this chapter.
(7)
All permanent signs must comply with all requirements of the State of Georgia as expressed in O.C.G.A. § 32-6-51, Signage on public roads, together with all other applicable state laws as amended from time to time, as well as the provisions of this subsection, whichever are more restrictive.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. II, § 1, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-22. - Sign area.
The area of a sign shall be the space within a single, continuous rectangular perimeter measured from the extreme lowest point of the sign to the extreme highest point of the sign and from the extreme left edge to the extreme right edge of the sign face or faces and shall not include the support structure. For double-faced signs, only one display face shall be measured in computing sign area when the sign faces are parallel, or where the interior angle formed by the faces is 60 degrees or less and attached to a common structure. If the two faces of a double-faced sign are of unequal area, the larger of the two faces shall be the area used for calculations.

Sec. 92-23. - Changeable copy.
Unless otherwise specified by this chapter, any sign herein allowed may use manual, automatic, or electrically or mechanically activated changeable copy. No permit is required to change copy on a legally permitted sign.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. II, § 3, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-24. - Setback requirements.
(1)
Signs which do not require a permit shall be located on private property and at least ten feet from any right-of-way line or property line except as otherwise allowed pursuant to this chapter.
(2)
Signs which require a permit:
(a)
Temporary, accessory, and principal use signs shall be at least ten feet from any right-of-way line if such sign is a ground sign. The roadside edge of an elevated sign may abut the edge of the right-of-way if such placement, as determined by the county, does not present a safety hazard.
(b)
Temporary, accessory, and principal use signs shall be at least ten feet from any other structure.
(c)
Freestanding off-premises signs shall be subject to the setback of the zoning district in which they are located unless otherwise specified in this section. No permit shall be issued for any off-premises sign closer than 100 feet to the property line of any residential, church, school, or publicly owned facility.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. II, § 4, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-25. - Illumination.
No sign shall give off light, which glares, blinds, or has any other adverse effect on traffic or adjacent properties. The light from an illuminated sign shall be established in such a way that adjacent properties and roadways are not adversely affected and that no direct light is cast upon adjacent properties and roadways. This shall be determined by measuring the footcandles (lumens per square foot) that fall on adjacent properties. No sign shall exceed one half (0.5) maintained footcandles at any adjacent property line in a residential district and two (2.0) maintained footcandles at any public right-of-way, unless otherwise authorized.
To reduce energy use, all sign illumination systems should incorporate contemporary energy saving techniques and materials. These could include, but are not limited to, solar panels, high-efficiency fixtures, reflective materials, etc. All monument signs which incorporate lighting shall have underground utility service. Commercial signs in residential districts shall not be illuminated.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. II, § 5, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-26. - Identification.
Each sign requiring a permit shall provide at ground level a permanent identification label, viewable to the unaided eye, which states the applicable sign permit number.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. II, § 6, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-27. - Maintenance and appearance of signs.
All signs shall be maintained in good condition so as to present a neat and orderly appearance. Sign faces and structural supports shall be free of any cracking, peeling paint, warping, rusting, and significant color fading. Owners notified in writing of signs needing required maintenance shall have 30 days to complete the specified maintenance and repairs.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. II, § 7, 7-10-06)

ARTICLE III. - TEMPORARY SIGNS

Sec. 92-31. - Portable signs and advertising devices.
Each occupant or tenant of a single commercial or industrial lot may display one temporary on-premises portable sign or any of the following types of signs or advertising devices upon the issuance of a temporary permit: air and gas filled (inflatable) devices, banners, balloons or streamers, searchlights and similar devices. Each occupant or tenant shall be allowed 20 calendar days per calendar year in which to legally display a temporary on-premises portable sign and/or advertising device. It shall be unlawful to display any temporary sign or advertising device without a permit or otherwise in violation of this chapter. Temporary signs shall not be allowed in residential districts.
The temporary permit shall be acquired prior to the display of any temporary sign or advertising device. The permit shall specify the first and last day of the period in which display of the temporary sign or advertising device is permitted. Display of the temporary sign or advertising device shall be allowed beginning at 12:01 a.m. on the first day specified on the permit. The permit shall expire at 11:59 p.m. on the last day specified on the permit. Signs placed pursuant to temporary permits shall be removed on or before the last day of the permit. The permit fee shall be set per the approved schedule of fees.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. III, § 1, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-32. - Semipermanent directional signs.
(1)
Signs which convey directions to a specific place under construction or to specific real estate for sale or to a noncommercial event, and are not intended to be permanent but rather are intended to provide direction to a place or event for a limited period of time shall be allowed in all signage districts.
(2)
Semipermanent directional signs shall be mounted on single or double poles of sufficient strength to safely support the sign and shall not be affixed in any manner to trees, natural objects, street light poles, utility poles or structures, federal, state, county or county signs or poles, or other permanent sign structures.
(3)
Semipermanent directional signs used to convey directions to a noncommercial event or a specific place under construction shall be removed within ten days after the event is over or the project is completed. Semipermanent directional signs providing direction to a subdivision or other real estate for sale shall be removed within ten days after the closing of the sale of that property or the closing of the last lot of the subdivision. No mounting materials may remain in the ground after the sign has been removed.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. III, § 2, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-33. - Temporary local directional signs.
(1)
Temporary local directional signs are short-term signs which convey directions to a specific place or civic event and shall be allowed in all signage districts. Temporary local directional signs do not require a permit or a permit fee.
(2)
Temporary local directional signs may be single or double faced. Each face may be up to four square feet. Temporary local directional signs shall not exceed three feet in height and shall be mounted on an independent single or double pole device. Temporary local directional signs shall not be affixed in any manner to trees, natural objects, utility poles or structures, federal, state, city or county signs or poles, or other permanent signs or sign structures. Temporary local directional signs shall be made of wood, metal, plastic, vinyl, or other durable and waterproof material. No such sign shall be made of paper.
(3)
Temporary local directional signs must be on private property with permission of the owner, except when authorized by the county manager or zoning administrator for city or county sanctioned civic events, signs may be located within public rights-of-way a minimum of ten feet from the paving edge or curb. A distance of 1,000 linear feet must be maintained between all temporary local directional signs. Each temporary local directional sign shall be legibly marked with the name and telephone number of the sign owner or person responsible for any code violations. All temporary local directional signs shall be removed within five days after the event to which it provided direction is completed.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. III, § 3, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-34. - Removal.
The county may, without notice, immediately remove or cause to be removed any and all temporary signs in violation of this section. Signs may be reclaimed within three business days of removal. Unclaimed signs may be disposed of after three business days without liability to the governing authority or their employees.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. III, § 4, 7-10-06)
Secs. 92-35—92-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - BILLBOARDS

Sec. 92-41. - Billboard regulations.
(1)
Billboards must comply with all requirements of the State of Georgia and the Georgia Outdoor Advertising Act (O.C.G.A. § 32-6-70 et seq., as amended from time to time), as well as the provisions of this subsection, whichever are more restrictive.
(2)
No portion of the billboard shall be located within 150 feet of the property line of any residential dwelling, church, school, park or publicly owned building.
(3)
Billboards shall be limited to two sign faces per travel direction.
(4)
Billboards shall be allowed along federal and state highway corridors and at no other locations within Tift County.
(5)
Except along I-75, which shall expressly be subject to the laws, rules and regulations of the State of Georgia, no billboards within the county shall be spaced closer than 300 feet per same side of the roadway. Spacing shall be measured laterally and not radially.
(6)
Subject to the locations for billboards along federal and state highway corridors, billboards shall be allowed as a matter of right in the following zoning districts: C-C, G-B, WLI, and H-I; and, shall be allowed as a matter of special exception in the following zoning district: N-C.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. IV, § 1, 7-10-06)
Secs. 92-42—92-50. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - PERMITTING AND INSPECTION

Sec. 92-51. - General requirements.
Required for all signs except as specifically excluded from the provisions of this chapter, it shall be unlawful for any person to post, display, substantially change or erect a sign or advertising device in any area of the county without first having obtained a sign permit. A change in only the copy of a sign or advertising device shall not constitute a substantial change.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. V, § 1, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-52. - Application.
Applications for sign permits required by this chapter shall be filed by the sign owner or his agent in the office of development support services on forms compiled by the same.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. V, § 2, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-53. - Permit denial and revocation.
The zoning administrator shall deny permits to applicants that submit applications for signs that do not comply with the provisions of this chapter, incomplete applications, and applications containing any false material statements. Should it later be determined that a sign permit was issued as a result of an incomplete application or an application containing false material, or that a permit was erroneously issued in violation of this statement, the zoning administrator shall revoke the permit. The reasons for denial or revocation shall be stated in writing and mailed by certified mail to the address on the permit application within five business days. The permit applicant or holder may then appeal to county council consistent with the provisions of section 92-63 of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. V, § 3, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-54. - Permit fees.
The county shall set fees from time to time. Fees shall be listed in the schedule of fees on file in the office of the county zoning administrator and finance director.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. V, § 4, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-55. - Electrical and structural safety.
All signs shall be built in compliance with all applicable building and electrical codes.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. V, § 5, 7-10-06)
Secs. 92-56—92-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - COMPLIANCE

Sec. 92-61. - General.
If the county believes that a sign is in violation of this chapter, a notice of noncompliance shall be issued to the permit holder if a permit was issued and, if no permit was issued, then to the sign owner if known and, if not known, then to the property owner. The notice of noncompliance shall list the reasons the county believes the sign is in noncompliance. The permit holder or owner, as applicable, of said sign shall have seven days from the receipt of the notice of noncompliance to either:
(1)
Bring the sign into compliance;
(2)
Remove the sign; or
(3)
Come to a written agreement with the county manager or his/her designee(s) as to a specific date in which the sign will be brought into compliance.
In the event the owner does not bring a permanent sign into compliance with the sign ordinance per one of the three items listed above, a citation shall be issued citing the owner for violating the sign ordinance and such citation shall be returnable to the Tift County Magistrate Court for disposition consistent with Tift County ordinances and applicable state law.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. VI, § 1, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-62. - Removal.
The court may order the removal of any permanent sign in violation of this chapter. The cost of removal shall be the responsibility of the sign owner and/or any other party that procured the erection of the sign. Any nonpermanent sign not in compliance with this ordinance is subject to immediate removal by the county and must be reclaimed within three business days of removal.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. VI, § 2, 7-10-06)
Sec. 92-63. - Appeals.
Any adverse decision with respect to an application or a sign permit will be delivered in writing by certified mail to the permit applicant within five business days of the adverse decision. Such notice shall contain the reason(s) for the permit denial or revocation. Thereupon, such permit applicant may then appeal the denial or revocation of the permit to the Tift County Board of Commissioners. Such appeal must be made in writing and filed with the county clerk within 30 days from the date of receipt of notice of such adverse decision. Thereafter, the board of commissioners shall consider such appeal consistent with its rules of procedure. An appeal from the final decision of the board of commissioners shall be taken by certiorari to Tift Superior Court within 30 days of the final decision of the board. The 30-day appeals time shall commence with the next day following receipt by certified mail of the final decision of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 2006-02, Art. VI, § 3, 7-10-06)
