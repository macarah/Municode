ARTICLE IV. - MOBILE HOME PARKS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 38-381. - Purpose and intent.
The regulations and standards established in this article are made for the purpose of promoting the health, safety, morals, and general welfare of the community. They are designed to promote desirable living conditions; to sustain the stability of neighborhoods; to protect property against blight and depreciation; to secure economy in governmental expenditures; to prevent overcrowding of land; to secure safety from fires, flooding, and other dangers; and to lessen congestion in the streets.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. I)
Secs. 38-382—38-400. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LOCATION, SITE AND DESIGN CRITERIA FOR MOBILE HOME PARKS

Sec. 38-401. - Application of article.
The provisions of this division shall apply to all new mobile home parks throughout the county. Before an applicant initiates any construction or development of a proposed mobile home park, or expansion of an existing mobile home park, such applicant shall first submit a proposed mobile home park plan to the county development office pursuant to the requirements of section 38-431 et seq. The county development officer will subsequently review and approve or disapprove the proposed mobile home park as specifically provided for in section 38-431 et seq. The following sections of this division shall serve as criteria and guidelines upon which the county development officer's review and decision shall be based.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.200)
Sec. 38-402. - Park planning site designs and development criteria.
All mobile home parks shall be planned, designed, and developed to meet or exceed conditions and factors conducive to the creation and maintenance of a healthful and safe residential environment. This should be done in such a manner that the quality of the overall environment is optimized and the amenities, facilities, service, and conveniences for the residents are maximized. To achieve these conditions, the design of the park should also be adaptive to the tract of land and its surroundings, especially with respect to topography, trees and vegetation, visual conditions, adjacent land uses, structures and activities. In order to meet these goals, objectives, and purposes all mobile home parks shall meet or exceed the criteria contained in the following sections.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.201)
Sec. 38-403. - Site grading and drainage.
Mobile home parks shall be properly graded and drained to provide adequate drainage of each space and the entire park. Any necessary drainage improvements shall be installed according to plans and specifications approved by the county development officer.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.202)

Sec. 38-405. - Improvements to mobile home spaces.
(a)
Each mobile home space in a park shall have a permanent mobile home stand of suitable material to support the mobile home. The size of the stand shall be equal in length and width to the size of the mobile home located upon it.
(b)
The chassis of each mobile home shall be supported on adequate masonry foundation which shall meet minimum requirements of the state building code.
(c)
Each mobile home space shall be clearly delineated on the ground with permanent markers such as steel pipes at the intersection of lot lines. Each space shall be given a lot number and marked accordingly to be visible from the street providing access thereto.
(d)
Each mobile home shall be anchored to the ground by methods approved by the mobile home manufacturers association.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.204)
Sec. 38-406. - Parking.
Each mobile home space shall be provided two offstreet paved parking spaces. Such spaces shall be provided on or adjacent to each mobile home space.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.205)
Sec. 38-407. - Signs.
The following nonflashing and nonanimated signs are permitted under the conditions specified:
(1)
Park identification sign. One park identification sign shall be permitted not to exceed 24 square feet in area for the purpose of denoting the name of the mobile home park;
(2)
Mobile home identification sign. One mobile home identification sign not exceeding one square foot in area shall be permitted for each mobile home. Such sign shall indicate only the name and address of the occupant of the mobile home.
(3)
Location of signs. No sign shall be located so as to impede vehicular visibility at any intersection of street lines with one another or the edge of driveways with street lines.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.206)
Sec. 38-408. - Streets, utilities and refuse collection.
All mobile home parks shall provide streets, underground utilities, and refuse collection facilities. The quality, design, and location of all such systems shall be done in such a manner so as to maximize the health and safety of occupants and users, and shall conform to the following:
(1)
Streets and access. All mobile home parks shall be provided a street system designed to provide convenient circulation within the park, and shall have unobstructed access to a public street or highway.
a.
Streets proposed for dedication to county shall meet the applicable requirements of section 38-207 et seq.
b.
All private streets within the mobile home park shall meet the applicable requirements of section 38-207 et seq., and shall be maintained in a good state of repair by the owner of the park.
(2)
Street lighting. All interior drives and walkways within the mobile home park shall be lighted at night with electric lamps spaced at intervals of no more than 300 feet. Such lighting shall meet the minimum requirements of the state building code.
(3)
Electrical supply. Each mobile home space shall be provided with an electrical outlet supplying a minimum of 120/240 volts AC at 200 amperes. All outlets and connections shall be grounded and weather-proofed, and installations shall meet the requirements of the state electrical code.
(4)
Water supply. All mobile home parks shall be required to provide an adequate, safe, and potable supply of water that is easily accessible for maintenance and meets the following minimum requirements:
a.
All mobile home parks shall connect to an existing public water system that is adjacent to or nearby the mobile home park.
b.
Each mobile home space shall be provided with at least one frost proof water riser located and arranged to permit convenient attachment to the mobile home. Riser pipes shall extend at least four inches above grade and have a minimum inside diameter of three-quarter inches.
c.
Each mobile home park shall have a minimum of one fire hydrant for every four mobile home spaces.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.207)
Sec. 38-409. - Sewage disposal and treatment.
All mobile home parks shall be required to provide a method of sewage disposal and treatment which meets the minimum requirements of the county health department. Where economically feasible, mobile home parks shall connect to an existing approved public or quasipublic sewer system that is adjacent to or nearby the mobile home park. Where such connection is not feasible, the applicant shall provide a treatment system consisting of one of the following:
(1)
A central treatment system which provides primary, secondary, and tertiary treatment of all effluent from the mobile home park, and meets the minimum discharge requirements of the department of natural resources and environmental protection division in addition to the following requirements:
a.
Such a system shall have a design capacity or the maximum possible population of the park. Such treatment facility shall also be located, protected, and screened in such a way that it will not create a nuisance, health hazard, or detraction from either the park or surrounding property or activities;
b.
All sewer lines shall be placed and covered sufficiently below grade to prevent breakage from pressures exerted from above. The entire system shall provide for a minimum disposal and treatment capacity of 350 gallons per day per mobile home space; or
(2)
Individual septic tank systems which meet the minimum requirements of the county health department and conform to the department of human resources "On-site Sewage Management Systems," Chapter 290-5-26.
a.
Such systems shall be designed so that mobile homes, driveways, parking spaces, streets, underground utilities, water lines, etc. do not cross septic tank and drain field lines nor come closer than ten feet to the septic tank and drain field lines.
b.
Prior to approval of such systems, the developer must submit to the county health department a mobile home park plan which shows proposed layout of lots, street locations, wells, if any, and soil percolation test data which is signed by a registered surveyor or engineer.
c.
The health department, after review of proposed plans, and soil percolation data may require larger lot sizes than the minimum specified in Table 1 of section 38-181.
(3)
All mobile home spaces shall be provided with at least a three-inch diameter sewer riser pipe which shall extend from the sewer connection to at least four inches above the ground in a vertical position. When lots are unoccupied, the risers shall be securely capped.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.208)
Sec. 38-410. - General requirements.
(a)
Service buildings. Service buildings such as shelters, restrooms, management offices and storage facilities are permitted in each mobile home park and are subject to the following requirements:
(1)
Such buildings and their parking areas shall not occupy more than ten percent of the gross land area of the park;
(2)
All service buildings shall be required to conform to the state building codes.
(b)
Minimum size of mobile homes. No mobile home shall be placed in a mobile home park which has less than 600 square feet of floor space and does not contain a built-in bathroom with water closet, lavatory and shower or tub which are in working condition.
(c)
Vector control. Mobile home park owners shall be required to exercise vector control to ensure that no disease-transmitting organism is present in the park. The county health officer, may at any time, require a park owner to take certain measures to ensure healthful and safe conditions.
(d)
Maintenance of mobile homes and mobile home spaces. All mobile homes shall be underpinned, anchored down and be kept in a good state of repair. The area around each mobile home shall be kept clean and free from collections of refuse, rubbish, glass bottles, or other unsightly materials. Unoccupied mobile home spaces shall be kept clear of debris, and shall not be used as temporary storage of damaged mobile homes, automobiles under repair, appliances, or other similar items.
(e)
Mobile home storage. The temporary storage of mobile homes intended for sale or repair shall be confined to mobile home sales lots or warehouses which are separated from the residential areas of the mobile home park.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. II, § 5.209)
Secs. 38-411—38-430. - Reserved.

Sec. 38-431. - Administration and provisions.
The administration of this article shall be the responsibility of the county development office. The provisions of this division are intended as legal guidelines and requirements for the establishment and operation of the administration of this article.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III)
Sec. 38-432. - Development permits required.
It shall be unlawful to develop, construct, locate, operate, expand, or enlarge any mobile home park, without first obtaining a mobile home development permit from the county development office.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III, § 5.300)
Sec. 38-433. - Permits for mobile home parks.
In order to obtain a development permit for a mobile home park, the applicant shall first submit an application and plan to the county development officer according to the following procedures:
(1)
Preapplication review. Prior to the submittal of a preliminary plan for the development of a mobile home park, the applicant shall meet with the health department staff for a review of the location, scope, and nature of the proposed mobile home park.
(2)
Submission of preliminary plan. Prior to making any street improvements or installing any utilities, or other improvements, the developer shall submit five copies of a preliminary plan prepared in accordance to the provisions of this chapter to the county development officer. The preliminary plan shall be drawn to a scale of one inch equals 100 feet and include the following information:
a.
Name of proposed park; name and address of owner; and name and address of surveyor or registered engineer.
b.
Date, north arrow, and scale.
c.
The boundary line of the tract to be developed with accurate linear and angular dimensions.
d.
Sketch map showing general location of the proposed mobile home park.
e.
Topography by contours at vertical intervals of not more than 20 feet.
f.
The location of existing and platted property lines, streets, buildings, water courses, railroads, bridges, water mains, sewers, culverts, drain pipe, and public utility easements, both on the land to be developed and on that portion of the land immediately adjoining which abuts the land to be developed, and any other pertinent characteristics of the land, and the names of adjoining subdivisions or the names of record owners of adjoining parcels of unsubdivided land.
g.
The location and dimensions of proposed streets, mobile home spaces or lots, easements, buildings, parking, recreation areas, yards, and other open spaces.
h.
Utility and drainage plans shall be provided including all information required by the county health department to determine that water, sewer, electricity, refuse collection, and storm drainage improvements will be made and located in accordance with the requirements of this article.
i.
The location, dimensions, and treatment of all required buffer or screening areas.
j.
Other information required by the county development officer to insure compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III, § 5.301)
Sec. 38-434. - Approval/disapproval of preliminary plan.
The county development officer shall have 30 days from the date of preliminary plan submission to approve or disapprove the preliminary plan for the proposed mobile home park. Failure of the development officer to act within 30 days shall be deemed approval of such plan.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III, § 5.302)
Sec. 38-435. - Submission of final plan.
(a)
Approval of the preliminary plan by the county development officer shall authorize the developer to proceed with actual construction and development of the proposed mobile home park. Upon completion of all required improvements, the developer shall submit the original and five copies of a final plan to the county development officer. The final plan shall conform substantially to the preliminary plan and shall meet all requirements of this article. The final plan shall be drawn on reproducible material to a scale of not less than one inch equals 100 feet and shall include the following:
(1)
Name of mobile home park.
(2)
Name and address of owner.
(3)
Scale, north arrow, and date.
(4)
Location sketch map.
(5)
Sufficient data to determine readily on the ground the location, bearing, and length of every street line, boundary line, and lot lines; and location of each mobile home and buildings.
(6)
Final plan of sewage treatment system including location and size of sewer lines, septic tanks, drain fields, and points of discharge (if applicable).
(7)
Final plan of storm sewer system with grade, pipe sizes, and location of outlets.
(8)
Final plan of the water supply system including location and size of water lines, wells, storage tanks, and pumping stations, if any.
(9)
Final plan of buffer areas and screening, if required.
(10)
Final plan of the refuse collection system including size and location of dumpsters.
(b)
The final plan shall also include the following certifications:
(1)
Certification by a registered engineer or surveyor attesting to the accuracy of the plan.
(2)
Certification by the county health department that the water supply and sewerage systems fully meet the requirements of the health department. Any specific lots which do not meet the requirements shall be identified.
(3)
Certification by the county highway department that all streets, and storm drainage improvements have been installed.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III, § 5.303)
Sec. 38-436. - Approval of final plan.
Within 30 days after submission of the final plan together with all required supporting data and certifications, the development officer shall review and approve or disapprove the plan. Failure of the development officer to act within 30 days shall be deemed approval of such plan.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III, § 5.304)
Sec. 38-437. - Development compliance.
(a)
At least 100 percent of each phase of all planned spaces, as well as all streets, utilities, and other required improvements necessary to serve them pursuant to these regulations must be completely constructed according to the mobile home park plan approved by the development officer before the first mobile home location permit will be issued.
(b)
Failure of the developer or applicant to construct this minimum phase within two years after approval by the development officer shall cause such approval to expire.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III, § 5.305)
Sec. 38-438. - Inspection of mobile home parks.
(a)
The health officer and/or development officer or their representative are hereby authorized and directed to make annual inspections to determine the condition of mobile home parks located within the county.
(b)
The health officer and/or development officer or their representative shall have the power to enter at reasonable time upon any private or public property for the purpose of inspecting and investigating conditions relating to the enforcements of this article.
(c)
The health officer and/or development officer shall have the power to inspect the register containing a record of all mobile homes and occupants using the mobile home park.
(d)
It shall be the duty of the owners or occupants of mobile home parks, or of the person in charge thereof, to give the health officer and/or development officer free access to such premises at reasonable times for the purpose of inspection.
(e)
It shall be the duty of every occupant of a mobile home park to give the owner thereof, or his agent or employee, access to any part of such mobile home park or its premises at reasonable times for the purpose of making such repairs or alterations as are necessary to effect compliance with this article, or with any lawful order issued pursuant to the provision of this article.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. III, § 5.306)
Secs. 38-439—38-458. - Reserved.

Sec. 38-459. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section.
Applicant means any person seeking to install a pre-owned manufactured home in the unincorporated area of Pickens County.
Building inspector means the person appointed, employed, or otherwise designated as the director of planning, permits and inspections; the county building official or any of his or her assistants.
Certificate of occupancy means a document issued by the building inspector certifying that a pre-owned manufactured home is in compliance with applicable requirements set forth by this division, and indicating it to be in a condition suitable for residential occupancy.
Install means to construct a foundation system and to place or erect a manufactured home on such foundation system. Such term includes, without limitation, supporting, blocking, leveling, securing, or anchoring such manufactured home and connecting multiple or expandable sections of such manufactured home.
Jurisdiction means the unincorporated areas of Pickens County, Georgia.
Manufactured home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air-conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein; except that such term shall include any structure which meets all the requirements of this paragraph except the size requirements and with respect to which the manufacturer voluntarily files a certification required by the secretary of housing and urban development and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401, et seq.
Pre-owned manufactured home means any manufactured home that has been previously used as a residential dwelling and has been titled.
(Res. of 8-26-2010, § 1)
Sec. 38-460. - Conditions.
All pre-owned manufactured homes located in the jurisdiction shall bear a label certifying it was constructed in compliance with the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401, et seq. (the HUD Code) and shall be installed in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160, et seq.
(Res. of 8-26-2010, § 2)
Sec. 38-461. - Permitting, inspection, certificate of occupancy and fees.
A permit shall be required to locate a pre-owned manufactured home in the jurisdiction.
(a)
Permit. To obtain a permit, applicants shall provide to the county planning and development office the following items:
(1)
A valid location decal must be issued by the county tax commissioner.
(2)
A sanitary permit must be obtained from the county health department for onsite sewage disposal, or in the event that a municipality will provide sewer hook-up, a letter must be provided by that municipality verifying that they will allow public sewer hook-up.
(3)
Satisfactory pre-move inspection, completed either by a county building inspector or by a Georgia licensed residential home inspector. Such pre-move inspection shall demonstrate compliance with section 38-462 of this division.
(4)
An affidavit from the owner of the real property upon which the home is to be placed, stating that the placement of the home is agreeable with them and acknowledgement of responsibility for all aspects of having the home placed on said property.
(5)
An affidavit from the licensed manufactured home mover who moves the home and certifies that the home will be placed in accordance with all applicable rules and laws and that such work will be done in accordance with standard level of care and timeliness that is normally expected of such work.
(6)
The permit and inspection fee required by subsection (d) of this section.
(b)
Inspection. Upon receipt of a permit, applicants may relocate the manufactured home on a residential site for the purposes of inspection. Applicant shall arrange for an inspection to be held once the installation of the manufactured home is complete.
(c)
Certificate of occupancy. A certificate of occupancy shall be issued to the applicant at such time that the building inspector certifies that the requirements of this division have been met.
(d)
Fee. A permit and inspection fee (see planning and development fee schedule) shall be charged to the applicant to cover the cost to the county to process the permit application and inspect the pre-owned manufactured home. Such fee shall cover the initial inspection and one followup inspection. The applicant shall be charged (see planning and development fee schedule) for each additional followup inspection that may be necessary.
(Res. of 8-26-2010, § 3)
Sec. 38-462. - Minimum health and safety standards.
All pre-owned manufactured homes shall comply with the following before being issued a certificate of occupancy by the building inspector:
(a)
HUD Code. Every pre-owned manufactured home located in the jurisdiction shall be in compliance with the Federal Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act, 42 U.S.C. 5401-5445 (the HUD Code) and shall not have been altered in such a way that the home no longer meets the HUD Code.
(b)
Interior condition. Every floor, interior wall, and ceiling of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in sound condition. Doors and windows shall be operable, watertight and in good working condition. The floor system shall be in sound condition and free of warping, holes, water damage, or deterioration.
(c)
Exterior condition. The exterior of all pre-owned manufactured homes shall be free of loose or rotting boards or timbers and any other conditions that might admit rain or moisture to the interior portions of the walls or to occupied spaces. The exterior siding shall be free of rot and rust. Roofs shall be structurally sound and have no obvious defects that might admit rain or cause moisture to collect on the interior portion of the home.
(d)
Sanitary facilities. Every plumbing fixture, water, and waste pipe of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in a sanitary working condition when properly connected, and shall be free from leaks and obstructions. Each home shall contain a kitchen sink. Each bathroom shall contain a lavatory and water closet. At least one bathroom shall contain a tub and/or shower facilities. Each of these fixtures shall be checked upon being connected to ensure they are in good working condition.
(e)
Heating systems. Heating shall be safe and in working condition. Un-vented heaters shall be prohibited.
(f)
Electrical systems (switches, receptacles, fixtures, etc.) shall be properly installed and wired and shall be in working condition. Distribution panels shall be in compliance with the approved listing, complete with required breakers, with all unused openings covered with solid covers approved and listed for that purpose. The home shall be subject to an electrical continuity test to assure that all metallic parts are properly bonded. Each pre-owned manufactured home shall contain a water heater in safe and working order.
(g)
Hot water supply. Each home shall contain a water heater in safe and working condition.
(h)
Egress windows. Each bedroom of a manufactured home shall have at least one operable window of sufficient size to allow egress if necessary.
(i)
Ventilation. The kitchen in the home shall have at least one operating window or other ventilation device.
(j)
Smoke detectors. Each pre-owned manufactured home shall contain one operable battery-powered smoke detector in each bedroom and in the kitchen, which must be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations.
(Res. of 8-26-2010, § 4)
Sec. 38-463. - Enforcement.
(a)
Permanent connection to utilities shall not be approved until the building inspector has issued a certificate of occupancy.
(b)
Owners of pre-owned manufactured homes that are not in compliance upon a third inspection shall have their permit revoked and shall be required to remove the home from the jurisdiction at their own expense.
(Res. of 8-26-2010, § 5)
Sec. 38-464. - Penalties.
Failure to remove a pre-owned manufactured home from the jurisdiction upon failure to receive a certificate of occupancy shall be punishable by a fine of up to $1,000.00. Each day any violation under this division continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(Res. of 8-26-2010, § 6)
Sec. 38-465. - Location.
All manufactured homes not in a mobile home park shall comply with section 38-181(5) of this Code.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. IV, § 5.404; Res. of 5-25-2001, § 1)
Sec. 38-466. - Tiedown requirements.
All manufactured homes shall be secured in accordance with the tiedown requirements approved by the mobile home manufacturers association.
(Ord. of 8-8-1997, ch. V, art. IV, § 5.405)
