DIVISION 1. - VICIOUS AND DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 14-31. - Responsibility for interpretation.
If any question arises concerning the application of the rules, regulations or any other provision of this article, the county magistrate court shall be responsible for such interpretation.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 5.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-32. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal means any domesticated animal, including, but not limited to, any dog or cat.
Board means the county department of health board.
Court means the county magistrate court.
Dangerous dog means any dog, after the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived, that, without provocation, bites a human being on public or private property, or any dog that has entered upon the property of someone other than its owner and, without provocation, has threatened harm to any person or animal by growling or other menacing conduct, or inflicts injury to any animal, and such dangerous dog returns to such property subsequent to the lapse of 24 hours after such dangerous dog's owner has been given actual notice of such threatening or injurious conduct.
Dog control officer means an individual selected by the board of commissioners pursuant to the provisions of this article and O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22(c) to aid in the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this article.
Local government means the county government or the local municipal government.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping or having custody or control of a vicious or dangerous dog or animal.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a vicious or dangerous dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent such dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height, and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection for the dog from the elements.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of:
(1)
Any state, county or municipal law enforcement agency;
(2)
Any county or municipal animal control agency;
(3)
Any county board of health;
(4)
Any federal, state or local court, including, specifically, the county magistrate court; or
(5)
A dog control officer.
Severe injury means any physical injury to a human being that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery, or a physical injury that results in death.
Vaccination means protection against rabies by inoculation with antirabies vaccine given by a licensed veterinarian in a sufficient amount to provide immunity from rabies for a minimum of one year. The term "vaccination" is synonymous with inoculation in this article.
Vicious dog means any dog that, according to the records of the dog control officer or the county magistrate court:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being, without provocation, on public or private property at any time after the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived; or
(2)
Bites, attacks or endangers the safety of a human being, without provocation, after the dog has been classified as a dangerous dog.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 5.01(A), 7-6-1995)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 14-33. - Exclusions.
A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties shall not be a vicious or dangerous dog. A dog shall not be a vicious or dangerous dog if the injury inflicted by the dog was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort, or was tormenting, abusing or assaulting the dog, or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused or assaulted the dog, or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 5.01(B), 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-34. - Jurisdiction.
(a)
The county's jurisdiction for the enforcement of this article shall be the unincorporated area of the county, except as otherwise provided by subsection (b) of this section.
(b)
The county may contract or enter into agreements with other local government entities for joint dog control services or for the provision of dog control services required by this article and for the separate or joint use of personnel, facilities and equipment used in the provision of such services.
(c)
The board of commissioners shall designate an individual to carry out the duties of a dog control officer. one individual may carry out the duties of a dog control officer for more than one local government pursuant to a contract or agreement as set forth in subsection (b) of this section. The board of commissioners may assign the additional duties of dog control officer to any officer or employee of the county who is subject to the jurisdiction of the board of commissioners. With the consent of the sheriff, the board of commissioners may assign the additional duties of dog control officer to the county sheriff or to a sheriff's deputy. With the consent of the county board of health and the rabies control officer, the board of commissioners may assign the additional duties of dog control officer to a rabies control officer appointed under O.C.G.A. § 31-19-7. A person carrying out the duties of a dog control officer shall not be authorized to make arrests unless the person is a law enforcement officer having the powers of arrest.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 6.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-35. - Designation of magistrate court.
(a)
The board of commissioners designates the county magistrate court to conduct any and all hearings as may be required under the provisions of this article. The court is authorized and shall have jurisdiction to conduct such hearings and determine matters provided in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24 and in this article.
(b)
The board of commissioners may alternatively, by appropriate resolution, provide for the creation of an animal control board to hold the hearings provided for in this article and in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24, or may assign such duties to the county department of health board. If an animal control board is created, the animal control board may hear and determine matters provided for in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24. No member of the animal control board may participate in a hearing on any matter in which such member previously participated in the classification of the dog at issue. An animal control board may be created jointly by two or more local governments.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 6.01, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-36. - Investigation; classification.
(a)
Upon receiving a report of a vicious or dangerous dog within a dog control officer's jurisdiction from any person, law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, county board of health or other county officer or agency, the dog control officer shall make such investigation and inquiry with regard to such report as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this article. All calls by any person regarding an animal which is subject to this article shall first be made to the appropriate official of the county health department. If such agent shall not be available, calls may be made to the county manager's office, and if there is no one to take the call at the county manager's office, calls may be made to the county sheriff's office.
(b)
When a dog control officer classifies a dog as a vicious dog or reclassifies a dangerous dog as a vicious dog, the dog control officer shall notify the dog's owner, in writing, by certified mail, to the owner's last known address, of such classification or reclassification. Such notice shall be complete upon its mailing. The notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's findings that formed the basis for the dog's classification. The notice shall also state that if the owner desires to contest such classification, he/she must give notice, in writing, within ten days, and a hearing will be held in the county magistrate court.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 7.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-37. - Procedures for classification.
(a)
The procedures provided for in this section for the owners of dangerous dogs must be carried out as a necessary condition for the enforcement of the provisions of this division against such dangerous dog owners. The procedures provided for in this section for the owners of vicious dogs shall not be an essential element of any crime provided for in this article.
(b)
When a vicious or dangerous dog is classified as such, the dog control officer shall notify the dog's owner of such classification.
(c)
The classification notice to the owner of such vicious or dangerous dog shall:
(1)
Be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the dog owner's last known address;
(2)
Include a summary of the dog control officer's findings that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a vicious or dangerous dog;
(3)
Be dated and shall state that the dog's owner, within ten days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that such dog is a vicious or dangerous dog;
(4)
State that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the county magistrate court and shall specify such court as the applicable agency which will conduct the hearing;
(5)
State that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination that such dog is a vicious or dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes under this article on a date specified in the notice, which shall be after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing;
(6)
Include a form to request a hearing before the court and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering such request to the court;
(7)
Provide that the dog's owner shall submit with the written notice of appeal a check or money order in the amount of forty-nine dollars and fifty cents ($49.50) for the court costs of such appeal and a warning that the dog's owner will be liable for all costs and expenses if the appeal is denied.
(d)
When the court receives a request for a hearing as provided in subsection (c) of this section, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The court shall notify the dog's owner, in writing, by certified mail, of the date, time and place of the hearing, and such notice shall be mailed to the dog's owner at least ten days prior to the date of the hearing. At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence, and the court shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as may be reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify or overrule the dog control officer's classification of such dog.
(e)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the court shall notify the dog's owner, in writing, by certified mail, of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a vicious or dangerous dog, the notice shall specify the date upon which that determination is effective.
(f)
If the determination by the court is that such dog is a vicious or dangerous dog, the owner of such dog shall pay to the general fund of the county the additional sum of seventy-five dollars ($75.00) within 30 days of such determination to defray the costs to the county.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 8.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-38. - Certificate of registration; insurance.
(a)
It is unlawful to have or possess within the county a vicious or dangerous dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with the provisions of this section.
(b)
In addition to the requirements of subsection (c) of this section, the dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a vicious or dangerous dog if the owner of such dog presents to the dog control officer or the dog control officer otherwise finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the vicious or dangerous dog; and
(2)
The posting of the premises where the vicious or dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a vicious or dangerous dog on the property. Such sign shall substantially comply with the state department of natural resource's uniform symbol for the purpose of implementing O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25(b)(2)(A).
(c)
In addition to the requirements of subsection (b) of this section, the owner of a vicious dog shall present to the county department of health board evidence of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00) issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of the vicious dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the vicious dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00), or more, issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in the state payable to any person injured by the vicious dog.
(d)
The owner of a vicious or dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if such dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died or has been sold or donated. If such dog has been sold or donated, the owner of such dog shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address and telephone number of the new owner of such dog within seventy-two (72) hours of such sale or donation.
(e)
The owner of a vicious or dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer if such owner is moving the dog from this jurisdiction. The owner of a vicious or dangerous dog who is a new resident of the county shall register such dog as required in this section within 30 days after becoming a resident of the county. The owner of a vicious or dangerous dog who moves from one jurisdiction within the state to this county shall register the vicious or dangerous dog in this county within ten days after becoming a resident of this county.
(f)
Issuance of a certificate of registration or the renewal of a certificate of registration by the county does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements set forth in subsections (b) and (c) of this section are maintained by the owner of a vicious or dangerous dog on a continuous basis following the date of the issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate.
(g)
A dog control officer is authorized to make whatever inquiry is deemed necessary to ensure compliance with the provisions of this article. Local law enforcement agencies of local governments and the county sheriff's department shall cooperate with dog control officers in enforcing the provisions of this article.
(h)
There shall be an annual fee in the amount as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges in the office of the county clerk to register vicious and dangerous dogs, payable to the clerk of the board of commissioners. Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of the annual renewal of a certificate of registration, the clerk shall require evidence from the owner or the dog control officer shall make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the vicious or dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner of such dog is continuing to comply with other provisions of this article.
(i)
The owner of a vicious or dangerous dog who desires to give away or donate such dog to another must first inform the other party of the dog's status under this division.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 9.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-39. - Restraint.
(a)
It is unlawful for an owner of a vicious dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person. The muzzle shall be made in a manner that will not cause injury to the dog or interfere with its vision or respiration, but will prevent it from biting any person.
(b)
It is unlawful for the owner of a dangerous dog to permit such dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless such dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 10.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-40. - Confiscation; extermination.
(a)
A vicious dog shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer, law enforcement officer or another person authorized by the dog control officer, if the:
(1)
Owner of the dog does not secure the liability insurance or bond required by section 14-38(c);
(2)
The dog is not validly registered as required by section 14-38;
(3)
The dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure; or
(4)
The dog is outside a proper enclosure in violation of section 14-39(a).
(b)
A dangerous dog shall be confiscated in the same manner as set forth in subsection (a) of this section for a vicious dog, if the dog is:
(1)
Not validly registered as required by section 14-38;
(2)
Not maintained in a proper enclosure; or
(3)
Outside a proper enclosure in violation of section 14-39(b).
(c)
Any dog that has been confiscated under the provisions of subsection (a) or (b) of this section shall be returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon the payment of reasonable confiscation costs. If the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within ten days of the date the dog was confiscated, such dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(d)
The dog control officer, the rabies control officer or any county agent, officer or employee shall be authorized to exterminate any rabid animal found within the unincorporated area of the county.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 11.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-41. - Violation; penalties.
(a)
Jurisdiction over the criminal offenses set forth in this article may be in the county magistrate court. Any person violating the terms of this article may be punished, for each offense, by a fine of not less than fifty dollars ($50.00) nor more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and/or imprisonment in the county jail, not to exceed 12 months. The sentence imposed may also include any restitution due to any victim of the vicious or dangerous dog together with any restitution due to the county. Prosecution under this section is not exclusive. Any person violating this article or the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq. may be prosecuted in either the county magistrate court or the county superior court.
(b)
The owner of a vicious dog who violates the applicable provisions of section 14-38 or 14-39, or whose vicious dog is subject to confiscation under section 14-40(a), shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this subsection, for the second conviction a fine of not less than five hundred dollars ($500.00) shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00) shall be imposed.
(c)
The owner of a dangerous dog who violates the applicable provisions of section 14-38 or 14-39, or whose dangerous dog is subject to confiscation under section 14-40(b), shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this subsection, for a second conviction a fine of not less than one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00) shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than three hundred dollars ($300.00) shall be imposed.
(d)
If an owner of a dog who has a previous conviction for a violation of this article or a previous conviction for violating the criminal provisions order of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28, knowingly and willfully fails to comply with the provisions of this article, and if the owner's vicious or dangerous dog attacks or bites a human being under circumstances constituting another violation of this article, then, in addition to any penalties provided in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28, the vicious or dangerous dog involved shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer, law enforcement officer or another person authorized by the dog control officer and placed in quarantine for the proper length of time as determined by the county board of health, and, thereafter, the vicious dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner. The owner of the vicious or dangerous dog shall be liable for all reasonable costs associated with the enforcement of this subsection.
(e)
No owner of a vicious dog shall be held criminally liable under this article for injuries inflicted by such owner's dog to any human being while on the dog owner's property.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly make a false report of a vicious or dangerous dog to any local government agency or official.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 13.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-42. - Authority to go upon private property.
Employees and agents of the county, and persons acting under their direction, control or supervision, shall have the right to go on private property within the unincorporated areas of the county to the extent that it is reasonably necessary for the enforcement of the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 14.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-43. - Supplement to state law.
(a)
The provisions of this article are in addition to and supplementary to any previously existing laws of the state, and shall not be construed to supersede such previously existing laws.
(b)
It is the intention of this article to establish minimum standards and requirements for the control of vicious and dangerous dogs and to provide for certain crimes for violations of such minimum standards and requirements.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 15.00, 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-44. - Liability for damages.
It is the intent of the board of commissioners that the owner of a vicious or dangerous dog, rabid animal or uninoculated animal shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog or animal. Under no circumstances shall the county or any employee or official of the county, or any cooperating local government which enforces or fails to enforce the provisions of this article be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog that has been identified as being a vicious or dangerous dog, or by a dog that has been reported to the proper authorities as being a vicious or dangerous dog, or by a dog that the county has failed to identify as a vicious or dangerous dog, or by a dog which has been identified as being a vicious or dangerous dog but has not been kept or restrained in the manner set forth in sections 14-38 and 14-39, or by a vicious or dangerous dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required in section 14-38(c), or by a rabid or uninoculated animal.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 16.00, 7-6-1995)

DIVISION 2. - RABIES CONTROL

Sec. 14-66. - Inoculation; tags.
(a)
All domestic pets within the unincorporated area of the county over 90 days old shall be inoculated annually so as to provide immunity from rabies for a period of at least one year. All inoculations shall be administered by a licensed veterinarian or an authorized representative of the county department of health. All domestic pets over 90 days old must have a rabies tag.
(b)
Rabies tags and a certificate of inoculation will be provided by a veterinarian upon inoculation of a domestic pet and collection of the fee as prescribed by the board of commissioners. The veterinarian shall remit the fees collected to the county on a monthly basis. The application for the rabies tag shall state the name and address of the owner and the name, breed, age, color and sex of the domestic pet. The rabies tag shall expire on March 31 of each calendar year and shall be renewed on or before April 1 of each year. Each rabies tag shall be so contrived as to permit it to be securely attached to the domestic pet's collar or harness and shall have its respective number, the year in which it was issued and the words "Dade County" indented and impressed thereon. The design or color of the rabies tag shall vary from year to year to permit easy ascertainment as to whether the tag is current.
(c)
The fee charged for such rabies tag shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk. The fee may be changed by the board of commissioners by appropriate resolution.
(d)
A valid rabies tag shall be securely attached to the domestic pet's collar or harness, which collar or harness must be secured to and on the domestic pet at all times and it shall be the duty and responsibility of the owner of such domestic pet to see that the rabies tag is so attached and secured at all times.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, §§ 12.00(A)—(C), (E), 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-67. - Confinement; surrender of suspicious domestic pets.
A domestic pet that has rabies or shows signs of having rabies, and every domestic pet bitten by another domestic pet afflicted with rabies or that has been exposed to rabies shall be confined at once in a secure place by the owner or keeper of such domestic pet. A person who knows or has reason to know a domestic pet is infected with rabies or has been exposed to rabies shall immediately notify the dog control officer, the local health department or the county sheriff's department as to the place where the domestic pet is confined and shall surrender such domestic pet to the proper authorities.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 12.00(D), 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-68. - Notification; observation.
The owner of a domestic pet that bites or scratches a person shall promptly notify the rabies control officer of the county health department. A domestic pet that bites or scratches a person shall be securely confined by its owner for not less than ten days or for such longer period as may reasonably be required by the rabies control officer. The owner of a domestic pet that bites or scratches a person shall surrender such domestic pet to the rabies control officer or dog control officer if the dog control officer shall deem it necessary to impound such domestic pet for a period of observation.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 12.00(F), 7-6-1995)
Sec. 14-69. - Responsibility for impoundment costs.
If a domestic pet is impounded as set forth in section 14-68 at a place other than the property of the owner, all reasonable and necessary expenses of such impoundment shall be borne by the owner of such impounded domestic pet.
(Ord. No. O-1-95, § 12.00(G), 7-6-1995)

ARTICLE III. - CRUELTY TO ANIMALS

Sec. 14-91. - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply to the interpretation and enforcement of this article:
Animal means any live creature, both domestic and wild, commonly kept as a pet, including but not limited to, dogs and cats. The term shall not include any fish or any pest that might be exterminated or removed from a business, residence, or other structure.
Adequate food and water means food and water sufficient in amount and appropriate for the particular animal to prevent starvation, dehydration, or a significant risk to the animal's health from lack of food and water.
Adequate ventilation means indoor housing facilities for pets which are adequately ventilated to provide for the health of the animals at all times. Such facilities must be provided with fresh air either by means of windows, vents, fans, or air conditioning, and should be ventilated so as to minimize drafts, odors, and moisture condensation.
Cruelty to animals means actions or omissions causing unjustifiable physical injury, pain, suffering, or death of an animal, including the failure to provide adequate food, water, ventilation, and sanitary conditions for animals, and that are inconsistent with what a reasonable person of ordinary knowledge would believe is the normal requirement and feeding habit for such animal's size, species, breed, age, and physical condition.
Sanitary conditions means conditions of good order and cleanliness, and to the maximum degree practical, remove and destroy agents injurious to health.
( Ord. No. 09-03-15 , § 1, 9-3-2015)
Sec. 14-92. - Violations.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any person:
(1)
Committing cruelty to an animal as defined in this article;
(2)
Leaving an animal in a parked vehicle when temperatures inside the vehicle may adversely affect the animal's health and welfare; or
(3)
Leaving an animal in any vehicle where it must stand, sit, or lie on extremely hot or cold surfaces, including but not limited to truck beds.
( Ord. No. 09-03-15 , § 2, 9-3-2015)
Sec. 14-93. - Enforcement of this article.
The county animal control officer and other county law enforcement officers are hereby authorized to enter upon any premises or parcel of land for the purpose of seizing and impounding any animal found therein or thereon in violation of this article. The animal control officers and the appropriate enforcement officers of the county are further authorized to take all steps that are reasonably necessary for the removal of any animal from a parked vehicle in violation of this article by any reasonable means under the circumstances after making reasonable efforts to locate the owner of the motor vehicle.
( Ord. No. 09-03-15 , § 3, 9-3-2015)
Sec. 14-94. - Penalties.
In addition to penalties which may be imposed under state law for the violation of O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4, any violation of the provisions of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor and shall be punishable by penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-19 of the Code of Ordinances of Dade County, Georgia. In the event any provision of the article has been violated or is being violated, in addition to other remedies, the county may institute an injunction, mandamus, or other appropriate action or proceeding to correct or stop such violation. Such penalties may also include, but not be limited, the forfeiture of such animals seized as part of the enforcement of this article and the restitution to the county for costs incurred in impounding the seized animals.
( Ord. No. 09-03-15 , § 4, 9-3-2015)
