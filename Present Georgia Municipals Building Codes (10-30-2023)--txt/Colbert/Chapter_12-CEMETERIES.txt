Chapter 12 - CEMETERIES[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Georgia cemetery and funeral services act of 2000, O.C.G.A. § 10-14-1 et seq.; cemeteries, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-4 et seq.

Sec. 12-1. - Lot sizes.
(a)
Family lots. Family lots are of four graves and with a measurement of 11 feet by 20 feet.
(b)
Family lots for couples only. Family lots for two graves measure 11 feet by ten feet.
(c)
Individual lots. Individual lots for one person will measure 11 feet by five feet.
(Ord. of 8-16-1968, § a)
Sec. 12-2. - Maintenance and care.
(a)
Owner will be responsible for his lot beginning at the time of purchase
(b)
The city is not responsible any further, as to the care, maintenance, improvements, and anything that affects its condition in any way.
(Ord. of 8-16-1968, § b)
Sec. 12-3. - Cemetery committee.
It will be the duty of the mayor and council to appoint someone to be responsible for the sale of lots and caring for the unsold lots. This can be any member of the governing body or any citizen of the city. This appointment shall be made at the first meeting of each year and shall remain in effect for one year, at which time he can be reappointed or replaced.
(Ord. of 8-16-1968, § d)
Sec. 12-4. - Funds from sale of lots.
Funds may be used for any purpose of the city, provided that all obligations of indebtedness have been satisfied during the fiscal year of requirement.
(Ord. of 8-16-1968, § e)
Sec. 12-5. - Driveways in cemetery.
It will be the duty of the council to see that all driveways are kept in good condition and usable, expending sufficient funds, if necessary.
(Ord. of 8-16-1968, § f)
Sec. 12-6. - Disputes, disagreements, deeds lost.
(a)
Unless deeds are recorded in the county clerk of court's records, city records will be valid.
(b)
The cemetery committee will, at the time of purchase of the lot, immediately record, on ledger, or the block, the name of the purchaser and give deed from the city for the lot purchased. This deed will serve as a receipt for funds given to the city clerk along with the name of purchaser.
(Ord. of 8-16-1968, § g)
Sec. 12-7. - Grave liners; vaults.
All burials in the city cemetery after the date of the enactment of the ordinance from which this section is derived shall require that the casket shall be placed in some type of grave liner or grave vault made of concrete, steel or other metal, or some other material designed to prevent the collapse of the grave site. Said grave liner or vault should surround the casket on all sides including the top and bottom and be sealed in such a way as to prevent dirt from entering the area around the casket. Grave lines or vaults made of wood or similar material subject to rot shall not be acceptable.
(Ord. of 10-6-2014)
Sec. 12-8. - Cemetery prices.
Cemetery prices shall be set by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. of 4-6-2015; Ord. of 9-16-2016)
