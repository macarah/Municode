ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 66-1. - Purpose.
The purpose of this chapter is to establish a system of personnel administration for the city consistent with the following principles:
(1)
Recruiting, selecting and advancing employees on the basis of their relative ability, knowledge, and skills, including open consideration of qualified applicants for initial appointment;
(2)
Establishing pay rates and schedules consistent with the principles of comparable pay for comparable worth;
(3)
Training employees, as needed, to ensure high quality performance;
(4)
Retaining employees on the basis of the competency of their job performance, correcting inadequate or inconsistent job performance and separating or where possible, reassigning employees whose inadequate or incompetent job performance cannot be corrected;
(5)
Establishing a uniform system of handling grievances and appeal rights based on the principles of due process;
(6)
Ensuring equal opportunity to applicants and employees, alike in all respects, without regard to race, sex, age, color, national origin, political, disability or religious affiliation; and
(7)
Ensuring that employees are protected against coercion for partisan political purposes and are prohibited from using their official authority for the purpose of interfering with or affecting the result of an election or a nomination for local municipal office.
(Code 1981, § 17-1; Ord. No. 1987-4, 12-7-1987)
Sec. 66-2. - Council to amend policies.
The city council shall, from time to time, promulgate, adopt and amend, by resolution, such policies as it deems necessary to carry into effect the purposes of this article. Such adopted policies shall have the same force and effect as an ordinance.
(Code 1981, § 17-2; Ord. No. 1987-4, 12-7-1987)
Sec. 66-3. - City manager to administer personnel system; duties.
(a)
The city manager shall be responsible for the administration of the personnel system adopted herein and shall direct all of its administrative and technical activities. It shall be the city manager's duty to:
(1)
Draft or have drafted new or changed personnel policies for submission to the council;
(2)
Advise the council on work force needs and utilization;
(3)
Foster and develop programs for the improvement of employee effectiveness including training, safety, health, counseling and services;
(4)
Establish and maintain records of all employees in the city government service, in which there will be set forth as to each employee the class, title, pay status and other relevant data;
(5)
Apply and carry out this article and the policies, rules and regulations adopted and promulgated hereunder, and to perform any other lawful act which may be necessary or desirable to carry out the purposes and provisions of this article.
(b)
The city manager or the city manager's authorized agent, shall be responsible for certification of the payroll vouchers that the persons named therein have been appointed and employed in accordance with the provisions of this article and the policies adopted hereunder. No city disbursing and auditing officer shall make or approve, or take any part in making or approving, any payment for personal services to any persons holding a position in the city unless such payroll voucher or account of such pay bears the certification of the city manager or the city manager's authorized agent.
(Code 1981, § 17-3; Ord. No. 1987-4, 12-7-1987)
Sec. 66-4. - Applicability of system; exceptions.
The provisions of this chapter shall be applicable to all employees and all tenured positions in the city now existing or hereafter established, except as follows:
(1)
The members of the council and other elected officials;
(2)
Members of boards and commissions, the city attorney, judges and city manager;
(3)
Persons employed to make or conduct a temporary or special inquiry, investigation or examination on behalf of the council, committee thereof, or the city manager;
(4)
Substitute, seasonal or otherwise temporary positions unless specifically covered by action of the council or city manager;
(5)
Positions which by their nature (confidential or key policy-making) cannot or should not appropriately be included in the classification system are also exempted.
(Code 1981, § 17-4; Ord. No. 1987-4, 12-7-1987)
Sec. 66-5. - Reciprocal agreements; intergovernmental cooperation.
(a)
The city is authorized and empowered to enter into reciprocal agreements upon such terms as may be agreed upon, for the use of equipment, materials, facilities and services with any public agency or body for purposes deemed of benefit to the public personnel system.
(b)
The city manager may cooperate with other governmental agencies charged with public administration in conducting personnel tests, recruiting personnel, training personnel, establishing lists from which eligibles shall be certified for appointment and for the interchange of personnel and their benefits.
(Code 1981, § 17-5; Ord. No. 1987-4, 12-7-1987)
Sec. 66-6. - Public records.
All personnel records, except examinations, employee performance reports, personnel histories and such other as may be specified by the council or city manager as confidential, shall be public records and shall be open for public inspection during office hours and reasonable times in accordance with such procedures as the city manager may prescribe.
(Code 1981, § 17-6; Ord. No. 1987-4, 12-7-1987)
Secs. 66-7—66-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - RETIREMENT[2]

--- (2) ---

Sec. 66-31. - Purpose; declaration of policy.
It is declared to be the policy and purpose of the city to extend, under the agreement entered into by the state and the federal social security administration, to the employees and officials of the city and its instrumentalities, not excluded by law or by this article, the benefits of the system of old age and survivors' insurance as authorized by the Federal Social Security Act, and the state enabling act, and amendments thereto. In pursuance of said policy, and for that purpose, the city shall take such action as may be required by applicable state or federal laws and regulations.
(Code 1969, § 2-46; Code 1981, § 17-40; Ord. of 5-16-1974, § 1)
Sec. 66-32. - Authority and directive to execute agreements.
The mayor or other chief executive officer of the city is authorized and directed to execute all necessary agreements and amendments thereto with the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia for coverage of said employees and officials as provided for in this article, to include any employees that are in positions covered by a public retirement system, in the manner by said law provided; said additional employees being namely the police and firefighters.
(Code 1969, § 2-47; Code 1981, § 17-41; Ord. of 5-16-1974, § 2)
Sec. 66-33. - Withholdings from compensation.
Withholdings from salaries or wages of employees and officials for the purpose provided for in this article are authorized to be made in the amounts and at such times as may be required by applicable state and federal laws and regulations, and shall be paid over to the state agency.
(Code 1969, § 2-48; Code 1981, § 17-42; Ord. of 5-16-1974, § 3)
Sec. 66-34. - Contributions by city.
There shall be appropriated by the city from general funds such amounts at such times as may be required by applicable state and federal laws and regulations for employer's contributions, administrative expenses and a guaranty fund to ensure that the state will not incur any debt or loss as a result of the agreement referred to in this article. Such funds shall be paid over to the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia in accordance with regulations established by said agency.
(Code 1969, § 2-49; Code 1981, § 17-43; Ord. of 5-16-1974, § 4)
Sec. 66-35. - Records and reports.
The city shall keep such records and make such reports under this article as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1969, § 2-50; Code 1981, § 17-44; Ord. of 5-16-1974, § 5)
Sec. 66-36. - Unauthorized employees excluded.
There is excluded from this article any authority to make any agreement with respect to any position, or any employee or official not authorized to be covered by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1969, § 2-51; Code 1981, § 17-45; Ord. of 5-16-1974, § 6)
