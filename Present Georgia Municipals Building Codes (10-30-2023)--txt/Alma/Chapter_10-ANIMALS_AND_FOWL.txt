Sec. 10-1. - Animal control officers—Animal control unit established; ex officio officers.
There is hereby created an animal control unit for the city which shall be under the direct supervision of the chief of police, who shall, with the approval of the city manager, hire such animal control officers as may be authorized by the city council from time to time. All fulltime police officers shall be ex officio animal control officers and be under the supervision of the chief of police on all matters pertaining to animal control.
(Code 1981, § 4-1; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 1; Ord. No. 1991-4, 10-7-1991)
Sec. 10-2. - Same—Authority to issue summonses.
Officers of the city police department, and officers of the animal control unit, are hereby authorized to issue a summons relative to the enforcement of this chapter.
(Code 1981, § 4-2; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 17)
Sec. 10-3. - Same—Records.
The animal control unit shall keep accurate written records showing the final disposition of all animals impounded by such unit, the date of impoundment, the description and other identifying data of the animal, the fees collected, the disposition of the animal and the date thereof, which record shall be open and available for inspection to citizens and residents of the city at a reasonable place during the regular business hours for a period of 24 months after the date of impoundment.
(Code 1981, § 4-3; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 14)
Sec. 10-4. - Same—Interference therewith.
Any person who interferes with any animal control officer in the performance of said officer's duty as provided for in this chapter shall be subject to punishment as described in section 1-8.
(Code 1981, § 4-4; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 11)
Sec. 10-5. - Enforcement—Complaint of citizen.
(a)
Any resident of the city may file a written complaint with the animal control unit against the owner of any animal who has violated the provisions of this chapter. Said complaint shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name and residence of the owner of such animal;
(2)
The facts surrounding the alleged violation;
(3)
The date of said violation.
(b)
The complaint shall be sworn to before an officer authorized to administer oaths and said complainant shall be required to be and appear in the municipal court at the time and place set by the animal control unit to testify as to facts alleged in said complaint.
(Code 1981, § 4-5; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 12)
Sec. 10-6. - Same—Procedure.
The provisions of this chapter shall be enforced by the officers of the animal control unit and the police department as established by the governing authority of the city. Upon information known to, or a sworn complaint lodged with, any officer or member of the animal control unit that any owner of a dog, cat or other domestic pet is in violation of this chapter, a summons may be served, by an officer of the animal control unit requiring the owner of said animal to appear before the municipal court of the city on a day and time certain, to stand trial for violation of this chapter. If such violation of this chapter has not been witnessed by an officer, a subpoena may be issued to the person supplying the information on which a formal charge may be made to appear at said time and date to testify on behalf of the city. In the event that the owner of any dog is not known and such dog is upon the streets, alleys, sidewalks, school grounds, public places or premises of another, as prohibited by this chapter, upon complaint made or information known, to the animal control unit, it shall be the duty of the code enforcement department to immediately take possession of such dog and impound it according to the rules and regulations provided herein.
(Code 1981, § 4-6; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 16)
Sec. 10-7. - Same—Summons in lieu of impoundment.
The animal control officer, at such officer's discretion, may decide not to impound a dog or other animal found in violation of this chapter, and may return the animal to its owner or keeper and issue or cause to be issued a summons directed to the owner or keeper of the dog or other animal to appear before the municipal court on a certain day to stand trial for the violation of this chapter.
(Code 1981, § 4-7; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 7)
Sec. 10-8. - Impoundment—Authorized.
All dogs, cats or other animals found to be in violation of this chapter shall be subject to impoundment by the city and, to this end, all animal control officers shall have the right to go upon private property as is reasonably necessary to enforce the provisions of this chapter.
(Code 1969, §§ 4-4, 4-22; Code 1981, § 4-9; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 6)
Sec. 10-9. - Same—Notice.
Notice of the impounding of any dog, cat or other animal shall be given to the owner or keeper of such animal, if known and if the owner is unknown, then notice shall be given by the posting of a notice on the bulletin board at the city hall or the city pound, which notice shall contain a general description of the dog, cat, or other animal impounded, with the kind, color and sex thereof.
(Code 1969, § 4-5; Code 1981, § 4-10; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 8)
Sec. 10-10. - Same—Holding period; redemption; disposition of unclaimed animals.
(a)
Any impounded dog, cat or other animal may be disposed of on the seventh day after the giving of notice to the owner or keeper as set forth in section 10-9, unless before such time the owner shall have claimed the animal and paid a fee to the city for impoundment, plus a per-day fee for the feeding of said animal plus all other costs incurred by the city in the impoundment of said animal; provided, however, no such animal shall be released by the city until said animal shall have been vaccinated as set forth in this chapter. Said animal shall be disposed of by the city in as humane and painless a manner as possible. The animal control unit may offer for adoption to any person, any dog, cat or other animal unredeemed or unclaimed by the owner after five days notice upon the payment of the required fees for impoundment and board.
(b)
The amounts of the fees described in this section shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(Code 1969, § 4-5; Code 1981, § 4-11; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 9)
Sec. 10-11. - Bird sanctuary—City designated.
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city is designated as a bird sanctuary.
(Code 1969, § 4-1; Code 1981, § 4-12)
Sec. 10-12. - Same—Birds protected; abating nuisance condition.
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, shoot or attempt to shoot or molest in any manner any bird or wild fowl or to rob birds' nests or wildfowl nests. However, if starlings, English sparrows, jays, crows, sharp-shinned hawks or Cooper's hawks are found in such numbers in any locality that they constitute a nuisance, or a menace to health, property or other birds, they may be destroyed, dispersed or controlled by the city.
(Code 1969, § 4-2; Code 1981, § 4-13)
Sec. 10-13. - Stock at large.
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow such person's cattle, hogs or other stock to run or be at large in the city.
(Code 1969, § 4-3; Code 1981, § 4-14)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-3.
Sec. 10-14. - Fowl at large.
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any domestic fowl to allow the same to run or be at large within the city off the premises of the owner thereof.
(Code 1969, § 4-6; Code 1981, § 4-15)
Sec. 10-15. - Hogs prohibited; exceptions.
No person shall keep in the city any hog or other swine for any purpose, except that this prohibition shall not apply to livestock markets and other related businesses duly licensed by the city, or as to hogs or swine kept or used in conjunction with a farm provided that they are far enough from any residence so that they do not cause a nuisance by odor or other means and at least 300 feet from adjoining landowner's property.
(Code 1981, § 4-16; Ord. of 6-12-1972, § 1)
Sec. 10-16. - Keeping horses.
Horses may be kept in the city provided that they are kept on a field that contains at least one acre per horse and provided that the horse is kept at least 100 feet from any residence.
(Code 1981, § 4-17; Ord. of 6-12-1972, § 3)
Sec. 10-17. - Number of dogs and cats limited.
(a)
Pemitted number of dogs. No resident of the city may keep more than two dogs upon their residence at any one time; provided, however, this section shall not apply to puppies under the age of three months.
(b)
Housing authority properties. No dogs shall be permitted to be kept on any property owned by the housing authority of the city. This includes dogs inside or outside of any apartment or other residence.
(c)
Pemitted number of cats. No resident of the city may keep more than two cats upon their residence at any one time; provided, however, this section shall not apply to kittens under the age of three months.
(Code 1981, § 4-18; Ord. of 6-12-1972, § 2; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 15; Ord. No. 1996-1, 2-20-1996; Ord. of 7-10-2006; Ord. No. 2008-19, 7-10-2006)
Sec. 10-18. - Nuisances—Generally; dogs at large.
(a)
Generally. The owner or keeper of each dog, cat or other domestic pet within the city shall be responsible for keeping it from becoming a nuisance or from endangering or injuring any other person or property, and to that end, each dog shall at all times:
(1)
Be kept in an enclosed structure from which it cannot escape;
(2)
Be tied on the owner's property so that it cannot reach the property of any adjoining owner or any public right-of-way;
(3)
When allowed off the property of the owner, be accompanied by the owner or other responsible person and be restrained on a leash held by such owner or other person; or
(4)
Be kept within a vehicle being driven or parked on the streets, or within the property limits of its owner or keeper.
(b)
Housing authority properties. No animal, domestic or otherwise, shall be kept by anyone on any property owned by the city housing authority. The keeping of any domestic or other animal on any property of the housing authority shall be deemed to be a nuisance.
(Code 1981, § 4-19; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 3; Ord. No. 1996-1, 2-20-1996)
Sec. 10-19. - Same—Examples.
It shall be unlawful for any owner to fail to exercise proper care and control of such person's animals to prevent them from becoming a public nuisance. Excessive, continuous or untimely barking, molesting passersby, chasing vehicles, habitually attacking other domestic animals, trespassing upon school grounds, or trespassing upon private property in such manner as to damage property, shall be deemed a nuisance.
(Code 1969, § 4-24; Code 1981, § 4-20)
Sec. 10-20. - Vaccination; tag.
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any dog or cat or any other animal which is kept as a pet, to keep or maintain such animal within the city unless it shall have been vaccinated by a licensed veterinarian with anti-rabies vaccine, within one year preceding the date on which such domestic or domesticated animal is kept or maintained. It shall be unlawful for any person to maintain or keep any such animal on any premises within the city unless such animal wears a collar or harness securely attached to its body to which shall be securely attached a tag issued for such animal by a licensed veterinarian showing that the animal has been, within the preceding 12 months, vaccinated against rabies.
(Code 1969, §§ 4-21—4-23; Code 1981, § 4-21; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 2)
Sec. 10-21. - Vicious animals.
Any animal that has attacked or bitten a person or other animal without provocation or has attempted to bite a person or other animal without provocation shall be deemed a vicious animal by any officer of the animal control unit and it shall be unlawful for any person to cause, permit, accompany or be responsible for the presence of any vicious animal on the streets or public places of the city or allow the same to run on the premises of another, at any time, unless and in addition to the other requirements of this chapter, such animal shall be securely muzzled to effectively prevent it from biting any person or other animal.
(Code 1981, § 4-22; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 4)
State Law reference— Mercedes' Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-40 et seq.
Sec. 10-22. - Confinement and disposition of biting animal.
All dogs, cats or other animals known to have bitten a person shall be confined to the animal control unit or veterinarian's hospital for a period of ten days. All expenses incurred for boarding of such animal for such period of time shall be paid by the owner or keeper of said animal. If said animal is not claimed by its owner or other responsible person within a period of 15 days, said animal may be destroyed by the animal control unit in the manner hereinafter set forth.
(Code 1981, § 4-23; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 5)
Sec. 10-23. - Disposal of injured, etc., animals.
The animal control unit shall have the authority to humanely dispose of dogs, cats or other domestic pets which are diseased or injured and lying in the streets, or public ways of the city, whenever it shall appear that any such animal is injured and suffering great pain, and whenever such animal does not bear a tag identifying the owner, and no determination as to the owner of such animal can be made with a reasonable inquiry or investigation.
(Code 1981, § 4-24; Ord. of 1-8-1979, § 13)
Sec. 10-24. - Animal quarters.
All animals and animal quarters are kept in a clean and sanitary condition and so maintained as to eliminate objectionable odors.
(Code 1981, § 4-25; Ord. of 7-10-2006; Ord. No. 2008-19, 7-10-2006)
