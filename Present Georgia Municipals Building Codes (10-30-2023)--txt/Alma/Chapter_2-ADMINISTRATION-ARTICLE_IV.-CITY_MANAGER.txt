ARTICLE IV. - CITY MANAGER

Sec. 2-109. - Appointment; bond.
Before taking office the city manager shall furnish a fidelity bond, with a corporation authorized to do business in the state, as surety, in an amount established by council, but not less than $100,000.00, conditioned upon the faithful performance of such officer's duties. Such bond shall be filed with the city clerk after being approved by the city council.
(Code 1969, § 2-20; Code 1981, § 2-40; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-110. - Duties as prescribed; enforcement of laws.
The city manager shall have and exercise all powers and duties assigned to such officer by the Charter, and such other authority as may be granted by the city council. Such officer shall be charged with the enforcement of all laws and ordinances within the city insofar as their enforcement is within the powers of the city.
(Code 1969, § 2-21; Code 1981, § 2-41; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-111. - General duties; residence requirement.
It shall be the duty of the city manager to execute and administer the policies, ordinances, rules, and resolutions of the city government as fixed by the city council. During such officer's tenure of office such officer shall reside within the city, and shall devote fulltime to duties as city manager.
(Code 1969, § 2-22; Code 1981, § 2-42; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-112. - Assisting council and committees—Making documents available.
It shall be the duty of the city manager to make such person's self available, during normal working hours, to the council and the various committees of the council and to furnish them such reports, either oral or written, as they may from time to time require. The city manager shall at all times make available to the committees of the council all of the books, papers, records, maps, plats, vouchers, plans, specifications, and other documents or data concerning any function of the city and shall cause to be made copies thereof for the use of such committee member, as they may require.
(Code 1969, § 2-25; Code 1981, § 2-45; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-113. - Same—Inspection tours; availability for advice and consent; calling special meetings; attending meetings.
The city manager shall make such inspection tours with the committees of the council as they may require, and shall at all times make such officer's self available to the council or its committees for advice and consent. The city manager shall, at the direction of the mayor or a majority of the council, call a special meeting of the council, and shall attend all regular and special meetings of the same, having with such officer such reports and data as are necessary for prompt understanding of any problem or situation before the city council.
(Code 1969, § 2-26; Code 1981, § 2-46; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-114. - Regular employees.
The city manager shall hire and discharge all regular employees authorized by the city council to be hired, at such compensation as may be set or authorized by the city council. All employees shall perform their duties subject to the direction of the city manager or that of the superior to whom such manager may assign such employee. Before the city manager shall hire or discharge any regular employee such officer shall first consult with the council committee member in charge of the department for which the employee is to be hired or discharged and shall advise the mayor and city council of their action at the next regular meeting of the council.
(Code 1969, § 2-27; Code 1981, § 2-47; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-115. - Examining, etc., contracts.
(a)
The city manager shall examine all proposed contracts to which the city may be a party, and may sign on behalf of the city any contract authorized by the city council, except where the council directs that some other officers shall do so.
(b)
It shall be the duty of such manager to see to it that all terms of any contract to which the city is a party are fully performed by all parties thereto.
(Code 1969, § 2-29; Code 1981, § 2-48; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-116. - Purchases.
(a)
The city manager shall be general purchasing agent of the city, and except where specific provision to the contrary is made by law or by the council, the city manager shall make all purchases of supplies, materials and equipment authorized by the council, in the manner prescribed by, and subject to the limitations imposed by law. No purchases shall be made or obligation incurred except upon authorization by the council, and no expense shall be incurred if the amount of the expense is not covered by an unexpended appropriation as required by law.
(b)
All purchases shall be made according to the purchasing policy as established by the city manager and/or mayor from time to time. Any purchase in excess of $2,000.00 must be made on the requisition of the department head, approved by the city manager and may be made only after the solicitation and receipt of competitive bids, and then only after the approval of the city council. Any expenditure over $200.00 requires the mayor and/or city manager's approval.
(Code 1969, § 2-30; Code 1981, § 2-49; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1; Ord. No. 1984-5, § 1, 10-1-1984)
Sec. 2-117. - Appropriation and levies.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the city manager to cause to be prepared each year the annual appropriation ordinance in time for consideration and enactment by the council during the quarter preceding the fiscal year, and to prepare the tax levy ordinance for passage, and file a certified copy thereof with the county tax commissioner.
(b)
The city manager shall keep current accounts of the amounts appropriated and the amounts spent out of each appropriation, showing the unexpended appropriations at all times.
(Code 1969, § 2-31; Code 1981, § 2-50; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-118. - Keeping accounts; collecting money.
It shall be the duty of the city manager to keep current accounts showing at all times the fiscal condition of the city, including the current and anticipated expenses, appropriations, cash on hand and anticipated revenue of all municipal funds and accounts; and such officer shall see to the collection of all money due the city.
(Code 1969, § 2-32; Code 1981, § 2-51; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-119. - Collection of business licenses.
The business license clerk shall be responsible for the collection of all business licenses.
(Code 1969, § 2-37; Code 1981, § 2-52; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-120. - Inventories of property.
The city manager shall keep a current inventory showing all real and personal property of the city and its locations, and shall be responsible for the care and custody of all such property, including equipment, buildings, parks and all other city property, which is not by law assigned to some other officer or body for care and control.
(Code 1969, § 2-33; Code 1981, § 2-53; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-121. - Preparing reports; publications.
It shall be the duty of the city manager to see to the publication of all notices, ordinances or other documents required by law to be published, and to prepare or cause to be prepared, all reports which the city or any of the officials thereof are required by law to prepare.
(Code 1969, § 2-34; Code 1981, § 2-54; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-122. - Furnishing election materials.
The city council, in its discretion, may authorize the city manager to prepare for the city clerk all notices, ballots and election supplies necessary in connection with municipal elections.
(Code 1969, § 2-35; Code 1981, § 2-55; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-123. - Records; certificates.
The city clerk shall prepare and have custody of all municipal records required by law to be kept by the city and not by a specific officer. Where the law makes it the duty of a specific officer to keep or maintain records the city clerk shall make available to such officer all necessary clerical assistance for the preparation of such records and shall make available a place for the custody of such records. And in compliance herewith, the city clerk shall make available to the clerk, stenographic or clerical assistance necessary for the preparation of the journal of council proceedings, and the record of ordinances and all other documents which by law the clerk is required to keep; and he/she shall perform similar service for the treasurer in the maintenance of bond registers, and all other records or documents which by law the treasurer is required to keep or prepare.
(Code 1969, § 2-36; Code 1981, § 2-56; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
State Law reference— Georgia Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq.
Sec. 2-124. - Water and sewer department duties.
(a)
The public works director shall, with the advice and consent of the council committee member for the water and sewer department, prepare such reasonable rules and regulations as are needful for the conduct of the water and sewer department.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the public works director to keep a running inventory of all pipes, fittings, supplies, machinery, and equipment used in connection with the water and sewer department and to run such cost analyses of the various operations therein conducted as are reasonable and needful for the clear understanding of the operation of such department.
(c)
The public works director shall be responsible for the collecting of all fees and assessments in connection with sewerage connections and water services as fixed by the council. In the event the city performs a service for a patron of the water or sewer services wherein a fee has not been fixed by the council, the city manager is authorized to fix a reasonable fee for such work done or services performed, collect therefor, and report same to the council at the next regular or special meeting.
(d)
The public works director may authorize city employees to make regular water and sewer connections at the property line of any patron of either system, but the public works director shall not cause to be run or permit to be run and constructed any sewer line or water line on private property and shall not extend over city property a water line longer than 300 feet or a sewer line longer than 300 feet from then existing facilities, without approval of the water and sewer council committee member.
(Code 1969, § 2-37; Code 1981, § 2-57; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-125. - Work on private property; competition with private enterprise; lending equipment.
In the absence of direction from the city council by resolution or ordinance, in no event shall the city manager cause city employees to do work of any nature on private property, nor shall the city manager at any time place the city in competition with private enterprise, nor shall the city manager lend or allow the use of city machinery or equipment by third parties.
(Code 1969, § 2-38; Code 1981, § 2-58; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
