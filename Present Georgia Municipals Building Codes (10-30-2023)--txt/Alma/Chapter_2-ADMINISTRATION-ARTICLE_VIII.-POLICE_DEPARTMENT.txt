ARTICLE VIII. - POLICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-288. - Created; composition.
There is hereby created a police department for the city which shall consist of the chief of police, who shall be ex officio superintendent of police, and such other members as may be provided for by the council.
(Code 1981, § 2-120; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § a)
Sec. 2-289. - Chain of command.
Levels of rank and authority may be created as needed at the discretion of the chief of police. Job descriptions and authority will be assigned for each position created.
(Code 1981, § 2-121; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § b)
Sec. 2-290. - Appointments and promotions.
The chief of police shall be appointed by the council. All other appointments to or promotions within the department shall be made as provided by law.
(Code 1981, § 2-122; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § c)
Sec. 2-291. - Records and reports; supervision of department.
The chief of police shall keep such records and make such reports concerning the activities of the department as may be required by statute or by the city council or by the city manager. The chief shall be responsible for the performance by the police department of its functions, and all persons who are members of the police department shall serve subject to the orders of the chief of police.
(Code 1981, § 2-123; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § d)
Sec. 2-292. - Responsibility of chief of police.
The chief of police shall be specifically charged with the supervision of all members of the department and shall be held strictly responsible for the conduct and efficiency of all such members for the general good order of the department, and shall be responsible to the mayor and the city council and the city manager. The chief shall have regular hours of duty during each working day. The chief shall have a thorough knowledge of all sections of the city, and when going on duty shall immediately familiarize such officer's self with all police business transacted since such officer's last tour of duty. It shall be the duty of the chief to inform the commanding officer coming on duty of all important matters which may require attention. The chief shall diligently inquire into all complaints and charges of laxity and misconduct in the performance of duty by members under such officer's command and shall take such steps as set forth in these rules and regulations, or as such officer may deem necessary when not in conflict with these rules and regulations.
(1)
The chief of police in the exercise of such officer's duties shall have the power to establish and enforce rules and regulations for the government of the members and employees of the department, which rules shall, however, be consistent with the ordinances of the city and the laws of the state.
(2)
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to cause the public peace to be preserved and to enforce all ordinances of the city of which the police department must take cognizance. The chief of police shall receive and give proper attention to complaints for all persons referred to such officer and perform such other duties as may be required from time to time as prescribed by the rules and regulations.
(3)
The chief of police shall have full power and authority to reprimand any member of the police department, and to file charges against any member of the police department with the city manager or city council for a hearing, for nonconformance with these rules and regulations.
(4)
The chief of police shall have custody and control of all books, records, machines, tools, apparatus or equipment of every kind necessary for the use of the department.
(5)
Further, all the rules and regulations set forth in sections 2-293 through 2-295 which are applicable to the members of the police department are also applicable to the chief of police.
(Code 1981, § 2-124; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 1)
Sec. 2-293. - Rules and regulations—Promulgation.
The city manager or chief of police may make or prescribe such rules and regulations as shall be deemed advisable; such rules, when approved by the city council, shall be binding on such members. Such rules and regulations may cover, besides the conduct of the members, uniforms and equipment to be worn or carried, hours of service, vacations and all other similar matters necessary or desirable for the better efficiency of the department.
(Code 1981, § 2-125; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § i)
Sec. 2-294. - Same—Posting.
Rules and regulations shall be posted in the police department quarters.
(Code 1981, § 2-126; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 32)
Sec. 2-295. - Same—Other laws; enforcement of rules.
(a)
In addition to the rules and regulations set forth in this section and sections 2-293 and 2-294 as established by the city manager and the city council, the laws of the state, ordinances of the city, and federal laws pertaining to and affecting police officers shall be the governing factors in the conduct of all members of the police department, who shall at all times be subject to the same.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the chief of police and the city manager to enforce the rules and regulations.
(c)
A copy of the rules and regulations shall be delivered as promptly as possible by the chief of police to each member of the police department.
(Code 1981, § 2-127; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 36)
Sec. 2-296. - Special police officers.
Any person of good character having duties which require it may be appointed a special police officer by the mayor, provided that, except upon authorization by council, no such special police officer shall be entitled to any compensation from the city, nor shall such special police officer be considered an employee of the city by reason of such appointment. Special police officers shall obey all rules of the police department applicable to them and may be removed from their position by the chief at any time.
(Code 1981, § 2-128; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § j)
Sec. 2-297. - General duties of members.
It shall be the duty of the members of the police department to see to the enforcement of all of the ordinances of the city and all statutes applicable therein; and to preserve order and prevent infractions of the law and arrest violators thereof.
(Code 1981, § 2-130; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 3)
Sec. 2-298. - Serving process.
No member of the police department, except the chief, shall serve any process except on command of the chief of police.
(Code 1981, § 2-132; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § f)
Sec. 2-299. - Appearing as witnesses.
Every member of the police department shall appear as witnesses whenever it is necessary in a prosecution for a violation of an ordinance or of any state or federal law. No such members shall retain any witness fee for service as witness in any action or suit to which the city is a party; any fees paid for such services shall be turned over to the chief of police, who shall deposit the same with the city treasurer or proper official.
(Code 1981, § 2-133; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § h)
Sec. 2-300. - False reports.
No member of the department shall knowingly make a misstatement or make a false report.
(Code 1981, § 2-138; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 10)
Sec. 2-301. - Care of equipment.
(a)
Members and employees of the police department shall be responsible for the good care of department property whether fixed or movable, assigned to their use or keeping, and shall promptly report to their commanding officers the loss or damage to, or unserviceable condition of such property. Roughness or carelessness in the handling of such property will not be tolerated and will be made the subject of charges of superior officers who will be responsible for the strict enforcement of this rule.
(b)
Any member found guilty of damages to or destruction of property, either through willfulness or negligence, shall be required to pay all costs of repairs or replacements thereto, besides suffering any penalty for the violation of this section.
(Code 1981, § 2-143; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 16)
Sec. 2-302. - Lost equipment.
All members of the department shall immediately report to the chief of police the loss of any city equipment that has been furnished to the individual.
(Code 1981, § 2-144; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 17)
Sec. 2-303. - Contraband; lost, stolen or abandoned property—Custody.
The chief of police shall have the custody of all lost, abandoned or stolen property and contraband recovered in the city. The council shall direct the deposition of all such property and no such property may be removed, sold or destroyed except upon direction of the council.
(Code 1981, § 2-148; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § k)
Sec. 2-304. - Same—Disposition by officers.
It shall be the duty of all officers, upon the finding or recovery of any contraband, abandoned vehicle or other abandoned property or stolen property, to bring such vehicle or property to the police station, placing such vehicle or property in the care of the chief of police.
(Code 1981, § 2-149; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 27)
Sec. 2-305. - Accepting rewards or presents—Reward for services in line of duty; condition to acceptance.
No member of the department shall receive or accept any reward from any person for any services rendered in the line of his/her duty. However, as to all rewards received for members of the department, where the individual has contributed outstanding work in the recovery of property, etc., a written report thereof will be made to the secretary of the city council outlining the case in question and submitting the reward to the city council to be acted upon by it.
(Code 1981, § 2-150; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 28)
Sec. 2-306. - Same—Gifts or presents.
No member of the police department shall accept any fee, reward or gift of any kind, from persons arrested or from any friend in such arrested person's behalf while such person is in custody, or after release or discharge. Members of the department shall not accept presents or gifts from other members of the department, groups, clubs, associations, societies, citizens, lodges or political parties. Members of the department shall not solicit contributions for a fund for the purposes of presenting other members of the department with a gift, unless authorized by the chief of police.
(Code 1981, § 2-151; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § 29)
Sec. 2-307. - Same—Permission.
The chief of police, for meritorious service rendered by any member of the police force in the due discharge of duty, may permit such member to retain for such member's own benefit, so far as may be permitted by law, any reward or present tendered therefor, and it shall be cause of removal for any member of the force to receive any such reward or present without notice thereof to the city council and without the city council's permission.
(Code 1981, § 2-152; Ord. of 10-7-1974, § m)
Secs. 2-308—2-334. - Reserved.
