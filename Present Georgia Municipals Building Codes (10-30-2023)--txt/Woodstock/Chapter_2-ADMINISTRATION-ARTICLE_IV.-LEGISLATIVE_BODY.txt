DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS OF GOVERNING BODY

Sec. 2-181. - Time and date of meetings; special meetings.
(a)
The city council shall hold regular meetings on the second and fourth Monday of each month beginning at 7:00 pm unless otherwise ordered by the city council. A regular work session will be held the third Monday of each month beginning at 7:00 pm unless otherwise ordered by the city council.
(b)
Special meetings of the council may be held on call of the mayor or two members of the council. Notice of such meetings shall be served on all other members by email at the email address designated by the council member and by telephone personally at the telephone number designated by the council member. In the event the staff member is unable to contact a council member to provide such notice by telephone personally the staff member shall, in the presence of another staff member for verification purposes, leave at least three messages, or attempt to leave at least three messages about the called meeting, not less than 15 minutes apart at least two hours in advance of the called meeting. Such notice shall not be required if the mayor and all councilmen are present when the special meeting is called. Notice of any special meeting may be waived in writing before or after such a meeting and attendance at the meeting shall also constitute a waiver of notice of any special meeting. Only the business stated in the call may be transacted at the special meeting, except by unanimous consent of all members present. With such consent, any business which may be transacted in a regular meeting may be conducted at the special meeting.
Personally: Shall mean a two-way verbal conversation between a staff member (either the mayor, city manager, city clerk or a staff member assigned the duty of contacting the elected officials and the elected official).
Telephone personally: Shall mean a two-way conversation between two parties (one being the elected official and one being a staff member assigned the duty of notifying the elected officials)— a voice mail or message left with spouse/family member is not sufficient.
Failure to personally contact each elected official as required by the Charter and this policy will cause the meeting to be cancelled or delayed until all members are notified.
(c)
Each councilmember shall attend each meeting of the city council, unless he is prevented by some unavoidable circumstance.
(d)
All meetings of the city council shall be open to the public except as otherwise provided by state law.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-1; Ord. No. 94-07-11, 7-11-1994; Ord. No. 04-03-23, 3-23-2004; Ord. of 11-28-2006(1) ; Ord. No. 2008-0001, 3-10-2008)
Charter reference— Regular and special meetings, § 2.31.
State Law reference— Open meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-182. - Presiding officer; rules of order.
(a)
The mayor, or in his absence, the mayor pro tempore, shall take the chair at the hour appointed for any regular, temporarily adjourned, special or called meeting and shall immediately call the council to order.
(b)
Except as otherwise provided in this section, Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised, shall govern the conduct of council meetings.
(c)
The presiding officer shall decide all questions of order, but any councilmember who is dissatisfied with the decision may appeal to the city council in the manner provided by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised, for appealing decisions of presiding officers.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-2)
Charter reference— Rules of procedure, § 2.32.
Sec. 2-183. - Roll call; quorum.
(a)
Before proceeding with the business of the council, the city clerk shall enter the names of those present in the minutes.
(b)
A majority of all the members elected to the council shall constitute a quorum at any regular or special meeting of the council and an affirmative vote of a majority of such number shall be sufficient to permit the conduct of all business except that for which a larger vote has been mandated by this code.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-3)
Charter reference— Quorum; voting, § 2.33.
Sec. 2-184. - Agenda; order of business.
(a)
An agenda shall be drawn up for all council meetings. The deadline for subjects being placed on the agenda shall be seven days prior to the date of the meeting. Persons interested in addressing the council must place their name, address and the nature of their business on the agenda.
(b)
The business of the city council shall be taken up in the following order unless otherwise determined by the mayor:
(1)
Meeting called to order by the mayor.
(2)
Pledge of allegiance and prayer.
(3)
Recognition of guests and visitors.
(4)
Consent agenda.
(5)
Petitions and communications.
(6)
Approval of minutes.
(7)
New business.
(8)
Informational/emergency items.
(9)
Mayor and city manager's moments.
(10)
Public comment.
(11)
Adjournment.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-4; Ord. No. 91-05-20, 5-20-1991; Ord. No. 04-03-23, 3-23-2004)
Sec. 2-185. - Minutes.
(a)
The city clerk shall be present at each meeting to record the minutes.
(b)
The yea and nays of the members present shall be recorded on the minutes on any question taken upon the request of a councilmember.
(c)
Unless a reading of the minutes of a council meeting is requested by a councilmember, such minutes may be approved without a reading if the city clerk has previously furnished each member with a copy.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-5)
Sec. 2-186. - Executive session.
The city council may, upon call therefor by the presiding officer or upon motion duly carried by a councilmember, meet in executive session to discuss matters exempted from state law open meeting requirements. Attendance at such sessions shall be limited to the mayor and members of the council and such invitee's as shall be invited with the unanimous consent of the city council.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-6)
State Law reference— Exceptions to open meeting requirements, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-3.
Sec. 2-187. - Compensation and expenses.
(a)
The mayor may receive as compensation for his services an amount of not more than $850.00 per month. Each city councilmember may receive as compensation for his services an amount of not more than $500.00 per month. The mayor and councilmembers shall be entitled to receive their actual and necessary expenses incurred in the performance of their duties of office.
(b)
The mayor and councilmembers may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in carrying out their duties. Elected officials will adhere to the same policy guidelines as outlined in the administrative policy and procedures of HRM-5 "Travel While on City Business," as now or hereafter amended with the exception of approval procedures for reimbursements. Such expenses shall include, without limitation, the following:
(1)
Mileage reimbursement for the use of a personal automobile while conducting city business.
(2)
Travel and lodging expenses and fees incurred in conjunction with training seminars, conventions or city business or city business conducted outside the city.
Expenses may be reimbursed only after the submission of receipts for such expenses to the city manager and the approval by majority vote of the city council at a regularly scheduled meeting.
(c)
No elected official may request disbursement of city funds for any advertisement, club dues, charities or other projects, functions or entities without first submitting the request in writing to the city manager and the approval by majority vote of the city council at a regularly scheduled meeting.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-7; Ord. No. 02-01-09, 1-9-2002)
Editor's note— Exercising its home rule powers, the mayor and council increased its level of compensation from the level provided in the Charter, § 2.13, on December 11, 2001.
Charter reference— Composition and expenses, § 2.13.
Sec. 2-188. - Indemnity.
(a)
The city shall undertake to defend all civil, criminal or quasicriminal actions, except those specifically excluded under O.C.G.A. § 45-9-21, as amended, brought or maintained against the mayor and members of the city council, arising out of the performance of their duties during their term of office, whether based upon negligence, violations of contract rights, or violations of civil, constitutional, common law or statutory rights, including, but not limited to, attorney's fees, court costs, deposition costs, witness fees and compensation and all other costs, expenses and fees.
(b)
The city shall pay any claim or civil judgment against the mayor and members of the city council whose defense the city undertook pursuant to subsection (a) of this section to the extent the claim or civil judgment exceeds any policy of liability insurance or contracts of indemnity insuring or indemnifying the mayor and members of the city council which may be in effect.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-8; Res. No. 90-12-17, 12-17-1990)

Sec. 2-211. - Standing committees.
(a)
The following shall be standing committees of the city council:
(1)
Community development.
(2)
Finance and budget.
(3)
Public services.
(b)
Each committee, except finance and budget, shall be composed of three council members, appointed by the mayor; who shall serve for one year terms. No council member shall serve on less than one committee. A council member shall serve on a committee on which he did not serve during the previous year. No council member shall serve more than two consecutive years on the same committee.
The finance and budget committee shall consist of the entire city council body.
(c)
The mayor shall serve as an ex officio member of each committee. He shall be informed of each committee meeting, entitled to participate fully in the discussions and meeting, and entitled to review any investigation conducted by any committee. In the event of a tie vote on any question, the mayor shall cast a vote to break the tie.
(d)
The mayor and council may appoint any ad hoc committees as they deem necessary or desirable.
(e)
The committees shall so meet when so directed by the mayor, the chairman of the committee or any two members of the committee.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-11; Ord. No. 91-05-20, 5-20-1991; Ord. No. 99-12-14, 12-14-1999; Ord. of 1-24-2006)
Charter reference— Boards, commissions and authorities, § 3.13.
Sec. 2-212. - Committee reports.
Any business coming before the city council relating to an area of concern of a committee may be referred to that committee for investigation, review and/or recommendation to the mayor and council. Each standing or special committee, whenever required by the mayor or council, shall examine any matter referred to such committee, and make a report thereof at the next regular meeting of the council. Such reports need not be in writing unless so directed by the mayor.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-12; Ord. No. 91-05-20, 5-20-1991)
Charter reference— Boards, commissions and authorities, § 3.13.
Sec. 2-213. - Chair appointments.
Notwithstanding any other provisions of existing ordinances of the city, the chairperson of any and all committees, boards or commissions, whose members are appointed by the city council, shall be elected by the members of such committee, board or commission for a term of one year, at the first scheduled meeting of each calendar year. Such election shall be made by a majority vote of the members present at such meeting, provided that a quorum of the appointed members is present at such meeting. If a quorum of the appointed members is not present, such election shall be postponed until a quorum of the appointed members is present.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-13; Ord. No. 96-01-09, 1-9-1996)
Charter reference— Boards, commissions and authorities, § 3.13.

DIVISION 4. - ORDINANCES AND RESOLUTIONS

Sec. 2-231. - Preparation.
(a)
All ordinances, resolutions, contracts and agreements shall be prepared by or referred to the city attorney as provided in subsection (b) of this section. No ordinances, resolutions, contracts and agreements shall be prepared for presentation to the council, or requested in writing by the mayor.
(b)
All ordinances, resolutions, contracts and agreements shall have been approved, before presentation to the council, as to form and legality by the city attorney or his representative and shall have been examined by the mayor or his representative where there are administrative matters involved.
(c)
All ordinances, resolutions, contracts and agreements shall have first been referred to the department head who has jurisdiction over the subject matter of the ordinance, resolution, contract or agreement.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-21)
Charter reference— Ordinances and resolutions must be in writing, § 2.34; procedures for adoption of ordinance book to be maintained, § 2.37.
Sec. 2-232. - Introduction and adoption.
(a)
Ordinances, resolutions and other matters or subjects requiring action by the city council must be introduced by a member of the council, provided that the mayor, city manager or city attorney may present ordinances, resolutions and other matters or subjects to the council, and any councilmember may assume sponsorship thereof by moving that such ordinance, resolution, matter or subject be adopted.
(b)
No ordinance, other than emergency ordinances, shall be passed by the city council unless it shall first have been presented at the preceding regular or special meeting of the city council. All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are, to the extent of the conflict, hereby repealed.
(c)
No ordinance shall relate to more than one subject, which shall be clearly expressed in its title.
(d)
No ordinance, or section thereof, shall be amended or repealed unless the new ordinance contains the title of the ordinance or section amended or repealed, and when practicable all ordinances shall be introduced as amendments to this Code.
(e)
An ordinance, resolution or contract shall be deemed adopted or approved when it receives the affirmative vote of a majority of the whole council.
(f)
All ordinances shall take effect on the day following passage by the city council unless otherwise provided therein.
(Code 1989, § 2-1-22; Ord. No. 93-03-22, 3-22-1993)
Charter reference— Emergency ordinances may be enacted without a delay, § 2.35.
