ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Bird and wildlife sanctuary.
(a)
The entire area within the corporate limits of the city is designated as a bird and wildlife sanctuary.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, molest or kill any wild bird or to rob any wild bird's nest on property owned or leased by the city; however, if nuisance birds such as starlings are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality so as to constitute a nuisance or a menace to the health or property in the opinion of the director of the animal control shelter, the city manager or the chief of police, those birds may be destroyed as humanely as possible, under appropriate supervision, in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by the city manager or chief of police of the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, molest or kill any other wild game on property owned or leased by the city except by order, in their official capacity, of the city's police officers, animal control officers of Cherokee County, or the deputies of the Cherokee County sheriff's office.
(Ord. of. 12-12-2016, Exh. A)
Sec. 14-2. - Fowl, livestock, swine restrictions within city limits.
modified
(a)
It shall be unlawful for livestock, swine or poultry to be kept for any purpose on a lot in a subdivision or a lot less than six acres in the city limits.
(b)
All properties with fowl or livestock that are less than six acres but more than two acres as of March 23, 2004 are grandfathered in.
(c)
Notwithstanding the prohibition in section (a) above, goats may be used for property maintenance, if all of the following requirements are met:
(1)
The owner of the property notifies the community development director or designee at least 24 hours in advance of the placement goats on the premises; and
(2)
The goats brought to the premises are sufficiently contained by a fence and do not leave premises; and
(3)
The goats shall only be allowed on a premises for no more than 14 days in a given six-month period without the consent of the city council; and
(4)
A property owner may request exceptions to this subsection with a direct appeal to the city council; and
(5)
Government property is expressly exempt from this section.
(Ord. of. 12-12-2016, Exh. A; Ord. No. 6967-2021 , § 1, 2-22-2021)

Sec. 14-3. - Enclosures for fowl.
Where the keeping of fowl is allowed pursuant to applicable zoning and/or acreage, any housing or enclosure used shall be well drained, free from accumulations of excrement and objectionable odors, and otherwise clean and sanitary.
(Ord. of. 12-12-2016, Exh. A)
Cross reference— Livestock, § 14-17.

ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL CONTROL GENERALLY[2]
1.
Articles II and III of Chapter 10 of the Code of Ordinances, Cherokee County, Georgia (hereinafter referred to as the "County Code") as they now exist and as they may be subsequently amended are hereby adopted by reference and incorporated herein as though they were fully copied in this article.
2.
Articles II and III as so incorporated may be collectively referenced to herein as the "animal control ordinance."
3.
All references in the animal control ordinance to "Cherokee County" or "the county" shall be deemed to include the city.
4.
All uses of the word "county" as an adjective in the animal control ordinance shall be deemed to include the city. For example, "county law enforcement officers" shall be deemed to include city law enforcement officers and a "county business license" shall be deemed to include a city business license.
5.
All references in the animal control ordinance to violations of a county ordinance shall be deemed to be violations of a city ordinance.
6.
The Cherokee County marshal's office is authorized to enforce the animal control ordinance within the city limits of the city. Pursuant to the intergovernmental agreement between the city and Cherokee County effective February 7, 2018, citizens in the city shall be subject to the fees charged by the Cherokee County marshal's office. All fees charged by the Cherokee County marshal's office in connection with enforcement of the animal control ordinance shall be retained by Cherokee County to defray the costs of the Cherokee County marshal's office related to animal control.
(Ord. No. 5035-2018, § 1, 3-26-2018)
