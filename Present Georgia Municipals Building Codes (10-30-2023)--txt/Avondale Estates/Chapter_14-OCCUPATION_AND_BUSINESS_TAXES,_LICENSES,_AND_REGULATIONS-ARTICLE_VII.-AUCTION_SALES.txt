ARTICLE VII. - AUCTION SALES[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Municipal regulations and licensing, O.C.G.A. § 43-6-25.1.

Sec. 14-191. - Permit; required, fee.
No person, personally or by agents or servants, shall conduct an auction sale (that is to say, a sale at which property of any kind or character whatever is exposed for sale by bid and the property is thereafter sold to the highest bidder) until after such person shall have obtained a permit from the city and shall have paid a fee of one hundred dollars ($100.00) per day or any part thereof upon which such sale is to be held.
(Code 1973, § 2-717)
Sec. 14-192. - Legitimate bidding required.
It shall be unlawful for any person, personally or by agent or servant, conducting an auction sale to have bids made by any person other than a bona fide purchaser or prospective purchaser and who has no interest in the property to be sold.
(Code 1973, § 2-717)
Sec. 14-193. - Sale to highest bidder.
All property offered at an auction sale and upon which two (2) or more bids are made shall be sold to the highest bidder.
(Code 1973, § 2-717)
Sec. 14-194. - Tents, bands, loudspeakers and prizes prohibited.
No auctioneer shall erect a tent from which to conduct a sale, shall employ the use of a band or of loudspeakers, or give prizes.
(Code 1973, § 2-717)
Sec. 14-195. - Information required to be divulged.
A clear and accurate description of all property offered for sale shall be stated prior to the acceptance of bids, and in the event of sale of real estate all encumbrances shall be stated and all building restrictions shall be clearly explained.
(Code 1973, § 2-717)
