ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 13-1. - Unauthorized sewer connections.
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or attempt to enter or connect or attempt to connect a private drain or sewer or a building or plumbing fixture into or to any portion of the public sewer system or of any of the mains of such system unless such person is authorized to do so in the manner prescribed by law.
(Code 1973, § 2-7138)

Sec. 13-20. - Applicability.
This article applies to the preparation and storage, collection, transportation and disposal of all refuse in the area under jurisdiction of the city as presently or hereafter established. It prescribes rules and regulations therefor, regulates the private collection of garbage and refuse, provides for the licensing and regulation of private garbage and refuse collection, prescribes rules and regulations for hauling garbage, refuse and other waste material within or through the city, and prohibits the deposit of litter within the city.
(Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-20), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-21. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases when used in this article shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrative officer means the city manager or the person authorized by the city manager, to enforce the provisions of this article.
Ashes means waste products from coal, wood and other fuels used for cooking and heating from private residences.
Building refuse means waste material resulting from construction, remodeling, repairs and demolition operations on houses, buildings and other structures, including driveways and walks and comprising waste and rejected matter such as excavated earth, stones, bricks, plaster, wallpaper, sheetrock and lathes, lumber, shingles, tile, concrete and waste parts occasioned by the installation or replacement of plumbing, heating systems, electrical work and roofing.
Commercial/industrial establishment means any hotel, motel, industrial, public or semipublic establishment of any nature or kind whatsoever other than residential dwelling units.
Disposal area means a site, location, tract of land, or facility, used or intended to be used for the final disposal of solid waste, garbage, putrescible waste, hazardous waste, or other waste, except that the term shall not include the land or facility used for the disposal of solid waste or other waste from a single-family dwelling on which the owner, occupant or lessee of such land or facility resides.
Garbage means food waste, including waste accumulation of animal or vegetable matter used for or intended for use as food or that attends the preparation, use, cooking, dealing in or storing of meat, fish, fowl, fruit or vegetables.
Hazardous waste means solid or liquid waste material resulting from the manufacture or use of pesticides and drugs (other than normal household use); pathological wastes; highly flammable or explosive wastes; toxic wastes; sewage sludges; and other waste material that the director determines to be a likely hazard to the public health, safety or environment, except radioactive waste materials as provided in rules of the Georgia Department of Human Resources, chapter 270-5-20, entitled "Radioactive Materials."
Industrial refuse means waste materials from factories, processing plants, wholesale establishments, assembling plants, food processing wastes, cinders and ashes, lumber scraps, excelsior, shavings, floor sweepings, metal scrap and shavings, and any other waste products from industrial and manufacturing processes.
Owner means any person, firm or corporation owning, leasing, renting or occupying any premises within the jurisdiction of the city.
Recyclable waste means all aluminum cans, newspaper print, all unbroken glass bottles, jars and containers, and all plastic bottles and containers. Recyclable waste shall also include any other items that may hereafter be designated as recyclable pursuant to the rules and regulations promulgated by the governing authority.
Rubbish means nonrecyclable, household waste derived from places of residence and includes paper, rags, plastics, cartons, boxes, tin and other metal cans, broken glass bottles and jars, crockery, excelsior, rubber, discarded clothing and similar materials.
Scavenge means uncontrolled picking from discarded solid waste materials.
Solid waste means mineral matter, metal and wooden furniture, appliances, large metal scraps and wires, automobile or other vehicle bodies, tires or parts, wooden crates, boxes or pallets, dead animals, and any other wastes in a solid or semisolid state (excluding human body excrement), not otherwise defined in this section.
Waste means any unwanted or discarded material including, but not limited to, ashes, building refuse, garbage, hazardous waste, industrial refuse, recyclable waste, rubbish, solid waste, and yard debris.
Waste management collector means any person authorized by the city to collect, recycle, and dispose of waste acceptable for collection.
Yard debris means tree limbs, branches, stumps and logs, twigs, grass and shrub trimmings and clippings, bushes, weeds, leaves and general yard and garden waste materials, excluding stone and dirt rakings and similar waste materials resulting from routine landscaping activities.
(Ord. No. 765, § 1(1), 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-21), 11-25-91)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 13-22. - Administration and enforcement of article.
(a)
Responsibility for administration. In order to protect the health and safety of the people in the city, the administrative officer shall implement and enforce the provisions of this article to control the storage, collection, and disposal of solid waste in the city. Such officer shall plan, organize, procure equipment and personnel, and manage a collection and disposal service and/or establish criteria for evaluation of private collection and disposal services, make recommendations to the governing body regarding contracts for waste collection and/or disposal between it and private collectors and/or disposal site owners and/or operators, and shall regulate the establishment, maintenance and operation of public or private waste collection and/or disposal systems, methods and sites.
(b)
Functions of administrative officer. Collection, transportation and disposal of waste shall be under the supervision and control of the administrative officer. Such officer shall have the authority to establish routes and days of collection, types and locations of waste containers, and such other matters pertaining to collection, transportation and disposal of waste as such officer may find necessary and to change and modify the same after notice as required by law, provided such measures are not contrary to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 765, § 1(2)(a), (b), 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-22), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-23. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
Upon determining that a violation of this article exists, the administrative officer shall cause a written notice of violation to be issued to the subject owner setting forth the condition existing and a reasonable time within which for the owner to correct the condition.
(b)
Upon determining that the owner has failed to correct the subject condition within the time limit prescribed in the notice, the administrative officer shall cause a summons to be issued directing the owner to appear in the municipal court to answer the charge of violation of the appropriate section of this article.
(c)
Any person violating or failing to comply with any of the provisions of this article shall be summoned to the municipal court for each violation and upon conviction shall be punished by a fine not exceeding one thousand dollars ($1,000.00), or by imprisonment for a period not to exceed six (6) months, either or both of such fines and imprisonment, or work on the streets, parks and/or public works, in the discretion of the municipal judge. Each day a violation of any provision of this article continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-23), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-24. - Transportation of refuse; licensing of commercial sanitation firms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person including city refuse collectors and other waste management collectors to haul, convey or cause to be conveyed any refuse, including discarded building material or discarded furniture, upon or along the public streets and roadways except when the material transported is adequately secured in such a manner as to prevent it from falling, leaking or being blown from transporting vehicles. The operator or owner of the offending vehicle shall be personally responsible for any violation of this section.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person not approved by the city to collect and haul any refuse other than that arising from such person's own accumulation within any areas of the city in which refuse collection service is maintained by the city. An application in compliance with this section shall be submitted to the administrative officer for approval or other disposition.
(1)
Each application shall have attached a list showing the type, size and location of stationary compactors, stationary compactor containers and/or industrial waste materials to be serviced by the applicant. A commercial sanitation firm applying for approval to operate stationary compactors, stationary compactor containers or industrial waste containers shall certify at the time of application that the applicant or any corporate officer of the firm is not engaged in any illegal servicing of any commercial containers or refuse collection of any type in the city.
(2)
Each authorized commercial sanitation firm shall pay a monthly charge based on the size of the stationary compactor container or industrial waste container in use on the last day of each month in the amount of one dollar ($1.00) per cubic yard. This fee shall be paid on or before the fifteenth day of each succeeding month for which the service was rendered. A schedule of the location of each stationary compactor shall accompany the payment of the fees.
(3)
Each stationary compactor, stationary compactor container or industrial waste container shall carry in a conspicuous location the name, telephone number and address of the firm permitted to service the stationary compactor, stationary compactor container or industrial waste container.
(4)
Plans and specifications for installing a stationary compactor unit shall be approved by the city codes enforcement officer prior to the installation. All stationary compactor containers shall be installed on class A, three thousand (3,000) psi concrete pads of not less than six-inch thickness. All installations shall comply with the requirements of the city and county mechanical codes, fire code and health regulations.
(5)
All stationary compactor containers shall be in good condition and repair and shall be washed and treated with appropriate chemicals as necessary to prevent nuisances.
(Ord. No. 765, § 4, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-24), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-25. - Pickup routes and districts established; collection days.
(a)
The administrative officer shall divide the city into pickup routes and shall prepare a list of the names of all the streets, roads, alleys, drives, highways and other public thoroughfares within each route.
(b)
In residential areas not more than four (4) days, nor less than two (2) days, shall elapse between one collection and the next, and routes of collection shall be arranged so that collections shall be made on the same days of each succeeding week. From commercial areas and institutions, collections shall be made daily except Saturday, Sunday and holidays. Collections shall not be made on the following holidays unless otherwise specified by the administrative officer:
New Year's Day,
Observance of Martin Luther King, Jr.'s birthday,
Memorial Day,
Independence Day,
Labor Day,
Thanksgiving Day and the day after, and
Observance of Christmas (two (2) days).
(c)
It shall be the duty of the occupant of any residential or commercial/industrial unit to place refuse for collection by the city at the proper collection site prior to 7:45 a.m. or prior to the arrival of the sanitation truck.
(d)
Any containers placed at curbside by the owner or occupant for collection shall be removed by the owner/occupant to a storage location located on the residential premises the day the contents are emptied and collected.
(Ord. No. 765, § 4, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-25), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-26. - Collection by city; disposal of unacceptable or commercial/industrial waste.
All refuse generated or accumulated in the city shall be collected, transported and disposed of by the city through its designated agents, employees, or contract representatives. No other person shall collect, transport or dispose of any refuse generated or accumulated in the city, except that these rules and regulations shall not prohibit the producers of waste unacceptable for routine collection, producers of commercial/industrial waste or the owner of premises upon which commercial/industrial waste has accumulated from collecting, transporting and disposing of such refuse, subject to laws and regulations pertinent thereto, if such service is not provided by the city and if any such private contractor has been approved by the city to conduct such business within the city.
(Ord. No. 765, § 1(2)(c), (d), 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-26), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-27. - Waste acceptable for routine collection by the city.
(a)
Subject to the conditions and requirements set forth in this article, garbage, recyclable waste, household rubbish and yard debris constitute waste acceptable for collection by the city's waste management collector.
(b)
Small dead animals, not over thirty (30) pounds in weight, if kept separate from garbage and rubbish, will be collected without charge upon notice to the administrative officer, provided that a charge will be made for all such animals collected from animal hospitals, kennels, pet shops and the like. Owners of large dead animals shall be responsible for their removal and disposal.
(Ord. No. 765, § 3, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-27), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-28. - Waste unacceptable for routine collection by the city.
(a)
Except as provided in this article, it shall be a violation of this article to place or cause to be placed in any container or at curbside for routine collection any of the following:
(1)
Building refuse;
(2)
Hazardous waste;
(3)
Industrial refuse;
(4)
Solid waste;
(5)
Yard debris that is not cut, placed in a container or bundled, and placed for pickup as required by section 13-31;
(6)
Waste of any type from vacant lots unless payment of sanitation or lot upkeep tax for the current year has been made.
(b)
Waste not acceptable for routine collection shall be the responsibility of the property owner. It shall be the duty of each owner to make arrangements as may be necessary with the city and/or the city's waste management collector for the collection and disposal of such waste on a special fee, on-call basis. In such instances, the city manager shall assess and collect from the owner a special fee commensurate with the cost of collecting the waste on a special basis.
(Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-28), 11-25-91; Ord. No. 20-11, § 1, 12-14-2020)
Sec. 13-29. - Containers—Generally.
(a)
Refuse containers adequate to hold the refuse normally accumulating between scheduled removals shall be provided on each premises by the owner or occupant of each residence, industry, place of business or congregation and shall be maintained in good condition. Each container shall be waterproof, galvanized metal or durable plastic of a capacity of not more than thirty-two (32) gallons, having two (2) handles on the side thereof or a bail by which it may be lifted, with a tightfitting metal or plastic top with a handle and so constructed as to permit the free discharge of its contents. Oil or grease drums, paint cans and similar salvaged containers shall not be acceptable.
(b)
Refuse containers shall be kept clean and free of accumulated waste and shall be treated with appropriate chemicals as necessary to prevent nuisance. Containers failing to comply with these provisions or with sharp or jagged edges or with defects liable to hamper or injure the person collecting the contents thereof shall be promptly replaced by the owner or occupant.
(Ord. No. 765, § 3, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-29), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-30. - Same—Preparation and storage for commercial/industrial waste.
(a)
Hazardous or noxious wastes shall be contained in a safe and sanitary manner.
(b)
Containers shall be consolidated to minimize the number of collection sites and located so as to reasonably equalize the distance from the building spaces they serve.
(c)
Containers and enclosures shall be located so as to allow ease of access for collection trucks and direct access to drive areas. Straight-in or circular drives are encouraged to reduce truck maneuvering problems. No parking or other obstructions shall be permitted in the access area for enclosures.
(d)
Containers and enclosures shall be placed away from public view insofar as is practical.
(e)
Containers and enclosures shall be situated so that they do not cause excessive nuisance or offense to occupants of nearby buildings.
(f)
All refuse containers shall be screened with a six-foot high (minimum) fence or wall which conceals containers from all sides.
(g)
Concrete pads of appropriate size and construction shall be provided for containers or groups of containers having a capacity of six (6) thirty-two-gallon cans or more. Adequate drainage shall be provided in and around the pad area.
(h)
Newspapers, aluminum cans, and all other recyclable waste should be separated from waste acceptable for routine collection. For storage of these and other recyclable materials, the enclosure area and pad size shall be increased to amply accommodate the extra materials and their containers.
(i)
The tops of trash enclosures which are directly visible from the second floor of any off-site building shall be screened with a roof or overhead trellis.
(j)
Screening and gates shall be of a durable construction; fences, walls, footings, slabs and curbs shall meet all applicable ordinances. Gates shall be constructed of heavy-gauge metal or of a heavy-gauge metal frame with a covering of wood or other suitable material. Gates shall be secured with sturdy hinges or sliders and latches. For enclosures of six (6) cans or more and for bins, the screening shall be protected at its base by curbs. If screening is to be situated directly adjacent to parking spaces or drives, it shall be protected by a concrete-curbed buffer strip (minimum three (3) feet wide) of landscaping or pavement.
(k)
The design of the screening shall be compatible with the architectural design of on-site buildings and shall be approved by the city's codes enforcement officer. Screening shall be constructed of concrete tilt-up or masonry materials or the primary material used on the on-site building walls.
(l)
Before a building permit shall be issued for construction of a commercial or industrial establishment, arrangements for the storage of refuse and location of containers must be approved by the city's codes enforcement officer.
(m)
All garbage and rubbish shall be free of liquid and placed in watertight paper or plastic bags. The bags shall be unbroken and securely sealed and placed in the container in a manner so that normal handling will not cause the bag to open.
(n)
The occupants, of commercial establishments shall be responsible for maintaining the area surrounding refuse containers clean and free of accumulations of refuse.
(Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-30), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-31. - Same—Preparation and storage of residential waste.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the occupant or owner of any premises to keep all refuse pending collection and disposal as follows:
(1)
All waste shall be free from liquid and placed in watertight paper or plastic bags of a type, size and material designed for waste storage and disposal. The bags shall be unbroken and securely sealed and placed in the container in a manner so that normal handling will not cause the bag to open.
(2)
All containers shall be stored at the side or rear of the residence for collection.
(3)
Tree limbs, branches, heavy brush and other similar types of yard debris shall be cut into lengths not exceeding six (6) feet and four (4) inches in diameter, and placed in containers not over fifty (50) pounds gross weight or tied into bundles not exceeding fifty (50) pounds each for curbside pickup.
(4)
Bundles or bags of yard debris shall be placed at curbside for collection and shall at no time extend into the streets, gutters, drains, walkways, alleys, rights-of-way, or other property of the city.
(5)
Leaves shall be separated from other yard debris for curbside collection.
(6)
Newspapers, aluminum cans, and all other recyclable waste shall be separated from other waste acceptable for routine collection for recycling collection.
a.
The interior of all aluminum cans, plastic, and glass bottles shall be rinsed and drained. All lids, tops, and caps shall be removed and properly disposed of as rubbish.
b.
All newspapers shall be tied or bagged for recycling collection and shall be maintained in a dry condition.
(b)
Residential refuse will not be collected by the city if such refuse is contained in a locked area or where dogs are loose.
(c)
Waste generated from the conduct of business or commercial enterprise carried on from residential units will not be collected by the city except on a special fee basis.
(d)
Reserved.
(e)
Waste of any type from vacant lots will not be collected unless payment of the sanitation or lot upkeep tax for the current year has been made.
(Ord. No. 765, § 3, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-31), 11-25-91; Ord. No. 20-11, § 2, 12-14-2020)
Sec. 13-32. - Scavenging prohibited.
It shall be deemed a violation of this article for any person, other than the owner, the city or the city's waste management collector to remove, collect, salvage or otherwise interfere with any containerized waste pending collection.
(Ord. No. 765, § 3, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-32), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-33. - Littering; accumulation prohibited.
(a)
No person shall throw, discharge, discard or otherwise cause to be deposited waste of any character into the streets, gutters, drains, walkways, alleys, rights-of-way, streams, lakes or impoundment, or other property of the city.
(b)
Except as otherwise set forth in this article, any accumulation of waste on any property within the city is hereby declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited.
(c)
The provisions of subsection (b) of this section do not apply to the dumping on private property with the owner's permission of sand, dirt, broken dirt, blocks, broken pavement or other suitable material for use as fill to raise the elevation of the land, provided it is not maintained in an unsightly condition and provided the owners of the property on which such material is dumped level such dumped material with appropriate grading equipment.
(Ord. No. 765, § 3, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-33), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-34. - Air pollution controls; burning of combustible materials.
No person shall set fire to or burn any papers or other combustible materials in any container or on the grounds thereof except as may be permitted by county regulations at the county incinerator or at state-approved sanitary landfills.
(Ord. No. 765, § 5, 2-28-77; Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-34), 11-25-91)
Sec. 13-35. - Sanitation tax; payment thereof.
(a)
The sanitation tax for the collection and disposal of waste by the city shall be as established in the city's tax ordinance, a copy of which is on file in the office of the city clerk.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the city clerk to prepare and mail to each property owner a tax bill showing the amount of sanitation tax due for the calendar year. In accordance with the city's tax ordinance, a late payment penalty and an assessment of interest shall be imposed upon each account for which payment is not made within the time limits prescribed in the tax ordinance.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the city clerk to prepare and maintain a complete record of the name of each owner, the address, tax map reference number, the amount due for the calendar year, and an entry showing when payment was made. This docket shall be called the sanitation tax docket.
(d)
It shall be the duty of the city clerk to make an annual audit of the sanitary docket not later than December 1 of each year, and issue a fi. fa. or execution against the property served, which fi. fa. or execution shall have the same lien dignity and priority as fi. fa.'s or executions issued for city real estate taxes.
(e)
On premises being assessed a sanitation tax, after the property owner has been notified that the sanitation tax is delinquent and the property owner refuses to promptly pay it, the city may refuse collection at those premises.
(Ord. No. 844, § 1(12-35), 11-25-91)
