Sec. 5-116. - Purpose of article.
This article is enacted for the purpose of establishing minimum standards governing the, condition and maintenance of property, dwellings, buildings, and structures in order to make dwellings, buildings, structures, and surrounding premises safe, sanitary, and fit for human use and habitation. Further, this article will promote and protect the public health and safety through the control of weeds and grass which constitute a fire or health hazard, fixing certain responsibilities and duties of owners, operators, and occupants of dwellings and authorizing the inspection of dwellings and structures.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-117. - Reserved.
Sec. 5-118. - Scope and application.
(a)
Every lot, building, dwelling, and structure in the city, whether occupied or vacant, shall conform to the requirements of this article, irrespective of when such building may have been constructed, altered or repaired. Where applicable, this article also governs the condition of unimproved property.
(b)
When a unit is vacant, the full application of this article may be held in abeyance for an indefinite period, provided the exterior complies with this article, the interior has been cleaned of trash, rubbish and debris, and the unit has been secured in a manner approved by the city and is maintained in a closed and inaccessible manner until it is fully repaired.
(c)
The provisions of this article shall apply to any building, equipment, appliance, or system installed, altered, repaired, maintained or used in the city except as may be otherwise specifically provided for by statute or by ordinance.
(d)
Where repairs, alterations, construction, maintenance, and work required to meet the provisions of this article are regulated and/or required to be permitted by other ordinances, such repairs, alterations, construction, maintenance, and work shall be done only in accordance with those ordinances.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-119. - Enforcement.
The city's codes enforcement officer and city manager shall enforce the provisions of this article. They shall be authorized to exercise such powers as may be necessary or convenient to carry out and effectuate the purpose and provisions of this article, including:
(1)
Investigating the condition of all properties in the city in order to determine which lots, dwellings, buildings, or structures therein are in violation of this article.
(2)
Entering upon premises for the purpose of making examinations; provided, however, that such entries shall be made in such manner as to cause the least possible inconvenience to the persons in possession.
(3)
Exercising the police power necessary for issuing citations and for prosecuting persons charged with violating provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-120. - Unsafe, unsanitary, etc., structures prohibited.
(a)
Owning or maintaining any dwelling, building, or structure which is not in compliance with applicable codes due to dilapidation or defect increasing the hazards of fire, accident, or other calamity shall be a violation of this article.
(b)
Owning or maintaining any dwelling, building or structure that lacks adequate ventilation, light, or sanitary facilities shall be a violation of this article.
(c)
Owning or maintaining any condition or use of real estate which renders adjacent real estate unsafe or inimical to safe human habitation shall be a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-121. - Windows to be kept in good repair.
It shall be a violation of this article for any owner or occupant of any building within the city to allow any window, window-pane, or portion of any window to remain cracked, missing or broken for thirty (30) consecutive days.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-122. - Reserved.
Sec. 5-123. - Compliance prerequisite to utility services for substandard structure.
Electrical utility service shall not be reconnected to any vacant building which is unfit for human habitation, and has been without electrical utility service, until such building has been brought into compliance with all applicable codes.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-124. - Owner's right of entry.
Every occupant of a building, dwelling or dwelling unit shall give the owner thereof, or the owner's agents or employees, access to any part of such building, dwelling or dwelling unit or its premises, at all reasonable times, for the purpose of maintenance, improving or making such repairs or alterations as are necessary to effect compliance with the provisions of this article or with any lawful rule or regulation adopted pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-125. - Inspection warrant for occupied premises.
(a)
The city manager, in addition to other procedures provided, may obtain an inspection warrant under the conditions specified in this section in order to inspect occupied buildings and premises. The warrant shall authorize the city manager, building official, codes enforcement officer, or agents thereof to conduct a search or inspection of property, either with or without the consent of the person whose property is to be searched or inspected, under the conditions set out in this section.
(b)
Inspection warrants may be issued by the municipal court when the issuing judge is satisfied that all of the following conditions are met:
(1)
The person seeking the warrant must establish under oath or affirmation that the property to be inspected is to be inspected as a part of a legally authorized program of inspection which includes that property or there is probable cause for believing there is a condition, object, activity, or circumstance which legally justifies such an inspection of that property.
(2)
The issuing judge determines that the issuance of the warrant is authorized by this section.
(c)
The inspection warrant shall not be valid unless it meets all of the following requirements:
(1)
The warrant is attached to the affidavit required to be made in order to obtain the warrant.
(2)
The warrant describes, either directly or by reference to the affidavit, the property upon which the inspection is to occur and is sufficiently accurate that the executor of the warrant and the owner or possessor of the property can reasonably determine from it the property for which the warrant authorizes an inspection.
(3)
The warrant indicates the conditions, objects, activities, or circumstances which the inspection is intended to check or reveal.
(4)
The warrant refers, in general terms, to the ordinance provisions sought to be enforced.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
Sec. 5-126. - Weeds, junk, etc., prohibited; notice of violations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator, or occupant of any property within the city to maintain such property with vegetation, dead limbs, branches from trees, underbrush, garbage, trash, stagnant water, or other things accumulated thereon that will constitute an unsightly, unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or occupant of any property within the city to utilize the premises of such property for the open storage of any abandoned or junked motor vehicle, ice box, refrigerator, stove, other appliance, glass, building material, building rubbish, or similar vehicles, implements, or items that will constitute an unsightly, unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard. For the purposes of this section, an abandoned or junked motor vehicle is defined as an automobile, truck or other vehicle that is wrecked or in a state of disrepair and incapable of being moved under its own power, or any vehicle that does not bear a current license tag.
(c)
Where parking in open areas is used jointly for the benefit of two (2) or more owners or tenants, the responsibility for maintaining these parking areas free of garbage, trash, and other materials prohibited in this section shall be the joint and severable responsibility of the owners and tenants of these premises.
(d)
A written warning notice shall be issued to the owner or occupant of any property maintaining such property in violation of the provisions of this section. The warning notice shall state what items constitute the unsightly, unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard that must be cleared or removed from the premises. If the owner or occupant of any such property shall fail to clean the premises or remove such items within the time specified in the warning notice, such failure on the part of the owner or occupant of the property shall constitute a violation of this section. Warning notices shall be:
(1)
Delivered to the owner or occupant personally or to any other person of suitable age and discretion regularly residing within the household of the occupant or owner; or
(2)
Sent by regular United States mail, with sufficient postage prepaid to assure delivery, and addressed to the owner or occupant at his last known address.
(e)
Any violation as defined in this section shall be punishable as other ordinance violations. A citation may be issued by any city law enforcement officer or other duly authorized and empowered city official, and service of such citation upon an owner or occupant shall be deemed sufficient if it is:
(1)
Delivered to the owner or occupant personally or to any other person of suitable age and discretion regularly residing within the household of the occupant or owner; or
(2)
Sent by regular and certified United States mail, return receipt requested, with sufficient postage prepaid to assure delivery, and addressed to the owner or occupant at his last known address. A failure or refusal of such addressee to accept delivery shall constitute valid service under this section.
(f)
Citations issued pursuant to the provisions of this section may be so written as to reflect separate and additional violations for each and every succeeding day or portion thereof during which the unlawful conditions continue unabated.
(g)
In addition to other remedies, the codes enforcement officer may make written report of his findings to the city manager with recommendation that the city have the premises cleaned and have removed therefrom such items as described in this section that constitute an unsightly, unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard. When such report with recommendation has been filed:
(1)
A copy of the written report aforesaid and a written notice of the time and place fixed for hearing the matter shall be served upon the owner or occupant of such property at least ten (10) but no more than thirty (30) days prior to the date fixed for the hearing, such service to be made by the codes enforcement officer or other designated officer of the city in the manner specified in subsection (e) of this section. The hearing may be continued upon request of either party for reasonable cause.
(2)
The city manager shall hear the matter, including the response of such owner or occupant and witnesses introduced, and make his finding.
(3)
The city manager shall have full power and authority to order that the offending premises be cleaned and to authorize entry upon such premises for such purpose and to have such premises cleaned and to have removed therefrom vegetation, dead limbs and branches from trees, underbrush, garbage, trash, stagnant water, or other things thereon that constitute an unsightly, unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard, and to assess the property for cost of the cleaning and removal, and the assessment shall constitute a lien on the property until paid. An execution in favor of the city in the amount of such assessment shall be issued by the city clerk and be collected in accordance with the applicable provisions of law.
(Ord. No. 12-08, § 2(Att.), 10-22-2012)
