Title 7 - PARKS, RECREATION AND CULTURAL AFFAIRS[1]
The City of Morrow provides for residents, neighbors, and visitors many public parks, recreation and gathering spaces. Everyone who visits these spaces is expected to be respectful of themselves, others, and the property. This regulation is created to ensure pleasant and enjoyable experiences for everyone by prohibiting inappropriate, unruly, damaging, or obnoxious behavior. It is the intention of the city to enforce this legislation fairly, equally and to the fullest extent allowed by law.

CHAPTER 1. - CITY PARKS AND FACILITIES

Sec. 7-1-1. - Use of parks generally.
The following regulations govern the public use of all city parks (city sponsored events are not subject to these regulations):
(1)
The following items are prohibited from any city park:
•
Glass containers - It shall be unlawful for any person to bring in, carry, possess glass containers in city parks except for authorized vendors who shall retain and remove from the park all glass containers utilized in their commercial activities;
•
Firearms - Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §16-11-127, it is unlawful to carry a firearm to or while at a public gathering, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-11-127;
•
Amplified sound equipment - It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to play, use, operate or permit to be played, used or operated any radio receiving device, television, stereo, musical instrument for the producing, reproducing or amplifying of sound and/or noise at such a volume and in such a manner so as to create, or cause to be created, any noises or sounds which are plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more;
•
Fireworks - It shall be unlawful for any person to handle or launch any simple or complex device containing combustibles that liberate gases that on being ignited and/or whose action propels that device through the air. Examples of such prohibited devices include, but are not limited to: bottle rockets, firecrackers, smoke balls, sparklers, roman candles, strobes and snaps;
•
Alcoholic beverages;
•
Drugs or illegal substances;
(2)
No person shall do any of the following in any city park:
•
Playing music without the use of earphones;
•
Playing musical instruments;
•
Littering;
•
Children under ten years of age must be accompanied by an adult; parents and guardians shall not be more than 50 yards from their children, shall maintain visual contact with their children at all times, and shall not sit in their vehicle while their children play in the park;
•
Sleeping, laying down, or sun bathing;
•
Obnoxious behavior or excessive noise - It shall be unlawful for any person to use vulgar, profane, loud or unbecoming language within a city park. No person shall commit any disorderly, obscene or indecent act or commit any act of nuisance in a city park;
•
It shall be unlawful for any person to smoke or vape outside of designated areas within City of Morrow outdoor parks and recreational facilities;
•
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink vinous, malt or other alcoholic beverages while in a city park. It shall also be unlawful for any person to consume or use illegal drugs and/or narcotics in a city park.
(3)
Parks are open daylight hours only, unless posted otherwise.
(4)
Vendors and/or individuals are prohibited from selling products or services of any kind without first obtaining written permission from the city manager; provided, however, that no person shall sell spirituous, vinous or malt liquor in the city parks, except as otherwise provided by ordinance.
(5)
Shoes, sandals or other appropriate footwear are required to be worn.
(6)
Pedestrians only are allowed on the "jogging" track; bicycles are prohibited on the track.
(7)
Park facilities are available on a "first come-first served" basis unless reserved for a city sponsored event.
(8)
Areas shall be left clean; users must properly dispose of all trash, including animal excrement.
(9)
No overnight parking.
(10)
All parking is reserved for visitors using the adjacent municipal facilities (parks, recreation areas, city hall, welcome center, and the like).
(11)
Parking spaces that have been converted to electric charging stations can only be occupied by electric cars actively charging.
(Ord. No. 2019-04 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-11-19)
Sec. 7-1-2. - Penalties.
The penalties for any violation of this article will be as stated in section 1-1-8 of the City Code of Ordinances and may result in an individual being banned from visiting the parks.
(Ord. No. 2019-04 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-11-19)
