ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - When ordinances take effect.
Ordinances which do not provide for their taking effect at a different time, take effect immediately after their passage, without reference to their publication, which shall be in the discretion of the city commission.
(Code 1969, § 2-8)
State Law reference— Exemplification of municipal minutes and other records, O.C.G.A. § 24-7-21; authority of municipality to adopt ordinances, resolutions or regulations, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-3.
Sec. 2-2. - Reimbursement for actual and necessary expenses.
Members of the city commission, the city manager, the city clerk, and all other employees of the city shall be authorized to receive reimbursement for all actual and necessary expenses in carrying out their official duties. This shall include, and not be limited to, the attending of meetings not normally held in the ordinary course of business, educational seminars, trips, public relations activities, governmental or community affair meetings or seminars, and any and all other types of activities in which these persons expend their own personal funds and time or otherwise require remuneration for the actual and necessary expenses they incur in these endeavors.
(Code 1969, § 2-19)
Sec. 2-3. - City depositories designated.
SouthCrest Bank, Regions Bank and EastWest Bank, all being chartered national banks, are designated as official city depositories in which shall be deposited all funds of the city as provided by law.
(Code 1969, § 2-16; Ord. No. 3-1996, § 1, 3-11-96; Ord. No. 2-2013, § 1, 3-11-13; Ord. No. 5-2017 , § 1, 7-10-17)
