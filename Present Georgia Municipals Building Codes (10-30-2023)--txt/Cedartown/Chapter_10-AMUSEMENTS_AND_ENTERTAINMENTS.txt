DIVISION 2. - LICENSING

Sec. 10-56. - Penalty.
Any person violating the provisions of the license granted under this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable pursuant to the provisions of section 1-13. Further, any person attempting to operate, conduct or maintain an entertainment establishment contrary to the provisions of this article is hereby declared to be acting unlawfully and creating a public nuisance. The city may (in addition to, or in lieu of, prosecuting a criminal action pursuant to the penalty provisions set forth above) take such other action to stop such activity. The city manager may apply to the municipal court for abatement of a nuisance, or to such other court of competent jurisdiction, to grant such relief as will abate or close such entertainment and/or restrain and enjoin any person from operating, conducting or maintaining the establishment contrary to the provisions of this article. Any violation of the provision of this article shall be per se grounds for suspension revocation of a license granted under this article.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-210), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-57. - License required; exceptions; application fee.
(a)
The city commission does hereby establish the requirement of a license for any business within the city to operate a nightclub, bar, social gathering place, dancehall, private club or similar operation in which entertainment is to be provided to the patrons in the establishment. The city commission finds that it is in the public interest of the city to regulate such establishments, especially to prevent nude dancing or similar conduct which is prohibited under section 10-86. Further, the city desires to regulate closely in establishments which provide alcoholic beverages, entertainment so as to prevent criminal activity, nuisances, fighting and similar disruptive activities. For purposes of this article, "entertainment" shall be defined as dancing, live music, live entertainers, drama, vocal groups, instrumental groups or other performances which might be videotaped, stereo-recreated music or similar such entertainment which allows the patrons to dance, watch the entertainment (whether for a fee or not) or otherwise be present for a performance within the business establishment.
(b)
No license shall be required for any nonprofit civic organization, club, the civic arts commission or such similar organization which is engaged in eleemosynary, religious or fundraising activities, which activities may include entertainment within the general context of the definition provisions of subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
Any person, association, partnership or corporation desiring to obtain a license to operate, engage in, conduct or carry on any entertainment establishment shall make application to the chief of police or his designated representative. Prior to submitting such application, a nonrefundable fee in the amount as approved by the City of Cedartown Commission in the schedule of fees and charges shall be paid to the city to defray, in part, the cost of the investigation and report required by this article.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-201), 2-8-93; Ord. No. 7-2019 , § 2(Exh. A), 12-9-19)
Sec. 10-58. - Application contents.
Each application for an entertainment establishment permit under this article shall contain the following information:
(1)
The applicant's full true name, social security number and date of birth.
(2)
The present address and telephone number of the applicant.
(3)
Acceptable written proof that the applicant is at least 18 years of age.
(4)
Business, occupation or employment history of the applicant for the five years immediately preceding the date of application; and business or employment records of the applicant, partners in a partnership, directors and officers of a corporation.
(5)
The business license history of the applicant and whether the applicant, in previous licensed operations in this city or any other city, state or territory, has had such license or permit for a business (especially a similar type of business) revoked or suspended, the reason therefor, and the business activity or occupation subsequent to such action of suspension or revocation of the applicant.
(6)
If the applicant is a corporation, the name of the corporation shall be set forth exactly as shown in its articles of incorporation, together with the place and date of incorporation, and the names and addresses of each of its current officers and directors. If the applicant is a partnership, the applicant shall set forth the same, residence address and dates of birth of all limited and/or general partners. If one or more of the partners is a corporation the provisions of this subsection pertaining to corporations shall apply. The applicant corporation or partnership shall designate one of its officers or general partners to act as its responsible managing officer. Such designated persons shall complete and sign all application forms required of an individual applicant under this article, but only one application fee shall be charged.
(7)
If the applicant, any partners or any corporate officers or directors, if the applicant is a corporation, have been convicted of any crime involving good moral character and/or moral turpitude in the past five years and, if so, a complete description of any such crime including date of violation, date of conviction, jurisdiction and any disposition, including any fine or sentence imposed and whether terms of disposition have been fully completed.
(8)
If the applicant is a person doing business under a trade name, a copy of the trade name properly recorded. If the applicant is a corporation using a trade name (other than the corporate name), a copy of the authority to do business in Georgia, trade name affidavit, if any, and last annual report, if any.
(9)
At least three character references from individuals who are in no way related to the applicant or individual shareholders, officers or directors of a corporation and who are not or will not benefit financially in any way from the application if the license is granted and who have not been convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude and/or character.
(10)
Address of the premises to be licensed.
(11)
Whether the premises are owned or rented.
(12)
Each applicant for an entertainment establishment license shall be verified and acknowledged under oath to be true and correct by:
a.
If the applicant is an individual, the individual.
b.
If by a partnership, by the manager or general partner.
c.
If a corporation, by the president of the corporation.
d.
If any other organization or association, by the chief administrative official.
(13)
In addition to the information required in this section, the application shall disclose the name, address, social security number and date of birth of any and all persons who have a financial interest of any type in the entity which is applying for the license.
(14)
Any other matter reasonably required by the chief of police.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-202), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-59. - Investigation; granting of license.
(a)
The chief of police shall have 30 days to investigate the application and the background of the applicant. Upon completion of the investigation and recommendation of the chief of police, the city commission may grant the license at its next duly called and held meeting if it finds that:
(1)
The required fee has been paid.
(2)
The application conforms in all respects to the provisions of this article.
(3)
The applicant has not knowingly made a material misrepresentation in the application.
(4)
The applicant, if an individual, or any officers or directors, if the applicant is a corporation, or any of the partners, including limited partners, if the applicant is a partnership, has not been convicted in a court of competent jurisdiction of an offense involving a sex-related crime, drug-related or alcohol-related felony or convicted of an attempt to commit any of the abovementioned offenses; or convicted in any state of any offense which is a crime involving moral character and/or moral turpitude.
(5)
The applicant has not had an entertainment establishment permit, general entertainment license, beer license or other similar license or permit denied or revoked for cause involving moral character in this city or other local government jurisdiction located in or out of this state prior to the date of application.
(6)
The building, structure, equipment or location of such business, as proposed by the applicant, would comply with all applicable zoning and distance laws relating to the sale of malt beverages and would qualify to receive a certificate of occupancy.
(7)
The applicant is at least 18 years of age.
(8)
On the date the business for which a permit is required in this article commences, and thereafter, there will be a responsible person on the premises to act as manager at all times during which the business is open.
(9)
If alcoholic beverages are being served upon the premises, there has been no disorderly conduct, disruption, breach of the peace or significant previous problems with the establishment where the entertainment license is requested to be granted.
(10)
The applicant does not propose, and so states in writing, to allow nude dancing or other adult-type entertainment within the premises, which is prohibited by section 10-86.
(11)
The grant of such license will not cause a violation of this article or any other ordinance or regulation of the city, the state or the United States and has met the approval of the chief of police.
(12)
The licensee shall maintain at all times a written notification of the name, address and phone number of the manager or assistant manager in charge of the licensed premises and there shall at all times be a manager in charge and on the premises during operating hours.
(b)
There is hereby created a continuing obligation on the licensee to comply with all provisions of this Code.
(c)
The applicant shall appear before the city commission at any hearing on this issue of the grant or denial of the requested license.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-203), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-60. - Persons ineligible for license.
No license provided for by this article shall be issued to or held by:
(1)
An applicant who has not paid all required fees and taxes for a business at that location and/or property taxes.
(2)
Any person who is not of good moral character.
(3)
Any corporation, any of whose directors are not of good moral character.
(4)
Any partnership or association, any of whose partners or members are not of good moral character.
(5)
Any applicant who is not qualified to hold and conduct business according to the laws of the United States, the state or the city.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-204), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-61. - Renewal.
Licenses for entertainment establishments may be renewed on a calendar-year basis provided that the business continues to meet the requirements set out in this article. Renewal applications shall be submitted by November 15 of each year prior to January 1 of the year for which such license is requested.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-205), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-62. - License nontransferable.
No entertainment establishment license may be sold, transferred or assigned by a license holder, or by operation of law, to any other person. Any such sale, transfer or assignment, or attempted sale, transfer or assignment, shall be deemed to constitute a voluntary surrender of the license and the license shall thereafter be null and void; provided and excepting, however, that if the licensee is a partnership and one or more of the partners should die, one or more of the surviving partners may acquire, by a purchase or otherwise, the interest of the deceased partner without affecting a surrender or termination of the license, and in such case the license, upon notification to the city, shall be placed in the name of the surviving partner.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-206), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-63. - Prohibition against minors.
It shall be unlawful for any license holder to admit or permit the admission of minors within the licensed premises. It shall further be unlawful for any person to sell, barter or give to any minor any alcohol, ticket for entertainment or to otherwise allow such person to be upon the premises where alcohol is being sold and such live entertainment, as may be permitted by this article, is being conducted.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-207), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-64. - Change of name.
(a)
No licensee under this article shall advertise, operate, conduct, manage, engage in or carry on an entertainment establishment under any name other than his name and the name of the business as specified on his license.
(b)
Any application for an extension or expansion of a building or other place of business where an entertainment establishment is located shall require inspection and shall comply with the provisions and regulations of this article.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-208), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-65. - Refusal; appeal.
(a)
If the chief of police following investigation of the applicant deems that the applicant does not fulfill the requirements as set forth in this article, the chief shall notify the city clerk of such opinion and, within 30 days of the date of application, provide copies of the investigation report to the city clerk. The city clerk shall notify the applicant by certified mail of such denial. Any applicant who is denied a license may appeal the denial to the city commission by filing a written notice of appeal within ten days of the receipt of the notice from the city clerk. A hearing before the commission shall be scheduled within 45 days of the notice.
(b)
At any such hearing, the applicant shall have the right to cross examine any witnesses produced by the city concerning the denial of the application, and to present any evidence the applicant may feel pertinent to the issue of granting, suspension or denial, or nonrenewal of any entertainment license. The decision of the city commission made thereon shall be in writing, and shall be final, subject to appeal to the superior court of the county.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-209), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-66. - Revocation; appeal.
(a)
The chief of police is authorized to suspend or revoke a license previously granted under this article. If the chief of police seeks to suspend or to revoke a license, the chief of police shall give written notification to the licensee of such action and the notice shall contain a specification of the violation and shall be served upon the licensee at least five days prior to a hearing on the issue. The licensee shall be given written notice of the time and place of the hearing.
(b)
The chief of police shall be authorized to suspend or revoke a license in the event of any one or more of the following occurrences:
(1)
A licensee gave false or misleading information in the original application.
(2)
A licensee has knowingly allowed possession, use, or sale of controlled substances on the premises and/or knowingly allowed possession, use or sale of controlled substances or alcohol to a minor on the premises.
(3)
A licensee has knowingly allowed the violation of an ordinance of the city or a violation of any criminal law of the state to occur on the premises.
(4)
A licensee has violated any provision of this article.
(5)
A licensee has been convicted of any drug-related, alcohol-related or sex-related crime or crime of moral turpitude (federal, state or local) regarding an offense which was committed on the premises or which would otherwise violate the provisions of this article.
(6)
A licensee fails to pay any fee, license fee, fine or other amount of money due to the city under this article or any other licensing ordinance of the city.
(c)
If the chief of police suspends or revokes any license under this article, the suspension or revocation shall be for a period of not less than one day nor more than 365 days, within the discretion of the chief of police. However, the licensee shall be authorized to continue its business operations until the date of the hearing scheduled in accordance with subsection (d) of this section. No licensee or other applicant may apply for a new license during any period of suspension or revocation. In any hearing conducted by the chief of police, the chief shall consider, among other things, the severity of the allegations, the evidence submitted and the testimony presented, in making any decision on suspension, revocation and the duration of either.
(d)
In the event of a suspension or revocation by the chief of police, the licensee may appeal the decision of the chief of police to the city commission by filing a written notice of appeal within ten days from the date of the decision of the chief of police. Thereafter, a hearing shall be scheduled before the commission within 30 days after the date of the notice of the appeal by the applicant. After hearing by the commission, it may take such action as it deems appropriate, including the upholding of the action of the chief of police or the imposition of such action as the commission may deed appropriate under the facts. The decision of the commission shall be final. Appeals from the decision of the commission shall be to the superior court of the county, filed within 30 days of the final action of the commission. If the licensee does not file an appeal from any decision of the chief of police, as provided in this section, the decision of the chief of police shall be final.
(Ord. No. 3-1993, § 3(13-211), 2-8-93)

DIVISION 3. - ENTERTAINMENT ACTIVITIES

Sec. 10-86. - Prohibited activities enumerated.
The following types of entertainment, attire and conduct are prohibited upon any premises licensed to sell, serve or dispense alcohol beverages, or so-called juice bars or any other entertainment type or location (public or private) within the city:
(1)
The employment or use of any person, in any capacity, in the sale or service of alcohol and/or other beverages while such person is unclothed or in such attire, costume or clothing as to expose to view any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola or of any portion of the male or female pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva and genitals.
(2)
Live entertainment where any person appears in the manner described in subsection (1) of this section or where such person performs acts of or acts which simulate any of the following:
a.
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation or any sexual act which is prohibited by law.
b.
The touching, caressing or fondling of the breasts, buttocks, anus or genitals.
c.
The displaying of the male or female pubic hair, anus, vulva or genitals.
(3)
The holding, promoting, sponsoring or allowing of any contest, promotion, special night, event or any other activity where patrons of the establishment are encouraged or allowed to engage in any of the conduct described in subsections (1) and (2) above.
(Code 1969, § 15-49; Ord. No. 2-1993, § 2(15-49), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-87. - Authorized entertainment activities; correlation with license.
(a)
Any person may apply for a license in an establishment selling alcoholic beverages (pursuant to proper licenses within the city) or any other business that wishes to have entertainment, for a license authorizing entertainment on the premises. This entertainment may be in the form of live music, drama, vocal groups, instrumental groups, videos, stereo-recreated music or similar such entertainment; provided that none of the entertainment, nor the employees within the establishment, shall violate any of the provisions of a properly granted entertainment license.
(b)
Nothing contained in this section shall act as a prohibition to a properly granted entertainment license obtained through the provisions of section 10-57 et seq. However, no person granted such entertainment license shall engage in any of the activities prohibited by section 10-86.
(Ord. No. 2-1993, § 2(15-49.1), 2-8-93)
Sec. 10-88. - Violations; hearing, penalties.
(a)
Any party who violates the provisions of section 10-86 shall be punished, upon conviction thereof, pursuant to the provisions of section 1-13. Each day any such conduct occurs which is violative of section 10-86 shall be considered a separate and continuous violation for which criminal punishment pursuant to section 1-13 may be imposed.
(b)
Any person attempting to operate, conduct or maintain a business or entertainment establishment which violates the prohibited acts of section 10-86 is hereby declared to be an unlawful activity and a public nuisance. The city may (in addition to, or in lieu thereof, proceeding with a criminal action pursuant to the penalty provisions hereinabove set forth) take such other action to stop such activity. The city manager may apply to the municipal court for abatement of a nuisance or to such other court of competent jurisdiction to grant such relief as will abate or close the entertainment establishment and/or restrain and enjoin any person from operating, conducting or maintaining such business or establishment contrary to the provisions of section 10-86.
(c)
Any person who may hold a valid entertainment license, or otherwise hold a valid business license, within the city who is alleged to be violating the provisions of section 10-86 shall be entitled to a hearing for any suspension or revocation of any business license within the city under the same terms and conditions as hearings contemplated in section 10-65 concerning the granting of entertainment licenses.
(Code 1969, § 15-49; Ord. No. 2-1993, § 2(15-49.2), 2-8-93)

Sec. 10-111. - Definition.
The terms "billiards," or "pool," as used in this article, shall mean any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions, with balls which are impelled by a cue, and shall include all forms of the games known as carom billiards, pocket billiards (formerly called pool), and English billiards. The term "billiard room" shall mean any public place where the game of billiards is permitted to be played and for which a charge is made for use of equipment.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 10-112. - Provision for local licensing.
The purpose of this article is to provide for local licensing and regulation of billiard rooms. Pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 43-8-16 the provisions of O.C.G.A. ch. 8, tit. 43 shall not apply within the corporate limits of the city.
Sec. 10-113. - Applicability of provisions.
The provisions of this article shall not be construed to include billiard tables or billiard rooms operated by private or industrial concerns, religious orders, charitable institutions, state, county or city institutions, fraternal orders or bona fide clubs, using such tables for members or employees only.
Sec. 10-114. - License required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to maintain or operate within the city any pool room or billiard room or any place of like character, without first having made application to the city clerk and, after paying the prescribed fees, having received a license to maintain and operate such business. No license shall be issued to any person under the age of 18 years or to any person who has been convicted of a felony within the past five years. All licensees must be bona fide residents of the county or the city.
Sec. 10-115. - Obstructing view of interior.
It shall be unlawful for any person running or operating a billiard room to use in such room any screens at the doors and windows, or to use on the doors or windows any painted or stained glass so as to obstruct a clear view of the interior.
Sec. 10-116. - Enclosed rooms prohibited; exceptions.
No closed rooms, stalls or other enclosures wherein the game of pool is played shall be permitted within the licensed premises. This provision, however, shall not be construed to prohibit the maintenance of washrooms and toilet rooms for proper purposes, nor the maintenance of closets for storage purposes exclusively.
Sec. 10-117. - Access by police officers; reports of disorderly conduct.
(a)
All law enforcement officers shall have free access to the licensed premises at any time any person is present in the licensed premises.
(b)
It shall be the duty of every person owning or managing a pool or billiard room in the city to report to the police department every person guilty of disorderly conduct in the pool or billiard room immediately after the conduct comes to the knowledge of the owner or manager.
Sec. 10-118. - Separate license required to serve alcoholic beverages.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or operator of any establishment licensed under the provisions of this article to permit any alcoholic beverages being brought or kept upon the premises of the licensed establishment without first obtaining a license to permit the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises.
Sec. 10-119. - Multiple license requirements.
Any person who holds an alcoholic beverage pouring and/or alcoholic beverage consumption license on the premises and in which establishment billiards or pool are allowed to be played by patrons of the establishment shall also have a license under this article. The licensee shall comply with all the requirements of this article, and in addition thereto, those requirements concerning coin-operated amusement devices if such pool or billiard tables are coin-operated.
Sec. 10-120. - Operation during legal hours for drinking.
It shall be unlawful for any person running or operating a pool room or billiard room within the city wherein alcoholic beverages are licensed to be consumed on the premises to keep the room open or permit the playing of any pool or billiards within the licensed premises at any time other than the legal hours for drinking and possession of open containers as set out in chapter 6 of this Code.
Sec. 10-121. - Restriction on minors' presence.
It shall be unlawful for any person running or operating a pool room or billiard room within the city to permit persons under 18 years of age, unless accompanied by a parent or guardian, to enter the licensed premises of the pool room if the establishment also holds an alcoholic beverage license for consumption on the premises.

ARTICLE IV. - COIN-OPERATED AMUSEMENT MACHINES

Sec. 10-141. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this Section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Amusement game room means any location where one or more bona fide coin-operated amusement machines are operated that permit noncash redemption as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-25-35(d)(1).
Bona fide coin-operated amusement machine means the same as this term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-17-1(2) and any applicable regulations of the state and includes Vegas-style video machines as defined herein.
Location means a business within the city that has complied with the provisions of the ordinances of the city and all requirements as to occupation taxes and business licenses required by local and or state law.
Operator means any person who operates an amusement game room, owns the property on which the game room is located, sets up an amusement game room for operation by themselves or by another, or leases or distributes for the purpose of operation by another any amusement game room whether any setting up for operation, leasing or distribution is for a fixed charge or rental; or on the basis of a division of the income received from such game rooms, or otherwise.
Vegas-style video machines means any video game machine or device, operated for any consideration, for the simulated play of slot machines (matchup or lineup games) poker, blackjack, or any other card game described in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20, that except for meeting the payout exceptions in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(d), would otherwise classify as an illegal gaming device and activity.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-142. - Owner information.
Operators of an amusement game room within the corporate limits of the city shall submit to the city clerk the following information:
(a)
Name, address, and age of the operator of the business and owner of the premises.
(b)
Address or place where the bona fide coin-operated amusement machine or machines are to be offered to the public for play and the other business or businesses operated at that address or place;
(c)
Name and address of the owner of the machine or machines and a copy of the owner's master license(s);
(d)
List of any other licenses or permits from the city held by the operator and/or by the owner of the premises.
(e)
A detailed list of each machine operating at the location.
The operator shall update this information when any changes are made and shall provide updated information to the city clerk yearly.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-143. - Violations.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of an offense, subject to the penalties provisions contained in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-144. - Inspection.
Each operator under this article shall, at all times, keep every portion of the premises being used for the operation of an amusement game room open for inspection by the police department and other city departments for the purpose of enforcing any provisions of this article or other laws.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-145. - Distance.
Every amusement game room in the city shall comply with the proximity provisions for businesses licensed to sell alcohol set out in section 6-131 of the Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-146. - Number of bona fide coin-operated amusement machines at a location.
No amusement game room in the city shall offer to the public more than six Vegas-style video machines at the same location.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-147. - Gambling prohibited.
Nothing herein shall be deemed to authorize, license or permit any gambling devices whatsoever, or any mechanism which has been judicially determined to be a gambling device; or in any way contrary to law or that may be contrary to any future law of the state. Any operator, proprietor, individual, firm, partnership or corporation that shall permit in any way, directly or indirectly, gaming or gambling in the use of any device or machines shall be subject to immediate confiscation of such device or machines by the police department as contraband, and shall be punishable as a misdemeanor, unless other punishment is authorized under the laws of the state.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-148. - View of machines; prohibited use.
Bona fide coin-operated amusement machines shall be operated in areas clearly visible from the entrance and windows of the building in which they are operated. No screens, shades, partitions or other devices of like character shall be allowed which shall have the effect of obstructing the view through the windows or doors of the location of the amusement game room. Further, no bona fide coin operated amusement games shall be allowed to be located in back rooms or other restricted access rooms not accessible or open to the general public.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-149. - Gross receipts from amusement machines and from business.
Income from Vegas-style video machines offering noncash redemption in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 shall not constitute more than 50 percent of the income from any location. Compliance with this section requires both the availability of records for inspection and compliance with 50-percent-of-income requirement.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-150. - Notice requirement.
Every amusement game room shall post a conspicuous sign with the following similar words: "Georgia law prohibits giving or receipt of any money for winning a game or games on an amusement machine; giving or receipt of any money for free replays won on an amusement machine; giving or receipt of money for any merchandise, prize, toy, gift certificate, or novelty won on an amusement machine; or awarding any merchandise, prize, toy, gift certificate, or novelty of a value exceeding $5.00 for a single play of an amusement machine."
The owner or operator of each amusement game room shall inform every employee of the acts and omissions prohibited by O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 and by this article, and of the penalties for violation O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 and this article.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-151. - Compliance with O.C.G.A. provisions relating to master licenses and stickers for individual machines.
Bona fide coin-operated amusement machines may be used in an amusement game room within the city only if the machines are owned by a person who holds a valid master license in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-17-2, and each machine offered to the public for play has a valid permit sticker in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-17-9. The city official in charge of issuing business licenses shall notify the State Commissioner of Revenue of any observed violation of O.C.G.A. § 48-17-2 or 48-17-9.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
Sec. 10-152. - Penalties for violations by owners or operators of amusement game rooms.
In addition to penalties set out by Georgia law for failure to comply with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 (a) through (j), the owner or operator of an amusement game room, upon violation of any requirement of this article or any law, which violation is directly attributable to the operation of an amusement game room or bona fide coin-operated amusement machine, may be subject to the general penalty section of the Cedartown Code for each such violation and further may be subject to the following administrative restrictions to be imposed by the Cedartown City Commission at their discretion:
(1)
First offense. Suspension for not more than six months of the owner or operator's conditional use permit for operating an amusement game room at the location where the violation occurred.
(2)
Second offense. Suspension for more than six months or permanent revocation of the owner or operator's conditional use permit for operating an amusement game room in the location where the violation occurred, and possible suspension of other permits and licenses granted to the operator or owner of the premises by the city, including licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages for up to six months if the same premises is involved. Prior to any action being taken against a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages, the notice and hearing requirements of the City Code shall be followed.
(3)
Multiple offenses. Offering one or more bona fide coin-operated amusement games in violation of any suspension or revocation of a conditional use permit for the operation of any amusement game room at the location is punishable as a separate violation of this section and if said violation occurs during a suspension period, shall result in revocation of the conditional use permit for an amusement game room at the location.
Further, any person found to have received money or any other illegal payout as a reward for the successful play or winning of any coin-operated amusement machine shall be in violation of this ordinance and subject to the general penalty section of the Cedartown Code for each violation.
(Ord. No. 9-2009, § 2, 8-10-09)
