Sec. 2-31. - Regular meetings.
(a)
The regular meetings of the city commission shall be held on the second Monday of each month at 6:00 p.m.
(b)
Due notice of an alternative day or time will be given in accordance with O.C.G.A. 50-14-1(d).
(Code 1969, § 2-1; Ord. No. 4-2011, 2-14-11)

Sec. 2-32. - Parliamentary practice.
In all meetings of the city commission the members shall be generally governed in their deliberations by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised or as hereafter amended, as far as is practicable.
(Code 1969, § 2-6)
Sec. 2-33. - Duties of commission secretary.
The duties of the secretary of the city commission, who shall be the city clerk, shall be to:
(1)
Attend every meeting of the commission and keep a complete record of all the proceedings in the minute book.
(2)
Issue all summons, writs, processes and other papers which shall lawfully be required of him for the enforcement of the laws and ordinances.
(3)
Preserve and keep all records, communications, petitions, ordinances and all original documents of every kind and description, filed alphabetically, so as to be easily and quickly accessible for reference and examination.
(4)
Publish such citation as shall be lawfully required.
(5)
Receive tax returns, make up the tax digest and collect taxes due the city.
(6)
Collect all fines and forfeitures, unless the city commission requires such duty to be done by some other officer.
(7)
Collect all rents for water, and all monies due the city not otherwise provided for in this section.
(8)
Keep a cash book, in which shall be entered every item of money collected, from whom, for what purpose, and the date collected, and such cash book shall also show when, to whom, and for what purpose each and every disbursement of such funds is made.
(9)
Prepare and submit at the first meeting of the commission in each month a trial balance, showing all monies received and disbursed pursuant to subsections (7) and (8) of this section.
(10)
Keep an account book, in which shall be recorded an itemized statement of all accounts paid by the city and the date when each account is paid.
(11)
Perform all duties which shall be lawfully required of him.
(12)
Give such bonds as may be required and approved by the city commission.
(Code 1969, § 2-17(1), (2), (4), (6)—(14))
