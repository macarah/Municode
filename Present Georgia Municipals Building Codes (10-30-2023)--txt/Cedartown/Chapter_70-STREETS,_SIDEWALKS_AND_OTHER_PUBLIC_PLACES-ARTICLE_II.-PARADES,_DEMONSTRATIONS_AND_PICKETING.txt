ARTICLE II. - PARADES, DEMONSTRATIONS AND PICKETING

Sec. 70-31. - Unlawful assembly, picketing, demonstration; penalty.
It shall be unlawful and a misdemeanor for any person to knowingly participate in any assembly, demonstration, picketing, or parade on or through the streets and/or alleys of the city at public places or upon private property under certain conditions and without a permit as set forth in sections 70-32 and 70-35. A person guilty of violating these provisions shall, upon conviction, be punished as prescribed in section 1-13.
(Code 1969, § 15-70.1)
Sec. 70-32. - Types of unlawful assembly, picketing, demonstrations and parades enumerated.
The following courses of conduct, acts or occurrences, by any person who knowingly participates in the acts or occurrences, shall be considered unlawful and subject that person, upon conviction, to the punishment prescribed in section 70-31. The acts or occurrences are as follows:
(1)
The assembly of two or more persons for the purposes of committing an unlawful act.
(2)
The failure of two or more persons to withdraw or disperse from any assembly, picketing, demonstration or parade on being lawfully commanded to do so by a peace officer and before any member of the assembly has inflicted injury to the person or property of another.
(3)
The gathering of two or more persons, without authority of law or without a permit pursuant to the provisions contained in section 70-35 for the issuance of the permit, for the purpose of doing violence to the person or property of one supposed by the accused to have been guilty of a violation of the law.
(4)
The assembly of two or more persons, without authority of law or without a permit pursuant to the provisions contained in this article, for the purpose of exercising correctional or regulative powers over any person by violence, threat or intimidation.
(5)
Any persons who shall assemble, loiter or remain, in sufficient numbers or in such a manner as to be an obstruction to free passage on the streets, sidewalks, highways or within any public place, within the corporate limits of the city.
(6)
Any person who, upon being assembled, picketing, parading or otherwise engaging in any acts covered by this article, shall engage in any acts, statements or other public utterance by which they attempt to incite a riot, revolt, a general disturbance of the peace or otherwise engage in any conduct which tends to incite or provoke riots, public disturbances, affrays or other similar conduct prohibited by law.
(7)
Any persons who have previously gathered within the corporate limits of the city, upon proper authority and with a permit, and have, on that occasion, committed any unlawful acts, or engaged in any conduct which incited a public disturbance, created an affray, resulted in a breach of the peace, or otherwise disrupted the good order and peace of citizens within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1969, § 15-70.2)
Sec. 70-33. - Exceptions and defenses to prosecution.
It shall be an affirmative defense to a prosecution for unlawful conduct as defined in section 70-32:
(1)
That the accused withdrew from the assembly, parade, picketing or other acts, on being lawfully commanded to do so by a peace officer or before any member of the assembly had inflicted injury to the person or property of another.
(2)
Any person who has applied, and received prior approval, for a permit pursuant to section 70-35, and conducts the demonstration, parade, assembly or other act under the supervision of the police department of the city, or other law enforcement officials who may be involved in the supervision of such conduct to ensure that it does not violate any conditions or provisions of the permit, or any of the laws of the state.
(Code 1969, § 15-71.1)
Sec. 70-34. - Conduct included.
For the purposes of sections 70-31, 70-32 and sections 70-35 through 70-39, the conduct which is to be regulated shall include any march, demonstration, picketing, assembly, procession, or any other public gathering on any public street, highway or sidewalk or in any public place within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1969, § 15-71.2)
Sec. 70-35. - Permits and registration.
(a)
Any person who wishes to organize, form or conduct any march or demonstration regulated in section 70-34 shall be required to register with the chief of police at least 48 hours in advance of the event, on forms to be provided by the chief of police, to obtain a permit therefor.
(b)
An application for a permit to conduct the acts set forth in this article shall be made to the chief of police in writing, shall be signed by the person responsible for the conduct of the event, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The date and time proposed for the event.
(2)
The route, place or other area of the proposed event.
(3)
The number of vehicles (if any), and the approximate number of persons who would participate in the proposed event.
(4)
The name and address of the person sponsoring or promoting the proposed event.
(5)
The name and address of the person making the application for the permit, and proper identification of that person.
(6)
The name and address of at least five principal parties who will also be responsible for, or who may participate in, the event, including proper identification of these parties.
(7)
The name, address, telephone number and authorization of the sponsoring organization and its leaders, if the activity is designed to be held by, for or on behalf of any person, group or organization other than the applicant.
(8)
A statement as to whether the parade, assembly or other activity will occupy all or only a portion of the width of the streets, the location of such streets, and any assembly areas for the group.
(Code 1969, § 15-72.1)
Sec. 70-36. - Review of permit application; procedure for approval or denial.
(a)
If the chief of police determines that the application has failed to meet the criteria set forth in section 70-35, it shall be returned to the applicant and no permit shall be granted until the application is completed in proper form.
(b)
The chairman of the city commission shall have authority, in consultation with the chief of police or designated officer in charge and pursuant to authority granted him by the city commission pursuant to Ordinance No. 4-1984, to approve the permit after consideration of the criminal record, if any, of the applicant and the following criteria:
(1)
The extent of vehicular and pedestrian traffic, and street use by the group anticipated at the time and place of, and on the route of, the proposed occurrence.
(2)
The availability of appropriate police forces and other law enforcement agency protection to escort the proposed group or assembly, to direct traffic in conjunction with the proposed event and to otherwise properly handle the assembly to protect the general welfare of the participants, the public who may be watching the event, and other persons who may be affected by the conducting of this event within the city.
(3)
Whether the proposed event will unreasonably burden or interfere with the normal use of the streets, sidewalks or other public areas of the city.
(4)
Whether the concentration of persons, animals and/or vehicles at assembly points or anticipated to be a part of the activity will not unduly interfere with the movement of ambulances, police protection equipment, firefighting equipment, and similar emergency vehicles.
(5)
Whether the conduct of the event would be reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, to provoke disorderly conduct or create a disturbance.
(6)
Whether the activity or purpose as stated in the application would violate any provision of this Code or any other ordinance, statute or federal law.
(Code 1969, § 15-72.2)
Sec. 70-37. - Disposition; appeal.
If the chief of police approves the forwarded application to the chairman of the city commission, pursuant to the criteria established above and the chairman determines, in view of the criteria, that the proposed event meets the criteria established pursuant to the provisions of section 70-36, he may grant the request. If there remains some determination concerning the criteria of the preceding section being met by the application, the chairman may set the matter over for a determination by the city commission at its next regular or specially scheduled meeting. The commission shall, upon a review of the application in conjunction with the criteria of section 70-36, make a final decision concerning the application. Any party aggrieved of a denial of a permit may, upon formal notification of the commission in writing of the denial and the reasons therefor, appeal to the superior court of the county for authorization of the proposed event.
(Code 1969, § 15-72.3)
Sec. 70-38. - Permit content and conditions.
The permit issued under this article shall contain the following with respect to the parade, picket line or demonstration:
(1)
Starting time.
(2)
Duration.
(3)
Space between persons or vehicles involved.
(4)
Portions or areas of the streets, alleys, sidewalks or other public places to be used.
(5)
Other reasonable requirements as may be necessary to promote free movement of pedestrian or vehicular traffic or to protect the health, safety and property rights of participants and the general public.
(Code 1969, § 15-72.4)
Sec. 70-39. - Exemptions.
The permit provisions of this article shall not apply to the following activities:
(1)
Funeral processions.
(2)
Students going to and from school classes or participating in educational activities that are under the immediate supervision and direction of proper school authorities.
(3)
Any state or federal governmental agency acting within the scope of its functions.
(4)
Regular church services on church premises.
(Code 1969, § 15-72.5)
Sec. 70-40. - Pickets and picketing allowed.
Peaceful picketing in furtherance of a lawful purpose shall be permitted in the city, and permits shall be obtained in the manner prescribed in this article.
(Code 1969, § 15-72.6)
Sec. 70-41. - Rules of picketing.
In addition to the foregoing rules and regulations in this article, the following shall apply specifically to pickets and picketing:
(1)
Any picketing shall be in single file and may not involve more than 15 persons promoting the same objective.
(2)
Picketers may not carry written or printed placards exceeding three feet in width and three feet in length promoting the objective for which the picketing is done. The staff on any such placard may not exceed 40 inches in length or three-fourths of an inch in width. Such staffs must be made of wood and must be blunt at both ends.
(3)
No picketer shall physically interfere with or attempt to interfere with another person in the exercise of his right to work or enjoyment of property or any other lawful activity by threat of force, violence, intimidation or other conduct tending to cause a breach of the peace.
(4)
No person shall physically interfere with persons engaged in peaceful picketing in the furtherance of a lawful purpose or engage in conduct toward such picketers that would tend to cause a breach of the peace.
(5)
Picketing shall not be conducted on a portion of a street used primarily for vehicular traffic.
Secs. 70-42—70-60. - Reserved.
