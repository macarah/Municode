DIVISION 11. - LEAVE

Sec. 2-341. - Types of leave.
The county recognizes five types of leave that are available to employees. They are: annual leave, sick leave, military leave, court leave, and leave of absence.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.01)
Sec. 2-342. - Annual (vacation) leave.
modified
Employees are encouraged to use their vacation time. No employee shall receive pay in lieu of vacation unless recommendation is made by the department head and specific approval is given by the county manager.
(1)
Eligibility. All full-time employees in the personnel system shall be entitled to accrue annual leave. Permanent part-time employees shall be entitled to leave in proportion to the number of hours worked. Temporary employees shall not be eligible for annual leave.
(2)
Rate of leave accrual. Full-time and permanent part-time employees accrue annual (vacation) leave immediately upon employment. Employees under temporary, substitute or emergency appointments, and seasonal employees will not be granted annual leave under these policies. Vacation is accrued per the following schedule:
EXPAND
Years of Service Days Accrued
1 to 10 years 12 days (1 day per month)
10 years or more 15 days (1¼ days per month)
  (3)
Request for leave. A request for leave shall be submitted to the employee's immediate supervisor. Leave may be taken only after approval by the appropriate department head so that the department can function without hiring additional temporary help.
(4)
Maximum allowable accumulation. Unused vacation leave not to exceed 30 days may be carried into the next calendar year.
(5)
Payment for unused leave. When an employee is separated from service, said employee shall be paid for all unused annual leave.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.02)
Sec. 2-343. - Sick leave.
modified
An employee may use accrued sick leave in the case of actual sickness or disability or for medical, dental or eye examination for which arrangements cannot be made outside of working hours; when the employee is required to care for a sick or injured spouse, child or relative who is domiciled in the employee's household. The employee shall report the illness prior to the employee's scheduled work time if possible. If not, the employee shall see that the illness is reported within 30 minutes after the time the employee is scheduled to have reported for work.
(1)
Eligibility. Those employees entitled to earn annual leave shall also be eligible to earn sick leave.
(2)
Rate of leave accrual. Full-time employees begin to accrue sick leave immediately upon employment at the rate of one day per month.
(3)
Certification by physician. A medical certificate signed by a licensed physician may be required by a department head to substantiate a request for sick leave for the following reasons:
a.
Any period of absence consisting of three or more consecutive working days;
b.
To support a request for sick leave during a period when the employee is on vacation leave;
c.
Leave of any duration if absence from duty recurs frequently or habitually provided the employee has been notified or warned that a certificate will be required.
(4)
Maximum allowable accumulation. Sick leave may not be accumulated in excess of 90 days.
(5)
Forfeiture of sick leave. An employee who separates from the personnel system shall forfeit all unused sick leave; and no employee shall be paid for unused sick leave.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.03)
Sec. 2-344. - Military leave.
modified
Georgia law requires that paid leave be granted to members of the Reserve and National Guard under certain conditions.
(1)
Ordered duty. In compliance with O.C.G.A. § 38-2-279, any employee ordered to military duty shall be placed on military leave with pay for a period of time not exceeding a total of 18 days in any one calendar year and not exceeding 18 days in any one continuous period of absence.
(2)
Declared emergency. According to O.C.G.A. § 38-2-279, in the event the governor declares an emergency and orders any employee to state active duty as a member of the National Guard, the employee shall receive pay for a period not exceeding 30 days in any one calendar year and not exceeding 30 days in any one continuous period of active duty service.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.04)
Sec. 2-345. - Jury leave.
An employee performing jury duty will be granted leave with pay. The employee will be permitted to retain all fees and allowances as well as retaining full county pay provided they are in fact selected for jury duty and submit written documentation of such service. An employee called for jury duty but not actually selected to serve on a jury or otherwise relieved from service during working hours will be expected to return to work that day.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.05)
Sec. 2-346. - Leave of absence.
modified
(a)
For military purposes. According to O.C.G.A. § 38-2-279, any voluntary member of the Reserve or National Guard shall be entitled to be and shall be deemed to have a leave of absence while in attendance at any service school conducted by the armed forces of the United States for a period up to six months during any four-year period.
(b)
For temporary disability and other reasons. A disabled employee may exhaust all accrued sick and annual leave. When paid leave is exhausted, the employee may be placed on leave of absence, depending on the duration of the employee's disability and the need to fill the position. A doctor's statement will be requested to determine the length of leave necessary. Maternity and other temporarily disabling conditions associated with pregnancy will be treated as any other disability.
(c)
Benefits during leave of absence. A leave of absence prevents a break in service, but no benefits such as leave or time toward retirement shall accrue during leave of absence.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.06)
Sec. 2-347. - Family and medical leave.
modified
Federal law requires that employees be permitted to take unpaid leave to care for the illness of themselves or a close family member under certain circumstances.
(1)
Eligibility. Employees of the county who have been employed for 12 months or more and who worked at least 1,250 hours during that time are entitled to 12 weeks of unpaid leave per year in connection with:
a.
The birth and first year care of a child;
b.
The adoption or foster parent placement of a child;
c.
The illness of an employee's spouse, child or parent with respect to a serious health condition, defined as one that requires in patient care in a hospital, hospice, or residential medical care facility, or which requires continuing treatment by a health care provider; or
d.
The employee's own illness. In the instance of birth, adoption, and/or foster placement, the entitlement for child-care ends:
1.
After the child reaches the age of one year; or
2.
12 months after the adoption or placement;
Entitlement for leave associated with the illness of a child occurs only where the child is under 18 years of age or incapable of self-care due to mental or physical disability.
(2)
Amount of leave available. In cases where both spouses are employed by the county, the combined amount of leave for child birth, adoption, and family illness is limited to 12 weeks.
(3)
Notification of anticipated leave. Except where circumstances are such that reasonable advance planning is not possible, employees must provide the county manager at least 30 days' notice of the date when the leave is to begin. With respect to foreseeable family or employee illness, the employee shall make a reasonable effort to schedule treatment, including intermittent and reduced hour leave, so as not to disrupt unduly the operations of the county, subject to approval of the employee's or family member's health care provider.
(4)
Benefits. Benefits accrued by the employee before leave is taken will not be altered by the employee's absence under this policy. The employee is entitled to continuation of any health benefits, if such benefits are available, during the leave period. Upon return, the employee is entitled to be restored to an equivalent position with equivalent pay, benefits, and conditions of employment.
(5)
Required certification.
a.
The county requires that a request for leave be supported by a certification issued by the appropriate health care provider of the eligible employee or of the son, daughter, spouse, or parent of the employee. The certification shall include:
1.
The date that the conditions commenced;
2.
The duration;
3.
The necessity for the employee's leave; and
4.
The employee's inability to perform said employee's job functions.
b.
The county reserves the right, at its own expense, to designate a second health care provider to provide a second opinion. A third such option, should it be necessary, shall be binding. Upon the employee's return to work, the county may require the employee to provide certification by the employee's health care provider that the employee is able to resume work.
(6)
Special provisions. The county manager shall make, keep, and preserve records showing compliance with the Family and Medical Leave Act.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.07)
Sec. 2-348. - Funeral leave.
Funeral leave of up to three working days shall be granted with pay for an employee absent from duty as a result of death in the immediate family. For purposes of this section, immediate family shall mean a spouse, child, father, mother, father-in-law, mother-in-law, brother, sister, grandmother, grandfather, or any relative who is domiciled in the employee's household.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 11.08)
