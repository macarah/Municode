Sec. 13-501. - Proximity to residences, complaint necessary for enforcement.
(a)
Except as hereinafter set forth, it shall be unlawful for any person to maintain any bait bed for the keeping or propagation of fishing worms at any place in the city which is within a radius of 150 feet of the nearest part of any dwelling house occupied by a person other than the owner or keeper of such bait bed.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not be applicable where no complaint is hereafter made to the city commission, or to the police department, by an occupant of a dwelling house within such prohibited distance.
(Ord. of 2-27-51, § 1)
Sec. 13-502. - Proximity to food establishments.
No bait bed for the keeping or propagation of fishing worms shall, under any circumstances, be maintained within 250 feet of any building or grounds where food is served to the public for human consumption.
(Ord. of 2-27-51, § 2)
Sec. 13-503. - Maximum area; not allowed when a nuisance.
Bait beds not exceeding an aggregate of ten square feet in area, where worms are kept or propagated for other than commercial purposes, and which comply with the following provisions of this article, may be maintained within the city, unless it be made to appear upon trial that their maintenance constitutes a nuisance.
(Ord. of 2-27-51, § 3)
Sec. 13-504. - Protection from insects.
In all cases, whether or not bait beds are maintained for commercial or other purposes, and whether or not complaint is made or nuisance alleged, all bait beds shall be kept covered by wire screening containing not less than 16 mesh to each linear inch, in order to keep out flies and other insects.
(Ord. of 2-27-51, § 4)
Sec. 13-505. - Effect on other Code provisions.
Nothing herein is intended to repeal or to limit the force of any other provision of this Code or any ordinance of the city prohibiting public or private nuisances.
(Ord. of 2-27-51, § 5)
