Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this chapter, the following words and terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed:
Dealer in gold and silver shall mean any person engaged in whole or in part, in the business of buying, trading or selling coins, jewelry, gold, silver, precious metals, etc., of any description from persons or sources other than manufacturers or licensed dealers.
Gems. The following stones are hereby designated "gems" for the purpose of this chapter: Diamonds, emeralds, rubies and sapphires.
Person shall mean an individual, partnership, corporation, joint venture, trust, association or any other legal entity, however organized.
Precious metals shall mean the following:
(1)
Gold:
(a)
Metallic items, 100 percent gold—24-karat;
(b)
Metallic items, 75 percent gold—18-karat;
(c)
Metallic items, 56.25 percent gold—14-karat;
(d)
Metallic items, 39.1 percent gold—10-karat;
(e)
Metallic items, 29 percent to 39 percent gold—7-to 9-karat;
(f)
Metallic items, 20 percent gold or greater by weight.
(Metallic items of less quantity than 20 percent gold by weight or content shall be nonprecious metal.)
(2)
Silver:
(a)
All 100 percent silver metal items—Pure silver;
(b)
All 92.5 percent silver metal items—Sterling silver;
(c)
All 90 percent silver metal items—Coin silver;
(d)
All 80 percent silver metal items—European silver;
(e)
All metallic items of silver containing 50 percent or more silver by weight or content.
(Metallic items of less quantity than 50 percent silver by weight or content are not termed precious metals.)
(3)
Platinum: All metallic items containing platinum are declared to be precious metals.
(4)
Iridium: All metallic items containing iridium are declared to be precious metals.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-2. - Business license—Required; application.
Any person within Columbus, Georgia, shall, before engaging in whole or in part, in the business of buying, trading or selling coins, jewelry, gold, silver, precious metals, etc., from persons or sources other than manufacturers or licensed dealers, file with the revenue collection division of Columbus, Georgia, an application for a business license to conduct and carry on said business as a dealer in gold and silver. The revenue collection division shall furnish the Columbus Police Department with a copy of said application at which time the police department shall verify the criminal criteria in the application and furnish the revenue collection division with their findings and recommendations.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-3. - Same—Exemptions.
The provisions hereof shall not apply when the item traded in is an item originally purchased from the dealer to whom it is traded. Said dealer shall be required, however, to demand positive identification of person trading in such item to demand proof that said person is the legal owner of such item.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-4. - Same—Restrictions.
No such license shall be issued to:
(1)
Reserved.
(2)
A person who is not of good character and reputation in the community;
(3)
A person who has been convicted of a felony or crime involving theft-related offenses within five years of issuance or renewal of license;
(4)
A person whose license under this chapter has been revoked for cause;
(5)
A person who, at the time of the application for renewal of any license issued hereunder, would not be eligible for such license under first application;
(6)
A copartnership, unless one of the members of such copartnership owning not less than five percent interest therein shall be qualified to obtain such a license;
(7)
A corporation if any officer, manager or director thereof, or any stockholder or stockholders owning in the aggregate of more than five percent of the stock in such corporation would not be eligible to receive a license hereunder for any reason other than a residence within Columbus, Georgia;
(8)
A person whose place of business is conducted by a manager or agent, unless said manager or agent possesses the same qualifications required of the licensee.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81; Ord. No. 83-4, § 1, 2-22-83; Ord. No. 95-26, 4-18-95)
Sec. 10-5. - Same—Application requirements; oath.
(A)
The application for said business shall be signed and dated by the applicant and contain answers to the following questions:
(1)
What is the kind of business to be operated?
(2)
What is the name of the business?
(3)
What is the location where such business is proposed to be carried on and the telephone number of said location?
(4)
What is the name of the applicant for the license, and the home address and telephone number of said applicant?
(5)
Where has applicant resided for the six months preceding the date of this application?
(6)
What was the applicant's previous home address, and how long was applicant a resident there?
(7)
What is the Social Security number of said applicant?
(8)
Applicant intends to do business as:
Individual; or
Partnership; or
Corporation?
(9)
Personnel of applicant: If the applicant is an individual, name the individual applying; if a partnership, name all the members of the partnership; if a corporation, name the president, vice-president, secretary and treasurer, as well as the board of directors; list all employees by name, title (individual, partner or corporation), age and residence address.
(10)
Who is the landlord or owner of said location, and what is the address and telephone number of said landlord or owner?
(11)
Has the applicant or any person connected with, employed by or having any interest in said business:
(a)
Been charged or convicted of any violation of law (other than minor traffic violations) in any locality?
(b)
Served time in prison, or other correctional institution?
(12)
If the answer to section (a) of the above question is "yes," state the circumstances in detail. Information must be complete as to dates, charges, court jurisdiction and disposition for each person. If the answer to section (b) of the above question is "yes," state the circumstances in detail. Information must be complete as to the charge on which convicted, name of prison or correctional institution, length of time served, date of release from prison or correctional institution, whether sentence has been completed, or whether on probation or parole, and the terms thereof.
(13)
Give the name, home address and telephone number and place of employment of two persons who are residents of Columbus, Georgia, who are familiar with your character and reputation.
(B)
The following oath shall be required on the application:
"All of the following information is hereby given and all of the foregoing statements are hereby made on oath, willfully, knowingly and absolutely, and the same is hereby sworn to be true under penalty for false swearing, as provided by law.
EXPAND
/s/ Applicant's Signature
    Sworn to and subscribed before me, this_______day of_______, 19_______.
EXPAND
/s/ Notary Public"
    (Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-6. - Same—Suspension or revocation.
(a)
The Council of Columbus, Georgia, shall have the right and privilege of granting or declining, within 60 days of the date of application, a business license to conduct said business, and if the same shall be granted, it shall be subject, however, to suspension or revocation for due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing and upon three days' prior written notice to holder of such receipt, the time, place and purpose of such hearing, and a statement of the charges upon which such hearing shall be held. Due cause for the suspension or revocation of such business license shall consist of a violation of any provision of this chapter or violation of any state or federal law involving theft-related offenses by the person or any officer, agent, representative or employee of the person.
(b)
Said business license shall not be transferable from person to person or location to location and shall expire at the end of each calendar year and may be renewable only in the manner and form provided in this section.
(c)
Those persons who held valid 1980 licenses pertaining to secondhand jewelry, gold and precious metals and who are in good standing shall, upon filing an application under this chapter, be allowed to continue an operation until such time as a 1981 license has been issued or denied without suffering any penalty for late purchase of such license under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-7. - Same—Rate.
The business license rate for the above-described business shall be $300.00 for each location. Any licensed pawnbroker or jeweler wishing to transact business under this chapter shall be required to fulfill all requirements under this chapter and shall pay a fee of $100.00 per year in addition to any other license fee issued under any other ordinance or ordinances. Provided, however, that all such licenses shall be issued to the same location.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-8. - Gross receipts tax.
The gross receipts tax under this chapter shall be $2.50 per $1,000.00.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-9. - Rules and regulations.
The following rules and regulations are hereby established, and shall govern the conduct and operation of every such place of business, and every person owning and operating such place of business:
(1)
Contents of record book. Every person shall maintain a book, in permanent form in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each purchase or sale the following information:
(a)
The date of the transaction;
(b)
The name of the person conducting the transaction;
(c)
The name and address of the customer, a description of the general appearance of the customer; and the number from the customer's valid driver's license, military I.D. card, alcoholic beverage card, or any other means of identification that contains birthdate, sex, race and address;
(d)
An identification and description of the purchased goods, or sold goods, including, if reasonably available, the serial, model or other number, and all identifying marks inscribed thereon. In addition, the dealer shall furnish any other identifying information that may be required on forms prescribed and furnished to dealer by the Columbus Police Department;
(e)
The price paid;
(f)
The number of the check issued for the purchase price; and
(g)
The signature of the customer.
(2)
Recorded entries; availability for inspection. Entries shall appear in chronological order in ink. No blank lines may be left between entries. No obliterations, alterations or erasures may be made. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying its legibility. The book shall be open to the inspection of any officer of the Columbus Police Department or representative of the revenue collection division's office during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time.
(3)
Maintenance period for records. The record of each purchase or sale transaction, as herein provided, shall be maintained for a period of not less than two years.
(4)
Holding period for merchandise. All merchandise purchased shall be maintained for inspection by an officer of the Columbus Police Department or representative of the revenue collection division's office for a period of not less than 15 days after the date of purchase. All items shall be maintained in the same condition as they existed when purchased by dealer; provided, however, that a licensee under this chapter need not hold such items for 15 days if he has purchased them from another licensee under this chapter who has held the same items for the required 15 days.
(5)
Purchases from minors. No merchandise shall be purchased from any individual under 18 years of age.
(6)
List of employees required. Each person shall be required to furnish an updated list to the Columbus Police Department of all new employees, whether temporary or permanent. All employees shall be subject to a thorough police check along the same standards as set forth for the licensee, except for residency.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
Sec. 10-10. - Violation; penalty.
All persons failing to comply with this chapter shall be punished according to section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of Columbus, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 81-20, § 1, 3-5-81)
