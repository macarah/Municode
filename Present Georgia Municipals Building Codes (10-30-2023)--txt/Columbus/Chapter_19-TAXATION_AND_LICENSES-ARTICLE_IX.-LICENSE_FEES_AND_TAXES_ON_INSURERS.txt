ARTICLE IX. - LICENSE FEES AND TAXES ON INSURERS[8]

Footnotes:

Editor's note— Ordinance No. 83-82, enacted Oct. 18, 1983, did not specifically amend this Code; hence, codification of §§ 1—6 of said ordinance as Art. IX, §§ 19-131—19-136, is at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 19-131. - Insurer license fees.
There is hereby levied, for the year 1984 and for each year thereafter, an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within Columbus, Georgia in the amount of $150.00. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 19-132, which is operating on behalf of insurers within Columbus, Georgia, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $150.00. For the purposes of this article, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. section 33-3-5.
(Ord. No. 83-82, § 1, 10-18-83; Ord. No. 84-47, § 1, 5-29-84)
Sec. 19-132. - License fee for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and, in connection with such loans or sales, offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $52.50 per location, for the year 1984 and for each year thereafter.
(Ord. No. 83-82, § 2, 10-18-83; Ord. No. 84-47, § 2, 5-29-84)
Sec. 19-133. - Insurance agency license fees; independent insurance agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed.
There is hereby levied, for the year 1984 and for each year thereafter, an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 19-131 hereof, in the amount of $150.00 for each such location within Columbus, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 83-82, § 3, 10-18-83; Ord. No. 84-47, § 3, 5-29-84)
Sec. 19-134. - Gross premiums tax imposed—On life insurers.
There is hereby levied, for the year 1984 and for each year thereafter, an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within Columbus, Georgia, in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. section 33-8-8.1. "Gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. section 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 19-131 of this article.
(Ord. No. 83-82, § 4, 10-18-83)
Sec. 19-135. - Same—On all other insurers.
There is hereby levied, for the year 1984 and for each year thereafter, an annual tax upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in subsection 1 of O.C.G.A. section 33-3-5, doing business within Columbus, Georgia, in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. section 33-8-8.2. "Gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. section 33-8-8.2(a).
(Ord. No. 83-82, § 5, 10-18-83)
Sec. 19-136. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 19-131, 19-132 and 19-133 of this article shall be due and payable on the first day of 1984 and on January 1 of each subsequent year.
(Ord. No. 83-82, § 6, 10-18-83)
Secs. 19-137—19-150. - Reserved.
