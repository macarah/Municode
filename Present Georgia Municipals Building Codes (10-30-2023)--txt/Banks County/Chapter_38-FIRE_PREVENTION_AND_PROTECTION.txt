ARTICLE II. - FIRE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 38-31. - Established.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 5-3-4, the board of commissioners (the board) hereby organizes and establishes a fire department for the unincorporated areas of the county for the following:
(1)
Protection of life and property against fires, explosions, hazardous materials, or electrical hazards;
(2)
Detection and prevention of arson;
(3)
Administration and enforcement of the laws of this state; the rules and regulations adopted by the board as related to the prevention and suppression of fires, explosions, or injuries from hazardous materials and the protection of life and property from such hazards;
(4)
Conducting programs of public education and fire prevention and safety;
(5)
Conducting emergency medical services and rescue assistance subject O.C.G.A. § 31-11-1 et seq.;
(6)
Control and regulation of the flow of traffic in existing areas of existing emergencies, including rail, highway, water and air;
(7)
To take the actions as outlined in O.C.G.A. § 25-3-1 et seq. necessary for the protection and preservation of life and/or property;
(8)
Adoption of any and all other rules and regulations necessary to the protection of the citizens of the county in accordance with the laws of the state.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § I, 11-12-1996)
Sec. 38-32. - Appointment of fire chief; duties.
The board of commissioners may appoint a fire chief, delegate his duties and responsibilities, and determine his compensation and term of employment. Notwithstanding such appointment, the fire chief shall serve at the pleasure of the board, supervise the activities of the county fire department, and perform such other duties as directed by the board.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § II, 11-12-1996)
Sec. 38-33. - Contracting for services.
Notwithstanding section 38-31, the board of commissioners may contract with various municipalities for fire protection in certain unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § III, 11-12-1996)
Sec. 38-34. - Qualification of members of department.
All members of the county fire department shall meet the qualifications of the Georgia Firefighter Standards and Training Act. All candidates for membership in the county fire department shall submit an application for membership and all candidates must be approved by the fire chief.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § IV, 11-12-1996)
Sec. 38-35. - Volunteer fire stations.
The volunteer fire stations now in existence in the unincorporated areas of the county will not receive any additional funding for the operation of such fire stations. The county shall not assume any existing obligations or liabilities of the volunteer fire stations unless the board specifically assumes such obligations and liabilities in writing.
(Ord. No. 96-12, § V, 11-12-1996)
Sec. 38-36. - Prohibited burning; exceptions.
No person shall cause, suffer, allow or permit open burning at any place in the unincorporated areas of the county except as follows:
(1)
Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for production or harvesting of crops;
(2)
The burning over of any forest land by the owners of such land;
(3)
For recreational purposes or cooking food for immediate human consumption;
(4)
Fires set for purposes of training firefighting personnel when authorized by the appropriate governmental entity and the guidelines set forth by the fire marshal are strictly observed;
(5)
Operation of devices using open flames such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters and other flame-making equipment;
(6)
Setting and maintenance by contractors and tradesman of miscellaneous small fires necessary to such activities as street paving work, and installation or repair of utilities, provided that such fires are kept small in size, that no smoke emissions exceed 40 percent opacity, and that local ordinances and regulations do not prohibit such actions;
(7)
Disposal of all packing materials previously containing explosives, in accordance with U.S. Department of Labor Safety regulations.
(Res. No. 2013-15, § 1, 9-10-2013)
Sec. 38-37. - Permitted burning.
The following shall be permitted if adequate disposal facilities for the particular combustible materials involved are not reasonably available:
(1)
Reduction of leaves on the premises on which they fall by the person in control of the premises;
(2)
Destruction of combustible demolition or construction materials either on sight or transported to a burning facility approved by the fire marshal;
(3)
Disposal of tree limbs from storm damage;
(4)
For weed abatement, disease, and pest prevention;
(5)
Open burning in other than predominately residential areas for the purpose of land clearing or construction or right-of-way maintenance provided the following conditions are met:
a.
Prevailing winds at the time of the burning are away from the major portion of the area's population;
b.
The location of the burning is at least 1,000 feet from any dwelling located in a predominately residential area;
c.
The amount of dirt on or in the material being burned is minimized;
d.
Heavy oils, asphaltic materials, items containing natural or synthetic rubber, or any materials other than plant growth are not being burned;
e.
Not more than one pile 60 feet by 60 feet, or equivalent, is being burned within a nine-acre area at one time; provided, however, that in no event shall the open burning of more than 100 cubic yards per day of material described in subsection (5) of this section be permitted unless the person performing such burning shall have first given two days' written notice of the time and place of such burning to the fire marshal.
(Res. No. 2013-15, § 1, 9-10-2013)
Sec. 38-38. - Prohibited smoke.
Except for a reasonable period to get a fire started, no smoke of a shade darker than a #2 of the Ringelmann Chart, or equivalent opacity, shall be emitted by any source of open burning listed in subsections 38-37(1) and (2).
(Res. No. 2013-15, § 1, 9-10-2013)
Sec. 38-39. - Open burning during air pollution episodes.
During an air pollution episode declared by the proper authorities, no open burning of any kind shall be permitted unless open burning is required in the performance of an official duty of any public office, or a fire is necessary to thwart or prevent a hazard which cannot be properly managed by any other means, or is necessary for the protection of public health.
(Res. No. 2013-15, § 1, 9-10-2013)
Sec. 38-40. - Open burning regulations.
The following regulations shall apply to open burning which is authorized in the unincorporated areas of the county:
(1)
No person shall kindle or maintain an open fire or authorize any such fire to be kindled or maintained without a permit or other proper authorization.
(2)
No person shall kindle or maintain an open fire or authorize any such fire to be kindled or maintained on any private land unless:
a.
The location is not less than 50 feet from any structure and adequate provision is made to prevent fire from spreading within 50 feet of any structure; or
b.
The fire is contained in an approved waste burner located safely not less than 15 feet from any structure.
(3)
Open fires shall be constantly attended by a competent person until such fire is extinguished. Such person shall have a garden hose connected to the water supply, or other fire extinguishing equipment, readily available for use.
(4)
The fire marshal may further regulate or prohibit any or all open burning when atmospheric conditions or local circumstances make such fires hazardous and may determine whether a permit is required or otherwise give authorization for open burning authorized by sections 38-36 and 38-37.
(Res. No. 2013-15, § 1, 9-10-2013)
Sec. 38-41. - Violations; penalty.
Any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership violating any provision of this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereinafter be amended, or shall fail to do anything required by this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the Magistrate Court of Banks County, and upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in the Official Code of Banks County, Georgia for each violation in the discretion of the court.
(Res. No. 2013-15, § 1, 9-10-2013)

Sec. 38-61. - Participation in mutual aid pact.
(a)
The county resolves to provide the best possible protection to its citizens in accordance with O.C.G.A. §§ 25-6-1—25-6-11. In order to maximize the saving of life and property when disastrous incidents occur, the county resolves to join the Georgia Mutual Aid Resources Pact as provided in O.C.G.A. § 25-6-1 et seq.
(b)
The county may withdraw its membership by delivering written notice to an officer of the pact given 90 days' notice.
(c)
There shall be no liability imposed on the county for any action or failure to act as a party of the Georgia Mutual Aid Resource Pact.
(Res. No. 2001-01, § 6, 1-9-2001)
