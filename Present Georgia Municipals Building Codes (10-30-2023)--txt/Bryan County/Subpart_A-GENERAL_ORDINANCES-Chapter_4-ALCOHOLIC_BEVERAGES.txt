Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]

ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Title.
The title of this chapter shall be "The Bryan County Alcoholic Beverage Code."
(Code 1996, § 8-101)
Sec. 4-2. - General terms.
When the terms "person," "retail consumption dealer," "wholesale dealer," or "wholesaler," "alcoholic beverage," "distilled spirits," "malt beverage" and "wine" are used in this chapter, they shall be used and applied as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
(Code 1996, § 8-102)
Sec. 4-3. - Prosecution and penalty for violations.
Any person convicted of violating any of the provision of this chapter shall be punished as provided in section 1-8; and further, upon the conviction of any holder of any alcoholic beverage license of a violation of this chapter, the commissioners shall be authorized to revoke the license of the violator. For the purpose of prosecution of violators of the chapter, each and every violation of every separate section hereof shall be considered a separate offense.
(Code 1996, § 8-115)
Secs. 4-4—4-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Alcoholic beverage licensing, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-3-2, 3-3-40 et seq., 3-4-110, 3-5-40 et seq., 3-6-40.

Sec. 4-25. - General provision.
Every person, firm or corporation who shall become eligible for any of the categories in section 4-27 after January 1, 1985, shall, before commencing business, apply to the office of the clerk of the county commissioners, or their representative(s), for the appropriate license(s).
(Code 1996, § 8-119)
Sec. 4-26. - Business must apply for license.
From and after the passage of the ordinance from which this article is derived, every person engaged in either a retail or wholesale alcoholic beverage business shall file an application for and obtain a license to conduct such business with the county commissioners, to be approved by the county commissioners before engaging in or continuing said business.
(Code 1996, § 8-104)
Sec. 4-27. - Fees.
(a)
The annual license fees are hereby fixed and assessed on an annual basis or on a prorated basis as set forth in subsection (b) of this section. Fees shall be as established by the board and shall be paid annually to the county commissioners. The following classification of each business is hereby established:
(1)
Retail beer dealer.
(2)
Wholesale beer dealer. This license shall include the right to sell only to wholesalers or retailers holding licenses to sell beer.
(3)
Retail liquor dealer selling liquor by the package only.
(4)
Retail establishment selling liquor by the drink (pouring).
(5)
Wholesale liquor dealer. This license shall include the right to sell only to wholesalers or retailers holding licenses to sell liquor.
(6)
Distiller or manufacturer of alcoholic beverages.
(7)
Retail wine dealer.
(8)
Wholesale wine dealer. This license shall include the right to sell only to wholesalers and retailers holding licenses to sell wine.
(b)
Every person engaged in any of the businesses provided for in this section and in this article shall pay the aforesaid license fees for every separate place in which he transacts or engages in said business. In the event that an application for a license shall be filed later than January 1 of any year, there shall be no pro-ration of the license fees.
(c)
There shall be no transfer of any alcoholic beverage license from one person or entity to another; if a business is sold or otherwise transferred during the calendar year, the new owner shall apply for a new license in his name.
(d)
Applications for a new alcoholic or malt beverage license must be accompanied by an application fee as established by the board.
(Code 1996, § 8-106; Ord. of 10-13-2009)
State Law reference— Authorized license fees, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-4-50, 3-5-41.
Sec. 4-28. - Expiration date.
Licenses shall expire at the end of each calendar year, and may be renewed only in the manner and form as provided in this article for the original granting of licenses. Failure to renew a business license by January 31 of each year shall incur the following penalty charges:
(1)
An execution of 50 percent of the regularly required fees.
(2)
A subpoena to the magistrate court of the county for violation of this article.
(Code 1996, § 8-105)
Sec. 4-29. - Granting the license.
(a)
The commissioners of the county shall grant a license hereunder when the same is found to be in the public interest and general welfare.
(b)
The commissioners of the county, in determining whether or not any license or permit applied for hereunder shall be granted, shall consider the following standards:
(1)
The applicant's reputation, character, and mental and physical capacity to conduct a business engaged in the sale of wine, beer, and/or alcoholic beverages.
(2)
The location for which the license is sought as to traffic congestion, general character of neighborhood, and the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values.
(3)
The number of licenses previously granted for similar businesses in the trading area of the place for which the license is sought.
(4)
The proximity of the location of said establishment to schools, churches, libraries, and other public buildings in the surrounding area as provided for in section 4-77.
(5)
Whether the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell wine, beer and/or alcoholic beverages, and whether the applicant has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
(6)
If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell wine, beer, and/or alcoholic beverages, whether unusual police observation and inspection were required, including the number and frequency of complaints filed by citizens objecting to the manner of conducting business at said location.
(7)
No licenses shall be granted to any applicant who is an illegal alien, and if the applicant is a partnership or corporation, then at least one of the active partners, or the principal stockholder, must not be an illegal alien.
(8)
No license shall be issued to any applicant if any individual applicant or any individual having an interest as either an owner, partner or principal stockholder, whether such interest is direct or indirect, or such individual's spouse shall have been convicted of or had accepted plea of nolo contendere to any felony or misdemeanor either in the state, or any other state or possession of the United States within the past ten years, or of any municipal ordinance except traffic violations. The term "conviction," as used herein, shall include the forfeiture of an appearance bond.
(Code 1996, § 8-110)
Sec. 4-30. - Revocation of license.
(a)
In addition to any criminal penalty that may be imposed by section 4-3, the commissioners of the county shall have the right to deny, suspend or cancel any license if:
(1)
The license application is not filed in good faith or is filed by some person as a subterfuge for any other person.
(2)
Any applicant for a license or any licensee under this article willfully fails to comply with any provisions of this article.
(3)
Any person to whom a license has been issued is no longer engaged in the dealing of alcoholic beverages or no longer qualifies as a licensee under this article.
(b)
Before any denial, suspension or cancellation of a license granted under this article, the applicant or licenses shall be afforded notice and hearing as follows:
(1)
The notice shall be served personally or by certified mail not less than 20 days before the hearing. The notice shall include:
a.
A statement of the time, place and nature of the hearing;
b.
A statement of the legal authority under which the hearing is to be held;
c.
A reference to the section of ordinances and statutes involved;
d.
A short and plain statement of the matters asserted;
e.
A statement as to the right of any party to representation by legal counsel at the hearing, the right of any party to present evidence on all issues, and the right of any party to subpoena witnesses and documentary evidence.
(2)
At the hearing, all parties may be represented by legal counsel, and may respond to and present evidence on all issues involved.
(3)
The commissioners of the county, as the hearing agency, shall have the right to exercise the powers provided in O.C.G.A. § 50-13-13, to promulgate such other rules for the orderly disposition of the hearing as they deem appropriate.
(Code 1996, § 8-116)

Sec. 4-49. - Tax on wholesalers.
Each wholesale dealer in malt beverages, spirituous liquors, or wines shall pay, in addition to the regular license provided by this chapter, a tax on beverages sold to retailers located within the unincorporated area of the county, in accordance with the following schedule. Rates are established by this article in accordance with law.
(1)
Malt beverages.
a.
$1.20 per case of 48 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains six ounces.
b.
$1.40 per case of 48 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains seven ounces.
c.
$1.20 per case of 36 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains eight ounces.
d.
$1.00 per case of 24 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains ten ounces.
e.
$1.20 per case of 24 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains 12 ounces.
f.
$1.40 per case of 24 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains 14 ounces.
g.
$1.60 per case of 24 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains 16 ounces.
h.
$1.60 per case of 12 bottles or cans when each bottle or can contains 32 ounces.
i.
$0.44 per barrel, keg, or drum with a capacity of 1.125 gallons.
j.
$0.87 per barrel, keg, or drum with a capacity of 2.25 gallons.
k.
$1.50 per barrel, keg, or drum with a capacity of 3.875 gallons (one-eighth barrel).
l.
$3.00 per barrel, keg, or drum with a capacity of 7.75 gallons (one-fourth barrel).
m.
$6.00 per barrel, keg, or drum with a capacity of 15.5 gallons (one-half barrel).
n.
$12.00 per barrel, keg, or drum with a capacity of 31 gallons (one barrel).
(2)
Distilled spirits. For liquor sold to retailers located within the unincorporated area of the county for sale either by the package or by the drink, the tax shall be $0.22 cents per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(3)
Wines. For wine sold to retailers located within the unincorporated area of the county, an excise tax shall be paid at the rate of $0.22 per liter. The tax on wine sold in other than one gallon containers shall be computed proportionately.
(Code 1996, § 8-107)
State Law reference— Wholesale taxes, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-4-80, 3-4-131, 3-5-80 et seq., 3-6-60.
Sec. 4-50. - Sale prohibited if taxes are not paid.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell at retail or for consumption any malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors on which the taxes provided for in this article have not been paid to the wholesale dealer or distributor for the county.
(b)
It shall likewise be unlawful for any wholesale dealer or other person to deliver any malt beverages, wines or distilled spirits to any retail dealer in the county without first collecting the taxes as provided in this article.
(Code 1996, § 8-108)
Sec. 4-51. - Possession by retailers prohibited unless taxes are paid.
It shall be unlawful for any retail dealer to have in his possession any alcoholic beverage unless the tax required by the county has been paid to the wholesaler, distributor or other source delivering or causing the beverages to be delivered to the retail dealer. Whenever any such beverages are found in the possession of a retail dealer and the records of the payment of the taxes do not appear upon the records of the wholesaler, it will be presumed that the tax has not been paid and the burden shall be upon the retailer or seller to prove such fact. All aforesaid beverages found in the possession of any retail dealer on which the tax has not been paid shall be subject to confiscation by county authorities and shall be sold by county authorities and the receipts thereof paid into the county treasury.
(Code 1996, § 8-109)

ARTICLE IV. - REGULATIONS

Sec. 4-76. - Possession and reading of this chapter.
It shall be the duty of the management of all premises licensed hereunder to maintain a copy of this chapter on such premises and to instruct all employees on the terms thereof, and no license shall be issued to any applicant until such applicant shall have read the entire text of this chapter and certify in writing such reading and knowledge thereof.
(Code 1996, § 8-118)
Sec. 4-77. - Rules and regulations.
The following rules and regulations are hereby established and shall govern the conduct and operation of every such place of business and of every person owning and operating such place of business:
(1)
All establishments duly licensed hereunder must fully comply with all sanitary requirements promulgated by the county health department.
(2)
All musical units or amusement machines installed in any establishment licensed hereunder must be properly registered with the office of the county commissioners.
(3)
Illegal gambling conducted on the premises of any establishment licensed hereunder shall be cause for revocation of such license.
(4)
Disorderly conduct in any place of business or other place where the beverages provided for in the chapter are permitted to be sold is expressly forbidden by this article, and every person owning and/or operating a place of business for which a license has been granted, and for which the license fee has been paid, shall be subject to license revocation, in the manner provided for in this chapter, for permitting any disorderly conduct in such places of business.
(5)
Retail establishments shall not sell alcoholic beverages before 8:00 a.m. or after 12:00 midnight, except on Thanksgiving and Christmas Day when the sale of alcoholic beverages are not permitted.
(6)
No person shall serve, sell or give away distilled spirits or alcoholic beverages on Sunday unless expressly permitted below.
a.
The sale of alcoholic beverages on the premises is permitted on Sundays from 12:30 p.m. until 12:00 midnight in:
1.
Any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served; or
2.
Any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging.
b.
The sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption off premises is permitted on Sundays from 12:30 p.m. until 11:30 p.m. by any licensed retail dealer.
c.
A violation of subsection (6)a or b of this section will be grounds for immediate suspension of the establishment's license to sell alcohol.
(7)
Retail dealers shall keep their place of business sufficiently lighted to protect the health and safety of patrons and persons lawfully on the premises.
(8)
Retail grocery establishments selling malt beverages and/or wine shall permit sales only between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on every day except Sundays, when such hours shall be between 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m., and on Thanksgiving Day, and Christmas Day, when the sale of alcoholic beverages are not permitted.
(9)
No person shall sell, serve or give any alcoholic beverage to any person who is a minor, or who is physically or mentally incapacitated, or who is intoxicated, or to any person who is procuring such alcoholic beverages for such persons.
(10)
In all establishments serving alcoholic beverages by the drink, or permitting consumption on the premises, entertainment may be provided, but entertainment which is immoral, indecent or lewd and is considered obscene according to legal standards is forbidden, and all entertainers in such establishments shall be fully clothed at all times.
(11)
The county reserves the right to promulgate additional regulations or ordinances pertaining to the conduct of the business of selling alcoholic beverages and any person licensed hereunder shall be subject to such additional regulations that may be promulgated from time to time by the commissioners of the county.
(Code 1996, § 8-111; Ord. of 10-7-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2010; Ord. of 1-13-2015)
Sec. 4-78. - Bar cards.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any holder of wine, beer, or alcoholic beverages licenses as aforementioned to permit himself or any other person(s) to work or to serve in or about his place of business, in which said wine, beer and alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises, unless said person has a registration card issued by the county commissioners, setting forth therein the name of the employer, employee and the location of the place of employment.
(b)
The registration card, or bar card, shall be issued annually and shall not be transferable from one bar owner to another, nor from one location to another. Each person holding a registration card, or bar card, shall annually, likewise, get a health certificate from the county health department.
(c)
The fee shall be as established by the board for each registration card, or bar card, and shall be paid by the bar owner.
(Code 1996, § 8-112)
Sec. 4-79. - Sunday sales.
(a)
The intent of this section is to allow for the regulated, retail package sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption off premises, and to permit those full service restaurants that serve the public to sell alcoholic beverages with meals on Sunday. It is not the intent of this section to encourage local taverns and bars to serve alcoholic beverages on Sunday via the mechanism of serving incidental meals on Sunday. Therefore, there shall be no Sunday sales of alcoholic beverages in the unincorporated areas of the county, except as provided in section 4-77(6), and in compliance with the following.
(b)
In order to be eligible to pour or serve alcohol for consumption on the premises, the licensed establishment must satisfy each of the following requirements:
(1)
Food service establishments shall:
a.
Be licensed to sell distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine;
b.
Derive at least 50 percent of their total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food, as demonstrated by the establishment's gross receipts, all records and reports of which shall be made available for inspection and audit as needed upon reasonable demand by such officers designated by the county for the enforcement of this section;
c.
Have an area specifically designed, set aside, set up and operating to serve meals and food on the premises and shall have a fully-equipped commercial kitchen to include an appropriate stove, refrigerator, food preparation area, sink, and other items required by the county health department and inspections department for the preparation of foods;
d.
Have a printed or posted menu from which selections of prepared meals can be made; and
e.
Have a valid county health department food service permit and any other applicable local, state or federal permits, licenses, etc., required for food service establishments.
(2)
Lodging establishments shall:
a.
Be licensed to sell distilled spirits, malt beverages, or wine; and
b.
Derive at least 50 percent of their total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging as demonstrated by the establishment's gross receipts, all records and reports of which shall be made available for inspection upon reasonable demand by such officers designated by the county for the enforcement of this section.
(3)
Establishments shall annually certify that they meet the requirements set forth above by submission of the annual affidavit of Sunday sales to the board of commission.
(4)
The sale of alcohol in violation of this section will be grounds for immediate suspension of the establishment's license to sell alcohol.
(Code 1996, § 8-113; Ord. of 1-13-2015)
State Law reference— Sales on Sunday, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-3-7, 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-80. - Public facilities.
It shall be unlawful to sell, possess or consume any alcoholic beverages at the Hendrix Park or Richmond Hill Park or any other public facility during any public gathering. It shall be unlawful to consume any alcoholic beverages or controlled substances in any of the neighborhood parks, grounds, boat ramps, public facilities, or areas which are owned and/or operated under the jurisdiction of the county.
(Code 1996, § 8-114)
