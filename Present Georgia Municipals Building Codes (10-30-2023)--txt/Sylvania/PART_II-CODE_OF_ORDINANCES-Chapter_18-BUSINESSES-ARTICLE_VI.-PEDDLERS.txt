ARTICLE VI. - PEDDLERS[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Disabled veterans and blind persons engaging in peddling, O.C.G.A. § 43-12-1; Georgia Charitable Solicitations Act of 1988, O.C.G.A. § 43-17-1 et seq.; peddlers and itinerant traders, O.C.G.A. § 43-32-1 et seq.; Transient Merchants Act of Georgia, O.C.G.A. § 43-46-1 et seq.

Sec. 18-131. - Definition.
For purposes of this article, the term "peddler" shall be defined as any person or entity which sells goods, wares or merchandise from a location which is not owned or rented by such person or entity or from a vehicle or structure which is not permanently affixed to real estate.
(Ord. No. 264, § I, 6-17-86)
Sec. 18-132. - Restrictions.
No peddler may sell or display his goods, wares or merchandise on any street or sidewalk within the municipal boundaries of the city. Peddlers may sell and display their goods, wares or merchandise only upon private property with the permission of the property owner upon compliance with the other provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 264, § II, 6-17-86)
Sec. 18-133. - Licensing requirements.
All peddlers shall be granted a business license by the city upon the payment of a license fee of $300.00 together with the applicant's providing written proof satisfactory to the city clerk that such applicant has permission from a private property owner to display and sell his goods, wares or merchandise.
(Ord. No. 264, § III, 6-17-86)
Sec. 18-134. - Exceptions.
None of the provisions of this article shall extend to persons or entities selling agricultural products grown in the county.
(Ord. No. 264, § IV, 6-17-86)
