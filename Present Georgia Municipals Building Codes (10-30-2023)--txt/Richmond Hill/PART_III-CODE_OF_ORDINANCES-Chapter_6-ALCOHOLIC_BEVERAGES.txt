ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Sale to minors or incapacitated persons prohibited.
modified
No person who holds a license to sell alcoholic beverages shall sell or give away, or permit the sale or gift to, or the procuring for, any such alcoholic beverages to any minor, to any person who is mentally incompetent, or to any person who is physically or mentally incapacitated due to the consumption of liquor.
(a)
Government identification required. It shall be the duty of every licensee in the city to insure that every person acting on behalf of said licensee who is engaged in selling, bartering, trading, giving or otherwise furnishing any alcoholic beverage to any customer to require said customer to furnish and display an identification card issued by a governmental agency to verify his/her age.
(Ord. No. 35, 5-21-74; Ord. of 12-1-08, §§ I, II)
Sec. 6-2. - Persons under twenty-one years of age not permitted on certain premises.
(a)
No person under the age of 21 years shall be permitted to enter upon the premises of or remain on the premises of any business establishment operating under a class II on premises alcoholic beverage license, except that persons at least 18 years of age, who are bona fide employees of the establishment and who hold valid alcoholic beverage worker's cards, may be on such premises while they are on duty.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person under 21 years of age to possess any alcoholic beverage, except as provided in this chapter.
(c)
No licensee shall permit, allow or require any person in his employment or under his control who is under the age of 18 years to dispense, sell, serve or take orders for any alcoholic beverage, except that persons 16 years of age or older who are employed in business establishments which operate only under licenses issued for off premises consumption, except package shops, may sell or handle alcoholic beverages while on duty and during the course of their employment.
(d)
No package shop shall employee or permit any person under the age of 21 years to enter upon or remain in its premises.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person under 21 years of age to willfully misrepresent his/her age to any other person for the purpose of inducing such other person to dispense, sell, give or otherwise furnish to the person making such false representation, either directly or indirectly, any alcoholic beverage of any kind or description.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person under 21 years of age to consume any alcoholic beverage.
(Ord. No. 35, 5-21-74; Ord. of 12-1-08, § III)

ARTICLE II. - LICENSES

Sec. 6-21. - License required; fee.
(a)
Each and every business, individual, corporation, partnership, association or other entity desiring to engage in the sale or dispensing of alcoholic beverages in the city shall be required to be licensed by the city and shall be required to pay all applicable license fees.
(b)
The city shall issue the following classifications of alcoholic beverages licenses:
(1)
Package shop licenses for the sale of whiskey, spirits and liquor by the package for consumption off premises only.
(2)
Sale of beer by the package for consumption off premises only.
(3)
Sale of wine by the package for consumption off premises only.
(4)
Alcoholic beverage caterers license for the sale of any alcoholic beverages for consumption at catered events only.
(5)
Sale of any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises. These licenses shall be further sub-classified into three categories as follows:
a.
Class I—This class of consumption on premises license shall be held only by restaurants featuring a full service menu, and having a full kitchen and food preparation facilities.
b.
Class II—This class of consumption on premises license shall be held by taverns, nightclubs, bars and similar establishments whose primary business is the sale of alcohol for consumption on premises and who do not classify as "full service restaurants".
c.
Class III—This class of consumption on premises license shall be held by businesses other than full service restaurants where the sale of alcohol on the premises is coincidental to some other non-alcohol business, such as bowling alleys, billiard parlors, golf courses, and similar businesses.
d.
The holder of any class I license for consumption on premises, or any caterers license desiring the privilege to sell or otherwise dispense any alcoholic beverage on Sunday may apply to city council for a special endorsement on his/her/its class I or caterers license to permit the sale or dispensing of such alcoholic beverages on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. on Sundays and 12:00, midnight, if and only if such class I or caterers license holder meets all of the following requirements:
1.
The licensee shall hold a current business license for a restaurant or catering business.
2.
Such restaurant or catering business, within the last year, derived at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served or catered from, or in the case of a new business, fully intends and expects to derive at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is to be served or to be catered from.
3.
Such restaurant or catering business shall be a licensed bona fide, public restaurant or catering business which meets all health department standards and actually and regularly prepares and serves food on the premises or at catered locations from a full, on premises, operating kitchen. Additionally, in the case of a restaurant, such restaurant, in order to qualify for a Sunday sales endorsement must meet be equipped with a full kitchen facility that is staffed during all hours of said restaurants operation, and must include, at minimum, one cooking stove and one standard sized oven (this requirement is not met by a microwave oven and/or food rotisserie), one standard sized refrigerator and freezer and one kitchen sink. Said restaurant must further have adequate staff on duty at all times to both cook and serve meals to the general public, have a dedicated public dining room, offering substantial entrees and must have the full staff and ability to serve such entrees at any time said restaurant is open. Further, such restaurant or catering business shall provide a full service menu, and shall provide full food service to the public on any Sunday when alcoholic beverages are sold and dispensed. Provided further, that no restaurant may meet these food service requirements with any food that is catered or otherwise not fully prepared on the premises and served by said restaurant's own staff.
4.
Any restaurant or catering business applying for such special endorsement shall at least annually, affirm its meeting these conditions by the filing of an affidavit with the city licensing department. Such affidavit may be filed at any time the above conditions are met by the establishment, but must be filed at least annually, with its license application, and city council may, in its discretion, require monthly filing from any restaurant or catering business applying for or holding such special endorsement.
5.
Any establishment not fully complying with this section is hereby specifically prohibited from selling or dispensing any alcoholic beverage on Sunday.
(c)
The annual license fees for such licenses shall be as follows:
(1)
Package shop license, consumption off premises only, $1,500.00.
(2)
Sale of beer by the package, consumption off premises only, $500.00.
(3)
Sale of wine by the package, consumption off premises only, $500.00.
(4)
Any beverage, consumption on premises only, class I, $1,750.00.
(5)
Any beverage, consumption on premises only, class II, $1,750.00.
(6)
Any beverage, consumption on premises only, class III, $1,750.00.
(7)
Any beverage, consumption on premises only, caterers license, $1,750.00
(8)
Special endorsement for Sunday sales, class I and caterers license, consumption on premises only, $250.00.
(9)
Special endorsement for sales on adjacent and contiguous premises, consumption on premises only, $250.00.
(10)
Caterers license for any beverage for one event only (this license is not renewable), $250.00.
(d)
All such licenses, except the single-event, caterers license specified at subsection (c)(10), which shall not be renewable, shall be issued and renewed on an annual basis only, and, in the event of an application for less than a calendar year period, the license fee shall be prorated down to the current month.
(e)
Should any license be revoked for cause, surrendered, or should any establishment holding a license for any other reason cease to do business, there shall be no proration or refund of any license fee paid.
(f)
Each license issued under this section shall be issued for a specific location only and may not be transferred to another location. A separate license and application is required for each licensed premises, except that a caterers license holder may cater alcoholic beverages to any lawful location in the city under that caterers license.
(Ord. No. 3, 8-28-62; Ord. of 2-18-75; Ord. of 12-16-97, §§ 1—4; Ord. of 12-1-08, § IX; Ord. of 4-7-09, § III)

Sec. 6-24. - Standards for issuance.
With respect to the issuance of licenses or the renewal of licenses, the mayor and council may, in its discretion, issue or deny any license or renewal of an existing licensed location based on the following standards, in addition to standards otherwise ordained herein:
(1)
Where there is evidence that, even though there is compliance with the minimum distances from schools, libraries and churches as provided in section 6-30, the type and number of schools, libraries or churches in the vicinity causes minors to frequent the immediate areas.
(2)
Where there is evidence that the location or type of structure could create difficulty in police supervision.
(3)
Where there is evidence that there are no licenses granted in the area or where there is evidence that the proposed area is adequately supplied with such licenses.
(4)
Where there is evidence that a license for the location would be detrimental to the property values in the area.
(5)
Where there is evidence that the license in that location would be detrimental to traffic conditions or that there is a lack of sufficient parking spaces for automobiles.
(6)
If the applicant was the previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages, whether or not he has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to that business.
(7)
If the applicant was the previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages, the manner in which he conducted the business, including but not limited to, the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection of the premises.
(8)
Where there is evidence of prior arrest or conviction of any of the employees at the location of the licensee.
(9)
Whether a person's license issued under the police powers of the city has been previously suspended or revoked.
(Ord. No. 35, 5-21-74; Ord. of 12-20-05; Ord. of 12-1-08, § XI)

Sec. 6-30. - Distance requirements.
(a)
No license shall be issued hereunder where the place of business of the licensee is located in any area prohibited by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21, as amended, nor where the building of the proposed alcohol business is situated less than 200 yards from the nearest church, shrine, chapel or other place used exclusively for religious services or any school, college, state licensed kindergarten, where more than five children are kept on a regular basis, or state licensed daycare center, where more than five children are kept on a regular basis.
(b)
All distances set out hereunder shall be measured in the following manner:
1.
From the exterior edge of the closed door of the main public entrance of the premises in which the beverage alcohol is to be sold or offered for sale, measure, in a straight line to the center of the nearest public street, road, or highway, including, but not limited to, the semi-public lanes of parking lots, driveways and other ways of vehicular ingress and egress available to patrons of the subject premises. This measurement establishes component "A" of the total distance.
2.
From the point established in subpart (b)(1) above, measure by the nearest lawful means of vehicular travel to the center of the nearest public street, road or highway, including, but not limited to, the semi-public lanes of parking lots, driveways and other ways of vehicular ingress and egress available to patrons of the prohibiting premises. This measurement establishes component "B" of the total distance.
3.
From the point established in subpart (b)(2) above, measure in a straight line to the exterior edge of the closed door of the main public entrance of the prohibiting premises. This measurement establishes component "C" of the total distance.
4.
Where either the premises where the beverage alcohol is to be sold or offered for sale, or the prohibiting premises is located above the ground floor level of a multi-story building, measure vertically from the ground floor elevation of the main public entrance of the multi-story building to the finished floor elevation of either the premises in which the beverage alcohol is to be sold or offered for sale, or the prohibiting use. This measurement established component "D" of the total distance.
5.
Add components "A", "B", "C" and "D" to determine the total distance for the purposes of this section.
(c)
No person, specifically including any alcoholic beverage caterer, shall sell, dispense or serve for compensation any alcoholic beverage within the prohibited area set out above.
(d)
Notwithstanding any other provision to the contrary, when and only when council shall, pursuant to section 50-8(b) of this Code of Ordinances, issue a special event permit for the same, a bona fide church may, within an enclosed building located entirely on its premises, dispense alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises only at such special event, held only by such bona fide church, and so permitted under section 50-8(b).
(Ord. No. 35, 5-21-74; Ord. of 12-20-05; Ord. of 7-3-07(1); Ord. of 4-7-09, § IV; Ord. of 6-21-11)
Sec. 6-31. - Suspension; revocation.
(a)
No license or special endorsement issued hereunder shall be suspended or revoked except for due cause as hereinafter defined and after a hearing before city council with at least a three-day written notice to the licensee of the time, place and purpose of the hearing, and a statement of said alleged due cause. Said licensee shall be entitled to appear before council at the hearing both personally and by attorney, shall be entitled to cross-examine witnesses against him/her/it, and shall be entitled to present witnesses in his/her/its own behalf. Due cause for the suspension or revocation of a license shall consist of any of the following:
(1)
Violation of any state or federal law, except traffic violations;
(2)
Violation of any city or local ordinance other than traffic violations;
(3)
Violation of any provision of this chapter;
(4)
Any material, false statement, or material omission relating to any license issued hereunder, including, but not limited to, any material, false statement or material omission on any application.
(b)
At all hearings for suspension or revocation of any license issued under this Code, council shall consider the following criteria:
(1)
The alleged violation(s);
(2)
The past history of the licensee;
(3)
The materiality of any false statement or omission that may be involved;
(4)
The harm or potential harm of the alleged violation;
(5)
The number and nature of calls to the police department relating to the licensee;
(6)
The harm or potential harm to the public;
(7)
The harm or potential harm to the licensee;
(8)
The number of past violations or warnings;
(9)
The number of past incidents occurring on the premises.
(c)
Following said hearing, and after consideration of the above criteria, council may take any of the following actions:
(1)
Suspend the license or special endorsement for any period of time, up to the remainder of the license or special endorsement;
(2)
Revoke the license or special endorsement;
(3)
Issue a warning;
(4)
Table the matter.
(d)
Any special endorsement shall be automatically suspended without the need for a hearing upon the licensee's failure to timely file any required affidavit.
(Ord. No. 35, 5-21-74; Ord. of 12-1-08, § XIV)

(a)
Licenses hereunder shall not be transferable, except as otherwise provided herein. In case of the death of any person holding a license, or any interest therein, the same may be transferred to the administrator, executor or the lawful heirs of the deceased person, if otherwise qualified.

ARTICLE III. - PREMISES SERVING ALCOHOL

Sec. 6-51. - Definitions.
modified
Alcoholic beverages. Any liquid intended for human consumption which contains ethyl or drinking alcohol in any amount or concentration, and includes, but is not limited to, distilled spirits, wines, ciders and all malt beverages containing any amount of alcohol.
Licensee. As used in this article, a licensee is an establishment holding a license to engage in the sale of distilled spirits or spirituous liquors, wines, champagnes or malt beverages for consumption on the premises by the drink.
Premises. The definite, closed or partitioned in location of the licensed premises, whether room, shop or building, where alcoholic beverages are sold by the drink, or in the case of a catered event, the location of the event, as set out in the permit for the event. In addition, the term "premises" may include, but only where specifically approved by council, and so endorsed on the license, any area that is directly adjacent and contiguous to the licensed premises, and which meets all of the following requirements of section 6-54 of this Code.

Sec. 6-54. - Sales off premises prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any sales to be made outside of the building, premises or place of business licensed for such sale.
(a)
If, and only if, approved by council and so endorsed on the license, the licensee of a consumption on premises license may serve alcoholic beverages outside the premises in any area that is directly adjacent and contiguous to the licensed premises and which meets all of the following requirements:
(1)
Full approval by council;
(2)
Has the same address as the licensed premises;
(3)
Is owned or leased and is exclusively controlled by the licensee;
(4)
Is not public domain nor only nominally under the exclusive control of the licensee;
(5)
The alcoholic beverages must be served from the same bar or serving location that services the main premises;
(6)
Any consumption on premises licensee applying for such special endorsement shall at least annually, affirm his/her/its meeting these conditions by the filing of an affidavit with the city licensing department. ARTICLE IV. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE WORKER'S CARDS

Sec. 6-70. - Alcoholic beverage worker's cards.
modified
(a)
Employees. Every person selling, serving or dispensing any alcoholic beverage in every licensee's premises shall at all times have and maintain on their person a current, valid alcoholic beverage worker's card issued by the city police department.
(b)
Operation without. It shall be unlawful for the holder of any alcoholic beverage license to personally sell, serve or dispense any alcoholic beverage or allow any other person to sell, serve or dispense any alcoholic beverage in or about his/her/its place of business unless such person has a valid and current alcoholic beverage worker's card.
(c)
Persons requesting an alcoholic beverage worker's card shall make application for such on forms provided by the police department. Registration cards are valid for one year from the date of issuance. There is a ten dollar application fee for each card. Only one registration card is required per person, regardless of the number of places of employment. Registration cards are required for all classes of alcoholic beverage licenses.
(d)
Background checks, disqualifying offenses. No alcoholic beverage worker's card shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of any felony offense under the laws of this state or the United States, or of any violation described in section 6-55 of this chapter, or of any violation of this Code. Background checks will be conducted on all applicants, both for new cards and renewals. Provided further that any person who on the effective date of this chapterwas bona fide employed in a position requiring an alcoholic beverage worker's card shall not be denied either the initial issuance or renewal of any alcoholic beverage worker's card for any disqualifying offense the arrest date of which occurred prior to the effective date of this chapter.
(e)
Card to be carried on person. The holder of an alcoholic beverage worker's card shall keep the same on their person at all times while working in any licensed premises, and shall display the same upon the request of any law enforcement officer. Expired or suspended cards will be collected by any law enforcement officer.
(f)
Criminal acts. Any card holder who is adjudicated guilty of, or pleads guilty or nolo contendre to, or forfeits any bond for any act described in subsection (d) hereof, shall be subject to revocation or suspension of their alcoholic beverage worker's card as provided in section 6-71 of this Code.
(g)
Parental consent. No alcoholic beverage worker's card shall be issued to or renewed for any person under the age of 18 years unless such person tenders to the police department a bona fide and valid consent of a parent or legal guardian duly executed and upon forms furnished by the police department.
(Ord. of 12-1-08, § XXV)
Sec. 6-71. - Suspension; revocation.
(a)
No alcoholic beverage worker's card issued hereunder shall be suspended or revoked except for due cause as hereinafter defined and after a hearing in city municipal court with at least a three-day written notice to the holder of the time, place and purpose of the hearing, and a statement of said alleged due cause. Said holder shall be entitled to appear before the court at the hearing both personally and by attorney, shall be entitled to cross-examine witnesses against him/her, and shall be entitled to present witnesses in his/her own behalf. Due cause for the suspension or revocation of an alcoholic beverage worker's card shall consist of any of the following:
(1)
Violation of any state or federal law, except traffic violations up to its expiration date;
(2)
Violation of any city or local ordinance other than traffic violations;
(3)
Violation of any provision of this chapter;
(4)
Any material, false statement, or material omission relating to any alcoholic beverage worker's card issued hereunder, including, but not limited to, any material, false statement or material omission on any application.
(b)
At all hearings for suspension or revocation of any alcoholic beverage worker's card issued under this Code, the court shall consider the following criteria:
(1)
The alleged violation(s);
(2)
The past history of the card holder;
(3)
The materiality of any false statement or omission that may be involved;
(4)
The harm or potential harm of the alleged violation;
(5)
The number and nature of calls to the police department relating to the card holder;
(6)
The harm or potential harm to the public;
(7)
The harm or potential harm to the card holder;
(8)
The number of past violations or warnings;
(9)
The number of past incidents involving the card holder.
(c)
Following said hearing, and after consideration of the above criteria, the court may take any of the following actions:
(1)
Suspend the alcoholic beverage worker's card for any period of time;
(2)
Revoke the alcoholic beverage worker's card;
(3)
Issue a warning;
(4)
Table the matter.
(Ord. of 12-1-08, § XXV)
