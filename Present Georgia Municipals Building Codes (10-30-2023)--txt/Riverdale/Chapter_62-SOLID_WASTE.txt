ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Solid waste management plan.
The city's comprehensive solid waste management plan is hereby adopted as the solid waste management plan for the city.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-2. - Definitions.
Backdoor service means service provided at no additional charge to persons with a disability and no able-bodied person in the household.
Bags means plastic or paper sacks designed to refuse with sufficient wall strength to maintain physical integrity when lifted by top. Total weight of a bag and its contents shall not exceed 50 pounds.
Bulky waste means stoves, certified CFC free refrigerators, CFC free air conditioning units, hot water tanks, washing machines, furniture, and other waste materials other than construction debris, hazardous waste, or auto parts with weights or volumes greater than those allowed for carts or bags.
Cart means a 95-gallon roll receptacle, with wheels and lid, designed for the purpose of curbside collection of refuse.
City means the City of Riverdale, Georgia
Commercial and industrial refuse means all garbage and rubbish generated by a producer at a commercial and industrial unit, but in all cases to exclude hazardous waste.
Commercial and industrial unit means all premises, locations or entities, public or private requiring refuse collection within the service area for which refuse collection using dumpsters, compactors, roll offs, top loaders and carts is deemed to be appropriate, other than a residential unit.
Construction debris means waste building materials resulting from construction, remodeling, repair or demolition operations.
Contractor means the person, corporation or partnership performing refuse collection and disposal, under contract with the city.
Control means the possession, directly/indirectly, of the power to director cause the direction of the management and policies of a person or entity, whether through ownership of voting securities, by entreat, or otherwise.
Disposal site means a refuse depository including but not limited to sanitary landfills, transfer stations and waste processing separation centers licensed, permitted or approved by all governmental bodies and agencies having jurisdiction and requiring such licenses, permits or approvals to receive refuse for processing or final disposal.
Dumpster means metal receptacles, with a well fitted lid, designed to be lifted and emptied mechanically for use primarily for commercial and industrial refuse.
Drop-off carts means containers to be placed at multi-family and condominium units for collecting recyclable materials from producers therein.
Garbage means every accumulation of waste (animal, vegetable and/or other matter) that results from the preparation, processing, consumption, dealing in, handling, packing, canning, storage, transportation, decay or decomposition of meats, fish, fowl, birds, fruits, grains or other animal or vegetable matter (including, but not by way of limitation, used tin cans and other food containers; or easily decomposable animal of vegetable waste matter which is likely to attract flies or rodents); except (in all cases) any matter included in the definition of construction debris, hazardous waste, yard trimmings, or rubbish.
Hazardous waste means waste, in any amount, which is defined, characterized or designated as hazardous by the United States Environmental Protection Agency or appropriate state agency by or pursuant to federal or state law, or waste, in any amount, which is regulated under federal or state law. For purposes of this chapter, the term hazardous waste shall also include motor oil, gasoline, paint, paint cans and items containing Freon or chlorofluorocarbons.
Multi-family unit means individual residential units in a multi-family structure (i.e. apartment building) which units are not separately owned by are owned by one common entity, for which refuse collection using dumpsters and recycling using drop-off carts, is deemed appropriate. For the purposes of this chapter, all multi-family units will be deemed "commercial and industrial units," and all applicable provisions shall apply accordingly.
Producer means an occupant of a residential unit or commercial and/or industrial unit who generates refuse and/or recyclable materials.
Recyclable material(s) means corrugated cardboard, chipboard, office paper, computer paper, colored paper, newsprint, magazines, glass bottles and jars (green, brown, clear) aluminum and bi-metal cans; HDPE clear plastic milk jugs and PET plastic soft drink containers and (used motor oil containers); or any other commodity agreed upon in writing by the city and vendor.
Recycling waste bin means a receptacle with a capacity of 18 gallons made of plastic and designed for the purpose of residential curbside collection of recyclable materials.
Recycling waste cart means a receptacle with a capacity of 65 gallons made of plastic and designed for the purpose of commercial collection of recyclable materials.
Refuse means all garbage and/or rubbish and/or bulky waste and/or yard waste generated by producers.
Residential unit means (a) a free standing dwelling within the corporate limits of the city occupied by a person or group of persons: and (b) a residential unit contained in a building consisting of not more than four such units, and for which Refuse collection service using carts is appropriate, as determined by the city. A residential unit shall be deemed occupied when either water or domestic light and power services are being supplied thereto.
Rubbish means all chips, shavings, sawdust, printer matter, paper, pasteboard, rags, straw, used and discarded clothing, shoes and boots, combustible waste pulp and other products such as are used for packaging, or wrapping crockery and glass, ashes, cinders, floor sweepings, glass, mineral or metallic substances, and any and all other similar waste materials not included in the definition of construction debris, garbage, or hazardous waste.
Service area means the entire geographic area within the corporate limits of the city, unless otherwise agreed to by the parties in writing.
Unacceptable waste means highly flammable substances, hazardous waste (as defined herein), liquid waste, special waste, certain pathological and biological waste, explosives, toxic materials, radioactive materials, material that the disposal facility is not authorized to receive and/or dispose of, and other materials deemed by state, federal or local law, or in the reasonable discretion of vendor, to be dangerous or threatening to health or the environment, or which cannot be legally accepted.
Yard waste means all tree trimmings, dead plants, weeds, leaves, and dead tree debris not exceeding four feet in length, four inches in diameter or forty pounds. In addition, yard waste shall include Christmas trees, of any length, diameter or weight.
Yard waste bags means biodegradable paper bags especially made to dispose of yard waste.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )

ARTICLE II. - REFUSE COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL NON-RESIDENTIAL SERVICES

Sec. 62-26. - Commercial and multi-family collection—Rules governing owners and occupants.
(a)
All owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property shall be responsible for:
(1)
The collection and transportation of all refuse accumulated at such place as set forth in sections 62-28—62-30; and;
(2)
Preventing overflow of refuse from containers or other nuisance-causing conditions. No occupation tax permit or renewal thereof shall be issued to the owner or occupant of commercial and industrial property or owner of multi-family property until such person has arranged for adequate refuse collection for the property in compliance with sections 62-28—62-30. No occupation tax permit or renewal thereof shall be issued to the owner or occupant of commercial and industrial property or owner of multi-family property that has outstanding payments due for commercial sanitation services or related fees.
(b)
All owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property shall be responsible for procuring adequate refuse receptacles (i.e. dumpsters, bulk containers or similar) in good condition for use by the property. Such refuse receptacles must meet the conditions set forth in sections 62-31 and 62-32 below.
(c)
All owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property shall maintain the area immediately surrounding the refuse receptacles free of unsightly refuse at all times, ensuring that all refuse is placed completely within the receptacle with all doors and lids to be closed at all times the receptacle is not being loaded or emptied. Said persons shall maintain the refuse receptacles in good condition. All owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property shall be responsible for the cost of repair or replacement of any refuse receptacles damaged by abuse, fire, theft, negligence or other cause of damage.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-27. - Same—Rules governing refuse haulers.
All persons or businesses that collect, transport or dispose of refuse from commercial, industrial or multi-family property in the city shall, before entering into any contract for such service, apply for and obtain a city occupation tax permit. All such persons shall conduct their activities in full compliance with state laws and regulations and city ordinances regulating the collection, transportation or disposal of such waste and, upon failure to do so, in addition to prosecution for such violations in the appropriate court, shall be subject to revocation of their occupation tax permit following notice and opportunity to be heard.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-28. - Use of city selected refuse hauling company—City selected refuse hauling company.
Pursuant to the City of Riverdale Charter, section 54, all owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property shall be required to exclusively use a refuse hauling company selected by the City of Riverdale as of January 1, 2009.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-29. - Same—Current agreement with refuse hauling company.
All owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property with executed contracts with refuse hauling companies effective on or before January 1, 2009 shall be allowed to continue with those contracts until the end of the contract term. Immediately upon expiration of the contract term, said owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property shall be required to exclusively use a refuse hauling company selected by the City of Riverdale as set forth in section 62-28.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-30. - Same—Penalty for failure to use city selected refuse hauling company.
Failure to perform any act required by this article, or the performing of any act prohibited by this article, shall constitute an offense and shall subject the offender to prosecution in the municipal court of the city. The maximum penalty imposed for any violation of this article shall be as provided in chapter 1, section 1-12.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-31. - Placement of receptacles.
All refuse receptacles must be placed to the rear of any buildings on the property unless otherwise approved by the city, in writing.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-32. - Rules specific for food service establishments.
(a)
In addition to meeting the requirements of section 62-28, food service establishments shall utilize screening material that is architecturally compatible with the food service establishment building, either block or brick, and matches the building color.
(b)
Food service establishments are required to have drain plugs and meet all other requirements of the Clayton County Health Department
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-33. - Other provisions.
(a)
The city reserves the right at all times to direct and control all relevant aspects of the time, place and manner of refuse collection service. Nothing in this section is intended to abridge the city's right to control of the waste stream.
(b)
No building permit shall be issued by city for new construction unless appropriate plans and specifications are submitted setting forth and meeting the requirements of this section.
(c)
The fees related to commercial sanitation are set forth in the fee schedules adopted and updated by the mayor and council. All owners and occupants of commercial and industrial property and all owners of multi-family property utilizing the city-selected sanitation refuse hauling company must timely pay all fees associated with this service. Customer must reimburse the city for all costs associated with checks returned for lack of adequate funds. Late fees and ten-percent interest apply to all account balances not paid in full by the due date.
After 45 days of nonpayment in full, the customer's commercial sanitation will be suspended. The customer is responsible for all fees associated with the suspension and restart of service. In the event any sanitation charges remain unpaid that were provided to the real estate owner of record as reflected in the Office of the Superior Court of Clayton County, Georgia, Land Records, said charge shall constitute a lien against the real estate. In the event the sanitation charges herein have been provided to a subscriber who is not the owner of record title as referenced above, said charges shall constitute a lien against the personal property of the subscriber.
(d)
Any violation of this chapter subjects the owner and/or occupant to citation by the city, and prosecution in environmental court. Violators are subject to the penalties outlined in chapter 1, section 1-12.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-34—62-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - REFUSE COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL RESIDENTIAL SERVICES

Sec. 62-61. - Collection by city required; exceptions.
(a)
All garbage and trash accumulated in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed of by the city or city hauler, except as otherwise expressly authorized in this article. Use of the city collection and disposal system shall be required of all residential units in the city.
(b)
No person shall collect, convey over any of the streets or alleys of the city or dispose of any refuse accumulated in the city, except as expressly authorized in this article or as allowed by pertinent and appropriate actions of the city council.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-62. - Supervision/administration.
(a)
All garbage and trash accumulated by residential units in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed of by the city, which shall prescribe the days and times of collection, and such other matters pertaining to the storage, location, conveyance and disposal as are necessary.
(b)
The city manager shall implement and enforce the provisions of this chapter pertaining to the control, storage, collection and disposal of solid waste in the city.
(c)
Collection by city. The city shall have the exclusive right to operate and maintain within the corporate limits of the city, either through its own forces or through a contractor, a solid waste removal system for the purpose of providing solid waste removal services for the use and benefit of its residents. No person other than the city or its collector (franchisee) shall engage in the business of solid waste removal or disposal for the residents of this city.
(d)
Rules and regulations for operation and maintenance of removal system. The city shall promulgate and establish rules and regulations governing the operation and maintenance of the refuse removal system. These rules and regulations shall be kept on file, open to the public, at city hall.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-63. - Ownership of refuse for collection.
Ownership of refuse set out for collection shall be vested in the city.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-64. - Placement, maintenance of containers, preparation of refuse
(a)
Placement. No person shall place any refuse in any street, alley, sidewalk or other public place, or upon any private property, whether owned by such person or not, within the city, unless it is placed in approved containers, for collection as authorized in this article or under express written approval by the city manager or his/her agent. All containers are supplied by the refuse pickup service selected by the city.
(b)
Maintenance of containers. Owners of single dwelling units shall store garbage, ashes and trash in containers which shall be provided by the refuse pickup service selected by the city and which shall at all times be maintained in a good state of repair. Each owner shall prevent the continued, excessive and unsightly accumulation or refuse upon the property occupied by him or any public place adjoining his property.
(c)
Preparation. Waste must first be placed into secured bags before being placed inside watertight containers. All bags must be of a type, size and material designed for waste storage and disposal. The bags shall be unbroken, securely sealed and placed in the container in a manner so that normal handling will not cause the bag to open. Waste may be placed in containers not over 50 pounds gross weight when placed for collection.
(d)
Place of collection.
(1)
Containers should be placed not more than 100 feet from the curb for manual removal. Residents which have a driveway length of more than 150 feet will be served by a pickup truck. The collector shall not pick up garbage within fences, enclosed carports or other enclosed areas. Disposable plastic bags meeting the requirements of subsection (d)(4) may be used to store solid waste in excess of that contained in the standard container. Such disposable bags shall be placed at curbside on the day of collection.
(2)
Containers shall be returned to their storage position by 9:00 p.m. on the day of collection.
(3)
Limbs up to four feet in length, not more than four inches in diameter and pile not to exceed 40 pounds in weight will be picked up if placed on the curb on pickup days. Maximum of one pile of limbs not to exceed the 40 pounds in weight per pickup day.
(4)
Grass clippings, pine straw, leaves and similar items will be picked up if placed on the curb on pickup day. The items must be secured in disposable bio-degradable paper or plastic bags of a type, size and material designed for yard waste storage and disposal. Disposable bags shall be unbroken and securely sealed and loaded in a manner that normal handling will not cause the bag to open. The limit is ten bags per pickup day.
(5)
Any unauthorized accumulation of solid waste on any premises is hereby declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. Failure to remove any existing accumulation of solid waste within such reasonable time as may be fixed by written notice of the city manager shall be deemed a violation of this code.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-65. - Unauthorized accumulations.
Any unauthorized accumulation of refuse on any premises is herby declared to be a violation of this article. Failure to remove any existing accumulation of refuse within 24 hours after being directed to do so by the city manager or his/her agent shall be deemed a violation of this article.
(1)
Solid waste acceptable for collection:
a.
The following shall constitute waste acceptable for collection:
1.
Garbage;
2.
Rubbish;
3.
Ashes;
4.
Leaves.
(2)
Solid waste unacceptable for collection:
a.
The following shall constitute waste unacceptable for collection:
1.
Dangerous materials or substances such as poisons, acids, caustics and explosives;
2.
Materials resulting from the repair, excavation or construction of buildings such as earth, plaster, etc.;
3.
Solid wastes resulting from industrial processes;
4.
Trees, bushes, brush and any and all types of vegetation cut by commercial tree trimmers, landscapers or building contractors; and
5.
All other refuse not specifically authorized herein.
6.
Responsibility for unacceptable refuse. Any person responsible for refuse not acceptable for collection by the collector shall make such arrangements as may be necessary for the collection and disposal of the unacceptable refuse and in no case shall the unacceptable refuse be stored for more than seven days by any person prior to the disposal of same.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-66. - Construction debris.
No debris resulting from repairs, remodeling or construction will be moved by the city, but must be disposed of by the contractor or, on the contractor's failure, by the owner of the property, or other person having such work performed.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-67. - Scavenging.
No person, other than the owner thereof, or an agent of the city manager, or any employee of a person holding a business license from the city for collection of refuse, shall interfere with any container placed for the purpose of storing refuse pending collection, or remove or take any of the contents thereof, or remove any such container from the location where the container has been placed by the owner thereof.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-68. - Collection.
(a)
All collections from residential property in the city shall be made by the city contractor.
(b)
Collections from residential property shall be made weekly and shall conform to the schedule approved by the city manager.
(c)
Collection service shall be discontinued where standard or special containers are inadequate or unfit and the owner or occupant of the premises has failed or refused to correct the situation within ten days after notice of the inadequacy or unfitness has been given.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-69. - Collection charges.
(a)
Charges for solid waste collection shall be as fixed from time to time by resolution of the mayor and council and shall be billed by the city.
(b)
The city shall maintain all records of pickups and shall have the responsibility for collecting from each of its citizens benefiting from the garbage pickups by the collector.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-70. - Smoke control.
No person shall set fire to or burn waste in the city except as permitted by the city fire marshal in accordance with EPA and EPD restrictions.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Cross reference— Outdoor burning generally, chapter 34, § 34-64.
Sec. 62-71. - Control of private disposal sites.
There shall be no solid waste disposal sites within the city limits.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-72. - Exemptions.
Residents who qualify for a homestead exemption pursuant to section 68-133(b)(2)a. ("Exemption for applicants 62 years of age or older with certain incomes") and section 68-133(b)(2)b. ("Exemption for disabled veterans") of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Riverdale shall be exempt from single container residential sanitation fees.
( Ord. No. 12-2013, § 1, 11-25-2013 )
Sec. 62-73—62-90. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - RECYCLE SERVICES

Sec. 62-91. - Recycled collection by city.
(a)
At the request for commercial location service for recycling the customer will be charged additional fees as based in the schedule of fees set by the mayor and council.
(b)
Commercial locations will be provided one 95-gallon bin or cart at the request to setup service for recycling.
(1)
The contractor shall collect recyclables separate from all other commercial refuse.
(2)
The contractor will only collect one recycle container or bin per commercial unit bi-weekly of commercial recycled items.
(c)
Each residential single-family dwelling will be provided an 18-gallon bin for recycled material.
(1)
Contractor will provide bi-weekly collection of recyclable material.
(d)
The residential recycling is included in the property owners sanitation fees paid annually.
(e)
Recyclables shall be placed in provided recycling containers and shall not be commingled with non-recyclable solid waste.
(f)
Recyclable containers shall be placed at curbside for pick-up on designated collection days or at some other location on the property designated.
(g)
In the alternative, residents, businesses and institutions may bring their recyclables to our city recycling facility designated by the police and fire department on Church Street.
(h)
Items to recycle are: Newspapers and inserts, cardboard, boxes, aluminum beverage containers, steel and tin food containers, and #1, #2 and #3 plastic bottles.
(i)
All recyclables shall fit inside the recycling container.
( Ord. No. 12-2013 , § 1, 11-25-13; Ord. No. 01-01-2020 , § 1, 1-13-20)
