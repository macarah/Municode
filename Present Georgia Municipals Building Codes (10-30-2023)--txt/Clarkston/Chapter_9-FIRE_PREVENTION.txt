Sec. 9-1. - Adoption of rules and regulations of state safety fire commissioner.
The rules and regulations promulgated for the prevention of destruction of life and property by fire, explosion and related hazards in this state by the state fire commissioner, under authority of the powers vested in and duties imposed upon the state safety fire commissioner by O.C.G.A. § 25-2-1 et seq., are hereby adopted as the rules and regulations of this city.
(Code 1968, § 9-2)
Sec. 9-2. - Duty of fire marshal, etc., to cooperate with state safety fire commissioner, etc.
The city fire marshal, together with all other city officials, department heads and employees, shall cooperate with the state safety fire commissioner and the state fire marshal and their deputies or assistants, whenever called upon by them in the enforcement of the state safety fire law and the rules and regulations promulgated pursuant thereto.
(Code 1968, § 9-3)
Sec. 9-3. - Adoption of state minimum standards.
(a)
It is hereby declared to be the intention of the city council to enforce within the City of Clarkston the latest edition the International Fire Code, a part of the Georgia State Minimum Standard Codes as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
(b)
When a conflict arises between the provisions of the International Fire Code and the rules and regulations promulgated by the state fire marshall under authority vested in him by O.C.G.A § 25-2-1 et. seq., the more restrictive provisions shall apply.
(c)
Any person violating said fire prevention code within the city shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-7 of this Code of Ordinances.
(Code 1968, § 9-4; Ord. No. 69, 9-8-75; Ord. No. 115, § 1, 8-2-83; Ord. No. 127, § 1, 12-3-86; Ord. No. 173, 5-5-92; Ord. No. 303, § 1, 4-4-06; Ord. No. 316, § 1, 12-7-06)

Sec. 9-5. - Gasoline in glass containers—Sale, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person in the city to sell, give, supply or furnish gasoline in glass containers.
(Code 1968, § 9-5)
Sec. 9-6. - Same—Purchase, possession and storage.
It shall be unlawful for any person to purchase, possess or store gasoline in glass containers.
(Code 1968, § 9-6)
Sec. 9-7. - Same—Transportation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to transport in or through the city gasoline in glass containers.
(Code 1968, § 9-7)
Sec. 9-8. - Self-service gasoline dispensing prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person other than the owner of a service station, manager, operator or agent, servant or employee, on duty at such station at the time, to dispense motor fuel to the general public within the city limits.
(Ord. No. 71, 11-9-75)
State Law reference— Authority to require license, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-1 et seq.
Sec. 9-9. - Fireworks—Use prohibited.
It shall be unlawful to burn or discharge rockets or firecrackers or any kind of fireworks, or to explode caps or torpedoes within the city.
(Code 1968, § 16-8)
Sec. 9-10. - Same—Sale, etc., prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, distribute, give away or in any way dispose of any type of fireworks, or any rockets, firecrackers, dynamite caps or torpedoes within the city.
(Code 1968, § 16-9)
State Law reference— Fireworks control, O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1 et seq.
Sec. 9-11. - Fire lanes.
(a)
Fire lanes shall be established for all buildings set back more than one hundred fifty (150) feet from a main roadway, or in any circumstance where the city determines that any property requires a minimum access corridor for emergency vehicles regardless of set back. The minimum width for any fire lane shall be twenty (20) feet.
(b)
All fire lanes shall be marked with approved signs.
(c)
Fire lanes shall not be obstructed at any time or in any manner, including automobile or truck parking, attended or unattended.
(Ord. No. 310, § 1, 6-6-06)
Sec. 9-12. - Outdoor burning prohibited; exceptions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful, except as provided herein, to engage in any act of outdoor burning including the burning of trash, leaves, or yard debris whether contained or uncontained within the City of Clarkston.
(b)
Subject to the conditions in subsection (c) of this section 9-12, the provisions of this Code section shall not apply to the following:
(1)
Warming fires;
(2)
Fires used for cooking food intended for immediate consumption;
(3)
Fires used as part of the process of utilization of smokehouses.
(c)
Outdoor burnings allowed under subsection (b) above are permitted upon the following conditions:
(1)
No fire shall be kindled unless contained within a non combustible container maintained at a safe distance from any structure, wood line, or property;
(2)
No fires shall be left unattended;
(3)
A water source sufficient to completely extinguish the fire shall be maintained within easy reach at all times the fire is burning;
(4)
No pressure treated wood, construction debris, or trash shall be burned;
(5)
Any fires allowed shall create no smoke nuisance or endanger other property or structures; and
(6)
A complaint of smoke nuisance for health reasons shall be sufficient to cause a fire to be disallowed.
(Ord. No. 313, § 1, 7-11-06)
Sec. 9-13. - Obstruction of fire hydrants.
No person shall erect any fence or other structure which would obstruct the approach, observation, spoting or operation of any fire hydrant with the City of Clarkston. Further, no one shall plant or allow any vegetation which would prevent or obscure the otherwise immediate view of any fire hydrant. Any obstruction place within the immediate area around any fire hydrant shall not be placed within eighteen (18) inches of the fire hydrant.
(Ord. No. 312, § 1, 7-11-06)
