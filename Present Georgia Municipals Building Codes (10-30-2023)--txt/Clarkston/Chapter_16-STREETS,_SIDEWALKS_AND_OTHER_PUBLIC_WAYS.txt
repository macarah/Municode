ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 16-1. - Numbering of buildings.
(a)
All persons owning or occupying buildings in the city fronting on the various streets shall be required to obtain from the mayor or the mayor's designee a number for their respective buildings, and anyone erecting a new building shall obtain from the mayor or the mayor's designee the number for such building erected.
(b)
For each business house or place of business occupying twenty (20) feet, a number shall be obtained, and each residence lot which is improved will require a number for every fifty (50) feet, but if more than one (1) house is on the fifty (50) feet, separate numbers for each house will be required.
(c)
The mayor or the mayor's designee engineer shall furnish numbers as required by this section.
(d)
All persons owning or occupying buildings in the city fronting on the various streets and alleys shall be required to display in a conspicuous place the number assigned to such owner or occupant of the property by the mayor or the mayor's designee in accordance with the centenary system. The number shall be erected by the owner or occupant within ten (10) days after being served with notice of the proper number assigned to the property. Anyone erecting a new building shall obtain from the mayor or mayor's designee the number for such building at the time the building permit is issued. The street number shall be conspicuously posted on a sign in the front of the property prior to undertaking any new construction.
(Code 1968, § 6-10)
Sec. 16-2. - Obstruction in streets or sidewalks—Permit required.
No person shall put any obstruction of any kind in any public street or sidewalk in the city without special written permission of the chief of police. The chief of police shall grant no such permit except in cases of obvious necessity. The word "obstruction" shall be construed to include any trash, lumber, wood, glass, other object of any kind, or person which will interfere with the free use by the public of such street or sidewalk. The chief of police is authorized and directed to cause any unauthorized obstruction to be immediately removed upon discovery.
(Code 1968, § 14-17)
State Law reference— Obstructing street, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-43.
Sec. 16-3. - Same—Obstruction of sidewalk; maximum time; without permission.
No obstructions shall be placed on any sidewalks in the city for a period exceeding thirty (30) minutes without special written permission of the chief of police.
(Code 1968, § 20-3)
Cross reference— Hauling dirt, etc., § 10-12; drainage into street, § 10-18; clean sidewalks, § 10-10; obstructing cemetery drives, § 7-13.
Sec. 16-4. - Same—Merchants to keep produce, etc., off sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any merchant or individual offering for sale any kind of produce, fruits or merchandise, to place the same in front of their respective places of business or premises on the sidewalks.
(Code 1968, § 20-2)
Sec. 16-4.1. - Same—Street sales require council permission.
The streets, sidewalks, and alleys of the city shall not be used for the sale of any merchandise of any character. The city council may, from time to time make exception to this rule for a period of one day only.
(Ord. No. 63, § 23(g), 12-2-74)
Editor's note— Former §§ 11-27 and 11-40 has been renumbered as §§ 16-4.1 and 16-4.2 by the editor for purposes of classification.
Sec. 16-4.2. - Same—Curb service of food and drink.
It shall be unlawful for any person doing business in the city, hereinafter referred to in this section as "seller," to serve any soft drinks, ice cream or prepared foods to a customer while such customer is parked in an automobile on the street in front of or adjacent to the place of business of such seller, or for such seller or engage in what is commonly termed curb service in the dispensing of such drinks, ice cream or prepared foods. However, this prohibition shall not apply to any seller operating a food truck at a date, time and place approved in writing in advance by the city manager or designee.
(Code 1968, § 16-2; Ord. No. 390, § 1, 9-1-15 )
Note— See the editor's note for § 16-4.1.
Sec. 16-5. - Same—Excavations.
(a)
It shall be the duty of every contractor, plumber or other person making excavations in and upon the sidewalks or streets of the city for any purpose whatever, to first obtain a permit from the city, and after the completion of the work, to so fill and compact such excavations as to prevent the same or any portion thereof from sinking, caving, or settling in any degree, such filling and compacting to be approved by city, and restore the surface to as good condition as existed prior to the excavation being made.
(b)
It shall also be the duty of all persons making such excavations, until the excavation is pronounced safe by the city, to place thereon and keep placed and lighted thereon between dark and daylight, a sufficient number of red lanterns or torches to warn the public, and in the day to rail or otherwise protect such excavations.
(Code 1968, § 20-7)
Cross reference— See also §§ 16-7, 16-8, 16-9.
Sec. 16-6. - Same—Red lights to be placed around obstructions at night.
Whenever any contractor or other person is building or repairing buildings in the city and has material of any kind piled on the sidewalks or streets, it shall be the duty of the contractor, builder or other person, to place red lights or torches around such obstruction at night in sufficient numbers to warn pedestrians and other people on such streets.
(Code 1968, § 20-9)
Sec. 16-7. - Utility poles.
No telephone company, telegraph company or electric company shall erect any poles for the construction of lines or the changing of lines on any of the streets of the city without first obtaining a permit from the city engineer.
(Code 1968, § 20-11)
Charter reference— Authority to require removal, § 1.03(x).
Sec. 16-8. - Construction and reconstruction of streets and sidewalks.
After proper procedure, as set out in the ordinances, has been complied with and after the necessary authorization has been obtained for the opening or reconstruction of any old or new street, sidewalk or alley, it shall be unlawful for any person to enter upon the work and construct grading, draining, paving, curbing, sidewalks or any appurtenance or structure pertaining thereto without first submitting plans and specifications in detail for such work to the city engineer and seeking approval on the type, size, dimensions and kind of materials to be used in such construction and the grades and elevations of such new or old construction or reconstruction. The city engineer may withhold such approval until the constructing party has met the minimum requirements of the standard specifications of the city, in use at the time, relating to such construction, and may enter upon the work during the construction thereof to see that the requirements of the city are fully complied with.
(Code 1968, § 20-8)
Charter reference— Authority as to street improvements, §§ 1.03(u), 4.11.
Cross reference— As to excavations, see § 16-5.
Sec. 16-9. - Driveway requirements.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any property owner, lessee or tenant to construct or cause to have constructed a driveway entering upon a city street without first obtaining a permit for such work.
(b)
When such permit is issued the owner, lessee, tenant or other constructing party shall conform to all requirements of the standard specifications of the city, in use at the time, for that portion of the driveway from the curb or gutter line to the property line and such constructing party shall conform to all grades established and other special requirements as may be set out at the time by the city engineer.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any property owner, lessee or tenant to maintain, within the city, any driveway, opening onto the sidewalks or streets of the city unless such driveway is covered with chert, stone, concrete, brick, or some similar material that will prevent dirt from such property from washing on the sidewalk or street. In the event such covering does not prevent dirt from such property washing onto the sidewalk or street, the sidewalk or street at such place shall be kept free of all dirt or washings by such owner, lessee or tenant.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any such property owner, lessee or tenant to fail to remove such dirt or washings within twenty-four (24) hours after the same have been deposited upon the street or sidewalk and after the attention of such owner, lessee or tenant has been called thereto. Where a driveway is joint, all of such joint owners, lessees or tenants shall be liable for any noncompliance with the provisions of this section.
(Code 1968, § 20-10)
Sec. 16-10. - Utility connections generally—Application and deposit required.
Any person desiring to enter any street to make any utility connection or tap any main in any paved street of the city shall make an application to make such connection or tap to the city engineer or to such other official as may be designated by the mayor and shall deposit with such official the sum of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) as a guaranty for the replacement of such street in the same condition as it was prior to the making of such connection.
(Code 1968, § 20-12)
Sec. 16-11. - Same—Repair of streets.
(a)
When any paved street of the city is entered by any person for making utility connections, such street shall be repaired by such person and placed in the same condition as it was prior to such entrance within five (5) days after such entrance, and if not done within such time, the city engineer shall make such repairs, deducting the cost thereof from the one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) deposited as a guaranty, and refunding to such person the amount of such deposit remaining, if any, such refunding not to be made until the expiration of thirty (30) days after such entrance. Any overage shall be paid by the person making the entrance.
(b)
Subject to the provisions of subsection (a) of this section, the money deposited shall remain on deposit for the space of thirty (30) days after entrance into any street is made, and shall then be refunded to the depositor provided the repairs of the street have been properly made.
(Code 1968, §§ 20-13, 20-14)
Sec. 16-12. - Utility connection prior to street paving—Required; notice; subsequent disturbing of streets; performance of work.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the chief of construction, when any street of the city has been ordered paved or repaved by the city to give notice to persons owning property on such street ordered paved or repaved, requiring such owners to enter into the street and make all connections required for their property with pipes, conduits, or similar service furnished either by the city or public service corporations, for water, sewer, gas, and other utility connections, before the time at which such pavement is begun, and thereafter such pavement or public improvement shall not be disturbed for the purpose of laying pipes or conduits or connecting therewith, except by special permit of the city council.
(b)
The notice required in subsection (a) of this section shall provide for the property owner to begin work on the connections referred to in such section within five (5) days. If the work is not begun within such time, the city shall proceed to make such connections at the expense of such property owner.
(c)
If any property owner begins work on any connections referred to in subsection (a) of this section and fails and refuses to complete such work within a reasonable time, the city shall take up such work and complete it at the expense of such property owner.
(Code 1968, §§ 20-20—20-22)
Sec. 16-13. - Same—Assessment of costs; issuance of execution against property owner and property.
Should it be necessary for the city to make any of the connections referred to in section 16-12, the city clerk shall render to each property owner a bill for the cost of such improvement and if such bill is not paid within ten (10) days after receipt, the city clerk shall issue an execution against such property owner and against such property so improved for the cost of such improvement.
(Code 1968, § 20-23)
Sec. 16-14. - Same—Levy and sale of property.
The executions issued under the provisions of section 16-13 shall be turned over to the chief of police who shall proceed to levy and sell the property so improved to satisfy such execution in the same manner as is provided by the charter for executions issued for street improvement and for the cost of laying sewers in the streets of the city.
(Code 1968, § 20-24)
Sec. 16-15. - Same—Affidavit of illegality.
Any property owner shall have the right to file an affidavit of illegality to any execution issued under the provisions of section 16-13 returnable to the superior court of the county, as provided by the charter of the city to be done in the case of executions issued for street improvements of the city.
(Code 1968, § 20-25)
Secs. 16-16—16-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - SIDEWALK CAFÉS

Sec. 16-21. - Sidewalk cafés—Purpose and scope.
The purpose of this article is to provide for the authorization and regulation of sidewalk cafés as operated by qualified and licensed businesses on public sidewalks within the city. This article shall not apply to any patio or other outdoor seating area located on private property.
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Sec. 16-22. - Sidewalk cafés—Permit and application.
A business licensed to serve food and/or drinks may apply to the city manager for an annual permit to utilize public sidewalks abutting the business location for outdoor seating and service. Permits grant a privilege for one calendar year, or the remaining portion thereof, and are subject to annual review and renewal. The permit application shall include:
(1)
Name of applicant;
(2)
A copy of a valid City of Clarkston business license to operate a food and/or beverage service business in front of which the sidewalk cafe will be located;
(3)
A copy of the current certificate of insurance in the amounts and categories required by this chapter;
(4)
A diagram identifying the perimeter of the proposed sidewalk cafe area, the dimensions of the area, the distance from perimeter to curb or nearest obstacle, number and placement of tables, and the design and materials of any requested divider; and
(5)
Payment of the annual permit fee of one hundred dollars ($100.00).
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Sec. 16-23. - Sidewalk café permits—Criteria and conditions of issuance.
(a)
In reviewing the application for a sidewalk café, the city manager or his/her designee will consider the following criteria in determining whether a permit will be issued:
(1)
Qualifications. Permits may only be issued incidentally with the operation of a properly licensed business.
(2)
Location. The outdoor seating area of the sidewalk café may only extend the width of the storefront.
(3)
Arrangement. The permit application shall include a diagram showing the location of the sidewalk café in relation to the surrounding buildings, streets, and sidewalks, along with the location of all furnishings to be placed in the sidewalk café.
(4)
Barriers. If approved, applicants may include non-permanent barriers separating the sidewalk café from the remaining sidewalk that can be disassembled and removed if necessary. The design of the barrier if requested must be included in the permit application and shown on the diagram submitted with the permit application. If requested, the barrier should be of a simple post and chain construction, with posts of thirty-three (33) to thirty-six (36) inches in height above the surface of the sidewalk.
(b)
The applicant must acknowledge by signature that compliance with the regulations provided for by this article are a condition of operating a sidewalk café.
(c)
The issuance of a permit shall not be construed or interpreted to convey any property rights or any estate in land to any person or business. Sidewalk café permits are a non-transferable privilege that will expire upon change in use of the facility or on December 31 of each year.
(d)
Indemnification. As a condition of issuance, the approved applicant and any person acting under or pursuant to said approval, agrees to indemnify, hold harmless, release, and defend (even if the allegations are false, fraudulent, or groundless) to the maximum extent allowed by law, the city and its agents, officers, employees and representatives, from and against all liability, loss, suits, claims, damages, costs, judgments, and expenses (including attorneys' fees and costs of litigation) which in whole or in part result from arise out of, or are claimed to result from or arise out of the operation, existence or approval of a sidewalk café or in any matter connected with any acts, operations, privileges authorized, allowed, or undertaken pursuant to the use of city sidewalks pursuant to this article, including, without limitation, any condition of property used as a sidewalk café.
(e)
Insurance. The approved sidewalk cafe permittee shall be required to have general liability insurance providing for the following limits naming the city as an additional insured in the following minimum amounts: fifty thousand dollars($50,000.00) per person; and one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) per occurrence.
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Sec. 16-24. - Sidewalk café regulations.
(a)
Limitations on location. No obstruction may be placed within two (2) feet of a curb or within five (5) feet of a fire hydrant, pedestrian crosswalk, or handicapped comer curb cut.
(b)
Sanitation. The permittee's business shall keep the area covered by the permit clean, neat, and orderly at all times. The business is responsible for removing all debris and trash from the sidewalk café at all times. The permit holder shall keep the sidewalk cafe and the pedestrian corridor surrounding it clean of any solid waste, including, food debris, paper, cups, bottles, cans, and other garbage associated with the operation of the sidewalk cafe space on a daily basis. The public right-of-way within and adjacent to the sidewalk café must be washed down with water and detergent when needed and cleaned on a daily basis. Any stains from spills must be removed promptly by the permittee. The permittee must properly dispose of all waste and trash.
(c)
Furnishings. Furnishings shall not be secured to lamp posts, streetlights, trees, or any public fixtures. All furnishing shall be removed from the sidewalk café from time to time as required by the city. Should a severe storm warning be issued, all outdoor furniture shall be removed from the sidewalk café. Additionally, the following regulations shall apply:
(1)
Tables and chairs. Tables and chairs shall match and be made of safe, sturdy, and durable material. All furniture shall be commercial grade and manufactured for outdoor use. Plastic or PVC furniture shall be prohibited. Where the nearest curb abuts on-street parking, tables shall be oriented parallel to the curb so as to allow ingress and egress between the sidewalk and the on-street parking.
(2)
Umbrellas. Each table may be equipped with one (1) umbrella which shall be maintained in good, clean, and operable condition. Table umbrellas shall have no signs or advertisements on them,, and the lowest dimension of the umbrella shall maintain a minimum of seven (7) feet vertical clearance above the sidewalk, when extended, to allow for patron and server circulation.
(3)
Signs. Any signs must be kept within the sidewalk café and are subject to sign permit approval. All such signs must comply with City Code.
(4)
All kitchen equipment and refuse containers used to service the sidewalk café shall be located inside the primary business.
(d)
Alcohol. Only a properly permitted business which holds a valid alcohol license for consumption on the premises may serve alcohol to customers in the sidewalk café. Such service of alcohol shall be subj ect to all restrictions set out in Chapter 3 of the Code of Ordinances, except as outlined below, and the following restrictions:
(1)
Alcohol may only be served in the sidewalk café during the hours allowed under the city's ordinance for service.
(2)
Patrons may not be in possession of more than one (1) alcoholic beverage.
(3)
Patrons may not remove alcohol from the premises of the business or sidewalk café. The area set aside for the sidewalk café shall be considered part of the premises for the purposes of consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(4)
Sidewalk cafés permitted pursuant to this article shall not be considered "open areas," "decks" or "patios" as those terms are used in City Code section 3-22 and shall not be subject to the regulations set forth in Code section 3-22.
(e)
Smoking. Patrons shall not be allowed to smoke within the sidewalk café.
(f)
Sound. Music, live or recorded, or other amplified sound shall be allowed within the sidewalk café, provided it does not violate the city's ordinances related to noise.
(g)
Hours of operation. No sidewalk café shall operate after 2:55 a.m. on Saturday or Sunday morning. No sidewalk cafe shall operate after 2:00 a.m. on any other day of the week. Permit holders shall ensure that their sidewalk cafés are not available for use after the hours specified in this subsection.
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Sec. 16-25. - Sidewalk cafés—Permit revocation or suspension.
Issuance of a sidewalk cafe permit is conditional. A permit may be suspended or revoked by the city manager or his designee if it is determined that:
(1)
Any necessary business or health permit has been suspended, revoked, or cancelled.
(2)
The permit holder is not in compliance with any of the regulations with respect to the sidewalk café.
(3)
The permit holder has failed to correct violations of this article or any other ordinance within forty-eight (48) hours of receipt of the notice of same delivered in writing to the permit holder.
(4)
The permit holder has a history of violations of this article of three (3) or more within a two-year period, or immediately upon any violation depending upon the severity of the violation.
(5)
Permits may be suspended for a period up to twelve (12) months depending upon history and severity of the violation.
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Sec. 16-26. - Sidewalk cafés—Penalties for violations.
In addition to possible suspension or revocation of the permit, the following minimum fines shall be imposed for violations of this chapter:
(1)
First citation: One hundred dollar ($100.00) fine.
(2)
Second citation (within one-year period): Two hundred fifty dollar ($250.00) fine.
(3)
Third and subsequent citations (within one-year period): Five hundred dollar ($500.00) fine.
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Sec. 16-27. - Sidewalk Cafés—Compliance with Americans with Disabilities Act.
Any person receiving a permit hereunder agrees to fully comply with all requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act as currently existing or as may be hereafter amended.
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Sec. 16-28. - Permitted sidewalk cafés not a violation of other Provisions.
The installation, operation or maintenance of a permitted sidewalk café shall not be construed as a violation of City Code sections 16-2 or 16-3 regarding obstructing sidewalks, nor shall a permitted sidewalk café be construed as a violation of section 16-4.1 regarding keeping or selling merchandise on the sidewalk.
( Ord. No. 409 , § 1, 6-6-17)
Secs. 16-29, 16-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SPECIAL EVENTS

Sec. 16-31. - Purposes.
The purposes of this article are to facilitate and coordinate the review of special events within the City of Clarkston and to ensure citizen and visitor safety by establishing public safety standards for special events. This article focuses on public safety and adherence to local, state and federal laws and provides for special event public safety equipment, event sanitation and health, medical care and fire rescue, law enforcement, public safety event plans and traffic management plans and other city services. This article enhances the positive image of the city while stimulating economic growth, supporting tourism and promoting the health, safety and welfare of the city's residents, businesses and visitors.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-32. - Definitions.
The following words and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this Sec., except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means a person who has filed a written application for a special event permit.
City means the City of Clarkston, Georgia.
City staff means the city employee designated by the city manager who is responsible for reviewing the initial special event permit application, coordinating meetings between the applicant/promoter and city representatives, collecting special event permit fees, and helping enforce this article. The city manager or his/her designee shall chair the special event review committee.
Non-profit means an association, corporation, company or organization that is not maintained or organized for profit, as recognized by the State of Georgia and the Federal Tax Code.
Person means any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company or organization of any kind, but does not include the City of Clarkston or its departments, officials or employees.
Promoter means the person seeking to hold a special event, including the person's employees, agents, affiliates, successors, permitted assigns, and other persons controlled by the promoter.
Public property means real property that is owned, leased, operated, maintained or controlled by the city.
Public right-of-way means the land owned or controlled by a city, state or political subdivision of the state for the purposes of providing transportation facilities such as streets, sidewalks or hike-and-bike trails. The public right-of-way typically extends at least eleven (11) feet from the edge of any public street and includes the sidewalk adjacent to the street. Any city hike-and-bike trail that is not adjacent to a public street is also contained within the public right-of-way.
Special event or event means a preplanned, single gathering or event or series of related, consecutive daily gatherings or events of an entertainment, cultural, recreational, political, sporting or any other nature, to be held in whole or in part on public property, for a preplanned, single gathering or event or series of related, consecutive daily gatherings or events of an entertainment, cultural, recreational, political, sporting or any other nature to be held wholly on non-public property, the gathering or event shall be considered a "special event" under this article if the event or gathering is expected to draw one hundred (100) or more people as participants, attendees or spectators, or if the person promoting or sponsoring the event or gathering requests or requires one or more city services, including public safety equipment, event sanitation or health, medical care or fire rescue, law enforcement or traffic management.
Special events may include festivals, fundraisers, parades, runs, walks, relays, marathons and similar events.
Special event permit means the document evidencing approval from the city or its designated representative for a special event.
Special event review committee means a city committee that consists of one (1) or more representatives from each department within the city. The special event review committee shall review special event applications and procure from each applicant clarifications or additional information deemed necessary for the approval, denial or revocation of special event permits.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-33. - Special event permit applications.
A person desiring to hold, sponsor or promote a special event shall apply for a special event permit by filing a completed special event permit application with the city manager or his/her designee on the form provided by the city.
(a)
Each special event permit application shall be accompanied by a non-refundable application fee in the amount of one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00). If the application is submitted fewer than sixty (60) days before the start of the event, an additional late fee of one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00) shall be assessed.
(b)
The special event permit application must be completed in its entirety. An incomplete special event permit application will not be accepted by the city.
(c)
The special event permit application submittal must include the following information:
(1)
Completed special event permit application form;
(2)
Applicant/promoter information;
(3)
The name, address and telephone number of the Person seeking to conduct the special event;
(4)
The names, addresses and telephone number of the headquarters of the organization for which the special event is to be conducted, if any, and the authorized and responsible heads of the organization;
(5)
The requested date(s) of the special event;
(6)
If applicable, the route to be traveled, including the starting point and the termination point;
(7)
The approximate number of vehicles which will constitute the special event and a description of the types of vehicles;
(8)
The hours when such special event will start and terminate;
(9)
If applicable, a statement as to whether the special event will occupy all or only a portion of the width of the streets or public facility or public property proposed to be traversed;
(10)
The location by street of any assembly areas for the special event;
(11)
The time at which units of the special event will begin to assemble at any area;
(12)
If the special event is designed to be held by, or on behalf of, any person other than the applicant, the applicant for such permit shall file a letter from that person with the sponsorship and event management director authorizing the applicant to apply for the permit on his behalf;
(13)
The type of special event, including a description of activities planned during the event;
(14)
The approximate number of participants;
(15)
The approximate number of spectators;
(16)
A designation of any public facilities or equipment to be utilized; and
(17)
Any additional information that the sponsorship and event;
(18)
Brief description of the event and its purpose, general and specific;
(19)
Special event application fee;
(20)
The estimated number of participants, attendees and spectators;
(21)
Detailed, to scale, site map of the entire event area identifying all items to be used for the execution of the event including, but not limited to, parking, proposed street closings, event routes, stages, tents, concessions, restrooms, structures, electrical items, signage and vendor booth locations.
(e)
The following additional submittal requirements shall accompany the special event permit application:
(1)
Proof of non-profit status, if applicable.
(2)
Proof of insurance required by this article
(3)
Completed park pavilion application or field rental agreement, if applicable.
(4)
Evidence that the applicant has obtained prior written approval from the private property owner if any portion of the event is to be held on private property.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-34. - Application Exemptions.
A special event permit may not be required under this article for the following:
(a)
An event hosted by the City where the City is the promoter. This does not include events for which the City is only a sponsor of an event.
(b)
An event wholly contained on property specifically designed or suited for the event where the city has issued a certificate of occupancy for such activity. All such exemptions must meet applicable zoning regulations.
(c)
City officials are acting within the scope of their duties is the promotor.
(d)
A funeral procession proceeding by a vehicle under the most reasonable route from a funeral home, church or residence to the place of service or place of interment.
All exemptions must meet applicable zoning regulations.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-35. - Attendance estimates.
For purposes of this article, attendance estimates by the special event permit applicant shall be accompanied by a written statement for the basis of the estimate. Each estimate shall be based upon all the relevant factors known at the time, including past attendance at similar events or functions having the same or similar elements, both in the city and in comparable communities; the price of admission; and the extent of advertising and promotion contemplated. The special event review committee shall accept the applicant's attendance estimate representation unless it clearly appears to the special event review committee from the written statements that the estimate is understated. In such instance, the special event review committee shall substitute an estimate for that of the special event permit applicant.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-36. - Approval of application.
(a)
When considering approval of a special event permit application, the special event review committee shall consider the following factors:
(1)
Whether the application allows for ample opportunity to properly plan and prepare for the special event;
(2)
Whether the special event is likely to promote the city in a positive manner;
(3)
Whether the special event is likely to promote tourism by attracting visitors;
(4)
Whether the special event is likely to benefit businesses in the city;
(5)
Whether the special event is likely to positively promote family entertainment;
(6)
Whether the special event is likely to enhance a sense of community;
(7)
Whether, police, and/or other city services or the city's residents will be unduly burdened or adversely affected by the special event;
(8)
Whether the special event is reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, to provoke disorderly conduct or to create a disturbance; and
(9)
Any additional information that the special event review committee deems relevant to make a fair determination as to whether a permit should be issued.
(b)
The special event review committee shall grant or deny the special event permit application no fewer than thirty (30) days prior to the start of the event.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-37. - Permits required.
(a)
No special event may be held within the city without first obtaining a special event permit. Special event permits are issued through the City of Clarkston. A special event permit application form may be obtained from the city. All applications shall be reviewed on a case-by-case basis.
(b)
A special event application must be completed and submitted to the city no fewer than ninety (90) days before the start of a proposed special event.
(c)
The issuance of a special event permit grants permission to use public property proposed to be used in connection with the special event. The use of such property shall be solely for the purpose of constructing, installing, operating and maintaining the special event, and for such other purposes consistent with promoting and conducting the special event.
(d)
Issuance of a special event permit does not authorize the removal or alteration of public property. Any such removal or alteration is strictly prohibited.
(e)
In addition to complying with all conditions of the permit and all applicable city ordinances, regulations, rules, policies and guidelines, the applicant must comply with all applicable federal, state, county and local laws, rules and regulations. It is the responsibility of the applicant to obtain all permits necessary to conduct the event and all permits required by other governmental authorities shall be obtained and adhered to.
(f)
Special event permits are applicant- and place-specific. A special event permit holder may not, and shall have no authority to, assign, sell, transfer, pledge, encumber or otherwise convey the permit or any rights, duties, responsibilities or obligations thereunder, and no permit may be transferred from place to place. Any such conveyance or transfer shall be null and void and may, in the discretion of the city, result in the revocation of the permit.
(g)
No rights granted by a permit shall create rights in any person other than the permittee.
(h)
A person commits an offense if the person engages, participates, aids or commences in a special event within the city without first making written application for and receiving a special event permit from the city where a permit is required under this article.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-38. - Issuance of permit.
Upon receipt of an application for a special event permit, city staff shall review the application.
(a)
The special event review committee shall review pending special event permit applications.
(b)
The special event review committee may prescribe licenses and permits required by other city ordinances or applicable law, restrictions, regulations, cost for city services, safeguards and other conditions necessary for the safe and orderly conduct of a special event.
(c)
If additional fees are warranted for City services provided by the city's respective departments to ensure the safe and orderly conduct of the special event the city staff shall prepare an estimate identifying these estimated costs. The applicant shall pay all fees in full no later than seven (7) days before the start of the special event. Failure to pay such costs shall be grounds for denial or revocation of a permit.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-39. - Denial, cancellation or revocation of permit.
(a)
The special event review committee may deny a special event permit when the event for which the permit is requested would:
(1)
Unduly burden city services;
(2)
Cross or use as a route, or as part of a route, any major thoroughfare within the city's corporate limits or within the city's jurisdiction that would interfere with or burden the orderly flow of traffic;
(3)
Unreasonably disrupt the orderly flow of traffic and no reasonable means of rerouting traffic or otherwise meeting traffic needs is available;
(4)
Take place at the same location or time as a previously approved special event;
(5)
Begin or end outside the city limits, unless or until the applicant/promoter receives approval from the adjacent city or county where the special event begins or ends; or
(6)
Not benefit the city or its citizens if the special event review committee determines that:
a.
The application does not allow for ample opportunity to properly plan and prepare for the special event;
b.
Police and other city services or the city's residents will be unduly burdened or adversely affected by the special event;
c.
The special event is reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, to provoke disorderly conduct or create a disturbance.
B.
The special event review committee may deny a special event permit if the applicant/promoter:
(1)
Fails to provide a completed special event permit application without all of the additional requested submittal information;
(2)
Fails to provide a special event permit application in a timely manner which would not allow the city to properly plan for and provide required services for the event;
(3)
Fails to submit the required special event permit fee;
(4)
Fails to adequately plan and provide for:
a.
The protection of event participants;
b.
Maintenance of public order in and around the special event location;
c.
Crowd security, taking into consideration the size and character of the event;
d.
Emergency vehicle access; or
e.
Safe, sanitary conditions for preparation or operation of food concessions.
(5)
Fails to provide proof of insurance required under this article;
(6)
Fails to comply with, or the proposed special event will violate, a city ordinance or other applicable law;
(7)
Makes a false statement of material fact on the special event permit application;
(8)
Fails to provide proof that the applicant possesses or is able to obtain all licenses and permits required by this article and other city ordinances or other applicable law for the conduct of all activities included as part of the special event;
(9)
Has had a special event permit revoked within the preceding twelve (12) months or the applicant/promoter has committed two (2) or more violations of a condition or provision of a special event permit or this article within the preceding twelve (12) months;
(10)
Fails to pay any outstanding costs owed to the city for past special events;
(11)
Fails to provide payment due for city services associated with the event no fewer than 7 days before the start of the event;
(12)
Publicly promotes the event before approval of the event permit application; or
(13)
Makes unauthorized use of the city logo or other intellectual property.
(c)
The city may postpone, cancel, suspend or close any special event or revoke a permit for any force majeure event. The city shall have no liability for such postponement, cancellation, suspension or closure.
(d)
The city may revoke a permit at any time due to the failure of the applicant/promoter to comply with any of the terms or conditions of the special event permit, this article or any other city ordinances, rules or regulations.
(e)
The failure of the city to revoke a permit or to exercise any right, power or authority under this article shall not constitute a waiver of the terms or conditions of the permit, this article or any other city ordinances, rules or regulations and shall not affect the rights of the city to enforce the same against any other person or a subsequent breach by the applicant/promoter.
(f)
The revocation of a permit does not prohibit the city from exercising any and all additional rights and remedies available by law or equity against the applicant/promoter for failure to comply with the terms and conditions of the permit, or other city ordinances, rules or regulations.
(g)
The city may revoke a permit if the special event deviates from the approved timeline as determined by the special event review committee, the city manager or his/her designee.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-39. - Appeal, denial or revocation of permit.
If the special event review committee denies or revokes a permit issued under this article the city manager, or his/her designee shall send to the applicant/promoter or permit holder written notice of the denial or revocation and of the right to an appeal. The decision of the special event review committee is final unless the applicant/promoter or permit holder submits a written notice of appeal to the city manager within five (5) business days of the decision. The city manager, or his/her designee, shall consider all the evidence in support of or against the action appealed and render a decision either sustaining or reversing the denial or revocation. The decision of the city manager shall be final.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-40. - Site plan.
The applicant/promoter shall submit a detailed plan/map of the entire event area identifying all items to be used for the execution of the event including, but not limited to parking, proposed street closings, event routes, stages, tents, concessions, restrooms, structures, electrical items, signage, vendor booth locations, et(c) during the initial application submittal. Before the event, the applicant/promoter shall submit a final site plan/map addressing all comments and recommendations made by the special event committee.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-41. - Street closure plan.
(a)
Certain streets within the city may be temporarily closed to limit or exclude vehicular or pedestrian traffic before, during or after a special event. An applicant requesting one or more street closures for a special event shall submit for approval a street closure plan showing the layout of all barricades and signs as part of the permit application. The special event review committee shall consider such request in evaluating the application and may require additional or fewer street closures. Some street closures may require the applicant to obtain the consent of businesses, property owners or other persons in the area. Should street closures be approved, the city shall supervise the placement of all barricades and signs placed on public streets or other public rights-of-way.
(b)
The city shall determine the type and number of traffic control devices required to close a public street or other public right-of-way.
(c)
Electronic changeable message signs shall be used within the public right-of-way when road closure or special parking situations require them. They shall not be used to identify or promote an event.
(d)
Any event equipment or other property that is permitted to be located within the public right-of-way during the event shall be removed in a timely manner to facilitate the reopening of a closed street as soon as possible. For races and rallies, equipment and other property shall be removed as soon as the last participant passes that point of rout(e) For other events, the promotor shall start removing equipment and other property no later than the advertised end time of the event. The promotor shall prioritize removing equipment and other property that is in the public right-of-way before taking down equipment or other property in other areas.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-42. - Parking.
(a)
If parking needs for the event are expected to exceed the area owned by or assigned to the host establishment, the promotor is responsible for making appropriate parking arrangements with affected properties or establishments.
(b)
The applicant/promoter is responsible for obtaining and submitting written verification from all property owners evidencing that they have agreed to provide their property to serve some or all of the event's parking needs.
(c)
Each required accessible/handicapped parking space affected by the event must be temporarily replaced using one or more spaces outside the event area that is as close as feasible to the entrance to the establishment. Such temporary spaces must meet all then-current ADA requirements.
(d)
Remote parking and shuttle routes must be submitted with the proposed site plan for the event and approved by City Manager or his/or her designee.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-43. - Special events in city parks.
All special events taking place in a city park must complete a parks rental application and pay applicable rental fees. All events are subject to a priority use fee depending on the scope and size of event. Please refer to the parks rental application.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-44. - Police/security services.
(a)
Sufficient security measures, approved solely by the city shall be provided by a qualified organization that is capable of handling the patronage of the special event.
(b)
The city shall have final say on the number of personnel required for the event and the manner in which they are deployed.
(c)
The city may require the applicant to deploy certain security measures during the event at the expense of the applicant.
(d)
The city shall maintain a fee schedule for the City of Clarkston Police Department personnel and requirements for the timeline and method of payment.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-45. - Fire/emergency medical services.
Fire and emergency medical services for special events shall be provided by the applicant/event promoter. The applicant/promoter must coordinate with the fire department and EMS to discuss the specific needs of each event and make arrangements to retain the services of the DeKalb County fire department personnel. The DeKalb County Fire Department and EMS shall have final say as to the extent of the services required to properly administer the special event.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-46. - Fireworks and pyrotechnic displays.
An event with a firework display must coordinate permit approval through the city.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-47. - Trash and recycling services.
(a)
Green event. An event in excess of seventy-five (75) participants and attendees shall be designated as a "green event." The applicant/promotor of such an event shall provide recycling services in addition to trash services. Recycling receptacles shall be provided for the separation, storage and collection of recyclables, including plastic bottles, aluminum and steel cans, cardboard and paper products (i.e., fliers, brochures). Recyclables shall be collected by an approved City contractor and transported to a materials recovery facility for processing. All costs associated with this service shall be the sole responsibility of the applicant/promoter. An event of less than seventy-five (75) participants and attendees may be designated, at the promotor's request, as a green event and all requirements in this section shall apply.
(b)
Trash services. The applicant/promoter shall be responsible for the procurement, distribution, placement and assembly (upon completion of event) of trash receptacles for use at the event shall be determined, before the event, by the city manager, or his/her designee. Trash service shall be provided by the city's contracted commercial service provider. All costs associated with this service shall be the sole responsibility of the applicant/promoter. Pricing may vary according to the event needs.
(c)
Recycling services. The applicant/promoter shall be responsible for the procurement, distribution and placement of city-approved recycling receptacles for the storage and collection of recyclables, including plastic bottles, aluminum and steel cans, glass, cardboard and paper products (i.e., fliers, brochures). Recycling service shall be provided by the city's contracted recycling service provider. All costs associated with this service shall be the sole responsibility of the applicant/promoter. Pricing may vary according to the event needs. Recyclables must be transported to a city-approved materials recovery facility for sorting and processing.
(d)
Set up and take down. The applicant/promoter shall be responsible for the set up and assembly for collection, (upon completion of event) and take down of all receptacles provided on site.
(e)
Prior approval. The applicant/promoter shall submit a written plan, outlining trash and recycling services and their implementation, to the city manager, or his/her designee for review no fewer than thirty (30) days before the start of the event. In the event that said plan fails to meet city requirements, the public works director or representative reserves the right to require further trash/recycling provisions. Failure to comply with recommendations may result in the denial, cancellation or revocation of the special event permit.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-48. - Food.
All food service vendors, including caterers, at an event must be approved by the DeKalb County Health Department, Food Safety Division.
(a)
Food service vendors must obtain a health permit from the DeKalb County Health and Food Safety Division no fewer than thirty (30) days before the start of the event and pay any required fees applicable to food service permits.
(b)
A permit is required for every fixed, temporary or mobile food service vendor.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-49. - Alcohol.
The distribution and consumption of alcohol at a special event must be coordinated with the City of Clarkston, and must comply with city alcohol permit requirements and other applicable state and local laws, ordinances and regulations permits. The promoter must obtain a temporary alcohol license for the event and each individual that will serve alcohol at the event must have permission from the city to serve alcohol before alcohol may be served at an event.
(a)
Temporary license for event. The promoter of an event must obtain a temporary license specific to the event as a prerequisite to any alcohol being served at the event. See: City Code section 3-8 regarding temporary alcohol licenses for non-profit civic organizations.
(b)
Pouring license(s) for individual(s). No individual person may serve alcohol at an Event unless such person:
(1)
Holds a current "work permit" for serving alcohol issued by the City pursuant to City Code section 3-14; or
(2)
Obtains an "individual event pouring license" for the event pursuant to this subsection The application fee for an individual event pouring license is seventy-five dollars ($75.00). Such applications shall be evaluated based on the criteria set forth in City Code section 3-5. An individual event paring license may only be issued to persons twenty-one (21) years of age or older.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-50. - Restroom facilities.
(a)
The applicant/promoter shall provide ample restroom facilities for the event, which shall be approved by the city's building inspections division. If the event site does not provide sufficient permanent restroom facilities, the applicant/promoter shall arrange for a sufficient number of portable restrooms to be on site.
(b)
Restrooms shall be located in close proximity to the event site.
(c)
Portable restrooms shall be delivered no earlier than twenty-four (24) hours before the start of the event and shall be removed from the site no more than twenty-four (24) hours after the conclusion of the event.
(d)
The number and type of required restroom facilities shall be based on the event participants.
(e)
Portable restrooms shall comply with all applicable requirements under the American Disabilities Act, as it exists or may be amended.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-51. - Noise.
The applicant/promotor shall be responsible for ensuring that the event complies with all applicable provisions of the city's noise ordinance at all times. Failure to comply with a requirement from the Clarkston Police Department concerning noise may result in the immediate revocation of the special event permit and/or prosecution under applicable law.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-52. - Signs.
All signs used to advertise, promote or provide information relating to a special event shall be professionally executed and must comply with all current applicable ordinances, rules, and regulations of the city. Please refer to the city's sign ordinance [Ch. 15.5 of the City Code].
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)

Without a special event permit, where a permit is required under this article;
(b)
In violation of any provision of a special event permit, this article or any other city ordinance or applicable law, rule, standard or regulation; or
(c)
That makes unauthorized use of the city logo or other intellectual property.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)
Sec. 16-54. - Directives of the special event review committee.
The applicant/promoter shall comply with the printed guidelines and with all other rules, regulations and directives of the special event review committee or other authorizing officials.
( Ord. No. 446 , § 1(Att.), 11-7-19)

(a)
The applicant/promoter of an event to be held in a whole or in part on public property, as well as all contractors and subcontractors performing work on public property for or on behalf of the applicant/promotor in connection with the event, shall procure and maintain insurance at their own expense for and during the event, including its setup and teardown. All such insurance and certificates of insurance shall contain the following provisions:
(1)
Name the city, its officers, agents, representatives and employees as additional insureds as to all applicable coverage. Such coverage shall be primary to all other coverage the city may possess.

Provide for a waiver of subrogation against the city for injuries, including death, property damage or any other loss to the extent the same is covered by the proceeds of insurance. All insurance policies that are required to name the city as an additional insured must be endorsed to read as primary and noncontributory coverage regardless of the application of other insurance.
(b)
A certificate of insurance evidencing the required insurance shall be submitted at least ten (10) business days before the start of the event. The event is subject to cancellation without the required insurance.
(c)
Type and amount of insurance required: General liability insurance for personal injury (including death) and property damage with a minimum of _______ dollars per occurrence and _______ dollars aggregate, including coverage for advertising injury and products coverage.
