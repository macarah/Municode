ARTICLE II. - DOWNTOWN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.; downtown development authority created, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-4.

Sec. 34-19. - Declaration of need.
There is hereby determined and declared to be a present and future need for a downtown development authority, as more fully described and defined in the Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq., to function in the city.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 6-2-1)
Sec. 34-20. - Activation.
There is hereby activated in the city the public body corporate and politic known as the "Downtown Development Authority of Lincolnton, Georgia," which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 6-2-2)
Sec. 34-21. - Membership.
The authority shall consist of seven directors appointed for staggered terms of six years, as provided by O.C.G.A. § 36-42-4. Each of the directors shall be a taxpayer of the city, residing in the city, and not less than four of the directors shall either have or represent a party who has an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization of the downtown development area.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 6-2-3)
Sec. 34-22. - Organization; powers; duties.
The board of directors shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 6-2-4)
Sec. 34-23. - Downtown development area.
The downtown development area shall be that geographical area described in exhibit A to the resolution of February 11, 1985, which is made a part hereof by reference, which area, in the judgment of the mayor and council of the city, constitutes the central business district of the city as contemplated by the Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq. A copy of exhibit A shall be maintained on file in the office of the city clerk where it shall be available for public inspection.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 6-2-5)
