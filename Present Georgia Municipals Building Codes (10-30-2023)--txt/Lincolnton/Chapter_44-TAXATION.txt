ARTICLE II. - AD VALOREM TAXES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Ad valorem taxation of property generally, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-1 et seq.

Sec. 44-19. - Tax rate.
An annual ad valorem tax upon all real and personal property within the city is hereby levied. The millage rate therefor shall be established each year by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-5-11)
Sec. 44-20. - Tax due and payable.
All ad valorem taxes due the city shall be billed and paid in one payment. Ad valorem tax bills, as adjusted to conform to the tax digest of the county shall be payable on or before December 20 of each year, unless further time is allowed, but in the event of adjustment by the state revenue commissioner, the city shall, within a reasonable time thereafter, as may be determined by resolution of the mayor and council, after receipt from the county of the adjustment information, refund any overpayments or bill for any underpayments.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-5-12)
Sec. 44-21. - Collection of delinquent taxes, fees or other revenue; interest.
(a)
For the collection of delinquent taxes, fees and other charges, a fi. fa. shall be issued by the city clerk and executed by the chief of police under the procedure provided by state law governing execution of process from the superior court, or by use of any other available process and remedy.
(b)
A lien shall exist against all property on which city property taxes are levied, as of the assessment day of January 1 of each year, which shall be superior to all other liens except that it shall have equal dignity with those for federal, state or county taxes.
(c)
The amount due on all delinquent fi. fas. issued under subsection (a) of this section, as well as any interest or penalty thereon, shall be listed as a part of and in addition to the annual ad valorem tax bill.
(d)
Any license, privilege or permit granted by the city shall be revoked by the city clerk for the failure to pay any money due the city for taxes, fees, penalties, interests, fines or other revenue within three calendar months plus ten days after any payment is due; provided, that any person aggrieved by the order may, within ten days thereof, appeal to the mayor and council, who, after a hearing thereon, may approve or modify the order or restore the license privilege or permit by adopting a motion to that effect, but only upon finding that the city clerk's action was not factually or legally correct.
(e)
Unless otherwise provided in this Code, all fi. fas. issued hereunder shall earn interest at the maximum rate allowed by state law and no penalty shall be charged except in lieu of interest.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-5-13)
State Law reference— Administration generally, O.C.G.A. § 48-2-30 et seq.; interest on past due taxes, O.C.G.A. § 48-2-40.

ARTICLE III. - HOTEL-MOTEL EXCISE TAX[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Authority of counties and municipalities to impose excise tax on lodging and accommodations, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51.

Sec. 44-46. - Levied.
There is levied and imposed an excise tax at the rate of five percent of the charge of the public for and upon the furnishing for value to the public of any room or rooms, lodging, or accommodations furnished by any person or legal entity in the city operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin or other place in which rooms, lodging, or accommodations are regularly furnished for value. Said tax shall not include the sale or charges for any rooms, lodging, or accommodations furnished for a period of more than 30 consecutive days, or for the use of meeting rooms.
(Ord. of 12-4-1995, § 1; Ord. of 7-1-2014)
Sec. 44-47. - Due date; late filing and payment penalties.
(a)
The excise tax levied under this article shall be paid monthly on the first day of each month. Operators shall pay the tax with such remittance form as prescribed by the city. Each operator shall remit the tax to the city clerk.
(b)
A penalty of 25 percent per month shall be due for failure to pay the excise tax within ten days of the due date.
(c)
Upon the failure to make a timely report and remittance by the 20th day of the month next succeeding the month in which such sales were made, the collection fee permitted operators under this article shall be forfeited.
(Ord. of 12-4-1995, § 2)
State Law reference— Filing of returns and payment of taxes, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-53.2; failure, neglect or refusal to collect tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-59; making false or fraudulent return, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-60; failure or refusal to furnish return or supplemental return, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-61; failure to keep records or open records to inspection, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-62.
Sec. 44-48. - Dealers' collection fee.
Operators collecting said tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for in the amount of three percent in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and paying the amount due, if said amount is not delinquent at the time of payment.
(Ord. of 12-4-1995, § 2)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-52.
Sec. 44-49. - Inspection of books and records.
All books and records of each dealer shall be subject to inspection and audit by the mayor and city council or their designee to verify compliance with this article.
(Ord. of 12-4-1995, § 2)
State Law reference— Preservation of books and records and examination by governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-53.4.
Sec. 44-50. - Tax is in addition to other business taxes.
The excise tax provided for hereinabove shall be in addition to any license fee or occupation tax or charge which may now or in the future be imposed upon operators within the city.
(Ord. of 12-4-1995, § 3)
Sec. 44-51. - Disposition of tax proceeds by city.
Two-fifths of all proceeds from the hotel-motel excise tax imposed by this article shall be used to promote tourism as directed by the city council and the remaining three-fifths shall be used to promote industry and trade and other qualified purposes as directed by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of 12-4-1995, § 4)
