DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 3-276. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Hotel means every building or other structure kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public to be a place where food is actually served and consumed and sleeping accommodations are offered for adequate pay to travelers and guests, whether transient, permanent or residential, and whether conducted in the same building or in separate buildings or structures used in connection therewith that are on the same premises and are a part of the hotel operation:
(1)
Which maintains 50 or more rooms used for the sleeping accommodations of such guests;
(2)
Which maintains an adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment to serve food as required therein;
(3)
Which operates one or more public dining rooms (excluding banquet rooms) with a combined seating capacity of at least 30, where meals are regularly served to guests; provided that consistent with the definition of "lounge" in this section, in no event shall the seating capacity of the lounge exceed that of such public dining rooms;
(4)
Which employs sufficient personnel to serve food as required in this article; and
(5)
Which derives at least as much gross income from the sale of such meals prepared, served, and consumed in the hotel as it does from its sale of alcoholic beverages.
Mobile food vehicle means a unit mounted on or pulled by a motorized vehicle where food including prepackaged foods and nonalcoholic beverages for individual portion service are prepared, stored, served, or dispensed.
Motels meeting the qualifications set out in this definition for hotels shall be classified in the same category as hotels. Hotels shall have the privilege of granting franchises for the operation of a lounge, restaurant or nightclub in their premises, and the holder of such franchise shall be included in this definition of "hotel."
Lounge means a separate room connected with, a part of and adjacent to a restaurant (as defined in this section) or located in a hotel (as defined in this section), provided that in no event shall the seating capacity of the lounge exceed that of its connected restaurant. The bar area incorporated within a restaurant and where food is served and consumed shall not be considered a lounge.
Pour means sell for beverage purposes, sell for consumption on the premises, sell by the drink, and allow patrons to bring their own liquor, wine or malt beverages for the purposes of mixing drinks and/or consuming same on the premises. The sale of mixes or setups or advertising to "bring your own" or the possession by patrons on the premises of liquor, wine or malt beverages, shall be prima facie evidence of pouring.
Pouring outlet means any place where liquor, wine and/or malt beverages (unless specifically modified) are poured or proposed to be poured and in the case of liquor and wine, "pouring outlet" shall mean only a restaurant, hotel, private club, lounge, public stadium, coliseum or auditorium as defined in this section, or any retail shop or establishment that is specifically approved by the city commission.
Premises means the definite, closed-in or partitioned-in locality (whether room or building) wherein pouring takes place.
Private club means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state which:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter.
(2)
Has at least 75 regular dues-paying members.
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment.
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests.
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Public stadium, coliseum or auditorium means any facility owned or controlled by the City of Rome, Floyd County, or a public authority having a seating capacity in excess of 2,500 people.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, which:
(1)
Maintains an adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment to serve food as required in this article and/or maintains an affiliation with a mobile food vehicle that is parked on the premises to serve food as required in this article.
(2)
Provides a regular seating capacity for at least 30 persons; provided, that consistent with the definition of "lounge" in this section, in no event shall the seating capacity of the lounge exceed that of its connected restaurant.
(3)
Employs sufficient personnel to serve food as required in this section.
(4)
Derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and liquor sales from the sale of such meals prepared, served and consumed on the premises, including the sale of meals prepared by an affiliated mobile food vehicle that are served and consumed on the premises. The city clerk shall review the gross income figures from each establishment and determine if the annual sales meet the required ratio between food and liquor and make appropriate recommendations to the alcohol control commission.

Sec. 3-277. - Pouring outlets generally.
The following regulations shall apply to pouring outlets:
(1)
The building or proposed building where pouring will take place shall meet all requirements of the building inspector, the fire marshal and the traffic engineer.
(2)
No pouring of liquor and/or wine shall be permitted between 1:30 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 1:30 a.m. and 10:00 a.m. on other days.
(3)
No pouring of malt beverages shall be permitted between 1:30 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 1:30 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. on other days.
(4)
No liquor or wine may be consumed on the premises of the pouring outlet between the hours of 2:00 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 10:00 a.m. on other days.
(5)
No malt beverage may be consumed on the premises of the pouring outlet between the hours of 2:00 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. on other days.
(6)
Lounges and pouring outlets, except for restaurants, private clubs, and bowling alleys, shall not keep their doors open to the public, or allow members of the public to remain on the premises, beyond the prohibited hours of consumption.
(7)
In no event shall drinks be mixed or sold, nor malt beverages sold, during the prohibited hours, based upon the timely sale of tickets, chits, decanters or other devices.
(8)
No outlet manager, nor other employee of an outlet, shall permit violations of subsections (2) through (7).
(9)
Pouring licensees may sell malt beverages by the pitcher, or wine by the bottle or decanter, for consumption on the premises.
(10)
Hotels shall have the privilege of granting franchises for the operation of a lounge or restaurant in their premises, provided the hotel and the franchisee meet all the requirements of this article.
(11)
It shall be unlawful for any person to employ knowingly in any pouring outlet in any capacity whatsoever, including performers, entertainers and musicians, any person who has pleaded guilty or has been convicted of soliciting for prostitution, pandering, letting premises for prostitution, keeping a disorderly place, illegally dealing in drugs, sex offenses or for any charge relating to the manufacture or sale of intoxicating liquors.
(12)
No signs (advertising sale by the drink or soliciting patrons to bring their own or to brownbag) affixed to the premises shall be permitted; provided, however, there shall be no prohibition against utilizing signs showing the name of the pouring outlet.
(13)
It shall be the duty of the management of a pouring outlet to maintain a copy of this article at the outlet and to instruct each employee on its terms.
(14)
The state laws and regulations relating to the sale and distribution of distilled spirits, as revised, promulgated by the state department of revenue, and especially as related to retail sale for consumption, are hereby incorporated into and made a part of this article as if fully set out in this section.
(15)
A pouring licensee shall make immediate report to, and receive approval by, the city commission of any change in the interests in or ownership of the pouring outlet and/or any change in the information as stated in the original application for license.
(16)
No person already holding a class W license shall be granted a pouring license.
(17)
A beer pouring license and a beer and wine package license may not be issued at the same outlet location unless said outlet also sells food prepared on the premises of the outlet for consumption on the premises of the outlet.
(18)
On or before the 20th day of the month, the licensee of each pouring outlet which pours liquor (except private clubs) shall file with the secretary a gross income report of the outlet for the preceding month, certified by the licensee, which contains a percentage breakdown of the amount of liquor sales of the outlet relative to the outlet's total gross income from the sale of food or non-food goods, if specifically approved by the city commission, for the applicable month. Failure to file a timely report as provided herein, or the filing of a fraudulent report, shall constitute grounds for the suspension or revocation of the alcohol pouring license issued by the city to such licensee.
(19)
Sunday alcohol sales will be permitted in a licensed pouring establishment which (a) obtains a Sunday sales license, as provided in section 3-303; and (b) derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served or in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging.

Sec. 3-278. - Entertainment at pouring outlets.
(a)
Bands or orchestras and patron dancing shall be permitted at pouring outlets:
(1)
Where adequate space exists;
(2)
Where all fire and safety regulations are met;
(3)
Where the appropriate license fee has been fully paid; and
(4)
Where prior approval of the alcohol control commission is obtained.
(b)
Prior approval may be given only after notice of dates, time, approximate number of guests expected, nature of musical source and such other information as may be required is given in writing to the alcohol control commission.
(c)
Where music or dancing is provided on a regular basis, the alcohol control commission may singly approve such regular basis, provided notice of any substantial departure from such regular basis is given to the alcohol control commission. In all cases wherein music or dancing is provided on other than a regular basis or entertainment other than bands or patron dancing is provided, prior approval for each such occasion shall be obtained from the alcohol control commission.
(d)
The alcohol control commission may require a pouring outlet providing such music and dancing to provide such police attendance and protection as, in the opinion of the chief of police, is adequate to meet the need therefor.
(Code 1959, § 4-52; Code 1981, § 9-2079)

Sec. 3-279. - Temporary permit; standards for issuance; accounting.
(a)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this Code, upon proper application, the alcohol control commission may grant a permit for an isolated or casual pouring of liquor, malt beverages and wine, provided that there is no objection from the state and provided that the proceeds from the sales under such permit shall be donated to a corporation or organization qualified as an exempt organization under applicable provisions of the Internal Revenue Code. The alcohol control commission is authorized to delegate its authority to grant or deny such permits to the chief of police or to the city clerk.
(b)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Proper application means an application containing the following information:
(1)
As to the corporation or organization receiving donations:
a.
Name.
b.
Address.
c.
Nature and purpose.
(2)
As to the applicant (who must be an authorized member of the applying organization):
a.
Name.
b.
Address.
(3)
Proposed location.
(4)
Dates of sales.
(c)
No permit shall be granted under this section unless the following requirements are satisfied:
(1)
The applicant shall be qualified to hold a permanent alcoholic beverage pouring license of the same classification under this Code.
(2)
The proposed location shall not be a prohibited location in violation of this Code.
(3)
The dates of sale under the permit shall not violate laws and regulations of the city or the state.
(4)
A permit fee shall be paid by the applicant to the city in an amount as set forth in the fee schedule of the city.
(5)
The application shall comply with regulations set forth in section 3-277.
(6)
When applicable, the application shall comply with section 3-278.
(d)
All applicants shall, within five working days from the expiration of the permit, file with the alcohol control commission an accounting of the sales under any temporary granted permit.

Sec. 3-280. - Off-premises licenses.
(a)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this Code, the alcohol control commission may grant a license to permit the off-premises pouring of malt beverages, wine, and liquor under the following terms and conditions:
(1)
An off-premises license may only be issued to licensees who hold in good standing an alcohol pouring license issued by the city, and no such licensee shall be permitted to pour any alcoholic beverage off-premises which cannot be lawfully poured on the licensee's premises.
(2)
An application for an off-premises pouring license shall contain the following information:
a.
Name of licensee.
b.
Address of licensee.
c.
Type of on-premises pouring license held by licensee.
d.
Type of off-premises license required by licensee.
(3)
No off-premises license shall be valid until the licensee pays an annual license fee in the amount established in the license fee scheduled in the city as provided in section 8-36 of this Code.
(4)
The amount of all sales of alcoholic beverages and food sold off-premises need not be included in the licensee's gross income figures for the purpose of establishing the licensee's food drink ratio reports that the licensee files with the city pursuant to this Code. However, the amount of such sales shall be included in the licensee's gross income figures for the purpose of establishing the amount of the mixed drink taxes due from the licensee to the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for an off-premises licensee to pour any malt beverages, wine, or liquor at any off-premises location until the licensee shall first obtain an off-premises pouring permit. Such permit may be issued by the city clerk for any period up to three days. Any permit for a period longer than three days must be issued by the alcohol control commission. An off-premises permit may be issued in accordance with the following:
(1)
An application for an off-premises pouring permit shall contain the following information:
a.
Name of licensee.
b.
Date and description of licensee's off-premises license.
c.
Street address of the proposed off-premises location. If the location has not been previously approved, a current survey shall be filed with the application which meets the survey requirements of section 3-74(6).
(2)
No permit for a particular off-premises location shall be issued if the requested building is a "prohibited location" as that term is defined in section 3-180(a), or fails to meet all requirements of the building inspector, the fire marshal and the traffic engineer for an on-premises pouring outlet.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any off-premises licensee to sell liquor at an off-premises location unless food is served at the event.
(4)
No off-premises permit shall be valid until the licensee pays a permit fee in the amount established in the license fee schedule of the city as provided in section 8-36 of this Code. The permit fee shall be based on a per day usage charge.
(5)
Unless otherwise provided for in this section, all regulations of this Code controlling the pouring of alcoholic beverages on-premises shall govern the pouring of alcoholic beverages off-premises.
(Ord. No. 95-1-1, § I, 1-17-95)

Sec. 3-301. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to pour or offer to pour any distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages within the corporate limits of the city without having a pouring license, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or this chapter.
(Code 1959, § 4-47; Code 1981, § 9-2072)

Sec. 3-302. - Separate license for each outlet; transfer; surrender.
(a)
A separate license shall be required for each pouring outlet, and a separate application shall be made for each such place.
(b)
Licenses for approved outlets under this article shall be transferable upon:
(1)
Approval of the transferee in accordance with the procedures established in section 3-169 for new applicants; provided, that except for the advertisement requirements of section 3-174, the approval as to location shall not be required.
(2)
Payment of a transfer fee of $400.00.
(c)
Immediately upon the sale or closing of a pouring outlet, it shall be the duty of the licensee to surrender his license to the city commission.
(d)
Notwithstanding subsections (a) through (c), upon the resignation or termination of employment of a person who holds the license for an outlet who is not himself the owner of the outlet, the outlet's owner or board of directors, if applicable, shall immediately file an application for the transfer of the license to another licensee. During the pendency of such application, and until its approval, the outlet may continue to operate under the authority of its license, upon the designation by the outlet of an interim licensee who meets the same qualifications of a licensee provided in this article, and upon the approval of such proposed interim licensee by the chief of police and the chairman of the alcohol control commission.
(Code 1959, § 4-48; Ord. No. 78-5, § 3, 10-17-77; Ord. No. 82-5-3, § 1, 5-17-82; Ord. No. 82-6-1, § 1, 6-7-82; Code 1981, § 9-2073; Ord. No. 2016-12-01, § IV, 12-12-16)
Sec. 3-303. - License for pouring; fee.
(a)
Licenses under this article shall be classified as follows:
(1)
Liquor pouring. Outlet for pouring liquor, which shall permit only the sale of liquor by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(2)
Wine pouring. Outlet for pouring wine, which shall permit only the sale of wine by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(3)
Malt beverage pouring. Outlet for pouring malt beverages, which shall permit only the sale of malt beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(4)
Sunday pouring. Outlet possessing one or more of the preceding licenses which shall also permit Sunday sales of alcohol by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(b)
The amount of the license fee for each class shall be as set forth in the license fee schedule of the city as provided in section 8-36.

Sec. 3-304. - Application, qualification; regulation.
(a)
Applications for all pouring licenses (malt beverage, liquor, and/or wine) shall be the same as, and in accordance with, the requirements applicable to the sale of liquor by the package at retail. All provisions of this chapter pertaining to the qualifications and licensing for a liquor retail package store license, except as provided herein, shall apply and be enforced in the sale of malt beverages, liquor, and/or wine for consumption on the premises; provided, that the residence requirements for such pouring licenses shall be as set out in section 3-78(b)(1); and, provided, further, that the 500-yard limitation restricting the location of liquor package outlets, as provided in section 3-180(a), shall not apply to pouring establishments.
(b)
Unless otherwise provided in this article, all regulations controlling the sale of liquor at retail by the package (section 3-137 shall be applicable as if fully set out as a separate section in this article), together with the additional provisions contained in sections 3-277 and 3-278 and the penal provisions of this chapter shall govern the sale of liquor, wine and/or malt beverages for consumption on the premises.
(c)
No application shall be considered from, and no license shall be granted, to an applicant whose business location for a liquor pouring outlet is anything other than a restaurant, hotel, private club, lounge, or any retail shop or establishment that is specifically approved by the city commission.
(d)
It is the intention of this article that liquor by the drink be sold only at bona fide restaurants, hotels, private clubs, and any retail shop or establishment that is specifically approved by the city commission under the restrictions set out in this article and not at walk-in bars or sham establishments.

Sec. 3-306. - Renewal, suspension, revocation, and monetary sanction.
(a)
Renewal. The provisions set forth in sections 3-80 and 3-182 shall apply to the renewal, suspension, and revocation of licenses under this article.
(b)
Summary action. There shall be no summary seizure of licenses under this division, unless there exists in the city a state of emergency duly declared; after any such seizure, a hearing shall be held as soon as practicable.
(c)
Action by city commission. The alcohol control commission may recommend to the city commission to suspend, refuse to renew, place on probation, or revoke a license under this article, and to impose a monetary sanction not to exceed $2,000.00, under the conditions set forth in this section. Then the city commission shall have full power and authority to suspend, refuse to renew, place on probation, or revoke a license under this article, and to impose a monetary sanction not to exceed $2,000.00 under the conditions set forth in this section. Before taking any such action except suspending a license for failure to pay an imposed monetary sanction when due, the city commission shall require a hearing, either before it or the alcohol control commission, at least five days' notice of which hearing shall have been given to the affected licensee. Sufficient grounds for the suspension, refusal to renew, probation, revocation of a license, or imposition of a monetary sanction include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
A licensee has failed to open a business within six months after approval of the license.
(2)
A licensee has ceased to operate the business for a period of three consecutive months.
(3)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has sold alcoholic beverages during a period of suspension.
(4)
A licensee gave false or misleading information in the original application or renewal process.
(5)
A licensee has failed to pay any fee, license fee, or other amount of money due to the city under this chapter or any other city-licensing ordinance.
(6)
A licensee has failed to maintain any and all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a license as set forth in this article.
(7)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person that the licensee or the licensee's employee or agent, knew or should have known to be in a state of intoxication.
(8)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person under the age of legal consumption in the State of Georgia.
(9)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other law, ordinance, or regulation governing the operation of an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages or which is reasonably related to the operation of such establishment.
(10)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other section of this chapter.
(11)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has been convicted or pled guilty or no contest to any violation of its license to sell or distribute alcoholic beverages issued by the State of Georgia Department of Revenue, City of Rome, Floyd County and/or Floyd County Sheriffs Office.
(12)
A licensee has failed to satisfy the criteria for obtaining a license to sell alcoholic beverages under this article.
(13)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any federal, state, or local laws or ordinances relating to the use and sale of drugs and alcoholic beverages.
(14)
When the continued operation of the licensed business is detrimental to the health, safety, or welfare of the public.
(d)
The failure of a licensee to pay when due any monetary sanction assessed pursuant to the terms of this chapter of the Code shall result in the immediate suspension of all alcohol licenses issued by the city to such licensee. A suspension under this subsection shall continue until all monetary sanctions and other sums due the city by the licensee are paid in full.
(Code 1959, § 4-50.1; Code 1981, § 9-2076; Ord. No. 2013-2-1, § V, 2-11-13)
