ARTICLE 8-12-4 - PRIVATE DETECTIVES AND SECURITY BUSINESSES

8-12-4-010 - Definitions.
A.
"Private detective business" means engaging in the business of or accepting employment to obtain or furnish information with reference to:
1.
Crimes or wrongs done or threatened against the United States of America or any state or territory thereof;
2.
The identity, habits, conduct, business, occupation, honesty, integrity, credibility, knowledge, trustworthiness, efficiency, loyalty, activity, movement, whereabouts, affiliations, associations, transactions, acts, reputation or character of any person;
3.
The location, disposition or recovery of lost or stolen property;
4.
The cause or responsibility for fires, libels, losses, accidents, damage or injury to persons or property; or
5.
The securing of evidence to be used before any court, board, officer or investigating committee.
Private detective business shall not include persons employed exclusively and regularly by only one employer in connection with the affairs of that employer only and where there exists an employer-employees relationship unless the employer is in the detective business.
B.
"Private security business" means engaging in the business as or accepting employment as a private patrol, watchman or guard, for consideration on a private contractual basis and not as an employee. Private security business shall not include persons employed exclusively and regularly by only one employer in connection with the affairs of that employer only and where there exists an employer-employees relationship unless the employer is in the private security business.
(Code 1978, § 8-3021; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-4-020 - Application form.
Registration for the business of private detective and private security business in the city shall be made by the applicant upon a form prescribed by the city manager. The application shall be addressed to and deposited with the city manager for verification of information and compliance with this Code section. The form shall include, but shall not be limited to the name and residential address of the applicant; the address where the business is to be conducted; the amount of bond or insurance posted, if required; a statement by the applicant as to arrests and dispositions for law violations by the applicant. The form shall also have attached thereto the statement of the chief of police as to the results of his or her investigation; a statement from the tax collector that there are no outstanding taxes, special assessments, fines or other moneys owing to the city; and the applicant shall meet all other code requirements of the city. No application shall be accepted by the city unless all the information and statements provided for by this section accompany the application. Upon the filing of a new or change of ownership license, the city shall require the applicant to deposit a processing fee of $100.00 that is nonrefundable.
(Code 1978, § 8-3022; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 5641, 1/8/97; Ord. No. 5762, 10/8/97, § 10; Ord. No. 5882, 8/12/98, § 4)
8-12-4-030 - State license required.
Each applicant shall file annually with the city a copy of his or her state license prior to city registration.
(Code 1978, § 8-3023; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-4-040 - City registration required.
No person shall engage in the private detective business and the private security business in the city without first registering with the city.
(Code 1978, § 8-3024; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-4-050 - License application.
Consideration of a license application by the city manager or his designee shall be in accordance with Section 80-4-080(E) of this Code.
(Ord. No. 5762, 10/8/97, § 11)
