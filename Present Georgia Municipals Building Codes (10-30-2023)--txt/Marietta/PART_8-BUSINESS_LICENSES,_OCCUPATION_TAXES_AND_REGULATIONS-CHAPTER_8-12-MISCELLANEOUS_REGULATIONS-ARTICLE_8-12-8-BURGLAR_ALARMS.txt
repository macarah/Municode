ARTICLE 8-12-8 - BURGLAR ALARMS

8-12-8-010 - Burglar alarm devices, operation.
It is unlawful for any person, partnership, corporation or any other type or class or association of persons to maintain, operate, attempt to use or operate, or cause to be used or operated, arrange, adjust, program or otherwise provide or install any device or combination of devices that will upon action either mechanically, electrically or by any other automatic means initiate a call and deliver a recorded message to any telephone number or numbers assigned to the city by a public telephone company unless the applicant has been approved by the city manager and an occupation tax certificate has been issued.
(Code 1978, § 8-3051; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 5762, 10/8/97, § 13)
8-12-8-020 - Application, form.
An application for an occupation tax certificate to engage in the business of burglar alarm installation in the city shall be made by the applicant upon a form prescribed by the city manager. The application shall be addressed to and deposited with the city manager for verification of information contained thereon. An investigation shall be conducted and a report issued regarding the findings of the investigation. The city manager shall grant or deny the license to the applicant upon completion of the investigation. The form shall include, but shall not be limited to, the name and residential address of the applicant; the address where the business is to be conducted; the amount of bond or insurance posted, if required; a statement by the applicant as to arrests and dispositions for law violations by the applicant. The form shall also have attached thereto the statement of the chief of police as to the results of his or her investigation; a statement from the tax collector that there are no outstanding taxes, special assessments, fines, or other monies owing the city; and the applicant must meet all other code requirements of the city. No application shall be accepted by the city unless all the information and statements provided for by this section accompany the application. Upon the filing of an application for a new or change of ownership license, the city shall require the applicant to deposit a processing fee of $100.00 per applicant and that fee is nonrefundable.
(Code 1978, § 8-3052; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 5641, 1/8/97)
8-12-8-030 - Cause for refusal to grant.
No application for any occupation tax certificate to engage in the business of installing burglar alarms shall be granted when the application or the evidence from the city manager's investigation shows any of the following conditions to exist:
A.
The applicant has had any occupation tax certificate issued under the police powers of the city previously suspended or revoked.
B.
The applicant, as a previous holder of an occupation tax certificate to engage in the business of burglar alarm installation, has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to the business, within a five-year period immediately preceding the date of application.
C.
Any material omission, untrue or misleading information contained in or left out of an original, renewal or transfer application for an occupation tax certificate hereunder shall be cause for denial thereof.
D.
Any of the provisions of this or any other section of this article are not fully complied with.
E.
If application for an occupation tax certificate to engage in the business of burglar alarm installation or service is made and refused for any of the reasons outlined in this section, no application shall be accepted covering the same person for a period of 12 months from the date of refusal to grant the application unless the application contains substantial changes therein related to the reasons for the original denial.
F.
Consideration of a license application by the city manager or his designee shall be in accordance with Section 8-4-080(E) of this Code.
(Code 1978, § 8-3053; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 5762, 10/8/97, § 14)
8-12-8-040 - Employees—Fingerprinting.
Each company or applicant shall file a list of all employees annually with the police department. All employees shall be fingerprinted before doing business within the city.
(Code 1978, § 8-3054; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-8-050 - Grounds for suspension or revocation—Notice; Hearings; Refund.
A.
No occupation tax certificate which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any applicant hereunder shall be suspended or revoked, except for due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing before the municipal court and upon a prior five-day written notice to the holder of the occupation tax certificate of the time, place and purpose of the hearing and statement of the charge upon which the hearing shall be held.
B.
"Due cause" for the suspension or revocation of the occupation tax certificate shall consist of the violation of any laws or ordinances regulating the businesses, or violation or regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating the businesses.
C.
The city manager is delegated the authority to suspend any occupation tax certificate hereunder for due cause in any emergency situation, and the suspension may be made effective immediately and remain in force until the next session of the municipal court.
D.
When an occupation tax certificate is so revoked, the city shall not be required to refund any portion of the occupation tax.
(Code 1978, § 8-3055; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
