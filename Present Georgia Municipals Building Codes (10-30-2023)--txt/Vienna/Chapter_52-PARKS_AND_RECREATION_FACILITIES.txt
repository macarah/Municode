Chapter 52 - PARKS AND RECREATION FACILITIES

Sec. 52-1. - General provisions.
The mayor and council may establish designated and named parks and recreation facilities. At the time of the adoption of the ordinance codified in this chapter, the parks and recreation facilities located within the corporate limits of the City of Vienna are as follows:
George Busbee Park
Anthony Bowens Park
Vienna Community Center/Oakdale Center
Burton Street Park
The city may at any time add, delete, or enlarge any existing or new parks and recreation facilities, without necessity of amending the ordinance codified in this chapter.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-2. - Rules and regulations generally.
The mayor and council may establish reasonable rules and regulations covering the operation of the named city parks and recreation facilities. When these rules and regulations are initially posted and/or displayed on the facility premises involved, such rules and regulations shall have the force and effect of law and any person committing any acts in violation hereof shall be deemed to have violated this section and shall be punished as set forth in section 1-11.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Secs. 52-3—52-5. - Reserved.
Sec. 52-6. - Group use of facilities.
(a)
City park and recreation facilities are available to the public on a "first available, first use" basis.
(b)
Any group of persons, not for profit organizations, or governmental entities may reserve use of the pavilion(s) located in the city park and recreation facilities by written request to the designated employee at the Vienna City Hall.
(c)
Not for profit groups or entities or governmental entities wishing to use the city park and recreation facilities as part of any fundraising activity may do so only after securing prior written approval from the appropriate designated employee at the Vienna City Hall.
(d)
No commercial use of the city park and recreation facilities shall be permitted.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-7. - Hours of operation.
The park and recreation facilities of the city shall be open to the public between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 10:00 p.m. daily for use by the citizens of the City of Vienna and the public.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-8. - Presence in park at night.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be in or occupy any part or portion of a city park or recreation facility from 10:01 p.m. to 7:59 a.m.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-9. - Fighting and obscene language prohibited.
Conduct which would constitute fighting or obscene language will not be permitted at any time in or on the city park and recreation facilities. The conduct as defined in section 50-1 of the Code of Ordinances is prohibited.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-10. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
(a)
Alcoholic beverages as used in this chapter are defined in section 6-1 of the Code of Ordinances.
(b)
Per section 6-2 of the Code of Ordinances, it shall be unlawful to possess alcoholic beverages in or about any city park or recreation facility.
(c)
Per section 6-3 of the Code of Ordinances, it shall be unlawful for any person to drink or have possession of alcoholic beverages in or about any city park or recreation facility.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-11. - Prohibited drugs.
As per O.C.G.A. ch. 13, delineated as "Controlled Substances" and more specifically as set forth in Article 2, Part 1, being O.C.G.A. § 16-13-21 et seq., it shall be unlawful to possess or consume any pharmaceutical or other controlled substance as listed in said Part 1 in any city park or recreation facility without a valid written prescription issued by a licensed medical provider with appropriate DEA number.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-12. - Damaging public property.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to destroy, deface, or damage any city park or recreation facility equipment or furnishings or playground equipment located thereon.
(b) The relocations or removal of any city park and recreation facility benches, furniture, or other equipment shall be considered unlawful and considered to be damaging public property.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-13. - Damaging vegetation.
(a) It shall be unlawful to break, cut or otherwise damage any shrubbery, flowers, trees, or other growing vegetation located in any city park or recreation facility.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to destroy, break, cut or otherwise damage any shrubbery, hedges, flowers, trees, or poles on city park and/or recreation facility by use of motor vehicle and the same shall be considered and constitute reckless driving.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-14. - Vehicles.
It shall be unlawful to drive or operate any motorized vehicle in a city park or recreation facility other than upon marked roadways and parking facilities furnished for such purposes of driving or operating.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-15. - Littering.
As set forth in section 62-38, dumping or depositing of litter shall be unlawful according to the acts set forth in subsections (a) and (b) of said section 62-38. Litter is defined in section 62-36, as all discarded sand, gravel, slag, brick bats, rubbish, waste material, tin cans, aluminum cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals, or other discarded materials of every kind and description except by depositing the same in receptacles provided for the purpose of receiving such without overflow.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-16. - Animals.
Pets are permitted in city park and recreations facilities. However, any animal which is required to be registered by either this Code of Ordinances in section 10-32, or Georgia law must have evidence of registration. It shall be unlawful for the owner or custodian of any animal to not have physical control of animals by leash within the confines or on the premises of any city park or recreation facility. The owner or custodian of any animal shall remove any excrement created by said animals while in the city park or recreations facility.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-17. - Advertising.
It shall be unlawful to place any sign or placard in any city park and recreation facilities or distribute any circulars or other advertising matter therein.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Sec. 52-18. - Noise control.
Unless approved by the mayor and city council and written shown, no person shall generate or allow to be generated any loud unnecessary, unusual noise within any city park or recreation facility as listed and defined in section 34-101 of the Code of Ordinances by any devices as enumerated in section 34-102 of the Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Secs. 52-19—52-25. - Reserved.
Sec. 52-26. - Enforcement and penalty.
The police department shall have jurisdiction to enforce municipal and state laws in city parks and recreation facilities. The violation of any of the provisions of this chapter of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Vienna is hereby declared to be a misdemeanor and shall be punishable as set forth in section 1-11.
(Ord. of 7-22-13)
Secs. 52-27—52-30. - Reserved.
