ARTICLE VI. - TREES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 34-251. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Official tree species list means the official list of trees authorized for planting, class, and distances of each specie to curbs or curbings and sidewalks as maintained by the city clerk as approved by the mayor and city council.
Park trees means those trees in public parks having individual names, and all areas owned by the city, or to which the public has free access as a park.
Street trees means those trees lying between the property lines on either side of all streets, avenues, drives, or ways within the city.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-252. - Specifications for tree plantings.
(a)
Species. No species of street tree shall be planted unless specifically listed on the official species tree list.
(b)
Spacing. Spacing of street and park trees shall be in accordance with the tree species size and class listed on the official tree species list and no trees may be planted closer together than specified on the official tree species except in special plantings designed or approved by the tree commission of the city.
(c)
Distance from curbs, sidewalks. The distance trees may be planted from curbs, curbings, and sidewalks shall be in accordance with the distance specified on the official tree species list. No street trees, other than those species listed as small trees on the official tree species list, may be planted closer than 35 feet from any curbs, curblines or sidewalks. No street trees shall be planted closer than ten feet from any fireplug or fire hydrant.
(d)
Distance from utility installations. No street trees, other than those species listed as small trees on the official tree species list, may be planted under or within ten lateral feet of any overhead utility wire, or over or within five lateral feet of any underground water line, sewer line, gas line, transmission line or other utility line and/or communication line.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-253. - City authority to plant, maintain, and remove trees.
(a)
The city shall have the right to plant, prune, maintain, and remove trees, plants, and shrubs within the lines of all streets, alleys, avenues, lanes, squares, and public grounds as may be necessary to ensure public safety, to preserve public properties, or to enhance the symmetry and beauty of the public grounds. The city tree commission, subject to obtaining the express written approval of the mayor and city council, may remove or cause or order to be removed, any tree or part thereof which is in an unsafe condition which by reason of its nature is injurious to sewers, electric power lines, gas lines, water lines, or other public utilities and improvements, or is affected with any injurious fungus, insect, or other definable pest.
(b)
No tree within the lines of all streets, alleys, avenues, lanes, squares, and public grounds, except any tree which is in immediate threat to the safety and welfare of the citizens of Vienna, due to damage to the tree by storm, lightning, or other calamity, shall be removed and/or cut down without the approval of the mayor and city council. The tree commission of the city may recommend removal of any such tree to the mayor and city council. The final authority concerning the removal of such tree is vested solely in the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-254. - Tree topping requiring authorization.
It shall be unlawful as a "normal practice" for any person, company, entity or city department to "top" any street tree, park tree, or other tree on public property. "Topping" is defined as the severe cutting back of limbs to stubs larger than three inches in diameter with the crown of the tree to such a degree as to remove the normal and natural canopy of the tree and disfigure the tree. Trees severely damaged by lightning, storms, or other causes, or certain trees located under utility wires or other public obstructions where other pruning practices are impractical may be exempted from this section at the determination of the city tree commission.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-255. - Pruning; corner clearance; clear space along public way.
The owner of land whereon any tree overhangs any street or public right-of-way within the city shall prune the branches of said tree to an extent that such branches shall not obstruct the view any street and/or public right-of-way intersections and further to the extent that no overhang shall be allowed which would obstruct a clear space of eight feet above the surface of any street, sidewalk, or other public right-of-way. The owner of land shall remove all dead, diseased, or dangerous trees or broken or decayed limbs on such land which constitutes a menace to the public health and safety of the public. The city shall have the right to prune any tree or shrub within the city when such tree or shrub obstructs or interferes with the lighting from a street light or lights along any street or public right-of-way and/or such tree or shrub obstructs or interferes with the visibility of any traffic control device or sign, including, but not limited to street name signs.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-256. - Tree removal and pruning on private property.
The city shall have the right to remove or cause the removal of any dead or diseased trees growing on private property within the city when such dead or diseased tree or trees constitute a threat or hazard to the health, safety or welfare of the public and/or other property. The city shall have the right to remove or cause the removal of any dead or diseased trees growing on private property within the city when such dead or diseased tree or trees harbor insects, disease, or other identifiable pests which constitute a threat to the life of other trees within the city based on published botanical criteria, such as publications of the University of Georgia Extension Service. The city tree commission shall notify the owner or owners of the dead or diseased tree or trees as described in this section in writing by either hand delivery or certified mail, return receipt requested. The owner or owners of such identified tree or trees identified in this section shall remove or cause the same to be removed at the owner or owners expense within 60 days of the date of receipt of the written notice from the city tree commission. In the event that the owner or owners fail to remove such tree or trees within the prescribed time, the city shall have the authority to remove the same and charge the cost of such removal to the owner or owners of the lands on which such tree or trees were located and/or place a lien against such lands as provided by law for collection of ad valorem taxes.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-257. - Removal of stumps.
All stumps of street and park trees which have been removed shall be removed in such manner and to such extent that topmost remaining portion of said stumps shall be below the surface level of the immediately surrounding land.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-258. - Interference with the city or city tree commission prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to prevent, delay, or interfere with the city, the city tree commission, and any of their members, employees or agents while such are within the scope of their authority and within the corporate limits of the city in the planting, cultivating, mulching, pruning, spraying, identifying, or removal of any street trees and park trees, as well as any trees on private lands which are within and authorized by this article.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-259. - Penalty.
Any person who shall violate section 34-258 of this article may, according to the nature of the offense, be charged with such offense as defined and proscribed in O.C.G.A. title 16.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Secs. 34-260—34-279. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - TREE COMMISSION

Sec. 34-280. - Created; membership.
(a)
There is hereby created and established a city tree commission for the City of Vienna which shall consist of five members who shall be residents of the City of Vienna or serve on the Keep Vienna Beautiful Commission, over the age of 18 years, and not incarcerated, on probation, or on parole for a conviction of or guilty plea to any felony as such are defined by the Official Code of Georgia Annotated and said commission shall be "Vienna Tree Commission".
(b)
The members shall be appointed by a majority vote of the mayor and city council at the organizational meeting of the mayor and city council each calendar year. The term of a member shall be three years. For the initial appointment to the first Vienna Tree Commission, one member shall be appointed for a one-year term, two members shall be appointed for a two-year term, and two members shall be appointed for a three-year term.
(c)
If a vacancy on the commission shall occur during the term of any member, the successor shall be appointed by a majority vote of the mayor and city council for the unexpired portion of the term. Vacancy shall be deemed to have occurred upon the death, incapacity, or resignation of a member, the member moving residence outside the corporate limits of the City of Vienna, or removal of the member for cause by the Vienna Tree Commission and/or the mayor and city council.
(d)
The members of the Vienna Tree Commission shall serve without compensation. However, the mayor and city council may at its discretion provide for reimbursement to members of actual expenses and costs incurred by a member or members.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-281. - Organization; records; rules of procedure.
(a)
The duly appointed members of the Vienna Tree Commission shall choose their own officers at the first meeting each calendar year and at the initial meeting of the first Vienna Tree Commission organizational meeting.
(b)
The duly appointed members of the Vienna Tree Commission shall set its meeting times and locations and promulgate its rules of procedure and its policies, procedures and regulations, subject only to the requirements of: (1) being in compliance with all due process requirements; and (2) not being in violation of and/or conflict with federal law, laws of the State of Georgia, and the Charter and Code of Ordinances of the City Vienna.
(c)
A majority of the members of the Vienna Tree Commission shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Meetings of the commission shall be in compliance with the Georgia "Open Meetings" Law.
(d)
The Vienna Tree Commission shall take, record, and maintain minutes of its meetings in a book designated for such purposes. Records of the commission shall be subject to the requirements of the Georgia "Open Records" Law.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-282. - Responsibilities; duties.
The Vienna Tree Commission shall study, investigate, counsel and develop and/or update annually and administer a written plan of action for the care, preservation, pruning, planting, replanting, removal, and disposition of trees in the public parks, within all public rights-of-way, and in other public owned areas. The plan and any amendments to the plan shall be presented annually to the mayor and city council. Upon the approval of the plan by the mayor and city council, the plan shall constitute the official comprehensive city tree plan for the City of Vienna. The Vienna Tree Commission, at the request of the mayor and city council, shall consider, investigate, make findings, report and recommend to the mayor and council upon any special matter or questions which is within the scope of the duties of the commission.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-283. - Review by the mayor and city council.
The mayor and city council shall have the right to review the conduct, acts, decisions of the Vienna Tree Commission upon its own motion or upon an appeal by an affected party.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Sec. 34-284. - Appeals.
(a)
Any person affected by the conduct, acts, findings or decisions of the Vienna Tree Commission may appeal such to the mayor and city council.
(b)
All appeals from the Vienna Tree Commission to the mayor and city council shall be filed with the Clerk of the City of Vienna at city hall and a copy served upon the presiding officer of the Vienna Tree Commission. All appeals shall be filed within 30 days of the date of the conduct, acts, findings, or decisions which are being appealed. All appeals shall be in writing and shall set forth: (1) the specific conduct, acts, findings or decisions which is being appealed; (2) the specific type of relief sought; (3) a short statement of the facts involved; and (4) copies of all documents, maps, plats, and/or photographs which the appealing party desires the mayor and city council to review.
(c)
The city clerk will notify in writing the appealing party and the Vienna Tree Commission as to the time, date and location of any hearing on the appeal from the Vienna Tree Commission to the mayor and city council. Said hearing will be held within 30 days of the receipt of the appeal by the city clerk.
(Ord. of 3-26-07)
Secs. 34-285—34-299. - Reserved.
