ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Enforcement.
Whenever the fire department is answering an alarm or operating at the scene of a fire or other emergency, every enrolled member of the department is hereby empowered and authorized to control and direct motor vehicle traffic, stop or move vehicles, and enforce all provisions of this article and any other code provisions the enforcement of which is deemed necessary to assist in the control of the fire or other emergency.
(Code 1980, § 3-2-4)
Sec. 38-2. - Fire limits.
The fire limits of the city are hereby defined as all that area within the city limits.
(Code 1980, § 3-2-22)
Sec. 38-3. - Tampering with alarm system.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to willfully, maliciously or mischievously interfere or tamper with any fire alarm box or any of the appliances or apparatus connected therewith, located within the city limits.
(Code 1980, § 3-2-5)
Sec. 38-4. - False fire alarms.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to willfully, maliciously or mischievously turn in or give or cause to be turned in or given a false fire alarm within the city limits.
(Code 1980, § 3-2-6)
Secs. 38-5—38-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[2]

Sec. 38-36. - Fire chief and members.
The fire department shall be headed by a fire chief and such other paid and volunteer members and employees as may be determined necessary by the mayor and council.
(Code 1980, § 3-2-1)
Sec. 38-37. - Workers' compensation extended to volunteers.
Volunteer firefighters who are members of the fire department providing services to the residents and taxpayers of the city are hereby extended coverage for workers' compensation in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 34-9-1 et seq.
(Res. of 9-12-83, § 1)
