Chapter 54 - PEDDLERS[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
Cross reference— Occupation taxes, § 70-181 et seq.

Sec. 54-1. - Refusing to leave.
Any peddler who enters upon premises owned, leased or rented by another and refuses to leave such premises after having been notified by the owner or occupant of such premises, or his agent, to leave the premises and not return thereto shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor.
Sec. 54-2. - Entrance to premises restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any peddler to enter upon any private premises when such premises are posted with a sign stating, "no peddlers allowed" or "no solicitations allowed" or other words to that effect.
Sec. 54-3. - Misrepresentations concerning goods or services.
It shall be unlawful for any peddler to make false or fraudulent statements concerning the quality or nature of his goods, wares, merchandise or services for the purpose of inducing another to purchase the same.
Sec. 54-4. - Hours of business.
It shall be unlawful for any peddler to engage in the business of peddling within the city between the hours of one-half hour before sunset and 9:00 a.m., except by specific appointment with or invitation from the prospective customer, or at any time on Sunday.
Sec. 54-5. - Activity in roadways.
It shall be unlawful for any person to peddle any goods, wares, merchandise or services, or to solicit any contributions of any kind for any charitable, educational or other purpose, while standing in or upon any public roadway.
