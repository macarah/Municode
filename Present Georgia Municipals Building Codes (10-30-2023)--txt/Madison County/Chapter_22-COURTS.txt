ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Vital records fees.
The total amount of fees authorized to be retained as personal compensation by the probate judge for service as local custodian, local registrar, or special abstracting agent pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 31-10-8 or 31-10-27 is hereby limited to an aggregate amount of the fees collected or $7,500.00, whichever is less, in any calendar year beginning on or after January 1, 2018. The probate judge shall prepare and submit a report at least quarterly to the board of commissioners specifying the amount received during the quarter for which the report is submitted.
( Res. of 4-30-2018(1), § 1 )
Secs. 22-2—22-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ENFORCEMENT OF COUNTY ORDINANCES

Sec. 22-31. - Accusation and court procedure.
(a)
Any person charged with violation of a county ordinance shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county, in accordance with the provisions of this article, O.C.G.A. title 15, chapter 10, article 4, (O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.) and other applicable law.
_____
(b)
Prosecutions for violations of this Code or other ordinances of the county shall commence either upon citations or accusations. Citations are to be issued and personally served upon the accused violator by the sheriff, any deputy so authorized by the sheriff, any county police officer or any other officer, employee or agent of the county as may be so empowered by the board of commissioners. Such citations shall be in substantially the following form:
"MADISON COUNTY, GEORGIA
CITATION FOR VIOLATION OF COUNTY ORDINANCE
Case#___________
TO: NAME _____
LAST       FIRST       MIDDLE
ADDRESS _____ Street or P.O. Box _____ City _____ State _____
D.O.B. _____ GENDER_______HGT._______WGT._______
DRIVER'S LICENSE NO. _____
You are hereby cited for violation of section_____of an ordinance adopted by the Board of Commissioners of Madison County, Georgia on___________, as amended, concerning (title or description of ordinance) , which violation occurred on the_____day of________, ___at (location) Madison County, Georgia.
You must appear for trial of this offense in the Magistrate's Court of Madison County, Georgia on the first floor of the Madison County Courthouse, Danielsville, Georgia on the_____day of________,___ at_____ .m. If you fail to appear at that time and place, a bench warrant may issue for your arrest and you may be required to post a bond for your future appearance.
County Officer Issuing Citation
RETURN OF SERVICE
I hereby certify that I served the foregoing citation personally upon___________at_______,on the_____day of__________.
Officer Serving Citation" _____
(c)
Upon the direction of the county commission chairman, prosecutions may proceed by accusation by the county attorney or his assistant who shall prosecute such cases and the accusation will be personally served upon the accused violator by the sheriff, any sheriff's deputy, county police, other officer, employee or agent of the county as may be so empowered by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 7-9-1991, § 1)
_____
Sec. 22-32. - Citation—When lawful.
If the form of citation provided under section 22-31 includes information and is sworn to as required under the general laws of this state in respect to a complaint charging commission of the offense alleged in such citation to have been committed, then such citation when filed with a court having jurisdiction shall be deemed to be a lawful complaint for the purpose of prosecution.
(Ord. of 7-9-1991, § 2)
Sec. 22-33. - Same—Failure to obey.
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate his written promise to appear in the county magistrate's court regardless of the disposition of the charge for which such citation was originally issued.
(Ord. of 7-9-1991, § 3)
State Law reference— Failure to appear for trial, O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.1.
Sec. 22-34. - Disposition of fines and forfeitures.
All fines or forfeitures collected upon conviction or upon the forfeiture of bail of any person charged with a violation of any of the provisions of this Code shall be paid into the county treasury and deposited in the general fund.
(Ord. of 7-9-1991, § 4)
State Law reference— Fees and costs to be deposited into county treasury, O.C.G.A. § 15-10-85; jurisdiction over violations of county ordinances, O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20.
Sec. 22-35. - Other remedies.
This article shall not limit the county's right to enforce county ordinances through other remedies.
(Ord. of 7-9-1991, § 5)
State Law reference— Violations of county ordinances, O.C.G.A. §§ 15-10-60 et seq., 36-1-20; punishment for misdemeanors, O.C.G.A. § 17-10-3; nuisances, O.C.G.A. tit. 41.
