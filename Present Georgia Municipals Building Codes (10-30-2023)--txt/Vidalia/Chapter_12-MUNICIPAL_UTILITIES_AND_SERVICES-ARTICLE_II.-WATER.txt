ARTICLE II. - WATER

Sec. 12-20. - Requirements incorporated in contracts.
The rules and regulations contained in this article shall be considered a part of the contract with every builder, developer and consumer of city water. Such builder, developer and consumer of city water shall be governed by and subject to such rules and regulations.
Sec. 12-21. - Compliance with building regulations prerequisite to issuance of water meter.
The city shall not issue any water meter within the city unless all requirements of the building chapter of this Code have been met. The city shall not be authorized to issue any water meter within the city where the same appears to be detrimental to the health and welfare of the city.
Cross reference— Buildings; construction and related activities, ch. 6.
Sec. 12-22. - Connection—Without authority.
It shall be unlawful for any person to connect any pipe to the city water system without express permission of the city.
Sec. 12-23. - Same—Application.
(a)
Connection with the mains of the city will be made upon the written application of the owner of the premises to be supplied with a connection, or of the owner's duly authorized agent upon blanks approved by city and upon compliance by the applicant with the rates, rules and regulations of city, and after the approval of the application by the city endorsed thereon. The size of service lines, valves, meters and other fittings, fixtures or appliances necessary to give the service applied for shall be determined by the city inspector.
(b)
Application for water service connections shall be made at the city offices in the city hall by the owner or agent of the property to be served, upon blank forms furnished therefor. Such application shall state the official street number or numbers of the premises, the size of the pipe required and the approximate location where such services shall enter the premises.
Sec. 12-24. - Same—Making.
The making of all service connections from the meter to improvements on premises including laying pipes, setting of water service fixtures, whether on private property or on public grounds, to be served by city water shall be made by duly licensed plumbers under the supervision of the building official.
Sec. 12-25. - Service taps—Authority; meter and back flow installation.
(a)
No service taps shall be made unless first authorized by the city. All such taps shall be made in accordance to city "Standard Plans, Standard Construction Specifications," or as otherwise directed by the city.
(b)
All new service taps shall include a water meter and back flow preventer provided by the city. All back flow preventers shall be of a double valve design and shall be approved by the American Waterworks Association. The cost for such service tap shall be included in the city's fees for service tap and shall be not less than the city's cost of hardware, labor, overhead, and administration.
(c)
Any meter replaced within the city's water system shall be equipped with a back flow preventer. All back flow preventers shall be of a double valve design and shall be approved by the American Waterworks Association. The cost for replacement meters shall be borne by the city.
Sec. 12-26. - Same—Charges for meters and back flow preventers.
(a)
Installation for new meters with back flow preventers shall be paid by the property owners. The cost for installation shall be reviewed and established by the city council and set out on a schedule to be maintained by the city clerk.
(b)
Installation of backflow preventers for fire meter assemblies shall be set as follows:
All such installations shall be cost of material plus fifteen (15) percent.
(c)
Additional costs may be added for boring under roadways.
(d)
These costs may be adjusted from time to time by resolution of the city council.
(e)
The above costs shall be paid at the time the application for metered service is made to the city. All meters and back flow preventers shall remain the property of the city and may be replaced at the discretion of the city.
(Code 1968, § 19-5; Ord. of 5-8-95(2); Ord. of 2-24-09)
Sec. 12-27. - Same—For water used for swimming pools; sewer charge deduction.
The city shall charge the normal rate for water used for the filling of swimming pools. The amount of water used for the filling of swimming pools shall be deducted from the total number of gallons used for figuring the sewer charge.
(Code 1968, § 19-14.1)
Sec. 12-28. - Number of meters.
(a)
All water customers of the city residing in single-family dwellings will have a water meter installed to record the amount of water used so as to pay the city for the total amount of water consumed.
(b)
All water customers residing in a duplex or similar place of abode will comply with the rule in subsection (a) unless it is not practical; then the city will determine the disposition of each water customer, if there is a reason to do so.
(c)
Where there are three (3) or more families living in an apartment unit and where the apartment owner charges for water as a part of the cost to the tenants, then the city will be satisfied with one (1) meter and will charge the apartment owner for the total water consumed by the tenants and the owner will either collect a share from each tenant or will absorb the cost personally if this cost is a part of the lease agreement, but it will be the owner's responsibility to pay the city for total water registered on the water meter.
(d)
Water meters shall be installed on all municipal and public buildings, including churches, and any other such buildings that use water, and the city clerk shall render a bill every thirty (30) days for such service.
(Code 1968, §§ 19-13, 19-14)
Sec. 12-29. - Tampering with meter.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to tamper with or deliberately damage a city water meter. Any such tampering or deliberate destruction shall be deemed to be a misdemeanor and shall be penalized as follows:
(1)
Twice the city's estimate of water consumed in excess of actual reading in the case of property owner or consumer tampering; or
(2)
Twice the cost of meter installation in the case of nonproperty owner or consumer destruction.
(b)
Any subsequent tampering or deliberate destruction shall result in a citation to appear in municipal court and, upon a determination of guilt, to punishment as prescribed in section 1-8.
Sec. 12-30. - Application for service—Submitting; contents.
(a)
Upon the application of the owner, or any authorized agent, or tenant of the owner, premises will be furnished through the connections already made, or applied for by the owner, and upon compliance by the applicant with the rates, rules and regulations of city, as established from time to time as the need requires by resolution, and after approval of the application by city endorsed thereon.
(b)
The application shall state the name of the owner of the premises, the character and extent of the service desired, a description of the premises, including the name of the street, and house number, if any, the number and kind of fixtures through which water will be supplied, and such other information as may be reasonably required by the city to enable it to give the desired service.
(c)
Such application may be in substantially the form on file at city hall.
Sec. 12-31. - Same—On change in ownership, tenancy or service.
A new application shall be made and approved by the city upon any change in ownership or tenancy of the property, or in the service, as described in the application. The city shall have the right to discontinue the water supply until such new application is made and approved.
Sec. 12-32. - Deposit for water service.
(a)
All water customers of the city shall deposit with the city the amount indicated below for each meter to guarantee the prompt and full payment of all charges imposed by the city for water furnished by the city to the customer:
(1)
Residential customers, one hundred dollars ($100.00).
(2)
Commercial customers, an amount determined by the city manager based upon the projected use.
Any account to which water service is terminated for nonpayment of a city water bill shall be revaluated and water service shall not be reestablished until a deposit in the amount determined by the city manager to be sufficient to cover the average account balance is paid to the city.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall apply to all existing deposits held by the city in connection with water service being furnished by the city and to all present and future customers obtaining water service from the city.
(Code 1968, §§ 19-6, 19-9; Mo. of 11-9-92; Ord. of 2-10-14 )
Sec. 12-33. - Schedule of charges for water service.
The charges for water service from the city shall be based on rate studies conducted every two (2) years or more often; the charges shall be fixed after each such study.
(Code 1968, §§ 19-11, 19-12)
Sec. 12-34. - Payments.
(a)
At the time of making application for connection or service, applicant must make payment to city of the fees, deposits, or rates, as the case may be, as fixed by the city council.
(b)
Rates for water service shall be subject to change according to the fiscal needs of the city from time to time as determined by the city council; such rates shall be published in the office of the city hall where utility bills may be paid and in accordance with the most recent resolution by the city council pertaining to such rates.
(c)
The rate shall have a minimum period charge based on minimum gallons provided plus a charge for each one thousand (1,000) gallons of water use over the minimum per period.
Sec. 12-35. - Due date; shut-off for nonpayment; restoration of service.
Water bills are due on the twentieth day of each month. If a water bill is not paid by the fifth day of the next month, water service to the premises for which the bill was rendered may be terminated by the city; once terminated service shall not be restored until the bill has been paid. In addition, when a bill is not paid by the second Monday of the following month, no water service shall be furnished until the bill is paid to the customer responsible for payment of the bill at any other location or to any member of the customer's household. A fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) is added to the bill if the bill is not paid by such second Monday of the following month. In addition, if there is a second termination within six (6) months, an amount of seventy-five dollars ($75.00) will be added to the bill, twenty-five dollars ($25.00) of which will be applied to the customer's deposit.
( Ord. of 2-10-14 )
Sec. 12-36. - Fixtures and equipment.
(a)
The city will furnish and maintain at its own cost all necessary fittings, pipes, appliances, including all meters and meter boxes, to deliver water to the consumer's pipeline, at a point just inside the curb line on the street, and just outside the property line in alleys or other public places; all such fittings, pipes, appliances, meters and meter boxes shall remain the property of city, and shall at all times be accessible to city and under its control. The service line from the curb box or meter to the consumer's premises shall be laid and maintained by the applicant at applicant's own cost. The line shall be of ample size, of standard weight and quality, and all cutoffs, valves, fixtures and appliances furnished by the consumer shall be maintained in good order and condition. All builders or developers, as the case may be, must install a "bleed valve" on all water lines installed in street cul-de-sacs.
(b)
All service laterals are the pipes that run from the property owner's home and connect to the water and sewer services provided by the City of Vidalia. Property owners are responsible for maintaining the water service laterals that run through their property which begin at the point where it connects to the city owned water meter, also known as the water service connection. Should a leak, break or blockage occur on the property owner's side of the water service connection, the property owner is responsible for damages, repairs and all associated costs. Should such leak, break or blockage occur on the property owner's side of the water service connection within the city's right-of-way, then the property owner's repairs can only be conducted under the direct supervision of the appropriate authorized or designated city official. It shall be unlawful for any property owner to conduct any water maintenance within the city right-of-way regardless of where the water connection point might be, without permission and direction of the city, even though all such work shall be at the sole expense of the property owner.
( Ord. of 7-14-14(1) )
Sec. 12-37. - Leaks.
All leaks in the service or any other pipe, or fixture, on the premises of the consumer shall be immediately repaired. Upon failure to repair any such leak, the service may be discontinued until repairs are made.
Sec. 12-38. - Inspection.
All pipes, meters and fixtures shall at all reasonable hours be subject to inspection by city or by its duly authorized agents.
Sec. 12-39. - Damages.
(a)
The city shall in no event be responsible for maintaining any service line owned by the consumer, nor for damage done by water escaping therefrom, nor for defects in lines or fixtures, on the property of the consumer. The consumer shall at all times comply with all regulations of the city and of regulations relating to the service lines, and shall make all changes in the line as required on account of change in grade or otherwise.
(b)
All damage to service pipes or street mains caused by careless or negligent work, or improper filling at excavations, shall be chargeable to the persons causing such damage, and the expense of repairing the same shall be recovered from such persons.
Sec. 12-40. - Installation, maintenance, etc.
(a)
All service pipes and fixtures from the water meter to the premises, including the corporation cocks at the mains, shall be installed and maintained at the expense of the property owners, and all leaks and other defects in the same shall be promptly repaired by them, or if not promptly repaired, then water will be turned off until such repairs have been made, and the expense thereof shall be charged against the owners, and must be paid before water shall be turned on again.
(b)
The city shall not be held responsible by reason of the breaking of any service pipe or apparatus, water coil, or for failure in the supply of water.
Sec. 12-41. - Illegally turning on water after cut-off for nonpayment.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cut on or use any water after such person's water service has been discontinued for nonpayment of bills.
Sec. 12-42. - Bypassing meter.
It shall be unlawful for any person to run any pipe around any meter with intent to defraud the city of water revenue.
State Law reference— Theft of service, O.C.G.A. § 16-8-5.
Sec. 12-43. - Injury to or tampering with water meters, pipes, etc.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to intentionally injure, destroy or permit to be injured or destroyed any water meter, pipe, conduit, line or other apparatus belonging to or operated by the city. It shall likewise be unlawful for any person to intentionally prevent a water meter belonging to or operated by the city from duly registering the quantity of water supplied to or in any way interfere with the proper action or just registration of such meters. It shall likewise be unlawful for any person to intentionally divert any water from any place of the city, or do otherwise, intentionally use or cause to be used, without the consent of the city, any water manufactured or distributed by the city.
(b)
Any person who intentionally injures or destroys, or permits same, to any water meter, pipe, conduit, line or other apparatus belonging to the city and/or the existence of any device, by whatever name used, for the purpose of diverting any water from any pipe of the city without the consent of the city, shall be prima facie evidence of the intent to defraud by the person receiving the benefits from such action. The destruction or damage to city property or the existence of such a device shall be deemed presumptive evidence of the intention referred to in this section.
(c)
Any charge or violation of the above provisions shall result in a citation to appear in municipal court and, upon a determination of guilt, to punishment as prescribed in sections 12-29 and 1-8 herein.
(Ord. of 4-13-92(2), § 19-80; Ord. of 12-19-13, §§ 1, 2 )
Sec. 12-44. - Temporary water service.
A fee of ten dollars ($10.00) shall be paid to the city for temporary water service. No such service shall be continued for a period of time in excess of ten (10) consecutive days.
(Mo. of 4-20-93)
