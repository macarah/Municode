ARTICLE B. - LICENSES AND PERMITS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 9-5-31. - Operation of business; driving vehicle; permit and license prerequisite.
No person shall operate any taxicab business within the city until the license provided for in this chapter has been granted and issued. No person shall drive a taxicab within the city without first having obtained a driver's permit as provided in this chapter. Incidental, intermittent and unscheduled passenger deliveries into the city by taxicab operators located and situated exterior to the city shall not require the driver's permit as described herein.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-32. - Investigation of applications and renewals.
The chief of police shall cause an investigation to be made into the truthfulness of the information provided by the applicant and into the personal and occupational history of the applicant. The chief of police shall determine on the basis of the investigation whether the license or permit should be issued or renewed. If the chief of police decides that the license or permit should be issued or renewed, he shall immediately notify the applicant. If the chief of police decides that the permit or license should not be issued or renewed, he shall immediately notify the applicant and inform the applicant of the reasons for the denial.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-33. - Suspension or revocation; preliminary investigation; show cause hearing.
(a)
Grounds for suspension or revocation. A business license or driver's permit may be suspended or revoked for the following reasons:
(1)
The applicant furnishes fraudulent or untruthful information or omits information requested in the application for a license or permit.
(2)
Failure to pay all fees, taxes or other charges.
(3)
Failure to maintain all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a license or driver's permit.
(4)
Failure to have the driver's permit posted in the taxicab in a place and manner clearly visible to all passengers at all times.
(5)
Charging a fare in excess of those fares on file with the chief of police.
(6)
Having four (4) or more moving traffic violations while operating any taxicab, limousine or passenger-carrying vehicle, during any twelve-month period.
(7)
Refusing to accept a passenger solely on the basis of race, color, national origin, religious belief, gender or sexual orientation. Sexual orientation shall mean the state of being heterosexual, homosexual or bisexual. Operators shall not refuse to accept a passenger unless the passenger is obviously intoxicated or dangerous.
(8)
Allowing the required insurance coverage to lapse or allowing a vehicle to operate in the city without an inspection sticker.
(9)
For violation of any part of this chapter.
(b)
Preliminary investigation; show cause hearing; findings of fact. Where it is reported to the chief of police that a business licensee or driver permittee is engaged in any conduct set forth in subsection (a) of this section, the chief of police shall conduct a preliminary investigation in order to determine whether there is a basis for the report. If his preliminary investigation reveals that there may be a basis for revocation or suspension of the license or permit, the license or permit holder shall be notified to appear before the chief of police on a date certain and show cause why the license or permit should not be revoked or suspended. The license or permit holder may appear in person at such hearing or be represented by counsel. At the conclusion of the hearing, the chief of police, based upon the evidence submitted at such hearing, shall enter an order make a finding of fact, and then:
(1)
Find that the evidence does not authorize a revocation or suspension;
(2)
Issue a warning to the license or permit holder;
(3)
Suspend the license or permit and probate suspension;
(4)
Suspend the license or permit for up to thirty (30) days; or
(5)
Suspend the license or permit pending a hearing by the mayor and counsel on revocation.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-34. - Appeals from decisions of chief of police as to applications, renewal, revocation or suspension.
(a)
Appeals. Decisions of the chief of police that adversely affect or aggrieve any applicant, renewal applicant, licensee, or permittee may be appealed to the city council. Such appeal shall be by written petition, filed in the office of the chief of police within ten (10) days after the decision of the chief of police. The chief of police shall schedule a hearing before the mayor and council and notify the petitioner of the time and date of the hearing. Either party shall have the right to subpoena witnesses at the hearing. At the conclusion of the hearing, the mayor and council may affirm, overrule, partially affirm and partially overrule the decision of the chief of police.
(b)
Remedies within the authority of the mayor and council. The mayor and council shall be authorized to:
(1)
Find in favor of the applicant, licensee or permittee;
(2)
Deny the grant or renewal of the application;
(3)
Issue a warning to the licensee or permittee;
(4)
Suspend the license or permit and probate suspension;
(5)
Revoke the license or permit and probate revocation;
(6)
Suspend the license or permit for a definite period of time;
(7)
Revoke the license or permit;
(8)
Take any other appropriate action regarding the licensee or permittee.
(c)
Disposition of decisions. The decisions of the mayor and council shall be in writing, indicating therein the reasons for the hearing, the evidence and/or testimony presented, a finding of fact, the final decision, and the reasons therefor. A copy of the decision shall be sent by certified or registered mail to the last address furnished by the applicant, licensee, or permittee.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 9-5-46. - Taxicab owner's business license; application.
(a)
No owner shall operate, or allow others to operate a taxicab on the streets of Forest Park, Georgia, without first obtaining a taxicab owner's business license. The taxicab owner's business license application form will be obtained from the office of the Police Chief of Forest Park, Georgia. The application form will require the following information:
(1)
Full name and address of person proposing to operate or conduct a taxicab business within the city. A statement that the applicant is twenty-one (21) years of age or older. No license to operate a taxicab business shall be granted to any person under twenty-one (21) years of age.
(2)
A full and complete statement of the business to be engaged in and the type of equipment to be used.
(3)
Addresses of the dispatch location or place from which the business is to be operated or conducted, the telephone switchboard location, and the parking area location for the taxicab businesses' vehicles.
(4)
Whether the operator of the taxicab business for which the license is sought is a corporation, partnership, or individual.
(5)
Trade name of the taxicab business to be operated.
(6)
If an individual, the owner's name and home address.
(7)
If a partnership, the names and addresses, including street and post office boxes, of all partners and the extent of each partner's interest.
(8)
If a corporation, the names and addresses of all officers and the amount of its capital stock.
(9)
General description of identifying words and color scheme of the taxicab fleet.
(10)
A list of all drivers who will be scheduled for licensing under this chapter. The list must indicate whether the drivers are employees of the business or independent contractors.
(11)
The make, model, year, and passenger capacity of all vehicles to be operated under the license.
(12)
Name and policy number of the insurance carrier providing liability coverage for the taxicabs, as well as the amount of coverage, which in no event shall be less than the amount of coverage required under section 9-5-48 of this chapter.
(13)
An affirmative statement that any business conducted under the license granted upon such application shall be in full and complete compliance with all national, state, county, municipal and local laws, ordinances, rules and regulations, with a particular reference to wages, hours, conditions of employment, and the protection by liability insurance of all persons whom the law requires the operators of such a business to protect.
(14)
A copy of the rate schedule.
(15)
The daily hours of operation for the business.
(16)
Any and all other information as may be required by the chief of police.
(b)
Upon approval by the chief of police of the taxicab owners' business license, the owner must have on record with the chief of police the original and a copy of the insurance policy of the business covering all taxicabs, evidence that the business has purchased the required business license from the city, and each taxicab to be operated must have an inspection sticker properly affixed, before beginning operation in the city.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-47. - License fee and business tax.
Every person engaged in the business of conducting a taxicab business in the city shall pay to the city an annual license fee in advance for the privilege of using the streets for carrying on the same as provided in chapter 1 of this title. In addition, the license holder shall pay such business and occupation tax as provided in title 3, chapter 3.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-48. - Insurance required.
No license to operate any taxicab business within the city shall be issued or continued in operation unless the operator of the taxicab business has in full force and effect a liability insurance policy for each taxicab in an amount that shall not be less than twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) for bodily injury to any one (1) person; fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) for injuries to more than one (1) person which are sustained in the same accident; and twenty-five thousand ($25,000.00) for property damage resulting from any one (1) accident. Such insurance policy shall inure to the benefit of any person who shall be injured, or who shall sustain damage to property proximately caused by the negligence of the holder of any taxicab license, his servants, or agents. Such insurance shall be with a company authorized to do business in the state, and such insurance shall be kept in force. It shall be the duty of the chief of police of the city to enforce this requirement. Such insurance shall be subject to approval of the city, and termination of the insurance protection shall automatically operate as a revocation of the license to operate a taxicab business within the city. License holders shall file policies of insurance with the police department, and license holders shall ensure that their insurance carriers provide the city a written notice at least ten (10) days before the cancellation of any policy.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)

DIVISION 3. - PERMIT

Sec. 9-5-60. - Taxicab driver permits; application.
No driver shall operate or be allowed to operate a taxicab for hire on the streets of Forest Park, Georgia, unless the operator shall have a current and unrevoked taxicab driver's permit. The taxicab driver's permit application form will be obtained from the office of the police chief. The application form will require the following information:
(1)
Name and home address of driver.
(2)
A statement that the applicant is twenty-one (21) years of age or older. No driver's permit shall be granted to any person under twenty-one (21) years of age.
(3)
Name, address, and license number of the taxicab company under which the driver operates or is to operate the taxicab.
(4)
A statement that the applicant is a citizen of the United States or an alien admitted for permanent residence or who has otherwise been granted employment authorization by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service.
(5)
A statement that the applicant is proficient with the English language so as to be able to comprehend and interpret traffic signs, issue written receipts to passengers, and obey lawful orders of police and others in lawful authority. No driver's permit shall be granted to any person who does not exhibit such a proficiency.
(6)
A statement that the applicant has not been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to or forfeited bond as to any of the violations and offenses set forth in section 9-5-61 of the Code of Ordinances. No driver's permit shall be granted to any person who has committed any such violations and offenses.
(7)
A statement from a licensed physician that the driver has no physical infirmities which will adversely affect his or her ability to drive.
(8)
Driver's license number issued by the State of Georgia and its date of expiration, as well as a statement that the applicant's license is not limited as defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-5-58 and O.C.G.A. § 40-5-64. No driver's permit shall be granted to any person who does not possess a current valid Georgia driver's license or whose license is limited as defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-5-58 and O.C.G.A. § 40-5-64.
(9)
A copy of chapter 5 of the Code of the city shall be provided to each taxicab driver by the city police department at the time a taxicab driver receives an original taxicab driver permit.
(10)
A signed statement consenting and granting to the city the right to investigate the applicant's personal and occupational history, including, but not limited to, the right to fingerprint the applicant and to conduct a criminal history background check.
(11)
Any and all other information as may be required by the city manager or chief of police which in his opinion is needed to protect the health and welfare of the persons in the city.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-61. - Criminal record of driver applicant.
(a)
Before issuing any driver permit to a taxicab driver applicant, the police chief shall make a background check of the applicant to determine whether or not he has a criminal record.
(b)
No person who has been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to or forfeited bond on any federal or state felony offense within five (5) years of the application for a driver permit shall be issued such a permit, nor shall such permit be renewed.
(c)
No person who has been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to or forfeited bond on any federal or state misdemeanor offense involving moral turpitude or alcohol or drugs within three (3) years of the application for a driver permit shall be issued such a permit, nor shall any such permit be renewed.
(d)
No person who has been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to or forfeited bond on any ordinance violation involving alcohol or drugs or sexual conduct in Forest Park, Georgia, or any other political subdivision within one (1) year of the application for a driver permit, shall be issued such a permit, nor shall any such be renewed.
(e)
No person who has been convicted of or pled guilty or nolo contendere to or forfeited bond on four (4) or more moving traffic violations under either state or local or federal law within one (1) year of the application for a driver permit shall be issued such a permit, nor shall any such permit be renewed.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-62. - Report of changes in qualifications or permitting information.
Drivers are responsible for reporting any change(s) in the qualifications or other permitting information previously supplied to the chief of police within ten (10) days of the change.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-63. - Notification of dismissal of taxicab driver.
Whenever a taxicab driver is dismissed from the employment of a taxicab business, the licensee shall notify the chief of police in writing within forty-eight (48) hours of such dismissal.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-64. - Application fee and permit renewal.
The applicant shall pay to the city such sum as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council when the application is filed. Should it be granted, the applicant's license fee will then be considered as paid in full for the remainder of the calendar year. Within the first ten (10) days of December of each year, all taxicab drivers who expect to drive in the following year shall make application for renewal of their permits. The chief shall provide a form for this purpose, and the applicant shall pay an additional fee as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council with each renewal application filed.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
Sec. 9-5-65. - Content of permit and the posting of permit and inspection sticker; responsibility of licensees.
(a)
A taxicab driver's permit shall bear the name, address and age of the driver. Additionally, the permit shall show the name of the driver's employer and shall have thereon a specimen signature of the driver and shall display a current photograph of the driver.
(b)
The permit shall be posted in the taxicab being driven in such a place as to be in full view of all passengers while the taxicab is being operated.
(c)
Licensees under this chapter are responsible for checking to ensure that each driver has in his or her possession a current driver permit and that the permit is posted in the taxicab in such a place as to be in full view of all passengers along with a visible vehicle inspection sticker.
(Ord. No. 01-05, § 1, 5-21-2001)
