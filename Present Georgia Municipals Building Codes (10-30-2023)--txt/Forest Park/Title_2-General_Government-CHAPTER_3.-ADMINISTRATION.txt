Sec. 2-3-1. - Departments established.
(a)
The following departments of the city are hereby established:
(1)
Economic development;
(2)
Finance;
(3)
Fire;
(4)
Human resources;
(5)
Planning and community development;
(6)
Public works;
(7)
Police;
(8)
Recreation and leisure services; and
(9)
Technology services.
(b)
Each department may be headed by a director who shall exercise such powers and perform such duties as provided by law or as prescribed from time to time by the city manager. Department directors shall report to the city manager.
(Ord. No. 17-11, § 1, 3-20-2017; Ord. No. 20-15 , Exh. B, 6-15-2020)
Sec. 2-3-2. - City manager.
(a)
The position of city manager is hereby created. The city manager shall devote his time and attention to the affairs of the city and shall be responsible to the mayor and council for the efficient administration of all the affairs of the city over which he has jurisdiction.
(b)
The city manager may, in consultation with each agency and department of the city, propose written agency or department rules and regulations, or amendments thereto, to organize and operate each agency or department of the city in the most efficient manner reasonably possible, consistent with the charter and ordinances of the city.
(c)
The city manager may propose written rules and regulations, or amendments thereto, for city government wide application on subjects under the city manager's responsibilities to organize and operate the city in the most efficient manner reasonably possible, consistent with the charter and ordinances of the city.
(d)
All such rules and regulations, or amendments thereto, shall become effective thirty (30) days after being sent to the mayor and council for review unless, by motion, such rules and regulations are disapproved in whole or in part within such time by the mayor and council.
(e)
All such rules and regulations may be amended by a resolution of the mayor and council.
Sec. 2-3-3. - City clerk and finance director.
(a)
The city clerk shall:
(1)
Issue warrants, summons, receipts, executions, and other processes in the name of the city.
(2)
Maintain an accurate and legible minute book in which is recorded every official act of the governing body and reveals which persons were present. The minute book shall be in sufficient detail to allow ready reference and shall reflect the proper adoption of resolutions, motions and ordinances as required by law.
(3)
Be the official custodian of city records.
(4)
Be the official custodian of city property, real and personal, seeing that it is maintained, insured and protected.
(5)
Perform such other duties and functions as are required by the City Charter, Code of Ordinances, and the motions and resolutions of the governing body.
(b)
The finance director shall:
(1)
Be the tax collector for the city.
(2)
Receive, collect, and deposit all funds due the city from every source.
(3)
Maintain the city's bank accounts and see that no withdrawal or payment is made from same except as authorized by the governing body.
(4)
Pay the legitimate and due bills of the city taking advantage at all times of discounts.
(5)
Require adherence to the city budget unless deviation is authorized by the governing body.
(6)
Provide monthly reports of every account and claim payable to the city which has remained unpaid for thirty (30) days after the same became due.
(7)
Perform such other duties and functions as are required by the City Charter, Code of Ordinances, and the motions and resolutions of the governing body.
(c)
The city clerk and the finance director may delegate to such deputies, as authorized by the governing authority or the city manager, such of their duties as good judgment dictates should be so delegated.
(Code 1959, § 2-6; Ord. No. 18-13, § 1 (Exh. A), 7-16-2018)
Sec. 2-3-4. - Monies belonging to city; receipt and deposit.
In accordance with Georgia law, all funds received by officers and employees of the city shall be properly receipted and promptly deposited into the appropriate account under the direction of the director of finance.
Sec. 2-3-5. - Collection of money due the city.
The city manager is authorized to engage the services of any reputable collection agency to collect money due the city and to compensate the agency or agencies as is customary in the trade whenever in his judgment such services are the most advantageous to collect the money.
(Code 1959, § 2-71)
Sec. 2-3-6. - Bonds required of certain officers and employees.
Any elected official, appointed officer or employee who, in the course of his duties, handles funds belonging to the city, shall give good and sufficient bond to the city, in accordance with applicable administrative regulations, conditioned upon the faithful performance and discharge of his duties, and for proper application of all monies or property coming into his hands by virtue of his office or employment in an amount as established by the mayor and council.
Sec. 2-3-7. - Use of city labor or property for private purposes.
It shall be unlawful to use, furnish, cause or permit any city labor or property to be used or furnished for the benefit of anyone except the city, unless such use is authorized by the governing body and entered on the minutes.
(Code 1959, § 2-27)
Sec. 2-3-8. - Code enforcement.
(a)
There is hereby created within the City of Forest Park a position known as "code enforcement officer."
(b)
The duties of the code enforcement officer shall be as follows:
(1)
The enforcement of all ordinances contained within the Code of Ordinances for the City of Forest Park.
(2)
To issue citations for violations of said Code of Ordinances, in order to insure compliance with said Code.
a.
Any person found to be in violation of said ordinance may receive a warning citation in the first instance,
b.
In the event said violation is not corrected, the code enforcement officer shall then cite that person for violation of said ordinance,
c.
Any citations issued by the code enforcement officer shall contain a summons and shall be prosecuted as set forth in section 2-5-4 et seq. of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Forest Park.
(c)
Retention of authority. Each and every department head, where otherwise authorized to do so, shall retain their individual authority to cite persons for violations of same, and nothing contained herein shall in any manner delete or remove any authority given to any other person pursuant to the Code of Ordinances of the City of Forest Park. Further, the position of code enforcement officer and the authority to cite persons for violations of the code is created in order to provide an additional source by which persons found to be in violation of any part or parts of the Code of Ordinances shall be compelled to comply with same.
(Ord. No. 87-014, 8-7-87)
Sec. 2-3-9. - Use of credit card to pay monies due city.
(a)
All sums due to the city including, but not limited to, ad valorem taxes, occupation taxes, fines, forfeitures, penalties, license fees, permit fees and registration fees, may be paid by the use of credit cards. The city finance director, with the approval of the city manager, shall establish the procedures for acceptance of credit card payments for amounts due the city. The mayor is authorized to enter into agreements with credit card issuers to facilitate the acceptance of credit card payments by the city.
(b)
A credit card agreement may provide for the acceptance of the credit card payments from the credit card company at a discount from the face amount of the payment or for the withholding of administrative fees from the face amount of the payment. Such discounted fee is granted in exchange for the improved governmental cash flow, reduction of administrative expenses and increased public convenience as a result of credit card payments.
(c)
A surcharge equal to the administrative fees charged by the credit card issuer to the city shall be imposed upon any person making a payment by credit card, when such surcharges are permitted by the operating rules and regulations of credit card issuer.
(d)
Any agreement with a credit card issuer for the acceptance of payments via credit shall contain a provision that the agreement may be canceled at any time by the city.
(e)
No person making payment by credit card to the city is relieved from liability from the underlying obligation except to the extent that the city actually receives final payment of the obligation in cash or its equivalent. If final payment is not made by the credit card issuer or other guarantor of payment in the credit card transaction, the unpaid amount shall be due from the person making the payment by credit card.
Such penalties, interest and fees on an unpaid amount shall accrue from the date the unpaid obligation was first due under the applicable statute or ordinance. The city shall follow the collection procedure for the unpaid amount that would otherwise have been applicable had the credit card payment not been attempted.
(Ord. No. 99-014, § 1, 8-2-99)

ARTICLE B. - PUBLIC RECORDS[8]

Sec. 2-3-21. - Official records of city; keeping and maintaining.
It shall be the policy of the governing body to make each and every record kept and maintained by the governing body and the officers, agents, and employees of the city available to the public for reading, inspecting, examining and copying the same, provided that those records which by order of a court of this state or by law are prohibited from being open to inspection by the public shall be exempt from the provisions of this article.
Sec. 2-3-22. - Custodian designated.
The city clerk is hereby made the official custodian of the records of the city, including records made by its officers, agents and employees of every nature whatsoever.
(Code 1959, § 2-14)
Sec. 2-3-23. - Removal from city hall restricted.
The custodian of the records of the city shall not allow any person to take away from the city hall or any other place where the records are lawfully stored any records for any purpose whatsoever, unless by competent and lawful court order, except by motion of the governing body.
(Code 1959, § 2-15)
Sec. 2-3-24. - Examination or inspection.
Any person desiring to see, inspect, examine, or copy any record made by any officer, agent, or employee of the city or any record of the governing body of the city shall make written request and present the same in person to the city clerk, and it shall be the duty of the record custodian at that time to designate a time certain within a period of seventy-two (72) hours from the date of such request when such records will be available to the applicant; provided that persons desiring to inspect records of the police and fire services departments which are open to public inspection shall present the inspection request directly to the appropriate department.
State Law reference— Inspection of public records, O.C.G.A., § 50-18-70 et seq.
Sec. 2-3-25. - Inspection, examination or copying, presence of custodian required, hours limited.
In order to preserve the authenticity and correctness of any and all records of the city no person shall be allowed to see, inspect, examine or copy the same unless within the presence of the records custodian of the city or some person designated by the records custodian, and such examination shall be made during regular business hours when city offices are kept open to the public.
Sec. 2-3-26. - Cost of certified copies.
When the city clerk is called upon from time to time to prepare and certify any copies of official records in his office, the costs of said preparation and certification shall be paid at the rate of one dollar ($1.00) per page.
(Code 1986, § 2-3-26; Ord. No. 88-015, 10-17-88)
Sec. 2-3-27. - "Records" construed.
The records referred to in this article shall include not only the minutes of the governing body, all contracts of the city, all ordinances and resolutions enacted by the governing body, all memoranda and records kept by the city clerk of every nature whatsoever, all records of the public services department, all records of every other department of the city, all records of the police and fire services departments, including municipal court records and police docket, but shall also include all other records kept and maintained by any officer, agent, servant or employee of the city concerning matters pertaining to the city except those records not open to public inspection by order of a court of this state or by law.
Sec. 2-3-28. - Retention of public records; destruction of obsolete records.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99, the city has adopted the Records Retention Schedule for Local Government Records as published by the Georgia Secretary of State's Department of Archives and History, and any such amendments or updates as may be made to such retention schedule for local government records.
(b)
All city records shall be maintained in accordance with approved retention schedules, in the most updated version of the retention schedule for local government records.
(c)
The support services director shall serve as the coordinator of the records management plan and is authorized to act for and on behalf of the city in directing and coordinating all records management matters under the Georgia Records Retention Act. In discharging the records management and records destruction duties, the director shall, whether personally or by delegation, maintain all records in accordance with the Georgia Secretary of State Retention Schedules for Local Government Records adopted by the mayor and council, and shall not destroy, allow, or direct any employee to destroy, any record except as provided on the aforesaid retention schedule.
(d)
Whenever any records are destroyed, a certificate of destruction shall be prepared and forwarded to the mayor and council which shall show the type of records destroyed, the approved retention schedule number, the dates covered, and the volume destroyed. These memoranda of destruction shall be kept by the support services director in a central location, and may be viewed by any member of the public as provided by law.
(Ord. No. 98-009, § 1, 6-1-98; Ord. No. 05-27, 5-25-2005)
State Law reference— Records management programs for local governments, O.C.G.A., § 50-18-99.
Sec. 2-3-29. - Records management committee.
(a)
Creation: A records management committee consisting of the following; director of support services, director of finance, director of public works, director of recreation and leisure services, director of planning, building and zoning, director of police, director of fire and city manager shall be established.
(b)
The committee shall be responsible for assisting the support services director in the development of policies and procedures governing the city's records management program; reviewing the performance of the program on an annual basis; proposing changes and improvements to the records retention policy if needed; planning, formulating and prescribing records disposition policies, systems, standards, and procedures; monitoring records retention schedules in compliance with the State of Georgia Records Retention Schedule for Local Government Records.
(c)
The committee will review all lists of records proposed to be destroyed, for pending open records requests, records pertinent to a pending law suits, or when a department head requests in writing to the records management committee that certain records be retained for a period longer than that established by ordinance.
(Ord. No. 05-35, § 1, 6-20-2005)
ARTICLE C. - BOARDS AND AUTHORITIES

Sec. 2-3-30. - Oath of office for board and authority members.
Prior to taking office, the oath of office shall be administered to each board or authority member of the city by the mayor as provided for in section 3.15(f) of the City Charter and shall, to the extent that it comports with federal and state law, including O.C.G.A. § 45-3-1, be as follows:
"I solemnly swear (or affirm) that I will support the Constitution of the United States and of the State of Georgia; that I will in all respects observe the provisions of the Charter and Ordinances of the City of Forest Park; that I will well and truly perform the duties as a ________________ _____________ Board Member; that I am not the holder of any unaccounted for public money due this state or any political subdivision or authority thereof; that I am not the holder of any office of trust under the government of the United States, any other state, or any foreign state which by the laws of the State of Georgia I am prohibited from holding; that I am otherwise qualified to hold said office according to the Constitution and laws of Georgia; and that I will to the utmost of my skill and ability endeavor to promote the interest and property of said city, without fear, favor, or affection. So help me God."
(Ord. No. 18-10, § 1 (Exh. A), 7-2-2018)
Sec. 2-3-31. - Residency affidavit.
(a)
Every board or authority member of the city shall annually execute an affidavit in a form approved by the city attorney that such board or authority member (1) is a resident of the city; (2) has maintained their residency in the city during their term of office; (3) has otherwise maintained all of the requirements for membership on such board or authority during their terms of office; and (4) reaffirming their oath of office.
(b)
Failure to provide the affidavit within five (5) business days of its request by the city shall be grounds for removal from any such board or authority.
(Ord. No. 18-10, § 1 (Exh. A), 7-2-2018)
