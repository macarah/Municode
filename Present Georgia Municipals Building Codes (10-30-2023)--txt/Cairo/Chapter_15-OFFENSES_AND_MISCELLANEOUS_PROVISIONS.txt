Sec. 15-1. - Aiding and abetting violation.
No person shall aid or abet another to violate any provision of this Code.
(Code 1965, § 20-1)
State Law reference— Parties to crime, OCGA § 16-2-20 et seq.
Sec. 15-2. - Conspiracy.
No two (2) or more persons shall conspire together or plot to violate any of the provisions of this Code.
(Code 1965, § 20-3)
State Law reference— Conspiracy, OCGA § 16-4-8.
Sec. 15-3. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
A person commits the crime of disorderly conduct, if, with intent to cause public inconvenience, annoyance or alarm, or recklessly creating a risk thereof, such person shall:
(1)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby any person is placed in fear of safety of his life, limb or health; or
(2)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged; or
(3)
Endanger the lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence, angry threats and abusive conduct; or
(4)
Cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another; or
(5)
Assemble or congregate with another or others for the purpose of causing, provoking or engaging in any fight or brawl; or
(6)
Jostle or roughly crowd or push any person in any public place; or
(7)
Collect in bodies or crowds for unlawful purposes as defined by the ordinances of the city; or
(8)
Assemble or congregate with another or others for the purpose or with the intent to engage in unlawful gaming; or
(9)
Frequent any public place with intent to take money from other persons by illegal and fraudulent schemes, tricks, artifices or devices; or
(10)
Assemble for the purpose of engaging in any fraudulent scheme, devise or trick to obtain any valuable thing in any place from any person in the city or who shall aid or abet therein; or
(11)
Accost or attempt to force his company upon any person, male or female, or attempt to pickup any person, male or female; or
(12)
Utter or use in the presence of another any bawdy, lewd, abusive or obscene language or make an obscene gesture; or
(13)
Frequent any place where gaming or the illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed or tolerated; or
(14)
Use "fighting words" directed towards any person or becomes outraged and thus creates a turmoil; or
(15)
Assemble or congregate for the purpose of trouncing on or accosting another; or
(16)
By act of violence interfere with the pursuit of the lawful occupation of another; or
(17)
Congregate with another or others on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and/or refuses to clear the public way when ordered by the city police or other lawful authority; or
(18)
Congregate with another or other persons in a public place and refuse to comply with the lawful order of the police to disburse; or
(19)
Operate a motor vehicle upon a parking area, public or private, vehicle access, or pedestrian walkway of any parking area and by sudden starting, stopping, or turning so as to endanger the person or property of another; or
(20)
Operate a motor vehicle upon any parking area, public or private, vehicle access or pedestrian walkway of any parking facility at a speed great enough to endanger the person or property of another; or
(21)
Operate a motor vehicle upon any pedestrian walkway or sidewalk, public or private in any manner; or
(22)
a.
Consume alcoholic beverages or possess or have under his control an open container of any alcoholic beverage in any public area including property of the city, state, or county by and through its elected or appointed political agencies or boards.
b.
Exception: This section shall not apply to the following:
1.
Restaurants with permitted sidewalk cafes, or permitted open-air cafes, that have been issued a valid alcohol consumption license as defined in chapter 4 of this Code; provided that all outdoor activities are contained within the permitted establishment boundaries, or
2.
Alcoholic beverages consumed at a publically owned or privately owned golf course, or
3.
At a city sanctioned or sponsored, or non-profit charitable, special event held within the boundaries of certain event districts designated by the city and described in chapter 4; subject to all rules and regulations of the city and state.
(23)
Operate a motor vehicle and have therein under his possession and/or control or the possession and/or control of any person within the automobile an open container of any type containing an alcoholic beverage; or
(24)
Be and appear in an intoxicated condition in any public place or within the curtilage of any private residence not his own other than by invitation of the owner or lawful occupant or within a place of business patronized by the public, such condition made manifest by boisterousness, indecent condition or act or by vulgar, profane, loud or unbecoming language; or
(25)
Aid, abet or encourage a minor to do any act which constitutes disorderly conduct or a breach of the peace.
(b)
A person commits the offense of public indecency when he or she performs any of the following acts in a place where a person should reasonably expect to be in view of the public:
(1)
An actual or simulated act of sexual intercourse or masturbation;
(2)
A lewd exposure of the sexual organs;
(3)
A lewd appearance in a state of partial or complete nudity;
(4)
A lewd caress or indecent fondling of the body of another person; or
(5)
Urination or defecation.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to appear in public wearing pants, shorts, or skirts more than three (3) inches below the top of the hips (crest of the ilium) exposing the skin or undergarments. For the purpose of this subsection, the term "undergarments" shall include any article of clothing worn under the outer garments and next to the skin. Any person(s) violating this subsection may be warned by the law enforcement officer to pull up their pants, shorts, or skirts, and if they comply, said warning shall be the penalty for violation of this subsection. Failure by said person(s) to comply with said warning shall result in a charge of "disorderly conduct;" and a citation shall be issued to the offender, and the person shall be subject to a penalty of not less than twenty-five dollars ($25.00) on the first offense and not more than two hundred dollars ($200.00) for each subsequent offense. Any violation for this offense shall not, in and of itself, be grounds for an arrest or for search of the person cited.
(Code 1965, § 20-4; Ord. No. 377, § 1, 7-23-90; Ord. No. 743, 11-12-12; Ord. No. 811, § 1, 2-11-19)
State Law reference— Offenses against public order, OCGA § 16-11-30 et seq.
Sec. 15-4. - Defacing city hall.
No person shall spit upon, or make pencil marks or other marks upon the floors, walls, staircases or other parts of the city hall or deface or damage in any other manner any part of such building.
(Code 1965, § 20-2)
State Law reference— Criminal damage to property, OCGA § 16-7-21.
Sec. 15-5. - Weapons; discharging.
(A)
No person shall negligently or purposely discharge any firearm, BB gun or slingshot within the city, except:
(1)
In necessary self defense.
(2)
A law enforcement officer in necessary performance of official duty.
(3)
For the purpose of target shooting or practice on a range operated by qualified personnel. "Qualified personnel" shall consist of a certified firearms safety instructor, rifle or pistol marksmanship instructor certified by the national rifle association, or a person designated by a rifle or pistol club, public or private school or military agency.
(4)
Target shooting on private premises with air or carbon dioxide-operated BB or with a pellet gun or slingshot, providing:
(a)
The target area is enclosed in such manner and with such materials that will stop the projectiles.
(b)
Such target shooting is supervised by an adult at all times.
(c)
Any safety precautions recommended by the police chief are complied with.
(5)
Where a permit has been issued by the police chief.
(B)
"Firearms," as used in this section, shall mean any device that expels a projectile by means of expending gases.
(Code 1965, §§ 20-5, 20-6)
State Law reference— Discharge of firearms, OCGA § 16-11-103 et seq.
Sec. 15-6. - Gambling.
No two (2) or more persons shall assemble at any place within the city for the purpose of playing and betting for money or other thing of value on any game played with cards, dice or balls, or for the purpose of playing and betting for money or other thing of value on any game whatever.
(Code 1965, § 20-7)
State Law reference— Gambling, OCGA § 16-12-20 et seq.
Sec. 15-7. - Lewd houses.
(a)
No person shall keep or maintain a lewd or disreputable house for the purpose of engaging in lewd or disreputable acts.
(b)
No person shall lodge or live in any such house.
(c)
In any prosecution for violation of this section, it shall be considered sufficient evidence that such place is a rendezvous for persons, either male or female, of notoriously bad, disreputable or lewd habits.
(Code 1965, §§ 20-8, 20-9)
State Law reference— Keeping place of prostitution, OCGA § 16-6-10; disorderly house, § 16-11-44.
Sec. 15-8. - Congregating on sidewalks.
No two (2) or more persons shall congregate on the sidewalks of the city so as to obstruct such sidewalks.
(Code 1965, § 28-3)
Sec. 15-9. - Loitering.
(a)
No boy or girl under the age of sixteen (16) years shall loiter or remain upon the streets of the city after 11:00 p.m. without good cause or necessity.
(b)
No person shall be in or remain in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for law-abiding individuals under circumstances that warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity.
Such circumstances that may be considered in determining whether alarm is warranted is the fact that a person takes flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer, refuses to identify himself, or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself or any object. Unless flight by the person or other circumstances make it impractical, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest for an offense under this section, afford the person an opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern that would otherwise be warranted by requesting the person to identify himself and explain his presence and conduct. No person shall be convicted of an offense under this section if the law enforcement officer failed to comply with the foregoing procedure or if it appears at trial that the explanation given by the person was true and would have dispelled the alarm or immediate concern.
(Code 1965, § 20-10; Ord. No. 329, § 1, 8-8-88)
Cross reference— Loitering in city parks and recreation areas, § 24-1.
State Law reference— Loitering, OCGA § 16-11-36.
Sec. 15-10. - Political activities on public streets and ways.
No person shall stand on any public street or way in the city for the purpose of distributing political campaign literature, campaign cards, or soliciting votes. Such act shall be an offense and upon conviction the defendant shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1965, § 28-6; Ord. No. 248, 8-25-80)
Sec. 15-11. - Parades—Definitions.
As used in section 15-12, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Assemble: The action of any group of persons who congregate on any public street, alley or sidewalk in the city for the purpose of displaying, advertising, promoting, exhibiting, advocating or otherwise attracting public attention to or for any cause or thing.
Parade: The action of any group of persons marching or promenading on and along any public street, alley or sidewalk in the city for the purpose of displaying, advertising, promoting, exhibiting, advocating or otherwise attracting public attention to or for any cause or thing.
(Code 1965, § 20-14)
Sec. 15-12. - Same—Permit required; participation in unlawful parade.
(a)
No two (2) or more persons shall parade or assemble or congregate on any public street, alley or sidewalk in the city for a purpose set out in section 15-11 unless the governing body shall have previously approved and authorized such parade or assembly.
(b)
Any person participating in or attending any parade or assembly which has not been so authorized and approved shall be considered a part of the group constituting such parade or assembly, and shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1965, § 20-15)
State Law reference— Unlawful assembly, OCGA § 16-11-33; refusal to disperse, § 16-10-30.
Sec. 15-13. - Same—Permit application.
Any two (2) or more persons proposing to parade or assemble on any of the public streets, alleys or sidewalks of the city for any of the purposes defined in section 15-11 shall file an application with the governing body setting out the time, place and purpose of such parade or assembly. Any application for a permit hereunder must be presented and action taken thereon at the next regular meeting of the governing body.
(Code 1965, § 20-16; Ord. No. 172, § 1, 5-24-71)
Sec. 15-14. - Same—Wearing masks.
(a)
No two (2) or more persons shall parade upon the streets or within the city wearing masks or any other disguise or device whereby the identity of such persons is obscured or hidden or the license plate of automobiles are covered.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to parades at Halloween, Christmas, and other like traditional celebrations wherein it is the custom to wear masks, provided permission to do so is first obtained from the governing body.
(Code 1965, §§ 20-12, 20-13)
State Law reference— Wearing masks, OCGA § 16-11-38.
Sec. 15-15. - Entering and disturbing premises.
(a)
No person shall enter the premises of another for the purpose of creating or provoking a disturbance or breach of the peace.
(b)
In the interpretation and enforcement of this section, when any person enters upon the premises of another for a certain purpose and the fulfillment of accomplishment of such purpose, or attempt thereof, gives rise to, causes or provokes a disturbance or breach of peace, it shall be presumed that the entering upon the premises was for the purpose of creating or provoking such disturbance or breach of the peace.
(Code 1965, §§ 20-22, 20-23)
Sec. 15-16. - Energy load management program; disconnecting radio switches.
(a)
No person, including specifically the following: Owner, tenant, occupant, electrician, air conditioner mechanic or maintenance person or any officer, agent or employee of any of the above, shall disconnect the radio switch on the control thermostat circuit on air conditioners that has been installed by the city as a part of the load management program, except by written authorization of the city manager or the designated agent of the city manager.
(b)
No person shall claim or receive a refund from the city paid as incentive for cooperation with the load management program after the radio switch on the control thermostat circuit on air conditioners has been disconnected.
(c)
The disconnection of said radio switch by any person, as above defined or identified in subsection (a) is an offense and upon conviction the defendant shall be punished as provided in section 1-8, and each week that said switch is disconnected shall be a new offense.
(d)
The claiming or receiving of the refund set forth in subsection (b) above by any person shall be an offense and upon conviction the defendant shall be punished as provided in section 1-8, and each such claim for a refund or receipt of said refund shall be a new offense.
(Code 1965, § 20-25; Ord. No. 247, 7-28-80)
Sec. 15-17. - Possession of marijuana.
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess one ounce, or less, of marijuana within the corporate limits of the City of Cairo, Georgia.
(a)
Any person violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances, City of Cairo, Georgia.
(b)
The jurisdiction of the municipal court of the City of Cairo shall be concurrent with the jurisdiction of any other court within the county having jurisdiction to try and dispose of such cases.
(c)
Any fines and forfeitures arising from the prosecution of such cases shall be retained by the municipality and shall be paid into the treasury of such municipality.
(d)
Any defendant charged with possession of an ounce or less of marijuana in a municipal court of the City of Cairo shall be entitled on request to have the case against him transferred to the court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county wherein the alleged offense occurred.
(Ord. No. 284, § 1, 2-25-85)
Sec. 15-18. - Theft by shoplifting.
(a)
No person acting alone or in concert with another person, with the intent of appropriating merchandise to his own use without paying for same or to deprive the owner of possession thereof or of the value thereof, in whole or in part, [shall commit the offense of shoplifting] by doing any of the following:
(1)
Concealing or taking possession of the goods or merchandise of any store or retail establishment;
(2)
Altering the price tag or other price marking on goods or merchandise of any store or retail establishment;
(3)
Transferring the goods or merchandise of any store or retail establishment from one container to another;
(4)
Interchanging the label or price tag from one item of merchandise with a label or price tag for another item of merchandise; or
(5)
Wrongfully causing the amount paid to be less than the merchant's stated price for the merchandise.
(b)
A person convicted of this offense shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances, City of Cairo, Georgia.
(c)
The jurisdiction of the municipal court of the City of Cairo shall be concurrent with the jurisdiction of any other court within the county having jurisdiction to try and dispose of such cases.
(d)
Any fines and forfeitures arising from the prosecution of such cases shall be retained by the municipality and shall be paid into the treasury of such municipality.
(e)
Any defendant charged with the crime of theft by shoplifting in the municipal court of the City of Cairo shall be entitled on request to have the case against him transferred to the court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county wherein the alleged offense occurred.
(Ord. No. 331, § 1, 8-8-88)
Sec. 15-19. - Simple assault.
(a)
No person shall commit the offense of simple assault which shall be defined as an attempt to commit a violent injury to the person of another or committing an act which places another in reasonable apprehension of immediately receiving a violent injury.
(b)
A defendant charged with simple assault in the Municipal Court of the City of Cairo shall be entitled upon request to have the case against the defendant transferred to a court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county where the alleged offense occurred.
(c)
Any person violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Cairo.
(Ord. No. 693, § 1, 6-9-08)
Sec. 15-20. - Simple battery.
(a)
No person shall commit the offense of simple battery which shall be defined as:
(1)
An intentional physical contact of an insulting and provoking nature to another person;
(2)
Intentional physical harm to another; or
(3)
Intentionally causing visible harm to another which may include, but is not limited to, substantially blackened eyes, swollen lips or other facial or body parts or substantial bruises to body parts.
(b)
A defendant charged with simple battery in the Municipal Court of the City of Cairo shall be entitled upon request to have the case against the defendant transferred to a court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county where the alleged offense occurred.
(c)
Any person violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Cairo.
(Ord. No. 693, § 2, 6-9-08)
Sec. 15-21. - Criminal trespass.
(a)
No person shall commit the offense of criminal trespass. Criminal trespass shall be defined as:
(1)
Intentionally damaging any property in the amount of five hundred dollars ($500.00) or less of another without consent of that person or knowingly and maliciously interfering with the possession or use of the property of another person without the consent of that person;
(2)
Knowingly and without authority entering upon the land or premises of another person or any part of a vehicle, watercraft or aircraft, of another person for an unlawful purpose;
(3)
Entering upon the land or premises of another person or any part of any vehicle, watercraft or aircraft, after receiving prior to such entry, notice from the owner, rightful occupant or upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner, that such entry is forbidden; or
(4)
Remaining upon the land or premises of another or within any vehicle, watercraft or aircraft, after receiving notice from the rightful owner, rightful occupant or upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant, to depart.
(b)
A defendant charged with criminal trespass in the Municipal Court of the City of Cairo shall be entitled upon request to have the case against the defendant transferred to a court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county where the alleged offense occurred.
(c)
Any person violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Cairo.
(Ord. No. 693, § 3, 6-9-08)
Sec. 15-22. - Theft by taking.
(a)
No person shall unlawfully take or be in unlawful possession thereof or unlawfully appropriate any property in the amount of five hundred dollars ($500.00) or less of another with intention of depriving that person of the property, regardless of the manner in which the property is taken or appropriated.
(b)
A defendant charged with theft by taking in the Municipal Court of the City of Cairo shall be entitled upon request to have the case against the defendant transferred to a court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county where the alleged offense occurred.
(c)
Any person violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Cairo.
(Ord. No. 693, § 4, 6-9-08)
