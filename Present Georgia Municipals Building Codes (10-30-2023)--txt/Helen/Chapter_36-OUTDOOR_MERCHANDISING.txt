Chapter 36 - OUTDOOR MERCHANDISING

Sec. 36-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall mean:
Goods mean objects or materials for sale.
Merchandise means all goods or services for sale by the business or goods or services used to draw attention to the business, or support structures used to display these.
Perimeter means the outermost walls of the business.
Premises means the building, or portion of the building, which occupies the business.
Public walkway means any sidewalk, alley, corridor, or path open to pedestrian foot traffic.
Shop means the leased or owned area occupying a place of business.
(Ord. No. 05-09-01, 9-20-05)
Sec. 36-2. - Application.
This article applies to all businesses, active or inactive, within the city limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 05-09-01, 9-20-05)
Sec. 36-3. - Regulation, outdoor display for merchandise prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any business to display goods or merchandise outside. This means standing on the ground outside the shop, hung or fastened to the exterior wall, or hung or fastened to any door which, whether in the open or closed position, puts the goods or merchandise on any face of the door which is then outside the perimeter, except for goods or merchandise contemplated by section 34-1114, or which is of such a nature that it ordinarily remains out of doors after business hours such as produce, firewood, fertilizers, outdoor furniture, or garden plants. Such goods or merchandise shall in no way obstruct a public walkway except as provided in section 34-1114.
(Ord. No. 05-09-01, 9-20-05)
Sec. 36-4. - Open decks.
Should an open deck be used to display merchandise, the perimeter of the deck must have a rail constructed with a material and style consistent with the architectural and design requirements of the City Code, and permitted by the planning design and review board. Any merchandise displayed thereon is limited to goods or merchandise which is of such a nature that it ordinarily remains out of doors after business hours such as produce, firewood, fertilizers, outdoor furniture, or garden plants.
(Ord. No. 05-09-01, 9-20-05)
Sec. 36-5. - Exceptions.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, and in order to provide opportunities for seasonal closeout and other sales, for a ten-day period beginning on the last Saturday of March, June, September, and December, each shop may display outdoors, on private property, without restrictions imposed by this chapter, goods and merchandise usually and customarily offered for sale by such shop.
(Ord. No. 05-09-01, 9-20-05)
Sec. 36-6. - Penalty.
Any person who violates the provisions of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction shall be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 05-09-01, 9-20-05)
