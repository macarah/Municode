Sec. 42-3. - Camping prohibited within city limits.
(a)
Definitions.
Camp or camping means to use property within the city for the purposes of habitation, as evidenced by the use of camp facilities or paraphernalia.
Camp facilities include, but are not limited to, temporary shelters, tents, huts, lean-tos, shacks, trailer campers, motor homes or any other type vehicle, trailer, or any other type shelter or structure.
Camp paraphernalia includes, but is not limited to, tarpaulins, cots, beds, sleeping bags, blankets, mattresses, hammocks, lanterns, stove, or other cooking facilities and similar equipment.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to camp within the municipal limits of the City of Helen. This shall exclude security personnel for special events approved by the planning design and review board and the city commission. It shall also exclude residents of the City of Helen or their guest located in a residential zoning who are camping on their property overnight for recreational purposes.
(Code 1987, §§ 32-101, 32-102; Ord. No. 13-07-02, 7-16-13)

(a)
A person commits the offense of public indecency when he or she performs any of the following acts in a public place:
(1)
An act of sexual intercourse;
(2)
A lewd exposure of the sexual organs;
(3)
A lewd appearance in a state of partial or complete nudity; or

A lewd caress or indecent fondling of the body of another person.
(b)

(c)

(a)

Spits;
(2)
Discards litter, except into receptacles designated for that purpose;

Sec. 42-22. - Containers of certain types prohibited in Chattahoochee River.
modified
(a)
The purpose and intent of this section is to protect the cleanliness and natural beauty of the Chattahoochee River, and to reduce the problem of trash accumulation in the Chattahoochee River, while at the same time allowing visitors and residents of the city to enjoy the tubing and other recreational activities on the river within the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use or possess any cooler, backpacks or bags, or other types of similar container either in, or upon, the Chattahoochee River and within the city limits. Such prohibited containers shall include, but are not limited to, those containers made of glass, plastic, paper, or metal. Specifically, all cups, bottles and cans, backpacks, bags or other types of containers are prohibited in or upon the river, other than those designated by the below exceptions.
(1)
An exception to subsection (b) shall be made for one drink container, not to exceed 32 ounces, that is of a non-disposable nature, and contains a loop or strap as an integral part of the container itself, such that the drink container may be affixed to either the person or to the intertube itself. Alternatively, an exception to subsection (b) shall be made for one disposable plastic water bottle, not to exceed 32 ounces. It is recommended that in conjunction with the use of the disposable plastic water bottle, a commercially produced hook or strap is utilized with it, such that the disposable bottle may be affixed to either the person or to the intertube itself. An illustrative display chart of permitted containers of such type, as approved by the city commission, will be maintained at Helen City Hall and such a sign of the display chart of permitted containers will be erected at the location of each tubing company within the city.
(2)
An exception to subsection (b) shall also be made for one commercially produced waterproof pouch, not to exceed 10" x 15" in size, for tubing guests to utilize to carry such necessary items as keys, cameras, medical supplies, or other items which require a waterproof container. Said pouch should be capable of being affixed to the person or the intertube by a strap, hook or other means. An illustrative display chart of permitted containers of such type, as approved by the city commission, will be maintained at Helen City Hall and such a sign of the display chart of permitted containers will be erected at the location of each tubing company within the city.
(Ord. No. 06-07-02, 8-1-06; Ord. No. 06-11-01, 12-19-06; Ord. No. 08-06-03, 7-15-08; Ord. No. 19-02-01 , 3-12-19)

DIVISION 2. - NOISE

Sec. 42-41. - Prohibited generally.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully make, continue, or cause to be made or continued any excessive, unnecessary or unusually loud noise which disturbs the peace or quiet of any neighborhood or which causes discomfort or annoyance to any reasonable person of normal sensitiveness residing within the city limits.
(Code 1987, § 13-101)
Sec. 42-42. - Unlawful noises enumerated.
The following acts are declared to be a loud, disturbing and unnecessary noise in violation of this article, but this enumeration shall not be deemed to be exhaustive:
(1)
Motor vehicle horns. The sounding of any horn on any automobile, motorcycle, or other motor vehicle on any street or public place of the city except as a warning signal.
(2)
Radios, television sets, similar devices. The using, operating or permitting to be played, used or operated, any radio receiving set, musical instrument, phonograph, television set or other machine or device for the production or reproduction of sound between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. in such manner as to disturb the peace, quiet, and comfort of neighboring residents, unless a permit for such sound amplification has been obtained from the manager or chief of police.
(3)
Loudspeakers and amplifiers. The using or operating of any loudspeaker, or sound-amplifying, or broadcasting device mounted upon any vehicle, upon any building, or set outside upon the premises of any property within the city, for the purpose of broadcasting or advertising any information about any business or activity, amplifying the sound of any entertainment thereon or therein, or for any other purpose, when, at a distance of 50 feet from the source of any such broadcast or amplification, the noise level, six (6) feet above the ground level, exceeds 70 decibels.
(4)
Equipment and activities. The operating of any equipment for the loading or unloading of solid waste dumpsters (or any similar contrivance), either private or commercial, and the act of loading or unloading of solid waste dumpsters (or any similar contrivance), either private or commercial, and the act of connecting road tractors to freight trailers, and the operating of any equipment or the performing of any outside construction or repair work on buildings, structures, roads, or projects within the city between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
(5)
Exhausts. The discharging into the open air of the exhaust of any internal combustion engine, motor boat, or motor vehicle except through a muffler or other device which will effectively prevent loud or explosive noises therefrom.
(6)
Animals and birds. The keeping of any animal or bird which by frequent or continuous barking, chirping or other means of communication disturbs the comfort or repose of the residents of any residential neighborhood.
(7)
Vehicle repair in residential areas. The repairing, rebuilding, or testing of any motor vehicle between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. within any residential area in such a manner as to disturb the peace, quiet and comfort of the residents of the area.
(8)
Schools, courts, churches, hospitals. The creating of any excessive noise on any street adjacent to any school, institution of learning, church or court while the same are in use, or adjacent to any hospital, which unreasonably interferes with the workings of such institution, or which disturbs or unduly annoys patients in the hospital, provided conspicuous signs are displayed in such streets indicating that the same is a school, hospital or court street.
(9)
Hawkers and peddlers. The selling of anything by outcry within the city, except at licensed sporting events, parades, fairs, circuses and other similarly licensed public entertainment events.
(10)
Drums. The using of any drum or other instrument or device for the purpose of attracting attention by the creation of noise within the city, unless a permit for such use has been obtained from the city manager or chief of police.
(Code 1987, § 13-102; Ord. No. 97-09-01, 10-21-97; Ord. No. 97-10-01, 11-8-97)
Sec. 42-43. - Exemptions.
The following uses and activities shall be exempt from the noise regulations set forth in this article:
(1)
Noises of safety signals and warning devices.
(2)
Noises resulting from any authorized emergency vehicle, when responding to an emergency call or acting in time of emergency.
(3)
Noises resulting from emergency work, to be construed as work made necessary to restore property to a safe condition following a public calamity, or work required to protect persons or property from an imminent exposure to danger.
(Code 1987, § 13-103)
Sec. 42-44. - Injunctions.
The operation or maintenance of any device, vehicle or machinery in violation of any provision of this article which causes discomfort or annoyance to reasonable persons of normal sensitiveness or which endangers the comfort, repose, health or peace of residents of this city shall be deemed, and is declared to be, a public nuisance, and may be subject to abatement summarily by a restraining order or injunction issued by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(Code 1987, § 13-105)
Sec. 42-45. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person violating any provision of this article shall be punished as provided in chapter 1, section 1-7, of this Code.
(b)
Each day of violation of any provision of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 97-09-01, 10-21-97)
DIVISION 3. - CURFEW FOR MINORS

Sec. 42-46. - Short title and definitions.
(a)
Short title. This Division 3 of Article II shall be known and recited as "The Curfew for Minors Ordinance."
(b)
Definitions. The following words and terms, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except when the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Curfew hours are the hours between 12:00 midnight of any day and 6:00 a.m. on the following day.
Establishment is any privately owned place of business operating for a profit to which the public is invited, including, but not limited to, any place of amusement or entertainment.
Minor is a person who has not yet reached his or her 18th birthday.
Parent is the lawful father or mother of a minor, and for purposes of this division, a legal guardian or other adult person having responsibility for the lawful care and custody of the minor.
Public place is defined as any place to which the public or a substantial group of the public has access, and includes streets, alleys, highways, and the common areas of schools, hospitals, apartment houses, condominiums, common properties, office buildings, transportation facilities, open business establishments, municipal, state, and federal buildings and grounds, and for purposes of this division, includes privately owned vehicles, carriages, and any mode of public transportation while upon the streets, roads, alleys, and other public ways in the city.
(Ord. No. 95-02-01, 7-18-95)
Sec. 42-47. - Purpose.
The purpose of this division is to promote juvenile safety and well being, while reducing juvenile crimes, juvenile violence, and juvenile victimization.
(Ord. No. 95-02-01, 7-18-95)
Sec. 42-48. - Penalty.
Anyone found guilty of violating any provision of this division shall be punished as provided in Section 1-7 of the Code of the City of Helen and/or as otherwise provided for juveniles and juvenile proceedings allowable under applicable sections of the laws of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 95-02-01, 7-18-95)
Sec. 42-49. - Violation.
(a)
Any minor who remains in a public place or on the premises of any establishment as herein defined during curfew hours, and whose activities are not excepted or exempted from coverage as enumerated in section 42-50, shall be in violation of this division.
(b)
A parent, guardian, or other adult having responsibility for the lawful care and custody of a minor, shall be in violation of this division if he or she or they knowingly permit, or by insufficient control, allow a minor or minors in his, her, or their charge to remain on a public place or on the premises of any establishment herein defined during curfew hours.
(c)
Any business owner or licensee or authorized agent of a business owner or licensee who allows a minor to remain on the premises of a defined place or establishment during curfew hours shall be in violation of this division except as otherwise set out hereinafter in section 42-50(b) thereof.
(Ord. No. 95-02-01, 7-18-95)
Sec. 42-50. - Exceptions and exemptions enumerated.
(a)
This division shall not apply to any person under 18 years of age who:
(1)
Is or has been emancipated by marriage;
(2)
Is accompanied by his/her parent, parents, legal guardian or other adult having responsibility for lawful custody and care of the minor;
(3)
Is engaged in employment or employment-related activities;
(4)
Is engaged in interstate or intrastate travel;
(5)
Is attending school, a religious or civic organization function that is supervised;
(6)
Is attending or traveling directly to or from an activity involving the exercise of First Amendment rights of free speech, freedom of assembly, or free exercise of religion;
(7)
Is in a public place because of an emergency;
(8)
Is on the sidewalk in front of his or her home or an adjacent dwelling; and
(9)
Is on an errand directed by his or her parent, parents, legal guardian or other adult person having responsibility for the lawful care and custody of the minor.
(b)
Business owners also commit a violation of this division if they allow a minor to remain on the premises of an establishment defined herein during curfew hours. It shall be a defense or an exception for the business owner if the business owner, the agent, or employee of the business owner, notified the city police about the minor remaining on the business premises after he or she had been requested to leave.
(Ord. No. 95-02-01, 7-18-95)
Sec. 42-51. - Conflicts.
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby expressly repealed.
(Ord. No. 95-02-01, 7-18-95)
