ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 34-26. - Enforcement officer.
(a)
Unless otherwise stated, the provisions of this chapter shall be enforced by the city manager or his designees. All references in this chapter to an enforcement officer are references to the city manager or his designees in the absence of provisions to the contrary.
(b)
The enforcement officer shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
To inspect all buildings or structures being erected or altered as frequently as may be necessary to ensure compliance with the city ordinances or codes.
(2)
To make or cause to be made, at any reasonable hour, entries into buildings or premises where the work of altering, repairing or constructing is going on, for the purpose of making inspections.
(3)
To issue written stop work orders on construction, alteration or repair of buildings in the city when such work is being done in violation of any provision of this chapter.
(Code 1987, § 3-705)

DIVISION 2. - PLANNING AND DESIGN REVIEW BOARD[2]

Sec. 34-51. - Established.
There is a planning and design review board.
Sec. 34-52. - Membership.
(a)
The planning and design review board shall be comprised of seven members appointed by the city commission. Members of the board must be residents of the city, or a resident and registered voter of the county who either owns property within the city and has done so for one year or more, or has held a city business license and has done so for two years or more, or is an officer of a corporation which either owns property within the city and has done so for one year or more, or holds a city business license and has done so for two years or more.
(b)
Except for the initial term, which shall be one year for the first two members, the terms for the members shall be for two years each. Members may be reappointed.
(c)
Vacancies shall be filled by appointments for unexpired terms only and in the same manner as for original appointments.
(d)
Any member of the planning and design review board may be removed by the city commission for cause after written notice and a public hearing.
(e)
All members of the planning and design review board shall receive compensation in an amount to be determined by the city commission.
(f)
No member of the board may hold any other public office or position within the city.
(Ord. No. 92-5, 9-15-92; Ord. No. 94-03-01, § 7(1), 7-19-94)
Sec. 34-53. - Chairperson.
The municipal planning and design review board shall elect one of its members to serve as chairperson for a period of one year. The chairperson may serve in this position for more than one term. The vice-chairperson shall be a member of the board elected by the board and shall serve for a period of one year. The vice-chairperson may serve in this position for more than one term.
(Ord. No. 92-5, 9-15-92)
Sec. 34-54. - Secretary.
The city manager or his designee shall act as the secretary of the planning and design review board.
(Ord. No. 92-5, 9-15-92)
Sec. 34-55. - Meetings and rules.
(a)
The planning and design review board shall meet at least once each month at the call of the chairperson and at such other times as the chairperson or majority of members shall determine.
(b)
All meetings shall be convened in the city commission chambers. The date, time and location of the monthly meeting shall be at the same time, date and location. Any change shall be publicized at the city hall. Notice of a change in the meeting shall be done at least 15 days in advance.
(c)
Written notice of a called meeting shall be given to the members 48 hours prior to the meeting. A public notice of such a meeting will be posted at city hall 48 hours prior to the meeting. The board may only act on old business at a called meeting.
(d)
The board may take action only when a quorum is present. A quorum is three members.
(e)
The board shall keep minutes of its proceedings showing the absent vote or the failure to vote of each member. Records shall be kept of the board's examination and other official actions.
(f)
The minutes and records shall be on file in city hall and shall be open for public inspection.
(g)
The board shall adopt Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised as a guideline for the conducting and administration of its meetings. The board may adopt such other rules as may be necessary.
(h)
Each of the members of the board shall have one vote on each issue that comes before the board except that the chairperson shall only exercise his vote for the purpose of breaking a tie.
Sec. 34-56. - Staff advisor.
The city manager or his designee shall serve in an advisory capacity to the planning and design review board. In this capacity, he may provide information, comments or other data pertinent to a particular issue; however, he shall not have any voting powers.
(Ord. No. 92-5, 9-15-92; Ord. No. 94-03-01, § 7(2), 7-19-94)
Sec. 34-57. - Powers and duties.
(a)
The planning and design review board shall make careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and prepare plans for physical, social, and economic growth in an effort to promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, or general welfare of the municipality. In carrying out its objectives the planning and design review board shall have the following specific powers and duties:
(1)
To prepare a master plan or parts thereof for the development of the city;
(2)
To prepare and recommend for adoption a land development ordinance and map for the city;
(3)
To prepare and recommend for adoption a soil erosion and sediment control ordinance for the city;
(4)
To prepare and recommend for adoption a sign ordinance for the city;
(5)
To prepare and recommend for adoption regulations for the subdivision of land within the city and to administer the regulations that may be adopted;
(6)
To prepare and recommend for adoption a plat or plats or an official map showing the exact location of the boundary lines of existing, proposed, extended, unlined or narrowed streets, public open spaces, or public building sites and provide for the regulation of construction of buildings or other structures within such lines; and
(7)
To cooperate with federal, state, or local public or quasi-public agencies.
(b)
The planning and design review board shall act as an advisory council to the city commission on any and all matters of planning and land development.
(c)
The board shall make recommendations to the city commission on zoning issues and changes, conditional uses, subdivision approvals, future land use, and regulations pertaining to the use and development of land and buildings.
(d)
The board shall make recommendations to the city commission on placing conditions on property that would fall within the conditional use area of the use provisions of article III at the time of development.
(e)
The board shall review, approve or disapprove all building plans for compliance with design standards in article IV.
(f)
The board shall review all sign applications for compliance with article VII.
(Ord. No. 92-5, 9-15-92)
Secs. 34-58—34-75. - Reserved.

Sec. 34-76. - Established.
There is a board of appeals.
Sec. 34-77. - Membership.
(a)
The board of appeals shall consist of five members, appointed by the city commission. Members of the board of appeals, must be residents of the city, or a resident and registered voter of the county who either owns property within the city and has done so for one year or more, or has held a city business license and has done so for two years or more, or is an officer of a corporation which either owns property within the city and has done so for one year or more, or holds a city business license and has done so for two years or more.
(b)
Except for the initial term, which shall be for one year for the first three members, the terms for the members shall be for two years each.
(c)
Members may be reappointed.
(d)
Vacancies shall be filled by appointment, for unexpired terms only, and in the same manner as the original appointment.
(e)
No member of the board of appeals may hold any other public office or position in the city.
(f)
Any member may be removed by the city commission for cause after written notice and a public hearing.
(g)
All members shall receive compensation in an amount to be determined by the city commission.
(h)
The board shall elect one of its members to serve as chairperson for one year.
(i)
The chairperson may serve in this position for more than one term.
(j)
The enforcement officer shall serve as the secretary of the board of appeals.
(Ord. No. 90-3, § 2, 6-19-90; Ord. No. 90-9, 1-22-91; Ord. No. 14-03-01, 4-1-14 )
Sec. 34-78. - Duties and powers.
The board of appeals shall have the following powers:
(1)
To hear and decide when an official allegation is made that there is an error in any order, requirement, decision, or determination made by an administrative official in the enforcement of this chapter.
(2)
To hear and decide variances to the terms of this chapter except as to the use of property upon which such board is required to pass under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 90-3, § 2(D)(1), 6-19-90)
Sec. 34-79. - Rules of procedure.
(a)
The board of appeals shall meet at the call of the chairperson or at the call of a majority of its members.
(b)
The date, time and location of any meeting of the board of appeals shall be publicized at the city hall at least 15 days in advance of the date of the meeting.
(c)
The board shall keep minutes of its proceedings, showing the absent vote or the failure to vote of each member, and records shall be kept of the board's examination and other official actions.
(d)
The minutes and records shall be on file in the offices of the city hall, and shall be open for public inspection.
(Ord. No. 90-3, § 2(D)(4), 6-19-90)
Secs. 34-80—34-100. - Reserved.
