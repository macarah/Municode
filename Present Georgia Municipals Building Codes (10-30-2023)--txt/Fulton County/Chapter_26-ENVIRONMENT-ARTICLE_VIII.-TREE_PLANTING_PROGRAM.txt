Sec. 26-471. - Creation.
There is hereby created a Fulton County Tree Planting Program.
(00-1458, § 1, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 1, 2-1-06)
Sec. 26-472. - Definitions.
For purposes of this article, the following terms shall have the following meanings:
Capital project means any project or work involving the construction, acquisition, or renovation of any building, structure, road, sidewalk, or other facility owned or to be acquired by the county, where such project is funded through the General Fund—Cash Only Projects portion of the Fulton County budget (i.e., "Fund 500" projects).
Construction costs means the total costs of acquiring and constructing a capital project including, but not limited to, legal, architectural, engineering and other professional fees, and site work.
Commission district means one of the five geographic areas corresponding to the boundaries of the county commission districts Three, Four, Five, Six or Seven.
Public places means buildings, real property, or other facilities located within the boundaries of the county and owned by the county or another governmental entity.
Renovation means any construction, work, or cosmetic change to existing public places costing over $100,000.00 in labor and/or supplies, excluding regular maintenance of facilities such as painting.
(00-1458, § 2, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 2, 2-1-06)
Sec. 26-473. - Contracts.
(a)
No contract shall be approved or executed by the county for any capital project unless at least one percent of the construction costs for such capital project is used for the purchase, installation/planting, and/or maintenance of trees in public places, which amounts are referred to as tree funds.
(b)
No contract for a capital project shall be presented for approval by the county unless the department of environment and community development certifies that the contract complies with the provisions of this article.
(00-1458, § 3, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 3, 2-1-06)
Sec. 26-474. - Use of tree funds.
The tree fund portion of each contract referred to in section 26-473 shall be placed into one or more trust funds to be established and administered by the county finance department. Tree funds shall be equally allocated to each commission district. Such funds shall be used to plant, install, and/or maintain trees and other landscaping (including labor, permitting and/or other associated expenses) on the site of a capital project or at other public places in the commission district, including:
(1)
All county, publicly owned and maintained properties (public rights-of-way, medians, sidewalks, buildings, real properties, facilities, major collector and arterial roads, etc.);
(2)
Conservation areas dedicated to and maintained by the county;
(3)
Greenspace areas owned and maintained by the county.
No tree funds shall be expended, and no trees shall be planted, with respect to property owned by a governmental entity other than the county unless and until an intergovernmental agreement between the county and said other governmental entity has been duly approved and executed by the governing bodies of both governments.
(00-1458, § 4, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 4, 2-1-06)
Sec. 26-475. - Administration.
The county department of environment and community development, development services division, shall develop recommendations regarding proposed sites in each commission district for planting of trees using tree funds. Such recommendations shall be submitted to the board of commissioners for final approval or rejection.
(00-1458, § 5, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 5, 2-1-06)
Sec. 26-476. - Standards.
Trees planted pursuant to the Fulton County Tree Planting Program shall have a minimum three-inch caliper and shall be of a species of hardwoods chosen from an approved list to be prepared by the county arborist. Placement of any trees adjacent to or within road rights-of-way shall be subject to the approval of the county traffic engineer.
(00-1458, § 6, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 6, 2-1-06)
Sec. 26-477. - Voluntary contributions.
Private landowners and communities shall be allowed to make monetary donations to the tree fund.
(00-1458, § 7, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 7, 2-1-06)
Sec. 26-478. - Program evaluation.
The county department of environment and community development and finance department shall submit an annual evaluation report of the tree planting program, including the location and number of trees planted and amount of funds expended in each commission district. Without limiting the authority of the board of commissioners, environment and community development will reevaluate the tree planting program at the conclusion of the 2008 budget year.
(00-1458, § 8, 12-6-00; Res. No. 06-0157, § 8, 2-1-06)
Secs. 26-479—26-500. - Reserved.
