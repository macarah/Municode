ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Riding horses, mules, ponies or other animals on sidewalks, roadways or rights-of-way; penalty for violation of section.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to ride horses, mules, ponies and any other animal upon the sidewalks, roadways or rights-of-way in the city. The city may make exceptions to the preceding sentence for permitted parades.
(b)
Violation of this article shall be punishable, upon conviction, as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. of 5-20-1986(2))
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.
Sec. 10-2. - Keeping certain animals; penalty for violation of section.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to have or keep, to pasture, or confine within the corporate limits of the city any horses, mules, donkeys, burros, ponies, cows, calves, sheep or goats.
(b)
It shall also be unlawful for any person to have or keep within the corporate limits of the city any fowl such as chickens, ducks, turkeys, geese, guinea fowl, pea fowl, peacocks, pigeons, doves, quail or any birds of prey.
(c)
Any person found to be in violation of this section shall be punished, upon conviction, according to section 1-11.
(Ord. of 3-20-1978; Res. of 1-16-2007)
Sec. 10-3. - Tethering.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any dog or anyone having a dog in his possession and control to restrain or anchor a dog for an excessive amount of time to a stationary object by means of a tether, chain, cable, rope or cord. Employees of the animal control division maintain the right to interpret and enforce this section.
(b)
As a secondary means of restraint to proper enclosure, a dog may be attached to a running cable line or trolley system provided that:
(1)
Only one dog may be attached to each running cable line or trolley system;
(2)
Tethers and cables attaching the dog to the running cable line or trolley system must be made of a substance which cannot be chewed by the dog and shall not weigh more than five percent of the body weight of the dog tethered;
(3)
A running cable line or trolley system must have a swivel installed at each end and be attached to a stationary object that cannot be moved by the dog;
(4)
The running cable line or trolley system must be at least ten feet in length and mounted at least four feet and no more than seven feet above ground level;
(5)
The length of the tether from the running cable line or trolley system to the dog's collar should allow access to the maximum available exercise and allow the dog free access to food, water and shelter;
(6)
Be attached to a properly fitted harness or collar not used for the display of a current rabies tag and other identification; and with enough room between the collar and the dog's throat through which two fingers may fit. Choke collars and pinch collars are prohibited for the purpose of tethering a dog to a running cable line or trolley system; and
(7)
Be tethered at sufficient distance from any other objects to prohibit the tangling of the cable, from extending over an object or an edge that could result in injury or strangulation of the dog and be of sufficient distance from any fence so as to prohibit the dog access to the fence.
(Ord. No. 2011-11-02, 11-15-2011)

ARTICLE II. - DANGEROUS DOGS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Animal bites, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-4; Dangerous Dog Control Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.; liability of owner or keeper of vicious or dangerous animal for injuries caused by animal, O.C.G.A. § 51-2-7.

Sec. 10-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dangerous dogs means any dogs who are on record with any law enforcement agency, animal control agency, local, state or federal court or a dog control officer for having bitten or endangering a human being without provocation, except dogs used by law enforcement officers, or dogs who attack in response to willful trespass or other tort. Dogs who attack in response to torment, abuse or assault shall not be included in this section within the meaning of a dangerous dog.
Dog control officer means a person appointed by the county board of commissioners by and through the director of the county environmental department, along with all police officers of the city, who are given the authority and responsibility for making whatever investigations or inquiries are necessary to locate and identify classified dogs who are potentially dangerous or dangerous.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that has the potential for biting human beings on public or private property or chasing human beings in a vicious manner on public grounds without provocation.
(Ord. of 2-6-1989, § 4.1; Amend. of 4-4-2000)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 10-32. - Purpose of article.
The mayor and the city council, in furtherance of their responsibility to protect the public health, safety and well-being of the citizens, enact this article providing for requirements to classify and regulate dangerous or potentially dangerous dogs and to establish certain conditions for the ownership of such dogs.
(Ord. of 2-6-1989)
Sec. 10-33. - Enforcement of article; officers; citations.
(a)
The county animal control officer shall serve as the dog control officer of the city in the enforcement of this article, along with all police officers of the city.
(b)
The mayor and city council shall name by appropriate resolution a dog control officer or any number of dog control officers required to enforce this article.
(c)
The dog control officer or officers shall be authorized to issue citations to any violators.
(Res. of 2-6-1989; Ord. of 2-6-1989, § 6.0; Amend. of 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-34. - Penalty for violation of article.
(a)
Dangerous dog owners who violate this article are guilty of a misdemeanor. Upon conviction of the violation of this article, the owner shall be fined a minimum of $100.00 for the first conviction, $500.00 for the second conviction, $750.00 for the third conviction and $1,000.00 for a fourth or subsequent conviction. No fines may be suspended in whole or in part.
(b)
Potentially dangerous dog owners who violate this article are guilty of a misdemeanor. Upon the first conviction, the owner shall be required to pay a fine in the amount of $50.00, upon the second conviction the fine of $150.00, upon the third conviction the fine of $300.00 and upon the fourth conviction the fine of $1,000.00. No fines shall be suspended in whole or in part.
(Ord. of 2-6-1989, § 5.3)
Sec. 10-35. - Court proceedings.
(a)
Violations tried upon citations. Violations of this article shall be tried upon citations, before the municipal court, with or without a prosecuting attorney. The city attorney may serve as a prosecuting attorney.
(b)
Warrants issued if felony is charged. The dog control officer shall be entitled to have warrants taken as provided in O.C.G.A. §§ 4-8-4—4-8-29 if felony charges are warranted by those O.C.G.A. sections.
(c)
Civil remedies. The dog control officer shall also be entitled to any civil remedies for injunctions, or restraints provided by the superior court.
(Ord. of 2-6-1989, § 6.1)
Sec. 10-36. - Classification procedure.
(a)
Complaint; investigation; notification of owner of classification and right of appeal. When any complaint concerning a potentially dangerous or dangerous dog has been filed with the dog control officer, or if he has received information concerning the attack of a dog on a human being, the officer shall, after any investigation and injuries, notify the owner by certified mail concerning the facts of his investigation that the animal has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and what requirements the owner shall be expected to meet. The officer shall also notify the owner that he may request a hearing to contest the classification within 15 days from receipt of the notice. If no hearing is requested, the dog's classification shall go into effect on the 15th day.
(b)
Hearing by board of health or animal control board. If the owner requests a hearing contesting the classification of his dog to that of a potentially dangerous or dangerous dog, a hearing must be provided within 30 days of the receipt of the request by the dog control officer. The city may designate the board of health to conduct such hearings or may create by resolution or ordinance an animal control board to hold hearings. If an animal control board is created, the dog control officer or anyone else involved in dog classification cannot sit on such board.
(c)
Notification of owner of hearing date; presentation of evidence; final determination. The owner must be notified of the hearing date and location by certified mail. During the hearings, the owner may have the opportunity to present any evidence or testimony on their behalf. The final determination must be provided by the city or its duly appointed representative within ten days of the hearing by certified mail to the owner. If the animal is to be given the classification as dangerous or potentially dangerous the effective date of such classification must be given.
(Ord. of 2-6-1989, § 5.0; Amend. of 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-37. - Registration of classified dog.
Dogs that have been classified as dangerous or potentially dangerous must be registered by their owners or confiscated by the dog control officer. To register such an animal, the owner must present or provide evidence to the dog control officer of the following:
(1)
An enclosure to confine the dog, such as a pen, fence or structure, which prevents the dog from escaping and children from getting in. All structures or pens shall have secure sides and a secure top. If the dog is enclosed within a fence the sides of the fence shall be a sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. The enclosure must protect the dog from the elements;
(2)
A warning sign on the owner's premises which includes a picture symbol to inform small children about the dog. The warning sign shall be provided by the city to the owner;
(3)
Liability insurance in the amount of $15,000.00 or a surety bond in the amount of $15,000.00; and
(4)
An annual registration fee levied by the city council. The annual registration fee shall be an amount as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, which are on file in the city hall.
(Ord. of 2-6-1989, § 5.1; Ord. No. 01-04-2008, § 1, 4-15-2008)
Sec. 10-38. - Regulations for dogs registered dangerous and potentially dangerous.
The dog control officer is authorized to make whatever inquiries are necessary to ensure compliance among dangerous and potentially dangerous dog owner of the items listed in this article. The City of Jackson Police Department and City of Jackson Code Enforcement are required to cooperate. Dogs that are not maintained in compliance with this article must be confiscated by the dog control officer.
(1)
The owner must notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a person, has died, or has been sold or donated.
(2)
The owner must keep the dog in an enclosure unless such dog is restrained by a strong leash and accompanied by a responsible person. Dangerous dogs must also be muzzled.
(3)
The owner must maintain all registration requirements including liability insurance or a surety bond.
(4)
Dogs that have been confiscated are to be held for 20 days. The owner may reclaim their dogs by complying with requirements and paying a confiscation fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, which are on file in the city hall, as well as any expenses incurred in caring for the dog. If the dog has not been reclaimed by its owner within 20 days, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(Ord. of 2-6-1989, § 5.2; Ord. No. 01-04-2008, § 2, 4-15-2008)

ARTICLE III. - SWINE

Sec. 10-61. - Prohibited generally.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, maintain or confine swine of any kind, in any number or in any manner, within the city.
(Ord. No. 96-15, art. I(A), 5-7-1996)
Sec. 10-62. - Potbellied pigs.
The provisions of section 10-61 shall not apply to Vietnamese potbellied miniature pigs ("potbellied pigs") that are maintained as household pets. However, to maintain a potbellied pig as a pet, the following requirements, restrictions and conditions shall apply:
(1)
All potbellied pigs that are maintained within the city limits must be registered with the city clerk within 14 days from the time the pig is taken to its place of residence. Such registration shall constitute a permit to keep and maintain potbellied miniature pigs within the city limits. Such registration shall include the name, address and telephone number of the owner, location of the pig, identification number and description of the pig and a health certificate. All potbellied pigs must have permanent identification numbers by either ear tag, fire brand, freeze brand or tattoo.
(2)
The owner of a potbellied pig must inform the city clerk within ten days of the death, change in ownership or change in location of such animal.
(3)
It shall be unlawful to keep, harbor, maintain or raise more than two potbellied pigs in any location within the city limits.
(4)
All such potbellied pigs over six months of age must be blood tested by an accredited veterinarian and certified free of brucellosis and pseudo-rabies when first registered and again upon each change of ownership. It shall be the responsibility of the owner of the pig to provide to the city clerk the initial health certificate and all change of ownership health certificates from an accredited veterinarian, which shall include the following:
a.
The name and address of the owner;
b.
The name, address and telephone number of the accredited veterinarian;
c.
Identification number and description of the animal;
d.
Certification that the animal is free of brucellosis and pseudo-rabies and date of last blood test; and
e.
Statement of general health of animal.
(5)
For purposes of this section, potbellied pigs that are kept or maintained within the city limits cannot exceed 18 inches in height at shoulder level or weigh more than 100 pounds. All potbellied pigs in excess of such height and weight restrictions are prohibited.
(6)
All potbellied pigs within the city limits must be under the owner's control and restraint by either a fenced enclosure or on a leash.
(7)
The breeding of potbellied pigs for any reason or purpose is strictly prohibited within the city limits.
(8)
All locations where potbellied pigs are kept shall be kept in a clean and sanitary condition. Exercise areas shall be cleaned of excrement on a daily basis and continuously maintained in such manner as not to cause, by odor or noise, material distress, discomfort or injury to persons of ordinary sensibilities in the immediate vicinity thereof.
(Ord. No. 96-15, art. I(B), 5-7-1996)
Sec. 10-63. - Penalty.
Any violation, disobedience, omission, neglect, failure or refusal to comply with the enforcement of any of the provisions of this article may result in the revocation of the permit to maintain such potbellied pigs within the city limits. Any person violating this article may be cited to appear before the municipal court and, upon conviction of any violation of this article, may be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 96-15, art. I(C), 5-7-1996)

While the animal control department shall be under the direction of the county board of commissioners, there is nothing in this chapter that prevents the local jurisdiction and the agents of the jurisdiction to make contact to communicate about animals through visits, letters, warnings, citations, emails, or phone calls. Sec. 10-77. - Definitions.
modified
For the purpose of this article, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them by this section:
Abandoned animal shall mean any animal which shall have been placed upon public property or within a public building unattended and uncared for, or upon or within the private property of another without the express permission of the owner, custodian or tenant of the private property and is unattended or without care. An animal shall also be considered as abandoned, for the purposes of this article, which has been upon or within the property of the owner or custodian of this animal for a period of time in excess of 36 hours unattended and without proper food, water and shelter. Abandonment shall also include failure to provide reasonable remedy or relief for any injured or diseased animal.
Animal shall be defined as follows and shall be limited to only those animals which are allowed to be kept within the corporate boundaries of the city by city ordinance or state law:
Domestic animals: Animals that are accustomed to live in or about the habitation of humans, that are not found naturally in the wild state.
Domestic/wild animals: An established strain of wild animal which is generally used in the fur or pet industry.
Feral animals: Domestic animals which have reverted back to a wild state and do not exhibit traits consistent with their intended use.
Native wildlife animals: A nondomesticated animal which is normally found in the natural state within the confines of Georgia.
Non-native or exotic wildlife animals: A nondomesticated animal which is not normally found in the natural state within the confines of Georgia.
Animal control officer shall mean any person designated by the county board of commissioners by and through the director of the county environmental department to enforce the provisions of this article. This is also any agent of the City of Jackson who is charged with addressing unlawful acts involving animals.
Animal nuisance shall mean any animal which: (1) is dangerous; (2) causes unsanitary conditions; (3) attacks passersby or passing vehicles; (4) is found at large and not under restraint three or more times within a 12-month period; (5) damages property of others; (6) excessively produces disturbing noises; (7) is a female dog while in estrus (in heat, in season) not restrained in a manner which can reasonably be expected to keep away or not attract male dogs; or (8) is inimical to the public health, welfare or safety according to the rules and regulations promulgated by the county board of health, which rules and regulations are incorporated herein and made a part hereof as if fully set out in this chapter.
Animal shelter or pound shall mean the facilities operated by the county for confining animals impounded or surrendered under the provisions of this article.
Animal under restraint means: (a) any animal secured by a leash or lead with a collar, or under supervision of a responsible and competent person at least 16 years of age, and obedient to the person's commands; (b) a dog being trained or hunted in conformance with the game laws of the state or those dogs considered to be work animals.
Certificate shall mean a certificate or document issued at the time of vaccination for rabies of a dog or cat and bearing thereon the signature of the vaccinator, the vaccination tag number, the name, color, breed and sex of the dog or cat, the name and address of the owner, and the date of the vaccination.
Current vaccination tag shall mean a metal tag issued by the state department of human resources, or other state agency, which bears a number showing that it is current for a period of one year up to three years for immunization of rabies.
Dangerous animal means an animal whose owner knows or reasonably should know presents a danger to humans or other animals because: (a) it has, without provocation, bitten or attacked a person or another animal in the past; (b) it has exhibited threatening or vicious conduct or propensities toward humans or other animals, or (c) it qualifies as a "dangerous dog" as that term is defined at O.C.G.A. § 4-8-21(a)(1).
Fence means a structure of wire, wood, stone, or other materials which is of sufficient height and strength to act as a barrier against passage of the animal or animals it is intended to enclose. "Fence" also includes so-called "invisible fence" systems which provide an electric shock which prevents an animal from leaving the property of its owner, provided the animal is a dog which is neither dangerous nor in estrus. (Dogs which are dangerous as defined in this section shall be restrained in an enclosure which not only restrains the animal from leaving the owner's property, but also protects persons who may come onto the property from harm. Female dogs in estrus (in heat, in season) shall be confined in a conventional enclosure or otherwise restrained in a way which prevents breeding not intended by the owner.)
Guard dog shall mean any dog which has been trained to attack persons or other animals independently or upon oral command and any dog which, while not so trained, is reasonably expected to perform as a guardian of the property upon and/or within which it is located.
Humane manner shall mean to provide care for an animal to include, but not necessarily limited to, adequate heat, ventilation and sanitary shelter or manner of restraint, wholesome food and water consistent with the normal requirements and feeding habits of the animal's size, species and breed; also providing of necessary veterinary medical care to insure reasonable thriftiness and relief from undue pain or suffering.
Inhumane manner means the failure to provide humane care and shall also mean restraining an animal by affixing to its collar or otherwise attaching to its body a chain, rope, cord, or other lead which is attached to the ground or a fixed object and which is not of sufficient length to allow the animal access to food and water or to move about freely in an area of reasonable size considering the size and nature of the animal.
Owner shall mean any person owning, keeping, harboring or acting as custodian or an animal for a period of 72 hours or more.
Person shall mean an individual, firm, corporation, municipality, society, or owner.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that has the potential for biting human beings on public or private property or chasing human beings in a vicious manner on public grounds without provocation at any time after February 6, 1989.
Vaccinate shall mean the proper administration, by a licensed veterinarian, of a specified dose of antirabies vaccine into a dog or cat, such vaccine having the U.S. license number approval stamped on the label of the vaccine container and having been approved by the state department of human resources, or other state agency.
Veterinarian shall mean any person who holds a license to practice the profession of veterinary medicine in the state.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 3, 4-4-2000; Ord. No. 2023-004 , 3-7-2023)
Sec. 10-78. - When collar and tag required, dog or cat.
It shall be the duty of each dog or cat owner, possessor, or custodian thereof, to provide a collar for their dog or cat to which a current rabies vaccination tag shall be attached. The collar, together with the tag, shall be worn by the dog or cat at all times, except as provided herein. Failure to comply with this section shall be unlawful and shall be punished as hereinafter provided.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 4, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-79. - Enforcement.
modified
Enforcement of this article shall be carried out substantially as follows:
(1)
The primary responsibility for the enforcement of this article shall be vested in the county board of commissioners, by and through the director of the county environmental department. The director of the environmental department may be designated by the county board of health as the official rabies control authority. (2)
Jackson Code Enforcement Officer, Public Works Director, or police officer can enforce all local ordinances including those related to animals except those which involve physical contact with the animal, transporting the animal, or when animals are dangerous or suspected to be dangerous or diseased.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 5, 4-4-2000; Ord. No. 2023-004 , 3-7-2023)
Sec. 10-80. - Impounding.
The county animal control officers are hereby authorized to impound any dangerous dog or guard dog not confined, chained or muzzled as herein provided; any female dog while in estrus (in heat, in season) not restrained within an enclosure, which can reasonably be expected to keep away or not attract male dogs; any animal not wearing the rabies inoculation tag required by the statutes of the state; and any animal deemed to be a nuisance in the city. The animal shall be confined in the animal shelter or handled as otherwise provided herein.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 6, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-81. - Notice to owners of impounded animals.
Upon impounding any dog or other animal, the county board of commissioners, by and through the director of the county environmental department or its authorized representative, shall cause to be made a prompt and reasonable effort to locate the animal's owner, possessor or custodian. Upon location thereof, notification of impounding shall be sent to this owner, possessor or custodian. For the purpose of this section, it shall be deemed sufficient notice if a notification of impounding is sent by certified mail to the owner named on the most recent rabies certificate on file at the address indicated thereon within three days. If the dog or animal is not claimed by the owner, possessor or custodian thereof within the time hereinafter specified, or if the impounded dog or animal is not wearing a rabies tag or a tag identifying the name, address and telephone number of the owner, possessor or custodian, then and in that event, the director or his authorized representative may dispose of the dog or animal as provided herein. Animals deemed in pain and suffering or to be diseased with contagious pathogens may be sacrificed expeditiously.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 7, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-82. - Fees and fines.
The personnel of the animal control shelter shall not release any animal impounded until the owner of said animal has paid all fees and charges assessed, and until the owner has prepaid for a rabies tag or tags as required by state law and by county health department rules. The fees and fines shall be established by the council and shall be amended from time to time.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 8, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-83. - Failure to claim.
(a)
Any animal which has been impounded, and is not reclaimed by its owner within seven calendar days of the date impounded, shall be subject to adoption, or to euthanasia by humane methods by the personnel of the animal control shelter. If such animal is adopted by a person other than its owner, it may be released upon prepayment of the cost of rabies vaccination and tags where applicable and an adoption fee.
(b)
Surrendered animals may be made available for adoption immediately, and the above fees shall apply.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 9, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-84. - Vaccination and licensing of dogs and cats.
(a)
The owner or possessor of each dog or cat over four months of age kept, maintained or harbored within the city or the unincorporated areas of the county shall apply for and obtain a rabies tag for such dog or cat which shall be affixed to a collar worn by such dog or cat at all times. This provision does not apply to show dogs or cats wherein the wearing of a collar may cause damage to the coat of the animal. Owners of show dogs or cats shall have in their possession the rabies tag where it may be produced on demand of animal control personnel.
(b)
Proceeds from the sale of licenses and shelter fees shall be applied to the general operating budget of the county.
(c)
Any licensed veterinarian shall be authorized to vaccinate dogs or cats against rabies; provided that at the time of such vaccination he furnish a vaccination tag issued by the state department of human resources, bearing an official serial number, to the owner. He shall furnish a certificate or certificates certifying to the vaccination of each dog or cat, sending one copy to the county animal control division and keeping a copy for his files.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to attach a vaccination tag or license tag to the collar of any animal for which it was not issued.
(e)
A license fee shall be charged for the certificate of registration as required by chapter 8 of title 4 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25) for a potentially dangerous dog.
(f)
A license fee shall be charged for a certificate of registration as required by chapter 8 of title 4 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25) for a dangerous dog.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 10, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-85. - Rabies cases to be reported.
It shall be the duty of any person knowing of a rabid animal, or of an animal showing rabid symptoms, to immediately report such animal to the animal control shelter and give as much pertinent information as possible.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 11, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-86. - Quarantine.
(a)
Where rabies has been found to exist in any animal, or where its existence is suspected, the director of the county environmental department may designate an area within which quarantine of all such animals shall be maintained and all such animals shall thereupon be immediately confined to the premises designated by the director of the environmental department in a manner approved by state and county health officials, whether or not such animal has been vaccinated against rabies.
(b)
No animal shall be removed from or brought into a quarantined area or premises without prior approval of the director of the county environmental department.
(c)
Quarantine ordered by the director of the county environmental department shall be maintained for such period as the director of the environmental department deems necessary to protect the public health.
(d)
Quarantined areas or premises, and areas where rabid animals or animals suspected of rabies remain at large, shall be posted by the director of the county environmental department with signs which read as follows: "Rabies Suspected" or "Rabies—Keep Away From Animals." Such signs shall be conspicuously displayed in a place designated by the director of the environmental department and shall not be defaced or removed except by the director of the environmental department. Signs furnished and approved by the director of the environmental department shall be used.
(e)
The owner or custodian of each animal subject to a quarantine invoked by the director of the county environmental department under terms of this regulation shall be notified of the quarantine, the particular animals subject thereto, and shall be given such other information as the director of the environmental department deems necessary.
(f)
The heads of all animals having had rabies or suspected of having had rabies at time of death shall be submitted to the director of the county environmental department for examination by the department of human resources laboratory. When necessary, the director of the environmental department can sacrifice an animal for the purpose of submitting brain tissue for rabies analysis.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 12, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-87. - Penalties for violation of article.
Any person violating the terms of this article shall be punished in the municipal court of the city as provided in section 1-11 for each separate violation.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 13, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-88. - Injunctions and claims for damage.
No injunction or claim for damage may be filed against the city, its officers and employees, or the animal control division and its employees for alleged wrong-doing arising from the enforcement of this article except upon court order after due notice and proper hearing.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 14, 4-4-2000)
Sec. 10-89. - Unlawful acts.
modified
It shall be unlawful for any person to do any of the following acts:
(1)
Abandon any animal;
(2)
Fail to keep an animal which has been deemed to be a nuisance animal under restraint;
(3)
Fail to provide a collar and display on said collar of the animal a current vaccination tag;
(4)
Fail to provide care for an animal in a humane manner;
(5)
Allow any animal to become a public nuisance;
(6)
Fail to have any animal vaccinated against rabies;
(7)
Treat any animal in an inhumane manner;
(8)
Maintain, keep or harbor any dangerous animal, except a dangerous dog registered under article II of this chapter;
(9)
Failure to clean up after animals in public spaces, including parks, sidewalks, rights-of-way, facility grounds, and the like;
(10)
Allow any animal to roam beyond the yard of the owner without supervision;
(11)
Allow any animal to defecate in a yard or on the grounds of a parcel not owned or rented by the owner of the animal without cleaning up the area completely.
(12)
Take any animal inside a business where no animals are allowed;
(13)
Cause to free any animal who is secured in animal owner's yard or home; or
(14)
Walk dogs or play with dogs in unsecured areas without a leash.
(Ord. No. 2000-2, § 15, 4-4-2000; Ord. No. 2023-004 , 3-7-2023)
Sec. 10-90. - Fines.
new
For local violations (not regulated through Butts County Animal Control), the first violation that results in a citation will have a fine of $50.00 that will be charged to the owner of the animal or the owner/renter of the home where the animal is kept or is being kept. The second violation will have a fine of $100.00 that will be charged to the owner of the animal or the owner/renter of the home where the animal permanently lives or is being temporarily housed.
Further violations will be a court appearance only and the judge will determine the fine not to exceed the maximum for code enforcement violations.
If there are multiple owners of one animal, each owner may receive a citation. If an owner cannot be established, the violation will be directed to the owner or renter where the animal permanently lives or is being temporarily housed.
( Ord. No. 2023-004 , 3-7-2023)
