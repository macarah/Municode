ARTICLE XV. - OUTDOOR GROUP PARTIES[8]

Footnotes:
--- (8) ---
Editor's note— An ordinance adopted July 7, 2020, set out provisions intended for use as §§ 42-307—42-313. For purposes of preserving the style of this Code, and at the editor's discretion, these provisions have been included as §§ 42-311—42—317.

Sec. 42-311. - Definitions.
(a)
Party shall mean a social gathering of guests.
(b)
Outdoor group party shall mean any outdoor party, including but not limited to block parties, birthday parties, celebrations, promoted gatherings and group assemblies, which would be reasonably anticipated to gather 50 or more people in an outdoor setting and is either held in any public place or is held upon private property not open to the public, and will produce sounds which would be clearly audible to persons located outside of the premises or boundaries of the lot or tract of real estate upon which such gathering takes place. This definition shall also encompass any party that interferes with or has a tendency to interfere with the normal flow or regulation of pedestrian or vehicular traffic or that occupies any public area in a place open to the public.
(c)
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company or group of any kind.
(d)
Sidewalk means that portion of the street, public easement or right-of-way lying between the portion thereof used primarily for vehicular traffic and the property line abutting such street, public easement, or right-of-way.
(Ord. of 7-7-2020 )
Sec. 42-312. - Administration and enforcement; penalties.
(a)
The provisions of this article shall be administered by any of the police department officers, code enforcement or other person officially designated by the city council to enforce the provisions of the official Code of the city or this article. The police department and code enforcement officers or other person officially designated by the city council is authorized to enter upon private or public property to examine any outdoor group party and to issue any citation for a violation of this article.
(b)
Proceedings to enforce this article shall be tried in the municipal court. Any person, firm, or corporation who pleads guilty, nolo contendre or is convicted of violating the provisions of this article may be punished according to the provisions of section 1-11 of the official Code of the city.
(c)
In addition to its right to seek all legal and equitable remedies, the city shall have the express right to seek injunctive relief against any person holding or conducting an outdoor group party in violation of this article. Nothing contained in this article shall be construed as granting or vesting in any person or entity other than the city, its council or agents, any right of enforcement by legal, equitable or injunctive process.
(Ord. of 7-7-2020 )
Sec. 42-313. - Parking of vehicles.
The applicant and person in charge of the premises upon which an outdoor group party is conducted shall control the parking of vehicles of patrons so as to avoid congestion and hazardous conditions. No parking shall be allowed on city sidewalks. Failure to comply with this provision shall be sufficient cause for the outdoor group party to be immediately terminated.
(Ord. of 7-7-2020 )
Sec. 42-314. - Required.
(a)
Any person who owns the property upon which an outdoor group party will take place or any person conducting or promoting an outdoor group party must obtain a permit from the city clerk prior to the date of the outdoor group party.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow, conduct or participate in an outdoor group party in the city without first obtaining a permit from the city clerk.
(c)
Each permit issued under this article must be displayed on the premises upon which the outdoor group party is conducted throughout the entire period of the permitted outdoor group party.
(Ord. of 7-7-2020 )
Sec. 42-315. - Applications.
All applications must be received by the clerk two weeks prior to any outdoor group party.
At the time any person makes a request for the issuance of a permit under this article, the clerk shall obtain the following information:
(1)
Name of the person that will be organizing and/or conducting the outdoor group party.
(2)
Name of the owners of property upon which the outdoor group party will be conducted.
(3)
Location at which the outdoor group party will be conducted.
(4)
Date of the outdoor group party.
(5)
Time the outdoor group party will begin and end.
(Ord. of 7-7-2020 )
Sec. 42-316. - Interruption of outdoor group party.
If a permit holder commences an outdoor group party and the outdoor group party is interrupted or terminated for any reason, the holder of the permit shall not be allowed to restart the outdoor group assembly after the date and time permitted without obtaining a new permit.
(Ord. of 7-7-2020 )
Sec. 42-317. - Fees.
(a)
If the outdoor group party is conducted on residential property or on church/civic/nonprofit organization property, two free permits may be obtained by an applicant during a calendar year period without payment of a fee. Any additional permits will be issued upon the payment to the clerk of a fee of $25.00.
(b)
If the outdoor group party is conducted on commercial property a permit will be issued upon payment of a fee of $25.00. A letter of approval must be presented with the application from the property owner or manager of any business located on the property.
(c)
Fees paid for a permit under this section are nonrefundable.
(Ord. of 7-7-2020 )
