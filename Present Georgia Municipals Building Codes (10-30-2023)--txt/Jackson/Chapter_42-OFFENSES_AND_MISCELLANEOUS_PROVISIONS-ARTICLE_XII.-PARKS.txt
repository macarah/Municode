Sec. 42-267. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the term "park" or "recreational area" means a park or recreational area which is under the custody or control of the city or the county board of commissioners, whether owned or leased by the city or the county board of commissioners, and which is used for recreation by the general public.
(Ord. No. 2000-3, 7-18-2000)
Sec. 42-268. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, in any manner, to mark on, deface, injure, displace, dig, excavate, remove, or construct on any real or personal property in any park or recreational area, except when done with the written permission of the city council, the county board of commissioners or the director of the parks and recreation department.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive a vehicle in excess of 15 miles per hour within 200 feet of any intensive-use area in the park. As used in this subsection, the term "vehicle" means any wheeled conveyance for the transportation of persons or materials. As used in this subsection, the term "intensive-use area" means a picnic area, a pool area, a check-in station, or a ball or sporting event field.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to hunt, trap, or otherwise pursue or catch any wildlife in any park or recreational area. It shall also be unlawful to shoot into a park or recreational area from beyond its boundaries.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any intoxicated person to enter or remain in any park or recreational area. It shall also be unlawful for any person to consume or use alcoholic beverages or intoxicants in any park or recreational area.
(e)
It shall be unlawful in any park or recreational area to be under the influence of any illegal drug or to solicit the purchase of drugs or to sell drugs.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use or possess in any park or recreational area any fireworks, explosive, or firecrackers unless such use has been granted prior written approval by the city council or the county board of commissioners. It shall also be unlawful for any person to use or possess in any park or recreational area any firearms, bows and arrows, spring guns, air rifles, slingshots, or any other device which discharges projectiles by any means unless the device is unloaded and stored so as not to be readily accessible.
(g)
It shall be unlawful to bring any animals, pets, domesticated animals, or reptiles, including, without limitation, dogs and cats, into any park or recreational area, with the exception of trained guide dogs or service dogs. Certain events, however, that by their nature require the presence of animals are exempt from this prohibition, such as rodeo events, horse shows, dog shows and farm exhibitions. To be exempt, persons attending such events must have the prior written approval of the director of the parks and recreation department.
(h)
It shall be unlawful to refuse to leave a park or recreational area after being directed to do so by any law enforcement officer or any authorized representative of the parks and recreation department.
(i)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle overnight in any park or recreational area without the prior written approval of the director of the parks and recreation department.
(j)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle at any place within any park or recreational area where signs are placed prohibiting such parking unless displaying a valid parking permit.
(k)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any person to swim or wade in any lake or pond located on property belonging to the City of Jackson.
(l)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any person to fish in any lake or pond located on property belonging to the City of Jackson.
(Ord. No. 2000-3, 7-18-2000; Ord. No. 08(01)-2012, 8-7-2012)
Sec. 42-269. - Tobacco use policy.
(a)
The city council and the county board of commissioners have a responsibility to provide and maintain a healthful and comfortable environment for participants and spectators at the various centers, sports arenas and athletic fields utilized in the parks and recreational department programs; and the parks and recreation department has facility joint use agreements involving the city (cities) and county government and the public school system and has a responsibility to uphold the usage policies and procedures of these facility owners.
Accordingly, based on these responsibilities, the use of tobacco products shall be restricted as follows:
(1)
In facilities owned by the city and the county board of commissioners there shall be no tobacco use:
a.
Inside any center or gymnasium or public area within the same. "Public area" means any area or portion thereof, to which the public is invited or in which the public is permitted, including but not limited to lobbies, reception areas, offices and restrooms.
b.
On athletic fields, courts and tracks, to include the coaching areas and playing field, inside dugouts, spectator viewing areas, restrooms, concession stands or press boxes.
Note: Designated smoking areas will be established for each of the outdoor athletic fields.
(2)
In joint-use facilities belonging to the public school system, the city (cities) or private, etc., which have their own tobacco use policies, parks and recreation participants, officials and spectators shall abide by the facility owner's policy regarding the use of tobacco products. In the absence of an owner's policy regarding tobacco use, the policies established for facilities managed by the parks and recreation department (subsection (1) above) shall be followed.
(Ord. No. 2000-3, 7-18-2000)

Sec. 42-270. - General penalty; continuing violations; authority granted to court.
(a)
The violation of any provision of this article shall be punishable in the municipal court of the city by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment in the city jail not exceeding six months or both.
(b)
The judge of the municipal court of the city shall have the power and authority to:
(1)
Impose upon persons convicted in the municipal court the fines provided for in this article, or as otherwise provided by state law;
(2)
Sentence such person to community service work; or
(3)
Impose a sentence consisting of any combination of the penalties provided for in this section.
(Ord. No. 2000-3, 7-18-2000)
Sec. 42-271. - Jackson Veterans Memorial Park rules and regulations.
(a)
Closing hours. Jackson Veterans Memorial Park shall be closed from midnight until 6:00 a.m. of each day.
Fine: $125.00
(b)
Functions and gatherings. Functions, gatherings or other group activities in the park area are prohibited except with written permission in advance from the council of the City of Jackson. Groups are defined as ten people or more. Such activities shall be limited to recognized organizations, residents or other groups obtaining proper city council authorization. The person named on the permit agrees to assume responsibility for the group, and shall also be responsible for clean-up of the area and for the conduct of all members of the using group. Persons requesting permission for group use must be at least 18 years of age. A police officer will be on duty if deemed necessary by the Jackson Police Department and/or city council and the user shall pay all costs associated to providing police coverage. A fee, and/or security deposit may be required to cover clean-up or any other expenses, as determined by the city council. Special functions in the park may extend beyond the normal open hours of 6:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight by permit approved by the chief of police and city council when received in advance and in writing; provided assurance of adequate supervision is made in advance. After authorized use whatsoever of the park grounds, the grounds shall be left clean and orderly.
Fine: $555.00
(c)
Eternal flame and fires. Interference with the eternal flame is forbidden. It shall be unlawful for any person to touch the flame for any reason. It shall be unlawful for any person to build a fire in the park.
Fine: $335.00
(d)
Alcoholic beverages. It shall be unlawful for any person to have in his possession, custody or control any alcoholic beverage of any kind whatsoever in the park. It shall also be unlawful for any intoxicated person to enter or remain in the park.
Fine: $260.00
(e)
Admittance of pets. It shall be unlawful for any person to bring any pet into the park and let it run at large, or to leave any pet unattended. Horses and livestock are not permitted in the park. Animals in the park must be restrained. Defecation must be picked up and removed. Urination on monuments, flag poles, benches, eternal flame, light poles and podium is prohibited.
Fine: $335.00
(f)
Solid waste, litter control. It shall be unlawful for any person to place or leave any rubbish, litter, garbage, refuse, cigarette butts or debris of any nature within the park except within designated receptacles provided by the city. No person or persons shall throw or deposit any type of debris or waste material on or along any city park roadway or park.
Fine: $300.00
(g)
Posting, distributing circulars. It shall be unlawful for any person to post or distribute any circular or pamphlet, or place any circular or pamphlet in or on any vehicle in the park area whether the vehicle is occupied or not.
Fine: $335.00
(h)
Protection of vegetation. It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully or maliciously injure, cut down or destroy any fruit, shade or ornamental tree or shrub standing or being within the park.
Fine: $555.00
(i)
Vandalism. It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with, break down, deface or remove, or cause to be interfered with any park fixtures, grounds and property. Any damage to any park property will be considered vandalism.
Fine: $1,000.00
(j)
Resisting officers. It shall be unlawful for any person to disobey or disregard the orders of any police officer when commanding any person to desist in violation of any provisions of this chapter or the regulations prescribed by the city or state statutes.
Fine: $805.00
(k)
Tents, trailers, camping and sleeping. The erection of tents and/or parking of trailers or mobile homes in park area are prohibited. No camping or sleeping is permitted in the park. Stakes that puncture the ground are prohibited.
Fine: $555.00
(l)
Climbing restricted. It shall be unlawful for any person to climb on park structures or any park equipment.
Fine: $225.00
(m)
Discharge of firearms. It shall be unlawful for any person to fire or discharge any firearms of any description in any part of the city, or have any firearm in his possession or under his control, within the confined area of the park unless you have the written permission from the chief of police and city council.
Fine: $355.00
(n)
Commercial vendors. No commercial vendors will be allowed in the park.
Fine: $505.00
(o)
Loitering. Jackson Veterans Memorial Park has been established as a memorial. There shall be no loitering or picnicking.
Fine: $125.00
(p)
Solicitation. No solicitation of any type is permitted in the park. All commercial sale activities are prohibited except with the written permission of the city council.
Fine: $505.00
(q)
Smoking. It shall be unlawful for any person to inhale, exhale, and burn or carry any lighted cigar, cigarette, pipe, weed, plant or other combustible substance in any manner or in any form within the park area.
Fine: $125.00
(r)
Skateboards, bicycles, unlicensed motorized vehicles. With the exception of wheelchairs, all skateboards, bicycles and unlicensed motorized vehicles shall be prohibited in the park.
Fine: $225.00
(s)
Additions to memorial. It shall be unlawful to add anything to the memorial park without the written permission from the city council.
Fine: $225.00
(t)
Penalty. Any person violating any provision of this article may be cited to appear before the municipal court. Upon conviction of any violation of this article, the offender may be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 2011-11-01, 11-1-2011)
